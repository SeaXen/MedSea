## 1️⃣ HLA System

### HLA Structure & Expression
| Class | Genes | Expression | Function |
|-------|-------|------------|----------|
| **Class I** | **HLA-A, HLA-B, HLA-C** | **All nucleated cells** | Present **endogenous peptides** (8-10aa) to **CD8+ T cells** |
| **Class II** | **HLA-DR, HLA-DQ, HLA-DP** | **APCs** (DC, B cells, Macrophages, Thymic epithelium) | Present **exogenous peptides** (13-25aa) to **CD4+ T cells** |
| **Class III** | Complement (C2, C4, Factor B), TNF, LTA, HSP70 | Various | Inflammation, Complement |

### HLA Nomenclature
| Format | Example | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Allele** | HLA-A*02:01 | HLA-A locus, allele group 02, specific protein 01 |
| **Serological** | HLA-A2 | Historical serological equivalent |
| **Haplotype** | HLA-A1, B8, DR3 | Set of alleles inherited together |

### HLA Matching in Transplantation
| Transplant Type | Matching Priority |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Kidney** | **HLA-A, -B, -DR** (6/6 ideal) — HLA-DR most important |
| **Liver** | **HLA-A, -B, -DR** (Less critical, HLA matching less critical) |
| **Heart/Lung** | **HLA-A, -B, -DR** (Size/urgency prioritized) |
| **Pancreas** | **HLA-A, -B, -DR** (Similar to kidney, DR priority) — Often simultaneous kidney-pancreas (SPK) |
| **Intestine** | **HLA-A, -B, -DR** (Less studied, high rejection risk) — Multivisceral often |
| **HSCT** | **HLA-A, -B, -C, -DRB1, -DQB1** (10/10 ideal) — **High-resolution** |

### Epitope Matching (Eplet Mismatch)
| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Eplet** | **Polymorphic amino acid configuration** on HLA surface recognised by antibodies |
| **Eplet Mismatch Load** | **Number of mismatched eplets** — Better predictor of DSA development than antigen mismatch |
| **Permissible Mismatches** | Eplets shared between donor/recipient → **Acceptable** |

## 2️⃣ Allorecognition Pathways

| Pathway | Mechanism | Key Features | Relevance |
|---------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| **Direct** | Recipient T cells recognise **Intact Donor HLA** on Donor APCs (Passenger leukocytes) | **Strong, Early** (Peaks 1-2w), Dominates **Acute Cellular Rejection** | Dominant in **early ACR** |
| **Indirect** | Recipient T cells recognise **Processed Donor HLA Peptides** on **Recipient APCs** | **Weaker, Chronic**, Drives **Chronic Rejection**, **Antibody-Mediated Rejection** | Dominant in **Chronic Rejection**, **AMR** |
| **Semi-Direct** | Recipient APCs acquire **Intact Donor HLA** (Exosomes, Trogocytosis) → Present to Recipient T cells | **Intermediate**, Contributes to both ACR and AMR | Emerging role |

## 3️⃣ Rejection — Classification & Mechanisms

### Banff Classification (Kidney) — Major Categories
| Type | Mechanism | Timing | Key Features |
|------|-----------|--------|--------------|
| **Hyperacute** | **Pre-formed DSA** → Complement activation → **Immediate thrombosis, Necrosis** | **Minutes-Hours** | **Graft loss** (Irreversible), **CDC-XM Positive** |
| **Acute Cellular Rejection (ACR)** | **Host T cells → Donor HLA** (Direct > Indirect) | **Days-Weeks** (Peak 1-3m) | **Banff t-Score** (t1/t2/t3), **Tubulitis (t), Interstitial Inflammation (i)** |
| **Antibody-Mediated Rejection (AMR)** | **Donor-Specific Antibodies (DSA)** → Complement Activation, Endothelial Injury | **Hours-Months** | **C4d+ (C4d2/3), DSA+, Microvascular Inflammation (g/ptc), Endothelial Changes** |
| **Chronic Active AMR** | **DSA + Chronic Endothelial Injury** | **Months-Years** | **Transplant Glomerulopathy (cg), Peritubular Capillary Basement Membrane Multilayering** |
| **Chronic Active T-Cell Mediated** | **Persistent T-cell Alloresponse** | **Months-Years** | **Arterial Intimal Fibrosis (cv), Interstitial Fibrosis/Tubular Atrophy (IF/TA)** |

### Banff Scoring (Kidney) — Key Lesions
| Lesion | Score | Significance |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| **t (Tubulitis)** | t0 (0), t1 (1-4), t2 (5-10), t3 (>10) | ACR Severity |
| **i (Interstitial Inflammation)** | i0 (0%), i1 (10-25%), i2 (26-50%), i3 (>50%) | ACR Severity |
| **g (Glomerulitis)** | g0-g3 | AMR Activity |
| **ptc (Peritubular Capillaritis)** | ptc0-ptc3 | AMR Activity |
| **cg (Transplant Glomerulopathy)** | cg0-cg3 | Chronic AMR |
| **cv (Vascular Intimal Fibrosis)** | cv0-cv3 | Chronic Vascular Rejection |
| **C4d (Peritubular Capillaries)** | C4d0 (Negative), C4d1 (Minimal), C4d2 (Focal), C4d3 (Diffuse) | **AMR Marker** |

### Rejection — Diagnosis & Treatment

| Rejection Type | Diagnosis | First-Line Treatment |
|----------------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Hyperacute** | **CDC-XM Positive**, Immediate graft failure | **None** (Graft loss) — Prevention: **CDC-XM Negative** |
| **ACR (TCMR)** | **Biopsy (Banff t/i scores)**, DSA Negative | **Pulse Methylpred 500-1000mg x3-5d** → Optimise Maintenance IS |
| **AMR** | **DSA+**, C4d+, Microvascular Inflammation (g+ptc) | **Pulse Methylpred**, **PLEX/IA** (5-7 exchanges), **IVIG 2g/kg**, **Rituximab** (Anti-CD20), **Eculizumab** (Refractory) |
| **Chronic AMR** | DSA+, cg>0, ptcml, C4d+ | **Optimise IS**, **PLEX/IVIG**, **Bortezomib** (Plasma cells), **Eculizumab** (Refractory) |
| **Chronic TCMR** | cv>0, IF/TA | Optimise IS, **Belatacept** (CNI-sparing), Control BP/Lipids |

## 4️⃣ Immunosuppression

### Induction Therapy (Peri-Transplant)
| Agent | Mechanism | Indication | Key Points |
|-------|-----------|------------|------------|
| **Basiliximab** | Anti-CD25 (IL-2Rα) | **Standard risk** (Low immunologic risk) | **Non-depleting**, Low infection risk |
| **Alemtuzumab** (Campath) | Anti-CD52 (Pan-lymphocyte depletion) | **High immunologic risk** (Re-transplant, PRA>80%, ABOi) | **Profound Lymphodepletion**, ↑ Infection/CMV |
| **ATG (Thymoglobulin)** | Anti-Thymocyte Globulin (Polyclonal) | **High Risk**, Delayed Graft Function | **Profound Depletion**, Cytokine Release Syndrome |
| **Rituximab** | Anti-CD20 (B-cell depletion) | **ABOi, HLAi, AMR Prophylaxis** | B-cell depletion, IgG decline |

### Maintenance Immunosuppression (Triple Therapy Standard)

| Drug Class | Agents | Mechanism | Key Monitoring | Key Toxicities |
|------------|--------|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| **CNI (Calcineurin Inhibitors)** | **Tacrolimus (Tac)**, **Cyclosporine (CsA)** | Inhibit Calcineurin → ↓ IL-2 Transcription | **Trough Levels (C0)**, Renal Function, Glucose, Lipids, K⁺, Mg²⁺ | **Nephrotoxicity**, Neurotoxicity, HTN, Diabetes, Hyperkalaemia |
| **mTOR Inhibitors** | **Sirolimus (SRL)**, **Everolimus (EVR)** | Inhibit mTOR → ↓ Protein Synthesis, Cell Cycle Arrest | **Trough Levels**, Lipids, CBC, Wound Healing | **Hyperlipidaemia**, Thrombocytopenia, **Impaired Wound Healing**, Proteinuria, Pneumonitis |
| **Antimetabolites** | **Mycophenolate (MMF/MPA/EC-MPS)**, **Azathioprine (AZA)** | Inhibit IMPDH (MMF) / Purine Synthesis (AZA) | **CBC, LFT, Renal**, TPMT (AZA) | **GI (Diarrhoea/Nausea)**, Leukopenia, Teratogenicity (MMF) |
| **Steroids** | **Prednisolone**, **Methylprednisolone** | Genomic/Non-genomic Anti-inflammatory | **BP, Glucose, Bone, Eyes, Weight**, Cataracts | **Infection, Diabetes, Osteoporosis, Avascular Necrosis, Cataracts** |
| **Costimulation Blocker** | **Belatacept** (CTLA-4-Ig) | Blocks CD28-CD80/86 → **No Co-stimulation** | **Monthly IV**, Renal Function, EBV IgG (Pre-req) | **PTLD (EBV-), Infection**, Infusion Reactions |

### CNI Minimisation / Belatacept Conversion
| Scenario | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| **CNI Nephrotoxicity** | **CNI → Belatacept** (BENEFIT/EXTEND Trials), or **CNI → mTOR** (Conversion) |
| **CNI Minimisation** | **Low-dose CNI + MMF + Belatacept** (Viking Trial) |
| **CNI-Free** | **Belatacept + MMF + Steroid** (EBV+ Recipients Only) |

### Tacrolimus vs Cyclosporine
| Feature | Tacrolimus | Cyclosporine |
|---------|------------|--------------|
| **Potency** | **More Potent** (2-4x) | Less Potent |
| **Nephrotoxicity** | Similar | Similar |
| **Neurotoxicity** | **Tremor, Headache, Insomnia, PRES** | Less |
| **Diabetes (PTDM)** | **Higher Incidence** | Lower |
| **Hyperlipidaemia** | Lower | **Higher** |
| **Hypertension** | Lower | Higher |
| **Cosmetic** | Alopecia, Hirsutism | **Gingival Hyperplasia, Hirsutism** |
| **Levels** | **C0 (Trough)** | **C0 or C2 (2h Post-dose)** |

## 5️⃣ Haematopoietic Stem Cell Transplantation (HSCT)

### Indications
| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Malignant** | AML, ALL, Lymphoma (HL, NHL), Myeloma, MDS, MPN, CML (TKI Failure) |
| **Non-Malignant** | **SCID**, **Thalassaemia Major**, **Sickle Cell**, **Aplastic Anaemia**, **HLH**, **Inborn Errors of Metabolism**, **Autoimmune (Refractory)** |

### Donor Sources (Hierarchy)
| Source | Match | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|--------|-------|------------|----------------|
| **MSD (Matched Sibling Donor)** | 10/10 HLA | Best OS, Lowest GvHD | Only 25-30% have MSD |
| **MUD (Matched Unrelated Donor)** | 10/10 or 9/10 | Large Registries (Anthony Nolan, DKMS) | Slightly Higher GvHD |
| **Haploidentical** | **Haplo (5/10)** | **Universal Donor (Parents, Siblings, Children)** | **PTCy Required** (Post-Transplant Cyclophosphamide) |
| **Cord Blood** | 4-6/6 | **Rapid Availability**, Lower GvHD | **Low Cell Dose**, Delayed Engraftment, Higher Relapse |
| **Mismatched Unrelated** | 9/10, 8/10 | Alternative if no MUD | Higher GvHD |

### Conditioning Regimens
| Type | Regimen | Indication | Key Features |
|------|---------|------------|--------------|
| **Myeloablative (MAC)** | **Busulfan/Cyclophosphamide**, **TBI/Cyclophosphamide** | **Young, Fit, AML/ALL** | **Full Ablation**, High Toxicity, Faster Engraftment |
| **Reduced Intensity (RIC)** | **Fludarabine + Melphalan/Busulfan (Low Dose)** | **Older, Comorbid, AML in CR** | **Less Toxicity**, Relies on GvL Effect, Slower Engraftment |
| **Non-Myeloablative (NMA)** | **Fludarabine + Low-Dose TBI (2Gy)** | **Elderly, Comorbid** | **Minimal Toxicity**, Relies Entirely on GvL |

### Graft-versus-Host Disease (GvHD)

| Type | Timing | Key Features | Grading |
|------|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Acute GvHD (aGvHD)** | **<100 Days** (Classical), **Late aGvHD** (100d-1y) | **Skin (Rash)**, **GI (Diarrhoea, Nausea)**, **Liver (Bilirubin ↑)**, **Lung (Bronchiolitis Obliterans — Late)** | **MAGIC/Glucksberg** (Skin 1-4, GI 1-4, Liver 1-4) → **Overall Grade I-IV** |
| **Chronic GvHD (cGvHD)** | **>100 Days** | **Sclerotic (Scleroderma-like), Sicca, Bronchiolitis Obliterans, Fasciitis, Ocular**, Autoimmunity | **NIH Consensus 2014/2020** (Mild/Moderate/Severe) |

### GvHD Prophylaxis
| Regimen | Components | Key Points |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Standard (MSD/MUD)** | **CNI (Tac) + Antimetabolite (MMF/MTX)** + **Steroid** | MTX (Days 1,3,6,11) or MMF |
| **Haploidentical** | **PTCy (Post-Transplant Cyclophosphamide 50mg/kg Day +3, +4)** + **Tac + MMF** | **Standard of Care**, Reduces GvHD without increasing relapse |
| **Cord Blood** | **CNI + MMF/MTX** | Higher GVHD Risk |
| **ATG/Serotherapy** | **ATG (Thymoglobulin) or Alemtuzumab** | In RIC, Haplo, T-cell Depleted Grafts |

### GvHD Treatment
| Severity | Acute GvHD | Chronic GvHD |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| **Mild (Grade I)** | **Topical Steroids** (Skin), **Budesonide/Beclomethasone** (GI) | **Topical/Steroids**, **Tacrolimus** (Systemic low-dose) |
| **Moderate (Grade II)** | **Systemic Steroids** (Pred 1-2mg/kg) | **Systemic Steroids** (Pred 0.5-1mg/kg), **Ecp** (ECP) |
| **Severe (Grade III-IV)** | **High-Dose Steroids** (MP 2mg/kg), **Add Antithymocyte Globulin (ATG)** / **Ruxolitinib** (JAK1/2i) | **High-Dose Steroids**, **Ruxolitinib (JAK1/2i)**, **Ecp**, **Ibrutinib** (BTKi), **Belumosudil** (ROCK2i) |
| **Refractory** | **Ruxolitinib (REACH2/3)**, **Ecp**, **Tofacitinib**, **MSC**, **Anti-CD47** | **Ruxolitinib**, **Ibrutinib**, **Belumosudil**, **Ecp**, **Extracorporeal Photopheresis** |

### Engraftment & Chimerism
| Milestone | Timing | Significance |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| **Neutrophil Engraftment** | **ANC >0.5x10⁹/L** (3 consecutive days) | **Day +14 to +21 (MAC)**, **+21 to +28 (RIC)** |
| **Platelet Engraftment** | **Plt >20x10⁹/L** (Unsupported 7d) | **Day +21 to +35** |
| **Full Donor Chimerism** | **>95% Donor** | **Day +30 to +100** |
| **Mixed Chimerism** | **Recipient + Donor** | **RIC/NMA**, May be Stable (DLI if Relapse) |
| **Chimerism Monitoring** | **STR/NGS (Peripheral Blood, BM)** | **Monthly x3m**, then q3m x1y, then q6m |

## 6️⃣ Post-Transplant Monitoring

### Kidney Transplant
| Parameter | Frequency | Action Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|------------------|
| **Serum Creatinine / eGFR** | **Weekly x1m, Biweekly x3m, Monthly x1y, q3m** | **↑>20% from Baseline** → Biopsy |
| **Proteinuria (uPCR/uACR)** | Monthly x3m, q3m | **>0.5g/g** → Biopsy |
| **DSA (HLA Class I/II)** | **Month 1, 3, 6, 12, then q6-12m** | **New DSA / Rising MFI** → Biopsy ± PLEX/IVIG |
| **dd-cfDNA** | **Month 1, 3, 6, 12, q6m** | **>1% (Kidney), >0.5% (Heart)** → Biopsy |
| **Doppler US (Renal Arterial/ Venous Flow)** | Post-op Day 1, 1w, 1m, 3m, 6m, 1y | **Resistive Index >0.8**, Arterial Stenosis |
| **BK Virus (Urine/Plasma PCR)** | **Monthly x6m, q3m x2y** | **>10⁴ copies/mL** → Reduce IS, Leflunomide/Cidofovir |
| **CMV/EBV PCR** | **Weekly x3m, q2w x6m** | **CMV >1000 IU/mL** → Pre-emptive Valganciclovir |

### Heart/Lung Transplant Monitoring
| Parameter | Frequency | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **Endomyocardial Biopsy (EMB)** | **Weekly x4w, Biweekly x3m, Monthly x1y, q3m** | **ISHLT Grade ≥2R** → Augment IS |
| **DSA / dd-cfDNA** | q3-6m | **DSA+ / dd-cfDNA >0.5%** → Biopsy |
| **Spirometry (FEV1)** | **Weekly x1m, Monthly x6m, q3m** | **FEV1 Decline >10%** → Biopsy, Augment IS |
| **Bronchoscopy + BAL** | Surveillance (3m, 12m) | Infection, Rejection, Lymphocytic Bronchitis |

### Infection Prophylaxis (Post-Transplant)

| Period | Key Prophylaxis |
|--------|-----------------|
| **0-1 Month** | **PJP (Co-trimoxazole)**, **HSV/VZV (Acyclovir/Valaciclovir)**, **Antibacterial** (Co-trimoxazole covers PJP+UTI) |
| **1-6 Months** | **CMV (Valganciclovir Pre-emptive / Prophylaxis)** — **High Risk (D+/R-): 3-6m**, **BK Virus Monitoring**, **Fungal (Posaconazole/Voriconazole if High Risk)**, **Adenovirus PCR monthly** (Pediatric/HSCT), **HHV-6 PCR** (HSCT), **Aspergillus** (Posaconazole/Voriconazole if high risk) |
| **6-12 Months** | **CMV Prophylaxis Ends**, **BK Virus Monitoring**, **Annual Flu**, **Pneumococcal** |
| **>1 Year** | **Annual Flu**, **Pneumococcal (PCV20 → PPV23)**, **Shingrix (If >50)**, **COVID Boosters** |

## 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"Hyperacute = ACR"** | **NO.** Hyperacute = Pre-formed DSA, Minutes-Hours, Irreversible; ACR = T-cell, Days-Weeks, Reversible |
| **"C4d+ = AMR Always"** | **NO.** C4d can be negative in AMR (C4d-negative AMR); **C4d+ without DSA** = Not AMR (Accommodation) |
| **"ACR = Only T-Cell"** | **NO.** ACR is **T-cell mediated** but **AMR** can coexist (**Mixed Rejection**) |
| **"ACR = Steroid Resistant = AMR"** | **NO.** Steroid-Resistant ACR may need **Ruxolitinib/ATG**, Not automatically AMR |
| **"Belatacept = For All Transplants"** | **NO.** **EBV+ Only** (PTLD Risk if EBV-), **Kidney Only** (Not Heart/Lung approved), Monthly IV |
| **"PTCy = Only for Haplo"** | **Also Used** in MUD with ATG, Some Cord Blood Protocols, Mismatched Unrelated |
| **"aGvHD = Only Skin"** | **NO.** Triad: **Skin (Rash), GI (Diarrhoea), Liver (Bilirubin)** — Lung (BOOP) in Late aGvHD |
| **"cGvHD = Just Skin"** | **NO.** **Multi-organ**: Skin (Sclerotic), Eyes (Sicca), Mouth (Lichenoid), Lung (BOS), Liver, GI, Fascia, Genital |
| **"Chronic Rejection = Only AMR"** | **NO.** **Chronic Active TCMR** (cv, IF/TA) + **Chronic Active AMR** (cg, ptcml) often Coexist |
| **"dd-cfDNA = Replaces Biopsy"** | **NO.** **Adjunct Only** — **Biopsy = Gold Standard**; dd-cfDNA = Surveillance/Rule-Out |
| **"Belatacept = CNI Replacement for All"** | **NO.** **EBV+ Only**, Not for Heart/Lung (Higher Rejection), Monthly IV Infusion |
| **"PTCy = No GvHD"** | **Reduces GvHD** (Grade III-IV ↓) but **Does Not Eliminate**; cGvHD Still Occurs |
| **"cGvHD = Just Fibrosis"** | **NO.** **Multisystem**: Skin (Sclerotic), Eyes (Sicca), Mouth (Lichenoid), Lung (BOS), GI, Liver, Fascia, Genital |
| **"Belatacept = No Infection Risk"** | **NO.** ↑ **PTLD (EBV-)**, ↑ **CMV**, ↑ **BK Virus** vs CNI (Different Profile) |
| **"cGvHD = Treated with Steroids Only"** | **NO.** **Ruxolitinib** (1st-line Refractory), **Ibrutinib**, **Belumosudil**, **ECP**, **Extracorporeal Photopheresis** |

> **Mnemonic: TRANSPLANT IMMUNOLOGY**  
> **T**ransplant Immunology: **HLA** (Class I A/B/C → CD8; Class II DR/DQ/DP → CD4)  
> **R**ejection: **Hyperacute (DSA, CDC-XM+), ACR (Banff t/i, Pulse Steroid), AMR (DSA+, C4d+, g/ptc, PLEX/IVIG/Ritux)**  
> **A**llorecognition: **Direct (Donor APC), Indirect (Recipient APC), Semi-Direct**  
> **N**ephrology Rejection: **Banff t/i (ACR), cg/cv/ptc (Chronic), C4d/g/ptc (AMR)**

## 📌 Summary
- **HLA**: Class I (A,B,C) → CD8; Class II (DR,DQ,DP) → CD4; Matching: Kidney A,B,DR; HSCT 10/10 (A,B,C,DRB1,DQB1)
- **Allorecognition**: **Direct** = Intact Donor HLA on Donor APC → **Early ACR**; **Indirect** = Processed Donor Peptides on Recipient APC → **Chronic Rejection/AMR**
- **Rejection**: **Hyperacute** (DSA, CDC-XM+, Minutes); **ACR** (Banff t/i, Pulse Steroids); **AMR** (DSA+, C4d+, g+ptc, PLEX/IVIG/Rituximab); **Chronic** (cg, cv, IF/TA)
- **Immunosuppression**: **Induction** (Basiliximab Standard, ATG/Alemtuzumab High Risk); **Maintenance**: **Tac + MMF + Pred** (Standard), **Belatacept** (EBV+, CNI-Free, Monthly IV); **mTOR** (Sirolimus/Everolimus — Lipids, Wound, Proteinuria)
- **HSCT**: Indications (AML, Thal, SCD, Aplastic, SID); **Donor**: MSD > MUD > Haplo (PTCy) > Cord; **Conditioning**: MAC (Bu/Cy) vs RIC (Flu/Mel); **GvHD Prophylaxis**: PTCy (Haplo), Tac+MTX/MMF (Standard)
- **GvHD**: aGvHD <100d (Skin/GI/Liver, MAGIC Grading); cGvHD >100d (Sclerotic/Sicca/BOS, NIH Consensus); **Treatment**: Steroids → **Ruxolitinib** (1st-line Refractory) → ECP/Ibrutinib/Belumosudil/ECP
- **Monitoring**: Kidney (Cr, DSA, dd-cfDNA>1%, BKV, CMV); Heart (EMB ISHLT, DSA, dd-cfDNA>0.5%); HSCT (Chimerism STR, Immune Reconstitution)
- **Infections**: PJP (Cotrimoxazole), CMV (Valganciclovir D+/R-), HSV/VZV (Acyclovir), BKV (PCR, Reduce IS)
- **Belatacept**: **EBV+ Only**, CNI-Free, Monthly IV, ↑ PTLD if EBV-

## ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **Hyperacute rejection mechanism?**  
   A. T-cell mediated  B. **Pre-formed DSA + Complement Activation**  C. Cytokine Storm  D. Macrophage Activation  
   *Answer: B. Pre-formed DSA bind donor endothelium → Complement activation → Thrombosis/Necrosis within minutes-hours.*

2. **Direct allorecognition involves:**  
   A. Recipient T cells + Donor peptides on Recipient APC  B. **Recipient T cells + Intact Donor HLA on Donor APC**  C. Donor T cells + Recipient HLA  D. Antibody-mediated  
   *Answer: B. Direct = Recipient T cells recognise Intact Donor HLA on Donor APCs (Passenger Leukocytes).*

3. **AMR diagnostic triad (Banff)?**  
   A. DSA+, C4d+, Glomerulitis  B. **DSA+, C4d+, Microvascular Inflammation (g+ptc)**  C. DSA+, Tubulitis, Interstitial Inflammation  D. C4d+, Tubulitis, Fibrosis  
   *Answer: B. AMR = DSA+ + C4d+ (C4d2/3) + Microvascular Inflammation (g>0 and/or ptc>0).*

4. **Belatacept — contraindication?**  
   A. Diabetes  B. **EBV Seronegative**  C. Age >65  D. Prior Transplant  
   *Answer: B. EBV Seronegative → High PTLD Risk. Belatacept only for EBV+ recipients.*

5. **PTCy (Post-Transplant Cyclophosphamide) — indication?**  
   A. All HSCT  B. **Haploidentical HSCT (Standard)**  C. Only MUD  D. Only Cord Blood  
   *Answer: B. PTCy Day +3 and +4 is Standard GVHD Prophylaxis for Haploidentical HSCT.*

5. **Acute GvHD — classic triad?**  
   A. Fever, Rash, Diarrhoea  B. **Skin (Rash), GI (Diarrhoea), Liver (Bilirubin)**  C. Fever, Cough, Rash  D. Neuropathy, Rash, Diarrhoea  
   *Answer: B. Classic Triad: Skin (Rash), GI (Diarrhoea/Nausea), Liver (Bilirubin ↑).*

6. **Chronic GvHD — characteristic features?**  
   A. Acute rash + Diarrhoea  B. **Sclerotic Skin, Sicca, Bronchiolitis Obliterans (BOS), Fasciitis, Ocular**  C. Acute Hepatitis + Rash  D. Neutropenia + Fever  
   *Answer: B. cGvHD = Sclerotic Skin, Sicca Syndrome, Bronchiolitis Obliterans (BOS), Fasciitis, Ocular Involvement.*

7. **aGvHD Grade II — first-line treatment?**  
   A. ATG  B. **Prednisolone 1-2mg/kg**  C. Ruxolitinib  D. IVIG  
   *Answer: B. Steroid (Pred 1-2mg/kg) first-line; Refractory → Ruxolitinib/ATG.*

7. **Belatacept — mechanism of action?**  
   A. Calcineurin Inhibitor  B. **CTLA-4-Ig (Blocks CD28-CD80/86 Costimulation)**  C. mTOR Inhibitor  D. Antimetabolite  
   *Answer: B. CTLA-4-Ig → Blocks CD28-CD80/86 Costimulation Signal 2 → T Cell Anergy.*

8. **Chronic Antibody-Mediated Rejection — Banff features?**  
   A. Tubulitis, Interstitial Inflammation  B. **Transplant Glomerulopathy (cg), Peritubular Capillary Basement Membrane Multilayering (ptcml), DSA+**  C. C4d+ Only  D. Endarteritis Only  
   *Answer: B. Chronic AMR = cg (Transplant Glomerulopathy) + ptcml + DSA+.*

9. **dd-cfDNA threshold for Kidney Transplant Rejection?**  
   A. >0.5%  B. **>1%**  C. >2%  D. >5%  
   *Answer: B. >1% for Kidney Transplant (Heart >0.5%).*

10. **Belatacept — mechanism?**  
    A. Calcineurin Inhibitor  B. **CTLA-4-Ig (Blocks CD28-CD80/86)**  C. mTOR Inhibitor  D. Antimetabolite  
    *Answer: B. CTLA-4-Ig binds CD80/86 → Blocks CD28 Costimulation → T Cell Anergy.*
