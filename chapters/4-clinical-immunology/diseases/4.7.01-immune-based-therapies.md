## 1️⃣ Immunoglobulin Therapy (IVIG / SCIG)

### Mechanisms of Action
| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **FcRn Blockade** | Saturates FcRn → ↑ Catabolism of Endogenous IgG (Including Autoantibodies) |
| **FcγRIIb Engagement** | Inhibits B-Cell Activation, ↓ Autoantibody Production |
| **Complement Scavenging** | Binds C3b/C4b → Prevents MAC Formation |
| **Idiotypic Network** | Anti-Idiotypic Antibodies Neutralise Pathogenic Autoantibodies |
| **Treg Expansion** | ↑ Treg Function, ↑ IL-10/TGF-β |
| **Cytokine Modulation** | ↓ Pro-inflammatory (TNF, IL-1, IL-6, IL-17), ↑ IL-10, IL-1Ra |
| **FcγR Blockade** | Blocks FcγR on Macrophages/Neutrophils → ↓ Phagocytosis, ADCC |
| **Complement Inhibition** | Prevents C5a/C5b-9 Formation |

### Indications (UK / International Guidelines)

| Category | Indications | Dose & Route |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Primary Immunodeficiencies** | XLA, CVID, Hyper-IgM, IgG Subclass Deficiency, Specific Antibody Deficiency | **IVIG 400-600 mg/kg q3-4w** OR **SCIG 100-200 mg/kg/wk** |
| **Neurological** | **CIDP** (1st line), **MMN**, **GBS** (1st Line with PLEX), **Myasthenia Gravis Crisis**, **LEMS**, **Autoimmune Encephalitis** (Anti-NMDA, LGI1, CASPR2) | **IVIG 2g/kg over 2-5d** (Course), **SCIG Maintenance** |
| **Haematological** | **ITP** (1st Line + Steroids), **AIHA** (Refractory), **NAIT**, **HLA Alloimmunisation** | **IVIG 1g/kg/d x2d** (ITP), **2g/kg/d x2-5d** (GBS) |
| **Autoimmune** | **Kawasaki Disease** (Acute), **Dermatomyositis**, **SLE** (Refractory), **ANCA Vasculitis** (Refractory), **Pemphigus/Pemphigoid**, **SJS/TEN** | **IVIG 2g/kg** (Kawasaki + Aspirin), **1-2g/kg** (Autoimmune) |
| **Transplant** | **ABOi/HLAi Desensitisation**, **AMR Treatment**, **Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder** | **IVIG 1-2g/kg** ± PLEX/RTX |
| **Infectious** | **Toxic Shock**, **Staphylococcal Scalded Skin**, **Enteroviral Meningitis**, **Parvovirus B19 (Pure Red Cell Aplasia)** | **IVIG 1-2g/kg** |

### IVIG vs SCIG

| Feature | IVIG | SCIG |
|---------|------|------|
| **Route** | Intravenous | Subcutaneous |
| **Frequency** | Every 3-4 weeks | Weekly (or 2x/week) |
| **Peak IgG** | High (Rapid) | Steady State (Lower Peak) |
| **Adverse Effects** | **Headache, Aseptic Meningitis, Thrombosis, AKI, Haemolysis** | **Local Site Reactions**, Less Systemic |
| **Convenience** | Hospital/Outpatient Infusion | **Home Self-Administration** |
| **IgG Trough Levels** | Fluctuating (Peak 2-3d, Trough Day 21) | Stable (Steady State) |
| **Cost** | Higher (Infusion Suite) | Lower (Home) |

### Adverse Effects & Monitoring

| Adverse Effect | Incidence | Prevention/Management |
|----------------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Headache / Aseptic Meningitis** | 10-15% | **Slow Infusion Rate**, Pre-hydration, Paracetamol/NSAID |
| **Thrombosis (Arterial/Venous)** | <1% | **Hydration**, Avoid in High Risk (Immobility, Malignancy, History VTE) |
| **Acute Kidney Injury** | 1-5% (Sucrose-containing) | **Avoid Sucrose Preparations**, Hydration, Monitor Creatinine |
| **Haemolysis** | <1% (ABO Mismatch) | **Avoid High Doses in Non-O Blood Groups** |
| **Anaphylaxis** | Rare (IgA-Deficient + Anti-IgA) | **IgA-Deficient → IgA-Depleted IVIG** |
| **Transfusion-Related Acute Lung Injury (TRALI)** | Rare | **Monitor Respiratory Status** |

### Monitoring on IVIG/SCIG
| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Pre-Infusion** | IgG Level, Renal Function, FBC, IgA (Screen Deficiency) |
| **During Infusion** | Vital Signs q15-30min, Infusion Rate Monitoring |
| **Post-Infusion (IVIG)** | IgG Trough (Pre-Next Dose), Renal Function (If Risk) |
| **Long-Term (SCIG)** | IgG Trough q3-6m, Local Site Assessment, Patient-Reported Outcomes |

## 2️⃣ Monoclonal Antibodies (Therapeutic)

### Anti-TNF Agents
| Drug | Type | Indications | Monitoring |
|------|------|-------------|------------|
| **Infliximab** | Chimeric Mouse-Human IgG1 | RA, AS, PsA, Crohn's, UC, Uveitis, Behçet's, Sarcoidosis | **TB Screen (IGRA/TST) Pre-Tx**, LFT, CBC q3-6m, **LFT q3m** |
| **Adalimumab** | Fully Human IgG1 | RA, PsA, AS, Crohn's, UC, Psoriasis, Hidradenitis, Uveitis | TB Screen, LFT, CBC q3-6m |
| **Certolizumab Pegol** | PEGylated Fab' Fragment (No Fc) | **Pregnancy Safe (No Placental Transfer)**, RA, PsA, AS, Crohn's | TB Screen, LFT, CBC q3-6m |
| **Golimumab** | Human IgG1 | RA, PsA, AS, UC | TB Screen, LFT, CBC q3-6m |
| **Etanercept** | Fusion Protein (TNFR2-Fc) | RA, PsA, AS, JIA, Psoriasis | TB Screen, LFT, CBC q3-6m |

### Key Adverse Effects (Anti-TNF)
| Adverse Effect | Incidence | Management |
|----------------|-----------|------------|
| **TB Reactivation** | 1-5% (Highest with Infliximab/Adalimumab) | **Mandatory LTBI Screen (IGRA/TST) Pre-Tx**; Treat LTBI Before Start |
| **Serious Infections** | 3-5% | Hold if Serious Infection; Prophylactic Antibiotics in High Risk |
| **Malignancy** (Lymphoma, Skin Cancer) | Slight ↑ | Age-Appropriate Cancer Screening; Sun Protection |
| **Demyelination** (MS-like) | Rare | Discontinue if Confirmed |
| **Heart Failure** (Worsening/De Novo) | Rare | Avoid in NYHA III/IV HF |
| **Drug-Induced Lupus** | Rare | Discontinue, Usually Resolves |

### Anti-IL-6R (Tocilizumab, Sarilumab)
| Feature | Tocilizumab | Sarilumab |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| **Target** | IL-6R (Membrane + Soluble) | IL-6R (Membrane + Soluble) |
| **Indications** | **RA** (Inadequate MTX), **GCA** (1st Line + GC), **sJIA**, **AOSD**, **Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS)**, **COVID-19 (Severe)** | **RA** (Inadequate MTX), **GCA** |
| **Dosing** | **IV 8mg/kg q4w** (RA), **162mg SC q2w** (RA/GCA) | **200mg SC q2w** |
| **Key Monitoring** | **LFT (ALT/AST ↑)**, **Lipids (↑ TG/TC), Neutrophils, Platelets** | Same |
| **Key Adverse Effects** | **GI Perforation** (Diverticulitis), **Neutropenia**, **Hyperlipidaemia**, **Infections**, **Hepatotoxicity** | Same |

### Anti-IL-17/IL-23 Pathway

| Drug | Target | Indications | Dosing |
|------|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Secukinumab** | IL-17A | **PsA, AS, Psoriasis (Mod-Sev), nr-axSpA** | 300mg SC q4w (Load 300mg w0,1,2,3,4) |
| **Ixekizumab** | IL-17A | **PsA, AS, Psoriasis** | 160mg SC Load → 80mg q4w |
| **Brodalumab** | IL-17RA | Psoriasis | 210mg SC q2w (Load w0,1,2) |
| **Ustekinumab** | IL-12/23p40 | **Psoriasis, PsA, CD, UC** | 45/90mg SC q12w (Weight-Based) |
| **Guselkumab / Risankizumab / Mirikizumab** | **IL-23p19** | **Psoriasis, PsA (Gus/Risa), CD/UC (Miri/Risa)** | q8w (Gus), q12w (Risa/Miri) |

> **Key Difference**: **IL-23i (Ustekinumab/Guselkumab/Risankizumab) effective in PsA, NOT in AS/axSpA**; **IL-17i effective in AS/axSpA/PsA**

### Anti-IL-1 (Anakinra, Canakinumab)

| Drug | Target | Indications | Dosing |
|------|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Anakinra** | IL-1R | **CAPS** (NLRP3), **Still's Disease (AOSD)**, **Gout (Refractory)**, **COVID-19 (Hyperinflammation)** | **100mg SC Daily** (Short Half-Life ~4-6h) |
| **Canakinumab** | IL-1β | **CAPS**, **Still's (AOSD)**, **Gout (Frequent Flares)**, **COVID-19** | **150mg SC q4-8w** (Long Half-Life ~26d) |

### Anti-CD20 (Rituximab, Ofatumumab, Obinutuzumab)

| Drug | Type | Indications | Key Monitoring |
|------|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Rituximab** | Chimeric Mouse-Human IgG1 | **RA (RF+), ANCA Vasculitis, Pemphigus, ITP (Refractory), CIDP, NHL, CLL, Post-Transplant PTLD** | **CD19+ Count**, **IgG Levels**, **HBV DNA/HBsAg**, **PML (JCV)** |
| **Ofatumumab** | Human IgG1 (Type II) | **CLL**, **MS (Relapsing)** | Similar to Rituximab |
| **Obinutuzumab** | Humanised IgG1 (Type II, Glycoengineered) | **CLL, FL**, **Lupus Nephritis** | Enhanced ADCC/CDC |

> **Key Adverse Effects**: **Infusion Reactions** (1st Infusion), **Hypogammaglobulinaemia**, **VZV Reactivation**, **HBV Reactivation**, **PML (JCV)**, **Late-Onset Neutropenia**

### Anti-IL-6R (Tocilizumab) — Already Covered Above

### Anti-IL-4/13 (Dupilumab)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Target** | IL-4Rα (Shared by IL-4 & IL-13) |
| **Indications** | **Atopic Dermatitis (Mod-Severe)**, **Asthma (Type 2 High)**, **CRSwNP**, **EoE** (Eosinophilic Oesophagitis), **Prurigo Nodularis** |
| **Dosing** | **600mg Load → 300mg SC q2w** (Atopic Dermatitis); **400mg Load → 200mg q2w** (Asthma) |

### Anti-Integrins (Vedolizumab, Natalizumab)

| Drug | Target | Indications | Key Risk |
|--------|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Vedolizumab** | **α4β7 Integrin** (Gut-Selective) | **UC, Crohn's** | **PML Risk Very Low** (Gut-Selective) |
| **Natalizumab** | **α4 Integrin** (α4β1 + α4β7) | **MS (Highly Active)**, **Crohn's (Refractory)** | **PML (JCV Ab+) — Significant Risk** |

> **JCV Antibody Testing Mandatory Before Natalizumab**; Stratify PML Risk

### JAK Inhibitors (Tofacitinib, Baricitinib, Upadacitinib, Filgotinib)

| Drug | Selectivity | Indications | Key Monitoring |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Tofacitinib** | JAK1/3 | **RA, PsA, UC, AD** | **CBC, LFT, Lipids, CK, VZV IgG, TB Screen, CVD Risk** |
| **Baricitinib** | JAK1/2 | **RA, AD, COVID-19 (Hospitalised)** | Similar + **CK**, **Lymphocytes** |
| **Upadacitinib** | JAK1 | **RA, PsA, AD, UC, Crohn's** | Similar + **CPK** |
| **Filgotinib** | JAK1 | **RA, UC** | Similar |

> **Key Adverse Effects (Class Effect)**: **Herpes Zoster (↑↑)**, **VTE (PE/DVT)**, **MACE (CV Events)**, **Lipids ↑**, **Cytopenias**, **Liver Toxicity**, **Creatinine ↑**  
> **Contraindicated**: Active TB, Active Serious Infection, Severe Hepatic Impairment, Pregnancy, **Lymphocytes <500/μL**, **Hgb <8g/dL**, **ANC <1000/μL**

## 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"IVIG = Just Antibody Replacement"** | **NO.** Multiple Mechanisms: FcRn Blockade, FcγRIIb, Complement Scavenging, Idiotypic Network, Treg Expansion, Cytokine Modulation |
| **"SCIG = Just Convenient IVIG"** | **Different PK**: Steady State IgG, Lower Peak, Less Systemic SEs, Home Admin; **Not Interchangeable** for All Indications (e.g., Acute ITP = IVIG Preferred) |
| **"All Anti-TNF = Same"** | **NO.** **Infliximab (Chimeric) > Immunogenicity**, **Certolizumab (No Fc) = Pregnancy Safe**, **Etanercept (Fusion Protein) = Lower TB Risk than Monoclonal** |
| **"Rituximab = Only B-Cell Depletion"** | **NO.** Also **Hypogammaglobulinaemia**, **VZV Reactivation**, **HBV Reactivation**, **PML (JCV)**, **Late-Onset Neutropenia** |
| **"JAK Inhibitors = All Same"** | **NO.** **Tofacitinib (JAK1/3)**, **Baricitinib (JAK1/2)**, **Upadacitinib/Filgotinib (JAK1 Selective)** — Different Selectivity, Dosing, Adverse Profiles |
| **"S1P Modulators = All Same"** | **NO.** **Fingolimod**: 6h 1st Dose Obs, Bradycardia; **Siponimod**: CYP2C9/3A4 Genotype; **Ozanimod/Ponesimod**: No 1st Dose Monitor |
| **"BTK Inhibitors = All Same"** | **NO.** **Ibrutinib**: ↑ AFib, Bleeding; **Acalabrutinib/Zanubrutinib**: Less AFib/Bleeding, Different CYP3A4 Interaction |
| **"S1P Modulators = Lymphopenia = Good"** | **NO.** **Lymphopenia = Mechanism**, But **Infection Risk ↑**, **VZV Reactivation Risk** — Screen VZV IgG First |
| **"Venetoclax = Just CLL Drug"** | **NO.** Also **AML (Unfit + Aza)**, **MCL, WM**; **TLS Risk (Allopurinol/Rasburicase Prophylaxis Essential)** |
| **"IVIG = No Adverse Effects"** | **NO.** **Thrombosis, AKI, Haemolysis, Aseptic Meningitis, Anaphylaxis (IgA Def)** — Monitor Creatinine, Platelets, Hb |

## ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **IVIG mechanism — FcRn blockade leads to:**  
   A. ↑ IgG Synthesis  B. **↑ Catabolism of Endogenous IgG (Autoantibodies)**  C. ↓ B Cell Production  D. ↑ Complement Activation  
   *Answer: B. FcRn Saturation → ↓ IgG Recycling → ↑ Catabolism of Pathogenic Autoantibodies.*

2. **SCIG vs IVIG — key advantage of SCIG?**  
   A. Higher Peak IgG  B. **Home Self-Administration, Stable Trough Levels**  C. Less Frequent Dosing  D. Lower Cost  
   *Answer: B. SCIG = Home Self-Administration, Stable Steady-State IgG, Less Systemic Adverse Effects.*

3. **Anti-TNF — mandatory pre-treatment screening?**  
   A. HIV Only  B. **LTBI (IGRA/TST) + HBV**  C. HCV Only  D. VZV IgG Only  
   *Answer: B. LTBI Screen (IGRA/TST) + HBV Screen Mandatory Before Anti-TNF.*

4. **Rituximab — key monitoring parameter not required?**  
   A. CD19+ B Cell Count  B. **CD4+ T Cell Count**  C. IgG Levels  D. HBV DNA/HBsAg  
   *Answer: B. Rituximab Depletes B Cells (CD19+), Not T Cells (CD4+). Monitor CD19+, IgG, HBV.*

5. **Tocilizumab — laboratory abnormality requiring monitoring?**  
   A. Hyperkalaemia  B. **Hyperlipidaemia (TG/TC ↑) & Neutropenia**  C. Hypocalcaemia  D. Hypernatraemia  
   *Answer: B. Tocilizumab → ↑ Lipids (TG/TC), ↓ Neutrophils, ↑ LFT.*

6. **JAK Inhibitor — class-specific adverse effect?**  
   A. Hypoglycaemia  B. **Herpes Zoster (↑↑ Incidence)**  C. Hyperkalaemia  D. Hypermagnesaemia  
   *Answer: B. JAK Inhibitors → ↑ Herpes Zoster (2-3x Risk).*

7. **Fingolimod — first-dose monitoring?**  
   A. LFT  B. **ECG/BP for 6 Hours (Bradycardia, AV Block)**  C. Lipids  D. CBC  
   *Answer: B. 6-Hour Cardiac Monitoring Post-First Dose (Bradycardia, AV Block).*

8. **Ibrutinib — characteristic adverse effect?**  
   A. Hyperglycaemia  B. **Atrial Fibrillation & Bleeding**  C. Hypothyroidism  D. Hyperkalaemia  
   *Answer: B. Ibrutinib → ↑ Atrial Fibrillation (2-3x), ↑ Major Bleeding.*

8. **Venetoclax — tumour lysis syndrome prophylaxis?**  
   A. Hydration Only  B. **Hydration 3L/Day + Allopurinol 300mg or Rasburicase**  C. Rasburicase Only  D. Dialysis Prophylaxis  
   *Answer: B. Hydration 3L/Day + Allopurinol 300mg (or Rasburicase).*

9. **Rituximab — PML risk factor?**  
   A. Low CD4  B. **Prolonged/CD19+ Depletion, Rituximab >1 Year, Prior Immunosuppression**  C. High IgG  D. Low CD8  
   *Answer: B. Prolonged B-Cell Depletion, Prior Immunosuppression, Rituximab Duration.*

10. **S1P Modulator — no first-dose cardiac monitoring required?**  
    A. Fingolimod  B. Siponimod  C. **Ozanimod**  D. Ponesimod  
    *Answer: C. Ozanimod (and Ponesimod) — No First Dose Cardiac Monitoring Required.*

## 📋 SBAs (10)

1. **Patient on infliximab for Crohn's develops fever, cough, weight loss 3 months after starting. CXR: upper lobe cavitation. Most likely?**  
   A. Crohn's Flare  B. **TB Reactivation**  C. Fungal Pneumonia  D. Lymphoma  
   *Answer: B. Anti-TNF → TB Reactivation (Typically 3-12 Months Post-Start).*

2. **Patient on tofacitinib 5mg BD for RA develops Herpes Zoster. Management?**  
   A. Stop Tofacitinib Permanently  B. **Hold Tofacitinib, Treat with Valaciclovir, Restart After Resolution**  C. Continue Tofacitinib, Add Aciclovir  D. Switch to Biologic  
   *Answer: B. Hold JAKi, Treat HZ, Restart When Resolved if Benefit > Risk.*

3. **CLL patient starting venetoclax + obinutuzumab. WBC 80x10⁹/L. TLS Risk?**  
   A. Low  B. **High (Tumour Burden + Venetoclax)**  C. Moderate  D. None  
   *Answer: B. High Tumour Burden + Venetoclax = High TLS Risk → Hydration + Allopurinol/Rasburicase.*

4. **MS Patient on Fingolimod. Day 1 Post-First Dose. HR 42, BP 90/60. Action?**  
   A. Continue  B. **Hold, Cardiac Monitoring, Atropine if Symptomatic**  C. Switch to Siponimod  D. Reduce Dose  
   *Answer: B. First-Dose Bradycardia/AV Block → Hold, Monitor 6h, Atropine if Symptomatic.*

5. **Patient on Rituximab for RA develops Hypogammaglobulinaemia (IgG 3.5 g/L) with Recurrent Infections. Management?**  
   A. Stop Rituximab  B. **IVIG Replacement (IgG <4 + Recurrent Infections)**  C. Increase Rituximab Dose  D. Switch to Tocilizumab  
   *Answer: B. IVIG Replacement if IgG <4 g/L + Recurrent Infections.*
