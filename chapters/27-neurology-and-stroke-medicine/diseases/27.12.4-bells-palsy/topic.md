# Bell's Palsy

> **High-Yield Definition:** Bell's palsy: acute unilateral lower motor neuron facial nerve (VII) palsy, idiopathic, often post-viral (HSV reactivation). Distinguished from stroke (forehead sparing - LMN) and Ramsay Hunt syndrome (VZV, vesicles, ear).

## Definition
- Acute idiopathic peripheral (LMN) facial nerve palsy
- Often post-viral (HSV-1 reactivation in geniculate ganglion)
- Diagnosis of exclusion (other causes excluded)
- Unilateral in 99% (bilateral in 1%)

## Epidemiology
- Incidence: 20-30/100,000/year
- F:M 1:1
- Age: peak 15-60y
- Pregnancy (especially 3rd trimester, postpartum - 3x increased risk)
- Diabetes, hypertension (risk factors)
- Recurrence: 5-10%

## Aetiology
- **HSV-1 reactivation** in geniculate ganglion (most common)
- VZV without vesicles (zoster sine herpete)
- Other viruses: EBV, CMV, HIV, coxsackievirus, adenovirus
- Ischaemic neuropathy (vasa nervorum)
- Autoimmune (CIDP, MS, GBS variants)
- **Ramsay Hunt syndrome** (VZV, distinguished by vesicles)

## Clinical Features
- **Acute onset** (hours-days), often upon waking
- **Unilateral facial weakness** (forehead + lower face - LMN)
- **Inability to close eye** on affected side (key feature - LMN)
- **Hyperacusis** (stapedius weakness)
- **Loss of taste** anterior 2/3 tongue (chorda tympani)
- **Decreased lacrimation** (greater petrosal nerve)
- **Post-auricular pain** (60%)
- **Increased tears** (crocodile tears - rare, late, aberrant regeneration)
- **Facial synkinesis** (late, aberrant regeneration - eye closes with mouth movement, etc.)
- **Hemifacial spasm** (late, aberrant regeneration)
- **No other neurological signs**

## Investigations
- **Clinical diagnosis** (history, examination)
- Exclude: stroke, Ramsay Hunt, tumour, GBS, otitis media, Lyme
- **MRI brain + IAM** (exclude stroke, tumour, schwannoma)
- **Audiometry** (if hearing loss, vertigo - Ramsay Hunt, schwannoma)
- Bloods: ESR (GCA), glucose, HbA1c (DM), Lyme serology, HIV, syphilis, autoimmune
- LP (GBS, Lyme)
- CT temporal bone (if trauma, cholesteatoma)

## Management — ACUTE
- **Corticosteroids** (KEY):
  - **Prednisolone 50mg daily × 10 days** (start within 72 hours, optimal benefit)
  - OR Prednisolone 60mg × 5 days, then 40mg × 5 days, then taper
  - Deflazacort 0.5-1mg/kg/day × 10 days (alternative)
- **Antivirals** (controversial, consider if severe):
  - Valaciclovir 1g TDS × 7 days
  - Aciclovir 400mg 5x daily × 7 days
  - Often combined with steroids, especially severe (House-Brackmann IV-VI), Ramsay Hunt
- **Eye care** (CRITICAL - prevent exposure keratopathy):
  - Artificial tears (preservative-free, hourly during day)
  - Lubricating ointment (Lacri-Lube, vitamin A) at night
  - Tape eye shut at night (lateral canthus)
  - Eye patch (during day if no protection)
  - Glasses/sunglasses (dust, wind)
  - Moisture chamber
- **Pain**: paracetamol, codeine
- **Psychological support**: facial disfigurement

## Recovery
- **70% complete recovery** (House-Brackmann I/II)
- **12% permanent significant deficit** (House-Brackmann III-VI)
- **Recovery time**: most by 6-9 months
- **Poor prognostic factors**:
  - Severe at onset (House-Brackmann V-VI)
  - No recovery by 3 months
  - Older age (>60)
  - Diabetes
  - Pregnancy
  - Complete paralysis (no movement)
  - Loss of taste anterior 2/3 tongue
  - Posterior auricular pain
- **Good prognostic factors**: partial paralysis, young, no DM, early treatment

## Complications
- **Exposure keratopathy** (corneal ulcer, vision loss - prevent with eye care)
- **Aberrant regeneration**:
  - **Synkinesis** (eye-mouth, mouth-eye)
  - **Crocodile tears** (tearing with eating)
  - **Hemifacial spasm** (involuntary facial muscle contraction)
- **Persistent weakness** (House-Brackmann III-VI)
- **Psychological**: depression, anxiety, social isolation

## Ramsay Hunt Syndrome (VZV)
- Distinguished from Bell's palsy
- **Vesicles** (external ear, mouth, tongue, palate) - may appear 1-3 days after facial weakness
- **Hearing loss** (CN VIII cochlear)
- **Vertigo** (CN VIII vestibular)
- **Tinnitus**
- Loss of taste anterior 2/3 tongue
- **Worse prognosis** than Bell's palsy (only 50% complete recovery)
- Treatment: **aciclovir 800mg 5x/day × 7-10 days + prednisolone 1mg/kg × 5 days, taper**

## Multidisciplinary
- Neurology
- ENT (Ramsay Hunt, ear pathology)
- Ophthalmology (eye care)
- Plastic surgery (facial reanimation, botulinum toxin for synkinesis)
- Physiotherapy (facial exercises)
- Psychology (counselling)

## Red Flags
- Bilateral facial palsy (GBS, Lyme, sarcoid, HIV, Mycoplasma)
- No recovery by 3 months
- Progressive weakness (tumour)
- Associated cranial nerve palsies (tumour, meningitis)
- Vesicles (Ramsay Hunt)
- Hyperacusis, severe (worse prognosis)
- Recurrent (other cause, Melkersson-Rosenthal syndrome)
