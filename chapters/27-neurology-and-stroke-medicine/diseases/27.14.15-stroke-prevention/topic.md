# Stroke Prevention

> **High-Yield Definition:** Stroke prevention: primary (prevent first stroke in at-risk patients) and secondary (prevent recurrence after TIA/stroke). Risk factor modification, antiplatelet, anticoagulation (AF), carotid revascularisation, lifestyle.

## Primary Prevention
- **Risk factor modification** (population-level and high-risk):
  - **Hypertension**: target <130/80 (SPRINT trial - lower better), lifestyle + medication
  - **Diabetes**: HbA1c <7%, intensive control in younger, less stringent in elderly
  - **Hyperlipidaemia**: statins for high-risk (10-year CVD risk >10%), familial hypercholesterolaemia
  - **Smoking cessation**: most important modifiable risk factor, 2x risk, reduces within 2-5 years
  - **Alcohol**: moderation (≤2 drinks/day men, ≤1/day women), avoid binge
  - **Diet**: Mediterranean diet, DASH, low sodium (<2g/day), fruits, vegetables, fish, olive oil
  - **Exercise**: 150 min/week moderate aerobic, 2 days/week strength
  - **Weight**: BMI 18.5-24.9
  - **Sleep**: OSA screening (CPAP if moderate-severe, reduces stroke risk)
- **AF screening**: opportunistic pulse palpation, ECG in high-risk (>65y)
- **Carotid stenosis**: screen with carotid Doppler if symptomatic bruit, TIA, stroke (consider in asymptomatic >50% stenosis)
- **Antiplatelet**: aspirin 75mg daily for primary prevention in high-risk (10-year CVD risk >10%) - controversial, may increase bleeding, not routinely recommended
- **Statins**: primary prevention in high-risk (10-year risk >10%)
- **PFO closure**: not routinely for primary prevention, only after cryptogenic stroke (RoPE score)
- **Aspirin**: NOT recommended for primary prevention in diabetes (no benefit, increased bleeding - ASCEND, ARRIVE, ASPREE trials)

## Secondary Prevention
- **Antiplatelet** (ischaemic stroke/TIA, non-cardioembolic):
  - **Aspirin 75mg daily** OR **Clopidogrel 75mg daily**
  - **DAPT** (aspirin + clopidogrel) for **21 days** (CHANCE, POINT trials) then monotherapy for minor stroke/high-risk TIA
  - **Aspirin + dipyridamole MR** (second-line)
- **Anticoagulation** (cardioembolic - AF, PFO, mechanical valve, LV thrombus):
  - **DOAC** (apixaban 5mg BD, rivaroxaban 20mg OD, dabigatran 150mg BD, edoxaban 60mg OD) preferred over warfarin for non-valvular AF (RE-LY, ROCKET-AF, ARISTOTLE, ENGAGE-AF)
  - **Dose adjustment** in renal impairment
  - **Apixaban 2.5mg BD** if ≥2 of: age ≥80, weight ≤60kg, Cr ≥133 (women) or ≥1.5mg/dL
  - **Warfarin** (INR 2-3): mechanical valve, mod-severe mitral stenosis, severe renal impairment
  - **PFO closure** (cryptogenic stroke, age <60, RoPE score >7): RESPECT, CLOSE, REDUCE trials
- **Statin** (all ischaemic stroke/TIA):
  - **High-intensity** (atorvastatin 40-80mg, rosuvastatin 20-40mg)
  - LDL reduction ≥50%, target <1.8 mmol/L (70 mg/dL)
  - Aim <1.4 mmol/L (55 mg/dL) for very high risk
  - Ezetimibe add-on if needed
  - PCSK9 inhibitors (evolocumab, alirocumab) for very high risk
- **BP control** (after acute phase, days-weeks):
  - Target <130/80 (if tolerated, PROGRESS trial, SPS3)
  - ACEi, ARB, calcium channel blocker, thiazide
  - Perindopril + indapamide (PROGRESS) - effective
  - Lower BP gradually (avoid hypotension)
- **Glucose control**: HbA1c <7%
- **Carotid revascularisation** (symptomatic stenosis):
  - **CEA**: 70-99% (NASCET), 50-69% in selected, within 2 weeks (ideally <14 days)
  - **CAS**: alternative, high surgical risk, anatomy
  - **Asymptomatic stenosis >60%**: controversial, individualise (CEA if life expectancy >5y, surgical risk <3%)
- **Intracranial atherosclerosis** (SAMMPRIS trial): aggressive medical therapy > stenting
- **Vertebral stenosis**: medical (rare surgical)
- **PFO closure**: cryptogenic stroke, age <60, high RoPE score
- **Antiphospholipid syndrome**: warfarin (INR 2-3) after first ischaemic stroke
- **CADASIL**: antiplatelet, avoid anticoagulants (ICH risk)
- **Sickle cell disease**: exchange transfusion (children), hydroxyurea, transfusions
- **Fabry disease**: enzyme replacement (agalsidase alfa, beta)
- **CADASIL, MELAS, homocystinuria**: specific management

## Lifestyle
- **Smoking cessation**: most important
- Alcohol moderation
- Mediterranean diet, low sodium
- Exercise 150 min/week
- Weight loss
- OSA treatment
- Stress management

## Monitoring
- BP, glucose, lipids (lipid panel, HbA1c)
- Adherence to medication
- Side effects (bleeding, myalgia, LFTs)
- Recurrent symptoms
- Depression, anxiety, cognitive
- Functional outcome
- Imaging: carotid (post-CEA, post-CAS), intracranial (post-stenting)
- Vascular events (recurrent stroke, MI, PAD)

## Risk Stratification
- **CHA2DS2-VASc score** (AF): CHF, HTN, Age≥75 (2), DM, Stroke/TIA/thromboembolism (2), Vascular disease, Age 65-74, Sex (female)
  - 0 (male) or 1 (female): no anticoagulation
  - 1 (male) or 2 (female): consider
  - ≥2 (male) or ≥3 (female): anticoagulate
- **HAS-BLED score** (bleeding risk): HTN, Abnormal LFTs, Stroke, Bleeding, Labile INR, Elderly (>65), Drugs/alcohol
  - ≥3: caution but NOT contraindication
- **ABCD2** (TIA)
- **ABCD3-I** (improved)
- **CHA2DS2-VASc ≥2 = anticoagulation**

## Specific Populations
- **Pregnancy**: avoid statins, ACEi, warfarin (teratogenic, switch to LMWH), DOAC not recommended
- **Elderly**: balance benefit vs bleeding risk, lower targets may not be tolerated
- **Renal impairment**: dose adjust DOACs (dabigatran avoid if CrCl <30)
- **Liver impairment**: avoid warfarin (variable INR), DOACs
- **Cognitive impairment**: polypharmacy, adherence, supervision
- **Pregnancy/lactation**: limited options

## Red Flags
- Recurrent TIA/stroke despite optimal therapy
- New AF, structural cardiac disease
- Bleeding complications (GI, intracranial)
- Drug interactions (warfarin, DOACs, statins, antiplatelets)
- Poor adherence
- Drug resistance (clopidogrel - CYP2C19)
- Fall risk
- Cognitive decline, dementia
