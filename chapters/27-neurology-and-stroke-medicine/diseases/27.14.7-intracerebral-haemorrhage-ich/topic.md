# Intracerebral Haemorrhage (ICH)

> **High-Yield Definition:** ICH: bleeding into brain parenchyma. EMERGENCY - high mortality. Causes: hypertensive (most common, deep), lobar (amyloid angiopathy - elderly), AVM, cavernoma, tumour, coagulopathy, trauma.

## Definition
- **Primary ICH** (78-88%): hypertensive (deep), cerebral amyloid angiopathy (lobar - elderly)
- **Secondary ICH** (12-22%): AVM, cavernoma, dural AVF, tumour, aneurysm (if bleeds into brain), coagulopathy, trauma, haemorrhagic transformation of ischaemic stroke, venous sinus thrombosis, vasculitis, drugs (cocaine, amphetamines), mycotic aneurysm (endocarditis)

## Epidemiology
- Incidence: 15-25/100,000/year
- 10-15% of all strokes
- Mortality 30-50% (higher than ischaemic)
- Bimodal: young (AVM, hypertension, drugs), elderly (amyloid, hypertension)

## Aetiology
- **Hypertensive ICH**: most common, deep locations:
  - Basal ganglia (putamen 35-50%, thalamus 10-15%, caudate, internal capsule)
  - Cerebellum (10%)
  - Pons (5-10%)
  - Other deep locations
  - Mechanism: lipohyalinosis, Charcot-Bouchard microaneurysms rupture
- **Cerebral amyloid angiopathy (CAA)**: lobar (cortical/subcortical), elderly (>55y), APOE ε4 allele
- **AVM, cavernoma, dural AVF**: young, lobar
- **Aneurysm rupture with parenchymal extension**
- **Tumour**: primary (GBM, metastasis), bleed into tumour
- **Coagulopathy**: anticoagulants (warfarin, DOACs - especially in elderly), antiplatelet, thrombocytopenia, DIC, haemophilia, leukaemia
- **Trauma**
- **Drugs**: cocaine, amphetamines, sympathomimetics
- **Haemorrhagic transformation** of ischaemic stroke
- **Cerebral venous sinus thrombosis** (venous infarction with haemorrhage)
- **Vasculitis, mycotic aneurysm (endocarditis)**

## Clinical Features
- **Headache** (40%, sudden onset, severe, often with vomiting)
- Vomiting (often, increased ICP)
- Focal neurological deficit (depends on location): hemiparesis, hemisensory loss, aphasia, hemianopia, ataxia, cranial nerve palsy
- Seizures (10%, more common with lobar - CAA, tumour)
- Altered consciousness (50%, large haematoma, brainstem, raised ICP)
- Coma (large haematoma, brainstem, herniation)
- **Progressive over minutes-hours** (vs sudden in SAH)
- Specific syndromes:
  - **Putamen/internal capsule**: contralateral hemiparesis, hemisensory, hemianopia
  - **Thalamus**: contralateral hemisensory (sensory > motor), thalamic pain syndrome
  - **Cerebellum**: ataxia, vertigo, headache, vomiting, gaze palsy, **risk of brainstem compression** - EMERGENCY
  - **Pons**: coma, pinpoint pupils, decerebrate, quadriparesis, hyperthermia
  - **Lobar**: focal deficit, headache, seizures, gradual
- Hypertension common

## Investigations — EMERGENCY
- **Non-contrast CT head** (hyperdense bleed, surrounding oedema, mass effect, midline shift, intraventricular extension, hydrocephalus)
- **CTA**: spot sign (contrast extravasation - predicts haematoma expansion), identify AVM, aneurysm
- **MRI brain** (later, especially for CAA - microbleeds, lobar; cavernoma - 'popcorn', blooming on SWI/GRE; tumour; ischaemic stroke with haemorrhagic transformation)
- **MR angiography / venography**
- **Bloods**: FBC, ESR, coagulation (PT, aPTT, INR), platelets, LFTs, creatinine, urea, electrolytes, glucose, troponin, toxicology (cocaine, amphetamines), autoimmune, antiphospholipid
- **ECG**
- **Echocardiogram** (endocarditis)
- Consider: thrombophilia (CVST), vasculitis, mycotic aneurysm
- Exclude: trauma (with history), haemorrhagic transformation (with ischaemic stroke history, large embolic, anticoagulant)

## Management — EMERGENCY
- **ABCDE**: airway, breathing, circulation, disability, exposure
- **BP control** (INTERACT3, ATACH-2 trials):
  - **Target SBP 130-150 mmHg** acutely
  - IV agents: labetalol (10-20mg increments), nicardipine infusion, esmolol, GTN
  - Avoid hypotension (cerebral hypoperfusion)
- **Reverse anticoagulation**:
  - Warfarin: vitamin K 10mg IV + PCC/FFP (4-factor PCC 25-50U/kg)
  - DOACs: idarucizumab (dabigatran), andexanet alfa (factor Xa inhibitors), PCC if unavailable
  - Antiplatelet: platelet transfusion (controversial), DDAVP
  - Thrombolytics: cryoprecipitate, antifibrinolytics (tranexamic acid - TICH-2 trial)
- **Surgical**:
  - **Cerebellar ICH >3cm**: EMERGENCY surgical evacuation (brainstem compression risk)
  - Lobar ICH >30ml within 1cm of cortex + neurological deterioration: evacuation (STICH trials - benefit uncertain, but may help selected)
  - Intraventricular haemorrhage with hydrocephalus: EVD
  - Brainstem, thalamic, deep: medical
  - AVM/cavernoma: definitive treatment (resection, SRS, embolisation)
- **Seizure prophylaxis**: controversial (often used acutely, levetiracetam preferred)
- **Increased ICP**: head elevation, mannitol 1g/kg, hypertonic saline, sedation, hyperventilation (brief), EVD, surgical decompression
- **Reversal of coagulopathy**: critical
- **Rehabilitation**: critical
- Multidisciplinary: neurosurgery, neurology, ICU, haematology, neuroradiology, OT, PT, SLT, dietitian, palliative, social, psychology, chaplaincy

## Red Flags
- EMERGENCY: cerebellar (brainstem compression, hydrocephalus), large haematoma (raised ICP, herniation), brainstem (coma, decerebrate), intraventricular (hydrocephalus), anticoagulation, coagulopathy, young (AVM, tumour, drugs), rebleeding (especially CAA), haematoma expansion (first 24 hours, 30%, especially with anticoagulation, spot sign on CTA)

## Prognosis
- Mortality 30-50% (depends on size, location, age, GCS, comorbidities)
- 30-day mortality:
  - Lobar 1-3cm: 10-20%
  - Deep 1-3cm: 20-30%
  - Lobar >4cm: 30-50%
  - Deep >4cm: 50-70%
  - Brainstem: 80-90%
- Functional outcome: ICH score (0-6)
- Survivors: 50% independent, 30% moderate disability, 20% severe
- Recurrence: 2-5% per year (higher with CAA, anticoagulants, uncontrolled hypertension)
- Secondary prevention: BP control (<130/80), lifestyle, avoid anticoagulants if possible, treat underlying (CAA, AVM, cavernoma)
