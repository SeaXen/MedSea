# CSF Analysis & Interpretation

> [!tip] **Always correlate CSF with clinical picture, neuroimaging, and serum.** A "normal" CSF does not exclude early disease.
> [!tip] **CSF glucose = 2/3 serum glucose.** Low CSF glucose always = pathology (bacterial, fungal, TB, malignancy, sarcoid). Always send paired serum glucose.

## 1. Definition / Epidemiology / Classification
**Definition:** CSF is produced by choroid plexus (70%) and brain interstitium (30%) at ~500 mL/day; volume 150 mL; turns over 3-4 times/day. LP samples the lumbar subarachnoid space (L3/4 or L4/5) and provides diagnostic information on CNS infection, inflammation, haemorrhage, and malignancy.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| LP rate | ~5000-10000/yr per general hospital |
| Post-LP headache | 10-30% (lower with 22G atraumatic needle) |
| LP-related epidural haematoma | <1/200,000 |
| Traumatic tap rate | 10-20% |

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Infectious | Bacterial, viral, TB, fungal, parasitic |
| Inflammatory | MS, NMOSD, ADEM, autoimmune encephalitis |
| Neoplastic | Leptomeningeal, primary CNS lymphoma |
| Haemorrhagic | SAH (CT-negative), spinal dural AVF |
| Pressure disorders | Idiopathic intracranial hypertension, NPH, spontaneous intracranial hypotension |

## 2. Aetiology / Pathophysiology
**Pathophysiology of abnormalities:**
- **Pleocytosis:** BBB disruption, meningeal inflammation, infection
- **High protein:** BBB disruption, increased production (meningitis), decreased reabsorption (Guillain-Barré - albuminocytologic dissociation)
- **Low glucose:** Bacterial/fungal consumption, impaired glucose transport
- **High opening pressure:** ↑ICP, venous sinus thrombosis, meningitis
- **Low pressure:** CSF leak, dehydration, post-LP
- **Xanthochromia:** RBC breakdown (oxyhaemoglobin → bilirubin, takes 6-12 hr)
- **OCBs:** Intrathecal IgG synthesis (chronic inflammation, MS)

## 3. Clinical Features
**Indications for LP:**
- Suspected CNS infection (meningitis, encephalitis)
- Suspected SAH with normal CT (within 7 days)
- Suspected Guillain-Barré syndrome
- Suspected MS, NMOSD, autoimmune encephalitis
- Suspected leptomeningeal disease
- NPH (large-volume tap test)
- Therapeutic (idiopathic intracranial hypertension)
- Intrathecal drug administration (chemo, antibiotics)

**Contraindications:**
- **Absolute:** Raised ICP with mass effect (papilloedema + focal signs, ↓GCS), skin infection at site
- **Relative:** Coagulopathy (platelets <50, INR >1.5, anticoagulants), spinal deformity, suspected spinal cord compression

**Side effects:** Post-LP headache (10-30%, worse upright), back pain, radicular pain, infection, bleeding, cerebral herniation, abducens nerve palsy (transient), epidermoid cyst (poor technique).

## 4. Diagnostic Approach
**Pre-LP checklist:**
1. CT head first if: immunocompromised, history of CNS disease, new seizure, papilloedema, altered consciousness, focal neuro signs
2. Coagulation: platelets >50, INR <1.5, stop anticoagulants per local guidance
3. Position: lateral decubitus (preferred for OP measurement) or sitting

**LP procedure (brief):**
- Aseptic technique, L3/4 or L4/5 interspace
- Atraumatic Sprotte/Whitacre 22G needle (less PDPH)
- 4 tubes: #1 cell count, #2 chemistry (protein, glucose), #3 microbiology/serology, #4 cell count (re-count to detect traumatic tap)
- Opening pressure measured in lateral decubitus with legs extended
- Tubes 1 and 4 RBC differ by <25% = no traumatic tap; difference >25% = subarachnoid bleed (suspicious)

**Post-LP:** Lie flat 1-2 hr; encourage oral fluids; caffeine; monitor for PDPH; if severe PDPH → epidural blood patch (90% effective).

## 5. Investigations
**Normal CSF values:**
| Parameter | Adult | Neonate |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| Opening pressure | 6-20 cm H2O (lateral) | <10 cm H2O |
| Appearance | Clear, colourless | Clear |
| RBC | 0-5/mm³ | 0-50/mm³ |
| WBC | 0-5/mm³ (lymphocytes) | 0-30/mm³ |
| Protein | 0.15-0.45 g/L | 0.2-1.0 g/L |
| Glucose | 2.2-3.9 mmol/L (60% serum) | 60-80% serum |
| IgG index | <0.7 | - |
| OCBs | Negative | - |

**Special tests:**
| Test | Indication |
|------|------------|
| PCR | HSV, VZV, EBV, CMV, enterovirus, TB, Toxoplasma, cryptococcus |
| Culture | Bacterial, fungal, mycobacterial |
| Cytology | Malignancy, lymphoma (3 samples increase yield to 90%+) |
| OCBs | MS (paired serum) |
| Aβ42, tau, p-tau | AD biomarkers (research, clinical in some centres) |
| 14-3-3, RT-QuIC | CJD (sensitivity >90%) |
| Anti-NMDAR/LGI1/CASPR2 | Autoimmune encephalitis |
| Lactate | Bacterial vs viral meningitis (>3.5 mmol/L favours bacterial) |
| Flow cytometry | CNS lymphoma |
| ACE | Neurosarcoid (sensitivity low) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis (Patterns)

| Pattern | WCC | Protein | Glucose | Causes |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|--------|
| **Normal** | <5 | <0.45 | Normal | Healthy |
| **Bacterial** | ↑↑ (1000+), PMN | ↑↑ (1-5) | ↓↓ (<40% serum) | S. pneumo, N. meningitidis, Listeria, H. influenzae, E. coli, Group B Strep |
| **Viral** | ↑ (10-500), lymph | ↑/normal (<1) | Normal | Enterovirus, HSV, VZV, mumps, HIV |
| **TB** | ↑ (100-500), lymph | ↑↑ (1-5) | ↓ (30-40% serum) | M. tuberculosis |
| **Fungal** | ↑ (10-500), lymph | ↑ | ↓ | Cryptococcus, Candida, Histoplasma |
| **Subarachnoid haemorrhage** | ↑ RBC, xanthochromia | ↑ | Normal | SAH, traumatic tap (no xanthochromia) |
| **Guillain-Barré** | <10 (or 0) | ↑↑ (>1) | Normal | Albuminocytologic dissociation |
| **MS** | <50, lymph | <1 | Normal | OCBs positive (95%) |
| **Polymorphonuclear pleocytosis** | ↑ PMN | ↑ | Normal | Early bacterial, NMOSD, ADEM, SLE, vasculitis, SAH, tumour |
| **Malignancy** | ↑ lymph | ↑ | ↓ (low) | Leptomeningeal, lymphoma, glioma |
| **Idiopathic intracranial hypertension** | Normal | Normal (or low) | Normal | IIH, cerebral venous thrombosis |

**Classic findings:**
- **Albuminocytologic dissociation** (GBS): high protein with normal cells
- **Xanthochromia** (yellow CSF) = SAH (6-12 hr post-bleed); absent = likely traumatic
- **PMN pleocytosis + low glucose** = bacterial, TB (early), fungal
- **Lymphocytic + low glucose** = TB, fungal, malignancy
- **OCBs in CSF only** (not serum) = intrathecal synthesis = MS (95%), also ADEM, neurosarcoid, lupus, Sjögren
- **↑IgG index** (>0.7) = intrathecal IgG synthesis
- **High opening pressure** (>25 cm H2O) = meningitis, IIH, CVST, mass
- **Very high protein** (>5 g/L) = Froin's syndrome (spinal block), GBS, severe infection

## 7. Management
**Meningitis:**
- Empirical: ceftriaxone 2g IV 12-hrly + dexamethasone 10mg IV 6-hrly + ampicillin 2g IV 4-hrly (if >50 yr or Listeria risk)
- Add aciclovir 10 mg/kg IV 8-hrly if HSV encephalitis suspected
- Adjust for local resistance / culture

**Specific management:**
| Condition | Rx |
|-----------|----|
| Bacterial meningitis | Empirical 3rd-gen cephalosporin + vancomycin ± ampicillin (Listeria); adjust to sensitivities; 7-14 days |
| TB meningitis | HRZE + adjunctive dexamethasone × 4-6 wk (prednisolone reducing over 6-8 wk) |
| Cryptococcal | AmBisome + flucytosine → fluconazole consolidation/maintenance |
| HSV encephalitis | IV aciclovir 10mg/kg 8-hrly × 14-21 days |
| GBS | IVIG 0.4g/kg/day × 5 days OR plasmapheresis |
| MS acute relapse | Methylprednisolone 1g IV × 3-5 days |
| NMOSD | Methylpred + PLEX; chronic: rituximab, eculizumab, satralizumab |
| Autoimmune encephalitis | Methylpred + IVIG/PLEX; chronic: rituximab |
| IIH | Weight loss, acetazolamide, topiramate, VP shunt/optic nerve fenestration |

## 8. Drug Interactions / Cautions
| Drug | Issue | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| Anticoagulants (warfarin, DOAC) | Spinal haematoma | Stop per local (warfarin 5 days, INR <1.5; DOAC 48 hr) |
| Antiplatelets (aspirin) | Low risk | Continue if single agent; stop clopidogrel 7 days |
| LMWH (prophylactic) | Low risk | Hold 12 hr |
| LMWH (therapeutic) | High risk | Hold 24 hr |
| Contrast media | Not given intrathecally | - |

## 9. Procedures (Lumbar Puncture)
- **Indication:** Diagnostic LP, therapeutic LP (IIH, NPH tap test)
- **Contraindications:** ↑ICP with mass effect, skin infection, coagulopathy
- **Preparation:** Asepsis, local anaesthetic (1% lidocaine); position (lateral decubitus for OP; sitting for older)
- **Needle:** Atraumatic 22G (Whitacre/Sprotte); 18G only for large volume
- **Complications:** PDPH (10-30%, less with atraumatic), back pain, infection, bleeding, herniation
- **Viva Pearls:** Use paired serum glucose; traumatic tap - RBC clearance from tube 1 to 4; xanthochromia takes 6-12 hr

## 10. Complications
| Complication | Frequency | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| PDPH | 10-30% | Bedrest, fluids, caffeine, paracetamol; blood patch 90% effective |
| Back pain | Common | Analgesia |
| Infection (meningitis) | <1/50,000 | Antibiotics |
| Spinal haematoma | <1/200,000 | Surgical evacuation urgently |
| Cerebral herniation | Rare | Prevent with pre-LP CT |
| CN VI palsy | Transient | Resolves |

## 11. Red Flags
| Red Flag | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Papilloedema + focal signs | **CT before LP** (risk of herniation) |
| ↓GCS, immunocompromised, new seizure | CT before LP |
| Coagulopathy/bleeding diathesis | Treat first |
| Suspected SAH with normal CT | LP 6-12 hr post-bleed |
| Suspected meningitis in ED | Antibiotics BEFORE LP; do not delay |

## 12. Prognosis
| Condition | Outcome |
|-----------|---------|
| Bacterial meningitis | Mortality 10-30% (higher pneumococcal) |
| TB meningitis | Mortality 15-40% (HIV co-infection worse) |
| HSV encephalitis | Mortality 20-30% untreated; aciclovir improves |
| GBS | 5% mortality; 20% disability |
| MS | Variable; depends on subtype |

## 14. Special Situations
- **Pregnancy:** LP safe; X-ray/CT avoided unless life-threatening (gadolinium crosses placenta)
- **Paediatric:** Lower OP (<10 cm); normal WBC up to 30/mm³; different pathogens (GBS, E. coli, Listeria)
- **Elderly:** Listeria, pneumococcus; ensure adequate volume status
- **Coagulopathy:** Platelets >50, INR <1.5; LMWH hold 24 hr; warfarin 5 days + bridge
- **HIV:** Cryptococcus, TB, syphilis, PML, primary CNS lymphoma
- **Immunocompromised:** Consider CMV, PML, fungal, Listeria, mycobacterial

## Viva Questions
1. **Q:** What is the normal CSF opening pressure? **A:** 6-20 cm H2O (lateral decubitus); up to 25 considered normal in some.
2. **Q:** CSF glucose - what to compare? **A:** Paired serum glucose. CSF = 60-70% serum. Low if <50% serum.
3. **Q:** Causes of low CSF glucose? **A:** Bacterial/fungal/TB meningitis, malignancy, sarcoidosis, severe inflammation.
4. **Q:** Albuminocytologic dissociation - what and when? **A:** High protein, normal cells. GBS (95%), also early infection, transverse myelitis.
5. **Q:** When does xanthochromia develop after SAH? **A:** 6-12 hours (oxyhaemoglobin → bilirubin); persists 2-4 weeks.
6. **Q:** How to differentiate traumatic tap from SAH? **A:** RBC clearance tube 1 to 4 (clearing in trauma); xanthochromia present in SAH, absent in trauma.
7. **Q:** OCB significance? **A:** Intrathecal IgG synthesis. Positive in 95% MS, but non-specific (ADEM, lupus, sarcoid, CIDP).
8. **Q:** Indications for CT before LP? **A:** Immunocompromised, new seizure, papilloedema + focal signs, ↓GCS, focal neuro signs, history of CNS disease.
9. **Q:** Common causes of raised CSF protein? **A:** Infection, GBS, transverse myelitis, tumour, MS, haemorrhage.
10. **Q:** Empirical meningitis treatment? **A:** Ceftriaxone 2g IV 12-hrly + dexamethasone 10mg IV 6-hrly + ampicillin (Listeria risk >50 yr).
11. **Q:** Complications of LP? **A:** PDPH (10-30%), back pain, infection, bleeding, herniation, CN VI palsy.
12. **Q:** CSF findings in cryptococcal meningitis? **A:** ↑ lymph, ↑ protein, ↓ glucose; India ink positive; CrAg positive in CSF/serum.

## Common Confusions
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| High protein ≠ infection | GBS, transverse myelitis, tumour, MS |
| Lymphocytic ≠ always viral | TB, fungal, malignancy, early bacterial all can be lymphocytic |
| OCB = MS-specific | 95% in MS, but also ADEM, lupus, sarcoid, CIDP, infection |
| Traumatic tap ≠ SAH | No xanthochromia; RBC clears tube 1→4 |
| Glucose = absolute | Always compare to serum (CSF 60%) |
| IIH = diagnosis of exclusion | ↑ OP, normal CSF, papilloedema, exclude CVST, mass |
| GBS CSF = NORMAL protein in first week | Protein rises over 1-2 wk |
| OP 25 cm H2O = normal if obese | Use 25-28 as borderline; >25 pathologic |

## Mnemonics
1. **PLEO** — **P**ressure, **L**ook (clarity), **E**lements (cells), **O**rganics (protein/glucose)
2. **BTVFM** (low glucose) — **B**acterial, **T**B, **V**ery rare viral, **F**ungal, **M**alignancy
3. **PLACS** (lymphocytic CSF) — **P**olys (early bacterial), **L**yme, **A**utoimmune, **C**ancer, **S**low viruses (CJD)

6. **COCOA** (OCBs) — **C**SF **O**ligoclonal **C**loning **O**f **A**ntibodies
