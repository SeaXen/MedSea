# Subarachnoid Haemorrhage (SAH)

> **High-Yield Definition:** SAH: bleeding into subarachnoid space (between arachnoid and pia). 85% spontaneous (aneurysmal). 15% non-aneurysmal (perimesencephalic, AVM, trauma). EMERGENCY - high mortality.

## Definition
- **Aneurysmal SAH**: bleeding from ruptured saccular (berry) aneurysm, usually at Circle of Willis
- **Non-aneurysmal SAH (perimesencephalic)**: venous, better prognosis
- **Traumatic SAH**: head injury

## Epidemiology
- Incidence: 6-10/100,000/year
- 85% spontaneous aneurysmal
- Female:male 2:1
- Peak: 40-60y
- Mortality: 35-50% in first event
- 50% overall

## Aetiology
- **Saccular (berry) aneurysm**: 85% (Circle of Willis - ACoM 30-35%, PCom 30-35%, MCA bifurcation 20%, basilar tip 5-10%, others 5%)
- **Mycotic aneurysm** (endocarditis)
- **AVM, dural AVF**
- **Perimesencephalic non-aneurysmal**: venous bleeding around midbrain
- **Trauma** (most common cause overall)
- Risk factors: hypertension, smoking, family history (2x if first-degree relative), ADPKD, Ehlers-Danlos, NF1, coarctation, cocaine, alcohol

## Clinical Features
- **Thunderclap headache** (97%, worst headache of life, sudden onset, peak in seconds-minutes)
- Nausea, vomiting (often)
- Photophobia, neck stiffness (meningism, develops over hours)
- LOC (53%, may be transient)
- Focal neurological deficit (cranial nerve palsy - III = PComm aneurysm, focal = intracerebral extension)
- Seizures (up to 25%)
- **Sentinel warning leak (sentinel headache)**: 30-50%, days-weeks before major SAH
- Autonomic: hypertension, bradycardia, tachycardia, diaphoresis
- **Coma** (severe)
- Death (30% before reaching hospital)

## Investigations — EMERGENCY
- **Non-contrast CT head** (within 6 hours): sensitivity 95-98% (declines over time - 80% at 24h, 50% at 7 days)
- **CT angiography** (CTA): identify aneurysm
- **MR angiography** (MRA): alternative
- **Digital subtraction angiography (DSA)**: gold standard, allows intervention
- **Lumbar puncture** (if CT negative but high suspicion): xanthochromia (sensitive 7-14 days), RBCs (less specific - traumatic tap)
- ECG: ST changes, T wave inversion (cerebral T waves), QT prolongation, arrhythmias

## Management — EMERGENCY
- **ABCDE**: airway, breathing, circulation, disability, exposure
- **Nimodipine 60mg PO q4h × 21 days** (reduces vasospasm, improves outcome)
- BP control: avoid hypotension and severe hypertension (target SAP <160, MAP <110, depending on guidance)
- **Neurosurgical/endovascular**:
  - **Endovascular coiling** (most aneurysms, especially posterior circulation, elderly, poor surgical candidates - ISAT trial)
  - **Surgical clipping** (anterior circulation, accessible, complex morphology, haematoma)
  - Timing: early (<72 hours) once stable
- **External ventricular drain (EVD)** for hydrocephalus
- **Vasospasm prevention**: nimodipine, euvolaemia, magnesium (controversial)
- **Delayed cerebral ischaemia**: clinical deterioration days 4-14, nimodipine, induced hypertension, endovascular angioplasty
- **Seizure prophylaxis**: controversial (often used acutely, not long-term)
- **Hyponatraemia** (SIADH, CSW): fluid restriction, hypertonic saline
- **Rebleeding**: highest risk first 24 hours (4%), treat with antifibrinolytics (tranexamic acid) short-term, urgent treatment of aneurysm
- **Multidisciplinary**: neurosurgery, neuroradiology, ICU, neurology, OT, PT, SLT, dietitian, palliative
- Long-term: rehabilitation, psychological, cognitive, fatigue

## Red Flags
- EMERGENCY: thunderclap headache, sentinel headache, focal deficit, seizures, coma, rebleeding, vasospasm, hydrocephalus, hyponatraemia, delayed cerebral ischaemia, complications (DVT, PE, pneumonia, sepsis)

## Prognosis
- Mortality 35-50% (first event), 50% overall
- Outcome based on:
  - World Federation of Neurological Surgeons (WFNS) grade
  - Hunt and Hess grade
  - Amount of blood on CT (Fisher grade)
  - Age, comorbidities
- Vasospasm/delayed cerebral ischaemia: 30%, peaks days 4-14
- Rebleeding: 4% first 24h, 1-2% per day thereafter until secured
- Hydrocephalus: 20-30% (acute, subacute, chronic)
- Hyponatraemia: 30-50% (SIADH, CSW)
- Epilepsy: 5-10% (higher if MCA aneurysm, haematoma)
- Cognitive impairment: common (memory, executive, fatigue)
- Survivors: 50% independent, 30% moderate disability, 20% severe
