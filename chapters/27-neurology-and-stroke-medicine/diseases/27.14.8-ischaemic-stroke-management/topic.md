# Ischaemic Stroke Management

> **High-Yield Definition:** Ischaemic stroke: acute focal neurological deficit due to cerebral infarction (arterial occlusion, ~85% of strokes). Time-critical emergency. Reperfusion therapy (thrombolysis, thrombectomy) + supportive care + secondary prevention.

## Definition
- Acute focal neurological deficit due to ischaemic brain injury
- 85% of all strokes
- Time = brain: 1.9 million neurons lost per minute

## Classification (TOAST)
- **Large artery atherosclerosis** (carotid, vertebral, intracranial)
- **Cardioembolic** (AF, PFO, valvular)
- **Small vessel occlusion** (lacunar)
- **Other determined** (dissection, vasculitis, hypercoagulability)
- **Undetermined** (cryptogenic, multiple causes)

## Acute Management — EMERGENCY

### Pre-hospital
- "Time is brain"
- 999/911 emergency
- FAST/FACE assessment
- Direct transport to comprehensive stroke centre
- Avoid aspirin pre-hospital (unless haemorrhagic stroke excluded)
- Pre-hospital notification, CT on arrival

### ED Assessment
- ABCDE
- Immediate non-contrast CT head (exclude haemorrhage, mass)
- **Time of onset / last known well** (critical for thrombolysis eligibility)
- **NIHSS** (National Institutes of Health Stroke Scale, 0-42, severity)
- Bloods: glucose (essential), FBC, U&Es, coagulation, troponin, ABG if indicated
- ECG, cardiac monitoring
- IV access, fluids (0.9% saline)

### Reperfusion Therapy

#### IV Thrombolysis (Alteplase / Tenecteplase)
- **Eligibility** (within 4.5 hours of onset / last known well):
  - **Age ≥18** (no upper limit if otherwise eligible)
  - Clinical diagnosis of stroke with measurable neurological deficit
  - **CT excludes haemorrhage** (and large established infarct)
- **Dose**:
  - **Alteplase 0.9 mg/kg IV** (max 90mg), 10% bolus, rest over 60 min
  - **Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg IV** (max 25mg), single bolus (alternative, non-inferior in some studies, easier)
- **Time windows**:
  - **0-4.5 hours**: standard
  - **4.5-9 hours** (wake-up stroke, unknown onset): may benefit with penumbra imaging (CTP/MRP DWI-FLAIR mismatch)
- **Contraindications**:
  - Intracranial haemorrhage
  - Recent major surgery/trauma (<14 days)
  - Active bleeding, GI bleed (<21 days)
  - Recent stroke/head injury (<3 months)
  - BP >185/110 (must lower first)
  - INR >1.7, platelets <100,000
  - DOAC within 48 hours
  - Glucose <2.7 or >22.2 mmol/L
- **Risks**: symptomatic intracerebral haemorrhage ~6%, angioedema, allergic reaction

#### Mechanical Thrombectomy (MT)
- **Large vessel occlusion** (LVO): ICA, M1, A1, basilar, vertebral, P1
- **Eligibility** (up to 6 hours standard, 6-24 hours with penumbra):
  - Pre-stroke mRS 0-1 (functional independence)
  - NIHSS ≥6
  - ASPECTS ≥6 (CT collateral score)
  - Within 6 hours of onset (standard), or 6-24 hours (DAWN, DEFUSE-3 trials - penumbra imaging)
- **Stent retrievers** (Solitaire, Trevo), aspiration (Penumbra)
- **TICI 2b-3** (reperfusion goal)
- **Combined with IV thrombolysis** if within window (bridging therapy)

### Supportive Care
- **BP management**:
  - Non-thrombolysis: <220/120 (allow permissive hypertension), lower if <220/120 with end-organ damage
  - Post-thrombolysis: <180/105 × 24h
  - Post-thrombectomy: <180/105 × 24h
  - Labetalol, nicardipine, GTN (avoid hypotension)
- **Glucose**: 4-11 mmol/L (treat >11 with insulin, avoid <4)
- **Temperature**: normothermia, treat fever (>38°C) with paracetamol, cooling
- **Oxygen**: only if SpO2 <94%
- **Hydration**: 0.9% saline (avoid hypotonic)
- **Swallow assessment** (within 24 hours, before oral intake)
- **DVT prophylaxis**: LMWH (after 24-48h if immobile, no haemorrhagic transformation), intermittent pneumatic compression
- **Positioning**: head of bed 30°, avoid flat
- **Avoid**: urinary catheters (if possible, urinary retention), sedatives, antihypertensives (unless >220/120)
- **Seizure management**: ASMs (levetiracetam preferred), no prophylaxis
- **ICP management**: mannitol, hypertonic saline, hyperventilation (brief), surgical decompression (malignant MCA, cerebellar)

### Imaging
- **CT head** (immediate, exclude haemorrhage)
- **CTA** (identify LVO, guide thrombectomy)
- **CTP/MRP** (penumbra, mismatch)
- **MRI brain + DWI** (especially wake-up stroke, posterior circulation)
- **MRA** (large vessel, dissection)

## Secondary Prevention

### Antiplatelet
- **Aspirin 300mg loading within 24-48h** (if not thrombolysed, or 24h after thrombolysis)
- **Aspirin 75mg daily** long-term OR
- **Clopidogrel 75mg daily** (alternative)
- **DAPT (aspirin + clopidogrel) for 21 days** (CHANCE, POINT trials) then monotherapy for minor stroke/high-risk TIA
- Avoid in haemorrhagic stroke

### Anticoagulation
- **AF**: DOAC (apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban) preferred over warfarin
- PFO: consider closure (young, cryptogenic, RoPE score)
- Other cardioembolic: mechanical valve (warfarin), LV thrombus (warfarin or DOAC)

### Statin
- **High-intensity** (atorvastatin 40-80mg, rosuvastatin 20-40mg) regardless of LDL
- Reduce LDL by ≥50%, target <1.8 mmol/L (70 mg/dL)
- Aim <1.4 mmol/L (55 mg/dL) for very high risk

### BP Control
- After acute phase (days to weeks)
- Target <130/80 (if tolerated)
- ACEi, ARB, calcium channel blocker, thiazide

### Glucose Control
- HbA1c <7% (individualised)

### Lifestyle
- Smoking cessation
- Alcohol moderation
- Mediterranean diet
- Exercise (150 min/week)
- Weight loss

### Surgical
- **Carotid endarterectomy (CEA)**: symptomatic stenosis 70-99% (NASCET), 50-69% in selected, within 2 weeks (ideally <14 days)
- **Carotid artery stenting (CAS)**: alternative, high surgical risk, anatomy

## Complications
- Haemorrhagic transformation (especially large, anticoagulation, thrombolysis)
- Cerebral oedema, raised ICP, herniation (malignant MCA, cerebellar)
- Seizures, status epilepticus
- Hydrocephalus (cerebellar)
- Aspiration pneumonia
- DVT/PE
- Pressure sores
- Spasticity, contractures
- Shoulder pain (hemiplegic)
- Depression, anxiety, PTSD, emotional lability (PSE)
- Cognitive impairment, vascular dementia
- Recurrent stroke (5-10%/year)
- Autonomic dysfunction

## Prognosis
- Mortality 15-30% at 30 days (variable by stroke severity)
- 5-year mortality 40-60%
- 5-year recurrence 25%
- Functional outcome: NIHSS, mRS
- Recovery: most rapid in 3-6 months, continues 12-24 months
- Rehabilitation: critical (multidisciplinary, OT, PT, SLT, psychology, social)
- Quality of life: significant impact on patient and family
- Long-term: secondary prevention, lifestyle, monitor
