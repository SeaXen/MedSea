# Chorea

Related: [[Movement Disorders Hub]], [[Hyperkinetic Movement Disorders Hub]], [[Huntingtons Disease]], [[Sydenhams Chorea]], [[Wilsons Disease]], [[Dystonia]]

> [!tip] **Chorea = irregular, non-repetitive, brief, flowing, purposeless involuntary movements** that appear to flow from one body part to another. From Greek "χορεία" (dance). Always consider **Huntington's disease** in adults and **Sydenham's chorea** in children.

## Learning Objectives
- [ ] Define chorea and recognise clinical features
- [ ] Identify major causes: Huntington's, Sydenham's, drug-induced, metabolic, autoimmune
- [ ] Recognise chorea gravidarum, lupus chorea, post-streptococcal
- [ ] Treat with tetrabenazine, deutetrabenazine, valbenazine, dopamine antagonists
- [ ] Differentiate from athetosis, dystonia, tics, myoclonus

## 1. Definition / Epidemiology / Classification

**Definition:** Irregular, non-repetitive, brief, flowing, random, purposeless involuntary movements that **appear to flow from one body part to another** (continuous, "dance-like"). Movements are non-stereotyped, often incorporated into semi-purposeful acts (parakinesia).

**Epidemiology:**
- **Huntington's disease (HD):** 5-10/100,000; onset typically 30-50 years
- **Sydenham's chorea:** 5-10% of rheumatic fever; declining in developed countries
- **Drug-induced:** Increasing (anti-psychotics, levodopa, anticonvulsants, OCP)
- **Lupus chorea:** 1-3% of SLE patients

**Classification:**

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Genetic (Huntington's, HD-like 1,2)** | HD, HDL1-4, DRPLA, chorea-acanthocytosis |
| **Infectious/post-infectious** | Sydenham's, encephalitis, CJD, HIV |
| **Autoimmune** | SLE, antiphospholipid, anti-basal ganglia Ab, post-strep, PANDAS |
| **Metabolic** | Hyperthyroidism, hypoglycaemia, hepatic/renal failure, electrolyte |
| **Drug-induced** | Levodopa, antipsychotics, anticonvulsants, OCP, cocaine, amphetamines |
| **Pregnancy-related** | Chorea gravidarum |
| **Vascular (post-stroke)** | Basal ganglia infarct |
| **Neoplastic/paraneoplastic** | Anti-CRMP5, anti-Hu, lymphoma |
| **Degenerative** | HD, SCA17, DRPLA, Fahr's, NBIAs |

## 2. Aetiology / Pathophysiology

**Mechanism:**
- **Striatal dysfunction** with selective loss of **GABAergic medium spiny neurons** in the indirect pathway
- Disinhibition of thalamo-cortical drive → excess movement
- Dopamine excess in striatum (relative or absolute)

**Specific mechanisms:**

| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Huntington's** | CAG repeat in HTT gene; loss of striatal GABAergic neurons (indirect pathway); mutant huntingtin inclusions |
| **Sydenham's** | Anti-basal ganglia antibodies post Group A strep; cross-reactivity with striatum |
| **Levodopa-induced** | Excess dopaminergic stimulation; sensitisation |
| **HDC (chorea-acanthocytosis)** | VPS13A mutation; acanthocytes; basal ganglia degeneration |
| **HDL2** | JPH3 gene; CTG expansion |
| **DRPLA** | ATN1; CAG expansion |
| **McLeod syndrome** | XK gene; acanthocytes; Kell antigens |

## 3. Clinical Features

**Cardinal features:**
- **Irregular, brief, flowing movements** (not stereotyped, not patterned)
- **Continuous**, but may be worsened by stress, voluntary action
- **Parakinesia:** Movements incorporated into semi-purposeful acts to disguise
- **Motor impersistence:** Inability to sustain voluntary contraction (milkmaid's grip, darting tongue)
- **Hypotonia** (variable)
- **Dysarthria:** Jerky, explosive speech
- **Gait:** Dance-like, unstable

**Examination:**
- **General inspection:** Face, hands, trunk, legs
- **Motor impersistence:** Tongue protrusion, sustained grip
- **Provocation:** Talking, walking, calculations worsen
- **Gait:** Observe walk, tandem, balance

**Specific syndromes:**

| Syndrome | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Huntington's** | Adult-onset chorea, cognitive decline, psychiatric features; family history; caudate atrophy on MRI |
| **Sydenham's** | Child/adolescent, post-strep, often females, hypotonia, emotional lability, self-limiting |
| **Chorea gravidarum** | Pregnancy (1st/2nd trimester), often previous Sydenham's; self-limiting |
| **Lupus chorea** | SLE/APLS, may be first manifestation; often bilateral; responds to steroids |
| **Drug-induced** | Drug history; reversible on stopping |
| **HDL2 (JPH3)** | Adult-onset, African descent, similar to HD |

## 4. Diagnostic Approach

**History:** Onset, progression, family history, drug history, pregnancy, recent infection, cognitive/psychiatric symptoms, autoimmune features

**Examination:** Distribution, motor impersistence, gait, neurological exam (cognition, eye movements, tone, reflexes, planters)

**Investigations:** Directed by clinical suspicion

## 5. Investigations

| Test | Purpose | Findings |
|------|---------|----------|
| **MRI brain** | Caudate atrophy in HD; basal ganglia lesions in vascular, metabolic | Caudate atrophy in HD (boxcar ventricles); T1 hyperintensity in hepatic |
| **Genetic test (HTT)** | Suspected HD | CAG repeats >36 (pathogenic) |
| **Acanthocytes (blood film)** | Chorea-acanthocytosis, McLeod | Acanthocytes present |
| **Anti-streptolysin O, anti-DNase B** | Sydenham's | Elevated |
| **Antiphospholipid Ab, ANA, anti-dsDNA** | Lupus chorea | Positive |
| **TFTs** | Hyperthyroidism | Hyperthyroid |
| **Glucose, U&E, LFT, Ca2+, Mg2+** | Metabolic | Identify abnormality |
| **Anti-basal ganglia Ab** | Sydenham's, autoimmune | Research only |
| **CK** | Chorea-acanthocytosis | Elevated |
| **LP** | Encephalitis, autoimmune | Cells, OCB, antibodies |
| **Ceruloplasmin, 24h Cu, KF** | Wilson's | Abnormal |
| **HIV, syphilis serology** | Infectious | May be positive |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Athetosis** | Slow, writhing, sustained (in CP) |
| **Dystonia** | Patterned, twisting, sustained; sensory trick |
| **Tics** | Suppressible, premonitory urge, stereotyped |
| **Myoclonus** | Brief, shock-like, <100 ms |
| **Ballism** | Wild, large-amplitude proximal flinging (subthalamic) |
| **Restless legs** | Worse at rest, evening, urge to move |
| **Functional chorea** | Variable, distractible, incongruous |
| **Dyskinesia (tardive/levodopa)** | Drug history, often orofacial |
| **Stereotypy** | Repetitive, fixed pattern, in autism/LD |
| **Spasm** | Sustained, painful |

## 7. Management

**1. Treat underlying cause:**
- **Drug-induced:** Stop offending drug
- **Hyperthyroidism:** Antithyroid drugs
- **SLE chorea:** Steroids, IVIG, plasma exchange
- **Wilson's:** Penicillamine, trientine
- **Sydenham's:** Treat strep, supportive (self-limiting)
- **Hepatic/renal:** Treat metabolic

**2. Symptomatic (dopamine-depleting agents):**

| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Tetrabenazine** | 12.5-200 mg/day | VMAT2 inhibitor; depression risk; FDA/EMA-approved for HD |
| **Deutetrabenazine** | 6-48 mg/day | Deuterated form; longer half-life, fewer side effects |
| **Valbenazine** | 40-80 mg/day | VMAT2 inhibitor; approved for tardive dyskinesia |
| **Haloperidol** | 0.5-5 mg/day | Typical antipsychotic; risk of tardive, EPSE |
| **Olanzapine** | 2.5-15 mg/day | Atypical; weight gain, metabolic |
| **Risperidone** | 0.5-4 mg/day | Atypical |
| **Pimozide** | 1-6 mg/day | Older, QTc prolongation |
| **Clonazepam** | 0.5-6 mg/day | Adjunct, sedation |
| **Levetiracetam** | 1000-3000 mg/day | Limited evidence |
| **Amantadine** | 100-300 mg/day | NMDA antagonist |

**3. Disease-specific:**
- **HD:** Tetrabenazine/deutetrabenazine + supportive care; genetic counselling
- **Sydenham's:** Benzodiazepine, valproic acid for severe; usually self-limiting
- **Lupus chorea:** Steroids; anticoagulation if APS
- **Chorea-acanthocytosis:** No specific treatment; botulinum for dystonia

**4. Supportive:**
- PT, OT, falls prevention
- Speech therapy
- Nutritional support (high calorie due to movements)
- Psychological support; screen for depression
- **Genetic counselling** (HD, SCAs)

## 8. Drug Considerations
- **Tetrabenazine:** Depression, parkinsonism, sedation; contraindicated in active depression/suicidality
- **Haloperidol:** Tardive dyskinesia risk; QTc
- **Antipsychotics:** All can cause tardive (use cautiously)
- **Levodopa:** Can cause chorea (especially in HD)

## 9. Procedures
- **Genetic testing** with counselling
- **DBS of GPi** (refractory HD, some case reports)
- **Botulinum toxin** for associated dystonia

## 10. Complications
- **HD:** Progressive cognitive decline, psychiatric, dysphagia, aspiration, falls, suicide risk
- **Sydenham's:** Self-limiting; rheumatic heart disease (separate)
- **Chorea-acanthocytosis:** Acanthocytes, myopathy, axonal neuropathy, lip/tongue-biting
- **Lupus chorea:** Recurrent, stroke risk if APS
- **Drug-induced:** Withdrawal chorea (variable)
- **Injury** from falls

## 11. Red Flags
- New chorea + cognitive/psychiatric decline = HD
- New chorea + pregnancy = chorea gravidarum
- New chorea + SLE = lupus chorea
- New chorea + oral contraceptive / pregnancy
- New chorea + acanthocytes = chorea-acanthocytosis
- **Suicide risk in HD** — high

## 12. Prognosis
- **HD:** Progressive, fatal 15-20 years post-diagnosis
- **Sydenham's:** Self-limiting (weeks to months); 20% recur; rheumatic heart disease separate
- **Chorea gravidarum:** Self-limiting (resolves postpartum); can recur
- **Drug-induced:** Reversible on stopping (may take weeks)
- **Lupus chorea:** Responsive to treatment
- **Vascular chorea:** Variable, often improves

## 13. Topic Correlation
- [[Huntingtons Disease]] — main cause
- [[Sydenhams Chorea]] — child
- [[Wilsons Disease]] — treatable
- [[Dystonia]] — differential
- [[Hyperkinetic Movement Disorders Hub]] — classification

## 14. Special Situations
- **Pregnancy:** Chorea gravidarum (oestrogen effect); treat with low-dose haloperidol or benzodiazepine; avoid tetrabenazine
- **Paediatric:** Sydenham's, PANDAS, drug-induced, HD (juvenile/Westphal variant)
- **Elderly:** Drug-induced, vascular, HD
- **Pre-symptomatic HD testing:** Requires extensive counselling
- **Suicide risk:** HD, depression screening essential
- **Driving:** Report; assess fitness

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
- **Definition:** Irregular, flowing, purposeless, non-stereotyped movements
- **Most common cause (adult):** Huntington's disease (AD, CAG repeat, HTT gene)
- **Most common cause (child):** Sydenham's chorea (post-strep)
- **Investigations:** MRI (caudate), HTT gene, acanthocytes, ASO, ANA/APS, TFTs, ceruloplasmin
- **Treatment:** Stop cause + tetrabenazine/deutetrabenazine (VMAT2) + antipsychotics
- **HD gene:** HTT, CAG repeat, AD, anticipation (paternal)
- **Caudate atrophy** = HD on MRI (boxcar ventricles)

## Common Confusions
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Chorea vs athetosis | Chorea: brief, flowing; athetosis: slow, writhing, sustained |
| Chorea vs tics | Tics: suppressible, premonitory urge; chorea: not suppressible |
| Chorea vs ballism | Ballism: large proximal flinging (subthalamic); chorea: distal small |
| Chorea vs dyskinesia | Dyskinesia: drug-related; chorea: various causes |
| HD vs Sydenham's | HD: genetic, progressive; Sydenham's: post-infectious, self-limiting |
| Chorea-acanthocytosis vs HD | Both: chorea, neurodegeneration; acanthocytosis: acanthocytes, lip-biting, myopathy |

## Mnemonics
- **Chorea** = **C**ontinuous **H**yperkinetic **O**rganised **R**andom **E**xtraneous **A**ctivity
- **HD features** = **C**horeoathetosis, **D**ementia, **D**epression (**CDD**)
- **HD gene** = **H**untingtin (**HTT**) = **H**igh number of **CAG**
- **Causes mnemonic** = **H**D, **S**ydenham, **A**utoimmune, **M**etabolic, **D**rug, **P**regnancy, **V**ascular (**HSAMDPV**)
- **Treatment mnemonic** = **T**etrabenazine, **D**eutetrabenazine, **V**albenazine, **A**ntipsychotics, **B**enzodiazepines (**TDVAB**)
