# NCS-EMG & Neuromuscular Disorders

> [!tip] **NCS = peripheral nerves**; **EMG = muscle**. Combined, they differentiate **neuropathy (axonal vs demyelinating), myopathy, NMJ disorders**, and localise lesions (root, plexus, peripheral nerve).

## Learning Objectives
- [ ] Describe NCS and EMG methodology, parameters, normal values
- [ ] Differentiate axonal vs demyelinating polyneuropathy
- [ ] Recognise NMJ patterns (presynaptic vs postsynaptic)
- [ ] Identify myopathic EMG patterns
- [ ] Localise lesion: root, plexus, peripheral nerve, anterior horn cell
- [ ] Apply findings to common clinical scenarios (GBS, CIDP, ALS, myositis)

---

## 1. Definition / Classification

### Definition
- **NCS (Nerve Conduction Studies):** Surface electrodes stimulate peripheral nerves and record response (CMAP for motor, SNAP for sensory). Measures **conduction velocity, amplitude, latency, F-waves**.
- **EMG (Electromyography):** Needle electrode records **insertional activity, spontaneous activity, motor unit action potentials (MUAPs), recruitment pattern**.
- **Repetitive Nerve Stimulation (RNS):** Tests NMJ (decremental/incremental response).
- **Single Fibre EMG (SFEMG):** Most sensitive for NMJ disorders.

### Classification of Disorders
| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Anterior horn cell** | ALS, SMA, polio, Kennedy |
| **Root** | Radiculopathy (C5/6, L5/S1) |
| **Plexus** | Brachial/lumbosacral plexopathy, neuralgic amyotrophy |
| **Peripheral nerve** | Mononeuropathy (carpal tunnel), polyneuropathy (CIDP, GBS, diabetic) |
| **NMJ** | Myasthenia gravis, LEMS, botulism |
| **Myopathy** | Inflammatory (PM, DM, IBM), dystrophy, metabolic, congenital |
| **No pathology** | Functional weakness |

---

## 2. NCS Parameters

| Parameter | Tests | Normal |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| **CMAP amplitude** | Motor axon loss / muscle | >4 mV (median) |
| **SNAP amplitude** | Sensory axon loss | >10 µV (median) |
| **Distal motor latency (DML)** | Distal conduction (demyelination) | <4.5 ms (median) |
| **Motor conduction velocity (MCV)** | Myelination | >50 m/s |
| **F-wave** | Proximal nerve root | <30 ms (median) |
| **H-reflex** | S1 root / monosynaptic reflex | Absent or normal latency |

### Key Concept: Axonal vs Demyelinating
| Feature | Axonal | Demyelinating |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| **CMAP amplitude** | ↓↓ | Normal or mildly ↓ |
| **SNAP amplitude** | ↓↓ | Normal or mildly ↓ |
| **Conduction velocity** | Normal or mildly ↓ | ↓↓ (<70% of normal) |
| **DML** | Normal | ↑ (prolonged) |
| **F-wave** | Normal/mildly delayed | ↑↑ delayed / absent |
| **Conduction block** | Absent | Present (key) |
| **Temporal dispersion** | Absent | Present |

> **Axonal = loss of axons** (amplitude ↓). **Demyelinating = slowed conduction** (velocity ↓, latency ↑).

---

## 3. EMG Parameters

| Parameter | Active | Chronic Neurogenic | Myopathic |
|-----------|--------|--------------------|-----------|
| **Insertional activity** | ↑ | Normal | Normal/↑ |
| **Fibrillations / positive sharp waves** | ↑ (denervation) | Mild | ± |
| **Fasciculations** | ± | ↑↑ (anterior horn cell) | Absent |
| **MUAP amplitude** | ± | ↑ (large, reinnervated) | ↓ (small) |
| **MUAP duration** | Short | ↑ (long) | ↓ (short) |
| **Polyphasia** | ± | ↑ | ↑ |
| **Recruitment** | Reduced (fast firing) | Reduced (depleted units) | Early/full (small units fire fast) |
| **Pattern** | Denervation | Chronic reinnervation | Myopathic |

## 4. Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (Most Common)

### NCS Findings
| Parameter | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| **Median DML** | ↑ >4.5 ms |
| **Median sensory velocity** | ↓ <50 m/s |
| **Median CMAP** | ↓ (in severe cases) |
| **Ulnar** | Normal |
| **EMG (APB)** | ± denervation in chronic |

### Severity Grading
| Grade | DML | SNAP |
|-------|-----|------|
| **Mild** | <4.5 ms | Borderline |
| **Moderate** | 4.5-6 ms | ↓ |
| **Severe** | >6 ms | Absent |
| **Very severe** | CMAP ↓ | Absent |

---

## 5. Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)

### NCS/EMG Pattern
| Time | Findings |
|------|----------|
| **Day 1-7** | Often normal ("nerve conduction failure") |
| **Day 7-14** | ↓ SNAP, prolonged/absent F-waves, prolonged H-reflex (early changes) |
| **Day 14-21** | AIDP: ↓ MCV, prolonged DML, conduction block, temporal dispersion |
| **AMAN/AMSAN** | Axonal: ↓ CMAP/SNAP amplitudes, relatively preserved velocity |
| **Chronic phase** | Reinnervation, ↑ MUAP amplitude (recovery) |

### AIDP vs AMAN
| Feature | AIDP (demyelinating) | AMAN (axonal) |
|---------|----------------------|---------------|
| **Campylobacter** | Less common | Common (Asia, China) |
| **MCV** | ↓ | Normal |
| **F-wave** | ↑↑ delayed/absent | Normal/mildly delayed |
| **Conduction block** | Present | Absent |
| **Prognosis** | Slower, better | Faster but more severe |

---

## 6. CIDP (Chronic Inflammatory Demyelinating Polyneuropathy)

### NCS Criteria (EFNS/PNS)
- **Motor MCV** ↓ (<70% of LLN, or <80% if CMAP <80%)
- **DML** ↑ (>130% of ULN)
- **F-wave** ↑ (>130% of ULN)
- **Conduction block** present (>30% reduction in proximal vs distal CMAP amplitude, excluding entrapment sites)
- **Temporal dispersion** present

> Distinguishing from GBS: **progression >8 weeks, relapsing-remitting**.

---

## 7. Myasthenia Gravis (MG) — NMJ Disorder

### RNS Findings
- **Decrement** on 2-3 Hz stimulation (≥10% decrease in CMAP amplitude between 1st and 4th response)
- **Post-exercise facilitation:** Brief improvement after 10 sec exercise (pathognomonic but absent in many)
- **SFEMG:** ↑ jitter, blocking — **most sensitive test** (>95% sensitivity in MG)

### Pattern
- **Postsynaptic NMJ disorder** (AChR antibodies)
- Often proximal weakness, fatigability
- Normal SNAP, normal CMAP at rest (or mildly ↓ if severe)

---

## 8. Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome (LEMS)

### RNS Findings
- **Baseline CMAP ↓** (at rest)
- **Incremental response** at 20-50 Hz stimulation (≥100% increase) or post-exercise (>60%)
- **SFEMG:** ↑ jitter, blocking

### Pattern
- **Presynaptic NMJ disorder** (anti-VGCC antibodies, paraneoplastic)
- Often with small cell lung cancer
- Proximal weakness, autonomic symptoms, ↓ reflexes (improve with exercise)

---

## 9. Botulism

### Pattern
- **↓ CMAP** (presynaptic NMJ block)
- **Incremental response** at high-frequency stimulation (similar to LEMS)
- **SFEMG:** ↑ jitter, blocking
- Post-synaptic miniature endplate potentials absent

---

## 10. Myopathies

### EMG Pattern
| Feature | Myopathy |
|---------|----------|
| **MUAP amplitude** | ↓ (small) |
| **MUAP duration** | ↓ (short) |
| **Polyphasia** | ↑ |
| **Recruitment** | **Early/full** (many small units fire fast) |
| **Fibrillations** | ± (inflammatory/dystrophic) |
| **Insertional activity** | ↑ |

### Specific Patterns
| Myopathy | Special Feature |
|----------|----------------|
| **Polymyositis / DM** | Fibrillations + myopathic MUAPs |
| **Inclusion body myositis** | Mixed myopathic + neurogenic |
| **Dystrophy** | Myopathic + fibrillations |
| **Myotonic dystrophy** | **Myotonic discharges** (dive-bomber) |
| **Mitochondrial** | May be normal or mildly myopathic |

---

## 11. Motor Neurone Disease (ALS)

### EMG Pattern
- **Active denervation** (fibrillations, positive sharp waves) in **≥3 regions** (bulbar, cervical, thoracic, lumbosacral)
- **Chronic neurogenic changes** (large, long, polyphasic MUAPs, reduced recruitment)
- **Fasciculations** (frequent)
- **Normal SNAP** (sensory spared — key diagnostic clue)
- **Normal MCV** (or slightly slowed if axon loss severe)

### Awaji / El Escorial Criteria
- Clinical + EMG evidence of **UMN + LMN** in ≥3 regions → definite ALS

---

## 12. Radiculopathy

### Pattern
- **Normal SNAP** (root lesion is preganglionic → sensory ganglion spared)
- **↓ CMAP** if motor root affected
- **EMG denervation** in paraspinal muscles + myotomal pattern
- **F-wave** prolonged (proximal lesion)

### Common Levels
| Root | Myotome | Reflex |
|------|---------|--------|
| **C5/6** | Deltoid, biceps, brachioradialis | Biceps ↓ |
| **C7** | Triceps, wrist extensors | Triceps ↓ |
| **L4** | Quadriceps | Knee ↓ |
| **L5** | EHL, tibialis anterior | Medial hamstring |
| **S1** | Gastrocnemius, peronei | Ankle ↓ |

---

## 13. Plexopathy

| Pattern | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| **Upper trunk (Erb's)** | ↓ biceps/deltoid EMG; sensory preserved |
| **Lower trunk (Klumpke)** | ↓ hand intrinsics + flexor carpi ulnaris |
| **Lumbosacral (lumbosacral trunk)** | ↓ L5/S1 muscles |

---

## 14. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| **Axonal vs demyelinating** | Axonal = amplitude ↓; Demyelinating = velocity ↓, latency ↑, block |
| **AIDP** | Demyelinating; F-wave prolonged; conduction block; temporal dispersion |
| **AMAN** | Axonal; ↓ CMAP/SNAP amplitude; normal velocity; C. jejuni associated |
| **CIDP** | >8 weeks; demyelinating; relapsing; responds to steroids/IVIG |
| **MG (postsynaptic)** | Decrement on 2-3 Hz RNS; SFEMG most sensitive |
| **LEMS (presynaptic)** | Low CMAP; INCREMENT on high-frequency RNS; small cell lung cancer |
| **Botulism** | ↓ CMAP, increment at high frequency; infant/infant food |
| **ALS** | LMN + UMN in ≥3 regions; fasciculations; SNAP preserved |
| **Carpal tunnel** | Median DML ↑, sensory velocity ↓ |
| **Radiculopathy** | SNAP normal (preganglionic); EMG myotomal |
| **Myopathy** | Small, short, polyphasic MUAPs; early recruitment; ± fibrillations |
| **Myotonic dystrophy** | Dive-bomber myotonic discharges |

---

## 15. Special Situations

| Situation | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Pregnancy** | NCS/EMG safe; postpone if possible in 1st trimester |
| **Anticoagulated** | Avoid needle EMG (bleeding risk) |
| **Pacemaker** | Avoid magnetic stimulation; surface stimulation safe |
| **Paediatric** | SFEMG rarely done; tolerance limited; sedation needed for MRI |
| **Oedema** | May affect amplitudes (false low) |
| **Hypothermia** | Slows conduction |

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** Difference between axonal and demyelinating neuropathy on NCS?
   **A:** Axonal = amplitude ↓ (axon loss), velocity preserved; Demyelinating = velocity ↓, latency ↑, conduction block, temporal dispersion.
2. **Q:** RNS findings in MG vs LEMS?
   **A:** MG = decremental response at 2-3 Hz; LEMS = low baseline CMAP + INCREMENTAL response at high-frequency or post-exercise.
3. **Q:** What NCS findings are earliest in GBS?
   **A:** Prolonged F-wave and absent H-reflex (proximal demyelination).
4. **Q:** What is the difference between AIDP and AMAN?
   **A:** AIDP = demyelinating; AMAN = axonal (C. jejuni, Asia).
5. **Q:** How is ALS diagnosed on EMG?
   **A:** Active denervation + chronic neurogenic changes in ≥3 regions (bulbar, cervical, thoracic, lumbosacral); SNAP preserved.

## Common Confusions

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Carpal tunnel = axonal | Usually demyelinating; axonal loss in severe chronic |
| Normal NCS in early GBS | Repeat at day 7-14; initial NCS may be normal |
| MG = myopathy | NMJ disorder; muscle and nerve studies normal |
| ALS sensory sparing | SNAP normal despite severe weakness |

---

## Mnemonics

1. **AXON DOWN, MYELIN SLOW** — axonal = amplitude ↓; demyelinating = velocity ↓
2. **AIDP = DEMYELINATE, AMAN = AXON** — AIDP has block, AMAN has amplitude ↓
3. **MG = DOWN, LEMS = UP** — MG decrement; LEMS increment
4. **SNAP NORMAL IN RADICULOPATHY** — preganglionic lesion
5. **FIRES FIRST IN GBS** — F-wave and H-reflex earliest abnormalities

## MCQs (10)

1. **Q:** Carpal tunnel syndrome is characterised by:
   **A:** ↓ Ulnar DML **B:** ↑ Median DML **C:** ↓ Peroneal MCV **D:** Absent F-wave
   **Ans:** B — Median DML prolonged at the wrist.

2. **Q:** Earliest NCS abnormality in GBS:
   **A:** ↓ SNAP **B:** ↓ CMAP **C:** ↑ F-wave latency / absent H-reflex **D:** ↓ MCV
   **Ans:** C — F-wave and H-reflex earliest (proximal demyelination).

3. **Q:** Which pattern suggests demyelinating neuropathy?
   **A:** ↓ amplitude only **B:** Normal amplitude with ↓ velocity, ↑ latency, conduction block **C:** ↓ SNAP with normal velocity **D:** Fasciculations
   **Ans:** B

4. **Q:** Lambert-Eaton syndrome RNS shows:
   **A:** Decrement at low-frequency stimulation **B:** Increment at high-frequency stimulation **C:** Normal response **D:** Absent CMAP
   **Ans:** B — Presynaptic NMJ → low baseline + increment.

5. **Q:** Myotonic dystrophy EMG shows:
   **A:** Polyphasic MUAPs **B:** Myotonic (dive-bomber) discharges **C:** Fibrillations **D:** Fasciculations
   **Ans:** B

6. **Q:** Which is most sensitive for myasthenia gravis?
   **A:** Routine NCS **B:** RNS **C:** SFEMG **D:** EMG
   **Ans:** C — SFEMG: >95% sensitivity for MG.

7. **Q:** ALS classically shows:
   **A:** ↓ SNAP **B:** Preserved SNAP with diffuse denervation on EMG **C:** Conduction block **D:** Decrement on RNS
   **Ans:** B — Sensory sparing (SNAP normal) is a hallmark.

8. **Q:** In radiculopathy, the SNAP is:
   **A:** ↓↓ **B:** Normal (preganglionic lesion) **C:** Absent **D:** Variable
   **Ans:** B — Root is preganglionic → dorsal ganglion spared → SNAP normal.

9. **Q:** Myopathic EMG shows:
   **A:** Large, long MUAPs with reduced recruitment **B:** Small, short, polyphasic MUAPs with early recruitment **C:** Fibrillations with normal MUAPs **D:** Fasciculations
   **Ans:** B — Small short units, full early recruitment.

10. **Q:** LEMS is associated with:
    **A:** Anti-AChR antibodies **B:** Anti-VGCC antibodies (often small cell lung cancer) **C:** Anti-MuSK antibodies **D:** Anti-GM1 antibodies
    **Ans:** B — Anti-VGCC (P/Q type) in LEMS.

## SBA Questions (10)

1. **Scenario:** A 30-year-old man with progressive proximal weakness, CK 8000. EMG shows small, short polyphasic MUAPs with early recruitment. Diagnosis?
   **A:** ALS **B:** Inflammatory myopathy (polymyositis) **C:** Myasthenia gravis **D:** GBS
   **Ans:** B — CK + myopathic EMG = inflammatory myopathy; consider polymyositis.

2. **Scenario:** A 50-year-old with weakness improving with activity, ↓ reflexes, dry mouth. Anti-VGCC antibodies positive. Diagnosis?
   **A:** MG **B:** LEMS **C:** Polymyositis **D:** ALS
   **Ans:** B — Proximal weakness, autonomic features, anti-VGCC = LEMS. Screen for small cell lung cancer.

3. **Scenario:** A 35-year-old 2 weeks post gastroenteritis with ascending weakness, areflexia. NCS: absent F-waves, prolonged DML, ↓ MCV. Pattern?
   **A:** Axonal (AMAN) **B:** Demyelinating (AIDP) **C:** NMJ **D:** Myopathic
   **Ans:** B — AIDP (most common form of GBS in West). Treatment: IVIG.

4. **Scenario:** A 60-year-old with progressive painless distal weakness, fasciculations in 4 regions, normal SNAP. Diagnosis?
   **A:** CIDP **B:** ALS **C:** GBS **D:** MG
   **Ans:** B — UMN + LMN signs in ≥3 regions, SNAP preserved = ALS.

5. **Scenario:** A 40-year-old with fatigable ptosis, diplopia. NCS normal at rest. SFEMG shows ↑ jitter. Diagnosis?
   **A:** LEMS **B:** MG **C:** Myopathy **D:** MND
   **Ans:** B — SFEMG most sensitive for MG. Confirm with anti-AChR antibodies.

6. **Scenario:** A 70-year-old with weight loss, proximal weakness, dry mouth. RNS: low baseline CMAP, 200% increment at high-frequency stimulation. Diagnosis?
   **A:** MG **B:** LEMS **C:** Botulism **D:** Polymyositis
   **Ans:** B — Increment = presynaptic NMJ = LEMS. Search for small cell lung cancer.

7. **Scenario:** A 45-year-old with progressive sensory symptoms over 6 months, distal weakness, foot drop. NCS: ↓ amplitude, normal velocity. Pattern?
   **A:** Demyelinating **B:** Axonal **C:** NMJ **D:** Myopathic
   **Ans:** B — Axonal pattern: amplitude ↓, velocity normal. Consider diabetic, B12, idiopathic.

8. **Scenario:** A 60-year-old with chronic progressive proximal/distal weakness over 2 years, relapsing course. NCS: ↓ MCV, conduction block. Diagnosis?
   **A:** GBS **B:** CIDP **C:** ALS **D:** MG
   **Ans:** B — CIDP: >8 weeks, demyelinating (conduction block), relapsing.

9. **Scenario:** A 25-year-old with foot drop after leg crossing. NCS: ↓ peroneal CMAP at EDB, normal peroneal at popliteal fossa. Diagnosis?
   **A:** L5 radiculopathy **B:** Common peroneal neuropathy at fibula head **C:** Sciatic neuropathy **D:** L4 radiculopathy
   **Ans:** B — Conduction block across fibula head = peroneal compression.

10. **Scenario:** A 55-year-old with hand numbness, worse at night, positive Tinel/Phalen. NCS: median sensory velocity ↓, DML ↑. Diagnosis?
    **A:** Cubital tunnel **B:** Carpal tunnel **C:** C6 radiculopathy **D:** Thoracic outlet
    **Ans:** B — Carpal tunnel (median nerve at wrist).
