# Essential Tremor

Related: [[Movement Disorders Hub]], [[Parkinsonism Hub]], [[Hyperkinetic Movement Disorders Hub]], [[Enhanced Physiological Tremor]], [[Cerebellar Tremor]]

> [!tip] **Essential tremor (ET)** = bilateral, largely symmetric **postural/kinetic tremor** of upper limbs (± head, voice), with **family history in 50%** and **alcohol responsiveness**. Most common movement disorder.

## 1. Definition / Epidemiology / Classification

**Definition:** Chronic, progressive neurological disorder characterised by bilateral, largely symmetric **postural and kinetic tremor** of the upper limbs, often with head, voice, or lower limb involvement. Absence of other neurological signs (no bradykinesia, rigidity, dystonia, ataxia).

**Epidemiology:**
- **Prevalence:** 0.4-5% (increases with age); up to 20% in elderly
- **Age of onset:** Bimodal — early-onset (5-20 years) and late-onset (>60 years)
- **Sex ratio:** Slight male predominance
- **Family history:** 50-70% (autosomal dominant with variable penetrance); several susceptibility genes (ETM1-4, LINGO1, FUS)
- **Risk factors:** Aging, family history; possible environmental (harmane, lead)

**Classification:**
- **Classical ET:** Isolated bilateral upper limb tremor, no other neurological signs
- **ET-plus:** Tremor + additional soft signs (questionable dystonia, impaired tandem gait, mild memory impairment, rest tremor)
- **Indeterminate tremor**

## 2. Aetiology / Pathophysiology

**Mechanism:**
- Likely **cerebello-thalamo-cortical pathway** dysfunction
- Hyperactivity of inferior olive → GABAergic dysfunction in cerebellar Purkinje cells
- Decreased GABA in cerebellum
- Lewy bodies in locus coeruleus (subset of cases) — overlaps with Parkinson's

**Genetics:**
- **Familial:** Autosomal dominant with variable penetrance
- **Genes:** ETM1 (3q13), ETM2 (2p25), LINGO1 (risk variant), FUS, TENM4
- **Sporadic:** Common, possible somatic mosaicism

**Risk factors:** Aging, family history, possibly β-adrenergic drugs, hyperthyroidism (mimics)

## 3. Clinical Features

**Core feature:** Bilateral, largely symmetric **postural/kinetic tremor** of upper limbs (4-12 Hz)
- **Worsened by:** Sustained posture (holding objects, writing), action, stress, fatigue, caffeine
- **Improved by:** Alcohol (in 50-70%), rest, relaxation
- **Most disabling:** Writing, eating, drinking (spillage)

**Distribution:**
- **Upper limbs:** 95% (always present)
- **Head (neck):** 30% — "yes-yes" or "no-no" (titubation)
- **Voice:** 20-30% — vocal tremor
- **Face/jaw:** Rare
- **Lower limbs:** Rare
- **Tongue:** Rare

**Examination:**
- **Postural tremor:** Arms outstretched → tremor
- **Kinetic tremor:** Finger-nose test → tremor increases approaching target (but no terminal oscillation/dysmetria of cerebellar)
- **No rest tremor** (may be subtle in late disease)
- **No bradykinesia, rigidity, dystonia, ataxia**
- **Tandem gait** may be impaired in elderly (ET-plus)
- **Mild gait abnormality** possible

**Disease course:** Slowly progressive over decades; significant disability in 15-25%.

## 4. Diagnostic Approach

**MDS 2018 Criteria (International Parkinson and Movement Disorders Society):**

**Isolated tremor syndrome of bilateral upper limb action tremor + at least one of:**
- Head tremor
- Voice tremor
- Tremor in lower limbs
- Task-specific tremor
- Tendon reflex tremor

**AND duration >3 years; no other neurological signs.**

**TRIG (Tremor Investigation Group) Criteria:**
- **Definite ET:** Bilateral postural tremor of moderate amplitude, present in 2+ body parts (hands, head, voice, legs) for 5+ years
- **Probable ET:** Bilateral postural tremor (mild amplitude) ± head/voice
- **Possible ET:** Bilateral postural tremor (mild), head/voice absent

**Tremor recording / accelerometry:** Objective measurement (Hz, amplitude)

## 5. Investigations

| Investigation | Indication | Findings |
|---------------|------------|----------|
| **Clinical diagnosis** | Most cases | Bilateral postural/kinetic; no other signs |
| **Tremor analysis (accelerometry, EMG)** | Atypical, research | Frequency 4-12 Hz; synchronous antagonist bursts |
| **MRI brain** | Atypical features (asymmetry, dystonia, ataxia) | Excludes structural lesion |
| **DAT-SPECT (FP-CIT)** | Suspected Parkinson's | Normal in ET; reduced in PD |
| **TFTs** | Rule out thyrotoxicosis | Normal |
| **LFTs, electrolytes, B12** | Rule out metabolic | Normal |
| **Drug/toxin screen** | Rule out drug-induced | Excludes lithium, valproate, SSRIs, β-agonists |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Parkinson's disease** | Resting tremor (4-6 Hz), unilateral onset, bradykinesia, rigidity, response to levodopa |
| **Enhanced physiological tremor** | High-frequency (8-12 Hz), reversible, drug/thyroid-induced |
| **Cerebellar tremor** | Intention, dysmetria, dysdiadochokinesia, ataxia, slow |
| **Dystonic tremor** | Dystonic posturing, irregular, position-specific, geste antagoniste |
| **Drug-induced tremor** | Symmetrical postural, drug history (lithium, valproate, SSRIs, salbutamol) |
| **Hyperthyroidism** | Symmetrical, fine, with weight loss, heat intolerance |
| **Rubral (midbrain) tremor** | Rest + postural + intention, broad; "wing-beating" |
| **Functional tremor** | Variable, distractible, entrainable, inconsistent |
| **Holmes tremor** | Combined rest/postural/intention, slow, brainstem lesion |
| **Wilson's disease** | Wing-beating, young patient, Kayser-Fleischer rings |

## 7. Management

**Non-pharmacological:**
- **Reassurance** (mild cases)
- **Avoidance:** Caffeine, β-agonists, stress, fatigue
- **Weighted utensils, drinking aids**
- **Occupational therapy**

**First-line drugs:**

| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Propranolol** | 40-320 mg/day (long-acting) | Non-selective β-blocker; contraindications: asthma, bradycardia, heart block |
| **Primidone** | 50-750 mg/day | Start low (25 mg) to avoid acute tolerance/sedation; phenobarbital metabolite |

**Second-line drugs:**

| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Topiramate** | 50-300 mg/day | Modest effect; cognitive side effects |
| **Gabapentin** | 1200-2400 mg/day | Useful for neuropathic ET |
| **Pregabalin** | 150-600 mg/day | Alternative |
| **Alprazolam** | 0.5-3 mg/day | Short-term use; dependence |
| **Clonazepam** | 0.5-4 mg/day | For kinetic/postural |
| **Nadolol** | 40-240 mg/day | Alternative β-blocker |
| **Atenolol** | 50-100 mg/day | β1 selective (less bronchospasm) |
| **Botulinum toxin** | Focal (voice, head) | Specialised use |
| **Levetiracetam** | 1000-3000 mg/day | Variable evidence |
| **Mirtazapine** | 15-30 mg/day | Limited evidence |

**Surgical (refractory, disabling):**
- **Deep brain stimulation (DBS)** of **VIM nucleus of thalamus** — most effective
- **MRI-guided focused ultrasound (MRgFUS)** — thalamotomy (incisionless, 2020+)
- **Thalamotomy** (radiofrequency)

**Treatment response:** Propranolol & primidone effective in 50-70%; alcohol improves 50-70%.

## 8. Drug Interactions
- **Propranolol:** Verapamil/diltiazem (bradycardia), insulin (masks hypoglycaemia), MAOIs, clonidine
- **Primidone:** Valproate (↑ levels), warfarin (↓ effect via enzyme induction)
- **Avoid in asthma** (propranolol)

## 9. Procedures
- **DBS of VIM thalamus:** For drug-refractory ET (≥30% improvement)
- **MRgFUS thalamotomy:** Incisionless; approved for ET
- **Botulinum toxin:** Focal voice/head tremor

## 10. Complications
- **Disability:** Writing, eating, drinking, social embarrassment
- **Psychological:** Anxiety, depression, social isolation
- **Drug side effects:** Sedation, bradycardia, dizziness
- **Surgical:** Intracranial haemorrhage (DBS), dysarthria, paraesthesia (MRgFUS)

## 11. Red Flags
- Sudden onset (consider stroke, drug)
- Unilateral (consider PD, dystonia, structural)
- Rest tremor dominant (PD)
- Additional neurological signs (consider MSA, cerebellar disease, Wilson's)
- Onset <20 years (consider Wilson's, dystonia)

## 12. Prognosis
- Slowly progressive over decades
- 15-25% have significant functional disability
- No effect on mortality
- Quality of life impact variable; surgery improves in 80%

## 14. Special Situations
- **Pregnancy:** Avoid primidone (teratogenic); propranolol with caution (IUGR, neonatal bradycardia)
- **Paediatric:** Consider Wilson's, dystonia, juvenile PD
- **Elderly:** Start low; falls risk with benzodiazepines
- **Asthma:** Avoid non-selective β-blockers
- **Driving:** Report if significant; safety implications
- **Alcohol:** May help but dependence risk
- **Liver disease:** Reduce primidone dose

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
- **Definition:** Bilateral postural/kinetic tremor of upper limbs ± head/voice
- **Differential diagnosis main:** Parkinson's (resting), Cerebellar (intention), Enhanced physiological (drug/thyroid)
- **First-line drugs:** Propranolol 40-320 mg/day, Primidone 50-750 mg/day
- **Alcohol** improves 50-70%
- **Genetics:** Autosomal dominant, ETM1/2, LINGO1
- **Surgery (refractory):** DBS of VIM thalamus, MRgFUS thalamotomy
- **Avoid:** Non-selective β-blocker in asthma
- **Investigation:** DAT-SPECT normal in ET (vs reduced in PD)

## Viva Questions
1. Differentiate ET from PD — ET: postural/kinetic, bilateral, no bradykinesia. PD: rest, unilateral, with bradykinesia, rigidity.
2. First-line drugs — Propranolol, primidone
3. Target of DBS — VIM nucleus of thalamus
4. Special features in young — Wilson's, dystonia
5. Pathophysiology — Cerebello-thalamo-cortical; GABAergic dysfunction
6. Why alcohol helps — Central GABA potentiation (mechanism unclear)

## Common Confusions
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| ET vs PD tremor | ET: postural/kinetic, bilateral; PD: rest, unilateral onset, with bradykinesia |
| ET vs cerebellar tremor | ET: postural/kinetic, no dysmetria; cerebellar: intention, dysmetria, ataxia |
| ET vs enhanced physiological | ET: persistent, genetic; enhanced: reversible, drug/thyroid |
| ET vs dystonic tremor | Dystonic: posturing present, position-specific, geste antagoniste |
| ET-plus | Has additional soft signs (gait, memory) but does not change management |

## Mnemonics
- **Treatment ladder** = **P**ropranolol, **P**rimidone, then **T**opiramate, **G**abapentin, **B**enzos, **D**BS (**PPTGBD**)
- **Differential** = **P**arkinson's, **E**nhanced physiological, **C**erebellar, **D**ystonic, **H**yperthyroid, **D**rug-induced (**PECDHD**)
