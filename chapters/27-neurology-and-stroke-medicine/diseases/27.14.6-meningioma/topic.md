# Meningioma

> [!tip] **Definition (WHO CNS5 2021)** — Most common **primary intracranial tumour** (~36%). Arises from **arachnoid cap cells**. **Meningothelial (Grade 1)**: 80% — typical, fibrous, transitional, psammomatous, angiomatous, microcystic, secretory, lymphoplasmacyte-rich, metaplastic. **Intermediate (Grade 2)**: atypical, clear cell, chordoid. **Malignant (Grade 3)**: anaplastic, papillary, rhabdoid. Pearl: **dural tail**, **hyperostosis**, **psammoma bodies**, **"meningioma" = NF2 in young/bilateral**.

## 1. Definition / Epidemiology / Classification
**Definition:** Slow-growing, extra-axial, dural-based tumour of arachnoid cap cells; WHO CNS5 (2021) 3 grades based on histology + molecular.

**Epidemiology**
- Incidence: **~8-10/100,000/year**; prevalence much higher (autopsy: 1-3%)
- Age: median **65y**; rare <20y
- Sex: **F:M = 2-3:1** (higher in spinal, NF2)
- Risk factors: **ionising radiation** (atomic bomb survivors, treated tinea capitis), **NF2** (bilateral, multiple), hormonal (oestrogen/progesterone receptors — growth in pregnancy), breast cancer association
- Location: parasagittal (25%), convexity (20%), sphenoid wing (15-20%), olfactory groove (10%), tuberculum sellae (10%), posterior fossa (10%), spinal (15%)

**Classification (WHO CNS5 2021)**
| Grade | Subtype | Behaviour | Recurrence | Notes |
|-------|---------|-----------|------------|-------|
| **1 (80%)** | Meningothelial, fibrous, transitional, psammomatous, angiomatous | Benign | 5-25% | Most common |
| **2 (15-20%)** | Atypical, clear cell, chordoid | Intermediate | 40-50% | ≥4 mitoses/10 HPF OR brain invasion OR 3 of 5 atypia |
| **3 (1-3%)** | Anaplastic, papillary, rhabdoid | Malignant | 50-90% | ≥20 mitoses/10 HPF OR anaplasia |

**Molecular (CNS5):** **SMARCE1** (clear cell), **BAP1** (rhabdoid), **KLF4/TRAF7** (secretory), **CDKN2A/B** homozygous deletion = grade 3 (anaplastic), **TERT promoter** mutation = aggressive, **NF2** mutation = grade 1 NF2-associated.

---

## 2. Aetiology / Pathophysiology
**Pathogenesis:** Arachnoid cap cell monoclonal proliferation → slow growth (years) → compression of brain. **NF2 gene** (22q12) loss → merlin/schwannomin dysfunction → multiple meningiomas + schwannomas. **Hormonal influence** = oestrogen (PR+ in 70%); growth during pregnancy.

**Molecular/Genetic:** NF2 mutation in 50-60% sporadic; chromosome 22 loss; **SMARCE1** clear cell; **BAP1** rhabdoid; **TERT/CDKN2A** aggressive.

---

## 3. Clinical Features
**History (slow, subacute-months-years):**
- Asymptomatic (incidental): 30%
- Headache (60%): dull, non-specific
- Seizures (30-40%): focal; often presenting symptom
- Focal deficit: hemiparesis, aphasia, hemianopia (location-dependent)
- Cognitive change (frontal)
- Personality change (frontal)
- Anosmia (olfactory groove)
- Visual loss (tuberculum sellae, sphenoid wing)

**Examination (Localisation):**
| Domain | Findings | Localisation |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| **Higher cortical** | Dysphasia, neglect, abulia | Convexity, frontal |
| **Cranial nerves** | Anosmia (CN I), III palsy (cavernous), V (CPA), VIII (CPA) | Skull base |
| **Motor** | Contralateral hemiparesis | Convexity, parasagittal (leg > arm) |
| **Sensory** | Paraesthesia, hemianaesthesia | Convexity |
| **Visual fields** | Bitemporal hemianopia, homonymous hemianopia | Tuberculum, occipital |
| **Cerebellar** | Ataxia | Posterior fossa, CPA |

**Specific Variants:**
| Variant | Features | Location |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **Convexity** | Seizure, hemiparesis | Lateral hemisphere |
| **Parasagittal** | Leg weakness, urinary urgency | Falx |
| **Olfactory groove** | Anosmia, Foster-Kennedy (ipsilateral optic atrophy + contralateral papilloedema) | Anterior fossa floor |
| **Sphenoid wing (outer)** | Temporal lobe epilepsy, hyperostosis → exophthalmos | Lateral sphenoid |
| **Sphenoid wing (inner/cavernous)** | CN III, IV, V1, V2, VI palsies | Cavernous sinus |
| **Tuberculum sellae** | Visual loss, Foster-Kennedy | Suprasellar |
| **Posterior fossa (CPA)** | Hearing loss, vertigo, V, VII | Cerebellopontine angle |
| **Foramen magnum** | Suboccipital pain, lower cranial nerves, myelopathy | Craniocervical |
| **Intraventricular** | Headache, hydrocephalus | Lateral ventricle (atrium) |

## 4. Diagnostic Approach
**Algorithm:** Asymptomatic (incidental) vs symptomatic → MRI brain with gadolinium → identify dural-based, extra-axial, homogeneous enhancement → consider MDT (neurosurg, oncology, radiology) → observe (small, asymptomatic, elderly) vs surgery (symptomatic, growing, young) → histology → surveillance.

**Scoring:** Simpson grade (I-V) for extent of resection; recurrence correlates with grade. KPS for prognosis.

**Simpson Grading:**
- **I:** Complete removal including dural attachment and abnormal bone
- **II:** Complete + coagulation of dural attachment
- **III:** Complete without dural coagulation
- **IV:** Subtotal resection
- **V:** Biopsy only

---

## 5. Investigations
**First-line:** MRI brain with gadolinium (T1, T2, FLAIR, DWI, post-contrast); CT head (emergency, hyperostosis, calcification); CT/MRI spine if NF2 suspected.

**MRI Findings:**
- T1 iso- to hypointense; T2 iso- to hyperintense
- **Homogeneous, intense contrast enhancement** (98%)
- **Dural tail** (60-70%) — not specific
- **CSF cleft sign** (extra-axial)
- **Pial vessels** between tumour and brain
- **Hyperostosis** of adjacent bone (sphenoid wing)
- **Calcification** (25%)
- DWI: variable; low ADC = more aggressive

**Angiography (DSA):** Sunburst pattern; prolonged venous phase; pre-surgical embolisation for vascular tumours.

**Histopathology:**
- **Meningothelial:** Lobules, whorls, psammoma bodies (calcified), syncytial cells with intranuclear inclusions
- **Fibrous:** Spindle cells, collagen
- **Transitional:** Mixed whorls + fascicles
- **Psammomatous:** Psammoma bodies
- **Atypical (G2):** ≥4 mitoses/10 HPF OR brain invasion OR 3+ atypia (high cellularity, prominent nucleoli, sheet-like growth, spontaneous necrosis)
- **Anaplastic (G3):** ≥20 mitoses/10 HPF OR anaplasia (sarcoma/carcinoma/ melanoma-like); TERT mutation; CDKN2A/B homozygous deletion

**IHC:** EMA+, vimentin+, SSTR2A+, PR+ (loss = aggressive); Ki-67 correlates with grade.

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Differential | Distinguishing Features | Key Test |
|--------------|------------------------|----------|
| **Solitary fibrous tumour/haemangiopericytoma** (CNS5 = SFT) | More vascular, dural-based, NAB2-STAT6 fusion, younger | MRI, STAT6 IHC |
| **Dural metastasis** | Known primary, multiple, heterogeneous | CT CAP, PET |
| **Schwannoma** | Vestibular CN VIII, heterogeneous, Antoni A/B, intracanalicular | MRI IAM |
| **Pituitary macroadenoma** | Sellar origin, hormonal, snowman | MRI, hormones |
| **Craniopharyngioma** | Cystic, calcified, DI | MRI |
| **Lymphoma** | Homogeneous, periventricular, EBV+ | CSF, biopsy |
| **Sarcoidosis** | Dural/leptomeningeal, systemic | ACE, biopsy |
| **IgG4 disease** | Pachymeningitis, hypertrophic | Serum IgG4, biopsy |

## 7. Management
**Emergency/Acute**
| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Mass effect (oedema, herniation) | IV dexamethasone 4 mg q6h |
| Seizure | Levetiracetam 1-1.5 g IV load; avoid phenytoin (CYP inducer) |
| Hydrocephalus (IV ventricular) | External ventricular drain (EVD) |

**Management Decision:**
| Scenario | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| **Incidental, small (<3 cm), asymptomatic, elderly** | Observe; MRI 6-12 monthly |
| **Symptomatic, growing, young** | **Surgery** (Simpson I-II) |
| **Grade 1, gross total** | Observe |
| **Grade 1, subtotal** | RT/SRS to residual |
| **Grade 2, gross total** | ± adjuvant RT (controversial) |
| **Grade 2, subtotal** | Adjuvant RT |
| **Grade 3** | Maximal safe resection + adjuvant RT |
| **Recurrent** | Re-resection ± RT; SRS for small |
| **NF2, multiple, unresectable** | Conservative; Sunitinib (SMO inhibitor); bevacizumab |

**Surgical Approach:** Craniotomy for convexity/parasagittal; skull base approaches (pterional, subtemporal, retrosigmoid, transsphenoidal) for skull base. **5-ALA** limited utility (extrinsic). Intraoperative monitoring (Eloquent areas).

**Radiotherapy:**
- **Stereotactic radiosurgery (SRS / Gamma Knife):** For small (<3 cm) lesions, residual, recurrent, skull base; 12-13 Gy marginal
- **Fractionated RT (IMRT/VMAT):** For larger or grade 2-3; 50.4-54 Gy
- **Proton therapy:** Selected cases (skull base, paediatric)

**Medical Therapy (Limited):**
- **Sunitinib** (VEGFR/PDGFR): NF2, recurrent (some response)
- **Bevacizumab:** NF2, refractory (oedema)
- Hydroxyurea, somatostatin analogues, interferon: limited efficacy
- Hormonal therapy: under investigation (PR+)

**Symptomatic:**
- Seizure: levetiracetam (preferred; not enzyme inducer)
- Oedema: dexamethasone 4 mg q6h, taper
- Headache: paracetamol, codeine

## 8. Drug Interactions / Cautions
| Drug | Caution |
|------|---------|
| Phenytoin/carbamazepine | CYP inducers — avoid (perimenopausal drug interactions) |
| Dexamethasone | Hyperglycaemia, myopathy, infection |
| Sunitinib | Hypertension, hypothyroidism, hand-foot, hepatotoxicity |
| Bevacizumab | Hypertension, proteinuria, thrombosis |
| **Oestrogen, OCP, HRT** | Theoretical ↑growth (PR+); individualised |

---

## 9. Procedures
- **Craniotomy + Simpson I-II resection:** Definitive for accessible tumours
- **Endoscopic endonasal:** Tuberculum sellae, olfactory groove
- **Stereotactic biopsy:** Deep, skull base where surgery risky
- **Pre-operative embolisation:** Highly vascular tumours (SDA feeders)
- **SRS (Gamma Knife, CyberKnife, Linac):** Outpatient; small volume
- **Intraoperative MRI / neuronavigation:** Maximise resection

## 11. Red Flags / Emergencies
| Red Flag | Action | Window |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Sudden severe headache + ↓GCS | Tumour haemorrhage → CT + reversal + surgery | Hours |
| New onset seizure (with mass) | Levetiracetam; investigate | Days |
| Rapid progression in young patient | NF2 workup; aggressive histology; biopsy | Days |
| Herniation signs | IV mannitol/3% NaCl + Dex + surgery | Minutes |
| Spinal cord compression (spinal meningioma) | High-dose steroids + surgery | Hours |

## 12. Prognosis
| Factor | Good | Poor |
|--------|------|------|
| **Grade** | 1 | 2-3 |
| **Resection** | Simpson I-II | III-V |
| **Location** | Convexity | Skull base, cavernous |
| **Size** | <3 cm | >5 cm |
| **Age** | <65 y | >75 y |
| **KPS** | ≥80 | ≤60 |
| **Molecular** | NF2, KLF4/TRAF7 | TERT, CDKN2A/B |

- **10-year PFS:** G1 + GTR: 75-90%; G1 + STR: 50-60%; G2: 30-50%; G3: 10-30%
- **Meningiomatosis:** NF2; multiple; poor for cure

---

## 13. Topic Correlation
- **NF2:** Bilateral vestibular schwannomas + multiple meningiomas + ependymomas + cataracts
- **Schwannoma:** Different dural attachment, more heterogeneous
- **SFT/haemangiopericytoma:** STAT6, NAB2-STAT6 fusion, hypervascular
- **Dural metastasis:** Breast, prostate, lymphoma
- **IgG4 pachymeningitis:** Mimicker; responds to steroids

---

## 14. Special Situations
| Situation | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Pregnancy** | May grow (oestrogen); MRI without gadolinium; consider early delivery; progesterone receptor modulators (mifepristone) experimental |
| **Elderly** | Observe if asymptomatic; SRS if growing |
| **NF2** | Multiple meningiomas + schwannomas; bevacizumab; sunitinib; hearing rehabilitation |
| **Spinal meningioma** | 25% of spinal tumours; thoracic most common; F:M = 4-8:1 |
| **Optic nerve sheath** | NF2, painful visual loss |
| **Perioperative** | DVT prophylaxis (LMWH 24-48h post-op); stress-dose steroids; AED |
| **Driving (DVLA)** | **1 year seizure-free** off meds if seizure history; visual field for suprasellar; Group 2 strict |

## Viva Questions
1. **Most common primary intracranial tumour:** **Meningioma** (~36%).
2. **Dural tail significance:** Suggestive of meningioma but **not specific** (also dural metastasis, lymphoma, sarcoid).
3. **WHO CNS5 grading of meningioma:** 1 (80%) typical, 2 (15-20%) atypical, 3 (1-3%) anaplastic. **Atypical** = ≥4 mitoses/10 HPF OR brain invasion OR 3+ atypia.
4. **NF2 associations:** Bilateral vestibular schwannomas, **multiple meningiomas**, ependymomas, cataracts. **Merlin** (22q12) loss.
5. **Simpson grading:** I-V based on extent of dural/bone resection; recurrence correlates inversely.
6. **Spinal meningioma:** Thoracic most common; F:M = 4-8:1; excellent prognosis with surgery.
7. **Sunitinib in meningioma:** For NF2-associated, recurrent; partial responses.
8. **Hyperostosis meaning:** Reactive bone thickening from invasion; important for surgical planning (drill out).
9. **Atypical meningioma marker for grade 3:** **TERT promoter mutation, CDKN2A/B homozygous deletion**.
10. **Differential dural-based:** Dural metastasis (breast, prostate), SFT/haemangiopericytoma, schwannoma, lymphoma, sarcoid, IgG4 disease.
11. **Olfactory groove meningioma signs:** Anosmia, **Foster-Kennedy** (ipsilateral optic atrophy + contralateral papilloedema).
12. **Cavernous sinus meningioma:** Often unresectable; **SRS** for residual; CN III, IV, V1, V2, VI palsies.

## Common Confusions
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Dural tail = meningioma | Not specific — also metastasis, lymphoma |
| Atypical vs anaplastic | G2 vs G3; G3 ≥20 mitoses/10 HPF |
| Recurrence rate by grade | G1 5-25%, G2 40-50%, G3 50-90% |
| Radiation-induced | Ionising radiation is established risk factor; mobile phones NOT |
| Pregnancy growth | Yes — PR+, slow growth; usually observed |
| Spinal meningioma vs schwannoma | Meningioma is dural-based, thoracic, F; Schwannoma is intradural, often cervical, dumbbell |
| NF2 = bilateral VS | Yes, plus multiple meningiomas |

## Mnemonics
1. **CNS5 = 1-2-3**: 1 = most (typical), 2 = atypical, 3 = anaplastic
2. **DURAL** — **D**ural tail, **U**niform enhancement, **R**elate to NF2, **A**ttach to dura, **L**obulated
3. **MENINGIOMA locations** — **O**lfactory, **P**arasagittal, **C**onvexity, **S**phenoid, **T**uberculum, **P**osterior fossa
4. **Simpson I** = **cure** (gross total + dural attachment + bone)
5. **NF2 = MISME** — **M**ultiple **I**nherited **S**chwannomas, **M**eningiomas, **E**pendymomas

## Summary
**Meningioma** = most common primary brain tumour (36%); arachnoid cap cells. **WHO CNS5 grades 1-3** (typical/atypical/anaplastic). **Dural-based, homogeneous enhancement, dural tail, hyperostosis**. **Simpson I-II surgery** for symptomatic/growing; **SRS** for small/skull base; **RT** for G2-3. NF2 = multiple meningiomas + schwannomas. Observe asymptomatic small lesions, especially in elderly.

   A. Observe  B. **Adjuvant RT 60 Gy**  C. Chemo  D. Sunitinib

   A. Surgery immediately  B. **Observe, MRI without gadolinium, plan delivery**  C. RT  D. Chemo
