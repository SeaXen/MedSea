# Glioblastoma (IDH-wildtype, WHO CNS5 Grade 4)

> [!tip] **Definition (WHO CNS5 2021)** — Most aggressive primary brain tumour (Grade 4); **IDH-wildtype** diffuse astrocytoma with **microvascular proliferation and/or necrosis**. IDH-mut = "Astrocytoma IDH-mut G4" (NOT GBM). Pearl: **ring enhancement + necrosis + oedema**; "butterfly" if crosses corpus callosum.

## 1. Definition / Epidemiology / Classification
**Definition:** WHO CNS5 (2021) Grade 4 diffuse astrocytic glioma, **IDH-wildtype**, with microvascular proliferation and/or necrosis.

**Epidemiology**
- Incidence: **3-4/100,000/year** (~50% of all primary malignant brain tumours)
- Age: median **64y**; bimodal (smaller peak in children — DIPG)
- Sex: **M:F = 1.6:1**
- Risk factors: **ionising radiation** (only established), NF1, Lynch, Li-Fraumeni (TP53), Turcot; mobile phones NOT proven

**Classification (WHO CNS5 2021)**
| Type | Key Features | Prognosis |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| **Glioblastoma, IDH-wildtype, G4** | IDH-wt + TERT/EGFR/+7−10; most common | OS 12-15 mo; MGMT-methylated 18-24 mo |
| Astrocytoma, IDH-mutant, G4 | IDH-mut; previous "GBM IDH-mut" | OS 24-31 mo |
| Gliomatosis cerebri (legacy) | ≥3 lobes | Poor (months) |

---

## 2. Aetiology / Pathophysiology
**Pathogenesis:** IDH-wildtype stem cell (SVZ) → EGFR amplification (~40%), TERT promoter mutation (~70-80%), CDKN2A/B loss, +7/−10, PTEN loss → anaplastic transformation → microvascular proliferation (VEGF) + pseudopalisading necrosis.

**Molecular/Genetic:** IDH-wildtype (mandatory); **TERT** promoter mut ~70-80%; **EGFR** amplification/EGFRvIII ~40%; **+7/−10**; **MGMT** promoter methylation (predicts TMZ); **CDKN2A/B** (grade-defining in IDH-mut).

---

## 3. Clinical Features
**History:** Subacute (weeks-months). Morning headache (worse valsalva/recumbency), seizures (30-50%), focal deficit (40-60%), cognitive/personality change (40%), raised ICP.

**Examination:** Higher cortical (dysphasia, neglect — frontal/temp/parietal); cranial nerves (homonymous hemianopia — optic radiation); motor (contralateral hemiparesis UMN — corticospinal tract); cerebellar (ataxia — compression); cognitive (abulia — frontal).

**Syndromes:** Butterfly (corpus callosum); frontal lobe; Gerstmann (dominant parietal); Foster-Kennedy (olfactory groove).

---

## 4. Diagnostic Approach
**Algorithm:** Subacute neuro deficit → urgent MRI brain with contrast → ring-enhancing + necrosis → exclude metastasis (CT CAP ± PET) → MDT → maximal safe resection → histology + molecular (IDH, MGMT, EGFR, TERT, 1p/19q, CDKN2A) → Stupp.

**Scoring:** KPS 0-100 (≥70 for Stupp); RPA (RTOG); MGMT methylation; IDH status; RANO criteria for response.

---

## 5. Investigations
**First-line:** MRI brain with gadolinium (T1, T2, FLAIR, DWI, MRS, perfusion); CT head (emergency); CT CAP or PET (exclude metastasis); MRI spine if drop mets suspected.

**MRI:** T1+Gd = **ring/rim enhancement + central necrosis**; T2/FLAIR = extensive vasogenic oedema; DWI = restricted (high cellularity; less than lymphoma/abscess); MRS = ↑choline, ↓NAA, **lipid-lactate peak** (necrosis); perfusion = **↑rCBV** (helps vs radionecrosis).

**Biopsy/Histopathology:** Pleomorphic astrocytic cells, brisk mitoses, **pseudopalisading necrosis**, **microvascular proliferation** (glomeruloid; grade-defining). IHC: GFAP+, S100+, Ki-67 high, **IDH1 R132H negative**. Molecular: IDH1/2 wildtype, TERT mutation, EGFR amplification, +7/−10, MGMT.

---

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Differential | Distinguishing Features | Key Test |
|--------------|------------------------|----------|
| **Brain metastasis** | Known primary, multiple, grey-white junction | CT CAP, PET |
| **Cerebral abscess** | Fever, ↑WCC, **marked central DWI restriction** | DWI, blood cultures |
| **Primary CNS lymphoma** | Periventricular, **homogeneous** enhancement, EBV+ if HIV | CSF cytology, biopsy (AVOID steroids pre-biopsy) |
| **Tumefactive demyelination** | Young, **incomplete ring** open to ventricle | OCB, MRZ reaction |
| **Subacute infarct** | Vascular territory, gyriform | DWI/ADC |
| **Radionecrosis** | Prior RT, "soap bubble", **low rCBV** | Perfusion, PET |

**Mnemonic — MAGIC DR**: **M**etastasis, **A**bscess, **G**lioblastoma, **I**nfarct, **C**ontusion, **D**emyelination, **R**adiation necrosis/Resolving haematoma.

---

## 7. Management
**Emergency/Acute**
| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Raised ICP/herniation | IV mannitol 1g/kg OR 3% NaCl 150ml; head-up; **NEVER LP** |
| Seizure | Levetiracetam 1-1.5g IV load |
| Steroid | Dexamethasone 4mg q6h for oedema (not prophylactic; avoid pre-biopsy if lymphoma possible) |
| DVT prophylaxis | LMWH 24-48h post-op |

**Stupp Protocol (Standard of Care)**
| Phase | Regimen |
|-------|---------|
| Surgery | Maximal safe resection (5-ALA, awake, iMRI, DTI) |
| Concurrent | **RT 60 Gy/30 fractions** over 6 wk + **Temozolomide 75 mg/m² daily** PO |
| Adjuvant | **Temozolomide 150-200 mg/m² D1-5 q28d × 6 cycles** |
| Maintenance | TTFields (Optune) — modest OS benefit (EF-14) |

**Recurrent GBM:** Re-resection (good KPS, >6mo); re-irradiation (stereotactic 35 Gy/10); **lomustine + bevacizumab** (BELOB); clinical trials; palliative RT; best supportive care.

**Elderly (>70y, Perry 2017):** Hypofractionated RT **40 Gy/15 ± TMZ**; better tolerated than Stupp.

**Symptomatic:** Levetiracetam (avoid phenytoin/carbamazepine — enzyme inducers ↓TMZ); dexamethasone (lowest effective); LMWH (VTE rate 20-30%).

---

## 8. Drug Interactions / Cautions
| Drug | Caution |
|------|---------|
| Temozolomide | Myelosuppression (nadir D21-28); **PJP risk** with concurrent RT |
| Steroids | Hyperglycaemia, myopathy, osteoporosis, infection |
| AEDs | **Avoid enzyme inducers** (phenytoin, carbamazepine, phenobarb — ↓TMZ/Dex); use **levetiracetam/lamotrigine/lacosamide** |
| Bevacizumab | Hypertension, proteinuria, GI perforation, wound dehiscence, PRES |
| Co-trimoxazole | Skin reactions, hyperkalaemia, AKI |

---

## 9. Procedures
- **Image-guided biopsy:** deep/eloquent/multifocal; haemorrhage 1-3%
- **Awake craniotomy:** eloquent cortex mapping; max safe resection
- **5-ALA fluorescence:** highlights malignant tissue (purple under blue light)
- **LP:** NEVER with raised ICP; only if meningitis/encephalitis/lymphoma AFTER CT

---

## 10. Complications
| Complication | Frequency | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| VTE/PE | 20-30% | LMWH; IVC filter if contraindicated |
| Seizures | 30-50% | Levetiracetam |
| Steroid myopathy | Common (prolonged) | Taper, physio |
| TMZ myelosuppression | Neutropenia, thrombocytopenia | Weekly FBC; G-CSF |
| PJP | Risk >2% | Co-trimoxazole prophylaxis |
| Radionecrosis | 5-10% (later) | Bevacizumab, hyperbaric O2 |
| Pseudoprogression | 20-30% (within 3 mo) | Continue TMZ, repeat MRI |

---

## 11. Red Flags / Emergencies
| Red Flag | Action | Window |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Pupil asymmetry + ↓GCS | Mannitol/3%NaCl + urgent CT + neurosurgery | Minutes |
| Status epilepticus | Status pathway (Lorazepam → Levetiracetam) | Minutes |
| Tumour haemorrhage | Reverse anticoagulation, surgical evacuation | Hours |
| Massive PE | Thrombolysis/catheter | Hours |

---

## 12. Prognosis
| Factor | Good | Poor |
|--------|------|------|
| Age | <50y | >70y |
| KPS | ≥80 | ≤60 |
| MGMT | **Methylated** | Unmethylated |
| Resection | Gross total | Biopsy only |
| IDH | Mutant (= astrocytoma) | Wildtype |

- Median OS: **12-15 months** (Stupp); 18-24 mo if MGMT methylated
- 5-year survival: 5-7% (IDH-wt); 15-20% (IDH-mut)
- Long-term survivors (>10y): <1%

---

## 13. Topic Correlation
- **Astrocytoma**: IDH-mut counterpart; better prognosis
- **Oligodendroglioma**: 1p/19q codeleted; chemosensitive (PCV)
- **Metastases**: most common adult brain tumour; multiple
- **Abscess**: ring-enhancing differential; marked DWI restriction
- **CNS lymphoma**: periventricular, homogeneous; steroid-sensitive

---

## 14. Special Situations
| Situation | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Pregnancy** | MRI without gadolinium; surgery safe 2nd/3rd trimester; defer RT/TMZ postpartum; levetiracetam safe; folic acid 5mg |
| **Elderly (>70y)** | Hypofractionated RT (40 Gy/15) ± TMZ (Perry); Stupp only if very fit |
| **Poor KPS (≤60)** | Best supportive care; consider short-course RT |
| **HIV** | Exclude CNS lymphoma (EBV+); toxoplasmosis differential |
| **Driving (DVLA)** | **1-year seizure-free** off meds; Group 2: 10 years off meds |

## Viva Questions
1. **Define glioblastoma per WHO CNS5 2021:** Grade 4 diffuse astrocytic glioma, **IDH-wildtype**, with microvascular proliferation and/or necrosis.
2. **Stupp protocol:** Maximal safe resection → **RT 60 Gy/30** + **TMZ 75 mg/m² daily** (6 wk) → adjuvant **TMZ 150-200 mg/m² D1-5 q28d × 6**.
3. **MGMT methylation significance:** Silences DNA repair → chemosensitive to TMZ; OS 18-24 mo vs 12-15 mo.
4. **Why avoid enzyme-inducing AEDs:** Phenytoin/carbamazepine induce CYP → ↓TMZ/Dex → worse outcomes.
5. **Butterfly glioma:** GBM crossing **corpus callosum**.
6. **GBM vs abscess on MRI:** Abscess: **marked central DWI restriction**; GBM: heterogeneous with solid rim restriction.
7. **TTFields role:** Alternating electric fields disrupt mitosis; EF-14 = 5-mo OS benefit.
8. **Recurrent GBM management:** Re-resection, re-irradiation, lomustine + bevacizumab, trials, BSC.
9. **Elderly regimen:** **40 Gy/15 ± TMZ** (Perry 2017) — better tolerated than Stupp.
10. **Ring-enhancing differential:** **MAGIC DR** — Metastasis, Abscess, GBM, Infarct, Contusion, Demyelination, Radiation necrosis.

---

## Common Confusions
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| GBM IDH-mut vs IDH-wt | IDH-mut = Astrocytoma G4 (NOT GBM); only IDH-wt = GBM |
| "Primary" vs "secondary" GBM | Legacy; primary = IDH-wt, secondary = IDH-mut; abandoned |
| Oligodendroglioma with necrosis | Still oligodendroglioma G3 (if 1p/19q codeleted) — NOT GBM |
| MGMT ≠ IDH | MGMT = predicts TMZ; IDH = defines tumour type |
| Pseudoprogression | Early post-RT ↑enhancement; RANO; continue TMZ |
| Steroids mask CNS lymphoma | AVOID steroids before biopsy if lymphoma possible |

---

## Mnemonics
1. **Stupp = SR + RT + TMZ ×2** — **S**urgery → **R**adio**T**herapy 60/30 + **TMZ** → adjuvant **TMZ** ×6
2. **TEG-P** — **T**ERT, **E**GFR, **G**ain +7/**P**TEN loss (GBM genetics)
3. **MAGIC DR** — ring-enhancing differential
4. **MGMT Methylated = More Time** — better TMZ response

---

## Summary
Glioblastoma (CNS5) = Grade 4 astrocytoma, IDH-wildtype; subacute neuro decline + ring-enhancing lesion. **Stupp protocol** (resection + RT 60 Gy + concurrent/adjuvant temozolomide) is standard; **MGMT methylation** predicts chemotherapy response. Median OS 12-15 months.

## MCQs (10)

1. **64y man, 6-week progressive headache, right hemiparesis, focal seizure. MRI: left frontal ring-enhancing lesion with central necrosis. IDH1 R132H negative. Diagnosis?**
   A. Anaplastic oligodendroglioma  B. Glioblastoma  C. Brain abscess  D. Primary CNS lymphoma
   **Answer: B.** Ring enhancement, age >60, IDH-wildtype = **GBM (CNS5)**. Lymphoma is periventricular homogeneous. Abscess: marked DWI restriction. Oligodendroglioma: 1p/19q codeleted (IDH-mut).

2. **Stupp protocol radiation dose?**
   A. 30 Gy/10  B. 40 Gy/15  C. **60 Gy/30**  D. 70 Gy/35
   **Answer: C.** Stupp = **60 Gy/30** over 6 weeks with concurrent TMZ 75 mg/m². 40 Gy/15 = elderly (Perry). 70 Gy = toxic, no benefit.

3. **MGMT promoter methylation predicts?**
   A. Better surgical outcome  B. **Temozolomide response**  C. Seizure risk  D. Recurrence
   **Answer: B.** Methylated MGMT → silenced repair enzyme → chemosensitive → OS 18-24 mo vs 12-15 mo.

4. **AED to AVOID with temozolomide?**
   A. Levetiracetam  B. Lamotrigine  C. **Phenytoin**  D. Lacosamide
   **Answer: C.** Phenytoin (and carbamazepine, phenobarb) **induce CYP3A4/UGT** → ↓TMZ and dexamethasone.

5. **Butterfly glioma = tumour crossing?**
   A. Fornix  B. **Corpus callosum**  C. Anterior commissure  D. Optic chiasm
   **Answer: B.**

6. **CNS5 2021 requirement for GBM diagnosis?**
   A. IDH-mut  B. **IDH-wildtype**  C. EGFR amplification  D. TERT mutation
   **Answer: B.** CNS5 mandates IDH-wildtype for "glioblastoma"; IDH-mut = Astrocytoma G4 (different entity, better prognosis).

7. **Concurrent RT+TMZ, week 4, dry cough + hypoxia. Likely organism?**
   A. CMV  B. **Pneumocystis jirovecii**  C. Aspergillus  D. Cryptococcus
   **Answer: B.** Lymphopenia → **PJP** risk >2%; prophylaxis with co-trimoxazole TDS weekly during RT.

8. **Elderly >70y, KPS 80. Best regimen (Perry 2017)?**
   A. Stupp 60/30+TMZ  B. **40 Gy/15 ± TMZ**  C. TMZ alone  D. BSC
   **Answer: B.**

9. **Temozolomide mechanism?**
   A. Topo II inhibition  B. **DNA alkylation at O6-guanine**  C. Microtubule stabilisation  D. Thymidylate synthase inhibition
   **Answer: B.** Imidazotetrazine prodrug → methyl diazonium → methylates O6-guanine; MGMT removes this — if methylated, more damage.

10. **Defines oligodendroglioma (not GBM)?**
    A. TERT mutation  B. **1p/19q codeletion**  C. EGFR amplification  D. CDKN2A/B deletion
    **Answer: B.** Oligodendroglioma = IDH-mut + **1p/19q whole-arm codeletion**.

## SBA Questions (10)

1. **58y, 3-month progressive left hemiparesis, new seizures. MRI: right frontal ring-enhancing lesion with necrosis. Stupp protocol started. 6 weeks post-chemoradiotherapy, MRI shows ↑enhancement. Asymptomatic. Most likely?**
   A. True progression  B. **Pseudoprogression**  C. Radiation necrosis  D. Metastasis
   **Answer: B.** **Pseudoprogression** in 20-30% within 3 mo of RT/TMZ; BBB breakdown; RANO; continue TMZ; repeat MRI 4-8 wk. Radionecrosis typically 6-12 mo, symptomatic, "soap-bubble", low rCBV.

2. **67y, IDH-wt GBM, KPS 90, gross total resection, MGMT methylated. Best adjuvant?**
   A. Observation  B. **Stupp protocol**  C. Bevacizumab alone  D. Lomustine
   **Answer: B.** Standard Stupp post-resection in fit adults; MGMT methylated = best responders to TMZ.

3. **45y, seizures. MRI: heterogeneous T2 hyperintense lesion crossing corpus callosum, patchy enhancement. Biopsy: microvascular proliferation + necrosis. IDH1 R132H positive. MGMT unmethylated. Diagnosis and prognosis?**
   A. GBM IDH-wt, OS 12-15 mo  B. **Astrocytoma IDH-mut G4, OS 24-31 mo**  C. Oligodendroglioma G3, OS >10y  D. Gliosarcoma
   **Answer: B.** **CNS5**: IDH-mut tumour with necrosis = **Astrocytoma IDH-mut G4** (NOT GBM). IDH mutation = better prognosis.

4. **Adjuvant TMZ, day 21, fever + petechiae. FBC: neutrophils 0.3, platelets 35. Management?**
   A. Continue TMZ  B. **Hold TMZ, G-CSF, transfuse, dose-reduce**  C. Switch lomustine  D. Add prophylactic antibiotics only
   **Answer: B.** **TMZ myelosuppression** (nadir D21-28). Hold until recovery; G-CSF; transfuse platelets; **dose-reduce 50 mg/m²** next cycle.

5. **Right frontal GBM, worsening headache, vomiting, fixed dilated left pupil, CT 6 mm midline shift. Immediate management?**
   A. Oral dexamethasone  B. **IV mannitol 1g/kg + IV Dex 8mg + urgent neurosurgery**  C. LP  D. Aspirin
   **Answer: B.** Impending **uncal herniation** — **NEVER LP** (tonsillar herniation). Mannitol + Dex + urgent decompression. Aspirin contraindicated (haemorrhage).

6. **72y, new GBM, KPS 60, multiple comorbidities. Best management?**
   A. Stupp  B. 40 Gy/15  C. **Best supportive care**  D. TMZ alone
   **Answer: C.** KPS ≤60 + comorbidities → Stupp not tolerated. KPS 60-70: hypofractionated RT possible. KPS 60 + comorbidities → **BSC** (dex, analgesia, palliative).

7. **50y, IDH-wt GBM, KPS 80, 8 mo post-Stupp, enlarging right temporal enhancement, worsening aphasia. Perfusion ↑rCBV. Diagnosis?**
   A. Pseudoprogression  B. **True recurrence**  C. Radiation necrosis  D. Tumefactive demyelination
   **Answer: B.** >6 mo + **↑rCBV** + clinical progression = **true recurrence**. Radionecrosis has **↓rCBV**. Pseudoprogression usually <3 mo.

8. **Recurrent GBM on bevacizumab. Specific monitoring?**
   A. LFT weekly  B. **BP + urine protein + wound healing**  C. Audiometry  D. PFTs
   **Answer: B.** Bevacizumab toxicities: **hypertension**, **proteinuria**, **impaired wound healing** (avoid <28d pre/post-op), GI perforation, haemorrhage, PRES. BP/UPCR baseline + q2-3 wk.

9. **Day 14 RT, WBC 1.5, neutrophils 0.8. Prophylaxis?**
   A. Acyclovir  B. **Co-trimoxazole**  C. Itraconazole  D. Azithromycin
   **Answer: B.** Concurrent RT+TMZ lymphopenia → **PJP prophylaxis** with co-trimoxazole 960 mg TDS weekly during RT.

10. **35y woman, left temporal GBM 18 mo ago, stable, no chemo, on levetiracetam, asks about pregnancy. Advice?**
    A. Pregnancy safe  B. Pregnancy contraindicated  C. **Avoid pregnancy 2 yr; folic acid 5mg; MDT**  D. Stop levetiracetam
    **Answer: C.** Highest recurrence risk first 2 yr; avoid pregnancy. Levetiracetam is safe in pregnancy; **folic acid 5 mg** (higher than 400 mcg). MDT essential.
