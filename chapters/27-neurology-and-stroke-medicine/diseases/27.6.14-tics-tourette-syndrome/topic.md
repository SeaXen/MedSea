# Tics & Tourette Syndrome

Related: [[Movement Disorders Hub]], [[Hyperkinetic Movement Disorders Hub]], [[ADHD]], [[OCD]], [[Chorea]], [[Dystonia]]

> [!tip] **Tics = sudden, rapid, recurrent, non-rhythmic movements (motor) or vocalisations (vocal/phonic)** with **suppressibility** and **premonitory urge**. **Tourette syndrome (TS) = multiple motor + ≥1 vocal tic for >1 year, onset <18 years**.

## 1. Definition / Epidemiology / Classification

**Definition (Tic):** Sudden, rapid, recurrent, non-rhythmic, stereotyped movement (motor tic) or vocalisation (vocal/phonic tic). Tics are **suppressible** (at least briefly) and usually preceded by a **premonitory urge** (sensory phenomenon).

**Tourette syndrome (DSM-5):**
- Both **multiple motor tics AND ≥1 vocal/phonic tic** (not necessarily concurrent)
- Tics may wax and wane in frequency but persist for **>1 year**
- Onset **before age 18**
- Not attributable to substance or other medical condition

**Epidemiology:**
- **Tics:** 4-20% of children transiently; chronic tics 1-5%
- **Tourette syndrome:** 0.5-1% of children
- **Sex ratio:** 3-4:1 male predominance
- **Age of onset:** Mean 5-7 years; peak severity 10-12 years; 50% improve by adulthood

**Classification:**

| Type | Features | Examples |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Motor — Simple** | Brief, single muscle group | Eye blink, head jerk, shoulder shrug, facial grimace |
| **Motor — Complex** | Coordinated, purposeful | Touching, jumping, copropraxia, echopraxia, self-injurious |
| **Vocal/Phonic — Simple** | Single sound | Throat clearing, sniffing, grunting, barking |
| **Vocal/Phonic — Complex** | Words/phrases | Coprolalia, echolalia, palilalia |
| **Provisional tic disorder** | <1 year | Transient |
| **Persistent (chronic) motor/vocal tic** | >1 year, one type | Chronic motor tic |
| **Tourette syndrome** | Multiple motor + vocal >1 year | TS |

**Special features:**
- **Coprolalia:** Complex vocal tic with obscene words (occurs in 10-15% of TS, NOT required)
- **Echopraxia, echolalia:** Imitation
- **Palilalia:** Repetition of one's own words
- **Self-injurious behaviour:** Common

## 2. Aetiology / Pathophysiology

**Mechanism:**
- **Cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) circuit** dysfunction
- Hyperdopaminergic state in striatum
- Reduced GABAergic inhibition
- Abnormal sensorimotor gating
- Premonitory urge arises from abnormal sensory processing

**Genetics:**
- **Polygenic inheritance**
- **SLITRK1, HDC, CNTNAP2** implicated
- Family history of tics in 50%; ADHD/OCD in relatives

**Risk factors:**
- **Male sex**
- **Perinatal adversity** (hypoxia, prematurity)
- **Maternal smoking, stress**
- **PANDAS** (controversial) — anti-basal ganglia antibodies post-strep

**Comorbidities:** ADHD (50-60%), OCD (30-50%), anxiety, depression, learning disability, sleep disturbance

## 3. Clinical Features

**Cardinal features:**
- **Suppressibility** (briefly, with effort)
- **Premonitory urge:** Sensory phenomenon (itch, tension) preceding tic, relieved by tic
- **Suggestibility, distractibility**
- **Wax and wane** in frequency and severity
- **Worsened by:** Stress, fatigue, anxiety, excitement, stimulants
- **Improved by:** Focused activity, alcohol, sleep (some)
- **Tic-free periods:** Common (e.g., when at doctor)

**Examination:**
- Observe at rest, talking, walking, writing
- Watch for suppression
- Assess for comorbidities (ADHD, OCD)
- Exclude other movement disorders

**Clinical course:**
- Onset 5-7 years (motor first, then vocal 1-2 years later)
- Peak severity 10-12 years
- 50% have significant improvement by age 18-20
- Adult TS may persist but less severe

## 4. Diagnostic Approach

**Clinical (DSM-5):**
- Multiple motor + ≥1 vocal tic
- >1 year (may wax and wane)
- Onset <18 years
- Not due to substance/other condition

**Differential diagnosis:** Essential for diagnosis (chorea, myoclonus, dystonia, stereotypies, functional tics)

**Workup:** Usually clinical; investigations to exclude other causes (e.g., Wilson's, Huntington's in atypical)

## 5. Investigations

| Test | Purpose | Findings |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Clinical diagnosis** | Most cases | Waxing/waning, suppressible, premonitory urge |
| **YGTSS (Yale Global Tic Severity Scale)** | Severity monitoring | Score 0-50 (motor 0-25, vocal 0-25) |
| **MRI brain** | Atypical features | Excludes structural; often normal |
| **Copper studies, ceruloplasmin** | Exclude Wilson's | Normal |
| **ASO, anti-DNase B** | PANDAS (controversial) | Elevated post-strep |
| **EEG** | If seizure suspected | Normal |
| **Cognitive testing (WISC, etc.)** | School problems | Profile of comorbidities |
| **ADHD/OCD screening** | Comorbidities | Conners, CY-BOCS |

**Diagnosis is clinical; investigations to exclude differentials.**

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Chorea** | Continuous, non-suppressible, no urge |
| **Myoclonus** | Brief, shock-like, non-suppressible |
| **Dystonia** | Patterned, sustained, sensory trick |
| **Stereotypy** | Fixed, repetitive, no urge, autism/LD |
| **Habit disorder** | Voluntary, no urge |
| **Functional tics** | Variable, adult onset, social media-driven; inconsistent |
| **Compulsions (OCD)** | Complex, ego-dystonic, ritualised |
| **Akathisia** | Inner restlessness, motor restlessness (from antipsychotics) |
| **Hemifacial spasm** | Unilateral, rhythmic, eye + mouth |
| **Myoclonus** | Non-suppressible |

## 7. Management

**1. Education and reassurance (first step):**
- Most tics improve with age
- Education for child, parents, school
- Avoid triggers (stress, fatigue, screen time)

**2. Behavioural therapy (first-line):**
- **Comprehensive Behavioural Intervention for Tics (CBIT)**
- **Habit Reversal Training (HRT):** Awareness + competing response
- **Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP)**

**3. Pharmacological (if disabling):**

| Drug | Class | Dose | Notes |
|------|-------|------|-------|
| **Clonidine** | Alpha-2 agonist | 0.05-0.4 mg/day | First-line drug; also helps ADHD |
| **Guanfacine** | Alpha-2 agonist | 0.5-4 mg/day | Alternative; longer-acting |
| **Tetrabenazine** | VMAT2 inhibitor | 25-200 mg/day | Effective; depression risk |
| **Haloperidol** | Typical antipsychotic | 0.5-10 mg/day | FDA-approved; EPSE, tardive |
| **Pimozide** | Typical antipsychotic | 1-10 mg/day | QTc prolongation; ECG |
| **Aripiprazole** | Atypical antipsychotic | 2.5-30 mg/day | Approved for TS in some countries; better tolerability |
| **Risperidone** | Atypical | 0.5-4 mg/day | Effective; weight, metabolic |
| **Fluphenazine** | Typical | 1-10 mg/day | Alternative |
| **Topiramate** | Anticonvulsant | 50-300 mg/day | Limited evidence |
| **Botulinum toxin** | Focal tics | Per muscle | For focal motor/vocal tics |

**4. Severe/refractory:**
- **Deep brain stimulation (DBS):** Thalamus (centromedian-parafascicular), GPi, or anterior limb of internal capsule
- Reserved for severe, refractory, adult TS

**5. Treat comorbidities:**
- **ADHD:** Methylphenidate, atomoxetine, clonidine (some drugs worsen tics, but evidence is weak; treat ADHD)
- **OCD:** CBT, SSRIs
- **Anxiety/depression:** SSRIs, CBT
- **Sleep:** Sleep hygiene, melatonin

**6. School support:**
- Extra time for exams
- Breaks if tics worsen
- Avoid stress

## 8. Drug Considerations
- **Antipsychotics:** EPSE, tardive, metabolic, sedation
- **Clonidine:** Hypotension, sedation, dry mouth
- **Tetrabenazine:** Depression risk
- **Pimozide:** QTc prolongation, ECG monitoring
- **Stimulants (ADHD):** May worsen tics slightly, but treat ADHD; clonidine can offset

## 9. Procedures
- **DBS:** Thalamic CM-Pf or GPi for severe refractory adult TS
- **Botulinum toxin:** Focal tics (e.g., cervical)

## 10. Complications
- **Self-injurious behaviour** (head-banging, self-hitting)
- **Psychological:** Depression, anxiety, social phobia, low self-esteem
- **School/occupational:** Impaired concentration, bullying
- **Iatrogenic:** Tardive dyskinesia, metabolic syndrome
- **Functional tics:** Social media-related (recent)

## 11. Red Flags
- New tics in adulthood (consider functional, drug-induced, Huntington's, Wilson's, CJD)
- Self-injurious behaviour (assess severity)
- Comorbid severe OCD/depression
- Suicidal ideation

## 12. Prognosis
- 50% significantly improve by late adolescence/early adulthood
- 20-30% continue with persistent tics
- Comorbidities affect quality of life more than tics
- DBS effective in severe refractory cases

## 13. Topic Correlation
- [[ADHD]] — most common comorbidity
- [[OCD]] — second common
- [[Chorea]] — differential

## 14. Special Situations
- **Pregnancy:** Avoid antipsychotics; behavioural therapy preferred
- **Paediatric:** Behavioural therapy first; clonidine second; antipsychotics for severe
- **School:** Educational support, address bullying
- **Functional tics:** Recent phenomenon in adolescents/young adults (social media); consider CBT
- **Driving:** May be affected; report
- **PANDAS:** Controversial; not generally recommended to treat with antibiotics

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
- **Definition:** Sudden, rapid, non-rhythmic, stereotyped movements/vocalisations
- **Tourette syndrome:** Multiple motor + vocal tics >1 year, onset <18
- **Key features:** Premonitory urge, suppressibility, wax and wane
- **Comorbidities:** ADHD (50-60%), OCD (30-50%)
- **First-line treatment:** Behavioural therapy (CBIT/HRT) + alpha-2 agonist (clonidine)
- **Pharmacology:** Clonidine, guanfacine, tetrabenazine, aripiprazole, haloperidol, pimozide
- **Coprolalia:** Occurs in 10-15% (not required for diagnosis)
- **DBS:** Severe refractory (thalamus CM-Pf, GPi)

## Common Confusions
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Tics vs myoclonus | Tics: suppressible, urge, stereotyped; myoclonus: not suppressible |
| Tics vs chorea | Tics: suppressible, urge, brief; chorea: continuous, not suppressible |
| Tics vs OCD compulsions | Tics: simpler, urge; OCD: complex, ego-dystonic, ritualised |
| Tics vs stereotypy | Stereotypy: fixed, no urge, autism/LD; tics: urge, suppressible |
| Tourette vs chronic motor tic | TS: motor + vocal; chronic: one type only |
| Adult-onset tics | Not TS; consider functional, drug, Huntington's |

## Mnemonics
- **Tic features** = **S**uppressible, **U**rge premonitory, **S**tereotyped, **S**uggestible (**SUSS**)
- **Tourette criteria** = **M**otor + **V**ocal, **>1** year, **<18** onset (**MV<1<18**)
- **Treatment ladder** = **E**ducation → **B**ehavioural (CBIT) → **C**lonidine → **A**ntipsychotic → **D**BS (**EBCAD**)
- **Comorbidities** = **A**DHD, **O**CD, **A**nxiety, **D**epression (**AOAD**)
