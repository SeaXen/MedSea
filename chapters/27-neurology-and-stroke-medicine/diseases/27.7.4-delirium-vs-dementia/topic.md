# Delirium vs Dementia

> [!tip] **Key Distinction**
> **Delirium** = **ACUTE** onset (hours-days), **FLUCTUATING**, **INATTENTION** + global cognitive impairment, due to **medical cause** — **MEDICAL EMERGENCY** (mortality 25-33% if untreated).
> **Dementia** = **CHRONIC** (months-years), **PROGRESSIVE**, **IRREVERSIBLE** cognitive decline with **PRESERVED consciousness** until late stages.

## 1. Definition / Epidemiology / Classification

**Delirium (DSM-5):** Disturbance in attention, awareness, and cognition developing over hours-days with fluctuating severity, due to a direct physiological cause.
**Dementia:** Acquired progressive cognitive decline in ≥1 domain severe enough to impair function.

| Metric | Delirium | Dementia |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Prevalence (hospitalised elderly) | 20-30% medical; 50% surgical; 75% ICU | 5-8% >65y; 15-20% >80y |
| Mortality | 25-33% at 6 months | Variable by subtype |
| Peak age | >65y | >65y |

**Delirium Subtypes:**
- **Hyperactive (25%):** Agitation, hallucinations, autonomic hyperactivity
- **Hypoactive (65%):** Quiet confusion, lethargy, inattention (often missed, **worst prognosis**)
- **Mixed (10%):** Fluctuate between hyper/hypoactive

## 2. Aetiology / Pathophysiology

**Delirium — Mnemonic: DIMES**
- **D**rugs (anticholinergics, benzodiazepines, opioids, steroids)
- **I**nfection (UTI, pneumonia, sepsis, COVID)
- **M**etabolic (Na, Ca, glucose, B12, thyroid, hepatic, renal)
- **E**nvironment (ICU, post-op, sleep deprivation)
- **S**tructural (stroke, ICH, trauma, tumour)

**Dementia Classification:**
| Type | Pathology | Frequency |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Alzheimer (AD) | Aβ plaques, tau tangles | 60-70% |
| Vascular (VaD) | Multi-infarct, strategic | 15-20% |
| Lewy Body (DLB) | α-synuclein | 10-15% |
| Frontotemporal (FTD) | Tau/TDP-43 | 5-10% (<65y) |
| Reversible | B12, thyroid, NPH, infection | 2-5% |

**Pathophysiology:** Acute insult → ↑dopamine, ↓acetylcholine, ↑glutamate → DMN disruption → attention failure (delirium). Chronic insult → Aβ/tau/α-syn accumulation → synaptic loss → dementia.

## 3. Clinical Features

| Feature | Delirium | Dementia |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **Onset** | Acute (hours-days) | Insidious (months-years) |
| **Course** | Fluctuating, lucid intervals | Progressive decline |
| **Consciousness** | **Clouded, reduced** | **Clear until late** |
| **Attention** | **Severely impaired** | Relatively preserved (early) |
| **Memory** | Impaired (registration, recall) | Recent first; registration OK |
| **Hallucinations** | Visual, fleeting | Visual (DLB), may be absent |
| **Sleep-wake** | **Severely disrupted** | Fragmented (sundowning) |
| **Insight** | Often partial | Often absent (anosognosia) |

## 4. Diagnostic Approach

**DSM-5 Delirium Criteria:**
1. Attention and awareness disturbance
2. Develops over hours-days, fluctuates
3. Additional cognitive disturbance
4. Not better explained by pre-existing dementia (but may be superimposed)
5. Direct physiological cause

**Cognitive Screening Tools:**
| Tool | Cut-off | Time | Use |
|------|---------|------|-----|
| **4AT** | ≥4 | 2 min | **NICE recommended** |
| **CAM** | 4 features | 5 min | Bedside |
| **AMTS** | ≤6/10 | 5 min | Quick screen |
| **MMSE** | ≤24/30 | 10 min | Dementia |
| **MoCA** | ≤25/30 | 10 min | MCI, FTD, vascular |
| **ACE-III** | Cut-offs | 15-20 min | Comprehensive |

## 5. Investigations

**Delirium (Always Investigate Cause):**
- **Bloods:** FBC, U&E, Ca, glucose, LFT, TSH, B12, folate, ammonia
- **Infection screen:** MSU, CXR, blood cultures
- **ECG, CXR:** Sepsis, MI
- **CT head:** Focal signs, trauma, no cause, anticoagulated
- **LP:** Fever, immunocompromised, no cause
- **EEG:** **Highly sensitive (90-95%)** — diffuse slowing; rule out NCSE

**Dementia — Reversible Causes (Always Exclude):**
- **Bloods:** TSH, B12, folate, syphilis (VDRL), HIV, anti-TPO, Ca
- **MRI:** Atrophy pattern, vascular, tumours, NPH
- **CSF (research/NICE NG97):** Aβ42 ↓, total-tau ↑, p-tau ↑ (AD biomarkers)
- **FDG-PET:** Hypometabolic patterns (AD, DLB, FTD)
- **DAT-SPECT:** ↓striatal uptake in DLB
- **Amyloid/Tau PET:** Florbetapir, flortaucipir
- **Genetic:** APOE (AD risk), PSEN1/2, APP (FAD), MAPT/GRN (FTD)

## 6. Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Features | Key Test |
|--------------|------------------------|----------|
| **Delirium** | Acute, fluctuating, medical cause, ↓attention | 4AT, CAM, EEG |
| **Dementia** | Chronic, progressive, ↓memory | MMSE, MoCA, MRI |
| **Depression (pseudodementia)** | Low mood, "don't know" answers, reversible | HAMD, BDI, antidepressant trial |
| **NCSE** | Subtle motor, no cause, cEEG abnormalities | cEEG (Salzburg) |
| **Acute psychosis** | No fluctuation, primary perceptual | Psychiatric assessment |
| **Wernicke** | Ataxia, ophthalmoplegia, confusion, alcoholism | Thiamine trial, MRI |

## 7. Management

**Delirium Management:**
| Strategy | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Treat cause** | Antibiotics, electrolytes, antidotes |
| **Reorientation** | Clock, calendar, family photos, reassurance |
| **Environment** | Single room, quiet, well-lit, avoid restraints |
| **Sleep-wake** | Daylight, avoid night sedation |
| **Sensory** | Glasses, hearing aids, dentures |
| **Mobilisation** | Early ambulation, avoid catheters |
| **Medication review** | Stop anticholinergics, sedatives |
| **Antipsychotic (low-dose)** | **Only** severe distress: Haloperidol 0.5-1mg; Olanzapine 2.5-5mg; Quetiapine 12.5-25mg (DLB: AVOID) |
| **Benzodiazepines** | **Only** alcohol/benzo withdrawal; otherwise AVOID |

**Dementia Management:**
| Domain | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| Disclosure | Sensitive, with family, written info |
| AChE inhibitors | Donepezil/Rivastigmine/Galantamine — AD (mild-mod), DLB, PDD |
| NMDA antagonist | Memantine — moderate-severe AD |
| Behavioural | Non-pharm first; antipsychotic for severe BPSD |
| Driving | DVLA notification mandatory |
| Legal | Power of attorney, advance care planning |
| Support | Admiral nurses, Alzheimer's Society |

## 8. Drug Interactions / Contraindications

| Drug | Cautions |
|------|----------|
| Haloperidol | QT prolongation, EPS, NMS — avoid in DLB |
| Anticholinergics | Worsen delirium, falls, dementia |
| Benzodiazepines | Worsen delirium, falls |
| Opioids | Anticholinergic, respiratory depression |
| AChE inhibitors | Bradycardia, syncope, GI upset, weight loss |
| Memantine | Agitation, dizziness |

## 9. Procedures

**LP in Delirium/Cognitive Decline:**
- **Indication:** Suspected CNS infection, autoimmune encephalitis, CJD, NPH (large-volume tap)
- **Viva Pearl:** AD biomarkers (Aβ42 ↓, tau ↑) now in NICE NG97

## 10. Complications

| Complication | Delirium | Dementia |
|--------------|----------|----------|
| **Falls** | 2-3x increased | Increased (visuospatial) |
| **Pressure sores** | Common (immobility) | Late stage |
| **Aspiration** | High (impaired swallow) | Late (FTD, advanced) |
| **Institutionalisation** | 3x risk | 70% in 7-10 years (AD) |
| **Death** | 25-33% at 6 months | 5-10y survival 50% (AD) |

## 11. Red Flags / Emergencies

| Red Flag | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Acute confusion in elderly | Treat as **delirium** (medical emergency) |
| Hypoactive delirium | High mortality, often missed; actively exclude |
| Delirium + antipsychotic in DLB | **STOP immediately** — life-threatening sensitivity |
| Sudden decline in dementia | Exclude **delirium superimposed** (most common) |
| Hyperthermia + rigidity | Consider **NMS** (stop antipsychotic) |
| Wernicke's triad | **Thiamine 500mg IV TDS** before glucose |

## 12. Prognosis

| Factor | Delirium | Dementia |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Recovery** | Days-weeks (50% in hospital) | Progressive, irreversible |
| **Long-term cognition** | 50% persistent deficit | Continued decline |
| **Mortality** | 1-year: 35-40% | AD: 5-10y; FTD: 6-11y; VaD: 5y |

## 14. Special Situations

| Situation | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Pregnancy** | Eclampsia, Wernicke, electrolyte; dementia rare |
| **Paediatric** | Autism, ADHD, intellectual disability, post-ictal, intoxication |
| **Elderly** | Highest risk; cholinergic load, polypharmacy, sensory |
| **ICU** | 75% incidence; daily sedation holds, orientation, sleep hygiene |
| **Post-operative** | 30-50% elderly hip fracture; multi-component prevention |
| **Palliative** | Terminal delirium common; haloperidol + non-pharm |
| **Driving (DVLA)** | Dementia = **must notify**; Group 1: cease |

## Viva Questions
1. **Q:** Define delirium per DSM-5. **A:** Acute onset (hours-days) of disturbance in attention, awareness, and cognition that fluctuates during the day, due to direct physiological cause.
2. **Q:** What is the most common delirium subtype and why is it dangerous? **A:** **Hypoactive (65%)** — quiet/lethargic, often missed, highest mortality.
3. **Q:** How do you differentiate delirium from dementia? **A:** **Delirium** = acute, fluctuating, impaired attention/consciousness. **Dementia** = chronic, progressive, preserved consciousness until late, memory loss prominent.
4. **Q:** List 5 causes of delirium. **A:** **DIMES**: Drugs, Infection, Metabolic, Environment, Structural.
5. **Q:** Why are antipsychotics dangerous in DLB? **A:** Severe **neuroleptic sensitivity** — typical antipsychotics cause severe parkinsonism, autonomic dysfunction, NMS. Use quetiapine low-dose or pimavanserin.
6. **Q:** What is the 4AT test? **A:** 4-item (Alertness, AMT4, Attention, Acute change): 0 normal; 1-3 cognitive impairment; **≥4 delirium**. NICE recommended.
7. **Q:** Differentiate dementia from pseudodementia. **A:** Depression: "don't know" answers, low mood, diurnal variation, **reversible**. Dementia: confabulation, tries to answer, progressive, **irreversible**.
8. **Q:** Wernicke triad and management? **A:** **Triad**: confusion + ataxia + ophthalmoplegia. **Thiamine 500mg IV TDS** BEFORE glucose.
9. **Q:** Cholinesterase inhibitors names and indications? **A:** **Donepezil** (AD), **Rivastigmine** (AD, PDD, DLB), **Galantamine** (AD). All for mild-moderate AD/DLB/PDD.
10. **Q:** Role of memantine? **A:** NMDA antagonist for **moderate-severe AD** (MMSE <15-20). Monotherapy or add-on to AChE inhibitor.
