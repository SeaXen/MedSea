# Adrenal Crisis

> [!info]
> **Adrenal crisis = acute life-threatening adrenal insufficiency.** Most common in known primary AI (Addison's) but can be first presentation. Mortality 5-10% if untreated. **Immediate hydrocortisone 100mg IV + fluids is life-saving.**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise clinical features of adrenal crisis
- [ ] Execute emergency management protocol (hydrocortisone + fluids)
- [ ] Identify precipitating factors
- [ ] Differentiate from septic shock and other causes of hypotension
- [ ] Manage steroid withdrawal and stress dosing

---

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Definition** | Acute deterioration in known or undiagnosed adrenal insufficiency with hypotension unresponsive to fluids, electrolyte disturbance, and/or hypoglycaemia |
| **Incidence** | 5-10 per 100 patient-years in known AI; 6% present with crisis as first manifestation |
| **Mortality** | 5-10% (higher if delayed treatment); higher in elderly, sepsis, delayed treatment |
| **Peak Age** | Any age; more common in known AI patients during stress |

---

## 3. Pathophysiology

| Mechanism | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Glucocorticoid deficiency** | ↓ Vascular tone (↓ vascular sensitivity to catecholamines) → Hypotension; ↓ Gluconeogenesis → Hypoglycaemia; ↓ Free water clearance → Hyponatraemia |
| **Mineralocorticoid deficiency (Primary AI)** | ↓ Aldosterone → Renal Na⁺ loss, K⁺ retention → Hyponatraemia, Hyperkalaemia, Volume depletion |
| **Cortisol deficiency** | ↓ Cortisol → Loss of negative feedback → ↑ CRH, ACTH → Hyperpigmentation (Primary AI) |
| **HPA axis suppression** | Exogenous steroids → Adrenal atrophy → Inability to mount stress response |

---

## 4. Clinical Presentation

| System | Features |
|--------|----------|
| **Cardiovascular** | **Hypotension** (SBP <100 mmHg, postural drop >20 mmHg), Tachycardia, Shock refractory to fluids |
| **Metabolic** | **Hypoglycaemia** (often profound), **Hyponatraemia** (<135 mmol/L), **Hyperkalaemia** (>5.5 mmol/L, primary only) |
| **Gastrointestinal** | Nausea, Vomiting, Abdominal pain, Anorexia, Weight loss |
| **Neurological** | Confusion, Lethargy, Fatigue, Syncope, Seizures (hypoglycaemia) |
| ** Dermatological (Primary AI)** | **Hyperpigmentation** (palmar creases, buccal mucosa, scars, knuckles) |
| **Precipitating History** | Infection (sepsis, GI, respiratory), Trauma, Surgery, Missed steroid doses, Steroid withdrawal |

**Red Flags**: Any patient on steroids presenting with hypotension + hyponatraemia + hypoglycaemia
**Differential**: Septic shock, Cardiogenic shock, Haemorrhage, Anaphylaxis, Neurogenic shock

---

## 5. Diagnostic Criteria

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **1. Acute deterioration** | Acute illness in known AI or undiagnosed |
| **2. Hypotension** | SBP <100 mmHg (or drop >20 mmHg from baseline), refractory to 1L fluid |
| **3. Electrolytes** | **Hyponatraemia + Hyperkalaemia** (Primary AI) or Hyponatraemia alone (Secondary) |
| **4. Hypoglycaemia** | Often present (<3.0 mmol/L) |
| **4. Response to Hydrocortisone** | **Rapid BP improvement within 1-2h confirms** |

**Diagnosis is CLINICAL — do not delay treatment for tests**

---

## 6. Emergency Management Protocol

### IMMEDIATE (First 30 Minutes)
| Step | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| **1. ABCDE** | Airway, Breathing, **Circulation**, Disability, Exposure | High-flow O₂, Cardiac monitoring, IV access ×2 |
| **2. Hydrocortisone** | **100mg IV STAT** (bolus) | **DO NOT DELAY FOR TESTS** |
| **3. Fluids** | **1L 0.9% NaCl over 1 hour** | Then 2-3L over 24h (monitor JVP, UOP, U&Es) |
| **4. Glucose** | 5-10% Dextrose if hypoglycaemic | 50-100ml 20% dextrose if severe; then 5-10% infusion |
| **5. Monitoring** | U&Es, Glucose q1-2h; Cardiac monitor; Strict fluid balance | Target UOP >0.5ml/kg/h |

### ONGOING (First 24 Hours)
| Time | Hydrocortisone | Fluids | Monitoring |
|------|----------------|--------|------------|
| **0-6h** | 50-100mg IV q6h | 1L 1st hour, then 150-250ml/h | U&Es, Glucose q1-2h; BP, HR q15min |
| **6-24h** | 50mg IV q6h | 150-250ml/h | U&Es 4-6h; Glucose 4-6h; UOP |
| **24-48h** | 50mg IV q8h | As clinical | U&Es 6-8h; Glucose 6-8h |

### TAPER (Once Stable)
| Day | Regimen |
|-----|---------|
| **Day 3-5** | 50mg q8h → 25mg q8h |
| **Day 5-7** | 20mg AM, 10mg PM |
| **Week 2** | 15mg AM, 10mg PM |
| **Week 3-4** | 15mg AM, 5mg PM |
| **Maintenance** | 15-20mg AM + 5-10mg PM (or 20-30mg OD) |

## 7. Precipitating Factors

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Infection** | Sepsis (pneumonia, UTI, GI), TB, HIV, COVID-19 |
| **Trauma/Surgery** | Major surgery, Fractures, Burns |
| **Medication** | **Missed steroid doses**, Abrupt steroid withdrawal, Drug interactions (enzyme inducers), Anticoagulation |
| **Endocrine** | Thyrotoxicosis, Diabetic ketoacidosis, Hypothyroidism |
| **Gastrointestinal** | Vomiting, Diarrhoea (impaired absorption), GI bleed |
| **Obstetric** | Labour, Delivery, Postpartum (Sheehan's) |

---

## 8. Steroid Withdrawal & Stress Dosing

### HPA Axis Suppression
| Steroid Exposure | Suppression Risk | Recovery |
|------------------|-----------------|----------|
| **<3 weeks** | Minimal | Days |
| **>3 weeks (any dose)** | **Significant** (adrenal atrophy) | **Weeks to months** |
| **High dose (>40mg prednisolone equivalent)** | Severe | Months |
| **Inhaled/Topical (high dose)** | Moderate | Weeks |

### Stress Dose Hydrocortisone Protocol
| Stress Level | Hydrocortisone Dose |
|--------------|---------------------|
| **Minor (Dental, Minor surgery)** | 25mg IV/IM pre-op + usual oral dose |
| **Moderate (Major surgery, Sepsis, Trauma)** | **50-100mg IV pre-op** then 50mg q6-8h × 24-48h → Taper |
| **Major (Cardiac surgery, Major trauma, Septic shock)** | **100mg IV bolus** then 50-100mg q6-8h × 48-72h → Taper |
| **Labour/Delivery** | 100mg IV at onset of labour → 50mg q6h during labour → 100mg IV at delivery |

**Oral Stress Dosing (if IV not available)**: Triple usual oral dose for duration of illness

---

## 9. Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Similarities | Key Differentiators |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Septic Shock** | Hypotension, Fever, Confusion | **Hyperkalaemia rare**, Lactate ↑↑, Procalcitonin ↑, Blood cultures +ve |
| **Cardiogenic Shock** | Hypotension, Confusion | **JVP ↑, Pulmonary oedema, ECG changes**, BNP ↑, Echo: ↓ EF |
| **Hypovolaemic Shock** | Hypotension, Tachycardia | **History of loss**, JVP low, Urea:Cr ratio >20, No hyperkalaemia |
| **Anaphylaxis** | Hypotension, Wheeze, Urticaria | **Acute onset**, Triggers, Tryptase ↑, Responds to adrenaline |
| **Neurogenic Shock** | Hypotension, Bradycardia | **Spinal injury**, Warm peripheries |

---

## 10. Investigation (After Stabilisation)

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **0900 Cortisol + ACTH** | Confirm AI; Differentiate Primary vs Secondary |
| **Electrolytes** | Na⁺, K⁺, Urea, Creatinine, Glucose |
| **FBC, CRP, Blood Cultures** | Identify precipitant |
| **TSH, fT4** | Rule out concurrent thyroid disease |
| **Adrenal Antibodies** | Anti-21-hydroxylase (Autoimmune) |
| **Adrenal CT** | Adrenal haemorrhage, TB, Metastases |
| **Pituitary MRI** | If Secondary AI suspected |

---

## 11. Long-term Management

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Glucocorticoid Replacement** | Hydrocortisone 15-20mg AM + 5-10mg PM (or 20-30mg OD); Prednisolone 5mg OD |
| **Mineralocorticoid (Primary AI)** | **Fludrocortisone 50-100µg OD** (adjust for BP, K⁺, Renin) |
| **Monitoring** | 6-monthly: BP, Weight, Electrolytes, Renin, HbA1c, Bone density (DEXA q2-3yr) |
| **Patient Education** | **Medical alert bracelet**, Steroid card, **Triple dose for illness**, IM hydrocortisone 100mg pen for emergency |
| **Vaccinations** | Influenza annually, Pneumococcal, COVID-19 |
| **Bone Protection** | Calcium + Vitamin D; DEXA q2-3yr; Bisphosphonate if T-score <-2.5 |
| **Pregnancy** | Increase hydrocortisone 20-40% in 2nd/3rd trimester; Stress doses for delivery |

## 12. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **Adrenal Crisis Mortality** | 5-10% if untreated |
| **First-Line Treatment** | **Hydrocortisone 100mg IV STAT** + 1L 0.9% NaCl |
| **Never Delay** | Hydrocortisone **before** tests |
| **Hyponatraemia + Hyperkalaemia** = Primary AI (Mineralocorticoid deficiency) |
| **Hyponatraemia alone** = Secondary AI (Glucocorticoid deficiency only) |
| **Hyperpigmentation** = Primary AI (High ACTH → MSH cross-reactivity) |
| **Precipitants** | Infection, Trauma, Surgery, Missed doses, Steroid withdrawal |
| **Steroid Withdrawal** | >3 weeks = HPA suppression; Taper over weeks-months |
| **Stress Dosing** | Minor: 25mg IV; Moderate: 50-100mg IV; Major: 100mg IV + 50mg q6h |
| **Fludrocortisone** | Only in Primary AI; Dose 50-100µg OD; Monitor BP, K⁺, Renin |
| **Medical Alert** | Bracelet + Steroid card + IM Hydrocortisone 100mg pen |
| **Adrenal Crisis vs Septic Shock** | Crisis: Hyperkalaemia, Hyponatraemia, Hypoglycaemia, Responds to Hydrocortisone |
| **Pregnancy** | Increase dose 20-40% in 2nd/3rd tri; Stress doses for delivery |

## 13. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **AI vs Crisis** | AI = Chronic insufficiency; Crisis = Acute decompensation requiring emergency treatment |
| **Primary vs Secondary Crisis** | Primary: Hyperkalaemia + Hyperpigmentation; Secondary: No hyperkalaemia, No pigmentation |
| **Steroid Taper** | No fixed formula; Individualised by duration/dose; Generally 2.5-5mg prednisolone reduction every 1-2 weeks |
| **Stress Dosing** | "Triple the dose" for oral; IV hydrocortisone for major stress |
| **Crisis vs Shock** | Crisis = AI decompensation; Septic shock = Infection-driven; Crisis responds dramatically to hydrocortisone |

**Mnemonic: CRISIS MANAGEMENT**
- **C**ortisol (Hydrocortisone 100mg IV)
- **R**esuscitate (1L 0.9% NaCl fast)
- **I**nvestigate (After stabilisation: Cortisol, ACTH, U&Es)
- **S**tress doses (Taper over days)
- **I**dentify precipitant (Sepsis, Trauma, Missed doses)
- **S**teroid card + Alert bracelet

**Mnemonic: ADDISON'S CRISIS FEATURES**
- **A**bdomen pain, **D**ehydration, **D**izziness (hypotension)
- **I**nfection precipitant, **S**odium low, **O**rganism (infection)
- **N**ausea/vomiting, **'**s (Hyperpigmentation), **S**hock
