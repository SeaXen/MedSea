# Bronchial Neuroendocrine Tumours (NETs)

> [!info]
> **Bronchial NETs (Carcinoids) = 2nd Most Common Lung NET** (After Small Cell). **25-30% of All Lung NETs.** **Typical (Low-Grade) vs Atypical (Intermediate-Grade).** **Carcinoid Syndrome Rare (<10%)** (Foregut Origin → Systemic Drainage). **Surgical Resection = Curative for Localised Disease.**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Classify bronchial NETs (Typical vs Atypical Carcinoid)
- [ ] Recognise clinical presentation (Cough, Haemoptysis, Obstruction, Carcinoid Syndrome)
- [ ] Apply diagnostic workup (CT, Bronchoscopy, Biopsy, DOTATATE PET)
- [ ] Apply surgical and medical management algorithms
- [ ] Differentiate from Small Cell Lung Cancer and Metastatic NETs

## 2. Classification (WHO 2019)

| Category | Ki-67 Index | Mitotic Count (per 2mm²) | Former Terminology |
|---------|-------------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| **Typical Carcinoid (TC)** | **<5%** | **<2** | Low-Grade (Typical Carcinoid) |
| **Atypical Carcinoid (AC)** | **5-20%** | **2-20** | Intermediate-Grade (Atypical Carcinoid) |
| **Large Cell Neuroendocrine Carcinoma (LCNEC)** | **>20%** | **>20** | High-Grade (LCNEC) |
| **Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC)** | **>20%** | **>20** | High-Grade (SCLC) |

> **Key**: **Typical Carcinoid (TC) = Low-Grade**; **Atypical Carcinoid (AC) = Intermediate-Grade**; Both = Bronchial NETs. LCNEC/SCLC = High-Grade Neuroendocrine Carcinomas.

## 3. Epidemiology & Risk Factors

| Feature | Typical Carcinoid (TC) | Atypical Carcinoid (AC) |
|-----------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Incidence** | ~1.5-2/100,000/year | ~0.5-1/100,000/year |
| **Age** | 40-60 Years | 50-70 Years |
| **Sex** | F = M | Slight Female Predominance |
| **Smoking** | **No Association** | **Strong Association** (Current/Former) |
| **Location** | Central (Main Bronchi) > Peripheral | Peripheral > Central |

---

## 4. Clinical Presentation

| Feature | Typical Carcinoid (TC) | Atypical Carcinoid (AC) |
|---------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Symptoms** | Cough, Haemoptysis, Wheezing, Recurrent Pneumonia, Chest Pain | Similar + Weight Loss, Dyspnoea |
| **Carcinoid Syndrome** | **Rare (<5-10%)** (Foregut → Systemic Drainage) | **Rare** |
| **Carcinoid Syndrome** | Flushing, Diarrhoea, Valvular HD (If Liver Mets) | Similar |
| **Paraneoplastic** | Rare (Cushing - ACTH, SIADH) | Cushing (ACTH), SIADH |
| **Presentation** | Often Incidental (CT Screening) or Symptomatic (Obstruction) | Symptomatic (Invasive) |

---

## 5. Clinical Presentation by Location

| Location | Presentation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Central (Main Bronchi)** | **Cough, Haemoptysis, Wheezing, Stridor, Post-Obstructive Pneumonia** |
| **Peripheral** | Often **Incidental** (Screening CT); May Present with Pleural Effusion, Chest Pain |

### Paraneoplastic Syndromes
| Syndrome | Mechanism | Clinical |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Cushing Syndrome** | **ACTH Ectopic** (More Common in AC) | Hypercortisolism, Hypokalaemia, Alkalosis |
| **SIADH** | ADH Secretion | Hyponatraemia, Confusion |
| **Carcinoid Syndrome** | **Rare** (<10%); Foregut → Systemic Drainage | Flushing, Diarrhoea, Valvular HD (If Liver Mets) |

## 6. Diagnosis — Biopsy & Grading

### Bronchoscopy
| Feature | Typical Carcinoid (TC) | Atypical Carcinoid (AC) |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Appearance** | Polypoid, Endobronchial, Well-Circumscribed | Infiltrative, Ulcerated, Bleeding |
| **Biopsy** | **Forceps / Transbronchial Needle Aspiration (TBNA)** | Same |
| **Histology** | **Organoid/Nesting/Trabecular**; **<2 Mitoses/2mm²**; **Ki-67 <5%** | **Mitotic 2-20/2mm²**; **Ki-67 5-20%**; Necrosis Possible |

### Immunohistochemistry (IHC)
| Marker | Typical Carcinoid | Atypical Carcinoid |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Chromogranin A** | **+** | **+** |
| **Synaptophysin** | **+** | **+** |
| **CD56 (NCAM)** | **+** | **+** |
| **Ki-67** | **<5%** | **5-20%** |
| **TTF-1** | **+ (Variable)** | **+ (Variable)** |
| **Ki-67** | **<5%** | **5-20%** |

## 7. Staging (TNM 8th Edition)

| Stage | Tumour (T) | Nodes (N) | Metastasis (M) |
|-------|------------|-----------|----------------|
| **I** | T1 (≤3cm) | N0 | M0 |
| **II** | T2a (>3-4cm) / T2b (>4-5cm) | N0 | M0 |
| **IIIA** | T3 (>5-7cm) OR N1 | N1 | M0 |
| **IIIB** | T4 (Invasion) OR N2 | N2 | M0 |
| **IV** | Any T | Any N | **M1** |

| N Category | Typical | Atypical |
|------------|---------|----------|
| **N1** | Ipsilateral Hilar/Peribronchial | Same |
| **N2** | Ipsilateral Mediastinal/Subcarinal | Same |

---

## 8. Imaging & Staging

| Modality | Role |
|----------|--------|
| **CT Chest (Contrast)** | **Primary Staging**; Tumour Size, Location, Nodes, Liver/Bone Mets |
| **⁶⁸Ga-DOTATATE PET/CT** | **Gold Standard Functional Imaging** (SSTR2+); Occult Mets, Nodal Staging |
| **PET-CT (FDG)** | **Low Avidity** (Low-Grade); **High FDG Avidity = Higher Grade/Transformation** |
| **Bronchoscopy** | **Diagnosis + Local Extent** (Endobronchial Extent, Carinal Involvement) |
| **EBUS/EUS** | **Nodal Staging** (N1/N2) |
| **MRI Brain** | If Neurological Symptoms / Staging IIIB/IV |

---

## 9. Management by Stage

### Stage I-II (Resectable)
| Procedure | Indication | Lymphadenectomy |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Sleeve Lobectomy / Lobectomy** | T1-2, N0 | **Systematic N1/N2 Dissection** |
| **Pneumonectomy** | T3, Central Tumour | Systematic |
| **Sublobar (Segmentectomy/Wedge)** | **TC Peripheral ≤2cm, Node-Negative** | **Sampling/Dissection** |

**Lymph Node Dissection**: **Systematic N1/N2** (Mandatory for Staging/Prognosis).

### Adjuvant Therapy
| Scenario | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **TC, R0, N0** | **Observation** (No Adjuvant) |
| **AC, R0, N0** | **Consider Adjuvant Chemo** (Cisplatin + Etoposide) — Controversial |
| **N1/N2 Disease** | **Adjuvant Chemo** (Cisplatin + Etoposide ×4 Cycles) |
| **Positive Margins** | Re-resection / Adjuvant RT (54Gy) + Chemo |

---

## 10. Advanced/Metastatic Disease

### Medical Therapy
| Line | Regimen | Indication |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **1st Line (TC/AC)** | **Somatostatin Analogues** (Octreotide LAR / Lanreotide) | Symptom Control, Tumour Stabilisation |
| **2nd Line** | **Everolimus** (mTOR Inhibitor) | Progressive, Well-Diff (G1/G2) |
| **2nd Line** | **Sunitinib** (TKI) | Progressive, Well-Diff |
| **Chemotherapy (AC/LCNEC/SCLC)** | **Cisplatin/Etoposide** (or Carboplatin) | **1st Line** (AC/LCNEC/SCLC) |
| **PRRT** | **¹⁷⁷Lu-DOTATATE** | SSTR2+, G1/G2, Progressive |

### Chemotherapy Regimens
| Histology | Regimen | Cycles |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Atypical Carcinoid** | **Cisplatin + Etoposide** | 4-6 |
| **LCNEC** | **Cisplatin + Etoposide** (or Carboplatin) | 4-6 |
| **SCLC** | **Cisplatin + Etoposide** | 4-6 |

### PRRT (Peptide Receptor Radionuclide Therapy)
| Indication | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **SSTR2+** | **⁶⁸Ga-DOTATATE PET Positive** |
| **Grade** | **NET G1/G2** (Well-Differentiated) |
| **Status** | **Progressive** Despite SST Analogues |
| **Agent** | **¹⁷⁷Lu-DOTATATE (Lutathera)** |
| **Protocol** | **7.4 GBq IV q8wk × 4 Cycles** + Amino Acid Infusion |

## 11. Follow-Up & Surveillance

| Stage | Surveillance |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Stage I (TC)** | CT Chest **Annual ×5yr**, then q2-3yr |
| **Stage I (AC)** | CT Chest **6-Monthly ×2yr**, Then Annually |
| **Stage II-III** | CT Chest **6-Monthly ×2yr**, Then Annually |
| **Stage IV** | **3-Monthly** CT + ⁶⁸Ga-DOTATATE PET + Chromogranin A / NSE |

### Surveillance Biomarkers
| Biomarker | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Chromogranin A** | q3-6mo |
| **NSE (Neuron-Specific Enolase)** | q6mo (AC/LCNEC/SCLC) |
| **ProGRP** | q6mo (SCLC) |

## 12. Carcinoid Syndrome in Bronchial NETs

| Feature | Bronchial NET (Foregut) |
|---------|-------------------------|
| **Frequency** | **<10%** (Rare) |
| **Mechanism** | **Systemic Venous Drainage** (Pulmonary Veins → Left Heart → Systemic) |
| **Clinical** | Flushing (Dry/Wet), Diarrhoea, Bronchospasm, Valvular HD (Right > Left) |
| **Management** | Octreotide LAR / Lanreotide (Same as GI Carcinoid) |

## 13. Differential Diagnosis

| Feature | Typical Carcinoid | Atypical Carcinoid | LCNEC | SCLC |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------|-------|------|
| **Mitotic Count** | <2/2mm² | 2-20/2mm² | >20/2mm² | >20/2mm² |
| **Ki-67** | <5% | 5-20% | >20% | >20% |
| **Necrosis** | Absent | Focal/Punctate | Necrosis Common | Extensive Necrosis |
| **Growth** | Slow | Moderate | Rapid | Very Rapid |
| **Metastasis** | Late | Intermediate | Early | Early |
| **Smoking Link** | No | Yes | Strong | Strong |
| **FDG-PET** | Low Uptake | Variable | High | Very High |
