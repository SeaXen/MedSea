# Primary Hyperparathyroidism (PHPT)

> [!info]
> **Primary Hyperparathyroidism (PHPT) = Autonomous PTH Overproduction** by Parathyroid Gland(s). **Commonest Cause of Hypercalcaemia in Outpatients.** **Female:Male = 2:1**, Peak 50-70y. **Single Adenoma (80-85%)** Commonest; **Surgical Cure** Definitive.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Apply diagnostic criteria (Hypercalcaemia + Inappropriately High/Normal PTH)
- [ ] Differentiate PHPT from Familial Hypocalciuric Hypercalcaemia (FHH) and Malignancy
- [ ] Apply surgical criteria (Asymptomatic vs Symptomatic)
- [ ] Outline surgical approach (Focused Parathyroidectomy) and intraoperative PTH monitoring
- [ ] Manage medical therapy for non-surgical candidates

## 2. Aetiology & Pathophysiology

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Aetiology** | **Single Adenoma (80-85%)**; **Double Adenoma (5-10%)**; **Hyperplasia (10-15%)**; Carcinoma (<1%) |
| **Genetics** | **MEN1** (MEN1 Gene); **Cyclin D1 (PRAD1)** Rearrangement (15-40% Sporadic Adenomas); **CDC73** (Parathyroid Carcinoma); **CaSR** (FHH) |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Autonomous PTH Secretion** → ↑ Bone Resorption (Ca²⁺ Release), ↑ Renal Ca²⁺ Reabsorption, ↑ 1,25-(OH)₂D Synthesis → Hypercalcaemia |
| **Demographics** | **Female:Male = 2:1**; Peak **50-70 Years** |

---

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Asymptomatic (Majority at Diagnosis)
| Presentation | Frequency |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Incidental Hypercalcaemia** | **80% Identified on Routine Bloods** |

### Symptomatic (Classical "Moans, Groans, Stones, Bones")
| System | Features |
|--------|----------|
| **Renal (Stones)** | **Nephrolithiasis (15-20%)**; Nephrocalcinosis; Renal Impairment |
| **Skeletal (Bones)** | **Osteoporosis**, Osteitis Fibrosa Cystica (Brown Tumours), Pathological Fractures, Bone Pain |
| **GI (Moans)** | Nausea, Vomiting, Constipation, Anorexia, Peptic Ulcers, Pancreatitis |
| **Neuropsychiatric (Groans)** | Fatigue, Weakness, Depression, Confusion, Cognitive Decline |
| **Cardiovascular** | Hypertension, Shortened QT, Vascular Calcification |

---

## 4. Diagnosis — Biochemical Criteria

| Test | Expected in PHPT |
|------|------------------|
| **Albumin-Corrected Calcium** | **>2.6 mmol/L (10.4 mg/dL)** |
| **Ionised Calcium** | **>1.3 mmol/L** (More Accurate if Albumin Low) |
| **PTH** | **Elevated** (>6.8 pmol/L) OR **Inappropriately Normal** (Inappropriately Normal = Inappropriately Normal for Degree of Hypercalcaemia) |
| **Phosphate** | **Low/Normal** (PTH → Phosphaturia) |
| **Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP)** | Normal or Elevated (Bone Turnover) |
| **25-OH Vitamin D** | Often Low (Secondary to ↑ 1,25-(OH)₂D) |
| **24h Urinary Calcium** | **>7.5 mmol/day (300 mg/day)** = Hypercalciuria |

---

## 5. Differentiation — The "Big Three" Causes of Hypercalcaemia

| Feature | **PHPT** | **Malignancy** | **Familial Hypocalciuric Hypercalcaemia (FHH)** |
|---------|----------|----------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **PTH** | **High / Inappropriately Normal** | **Suppressed (<1 pmol/L)** | **Normal / Mildly Elevated** |
| **Calcium** | High | High | Mildly High (Often Normal) |
| **Phosphate** | Low/Normal | Normal/Low | Normal/Low |
| **24h Urine Calcium** | **High** (>7.5 mmol/24h) | Variable | **Low** (<2.5 mmol/24h) |
| **Urinary Calcium/Creatinine Clearance Ratio** | **>0.01** | Variable | **<0.01** (Key Discriminator) |
| **PTHrP** | Negative | **Positive** (Humoral Hypercalcaemia) | Negative |
| **25-OH Vit D** | Low/Normal | Low/Normal | Normal |
| **Family History** | Rare | Rare | **Autosomal Dominant** (Multiple Affected) |

## 6. Diagnostic Algorithm

```
Hypercalcaemia (Corrected Ca >2.6 mmol/L)
         │
         ▼
Measure PTH (Intact)
         │
         ├── PTH HIGH / Inappropriately Normal → PRIMARY HYPERPARATHYROIDISM
         │       │
         │       ├── 24h Urine Ca/Cr >0.02 → CONFIRM PHPT
         │       └── Ca/Cr <0.01 → CONSIDER FHH
         │
         ├── PTH SUPPRESSED (<1 pmol/L) → MALIGNANCY / GRANULOMATOUS / VIT D TOXICITY
         │       ├── PTHrP Positive → HUMORAL HYPERCALCAEMIA OF MALIGNANCY
         │       ├── 1,25-(OH)₂ Vit D High → SARCOID / LYMPHOMA / TB
         │       └── 25-OH Vit D High → VIT D TOXICITY
         │
         └── PTH NORMAL / MILDLY ELEVATED + LOW URINE CA → FHH
                 Genetic Testing (CaSR Mutation) → Confirm
```

## 7. Localisation & Pre-Operative Imaging

| Modality | Sensitivity | Specificity | Role |
|----------|-------------|-------------|------|
| **Sestamibi Scan (⁹⁹mTc-MIBI)** | **70-90%** (Single Adenoma) | 80-90% | **First-Line** |
| **Neck Ultrasound** | 60-80% | 80-90% | Complementary; Operator-Dependent |
| **4D-CT** | 85-95% | 90-95% | Problem-Solving / Re-operative |
| **MRI** | 75-85% | 85-95% | Alternative (No Radiation) |
| **PET-CT (Choline/Choline-11/¹⁸F-Fluorocholine)** | 90%+ | 90%+ | Problem Cases |

**Key**: **No Single Test 100%**; **Concordant Dual Imaging (Sestamibi + US)** → High Confidence for Focused Surgery.

## 8. Surgical Indications (NICE / AAES / KDOQI Guidelines)

### Symptomatic PHPT (Absolute Indications)
| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Symptomatic** | Stones, Bone Disease (Fracture/Osteoporosis), Pancreatitis, Neuropsychiatric |

### Asymptomatic PHPT (Surgical If ANY Met)
| Criterion | Threshold (AAES 2022 / NICE) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **Age** | **<50 Years** |
| **Serum Calcium** | **>2.85 mmol/L (11.5 mg/dL)** |
| **Renal** | eGFR **<60 mL/min**; Nephrolithiasis; Nephrocalcinosis |
| **Bone** | **T-score ≤-2.5** (Spine/Hip/Forearm); Vertebral Fracture (VFA) |
| **24h Urine Calcium** | **>10 mmol/day (400 mg/day)** |
| **Age >50** | Not an Absolute Indication (Individualise) |

---

## 9. Surgical Management

### Focused Parathyroidectomy (Minimally Invasive)
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Indication** | **Pre-Op Concordant Localisation** (Sestamibi + US ± 4D-CT) |
| **Incision** | 2-3 cm Cervical Incision (or Endoscopic) |
| **Intraoperative PTH (IOPTH)** | **Miami Criterion**: **>50% Drop from Peak at 10min Post-Excision** = Cure Prediction |
| **Success Rate** | **95-98%** (Single Adenoma); Lower for Hyperplasia |

### Bilateral Neck Exploration (Traditional)
| Indication | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Discordant Imaging** | No Concordant Localisation |
| **Hyperplasia Suspected** | MEN1, Familial, Renal Failure |
| **Re-operation** | Previous Failed Surgery |
| **Carcinoma Suspected** | En Bloc Resection |

### Intraoperative PTH (IOPTH) Criteria
| Criterion | Miami Criterion (Most Common) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **>50% Drop** from Highest Pre-Excision PTH at **10 Minutes** Post-Excision | **Predicts Cure** |
| **Alternative** | **>50% Drop from Baseline** at 10min |

## 10. Medical Management (Non-Surgical Candidates)

| Indication | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **Age >75** | Frailty, Comorbidities |
| **Refusal/Unfit for Surgery** | Patient Choice / High Anaesthetic Risk |
| **Mild Disease** | Ca <2.85, No Renal/Bone/Stone Complications |

### Medical Therapy Options
| Drug | Dose | Mechanism | Monitoring |
|------|------|-----------|------------|
| **Cinacalcet** (Calcimimetic) | 30-90mg BD (Max 180mg/day) | **CaSR Agonist** → ↓ PTH Secretion | Ca, PTH q1-3mo; Adverse: Nausea, Hypocalcaemia |
| **Bisphosphonates** (Alendronate/Zoledronate) | Standard Doses | Inhibit Bone Resorption | BMD, Renal Function |
| **Denosumab** | 60mg SC 6-Monthly | RANKL Inhibition | Hypocalcaemia Risk (Supplement Ca/Vit D) |
| **Hydration + Loop Diuretic** | Acute Severe Hypercalcaemia | Acute Management | Not Chronic |

---

## 11. Parathyroid Carcinoma (Rare, <1%)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Clinical** | Severe Hypercalcaemia (Ca >3.5), Large Neck Mass, Hoarseness (RLN Invasion) |
| **PTH** | **Very High** (Often >100 pmol/L) |
| **Calcium** | **>3.5 mmol/L** (Often >4.0) |
| **Imaging** | Large Mass, Invasion (Trachea, Oesophagus, RLN), Distant Mets |
| **Genetics** | **CDC73 (HRPT2)** Mutation (Tumour Suppressor) |
| **Management** | **En Bloc Resection** (Thyroid Lobectomy + Lymphadenectomy); Adjuvant RT; **No Role for Cinacalcet** |

---

## 12. Post-Operative Management

### Immediate Post-Op (Hungry Bone Syndrome)
| Complication | Mechanism | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| **Hypocalcaemia** | **Rapid Bone Mineralisation** → Sudden Ca²⁺ Uptake | **IV Calcium Gluconate** (10% 10-20mL Bolus) → Oral Ca/Vit D |
| **Hypomagnesaemia** | Co-existent | IV MgSO₄ |
| **Hypophosphataemia** | Bone Remineralisation | Oral Phosphate |

### Post-Op Hypocalcaemia Protocol
| Calcium Level | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| **Ionised Ca <1.0 mmol/L** or **Symptomatic** | **10% Calcium Gluconate 10-20mL IV Bolus** → Infusion 1-2mg/kg/hr |
| **Ionised Ca 1.0-1.1** | Oral Calcium + Alfacalcidol/Calcitriol |
| **Monitoring** | Ionised Ca q2-4h (First 24-48h) |

## 15. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **PHPT vs FHH** | **Urine Ca/Cr Ratio**: PHPT >0.02; FHH <0.01 |
| **PHPT vs Malignancy** | PHPT: PTH High; Malignancy: PTH Suppressed, PTHrP+ |
| **PTH in FHH** | Normal/Mildly ↑; In PHPT: Inappropriately High/Normal |
| **Cinacalcet** | Calcimimetic; ↓ PTH; Risk: Hypocalcaemia, Nausea |
| **Carcinoma vs Adenoma** | Ca >3.5, Mass >3cm, RLN Invasion, CDC73 Mutation |
| **Surgical Indications** | Age <50, Ca >2.85, eGFR <60, BMD ≤-2.5, Stones, Urine Ca >10mmol |
| **IOPTH Miami Criterion** | >50% Drop from Peak at 10min Post-Excision = Cure |
| **Hungry Bone Syndrome** | Post-Op Hypocalcaemia (Rapid Bone Uptake) → IV Ca Gluconate |

**Mnemonic: PHPT SURGICAL CRITERIA (AAES)**
- **A**ge <50
- **C**alcium >2.85
- **K**idney (eGFR <60 / Stones)
- **B**one (T-score ≤-2.5 / Fracture)
- **U**rine Ca >10 mmol/day

**Mnemonic: FHH vs PHPT DISCRIMINATORS**
- **F**HH: **F**amily History (AD), **H**ypocalciuria (<0.01), **F**H
- **P**HPT: **H**igh Urine Ca (>7.5), **P**TH High/Inappropriately Normal
