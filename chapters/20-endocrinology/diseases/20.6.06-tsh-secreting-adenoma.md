# TSH-Secreting Adenoma (TSH-oma)

> [!info]
> **TSH-oma = TSH-Secreting Pituitary Adenoma.** **Rare (1-2% of Pituitary Adenomas).** Causes **Central Hyperthyroidism**: fT4/fT3 ↑ with **Inappropriately Normal/High TSH**. **TRH Test: Paradoxical TSH Rise**. **Surgery = 1st Line.**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise clinical presentation of central hyperthyroidism (thyrotoxicosis with non-suppressed TSH)
- [ ] Differentiate TSH-oma from Graves disease and thyroid hormone resistance (RTH)
- [ ] Apply diagnostic criteria (TSH inappropriately normal/high with elevated fT4/fT3)
- [ ] Apply TRH stimulation test and MRI pituitary for diagnosis
- [ ] Outline management (surgery 1st line, somatostatin analogues, radiotherapy)

---

## 2. Epidemiology & Pathophysiology

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Frequency** | **1-2%** of Pituitary Adenomas |
| **Demographics** | Adults (Peak 40-50y); No Sex Predilection |
| **Cell Origin** | **Thyrotroph Adenoma** (TSH-Secreting) |
| **Genetics** | **GNAS** (Activating), **GNA11**, **USP8**, **ATP1A1**; **MEN1** (Rare) |
| **Secretion** | **TSH + α-Subunit** (Often Co-Secretion) |
| **Feedback Resistance** | **Impaired Negative Feedback** — TSH Fails to Suppress with High fT4/fT3 |

---

## 3. Clinical Presentation

| Feature | TSH-oma | Graves Disease |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **Thyrotoxic Symptoms** | Weight Loss, Tachycardia, Tremor, Heat Intolerance | Identical |
| **Goitre** | **Present** (Usually) | Present |
| **Ophthalmopathy** | **Absent** (Rare) | **Present (25-50%)** |
| **Dermopathy/Acropachy** | Absent | May Be Present |
| **TSH** | **Inappropriately Normal/High** | **Suppressed (<0.01 mIU/L)** |
| **TRAb** | Negative | **Positive (>95%)** |
| **α-Subunit** | **Elevated** (Co-Secreted) | Normal |
| **TRH Test** | **Paradoxical TSH Rise** | Blunted (No Rise) |

---

## 4. Biochemical Profile

| Parameter | TSH-oma | Graves Disease | RTH (THRβ Mutation) |
|---------|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| **TSH** | **Normal / High** (Inappropriately Normal) | **Suppressed (<0.01)** | **Normal / High** |
| **fT4 / fT3** | **Elevated** | Elevated | **Elevated** |
| **TRAb** | **Negative** | **Positive (>95%)** | Negative |
| **α-Subunit** | **Elevated** (Co-Secreted) | Normal | Normal |
| **α-Subunit/TSH Ratio** | **>1.0** (Often >1.0) | <1.0 | Variable |
| **TRH Test (100µg IV)** | **Paradoxical TSH Rise** (>2mIU/L or >50% ↑) | Blunted (No Rise) | Exaggerated (Central Resistance) |
| **RAI Uptake** | Diffuse ↑ | Diffuse ↑ | Normal/↑ |
| **MRI Pituitary** | **Macroadenoma (Usually >10mm)** | Normal | Normal |

---

## 5. Diagnostic Algorithm

```
Thyrotoxicosis (fT4/fT3 ↑ + Symptoms)
         │
         ▼
TSH LEVEL
         │
         ├── TSH SUPPRESSED (<0.01) → **GRAVES DISEASE** (TRAb+)
         │
         └── TSH NORMAL / HIGH → CENTRAL HYPERTHYROIDISM
                 │
                 ├── TRAb POSITIVE → GRAVES (Rare Co-Existence)
                 │
                 ├── TRAb NEGATIVE + **TSH NORMAL/HIGH**
                 │       │
                 │       ├── **TRH TEST: PARADOXICAL TSH RISE** → TSH-OMA
                 │       │
                 │       └── **MRI PITUITARY** → Macroadenoma (>10mm Usually)
                 │
                 └── **THRA MUTATION (RTH)** → TSH Normal/High + fT4/fT3 ↑ + TRAb Negative + FHx
```

---

## 6. Diagnostic Criteria (Consensus)

| Criterion | TSH-oma |
|-----------|---------|
| **TSH** | **Inappropriately Normal/High** (≥1.0 mIU/L) with fT4/fT3 ↑ |
| **fT4/fT3** | **Elevated** |
| **TRAb** | **Negative** |
| **α-Subunit** | **Elevated** (Often >TSH) |
| **α-Subunit/TSH Ratio** | **>1.0** (Often >>1.0) |
| **TRH Test** | **Paradoxical TSH Rise** (>2 mIU/L or >50% Rise) |
| **MRI Pituitary** | **Macroadenoma** (>10mm) Usually |

---

## 7. TRH Stimulation Test

| Protocol | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Dose** | TRH 100-200µg IV Bolus |
| **Sampling** | TSH at 0, 20, 40, 60, 90, 120min |
| **Normal Response** | TSH Peak 5-15 mIU/L at 20-30min; Return to Baseline by 60-120min |
| **TSH-oma Response** | **Paradoxical Rise** → TSH ↑↑ (Often >50% Rise or >2 mIU/L Above Baseline) |
| **Graves** | Blunted/No Rise (TSH Suppressed) |
| **RTH** | Exaggerated Rise (Central Resistance) |

---

## 8. Diagnosis & Localisation

### MRI Pituitary (Gold Standard)
| Feature | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| **Lesion** | **Macroadenoma** (>10mm) in >90% |
| **Enhancement** | **Hypointense on T1 Post-Gad** (Delayed Enhancement vs Normal Pituitary) |
| **Extension** | Suprasellar Extension, Cavernous Sinus Invasion (Knosp Grading) |

### α-Subunit
| Feature | Significance |
|---------|------------|
| **Elevated** | Co-Secreted with TSH by Thyrotroph Adenoma |
| **α-Subunit/TSH Ratio** | **>1.0** (Often >>1.0) — Highly Suggestive |

---

## 9. Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | TSH | fT4/fT3 | TRAb | α-Subunit | TRH Test |
|---------|-----|---------|------|------------|
| **TSH-oma** | **Normal/High** | **High** | **Negative** | **High** | **Paradoxical Rise** |
| **Graves** | **Suppressed (<0.01)** | High | **Positive** | Normal | Blunted |
| **RTH (THRβ)** | Normal/High | High | Negative | Normal | Exaggerated Rise |
| **T3 Thyrotoxicosis** | Suppressed | fT3↑, fT4 Normal | Negative | Normal | Blunted |

---

## 10. Management

### First-Line: Transsphenoidal Surgery (TSS)
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Indication** | **All TSH-omas** (Micro/Macro) |
| **Approach** | **Endoscopic Endonasal TSS** (Preferred) |
| **Goal** | **Gross Total Resection** (GTR) |
| **Cure Criteria** | **TSH Normalisation + fT4/fT3 Normalisation** |

### Surgery Outcomes
| Tumour Size | GTR Rate | Remission Rate |
|-------------|----------|----------------|
| **Microadenoma (<10mm)** | 80-90% | 70-80% |
| **Macroadenoma (>10mm)** | 50-70% | 40-60% |

### Pre-Op Medical Preparation
| Agent | Dose | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Somatostatin Analogues** (Octreotide/Lanreotide) | Standard Doses | **Pre-Op** ↓ TSH → ↓ Vascularity; ↑ Resection Rate |
| **Beta-Blockers** | Propranolol 40-80mg QDS | Symptomatic Control (Tachycardia) |
| **Antithyroid Drugs** | **NOT Routine** (May Worsen Goitre) |  |

---

## 11. Medical Therapy (If Surgery Not Feasible / Residual Disease)

### Somatostatin Analogues (1st Line Medical)
| Agent | Dose | Regimen |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Octreotide LAR** | 20-40mg IM | q4 Weeks |
| **Lanreotide Autogel** | 60-120mg SC | q4 Weeks |
| **Pasireotide LAR** | 40-60mg IM | q4 Weeks (2nd Line; ↑ Hyperglycaemia Risk) |

| Effect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **TSH Suppression** | 50-70% Normalise TSH/fT4 |
| **Tumour Shrinkage** | 30-50% Volume Reduction |
| **α-Subunit** | Normalises with TSH |

### Dopamine Agonists (Adjunct)
| Drug | Dose | Efficacy |
|------|------|---------|
| **Cabergoline** | 0.5-2mg BD | Modest TSH Suppression (30-40%) |
| **Bromocriptine** | 2.5-15mg/day | Less Effective |

---

## 12. Radiotherapy

| Indication | Modality | Dose |
|------------|----------|------|
| **Residual/Recurrent Post-TSS** | **Stereotactic Radiosurgery (SRS)** | 15-25 Gy (Single Fraction) |
| **Unresectable / Recurrent** | **Fractionated RT** | **45-50 Gy / 25-28 Fractions** |
| **Latency** | **5-10 Years** for Biochemical Control |

---

## 13. Post-Operative Follow-up

| Timepoint | Investigations |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Day 1** | TSH, fT4, fT3, α-Subunit |
| **Week 1-2** | TSH, fT4, fT3, α-Subunit |
| **6 Weeks** | TSH, fT4, fT3, α-Subunit, MRI |
| **6 Months** | Full Panel + MRI |
| **Annual (Lifelong)** | TSH, fT4, fT3, α-Subunit, MRI (Yearly ×5yr, then 2-3yrly) |

### Remission Criteria
| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| **TSH** | **Normalised** (Within Reference Range) |
| **fT4/fT3** | **Normal** |
| **α-Subunit** | **Normalised** |

## 15. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **TSH-oma vs Graves** | TSH-oma: TSH Normal/High + TRAb(-); Graves: TSH Suppressed + TRAb+ |
| **TSH-oma vs RTH** | TSH-oma: Pituitary Adenoma; RTH: THRβ Mutation, No Pituitary Mass |
| **TRH Test** | TSH-oma: ↑ TSH (Paradoxical); Graves: Blunted; RTH: Exaggerated |
| **α-Subunit Ratio** | **α/TSH >1.0** = TSH-oma; <1.0 = Normal/Other |
| **α-Subunit Source** | Co-Secreted with TSH by Thyrotroph Adenoma |
| **Medical Rx** | **Somatostatin Analogues 1st Line** (Octreotide/Lanreotide) |
| **Cabergoline** | Modest TSH Suppression (30-40%); Adjunct |
| **Radiotherapy** | Delayed Effect (5-10yr); For Residual/Recurrent |
