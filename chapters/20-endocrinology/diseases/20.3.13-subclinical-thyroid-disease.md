# Subclinical Thyroid Disease

> [!info]
> **Subclinical Thyroid Dysfunction = Abnormal TSH with Normal fT4/fT3.** **Subclinical Hypothyroidism: TSH 4.5-10 (or >10), fT4 Normal.** **Subclinical Hyperthyroidism: TSH <0.1-0.4, fT4/fT3 Normal.** High progression risk if TSH >10 or antibodies positive.

## 2. Definitions & Diagnostic Criteria

### Subclinical Hypothyroidism (SCH)
| TSH Range | fT4 | Classification |
|-----------|-----|----------------|
| **4.5-10 mIU/L** | Normal | **Mild SCH** |
| **>10 mIU/L** | Normal | **Significant SCH** (High progression risk) |

**Diagnostic Criteria**: TSH > Upper Reference Limit (usually 4.0-4.5 mIU/L) + **Normal fT4/fT3** on ≥2 occasions 3-6 months apart.

### Subclinical Hyperthyroidism (SCHyper)
| TSH Range | fT4/fT3 | Classification |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| **0.1-0.4 mIU/L** | Normal | **Mild** |
| **<0.1 mIU/L** | Normal | **Significant** (High progression risk) |

**Diagnostic Criteria**: TSH < Lower Reference Limit (usually <0.4 mIU/L) + **Normal fT4/fT3** on ≥2 occasions.

---

## 3. Subclinical Hypothyroidism (SCH)

### Epidemiology & Progression
| TSH Range | Prevalence | Annual Progression to Overt Hypothyroidism |
|-----------|------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **4.5-10 mIU/L, Ab Negative** | ~5% population | **2-3%/yr** |
| **4.5-10 mIU/L, TPOAb+** | ~3% population | **5-10%/yr** |
| **>10 mIU/L, Any Ab Status** | ~1-2% population | **10-20%/yr** |

**Key Predictors of Progression**:
- TPOAb positivity (HR 8-10)
- Higher baseline TSH (>10 mIU/L)
- Goitre presence
- Female sex

---

## 4. Subclinical Hyperthyroidism (SCHyper)

| TSH Range | Prevalence | Annual Progression to Overt Hyperthyroidism |
|-----------|------------|---------------------------------------------|
| **0.1-0.4 mIU/L** | ~1-2% population | **1-2%/yr** |
| **<0.1 mIU/L** | <1% population | **5-10%/yr** |

**Risk Factors for Progression**: TSH <0.1, Nodular Goitre, Elderly, TPOAb+.

---

## 5. Management — Subclinical Hypothyroidism (SCH)

### Treatment Decision Algorithm
```
Subclinical Hypothyroidism (TSH 4.5-10, fT4 Normal)
         │
         ├── TSH >10 mIU/L
         │       → **TREAT** (Levothyroxine 25-50µg/day)
         │       (High progression risk: 10-20%/yr; CV risk reduction)
         │
         ├── TSH 4.5-10 mIU/L
         │       ├── TPOAb POSITIVE
         │       │       → **TREAT** (Levothyroxine 25-50µg/day)
         │       │       (Progression risk 5-10%/yr)
         │       │
         │       ├── PREGNANT / PLANNING PREGNANCY
         │       │       → **TREAT** (Target Trimester-Specific TSH)
         │       │
         │       ├── LIPIDS ↑ / CARDIAC RISK / SYMPTOMS
         │       │       → **TREAT** (Levothyroxine 25-50µg/day)
         │       │
         │       └── ASYMPTOMATIC, Ab NEGATIVE, NO RISK FACTORS
         │               → **OBSERVE** (TSH/fT4 q6-12mo)
         │
         └── ELDERLY (>70-80y) / FRAIL / COMORBIDITIES
                 → **RELAXED TARGET** (TSH up to 5-7 mIU/L acceptable)
                 Avoid overtreatment (AF, falls, fracture risk)
```

### Levothyroxine Dosing for SCH
| Population | Starting Dose | Titration |
|------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Young/Healthy** | 25-50µg/day | Titrate by 25µg q6-8wk to TSH 0.5-2.5 |
| **Elderly/Frail** | 12.5-25µg/day | Titrate slowly (12.5µg q8-12wk) |
| **Cardiac Disease** | 12.5-25µg/day | Cautious; Monitor ECG/BP |

## 6. Subclinical Hyperthyroidism — Management

### Treatment Thresholds
| TSH Range | Age / Risk Factors | Action |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| **<0.1 mIU/L** | **All ages** | **TREAT** (High progression risk 5-10%/yr) |
| **0.1-0.4 mIU/L** | **Age >65** or **Cardiac Risk / Osteoporosis / Symptoms** | **TREAT** |
| **0.1-0.4 mIU/L** | **Age <65, Asymptomatic, No Risk Factors** | **OBSERVE** (TSH/fT4 q6mo) |

### Treatment Options
| Aetiology | Preferred Treatment |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Graves Disease** | Carbimazole (Low Dose 5-10mg/day) OR RAI |
| **Toxic MNG** | **RAI (Definitive)** |
| **Toxic Adenoma** | **Surgery (Hemithyroidectomy)** |
| **All** | **β-Blocker** for Symptomatic Control |

## 7. Special Populations

### Pregnancy — Trimester-Specific Targets
| Trimester | TSH Upper Limit | Action if TSH > Limit |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **1st Trimester** | **2.5 mIU/L** | Treat if TSH >2.5 |
| **2nd Trimester** | **3.0 mIU/L** | Treat if TSH >3.0 |
| **3rd Trimester** | **3.0 mIU/L** | Treat if TSH >3.0 |

**SCH in Pregnancy**:
- **Treat all SCH** (TSH > Trimester-Specific Limit)
- **Levothyroxine 25-50µg/day** → Titrate to Trimester-Specific Target
- **Increase Dose ~30% at Positive Test**

**Subclinical Hyperthyroidism in Pregnancy**:
- **Rare**; Usually Graves; **PTU 1st Trimester** if Treatment Needed
- β-Blockers Preferred Over ATDs for Symptomatic Control

## 8. Elderly (>65-70 Years)

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **TSH Target** | **0.5-5.0** (Up to 7.0 if Frail/Comorbidities) |
| **SCH Management** | **Relaxed Target** (TSH up to 5-7 acceptable) |
| **Rationale** | Avoid Overtreatment → AF, Falls, Fractures, Osteoporosis |
| **Treatment Threshold** | TSH >7-10 (Symptomatic) or >10 (Asymptomatic) |

---

## 9. Cardiovascular Risk

| Condition | CV Risk | Evidence |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| **SCH (TSH >10)** | **↑ CHD Events, HF, CV Mortality** | Meta-analyses; Treat if TSH >10 |
| **SCH (TSH 4.5-10)** | **Modest ↑ CHD** (If TPOAb+ or Cardiac Risk) | Controversial; Treat if Risk Factors |
| **Subclinical Hyper (TSH <0.1)** | **↑ AF (3-5x), HF, Osteoporosis** | Treat if TSH <0.1 or Age >65 |

## 12. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **SCH vs Overt** | SCH = TSH Abnormal + fT4 **Normal**; Overt = TSH High + fT4 **Low** |
| **TSH >10 vs 4.5-10** | >10 = Treat Always; 4.5-10 = Treat if Ab+/Pregnant/Lipids/Symptoms |
| **Elderly TSH** | Relaxed Target (Up to 5-7) — Avoid Overtreatment (AF, Falls, Fracture) |
| **Subclinical Hyper TSH <0.1** | Always Treat (High Progression Risk); 0.1-0.4 → Treat if Risk Factors |
| **Pregnancy SCH** | Treat if TSH >2.5 (1st) / >3.0 (2nd/3rd) |
| **TSH Targets** | Adult 0.4-4.0; Elderly 0.5-5.0; Pregnancy Trimester-Specific |

**Mnemonic: SCH TREATMENT**
- **S**ubclinical **H**ypothyroidism
- **C**heck **T**SH: >10 = Treat; 4.5-10 = Ab+/Preg/CVD → Treat
- **P**regnancy = Treat All (TSH >2.5/3.0)
- **E**lderly = Relax (TSH up to 5-7 OK)
- **A**ntibody +ve = Treat
- **M**onitoring = TSH q6-12mo (Obs) or q6-8wk (Rx)
