# Endocrine Management in CKD and Dialysis

> [!info]
> **CKD = Systemic Endocrine Disorder.** **CKD-MBD (Mineral and Bone Disorder)** is universal in G4-5. **Glucose, Adrenal, Thyroid, Gonadal Axes All Affected.** **Dialysis Adds Unique Challenges** (Clearance, Fluid Shifts, Catabolism).

## 2. CKD-Mineral and Bone Disorder (CKD-MBD) — KDIGO 2024

### Pathophysiology — The Vicious Cycle
```
CKD ↓ GFR
    │
    → Phosphate Retention → FGF23 ↑ → 1α-Hydroxylase Inhibition → 1,25-(OH)₂D ↓
    │
    → Hypocalcaemia → PTH ↑↑ (Secondary HPT)
    │
    → Bone Resorption ↑ → High Turnover Bone Disease
    │
    → Vascular Calcification (Ca × P > 4.5)
    │
    → Adynamic Bone (Over-Suppression) ↔ High Turnover (High PTH)
```

### KDIGO 2024 Targets (CKD G3-5D)

| Parameter | Target (CKD 3-5D) | Notes |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| **PTH** | **2-9x ULN** (Avoid <2x = Adynamic) | Dialysis: 2-9x ULN |
| **Serum Calcium** | **2.1-2.5 mmol/L** (Corrected) | Avoid >2.6; Avoid <2.1 |
| **Phosphate** | **0.8-1.5 mmol/L** (G3-4) | Dialysis: 1.1-1.5 mmol/L |
| **Ca × P Product** | **<4.5 mmol²/L²** | <4.5 = Lower CV Risk |
| **25-OH Vitamin D** | **≥30 ng/mL (75 nmol/L)** | Supplement if Low |
| **Alkaline Phosphatase** | Monitor Trend | Marker of Bone Turnover |

## 3. CKD-MBD Management Algorithm (KDIGO 2024)

```
CKD G3-5 / Dialysis + Abnormal Ca/Phos/PTH
         │
         ▼
STEP 1: DIETARY PHOSPHATE RESTRICTION (800-1000 mg/day)
         │
         ▼
STEP 2: PHOSPHATE BINDERS (With Meals)
         ├── CALCIUM-BASED (Carbonate/Acetate)
         │       1st Line if Ca²⁺ Normal/Low; Cheap
         │       Max 1.5-2g Elemental Ca/day
         │
         ├── NON-CALCIUM (Sevelamer, Lanthanum, Sucroferric Oxyhydroxide, Ferric Citrate)
         │       1st Line if Ca²⁺ High / Vascular Calcification / Adynamic Bone
         │       Sevelamer 800-2400mg TDS; Lanthanum 750-2250mg/day
         │
         └── FERRIC CITRATE (Newer; Also Treats Anaemia)
                 Dose: 1g TDS with Meals
         │
         ▼
STEP 3: VITAMIN D ANALOGUES (If PTH >9x ULN Despite Phosphate Control)
         ├── ALFACALCIDOL (1α-OH D3) 0.25-1µg/day
         ├── CALCITRIOL 0.25-1µg/day
         └── PARICALCITOL (Selective VDR) 1-2µg 3x/week (Post-HD)
         │   Advantage: Less Hypercalcaemia/Hyperphosphataemia
         │
         ▼
STEP 4: CALCIMIMETICS (If PTH >9x ULN Despite Above)
         │
         ├── CINACALCET 30-180mg/day (BD) — CaSR Agonist
         │       Monitor: Ca²⁺, PTH q1-2mo; Hypocalcaemia Risk
         └── ETELCALCETIDE (IV, Post-HD) 5-15mg 3x/week (HD Only)
                 Monitor Ca²⁺, PTH Monthly
```

## 4. Phosphate Binder Selection Algorithm

| Patient Profile | 1st Line Binder | Alternative |
|------------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Normal Calcium, No Vascular Calcification** | **Calcium Carbonate/Acetate** (Cheap) | — |
| **Hypercalcaemia / Vascular Calcification / Adynamic Bone** | **Sevelamer / Lanthanum / Ferric Citrate** | Sucroferric Oxyhydroxide |
| **Dialysis + Hyperphosphataemia** | **Sevelamer / Lanthanum / Ferric Citrate** | Ferric Citrate (Also Treats Anaemia) |
| **Low Calcium** | **Calcium-Based** (Calcium Carbonate/Acetate) | — |

> **Key Principle**: **Avoid Calcium-Based Binders if Ca²⁺ High or Vascular Calcification Present.**

## 5. Vitamin D Analogues in CKD

| Agent | Indication | Dose | Advantage |
|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Alfacalcidol (1α-OH D3)** | CKD G3-5 | 0.25-1µg/day | Rapid 1α-Hydroxylation; Reliable |
| **Calcitriol (1,25-(OH)₂D₃)** | CKD G4-5, Dialysis | 0.25-1µg/day | Active Form; Short t½ |
| **Paricalcitol** | CKD G4-5D / Dialysis | 1-2µg 3x/week (Post-HD) | **Selective VDR** → Less Hypercalcaemia/Phosphataemia |
| **Doxercalciferol** | CKD G4-5 | 10-30µg TIW | 1α-OH D2 Analogue |

> **Key**: **Nutritional D2/D3 (Cholecalciferol/Ergocalciferol) INSUFFICIENT in CKD G4-5/Dialysis** — Requires Renal 1α-Hydroxylation.

## 7. Adynamic Bone Disease — Over-Suppression

| Feature | Recognition | Management |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **Clue** | PTH **<2x ULN** (or <100 pg/mL) + Low ALP + Low Bone Turnover | **Stop/Reduce Vitamin D Analogues & Calcimimetics** |
| **Risk** | **Fracture ↑** (Low Bone Turnover), ↑ Vascular Calcification | Reduce/Stop Vit D Analogues & Calcimimetics |
| **Diagnosis** | **Bone Biopsy (Gold Standard)**; Low PTH + Low ALP | PTH Target **2-9x ULN** (Not Lower) |

---

## 8. Calciphylaxis (Calcific Uraemic Arteriolopathy)

| Feature | Management |
|---------|-----------|
| **Diagnosis** | Painful Violaceous Skin Lesions → Necrosis/Ulceration; Biopsy (Medial Calcification) |
| **Risk Factors** | Ca×P >4.5, Obesity, Diabetes, Warfarin, High PTH, High Calcium/Phosphate |
| **Emergency Management** | **Urgent Parathyroidectomy** (If Severe SHPT) + Intensive Wound Care |
| **Medical** | **Sodium Thiosulfate** 25g IV 3x/week (Post-HD); **Cinacalcet** (↓ PTH, Ca, Phos); **Bisphosphonates** (IV Zoledronic) |
| **Wound Care** | Multidisciplinary (Nephrology, Dermatology, Surgery, Plastics) |
| **Mortality** | **50-80%** (Sepsis, Sepsis) |

## 9. Glucose Management in CKD/Dialysis

### HbA1c Targets
| Population | Target HbA1c |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Non-Dialysis CKD** | **<53 mmol/mol (7.0%)** (Individualised) |
| **Dialysis** | **<58 mmol/mol (7.5%)** (Higher Due to ↓ RBC Survival) |

### Drug Selection & Dosing (CKD/Dialysis)

| Drug Class | CKD G3 (30-59) | CKD G4 (15-29) | CKD G5 / Dialysis |
|-----------|----------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Metformin** | **Full Dose** (eGFR ≥30) | **Max 1g/day** (eGFR 15-29) | **Contraindicated** (eGFR <15) |
| **SGLT2i** (Dapagliflozin/Empagliflozin) | ✅ Full Dose (eGFR ≥20) | ✅ (eGFR ≥20) | ✅ **HD/PD: YES** (Empagliflozin/Dapagliflozin) |
| **GLP-1 RA** (Semaglutide/Dulaglutide) | ✅ No Adjustment | ✅ No Adjustment | ✅ **Safe** (No Renal Clearance) |
| **DPP-4i** | Linagliptin: No Adjust | Linagliptin: No Adjust | ✅ Linagliptin Safe |
| **Sulfonylureas** | Gliclazide Preferred | **Avoid** (Hypoglycaemia) | **Avoid** |
| **Insulin** | Standard | ↓ Dose (25-50%) | **↓ 30-50%**; Basal Preferred |
| **DPP-4i (Sitagliptin, Vildagliptin, Saxagliptin, Alogliptin)** | Dose Reduce | Dose Reduce | Dose Reduce (Except Linagliptin) |
| **SGLT2i** | **DAPA/EMAPA ≥20** | **DAPA/EMAPA ≥20** | **HD/PD: Yes (DAPA/EMPA)** |

## 12. Gonadal Axis in CKD/Dialysis

| Axis | Changes | Management |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **Male** | ↓ Testosterone (↑ SHBG, ↓ Leydig Function) | TRT If Symptomatic + Low T (<12 nmol/L) |
| **Female** | Menstrual Irregularity, Anovulation | HRT if Indicated; Fertility Counselling |
| **Erectile Dysfunction** | Common | PDE5 Inhibitors (Sildenafil) — Caution with Nitrates |
| **Fertility** | ↓ in Both Sexes | Pre-Dialysis Counselling; Oocyte/Sperm Cryopreservation |

## 22. Flashcards
- **Q: CKD-MBD definition**
  **A: Systemic disorder of mineral/bone metabolism: Ca/P/PTH/Vit D abnormalities + bone disease + vascular calcification**

- **Q: Secondary HPT pathophysiology**
  **A: Phosphate retention → ↓ ionised Ca²⁺ → PTH↑ + ↓ renal 1α-hydroxylase → ↓ calcitriol → ↓ Ca²⁺ absorption → PTH↑↑**

- **Q: FGF23**
  **A: Osteocyte hormone; ↑ early in CKD; inhibits 1α-hydroxylase; promotes phosphaturia; predicts CVD mortality**

- **Q: KDIGO PTH targets**
  **A: CKD 3-5 non-dialysis: 2-9x ULN; Dialysis: 2-9x ULN (individualised)**

- **Q: Phosphate binders**
  **A: Calcium-based (acetate/carbonate) vs non-calcium (sevelamer, lanthanum, sucroferric oxyhydroxide)**

- **Q: Calcitriol/Alfacalcidol**
  **A: Active Vit D analogues; suppress PTH; risk hypercalcaemia/hyperphosphataemia; use if PTH rising despite binders**

- **Q: Cinacalcet**
  **A: Calcimimetic (CaSR agonist); ↓ PTH, ↓ Ca, ↓ P; EVOLVE trial: no CV mortality benefit but ↓ parathyroidectomy**

- **Q: Adynamic bone**
  **A: Low PTH (<100-150), low turnover; ↑ fracture risk; avoid oversuppression, minimise calcium load**

- **Q: Tertiary HPT**
  **A: Autonomous PTH secretion after prolonged secondary HPT; hypercalcaemia; post-transplant may persist**

- **Q: Renal osteodystrophy types**
  **A: High turnover (osteitis fibrosa), Low turnover (adynamic, osteomalacia), Mixed**

- **Q: Transplant HPT**
  **A: Usually resolves 6-12mo; persistent → cinacalcet or parathyroidectomy if severe hypercalcaemia**

- **Q: Vitamin D in CKD**
  **A: Cholecalciferol (nutritional) for 25-OH D <30; Calcitriol/alfacalcidol for secondary HPT with ↑ PTH**

- **Q: Aluminium bone disease**
  **A: Historical: aluminium-containing binders → osteomalacia, adynamic bone, dementia**
