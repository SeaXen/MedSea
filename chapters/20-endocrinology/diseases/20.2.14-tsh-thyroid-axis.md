# TSH/Thyroid Axis (HPT Axis)

> [!info]
> **HPT axis = TRH → TSH → T4/T3.** The most commonly tested endocrine axis in clinical practice. Mastering TSH/fT4/fT3 patterns, TRH physiology, and thyroid dysfunction patterns is essential for FCPS/MRCP.

## 2. HPT Axis — Anatomy & Physiology

### Hormone Synthesis & Secretion

| Step | Hormone | Source | Regulation |
|------|---------|--------|------------|
| **1. TRH** | Thyrotropin-Releasing Hormone | Hypothalamus (PVN) | Cold, Stress ↑; T3/T4 (negative feedback); Somatostatin, Dopamine (inhibitory) |
| **2. TSH** | Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone | Anterior Pituitary (Thyrotrophs) | **TRH** (stimulatory); **T3/T4** (negative feedback, mainly T3 at pituitary); Somatostatin, Dopamine (inhibitory) |
| **3. T4/T3** | Thyroxine (T4) / Triiodothyronine (T3) | Thyroid Follicular Cells | **TSH** (stimulatory); Iodine availability; **Negative feedback** on TRH + TSH |

### Thyroid Hormone Synthesis (Follicular Cell)
```
Iodide Uptake (NIS) → Thyroglobulin Synthesis (RER) → Iodination (TPO + H₂O₂) → MIT + DIT
         ↓
Coupling (TPO) → T4 (2 DIT) / T3 (MIT+DIT) → Stored in Colloid
         ↓
TSH Stimulation → Endocytosis of Colloid → Lysosomal Proteolysis → T4/T3 Released
```

| Step | Enzyme/Process | Clinical Relevance |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Iodide Uptake** | **NIS (Sodium-Iodide Symporter)** | Perchlorate discharge test; NIS mutations → dyshormonogenesis |
| **Iodination** | **TPO (Thyroid Peroxidase)** | **Anti-TPO antibodies** (Hashimoto); PTU inhibits TPO |
| **Coupling** | TPO | TPO mutations → dyshormonogenesis |
| **Proteolysis** | Lysosomal enzymes | Iodine excess inhibits (Wolff-Chaikoff effect) |
| **Deiodination** | **DIO1, DIO2** (activate T4→T3); **DIO3** (inactivate) | Selenium deficiency impairs DIOs; Amiodarone inhibits DIO1 |

**Key Ratios**: **T4:T3 secretion ≈ 10:1**; but **T3 is 4x more potent**. **Peripheral conversion** (DIO1/DIO2) provides ~80% of circulating T3.

## 3. HPT Axis Regulation & Secretion

### Regulation
| Hormone | Primary Stimulus | Primary Inhibitor | Feedback Level |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **TRH** | Cold, Stress | T3/T4, Somatostatin, Dopamine | Pituitary + Hypothalamus |
| **TSH** | TRH | T3/T4, Somatostatin, Dopamine | Pituitary + Hypothalamus |
| **T4/T3** | TSH | Iodine deficiency, Wolff-Chaikoff | Pituitary + Hypothalamus |

### Secretion Patterns
| Hormone | Pattern | Key Features |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **TRH** | Pulsatile | Cold exposure ↑; T3/T4 negative feedback |
| **TSH** | **Pulsatile**, **Diurnal peak 02:00-04:00**, nadir 17:00-18:00 | Sleep, Cold ↑; T3/T4 negative feedback |
| **T4** | Stable, long half-life (5-7 days) | Bound to TBG (99.97%), Albumin, Transthyretin |
| **T3** | Shorter half-life (1 day) | 99.7% bound; 80% from peripheral T4→T3 conversion |

## 4. Thyroid Function Test Interpretation — Pattern Recognition

### Standard Patterns

| Pattern | TSH | fT4 | fT3 | Interpretation |
|---------|-----|-----|-----|----------------|
| **Primary Hyperthyroidism** | **↓↓ (suppressed)** | ↑ | ↑↑ | Graves, Toxic nodule, Thyroiditis |
| **Primary Hypothyroidism** | **↑↑** | ↓ | ↓/N | Hashimoto, Post-ablation, Iodine deficiency |
| **Central Hypothyroidism** | **↓/N (inappropriately low)** | ↓↓ | ↓ | Pituitary/Hypothalamic disease |
| **Subclinical Hyperthyroidism** | **↓↓** | Normal | Normal | Early Graves, Toxic nodule, Excess thyroxine |
| **Subclinical Hypothyroidism** | **↑** | Normal | Normal | Early Hashimoto |
| **T3 Thyrotoxicosis** | **↓↓** | Normal | **↑** | Early Graves, T3-secreting nodule |
| **Sick Euthyroid Syndrome** | N/↓/↑ | N/↓ | ↓ | Non-thyroidal illness |

## 5. Diagnostic Patterns — Quick Reference

### Primary Hyperthyroidism
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **TSH** | **Suppressed (<0.01 mIU/L)** |
| **fT4/fT3** | **Elevated** |
| **Aetiology** | Graves (TRAb+), Toxic Nodular Goitre, Thyroiditis, Drug-induced (Amiodarone) |
| **TRAb** | Positive in Graves (>90%) |

### Primary Hypothyroidism
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **TSH** | **Elevated (>10 mIU/L = overt)** |
| **fT4** | **Low** |
| **Aetiology** | Hashimoto (Anti-TPO/TgAb+), Post-ablation, Iodine deficiency, Drug-induced |
| **Anti-TPO/TgAb** | Positive in Hashimoto (>90%) |

### Central (Secondary/Tertiary) Hypothyroidism
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **TSH** | **Low or Inappropriately Normal** (not elevated despite low fT4) |
| **fT4** | **Low** |
| **Aetiology** | Pituitary adenoma, Surgery, Radiotherapy, Infiltration, Sheehan syndrome |

### Subclinical Thyroid Disease
| Condition | TSH | fT4/fT3 | Management |
|-----------|-----|---------|------------|
| **Subclinical Hyperthyroidism** | **<0.1 mIU/L** | Normal | Treat if TSH <0.1 + Age>65/Cardiac risk/Osteoporosis |
| **Subclinical Hypothyroidism** | **4.5-10 mIU/L** | Normal | Treat if TSH >10 or 5-10 + Ab+/Pregnant/Lipids/Symptoms |

## 6. TRH Stimulation Test

| Protocol | TRH 200-500µg IV → TSH at 0, 20, 40, 60, 90, 120min |
|----------|------------------------------------------------------|
| **Hyperthyroidism** | **Blunted TSH** (suppressed) |
| **Primary Hypothyroidism** | **Exaggerated/Prolonged TSH** rise |
| **Central Hypothyroidism** | **Blunted/Delayed** TSH |
| **TSH-oma** | **Paradoxical Rise** in TSH |

## 7. Thyroxine (T4) vs Triiodothyronine (T3) — Key Differences

| Property | **T4 (Thyroxine)** | **T3 (Triiodothyronine)** |
|----------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **Secretion Ratio** | **10:1** (T4:T3) | Minor direct secretion |
| **Potency** | 1x | **4x more potent** |
| **Half-life** | **5-7 days** | **1 day** |
| **Protein Binding** | TBG 99.97%, Albumin, Transthyretin | TBG 99.7%, Albumin |
| **Source** | **90% direct thyroid secretion** | **80% peripheral conversion** (DIO1/DIO2) |
| **Activity** | Prohormone | **Active hormone** |

**Clinical Pearl**: **T3 is the active hormone**; T4 is a prohormone. In illness (NTIS), T4→T3 conversion impaired → Low T3, Normal/High T4, Normal/Low TSH.

## 8. Thyroid Function in Pregnancy

### Physiological Changes
| Change | Mechanism | Clinical Impact |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **↑ TBG** (2-3x) | Oestrogen ↑ TBG synthesis | ↑ Total T4/T3; **fT4/fT3 reliable** |
| **hCG Cross-reactivity** | hCG = weak TSH agonist | **TSH ↓ 1st trimester** (often <0.1) |
| **Iodine Clearance** | ↑ Renal iodine excretion | ↑ Iodine requirement (250 µg/day) |
| **Placental Transfer** | T4 crosses (limited); T3 crosses poorly | Fetal brain depends on maternal T4 in 1st trimester |

### TSH Targets in Pregnancy
| Trimester | TSH Target (mIU/L) | Action |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| **Pre-conception** | **<2.5** | Optimise Levothyroxine |
| **1st (0-12w)** | **0.1-2.5** | ↑ Dose ~30% at +ve test; Monitor q4w |
| **2nd (13-26w)** | **0.2-3.0** | Monitor q4-6w |
| **3rd (27w+)** | **0.3-3.0** | Monitor q4-6w; Plan postpartum reduction |
| **Postpartum** | Pre-pregnancy dose | Reduce to pre-pregnancy; Check TSH 6wk |

## 9. Anti-Thyroid Antibodies

| Antibody | Target | Conditions | Clinical Utility |
|----------|--------|------------|------------------|
| **Anti-TPO** | Thyroid Peroxidase | **Hashimoto (>90%)**, Graves (80%), Postpartum thyroiditis | **Diagnosis of autoimmune thyroid disease** |
| **Anti-Tg** | Thyroglobulin | Hashimoto, Post-thyroidectomy monitoring | Tg monitoring after thyroid cancer |
| **TRAb (TSHR-Ab)** | TSH Receptor (Stimulating) | **Graves Disease (>95%)** | **Diagnosis of Graves**; Predict neonatal thyrotoxicosis |
| **TBII** | TSH Receptor (Binding/Blocking) | Graves, Atrophic thyroiditis | Alternative to TRAb |

## 10. Thyroid Nodule — TI-RADS & Bethesda

### TI-RADS (ACR) — Ultrasound Risk Stratification
| TR Level | Features | Malignancy Risk | Action |
|----------|----------|-----------------|--------|
| **TR1** | Benign (cystic, spongiform) | ~0% | No FNA |
| **TR2** | Not suspicious | <2% | No FNA |
| **TR3** | Mildly suspicious | 2-5% | FNA if ≥2.5 cm |
| **TR4** | Moderately suspicious | 5-20% | FNA if ≥1.5 cm |
| **TR5** | Highly suspicious | >20% | FNA if ≥1 cm |

### Bethesda System — FNA Cytology
| Category | Diagnosis | Malignancy Risk | Management |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| **I** | Non-diagnostic | — | Repeat FNA |
| **II** | Benign | 0-3% | Surveillance |
| **III** | AUS/FLUS | 10-30% | Repeat FNA / Molecular |
| **IV** | FN/SFN | 15-30% | Diagnostic Lobectomy / Molecular |
| **V** | Suspicious | 60-75% | Near-Total Thyroidectomy |
| **VI** | Malignant | 97-99% | Total Thyroidectomy ± RAI |

## 12. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **TSH ↑ = Hypothyroidism?** | **YES** if fT4 low (Primary). **NO** if fT4 also low → Central (TSH inappropriately normal) |
| **TSH Suppressed = Hyperthyroidism?** | **YES** if fT4/fT3 high. **NO** if fT4/fT3 normal → Subclinical |
| **Central vs Primary Hypo** | Central: TSH **Low/Normal** + fT4 Low; Primary: TSH **High** + fT4 Low |
| **Subclinical vs Overt** | Subclinical = TSH abnormal + fT4/fT3 **Normal**; Overt = TSH abnormal + fT4/fT3 **Abnormal** |
| **Pregnancy TSH** | **Normal 1st tri TSH can be <0.1** (hCG effect) — Assess with fT4 |
| **TSH in Pregnancy** | Target: 1st tri 0.1-2.5; 2nd: 0.2-3.0; 3rd: 0.3-3.0 mIU/L |
| **Sick Euthyroid** | Do **NOT** treat — Self-resolving with recovery from illness |
| **Biotin Interference** | High-dose Biotin → **Falsely Low TSH**, Falsely High fT4/fT3 — Stop 48h before test |

**Mnemonic: THYROID PATTERNS**
- **P**rimary **H**ypo: **T**SH **H**igh, **f**T4 **L**ow
- **C**entral **H**ypo: **T**SH **L**ow/**N**ormal, **f**T4 **L**ow
- **G**raves: **T**SH **L**ow, **f**T4 **H**igh, **T**R**A**b **P**ositive
- **S**ubclinical **H**ypo: **T**SH 5-10, **f**T4 **N**ormal
- **S**ubclinical **H**yper: **T**SH <0.1, **f**T4 **N**ormal

**Mnemonic: THYROID IN PREGNANCY**
- **1**st Tri: TSH 0.1-2.5 (hCG ↓ TSH)
- **2**nd Tri: TSH 0.2-3.0
- **3**rd Tri: TSH 0.3-3.0
- **L**evothyroxine: **Increase 30%** at +ve test
