# FSH/LH/Gonadal Axis (HPG Axis)

> [!info]
> **HPG axis = GnRH → FSH/LH → Oestradiol/Testosterone.** Central to puberty, reproduction, gonadal function, and sex steroid feedback. Dysregulation → Hypogonadism, PCOS, precocious/delayed puberty, infertility.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Describe the HPG axis anatomy, hormone synthesis, and regulation
- [ ] Interpret FSH/LH patterns for primary vs secondary hypogonadism
- [ ] Apply GnRH stimulation and sex steroid feedback tests
- [ ] Diagnose and manage hypogonadism, PCOS, and puberty disorders
- [ ] Interpret inhibin B, anti-Müllerian hormone (AMH) in fertility assessment

## 2. HPG Axis — Anatomy & Physiology

### Hormone Synthesis & Secretion

| Step | Hormone | Source | Regulation |
|------|---------|--------|------------|
| **1. GnRH** | Gonadotropin-Releasing Hormone | Hypothalamus (Preoptic Area) | Pulsatile; **Kisspeptin** (key upstream); Sex steroids (negative/positive feedback) |
| **2. FSH** | Follicle-Stimulating Hormone | Anterior Pituitary (Gonadotrophs) | **GnRH** (stimulatory); **Inhibin B** (selective negative feedback); Oestradiol/Testosterone (general) |
| **3. LH** | Luteinizing Hormone | Anterior Pituitary (Gonadotrophs) | **GnRH** (stimulatory); Oestradiol/Testosterone (negative/positive feedback) |
| **4. Sex Steroids** | Oestradiol / Testosterone | Gonads (Ovary: Theca+Granulosa; Testis: Leydig) | **LH/FSH** (stimulatory); **Negative feedback** on GnRH/LH/FSH; **Inhibin B** (FSH specific) |

### Hormone Structure
| Hormone | Structure | Shared Subunit |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| **FSH** | α-subunit (shared) + **β-subunit (FSH-specific)** | α-subunit (shared with LH, TSH, hCG) |
| **LH** | α-subunit (shared) + **β-subunit (LH-specific)** | α-subunit (shared with FSH, TSH, hCG) |
| **hCG** | α-subunit + β-subunit | Similar to LH (cross-reacts with LH receptor) |

---

## 2. HPG Axis Regulation

### Feedback Loops
```
GnRH (Hypothalamus) → FSH + LH (Pituitary) → Oestradiol/Testosterone (Gonads)
    ↑_______________|________________|
          Negative Feedback (Oestradiol/Testosterone + Inhibin)
          Positive Feedback (Oestradiol → LH Surge)
```

### Feedback Patterns
| Gonadal Status | LH/FSH | Oestradiol/Testosterone | Inhibin B | Key Features |
|----------------|--------|------------------------|-----------|--------------|
| **Primary Hypogonadism** | **↑↑** | ↓ | ↓ | Gonadal failure |
| **Secondary Hypogonadism** | **↓/N** | ↓ | N/↓ | Pituitary/Hypothalamic failure |
| **PCOS** | **LH ↑↑, FSH N/↓** (LH:FSH >2:1) | Testosterone ↑ | N/↓ | Ovarian resistance + Insulin resistance |
| **Menopause** | **↑↑** | ↓↓ (Oestradiol) | ↓ | Ovarian failure |
| **Pregnancy** | Suppressed | Oestradiol/Progesterone ↑↑ | ↓ | High oestrogen/progesterone negative feedback |
| **Ageing Male** | Slight ↑ | Gradual ↓ | Slight ↓ | Late-onset hypogonadism |

## 3. Gonadotropin Physiology

### GnRH
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Source** | Hypothalamus (Medial preoptic area) |
| **Secretion** | **Pulsatile** (every 60-90 min); **Frequency encodes signal** |
| **Regulation** | **Kisspeptin** (Key upstream stimulator); Sex steroids (Negative/Positive feedback); Stress, Energy status, Leptin (modulatory) |
| **Pulse Frequency** | **Low (1-2/h) → FSH dominance**; **High (1/30-60min) → LH dominance** |

### FSH
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Structure** | α-subunit (shared) + **β-subunit (FSH-specific)** |
| **Primary Regulator** | **GnRH** (frequency-dependent); **Inhibin B** (selective negative feedback) |
| **Actions — Female** | **Folliculogenesis** (Granulosa cells); Oestradiol synthesis (with LH); **Inhibin B** production |
| **Actions — Male** | **Spermatogenesis** (Sertoli cells); Inhibin B production |
| **Regulation** | **Selective negative feedback by Inhibin B** (Granulosa/Sertoli cells) |

### LH
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Structure** | α-subunit (shared) + **β-subunit (LH-specific)** |
| **Primary Regulator** | **GnRH** (frequency-dependent); Oestradiol/Testosterone (negative/positive feedback) |
| **Actions — Female** | **Theca cells** → Androgen synthesis → Granulosa → Oestradiol; **Ovulation** (LH surge); Corpus luteum → Progesterone |
| **Actions — Male** | **Leydig cells** → **Testosterone** production |
| **Positive Feedback** | **Sustained Oestradiol >200 pg/mL × 50h** → **LH Surge** (Ovulation) |

## 3. Gonadal Steroid Physiology

### Testosterone
| Feature | Male | Female |
|---------|------|--------|
| **Source** | Leydig cells (95%), Adrenal (5%) | Ovary (Theca), Adrenal |
| **Binding** | **SHBG** ~60%, Albumin ~38%, Free ~2% | SHBG ↑ in women |
| **Metabolism** | 5α-Reductase → DHT (potent); Aromatase → Oestradiol | Aromatisation |
| **Regulation** | LH → Leydig cells → Testosterone; Negative feedback on LH/FSH | Part of ovarian steroidogenesis |

### Oestradiol
| Feature | Female | Male |
|---------|--------|------|
| **Source** | Ovary (Granulosa: Aromatisation of Androstenedione) | Testis (Sertoli), Adipose (Aromatase) |
| **Binding** | SHBG ~60%, Albumin ~38%, Free ~2% | SHBG ~60% |
| **Regulation** | FSH + LH → Theca Androgens → Granulosa Aromatase → Oestradiol | Aromatase in Adipose, Brain, Bone |

## 4. HPG Axis Patterns — Diagnostic Key

### Hypogonadism Classification

| Type | LH/FSH | Testosterone/Oestradiol | Inhibin B/AMH | Common Causes |
|------|--------|------------------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Primary (Hypergonadotropic)** | **↑↑** | ↓ | ↓ | Klinefelter, Turner, Mumps, Chemo, Autoimmune, Genetic |
| **Secondary (Hypogonadotropic)** | **↓/N** | ↓ | N/↓ | Pituitary adenoma, Hyperprolactinaemia, Kallmann, Functional |
| **Functional (Stress/Weight/Exercise)** | ↓/N | ↓/N | ↓ | Anorexia, Athletics, Stress, Obesity |

### Male Hypogonadism — Key Patterns
| Type | Testosterone | LH/FSH | Clinical Features |
|------|--------------|--------|-------------------|
| **Primary** | <12 nmol/L | **↑↑** | Klinefelter, Mumps, Chemo, Autoimmune, Ageing |
| **Secondary** | <12 nmol/L | **↓/N** | Pituitary adenoma, Hyperprolactinaemia, Kallmann, Obesity |
| **Late-Onset (LOH)** | 8-12 nmol/L | N/↑ | Ageing, Obesity, Comorbidities |

### Female Hypogonadism — Key Patterns
| Type | Oestradiol | LH/FSH | Clinical Features |
|------|------------|--------|-------------------|
| **POI (<40y)** | ↓ | **↑↑** | Turner, FMR1, Autoimmune, Chemo, Idiopathic |
| **Secondary Amenorrhoea** | ↓ | **↓/N** | PCOS, Hyperprolactinaemia, Functional, Thyroid |
| **Menopause** | ↓↓ | **↑↑** | Natural ovarian failure |

## 4. PCOS — Rotterdam Criteria (2 of 3)

| Criterion | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **1. Oligo/anovulation** | <8 cycles/year or Cycle >35 days |
| **2. Hyperandrogenism** | Clinical (Hirsutism, Acne, Alopecia) **OR** Biochemical (Testosterone >2.5-3 nmol/L, FAI >5) |
| **3. Polycystic Ovaries (US)** | **≥20 follicles (2-9mm) per ovary** OR Ovarian volume >10 mL |

**Must Exclude**: CAH (17-OHP), Androgen-secreting tumours, Cushing, Thyroid disease, Hyperprolactinaemia.

---

## 4. PCOS — Management

| Target | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| **Menstrual Regulation** | COCP (Oestradiol + Cyproterone/Drospirenone) **OR** Cyclical Progesterone (Medroxyprogesterone 10mg 10d/month) |
| **Hirsutism/Acne** | COCP → Add Anti-androgen (Spironolactone 50-100mg, Cyproterone, Flutamide) + Topical Eflornithine |
| **Metabolic/IR** | **Metformin 500mg BD → 1g BD** (if BMI ≥25 or IGT) |
| **Weight Loss** | **5-10% wt loss** → Restores ovulation in 50% |
| **Ovulation Induction** | **Letrozole 2.5-5mg Days 2-6** (1st line) → Clomifene → Gonadotrophins → IVF |
| **Long-term** | Screen T2DM (OGTT q1-3y), Lipids, BP, Endometrial cancer risk |

## 5. Puberty & Growth

### Normal Puberty Onset
| Sex | Thelarche/Genital Growth | Peak Height Velocity | Menarche/Spermarche |
|-----|-------------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Female** | 8-13 years (Avg 10-11) | 11-12 years | 12-13 years (Avg 12.5) |
| **Male** | 9-14 years (Avg 11-12) | 13-14 years | 13-14 years (Spermarche) |

### Delayed Puberty
| Definition | Female: No thelarche by 13; Male: No testicular enlargement by 14 |
|------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Constitutional Delay** | Most common; Family history; Normal growth velocity; Bone age delayed |
| **Hypogonadotropic Hypogonadism** | Kallmann syndrome (Anosmia + HH); Isolated HH; Chronic disease |
| **Hypergonadotropic Hypogonadism** | Turner (45,X), Klinefelter (47,XXY), Autoimmune, Chemotherapy |

### Precocious Puberty
| Definition | Female: <8 years; Male: <9 years |
|------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Central (GnRH-dependent)** | True puberty early; CPP; Idiopathic, CNS tumours, Hamartoma |
| **Peripheral (GnRH-independent)** | McCune-Albright (GNAS), Adrenal androgen excess, Ovarian/Testicular tumours |

## 5. Infertility — Endocrine Workup

| Partner | Tests |
|---------|-------|
| **Female** | TSH, Prolactin, FSH/LH (Day 2-4), Oestradiol (Day 2-4), **AMH**, Progesterone (Day 21), US Pelvis (AFC, Fibroids), HSG |
| **Male** | **Semen Analysis** (WHO 2021), Testosterone, LH, FSH, Prolactin, US Scrotum, Genetic (if severe) |
| **Timing** | Investigate after **12 months** (or 6 months if female >35) |

## 6. Contraception — Endocrine Methods

| Method | Efficacy (Typical) | Key Contraindications |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **COCP** | 91% | VTE, Breast cancer, Migraine with aura, Smoker >35, BP >160/100 |
| **POP (Desogestrel)** | 91% | Active breast cancer, Unexplained bleeding |
| **Injectable (DMPA)** | 94% | Osteoporosis risk (long-term), Weight gain, Delayed fertility return |
| **Implant (ENG)** | 99.9% | Active VTE, Breast cancer |
| **IUS (LNG 52mg)** | 99.8% | Uterine anomaly, Active pelvic infection |
| **Copper IUD** | 99.2% | Heavy periods, Copper allergy, Wilson's disease |

## 7. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **HPG Axis** | GnRH → FSH/LH → Oestradiol/Testosterone; Negative feedback + Positive feedback (LH surge) |
| **GnRH Pulsatility** | Low freq (1-2/h) → FSH dominant; High freq (1/30-60min) → LH dominant |
| **Primary Hypogonadism** | **LH/FSH ↑↑, Testosterone/Oestradiol ↓** — Gonadal failure |
| **Secondary Hypogonadism** | **LH/FSH ↓/N, Testosterone/Oestradiol ↓** — Pituitary/Hypothalamic failure |
| **PCOS** | 2/3 Rotterdam: Oligo-ovulation + Hyperandrogenism + PCO morphology |
| **PCOS 1st Line Ovulation** | **Letrozole 2.5-5mg Days 2-6** (Superior to Clomifene) |
| **Male Hypogonadism** | Testosterone <12 nmol/L; LH/FSH ↑ = Primary; LH/FSH ↓ = Secondary |
| **Kallmann Syndrome** | Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism + Anosmia (KAL1/ANOS1 gene) |
| **POI** | FSH >40 IU/L on 2 occasions 4-6wk apart; Age <40; HRT until 50 |
| **Menopause HRT** | Oestrogen only (Hysterectomy); Combined (Intact uterus); Lowest effective dose |
| **Infertility** | Investigate after 12mo (6mo if female >35); AMH = Ovarian reserve marker |
| **AMH** | Granulosa cell product; Reflects antral follicle count; ↓ with age |

## 8. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **FSH vs LH in PCOS** | **LH > FSH** (LH:FSH >2:1); FSH relatively lower |
| **Primary vs Secondary Hypogonadism** | Primary = Gonad failure → LH/FSH ↑; Secondary = Pituitary failure → LH/FSH ↓ |
| **GnRH Pulse Frequency** | Low freq (1-2/h) → FSH dominant; High freq (1/30-60min) → LH dominant |
| **AMH vs FSH** | AMH = Ovarian reserve (stable through cycle); FSH = Cycle-dependent (Day 2-4) |
| **Letrozole vs Clomifene** | Letrozole superior for ovulation in PCOS (higher live birth, lower multiples) |
| **Kallmann Syndrome** | Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism + Anosmia (KAL1/ANOS1 gene) |
| **FAI** | Free Androgen Index = (Testosterone/SHBG) × 100; >5 = Hyperandrogenism |

## Flashcards
- **Q: GnRH pulse generator**
  **A: Hypothalamic arcuate nucleus (KNDy neurons: kisspeptin/neurokinin B/dynorphin)**

- **Q: GnRH frequency**
  **A: High freq → LH; Low freq → FSH**

- **Q: FSH action**
  **A: Sertoli (M): inhibin B, spermatogenesis support; Granulosa (F): follicular growth, estradiol**

- **Q: LH action**
  **A: Leydig (M): testosterone; Theca (F): androgens → granulosa aromatase → estradiol**

- **Q: Inhibin B**
  **A: Sertoli/Granulosa → selectively suppresses FSH**

- **Q: Testosterone feedback**
  **A: Hypothalamus (↓ GnRH pulse) + Pituitary (↓ LH)**

- **Q: Estradiol feedback**
  **A: Early follicular: -ve on FSH; Mid-cycle sustained high: +ve LH surge**

- **Q: Progesterone feedback**
  **A: Luteal phase: -ve on GnRH pulse frequency (↓ LH pulse)**

- **Q: Kallmann syndrome**
  **A: GnRH deficiency + anosmia; X-linked (KAL1/ANOS1) or autosomal; treat: hCG/FSH or testosterone**

- **Q: PCOS gonadotropins**
  **A: LH:FSH >2-3:1; ↑ LH pulse amplitude/frequency; ↑ ovarian androgen production**

- **Q: Menopause**
  **A: Loss of inhibin B + estradiol → FSH ↑↑ > LH ↑; FSH > LH**

- **Q: Premature ovarian insufficiency**
  **A: <40y; FSH >25 on 2 occasions 4wk apart; karyotype, FMR1, autoimmune screen**

- **Q: Functional hypothalamic amenorrhoea**
  **A: Energy deficit (low BMI, exercise, stress) → ↓ GnRH pulse → low LH/FSH/estradiol**

- **Q: Male hypogonadism types**
  **A: Primary: high LH/FSH, low T; Secondary: low/normal LH/FSH, low T**
