# Tertiary Hyperparathyroidism (THPT)

> [!info]
> **Tertiary Hyperparathyroidism (THPT) = Autonomous PTH Hypersecretion** after Long-Standing Secondary HPT. **Autonomous PTH Secretion Persists Despite Correction of Stimulus (Hypercalcaemia).** Occurs after Long-Standing SHPT (Usually >5-10 Years on Dialysis). **Autonomy = PTH Fails to Suppress with Hypercalcaemia.**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Define THPT and differentiate from primary and secondary HPT
- [ ] Recognise the pathophysiology of autonomous PTH secretion
- [ ] Apply diagnostic criteria (Hypercalcaemia + Inappropriately High PTH)
- [ ] Apply parathyroidectomy criteria and surgical approach
- [ ] Manage post-operative hypocalcaemia (Hungry Bone Syndrome)

---

## 2. Pathophysiology — From Secondary to Autonomous

| Stage | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **1. Secondary HPT** | Phosphate ↑ → FGF23 ↑ → 1,25-(OH)₂D ↓ → Hypocalcaemia → **Reactive PTH ↑↑** |
| **2. Prolonged Stimulation** | **Monoclonal Expansion** of Parathyroid Chief Cells → Clonal Autonomy |
| **3. Autonomous Secretion** | **PTH Secretion Becomes Calcium-Independent** → PTH ↑ Despite Hypercalcaemia |
| **Genetics** | **Clonal Expansion** (Monoclonal > Polyclonal); **MEN1, CDC73, PRAD1** Mutations (Rare in Sporadic) |

---

## 2. Natural History & Progression

| Stage | Duration | Calcium | PTH | Autonomy |
|-------|----------|---------|-----|----------|
| **Early SHPT** | <2 Years on Dialysis | Low/Normal | ↑↑ (Reactive) | **None** (Calcium-Dependent) |
| **Advanced SHPT** | 5-10 Years | Normal/Mild ↑ | ↑↑↑ (High) | **Developing Autonomy** |
| **Tertiary HPT** | **>5-10 Years / Post-Transplant** | **High (Hypercalcaemia)** | **Inappropriately High** | **Full Autonomy** (PTH Fails to Suppress) |

---

## 2. Diagnostic Criteria — THPT vs Primary vs Secondary HPT

| Feature | **Primary HPT** | **Secondary HPT** | **Tertiary HPT** |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Calcium** | High | Low/Normal | **High (Hypercalcaemia)** |
| **PTH** | High / Inappropriately Normal | High (Reactive) | **High (Autonomous)** |
| **Phosphate** | Low | High (CKD) | Low/Normal (Variable) |
| **Calcium x Phosphate** | Normal/High | High (CKD) | High |
| **Vitamin D** | Low/Normal | Very Low | Low/Normal |
| **Renal Function** | Normal | **CKD G4-5 / Dialysis** | **CKD G4-5 / Post-Transplant** |
| **PTH Response to Calcium** | Suppresses | Suppresses (Initially) | **Fails to Suppress** |
| **Autonomy** | Yes (Adenoma) | No (Reactive) | **Yes (Autonomous)** |

---

## 2. Clinical Presentation

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Hypercalcaemia** | **Persistent** (Ca²⁺ >2.6 mmol/L) Despite Dialysis/Transplant |
| **PTH** | **Inappropriately High** (Often >500-1000 pg/mL) |
| **Bone Disease** | **Renal Osteodystrophy** — Osteitis Fibrosa, Fractures, Bone Pain |
| **Vascular Calcification** | **Severe** (Coronary, Valvular, Peripheral) |
| **Calciphylaxis** | High Risk (Ca × Po₄ >4.5 mmol²/L²) |
| **Post-Transplant** | **Persistent Hypercalcaemia** >3-6 Months Post-Transplant |

---

## 3. Diagnosis — THPT vs Primary vs Secondary HPT

| Feature | **Primary HPT** | **Secondary HPT** | **Tertiary HPT** |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Calcium** | High | Low/Normal | **High** |
| **PTH** | High | **High (Reactive)** | **High (Autonomous)** |
| **Phosphate** | Low | **High** | Low/Normal |
| **Renal Function** | Normal | **CKD G4-5 / Dialysis** | **CKD G5 / Post-Transplant** |
| **PTH Response to High Ca** | Suppresses | Suppresses (Eventually) | **Fails to Suppress** |
| **Autonomy** | Yes (Adenoma) | No (Reactive) | **Yes (Autonomous)** |
| **Setting** | Normal Renal Function | **CKD G4-5 / Dialysis** | **Long-Standing SHPT / Post-Transplant** |

---

## 3. Diagnostic Algorithm

```
CKD G4-5 / Dialysis / Post-Transplant + Hypercalcaemia + High PTH
         │
         ▼
EXCLUDE PRIMARY HPT
         │
         ├── Renal Function NORMAL → PRIMARY HPT
         │
         └── Renal Function IMPAIRED
                 │
                 ├── HYPERCALCAEMIA + HIGH PTH + CKD/DIALYSIS HISTORY
                 │       → TERTIARY HPT
                 │
                 └── NORMAL CALCIUM + HIGH PTH + CKD/DIALYSIS
                         → SECONDARY HPT
```

### Functional Autonomy Test
| Test | Protocol | Tertiary HPT Result |
|------|----------|---------------------|
| **Calcium Infusion** | 10% Ca Gluconate 10-20mL IV over 10min → PTH at 0, 15, 30, 60min | **PTH Fails to Suppress** (<30% Drop) |
| **Oral Calcium Load** | 2g CaCO₃ Oral → PTH at 0, 30, 60, 120min | **PTH Fails to Suppress** |

---

## 3. Clinical Features Specific to THPT

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Hypercalcaemia** | Ca²⁺ >2.6 mmol/L (Often >3.0) Persisting Despite Dialysis/Transplant |
| **PTH** | **>500-1000 pg/mL** (Inappropriately High for Calcium Level) |
| **Bone Disease** | Severe Renal Osteodystrophy (Osteitis Fibrosa, Brown Tumours, Fractures) |
| **Vascular Calcification** | **Severe** — Coronary, Valvular, Peripheral Arteries |
| **Calciphylaxis Risk** | **High** (Ca × Po₄ >4.5 mmol²/L²) |
| **Post-Transplant** | Hypercalcaemia Persists **>3-6 Months** Post-Transplant = THPT |

---

## 3. Management — Parathyroidectomy (PTX) is Definitive

### Indications for PTX in THPT
| Indication | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Persistent Hypercalcaemia** | Ca²⁺ >2.75 mmol/L (Post-Transplant >3-6 Months) |
| **Severe Bone Disease** | Fractures, Bone Pain, Osteitis Fibrosa |
| **Vascular Calcification** | Progressive Coronary/Valvular/Peripheral Calcification |
| **Calciphylaxis** | Urgent Indication |
| **Symptomatic Hypercalcaemia** | Polyuria, Confusion, Renal Stones, Pancreatitis |
| **Failed Medical Management** | Refractory Hypercalcaemia on Dialysis/Transplant |

### Surgical Approach
| Procedure | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Subtotal (3.5-Gland) PTX** | **Standard for THPT** — Leave 50-70mg Remnant (≈50mg) |
| **Total PTX + Autotransplant** | Forearm (Brachioradialis); If All 4 Glands Enlarged |
| **IOPTH (Miami Criterion)** | **>50% Drop from Peak at 10min Post-Excision** |

### Post-Op Management (THPT Specific)
| Complication | Management |
|-------------|------------|
| **Hypocalcaemia** | **Severe & Prolonged** (Hungry Bone Syndrome More Intense) |
| **IV Calcium** | 10% Ca Gluconate 10-20mL Bolus → Infusion 1-2mg/kg/hr |
| **Oral Calcium + Vit D** | High-Dose (Calcitriol 0.5-1µg + Ca 2-3g/day) |
| **Phosphate** | Hypophosphataemia Common → Oral Phosphate Supplements |

---

## 5. Post-Transplant THPT Management

| Timeframe | Management |
|-----------|-----------|
| **0-3 Months** | Observe (May Resolve Spontaneously) |
| **3-6 Months** | If Ca²⁺ >2.75 + PTH High → **THPT Likely** → Workup for PTX |
| **>6 Months** | **Definitive THPT** → PTX Indicated |

### Post-Transplant Monitoring
| Timepoint | Tests |
|-----------|-------|
| **Week 1** | Ca, PTH, Phosphate, Mg, Alk Phos Daily |
| **Month 1** | Ca, PTH, Phosphate, ALP, 25-OH Vit D |
| **Month 3** | Full Bone Profile; Assess for THPT |
| **Month 6** | If Hypercalcaemia Persists → PTX Workup |

---

## 5. Medical Management (If PTX Not Feasible)

| Drug | Dose | Role |
|------|------|------|
| **Cinacalcet** | 30-180mg/day (BD) | **Calcimimetic** → ↓ PTH; Lowers Ca & Phos |
| **Phosphate Binders** | Sevelamer/Lanthanum/Ferric Citrate | Control Phosphate (Avoid Ca-Based if Hypercalcaemia) |
| **Vitamin D Analogues** | **Avoid** (Worsens Hypercalcaemia) | |
| **Bisphosphonates** | Zoledronic Acid 5mg IV Annual | Bone Protection (Not for Acute Hypercalcaemia) |

## 5. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **THPT Definition** | **Autonomous PTH Secretion** after Long-Standing SHPT → PTH Fails to Suppress with Hypercalcaemia |
| **Key Differentiator** | **Hypercalcaemia + High PTH** (vs Secondary: Normal/Low Ca; Primary: Normal Renal Function) |
| **Pathogenesis** | Monoclonal Parathyroid Expansion → Autonomous PTH Secretion |
| **Key Diagnostic** | **Hypercalcaemia + Inappropriately High PTH** + **CKD/Dialysis/Post-Transplant History** |
| **Post-Transplant** | Hypercalcaemia >3-6 Months Post-Tx = THPT (vs Transient <3 Months) |
| **Surgical Indication** | Persistent Hypercalcaemia >3-6mo Post-Tx; Ca >2.75; Bone Disease; Calciphylaxis |
| **Surgery** | **Subtotal (3.5-Gland) PTX** (Standard); IOPTH >50% Drop at 10min |
| **Post-Op Hypocalcaemia** | **Severe & Prolonged** (Hungry Bone Syndrome) → Aggressive Ca/Vit D Replacement |
| **Medical Rx (If No Surgery)** | Cinacalcet (Calcimimetic) → ↓ PTH, ↓ Ca, ↓ Phos; Phosphate Binders (Non-Ca) |
| **Key Differentiator** | **Primary HPT**: Normal Renal Function; **THPT**: CKD/Dialysis/Post-Transplant History |

## 8. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Tertiary vs Primary HPT** | Tertiary = CKD/Dialysis/Post-Transplant History + Autonomous PTH; Primary = Normal Renal Function |
| **Tertiary vs Secondary HPT** | Secondary: Ca Normal/Low + PTH High (Reactive); Tertiary: Ca High + PTH High (Autonomous) |
| **Post-Transplant Hypercalcaemia** | <3 Months = Transient (Tertiary Hypoparathyroidism Recovery); >3-6 Months = THPT |
| **THPT vs Primary HPT** | Primary: Normal Renal Function; THPT: CKD/Dialysis/Post-Transplant History |
| **Autonomy Test** | Calcium Infusion → Tertiary: PTH Fails to Suppress; Primary: PTH Suppresses |

**Mnemonic: TERTIARY HPT**
- **T**ime: Long-Standing SHPT (>5-10 Years Dialysis)
- **E**scape: PTH **Escapes** Calcium Control (Autonomous)
- **R**enal: **CKD/Dialysis/Post-Transplant** Setting
- **T**ertiary = **T**ertiary (Autonomous PTH)
- **I**mportant: **I**mmobilization of Calcium Control
- **A**utonomy: PTH **Fails to Suppress** with High Calcium
- **R**equires: **P**arathyroidectomy (Subtotal)
- **Y**ear: >5-10 Years Dialysis History
