# Hypocalcaemic Tetany

> [!info]
> **Hypocalcaemic Tetany = Acute Neuromuscular Excitability** due to Ionised Hypocalcaemia. **Medical Emergency** Requiring **Immediate IV Calcium Gluconate**. Key Differential: Hypoparathyroidism, Vitamin D Deficiency, Alkalosis, Magnesium Deficiency.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise clinical features of hypocalcaemic tetany (Trousseau's, Chvostek's signs)
- [ ] Execute immediate IV calcium gluconate administration
- [ ] Differentiate causes (hypoparathyroidism, vitamin D deficiency, alkalosis, magnesium deficiency)
- [ ] Apply chronic replacement strategies (calcium + active vitamin D analogues)
- [ ] Identify special causes (hungry bone syndrome, massive transfusion, pancreatitis)

---

## 2. Definitions & Diagnostic Criteria

| Parameter | Normal | Hypocalcaemia | Severe (Tetany) |
|-----------|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Corrected Serum Calcium** | 2.15-2.55 mmol/L (8.6-10.2 mg/dL) | **<2.15 mmol/L** (<8.6 mg/dL) | **<1.75 mmol/L** (<7.0 mg/dL) |
| **Ionised Calcium** | 1.15-1.30 mmol/L (4.5-5.2 mg/dL) | **<1.10 mmol/L** | **<1.0 mmol/L** |

> **Correction Formula**: Corrected Ca = Measured Ca + 0.02 × (40 - Albumin g/L) [mmol/L]

---

## 2. Aetiology & Differential Diagnosis

| Category | Cause | Key Features |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| **Hypoparathyroidism** | Post-Thyroidectomy (75%), Autoimmune (APS-1), DiGeorge (22q11.2), Mg Deficiency | Low PTH, High Phosphate |
| **Vitamin D Deficiency** | **High PTH** (Secondary Hyperparathyroidism) | Low 25-OH D, High ALP, Low Phosphate |
| **Renal Failure** | **High Phosphate, High PTH, Low 1,25-(OH)₂D** | uraemia |
| **Acute Pancreatitis** | Saponification (Ca²⁺ + FFA → Calcium Soaps) | High Amylase/Lipase, Abdominal Pain |
| **Alkalosis** | **Respiratory** (Hyperventilation) / **Metabolic** (Vomiting) | ↑ Protein Binding → ↓ Ionised Ca²⁺ |
| **Massive Transfusion** | Citrate Chelation (1 Unit = ~3g Citrate = 45 mmol Ca²⁺) | Ionised Ca²⁺ Drop |
| **Rhabdomyolysis** | Ca²⁺ Sequestration in Damaged Muscle | High CK, Myoglobinuria |
| **Tumour Lysis Syndrome** | Phosphate Release → Ca²⁺ Precipitation | High Phosphate, Uric Acid, K⁺ |
| **Hungry Bone Syndrome** | Post-PTX/Thyroidectomy; Rapid Bone Mineralisation | Severe HypoCa²⁺, HypoPhos, HypoMg |
| **Mg²⁺ Deficiency** | **Functional Hypoparathyroidism** (Mg Needed for PTH Secretion/Action) | Hypokalaemia, Hypocalcaemia Resistant to Ca²⁺ |

## 2. Clinical Presentation

### Acute Tetany (Emergency)
| Sign | Description | Elicitation |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| **Trousseau's Sign** | Carpal Spasm (Adduction Thumb, Flexion Wrist/MCPs, Extension Fingers) | **BP Cuff Inflated >SBP for 3 Min** |
| **Chvostek's Sign** | Facial Muscle Twitch (Lips, Nose, Eye) | **Percussion Over Facial Nerve** (Anterior to Ear) |
| **Laryngospasm** | Stridor, Airway Obstruction | **Emergency — Intubation May Be Needed** |
| **Generalised Seizures** | Tonic-Clonic | — |
| **Cardiac** | **Prolonged QT** → Torsades de Pointes, VF | ECG Monitoring |
| **Neuropsychiatric** | Anxiety, Confusion, Irritability, Depression | — |

### Chronic Hypocalcaemia
| Feature | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Basal Ganglia Calcification** | CT Brain (Fahr's Syndrome) |
| **Cataracts** | Posterior Subcapsular |
| **Dental** | Enamel Hypoplasia, Caries |
| **Neuropsychiatric** | Depression, Anxiety, Dementia, Parkinsonism |
| **Skin/Hair/Nails** | Dry Skin, Coarse Hair, Brittle Nails |
| **Cataracts** | Posterior Subcapsular |

| Cause | PTH | Phosphate | Magnesium | Key Feature |

| **Hypoparathyroidism** | **Low** | High | Normal | Post-Surgical (75%), Autoimmune, Genetic |

| **Renal Failure** | High (2° HPT) | **High** | Normal | High Creatinine |
| **Pseudohypoparathyroidism** | **High** (Resistance) | High | Normal | PHP1a = AHO (Short Stature, Obesity) |
| **Vitamin D Deficiency** | High (2° HPT) | Low/Normal | Normal | Low 25-OH D |
| **Acute Pancreatitis** | Normal/High | High | Normal | High Amylase/Lipase |
| **Alkalosis** | Normal/High | Normal | Normal | Respiratory (>7.45) / Metabolic |
| **Hypomagnesaemia** | Low/Normal | Low/Normal | **Low** | **Correct Mg First** |

## 3. Acute Management — Hypocalcaemic Tetany (Emergency)

### Immediate IV Calcium (Life-Saving)
| Drug | Dose | Administration |
|--------|------|----------------|
| **10% Calcium Gluconate** | **10-20 mL (1-2g) IV** | **Slow IV Over 10 Min** (Cardiac Monitor) |
| **Repeat** | If Persistent Tetany | Every 10-20 Min Until Controlled |

### Follow-Up Infusion
| Infusion | Rate |
|----------|-------|
| **10% Calcium Gluconate** | **1-2 g/hr (10-20 mL/hr)** in 5% Dextrose / 0.9% Saline |
| **Target** | Ionised Ca²⁺ >1.1 mmol/L; Symptom Relief |

### Adjuncts
| Drug | Dose | Indication |
|-------|------|------------|
| **Magnesium Sulphate** | 2-4g IV | Mg <0.7 mmol/L |
| **Vitamin D (Alfacalcidol/Calcitriol)** | 1-2 µg IV/PO | Accelerate Ca²⁺ Absorption |
| **Oral Calcium** | Calcium Carbonate 1-2g TDS | After IV Stabilisation |

## 4. Chronic Management

### Calcium Supplementation
| Preparation | Elemental Ca²⁺ | Dose |
|----------------|----------------|------|
| **Calcium Carbonate** | 40% (400mg/tablet) | 1.5-2g TDS with Meals |
| **Calcium Citrate** | 21% | 1-2g TDS (Better Absorption if Achlorhydria) |
| **Target** | **Total Elemental Ca²⁺ 1.5-2g/day** | Divided Doses |

### Active Vitamin D Analogues (Essential for Absorption)
| Agent | Dose | Advantage |
|--------|------|-----------|
| **Alfacalcidol (1α-OH D3)** | **0.25-1 µg/day** | Rapid Onset; No Renal Activation Needed |
| **Calcitriol (1,25-(OH)₂D₃)** | **0.25-0.5 µg/day** | Active Form; Short Half-Life |
| **Dose Titration** | **Target: Ionised Ca²⁺ 1.1-1.3 mmol/L** | Adjust q2-4wk |

### Magnesium
| Indication | Dose |
|------------|------|
| **Hypomagnesaemia** | **MgSO₄ 2-4g IV** (Acute); Oral Mg 300-600mg/day (Chronic) |

### Thiazide Diuretic (Adjunct)
| Role | Dose |
|------|------|
| **Reduce Urinary Ca²⁺ Excretion** | **Hydrochlorothiazide 25mg OD** (If Hypercalciuria) |

## 5. Monitoring & Targets (Chronic)

| Parameter | Target | Frequency |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Serum Calcium (Corrected)** | **2.2-2.5 mmol/L** (Slightly Low Normal) | Monthly (Stable) |
| **Ionised Calcium** | **1.1-1.3 mmol/L** | q1-2wk (Titration) |
| **Phosphate** | **<1.5 mmol/L** | Monthly |
| **24h Urine Calcium** | **<7.5 mmol/day (300mg)** | 3-6 Monthly |
| **Renal Function** | eGFR, Creatinine | 6-Monthly |
| **25-OH Vitamin D** | >50 nmol/L (20 ng/mL) | Annually |
| **Bone Density** | DEXA (Spine/Hip) | q2-3 Years |

## 5. Special Situations

### Hypocalcaemic Tetany (Emergency)
| Step | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **1. IV Calcium Gluconate 10%** | **10-20 mL (1-2g) IV Over 10 Min** (Cardiac Monitor) |
| **2. Repeat** | Every 10-20 Min Until Tetany Controlled |
| **3. Infusion** | 10% Calcium Gluconate 100mL (10g) in 500mL 5% Dextrose @ 50-100mL/hr |
| **4. Magnesium** | If Mg <0.7 → 2-4g MgSO₄ IV |
| **5. Vitamin D** | Alfacalcidol/Calcitriol 1-2µg IV/PO |

### Hungry Bone Syndrome (Post-Parathyroidectomy)
| Feature | Management |
|---------|------------|
| **Severe Hypocalcaemia** | **IV Calcium Gluconate Infusion** (1-2g/hr) |
| **Duration** | Days-Weeks (Until Bone Remineralisation) |
| **Phosphate** | Hypophosphataemia → Oral Phosphate Supplements |
| **Magnesium** | Often Co-existing Hypomagnesaemia |

### Magnesium Deficiency
| Feature | Management |
|---------|------------|
| **Functional Hypoparathyroidism** | **Correct Mg First** |
| **Prophylactic** | 100mg/day Oral Mg (If on Loop Diuretics/PPI) |
| **Acute** | **IV MgSO₄ 2-4g** (Repeat if Needed) |

### Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|-----------|
| **Phosphate Binders** | Calcium-Based (If Ca Normal) / Non-Ca (Sevelamer/Lanthanum) if Hypercalcaemia |
| **Vitamin D** | **Alfacalcidol/Calcitriol** (Active Analogues) |
| **Cinacalcet** | If PTH ↑↑ Despite Binders |

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|

| **Acute Tetany Rx** | **IV 10% Ca Gluconate 10-20mL Over 10min** (Cardiac Monitor) |

| **Target Ionised Ca²⁺** | **1.1-1.3 mmol/L** |
| **Phosphate Target** | **<1.5 mmol/L** |

| **Pseudohypoparathyroidism** | **High PTH + Hypocalcaemia**; PHP1a = AHO Phenotype |

| **Vitamin D in Hypopara** | **Alfacalcidol/Calcitriol** (Active Analogues) — Not Cholecalciferol |
