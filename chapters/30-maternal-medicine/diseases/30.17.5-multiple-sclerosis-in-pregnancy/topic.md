# Multiple sclerosis in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Multiple sclerosis affects women 2-3x more than men, often in childbearing years. Pregnancy is associated with reduced relapse rate (especially 3rd trimester) and increased relapse postpartum. Disease-modifying drugs require careful planning around conception.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
1 in 1,000 women; often diagnosed in 20s-30s

### Effect of pregnancy
Relapse rate falls (esp. 3rd trimester); rises 3-6 months postpartum (transient)

### Effect of MS on pregnancy
No increase in fetal malformations; possible ↑caesarean; small ↑low birth weight

### Disease-modifying drugs
Stop before conception: fingolimod (2 months), natalizumab (3 months); interferon-β and glatiramer safe until pregnancy confirmed

### Teratogenic
Cladribine; teriflunomide; contraindicated; washout with cholestyramine for teriflunomide

### Relapse treatment
Methylprednisolone IV 3-5 days; consider IVIG if severe; MRI without gadolinium if needed

### Labour and delivery
Vaginal preferred; epidural does not worsen MS; forceps/ventouse per obstetric indication

### Postpartum
Restart DMD if breastfeeding not chosen; exclusive breastfeeding may reduce relapses

### Fatigue
Common in pregnancy; manage with rest, support, vitamin D

### Long-term
No long-term effect of pregnancy on MS progression
