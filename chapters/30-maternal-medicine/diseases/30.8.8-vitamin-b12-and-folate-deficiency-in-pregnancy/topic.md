# Vitamin B12 and folate deficiency in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Vitamin B12 and folate deficiency in pregnancy cause megaloblastic anaemia and are associated with neural tube defects (folate), preterm birth, and neurodevelopmental delay (B12). Preconception folate supplementation reduces NTD risk. B12 deficiency is uncommon but important in vegan/vegetarian mothers, after bariatric surgery, or with pernicious anaemia.

## Core Clinical Content

### Folate requirements
Increased 5-10x in pregnancy; deficiency common if poor diet, malabsorption, anticonvulsants

### Folate functions
DNA synthesis; cell division; NTD prevention; placental growth

### B12 functions
Myelin synthesis; DNA synthesis; neurological function

### Causes folate deficiency
Poor diet; multiple pregnancy; haemolysis; malabsorption; drugs (anticonvulsants, methotrexate, trimethoprim)

### Causes B12 deficiency
Vegan/vegetarian diet; pernicious anaemia; post-bariatric; ileal disease (Crohn's); metformin

### Folate deficiency effects
Megaloblastic anaemia; NTD (anencephaly, spina bifida); cleft lip/palate; cardiac defects; placental abruption; recurrent miscarriage

### B12 deficiency effects
Megaloblastic anaemia; neurological (paresthesia, ataxia, dementia); infertility; preterm; low birth weight

### Investigations
FBC (MCV >100); blood film (macroovalocytes, hypersegmented neutrophils); serum folate; B12; MMA; homocysteine; anti-IF antibody

### Supplementation
Folic acid 400 μg daily preconception to 12 weeks; 5 mg if high risk (prior NTD, diabetes, antiepileptics, BMI >30, sickle); B12 IM for severe deficiency; oral if dietary

### Postpartum
Continue supplementation if breastfeeding; assess mother's B12/folate if breastfed infant symptomatic
