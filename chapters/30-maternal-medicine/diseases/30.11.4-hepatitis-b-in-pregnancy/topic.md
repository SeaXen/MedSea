# Hepatitis B in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Hepatitis B in pregnancy can cause vertical transmission (90% in HBeAg-positive mothers without intervention), leading to chronic infection in 90% of infected infants. Antenatal screening, maternal viral load monitoring, and neonatal immunoprophylaxis are highly effective.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
0.5-2% in UK; up to 10-20% in endemic regions (Asia, Africa)

### Vertical transmission risk
90% HBeAg+; 10-20% HBeAg- without prophylaxis

### Screening
Universal at booking; HBsAg, anti-HBs, anti-HBc; if HBsAg+ — HBeAg, anti-HBe, HBV DNA

### Maternal management
Tenofovir from 28 weeks if HBV DNA >200,000 IU/mL or HBeAg+; monitor LFT

### Mode of delivery
Vaginal acceptable with prophylaxis; caesarean does not prevent transmission if immunoprophylaxis given

### Neonatal prophylaxis
Hepatitis B vaccine + hepatitis B immunoglobulin (HBIG) within 12 hours of birth — 90% effective

### Follow-up
Anti-HBs at 12 months; HBsAg and anti-HBc at 12-18 months to confirm status

### Breastfeeding
Safe with prophylaxis; not contraindicated

### Acute HBV in pregnancy
Severe hepatitis risk; tenofovir; consider for fulminant disease

### Hepatitis C
No vertical prevention; avoid invasive procedures; treat with DAAs postpartum
