# Mastitis and breast abscess

---

## Definition

Mastitis (inflammation of breast tissue, often with infection) affects 1 in 5 breastfeeding women, usually in the first 3 months. Staphylococcus aureus is the most common pathogen. Most cases respond to antibiotics and continued breastfeeding; abscesses require drainage.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
Lactational mastitis 10-20%; breast abscess 0.1-3%

### Risk factors
Cracked nipples; poor attachment; engorgement; blocked ducts; infrequent feeding; rapid weaning; immunocompromise

### Pathogens
S. aureus (most); coagulase-negative staph; Strep; rarely MRSA; community-acquired

### Presentation
Unilateral breast pain, swelling, erythema, warmth; systemic (fever, malaise, myalgia); wedge-shaped area

### Diagnosis
Clinical; milk culture if recurrent/severe; USS if abscess suspected; aspiration if fluid

### Differential
Engorgement; blocked duct; breast abscess; inflammatory breast cancer; Paget's; mastitis from other organisms

### Management
Continue breastfeeding (drain affected side); analgesia (paracetamol; ibuprofen); cold compresses; ensure attachment; frequent feeding

### Antibiotics
Flucloxacillin 500 mg QDS 10-14 days; erythromycin or clarithromycin if allergic; co-amoxiclav if severe

### Breast abscess
Needle aspiration (US-guided) or incision and drainage; antibiotics; continue feeding

### Prevention
Correct attachment; frequent feeding; treat cracked nipples; avoid sudden weaning
