# Postpartum thyroiditis

---

## Definition

Postpartum thyroiditis is an autoimmune thyroiditis occurring within 12 months of delivery, often presenting in a biphasic pattern of hyperthyroidism followed by hypothyroidism. It affects 5-10% of postpartum women and is associated with anti-TPO antibodies. Most women return to euthyroid but a proportion develop permanent hypothyroidism.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Autoimmune thyroiditis within 12 months postpartum

### Incidence
5-10% of postpartum women; up to 25% if TPOAb+

### Risk factors
Anti-TPO antibodies (strongest); prior postpartum thyroiditis; type 1 DM; other autoimmune disease (SLE); family history

### Phases
Biphasic: hyperthyroid (1-4 months postpartum) → euthyroid → hypothyroid (4-8 months) → recovery; 20-30% have isolated hypothyroid

### Hyperthyroid phase
Symptoms: fatigue, palpitations, weight loss, anxiety; thyrotoxic but no ophthalmopathy; due to thyroid hormone release from inflammation

### Hypothyroid phase
Symptoms: fatigue, weight gain, depression, constipation, dry skin, hair loss; depression may be misattributed to PND

### Diagnosis
TSH, free T4, free T3; TPO antibodies; thyroid USS; low uptake on radioisotope scan (avoid in breastfeeding if possible)

### Differential
Graves' disease (ophthalmopathy, persistently high uptake); PND; anaemia

### Management hyperthyroid
β-blocker (propranolol); no antithyroid drugs (destructive, not synthetic); usually self-limiting

### Management hypothyroid
Levothyroxine if symptomatic or TSH >10; weaning trial 6-12 months; monitor

### Long-term
20-30% permanent hypothyroidism; recurrence in 70% subsequent pregnancies; screen if symptomatic
