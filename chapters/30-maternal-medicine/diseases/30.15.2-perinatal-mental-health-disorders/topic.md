# Perinatal mental health disorders

---

## Definition

Perinatal mental health disorders — including antenatal and postnatal depression, anxiety, OCD, PTSD, postpartum psychosis, and tokophobia — are common and treatable but under-recognised. Untreated illness has significant impact on mother, baby, and family.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
Antenatal depression 10-15%; postnatal depression 10-15%; anxiety 15%; postpartum psychosis 0.1-0.2%

### Risk factors
Prior mental illness (strongest); recent stressful events; lack of support; domestic abuse; teenage; substance use

### Screening
Whooley questions + GAD-2 at booking and 6 weeks postnatal; Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS)

### Postpartum depression
Onset within 4-6 weeks; persistent low mood, anhedonia, anxiety, guilt; suicidal ideation; impact on bonding

### Postpartum psychosis
Onset within 2-4 weeks; mania, delusions, hallucinations, disorganised behaviour — psychiatric emergency

### Anxiety disorders
Common; generalised anxiety, OCD (intrusive thoughts of harm to baby — ego-dystonic), PTSD (especially after traumatic birth)

### Tokophobia
Severe fear of childbirth; consider delivery by caesarean if persistent

### Treatment
Psychological therapies first-line (CBT, IPT); SSRIs (sertraline, citalopram — safe in pregnancy/lactation); ECT for severe/psychotic

### Drug safety
Paroxetine (cardiac risk — 1st trimester); benzodiazepines (neonatal withdrawal); lithium (cardiac — monitor levels); valproate contraindicated

### Multidisciplinary care
Perinatal mental health team; GP; HV; obstetrician; psychiatrist; crisis plan; safeguarding

- **'CHAP' trial target** — Chronic HTN treated to <140/90 mmHg
- **'M-M-R-V' safe vaccines in pregnancy** — Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR, avoid), inactivated influenza, COVID-19, Whooping cough (dTpa)
