# Ischaemic heart disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Ischaemic heart disease in pregnancy — including stable angina, acute coronary syndrome (ACS), and myocardial infarction — is rare but increasing due to advanced maternal age, obesity, and pre-existing risk factors. Pregnancy is hypercoagulable and hyperdynamic, increasing cardiovascular risk. Presentation may be atypical and is often missed.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
MI 1-3 per 100,000 deliveries; rising; leading non-obstetric cause of maternal death in some series

### Risk factors
Advanced maternal age; obesity; smoking; hypertension; diabetes; hyperlipidaemia; family history; APS; previous MI

### Mechanism
Atherosclerosis (50%); coronary dissection (P-SCAD; 30% — especially postpartum); thrombosis (coronary spasm); embolism

### Presentation
Chest pain (atypical — epigastric, jaw, back); dyspnoea; diaphoresis; nausea; fatigue; collapse

### Diagnosis
ECG (ST changes; may be hard to interpret in pregnancy); troponin (valid); echo (regional wall motion abnormalities); CT coronary angiography (low dose)

### Management ACS
Aspirin 300 mg; clopidogrel; LMWH; β-blocker; nitrates; oxygen; PCI if STEMI (preferred over thrombolysis); radial approach preferred

### Drugs to avoid
Statins (teratogenic — stop at pregnancy diagnosis); ACEI/ARB; warfarin in 1st trimester; glycoprotein IIb/IIIa — limited data

### PCI
Bare metal stent preferred (avoid drug-eluting for 1st trimester); dual antiplatelet; LMWH cover for delivery

### Delivery planning
MDT; vaginal preferred (avoid tachycardia, hypotension); epidural; assisted second stage; avoid ergometrine (causes vasospasm)

### Prognosis
Maternal mortality 5-10% (higher if peripartum or anterior MI); recurrence 15-30% in subsequent pregnancies
