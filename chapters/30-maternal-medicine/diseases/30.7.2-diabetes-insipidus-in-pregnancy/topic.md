# Diabetes insipidus in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Diabetes insipidus (DI) in pregnancy may be pre-existing (central or nephrogenic) or develop de novo in pregnancy (gestational/transient DI of pregnancy). Pregnancy unmask subclinical DI due to rising vasopressinase from placenta. Distinguish from physiological polyuria of pregnancy.

## Core Clinical Content

### Forms
Central (pituitary); nephrogenic (kidney); gestational/transient (placental vasopressinase); pre-existing may worsen

### Incidence
Pre-existing DI rare; gestational DI 1-6 per 100,000 pregnancies

### Pathophysiology
Placental vasopressinase degrades ADH; unmasked in subclinical central DI; transient DI when placental mass high (twins, pre-eclampsia)

### Presentation
Polyuria (>3 L/day); polydipsia; dehydration; hypernatraemia if no access to water

### Investigations
Urine osmolality (<300); plasma osmolality (>287); sodium; water deprivation test (cautious in pregnancy); copeptin; serum vasopressin

### Differential
Physiological polyuria; gestational diabetes (glycosuria); psychogenic polydipsia; hypercalcaemia; hypokalaemia

### Treatment
Desmopressin (DDAVP) — safe, not degraded by vasopressinase; titrate dose; thiazide + low-salt for nephrogenic

### Dose adjustment
Often need higher dose in pregnancy (vasopressinase); reassess postpartum

### Monitoring
Sodium; fluid balance; weight; urine output; symptomatic review

### Postpartum
Gestational DI resolves; pre-existing may revert to pre-pregnancy dose; breastfeeding may worsen (oxytocin)
