# Thrombocytopenia in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Thrombocytopenia in pregnancy affects 6-10% of pregnancies. Gestational thrombocytopenia is the most common cause, but pre-eclampsia, HELLP, ITP, TTP, and HUS require urgent differentiation. Severe thrombocytopenia (<50 × 10^9/L) increases bleeding risk and affects delivery mode.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
6-10% of pregnancies; 1% have platelets <100 × 10^9/L

### Causes (mnemonic)
GESTATIONAL — Gestational, Eclampsia, Sepsis, TTP, HELLP, AFLP, ITP, DIC, Neonatal alloimmune, APLS, Malignant marrow

### Gestational
Mild (>70); 2nd/3rd trimester; no bleeding; resolves postpartum

### ITP
Autoimmune; platelets <100; 1st/2nd trimester onset; bleeding tendency; steroids/IVIG for <30

### TTP
Pentad — MAHA, thrombocytopenia, neuro, renal, fever; ADAMTS13 <10%; plasma exchange; mortality 90% untreated

### Pre-eclampsia/HELLP
Severe features; LFT deranged; coagulopathy in HELLP

### Neonatal alloimmune
Mother makes antibodies against fetal platelets; risk ICH; treat with IVIG

### Investigation
FBC, blood film, LFT, LDH, haptoglobin, coagulation, autoantibodies, viral screen

### Management
Treat cause; ITP — steroids/IVIG for <30 or bleeding; platelet transfusion only for surgery/bleeding

### Delivery threshold
Vaginal safe >50; C-section >50; epidural/anaesthesia requires >70-80

### Postpartum
ITP may flare; monitor; neonatal platelet count in ITP
