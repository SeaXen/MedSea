# Obesity in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Maternal obesity (BMI ≥30) is associated with increased risk of miscarriage, congenital anomalies, GDM, pre-eclampsia, macrosomia, caesarean section, VTE, and fetal death. Preconception weight loss and lifestyle modification improve outcomes.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
20-30% of pregnant women in developed countries; rising

### Risks to mother
Miscarriage; GDM (3-4x); pre-eclampsia (2-3x); caesarean; PPH; VTE; anaesthetic difficulty; wound infection

### Risks to fetus
NTD; cardiac defects; stillbirth (2x); macrosomia; shoulder dystocia; childhood obesity

### Preconception
Weight loss (5-10%); folic acid 5 mg; screen for diabetes; counsel on risks

### Antenatal care
Anomaly scan quality may be limited; consider repeat or alternative imaging; serial growth scans

### Thromboprophylaxis
Risk assess; LMWH if BMI >40 or additional risk factors; consider for 10 days postpartum

### GDM screening
Earlier OGTT at 24-28 weeks (or earlier if risk factors)

### Weight gain
Institute of Medicine guidelines — underweight 12.5-18 kg; normal 11.5-16; overweight 7-11.5; obese 5-9

### Bariatric surgery
Conceive 12-18 months post-surgery; monitor iron, B12, folate, calcium, vitamin D; risk of internal hernia

### Labour
Higher caesarean; anaesthetic review; consider early epidural; PPH risk
