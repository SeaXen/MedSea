# Preterm labour and birth

---

## Definition

Preterm labour is onset of regular uterine contractions with cervical change before 37 weeks' gestation. Preterm birth is the leading cause of neonatal mortality and morbidity. Prevention (progesterone, cerclage) and management (tocolysis, corticosteroids, magnesium) are key.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Delivery before 37 weeks; very preterm <32; extremely preterm <28

### Prevalence
7-10% of births; rising; leading cause of neonatal mortality

### Risk factors
Prior PTB (strongest); multiple pregnancy; infection (UTI, chorioamnionitis); cervical insufficiency; smoking; substance use; short cervix

### Prediction
Cervical length USS 16-24 weeks; short cervix <25 mm at 24 weeks — intervention

### Prevention
Progesterone (vaginal 200 mg nocte) for short cervix or prior PTB; cerclage for prior PTB + short cervix; treat infection

### Corticosteroids
Betamethasone 12 mg IM 24 h apart or dexamethasone 6 mg IM 12 h × 4 — between 24-34 weeks; reduces RDS, IVH, NEC, death

### Tocolysis
Nifedipine (first-line); atosiban; indomethacin (<32 weeks only); beta-agonists; allow 48 h for steroids

### Magnesium sulphate
For neuroprotection if delivery <30 weeks; 4 g IV loading

### Antibiotics
For group B Strep prophylaxis if previous GBS, GBS in current pregnancy, or unknown status in preterm labour

### Mode of delivery
Vaginal if cephalic and reassuring; caesarean for breech, fetal distress, or obstetric indication
