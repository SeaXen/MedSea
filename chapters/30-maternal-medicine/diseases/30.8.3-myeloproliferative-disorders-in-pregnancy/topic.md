# Myeloproliferative disorders in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Myeloproliferative neoplasms (MPN) — essential thrombocythaemia (ET), polycythaemia vera (PV), and primary myelofibrosis — are clonal haematopoietic disorders. In pregnancy they are associated with increased risk of thrombosis, haemorrhage, miscarriage, IUGR, stillbirth, and pre-eclampsia. Specialist MDT care is essential.

## Core Clinical Content

### Epidemiology
ET most common MPN in pregnancy; median age 30-40; affects women in childbearing years

### ET pathophysiology
JAK2 V617F (50%); CALR (25%); MPL (5%); triple negative (20%); platelets >450

### PV pathophysiology
JAK2 V617F in 95%; raised Hb/Hct; EPO low

### Complications pregnancy
Arterial and venous thrombosis (DVT, PE, stroke, MI); haemorrhage; miscarriage; IUGR; stillbirth; pre-eclampsia; placental abruption; preterm

### Preconception
MDT (haematologist, obstetrician); optimise counts; switch teratogens (hydroxyurea → IFN-α); aspirin; review

### Treatment
Aspirin 75-150 mg; LMWH (prophylactic or therapeutic depending on risk); cytoreduction if counts very high (IFN-α preferred); venesection for PV; avoid hydroxyurea; avoid anagrelide 1st trimester

### Monitoring
FBC 2-4 weekly; U&E; LFT; uric acid; growth scans; uterine artery Doppler; MCA Doppler if needed

### Delivery
Planned; discontinue LMWH 12-24 h before if regional anaesthesia; consider platelet count if needed

### Postpartum
Continue LMWH 6 weeks; aspirin; breastfeeding compatible with IFN-α; resume prior therapy

### Counselling
Recurrence risk; consider PGD if heritable component; multidisciplinary follow-up lifelong
