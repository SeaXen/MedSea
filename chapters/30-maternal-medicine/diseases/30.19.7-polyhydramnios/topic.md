# Polyhydramnios

---

## Definition

Polyhydramnios is excessive amniotic fluid volume (AFI >25 cm or deepest vertical pocket >8 cm). It affects 1-2% of pregnancies and is associated with fetal anomalies (especially GI obstruction and CNS), maternal diabetes, and multiple pregnancy. Severe cases present with abdominal discomfort, preterm labour, and cord prolapse.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Amniotic fluid index (AFI) >25 cm or deepest vertical pocket (DVP) >8 cm

### Incidence
0.5-2% of pregnancies; mild common, severe rare

### Causes
Idiopathic (60%); fetal anomalies (15-20% — GI obstruction, oesophageal atresia, duodenal atresia, anencephaly, NTDs); maternal diabetes (10-20%); multiple pregnancy (TTTS); fetal infection; anaemia (parvovirus, haemolysis)

### Mild polyhydramnios
Often idiopathic; AFI 25-30; close surveillance

### Severe polyhydramnios
AFI >35; preterm labour risk; cord prolapse; abruption; PPH; respiratory compromise

### Presentation
Fundal height > dates; tense abdomen; fluid thrill; dyspnoea; preterm contractions

### Investigations
Detailed anomaly scan; OGTT for diabetes; Kleihauer; maternal serology (parvovirus, CMV); fetal karyotype if indicated

### Monitoring
Regular scans; growth; wellbeing; symphysis-fundal height

### Management mild
Expectant; treat underlying cause if possible

### Management severe
Amnioreduction (therapeutic amniocentesis); indomethacin (reduces fetal urine output — <32 weeks); close monitoring

### Delivery
Risk of cord prolapse (ARM with controlled drain); PPH risk; breech presentation
