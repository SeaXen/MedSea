# Placenta accreta spectrum

---

## Definition

Placenta accreta spectrum (PAS) covers placenta accreta, increta, and percreta — abnormal placental invasion into or through the myometrium. It is associated with significant peripartum haemorrhage, hysterectomy, and maternal mortality. Risk is greatest in women with prior caesarean section and placenta praevia.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Placenta abnormally adherent: accreta (myometrium); increta (through myometrium); percreta (to serosa/adjacent organs)

### Incidence
0.2 per 1000; rising with C-section rates

### Risk factors
Prior C-section (5% if 1; 25% if 2; 50%+ if ≥3 with praevia); placenta praevia; prior uterine surgery; Asherman; multiparity; advanced maternal age

### Presentation
Often asymptomatic antenatally; diagnosed on USS; failed placental separation at delivery; PPH

### USS features
Loss of clear retroplacental hypoechoic zone; placental lacunae ('Swiss cheese'); bladder line interruption; hypervascularity

### MRI
Confirms depth; useful for posterior placenta and surgical planning

### Management
MDT (tertiary centre) — obstetrics, gynae-oncology, vascular surgery, haematology, anaesthetics, neonatology, IR

### Delivery
Planned C-section hysterectomy at 34-36 weeks; leave placenta in situ if extensive; avoid attempts at removal

### Conservative
Leave placenta in situ + methotrexate (controversial) ± UAE; successful in 60-80% but risk of sepsis/hemorrhage

### Future pregnancy
Fertility preserved if uterus retained; recurrence risk; need careful counselling
