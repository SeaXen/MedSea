# Ectopic pregnancy

---

## Definition

Ectopic pregnancy is implantation of a fertilised ovum outside the uterine cavity, most commonly in the fallopian tube (95%). It is a leading cause of first-trimester maternal mortality due to rupture and haemorrhage. Early diagnosis using β-hCG and transvaginal ultrasound is essential.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Pregnancy implanted outside uterine cavity

### Incidence
1-2% of pregnancies; rising with IVF, PID, smoking

### Sites
Ampullary (70%); isthmic (12%); fimbrial (11%); interstitial (2-4%); cervical, ovarian, abdominal (rare)

### Risk factors
Prior ectopic (10x); tubal damage (PID, surgery, endometriosis); IUD; smoking; IVF; tubal ligation

### Presentation
Amenorrhoea 6-8 weeks; abdominal pain; vaginal bleeding; syncope; shoulder tip pain (diaphragmatic irritation); hypovolaemic shock if ruptured

### Diagnosis
β-hCG (often <1000 and falling/rising abnormally); TVUS (no IUP + adnexal mass + free fluid); progesterone; diagnostic laparoscopy gold standard

### Differential
Miscarriage; molar pregnancy; appendicitis; PID; ovarian torsion; corpus luteum cyst

### Management unruptured
Methotrexate (50 mg/m² IM; stable; mass <3.5 cm; no fetal heart; β-hCG <5000); mifepristone + misoprostol

### Surgical management
Salpingectomy (ruptured, unstable, large mass, fetal heart); salpingostomy (conservative; risk of persistent trophoblast)

### Follow-up
Serial β-hCG until <5; counsel future fertility; recurrence 10%
