# Glomerulonephritis in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Glomerulonephritis (GN) in pregnancy — including IgA nephropathy, membranous nephropathy, focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS), and lupus nephritis — requires careful management. Pregnancy is generally safe with preserved renal function but carries risks of disease progression, hypertension, and adverse fetal outcomes.

## Core Clinical Content

### Preconception
Stable renal function >1-2 years; creatinine <125 μmol/L; proteinuria <1 g/24h; BP controlled; switch teratogens (MMF → azathioprine)

### IgA nephropathy
Most common primary GN; often well tolerated if Cr <125; pre-eclampsia risk increased

### Membranous nephropathy
Nephrotic syndrome; risk of VTE; ACEI/ARB pre-conception; monitor

### FSGS
Risk of disease progression; nephrotic-range proteinuria worsens outcomes

### Lupus nephritis (class III/IV/V)
Active disease at conception → 50% flare; quiescent for 6 months → 5-10% flare; careful MDT

### Maternal risks
Worsening renal function; hypertension; pre-eclampsia; nephrotic syndrome; VTE; infection; CKD progression

### Fetal risks
Preterm; IUGR; miscarriage; stillbirth; neonatal complications of prematurity

### Investigations
Monthly U&E; urinalysis; urine PCR; 24-h proteinuria; anti-dsDNA, C3/C4 (lupus); anti-PLA2R (membranous); complement

### Management
Treat hypertension (labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa); avoid ACEI/ARB; steroids; azathioprine; calcineurin inhibitors; LMWH if nephrotic

### Postpartum
Resume ACEI/ARB; review immunosuppression; breastfeeding; contraception (avoid oestrogen if high VTE risk)
