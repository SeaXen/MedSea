# Hyperemesis gravidarum

---

## Definition

Hyperemesis gravidarum is severe nausea and vomiting in pregnancy causing dehydration, electrolyte disturbance, and weight loss >5% of pre-pregnancy weight, requiring hospital admission. It typically begins before 12 weeks and resolves by 16-20 weeks.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Severe nausea/vomiting before 20 weeks; dehydration; ketones; >5% weight loss; needs admission

### Epidemiology
0.3-2% of pregnancies; commoner in younger, primigravid, non-smokers, multiple pregnancy

### Aetiology
hCG, oestrogen, GDF15, thyroid, H. pylori, psychosomatic

### Differential
Molar pregnancy; UTI; thyrotoxicosis; Addison's; pancreatitis; hepatitis; GI obstruction

### Complications
Wernicke's (thiamine); Mallory-Weiss; oesophageal tear; hypokalaemia; hyponatraemia; renal impairment

### Investigations
U&E, LFT, TFT (transient hyperthyroidism in 50%), USS (molar/twins)

### First-line treatment
Reassurance; lifestyle; ginger; P6 acupressure; antihistamines (cyclizine, promethazine)

### Second-line
Metoclopramide; ondansetron (data reassuring but theoretical cardiac risk); prochlorperazine

### Third-line
Corticosteroids (prednisolone/prednisone — avoid 1st trimester if possible); enteral/parenteral nutrition

### Prevention
Pre-emptive multivitamin + ginger at conception in high-risk women
