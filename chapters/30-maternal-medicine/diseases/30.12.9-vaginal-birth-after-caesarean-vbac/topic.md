# Vaginal birth after caesarean (VBAC)

---

## Definition

Vaginal birth after caesarean (VBAC) is a successful vaginal delivery in a woman with a previous caesarean section. Trial of labour after caesarean (TOLAC) is appropriate in selected women with one prior low-transverse C-section. Success rates are 60-80% with appropriate selection; uterine rupture risk is ~0.5%.

## Core Clinical Content

### Eligibility
One prior low-transverse C-section; no other uterine scar; adequate pelvis; no recurrent indication; obstetrician + anaesthesia available

### Success rate
60-80% overall; 70-90% if prior vaginal delivery; 60% if no prior vaginal

### Uterine rupture risk
0.5% for TOLAC vs 0.03% for elective repeat C-section; 1% with prostaglandin induction

### Contraindications
Prior classical/vertical/T incision; prior uterine rupture; prior extensive surgery; placenta praevia/accreta; multiple pregnancy (relative); patient refusal

### Benefits of success
Avoid repeat C-section; lower maternal morbidity; lower VTE/infection; faster recovery; future pregnancies less complex

### Risks of TOLAC
Uterine rupture (emergency hysterectomy, fetal death, maternal haemorrhage); failed TOLAC → C-section (higher morbidity)

### Place of birth
Tertiary unit with on-site obstetrician, anaesthetist, theatre, blood bank; continuous CTG; IV access; 1:1 midwifery care

### Monitoring
Continuous CTG; review 2nd stage progress; avoid prolonged 2nd stage; consider early ARM; avoid prostaglandin if possible

### Analgesia
Epidural not contraindicated; allows faster intervention if needed

### Future pregnancies
Each repeat C-section increases placenta praevia/accreta risk; generally 2-3 VBACs accepted; shared decision-making
