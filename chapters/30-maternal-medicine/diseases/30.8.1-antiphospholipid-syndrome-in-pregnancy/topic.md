# Antiphospholipid syndrome in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Antiphospholipid syndrome (APS) in pregnancy is associated with recurrent miscarriage, fetal loss, pre-eclampsia, IUGR, and thrombosis. Diagnosis requires persistent antiphospholipid antibodies plus clinical features. Treatment with aspirin ± LMWH improves pregnancy outcomes.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Persistent aPL antibodies + clinical event (thrombosis or pregnancy morbidity)

### Antibodies
Lupus anticoagulant (strongest); anticardiolipin; anti-β2 glycoprotein I

### Obstetric morbidity
≥3 consecutive <10-week losses; ≥1 ≥10-week loss; preterm <34 weeks with PE/placental insufficiency

### Thrombotic risk
Arterial (stroke, MI) and venous (DVT, PE); pregnancy adds risk

### Catastrophic APS
Rapid multi-organ thrombosis; mortality ~30%; intensive care; triple therapy

### Preconception
Confirm antibodies 12 weeks apart; counsel; plan aspirin + LMWH

### Treatment
Aspirin 75-150 mg daily + prophylactic LMWH throughout pregnancy; therapeutic LMWH if prior VTE

### Monitoring
Uterine artery Doppler at 20-24 weeks; growth scans; pre-eclampsia surveillance

### Postpartum
Continue LMWH 6 weeks; lifelong anticoagulation if thrombosis

### Prognosis
Live birth 70-80% with treatment; recurrence 5-10%
