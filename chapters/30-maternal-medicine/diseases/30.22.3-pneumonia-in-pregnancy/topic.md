# Pneumonia in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Pneumonia in pregnancy is a leading non-obstetric cause of maternal mortality. Physiological changes (raised diaphragm, reduced functional residual capacity, altered immunity) increase susceptibility and severity. Prompt empirical antibiotics, oxygen, and supportive care are essential.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
0.5-1.5% of pregnancies; leading non-obstetric infection

### Pathogens
Strep pneumoniae; H. influenzae; atypicals (Mycoplasma, Chlamydia); Legionella; viral (influenza, COVID-19)

### Risk factors
Asthma; anaemia; GDM; HIV; immunosuppression; smoking; substance misuse

### Presentation
Fever, productive cough, dyspnoea, pleuritic chest pain, tachypnoea, hypoxia

### Investigations
CXR (safe, lead shield); blood cultures; sputum culture; urinary antigens; viral PCR

### Empirical therapy
Co-amoxiclav + clarithromycin IV; or ceftriaxone + azithromycin

### Severe disease
Oxygen to maintain SpO2 ≥94%; consider CPAP; ITU/HDU; early delivery if deteriorating

### Influenza
Treat with oseltamivir within 48 h of symptoms

### Complications
ARDS; empyema; lung abscess; preterm labour; fetal loss

### Prevention
Influenza and pneumococcal vaccination preconception; smoking cessation
