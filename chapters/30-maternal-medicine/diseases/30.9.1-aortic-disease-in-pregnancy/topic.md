# Aortic disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Aortic disease in pregnancy, including aortic aneurysm, aortic dissection, and conditions predisposing to dissection (Marfan, Loeys-Dietz, vascular Ehlers-Danlos, bicuspid aortic valve, coarctation), carries significant mortality. Pregnancy increases aortic wall stress due to hyperdynamic circulation and hormonal effects on connective tissue.

## Core Clinical Content

### Aortic dissection
1 per 10,000 pregnancies; 50% occur in 3rd trimester or postpartum; type A 2/3

### Risk factors
Marfan syndrome (5-10% risk); bicuspid aortic valve; coarctation; HTN; cocaine; trauma; vascular Ehlers-Danlos

### Marfan in pregnancy
Aortic root >45 mm — pregnancy contraindicated; 40-45 mm — individualised; <40 mm — generally safe with β-blocker

### Presentation dissection
Severe tearing chest/back pain; pulse deficits; BP differential; aortic regurgitation murmur; shock; neurological signs

### Diagnosis
CT aortogram (gold standard); TTE/TEE; MRI; ECG (LVH, ischaemia); D-dimer may be raised

### Management dissection
Type A: emergency surgery (Caesarean if viable fetus first); Type B: medical (β-blocker, analgesia) ± TEVAR

### Medical management
β-blocker (labetalol, metoprolol) to reduce dP/dt; target HR <70; systolic BP 100-120 mmHg; analgesia

### Surgical
Aortic root replacement; valve-sparing; Caesarean at same time if needed

### Preconception
Aortic root imaging; replace if >45 mm; genetic counselling; switch teratogens; β-blocker preconception

### Delivery
MDT; epidural (reduces stress response); assisted second stage; avoid ergometrine; consider C-section in Marfan >40 mm
