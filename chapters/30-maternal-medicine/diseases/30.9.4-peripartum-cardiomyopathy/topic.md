# Peripartum cardiomyopathy

---

## Definition

Peripartum cardiomyopathy is an idiopathic cardiomyopathy presenting with heart failure in the last month of pregnancy or within 5 months postpartum, in the absence of other identifiable causes. Diagnosis requires LV systolic dysfunction (LVEF <45%).

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Idiopathic cardiomyopathy with LVEF <45% presenting last month of pregnancy or within 5 months postpartum

### Epidemiology
1 in 1,000-4,000 births; higher in Black women, age >30, multiparity, pre-eclampsia

### Pathophysiology
Likely cleaved prolactin, sFlt-1, vasculopathy, viral, autoimmune

### Presentation
Dyspnoea, orthopnoea, fatigue, oedema, palpitations, chest pain — often misdiagnosed as 'normal pregnancy'

### Diagnosis
Echo (LVEF <45%); ECG; BNP/NT-proBNP; exclude other causes (TTE, CMR)

### Differential
Pre-existing dilated cardiomyopathy; pre-eclampsia/HELLP with cardiac involvement; PE; sepsis

### Acute management
Oxygen, diuretics (furosemide), vasodilators; ACEI/ARB/ARNI postpartum; beta-blocker; bromocriptine 2.5 mg BD 2 weeks (reduces mortality)

### Anticoagulation
LMWH if LVEF <35% or AF

### Subsequent pregnancy
Contraindicated if LVEF <50%; if LVEF recovers to >50% — close monitoring

### Prognosis
~50-70% recover LV function by 6 months; 10-25% deteriorate; 5-year mortality 5-25%

- **Mnemonic for Peripartum cardiomyopathy** — see key points in Core Clinical Content
- **'PRE-ECLAMPSIA' risk factors** — Previous PE, Renal disease, Extremes of age, Chronic HTN, Ethnicity, Antiphospholipid, nulliparity, Multiple, Obesity, Smoking
- **'Mnemonic: H-E-L-L-P'** — Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets
- **'CHAP' trial target** — Chronic HTN treated to <140/90 mmHg
- **'M-M-R-V' safe vaccines in pregnancy** — Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR, avoid), inactivated influenza, COVID-19, Whooping cough (dTpa)
