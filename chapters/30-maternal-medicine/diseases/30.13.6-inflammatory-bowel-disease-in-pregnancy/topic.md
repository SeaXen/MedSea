# Inflammatory bowel disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) — Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis — commonly affects women in childbearing years. Active disease at conception is the strongest predictor of flare in pregnancy and adverse outcomes. Most medications (5-ASA, corticosteroids, anti-TNF) are safe; methotrexate is teratogenic and contraindicated.

## Core Clinical Content

### Fertility
Generally normal in UC and quiescent Crohn; decreased in active disease; pelvic surgery affects fertility

### Preconception
Achieve remission ≥3-6 months before conception; continue maintenance therapy; switch teratogens (MTX stop 3 months)

### Disease activity
Active disease at conception → 70% active during pregnancy; remission → 70% stay in remission

### Maternal risks
Flare; malnutrition; anaemia; thromboembolism; surgical complications; need for surgery

### Fetal risks
Active disease → preterm; LBW; stillbirth; miscarriage; congenital abnormalities if on teratogens

### Safe medications
Mesalazine (5-ASA); sulfasalazine (with folate); corticosteroids (prednisolone, budesonide); azathioprine/6-MP; infliximab/adalimumab (limited placental transfer 1st trimester, then crosses — check levels)

### Avoid
Methotrexate (teratogenic); mycophenolate (teratogenic — switch pre-conception); metronidazole in 1st trimester (some concerns); ciclosporin (limited data but used in severe UC)

### Investigations during pregnancy
Faecal calprotectin; CRP; USS; MRI without gadolinium; flexible sigmoidoscopy safe; colonoscopy rarely if essential

### Management of flare
Similar to non-pregnant: 5-ASA; steroids; biologic; consider colectomy if life-threatening (UC)

### Delivery
Vaginal usual; consider C-section if active perianal Crohn; mode per obstetric indication; continue medications intrapartum
