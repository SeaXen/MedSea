# Arrhythmias in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Arrhythmias in pregnancy include atrial and ventricular ectopics, supraventricular tachycardia (SVT), atrial fibrillation/flutter, and ventricular tachycardia. They are usually benign but may indicate underlying heart disease, thyroid dysfunction, or electrolyte disturbance. Treatment is similar to non-pregnant with attention to fetal safety of drugs.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
Palpitations common (50%); AF rare (2/1000) — usually with structural heart disease or hyperthyroidism

### Causes
Structural heart disease; hyperthyroidism; electrolyte disturbance; stimulants; caffeine; alcohol; drug use; pre-excitation (WPW); congenital arrhythmia syndromes

### SVT
Most common sustained arrhythmia in pregnancy; vagal manoeuvres first; adenosine if fails (safe in pregnancy); β-blocker; verapamil; DC cardioversion if unstable

### Atrial fibrillation/flutter
Often with structural heart disease (MS, congenital); anticoagulate (LMWH); rate control (β-blocker, digoxin); cardioversion if unstable

### Ventricular tachycardia
Rare; assess for structural disease and channelopathy (Long QT, Brugada, CPVT); DC cardioversion if unstable; ICD if recurrent; avoid QT-prolonging drugs

### Bradyarrhythmias
Rare; consider heart block (congenital or SLE); pacing if symptomatic; complete heart block often needs permanent pacing

### Drug safety
Adenosine, β-blockers (labetalol, metoprolol), verapamil, diltiazem, digoxin, lidocaine — generally safe; amiodarone avoid (thyroid, fetal); flecainide safe; sotalol safe

### Investigation
ECG; 24-h Holter; echo; TFT; electrolytes; consider EP study postpartum

### Anticoagulation
Score and consider LMWH if AF >48 h or high risk; cardioversion with TOE if duration unclear

### Delivery planning
Avoid Valsalva; consider assisted second stage; consider epidural; continuous CTG; postpartum monitoring
