# Placental abruption

---

## Definition

Placental abruption (abruptio placentae) is the premature separation of a normally implanted placenta from the uterine wall after 20 weeks' gestation and before delivery. It presents with painful vaginal bleeding, uterine tenderness, and hypertonic ('woody') uterus, and is a major cause of maternal and fetal morbidity and mortality.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Premature separation of normally sited placenta after 20 weeks

### Incidence
0.5-1% of pregnancies; 10-15% of perinatal mortality

### Risk factors
Prior abruption (10x); hypertension (strongest); smoking; cocaine; trauma; PPROM; multiple pregnancy; polyhydramnios; thrombophilia

### Presentation
Painful vaginal bleeding (80%); uterine tenderness/rigidity ('woody'); hypertonic uterus; absent/reduced fetal movements; shock; DIC; fetal distress/death

### Concealed abruption
Retroplacental clot; no visible bleeding; 20%

### Diagnosis
Clinical; USS (sensitivity <50%); CTG abnormal; exclude praevia

### Investigations
FBC, coagulation (DIC screen), crossmatch, U&E, Kleihauer (if Rh-)

### Management
Resuscitate (ABC, IV access, fluids); correct coagulopathy; delivery if unstable (vaginal if possible; C-section if fetal salvage required); steroids if preterm

### Complications
Maternal: shock, DIC, AKI, PPH, Couvelaire uterus, Sheehan; Fetal: 15-20% mortality; preterm; asphyxia

### Future pregnancy
Recurrence 5-15% (10% if one, 25% if two); aspirin prophylaxis; close surveillance
