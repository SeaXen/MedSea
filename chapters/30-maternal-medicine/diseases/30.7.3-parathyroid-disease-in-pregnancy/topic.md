# Parathyroid disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Parathyroid disease in pregnancy includes primary hyperparathyroidism (usually parathyroid adenoma) and hypoparathyroidism. Hyperparathyroidism in pregnancy is associated with miscarriage, pre-eclampsia, neonatal hypocalcaemia, and renal stones. Hypoparathyroidism is usually iatrogenic post-thyroidectomy.

## Core Clinical Content

### Primary hyperparathyroidism
Rare in pregnancy; most common cause: parathyroid adenoma (85%); associated with MEN1, MEN2A

### Presentation
Often asymptomatic; stones, bones, groans, psychiatric overtones; nausea, vomiting, fatigue, depression

### Risks to mother
Hyperemesis; renal stones; pancreatitis; pre-eclampsia; hypercalcaemic crisis; fractures

### Risks to fetus/neonate
Miscarriage; IUGR; preterm birth; neonatal hypocalcaemia (suppressed parathyroids) — tetany, seizures

### Diagnosis
Corrected calcium (albumin-adjusted or ionised); PTH; 24-h urinary calcium; vitamin D; phosphate; consider sestamibi scan/US if needed

### Management
Hydration; oral phosphate; cinacalcet (limited data); parathyroidectomy in 2nd trimester if severe/refractory

### Severe hypercalcaemia (>3.0)
IV fluids; bisphosphonates avoid; calcitonin; consider surgery; parathyroidectomy preferred

### Hypoparathyroidism
Usually iatrogenic post-thyroidectomy or autoimmune; replacement with calcitriol + calcium

### Risks of hypoparathyroidism
Hypocalcaemia → tetany, seizures, laryngospasm; long-QT; preterm labour

### Postpartum
Monitor calcium; breastfed infants at risk of hypocalcaemia; re-evaluate replacement doses
