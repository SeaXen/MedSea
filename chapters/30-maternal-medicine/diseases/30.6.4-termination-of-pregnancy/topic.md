# Termination of pregnancy

---

## Definition

Termination of pregnancy (induced abortion) is the medical or surgical ending of pregnancy. In the UK it is governed by the Abortion Act 1967 (with subsequent amendments). Most terminations are performed in the first trimester using medical (mifepristone + misoprostol) or surgical (suction curettage) methods. Complications are uncommon but include bleeding, infection, and uterine perforation.

## Core Clinical Content

### Legal (UK)
Abortion Act 1967: <24 weeks if risk to mother, child, or woman's health; any time if serious risk

### First-trimester methods
Medical (mifepristone 200 mg oral then misoprostol 800 μg PV/buccal 24-48 h later) OR surgical (manual vacuum aspiration up to 12 weeks)

### Second-trimester methods
Medical (mifepristone + repeated misoprostol); D&E (dilation and evacuation)

### Feticide
For terminations >22 weeks; intracardiac potassium chloride or lidocaine

### Complications
Haemorrhage (1%); infection (1-5%); retained products (1%); uterine perforation (<0.1%); Asherman syndrome (rare); psychological impact

### Pre-procedure
Counselling; consent; USS for gestation; blood group + anti-D; STI screen; contraception plan

### Post-procedure
Anti-D if Rh-; contraception initiated; β-hCG follow-up if medical; bleeding up to 2 weeks

### Contraindications medical
Severe anaemia; coagulopathy; ectopic; adrenal/liver disease; long-term steroids; IUD in situ

### Subsequent pregnancy
Usually no effect on fertility; small ↑preterm birth if multiple surgical; psychological support

### Recurrent
Provide contraception; LARC methods; emergency contraception plan
