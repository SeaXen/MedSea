# Fetal monitoring in labour

---

## Definition

Fetal monitoring in labour detects fetal hypoxia/acidosis to allow timely intervention (operative delivery) and prevent perinatal death and long-term neurological injury. Intermittent auscultation (low risk) and continuous electronic fetal monitoring (CTGs) are used. CTG interpretation requires pattern recognition and adherence to guidelines (NICE).

## Core Clinical Content

### Methods
Intermittent auscultation (low-risk labours); continuous electronic fetal monitoring (CTGs) for high-risk; fetal scalp electrode if needed

### Indications for continuous CTG
Previous C-section; oxytocin augmentation; epidural; meconium; abnormal admission CTG; maternal medical conditions; multiple pregnancy; IUGR; breech

### Fetal heart rate features
Baseline (110-160 bpm); variability (5-25); accelerations; decelerations (early, late, variable, prolonged)

### Reassuring
Baseline 110-160; variability >5; accelerations present; no decelerations

### Non-reassuring
Reduced variability; tachycardia (>160); bradycardia (<110); variable decelerations; late decelerations; complicated FHR

### Pathological
Bradycardia <100; absent variability with decelerations; prolonged deceleration >3 min; sinusoidal pattern

### Management suspicious/pathological
Maternal reposition; oxygen; stop oxytocin; IV fluids; FBC; consider FBS (fetal blood sampling — pH <7.2 = deliver)

### FBS
Fetal scalp blood pH; pH >7.25 continue; <7.2 deliver; 7.2-7.25 repeat in 30 min

### When to deliver
Persistent pathological trace despite resuscitation; prolonged bradycardia; FBS pH <7.2

### Limitations
High false positive; inter-observer variation; 50-60% of babies with non-reassuring CTG are not acidotic
