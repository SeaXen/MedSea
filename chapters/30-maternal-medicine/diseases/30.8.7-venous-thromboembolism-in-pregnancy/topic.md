# Venous thromboembolism in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Venous thromboembolism (VTE) — deep vein thrombosis and pulmonary embolism — is a leading direct cause of maternal mortality. Pregnancy increases VTE risk 4-5 fold due to venous stasis, hypercoagulability, and vascular damage. Prompt investigation and treatment with LMWH are essential.

## Core Clinical Content

### Risk
5-6x non-pregnant; 15-35x postpartum (first 6 weeks)

### Sites
DVT: 80% left leg; iliofemoral > calf in pregnancy; PE: postpartum > antenatal

### Risk factors
Prior VTE (strongest); obesity; age >35; thrombophilia; APS; caesarean; pre-eclampsia; multiple pregnancy; immobility; smoking

### Clinical features DVT
Unilateral leg swelling/pain; tenderness along venous tract; pitting oedema; low-grade fever

### Clinical features PE
Pleuritic chest pain; dyspnoea; tachycardia; haemoptysis; syncope; hypoxia

### Diagnosis DVT
Compression duplex ultrasound (no radiation); iliac vein imaging if negative

### Diagnosis PE
CXR; V/Q scan or CTPA (low fetal radiation); bilateral leg Doppler; D-dimer not useful

### Treatment
LMWH (enoxaparin 1 mg/kg BD); warfarin teratogenic; DOACs contraindicated; UFH if haemodynamic instability or delivery imminent

### Duration
Minimum 3 months and until 6 weeks postpartum

### Prevention
Risk assessment at booking and delivery; LMWH prophylaxis for intermediate/high risk; compression stockings; early mobilisation
