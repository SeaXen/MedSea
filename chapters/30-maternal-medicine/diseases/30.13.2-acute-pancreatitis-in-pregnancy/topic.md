# Acute pancreatitis in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Acute pancreatitis in pregnancy is uncommon (1 in 1000-3000 pregnancies) but carries significant maternal and fetal morbidity. Most cases are due to gallstones; hypertriglyceridaemia, alcohol, and idiopathic causes account for the remainder. Management is supportive with delivery considered in severe cases.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
1 in 1000-3000 pregnancies; rare but serious

### Causes
Gallstones (60-70% — commonest); hypertriglyceridaemia (familial or secondary); alcohol; idiopathic; drugs; hyperparathyroidism; post-ERCP; trauma

### Presentation
Severe epigastric pain radiating to back; nausea/vomiting; peritonism; fever; shock; jaundice

### Diagnosis
Serum amylase >3x ULN; lipase more specific; USS to detect gallstones; MRCP if needed; CT rarely; differential: PUD, biliary colic, abruption, PE

### Severity assessment
Atlanta criteria; BISAP score; CRP >150; organ failure (Atlanta severe)

### Complications
Pancreatic necrosis; pseudocyst; ARDS; AKI; DIC; sepsis; death; preterm labour; fetal loss

### Hypertriglyceridaemia-induced
Triglycerides >1000 mg/dL (>11 mmol/L); risk at >2000; usually with familial hypertriglyceridaemia

### Management
NPO + IV fluids; analgesia (pethidine or morphine); NG tube if vomiting; antibiotics if infected necrosis; supportive care

### ERCP/surgical
ERCP with sphincterotomy if biliary obstruction with cholangitis; cholecystectomy in 2nd trimester if stones; avoid in 1st and 3rd

### Delivery
Vaginal preferred if stable; consider early delivery in severe disease; mode per obstetric; close fetal monitoring
