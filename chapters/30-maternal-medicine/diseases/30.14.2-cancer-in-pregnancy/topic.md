# Cancer in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Cancer complicates 1 in 1,000-2,000 pregnancies. The most common malignancies are breast, cervical, melanoma, thyroid, lymphoma, and leukaemia. Management requires MDT balancing maternal prognosis, fetal viability, and gestational age.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
1 in 1,000-2,000 pregnancies; commoner as maternal age rises

### Common cancers
Breast (most common), cervical, melanoma, thyroid, lymphoma, leukaemia, colorectal

### Diagnosis
Often delayed because symptoms overlap with pregnancy; imaging without ionising radiation; tissue diagnosis essential

### Staging
MRI without gadolinium; ultrasound; CXR with shielding; avoid CT/PET where possible

### Surgery
Safe all trimesters; optimal 2nd trimester

### Chemotherapy
Avoid 1st trimester (teratogenicity); safe in 2nd/3rd trimesters; avoid chemo 3 weeks before delivery (myelosuppression)

### Radiotherapy
Generally avoided in pregnancy; scatter risks; only if fetus can be shielded and life-saving

### Targeted/immunotherapy
Limited data; trastuzumab causes oligohydramnios; checkpoint inhibitors can cause miscarriage

### End-of-pregnancy care
Delivery by 35-37 weeks to allow postpartum treatment; consider corticosteroids for fetal lung maturity

### Termination
Considered if aggressive malignancy; patient's choice; MDT input
