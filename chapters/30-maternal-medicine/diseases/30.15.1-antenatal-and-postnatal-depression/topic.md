# Antenatal and postnatal depression

---

## Definition

Antenatal (prenatal) and postnatal (postpartum) depression are common and treatable mood disorders affecting 10-15% of women in pregnancy and the year after birth. Distinguishing from normal postpartum adjustment ('baby blues'), anxiety, and postpartum psychosis is critical. Untreated illness has significant impact on mother, baby, and family.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
Antenatal depression 10-15%; postnatal depression 10-15%; underdiagnosed

### Risk factors
Prior depression (strongest); family history; lack of support; domestic violence; recent stress; teenage; substance use; social isolation

### Screening
Whooley + GAD-2 at booking and 6 weeks; EPDS ≥13 or positive Q10 (suicidal ideation) prompts further assessment

### Symptoms
Persistent low mood; anhedonia; sleep disturbance (despite baby sleeping); appetite change; fatigue; guilt; poor concentration; suicidal thoughts; poor bonding

### Differential
Baby blues (3-5 days, self-limiting); anxiety; OCD; PTSD; thyroid dysfunction; anaemia; postnatal psychosis (psychotic features)

### Treatment
Stepped care: self-help, peer support; psychological therapies (CBT, IPT — first-line); SSRIs (sertraline, citalopram — safe in pregnancy/lactation)

### Sertraline
First-line SSRI in pregnancy/lactation; lowest transfer to breast milk; data reassuring

### Avoid
Paroxetine (1st-trimester cardiac risk); benzodiazepines (neonatal withdrawal, floppy baby); lithium (cardiac — monitor); valproate (developmental delay)

### ECT
For severe, psychotic, or treatment-resistant depression; safe in pregnancy

### Multidisciplinary care
GP; HV; obstetrician; psychiatrist; perinatal mental health team; safeguarding; crisis plan
