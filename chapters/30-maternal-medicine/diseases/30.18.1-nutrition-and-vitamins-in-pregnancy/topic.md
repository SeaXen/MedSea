# Nutrition and vitamins in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Adequate nutrition and supplementation in pregnancy support maternal health and fetal development. Routine supplementation with folic acid and vitamin D is recommended; iron is added for anaemia. Avoid excess vitamin A (teratogenic) and foodborne pathogens.

## Core Clinical Content

### Energy needs
+200-300 kcal/day in 2nd/3rd trimesters; obese women do not need additional calories

### Folic acid
400 μg/day preconception to 12 weeks (reduce NTDs); 5 mg if high risk (prior NTD, antiepileptics, diabetes, obesity)

### Vitamin D
10 μg/day (400 IU) for all; monitor 25-OH-D if at risk; deficiency → neonatal rickets

### Iron
Routine from 16 weeks; 200 mg ferrous sulphate; IV if intolerance or severe

### Vitamin A
Avoid excess (>700 μg retinol); teratogenic; avoid liver, high-dose supplements; beta-carotene safe

### Calcium
1 g/day; supplementation in low-intake populations reduces pre-eclampsia

### Iodine
140 μg/day; iodised salt; avoid kelp supplements

### Foods to avoid
Soft cheeses, pâté, undercooked meat (listeria, toxoplasma); raw fish, high-mercury fish; alcohol

### Caffeine
<200 mg/day (2 cups coffee) — high intake may ↑miscarriage, IUGR

### Vegetarian/vegan
B12 supplementation; monitor iron, zinc, protein, omega-3
