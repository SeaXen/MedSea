# Malaria in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Malaria in pregnancy is a major cause of maternal and fetal morbidity in endemic regions (sub-Saharan Africa, Asia). Placental sequestration of Plasmodium falciparum causes severe anaemia, low birth weight, and increased stillbirth and neonatal mortality. Prevention with intermittent preventive treatment (IPT) and insecticide-treated nets is essential.

## Core Clinical Content

### Epidemiology
Endemic in 90+ countries; 30 million pregnancies at risk annually; 10,000 maternal deaths/year

### Pathophysiology
P. falciparum expresses VAR2CSA on RBCs; binds CSA on placenta; sequestration → placental malaria

### Complications maternal
Severe anaemia; hypoglycaemia; ARDS; cerebral malaria; AKI; death; pre-existing immunity reduced in pregnancy

### Complications fetal
Low birth weight (most common); IUGR; preterm; stillbirth; neonatal malaria; congenital malaria

### Diagnosis
Thick and thin blood films; rapid diagnostic test (RDT); PCR for species

### Treatment uncomplicated (P. falciparum)
Quinine + clindamycin (1st trimester); ACT (artemether-lumefantrine) 2nd/3rd trimesters

### Treatment severe
IV artesunate (all trimesters); exchange transfusion; ITU; supportive

### P. vivax/ovale
Chloroquine; avoid primaquine in pregnancy (G6PD deficiency and foetal haemolysis); radical cure postpartum

### Prevention
IPTp with sulfadoxine-pyrimethamine (2-3 doses after 1st trimester; not in HIV on cotrimoxazole); insecticide-treated nets; mefloquine prophylaxis (if resistant)

### Postpartum
Treat vivax/ovale; contraception; preconception counselling; HIV/screening
