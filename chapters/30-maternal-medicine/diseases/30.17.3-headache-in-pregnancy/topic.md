# Headache in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Headache is the most common neurological symptom in pregnancy. Most are benign (tension, migraine), but pregnancy-specific and serious causes include pre-eclampsia, subarachnoid haemorrhage, cerebral venous sinus thrombosis, and pituitary apoplexy. New severe headache needs urgent evaluation.

## Core Clinical Content

### Common causes
Tension (most common); migraine (improves in 60%); sinusitis; neck pain

### Red flags
Thunderclap onset; worst-ever; new after 20 weeks; fever; focal deficits; seizures; post-partum onset; papilloedema

### Migraine
Often improves in 2nd/3rd trimesters (rising oestrogen); aura unchanged; severe — exclude CVST

### Pre-eclampsia headache
Bifrontal, throbbing, worse in morning; new after 20 weeks; check BP, urate, urine PCR

### Subarachnoid haemorrhage
Thunderclap; vomiting; photophobia; neck stiffness; CT then LP if negative

### CVST
Headache progressive over days; seizures; focal deficits; postpartum; risk factors — dehydration, infection, prothrombotic

### Pituitary apoplexy
Sudden severe headache; visual loss; ophthalmoplegia; cortisol deficiency — emergency

### Imaging
MRI without contrast preferred; CT if MRI unavailable (low fetal dose)

### Treatment migraine
Paracetamol 1st; ibuprofen 2nd trimester; triptans limited data; metoclopramide; avoid ergot

### Prevention
Avoid triggers; propranolol safe; topiramate teratogenic
