# Placenta praevia

---

## Definition

Placenta praevia is implantation of the placenta in the lower uterine segment, covering or near the internal cervical os. It presents with painless, recurrent, bright-red vaginal bleeding in the second half of pregnancy. Diagnosis is by transabdominal and transvaginal ultrasound; delivery is by planned caesarean section.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Placenta covering or within 2 cm of internal os (major if covers, minor if low-lying)

### Incidence
0.5-1% of pregnancies; rising with caesarean and advanced maternal age

### Risk factors
Prior caesarean (major risk); prior placenta praevia; multiparity; multiple pregnancy; advanced maternal age; smoking; prior uterine surgery/curettage

### Presentation
Painless bright-red bleeding (often recurrent); may be asymptomatic; malpresentation (breech/transverse)

### Diagnosis
Transabdominal + transvaginal USS (definitive); MRI if accreta suspected; do NOT do digital vaginal examination

### Management
Expectant if preterm (transfuse, steroids <34 weeks); iron supplementation; thromboprophylaxis; delivery by caesarean

### Timing of delivery
Elective caesarean 36-37 weeks; emergency if heavy bleeding or fetal/maternal compromise

### Placenta accreta
Placenta abnormally adherent (accreta/increta/percreta); risk with prior C-section + praevia; may need hysterectomy

### Complications
Haemorrhage; shock; hysterectomy; preterm birth; fetal growth restriction; vasa praevia; recurrence 4-8%

### Antenatal care
MDT (obstetrician, anaesthetist, haematologist); blood available; cell salvage; consider interventional radiology
