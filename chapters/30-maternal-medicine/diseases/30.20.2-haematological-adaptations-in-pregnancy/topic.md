# Haematological adaptations in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Haematological changes in pregnancy include plasma volume expansion outpacing red cell mass (dilutional anaemia), leucocytosis, thrombocytopenia (gestational), and a prothrombotic shift in coagulation factors. These influence normal reference ranges and threshold for investigation.

## Core Clinical Content

### Plasma volume
+40-50%; peaks 32 weeks

### Red cell mass
+20-30% (greater with iron supplementation)

### Physiological anaemia
Hb ~110 g/L (trimester 1) and 105 g/L (trimester 3) is normal

### MCV
Rises; mean ~85-95 fL

### White cell count
Rises to 9-15 × 10^9/L; neutrophils predominate

### Platelets
Mild fall (gestational thrombocytopenia); <100 × 10^9/L needs workup

### Coagulation factors
I, II, VII, VIII, IX, X, XII, XIII rise; protein S falls; APC resistance rises

### Fibrinolysis
Reduced (PAI-1, PAI-2 rise) — net prothrombotic state

### ESR
Markedly elevated; not useful in pregnancy

### Clinical implications
VTE risk 4-5x; consider LMWH prophylaxis at delivery
