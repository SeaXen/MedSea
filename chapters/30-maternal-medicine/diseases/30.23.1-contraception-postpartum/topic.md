# Contraception postpartum

---

## Definition

Postpartum contraception should be discussed antenatally and initiated before hospital discharge. Breastfeeding, VTE risk, and future fertility influence choice. Lactational amenorrhoea is effective only if exclusively breastfeeding and amenorrhoeic.

## Core Clinical Content

### Counselling
Discuss antenatally; document plan; offer before discharge; consider breastfeeding, VTE, future fertility

### Lactational amenorrhoea
98% effective if <6 months, exclusive breastfeeding, amenorrhoeic; LARC fails if any of these relax

### Postpartum timing
Ovulation can return at 6 weeks (earlier in non-breastfeeding); contraception needed from 21 days

### Combined hormonal contraception (CHC)
Avoid <21 days; if VTE risk factors, avoid <42 days; not generally recommended in breastfeeding <6 months

### Progestogen-only pill (POP)
Safe from 21 days; breastfeeding compatible; desogestrel POP preferred for efficacy

### Injectable (DMPA)
Safe; breastfeeding compatible; delayed return of fertility (6-12 months)

### Implant (Nexplanon)
Safe; breastfeeding compatible; quick to insert; lasts 3 years

### IUD (copper)
Insertion 4-6 weeks postpartum; safe; non-hormonal; lasts 10 years; increased bleeding

### IUS (Mirena)
Insertion 4-6 weeks postpartum (or at caesarean); safe; breastfeeding; reduced bleeding; lasts 8 years (off-licence 5)

### Sterilisation
Female sterilisation at caesarean or laparoscopy; male vasectomy ideal; counsel carefully — regret 5-10%
