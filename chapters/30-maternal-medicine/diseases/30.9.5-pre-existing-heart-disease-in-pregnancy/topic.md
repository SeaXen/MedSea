# Pre-existing heart disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Pre-existing heart disease affects 1-4% of pregnancies and is a leading cause of maternal mortality. Risk assessment (modified WHO classification) and multidisciplinary care in a specialist centre are essential. Pregnancy is generally contraindicated in WHO class IV conditions.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
1-4% of pregnancies; cardiac disease is leading non-obstetric cause of maternal death

### Modified WHO classification
I: small risk; II: small-moderate; III: significant (specialist care); IV: pregnancy contraindicated

### WHO IV (contraindicated)
Severe systemic ventricular dysfunction (EF <30%); severe MS/AS; previous PPCM with residual EF <50%; cyanotic CHD; Marfan with aortic root >45 mm

### Common conditions
Congenital (ASD, VSD, ToF, Marfan, bicuspid aortic valve); acquired (rheumatic, MVP, ischaemic); cardiomyopathy (PPCMP)

### Preconception
Echo, ECG, medication review; switch ACEI/warfarin; genetic counselling if inherited

### Antenatal care
Multidisciplinary team; review every 4-6 weeks; serial BNP/echo; anaemia prevention

### Labour and delivery
Vaginal usually preferred; assisted second stage; avoid supine position; early epidural

### Antibiotic prophylaxis
For delivery in women with prosthetic valves or unrepaired cyanotic CHD

### VTE prophylaxis
LMWH if class III/IV; immobility; mechanical valves

### Postpartum
Haemodynamic monitoring 24-48 h; warfarin can be restarted; breastfeeding compatible with most cardiac drugs
