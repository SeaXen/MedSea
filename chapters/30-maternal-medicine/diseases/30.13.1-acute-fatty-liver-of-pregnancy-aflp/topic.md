# Acute fatty liver of pregnancy (AFLP)

---

## Definition

Acute fatty liver of pregnancy is a rare but life-threatening mitochondrial disorder presenting in the 3rd trimester with nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, jaundice, hepatic failure, encephalopathy, and DIC. Delivery is curative but supportive care is critical.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Microvesicular fatty infiltration of liver; 3rd trimester; liver failure, encephalopathy, DIC

### Epidemiology
1 in 7,000-20,000; associated with LCHAD fetal enzyme deficiency

### Symptoms
Nausea, vomiting, anorexia, abdominal pain, jaundice, thirst, polyuria, encephalopathy

### Investigations
LFT (AST/ALT ↑); bilirubin ↑; coagulopathy (INR ↑); hypoglycaemia; hyperammonaemia; leucocytosis; renal failure; elevated creatinine

### Swansea criteria
≥6 of: vomiting, abdominal pain, polydipsia/polyuria, encephalopathy, bilirubin >14, hypoglycaemia <4, uric acid >340, leucocytosis >11, ascites, hepatorenal syndrome, microvesicular steatosis on biopsy, coagulopathy

### Differential
HELLP, viral hepatitis, drug-induced, TTP, autoimmune hepatitis

### Management
Stabilise (glucose, FFP, platelets); ITU; delivery once stabilised; supportive care

### Delivery
Vaginal preferred if stable; may need LSCS

### Postpartum
Recovery over days to weeks; LFT and coagulopathy; monitor for pancreatitis, AKI

### Future pregnancy
Recurrence low; screen fetus for LCHAD; avoid in subsequent
