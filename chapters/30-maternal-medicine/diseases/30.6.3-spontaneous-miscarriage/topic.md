# Spontaneous miscarriage

---

## Definition

Spontaneous miscarriage (spontaneous abortion) is the loss of pregnancy before 24 weeks' gestation. It affects 10-20% of confirmed pregnancies and presents with vaginal bleeding, abdominal pain, and passage of products of conception. Management is supportive, medical (misoprostol), or surgical (ERPC) according to clinical scenario and patient preference.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Pregnancy loss before 24 weeks' gestation (UK); 20 weeks (WHO)

### Incidence
10-20% of confirmed pregnancies; 80% occur in 1st trimester

### Types
Threatened; inevitable; incomplete; complete; missed; septic; recurrent (≥3)

### Causes
Chromosomal (50%, esp. trisomies); uterine anomalies; endocrine; autoimmune; infection; thrombophilia; trauma; idiopathic

### Risk factors
Advanced maternal age; prior miscarriage; smoking; alcohol; obesity; uterine anomalies

### Presentation
Vaginal bleeding; lower abdominal pain; products of conception passed; cervical dilation

### Investigations
β-hCG serial (doubles 48 h normally); TVUS (fetal heart from 5-6 weeks); blood group; FBC; coagulation if severe

### Management
Expectant; medical (misoprostol 800 μg PV); surgical (ERPC/VAE) — patient choice; anti-D if Rh-

### Complications
Haemorrhage; infection (septic miscarriage); DIC; psychological impact

### Follow-up
Bereavement support; recurrent miscarriage clinic if ≥3; pre-conception counselling; folic acid
