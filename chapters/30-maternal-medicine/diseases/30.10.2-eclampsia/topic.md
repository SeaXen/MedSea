# Eclampsia

---

## Definition

Eclampsia is the occurrence of new-onset generalised tonic-clonic seizures in a woman with pre-eclampsia. It is a life-threatening obstetric emergency requiring immediate stabilisation, magnesium sulphate, control of severe hypertension, and delivery.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Generalised seizures in PE/pregnancy; excludes other causes

### Timing
Antepartum (50%); intrapartum (25%); postpartum (25%, often within 48 h)

### Risk factors
Severe PE; nulliparity; multiple pregnancy; pre-existing HTN; renal disease

### Differential
Epilepsy; cerebral venous sinus thrombosis; stroke; SAH; encephalitis; metabolic

### Immediate management
ABC; left lateral tilt; oxygen; IV access; stop fitting

### Magnesium sulphate
4-6 g IV loading over 15-20 min; 1-2 g/h maintenance; 24 h post-delivery or post last fit

### Recurrent fits
Further 2 g IV bolus; if persists, consider diazepam or phenytoin

### BP control
Labetalol IV or hydralazine IV; target <160/110

### Definitive treatment
Delivery once stabilised; mode per obstetric indications

### Complications
Maternal: stroke, ARDS, DIC, HELLP, abruptio. Fetal: prematurity, asphyxia, death

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Eclampsia** — see key points in Core Clinical Content
- **'PRE-ECLAMPSIA' risk factors** — Previous PE, Renal disease, Extremes of age, Chronic HTN, Ethnicity, Antiphospholipid, nulliparity, Multiple, Obesity, Smoking
- **'Mnemonic: H-E-L-L-P'** — Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets
- **'CHAP' trial target** — Chronic HTN treated to <140/90 mmHg
- **'M-M-R-V' safe vaccines in pregnancy** — Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR, avoid), inactivated influenza, COVID-19, Whooping cough (dTpa)
