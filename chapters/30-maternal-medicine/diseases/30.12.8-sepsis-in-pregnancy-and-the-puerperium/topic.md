# Sepsis in pregnancy and the puerperium

---

## Definition

Sepsis is a leading direct cause of maternal mortality. Pregnancy-specific sources include chorioamnionitis, endometritis, urinary tract infection, wound infection, and mastitis. Recognition (modified SIRS/qSOFA), early antibiotics, and source control are critical.

## Core Clinical Content

### Epidemiology
Leading direct cause of maternal death in UK MBRRACE; 5-10% of severe maternal morbidity

### Sources
Genital tract (chorioamnionitis, endometritis); UTI; wound infection; mastitis; pneumonia; line infection

### Screening tools
SIRS modified for pregnancy (WCC >16, HR >110, T >38 or <36, RR >22); qSOFA — altered mental state, RR >22, BP <100

### Red flags
Fever, hypothermia, tachycardia, tachypnoea, hypotension, altered mental state, abdominal pain, foul lochia

### Microbiology
Group A Strep (GAS); E. coli; S. aureus; anaerobes; Clostridium

### Initial management
Sepsis 6 — oxygen; blood cultures; IV antibiotics within 1 h; IV fluids; lactate; urine output

### Empirical antibiotics
Piperacillin-tazobactam + gentamicin; consider metronidazole; add clindamycin if GAS suspected

### Source control
Evacuation of retained products; surgical intervention; line removal

### Escalation
ITU; inotropes; mechanical ventilation; multidisciplinary team

### Prevention
Hand hygiene; GBS prophylaxis; aseptic technique; reduce induction duration
