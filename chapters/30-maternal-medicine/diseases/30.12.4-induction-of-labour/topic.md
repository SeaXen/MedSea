# Induction of labour

---

## Definition

Induction of labour (IOL) is the artificial initiation of labour before spontaneous onset. Indications include post-term pregnancy, pre-eclampsia, maternal medical conditions, fetal growth restriction, and prolonged rupture of membranes. Methods include mechanical (Foley catheter) and pharmacological (prostaglandins, oxytocin), with choice based on cervical favourability and indication.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
20-30% of pregnancies in developed countries; rising

### Indications
Post-term (≥41 weeks); PE; maternal medical (diabetes, renal, lung); IUGR; cholestasis; PPROM; multiple pregnancy; Rhesus; previous stillbirth; maternal request (≥39 weeks)

### Contraindications
Placenta praevia; vasa praevia; transverse lie; cord prolapse; prior classical C-section or T-incision; active genital herpes; invasive cervical cancer

### Bishop score
Cervical favourability (0-13): dilation, effacement, station, position, consistency; <6 favours ripening

### Cervical ripening
Prostaglandin E2 (dinoprostone) vaginal gel/pessary; misoprostol (off-label in UK); mechanical (Foley catheter); safe in most settings

### Amniotomy
Artificial rupture of membranes (ARM); only if cervix favourable; combined with oxytocin for augmentation

### Oxytocin
Syntocinon infusion for induction/augmentation; titrate to contractions (4-5 per 10 min); monitor continuous CTG; risk of hyperstimulation

### Monitoring
Continuous CTG; partogram; maternal observations; pain control; review progress every 4 h (delay if <1 cm/2-4 h nullip, 1 cm/4 h multip)

### Failed induction
Rest; repeat ripening; consider C-section if failed after adequate trial

### Complications
Hyperstimulation (treat with tocolysis, terbutaline); uterine rupture (rare); failed induction; PPH; cord prolapse with ARM; uterine atony

- **Mnemonic for Induction of labour** — see key points in Core Clinical Content
- **'PRE-ECLAMPSIA' risk factors** — Previous PE, Renal disease, Extremes of age, Chronic HTN, Ethnicity, Antiphospholipid, nulliparity, Multiple, Obesity, Smoking
- **'Mnemonic: H-E-L-L-P'** — Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets
- **'CHAP' trial target** — Chronic HTN treated to <140/90 mmHg
- **'M-M-R-V' safe vaccines in pregnancy** — Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR, avoid), inactivated influenza, COVID-19, Whooping cough (dTpa)
