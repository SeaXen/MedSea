# Amniotic fluid embolism (AFE)

---

## Definition

Amniotic fluid embolism is a rare but catastrophic obstetric emergency in which amniotic fluid, fetal cells, or debris enter the maternal circulation causing anaphylactoid reaction, cardiovascular collapse, respiratory distress, DIC, and often death. Despite resuscitation, mortality is 20-60%.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Anaphylactoid reaction to amniotic fluid/debris in maternal circulation

### Epidemiology
1-2 in 40,000 deliveries; mortality 20-60%; leading cause of maternal death in some series

### Risk factors
Older age; multiparity; caesarean; induction; multiple pregnancy; placenta praevia; abruption; uterine rupture

### Classic triad
Sudden hypoxia and respiratory distress; shock/hypotension; DIC/bleeding — often with seizures

### Pathophysiology
Amniotic fluid → pulmonary vasculature → anaphylactoid reaction, ARDS, cardiac failure, DIC

### Differential
PE; eclampsia; aortic dissection; anaphylaxis; sepsis; haemorrhage; myocardial infarction

### Management
ABC; high-quality CPR; oxygen 100%; immediate delivery; ITU; massive transfusion; supportive

### Monitoring
Arterial line; CVP; urinary catheter; ABG; coagulation; echo

### Complications
ARDS; AKI; DIC; HIE; death; neurological sequelae in survivors

### Survivors
May need hysterectomy; psychological support; bereavement care
