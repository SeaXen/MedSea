# HELLP syndrome

---

## Definition

HELLP syndrome (Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets) is a severe variant of pre-eclampsia characterised by microangiopathic haemolysis, hepatic dysfunction, and thrombocytopenia. It carries high maternal and fetal mortality and is a medical emergency.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Haemolysis + Elevated Liver enzymes (AST/ALT >70) + Low Platelets (<100 × 10^9/L)

### Epidemiology
Affects 0.5-0.9% of pregnancies; 10-20% of severe PE

### Onset
Usually 27-37 weeks; 30% postpartum

### Symptoms
RUQ/epigastric pain, nausea, vomiting, headache, visual disturbance, oedema

### Diagnosis
Blood film (schistocytes, helmet cells); ↑LDH; ↑AST/ALT; ↓platelets; ↑bilirubin

### Differential
TMA — TTP (ADAMTS13 <10%), HUS, AFLP, ITP, acute fatty liver

### Management
Stabilise; magnesium sulphate; control BP; transfer to tertiary unit; corticosteroids if <34 weeks

### Delivery
Definitive treatment; mode based on cervical favourability and severity

### Postpartum
May worsen 24-48 h post-delivery; monitor platelets, LFT, LDH, creatinine

### Long-term
Increased CV risk; recurrence risk 5-20%; avoid oestrogen contraception
