# Caesarean section

---

## Definition

Caesarean section is a surgical procedure for delivery through an abdominal and uterine incision. Rates are rising globally; Robson classification allows comparison. Indications include fetal distress, malpresentation, placenta praevia, previous classical section, and maternal request.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
20-30% in developed countries; up to 50%+ in some regions; WHO ideal 10-15%

### Classification
Elective (planned) vs emergency (category 1-4); Robson classification (10 groups)

### Indications
Fetal distress; malpresentation; previous classical section; placenta praevia; multiple pregnancy; failure to progress; maternal request

### Pre-op
Anaesthetic review; consent; crossmatch; thromboprophylaxis; antibiotic prophylaxis; bladder catheter; WHO checklist

### Anaesthesia
Spinal (most common for elective); epidural top-up for emergency; GA for emergencies or coagulopathy

### Surgical technique
Pfannenstiel or low midline; lower segment transverse incision; peritoneal closure debated

### Complications
Haemorrhage; infection (5-10%); wound haematoma; urinary tract injury; VTE; Hysterectomy rare

### Post-op
Early mobilisation; VTE prophylaxis; regular analgesia; wound care; remove catheter early

### Recovery
2-3 days hospital; 6 weeks postnatal review; driving after 4-6 weeks; contraception discussion

### Subsequent pregnancy
Vaginal birth after caesarean (VBAC) — 60-80% success; risk of uterine rupture 0.5%

- **Mnemonic for Caesarean section** — see key points in Core Clinical Content
- **'PRE-ECLAMPSIA' risk factors** — Previous PE, Renal disease, Extremes of age, Chronic HTN, Ethnicity, Antiphospholipid, nulliparity, Multiple, Obesity, Smoking
- **'Mnemonic: H-E-L-L-P'** — Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets
- **'CHAP' trial target** — Chronic HTN treated to <140/90 mmHg
- **'M-M-R-V' safe vaccines in pregnancy** — Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR, avoid), inactivated influenza, COVID-19, Whooping cough (dTpa)
