# HIV in pregnancy

---

## Definition

HIV in pregnancy requires combined obstetric, HIV, and paediatric care to prevent vertical transmission. With effective combination antiretroviral therapy (cART), undetectable viral load, and appropriate delivery/feeding, the transmission rate falls below 0.5%.

## Core Clinical Content

### Vertical transmission rate
Without intervention 15-45%; with cART <0.5% (undetectable VL at delivery)

### Antenatal cART
All women regardless of CD4; tenofovir + emtricitabine + dolutegravir (1st trimester acceptable)

### Monitoring
CD4; viral load at booking, 28, 36 weeks, 4-6 weeks post-cART change; resistance if detectable VL

### Viral load at delivery
If <50 copies/mL — vaginal delivery acceptable; if >400 — planned caesarean at 38 weeks

### Intrapartum
Avoid invasive procedures; IV zidovudine if VL >1000; no artificial rupture of membranes; avoid instrumental

### Neonatal prophylaxis
Zidovudine for 4 weeks; if maternal VL >1000 — add nevirapine or raltegravir 3-drug regimen for 4-6 weeks

### Breastfeeding
Avoid in high-income settings; safe with cART in low-income (WHO)

### Maternal health
Continue cART; opportunistic infection prophylaxis if CD4 <200

### Preconception
Optimise cART; consider pre-exposure prophylaxis for partner; partner testing

### Postpartum
Continue cART; contraception; viral load 4-6 weeks; review resistance if failing
