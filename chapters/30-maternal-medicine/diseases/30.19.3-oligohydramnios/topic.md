# Oligohydramnios

---

## Definition

Oligohydramnios is reduced amniotic fluid volume (AFI <5 cm or DVP <2 cm). It is associated with fetal renal anomalies, IUGR, pre-labour rupture of membranes, and post-term pregnancy. Severe early-onset oligohydramnios causes pulmonary hypoplasia, skeletal deformities (Potter sequence), and high perinatal mortality.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
AFI <5 cm (or <5th centile) or DVP <2 cm

### Incidence
0.5-5% at term; higher post-term (12%)

### Causes
PPROM (most common at term); fetal renal anomalies (Potter, ARPKD, posterior urethral valves); IUGR/placental insufficiency; post-term; maternal drugs (NSAIDs, ACEI); twin-twin transfusion (donor); idiopathic

### Presentation
Fundal height < dates; fetal parts easily palpable; reduced fetal movements

### Investigations
Detailed anomaly scan (renal); UA Doppler (placental insufficiency); Kleihauer; review for PPROM; review drug history

### Severe early (<24 weeks)
Pulmonary hypoplasia (high mortality); Potter facies; limb deformities; renal agenesis lethal

### Monitoring
Serial USS; AFI twice weekly; CTG; consider admission if <34 weeks or severe

### Management
Treat cause if possible; amnioinfusion in labour (equivocal); hydration (maternal oral/IV may increase AFI transiently)

### Delivery
Induce at 36-37 weeks for isolated oligohydramnios; earlier if IUGR or non-reassuring; C-section for malpresentation; cord compression risk

### Antenatal corticosteroids
If preterm delivery planned and <34 weeks

### Prognosis
Depends on cause and gestation; isolated at term generally good
