# Thyroid disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Thyroid disease in pregnancy is common and includes overt hypothyroidism, subclinical hypothyroidism, isolated hypothyroxinaemia, hyperthyroidism (usually Graves'), and postpartum thyroiditis. Both maternal hypo- and hyperthyroidism adversely affect pregnancy and fetal development.

## Core Clinical Content

### Physiology
TBG rises (oestrogen); total T4/T3 rise; free T4 unchanged; TSH falls in 1st trimester (hCG)

### Trimester-specific ranges
TSH: 1st 0.1-2.5; 2nd 0.2-3.0; 3rd 0.3-3.0 mIU/L (population-specific)

### Hypothyroidism
Prevalence 1-2%; levothyroxine dose often needs increase 30-50% in 1st trimester; target TSH <2.5

### Hashimoto's
Most common cause; anti-TPO antibodies; monitor after delivery

### Hyperthyroidism
Prevalence 0.1-0.4%; 90% Graves' disease; risks: pre-eclampsia, IUGR, preterm, stillbirth, fetal thyrotoxicosis

### Treatment hyperthyroidism
Propylthiouracil (1st trimester) then switch to carbimazole; avoid methimazole in 1st trimester; target FT4 in upper normal range

### Side effects
PTU: hepatotoxicity; carbimazole: aplasia cutis, choanal atresia (1st trimester)

### Fetal monitoring
Fetal heart rate (>160 — thyrotoxicosis); growth; goitre on US

### Postpartum thyroiditis
Affects 5-10%; hyper then hypo phase; anti-TPO; screen at 6 weeks if high risk

### Postpartum care
Reassess thyroid function 6 weeks postpartum; reduce levothyroxine to pre-pregnancy dose
