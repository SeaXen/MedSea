# Renal and urinary tract adaptations in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Renal adaptations in pregnancy include marked increases in renal plasma flow and GFR, leading to lower serum creatinine and urea, glycosuria, and proteinuria. Hydronephrosis (especially right-sided) and ureteric dilatation are common.

## Core Clinical Content

### Renal plasma flow
+50-80% by mid-pregnancy

### GFR
+50%; peaks mid-pregnancy; falls towards term

### Serum creatinine
Falls (~35-70 μmol/L); values >77 μmol/L abnormal

### Urea
Falls (~1-4 mmol/L); >4.5 mmol/L suggests renal impairment

### Uric acid
Falls in 1st/2nd trimesters; rises in pre-eclampsia

### Glycosuria
Common (50%) — reduced tubular reabsorption, not necessarily diabetes

### Proteinuria
Up to 300 mg/24h normal; >300 mg/24h = significant

### Hydronephrosis
Right > left; mechanical obstruction; risk of pyelonephritis

### UTI risk
Increased urinary stasis + glycosuria; asymptomatic bacteriuria needs treatment

### Drug clearance
Renally cleared drugs (e.g. lithium, digoxin, β-lactams) may need dose adjustment
