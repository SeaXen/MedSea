# Urinary tract infection in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Urinary tract infection in pregnancy is common due to urinary stasis, glycosuria, and altered immunity. Asymptomatic bacteriuria (2-10%) must be treated because it frequently progresses to pyelonephritis (20-40%), with risk of preterm labour and low birth weight.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
Asymptomatic bacteriuria 2-10%; cystitis 1-4%; pyelonephritis 1-2%

### Pathogens
E. coli (75-90%); Klebsiella, Proteus, group B Strep, Enterococcus

### Asymptomatic bacteriuria
>10^5 CFU/mL on MSU — must be treated even without symptoms

### Cystitis
Dysuria, frequency, urgency, suprapubic pain; no fever or systemic features

### Pyelonephritis
Fever, flank pain, nausea, vomiting; can cause sepsis, preterm labour, ARDS

### Investigations
MSU culture; treat empirically in pyelonephritis; blood cultures if febrile

### Treatment ASB/cystitis
Nitrofurantoin (avoid near term), cephalexin, amoxicillin — 7 days

### Pyelonephritis
IV ceftriaxone or co-amoxiclav; admit; IV fluids; switch to oral when afebrile 24-48 h

### Drug safety
Avoid: trimethoprim (1st trimester — neural tube defects), quinolones, tetracyclines

### Recurrence
Monthly MSU until delivery; prophylactic nitrofurantoin or cephalexin if recurrent
