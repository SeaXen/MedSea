# Chronic kidney disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Chronic kidney disease in pregnancy poses risks of accelerated maternal renal decline, hypertension, pre-eclampsia, fetal growth restriction, and preterm birth. Preconception counselling and tight BP control are key. Pregnancy is generally avoided if creatinine >180 μmol/L (CKD 3b-5).

## Core Clinical Content

### Definitions
CKD stages 1-5 by GFR; pregnancy generally avoided if Cr >180 μmol/L or GFR <30

### Preconception
Optimise BP; switch ACEI/ARB; review immunosuppression; counsel on outcomes

### Maternal risks
Worsening renal function (especially Cr >125 μmol/L); pre-eclampsia (40-80%); hypertension

### Fetal risks
IUGR; preterm birth; stillbirth; miscarriage; neonatal morbidity

### CKD stage prognosis
Stage 1-2: 90-95% live birth; Stage 3: 70-90%; Stage 4-5: 30-50%

### BP control
Labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa; target <130/80; avoid ACEI/ARB

### Monitoring
U&E 4-6 weekly; urate; urine PCR; growth scans; umbilical artery Doppler; anaemia management (EPO)

### Immunosuppression
Azathioprine, prednisolone, calcineurin inhibitors safe; MMF teratogenic — switch to azathioprine pre-conception

### Delivery
Vaginal preferred; consider induction at 37 weeks; ITU availability

### Postpartum
Monitor renal recovery; some never return to baseline; future pregnancy counselling
