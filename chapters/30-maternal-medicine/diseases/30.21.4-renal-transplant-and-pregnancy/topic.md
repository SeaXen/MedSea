# Renal transplant and pregnancy

---

## Definition

Pregnancy in renal transplant recipients is achievable with stable graft function but requires multidisciplinary care. Optimal outcomes require stable graft function for >1-2 years, no recent rejection, baseline creatinine <125 μmol/L, and stable immunosuppression.

## Core Clinical Content

### Eligibility
Stable graft >1-2 years; Cr <125 μmol/L; proteinuria <0.5 g/24h; no recent rejection

### Maternal risks
Pre-eclampsia (30-50%); infection; graft rejection (10%); deterioration of renal function

### Fetal risks
Preterm birth; IUGR; low birth weight; neonatal immunosuppression

### Safe immunosuppression
Calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus, ciclosporin); azathioprine; prednisolone — all pregnancy-compatible

### Avoid in pregnancy
Mycophenolate (teratogenic — switch pre-conception); mTOR inhibitors (sirolimus, everolimus)

### Monitoring
Monthly U&E; trough tacrolimus levels; proteinuria; CMV/ BK viral load; growth scans

### BP control
Labetalol; nifedipine; target <130/80

### Delivery
Vaginal if obstetrically suitable; caesarean if pelvic kidney obstructing birth canal

### Postpartum
Continue immunosuppression; breastfeeding generally safe; re-introduce MMF after weaning

### Contraception
Avoid oestrogen (VTE/immunosuppression); progesterone-only or IUD preferred
