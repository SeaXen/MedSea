# Varicella zoster in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Varicella zoster virus (VZV) in pregnancy causes significant maternal and fetal morbidity. Maternal infection in the first 20 weeks can cause fetal varicella syndrome (limb hypoplasia, microcephaly, skin scarring, ocular abnormalities). Perinatal infection (5 days before to 2 days after delivery) risks severe neonatal varicella. VZ immunoglobulin (VZIG) and aciclovir are key interventions.

## Core Clinical Content

### Maternal varicella
Pneumonia (10-20%) — leading cause of death; hospitalisation; ARDS; treat with aciclovir

### Fetal varicella syndrome (FVS)
<2% if <20 weeks; cutaneous scarring; limb hypoplasia; microcephaly; ocular (cataract, chorioretinitis); IUGR

### Neonatal varicella
Severe if maternal rash 5 days before to 2 days after delivery (no time for IgG transfer); mortality 30% without treatment

### VZIG
For susceptible (VZV IgG negative) exposed pregnant women within 96 h; reduces severity; does not prevent infection

### Aciclovir
800 mg 5x daily 7 days if varicella in pregnancy (within 24 h of rash); IV if severe or pneumonitis

### Diagnosis
Clinical; PCR vesicular fluid; serology (VZV IgG/IgM); CXR if pneumonitis

### Exposure in pregnancy
Check VZV IgG status urgently (most adults immune); if non-immune and significant exposure, VZIG

### Vaccination
Live vaccine (Varilrix, Varivax) — contraindicated in pregnancy; advise avoidance; 2 doses 4-8 weeks apart; avoid pregnancy 1 month after

### Zoster (shingles)
Less risk to fetus (cell-mediated); aciclovir if severe

### Postpartum
VZIG to neonate if maternal rash 5 days before to 2 days after; isolate mother and baby; aciclovir for baby
