# COVID-19 in pregnancy

---

## Definition

COVID-19 in pregnancy generally causes mild disease, but pregnancy is a risk factor for severe disease, hospitalisation, ICU admission, and preterm birth. Vaccination in pregnancy is safe and effective; monoclonal antibodies and antivirals reduce progression in high-risk women.

## Core Clinical Content

### Severity in pregnancy
Increased risk severe disease, ICU admission, ventilation, death vs non-pregnant — especially 3rd trimester

### Maternal complications
Pre-eclampsia; thromboembolism; preterm birth; caesarean; maternal death

### Fetal risks
Miscarriage (1st trimester severe disease); stillbirth; IUGR; prematurity; vertical transmission possible

### Vaccination
mRNA vaccines (Pfizer/Moderna) safe and effective in any trimester; recommended

### Treatment
Antivirals (nirmatrelvir/ritonavir, remdesivir, molnupiravir); monoclonal antibodies (sotrovimab, tixagevimab) for high-risk

### Antenatal care
Continue routine care; consider remote consultations; postpone if mild; safety-netting

### Thromboprophylaxis
VTE risk high; LMWH for hospitalised; consider 10-14 days postnatal

### Delivery
Mode per obstetric indication; vaginal acceptable if stable; preterm delivery only if deteriorating

### Neonatal
Vertical transmission 1-2%; routine swabbing; breastfeeding encouraged with hygiene

### Postnatal
Vaccination; mental health; post-COVID/long-COVID clinics
