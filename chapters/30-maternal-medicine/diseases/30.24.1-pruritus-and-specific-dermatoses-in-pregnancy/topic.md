# Pruritus and specific dermatoses in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Pruritus in pregnancy has many causes, including specific pregnancy dermatoses (polymorphic eruption of pregnancy, pemphigoid gestationis, intrahepatic cholestasis), eczema, and systemic disease. Recognition of the specific dermatosis guides management and prognosis.

## Core Clinical Content

### Polymorphic eruption of pregnancy (PEP/PUPPP)
Late pregnancy; abdominal striae; urticarial papules and plaques; no maternal/fetal risk; resolves postpartum

### Pemphigoid gestationis (HG)
Autoimmune BP180 antibodies; blistering; periumbilical; risk of preterm, SGA; neonatal blistering

### Intrahepatic cholestasis
Severe pruritus; no rash; raised bile acids; see Liver chapter

### Atopic eruption of pregnancy (AEP)
Eczema in pregnancy; face, neck, flexures; topical steroids; safe

### Prurigo of pregnancy
Excoriated papules; limbs; no maternal/fetal risk; topical steroids

### Investigations
LFT, bile acids (rule out ICP); skin biopsy if bullae; direct immunofluorescence for HG

### Treatment PEP/AEP
Topical steroids; antihistamines; usually resolves within weeks postpartum

### Treatment HG
Topical/oral steroids; prednisolone; cyclosporin if severe; monitor fetal growth

### Other skin changes
Melasma; linea nigra; striae; spider naevi; palmar erythema — normal

### Postpartum
Most resolve; HG may flare postpartum; recurrence with COC, menses, subsequent pregnancy
