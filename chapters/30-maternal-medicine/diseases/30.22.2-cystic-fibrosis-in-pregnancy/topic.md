# Cystic fibrosis in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Cystic fibrosis (CF) in pregnancy is increasingly common as life expectancy improves (now >40 years). Pre-conception optimisation and multidisciplinary care are essential. Pregnancy is usually well tolerated in well-nourished women with good lung function (FEV1 >50-60% predicted) but carries significant risk if lung disease is advanced.

## Core Clinical Content

### Epidemiology
CF carriers 1 in 25 (Caucasians); affected patients now survive to reproductive age

### Preconception
Optimise lung function (FEV1 >60%); nutritional status (BMI >20); screen for CFRD; genetic counselling; partner screening; review medications

### Fertility
Women with CF — normal fertility (thick cervical mucus may be issue); men — 98% azoospermia (CBAVD)

### Maternal risks
Worsening lung function; cor pulmonale; malnutrition; CFRD; CFRD-related complications; infection (Pseudomonas, Staph aureus)

### Fetal risks
IUGR; preterm birth; low birth weight; congenital malformations (CF if partner carrier)

### Antenatal care
MDT (respiratory, obstetric, CF, dietician, physio); monthly spirometry; weight; sputum culture; CFTR modulators (limited data; case-by-case)

### Treatment
Continue airway clearance; nebulisers (safe); dornase alfa; azithromycin; IV antibiotics for exacerbations; insulin for CFRD; pancreatic enzymes; vitamins (ADEK)

### Drug safety
Avoid tetracyclines; fluoroquinolones (limited data); aminoglycosides (ototoxic); CFTR modulators — limited data, consider benefit

### Delivery
Vaginal preferred; assisted second stage (avoid exhaustion); consider epidural; high-risk anaesthesia review

### Prognosis
Pregnancy safe if FEV1 >60%; 10-20% decline in FEV1 may be permanent in advanced disease; 80%+ live birth rate
