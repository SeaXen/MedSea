# Breast cancer in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Breast cancer in pregnancy is the most common malignancy in pregnancy (1 in 3,000-10,000 pregnancies), often presenting at advanced stage due to pregnancy-related breast changes. Treatment is individualised according to gestational age, stage, and tumour biology, balancing maternal prognosis with fetal safety.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
1 in 3,000-10,000; most common cancer in pregnancy; median age 33

### Risk factors
BRCA1/2; family history; older maternal age; not associated with pregnancy itself

### Presentation
Lump (most common); nipple discharge; skin changes; often delayed diagnosis (mimics physiological changes)

### Diagnosis
Triple assessment: clinical exam; imaging (USS first-line; mammogram with shielding); core biopsy

### Staging
MRI without gadolinium; CXR; liver USS; bone scan deferred; sentinel node biopsy with technetium (limited); blue dye avoid (anaphylaxis)

### Treatment surgery
Mastectomy or wide local excision; safe all trimesters; SLNB with technetium

### Chemotherapy
Avoid 1st trimester (teratogenic); AC or FAC regimens from 2nd trimester; stop 3 weeks before delivery; not after 34-35 weeks

### Avoid in pregnancy
Radiotherapy; trastuzumab (HER2+ — oligohydramnios); endocrine therapy (tamoxifen — teratogenic); methotrexate

### Delivery
Timing based on treatment; if possible delay until fetal maturity; vaginal if obstetric; avoid iatrogenic prematurity

### Prognosis
Often advanced at diagnosis; stage-matched similar to non-pregnant; prognosis improving with modern treatment
