# Gallstones and biliary disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Gallstone disease is the most common non-obstetric cause of abdominal pain in pregnancy due to elevated oestrogen (increased cholesterol secretion) and progesterone (biliary stasis). Most cases are asymptomatic; symptomatic disease includes biliary colic, acute cholecystitis, choledocholithiasis, and gallstone pancreatitis.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
Asymptomatic gallstones in 3-4% pregnancies; symptomatic 0.1%

### Risk factors
Pre-existing gallstones; obesity; high-fat diet; rapid weight loss; multiparity; family history; oral oestrogens

### Biliary sludge
Common in pregnancy; usually resolves postpartum

### Biliary colic
Episodic RUQ/epigastric pain; resolves within hours; no fever; no Murphy's sign; no LFT derangement

### Acute cholecystitis
RUQ pain; fever; Murphy's sign; leucocytosis; LFT may be mildly deranged; USS shows gallstones + wall thickening + pericholecystic fluid

### Choledocholithiasis
Biliary colic + jaundice; cholangitis if Charcot's triad (fever, jaundice, RUQ pain)

### Investigations
USS (first-line; safe); LFT; FBC; amylase; MRCP if needed; ERCP if cholangitis

### Management asymptomatic
Reassure; no surgery

### Management symptomatic
Conservative (NPO, IV fluids, analgesia, antibiotics); ERCP if cholangitis (radiation-minimising technique); cholecystectomy in 2nd trimester if recurrent/refractory

### Postpartum
Symptoms often resolve; consider interval cholecystectomy if recurrent; contraception (avoid oestrogen)
