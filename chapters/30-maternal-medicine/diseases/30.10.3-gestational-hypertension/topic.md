# Gestational hypertension

---

## Definition

Gestational hypertension is new-onset BP ≥140/90 mmHg after 20 weeks' gestation in a previously normotensive woman, without proteinuria or other features of pre-eclampsia. It usually resolves within 6 weeks postpartum but ~25% progress to pre-eclampsia.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
New hypertension after 20 weeks; no proteinuria; no end-organ involvement

### Onset
After 20 weeks' gestation; usually third trimester

### Prevalence
6-17% of pregnancies; rising

### Progress to pre-eclampsia
~25% develop proteinuria / end-organ dysfunction

### Risk factors
Nulliparity, age >35, BMI >30, multiple pregnancy, family history, prior GH/PE

### Differential
Chronic HTN, pre-eclampsia, white-coat HTN (ABPM)

### Investigations
Urine PCR; bloods (FBC, U&E, LFT, urate); growth scans; umbilical artery Doppler

### Management
Watchful waiting for mild (<150/100); antihypertensives for moderate/severe

### Delivery planning
Deliver by 37 weeks if BP controlled; severe features warrant earlier delivery

### Postpartum
BP usually normalises by 6 weeks; ongoing surveillance; CV risk in later life
