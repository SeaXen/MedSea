# Gestational trophoblastic disease (GTD)

---

## Definition

Gestational trophoblastic disease encompasses hydatidiform mole (complete and partial), invasive mole, choriocarcinoma, and placental site trophoblastic tumour. hCG is the key biomarker. Most are cured with appropriate management and surveillance.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Spectrum from molar pregnancy to metastatic GTN

### Complete mole
46,XX (or XY) androgenetic; empty egg + sperm duplication; no fetus; hCG very high; 2-3% choriocarcinoma

### Partial mole
69,XXX/XXY/XYY; triploid; fetus present but abnormal; hCG mildly raised; 0.5% choriocarcinoma

### Presentation
Vaginal bleeding; hyperemesis; hyperthyroidism (hCG); uterine size > dates; theca lutein cysts; pre-eclampsia <20 weeks

### Diagnosis
USS — 'snowstorm' (complete); cystic spaces with fetus (partial); hCG >100,000

### Management molar
Suction curettage (evacuation); Rh prophylaxis if Rh-; send for histology

### Surveillance
Serial hCG weekly until normal, then monthly for 6 months (complete) or 1 month (partial); reliable contraception

### GTN
Rising or plateau hCG; choriocarcinoma; metastasis to lung (80%), vagina, liver, brain

### Treatment GTN
Low-risk: methotrexate or actinomycin-D; high-risk: EMA-CO (etoposide, methotrexate, actinomycin, cyclophosphamide, vincristine)

### Future pregnancy
Normal fertility; 1% recurrence; hCG 6-8 weeks postpartum; send histology

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Gestational trophoblastic disease (GTD)** — see key points in Core Clinical Content
- **'PRE-ECLAMPSIA' risk factors** — Previous PE, Renal disease, Extremes of age, Chronic HTN, Ethnicity, Antiphospholipid, nulliparity, Multiple, Obesity, Smoking
- **'Mnemonic: H-E-L-L-P'** — Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets
- **'CHAP' trial target** — Chronic HTN treated to <140/90 mmHg
- **'M-M-R-V' safe vaccines in pregnancy** — Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR, avoid), inactivated influenza, COVID-19, Whooping cough (dTpa)
