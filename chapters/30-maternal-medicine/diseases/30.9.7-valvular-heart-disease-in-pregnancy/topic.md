# Valvular heart disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Valvular heart disease — most commonly rheumatic mitral stenosis in developing countries and congenital bicuspid aortic valve/stenosis in developed countries — is a leading cause of maternal morbidity. Stenotic lesions are higher risk than regurgitant lesions because the fixed output cannot accommodate pregnancy demands.

## Core Clinical Content

### Mitral stenosis
Most common rheumatic lesion; pre-conception balloon valvuloplasty if severe (MVA <1.5 cm²)

### Symptoms MS
Dyspnoea, orthopnoea, PND, haemoptysis, AF — often worsen in 2nd/3rd trimesters

### Aortic stenosis
Bicuspid valve; symptoms (angina, syncope) may develop as CO fails to rise

### Mitral/aortic regurgitation
Lower risk — reduced SVR is protective; treat medically

### Anticoagulation
AF, mechanical valve, prior thrombus — LMWH throughout pregnancy; switch warfarin at 6-12 weeks

### Mechanical valve
High risk: 1-2% maternal mortality; valve thrombosis; LMWH requires anti-Xa monitoring; warfarin 5 mg max preferred for mechanical AV after 12 weeks

### Antibiotic prophylaxis
Not routinely for delivery; per current guidelines

### Labour and delivery
Vaginal preferred; haemodynamic monitoring; assisted second stage; avoid tachycardia/hypotension

### Percutaneous valvotomy
Option for severe MS failing medical therapy; experienced centre

### Postpartum
VTE prophylaxis; close follow-up; valve surgery deferred if possible until after delivery
