# Drug safety in breastfeeding

---

## Definition

Most drugs are compatible with breastfeeding; few are absolute contraindications. Assess risk-benefit, use lowest dose, schedule feeds around peak drug levels, and consult LactMed or UKDILAS.

## Core Clinical Content

### Principles
Most drugs safe; few absolute contraindications; assess risk-benefit; monitor infant

### Safe analgesics
Paracetamol; ibuprofen; codeine in short courses (caution CYP2D6 ultra-rapid metabolisers)

### Safe antibiotics
Penicillins, cephalosporins, erythromycin, azithromycin, nitrofurantoin

### Avoid
Cytotoxic drugs (chemotherapy); radioisotopes (withhold breastfeeding for 5 half-lives); ergot alkaloids; chloramphenicol (neonate grey baby)

### Caution
Codeine; tramadol; benzodiazepines (accumulation, sedation); lithium (monitor infant levels); lamotrigine (monitor); SSRIs (sertraline preferred)

### Safe antihypertensives
Labetalol, nifedipine, enalapril (small amounts), methyldopa

### Safe antiepileptics
Lamotrigine, levetiracetam, valproate (avoid — developmental delay); carbamazepine; monitor infant

### Safe anticoagulants
LMWH, warfarin (small amount, monitor INR); DOACs avoid (limited data)

### Drug entry into milk
Lipid-soluble, low molecular weight, low protein-bound drugs cross more readily

### Resources
LactMed (NIH); UKDILAS; BNF 'Breast feeding' appendix
