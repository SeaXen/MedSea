# Iron deficiency anaemia in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Iron deficiency anaemia in pregnancy is the most common anaemia in pregnancy worldwide and a major cause of maternal morbidity, preterm birth, and low birth weight. Diagnosis uses trimester-specific cut-offs; treatment combines oral iron with parenteral iron when needed.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definitions
1st trimester Hb <110; 2nd/3rd <105 g/L; postpartum <100 g/L (WHO)

### Prevalence
20-40% of pregnancies; 50%+ in LMIC

### Causes
Iron deficiency (90%); folate/B12 deficiency; haemoglobinopathies; haemolysis

### Investigations
FBC; MCV (microcytic); ferritin (<30 μg/L = depleted); serum iron; TIBC

### Maternal impact
Fatigue, breathlessness; pre-eclampsia; postpartum haemorrhage; infection; transfusion need

### Fetal impact
IUGR; preterm birth; low birth weight; poor neurodevelopment

### Oral iron
Ferrous sulphate 200 mg BD-TDS; alternate-day dosing improves absorption; take with vitamin C

### IV iron
Iron sucrose, ferric carboxymaltose, iron isomaltoside — for intolerance/non-response/severe

### Blood transfusion
Reserved for severe symptomatic anaemia near term; aim Hb >70-80 g/L

### Prevention
Routine oral iron from 16 weeks; high-risk women from 1st trimester
