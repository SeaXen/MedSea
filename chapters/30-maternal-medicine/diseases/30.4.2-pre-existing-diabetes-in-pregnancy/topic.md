# Pre-existing diabetes in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Pre-existing (overt) diabetes in pregnancy refers to type 1 or type 2 diabetes present before conception. Pregnancy is associated with increased insulin resistance and risk of complications; preconception optimisation and tight glycaemic control are essential.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
1-2% of pregnancies; rising with obesity and T2DM

### Preconception
HbA1c <6.5% (ideally <48 mmol/mol); folic acid 5 mg; switch to insulin; review drugs

### Risks to mother
Miscarriage; pre-eclampsia (2x); infection; caesarean; DKA; hypoglycaemia

### Risks to fetus
Congenital malformation (4-10%); macrosomia; IUGR (T1DM with vasculopathy); stillbirth; preterm; neonatal hypoglycaemia

### Fetal surveillance
Anomaly scan 18-20 weeks; growth scans 28, 32, 36 weeks

### Glycaemic targets
Fasting <5.3 mmol/L; 1-h postprandial <7.8 mmol/L; 2-h <6.4 mmol/L

### Insulin
First-line; rapid-acting (aspart, lispro) + NPH; basal-bolus; CSII pumps

### Metformin
Can continue; crosses placenta; long-term offspring data reassuring

### Labour
IV insulin + dextrose sliding scale; target glucose 4-7 mmol/L

### Postpartum
Insulin sensitivity rises immediately — reduce insulin by ~50% on day 1
