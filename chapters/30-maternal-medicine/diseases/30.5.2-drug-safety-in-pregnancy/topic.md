# Drug safety in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Drug use in pregnancy requires balancing maternal benefit with fetal risk. Most drugs cross the placenta; the first trimester carries the highest teratogenic risk. Use the lowest effective dose, avoid new drugs where possible, and consult specific resources.

## Core Clinical Content

### Principles
Lowest effective dose; minimal duration; avoid new drugs; consider non-pharmacological; benefit > risk

### FDA categories (legacy)
A: safe; B: animal safe; C: risk can't be ruled out; D: evidence of risk; X: contraindicated (B3 UK)

### Safe drugs (common)
Paracetamol; penicillins/cephalosporins; labetalol/nifedipine/methyldopa; levothyroxine; insulin; aciclovir; ondansetron

### Teratogenic
Valproate (NTD, neurodevelopment); carbimazole (aplasia cutis); ACEI/ARB (renal); warfarin (1st trimester); methotrexate; retinoids; misoprostol; thalidomide; lithium (cardiac)

### First-trimester risks
Organogenesis; congenital malformations; avoid all unnecessary drugs

### Late-pregnancy risks
NSAIDs (closure of ductus, oligohydramnios); ACEI/ARB; opioids (neonatal abstinence); benzodiazepines (floppy baby)

### Antimicrobials safe
Penicillins, cephalosporins, erythromycin, azithromycin, nitrofurantoin (avoid near term)

### Antimicrobials avoid
Tetracyclines (teeth, bones); quinolones (cartilage); aminoglycosides (ototoxic); metronidazole (1st trimester caution)

### Resource
UKTIS (UK Teratology Information Service); BNF; LactMed for breastfeeding; app-based decision support

### Vaccination in pregnancy
Safe — inactivated influenza, COVID-19, pertussis (dTpa, 16-32 weeks). Avoid live: MMR, varicella, BCG, yellow fever (if needed) live OK
