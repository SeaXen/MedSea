# Physiological changes in pregnancy overview

---

## Definition

Comprehensive overview of the multisystem physiological adaptations to pregnancy, including cardiovascular, respiratory, renal, haematological, metabolic, and endocrine changes that distinguish the pregnant state and influence disease presentation, investigation, and management.

## Core Clinical Content

### Cardiac output
+30-50%; peaks at 32-34 weeks; falls to baseline by 2 weeks postpartum

### Plasma volume
+40-50% by term; mediates dilutional 'physiological anaemia'

### Heart rate
+10-20 bpm by third trimester

### Blood pressure
Falls in 1st/2nd trimesters; nadir at ~20 weeks; returns to pre-pregnancy by term

### Systemic vascular resistance
Falls ~30% due to progesterone-mediated vasodilation

### Renal blood flow and GFR
+50-60%; creatinine falls (normal pregnancy creatinine <77 μmol/L)

### Tidal volume and minute ventilation
+30-50%; respiratory alkalosis with PCO2 ~30 mmHg

### Coagulation
Hypercoagulable state; factors I, VII, VIII, IX, X rise; protein S falls

### Thyroid-binding globulin
Rises (oestrogen effect); total T4/T3 rise; free T4 unchanged

### Drug pharmacokinetics
Increased Vd, GFR, hepatic flow; altered protein binding; clinical dose adjustment
