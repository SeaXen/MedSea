# Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM)

---

## Definition

Gestational diabetes mellitus is glucose intolerance first recognised during pregnancy that is not overt pre-existing diabetes. It typically resolves after delivery but confers long-term maternal risk of type 2 diabetes and fetal risk of macrosomia and metabolic disease.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Glucose intolerance first recognised in pregnancy; excludes overt pre-pregnational diabetes

### Epidemiology
Affects 5-18% of pregnancies; rising with obesity

### Risk factors
BMI >30, age >25, prior GDM, family history, prior macrosomic baby, PCOS, ethnicity (South Asian)

### Screening
Universal at 24-28 weeks (NICE); 2-step 50 g GCT → 100 g OGTT or 1-step 75 g OGTT

### Diagnostic OGTT (IADPSG)
Fasting ≥5.1; 1-h ≥10.0; 2-h ≥8.5 mmol/L — one abnormal value = GDM

### Complications
Macrosomia; shoulder dystocia; pre-eclampsia; caesarean; neonatal hypoglycaemia; stillbirth

### Management
Diet and exercise first-line; metformin or insulin if glycaemic targets not met

### Targets
Fasting <5.3; 1-h <7.8; 2-h <6.4 mmol/L

### Fetal monitoring
Growth scans 28, 32, 36 weeks; daily fetal movement count from 28 weeks

### Postpartum
75 g OGTT at 6-12 weeks; 50% progress to T2DM over 5-10 years; lifestyle
