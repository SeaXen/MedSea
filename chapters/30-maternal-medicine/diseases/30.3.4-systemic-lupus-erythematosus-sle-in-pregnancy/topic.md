# Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE) in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Systemic lupus erythematosus affects women in childbearing years and is a leading cause of maternal mortality. Pregnancy is safe in quiescent disease but flares (especially renal) and increased rates of pre-eclampsia, miscarriage, preterm birth, and fetal loss are seen.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
F:M = 9:1; common in 20s-40s; affects 1 in 1,000 pregnancies

### Preconception
Disease quiescent >6 months; renal function stable; switch MMF to azathioprine; stop ACEI

### Maternal risks
Flare (esp. renal); pre-eclampsia (20-30%); thrombosis; infection; hypertension

### Fetal risks
Miscarriage; IUGR; preterm birth; stillbirth; neonatal lupus; congenital heart block (anti-Ro/La)

### Anti-Ro/La antibodies
Risk of neonatal lupus (1-2%) and congenital heart block (1-2%); serial fetal echocardiography 16-34 weeks

### Monitoring
Monthly U&E, urinalysis, anti-dsDNA, complement (C3/C4); growth scans; umbilical artery Doppler

### Treatment
Hydroxychloroquine continued (reduces flares); azathioprine; prednisolone; aspirin 75-150 mg; LMWH if APS

### Avoid
Cyclophosphamide (1st trimester); MMF; methotrexate; ACEI/ARB

### Lupus nephritis flare
Often mimics pre-eclampsia; treat with steroids/azathioprine; check anti-dsDNA/complement

### Postpartum
Restart MMF; breastfeeding compatible with HCQ/azathioprine/prednisolone; contraception
