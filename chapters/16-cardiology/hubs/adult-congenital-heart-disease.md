# Adult Congenital Heart Disease (ACHD)
<callout icon="🫀" color="red_bg">
	**Topic:** Adult Congenital Heart Disease — Cardiology
	**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
	**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Introduction & Definition
**ACHD** = congenital heart disease surviving to adulthood. Prevalence rising (>90% of children with CHD now reach adulthood) due to surgical / interventional advances. Most common lesions: **bicuspid aortic valve (BAV)** (~1%), ASD, VSD, PDA, PFO, repaired TOF, coarctation, Marfan, Fontan, TGA, Eisenmenger.

---

## 2. Pathophysiology
- **Shunt lesions:** L→R (Qp:Qs > 1.5) → RV volume overload → pulmonary overcirculation → pulmonary vascular disease → **Eisenmenger** (R→L shunt reversal) → cyanosis, erythrocytosis
- **Obstructive lesions** (AS, PS, coarctation): pressure overload → concentric hypertrophy → systolic / diastolic dysfunction
- **Regurgitant lesions** (PR post-TOF, AR, MR): volume overload → eccentric hypertrophy → dilation
- **Cyanotic lesions** (TOF, TGA, Eisenmenger, Fontan): hypoxaemia, secondary erythrocytosis, multi-organ effects (brain, kidney, liver)
- **Single ventricle / Fontan:** passive pulmonary flow; no sub-pulmonary ventricle → hepatic congestion, PLE, plastic bronchitis, cirrhosis

---

## 3. Classification
| Group | Examples | Key Issue |
|---|---|---|
| **Shunt lesions** | ASD, VSD, PDA, PFO | L→R shunt, RV volume overload, Eisenmenger risk |
| **Right-sided obstructive** | Pulmonary stenosis, PR (post-TOF), Ebstein | RV pressure / volume overload |
| **Left-sided obstructive** | AS (BAV), Sub-AS, Coarctation | LV pressure overload, HTN, aortopathy |
| **Regurgitant** | AR, MR, PR | Volume overload |
| **Cyanotic** | TOF, TGA, Eisenmenger, Fontan, Truncus | Hypoxaemia, multi-organ |
| **Aortopathy** | BAV, Marfan, Loeys-Dietz | Aortic root dilation / dissection |
| **Repaired** | Post-TOF, post-Switch, post-Fontan | Residual lesions, arrhythmia, HF |

---

## 4. Clinical Features
- **Asymptomatic** with murmur (incidental)
- **Exertional dyspnoea, fatigue**, exercise intolerance
- **Palpitations / AF** (ASD, post-repair)
- **Cyanosis, clubbing** (cyanotic CHD, Eisenmenger)
- **Syncope** (AS, HCM, PS, arrhythmia)
- **HF** (long-standing volume / pressure overload, ventricular dysfunction)
- **Stroke / TIA** (paradoxical embolism via PFO / ASD)
- **Pregnancy complications** (HF, arrhythmia, aortic dissection in Marfan)
- **Aortic dissection** (BAV, Marfan, Loeys-Dietz)
- **Endocarditis** (cyanotic, prosthetic valve, prior IE)
- **Erythrocytosis, hyperviscosity, gout** (cyanotic)

---

## 5. Investigations
- **ECG** — axis (LAD in AV canal, RBBB in ASD, post-TOF), QRS duration (TOF QRS > 180 ms → VT risk), RVH, arrhythmia
- **CXR** — cardiomegaly, pulmonary plethora (L→R), oligaemia (Eisenmenger), rib notching (coarctation), boot-shaped (TOF)
- **Echocardiogram (TTE / TEE)** — **first-line** — anatomy, shunts (colour / agitated saline), valve function, RV/LV function, aortic root
- **CMR** — **gold standard for RV volume, function, Qp:Qs**, scar (post-repair VT substrate), anomalous coronary, aortic dimensions
- **CT angio** — coronaries (post-Switch), aorta (coarctation, BAV), pulmonary arteries (CTEPH-like)
- **Cardiac catheterisation** — haemodynamics, Qp:Qs, PVR, test occlusion, intervention
- **Exercise testing / CPET** — functional capacity, arrhythmia, desaturation (Eisenmenger)
- **Genetic testing** — Marfan (FBN1), Loeys-Dietz (TGFBR1/2), 22q11.2 deletion
- **Holter** — arrhythmia surveillance (post-TOF, post-Fontan)
- **ABG, FBC, iron studies, ferritin, urate** (cyanotic)
- **LFT, AFP, FibroScan** (Fontan liver surveillance q6–12mo)

---

## 6. Differential Diagnosis of Cyanosis / Murmur
| Condition | Distinguishing Feature | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **Eisenmenger syndrome** | Cyanosis + PAH + shunt reversal | Echo, RHC (mPAP > 20, PVR > 5 WU) |
| **Cyanotic CHD (TOF, TGA)** | Cyanosis since birth or repair | Echo, CMR |
| **Pulmonary embolism** | Acute, pleuritic, ↑HR | CTPA, D-dimer |
| **Pulmonary AV malformation / HHT** | Telangiectasia, epistaxis | CT chest, contrast echo |
| **Methaemoglobinaemia** | "Chocolate" blood, drug exposure | Co-oximetry |
| **Polycythaemia vera** | ↑Hb, ↑Hct, splenomegaly | JAK2, haematology workup |
| **Viral / bacterial pneumonia** | Fever, consolidation | CXR, blood cultures |
| **Pneumothorax** | Sudden, unilateral, hyperresonant | CXR |
| **High-altitude** | Recent ascent | History, SpO₂ |

---

## 7. Complications
- **Eisenmenger** — shunt reversal, erythrocytosis, thromboembolism, haemoptysis, sudden death; **pregnancy contraindicated**
- **Aortic dissection** — BAV, Marfan, Loeys-Dietz (surgery at aortic root > 55 mm, or > 50 mm with risk factors; Marfan > 45 mm)
- **Arrhythmia / SCD** — post-TOF VT (QRS > 180 ms), AF in ASD / Ebstein, post-Fontan IART
- **Heart failure** — late RV / LV dysfunction
- **Endocarditis** — high risk: prosthetic valve, prior IE, cyanotic, unrepaired cyanotic
- **Stroke** — paradoxical embolism (PFO, ASD), AF
- **Pregnancy** — high-risk in mWHO Class III–IV; aortic dissection in Marfan / BAV
- **Fontan complications** — PLE, plastic bronchitis, liver cirrhosis / HCC, thromboembolism

---

## 8. Management
| Lesion | Closure / Intervention Criteria | Method |
|---|---|---|
| **ASD (secundum)** | **Qp:Qs > 1.5:1, RV dilation, paradoxical embolism, PAH** | Transcatheter device (rim > 5 mm, < 36 mm); surgical if large / deficient rim |
| **VSD (perimembranous)** | Qp:Qs > 1.5, LV/RV dilation, aortic valve prolapse, recurrent RTI, AR | **Surgical patch**; device for muscular VSD |
| **PDA** | Symptomatic, LV dilation, PAH, IE, Eisenmenger workup | **Device (Amplatzer)**; surgical if premature / large |
| **PFO** | **Cryptogenic stroke age 18–60 + no other cause + ASA / large shunt** (CLOSURE, RESPECT, REDUCE) | Device closure — ~50% ↓ recurrence |
| **BAV with aortopathy** | **Aortic root > 55 mm** (or **> 50 mm** with risk factors) | Aortic root replacement; TAVI for AS |
| **Coarctation** | Gradient **> 20 mmHg**, HTN, collaterals | Balloon + **stent**; surgery if recoarctation |
| **TOF post-repair** | Severe PR + RV dilation / dysfunction / QRS > 180 ms / VT | **Pulmonary valve replacement (PVR)**; ICD if sustained VT |
| **Eisenmenger** | **Avoid pregnancy**; phlebotomy only if Hct > 65% + symptoms; **anticoagulate** if AF / thrombosis; **O₂ NOT beneficial** | Advanced therapy (bosentan, sildenafil); transplant |
| **Fontan** | PLE, plastic bronchitis, arrhythmia, thrombosis, liver | Heparin, budesonide, sildenafil, Fontan revision; liver transplant |

---

## 9. Secondary Prevention / Long-term Follow-up
- **Lifelong ACHD specialist clinic** (ESC 2020 Class I)
- **Echocardiography q1–2 yr**; **CMR** q3–5 yr (TOF, repaired lesions, systemic RV)
- **Exercise prescription** — most benefit, avoid isometric in aortopathy / Eisenmenger
- **Endocarditis prophylaxis** — prosthetic valve, prior IE, unrepaired cyanotic, repaired CHD with prosthetic material < 6 mo, residual defect at repair site (NICE / ESC)
- **Pregnancy counselling** — mWHO risk classification; pre-conception review; LMWH if anticoagulation needed
- **Anticoagulation in pregnancy:** LMWH (anti-Xa monitoring) throughout; **warfarin teratogenic 6–12 wks**; mechanical valve — LMWH + warfarin 2nd / 3rd trimester
- **Iron supplementation** if iron-deficient erythrocytosis (avoid dehydration)
- **Vaccinations** — influenza, pneumococcal, COVID-19
- **Aortic surveillance** — annual echo/CT; family screening in Marfan / Loeys-Dietz

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield
<callout icon="⭐" color="purple_bg">
	**12 high-yield points** for FCPS / MRCP ACHD questions.
</callout>

- **ASD = fixed split S2 + ejection systolic murmur** (pulmonary flow); VSD = **holosystolic LLSB**; PDA = **continuous "machinery" murmur** (left infraclavicular)
- **ASD closure:** **Qp:Qs > 1.5:1** + RV dilation; **device** if secundum with adequate rim
- **PFO closure** after cryptogenic stroke age 18–60: **~50% reduction in recurrence** (RESPECT, CLOSE, REDUCE)
- **BAV** = 1% of population; screen first-degree relatives; surgery for aortic root **> 55 mm** (or > 50 mm with risk factors)
- **Coarctation** = rib notching (posterior ribs 3–8) + BP differential upper vs lower limb + collaterals; treat if gradient > 20 mmHg
- **Eisenmenger** = **L→R → R→L shunt reversal** with cyanosis, erythrocytosis, clubbing; **pregnancy absolutely contraindicated**; phlebotomy only if Hct > 65% + symptoms; O₂ unhelpful
- **TOF post-repair** = monitor for **severe PR** (→ PVR), **RV dilation**, **VT** (QRS > 180 ms → consider ICD)
- **Fontan** = single ventricle, passive pulmonary flow; complications = **PLE, plastic bronchitis, liver fibrosis, arrhythmia, thrombosis**
- **mWHO Class IV (pregnancy contraindicated):** Eisenmenger, severe AS, severe PAH, Marfan aortic > 45 mm, NYHA III–IV
- **Pregnancy anticoagulation:** LMWH throughout; warfarin teratogenic 6–12 wks; mechanical valve = LMWH + warfarin 2nd/3rd trimester
- **Endocarditis prophylaxis:** prosthetic valve, prior IE, unrepaired cyanotic CHD, repaired with prosthetic material < 6 mo or residual defect
- **Bicuspid aortic valve (BAV) + coarctation** association — screen the aorta in coarctation and the valve in BAV

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
	**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** ACHD prevalence now exceeds paediatric CHD — over 90% of children with CHD reach adulthood, creating a growing adult population needing lifelong specialist care. **Always consider ACHD in young stroke** (PFO), young aortic dissection (BAV, Marfan), and any unexplained cyanosis or pulmonary hypertension. **mWHO Class IV conditions (Eisenmenger, severe PAH, Marfan aortic > 45 mm) make pregnancy life-threatening**. **Fontan and repaired TOF patients** need structured surveillance for arrhythmia, ventricular dysfunction, and prosthetic-valve dysfunction. **Endocarditis prophylaxis** is restricted to the highest-risk lesions.
</callout>
