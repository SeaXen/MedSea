# Pulmonary Hypertension (PH)
<callout icon="🫁" color="purple_bg">
	**Topic:** Pulmonary Hypertension — Cardiology

	**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Introduction & Definition
**Pulmonary hypertension (PH)** = **mean pulmonary artery pressure (mPAP) > 20 mmHg at rest** on right heart catheterisation (RHC). Divided into 5 groups (ESC/ERS 2022, 6th group added). **Pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH, Group 1)** = pre-capillary PH (**mPAP > 20, PAWP ≤ 15, PVR > 2 WU**). Rare, progressive, fatal if untreated; modern therapy has improved median survival from 2.8 → ~7 years.

---

## 2. Pathophysiology
- **Vasoconstriction** — ↓NO, ↓prostacyclin, ↑endothelin, ↑thromboxane, ↑serotonin
- **Vascular remodelling** — intimal proliferation, smooth-muscle hypertrophy, fibrosis, **plexiform lesions**
- **Inflammation** — cytokines, immune cells, autoantibodies
- **In-situ thrombosis** (small-vessel)
- **Result:** ↑PVR → ↑RV afterload → RV hypertrophy (adaptive) → **RV dilation / failure** (maladaptive) → ↓CO → death
- **Genetics:** **BMPR2** mutation in 70–80% of heritable PAH (autosomal dominant, low penetrance ~20%); ACVRL1, ENG (HHT), SMAD9, CAV1, KCNK3
- **Drugs / toxins:** anorexigens (fenfluramine, benfluorex, withdrawn), dasatinib, amphetamines, methamphetamines, cocaine, interferon

---

## 3. Classification — WHO Groups
| Group | Name | Mechanism | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **PAH** | Pre-capillary | Idiopathic, heritable, drug-induced, CTD (SSc), HIV, portopulmonary, CHD, schistosomiasis, PVOD |
| **2** | **PH-LHD** | Post-capillary | LV systolic / diastolic HF, valve disease (MS, MR, AS) |
| **3** | **PH-lung** | Lung disease / hypoxia | COPD, ILD, OSA, high-altitude |
| **4** | **PH-CTEPH** | Obstructive | Chronic thromboembolic, sarcoma, vasculitis |
| **5** | **Unclear / multifactorial** | Variable | Sarcoidosis, CKD, haematological (sickle, thalassaemia, MPN), metabolic |

---

## 4. Clinical Features
- **Symptoms:** progressive **exertional dyspnoea** (most common), fatigue, exercise intolerance, syncope (exertional — severe), **chest pain** (RV ischaemia), palpitations, oedema, abdominal distension
- **Examination:** **loud P2**, **right ventricular heave (lift)**, **TR murmur** (LLSB, ↑inspiration — Carvallo), **JVP ↑ with prominent a wave**, hepatomegaly, peripheral oedema, ascites, pericardial effusion (severe), cyanosis (late)
- **Signs of underlying cause:** sclerodactyly, telangiectasia (SSc, HHT), digital clubbing, Raynaud's
- **Functional class (WHO/NYHA I–IV)** — used for risk stratification
- **Syncope on exertion** = poor prognosis, severe RV dysfunction

---

## 5. Investigations
- **ECG** — RVH (R V1, S V5–6), RAD, RBBB, P pulmonale, right-axis; NT-proBNP / BNP (severity, prognosis)
- **CXR** — RV / RA enlargement, prominent pulmonary arteries, pruning of peripheral vessels (pruning sign)
- **Echocardiogram (TTE)** — **first-line screen** — PASP (TR velocity + RAP), **TAPSE < 1.7 cm**, S′ < 9.5 cm/s, RVFAC < 35%, RA size, septal flattening (D-shape LV), pericardial effusion, LV eccentricity
- **RHC — gold standard:** mPAP > 20 mmHg, PAWP ≤ 15 (pre-capillary), PVR > 2 WU, CO / CI, **vasoreactivity testing** (inhaled NO, IV epoprostenol, adenosine) — positive if mPAP ↓ ≥ 10 mmHg to ≤ 40 with maintained / ↑ CO (only in IPAH, HPAH, drug-induced)
- **V/Q scan** — **gold standard for CTEPH** (mismatched perfusion defects)
- **HRCT chest** — parenchymal lung disease, PVOD (centrilobular ground glass, septal lines)
- **CTPA** — thromboembolic disease (CTEPH)
- **CMR** — RV volume / function, PVR estimation, scar
- **PFTs + DLCO** — obstructive / restrictive pattern, **DLCO ↓ in PVOD / SSc PAH**
- **Polysomnography** — OSA
- **Bloods** — ANA, anti-centromere, Scl-70, RF, **HIV**, hepatitis, LFT (portal HTN), **BNP / NT-proBNP**, urate, iron, TSH
- **6MWD, CPET** — severity (6MWD > 440 m = low, 165–440 = intermediate, < 165 = high; VO₂ peak < 10.5 ml/kg/min = high risk)
- **Genetic testing** — BMPR2 if familial
- **Abdominal US** — portal hypertension (Group 1.4)

---

## 6. Differential Diagnosis (Dyspnoea + RV signs)
| Condition | Distinguishing Feature | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **PAH (Group 1)** | Pre-capillary, no LHD, no lung disease | RHC: mPAP > 20, PAWP ≤ 15 |
| **PH-LHD (Group 2)** | PAWP > 15, LV dysfunction / valve | Echo, BNP, PCWP |
| **PH-Lung (Group 3)** | COPD / ILD / OSA | PFT, HRCT, sleep study |
| **CTEPH (Group 4)** | Mismatched V/Q defects | V/Q scan, CTPA, RHC |
| **PVOD** | ↑ PAWP response to PAH Rx, ↓DLCO severe, ground glass | HRCT, biopsy, genetic (EIF2AK4) |
| **Recurrent PE** | Acute episodes, DVT, hypercoagulable | CTPA, thrombophilia |
| **Cardiomyopathy (HCM, RCM)** | LV / RV thickening / diastolic dysfunction | Echo, CMR |
| **Constrictive pericarditis** | Pericardial thickening, Kussmaul, pericardial knock | Echo, CT, RHC |
| **Severe MS / AS** | Loud S1 / murmur, valve area | Echo |
| **Anaemia / thyrotoxicosis** | High-output state | FBC, TFT |

---

## 7. Complications
- **Right heart failure** (most common cause of death)
- **Arrhythmia** — AF, VT, sudden cardiac death
- **Thromboembolism** (in-situ, PE)
- **Haemoptysis** (pulmonary artery rupture — rare, often fatal)
- **Pregnancy** — **contraindicated** (mortality 30–50%)
- **Syncope / SCD**
- **Death** (right HF, sudden)

---

## 8. Management

| Step | Strategy | Examples / Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **General** | Supervised exercise, O₂ if PaO₂ < 60, diuretics, digoxin (AF), iron, vaccination (flu, pneumococcal) | **Pregnancy avoidance** — teratogenic drugs |
| **Anticoagulation** | Controversial — may benefit **IPAH**; **avoid in CTD-PAH** | Individualise |
| **Vasoreactivity +ve** (IPAH, HPAH, drug) | **High-dose CCB** — nifedipine / amlodipine / diltiazem (long-acting) | Sustained response ~50% |
| **Low / intermediate risk** | **Oral combination: ERA + PDE5i** (ambrisentan + tadalafil — AMBITION; macitentan + tadalafil) | First-line per ESC 2022 |
| **High risk** | **Parenteral prostacyclin** (IV epoprostenol, SC treprostinil) ± oral combination | IV epoprostenol most potent |
| **Sequential add-on** | ERA → PDE5i → prostacyclin → selexipag → **sotatercept** (STELLAR 2023, activin trap) | Sotatercept add-on for refractory |
| **CTEPH** | **Anticoagulation 3–6 mo**; **Pulmonary endarterectomy (PEA)** if operable (curative); **Riociguat** (CHEST-1/-2) inoperable / residual; **balloon pulmonary angioplasty (BPA)** for inoperable | Riociguat is the only approved medical Rx for CTEPH |
| **Refractory** | Balloon atrial septostomy (R-to-L pop-off), Pott shunt, **lung / heart-lung transplant** | MDT |
| **Avoid** | PAH-specific drugs in **Group 2 / 3** PH (harmful, worsen V/Q) | Treat underlying |

## 9. Secondary Prevention / Risk Stratification
- **3-strata risk** (ESC/ERS 2022) — low / intermediate / high — assessed at baseline + every follow-up
- **Variables (REVEAL 2.0 / COMPERA 2.0):** WHO FC, 6MWD, BNP / NT-proBNP, RAP, CI, SV/BSA, echocardiography (RV, RA, pericardial effusion), CPET (VO₂, VE/VCO₂)
- **Treatment goal:** **low-risk profile at follow-up**
- **Monitoring:** clinical, WHO FC, 6MWD, BNP, echo (TAPSE, RV size, pericardial effusion), CPET; **RHC re-evaluation** if deteriorating
- **Survival:** modern 1 y 85%, 3 y 67%, 5 y 57%; IPAH median ~7 y
- **Pregnancy:** contraindicated; if late — termination or multidisciplinary delivery; avoid endothelin antagonists (Pregnancy X), riociguat (Pregnancy X)

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield
<callout icon="⭐" color="purple_bg">
	**12 high-yield points** for FCPS / MRCP PH questions.
</callout>

- **PH definition:** **mPAP > 20 mmHg at rest** on RHC (revised from 25, ESC 2022)
- **PAH (pre-capillary):** **mPAP > 20 + PAWP ≤ 15 + PVR > 2 WU** — by definition excludes LHD / lung / CTEPH
- **RHC is the gold standard** — echo is **screening only**
- **5 (now 6) WHO groups** — PAH, PH-LHD, PH-Lung, CTEPH, multifactorial (+ Group 6 mixed)
- **V/Q scan** is the **screening test for CTEPH** (mismatched perfusion defects)
- **Vasoreactivity testing** is performed **only in IPAH, HPAH, drug-induced PAH** — positive = mPAP ↓ ≥ 10 to ≤ 40 mmHg with maintained / ↑ CO
- **BMPR2** mutation in 70–80% of heritable PAH
- **Loud P2 + RV heave + TR + JVP↑** = classic PH examination
- **PAH therapy ladder:** **ERA** (bosentan, ambrisentan, macitentan) + **PDE5i** (sildenafil, tadalafil) + **riociguat** (sGC stim) + **prostacyclin** (epoprostenol IV, treprostinil, iloprost, selexipag) + **sotatercept** (activin trap, add-on)
- **CTEPH** is the only **curable** PH — **PEA surgery** in operable disease; **riociguat** for inoperable
- **PAH-specific drugs are HARMFUL in Group 2 (LHD) and Group 3 (lung) PH** — treat the underlying cause
- **Pregnancy contraindicated** in PAH — mortality 30–50%; endothelin antagonists and riociguat are **Pregnancy X**

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
	**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** PH is uncommon but devastating — median survival without therapy is 2.8 years. **Always consider PH in unexplained progressive dyspnoea**. **RHC is mandatory** for diagnosis and to classify the group. **CTEPH is the only curable form** — screen with V/Q scan in any unexplained PH. **PAH therapy** has transformed outcomes: **initial oral combination** (ERA + PDE5i) is now standard for low/intermediate risk; **IV prostacyclin** for high risk; **sotatercept** (STELLAR 2023) is the newest add-on. **Avoid PAH-specific drugs in Group 2 / 3 PH** and **pregnancy is contraindicated**. **3-strata risk assessment at every follow-up** guides escalation.
</callout>
