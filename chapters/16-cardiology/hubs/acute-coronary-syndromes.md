# Acute Coronary Syndromes (ACS)

**Topic:** Acute Coronary Syndromes — Cardiology

**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep

## 1. Introduction
ACS is the **spectrum of acute myocardial ischaemia** from plaque rupture/erosion + thrombosis: **STEMI** (transmural, complete occlusion), **NSTEMI** (subendocardial, partial occlusion) and **unstable angina** (ischaemia without necrosis). It is a **medical emergency** — "time is muscle".

### Pathogenesis
**Plaque rupture (60–70%) or erosion (25–30%)** → lipid core exposure → platelet activation (TxA₂, ADP, thrombin) → aggregation (GP IIb/IIIa) → fibrin → occlusive thrombus. **STEMI = complete occlusion** (TIMI 0–1); **NSTEMI/UA = partial** (TIMI 1–3).

---

## 2. Pathophysiology & ECG Correlation
| Stage | Time | ECG | Pathology |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hyperacute T | <30 min | Tall, peaked T | Reversible ischaemia |
| ST elevation | min–hours | ST ↑ in territory | Injury (transmural) |
| Q wave | hours–days | Pathological Q | Necrosis (STEMI) |
| T inversion | days–weeks | TWI | Peri-infarct |
| Normalised | weeks | Q wave persists | Scar |

### ECG Anatomy of MI
- **Anterior MI** (V1–V4) = **LAD**
- **Lateral MI** (I, aVL, V5–V6) = **LCx**
- **Inferior MI** (II, III, aVF) = **RCA** (LCx in 10%)
- **Posterior MI** (ST ↓ V1–V4) = **RCA / LCx**; confirm with V7–V9
- **RV infarct** → ST ↑ V4R; avoid nitrates, give IV fluids

---

## 3. Classification (Fourth Universal Definition 2018)
| Type | ECG | Troponin | Culprit lesion |
|---|---|---|---|
| **STEMI** | Persistent ST ↑ ≥20 min, new LBBB | ↑↑ | **Total occlusion** (TIMI 0–1) |
| **NSTEMI** | ST ↓, T inversion, or normal | ↑ | **Partial** (TIMI 1–3) |
| **Unstable angina** | ST ↓ / T inv / normal | **Normal** | Partial, dynamic |

### Special Subtypes
- **MINOCA** — MI with non-obstructive coronaries (5–10%); think SCAD, spasm, myocarditis
- **Type 2 MI** — supply/demand mismatch (anaemia, tachyarrhythmia, hypotension)
- **SCAD** — spontaneous coronary artery dissection (young women, peripartum)

---

## 4. Clinical Features
- **Crushing central chest pain** >20 min, radiation to jaw/left arm, diaphoresis, nausea
- **Painless in 20–30%**: diabetics, elderly, women, post-CABG
- **Signs:** tachycardia, S4, new MR murmur, hypotension (shock)
- **Atypical:** syncope, fatigue, abdominal pain, confusion (elderly/diabetics)

### Risk Stratification Scores
| Score | Variables | High-risk |
|---|---|---|
| **GRACE** | 8 (age, HR, SBP, Cr, Killip, arrest, ST, enzymes) | >140 |
| **TIMI (STEMI)** | 8 (age, risk factors, prior CAD, etc.) | ≥5 |
| **TIMI (NSTEMI/UA)** | 7 (age ≥65, ≥3 RF, ST dev, ≥2 angina, ↑ Tn, prior CAD, ASA) | ≥5 |
| **HEART** | History, ECG, Age, Risk factors, Troponin | ≥7 |
| **Killip** | Clinical HF post-MI (I–IV) | IV (S₃, shock) |

---

## 5. Investigations
- **ECG within 10 min** — STEMI criteria, posterior leads V7–V9 if suspected, right-sided V4R for inferior
- **hs-cTn 0/1 h algorithm** — rule-in (↑↑ at 0 h or Δ 0→1 h), rule-out (low at 0 & 1 h)
- **Bedside echo** — RWMA, LVEF, mechanical complications
- **FBC, U&E, LFT, lipids, HbA1c, coags, BNP, troponin**
- **CXR** — cardiomegaly, pulmonary oedema, mediastinal widening (dissection?)
- **Coronary angiography** — diagnostic & therapeutic (PCI)
- **CT aortogram** — if dissection suspected (rule out before antiplatelets/thrombolysis)
- **CMR** — for MINOCA workup

## 6. Differential Diagnosis of Acute Chest Pain
| Condition | Key Clues | Confirm |
|---|---|---|
| **STEMI / ACS** | ST ↑ territory, ↑Tn, diaphoresis | Angio |
| **Aortic dissection** | Tearing → back, BP differential, wide mediastinum | CT angio |
| **Pulmonary embolism** | Pleuritic, dyspnoea, hypoxia, S1Q3T3, ↑D-dimer | CTPA |
| **Pericarditis** | Pleuritic, worse leaning forward, rub, diffuse STE + PR ↓ | Echo, troponin |
| **Tension pneumothorax** | Sudden dyspnoea, unilateral, ↓BS, tracheal shift | CXR, clinical |
| **Oesophageal rupture** | Vomiting, subcutaneous emphysema | CT, contrast study |
| **Tako-tsubo** | Post-stress, apical ballooning, normal coronaries | LV gram, CMR |
| **MSK / anxiety** | Reproducible, no ECG change, normal Tn | Trial of therapy |

## 7. Complications of MI
### Arrhythmic (24–48 h)
- **VF / VT** (5–10%) — most common **early** cause of death
- **Bradyarrhythmias / AV block** (inferior MI, RCA) — usually transient
- **AF** (10–20%) — new-onset post-MI worsens prognosis

### Mechanical (3–7 d)
- **Free wall rupture** (5–14 d) → tamponade (90% fatal)
- **Papillary muscle rupture** (inferior MI, posteromedial) → acute severe MR
- **Ventricular septal rupture (VSR)** → harsh holosystolic murmur + thrill

### Inflammatory
- **Peri-infarction pericarditis** (1–3 d, 10–20%) — early
- **Dressler syndrome** (2–10 wk) — autoimmune, fever, pleuritic pain

### Other
- **Cardiogenic shock** (>40% LV infarct; 5–10% STEMI)
- **LV aneurysm** — persistent ST ↑ >6 weeks
- **Mural thrombus** → systemic emboli (stroke)
- **Stent thrombosis** — acute (<24 h), subacute (1–30 d), late (1–12 m), very late (>1 y)

## 8. Management — MONA-B + Reperfusion
### Initial (MONA-B)
- **M**orphine 2.5–5 mg IV titrated (pain, SOB)
- **O**xygen only if SpO₂ <90%
- **N**itrates SL/IV (avoid if SBP <90, RV infarct, PDE-5 use)
- **A**spirin 300 mg chewed
- **B**eta-blocker PO within 24 h (oral, no acute IV)
- + P2Y₁₂: **Ticagrelor 180 mg LD** (PLATO) or **Prasugrel 60 mg LD** (TRITON) or Clopidogrel 600 mg
- + Anticoagulant: UFH 70 U/kg, enoxaparin 1 mg/kg SC, bivalirudin
- + High-intensity **statin** (atorvastatin 80 mg)
- + **ACEi** if LVEF ≤40%, anterior MI, HTN, DM
- + **Radial access** (MATRIX) for PCI

### Reperfusion Strategy
| Strategy | Goal | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary PCI** with DES | Door-to-balloon **<90 min** | Preferred if FMC-to-device <120 min |
| **Fibrinolysis** (tenecteplase, alteplase, streptokinase) | Door-to-needle **<30 min** | If PCI unavailable within 120 min |
| **Rescue PCI** | After failed thrombolysis | Persistent ST ↑ / pain at 60–90 min |
| **CABG** | Mechanical complications, left main, 3VD | After stabilisation |

### NSTEMI / UA Strategy
- Risk-stratify (GRACE, TIMI, HEART)
- **Early invasive <24 h** if high risk (GRACE >140, TIMI ≥5, dynamic ST, ongoing pain)
- **Conservative** if low risk
- **DAPT 12 months** post-ACS (ticagrelor preferred)

## 9. Secondary Prevention (Post-ACS)
- **DAPT** Aspirin + P2Y₁₂ inhibitor for **12 months** (PRECISE-DAPT-guided: 3–6 mo if high bleed risk)
- **High-intensity statin** — LDL target **<1.4 mmol/L** (ESC 2023), <1.8 (very high-risk)
- **ACEi / ARB** — LVEF ≤40%, HTN, DM, anterior MI
- **Beta-blocker** indefinitely if LVEF ≤40% (or post-MI without HF for at least 1 year)
- **MRA** (spironolactone / eplerenone) if LVEF ≤40% + HF or DM
- **SGLT2i** (empagliflozin / dapagliflozin) if LVEF ≤40% ± DM (DAPA-MI, EMPACT-MI)
- **Cardiac rehab** — exercise 150 min/wk, Mediterranean diet
- **BP <130/80, HbA1c <7%, smoking cessation, alcohol <14 U/wk**
- **Vaccinations** — influenza, pneumococcal
- **Screen & treat depression**

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield
<callout icon="⭐" color="purple_bg">
**12 high-yield points** that appear on every FCPS / MRCP ACS question paper.
</callout>

- **ECG within 10 min** of arrival; do not wait for troponin in STMI to activate cath lab.
- **Posterior MI** = ST ↓ V1–V4 + take V7–V9 (ST ↑ ≥0.5 mm) — frequently missed.
- **de Winter T-waves** = upsloping ST ↓ at J-point in V1–V6 = **STEMI equivalent**.
- **Wellens syndrome** (A biphasic, B deep TWI V2–V3) = critical LAD — admit and image.
- **New LBBB + symptoms** = treat as STEMI (use Sgarbossa criteria).
- **RV infarct** → avoid nitrates, give IV fluids (preload-dependent).
- **Inferior MI = RCA; Lateral MI = LCx; Anterior MI = LAD.**
- **Most common early post-MI cause of death = VF / VT.**
- **Most common late post-MI cause of death = heart failure.**
- **Papillary muscle rupture** → acute severe MR (inferior MI, posteromedial papillary).
- **Dressler syndrome** = autoimmune pericarditis 2–10 weeks post-MI.
- **DAPT duration** = 12 months post-ACS; 6 months for stable CAD post-DES (ESC 2023).

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** ACS is a time-critical diagnosis. ECG + troponin at 0/1 h guides reperfusion (PCI <90 min for STEMI, early invasive <24 h for high-risk NSTEMI). Always **rule out aortic dissection** before thrombolysis or antiplatelets (CT aortogram). Aggressive secondary prevention (DAPT 12 mo, statin, ACEi/ARB, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i, cardiac rehab) halves recurrent events.
</callout>
