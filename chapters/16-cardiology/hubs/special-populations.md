## 1. Introduction
Cardiology decisions must be tailored to the patient in front of you. Four populations need modified thinking: **Pregnancy**, **Elderly**, **CKD/ESRD**, and **Cardio-Oncology**. Each has altered **physiology, pharmacology, and risk thresholds**.

### Why it matters
- **Pregnancy:** maternal mortality from cardiac disease is now the **leading cause of indirect maternal death** in the UK (MBRRACE).
- **Elderly:** ≥70% of CV patients are >65; frailty and polypharmacy drive outcomes more than LVEF.
- **CKD:** CV disease is the **leading cause of death** in CKD/ESRD.
- **Cardio-Oncology:** cancer therapy improves survival but cardiotoxicity is dose-limiting.

## 2. Pathophysiology — Key Population Physiology

### Pregnancy (Haemodynamic changes)
- **↑ Plasma volume 50%**, **↑ CO 30–50%** (peaks 32 wk) · **↓ SVR** · **↑ HR 10–20 bpm**
- Hypercoagulable (↑ factors I, VII, VIII, X; ↓ protein S) → ↑VTE
- Aortic root dilates ~1 mm — risk of dissection in aortopathy

### Elderly
- ↓ β-adrenergic responsiveness · ↓ renal/hepatic clearance · ↑ arterial stiffness
- **HFpEF**, AS, AF, conduction disease dominate
- **Frailty** + polypharmacy + cognitive impairment alter risk/benefit

### CKD / ESRD
- Uraemic milieu → **LVH, medial vascular calcification, accelerated atherosclerosis**
- Anaemia, AV fistula (high-flow → high-output failure), volume overload
- **Hyperkalaemia** limits RAAS-i use

### Cardio-Oncology
- **Type I (anthracycline):** dose-dependent, **irreversible** myocyte apoptosis (topoisomerase IIβ)
- **Type II (trastuzumab):** non-dose-dependent, **reversible** myocyte dysfunction (HER2-ErbB2)
- **VEGF-i** → endothelial dysfunction → HTN, proteinuria
- **ICI** (anti-PD-1 / PD-L1 / CTLA-4) → T-cell mediated **myocarditis** (high mortality 30–50%)
- **Radiation** (>30 Gy) → microvascular injury, fibrosis, pericardial disease, premature CAD

## 3. Classification

### Pregnancy — mWHO Risk (ESC 2018)
| Class | Risk of CV event | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **I** | No increase | Small ASD/VSD/PDA, mild isolated PS, MVP no MR |
| **II** | Small ↑ (5–10%) | Mild AS, unrepaired ASD/VSD, mild LV impairment |
| **III** | Significant ↑ (10–19%) | Mechanical valve, systemic RV, Marfan no aortic dilation, moderate MS |
| **IV** | **Pregnancy contraindicated** (≥40%) | Eisenmenger, severe AS / MS / PAH, Marfan with aorta >45 mm, severe LV dysfunction (EF <30%), prior PPCM with residual EF <30% |

### Cardiotoxicity (ESC 2022)
| Type | Drug | Mechanism | Reversibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I** | Anthracyclines (doxorubicin) | Free-radical, topoisomerase IIβ | **Irreversible** |
| **II** | Trastuzumab, Sunitinib | Mitochondrial, ErbB2 inhibition | **Reversible** |

### CKD Staging & CV Risk
| Stage | eGFR | CV implication |
|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | ≥60 | Risk factor-driven |
| 3a/b | 30–59 | ACEi/ARB dose adjust; statin still beneficial |
| 4 | 15–29 | SGLT2i (if eGFR >20); avoid nephrotoxins |
| 5 (ESRD) | <15 / dialysis | SGLT2i no benefit; high-output AVF; pericarditis risk |

## 4. Clinical Features

### Pregnancy Red Flags
- **Exertional dyspnoea, orthopnoea, nocturnal cough** — early HF
- **Palpitations + new murmur + AF** — look for structural disease
- **Syncope, chest pain** — exclude PE, dissection, ACS
- **PPCM:** HF in **last month pregnancy – 5 mo postpartum**, EF <45%

### Elderly Red Flags
- Falls, delirium, incontinence, weight loss (frailty)
- Orthostatic dizziness (over-treatment)
- Polypharmacy (>5 drugs) — Beer's / STOPP-START

### CKD Red Flags
- Uraemic pericarditis (fever, pleuritic pain, rub)
- Hyperkalaemic ECG changes → arrhythmia
- Volume overload (oedema, pulmonary oedema, HTN)
- Sudden cardiac death (K⁺ shifts on dialysis)

### Cardio-Oncology Red Flags
- Dyspnoea + recent chemo → **troponin + echo**
- Chest pain + ICI → **myocarditis** (often fulminant)
- HTN + VEGF-i / TKI → BP monitoring essential
- Chest radiation >30 Gy → CAD, valvular, constriction (years later)

---

## 5. Investigations

| Population | First-line | Special |
|---|---|---|
| **Pregnancy** | ECG, echo, BNP/NT-proBNP, Hb, ferritin | **Avoid radiation** (CT); use echo/MRI without gad; **ionising radiation only if essential** |
| **Elderly** | ECG, echo, orthostatic BP, frailty score (Rockwood, gait speed, grip) | Renal function, drug levels, falls assessment |
| **CKD/ESRD** | ECG (K⁺, Ca²⁺ changes), echo (LVH, EF), troponin | **Avoid nephrotoxins** (NSAIDs, contrast); non-contrast CT for pulmonary oedema |
| **Cardio-Oncology** | **Baseline echo + troponin** before chemo; **LVEF + GLS** q3 cycles (anthracycline / trastuzumab); troponin q-cycle | **CMR** if echo equivocal; **FDG-PET** for ICI myocarditis; endomyocardial biopsy if uncertain |

### Dosing & Monitoring Reference
- **Anthracycline cumulative dose limits:** Doxorubicin 400–450 mg/m², Epirubicin 900, Idarubicin 150, Mitoxantrone 140
- **Dexrazoxane** if cumulative doxorubicin >300 mg/m² (cardioprotectant)
- **Trastuzumab:** hold if LVEF drop >10% to <50%, or absolute <40%; resume if recovered
- **Pregnancy safe echo/MRI:** preferred over CT/nuclear

## 6. Differential Diagnosis — Chest Pain in Pregnancy / Special Groups

| Condition | Clue | Workup |
|---|---|---|
| **PE** | Pleuritic, dyspnoea, hypoxia, ↑HR, swollen leg | D-dimer (less useful), **bilateral leg Doppler**, **CTPA** (acceptable) or V/Q scan |
| **Aortic dissection** | Tearing, BP differential, pregnancy risk (3rd tri) | **CT/MRI angio** — same urgency as non-pregnant |
| **ACS (SCAD common)** | Young woman, peri-partum, no traditional RF | Angio; **SCAD** managed conservatively usually |
| **Peripartum CM** | Last month – 5 mo post-partum, EF <45% | Echo, BNP, troponin |
| **Pulmonary oedema (PPCMO)** | Pre-eclampsia, HTN, fluid overload | CXR (lead shield), echo, BNP |
| **Reflux / MSK** | Positional, food-related, no ECG change | Trial of antacid / analgesia |

> **Suspect SCAD in any pregnant/postpartum woman with ACS and no risk factors — avoid thrombolysis; percutaneous management preferred.**

## 7. Complications

### Pregnancy
- **Maternal HF, arrhythmia, stroke, death** (mWHO IV)
- **Foetal:** prematurity, IUGR, foetal loss (warfarin embryopathy, β-blocker IUGR)
- **PPCM** — thromboembolism, persistent LV dysfunction

### Elderly
- Falls, fractures (orthostasis), delirium (drug-induced), AKI, drug toxicity
- Procedural complications, post-op delirium

### CKD
- **Sudden cardiac death** (K⁺, fluid shifts), HF, uraemic pericarditis, AF, calciphylaxis
- **Hyperkalaemia** — fatal arrhythmia

### Cardio-Oncology
- **Anthracycline:** dilated CM, HF, arrhythmia
- **Trastuzumab:** reversible LV dysfunction
- **VEGF-i:** HTN crisis, HF, arterial thromboembolism
- **ICI myocarditis:** complete AV block, VT, cardiogenic shock, **death in 30–50%**
- **Radiation:** CAD (years later), valvular, pericardial constriction, conduction disease

## 8. Management

### A. Pregnancy
| Action | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Pre-conception counselling** | mWHO IV — contraindicated; discuss contraception / termination |
| **Drug safety** | **AVOID** ACEi/ARB, ARNI, warfarin (1st tri + near term), statins, amiodarone, DOACs, mycophenolate, spironolactone |
| **Safe drugs** | **Labetalol**, **methyldopa**, **nifedipine**, **hydralazine**, digoxin, amiodarone (if essential), LMWH |
| **Anticoagulation** | **LMWH** throughout; mechanical valve → LMWH + UFH bridging; warfarin 2nd–3rd tri allowed (some centres) |
| **PPCM** | Standard HF GDMT (**bromocriptine 2.5 mg BD × 2 wk then 2.5 mg OD × 6 wk**), avoid ACEi/ARB/Warfarin if breastfeeding; consider LVAD / transplant |
| **Delivery** | Vaginal preferred (avoid aortic dissection, pericardial effusion); C-section for obstetric / acute CV indication |
| **Endocarditis prophylaxis** | Only for highest-risk prosthetic valves / prior IE |

### B. Elderly
| Action | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Deprescribing** | Apply **STOPP/START** + **Beer's criteria**; remove anticholinergics, benzodiazepines, sulfonylureas, duplicate classes |
| **Frailty assessment** | Rockwood, Clinical Frailty Scale, gait speed, grip strength |
| **BP targets** | <140/90 (or <130/80 if tolerated) — **avoid over-aggressive lowering** (falls) |
| **Orthostatic hypotension** | Midodrine / fludrocortisone; compression stockings; review antihypertensives |
| **TAVI** preferred >75–80 or high surgical risk | Heart Team decision |
| **AF anticoagulation** | **DOACs** (apixaban / rivaroxaban) preferred over warfarin; fall risk is **not** a contraindication |

### C. CKD / ESRD
| Action | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Statin** | Atorvastatin 20 mg (or 40 if eGFR >30); avoid if on interacting drugs |
| **BP** | **ACEi/ARB** first; **SGLT2i** (empagliflozin / dapagliflozin) if eGFR >20 — cardiorenal benefit |
| **Volume** | Daily weight; ultrafiltration if refractory; AVF high-output → banding |
| **Hyperkalaemia** | Low-K⁺ diet, **patiromer** / **sodium zirconium cyclosilicate**; avoid K-sparing diuretics |
| **Anticoagulation** | **Apixaban** 2.5 mg BD if ≥2 of: age ≥80, weight ≤60 kg, CrCl 15–29; warfarin for mechanical valve |
| **Uraemic pericarditis** | **Intensify dialysis** (daily ×2 wk), NSAIDs, **avoid anticoagulation**, pericardiocentesis if tamponade |
| **Avoid** | NSAIDs, metformin (eGFR <30), gadolinium (nephrogenic systemic fibrosis) |

### D. Cardio-Oncology
| Setting | Management |
|---|---|
| **Pre-anthracycline** | **Baseline echo + troponin**; **dexrazoxane** if expected >300 mg/m²; liposomal doxorubicin |
| **LVEF monitoring** | Echo (3D / GLS) **q3 mo** during trastuzumab |
| **LVEF drop >10% to <50%** | **Hold chemo**; start **ACEi + β-blocker**; resume if recovered |
| **VEGF-i / TKI** | Baseline BP; **ACEi/ARB/CCB** first line; reduce / hold if uncontrolled |
| **ICI myocarditis (suspected)** | **STOP ICI immediately**; **methylpred 1–2 mg/kg/day**; if refractory → **infliximab, MMF, tacrolimus, ATG**; **permanent ICI discontinuation** |
| **Radiation** | **Echo q5 y** post chest RT >30 Gy; CAC scoring; statin; BP control |

## 9. Secondary Prevention & Long-term
- **Pregnancy:** multidisciplinary cardio-obstetric clinic; peripartum plan; follow-up echocardiogram post-PPCM
- **Elderly:** annual medication review; fall prevention; advance care planning
- **CKD:** aggressive risk factor control; transplant workup if appropriate
- **Cardio-Oncology:** lifelong surveillance for childhood cancer survivors; **AICR** lifestyle; consider cardio-protectants (carvedilol, ACEi, statins) prophylactically in high-risk regimens
- **Vaccinations** (influenza, pneumococcal, COVID-19) — all groups
- **Lifestyle:** Mediterranean diet, exercise, smoking cessation
- **Psychosocial support** — especially postpartum, frail elderly, and cancer patients

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield

- **mWHO IV = pregnancy contraindicated** (Eisenmenger, severe AS, severe PAH, Marfan aorta >45 mm, EF <30%, prior PPCM EF <30%)
- **Avoid in pregnancy:** ACEi/ARB (fetal renal dysplasia), warfarin (6–12 wk chondrodysplasia), statins, amiodarone, DOACs, MMF, spironolactone
- **Safe antihypertensives in pregnancy:** **labetalol**, methyldopa, nifedipine, hydralazine
- **PPCM:** EF <45% in last month – 5 mo postpartum; treat with **GDMT + bromocriptine 2.5 mg BD**
- **LMWH preferred** over warfarin in pregnancy (mechanical valve → LMWH + UFH bridging + planned delivery)
- **Elderly deprescribing:** STOPP/START + Beer's criteria; **anticholinergics, benzos, long-acting sulfonylureas** — high risk
- **CKD + AF:** apixaban 2.5 mg BD if ≥2 of age ≥80, weight ≤60 kg, CrCl 15–29
- **Anthracycline cardiotoxicity** is **dose-dependent + IRREVERSIBLE**; **dexrazoxane** if >300 mg/m²
- **Trastuzumab** is **non-dose-dependent + REVERSIBLE**; hold if LVEF drop >10% to <50%
- **ICI myocarditis** = chest pain/troponin ↑ on ICI → **STOP ICI + high-dose steroids (1–2 mg/kg) ± infliximab/MMF/tacrolimus**; mortality 30–50%
- **VEGF-i (sunitinib, pazopanib)** → HTN; manage with **ACEi/ARB/CCB**
- **Radiation >30 Gy** → coronary + valvular + pericardial + conduction disease; **echo q5 y**

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Always ask "**who is this patient?**" before any drug or intervention. The same HF regimen can be **life-saving** in a 50-year-old and **life-threatening** in a frail 85-year-old with CKD. **Pregnancy, elderly, CKD, and cancer patients each demand a tailored cardiology approach** — teratogens, frailty, renal dosing, and cardiotoxicity are the four pitfalls to avoid.
