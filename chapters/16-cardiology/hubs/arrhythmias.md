# Arrhythmias

## 1. Introduction & Definition
An **arrhythmia** is any rhythm disturbance originating from abnormal impulse generation (automaticity, triggered activity) or propagation (re-entry, block). Classified as **bradyarrhythmia** (HR < 60) or **tachyarrhythmia** (HR > 100), and by origin: **supraventricular (SVT)** vs **ventricular (VT/VF)**. The most clinically important: **atrial fibrillation** (1–2% of population, ↑stroke risk).

---

## 2. Pathophysiology
- **Enhanced automaticity** — ectopic focus firing faster than SA node (e.g., atrial tachycardia, idiojunctional)
- **Triggered activity** — early / delayed afterdepolarisations (digoxin toxicity, long QT, catecholamines)
- **Re-entry** — circuit of activation (90% of arrhythmias: AF, flutter, AVNRT, AVRT, VT, WPW)
- **Conduction block** — sinus node disease, AV block
- **Substrate:** ischaemia, scar, fibrosis, inflammation, infiltrative disease, ion-channel dysfunction, congenital accessory pathways, electrolyte disturbance (↓K, ↓Mg)

---

## 3. Classification
| Type | Examples | ECG Clue |
|---|---|---|
| **SVT (narrow QRS)** | AF, AFlutter, AVNRT, AVRT, AT, MAT | QRS < 120 ms |
| **VT (wide QRS)** | Monomorphic, polymorphic, Torsades | QRS > 120 ms, AV dissociation |
| **VF** | — | Chaotic, no QRS |
| **Bradyarrhythmia** | Sinus brady, SSS, AV block (1°, 2° I/II, 3°) | Slow rate, dropped beats |
| **Channelopathies** | LQTS, Brugada, CPVT, SQTS, ERS | Abnormal JT/ST morphology |

---

## 4. Clinical Features
- **Palpitations** (paroxysmal vs persistent), **chest pain**, **dyspnoea**, **fatigue**
- **Presyncope / syncope** (Stokes-Adams in 3° AV block; VT)
- **HF symptoms, pulmonary oedema** (rapid AF, VT)
- **Polyuria** (AF — ↑ANP)
- **Embolic stroke / TIA** (AF, AFlutter)
- **Sudden cardiac death** (VF, pulseless VT, Torsades, Brugada)
- **Examination:** rate, rhythm, regularity, JVP (cannon A waves in AV dissociation), murmurs, signs of HF, signs of underlying cause (thyroid, infection, PE)

---

## 5. Investigations
- **12-lead ECG** — rhythm strip + 12-lead; check P waves, QRS width, regularity, AV relationship
- **Longer monitoring:** 24-h Holter (paroxysmal), event recorder, **implantable loop recorder (ILR)** for infrequent
- **Echo** — structural HD, EF, valve disease, HCM, ARVC, Ebstein (WPW)
- **Bloods:** U&E, Mg²⁺, Ca²⁺, TFT (afib/AF/PSVT — ↑ or ↓TSH), troponin (ischaemia), BNP, D-dimer (PE), drug levels (digoxin), **toxicology**, blood cultures
- **Exercise test** — chronotropic incompetence, exercise-induced VT, ischaemia, loss of pre-excitation (WPW)
- **EP study** — inducibility, mapping, ablation, refractory period assessment
- **Tilt-table** — vasovagal syncope
- **CMR / cardiac CT** — ARVC, scar, infiltrative disease
- **Genetic testing** — channelopathies (KCNQ1, KCNH2, SCN5A, RYR2)

---

## 6. Differential Diagnosis of Tachyarrhythmia
| Condition | Clue | Confirm |
|---|---|---|
| **AF** | Irregularly irregular, no P, fibrillatory baseline | ECG |
| **Atrial flutter** | Sawtooth 300/min, 2:1/4:1 block | ECG, adenosine (unmask) |
| **AVNRT** | Narrow complex, regular, RP < PR, P buried in QRS | Adenosine (terminate) |
| **AVRT (WPW)** | Short PR, delta wave, QRS wide if pre-excited | ECG, EP study |
| **Atrial tachycardia** | Regular, abnormal P, 1:1 or block | Adenosine (may terminate) |
| **MAT** | ≥3 P morphologies, irregular, COPD | ECG, treat underlying |
| **VT (monomorphic)** | Wide QRS, AV dissociation, capture beats | ECG; treat as VT if uncertain |
| **SVT with aberrancy** | RBBB/LBBB pattern + SVT features | Adenosine (cautious) |
| **Torsades** | Polymorphic, QTc > 500, twisting points | ECG, IV Mg |

---

## 7. Complications
- **Stroke / systemic embolism** (AF: 5× ↑)
- **Heart failure** (tachycardia-mediated cardiomyopathy if persistent)
- **Syncope / SCD** (VT, VF, Torsades, Brugada, SSS, high-grade AV block)
- **Angina / MI** (supply-demand mismatch in CAD)
- **Pacemaker complications** — infection, lead failure, Twiddler
- **Drug toxicity** — amiodarone (lung/liver/thyroid/cornea), sotalol (QT), digoxin (visual disturbance, ↓K)

---

## 8. Management

### Acute Management
| Rhythm | Stable Strategy | Unstable Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **AF (acute)** | Rate control (BB / CCB / digoxin); anticoagulate; **cardioversion if <48 h** | **Synchronised DCCV 200J biphasic** |
| **AFlutter** | Rate control; **cavotricuspid isthmus ablation (curative, 95%)** | DCCV 100–200J |
| **AVNRT / SVT** | Vagal → **Adenosine 6 mg → 12 mg → 12 mg IV bolus** | DCCV |
| **VT (sustained)** | **Amiodarone 300 mg IV over 20–60 min** ± Procainamide / Lidocaine | **Synchronised DCCV 100J → 200J** |
| **VF / pulseless VT** | — | **Defibrillation 200J biphasic → CPR** (per ALS) |
| **Torsades** | **IV MgSO₄ 2 g** over 15 min, stop QT drugs, **overdrive pacing / isoprenaline** (pause-dep) | Defibrillation |
| **Symptomatic brady** | **Atropine 0.5 mg IV** (max 3 mg) → pacing | Transcutaneous pacing |
| **AF + WPW** | **AVOID** adenosine / verapamil / diltiazem / digoxin / BB — use **procainamide / ibutilide / DCCV** | DCCV 200J |

### Chronic Management
- **AF — Rate vs Rhythm:** AFFIRM, RACE → no mortality difference. Rhythm preferred if young, symptomatic, HFrEF, new AF. **EAST-AFNET 4**: early rhythm ↑ CV outcomes
- **Anticoagulation per CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥ 2 (M) / ≥ 3 (F) → DOAC** (apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban) preferred over warfarin. Warfarin if **mechanical valve** or **moderate–severe MS**
- **HAS-BLED** identifies modifiable bleeding risk (do not withhold OAC just because score ≥ 3)
- **Catheter ablation**: PVI cornerstone for AF (EARLY-AF, STOP-AF, ADVENT — pulsed field), accessory pathway (WPW), CTI for flutter, slow-pathway for AVNRT, substrate ablation for VT

---

## 9. Secondary Prevention
- **ICD** secondary prevention: survivors of **VT / VF** (AVID, CASH, CIDS) — reduces mortality
- **SCD primary prevention:** LVEF ≤ 35% on GDMT ≥ 3 months + NYHA II–III (MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT); HCM Risk-SCD ≥ 6%; ARVC; channelopathies
- **Beta-blocker** in LQT1, LQT2, CPVT (nadolol/propranolol)
- **Avoid QT-prolonging drugs** in LQTS; **avoid sodium-channel blockers** in Brugada
- **Lifestyle**: trigger avoidance (alcohol, caffeine, stimulants, electrolyte correction)
- **Channelopathy family screening** (1st-degree relatives)
- **Driving restrictions** (DVLA — 6 months after ICD for secondary prevention, 1 month after primary)

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield
<callout icon="⭐" color="purple_bg">
	**12 high-yield points** for FCPS / MRCP arrhythmia stations.
</callout>

- **AF** = **irregularly irregular** narrow-complex tachycardia; **no P waves**, fibrillatory baseline
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc** ≥ 2 (men) or ≥ 3 (women) → anticoagulate; **DOAC > warfarin** unless mechanical valve / MS
- **AF + WPW** is an **emergency** — **AVOID** AV nodal blockers (adenosine, verapamil, diltiazem, digoxin, BB) → use procainamide/ibutilide or DCCV
- **Torsades de Pointes** → **IV MgSO₄ 2 g** regardless of serum Mg; stop offending drugs; correct K⁺ > 4.5
- **Wide-complex tachycardia = VT until proven otherwise** (Brugada criteria, Vereckei algorithm)
- **Adenosine** is diagnostic + therapeutic for SVT (AVNRT/AVRT) — **never in AF + WPW**
- **Mobitz II, 3° AV block, bifascicular block + syncope** → **permanent pacemaker** (Class I)
- **Sick sinus syndrome** → pacemaker if symptomatic (fatigue, syncope, pauses > 3 s)
- **VF / pulseless VT** → **shock** (200J biphasic) — not amiodarone first
- **Brugada pattern** (coved ST↑ V1–V3) — avoid fever, Na⁺-channel blockers, treat VF with ICD
- **Long QT** (QTc > 480 ms) — beta-blocker (nadolol), K⁺ > 4.5, ICD if aborted SCD
- **CPVT** (normal QT, exercise-induced VT) — beta-blocker + **flecainide**; avoid competitive sport

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
	**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Arrhythmias are the **leading cardiac emergency**. AF affects 1–2% of the population and quintuples stroke risk. **DOACs are now first-line** for stroke prevention. **AF + WPW with rapid ventricular response is a true emergency** — AV nodal blockade can precipitate VF. **Torsades** ischaemic substrate is prolonged QT — IV magnesium is the universal antidote. Always search for reversible causes (electrolytes, drugs, ischaemia, thyroid). Risk-stratify channelopathies (family screening, ICD) and HCM (HCM Risk-SCD calculator) for SCD prevention.
</callout>
