# Heart Failure (HF)

<callout icon="🫁" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Heart Failure — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep

## 1. Introduction
HF is a **clinical syndrome** of structural / functional cardiac abnormality → reduced CO and/or elevated filling pressures at rest or on exertion, causing **dyspnoea, fatigue, ankle swelling** and signs of congestion / hypoperfusion. Affects **1–2% of adults**, >10% over 70 y. **HF is a clinical syndrome, not just a low EF.**

### Definition Pillars
1. Symptoms (dyspnoea, orthopnoea, fatigue)
2. Signs (↑JVP, crackles, peripheral oedema, displaced apex)
3. Objective evidence — **EF on echo, BNP/NT-proBNP, structural disease**

---

## 2. Pathophysiology
**Initial insult** (ischaemia, HTN, valve, toxin) → ↓contractility or ↓compliance → ↓CO and/or ↑filling pressure → **neurohormonal activation** (SNS, RAAS, ADH) → initially compensatory, then **maladaptive** (remodelling, fibrosis, arrhythmia, progression).

### Frank-Starling in HF
- Flattened Starling curve → extra preload → congestion with little gain in output
- **CO = SV × HR** — driven by preload, afterload, contractility, compliance
- **Remodelling** — chamber dilates, MR/TR worsen, spiral muscle fibres de-torsion

### Key Biomarkers
| Marker | Cut-off | Caveat |
|---|---|---|
| **BNP** | <100 pg/mL rules out HF (acute) | ↑in AF, CKD, elderly, PH; ↓in obesity |
| **NT-proBNP** | <300 pg/mL rules out HF | Age- and renal-adjusted |

---

## 3. Classification

### By Ejection Fraction
| Type | LVEF | GDMT Pillars |
|---|---|---|
| **HFrEF** | **≤40%** | ARNI/ACEi/ARB + BB + MRA + SGLT2i |
| **HFmrEF** | **41–49%** | SGLT2i, diuretics, treat cause |
| **HFpEF** | **≥50%** | SGLT2i, diuretics, BP control, treat comorbidities |

### By Side / Time / Profile / Function
- **Left** (dyspnoea, orthopnoea, pulmonary oedema) vs **Right** (oedema, JVP, hepatomegaly, ascites)
- **Acute / Chronic / Acute-on-chronic**
- **NYHA I–IV** (functional): I none, II slight, III marked, IV at rest
- **Hemodynamic profile:** Warm & Dry · Warm & Wet · Cold & Dry · Cold & Wet

### Common Precipitants of Decompensation
ACS · AF with rapid VR · Infection · HTN crisis · Non-adherence · NSAIDs · PE · Anaemia · Thyrotoxicosis · Pregnancy

## 4. Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- Exertional dyspnoea, orthopnoea, PND
- Fatigue, ↓exercise tolerance
- Nocturnal cough (cardiac asthma)
- Ankle swelling, abdominal distension, early satiety
- In low output: dizziness, confusion, oliguria

### Signs
- Tachycardia, tachypnoea, ↑JVP
- Bibasal crackles, pleural effusion
- Displaced apex, S3 gallop (HFrEF)
- MR/TR murmur, peripheral oedema, hepatomegaly, ascites
- Cool peripheries, narrow pulse pressure (low output)

### Left vs Right
| Feature | Left HF | Right HF |
|---|---|---|
| Dyspnoea / orthopnoea | ++ | – |
| Pulmonary crackles | ++ | – |
| ↑JVP, peripheral oedema | late | ++ |
| Hepatomegaly, ascites | – | ++ |

---

## 5. Investigations
- **ECG** — ischaemia, prior MI, AF, LBBB (a normal ECG makes HF less likely)
- **CXR** — cardiomegaly, upper-lobe diversion, Kerley B, bat-wing oedema, effusions
- **BNP / NT-proBNP** — diagnosis, prognosis, guiding therapy
- **Bloods** — FBC (anaemia), U&E, LFT, TFT, HbA1c, lipids, iron studies, troponin
- **ABG** — if severe distress (rising CO₂ + acidosis = fatigue / impending failure)
- **TTE** — EF, LV size, WMA, diastolic dysfunction, valves, RV, PASP, pericardium
- **TOE** — for valves, LAA, thrombus
- **CMR** — gold standard for tissue (viability, scar, myocarditis, amyloid, sarcoid)
- **Coronary angio** — if ischaemic cause suspected
- **RHC** — gold standard for haemodynamics (PH, shock, complex)

---

## 6. Differential Diagnosis of HF Syndrome
| Condition | Distinguishing Clue | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **COPD / asthma** | Wheeze, smoking, hyperinflation | PFTs |
| **Pneumonia** | Fever, focal consolidation, ↑WCC | CXR, procalcitonin |
| **PE** | Pleuritic, hypoxia, ↑D-dimer | CTPA |
| **Nephrotic / cirrhosis** | Ascites, oedema, normal LV | Urine, LFT, echo |
| **Constrictive pericarditis** | Pericardial knock, Kussmaul | CMR, RHC |
| **Tamponade** | Pulsus paradoxus, muffled heart sounds | Echo |
| **Anaemia / thyrotoxicosis** | High-output state | FBC, TFT |
| **Obesity hypoventilation** | BMI, daytime sleepiness | ABG, sleep study |

---

## 7. Complications
- Acute pulmonary oedema
- Cardiogenic shock (SCAI A → E)
- Arrhythmia — AF, VT, SCD
- Cardiorenal syndrome, diuretic resistance
- Congestive hepatopathy, cardiac cirrhosis
- Pleural effusion, ascites
- LV thrombus → systemic emboli
- Hyponatraemia, cachexia, frailty (advanced)

---

## 8. Management
### A. Acute Decompensated HF (ADHF)
- Sit upright, O₂ only if SpO₂ <90%, IV access, ECG, monitor
- **IV loop diuretic** (Furosemide 20–40 mg IV) — repeat / infusion
- **Vasodilator** (nitroglycerin IV) if SBP >100 and hypertensive / ischaemic
- **NIV (CPAP / BiPAP)** in acute pulmonary oedema with respiratory distress
- **Inotrope** (dobutamine, milrinone) if cold / hypotensive (ICU)
- **Vasopressor** (noradrenaline) in shock
- **Treat precipitant** — ACS, AF, sepsis, PE, anaemia, non-adherence
- **Mechanical support** (IABP, Impella, VA-ECMO) in refractory shock

### B. Chronic HFrEF — Quadruple Therapy (Pillars)
| Pillar | Drug & Target Dose |
|---|---|
| **ARNI** | Sacubitril/valsartan 97/103 mg BD (or ACEi/ARB if intolerant) |
| **Beta-blocker** | Bisoprolol 10 mg · Carvedilol 25 mg BD · Metoprolol succ 200 mg |
| **MRA** | Spironolactone 25 mg · Eplerenone 50 mg |
| **SGLT2i** | Dapagliflozin 10 mg · Empagliflozin 10 mg |

- **Loop diuretic** for symptom/congestion control (no mortality benefit alone)
- **Ivabradine** if sinus rhythm, HR ≥70 despite max BB
- **Hydralazine + ISDN** if ACEi/ARB/ARNI not tolerated (especially African-Caribbean)
- **Digoxin** — selected symptomatic patients (narrow therapeutic window)
- **IV iron** (ferric carboxymaltose) if ferritin <100 or TSAT <20% (FAIR-HF, AFFIRM-AHF)
- **Anticoagulation** only if AF, LV thrombus, VTE

### C. HFpEF Management
- **SGLT2i** (EMPAG-EF, DELIVER) — first disease-modifying therapy
- Treat congestion (diuretics), HTN, AF
- Manage comorbidities — obesity, DM, CKD, sleep apnoea, CAD
- Exercise, weight loss, cardiac rehab

### D. Devices
| Device | Indication |
|---|---|
| **ICD primary** | LVEF ≤35% on GDMT ≥3 mo, NYHA II–III, expected survival >1 y |
| **ICD secondary** | Survived VF / sustained VT |
| **CRT (LBBB)** | LVEF ≤35%, LBBB QRS ≥150 ms, NYHA II–IV on GDMT |
| **LVAD** | Advanced HF, bridge to transplant / destination therapy |
| **Heart transplant** | Refractory HF, suitable candidate |

---

## 9. Secondary Prevention & Monitoring
- **Salt moderation** (<6 g/day; fluid restriction in severe congestion)
- **Daily weight monitoring** — alert if +2 kg in 3 d
- **Vaccinations** — influenza annually, pneumococcal
- **Cardiac rehabilitation** — supervised exercise, education
- **Screen for & treat** — iron deficiency, depression, sleep apnoea, CKD
- **Avoid** — NSAIDs, non-dihydropyridine CCB (verapamil/diltiazem), thiazolidinediones, class I AAD
- **Regular review** — clinical, BNP, echo, U&E (especially on ACEi/ARNI/MRA)
- **Palliative care** discussion in advanced / refractory HF

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield
<callout icon="⭐" color="purple_bg">
**12 high-yield points** that show up on every FCPS / MRCP HF question paper.
</callout>

- **HF is a clinical syndrome, not just a low EF** — symptoms + signs + objective evidence required.
- **4 pillars of HFrEF GDMT:** ARNI + BB + MRA + SGLT2i — all reduce mortality.
- **SGLT2i** is the **only** disease-modifying drug for HFpEF.
- **NYHA IV = at rest, III = marked, II = slight, I = none.**
- **Warm & Wet** = diuretics ± vasodilator. **Cold & Wet** = inotrope ± MCS.
- **Killip class** = post-MI clinical HF; **SCAI** = cardiogenic shock stage A–E.
- **Iron deficiency in HF** = IV iron (FAIR-HF, AFFIRM-AHF, IRONMAN).
- **BNP <100 pg/mL** rules out acute HF; obesity lowers BNP (interpret with BMI).
- **LBBB with QRS ≥150 ms + LVEF ≤35% + NYHA II–IV** = CRT.
- **LVEF ≤35% on GDMT ≥3 mo + NYHA II–III + survival >1 y** = ICD.
- **Avoid in HFrEF:** NSAIDs, verapamil/diltiazem, class I AAD, thiazolidinediones.
- **Cardiorenal syndrome:** diuretic resistance → consider metolazone, ultrafiltration, inotrope.

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** HF is a **chronic, progressive, lethal** syndrome with 5-y mortality ~50%. **Quadruple therapy** (ARNI/ACEi + BB + MRA + SGLT2i) initiated within 4 weeks of diagnosis and titrated to target dose is the single biggest advance in modern HF care. **SGLT2i** works across the entire EF spectrum. **Treat the cause and the comorbidities** — HTN, ischaemia, valve, AF, iron deficiency, CKD. **Device therapy** (ICD, CRT, LVAD, transplant) for selected advanced patients.
</callout>
