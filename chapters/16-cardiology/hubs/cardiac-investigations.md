## 1. Introduction
Cardiac investigations span **clinical exam, ECG, biomarkers, echocardiography, stress testing, CT/MRI and cardiac catheterisation**. The art of cardiology is choosing the **right test at the right time** for the right patient — to confirm diagnosis, assess severity, guide therapy, and risk-stratify.

### Initial Workup
- **History** + targeted **CV exam** (IPPA: Inspection, Palpation, Auscultation)
- **12-lead ECG** within minutes of presentation
- **Bedside bloods**: troponin, BNP/NT-proBNP, FBC, U&E, lipids, HbA1c, TFT
- **Echocardiogram** (TTE) for any suspected structural / functional disease

## 2. Investigations Framework

### A. Bedside & Bloods
- **Troponin I/T (hs-cTn)** — most specific marker of myocyte injury; rises 3–12 h, peaks 24 h, normalises 7–10 d
- **BNP / NT-proBNP** — supports HF diagnosis; rule-out cut-off <100 pg/mL (BNP) / <300 pg/mL (NT-proBNP)
- **D-dimer** — for PE workup, sensitive not specific
- **ABG** — in acute pulmonary oedema, PE, sepsis

### B. ECG — the 5-step read
1. **Rate** (300 / number of big squares between R-R)
2. **Rhythm** (P before every QRS, regular?)
3. **Axis** (LAD: I ↑, II ↑, III ↓; RAD: I ↓, II ↑, III ↑)
4. **Intervals** (PR 120–200 ms, QRS <120 ms, QTc <440 ms ♂ / <460 ms ♀)
5. **Morphology** — P, QRS, ST, T, U waves, hypertrophy criteria

### C. Echocardiography
- **TTE** (transthoracic) — first-line for structure / function / valves
- **TOE** (transoesophageal) — better for LAA, valves, aorta, IE, prosthetic
- **Stress echo** — exercise / dobutamine for ischaemia / viability
- **3D echo / strain (speckle tracking)** — subclinical dysfunction, cardio-onc, amyloid

### D. Stress Testing
- **Exercise ECG** — positive: ≥1 mm ST ↓ at 80 ms post-J in ≥2 contiguous leads
- **Stress echo** — regional wall-motion abnormality (RWMA) on stress
- **Stress nuclear (SPECT/PET)** — perfusion defect
- **Stress CMR** — perfusion + LGE for scar

### E. Advanced Imaging
- **CT coronary angio (CTCA)** — anatomy; calcium score (Agatston) for risk
- **Cardiac MRI (CMR)** — LGE for scar/viability, T1/T2 mapping, infiltrative disease
- **Coronary calcium score (CAC)** — 0 (low), 1–100 (moderate), >400 (high)

### F. Invasive
- **Coronary angiography** — gold standard for CAD
- **Right heart cath (Swan-Ganz)** — PA pressures, PCWP, CO, PVR
- **FFR / iFR** — functional significance of intermediate lesions (50–90%)

## 3. Indications by Scenario
| Scenario | First-line | Confirmatory |
|---|---|---|
| Chest pain (suspected ACS) | ECG + hs-cTn 0/1 h | Coronary angio |
| Stable angina | Exercise ECG / stress echo | CTCA / invasive angio |
| Suspected HF | BNP + TTE | CMR (cause) |
| Valve disease | TTE | TOE if poor windows |
| IE | TTE → TOE ± blood cultures | PET-CT (prosthetic) |
| Cardiomyopathy | TTE | CMR (LGE, T1 mapping) |
| Pulmonary HTN | Echo (PASP) | RHC (gold standard) |

---

## 4. ECG Patterns You Must Know
- **STEMI:** ST ↑ ≥1 mm (≥2 mm V2–V3 ♂, ≥1.5 mm V2–V3 ♀) in ≥2 contiguous leads; new LBBB
- **NSTEMI:** ST ↓ / T inversion + ↑ troponin
- **Wellens (LAD):** deep symmetric T inversion V2–V3
- **de Winter T-waves:** upsloping ST ↓ at J-point V1–V6 = STEMI equivalent
- **Posterior MI:** ST ↓ V1–V4, take V7–V9 (ST ↑ ≥0.5 mm)
- **LVH (Sokolow-Lyon):** S V1 + R V5/V6 >35 mm
- **RBBB:** rsR' V1, wide S I, V6; QRS >120 ms
- **LBBB:** broad monomorphic R I, V6; QS V1; QRS >120 ms
- **Hyperkalaemia:** peaked T → flat P → wide QRS → sine wave
- **Digoxin effect:** scooped ST (Salvador Dali sign)

## 5. Echocardiography — Key Measurements
| Parameter | Normal | Severe |
|---|---|---|
| **LVEF** | 55–70% | ≤30% severe ↓ |
| **LVEDD** | <55 mm | >70 mm (dilation) |
| **LA volume index** | <34 mL/m² | >40 (HFpEF criterion) |
| **E/e' (avg)** | <8 | >14 (↑ filling pressure) |
| **TAPSE (RV)** | >17 mm | <15 mm (RV dysfunction) |
| **PASP** | <35 mmHg | >50 mmHg (PH) |

### Doppler & Valve Quantification
- **Aortic stenosis:** Vmax >4 m/s, mean gradient >40 mmHg, AVA <1.0 cm²
- **MR severity:** VC >0.7 cm, EROA >0.4 cm², RV >60 mL
- **MS severity:** MVA <1.5 cm², PHT >220 ms

## 6. Differential of Common Findings
| Finding | Top Differentials | Best Confirmatory Test |
|---|---|---|
| ST elevation | STEMI, pericarditis, BER, Brugada, LVH, LBBB | Serial ECG + troponin + echo |
| Low EF | Ischemia, DCM, myocarditis, tachycardia-induced, valve | CMR with LGE |
| Troponin ↑ | MI, myocarditis, PE, Tako-tsubo, aortic dissection, sepsis | Clinical + imaging |
| ↑ BNP | HF, PE, AF, CKD, sepsis, anaemia | Echo + clinical context |
| Wide complex tachycardia | VT, SVT with aberrancy, hyperkalaemia, Na-blocker toxicity | Adenosine (if stable) / EP study |
| Cyanotic + murmur | TOF, TGA, Eisenmenger, Ebstein | Echo / CMR |

---

## 7. Limitations & Pitfalls
- **ECG:** normal in 50% of stable angina; LBBB masks ischaemia (use Sgarbossa)
- **Troponin:** ↑ in renal failure, PE, sepsis, myocarditis, Tako-tsubo — **not specific to MI**
- **BNP:** falsely low in obesity, high in AF/CKD/elderly
- **TTE:** poor windows in obesity, COPD — use TOE / contrast
- **CTCA:** high calcium score limits accuracy; radiation + iodine contrast
- **Stress test:** false-positive in women/LVH/digoxin; false-negative if not achieved 85% predicted HR

## 8. Choosing the Right Test — Quick Table
| Question | Best Test |
|---|---|
| Diagnose ACS? | ECG + hs-cTn (0/1 h algorithm) |
| Quantify LV function? | TTE (Simpson's biplane EF) |
| Ischaemia / viability? | Stress echo, SPECT, CMR |
| Anatomy of coronaries? | CTCA (low–intermediate) or invasive angio |
| Tissue characterisation? | CMR (LGE, T1/T2 mapping) |
| Haemodynamics? | RHC (gold standard) |
| Valve severity? | TTE → TOE if needed |
| Pulmonary HTN? | Echo (PASP) → confirm with RHC |

---

## 9. Risk Scores & Reporting
- **GRACE** — in-hospital mortality in ACS
- **TIMI** — 14-day event risk in NSTEMI/UA
- **HEART score** — chest pain in ED (H/E/A/R/T)
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc** — stroke risk in AF
- **HAS-BLED** — bleeding risk on anticoagulation
- **EuroSCORE II / STS** — cardiac surgical risk
- **PRECISE-DAPT / DAPT score** — bleeding vs ischaemic trade-off for DAPT duration
- **EF (Simpson's biplane)** — always report LV/RV function, valve severity, and PASP

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield
<callout icon="⭐" color="purple_bg">
**12 high-yield points** every FCPS / MRCP candidate must know for cardiac investigations.
</callout>

- **hs-cTn 0/1 h algorithm** is the current standard for ruling in / out MI in ED.
- **Transthoracic echo (TTE)** is the first imaging test for almost every cardiac problem.
- **TOE** is superior for LAA thrombus, valves, aorta, prosthetic IE, and endocarditis complications.
- **CTCA** is the test of choice to **rule out** CAD in low–intermediate risk chest pain.
- **CMR** is the gold standard for **viability, scar, myocarditis, amyloid, ARVC**.
- **Coronary angio** is the gold standard for **defining** CAD; FFR/iFR for physiology.
- **Right heart cath** is the **gold standard** for pulmonary hypertension diagnosis.
- **Sgarbossa criteria** diagnose MI in LBBB (concordant ST ≥1 mm = 5 pts is specific).
- **Wellens syndrome** = critical LAD stenosis, pain-free; admit and image.
- **de Winter T-waves** = STEMI equivalent → activate cath lab.
- **A normal ECG does not exclude ischaemia**; a normal stress test does not exclude CAD.
- **Strain imaging (GLS)** detects subclinical LV dysfunction (cardio-oncology, amyloid, pre-HF).

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Cardiac investigations must be **patient-centred, time-appropriate and economically rational**. Always start with history, exam, ECG and bedside echo. Choose the next test based on **what the answer will change**, not protocol reflex. Imaging is a tool to confirm or refute a clinical hypothesis — never order what you cannot interpret.
</callout>
