# Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)

<callout icon="🫀" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** HCM — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**LV wall thickness ≥15 mm (or ≥13 mm with family history)** unexplained by abnormal loading. Most common **genetic cardiovascular disease** (1:500). **Sarcomeric disease** in ~60% (autosomal dominant, MYH7, MYBPC3).

## 2. Pathophysiology
- **Sarcomere mutations** → ↑Ca²⁺ sensitivity, energy depletion → myocyte disarray, hypertrophy, fibrosis
- **Dynamic LVOT obstruction** (~70%): septal hypertrophy + **SAM** (systolic anterior motion of MV) → MR + gradient
- **Diastolic dysfunction** (stiff, hypertrophied LV)
- **Microvascular ischaemia** (capillary rarefaction)
- **Arrhythmia** — AF 20–30%, NSVT, VF

## 3. Subtypes
- **Asymmetric septal** (most common)
- **Apical** (Yamaguchi, Asians) — giant TWI V3–V4, "spade-like" LV
- **Mid-ventricular** (apical aneurysm, mural thrombus, SCD risk)
- **Concentric**
- **Massive LVH ≥30 mm** — high SCD risk
- **End-stage / "burned-out"** — LVEF <50%, dilation (5–10%)

## 4. Clinical Features
- **Asymptomatic** in most
- **Dyspnoea** (diastolic dysfunction, MR, LVOT)
- **Chest pain** (microvascular ischaemia)
- **Syncope** (LVOT obstruction or arrhythmia) — exertional
- **Palpitations** (AF, VT)
- **SCD** (often first presentation, young athletes)
- **Examination:** **jerky pulse** (spike-and-dome), **double apex beat**, **harsh systolic murmur LLSB ↑Valsalva**, **S4**

## 5. Diagnosis
- **ECG** — LVH, deep T inversion, septal Q waves; **giant TWI V3–V4 in apical HCM**
- **Echo** — wall thickness ≥15 mm, SAM, LVOT gradient (rest, Valsalva, exercise), MR, EF
- **CMR** — gold standard (apical, RV, atypical cases, fibrosis/LGE, apical aneurysm)
- **Holter** (24–48 h) — NSVT
- **Exercise test** — BP response (flat/fall in <40 y = SCD risk), gradient
- **Family screening** — 1st-degree relatives, ECG + echo every 1–5 y, age 12–18
- **Genetic testing** — sarcomere panel

## 6. SCD Risk Stratification (ESC 2022 HCM Risk-SCD)
Variables: age, max wall thickness, LA diameter, LVOT gradient, NSVT, unexplained syncope, family SCD
- **<4%** — low (no ICD)
- **4–6%** — intermediate
- **≥6%** over 5 y — high → **ICD**

> Apical aneurysm: ~5%/y SCD. EF <50% (end-stage): ~1–2%/y. Massive LVH ≥30 mm: high risk.

## 7. Management
### Asymptomatic / Low Risk
- Monitor (echo 1–2 y), family screening, education
- Avoid competitive sports (ESC 2020)

### Symptomatic Obstructive (HOCM)
1. **Beta-blocker** — first-line (bisoprolol, nadolol, propranolol, metoprolol)
2. **Non-dihydro CCB** — verapamil, diltiazem (caution if severe gradient)
3. **Disopyramide** — add-on (class IA, safe in structural HD unlike flecainide; monitor QT)
4. **Septal reduction therapy** for refractory symptoms with LVOT gradient ≥50 mmHg at rest or with Valsalva:
   - **Surgical myectomy** (gold standard, gold-standard mortality)
   - **Alcohol septal ablation (ASA)** — alternative in elderly/comorbid

### Symptomatic Non-Obstructive
- Beta-blocker, verapamil, cautious diuretics

### AF
- Rate or rhythm (amiodarone)
- **Anticoagulate regardless of CHA₂DS₂-VASc**

### End-stage (EF <50%)
- Standard GDMT, transplant evaluation, ICD for SCD

## 8. Avoid
- **Inotropes, afterload reduction** (worsens gradient), **diuretics** (cautious), **digoxin** (worsens obstruction), **ACEi/ARB** (no benefit, may worsen)

> **Pearls**
> - **HCM = wall ≥15 mm unexplained, sarcomere disease**
> - **HOCM**: gradient ≥30 mmHg, SAM, MR, **harsh systolic murmur ↑Valsalva**
> - **Apical HCM** (Yamaguchi) — giant TWI V3–V4, spade-like LV (use CMR)
> - **HCM Risk-SCD ≥6% over 5 y** → ICD
> - **Avoid** inotropes, afterload reduction, diuretics in HOCM
> - **Disopyramide** is class IA but safe in structural HD (unlike flecainide)
> - **ATTR amyloid** mimics HCM — screen elderly with bone scintigraphy

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** HCM is the **most common cause of SCD in young athletes**. Always screen family members. ESC 2022 HCM Risk-SCD calculator guides ICD. **Septal reduction therapy** (surgery or ASA) is reserved for refractory symptoms with severe gradient. **Differentiate from ATTR amyloid** — treatable mimicker in elderly.
</callout>
