# Cardiac Sarcoidosis

## 1. Definition
- Non-caseating granulomas in heart; systemic sarcoidosis in 30-50%
- AV block in young adult + bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy = think cardiac sarcoid

## 2. Key Features
- **Conduction disease**: 3rd-degree AVB in young (<55) — classic
- **VT/VF**: monomorphic VT from RV/LV granulomas
- **Heart failure**: HFrEF or HFpEF
- **Sudden cardiac death** can be first presentation
- **Lung disease**, lymphadenopathy, skin (EN, lupus pernio)

## 3. Diagnosis (HRS criteria)
- **CMR**: LGE, basal septal/RV free wall, edema
- **FDG-PET**: myocardial uptake after fasting (suppress physiological uptake)
- **EMB**: non-caseating granulomas (low yield, patchy)
- Labs: ↑ACE (low sensitivity), troponin, ↑CD4:CD8 in BAL

## 4. Management
- **Steroids** ± methotrexate/MMF for active inflammation
- **ICD** for: AVB, sustained VT, SCD, LVEF <35%
- Standard HF GDMT
- **Avoid**: amiodarone toxicity can mimic; Pacing may be needed in AVB

> **Pearls**: New **AVB <55** with systemic features → cardiac sarcoid. **FDG-PET** for activity, **CMR** for fibrosis. ICD for secondary prevention.
