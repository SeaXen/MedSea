# Coronary Artery Anomalies

## 1. Definition
**Anomalous origin, course, termination, or structure** of a coronary artery. Most are **incidental and benign**, but some cause SCD in young athletes.

### Classification
- **Anomalous origin:**
  - AAOCA from opposite sinus (ACAOS) — interarterial course → SCD risk
  - RCA from LMS / LCA from RCS
- **Anomalous course:** prepulmonic, septal, retro-aortic, retrocardiac
- **Anomalous termination:** fistulas
- **Myocardial bridge** (LAD tunneled in myocardium)

---

## 2. High-Risk Anomalies
- **Interarterial course** (between aorta and PA) — especially if slit-like, intramural, acute-angle take-off
- **RCA from left cusp** with interarterial course — most common cause of exercise-related SCD
- **Single coronary artery**
- **Anomalous LCA from pulmonary artery (ALCAPA / Bland-White-Garland)** — infant presentation

---

## 3. Clinical Features
- Often asymptomatic
- Exertional angina, syncope, **SCD** in young athletes
- Symptoms usually before age 30–35

---

## 4. Diagnosis
- **CTCA** — gold standard for anatomy
- **Invasive angiography** with IVUS / FFR for functional significance
- **Stress testing** for ischaemia
- **CMR** for viability, anomalous origin

---

## 5. Management
- **Interarterial course + ischaemia / SCD:** **surgical unroofing or reimplantation**
- **Myocardial bridge:** beta-blocker (↓systolic compression); stenting controversial
- **Coronary fistula:** close if large / symptomatic (surgical or coil embolisation)
- **ALCAPA:** surgical reimplantation in infancy
- Activity restriction in high-risk anomalies

---

## 6. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **Interarterial course** of anomalous coronary = SCD risk in athletes
- **CTCA = gold standard** for anatomical definition
- Surgical unroofing / reimplantation for high-risk interarterial course
- **ALCAPA** (LCA from PA) presents in infancy — surgical emergency
- Myocardial bridge: BB first-line; stenting controversial
