# Hypertension — Classification

## 1. Definition
**Hypertension** = persistent elevation of blood pressure. **ACC/AHA 2017**: ≥130/80 mmHg. **ESC/ESH 2018**: ≥140/90 mmHg (office); ≥135/85 (home/ABPM). Classification differs in thresholds and targets.

## 2. Key Features
- **ACC/AHA stages**: Normal <120/80; Elevated 120–129/<80; **Stage 1** 130–139/80–89; **Stage 2** ≥140/90; Hypertensive crisis >180/120
- **ESC/ESH categories**: Optimal <120/80; Normal 120–129/80–84; High-normal 130–139/85–89; **Grade 1** 140–159/90–99; **Grade 2** 160–179/100–109; **Grade 3** ≥180/110
- **Isolated systolic** (ISH): SBP ≥130, DBP <80 (esp. elderly)
- **White-coat HTN**: elevated in office, normal at home
- **Masked HTN**: normal in office, elevated at home
- **ABPM** = 24h ambulatory BP; gold standard for diagnosis

## 3. Pearls
- **ACC/AHA** uses lower threshold (130/80); identifies more at-risk individuals
- **ESC/ESH** uses 140/90; greater emphasis on global CV risk
- **BP measurement** — proper technique critical: seated, 5 min rest, both arms, correct cuff size
- Out-of-office BP (home/ABPM) recommended to confirm diagnosis and detect white-coat/masked

> **Pearls**
> - **Threshold differs**: ACC/AHA 130/80 vs ESC/ESH 140/90
> - **ABPM** is the gold standard — confirms dx, detects white-coat/masked
> - **Isolated systolic HTN** common in elderly (loss of aortic compliance)
> - Always measure **both arms** at first visit
