# Cardiomyopathies — ICD Primary Prevention

## 1. Ischemic CM
- LVEF ≤35% + NYHA II–III + ≥40 d post-MI + ≥3 mo post-revascularization + ≥3 mo optimal GDMT
- Class IA (MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT)
- Expected survival >1 year

## 2. Non-Ischemic DCM
- LVEF ≤35% + NYHA II–III + ≥3 mo optimal GDMT
- Class IA (SCD-HeFT, DEFINITE)
- Reassess LVEF after 3 mo GDMT — may improve

## 3. HCM
- **5-year SCD risk** ≥6% (HCM-SCD calculator)
- Risk factors: NSVT, unexplained syncope, massive LVH (≥30 mm), family history of SCD, abnormal BP response to exercise
- **Class IIa** if risk 4–6%, **IIa** if >6%

## 4. ARVC
- ≥3 major, or 2 major + 2 minor, or 1 major + 4 minor Task Force criteria
- History of VF/sustained VT → ICD
- High-risk features: extensive RV involvement, LV involvement, NSVT, syncope

## 5. Other
- **LQT, Brugada, CPVT** after cardiac arrest or breakthrough on therapy
- **Cardiac sarcoidosis** with sustained VT or LVEF <35% despite immunosuppression

## 6. Bridge
- **WCD** for high-risk waiting periods (post-diagnosis, awaiting reassessment)

> **Pearls** — **Always optimize GDMT 3+ months first** before implanting ICD for non-ischemic CM (EF may recover). Use **HCM-SCD calculator** for HCM. Reassess EF after recovery periods.

**Q1.** What is the most appropriate first-line therapy for ICD for primary prevention?

  - **A.** Implant technique
  - **B.** An advanced/surgical therapy reserved for refractory disease
  - **C.** Symptomatic treatment only, no disease-modifying therapy

  > **Answer: A** — Implant technique
  >
  > *Source:* **Implant technique**: left pre-pectoral, subclavian or axillary vein access, RV lead to apex or septum, atrial lead to RA appendage, CS lead for LV (CRT-D).
