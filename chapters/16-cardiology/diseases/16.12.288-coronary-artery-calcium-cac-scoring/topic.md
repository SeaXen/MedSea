# Coronary Artery Calcium (CAC) Scoring

## 1. What It Measures
- **Agatston score** from non-contrast cardiac CT — quantifies coronary calcified plaque
- Components: 1-3 (minimal), 4-100 (mild), 101-400 (moderate), >400 (severe)

## 2. When to Use
- **Asymptomatic adults** age 40-75 with **intermediate** (7.5-20%) 10-yr ASCVD risk
- Helps decide on statin therapy when risk is uncertain
- **CAC = 0**: defer statin (if no DM, smoking, FH); repeat in 5-10 yr
- **CAC 1-99**: statin favored
- **CAC ≥100 or ≥75th percentile**: high-risk, statin + aspirin considered

## 3. Limitations
- Doesn't detect **non-calcified** plaque
- Not useful in established CVD, DM (calcify differently), or very high risk
- May be falsely low in statin users

## 4. Integration
- Combine with **risk score** and **coronary CTA** when needed
- Use **MESA** calculator for percentile by age/sex/race

> **Pearls**: **CAC = 0** has 10-yr warranty of <1% CV event in low-risk. **CAC >400** = risk equivalent to established CAD.
