# Sleep Apnea in Heart Failure

<callout icon="😴" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Sleep Apnea in HF — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Repetitive episodes of upper airway collapse (OSA) or loss of respiratory drive (CSA) during sleep** causing hypoxia, hypercapnia, arousals, and sympathetic activation. **Highly prevalent (50–75%) in HF**, often undiagnosed.

## 2. Types

### Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)
- **Upper airway collapse** despite respiratory effort
- **Snoring, witnessed apnoeas, daytime sleepiness**
- Risk factors: obesity, large neck circumference, male, retrognathia
- Prevalent in HFpEF (often obese, hypertensive)

### Central Sleep Apnea (CSA)
- **Loss of respiratory drive** — no effort
- **Cheyne-Stokes respiration** — crescendo-decrescendo pattern
- Associated with **low EF, low PaCO₂, high LV filling pressure**
- Pathophysiology: delayed circulation → mis-sensing CO₂ → apnoea → arousal → hyperventilation
- Prevalent in **HFrEF** (40%)

## 3. Pathophysiology in HF
- **OSA** → negative intrathoracic pressure → ↑ LV afterload, ↑ venous return → wall stress
- **CSA / Cheyne-Stokes** → cyclical hypoxia → sympathetic surges → arrhythmia, ischaemia
- **Sleep fragmentation** → ↓ growth hormone, ↑ cortisol
- **RAAS activation** → vasoconstriction, sodium retention
- **Endothelial dysfunction** — atherosclerosis, PH

## 4. Clinical Features
- **Snoring, witnessed apnoeas** (collateral history)
- **Daytime sleepiness, fatigue** — Epworth Sleepiness Scale
- **Morning headaches** (hypercapnia)
- **Nocturnal dyspnoea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea**
- **Worsening HF** despite optimal GDMT
- **Nocturnal arrhythmias** — AF, non-sustained VT
- **Pulmonary hypertension**

## 5. Diagnosis
- **Polysomnography (PSG)** — gold standard
  - **Apnoea-Hypopnoea Index (AHI)** — events per hour
  - Mild: 5–15; Moderate: 15–30; Severe: >30
- **Home sleep apnoea testing** — for high suspicion of OSA
- **Overnight oximetry** — desaturations + Cheyne-Stokes pattern suggest CSA
- **Questionnaires** — Epworth, STOP-BANG, Berlin

## 6. Treatment

### OSA in HF
- **CPAP** — first-line
- **Weight loss** (often dramatic improvement)
- **Avoid alcohol / sedatives**
- **Positional therapy** (lateral sleeping)
- **MADs (mandibular advancement devices)** — alternative

### CSA in HF
- **Optimise GDMT** — improves CSA (better cardiac output)
- **Adaptive servo-ventilation (ASV)** — **CONTRAINDICATED** in HFrEF with LVEF <45% (SERVE-HF: ↑ mortality)
- **Nocturnal oxygen** — modest benefit
- **Phrenic nerve stimulation** — **remedē System** (FDA approved 2017)
- **Avoid** — CPAP and ASV may worsen CSA; treat underlying HF first
- **Theophylline** — historical; not first-line
- **Transvenous phrenic stimulation** — emerging evidence

## 7. Effects of Treating Sleep Apnea
- **OSA + HF** — CPAP improves EF (~5%), BP, QoL (CANPAP sub-analyses)
- **CSA** — treatment effect on mortality uncertain
- **Improved LVEF** in some studies
- **Reduced AF recurrence** post-cardioversion
- **Improved nocturnal oxygenation**

## 8. Screening
- **All HF patients** should be screened for sleep apnea
- **Specific groups** — resistant HTN, AF, LVH, unexplained PH
- **Symptoms + questionnaires** + overnight oximetry first

## 9. Prognosis
- **Untreated severe OSA** in HF — ↑ mortality, hospitalisation
- **CSA** — independent marker of poor prognosis
- **Treatment improves outcomes**, especially in OSA

> **Pearls**
> - **50–75% of HF patients** have sleep apnea
> - **OSA** — airway collapse; **CSA** — loss of drive (Cheyne-Stokes)
> - **Polysomnography** is the gold standard
> - **CPAP** is first-line for OSA in HF
> - **ASV is CONTRAINDICATED in HFrEF <45%** (SERVE-HF: ↑ mortality)
> - **Phrenic nerve stimulation (remedē)** — new option for CSA
> - **Treat underlying HF** — improves CSA
> - **Screen all HF patients** — Epworth, STOP-BANG, oximetry

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Sleep apnea is **highly prevalent and underdiagnosed** in HF. **ASV is contraindicated** in HFrEF <45% (SERVE-HF). **CPAP for OSA** and **GDMT optimisation** for CSA are mainstays. **Phrenic nerve stimulation** is a new option for CSA. Screen all HF patients.
</callout>
