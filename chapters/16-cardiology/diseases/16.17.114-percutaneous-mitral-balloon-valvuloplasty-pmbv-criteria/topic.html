CARDIOLOGY
Percutaneous mitral balloon valvuloplasty (PMBV) criteria
1 Background

PMBV (Inoue balloon technique) is the first-line intervention for symptomatic rheumatic MS with suitable anatomy. It splits the fused commissures, increasing MVA and relieving obstruction — without surgery or prosthesis.

2 Indications
  • Symptomatic severe MS (MVA ≤ 1.5 cm², NYHA II-IV)
  • Asymptomatic severe MS with:
  • High thromboembolic risk (prior embolism, AF with dense SEC, LA thrombus resolving on anticoagulation)
  • Haemodynamic decompensation on exercise (sPAP > 60 mmHg, MV gradient > 15 mmHg)
  • Plan for pregnancy (intervene before conception)
  • Restenosis after surgical commissurotomy (selected)
3 Contraindications
Absolute
  • LA thrombus (especially in LAA) — risk of systemic embolism
  • More than mild MR (≥moderate MR is a contraindication)
  • Severe concomitant aortic or tricuspid disease requiring surgery
  • Heavy mitral calcification with commissural calcification (Wilkins > 8)
  • Massive / extensive subvalvular disease (chordal fusion / calcification)
Relative
  • Severe pulmonary hypertension (still proceed with care; some operators avoid)
  • Pregnancy (radiation exposure — use echo guidance, abdominal shielding)
  • Unfavourable anatomy (Wilkins > 8, calcified commissures)

  • PACman (Predictors of Atrial Crest Commissural Mitral ANatomy) — emerging
5 Pre-Procedural Workup
  • TTE with detailed MV assessment, MVA, mean gradient, MR severity
  • TOE (mandatory):
  • Exclude LA / LAA thrombus (essential)
  • Confirm MR severity (≤mild)
  • Wilkins score
  • Assess commissural fusion pattern
  • Coronary angio if >40 yr or risk factors (concomitant PCI / CABG)
  • Pregnancy test in women of childbearing age (use echo / minimal fluoro)
6 The Inoue Technique
  • Balloon — stepwise inflation across the MV
  • Transseptal puncture (TOE / TTE / fluoro guided)
  • Inflation steps:
  1. Step 1 (small diameter) — check positioning
  2. Step 2 — main dilation
  3. Step 3 — final check (final MVA ≥ 1.5 cm², MR ≤ mild, LA pressure ↓)
  • Stop if: MR worsens ≥moderate, hemodynamics deteriorate, MVA acceptable reached
7 Outcomes and Success
  • Procedural success = MVA ≥ 1.5 cm², MR ≤ mild, gradient ↓ ≥ 50%
  • Echo score ≤ 8: success >90%
  • Echo score 9–11: success 70–80%
  • Echo score > 11: success <50% (consider surgery)
  • Restenosis rate: ~10–30% at 5 years, 50% at 10 years
8 Complications
ComplicationFrequency
------
**Significant MR** (≥moderate)2–10%
**Cardiac tamponade** (transseptal)1–2%
**Stroke / embolism**0.5–1%
**Severe MR requiring surgery**1–2%
**ASD at puncture site**small, often closes
**Death**< 1% (experienced centres)
9 Post-Procedural Care
  • Bed rest 6–12 hours, monitor puncture site
  • TTE next day (MVA, MR, gradient)
  • Anticoagulation:
  • AF + MS → warfarin (INR 2.0–3.0) lifelong
  • Sinus rhythm, no LA thrombus → aspirin (some centres)
  • Endocarditis prophylaxis for 6 months (some recommendations)
  • Surveillance — TTE at 6 months, 1 year, then yearly
10 Special Populations
Pregnancy
  • Severe MS + pregnancy = high risk
  • PMBV in 2nd trimester feasible, often echo-guided (no / minimal fluoro)
  • Diuretics, beta-blockers as bridge
Children / Adolescents
  • PMBV feasible in rheumatic MS (RHD endemic areas)
  • Avoid in congenital MS (different anatomy — parachute valve)
Re-do PMBV
  • Restenosis after previous PMBV → consider re-do if anatomy still suitable
  • Higher complication rate; careful selection
MS with AF
  • Always TOE to exclude thrombus
  • Warfarin (not DOAC) — INRs may need 2.5–3.5
  • Some centres require 4–6 weeks of therapeutic INR before PMBV
Pearls - PMBV is first-line for rheumatic MS with Wilkins ≤ 8, no LA thrombus, ≤mild MR - Always TOE before PMBV — exclude LAA thrombus (Class I) - LA thrombus + MR ≥moderate = absolute contraindications - Procedural success = MVA ≥ 1.5 cm², MR ≤ mild, gradient ↓ ≥ 50% - AF + MS = warfarin (INR 2.0–3.0), DOAC contraindicated in rheumatic MS
