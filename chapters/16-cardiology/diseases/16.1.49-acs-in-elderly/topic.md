# ACS in Elderly

## 1. Definition
**Elderly ACS** (≥75 y) accounts for >30% of MI. Atypical presentation, higher comorbidity, more complications, higher mortality. **Under-represented in trials** — treatment decisions often extrapolated.

## 2. Key Features
- **Atypical presentation**: dyspnoea, fatigue, syncope, confusion, falls — **less chest pain** (~40%)
- **ECG**: more nonspecific changes, baseline abnormalities
- **Biomarkers**: troponin may be chronically elevated; serial Δ for diagnosis
- **Higher risk**: more multivessel disease, prior MI, HF, CKD
- **Bleeding risk**: high; **PRECISE-DAPT** score
- **Mortality**: in-hospital 2–3× higher than younger
- **Type**: NSTEMI more common with age; STEMI under-diagnosed

## 3. Management
- **Same evidence-based therapies** — but dose-adjusted
- **Reperfusion in STEMI**: PCI preferred; if not available, consider fibrinolysis (age alone not CI)
- **Drug choice**: clopidogrel often preferred over ticagrelor (less bleeding); avoid prasugrel if age ≥75 or <60 kg
- **Anticoagulation**: UFAR or enoxaparin (adjusted for CrCl); fondaparinux in NSTEMI
- **Statin**: high-intensity if tolerated
- **Cautious**: β-blocker (start low), ACEi, contrast load
- **Invasive strategy**: benefit in NSTEMI, but balance against frailty, comorbidities

> **Pearls**
> - **Atypical presentation** common — dyspnoea, falls, confusion
> - **Clopidogrel** preferred over ticagrelor in many elderly
> - Avoid **prasugrel** if age ≥75 or weight <60 kg
> - Treat the same but with **dose adjustment** and frailty assessment
