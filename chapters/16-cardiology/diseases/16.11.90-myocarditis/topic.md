# Myocarditis

<callout icon="🔥" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Myocarditis — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Inflammatory disease of the myocardium** diagnosed by histological, immunological, and immunohistochemical criteria. Most commonly **viral**; can present as acute HF, mimic MI, cause arrhythmia, or sudden death.

## 2. Aetiology
### Infectious
- **Viral** — most common: **parvovirus B19, HHV6, coxsackie B, adenovirus, SARS-CoV-2, influenza, HIV**
- **Bacterial** — *Corynebacterium diphtheriae*, *Staphylococcus*, *Borrelia* (Lyme), *Rickettsia*
- **Fungal**, **protozoal** — *Trypanosoma cruzi* (Chagas), *Toxoplasma*

### Non-Infectious
- **Autoimmune** — SLE, sarcoidosis, giant cell myocarditis, eosinophilic (Churg-Strauss)
- **Drug / toxin** — anthracyclines, immune checkpoint inhibitors, clozapine, cocaine
- **Hypersensitivity** — antibiotics, diuretics
- **Giant cell myocarditis** — fulminant, young adults, often fatal without transplant

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- **Asymptomatic** — subclinical
- **Chest pain** (often pericarditic) — viral prodrome
- **Acute HF** — dyspnoea, orthopnoea
- **Arrhythmia** — heart block, VT, AF
- **Mimics MI** — ST elevation, troponin rise
- **Sudden cardiac death** — fulminant cases
- **Preceding viral illness** in ~60%

## 4. Diagnosis
### Investigations
- **ECG** — ST elevation (diffuse), TWI, AV block, low voltages, QRS widening
- **Troponin / CK** — elevated (often persistently)
- **NT-proBNP** — elevated
- **ESR, CRP** — raised inflammatory markers
- **Echo** — LV dysfunction (global or segmental), pericardial effusion, LV dilatation
- **CMR (gold standard)** — **Lake Louise Criteria** (2018 update)
  - **Oedema** (T2-weighted) — tissue oedema
  - **Hyperaemia / capillary leak** (early gadolinium enhancement)
  - **LGE** (non-ischaemic pattern — subepicardial / mid-wall)
  - **LV dysfunction** (cine)
  - **Definite** if ≥2 of 3 tissue-based criteria positive
- **Endomyocardial biopsy** — invasive, reserved for fulminant/unclear cases; **Dallas criteria** + immunohistochemistry (CD3, CD68)

### Differential
- **MI** — coronary territory, regional, subendocardial LGE
- **Takotsubo** — no LGE, characteristic wall motion
- **Pericarditis** — pericardial, no LV dysfunction

## 5. Treatment
### Acute
- **Supportive** — rest, avoid exercise (3–6 months)
- **GDMT** — standard HF therapy once stable
- **Arrhythmia management** — avoid ICD in acute phase (recovery possible)
- **Anticoagulation** if LV thrombus
- **Mechanical support** — IABP, Impella, VA-ECMO in cardiogenic shock
- **Heart transplant** — for refractory cases (esp. giant cell myocarditis)

### Specific
- **Giant cell / autoimmune** — high-dose steroids, **ATG**, cyclosporine, azathioprine (w/o transplant)
- **Viral** — no proven antiviral therapy; **IVIG** sometimes used
- **Avoid NSAIDs** in acute HF
- **Avoid exercise** for 3–6 months (per AHA — risk of SCD)

## 6. Complications
- **Dilated cardiomyopathy** (10–20%)
- **Recurrent myocarditis**
- **Heart failure** (chronic)
- **Sudden cardiac death**
- **Heart block** (sarcoid, Lyme)

> **Pearls**
> - **Most common cause: viral** — parvovirus B19, HHV6, coxsackie B
> - **CMR Lake Louise Criteria** — oedema + LGE (subepicardial) + dysfunction
> - **Endomyocardial biopsy** — gold standard but invasive; reserve for unclear/fulminant
> - **Avoid NSAIDs** in acute HF
> - **No exercise for 3–6 months** — risk of SCD
> - **Giant cell** — fulminant, autoimmune, young — high-dose steroids ± transplant
> - **Don't implant ICD early** — recovery possible

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Myocarditis is a great mimicker — can present as MI, HF, or arrhythmia. **CMR** differentiates from MI (subepicardial LGE) and Takotsubo (no LGE). **Avoid exercise** — risk of SCD. **Biopsy** for fulminant or unclear cases; giant cell variant responds to immunosuppression.
</callout>
