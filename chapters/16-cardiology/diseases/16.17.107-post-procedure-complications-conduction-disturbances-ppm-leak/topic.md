# Post-Procedure Complications of TAVI / SAVR

## 1. Why It Matters
Modern AVR is safe but not complication-free. Anticipating and managing post-procedure complications is essential — particularly **conduction disturbances** (the most common after TAVI) and **paravalvular leak** (which has prognostic implications).

## 2. Conduction Disturbances

| Complication | Frequency | Mechanism | Management |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LBBB (new)** | 10–25% (TAVI) | Compression of LBB by prosthesis / calcium | Monitor ≥3 days; consider PPM if EF ↓ or syncope |
| **High-degree AV block** | 5–15% (TAVI), 1–3% (SAVR) | Direct trauma to conduction tissue | **Permanent pacemaker** |
| **RBBB (new)** | Less common, ominous | More often pre-existing | Monitor |

### Indications for Permanent Pacemaker (PPM) After TAVI
- Persistent high-degree AV block
- New LBBB + QRS >150 ms + PR >240 ms + **ambulatory monitoring** showing 2nd/3rd degree block
- Pre-existing RBBB + new LBBB = **high risk** of progression → PPM frequently needed
- **Prophylactic PPM** controversial; many centres use **ambulatory ECG** for 7–14 days

> **Deep implantation** and **self-expanding valves** (CoreValve, Evolut) carry highest conduction risk.

## 3. Paravalvular Leak (PVL) / Aortic Regurgitation
- **TAVI:** 5–15% (mild); 1–3% (moderate/severe) — more common with early devices
- **SAVR:** <2% (mild+)
- **Predictors:** annular undersizing, heavy LVOT calcification, bicuspid anatomy, malposition
- **Assessment:** TTE (pressure half-time, circumferential extent), TEE if needed, **cardiac MRI** for quantification

### Management
- **Mild:** observe
- **Moderate/severe:** redilation (valvuloplasty balloon), **plug closure** (Amplatzer), or **valve-in-valve** TAVI
- Severe PVL → increased mortality → re-intervention indicated

## 4. Vascular Complications (TAVI)
- **Major:** dissection, perforation, retroperitoneal bleed, access-site surgery (2–5%)
- **Predictors:** small iliofemoral arteries, severe calcification, female sex
- **Prevention:** CT pre-procedure for sizing; ultrasound-guided access; closure device (ProGlide, MANTA)
- **Management:** covered stent, surgical repair, reversal of anticoagulation, transfusion

## 5. Bleeding
- **TAVI:** less bleeding than SAVR (no sternotomy)
- **SAVR:** chest tube drainage, mediastinal re-exploration (~2%)
- **Anticoagulation management:** resume aspirin day 1; warfarin per indication; avoid DOAC

## 6. Stroke / Neurological Injury
- **Periprocedural stroke:** 1–3% (TAVI and SAVR)
- **Mechanism:** embolic debris from valve / aorta
- **Cerebral embolic protection** (Sentinel device) — PROTECTED TAVR showed stroke reduction
- **Silent cerebral infarcts** on MRI: ~70% (TAVI) — uncertain clinical significance

## 7. Acute Kidney Injury
- 3–5% (TAVI), up to 25% (SAVR with CPB)
- Risk: contrast (TAVI), hypoperfusion, cholesterol emboli
- **Hydration** pre- and post-procedure; minimise contrast; hold nephrotoxins
- Pre-TAVI eGFR <30 = higher mortality; consider dialysis access planning

## 8. Coronary Obstruction (TAVI)
- Rare (<1%) but devastating (50% mortality)
- **Risk factors:** low coronary ostium (<10 mm), narrow sinus, prior bioprosthetic valve (valve-in-valve)
- **Prevention:** pre-TAVI CT; BASILICA procedure (bioprosthetic scallop intentional laceration)
- **Treatment:** emergency PCI, snorkel stenting, immediate SAVR

## 9. Annular Rupture (TAVI)
- Rare (<1%) — catastrophic (cardiac tamponade)
- **Risk:** heavy annular / LVOT calcification, oversized valve, balloon post-dilatation
- **Treatment:** pericardiocentesis, surgical repair (often fatal)

## 10. Other Complications
- **Atrial fibrillation:** 30–60% post-SAVR, 5–10% post-TAVI
- **Pneumothorax, pleural effusion, phrenic nerve injury**
- **Pannus / thrombosis** (mechanical valves)
- **Endocarditis** (0.5–2% per year, lifetime risk)
- **Structural valve deterioration** (bioprosthetic, 8–15 yr typical)

## 11. Post-Discharge Surveillance
- **TTE at 30 days, 6 months, then yearly** (bioprosthetic) or per protocol
- **Anticoagulation clinic** for mechanical valves
- **Endocarditis prophylaxis** if prosthetic valve (Class I, dental procedures)

> **Pearls**
> - **New LBBB** after TAVI → monitor ≥3 days; PPM if persistent high-grade block
> - **Paravalvular leak** >moderate post-TAVI → re-intervention (plug, valve-in-valve)
> - **Cerebral embolic protection** reduces stroke risk — increasingly routine
> - **Annular rupture** = catastrophic, beware heavy annular calcification
> - **Annual TTE follow-up** for life; bioprosthetic SVD typically 8–15 yr
