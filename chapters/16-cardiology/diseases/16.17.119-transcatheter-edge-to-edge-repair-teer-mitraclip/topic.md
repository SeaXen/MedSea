# Transcatheter Edge-to-Edge Repair (TEER / MitraClip)

## 1. Definition
**TEER** = percutaneous **transcatheter edge-to-edge mitral valve repair** using a clip device (MitraClip, PASCAL) to approximate the free edges of the MV leaflets, reducing the regurgitant orifice. Used predominantly for **secondary (functional) MR**, also high-risk primary MR.

## 2. Key Features
- **Indications**:
  - **Severe secondary (functional) MR** in symptomatic HF patients on GDMT
  - **COAPT criteria**: LVEF 20–50%, LVESD <70 mm, MR grade 3+/4+, PASP <70 mmHg, NYHA II–IV, no severe RV dysfunction
  - **High-risk primary MR** in non-surgical candidates
- **Anatomy**: leaflet anatomy must be suitable — central A2-P2, sufficient leaflet tissue, no severe calcification
- **Procedure**: transseptal, trans-THV or trans-jugular approach; clip deployed under TOE/fluoro guidance
- **Outcomes**: COAPT showed **mortality benefit** and ↓ HF hospitalisation; **MITRA-FR** (negative) — patient selection critical
- **Complications**: leaflet tear, partial clip detachment, residual MR, ASD from transseptal, stroke

## 3. Management
- **Heart team** decision (cardiologist, surgeon, imaging expert)
- **Pre-procedure TEE** to assess anatomy, severity
- **GDMT must be optimised** first (≥3 months); only proceed if symptoms persist
- **Post-procedure**: antiplatelet (aspirin ± clopidogrel 1 month); lifelong GDMT
- **Follow-up**: clinical, TTE at 1, 6, 12 months then annual
- **Re-intervention**: redo TEER or surgery
- **Emerging**: tricuspid TEER, annuloplasty devices

> **Pearls**
> - **COAPT, not MITRA-FR** — patient selection is everything
> - **LVEF 20–50%, LVESD <70 mm** = ideal candidate
> - **GDMT first** (≥3 months) before TEER
> - **Heart team** decision mandatory
