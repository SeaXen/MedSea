# Lung Transplant Referral in PAH

## 1. Indications for Referral
- Inadequate response to **maximal medical therapy** (continued intermediate/high-risk profile)
- Progressive RV failure despite parenteral prostacyclin
- Hospitalisation for **right heart failure** or haemoptysis
- Severe PAH (CI <2.0 L/min/m², RAP >15 mmHg, SvO₂ <60%)
- Need for IV inotrope support

## 2. Transplant Options
- **Bilateral lung transplant:** Preferred in PAH (preserves cardiac function)
- **Heart-lung transplant:** Reserved for Eisenmenger physiology with irreversible cardiac disease or complex CHD
- **Single lung:** Rarely used in PAH (high V/Q mismatch, native lung complications)

## 3. Timing
- **Early referral is key** — wait-list mortality is high in PAH
- ESC/ERS: Refer when **intermediate-high risk** despite optimal therapy
- ISHLT criteria: WHO FC III/IV, 6MWD <332 m, RAP >15 mmHg, CI <2.0 L/min/m²

## 4. Contraindications
- Active infection, malignancy (within 2–5 years)
- Severe obesity (BMI >35)
- Active substance use, non-adherence
- Significant psychosocial barriers
- Severe extra-pulmonary disease

## 5. Bridging Strategies
- **IV inotrope** (dobutamine, milrinone)
- **Mechanical support:** VA-ECMO or VV-ECMO (bridge to transplant) — selected centres
- **Pulmonary artery to left atrium shunt** (Potts shunt, palliative)
- **Atrial septostomy** (palliative, creates right-to-left shunt to decompress RV)

## 6. Outcomes
- 1-year survival post-lung transplant: ~80–85%
- 5-year survival: ~50–60%
- Significant morbidity from BOS (bronchiolitis obliterans)

> **Pearls**
> - **Bilateral** lung transplant is the standard for PAH.
> - Refer **early** — PAH has the highest wait-list mortality of any transplant indication.
> - VA-ECMO bridge is feasible in selected centres.
