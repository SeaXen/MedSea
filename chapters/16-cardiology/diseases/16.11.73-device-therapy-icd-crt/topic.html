CARDIOLOGY
Device therapy (ICD, CRT)
1 ICD (Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator)

Primary prevention of sudden cardiac death (SCD) in ischaemic and non-ischaemic cardiomyopathy.

Primary Prevention Criteria (ESC 2021 / ACC/AHA 2022)
  • LVEF ≤35% despite ≥3 months of optimal GDMT
  • NYHA II–III (or NYHA I with ischaemic aetiology)
  • Expected survival >1 year with good functional status
  • ≥40 days post-MI, ≥3 months post-revascularisation
  • Landmark trials: MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT
Secondary Prevention
  • Survivors of VF or sustained VT with haemodynamic compromise
  • Trials: AVID, CIDS, CASH
ICD does NOT improve EF or HF symptoms — it only aborts lethal arrhythmias.
2 CRT (Cardiac Resynchronisation Therapy)

Biventricular pacing to restore ventricular synchrony in patients with dyssynchronous LV contraction (broad LBBB). Improves symptoms, EF, and survival.

Indications (ESC 2021 — Class I)

**LBBB****≥150 ms**≤35%II–IV (ambulatory)
**LBBB**130–149 ms≤35%II–IV (ambulatory)
**Non-LBBB**≥150 ms≤35%II–IV (Class IIa)
CRT-D = CRT + ICD (preferred if ICD also indicated). CRT-P = CRT alone (frail, advanced age, comorbidities).
Landmark Trials
  • COMPANION, CARE-HF, MADIT-CRT, REVERSE — mortality/morbidity benefit
  • Response: ~70% improve (NYHA, EF ↑, LVESV ↓); reverse remodelling
  • Non-response — extensive scar, non-LBBB, sub-optimal lead position, sub-optimal GDMT
3 When to Choose CRT-D vs ICD Alone
  • CRT-D if QRS ≥150 ms, LBBB morphology, AND LVEF ≤35% AND NYHA II–III AND ICD also indicated
  • ICD alone if narrow QRS or no LBBB morphology
  • CRT-P if advanced age, frailty, severe comorbidity (e.g. CKD on dialysis) where ICD benefit is questionable
4 Practical Issues
  • Pneumothorax, lead displacement, infection, haematoma (~1–2%)
  • Battery life 6–10 years; generator change at end of service
  • Driving restrictions (1 month post-implant for primary, 6 months for secondary prevention — DVLA/MVC rules)
  • MRI-conditional devices — most modern devices are
  • Remote monitoring for arrhythmia, lead parameters, battery
5 ECG After Device
  • LBBB pattern after RV pacing (avoid — use CRT if pacing-dependent)
  • Paced rhythm — wide QRS with pacing spike
  • Failure to capture — visible spike but no QRS — check lead, K⁺, threshold
  • Failure to sense — inappropriate shocks / no shock in VT
Pearls - ICD prevents SCD; CRT improves pump function — different roles - LBBB ≥150 ms + LVEF ≤35% + NYHA II–IV = strongest CRT indication - Always optimise GDMT first and re-assess EF at 3 months before implanting ICD - QRS <120 ms → no benefit from CRT (ECHO-CRT trial) - Right ventricular pacing (e.g. AV block) can worsen HF — upgrade to CRT if EF drops - LBBB only morphology — non-LBBB (RBBB, IVCD) responds poorly

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg"> CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE — ICD reduces arrhythmic death but not HF death. CRT reduces both HF hospitalisation and all-cause mortality in LBBB ≥150 ms. Device therapy complements GDMT — it does not replace it. Always optimise medical therapy first and confirm LVEF after 3 months. </callout>
