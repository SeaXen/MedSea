## 1. Introduction
The IPPA framework (**I**nspection, **P**alpation, **P**ercussion, **A**uscultation) remains the bedside standard. PACES expects a **systematic, fluent exam in 5–8 minutes** with the patient sitting at 45°.

## 2. Inspection
- **General:** distress, dyspnoea at rest, body habitus, BMI
- **Hands:** clubbing (congenital, IE), cyanosis, splinter haemorrhages, Janeway lesions, Osler nodes, tar staining
- **Face:** malar flush (MS), central cyanosis, xanthelasma, corneal arcus
- **Neck:** JVP, carotid pulsation, goitre
- **Chest wall:** scars (midline sternotomy, lateral thoracotomy, pacemaker/ICD pocket, CABG vein harvest leg), pectus excavatum / carinatum
- **Abdomen:** hepatomegaly, ascites, caput medusae
- **Legs:** pitting oedema, varicose veins, ulceration, peripheral cyanosis

## 3. Palpation
- **Apex beat:** 5th ICS mid-clavicular line; **displaced** (LV dilatation), **tapping** (MS), **double** (HCM), **sustained/heaving** (LV pressure overload — AS, HTN)
- **Right ventricular heave** (parasternal, left lower sternal edge) — RV pressure overload (PH, PS)
- **Thrills** (palpable murmurs) — aortic area, pulmonary area, apex, left sternal edge
- **Left parasternal lift** — severe MR, LV aneurysm
- **Pulses:** radial, brachial, carotid, femoral, popliteal, posterior tibial, dorsalis pedis
- **Peripheral oedema:** pitting, level (ankle → shin → thigh → sacrum)
- **Hepatomegaly, splenomegaly**
- **Temperature of peripheries** (cold = low output)

## 4. Auscultation

### Heart Sounds
- **S1** — mitral + tricuspid closure; loud in MS; soft in MR, severe AS, LBBB
- **S2** — A2 + P2; loud P2 in PH; soft A2 in severe AS; **fixed splitting** in ASD; **paradoxical splitting** in LBBB, severe AS, HOCM
- **S3** (after S2) — rapid ventricular filling; **normal <30 y**; HFrEF, MR, AR
- **S4** (before S1) — atrial kick into stiff ventricle; LVH, HCM, ischaemia, AS
- **Pericardial knock** — constrictive pericarditis
- **Tumour plop** — atrial myxoma
- **Prosthetic sounds** — mechanical clicks

### Murmurs
| Lesion | Site | Radiation | Character | Maneouvre |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **AS** | Right 2nd ICS | Carotids | Ejection systolic | Louder expiration |
| **AR** | Left sternal edge | Apex | Early diastolic, decrescendo | Leaning forward, expiration |
| **MS** | Apex | Axilla | Diastolic rumble + opening snap | Left lateral, expiration |
| **MR** | Apex | Axilla | Pansystolic | Louder expiration |
| **PS** | Left 2nd ICS | — | Ejection systolic | Louder inspiration |
| **PR** | Left sternal edge | — | Early diastolic | Louder inspiration |
| **TS** | Left lower sternal edge | — | Pansystolic | Louder inspiration |
| **TR** | Left lower sternal edge | — | Pansystolic | Louder inspiration |
| **VSD** | Left sternal edge | — | Pansystolic, harsh | — |
| **HOCM** | Left sternal edge | — | Systolic, ↑ with Valsalva/standing | ↓ with squatting |
| **PDA** | Left infraclavicular | Back | Continuous, machinery | — |

### Dynamic Maneouvres
- **Inspiration** → ↑right-sided murmurs (Carvallo's sign)
- **Handgrip** → ↑MR, AR, VSD; ↓HOCM, AS
- **Valsalva (strain)** → ↓preload → ↑HOCM, ↓AS, ↓MS
- **Standing → squatting** → ↑preload/afterload → ↓HOCM, ↑AS, ↑MR

> **Pearls**
> - **Radio-femoral delay** → coarctation of aorta.
> - **Slow-rising pulse** → severe AS; **bisferiens** → HOCM; **pulsus paradoxus** → tamponade; **pulsus alternans** → severe LV dysfunction.
> - **Dynamic manoeuvres** can resolve 80% of murmur aetiologies at the bedside.
