# Diabetes and CV Risk (SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA)

## 1. CV Risk in DM
- DM is a **CAD equivalent** — 2-4× higher CV mortality
- High prevalence of HF (HFpEF > HFrEF), AF, CKD

## 2. SGLT2 Inhibitors (-flozin)
- **Mechanism**: block proximal tubule glucose reabsorption → natriuresis, glucosuria
- **CV benefits**: ↓ HHF, ↓ MACE, ↓ CV death, ↓ CKD progression (EMPA-REG, DAPA-HF, EMPEROR, DAPA-CKD)
- **Indication**: T2DM + ASCVD, HFrEF, CKD
- **Side effects**: euglycemic DKA, genital infections, volume depletion

## 3. GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (-tide)
- **Mechanism**: incretin mimetic — ↑insulin, ↓glucagon, ↓ appetite, ↓ weight
- **CV benefits**: ↓ MACE, ↓ stroke, ↓ CV death (LEADER, REWIND, SUSTAIN-6)
- **Indication**: T2DM + ASCVD; obesity
- **Side effects**: nausea, pancreatitis (rare), medullary thyroid C-cell caution

## 4. Pearls
> **Pearls**: **SGLT2i = HFrEF/cardioprotective** regardless of DM. **GLP-1 RA = ASCVD/obesity**. Combine for additive benefit. Also consider **finerenone** (non-steroidal MRA) in DM-CKD (FIDELIO).
