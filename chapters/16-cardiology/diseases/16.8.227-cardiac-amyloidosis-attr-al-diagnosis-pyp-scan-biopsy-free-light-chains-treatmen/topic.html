CARDIOLOGY
Cardiac Amyloidosis (ATTR, AL) - diagnosis (PYP scan, biopsy, free light chains), treatment (tafamidis, chemotherapy)
1 Definition

Cardiac amyloidosis is infiltration of myocardium by misfolded protein fibrils causing restrictive cardiomyopathy. Two main types involve the heart:

  • AL amyloidosis — light chains (plasma cell dyscrasia); rapid
  • ATTR amyloidosis — transthyretin (TTR); wild-type (ATTRwt, "senile") or mutant (ATTRm, hereditary)
2 Red Flag Clues
  • HFpEF with thick LV walls (>12 mm) but low voltage on ECG
  • Apical sparing on echo strain (longitudinal strain base < apex)
  • Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome (especially bilateral or recurrent)
  • Spinal stenosis (lumbar)
  • Biceps tendon rupture + thick heart
  • Peripheral neuropathy (small fibre)
  • AF + low voltage + thick walls
  • Family history of neuropathy / CM (ATTRm)
3 ECG
  • Low QRS voltage (<5 mm in limb leads) despite thick walls (key clue)
  • Pseudo-infarct pattern (anterior Q waves)
  • AV block (especially AL)
  • AF in 30–50% (especially ATTRwt)
4 Echo
  • Concentric LVH with normal/small cavity
  • Speckled/granular myocardium
  • Thickened valves, IAS, RV free wall
  • Apical sparing on speckle-tracking (2–3 segment ratio >1.0 is sensitive)
  • Diastolic dysfunction ≥ grade 2; small pericardial effusion
  • Strain pattern: basal ↓, apex preserved ("cherry on top")
5 Cardiac MRI
  • Diffuse subendocardial or transmural LGE
  • Native T1 ↑↑ (not nulled easily with TI scout)
  • ECV >0.40 is highly suggestive
6 Diagnostic Pathway
  1. Suspect → ECG + echo + clinical clues
  2. Rule out AL first — serum free light chains (FLC) + SPEP + UPEP + immunofixation
  3. Bone scintigraphy (PYP, DPD, HMDP) — Perugini grade 0–3
  • Grade 2–3 + absent monoclonal protein = ATTR-CM (no biopsy needed)
  • Grade 0–1 → endomyocardial biopsy to rule out AL or ATTR
  1. Biopsy with mass spec typing — gold standard if doubt
  2. Genetic testing for TTR variant (ATTRm vs ATTRwt)
7 Treatment — AL Amyloidosis
  • Chemotherapy (proteasome inhibitors ± alkylators ± daratumumab)
  • Stem cell transplant in eligible
  • Standard HF therapy (cautious: avoid CCB, digoxin — bind amyloid → toxicity)
  • Avoid β-blockers in low-output state
8 Treatment — ATTR Amyloidosis
  • Tafamidis 80 mg (or 61 mg free base) — TTR stabiliser; ATTR-ACT trial: ↓ mortality and hospitalisation
  • Acoramidis (BridgeBio) — new stabiliser, positive phase 3 (ATTRIBUTE-CM)
  • Patisiran / vutrisiran — RNA silencer for ATTRm with polyneuropathy (APOLLO)
  • Diflunisal — NSAID, off-label TTR stabiliser (renal/GI toxicity)
  • Supportive: diuretics, anticoagulation in AF
9 Monitoring
  • 6-min walk, NT-proBNP, troponin, eGFR
  • Echo strain and ECV at 6–12 months
Pearls - Red flags: thick walls + low voltage + apical sparing + carpal tunnel. - Always rule out AL first with FLC + SPEP + UPEP. - Bone scan Perugini ≥2 + no monoclonal protein = ATTR-CM (no biopsy needed). - Tafamidis is the first disease-modifying drug for ATTR-CM. - Avoid CCB and digoxin in amyloidosis — bind amyloid fibrils.
