# Constrictive Pericarditis — Hemodynamics

## 1. Definition
**Constrictive pericarditis (CP)** = chronic pericardial inflammation and fibrosis → rigid, non-compliant pericardium that impedes ventricular filling. Causes: **TB, post-cardiac surgery, radiation, chronic idiopathic, CKD, malignancy**.

## 2. Key Features
- **Hemodynamic hallmarks**:
  - **Equalisation of diastolic pressures**: RA, RV diastolic, PA diastolic, PCWP — all within 5 mmHg
  - **Dip-and-plateau / "square root" sign** on ventricular pressure tracing: rapid early filling then abrupt stop
  - **Ventricular interdependence**: pulsus paradoxus (RV vs LV); discordant respiratory changes (RV ↑ with inspiration, LV ↓; on echo, septal bounce)
  - **Kussmaul's sign**: ↑ or no ↓ in JVP with inspiration (loss of normal inspiratory fall)
- **Echo**:
  - Septal "bounce"/shimmer with respiration
  - Pericardial thickening (>3 mm), calcification
  - **Preserved** tissue Doppler mitral annular velocity (e' ≥7 cm/s)
  - **Hepatic vein** expiratory diastolic flow reversal
- **CT/MR**: pericardial thickening ≥3–4 mm
- **BNP**: typically **normal** (vs HFpEF, restrictive)
- **Differentiation** from restrictive CM: e' normal, septal bounce, Kussmaul's, equalised pressures
- **Catheterisation**: gold standard — confirms haemodynamics

## 3. Management
- **Treat underlying cause** (TB, autoimmune)
- **Diuretics** for volume overload (careful — over-diuresis worsens)
- **Pericardiectomy** = only definitive treatment (high-risk; strip visceral + parietal pericardium)
- **Surgical mortality** 6–12%
- **Avoid** in acute pericarditis; not yet constrictive physiology
- **Transient CP** (eg, after acute pericarditis) may resolve with medical therapy

> **Pearls**
> - **Dip-plateau / square root sign** on cath
> - **e' preserved on tissue Doppler** (vs restrictive)
> - **Kussmaul's sign** = JVP doesn't fall with inspiration
> - **Pericardiectomy** is definitive — high-risk surgery
