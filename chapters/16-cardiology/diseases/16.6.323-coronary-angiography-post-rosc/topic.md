# Coronary angiography post-ROSC

## 1. Rationale
Up to **60–80% of cardiac arrests** have an underlying coronary lesion, and acute coronary occlusion is the most common cause. Early coronary angiography (with PCI if needed) is recommended in selected post-ROSC patients to identify and treat the culprit lesion.

## 2. Indication
- **ST elevation on post-ROSC ECG** → **emergent coronary angiography** (Class I)
- **Cardiac arrest with shockable rhythm** (VF/pVT) + high suspicion of CAD → **emergent angiography** reasonable (Class IIa)
- **Hemodynamic/electrical instability** (recurrent VT, shock) → urgent angiography

## 3. COACT, TOMAHAWK, EMERGE Trials
- **COACT (2019)**: No benefit of immediate angiography in OHCA without STEMI
- **TOMAHAWK (2022)**: Immediate angiography associated with **higher mortality** in stable patients
- **ARREST (2022)**: Early angiography beneficial in STEMI-equivalent arrest

**Current consensus**: 
- **STEMI post-ROSC** → immediate coronary angiography
- **No STEMI + hemodynamically stable** → individualized; can be delayed

## 4. Selection for Angiography
**Favor early angiography**:
- ST elevation on ECG
- Hemodynamic instability (shock)
- Recurrent VT/VF
- History of CAD/prior PCI
- Chest pain preceding arrest
- Older age with risk factors

**Defer angiography**:
- Definite non-cardiac cause (PE, drowning, asphyxia)
- Severe neurologic injury (poor prognosis)
- Comorbidities, DNAR
- Late presentation, prolonged resuscitation

## 5. Procedure
- **Radial** or femoral access
- **Therapeutic anticoagulation** (UFH or bivalirudin)
- **Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor** if PCI planned
- **Stent selection**: Drug-eluting preferred
- **Hemodynamic support**: IABP, Impella, VA-ECMO as needed

## 6. Special Considerations
- **Antiplatelet therapy** risk: TTM, possible bleeding
- **Contrast nephropathy**: Hydration, minimize contrast
- **Neurologic status**: Unconscious — no symptomatic guide
- **Multidisciplinary decision** (cardiology, ICU, neurology)

> **Pearls**
> - **STEMI post-ROSC** → always go to the cath lab (Class I)
> - **Shockable rhythm + no STEMI**: individualized decision
> - **Asystole/PEA arrest** without STEMI: low yield of culprit lesion
> - Even in shockable arrest, only ~30% have acute coronary occlusion
> - **TTM is not a contraindication** to angiography
