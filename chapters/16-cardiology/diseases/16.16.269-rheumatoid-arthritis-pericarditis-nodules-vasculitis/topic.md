# Rheumatoid Arthritis and the Heart

## 1. Definition
- Chronic autoimmune inflammatory arthritis (RF+, anti-CCP)
- ↑ CV risk — 1.5-2× for MI, stroke; CV is #1 cause of death

## 2. Cardiac Manifestations
- **Pericarditis**: most common cardiac manifestation; often subclinical
- **Myocarditis**: rare but serious
- **Valvular nodules**: granulomatous, rarely symptomatic
- **Vasculitis**: rheumatoid vasculitis — can involve coronaries, kidneys
- **Amyloidosis (AA)**: chronic inflammation
- **CAD**: accelerated, often silent ischemia

## 3. Management
- Tight control of **inflammation** (DMARDs) reduces CV risk
- **Methotrexate** is CV-protective (vs. sulfasalazine)
- **TNF inhibitors** reduce MI risk
- **JAK inhibitors** (tofacitinib) → ↑ CV events, **avoid** in high CV risk (FDA black box)
- Standard CV risk factor modification — **statin** in most
- NSAIDs minimize (HTN, CKD, ↑ events)

## 4. Pearls
> **Pearls**: Treat RA **aggressively** to ↓ CV events. ASCVD calculator **underestimates** RA risk — multiply by 1.5. Reassess risk at age 40+.
