# AVNRT & AVRT (WPW) Ablation


Catheter ablation is the **first-line curative therapy** for most AV nodal-dependent SVTs.

## 1. Definition & Background

- **AVNRT (AV nodal re-entrant tachycardia)**: re-entrant circuit within / around AV node — slow + fast pathways. Most common SVT (~60% of paroxysmal SVT).
- **AVRT (AV re-entrant tachycardia)**: re-entrant circuit uses AV node + accessory pathway. WPW = accessory pathway with **antegrade conduction** (short PR, delta wave).
- **Ablation** uses RF (heating), cryo (freezing), or pulsed field (PFA) energy to destroy the arrhythmogenic tissue.

## 2. Indications for Ablation

- **Symptomatic** AVNRT/AVRT refractory to or intolerant of drug therapy (or patient preference for cure).
- **Recurrent** SVT impacting QoL, occupation (pilots, drivers), pregnancy planning.
- **WPW with high-risk features**: shortest pre-excited RR interval (SPERRI) <250 ms, or accessory pathway ERP <250 ms, syncope, atrial fibrillation with rapid pre-excited conduction.
- **Documented tachycardia** — provide 12-lead of SVT or documentation on device/loop recorder.
- **Pregnancy**: consider ablation with minimal fluoroscopy / zero-fluoro (cryo or RF) in refractory cases.

## 3. Pre-Ablation Workup

- **12-lead ECG** in sinus rhythm (delta wave, short PR) and **during tachycardia** (RP interval, P-wave morphology).
- **Holter / event monitor** to document arrhythmia.
- **EP study** is essentially the procedure — diagnostic + therapeutic in one.
- **Echocardiogram** — exclude structural heart disease (especially in WPW — Ebstein anomaly association).
- **Coagulation, pregnancy test**, consent (risk of AV block, pacemaker).

## 4. AVNRT Ablation

- **Target**: **slow pathway** — inferior septum between CS ostium and tricuspid annulus (Triangle of Koch).
- **Endpoint**: non-inducibility of AVNRT with/without isoprenaline; residual slow-pathway echoes acceptable.
- **Energy**: **RF** standard; **cryo** preferred near compact AV node (less AV block risk) — especially in children, athletes.
- **Acute success**: >95%; **recurrence**: ~3–5%.
- **Risk of complete AV block**: <1% (RF); <0.5% (cryo).

## 5. AVRT / WPW Ablation

- **Target**: accessory pathway insertion site — mapped by earliest atrial or ventricular activation / pathway potential.
- **Sites**: left lateral (most common), posteroseptal, right lateral, anteroseptal (near His — riskier), midseptal.
- **Approach**: trans-septal (or retrograde aortic) for left-sided; inferior approach for posteroseptal.
- **Energy**: **RF** standard; cryo for septal/parahissian.
- **Acute success**: ~95% overall, lower for septal/anteroseptal pathways.
- **Complications**: AV block (septal), cardiac tamponade (<1%), coronary artery injury (rare), aortic root damage (retrograde).

- **AVNRT**: non-inducibility; single AV-nodal echo acceptable; no PR prolongation.
- **AVRT/WPW**: loss of pre-excitation (delta wave absent), non-inducibility of AVRT/AF conduction over AP.
