# Hemodynamic Assessment of Aortic Stenosis

## 1. The "4-40-1" Rule
Severe AS is defined by echocardiographic haemodynamics:

| Parameter | Severe Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Peak velocity (Vmax)** | **> 4.0 m/s** | CW Doppler through valve |
| **Mean transvalvular gradient** | **> 40 mmHg** | Time-velocity integral averaged |
| **Aortic valve area (AVA)** | **< 1.0 cm²** | Continuity equation |
| **Indexed AVA** | < 0.6 cm²/m² | For small body size |
| **Doppler velocity index (DVI)** | < 0.25 | LVOT VTI / AV VTI |

## 2. Continuity Equation (AVA)
**AVA = (LVOT area × LVOT VTI) / AV VTI**
- LVOT area = π × (LVOT diameter/2)² — measure in mid-systole, parasternal long axis
- LVOT diameter is the **main source of error** (squared in equation)
- **Planimetry** by TEE is an alternative (esp. for rheumatic)

## 3. Pressure Gradients
- **Peak gradient** = 4 × (Vmax)² (simplified Bernoulli)
- **Mean gradient** = average of instantaneous gradients; preferred clinically
- Gradient is **flow-dependent** — low-flow states reduce gradient despite severe AS

## 4. Classification of Severity

| Category | Vmax (m/s) | Mean grad (mmHg) | AVA (cm²) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mild** | 2.6–2.9 | <20 | >1.5 |
| **Moderate** | 3.0–3.9 | 20–39 | 1.0–1.5 |
| **Severe** | ≥4.0 | ≥40 | <1.0 |
| **Very severe** | ≥5.0 | ≥60 | <0.6 (or Vmax >5.5) |

## 5. Low-Flow, Low-Gradient AS
The classic clinical conundrum: severe AS on area criteria but low gradient because of low flow.

**Four scenarios:**
| Subtype | LVEF | Flow (SVi) | Gradient | Confirm with |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Classical LF-LG** | <50% | <35 mL/m² | <40 | Dobutamine stress echo |
| **Paradoxical LF-LG** | ≥50% | <35 mL/m² | <40 | CT-AVC >1200 AU (♀) / >2000 AU (♂) |
| **Normal-flow, low-gradient** | ≥50% | ≥35 mL/m² | <40 | Recheck; possible measurement error |
| **Low-flow, low-gradient with reduced EF** | <50% | <35 mL/m² | <40 | Dobutamine — increase SV → gradient ↑ if true severe |

## 6. Dobutamine Stress Echocardiography
- **Indications:** LVEF <50% + AVA <1.0 + mean grad <40 (suspected pseudo-severe)
- **True severe:** AVA <1.0 cm², gradient ↑ to >40, no change in AVA with ↑flow
- **Pseudo-severe:** AVA >1.0 cm², gradient ↑, EF improves — is actually cardiomyopathy
- **No contractile reserve** (ΔSV <20%) = high surgical risk, consider TAVI/Bridge

## 7. CT Aortic Valve Calcium Scoring (CT-AVC)
- Used in **paradoxical low-flow low-gradient AS** to confirm severity
- Severe: women ≥1200 AU, men ≥2000 AU (Agatston)
- **CT is the gold standard** when echo is discordant

## 8. Other Modalities
- **Cardiac MRI:** planimetry, regurgitant fraction
- **Invasive cath:** Gorlin formula — reserved for discordant echo
- **Exercise testing:** asymptomatic severe AS — symptom development / BP drop = indication for intervention

> **Pearls**
> - Remember the **"4-40-1"** rule for severe AS
> - AVA is **flow-independent**; gradient is **flow-dependent** → area more reliable
> - Always consider **low-flow, low-gradient** AS when AVA <1 but gradient <40
> - **Dobutamine stress echo** distinguishes true severe from pseudo-severe in low EF
> - **CT-AVC** solves the paradoxical low-flow, low-gradient puzzle
