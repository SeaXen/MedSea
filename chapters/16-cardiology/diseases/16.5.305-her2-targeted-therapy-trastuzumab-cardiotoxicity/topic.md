# Trastuzumab (HER2-targeted) Cardiotoxicity

## 1. Mechanism
- **HER2/ErbB2** signaling important for cardiomyocyte survival
- **NOT dose-dependent** (vs anthracyclines)
- Usually **reversible** with HF therapy
- **Synergistic** cardiotoxicity with anthracyclines

## 2. Incidence
- **Monotherapy**: 2–7% LV dysfunction, 0.5–2% severe HF
- **With anthracycline**: up to 27% LV dysfunction
- Trastuzumab + pertuzumab + chemo: ~2% severe HF

## 3. Risk Factors
- **Prior anthracycline** (most important)
- Age >65
- Pre-existing cardiac disease
- HTN, DM
- BMI >30
- **Sequential vs concurrent** with anthracycline

## 4. Monitoring
- **Baseline echo + GLS**
- Every 3 months during therapy
- Troponin, BNP (adjuncts)

## 5. Management
- **Hold trastuzumab** if LVEF drops ≥10% to <50%
- Start **ACEi + β-blocker** (usually reversible)
- **Re-challenge** after recovery is often possible
- **Permanently discontinue** if severe or recurrent

> **Pearls** — **Reversible** (vs anthracycline). **Hold if LVEF drops 10% to <50%**. Start **ACEi + β-blocker**. Re-challenge often possible. **Prior anthracycline** = biggest risk.
