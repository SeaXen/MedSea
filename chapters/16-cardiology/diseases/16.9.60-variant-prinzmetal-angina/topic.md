## 1. Definition
**Epicardial coronary vasospasm** → transient transmural ischaemia at rest, often with **ST elevation**. Normal / minimally diseased coronaries at baseline angiography.

### Triggers
- **Circadian pattern** (late night / early morning)
- **Cocaine, amphetamines, triptans, ergot alkaloids**
- **Smoking, alcohol**
- Hyperventilation, cold pressor test
- 5-FU, capecitabine, bleomycin

---

## 2. Clinical Features
- Chest pain at **rest**, often nocturnal
- Transient ST elevation during pain (returns to baseline)
- Can be associated with **malignant arrhythmias** (VT, VF, AV block) → SCD
- Young to middle-aged, often smokers

---

## 3. Diagnosis
- **Ambulatory ECG** for ST shifts
- **Provocation test** during angiography — acetylcholine / ergonovine (focal or diffuse >90% constriction with symptoms + ECG changes)
- Calcium channel blocker challenge reverses

---

## 4. Treatment
- **CCB first line** (amlodipine, nifedipine, diltiazem) — long-acting
- **Nitrates** (sublingual + long-acting) for breakthrough
- **AVOID beta-blockers** (unopposed alpha → worse spasm)
- **Smoking cessation** (mandatory)
- Statin (improves endothelial function)
- Aspirin controversial (some evidence of worsening)
- Magnesium supplementation (some benefit)

---

## 5. Prognosis
- Generally good with treatment
- **Malignant arrhythmias** can occur — consider ICD if VF documented

---

## 6. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **ST elevation at rest + normal coronaries** = think vasospasm
- First-line: **CCB** (long-acting); **AVOID beta-blockers**
- Triggers: cocaine, triptans, smoking, 5-FU, alcohol
- Provocation test: intracoronary acetylcholine or ergonovine
- Risk: malignant arrhythmia (VT/VF/AV block) → SCD
