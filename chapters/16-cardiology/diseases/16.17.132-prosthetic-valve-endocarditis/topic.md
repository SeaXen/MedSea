# Prosthetic Valve Endocarditis (PVE)

## 1. Epidemiology
- 1–3% of prosthetic valves
- Mechanical ≈ bioprosthetic
- **Early PVE** (<60 d, nosocomial) vs **Late PVE** (>60 d, community)
- Early: Staph aureus, coagulase-negative staph, GNR
- Late: similar to native (Strep, Staph, enterococci)

## 2. Diagnosis
- **Modified Duke criteria** — same as native IE
- Echo: TTE sensitivity ~50%, **TEE 90%+**
- Look for: vegetations, paravalvular leak, dehiscence, abscess

## 3. Management
- Empiric: vancomycin + gentamicin + rifampin (covers Staph including MRSA)
- Directed by cultures
- **Surgery indications:**
  - Heart failure
  - Uncontrolled infection
  - Perivalvular extension/abscess
  - Large vegetations (>10 mm)
  - Prosthetic dehiscence
  - Relapse

## 4. Outcomes
- Mortality 30–50%
- Worse with Staph, root abscess, early surgery

> **Pearls** — **TEE is essential** (TTE misses 50%). Surgery often needed. Staph aureus PVE mortality ~50%. **Endocarditis team** improves outcomes.
