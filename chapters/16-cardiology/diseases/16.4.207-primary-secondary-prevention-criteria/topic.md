# ICD — Primary/Secondary Prevention Criteria

## 1. Secondary Prevention
- **Documented VF** or hemodynamically significant VT not within 48 h of MI/reversible cause
- Survivors of SCD (with reversible cause identified and treated)
- **Class IA** — benefit proven (AVID, CIDS, CASH trials)

## 2. Primary Prevention
**Ischemic CM (post-MI):**
- LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II–III, ≥40 d post-MI, ≥3 mo post-revascularization
- Optimal GDMT for ≥3 months before ICD
- Expected survival >1 year
- **Class IA** (MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT)

**Non-ischemic DCM:**
- LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II–III, ≥3 mo optimal GDMT
- **Class IA** (SCD-HeFT, DEFINITE)

**Other:**
- HCM with HCM-SCD risk ≥6%
- ARVC with ≥3 major or 2 major + 2 minor Task Force criteria
- LQTS, Brugada, CPVT after cardiac arrest or recurrent syncope
- Post-TEER (MitraClip) — not standard indication

## 3. Bridge Therapy
- **WCD (LifeVest)** — for the 40-day waiting period post-MI or pending reassessment

## 4. Not Indicated
- NYHA IV not candidate for CRT-D, LVAD, or HTx
- Life expectancy <1 year
- Incessant VT/VF
- Significant psychiatric illness

> **Pearls** — **Secondary prevention** (after VF/VT) = always ICD if reversible cause treated. **Primary prevention** = LVEF ≤35% + symptoms + optimal GDMT ≥3 mo + expected survival >1 yr. **Don't** implant <40 d post-MI or <3 mo post-PCI/CABG.
