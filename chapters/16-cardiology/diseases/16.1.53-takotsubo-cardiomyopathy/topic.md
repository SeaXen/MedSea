# Takotsubo Cardiomyopathy

## 1. Definition
**Takotsubo (stress) cardiomyopathy** = acute, transient LV systolic dysfunction (**apical ballooning** or other patterns) mimicking ACS, in absence of obstructive CAD, typically triggered by **emotional/physical stress**. Predominantly **post-menopausal women**.

## 2. Key Features
- **Pathogenesis**: catecholamine surge, microvascular spasm, myocardial stunning
- **Anatomic variants** (apical is classic): **apical** (most common), mid-ventricular, basal (reverse), focal, biventricular
- **Diagnosis (Mayo criteria)**: (1) transient regional wall motion abnormality extending beyond single coronary territory; (2) absence of obstructive CAD/plaque rupture; (3) new ECG abnormalities or modest troponin; (4) absence of pheochromocytoma/myocarditis
- **Triggers**: emotional stress (death of loved one, surprise), physical (asthma, surgery, sepsis), **"broken heart"**
- **ECG**: ST↑ (often anterior), TWI, QT prolongation; can mimic STEMI
- **Echo**: apical akinesis, hypercontractile base; LVEF ↓
- **CMR**: LGE typically absent (helps differentiate from MI/myocarditis)
- **Complications**: LV outflow obstruction, MR, cardiogenic shock, VT, mural thrombus, ventricular rupture (rare)

## 3. Management
- **Supportive** — most recover in days–weeks
- **GDMT** for HFrEF if reduced EF; β-blocker and ACEi during recovery
- **Anticoagulation** if apical thrombus
- **Avoid**: inotropes (catecholamines), diuretics; mechanical support (IABP, Impella) if shock
- **β-blockers** continued long-term may prevent recurrence
- **Avoid** triggers; psychological support
- **Prognosis**: good overall; in-hospital mortality 5%; recurrence 5% per year

> **Pearls**
> - **Apex balloons, base contracts** — pathognomonic
> - **Post-menopausal women** most common
> - CMR: **no LGE** (vs MI has LGE)
> - **Catecholamines** can worsen; avoid inotropes if possible
