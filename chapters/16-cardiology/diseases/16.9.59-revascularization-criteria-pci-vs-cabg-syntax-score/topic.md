## 1. When to Revascularise
- **Refractory symptoms** despite optimal medical therapy
- **CCS III–IV** angina
- **Left main** disease (≥50% stenosis)
- **Proximal LAD** + multivessel
- **3-vessel disease**, especially with LV dysfunction (EF <40%)
- **Large ischaemic burden** (>10% myocardium on stress imaging)
- **LV dysfunction** with viable myocardium

---

## 2. SYNTAX Score (Anatomy-Based)
Quantifies coronary complexity. Used to choose between PCI and CABG.

| SYNTAX | Preferred Strategy |
|---|---|
| **≤ 22 (low)** | PCI or CABG (equipoise) |
| **23–32 (intermediate)** | MDT discussion; CABG often preferred |
| **≥ 33 (high)** | **CABG** preferred |

### Diabetes + Multivessel
**FREEDOM, SYNTAX, FUTURE** → **CABG preferred** for DM + 3-vessel (survival benefit, fewer repeat revasc)

---

## 3. Left Main Disease
- **Low SYNTAX (≤22)**: PCI acceptable (EXCEL, NOBLE)
- **High SYNTAX (≥33)**: **CABG** preferred
- Heart team decision mandatory

---

## 4. ISCHEMIA Trial (2020)
- Stable CAD + moderate ischaemia: **routine invasive** did NOT reduce death / MI vs optimal medical therapy
- Exceptions favouring revascularisation: left main, EF <40%, refractory symptoms, large ischaemic burden, high-risk anatomy

---

## 5. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **SYNTAX score** guides revascularisation: ≤22 → PCI, ≥33 → CABG
- **DM + 3-vessel → CABG** preferred (FREEDOM)
- Left main: low SYNTAX → PCI; high SYNTAX → CABG (heart team)
- ISCHEMIA: routine invasive adds no mortality benefit over optimal medical therapy (except high-risk anatomy)
- Revascularise for refractory symptoms, large ischaemia, LV dysfunction, LM/proximal LAD/3VD
