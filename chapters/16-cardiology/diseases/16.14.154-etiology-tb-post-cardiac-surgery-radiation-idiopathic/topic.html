CARDIOLOGY
Etiology (TB, post-cardiac surgery, radiation, idiopathic)
1 Definition

Constrictive pericarditis: Chronic scarring and thickening of the pericardium leading to impaired ventricular filling. The fibrotic, often calcified pericardium encases the heart, restricting diastolic expansion.

2 Etiology (Worldwide)
Common Causes
  • TB (most common worldwide, especially developing countries)
  • Post-cardiac surgery (most common in developed countries)
  • Radiation therapy (mediastinal — Hodgkin, breast, lung)
  • Idiopathic/chronic pericarditis (presumed viral)
  • Connective tissue disease: SLE, RA
Less Common
  • Uremia (chronic)
  • Malignancy (with pericardial invasion)
  • Post-MI (chronic inflammatory)
  • Bacterial (purulent)
  • Fungal
Rare
  • Drugs (practolol-induced — withdrawn)
  • Trauma, sarcoidosis
  • Asbestosis
3 Pathophysiology
  • Thickened, fibrotic, often calcified pericardium
  • ↓Compliance → impaired diastolic filling
  • Equalization of pressures: RA = RV diastolic = PA diastolic = PCWP
  • Ventricular interdependence (septum shifts with respiration)
  • Preserved systolic function (early)
4 Clinical Features
Right Heart Failure Predominant
  • Peripheral edema
  • Ascites (often disproportionate)
  • Hepatic congestion, hepatomegaly
  • JVD with rapid y-descent (Friedreich sign)
  • Kussmaul sign (↑JVP on inspiration)
  • Pericardial knock (early diastolic sound)
Other
  • Dyspnea, fatigue
  • Weight loss, cachexia (chronic)
  • Pleural effusions

6 Diagnosis
  • Echo: Septal bounce, respirophasic septal shift, preserved tissue Doppler
  • CT/MRI: Pericardial thickening (≥3 mm), calcification
  • Cardiac cath: Dip-plateau (square root sign), ventricular interdependence
  • Differential: Restrictive cardiomyopathy (most important)
7 Management
  • Diuretics for congestion (symptom relief only)
  • Treat underlying cause (TB → ATT)
  • Definitive: Pericardiectomy (surgical removal)
Pearls - TB = #1 cause worldwide; post-cardiac surgery = #1 in developed countries - Kussmaul sign + pericardial knock = constriction - Pulsus paradoxus favors tamponade over constriction - Pericardial calcification on CXR is pathognomonic - Pericardiectomy is the only definitive Rx
