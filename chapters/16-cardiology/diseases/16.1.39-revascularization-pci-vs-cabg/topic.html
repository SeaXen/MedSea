CARDIOLOGY
Revascularization (PCI vs CABG)
1 Definitions
  • PCI (Percutaneous Coronary Intervention): catheter-based, balloon + stent
  • CABG (Coronary Artery Bypass Graft): surgical, LIMA/RIMA + vein grafts

The choice depends on anatomy, comorbidities, risk, and patient preference, decided by the Heart Team.

2 Indications for CABG (Preferred)
PatternReasoning
------
**Left main disease (≥50%)**SYNTAX, NOBLE, EXCEL — CABG preferred for >3VD + LM, lower LM only with low SYNTAX
**Three-vessel disease (3VD)**SYNTAX: CABG > PCI in 3VD, esp. with DM
**2VD with proximal LAD**Surgery superior (anatomic LAD territory)
**DM + multivessel**FREEDOM: CABG > PCI for mortality, MI, repeat revascularisation
**LV dysfunction + multivessel**CABG + medical (STICH)
**Inability to take DAPT**Surgical
3 Indications for PCI (Preferred)
PatternReasoning
------
**Single-vessel disease**PCI first-line
**2VD without proximal LAD**PCI reasonable
**Left main + low SYNTAX (≤22)**EXCEL — PCI non-inferior for composite at 5 yr
**High surgical risk**Age, frailty, EF <30%, COPD, CKD
**Acute STEMI**Primary PCI
**Recurrent ischaemia, shock**Urgent PCI
**Patient preference**Faster recovery with PCI
4 SYNTAX Score

Anatomic scoring system to grade lesion complexity (1–3 vessels, bifurcation, length, calcification, etc.). Guides revascularisation:

  • Low (0–22): PCI or CABG
  • Intermediate (23–32): discussion; consider CABG
  • High (≥33): CABG preferred

6 Diabetes
  • Diabetics with multivessel disease → CABG preferred (FREEDOM)
  • DES + aggressive medical therapy still inferior to CABG in DM
  • SGLT2i / GLP-1 RA improve outcomes irrespective of strategy
7 Surgical Risk Assessment
  • STS score, EuroSCORE II — operative mortality
  • High risk: elderly, frail, prior CABG, severe COPD, EF <30%
  • CABG with cardiopulmonary bypass (on-pump) vs OPCAB (off-pump) — no clear mortality difference
8 PCI Practicalities
  • DES (drug-eluting) standard
  • Intravascular imaging (IVUS, OCT) for LM, complex lesions
  • FFR / iFR for borderline lesions
  • Radial access preferred
  • DAPT required ≥6–12 months
9 CABG Practicalities
  • LIMA → LAD (gold standard, >90% patency at 10 yr)
  • Vein grafts (SVG) → other targets (50% occlusion at 10 yr)
  • Cardiopulmonary bypass vs OPCAB
  • Avoid aspirin cessation (continue)
10 Post-Revascularisation Care
  • PCI: DAPT 6–12 months, then aspirin indefinitely
  • CABG: aspirin 75–100 mg indefinitely (continue through surgery)
  • Statin, ACEi, beta-blocker — both strategies
  • Cardiac rehab — both strategies
11 Hybrid Approach
  • LIMA–LAD (CABG) + PCI to non-LAD lesions — for high-risk surgical patients
  • Limited adoption, specialised centres
Pearls - CABG preferred in: 3VD, LM + high SYNTAX, DM + multivessel, LV dysfunction + 3VD - PCI preferred in: 1VD, 2VD non-proximal LAD, LM low SYNTAX, STEMI, surgical high-risk - Heart Team decides (surgeon, interventionalist, non-invasive) - LIMA → LAD = gold standard in CABG - SYNTAX score quantifies lesion complexity - Diabetics with 3VD = CABG (FREEDOM) - Always DES in PCI
