# Cardiac Sarcoidosis

## 1. Epidemiology
- 2–5% of sarcoidosis patients have cardiac involvement clinically
- **20–25%** at autopsy
- Higher in Japanese, Northern European populations
- Can be **isolated** cardiac sarcoidosis (no pulmonary disease)

## 2. Diagnosis (Heart Rhythm Society 2014)
**Histologic** (gold standard): non-caseating granulomas on biopsy
**Clinical** (no biopsy needed):
- Definite: biopsy-proven extracardiac sarcoidosis + cardiac involvement
- Probable: characteristic cardiac findings + biopsy-proven extracardiac
- Isolated: biopsy-proven cardiac sarcoidosis only

**Diagnostic findings:**
- **CMR with LGE** (basal septal, lateral, mid-myocardial/epicardial)
- **FDG-PET** — active inflammation (focal uptake)
- Echo: RWMA, basal septum thinning, LV dysfunction
- AV block <50y
- Sustained VT of unknown cause

## 3. Treatment
- **Corticosteroids** (prednisolone 0.5–1 mg/kg/day, taper over 6–12 mo)
- **Steroid-sparing**: methotrexate, azathioprine, mycophenolate, infliximab
- **GDMT** for HFrEF if present
- **ICD** for: sustained VT, VF, AV block with syncope, LVEF <35%
- **Catheter ablation** for recurrent VT

## 4. Outcomes
- 5-year survival 60–90% with treatment
- AV block may reverse with immunosuppression
- Monitor with PET, echo, Holter

> **Pearls** — Suspect in **young AV block or VT of unknown cause**. **CMR + PET** is the diagnostic workup. **Corticosteroids** are mainstay. **ICD** for sustained arrhythmia or LV dysfunction. Monitor response with PET.
