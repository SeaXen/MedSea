CARDIOLOGY
3D echocardiography
1 Introduction

3D echo (real-time 3D / RT3D / 4D) acquires a pyramidal volume dataset that can be cropped in any plane. Uses matrix-array transducer (~3000 elements). Provides more accurate volumes, EF, and valvular anatomy than 2D, with no geometric assumptions.

2 Acquisition Modes
  • Live 3D — narrow sector, single beat (best for TEE, fast rhythms)
  • Multi-beat gated — wide sector, ECG-gated stitched; arrhythmia can cause artefact
  • Zoom 3D — focused on a single structure (MV, LAA)
  • Full volume — large pyramidal dataset; for EF, mass, volumes
  • 3D colour Doppler — regurgitant/shunt jets in 3D
3 Clinical Applications
LV Function
  • 3D LV EF — most accurate non-invasive EF (no foreshortening, no geometric assumption)
  • Normal 3D EF ≥55%
  • 3D LV volumes — most reproducible (essential for chemotherapy monitoring, trials)
  • 3D GLS — less vendor variability than 2D
Mitral Valve
  • Anatomy from LA side ("surgeon's view") — for repair planning
  • Scallop identification — P1, P2, P3; A1, A2, A3
  • Clefts, prolapse, perforation, vegetations, masses
  • 3D vena contracta area for MR severity
  • Annular sizing for MitraClip (transcatheter edge-to-edge repair)
Aortic Valve
  • TAVR / TAVI sizing — annulus area, perimeter, dimensions
  • Bicuspid aortic valve cusp morphology (Sievers classification)
  • Paravalvular leak localisation
LA / LAA
  • LAA volume and morphology (Cactus, Chicken Wing, Windsock, Cauliflower)
  • LAA thrombus
  • Watchman sizing for LAAC
RV / RA
  • 3D RV EF — increasingly used
  • Volumes validated against CMR
Congenital
  • ASD / VSD shape, size, rims
  • 3D TEE guidance for percutaneous closure
Masses
  • Tumours, thrombi, vegetations — spatial relationships, attachment points
4 3D Transoesophageal (3D TOE)
  • Gold standard for MV repair planning, TAVR/TAVI, LAA closure
  • Higher frame rate than TTE 3D
  • Real-time guidance for MitraClip, TricuspidClip, ASD/PFO closure

6 Limitations
  • Lower temporal and spatial resolution than 2D
  • Larger datasets, longer analysis time
  • Arrhythmia artefacts in multi-beat mode
  • Image quality dependency
  • Cost, expertise
  • Vendor-specific analysis software
7 Comparison with Other Modalities
  • 3D echo EF correlates well with CMR (gold standard for volumes)
  • 3D echo MV area similar to planimetry
  • 3D TEE comparable to CT for TAVR sizing
Pearls - 3D EF = most accurate non-invasive LVEF; no geometric assumption, no foreshortening - 3D TEE is mandatory for MitraClip and increasingly for TAVR - Surgeon's view of MV from LA side — P1/A1 (anterolateral) to P3/A3 (posteromedial) - 3D GLS reduces vendor variability vs 2D - Full volume mode gated to ECG — beware arrhythmias causing stitch artefact
