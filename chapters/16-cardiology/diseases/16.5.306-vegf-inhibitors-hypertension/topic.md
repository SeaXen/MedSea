# VEGF Inhibitor Hypertension

## 1. Agents
- **Anti-VEGF antibodies** (bevacizumab, ramucirumab)
- **Tyrosine kinase inhibitors** (sunitinib, sorafenib, pazopanib, axitinib, lenvatinib, cabozantinib)
- **VEGFR2 inhibitors** (ramucirumab)

## 2. Mechanism
- **VEGF** maintains nitric oxide production, vasodilation
- Inhibition → **↑ endothelin-1**, **↓ NO** → vasoconstriction
- **↓ capillary density** → ↑ peripheral resistance
- **Rare**: posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome (PRES)

## 3. Incidence
- **Sunitinib**: 15–47% HTN, 2–8% severe
- **Sorafenib**: 17–43% HTN
- **Bevacizumab**: 4–35% HTN
- **Lenvatinib**: 73% HTN (very high)
- **Pazopanib**: 40% HTN

## 4. Risk Factors
- Pre-existing HTN
- Age >65
- Renal cell carcinoma (especially with sunitinib)
- Combination therapy

## 5. Management
- **Baseline BP** + aggressive monitoring
- **Pre-treat** with ACEi or ARB
- Target <140/90 (some guidelines <130/80)
- **Avoid** non-dihydropyridine CCB (diltiazem, verapamil) — CYP3A4 interactions
- **ACEi, ARB, dihydropyridine CCB** (amlodipine) preferred
- **Dose reduction** of anticancer drug if BP uncontrolled

> **Pearls** — **VEGF inhibitors cause HTN** in up to 70% of patients (esp. lenvatinib). **Pre-treat with ACEi/ARB**. Avoid diltiazem/verapamil (CYP3A4). Dose-reduce cancer drug if needed. Monitor for PRES, LV dysfunction, thrombosis.
