CARDIOLOGY
Arrhythmia diagnosis on ECG
1 Introduction

Systematic approach: Rate → Rhythm → P wave → PR → QRS → Response to carotid sinus. Always: is the patient stable? — if unstable (chest pain, hypotension, syncope, signs of shock, ischaemia), DC cardioversion.

2 Sinus Rhythms
  • Sinus rhythm — rate 60–100, P upright in I, II, aVF, P before every QRS
  • Sinus bradycardia — <60 bpm
  • Sinus tachycardia — >100 bpm (max expected = 220 − age)
  • Sinus arrhythmia — phasic variation with respiration (normal in young)
3 Supraventricular Tachycardias (SVT)

Narrow complex (<120 ms), regular.

4 Atrial Fibrillation
  • Irregularly irregular, no discrete P waves, fibrillatory baseline
  • AF with slow ventricular response = complete heart block + AF = AF-CHB
  • AF with WPW = wide complex, irregular, very fast → DC cardioversion, avoid AV nodal blockers (adenosine, β-blockers, CCB, digoxin)
  • AF with aberrancy vs AF + WPW vs AF + VT — clinical context, prior ECGs
5 Atrial Flutter
  • Sawtooth F waves at 300 bpm
  • AV block 2:1 (150 bpm), 3:1 (100), 4:1 (75)
  • Typical (Type I): negative F in II, III, aVF; rate 250–350
  • Atypical: positive in II, III, aVF
  • Treatment: DC cardioversion, ibutilide, dofetilide, rate control, ablation (CTI)
6 Ventricular Tachyarrhythmias (Wide QRS, >120 ms)
Ventricular Tachycardia (VT)
  • Wide QRS >120 ms, regular
  • Rate >100 (usually 150–250)
  • AV dissociation, capture beats, fusion beats = diagnostic
  • Concordant (all QRS same direction) — more specific
  • RBBB-pattern VT = LBBB morphology = idiopathic RVOT, ARVC
  • LBBB-pattern VT = RBBB morphology = LV origin
  • Polymorphic VT = changing QRS axis (QRS twisting around baseline = Torsades de Pointes)
  • Brugada criteria for VT in WCT
Ventricular Fibrillation (VF)
  • Chaotic baseline, no QRS — incompatible with life
  • Defibrillation (unsynchronised) immediately
7 Wide Complex Tachycardia (WCT) — VT vs SVT with Aberrancy

Assume VT until proven otherwise (especially in over-40s with structural heart disease).

  • Brugada criteria: no RS in any precordial lead → VT
  • AV dissociation → VT
  • RS interval >100 ms → VT
  • Morphology criteria (RBBB-like: taller left rabbit ear, no triphasic; LBBB-like: notching, initial r >30 ms, etc.)
8 Bradyarrhythmias
  • Sinus bradycardia <60
  • Sinus arrest / pause >2 s
  • Sick sinus syndrome — bradycardia-tachycardia syndrome
  • Junctional escape — 40–60 bpm, no P or retrograde P
  • Ventricular escape — 20–40 bpm, wide QRS
9 Pacemaker Rhythms
  • Pacing spike → capture
  • Failure to capture = spike without QRS/P
  • Failure to sense = inappropriate firing
  • Failure to pace = no spike when expected
Pearls - Irregularly irregular + no P = AF (until proven otherwise) - Wide complex tachycardia in a patient with structural heart disease = VT until proven otherwise. - Adenosine in AF + WPW = VF risk (do not give). - Torsades de Pointes = polymorphic VT with QT prolongation → IV magnesium 2 g, overdrive pacing, correct K+, withdraw offending drugs. - Pulsatile, broad complex, irregular tachycardia = AF + WPW (or pre-excited AF) → electrical cardioversion, avoid digoxin/β-blocker/CCB/adenosine.
