# Tuberculous Pericarditis

## 1. Epidemiology
- Common in TB-endemic areas
- Leading cause of constrictive pericarditis worldwide
- Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, HIV+ patients

## 2. Diagnosis
- **Lymphocytic exudative** effusion
- **ADA (adenosine deaminase) >40 U/L** in pericardial fluid
- **PCR for M. tuberculosis** in fluid
- **Culture** (slow, sensitivity <50%)
- **QuantiFERON-TB Gold** (supportive)
- Pericardial biopsy: caseating granulomas (gold standard)

## 3. Treatment
- **Standard ATT** (HRZE → HR) for 6–9 months
- **Prednisolone** 1 mg/kg, taper over 6–8 weeks (IMPI trial: ↓ mortality, ↓ progression to constriction)
- Pericardiocentesis for large effusions/tamponade
- Pericardiectomy if develops constriction

## 4. Complications
- **Constrictive pericarditis** (20–40% without steroids, 10–20% with)
- Tamponade
- Mortality 20–40% in HIV+ with advanced disease

> **Pearls** — **ADA + lymphocytic exudate** = suspect TB pericarditis in endemic regions. **Prednisolone reduces progression to constriction** (IMPI trial). **Pericardiocentesis** for tamponade. **Pericardiectomy** if constrictive phase develops.
