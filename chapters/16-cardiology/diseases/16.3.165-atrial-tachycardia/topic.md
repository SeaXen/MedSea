# Atrial Tachycardia

## 1. Definition
**Atrial tachycardia (AT)** = supraventricular rhythm originating from a discrete atrial focus (outside the AV node), regular, rate >100/min. Subtypes: **focal, macroreentrant, multifocal** (see MAT).

## 2. Key Features
- **Focal AT**:
  - Discrete focus, often from crista terminalis, PVs, septum, CS ostium
  - Mechanism: enhanced automaticity, triggered activity, microreentry
  - P-wave morphology different from sinus (depends on focus)
  - Usually paroxysmal; can be sustained
  - Sensitivity to **adenosine** (transient AV block) helps confirm atrial origin
- **Macroreentrant AT**:
  - Reentry around an anatomical obstacle (scar, suture line, prior surgery)
  - Includes "atypical" atrial flutter, post-ablation tachycardias
  - Continuous P-wave morphology during tachycardia
- **P-wave**: usually upright in inferior leads if from high right atrium; inverted if from low atrial/AVN
- **Differential**: AVNRT, AVRT, atrial flutter, sinus tachycardia
- **Causes**: digitals toxicity, electrolyte disturbance, structural heart disease, post-ablation
- **Diagnosis**: ECG, ambulatory monitoring, EP study

## 3. Management
- **Acute**:
  - Vagal manoeuvres
  - **Adenosine** (6 → 12 mg) — terminates triggered AT, transient block in others
  - **β-blocker or CCB** (verapamil, diltiazem) for rate control
  - **Synchronised cardioversion** if unstable
- **Chronic**:
  - **β-blocker or CCB** first-line
  - **Class IC** (flecainide, propafenone) for "pill-in-pocket"
  - **Catheter ablation** (highly effective, >90% for focal AT)
- **Treat underlying**: electrolyte correction, stop offending drugs
- **Anticoagulation** if persistent (per CHA2DS2-VASc)

> **Pearls**
> - **AT has abnormal P-wave** (atrial origin)
> - **Adenosine** = diagnostic (transient AV block exposes AT)
> - **Focal AT** ablation = highly effective
> - **Macroreentrant** AT = scar-related (post-ablation/surgery)
