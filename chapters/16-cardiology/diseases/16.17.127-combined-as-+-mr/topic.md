# Combined AS + MR

## 1. Hemodynamics
- LV faces combined pressure (AS) + volume (MR) overload
- **Low-flow, low-gradient** states common
- AS severity may be underestimated by gradient alone

## 2. Assessment
- **Echo** — careful measurement of both
- AVA, mean gradient, dimensionless index (DI)
- MR severity (EROA, regurgitant volume)
- **AS is more often the driver** of symptoms

## 3. Management
- Treat symptomatic severe AS first (dominant lesion)
- If both severe, may need combined AVR + MV surgery
- **TAVI for AS** — what about MR?
  - TAVI may improve MR in ~50% (LV remodeling)
  - Persistent severe MR after TAVI = high risk

## 4. Surgical Risk
- Combined AVR + MVR: STS risk additive
- Consider TAVI + future TEER (MitraClip) for high-risk

> **Pearls** — AS usually the **dominant lesion** in AS+MR. TAVI may improve functional MR over time. Combined surgery for true severe dual valve disease. **Low-flow AS** common — measure with dobutamine stress if EF <50%.
