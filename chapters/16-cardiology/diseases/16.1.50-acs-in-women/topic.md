# ACS in Women

## 1. Differences from Men
- **Atypical presentation** — dyspnea, fatigue, epigastric/back pain
- Older age at presentation (~7 years)
- More comorbidities (HTN, DM, AF, CKD)
- More MINOCA (~10% vs 5%)
- SCAD much more common <50y

## 2. Diagnosis
- **hs-Troponin** — same cutoff
- **ECG** — same criteria
- **CTA** — reasonable first-line in low-intermediate risk
- **MINOCA workup** — IVUS, OCT, CMR, spasm testing

## 3. Management
- PCI equally effective but underused
- Same DAPT, statin targets
- **Pregnancy** — avoid warfarin (teratogenic), ACEi/ARB, statins

## 4. Sex-Specific Risks
- PCOS, preeclampsia, gestational HTN/DM, premature menopause, OCP + smoking

> **Pearls** — Women **2× more likely to die** from MI. Don't dismiss atypical symptoms. MINOCA needs workup of all 4 mechanisms (plaque, spasm, thromboembolism, microvascular).
