# Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor (ICI) Myocarditis

## 1. Definition
Inflammatory myocarditis from **anti-CTLA4** (ipilimumab) + **anti-PD1/PD-L1** (nivolumab, pembrolizumab, atezolizumab) ± combination.

## 2. Incidence
- **Monotherapy**: 1–2%
- **Combination** (anti-CTLA4 + anti-PD1): up to 2.4%
- **Mortality**: 30–50% (high!)

## 3. Presentation
- **Often fulminant** — HF, cardiogenic shock, AV block, VT
- Can occur any time after first dose (median 2–3 months)
- Often with **myositis + myasthenia-like syndrome** (overlap)

## 4. Diagnosis
- **Troponin elevation** (often very high)
- **ECG**: conduction disease, VT
- **Echo**: LV dysfunction
- **CMR**: LGE, T2 edema
- **Endomyocardial biopsy** — lymphocytic infiltration, T cell predominant
- **High suspicion** in any CV symptoms on ICI

## 5. Management
- **STOP ICI immediately**
- **High-dose methylprednisolone** (1–2 mg/kg/day, sometimes pulse 1 g/day x 3)
- **Steroid-refractory**: IVIG, anti-thymocyte globulin, infliximab, abatacept, ruxolitinib
- **HF therapy** (avoid high-dose β-blocker initially if unstable)
- **Temporary pacing** for AV block
- **MCS** for cardiogenic shock
- May resume ICI after recovery (controversial)

> **Pearls** — **HIGH mortality (30–50%)**. **Stop ICI + start high-dose steroids** immediately. Can be **fulminant** — monitor closely. Often associated with **myositis/myasthenia** (overlap syndrome). Early recognition critical.
