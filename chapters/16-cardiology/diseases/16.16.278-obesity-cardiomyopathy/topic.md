# Obesity Cardiomyopathy

## 1. Definition
- HF due to obesity (typically BMI >40)
- Often **HFpEF** with concentric LV remodeling; can progress to HFrEF

## 2. Pathophysiology
- **Volume overload** (↑blood volume, ↑CO) → eccentric remodeling
- **Pressure overload** (HTN, OSA) → concentric remodeling
- **Adipokines** (TNF-α, leptin) → inflammation, fibrosis
- **Insulin resistance**, **RAAS activation**
- **Obesity hypoventilation / OSA** → pulmonary HTN, RV strain

## 3. Clinical Features
- HFpEF > HFrEF, but mixed picture common
- Exertional dyspnea, **lateralized edema** (e.g. unilateral leg)
- Comorbidities: HTN, DM, OSA, AF, VTE
- "**Fluid in the fat**" — diffuse edema, often resistant

## 4. Management
- **Weight loss** (lifestyle, **GLP-1 RA** semaglutide, **tirzepatide**)
- **Bariatric surgery** improves LV function, symptoms
- Standard GDMT (RAAS blockers, β-blockers cautiously if hypoventilation)
- **CPAP/BiPAP** for OSA/OHS
- **Treat HFpEF**: SGLT2i (empagliflozin STEP-HFpEF), MRA
- Diuretics for congestion

> **Pearls**: Treat **GLP-1 RA** (semaglutide, tirzepatide) as anti-obesity + CV drugs. Address **OSA** in all obese HFpEF. Weight loss >10% reverses remodeling.
