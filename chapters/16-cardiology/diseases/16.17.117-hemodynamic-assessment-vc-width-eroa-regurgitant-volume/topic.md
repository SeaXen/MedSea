# Mitral Regurgitation — Hemodynamic Assessment

## 1. Definition
**Echocardiographic assessment of MR severity** uses **qualitative, semi-quantitative, and quantitative** parameters. Integration of multiple measures is essential; no single parameter is sufficient.

## 2. Key Features
- **Qualitative**: colour jet area (central vs eccentric); pulmonary vein flow reversal (systolic = severe)
- **Semi-quantitative**:
  - **Vena contracta width** (VC): <3 mm mild, 3–6.9 mm moderate, **≥7 mm severe**
  - **PISA radius** (EROA calculated from radius × aliasing velocity / peak MR velocity)
  - **CW Doppler intensity** and contour (dense, triangular = severe)
- **Quantitative**:
  - **EROA (effective regurgitant orifice area)**: <0.20 cm² mild, 0.20–0.39 moderate, **≥0.40 cm² severe**
  - **Regurgitant volume (R Vol)**: <30 mL mild, 30–59 moderate, **≥60 mL severe**
  - **Regurgitant fraction (RF)**: <30% mild, 30–49% moderate, **≥50% severe**
- **Primary vs secondary** MR (mechanism matters for treatment):
  - **Primary**: leaflet pathology (prolapse, flail, endocarditis, rheumatic)
  - **Secondary (functional)**: LV remodelling → leaflet tethering, annular dilation; HF-driven
- **Echo**: TTE first; **TOE** for ambiguous cases, pre-surgical planning

## 3. Management
- **Mild-moderate primary MR**: monitor
- **Severe primary MR + symptoms OR LV dysfunction (LVEF ≤60%, LVESD ≥40 mm)**: surgery (repair preferred over replacement)
- **Secondary MR**: optimise GDMT; consider **TEER (MitraClip)** if HF + severe MR + appropriate anatomy (COAPT criteria)
- **TEER contraindicated** if LVEF <20%, severe RV dysfunction, severe pulmonary HTN
- **Serial echo**: annual for moderate, every 6 months for severe/asymptomatic

> **Pearls**
> - **Severe MR**: VC ≥7 mm, EROA ≥0.4 cm², R Vol ≥60 mL
> - **Primary MR** → surgery; **secondary MR** → GDMT + TEER (COAPT)
> - **PISA** is the workhorse for EROA calculation
> - **Pulmonary vein systolic reversal** = severe MR
