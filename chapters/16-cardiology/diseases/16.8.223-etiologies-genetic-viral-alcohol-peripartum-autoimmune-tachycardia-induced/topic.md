# Cardiomyopathy Etiologies

## 1. Genetic
- **HCM** — sarcomere mutations (β-myosin heavy chain, MYBPC3)
- **DCM** — TTN truncations (25%), LMNA, FLNC, PLN
- **ARVC** — desmosomal (PKP2, DSP, DSC2, DSG2)

## 2. Viral / Infectious
- **Myocarditis** — Coxsackie B, adenovirus, parvovirus B19, HHV6, SARS-CoV-2, HIV, Chagas (T. cruzi)

## 3. Toxic
- **Alcohol** — >80 g/day for >5 years (reversible if early abstinence)
- **Chemotherapy** — anthracyclines (doxorubicin >400 mg/m²), HER2-targeted (trastuzumab), VEGF inhibitors
- **Cocaine, methamphetamine**
- **Heavy metals** (cobalt, lead, arsenic)

## 4. Endocrine / Metabolic
- **Thyroid** — hyper/hypo
- **Pheochromocytoma**
- **Diabetes**
- **Obesity**

## 5. Infiltrative
- **Amyloid** — AL, ATTR
- **Sarcoidosis**
- **Hemochromatosis**

## 6. Other
- **Peripartum** (PPCM, last month - 5 mo postpartum)
- **Tachycardia-induced** (rate >100 bpm for prolonged periods)
- **Stress (Takotsubo)**
- **Autoimmune** (SLE, systemic sclerosis)
- **Nutritional** (thiamine, selenium, carnitine)

> **Pearls** — Always consider **TTR amyloidosis** in HFpEF with thick walls. **Chagas** cardiomyopathy = apical aneurysm + conduction disease. **Peripartum** = last month of pregnancy to 5 mo postpartum. **Alcohol** = reversible with abstinence if caught early.
