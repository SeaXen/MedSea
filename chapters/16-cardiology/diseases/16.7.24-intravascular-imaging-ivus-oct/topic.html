CARDIOLOGY
Intravascular imaging (IVUS, OCT)
1 Introduction

Intracoronary imaging complements angiography by providing cross-sectional views of vessel wall, plaque, and stent apposition. IVUS (ultrasound) and OCT (optical coherence tomography) are the two modalities — both essential for complex PCI optimisation.

2 IVUS (Intravascular Ultrasound)
Principles
  • 40–60 MHz catheter (mechanical or phased array)
  • Pullback at 0.5–1.0 mm/s (motorised)
  • Penetration ~6 mm (good for deeper layers, entire vessel wall, large vessels)
  • Image of adventitia, media, plaque, lumen
Measurements
  • Minimum lumen area (MLA) — most important
  • Plaque burden (plaque + media / EEM area)
  • Lumen, EEM diameters/areas
  • Stent expansion, apposition, edge dissection, malapposition
Plaque Characterisation
  • Hypoechoic (lipid-rich, TCFA)
  • Hyperechoic (fibrous)
  • Calcified — acoustic shadowing
  • Mixed
Clinical Uses
  • LM PCI (especially distal LM, ambiguous on angio)
  • Stent optimisation (underexpansion, malapposition)
  • ISR assessment
  • OCT inferior in LM → IVUS often preferred (penetration, less artefact)
  • MLA <6 mm² LM = significant
3 OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography)
Principles
  • Near-infrared light (wavelength 1300 nm)
  • Resolution 10–20 μm (10× better than IVUS)
  • Penetration 1–3 mm (poor for full vessel wall in large vessels)
  • Requires blood clearance with contrast flush (balloon occlusion or continuous injection)
  • Pullback at 20–40 mm/s — fast
Measurements
  • MLA, fibrous cap thickness (key for vulnerable plaque)
  • Lipid pool (signal-poor, diffuse border)
  • Calcification (signal-poor, sharp border)
  • TCFA — thin-cap fibroatheroma: cap <65 μm, lipid pool >90° arc
  • Macrophages, neovascularisation, cholesterol crystals
  • Stent strut coverage, malapposition, neointimal hyperplasia
Clinical Uses
  • Mechanism of ACS — plaque rupture, erosion, calcified nodule
  • Stent optimisation — detect malapposition, underexpansion, edge dissection
  • Bioresorbable scaffold imaging (now withdrawn but historically)
  • TCFA identification (vulnerable plaque, not yet clinical)
  • Intracoronary thrombus
  • Spontaneous coronary artery dissection (SCAD) — high resolution of intimal flap

5 Plaque Rupture / Erosion / Calcified Nodule (ACS Mechanism)
  • Plaque rupture — disrupted fibrous cap, thrombus (most common)
  • Plaque erosion — intact fibrous cap, luminal thrombus
  • Calcified nodule — eruptive calcification protruding into lumen (elderly, dialysis)
6 Stent Optimisation (IVUS/OCT Criteria — MUSIC, IVIUS-XPL)
  • Stent expansion: minimum stent area (MSA) >5.5 mm² (or >5.0 mm² if small), or >90% of distal reference
  • Apposition: complete apposition of all struts
  • No edge dissection >60°, no MLA <4 mm²
  • No malapposition >0.4 mm axial distance
  • No thrombus
7 Limitations
  • Cost, additional procedure time
  • Contrast load (OCT)
  • Inter-observer variability in interpretation
  • Artefacts (ring-down, motion)
  • Need for additional training
8 Guidelines
  • Class IIa for LM PCI (IVUS), stent optimisation (IVUS/OCT), uncertain stent result
  • Class I for guidance in ISR, LM, complex bifurcation (some centres)
Pearls - IVUS = penetration, OCT = resolution - OCT = best for stent optimisation, TCFA, plaque erosion, SCAD - IVUS = best for LM (penetration), large vessels, plaque burden - MLA <6 mm² in LM = significant (IVUS) - MSA >5.5 mm² = adequate stent expansion - TCFA: cap <65 µm, lipid arc >90° (OCT)
