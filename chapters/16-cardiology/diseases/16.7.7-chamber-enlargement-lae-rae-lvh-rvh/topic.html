CARDIOLOGY
Chamber enlargement (LAE, RAE, LVH, RVH)
1 Introduction

Chamber enlargement criteria on ECG are sensitivity-limited (LVH only ~50%) but remain a core PACES / FCPS topic. Echo is gold standard. Patterns reflect increased muscle mass or dilated chamber altering the depolarisation vector.

2 Left Atrial Enlargement (LAE)
  • P mitrale — broad, notched P wave in lead II ≥120 ms ("P mitrale")
  • Biphasic P in V1 — terminal negative portion >1 mm² (40 ms × 1 mm deep)
  • Causes: MS, HCM, hypertensive heart disease, severe AS, severe AR
  • Echo: LA volume index >34 mL/m² (gold standard)
3 Right Atrial Enlargement (RAE)
  • P pulmonale — tall, peaked P wave ≥2.5 mm in lead II (>0.25 mV)
  • Causes: COPD, cor pulmonale, TS, PS, pulmonary HTN
  • Echo: RA area >18 cm²
4 Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (LVH)
Sokolow-Lyon
  • S in V1 + R in V5 or V6 ≥35 mm
  • Sensitivity ~30%, specificity ~90%
Cornell
  • R in aVL + S in V3 >28 mm ♂, >20 mm ♀
Romhilt-Estes (point score, ≥5 = LVH)

5 Right Ventricular Hypertrophy (RVH)
Diagnostic Patterns
  • R/S ratio >1 in V1 (R > S in V1)
  • R in V1 ≥7 mm
  • S in V5/V6 > R in V5/V6
  • RBBB pattern with RAD
  • Right axis deviation
  • P pulmonale (often coexists)
  • S1Q3T3 pattern (PE)
Causes
  • Pulmonary HTN, cor pulmonale, COPD, PE, PS, ASD, MR, TR, Fallot
6 Biventricular Hypertrophy
  • Tall R in V5/V6 + tall R in V1
  • Katz-Wachtel phenomenon — large equiphasic complexes in mid-precordial leads
  • Causes: VSD, PDA, AV canal defect, severe MR/AR, dilated cardiomyopathy
7 Differential Patterns
  • LVH vs LBBB — QRS >120 ms, broad monomorphic R in I, V6 with QS V1 = LBBB; Sokolow-Lyon positive + strain = LVH
  • RVH vs RBBB — rSR' in V1 with wide QRS = RBBB; dominant R in V1 with normal QRS = RVH
  • HCM vs LVH — deep narrow Q in lateral leads, "dagger-like" septal Qs, giant T inversions (apical HCM)
Pearls - Sokolow-Lyon ≥35 mm is the most-used LVH criterion. - Strain pattern = LVH with ischaemia, correlates with severity and prognosis. - P mitrale + LVH + AF = mitral stenosis with secondary PH and tricuspid involvement. - R/S >1 in V1 in adult = always pathological (RVH, RBBB, posterior MI, WPW, HCM, lead reversal).
