CARDIOLOGY
Medical therapy (beta-blockers, verapamil, disopyramide, mavacamten, aficamten)
1 Goals
  • Relieve symptoms (dyspnoea, chest pain, presyncope)
  • Reduce LVOT gradient (target <30 mmHg at rest)
  • Improve diastolic filling
  • No proven mortality benefit in HCM (unlike HF GDMT)
2 First-Line: β-Blockers
DrugDoseNotes
---------
**Propranolol**40–80 mg TDSNon-selective; historical
**Metoprolol**25–100 mg BDCardioselective
**Bisoprolol**2.5–10 mg dailyOnce-daily option
**Nadolol**40–160 mg dailyLong-acting, often preferred

Mechanism: negative inotropy + chronotropy → ↓LVOT gradient, ↑diastolic filling time. Avoid verapamil + β-blocker together (bradycardia/heart block).

3 Verapamil
  • 120–480 mg daily (sustained-release)
  • Use if β-blockers fail or are contraindicated
  • Caution: avoid in severe outflow obstruction (gradient >100 mmHg) or high LA pressure — risk of pulmonary oedema
  • Never combine with β-blocker
4 Disopyramide
  • 100–200 mg BD (modified release)
  • Class Ia antiarrhythmic with strong negative inotropy → reduces LVOT gradient by ~50%
  • Often added when β-blocker alone is insufficient
  • Anticholinergic side effects (urinary retention, dry mouth) — limit use in older men
  • Proarrhythmic risk — monitor QTc
5 Mavacamten (Cardiac Myosin Inhibitor)
  • First-in-class myosin modulator that reduces actin-myosin cross-bridge formation
  • Dose: 2.5–15 mg daily; REMS program (risk of HF due to excessive EF reduction)
  • EXPLORER-HCM trial: improved symptoms, exercise capacity (pVO₂), NYHA class; reduced LVOT gradient
  • VALOR-HCM: reduced eligibility for septal reduction
  • Monitor: echo LVEF at baseline, 4 wks, 8 wks, then q3 monthly; stop if LVEF <50%
  • Avoid with CYP2C19 poor metabolisers (lower dose) and concomitant strong CYP inhibitors/inducers
  • Contraindications: LVEF <55%, severe HTN, OHT/surgery, pregnancy
6 Aficamten
  • Next-generation myosin inhibitor
  • SEQUOIA-HCM (2024) — improved pVO₂ and NYHA class
  • Shorter half-life than mavacamten, no REMS required
  • Dose 5–20 mg daily, titrate based on LVEF (4-wk intervals)
7 Drugs to Avoid
  • Nitrates & PDE-5 inhibitors (worsen obstruction)
  • Diuretics (high dose) — reduce preload, worsen gradient
  • Digoxin — contraindicated in obstructive HCM
  • ACEi / ARB — use cautiously (vasodilatation → ↑gradient)
Pearls - β-blockers are first-line; verapamil as alternative (avoid in severe obstruction). - Disopyramide is the most potent gradient-reducer; watch QTc. - Mavacamten reduces gradient and improves symptoms (EXPLORER-HCM) — REMS-monitored. - Aficamten is next-gen, shorter half-life, no REMS (SEQUOIA-HCM). - Avoid nitrates, PDE-5i, digoxin in obstructive HCM.
