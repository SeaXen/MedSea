## 1. Introduction
ACS is the **spectrum of acute myocardial ischaemia** due to sudden reduction in coronary flow. Three entities, classified by **ECG and biomarkers**:
- **STEMI** — ST elevation MI (transmural, complete occlusion)
- **NSTEMI** — non-ST elevation MI (subendocardial, partial occlusion, troponin ↑)
- **UA** — unstable angina (ischaemia, no necrosis, troponin normal)

## 2. Spectrum

| Feature | STEMI | NSTEMI | UA |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pathology** | Complete occlusion | Partial occlusion | Partial / dynamic |
| **ECG** | ST elevation | ST depression, TWI, non-specific | ST depression, TWI, normal |
| **Troponin** | ↑↑ | ↑ | **Normal** |
| **Pain** | At rest, prolonged | Rest, prolonged | Rest, brief |
| **Risk** | Highest | Intermediate | Lower |

## 3. STEMI — Universal Definition (2018, 4th UDMI)
- **ST elevation** at J-point in two contiguous leads:
  - **♂ <40 yr**: ≥2.5 mm V2–V3, ≥1 mm other
  - **♂ ≥40 yr**: ≥2 mm V2–V3, ≥1 mm other
  - **♀**: ≥1.5 mm V2–V3, ≥1 mm other
  - **V7–V9 (posterior)**: ≥0.5 mm (♂ <40: ≥1 mm)
  - **V3R, V4R (RV)**: ≥0.5 mm (esp. ♂ <30: ≥1 mm)
- **New LBBB** + symptoms (no longer diagnostic alone — Sgarbossa criteria help)
- **Trop ↑ + clinical** evidence (in absence of ST elevation)
- Posterior MI: ST depression V1–V3 with ST elevation V7–V9 ≥0.5 mm

## 4. NSTEMI Definition
- **ACS** without ST elevation
- **Troponin elevation** (above 99th percentile) with rise/fall
- ECG: ST depression, T-wave inversion, or non-specific changes
- Type 1 (plaque rupture) vs **Type 2 (supply-demand mismatch)**

## 5. UA Definition
- **Rest angina** (>20 min at rest)
- **New-onset severe angina** (CCS III)
- **Crescendo angina** (↑ frequency, duration, threshold)
- **Troponin normal** (key distinction from NSTEMI)
- Often progresses to NSTEMI if untreated

## 6. Type 1 vs Type 2 MI
- **Type 1** — primary coronary event (plaque rupture/erosion) → **NSTEMI/STEMI**
- **Type 2** — supply-demand mismatch (anaemia, AF with RVR, hypotension, anaesthetic) — common in hospitalised
- **Type 3** — sudden cardiac death with ischaemia (no biomarker)
- **Type 4a** — PCI-related (trop >5x ULN + new ECG/Q)
- **Type 4b** — stent thrombosis
- **Type 5** — CABG-related

## 7. Other ACS Mimics (Troponin ↑ but non-ACS)
- **Myocarditis**, Takotsubo
- **Pulmonary embolism**
- **Aortic dissection**
- **Acute HF**, **tachyarrhythmia**
- **Renal failure** (chronic troponin elevation)
- **Cardiac contusion**, **cardioversion**

## 8. Risk Stratification
- **GRACE** score — in-hospital and 6-month mortality
- **TIMI** score — 30-day death/MI/UR
- **HEART** score — 6-week MACE
- All incorporate **age, haemodynamics, ECG, biomarkers**

## 9. ECG Territory (STEMI)

| Leads | Artery |
|---|---|
| V1–V4 (anteroseptal) | LAD |
| I, aVL, V5–V6 (lateral) | LCx |
| II, III, aVF (inferior) | RCA (90%), LCx (10%) |
| V1–V2 ST depression (posterior) | RCA / LCx |
| V3R, V4R | RV branch of RCA |
| New LBBB | LAD |

## 10. Initial Management
- **STEMI** — emergent reperfusion: **primary PCI <90 min** (or fibrinolysis if not feasible <120 min)
- **NSTEMI/UA** — risk-stratify → early invasive (<24 h GRACE >140, refractory ischaemia, dynamic ST) vs ischaemia-guided
- **All**: MONA-B (morphine, O₂, nitrate, aspirin, β-blocker) — modern adjust: O₂ only if SpO₂<90, nitrates cautiously

> **Pearls**
> - **STEMI = occlusive**; **NSTEMI/UA = partial** thrombus
> - **Trop** distinguishes NSTEMI (↑) from UA (normal)
> - **Type 1 MI** = plaque rupture; **Type 2** = supply-demand mismatch
> - **Posterior MI** = ST depression V1–V3 + ST elevation V7–V9
> - **New LBBB** alone no longer diagnostic — use **Sgarbossa criteria**
> - **GRACE score** best validated for in-hospital and 6-month mortality
