CARDIOLOGY
Transesophageal echo (TOE) indications
1 Introduction

TOE places the probe in the oesophagus (posterior to LA), giving high-resolution images because of proximity and lack of lung/bone interference. Requires fasting ≥6 h, topical pharyngeal anaesthesia ± conscious sedation (midazolam ± fentanyl). Continuous monitoring (BP, ECG, SpO₂, ETCO₂).

2 Major Indications
A. Source of Embolism
  • Stroke / TIA, especially in young patients (<55 y)
  • Suspected LA / LAA thrombus before cardioversion of AF
  • PFO / ASD with right-to-left shunt (bubble study with agitated saline)
  • Aortic atheroma (plaques ≥4 mm = high risk)
  • Endocarditis (valve vegetations, especially prosthetic)
B. Infective Endocarditis
  • Gold standard for vegetations (sensitivity >90% vs 60% TTE)
  • Prosthetic valve endocarditis (TTE limited by acoustic shadowing)
  • Device leads (pace-maker, ICD vegetations)
  • Perivalvular abscess (early surgery indication)
  • Fungal / culture-negative endocarditis
C. Valvular Assessment
  • Mitral valve — pre-repair planning (anatomy, jet direction, scallop involvement P1/P2/P3)
  • Aortic valve — pre-TAVR sizing, regurgitation mechanism
  • Prosthetic valve dysfunction (TTE acoustic shadowing)
D. Aortic Pathology
  • Aortic dissection (Type A vs B, entry tear, true/false lumen)
  • Aortic intramural haematoma, penetrating ulcer
  • Aortic atheroma (especially arch in stroke workup)
E. Cardiac Masses & Pericardium
  • Characterise myxoma, fibroelastoma, thrombus, mets
  • Pericardial effusion / tamponade when TTE suboptimal
F. Intraoperative
  • Mitral valve repair, aortic valve surgery, PFO/ASD closure, HCM septal myectomy — confirm result, check for residual regurgitation
G. Critical Care
  • Unexplained haemodynamic instability, suspected aortic dissection, endocarditis with poor TTE windows (ventilated, post-cardiac surgery)
3 Absolute Contraindications
  • Unstable cervical spine, oesophageal stricture / tumour, active oesophageal bleeding, perforated viscus, severe coagulopathy (relative)
  • Caution: oesophageal varices, prior oesophageal surgery, active gastritis
4 Key Views (20 mid-oesophageal, 2 transgastric)
  • Mid-oesophageal 4-chamber (0°) — MV, TV, all chambers
  • ME AV SAX (30–60°) — aortic valve short axis
  • ME AV LAX (120°) — aortic valve long axis
  • ME LAA (90°) — LAA thrombus (the classic indication)
  • ME bicaval (90°) — SVC, IVC, interatrial septum
  • Transgastric SAX (0°) — LV (for EF in theatre)
5 Complication Rates
  • Major (oesophageal perforation, major bleed): <0.01%
  • Minor (sore throat, transient hypoxia, arrhythmias): 0.2–1%
Pearls - TOE > TTE for: LAA thrombus, prosthetic valve endocarditis, mitral valve repair planning, aortic dissection, PFO/ASD - LAA thrombus view at 0° and 90°; flow ≤0.4 m/s predicts thrombus - AF cardioversion: TOE-guided OR 3 weeks therapeutic anticoagulation before cardioversion
