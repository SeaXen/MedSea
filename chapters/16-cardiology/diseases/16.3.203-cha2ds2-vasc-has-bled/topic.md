# CHA2DS2-VASc & HAS-BLED Scores — Stroke & Bleeding Risk in AF

## 1. Purpose
Two complementary scores for AF anticoagulation decisions.
- **CHA2DS2-VASc**: stroke risk → who to anticoagulate
- **HAS-BLED**: bleeding risk → who needs closer monitoring / modifiable risk factor correction

## 2. CHA2DS2-VASc (Stroke Risk)
**Maximum score: 9**

| Letter | Criterion                                                  | Points |
|--------|-----------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| **C**  | **C**ongestive Heart Failure (HF or LVEF ≤40%)             | 1      |
| **H**  | **H**ypertension                                            | 1      |
| **A₂** | **A**ge ≥75                                                 | 2      |
| **D**  | **D**iabetes mellitus                                       | 1      |
| **S₂** | Prior **S**troke / TIA / systemic thromboembolism          | 2      |
| **V**  | **V**ascular disease (prior MI, PAD, aortic plaque)        | 1      |
| **A**  | **A**ge 65–74                                               | 1      |
| **Sc** | **S**ex **c**ategory (female)                               | 1      |

## 3. CHA2DS2-VASc → Anticoagulation Decision (ESC 2024 / ACC-AHA 2023)

| Score (Male) | Score (Female) | Recommendation                                |
|--------------|----------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| 0            | 0              | No anticoagulation                            |
| 1            | 1              | Consider oral anticoagulation (no female sex point alone) |
| ≥2           | ≥2 (or ≥3 with sex point) | Oral anticoagulation recommended |

> **Note:** In females, 1 point from "female sex" alone does not mandate anticoagulation; CHA2DS2-VASc score of 2 in females = same as score 1 in males (sex itself is modifiable risk factor accounting).

## 4. HAS-BLED (Bleeding Risk)
**Maximum score: 9** — score **≥3 = HIGH bleeding risk** (NOT a contraindication to anticoagulation, but flags need for closer monitoring + modifiable risk factor correction)

| Letter | Criterion                                                   | Points |
|--------|------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
| **H**  | **H**ypertension (uncontrolled, SBP >160)                   | 1      |
| **A**  | **A**bnormal renal function (Cr >200 µmol/L or dialysis)    | 1      |
|        | **A**bnormal liver function (cirrhosis, bilirubin >2×ULN)   | 1      |
| **S**  | Prior **S**troke                                            | 1      |
| **B**  | Prior major **B**leeding or predisposition                  | 1      |
| **L**  | **L**abile INR (TTR <60% on warfarin)                       | 1      |
| **E**  | **E**lderly (>65)                                           | 1      |
| **D**  | **D**rugs (antiplatelets, NSAIDs) or alcohol (≥8 drinks/wk) | 1 each |

## 5. Modifiable Bleeding Risk Factors to Address
- Uncontrolled hypertension (target <130/80 on anticoag)
- Labile INR (switch to DOAC if appropriate)
- Concurrent antiplatelet therapy (avoid unless ACS/stent)
- Alcohol excess
- NSAIDs (avoid)
- Fall risk (rarely contraindicates anticoagulation — benefits usually outweigh fall risk)

## 6. Other Stroke Risk Scores (for reference)
- **ABC-AF** (Age, Biomarkers [NT-proBNP, troponin], Clinical history) — better than CHA2DS2-VASc in some studies
- **GARFIELD-AF**, **ATRIA** — less commonly used in practice
- **CHA2DS2-VASc remains the standard** for ESC & ACC/AHA guidelines

## 7. Anticoagulation Choice
Once decision made to anticoagulate, choose:
- **DOAC preferred** over warfarin in non-valvular AF (RE-LY, ROCKET-AF, ARISTOTLE, ENGAGE-AF)
  - Dabigatran (thrombin inhibitor)
  - Rivaroxaban (FXa inhibitor)
  - Apixaban (FXa inhibitor)
  - Edoxaban (FXa inhibitor)
- **Warfarin** if: mechanical valve, moderate–severe MS, CrCl <30 (apixaban OK), poor adherence to DOAC monitoring

> **Pearls**
> - **CHA2DS2-VASc ≥2 (M) / ≥3 (F) → anticoagulate.**
> - **HAS-BLED ≥3 = high bleed risk, NOT a contraindication — correct modifiable factors.**
> - **DOAC > warfarin** in non-valvular AF for most patients.
> - **Annual review** of stroke + bleeding risk + anticoagulation indication.
> - **Female sex alone (score 1) does not mandate anticoagulation** (ESC 2024).
