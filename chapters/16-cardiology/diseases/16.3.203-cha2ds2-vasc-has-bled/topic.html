CARDIOLOGY
CHA2DS2-VASc & HAS-BLED Scores
1 Purpose

Two complementary scores for AF anticoagulation decisions.

  • CHA2DS2-VASc: stroke risk → who to anticoagulate
  • HAS-BLED: bleeding risk → who needs closer monitoring / modifiable risk factor correction

4 HAS-BLED (Bleeding Risk)

Maximum score: 9 — score ≥3 = HIGH bleeding risk (NOT a contraindication to anticoagulation, but flags need for closer monitoring + modifiable risk factor correction)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------
**H****H**ypertension (uncontrolled, SBP >160)1

5 Modifiable Bleeding Risk Factors to Address
  • Uncontrolled hypertension (target <130/80 on anticoag)
  • Labile INR (switch to DOAC if appropriate)
  • Concurrent antiplatelet therapy (avoid unless ACS/stent)
  • Alcohol excess
  • NSAIDs (avoid)
  • Fall risk (rarely contraindicates anticoagulation — benefits usually outweigh fall risk)
6 Other Stroke Risk Scores (for reference)
  • ABC-AF (Age, Biomarkers [NT-proBNP, troponin], Clinical history) — better than CHA2DS2-VASc in some studies
  • GARFIELD-AF, ATRIA — less commonly used in practice
  • CHA2DS2-VASc remains the standard for ESC & ACC/AHA guidelines
7 Anticoagulation Choice

Once decision made to anticoagulate, choose:

  • DOAC preferred over warfarin in non-valvular AF (RE-LY, ROCKET-AF, ARISTOTLE, ENGAGE-AF)
  • Dabigatran (thrombin inhibitor)
  • Rivaroxaban (FXa inhibitor)
  • Apixaban (FXa inhibitor)
  • Edoxaban (FXa inhibitor)
  • Warfarin if: mechanical valve, moderate–severe MS, CrCl <30 (apixaban OK), poor adherence to DOAC monitoring
Pearls - CHA2DS2-VASc ≥2 (M) / ≥3 (F) → anticoagulate. - HAS-BLED ≥3 = high bleed risk, NOT a contraindication — correct modifiable factors. - DOAC > warfarin in non-valvular AF for most patients. - Annual review of stroke + bleeding risk + anticoagulation indication. - Female sex alone (score 1) does not mandate anticoagulation (ESC 2024).
