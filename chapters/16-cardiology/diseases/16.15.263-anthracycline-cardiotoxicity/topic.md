# Anthracycline cardiotoxicity

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 15_Special_Populations | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**Anthracycline cardiotoxicity** (Dilated Cardiomyopathy, DCM): left ventricular (or biventricular) dilation and systolic dysfunction (LVEF <40-45%) not explained by abnormal loading conditions (HTN, valvular) or coronary artery disease. **Prevalence**: 1:500-2500. **Most common cause of HF in young adults**. **Aetiology**: **Genetic** (30-40%, autosomal dominant, TTN truncating 20-25% most common, LMNA, PLN, DSP, RBM20, others), **Inflammatory** (myocarditis, viral — parvovirus B19, HHV6, coxsackie, HIV), **Toxic** (alcohol, anthracycline, cocaine, amphetamines, cobalt, lead, mercury, methysergide, cyclophosphamide, chloroquine), **Metabolic** (thyroid, phaeochromocytoma, thiamine, selenium, carnitine), **Tachycardia-mediated** (chronic tachyarrhythmia, AF, SVT), **Peripartum** (last month pregnancy - 5m postpartum), **Endocrine** (acromegaly, Cushing), **Autoimmune** (SLE, RA, dermatomyositis, scleroderma), **Nutritional** (thiamine — wet beriberi, selenium deficiency, carnitine), **Iron overload** (haemochromatosis), **Idiopathic** (50%).


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Genetic**: TTN truncating variants (titin, sarcomere), LMNA (lamin A/C, nuclear envelope — conduction disease, AF, SCD), PLN (phospholamban, sarcoplasmic reticulum — arrhythmogenic, low voltage), DSP (desmoplakin, ARVC overlap), RBM20. **Viral**: direct cytotoxicity + immune-mediated. **Alcohol**: dose-dependent (>80-90g/day >5y), toxic to myocyte. **Anthracycline**: dose-dependent, free radical, irreversible. **Tachycardia**: rate >100-110bpm for weeks-months, reversible. **Peripartum**: late pregnancy - early postpartum, possibly 16-kDa prolactin fragment. **Mechanism**: myocyte loss/atrophy, fibrosis, dilation, eccentric remodelling, functional MR, HF.


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

**Symptoms**: dyspnoea (exertional, orthopnoea, PND), fatigue, exercise intolerance, palpitations, syncope, peripheral oedema, chest pain. **Examination**: displaced apex, S3, S4, JVP, TR, hepatomegaly, peripheral oedema, crepitations. **Signs of underlying**: alcohol, thyrotoxicosis, acromegaly, SLE, anthracycline. **Complications**: HF, thromboembolism (LV thrombus, AF, embolic stroke 1-3%/y), arrhythmia (VT/VF, AF, AV block especially LMNA), SCD. **SCD risk**: LVEF <35%, NSVT, syncope, family Hx, LMNA/PLN mutations.


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

**ECG**: LVH, QRS widening, LBBB, AF, low voltage (infiltrative), Q waves (non-specific), PVCs. **CXR**: cardiomegaly, pulmonary oedema. **BNP/NT-proBNP** elevated. **Echo** (essential): LV dilation (EDD >55-60mm or >2SD), LVEF <40-45%, global hypokinesia, MR (functional), RV function, thrombus. **CMR** (gold standard): LV/RV volumes, function, fibrosis (mid-wall LGE in DCM, vs subendocardial LGE in ischaemic), oedema (T2), infiltrative, myocarditis (Lake Louise criteria), fat (ARVC). **Coronary angiography** (exclude ischaemic, esp. >40y). **Endomyocardial biopsy** (gold standard for aetiology, but rarely done — myocarditis, amyloid). **Genetic testing** (TTN, LMNA, PLN, DSP, others, family cascade). **Family screening** (1st degree, echo, ECG). **Biomarkers**: BNP, troponin (chronic, myocarditis), iron studies, thyroid.


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**LVEF severity**: mild 35-45%, moderate 30-35%, severe <30%. **MAGGIC score**, **Seattle HF Model**, **INTERMACS**. **LMNA**: 10%/y SCD, ICD regardless of LVEF. **PLN**: 5%/y SCD, ICD. **TTN**: 1-2%/y SCD, ICD if LVEF<35%. **RBM20, DSP**: variable.


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**4 pillars of HF GDMT** (PARADIGM-HF, DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced, EMPEROR-Preserved): (1) **ARNI/ACEi/ARB**, (2) **Beta-blocker** (bisoprolol, carvedilol, metoprolol succinate), (3) **MRA** (spironolactone, eplerenone), (4) **SGLT2i** (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin). **Diuretics** for congestion. **Ivabradine** (HR≥70, sinus, max BB). **Digoxin** (symptomatic, sinus). **Anticoagulation** if AF (DOAC). **Device**: **ICD** primary prevention (LVEF ≤35% on GDMT 3m, NYHA II-III, life expectancy >1y, MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT). **Special** — **LMNA, PLN, DSP**: ICD regardless of LVEF (SCD high). **CRT** (LBBB QRS>150ms, LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II-IV). **LVAD, transplant** for advanced HF. **Avoid** in DCM: cardiotoxic drugs (alcohol, anthracyclines, cocaine), tachycardia (control rate). **Family screening**. **Exercise** (supervised, moderate).


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

**HF progression** (decompensated, ADHF, cardiogenic shock). **Thromboembolism** (LV thrombus, stroke, systemic, ~1-3%/y). **Arrhythmia** (AF 20-30%, VT/VF, SCD — 5-10%/y if severe). **Functional MR**. **Death** (5-yr 50% overall, worse with advanced).


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

**Pregnancy**: peripartum cardiomyopathy (last month - 5m postpartum, LVEF <45%, separate entity but DCM-like), bromocriptine (2.5mg BD), standard HF GDMT (avoid ACEi/ARB, MRA, ARNI, SGLT2i in pregnancy; safe: hydralazine, nitrates, beta-blocker, digoxin). **LMNA**: conduction disease (AV block, AF, SCD), screen annually. **TTN**: most common genetic, variable penetrance. **PLN**: Dutch founder mutation, low voltage, ventricular arrhythmias. **DSP**: ARVC overlap, fibrofatty. **Athletes**: differentiate athletic heart (eccentric, biventricular, bradycardia, normal diastolic function) from DCM (pathological).


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

**ESC 2021 HF**: DCM management. **2022 ACC/AHA/HFSA**: same. **2022 ESC/HFA**: cardiotoxicity. **2018 ESC Cardiomyopathy**: 4 phenotypes (DCM, HCM, ARVC, RCM), genetic testing, family screening. **MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT, DEFINITE**: ICD primary prevention. **DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced**: SGLT2i.


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

**Exclude ischaemic** in >40y. **CMR** for tissue characterisation, scar, infiltrative. **Family screening** (1st degree relatives, echo + ECG). **Genetic testing** if familial, young, conduction disease (LMNA), arrhythmogenic. **SCD risk** in LMNA, PLN, DSP — ICD regardless of LVEF. **Avoid** cardiotoxic (alcohol, anthracycline, cocaine). **Peripartum**: bromocriptine, anticoagulation (LVEF<35% during pregnancy and 6-8w postpartum), avoid ACEi/ARB/MRA. **LVEF in pregnancy**: 45% as threshold. **Don't delay** GDMT.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

**TTN**: 20-25% genetic. **LMNA**: conduction disease, SCD 10%/y, ICD regardless of LVEF. **PLN**: low voltage, SCD. **DSP**: ARVC overlap. **4 pillars**: ARNI, BB, MRA, SGLT2i. **Peripartum**: last month pregnancy - 5m postpartum, bromocriptine, avoid ACEi/ARB/MRA. **Anthracycline**: dose-dependent, irreversible. **Alcohol**: >80-90g/day >5y. **Athletic heart** vs DCM: eccentric, brady, normal diastolic, reversible detraining. **CRT**: LBBB QRS>150ms, LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II-IV. **ICD**: LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II-III, GDMT 3m, >1y. **MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT, DEFINITE**: primary prevention. **Avoid** in DCM: cardiotoxic, tachycardia.


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Ischaemic cardiomyopathy (CAD, Q waves, regional WMA), valvular (severe AS, AR, MR), hypertensive (LVH, then dilation), tachycardia-mediated, peripartum, myocarditis (acute, viral), infiltrative (amyloid, sarcoid, haemochromatosis), endocrine (thyrotoxicosis, acromegaly, phaeo), alcoholic, anthracycline, HIV, Chagas, ARVC, congenital, ventricular non-compaction (LVNC).


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

ECG, CXR, echo (essential, serial), CMR (gold standard, LGE patterns, infiltrative, myocarditis), coronary angio (exclude ischaemic), endomyocardial biopsy (selected), genetic testing, family screening, BNP, troponin, iron studies, thyroid, autoimmune, viral serology, HIV, RPR, selenium, thiamine, carnitine, urinary/plasma catecholamines (phaeo).

### 14.1 MCQs

**Q1.** What is the most common aetiology of Anthracycline cardiotoxicity?
A. Genetic predisposition
B. Acquired disease
C. Mixed aetiology
D. Idiopathic
E. Familial

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Most cases are acquired with genetic/environmental interaction. Recognise risk factors, presentation, and management.

**Q2.** First-line investigation for Anthracycline cardiotoxicity:
A. ECG
B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography
E. Cardiac biomarkers

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Echo is first-line for structural/functional assessment; additional tests as indicated.

**Q3.** Most appropriate first-line therapy for Anthracycline cardiotoxicity:
A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy
D. Surgical intervention
E. Multidisciplinary care

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Guideline-directed medical therapy is cornerstone; other modalities as indicated.

**Q4.** Which is a contraindication to therapy in Anthracycline cardiotoxicity?
A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

**Q5.** The most common complication of Anthracycline cardiotoxicity is:
A. Heart failure
B. Arrhythmia
C. Thromboembolism
D. Death
E. Quality of life reduction

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.
