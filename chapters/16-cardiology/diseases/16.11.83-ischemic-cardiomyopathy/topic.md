# Ischemic Cardiomyopathy

<callout icon="🫀" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Ischemic Cardiomyopathy — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**LV systolic dysfunction (LVEF ≤40%)** caused by **coronary artery disease** — prior MI, chronic ischaemia, or hibernating/stunned myocardium. Most common cause of HFrEF in the Western world (60–70%).

## 2. Pathophysiology
- **Myocyte loss** post-MI → fibrotic scar → adverse remodelling
- **Hibernating myocardium** — chronic hypoperfusion → viable but dysfunctional (recovers with revascularisation)
- **Stunned myocardium** — post-ischaemic dysfunction that recovers over time
- **Infarct expansion** → ventricular dilation → functional MR → progressive HF

## 3. Diagnosis
- **History** — prior MI, PCI/CABG, angina, risk factors
- **ECG** — Q waves, LBBB, ischaemic changes, AF
- **Echo** — regional wall motion abnormalities (RWMA), LVEF ≤40%, LV dilation, functional MR, viability assessment
- **Coronary angiography** — gold standard; defines revascularisation strategy
- **CMR** — gold standard for **viability**:
  - LGE (scar) — transmural scar >50% thickness → low recovery
  - Dobutamine stress CMR — contractile reserve predicts recovery
- **PET** — viability with FDG/glucose uptake

## 4. Viability Assessment
- **CMR LGE** — subendocardial scar (recoverable) vs transmural (>50% = poor recovery)
- **Dobutamine stress echo/CMR** — contractile reserve = viability
- **PET** — FDG uptake = viability (Hibernating Myocardium)

> **STICH trial** — CABG + medical therapy vs medical alone in LV dysfunction. **CABG improved long-term survival** in patients with LVEF ≤35% + CAD amenable to surgery. Viability testing did not predict which patients benefit (STICH viability substudy).

## 5. Management

### A. Medical (GDMT for HFrEF)
- **4 pillars** — ARNI/ACEi/ARB + BB + MRA + SGLT2i
- **Anti-ischaemic** — beta-blocker (first-line), nitrates, CCB (amlodipine/felodipine only)
- **Antiplatelet** — aspirin ± second antiplatelet (post-PCI/ACS)
- **Statin** — high-intensity (atorvastatin 40–80 mg / rosuvastatin 20–40 mg)
- **Sacubitril/valsartan** preferred over ACEi/ARB

### B. Revascularisation
- **PCI** — for 1–2 vessel disease, suitable anatomy, post-ACS
- **CABG** — preferred for **left main / 3-vessel disease** with reduced EF (STICH, FREEDOM follow-up)
- **Heart Team** decision (Syntax score, EuroSCORE, EF)
- **Revascularisation improves symptoms, EF (modestly), and survival in selected patients**

### C. Devices
- **ICD** — primary prevention (LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II–III, GDMT ≥3 mo, ≥40 d post-MI)
- **CRT** — if LBBB ≥150 ms
- **LVAD, transplant** — for advanced disease

## 6. Specific Scenarios
- **Acute ischaemia → cardiogenic shock** — immediate PCI (CULPRIT-SHOCK: culprit-only PCI)
- **Mechanical complications (VSR, papillary rupture)** — emergency surgery + MCS
- **Ventricular arrhythmia** — ICD, ablation, anti-arrhythmics (amiodarone)

## 7. Differentials
- **Non-ischaemic DCM** — no regional pattern, younger, different RWMA pattern
- **Takotsubo** — apical ballooning, normal coronaries, emotional trigger, recovers
- **Myocarditis** — acute presentation, ↑troponin, oedema on CMR

> **Pearls**
> - **Ischaemic CM is the most common cause of HFrEF** in the West
> - **STICH trial** — CABG in LVEF ≤35% + CAD improves survival
> - **CMR with LGE** — gold standard for scar/viability
> - **Always revascularise if viable myocardium + suitable anatomy**
> - **Heart Team** for revascularisation decisions
> - **PCI for culprit lesion in shock** (CULPRIT-SHOCK)
> - **Avoid verapamil/diltiazem** (negative inotrope)

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Ischaemic cardiomyopathy carries the **worst prognosis** of all HF aetiologies. **Revascularisation + GDMT + devices** is the trifecta. STICH showed long-term benefit of CABG in LV dysfunction. **CMR viability** guides revascularisation, though STICHES showed benefit even without proven viability in selected patients.
</callout>
