# Takotsubo Cardiomyopathy

<callout icon="🌸" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Stress Cardiomyopathy — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Transient LV systolic dysfunction** with characteristic **apical ballooning** (or other patterns) in the absence of obstructive CAD, triggered by **emotional or physical stress**. Also called **stress cardiomyopathy** or **broken-heart syndrome**. Predominantly postmenopausal women (~90%).

## 2. Mayo Clinic Diagnostic Criteria
1. Transient hypokinesis, akinesis, or dyskinesis of **LV mid-segments ± apex**
2. **Absence of obstructive CAD** or plaque rupture
3. **New ECG abnormalities** (ST elevation / TWI) or modest troponin elevation
4. **Absence of pheochromocytoma** or myocarditis

> **InterTAK score** — predicts probability of Takotsubo vs ACS (female sex, emotional trigger, psychiatric/neurological disorder, QTc prolongation, etc.).

## 3. Pathophysiology
- **Catecholamine surge** → direct myocyte toxicity, microvascular spasm, epicardial spasm, Ca²⁺ overload
- **Oestrogen deficiency** (postmenopausal) → loss of cardioprotection
- **Brain-heart axis** — emotional stress → amygdala activation → sympathetic discharge

## 4. Patterns (AHA Scientific Statement 2018)
| Type | Apex | Base |
|---|---|---|
| **Apical (apical ballooning) — most common** | Akinesis | Hyperkinesis |
| **Mid-ventricular** | Normal | Normal |
| **Basal (reverse)** | Hyperkinesis | Akinesis |
| **Focal / biventricular** | Variable | Variable |

## 5. Triggers
- **Emotional** — grief, anger, surprise, fear
- **Physical** — surgery, severe illness, ICU, SAH, stroke, sepsis, cocaine, adrenaline
- **No identifiable trigger** in ~30% (primary)

## 6. Clinical Features
- **Chest pain** (mimics ACS)
- **Dyspnoea**
- **Syncope, arrhythmia, cardiogenic shock** (rare but serious)
- ECG: ST elevation (anterior), TWI, QTc prolongation
- Troponin mildly elevated (disproportionate to wall motion abnormality extent)

## 7. Diagnosis
- **ECG** — ST elevation (anterior) or diffuse TWI, QTc ↑
- **Troponin** — modest rise (mismatch with extensive WMA)
- **NT-proBNP** — markedly elevated (mismatch)
- **Echo** — characteristic wall motion pattern, EF reduced
- **Coronary angio / CTCA** — **normal** (mandatory)
- **CMR** — gold standard: oedema (T2), no LGE, characteristic "apical ballooning"; excludes MI and myocarditis
- **Left ventriculogram** — "octopus pot" (takotsubo = Japanese octopus trap)

## 8. Complications
- **LV outflow tract obstruction** (~25% — basal hyperkinesis + SAM)
- **Cardiogenic shock**
- **LV thrombus** → systemic embolism
- **Ventricular arrhythmia** (torsades, VF)
- **Apical aneurysm** (rare)
- **Mortality** ~5% in-hospital (similar to ACS)

## 9. Management
- **Supportive** — most recover within days to weeks
- **Heart failure** — diuretics, standard GDMT (controversial — beta-blocker may help)
- **LVOTO** — beta-blocker (↓hyperkinesis), IV fluids, **avoid inotropes, vasodilators**
- **Cardiogenic shock** — small fluid bolus, **avoid inotropes** (worsen gradient) — consider **mechanical support** (IABP, Impella)
- **Anticoagulation** if LV thrombus or apical aneurysm
- **Avoid** — inotropes, vasodilators if LVOTO
- **Recovery** — EF normalises within 1–4 weeks in 90%
- Recurrence rate ~1–2%/year

> **Pearls**
> - **Takotsubo = "octopus pot"** — apical ballooning, hyperkinetic base
> - **90% postmenopausal women**, often with emotional trigger
> - **Troponin-BNP mismatch** — modest troponin, high BNP
> - **CMR** is the gold standard (no LGE, distinguishes from MI and myocarditis)
> - **LVOTO in 25%** — beta-blocker helps, inotropes harm
> - **Avoid inotropes, vasodilators** if LVOTO present
> - **Cardiogenic shock** — small fluid challenge, mechanical support
> - **Anticoagulation** if LV thrombus
> - **Recurrence** ~1–2%/year; **mortality** similar to ACS

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Takotsubo mimics ACS — **always perform coronary angio**. CMR differentiates from MI (no LGE) and myocarditis. **LVOTO occurs in 25%** and dictates therapy (beta-blocker, avoid inotropes). In-hospital mortality ~5%, similar to ACS — manage aggressively.
</callout>
