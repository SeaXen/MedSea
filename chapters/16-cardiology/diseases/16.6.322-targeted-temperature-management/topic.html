CARDIOLOGY
Targeted Temperature Management
1 Definition

Targeted Temperature Management (TTM): Active control of core body temperature in comatose survivors of cardiac arrest to improve neurological outcomes and survival. Replaces older "therapeutic hypothermia" terminology.

2 Indications
  • Comatose (GCS <8) adult survivors of out-of-hospital or in-hospital cardiac arrest
  • Shockable (VF/pVT) AND non-shockable rhythms
  • ROSC achieved; exclude other causes of coma (drugs, stroke, trauma)
3 TTM Protocols (TTM-2 Trial Era)
PhaseTargetDurationMethod
---------------------------------
**Induction**Rapid cooling to targetAs fast as possibleCold IV fluids (30 mL/kg), ice packs, cooling pads
**Maintenance****36.0°C** (current standard) OR 33°C24 hoursExternal cooling (Arctic Sun), intravascular
**Rewarming**0.25–0.5°C/hour~12–24 hControlled, slow rewarming
**Normothermia**<37.5°C72 h post-ROSCActive fever prevention
TTM-1 trial (2013): 33°C vs 36°C — no difference. TTM-2 trial (2021): Hypothermia (33°C) vs normothermia (<37.5°C) — no mortality or neurological benefit of deep hypothermia. Current practice: avoid fever (≤37.5°C) for 72 h.
4 Cooling Methods
  • External: Cooling blankets, ice packs, Arctic Sun (water-circulating pads)
  • Internal: IV cold saline (1–2 L), endovascular cooling catheters
  • Adjuncts: Ice to groin/axillae, cooling helmet
5 Monitoring
  • Core temperature: Bladder, esophageal, or rectal probes (NOT tympanic/axillary)
  • Continuous EEG (seizures occur in 20–30%)
  • Hemodynamics, MAP target >65 mmHg
  • Glucose, electrolytes, lactate
6 Complications
  • Shivering (counter-warming of extremities, buspirone, meperidine)
  • Bradycardia, hypotension
  • Coagulopathy, infection (pneumonia, sepsis)
  • Electrolyte shifts (K⁺, Mg²⁺, Ca²⁺)
  • Hyperglycemia
  • Rebound hyperthermia if rewarming too fast
7 Contraindications
  • Active bleeding, severe coagulopathy
  • Pregnancy (relative)
  • Refractory shock, multi-organ failure
  • DNAR status
Pearls - Avoid fever is the new mantra — keep ≤37.5°C for 72 h - Shivering ↑ metabolic demand — treat aggressively - Seizure prophylaxis controversial — treat if clinical/EEG seizures - Don't delay TTM for imaging, intervention if not needed - TTM improves survival with good neuro outcome (CPC 1–2 at 6 months)
