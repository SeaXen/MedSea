# Vascular Calcification in CKD

## 1. Types
- **Intimal** (atherosclerotic) — common in CKD + general atherosclerosis
- **Medial** (Mönckeberg's) — CKD-specific, vessels become "pipestem"
- Both contribute to CV risk

## 2. Pathogenesis
- ↑ phosphate → vascular smooth muscle cell osteogenic transformation
- ↑ calcium-phosphate product
- ↑ PTH (secondary hyperparathyroidism)
- ↓ vitamin D, ↑ FGF-23
- Uremic toxins

## 3. Clinical Impact
- **Arterial stiffness** → ↑ SBP, ↓ DBP, widened PP
- LV hypertrophy from pressure overload
- **Coronary artery calcification** → MI risk
- **Valve calcification** (AS)
- **Vascular access failure** in dialysis

## 4. Diagnosis
- **Coronary artery calcium score (CT)** — very high in dialysis
- Plain X-ray (vessel calcifications)
- Echo (valve calcification)
- **CAC score** useful for risk stratification

## 5. Management
- **Phosphate control** (binders, diet, dialysis)
- **Vitamin D analogs** (paricalcitol)
- **Calcimimetics** (cinacalcet) for SHPT
- **Avoid** calcium-based phosphate binders if possible
- **Statins** (modest benefit)
- **No proven benefit** of bisphosphonates
- **Treat BP** carefully

> **Pearls** — **Medial calcification** (Mönckeberg's) = "pipestem vessels" in CKD. Driven by **↑ phosphate**. Use **non-calcium phosphate binders**. CAC score often very high in dialysis but not always a clear action point.
