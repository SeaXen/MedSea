SEA KNOWLEDGE STUDY NOTES
CARDIOLOGY
Cardiovascular history taking
1 Introduction

History is the single highest-yield diagnostic tool in cardiology. Up to 80% of cardiac diagnoses are made on history alone, before any investigation. A structured, systematic approach is essential for FCPS / MRCP PACES.

2 Presenting Complaint
  • Chest pain / discomfort — site, character, radiation, duration, onset, precipitating/relieving factors, associated symptoms
  • Dyspnoea — exertional, orthopnoea, PND, trepopnoea
  • Palpitations — rate, regularity, onset/offset, duration, triggers
  • Syncope / presyncope — prodrome, witness account, recovery, injury
  • Oedema — ankles, sacrum, ascites
  • Fatigue, reduced exercise tolerance
  • Claudication, cyanosis, haemoptysis

Dyspnoea — NYHA Classification
  • I — No limitation of ordinary activity
  • II — Slight limitation (angina/dyspnoea on vigorous activity)
  • III — Marked limitation (symptoms on ordinary activity)
  • IV — Symptoms at rest
4 Past Medical & Risk Factor History
  • Ischaemic heart disease, MI, revascularisation (PCI / CABG dates)
  • Heart failure, valvular disease, arrhythmias, hypertension, diabetes, dyslipidaemia, stroke / TIA
  • Rheumatic fever, congenital heart disease, IE
  • Risk factors: age, sex, family (first-degree <55 ♂ / <65 ♀), smoking (pack-years), alcohol, obesity, sedentary lifestyle, OSA, CKD, autoimmune disease
  • Drug history: antiplatelets, anticoagulants, statins, antihypertensives, anti-anginals, anti-arrhythmics, diuretics, NSAIDs, COX-2, oral contraceptives, HRT, chemotherapy (anthracyclines, trastuzumab, immune checkpoint inhibitors)
5 Family & Social History
  • Premature CAD (<55 ♂ / <65 ♀ first-degree), SCD, familial hypercholesterolaemia (tendinous xanthomas, corneal arcus <45 y)
  • Smoking — current / ex / never, pack-years, passive
  • Diet — Mediterranean vs Western, salt intake
  • Exercise — METs per week
  • Occupation — sedentary, shift work, stress
  • Ethnicity — South Asian ↑CAD; African-Caribbean ↑HTN, HF; Afro-Caribbean response to ACEi/ARB
  • Travel — DVT risk, rheumatic fever exposure
6 Functional Inquiry
  • Constitutional: weight change, fever, night sweats
  • Respiratory: cough, wheeze, haemoptysis, PE symptoms
  • GI: nausea, vomiting, epigastric pain (inferior MI mimic)
  • Neurological: TIA/stroke symptoms, dizziness
  • MSK: claudication, rest pain
  • Psychiatric: anxiety, depression, panic (often co-exist with cardiac disease)
Pearls - In women, diabetics, elderly — typical angina symptoms are often replaced by dyspnoea, fatigue, nausea, back/jaw pain. - Always ask about dental procedures, recent infections, IV drug use (IE risk). - Family history of SCD <40 y → consider HCM, Long QT, Brugada, ARVC. - Recurrent admissions with "anxiety" — think paroxysmal AF / SVT.
