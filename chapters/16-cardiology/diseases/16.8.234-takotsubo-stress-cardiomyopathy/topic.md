# Takotsubo (Stress) Cardiomyopathy

## 1. Definition
**Takotsubo cardiomyopathy** ("broken heart syndrome", stress cardiomyopathy) = acute, reversible LV dysfunction (typically **apical ballooning** with preserved basal function) triggered by emotional/physical stress, mimicking ACS in absence of obstructive CAD.

## 2. Key Features
- **Demographics**: post-menopausal women (~90%); mean age 65–70
- **Trigger**: emotional stress (40%), physical stress (35%), no identifiable (30%)
- **Mechanism**: catecholamine surge → microvascular spasm, myocardial stunning, direct myocyte toxicity
- **Variants**: **apical** (classic, 80%), mid-ventricular, basal ("reverse"), focal, biventricular
- **ECG**: ST↑ (often anterior), T-wave inversion, QT prolongation
- **Biomarkers**: troponin mildly ↑ (out of proportion to ECG)
- **Echo**: apical akinesis/dyskinesis, hypercontractile base; LVEF ↓
- **CMR**: **no LGE** (helps differentiate from MI); myocardial oedema
- **Complications**: LV outflow tract obstruction, MR, cardiogenic shock, mural thrombus, VT, rare cardiac rupture

## 3. Management
- **Supportive** — most recover within days–weeks
- **GDMT for HFrEF** if reduced EF (ACEi/ARB, β-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i)
- **Anticoagulation** for LV thrombus (3 months)
- **Avoid inotropes** if possible (catecholamines worsen); use **mechanical support** (IABP, Impella) for shock
- **β-blocker** long-term may prevent recurrence
- **Psychological support**
- **Prognosis**: LVEF usually recovers by 1–4 weeks; recurrence 5%/year; mortality 5% in-hospital (driven by complications)

> **Pearls**
> - **Apical ballooning with basal sparing** on ventriculogram/echo
> - **Post-menopausal women** with emotional trigger
> - **CMR shows no LGE** (vs MI)
> - **Avoid catecholamines** in shock — use mechanical support
