# Fabry Disease

## 1. Definition
- **X-linked** lysosomal storage disease — **α-galactosidase A** deficiency
- Globotriaosylceramide (Gb3) accumulates in kidney, heart, brain, nerves
- Heterozygous females can have variable disease

## 2. Key Features
- **Cardiac**: LVH (mimics HCM), valve thickening, AR, conduction disease
- **Renal**: proteinuria, CKD
- **Neurologic**: acroparesthesia (burning hands/feet), stroke (vertebrobasilar)
- **Dermatologic**: angiokeratomas (bathing trunk), hypohidrosis
- **Cochleovestibular**: tinnitus, hearing loss
- **Ocular**: cornea verticillata

## 3. Diagnosis
- **α-Gal A enzyme** (males) — markedly low
- **GLA gene** testing (females, confirm)
- **Lyso-Gb3** biomarker (elevated)
- **CMR**: basal inferolateral LGE (not in HCM)
- **ECG**: short PR, LVH

## 4. Management
- **ERT**: agalsidase α, agalsidase β (regular infusion)
- **Oral chaperone** (migalastat) — only for amenable GLA mutations
- Standard HF, BP control
- ACEi/ARB for proteinuria
- Family screening

> **Pearls**: LVH in young + **neuropathic pain + proteinuria + stroke** → Fabry. **Cornea verticillata** is pathognomonic. **Basal inferolateral LGE** distinguishes from HCM.
