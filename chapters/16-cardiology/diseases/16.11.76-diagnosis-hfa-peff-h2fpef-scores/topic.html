CARDIOLOGY
Diagnosis (HFA-PEFF, H2FPEF scores)
1 Why Diagnose HFpEF?

HFpEF is underdiagnosed because symptoms are non-specific (dyspnoea, fatigue) and LVEF is "normal". Diagnostic scores stratify probability and guide further testing.

2 H2FPEF Score (Mayo Clinic)

Simple, non-invasive — 6 variables, each scoring 1–3, total 0–9.

VariablePoints
------
**H** — **Heavy** (BMI >30 kg/m²)2
**H** — **Hypertensive** (≥2 antihypertensives)1
**F** — **Atrial Fibrillation** (paroxysmal or persistent)3
**P** — **Pulmonary Hypertension** (PASP >35 mmHg)1
**E** — **Elder** (age >60 y)1
**F** — **Filling pressure** (E/e' >9)1

Interpretation:

  • 0–1 — low probability
  • 2–5 — intermediate (further testing)
  • 6–9 — high probability
3 HFA-PEFF Algorithm (ESC 2021)

Stepwise, 4 domains — Pre-test, Echocardiographic, Functional (stress), Final aetiology.

Step 1 — P (Pre-test assessment)
  • HF symptoms/signs, comorbidities, BNP/NT-proBNP
  • Exclude other causes of symptoms
Step 2 — E (Echocardiographic & natriuretic peptides)

Major criteria (2 points each):

  • Septal e' <7, lateral e' <10, E/e' ≥15, TR velocity >2.8 m/s
  • LA volume index >34 mL/m², LV mass index ≥115 g/m² (men) / 95 g/m² (women), RWT >0.42
  • NT-proBNP ≥220 pg/mL (SR) or ≥660 (AF)
  • LV GLS absolute <16%

Minor criteria (1 point each): moderate abnormalities in same parameters

Score ≥5 → diagnostic; 2–4 → functional testing

Step 3 — F (Functional testing)
  • Diastolic stress echo — exercise E/e' ≥15 or TR velocity >2.8 mHg
  • Invasive haemodynamics — PCWP >15 mmHg at rest, >25 with exercise (gold standard)
  • CPET — peak VO₂ <20 mL/kg/min, ↑VE/VCO₂ slope
Step 4 — F (Final aetiology)
  • CMR — fibrosis, amyloid, sarcoid, iron
  • DPD/PYP scan for ATTR amyloid
  • Genetic testing, biopsy if indicated
4 Key Echo Markers of HFpEF
  • LVEF ≥50% (mandatory)
  • LVH (concentric) — LV mass index ↑
  • LA enlargement — LAVI >34 mL/m²
  • Diastolic dysfunction — E/e' ≥14, septal e' <7
  • PASP >35 mmHg
  • TAPSE <17 — RV dysfunction
  • GLS <16% — subclinical systolic dysfunction
  • Septal flash / apical rocking — dyssynchrony
5 Differentials to Exclude
  • ATTR cardiac amyloidosis (always consider in elderly LVH + low voltage)
  • Cardiac sarcoid (CMR with LGE, FDG-PET)
  • Haemochromatosis (iron studies, CMR T2*)
  • Constrictive pericarditis (respiratory septal shift, Kussmaul, pericardial thickness)
  • Hypertensive heart disease with severe diastolic dysfunction
  • High-output states (anaemia, AV fistula, thyrotoxicosis)
  • Lung disease (COPD, ILD) — pulmonary causes of dyspnoea
Pearls - H2FPEF — quick bedside rule-out (BMI, HTN, AF, PASP, age, E/e') - HFA-PEFF — ESC gold standard, stepwise - Invasive PCWP with exercise is the gold standard for diagnosis - ATTR amyloid must be excluded in elderly with LVH (PYP/DPD scan) - GLS is more sensitive than EF for early dysfunction - NT-proBNP ≥220 (SR) or ≥660 (AF) is the ESC threshold

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg"> CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE — HFpEF is missed in up to 50% of cases. HFA-PEFF and H2FPEF scores guide further workup. Always exclude ATTR amyloid in elderly HFpEF with LVH — it's treatable (tafamidis). Invasive exercise haemodynamics (PCWP >25) remains the gold standard for difficult cases. </callout>
