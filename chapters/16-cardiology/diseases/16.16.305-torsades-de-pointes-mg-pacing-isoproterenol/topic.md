# Torsades de pointes Mg pacing isoproterenol

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 16_Cardiac_Emergencies | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**Torsades de pointes Mg pacing isoproterenol**: Ventricular Tachycardia (VT) — wide complex tachycardia (>120ms QRS, typically >140ms) of ventricular origin, ≥3 consecutive beats, rate >100 bpm (typically 150-250). **Sustained VT** ≥30s or requires intervention. **Non-sustained (NSVT)**: 3 beats to <30s. **Monomorphic** (single morphology) vs **polymorphic** (variable). ICD-terminated in ICD patients (VT/VF zone).


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Mechanisms**: (1) **Re-entry** (scar from MI, cardiomyopathy, after surgery), (2) **Abnormal automaticity** (ischaemia, hypoxia, electrolyte), (3) **Triggered activity** (early/late afterdepolarisations — torsades, digitalis). **Substrate**: post-MI scar, fibrosis (DCM, HCM, ARVC), infiltrative disease, RV dysplasia. **Triggers**: ischaemia, electrolyte (K+, Mg2+), drugs (Class I, III), catecholamines, bradycardia (pause-dependent), RV stretch (ARVC), alcohol.


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

Palpitations, dyspnoea, chest pain, syncope, presyncope, fatigue. **Haemodynamic instability** in 50% (SBP<90, shock, ischaemia, pulmonary oedema). **SCD** in untreated sustained VT (10-20%/year in post-MI). **NSVT**: often asymptomatic. **Examination**: tachycardia, hypotension, signs of HF, cannon A waves (AV dissociation).


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

**12-lead ECG**: wide complex QRS (>120ms), AV dissociation (P waves marching through), capture/fusion beats, concordance (all positive or all negative V1-V6), extreme axis (northwest), Brugada criteria (RS absent in precordials, RS interval >100ms, AV dissociation). **Holter** for NSVT. **EP study** for risk stratification and ablation. **Echo** (LVEF, scar, infiltrative). **CMR** (scar, fibrofatty infiltration — ARVC).


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**Haemodynamic instability** is the immediate trigger for emergency cardioversion. **Underlying substrate severity** dictates long-term risk: post-MI scar >30% LV EF, NSVT, syncope → 20% SCD/year. **EP study** inducibility predicts risk. **Brugada ECG pattern** = high SCD risk.


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**Stable VT**: IV amiodarone 5mg/kg over 20min, then 15mg/kg/24h; or procainamide (preserved EF, monomorphic), lidocaine (ischaemia/CM). **Unstable/VF**: DCCV synchronised 100-200J biphasic. **Pulseless VT/VF**: VF algorithm, shock + CPR. **Recurrent/electrical storm**: intubate, deep sedation, amiodarone, overdrive pacing, sympathetic blockade (esmolol, stellate ganglion block). **Long-term**: ICD (secondary prevention if survived SCD, sustained VT with haemodynamic compromise; primary prevention if LVEF≤35% + NYHA II-III + GDMT 3m + life expectancy >1y — MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT). **Catheter ablation** (substrate modification, scar re-entry — VTACH, VANISH, BERLIN VT). **Antiarrhythmics**: amiodarone, sotalol (preserved EF), mexiletine (LQT3).


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

**SCD**. **Cerebrovascular event** (hypoperfusion, AF/VF). **Heart failure** (cardiomyopathy, post-shock). **Post-shock complications** (skin burns, lead displacement, infection, anxiety). **Storms** (≥3 VT/VF in 24h — 50% mortality). **Cardiac arrest**.


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

**Post-MI**: most common in first year, especially with LVEF<35% — ICD primary prevention. **Bradycardia-dependent (pause-dependent) torsades**: pacing, withdraw offending drug. **Catecholaminergic (CPVT)**: beta-blocker, ICD, sympathetic denervation. **Short-coupled Torsades**: IV verapamil. **Brugada**: ICD, quinidine for arrhythmia suppression. **ARVC**: ICD, exercise restriction, ablation.


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

**ESC 2022 VA (EHJ)**: classification, SCD risk, ICD, ablation. **AHA/ACC/HRS 2017 VA**: similar. **MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT**: ICD primary prevention. **AVID, CIDS, CASH**: ICD secondary prevention. **VTACH, VANISH**: ablation. **Antiarrhythmic drugs**: amiodarone (survival benefit?), sotalol. **NICE NG211 (2021)**: VA.


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

Distinguish **VT vs SVT with aberrancy** (Brugada criteria, lead V1-V2 in RBBB, lead V6 in LBBB; if RBBB morphology — rsR' in V1, deep S in V6; if LBBB — wide R, no Q in V1, qR in V6). **Assume VT** in any wide complex tachycardia. **Polymorphic VT in ischaemia** = emergency revascularisation. **Torsades** = IV MgSO4 2g, withdraw drug, overdrive pacing. **Electrical storm** = deep sedation + amiodarone + sympathetic blockade + ablation. **Verify defibrillator function** (lead fracture, battery). **EP study in ARVC** — high risk. **Idiopathic VT** (RVOT — RBBB, inferior axis, young) — good prognosis, ablation.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

**VT vs SVT+aberrancy**: assume VT if wide complex, AV dissociation, capture/fusion, extreme axis, concordance. **Torsades**: polymorphic with sinusoidal axis, prolonged QT, MgSO4. **Brugada** ECG: pseudo-RBBB + ST elevation V1-V3. **CPVT**: stress-induced polymorphic VT, normal QT, RYR2 mutation. **ICD primary prevention**: LVEF≤35%, NYHA II-III, GDMT 3m, life expectancy >1y. **ICD secondary prevention**: survived SCD/sustained VT. **MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT**: ICD primary prevention. **VANISH, BERLIN VT**: ablation in ischaemic VT.


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

SVT with aberrancy (RBBB, LBBB, pre-existing), SVT with pre-excitation (WPW antidromic), antidromic AVRT, paced rhythm, ventricular paced, hyperkalaemia, severe metabolic disturbance, drug toxicity (sodium channel blockers — TCA, cocaine, flecainide).


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

12-lead ECG (during and after), Holter/event recorder (intermittent), echo (LVEF, scar, infiltrative), CMR (scar, infiltrative, ARVC, myocarditis), coronary angiography (ischaemia), EP study (inducibility, mapping, ablation), TFT, electrolyte, drug levels, genetic testing (LQT, Brugada, CPVT, ARVC).
