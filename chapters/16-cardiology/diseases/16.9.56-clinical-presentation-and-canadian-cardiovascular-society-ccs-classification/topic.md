## 1. Typical Angina — The Classic Triad
1. **Substernal** chest discomfort (pressure, heaviness, squeezing)
2. **Provoked** by exertion or emotional stress
3. **Relieved** by rest or nitrates within minutes

### Atypical (women, elderly, DM)
- Dyspnoea, fatigue, nausea, jaw / back / epigastric pain
- Higher threshold for suspicion

---

## 2. CCS Functional Class
| Class | Limitation | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **I** | None | Ordinary activity |
| **II** | Slight | Vigorous / rapid exertion |
| **III** | Marked | < Ordinary activity |
| **IV** | Symptoms at rest | **Unstable equivalent** — urgent workup |

### Atypical Chest Pain
- Pleuritic, positional, reproducible, brief (seconds) — usually **non-cardiac**
- Constant for hours — usually **not** angina

---

## 3. Examination & Risk Factors
- Usually normal; **S4, MR murmur** (papillary ischaemia), carotid bruit
- Signs of dyslipidaemia: xanthelasma, corneal arcus
- **Risk factors:** HTN, DM, dyslipidaemia, smoking, FHx of premature CAD, CKD, obesity

---

## 4. Pre-Test Probability (PTP)
ESC 2019 PTP table considers age, sex, symptom type (typical / atypical / non-anginal):
- **< 5%** — defer testing, treat risk factors
- **5–15%** — consider CAC scoring or stress imaging
- **> 15%** — non-invasive ischaemia testing or CTCA

---

## 5. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **Typical angina = all 3** of: substernal, exertional, relieved by rest/nitrates
- **CCS IV = angina at rest** — treat as unstable
- Atypical: think women, elderly, diabetics (dyspnoea, jaw / back pain)
- Use **ESC PTP table** to triage testing
- Risk factors: HTN, DM, smoking, dyslipidaemia, family, CKD
