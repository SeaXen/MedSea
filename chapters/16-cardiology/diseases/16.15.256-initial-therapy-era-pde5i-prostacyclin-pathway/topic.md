# Initial Therapy: ERA, PDE5i, Prostacyclin Pathway

## 1. Three Therapeutic Pathways in PAH

- **Endothelin Receptor Antagonists (ERAs):** Bosentan, ambrisentan, macitentan
- **Phosphodiesterase-5 inhibitors (PDE5i) / sGC stimulators:** Sildenafil, tadalafil; riociguat
- **Prostacyclin pathway agents:** Epoprostenol (IV), iloprost (inhaled), treprostinil (SC/IV/oral/inhaled), selexipag (oral)

## 2. Mechanism of Action
- **ERAs:** Block ET-1 mediated vasoconstriction and proliferation
- **PDE5i:** Increase cGMP → pulmonary vasodilation; riociguat directly stimulates sGC
- **Prostacyclins:** Vasodilation, anti-platelet, anti-remodeling via IP receptor

## 3. Drug-Specific Notes
- **Bosentan:** Monthly LFTs (hepatotoxicity), monthly pregnancy test (teratogenic)
- **Ambrisentan:** Lower hepatotoxicity, peripheral oedema
- **Macitentan:** Once-daily, no major LFT signal, robust event reduction (SERAPHIN)
- **Sildenafil:** 20 mg TID; avoid with nitrates; ocular/blue-tint side effect
- **Tadalafil:** Once-daily 40 mg; avoid with nitrates
- **Riociguat:** Useful in CTEPH and PAH; contraindicated with PDE5i
- **Epoprostenol:** Continuous IV infusion via pump; first-line in severe/high-risk PAH
- **Treprostinil:** SC preferred, or IV, oral, inhaled; site pain common with SC
- **Selexipag:** Oral IP-receptor agonist; start low, titrate

## 4. Initial Therapy Choice
Based on risk profile:
- **Low-risk PAH:** Oral monotherapy (ERA or PDE5i) acceptable
- **Intermediate:** Upfront **oral dual** (ERA + PDE5i) — AMBITION trial
- **High-risk / severe:** Parenteral prostacyclin ± oral combination; transplant referral

> **Pearls**
> - All PAH drugs are **teratogenic** — effective contraception mandatory.
> - Avoid combining riociguat with PDE5i (hypotension).
> - Upfront combination therapy is now standard for most PAH.
