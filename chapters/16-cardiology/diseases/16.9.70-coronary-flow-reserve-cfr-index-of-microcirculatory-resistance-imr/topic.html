CARDIOLOGY
Coronary flow reserve (CFR), index of microcirculatory resistance (IMR)
1 Definitions
  • CFR (Coronary Flow Reserve): ratio of hyperaemic to resting coronary flow
  • IMR (Index of Microcirculatory Resistance): distal pressure × mean transit time during hyperaemia
Cut-offs
IndexNormalAbnormal
---------
**CFR (thermodilution)**≥ 2.0< 2.0
**CFR (Doppler)**≥ 2.5< 2.5
**IMR**≤ 25> 25
**RFR (resistive reserve ratio)**≥ 2.0< 2.0
2 What They Measure
  • CFR integrates epicardial + microvascular function
  • IMR is specific to the microcirculation (independent of epicardial stenosis when FFR measured simultaneously)
  • Used together to phenotype INOCA
INOCA Endotypes (HMRF 2023)
  • Microvascular angina: FFR >0.80, CFR <2.0, IMR ≥25 (structural CMD)
  • Vasospastic angina: Acetylcholine → reproduction of pain + ECG changes + >90% constriction
  • Microvascular spasm: Acetylcholine → pain + ECG, normal epicoronary
  • Endothelial dysfunction: Acetylcholine → epicardial AND microvascular constriction
3 Clinical Use
  • Diagnose microvascular angina in patients with abnormal stress + normal angio
  • Prognostication in STEMI (IMR predicts LV recovery)
  • DM, hypertension, SSc, post-PCI — microvascular dysfunction common
  • Guide therapy (β-blocker vs CCB)
4 Non-Invasive Assessment
  • CMR stress perfusion → MBF (myocardial blood flow) quantification
  • PET — gold standard non-invasive CFR
  • Stress echocardiography (indirect)
5 Key Pearls
Pearls
  • CFR < 2.0 = microvascular dysfunction
  • IMR > 25 = structural microvascular disease (specific to microcirculation)
  • INOCA = Ischaemia + No Obstructive CAD — phenotype by FFR / CFR / IMR
  • Used for prognostication post-STEMI (IMR predicts LV recovery)
  • CMR / PET can measure myocardial blood flow non-invasively
