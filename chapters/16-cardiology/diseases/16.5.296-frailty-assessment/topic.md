# Frailty Assessment in Cardiovascular Disease

## 1. Why It Matters
- Frailty ↑ mortality, ↓ QoL, ↑ procedural risk
- Affects treatment decisions in elderly CV patients
- Reversible with intervention in some cases

## 2. Assessment Tools
- **Fried Frailty Phenotype** (5 criteria): unintentional weight loss, exhaustion, weakness (grip), slow walking speed, low activity
  - 0 = robust, 1–2 = pre-frail, ≥3 = frail
- **Clinical Frailty Scale** (CFS, Rockwood): 1–9 scale, quick
- **Essential Frailty Toolset (EFT)** — for TAVR, 4 items
- **FRAIL scale** — quick screening
- Gait speed (≤0.8 m/s = slow)
- Grip strength

## 3. In Cardiovascular Practice
- **TAVI** — frailty predicts outcomes; EFT used
- **Surgical AVR** — frailty predicts mortality
- **CABG** — frail pts may not benefit
- **HF** — frailty common (40% in HFpEF), affects outcomes
- **Anticoagulation decisions** in AF

## 4. Management
- **Pre-habilitation** — exercise, nutrition, optimize comorbidities
- **Multidisciplinary care** — geriatricians, PT/OT, nutrition
- **Shared decision-making** — discuss goals of care
- **Avoid over-treatment** in frail patients

> **Pearls** — **Assess frailty** in all elderly CV patients. **Fried criteria** (5 items) or **CFS** (1–9) are quick. Frailty is **modifiable** with pre-hab. Use for TAVI/SAVR decisions.
