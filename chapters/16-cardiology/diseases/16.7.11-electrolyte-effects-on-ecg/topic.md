## 1. Introduction
Electrolyte disturbances produce **predictable ECG patterns** and can precipitate life-threatening arrhythmias. Recognition is essential in PACES and emergency settings.

## 2. Potassium Disorders

### Hyperkalaemia (K+ >5.5 mmol/L)
| K+ (mmol/L) | ECG |
|---|---|
| 5.5–6.5 | Tall peaked T waves (narrow base, "tented") |
| 6.5–7.5 | Loss of P wave, PR prolongation, 1° AV block |
| 7.0–8.0 | Wide QRS, sine wave, severe conduction disease |
| >8.0 | Sine wave → VF/asystole |

**Treatment:**
- **Membrane stabilising:** Calcium gluconate 10% 10 mL IV (over 2–5 min) or calcium chloride 10 mL
- **Shift K+ into cells:** Insulin 10 U + 50 mL 50% dextrose; salbutamol nebulised; sodium bicarbonate if acidotic
- **Remove K+:** Cation exchange resins (calcium resonium), diuretics, dialysis

### Hypokalaemia (K+ <3.5 mmol/L)
- **ST depression, T wave flattening, prominent U waves** (V2–V4)
- Prolonged QT(U) → **torsades de pointes**
- Atrial and ventricular ectopy, AF
- Causes: diuretics, vomiting, diarrhoea, Cushing, Conn, RTA
- **Treatment:** IV KCl (max 20 mmol/h via central line); oral replacement; correct Mg

### Magnesium
- **Hypomagnesaemia:** prolonged QT, T wave changes, U waves, torsades, ventricular arrhythmia
- Often co-exists with hypokalaemia (refractory to K+ replacement alone)
- **IV MgSO4 2 g IV** in torsades, polymorphic VT, AF with WPW

### Calcium
- **Hypercalcaemia:** shortened QT, J waves (Osborn-like), Osborn wave (also hypothermia)
- **Hypocalcaemia:** prolonged QT, T wave changes, tetany, Chvostek, Trousseau

## 3. Sodium Disorders
- **Hypernatraemia / hyponatraemia** rarely cause direct ECG changes
- Severe hyponatraemia → non-specific ST-T changes, bradycardia

## 4. Other
- **Acidosis** → hyperkalaemia-like changes
- **Alkalosis** → hypokalaemia-like changes

> **Pearls**
> - **Tented T waves** in a patient with CKD or on ACEi/ARB/spironolactone → **hyperkalaemia until proven otherwise**.
> - **Prominent U waves + prolonged QT** → think hypokalaemia/hypomagnesaemia — give K+ and Mg²⁺.
> - **Sine wave ECG** (wide QRS merging with ST/T) is **pre-terminal** — treat as cardiac arrest.
> - Always correct **K+ and Mg²⁺ together** in arrhythmia management.
