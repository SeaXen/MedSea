# Peripartum Cardiomyopathy (PPCM)

## 1. Definition
- **Idiopathic cardiomyopathy** presenting with **LV systolic dysfunction** (LVEF <45%) toward the end of pregnancy or in the months following delivery, when no other cause of HF is identified
- Diagnostic criteria (ESC): development of HF in last month of pregnancy or within 5 months postpartum, **no other identifiable cause**

## 2. Epidemiology & Risk Factors
- Incidence: 1 in 1000–4000 births (geographic variation; ↑ in Nigeria, Haiti)
- Risk factors: **Multiparity, multifetal pregnancy, age >30, African ancestry, pre-eclampsia, hypertension, cocaine use, prolonged tocolysis**

## 3. Pathophysiology
- **Vascular hypothesis:** 16-kDa prolactin fragment (cleaved by cathepsin D) → endothelial apoptosis, capillary rarefaction
- **sFlt-1** (anti-angiogenic) overexpression in pre-eclampsia/PPCM
- Genetic susceptibility (TTN truncating variants)
- Possible viral and autoimmune triggers

## 4. Clinical Features
- Dyspnea, orthopnea, fatigue (often attributed to "normal" pregnancy)
- Palpitations, chest pain
- **Signs of LV failure:** S3, JVP ↑, pulmonary crackles, peripheral edema
- **Thromboembolism** (LV thrombus, mural) — 5–10%

## 5. Diagnosis
- **ECG:** LVH, nonspecific ST-T changes
- **Echocardiography:** LVEF <45% (often <30%), LV end-diastolic diameter >2.7 cm/m², RV involvement
- **Cardiac MRI** with LGE (avoid gadolinium in pregnancy/lactation)
- **NT-proBNP** markedly elevated
- Rule out: pre-existing DCM, hypertensive heart disease, amniotic fluid embolism

## 6. Management
- **Acute HF:** Oxygen, diuretics (furosemide — safe), β-blockers (avoid atenolol), hydralazine + ISDN (avoid ACEi/ARB/ARNI in pregnancy)
- **Postpartum:** Standard GDMT (ACEi, β-blockers, MRA, SGLT2 — stop breastfeeding if SGLT2i)
- **Bromocriptine** 2.5 mg BID × 2 weeks (off-label; suppress prolactin)
- **Anticoagulation** if LVEF <35% (LMWH antepartum, warfarin postpartum)
- **Devices:** Wearable defibrillator (life-vest) if LVEF <35% initially; reassess at 3–6 months
- **Delivery:** Planned; consider early delivery if severe

## 7. Prognosis
- **~50–70% recover** LV function within 6 months
- Poor prognosis if LVEF remains <30% at 6 months, LV end-diastolic diameter >6.0 cm, RV involvement
- **Subsequent pregnancy:** High recurrence (20–50%) — counsel carefully

> **Pearls**
> - "New HF in last month of pregnancy or 5 months postpartum" = think PPCM
> - Bromocriptine is mechanistically appealing but not universally guideline-mandated
> - NT-proBNP is the most useful biomarker to distinguish cardiac dyspnea from "normal pregnancy"
