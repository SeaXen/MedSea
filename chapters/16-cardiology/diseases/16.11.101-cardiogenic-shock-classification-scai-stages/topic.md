# Cardiogenic Shock — SCAI Classification

## 1. SCAI Staging (2019)
| Stage | Description | Hemodynamics |
|---|---|---|
| **A — At risk** | Large MI, acute on chronic HF | Normal |
| **B — Beginning** | Hypotension, no hypoperfusion | SBP <90, no signs |
| **C — Classic** | Hypoperfusion requiring intervention | CI <2.2, PCWP >15, hypotension |
| **D — Deteriorating** | Not responding to initial measures | Worsening despite drugs ± IABP |
| **E — Extremis** | Cardiac arrest, refractory shock | CPR, ECMO candidates |

## 2. Etiology
- Acute MI with LV failure (~80%)
- Mechanical complications (VSD, papillary rupture, free wall)
- Acute decompensated chronic HF
- Myocarditis, takotsubo
- Post-cardiotomy

## 3. Initial Management
- **Inotropes** — dobutamine, milrinone, norepinephrine
- **Fluid** — cautious; avoid in RV infarct if not preload-dependent
- **Mechanical circulatory support** — escalate by stage (see MCS topic)

## 4. Mortality
- Stage C: 30–40%
- Stage D: 50–60%
- Stage E: >80%

> **Pearls** — **Norepinephrine** preferred over dopamine (less arrhythmia). Escalate to MCS early in D/E. Address **mechanical complications** (echo!). Multidisciplinary shock team improves outcomes.
