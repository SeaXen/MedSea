# Physiologic changes in pregnancy

## 1. Cardiovascular Adaptations
Pregnancy induces profound hemodynamic changes that peak in the **second trimester** and plateau near term:
- **Blood volume:** ↑ 30–50% (peaks ~32 weeks), driven by renin-angiotensin-aldosterone activation and estrogen-mediated sodium retention
- **Cardiac output (CO):** ↑ 30–50% (5–7 L/min), from ↑ stroke volume and ↑ heart rate (+10–20 bpm)
- **Systemic vascular resistance (SVR):** ↓ 25–30% (progesterone-mediated vasodilation, placental low-resistance circuit)
- **Blood pressure:** Mid-trimester drop (~10 mmHg), returns to baseline near term
- **Renal blood flow:** ↑ 50–80%, GFR ↑ 50% (creatinine falls, normal <0.85 µmol/L)

## 2. Labor and Delivery
- **Each contraction** autotransfuses ~300–500 mL, ↑ CO by 15–30% and ↑ SBP
- **Immediate post-delivery:** Relief of caval compression + autotransfusion → transient ↑ CO (can rise 60–80% above pre-labor) and ↑ in venous return
- **Risk window:** Highest cardiac risk in the **first 24–48 h postpartum** and up to 6 weeks (mWHO risk persists)

## 3. Physical Exam Findings (Often Misinterpreted)
- **Flow murmurs** (pulmonic, supraclavicular) — common, benign
- **S3 gallop** in ~80% — usually physiologic
- **Loud S1, split S2**
- **Peripheral edema**, mild dyspnea, fatigue — overlap with HF symptoms
- **Mild cardiomegaly** on CXR, left-axis deviation on ECG

## 4. Implications for Women with Heart Disease
- Patients with **limited cardiac reserve** (severe AS, Eisenmenger, PH, LV dysfunction) may decompensate
- Pre-conception counseling and **mWHO risk classification** guide management
- Multidisciplinary team (MDT) planning for delivery mode, analgesia, monitoring

> **Pearls**
> - CO peaks at ~32 weeks; delivery does not instantly reverse the cardiovascular burden
> - Always interpret NT-proBNP and ECG/echo against pregnancy-adjusted reference ranges
> - Normal pregnancy: ↓ SVR + ↑ CO = high-output, low-resistance state
