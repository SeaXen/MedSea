CARDIOLOGY
Doppler echocardiography (valvular gradients, shunt quantification)
1 Introduction

Doppler uses the frequency shift of reflected ultrasound to measure blood velocity. The modified Bernoulli equation (ΔP = 4v²) converts velocity to pressure gradient — the cornerstone of valvular assessment.

2 Doppler Modalities
ModeUse
------
**Pulsed-wave (PW)**Low velocities; **localised** (sample volume at specific depth); aliasing above Nyquist limit
**Continuous-wave (CW)**High velocities; **no aliasing**; cannot localise
**Colour flow**Direction + turbulence; BART (Blue Away, Red Towards) convention; **mosaic** = turbulence
**Tissue Doppler (TDI)**Low-velocity myocardial motion (e', a', s')
3 Key Equations
  • Modified Bernoulli: ΔP = 4v² (peak gradient)
  • Mean gradient: planimetered from CW trace
  • Continuity equation (AS area): AVA = LVOT area × (VTI_LVOT / VTI_AV)
  • PISA (regurgitation): EROA = 2πr² × aliasing velocity / peak regurgitant velocity
  • RVSP (PASP): 4 × (TR Vmax)² + RAP (RAP from IVC: 3 / 8 / 15 mmHg)
4 Valvular Stenosis Assessment
Aortic Stenosis
  • Peak velocity (Vmax): mild <3.0, moderate 3.0–4.0, severe >4.0 m/s
  • Mean gradient: severe >40 mmHg
  • AVA (continuity): severe ≤1.0 cm²; indexed ≤0.6 cm²/m²
  • Dimensionless index (LVOT V/AV V): severe <0.25
  • DVI <0.25 = severe even with low EF
Mitral Stenosis
  • Mean gradient: severe >10 mmHg
  • MVA planimetered (severe <1.5 cm²)
  • MVA by PHT = 220 / PHT; severe <1.5 cm²
  • Wilkins score for balloon valvuloplasty suitability

6 Shunt Quantification
ASD / VSD
  • Qp/Qs = (RVOT diameter × VTI_RVOT) / (LVOT diameter × VTI_LVOT)
  • Qp/Qs 1.0–1.5 = small; ≥1.5 = significant (closure indicated)
  • PASP from TR jet
PFO
  • Bubble study with agitated saline + Valsalva: bubbles cross to LA within 3–5 cardiac cycles
  • Massive shunt if uncountable bubbles
PDA
  • Continuous flow in main PA from LPA ostium
  • Qp/Qs calculation; mean PA pressure from CW
7 Diastolic Function (Simplified)
  • E/A ratio (MV inflow): 0.8–1.5 normal; <0.8 = impaired relaxation; >2 = restrictive
  • E/e' septal: <8 normal, 8–14 grey, >14 elevated LV filling pressure
  • TR velocity >2.8 m/s → pulmonary hypertension
  • LAVI >34 mL/m² → chronic LA pressure elevation
8 Pitfalls
  • Pressure recovery (especially in AS) — overestimates gradient in small roots
  • Underestimation of regurgitation with eccentric jets (use PISA)
  • Echocardiographic vs catheter gradient differ; Doppler is higher (no pressure recovery)
  • Swan-Ganz vs echo PASP can differ by 5–10 mmHg
Pearls - ΔP = 4v² is the universal haemodynamic equation - AS severe: Vmax >4 m/s, mean PG >40 mmHg, AVA <1.0 cm² - PISA = 2πr² × Va / Vmax; EROA ≥0.4 cm² = severe MR/TR - E/e' >14 = elevated LV filling pressure (diastolic dysfunction) - Qp/Qs >1.5 = significant left-to-right shunt (ASD/VSD)
