# DOACs vs Warfarin — Oral Anticoagulation in Non-Valvular AF

## 1. Overview
**DOACs** (Direct Oral AntiCoagulants) = NOACs = target-specific oral anticoagulants.
Four agents approved for non-valvular AF (NVAF):
- **Dabigatran** — direct **thrombin (IIa)** inhibitor
- **Rivaroxaban** — direct **factor Xa** inhibitor
- **Apixaban** — direct **factor Xa** inhibitor
- **Edoxaban** — direct **factor Xa** inhibitor

Warfarin = vitamin K antagonist (VKA), still used in specific situations.

## 2. Landmark Trials (vs Warfarin in NVAF)

| Trial     | Drug        | Dose            | Stroke/SE vs Warfarin | Major Bleed vs Warfarin | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|------------------------|--------------------------|-------|
| RE-LY     | Dabigatran  | 150 mg BID      | ↓ 35%                  | similar                  | 110 mg BID = non-inferior |
| ROCKET-AF | Rivaroxaban | 20 mg OD        | non-inferior           | similar                  | CrCl 30–49 → 15 mg |
| ARISTOTLE | Apixaban    | 5 mg BID        | ↓ 21%                  | ↓ 31%                    | Best safety profile |
| ENGAGE-AF | Edoxaban    | 60 mg OD        | non-inferior           | ↓ 20%                    | CrCl 15–50 → 30 mg |

## 3. DOAC Dosing in Renal Impairment

| Drug       | Standard | CrCl 30–49       | CrCl 15–29         | CrCl <15 / Dialysis |
|------------|----------|------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Dabigatran | 150 mg BID | 150 mg BID       | **Avoid** (75 mg BID in US only) | Avoid |
| Rivaroxaban | 20 mg OD | **15 mg OD**     | **Avoid**           | Avoid |
| Apixaban   | 5 mg BID | 5 mg BID         | **2.5 mg BID** if ≥2: age ≥80, weight ≤60 kg, Cr ≥1.5 | 2.5 mg BID (limited data) |
| Edoxaban   | 60 mg OD | **30 mg OD**     | **30 mg OD**        | Avoid |

## 4. Warfarin — When Still Preferred
- **Mechanical heart valve** (RE-ALIGN: dabigatran ↑ events) — DOACs **contraindicated**
- **Moderate–severe mitral stenosis** (rhythm control issue + high stroke risk)
- **CrCl <30 mL/min** (apixaban may be used with caution)
- **Significant drug interactions / poor adherence** to DOAC monitoring (warfarin INR check enforces compliance)
- **Pregnancy** (warfarin teratogenic; LMWH preferred)
- **Cost** — warfarin cheap, DOACs expensive (generic versions now available)

## 5. Practical DOAC Initiation Checklist
- **Weight, age, renal function** (Cockcroft-Gault, NOT eGFR)
- **Bleeding risk** (HAS-BLED, prior bleed, anaemia)
- **Concomitant drugs** (check P-gp/CYP3A4 interactions)
  - Strong P-gp/CYP3A4 inhibitors (ketoconazole, ritonavir) → avoid or dose-reduce
  - Strong CYP3A4 inducers (rifampin, carbamazepine, phenytoin, St John's Wort) → **avoid all DOACs**
- **Adherence assessment** (DOACs have short half-life; missed doses → loss of protection)
- **Baseline labs**: CBC, BMP (Cr), LFTs, coagulation
- **Avoid** if active bleeding, recent high-risk surgery, plt <50

## 6. Reversal Agents

| Drug        | Reversal Agent | Mechanism                          |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------------------------|
| Dabigatran  | **Idarucizumab** (Praxbind) | monoclonal antibody fragment |
| Rivaroxaban / Apixaban | **Andexanet alfa** (Andexxa) | recombinant factor Xa decoy |
| Edoxaban    | Andexanet alfa (off-label)   |                                   |
| Warfarin    | **Vitamin K** (slow) + **4-factor PCC** (urgent) or FFP | replace clotting factors |

## 7. Perioperative Management
- **Low bleeding-risk procedure**: stop DOAC 24 h before; resume 24 h after
- **High bleeding-risk procedure / neuraxial**: stop 48 h before (CrCl normal)
- **CrCl <30**: stop **≥72 h** before high-risk procedure
- **Bridging with LMWH**: NOT needed for DOACs (short half-life); may ↑ bleeding
- **Spinal/epidural**: specific timing rules — minimum 24–72 h gap depending on DOAC

> **Pearls**
> - **DOACs are first-line** for non-valvular AF (better efficacy/safety, fixed dosing, no monitoring).
> - **Apixaban** has the best bleeding profile in ARISTOTLE.
> - **Renal function by Cockcroft-Gault**, not eGFR.
> - **Idarucizumab** reverses dabigatran; **andexanet** reverses anti-Xa DOACs.
> - **Warfarin remains preferred** for mechanical valves and moderate–severe MS.
> - **No routine monitoring** for DOACs, but check adherence + renal function **annually** (more often if CrCl <60).
