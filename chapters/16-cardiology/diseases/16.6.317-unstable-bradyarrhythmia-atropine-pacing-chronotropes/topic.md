# Unstable bradyarrhythmia (atropine, pacing, chronotropes)

## 1. Definition
**Unstable bradyarrhythmia**: HR <50 bpm with signs of hypoperfusion (hypotension, altered mental status, chest pain, shock, syncope). Includes sinus bradycardia, AV blocks (2° Mobitz II, 3°/complete), junctional rhythm, idioventricular rhythm.

## 2. Etiology
- **MI** (esp. inferior — RCA supplies SA/AV node)
- **Drugs**: Beta-blockers, CCBs, digoxin, amiodarone
- **Electrolyte**: Hyperkalemia
- **Increased vagal tone** (carotid sinus, vasovagal)
- **Sinus node dysfunction** (sick sinus syndrome)
- **Conduction disease** (Lenègre, Lev disease)
- **Post-cardiac surgery**, hypothyroidism, hypothermia, ↑ICP

## 3. Clinical Features
- Hypotension, syncope, pre-syncope
- Altered mental status, fatigue
- Chest pain, dyspnea
- Stokes-Adams attacks (syncope in complete heart block)

## 4. Management Algorithm
1. **Assess ABCs**, IV access, cardiac monitor, 12-lead ECG
2. **Identify and treat reversible causes** (drugs, ischemia, electrolytes)
3. **Atropine 1 mg IV** (repeat q3–5 min, max 3 mg)
   - Effective in **symptomatic sinus bradycardia, junctional rhythm, Mobitz I (Wenckebach)**
   - **Often ineffective in Mobitz II and complete heart block** (AV node issue)
4. **If atropine fails**:
   - **Transcutaneous pacing** (TCP) — immediate bridge
   - **Chronotropes**: Dopamine 5–20 µg/kg/min OR epinephrine 2–10 µg/min
   - **Isoproterenol** 2–10 µg/min (avoid in ischemic disease)
5. **Transvenous pacing** if persistent — placement by experienced operator
6. **Permanent pacemaker** for irreversible causes

## 5. AV Block Management
| Block | Atropine Response | Treatment |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|
| **1° AV block** | Usually no issue | Observe, treat cause |
| **Mobitz I (Wenckebach)** | May respond | Atropine; rarely pacing |
| **Mobitz II** | **Poor** | **Pacing (TCP → TV → PPM)** |
| **3° (Complete)** | **Poor** | **Pacing urgently** |
| **Inferior MI + block** | May respond | Atropine; usually resolves |

## 6. Transcutaneous Pacing (TCP)
- Apply pads (anterior-posterior or sternal-apex)
- Set demand mode at HR 60–80
- Increase mA until capture (visible QRS with T wave opposite to pacer spike)
- Confirm mechanical capture (pulse, BP)
- Sedation for comfort

> **Pearls**
> - **Mobitz II and 3° block**: atropine often fails — go directly to pacing
> - **Inferior MI** with block: usually AV nodal (responds to atropine, resolves)
> - **Anterior MI** with block: infranodal (wide QRS, escape) — needs pacing
> - **Isoproterenol** avoided in CAD/STEMI (↑ O₂ demand)
> - Don't forget **"PACE"** mnemonic — Prepare, Alert, Capture, Effective
