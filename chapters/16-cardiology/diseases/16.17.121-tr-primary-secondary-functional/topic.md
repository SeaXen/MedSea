# Tricuspid Regurgitation (TR)

## 1. Primary (organic, ~10%)
- Endocarditis (IVDU), rheumatic, carcinoid, Ebstein anomaly, trauma, iatrogenic (lead/pacing)

## 2. Secondary (functional, ~90%)
- **Annular dilation** from RV pressure/volume overload
- Causes: L-sided heart disease (most common), pulmonary HTN, AF, RV dilation
- Leaflets are structurally normal

## 3. Severity
- **Mild** — normal-looking echo
- **Moderate** — incomplete coaptation
- **Severe** — wide jet, large VC, hepatic vein systolic reversal, RA/RV dilation

## 4. Management
- **Medical**: diuretics, treat underlying cause (LV dysfunction, pulmonary HTN)
- **Surgical** (TV repair, usually ring annuloplasty) at time of left-sided surgery OR isolated if severe + symptomatic
- **Transcatheter** (TriClip) — for high-risk surgical candidates (TRILUMINATE trial)

> **Pearls** — TR is usually **secondary**, treat the **left side** first. Isolated TV surgery high-risk. New **TriClip** for inoperable patients. Severe TR after left-sided surgery = poor prognosis.
