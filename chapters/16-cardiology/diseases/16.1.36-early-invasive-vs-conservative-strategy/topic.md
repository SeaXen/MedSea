# Early Invasive vs Conservative Strategy

<callout icon="🎯" color="blue_bg">
**Topic:** Early Invasive vs Conservative Strategy in NSTE-ACS — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Early invasive strategy** = routine coronary angiography ± revascularisation within 24–72 h of admission in NSTE-ACS. **Conservative (ischaemia-guided)** strategy = medical therapy first, with angiography reserved for recurrent ischaemia or high-risk findings.

## 2. Strategy Comparison

| Feature | Early Invasive | Conservative |
|---|---|---|
| **Angiography timing** | Within 24–72 h | Only if recurrent ischaemia or failed medical therapy |
| **Recurrent ischaemia** | Lower | Higher |
| **Length of stay** | Shorter (if uncomplicated) | Longer (often) |
| **Bleeding risk** | Higher (procedural) | Lower |
| **Mortality benefit** | Yes (high-risk) | Less clear |
| **Revascularisation** | Higher rate | Lower rate |

## 3. Who Benefits Most (Class I) — Early Invasive
- **GRACE score >140** (high risk)
- **Recurrent/refractory ischaemia** at rest
- **Dynamic ST changes**
- **Elevated cardiac biomarkers**
- **Haemodynamic instability** (shock, pulmonary oedema)
- **Life-threatening arrhythmia** (VF, sustained VT)
- **Post-MI angina**
- **New LBBB**
- **High TIMI risk score (≥3)**
- **Diabetes mellitus**
- **CKD (eGFR 30–60)** (cautiously)
- **LVEF <40%**

## 4. Delayed vs Immediate Invasive

| Timing | Indication | Trials |
|---|---|---|
| **<2 h (immediate)** | Refractory ischaemia, shock, mechanical complication | Clinical urgency |
| **<24 h (early)** | GRACE >140, dynamic ST change, high troponin | TIMACS, VERHEGGEN |
| **<72 h** | GRACE 109–140, intermediate risk | Standard |
| **>72 h / non-invasive** | GRACE <109, low risk, women (controversial) | ICTUS, RITA-3 |

## 5. Conservative Strategy — When Appropriate
- **GRACE <109** (low risk)
- **No recurrent ischaemia**
- **Normal troponin**
- **Significant comorbidities** where invasive risk outweighs benefit
- **Patient preference**
- **No high-risk features**

## 6. Special Populations
- **Elderly** (>75): benefit maintained but higher bleeding — careful selection
- **Women**: less robust benefit (ICTUS); not worse with invasive if high-risk
- **CKD**: benefit may be greater, but contrast risk; weigh carefully
- **Diabetes**: clear benefit (FREEDOM, but in stable CAD)
- **Post-CABG**: consider graft disease; native vessel PCI often preferred

## 7. Trial Evidence
- **FRISC-II**, **TACTICS-TIMI 18**, **RITA-3**, **ICTUS** — early invasive <conservative in high-risk
- **TIMACS** — early (<24 h) best in GRACE >140
- **VERHEGGEN / ABOARD** — immediate not superior to early (unless refractory)

## 8. Practical Pathway
1. **Diagnose** NSTE-ACS (ECG + troponin)
2. **Risk-stratify** (GRACE / TIMI)
3. **Initiate DAPT + anticoagulation** (see other notes)
4. **High-risk → early invasive (<24 h)**
5. **Intermediate → invasive within 72 h**
6. **Low-risk → stress test or CTCA**; invasive if positive
7. **Angiography → PCI, CABG, or medical therapy** based on anatomy

## 9. Complications of Invasive Strategy
- **Access site bleeding** (radial < femoral)
- **Contrast nephropathy** (esp. CKD, diabetics)
- **Stent thrombosis / restenosis**
- **Stroke** (rare, ~0.1%)
- **Periprocedural MI**

> **Pearls**
> - **GRACE >140** = early invasive (<24 h); **GRACE <109** = conservative
> - **Recurrent ischaemia, dynamic ST change, shock** = urgent (<2 h) angiography
> - **Routine early invasive** reduces death/MI in high-risk NSTE-ACS
> - **Conservative strategy** reserved for low-risk patients
> - **Radial access** preferred (lower bleeding, MATRIX trial)
> - **CKD/diabetics** benefit from invasive; **bleeding risk** higher
