# Penetrating Aortic Ulcer (PAU)

<callout icon="🩺" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Penetrating Aortic Ulcer — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Atherosclerotic ulcer that erodes through the intima into the media** — focal, usually in the **descending thoracic aorta**. Forms part of acute aortic syndrome spectrum.

### Distinguishing from IMH and Dissection
- **Ulcer crater** visible in aortic wall (vs no flap in IMH)
- Localised, not propagated
- Often multiple, in heavily calcified aorta

---

## 2. Risk Factors
- **Elderly hypertensive** (typical patient)
- **Atherosclerosis** (heavy aortic plaque burden)
- Smoking, hyperlipidaemia
- Less common in connective tissue disease

---

## 3. Clinical Features
- Acute chest / back pain (similar to dissection)
- Can be asymptomatic (incidental CT finding)
- May cause **haemothorax** if ruptures
- May progress to IMH, dissection, pseudoaneurysm, or rupture

---

## 4. Imaging
- **CT angiography:** focal contrast-filled outpouching in aortic wall, often with surrounding haematoma
- **MRI:** crater with surrounding high signal (oedema / blood)
- **TEE:** useful in unstable patients

### Follow-up
- Serial imaging at 1, 3, 6, 12 months then yearly (small, asymptomatic)

---

## 5. Management
- **Asymptomatic, small:** medical management + surveillance
- **Symptomatic, expanding, or large:** **TEVAR** (endovascular repair)
- **Ruptured:** emergent TEVAR or open repair
- Medical therapy: HR < 60, SBP 100–120, statin, smoking cessation

### Indications for TEVAR
- Persistent pain
- Rapid growth
- Pleural effusion / haemothorax
- Diameter > 20–25 mm, depth > 10 mm
- Associated IMH with expansion

---

## 6. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **PAU = atherosclerotic ulcer crater** in aortic wall (usually descending)
- **Elderly hypertensive** with atherosclerosis
- Small / asymptomatic → **medical + surveillance**
- Symptomatic / expanding → **TEVAR**
- May progress to IMH, dissection, rupture
