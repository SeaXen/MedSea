# Atrial Fibrillation in Heart Failure

<callout icon="🫀" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** AF in HF — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Epidemiology
- **AF occurs in 30–50%** of HF patients (prevalence rises with NYHA class)
- **HFrEF**: ~25% paroxysmal, ~25% persistent
- **HFpEF**: up to 60% (older, hypertensive, obese)
- **AF worsens HF** by loss of atrial kick, tachycardia, irregular RR, neurohormonal activation

## 2. Pathophysiology
- **Atrial remodelling** — fibrosis, dilatation from elevated LA pressure
- **Neurohormonal activation** — RAAS, SNS
- **Inflammation** — common substrate for both AF and HF
- **AF → HF** and **HF → AF** — vicious cycle
- **Loss of atrial contraction** → ↓ LV filling → ↓ CO (15–25%)

## 3. Clinical Features
- **Palpitations**
- **Worsening HF symptoms** — dyspnoea, fatigue
- **Reduced exercise capacity**
- **Thromboembolism** — stroke, systemic embolism
- **Tachycardia-induced cardiomyopathy** — from rapid ventricular response
- **Often asymptomatic** — found on ECG

## 4. Diagnosis
- **12-lead ECG** — irregular RR, absent P waves, fibrillatory baseline
- **Holter / event monitor** — for paroxysmal
- **Echo** — LA size, LV function, valvular disease
- **NT-proBNP** — often higher with concurrent AF
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc** — stroke risk

## 5. Rate vs Rhythm Strategy

### RATE Control (often preferred)
- **Beta-blockers** — bisoprolol, carvedilol, metoprolol
- **Digoxin** — useful in HFrEF with AF, especially if symptomatic at rest
- **Non-dihydropyridine CCBs** (verapamil, diltiazem) — **avoid in HFrEF** (negative inotrope)
- **Target**: resting HR <110 bpm (lenient) or <80 bpm (strict, RACE II — no difference)

### RHYTHM Control
- **Cardioversion** — pharmacological or electrical, in symptomatic patients
- **Antiarrhythmic drugs** — **amiodarone** (only option in HFrEF — safe)
- **Catheter ablation** (pulmonary vein isolation) — **CASTLE-AF** showed ↓ mortality and HF hospitalisation in HFrEF + AF
- **AV node ablation + CRT** — for refractory cases with persistent AF

### Trial Evidence
- **AFFIRM** — no survival benefit of rhythm over rate (general population)
- **CASTLE-AF** — ablation ↓ mortality and HF hospitalisation in HFrEF
- **CABANA** — ablation reduced CV hospitalisation
- **EAST-AFNET 4** — early rhythm control ↓ CV outcomes

## 6. Anticoagulation

### Indication (ESC 2024)
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥2** (men) or ≥3 (women) — anticoagulate
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc = 1** (men) or 2 (women) — consider
- **CHA₂DS₂-VASc = 0** — no anticoagulation
- **Same thresholds regardless of HF** (HF per se is a risk factor)

### Choice
- **DOACs (preferred)** — **apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban, dabigatran**
  - All non-inferior or superior to warfarin for stroke prevention
  - **Lower bleeding** (especially intracranial)
- **Warfarin** — if mechanical valve, severe MS, CrCl <15
- **DOAC dose adjustment** — renal function, age, weight

## 7. Management Algorithm
```
AF + HFrEF
  ├── Anticoagulate (CHA₂DS₂-VASc)
  ├── Rate vs rhythm control
  │     ├── Symptomatic, recent onset → consider cardioversion
  │     ├── Persistent → rhythm control (ablation, amiodarone)
  │     └── Asymptomatic → rate control + GDMT
  ├── Optimise GDMT
  │     └── SGLT2i ↓ AF burden
  └── Catheter ablation (CASTLE-AF criteria)
```

## 8. Tachycardia-Induced Cardiomyopathy
- **Suspect** — new AF with rapid ventricular response + reduced EF
- **Management** — rate control, cardioversion
- **Recovery** — EF normalises in 4–6 weeks if rate controlled
- **Treat aggressively** — often fully reversible

> **Pearls**
> - **30–50%** of HF patients have AF
> - **CASTLE-AF** — catheter ablation ↓ mortality and HF hospitalisation in HFrEF
> - **Amiodarone** is the only safe AAD in HFrEF
> - **Avoid verapamil/diltiazem** in HFrEF (negative inotrope)
> - **Digoxin** useful for rate control in HFrEF, especially at rest
> - **DOAC preferred** for anticoagulation — not warfarin unless specific indication
> - **CHA₂DS₂-VASc** drives anticoagulation decision
> - **Tachycardia-induced CM** is reversible — control rate/rhythm early

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** AF and HF are **interlinked and worsen each other**. **CASTLE-AF** established ablation as a Class I/IIa option in HFrEF + AF. **Amiodarone** is the only safe AAD in HFrEF. **DOACs** are the anticoagulants of choice. **Anticoagulate based on CHA₂DS₂-VASc**, not rhythm.
</callout>
