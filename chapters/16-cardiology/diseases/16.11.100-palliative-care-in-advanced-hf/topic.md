# Palliative Care in Advanced HF

## 1. Indications
NYHA III–IV despite optimal GDMT, recurrent hospitalizations, ICD shocks, cachexia, refractory symptoms, consideration of LVAD/HTx.

## 2. Symptom Management
- **Dyspnea** — opioids (morphine 2.5–5 mg PO q4h PRN), oxygen, diuretics
- **Pain** — acetaminophen, opioids; avoid NSAIDs
- **Nausea** — haloperidol, ondansetron
- **Anxiety/depression** — SSRIs, mirtazapine (also ↑ appetite)
- **Fatigue** — energy conservation, treat anemia/sleep apnea
- **Cachexia** — nutritional support, omega-3, treat depression

## 3. Advance Care Planning
- Document goals of care early (not just at end of life)
- Discuss ICD deactivation at end of life (shocks are distressing)
- Code status, mechanical circulatory support preferences
- Designate surrogate decision-maker

## 4. End-of-Life
- Hospice referral when life expectancy <6 months
- Discontinue drugs that don't provide symptomatic benefit
- Continue symptom-focused drugs (diuretics, opioids)
- Compassionate withdrawal of inotropes/ICD as appropriate

> **Pearls** — Palliative care is **not** end-of-life care only. Start early in advanced HF. **Deactivate ICD** before death to prevent distressing shocks. Discuss goals of care at every hospitalization.

  > *Source:* **Devices**: ICD (primary prevention, LVEF ≤35% on GDMT 3m, NYHA II-III, life expectancy >1y — MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT), CRT (LBBB with QRS >150ms, LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II-IV — COMPANION, CARE-HF, MADIT-CRT).
