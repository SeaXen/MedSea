# Diagnostic criteria (chest pain, rub, ECG, effusion, biomarkers)

## 1. Definition
**Acute pericarditis** diagnosis requires ≥2 of 4 criteria:

## 2. Four Diagnostic Criteria
1. **Pleuritic chest pain** (typical quality)
2. **Pericardial friction rub** (pathognomonic)
3. **Characteristic ECG changes** (typical evolution)
4. **New or worsening pericardial effusion**

**≥2 of 4** = definitive diagnosis. **All 4** present in ~25% only.

## 3. Chest Pain Characteristics
- **Pleuritic** (worse with inspiration, coughing)
- **Improved leaning forward** (positional — sitting up, leaning over)
- **Worse lying supine** (relieved by sitting forward)
- Sharp, retrosternal, may radiate to trapezius ridge (pathognomonic — phrenic nerve)
- **Distinguishes from MI**: gradual onset, pleuritic, positional

## 4. Pericardial Friction Rub
- **Pathognomonic** — best heard at **left lower sternal border**, leaning forward
- **3 components**: Atrial systole, ventricular systole, ventricular diastole ("locomotive")
- High-pitched, scratchy, may be transient
- **Best heard** with diaphragm, breath held in expiration
- Found in ~50% — may be intermittent

## 5. ECG Evolution (4 Stages)
| Stage | Timing | Findings |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **1** | Hours–days | **Diffuse concave ST elevation**, PR depression (sensitive) |
| **2** | Days–weeks | ST normalizes, T wave flattening |
| **3** | Weeks | **Diffuse T wave inversion** (after ST normalized) |
| **4** | Weeks–months | Resolution (or persistent if chronic) |

**Key**: 
- ST elevation is **diffuse** (not territorial like MI)
- **PR depression** is sensitive and specific
- **Reciprocal changes absent** (aVR, V1 may have reciprocal ST depression/PR elevation)

## 6. Pericardial Effusion
- **Echo** is the test of choice
- Small, posterior, or circumferential
- May progress to tamponade
- **Hemorrhagic effusion** = TB, malignancy, trauma, post-cardiac surgery

## 7. Biomarkers
- **Troponin**: Elevated in ~30% (myopericarditis — epicardial involvement)
- **CRP/ESR**: Markedly elevated, useful for monitoring
- **BNP**: Helps differentiate pericardial from myocardial cause of symptoms
- **WBC**: Mildly elevated in viral/idiopathic

## 8. Supportive Findings
- **Fever** (low-grade in viral/idiopathic)
- **Leukocytosis** (modest)
- **Pleural effusion** (often left-sided, sympathetic)

> **Pearls**
> - **PR depression** = most specific ECG sign
> - **Trapezius ridge pain** = pathognomonic
> - **Diffuse ST elevation** + **PR depression** = pericarditis
> - **Reciprocal ST depression in aVR/V1** (vs. lateral leads in MI)
> - **Troponin elevation** = myopericarditis (not pure pericarditis) — usually no coronary distribution
