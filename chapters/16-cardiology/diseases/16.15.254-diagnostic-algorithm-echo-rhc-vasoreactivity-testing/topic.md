# Diagnostic Algorithm: Echo, RHC, Vasoreactivity Testing

## 1. Stepwise Diagnostic Pathway

**Step 1 — Suspicion:** Unexplained dyspnoea, exertional syncope, signs of RV strain.

**Step 2 — Transthoracic Echocardiography (TTE):** Initial non-invasive screen.
- Estimated PASP from TR jet velocity (4 × v² + RAP)
- RV size/hypertrophy, TAPSE, RV fractional area change
- Septal flattening ("D-shaped" LV in pressure overload)
- PA diameter >25 mm, RV outflow Doppler acceleration time <120 ms

**Step 3 — Confirm with Right Heart Catheterization (RHC):** Mandatory for PAH diagnosis.
- mPAP, PAWP, PVR, cardiac output (thermodilution/Fick)
- Vasoreactivity testing with inhaled NO (or IV epoprostenol/adenosine)

## 2. Vasoreactivity Testing

**Definition of positive response:** mPAP decreases ≥10 mmHg to absolute value ≤40 mmHg **with unchanged or improved cardiac output**.

- Performed only in **idiopathic, heritable, or drug-induced PAH**
- Positive → use **CCBs** (nifedipine, amlodipine, diltiazem) — ~10–15% of patients
- Non-responders → proceed to targeted PAH therapy

## 3. Additional Workup to Assign Group
- V/Q scan → rule out CTEPH (Group 4)
- PFTs, HRCT → Group 3 (lung disease)
- LFTs, HIV, ANA, anti-centromere/Scl-70 → connective tissue disease
- Thrombophilia screen if CTEPH suspected
- Sleep study if risk factors for OSA

## 4. Risk Stratification
Use **REVEAL 2.0** or **ESC/ERS 3-strata risk table** (low/intermediate/high) to guide initial therapy and follow-up.

> **Pearls**
> - RHC is **mandatory** to confirm PAH and start targeted therapy.
> - Vasoreactivity testing is done only in IPAH/HPAH/drug-induced PAH.
> - Positive vasoreactivity = sustained CCB response in <10% of cases.
