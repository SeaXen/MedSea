# Peripartum Cardiomyopathy

<callout icon="🤰" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Peripartum Cardiomyopathy — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Idiopathic LV systolic dysfunction** (LVEF <45%) presenting in the **peripartum period** in the absence of another identifiable cause. Develops in the **last month of pregnancy** or within **5 months postpartum**; no other cause of HF.

## 2. Epidemiology
- Incidence: **1 in 1,000–4,000** deliveries (geographic variation — higher in Africa, Haiti)
- Higher in women **>30 years**, **multiparous**, **twin pregnancies**, **African descent**
- Mortality: **5–25%** (up to 50% in some African cohorts)

## 3. Pathophysiology
- **Vascular/hormonal imbalance** — excess **cleaved prolactin** (16-kDa prolactin) → vasodilation + apoptosis
- **sFlt-1** (anti-angiogenic) — same molecule implicated in pre-eclampsia
- Cathepsin D, microRNAs, oxidative stress, selenium deficiency
- **Genetic susceptibility** — TTN truncating variants in 10–15%

## 4. Clinical Features
- **Dyspnoea, orthopnoea, PND** — classic HF
- **Cough, fatigue, ankle oedema**
- **Palpitations, chest pain**
- **Thromboembolism** — LV thrombus → stroke, peripheral embolism
- Often misdiagnosed as physiological pregnancy symptoms
- Pre-eclampsia / eclampsia coexists in ~25%

## 5. Diagnosis
- **Echo** — LVEF <45% (or LV end-diastolic dimension >2.7 cm/m²)
- **NT-proBNP** — markedly elevated (NB: BNP physiologically high in pregnancy)
- **Troponin** may be mildly raised
- **CMR** — oedema, no LGE (LGE if present suggests alternative aetiology)
- **Rule out** — pre-existing cardiomyopathy, pre-eclampsia, valvular, PE, MI, thyroid

## 6. Management — Acute / Decompensated
- **Standard HF therapy** (with pregnancy considerations)
- **Diuretics** — frusemide (safe)
- **Vasodilators** — hydralazine, nitrates (ACEi/ARB/ARNI **contraindicated** in pregnancy)
- **Beta-blockers** — metoprolol, bisoprolol (labetalol also)
- **Inotropes** — dobutamine, milrinone if cardiogenic shock
- **Anticoagulation** — **LMWH** if LVEF <35% or LV thrombus (high VTE risk in pregnancy)
- **Avoid** — ACEi, ARB, ARNI, **warfarin** (teratogenic 1st trimester), amiodarone, MRAs (spironolactone — limited data; eplerenone not recommended)

## 7. Management — Post-Delivery / Long-Term
- **GDMT** — ACEi/ARB/ARNI, beta-blocker, MRA, **SGLT2i** (caution — limited data but increasingly used)
- **Bromocriptine** — 2.5 mg bd × 2 weeks (or longer); suppresses prolactin
- **Anticoagulation** until LVEF recovered
- **ICD** — only if persistently low LVEF after 6–12 months (avoid early)
- **Avoid future pregnancy** if LVEF <50% (high recurrence); contraception counselling

## 8. Recovery & Prognosis
- **~50–70% recover** LV function within 6 months
- **Persistent LV dysfunction** in 30% — risk of progression to standard DCM
- **Worse prognosis** — LVEF <30%, RV involvement, African descent, late presentation
- Subsequent pregnancy: **20–50% relapse** if LVEF not fully recovered

> **Pearls**
> - **Diagnosis**: LVEF <45% in last month of pregnancy or 5 months postpartum, no other cause
> - **Bromocriptine** — cleaved prolactin is pathogenic; bromocriptine blocks it
> - **Avoid in pregnancy**: ACEi, ARB, ARNI, warfarin (1st trimester), MRAs, amiodarone
> - **Anticoagulate** if LVEF <35% — high thromboembolic risk
> - **Don't implant ICD early** — 50–70% recover LV function in 6 months
> - **Subsequent pregnancy** contraindicated if LVEF remains <50%
> - **sFlt-1 / cleaved prolactin** — shared with pre-eclampsia pathophysiology

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** PPCM is reversible in most — **allow time before implanting ICD**. **Bromocriptine** is the unique disease-modifying therapy. **Counselling on future pregnancy** is essential. Avoid teratogenic drugs (ACEi/ARB/warfarin) during pregnancy.
</callout>
