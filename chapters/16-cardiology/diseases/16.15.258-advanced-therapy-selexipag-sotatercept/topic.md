# Advanced Therapy: Selexipag and Sotatercept

## 1. Selexipag
- **Class:** Selective IP prostacyclin-receptor agonist (oral)
- **Evidence:** GRIPHON trial — reduced composite morbidity/mortality event by 40%
- **Use:** Add-on to ERA + PDE5i in WHO FC II–III PAH
- **Dosing:** Start 200 µg BID, titrate weekly to highest tolerated (max 1600 µg BID)
- **Side effects:** Headache, diarrhoea, jaw pain, flushing — manageable with titration
- **Tolerability:** ~80% reach maintenance dose

## 2. Sotatercept
- **Class:** First-in-class **activin signalling inhibitor** (fusion protein)
- **Mechanism:** Binds activin A/B and other TGF-β superfamily ligands, rebalances BMP/activin signalling → reverse vascular remodelling
- **Evidence:** **STELLAR (Phase 3)** — added to background therapy improved 6MWD (median +34 m vs +1 m), reduced time to clinical worsening, and improved NT-proBNP, WHO FC, and risk score
- **Dosing:** SC injection every 3 weeks (0.3 mg/kg → 0.7 mg/kg target)
- **Adverse effects:** Telangiectasias, epistaxis, gingival bleeding, Hb increase, hypertension
- **Status:** FDA-approved 2024 for adult PAH (WHO FC II–III) to improve exercise capacity
- **Transformative:** First novel PAH mechanism in decades; complementary to existing pathways

## 3. Position in Treatment Algorithm
- **Selexipag:** Add-on when dual oral therapy insufficient (or as part of upfront triple in higher risk)
- **Sotatercept:** Add-on to background ERA/PDE5i/prostacyclin; valuable when residual risk despite optimal therapy

## 4. Emerging Strategies
- Ralinepag (oral long-acting prostacyclin)
- Inhaled imatinib (tyrosine kinase)
- Gene therapy (BMPR2)

> **Pearls**
> - **Sotatercept** is a paradigm-shifting agent; tracks Hb, BP, bleeding.
> - Selexipag tolerability relies on **slow titration**.
> - Newer therapies are layered on top of established ERA/PDE5i/prostacyclin combinations.
