# AV Block

## 1. Definition
**Atrioventricular (AV) block** = delayed or absent conduction from atria to ventricles. Classified by severity: **1st degree, 2nd degree (Mobitz I/Wenckebach, Mobitz II), 3rd degree (complete)**. Localisation (AV node vs His-Purkinje) determines prognosis and treatment.

## 2. Key Features
- **1st degree**: PR >200 ms; every P conducted; usually benign
- **2nd degree Mobitz I (Wenckebach)**: progressive PR prolongation then dropped beat; usually **AV nodal**; benign, often vagal; **rarely needs pacing**
- **2nd degree Mobitz II**: fixed PR with intermittent dropped beats; **infranisian (His-Purkinje)**; **unstable, can progress to 3rd**; **pacing indicated**
- **3rd degree (complete)**: AV dissociation; P and QRS independent
  - Narrow QRS escape (~40–60): **AV nodal** block; usually stable
  - Wide QRS escape (~20–40): **infranisian**; unstable → asystole
- **Causes**: ischaemia (inferior = AV node, anterior = infranisian), age-related fibrosis, drugs (β-blocker, CCB, digoxin), electrolyte (hyperkalaemia), Lyme disease, sarcoidosis, post-cardiac surgery, congenital
- **Symptoms**: asymptomatic, fatigue, syncope (Stokes-Adams), HF

## 3. Management
- **Asymptomatic 1st / Mobitz I**: observe; treat cause
- **Symptomatic bradycardia**: atropine (0.5–1 mg IV), isoprenaline infusion, **transcutaneous/transvenous pacing**
- **Permanent pacemaker**:
  - **Class I**: Mobitz II, 3rd degree AV block, post-MI persistent block (especially anterior)
  - **Class IIa**: bifascicular block + syncope + EP-infarction
- **Avoid** in infranisian block: treat with pacing (atropine may not work)
- **Stop** offending drugs (β-blocker, CCB, digoxin)
- **Reversible** causes: ischaemia, drugs, electrolytes, Lyme

> **Pearls**
> - **Mobitz I = AV node (benign)**; **Mobitz II = His-Purkinje (unstable)**
> - **Anterior MI + AV block** = pacing needed; **inferior MI + AV block** = often transient
> - **Narrow QRS escape** = AV node; **wide QRS escape** = infranisian (worse)
> - **3rd degree block + symptoms** = permanent pacemaker
