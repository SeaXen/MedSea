## 1. Two Pillars of Medical Therapy
**(A) Anti-anginal** (symptom relief) · **(B) Disease-modifying** (event reduction)

---

## 2. Anti-Anginal Ladder
1. **Beta-blocker** first line (bisoprolol, atenolol, metoprolol) — ↓HR, ↓contractility
2. **Dihydropyridine CCB** (amlodipine, nifedipine) — add/alternative; **avoid non-DHP** (diltiazem/verapamil) with BB (bradycardia/AV block)
3. **Long-acting nitrate** (ISMN) — nitrate-free interval 8–12 h to prevent tolerance
4. **Nicorandil** (K-ATP opener + nitrate) — useful in microvascular angina
5. **Ranolazine** (late Na⁺ inhibitor) — add-on; QT prolongation
6. **Ivabradine** (If blocker) — sinus rhythm with HR ≥70 despite BB
7. **Trimetazidine** (metabolic modulator) — shifts to glucose oxidation

### Special Considerations
- **Vasospastic (Prinzmetal):** **CCB** first; **avoid beta-blockers** (may worsen spasm)
- **Microvascular:** BB, CCB, nicorandil, ranolazine; standard anti-anginal less effective

---

## 3. Disease-Modifying Therapy
| Class | Indication | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Aspirin 75–100 mg** | All | GI protection if high bleed risk |
| **Statin** (high-intensity) | All | LDL <1.4 mmol/L (very-high risk) |
| **ACEi / ARB** | HTN, DM, CKD, LV dysfunction | Sacubitril-valsartan if HFrEF |
| **Clopidogrel** | Aspirin alternative | If aspirin-intolerant |
| **Ezetimibe / PCSK9i** | If LDL target not met | Add on top of statin |
| **SGLT2i / GLP-1 RA** | DM ± HF | CV mortality benefit |

---

## 4. Lifestyle & Risk Factors
- **Smoking cessation** (most powerful single intervention)
- BP <130/80, HbA1c <7%, BMI <25
- Mediterranean diet, ≥150 min/wk exercise, alcohol <14 U/wk

---

## 5. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- First-line anti-anginal: **beta-blocker** (or DHP-CCB)
- Avoid BB in **vasospastic** angina; use CCB
- High-intensity statin + LDL <1.4 mmol/L in very-high-risk
- Ranolazine: late Na⁺ inhibitor; prolongs QT
- Lifestyle: smoking cessation is the single most impactful modifiable factor
