# In-Stent Restenosis (ISR)

<callout icon="💔" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** In-Stent Restenosis — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Luminal narrowing ≥50% at stent site or within 5 mm** on angiography. Occurs in:
- **BMS:** 20–30% at 6 months
- **DES:** 5–10% at 1 year (drug elution ↓ neointimal hyperplasia)
- **DCB (drug-coated balloon):** <5%

### Classification (Mehran)
- **Type I (focal):** ≤10 mm — best prognosis
- **Type II (diffuse):** >10 mm within stent
- **Type III (proliferative):** >10 mm extending outside stent
- **Type IV (occlusive):** total occlusion

---

## 2. Pathophysiology
- **Neointimal hyperplasia** (smooth muscle + matrix)
- **Neoatherosclerosis** (late, resembles native atherosclerosis)
- **Stent underexpansion / malapposition** (mechanical)
- **Stent fracture** (rare)

### Risk Factors
- DM, CKD, small vessel (<2.5 mm), long lesion, bifurcation, vein graft, multiple/overlapping stents

---

## 3. Clinical Features
- **Recurrent angina** (usually 3–12 months post-PCI)
- Late presentation (>1 y) = neoatherosclerosis
- Acute MI is rare (unlike stent thrombosis)

---

## 4. Treatment
| Pattern | Treatment |
|---|---|
| **Focal ISR** | Repeat DES (DCB acceptable) |
| **Diffuse / proliferative** | DES (everolimus) ± intracoronary brachytherapy / IVUS-guided |
| **Occlusive ISR** | DES + intracoronary imaging (IVUS / OCT) |
| **Recurrent ISR** | CABG, brachytherapy, or drug-eluting stent |

### Imaging Guidance
- **IVUS / OCT** to assess **stent underexpansion** (most common cause of recurrent ISR)
- Optimise deployment with high-pressure NC balloon

---

## 5. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- DES ISR: 5–10% at 1 y; BMS: 20–30%
- **Type I (focal)** = best prognosis; **Type IV (occlusive)** = worst
- **IVUS / OCT** to detect underexpansion (most common cause of recurrence)
- Treatment: **repeat DES**; consider DCB for focal lesions
- CABG for recurrent, diffuse, multi-vessel ISR
