# Post-Cardiac Injury Syndromes

## 1. Definition
Inflammatory pericarditis following **myocardial injury**:
- **Post-MI pericarditis** (early, <2 wk)
- **Dressler syndrome** (autoimmune, 2–10 wk post-MI)
- **Post-pericardiotomy syndrome** (after cardiac surgery)
- **Post-percutaneous intervention** (rare)

## 2. Pathogenesis
- Early: direct inflammation from necrotic myocardium
- Late (Dressler): autoimmune reaction to cardiac antigens

## 3. Clinical
- Fever, pleuritic chest pain
- Pericardial friction rub
- Pericardial effusion (sometimes tamponade)
- ↑ ESR/CRP, sometimes ↑ troponin (mild)
- Echo: effusion, ± wall motion abnormalities

## 4. Management
- **High-dose NSAIDs** (aspirin preferred post-MI, ibuprofen, indomethacin)
- **Colchicine** (COPE, CORP trials) — add for prevention and treatment
- **Avoid** steroids early (may impair MI healing)
- **Avoid** anticoagulants if possible (risk of hemopericardium)
- Pericardiocentesis if tamponade

> **Pearls** — **Dressler = autoimmune**, occurs **2–10 weeks** post-MI. **Aspirin + colchicine** is mainstay. Steroids only if refractory or autoimmune. Anticoagulants can cause hemorrhagic effusion.
