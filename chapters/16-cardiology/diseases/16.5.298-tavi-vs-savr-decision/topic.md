# TAVI vs SAVR Decision

## 1. Indications
- **Severe symptomatic AS**
- TAVI approved for **all surgical risk categories** (2020+)
- SAVR preferred: younger age, bicuspid valve (relative), need for CABG/MVR, failed TAVI

## 2. Risk Stratification
- **STS score** (mortality risk)
- **Frailty** assessment (EFT)
- **Comorbidities** (lung disease, renal, porcelain aorta)
- **Anatomic factors** (annulus size, calcification, vessel access)

## 3. Age-Based Approach (ACC/AHA 2020)
- **<65y**: SAVR preferred (durability, lower stroke)
- **65–80y**: Shared decision (consider TAVI for higher-risk, SAVR for lower-risk + younger)
- **>80y**: TAVI preferred (less invasive)
- **Bicuspid**: TAVI possible but less data

## 4. Key Trials
- **PARTNER 1** (inoperable): TAVI > medical
- **PARTNER 2** (intermediate risk): TAVI = SAVR
- **Evolut Low Risk**: TAVI = SAVR at 2y, lower bleeding
- **NOTION**: TAVI = SAVR at 8y

## 5. Durability Concern
- TAVI valve durability beyond 5–7 years less clear
- Younger patients face re-intervention risk
- Lifetime management strategy needed

> **Pearls** — TAVI is approved for **all risk categories** now. **Age** is the strongest driver of choice. **Younger** = SAVR preferred (durability). **Older/frail** = TAVI preferred. Discuss **lifetime management**.
