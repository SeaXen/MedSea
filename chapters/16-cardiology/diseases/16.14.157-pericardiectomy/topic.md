# Pericardiectomy

## 1. Indications
- **Chronic constrictive pericarditis** refractory to medical therapy
- **Recurrent pericardial effusion** unresponsive to other measures
- **Effusive-constrictive pericarditis**
- **Tuberculous constrictive pericarditis** after completion of ATT (controversial)

## 2. Surgical Approach
- **Median sternotomy** (preferred, complete access)
- Left thoracotomy (less complete)
- **Total pericardiectomy** — removal of pericardium from LV, RV, RA
- Leave pericardium over SVC, aorta, atria (avoid bleeding)

## 3. Outcomes
- Operative mortality 5–10% (recent series)
- 70–80% symptomatic improvement
- **Worse outcomes** with radiation, prior cardiac surgery
- Best in idiopathic, post-viral, post-surgical

## 4. Post-op Care
- Hemodynamic monitoring for low CO
- Diuresis as RV function recovers
- Anticipate transient atrial arrhythmias
- Anticoagulation if AF persists

> **Pearls** — **Total pericardiectomy** is the goal. Leave pericardium over great vessels to avoid catastrophic bleeding. Recovery takes **3–6 months** for full hemodynamic improvement. Worst outcomes: radiation, uremia.
