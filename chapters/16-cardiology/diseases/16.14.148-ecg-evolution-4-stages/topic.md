# ECG evolution (4 stages)

## 1. Overview
Acute pericarditis has a **characteristic 4-stage ECG evolution** that distinguishes it from acute MI. The evolution occurs over **days to weeks** and reflects progression of inflammation and epicardial injury.

## 2. Stage 1 (Hours to Days)
- **Diffuse concave-up ST elevation** (not territorial)
- **PR depression** (sensitive, specific)
- **PR elevation in aVR** (reciprocal)
- **ST depression in aVR, V1** (reciprocal)
- T waves upright (initially)

## 3. Stage 2 (Days to 1–2 Weeks)
- **ST segment returns to baseline**
- **T wave flattening**
- PR depression resolves
- **Transitional phase**

## 4. Stage 3 (Weeks)
- **Diffuse T wave inversion** (after ST normalized)
- T waves symmetric, deep, inverted in multiple leads
- May mimic ischemia but **T inversion occurs AFTER ST normalizes** (in MI, T inversion follows ST elevation)

## 5. Stage 4 (Weeks to Months)
- **Resolution to normal ECG** (most cases)
- T waves normalize
- Persistent changes suggest chronic pericarditis

## 6. ECG vs. MI — Key Differences
| Feature | Pericarditis | STEMI |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| **ST elevation** | Diffuse, concave | Territorial, convex |
| **Reciprocal changes** | Absent (except aVR/V1) | Present |
| **PR depression** | **Present** (sensitive) | Absent |
| **Q waves** | Absent | Develop |
| **T inversion** | After ST normalizes (Stage 3) | Before ST normalizes |
| **T wave** | Upright in Stage 1 | Often inverted in STEMI |
| **Distribution** | Most leads | Vascular territory |

## 7. Atypical Patterns
- **Localized pericarditis** (post-MI): ST elevation localized to infarct leads
- **Early repolarization** mimic: Common in young, athletes — can be difficult to distinguish
- **No ECG changes** (~20%): Effusive or mild pericarditis
- **Electrical alternans**: Suggests large effusion/tamponade

## 8. Spodick's Sign
- **Downsloping TP segment** in Stage 2/3
- Specific but not sensitive

> **Pearls**
> - **PR depression is the most specific finding** (often missed)
> - **Diffuse ST elevation** = pericarditis until proven otherwise
> - **T wave inversion AFTER ST normalizes** = pericarditis (vs. Wellens in MI)
> - **Stages 1 and 3** look like MI — but timing distinguishes them
> - ECG **normalizes in 2 weeks** in 50%, 4 weeks in 75%
