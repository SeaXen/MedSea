# Atherosclerosis Pathogenesis

<callout icon="🚑" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Atherosclerosis Pathogenesis — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep

## 1. Introduction
Atherosclerosis is a **chronic inflammatory disease** of large/medium elastic and muscular arteries (aorta, coronaries, carotids, iliofemoral). Underlies **CAD, stroke, PAD**. Develops over decades; clinically silent until plaque rupture.

## 2. Risk Factors (InterHEART)
- **Modifiable**: HTN, DM, smoking, dyslipidaemia (↑LDL), obesity, sedentary, psychosocial
- **Non-modifiable**: age, male, family history (first-degree <55 ♂ / <65 ♀), ethnicity
- **Emerging**: hsCRP, Lp(a), homocysteine, air pollution

## 3. Pathogenesis — "Response to Injury" Hypothesis

### Stage 1: Endothelial Dysfunction
- Triggers: **LDL, smoking, HTN, diabetes, oxidative stress**
- Loss of **NO, PGI₂**; ↑ **endothelin-1, VCAM-1, ICAM-1**
- Increased **permeability** to LDL

### Stage 2: Lipid Accumulation
- **LDL infiltrates subendothelium**, oxidised → **oxLDL**
- Monocytes adhere → **M1 macrophages** → foam cells
- **Fatty streak** (earliest visible lesion, even in children)

### Stage 3: Inflammatory Response
- **T-cells, macrophages, mast cells** in intima
- Cytokines: **TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6, IFN-γ**
- **Foam cells** + extracellular matrix = **atherosclerotic plaque**

### Stage 4: Plaque Progression
- **Smooth muscle migration** from media → intima
- Synthesis of **collagen, elastin, proteoglycans** = **fibrous cap**
- **Lipid core** of cholesterol esters, foam cells, debris
- **Calcification** (osteoblast-like cells, dystrophic)

### Stage 5: Complicated Plaque
- **Fibrous cap thinning** by MMPs (matrix metalloproteinases)
- **Plaque rupture** (60–70%) or **erosion** (25–30%) → thrombosis
- **Calcified nodule** (rare, elderly, dialysis) → ACS

## 4. Plaque Morphology

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Fatty streak** | Lipid-laden macrophages, no clinical effect |
| **Fibroatheroma** | Lipid core + fibrous cap |
| **Thin-cap fibroatheroma (TCFA)** | Cap **<65 μm**, large lipid core — **vulnerable** |
| **Fibrous plaque** | Dense collagen, calcified, stable |
| **Calcified nodule** | Eruptive calcification → ACS |

## 5. Vulnerable Plaque
- **Large lipid core** (>40% of plaque)
- **Thin fibrous cap** (<65 μm)
- **Macrophage infiltration**
- **Spotty calcification**, **neovascularisation**
- **Positive remodelling** (outward)
- **TCFA** = the lesion that ruptures

## 6. Molecular Mechanisms
- **NF-κB** — central inflammatory transcription factor
- **LOX-1** — oxLDL receptor on endothelium
- **MCP-1** — monocyte recruitment
- **TGF-β** — SMC proliferation, collagen synthesis
- **MMPs** (MMP-1, MMP-9) — cap thinning
- **Tissue factor** — thrombogenicity on rupture
- **PCSK9** — LDL receptor degradation; PCSK9 inhibitors ↓ events

## 7. Stable vs Unstable Plaque
- **Stable**: thick cap, small lipid core, calcified, slow stenosis
- **Unstable** (vulnerable): thin cap, large lipid, inflamed, may not be flow-limiting (50–70% cause most MIs)

## 8. Regression
- **Statins** ↓ LDL → ↓ lipid core, ↑ cap thickness, ↓ inflammation
- **PCSK9i** — robust regression (GLAGOV, REVERSAL)
- **Anti-inflammatory**: CANTOS (canakinumab ↓ events), LoDoCo2 (colchicine ↓ events)

## 9. Atherothrombosis
- Plaque rupture → **exposed lipid core + TF** → **platelet adhesion (vWF, GPVI)**
- Activation (TxA₂, ADP, thrombin) → aggregation (GP IIb/IIIa) → fibrin
- **STEMI** if complete occlusion; **NSTEMI/UA** if partial

## 10. Localisation
- **Branching points, bifurcations, curvatures** — low shear stress
- LAD most affected; then RCA, LCx

> **Pearls**
> - **Atherosclerosis = inflammatory disease** of arteries (Ross, Libby)
> - **LDL** is the key driver; **oxLDL** in intima is the trigger
> - **TCFA = thin cap (<65 µm) + large lipid + macrophages** = vulnerable
> - **Most MIs from non-flow-limiting lesions** (50–70% stenosis)
> - **Statins & PCSK9i** can **regress** plaque (serial IVUS)
