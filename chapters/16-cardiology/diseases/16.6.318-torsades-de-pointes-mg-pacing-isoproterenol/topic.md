# Torsades de pointes (Mg, pacing, isoproterenol)

## 1. Definition
**Torsades de Pointes (TdP)**: Polymorphic VT with characteristic **"twisting of the points"** — QRS complexes that vary in amplitude and appear to rotate around the isoelectric line. Occurs in setting of **QT prolongation**.

## 2. Pathophysiology
- **Prolonged QT** → early afterdepolarizations (EADs) → triggered activity
- Reentry within His-Purkinje system → polymorphic VT
- Often self-terminating but can degenerate to VF

## 3. Etiology (Causes of QT Prolongation)
- **Antiarrhythmics**: Class IA (quinidine, procainamide), Class III (sotalol, amiodarone — less common)
- **Antibiotics**: Macrolides (erythromycin, azithromycin), fluoroquinolones
- **Antipsychotics**: Haloperidol, ziprasidone, thioridazine
- **Antidepressants**: TCAs, SSRIs (citalopram)
- **Antiemetics**: Ondansetron, droperidol
- **Electrolytes**: **Hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, hypocalcemia**
- **Other**: Methadone, cocaine, bradycardia, congenital long QT

## 4. Clinical Features
- Often self-terminating → syncope, dizziness, palpitations
- Sustained → hemodynamic collapse, cardiac arrest
- Documented on rhythm strip showing twisting morphology

## 5. Management
### Acute (Sustained TdP)
- **IV Magnesium sulfate 2 g** over 1–2 min (first-line, even if Mg²⁺ normal)
- **Defibrillation** (unsynchronized) if pulseless or sustained
- **Correct K⁺** to >4.5 mmol/L, Mg²⁺ to >2 mg/dL
- **Overdrive pacing** (HR 90–110) via transvenous or transcutaneous — suppresses EADs
- **Isoproterenol** 2–10 µg/min (temporary measure) — ↑HR, shortens QT
- **Withdraw offending drug**

### Chronic
- **Avoid QT-prolonging drugs**
- **Beta-blocker** (propranolol, nadolol) for congenital long QT
- **ICD** for high-risk patients (history of arrest)
- **Left cardiac sympathetic denervation** in refractory cases

## 6. Diagnostic Criteria
- **QTc prolongation** >500 ms (men), >480 ms (women) — high risk
- **Pause-dependent TdP** (short-long-short sequence initiating arrhythmia)
- **Polymorphic VT with twisting QRS** on ECG

> **Pearls**
> - **IV Magnesium** works even with normal Mg²⁺ — give empirically
> - **Stop all QT-prolonging drugs** in TdP
> - **Bradycardia worsens TdP** — pace or use isoproterenol to ↑HR
> - **Hypokalemia** is a major risk factor — always check and replete
> - **Congenital LQTS** patients: avoid competitive sports, QT-prolonging drugs
