# Tricuspid Intervention Timing

## 1. Concomitant with Left-Sided Surgery
- **Severe TR** at time of MV/AV surgery
- **Mild-moderate TR** with annular dilation (>42 mm or >21 mm/m²) at time of left-sided surgery
- **Mild-moderate TR** with pulmonary HTN at time of left-sided surgery

## 2. Isolated TR Surgery
- **Severe symptomatic TR** despite medical therapy
- Progressive RV dilation/dysfunction
- Afterload (pulmonary pressures) not severely elevated

## 3. Timing is Key
- Too early = unnecessary surgery
- Too late = irreversible RV dysfunction → high mortality

## 4. Transcatheter
- **TriClip** (TEER) for high-risk surgical candidates (TRILUMINATE 2023)
- Expanding indications

> **Pearls** — **Don't** wait too long — RV dysfunction + severe TR = high operative mortality. Repair (annuloplasty ring) preferred over replacement. New **TriClip** is changing practice for inoperable patients.
