# Aortic dissection (Stanford A - surgery, B - medical)

## 1. Definition
**Aortic dissection**: Tear in the aortic intima allowing blood to enter and propagate within the media, creating a false lumen. Classified by **Stanford** (A/B) or **DeBakey** (I, II, IIIa, IIIb).

## 2. Classification
| Type | Description | Management |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **Stanford A** | Involves ascending aorta (any) | **Emergency surgery** |
| **Stanford B** | Distal to left subclavian only | **Medical management** |
| DeBakey I | Ascending + descending | Surgery |
| DeBakey II | Ascending only | Surgery |
| DeBakey III | Descending only | Medical |

## 3. Risk Factors
- **Hypertension** (most important)
- **Connective tissue**: Marfan, Ehlers-Danlos, Loeys-Dietz
- **Bicuspid aortic valve**, coarctation
- **Atherosclerosis**, aortic aneurysm
- **Cocaine use**, trauma, pregnancy (3rd trimester)
- **Iatrogenic**: Catheterization, cardiac surgery

## 4. Clinical Features
- **"Tearing" or "ripping" chest/back pain** (sudden onset, maximal at onset)
- **Pulse deficits**, BP differential (>20 mmHg between arms)
- **Aortic regurgitation murmur** (new diastolic)
- **Neurologic deficits** (carotid, spinal)
- **Hemopericardium → tamponade** (proximal extension)
- **Horner's syndrome** (apical lung)
- **Hematuria, mesenteric ischemia** (renal/mesenteric)

## 5. Diagnosis
- **CT angiography** (gold standard in stable patient)
- **TEE** (gold standard in unstable patient; bedside)
- **MRI** (best accuracy; slow)
- **CXR**: widened mediastinum, pleural effusion (left)

## 6. Management
### Stanford A
- **Emergent surgical repair** (replacement of ascending aorta ± AVR)
- Pre-op: Beta-blocker first (esmolol, labetalol) — target HR <60, SBP 100–120
- Avoid vasodilator monotherapy (reflex tachycardia)

### Stanford B
- **Medical management**: Beta-blocker + vasodilator (target SBP 100–120)
- **Pain control** (IV opioid)
- **Endovascular repair (TEVAR)** if: end-organ ischemia, refractory pain, rapid expansion
- Long-term BP control, surveillance imaging

## 7. Complications
- **Aortic regurgitation** (40–75% of Type A)
- **Cardiac tamponade** (proximal rupture)
- **MI** (coronary ostial involvement)
- **Stroke** (carotid involvement)
- **Aortic rupture** → death
- **End-organ ischemia** (renal, mesenteric, limb)

> **Pearls**
> - **Stanford A = surgery**, **Stanford B = medical** (in general)
> - **Always give beta-blocker FIRST** before vasodilator (diltiazem, nitroprusside)
> - **D-dimer** elevated in dissection (sensitive but not specific)
> - Avoid **anticoagulation/antiplatelets** until dissection ruled out
> - **Bicuspid aortic valve**: ascending aortic aneurysm risk — screen with echo
