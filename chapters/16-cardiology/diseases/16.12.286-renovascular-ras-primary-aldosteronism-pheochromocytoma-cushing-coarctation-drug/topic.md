# Secondary Hypertension

## 1. When to Suspect
- Young onset (<30), sudden onset, malignant, or resistant HTN
- Specific clues: hypokalemia, abdominal bruit, episodic spells, café-au-lait, coarctation signs

## 2. Renal Causes
- **Renovascular (RAS)**: atherosclerotic (>65) or fibromuscular dysplasia (FMD, women <50)
  - Workup: renal Doppler US, CT/MR angio, captopril renography
  - Tx: ACEi/ARB (caution in **bilateral** RAS), PCI/stenting, FMD → angioplasty

## 3. Endocrine Causes
- **Primary aldosteronism**: ARR (aldo/renin >20-30), saline infusion test, adrenal CT, AVS
  - Tx: adrenalectomy (unilateral) or MRA
- **Pheochromocytoma**: episodic HTN + headache/sweating/palpitations
  - Workup: 24h urine/plasma metanephrines; MIBG/PET
  - Tx: α-blockade (phenoxybenzamine) → β-blocker → surgery
- **Cushing**: midnight cortisol, low-dose DST
- **Thyroid**: hyper (AF, sys HTN), hypo (diastolic HTN)

## 4. Other
- **Coarctation**: radio-femoral delay, rib notching, arm-leg BP gradient
- **Drugs**: NSAIDs, OCP, sympathomimetics, cocaine, licorice, steroids

> **Pearls**: Screen broadly in resistant HTN. **Always** rule out primary aldosteronism — it's the most common curable cause.
