CARDIOLOGY
PH-LHD, PH-lung disease, multifactorial
1 PH-LHD (Group 2)

Most common cause of PH (~65–80%). Driven by passive backward transmission of elevated left atrial pressure.

Causes: HFpEF, HFrEF, mitral valve disease, severe aortic stenosis.

Features:

  • Post-capillary (PAWP >15 mmHg)
  • Often combined pre-/post-capillary (PAWP >15, PVR >2)
  • Treatment: optimise underlying LHD (diuretics, GDMT, valve intervention)
  • PAH-specific drugs are NOT recommended — may worsen V/Q mismatch and cause pulmonary oedema
2 PH-Lung Disease (Group 3)

Pre-capillary PH from hypoxia-induced vasoconstriction and vascular remodelling.

Causes: COPD, ILD, combined pulmonary fibrosis + emphysema, OSA, obesity hypoventilation.

Features:

  • Pre-capillary (PAWP ≤15, PVR >2)
  • Severe PH is uncommon but poor prognostic marker
  • Treatment: optimise underlying lung disease (oxygen, CPAP, pulmonary rehab, bronchodilators)
  • PAH drugs: limited evidence; consider referral if PVR disproportionately high
  • Inhaled treprostinil has FDA approval for PH-ILD
3 Multifactorial/Group 5

PH with unclear or multifactorial mechanisms — heterogeneous group.

Examples:

  • Sarcoidosis (pulmonary vascular involvement vs LV compression by nodes)
  • Haematological: sickle cell, chronic haemolytic anaemia, myeloproliferative disorders
  • Metabolic: glycogen storage, Gaucher, thyroid disease
  • Renal failure, ESRD on dialysis
  • Fibrosing mediastinitis
  • Tumour embolism

Treatment: Address underlying condition; consider PAH therapy on a case-by-case basis, generally at expert centres.

4 Practical Approach
  • Always classify PH by group before initiating therapy
  • Group 2 and 3 dominate — target the underlying disease
  • PAH-specific therapies only for Group 1 (and selected Group 4 and 5)
  • Consider referral to expert PH centre for confirmation, classification, and treatment decisions
Key Points - Group 2 (PH-LHD) is the most common PH — treat LHD, not PH. - Group 3 (PH-lung): Oxygen, treat underlying lung disease; inhaled treprostinil option in ILD. - Group 5 is heterogeneous — refer to expert centre.
