## 1. Introduction
Cardiac biomarkers are released into the circulation following **myocardial injury**. **High-sensitivity troponin (hs-cTn)** is the **gold standard** for diagnosing MI, with **CK-MB** and **myoglobin** as adjuncts (mostly historical).

## 2. Troponin

### Structure
- **Cardiac troponin complex** = TnC, TnT, TnI
- **Regulates actin-myosin interaction** (calcium-mediated)
- **Cardiac isoforms**: **cTnT** and **cTnI** are cardiac-specific (different from skeletal)

### High-Sensitivity Troponin (hs-cTn)
- Detects **10–100× lower** concentrations than conventional assays
- **99th percentile** cutoff (sex-specific: ♀ <15.6 ng/L, ♂ <34.2 ng/L for hs-cTnI Abbott)
- **Coefficient of variation (CV) <10%** at 99th percentile
- **Detectable in normal** individuals (high sensitivity)

### Kinetics
- Rises **1 h** post-injury; peak **24 h**; remains elevated **7–14 days**
- **Half-life** cTnT ~120 min, cTnI ~120 min
- **Re-elevation** after initial fall = re-infarction (especially within 48 h)
- **Single measurement at 0 h can rule out** with some assays (limit of detection)

### Diagnosis of MI
- **Rise and/or fall** of troponin with at least one value >99th percentile
- + **clinical evidence** of ischaemia (symptoms, ECG, imaging)
- **Universal definition** (4th UDMI, 2018)

### ESC 0/1-hour or 0/2-hour Algorithm
- **hs-cTnT/I at 0 h**:
  - **< LoD (limit of detection)** → rule out (NPV >99%)
  - **> Rule-in cutoff** → rule in (MI)
  - **Intermediate** → repeat at **1 h or 2 h**
- **Δ values** (delta) at 1 h: rule-in if **Δhs-cTnT ≥3 ng/L** (early presenters) or **≥5–7 ng/L** (late presenters)
- **Rule-out** if **Δ <3 ng/L** + low baseline

### Non-MI Causes of Troponin Elevation
- **Cardiac**: myocarditis, Takotsubo, HF, AF with RVR, ablation, contusion, cardioversion, ACS-related (type 2), pericarditis
- **Non-cardiac**: PE, aortic dissection, sepsis, renal failure (chronic elevation), SAH, stroke, anaemia, hypoxia, drug toxicity (5-FU, anthracyclines)
- **Renal failure** — chronic troponin elevation, but a **rise** is still significant

## 3. CK-MB
- **Creatine kinase, muscle-brain** isoform
- Rises **3–6 h**, peaks **12–24 h**, normalises **48–72 h**
- **Mass assay** preferred over activity
- **Less specific** than troponin (skeletal muscle, brain, smooth muscle, renal)
- **Used for**: re-infarction (falls faster than troponin), infarct size estimation, post-PCI/CABG
- **CK-MB/total CK** ratio >5% suggests cardiac source

## 4. Myoglobin
- **Heme protein** in skeletal and cardiac muscle
- Rises **1–2 h**, peaks **6–9 h**, normalises **24 h**
- **Earliest** to rise but **non-specific** (rhabdomyolysis, exercise, renal failure)
- **Low sensitivity/specificity** — replaced by troponin
- May be useful in **early rule-out** when combined with troponin and ECG

## 5. Other Markers (Mostly Historical)
- **AST, LDH, total CK** — historical
- **BNP / NT-proBNP** — HF (rule in/out), prognostic in MI
- **CRP, hs-CRP** — inflammatory; prognostic
- **D-dimer** — PE rule-out
- **Lp(a), LDL, HDL** — risk assessment

## 6. Point-of-Care Troponin
- Rapid (<20 min) troponin I/T assays
- Sensitivity 80–90% (less than central hs-cTn)
- Used in **ED triage** for early rule-out
- **Negative POC + low risk** can discharge (HEART score)

## 7. Special Situations
- **Renal failure** — chronic elevation of troponin; **rise/fall** still diagnostic
- **Post-PCI** — type 4a MI: cTn >5x ULN (stable) or >10x ULN (if baseline elevated) + new ECG/Q
- **Post-CABG** — type 5 MI: cTn >10x ULN + new Q/LBBB/LV dysfunction
- **Stroke** — central catecholamine surge → troponin leak (not ACS)

> **Pearls**
> - **hs-cTn = gold standard** for MI diagnosis (sex-specific 99th percentile)
> - **0/1 h or 0/2 h algorithm** rules out MI rapidly (NPV >99%)
> - **Troponin ↑** is NOT specific for ACS — must have **rise AND fall** + clinical context
> - **CK-MB** useful for **re-infarction** (faster fall than troponin)
> - **Renal failure** — chronic troponin elevation, but rise/fall still significant
> - **Type 4a MI** post-PCI: cTn >5x ULN + new Q/ischaemia
