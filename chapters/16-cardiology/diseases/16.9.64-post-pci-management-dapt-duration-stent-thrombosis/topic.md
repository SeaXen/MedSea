## 1. DAPT Duration
| Scenario | DAPT Duration |
|---|---|
| **Stable CAD + DES** | 6 months (shorten 1–3 mo if high bleed risk) |
| **Stable CAD + BMS** | ≥1 month |
| **ACS + DES** | 12 months (consider shorter or longer by risk) |
| **DCB-only PCI** | 1–6 months |
| **High bleeding risk (PRECISE-DAPT ≥25)** | 1–3 months |
| **High ischaemic risk (DAPT score ≥2)** | ≥12 months, up to 30 months |

### Risk Tools
- **DAPT score:** predicts ischaemic vs bleeding trade-off
- **PRECISE-DAPT:** predicts bleeding on DAPT
- **ARC-HBR:** high-bleeding-risk criteria

---

## 2. Stent Thrombosis
- **Acute (<24 h):** mechanical, dissection, residual thrombus
- **Subacute (1–30 d):** premature DAPT stop, malapposition
- **Late (30 d–1 y):** delayed healing, malapposition
- **Very late (>1 y):** neoatherosclerosis, late malapposition

### Presentation
- STEMI / cardiac arrest (60–80% mortality)
- Treatment: **emergent PCI**, IVUS / OCT to identify mechanism

---

## 3. Post-PCI Pharmacology
- **Aspirin 75–100 mg** lifelong
- **P2Y₁₂ inhibitor:** clopidogrel / ticagrelor / prasugrel
- **High-intensity statin** lifelong
- **Beta-blocker** (≥1 y if LV dysfunction or ACS)
- **ACEi / ARB** if HTN, DM, LV dysfunction

---

## 4. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **DAPT after DES in stable CAD = 6 months** (ACS = 12 months)
- **Stent thrombosis presents as STEMI** (mortality 60–80%)
- **PRECISE-DAPT ≥25** = high bleed risk → shorten DAPT
- **DAPT score ≥2** = high ischaemic risk → extend DAPT
- Use **DAPT / PRECISE-DAPT / ARC-HBR** to tailor duration
