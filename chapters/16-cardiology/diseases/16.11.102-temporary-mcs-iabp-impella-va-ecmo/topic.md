# Temporary Mechanical Circulatory Support (MCS)

## 1. IABP (Intra-Aortic Balloon Pump)
- **Counterpulsation** — inflates in diastole, deflates in systole
- ↑ coronary perfusion, ↓ afterload
- Limited support (~0.5 L/min ↑ CO)
- **Use:** cardiogenic shock, bridge to PCI/CABG
- **Contraindications:** severe AR, aortic dissection

## 2. Impella (2.5, CP, 5.0, 5.5)
- **Axial flow pump** across aortic valve
- Unloads LV, ↑ forward flow
- 2.5 = 2.5 L/min; 5.0/5.5 = up to 6 L/min
- **Use:** cardiogenic shock, high-risk PCI, VT ablation
- **Contra:** LV thrombus, severe AS, mechanical AV

## 3. VA-ECMO
- **Veno-arterial** extracorporeal circulation
- Provides full cardiopulmonary support
- **Use:** refractory shock, cardiac arrest (ECPR)
- **Complications:** bleeding, limb ischemia, LV distension, Harlequin syndrome (differential hypoxia)
- **Weaning** — requires adequate LV ejection (consider Impella for LV venting)

## 4. Selection by SCAI Stage
- B/C: IABP, inotropes
- C/D: Impella, VA-ECMO
- E: VA-ECMO ± Impella, CPR/ECPR

> **Pearls** — **No mortality benefit** proven for routine IABP in cardiogenic shock (IABP-SHOCK II). VA-ECMO ↑ LV afterload — add Impella for venting. Multidisciplinary shock team essential. Time to MCS is critical.
