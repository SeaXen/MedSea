# Hemodynamic Assessment of Mitral Stenosis

## 1. Goals
Quantify **severity of MS** (MVA, gradient, PHT) and assess **secondary consequences** (LA enlargement, pulmonary HTN, RV function). Echo is the cornerstone.

## 2. Key Parameters

| Parameter | Severe Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Mitral valve area (MVA)** | **< 1.5 cm²** (some guidelines <1.0 very severe) | Planimetry or PHT |
| **Mean transvalvular gradient** | **> 10 mmHg** at rest | CW Doppler |
| **Pressure half-time (PHT)** | **> 220 ms** (some use >180 for severe) | MVA = 220 / PHT |
| **Pulmonary artery systolic pressure** | > 50 mmHg | TR jet + RAP |
| **Indexed MVA** | < 1.0 cm²/m² (in large patients) | Adjusted for BSA |

## 3. Methods of Measuring MVA

### Planimetry (Anatomical Area)
- **Parasternal short axis** at the **leaflet tips** (smallest orifice during diastole)
- Trace the inner edge of the orifice
- Gold standard for **rheumatic MS**
- **Errors:** imaging plane (off-axis = over-estimate), calcification, atrial view
- **3D TEE planimetry** — most accurate

### Pressure Half-Time (PHT)
- MVA = 220 / PHT
- **Assumption:** linear LA-LV pressure decay
- Affected by **LV compliance and LA pressure** (over-estimates MVA in older / diastolic dysfunction)
- Quick, reproducible, widely used

### Continuity Equation (MVA)
- MVA = (LVOT area × LVOT VTI) / MV VTI
- Less flow-dependent than PHT
- Useful when PHT / planimetry discordant

### Proximal Isovelocity Surface Area (PISA)
- PISA-based MVA = π r² × aliasing velocity / peak MV E velocity
- Less commonly used (technical difficulty in MS)

## 4. Severity Grading

| Severity | MVA (cm²) | Mean grad (mmHg) | PHT (ms) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mild** | > 1.5 | < 5 | < 130 |
| **Moderate** | 1.0–1.5 | 5–10 | 130–220 |
| **Severe** | **< 1.5** (or < 1.0 very severe) | **> 10** | **> 220** |

> Severe MS **with symptoms** = intervention. Severe MS asymptomatic with **good compliance** may be observed.

## 5. Associated Findings

### Left Atrium
- LA enlargement (AP diameter >40 mm, area >20 cm²/m²)
- **Spontaneous echo contrast** (SEC) — precursor to thrombus
- **LA thrombus** — most often in LAA; rule out by TOE

### Pulmonary Circulation
- **Pulmonary hypertension** (sPAP > 50 mmHg)
- RV dilation, hypertrophy
- Tricuspid regurgitation (functional)

### Right Heart
- RV systolic dysfunction
- RA enlargement
- Systemic venous congestion (hepatic congestion, peripheral oedema)

### Other Valves
- Co-existing rheumatic **AR / AS**
- Functional **TR** (from PH)

## 6. Stress / Exercise Testing
- **Asymptomatic severe MS** → exercise echo / right heart cath
- **Δ mean gradient > 15 mmHg** or **sPAP > 60 mmHg** with exercise = consider intervention
- Useful when symptoms are equivocal

## 7. Special Considerations

### MS with AF
- Loss of atrial "kick" → ↓ LV filling → ↓ cardiac output
- **Higher gradient** at any given MVA
- Higher LA thrombus risk → TOE before PMBV
- Anticoagulation with warfarin (INR 2.0–3.0)

### "Low-Flow, Low-Gradient" MS
- Severe MVA on planimetry but low gradient (low output, e.g., concurrent LV dysfunction)
- PHT unreliable in low cardiac output
- Use planimetry + continuity equation

### MacCallum Patch
- **Subendocardial fibrosis / calcification** in LA from chronic RHD
- Marker of advanced RHD
- Increases LA thrombus risk

## 8. Diagnostic Algorithm
```
Suspected MS
   ├── TTE (parasternal SAX for planimetry, CW for PHT / gradient, RV / TR jet for sPAP)
   ├── LA / LAA (TTE limited; TOE for thrombus)
   ├── Severity determined?
   │     ├── Yes → manage
   │     └── No (discordant) → TOE, stress echo, 3D planimetry
   └── Treat / surveillance
```

## 9. When TEE is Needed
- **Pre-PMBV** (exclusion of LA thrombus, assessment of MR, Wilkins score)
- **Suboptimal TTE windows**
- **Discordant TTE measurements**
- **Suspected prosthetic MS** (valve thrombosis, pannus)

> **Pearls**
> - Severe MS: **MVA < 1.5 cm²**, **mean grad > 10 mmHg**, **PHT > 220 ms**
> - **MVA = 220 / PHT** (Emory formula) — quick estimate
> - **3D planimetry** is the most accurate MVA
> - Always assess **LA size, sPAP, RV function, LAA thrombus** (TOE)
> - **Stress echo** in asymptomatic severe MS to unmask symptoms / pulmonary HTN
