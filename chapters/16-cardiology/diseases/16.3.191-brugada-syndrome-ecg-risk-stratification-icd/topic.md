# Brugada Syndrome

## 1. Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Etiology & Pathophysiology

**Accessory pathway** = embryonic connection between atria and ventricles bypassing AV node. **Anatomy**: left lateral (most common, ~50%), septal (30%), right (20%). **Conduction**: usually antegrade + retrograde (orthodromic AVRT most common, narrow QRS). **Antidromic AVRT**: antegrade via accessory, retrograde via AV node (wide QRS, resembles VT). **Pre-excitation**: antegrade conduction through pathway during sinus rhythm.

---

## 2. Classification

### Clinical Features

**Asymptomatic** (incidental ECG). **AVRT** (paroxysmal SVT, palpitations, dyspnoea, chest pain, syncope). **AF with rapid ventricular response** (irregular wide complex tachycardia, can degenerate to VF — risk of SCD in ~0.1-0.5%/year). **Examination**: usually normal. **Documentary**: episodes.

---

## 3. Clinical Features

### Diagnostic Approach

**12-lead ECG**: short PR (<120ms), delta wave, wide QRS (>110ms), secondary ST-T changes. **Location**: V1 (left vs right), inferior leads (anteroseptal vs posteroseptal), lead I (lateral). **Holter** for paroxysmal. **EP study** (gold standard, mapping for ablation, refractory period measurement). **Exercise test** (loss of pre-excitation with exercise = low risk).

---

## 4. Diagnosis & Investigations

### Severity Assessment & Risk Stratification

**Asymptomatic, no AF, no SVT** = low risk. **Symptomatic, recurrent AVRT** = ablation indicated. **AF with shortest RR <250ms, refractory period <240ms** = high risk SCD. **Cardiac arrest survivor** = highest risk. **Syncope in WPW** = concerning. **Family history of SCD** = concerning.

### Differential Diagnosis

LBBB, RBBB, ventricular paced rhythm, idiopathic VT (RVOT, LVOT), antidromic AVRT, VT, hyperkalaemia, MI with Q waves, early repolarisation, Brugada, ARVC.

### Investigations

12-lead ECG (delta wave, short PR, wide QRS), Holter (intermittent pre-excitation), exercise test (loss of pre-excitation), EP study (gold standard, ablation, refractory period), echo (structural, Ebstein — often associated with WPW).

---

## 5. Management

### Management Algorithm

**Asymptomatic**: may not need treatment, consider risk stratification. **Acute AVRT** (orthodromic, narrow): vagal manoeuvres (Valsalva, carotid sinus massage — avoid bilateral), adenosine 6mg IV bolus (then 12mg, then 12mg), verapamil/diltiazem (avoid if wide QRS = antidromic), beta-blocker.

### Special Populations

**Asymptomatic** in children: ablation if pathway demonstrates short refractory period. **Pregnancy**: ablation deferred if possible, medical management. **Athletes**: ablation recommended. **Syncope + WPW**: ablation. **AF + WPW**: ablation.

---

## 6. Complications & Prognosis

### Complications & Prognosis

**SCD/VF** (0.1-0.5%/year, especially in AF with short refractory period). **Recurrent AVRT**. **AF**. **Cardiomyopathy** (tachycardia-mediated, from chronic AVRT/AF). **Syncope**. **Stroke** (AF).
