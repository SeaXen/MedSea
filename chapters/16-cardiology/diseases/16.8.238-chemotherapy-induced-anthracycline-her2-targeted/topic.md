# Chemotherapy-Induced Cardiomyopathy

## 1. Anthracyclines (Doxorubicin, Epirubicin)
- **Dose-dependent**, irreversible
- Doxorubicin 400 mg/m² → 5% risk; 700 mg/m² → 50%
- Mechanism: oxidative stress, topoisomerase 2β inhibition
- **Prevention**: dexrazoxane, liposomal doxorubicin, infusion (vs bolus)
- Monitor: baseline + serial echo (GLS - global longitudinal strain most sensitive)

## 2. Trastuzumab (HER2-targeted)
- **Reversible** (mostly) — not dose-dependent
- Mechanism: HER2/ErbB2 signaling in cardiomyocytes
- **Risk factors**: prior anthracycline, age, obesity, HTN
- **Withhold** if LVEF drops 10% to <50% (or GLS drop)
- Can often **resume** after recovery with HF therapy
- Standard concurrent β-blocker / ACEi prevention debated

## 3. Other Agents
- **VEGF inhibitors** (bevacizumab, sunitinib) — HTN, ↓ LVEF, thrombosis
- **BTK inhibitors** (ibrutinib) — AF, HTN
- **Proteasome inhibitors** (carfilzomib) — HF
- **ICIs** (nivolumab, pembrolizumab) — fulminant myocarditis (1–2%)

## 4. Monitoring
- **Baseline echo + GLS** before therapy
- Serial every 3 months for high-risk
- **Troponin, BNP** as adjuncts
- Stop/reduce if GLS drop >15% or LVEF <50%

> **Pearls** — **Anthracycline = dose-dependent, irreversible**. **Trastuzumab = reversible**, can often resume. **GLS** is most sensitive early marker. **Dexrazoxane** for prevention in high-dose anthracycline regimens.
