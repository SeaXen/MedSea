# Neuroprognostication

## 1. Definition
**Neuroprognostication**: Assessment of neurologic recovery potential in comatose patients after cardiac arrest. Goal: identify those with poor prognosis who will not benefit from ongoing life support, vs. those who will recover.

## 2. Timing
- **Deferred** until **≥72 hours after ROSC** AND after **normothermia** AND after **sedation cleared**
- Avoid premature withdrawal of care (false positive predictions)

## 3. Clinical Examination
### Absent Brainstem Reflexes
- **Absent pupillary light reflex** at 72 h (highly specific for poor outcome)
- **Absent corneal reflex**
- **Absent oculocephalic/oculovestibular (cold caloric) reflex**

### Motor Response
- **GCS motor ≤2** (decorticate/decerebrate posturing or no response) at 72 h

### Myoclonus
- **Status myoclonus** (early, generalized, persistent) — poor prognosis
- **Lance-Adams syndrome** (late, intention myoclonus) — better prognosis

## 4. Ancillary Testing

| Test | Finding Suggesting Poor Outcome |
|------|-------------------------------|
| **EEG** | Diffuse suppression, burst-suppression, generalized periodic discharges, status epilepticus |
| **NSE** (neuron-specific enolase) | >33–60 µg/L at 48–72 h (high specificity) |
| **S100B** | Elevated at 24–48 h |
| **MRI brain** | Diffuse cortical-restricted diffusion on DWI at 2–7 days |
| **CT brain** (early) | Loss of gray-white differentiation, sulcal effacement |
| **SSEP (somatosensory)** | Absent bilateral N20 cortical response at 24–72 h (**most specific single test**) |

## 5. Multimodal Approach
No single test is sufficient — combine:
1. **Clinical exam** (brainstem reflexes, motor)
2. **EEG** (continuous or intermittent)
3. **SSEP** (most specific)
4. **Biomarkers** (NSE)
5. **Imaging** (MRI preferred)

## 6. Withdrawal of Life Support
- **Should NOT be based on single predictor**
- **Requires ≥2 independent poor prognostic findings**
- **Adequate time elapsed** (≥72 h post-ROSC)
- **Off sedation, normothermic**
- **Shared decision-making** with family

## 7. Targeted Temperature Considerations
- Wait **at least 72 h** post-rewarming
- Sedatives/analgesics may confound exam — allow clearance (5 half-lives)
- **TTM itself does not worsen prognosis**

> **Pearls**
> - **Absent N20 on SSEP** is the most specific poor prognosticator (~0% false positive)
> - **Pupillary reflex** at 72 h: if absent, specificity >80%
> - **Status myoclonus within 24 h** + other signs = very poor outcome
> - Don't prognosticate too early — sedatives, hypothermia confound
> - **MRI DWI at 2–7 days** highly predictive — but requires transport
