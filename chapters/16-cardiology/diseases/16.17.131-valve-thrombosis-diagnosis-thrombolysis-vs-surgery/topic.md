# Valve Thrombosis

## 1. Definition
**Prosthetic valve thrombosis (PVT)** = thrombus formation on a prosthetic valve, leading to obstruction or systemic embolism. More common in mechanical valves, especially with subtherapeutic anticoagulation, but can occur in bioprosthetic (early post-op).

## 2. Key Features
- **Mechanical valve thrombosis** (0.5–8%/y):
  - **Risk factors**: subtherapeutic anticoagulation, atrial fibrillation, hypercoagulable state, LV dysfunction, pregnancy
  - **Symptoms**: dyspnoea, HF, embolic event, valve click muffled/absent
  - **Diagnosis**: TTE (↓ opening, ↓ effective orifice area, ↑ gradients, mass on valve), **TOE** (gold standard, especially mitral position)
  - **Cinefluoroscopy**: stuck leaflet
- **Bioprosthetic valve thrombosis** (early, <1 y):
  - Often subclinical; presents as ↑ gradients mimicking stenosis
  - Improved with short-term anticoagulation post-op
- **Types**: obstructive (more severe) vs non-obstructive
- **Differential**: pannus (chronic, slow progression), endocarditis, structural degeneration

## 3. Management
- **Stable, non-obstructive**: optimise anticoagulation (IV UFH → warfarin); reimage
- **Obstructive (mechanical)**:
  - **Thrombolysis** (alteplase 10 mg bolus + 90 mg over 90 min) — first-line if **high surgical risk**; success 80%
  - **Urgent surgery** if low surgical risk, large thrombus (>10 mm), LA thrombus, failed lysis, bioprosthetic
- **Anticoagulation**: UFH → warfarin; target INR 3.0–4.0
- **Bioprosthetic**: anticoagulation (warfarin or DOAC) for 3–6 months; reimage
- **Adjuncts**: antiplatelet, treat underlying cause
- **Endocarditis**: must exclude

> **Pearls**
> - **Mechanical valve thrombosis** — urgent management
> - **Thrombolysis** first-line if high surgical risk; **surgery** if low risk
> - **DOACs are contraindicated** for mechanical valve anticoagulation
> - **Bioprosthetic thrombosis** often subclinical — check gradients
