# Culture-negative endocarditis

Related: [[../Cardiology MOC|Cardiology MOC]] · [[Endocarditis and intracardiac infection]] · [[Native-valve infective endocarditis]]

## Definition
Culture-negative endocarditis is infective endocarditis in which routine blood cultures remain negative, usually because of **prior antibiotics**, fastidious organisms, or inadequate microbiologic recovery.

## Anatomy
- Vegetations involve valves/endocardium as in other forms of IE.
- Destructive complications still occur despite negative cultures.

## Physiology
- Regurgitation, HF, emboli, and abscess can develop exactly as in culture-positive IE.
- Diagnostic delay is the main added danger.

## Classification
- Antibiotic-suppressed culture-negative IE
- Fastidious-organism IE
- Serology/PCR-diagnosed IE

## Etiology / risk factors
- Prior antibiotic exposure
- Coxiella, Bartonella and other fastidious organisms
- Fungal or atypical organisms in selected settings
- Prosthetic material or health-care exposure

## Pathophysiology
1. Endocardial infection occurs.
2. Standard cultures fail to identify the pathogen.
3. Delayed organism diagnosis risks prolonged uncontrolled damage.

## Clinical features
### Symptoms/signs
- Same as IE: fever, murmur, malaise, emboli, HF, systemic features.
- History of recent antibiotics is a clue.

## Investigations
- Repeat blood cultures if possible before more antibiotics
- **Echo**: TTE/TEE to document vegetations and complications
- Serology / PCR / specialized microbiology for fastidious pathogens
- Routine IE labs and embolic imaging when indicated

## Interpretation frameworks
### Diagnostic logic
- Negative cultures do **not** exclude IE if echo and clinical picture are convincing.
- Always ask about prior antibiotics.

## Diagnosis
Diagnosis is clinical + echocardiographic + specialized microbiologic. It remains a Duke-style diagnosis, just with harder pathogen recovery.

## Differential diagnosis
- Native-valve IE with delayed cultures
- Non-bacterial thrombotic endocarditis
- Marantic/autoimmune lesions
- Cardiac myxoma

## Management
- Involve microbiology/infectious disease early.
- Obtain tailored tests for fastidious organisms.
- Give empiric IE-directed therapy when suspicion is high.
- Surgery for standard IE indications: HF, abscess, uncontrolled infection, emboli.

## Complications
- HF
- Emboli
- Abscess
- Persistent infection
- Diagnostic delay-related mortality

## Red flags / emergencies
- Negative cultures but strong IE syndrome
- Prior antibiotics masking diagnosis
- New conduction abnormality or acute regurgitation

## One-Page Revision Summary
- Culture-negative IE is still **real IE**.
- Think prior antibiotics or fastidious organisms.
- Echo plus clinical suspicion remains central.
- Escalate microbiology testing early.
