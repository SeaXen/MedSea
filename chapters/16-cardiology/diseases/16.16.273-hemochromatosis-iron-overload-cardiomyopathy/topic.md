# Hemochromatosis (Iron Overload Cardiomyopathy)

## 1. Definition
- **Hereditary** (HFE C282Y homozygotes) or **secondary** (transfusion, thalassemia, hemolysis)
- Iron deposits in **liver, pancreas, heart**, joints, pituitary

## 2. Key Features
- **Dilated/restrictive cardiomyopathy** — biventricular, often with preserved EF early
- **HF with arrhythmias**: SVT, VT, conduction disease
- **Triad**: cirrhosis + DM + bronze skin
- **Arthropathy**: 2nd/3rd MCP joints
- **Hypogonadism**

## 3. Diagnosis
- **Iron studies**: ↑ferritin (>300 ♀, >400 ♂), ↑transferrin saturation (>45%)
- **HFE gene** testing
- **MRI**: **T2* <20 ms** = iron overload; CMR with T1 mapping
- **Liver biopsy** or MRI for hepatic iron
- Endomyocardial biopsy in selected cases

## 4. Management
- **Phlebotomy** (hereditary): target ferritin 50-100
- **Iron chelation** (secondary): deferoxamine, deferasirox, deferiprone
- Standard HF Rx; **avoid** Vit C, raw shellfish
- Family screening

> **Pearls**: Always check **transferrin saturation** in unexplained DCM/RCM. T2* CMR is gold standard for cardiac iron. **Therapeutic phlebotomy** improves cardiac function.
