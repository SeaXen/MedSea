# Culture-Negative Endocarditis

## 1. Definition
Endocarditis with **no causative organism identified** on standard blood cultures after 5–7 days.

## 2. Causes
- **HACEK** (Haemophilus, Aggregatibacter, Cardiobacterium, Eikenella, Kingella) — fastidious, need prolonged incubation
- **Bartonella** (B. henselae, B. quintana) — homelessness, cat exposure
- **Coxiella burnetii** (Q fever) — farm animals, parturient cats
- **Tropheryma whipplei** (Whipple's disease)
- **Mycoplasma, Legionella, Chlamydia**
- **Fungi** (Candida, Aspergillus)
- **Prior antibiotics** (most common overall)

## 3. Workup
- **Prolonged blood cultures** (2 weeks)
- **Serology** — Bartonella, Coxiella, Chlamydia, Brucella, Mycoplasma
- **PCR** on valve tissue (16S rRNA, broad-range)
- **Specialized culture media** for HACEK, fungi
- **Histopathology** on surgical specimens

## 4. Treatment
- Empiric if undetermined: doxycycline + rifampin ± gentamicin (covers Bartonella, Coxiella)
- Directed based on identified pathogen
- **Surgery often needed** for diagnosis + management

> **Pearls** — Most common cause: **prior antibiotics**. HACEK and **Bartonella** are classically missed. **Q fever** needs phase I IgG titer >1:800. PCR on valve tissue is becoming standard. Empiric: **doxycycline + rifampin** ± gentamicin.
