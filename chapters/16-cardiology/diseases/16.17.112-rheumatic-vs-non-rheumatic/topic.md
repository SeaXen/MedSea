# Mitral Stenosis — Rheumatic vs Non-Rheumatic Etiology

## 1. Background
**Mitral stenosis (MS)** is dominated by **rheumatic heart disease (RHD)** globally. Non-rheumatic causes are uncommon but recognised; they have different pathologies, treatment, and prognosis.

## 2. Rheumatic MS — The Global Standard

### Epidemiology
- Most common cause of MS worldwide (~95% of cases)
- Affects ~40 million people (RHD overall)
- Highest burden: sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, Pacific Islands, indigenous populations
- Female predominance (2:1)

### Pathogenesis
- **Autoimmune** sequela of Group A Streptococcal pharyngitis (M-protein cross-reactivity)
- Recurrent episodes → chronic inflammation
- **Commissural fusion** (pathognomonic), leaflet thickening, chordal shortening, calcification

### Pathology
- "**Fish-mouth**" or "**buttonhole**" mitral orifice
- **Leaflet thickening**, particularly at free edges
- **Chordal fusion and shortening**
- **Subvalvular** involvement
- **Left atrial** dilation, hypertrophy, **calcification of wall** (MacCallum patch)
- Often co-exists with **aortic valve** involvement (RHD multi-valvular)

### Clinical Implications
- Almost always with **history of ARF** (recurrent)
- May co-exist with **MR, AS, AR** (rheumatic multi-valvular)
- **Lone MS** is rheumatic until proven otherwise
- Secondary prophylaxis (benzathine penicillin G)

## 3. Non-Rheumatic MS

### Calcific Mitral Annular Calcification (MAC)
- **Degenerative** calcification of mitral annulus
- Predominantly **elderly**, especially women
- **Calcium extends into leaflets** → functional narrowing
- Often associated with **aortic stenosis**, hypertension, diabetes, CKD
- Echo shows bright, echogenic annulus
- Often mild → moderate; rarely severe

### Congenital MS
- **Parachute mitral valve** (all chords insert on single papillary muscle)
- **Supravalvular mitral ring**
- **Double-orifice mitral valve**
- **Shone complex** (multiple left-sided obstructions)
- Presents in infancy / childhood

### Inflammatory / Autoimmune
- **SLE** (Libman-Sacks endocarditis → leaflet thickening)
- **Rheumatoid arthritis** (rare)
- **Ankylosing spondylitis**
- **IgG4-related disease**
- **Carcinoid** (typically right-sided, but pulmonary clearance defect can affect left)

### Infiltrative
- **Amyloidosis** (leaflet thickening)
- **Mucopolysaccharidoses**

### Iatrogenic
- **Post-mitral valve repair** (surgical stenosis)
- **Post-TEER** (iatrogenic MS, especially with baseline annular calcification)
- **Radiation** (mantle field)

### Others
- **Tumour** (atrial myxoma obstructing mitral inflow)
- **Ball-valve thrombus** (in LA, mimics MS)
- **Cor triatriatum** (congenital LA membrane)

## 4. Comparative Table

| Feature | **Rheumatic MS** | **Non-Rheumatic MS** |
|---|---|---|
| **Age** | Younger | Older (calcific) / Childhood (congenital) |
| **Aetiology** | Post-streptococcal autoimmune | Degenerative, congenital, inflammatory |
| **Commissural fusion** | **Yes** (pathognomonic) | No |
| **Subvalvular disease** | Yes (chordal) | No (mostly annular) |
| **Co-existing valve disease** | Aortic, tricuspid | Aortic stenosis (calcific) |
| **LAA thrombus** | Common | Rare |
| **AF prevalence** | High | Lower |
| **Wilkins score** | Applicable | Not really |
| **PMBV** | Effective | **Not effective** |
| **Prophylaxis** | Benzathine Pen G | Treat underlying cause |

## 5. Diagnosis
- **TTE** — first-line
  - **MVA planimetry** (rheumatic: orifice at leaflet tips)
  - **Pressure half-time** for MVA (220/PHT)
  - **Mean gradient** (continuous wave Doppler)
  - **Commissural fusion** (parasternal short axis) — rheumatic hallmark
  - **MAC** — calcific deposits at annulus
- **TOE** — pre-PMBV (exclude LA thrombus), assess MAC
- **Wilkins score** (rheumatic) — leaflet thickness, mobility, calcification, subvalvular

## 6. Therapeutic Implications
- **Rheumatic MS** → PMBV if suitable (Wilkins ≤ 8, no MR, no LA thrombus, pliable)
- **MAC MS** → PMBV less effective (heavy calcification, no commissural fusion); surgery often needed
- **Congenital MS** → surgical repair / replacement
- **Inflammatory** → treat underlying condition

## 7. Global Health Note
- **RHD is the leading cause of MS worldwide** and a disease of poverty
- WHO, World Heart Federation, and RHD Action push for **RHD control** in endemic countries
- **Secondary prophylaxis** with benzathine penicillin G prevents progression
- **Echocardiographic screening** in schoolchildren in endemic regions picks up subclinical RHD

> **Pearls**
> - **Rheumatic MS = commissural fusion** (pathognomonic)
> - **Non-rheumatic MS** = calcific MAC, congenital, inflammatory
> - **PMBV** works for rheumatic MS — not for calcific MAC
> - **Rheumatic MS** often co-exists with AR / AS / TR
> - **Lone MS** is rheumatic until proven otherwise
