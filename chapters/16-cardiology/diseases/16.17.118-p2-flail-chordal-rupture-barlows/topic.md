# P2 Flail, Chordal Rupture, Barlow's

## 1. Definitions
- **Flail leaflet** — leaflet reverses into LA during systole (chordal rupture)
- **P2 flail** — most common (middle scallop of posterior leaflet)
- **Barlow's disease** — diffuse myxomatous degeneration, multi-segment prolapse, redundant tissue, thick leaflets
- **Fibroelastic deficiency (FED)** — focal, thin leaflets, single segment, elderly

## 2. Pathology
- Myxomatous degeneration, chordal elongation/rupture
- Posterior leaflet prolapse 2–3× more common than anterior

## 3. Clinical
- Acute severe MR from chordal rupture → acute pulmonary edema
- Mid-systolic click ± late systolic murmur
- Holosystolic murmur if severe MR

## 4. Management
- **Acute severe MR** — emergency surgery if unstable
- **Chronic severe primary MR** — surgery at symptoms, EF 30–60%, ESD <40, AF
- **Repair technique** — quadrangular/triangular resection, neochordae, annuloplasty ring
- Repair success >90% in expert centers, mortality <1%
- Percutaneous edge-to-edge (MitraClip) for high-risk

> **Pearls** — **P2 flail** = most common cause of acute severe primary MR. Repair has **lower mortality** than replacement and avoids prosthetic complications. Refer to **high-volume mitral surgeons** for best outcomes.
