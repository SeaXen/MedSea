# Hypertension — Workup

## 1. Definition
Hypertension workup has two goals: (1) identify **secondary causes** (5–10% of cases, often curable) and (2) assess **target organ damage** (TOD) and overall CV risk.

## 2. Key Features
- **Routine workup**: ECG, echo (LVH), urinalysis, **spot urine albumin:creatinine ratio (UACR)**, BMP (K⁺, Cr, eGFR), fasting glucose/HbA1c, lipid panel, fundoscopy
- **Secondary causes — clues**:
  - **Renal artery stenosis**: young woman (fibromuscular dysplasia) or older with atherosclerotic disease; abdominal bruit
  - **Primary aldosteronism**: refractory HTN + spontaneous hypokalaemia
  - **Pheochromocytoma**: paroxysmal HTN, headache, sweating, palpitations
  - **Cushing**: typical clinical features
  - **Coarctation**: BP differential upper/lower limbs, radio-femoral delay
  - **OSA**: obesity, snoring, daytime sleepiness
  - **Drugs**: NSAIDs, OCP, cocaine, decongestants, steroids
- **Screen for TOD**: brain (stroke/TIA), heart (LVH, CAD), kidney (CKD), eye (retinopathy), peripheral arteries (PAD)

## 3. Management
- Treat based on cause
- **Renovascular**: imaging (CT/MR/US) → angioplasty (FMD), medical (atherosclerotic)
- **Primary aldosteronism**: aldosterone:renin ratio → adrenal CT → surgery vs mineralocorticoid antagonist
- **Pheochromocytoma**: 24h urinary metanephrines → CT/MIBG → α-blockade **before** β-blockade
- **Coarctation**: echo/CT/MR → repair
- **Sleep study** for OSA suspicion

> **Pearls**
> - Screen for **secondary causes** in: young onset, refractory, abrupt onset
> - **Aldosterone:renin ratio** for primary hyperaldosteronism (stop interfering drugs first)
> - Pheochromocytoma: **α-block first, β-block after**
> - **UACR** is the simplest screen for hypertensive nephropathy
