# Plaque Rupture and Thrombosis

## 1. Introduction
**Plaque rupture** (60–70%) and **plaque erosion** (25–30%) are the dominant mechanisms triggering ACS. The final common pathway is **intracoronary thrombus** — partial (NSTEMI/UA) or occlusive (STEMI).

## 2. Plaque Rupture

### Pathogenesis
- **Vulnerable plaque** (TCFA): thin fibrous cap (<65 μm), large necrotic core, macrophage-rich
- **Macrophage MMPs** (MMP-1, MMP-2, MMP-9) degrade collagen
- **Apoptosis of SMCs** in cap (IFN-γ from Th1) → cap thinning
- **Mechanical stress** (HR, BP, vasospasm) + inflammation → **fissure**
- Exposes **lipid core + tissue factor** to blood

### Trigger Factors
- **Morning surge** (sympathetic) — peak MI 6–12 am
- **Heavy exertion**, emotional stress
- **Cocaine, methamphetamine**
- **Infection** (flu, sepsis)
- **Cold weather**

## 3. Plaque Erosion
- **Intact fibrous cap** (unlike rupture)
- **Endothelial apoptosis** + neutrophil infiltration
- More common in **women, smokers, younger**
- **Smooth muscle-rich plaque**, less lipid core
- Thrombus forms on denuded intima

## 4. Calcified Nodule
- **Eruptive calcification** protrudes into lumen
- **Elderly, dialysis, diabetics**
- 5–10% of ACS
- Distinct from rupture/erosion

## 5. Thrombotic Cascade

### Platelet Adhesion
- **Subendothelial collagen + vWF** → **GPIb** (platelet) binds vWF
- **GPVI** binds collagen (most important)

### Platelet Activation
- **TxA₂, ADP (P2Y₁₂), thrombin**, serotonin, epinephrine
- Inside-out signalling → **GP IIb/IIIa (αIIbβ3)** activated (binds fibrinogen)

### Aggregation
- **Fibrinogen** bridges activated GP IIb/IIIa
- **Platelet plug** (primary haemostasis)

### Coagulation
- **Tissue factor** from plaque binds **FVIIa**
- Extrinsic pathway → **FXa → thrombin**
- Thrombin: **fibrinogen → fibrin** + activates FXIII (crosslinks)
- **Fibrin mesh** stabilises thrombus

## 6. STEMI vs NSTEMI
- **STEMI** = **complete, occlusive** thrombus (TIMI 0–1) → transmural ischaemia → ST elevation
- **NSTEMI/UA** = **partial, non-occlusive** thrombus (TIMI 2–3) → subendocardial ischaemia
- **Distal embolisation** of thrombus also contributes

## 7. Consequences
- **Myocardial ischaemia** → angina
- **Infarction** if sustained (STEMI >20–30 min)
- **Microvascular obstruction (MVO)** — distal embolisation, spasm, oedema
- **Reperfusion injury** — stunning, no-reflow, reperfusion arrhythmia
- **Remodelling** — LV dilation, aneurysm, HF

## 8. Vulnerable Blood (Thrombogenicity)
- ↑ **fibrinogen, FVII, PAI-1**
- Smoking, OCP, dehydration, sepsis
- **Hypercoagulable states** — Factor V Leiden, prothrombin gene, nephrotic syndrome

## 9. Prevention / Treatment Targets
- **Antiplatelet**: aspirin (COX-1 → ↓TxA₂), P2Y₁₂ inhibitors (clopidogrel, ticagrelor, prasugrel)
- **Anticoagulant**: heparin (UFH, enoxaparin, fondaparinux, bivalirudin)
- **Statins** — pleiotropic (plaque stabilisation, ↓ inflammation)
- **ACEi / ARB** — vascular protection
- **PCSK9i** — robust LDL reduction
- **Colchicine** (LoDoCo2) — anti-inflammatory

## 10. Plaque Stabilisation
- Statins → **↑ collagen, ↓ MMPs, ↓ macrophages, ↓ lipid**
- Reduces acute events even before stenosis regression

> **Pearls**
> - **Rupture 60–70%, erosion 25–30%, calcified nodule 5–10%**
> - **TCFA** = thin cap (<65 μm) + large necrotic core + macrophages
> - **GP IIb/IIIa (αIIbβ3)** = final common pathway of aggregation
> - **STEMI = occlusive thrombus**; **NSTEMI = partial** thrombus
> - **Morning surge** (sympathetic) — peak MI 6–12 am
> - **Statins stabilise plaque** independent of LDL reduction
