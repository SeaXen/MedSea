# Cushing Syndrome and the Heart

## 1. Definition
- Chronic **hypercortisolism** — ACTH-dependent (pituitary, ectopic) or independent (adrenal)
- Exogenous steroids are most common cause

## 2. Cardiovascular Effects
- **HTN**: 80% (Na+ retention, RAS activation, vascular reactivity)
- **Central obesity**, dyslipidemia, **impaired glucose/diabetes**
- **Metabolic syndrome** → accelerated atherosclerosis
- **LVH**, diastolic dysfunction
- **CAD**, stroke, VTE (hypercoagulable)

## 3. Diagnosis
- **Initial**: late-night salivary cortisol, 24h urine cortisol, low-dose DST
- **Confirmation**: midnight cortisol or 48h low-dose DST
- **Etiology**: ACTH → high-dose DST ± CRH test, MRI pituitary
- **Adrenal**: CT/MRI; **ectopic ACTH**: CT chest/abdomen

## 4. Management
- **Transsphenoidal surgery** (Cushing disease)
- **Adrenalectomy** (adrenal adenoma)
- **Medical** (pre-op or inoperable): ketoconazole, metyrapone, osilodrostat, mifepristone, pasireotide
- Aggressive **CV risk factor control**: HTN, lipids, glucose, weight
- Consider **aspirin/statin** based on risk

> **Pearls**: HTN + central obesity + DM + striae + **proximal myopathy** = Cushing. **Screening in any HTN + DM + obesity** if other features. Treat underlying cause; treat CV risk aggressively.
