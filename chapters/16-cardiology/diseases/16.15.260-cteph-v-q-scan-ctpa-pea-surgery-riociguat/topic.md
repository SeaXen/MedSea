# CTEPH: V/Q Scan, CTPA, PEA Surgery, Riociguat

## 1. Definition
**Chronic Thromboembolic Pulmonary Hypertension (CTEPH)** — pre-capillary PH (mPAP >20, PAWP ≤15, PVR >2) caused by organised thromboembolic material persisting ≥3 months after adequate anticoagulation. WHO **Group 4**.

## 2. Pathophysiology
- Incomplete resolution of acute PE → organised fibrotic webs/bands
- Progressive vascular remodelling in non-occluded segments
- Leads to right heart failure if untreated

## 3. Diagnosis

**V/Q scan (first-line screening):** At least one segmental perfusion defect. **More sensitive than CTPA** for chronic disease.

**CT pulmonary angiography (CTPA):** Webs, bands, intimal irregularities, organised thrombi, mosaic perfusion, hypertrophied bronchial collaterals.

**Pulmonary angiography (gold standard):** Defines surgical accessibility — webs, rings, complete occlusions, pouch defects.

**RHC:** Mandatory — confirm pre-capillary PH, measure PVR.

**Rule out** active PE, in-situ thrombosis, sarcoma.

## 4. Treatment

**A. Pulmonary Endarterectomy (PEA) — curative**
- Indication: surgically accessible disease, PVR >300 dynes·sec·cm⁻⁵
- Outcomes: dramatic PVR reduction, normalisation of haemodynamics in many, 80–90% 5-yr survival in expert centres
- Requires deep hypothermic circulatory arrest

**B. Riociguat (medical therapy) — CHEST trial**
- Soluble guanylate cyclase stimulator; FDA-approved for inoperable/persistent CTEPH
- Dose: 1 mg TID → titrate to 2.5 mg TID every 2 weeks (avoid if SBP <95)
- Improves 6MWD, PVR, WHO FC
- May also be used as bridge to PEA

**C. Balloon Pulmonary Angioplasty (BPA)**
- For inoperable segmental/subsegmental disease or residual PH post-PEA
- Multiple staged procedures; complications include reperfusion pulmonary oedema
- Emerging as adjunct/alternative at expert centres

**D. Lifelong anticoagulation** in all patients (warfarin preferred; DOACs acceptable but data less robust).

## 5. Follow-up
- Echo, 6MWD, BNP, RHC periodically
- Lifetime cardiology follow-up — recurrent disease possible

> **Pearls**
> - **V/Q scan is the screening test of choice** — don't miss CTEPH.
> - PEA is potentially **curative** — refer all to expert centre.
> - Riociguat and BPA are options for inoperable/persistent disease.
