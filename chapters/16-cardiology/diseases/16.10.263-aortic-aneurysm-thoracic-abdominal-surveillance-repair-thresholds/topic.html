CARDIOLOGY
Aortic aneurysm (thoracic, abdominal - surveillance, repair thresholds)
1 Definition

Localised dilatation > 1.5× normal aortic diameter. True (all 3 layers) vs false (contained rupture). Risk of rupture, dissection, compression.

Risk Factors
  • Age, male sex, smoking (strongest modifiable)
  • Hypertension, atherosclerosis
  • Connective tissue disease (Marfan, Loeys-Dietz, Ehlers-Danlos)
  • Bicuspid aortic valve (BAV)
  • Family history, vasculitis (Takayasu, GCA, IgG4)
2 Classification by Location
  • TAA (thoracic): ascending > root > arch > descending
  • AAA (abdominal): infrarenal (90%) > juxtarenal
  • Thoracoabdominal (Crawford I–V)
3 Clinical Features
  • Usually asymptomatic — incidental on CXR / US / CT
  • Expanding: deep, boring pain (chest / back / abdomen)
  • Compression: SVC, RLN (hoarseness), bronchi
  • AAA: pulsatile epigastric mass, hypotension if ruptured
  • Rupture: hypotension, abdominal / back pain — mortality 80–90% pre-hospital
4 Repair Thresholds
LocationThresholdRepair
---------
**Ascending / root (general)**≥ 55 mmOpen (Bentall / valve-sparing)
**BAV without risk**≥ 55 mmOpen
**Marfan**≥ 50 mm (45 if FHx / rapid growth)Open
**Loeys-Dietz**≥ 42 mmOpen (more aggressive)
**Descending thoracic**≥ 60 mm (55 if risk)**TEVAR**
**AAA (infrarenal)**≥ 5.5 cm (men), 5.0 (women)**EVAR** (if anatomy suitable) / open
**Rapid growth**> 5 mm/yearConsider earlier repair
5 Surveillance
  • AAA: US every 2–3 y if 3.0–3.9 cm; annually if 4.0–4.9 cm; repair at 5.5 cm
  • TAA: CT / MRI at 6–12 month intervals
  • Screening: USPSTF — one-time US in men 65–75 who ever smoked
6 Key Pearls
Pearls
  • AAA ≥ 5.5 cm (men) → repair; 5.0 cm (women)
  • EVAR preferred for AAA if anatomy suitable (lower peri-op mortality)
  • Marfan → 50 mm; Loeys-Dietz → 42 mm (more aggressive)
  • BAV → 55 mm (independent of valve severity)
  • AAA screening: men 65–75, ever-smokers (one-time US)
