# SCAI shock stages, MCS escalation

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 16_Cardiac_Emergencies | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**SCAI shock stages, MCS escalation** (Pulmonary Embolism, PE): acute obstruction of pulmonary artery or its branches by thrombus (usually from DVT — deep vein thrombosis of leg, pelvic, or upper extremity veins). **VTE** (venous thromboembolism) = PE + DVT. **Spectrum**: asymptomatic, symptomatic, massive (high-risk, haemodynamic instability), submassive (intermediate-risk, RV dysfunction without shock), low-risk. **Incidence**: 60-120/100,000. **Mortality**: high-risk 25-50%, intermediate 5-25%, low 1-2%.


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Virchow's triad**: stasis, endothelial injury, hypercoagulability. **Risk factors**: surgery (orthopaedic, abdominal, pelvic, neuro), trauma, immobilisation, malignancy, OCP/HRT, pregnancy/postpartum, prior VTE, thrombophilia (factor V Leiden, protein C/S, antithrombin, prothrombin G20210A, antiphospholipid), obesity, smoking, age, central lines, COVID-19, IBD, nephrotic. **Pathophysiology**: ventilation-perfusion mismatch (dead space), hypoxaemia, hyperventilation, RV afterload (vasoconstriction, mechanical obstruction), RV dilation/dysfunction, ↓LV preload, hypotension, shock, death. **Infarction** (1-10%, pleuritic chest pain, haemoptysis).


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

**Symptoms**: dyspnoea (sudden, 50-80%), pleuritic chest pain (40-65%), cough (20-30%), haemoptysis (5-15%), syncope (massive, 5-15%), leg pain/swelling (DVT 30-50%). **Examination**: tachypnoea, tachycardia, hypoxia, low-grade fever, pleural rub, loud P2, JVP, RV heave, signs of DVT (calf swelling, tenderness, Homan's sign). **Massive**: shock, hypotension, syncope, PEA arrest, sudden death.


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

**Wells score** (clinical probability): DVT signs (3), PE most likely (3), HR>100 (1.5), immobilisation/surgery (1.5), previous DVT/PE (1.5), haemoptysis (1), malignancy (1). **Score >6** high, **2-6** moderate, **<2** low. **PERC rule** (if all 8 negative, can avoid workup in low probability). **D-dimer** (high sensitivity, low specificity — ↑ in malignancy, infection, pregnancy, age, surgery, trauma). **CTPA** (gold standard, multidetector CT, sensitivity 95-99%, specificity 90%, contrast). **V/Q scan** (alternative if CTPA contraindicated, normal CXR, pregnancy — less radiation to foetus, no contrast). **Pulmonary angiogram** (gold standard, rarely done). **Bedside echo** (RV dysfunction, McConnell's sign, 60/60 sign, septal flattening, RV dilation, free wall hypokinesia, PASP). **CXR** (Westermark sign — oligaemia, Hampton's hump — wedge, pleural effusion, PA enlargement). **ECG** (sinus tachycardia most common, S1Q3T3, RV strain, RBBB, P-pulmonale, AF). **ABG** (hypoxaemia, hypocapnia, A-a gradient ↑). **Troponin, BNP** (RV dysfunction, prognosis). **Leg Doppler** (DVT, in 30-50% of PE). **MRPA** (alternative, no radiation, no contrast).


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**Massive (high-risk)**: SBP<90 mmHg or shock or cardiac arrest (mortality 25-50%). **Submassive (intermediate-risk)**: RV dysfunction (echo/CT/biomarker) WITHOUT shock (mortality 5-25%). **Low-risk**: no RV dysfunction, no shock, normal biomarkers (mortality 1-2%). **PESI/sPESI** (Pulmonary Embolism Severity Index): age, sex, cancer, COPD, HR, SBP, SpO2, temp. **Risk stratification**: 1) Clinical (PESI/sPESI), 2) Imaging (RV/LV ratio >0.9 on CT, echo), 3) Biomarkers (troponin, BNP), 4) Lactate. **Bova score** for intermediate risk.


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**Haemodynamic support**: oxygen, IV fluids (cautious — over-resuscitation worsens RV, monitor), vasopressors (norepinephrine first-line for shock), inotropes (dobutamine if low CO and adequate BP), mechanical support (VA-ECMO for refractory). **Anticoagulation** (immediate, if no contraindication): **LMWH** (enoxaparin 1mg/kg SC BD, preferred), **UFH** (if CrCl<30, thrombolysis candidate, haemodynamic instability, pregnancy — IV 80U/kg bolus, 18U/kg/h), **fondaparinux** (subcutaneous, factor Xa, alternative), **DOAC** (rivaroxaban — initial 15mg BD x 21d, then 20mg OD, EINSTEIN-PE; apixaban — 10mg BD x 7d, then 5mg BD, AMPLIFY; edoxaban, dabigatran — after 5-10d parenteral). **Thrombolysis** (massive/high-risk): **Alteplase** (100mg over 2h, or 0.6mg/kg over 15min if cardiac arrest imminent, or 50mg if <65kg), **Tenecteplase** (weight-adjusted, single bolus). Reduce dose if >75y (50mg). **Catheter-directed thrombolysis (CDT)**: catheter into PA, lower dose, less bleeding (single-arm, OPTALYSE, SEATTLE II). **Surgical embolectomy**: if thrombolysis contraindicated or failed. **IVC filter**: if anticoagulation contraindicated or failed or recurrent PE despite anticoagulation. **Duration**: provoked (reversible risk) — 3 months. Unprovoked — 3-6 months minimum, often indefinite if high risk. Cancer — LMWH or DOAC, indefinite or until cancer resolved. **Recurrent**: indefinite. **Thrombophilia** (factor V Leiden, protein C/S def, antiphospholipid) — indefinite if unprovoked, family Hx. **Compression stockings** — no longer recommended routinely (SOX trial). **Pulmonary thromboendarterectomy (PTE)** for CTEPH if surgically accessible.


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

**Mortality** 25-50% (high-risk), 5-25% (intermediate), 1-2% (low). **Recurrent VTE** (5-10% in 1y if unprovoked). **Chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension (CTEPH)** (1-5% of PE, screen with echo, V/Q scan). **Right heart failure** (acute, chronic). **Infarction** (pleuritic, haemoptysis). **Cardiac arrest** (PEA, refractory VF/VT). **Haemorrhagic transformation** of ischaemic stroke. **Anticoagulation complications**: major bleeding (1-3%/y), intracranial (0.1-0.5%/y).


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

**Pregnancy**: LMWH preferred (UFH if CrCl low, near delivery), DOAC contraindicated, V/Q scan if needed (low radiation to foetus, breastfeeding safe), IVC filter if anticoagulation contraindicated, fondaparinux limited data. **Cancer**: LMWH or DOAC (rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban), indefinite or until cancer resolved, Hokusai-VTE Cancer, SELECT-D, ADAM VTE, Caravaggio. **Thrombophilia**: indefinite if unprovoked, family Hx. **Elderly**: careful dosing, falls, renal function, lower DOAC dose. **COVID-19**: high VTE risk, standard or escalated anticoagulation (HEP-COVID, multiplatform trial). **HIT**: type II, days 5-10, >50% platelet fall, do PF4 antibody, stop heparin, switch to argatroban, bivalirudin, fondaparinux, DOAC. **IVC filter**: temporary, retrievable, in contraindication to anticoagulation.


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

**ESC 2020 PE (EHJ)**: full guideline. **2024 update**: PERT (Pulmonary Embolism Response Team), risk stratification. **NICE NG158 (2020, updated 2023)**: VTE. **CHEST 2019 (ACCP)**: antithrombotic guidelines. **Wells, Geneva, PESI, sPESI, Bova**: risk scores. **EINSTEIN-PE, AMPLIFY, Hokusai-VTE, RECOVER**: DOAC trials.


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

**D-dimer** is sensitive but not specific. **CTPA** if high pre-test probability. **PESI/sPESI** for risk. **Massive PE = SBP<90 or shock** — thrombolysis. **Submassive = RV dysfunction without shock** — anticoagulation, consider CDT if deteriorating. **DOAC dose** — follow loading (rivaroxaban, apixaban). **Pregnancy** — LMWH, not DOAC, not warfarin. **V/Q scan** in pregnancy is OK (lower foetal radiation than CTPA). **Cancer** — LMWH or DOAC. **CTEPH** — screen at 3-6m. **HIT** — switch to non-heparin anticoagulation. **IVC filter** — retrievable, in contraindication to anticoagulation. **Surgery, immobilisation, OCP, pregnancy, cancer** — VTE prophylaxis.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

**Virchow's triad**: stasis, endothelial injury, hypercoagulability. **Wells**: DVT signs, PE likely, HR>100, immobilisation, prior VTE, haemoptysis, malignancy. **PERC**: rule out low probability. **Massive = high-risk = SBP<90 or shock**: thrombolysis. **Submassive = intermediate = RV dysfunction no shock**: anticoag ± CDT. **Low**: no RV dysfunction. **LMWH > UFH > DOAC** (in acute). **DOAC** — rivaroxaban, apixaban (loading), edoxaban, dabigatran. **Alteplase 100mg/2h**. **Tenecteplase** weight-adjusted. **CTEPH** 1-5% of PE. **V/Q scan** for CTEPH. **IVC filter** if anticoag contraindicated. **HIT** — switch. **Pregnancy** — LMWH, V/Q scan.


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Aortic dissection, ACS, pericarditis, myocarditis, pneumonia, pleural effusion, pneumothorax, asthma/COPD exacerbation, panic attack, MSK chest pain, anxiety, syncope (vasovagal, cardiogenic, orthostatic), lung cancer, vasculitis.


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

Wells score, D-dimer (low-intermediate probability), CTPA (high probability or +d-dimer), V/Q scan (CTPA contraindicated, pregnancy), bedside echo (RV dysfunction), troponin, BNP, ABG, CXR, ECG, leg Doppler, thrombophilia workup (unprovoked — factor V Leiden, prothrombin, protein C/S, antithrombin, antiphospholipid, homocysteine, MTHFR).

B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography

A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy

A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.
