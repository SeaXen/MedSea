# SCAI shock stages, MCS escalation (IABP → Impella → VA-ECMO)

## 1. SCAI Shock Staging (A–E)
The Society for Cardiovascular Angiography and Interventions (SCAI) classification stratifies cardiogenic shock severity for triage and treatment escalation.

| Stage | Hemodynamics | Perfusion | Treatment |
|-------|--------------|-----------|-----------|
| **A — At risk** | Normal | Normal | Observation, treat underlying cause |
| **B — Beginning** | Hypotension, CI ≥2.2 | No hypoperfusion | Fluids, low-dose inotropes |
| **C — Classic** | Hypotension, CI <2.2 | Hypoperfusion | Inotropes + vasopressors, consider MCS |
| **D — Deteriorating** | Worsening despite therapy | Worsening | **Escalate MCS** urgently |
| **E — Extremis** | Refractory, cardiac arrest | Severe | **VA-ECMO + CPR** |

## 2. Escalation Pathway
**Stage A → B**: Fluid challenge, low-dose inotrope (dobutamine 2–5 µg/kg/min)
**Stage B → C**: Add vasopressor (norepinephrine 0.05–0.5 µg/kg/min), ↑ inotrope
**Stage C → D**: **Initiate MCS** — IABP, Impella, or VA-ECMO
**Stage D → E**: **Escalate to VA-ECMO** ± add LV vent (Impella), consider ECMO-CPR

## 3. Device Comparison
| Device | Support | Mechanism | Limitations |
|--------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **IABP** | 0.5–1 L/min | Counterpulsation, ↓ afterload | Modest CO support; no mortality benefit |
| **Impella 2.5/CP** | 2.5–3.5 L/min | LV→aorta axial pump | Hemolysis, limb ischemia |
| **Impella 5.0/5.5** | 5–6 L/min | Full LV support | Surgical cutdown, hemolysis |
| **TandemHeart** | 4–5 L/min | LA→femoral artery | Transseptal puncture, complex |
| **VA-ECMO** | 4–6 L/min | RV bypass + O₂ | ↑Afterload, no LV venting, complications |

## 4. VA-ECMO Indications
- Refractory cardiogenic shock
- Cardiac arrest (eCPR with <60 min downtime)
- Massive PE
- Bridge to LVAD/transplant
- Failure of other MCS

## 5. VA-ECMO Complications
- **Limb ischemia** (need distal perfusion cannula)
- **LV distension** (add Impella vent or atrial septostomy)
- **Harlequin (North-South) syndrome**: differential oxygenation to upper body
- Bleeding, thrombosis, hemolysis
- Infection, stroke

## 6. When to Escalate
- Persistent hypotension despite ≥2 vasopressors
- Lactate >4 mmol/L
- End-organ dysfunction (oliguria, AMS, ↑LFTs)
- Worsening hemodynamics on existing MCS
- Cardiac arrest (impending) → VA-ECMO

> **Pearls**
> - **IABP-SHOCK II**: IABP no mortality benefit in cardiogenic shock
> - **ECLS-SHOCK**: VA-ECMO may ↑ mortality; use selectively
> - Always consider **LV venting** with VA-ECMO if LV not ejecting
> - **Door-to-unloading** concept: early MCS improves outcomes
> - Escalate **before** cardiac arrest — once arrest, prognosis poor
