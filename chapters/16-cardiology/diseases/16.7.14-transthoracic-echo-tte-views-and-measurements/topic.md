# Transthoracic Echo (TTE) — Views and Measurements

## 1. Introduction
TTE is the **first-line cardiac imaging test** for structure, function, valves, and haemodynamics. Standard views follow ASE/BSE guidelines (17-segment model).

## 2. Standard Views

### Parasternal Long-Axis (PLAX)
- LV (inflow/outflow), LVOT, aortic valve, mitral valve, aortic root, proximal ascending aorta, pericardium
- Measurements: **LVIDd (≤5.5 cm ♂ / 5.0 cm ♀), LVIDs (≤4.0 cm), IVSd, PWd (≤1.0 cm), aortic root (≤3.7 cm), LA diameter (≤4.0 cm)**

### Parasternal Short-Axis (PSAX)
- **Great vessels level** — aortic valve, coronary ostia, RA, TV, PV
- **Mitral level** — MV (fish-mouth)
- **Papillary muscle level** — LV cavity, paps
- **Apex level** — apical LV segments

### Apical 4-Chamber (A4C)
- All 4 chambers, both AV valves, interatrial/interventricular septa
- LV and RV size, function, MV/TV
- **TAPSE** (RV systolic excursion) — normal ≥1.7 cm
- LV ejection fraction (Simpson's biplane)

### Apical 2-Chamber (A2C)
- LV (anterior + inferior walls), LA, MV
- Used with A4C for biplane Simpson's EF

### Apical 3-Chamber / Apical Long-Axis
- LVOT, AV, MV, aortic root
- Used for LVOT/AV Doppler

### Apical 5-Chamber
- A4C + LVOT/AV
- AV/LVOT Doppler

### Subcostal
- 4 chambers, IVC, pericardium, liver
- **IVC** — diameter <2.1 cm, collapse >50% = RA pressure 3 mmHg (normal)

### Suprasternal
- Aortic arch, branches, descending aorta, PDA

## 3. Key Measurements (BSE Normal Values)
| Parameter | Normal |
|---|---|
| **LVIDd** | ♂ ≤5.5 cm, ♀ ≤5.0 cm |
| **LVIDs** | ≤4.0 cm |
| **IVSd / PWd** | 0.6–1.0 cm |
| **LV EF (Simpson's)** | ≥55% |
| **LA volume index** | ≤34 mL/m² |
| **Aortic root** | ≤3.7 cm |
| **RA area** | ≤18 cm² |
| **RV basal diameter** | ≤4.1 cm |
| **TAPSE** | ≥1.7 cm |
| **PASP (TR jet + RAP)** | ≤35 mmHg |
| **MV E/A ratio** | 0.8–2.0 |
| **MV E/e' (septal)** | <8 normal, 8–14 grey zone, >14 elevated LVFP |
| **LV mass index** | ♂ ≤115, ♀ ≤95 g/m² |

## 4. LV Function Assessment
- **Visual estimation** (eyeball EF) — quick, accurate in experienced hands
- **Simpson's biplane** (A4C + A2C) — gold standard for EF
- **Fractional shortening** (M-mode) — LVIDd − LVIDs / LVIDd × 100; normal 25–45%
- **MAPSE** ≥1.0 cm — longitudinal LV function
- **GLS** (global longitudinal strain) — normal <−18%; subclinical dysfunction

## 5. Diastolic Function (Simplified)
| Normal | Grade I | Grade II | Grade III |
|---|---|---|---|
| E/A 0.8–1.5 | E/A <0.8 | E/A 0.8–1.5 with E/e' >14 | E/A >2 with E/e' >14 |
| LAVI normal, TR vel <2.8 m/s | LAVI normal | ↑LAVI, TR vel >2.8 | Restrictive |

## 6. Common Pathology Patterns
- **WMA** in vascular territory → ischaemia/infarct
- **Dilated LV + low EF** → DCM, tachycardia-induced, myocarditis
- **Asymmetric septal hypertrophy + SAM** → HCM/HOCM
- **Concentric LVH** → pressure overload (AS, HTN)
- **RV dilatation + TAPSE ↓** → PE, PH, ARVC
- **Pericardial effusion** → measure largest gap, look for tamponade physiology
- **Valve lesions** — see Doppler topic

> **Pearls**
> - **Simpson's biplane** is the standard for EF (avoid "eyeball EF" in formal reports).
> - **HYPERdynamic LV** in apical ballooning (Takotsubo) = "apical ballooning with hypercontractile base."
> - **IVC assessment** is the most reliable single bedside marker of RA pressure.
> - **GLS <−18%** is normal; values closer to zero are worse; "strain" is more sensitive than EF for early dysfunction.
