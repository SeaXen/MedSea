# Surgical Indications in Chronic Aortic Regurgitation

## 1. Principle
Surgery is indicated when the **LV can no longer compensate** — clinically (symptoms) or subclinically (declining EF, progressive dilation). Aortic valve replacement ± root repair is the definitive treatment.

## 2. Indications for Surgery (ESC 2021 / ACC/AHA 2022)

### Class I (Strong)
- **Symptomatic severe AR** (any LVEF) — operate regardless of EF
- **Asymptomatic severe AR with LVEF ≤ 50%** — operate (subclinical dysfunction)
- **Patients undergoing other cardiac surgery** (CABG, ascending aortic surgery) with severe AR

### Class IIa (Reasonable)
- **Asymptomatic severe AR with LVEF 50–55%** (declining)
- **Asymptomatic severe AR with progressive LV dilation:**
  - **LVESD > 50 mm** (or >25 mm/m² indexed)
  - **LVEDD > 65 mm** (or >33 mm/m² indexed)
- **Asymptomatic severe AR with progressive EF decline on serial echo** (≥3 consecutive measurements)

### Class IIb / Consider
- **Asymptomatic severe AR with progressive increase in AR severity**
- **New onset symptoms on stress testing** in previously asymptomatic
- **Rapid LV dilation** (>5 mm/yr in LVESD)

## 3. Decision Algorithm

```
SEVERE AR
   ├── Symptomatic ────────────────► OPERATE (any EF)
   ├── Asymptomatic, EF <50% ──────► OPERATE
   ├── Asymptomatic, EF 50–55% ────► Consider (serial echo)
   ├── Asymptomatic, EF >55%
   │     ├── LVESD >50 mm ────────► OPERATE
   │     ├── LVEDD >70 mm ────────► OPERATE
   │     ├── Progression rate ↑ ──► Consider
   │     └── Stable, normal LV ───► Follow (TTE q 6–12 mo)
   └── Concomitant root ≥55 mm ───► OPERATE
```

## 4. Choosing the Procedure

| Procedure | Indication |
|---|---|
| **Isolated SAVR** | Native valve disease, normal root |
| **AVR + Root replacement (Bentall)** | Root >45–55 mm (aetiology dependent) |
| **Valve-sparing root replacement (David / Yacoub)** | Young patient, normal cusps (e.g., Marfan) |
| **Ross procedure** | Children, young adults (autograft) |
| **TAVI** | High surgical risk, suitable anatomy — **AR less ideal** (no annular calcium) |

> ⚠️ **TAVI in pure AR** — challenging because device anchors to calcium. Newer devices (JenaValve, JValve) specifically designed for AR. Most still require SAVR.

## 5. Special Populations

### Bicuspid Aortic Valve
- **BAV with severe AR** — same LV criteria apply
- **Aortopathy** — operate on root if >55 mm (or 50 with risk factors)
- TAVI feasible for BAV in selected anatomy (Type 1, no excessive raphe calcium)

### Marfan / Connective Tissue
- **AR + root ≥45 mm** → root replacement (Bentall or valve-sparing)
- Family history of dissection or rapid growth → operate at **40–45 mm**
- Multidisciplinary team: cardiology, cardiac surgery, genetics

### Infective Endocarditis with AR
- Severe acute AR with **pulmonary oedema / shock** → emergency surgery
- **Perivalvular abscess, fistula** → surgery
- Continue antibiotics; surgery timing based on haemodynamics, not antibiotic duration

### Aortic Dissection
- Stanford A with severe AR → **emergent surgery** with valve resuspension or replacement
- Mortality high; rapid operative intervention saves lives

## 6. Pre-Operative Workup
- **TTE + TOE** for valve and LV detail
- **CT angio** of aorta (root, ascending, arch)
- **Coronary angiography** (>40 yr or risk factors — concomitant CABG)
- **PFTs** for high-risk patients
- **Frailty assessment** (grip, gait, ADL)
- **STS / EuroSCORE II** for risk counselling

## 7. Operative Mortality
- **Isolated AVR** in low-risk patients: 1–3%
- **AVR + CABG**: 3–5%
- **Bentall** in elective: 2–4%
- **Emergency surgery** (acute AR, dissection, IE): 8–20%

## 8. Long-Term Post-Operative Follow-up
- **TTE** at discharge, 6–12 months, then yearly
- **Bioprosthetic SVD** at 8–15 years — reintervention planning
- **Mechanical** — lifelong INR monitoring
- **Lifelong endocarditis prophylaxis** (dental procedures)

## 9. When NOT to Operate
- Patient refusal
- End-stage comorbidity (cancer, frailty, advanced dementia) — palliative
- EF <25% with severe chronic AR — very high risk; careful consideration

> **Pearls**
> - **Symptoms = operate** (any LVEF)
> - **EF < 50% = operate** (subclinical LV dysfunction)
> - **LVESD > 50 mm = operate** (pre-dysfunction dilation)
> - **Root ≥ 45–55 mm = consider** (aetiology dependent)
> - **TAVI** rarely first choice for pure AR — device anchoring depends on annular calcium
