# Anticoagulation in Elderly AF

## 1. Challenge
- AF prevalence ↑ with age
- Stroke risk ↑ with age (CHA₂DS₂-VASc)
- Bleeding risk ↑ with age (HAS-BLED)
- Falls risk concern (often overemphasized)

## 2. Evidence
- **DOACs** preferred over warfarin in non-valvular AF
- All 4 DOACs studied in elderly subgroups
- Apixaban + edoxaban have best bleeding profiles in elderly
- Lower-dose regimens available (e.g., apixaban 2.5 mg BID if 2 of 3: age ≥80, weight ≤60 kg, Cr ≥1.5)

## 3. Falls
- Falls are **NOT** an absolute contraindication
- Net clinical benefit favors anticoagulation if CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥3
- Intracranial bleeding risk actually ↓ with DOACs
- Practical: minimize falls risk, treat osteoporosis, vision checks

## 4. Practical Approach
- **Lifelong anticoagulation** if CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥2 (men) or ≥3 (women)
- **DOAC preferred** (apixaban 5 mg BID typical, or 2.5 mg BID if 2 of 3 criteria)
- Avoid warfarin if possible (interactions, monitoring, falls)
- Renal dose adjustment for CrCl <50 (esp. dabigatran)
- **Review medications** (interactions)

## 5. Special Considerations
- **Cognitive impairment** — pill boxes, family supervision
- **Polypharmacy** — avoid aspirin + OAC unless ACS/stent
- **Dementia** — consider goals of care
- **End-of-life** — review need for anticoagulation

> **Pearls** — **Falls ≠ contraindication** to anticoagulation. **Apixaban** often preferred in elderly (best bleeding profile). **Lower dose** if 2 of 3 (age ≥80, weight ≤60 kg, Cr ≥1.5). DOAC > warfarin in most elderly.
