CARDIOLOGY
GDMT quadruple therapy (ARNI/ACEi/ARB, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i)
1 The 4 Pillars

All class I, evidence A in HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%, NYHA II–IV) — all reduce mortality and hospitalisation. Initiate early and up-titrate to target within 4–6 weeks (STRONG-HF 2022).

PillarDrug & Target DoseLandmark Trial
---------
**ARNI**Sacubitril/valsartan **97/103 mg BD** (or ACEi/ARB)PARADIGM-HF
**Beta-blocker**Bisoprolol **10 mg** · Carvedilol **25 mg BD** · Metoprolol succ **200 mg**CIBIS-II, COPERNICUS, MERIT-HF
**MRA**Spironolactone **25 mg** · Eplerenone **50 mg**RALES, EMPHASIS-HF, EPHESUS
**SGLT2i**Dapagliflozin **10 mg** · Empagliflozin **10 mg**DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced
All-cause mortality dropped ~60–70% across 4 pivotal trials when all 4 pillars combined.
2 Initiation Order
  • Inpatient / de novo HF — start ARNI + BB + MRA + SGLT2i all at low dose; up-titrate every 2 weeks
  • SGLT2i is the easiest first drug (no titration, no monitoring)
  • ARNI requires 36-h washout from ACEi (risk of angioedema)
  • BB — start once euvolaemic, not in acute decompensation
3 Class-Specific Notes
  • ARNI preferred over ACEi/ARB — replaces ACEi/ARB, do not combine
  • ACEi/ARB if ARNI not tolerated (cough, angioedema, cost). ARB if ACEi cough
  • BB — only bisoprolol, carvedilol, metoprolol succinate (NOT atenolol, NOT propranolol)
  • MRA — monitor K⁺ and creatinine at 1, 4, 8 weeks then 3-monthly; hold if K⁺ >5.5
  • SGLT2i — works in HFrEF and HFpEF, diabetes-independent, modest HbA1c effect
4 Where to Add Other Therapies
  • Diuretics (loop ± thiazide) — for symptom/congestion only (no mortality benefit)
  • Ivabradine — if sinus rhythm, HR ≥70 despite max BB (SHIFT)
  • Vericiguat — recently hospitalised, worsening HF (VICTORIA)
  • Hydralazine + ISDN — if ACEi/ARB/ARNI intolerant, especially African-Caribbean (A-HeFT)
  • Digoxin — symptomatic despite GDMT; reduces hospitalisation, not mortality (DIG)
  • IV iron (ferric carboxymaltose) — if ferritin <100 or TSAT <20% (AFFIRM-AHF, FAIR-HFpEF)
  • Omega-3 PUFA — modest benefit (REDUCE-IT excluded HF, GISSI-HF neutral)
5 Drugs to **AVOID** in HFrEF
  • Verapamil / diltiazem — negative inotrope
  • Class I antiarrhythmics (flecainide) — pro-arrhythmic in structural HD
  • Thiazolidinediones — fluid retention
  • NSAIDs — Na⁺ retention, antagonism of diuretics and ACEi
  • Saxagliptin, alogliptin — ↑HF hospitalisation
6 Monitoring
  • U&E, K⁺, creatinine at 1, 4, 8 weeks then 3-monthly on ACEi/ARB + MRA
  • BP, HR, weight, symptoms at every visit
  • Echo at 3 months after major change, then yearly
  • BNP/NT-proBNP for prognosis and adherence
  • Recheck iron studies periodically
Pearls - GDMT = Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy; not a single drug - ARNI replaces ACEi/ARB — never combine - Washout 36 h ACEi → ARNI - Beta-blocker in HFrEF = bisoprolol, carvedilol, or metoprolol succinate ONLY - SGLT2i are the easiest drug to start — no titration, benefit within days - Avoid verapamil/diltiazem in HFrEF

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg"> CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE — Quadruple therapy within 4 weeks of HFrEF diagnosis is the standard of care. STRONG-HF (2022) showed early aggressive up-titration cuts 180-day HF readmission/mortality. SGLT2i are class I across the entire LVEF spectrum (HFrEF, HFmrEF, HFpEF). </callout>
