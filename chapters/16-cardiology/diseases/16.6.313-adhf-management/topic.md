# ADHF Management — Acute Decompensated Heart Failure

## 1. Definition
**Acute Decompensated Heart Failure (ADHF)**: Rapid onset or worsening of HF symptoms requiring urgent therapy.

## 2. Four Clinical Profiles (Stevenson)
- **Warm-dry**: compensated, no congestion (~25%)
- **Warm-wet**: congestion, perfusion OK (~50%, most common)
- **Cold-dry**: hypoperfused, no congestion (~5%)
- **Cold-wet**: hypoperfused + congested (~20%, worst prognosis — cardiogenic shock)

## 3. Initial Assessment
- **Vitals**: BP, HR, RR, SpO2, Temp
- **JVP, peripheral oedema, lung crackles, S3, cool peripheries**
- **ECG**: ischemia, arrhythmia, low voltage (effusion)
- **CXR**: cardiomegaly, pulmonary oedema, pleural effusion
- **Labs**: BNP/NT-proBNP, troponin, U&E, LFT, FBC, TSH, lactate (if shock)
- **Echo** if not recent: LVEF, valves, RV, effusion, PASP

## 4. Management by Profile

### Warm-Wet (Most Common) — Congestion with Adequate Perfusion
- **IV loop diuretic**: furosemide 40-80 mg IV bolus (or 2.5× home PO dose), repeat q15-30 min or continuous infusion 10-40 mg/h
- **Add thiazide** (metolazone 2.5-5 mg, chlorothiazide IV) for diuretic resistance
- **Vasodilator** if SBP >110: nitroglycerin or nitroprusside
- **Oxygen + NIV** (CPAP/BPAP) for pulmonary oedema
- **Acetazolamide** 500 mg IV (ADVOR trial, 2022) — improves decongestion

### Cold-Wet (Cardiogenic Shock Profile) — Worst Prognosis
- **Inotrope**: dobutamine 2-20 µg/kg/min (warm hypotension); milrinone 0.375-0.75 µg/kg/min (cold + CKD)
- **Vasopressor** if MAP <65: norepinephrine 0.05-0.5 µg/kg/min; consider vasopressin
- **Diuretic** cautiously (avoid if hypoperfused — diuretics need renal perfusion)
- **Mechanical circulatory support** if not responding:
  - **IABP**: modest support, afterload reduction
  - **Impella**: higher support, LV unloading
  - **VA-ECMO**: highest support, but ↑ afterload; consider Impella + ECMO (ECPELLA)
- **Revascularization** if ischaemia-driven (culprit PCI)

### Cold-Dry — Hypoperfusion without Congestion
- **Inotrope** (dobutamine, milrinone)
- **Cautious fluids** if hypovolaemic (small bolus trial)
- **Reassess** if truly dry vs under-filled (consider passive leg raise)

### Warm-Dry — Compensated
- **Optimise oral GDMT**, no IV therapy needed
- **Pre-discharge**: ensure on 4 pillars (ARNI/ACEi, BB, MRA, SGLT2i)

## 5. SCAI Cardiogenic Shock Staging

| Stage | Description | Management |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **A (At Risk)** | Risk factors, no symptoms | Monitor |
| **B (Beginning)** | Hypotension, no hypoperfusion | Close monitoring, treat cause |
| **C (Classic)** | Hypoperfusion, requiring intervention | Inotropes, vasopressors |
| **D (Deteriorating)** | Worsening despite escalating support | Escalate MCS (Impella, ECMO) |
| **E (Extremis)** | Cardiac arrest, refractory shock | ECMO-CPR, salvage |

## 6. GDMT 4 Pillars (Pre-Discharge Initiation)

| Drug Class | Examples | Key Trial |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| **ARNI / ACEi / ARB** | Sacubitril-valsartan, enalapril, losartan | PARADIGM-HF, CONSENSUS |
| **Beta-blocker** | Bisoprolol, carvedilol, metoprolol succinate | CIBIS-II, COPERNICUS, MERIT-HF |
| **MRA** | Spironolactone, eplerenone | RALES, EMPHASIS-HF |
| **SGLT2i** | Dapagliflozin, empagliflozin | DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced |

> **STRONG-HF (2022):** Rapid up-titration within 2 weeks of discharge reduces 180-day HF readmission + mortality.

## 7. Discharge Criteria
- **euvolaemia** (JVP not elevated, no oedema, weight back to dry weight)
- **On GDMT** or with clear titration plan
- **BNP improved**
- **Renal function stable** (not worsening)
- **Follow-up within 7-14 days**

> **Pearls**
> - **Profile first, then treat.** Warm-wet → diuresis; Cold-wet → inotrope + MCS.
> - **IV loop diuretic 2.5× home dose** for ADHF.
> - **Acetazolamide** (ADVOR) boosts diuresis.
> - **SCAI staging** guides escalation.
> - **GDMT up-titrated before discharge** (STRONG-HF).
> - **Cardiorenal syndrome:** WRF during decongestion — usually transient, continue diuresis.
