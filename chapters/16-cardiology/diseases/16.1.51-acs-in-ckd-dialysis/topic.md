# ACS in CKD / Dialysis Patients

## 1. Epidemiology
- CVD = leading cause of death in dialysis (~40% mortality)
- CKD 4–5: ~50% have CAD; dialysis: ~80%
- Accelerated atherosclerosis + vascular calcification

## 2. Diagnosis Challenges
- **Baseline troponin chronically elevated** — interpret **delta** not absolute
- ECG: baseline LVH may mask ischemia
- **Contrast-induced AKI** risk — minimize contrast, IV hydration

## 3. Management
- **Primary PCI** — preferred; use iso-osmolar contrast, IV saline
- DAPT same; **UFH not LMWH** if CrCl <30
- Statin modest benefit in dialysis (4D, AURORA) — start if eGFR >30
- **CABG > PCI** in multivessel + DM (SYNTAX, FREEDOM)
- ACEi/ARB if tolerated (monitor K+)

## 4. Avoid
- Metformin (eGFR <30, lactic acidosis)
- NSAIDs
- Gadolinium (NSF)

> **Pearls** — Don't withhold reperfusion for CKD. **UFH not LMWH** if CrCl <30. Use **troponin delta** in CKD. Mortality post-MI in dialysis ~50% at 1 year.
