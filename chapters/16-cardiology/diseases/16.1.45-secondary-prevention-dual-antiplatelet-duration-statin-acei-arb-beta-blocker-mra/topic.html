CARDIOLOGY
Secondary prevention (Dual antiplatelet duration, statin, ACEi/ARB, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i)
1 Definition

Secondary prevention after ACS = lifelong strategies to reduce recurrent CV events. Components: antiplatelet therapy, lipid-lowering, RAAS blockade, β-blockade, MRA, SGLT2i, lifestyle, cardiac rehab.

2 Key Features
  • DAPT (aspirin + P2Y12):
  • Aspirin: 75–100 mg lifelong
  • P2Y12: ticagrelor or prasugrel (preferred over clopidogrel) for 12 months in ACS; clopidogrel if high bleeding risk
  • DAPT duration: 12 months standard; shorter (1–6 mo) if high bleeding risk; longer (>12 mo) for high ischaemic/low bleeding risk
  • Statin: high-intensity (atorvastatin 40–80 mg or rosuvastatin 20–40 mg) — target LDL <1.4 mmol/L (<55 mg/dL)
  • ACEi/ARB: all patients with LV dysfunction, HTN, diabetes; consider in all
  • β-blocker: 1st year post-MI; lifelong if LVEF ≤40% or HFrEF
  • MRA: if LVEF ≤40% + HF/diabetes (similar to HFrEF GDMT)
  • SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA: emerging in non-diabetic post-MI (EMPA-TROP, SELECT)
  • Lifestyle: smoking cessation, Mediterranean diet, exercise, weight, alcohol moderation
  • Cardiac rehab: structured exercise programme
3 Management
  • All post-MI patients should be on: aspirin + P2Y12 + high-intensity statin + ACEi + β-blocker
  • Address BP, LDL, HbA1c, smoking, weight
  • Annual flu vaccine; pneumococcal
  • Depression screening post-MI
  • Cardiac rehab improves outcomes
  • Imaging surveillance: echo at 3–6 months
Pearls - Ticagrelor or prasugrel preferred over clopidogrel in ACS - LDL target <1.4 mmol/L (or <1.0 in very high risk) - β-blocker lifelong if LVEF ≤40% - DAPT duration personalised: ischaemic vs bleeding risk
