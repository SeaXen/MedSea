# ALVC / Biventricular ACM

## 1. Definition
**Left-dominant or biventricular** form of ARVC with LV (and often RV) fibro-fatty replacement.

## 2. Recognition
- LV dysfunction, often mimics DCM
- LGE on CMR in LV lateral wall
- Arrhythmias of LV origin (RBBB morphology VT)
- Often misdiagnosed as DCM or myocarditis

## 3. Genetics
- Same desmosomal mutations as classic ARVC (PKP2, DSP, DSG2)
- **DSP** more commonly associated with LV-dominant disease
- **Phospholamban (PLN)** — p.Arg14del mutation in Dutch/European founder populations
- **Filamin-C (FLNC)** truncating variants

## 4. Diagnosis
- **CMR with LGE** — subepicardial/mid-myocardial LGE in LV lateral wall
- ECG: TWI in lateral leads (V4–V6), low voltages
- Holter: PVCs from LV
- Family history critical

## 5. Management
- Standard HF therapy
- **ICD** if LVEF ≤35%, sustained VT, syncope
- **Avoid competitive sports**
- Family screening

> **Pearls** — **ALVC increasingly recognized** — often misdiagnosed as DCM. **DSP** mutations → LV-dominant. Look for LGE in **LV lateral wall** on CMR. PLN p.Arg14del is a major European founder mutation.
