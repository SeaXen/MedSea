# ICD indications

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 04_Heart_Failure | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**ICD indications** (Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator): small device implanted subcutaneously (or submuscular) in left pre-pectoral region with leads to right ventricle (and/or atrium, coronary sinus for CRT-D). **Detects** VT/VF and delivers therapy. **Therapies**: anti-tachycardia pacing (ATP), cardioversion (low-energy shock), defibrillation (high-energy shock). **Indications**: **Secondary** (survived SCD, sustained VT with haemodynamic compromise). **Primary** (LVEF ≤35% on GDMT 3m, NYHA II-III, life expectancy >1y — MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT). **Genetic** (LMNA, PLN, DSP — ICD regardless of LVEF in selected).


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**N/A** — device. **Detection**: VT zone (≥180-200 bpm, ATP first, then shock if no conversion), VF zone (≥250 bpm, immediate shock). **Modern devices**: dual-chamber (atrial + ventricular), CRT-D (biventricular for HF), subcutaneous (S-ICD, no transvenous lead, less infection, but no pacing/ATP).


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

**Pre-implant**: history (syncope, SCD, family Hx), ECG (QRS duration — for CRT-D, BBB morphology), echo (LVEF, structural), coronary angio (if ischaemic, revascularise first), electrolytes, BNP, medications. **Post-implant**: wound check (1-2 weeks), device interrogation (every 3-6 months), ECG (sensing, threshold, capture, lead function), chest X-ray (lead position), medications, driving restrictions (UK — 6 months primary prevention, 2 years secondary, vocational longer), MRI conditional (modern devices).


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**N/A**. **Indication severity** — primary prevention (LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II-III), secondary (SCD survivor). **Risk of device infection, lead failure, inappropriate shocks, generator change** (5-9y battery).


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**Implant technique**: left pre-pectoral, subclavian or axillary vein access, RV lead to apex or septum, atrial lead to RA appendage, CS lead for LV (CRT-D). **Settings**: VT zone (ATP first — 91% effective for VT ≤200bpm, less shocks, less pain, less anxiety, less mortality — MADIT-RIT, ADVANCE III, PAIN-FREE), VF zone (shock). **Detection enhancements**: longer detection (reduce inappropriate shocks), SVT-VT discriminators (stability, sudden onset, morphology, chamber onset). **Subcutaneous ICD (S-ICD)**: no transvenous lead, screening required (sensing vector), no pacing, no ATP, larger generator, used in younger patients, no venous access. **Wearable ICD**: temporary, vest, used as bridge (post-MI, transplant, explant).


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

**Infection** (1-3%, endocarditis, generator pocket, lead infection — antibiotics, extraction, replacement). **Lead failure** (5-15% at 10y, conductor fracture, insulation break, lead noise, inappropriate shocks). **Generator depletion** (5-9y, replacement). **Inappropriate shocks** (5-20%, AF with rapid response, SVT, T-wave oversensing, lead fracture — painful, anxiety, ↑mortality — MADIT-RIT, Hinkle). **Psychiatric** (anxiety, depression, PTSD, QoL — 20-30% have adjustment disorder). **Venous thrombosis**. **Pneumothorax** (subclavian access). **Cardiac perforation** (rare, tamponade). **Triad of device problems**: failure to sense, failure to capture, inappropriate shock. **Recalls** (Sprint Fidelis, Riata).


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

**Children**: ICD, S-ICD screening. **Pregnancy**: pre-pregnancy counselling, generator replacement before. **Elderly**: more complications, careful selection. **Athletes**: ICD approved, contact sports avoid. **Drivers**: UK 6 months primary, 2 years secondary. **MRI conditional**: most modern devices, magnet mode. **End-of-life**: deactivate in terminally ill (respect patient autonomy, no SCD, allow natural death).


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

**ESC 2022 VA (EHJ)**: ICD primary and secondary prevention. **AHA/ACC/HRS 2017 VA**: similar. **NICE NG211 (2021)**: VA and SCD prevention. **MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT, DEFINITE**: primary prevention. **AVID, CIDS, CASH**: secondary prevention. **MADIT-RIT, ADVANCE III, PAIN-FREE Rx**: programming to reduce shocks. **MADIT-CRT, COMPANION, CARE-HF**: CRT-D.


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

**Don't implant in acute setting** (within 40 days of MI, or 3m of revascularisation, GDMT initiation — DINAMIT, IRIS — no mortality benefit, maybe harm). **Re-vascularise first** if ischaemic. **GDMT 3m before** (improved LVEF in 25-30%). **LVEF recovery** — reassess, may not need ICD. **Inappropriate shocks** — common (SVT, AF, T-wave oversensing, lead noise). **Programming** matters — longer detection, ATP first, reduces shocks. **S-ICD** — screening needed, no pacing. **End-of-life** — deactivate (deactivation does NOT cause death, but allows natural death, do not give). **Generator replacement** — patient preference (some decline). **Driving restrictions** vary.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

**ICD** — primary prevention: LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II-III, GDMT 3m, life expectancy >1y. **Secondary**: SCD survivor, sustained VT with haemodynamic compromise. **MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT, DEFINITE**: primary. **AVID, CIDS, CASH**: secondary. **MADIT-RIT, ADVANCE III**: longer detection, ATP first. **S-ICD**: no pacing, no ATP, no transvenous lead. **End-of-life**: deactivate (allows natural death). **Driving**: 6mo primary, 2y secondary (UK). **Complications**: infection, lead failure, inappropriate shocks, generator change. **Triad**: failure to sense, capture, inappropriate shock. **Inappropriate shocks**: SVT, AF, T-wave oversensing, lead noise.


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

N/A. **Differential for inappropriate shock**: SVT, AF with rapid response, T-wave oversensing, lead fracture, EMI, myopotentials, atrial sensing of VT (in single chamber).


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

ECG (QRS, BBB, LVH), echo (LVEF, structural), coronary angio (if ischaemic), BNP, electrolytes, Holter (NSVT for risk), electrophysiology study (inducibility, ablation), CXR (post-implant), device interrogation (sensing, threshold, lead impedance, battery — every 3-6 months).

## 14. EXAM DRILLS

### 14.1 MCQs

**Q1.** What is the most common aetiology of ICD indications?
A. Genetic predisposition
B. Acquired disease
C. Mixed aetiology
D. Idiopathic
E. Familial

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Most cases are acquired with genetic/environmental interaction. Recognise risk factors, presentation, and management.

**Q2.** First-line investigation for ICD indications:
A. ECG
B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography
E. Cardiac biomarkers

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Echo is first-line for structural/functional assessment; additional tests as indicated.

**Q3.** Most appropriate first-line therapy for ICD indications:
A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy
D. Surgical intervention
E. Multidisciplinary care

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Guideline-directed medical therapy is cornerstone; other modalities as indicated.

**Q4.** Which is a contraindication to therapy in ICD indications?
A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

**Q5.** The most common complication of ICD indications is:
A. Heart failure
B. Arrhythmia
C. Thromboembolism
D. Death
E. Quality of life reduction

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.
