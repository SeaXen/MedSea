# Non-Sustained VT (NSVT)

## 1. Definition
**NSVT** = ≥3 consecutive ventricular beats at >100 bpm, lasting **<30 s** and **terminating spontaneously** without haemodynamic collapse. Common, often asymptomatic, but can be a marker of **underlying structural heart disease** and **risk of sustained VT/SCD**.

## 2. Key Features
- **Asymptomatic** in 80% (incidental on Holter/ambulatory ECG)
- **Symptomatic**: palpitations, presyncope, dyspnoea, chest pain
- **Causes**:
  - **Ischaemic heart disease** (post-MI scar) — most important
  - **Dilated CM** (any cause)
  - **HCM**
  - **ARVC**
  - **Electrolyte disturbance** (hypokalaemia, hypomagnesaemia)
  - **Drugs** (antiarrhythmics, QT-prolonging)
  - **Idiopathic** (RVOT, fascicular)
- **Significance**:
  - Post-MI: **LVEF is the main determinant** of prognosis
  - LVEF ≤35% + NSVT = ↑ SCD risk → **ICD** considered
  - In **MADIT-II** and **MUSTT**, NSVT + LVEF ≤35–40% benefited from ICD
- **Diagnosis**: 24–48 h Holter, event recorder, implantable loop recorder

## 3. Management
- **Identify underlying cause**: echo, CMR, stress test, coronary angiography
- **Correct electrolyte abnormalities** (K⁺ >4.0, Mg²⁺ >2.0)
- **Stop offending drugs** (QT-prolonging, antiarrhythmics)
- **β-blocker** for symptom/recurrence control
- **If structural heart disease + LVEF ≤35%**: **ICD** for primary prevention
- **LVEF >40% + NSVT**: usually no specific therapy beyond β-blocker
- **Catheter ablation** if recurrent symptomatic idiopathic NSVT (RVOT, fascicular)
- **EP study** may be considered in intermediate risk (e.g., LVEF 36–40%)

> **Pearls**
> - **NSVT = ≥3 beats, <30 s, self-terminating**
> - **Post-MI + LVEF ≤35% + NSVT = ICD** (primary prevention)
> - **Idiopathic NSVT** (RVOT, fascicular) = benign, ablation curative
> - Always **correct K⁺ and Mg²⁺** first
