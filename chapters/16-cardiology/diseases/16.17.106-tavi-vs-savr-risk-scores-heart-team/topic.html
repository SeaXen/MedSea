CARDIOLOGY
TAVI vs SAVR (risk scores, heart team)
1 Background

Transcatheter Aortic Valve Implantation (TAVI / TAVR) has revolutionised AS care. The PARTNER, CoreValve, Evolut, and NOTION trials progressively expanded TAVI indications from inoperable → high-risk → intermediate → low-risk cohorts. Current guidelines (ESC 2021, ACC/AHA 2020) consider TAVI in patients >65–75 years depending on risk, anatomy, and lifetime management considerations.

2 Definitions
  • SAVR — Surgical Aortic Valve Replacement (sternotomy / mini-thoracotomy)
  • TAVI — Percutaneous / transapical / transaortic valve delivery (most common: transfemoral)

5 The Heart Team

A multidisciplinary team is mandated for all AS interventions:

  • Interventional cardiologist (TAVI operator)
  • Cardiac surgeon (SAVR operator + TAVI backup)
  • Imaging specialist (echo, CT)
  • Cardiologist / geriatrician (patient assessment, frailty, comorbidity)
  • Anaesthetist (high-risk patients)

The Heart Team reviews each case, weighs TAVI vs SAVR, and individualises the decision.

7 Anatomic Considerations for TAVI
  • Annular sizing by CT — risk of annular rupture if oversizing
  • Calcium burden (LVOT, raphe) — predictor of paravalvular leak and rupture
  • Coronary heights — risk of coronary obstruction (low Bicuspid, valve-in-valve)
  • Vascular access — iliofemoral diameter, tortuosity, calcification
  • Bicuspid anatomy — previously relative contraindication; current TAVI devices acceptable in selected Type 1 BAV
8 Lifetime Management

For younger patients, the choice has long-term implications:

  • TAVI may commit to valve-in-valve TAVI later (small surgical risk of redo SAVR)
  • SAVR provides durable prosthesis (esp. mechanical in <50 yr, bioprosthetic in 50–70)
  • Patient preference and "lifetime strategy" discussed in Heart Team
9 Special Situations
  • Pure AR without annular calcification → TAVI less effective (lack of anchor) → SAVR preferred
  • Severe LV dysfunction — TAVI outcomes favourable; consider pre-TAVI MCS bridge
  • Prior CABG — TAVI preferred (avoids redo sternotomy)
  • Porcelain aorta — TAVI preferred (can't cross-clamp)
Pearls - TAVI is now first-line for patients >75 yr or high surgical risk - SAVR preferred in <75 yr, low risk, bicuspid, AR predominant, unsuitable anatomy - Heart Team decision is mandatory and must involve both operators + imaging - TAVI has higher PPM and paravalvular leak risk; SAVR has higher bleeding and AF risk - Discuss lifetime management with younger patients — durability favours SAVR
