# TAVI vs SAVR — Risk Scores, Heart Team, and Decision-Making

## 1. Background
**Transcatheter Aortic Valve Implantation (TAVI / TAVR)** has revolutionised AS care. The **PARTNER**, **CoreValve**, **Evolut**, and **NOTION** trials progressively expanded TAVI indications from inoperable → high-risk → intermediate → low-risk cohorts. Current guidelines (ESC 2021, ACC/AHA 2020) consider TAVI in patients **>65–75 years** depending on risk, anatomy, and lifetime management considerations.

## 2. Definitions
- **SAVR** — Surgical Aortic Valve Replacement (sternotomy / mini-thoracotomy)
- **TAVI** — Percutaneous / transapical / transaortic valve delivery (most common: transfemoral)

## 3. Risk Stratification Scores

| Score | Components | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **STS score** | Age, sex, EF, reoperation, MI, stroke, renal failure, DM, COPD, etc. | Surgical mortality |
| **EuroSCORE II** | Similar + critical state, PA pressure | Surgical mortality |
| **frailty** | Gait speed, grip strength, Katz ADL | Adjunctive |
| **STS/ACC TAVR in-hospital mortality** | Procedural risk | TAVI |
| **NYHA, CCS, pulmonary HTN** | Functional | Decision-making |

> Surgical risk: low <4%, intermediate 4–8%, high >8% (STS).

## 4. ESC 2021 Recommendations

| Patient Profile | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **>75 yr OR high surgical risk** (STS/EuroSCORE II >8%) OR frail OR prior sternotomy OR unsuitable for surgery | **TAVI preferred** (Class I) |
| **<75 yr, low surgical risk, suitable for surgery, bicuspid / AR predominant** | **SAVR preferred** (Class I) |
| **Age 65–75, intermediate risk, suitable for both** | **Heart Team** + patient preference |
| **Inoperable** | TAVI only option |
| **Bicuspid, severe AR (no annular calcification)** | SAVR (TAVI less effective) |

## 5. The Heart Team
A **multidisciplinary team** is mandated for all AS interventions:
- **Interventional cardiologist** (TAVI operator)
- **Cardiac surgeon** (SAVR operator + TAVI backup)
- **Imaging specialist** (echo, CT)
- **Cardiologist / geriatrician** (patient assessment, frailty, comorbidity)
- **Anaesthetist** (high-risk patients)

The Heart Team **reviews each case**, weighs TAVI vs SAVR, and **individualises** the decision.

## 6. TAVI vs SAVR — Trade-offs

| Factor | TAVI | SAVR |
|---|---|---|
| **Invasiveness** | Minimally invasive | Open chest |
| **Mortality (low-risk)** | Comparable (PARTNER 3, Evolut Low Risk) | Comparable |
| **Stroke** | Slightly higher in early trials; now similar | Slightly lower |
| **Conduction disturbance / PPM** | **Higher** (~10–25%) | Lower (~3–8%) |
| **Paravalvular leak** | More common (esp. early devices) | Less common |
| **Vascular complications** | Higher (TF access) | Lower |
| **Durability** | **5-yr data** reassuring; **10-yr** data still maturing | 10–20 yr well-documented |
| **Anticoagulation** | Aspirin (or DAPT short) lifelong | Mechanical→warfarin; bio→aspirin |
| **Recovery** | Days | Weeks |
| **Reintervention** | Higher (paravalvular leak, valve-in-valve) | Lower |
| **Repeat procedure in young patient** | Valve-in-valve TAVI feasible | Redo surgery higher risk |

## 7. Anatomic Considerations for TAVI
- **Annular sizing** by CT — risk of annular rupture if oversizing
- **Calcium burden** (LVOT, raphe) — predictor of paravalvular leak and rupture
- **Coronary heights** — risk of coronary obstruction (low Bicuspid, valve-in-valve)
- **Vascular access** — iliofemoral diameter, tortuosity, calcification
- **Bicuspid anatomy** — previously relative contraindication; current TAVI devices acceptable in selected Type 1 BAV

## 8. Lifetime Management
For **younger patients**, the choice has long-term implications:
- TAVI may **commit** to valve-in-valve TAVI later (small surgical risk of redo SAVR)
- SAVR provides **durable** prosthesis (esp. mechanical in <50 yr, bioprosthetic in 50–70)
- Patient preference and "lifetime strategy" discussed in Heart Team

## 9. Special Situations
- **Pure AR** without annular calcification → TAVI less effective (lack of anchor) → SAVR preferred
- **Severe LV dysfunction** — TAVI outcomes favourable; consider pre-TAVI MCS bridge
- **Prior CABG** — TAVI preferred (avoids redo sternotomy)
- **Porcelain aorta** — TAVI preferred (can't cross-clamp)

> **Pearls**
> - **TAVI** is now first-line for patients **>75 yr** or high surgical risk
> - **SAVR** preferred in <75 yr, low risk, bicuspid, AR predominant, unsuitable anatomy
> - **Heart Team** decision is mandatory and must involve both operators + imaging
> - **TAVI** has higher PPM and paravalvular leak risk; **SAVR** has higher bleeding and AF risk
> - Discuss **lifetime management** with younger patients — durability favours SAVR
