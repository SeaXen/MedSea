# Mechanical Circulatory Support in HF

<callout icon="⚙️" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** IABP, Impella, VA-ECMO — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Indications
- **Cardiogenic shock** refractory to inotropes (SCAI C–E)
- **High-risk PCI** (protected PCI)
- **Post-cardiotomy shock**
- **Bridge to transplant, LVAD, recovery**
- **Refractory ventricular arrhythmia**

## 2. IABP (Intra-Aortic Balloon Pump)
**Most common, lowest level of support.** Counterpulsation — balloon inflates in diastole (↑coronary perfusion), deflates in systole (↓afterload).

| | |
|---|---|
| **Access** | Femoral (8–9 Fr sheath) |
| **Augmentation** | ~0.5–1.0 L/min |
| **LV afterload** | ↓ |
| **Coronary flow** | ↑ |
| **Best for** | Cardiogenic shock, mechanical complications (VSR, acute MR) |
| **Contraindications** | Severe AR, aortic dissection, severe PVD |

> **IABP-SHOCK II** — no mortality benefit in cardiogenic shock post-AMI. ESC downgraded routine use to IIb; still useful in mechanical complications.

## 3. Impella (Microaxial catheter pump)
Continuous axial flow pump across the aortic valve — unloads LV, pumps blood from LV → aorta.

| Device | Flow |
|---|---|
| **Impella 2.5** | 2.5 L/min (high-risk PCI) |
| **Impella CP** | 3.5 L/min (cardiogenic shock) |
| **Impella 5.0/5.5** | 5.0–6.0 L/min (more support) |

- **Access:** femoral or axillary (5.0/5.5)
- **LV fully unloaded** — ↓LVEDP, ↓PCWP, ↓myocardial O₂ demand
- **Contraindications:** LV thrombus, severe AS, mechanical AV, severe AR
- **Complications:** bleeding, limb ischaemia, haemolysis, device migration
- **DanGer Shock (2024)** — Impella CP in STEMI-related cardiogenic shock reduced 180-day mortality vs standard care (45% vs 58%)

## 4. VA-ECMO (Veno-Arterial Extracromial Membrane Oxygenation)
**Highest support** — both circulatory and oxygenation support. Venous drainage + arterial return + membrane oxygenator.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Configuration** | Fem-fem (peripheral) or central |
| **Flow** | 3–7 L/min |
| **Provides** | Full cardiopulmonary support |
| **Indications** | Refractory cardiogenic shock, cardiac arrest (ECPR), bridge to recovery/transplant/LVAD |
| **Complications** | Limb ischaemia, bleeding, stroke, haemolysis, LV distension, "Harlequin" syndrome (differential hypoxia) |

- **LV distension** — must be vented (IABP, Impella, atrial septostomy)
- **Weaning** — when LVEF recovers, ↓flow, assess with echo
- **Survival** ~30–40% to discharge in cardiogenic shock

## 5. Other Devices
- **TandemHeart** — LA → aorta (surgical, transseptal); used in refractory shock
- **CentriMag** — extracorporeal, surgical, durable — bridge to recovery/transplant
- **Right ventricular support:** Impella RP, ProtekDuo, VA-ECMO with RV venting

## 6. Choosing the Device
| Severity | Device |
|---|---|
| Borderline hypotension, awaiting recovery | **IABP** |
| Cardiogenic shock, adequate oxygenation | **Impella** |
| Cardiac arrest, severe shock, hypoxia | **VA-ECMO** |
| Biventricular failure | VA-ECMO + Impella RP / IABP |

## 7. Complications (all MCS)
- **Bleeding** (anticoagulation, access site)
- **Limb ischaemia** (large-bore femoral access)
- **Infection**, **haemolysis**
- **Device thrombosis / failure**
- **Stroke** (thromboembolism, air, haemorrhage)
- **Vascular injury** requiring surgery

> **Pearls**
> - **IABP** — counterpulsation; modest support; great in mechanical complications
> - **Impella** — LV-to-aorta microaxial; better LV unloading than IABP
> - **VA-ECMO** — full bypass + oxygenation; ECMO is the most powerful but most complication-prone
> - **DanGer Shock (2024)** — Impella CP benefit in STEMI shock
> - **ECMO + Impella (ECPella)** — combines LV unloading with ECMO support
> - **Always exclude LV thrombus** before Impella

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** MCS is a **bridge**, not a destination. **SCAI staging** guides timing — early escalation (A → B → C) improves outcomes. **Impella** unloads the LV (DanGer Shock 2024), **VA-ECMO** provides full support but increases LV afterload (often paired with Impella). MCS decisions belong to the **Heart Team**.
</callout>
