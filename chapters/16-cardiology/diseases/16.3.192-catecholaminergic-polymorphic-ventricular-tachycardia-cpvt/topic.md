# Catecholaminergic Polymorphic Ventricular Tachycardia

## 1. Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Etiology & Pathophysiology

**Mechanisms**: (1) **Re-entry** (scar from MI, cardiomyopathy, after surgery), (2) **Abnormal automaticity** (ischaemia, hypoxia, electrolyte), (3) **Triggered activity** (early/late afterdepolarisations — torsades, digitalis). **Substrate**: post-MI scar, fibrosis (DCM, HCM, ARVC), infiltrative disease, RV dysplasia.

---

## 2. Classification

### Clinical Features

Palpitations, dyspnoea, chest pain, syncope, presyncope, fatigue. **Haemodynamic instability** in 50% (SBP<90, shock, ischaemia, pulmonary oedema). **SCD** in untreated sustained VT (10-20%/year in post-MI). **NSVT**: often asymptomatic. **Examination**: tachycardia, hypotension, signs of HF, cannon A waves (AV dissociation).

---

## 3. Clinical Features

### Diagnostic Approach

**12-lead ECG**: wide complex QRS (>120ms), AV dissociation (P waves marching through), capture/fusion beats, concordance (all positive or all negative V1-V6), extreme axis (northwest), Brugada criteria (RS absent in precordials, RS interval >100ms, AV dissociation). **Holter** for NSVT. **EP study** for risk stratification and ablation. **Echo** (LVEF, scar, infiltrative). **CMR** (scar, fibrofatty infiltration — ARVC).

---

## 4. Diagnosis & Investigations

### Severity Assessment & Risk Stratification

**Haemodynamic instability** is the immediate trigger for emergency cardioversion. **Underlying substrate severity** dictates long-term risk: post-MI scar >30% LV EF, NSVT, syncope → 20% SCD/year. **EP study** inducibility predicts risk. **Brugada ECG pattern** = high SCD risk.

### Differential Diagnosis

SVT with aberrancy (RBBB, LBBB, pre-existing), SVT with pre-excitation (WPW antidromic), antidromic AVRT, paced rhythm, ventricular paced, hyperkalaemia, severe metabolic disturbance, drug toxicity (sodium channel blockers — TCA, cocaine, flecainide).

### Investigations

12-lead ECG (during and after), Holter/event recorder (intermittent), echo (LVEF, scar, infiltrative), CMR (scar, infiltrative, ARVC, myocarditis), coronary angiography (ischaemia), EP study (inducibility, mapping, ablation), TFT, electrolyte, drug levels, genetic testing (LQT, Brugada, CPVT, ARVC).

---

## 5. Management

### Management Algorithm

**Stable VT**: IV amiodarone 5mg/kg over 20min, then 15mg/kg/24h; or procainamide (preserved EF, monomorphic), lidocaine (ischaemia/CM). **Unstable/VF**: DCCV synchronised 100-200J biphasic. **Pulseless VT/VF**: VF algorithm, shock + CPR. **Recurrent/electrical storm**: intubate, deep sedation, amiodarone, overdrive pacing, sympathetic blockade (esmolol, stellate ganglion block).

### Special Populations

**Post-MI**: most common in first year, especially with LVEF<35% — ICD primary prevention. **Bradycardia-dependent (pause-dependent) torsades**: pacing, withdraw offending drug. **Catecholaminergic (CPVT)**: beta-blocker, ICD, sympathetic denervation. **Short-coupled Torsades**: IV verapamil. **Brugada**: ICD, quinidine for arrhythmia suppression. **ARVC**: ICD, exercise restriction, ablation.

---

## 6. Complications & Prognosis

### Complications & Prognosis

**SCD**. **Cerebrovascular event** (hypoperfusion, AF/VF). **Heart failure** (cardiomyopathy, post-shock). **Post-shock complications** (skin burns, lead displacement, infection, anxiety). **Storms** (≥3 VT/VF in 24h — 50% mortality). **Cardiac arrest**.
