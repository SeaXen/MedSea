# HCM — Diagnosis (ECG, Echo, CMR with LGE)

## 1. Diagnostic Criteria
HCM = LV wall thickness **≥15 mm** (≥13 mm with HCM in first-degree relative) unexplained by loading conditions. Apical, mid-ventricular, and symmetric patterns all qualify.

## 2. ECG
- **Sensitivity ~90%** — abnormal in nearly all HCM
- **Massive LVH** voltages (Sokolow-Lyon often >50 mm)
- Deep narrow Q waves (lateral/inferior) from septal depolarisation
- **Giant negative T waves** in V3–V6 → apical HCM (Yamaguchi)
- WPW-like delta wave in some PRKAG2 / Danon (mimics, not true HCM)
- Normal ECG in genotype-positive/phenotype-negative relatives

## 3. Echocardiography — First-Line
| Parameter | Finding |
|---|---|
| **Max wall thickness** | ≥15 mm (septal: basal > mid > apical) |
| **LVOT gradient** | Peak **≥30 mmHg** at rest or with Valsalva defines obstructive HCM |
| **SAM** | Systolic anterior motion of MV → obstruction + MR |
| **MV anatomy** | Elongated leaflets, papillary anomalies |
| **LA size** | Marker of chronic diastolic dysfunction |
| **Diastolic function** | E/A, E/e', LA volume index |

### Provocation for Latent Obstruction
Valsalva, standing, exercise echo, or amyl nitrite — escalate gradient ≥50 mmHg is significant.

## 4. Cardiac MRI (CMR) — Gold Standard for Tissue
- **Max wall thickness** confirmation (better than echo, especially anterolateral wall & apex)
- **Late gadolinium enhancement (LGE)** — patchy mid-wall fibrosis at RV insertion points and segments of hypertrophy
- **Native T1 mapping & ECV** — early diffuse fibrosis
- **Extracellular volume (ECV) ≥30%** — overlap with cardiac amyloid; consider when thick walls + LGE
- Cine SSFP for **apical aneurysm** (mid-cavity variant) — high SCD risk

## 5. Differential ("Phenocopies")
| Mimic | Clue |
|---|---|
| **Athlete's heart** | Symmetric hypertrophy, normal diastolic function, detrains |
| **Hypertensive heart** | Con centric, controlled BP history |
| **Cardiac amyloidosis** | Thick walls + low voltage ECG + speckled myocardium |
| **Fabry disease** | Short PR, inferolateral LGE |
| **PRKAG2 / Danon** | WPW, very thick walls, early onset |

## 6. Holter & Exercise Testing
- **48-hour Holter** — assess NSVT (≥3 beats, ≥120 bpm) for SCD risk
- **Exercise stress** — BP response, gradient provocation, functional capacity

> **Pearls**
> - Echo first; CMR for confirmation, apical aneurysm, and **tissue characterisation (LGE)**.
> - **≥15 mm** wall thickness is the diagnostic threshold.
> - **LGE ≥15% of LV mass** is a risk-stratification flag (2014 ESC).
> - Distinguish HCM from **amyloid** (low voltage + thick walls) and **athlete's heart** (normal diastology).
> - Always exclude phenocopies — wrong diagnosis misses treatable infiltrative disease.
