# AVNRT / AVRT — Ablation

## 1. AVNRT (AV Nodal Reentrant Tachycardia)
- **Most common SVT** (~60%)
- Reentry within AV node (slow-fast, fast-slow, slow-slow)
- P wave often hidden in QRS (pseudo-R' in V1)
- **Ablation**: slow pathway modification
- Success >97%, AV block risk <0.5%

## 2. AVRT (AV Reentrant Tachycardia) — Accessory Pathways
- Reentry uses AV node + accessory pathway
- **Orthodromic** (antegrade through AV node) — narrow complex
- **Antidromic** (antegrade through AP) — wide complex
- **WPW syndrome** = pre-excitation + symptoms (palpitations/SCD)

## 3. Pathway Localization
- **Delta wave** at baseline = pre-excitation
- Algorithm: V1 polarity, transition, II, aVF

## 4. WPW + AF (High Risk)
- **Short AP refractory period** → very rapid ventricular response → VF
- Risk of SCD in symptomatic WPW with AF
- **Catheter ablation** is curative and indicated

## 5. Ablation Procedure
- **Electroanatomic mapping** (CARTO, Ensite, Rhythmia)
- **Ablate AP or slow pathway** with RF (or cryo for AVNRT near AV node)
- **Cryo** preferred for AVNRT near AV node (safer, reversible)

## 6. Outcomes
- Success 95–98%
- Recurrence 3–5%
- Major complications <1% (AV block, tamponade, stroke)

> **Pearls** — AVNRT ablation = **slow pathway modification**, near AV node → cryo often used. WPW with AF = **SCD risk**, ablate. **Asymptomatic WPW** with high-risk features → consider EP study + ablation.
