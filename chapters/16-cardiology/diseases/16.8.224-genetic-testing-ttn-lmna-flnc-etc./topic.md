# DCM — Genetic Testing (TTN, LMNA, FLNC, PLN, etc.)

## 1. Why Genetic Testing
- Up to **30–40% of "idiopathic" DCM** has a genetic basis
- Identifies at-risk **relatives** (cascade screening)
- Some genotypes carry **specific risks** that change management (e.g., LMNA → ICD earlier)

## 2. Whom to Test
- All probands with **idiopathic/familial DCM** (LVEF <50%, age <60)
- Consider in DCM with **conduction disease, AF, or SCD family history**
- Targeted **cascade testing** in first-degree relatives when variant known

## 3. Major DCM Genes
| Gene | Frequency | Specific Risk / Phenotype |
|---|---|---|
| **TTN** | ~25% of familial DCM | Truncating variants (TTNtv); **most common**; variable penetrance |
| **LMNA** | 5–10% | **High SCD risk with mild LV dysfunction**; conduction disease; ICD early |
| **FLNC** | ~3% | Arrhythmogenic; LV and RV fibro-fatty replacement |
| **PLN** | <2% (NL founder) | Arg14del variant; ARVC-like, low-voltage ECG, SCD |
| **RBM20** | ~2% | Aggressive, ARVC overlap |
| **DSP** | <1% | ARVC overlap |
| **BAG3** | <1% | Severe, early onset |
| **DMD/BMD** | rare (X-linked) | Skeletal myopathy; elevated CK |

## 4. Interpretation Caveats
- **TTN truncating variants** are common even in **healthy people** (~1%) → interpret in clinical context
- **Variant of uncertain significance (VUS)** is common — do not change management based on VUS alone
- Use **ACMG criteria** (pathogenic, likely pathogenic, VUS, likely benign, benign)
- Genetic **counselling** before and after testing

## 5. Genotype-Driven Management
| Variant | Action |
|---|---|
| **LMNA** | ICD at LVEF ≤45% or with AV block (Class IIa); early listing |
| **FLNC** | Consider ICD at LVEF ≤50% (high arrhythmia risk) |
| **PLN (Arg14del)** | Early ICD; specific Dutch experience |
| **TTN** | Standard GDMT; phenotype varies widely |
| **DMD** | Steroid protocol for skeletal muscle; cardiac surveillance from age 10 |

## 6. Family Screening Algorithm
1. **Proband** → genetic panel
2. If pathogenic variant → **cascade test** all first-degree relatives
3. **Variant-positive / phenotype-negative** → clinical surveillance (echo + ECG annually until 30, then 3–5 yrly)
4. **Variant-negative** relatives → no further cardiac screening

## 7. Pregnancy
- TTN, LMNA, and dystrophinopathy variants can be passed to offspring
- Pre-conception **counselling**; offer prenatal or preimplantation testing

> **Pearls**
> - **TTN truncating variants** are the most common DCM genotype (~25%).
> - **LMNA** → consider ICD earlier (LVEF ≤45%) due to disproportionate SCD risk.
> - **FLNC and PLN** are highly arrhythmogenic — consider ICD even with moderate LV dysfunction.
> - **VUS** should not change management — re-classify periodically.
> - **Cascade testing** identifies at-risk relatives before phenotype expression.
