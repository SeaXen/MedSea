# Radiation Heart Disease

## 1. Risk
- **Mediastinal radiation** for Hodgkin lymphoma, breast cancer (left), lung, esophageal
- Latency 10–30 years
- Dose-related (>30 Gy ↑ risk)
- Risk continues lifelong

## 2. Manifestations
- **Pericarditis** (acute, delayed, constrictive)
- **Accelerated CAD** (especially left main, ostial lesions)
- **Valvular disease** (AS, AR, MR) — calcific, fibrosis
- **Restrictive CM** (fibrosis)
- **Conduction disease** (AV block, BBB, RBBB)
- **Carotid disease** (stroke risk)

## 3. Diagnosis
- High suspicion in any patient with prior chest radiation
- **Echo** for valve, pericardial, LV function
- **Stress test or CTA** for CAD (often ostial)
- **CMR** for fibrosis

## 4. Management
- **Higher periprocedural risk** in surgery (adhesions, friable tissue, porcelain aorta)
- **TAVI** often preferred for radiation AS (less invasive)
- **CABG** may be high risk — consider PCI for ostial lesions
- **Pericardiectomy** challenging (radiation fibrosis)
- **Standard HF therapy** for restrictive CM

## 5. Screening
- **Lifelong** CV follow-up after mediastinal radiation
- **Echo every 5 years**
- **Stress test or CAC** at 5–10 years
- **Carotid Doppler**

> **Pearls** — Latency **10–30 years** after chest radiation. **TAVI preferred for AS** (porcelain aorta, surgical risk). **Lifelong CV follow-up** after mediastinal radiation. Multi-organ involvement common.
