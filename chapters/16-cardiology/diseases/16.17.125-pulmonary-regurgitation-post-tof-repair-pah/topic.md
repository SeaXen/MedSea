# Pulmonary Regurgitation

## 1. Etiology
- **Post-TOF repair** (most common in adults)
- **PAH** with annular dilation
- Endocarditis (rare)
- Carcinoid
- Iatrogenic (post-PV intervention)

## 2. Clinical
- Often asymptomatic for years
- Progressive RV dilation → TR → RV failure
- Exercise intolerance, AF, VT

## 3. Diagnosis
- Echo: diastolic flow reversal in branch PAs, RV enlargement
- **CMR** — gold standard for PR fraction (>25% severe) + RV volumes

## 4. Management
- **Severe symptomatic PR** with RV dilation (RVEDVi >160 mL/m²)
- **Transcatheter pulmonary valve** (Melody, Sapien) — preferred when feasible
- **Surgical PVR** (pulmonary valve replacement) for others
- Timing: before irreversible RV dysfunction

> **Pearls** — Most common cause of PR in adults = **post-TOF**. **CMR is gold standard** for quantification. **Transcatheter PV** is revolutionizing management. Operate before RV dysfunction becomes irreversible.
