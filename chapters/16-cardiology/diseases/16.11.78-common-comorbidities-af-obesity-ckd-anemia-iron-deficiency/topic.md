## 1. Why Comorbidities Matter
Comorbidities **drive symptoms, hospitalisation, and mortality** in HF. Treating them is part of GDMT.

## 2. Atrial Fibrillation (AF)
- **Prevalence:** 30–40% of HF patients; more in HFpEF
- **Bidirectional relationship** — AF worsens HF, HF worsens AF
- **Rate control** — beta-blocker first; digoxin in HFrEF with AF
- **Rhythm control** — amiodarone (safe in structural HD), DCCV
- **AV node ablation + CRT** for refractory rate
- **Anticoagulation** — DOAC preferred over warfarin in HF (no benefit for warfarin, more bleeding — no AFIB-VTE trial)
- **CASTLE-AF** — catheter ablation in HFrEF + AF reduces mortality
- **CABANA** — neutral overall, but HF subgroup benefit

## 3. Obesity
- **~40% of HFpEF** are obese; metabolic syndrome is a major driver
- **SGLT2i** + **GLP-1 RA** (semaglutide, liraglutide) — weight loss, improved HF outcomes
- **STEP-HFpEF (2023)** — semaglutide in obese HFpEF: improved symptoms, 6MWD, CRP
- **Bariatric surgery** improves functional capacity
- **Obesity paradox** — overweight HF patients have *better* short-term prognosis (caveat: cardiorespiratory fitness matters more)

## 4. Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
- **~50% of HF** patients have eGFR <60; cardiorenal syndrome is common
- **SGLT2i** — renoprotective + cardioprotective (DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY, CREDENCE)
- **Avoid nephrotoxins** — NSAIDs, contrast
- **Diuretic resistance** — sequential nephron blockade (loop + thiazide), ultrafiltration
- **Worsening creatinine on ACEi/ARB** — accept up to 30% rise; reduce/stop if >30%
- **Hyperkalaemia** — common on ACEi/ARB + MRA; novel binders (patiromer, sodium zirconium cyclosilicate) allow continuation

## 5. Anaemia
- **Prevalence:** 30–50% in chronic HF
- **Causes:** iron deficiency, CKD, chronic inflammation, haemodilution
- **Associated with:** worse symptoms, hospitalisation, mortality
- **Workup:** FBC, ferritin, TSAT, B12, folate, reticulocytes
- **Treatment:** IV iron (FAIR-HF, AFFIRM-AHF, IRONMAN)
- **ESAs** not routinely recommended in HF
- **Transfusion** if severe/symptomatic

## 6. Iron Deficiency
- **Prevalence:** 40–60% in HF — most common nutritional deficiency
- **Definition (ESC 2021):** ferritin <100 μg/L **OR** ferritin 100–299 + **TSAT <20%**
- **Functional iron deficiency** — ferritin normal/inflamed, TSAT <20%
- **IV iron** — **ferric carboxymaltose, ferric derisomaltose, iron sucrose**
- **FAIR-HF** — improved symptoms, functional class, QoL
- **AFFIRM-AHF** — reduced HF hospitalisation post-acute HF
- **IRONMAN (2022)** — IV ferric derisomaltose: reduced CV death + hospitalisation (COVID-truncated trial)
- **Avoid oral iron** — poor absorption in HF (bowel oedema, hepcidin ↑)

## 7. Other Important Comorbidities
- **Sleep apnoea** (CSA, OSA) — ~50% of HF; CSA carries worse prognosis; adaptive servoventilation (SERVE-HF) harmful in predominant CSA; OSA treated with CPAP
- **COPD** — 20–30% of HF; caution with beta-blocker (use cardioselective, e.g. bisoprolol)
- **Diabetes** — SGLT2i first
- **Depression** — 20% of HF; SSRIs safe, screen routinely
- **Frailty, cognitive impairment, falls** — common, especially elderly
- **Cachexia** — BMI <22 with weight loss; carries very poor prognosis

> **Pearls**
> - **Iron deficiency** is the **most common comorbidity** in HF — screen and replace with IV iron
> - **SGLT2i** treat HF, CKD, **and** diabetes simultaneously
> - **AF in HF** — anticoagulate (DOAC); CASTLE-AF favours catheter ablation
> - **Worsening creatinine on ACEi/ARB** — accept 30% rise; don't stop reflexively
> - **Hyperkalaemia** — patiromer / SZC enable continuation of RAAS inhibitor + MRA
> - **Avoid NSAIDs** in HF

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Comorbidity management is **as important as the 4 GDMT pillars**. **IV iron for iron deficiency** is one of the few interventions that improves symptoms AND hospitalisation. **SGLT2i** address HF, CKD, and DM in one drug.
</callout>
