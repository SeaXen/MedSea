# SLE and the Heart

## 1. Definition
- Multi-system autoimmune disease (anti-dsDNA, anti-Sm, low complement)
- Cardiac involvement is the **leading cause of death** in SLE

## 2. Cardiac Manifestations
- **Pericarditis**: most common — serositis with effusion, pleuritic pain
- **Libman-Sacks endocarditis**: sterile, verrucous, **both surfaces** of valves (esp. MV/AV); risk of embolic stroke
- **Myocarditis**: troponin rise, LV dysfunction; mimics ACS
- **Accelerated CAD**: 5-10× risk (young women!) — inflammation + steroids + dyslipidemia
- **Conduction disease**: neonatal lupus (anti-Ro/La) → CHB

## 3. Management
- **Pericarditis**: NSAIDs → colchicine → steroids; taper slowly
- **Libman-Sacks**: anticoagulation if embolic, treat inflammation
- **Myocarditis**: high-dose steroids ± cyclophosphamide, MMF
- **CAD**: aggressive risk factor control, statin, immunosuppression

## 4. Pearls
> **Pearls**: Young woman with **MI** — always think SLE/APLS. Anti-Ro/La in **pregnancy** → fetal echo at 16-26 wk for CHB.
