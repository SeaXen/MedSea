# Myocarditis

## 1. Definition
Inflammation of myocardium with **non-ischemic** injury. Multiple subtypes.

## 2. Subtypes
- **Lymphocytic** (most common, viral — Coxsackie, adenovirus, parvovirus B19, HHV6, SARS-CoV-2)
- **Giant cell** (rare, severe, often in middle-aged men, autoimmune)
- **Eosinophilic** (drug hypersensitivity, hypereosinophilic syndrome, Loeffler endocarditis, parasitic)
- **Granulomatous** (sarcoid, TB)
- **Necrotizing eosinophilic** (hypersensitivity)

## 3. Clinical Presentation
- **Asymptomatic** (subclinical)
- **Chest pain** mimicking MI (pericarditis + myocardial involvement)
- **Heart failure** (acute, fulminant)
- **Arrhythmias** (AV block, VT)
- **Sudden cardiac death**

## 4. Diagnosis
- **CMR with Lake Louise criteria** (T2 edema, LGE, T1 mapping, ECV)
- **Endomyocardial biopsy** — gold standard but invasive
- **Troponin elevation** (often mild-moderate)
- **PCR on biopsy** for viral genome

## 5. Management
- **Supportive** — HF therapy, rest, no exercise (3–6 months)
- **Avoid NSAIDs** (may impair healing)
- **Mechanical support** for fulminant (biopsy-negative at 2 wk: bridge to recovery)
- **Immunosuppression** — giant cell, eosinophilic, autoimmune, sarcoid (NOT viral)
- **Antiviral** — investigational
- **ICD** if persistent LV dysfunction after recovery
- **Avoid exercise** for 3–6 months (recover)

> **Pearls** — **CMR with LGE** = key diagnostic test. **Giant cell, eosinophilic, sarcoid** → immunosuppress. **Lymphocytic viral** → supportive, no immunosuppress. **No NSAIDs, no exercise** during acute phase. Fulminant may recover with MCS support.
