# ACS classification

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 02_Acute_Coronary_Syndromes | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

ACS: clinical spectrum from UA (ischaemia at rest, no necrosis) → NSTEMI (subendocardial necrosis, troponin rise) → STEMI (transmural necrosis, persistent ST elevation). Driven by plaque rupture/erosion → thrombosis. STEMI = complete occlusion (TIMI 0-1), NSTEMI/UA = partial occlusion (TIMI 2-3). Type 1 MI (plaque rupture) vs Type 2 MI (supply-demand). Fourth Universal Definition of MI (2018).


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

Plaque rupture (60-70%) or erosion (25-30%) → thrombogenic core exposure → platelet adhesion (GPIb-vWF) → activation (TxA2, ADP, thrombin) → aggregation (GPIIb/IIIa) → fibrin → thrombus. **Type 1 MI**: plaque rupture with thrombosis. **Type 2 MI**: supply-demand mismatch (anaemia, tachyarrhythmia, hypotension, hypertension crisis, hypoxia). **MINOCA** (5-10%): MI with non-obstructive coronaries.


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

**STEMI**: severe crushing central chest pain >20 min, diaphoresis, dyspnoea, nausea, radiation to jaw/left arm. **NSTEMI/UA**: crescendo angina, rest pain, new-onset severe. Atypical more common in women, elderly, diabetics. Examination: tachycardia, S3/S4, new MR murmur, hypotension in shock, mechanical complications 3-7d post-MI.


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

ECG within 10 min: persistent STE = STEMI; STD/TWI = NSTEMI/UA. **hs-cTn 0/1h algorithm** (ESC 2023): rule-out baseline <5 + Δ1h <3 ng/L; rule-in baseline ≥52 OR Δ1h ≥5. **GRACE score** for risk (low <108, intermediate 108-140, high >140). Echo for RWMA, LV function, mechanical complications. Coronary angiography definitive.


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**GRACE** in-hospital mortality: low <108 (<1%), intermediate 108-140 (1-3%), high >140 (>3%, up to 50%). **TIMI UA/NSTEMI** (0-7, high ≥3). **Killip class** I-IV (I 6%, IV 81%). **SCAI shock stages** A-E.


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**STEMI**: Primary PCI <90 min (or <120 if transfer) with DES. Fibrinolysis (tenecteplase weight-adjusted) if PCI >120 min. DAPT (aspirin 300mg + ticagrelor 180mg per PLATO, or prasugrel 60mg), UFH 70U/kg, GP IIb/IIIa bailout. **NSTEMI/UA**: Early invasive <24h if high-risk (GRACE >140). Fondaparinux 2.5mg SC (OASIS-5). DAPT 12mo. High-intensity statin, ACEi if LVEF≤40%.


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

Arrhythmic (24-48h): VF/VT 5-10%, bradyarrhythmias/AV block, AF 10-20%. Mechanical (3-7d): VSR (anterior 1-2%), papillary rupture (inferior 1-3%), free wall rupture (5-14d, 90% mort). Pericarditis (1-3d), Dressler (weeks-months). Cardiogenic shock 5-10%.


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

Elderly: atypical, more complications. Women: less typical pain, more mechanical complications. Diabetes: multivessel, smaller vessels, more HF, more bleeding. CKD: avoid enoxaparin if CrCl<20, use UFH, radial access. Pregnancy: SCAD common, primary PCI preferred.


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

ESC 2023 ACS (EHJ): 0/1h hs-cTn, cangrelor in PCI, ticagrelor/prasugrel > clopidogrel, complete revascularisation in STEMI multivessel (COMPLETE trial). ACC/AHA 2023: DAPT 12mo for ACS, 6mo for stable. NICE NG185: ticagrelor 1st line. 2018 ESC DAPT: shorter if HBR (PRECISE-DAPT >25), longer if HIR (DAPT score ≥2). Fourth Universal MI 2018.


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

Don't delay cath lab for troponin in STEMI. Posterior MI = STD V1-V4 + take V7-V9. de Winter T-waves = STEMI equivalent. Wellens = pre-infarction. aVR elevation >1mm + diffuse STD = LM/3-vessel. LBBB with Sgarbossa ≥3 = STEMI. SCAD in young women peripartum often missed. Cocaine: avoid beta-blockers.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

STEMI (transmural), NSTEMI (subendocardial), GRACE, TIMI, Sgarbossa 5-3-2, TCFA (Thin-Cap FibroAtheroma), DAPT, DES, de Winter (upsloping STD V1-V6 + tall T), Wellens (biphasic T V2-V3), SCAI (A-E).


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Aortic dissection (tearing, BP differential, wide mediastinum — give beta-blocker first, NOT antiplatelets). Pericarditis (diffuse concave STE, PR dep). PE (S1Q3T3, RV strain). Takotsubo (apical ballooning, no culprit). Oesophageal spasm. MSK pain. Myocarditis (viral prodrome, diffuse).


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

ECG (immediate, serial, right-sided, V7-V9 if posterior), hs-cTn 0/1h, FBC, U&E, LFT, TFT, lipids, HbA1c, CXR, bedside echo, formal echo 24h, coronary angiography, CTCA if low-intermediate probability, CMR if MINOCA, CT aortogram if dissection.

A. ECG
B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography
E. Cardiac biomarkers

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Echo is first-line for structural/functional assessment; additional tests as indicated.

**Q3.** Most appropriate first-line therapy for ACS classification:
A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy
D. Surgical intervention
E. Multidisciplinary care

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Guideline-directed medical therapy is cornerstone; other modalities as indicated.

**Q4.** Which is a contraindication to therapy in ACS classification?
A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

**Q5.** The most common complication of ACS classification is:
A. Heart failure

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.
