# Non-Compaction Cardiomyopathy (NCCM)

## 1. Definition
**Left ventricular non-compaction (LVNC / NCCM)** = cardiomyopathy with prominent **LV trabeculations and deep intertrabecular recesses** communicating with the ventricular cavity, with a thin compacted epicardial layer.

## 2. Key Features
- **Genetics**: heterogeneous; mutations in **MYH7, MYBPC3, TTN, ACTC1, NKX2-5**; also seen with other CMPs
- **Prevalence**: 0.05% of adults; asymptomatic in many
- **Diagnostic criteria (Jenni/Petersen)**:
  - 2-layer structure: thin compacted epicardium + thick non-compacted endocardium (ratio >2:1 at end-systole)
  - Deep intertrabecular recesses filled with blood on echo/CMR
  - Most often in **apical, mid-lateral, mid-inferior** segments
- **Triad of complications**: **HF** (HFrEF, HFpEF, restrictive), **arrhythmias** (VT, AF, CHB), **thromboembolism** (stroke)
- **Diagnosis**: echo (contrast improves), **CMR** (gold standard — Petersen criteria: NC/C ratio >2.3 in diastole)
- **Differential**: HCM, DCM, hypertensive heart, athlete's heart (in athletes, ratio usually <1.5)
- **Prognosis**: variable; depends on LV function and complications

## 3. Management
- **Standard HF GDMT** if LV dysfunction
- **Anticoagulation**: controversial; consider if LVEF <40%, AF, prior thromboembolism
- **ICD** for primary prevention if LVEF ≤35% + NYHA II–III
- **CRT** if LBBB + QRS ≥150 ms + LVEF ≤35%
- **Family screening**: first-degree relatives with echo ± genetic testing
- **Avoid** in athletes if abnormal — may need restriction
- **Genetic counselling**

> **Pearls**
> - **NCCM** = prominent trabeculations + recesses with NC/C >2.3
> - **Triad**: HF, arrhythmia, thromboembolism
> - **CMR** is gold standard for diagnosis
> - **Family screening** recommended (genetic)
