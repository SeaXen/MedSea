# HCM — Genetics (Sarcomere Genes) & Prevalence

## 1. Definition & Burden
**Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)** is defined by LV wall thickness **≥15 mm** (or ≥13 mm with family history) in the absence of another cause (HTN, valve disease, athlete's heart). It is the **most common inherited cardiac disease** with prevalence **~1:500** in adults (0.2%) using imaging criteria, and **~1:200** with genetic screening.

## 2. Inheritance
- **Autosomal dominant** with **age-dependent, incomplete penetrance** (~50% by age 30, ~75% by 50)
- Variable **expressivity** within families
- De novo mutations in ~20–30%

## 3. Sarcomere Genes (Pathogenic Variants in ~40–60% of HCM)
| Gene | Protein | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **MYH7** | β-myosin heavy chain | ~30–40% | Most common; thick filament |
| **MYBPC3** | Myosin-binding protein C | ~30–40% | Most common; truncating variants |
| **TNNT2** | Cardiac troponin T | ~5% | Mild hypertrophy, **high SCD risk** |
| **TNNI3** | Cardiac troponin I | ~5% | Apical HCM pattern |
| **TPM1** | α-tropomyosin | <5% | |
| **ACTC1** | Cardiac actin | <1% | |
| **MYL2/MYL3** | Regulatory/essential light chains | <2% | |

## 4. Pathophysiology
Sarcomere mutation → impaired myocyte relaxation + energy deficit → **myocyte disarray** (hallmark histology), interstitial fibrosis, and small-vessel dysplasia → diastolic dysfunction + dynamic LVOT obstruction + ischaemia + arrhythmogenic substrate.

## 5. Genotype-Positive / Phenotype-Negative
- ~5% of relatives carry the family variant but have **no LVH on imaging**
- These patients still need **lifelong surveillance** (echo every 3–5 yrs in adults, sooner in adolescents)
- Risk of SCD without LVH is very low but not zero

## 6. Family Screening
- **First-degree relatives**: ECG + echo from **age 10–12** (earlier if family SCD <18 yrs or competitive athlete)
- Repeat every **1–3 years during adolescence**, every **3–5 years in adults**
- **Genetic testing** offered to proband; if mutation identified, cascade testing in relatives
- If genotype-negative proband, screen relatives clinically (echo/ECG)

> **Pearls**
> - Prevalence ~1:500 (imaging); ~40–60% genotype-positive.
> - **MYH7** and **MYBPC3** account for ~70% of genotyped HCM.
> - **TNNT2** mutations → mild hypertrophy but disproportionate SCD risk.
> - Sarcomere mutations cause **myocyte disarray + fibrosis** — the substrate for re-entrant VT.
> - First-degree relatives need **clinical screening from age 10–12**; cascade genetic testing when family variant known.
