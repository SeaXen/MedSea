# STEMI (primary PCI <90 min, fibrinolysis if PCI >120 min)

## 1. Definition
**ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction (STEMI)**: Acute coronary syndrome with persistent ST-segment elevation indicating transmural myocardial necrosis from acute coronary artery occlusion. A **medical emergency** — "time is muscle."

## 2. Diagnostic Criteria
- **ST elevation ≥1 mm** (≥2 mm in V2–V3) in two contiguous leads
- **New LBBB** with Sgarbossa criteria
- **Posterior MI**: ST depression V1–V3 with upright T waves (add V7–V9)
- **Cardiac biomarkers**: Troponin elevation (rises within 3–6 h)
- Clinical: crushing chest pain >20 min, diaphoresis, dyspnea

## 3. Reperfusion Strategy (Time-Critical)
| Scenario | Strategy | Target |
|----------|----------|--------|
| PCI available | **Primary PCI** | Door-to-balloon ≤90 min (≤60 min if already in PCI hospital) |
| PCI not available within 120 min | **Fibrinolysis** | Door-to-needle ≤30 min |
| Failed fibrinolysis | Rescue PCI | Within 3–24 h |
| Cardiogenic shock, mechanical complication | **Emergency PCI** regardless of time | <90 min |

**Fibrinolysis preferred if**: symptom onset <3 h, PCI lab unavailable, transfer >120 min.

## 4. Adjunctive Pharmacotherapy
- **Dual antiplatelet**: Aspirin 300 mg + P2Y12 (ticagrelor 180 mg or prasugrel 60 mg)
- **Anticoagulation**: UFH bolus (with PCI) or enoxaparin (with fibrinolysis)
- **Statin**: High-intensity (atorvastatin 80 mg) regardless of LDL
- **Beta-blocker**: Within 24 h if no contraindications
- **ACEi/ARB**: Within 24 h in anterior MI, LV dysfunction

## 5. Fibrinolytic Agents
- **TNK-tPA** (tenecteplase): Single weight-adjusted bolus — preferred
- **Streptokinase**: Cheaper; causes hypotension, allergy
- **rPA** (reteplase): Double bolus
- **Contraindications**: Active bleeding, prior stroke <6 months, recent surgery, BP >180/110

## 6. Complications
- **Arrhythmias**: VF/VT (most common cause of early death), bradycardia, AV block
- **Cardiogenic shock** (esp. anterior MI)
- **Mechanical complications**: Free wall rupture, VSR, papillary muscle rupture (3–7 days)
- **Pericarditis** (Dressler's at 2–10 weeks)
- **Reinfarction**, mural thrombus, LV aneurysm

> **Pearls**
> - Goal: **First medical contact to device ≤90 min** (or ≤120 min if transfer needed)
> - Inferior STEMI with RV involvement: avoid nitrates, give IV fluids
> - Posterior MI often missed — always look for ST depression V1–V3
> - LBBB + Sgarbossa: discordant ST elevation ≥1 mm = STEMI equivalent
