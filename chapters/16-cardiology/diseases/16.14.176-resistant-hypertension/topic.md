# Resistant Hypertension

Related: [[../Cardiology MOC|Cardiology MOC]] · [[../Davidson Chapter 16 - Cardiology Hierarchy|Cardiology Hierarchy]] · [[../Hypertension and Cardiovascular Risk States|Hypertension and Cardiovascular Risk States]] · [[Hypertensive emergencies and target-organ risk]] · [[../Essential hypertension|Essential hypertension]] · [[../Secondary hypertension|Secondary hypertension]] · [[../White-coat and masked hypertension|White-coat and masked hypertension]] · [[../Hypertension with left ventricular hypertrophy|Hypertension with left ventricular hypertrophy]] · [[../Cardiorenal syndrome in heart failure|Cardiorenal syndrome in heart failure]]

> [!important]
> **Resistant hypertension** is a high-yield FCPS/MRCP topic. Exams test: **definition** (BP uncontrolled on ≥3 drugs including a diuretic at optimal doses), **pseudo-resistance exclusion** (white-coat, non-adherence, suboptimal dosing, measurement error), **secondary cause screening** (primary aldosteronism, renal artery stenosis, CKD, OSA), and **stepwise management** (spironolactone as 4th-line, then others). The **PATHWAY-2 trial** established spironolactone superiority over placebo/doxazosin/bisoprolol.

## Learning Objectives
- Define resistant hypertension and distinguish from pseudo-resistance
- Exclude pseudo-resistance systematically (ABPM, adherence, dosing, measurement)
- Screen for secondary causes using targeted algorithm
- Apply evidence-based 4th-line therapy (spironolactone per PATHWAY-2)
- Manage refractory cases (device therapy, specialist referral)
- Recognise prognosis: high CV risk, target-organ damage, requires multidisciplinary approach

## Definition

**Resistant Hypertension (RH)** = **Office BP ≥ 140/90 mmHg** despite **≥3 antihypertensive agents** of **different classes** at **optimal/tolerated doses**, **including a diuretic**, with **adherence confirmed**.

| Category | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| **Controlled resistant** | BP < 140/90 on ≥4 drugs |
| **Uncontrolled resistant** | BP ≥ 140/90 on ≥3 drugs (including diuretic) |
| **Refractory hypertension** | BP uncontrolled on ≥5 drugs (including diuretic) |
| **Pseudo-resistance** | Apparent RH due to measurement error, non-adherence, white-coat, suboptimal dosing |

> [!warning]
> **True resistant hypertension prevalence**: ~10–15% of treated hypertensives. **Pseudo-resistance accounts for ~30–50%** of apparent RH — **must exclude first**.

## Pseudo-Resistance — Exclusion Checklist (Mandatory Before Labeling RH)

| Cause | How to Exclude | Key Points |
|-------|----------------|------------|
| **White-coat effect** | **ABPM / HBPM** | ABPM: 24-hr avg < 130/80; daytime < 135/85 = white-coat uncontrolled |
| **Non-adherence** | Direct questioning, pharmacy refill data, urine drug screening | **Most common** cause of apparent RH; ~50% non-adherent at 1 year |
| **Suboptimal dosing** | Verify doses at maximum tolerated | Diuretic must be at **effective dose** (e.g., chlorthalidone 25 mg, HCTZ 25–50 mg) |
| **Inappropriate combinations** | Check: 3 drugs of different classes, including diuretic | Avoid duplicate class (e.g., ACEi + ARB); diuretic must match kidney function |
| **Measurement error** | Correct cuff size, patient position, rest 5 min, multiple readings | Automated oscillometric preferred; large cuff for obesity |
| **Medication interactions** | Review: NSAIDs, steroids, Oral contraceptives, liquorice, sympathomimetics, erythropoietin, antidepressants (SNRIs), herbal | **NSAIDs are top offender** — ↓ diuretic/ACEi effect |
| **Volume overload** | Dietary sodium excess, CKD, diuretic resistance | 24-hr urinary Na⁺ > 150 mmol = high salt intake |

> [!tip]
> **ABPM is the gold standard** to confirm true RH. If 24-hr average < 130/80 → **white-coat uncontrolled hypertension**, not true RH.

## Secondary Causes — Targeted Screening Algorithm

| Cause | Prevalence in RH | Screening Test | Positive Threshold |
|-------|------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Primary aldosteronism (PA)** | **15–25%** | **ARR: Aldosterone/Renin Ratio** (morning, seated, after 2 hr) | ARR > 30 (aldosterone in ng/dL, renin in ng/mL/hr) or > 700 (SI units) |
| **Renal parenchymal disease (CKD)** | 20–30% | eGFR, urine ACR | eGFR < 60, ACR > 30 mg/g |
| **Renovascular (RAS)** | 5–10% | Duplex US / CTA / MRA | >70% stenosis, RI > 0.8, flash flood |
| **Obstructive Sleep Apnoea (OSA)** | **30–50%** | STOP-BANG ≥ 3 → sleep study | AHI ≥ 15 |
| **Cushing's syndrome** | Rare | 1 mg dexamethasone suppression / late-night salivary cortisol | Cortisol > 1.8 µg/dL (50 nmol/L) post-dex |
| **Phaeochromocytoma** | Rare | Plasma free metanephrines / 24-hr urinary metanephrines | >2–3× ULN |
| **Thyroid disease** | Rare | TSH, free T4 | TSH suppressed (hyper) or ↑ (hypo) |
| **Coarctation of aorta** | Rare (young) | BP gradient arm-leg, CT/MRI | Systolic gradient > 20 mmHg |
| **Drug-induced** | Common | Medication review | NSAIDs, OCPs, steroids, SNRIs, liquorice, sympathomimetics |

> [!important]
> **Screen in this order for yield**: 1) **PA (ARR)** 2) **CKD** 3) **OSA** 4) **RAS** 5) **Others if clinical suspicion**.
> **ARR**: Withhold interfering drugs 4 weeks (diuretics, ACEi/ARB, beta-blockers, CCB). Can use **plasma renin activity (PRA)** instead of direct renin.

### Primary Aldosteronism Workup (If ARR Positive)

```mermaid
flowchart TD
A[ARR > 30] --> B[Confirmatory test]
B --> B1[Saline infusion test: 2L NS over 4h → aldosterone > 10 ng/dL]
B --> B2[Oral sodium loading: 3 days high salt → 24h urinary aldosterone > 12 µg]
B --> B3[Captopril challenge: captopril 25-50 mg → ARR remains elevated]
B1 --> C{Aldosterone suppressed?}
B2 --> C
B3 --> C
C -->|No| D[PA confirmed]
D --> E[Adrenal CT]
E --> F{Unilateral adenoma?}
F -->|Yes| G[Adrenal venous sampling → unilateral → adrenalectomy]
F -->|No| H[Bilateral hyperplasia → MR antagonist (spironolactone/eplerenone)]
```

## Management Algorithm

### Step 1: Exclude Pseudo-Resistance (Non-negotiable)
- **ABPM/HBPM** → confirm true uncontrolled BP
- **Adherence assessment** → open conversation, pharmacy records, urine screen
- **Medication review** → stop NSAIDs, adjust interacting drugs
- **Diuretic optimisation** → **chlorthalidone 12.5–25 mg** preferred over HCTZ (longer half-life, CV outcome data); if eGFR < 30 → **loop diuretic** (furosemide 40 mg BD/TDS)

### Step 2: Optimise 3-Drug Regimen (A + C + D)
| Class | Preferred Agent | Dose |
|-------|-----------------|------|
| **A: ACEi / ARB** | Ramipril 10 mg / Losartan 100 mg / Valsartan 320 mg | Max tolerated |
| **C: CCB** | Amlodipine 10 mg | Max tolerated |
| **D: Diuretic** | **Chlorthalidone 25 mg** (eGFR > 30) | **Preferred over HCTZ** |

> [!tip]
> **Chlorthalidone > HCTZ** for resistant HTN: longer duration, better 24-hr control, CV outcome evidence (ALLHAT). If eGFR < 30 → **furosemide 40 mg BD** (short half-life → BD/TDS dosing).

### Step 3: Add 4th-Line Agent — **Spironolactone (PATHWAY-2 Evidence)**

| Trial | Population | Result |
|-------|------------|--------|
| **PATHWAY-2** | RH on A+C+D, eGFR > 45, K⁺ < 4.5 | **Spironolactone 25 mg** ↓ home SBP **−8.7 mmHg** vs placebo; superior to doxazosin (−4.0) and bisoprolol (−4.5) |

| Agent | Dose | Monitoring | Contraindications |
|-------|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Spironolactone** | **25 mg OD** (max 50 mg) | **K⁺, eGFR at 1, 4, 8, 12 weeks, then 6-monthly** | eGFR < 45, K⁺ > 4.5, Addison's, concurrent K⁺-sparing |
| **Eplerenone** | 50–100 mg OD | Same | eGFR < 30, K⁺ > 5.0; **less gynaecomastia** |
| **Amiloride** | 5–10 mg OD | Same | eGFR < 30, K⁺ > 5.0 |

> [!warning]
> **Hyperkalaemia risk**: Check K⁺ at **1 week, 4 weeks, 8 weeks, 12 weeks**, then 6-monthly. If K⁺ > 5.5 → halve dose; if K⁺ > 6.0 → stop.

### Step 4: 5th-Line Options (If BP Still Uncontrolled on Spironolactone)

| Agent | Dose | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| **Alpha-blocker** (doxazosin) | 4–16 mg OD | GITS formulation; caution: first-dose hypotension, HF risk (ALLHAT) |
| **Beta-blocker** (bisoprolol/nebivolol) | 5–10 mg OD | If not already used; nebivolol preferred (vasodilatory) |
| **Central alpha-2 agonist** (clonidine) | 0.1–0.3 mg BD | Rebound hypertension on withdrawal; sedation; patch available |
| **Direct vasodilator** (hydralazine) | 25–50 mg TDS | Requires BB + diuretic (reflex tachycardia, fluid retention); lupus-like syndrome |
| **Minoxidil** | 5–40 mg OD | **Last resort**; hirsutism, pericardial effusion, fluid retention → needs loop diuretic + BB |

### Step 5: Device / Specialist Therapies (Refractory RH)
| Therapy | Indication | Evidence |
|---------|------------|----------|
| **Renal denervation (RDN)** | Refractory RH on ≥3 drugs incl. diuretic, eGFR > 40 | **SPYRAL HTN-OFF/ON, RADIANCE-HTN**: modest ↓ SBP 5–10 mmHg; NICE/IPG supported |
| **Baroreceptor activation therapy** | Refractory RH | Limited availability; surgical implantation |
| **Carotid body ablation** | Experimental | Not standard |
| **Specialist RH clinic** | Multidisciplinary (cardiology, nephrology, endocrine, pharmacy) | Best outcomes |

## Lifestyle Modifications (Adjunctive, Not Alternative)

| Intervention | Target | BP Reduction |
|--------------|--------|--------------|
| **Sodium restriction** | < 2 g/day (5 g salt) | 4–6 mmHg |
| **DASH diet** | High K⁺, Mg²⁺, fibre, low sat fat | 8–11 mmHg |
| **Weight loss** | BMI < 25, waist < 94 cm (M) / 80 cm (F) | 1 mmHg per kg |
| **Exercise** | 150 min/week moderate aerobic | 4–9 mmHg |
| **Alcohol** | ≤ 14 units/week (M), ≤ 8 (F) | 3–4 mmHg |
| **Potassium supplementation** | If K⁺ < 4.5, dietary K⁺ ↑ | Modest |

## Special Populations

### Chronic Kidney Disease (eGFR < 60)
- **Diuretic**: Loop diuretic (furosemide BD/TDS) ± thiazide-like (metolazone 2.5–5 mg) for sequential nephron blockade
- **Spironolactone**: Caution if eGFR < 45; monitor K⁺ closely; consider **finerenone** (non-steroidal MRA, FIDELIO-DKD) if diabetic CKD
- **Target BP**: < 130/80 (KDIGO/ESC)

### Obstructive Sleep Apnoea
- **CPAP** ↓ BP by 2–5 mmHg (greater in severe OSA, resistant HTN)
- Screen all RH with **STOP-BANG**; refer for sleep study if ≥ 3

### Black African / Caribbean Origin
- **Salt-sensitive**, low renin → **CCB + thiazide-like diuretic** first-line
- **ACEi/ARB less effective** as monotherapy; need combination

### Elderly (>80) / Frail
- **Start low, go slow**; target < 140/90 (or < 150/90 if frail)
- **Chlorthalidone 6.25–12.5 mg**; avoid overtreatment (falls, AKI)

## Drug Interactions / Contraindications / Comorbidity Cautions

| Interaction | Effect | Management |
|-------------|--------|------------|
| **NSAIDs + ACEi/ARB + diuretic** | **"Triple whammy"** → AKI, ↑ BP, ↓ diuretic effect | **Avoid NSAIDs**; use paracetamol, topical NSAIDs |
| **Spironolactone + ACEi/ARB** | ↑ K⁺ risk | Monitor K⁺ closely; if K⁺ > 5.5 → stop spironolactone |
| **Spironolactone + ACEi/ARB + trimethoprim** | Severe hyperkalaemia | Avoid combination |
| **Aliskiren + ACEi/ARB** | ↑ AKI, hyperkalaemia, hypotension | **Contraindicated** in diabetes/CKD |
| **ACEi + ARB** | ↑ AKI, hyperkalaemia, no CV benefit | **Avoid dual RAAS blockade** |
| **CCB (verapamil/diltiazem) + beta-blocker** | Bradycardia, AV block | Avoid combination; use amlodipine if BB needed |

## Procedures / Indications / Contraindications

### Adrenal Venous Sampling (AVS)
- **Indication**: Confirmed PA, CT shows unilateral adenoma or bilateral hyperplasia, candidate for surgery
- **Procedure**: Bilateral adrenal vein + IVC catheterisation; cortisol-normalised aldosterone ratio
- **Lateralisation**: Aldo/cortisol (dominant) / aldo/cortisol (non-dominant) > 4 → unilateral; < 3 → bilateral
- **Contraindication**: Uncorrectable coagulopathy, contrast allergy

### Renal Artery Angioplasty/Stenting
- **Indication**: Fibromuscular dysplasia (FMD) — **first-line**; atherosclerotic RAS — **only if flash pulmonary oedema, refractory HTN, or deteriorating renal function** (CORAL trial: no benefit over medical therapy alone for atherosclerotic RAS)
- **Contraindication**: Small kidney (< 8 cm), severe comorbidites

## Complications of Mismanagement
- **Missing pseudo-resistance** → unnecessary escalation, side effects, cost
- **Missing primary aldosteronism** → untreated → LVH, AF, stroke, CKD
- **Spironolactone without K⁺ monitoring** → life-threatening hyperkalaemia
- **Inappropriate RAS stenting** (atherosclerotic) → no benefit, procedural risk
- **Missing OSA** → persistent RH, CV risk, arrhythmias

## Red Flags / Emergencies
- **Hypertensive urgency/emergency** in RH patient → see Hypertensive emergency note
- **Hyperkalaemia (K⁺ > 6.0)** on spironolactone → stop, treat urgently
- **Acute kidney injury** (↑ creatinine > 30%) after diuretic/ACEi/ARB/spironolactone → review, hold if needed
- **Flash pulmonary oedema** → consider bilateral RAS or critical unilateral RAS

## Prognosis
- **True RH**: 2–3× higher CV event rate vs controlled HTN
- **Adequate control on 4+ drugs**: risk approaches controlled HTN
- **Primary aldosteronism**: highest stroke/AF/LVH risk if undiagnosed; curable if unilateral
- **Refractory RH**: consider RDN trial enrolment or specialist clinic

## Special Situations

### Pregnancy
- **Resistant HTN in pregnancy** = secondary cause until proven otherwise (pre-eclampsia, renal, endocrine)
- **Spironolactone contraindicated** (anti-androgenic); use methyldopa, labetalol, nifedipine
- **Methyldopa** first-line historically; **labetalol** preferred now

### Post-Transplant
- **Calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus/ciclosporin)** + steroids → hypertension
- **CCB (amlodipine)** preferred (↓ calcineurin inhibitor metabolism vs dihydropyridines)
- **Avoid** ACEi/ARB early post-transplant (renal artery stenosis risk)

### COVID-19 / Long COVID
- New-onset or worsening HTN reported; mechanism unclear (endothelial dysfunction, RAS dysregulation)
- Manage per standard algorithm; screen secondary causes if resistant

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- Labelling RH without ABPM (white-coat effect)
- Not checking adherence (most common pseudo-resistance)
- Using HCTZ instead of chlorthalidone in RH
- Forgetting to stop interfering drugs before ARR
- Giving spironolactone without K⁺ monitoring
- Stenting atherosclerotic RAS expecting BP cure (CORAL trial: no benefit)
- Missing OSA in resistant HTN
- Using ACEi + ARB together (dual RAAS blockade — avoid)
