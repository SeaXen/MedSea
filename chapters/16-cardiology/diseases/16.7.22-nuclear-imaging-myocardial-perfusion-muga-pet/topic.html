CARDIOLOGY
Nuclear imaging (myocardial perfusion, MUGA, PET)
1 Introduction

Nuclear imaging uses radiolabelled tracers to assess perfusion, metabolism, and function. SPECT (single-photon) and PET (positron emission tomography). SPECT most common (thallium, technetium); PET for metabolism/flow quantification.

2 Myocardial Perfusion Imaging (MPI)
SPECT Tracers
  • 201Thallium — K+ analogue; redistributes (viability)
  • 99mTc-sestamibi (MIBI) — trapped in mitochondria; minimal redistribution (separate stress/rest injections)
  • 99mTc-tetrofosmin — similar to MIBI
  • Dose ~10–20 mSv total
Protocol
  • Stress (exercise or pharmacological — adenosine/dobutamine)
  • Inject tracer at peak stress
  • Image 15–60 min later (rest image on separate day or same day low-dose)
  • Gated SPECT — adds EF, wall motion
Interpretation — 17-Segment Model
  • Summed Stress Score (SSS) — extent + severity of ischaemia
  • SSS 4–8 = mild, 9–13 = moderate, >13 = severe ischaemia → high risk, refer for angio
  • Summed Rest Score (SRS) — scar
  • Summed Difference Score (SDS) = SSS − SRS = ischaemia burden
Reversible Defect = Ischaemia
  • Fixed defect = scar
  • Partial reversibility = mixed ischaemia + scar
  • Reverse redistribution = microvascular dysfunction, post-MI
3 PET Myocardial Perfusion
  • 13N-ammonia, 82Rubidium, 18F-flurpiridaz — quantitative MBF (mL/min/g)
  • MBF <2.0 stress, MFR <2.0 = significant ischaemia
  • CFR (coronary flow reserve) = stress MBF / rest MBF
  • Higher resolution, less artefact (esp. in women — no breast attenuation)
  • Quantifies ischaemia in balanced ischaemia / multivessel disease
4 MUGA Scan (Multi-Gated Acquisition)
  • 99mTc-labelled RBCs (in vivo labelling)
  • Measures LV EF with high reproducibility (CV <5%)
  • Used for chemotherapy monitoring (anthracyclines — hold if EF <50% or drop ≥10%)
  • Summed EF <50% → consider stopping chemo
5 FDG-PET for Viability
  • 18F-FDG uptake = metabolically active (viable) myocardium
  • Perfusion-metabolism mismatch = hibernating myocardium (ischaemic but viable → revascularise)
  • Match defect (low perfusion + low FDG) = scar
  • Standard for ischaemic cardiomyopathy EF <35% to guide revascularisation (PARR-2, F-18 viability substudies)
6 Sarcoidosis / Inflammation
  • FDG-PET with high-fat low-carb diet to suppress physiological myocardial uptake
  • Focal FDG uptake = active inflammation
  • Use in cardiac sarcoidosis diagnosis (HRS criteria)
  • Perfusion + FDG = diseased/inflamed segments
7 Amyloid Imaging
  • 99mTc-pyrophosphate (PYP) scan for ATTR amyloidosis
  • Grade 2–3 myocardial uptake at 3 h = positive (heart-to-contralateral ratio >1.5)
  • Excludes AL amyloid if positive (when monoclonal screen negative)
  • DPD / HMDP / PYP — all bone tracers
8 Sympathetic Innervation
  • 123I-MIBG (meta-iodobenzylguanidine) — norepinephrine analogue
  • Heart/mediastinum ratio; predicts arrhythmia in HF
  • Used in ICD risk stratification (ADMIRE-HF)

10 Limitations
  • Radiation (5–15 mSv typical)
  • Attenuation artefact (breast, diaphragm) — prone imaging, attenuation correction
  • Balanced ischaemia (3-vessel disease) underestimates
  • Atrial fibrillation artefacts
  • Cost, availability (PET especially)
Pearls - SSS >13 = severe ischaemia → refer for angiography - Reversible defect = ischaemia; fixed = scar - FDG-PET mismatch = hibernating myocardium (viable) - 99mTc-PYP grade ≥2 = ATTR cardiac amyloid (after AL excluded) - MUGA = gold standard for serial EF (chemotherapy monitoring) - PET MFR <2.0 = significant ischaemia (quantitative)
