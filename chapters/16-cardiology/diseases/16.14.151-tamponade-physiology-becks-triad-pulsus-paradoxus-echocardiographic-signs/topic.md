# Tamponade physiology (Beck's triad, pulsus paradoxus, echocardiographic signs)

## 1. Definition
**Cardiac tamponade**: Compression of heart chambers by accumulated pericardial fluid under pressure, leading to impaired diastolic filling and reduced cardiac output. Severity depends on **rate of accumulation** and **volume**.

## 2. Pathophysiology
- Pericardial fluid ↑ → intrapericardial pressure ↑
- External pressure compresses **low-pressure chambers first** (RA, RV, LA)
- ↓Diastolic filling → ↓Stroke volume → ↓Cardiac output → shock
- **Compensatory**: Tachycardia, vasoconstriction, ↑contractility
- **Equalization of pressures**: RA, RV diastolic, LA, pericardial pressure

## 3. Beck's Triad (Classical — Late Signs)
1. **Hypotension** (low cardiac output)
2. **Muffled heart sounds** (fluid between heart and stethoscope)
3. **Jugular venous distension** (impaired RA emptying)

> All 3 present in only ~30% — late findings. Don't wait for Beck's triad.

## 4. Pulsus Paradoxus
- **Definition**: ≥10 mmHg drop in SBP during inspiration
- **Mechanism**: Inspiration → ↑venous return → RV fills more → septum shifts left → LV underfilled → ↓SBP
- **Tamponade is the most common cause**, but also: severe asthma, COPD, constrictive pericarditis, PE, hypovolemia
- **Measurement**: SBP at which Korotkoff sounds heard only in expiration vs. throughout respiration

## 5. Echocardiographic Signs
### 2D Echo
- **RA systolic collapse** (inversion during atrial systole) — sensitive
- **RV diastolic collapse** (free wall) — more specific
- **LA/LV collapse** (late)
- **IVC plethora** (dilated, <50% collapse) — universal
- **"Swinging heart"** (excessive motion in large effusion)
- **Respiratory variation** in chamber sizes

### Doppler
- **Exaggerated respiratory variation in mitral inflow** (>25%)
- **Exaggerated variation in tricuspid inflow** (>40%)
- **Hepatic vein flow reversal** in expiration

## 6. Clinical Stages
| Stage | Hemodynamics | Signs |
|-------|--------------|-------|
| **Mild** | Pericardial pressure < RA | Often asymptomatic, small effusion |
| **Moderate** | RA pressure = pericardial | Pulsus paradoxus, JVD, tachycardia |
| **Severe** | Equalization | Hypotension, Beck's triad, shock |

## 7. ECG Findings
- **Sinus tachycardia** (most common)
- **Low voltage QRS** (fluid attenuates)
- **Electrical alternans** (heart "swings" in fluid)
- PR depression, ST elevation (if pericarditis)

## 8. Management
- **Pericardiocentesis** (definitive)
- **IV fluids** to maintain preload (temporary bridge)
- **Avoid**: Diuretics, vasodilators, positive pressure ventilation
- **Treat cause**

> **Pearls**
> - **Beck's triad** is **late** — don't wait for it
> - **Pulsus paradoxus** > 10 mmHg = tamponade until proven otherwise
> - **IVC plethora** on echo is the most sensitive sign
> - **RV diastolic collapse** is the **most specific** sign
> - **Equalization of pressures** at cardiac catheterization is diagnostic
