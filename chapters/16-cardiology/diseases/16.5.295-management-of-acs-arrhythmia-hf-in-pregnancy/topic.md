# Management of ACS, Arrhythmia, and HF in Pregnancy

## 1. Acute Coronary Syndrome
- **Rare (1 in 16,000)** but rising (older mothers, IVF, hypertensive disorders)
- Main mechanisms: **SCAD** (spontaneous coronary artery dissection) most common in pregnancy; atherosclerosis, thrombosis
- **Diagnosis:** Troponin elevation is significant (cut-off similar to non-pregnant). ECG is valid. CT coronary angio with low radiation acceptable if needed.
- **Management:**
  - **SCAD:** Conservative (β-blockers, antiplatelets), avoid PCI unless ongoing ischemia or left main
  - **Atherothrombotic STEMI:** Primary PCI is preferred; radiation shielding of fetus
  - **Avoid:** Fibrinolysis (relative contraindication, especially peripartum)
  - **Aspirin** safe; **clopidogrel** limited data — discuss risk-benefit
  - **Statins** contraindicated
- **Delivery:** MDT decision; vaginal delivery usually preferred after stabilization

## 2. Arrhythmia
- **Atrial fibrillation/flutter:** Uncommon; if hemodynamically unstable → **cardioversion** (safe in pregnancy). Rate control: β-blockers (metoprolol, labetalol) or digoxin. **Avoid amiodarone** (fetal thyroid).
- **SVT:** Vagal maneuvers → adenosine (safe). If refractory: metoprolol, verapamil. **DC cardioversion safe at any gestation.**
- **VT:** Cardioversion if unstable. β-blockers, lidocaine (safe); avoid sotalol, amiodarone if possible
- **New arrhythmia workup:** Rule out structural disease, thyroid, drug use, peripartum cardiomyopathy

## 3. Heart Failure
- **Avoid teratogens:** ACEi, ARB, ARNI, warfarin (1st trimester), atenolol, amiodarone, statins
- **Safe drugs in pregnancy:**
  - **Diuretics:** Furosemide, bumetanide (monitor volume status)
  - **β-blockers:** Metoprolol, labetalol, carvedilol
  - **Vasodilators:** Hydralazine + isosorbide dinitrate (replaces ACEi)
  - **Anticoagulation:** LMWH
- **PPCM management:** Bromocriptine, GDMT postpartum, lactation considerations
- **Severe LV dysfunction (LVEF <30%) at term:** Consider planned C-section with general anesthesia

> **Pearls**
> - DC cardioversion is **safe in pregnancy at any stage**
> - **SCAD is the #1 cause of pregnancy MI** — think pregnancy-associated MI in any woman with chest pain, especially 3rd trimester/postpartum
> - Stop ACEi/ARB/ARNI **before conception** in heart failure patients — substitute hydralazine-ISDN
