# Hypertensive Emergencies/Urgencies

## 1. Definitions
- **Emergency**: severe HTN (often >180/120) with **acute TOD** — encephalopathy, ICH, stroke, ACS, ADHF, aortic dissection, AKI, eclampsia
- **Urgency**: severe HTN **without** TOD; no immediate danger

## 2. Clinical Features of TOD
- **Brain**: headache, confusion, visual loss, seizures, papilledema (PRES)
- **Heart**: ACS, pulmonary edema, dissection
- **Renal**: AKI, hematuria
- **Retina**: hemorrhage, exudates, papilledema (grade III/IV)

## 3. Management
- **Emergency**: IV therapy, **lower BP by 20-25% in 1st hour** (max), then to 160/100 over next 2-6h, then to target over 24-48h
  - **Stroke ICH**: target SBP <140 in 1h (INTERACT3)
  - **Ischemic stroke** (tPA candidate): <185/110
  - **ADHF/ACS**: nitroprusside, nitroglycerin
  - **Aortic dissection**: target HR <60 + SBP <120 (esmolol + nitroprusside)
- **Urgency**: oral Rx, gradual reduction over 24-48h; **not** sublingual nifedipine

## 4. Pearls
> **Pearls**: Goal in emergency is **not** to normalize BP — rapid drop causes ischemia. **Avoid** sublingual nifedipine (uncontrolled drop, stroke/MI).
