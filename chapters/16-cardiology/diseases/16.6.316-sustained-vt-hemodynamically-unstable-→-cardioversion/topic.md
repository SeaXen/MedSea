# Sustained VT (hemodynamically unstable → cardioversion)

## 1. Definition
**Sustained Ventricular Tachycardia (VT)**: Wide-complex tachycardia (QRS >120 ms) at >100 bpm lasting >30 sec OR requiring intervention due to hemodynamic compromise. Arises from below the bundle of His.

## 2. Classification
- **Monomorphic VT**: Single QRS morphology — usually scar-related reentry (post-MI, cardiomyopathy)
- **Polymorphic VT**: Multiple QRS morphologies — ischemia, prolonged QT, channelopathies
- **Sustained**: >30 sec or hemodynamically unstable
- **Non-sustained (NSVT)**: 3+ beats, <30 sec, hemodynamically stable

## 3. Clinical Features
- **Hemodynamically unstable**: Hypotension, altered mental status, chest pain, shock
- **Stable**: Palpitations, dyspnea, dizziness, lightheadedness
- **Cardiac arrest** if untreated

## 4. Management
### Unstable VT
- **Synchronized cardioversion** immediately
  - Start at **100 J biphasic** (escalate: 200 J, 300 J, 360 J)
  - Sedation if conscious (etomidate, midazolam)
- If cardioversion fails → antiarrhythmic + repeat
- **Pulseless VT** → defibrillation (unsynchronized) + CPR

### Stable VT
- **IV amiodarone** 150 mg over 10 min, then 1 mg/min × 6 h
- **IV procainamide** 10–15 mg/kg at 20–50 mg/min (preferred for stable VT with preserved EF)
- **IV lidocaine** 1.5 mg/kg (alternative)
- **IV sotalol** 1.5 mg/kg
- If drug-refractory → synchronized cardioversion

## 5. Long-Term Management
- **Underlying cause**: Ischemia (revascularize), electrolyte correction, drug withdrawal
- **ICD** for secondary prevention (VT/VF arrest survivors with EF ≤35%)
- **Antiarrhythmic therapy**: Amiodarone, sotalol (with ICD as backup)
- **Catheter ablation** for recurrent VT, electrical storm
- **GDMT for HF** if structural heart disease

## 6. Special Situations
- **Electrical storm**: ≥3 VT episodes in 24 h — IV amiodarone, beta-blockade, sedation, ablation
- **Idiopathic VT** (RVOT, LV fascicular): Catheter ablation curative
- **Bundle branch reentry**: After His ablation + ICD

> **Pearls**
> - **Unstable → cardioversion**; **Stable → IV antiarrhythmic first**
> - **Synchronized** shock for VT with pulse; **Unsynchronized** for VF/pulseless VT
> - Avoid procainamide in HF (long QT, negative inotrope)
> - **Amiodarone** is safe in structural heart disease (vs. many other AADs)
> - All post-VT arrest patients need **EF assessment, coronary angio, EP referral**
