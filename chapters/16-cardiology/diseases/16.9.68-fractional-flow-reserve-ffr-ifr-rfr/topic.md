## 1. Definitions
- **FFR (Fractional Flow Reserve):** ratio of distal coronary pressure (Pd) to aortic pressure (Pa) during **maximal hyperaemia** (adenosine)
- **iFR (instantaneous wave-Free Ratio):** Pd/Pa ratio during the **diastolic wave-free period** (no hyperaemia needed)
- **RFR (Resting Full-cycle Ratio):** lowest Pd/Pa across the cardiac cycle

### Cut-offs (intermediate 50–90% lesions)
| Index | Hyperaemia | Positive Cut-off |
|---|---|---|
| **FFR** | Required (adenosine) | **≤ 0.80** |
| **iFR** | Not required | **≤ 0.89** |
| **RFR** | Not required | **≤ 0.89** |

---

## 2. FAME Trials
- **FAME 1 (2009):** FFR-guided PCI vs angio-guided → ↓ MACE, ↓ stent use
- **FAME 2 (2012):** FFR-positive lesions → PCI + OMT vs OMT alone → PCI better (driven by urgent revasc)
- **iFR-SWEDEHEART, DEFINE-FLAIR:** iFR non-inferior to FFR

---

## 3. FFR-CT
**Non-invasive** CT-derived FFR using computational fluid dynamics. Avoids invasive cath for intermediate lesions. **PLATFORM, ADVANCE** trials.

---

## 4. When to Use
- **Intermediate 50–90% lesions** on angiography
- **Multivessel disease** — guides which lesions to treat
- **LM disease** — FFR < 0.80 favours revascularisation
- **Sequential lesions** — beware interaction; use iFR-FFR pullback

### Limitations
- Small vessels (<2 mm), diffuse disease, prior MI (microvascular dysfunction)
- **Microvascular angina:** FFR >0.80, CFR < 2.0, IMR >25

---

## 5. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **FFR ≤ 0.80** (with adenosine) — physiologically significant
- **iFR / RFR ≤ 0.89** (no adenosine needed) — non-inferior to FFR
- FFR-guided PCI ↓ MACE and stent use (FAME 1)
- FFR-CT is non-invasive alternative for intermediate lesions
- Avoid in small vessels, diffuse disease, prior MI
