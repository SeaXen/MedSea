CARDIOLOGY
Risk stratification (REVEAL 2.0, ESC/ERS risk table)
1 Why Risk Stratify

Risk assessment at baseline and follow-up guides treatment intensity, drug combinations, and transplant referral in PAH.

2 REVEAL 2.0 (12-variable)

Includes: WHO functional class, vital signs (SBP, HR), 6-min walk distance, BNP/NT-proBNP, echocardiographic signs of RV function, PVR, RAP, DLCO, eGFR, year of diagnosis, demographics.

  • Low risk (≤6): 5-yr survival >90%
  • Intermediate (7–8): ~70%
  • High (≥9): <50%
3 ESC/ERS 3-Strata Risk Table (2022)

Evaluates multiple parameters: WHO FC I–III, 6MWD >440 m, CPET (VO₂ peak >15 mL/kg/min, VE/VCO₂ slope <36), BNP <50 / NT-proBNP <300, RAP <8 mmHg, CI ≥2.5 L/min/m², SvO₂ >65%.

  • Low risk: All criteria low — annual follow-up
  • Intermediate risk: Mixed — closer follow-up, escalate therapy
  • High risk: ≥1 high criterion — aggressive therapy, transplant evaluation
4 Follow-up Risk Assessment
  • Repeat at 3–6 months after therapy change and at least yearly
  • Achieving/retaining low-risk status = treatment goal
  • Failure to reach low risk → escalate (oral → inhaled → parenteral prostacyclin, add 2nd/3rd agent)
5 Clinical Application
  • Low risk → monotherapy or oral dual therapy
  • Intermediate → upfront oral dual (ERA + PDE5i)
  • High → parenteral prostacyclin (IV/SC) ± lung transplant
Key Points - Goal of therapy: low-risk profile. - REVEAL 2.0 is more granular; ESC/ERS 3-strata is guideline-endorsed. - Reassess risk at every visit — non-improvement is a trigger to escalate.
