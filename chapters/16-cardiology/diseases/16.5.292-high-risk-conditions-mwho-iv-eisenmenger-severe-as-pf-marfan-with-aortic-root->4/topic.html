CARDIOLOGY
High-risk conditions (mWHO IV - Eisenmenger, severe AS, PF, Marfan with aortic root >45mm)
1 mWHO Risk Classification

The modified WHO (mWHO) classification stratifies pregnancy risk. mWHO IV contraindicates pregnancy or mandates termination counseling due to extremely high maternal mortality (25–50%):

  • mWHO I: no detectable increased risk
  • mWHO II: small increased risk
  • mWHO III: significantly increased risk
  • mWHO IV: pregnancy contraindicated
2 The Four "Killer" mWHO IV Conditions
  • Eisenmenger syndrome: Cyanotic shunt reversal; vasodilation + ↑ coagulability → thrombosis, paradoxical emboli, sudden death (mortality 30–50%)
  • Severe aortic stenosis (AS): Fixed output cannot match pregnancy's ↑ CO demand → pulmonary edema, shock
  • Pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH): Right ventricle fails as PVR cannot fall; peripartum mortality 30–56%
  • Marfan syndrome with aortic root >45 mm: Risk of aortic dissection (10%) and rupture; pregnancy ↑ wall stress
3 Pre-Conception Counseling
  • All four require specialist MDT (cardiology, obstetrics, anesthesia, neonatology)
  • Aortic root replacement before pregnancy if Marfan and diameter approaches 45 mm
  • Discuss contraception (avoid estrogen-containing if hypercoagulable)
  • Offer pregnancy termination as safe option; respect autonomy
4 Management if Pregnancy Continues
  • Admit for bedrest in 2nd–3rd trimester; consider hospitalization from 20–24 weeks
  • Targeted therapy: PAH — sildenafil, bosentan (teratogenic — switch), prostacyclins
  • Anticoagulation: LMWH for PAH, mechanical valves, prior VTE
  • Mode of delivery: Planned cesarean under regional anesthesia (avoid hemodynamic stress of labor in AS/PAH)
  • Postpartum ICU monitoring for ≥48 h
Pearls - Mortality risk persists up to 6 months postpartum; do not relax surveillance early - mWHO IV = pregnancy is essentially a life-threatening undertaking - Aortic root >45 mm in Marfan = dissect, not just enlarge
