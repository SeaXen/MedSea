CARDIOLOGY
Non-ischemic dilated cardiomyopathy
1 Definition

LV dilation + systolic dysfunction (LVEF ≤40%) in the absence of CAD or abnormal loading conditions sufficient to cause the dysfunction. By definition, DCM is not due to ischaemia, valve disease, or hypertension.

2 Etiology
CategoryCauses
------
**Genetic****TTN truncations (25%)**, LMNA, PLN, RBM20, FLNC, DES, DMD
**Inflammatory**Viral (parvovirus B19, HHV6, Coxsackie), autoimmune, lymphocytic, giant cell, eosinophilic
**Toxic****Alcohol** (most common reversible), anthracyclines (doxorubicin, epirubicin), trastuzumab, cyclophosphamide, cocaine, methamphetamines
**Metabolic**Thiamine deficiency, hypocalcaemia, hypophosphataemia, thyrotoxicosis, phaeochromocytoma
**Tachycardia-mediated**Chronic AF/SVT with rapid VR, RV pacing
**Peripartum**Late pregnancy / 5 mo post-partum
**Infiltrative**Amyloid, sarcoid, haemochromatosis
**Endocrine**Cushing, acromegaly
~30–40% of "idiopathic" DCM are genetic (TTN truncations most common).
3 Pathophysiology

Insult (genetic, viral, toxic) → myocyte dysfunction/loss → neurohormonal activation (RAAS, SNS) → adverse remodelling → dilation, ↓contractility → progressive HF.

4 Clinical Features
  • Symptoms of HFrEF — dyspnoea, fatigue, oedema
  • Embolic events — LV thrombus → stroke (↑ risk in DCM)
  • Arrhythmia — AF, VT/VF, bradyarrhythmia
  • Family history of SCD or HF
  • Examination: displaced apex, S3, MR, JVP, oedema
5 Diagnosis
  • ECG — LBBB, LVH, QRS widening, AF
  • Echo — LV dilation (EDD >58 mm men, >52 mm women), LVEF ≤40%, normal wall thickness, no regional pattern
  • CMR — gold standard for tissue: LGE (mid-wall, non-ischaemic pattern — distinguishes from ischaemic), myocarditis (Lake Louise criteria), amyloid, sarcoid
  • Coronary angio or CTCA — to exclude CAD
  • Endomyocardial biopsy — giant cell, eosinophilic, lymphocytic
  • Genetic testing — TTN, LMNA (high-risk SCD)
  • Viral serology / PCR — if active myocarditis suspected
  • Toxin screen — alcohol, drugs, chemotherapy
  • Family screening — 1st-degree relatives, ECG + echo
6 Management
  • 4 pillars of GDMT (ARNI, BB, MRA, SGLT2i) — up-titrate to target
  • Treat the cause:
  • Alcoholic — abstinence → recovery in 6 mo (LVEF improves ~10–15%)
  • Tachycardia-mediated — rate control / ablation → recovery
  • Peripartum — bromocriptine + GDMT
  • Thyrotoxicosis, thiamine — replace, recover
  • Anthracycline — stop if possible, dexrazoxane cardioprotection
  • ICD if LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II–III, GDMT ≥3 mo
  • CRT if LBBB + QRS ≥150 ms
  • LVAD / transplant for advanced disease
  • Avoid — alcohol, cocaine, anthracyclines
7 Family Screening
  • 1st-degree relatives — ECG + echo at presentation, then every 1–5 y
  • Genetic testing if pathogenic variant identified in proband
  • LMNA and FLNC — high-risk SCD regardless of EF (consider ICD at LVEF <45%)
Pearls - TTN truncations are the most common genetic cause (~25%) - Alcoholic CM is reversible with abstinence (6-month rule) - CMR distinguishes ischaemic (subendocardial LGE) from non-ischaemic (mid-wall LGE) - LMNA/FLNC = high SCD risk even at moderate EF — early ICD - Peripartum CM = late pregnancy to 5 mo post-partum - LVEF recovery possible in 30–40% of DCM (HFimpEF) — continue GDMT - Bromocriptine in peripartum CM

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg"> CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE — Non-ischaemic DCM is a heterogeneous group. CMR is the key — distinguishes ischaemic from non-ischaemic, identifies infiltrative and inflammatory causes. Treat the cause (alcohol abstinence, rate control) and the disease (GDMT + devices). LMNA and FLNC mutations warrant early ICD because of high SCD risk independent of EF. </callout>
