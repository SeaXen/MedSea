# Heart Transplantation

## 1. Definition
**Heart transplantation** remains the **gold-standard therapy** for end-stage refractory HF. Limited by donor supply. Most patients receive a **bi-atrial (Lower-Shumway) or bicaval** technique; orthotopic.

## 2. Key Features
- **Indications**: end-stage HF (NYHA IIIB/IV, INTERMACS 1–3) despite optimal GDMT, devices, and rehab
- **Contraindications**: malignancy (recent), irreversible PVR (>5 Wood units), active infection, severe non-cardiac comorbidity, substance abuse, psychosocial non-adherence
- **Listing criteria**: VO2 max <14 (≤12 if on β-blocker), or <10 if HFrEF
- **Allocation**: urgency (status 1–6 in US; in UK — super-urgent, urgent, routine)
- **Immunosuppression** (triple therapy):
  - **Induction**: ATG, basiliximab, alemtuzumab
  - **Maintenance**: calcineurin inhibitor (tacrolimus, ciclosporin) + antimetabolite (MMF, azathioprine) + corticosteroid
  - **mTOR inhibitors** (everolimus, sirolimus) — for rejection, malignancy
- **Rejection**: hyperacute (rare), acute cellular (lymphocytic infiltrate), acute humoral (donor-specific antibodies), chronic (CAV)
- **Cardiac allograft vasculopathy (CAV)**: leading late complication; annual surveillance (angiography/IVUS)

## 3. Management
- **Pre-transplant** workup: bloods, infectious screen, malignancy screen, psychosocial
- **Post-op monitoring**: endomyocardial biopsy (scheduled: 1, 2, 3, 6, 12 months; then as needed)
- **Rejection treatment**: pulse steroids ± ATG for severe cellular; plasmapheresis, IVIG, rituximab for humoral
- **CAV prevention**: statins (always), mTOR inhibitors
- **Prophylaxis**: PCP, CMV, antifungal
- **Median survival**: ~12–14 years
- **Long-term complications**: CAV, malignancy (PTLD), renal failure, hypertension, diabetes

> **Pearls**
> - **Triple immunosuppression** = calcineurin + antimetabolite + steroid
> - **CAV** = leading cause of late death; annual angiogram
> - **Endomyocardial biopsy** for rejection surveillance
> - **Statins** for all transplant patients
