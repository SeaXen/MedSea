# ECG Axis Determination

**Topic:** ECG Axis Determination — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep

## 1. Introduction
The **cardiac axis** is the mean direction of ventricular depolarisation. It is one of the **5 core steps** of ECG reading and gives vital diagnostic clues.

## 2. Normal Axis
- **Mean QRS vector** projected onto the hexaxial reference system
- **Normal: −30° to +90°**
- Defined by net QRS deflection in leads I and aVF:
  - **Lead I +ve, aVF +ve** = normal axis
  - **Lead I +ve, aVF –ve** = LAD
  - **Lead I –ve, aVF +ve** = RAD
  - **Lead I –ve, aVF –ve** = extreme / north-west axis

## 3. Hexaxial Reference System
- 6 limb leads (I, II, III, aVR, aVL, aVF) at 30° increments
- **0°** = lead I (horizontal, leftward)
- **+90°** = lead aVF (downward)
- **+180° / −180°** = lead –I
- **−30°** = lead aVL
- **+150°** = lead III

## 4. Rapid Bedside Method
1. **Lead I** +ve? If yes → axis not extreme
2. **aVF** +ve? If yes → normal axis
3. If aVF –ve, look at **lead II**:
   - Lead II +ve → LAD (0° to −30°)
   - Lead II isoelectric → −30° (use aVL as the new axis)
   - Lead II –ve → extreme LAD
4. If aVF +ve but Lead I –ve → RAD (use lead III: +ve = 0°–+90°, –ve = +90°–+180°)

## 5. Causes of Axis Deviation

### Left Axis Deviation (LAD) —0° to −90°
- **LVH, LBBB, LAHB (left anterior fascicular block)**
- Inferior MI, AS
- Severe COPD (sometimes)
- WPW (right-sided pathway)
- Hyperkalaemia
- Normal in obese, pregnancy

### Right Axis Deviation (RAD) —+90° to +180°
- **RVH, RBBB, LPHB (left posterior fascicular block)**
- Lateral MI, posterior MI
- PE, COPD, cor pulmonale
- ASD, VSD
- WPW (left-sided pathway)
- Dextrocardia, lead reversal

### Extreme / North-West Axis (−90° to −180°)
- Lead I –ve, aVF –ve
- **Acute MI** (esp. anterior or with LAHB)
- **Hyperkalaemia** (sine wave, deep S in I)
- **Lead transposition** (limb lead reversal) — check for normal P wave morphology
- Severe pectus excavatum
- Artificial pacing
- Atrial situs inversus

## 6. Practical Pearls
- **P wave axis** is normally +30° to +70° (P upright in I, II, aVF)
- **T wave axis** is normally within 0°–+90°
- **Discordant T axis** to QRS is abnormal

## 7. Specific Patterns
- **LAHB:** LAD + qR in aVL, rS in II, III, aVF
- **LPHB:** RAD + rS in I, aVL, qR in II, III, aVF
- **Bifascicular block:** RBBB + LAHB (or LPHB) = risk of progression to complete heart block
- **Trifascicular block:** bifascicular + prolonged PR

> **Pearls**
> - **LAD + RBBB** suggests **broaden the differential** — think severe CAD, anterior MI, cardiomyopathy.
> - **North-west axis + hyperacute T waves** → **sine wave ECG = imminent VF** from severe hyperkalaemia.
> - If axis looks bizarre — **check lead placement** (limb lead reversal — e.g. RA/LA swapped) is a common cause of "extreme axis" in a normal patient.
