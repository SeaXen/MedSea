# Cardiac tamponade (emergency pericardiocentesis)

## 1. Definition
**Cardiac tamponade**: Compression of the heart by accumulated pericardial fluid (or blood/pus/air) under pressure, impairing diastolic filling and reducing cardiac output. A **clinical diagnosis** requiring urgent intervention.

## 2. Etiology
- **Malignancy** (lung, breast, lymphoma, melanoma)
- **Infectious**: TB, viral, bacterial, HIV
- **Post-MI**: Free wall rupture, Dressler syndrome
- **Uremia**
- **Aortic dissection** (rupture into pericardium)
- **Iatrogenic**: Post-catheterization, post-cardiac surgery
- **Trauma** (penetrating/blunt)
- **Autoimmune**: SLE, RA
- **Hypothyroidism** (myxedema)
- **Radiation**

## 3. Clinical Features
### Beck's Triad
1. **Hypotension**
2. **Muffled heart sounds**
3. **Jugular venous distension**

### Other Signs
- **Pulsus paradoxus** (>10 mmHg drop in SBP on inspiration)
- **Tachycardia** (compensatory)
- **Hepatojugular reflux**
- **Electrical alternans** (ECG: alternating QRS amplitude)
- **Low voltage QRS** on ECG
- **Pericardial knock** absent (vs. constriction)
- **Cyanosis, dyspnea, shock**

## 4. Pathophysiology
- Pericardial fluid ↑ → intrapericardial pressure ↑ → compresses RA/RV
- ↓Diastolic filling → ↓Stroke volume → ↓Cardiac output
- **Equalization of pressures**: RA, RV diastolic, PCWP, pericardial

## 5. Diagnosis
- **Echocardiography** (gold standard): Echo-free space, RA/RV diastolic collapse, IVC plethora
- **CXR**: Enlarged "water-bottle" cardiac silhouette
- **ECG**: Low voltage, electrical alternans, sinus tachycardia
- **Doppler**: Respiratory variation in mitral/tricuspid inflow

## 6. Management
### Emergent Pericardiocentesis
- **Indication**: Hemodynamic compromise
- **Approach**: **Subxiphoid** (most common) or echo-guided apical/parasternal
- **Technique**: Under sterile conditions, needle angled toward left shoulder, 15–30° from skin
- **Drainage**: Remove enough to relieve tamponade
- **Echocardiographic guidance** preferred (reduces complications)
- **Pericardial catheter** for continuous drainage

### Pericardial Window (Surgical)
- Indicated for recurrent effusions, malignant effusions, traumatic hemopericardium
- Subxiphoid or VATS approach

### Other
- **IV fluids** to maintain preload (temporary)
- **Avoid**: Vasodilators, diuretics, positive pressure ventilation (worsen)
- **Treat underlying cause**

> **Pearls**
> - **Beck's triad** is often late — don't wait for all 3
> - **Pulsus paradoxus** is sensitive but not specific (also in severe asthma, PE)
> - **Muffled heart sounds** unreliable in noisy ED
> - **Echo** is the diagnostic test of choice
> - **Trauma tamponade** → emergent **pericardial window/thoracotomy** (not pericardiocentesis)
