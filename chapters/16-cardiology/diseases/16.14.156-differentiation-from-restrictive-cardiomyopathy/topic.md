# Pericardial Constriction vs Restrictive CM

## 1. Distinguishing Features
| Feature | Constriction | Restriction |
|---|---|---|
| **Septal bounce** | Present | Absent |
| **Resp variation in mitral inflow** | >25% (variation) | <15% |
| **Hepatic vein flow** | Inspiratory diastolic reversal | Systolic reversal |
| **Pericardial thickness** | >3–4 mm | Normal |
| **LV wall thickness** | Normal | ↑ (infiltrative) |
| **Atrial size** | Mildly enlarged | Markedly enlarged |
| **Septal/LGE on CMR** | Normal | Often abnormal (amyloid, sarcoid) |
| **BNP** | Normal/mildly ↑ | Markedly ↑ |
| **Inspiratory SBP drop** | Present (Kussmaul) | Variable |

## 2. Echo Markers of Constriction
- **Septal shift with respiration** (septal bounce)
- **Preserved tissue Doppler** (Ea >7 cm/s) — annulus paradoxus
- **Dilated IVC, hepatic vein diastolic reversal**
- **Respirophasic variation** in mitral (>25%), tricuspid (>40%) inflow

## 3. CMRI / CT
- Pericardial thickness (CT best)
- Pericardial LGE
- Septal bounce (CINE)

## 4. Cath
- **Dip-and-plateau** (square root) in RV/LV diastolic pressures
- **Equalization** of diastolic pressures (RA, RVEDP, PA diastolic, PCWP within 5 mmHg)
- **RVEDP > LVEDP** in inspiration (discordance)
- **Systolic area index** >1.1 (RV/LV)

> **Pearls** — **Preserved Ea on tissue Doppler** with thick pericardium = constriction (annulus paradoxus). BNP markedly ↑ favors restriction. **RVEDP/LVEDP discordance** in cath is diagnostic. Constriction is curable (pericardiectomy); restriction is usually managed medically.
