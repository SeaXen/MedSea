# Aortic Intramural Haematoma (IMH)

<callout icon="🩺" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Aortic Intramural Haematoma — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Variant of acute aortic syndrome** — haemorrhage within the **media without an intimal tear** (no false lumen). Caused by **rupture of vasa vasorum**.

### Distinguishing Features from Dissection
- **No** intimal flap, **no** false lumen
- **Crescentic wall thickening** on imaging
- May evolve into dissection (10–30%)

---

## 2. Classification (Same as Dissection)
- **Type A (Stanford A):** ascending aorta involvement → **surgery**
- **Type B (Stanford B):** descending only → medical management

---

## 3. Risk Factors
- Same as dissection: HTN (most common), age, atherosclerosis
- Anticoagulation, antiplatelet therapy (controversial)
- Connective tissue disease

---

## 4. Clinical Features
- Similar to dissection: **sudden severe chest / back pain**
- May be clinically indistinguishable
- D-dimer ↑ (less than dissection)
- Sometimes detected incidentally on CT

---

## 5. Imaging
- **CT / MRI:** crescentic aortic wall thickening (>5 mm), **no intimal flap**
- **TEE:** localised echolucent area in wall
- **MRI T1 fat-sat:** better for subacute / chronic
- Serial imaging for evolution (→ dissection, aneurysm, resolution)

---

## 6. Management
- **Type A IMH:** **surgical repair** (most centres) — same as Type A dissection
- **Type B IMH:** **medical therapy** (HR < 60, SBP 100–120) ± TEVAR if complicated
- Some centres observe small Type B IMH with close imaging

### Complicated Features (TEVAR)
- Persistent pain despite therapy
- Aortic expansion
- Pleural / pericardial effusion
- Malperfusion
- Rupture

---

## 7. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **IMH = no intimal flap, crescentic wall thickening** (vasa vasorum rupture)
- **Type A → surgery** (same as dissection)
- **Type B → medical** ± TEVAR if complicated
- May evolve to dissection (10–30%) — serial imaging essential
- D-dimer elevated (less than dissection)
