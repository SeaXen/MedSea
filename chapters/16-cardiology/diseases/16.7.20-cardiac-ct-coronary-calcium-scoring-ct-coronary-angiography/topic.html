CARDIOLOGY
Cardiac CT (coronary calcium scoring, CT coronary angiography)
1 Introduction

Cardiac CT is a non-invasive imaging test using ECG-gated multidetector CT (≥64-slice). Two main uses: Coronary Artery Calcium (CAC) scoring (non-contrast) and CT Coronary Angiography (CTCA) (contrast). Negative predictive value >99% for obstructive CAD.

2 Coronary Artery Calcium (CAC) Score
Agatston Score
  • Non-contrast ECG-gated CT, 3 mm slices
  • Scores calcified plaques: area × weighted density factor (130–400 HU)
  • Total = sum of LM + LAD + LCx + RCA
CACRiskManagement

**1–99**MildStatin if LDL above threshold; lifestyle
**100–299**Moderate**Statin** (Class I)

Use
  • Asymptomatic intermediate risk patients (MESA)
  • Reclassify risk; CAC = 0 → defer statin in selected patients
  • Not for symptomatic patients (use CTCA)
3 CT Coronary Angiography (CTCA)
Technique
  • 64-slice minimum; modern ≥128-slice / 320-row / dual-source
  • ECG-gated to diastole (HR ideally <65 bpm; give β-blocker)
  • Sublingual GTN for coronary dilatation
  • Iodinated contrast (≈80–100 mL); β-blocker to slow HR
Indications
  • Chest pain with low-intermediate pre-test probability of CAD
  • Equivocal stress test
  • Anomalous coronary artery evaluation
  • Pre-TAVR (annular sizing, valve morphology)
  • Cardiac mass / pericardial disease
  • Pulmonary vein anatomy pre-AF ablation
  • Triple rule-out (CAD, PE, AoD) in selected ED patients
Reporting — CAD-RADS
  • CAD-RADS 0 — no plaque
  • CAD-RADS 1 — minimal (<25% stenosis)
  • CAD-RADS 2 — mild (25–49%)
  • CAD-RADS 3 — moderate (50–69%)
  • CAD-RADS 4 — severe (≥70% / LM ≥50%)
  • CAD-RADS 5 — total occlusion
  • Modifier S (stent), G (graft), V (vulnerable plaque)
  • Modifier HRP = high-risk plaque (positive remodelling, low attenuation, napkin ring, spotty Ca)

5 Limitations
  • Severe calcification → blooming artefact (overestimates stenosis)
  • HR >75 bpm → motion artefact (despite modern scanners)
  • Obesity (BMI >40) → poor image quality
  • Atrial fibrillation → gating difficult
  • Renal impairment — contrast caution
  • Radiation (1–5 mSv modern scanners; 1 mSv with new protocols)
6 Per CTCA, FFR-CT (FFR derived from CT)
  • Computational fluid dynamics applied to CTCA dataset
  • FFR-CT ≤0.80 = haemodynamically significant
  • Avoids invasive FFR for intermediate lesions
  • Need good quality CTCA; HeartFlow = best validated
Pearls - CAC = 0 — very low risk; can defer statin in low-intermediate risk patients - CTCA NPV >99% — best for ruling out obstructive CAD - CAD-RADS is the universal reporting system (0–5) - High-risk plaque features: positive remodelling, low attenuation, napkin-ring sign, spotty Ca - Severe Ca → blooming artefact overestimates stenosis; consider calcium suppression algorithms - HR <65 bpm essential — give β-blocker pre-scan
