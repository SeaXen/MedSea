# HFpEF (Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction)

## Learning Objectives
- Understand the definition and diagnostic criteria of HFpEF
- Recognize the pathophysiology and phenotypes
- Apply evidence-based management strategies
- Differentiate from HFrEF and other causes of dyspnea

## Definition (ESC 2021)
Heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF): Symptoms ± signs of heart failure + LVEF ≥50% + objective evidence of cardiac dysfunction (elevated natriuretic peptides, diastolic dysfunction, left atrial enlargement, LV hypertrophy).

## Pathophysiology
- Stiff ventricle with impaired relaxation and filling
- Increased filling pressures at rest or with exertion
- Atrial dysfunction, pulmonary hypertension, right ventricular dysfunction
- Systemic inflammation, endothelial dysfunction, comorbidities (obesity, diabetes, hypertension, CKD)

## Common Phenotypes
- Hypertensive HFpEF
- Obese HFpEF
- Atrial fibrillation-related HFpEF
- Coronary artery disease-related HFpEF
- Valvular heart disease-related HFpEF

## Clinical Features
- Dyspnea on exertion, fatigue, orthopnea
- Signs of congestion (raised JVP, edema, crepitations)
- Atrial fibrillation (very common)
- History of hypertension, obesity, diabetes

## Diagnosis (HFA-PEFF Score or H2FPEF Score)
**H2FPEF Score** (0–9 points):
- Heavy (BMI >30) — 2 points
- Hypertensive (≥2 antihypertensives) — 1 point
- Atrial Fibrillation — 3 points
- Pulmonary hypertension (estimated PASP >35 mmHg) — 1 point
- Elderly (age >60) — 1 point
- Filling pressure (E/e' >9) — 1 point

Score ≥5 = High probability of HFpEF

## Management
**Non-pharmacological**:
- Weight loss, exercise, salt restriction, treat comorbidities

**Pharmacological**:
- SGLT2 inhibitors (Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin) — first disease-modifying therapy (EMPEROR-Preserved, DELIVER)
- Diuretics for congestion
- Blood pressure control (target <130/80)
- Rate/rhythm control for atrial fibrillation
- Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists (spironolactone) — modest benefit (TOPCAT)

**Emerging**:
- ARNI (sacubitril/valsartan) — some benefit in selected patients
- Vericiguat, Finerenone (under study)

## Prognosis
- Similar mortality to HFrEF in some studies
- High hospitalization rate
- SGLT2 inhibitors reduce HF hospitalization

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- HFpEF is a clinical diagnosis — LVEF alone is not enough
- SGLT2 inhibitors are the first therapy proven to reduce events in HFpEF
- Atrial fibrillation is both cause and consequence of HFpEF
- Comorbidity management is central (obesity, hypertension, diabetes)

## MCQs
1. First therapy proven to reduce HF hospitalization in HFpEF:
   a) Beta-blockers
   b) ACE inhibitors
   c) SGLT2 inhibitors
   d) Digoxin
   **Answer: c** — EMPEROR-Preserved and DELIVER trials showed benefit.

2. Which score is used to diagnose HFpEF?
   a) TIMI score
   b) H2FPEF score
   c) CHA2DS2-VASc score
   d) GRACE score
   **Answer: b**

3. HFpEF is characterized by:
   a) Reduced contractility
   b) Stiff ventricle with impaired relaxation
   c) Dilated ventricle
   d) Low filling pressures
   **Answer: b**

4. Common comorbidity strongly associated with HFpEF:
   a) COPD
   b) Obesity and atrial fibrillation
   c) Anemia
   d) Hypothyroidism
   **Answer: b**

5. SGLT2 inhibitors in HFpEF reduce:
   a) Only blood glucose
   b) Heart failure hospitalization
   c) Only mortality
   d) Only renal function
   **Answer: b**

6. Atrial fibrillation in HFpEF:
   a) Is protective
   b) Worsens symptoms and increases hospitalization
   c) Has no impact
   d) Is rare
   **Answer: b**

7. Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists in HFpEF (TOPCAT):
   a) Strongly reduce mortality
   b) Modest benefit, mainly in selected populations
   c) No benefit
   d) Increase mortality
   **Answer: b**

8. Non-pharmacological management of HFpEF includes:
   a) Strict bed rest
   b) Weight loss and exercise
   c) High salt diet
   d) Fluid restriction only
   **Answer: b**

9. Diastolic dysfunction on echo is:
   a) Not needed for HFpEF diagnosis
   b) Part of objective evidence for HFpEF
   c) Only seen in HFrEF
   d) Always normal in HFpEF
   **Answer: b**

10. Which drug class is first-line for BP control in HFpEF?
    a) Beta-blockers
    b) ACEI/ARB or CCB
    c) Alpha-blockers
    d) Central agents
    **Answer: b**

## SBA Questions
1. A 72-year-old obese woman with atrial fibrillation and dyspnea on exertion. Echo shows LVEF 55% and E/e' 14. Most likely diagnosis:
   a) HFrEF
   b) HFpEF
   c) COPD
   d) Pulmonary embolism
   **Answer: b**

2. A patient with HFpEF and resistant hypertension. Most effective add-on:
   a) Beta-blocker
   b) Spironolactone
   c) Alpha-blocker
   d) Hydralazine
   **Answer: b**
