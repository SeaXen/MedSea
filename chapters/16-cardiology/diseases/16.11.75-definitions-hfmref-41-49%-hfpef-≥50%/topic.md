# HFmrEF and HFpEF: Definitions

<callout icon="📏" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** HF with Mid-Range / Preserved EF — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. LVEF-Based Classification (ESC 2021)
| Category | LVEF | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **HFrEF** | **≤40%** | Reduced EF — clear benefit from GDMT |
| **HFmrEF** | **41–49%** | "Mid-range" — benefits from SGLT2i, diuretics |
| **HFpEF** | **≥50%** | "Preserved" — SGLT2i + diuretics + comorbidity management |
| **HFimpEF** | Previously ≤40% → now >40% | "Improved" — recovered EF on GDMT |

> **Key concept:** HF is a **clinical syndrome** — same diagnosis across the EF spectrum. EF-based categories predict response to therapies, not disease biology.

## 2. HFmrEF (41–49%)
- ~15–20% of HF population
- Sits on a **spectrum** between HFrEF and HFpEF
- Treatment evidence: **SGLT2i** (class I, EMPEROR-Preserved benefit preserved at lower EF)
- Beta-blocker, ACEi/ARB, MRA — modest benefit, no mortality signal in this range
- **Treat the cause** — many HFmrEF have recovered EF (HFimpEF) or evolving HFrEF

## 3. HFpEF (≥50%)
- **~50%** of all HF cases; the **dominant form** in elderly, women, hypertensive, obese
- **Pathophysiology:** diastolic dysfunction ± impaired systolic reserve, abnormal LV relaxation/stiffness, ↑filling pressures, atrial dysfunction, pulmonary HTN, RV dysfunction
- **Comorbidities drive the syndrome** — HTN, obesity, DM, AF, CKD, sleep apnoea, anaemia, iron deficiency
- **H2FPEF score** (0–9): Heavy (BMI >30), Hypertensive, F (atrial Fib), Pulmonary HTN, Elder (>60), Filling pressures — score ≥5 ⇒ HFpEF likely
- **HFA-PEFF algorithm** (ESC) — stepwise: pre-test → echocardiographic + natriuretic → functional testing → final aetiology

## 4. Diagnostic Red Flags for HFpEF
- **BNP / NT-proBNP** often modestly elevated (rule-out value intact)
- **Echo** — LVEF ≥50%, LVH (concentric), LA enlargement, E/e' >14, PASP >35, septal e' <7
- **Strain imaging** (GLS) — subclinical systolic dysfunction despite preserved EF
- **HFpEF can be difficult to diagnose** — exercise testing (CPET, stress echo) often needed
- **Invasive haemodynamics** — PCWP >15 mmHg at rest, >25 with exercise

## 5. Phenotypes of HFpEF
- **Obesity/metabolic** (most common in West)
- **Hypertensive / elderly** (concentric remodelling, ATTR amyloid common)
- **Pulmonary hypertension predominant**
- **Right-heart predominant**
- **Atrial functional** (AF-driven, especially in elderly)
- **Amyloid / infiltrative** (screen with bone scintigraphy + monoclonal screen)

> **Pearls**
> - **HFpEF = ~50% of HF**; obesity, HTN, women, elderly
> - **SGLT2i** are the **only** disease-modifying drugs for HFpEF (EMPEROR-Preserved, DELIVER)
> - **H2FPEF score** ≥5 = likely HFpEF
> - **Diagnose with HFA-PEFF** stepwise algorithm (ESC)
> - **GLS** on echo reveals subclinical dysfunction
> - **ATTR amyloid** — common cause in elderly with LVH; screen with DPD/PYP scan

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** HFpEF is increasingly common, often missed, and has no cure. **SGLT2i** (EMPEROR-Preserved, DELIVER) are the only disease-modifying therapy. **Treat the phenotype** — obesity, HTN, AF, iron deficiency, sleep apnoea. **ATTR amyloid** is a treatable mimicker — always consider in elderly LVH.
</callout>
