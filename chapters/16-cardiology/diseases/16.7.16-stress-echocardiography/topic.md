# Stress Echocardiography

<callout icon="🫀" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Stress Echocardiography — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Introduction
Stress echo combines **echocardiography with physiological (exercise) or pharmacological stress** to detect inducible ischaemia, viability, or valvular dysfunction. Uses **2D wall-motion analysis** (≥16-segment model). Sensitivity 80–85%, specificity 80–90% for CAD.

## 2. Indications
- **Suspected CAD** with intermediate pre-test probability and inconclusive exercise ECG
- **Risk stratification** post-MI (stress before discharge or 3–6 weeks)
- **Pre-op** assessment for non-cardiac surgery
- **Viability** assessment in low-EF / ischaemic cardiomyopathy
- **Valvular** — low-flow low-gradient AS, MR dynamic assessment
- **Unexplained dyspnoea**

## 3. Stress Modalities

| Modality | Agent | Mechanism | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Exercise** | Treadmill / bike | ↑HR, ↑contractility | Most physiological; preferred if able |
| **Dobutamine** | 5–40 µg/kg/min | β1 agonist | Viability (low dose), ischaemia (high dose) |
| **Dipyridamole** | 0.84 mg/kg | Adenosine reuptake blocker | Ischaemia, AF; avoid in asthma |
| **Adenosine** | 140 µg/kg/min | A2 agonist | Ischaemia; **first-line for CFR** |
| **Pacing** | Transvenous / TEE | ↑HR | If AV block / pace-maker dependent |

## 4. Protocol — Dobutamine Stress Echo (DSE)
- Start **5 µg/kg/min**, increase every 3 min to 10, 20, 30, 40 µg/kg/min
- **Target HR** 85% max predicted (220 − age)
- **Atropine** 0.5–1 mg if HR not achieved
- Stop: target HR, ischaemia (WMA, ST ↓≥1 mm), angina, SBP>220/<80, arrhythmia, symptomatic

## 5. Interpretation

### Ischaemia
- **New or worsening WMA** in ≥1 segment (hypokinesis → akinesis/dyskinesis)
- **Biphasic response** = ischaemia + viability (low-dose improvement, high-dose deterioration) → predicts recovery after revascularisation
- **Drop in EF** with stress → severe ischaemia

### Viability (low-dose DSE, 5–10 µg/kg/min)
- **Improved thickening in ≥4 of 16 segments** = viable myocardium
- Predicts functional recovery after CABG/PCI
- **≥60% segments viable** → consider revascularisation; **<20%** → medical therapy

### Valvular — Low-Flow Low-Gradient AS
- DSE differentiates **true severe AS** (AVA <1.0 cm², gradient ↑ with stress) from **pseudosevere** (AVA ↑ >1.0 cm² with stress)
- **Contractile reserve**: ↑SV ≥20% → good prognosis with AVR

## 6. Contraindications
- **Unstable angina / acute MI**, severe AS, HOCM, uncontrolled arrhythmia, severe HTN, recent PE
- **Dobutamine**: VT, severe LVOT obstruction, aortic dissection
- **Vasodilators**: asthma, severe hypotension, AV block (adenosine)

## 7. Limitations
- Image quality dependent; **contrast** (Definity, SonoVue) helps in 30%
- Operator-dependent interpretation
- Subendocardial ischaemia may be missed (use STE strain — see separate topic)

> **Pearls**
> - **Exercise > pharmacological** when possible (physiological, valves, no drug side-effects)
> - **DSE biphasic response** = viability + ischaemia → revascularisation
> - **Low-flow low-gradient AS + EF<50%**: DSE distinguishes true vs pseudo severe
> - **Stop DSE** at 85% max HR or for new WMA
