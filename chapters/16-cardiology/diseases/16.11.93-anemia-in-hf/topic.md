# Anemia in Heart Failure

<callout icon="🩸" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Anemia in HF — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**WHO anaemia**: Hb <13 g/dL (men), <12 g/dL (women). Common in HF, prevalence 30–50% (increases with HF severity).

## 2. Causes (Multifactorial)
- **Iron deficiency** — most common (see separate file)
- **Chronic kidney disease** — ↓ erythropoietin (EPO)
- **Chronic inflammation** — "anaemia of chronic disease" (IL-6, TNF-α, ↑ hepcidin)
- **GI blood loss** — anticoagulation, antiplatelets
- **Haemodilution** — pseudo-anaemia from volume overload
- **Bone marrow suppression** — drugs, uraemia
- **Haemolysis** — prosthetic valve, severe AS (MAHA)

## 3. Pathophysiology
- **Reduced oxygen delivery** to tissues → neurohormonal activation
- **RAAS/SNS** ↑ → progressive ventricular remodelling
- **Reduced myocardial oxygen** → impaired contractility
- **Cardio-renal-anaemia syndrome** — vicious cycle

## 4. Clinical Impact
- **Worse NYHA class, exercise capacity**
- **Increased hospitalisation, mortality**
- **Worse QoL, cognitive function**
- **Independent predictor** of adverse outcomes

## 5. Diagnosis
- **Full blood count** — Hb, MCV, MCH
- **Iron studies** — ferritin, TSAT (always)
- **Renal function** — eGFR, urea
- **B12, folate** — megaloblastic
- **Reticulocyte count** — marrow response
- **Haemolysis screen** — LDH, haptoglobin, bilirubin
- **Occult blood** — GI blood loss
- **Bone marrow** — rarely needed

## 6. Management
### Treat Underlying Cause
- **Iron deficiency** — IV iron (ferric carboxymaltose) — **Class I** in HFrEF
- **B12 / folate** — supplement
- **GI blood loss** — investigate (endoscopy, colonoscopy)
- **Renal anaemia** — see below

### ESA / EPO
- **Erythropoiesis-stimulating agents** (darbepoetin, epoetin) — improve Hb but trials (RED-HF) showed **increased thromboembolism and stroke**, no mortality benefit
- **Not routinely recommended** in HF
- Consider only in **CKD with severe anaemia**, under renal team guidance

### Transfusion
- **Restrictive transfusion strategy** — target Hb 7–8 g/dL
- **Packed RBC** only if haemodynamically unstable or severe symptomatic anaemia
- **Avoid in stable HF** — risk of volume overload

### HF Therapy Optimisation
- **GDMT** — improve renal perfusion, reduce inflammation
- **SGLT2i** — modest Hb rise (~0.5 g/dL) via increased EPO
- **Sacubitril/valsartan** — may improve anaemia
- **Iron repletion** even if "anaemia of chronic disease"

## 7. Special Considerations
- **Heart failure with preserved EF** — anaemia is common and worsens symptoms
- **Iron deficiency without anaemia** — treat with IV iron (FAIR-HF, CONFIRM-HF)
- **Pregnancy with HF** — physiological dilutional anaemia, treat ID aggressively

## 8. Prognosis
- **Anaemia is an independent predictor of mortality** in HF (HR ~1.5)
- **Worse outcomes** with each 1 g/dL Hb drop below 12
- **Reversal of anaemia** improves outcomes

> **Pearls**
> - **30–50% of HF patients** are anaemic
> - **Iron deficiency is the most common cause** — always check ferritin/TSAT
> - **IV iron (FCM)** is **Class I, Level A** — first-line treatment
> - **ESAs (EPO) are NOT recommended** — RED-HF trial showed harm
> - **SGLT2i** raise Hb by ~0.5 g/dL via increased EPO
> - **Transfusion** — restrictive strategy, target 7–8 g/dL
> - **Treat the cause** — iron, B12, folate, blood loss
> - **Cardio-renal-anaemia syndrome** — vicious cycle

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Anaemia in HF is **common, multifactorial, and independently prognostic**. **Always check iron status first** — IV iron (FCM) is the cornerstone of treatment. **Avoid ESAs** (RED-HF showed harm). Treat underlying causes (B12, blood loss). SGLT2i modestly raise Hb.
</callout>
