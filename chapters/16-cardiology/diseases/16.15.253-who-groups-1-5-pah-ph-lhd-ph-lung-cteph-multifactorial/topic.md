# WHO Groups 1-5: PAH, PH-LHD, PH-Lung, CTEPH, Multifactorial

## 1. Overview
Pulmonary hypertension (PH) is **mean pulmonary artery pressure (mPAP) >20 mmHg** at rest (previously ≥25). The **WHO/ESC 2022 classification** groups PH by underlying mechanism into 5 groups — essential for therapy.

## 2. WHO Group Classification

- **Group 1 — Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension (PAH):** Pre-capillary PH from pulmonary vasculopathy.
  - Idiopathic, heritable (BMPR2, ALK1), drug/toxin-induced
  - Associated: connective tissue disease (scleroderma), HIV, portal hypertension, congenital heart disease
- **Group 2 — PH due to Left Heart Disease (PH-LHD):** Most common cause (~65–80%). Post-capillary. LV systolic/diastolic dysfunction, valvular disease.
- **Group 3 — PH due to Lung Disease/Hypoxia:** COPD, ILD, OSA, chronic high altitude. Pre-capillary.
- **Group 4 — PH due to Pulmonary Artery Obstructions (CTEPH):** Chronic thromboembolic disease. Pre-capillary.
- **Group 5 — PH with Unclear/Multifactorial Mechanisms:** Sarcoidosis, hematologic disorders, metabolic disorders, renal failure.

## 3. Hemodynamic Definitions
- **Pre-capillary PH:** mPAP >20, PAWP ≤15, PVR >2 Wood units (Groups 1, 3, 4, 5)
- **Isolated post-capillary PH:** mPAP >20, PAWP >15, PVR ≤2 (Group 2)
- **Combined pre- and post-capillary PH:** mPAP >20, PAWP >15, PVR >2

## 4. Clinical Approach
- Suspect in unexplained dyspnoea, RV failure, loud P2
- Screen with echocardiography (TR velocity + other signs)
- Confirm with **right heart catheterization (RHC)** — gold standard
- Distinguish groups to direct therapy

> **Key Points**
> - Group 1 (PAH) is the only form with **PAH-specific therapies**.
> - Groups 2 and 3 dominate clinically — treat the underlying cause.
> - **CTEPH (Group 4)** must be excluded in all PH (V/Q scan).
