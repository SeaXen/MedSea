# Peripartum Cardiomyopathy (PPCM)

## 1. Definition
**PPCM** = idiopathic LV systolic dysfunction (LVEF <45%) presenting in **last month of pregnancy or within 5 months postpartum**, with no other identifiable cause. Distinct from pre-existing cardiomyopathy.

## 2. Key Features
- **Incidence**: 1 in 1000–4000 births; varies geographically (highest in Africa/Haiti)
- **Risk factors**: age >30, multiparity, African descent, pre-eclampsia, twin pregnancy, hypertension, family history
- **Pathogenesis**: 16-kDa prolactin fragment (vasculotoxic), sFlt-1 (anti-angiogenic), genetic predisposition
- **Presentation**: dyspnoea, orthopnoea, fatigue, leg swelling, cough, palpitations — often misattributed to pregnancy
- **Diagnosis**: echo with **LVEF <45%**, LV dilation; exclude other causes
- **NT-proBNP**: markedly elevated
- **Differential**: pre-existing DCM, hypertensive heart disease, PE, pulmonary HTN
- **Recovery**: ~50–70% recover (normal LVEF) by 6 months; worse prognosis if LVEDD >6.0 cm, LVEF <30%, RV involvement

## 3. Management
- **GDMT for HFrEF**: β-blocker (avoid during pregnancy if possible — carvedilol/metoprolol safe), ACEi (POSTPARTUM only — teratogenic), MRA (spironolactone safe postpartum)
- **Diuretics** for congestion
- **Bromocriptine**: 2.5 mg bid for 2+ weeks (inhibits prolactin) — per ESC guidelines, especially in severe cases
- **Anticoagulation**: if LVEF <35% (LMWH during pregnancy, warfarin postpartum)
- **Avoid**: ACEi/ARB/neprilysin inhibitors **during pregnancy** (teratogenic)
- **Subsequent pregnancy**: if LVEF recovered, monitor closely; if not recovered, **avoid pregnancy**
- **Cardiac transplant**: in non-recovery
- **Supportive care** multidisciplinary

> **Pearls**
> - **PPCM = last month of pregnancy or 5 months postpartum**
> - **Bromocriptine** + GDMT; consider in severe cases
> - **Avoid ACEi/ARB in pregnancy** (teratogenic)
> - **Subsequent pregnancy** high risk; counsel carefully
