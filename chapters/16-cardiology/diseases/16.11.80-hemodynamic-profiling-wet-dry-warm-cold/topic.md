# Hemodynamic Profiling in ADHF (Warm/Cold, Wet/Dry)

<callout icon="🌡️" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Hemodynamic Profiles in HF — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Why Profile?
Stevenson's bedside **2×2 haemodynamic matrix** guides initial therapy in minutes. Avoids blind diuresis in low-output patients.

| | **Wet (congested)** | **Dry (euvolaemic)** |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm (perfused)** | **Warm & Wet** — most common (~70%) | **Warm & Dry** — compensated |
| **Cold (hypoperfused)** | **Cold & Wet** — worst prognosis | **Cold & Dry** — uncommon |

## 2. Bedside Assessment

### Perfusion ("Warm or Cold")
| Warm | Cold |
|---|---|
| Warm peripheries | Cool / mottled peripheries |
| Normal pulse pressure | **Narrow pulse pressure** (<25%) |
| Normal mentation | Confused, drowsy |
| Normal urine output | Oliguria |
| No ACEi/BB hypotension | Symptomatic hypotension |

### Congestion ("Wet or Dry")
| Wet | Dry |
|---|---|
| ↑JVP, orthopnoea, bendopnoea | No orthopnoea, flat JVP |
| Peripheral / sacral oedema | No oedema |
| Hepatomegaly, ascites | No congestion |
| Crackles, pleural effusion | Clear lung bases |
| **S3 gallop** | No S3 |

## 3. Profile-Based Therapy

### Warm & Wet (most common)
- **IV loop diuretic** (furosemide 20–40 mg IV stat, repeat or infusion)
- Vasodilator if hypertensive (GTN)
- Continue oral GDMT (BB, ACEi/ARB if BP allows)

### Cold & Wet (cardiogenic shock spectrum)
- **Inotrope** — dobutamine (β1), milrinone (PDE3), levosimendan (calcium sensitiser)
- **Vasopressor** — noradrenaline (if SBP <80)
- **Diuretic** — to decongest (use with inotrope)
- **Mechanical support** — IABP, Impella, VA-ECMO if refractory
- **Right heart catheter** to titrate

### Cold & Dry
- **Cautious fluid challenge** (250 mL NS over 15 min)
- If no response → inotrope
- Often early decompensation of advanced HFrEF

### Warm & Dry
- Optimise oral therapy
- Discharge planning

## 4. Invasive Confirmation (Swan-Ganz)
| Profile | CI (L/min/m²) | PCWP (mmHg) |
|---|---|---|
| **Normal** | 2.5–4.0 | 6–12 |
| **Warm & Dry** | ≥2.5 | ≤15 |
| **Warm & Wet** | ≥2.5 | >18 |
| **Cold & Dry** | <2.5 | ≤15 |
| **Cold & Wet** | <2.5 | >18 |

> **Cardiac power output** = (MAP × CO)/451 — most powerful predictor of mortality in cardiogenic shock. CPO <0.6 W ⇒ very high mortality.

## 5. Monitoring
- Strict fluid balance, daily weight
- U&E, K⁺, creatinine on diuretics
- BP, HR, SpO₂, urine output
- BNP/NT-proBNP for response
- Reassess profile **at least twice daily**

> **Pearls**
> - **Warm & Wet = diuretic ± vasodilator**
> - **Cold & Wet = inotrope + diuretic ± MCS**
> - **Cold & Dry = fluid challenge then inotrope**
> - **Narrow pulse pressure** = cold (low stroke volume)
> - **Bendopnoea** (dyspnoea bending forward) — earliest sign of congestion
> - **S3 gallop** is the most specific sign of elevated LV filling pressure

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Stevenson's 2×2 matrix is the **bedside shortcut** to ADHF therapy. Most admissions are **Warm & Wet** → IV diuretics. **Cold & Wet** requires inotropes and consideration of mechanical support. RHC may be needed in complex cases (ESC IIa).
</callout>
