# Drug Effects on ECG

<callout icon="📈" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Drug Effects on ECG — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Introduction
Many cardioactive and non-cardiac drugs affect the ECG. Recognising iatrogenic patterns avoids misdiagnosis and prevents arrhythmias (e.g. torsades, digoxin toxicity).

## 2. Digoxin
- **"Scooped" ST depression** — hockey-stick or Salvador Dalí sign
- Shortened QT
- Flattened/inverted T
- Prolonged PR (1° AV block)
- **Toxicity:** bradycardia, AF with slow ventricular response, AT with block, bi-directional VT, regularised AF
- **Predisposing factors:** hypokalaemia, hypomagnesaemia, hypercalcaemia, renal failure, hypothyroidism, drug interactions (amiodarone, verapamil, diltiazem, quinidine)
- **Treatment of toxicity:** stop digoxin, correct K+, **digoxin-specific Fab (Digibind)** for severe

## 3. Class I Anti-Arrhythmics (Sodium Channel Blockers)

| Subclass | Drugs | ECG | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **IA** | Quinidine, procainamide, disopyramide | ↑PR, ↑QRS, ↑QT, **Torsades risk** | AF, VT |
| **IB** | Lignocaine, mexiletine | Shorten QT, mild QRS widening | VT |
| **IC** | Flecainide, propafenone | ↑PR, ↑QRS, no QT change | AF, SVT; **CAST trial** showed ↑ mortality post-MI |

## 4. Class III (K+ Channel Blockers)
- **Amiodarone:** ↑PR, ↑QRS, **↑QT** (and T inversion, U waves, bradycardia), sinus node suppression, thyroid effects
- **Sotalol:** ↑QT, bradycardia, Torsades risk (especially ♀, hypokalaemia, LV dysfunction)
- **Dofetilide, ibutilide:** ↑QT, Torsades risk

## 5. Beta-Blockers
- **Sinus bradycardia**, 1° AV block
- PR prolongation
- QRS not directly affected
- Treatment: stop drug, atropine, glucagon (overdose)

## 6. Calcium Channel Blockers
- **Verapamil, diltiazem:** bradycardia, AV block, negative inotropy
- **Dihydropyridines (nifedipine, amlodipine):** reflex tachycardia, flushing, oedema

## 7. QT-Prolonging Drugs (Torsades Risk)
- Antiarrhythmics: **sotalol, amiodarone, quinidine, disopyramide**
- Antibiotics: **macrolides (erythromycin, clarithromycin, azithromycin), fluoroquinolones, pentamidine**
- Antipsychotics: **haloperidol, thioridazine, pimozide, ziprasidone**
- Antidepressants: **TCAs (amitriptyline), citalopram (dose-dependent)**
- Antihistamines: **terfenadine, astemizole** (withdrawn)
- Antimalarials: **chloroquine, quinine**
- Methadone, ondansetron (high dose)

**Monitoring:** QTc, withdraw if >500 ms (or >530 ms with risk factors); correct K+ and Mg²⁺.

## 8. Other Cardiac Drugs
- **Adenosine:** transient AV block, asystole (seconds), flushing, chest discomfort — diagnostic and therapeutic
- **Ivabradine:** sinus bradycardia (no AV effect)
- **Nitrates:** reflex tachycardia, hypotension

## 9. Non-Cardiac Drugs Affecting ECG
- **Tricyclic antidepressants:** sinus tachycardia, ↑PR, ↑QRS, ↑QT, R in aVR >3 mm
- **Cocaine:** STEMI mimics, prolonged QT, vasospasm
- **Lithium:** T wave flattening, bradycardia, conduction abnormalities
- **Antipsychotics:** prolonged QT, Torsades
- **5-HT3 antagonists** (ondansetron): dose-dependent QT prolongation
- **Anticholinergics:** tachycardia
- **Stimulants (amphetamines, methylphenidate):** tachycardia, ischaemia

> **Pearls**
> - **"Scooped ST" + bradycardia + AF with slow VR** = think **digoxin toxicity**.
> - **Torsades** = polymorphic VT with **prolonged QT** → IV **magnesium 2 g**, overdrive pacing, correct K+/Mg²⁺.
> - **QRS >100 ms after flecainide** = reduce dose; **avoid in structural heart disease** (CAST).
> - **aVR R wave >3 mm** + tachycardia + post-ictal = **TCA overdose** (treat with NaHCO3).
