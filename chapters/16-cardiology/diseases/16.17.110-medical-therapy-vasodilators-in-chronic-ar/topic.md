# Medical therapy vasodilators in chronic AR

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 05_Valvular_Heart_Disease | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**Medical therapy vasodilators in chronic AR** (Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension, PAH, Group 1 PH): mean pulmonary artery pressure (mPAP) >20 mmHg at rest, pulmonary artery wedge pressure (PAWP) ≤15 mmHg, and pulmonary vascular resistance (PVR) >2 Wood units (WU) in the absence of other causes. **Classification** (ESC 2022, 6 groups): (1) **PAH** (idiopathic — IPAH, heritable — HPAH, drug-induced, associated with connective tissue, HIV, portal HTN, congenital, schistosomiasis), (2) **PH due to left heart disease**, (3) **PH due to lung disease/hypoxia**, (4) **PH due to pulmonary artery obstruction** (CTEPH, PE), (5) **PH with unclear/multifactorial mechanisms**, (6) **PH with multiple mechanisms** (rare). **Prevalence**: 15-50/million adult (PAH), more in connective tissue (10-15% of SSc), HIV (0.5%), portal HTN (2-6%).


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Mechanisms**: (1) **Vasoconstriction** (↓NO, ↓prostacyclin, ↑endothelin, ↑thromboxane, ↑serotonin), (2) **Vascular remodelling** (smooth muscle hypertrophy, intimal proliferation, fibrosis, plexiform lesions), (3) **Inflammation** (cytokines, immune cells, autoantibody), (4) **Thrombosis** in situ. **Result**: ↑PVR → ↑RV afterload → RV hypertrophy (adaptive) → RV dilation/failure (maladaptive, ↓CO). **Right HF, low output, death**. **Genetics**: **BMPR2** (idiopathic 70-80%, heritable — autosomal dominant, low penetrance 20%), **ACVRL1** (HHT), **ENG** (HHT), **SMAD9, CAV1, KCNK3**. **Drugs**: anorexigens (fenfluramine, benfluorex), dasatinib, amphetamines, methamphetamines, cocaine.


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

**Symptoms**: dyspnoea (exertional, then rest), fatigue, exercise intolerance, syncope (exertional, severe), chest pain (RV ischaemia), palpitations, oedema, abdominal distension. **Examination**: **loud P2**, **right ventricular heave (lift)**, **TR murmur** (left lower sternal border, ↑inspiration, Carvallo's sign), **JVP elevation** (a wave, v wave), **hepatomegaly**, **peripheral oedema**, **ascites**, **pericardial effusion** (severe), **cyanosis** (late). **Signs of underlying**: sclerodactyly, telangiectasia (SSc, HHT), digital clubbing, Raynaud's. **Functional class** (WHO/NYHA) I-IV.


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

**Screening** (high-risk — SSc, family Hx, HIV, portal HTN, drug Hx): annual echo, BNP. **Echo** (initial): PASP (from TR velocity + RAP), RV size and function (TAPSE <1.5cm, S' <9.5cm/s, RVFAC <35%), RA size, septal flattening, pericardial effusion, LV eccentricity. **Confirm**: **RHC** (gold standard, **must be done**) — mPAP >20 mmHg, PAWP ≤15 mmHg, PVR >2 WU, cardiac output. **Vasoreactivity testing** (only in IPAH, HPAH, drug-induced): inhaled NO, IV epoprostenol, or adenosine — positive if mPAP decreases ≥10 mmHg to ≤40 mmHg with maintained/increased CO. **Workup for aetiology**: ANA, anti-centromere, Scl-70, RF, HIV, hepatitis, LFT, abdominal ultrasound (portal HTn), V/Q scan (CTEPH), HRCT (interstitial lung disease), PFTs, polysomnography (OSA), PE workup, drug Hx. **CT** (HRCT for parenchymal, CTPA for thromboembolic, contrast for vasculature). **CMR** (RV function, PVR). **6MWD, CPET** (severity). **BNP/NT-proBNP** (severity, prognosis).


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**Risk stratification** (ESC 2022, 3 strata — low, intermediate, high based on 4 strata — COMPERA 2.0, REVEAL 2.0): **WHO FC I-II** (low), **III** (intermediate), **IV** (high). **6MWD** >440m (low), 165-440 (intermediate), <165 (high). **BNP** <50 (low), 50-300 (intermediate), >300 (high). **RAP** <8 mmHg (low), 8-14 (intermediate), >14 (high). **CI** ≥2.5 (low), 2.0-2.5 (intermediate), <2.0 (high). **Treatment goal**: low risk profile at follow-up. **Survival**: 1y 85%, 3y 67%, 5y 57% (with modern therapy). **IPAH median survival** now 7y (was 2.8y pre-modern therapy).


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**General**: exercise (supervised, rehabilitation), oxygen (if PaO2 <60), diuretics (oedema), anticoagulation (controversial — IPAH may benefit, NOT in connective tissue), digoxin (if AF), vaccinations (flu, pneumococcal), pregnancy avoidance. **Specific** (PAH-specific therapy, ESC 2022): **Vasoreactivity positive (IPAH, HPAH, drug)**: high-dose **CCB** (nifedipine, amlodipine, diltazem) — long-acting, sustained response in 50%. **Vasoreactivity negative** or non-responder: **Initial therapy** by risk (3-strata): **Low/intermediate risk** — **Oral combination** (ambrisentan + tadalafil, macitentan + tadalafil, selexipag + endothelin receptor antagonist or PDE5i). **High risk** — **Parenteral prostacyclin** (IV epoprostenol, SC treprostinil) ± oral combination. **Specific drugs**: (1) **Endothelin receptor antagonists (ERA)**: bosentan, ambrisentan, macitentan (macitentan 10mg OD, SERAPHIN trial). (2) **PDE5 inhibitors**: sildenafil, tadalafil. (3) **Soluble guanylate cyclase stimulator**: riociguat (PATENT-1/-2, REPLACE trial). (4) **Prostacyclin analogues**: epoprostenol (IV, short half-life), treprostinil (IV, SC, inhaled, PO), iloprost (inhaled), selexipag (PO, IP receptor agonist, GRIPHON). (5) **Activin signalling inhibitor**: **sotatercept** (activin receptor trap, add-on, ≥1 PAH therapy, STELLAR trial — improved 6MWD, PVR, time to clinical worsening). **Combination therapy**: AMBITION (initial ambrisentan + tadalafil better than monotherapy). **Sequential**: if not at goal, add second or third agent. **Balloon atrial septostomy, Pott shunt, lung transplant** for refractory. **Treatment response**: serial risk assessment, BNP, echo, 6MWD, CPET, RHC (intermediate re-evaluation).


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

**Right heart failure** (most common cause of death). **Arrhythmia** (AF, VT, SCD). **Thromboembolism** (in situ, PE). **Haemoptysis** (pulmonary artery rupture — rare, fatal). **Pregnancy** (high mortality, contraindicated). **Death** (right HF).


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

**Pregnancy**: contraindicated, mifepristone + curettage, or delivery if late. **CTEPH**: treat with anticoagulation 3-6m, then **riociguat** (CHEST-1/-2), or **balloon pulmonary angioplasty (BPA)** for inoperable. **Scleroderma PAH**: aggressive treatment, transplant evaluation. **HIV**: better prognosis with ART. **Portopulmonary**: treat portal HTN, PA-targeted therapy cautious. **Children**: similar approach, adult centres. **Elderly**: comorbidities, drug interactions.


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

**ESC/ERS 2022 PH (EHJ)**: comprehensive update, 6 groups, 3-strata risk. **2022 ACC/AHA**: similar. **NICE NG126 (2017, updated 2023)**: PH. **AMSTEL, AMBITION, SERAPHIN, PATENT, GRIPHON, REPLACE, STELLAR**: PAH trials. **PATENT-1, CHEST-1**: riociguat. **STELLAR (2023)**: sotatercept add-on. **Risks**: low/intermediate/high.


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

**Don't miss PAH** (progressive dyspnoea, normal exam, easy to attribute to deconditioning or anxiety). **Echo is screening, not diagnostic** (RHC required). **CTEPH** is curable, screen with V/Q. **Vasoreactivity** only in IPAH, HPAH, drug (not in connective tissue, HIV, etc.). **CCB** only for positive vasoreactivity. **Pregnancy contraindicated**. **Anorexigens** (fenfluramine, benfluorex) — REMOVED but historical exposure. **Bosentan hepatotoxic** (LFT monthly). **Sildenafil + nitrate** = severe hypotension. **Endothelin antagonists** teratogenic (Pregnancy X). **Right HF** is the main cause of death. **Risk assessment** at baseline and follow-up.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

**PAH Group 1** (5 categories): idiopathic, heritable (BMPR2), drug-induced, associated (CTD, HIV, portal, CHD, schistosomiasis), pulmonary veno-occlusive disease (PVOD), persistent pulmonary hypertension of the newborn. **mPAP >20, PAWP ≤15, PVR >2**. **Vasoreactivity** — IPAH, HPAH, drug only. **Therapy** — ERA, PDE5i, riociguat, prostacyclin (epoprostenol, treprostinil, iloprost, selexipag), **sotatercept** add-on. **Sotatercept** (STELLAR) — activin receptor trap. **Risk** — 3 strata (low/intermediate/high), 4 strata (low/intermediate-high/high), goal = low. **Symptoms**: dyspnoea, syncope, RV failure, oedema. **Loud P2, TR, RV heave, JVP**. **CTEPH** — V/Q scan, treat with anticoag + riociguat/BPA/surgery. **BMPR2** — heritable IPAH, 70-80%. **Anorexigens** — fenfluramine, benfluorex, dasatinib, amphetamines. **Pregnancy contraindicated**.


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

PH Group 2 (LHD), Group 3 (lung), Group 4 (CTEPH), Group 5 (unclear). **Pulmonary veno-occlusive disease (PVOD)**: rare, post-capillary, pulmonary oedema with PAH therapy. **Pulmonary capillary haemangiomatosis (PCH)**. **Sarcoidosis PH**. **Sickle cell disease PH**. **Chronic anaemia PH**.


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

Echo (screening), RHC (diagnostic, must), V/Q scan (CTEPH), HRCT (parenchymal), PFTs (lung disease, DLCO — ↓ in PVOD, SSc), DLCO, ANA, anti-centromere, Scl-70, RF, HIV, hepatitis, LFT, abdominal US, PNH workup, BNP/NT-proBNP, 6MWD, CPET, CMR (RV function), CTPA, genetic (BMPR2 if familial).

## 14. EXAM DRILLS

### 14.1 MCQs

**Q1.** What is the most common aetiology of Medical therapy vasodilators in chronic AR?
A. Genetic predisposition
B. Acquired disease
C. Mixed aetiology
D. Idiopathic
E. Familial

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Most cases are acquired with genetic/environmental interaction. Recognise risk factors, presentation, and management.

**Q2.** First-line investigation for Medical therapy vasodilators in chronic AR:
A. ECG
B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography
E. Cardiac biomarkers

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Echo is first-line for structural/functional assessment; additional tests as indicated.

**Q3.** Most appropriate first-line therapy for Medical therapy vasodilators in chronic AR:
A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy
D. Surgical intervention
E. Multidisciplinary care

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Guideline-directed medical therapy is cornerstone; other modalities as indicated.

**Q4.** Which is a contraindication to therapy in Medical therapy vasodilators in chronic AR?
A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

**Q5.** The most common complication of Medical therapy vasodilators in chronic AR is:
A. Heart failure
B. Arrhythmia
C. Thromboembolism
D. Death
E. Quality of life reduction

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.
