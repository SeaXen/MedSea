## 1. Definition
**Inflammation of the aortic wall** — may cause aneurysm, stenosis, dissection, or rupture. Classified by aetiology.

---

## 2. Large Vessel Vasculitis

### Giant Cell Arteritis (GCA)
- **> 50 y**, female > male
- **Headache, jaw claudication, scalp tenderness, vision loss** (giant cell involvement of temporal artery)
- **Polymyalgia rheumatica** (50%): shoulder / pelvic girdle pain
- ↑↑ ESR, ↑ CRP, anaemia of chronic disease
- **Treat:** high-dose prednisolone (40–60 mg); tocilizumab for relapse
- Aortic involvement (aneurysm, dissection) — late complication

### Takayasu Arteritis
- **Young female < 40 y**, Asian descent
- **"Pulseless disease"** — absent / weak upper limb pulses, BP discrepancy
- Systemic: fever, weight loss, malaise
- **Claudication**, carotidynia, syncope, renovascular HTN
- **Treatment:** high-dose steroids ± methotrexate / tocilizumab / anti-TNF
- Bypass / angioplasty for critical stenosis

---

## 3. Infectious Aortitis
- **Salmonella, Staph aureus, Strep, TB, syphilis**
- Pre-existing aneurysm / atherosclerosis risk factor
- Mycotic aneurysm formation
- **Treatment:** prolonged IV antibiotics + surgery

---

## 4. IgG4-Related Aortitis
- **Fibroinflammatory** with storiform fibrosis, obliterative phlebitis
- **↑ serum IgG4** (> 1.35 g/L)
- Often periaortitis / retroperitoneal fibrosis
- **Steroid-responsive**

---

## 5. Diagnosis
- **CTA / MRA:** wall thickening, aneurysm, stenosis
- **PET-CT:** active inflammation
- **ESR / CRP** elevated
- **Biopsy** (GCA: temporal artery)
- **IgG4** levels, ANA, ANCA, syphilis serology, blood cultures

---

## 6. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **GCA:** elderly, headache, jaw claudication, ↑ESR, **high-dose steroids**
- **Takayasu:** young Asian female, **"pulseless disease"**, BP discrepancy
- Infectious: **Salmonella** on aneurysm; IV antibiotics + surgery
- IgG4: ↑IgG4, **steroid-responsive**
- Image with CTA / MRA, PET-CT for activity
