# CRT Indications

## 1. Definition
**Cardiac resynchronisation therapy (CRT)** = biventricular pacing to correct dyssynchronous ventricular contraction in HF patients with conduction delay (wide QRS). Improves symptoms, LVEF, and **mortality** (CARE-HF, COMPANION).

## 2. Key Features
- **Indications** (ESC 2021):
  - **Sinus rhythm** (strongest benefit)
  - **LVEF ≤35%** despite ≥3 months GDMT
  - **LBBB with QRS ≥150 ms** — strongest benefit (Class I)
  - **LBBB with QRS 130–149 ms** (Class I)
  - **Non-LBBB with QRS ≥150 ms** (Class IIa — weaker benefit)
  - **NYHA II–IV** (ambulatory)
- **CRT-D** if LVEF ≤35% + ICD indication also met
- **CRT-P** if not (advanced age, frailty, comorbidity)
- **Predictors of response**:
  - **LBBB morphology** (best)
  - **QRS ≥150 ms** (better than 130–149)
  - **Female sex** (better)
  - **Non-ischaemic** aetiology
- **Non-responders** (~30%): suboptimal lead position, ischaemic scar, atrial fibrillation
- **AF patients**: AV node ablation often needed to ensure biventricular pacing

## 3. Management
- **Implant technique**: CS lead (LV lateral wall), RV apex, RA
- **Optimisation**: AV delay, VV delay
- **QRS narrowing** confirms effective resynchronisation
- **Follow-up**: ensure **>95% biventricular pacing** (treat AF, manage PVCs)
- **LV lead position**: lateral/posterolateral wall is optimal
- **Complications**: lead displacement, CS dissection, infection, phrenic nerve stimulation
- **His-bundle pacing** is emerging alternative (LBBAP)

> **Pearls**
> - **LBBB + QRS ≥150 ms + LVEF ≤35%** = best CRT candidate
> - **Female + non-ischaemic** = best responders
> - Ensure **>95% biventricular pacing** (may need AVN ablation in AF)
> - **RBBB** = poor CRT responder
