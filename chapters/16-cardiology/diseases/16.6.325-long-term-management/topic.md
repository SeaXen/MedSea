# Long-term management

## 1. Overview
Survivors of cardiac arrest require structured **post-cardiac arrest care** addressing neurologic recovery, cardiovascular optimization, and prevention of recurrence. Multidisciplinary follow-up is essential.

## 2. ICU Phase (First 72 h)
- **Targeted temperature management** (avoid fever >37.5°C × 72 h)
- **MAP target**: 65–100 mmHg (avoid hypotension)
- **Mechanical ventilation**: Lung-protective, normocapnia (PaCO₂ 35–45)
- **Glucose control**: 140–180 mg/dL
- **Seizure surveillance**: Continuous EEG, treat if present
- **Sedation**: Light, daily interruption
- **Cardiac monitoring**: 24–48 h minimum

## 3. Etiology Workup
- **ECG** (12-lead, look for ischemia, QT, Brugada)
- **Cardiac enzymes** (troponin — peak at 24–48 h)
- **Echocardiogram** (LV function, valvular, structural)
- **Coronary angiography** (if not done, or recurrent chest pain)
- **Electrophysiology study** (if no clear cause, esp. VF arrest)
- **CT/MRI brain** (if not done)
- **Tox screen**, electrolytes, thyroid, drug levels (digoxin)

## 4. Secondary Prevention
### If Ischemic Cause
- **Dual antiplatelet** (aspirin + P2Y12) for 12 months post-PCI
- **High-intensity statin**
- **ACEi/ARB**, beta-blocker if LV dysfunction
- **Risk factor modification**: BP, diabetes, smoking, LDL

### If Arrhythmic (No Reversible Cause)
- **ICD** for secondary prevention (EF ≤35% or VF/pVT arrest)
- **Antiarrhythmic** (amiodarone, sotalol) if recurrent
- **Catheter ablation** for VT (esp. if scar-related)
- **Avoid QT-prolonging drugs** if TdP

### Treat Underlying Cause
- **Electrolyte correction** (K⁺, Mg²⁺)
- **Treat sleep apnea** (CPAP)
- **Thyroid normalization**
- **Stop offending drugs** (QT-prolonging, sodium channel blockers)

## 5. Cardiac Rehabilitation
- **Phase I**: Inpatient mobilization
- **Phase II**: Supervised outpatient program (6–12 weeks)
- **Phase III**: Maintenance
- **Exercise prescription** based on stress test
- **Psychological support** (PTSD, anxiety, depression common)

## 6. Neurologic Recovery
- **Cognitive screening** (MoCA, MMSE)
- **PT/OT** for motor deficits
- **Speech therapy** if aphasia/dysphagia
- **Neuropsychology referral** (memory, executive function)

## 7. Family/Social Support
- **Bereavement support** if poor outcome
- **Genetic counseling** if heritable cause (LQTS, HCM, Brugada, ARVD)
- **Family screening** if inherited channelopathy
- **Driving restrictions** (varies by country/state)

## 8. Follow-up Schedule
- **2 weeks**: Wound check, basic evaluation
- **1 month**: ICD check, neurologic assessment
- **3 months**: Stress test, optimize GDMT
- **6 months**: Repeat echo, functional assessment
- **Annually**: ICD interrogation, echo, risk factor review

> **Pearls**
> - **PTSD occurs in 30%** of cardiac arrest survivors — screen and treat
> - **Driving restrictions**: typically 6 months after arrest (varies)
> - **Exercise testing** before return to vigorous activity
> - **Family screening** for inherited arrhythmia syndromes
> - ICD shock ≠ necessarily recurrent VT — always interrogate
