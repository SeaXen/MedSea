## 1. The Ischaemic Cascade
**Order of events** when coronary supply fails to meet demand: perfusion heterogeneity → regional wall motion abnormality → ECG changes (ST depression / T inversion) → angina. Silent ischaemia halts before pain.

### Determinants of Myocardial O₂ Demand
- **Heart rate** (HR)
- **Contractility**
- **Wall stress** (LV pressure × radius / 2 × wall thickness — Laplace)
- Preload, afterload, basal metabolic demand

### Determinants of Myocardial O₂ Supply
- **Coronary blood flow** (mainly diastolic in LV)
- **Coronary perfusion pressure** (aortic diastolic − LVEDP)
- **Coronary vascular resistance** — fixed by ≥70% stenosis
- **O₂-carrying capacity** (Hb, SaO₂)
- **Coronary vasodilator reserve** lost in microvascular disease

---

## 2. Fixed Supply Deficit (Atherosclerotic)
**Atheroma** narrows the epicardial artery (≥70% = haemodynamically significant). At rest, distal autoregulation maintains flow; on exertion, the **coronary flow reserve is exhausted** → subendocardial ischaemia. The ischaemic cascade then triggers symptoms.

### Pre-Test Probability Modifiers
- Age, sex, symptom type, risk factors (DM, dyslipidaemia, smoking, family history, CKD)

---

## 3. Demand-Side Ischaemia
Even with normal coronaries, **tachycardia + LVH + anaemia + thyrotoxicosis** push demand above fixed supply. Aortic stenosis & HCM produce similar mismatch.

---

## 4. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- Ischaemic cascade: **perfusion → wall motion → ECG → pain** (silent stops earlier)
- LV O₂ demand is driven by **HR, contractility, wall stress** (Laplace)
- Supply = **diastolic flow** (coronary perfusion pressure − LVEDP)
- ≥70% epicardial stenosis exhausts coronary flow reserve → exertional angina
- Demand-side: tachycardia, LVH, AS, anaemia, thyrotoxicosis
- INOCA = microvascular dysfunction → ↓CFR (<2.0), ↑IMR (>25)
