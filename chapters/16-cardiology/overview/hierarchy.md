## Hierarchy Structure

### 1. Cardiac Investigations (4 Heading Hubs, 16 Disease Topics)

#### 1.1 Clinical Assessment
- Cardiovascular history taking
- Cardiovascular examination (inspection, palpation, auscultation)
- Jugular venous pressure assessment
- Peripheral arterial pulse examination

#### 1.2 Electrocardiography (ECG)
- Normal ECG components and intervals
- ECG axis determination
- Chamber enlargement (LAE, RAE, LVH, RVH)
- Ischaemia, injury, infarction patterns
- Conduction abnormalities (AV blocks, bundle branch blocks, fascicular blocks)
- Arrhythmia diagnosis on ECG
- Electrolyte effects on ECG
- Drug effects on ECG
- Pacemaker ECG interpretation

#### 1.3 Echocardiography
- Transthoracic echo (TTE) views and measurements
- Transesophageal echo (TOE) indications
- Stress echocardiography
- Doppler echocardiography (valvular gradients, shunt quantification)
- Strain imaging and speckle tracking
- 3D echocardiography

#### 1.4 Advanced Cardiac Imaging
- Cardiac CT (coronary calcium scoring, CT coronary angiography)
- Cardiac MRI (viability, fibrosis, infiltrative diseases)
- Nuclear imaging (myocardial perfusion, MUGA, PET)
- Cardiac catheterization (right/left heart, coronary angiography)
- Intravascular imaging (IVUS, OCT)

### 2. Acute Coronary Syndromes (6 Heading Hubs, 22 Disease Topics)

#### 2.1 Pathophysiology & Risk Stratification
- Atherosclerosis pathogenesis
- Plaque rupture and thrombosis
- ACS classification (STEMI, NSTEMI, UA)
- Cardiac biomarkers (troponin, CK-MB, myoglobin)
- Risk scores (GRACE, TIMI, HEART)

#### 2.2 ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction (STEMI)
- STEMI ECG criteria (ST elevation, hyperacute T waves, reciprocal changes)
- STEMI mimics (LVH, LBBB, pericarditis, Brugada, early repolarization)
- Primary PCI protocol (door-to-balloon, stent choice, anticoagulation)
- Fibrinolysis (indications, contraindications, agents, rescue PCI)
- STEMI complications (mechanical, arrhythmic, HF, pericarditis)

#### 2.3 Non-ST Elevation ACS (NSTEMI/Unstable Angina)
- NSTEMI vs UA differentiation
- Early invasive vs conservative strategy
- Medical therapy (dual antiplatelet, anticoagulant, beta-blocker, ACEi, statin)
- Coronary angiography timing
- Revascularization (PCI vs CABG)

#### 2.4 ACS Complications
- Cardiogenic shock (IABP, Impella, VA-ECMO)
- Mechanical complications (VSR, papillary muscle rupture, free wall rupture)
- Arrhythmias (AF, VT/VF, heart block)
- Post-MI pericarditis (acute, Dressler's)
- Left ventricular aneurysm and thrombus

#### 2.5 Post-ACS Management
- Secondary prevention (Dual antiplatelet duration, statin, ACEi/ARB, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i)
- Cardiac rehabilitation
- Post-MI risk stratification (LVEF, NSVT, inducible VT)
- ICD indication post-MI

#### 2.6 Special ACS Populations
- ACS in elderly
- ACS in women
- ACS in CKD/dialysis
- Spontaneous coronary artery dissection (SCAD)
- Takotsubo cardiomyopathy
- Myocardial infarction with non-obstructive coronary arteries (MINOCA)

### 3. Chronic Coronary Syndromes (4 Heading Hubs, 16 Disease Topics)

#### 3.1 Stable Angina
- Pathophysiology (demand vs supply ischemia)
- Clinical presentation and Canadian Cardiovascular Society (CCS) classification
- Diagnostic testing (exercise ECG, stress echo, nuclear, CTCA, invasive angiography)
- Medical therapy (anti-anginal, anti-platelet, statin, ACEi)
- Revascularization criteria (PCI vs CABG, SYNTAX score)

#### 3.2 Coronary Artery Disease Variants
- Variant (Prinzmetal) angina
- Microvascular angina (CMD)
- Silent ischemia
- Coronary artery anomalies

#### 3.3 Post-Revascularization
- Post-PCI management (DAPT duration, stent thrombosis)
- Post-CABG management (graft surveillance, secondary prevention)
- In-stent restenosis
- Bioresorbable scaffolds

#### 3.4 Coronary Physiology & Imaging
- Fractional Flow Reserve (FFR), iFR, RFR
- Intravascular Ultrasound (IVUS), OCT
- Coronary flow reserve (CFR), index of microcirculatory resistance (IMR)

### 4. Heart Failure (7 Heading Hubs, 28 Disease Topics)

#### 4.1 Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF)
- Definition (LVEF ≤40%), epidemiology, pathophysiology
- GDMT quadruple therapy (ARNI/ACEi/ARB, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i)
- Device therapy (ICD, CRT)
- Advanced HF therapies (LVAD, transplant)

#### 4.2 Heart Failure with Mildly Reduced EF (HFmrEF) & Preserved EF (HFpEF)
- Definitions (HFmrEF 41-49%, HFpEF ≥50%)
- Diagnosis (HFA-PEFF, H2FPEF scores)
- Management (diuretics, SGLT2i, BP control, treat comorbidities)
- Common comorbidities (AF, obesity, CKD, anemia, iron deficiency)

#### 4.3 Acute Heart Failure
- Acute decompensated HF (ADHF) classification
- Hemodynamic profiling (wet/dry, warm/cold)
- IV diuretics, vasodilators, inotropes, vasopressors
- Mechanical support (IABP, Impella, VA-ECMO)

#### 4.4 Specific HF Etiologies
- Ischemic cardiomyopathy
- Non-ischemic dilated cardiomyopathy
- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy (HCM)
- Restrictive/infiltrative cardiomyopathies (amyloid, sarcoid, iron)
- Arrhythmogenic right ventricular cardiomyopathy (ARVC)
- Takotsubo cardiomyopathy
- Peripartum cardiomyopathy
- Myocarditis

#### 4.5 HF Comorbidities & Complications
- Cardiorenal syndrome
- Iron deficiency in HF
- Anemia in HF
- Atrial fibrillation in HF
- Sleep apnea in HF

#### 4.6 Advanced HF Therapies
- ICD indications (primary/secondary prevention)
- CRT indications (QRS duration, morphology, LVEF)
- LVAD (Bridge to transplant, destination therapy)
- Heart transplantation (listing, immunosuppression, rejection)
- Palliative care in advanced HF

#### 4.6 Acute Decompensation & CS
- Cardiogenic shock classification (SCAI stages)
- Temporary MCS (IABP, Impella, VA-ECMO)

### 5. Valvular Heart Disease (8 Heading Hubs, 32 Disease Topics)

#### 5.1 Aortic Stenosis (AS)
- Etiology (degenerative, bicuspid, rheumatic)
- Hemodynamic assessment (peak velocity, mean gradient, AVA, DVI)
- Symptomatic vs asymptomatic management
- TAVI vs SAVR (risk scores, heart team)
- Post-procedure complications (conduction disturbances, PPM, leak)

#### 5.2 Aortic Regurgitation (AR)
- Acute vs chronic AR
- Hemodynamic assessment (vena contracta, pressure half-time, regurgitant volume)
- Medical therapy (vasodilators in chronic AR)
- Surgical indications (LVEF, LV dimensions, symptoms)

#### 5.3 Mitral Stenosis (MS)
- Rheumatic vs non-rheumatic
- Hemodynamic assessment (MVA, mean gradient, PHT)
- Percutaneous mitral balloon valvuloplasty (PMBV) criteria
- Surgical options (commissurotomy, replacement)

#### 5.4 Mitral Regurgitation (MR)
- Primary (degenerative, rheumatic) vs secondary (functional)
- Hemodynamic assessment (VC width, EROA, regurgitant volume)
- P2 flail, chordal rupture, Barlow's
- Transcatheter edge-to-edge repair (TEER, MitraClip)
- Surgical repair vs replacement

#### 5.5 Tricuspid Valve Disease
- TR (primary, secondary/functional)
- Tricuspid stenosis (rare)
- Surgical/tricuspid intervention timing

#### 5.6 Pulmonary Valve Disease
- Pulmonary stenosis
- Pulmonary regurgitation (post-TOF repair, PAH)
- Transcatheter pulmonary valve replacement

#### 5.7 Multi-valve Disease & Combined Lesions
- Combined AS + MR
- Mixed valve disease management priorities

#### 5.8 Valve Prostheses & Anticoagulation
- Mechanical vs bioprosthetic valve choice
- Anticoagulation targets (INR, DOACs contraindicated in mechanical)
- Valve thrombosis (diagnosis, thrombolysis vs surgery)
- Prosthetic valve endocarditis

### 6. Infective Endocarditis (3 Heading Hubs, 12 Disease Topics)

#### 6.1 Diagnosis & Microbiology
- Modified Duke criteria (definite/possible/rejected)
- Blood culture techniques (timing, volume, bottles)
- Common organisms (Staph aureus, Viridans streptococci, Enterococcus, HACEK, fungi)
- Culture-negative endocarditis

#### 6.2 Complications
- Heart failure (valve destruction)
- Embolic phenomena (cerebral, splenic, renal, peripheral)
- Abscess formation (aortic root, mitral-aortic intervalvular fibrosa)
- Conduction abnormalities (AV block, abscess extension)
- Mycotic aneurysms

#### 6.3 Management
- Antibiotic regimens (empiric, directed, duration)
- Surgical indications (HF, uncontrolled infection, abscess, emboli, fungi)
- Timing of surgery (urgent, elective)
- Prophylaxis guidelines (dental, procedures)

### 7. Pericardial Diseases (4 Heading Hubs, 14 Disease Topics)

#### 7.1 Acute Pericarditis
- Etiology (viral, idiopathic, autoimmune, post-MI/Dressler's, uremic, drugs, TB, neoplastic)
- Diagnostic criteria (chest pain, rub, ECG, effusion, biomarkers)
- ECG evolution (4 stages)
- Treatment (NSAIDs, colchicine, steroids, pericardiocentesis if tamponade)

#### 7.2 Pericardial Effusion & Cardiac Tamponade
- Etiology and diagnostic approach
- Tamponade physiology (Beck's triad, pulsus paradoxus, echocardiographic signs)
- Pericardiocentesis technique (echo-guided, complications)
- Pericardial window surgery

#### 7.3 Constrictive Pericarditis
- Etiology (TB, post-cardiac surgery, radiation, idiopathic)
- Hemodynamics (dip-plateau, ventricular interdependence)
- Differentiation from restrictive cardiomyopathy
- Pericardiectomy

#### 7.4 Special Pericardial Conditions
- Effusive-constrictive pericarditis
- Post-cardiac injury syndromes (post-MI, post-pericardiotomy)
- TB pericarditis (diagnosis, antitubercular therapy, steroids)
- Malignant pericardial effusion

### 8. Arrhythmias (9 Heading Hubs, 38 Disease Topics)

#### 8.1 Supraventricular Arrhythmias
- Atrial Fibrillation (AF) - paroxysmal, persistent, long-standing persistent, permanent
  - CHA2DS2-VASc, HAS-BLED
  - Rate vs rhythm control
  - Anticoagulation (DOACs vs warfarin)
  - Catheter ablation, AV node ablation + pacemaker
- Atrial Flutter (typical, atypical)
- AVNRT, AVRT (WPW syndrome)
- Atrial Tachycardia
- Multifocal Atrial Tachycardia (MAT)

#### 8.2 Ventricular Arrhythmias
- Premature Ventricular Contractions (PVCs)
- Non-sustained VT (NSVT)
- Sustained VT (monomorphic, polymorphic/Torsades)
- Ventricular Fibrillation (VF)
- Idiopathic VT (RVOT, LVOT, fascicular)
- Channelopathies (Long QT, Brugada, CPVT, Short QT)

#### 8.3 Bradyarrhythmias & Conduction Disease
- Sinus node dysfunction (sick sinus syndrome)
- AV block (1st, 2nd type I/II, 3rd degree)
- Bundle branch blocks (LBBB, RBBB, bifascicular, trifascicular)
- Conduction system disease post-TAVI/Valve surgery

#### 8.4 Sudden Cardiac Death & ICD
- SCD epidemiology and risk stratification
- Primary prevention ICD (HFrEF, HCM, ARVC, channelopathies)
- Secondary prevention ICD
- ICD complications and management

#### 8.5 Atrial Fibrillation Specific
- Acute AF management (rate vs rhythm, cardioversion)
- Chronic AF management
- AF ablation (pulmonary vein isolation, beyond PVI)
- Post-op AF (post-cardiac surgery)
- AF in special populations (valvular, HFpEF, athletes)

#### 8.6 Device Therapy
- Permanent pacemaker indications (sinus node dysfunction, AV block, bifascicular block + symptoms)
- Pacemaker modes, programming, follow-up
- CRT (biventricular pacing) optimization
- Leadless pacemakers, His-bundle pacing, LBBAP

#### 8.7 Channelopathies & Genetic Arrhythmias
- Long QT Syndrome (types, triggers, beta-blockers, ICD)
- Brugada Syndrome (ECG, risk stratification, ICD)
- Catecholaminergic Polymorphic Ventricular Tachycardia (CPVT)
- Short QT Syndrome
- Early Repolarization Syndrome

#### 8.8 Arrhythmia in Special Populations
- Arrhythmia in pregnancy
- Arrhythmia in athletes
- Arrhythmia in congenital heart disease
- Arrhythmia post-cardiac surgery

#### 8.8 Procedural Ablation
- AF ablation techniques
- VT ablation (ischemic, non-ischemic)
- AVNRT/AVRT ablation
- PVC ablation

- His-bundle pacing, Left bundle branch area pacing (LBBAP)

#### 8.2 ICD & CRT-D

- Programming (shock, ATP, monitoring)

#### 8.4 Remote Monitoring & Device Follow-up
- Remote monitoring benefits

### 9. Cardiomyopathies (5 Heading Hubs, 18 Disease Topics)

#### 9.1 Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)
- Genetics (sarcomere genes), prevalence
- Diagnosis (ECG, echo, CMR with LGE)
- Risk stratification (HCM Risk-SCD score)
- Medical therapy (beta-blockers, verapamil, disopyramide, mavacamten, aficamten)
- Septal reduction therapy (myectomy, alcohol septal ablation)
- ICD for primary prevention

#### 9.2 Dilated Cardiomyopathy (DCM)
- Etiologies (genetic, viral, alcohol, peripartum, autoimmune, tachycardia-induced)
- Genetic testing (TTN, LMNA, FLNC, etc.)
- GDMT + device therapy
- Tachycardia-induced cardiomyopathy

#### 9.3 Restrictive & Infiltrative Cardiomyopathies
- Cardiac Amyloidosis (ATTR, AL) - diagnosis (PYP scan, biopsy, free light chains), treatment (tafamidis, chemotherapy)
- Sarcoidosis (diagnosis - PET, biopsy; treatment - steroids, immunosuppression)
- Hemochromatosis (iron overload, chelation)
- Endomyocardial fibrosis, Löffler endocarditis

#### 9.4 Arrhythmogenic Cardiomyopathies
- ARVC (Task Force Criteria, PKP2 mutations)
- ALVC, biventricular ACM
- Exercise restriction, ICD, catheter ablation

#### 9.5 Other Cardiomyopathies
- Takotsubo (stress) cardiomyopathy
- Peripartum cardiomyopathy (PPCM)
- Myocarditis (viral, autoimmune, giant cell, eosinophilic)
- Non-compaction cardiomyopathy
- Chemotherapy-induced (anthracycline, HER2-targeted)

### 10. Adult Congenital Heart Disease (4 Heading Hubs, 12 Disease Topics)

#### 10.1 Shunt Lesions
- Atrial Septal Defect (ASD) - types, closure criteria, transcatheter vs surgical
- Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD) - types, closure
- Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA)
- Patent Foramen Ovale (PFO) - closure criteria (cryptogenic stroke)

#### 10.2 Cyanotic & Complex CHD
- Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF) - post-repair follow-up (PR, RV dilation, arrhythmia)
- Transposition of Great Arteries (TGA) - post-Switch/Arterial switch
- Single ventricle / Fontan circulation complications
- Eisenmenger syndrome

#### 10.3 Valve & Vascular Lesions
- Bicuspid Aortic Valve (BAV) - aortopathy, surveillance
- Coarctation of Aorta - repair, recoarctation, hypertension
- Pulmonary valve stenosis/regurgitation

#### 10.4 ACHD in Pregnancy & Non-cardiac Surgery
- Risk assessment (mWHO classification)
- Anticoagulation in pregnancy
- Endocarditis prophylaxis

#### 11.3 CTEPH & Other Groups
- CTEPH (V/Q scan, CTPA, PEA surgery, riociguat)
- PH-LHD, PH-lung disease, multifactorial

- Treatment thresholds and targets (ACC/AHA vs ESC)
- Drug classes (ACEi/ARB, CCB, thiazide, beta-blocker, MRA)

- Coronary artery calcium (CAC) scoring
- Lipid management (statin intensity, ezetimibe, PCSK9i)
- Diabetes and CV risk (SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA)

### 16. Cardiac Emergencies & Critical Care (5 Heading Hubs, 16 Disease Topics)

#### 16.1 Acute Coronary Syndrome Emergencies
- STEMI (primary PCI <90 min, fibrinolysis if PCI >120 min)

#### 16.3 Life-Threatening Arrhythmias
- VF/pulseless VT (ACLS algorithm)

- Torsades de pointes (Mg, pacing, isoproterenol)

#### 16.4 Aortic & Pericardial Emergencies
- Aortic dissection (Stanford A - surgery, B - medical)
- Cardiac tamponade (emergency pericardiocentesis)
- Ruptured aortic aneurysm

#### 16.5 Post-Cardiac Arrest Care
- Targeted temperature management
- Coronary angiography post-ROSC
- Neuroprognostication
- Long-term management
