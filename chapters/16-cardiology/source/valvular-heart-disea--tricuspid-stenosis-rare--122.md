# Tricuspid Stenosis (TS)

## 1. Etiology
- **Rheumatic** (most common, almost always with MS)
- Carcinoid syndrome
- Congenital
- Prosthetic valve obstruction
- Endocarditis with large vegetation

## 2. Clinical
- Fatigue, peripheral edema, ascites
- Diastolic murmur at LLSB, ↑ with inspiration
- Giant a waves on JVP, abdominal JVP

## 3. Echo
- Thickened/restricted leaflets
- Mean gradient >5 mmHg
- RV/RA enlargement

## 4. Management
- Diuretics for congestion
- **Surgical** (open commissurotomy or replacement)
- **Percutaneous balloon** rarely feasible (large annulus)
- Treat underlying cause (rheumatic, carcinoid)

> **Pearls** — TS is **rare**, almost always **rheumatic + MS**. Don't confuse with TR (systolic murmur, not diastolic). Surgical replacement with bioprosthetic preferred (mechanical valves have high thrombosis risk in RV).
