## 1. Resting & Exercise ECG
- **Rest ECG** — often normal; Q waves, LVH, ST-T changes increase suspicion
- **Exercise ECG** — first-line for intermediate PTP, interpretable resting ECG, can exercise
  - **Positive:** ≥1 mm horizontal/downsloping ST ↓ at J + 80 ms in ≥2 contiguous leads
  - **High-risk features:** ↓ST ≥2 mm, ST ↑ aVR, early onset (<5 METS), ↓BP, sustained VT, prolonged ST ↓ into recovery

---

## 2. Stress Imaging
- **Stress echo** — RWMA > coronary territory; dobutamine if cannot exercise
- **SPECT / PET nuclear** — perfusion defect (reversible = ischaemia; fixed = infarct)
- **Stress CMR** — perfusion + wall motion + LGE (viability) in one

### Choosing Modality
- **Anatomy question** (low–intermediate PTP, young) → **CTCA**
- **Ischaemia question** (intermediate–high PTP, prior CAD) → **stress imaging**
- **Inconclusive** → consider FFR-CT, invasive angio with FFR/iFR

---

## 3. CT Coronary Angiography (CTCA)
- **Rule-out test** in low–intermediate PTP
- Calcium scoring (Agatston) refines PTP
- High NPV; **plaque characterisation** (high-risk: low-attenuation, positive remodelling, napkin-ring)
- Limitations: blooming artefact (calcium, stents), radiation, AF / HR > 65 bpm

---

## 4. Invasive Coronary Angiography
- **Gold standard** for anatomy
- Quantifies stenosis (%) and SYNTAX score
- Combine with **FFR / iFR** for intermediate 50–90% lesions

---

## 5. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **CTCA = rule-out test** in low–intermediate risk (high NPV)
- **Stress echo / nuclear / CMR = ischaemia test** in intermediate–high
- Positive exercise ECG: **≥1 mm ST ↓ at J+80 ms** in ≥2 leads
- FFR ≤0.80 / iFR ≤0.89 → physiologically significant → revascularise
- High-risk CTCA plaque: low-attenuation, positive remodelling, napkin-ring sign
