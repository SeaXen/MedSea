# Etiology and diagnostic approach (Pericardial Effusion)

## 1. Definition
**Pericardial effusion**: Accumulation of fluid in the pericardial space. Can be:
- **Transudate** (low protein, hydrostatic)
- **Exudate** (high protein, inflammatory)
- **Hemorrhagic** (blood)
- **Purulent** (pus)
- **Malignant** (cancer cells)
- **Chylous** (lymph)

## 2. Etiology (Same as Pericarditis)
### Transudative
- **Heart failure** (most common cause overall)
- **Hypoalbuminemia**: Cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome
- **Myxedema** (hypothyroidism)
- **Pregnancy**

### Exudative/Inflammatory
- **Infectious**: Viral, TB, bacterial
- **Autoimmune**: SLE, RA, scleroderma
- **Post-MI** (early, Dressler)
- **Uremia**
- **Radiation**
- **Drugs**

### Hemorrhagic
- **Malignancy** (most common)
- **TB**
- **Trauma, post-cardiac surgery**
- **Aortic dissection**
- **Anticoagulation**
- **Post-MI free wall rupture**

### Purulent
- **Bacterial infection** (esp. *Staph*, *Strep*, pneumococcus)
- **Immunocompromised**
- Often with sepsis

### Malignant
- **Lung, breast, lymphoma, melanoma**
- **Primary pericardial mesothelioma** (rare)

## 3. Clinical Features
- **Asymptomatic** (small effusions)
- **Dyspnea, chest discomfort**
- **Tamponade signs** (if large/rapid)
- **Constitutional**: Fever, weight loss (TB, malignancy)
- **Distant heart sounds**
- **Effusive-constrictive** features (rare)

## 4. Diagnostic Approach
### Initial
- **History**: Cancer, TB, autoimmune, recent MI/surgery
- **CXR**: Enlarged cardiac silhouette (water-bottle) — if >200 mL
- **ECG**: Low voltage, electrical alternans
- **Echocardiography** (gold standard):
  - Size, location, hemodynamic significance
  - RA/RV diastolic collapse (tamponade)
  - IVC plethora
  - Respiratory variation in mitral/tricuspid inflow

### Further
- **CT/MRI** for loculated, complex effusions
- **Pericardiocentesis** for:
  - Tamponade
  - Suspected purulent/malignant/TB
  - Large idiopathic effusion not responding to Rx
- **Pericardial fluid analysis**:
  - Cell count, differential
  - Gram stain, culture (bacterial, fungal, TB)
  - Cytology
  - Protein, LDH, glucose
  - **ADA** (TB)
  - **PCR** for viral (Coxsackie, etc.)

## 5. Fluid Analysis Interpretation
| Type | Appearance | Cell | Other |
|------|------------|------|-------|
| **Transudate** | Clear, straw | Low WBC | Low protein |
| **Exudate** | Cloudy | High WBC (lymphocytes or neutrophils) | High protein |
| **Hemorrhagic** | Bloody | RBCs | Hct >50% peripheral = hemorrhage |
| **Purulent** | Frank pus | PMNs | Positive culture |
| **Malignant** | Bloody/serous | Atypical cells | Positive cytology |
| **TB** | Serosanguinous | Lymphocytes | ↑ADA, +culture/PCR |

## 6. Management
- **Small, asymptomatic**: Treat underlying cause, follow-up
- **Moderate-large**: Pericardiocentesis ± catheter drainage
- **Recurrent**: Pericardial window
- **Tamponade**: Emergent pericardiocentesis

> **Pearls**
> - **Echo** is the gold standard for diagnosis and follow-up
> - **Hemorrhagic effusion** = malignancy, TB, or trauma until proven otherwise
> - **Exudate vs transudate** follows Light's criteria
> - **TB pericarditis** has lymphocytic exudate, ↑ADA (>40 U/L)
> - **Malignant effusion**: Pericardial window + systemic Rx
