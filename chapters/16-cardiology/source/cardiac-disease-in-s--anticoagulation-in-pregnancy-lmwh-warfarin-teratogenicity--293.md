# Anticoagulation in Pregnancy

## 1. Why Pregnancy Is Prothrombotic
- ↑ Clotting factors (I, II, VII, VIII, IX, X)
- ↓ Protein S, acquired protein C resistance
- Venous stasis (mechanical compression by gravid uterus)
- **Risk of VTE:** ↑ 4–5 fold (highest postpartum, especially first 6 weeks)

## 2. Warfarin — Teratogenicity
- **Vitamin K antagonist** crosses placenta; embryopathy in weeks 6–12
- **Warfarin embryopathy:** Nasal hypoplasia, stippled epiphyses (chondrodysplasia punctata), CNS abnormalities
- **Fetal hemorrhage** at any trimester
- **Safe only:** Between 13–36 weeks at lowest possible dose (≤5 mg/day) — and only in selected mechanical valve patients (ESC 2018)

## 3. LMWH — Drug of Choice
- **Enoxaparin 1 mg/kg SC twice daily** (weight-adjusted, NOT fixed)
- **Anti-Xa monitoring:** Peak 0.8–1.2 U/mL (mechanical valve 1.0–1.3)
- Does **not cross placenta** — safe for fetus
- Risk of valve thrombosis if underdosed in **mechanical valves** (older data, mitigated with anti-Xa monitoring and twice-daily dosing)
- Reversible with **protamine**

## 4. Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs)
- **Contraindicated** in pregnancy and breastfeeding
- Rivaroxaban, apixaban, dabigatran cross placenta — animal teratogenicity, no human safety data
- Switch to LMWH **before conception** or as soon as pregnancy confirmed

## 5. Postpartum Anticoagulation
- Resume LMWH 4–6 h after delivery (no bleeding)
- Warfarin safe during breastfeeding (does not enter milk)
- DOACs avoid during breastfeeding (insufficient data; do not use)

> **Pearls**
> - Mechanical mitral valve = highest thrombosis risk; ESC favors LMWH twice daily with anti-Xa
> - Warfarin embryopathy occurs in 5–10% of exposures in 1st trimester
> - DOACs are **out** for both pregnancy and lactation
