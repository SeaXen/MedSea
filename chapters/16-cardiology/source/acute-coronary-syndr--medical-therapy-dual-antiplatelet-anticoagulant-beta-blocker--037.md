# Medical Therapy (Dual Antiplatelet, Anticoagulant, Beta-Blocker, ACEi, Statin)

<callout icon="💊" color="blue_bg">
**Topic:** Medical Therapy in NSTE-ACS — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Overview
NSTE-ACS management relies on **antiplatelet + anticoagulant** therapy for acute thrombotic protection, plus **secondary prevention** (statin, ACEi, beta-blocker). The choice of agent and duration depends on the chosen strategy (invasive vs conservative) and bleeding/ischaemic risk.

## 2. Dual Antiplatelet Therapy (DAPT)

| P2Y12 | Loading | Maintenance | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ticagrelor** | 180 mg | 90 mg BD | **Preferred** (PLATO); reversible, dyspnoea |
| **Prasugrel** | 60 mg | 10 mg daily | Avoid if stroke/TIA, age >75, weight <60 kg |
| **Clopidogrel** | 300–600 mg | 75 mg daily | Use if fibrinolysis given, or other unavailable |

- **Aspirin** 150–300 mg loading → 75–100 mg daily
- **DAPT duration**: ≥12 months after NSTEMI (Class I)
- **PPI** (e.g., omeprazole) for gastroprotection

## 3. Anticoagulation (choose one, switch if PCI planned)

| Agent | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Fondaparinux** | 2.5 mg SC daily | **First choice** in NSTE-ACS (OASIS-5) |
| **Enoxaparin** | 1 mg/kg SC BD | Renally cleared; reduce in CKD |
| **UFH** | 60 U/kg bolus + 12 U/kg/h | Use if PCI planned, or CKD |
| **Bivalirudin** | Per protocol | Periprocedural; lower bleeding |

- **Fondaparinux** + UFH (for PCI) is the **standard** combination
- **Stop anticoagulant** after PCI (unless other indication)

## 4. Beta-Blocker
- Start within **24 h** if no contraindications
- **Target HR 50–60 bpm**
- **Oral** (not IV in first 24 h — increased cardiogenic shock risk; COMMIT)
- Continue indefinitely post-MI
- **Contraindications**: HR <60, SBP <100, signs of HF, AV block, asthma (relative)

## 5. ACE Inhibitor / ARB
- Start within **24 h** in STEMI / NSTE-ACS (especially LVEF <40%, anterior MI, HF)
- **Ramipril**, **enalapril**, **perindopril** (HOPE, EUROPA)
- If ACEi-intolerant → **ARB** (valsartan, losartan)
- Avoid in: hypotension, hyperkalaemia, bilateral RAS, angioedema history
- Continue indefinitely

## 6. Statin
- **High-intensity** (atorvastatin 40–80 mg or rosuvastatin 20–40 mg) for **all** ACS patients
- Start regardless of LDL level
- Reduces mortality (PROVE-IT, IMPROVE-IT)
- Target **LDL <1.8 mmol/L** (<70 mg/dL) — even <1.4 mmol/L very-high-risk

## 7. Additional Therapies

| Drug | Indication |
|---|---|
| **Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (MRA)** | LVEF ≤40% + HF or DM (EPHESUS) |
| **Nitrates (IV)** | Persistent ischaemia, hypertension, pulmonary oedema |
| **Morphine** | Severe pain (but slow absorption of clopidogrel — caution) |
| **Oxygen** | Only if SpO2 <90% (AVOID trial) |
| **Calcium channel blockers** | If β-blocker contraindicated (verapamil/diltiazem — avoid if LV dysfunction) |

## 8. Duration of Therapy
- **DAPT**: 12 months (then assess ischaemic vs bleeding risk)
- **Anticoagulant**: until PCI (or discharge)
- **Statin / ACEi / beta-blocker**: indefinite
- **PPI**: while on DAPT, especially if bleeding risk

## 9. Bleeding Risk Assessment (CRUSADE / ARC-HBR)
- **High bleeding risk**: consider shorter DAPT, radial access, fondaparinux
- Score variables: CrCl, HR, SBP, sex, CHF, prior vascular disease, DM

> **Pearls**
> - **DAPT = aspirin + ticagrelor (or prasugrel) ≥12 months**
> - **Fondaparinux** = preferred anticoagulant in NSTE-ACS; add UFH for PCI
> - **Beta-blocker** (oral) within 24 h, **avoid IV** in first 24 h
> - **ACEi** within 24 h in anterior MI, LV dysfunction, or HF
> - **High-intensity statin** for all (target LDL <1.4–1.8 mmol/L)
> - **PPI** for gastroprotection during DAPT
> - **Oxygen only if SpO2 <90%**
