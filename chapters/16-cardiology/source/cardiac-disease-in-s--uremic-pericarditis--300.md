# Uremic Pericarditis

## 1. Definition
Pericarditis in patients with **advanced renal failure** (BUN typically >60 mg/dL), before or shortly after starting dialysis.

## 2. Pathogenesis
- Uremic toxins, hyperuricemia, hyperparathyroidism
- **Not** fully understood
- Hemorrhagic effusion common (uremic platelet dysfunction)

## 3. Clinical
- Chest pain, fever, friction rub
- Effusion (often hemorrhagic)
- Tamponade risk significant
- May be silent in advanced uremia

## 4. Diagnosis
- Echo: pericardial effusion
- Pericardial fluid: hemorrhagic, lymphocytic
- **Rule out infection** (uremic patients are immunocompromised)
- **Rule out other causes** (TB, viral, autoimmune)

## 5. Management
- **Intensify dialysis** (daily, more frequent, longer sessions)
- **NSAIDs** — use cautiously (renal); aspirin preferred
- **Colchicine** — caution (renal dose adjustment)
- **Steroids** — usually avoid
- **Pericardiocentesis** for tamponade
- **Pericardiectomy** for refractory constriction (rare)

> **Pearls** — **Intensify dialysis** is the cornerstone. Effusion often **hemorrhagic** — careful with anticoagulation. Pericardiocentesis for tamponade. Avoid NSAIDs (renal).
