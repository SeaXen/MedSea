# Nutritional Deficiencies and the Heart

## 1. Thiamine (B1) — Beriberi
- **Wet beriberi**: high-output cardiac failure, vasodilation, edema, tachycardia
- **Dry beriberi**: neuropathy, muscle wasting
- **Shoshin beriberi**: acute fulminant HF, shock
- **Risk**: alcohol use, malnutrition, dialysis, bariatric surgery
- **Diagnosis**: clinical, ↑lactate, response to thiamine
- **Treatment**: **IV thiamine 100 mg** then daily — response in hours

## 2. Selenium — Keshan Disease
- Endemic cardiomyopathy in selenium-deficient areas (China)
- Causes **dilated cardiomyopathy** in children, can affect adults
- **Reversible** with selenium supplementation

## 3. Carnitine
- Required for FA transport into mitochondria
- **Carnitine deficiency**: DCM, myopathy, hypoglycemia
- **Carnitine-responsive DCM**: reversible with L-carnitine

## 4. Other
- **Vitamin D**: linked to HTN, HF; supplementing uncertain benefit
- **Vitamin B12**: ↑homocysteine → atherothrombosis
- **Hypophosphatemia**: in refeeding, TPN, alcohol — ↓contractility

> **Pearls**: Always give **thiamine before glucose** in alcohol use, malnutrition. **Wet beriberi** mimics septic shock — give empirical thiamine.
