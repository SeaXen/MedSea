# Arrhythmogenic Left Ventricular / Biventricular Cardiomyopathy (ALVC)

## 1. Definition
**ALVC** (left-dominant ACM) and **biventricular ACM** are variants of arrhythmogenic cardiomyopathy where **fibro-fatty replacement involves the LV predominantly or both ventricles**. Phenotype overlaps with DCM and ARVC.

## 2. Genetics
| Gene | Phenotype |
|---|---|
| **DSP** (desmoplakin) | LV-predominant; Carvajal-like; woolly hair, palmoplantar keratoderma |
| **FLNC** (filamin C) | LV and biventricular; high arrhythmia risk even with mild LV dysfunction |
| **PKP2** | Classic ARVC, but LV involvement in 30–50% on CMR |
| **PLN** (p.Arg14del) | Biventricular; low-voltage ECG; Dutch founder |
| **DES** (desmin) | Mixed; myopathy + CM |
| **TMEM43** | Severe, ARVC; Newfoundland founder |

## 3. Clinical Features
- **Sustained VT of LV origin** (RBBB morphology, superior axis)
- **Frequent PVCs** (LBBB or RBBB morphology)
- **SCD** as first presentation
- **Palpitations, syncope**
- **LV systolic dysfunction** may be mild or severe

## 4. ECG
- **Low QRS voltages** in limb and precordial leads (PLN, DSP)
- **T-wave inversion** in **V4–V6** (LV leads), inferior leads
- **PVCs / VT** of LV origin
- No epsilon wave usually (more RV in classic ARVC)

## 5. Imaging
- **Echo:** LV regional WMA (lateral, inferolateral), ± LV dilatation
- **CMR LGE:** **subepicardial / mid-wall**, often **inferolateral / lateral wall** ("ring-like" in DSP)
- **RV involvement** in biventricular forms

## 6. Diagnostic Criteria
- **2010 Padua criteria** for LV-ACM (and revised 2022 international criteria):
  - Morphofunctional: LV regional WMA, LVEF reduced
  - Tissue: LGE in LV (subepicardial/mid-wall ring)
  - Repolarisation: TWI V4–V6
  - Depolarisation: low voltages
  - Arrhythmias: VT of LV origin, ≥500 PVCs
  - Family / genetic: ARVC/ACM family history, pathogenic variant

## 7. Differential Diagnosis
- **Myocarditis** (subepicardial LGE) — viral prodrome, ↑troponin
- **Cardiac sarcoid** — FDG-PET uptake, AV block
- **DCM** — global hypokinesia, less arrhythmia
- **Athletic heart** — normal ECG, no LGE

## 8. Management
- **ICD** for SCD survivor, sustained VT, syncope, or LVEF ≤35%
- **β-blocker** for symptomatic PVCs
- **Catheter ablation** for recurrent VT (often epicardial)
- **Avoid competitive sports**
- Family screening + genetic counselling

## 9. Risk Stratification
- LV dysfunction + LGE + male + proband status = higher SCD
- DSP and FLNC variants may warrant earlier ICD

> **Pearls**
> - ALVC is **LV-dominant or biventricular** fibro-fatty replacement.
> - **DSP** mutation → **ring-like subepicardial LGE** in LV lateral wall.
> - **FLNC** variants have high SCD risk even with preserved LVEF.
> - **Low QRS voltage + TWI V4–V6** is the classic ECG clue.
> - Consider ALVC in unexplained LV arrhythmias, especially young patients.
