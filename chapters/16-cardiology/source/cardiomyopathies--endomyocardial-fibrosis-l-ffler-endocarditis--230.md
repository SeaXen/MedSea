# Endomyocardial Fibrosis & Löffler Endocarditis

## 1. Definition
**Endomyocardial fibrosis (EMF)** and **Löffler (Loeffler) endocarditis** are related restrictive cardiomyopathies characterised by **endocardial fibrosis** and **eosinophilic infiltration** of the myocardium.

## 2. Spectrum
- **Acute (Löffler):** eosinophilic infiltration with **acute necrosis** and mural thrombus
- **Subacute:** organising thrombus + early fibrosis
- **Chronic (EMF):** dense endocardial fibrosis, restrictive physiology

## 3. Etiology
| Type | Causes |
|---|---|
| **Löffler** | Hypereosinophilic syndrome, Churg-Strauss, drugs, parasites, malignancy |
| **EMF** | Idiopathic (tropical); linked to geophagy, malnutrition, parasitic infections (filariasis) |

## 4. Tropical EMF — Epidemiology
- Children/young adults in **tropical Africa, Asia, South America**
- Affects **apex of RV** and **inflow tract of LV**
- Causes restrictive CM with **apical obliteration**

## 5. Clinical Features
- **Right HF** signs (oedema, ascites, hepatomegaly) — RV involvement
- **Left HF** signs (dyspnoea, PND) — LV involvement
- **Atrial fibrillation**
- **Embolic events** (mural thrombus)
- **Tricuspid / MR** regurgitation from papillary involvement

## 6. Investigations
- **Echo:** apical obliteration, endocardial echo "bright" lining, thrombus, valve regurgitation
- **MRI:** LGE of endocardium (apical, subvalvular)
- **Cardiac catheterisation** (historical): characteristic ventriculogram
- **Biopsy:** endocardial fibrosis with or without eosinophilic infiltrate
- **CBC + differential** for eosinophilia (Löffler)

## 7. Löffler Endocarditis — Specifics
- Diagnose when **AEC >1500/µL** for ≥6 months + cardiac involvement
- Treat underlying cause (e.g., imatinib for FIP1L1-PDGFRA)
- **Steroids** for acute phase (reduce eosinophil-driven injury)
- **Anticoagulation** (LV thrombus risk)
- **Vincristine, mepolizumab** in selected

## 8. Management
| Phase | Therapy |
|---|---|
| **Acute (Löffler)** | Steroids ± immunosuppression; anticoagulation |
| **Subacute** | Diuretics, anticoagulation, rate control |
| **Chronic (EMF)** | Surgery (endocardiectomy) for severe HF; valve replacement |

## 9. Surgical Options
- **Endocardiectomy** with AV valve repair/replacement
- High operative risk in advanced disease
- Best in late EMF with severe HF, NYHA III–IV

## 10. Prognosis
- Variable; **5-year survival ~50–70%** in advanced disease
- Tropical EMF in children has poor prognosis without surgery
- **Löffler** has better outcome if hypereosinophilia controlled

> **Pearls**
> - **Löffler** = hypereosinophilic syndrome → acute endocardial injury.
> - **EMF** = tropical, idiopathic → chronic apical obliteration.
> - **Apical echo-bright lining** + apical thrombus is classic.
> - **Steroids** in acute Löffler; **endocardectomy + valve surgery** in chronic EMF.
> - **Anticoagulate** — high mural thrombus / embolic risk.
