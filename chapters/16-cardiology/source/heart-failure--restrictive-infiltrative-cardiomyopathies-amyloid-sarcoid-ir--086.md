# Restrictive / Infiltrative Cardiomyopathies

## 1. Definition
Non-dilated ventricles with **impaired diastolic filling** (restrictive physiology), preserved systolic function (initially).

## 2. Causes
- **Amyloidosis** — AL (light chain, plasma cell dyscrasia) or ATTR (transthyretin, wild-type or mutant)
- **Sarcoidosis** — non-caseating granulomas
- **Hemochromatosis** — iron overload (HFE C282Y)
- **Fabry disease** — α-galactosidase A deficiency (X-linked)
- **Endomyocardial fibrosis** — Löffler, tropical
- **Radiation, scleroderma**

## 3. Diagnosis
- Echo: ↑ wall thickness, speckled/gritty myocardium, biatrial enlargement, restrictive filling
- **CMR with LGE** — key for tissue characterization
- **Bone scintigraphy (DPD/PYP scan)** — ATTR (Grade 2–3 = highly specific)
- **Serum/urine immunofixation + FLC** — to exclude AL amyloid
- **Genetic testing** (TTR, Fabry)

## 4. Management
- Treat underlying cause (chemo for AL, tafamidis for ATTR, iron reduction for hemochromatosis, agalsidase for Fabry)
- Diuretics for congestion
- **Avoid** digoxin, CCB, vasodilators in amyloidosis (hypersensitivity)
- Standard HF drugs often poorly tolerated

> **Pearls** — Suspect amyloid in HFpEF with ↑ wall thickness + low voltage ECG + carpal tunnel. **AL must be excluded** before ATTR diagnosis (cardiac biopsy or bone scan + negative monoclonal screen). Tafamidis 80/61 mg for ATTR-CM.
