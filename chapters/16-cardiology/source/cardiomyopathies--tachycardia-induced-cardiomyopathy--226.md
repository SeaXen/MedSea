# Tachycardia-Induced Cardiomyopathy (TCM)

## 1. Definition
TCM is a **reversible cause of LV systolic dysfunction** caused by prolonged tachycardia. Also called "tachycardia-mediated" or "arrhythmia-induced" cardiomyopathy. Recovery of LV function is expected after rate/rhythm control.

## 2. Pathophysiology
- Chronic tachycardia → abnormal Ca²⁺ handling, oxidative stress, myocardial energy depletion
- LV dilatation + reduced contractility + HF symptoms
- Reversible if tachycardia eliminated within months

## 3. Common Causes
| Trigger | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Atrial fibrillation** | Persistent / permanent with rapid ventricular rate |
| **Atrial flutter** | Especially 2:1 with ventricular rate 150 |
| **AVNRT / AVRT** | Incessant SVT |
| **Frequent PVCs** | >10–15% PVC burden (PVC-induced CM) |
| **VT** | Idiopathic VT, fascicular VT |
| **Persistent sinus tachycardia** | Inappropriate sinus tach, POTS |

## 4. Clinical Clues
- **Heart rate** consistently >100 bpm (often 120–150+)
- Short duration of HF symptoms (weeks-months)
- Young patient with new HF + arrhythmia
- No other cause of LV dysfunction found
- Disproportionate LV dysfunction vs. presumed aetiology
- LV dysfunction improves with rate control

## 5. Workup
- **ECG + Holter** for arrhythmia burden
- **Echo** for LV size, LVEF (often severely reduced, ~25–35%)
- **TSH** to exclude thyrotoxicosis
- **CMR** if doubt — usually no LGE in pure TCM
- **Coronary angio** if risk factors (rule out ischaemia)

## 6. PVC-Induced Cardiomyopathy — A Subset
- Threshold typically **>10% PVC burden** (some at 5–10%)
- Worse with **epicardial, RVOT, long-coupled** PVCs
- **Catheter ablation** is curative; can also do **antiarrhythmic therapy** (flecainide, sotalol, amiodarone)

## 7. Management
| Strategy | Details |
|---|---|
| **Rate control** | β-blocker, non-DHP CCB, digoxin |
| **Rhythm control** | Antiarrhythmics, cardioversion, ablation |
| **GDMT** | Standard HFrEF four pillars (often continue post-recovery) |
| **Anticoagulation** | CHA₂DS₂-VASc-driven in AF |
| **Catheter ablation** | Curative for SVT, AFL, AF, PVCs |

## 8. Recovery & Prognosis
- **LVEF recovery** within **3–6 months** of rate/rhythm control
- Time to recovery depends on duration of tachycardia (months vs. years)
- Some patients have a "**two-hit**" picture — baseline subclinical CM unmasked by tachycardia
- Monitor LVEF post-recovery; recurrence of arrhythmia can cause relapse
- Continue **GDMT for ≥3–6 months** post-recovery; reassess thereafter

## 9. Counseling
- Emphasise **adherence** to rate/rhythm control
- Avoid triggers (caffeine, stimulants)
- **Alcohol moderation**
- Lifelong cardiology follow-up if recurrence

> **Pearls**
> - TCM is a **reversible cause of HF** — always consider in new DCM.
> - **PVC burden >10%** is a key threshold for PVC-induced CM.
> - **Ablation** is curative for many arrhythmias underlying TCM.
> - LVEF typically **recovers within 3–6 months** of rate/rhythm control.
> - Maintain **GDMT for 3–6 months** post-recovery, then reassess.
