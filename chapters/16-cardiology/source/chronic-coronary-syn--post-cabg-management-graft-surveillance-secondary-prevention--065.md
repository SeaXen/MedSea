# Post-CABG Management (Graft Surveillance, Prevention)

## 1. Graft Patency
| Graft | 1 y patency | 10 y patency |
|---|---|---|
| **LIMA → LAD** | 95–98% | ~90% (best) |
| **Radial artery** | 90% | 50–60% |
| **SVG** | 85% | 50% (worst — atherosclerosis) |

### Graft Failure Mechanisms
- **Early (<1 mo):** thrombosis (technical, competitive flow)
- **1 mo–1 y:** intimal hyperplasia
- **>1 y:** atherosclerosis (SVG most prone)
- **Vein graft disease** accelerates (lipid-rich, friable)

---

## 2. Medical Therapy
- **Aspirin 75–100 mg** lifelong (± P2Y₁₂ if ACS indication, 12 mo)
- **High-intensity statin** lifelong (LDL <1.4 mmol/L)
- **Beta-blocker** ≥1 year (indefinitely if LV dysfunction)
- **ACEi / ARB** if HTN, DM, CKD, LV dysfunction
- **Smoking cessation** (mandatory)
- BP <130/80, HbA1c <7%

---

## 3. Graft Surveillance
- **Routine stress testing** is **NOT** recommended in asymptomatic patients
- Symptom-driven evaluation (recurrent angina, new ischaemia)
- **CTCA** can be used (metal-clip artefact from LIMA harvesting)
- **Invasive angio** for recurrent symptoms

---

## 4. Repeat Revascularisation
- **PCI** for focal SVG or native disease (often with embolic protection in SVG)
- **Redo CABG** for diffuse graft disease (high risk)
- LIMA → LAD graft — **do not jeopardise**; redo with bilateral IMA if possible

---

## 5. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **LIMA → LAD is the gold-standard** graft (>90% patency at 10 y)
- **SVG** has worst long-term patency (~50% at 10 y)
- **Aspirin + high-intensity statin** mandatory lifelong
- **No routine stress testing** in asymptomatic post-CABG
- **CTCA** useful for surveillance; redo angio for symptoms
