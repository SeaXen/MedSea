# Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy (ARVC)

<callout icon="🫀" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** ARVC — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Inherited cardiomyopathy** characterised by **progressive fibrofatty replacement** of the RV myocardium (and sometimes LV). Causes **VT, VF, and SCD** in young adults and athletes. Autosomal dominant — desmosomal gene mutations (PKP2 most common, also DSP, DSG2, DSC2, JUP).

> Prevalence 1:5000. Most common cause of SCD in young athletes after HCM.

## 2. Pathophysiology
Desmosomal dysfunction → myocyte detachment → inflammation → fibrofatty replacement → RV dilation, aneurysm, arrhythmia.

## 3. Diagnosis (Modified Task Force Criteria 2010)
Six categories: **D**efinite / **B**orderline / **P**ossible.

1. **Global/regional dysfunction** (echo, CMR, angiography)
2. **Tissue characterisation** — fibrofatty replacement on biopsy / CMR LGE
3. **Repolarisation abnormalities** — TWI V1–V3 (or beyond, in absence of RBBB)
4. **Depolarisation abnormalities** — epsilon waves, late potentials on SAECG
5. **Arrhythmias** — VT of LBBB morphology (RV origin), VF
6. **Family history** — SCD <35 y, ARVC in 1st-degree relative, pathogenic mutation

> **Definite** = 2 major, or 1 major + 2 minor, or 4 minor.

## 4. Clinical Features
- **Palpitations**, syncope, **SCD** (often first presentation in young)
- **VT with LBBB morphology + superior axis** (RVOT)
- **Exercise-induced** — common trigger
- Heart failure (late)
- RV failure signs (JVP, hepatomegaly, oedema)

## 5. Investigations
- **ECG** — TWI V1–V3 (minor V1, V2 in >14 y, no RBBB), **epsilon waves** (small deflection at end of QRS in V1–V3), QRS duration V1+V2+V3 >180 ms
- **Signal-averaged ECG (SAECG)** — late potentials
- **Echo** — RV dilation, RV wall motion abnormalities, RVOT dilation
- **CMR** — gold standard: RV dilation, regional akinesia/dyskinesia, aneurysm, fibrofatty LGE
- **Holter / EP study** — VT morphology
- **Genetic testing** — desmosomal panel (PKP2, DSP, DSG2, DSC2, JUP)
- **Family screening** — 1st-degree relatives (ECG, echo, CMR, Holter)

## 6. Management

### Lifestyle
- **Avoid competitive sports / strenuous exercise** (Class I, ESC 2020) — exercise accelerates disease

### Arrhythmia
- **Beta-blocker** — first-line
- **Antiarrhythmics** — sotalol, amiodarone
- **Catheter ablation** — for recurrent VT (high recurrence rate)
- **ICD** — for secondary prevention (survived VF, sustained VT); primary prevention based on risk (syncope, NSVT, severe RV dysfunction, extensive LGE, family SCD)

### Heart Failure
- Standard GDMT (caution: limited trial data)
- **RV failure** — diuretics, anticoagulation if severe
- **Heart transplant** for end-stage biventricular failure

### Family
- **Genetic counselling**
- **Serial screening** of 1st-degree relatives

## 7. Differentials
- **Biventricular ARVC** (left-dominant, "ALVC" — DSP, FLNC)
- **Idiopathic RVOT VT** — normal RV, no fibrosis
- **Sarcoidosis** (RV involvement, systemic)
- **Congenital** (Ebstein anomaly — displaced TV)
- **Dilated cardiomyopathy** with biventricular involvement

> **Pearls**
> - **ARVC** = fibrofatty replacement of RV, desmosomal disease
> - **TWI V1–V3** in a young person with VT → think ARVC
> - **Epsilon waves** — small deflections after QRS in V1–V3 (major criterion)
> - **LBBB-morphology VT with superior axis** = RVOT
> - **Avoid competitive sports** (accelerates phenotype)
> - **PKP2** is the most common mutation
> - **ICD** for secondary prevention; primary prevention based on risk
> - **Catheter ablation** — high recurrence rate, often palliative

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** ARVC is a **major cause of SCD in young athletes**. The Modified Task Force Criteria integrate imaging, ECG, arrhythmia, family history, and genetics. **Avoid exercise** — it accelerates disease. **ICD for secondary prevention is mandatory**; primary prevention is individualised.
</callout>
