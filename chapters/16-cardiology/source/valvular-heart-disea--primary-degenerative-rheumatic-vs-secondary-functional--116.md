# MR: Primary vs Secondary

## 1. Primary (Degenerative) MR
- **Leaflet/chordal pathology** — prolapse, flail, Barlow's, fibroelastic deficiency
- Most common cause of surgical MR in developed world
- **Repair preferred** (95% success in expert centers)
- Treat symptoms or LV dysfunction (EF 30–60%, ESD <40 mm)

## 2. Secondary (Functional) MR
- **LV remodeling/dilation** causes annular dilation + leaflet tethering
- Leaflet itself is normal
- Cause: ischemic (post-MI) or non-ischemic DCM
- **GDMT first** — reverse remodeling may improve MR
- **CRT** can improve functional MR
- Surgery: annuloplasty (recurrence high) or replacement
- **MitraClip** (TEER) — for high-risk surgical candidates (COAPT, MITRA-FR trials)

## 3. Distinguishing Echo
- **Primary**: leaflet pathology, eccentric jet, normal LV size
- **Secondary**: normal leaflets, central jet, dilated LV, tenting

## 4. Timing
- Primary: surgery when symptomatic, EF 30–60%, ESD <40, AF, or pulmonary HTN
- Secondary: optimal medical therapy ± device first; consider TEER if persistent

> **Pearls** — In **secondary MR, treat the LV** first. Mitral surgery for secondary MR alone has poor outcomes. **COAPT** showed MitraClip benefit in select patients; **MITRA-FR** did not. Patient selection matters.
