# Hemodynamic Assessment of Aortic Regurgitation

## 1. Goals
Quantify AR severity, assess LV compensation (size, EF), and identify the **trigger for surgery**. Echo is the cornerstone; multiple parameters must be integrated because no single measurement is reliable in isolation.

## 2. Qualitative Echo Features of Severe AR
- **Central jet width** >65% of LVOT
- **Vena contracta (VC) width** >6 mm (most reliable single linear measure)
- **Pressure half-time (PHT)** <200 ms (rapid pressure equalisation)
- **Holodiastolic flow reversal** in descending aorta (PWT > proximal arch)
- **Premature mitral valve closure** (acute severe AR)
- **Dense CW Doppler** signal of regurgitant jet

## 3. Quantitative Measures of Severity

| Parameter | Severe Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Vena contracta (mm)** | **> 6 mm** | TTE parasternal LAX; small variations big impact |
| **Jet width / LVOT (%)** | > 65% | Qualitative |
| **Pressure half-time (ms)** | **< 200** | <300 mild, 200–300 moderate |
| **Regurgitant volume (mL/beat)** | **> 60** | RVol = SV(LVOT) − SV(AR valve) |
| **Regurgitant fraction (%)** | **> 50** | RVol / SV |
| **EROA (cm²)** | **> 0.30** | PISA-derived |
| **Angiography (cath)** | **4/4 Sellers** | Historical |

## 4. PISA Method (EROA)
- Use proximal isovelocity surface area on the **LV side** of aortic valve (aliasing at 30–40 cm/s)
- **EROA = 2πr² × aliasing velocity / peak AR velocity**
- **RVol = EROA × AR VTI**
- Best for **central jets**; eccentric jets under-estimate

## 5. LV Size & Function (The "Compensation" Assessment)

| Parameter | Threshold for Surgery (asymptomatic) |
|---|---|
| **LVEF** | <50% → surgery |
| **LV end-systolic diameter (LVESD)** | >50 mm (or >25 mm/m²) |
| **LV end-diastolic diameter (LVEDD)** | >65 mm (or >33 mm/m²) — late finding |
| **Indexed LVESD** | >2.7 cm/m² (some guidelines) |

> ⚠️ **Critical:** Symptoms or LV dysfunction at any threshold = operate. AR is a **volume overload** disease — chamber dilation precedes dysfunction.

## 6. Aortic Root Assessment
- **TTE** initial — measure annulus, sinuses, STJ, ascending aorta
- **CT / MRI** — accurate sizing, especially for **Marfan, bicuspid, dissection** surveillance
- **Surgery also indicated** when root diameter:
  - Bicuspid: ≥55 mm (or ≥50 mm with risk factors)
  - Marfan: ≥45 mm (or ≥40 with risk factors)
  - Other connective tissue: 40–50 mm depending on aetiology

## 7. Special Echo Views
- **Parasternal LAX** — best for VC width
- **Apical 5-chamber / 3-chamber** — for jet area, PISA
- **Suprasternal / high parasternal** — arch flow reversal
- **Subcostal** — for descending aorta diastolic reversal
- **TOE** — better for cusp perforation, prolapse, dissection flap, prosthetic AR

## 8. Stress Testing
- **Asymptomatic severe AR with normal LV** — exercise echo to unmask symptoms / LVEF fall
- **Cardiac MRI** for **regurgitant fraction** when echo is borderline
- **BNP / NT-proBNP** — rising levels predict decompensation

## 9. CMR — When Echo Discordant
- **Regurgitant fraction > 33%** = significant AR
- **Phase-contrast** of aortic and pulmonary flow — difference = RVol
- Accurate LV volumes, EF — avoids measurement noise
- Class IIa in **chronic AR with suboptimal echo windows**

## 10. Cardiac Catheterisation
- **Aortography** — Sellers grade 1–4 (1 mild, 4 severe)
- Now rarely needed for AR alone — used preoperatively for **CAD assessment**
- Useful in **endocarditis with abscess** or **suspected dissection**

## 11. Integrating Data
A **definite severe AR** requires multiple parameters pointing the same way:
- VC >6 mm + PHT <200 ms + holodiastolic aortic reversal = severe
- If discordant → **CMR, TOE, repeat TTE**
- Final decision = severity + LV response + symptoms + aetiology (root, valve, dissection)

> **Pearls**
> - **VC > 6 mm** is the most reliable single linear measure of severity
> - **PHT < 200 ms** = severe (rapid pressure equalisation)
> - **Holodiastolic flow reversal** in descending aorta = severe
> - Always assess the **aortic root** — root surgery may dominate AVR decision
> - Surgery indicated for **symptoms, LVEF <50%, LVESD >50 mm**
