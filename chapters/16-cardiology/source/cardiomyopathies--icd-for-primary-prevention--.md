# HCM — ICD for Primary Prevention

## 1. Why ICD
- **SCD is the most feared complication** of HCM, often the first manifestation in young patients
- ICDs do not prevent arrhythmia — they **terminate VT/VF** after onset
- ICDs are the only therapy proven to **reduce SCD mortality** in HCM

## 2. Risk Stratification Drives ICD Decision
### ESC 2014/2022 — HCM Risk-SCD Score
5-yr SCD risk calculated from: **age, max wall thickness, LA diameter, LVOT gradient, family SCD, NSVT, syncope**

| 5-yr Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| <4% | ICD not indicated |
| 4–6% | Consider (shared decision) |
| ≥6% | ICD recommended |

### AHA/ACC 2020 — ≥1 of:
- Family SCD in 1st-degree relative <50 yrs
- Max wall thickness **≥30 mm**
- Unexplained syncope
- NSVT on Holter (≥3 beats, ≥120 bpm)
- Apical aneurysm
- LGE **≥15%** of LV mass
- Abnormal BP response to exercise (age <40)

## 3. Secondary Prevention
- Survivors of **cardiac arrest** or **sustained VT** → **Class I** ICD
- Documented VF/VT with haemodynamic compromise

## 4. Device Selection
- **Single-chamber ICD** preferred (lower complication, longer battery)
- **Dual-chamber** if AV block, sinus node dysfunction, or need for AT/AF detection
- **CRT-D** if LVEF <35% + LBBB + symptoms (rare end-stage HCM)
- **Subcutaneous ICD (S-ICD)** option — no pacing indication, lower infection risk; screening required (sensing vector)

## 5. Programming to Reduce Shocks
- **VT zone ≥188 bpm** (monitor, no therapy)
- **VF zone ≥250 bpm** (shock)
- Long detection time (≥30 intervals) to reduce inappropriate shocks
- **Discriminators:** AF vs VT (irregularity, dissociated atrial activity)

## 6. Complications
- **Lead-related:** pneumothorax 1%, lead displacement 1–2%, lead fracture 0.5–1%/yr
- **Infection:** 1–2% (higher if generator change)
- **Inappropriate shocks:** 4–5%/yr (lead noise, AF, oversensing)
- **Generator change** every 5–9 yrs
- **Psychological impact:** anxiety, device dependence (offer support)

## 7. Special Populations
- **Children:** higher lead complications; consider S-ICD in larger children
- **Athletes:** ICD does not preclude exercise but counsel on contact sports
- **Pregnancy:** ICD safe; peripartum EP review
- **End-stage HCM** (LVEF <50%): reassess — transplant may be more appropriate

> **Pearls**
> - ICD indicated when **5-yr SCD risk ≥6%** (ESC) or **any high-risk feature** (AHA/ACC).
> - **Single-chamber** is standard unless pacing indication.
> - **Long detection + high VT zone** to minimise inappropriate shocks.
> - ICD complications occur in **~1–2% per year**; counsel patients carefully.
> - **S-ICD** is an option when no pacing is required.
