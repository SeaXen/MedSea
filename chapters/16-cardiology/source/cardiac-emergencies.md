## 1. Introduction
Cardiac emergencies are the **highest-stakes scenarios in medicine**: STEMI, cardiogenic shock, lethal arrhythmias, tamponade, aortic dissection, massive PE, and post-cardiac arrest. The first **"golden hour"** decides survival. Systematic **ABCDE assessment + time-critical bundles** (PCI, defibrillation, thrombolysis, surgical referral) are the framework.

### The "Don't Miss Six" Cardiac Emergencies
- **STEMI** · **Cardiogenic shock** · **Lethal arrhythmia** (VF / pVT / Torsades) · **Cardiac tamponade** · **Aortic dissection (Type A)** · **Massive PE**

### Universal Initial Bundle
- **O₂** if SpO₂ <90% · **2 large-bore IVs** · **Cardiac monitor + 12-lead ECG** · **IV access + bloods (troponin, FBC, U&E, coags, ABG, lactate)** · **Aspirin 300 mg PO** if ACS suspected · **Call for help early**

## 2. Pathophysiology

### Cardiogenic Shock
**Pump failure → ↓CO → ↓BP → ↓coronary perfusion → worsening ischaemia** — a vicious spiral. Causes: ACS (most common), mechanical complications (VSR, papillary rupture, free wall), myocarditis, end-stage CM, tamponade, massive PE, aortic dissection.

### Lethal Arrhythmias
- **VF / pVT** — disorganised ventricular depolarisation, no CO
- **Torsades de Pointes** — polymorphic VT with QT prolongation (drugs, electrolytes, congenital LQTS)
- **Complete heart block** — no AV conduction, bradycardia → syncope, low CO

### Cardiac Tamponade
Pericardial fluid → impaired diastolic filling → equalisation of diastolic pressures → ↓CO → obstructive shock. Pulsus paradoxus is the bedside hallmark.

### Aortic Dissection
Intimal tear → false lumen → may rupture into pericardium (tamponade) or pleura (haemothorax) or compromise coronary ostia / branch vessels.

### Massive PE
Sudden RV outflow obstruction → RV failure → obstructive shock + cardiac arrest (PEA).

## 3. Classification

### Cardiogenic Shock — SCAI Staging (2019)
| Stage | Description | Haemodynamics | Management |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A — At risk** | No signs/symptoms | Normal BP | Monitor, optimise |
| **B — Beginning** | Hypotension, no hypoperfusion | SBP <90, ↑HR, normal lactate | Inotropes (dobutamine, milrinone) |
| **C — Classic** | Hypoperfusion | Cool, lactate >2, AKI, ↑LFTs | **IABP** + inotropes, early MCS |
| **D — Deteriorating** | Worsening despite initial | Escalating vasopressors | **Impella CP / 5.0**, escalate |
| **E — Extremis** | Cardiac arrest or near-arrest | CPR, ECMO-CPR | **VA-ECMO ± Impella (ECPELLA)** |

### Acute Aortic Syndrome
- **Type A (Stanford)**: ascending aorta involved → **surgery**
- **Type B (Stanford)**: descending only → medical ± TEVAR

### Arrhythmia Severity
| Tier | Examples | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Immediately life-threatening** | VF, pVT, asystole, PEA | **ALS / ACLS** |
| **Unstable** | SVT / AF / VT with hypotension / chest pain / HF | **Synchronised cardioversion** |
| **Stable but urgent** | Torsades (long QT), bradycardia + low CO | IV Mg / atropine / pacing |
| **Stable** | AF / SVT / AT | Vagal → adenosine → β-blocker / CCB |

## 4. Clinical Features — Recognition

### STEMI
- **ST elevation ≥1 mm in ≥2 contiguous leads** (≥2 mm V2–V3 men, ≥1.5 mm women); new LBBB
- Persistent chest pain >20 min, diaphoresis, nausea
- Complications: **VF/VT, cardiogenic shock, free wall / papillary / septal rupture**

### Cardiogenic Shock
- **SBP <90 mmHg** · **oliguria** · **cool, mottled peripheries** · **confusion** · **↑lactate >2**
- ↑JVP, pulmonary oedema (wet/cold), or hypovolaemic (dry/cold)

### VF / pVT
- **Sudden collapse**, no pulse, no breathing, chaotic ECG

### Torsades
- Recurrent syncope, polymorphic VT with **"twisting of the points"** around isoelectric line; QTc >500 ms

### Tamponade
- **Beck's triad:** hypotension, muffled heart sounds, ↑JVP
- **Pulsus paradoxus** (>10 mmHg drop in SBP on inspiration)
- Dyspnoea, tachycardia, pulsus, pericardial rub if inflammatory

### Aortic Dissection
- **Tearing chest / back pain**, **BP differential >20 mmHg** between arms, **pulse deficit**, syncope, new AI murmur
- Type A: anterior chest, can mimic ACS — **always image before thrombolysis**

### Massive PE
- Sudden dyspnoea, pleuritic pain, syncope, **hypotension**, **RV strain on ECG** (S1Q3T3, RBBB, T-inversion V1–V4)
- Differential with STEMI: more dyspnoea, less chest pain, no ST elevation (mostly)

## 5. Investigations — Time-Critical

| Emergency | Test | Time Target |
|---|---|---|
| **STEMI** | 12-lead ECG | **Within 10 min** of arrival |
| **Troponin** | High-sensitivity | Result <60 min |
| **Cardiogenic shock** | Echo (bedside), ABG, lactate | Immediate |
| **Tamponade** | Bedside echo | Immediate |
| **Aortic dissection** | **CT angio** chest-abdo-pelvis | <30 min |
| **Massive PE** | **CTPA** (if stable) / bedside echo + ECG | Immediate |
| **Lethal arrhythmia** | Continuous ECG / defibrillator pads | Immediate |

### Adjuncts
- **D-dimer** sensitive but non-specific (rule out low-risk PE / dissection)
- **CXR** — mediastinal widening, pneumothorax, pulmonary oedema
- **ABG** — lactate, pH, K⁺
- **Bedside echo** — tamponade, RV dilatation, EF, wall motion
- **Lactate clearance** — guides resuscitation

## 6. Differential Diagnosis — Cardiac Arrest Rhythms

| Rhythm | Cause | Management |
|---|---|---|
| **VF / pVT** | Ischaemia, electrolyte, Long QT, HOCM | **Defibrillation 200 J biphasic** + ACLS |
| **Asystole** | Severe ischaemia, prolonged arrest, agonal | CPR + Adrenaline; **4H 4T** reversible causes |
| **PEA** | Tamponade, tension pneumothorax, PE, hypovolaemia, drugs, **5H 5T** | CPR + Adrenaline; treat reversible cause |
| **Sinus bradycardia → asystole** | High vagal tone, AV block, MI (inferior), drugs (β-blocker) | Atropine → pacing |

> **The 4 H's:** Hypoxia · Hypovolaemia · Hypo-/Hyper-kalaemia · Hypothermia
> **The 4 T's:** Tension pneumothorax · **Tamponade (cardiac)** · **Thrombosis (PE / coronary)** · Toxins

---

## 7. Complications
- **Death / cardiac arrest** (untreated VF, shock, dissection)
- **Anoxic brain injury** (post-arrest)
- **Multi-organ failure** (shock, prolonged hypoperfusion)
- **Re-perfusion injury** (post-PCI)
- **Mechanical MI complications:** free wall / papillary / septal rupture (3–7 d)
- **Stroke** (AF, mural thrombus, dissection)
- **Bleeding** (anticoagulation, ECMO, IABP)
- **AKI** (contrast, shock, sepsis)

## 8. Management

### A. STEMI
| Step | Target |
|---|---|
| **MONA-B** | Aspirin 300 mg, Ticagrelor 180 mg, Anticoag (UFH), β-blocker, Statin |
| **Primary PCI** | **Door-to-balloon <90 min**, first-medical-contact-to-device <120 min |
| **Fibrinolysis** | If PCI >120 min: **Tenecteplase** (weight-adjusted single bolus) / Alteplase / Streptokinase |
| **Rescue PCI** | If fibrinolysis fails (ST resolution <50% at 60–90 min) |
| **CABG** | Left main / 3-vessel disease, mechanical complications |
| **Avoid** | Thrombolysis if dissection possible (BP diff, TTE) |

### B. Cardiogenic Shock (SCAI)
| Stage | MCS Escalation |
|---|---|
| A–B | Monitor, fluid, inotropes (dobutamine / milrinone / noradrenaline) |
| C | **IABP** + inotropes (though IABP-SHOCK II neutral) |
| D | **Impella CP / 5.0** + inotropes |
| E | **VA-ECMO ± Impella (ECPELLA)** for biventricular failure |

> Culprit-only PCI in multivessel shock (CULPRIT-SHOCK).

### C. ACLS / Lethal Arrhythmias
| Rhythm | Action |
|---|---|
| **VF / pVT** | **Defib 200 J biphasic → CPR 2 min → Adrenaline 1 mg IV → repeat q3–5 min → Amiodarone 300 mg** after 3rd shock |
| **Asystole / PEA** | CPR 2 min → **Adrenaline 1 mg IV** q3–5 min → **treat reversible cause (4H 4T)** |
| **Torsades** | **IV MgSO₄ 2 g bolus** + isoprenaline / overdrive pacing 90–110 bpm; correct K⁺ >4.5; stop QT drugs |
| **Stable SVT** | Vagal → **Adenosine 6 mg → 12 mg → 12 mg** (rapid IV push + flush) |
| **Unstable SVT / VT** | **Synchronised cardioversion** 120–200 J (biphasic) |
| **Bradycardia** | **Atropine 0.5 mg IV** (max 3 mg) → transcutaneous pacing → transvenous pacemaker / isoprenaline |
| **Complete heart block** | Atropine → pacing → permanent pacemaker |

### D. Cardiac Tamponade
- **Emergency pericardiocentesis** (echo-guided, subxiphoid) — drain in 50–100 mL aliquots
- Pericardial window if recurrent / malignant / uraemic
- **Avoid** inotropes alone; **volume expansion** as bridge

### E. Aortic Dissection
| Type | Management |
|---|---|
| **Type A** | **Emergency open surgery** (Bentall / valve-sparing / arch replacement) |
| **Type B (uncomplicated)** | IV β-blocker first → HR <60 → SBP 100–120; then oral |
| **Type B (complicated)** | **TEVAR** (malperfusion, rupture, refractory pain, expanding) |

### F. Massive PE
| Setting | Action |
|---|---|
| **Cardiac arrest** | **Thrombolysis** (Alteplase 50 mg bolus, repeat if no ROSC) + CPR |
| **Haemodynamic instability (no arrest)** | **Systemic thrombolysis** — Alteplase 100 mg over 2 h |
| **Contraindication to lysis** | **Catheter-directed** thrombolysis / suction embolectomy / surgical embolectomy |

### G. Post-Cardiac Arrest Care
- **Targeted Temperature Management (TTM): 32–36 °C × 24 h** (TTM2: 36 °C non-inferior)
- **Coronary angiography** if STEMI / unstable / shockable rhythm
- **Avoid** hyperoxia (SpO₂ 94–98%), hyperventilation (PaCO₂ 35–45), hypotension (MAP >65)
- **Treat seizures** (eclampsia, electrolyte)
- **Neuroprognostication at ≥72 h** (pupils, SSEP, NSE, EEG, MRI)

## 9. Secondary Prevention & Long-term
- **Post-STEMI:** DAPT 12 mo (aspirin + ticagrelor / prasugrel / clopidogrel), high-intensity statin, ACEi, β-blocker
- **Post-shock:** cardiac rehab; address cause (revascularisation, device)
- **Survivors of VF / pVT:** **secondary-prevention ICD** (EF <35% or recurrent); LVEF optimisation
- **Torsades:** stop QT-prolonging drugs; correct electrolytes; β-blocker if congenital LQTS
- **Massive PE:** transition to DOAC / LMWH (3–6 mo minimum, indefinite if unprovoked)
- **Type B dissection:** lifelong β-blocker + surveillance CTA
- **Post-arrest:** cognitive rehab, family support, ICD evaluation
- **Risk-factor control** in all: BP, LDL, HbA1c, smoking, weight, exercise

---

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield

- **STEMI** = primary **PCI <90 min**; if PCI unavailable in 120 min → **fibrinolysis** within 30 min
- **Cardiogenic shock staging: SCAI A → B → C → D → E** with MCS escalation: inotropes → IABP → Impella → VA-ECMO
- **VF / pVT algorithm:** Defib 200 J → CPR 2 min → Adrenaline 1 mg IV q3–5 min → **Amiodarone 300 mg** after 3rd shock
- **Asystole / PEA:** **never defibrillate**; CPR + Adrenaline 1 mg q3–5 min; treat **4H 4T**
- **Torsades de Pointes** = polymorphic VT with QTc >500 ms; **IV Mg 2 g bolus** + isoprenaline / overdrive pacing
- **Complete heart block:** Atropine 0.5 mg IV (max 3 mg) → transcutaneous pacing → transvenous
- **Beck's triad** + **pulsus paradoxus** + echo = **cardiac tamponade** → **emergency pericardiocentesis**
- **Stanford A dissection = EMERGENCY SURGERY**; **Stanford B = medical** (HR <60 first, then SBP 100–120) ± TEVAR
- **Massive PE = thrombolysis** (Alteplase 100 mg / 2 h) — no time for delay
- **Post-ROSC:** TTM **32–36 °C × 24 h**, avoid hyperoxia + hyperventilation, **coronary angio** if shockable or STEMI
- **Avoid thrombolysis in suspected aortic dissection** — get CT first if any suspicion (BP diff, atypical pain)
- **Mechanical MI complications: 3–7 d post-MI** — free wall rupture (tamponade), papillary rupture (acute MR), septal rupture (new harsh holosystolic murmur with thrill)

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Cardiac emergencies reward **pattern recognition + protocolised action**: ECG in 10 min for STEMI, defibrillation in seconds for VF, pericardiocentesis at the bedside for tamponade, and **never thrombolyse a dissection**. **Time-to-therapy is the strongest predictor of survival** — know the bundles cold, and call for help early.
