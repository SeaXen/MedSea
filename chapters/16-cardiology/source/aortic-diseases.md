## 1. Introduction
The aorta is a high-pressure conduit; disease presents as **acute aortic syndrome (AAS)**, **aneurysm**, or **aortitis**. AAS = **dissection, intramural haematoma (IMH), penetrating aortic ulcer (PAU), rupture** — all present with severe tearing chest/back pain and are **lethal if missed**.

### Aortic Anatomy
- **Ascending aorta** → arch vessels (3 branches) → **descending thoracic** → abdominal (at diaphragm, T12)
- **Vasa vasorum** supplies outer media; **intima + inner media** avascular → vulnerable to cystic medial degeneration
- **Cystic medial necrosis / degeneration** = loss of elastic + smooth muscle → predisposition to aneurysm & dissection

### Risk Factors
- **Non-modifiable:** Age (>60–70) · Male sex · Bicuspid aortic valve (BAV) · Connective tissue disease (Marfan, Loeys-Dietz, Ehlers-Danlos)
- **Modifiable:** Hypertension (strongest) · Smoking · Dyslipidaemia · Atherosclerosis
- **Other:** Trauma · Iatrogenic (catheter, IABP, cardiac surgery) · Cocaine · Pregnancy (3rd tri) · Giant cell / Takayasu arteritis

## 2. Pathophysiology

### Aortic Dissection
**Intimal tear** → blood enters media → splits into **true lumen + false lumen** → may propagate antegrade or retrograde → branch vessel ischaemia.

### Intramural Haematoma (IMH)
**No intimal tear** — rupture of vasa vasorum → haemorrhage within media → crescentic wall thickening. Can evolve into dissection.

### Penetrating Aortic Ulcer (PAU)
Atherosclerotic ulcer **erodes through intima into media** — usually descending aorta in elderly hypertensives.

### Aneurysm
**Localised dilatation >1.5× normal** (ascending >5 cm, abdominal >3 cm). True (all 3 layers) vs false (contained rupture). Cystic medial degeneration + proteolytic remodelling (MMP-2, MMP-9).

---

## 3. Classification

### Stanford (clinical — drives treatment)
| Type | Involvement | Management |
|---|---|---|
| **Type A** | **Ascending aorta** ± descending | **EMERGENCY SURGERY** |
| **Type B** | **Descending only** (distal to LSCA) | Medical ± TEVAR if complicated |

### DeBakey (anatomical)
- **Type I** — Ascending + arch + descending
- **Type II** — Ascending only
- **Type III** — Descending only (± IIIa = above diaphragm, IIIb = below)

### Aneurysm by Location
- **TAA** (thoracic): ascending > root > arch > descending
- **AAA** (abdominal): infrarenal (90%) > juxtarenal
- **Thoracoabdominal** (Crawford I–V)

---

## 4. Clinical Features

### Acute Aortic Syndrome
- **Sudden "tearing" / "ripping"** chest or back pain (Type A = anterior chest; Type B = interscapular / back)
- **Pulse deficit** (30% Type A), **BP differential >20 mmHg** between arms
- **Aortic regurgitation murmur** (40% Type A) — acute AI
- Neurological: stroke, paraplegia (spinal ischaemia), syncope, Hoarseness (recurrent laryngeal)
- Hypotension / shock (rupture, tamponade); can mimic ACS, PE

### Aneurysm
- Usually **asymptomatic** — found incidentally on CXR / US / CT
- Expanding: deep, boring pain in chest / back / abdomen
- **AAA:** pulsatile epigastric mass, hypotension if ruptured
- Compression: SVC, RLN, bronchi

### Aortitis
- Systemic symptoms (fever, weight loss, malaise) + chest/back pain
- **GCA:** elderly, headache, jaw claudication, ↑ESR, polymyalgia
- **Takayasu:** young female, absent pulses, BP discrepancy, bruit

---

## 5. Investigations

### Imaging
- **CT angiography (CTA)** — **gold standard** for dissection & aneurysm; defines anatomy for surgery
- **TEE** — bedside, unstable patient, sensitivity ~98%
- **MRI/MRA** — young patients, surveillance (no radiation)
- **CXR** — widened mediastinum, pleural effusion, double-knuckle sign
- **Abdominal US** — AAA screening & surveillance

### Labs
- **D-dimer ↑↑** in acute dissection (sensitive, not specific; cut-off 500 ng/mL)
- CBC, urea, creatinine, troponin, lactate, ABG, coags
- **Blood cultures** + ESR / CRP (aortitis)
- **Quartile lactate** & base deficit (rupture)

### Aortitis workup
- TB (IGRA), syphilis (VDRL), Salmonella, IgG4, ANA, ANCA, RF

## 6. Differential Diagnosis (AAS Mimics)

| Condition | Key Features | Distinguishing Test |
|---|---|---|
| **Aortic Dissection** | Tearing pain → back, BP differential | CT angio / TEE — intimal flap |
| **Acute MI / STEMI** | Pressure-like, diaphoresis | ECG ST ↑ + ↑troponin (**avoid thrombolysis — get CT first**) |
| **Pulmonary embolism** | Pleuritic, dyspnea, hypoxia | CTPA, ↑D-dimer, S1Q3T3 |
| **Pericarditis** | Pleuritic, lean forward, rub | Diffuse ST ↑ + PR depression |
| **Tension pneumothorax** | Sudden dyspnea, unilateral | Tracheal deviation, hyperresonance |
| **Oesophageal rupture (Boerhaave)** | After vomiting, subcutaneous emphysema | CXR mediastinal air, gastrografin swallow |
| **Musculoskeletal** | Reproducible, positional, no haemodynamic change | Normal CT, normal troponin |

> **Rule out dissection BEFORE thrombolysis or antiplatelets in suspected STEMI.**

---

## 7. Complications

### Dissection
- **Aortic regurgitation** (ascending involvement) — acute AI
- **Cardiac tamponade** (rupture into pericardium — most common cause of death)
- **MI** (coronary ostial involvement, usually RCA)
- **Stroke** (carotid involvement)
- **Paraplegia** (spinal artery, Type B)
- **Aortic rupture** → exsanguination
- **Malperfusion syndromes** (mesenteric, renal, limb)

### Aneurysm
- **Rupture** → mortality 80–90% pre-hospital
- **Dissection**, **thromboembolism**, **compression** of adjacent structures, **aorto-caval / aorto-enteric fistula**

---

## 8. Management

### Acute Aortic Syndrome — Initial
| Action | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Analgesia** | IV morphine / fentanyl |
| **HR control first** | **IV beta-blocker** — esmolol or labetalol (target HR <60) |
| **BP control** | Add nitroprusside / nicardipine (target SBP 100–120) — only **after** HR controlled |
| **Imaging** | CTA chest-abdomen-pelvis (or TEE if unstable) |
| **Type A** | **Emergency open surgical repair** (Bentall if root + valve involved; valve-sparing if valve OK) |
| **Type B (uncomplicated)** | Medical therapy in HDU/ICU |
| **Type B (complicated)** | **TEVAR** — malperfusion, rupture, refractory pain / HTN |
| **Imaging follow-up** | CTA at 1, 6, 12 months then yearly |

### Aneurysm Surveillance & Repair Thresholds
| Location | Threshold | Preferred Repair |
|---|---|---|
| **Ascending / root (general)** | **≥55 mm** | Open (Bentall / valve-sparing) |
| **BAV without risk** | **≥55 mm** | Open |
| **Marfan** | **≥50 mm** (45 if FHx / rapid) | Open |
| **Loeys-Dietz** | **≥42 mm** | Open |
| **Descending thoracic** | **≥60 mm** (55 if risk) | TEVAR |
| **AAA (infrarenal)** | **≥5.5 cm** (men), 5.0 (women) | **EVAR** (if anatomy suitable) / open |
| **Rapid growth** | **>5 mm/yr** | Consider earlier repair |

> **EVAR = endovascular aneurysm repair** · **TEVAR = thoracic EVAR**

---

## 9. Secondary Prevention & Surveillance
- **Lifelong BP control** <130/80 mmHg · **HR <60** bpm
- **Beta-blocker** first line (reduces dP/dt, shear stress)
- **ACEi/ARB** + **statin** (atherosclerosis prevention)
- **Smoking cessation** (single most impactful modifiable risk)
- **Surveillance imaging** (CT/MRI at 1, 6, 12 months → yearly for Type B dissection; annual for small aneurysms)
- **Avoid** isometric exercise, heavy lifting, cocaine, sympathomimetics
- **Pregnancy**: pre-conception counselling in Marfan / Loeys-Dietz (aortic root <40 mm generally safe; >45 mm → repair before pregnancy)
- **Lifestyle:** Mediterranean diet, exercise (isotonic)

---

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield

- **Stanford A = Ascending = Surgery** (mortality 1–2% per hour untreated)
- **DeBakey 1 = Ascending + Descending; 2 = Ascending only; 3 = Descending only**
- **D-dimer** can help rule out dissection (low risk + negative D-dimer → low probability)
- **CXR** in dissection: **widened mediastinum** (>8 cm), pleural effusion, apical cap
- **Type B management: HR <60 first with IV beta-blocker, then SBP 100–120**
- **Marfan** (FBN1) — aortic root surgery at **50 mm** (45 mm if risk)
- **Loeys-Dietz** (TGFBR1/2) — much more aggressive: surgery at **42 mm**
- **BAV** — aortopathy is independent of valve severity; screen first-degree relatives
- **AAA screening** — one-time US in men 65–75 who have ever smoked (USPSTF)
- **AAA** is NOT a single disease: 90% infrarenal; **EVAR** preferred if anatomy suitable
- **Aortitis:** **Takayasu** (young Asian female, pulseless) vs **GCA** (>50, headache, jaw claudication, ↑ESR)
- **Always exclude dissection before giving thrombolytics, antiplatelets, or anticoagulants** in suspected STEMI with BP differential / atypical pain

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Aortic dissection kills 1–2% per hour in Type A — recognition must be instant. CTA within 30 min, HR <60 with IV beta-blocker, and immediate surgical referral for Type A are the three life-saving actions. **Never thrombolyse a Type A — it worsens rupture risk.**
