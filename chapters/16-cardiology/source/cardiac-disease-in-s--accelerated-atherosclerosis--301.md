# Accelerated Atherosclerosis

## 1. Definition
Atherosclerosis occurring at **younger age** (<55 in men, <65 in women) or in the context of systemic inflammatory disease (HIV, SLE, RA).

## 2. Causes
- **Familial hypercholesterolemia** (LDL receptor, APOB, PCSK9)
- **HIV** — direct + ART effects (protease inhibitors)
- **SLE, RA, psoriasis** — chronic inflammation
- **CKD** — uremic milieu
- **T1DM** (especially with nephropathy)
- **Radiation** to chest

## 3. Diagnosis
- **Coronary CTA** or **invasive angio**
- Aggressive risk factor screening
- **FH cascade screening** if suspected

## 4. Management
- **Aggressive LDL lowering** (target <1.4 mmol/L or <1.0 in very high risk)
- **Statin** + **ezetimibe** + **PCSK9 inhibitor** if needed
- **Lp(a) testing** (consider)
- Treat underlying condition (HIV, SLE)
- **Lifestyle** — diet, exercise, smoking cessation

## 5. Outcomes
- High recurrence after revascularization
- Aggressive prevention improves outcomes
- **Family screening** for FH (autosomal dominant)

> **Pearls** — Suspect in MI <55 (men) / <65 (women). Check **Lp(a), FH workup**. **Triple therapy** for LDL (statin + ezetimibe + PCSK9). Treat underlying inflammatory disease.
