# Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy (ARVC)

## 1. Definition
ARVC is an **inherited cardiomyopathy** characterised by **fibro-fatty replacement of the RV (and sometimes LV)**, predisposing to **VT of RV origin, SCD**, particularly in young athletes.

## 2. Genetics
- **Autosomal dominant** with incomplete penetrance
- **Desmosomal mutations:** **PKP2** (most common, ~40%), **DSP**, **DSG2**, **DSC2**, **JUP**
- Non-desmosomal: **TMEM43, PLN, TGFβ3, RYR2** (overlap with CPVT)
- **PKP2** — most common in Caucasians

## 3. Pathophysiology
- Desmosome dysfunction → myocyte detachment under mechanical stress
- Apoptosis + replacement by **fibro-fatty tissue**
- Creates re-entrant VT circuit, especially in "triangle of dysplasia" (inflow, outflow, apex)

## 4. 2010 Revised Task Force Criteria (TFC)
Diagnosis is **definite / borderline / possible** based on major + minor criteria in 6 categories:
1. **Global/regional RV dysfunction** (echo, MRI, angiography)
2. **Tissue characterisation** (fibro-fatty replacement on biopsy or CMR)
3. **Repolarisation abnormalities** (TWI V1–V3 in >14 yrs, in absence of RBBB)
4. **Depolarisation abnormalities** (epsilon wave, terminal activation duration)
5. **Arrhythmias** (LBBB VT, ≥500 PVCs/24 h)
6. **Family history** (autopsy, genetic)

### ECG
- **TWI V1–V3** (>14 yrs, in absence of RBBB) — major
- **Epsilon wave** (small deflection at end of QRS in V1–V3) — major
- **Localised prolongation** of QRS ≥110 ms in V1–V3
- **LBBB VT** with inferior axis — classic

### Imaging
- **Echo:** RV dilatation, RV akinesia/dyskinesia, RVEF <45%
- **CMR:** RV regional wall motion abnormalities, RV fatty infiltration (LGE), RVEF ≤40%

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
- **Idiopathic RVOT VT** — normal RV, no structural disease
- **Sarcoidosis** — pulmonary/systemic features, FDG-PET
- **Biventricular ARVC (ACM)** — LV involvement, FLNC/DSP
- **Dilated cardiomyopathy** with biventricular failure

## 6. Management
### Lifestyle
- **Avoid competitive / endurance sports** (Class I) — major trigger for arrhythmia
- Restrict to low-intensity (recreational walking, golf)

### Device
- **ICD** for: cardiac arrest survivor, sustained VT, syncope with VT, or major risk factors
- High inappropriate shock risk (T-wave oversensing) — use dual-coil, careful programming

### Antiarrhythmic
- **β-blocker** first-line
- **Sotalol** or **amiodarone** for VT suppression
- **Catheter ablation** for recurrent VT (often epicardial approach)
- High recurrence rate — multiple procedures often needed

### Family Screening
- ECG, echo, Holter, ± CMR, ± genetic testing in 1st-degree relatives
- Repeat every 1–3 years during adolescence

## 7. Prognosis
- 5–20% SCD risk in symptomatic probands without ICD
- **Earlier presentation (age <35) + male + extensive disease** = higher risk
- LV involvement worsens prognosis

> **Pearls**
> - **PKP2** is the most common gene (~40%).
> - **2010 Task Force Criteria** integrate imaging, ECG, arrhythmias, family history.
> - **Epsilon wave + TWI V1–V3** are classic ECG findings.
> - **Avoid competitive sports** — exercise accelerates disease and triggers VT.
> - **Catheter ablation** of VT often needs **epicardial** approach (high recurrence).
