# Mechanical complications of MI

## 1. Overview
Mechanical complications of acute MI are **life-threatening** and typically occur **3–7 days** post-infarction (rarely within 24 h). Highest risk with large anterior MI, delayed reperfusion, advanced age, female sex, HTN, diabetes.

## 2. Free Wall Rupture
- **Timing**: 3–7 days (peaks 5 days); also subacute (pericardial effusion → tamponade)
- **Risk factors**: Advanced age, HTN, first MI, late presentation
- **Presentation**: Sudden hemodynamic collapse, pulseless electrical activity (PEA), tamponade
- **Diagnosis**: Echo (pericardial effusion with tamponade physiology)
- **Management**: **Emergency pericardiocentesis** as bridge → **emergency surgical repair**

## 3. Ventricular Septal Rupture (VSR)
- **Timing**: 3–5 days
- **Location**: Anteroapical (anterior MI) or inferior (inferior MI)
- **Presentation**: New harsh holosystolic murmur, acute RV failure, biventricular failure
- **Diagnosis**: Echo with color Doppler (shunt), step-up in O₂ saturation (RV>RA)
- **Management**: Stabilize with **IABP, diuretics, afterload reduction** → **surgical repair** (delayed 3–4 weeks if possible)

## 4. Papillary Muscle Rupture
- **Timing**: 3–5 days
- **Most common**: Posteromedial papillary muscle (single blood supply from PDA) — inferior MI
- **Presentation**: Acute severe MR → pulmonary edema, shock
- **Diagnosis**: Echo (flail leaflet, severe MR, normal LV size)
- **Management**: **Emergency MVR/mitral repair** + IABP bridge

## 5. Other Complications
- **Cardiac tamponade**: From free wall rupture or hemorrhagic pericarditis
- **LV aneurysm**: Late complication (weeks-months); persistent ST elevation; treat with anticoagulation ± surgery
- **Pseudoaneurysm**: Contained rupture; thin-walled; high rupture risk → surgery
- **Mural thrombus**: Stasis in akinetic/dyskinetic segment; anticoagulate 3–6 months
- **Dressler syndrome**: Autoimmune pericarditis 2–10 weeks post-MI

## 6. Key Distinguishing Features
| Complication | Murmur | Echo Finding |
|--------------|--------|--------------|
| VSR | Harsh holosystolic, L sternal border | Shunt on color Doppler |
| Papillary rupture | Soft mid-systolic or silent | Severe MR, flail leaflet |
| Free wall rupture | None | Effusion, tamponade |

> **Pearls**
> - New loud murmur + hemodynamic collapse post-MI = **mechanical complication until proven otherwise**
> - **VSR and papillary rupture both need emergency surgery** — IABP as bridge
> - Pseudoaneurysm has **no myocardium** in wall (only pericardium) — does not contain true aneurysm
> - LV aneurysm: persistent ST elevation, paradoxical pulse, risk of mural thrombus → anticoagulate
