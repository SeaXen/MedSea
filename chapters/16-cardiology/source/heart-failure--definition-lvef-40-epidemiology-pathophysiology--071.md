# HFrEF: Definition, Epidemiology & Pathophysiology

<callout icon="🫀" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**HFrEF** = clinical syndrome of HF with **LVEF ≤40%**. Two pillars of diagnosis: (1) **symptoms/signs** of HF (dyspnoea, fatigue, oedema) AND (2) **objective evidence** of cardiac dysfunction (reduced LVEF on echo, ↑BNP/NT-proBNP). ~50% of all HF cases.

## 2. Epidemiology
- Prevalence ~1–2% of adults, **>10% over 70 y**
- Lifetime risk ~20% in those aged ≥40
- Commoner in men, African-Caribbean ethnicity
- Ischaemic aetiology accounts for **60–70%** of cases in the West
- 5-year mortality ~50%; prognosis worse than many cancers

## 3. Etiology
| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Ischaemic** | Prior MI, chronic ischaemia (most common) |
| **Dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM)** | Idiopathic, genetic (TTN, LMNA), viral, toxic (alcohol, chemo — anthracyclines, trastuzumab, cyclophosphamide) |
| **Valvular** | Late MR, AR, AS |
| **Tachycardia-mediated** | Chronic AF with rapid VR, SVT |
| **Inflammatory** | Myocarditis, peripartum, autoimmune |
| **Infiltrative** | Amyloid, sarcoid, haemochromatosis |
| **Metabolic** | Thyrotoxicosis, thiamine deficiency (wet beriberi) |

## 4. Pathophysiology
**Initial insult** (ischaemia, toxin, infection) → myocyte loss → **neurohormonal activation** (RAAS, SNS, ADH, endothelin) → vasoconstriction, Na⁺/H₂O retention → ↑preload and afterload → **adverse remodelling** (eccentric hypertrophy, dilation, fibrosis, MR annular dilation) → progressive pump failure. **Vicious cycle**: ↓CO → RAAS/SNS → vasoconstriction, retention → ↑preload, afterload → further ↓CO.

> **Vicious cycle:** Pump failure begets neurohormonal activation, which begets further pump failure.

## 5. Clinical Clues to Severity
- **NYHA III/IV** symptoms, S3 gallop, **elevated JVP**
- **BNP/NT-proBNP** ↑↑; troponin may be detectable
- Echo: LVEF ≤40%, LV dilation, functional MR, ↑PASP
- **MAGGIC**, **Seattle Heart Failure Model** for prognosis

## 6. Management Snapshot
- **4 pillars of GDMT** (all class I, mortality benefit): ARNI/ACEi/ARB + Beta-blocker + MRA + SGLT2i
- **Diuretics** for congestion (no mortality benefit)
- **ICD** if LVEF ≤35%, NYHA II–III, on GDMT ≥3 mo, life expectancy >1 y
- **CRT** if LBBB + QRS ≥150 ms + LVEF ≤35% + NYHA II–IV
- Treat cause: revascularisation, valve repair, abstinence, rate control

> **Pearls**
> - HFrEF is **not just a low EF** — must have symptoms/signs + objective evidence
> - **4 GDMT pillars** all reduce mortality and hospitalisation
> - **SGLT2i** work across the full EF spectrum
> - Ischaemic aetiology is the most common cause — always check coronaries
> - **ACEi/ARB + MRA** → monitor K⁺ and renal function (hyperkalaemia in 5–10%)

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** HFrEF is a chronic, progressive, lethal syndrome. Early initiation and up-titration of the **4 GDMT pillars** is the single biggest advance in modern HF care. STRONG-HF (2022) showed rapid up-titration within 2 weeks of discharge reduces readmission and mortality.
</callout>
