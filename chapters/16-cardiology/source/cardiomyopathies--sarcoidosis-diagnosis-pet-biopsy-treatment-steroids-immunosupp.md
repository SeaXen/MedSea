# Cardiac Sarcoidosis — Diagnosis & Treatment

## 1. Definition
**Sarcoidosis** is a multisystem granulomatous disease. **Cardiac involvement (CS)** is rare clinically (5%) but found in **~25% on autopsy/CMR**. It is a major cause of **AV block, VT, and SCD in young adults**.

## 2. Clinical Features
- **AV block** (often 2°/3°) in young patient
- **Sustained VT** of LV origin
- **Heart failure** (systolic or restrictive pattern)
- Sudden cardiac death
- **Sick sinus syndrome** in young

## 3. ECG
- AV block (often high-degree)
- RBBB, fascicular blocks
- **VT of LV origin** (RBBB morphology, inferior axis)
- T-wave inversions
- Q waves (mimic MI)

## 4. Echo
- Regional wall motion abnormalities (basal septum, lateral wall)
- LV aneurysm
- LV/RV systolic dysfunction
- Wall thickening (oedema) or thinning (scar)

## 5. Cardiac MRI
- **T2 STIR / T2 mapping** — myocardial oedema
- **Native T1 ↑**, ECV ↑
- **LGE pattern** — **mid-wall or epicardial**, often in **basal septum and lateral wall** (NOT coronary territory)
- RV LGE suggests extensive disease

## 6. FDG-PET (Active Inflammation)
- Patient preparation: **high-fat low-carb diet ≥24 h + prolonged fast ≥12 h** to suppress physiological myocardial uptake
- Focal or multifocal FDG uptake in myocardium
- Combined **perfusion + metabolism** patterns:
  - **Normal perfusion + no FDG** = no active disease
  - **Perfusion defect + FDG uptake** = active inflammation
  - **Perfusion defect + no FDG** = scar only

## 7. Diagnostic Criteria (HRS 2014 / JCS 2016)
- Histological: non-caseating granuloma on biopsy
- Clinical: probable CS if (a) extracardiac sarcoid + (b) cardiac involvement on PET/CMR/LGE and (c) other causes excluded
- Definite CS = histological proof (cardiac or extracardiac) + cardiac involvement

## 8. Treatment
### Immunosuppression
- **Prednisone 30–40 mg/day** for 2–4 weeks, taper over 6–12 months
- **Methotrexate** or **azathioprine** as steroid-sparing
- **Mycophenolate** alternative
- Infliximab for refractory

### Device Therapy
- **ICD** for: survived SCD, sustained VT, AV block + LV dysfunction, or significant LGE/PET uptake
- CRT for LBBB + LVEF ≤35%
- WCD as bridge in active inflammation

### Antiarrhythmic
- **Antiarrhythmics** for VT (sotalol, amiodarone)
- **Catheter ablation** for recurrent VT (often epicardial)

### HF GDMT
- Standard four pillars if LVEF <50%

## 9. Monitoring
- PET at 3–6 months after starting therapy
- Echo + ECG + Holter every 3–6 months
- MRI every 1–2 years

> **Pearls**
> - **Cardiac sarcoidosis** presents with AV block, VT, or SCD in young patients.
> - **CMR LGE** = mid-wall/epicardial, often basal septum/lateral wall.
> - **FDG-PET** requires high-fat low-carb + prolonged fast prep.
> - **Prednisone** is first-line; methotrexate/azathioprine for steroid-sparing.
> - **ICD** for SCD survivors, sustained VT, or high-risk features (LV dysfunction + LGE).
