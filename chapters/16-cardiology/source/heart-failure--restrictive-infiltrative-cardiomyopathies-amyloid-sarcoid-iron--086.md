# Restrictive & Infiltrative Cardiomyopathies

<callout icon="🔬" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Amyloid, Sarcoid, Iron Overload — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study note
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definition
**Restrictive cardiomyopathy (RCM)** = non-dilated ventricles with **impaired ventricular filling** (diastolic dysfunction) and **preserved or mildly reduced EF**. Infiltrative diseases (amyloid, sarcoid, iron) replace normal myocardium.

## 2. Cardiac Amyloidosis
**Extracellular deposition** of misfolded protein fibrils. Two main types:

| Type | Notes |
|---|---|
| **AL (light chain)** | Plasma cell dyscrasia; rapid, severe; chemotherapy + autologous SCT |
| **ATTR** (transthyretin) | **Wild-type (ATTRwt)** — elderly, "senile"; **Hereditary (ATTRv)** — TTR mutation (e.g. V122I in African-Caribbean) |

### Diagnosis
- **Echo** — LVH (concentric), **low voltage on ECG** (key clue), speckled myocardium, thick valves, biatrial enlargement, restrictive filling
- **ECG** — low voltage (despite LVH), pseudo-infarct pattern, AF
- **CMR** — diffuse subendocardial LGE, **T1 mapping ↑, ECV >0.4**
- **Bone scintigraphy (DPD/PYP/HMDP)** — **grade 2–3 in ATTR** (Perugini grading)
- **AL must be excluded** by **serum free light chains + immunofixation** before diagnosing ATTR
- **Genetic testing** — TTR sequencing
- **Biopsy** — Congo red (apple-green birefringence) + mass spec typing

### Treatment
- **AL** — chemotherapy (daratumumab-based), SCT
- **ATTR** — **tafamidis 80 mg** (ATTR-ACT trial: ↓mortality, ↓hospitalisation) — wild-type and variant
- **Supportive** — diuretics for congestion; avoid CCB, digoxin (binds amyloid)
- **Avoid** ACEi/ARB (hypotension)
- **Heart transplant** in selected young patients

## 3. Cardiac Sarcoidosis
**Non-caseating granulomas** in myocardium. **Multisystem** (lung, skin, eye, neuro).

### Diagnosis
- **CMR** — basal septal LGE, oedema (T2), RV involvement
- **FDG-PET** — active inflammation (after 24 h high-fat/low-carb + fasting)
- **Cardiac biopsy** — gold standard but low yield
- **Diagnostic criteria** (Heart Rhythm Society) — histology + clinical
- **ECG** — AV block (high-degree), VT, RBBB
- **Echo** — regional WMA, LV dysfunction

### Treatment
- **Corticosteroids** — initial 30–40 mg/day, taper
- **Steroid-sparing** — methotrexate, azathioprine, mycophenolate
- **ICD** if LVEF <35% or VT — high SCD risk
- **Methotrexate or infliximab** for refractory
- **Permanent pacing** for AV block
- **Anticoagulate** if AF or LV aneurysm with thrombus

## 4. Iron Overload (Haemochromatosis)
**Iron deposition** in myocardium (also liver, pancreas, joints, skin, pituitary).

### Etiology
- **Hereditary (HFE) — C282Y, H63D** (autosomal recessive)
- **Secondary** — chronic transfusions (thalassaemia, MDS, sickle cell)

### Diagnosis
- **Iron studies** — ferritin ↑↑, TSAT >45%
- **CMR T2* <20 ms** — gold standard for cardiac iron
- **Echo** — dilated/restrictive pattern
- **LFT** — hepatic involvement
- **HFE gene testing**

### Treatment
- **Venesection** (hereditary) — weekly until ferritin <50
- **Iron chelation** (secondary) — **deferoxamine, deferasirox, deferiprone**
- **Avoid** vitamin C, raw shellfish
- **GDMT** for HF

## 5. Other RCM Causes
- **Endomyocardial fibrosis** (tropical, eosinophilic)
- **Löffler endocarditis** — eosinophilia → thrombus + fibrosis
- **Loeffler / Davies** — endomyocardial fibrosis with eosinophilia
- **Pseudoxanthoma elasticum**
- **Glycogen storage** (Pompe, Danon, PRKAG2)
- **Fabry disease** — X-linked, α-gal A deficiency; **enzyme replacement (agalsidase)**
- **Scleroderma, radiation**

## 6. Differentiating RCM from Constrictive Pericarditis
| Feature | RCM | Constrictive Pericarditis |
|---|---|---|
| Pericardium | Normal | Thick (>3 mm) |
| Septal "bounce" | Absent | Present (respiratory shift) |
| **Resp. variation in mitral inflow** | Absent | Present (>25%) |
| Tissue Doppler e' | Normal/↑ (preserved) | ↓ |
| LV thickness | Often ↑ (amyloid) | Normal |
| Voltage (amyloid) | Low | Normal |

> **Pearls**
> - **Cardiac amyloid** — LVH + **low voltage** + CKD/proteinuria/bilateral carpal tunnel
> - **ATTR** — Perugini grade 2–3 on DPD/PYP + **no monoclonal protein** = diagnostic (no biopsy needed)
> - **Tafamidis** for ATTR-CM (ATTR-ACT)
> - **Always exclude AL first** (light chains + immunofixation) before attributing to ATTR
> - **Cardiac sarcoid** — high-grade AV block in young/middle-aged, FDG-PET
> - **Iron overload** — CMR T2* <20 ms; venesection or chelation
> - **Avoid CCB and digoxin in amyloid**

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Infiltrative cardiomyopathies are **underdiagnosed**. Always consider **ATTR amyloid** in elderly LVH with low voltage, AF, and bilateral carpal tunnel — bone scintigraphy diagnoses without biopsy. Cardiac sarcoid requires **multimodal imaging (CMR + PET)**. Iron overload is reversible with **venesection or chelation**.
</callout>
