# Cardiac Amyloidosis

## 1. Definition
- Extracellular deposition of misfolded proteins in myocardium
- **ATTR** (transthyretin) — wild-type (ATTRwt) or hereditary (ATTRv)
- **AL** (light chain) — most aggressive, plasma cell dyscrasia

## 2. Key Features
- **HFpEF** with thick LV walls (≥13 mm) + **LGE** on CMR (zebra pattern)
- **Low voltage** ECG + thick walls — **voltage-mass mismatch** is classic
- **Apical sparing** on strain (longitudinal strain)
- **Carpal tunnel** + biceps rupture (ATTRwt)
- **MGUS/AL**: anemia, renal dysfunction, fatigue

## 3. Diagnosis
- **AL**: serum/urine immunofixation, SFLC, **ALPS** score
- **ATTR**: **Tc-99m PYP** scan (Grade 2-3 = ATTR) + exclude AL
- **CMR** + LGE; **EMB** with mass spec typing (gold standard)

## 4. Management
- **ATTRwt**: **tafamidis** (TTR stabilizer) — ATTR-ACT trial ↓ mortality
- **ATTRv**: patisiran/vutrisiran (siRNA), inotersen
- **AL**: chemotherapy ± transplant (hematology-led)
- **Avoid**: digoxin, CCB, β-blockers (hypotension)
- Diuretics cautiously

> **Pearls**: **Suspect ATTR** in elderly HFpEF + thick walls. Bone scintigraphy (PYP/DPD/HMDP) + **no monoclonal protein = ATTR** diagnosis (no biopsy needed).
