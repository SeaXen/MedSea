## 1. Introduction & Definition
Cardiac implantable electronic devices (**CIEDs**) include **pacemakers (PPM)**, **implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (ICD)**, **cardiac resynchronisation therapy (CRT-P / CRT-D)**, and **subcutaneous ICD (S-ICD)**, plus newer leadless systems. **Electrophysiology (EP)** is the catheter-based study and ablation of arrhythmias. Device therapy has revolutionised management of bradyarrhythmia, HF, and SCD prevention.

---

## 2. Pathophysiology & Indications Overview
- **Bradyarrhythmia** → pacing restores rate / AV synchrony
- **High-grade AV block** / sinus node dysfunction → ventricular pacing (RV / His / LBBAP)
- **HF with dyssynchrony** (LBBB, QRS ≥ 130–150 ms, EF ≤ 35%) → **biventricular pacing** resynchronises LV contraction
- **VT / VF risk** → **ICD** detects and terminates (shock or ATP)
- **Tachyarrhythmia** → **catheter ablation** (RF, cryo, pulsed-field) destroys re-entry circuit / ectopic focus

---

## 3. Classification / Types of Devices
| Device | Function | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Pacemaker (PPM)** | Treat bradyarrhythmia | Single / dual chamber, leadless |
| **ICD** | Detect & terminate VT/VF | Shock + ATP; TV-ICD / S-ICD |
| **CRT-P** | Resynchronise HF (no defibrillation) | LV lead in coronary sinus |
| **CRT-D** | CRT + ICD | Combined resync + defibrillation |
| **S-ICD** | Subcutaneous, shock only | No pacing, no transvenous lead |
| **Leadless PM** (Micra, Aveir) | VVI(R) pacing, femoral implant | No lead complications |
| **His-bundle / LBBAP** | Physiological pacing | Preserves native conduction |
| **ILR** | Long-term rhythm monitor | Diagnostic only |

---

## 4. Clinical Features Indicating Device Need
- **Bradyarrhythmia:** syncope, presyncope, fatigue, dyspnoea, exercise intolerance, Stokes-Adams attacks (3° AV block), HF
- **SCD risk:** survived VT/VF, HCM with high Risk-SCD score, ARVC, Brugada, LQT with syncope, EF ≤ 35%
- **HF:** NYHA II–IV, LVEF ≤ 35%, LBBB QRS ≥ 150 ms, on optimal GDMT
- **Pacing indications:** SND, AV block, bifascicular block + syncope, post-TAVI conduction injury
- **Symptoms from device:** pacemaker syndrome (VVI in sinus rhythm), inappropriate shocks, anxiety

---

## 5. Investigations
- **12-lead ECG + Holter / event recorder / ILR** — diagnostic
- **Echocardiogram** — EF, valve, structural heart disease
- **Stress test** — chronotropic incompetence; loss of pre-excitation (WPW)
- **EP study** — diagnostic + ablation, refractory period assessment
- **CMR / cardiac CT** — scar (VT substrate), anomalous coronary sinus (CRT planning), pre-procedural
- **Device interrogation** — battery (ERI/EOL), lead impedance, threshold, % pacing, AT/AF burden, VT/VF episodes, alerts
- **CXR / fluoroscopy** — lead position, Twiddler, pneumothorax
- **Bloods** — K⁺, Mg²⁺, TFT, BNP, drug levels (amiodarone, digoxin)
- **Tilt-table** — vasovagal syncope differential

---

## 6. Differential Diagnosis — Syncope / Palpitations Workup
| Cause | Key Clue | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **Cardiac arrhythmic (brady)** | Sudden, no prodrome, Stokes-Adams | ECG, Holter, ILR, EP |
| **VT / VF** | Sudden, palpitations before, family SCD | ECG, EP, echo, CMR |
| **Vasovagal** | Prodromal, triggers, longer recovery | Tilt-table |
| **Orthostatic hypotension** | Postural, elderly, drugs | Lying-standing BP |
| **Carotid sinus hypersensitivity** | Carotid manipulation, shaving/turning | Carotid sinus massage |
| **Reflex / situational** | Cough, micturition, swallow | History |
| **Neurologic (seizure)** | Tongue-biting, incontinence, post-ictal | EEG, MRI brain |
| **Metabolic** | Hypoglycaemia, hypoxia, anaemia | Glucose, ABG, FBC |
| **Structural** | AS, HCM, tamponade, PE | Echo, CTPA |
| **Drug-induced** | QT-prolonging, AV blockers | Drug level, history |

## 7. Complications
- **Pacing-related:** lead dislodgement (1–5%), pneumothorax (1%), infection (0.5–2%), **Twiddler syndrome**, **pacemaker syndrome** (VVI in sinus), exit block, high threshold
- **CRT-related:** phrenic nerve stimulation, CS dissection / perforation, non-response (30%)
- **ICD-specific:** **inappropriate shocks** (AF, T-wave oversensing, EMI), lead fracture, **device infection / endocarditis** (refer to **EHRA** consensus)
- **S-ICD:** inappropriate shocks (T-wave oversensing, myopotentials), cannot pace
- **Procedural (EP study / ablation):** vascular access, tamponade, AV block (AVNRT ablation — 1%), stroke, radiation, death (<0.1%)
- **Battery depletion** (5–10 y for PPM; 6–9 y for ICD)

## 8. Management — Indications & Programming
| Device | Class I Indication | Programming Highlights |
|---|---|---|
| **DDD PPM** | Symptomatic SND; Mobitz II; 3° AV block; bifascicular block + syncope | AV hysteresis, rate response (DDDR) |
| **VVI PM** | Permanent AF + symptomatic brady | Single-chamber ventricular |
| **ICD (primary)** | **LVEF ≤ 35% on GDMT ≥ 3 mo + NYHA II–III** (MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT) | Detection zones: VF > 250 bpm, VT 180–250; ATP before shock |
| **ICD (secondary)** | Survivor of VT / VF / aborted SCD | Tailored to clinical VT |
| **CRT-P / CRT-D** | **EF ≤ 35%, sinus, LBBB QRS ≥ 150 ms, NYHA II–IV** (Class I); 130–149 ms / non-LBBB ≥ 150 ms (IIa) | AV delay (Ritter), VV delay optimisation, LV lead in lateral/posterolateral CS branch |
| **S-ICD** | ICD indication **without pacing need**; young, venous access issues, infection risk | Pre-implant ECG screening (sensing vector) |
| **Leadless PM (Micra / Aveir)** | Single-chamber pacing indication; no venous access; infection risk | VVI(R) mode |
| **His-bundle / LBBAP** | AV block, SND, avoid RV pacing (HF/EF ↓) | Corrective His capture, LBBAP threshold ≤ 1.5 V |

**EP Study & Ablation** — RF (heat), cryo (cold), **pulsed-field (PFA — ADVENT trial)**: AVNRT (slow pathway, 95% success), AVRT (accessory pathway, 95%), typical flutter (CTI, 95%), AF (PVI cornerstone), VT (substrate in ischaemic, epicardial in non-ischaemic).

## 9. Secondary Prevention / Follow-up
- **Remote monitoring** (Home Monitoring, Merlin, Latitude) — daily transmissions; alert on lead impedance, battery ERI, AT/AF, VT/VF
- **In-clinic checks** every 3–12 months (battery, threshold, impedance, sensing, pacing %)
- **MRI conditional** devices — most modern devices are 1.5T/3T conditional; check before scan
- **Driving restrictions (DVLA, UK):** PPM for syncope → 1 month; ICD primary prevention → 1 month; ICD secondary → 6 months
- **Sport:** contact sports avoided; ICDs may participate in low/moderate activity (HRS consensus)
- **Device infection:** complete extraction + antibiotics; leadless in selected
- **End-of-life:** deactivate ICD shocks (tachy therapies) to prevent inappropriate shocks at terminal illness; pacing may be continued if comfort

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield
<callout icon="⭐" color="purple_bg">
	**12 high-yield points** for FCPS / MRCP device and EP questions.
</callout>

- **Pacemaker code NBG:** Position 1 = chamber **P**aced (A/V/D), 2 = chamber **S**ensed, 3 = response to sensing (**I** inhibited / **T** triggered / **D** dual), 4 = rate modulation (**R**), 5 = multi-site
- **DDD** is the most common dual-chamber mode; preserves AV synchrony
- **CRT Class I:** **EF ≤ 35% + sinus rhythm + LBBB + QRS ≥ 150 ms + NYHA II–IV on GDMT**
- **CRT non-responders (~30%):** sub-optimal LV lead position, wide QRS not from LBBB, insufficient GDMT — consider **AV + VV delay optimisation**, multimodality imaging
- **ICD primary prevention:** **EF ≤ 35% on GDMT ≥ 3 months** + NYHA II–III (MADIT-II, SCD-HeFT) — *not* immediately post-MI
- **Inappropriate ICD shocks** are most often due to **AF with rapid ventricular response** — treat AF aggressively, reprogramme detection zones / SVT discriminators
- **S-ICD** = no pacing, no transvenous lead, **screening ECG mandatory** before implant
- **Leadless Micra** = VVI(R) pacing; for AF + brady or SND with infrequent pacing need
- **His-bundle / LBBAP** are **physiological pacing** — preserve narrow QRS, avoid long-term RV-pacing cardiomyopathy
- **Pacemaker syndrome** = VVI pacing in sinus rhythm → loss of AV synchrony → fatigue, dyspnoea, hypotension; upgrade to DDD
- **Twiddler syndrome** = patient manipulates generator → lead dislodgement / coiling
- **EP-guided ablation for AF (PVI)** is curative in **70–80% paroxysmal AF** (EARLY-AF, STOP-AF, ADVENT for pulsed-field)

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
	**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Devices are the cornerstone of bradyarrhythmia, HF, and SCD management. The **CRT Class I indication** (EF ≤ 35%, sinus, LBBB, QRS ≥ 150 ms) is the most exam-tested. **ICD primary prevention** requires **GDMT ≥ 3 months first** to allow reverse remodelling — many patients improve and no longer need ICD. Always consider **physiological pacing (His / LBBAP)** to avoid RV-pacing cardiomyopathy. **Remote monitoring** improves outcomes and reduces in-clinic visits. Discuss **device deactivation** in end-of-life care.
