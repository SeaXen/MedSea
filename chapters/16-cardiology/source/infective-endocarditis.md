# Infective Endocarditis (IE)

<callout icon="🦠" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Infective Endocarditis — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Introduction
IE is **infection of a cardiac valve (native or prosthetic), endocardial surface, or implanted device**. It is a **high-mortality condition** (in-hospital 15–30%, 1-year 30–40%) requiring urgent recognition. Presentation ranges from **fulminant acute** (Staph aureus) to **indolent subacute** (Viridans streptococci, HACEK, enterococci).

### Predisposing Factors
- **Valve disease** (rheumatic, bicuspid, degenerative, prolapse)
- **Prosthetic valve** (early <1 y — Staph epidermidis, late — native profile)
- **Previous IE** (highest lifetime risk)
- **Congenital heart disease** (VSD, PDA, bicuspid, TOF)
- **IV drug use** (tricuspid, Staph aureus)
- **Indwelling catheters, pacemakers, ICDs**, haemodialysis
- **Poor dentition, recent dental / surgical / GU / GI procedure**

---

## 2. Pathophysiology
1. **Endothelial injury** (turbulence, jets, catheters) → exposes subendothelial collagen
2. **Sterile thrombus (NBTE)** — fibrin + platelets deposit
3. **Bacteraemia** (transient, often subclinical) — adherence and colonisation
4. **Vegetation** grows — bacteria embedded in fibrin-platelet matrix
5. **Local invasion** — leaflet perforation, abscess, fistula
6. **Systemic embolisation** — fragments shower to brain, spleen, kidney, limb
7. **Circulating immune complexes** → vasculitis, glomerulonephritis, Osler nodes, Roth spots

---

## 3. Classification & Organisms

### By Course
| Type | Course | Typical Organism |
|---|---|---|
| **Acute** | Days, toxic, severe | **Staph aureus** |
| **Subacute** | Weeks, indolent | **Viridans streptococci** |
| **Prosthetic early (<1 y)** | Post-op, aggressive | Staph epidermidis, Staph aureus, fungi |
| **Prosthetic late (>1 y)** | Native-like | Strep, Staph, enterococci |
| **IVDU** | Tricuspid | Staph aureus |
| **Culture-negative** (5–20%) | Prior abx, fastidious | HACEK, Bartonella, Coxiella, Brucella, Tropheryma |

### HACEK Organisms (oropharyngeal flora, fastidious)
**H**aemophilus · **A**ggregatibacter · **C**ardiobacterium · **E**ikenella · **K**ingella

---

## 4. Clinical Features

### Classic (subacute)
- **Fever** >38°C, often the only sign (95%)
- **New murmur** (or change in pre-existing) — 50–80%
- **Constitutional** — malaise, anorexia, weight loss, night sweats
- **Splinter haemorrhages, petechiae**
- **Osler nodes** (tender, finger/toe pads)
- **Janeway lesions** (non-tender, palms/soles)
- **Roth spots** (retinal haemorrhages, pale centre)
- **Splenomegaly** (immune complex / infarct)
- **Clubbing** (chronic)

### Acute (Staph aureus)
- High fever, rigors, rapid valve destruction
- Heart failure, septic emboli, shock
- Often tricuspid (IVDU) — recurrent pulmonary emboli/septic emboli

### Complications at Presentation
- HF (valve destruction)
- Embolic (cerebral, splenic, renal, limb, mesenteric)
- Abscess (aortic root, mitral-aortic intervalvular fibrosa)
- AV block (abscess extension)
- Mycotic aneurysm (cerebral — rupture)
- Septic shock, AKI, multi-organ failure

---

## 5. Investigations

### A. Blood Cultures
- **3 sets from different sites**, ≥1 h apart, before antibiotics
- ≥10 mL per bottle (aerobic + anaerobic)
- Persistent positivity = same organism in ≥2 sets ≥12 h apart
- If culture-negative after 48–72 h → notify lab, special media (HACEK, Bartonella, fungi)

### B. Echocardiography
- **TTE first** — sensitivity 60–70%
- **TOE if:** prosthetic valve, negative TTE with high suspicion, poor windows, prior to surgery, suspected abscess / fistula / perforation
- **TOE sensitivity 90–100%**
- Look for: **vegetation** (oscillating mass on valve), **abscess** (echo-free space), **new regurgitation**, **leaflet perforation**, **prosthetic dehiscence**

### C. ECG & Imaging
- **ECG** — new AV block = aortic root abscess
- **CXR** — pulmonary oedema (HF), septic emboli (IVDU tricuspid)
- **PET-CT** (prosthetic valve IE, difficult diagnosis)
- **CT/MRI brain** — if neurological symptoms
- **CT angio** — mycotic aneurysm

### D. Labs
- FBC (anaemia, neutrophilia), ESR/CRP ↑
- U&E, LFT, blood culture
- Urine — proteinuria, microscopic haematuria
- Rheumatoid factor (immune complex), complement

---

## 6. Modified Duke Criteria

### Major
1. **Blood culture positive** for typical IE organism (×2 separate) or persistently positive
2. **Echo evidence** — vegetation, abscess, new partial dehiscence of prosthetic valve, or new valvular regurgitation

### Minor
- Predisposition (prosthetic valve, prior IE, CHD, etc.)
- Fever ≥38°C
- Vascular phenomena (major emboli, mycotic aneurysm, intracranial, Janeway)
- Immunologic phenomena (Osler, Roth, glomerulonephritis, RF+)
- Microbiological evidence not meeting major criterion

### Diagnosis
| Category | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Definite IE** | 2 Major · 1 Major + 3 Minor · 5 Minor · OR histopathology / molecular from valve |
| **Possible IE** | 1 Major + 1 Minor · 3 Minor |
| **Rejected** | Firm alternative diagnosis OR resolution ≤4 d of antibiotics |

---

## 7. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Clue |
|---|---|
| **Acute rheumatic fever** | Migratory polyarthritis, carditis, recent strep, Jones criteria |
| **SLE / Libman-Sacks** | ANA, dsDNA, sterile vegetations |
| **NBTE (marantic)** | Malignancy, mucin-producing adenocarcinoma |
| **Atrial myxoma** | Constitutional, "tumour plop", systemic emboli |
| **Vasculitis** | ANCA, multi-system |
| **Antiphospholipid syndrome** | Thrombosis, miscarriage, lupus anticoagulant |
| **Lymphoma / malignancy** | PUO, lymphadenopathy, weight loss |
| **Tuberculosis** | PUO, night sweats, lung disease |
| **Intravenous drug user — other sources** | Skin/soft tissue infection, osteomyelitis |

## 8. Management

### Empirical Antibiotics (before culture results)
| Scenario | Regimen |
|---|---|
| **Native valve, community** | Ampicillin + (Flu)cloxacillin + Gentamicin (or Vancomycin + Gentamicin if MRSA / severe sepsis) |
| **Prosthetic valve (<1 y) or nosocomial** | **Vancomycin + Gentamicin + Rifampicin** |
| **IVDU tricuspid** | Vancomycin + Gentamicin (Staph aureus) |
| **Culture-negative** | Vancomycin + Gentamicin + Doxycycline / Ciprofloxacin (covers Bartonella, Coxiella) |

### Targeted Therapy (native valve, sensitive)
| Organism | Regimen | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Viridans / S. bovis (MIC ≤0.12)** | Benzylpenicillin 2.4 g 4 h + Gentamicin 1 mg/kg 8 h (optional) | 4 wk (2 wk gent) |
| **Viridans (MIC >0.12)** | Benzylpenicillin + Gentamicin | 4–6 wk |
| **Enterococcus** | Ampicillin + Gentamicin (or Ceftriaxone) | 4–6 wk |
| **Staph aureus (MSSA)** | Flucloxacillin 2 g 4–6 h + Gentamicin optional | 4–6 wk |
| **Staph aureus (MRSA)** | Vancomycin + Rifampicin (prosthetic) | 6 wk |
| **HACEK** | Ceftriaxone 2 g OD | 4 wk |
| **Fungi (Candida, Aspergillus)** | Amphotericin B + Flucytosine / Caspofungin | 6+ wk, surgery usually |

> All doses for normal renal function; monitor vancomycin trough (15–20 mg/L), gentamicin levels.

### Surgical Indications (Heart Team)
- **Heart failure** due to valve destruction (most common)
- **Uncontrolled infection** — persistent bacteraemia, abscess, fistula, enlarging vegetation
- **Prevention of embolism** — vegetation >10 mm with embolic event, >15 mm mobile
- **Prosthetic valve IE** — especially S. aureus, fungi, early PVE
- **Fungal IE** — almost always
- **New AV block** (abscess)
- **Persistent vegetation** >10 mm after embolic event

### Surgical Timing
- **Emergency** — acute AR with pulmonary oedema, root abscess with conduction block
- **Urgent** — within days: uncontrolled infection, large vegetation + embolic event
- **Elective** — after stabilisation: completed antibiotic course, completed healing

---

## 9. Complications & Monitoring
- **HF** — most common; valve destruction (AR > MR)
- **Embolic stroke** — cerebral mycotic aneurysm → haemorrhage
- **Splenic / renal / mesenteric infarcts**
- **Abscess** (aortic root, MAIVF, intraventricular septum)
- **AV block** (progression of abscess)
- **Mycotic aneurysm** (intracranial, visceral, peripheral)
- **Glomerulonephritis** (immune complex)
- **Drug toxicity** — nephrotoxicity (vancomycin, gentamicin), ototoxicity (gentamicin)
- **Relapse** — especially prosthetic, enterococcal, fungal
- **Long-term** — re-do valve surgery, conduction disease, persistent vegetation

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield
<callout icon="⭐" color="purple_bg">
**12 high-yield points** that appear on every FCPS / MRCP IE question paper.
</callout>

- **Modified Duke Criteria** — 2 Major / 1 Major + 3 Minor / 5 Minor = **Definite IE**.
- **Major criteria:** positive blood culture (typical ×2) + echo (vegetation, abscess, new regurg).
- **3 sets of blood cultures** from different sites, ≥1 h apart, BEFORE antibiotics.
- **TOE is more sensitive** than TTE — mandatory for prosthetic valve and suspected abscess.
- **Most common organism** in **acute** = **Staph aureus**; in **subacute** = **Viridans streptococci**.
- **IVDU tricuspid IE** = Staph aureus — recurrent septic pulmonary emboli.
- **HACEK** are fastidious oropharyngeal organisms causing culture-negative / slow-growing IE.
- **New AV block** in IE = suspect **aortic root abscess** → surgery.
- **Most common complication = HF**; **most feared = embolic stroke / mycotic aneurysm rupture**.
- **Surgical indications** — HF, uncontrolled infection, abscess, large vegetation >10–15 mm, fungi, prosthetic.
- **DOAC contraindicated** in mechanical valves; warfarin only (INR per valve position).
- **Prophylaxis only for highest risk** — prosthetic, prior IE, cyanotic CHD, repaired CHD <6 mo, transplant valvulopathy.

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** IE is a **medical emergency** with persistent high mortality. **Take 3 sets of blood cultures before antibiotics**, then start empirical therapy. **TOE is the imaging gold standard**, especially for prosthetic valves. The **Heart Team** (cardiology, cardiac surgery, ID) should manage all definite cases. **Surgery saves lives** in HF, uncontrolled infection, large vegetations, fungi, and abscess. Always **screen for embolic complications** (CT/MRI brain) and look for **mycotic aneurysm**. **Prevention** is targeted — prophylaxis only in highest-risk patients and dental / oral procedures.
</callout>
