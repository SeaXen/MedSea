# HCM — Risk Stratification (HCM Risk-SCD Score)

## 1. Why It Matters
The leading cause of death in HCM is **sudden cardiac death (SCD)**, often the first clinical manifestation in young patients. The 5-year risk varies from <1% to >10%; ICD is the only proven therapy.

## 2. 2014 ESC HCM Risk-SCD Calculator
Estimates **5-year SCD risk (%)** using 5 variables:

| Variable | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | Years at evaluation |
| **Max wall thickness** | mm (echo/CMR) |
| **LA diameter** | mm (parasternal long axis) |
| **LVOT gradient** | mmHg (peak, rest + Valsalva) |
| **Family history SCD** | First-degree relative <50 yrs |
| **NSVT** | ≥3 beats, ≥120 bpm on Holter |
| **Unexplained syncope** | Recent, unexplained |

### Risk Thresholds (ESC 2022 simplified)
| 5-year Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **<4%** | ICD generally not indicated |
| **4–6%** | Consider ICD (individualise) |
| **≥6%** | ICD recommended |

## 3. AHA/ACC 2020 Approach
ICD reasonable (Class IIa) with **any one** of:
- Family history SCD in 1st-degree relative
- Massive LVH **≥30 mm**
- Unexplained syncope
- NSVT on Holter
- LGE **≥15% of LV mass** (CMR)
- Apical aneurysm
- Abnormal BP response to exercise (age <40)

## 4. Other Risk Modifiers
- **Apical aneurysm** (mid-cavity HCM) — high SCD risk
- **LVEF <50%** — end-stage HCM, consider transplant
- **Late LGE burden** (>15% mass)
- **NSVT** frequency on serial Holter

## 5. ICD Specifics
- **Primary prevention** — for risk-driven ICD
- **Secondary prevention** — cardiac arrest or sustained VT
- **Single-chamber** preferred (lower lead complications) unless pacing indication
- Generator changes every 5–9 yrs; infection 1–2%
- **Avoid** in patients with comorbidity limiting life to <1 year

## 6. Shared Decision-Making
Discuss: lead complications (1–2%/yr), inappropriate shocks, device infection, impact on lifestyle, and pregnancy.

> **Pearls**
> - 5-yr risk ≥6% → **ICD**; 4–6% → consider; <4% → reassure.
> - Massive LVH **≥30 mm**, family SCD, NSVT, syncope, apical aneurysm are the classic flags.
> - **LGE ≥15%** of LV mass on CMR is an emerging SCD risk marker.
> - Re-evaluate risk **every 1–2 yrs** or with new symptoms.
> - End-stage HCM (LVEF <50%) — reframe as HCM-HF; consider transplant.
