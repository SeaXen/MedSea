# Ruptured aortic aneurysm

## 1. Definition
**Ruptured aortic aneurysm**: Life-threatening emergency with tearing of all three layers of the aortic wall, leading to massive hemorrhage. Most commonly involves **abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA)** but can be thoracic.

## 2. Classification
- **Ruptured AAA** (most common): Into retroperitoneum (contained) or peritoneum (free)
- **Ruptured thoracic aortic aneurysm** (TAA): Hemothorax, hemopericardium
- **Mycotic aneurysm**: Infectious etiology (Salmonella, Staph, syphilis)
- **Traumatic pseudoaneurysm**: Deceleration injury (e.g., aortic isthmus)
- **Aortic dissection** can lead to aneurysm formation and rupture

## 3. Risk Factors
- **Smoking** (strongest modifiable risk)
- **Age >65**, male sex
- **Hypertension**, atherosclerosis
- **Family history** of AAA
- **Connective tissue disease**: Marfan, Ehlers-Danlos
- **Bicuspid aortic valve**, vasculitis (Takayasu, GCA)

## 4. Clinical Features
### Classic Triad (50% have all)
1. **Abdominal/back pain** (often sudden, severe, "tearing")
2. **Hypotension/shock**
3. **Pulsatile abdominal mass**

### Other Features
- **Syncope**, diaphoresis
- **Flank/groin pain** (retroperitoneal)
- **Hematuria** (renal involvement)
- **Lower limb ischemia** (iliac occlusion)
- **Anemia**

## 5. Diagnosis
- **Stable patient**: **CT angiography** (gold standard)
- **Unstable patient**: **Bedside ultrasound** (AAA >3 cm; free fluid)
- **CXR** (TAA): Widened mediastinum, left pleural effusion
- **Avoid**: DPL (in abdomen), delay for confirmation

## 6. Management
### Resuscitation
- **Permissive hypotension** (SBP 80–100, avoid large fluid bolus)
- **2 large-bore IVs**, crossmatched blood (O-negative until available)
- **Type and crossmatch 6+ units**
- Reverse anticoagulation if applicable

### Definitive
- **Emergent surgical repair** (open) OR **EVAR** (endovascular) if anatomy suitable
- **EVAR** for ruptured AAA: Lower mortality if available, especially in stable patients
- **Damage control resuscitation**: pRBC + FFP + platelets (1:1:1)

### Perimortem
- **Aortic occlusion balloon** (REBOA) as bridge to surgery
- **ED thoracotomy** in extremis

## 7. Screening & Prevention
- **One-time AAA ultrasound**: Men 65–75 who have ever smoked
- **BP control** (smoking cessation, statin)
- **Elective repair** for AAA ≥5.5 cm, rapid expansion (>0.5 cm/6 mo), symptomatic

> **Pearls**
> - **Classic triad** of pain + hypotension + pulsatile mass — only 50% present this way
> - **Perimortem**: consider AAA in unexplained cardiac arrest (PEA)
> - **Permissive hypotension** improves survival — avoid aggressive fluid resuscitation
> - **"Hard signs"** require immediate surgical consultation
> - **Back pain + hypotension** in elderly → AAA until proven otherwise
