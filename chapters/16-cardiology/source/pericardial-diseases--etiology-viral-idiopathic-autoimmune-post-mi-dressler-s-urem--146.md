# Etiology of Acute Pericarditis (viral, idiopathic, autoimmune, post-MI/Dressler's, uremic, drugs, TB, neoplastic)

## 1. Definition
**Acute pericarditis**: Inflammation of the pericardium with sudden onset, lasting <4–6 weeks. Most common pericardial disease.

## 2. Etiologic Classification
### Infectious
- **Viral** (most common in developed world): Coxsackie B, echovirus, adenovirus, HIV, EBV, CMV, influenza, SARS-CoV-2
- **Bacterial**: TB (most common worldwide), *Staphylococcus*, *Streptococcus*, *Pneumococcus*
- **Fungal** (rare): Histoplasmosis, coccidioidomycosis
- **Parasitic**: Echinococcus, toxoplasma

### Idiopathic
- Presumed viral — most common label after workup is negative
- ~80–90% of acute cases

### Autoimmune/Inflammatory
- **Systemic lupus erythematosus** (SLE): Most common CTD cause
- **Rheumatoid arthritis**, scleroderma, mixed connective tissue disease
- **Vasculitides**: Behçet, Takayasu, polyarteritis nodosa
- **Sarcoidosis**
- **Post-cardiac injury syndrome** (Dressler): post-MI, post-pericardiotomy

### Post-MI
- **Early (peri-infarction)**: Within 1–3 days — from transmural necrosis
- **Late (Dressler)**: Weeks to months — autoimmune

### Metabolic
- **Uremic** (most common cause in dialysis patients)
- **Myxedema** (hypothyroidism) — large, often asymptomatic effusions

### Drug-Induced
- **Lupus-like**: Procainamide, hydralazine, isoniazid, methyldopa
- **Hypersensitivity**: Penicillins, sulfonamides
- **Antineoplastics**: Doxorubicin, cyclophosphamide
- **Other**: Minoxidil, phenytoin, mesalazine

### Neoplastic
- **Lung, breast, lymphoma, melanoma** (most common)
- **Direct extension** or metastatic
- **Primary pericardial tumors** (rare): mesothelioma, fibrosarcoma

### Radiation
- **Mediastinal radiation** (Hodgkin, breast cancer)
- Acute (during/within weeks) or chronic (months-years, with effusion/constriction)

## 3. Typical Distribution
| Etiology | % (Developed) | % (Developing) |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| Idiopathic/Viral | 80–90% | 40–50% |
| TB | <5% | 50–80% |
| Neoplastic | 5–10% | 5–10% |
| Autoimmune | 5% | 5% |
| Uremic | 5% | 5% |

## 4. Diagnostic Clues
- **Viral/idiopathic**: Young, prodrome, transient, responds to NSAIDs
- **TB**: Subacute, weight loss, fever, lymphocytic exudate, ↑ADA
- **Neoplastic**: Older, weight loss, hemopericardium, cytology
- **Uremic**: On dialysis, refractory, large effusion
- **Autoimmune**: Multisystem features, ANA+

> **Pearls**
> - **Idiopathic ≠ unknown**: most are post-viral
> - **TB pericarditis** = #1 cause of constrictive pericarditis worldwide
> - **Dressler syndrome**: Triad of fever, pleuritic pain, pericardial friction rub 2–10 weeks post-MI
> - **Uremic pericarditis**: Does NOT improve with dialysis intensification alone; needs pericardiocentesis or pericardial window
> - **Purulent pericarditis** = emergency — IV antibiotics + drainage
