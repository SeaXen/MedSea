## 1. Introduction & Definition
The pericardium is a **two-layered sac** (fibrous outer + serous inner with parietal/visceral layers) surrounding the heart. **Pericardial disease** includes acute inflammation, effusion ± tamponade, constriction, and post-injury syndromes. Most common in clinical practice: **acute viral/idiopathic pericarditis** and **tamponade**.

### Etiology (mnemonic: **TARDI-GRIN-BS**)
- **T**rauma, **T**horacic aortic dissection
- **A**utoimmune (SLE, RA, scleroderma, sarcoid, IBD)
- **R**adiation
- **D**rugs (hydralazine, procainamide, isoniazid, methyldopa, minoxidil)
- **I**diopathic / **I**nfection (viral — coxsackie B, echovirus, HIV; bacterial — TB, pneumococcus; fungal)
- **G**out, uraemia, post-cardiac injury
- **R**enal failure (uremic pericarditis)
- **I**nfarction (early peri-infarction, late Dressler)
- **N**eoplasm (lung, breast, lymphoma, melanoma)
- **B**acterial (purulent)
- **S**urgery (post-pericardiotomy syndrome)

---

## 2. Pathophysiology
**Inflammation** of pericardium → ↑vascular permeability → **fibrin deposition** ("bread-and-butter" appearance at surgery) → fluid accumulation (serous, sanguineous, purulent, chylous) → **tamponade** if accumulation rate > production rate, or **constriction** (fibrosis ± calcification → ↓compliance → ventricular interdependence and equalised diastolic pressures).

**Pericardial pressure-volume curve:** initially compliant (small rise in pressure), then steep non-compliant phase (small volume → large pressure rise → tamponade).

---

## 3. Classification / Spectrum
| Type | Key Features | Tempo |
|---|---|---|
| **Acute pericarditis** | Pleuritic chest pain, pericardial rub, ST↑/PR↓ | <4–6 weeks |
| **Pericardial effusion** | Asymptomatic → tamponade | Variable |
| **Cardiac tamponade** | Beck's triad, pulsus paradoxus, echo signs | Emergency |
| **Constrictive pericarditis** | Kussmaul sign, pericardial knock, RV failure | Chronic |
| **Effusive-constrictive** | Effusion + constriction physiology after drainage | Subacute |
| **TB pericarditis** | Subacute effusion, 30–50% constrict | 2–6 months |
| **Uremic pericarditis** | ESRD, fibrinous, avoid anticoag | Acute |
| **Dressler syndrome** | Autoimmune, weeks–months post-MI | Subacute |

---

## 4. Clinical Features

### Acute Pericarditis
- **Chest pain**: sharp, **pleuritic**, worse lying flat, **better leaning forward**, radiates to **trapezius ridge** (highly specific, phrenic nerve)
- **Pericardial friction rub**: 3 components (atrial systole, ventricular systole, ventricular diastole); scratchy, best at LLSB end-expiration leaning forward
- Fever, dyspnoea, antecedent viral illness

### Cardiac Tamponade
- **Beck's triad:** **hypotension, ↑JVP, muffled heart sounds**
- **Pulsus paradoxus**: >10 mmHg drop in SBP on inspiration (also severe asthma, COPD, constriction)
- Tachycardia, tachypnoea, cool peripheries, oliguria, **electrical alternans** on ECG (swinging heart)

### Constrictive Pericarditis
- **Kussmaul sign:** ↑JVP on inspiration (paradoxical)
- **Pericardial knock:** early diastolic high-pitched sound
- Right HF: peripheral oedema, ascites (often >oedema), hepatomegaly, pleural effusion
- Severe disease with preserved LV function on echo

---

## 5. Investigations
- **ECG**: acute pericarditis → **diffuse concave ST↑ + PR depression** (PR depression is specific); sinus tachycardia, low voltage (effusion); **electrical alternans** (tamponade)
- **Troponin I/T**: mildly ↑ in 30% (perimyocarditis) — does not exclude pericarditis
- **Echocardiogram (TTE)**: **first-line** — effusion (size, location, haemodynamic impact); tamponade signs (RA systolic collapse, RV diastolic collapse, IVC plethora, respirophasic MV/TV inflow variation >25%)
- **CXR**: cardiomegaly if effusion >200 mL; pericardial calcification (chronic TB, uraemia)
- **CT / CMR**: pericardial thickening **>3–4 mm**, calcification, LGE in pericarditis
- **Bloods**: FBC, ESR/CRP, troponin, U&E, TFT, autoimmune screen (ANA, RF), blood cultures, viral serology, **HIV**
- **Pericardial fluid** (if tapped): cell count, Gram/culture, **cytology**, **ADA** (>40 U/L supports TB), PCR for TB, triglycerides (chylous)
- **RHC**: tamponade — equalisation of diastolic pressures; constriction — **dip-plateau / "square root" sign**

---

## 6. Differential Diagnosis of Acute Pericarditis
| Condition | Distinguishing Feature | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **ACS / STEMI** | Regional ST↑, reciprocal change, ↑↑ troponin | Serial ECG + troponin |
| **Aortic dissection** | Tearing pain → back, BP differential, mediastinal widening | CT angio |
| **Pulmonary embolism** | Pleuritic, dyspnoea, ↑HR, S1Q3T3, ↑D-dimer | CTPA |
| **Pneumothorax** | Sudden unilateral, hyperresonant, ↓breath sounds | CXR |
| **Myocarditis / perimyocarditis** | LV dysfunction, ↑↑ troponin, LGE on CMR | Echo + CMR |
| **Pleurisy / pneumonia** | Consolidation, fever, productive cough | CXR |
| **Oesophageal spasm / GERD** | Meal-related, no ECG change, relieved by NTG/antacids | Normal ECG/troponin |
| **MSK chest pain** | Reproducible on palpation, no ECG change | Clinical |

> **Mnemonic — 4 killers not to miss:** ACS, dissection, PE, **pericardial tamponade**.

---

## 7. Complications
- **Recurrence** (15–30% viral/idiopathic; 50% if no colchicine)
- **Cardiac tamponade** (1–3% viral, ↑ in bacterial / malignant / uraemic)
- **Constrictive pericarditis** (1–3% viral; **30–50% TB / bacterial / purulent**)
- **Perimyocarditis** — ventricular dysfunction
- **Atrial arrhythmia** (AF in 5–10%)
- **Death** in tamponade if untreated, in constrictive HF, in malignant disease

---

## 8. Management

| Condition | First-Line Therapy | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Acute pericarditis** | **NSAID** (Ibuprofen 600–800 mg TDS or Aspirin 750–1000 mg TDS) + **Colchicine 0.5 mg BD × 3 months** + PPI cover | Avoid steroids in viral/idiopathic (↑ recurrence — COPE, CORP) |
| **Refractory / recurrent** | Colchicine + NSAID → add **prednisolone**; if still recurrent → **anakinra** (IL-1 antagonist, AIRTRIP) or azathioprine | Avoid in bacterial |
| **TB pericarditis** | **ATT 6-drug 2 months → 4-drug 4–7 months + Prednisolone 2 months** (IMPI trial: ↓ constriction) | Pericardiocentesis if tamponade |
| **Uremic** | **Intensify dialysis**, **avoid anticoagulation**, NSAIDs | Pericardiocentesis if tamponade |
| **Malignant** | Pericardiocentesis + **pericardial window**, treat malignancy | Poor prognosis |
| **Tamponade** | **Emergency pericardiocentesis** (echo-guided, subxiphoid) | Surgical window if recurrent/loculated |
| **Constrictive** | **Pericardiectomy** (definitive); diuretics for symptom | Avoid in transient / effusive-constrictive early |
| **Post-MI (Dressler)** | High-dose aspirin, colchicine; **avoid NSAIDs** (CIRT — impair healing) | Pericardiocentesis if tamponade |
| **Exercise restriction** | No competitive sport × 3 mo (pericarditis) / 6 mo (perimyocarditis) | ESC 2023 |

---

## 9. Secondary Prevention / Long-term
- **Colchicine 0.5 mg BD × 3 months** with first episode and at every recurrence (COPE, CORP, ICAP, CORP-2)
- Treat underlying cause: TB, bacterial, autoimmune, malignancy, uraemia
- **Athletes**: avoid competitive sport 3 mo after acute pericarditis, **6 mo** after perimyocarditis (ESC 2023)
- **Long-term surveillance** echo in recurrent / TB pericarditis (constriction risk)
- **Anakinra** (IL-1 receptor antagonist) for colchicine-resistant recurrent pericarditis (AIRTRIP, IRAP trials)
- **Lifelong colchicine** may be considered in highly recurrent disease
- Vaccinations (influenza, pneumococcal) — limit triggers

## 10. Exam Pearls & High-Yield
<callout icon="⭐" color="purple_bg">
	**12 high-yield points** that show up on every FCPS / MRCP cardiology paper.
</callout>

- **Trapezius ridge radiation** is **pathognomonic** of pericardial pain (phrenic nerve).
- **PR depression** is the **most specific** ECG sign of acute pericarditis.
- **Diffuse concave ST↑** (not in a single territory — distinguishes from STEMI).
- **Beck's triad** = **hypotension, JVP↑, muffled sounds** — classic tamponade.
- **Pulsus paradoxus** >10 mmHg = tamponade (also severe asthma, constriction).
- **Electrical alternans** = swinging heart in large effusion / tamponade.
- **Avoid corticosteroids** in viral/idiopathic pericarditis (COPE, CORP) — ↑ recurrence.
- **Colchicine 0.5 mg BD × 3 months** with **all** acute pericarditis (first episode + recurrence).
- **NSAIDs are contraindicated** in early post-MI pericarditis (CIRT trial — impair myocardial healing).
- **TB pericarditis**: 30–50% progress to constriction — give **ATT + steroids** (IMPI trial).
- **Kussmaul sign + pericardial knock** = constriction; **pericardiectomy** is definitive.
- **Dressler syndrome** = autoimmune pericarditis 2–10 weeks post-MI; treat with high-dose aspirin + colchicine.

<callout icon="🎯" color="rose_bg">
	**CLINICAL SIGNIFICANCE —** Pericardial disease ranges from benign self-limiting pericarditis to life-threatening tamponade. **Always rule out tamponade** in any patient with hypotension and JVP elevation. **Echo is the cornerstone** investigation. The **NSAID + colchicine combination** has revolutionised management, drastically reducing recurrence. **TB pericarditis** must be actively sought in endemic areas — early ATT + steroids reduce the 30–50% risk of constriction. Constrictive pericarditis mimics restrictive cardiomyopathy and liver cirrhosis — think of it in any patient with right HF and preserved LV function.
</callout>
