## Definition

Dermatomyositis (DM) is an idiopathic inflammatory myopathy with characteristic cutaneous features. Affects 5-10/million/year, bimodal age distribution (5-15 and 45-65), female predominance (2:1). Strongly associated with malignancy (15-30% of adult-onset DM, especially ovarian, lung, gastric, colorectal, lymphoma, nasopharyngeal) — within 2 years of diagnosis. Pathogenesis: complement-mediated vascular injury, humoral and cellular immune response. Anti-Mi-2 (classic DM), anti-Jo-1 (antisynthetase syndrome with mechanic's hands), anti-TIF1-γ (malignancy-associated), anti-MDA5 (amyopathic DM with rapidly progressive ILD).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Pathognomonic features: (1) Heliotrope rash — violaceous erythema/edema of eyelids, periorbital; (2) Gottron papules — violaceous flat-topped papules over MCP/PIP joints; (3) Gottron sign — erythema over extensor joints. Other features: shawl sign, V-sign, holster sign, mechanic's hands (antisynthetase), nailfold changes (periungual telangiectasia, cuticular hypertrophy, dilated capillary loops on capillaroscopy), flagellate erythema, photosensitivity, poikiloderma, calcinosis (juvenile DM, late), lipodystrophy. Proximal muscle weakness (symmetrical, painless), elevated CK, aldolase, LDH, AST/ALT, EMG changes, MRI oedema, muscle biopsy perifascicular atrophy. Risk of interstitial lung disease (10-40%, especially anti-MDA5), cardiac (arrhythmias, CHF).

## 1. DEFINITION & EPIDEMIOLOGY

**Dermatomyositis (DM)** is an idiopathic inflammatory myopathy characterized by **proximal muscle weakness** and **pathognomonic cutaneous findings**. It is the **only myositis with specific skin manifestations**.

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Incidence** | 1-10/million/year |
| **Age** | Bimodal: **childhood 5-15 yrs** & **adult 45-65 yrs** |
| **Sex** | **F > M (2:1)** |
| **Malignancy Association** | **Adult DM: 15-30%** (ovarian, lung, breast, GI, pancreatic); **Juvenile DM: <1%** |
| **Genetics** | **HLA-DRB1*03, *07, *15**; **DRB1*03:01** (adult); **DQA1*05:01** |
| **Risk Factors** | Cancer, viral infections, drugs (hydroxyurea, statins, penicillamine, IFN-α, checkpoint inhibitors) |

> **Clinical Pearl:** **Malignancy screening mandatory in adult DM at diagnosis and annually for 3-5 years.**

## 2. CLASSIFICATION (EULAR/ACR 2017)

| Subtype | Features |
|---------|----------|
| **Classic DM** | Proximal weakness + classic skin signs |
| **Clinically Amyopathic DM (CADM / ADM)** | **Classic skin signs** with **no/ minimal muscle weakness** (CK normal, EMG/MRI normal) — **~20%** |
| **Juvenile DM (JDM)** | Onset <18 yrs; **calcinosis** (30-70%), lipodystrophy, vasculopathy |
| **DM with Overlap** | SLE, SSc, RA, Sjögren overlap features |
| **Cancer-Associated DM** | Rapid onset, refractory, older age, anti-TIF1γ/NXP2 |

## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

### 3.1 Pathognomonic Cutaneous Signs
| Sign | Description | Specificity |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Heliotrope Rash** | **Violaceous/erythematous** periorbital oedema + erythema; **upper > lower lid** | **Pathognomonic** |
| **Gottron Papules** | **Violaceous, flat-topped papules** over MCP/PIP/IP joints, elbows, knees | **Pathognomonic** |
| **Gottron Sign** | Erythematous, scaly patches over joints (vs papules) | Pathognomonic |
| **Shawl Sign** | Violaceous erythema over upper back/shoulders/neck | Highly specific |
| **V-Sign** | Anterior chest/neck erythema in V-distribution | Highly specific |
| **Mechanic's Hands** | **Hyperkeratotic, fissured, dirty-appearing** palms/fingers | Specific (also antisynthetase) |
| **Periungual Telangiectasia** | Dilated capillary loops at nail folds | Specific (also SSc) |
| **Nail Fold Changes** | Cuticular overgrowth, ragged cuticles | Supportive |

### 3.2 Other Cutaneous Features
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Photosensitivity** | Poikiloderma (atrophy, telangiectasia, dyspigmentation) |
| **Scalp Involvement** | Erythema, scaling, psoriasiform; **pruritic** (vs DLE) |
| **Mucosal** | Oral ulcers (rare) |
| **Calcinosis (JDM)** | Subcutaneous calcium deposits; **30-70%**; trauma sites, buttocks, extremities |
| **Lipodystrophy (JDM)** | Partial/generalized; insulin resistance, acanthosis nigricans |
| **Ulcerative DM** | Severe vasculopathic; digital ulcers, skin necrosis; high mortality |

### 3.3 Muscle Involvement
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Proximal Weakness** | Symmetric; neck flexors, deltoids, hip girdle; difficulty rising, climbing stairs |
| **Dysphagia** | Oropharyngeal/esophageal; **30-40%**; aspiration risk |
| **CK Levels** | **Elevated 10-50x** (normal in CADM) |
| **EMG** | Myopathic: short duration, low amplitude, fibrillation |
| **MRI** | T2/STIR hyperintensity in proximal muscles |

## 3. MYOSITIS-SPECIFIC AUTOANTIBODIES (MSA)

| Antibody | Frequency | Clinical Phenotype | Malignancy Risk |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Anti-Mi-2** | 10-20% | Classic DM; **good response**; photosensitivity; shawl/V-sign | Low |
| **Anti-TIF1γ (p155/140)** | **15-30%** | **Classic DM; highest malignancy risk** (adult); severe skin; mucocutaneous | **HIGH (ovarian, breast, lung, GI)** |
| **Anti-NXP2 (MJ)** | 10-20% | **JDM: calcinosis, contractures**; adult: malignancy, dysphagia | **HIGH** |
| **Anti-MDA5** | 10-30% (↑ Asian) | **CADM; rapidly progressive ILD**; skin ulcers, palmar papules, oral ulcers | Low (ILD dominant) |
| **Anti-SAE** | <5% | Severe skin, dysphagia; early skin before muscle | Moderate |
| **Anti-HMGCR** | 5-10% | **Statin-associated IMNM**; minimal skin | Low |
| **Anti-Jo-1 (Antisynthetase)** | 20-30% | **Antisynthetase syndrome**: ILD, mechanic's hands, arthritis, Raynaud's, fever | Low |

> **MDA5 = rapidly progressive ILD (RPILD) — emergency; start immunosuppression immediately.**
> **TIF1γ/NXP2 = malignancy screen mandatory.**

## 5. DIAGNOSTIC WORKUP

### 5.1 Laboratory
| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **CK, Aldolase, LDH, AST, ALT** | Muscle damage; CK normal in CADM/MDA5 |
| **Myositis Panel (MSA)** | **Anti-Mi-2, TIF1γ, NXP2, MDA5, SAE, MDAS, Jo-1, PL-7, PL-12, EJ, OJ, HMGCR** |
| **ANA, ENA, dsDNA, RF** | Exclude overlap (SLE, SSc, Sjögren) |
| **CRP, ESR** | Inflammatory activity (may be normal) |
| **LFT, Renal, FBC** | Baseline for immunosuppression |
| **Cancer Screen (Adult DM)** | **CT CAP, mammogram, pelvic US, CA-125, colonoscopy, age-appropriate** |

### 5.2 Electrophysiology & Imaging
| Test | Findings |
|------|----------|
| **EMG** | Myopathic MUP: short, polyphasic; fibrillation/positive sharp waves |
| **Muscle MRI (T2/STIR)** | Oedema in proximal muscles; **axilla-sparing** (vs inclusion body myositis) |
| **Muscle Biopsy** | **Perifascicular atrophy**; perifascicular inflammation; MHC-I upregulation; **capillary loss** |

### 5.3 Malignancy Screening (Adult DM at Diagnosis)
| Timeline | Investigations |
|----------|----------------|
| **At diagnosis** | **CT Chest/Abdomen/Pelvis**; **Mammogram + Breast US** (women); **Pelvic US + CA-125** (ovarian); **Upper GI Endoscopy + Colonoscopy** (GI); **Tumour markers** (CA-125, CA 19-9, CEA, HE4) |
| **Age-appropriate** | Pap smear, PSA, etc. |
| **Follow-up** | **Every 6 months × 2 years → annually × 3-5 years** |

> **Anti-TIF1γ / NXP2 → most intensive screening.**

## 4. MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Treatment Principles
1. **Control inflammation** (muscle + skin)
2. **Prevent/treat complications** (ILD, calcinosis, dysphagia)
3. **Screen/treat malignancy** (adult DM)
4. **Rehabilitation** (physio, OT, speech for dysphagia)

### 6.2 First-Line
| Condition | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Muscle Disease** | **Prednisolone 1 mg/kg/day** (max 60-80 mg) → taper over 6-12 months |
| **Skin Disease** | **Hydroxychloroquine 200-400 mg/day** + **Potent TCS/TCI**; **Photoprotection** |
| **ILD** | **High-dose steroids** (IV methylprednisolone 500-1000 mg × 3-5 days) + **Cyclophosphamide** (IV pulse 500-1000 mg/m² monthly × 6) OR **MMF 2-3 g/day**; **Rituximab** for refractory |
| **CADM** | **HCQ + TCS/TCI**; **Methotrexate / MMF / Azathioprine** for skin; **MDA5 + ILD → aggressive immunosuppression** |

### 6.3 Steroid-Sparing Agents (Start at onset in moderate-severe)
| Agent | Dose | Indication | Monitoring |
|-------|------|------------|------------|
| **Methotrexate** | 15-25 mg/week + Folate | First-line steroid-sparing | FBC, LFT q3m |
| **Mycophenolate (MMF)** | 2-3 g/day | First-line; **ILD, skin** | FBC, LFT, renal q3m |
| **Azathioprine** | 1.5-2.5 mg/kg/day | Alternative; pregnancy | TPMT, FBC, LFT |
| **Cyclosporine** | 2.5-5 mg/kg/day | Severe skin, ILD | Renal, BP, lipids |
| **Tacrolimus** | 0.05-0.1 mg/kg/day | Refractory skin, ILD | Trough level, renal |
| **Rituximab** | 375 mg/m² × 4 / 1000 mg × 2 | **Refractory DM/ILD**; **MDA5-ILD**; anti-synthetase | CD19/20, IgG, Hep B/C |
| **IVIG** | 2 g/kg over 2-5 days | Refractory, acute severe | Renal, IgA level |
| **JAK Inhibitors** (Tofacitinib, Baricitinib, Upadacitinib) | Standard doses | **Refractory skin/muscle/ILD** | CBC, LFT, lipids, VZV |
| **Plasma Exchange** | 5-7 exchanges | Severe refractory | — |

### 6.4 Skin-Directed Therapy
| Therapy | Indication |
|---------|------------|
| **Hydroxychloroquine 200-400 mg/day** | **First-line skin**; all DM |
| **Potent TCS** (Clobetasol) | Localized lesions; avoid face long-term |
| **TCI** (Tacrolimus 0.1%/Pimecrolimus 1%) | **Face, eyelids, flexures** |
| **Topical Calcineurin Inhibitors** | First-line face/eyelids |
| **Intralesional Triamcinolone** | Resistant plaques, calcinosis |
| **Methotrexate** | Refractory skin ± muscle |
| **IVIG** | Refractory cutaneous, ulcerative DM |
| **Rituximab** | Refractory skin/muscle/ILD |
| **JAK Inhibitors** (Tofacitinib, Baricitinib, Upadacitinib) | Refractory skin/muscle/ILD |

### 6.5 Juvenile DM (JDM) Specifics
| Feature | Management |
|---------|------------|
| **Calcinosis** | **No proven medical**; IVIG, bisphosphonates, probenecid, minocycline, diltiazem, surgery if symptomatic |
| **Lipodystrophy** | Metabolic monitoring; insulin sensitizers |
| **Vasculopathy** | Aggressive immunosuppression (IVIG, cyclophosphamide, rituximab) |
| **Methotrexate** | First-line steroid-sparing (15 mg/m²/week) |

## 5. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

| Complication | Frequency | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| **ILD** | **30-40%** (↑ MDA5, anti-synthetase) | **MDA5 = RPILD emergency**; steroids + CYC/MMF + RTX |
| **Malignancy** | **15-30% adult DM** | **Screen at diagnosis + 3-5 years** |
| **Dysphagia** | 30-40% | Speech therapy; feeding tube; cricopharyngeal myotomy |
| **Calcinosis (JDM)** | 30-70% | No proven medical; surgery if symptomatic |
| **Infection** | Steroid/IS related | Prophylaxis (TMP-SMX, antivirals) |
| **Mortality** | **ILD, malignancy, infection** | 5-yr survival ~75% (adult); >90% (JDM) |

## Flashcards

  **A:** Topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, or specific agents per disease); trigger avoidance; emollients.
