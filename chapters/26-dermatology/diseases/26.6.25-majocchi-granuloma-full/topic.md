# Majocchi granuloma

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Infections

## Definition

Majocchi granuloma is a deep dermatophyte infection (follicular and perifollicular granulomatous inflammation) caused by Trichophyton rubrum (most common). Presents as persistent erythematous, scaly, annular plaque with follicular pustules or nodules. Often follows topical steroid use on tinea (tinea incognito). Common in women who shave legs. Treatment: oral antifungals (terbinafine, itraconazole, griseofulvin) for 4-8 weeks.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Majocchi granuloma presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Cause | Trichophyton rubrum (most common) |
| Type | Deep dermatophyte infection (granulomatous) |
| Risk | Topical steroid use on tinea (tinea incognito), leg shaving (women) |
| Lesion | Persistent erythematous scaly plaque with follicular pustules/nodules |
| Distribution | Lower legs (women shaving), face, hands |
| Histology | Follicular and perifollicular granulomatous inflammation, hyphae in hair follicle |
| Diagnosis | KOH (hyphae in follicle), biopsy, fungal culture |
| Treatment | Oral antifungals: terbinafine 250mg OD 4-6w, itraconazole 200mg BD 1w/month, griseofulvin |
| Differential | Bacterial folliculitis, Majocchi (granulomatous), tinea incognito, MF |
| Prevention | Avoid topical steroids on undiagnosed tinea |
