# Sjogren syndrome (cutaneous)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Systemic Disease Manifestations

## Definition

Sjögren syndrome is a chronic autoimmune disorder with lymphocytic infiltration of exocrine glands (sicca syndrome: dry eyes/keratoconjunctivitis sicca, dry mouth/xerostomia), often with other autoimmune features. Primary (alone) or secondary (with SLE, RA, scleroderma). Cutaneous: dry skin (xerosis), annular erythema (more common in Asian Sjögren, anti-Ro+), purpura (hypergammaglobulinaemic or vasculitic), Raynaud's, livedo. Diagnosis: ACR/EULAR criteria (sicca symptoms + autoantibodies + labial gland biopsy).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Sjogren syndrome (cutaneous) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Type | Autoimmune exocrinopathy |
| Sicca | Keratoconjunctivitis sicca, xerostomia, dry skin |
| Primary | Alone |
| Secondary | With SLE, RA, scleroderma |
| Autoantibodies | Anti-Ro/SSA (70%), anti-La/SSB (40%), ANA, RF |
| Labial gland biopsy | Lymphocytic sialadenitis (focus score ≥1) |
| Skin | Xerosis, annular erythema (Asian, anti-Ro+), hypergammaglobulinaemic purpura (legs), vasculitic purpura, Raynaud's, livedo |
| Lymphoma | Increased risk (40x; MALT most common) |
| Treatment | Artificial tears, pilocarpine, cevimeline; hydroxychloroquine for systemic; rituximab for severe |
| Diagnosis | ACR/EULAR criteria: ≥4 of 6 (sicca, anti-Ro, ocular staining, Schirmer, salivary flow, biopsy) |
