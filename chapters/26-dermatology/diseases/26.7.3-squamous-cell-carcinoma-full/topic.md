## Definition

Cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma (cSCC) is the second most common skin cancer, arising from keratinocytes of the epidermis. Locally invasive with metastatic potential (5% overall, much higher in immunosuppressed, lip, ear, or poorly differentiated). Pre-malignant: actinic keratosis (AK), Bowen disease (SCC in situ). Risk factors: cumulative UV (chronic sun exposure), immunosuppression (transplant — 65-250x increased risk), HPV (especially 16/18 in genital SCC), arsenic, chronic wounds (Marjolin ulcer), xeroderma pigmentosum, albinism.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

SCC: scaly, hyperkeratotic papule/plaque/nodule, often ulcerated/crusted, on sun-exposed sites (face, ears, scalp, dorsal hands, lower lip). May arise within pre-existing AK or Bowen disease. Dermoscopy: glomerular vessels (Bowen), white circles, keratin masses, looped/linear-irregular vessels. High-risk features: location (ear, lip, non-sun-exposed), size >2cm, depth >6mm or beyond subcutaneous fat, poor differentiation, perineural invasion, immunosuppression. Treatment: surgical excision (4-6mm margin low-risk, 6-10mm high-risk), Mohs (high-risk), curettage+cryotherapy (low-risk in situ), radiotherapy (inoperable), cemiplimab (PD-1 inhibitor) for advanced/metastatic disease.

## 1. DEFINITION & EPIDEMIOLOGY

**Cutaneous Squamous Cell Carcinoma (cSCC)** is a malignant neoplasm of **suprabasal keratinocytes** with potential for local invasion and **metastasis (2-5%)**. Second most common skin cancer (20% of NMSC).

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Incidence** | Rising; 2nd most common cancer (after BCC); ~100/100,000/year |
| **Male:Female** | 2-3:1 |
| **Age** | Peak 60-80 yrs; rare <40 |
| **UV Radiation** | **Cumulative chronic exposure** (vs BCC intermittent) |
| **Immunosuppression** | **OTR: 65-100x risk** (SCC > BCC); HIV, lymphoma |
| **Precursors** | Actinic keratosis (AK), Bowen disease (SCC in situ) |
| **Genetic** | **Xeroderma Pigmentosum** (1000x); **Epidermolysis Bullosa** (recessive dystrophic) |

## 2. CLINICAL FEATURES & SUBTYPES

### 2.1 Classic SCC
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Morphology** | **Keratotic, indurated nodule/plaque**; hyperkeratotic surface; may ulcerate |
| **Growth** | Rapid (weeks-months) vs BCC (slow) |
| **Site** | **Sun-exposed: face, ears, scalp (bald), dorsum hands, forearms, lower lip** |
| **Lip SCC** | Vermillion border; higher metastatic risk (10-15%) |
| **Ear SCC** | Helix; higher metastasis (10-15%) |

### 2.2 SCC In Situ (Bowen Disease)
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Clinical** | Slow-growing, well-demarcated, erythematous **scaly plaque**; trunk/legs |
| **Histology** | **Full-thickness epidermal dysplasia** (no dermal invasion) |
| **Progression** | 3-5% → invasive SCC |
| **Dermoscopy** | Glomerular vessels, scaly surface, white circles |

### 2.3 Keratoacanthoma (KA)
- **Clinical:** Rapid growth (4-8 wks) → **keratin-filled crater** (central keratin plug) → **spontaneous regression** (months) → scar
- **Histology:** Central keratin crater + well-differentiated squamous epithelium; **often considered well-differentiated SCC variant**
- **Management:** Excision (diagnostic/therapeutic); not observation (cannot clinically distinguish from SCC)

### 2.4 High-Risk Variants
| Variant | Features |
|---------|----------|
| **Invasive SCC** | Depth >2 mm, Clark IV/V, perineural invasion |
| **Adenoid SCC** | Glandular differentiation; aggressive |
| **Spindle Cell SCC** | Spindle cells; desmoplastic; high recurrence |
| **Verrucous Carcinoma** | Exophytic, cauliflower-like; low metastasis but locally destructive |

## 3. RISK STRATIFICATION (BRIGHAM & WOMEN'S / AJCC)

| Risk Factor | High-Risk Definition |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **Thickness / Depth** | **>2 mm** (Breslow) or **Clark ≥IV** |
| **Diameter** | **≥2 cm** |
| **Histology** | Poorly differentiated, adenoid, spindle, desmoplastic, single-cell infiltration |
| **Perineural Invasion (PNI)** | **Nerve calibre ≥0.1 mm** or clinical nerve involvement |
| **Site** | **H-zone** (central face, eyelids, ears, lips, chin, temple, pre/postauricular), genital, hands/feet |
| **Recurrent** | Previously treated |
| **Immunosuppression** | OTR, HIV, lymphoma, chronic steroids |
| **Precursor** | Arising in chronic ulcer/scar (Marjolin's ulcer), radiation field, burn scar |

| Risk Category | Criteria |
|---------------|----------|
| **Low** | None of above; primary, <2 cm, well-differentiated, trunk/extremities |
| **High** | **Any one** high-risk feature |

## 4. DIAGNOSTIC WORKUP

### 4.1 Dermoscopy
| Feature | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Glomerular / coiled vessels** | **SCC in situ / early invasive** |
| **Keratotic surface** | White/yellow scale; keratin |
| **White structureless areas** | Hyperkeratosis |
| **Ulceration** | Invasive SCC |
| **Red dots / polymorphous vessels** | Invasive SCC |
| **Absent pigment network** | Distinguishes from melanoma |

> **Differentiation BCC vs SCC:** BCC = arborizing vessels; SCC = glomerular/coiled vessels + keratin.

### 4.2 Biopsy
| Technique | Indication |
|-----------|------------|
| **Incisional / Punch** | Most lesions (assess depth, differentiation) |
| **Excisional** | Small lesions (<1 cm) with 2-3 mm margins |
| **Shave** | Superficial / Bowen disease |

### 4.3 Histopathology
| Grade | Features |
|-------|----------|
| **Well-differentiated** | Abundant keratin pearls, intercellular bridges, minimal atypia |
| **Moderately** | Some keratinization, moderate atypia, mitotic figures |
| **Poorly** | Minimal keratin, high atypia, numerous mitoses, single cells |

**Key Features:** Atypical keratinocytes invading dermis; keratin pearls; intercellular bridges; **single-cell infiltration** (worst prognosis).

## 6. MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Surgical (Gold Standard)

| Technique | Indication | Margins | Cure Rate |
|-----------|------------|---------|-----------|
| **Standard Excision** | Low-risk primary | **4-6 mm** (T1) | 92-97% |
| **Mohs Micrographic Surgery** | High-risk, H-zone, recurrent, PNI, poorly diff, immunosuppressed | **Layer-by-layer** | **99%** primary, **94%** recurrent |
| **Electrodessication & Curettage (C&E)** | Low-risk in situ / superficial trunk | N/A | 90-95% |
| **Radiotherapy** | Non-surgical candidate, adjuvant (PNI+, positive margins), elderly | — | 90-95% |

### 6.2 Adjuvant Radiotherapy Indications
- **Positive margins** (re-excision not feasible)
- **Perineural invasion** (clinical or histologic)
- **Poorly differentiated** / high-grade
- **Depth >6 mm** or **Clark V**
- **Immunosuppressed**
- **Recurrent** after surgery

### 6.3 Non-Surgical (In Situ / Low-Risk Only)
| Modality | Indication | Cure Rate |
|----------|------------|-----------|
| **5-FU 5% cream** | Bowen disease / superficial SCC | 70-80% |
| **Imiquimod 5%** | Bowen disease (off-label) | 70-80% |
| **PDT (MAL/ALA)** | Bowen / superficial SCC | 70-85% |
| **Cryotherapy** | Small in situ / superficial | 85-95% |

> **Contraindicated** for high-risk invasive SCC.

### 6.4 Systemic Therapy (Advanced/Metastatic)

| Agent | Target | Indication | Key Trial |
|-------|--------|------------|-----------|
| **Cemiplimab** (anti-PD-1) | PD-1 | Locally advanced / metastatic SCC (unresectable) | EMPOWER-CSCC-1 (ORR 47%) |
| **Pembrolizumab** | PD-1 | Recurrent/metastatic | KEYNOTE-629 |
| **EGFR inhibitors** (Cetuximab) | EGFR | Combined with RT (definitive/adjuvant) | RTOG 0518 |
| **Cisplatin-based chemo** | Cytotoxic | Palliative (historical) | — |

> **Cemiplimab:** FDA-approved 2018 for metastatic/locally advanced cSCC. ORR ~47%, durable responses.

## 7. PERINEURAL INVASION (PNI) — Critical High-Risk Feature

| PNI Type | Definition | Management |
|----------|------------|------------|
| **Histologic (Incidental)** | Nerve calibre <0.1 mm, asymptomatic | Wide excision + adjuvant RT |
| **Clinical (Symptomatic)** | Nerve calibre ≥0.1 mm OR neurological symptoms (numbness, pain, paralysis) | **Multidisciplinary:** Surgery (nerve sacrifice if needed) + **RT (60-66 Gy)** ± Cemiplimab |

> **Brain MRI** if cranial nerve involvement suspected (V, VII, skull base).

---

## 8. LYMPH NODE MANAGEMENT

| Scenario | Management |
|----------|------------|
| **Clinical N0 (no palpable nodes)** | **Observation**; no routine SLNB (unlike melanoma) |
| **Clinical N+ (palpable nodes)** | **Therapeutic lymphadenectomy** (radical neck/parotid/axillary/inguinal) + adjuvant RT |
| **Occult nodal mets** | Imaging (US/CT/PET) for staging; SLNB **not standard** (unlike melanoma) |

---

## 9. IMMUNOSUPPRESSED PATIENTS (OTR, HIV)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **SCC:BCC ratio** | **Reversed** (SCC 3:1 BCC in OTR vs 1:4 immunocompetent) |
| **Aggressiveness** | Higher recurrence, metastasis, multiple primaries |
| **Field cancerization** | Multiple AKs, Bowen, SCCs |
| **Surveillance** | **3-6 monthly** skin exams; dermatologist-led |
| **Chemoprevention** | **Acitretin** 10-30 mg/day; **Nicotinamide** 500 mg BD (ONTRAC trial); Capecitabine topical |
| **mTOR inhibitors** | **Sirolimus** conversion reduces SCC risk (TUMORAP trial) |
| **HPV vaccination** | Pre-transplant if possible (beta-HPV oncogenic role) |

## 12. QUICK COMPARISON: SCC vs BCC vs MELANOMA

| Feature | **SCC** | **BCC** | **Melanoma** |
|---------|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Origin** | Suprabasal keratinocytes | Basal keratinocytes | Melanocytes |
| **Metastasis** | **2-5%** | 0.003% | **Common** |
| **Precursor** | Actinic keratosis, Bowen | None | Dysplastic naevus |
| **UV Pattern** | **Cumulative** | Intermittent | Intermittent |
| **Dermoscopy** | Glomerular vessels + keratin | Arborizing vessels | Atypical network |
| **High-risk features** | Size, depth, PNI, diff | Site, subtype, size | Thickness, ulceration |
| **Margins** | 4-6 mm (low) / Mohs | 4 mm / Mohs | Breslow-based |
