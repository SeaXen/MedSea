# Morphea

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Systemic Disease Manifestations

## Definition

Morphea (localised scleroderma) is a chronic autoimmune connective tissue disease limited to the skin, characterised by sclerotic plaques (often with violaceous border) without systemic involvement. Distinct from systemic sclerosis (no Raynaud's, no sclerodactyly, no internal organ involvement, no nailfold capillary changes, anti-centromere/Scl-70 negative). Subtypes: circumscribed (most common, oval/plaque), linear (limbs, head in children — en coup de sabre), generalised (more than 3 plaques, more than 3 cm), pansclerotic (rare, severe), mixed.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Morphea presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29 (and standard dermatology curriculum). The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures, trigger avoidance, and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Type | Localised scleroderma (skin-limited) |
| Distribution | Trunk (circumscribed), limbs/face (linear), widespread (generalised) |
| Lesion | Indurated sclerotic plaque, ivory-coloured centre, violaceous/lilac ring (active border) |
| Linear | Linear scleroderma; en coup de sabre (forehead, paramedian) |
| Generalised | More than 3 plaques affecting at least 2 of 7 body areas |
| Histology | Thickened collagen bundles in dermis, loss of adnexal structures, lymphoplasmacytic infiltrate (early) |
| ANA | Positive in 30-40% (anti-histone) |
| Differential | Systemic sclerosis (Raynaud's, systemic), lichen sclerosus, morphoeic BCC, post-radiation, drug (bleomycin) |
| Treatment | Topical/intralesional steroids, calcipotriol, methotrexate (linear, generalised), mycophenolate, PUVA, JAK inhibitors (emerging) |
| Prognosis | Self-limiting in 3-5 years usually; functional impairment in linear; cosmetic concern in en coup de sabre |
| Internal | Rare (more in generalised); annual review |
