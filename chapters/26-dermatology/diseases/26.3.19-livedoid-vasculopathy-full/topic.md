# Livedoid vasculopathy

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Urticaria Erythema and Purpura

## Definition

Livedoid vasculopathy (livedo reticularis with summer/winter ulceration) is a thrombotic disorder of cutaneous venules, characterised by livedo reticularis, atrophie blanche (white atrophic scars), and painful ulceration. Associated with hypercoagulable states (protein C/S deficiency, antiphospholipid syndrome, factor V Leiden), connective tissue disease (SLE).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Livedoid vasculopathy presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Type | Thrombotic vasculopathy (not true vasculitis) |
| Triad | Livedo reticularis, atrophie blanche, painful ulceration |
| Sites | Ankles, lower legs, dorsal feet |
| Pain | Severe, especially with ulceration |
| DIF | Fibrin in vessel walls (no immune complex) |
| Associations | Hypercoagulable states (protein C/S, factor V Leiden, antiphospholipid), SLE, hyperhomocysteinaemia |
| Differential | Medium-vessel vasculitis, livedo with ulceration in SLE/APS, cholesterol emboli |
| Treatment | Anticoagulation (warfarin, LMWH), antiplatelet, pentoxifylline, danazol, immunosuppression if associated |
| Prognosis | Chronic, recurrent, painful |
