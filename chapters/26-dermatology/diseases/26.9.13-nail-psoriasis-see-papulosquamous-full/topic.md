# Nail psoriasis (see Papulosquamous)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Hair and Nail Disorders

## Definition

Nail psoriasis affects 40-50% of psoriasis patients overall (up to 80% in PsA) and can be the only manifestation in 1-5%. Nail matrix involvement: pitting, onychorrhexis, leukonychia. Nail bed involvement: onycholysis, oil drop sign, subungual hyperkeratosis. Severity: NAPSI (Nail Psoriasis Severity Index).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Nail psoriasis (see Papulosquamous) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Prevalence | 40-50% of psoriasis (up to 80% in PsA) |
| Pitting | Irregular, deep, scattered (vs regular fine pits of alopecia areata) |
| Onycholysis | Separation of plate from bed, often with erythematous border |
| Oil drop sign | Salmon-pink patch under nail (pathognomonic) |
| NAPSI | Nail Psoriasis Severity Index 0-100 |
| PsA | 80% of psoriatic arthritis have nail changes |
| Treatment | Topical: potent steroids, vitamin D analogues, tazarotene, intralesional triamcinolone |
| Systemic | Biologics (most effective for nails), methotrexate, acitretin, ciclosporin |
| Differential | Onychomycosis (KOH+), lichen planus (ridging, pterygium), AA (regular pitting) |
| Co-infection | Up to 20% onychomycosis with psoriasis |
