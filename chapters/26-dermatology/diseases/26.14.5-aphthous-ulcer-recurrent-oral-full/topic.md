# Aphthous ulcer (recurrent oral)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Emergencies

## Definition

Recurrent aphthous stomatitis (RAS, canker sores) is a common condition with recurrent, painful, well-circumscribed, round/oval ulcers of the oral mucosa (non-keratinised: buccal, labial, floor of mouth, soft palate), with grey-white pseudomembrane and erythematous halo. Three types: minor (80%, <1cm, heal 7-14d, no scarring), major (Sutton disease, >1cm, deeper, heal weeks-months, scarring), herpetiform (clusters of 1-3mm ulcers). Differentiate from Behçet (genital, eye, skin), HSV (keratinised mucosa, gingiva, hard palate, vesicles), erosive LP (Wickham striae), SCC, systemic disease (Crohn's, coeliac, HIV, Behcet, cyclic neutropenia). Treatment: topical anaesthetics, topical steroids (triamcinolone), sucralfate, tetracycline mouthwash; refractory: colchicine, thalidomide, dapsone, pentoxifylline.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures, trigger avoidance, and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment
