## Definition

IgA vasculitis (Henoch-Schönlein purpura, HSP) is the most common childhood systemic vasculitis, characterized by IgA-dominant immune deposits affecting small vessels. It typically affects children 3-15 years (peak 4-6 years), often 1-2 weeks after an upper respiratory tract infection. Pathogenesis involves IgA1-containing immune complex deposition in vessel walls, mesangium, and glomeruli.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Classic tetrad: (1) Palpable purpura on buttocks and lower legs (100%); (2) Arthralgia/arthritis in large joints (75%, non-erosive); (3) Abdominal pain (50%, may include intussusception); (4) Renal involvement (30-50%, IgA nephropathy-like, ranging from haematuria to nephrotic-nephritic syndrome). Other features: scrotal pain, peripheral oedema, GI bleeding. Skin biopsy shows leukocytoclastic vasculitis with IgA deposits on DIF. Most cases self-limit in 4-6 weeks; chronic renal disease in ~1%.

## 1. DEFINITION & EPIDEMIOLOGY

**IgA Vasculitis (Henoch-Schönlein Purpura / HSP)** is the most common **small-vessel vasculitis of childhood**, characterized by **IgA-dominant immune complex deposition** in vessel walls.

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Incidence** | 10-20/100,000/year (children); 1-2/100,000 (adults) |
| **Age** | **Children 2-10 yrs** (peak 4-6); adults <10% |
| **Sex** | M = F (children); M > F (adults) |
| **Seasonal** | Winter/spring predominance |
| **Trigger** | **Upper respiratory infection** (Streptococcus, Mycoplasma, viruses) 50%; drugs, vaccines, foods |

---

## 2. PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

| Mechanism | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Immune Complex** | **IgA1** (galactose-deficient) + **anti-IgA (IgG)** → **IgA-IgG complexes** |
| **Deposition** | Dermal capillaries/venules → complement (C3) activation → neutrophil recruitment |
| **Genetic** | **HLA-DRB1*01, *11**; **CFH/CFHR** variants; **IGA1** galactosylation defects |
| **Mucosal Origin** | IgA from mucosal immune response (tonsils, gut) → systemic circulation |

## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES — The Classic TETRAD (PARK)

| Feature | Frequency | Details |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **P**alpable Purpura | **100%** | **Symmetric, lower extremities** (ankles, legs, buttocks); **non-blanching**; crops; may evolve to vesicles/bullae/ulcers |
| **A**bdominal Pain | 50-75% | **Colicky**, periumbilical; **intussusception** (ileo-ileal, 2-5%); melena/hematochezia |
| **R**enal (Haematuria/Proteinuria) | 40-50% | **Microscopic haematuria** ± proteinuria; **IgA nephropathy** on biopsy; nephrotic/nephritic syndrome |
| **K**nees/Ankles Arthritis/Arthralgia | 50-75% | **Migratory**, large joints (knees, ankles); non-erosive; self-limiting (days) |

### Other Features
- **Fever** (low-grade, 30%)
- **Scrotal edema** (boys, 10-20%)
- **Pulmonary** (rare: alveolar hemorrhage)
- **CNS** (rare: headache, seizures, encephalopathy)

### Adult vs Child Differences
| Feature | Children | Adults |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Renal** | Usually mild; 1-2% ESRD | **More severe**; 10-20% progressive CKD |
| **GI** | Common intussusception | Less common; more severe |
| **Recurrence** | 30-40% (4-6 months) | Lower |

## 3. DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA

### EULAR/PRINTO/PRES 2010 Classification Criteria
**Mandatory:** Palpable purpura (non-thrombocytopenic)

**Plus ≥1 of:**
1. **Diffuse abdominal pain**
2. **Arthritis/Arthralgia** (acute, migratory)
3. **Renal involvement** (haematuria/proteinuria)
4. **Biopsy:** Granulocytic infiltration + **IgA-dominant** vascular deposits

> **Sensitivity 87%, Specificity 88%**

## 4. INVESTIGATIONS

### 4.1 Routine
| Test | Purpose | Typical Findings |
|------|---------|------------------|
| **FBC** | Platelets, eosinophilia | Normal platelets (vs ITP); mild eosinophilia |
| **Coagulation (PT/APTT)** | Exclude coagulopathy | Normal |
| **Urine ACR / Dipstick** | **Renal screening** | **Haematuria ± proteinuria** (ACR >30 mg/mmol = significant) |
| **Renal Function (U&E, Creat)** | Baseline | Normal (unless severe nephritis) |
| **CRP/ESR** | Inflammation | Elevated |
| **ASOT / Throat Swab** | Streptococcal trigger | Often elevated |

### 4.2 Skin Biopsy (Leukocytoclastic Vasculitis + DIF) — **Gold Standard**
| Finding | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **H&E** | **Leukocytoclastic vasculitis (LCV):** Neutrophilic infiltrate, nuclear dust (leukocytoclasis), fibrin, RBC extravasation |
| **DIF** | **IgA-dominant deposits** in vessel walls (dermal capillaries/venules) ± C3, IgG, IgM |
| **Timing** | Early lesion (<24-48h); perilesional skin |

> **DIF Pattern:** **IgA > IgG/IgM** (dominant) — **pathognomonic for IgA vasculitis/HSP**

### 4.3 Renal Biopsy (Indications)
- Proteinuria >1 g/day or ACR >200 mg/mmol
- Nephrotic syndrome
- Impaired renal function
- Persistent haematuria/proteinuria >6 months

**ISKDC Classification (Histology):**
| Grade | Histology | Prognosis |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| **I** | Minimal changes | Excellent |
| **II** | Mesangial proliferation | Good |
| **III** | Focal/proliferative + crescents <50% | Variable |
| **IV** | Diffuse proliferative + crescents 50-75% | Guarded |
| **V** | Diffuse proliferative + crescents >75% | Poor |
| **VI** | Membranoproliferative / sclerosis | Poor |

## 5. MANAGEMENT

### 5.1 General Principles
- **Most cases self-limited** (4-6 weeks); supportive care
- **Hospitalize** if: severe GI (intussusception), renal (nephrotic/nephritic), severe arthritis, unable to tolerate oral

### 5.2 Treatment by Domain

#### Skin (Purpura)
- **Supportive:** Leg elevation, compression stockings, analgesia (paracetamol)
- **No routine steroids** for skin alone

#### Joints (Arthritis/Arthralgia)
- **NSAIDs** (ibuprofen, naproxen) — **avoid if renal impairment**
- **Short-course prednisolone** (1 mg/kg/day × 1-2 weeks) if severe

#### Abdomen (Pain/Intussusception)
- **Pain management:** Paracetamol, opioids (avoid NSAIDs if renal)
- **Prednisolone 1-2 mg/kg/day** (max 60 mg) → **rapid pain relief**; taper 2 weeks
- **Intussusception:** **Air enema reduction** (radiology); surgery if failed/peritonitis

#### Kidney (IgA Nephropathy)
| Severity | Treatment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Isolated haematuria** (ACR <30) | **Observation**; 6-monthly urine ACR, BP |
| **Mild proteinuria** (ACR 30-200) | **ACEi/ARB** (renoprotective); monitor 3-6 monthly |
| **Nephrotic / Nephritic / RPGN** (ACR >200, crescents) | **Prednisolone 1-2 mg/kg/day** (max 60 mg) × 4-8 wks + **Cyclophosphamide** (IV pulse 500-1000 mg/m² monthly × 6) OR **MMF** 1-2 g/day OR **Rituximab** (375 mg/m² × 4) |
| **Crescentic GN** | **Pulse methylprednisolone** 500-1000 mg IV × 3-5 days + oral taper + Cyclophosphamide |

> **ACEi/ARB first-line** for any proteinuria (BP control + antiproteinuric).

### 5.3 Refractory / Relapsing Disease
| Agent | Indication |
|-------|------------|
| **Dapsone** | Chronic/recurrent cutaneous; 50-100 mg/day |
| **Azathioprine** | Steroid-sparing; 1-2 mg/kg |
| **MMF** | Steroid-sparing; 1-2 g/day |
| **Rituximab** | Refractory renal (crescentic); 375 mg/m² × 4 |
| **Tonsillectomy** | Recurrent HSP + recurrent tonsillitis (Japanese data) |

## 6. MONITORING & FOLLOW-UP

| Parameter | Frequency | Duration |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Urine dipstick / ACR** | **Weekly × 1 month → monthly × 6 months → 6-monthly × 2 years** | **Minimum 2 years** (late renal onset up to 6-12 months) |
| **BP** | Each visit | Long-term |
| **Renal function (U&E, Creat)** | Monthly × 3 → 3-monthly | 2 years |
| **FBC, ESR/CRP** | Monthly × 3 | 6 months |

> **Late renal onset:** Up to 6-12 months post-onset; monitor for **2 years minimum**.

## 7. PROGNOSIS

| Outcome | Rate |
|---------|------|
| **Complete resolution** | 90% (children); 70% (adults) |
| **Recurrence** | 30-40% (within 4-6 months; rarely >1 yr) |
| **Chronic renal disease** | 1-2% children → ESRD; 10-20% adults |
| **Hypertension** | 5-10% (renal sequelae) |
| **Mortality** | <1% (rare: GI perforation, RPGN, CNS) |

**Poor Prognostic Factors (Renal):**
- Adult onset
- Nephrotic syndrome at presentation
- Crescents >50% on biopsy (ISKDC IV-VI)
- Persistent proteinuria >6 months
- Hypertension at onset

## 8. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

| Condition | Differentiating Features |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **IgA Vasculitis (HSP)** | IgA DIF; tetrad; children; self-limited |
| **ANCA Vasculitis (GPA/MPA)** | ANCA +ve; no IgA dominance; older; systemic |
| **Cryoglobulinaemic Vasculitis** | Cryoglobulins +ve; low C4; HCV association; older |
| **Urticarial Vasculitis** | Wheals >24h; burning; low C3/C4; C1q DIF |
| **Meningococcaemia** | Acute sepsis; purpura fulminans; DIC; Gram -ve diplococci |
| **ITP** | Thrombocytopenia; no vasculitis on biopsy; no IgA deposits |
| **Henoch-Schönlein vs IgA Vasculitis** | **Same disease**; HSP = clinical (paediatric); IgA Vasculitis = histopathological |
