# Actinic keratosis (solar keratosis)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Tumours

## Definition

Actinic keratosis (AK, solar keratosis) is a UV-induced premalignant lesion of the epidermis, common on sun-exposed sites (face, scalp, dorsal hands, forearms) in older fair-skinned individuals. Rough, scaly, often multiple. Risk of progression to cutaneous SCC (~0.1-10% per lesion, higher with multiple lesions). Field cancerisation concept. Treatment: cryotherapy, topical 5-FU, imiquimod, ingenol mebutate, photodynamic therapy.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Actinic keratosis (solar keratosis) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Cause | Cumulative UV exposure |
| Sites | Sun-exposed: face, scalp, dorsal hands, forearms, ears, lips |
| Appearance | Rough, scaly, erythematous or skin-coloured; may be palpated better than seen |
| Grading | I (mild), II (moderate), III (thick/hyperkeratotic) |
| Field cancerisation | Area with multiple AKs and sub-clinical changes |
| Risk to SCC | 0.1-10% per lesion per year; higher with multiple |
| Treatment | Cryotherapy (single lesions); field: 5-FU, imiquimod, ingenol mebutate, PDT, diclofenac, tirbanibulin |
| Prevention | Sun protection: SPF 30+, hat, clothing, avoid midday sun |
| Differential | Seborrhoeic keratosis, SCC (in situ or invasive), psoriasis, lupus |
| Histology | Atypical keratinocytes in lower epidermis, parakeratosis, solar elastosis |

## Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| SCC | Indurated, nodular, ulceration, faster growth |
