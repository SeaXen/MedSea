# Systemic sclerosis (limited, diffuse, calcinosis, telangiectasia)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Systemic Disease Manifestations

## Definition

Systemic sclerosis (SSc, scleroderma) is an autoimmune connective tissue disease with skin thickening (sclerosis) and internal organ involvement. Limited cutaneous (lcSSc, CREST: Calcinosis, Raynaud's, Esophageal dysmotility, Sclerodactyly, Telangiectasia) — limited skin involvement (distal to elbows/knees, face), anti-centromere. Diffuse cutaneous (dcSSc) — proximal skin involvement, anti-Scl-70 (topoisomerase I), rapid internal organ involvement (ILD, renal crisis, cardiac). Treatment: organ-specific (vasodilators for RP and PAH, immunosuppressants for ILD, ACEi for renal crisis).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Systemic sclerosis (limited, diffuse, calcinosis, telangiectasia) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| CREST | Calcinosis, Raynaud's, Esophageal dysmotility, Sclerodactyly, Telangiectasia (limited) |
| lcSSc | Limited skin (distal); anti-centromere; better prognosis; late PAH |
| dcSSc | Diffuse skin (proximal); anti-Scl-70 (topo I); ILD, renal crisis, cardiac |
| Raynaud's | Triphasic colour change (white-blue-red); digital ulcers, gangrene |
| Skin | Thickening, sclerodactyly, telangiectasia (face, lips, palms), calcinosis (FCR, extensor surfaces), pigment changes |
| Diagnosis | ACR/EULAR criteria; ANA, anti-centromere, anti-Scl-70; capillaroscopy (scleroderma pattern) |
| Treatment | RP: CCB, iloprost, sildenafil, bosentan; ILD: MMF, nintedanib, tocilizumab, rituximab; renal crisis: ACEi; PR: physiotherapy |
| Renal crisis | Accelerated HTN, ARF, microangiopathic haemolytic anaemia; ACEi; prognosis poor if untreated |
| Calcinosis | Calcium deposits in soft tissue; may ulcerate |
