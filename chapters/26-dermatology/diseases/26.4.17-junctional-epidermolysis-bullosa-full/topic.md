# Junctional epidermolysis bullosa

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Vesiculobullous Disorders

## Definition

Junctional epidermolysis bullosa (JEB) is a severe form of EB with blistering at the lamina lucida (within BMZ). Caused by mutations in LAMA3, LAMB3, LAMC2 (laminin-332/laminin-5) or COL17A1 (BP180). Severe (Herlitz) form often fatal in infancy; intermediate form is non-lethal.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Junctional epidermolysis bullosa presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Inheritance | Autosomal recessive |
| Genes | LAMA3, LAMB3, LAMC2 (laminin-332); COL17A1 (BP180) |
| Split | Lamina lucida |
| Severe (Herlitz) | Often fatal in infancy; widespread blistering, granulation tissue, nail dystrophy, enamel hypoplasia |
| Intermediate | Non-lethal; localised or generalised blistering, improves with age |
| EM | Split in lamina lucida; reduced hemidesmosomes |
| Associated | Enamel hypoplasia (tooth abnormalities), pyloric atresia (in JEB-PA with α6β4 integrin mutation) |
| Management | Wound care, infection prevention, nutritional support, genetic counselling |
| Laryngo-onycho-cutaneous syndrome | JEB variant with laryngeal and ocular granulation |
| Mortality | Severe (Herlitz) form 40-50% in first year |
