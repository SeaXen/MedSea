# Xeroderma pigmentosum

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Tumours

## Definition

Xeroderma pigmentosum (XP) is a rare autosomal recessive disorder with defective nucleotide excision repair of UV-induced DNA damage, leading to extreme photosensitivity and early skin cancers (BCC, SCC, melanoma) from childhood. Severe sunburn, freckling, photophobia, neurodegeneration (DeSanctis-Cacchione syndrome in some). Strict photoprotection essential. Treatment: isotretinoin (chemoprevention), frequent skin exams, surgery for cancers.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Xeroderma pigmentosum presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Cause | Defect in nucleotide excision repair (NER) of UV damage |
| Genes | XPA-XPG (7 complementation groups); XPV (polymerase eta) |
| Skin | Severe sunburn, freckling, poikiloderma, xerosis, lentigines from infancy |
| Eyes | Photophobia, keratitis, corneal opacities, lid cancers |
| Cancers | BCC, SCC, melanoma from childhood; 1000x normal risk |
| Neurological | DeSanctis-Cacchione (XPA) — progressive neurodegeneration |
| Prevention | Strict photoprotection: avoid sun, UV-protective clothing, window film, sunscreen |
| Treatment | Isotretinoin (chemoprevention), surgery, regular skin/eye exams |
| Prognosis | Median survival 30-40 years; often skin cancer deaths |
| Mosaicism | XP mosaics: revertant patches |
