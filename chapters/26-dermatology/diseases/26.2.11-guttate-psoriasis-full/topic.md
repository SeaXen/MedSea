# Guttate psoriasis

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Guttate psoriasis is an acute-onset psoriasis variant with numerous small (2-10mm) droplet-like erythematous scaly papules, typically 1-2 weeks after streptococcal pharyngitis. Common in children/young adults (peak 5-15). 30-40% progress to chronic plaque psoriasis; 50-70% resolve in 3-4 months. HHV-6 may also be implicated.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Sudden eruption of hundreds of small (2-10mm), round, salmon-pink, tear-drop papules with fine scale, symmetrically distributed on trunk and proximal limbs (sparing palms/soles). Lesions appear in crops over 1-2 weeks. Often preceded by URTI (1-2 weeks), especially streptococcal. Mild pruritus. Koebner phenomenon common.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Throat swab (ASO titre, anti-DNase B). Skin biopsy rarely needed (characteristic).

### Management

Tonsillectomy reduces recurrences in chronic cases. Topical: mild-moderate potency corticosteroids, vitamin D analogues, emollients. Phototherapy: NB-UVB (highly effective). Antibiotics if active strep infection (penicillin V, erythromycin).
