# DRESS-DIHS

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Emergencies

## Definition

DRESS-DIHS is a dermatological emergency discussed in Davidson 24e Ch 29. Includes severe cutaneous adverse reactions (SJS/TEN, DRESS, AGEP), necrotising fasciitis, staphylococcal scalded skin syndrome, eczema herpeticum, erythroderma, and acute presentations of autoimmune bullous disease.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

DRESS-DIHS is a clinical entity within the Emergencies group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Definition | Life-threatening skin condition requiring urgent intervention |
| Epidemiology | SJS/TEN 1-2/million; nec fasc 0.4/10000; anaphylaxis common |
| Aetiology | Drugs (SCARs), infection (nec fasc, SSSS), systemic disease (erythroderma) |
| Pathophysiology | Keratinocyte apoptosis (SJS/TEN), deep fascial infection, immune hyperactivation |
| Clinical features | Widespread blistering, skin sloughing, Nikolsky +ve, systemic toxicity |
| Diagnosis | Clinical urgency; SCORTEN, LRINEC; biopsy for SSSS vs TEN |
| Management (1st) | Stop culprit drug; ICU/burns unit; fluid resuscitation; supportive |
| Management (2nd) | IVIG, ciclosporin, etanercept (SJS/TEN); broad-spectrum antibiotics (nec fasc) |
| Management (3rd) | Plasmapheresis, surgical debridement, multidisciplinary ICU |
| Complications | Sepsis, ARDS, AKI, mortality 25-35% (SJS/TEN), 25% (nec fasc) |
