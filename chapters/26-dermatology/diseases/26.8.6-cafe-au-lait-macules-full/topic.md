# Cafe-au-lait macules

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Disorders of Pigmentation

## Definition

Café-au-lait macules (CALMs) are well-circumscribed, uniformly pigmented (light to dark brown) macules/patches present in 10-30% of normal population. Smooth border ('coast of California'). Multiple CALMs (≥3) may indicate neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1) — diagnostic criterion. Other associations: McCune-Albright syndrome (large, 'coast of Maine' border), Fanconi anaemia, Noonan syndrome, tuberous sclerosis (rare).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Cafe-au-lait macules presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Colour | Light to dark brown (coffee with milk) |
| Size | 0.5-20 cm |
| Shape | Round, oval, smooth border ('coast of California') |
| Prevalence | 10-30% normal population |
| NF1 criterion | ≥6 CALMs >5mm (prepubertal) or >15mm (postpubertal) |
| McCune-Albright | Large, irregular 'coast of Maine' border; ipsilateral bony abnormalities, precocious puberty |
| Other | Fanconi anaemia, Noonan, Legius, TS (rare) |
| Histology | Increased melanin in basal keratinocytes (no melanocyte increase) |
| Differential | Becker's naevus, congenital melanocytic naevus, mastocytoma, melanoma |
