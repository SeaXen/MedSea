# Laboratory investigations

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

## Definition

Dermatology laboratory investigations include haematology (FBC, eosinophils), biochemistry (LFT, renal), immunology (ANA, ANCA, complement, autoantibodies), microbiology (swabs, scrapings, culture), and serology (HIV, hepatitis). Pre-biologic screening is mandatory.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Laboratory investigations is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| FBC | Eosinophilia: drug eruption, DRESS, parasitosis, atopy, CTCL |
| LFT | Baseline and monitoring for methotrexate, retinoids, azathioprine |
| Renal | U&E, creatinine — pre-methotrexate, ciclosporin monitoring |
| ANA | SLE, Sjögren, MCTD, dermatomyositis; titre and pattern (homogeneous, speckled, nucleolar, centromere) |
| ANCA | c-ANCA (PR3) = GPA; p-ANCA (MPO) = MPA, EGPA |
| ENA panel | Anti-Ro/La, anti-Sm, anti-RNP, anti-Scl-70, anti-Jo-1, anti-Mi-2 |
| HIV/Hep B/C | Pre-biologic screening |
| TB screening | IGRA or Mantoux; CXR |
| Microbiology | Swab (bacterial), scrape (fungal KOH), Tzanck (HSV), Wood's lamp, culture |
| Histology | H&E for morphology; DIF for autoimmune; IHC for tumour typing |
