# Topical therapy (vehicles, potency, calculations)

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

Topical therapy uses vehicle (cream, ointment, lotion, gel, foam, paste) and active drug. Vehicle selection affects potency and suitability. Steroid potency is classified mild to very potent. Fingertip unit (FTU) standardises application quantity.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Topical therapy (vehicles, potency, calculations) is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Vehicles | Ointment (occlusive, dry skin), Cream (cosmetically acceptable), Lotion (hairy areas), Gel (acne, scalp), Foam (scalp, large areas), Paste (lichenified) |
| Steroid potency classes | Mild (hydrocortisone 1%), Moderate (clobetasone butyrate), Potent (betamethasone valerate 0.1%), Very potent (clobetasol propionate 0.05%) |
| Fingertip unit (FTU) | 0.5g — covers 2 palm-sized areas (~300cm²) |
| Adult dosing | Face/neck 2.5 FTU; trunk front/back 7+14 FTU; arm 3 FTU; leg 6 FTU |
| Mechanism | Anti-inflammatory, immunosuppressive, antiproliferative, vasoconstrictive |
| Side effects | Skin atrophy, striae, telangiectasia, perioral dermatitis, HPA suppression (potent, large areas) |
| TCI | Tacrolimus 0.03%/0.1% and pimecrolimus 1%; face/folds; no atrophy |
| Vitamin D analogues | Calcipotriol, calcitriol, tacalcitol; psoriasis; combination with steroid |
| Other topicals | Retinoids (acne, psoriasis), antifungals, antibiotics, dithranol, tar, keratolytics (urea, salicylic acid) |
| Rules | Use weakest effective; short courses potent; daily; taper; occlusion increases potency 10× |

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** What is a fingertip unit (FTU) and how is it used?
   **A:** 1 FTU = 0.5g = amount squeezed from tube onto adult fingertip; covers 2 palm-sized areas (300cm²); standardises dosing to avoid overuse.

## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **FTU** = 0.5g per adult fingertip; covers 2 palm-sized areas; face/neck 2.5 FTU; arm 3 FTU; leg 6 FTU
