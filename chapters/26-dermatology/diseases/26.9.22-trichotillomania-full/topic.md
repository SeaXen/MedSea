# Trichotillomania

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Hair and Nail Disorders

## Definition

Trichotillomania (TTM) is a psychiatric disorder (DSM-5 obsessive-compulsive related) characterised by recurrent pulling out of one's own hair, resulting in hair loss. May involve scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, pubic, axillary. More common in children (often self-limiting) and adult women. Associated with anxiety, depression, OCD. Treatment: CBT (habit reversal training), SSRIs, N-acetylcysteine.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Trichotillomania presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Type | Psychiatric (OCD-related) |
| Behaviour | Recurrent hair pulling |
| Site | Scalp (most common), eyebrows, eyelashes, pubic |
| Patch | Irregular, asymmetric, incomplete hair loss (broken hairs of varying length) |
| Trichoscopy | Hairs broken at different lengths, V-sign, trichoptilosis (split ends), no exclamation marks |
| Psychiatric | Anxiety, depression, OCD, body dysmorphic disorder |
| Children | More common; often self-limiting; consider habit |
| Adults | Often chronic, women more; comorbidity common |
| Treatment | CBT (habit reversal training), SSRIs, N-acetylcysteine, olanzapine |
| Differential | AA (smooth bald patch, exclamation marks), tinea (KOH+, scaling) |
| Classification | Body-focused repetitive behaviour (BFRB) |
