# Phototherapy (UVB, PUVA)

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

Phototherapy uses ultraviolet light to treat skin disease. NB-UVB (311nm) is first-line for psoriasis, atopic dermatitis, vitiligo. PUVA (psoralen + UVA 320-400nm) is more potent but carcinogenic. Excimer laser (308nm) targets individual plaques.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Phototherapy (UVB, PUVA) is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| NB-UVB | 311nm; psoriasis, eczema, vitiligo, pruritus; safer than PUVA |
| PUVA | Psoralen (8-MOP or 5-MOP) + UVA 320-400nm; psoriasis, CTCL, vitiligo |
| Mechanism | Immunosuppression (Langerhans cell depletion), antiproliferative, induction of apoptosis |
| Dosing | Start at MED/MPD; incremental 10-20% per session; 2-3x/week |
| Indications | Psoriasis, atopic dermatitis, vitiligo, CTCL (PUVA), pruritus, lichen planus |
| Contraindications | Photosensitivity (SLE, xeroderma pigmentosum), pregnancy (PUVA), melanoma history |
| Side effects | Erythema, burning, photoaging, skin cancer risk (PUVA>SCC>melanoma) |
| Eye protection | UV-blocking goggles during and 12-24h after PUVA |
| Combination | PUVA + acitretin (RePUVA), NB-UVB + biologics (caution) |
| Monitoring | Cumulative dose, lifetime PUVA <200 sessions, annual skin check |

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** Compare NB-UVB and PUVA.
   **A:** NB-UVB: 311nm, no psoralen, safer, first-line for psoriasis, vitiligo, eczema. PUVA: psoralen + UVA, more potent, higher skin cancer risk (SCC > BCC > melanoma), cataract risk; needs eye protection 12-24h.

## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **NB-UVB vs PUVA** = NB-UVB = safer, first-line; PUVA = more potent + more carcinogenic
