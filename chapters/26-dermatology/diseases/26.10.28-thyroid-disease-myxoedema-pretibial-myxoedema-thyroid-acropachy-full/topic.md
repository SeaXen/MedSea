# Thyroid disease (myxoedema, pretibial myxoedema, thyroid acropachy)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Systemic Disease Manifestations

## Definition

Cutaneous manifestations of thyroid disease: hypothyroidism (dry, cool, pale skin, coarse hair, alopecia, brittle nails, myxoedema; periorbital non-pitting oedema from glycosaminoglycan deposition; carotenoderma); hyperthyroidism (warm, moist, smooth skin, hyperpigmented, hair loss, onycholysis, Plummer nails, pretibial myxoedema in Graves — pink/pebbly plaques on shins with orange peel appearance, thyroid acropachy = digital clubbing + soft tissue swelling + periosteal reaction).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Thyroid disease (myxoedema, pretibial myxoedema, thyroid acropachy) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Hypothyroid | Dry cool pale skin, coarse hair, alopecia, brittle nails, carotenoderma (yellow palms), myxoedema |
| Hyperthyroid | Warm moist smooth skin, onycholysis (Plummer nail), hair loss, pretibial myxoedema (Graves) |
| Pretibial myxoedema | Pink waxy plaques, orange peel appearance, shins; Graves-specific (often with ophthalmopathy and acropachy) |
| Thyroid acropachy | Clubbing + soft tissue swelling + periosteal reaction; Graves |
| Hashimoto | Hypothyroid + anti-TPO/anti-TG antibodies; can have associated alopecia areata, vitiligo |
| Graves | Hyperthyroid + TSH receptor antibodies; pretibial myxoedema, ophthalmopathy, acropachy |
| Treatment | Treat underlying thyroid disease; pretibial myxoedema: potent topical steroids, compression |
| Investigation | TSH, free T4, T3; antibodies (anti-TPO, anti-TG, TRAb) |
| Associations | Other autoimmune (vitiligo, AA, coeliac) |
