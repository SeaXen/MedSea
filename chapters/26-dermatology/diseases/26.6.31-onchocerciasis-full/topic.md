# Onchocerciasis

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Infections

## Definition

Onchocerciasis ('river blindness') is a filarial infection by Onchocerca volvulus transmitted by blackfly (Simulium) bite, endemic in sub-Saharan Africa, Yemen, Latin America. Adult worms in skin produce microfilariae causing severe pruritus, skin atrophy, 'leopard skin', 'hanging groin', and ocular involvement leading to blindness (irreversible keratitis, iridocyclitis). Treatment: ivermectin (kills microfilariae, not adults); doxycycline (targets Wolbachia endosymbiont).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Onchocerciasis presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Cause | Onchocerca volvulus (filarial nematode) |
| Vector | Blackfly (Simulium) — breeds in fast-flowing rivers |
| Endemic | Sub-Saharan Africa, Yemen, Latin America (especially West Africa) |
| Skin | Severe pruritus, lichenified onchodermatitis, 'leopard skin' (depigmented), 'hanging groin' (lymphadenopathy) |
| Eye | Microfilariae in cornea/anterior chamber → keratitis, iridocyclitis, optic atrophy → blindness |
| Diagnosis | Skin snips (microfilariae); serology; PCR; slit lamp for eye |
| Treatment | Ivermectin 150µg/kg every 6-12 months (kills microfilariae); doxycycline 200mg OD 6w (targets Wolbachia) |
| Prevention | Insecticide, vector control, mass drug administration (ivermectin) |
| Mazzotti reaction | Severe reaction to ivermectin (fever, pruritus, rash, hypotension) in heavily infected |
| Diethylcarbamazine | Avoid (severe Mazzotti reaction); use ivermectin |
