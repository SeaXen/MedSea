# Cutaneous B-cell lymphoma

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Systemic Disease Manifestations

## Definition

Cutaneous B-cell lymphomas (CBCL) are a group of B-cell lymphomas primarily involving the skin, without systemic disease at diagnosis (primary CBCL). Three main types (WHO-EORTC): primary cutaneous follicle centre lymphoma (PCFCL, most common, indolent, head/back, excellent prognosis), primary cutaneous marginal zone B-cell lymphoma (PCMZL, indolent, trunk/arms), primary cutaneous diffuse large B-cell lymphoma, leg type (PCDLBCL-leg, aggressive, elderly, leg, worse prognosis). Diagnosis: skin biopsy with immunohistochemistry (CD20+ B-cell markers, BCL6, BCL2). Treatment: PCFCL/PCMZL: local excision, radiotherapy, rituximab (intralesional or systemic); PCDLBCL-leg: R-CHOP chemotherapy.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Cutaneous B-cell lymphoma presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29 (and standard dermatology curriculum). The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures, trigger avoidance, and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Types | PCFCL (most common, indolent), PCMZL (indolent), PCDLBCL-leg (aggressive) |
| PCFCL | Head, back, upper trunk; solitary or grouped; CD20+, BCL6+, BCL2−; 95% 5-yr survival |
| PCMZL | Trunk, arms; solitary or multiple; CD20+, BCL2+; 99% 5-yr survival |
| PCDLBCL-leg | Lower legs, elderly; rapidly growing; CD20+, BCL2+, MUM1+; 50% 5-yr survival |
| Vs systemic | Primary CBCL: no systemic disease at diagnosis; secondary: known nodal/extranodal disease |
| Differential | B-cell pseudolymphoma (drug-induced, vaccination site), cutaneous lymphoid hyperplasia, secondary cutaneous lymphoma |
| Treatment | PCFCL/PCMZL: excision, RT, intralesional rituximab, intralesional steroids; PCDLBCL-leg: R-CHOP ± RT |
| Rituximab | Anti-CD20 monoclonal antibody; effective in B-cell lymphomas |
