# Trichoscopy

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Hair and Nail Disorders

## Definition

Trichoscopy (dermoscopy of the scalp/hair) is a non-invasive technique for diagnosing hair and scalp disorders using a handheld dermatoscope. Key features: hair shaft thickness, hair density, follicular openings, peripilar signs, vessel patterns. Used for: AA (yellow dots, black dots, exclamation marks, tapered hairs), AGA (hair diameter diversity, peripilar signs, no yellow dots), LPP (perifollicular erythema, white dots), FFA (peripilar erythema at frontal hairline), TE (empty follicles, short regrowing hairs), trichotillomania (broken hairs, V-sign, trichoptilosis).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Trichoscopy presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Technique | Dermoscopy of scalp/hair (×10-70) |
| AA | Yellow dots, black dots, exclamation marks, tapered hairs, short vellus regrowth |
| AGA | Hair diameter diversity (>20%), peripilar signs (brown halos), anisotrichosis, no yellow dots |
| TE | Empty follicles, predominance of single-hair follicular units, short regrowing |
| LPP | Perifollicular erythema, perifollicular scale, white dots (fibrosis), loss of follicular openings |
| FFA | Peripilar erythema at frontal hairline, lonely hair sign |
| Trichotillomania | Broken hairs at different lengths, V-sign, trichoptilosis, no exclamation marks |
| Fibrosing | Loss of follicular openings = scarring (LPP, FFA, DLE, CCCA) |
| Diameter diversity | AGA hallmark (miniaturisation) |
| Yellow dot | Sebum/keratin in empty follicle (AA) |
