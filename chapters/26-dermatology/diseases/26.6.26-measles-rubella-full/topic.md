# Measles, rubella

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Infections

## Definition

Measles (rubeola) is a highly contagious paramyxovirus causing fever, cough, coryza, conjunctivitis (3 C's), Koplik spots (pathognomonic buccal), and maculopapular rash starting face (behind ears) → trunk → limbs. Rubella (German measles) is a togavirus causing mild illness with lymphadenopathy (posterior auricular, suboccipital) and rash (face → trunk). Both vaccine-preventable (MMR).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Measles, rubella presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Measles | Paramyxovirus; fever, 3 C's (cough, coryza, conjunctivitis), Koplik spots, rash |
| Koplik spots | Pathognomonic: white spots on buccal mucosa |
| Rash | Maculopapular; face (behind ears) → trunk → limbs; becomes confluent |
| Complications | Otitis media, pneumonia, encephalitis, SSPE (years later) |
| Vitamin A | WHO recommends vitamin A supplementation (reduces mortality) |
| Rubella | Togavirus; mild illness, posterior auricular/suboccipital lymphadenopathy, rash |
| Congenital rubella | Maternal infection in 1st trimester → cataracts, deafness, cardiac defects (PDA) |
| MMR vaccine | Live attenuated; 2 doses; 95% effective |
| Pregnancy | Avoid in pregnancy; check rubella IgG before pregnancy |
| Measles outbreak | Low vaccination rates; spread in unvaccinated populations |
