# Scalp psoriasis

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Scalp psoriasis affects 50-80% of psoriasis patients, often the first or only site. Red, scaly, well-demarcated plaques typically extending beyond the hairline onto forehead, neck, and ears. May cause significant psychological distress due to visible scaling ('dandruff').

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Well-demarcated erythematous plaques with thick, adherent silvery scale, often extending beyond the hairline (1-2 cm onto forehead, retroauricular, posterior neck) — this is a key distinguishing feature from seborrhoeic dermatitis. May be asymptomatic or pruritic. May cause temporary hair loss (telogen effluvium) but not scarring. Differential: seborrhoeic dermatitis (less well-defined, finer scale, often within hairline only), tinea capitis, contact dermatitis.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical. Skin biopsy if atypical. KOH if tinea suspected.

### Management

First-line: potent topical corticosteroids (betamethasone dipropionate, clobetasol) in lotion, foam, or solution; vitamin D analogues (calcipotriol); combination products. Tar-based shampoos (helpful adjunct). For thick scale: salicylic acid (5-10%) in oil/ointment, apply overnight, shampoo out. For severe/refractory: intralesional triamcinolone, systemic therapy.
