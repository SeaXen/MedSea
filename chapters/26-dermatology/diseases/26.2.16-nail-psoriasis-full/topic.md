# Nail psoriasis

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Nail psoriasis affects 40-50% of psoriasis patients overall (up to 80% in PsA) and can be the only manifestation in 1-5%. Nail matrix involvement: pitting, onychorrhexis, leukonychia. Nail bed involvement: onycholysis, oil drop sign, subungual hyperkeratosis. Severity: NAPSI (Nail Psoriasis Severity Index).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Matrix changes: pitting (irregular, deep, scattered), onychorrhexis, leukonychia, red spots in lunula, crumbling. Bed changes: onycholysis (separation of nail plate from bed, with erythematous border), oil drop sign (salmon patch), subungual hyperkeratosis (thickened debris under nail), splinter haemorrhages. Pustular psoriasis of nails: psoriatic onychopustulosis. Severity: NAPSI 0-100, 0=clear, >50 severe.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical. KOH and fungal culture to exclude onychomycosis (up to 20% co-infection). X-ray if psoriatic arthritis suspected.

### Management

Topical: potent steroids (clobetasol), vitamin D analogues (calcipotriol), tazarotene, intralesional triamcinolone (matrix lesions). Systemic for severe: biologics (most effective for nails), methotrexate, acitretin, ciclosporin. Avoid triggers. Treat concomitant onychomycosis.
