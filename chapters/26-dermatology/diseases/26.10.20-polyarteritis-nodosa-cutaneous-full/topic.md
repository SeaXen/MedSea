# Polyarteritis nodosa (cutaneous)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Systemic Disease Manifestations

## Definition

Polyarteritis nodosa (PAN) is a necrotising vasculitis of medium-sized arteries. Cutaneous PAN (cPAN) is skin-limited; systemic PAN (sPAN) affects kidney (no glomerulonephritis, microaneurysms on angiography), GI, nerves, heart. ANCA negative. Cutaneous: livedo reticularis, painful subcutaneous nodules, ulceration, digital gangrene. cPAN: post-strep (children), Hep B (rare). Treatment: NSAIDs, colchicine, dapsone; severe: steroids + cyclophosphamide.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Polyarteritis nodosa (cutaneous) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Type | Medium-vessel vasculitis (vs small AAV) |
| ANCA | Negative |
| Cutaneous | Livedo reticularis, tender subcutaneous nodules, ulceration, digital gangrene |
| Distribution | Lower legs, feet; livedo widespread |
| Systemic | Renal (HTN, microaneurysms, no GN), GI (abdominal pain, perforation, bleeding), nerve (mononeuritis multiplex), heart (MI, CHF) |
| Hep B | Associated (especially systemic) |
| Strep | Post-streptococcal (children, cutaneous) |
| Angiography | Microaneurysms in renal/mesenteric arteries (pathognomonic for sPAN) |
| Biopsy | Medium-vessel necrotising vasculitis (deep biopsy) |
| Differential | AAV (ANCA+), cholesterol emboli, calciphylaxis, livedoid vasculopathy |
| Treatment | cPAN: NSAIDs, colchicine, dapsone, low-dose steroids; sPAN: steroids + cyclophosphamide; Hep B PAN: antivirals |
