## Definition

Vitiligo is an acquired chronic autoimmune depigmentation disorder characterised by well-demarcated white macules and patches due to melanocyte loss. Affects 0.5-2% of population, all skin types, 50% onset before age 20. Pathogenesis: autoimmune destruction of epidermal melanocytes, with CD8+ T cells targeting melanocyte antigens; associated with other autoimmune diseases (thyroid disease most common, type 1 diabetes, pernicious anaemia, Addison's, alopecia areata).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Clinical: well-demarcated depigmented macules/patches, chalk-white, with sharp borders, often symmetrical. Wood's lamp examination: bright blue-white fluorescence enhances borders. Segmental vitiligo (unilateral, dermatomal, rapid onset then stable). Non-segmental (most common): acrofacial, vulgaris (scattered), universal (>80% BSA), mucosal. Sites: face (periorificial), hands, elbows, knees, genitals, areas of friction. Koebner phenomenon (new lesions at trauma sites). Associated features: leucotrichia (white hairs in patches — poor prognosis for repigmentation), halo naevi. Differential: piebaldism, tinea versicolor, pityriasis alba, post-inflammatory hypopigmentation, chemical leucoderma.

## 1. DEFINITION & EPIDEMIOLOGY

**Vitiligo** is an acquired, chronic, autoimmune depigmentation disorder characterized by **selective destruction of melanocytes** leading to well-defined **chalk-white macules/patches**.

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Prevalence** | 0.5-2% worldwide |
| **Age of onset** | **50% <20 yrs**; peak 10-30 yrs; can occur at any age |
| **Sex** | Equal |
| **Genetics** | **Polygenic**; 20-30% family history; **NLRP1, PTPN22, CTLA4, HLA-A*02, HLA-DRB1*04** |
| **Autoimmune associations** | **Thyroid (Hashimoto's, Graves') 15-25%**, Alopecia areata, Type 1 DM, Pernicious anaemia, Addison's, SLE |
| **Koebner phenomenon** | **Positive** (trauma-induced new lesions) — **true isomorphic response** |

## 2. CLASSIFICATION

| Classification | Subtypes | Features |
|----------------|----------|----------|
| **By Distribution** | **Segmental (SV)** | Unilateral, dermatomal/quasi-dermatomal; **early onset; rapid spread then stabilises**; no autoimmunity; **poor response to systemic therapy** |
|  | **Non-Segmental (NSV) = Generalised** | **Bilateral, symmetric**; progressive; autoimmune; includes: vitiligo vulgaris, acrofacial, mucosal, universal |
|  | **Mixed** | Segmental + non-segmental |
|  | **Focal** | Single/multiple macules in one area, not segmental |
| **By Extent** | **Vitiligo Vulgaris** | Generalised, scattered, symmetric |
|  | **Acrofacial** | Periorificial (eyes, mouth), distal extremities |
|  | **Mucosal** | Lips, genital mucosa |
|  | **Universal** | >80% BSA depigmented |

## 3. PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

| Mechanism | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Autoimmune (Main)** | **CD8+ cytotoxic T-cells** (IFN-γ, TNF-α) → melanocyte apoptosis; **Autoantibodies** against tyrosinase, TRP-1, TRP-2, SOX10 |
| **Oxidative Stress** | Impaired antioxidant defence (low catalase, SOD) → H₂O₂ accumulation → melanocyte damage |
| **Neural** | Neuropeptide (NPY, CGRP) dysregulation; segmental pattern suggests neural involvement |
| **Melanocyte Defect** | Intrinsic melanocyte fragility (ER stress, abnormal melanosomes) |
| **Genetic** | **NLRP1, PTPN22, CTLA4, TYR, MITF, HLA-A*02, HLA-DRB1*04** |

## 4. CLINICAL FEATURES

### 4.1 Lesion Morphology
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Colour** | **Chalk-white / milk-white** (complete depigmentation) |
| **Border** | **Sharply demarcated**, often hyperpigmented border (inflammatory) |
| **Shape** | Round, oval, geographic, confetti-like |
| **Distribution** | Symmetric (NSV), dermatomal (SV); **Koebner positive** |
| **Hair** | **Leukotrichia** (white hair in lesions) — poor prognostic sign |
| **Sensation** | Normal (vs leprosy — anaesthetic) |

### 4.2 Subtype-Specific Patterns
| Subtype | Distribution |
|---------|--------------|
| **Vitiligo Vulgaris** | Generalised, symmetric, trunk, limbs, face |
| **Acrofacial** | Periocular, perioral, lips, fingers, toes |
| **Lip-Tip** | Lips + distal fingers |
| **Segmental** | Unilateral, dermatomal/Blaschkoid; stops at midline |
| **Mucosal** | Lips, gingiva, genital mucosa |
| **Universal** | >80% BSA; rare |

### 4.3 Associated Signs
| Sign | Significance |
|------|--------------|
| **Koebner Phenomenon** | New lesions at trauma sites (scratches, burns, pressure) — **active disease** |
| **Leukotrichia** | White hair in lesions — **poor prognosis**, indicates follicular reservoir loss |
| **Halo Naevus** | Naevus with depigmented halo — may precede vitiligo |
| **Confetti-like** | Small 1-2 mm depigmented macules — active spread |

## 5. DIAGNOSTIC WORKUP

### 5.1 Clinical Diagnosis
- **Wood's Lamp (365 nm):** **Bright white fluorescence** (vs hypopigmentation — dull white)
- **Dermoscopy:** **Absent pigment network**; **perifollicular pigment retention** (early); **white structureless areas**; **residual perifollicular pigment** (repigmentation sign)

### 5.2 Laboratory Investigations
| Test | Purpose | Frequency |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **TSH, Free T4, Anti-TPO, Anti-Tg** | **Thyroid autoimmunity** (15-25%) | **Baseline + annual** |
| **FBC, HbA1c** | Type 1 DM, pernicious anaemia | Baseline |
| **ANA, ENA** | If SLE suspected | If clinical suspicion |
| **B12, Folate, Iron** | Pernicious anaemia, iron deficiency | If indicated |
| **Anti-melanocyte antibodies** | Research only | Not routine |

### 5.3 Skin Biopsy (Rarely Needed)
- **H&E:** Loss of melanocytes, **absence of melanin**; lymphocytic infiltrate at border (early)
- **Immunohistochemistry:** **Melan-A / HMB-45 / tyrosinase negative** in lesional skin
- **DIF:** Negative (vs lupus, lichen planus)

## 6. MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 General Measures
- **Strict Photoprotection:** **Broad-spectrum SPF 50+**; protective clothing
- **Camouflage:** Dermacolor, self-tanning lotions (dihydroxyacetone)
- **Psychological Support:** High impact on QoL; referral if needed
- **Koebner Avoidance:** Minimise trauma, friction, pressure

### 6.2 Topical Therapy (First-Line)

#### 6.2.1 Corticosteroids
| Potency | Site | Regimen |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Potent (Clobetasol 0.05%, Betamethasone dipropionate 0.05%)** | Body, scalp | Once daily × 2-3 months; **pulse** (2 weeks on/2 weeks off) |
| **Moderate (Mometasone, Fluticasone)** | Face, flexures, children | Once daily × 3-6 months |

> **Side effects:** Atrophy, striae, telangiectasia, perioral dermatitis — **avoid long-term potent on face/flexures**

#### 6.2.2 Calcineurin Inhibitors (TCI) — **Preferred for Face/Flexures**
| Agent | Strength | Regimen |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Tacrolimus 0.1%** (adults/children >15) | 0.1% ointment | BID × 6-12 months |
| **Tacrolimus 0.03%** (children 2-15) | 0.03% ointment | BID |
| **Pimecrolimus 1%** | 1% cream | BID |

> **Advantages:** No atrophy, safe long-term on face/flexures; **burning** initial; theoretical lymphoma risk (black box)

#### 6.2.3 Vitamin D Analogues
- **Calcipotriol / Tacalcitol** — adjunct to TCS/TCI; synergistic; no atrophy

#### 6.2.4 JAK Inhibitors (Topical) — **Breakthrough**
| Agent | Dose | Status |
|-------|------|--------|
| **Ruxolitinib 1.5% cream** | BID | **FDA approved 2022** (NSV ≥12 yrs); **>50% achieve F-VASI75 at 24 weeks** |

---

### 6.3 Phototherapy — **Gold Standard for Extensive NSV**

| Modality | Protocol | Indication | Response |
|----------|----------|------------|----------|
| **NB-UVB (311 nm)** | 2-3x/week; start 70-100 mJ/cm²; +10-20% per session | **Extensive NSV (>10% BSA)**; fail topicals | **Repigmentation 60-80%** at 6-12 months |
| **PUVA** | 2-3x/week; psoralen 0.6 mg/kg 2h prior | NB-UVB failure; darker skin | Similar to NB-UVB; but higher carcinogenic risk |
| **Excimer Laser (308 nm)** | 2x/week | Localised, resistant patches | Targeted; faster for small areas |

> **Repigmentation pattern:** **Perifollicular** (best), marginal, diffuse

### 6.4 Systemic Therapy (Rapidly Spreading / Extensive)

| Agent | Dose | Duration | Monitoring |
|-------|------|----------|------------|
| **Oral Mini-pulse Prednisolone** (OMP) | **Dexamethasone 2.5 mg / Betamethasone 5 mg** on 2 consecutive days/week | 3-6 months | Weight, BP, glucose, bone |
| **Oral Prednisolone** | 0.3-0.5 mg/kg/day (max 20-30 mg) | 3-6 months taper | Same |
| **Methotrexate** | 7.5-15 mg/week + Folate | 6-12 months | FBC, LFT q3m |
| **Cyclosporine** | 2.5-3 mg/kg/day | 6-12 months | Renal, BP, lipids |
| **Azathioprine** | 1-2 mg/kg/day | 12-18 months | TPMT, FBC, LFT |

> **OMP favoured** — fewer side effects than daily steroids; **stabilises active disease** within 1-3 months.

### 6.5 JAK Inhibitors (Oral) — **Emerging Systemic**
| Drug | Dose | Status |
|------|------|--------|
| **Baricitinib** | 4 mg OD (2 mg if renal/elderly) | Phase 3; rapid repigmentation |
| **Upadacitinib** | 15-30 mg OD | Phase 3 |
| **Ritlecitinib** | 30-50 mg OD | Phase 2/3 |

> **Monitoring:** CBC, LFT, lipids, CPK (upadacitinib), VZV IgG, thrombosis risk, pregnancy avoid

---

### 6.6 Surgical (Stable Vitiligo Only — No Koebner/Progression ≥1 Year)

| Technique | Indication | Success Rate |
|-----------|------------|--------------|
| **Autologous Non-Cultured Melanocyte Transfer** (NCMT) | Stable patches; large areas | 70-90% repigmentation |
| **Suction Blister Grafting** | Small areas, acral | 80-90% |
| **Punch Grafting** | Small patches | 70-80% |
| **Melanocyte Culture Transplant** | Large areas | 70-85% |

> **Prerequisites:** **Stable disease ≥1 year** (no new lesions, no Koebner); **negative Wood's lamp progression test**

## 7. SPECIAL SITES & SUBTYPES

### 7.1 Segmental Vitiligo (SV)
- **Unilateral, dermatomal/Blaschkoid**
- **Rapid spread → stabilises** (6-12 months)
- **No autoimmunity**, thyroid antibodies usually negative
- **Poor response to systemic therapy / phototherapy**
- **Treatment:** Topical TCS/TCI; **surgical** once stable; JAK inhibitors emerging

### 7.2 Acrofacial / Lip-Tip
- **Poor response** to topicals/phototherapy
- **Early JAK inhibitor / surgical** consideration

### 7.3 Mucosal Vitiligo
- **Lips, genital** — topical TCI (tacrolimus/pimecrolimus); excimer laser

### 7.4 Paediatric Vitiligo
- **NB-UVB first-line** for extensive; topical TCS/TCI for limited
- **Growth monitoring** if systemic steroids

### 7.5 Vitiligo in Pregnancy
- **Topical TCS/TCI safe**; **NB-UVB safe**
- **Avoid:** Systemic steroids, JAKi, MTX, cyclosporine, PUVA
