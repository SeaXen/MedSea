# Pityriasis rosea

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Pityriasis rosea (PR) is an acute, self-limiting papulosquamous eruption, possibly triggered by HHV-6/7 reactivation. Affects 0.5-2% of population, peak 15-30 years, slight female predominance, more common in winter/spring. Herald patch followed by secondary eruption. Self-limiting in 6-12 weeks.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Herald patch: solitary, 2-5cm, oval, salmon-pink, with collarette scale, appearing 1-14 days before secondary eruption (usually on trunk). Secondary eruption: multiple smaller (1-2cm), oval, salmon-pink papules with collarette scale, in 'Christmas tree' pattern on back (long axis of lesions follows cleavage lines). Typically spares face, palms, soles. Mild pruritus in 50%. May have prodrome (fever, malaise, lymphadenopathy). Variants: inverse (axillae, groin), papular, vesicular, purpuric.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical. Serology (RPR/VDRL) to exclude secondary syphilis. KOH if tinea suspected. Skin biopsy rarely needed.

### Management

Reassurance (self-limiting). Topical: emollients, mild-moderate steroids, calamine lotion for pruritus. Antihistamines. UVB phototherapy may hasten resolution. Aciclovir (if severe, especially with HHV-6/7 implication). No exclusion from school/work (not contagious).
