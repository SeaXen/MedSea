# Molluscum contagiosum

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Infections

## Definition

Molluscum contagiosum is a common, self-limiting viral skin infection caused by a poxvirus (MCV). Presents as pearly, dome-shaped, umbilicated papules 2-5mm, often with central caseous material. Common in children (face, trunk, axillae), sexually active adults (genital), and immunocompromised (extensive, larger, facial — consider HIV). Self-limiting in 6-12 months in immunocompetent. Treatment: cryotherapy, curettage, cantharidin, or observation.

Molluscum contagiosum presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Cause | Poxvirus (MCV) |
| Lesion | Pearly dome-shaped umbilicated papule, 2-5mm, central caseous core |
| Distribution | Children: face, trunk, axillae, extremities; adults: genital (STI) |
| Number | Few to hundreds (immunocompromised) |
| Transmission | Skin-to-skin, fomites (towels, toys), autoinoculation |
| Immunocompromised | Extensive, larger, facial, recalcitrant → consider HIV |
| Spontaneous resolution | 6-12 months in immunocompetent (may leave pitted scars) |
| Treatment | Observation, cryotherapy, curettage, cantharidin, topical agents (imiquimod, salicylic acid, KOH) |
| Differential | Wart, milium, acne (closed comedone), cryptococcus (HIV) |
| Complications | Secondary bacterial infection, eczema (molluscum dermatitis), scarring |
