# Lichen planus (cutaneous, mucosal, nail, scalp)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Lichen planus (LP) is a chronic inflammatory papulosquamous disease affecting skin, mucous membranes, nails, and scalp. Affects 1% of adults, peak 30-60, female predominance. Pathogenesis: T-cell mediated autoimmune attack on basal keratinocytes (CD8+ T cell cytotoxicity). Triggers: drugs (thiazides, gold, antimalarials, NSAIDs, ACEi, penicillamine), hepatitis C (especially oral LP), contact allergens.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

6 P's of cutaneous LP: Pruritic, Purple, Polygonal, Planar, Papules, Plaques. Distribution: flexor surfaces (wrists, ankles), lumbar, pretibial, trunk. Wickham striae: fine white lacy network on surface (pathognomonic). Variants: hypertrophic (lichen planus verrucosus, shins, very pruritic, scarring), annular, linear (Blaschkoid), actinic, atrophic, bullous, pigmented. Mucosa: oral LP (Wickham striae on buccal mucosa, erosive, painful; 10x SCC risk), genital, oesophageal. Nail: pterygium (scarring, nail dystrophy, ridging), trachyonychia. Scalp: lichen planopilaris (scarring alopecia, perifollicular erythema, follicular hyperkeratosis).

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical. Skin biopsy (H&E): saw-tooth rete ridges, hypergranulosis, band-like lymphocytic infiltrate, civatte bodies. DIF to exclude lupus. Hep C serology. Drug history.

### Management

First-line: potent topical steroids (clobetasol) ± topical calcineurin inhibitors (pimecrolimus, tacrolimus) for face/flexures. Intralesional triamcinolone for thick/hypertrophic. Extensive: phototherapy (NB-UVB), systemic (acitretin, methotrexate, hydroxychloroquine, mycophenolate). Biologics: IL-12/23 (ustekinumab), IL-23 (risankizumab), JAK inhibitors. Stop trigger drugs. Erosive oral LP: superpotent topical steroid in orabase, topical calcineurin inhibitor, hydroxychloroquine. Biopsy any persistent ulcer to exclude SCC.
