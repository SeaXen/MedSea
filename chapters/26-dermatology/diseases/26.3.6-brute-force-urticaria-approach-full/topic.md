# Brute force urticaria approach

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Urticaria Erythema and Purpura

## Definition

Brute force urticaria approach is a condition within the urticaria, erythema, or purpura spectrum discussed in Davidson 24e Ch 29. These conditions include wheals/flares (urticaria), figurate or reactive erythemas, and palpable purpura from vasculitis or coagulopathy.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Brute force urticaria approach is a clinical entity within the Urticaria Erythema and Purpura group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Definition | Urticaria = wheals; erythema = reactive red rashes; purpura = bleeding into skin |
| Epidemiology | Urticaria 20% lifetime; vasculitis rare |
| Aetiology | Mast cell degranulation (urticaria), immune complex (vasculitis), drugs, infection |
| Pathophysiology | Histamine, bradykinin (HAE), immune complex deposition (leukocytoclastic vasculitis) |
| Clinical features | Transient wheals (urticaria), palpable purpura (vasculitis), figurate erythema |
| Diagnosis | Clinical; FBC, ANA, ANCA, complement, skin biopsy for vasculitis |
| Management (1st) | Avoid triggers; H1 antihistamines (up to 4× dose) |
| Management (2nd) | Add H2 antagonist, LTRA; for vasculitis — treat underlying cause |
| Management (3rd) | Omalizumab, ciclosporin (CSU); for SCARs — supportive + IVIG/ciclosporin |
| Complications | Anaphylaxis (urticaria), renal involvement (IgA vasculitis), visceral KS (AIDS) |
