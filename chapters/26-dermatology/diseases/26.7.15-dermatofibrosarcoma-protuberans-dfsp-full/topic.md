# Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans (DFSP)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Tumours

## Definition

Dermatofibrosarcoma protuberans (DFSP) is a low-grade, locally aggressive cutaneous sarcoma of fibroblastic origin with characteristic storiform spindle cell pattern and CD34 positivity. Often presents as slowly growing, indurated plaque or nodule (skin-coloured to red-brown), most commonly on trunk. High local recurrence if incompletely excised (perineural/perivascular infiltration extends beyond clinical margins). Rarely metastasises. Treatment: Mohs micrographic surgery (MMS) is preferred for tissue-sparing complete removal.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Cause | t(17;22) translocation; PDGFB-COL1A1 fusion |
| Stain | CD34 positive; Factor XIIIa negative (vs dermatofibroma) |
| Distribution | Trunk (most common), proximal limbs |
| Appearance | Slowly growing, indurated plaque/nodule, skin-coloured to red-brown |
| Dimple sign | Negative (vs dermatofibroma positive) |
| Recurrence | High if incomplete excision (perineural/perivascular spread) |
| Metastasis | Rare (less than 1%) |
| Treatment | Mohs (preferred for tissue-sparing); WLE with 2-3cm margins; imatinib (PDGFR inhibitor) for advanced |
| Histology | Storiform spindle cell pattern, infiltrative margins, CD34+ |
| Biopsy | Punch or incisional; full depth |
