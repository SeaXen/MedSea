# Warts (verrucae - common, plantar, plane, genital)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Infections

## Definition

Warts (verrucae) are common benign skin tumours caused by human papillomavirus (HPV). Multiple types: common warts (verruca vulgaris, HPV 2, 4, 27, 29), plantar warts (myrmecia, HPV 1, 2, 4), plane warts (verruca plana, HPV 3, 10), genital warts (condyloma acuminata, HPV 6, 11), and others. Transmission: skin-to-skin, autoinoculation, fomites. Most spontaneously resolve in months to years. Treatment: cryotherapy, salicylic acid, immunotherapy, imiquimod (genital).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Warts (verrucae - common, plantar, plane, genital) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Cause | HPV (papillomavirus) |
| Common | Verruca vulgaris, HPV 2, 4, 27, 29; hands, fingers |
| Plantar | Myrmecia, HPV 1, 2, 4; soles; mosaic pattern |
| Plane | Verruca plana, HPV 3, 10; face, dorsum hands; flat, skin-coloured |
| Genital | Condyloma acuminata, HPV 6, 11; anogenital; low-risk |
| High-risk HPV | 16, 18 → cervical, anal, oropharyngeal SCC |
| Spontaneous resolution | 50% in 1 year, 70% in 2 years, 90% in 5 years (immunocompetent) |
| Treatment | Salicylic acid, cryotherapy, curettage, imiquimod, 5-FU, immunotherapy (squaric acid, DPCP), pulsed dye laser |
| Genital warts | Imiquimod, podophyllotoxin, trichloroacetic acid; cryotherapy; surgery for large |
| Recurrence | Common; multiple treatments often needed |
| Vaccine | HPV vaccine (Gardasil, Cervarix) for high-risk types |
