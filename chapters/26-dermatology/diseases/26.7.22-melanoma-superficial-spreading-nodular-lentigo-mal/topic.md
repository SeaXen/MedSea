# Melanoma (superficial spreading, nodular, lentigo maligna, acral lentiginous, amelanotic)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Tumours

## Definition

Cutaneous melanoma is a malignant tumour of melanocytes, accounting for 90% of skin cancer deaths. Four main subtypes: superficial spreading (most common), nodular, lentigo maligna, acral lentiginous. Risk factors: fair skin, intense sun exposure, large number of naevi, atypical naevi, family history. ABCDE for clinical detection. Treatment: wide local excision, SLNB for T1b+, adjuvant therapy (immunotherapy, BRAF/MEK inhibitors).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Melanoma (superficial spreading, nodular, lentigo maligna, acral lentiginous, amelanotic) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Subtypes | Superficial spreading (70%), nodular (15-20%), lentigo maligna (5-10%), acral lentiginous (5%) |
| ABCDE | Asymmetry, Border, Colour, Diameter >6mm, Evolution |
| Dermoscopy | Atypical network, blue-white veil, irregular streaks/dots, regression, atypical vessels |
| Breslow | Tumour thickness (mm) — most important prognostic factor |
| Ulceration | Worse prognosis (upstages) |
| SLNB | Sentinel lymph node biopsy for T1b or higher (≥0.8mm or ulcerated) |
| Mutations | BRAF V600E (60% cutaneous), NRAS (20%), KIT (acral, mucosal, LM) |
| Treatment | WLE (0.5-2cm margin); SLNB; adjuvant (nivolumab/pembrolizumab); targeted (BRAF/MEK) |
| Follow-up | Regular skin exams, lymph node, imaging as indicated |
| 5-yr survival | Stage I more than 90%; Stage IV ~25% |
| Immunotherapy | Anti-PD1 (nivolumab, pembrolizumab), anti-CTLA4 (ipilimumab); checkpoint inhibitors |
