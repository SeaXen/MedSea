# Deep fungal infections (sporotrichosis, chromoblastomycosis)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Infections

## Definition

Deep (subcutaneous) mycoses include sporotrichosis (Sporothrix schenckii, 'rose gardener's disease', nodular lymphangitis), chromoblastomycosis (dematiaceous fungi, verrucous plaques with muriform bodies), mycetoma (actinomycetoma bacterial, eumycetoma fungal, grains), lobomycosis, rhinosporidiosis. Often follow trauma (thorn, splinter). Diagnosis: culture, biopsy, histopathology. Treatment: prolonged antifungals (itraconazole, terbinafine, amphotericin B); surgery for limited disease.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Deep fungal infections (sporotrichosis, chromoblastomycosis) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Sporotrichosis | Sporothrix schenckii; rose gardener disease; nodular lymphangitis (ascending) |
| Chromoblastomycosis | Dematiaceous fungi; verrucous plaques; muriform bodies (sclerotic bodies) on histology |
| Mycetoma | Actinomycetoma (bacterial, Nocardia, Actinomadura) or eumycetoma (Madurella, etc.); triad of swelling, sinus tracts, grains |
| Transmission | Trauma (thorn, splinter, soil) |
| Sporotrichosis | Saturated solution of potassium iodide (SSKI) classic; itraconazole first-line now |
| Chromoblastomycosis | Itraconazole + cryotherapy; prolonged therapy |
| Mycetoma | Eumycetoma: itraconazole/terbinafine; actinomycetoma: cotrimoxazole, amikacin |
| Histology | Granulomatous inflammation, fungal elements (PAS, GMS stains) |
| Differential | Sporotrichoid spread: Nocardia, Mycobacterium marinum, Leishmania, Francisella |
| Occupation | Gardeners, farmers, outdoor workers |
