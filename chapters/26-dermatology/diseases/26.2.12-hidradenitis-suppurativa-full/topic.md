# Hidradenitis suppurativa

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Hidradenitis suppurativa (HS, acne inversa) is a chronic inflammatory skin disease of the apocrine gland-bearing areas (axillae, groin, perianal, inframammary) characterised by recurrent painful nodules, abscesses, sinus tracts, and scarring. Pathogenesis: follicular occlusion (NOT primary apocrine disease), keratin plugging of the hair follicle, follicular rupture with inflammation, secondary bacterial infection. Risk factors: obesity, smoking (strongest), female sex (3:1), genetic (PSEN1, NCSTN mutations — familial HS), friction, hormones. Diagnostic criteria: recurrent nodules/tender boils in typical sites (axillae, groin, perianal, inframammary). Hurley staging. Treatment: lifestyle (weight, smoking cessation); medical (topical clindamycin, oral tetracyclines, rifampicin + clindamycin, dapsone, hormonal — anti-androgen OCP, finasteride, spironolactone; biologics — adalimumab (anti-TNF, FDA/EMA approved), secukinumab, bimekizumab (IL-17); surgical (incision and drainage, deroofing, wide excision for severe).

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures, trigger avoidance, and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment
