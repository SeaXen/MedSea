# Skin examination technique

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

Complete skin examination requires adequate lighting (natural/daylight), full exposure, systematic inspection from head to toe including scalp, nails, palms, soles, and mucous membranes, palpation of lesions, and assessment of regional lymph nodes.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Skin examination technique is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Lighting | Natural daylight preferred; pen torch for mucosal/nail folds |
| Order | Head-to-toe systematic: scalp → face → neck → trunk → limbs → hands → nails → feet → genitalia; check mucous membranes |
| Palpation | Texture, depth, mobility, tenderness, temperature |
| Dermoscopy | ×10 handheld; oil immersion or polarised; assess pigment and vascular patterns |
| Wood's lamp | UVA 365nm; pigment (vitiligo bright), porphyria, tinea (some), erythrasma (coral red) |
| Lymph nodes | Cervical, axillary, epitrochlear, inguinal — relevant in melanoma, CTCL, infections |
| Nikolsky sign | Lateral pressure → epidermal detachment → pemphigus, SJS, SSSS |
| Darier sign | Rubbing → urtication → mastocytosis |
| Asboe-Hansen | Pressure on bulla extends it peripherally → pemphigus |
| Documentation | Body map; photographs with consent; measurements in mm |
