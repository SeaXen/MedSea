# Melanoma-associated hypopigmentation (vitiligo-like)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Disorders of Pigmentation

## Definition

Melanoma-associated hypopigmentation (vitiligo-like leukoderma) is an immune-mediated depigmentation associated with melanoma, often appearing as a paraneoplastic phenomenon or as a side effect of immunotherapy. Anti-melanoma immune response cross-reacts with normal melanocytes, causing depigmentation. Particularly common with anti-PD-1/PD-L1 immunotherapy. Indicates favourable response to therapy.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Melanoma-associated hypopigmentation (vitiligo-like) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Mechanism | Autoimmune cross-reactivity (anti-melanoma → anti-melanocyte) |
| Association | Melanoma, especially with anti-PD-1/PD-L1 therapy |
| Anti-PD-1 | Nivolumab, pembrolizumab: vitiligo-like in 10% |
| Anti-CTLA4 | Ipilimumab: less common vitiligo |
| Prognosis | FAVOURABLE (better response to immunotherapy) |
| Differential | Vitiligo, post-inflammatory hypopigmentation, halo naevus |
| Appearance | Mimics vitiligo; patches, often widespread |
| Treatment | Topical steroids, calcineurin inhibitors; usually continue immunotherapy |
| Other cancers | Reported with renal, ovarian, lung cancers |
