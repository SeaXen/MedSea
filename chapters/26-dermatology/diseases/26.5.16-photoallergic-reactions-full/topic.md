# Photoallergic reactions

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Drug Eruptions

## Definition

Photoallergic drug reactions are Type IV hypersensitivity reactions to drug photoproducts (drug + UV light). Common drugs: topical sunscreens (benzophenones, PABA, cinnamates), NSAIDs (ketoprofen), fragrances (musk ambrette), thiazide diuretics, sulfonamides, quinidine. Eczematous, pruritic, often spreads beyond sun-exposed areas. Resolves with drug withdrawal.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Photoallergic reactions presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

| Common drugs | Sunscreens (benzophenones, PABA, cinnamates), NSAIDs (ketoprofen), fragrances, thiazides, sulfonamides |
| Onset | Delayed (often days to weeks of use + sun) |
| Distribution | Sun-exposed, often spreads to covered areas |
| Clinical | Eczematous, pruritic, vesicular |
| Differential | Phototoxic (exaggerated sunburn, no spread), contact dermatitis |
| Diagnosis | Photopatch testing (duplicate series, one set exposed to UVA) |
| Treatment | Stop drug; sun avoidance; topical/superpotent steroids |
| Resolution | Weeks after drug withdrawal |
| Persistent light reaction | Photosensitivity persists after drug withdrawn |
