# Pityriasis rubra pilaris

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Pityriasis rubra pilaris (PRP) is a rare chronic papulosquamous disorder characterised by follicular hyperkeratosis, salmon-coloured plaques, and palmoplantar keratoderma. Six types (Griffiths): Type I (classic adult, 50% — sudden onset, clears 1-3 years), II (atypical adult), III (classic juvenile), IV (circumscribed juvenile), V (atypical juvenile), VI (HIV-associated).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Follicular hyperkeratosis (palpably rough 'nutmeg-grater' papules, especially dorsal fingers, elbows, knees), well-demarcated salmon-orange plaques with characteristic 'islands of sparing' (normal skin within plaques), palmoplantar keratoderma (yellowish, thick), fine diffuse scaling, may progress to erythroderma. Distribution: starts on face/scalp, spreads caudally. Type I (classic adult) clears in 1-3 years. Type VI (HIV): severe, with acneiform nodules, lichen spinulosus.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical. Skin biopsy ('checkerboard' parakeratosis, follicular plugging, acanthosis). HIV test for Type VI suspicion.

### Management

First-line: topical steroids, keratolytics (urea, salicylic acid), emollients, vitamin D analogues. Systemic: acitretin (first-line systemic), methotrexate, ciclosporin. Phototherapy: NB-UVB, PUVA. Biologics: TNF inhibitors (adalimumab, infliximab — particularly effective Type I), IL-12/23 (ustekinumab), IL-23 (guselkumab), IL-17 (secukinumab). For HIV-associated: ART plus systemic therapy.
