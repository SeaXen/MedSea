# Erythrodermic psoriasis

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Erythrodermic psoriasis is a severe, life-threatening variant with generalised erythema and scaling affecting >90% BSA, with systemic complications. Most common cause of psoriatic erythroderma. Mortality 10-20% if untreated (fluid/electrolyte loss, sepsis, high-output cardiac failure, hypothermia). Often triggered by withdrawal of systemic steroids, infection, drugs.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Generalised erythema and scaling affecting >90% BSA, often with sparing of the face, with oedema (especially lower legs), exfoliation, fissuring, and pre-existing psoriatic plaques that have expanded and coalesced. Systemic: fever, chills, rigors, dehydration, lymphadenopathy, high-output cardiac failure (especially elderly), hypothermia, sepsis. May be precipitated by withdrawal of systemic steroids, infection, drugs, sunlight, or progressive plaque disease.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical. FBC, U&E, LFT, albumin, calcium, blood culture, skin biopsy (to exclude other causes of erythroderma).

### Management

Hospital admission. Supportive: IV fluids, electrolytes, blankets (temperature regulation), topical steroids and emollients, treat infection. Systemic: acitretin, methotrexate, or ciclosporin. Infliximab/etanercept rapid onset. Avoid steroid withdrawal abruptly; taper slowly. Antihistamines for pruritus.
