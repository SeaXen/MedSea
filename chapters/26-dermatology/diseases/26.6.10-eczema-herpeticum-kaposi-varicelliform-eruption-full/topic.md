# Eczema herpeticum (Kaposi varicelliform eruption)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Infections

## Definition

Eczema herpeticum (Kaposi varicelliform eruption) is a disseminated HSV infection (usually HSV-1) complicating atopic dermatitis or other skin barrier disorders. Widespread punched-out vesicles/erosions, often fever, lymphadenopathy, and systemic illness. Can be life-threatening in infants/immunocompromised. Treatment: IV aciclovir.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Eczema herpeticum (Kaposi varicelliform eruption) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Cause | HSV-1 (usually) or HSV-2, VZV, coxsackie |
| Predisposing | Atopic dermatitis (most common), Darier, Hailey-Hailey, pemphigus, burns, immunosuppression |
| Lesions | Widespread monomorphic punched-out vesicles/erosions |
| Distribution | Face, neck, trunk, eczema-affected areas |
| Systemic | Fever, lymphadenopathy, malaise |
| Complications | Secondary bacterial infection, keratitis, encephalitis, viraemia, multi-organ failure |
| Diagnosis | PCR (gold standard), viral culture, Tzanck smear, DFA |
| Treatment | IV aciclovir 5-10mg/kg TDS 7-14 days; oral for milder cases |
| Mortality | Untreated severe cases can be 10-50% |
| Prevention | Avoid contact with active HSV; consider prophylaxis with valaciclovir in severe recurrent cases |
