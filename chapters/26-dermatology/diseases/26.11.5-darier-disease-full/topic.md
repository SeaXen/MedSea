# Darier disease

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Genodermatoses

## Definition

Darier disease (keratosis follicularis) is an autosomal dominant disorder due to ATP2A2 mutation (SERCA2, calcium pump). Onset in adolescence. Greasy, hyperkeratotic, skin-coloured/brown papules in seborrhoeic areas (chest, back, face, scalp), with characteristic 'dirty' appearance, coalescing into plaques, malodorous. Other: palmoplantar pits, nail changes (V-shaped nick at free edge, longitudinal red/white lines, subungual hyperkeratosis), oral papules, acrokeratosis verruciformis-like lesions on dorsa of hands/feet. Histology: acantholysis with corps ronds (large dyskeratotic cells in upper epidermis) and grains (smaller dyskeratotic cells). Treatment: topical retinoids, keratolytics, oral retinoids (acitretin), antibiotics for secondary infection.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Darier disease presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment
