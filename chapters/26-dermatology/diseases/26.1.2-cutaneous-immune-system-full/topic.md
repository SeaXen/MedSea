# Cutaneous immune system

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

The skin is a peripheral immune organ containing resident immune cells (Langerhans cells, dermal dendritic cells, T cells, mast cells) and circulating cells recruited during inflammation. It mounts innate (antimicrobial peptides, complement) and adaptive (T cell, B cell) responses.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Cutaneous immune system is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. ## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods: FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB (IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** What is the Th17/IL-23 axis and why is it important in dermatology?
   **A:** IL-23 from dendritic cells activates Th17 cells to produce IL-17/IL-22, driving keratinocyte hyperproliferation in psoriasis; targeted by ustekinumab, secukinumab, ixekizumab, guselkumab.
2. **Q:** Differentiate Th1, Th2, and Th17 immune responses in skin disease.
   **A:** Th1: granulomatous, lichen planus, CTCL. Th2: atopic dermatitis, urticaria. Th17: psoriasis, hidradenitis suppurativa.
3. **Q:** What is the role of Langerhans cells?
   **A:** Antigen-presenting cells in epidermis that capture antigens and migrate to lymph nodes to prime T cell responses.
