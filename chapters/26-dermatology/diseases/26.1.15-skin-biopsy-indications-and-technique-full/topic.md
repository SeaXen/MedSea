# Skin biopsy indications and technique

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

Skin biopsy is indicated for diagnostic uncertainty, atypical lesions, suspected malignancy, autoimmune bullous disease (DIF), vasculitis, and chronic inflammatory dermatoses unresponsive to treatment. Techniques include punch (most common), shave, excisional, and incisional.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Punch biopsy | Cylindrical 2-6mm; inflammatory and small lesions; suture for >3mm |
| Shave biopsy | Scalpel/razor; superficial lesions (SK, AK, superficial BCC) |
| Excisional biopsy | Whole lesion with 2-5mm margin; suspected melanoma |
| Incisional biopsy | Part of lesion (largest/deepest area); for large lesions |
| Site selection | Earliest, freshest, untreated lesion; avoid ulcerated/crusted areas |
| DIF site | Perilesional (1-2cm from blister edge), not the blister itself |
| Local anaesthetic | 1% lidocaine ± adrenaline (avoid end-arteries — fingers, toes, nose, ears, penis) |
| Handling | Formalin for H&E; Michel's medium/IFS for DIF; sterile saline for culture |
| Complications | Bleeding, infection, scarring, keloid, nerve damage |
| Documentation | Site, type, clinical differential, special handling required |

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** When would you choose a punch vs shave biopsy?
   **A:** Punch: inflammatory dermatoses, small lesions, DIF (perilesional), full-thickness histology. Shave: superficial lesions where depth not critical (SK, AK, superficial BCC).
2. **Q:** Where should you biopsy for DIF in bullous pemphigoid?
   **A:** Perilesional skin 1-2cm from a fresh blister (lesional skin has lost antigen); NOT the blister itself (false negative).

## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **Biopsy site** = Earliest untreated for routine; perilesional 1-2cm for DIF
