# Atopic dermatitis (adult, paediatric)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Atopic dermatitis (eczema) is a chronic, relapsing, intensely pruritic inflammatory skin disease, often beginning in infancy (60% in first year, 90% by age 5) and persisting into adulthood in 10-30%. Affects 15-20% of children, 1-3% of adults. Part of the 'atopic triad' (eczema, asthma, allergic rhinitis). Strong genetic component: filaggrin (FLG) loss-of-function mutations in 30% of moderate-severe (impaired epidermal barrier); Th2-skewed immune dysregulation (IL-4, IL-13, IL-31, TSLP). UK criteria: itchy skin + ≥3 of: history of flexural involvement, history of dry skin, onset before age 2, personal/family atopy, visible flexural dermatitis.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Acute eczema: erythema, vesicles, weeping, oedema, intense pruritus. Chronic eczema: lichenification, excoriations, dryness, scaling, pigmentary changes. Distribution: infants — face (cheeks), scalp, extensor surfaces; children — flexures (antecubital, popliteal), neck, wrists, ankles; adults — flexures, hands, head/neck. Associated features: xerosis, ichthyosis vulgaris (10%), keratosis pilaris, Dennie-Morgan folds, pityriasis alba, white dermographism, facial pallor, infraorbital darkening. Triggers: soaps, detergents, wool, heat, sweat, stress, infection, allergens.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical (UK criteria). EASI, SCORAD, POEM for severity. Skin biopsy if atypical. Patch testing if ACD suspected. IgE, RAST if allergy suspected. Swab if infection.

### Management

Stepped (E-T-P-S-C-D): Emollients (≥500g/week, mainstay), Topical corticosteroids (mild face/flexures, potent body), Topical calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus 0.03% 2-15yr, 0.1% >16yr; pimecrolimus), Phototherapy (NB-UVB), Systemic immunosuppressants (prednisolone for flares, ciclosporin, methotrexate, azathioprine, mycophenolate), Biologics (dupilumab anti-IL4/13 first-line; tralokinumab anti-IL13; lebrikizumab anti-IL13), JAK inhibitors (topical ruxolitinib 1.5%; oral abrocitinib, upadacitinib, baricitinib). Treat infection (bleach baths 2x/week).
