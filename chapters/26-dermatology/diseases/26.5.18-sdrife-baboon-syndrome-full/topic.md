# SDRIFE (baboon syndrome)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Drug Eruptions

## Definition

Symmetrical drug-related intertriginous and flexural exanthema (SDRIFE, formerly baboon syndrome) is a specific drug reaction pattern with symmetrical, well-demarcated erythema in the buttocks, inguinal, and flexural areas. Common drugs: aminopenicillins, other beta-lactams. Latency hours to days. No systemic involvement. Resolves with drug withdrawal.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

SDRIFE (baboon syndrome) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Distribution | Symmetric erythema in buttocks, inguinal, flexural, perianal |
| Common drugs | Aminopenicillins (ampicillin most common), beta-lactams, NSAIDs, terbinafine |
| Latency | Hours to days (often 24-72h) |
| Systemic | No systemic involvement (no fever, no organ involvement) |
| Mucosa | Spared |
| Differential | AGEP (pustular, systemic), DRESS (organ involvement, latency 2-6w) |
| Biopsy | Perivascular neutrophils, eosinophils |
| Treatment | STOP drug; topical steroids; antihistamines |
| Prognosis | Resolves in 1-2 weeks; recurrence on re-exposure |
| Baboon | Named for baboon-like red buttocks appearance |

|--------------|------------------------|
