# Distribution patterns

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

Skin disease distribution patterns (symmetric vs asymmetric, photosensitive, flexural, extensor, acral, dermatomal, Blaschkoid) provide critical diagnostic clues for narrowing the differential.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Distribution patterns is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Symmetric distribution | Endogenous disease (eczema, psoriasis, drug eruption) |
| Asymmetric | Exogenous (contact dermatitis, infection) |
| Photosensitive | Face, V of chest, dorsum hands; SLE, dermatomyositis, porphyria |
| Flexural | Atopic dermatitis (infants), inverse psoriasis, candida intertrigo |
| Extensor | Psoriasis, lichen planus |
| Acral | Palmoplantar: pompholyx, psoriasis, tinea; periungual: paronychia |
| Dermatomal | Herpes zoster, zosteriform metastasis |
| Blaschkoid | Linear along embryonic migration: lichen striatus, incontinentia pigmenti |
| Seborrheic | Scalp, eyebrows, nasolabial fold, presternal: seborrheic dermatitis |
| Livedo reticularis | Reticular vascular pattern: vasculitis, antiphospholipid, cholesterol emboli |

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** How does photosensitivity help narrow the differential?
   **A:** Sparing of submental area, behind ears, under nose — indicates true photosensitivity (SLE, dermatomyositis, porphyria, drug photosensitivity).
2. **Q:** What is a Blaschkoid distribution?
   **A:** Linear pattern following embryonic cell migration lines; seen in incontinentia pigmenti, lichen striatus, epidermal naevi.

## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **Asymmetric vs Symmetric** = Asymmetric = exogenous (contact, infection); Symmetric = endogenous (eczema, psoriasis, drug)
