## Definition

Drug reaction with eosinophilia and systemic symptoms (DRESS), also called drug-induced hypersensitivity syndrome (DIHS), is a severe T-cell mediated drug reaction with multisystem involvement. Latency 2-6 weeks (longer than most drug reactions). Common drugs: allopurinol, anticonvulsants (carbamazepine, phenytoin, lamotrigine), sulfonamides, minocycline, vancomycin, anti-TB drugs. Mortality 5-10% (organ failure).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Morbilliform rash (often >50% BSA) with facial oedema (characteristic), fever, lymphadenopathy, eosinophilia (≥700/μL or ≥10%), atypical lymphocytes, and internal organ involvement (liver most common — hepatitis 50-80%, kidney — AKI 10-30%, lung — pneumonitis, heart — myocarditis, thyroid — late autoimmune thyroiditis). HHV-6 reactivation common. RegiSCAR score: <2 no, 2-3 possible, 4-5 probable, ≥6 definite. Management: STOP drug; systemic steroids 0.5-1mg/kg/day; slow taper over 6-8 weeks; avoid rapid taper (relapse common); monitor LFTs, renal, thyroid for months.




# DRESS Syndrome (Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms)

**Tags:** `dermatology` `drug-reaction` `SCAR` `DRESS` `DIHS` `full-fcps-mrcp-note`

---

## 1. DEFINITION & CLASSIFICATION

**DRESS** = **Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms**  
**DIHS** = **Drug-Induced Hypersensitivity Syndrome** (Japanese term, broader)

A severe, potentially fatal **Type IVb/IVd delayed hypersensitivity reaction** characterized by:
- Fever (>38°C)
- Maculopapular rash → erythroderma
- **Eosinophilia** (≥1.5×10⁹/L or >20%)
- **Systemic organ involvement** (liver > kidney > lung > heart)
- **Prolonged latency** (2-8 weeks after drug start)
- **Reactivation of herpesviruses** (HHV-6, HHV-7, EBV, CMV)

**DRESS vs DIHS:** DIHS requires HHV-6 reactivation; DRESS is broader. Often used interchangeably.

## 5. CLINICAL FEATURES

### Typical Timeline
```
Drug start
    │
    ▼ Latency: 2-8 weeks (Allopurinol up to 12 weeks)
    │
    ▼ Prodrome: Fever, malaise, facial edema
    │
    ▼ Rash: Maculopapular → confluent → erythroderma
    │
    ▼ Organ involvement: Liver (hepatitis) > Kidney > Lung > Heart
    │
    ▼ HHV-6 reactivation (week 3-4) → secondary fever spike
    │
    ▼ Prolonged course: 4-12 weeks (relapse on steroid taper)
```

### Clinical Triad
| Feature | Frequency | Details |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Fever** | >90% | High (>38°C), may be biphasic (HHV-6 reactivation) |
| **Rash** | 95-100% | Maculopapular → confluent → **erythroderma** (70%); facial edema; **Nikolsky negative** |
| **Eosinophilia** | 90% | **≥1.5×10⁹/L or >20%**; may be absent if on steroids |
| **Organ involvement** | 70-90% | **Liver > Kidney > Lung > Heart** |

### Organ Involvement Details
| Organ | Manifestations | Labs |
|-------|----------------|------|
| **Liver** (90%) | Hepatitis, cholestasis, **acute liver failure** | **ALT/AST ↑↑**, ALP ↑, bilirubin ↑, INR ↑ |
| **Kidney** (30-50%) | Interstitial nephritis, ATN, proteinuria | Creatinine ↑, eGFR ↓, eosinophiluria, ANA +ve |
| **Lung** (10-20%) | Pneumonitis, pleural effusion, ARDS | CXR: bilateral infiltrates; BAL: eosinophils |
| **Heart** (5-10%) | Myocarditis, pericarditis, arrhythmia | Troponin ↑, BNP ↑, ECG: ST-T changes |
| **Thyroid** | Thyrotoxicosis → hypothyroidism (autoimmune) | TSH, T3/T4, anti-TPO, anti-Tg |

## 6. DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA

### 6.1 RegiSCAR Scoring (European) — **Most Used**

| Criterion | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| Fever ≥38°C | 0 / 1 |
| Lymphadenopathy (≥2 sites, >1 cm) | 0 / 1 |
| Eosinophilia: 0.7-1.49×10⁹/L | 1 |
| Eosinophilia: 1.5-2.99×10⁹/L | 2 |
| Eosinophilia: ≥3×10⁹/L | 3 |
| Atypical lymphocytes | 0 / 1 |
| Skin rash >50% BSA | 0 / 1 |
| Skin biopsy compatible | 0 / 1 |
| Organ involvement (liver/kidney/lung/heart/pancreas) | 1 each (max 2) |
| Resolution ≥15 days | 0 / 1 |
| Exclusion of other causes | 0 / 1 |

| Score | Diagnosis |
|-------|-----------|
| <2 | No DRESS |
| 2-3 | Possible |
| 4-5 | Probable |
| **≥5** | **Definite** |

### 6.2 J-SCAR (Japanese) — DIHS Criteria
**Typical DIHS:** All 7 criteria:
1. Maculopapular rash ≥3 weeks after drug start
2. Prolonged symptoms ≥2 weeks after drug stop
2. Fever >38°C
3. Liver/biliary abnormalities (ALT >100 U/L)
4. Leukocytosis (>11×10⁹/L) + eosinophilia >1.5×10⁹/L or atypical lymphs
5. Lymphadenopathy
6. HHV-6 reactivation (PCR/serology)

**Atypical DIHS:** 5/7 criteria (no HHV-6 reactivation)

---

## 7. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

| Condition | Key Differentiators |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **SJS/TEN** | Latency 1-4 wks; **necrosis/blisters**; mucosal ≥2 sites; Nikolsky+; **SCORTEN**; eosinophilia rare |
| **AGEP** | Latency 1-5 days; **sterile pustules**; neutrophilia; EUROSCAR; no organ involvement |
| **SJS/TEN overlap** | 10-30% BSA detachment + mucosal ≥2 sites |
| **Fixed Drug Eruption** | Same site recurrence; hyperpigmentation; no systemic features |
| **Serum Sickness-Like** | Urticaria, arthralgia, fever; C1q +ve; no eosinophilia |
| **Viral Exanthem** | No eosinophilia; self-limiting; no organ involvement |
| **Kawasaki** | Children; conjunctivitis, strawberry tongue, coronary aneurysm |
| **Acute Generalized Exanthematous Pustulosis (AGEP)** | See AGEP note |

> **Key:** **Eosinophilia + Organ involvement + Latency 2-8 weeks = DRESS**

---

## 8. INVESTIGATIONS

| Test | Purpose | Typical Findings |
|------|---------|------------------|
| **FBC + diff** | Eosinophilia | **≥1.5×10⁹/L or >20%**; atypical lymphs |
| **LFT** | Liver involvement | **AST/ALT ↑↑**, ALP ↑, bilirubin ↑ |
| **Renal** | Kidney | Creatinine ↑, eosinophiluria |
| **CXR / CT chest** | Lung | Bilateral infiltrates, effusion |
| **ECG / Echo / Troponin** | Heart | Myocarditis, arrhythmia |
| **Skin biopsy** | Histology | **Dermatitis with eosinophils**, interface change, keratinocyte apoptosis |
| **HHV-6 PCR (blood/saliva)** | Reactivation | **Positive in 70-80% (week 3-4)** |
| **DIF** | Exclude SJS/TEN/BP | Negative or nonspecific |
| **HLA typing** | Genetic risk | B*15:02, B*58:01, B*57:01 |

### Histopathology
- **Dense perivascular lymphohistiocytic infiltrate** with **eosinophils**
- **Interface dermatitis** (vacuolar change, keratinocyte apoptosis)
- **Dermal eosinophils** (key differentiator from SJS/TEN)
- **No full-thickness necrosis** (unlike TEN)

## 9. MANAGEMENT

### 9.1 Immediate Steps
1. **STOP CULPRIT DRUG IMMEDIATELY** (all suspect drugs)
2. **Admit** (HDU/ICU if organ failure)
3. **Supportive:** Fluids, electrolyte correction, wound care (if erythroderma)
4. **Infection surveillance** (blood cultures, HSV/VZV PCR)
5. **Daily LFT, renal, FBC, coagulation**

### 9.2 Systemic Corticosteroids — **Mainstay**

| Severity | Prednisolone Equivalent | Route |
|----------|-------------------------|-------|
| Mild (no organ involvement) | 0.5-1 mg/kg/day | Oral |
| Moderate (1 organ, mild renal) | 1-1.5 mg/kg/day | Oral/IV |
| **Severe (liver failure, multi-organ)** | **1-2 mg/kg/day** (max 60-80 mg) | **IV Methylprednisolone** |

> **Evidence:** No RCT; steroids standard. Early initiation reduces mortality.

### 9.3 Steroid-Sparing / Second-Line (If Steroid-Dependent/Failing)
| Agent | Dose | Indication |
|-------|------|------------|
| **IVIG** | 2 g/kg over 2-5 days | Steroid-refractory, multi-organ, contraindication to steroids |
| **Cyclosporine** | 3-5 mg/kg/day IV/oral | Steroid-refractory, rapid control needed |
| **Mycophenolate** | 2-3 g/day | Steroid-sparing |
| **Rituximab** | 375 mg/m² × 4 / 1000 mg×2 | Steroid-refractory, prolonged course |
| **Pulse Methylprednisolone** | 500-1000 mg IV × 3-5 days | Life-threatening (liver failure, myocarditis) |

> **Evidence:** Cyclosporine and IVIG have strongest case-series support.

### 9.4 HHV-6 Reactivation (Week 3-4)
- **Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir** if severe/reactivation syndrome
- **Foscarnet** if ganciclovir-resistant
- Most cases self-limited; treat only if severe/cytopenias

### 9.4 Tapering & Relapse Prevention
| Phase | Strategy |
|-------|----------|
| **Acute control** | Prednisolone 1-2 mg/kg/day until LFTs normalizing, eosinophilia resolved |
| **Taper** | Reduce 10 mg/week to 20 mg → then 5 mg/2-4 weeks → 2.5 mg |
| **Maintenance** | 5-10 mg pred for 3-6 months post-remission |
| **Relapse** | Restart previous effective dose + slow taper |

> **Relapse rate:** 20-30% during taper (esp. 2-4 weeks after drug stop). Monitor LFT weekly during taper.

## 10. COMPLICATIONS & MORTALITY

| Complication | Mortality Contribution |
|--------------|------------------------|
| **Acute liver failure** | Leading cause (40-50%) |
| **Sepsis** | 20-30% |
| **Myocarditis / Arrhythmia** | 10-15% |
| **Renal failure** | 5-10% |
| **Lung involvement (ARDS)** | 5-10% |

**Mortality:** **5-10%** overall; **>20%** if liver failure + age >60.

**Poor Prognostic Factors:**
- Age >60
- Liver failure (bilirubin >5x ULN, INR >1.5)
- Renal failure
- Delayed steroid initiation (>7 days from onset)
- High RegiSCAR score
- Continued culprit drug exposure
