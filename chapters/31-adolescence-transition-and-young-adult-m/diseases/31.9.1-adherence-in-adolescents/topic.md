# Adherence in adolescents

---

## Definition

Adherence (preferred term to compliance) is the extent to which a persons behaviour corresponds with agreed recommendations from a healthcare provider. Adolescent adherence is often poor (50% non-adherence in chronic conditions) due to developmental factors (risk-taking, peer influence, desire for normality), cognitive factors (poor future orientation), and disease factors (asymptomatic intervals, complex regimens). Consequences: disease progression, treatment failure, resistance (HIV, TB). Strategies: simplified regimens, reminder systems, education, motivational interviewing, family involvement, transition preparation.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
50% non-adherence in adolescent chronic disease; 25% in acute conditions

### Adolescent factors
Risk-taking, peer influence, desire for normality, poor future orientation

### Disease factors
Asymptomatic intervals, complex regimens, side effects, restricted lifestyle

### Measurement
Self-report (overestimates), pharmacy refill, MEMS caps, drug levels (e.g., antiretrovirals)

### Morisky scale
8-item validated self-report adherence scale

### Strategies
Simplify regimen, once-daily dosing, blister packs, alarms, app reminders

### Motivational interviewing
Elicit reasons for change; address ambivalence; family involvement

### Consequences of non-adherence
Disease progression, transplant rejection, antiretroviral resistance, death

### Informed dissent
Competent adolescent may decline treatment; respect while exploring reasons

### Transition prep
Self-management skills (meds, appointments, communication) start in early adolescence

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Adherence in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
