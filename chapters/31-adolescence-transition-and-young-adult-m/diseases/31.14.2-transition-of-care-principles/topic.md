# Transition of care principles

---

## Definition

Transition is the purposeful, planned movement of adolescents and young adults with chronic conditions from child-centred to adult-centred healthcare. Distinct from transfer (the actual event of moving), transition is a multi-year process. Failures in transition are associated with disengagement, non-adherence, emergency admissions and increased morbidity. National guidance (NICE 2016, Got Transition, AAP/AAFP/ACP consensus 2011) outlines 6 core elements: transition policy, tracking, readiness assessment, planning, transfer of care, and completion. Transition should be developmentally appropriate, coordinated, and start in early adolescence (12-14 yrs).

### Definition

### Transition vs transfer
Transition = process; Transfer = event

### 6 core elements
Policy, Tracking, Readiness assessment, Planning, Transfer, Completion

### NICE NG43 (2016)
UK guidance; transition planning from age 13-14

### Got Transition
US six-core-elements framework; widely used

### Timing
Start planning ~12-14 yrs; transfer typically 16-19 yrs; flexible

### Outcome data
Poor transition -> non-adherence, disengagement, ED visits, morbidity

### Multidisciplinary
Paediatric + adult teams + GP + adolescent + family

### Named worker
Transition coordinator helps navigation; improves outcomes

### Quality indicators
Successful transfer, attendance at adult clinic, disease control, satisfaction

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Transition of care principles** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
