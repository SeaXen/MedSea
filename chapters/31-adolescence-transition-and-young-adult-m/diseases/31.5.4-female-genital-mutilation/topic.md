# Female genital mutilation

---

## Definition

Female genital mutilation (FGM) comprises all procedures involving partial or total removal of external female genitalia or other injury to the female genital organs for non-medical reasons (WHO). Classified into 4 types (I-IV) by WHO. ~200 million girls/women in 30 countries affected, mainly Africa and Middle East; ~3.6 million at risk annually. UK practice: mandatory reporting for under-18s (FGM is a serious crime); mandatory health/education data collection. Health complications: acute (haemorrhage, infection, pain), obstetric (prolonged labour, fistula, neonatal death), psychological (PTSD, depression, anxiety), sexual (dyspareunia, sexual dysfunction). Management: deinfibulation (type III reversal) before pregnancy/vaginal delivery; multi-disciplinary team; safeguarding mandatory.

## Core Clinical Content

### WHO definition
All procedures removing/injuring female genitalia for non-medical reasons

### WHO classification
Type I (clitoridectomy), II (excision), III (infibulation), IV (other)

### Prevalence
~200 million affected; ~3.6 million at risk annually

### Geographic distribution
30 countries; mainly Africa, Middle East; some Asia

### UK legal
FGM Act 2003; criminal offence; mandatory reporting <18 (since 2015)

### Acute complications
Haemorrhage, infection (sepsis, tetanus), pain, urinary retention

### Long-term complications
Chronic pain, dyspareunia, recurrent UTI, dysmenorrhoea

### Obstetric complications
Prolonged labour, fistula, perinatal death

### Psychological
PTSD, depression, anxiety, sexual dysfunction

### Deinfibulation
Surgical reversal of type III; before pregnancy/delivery
