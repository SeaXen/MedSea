# Anxiety disorders in adolescents

---

## Definition

Anxiety disorders (generalised anxiety, panic, social anxiety, separation anxiety, specific phobias) are the commonest mental health disorders in adolescents with prevalence ~10-20%. They often co-occur with depression, OCD, eating disorders and substance use. Presentation may be somatic (headache, abdominal pain, fatigue, school refusal). Screening (GAD-7, SCARED) helps identify. Treatment: psychoeducation, parent training, CBT (first-line) including exposure therapy, SSRIs for moderate-severe. School avoidance/refusal is a common presentation and may require multidisciplinary input (school, mental health, family).

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
10-20% of adolescents; commonest mental health disorder

### Common types
GAD, social anxiety, separation anxiety, specific phobias, panic, OCD-related

### Somatic presentation
Headache, abdominal pain, fatigue, palpitations - medical workup may be normal

### School refusal
Common presenting feature; often driven by social anxiety or bullying

### Comorbidity
Depression, OCD, eating disorders, ADHD, substance use very common

### Screening
GAD-7, SCARED (Screen for Child Anxiety Related Disorders)

### First-line therapy
CBT (including exposure) - 8-12 sessions; parent involvement key

### SSRIs
Fluoxetine, sertraline, fluvoxamine for moderate-severe; allow 4-6 weeks

### School-based interventions
Psychoeducation, exam adjustments, gradual return, mentor support

### Prognosis
Half remit by adulthood; comorbidity worsens outcome

> First-line management: SSRIs - Fluoxetine, sertraline, fluvoxamine for moderate-severe; allow 4-6 weeks; School-based interventions - Psychoeducation, exam adjustments, gradual return, mentor support. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Anxiety disorders in adolescents?**
> Red flags and complications: Prognosis - Half remit by adulthood; comorbidity worsens outcome. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Anxiety disorders in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
