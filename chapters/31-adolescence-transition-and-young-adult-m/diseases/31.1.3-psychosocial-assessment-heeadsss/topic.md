# Psychosocial assessment - HEEADSSS

---

## Definition

HEEADSSS is the modern, evidence-based psychosocial assessment for adolescents, expanding the older HEADSS framework. It is a structured, non-linear interview that should be offered to all adolescents regardless of presenting complaint, with the order adapted to the individual. The 3.0 version adds Screen time reflecting the digital era. The 7-8-minute interview identifies risk behaviours, mental health concerns, and resilience factors. It is the recommended screening tool in FCPS/MRCP adolescent clinical skills stations and is the basis of the HEEADSSS-SSHADESS strength-based variations.

## Core Clinical Content

### H - Home
Who lives with you? Any recent changes? Safety at home?

### E - Education/Employment
School year, performance, attendance, work hours (<20 hr/wk protective)

### E - Eating
Diet, body image, weight concerns, screen for eating disorder

### A - Activities
Hobbies, sport, screen time, social media, peer group

### D - Drugs
Cigarettes, alcohol, cannabis, vaping, prescription, illicit drugs

### S - Sexuality
Gender identity, sexual activity, contraception, STIs, partners

### S - Suicide/Self-harm
Mood, sleep, hopelessness, prior attempts, ideation

### S - Safety
Abuse, bullying, seatbelt, weapons, online safety

### Plus Screen time (3.0)
Hours/day, content, social media, gaming, sleep impact

### Strengths/Resilience
Always end with strengths: what are you good at? protective factors

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Psychosocial assessment - HEEADSSS** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
