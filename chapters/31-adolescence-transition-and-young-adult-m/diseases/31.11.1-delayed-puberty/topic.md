# Delayed puberty

---

## Definition

Delayed puberty is defined as no secondary sexual characteristics by age 13 in girls (or 4 years post-thelarche) or age 14 in boys (or 5 years post-testicular enlargement). Causes are classified as hypogonadotropic (central, low LH/FSH) or hypergonadotropic (primary gonadal failure, high LH/FSH). Constitutional delay of growth and puberty (CDGP) is the commonest cause in boys. Investigations include bone age, LH/FSH, oestradiol/testosterone, prolactin, TSH, karyotype, MRI pituitary if central. Treatment depends on cause - observation for CDGP, sex-steroid replacement, or definitive management of underlying cause.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
No thelarche by 13 in girls; no testicular enlargement by 14 in boys

### Most common cause
Constitutional delay of growth and puberty (CDGP), especially boys

### Hypogonadotropic
Central; low LH/FSH; causes: CDGP, Kallmann, anorexia, hypopituitarism, chronic disease

### Hypergonadotropic
Primary gonadal failure; high LH/FSH; causes: Turner, Klinefelter, chemo/radiotherapy

### Kallmann syndrome
Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism + anosmia; KAL1, FGFR1 mutations

### Turner syndrome
45,X; streak ovaries; primary amenorrhoea, short stature, webbed neck

### Klinefelter syndrome
47,XXY; small firm testes, gynaecomastia, tall stature, infertility

### Investigations
Bone age (X-ray wrist), LH/FSH, oestradiol/testosterone, prolactin, TSH, karyotype

### Treatment CDGP
Observation; short course oxandrolone (boys) or oestradiol (girls)

### Permanent hypogonadism
Long-term sex-steroid replacement; fertility counselling

- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
