# Menstrual disorders in adolescents

---

## Definition

Menstrual disorders are common in adolescents due to immaturity of the hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian (HPO) axis in the first 2-3 years post-menarche. The most common presentations are primary amenorrhoea, secondary amenorrhoea, oligomenorrhoea, heavy menstrual bleeding (HMB), and dysmenorrhoea. After 2-3 years of regular cycles, anovulatory cycles become less common and pathology (PCOS, pregnancy, bleeding disorders, thyroid disease) should be considered. The PALM-COEIN classification is used for HMB. Pelvic ultrasound and examination are key investigations; management depends on cause and patient age.

## Core Clinical Content

### Anovulation common
HPO axis immature first 2-3 years post-menarche

### Primary amenorrhoea
No menses by 15 (or 3 years post-thelarche); 13 with no secondary sexual characteristics

### Causes primary
Constitutional delay, hypogonadotropic, hypergonadotropic, Mullerian agenesis, imperforate hymen

### Secondary amenorrhoea
No menses >=3-6 months after cycles established

### Causes secondary
Pregnancy, PCOS, hypothalamic (stress/weight/exercise), thyroid, hyperprolactinaemia

### HMB (heavy menstrual bleeding)
PALM-COEIN: Polyp, Adenomyosis, Leiomyoma, Malignancy / Coagulopathy, Ovulatory, Endometrial, Iatrogenic, Not classified

### Bleeding disorders
Von Willebrand disease in 10-20% of adolescent HMB

### Dysmenorrhoea
Primary: prostaglandin-mediated, NSAIDs first-line; Secondary: endometriosis, fibroids

### Endometriosis
Possible in adolescents with severe primary dysmenorrhoea; responds to NSAIDs/OCP

### Management
Treat underlying; tranexamic acid, NSAIDs, hormonal (OCP, Mirena)

> First-line management: Dysmenorrhoea - Primary: prostaglandin-mediated, NSAIDs first-line; Secondary: endometriosis, fibroids; Endometriosis - Possible in adolescents with severe primary dysmenorrhoea; responds to NSAIDs/OCP. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Menstrual disorders in adolescents?**
> Red flags and complications: Management - Treat underlying; tranexamic acid, NSAIDs, hormonal (OCP, Mirena). Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.
