# OCD in adolescents

---

## Definition

Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) in adolescents is characterised by obsessions (intrusive, unwanted thoughts causing anxiety) and/or compulsions (repetitive behaviours performed to reduce anxiety) that are time-consuming (>1 hr/day) or cause significant distress/impairment. Prevalence is 1-2% in adolescents; M=F; often comorbid with tics, ADHD, anxiety, depression. Onset typically 8-12 yrs. Diagnosis uses DSM-5 criteria adapted for developmental level. Management is multimodal: CBT with exposure and response prevention (ERP) first-line, SSRI (fluoxetine/fluvoxamine, higher doses than depression) as pharmacological adjunct. Family accommodation worsens prognosis. Prognosis: chronic waxing-waning course; ~40% recover.

> Red flags and complications: Family accommodation - Reducing family accommodation improves outcomes. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for OCD in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
