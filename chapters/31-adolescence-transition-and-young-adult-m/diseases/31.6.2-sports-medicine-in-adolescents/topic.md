# Sports medicine in adolescents

---

## Definition

Sports medicine in adolescents addresses the unique physiology of the young athlete, including open physes (growth plates), thermoregulation, and psychosocial development. Common conditions: apophysitis (Osgood-Schlatter, Severs), stress fractures, osteochondritis dissecans, concussion, anterior knee pain. Pre-participation evaluation screens for cardiac disease (HOCM, long QT), Marfan, asthma, and eating disorders in athletes. Sudden cardiac death is rare but the leading cause of death in young athletes. Concussion management is conservative with graduated return-to-play protocols (Concussion in Sport Group).

## Core Clinical Content

### Apophysitis
Traction injury at growth plate; Osgood-Schlatter (tibial tubercle), Severs (calcaneus)

### Osgood-Schlatter
Commonest cause of anterior knee pain in adolescents; self-limiting

### Stress fractures
Common in running athletes; tibial, metatarsal; low-risk vs high-risk

### Osteochondritis dissecans
Subchondral bone necrosis; knee, ankle; surgery if unstable

### Anterior knee pain
Most common MSK complaint; usually patellofemoral; physiotherapy

### Pre-participation evaluation
Cardiac history/exam; family history; asthma; eating disorder screen

### Sudden cardiac death
HOCM, anomalous coronary, long QT, Marfan, commotio cordis

### Concussion
Sport-related; SCAT5 assessment; rest 24-48 hr; graduated return-to-play

### Female athlete triad
Disordered eating, amenorrhoea, low BMD - RED-S (Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport)

### Recovery
Adequate rest, sleep, nutrition; overtraining syndrome - chronic fatigue, mood

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Sports medicine in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
