# Chronic fatigue syndrome and ME in adolescents

---

## Definition

Chronic fatigue syndrome (CFS), also known as myalgic encephalomyelitis (ME), is a complex, multi-system disorder characterised by profound, persistent fatigue lasting >3 months, post-exertional malaise (PEM), unrefreshing sleep, cognitive dysfunction ('brain fog'), and often orthostatic intolerance. CFS/ME commonly begins in adolescence (peak onset 11-19 yrs; F:M 3:1) and is a major cause of school absence and disability. Diagnosis is clinical (NICE 2021, CDC 2017, IOM 2015 criteria) after exclusion of alternative causes. Management is multi-component: pacing/activity management, sleep hygiene, symptom control, and multidisciplinary team input. Prognosis is variable - ~50% improve over time, but full recovery is uncommon.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Fatigue >3 months + PEM + unrefreshing sleep + cognitive dysfunction

### Prevalence
0.1-0.5% adolescents; F:M 3:1; peak onset 11-19 yrs

### Diagnostic criteria (NICE 2021)
All 4: fatigue, PEM, unrefreshing sleep, cognitive dysfunction

### Post-exertional malaise (PEM)
Worsening of symptoms 24-72 hr after physical/mental exertion; hallmark

### Orthostatic intolerance
POTS-like symptoms; standing test positive in ~50%

### Differential
Anaemia, thyroid, coeliac, IBD, depression, anxiety, sleep apnoea, malignancy

### Investigations
FBC, ferritin, TFTs, coeliac serology, B12, vitamin D, U&E, LFTs, ESR, urinalysis

### Red flag exclusions
Weight loss, lymphadenopathy, fever, night sweats - exclude malignancy/autoimmune

### Management
Pacing (energy envelope), sleep hygiene, graded activity, CBT for coping

### Prognosis
~50% improve over 5 yrs; full recovery uncommon; early intervention improves outcomes

- **Mnemonic for Chronic fatigue syndrome and ME in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
