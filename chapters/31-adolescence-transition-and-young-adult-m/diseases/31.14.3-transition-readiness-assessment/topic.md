# Transition readiness assessment

---

## Definition

Transition readiness assessment evaluates the adolescents knowledge, skills, and self-management behaviours needed for adult healthcare. Validated tools include the STARx (Self-Management and Transition to Adulthood Questionnaire), Am I ON TRAC, and Transition Readiness Assessment Questionnaire (TRAQ). Domains: disease knowledge, medication management, communication with providers, navigation of healthcare, self-advocacy, daily living skills. Assessment should start in early adolescence and repeat over time, informing transition planning. Health literacy, executive function, neurodevelopmental conditions (autism, ADHD) and learning disability need to be considered.

**Q3. How is Transition readiness assessment recognised clinically?**
> Presentation and key clinical features include: STARx - Self-Management and Transition to Adulthood with Rx = medication domain; Health literacy - Understanding of own condition, treatments, when to seek help. See Core Clinical Content for full details.

**Q4. What investigations are required for Transition readiness assessment?**
> Investigations include: Executive function - Planning, organisation, problem-solving; assess in ADHD, learning disability; Self-advocacy - Ability to ask questions, raise concerns, express preferences. See Core Clinical Content for full details.

**Q5. What is the first-line management of Transition readiness assessment?**
> First-line management: Communication - Talking to clinicians alone, telephone triage, asking about results; Practical - Own medications, prescription refills, appointment booking, transport. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Transition readiness assessment?**
> Red flags and complications: Reasonable adjustments - Mental capacity assessment, easy-read materials, advocates. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Transition readiness assessment** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
