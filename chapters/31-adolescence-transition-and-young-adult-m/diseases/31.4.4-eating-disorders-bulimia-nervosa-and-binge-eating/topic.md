# Eating disorders - bulimia nervosa and binge eating

---

## Definition

Bulimia nervosa (BN) is a recurrent binge-eating disorder with compensatory behaviours (vomiting, laxatives, exercise, fasting) at least weekly for 3 months, in normal-weight or overweight individuals. Binge eating disorder (BED) is recurrent binge eating without compensation. Both peak in late adolescence/early adulthood; F:M ratio lower than AN (3:1 for BN). Complications of BN: electrolyte disturbance (hypokalaemia), dental erosions, parotid hypertrophy, oesophagitis/gastritis, Russell sign (knuckle calluses). Management: CBT-E (enhanced CBT) first-line; fluoxetine 60 mg (only licensed medication for BN); addressing underlying mood/anxiety.

## Core Clinical Content

### BN DSM-5
Recurrent binges + compensatory behaviours, >=weekly, >=3 months, normal weight

### BED DSM-5
Recurrent binges without compensation; marked distress

### Compensatory behaviours
Self-induced vomiting, laxatives, diuretics, exercise, fasting

### Physical signs
Russell sign (knuckle calluses), parotid hypertrophy, dental erosions, eroded enamel

### Electrolytes
Hypokalaemia, hypochloraemic metabolic alkalosis, dehydration

### Complications
Oesophagitis, gastric dilatation, arrhythmias, sudden death (hypokalaemia)

### Differential AN binge-purge
AN with binge-purge: low weight; BN: normal or overweight

### CBT-E
Enhanced cognitive behavioural therapy - first-line for BN and BED

### Pharmacotherapy BN
Fluoxetine 60 mg (only licensed drug for adult BN)

### Prognosis
Better than AN; 50-70% recover; risk of relapse

> Red flags and complications: Prognosis - Better than AN; 50-70% recover; risk of relapse. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.
