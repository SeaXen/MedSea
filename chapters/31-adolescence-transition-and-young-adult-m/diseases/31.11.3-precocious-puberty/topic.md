# Precocious puberty

---

## Definition

Precocious puberty is the onset of secondary sexual characteristics before age 8 in girls or 9 in boys. It is classified as central (GnRH-dependent, true precocious puberty - premature HPG activation) or peripheral (GnRH-independent, false - sex steroids from gonads/adrenals/exogenous). Central is more common in girls (often idiopathic) and far more common in boys (CNS lesion until proven otherwise - hypothalamic hamartoma, glioma). Investigations: bone age (advanced), LH/FSH, oestradiol/testosterone, 17-OH progesterone (CAH), GnRH stimulation test, MRI brain. Treatment depends on cause - GnRH analogues for central, treat underlying cause for peripheral.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Secondary sexual characteristics <8 yrs (girls) or <9 yrs (boys)

### Central (true)
GnRH-dependent; premature HPG activation; idiopathic commonest in girls

### Peripheral (false)
GnRH-independent; sex steroids from gonads/adrenals/exogenous

### Boys with precocious
CNS lesion until proven otherwise - MRI brain mandatory

### Hypothalamic hamartoma
Classic cause; gelastic seizures + central precocious puberty

### CAH (congenital adrenal hyperplasia)
21-hydroxylase deficiency; virilisation in girls, premature in boys

### McCune-Albright
Peripheral precocious puberty + cafe-au-lait + polyostotic fibrous dysplasia

### GnRH stimulation test
Central: LH rises >5 IU/L; Peripheral: no response

### Treatment central
GnRH analogues (leuprorelin, triptorelin) - monthly/3-monthly IM/SC

### Treatment peripheral
Treat underlying cause: CAH -> hydrocortisone; tumour -> surgery

**Q3. How is Precocious puberty recognised clinically?**
> Presentation and key clinical features include: Boys with precocious - CNS lesion until proven otherwise - MRI brain mandatory; Hypothalamic hamartoma - Classic cause; gelastic seizures + central precocious puberty. See Core Clinical Content for full details.

**Q4. What investigations are required for Precocious puberty?**
> Investigations include: CAH (congenital adrenal hyperplasia) - 21-hydroxylase deficiency; virilisation in girls, premature in boys; McCune-Albright - Peripheral precocious puberty + cafe-au-lait + polyostotic fibrous dysplasia. See Core Clinical Content for full details.

**Q5. What is the first-line management of Precocious puberty?**
> First-line management: GnRH stimulation test - Central: LH rises >5 IU/L; Peripheral: no response; Treatment central - GnRH analogues (leuprorelin, triptorelin) - monthly/3-monthly IM/SC. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Precocious puberty?**
> Red flags and complications: Treatment peripheral - Treat underlying cause: CAH -> hydrocortisone; tumour -> surgery. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.
