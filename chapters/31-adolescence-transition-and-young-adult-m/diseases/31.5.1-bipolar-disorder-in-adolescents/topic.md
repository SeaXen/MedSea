# Bipolar disorder in adolescents

---

## Definition

Bipolar disorder in adolescents is characterised by episodes of elevated/irritable mood with increased energy/activity (mania/hypomania) alternating with periods of depression. DSM-5 distinguishes bipolar I (>=1 manic episode, duration >=7 days or hospitalisation) and bipolar II (>=1 hypomanic + >=1 major depressive episode). Mixed episodes and rapid cycling are common in adolescents. Prevalence 1-3% adolescents; M=F; high suicide risk (15-20x general population). Often misdiagnosed as ADHD, conduct disorder, or unipolar depression. Management: mood stabilisers (lithium, valproate, lamotrigine), atypical antipsychotics (quetiapine, olanzapine, risperidone), psychotherapy (CBT, family-focused, IPSRT). Comorbid ADHD/anxiety common. Prognosis: chronic relapsing course; treatment adherence a major challenge.

### Bipolar I

### Bipolar II

### Mania criteria (DIG FAST)
Distractibility, Irresponsibility, Grandiosity, Flight of ideas, Activity increased, Sleep decreased, Talkativeness

### Prevalence
1-3% adolescents; M=F

### Suicide risk
15-20x general population; highest in mixed episodes

### Misdiagnosis
Often confused with ADHD, conduct disorder, unipolar depression

### First-line pharmacotherapy
Lithium (gold standard), valproate, atypical antipsychotics

### Lithium monitoring
TFTs, U&E, Ca, pregnancy test; narrow therapeutic index

### Lamotrigine
Effective for bipolar depression; slow titration (Stevens-Johnson risk)

### Valproate
Avoid in females (teratogenicity); useful in males, severe mania
