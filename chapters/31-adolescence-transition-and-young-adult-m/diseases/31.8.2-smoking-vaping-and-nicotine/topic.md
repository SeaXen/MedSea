# Smoking, vaping and nicotine

---

## Definition

Tobacco use in adolescents has declined (UK 3% of 15-yr-olds smoke regularly), but vaping/e-cigarettes have surged, with 20-30% of 15-yr-olds having tried vaping. Nicotine is highly addictive, and adolescent brains are particularly susceptible. E-cigarettes deliver high nicotine concentrations; long-term respiratory and cardiovascular harms are emerging. Cannabis is typically smoked with tobacco. Management: brief advice, nicotine replacement therapy (NRT) for dependent smokers, behavioural support, school-based prevention. Vaping-related lung injury (EVALI in US) and popcorn lung (bronchiolitis obliterans) are recognised harms.

## Core Clinical Content

### Smoking prevalence (UK)
3% of 15-yr-olds regular smokers; 20-30% have tried vaping

### Nicotine addiction
High; adolescent brains more susceptible (prefrontal maturation)

### Vaping harms
EVALI (US outbreak 2019), popcorn lung (diacetyl), acute lung injury

### Cannabis
Usually smoked with tobacco; 20% of 15-yr-olds have tried; linked to psychosis risk

### NRT in adolescents
Safe and effective; patches, gum, lozenges; behavioural support first-line

### Brief advice
3 As: Ask, Advise, Arrange (referral)

### Behavioural support
Motivational interviewing, NRT, school-based programmes

### Pharmacotherapy
Varenicline/bupropion not licensed <18; NRT is the option

### School-based prevention
Evidence-based programmes; social norms approach

### Popcorn lung
Bronchiolitis obliterans from diacetyl; some early reports in vapers
