# Eating disorders - anorexia nervosa

---

## Definition

Anorexia nervosa (AN) is a restrictive eating disorder with deliberate weight loss, intense fear of gaining weight, and disturbance in self-perceived body shape (DSM-5). Two subtypes: restricting and binge-purge. Diagnostic threshold: significantly low weight (BMI <18.5 in adults; <5th centile in adolescents). Adolescent onset is typical; F:M ratio 10:1. Medical complications: bradycardia, hypotension, hypothermia, amenorrhoea, osteoporosis, electrolyte disturbance. Management: medical stabilisation, family-based therapy (FBT/Maudsley) first-line for adolescents, CBT-E, nutritional rehabilitation, SSRI (fluoxetine) for comorbid OCD/depression. Inpatient care for medical instability.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
Restriction -> low weight; intense fear of gaining weight; body image disturbance

### Subtypes
Restricting; Binge-eating/purging (compensatory behaviours)

### BMI threshold
Adult <18.5; adolescent <5th centile (less reliable than weight history)

### Prevalence
0.5-1% F; M:F = 1:10; peak onset 13-18 yrs

### Risk factors
Perfectionism, family history, low self-esteem, ballet/athletics, dieting, ACEs

### Medical complications
Bradycardia, hypotension, hypothermia, amenorrhoea, osteoporosis, lanugo, electrolytes

### Refeeding syndrome
Hypophosphataemia, hypokalaemia, hypomagnesaemia; start at low kcal, monitor

### Family-based therapy (FBT)
Maudsley model; first-line for adolescents; 3 phases (weight restoration -> control -> adolescent)

### Medical admission criteria
BMI <13, HR <40, BP <80/50, electrolytes, temperature <35, syncope

### Prognosis
50% recover, 25% chronic, 5-10% mortality (highest of psychiatric disorders)

- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
