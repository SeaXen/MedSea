# Tic disorders and Tourette syndrome

---

## Definition

Tic disorders are neurodevelopmental conditions characterised by sudden, rapid, recurrent, non-rhythmic motor movements or vocalisations. Provisional tic disorder (<1 year), persistent (chronic) motor or vocal tic disorder (>1 year, one type), and Tourette syndrome (>1 year, multiple motor + at least one vocal tic) are the main DSM-5 categories. Onset before age 18. Prevalence: Tourette 1%, chronic motor tic 1-2%, provisional tic 5-20% (transient in childhood). M:F 3:1. Strong comorbidity with ADHD (50%), OCD (30-50%), anxiety, learning disability. Management: reassurance for mild; behavioural therapy (CBIT — Comprehensive Behavioral Intervention for Tics); alpha-2 agonists (clonidine, guanfacine) for moderate; antipsychotics (risperidone, aripiprazole) for severe refractory. Most tic disorders improve in adolescence/adulthood.

## Core Clinical Content

Sudden rapid recurrent non-rhythmic movements (motor) or vocalisations (vocal)

### Provisional tic disorder
Duration <1 year (often transient in childhood)

### Persistent tic disorder

### Tourette syndrome

### Prevalence

### Onset

### Comorbidity

### First-line

### Pharmacotherapy
Clonidine, guanfacine; risperidone, aripiprazole for severe

### Prognosis
Most improve by late adolescence/adulthood; 1/3 persist
