# Sexual health and gender identity

---

## Definition

Adolescent sexual and gender identity development is a normal part of adolescence, with sexual orientation (who one is attracted to), gender identity (ones sense of self as male, female, both, neither) and gender expression (how one presents) evolving through adolescence. LGBT+ adolescents have higher rates of mental health problems, self-harm, substance use, and homelessness due to stigma and minority stress. Gender dysphoria requires specialist multidisciplinary input (CAMHS, endocrinology for puberty suppression, surgical). All adolescents need a safe, confidential space to discuss identity.

## Core Clinical Content

### Sexual orientation
Lesbian, gay, bisexual, queer (LGBQ); usually emerges mid-adolescence

### Gender identity
Internal sense; distinct from assigned sex at birth

### Transgender
Gender identity differs from assigned sex; may experience dysphoria

### Non-binary
Gender identity outside male/female binary

### Coming out
Process of disclosing identity; varies; may be safe or risky

### Minority stress
Stigma, discrimination, rejection drive higher mental health risk

### Mental health
LGBTQ+ 2-3x higher rates of depression, anxiety, self-harm, suicide

### Puberty suppression
GnRH analogues (triptorelin) for adolescents with persistent gender dysphoria

### CAMHS
Child and adolescent mental health service; specialist gender clinics (GIDS in UK)

### Supportive care
Affirming name/pronouns, family acceptance, school support - improves outcomes
