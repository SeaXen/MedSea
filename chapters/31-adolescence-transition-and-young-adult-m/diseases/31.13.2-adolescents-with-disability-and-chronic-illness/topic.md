# Adolescents with disability and chronic illness

---

## Definition

Adolescents with physical, learning, or developmental disabilities face higher rates of health problems, mental health issues, social isolation, and barriers to healthcare. They may have age-specific concerns about puberty (period management, fertility, sexual function), transition, education (EHCP in UK), employment, independent living, and social relationships. Care is multidisciplinary (paediatrician, GP, OT, physio, SALT, social work, special educational needs, mental health). Reasonable adjustments (Equality Act 2010) are mandatory. Transition to adult services is often more complex and starts earlier.

**Q5. What is the first-line management of Adolescents with disability and chronic illness?**
> First-line management: Transition - More complex; adult disability services, social care, employment support; Carers - Parents/carers need support; young carer assessment; respite. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Adolescents with disability and chronic illness?**
> Red flags and complications: Sexual health - Often overlooked; Fraser principles still apply; contraception, STI prevention. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Adolescents with disability and chronic illness** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
