# ADHD in adolescents

---

## Definition

Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterised by persistent inattention, hyperactivity and impulsivity that interferes with function. It affects 5-7% of children/adolescents, F:M 1:2-3, often persisting into adulthood. Diagnosis is clinical (DSM-5/ICD-11) with symptoms present before age 12, in >=2 settings (school, home), and causing impairment. ADHD has high comorbidity with oppositional defiant disorder (ODD), conduct disorder, anxiety, depression, substance use, and learning disability. Management is multimodal: psychoeducation, school accommodations, behavioural therapy, and pharmacotherapy (methylphenidate first-line, lisdexamfetamine, atomoxetine, guanfacine).

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for ADHD in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
