# Adolescent pregnancy

---

## Definition

Adolescent pregnancy (10-19 years) is associated with higher maternal and neonatal complications - pre-eclampsia, preterm birth, low birth weight, caesarean section, postnatal depression, and social/educational disruption. UK has the highest teenage pregnancy rate in Western Europe. Risk factors include deprivation, low educational attainment, sexual abuse, substance use, and mental health issues. Management requires a multidisciplinary team (obstetrician, midwife, paediatrician, social work, mental health). Contraception, antenatal care, safeguarding, and post-natal support are the four pillars. Confidential care and Fraser guidelines apply.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence (UK)
Declining; ~13/1000 in under-18s; highest in Western Europe historically

### Risk factors

### Complications maternal

### Complications fetal

Discussed antenatally; LARC (depot/IUS) suitable for adolescent mothers

### Termination

### Prevention
Comprehensive RSE, LARC access, school-based clinics, support for looked-after children

> Red flags and complications: Prevention - Comprehensive RSE, LARC access, school-based clinics, support for looked-after children. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Adolescent pregnancy** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
