# Alcohol and drug use in adolescents

---

## Definition

Adolescent alcohol and drug use is common - by age 15, ~50% have tried alcohol and ~20% cannabis. Most use is experimental and short-lived; ~5-10% develop harmful use patterns or dependency. The developing brain (prefrontal cortex maturation) makes adolescents particularly vulnerable to addiction. Common substances: alcohol, cannabis, MDMA, cocaine, amphetamines, ketamine, nitrous oxide. Management: brief intervention, motivational interviewing, harm reduction (never alone, drink water, test drugs, naloxone for opioids), referral to specialist CAMHS or young persons drug service. Substance use is a safeguarding concern under 18.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Alcohol and drug use in adolescents?**
> Red flags and complications: Safeguarding - Substance use in <18 is a safeguarding concern; multi-agency assessment. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
