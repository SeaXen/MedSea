# Headache in adolescents

---

## Definition

Headache is a common adolescent presentation; 50-75% of 12-17 year-olds report recurrent headache. Primary headaches (tension, migraine, cluster) are most common; secondary causes (raised ICP, infection, vascular) are rare but must be excluded. Migraine affects 5-10% of adolescents, F > M, often familial. Tension-type headache is the most common cause of recurrent headache. Red flags: sudden ('thunderclap'), worst-ever, focal neuro deficit, fever, neck stiffness, papilloedema, weight loss, vomiting on waking - all need urgent investigation (CT/MRI, LP if indicated).

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
50-75% adolescents report recurrent headache; 5-10% migraine

### Primary types
Tension (most common), migraine, cluster, medication-overuse

### Migraine criteria
>=5 attacks; 4-72 hr; >=2 of unilateral, throbbing, mod-severe, aggravated by activity

### Migraine associated symptoms
Nausea, photophobia, phonophobia, aura (visual/sensory)

### Tension headache
Bilateral, pressing/tightening, mild-moderate, no nausea, no photophobia

### Cluster headache
Severe unilateral periorbital, autonomic features, 15-180 min, clusters

### Red flags (urgent imaging)
Thunderclap, worst-ever, focal deficit, fever, papilloedema, weight loss

### Triggers
Stress, sleep change, missed meals, dehydration, hormonal, screen, alcohol

### Acute migraine Rx
Ibuprofen 400-600 mg or sumatriptan nasal 10-20 mg; early use effective

### Prophylaxis
Topiramate, propranolol, amitriptyline, CBT, riboflavin 400 mg

**Q5. What is the first-line management of Headache in adolescents?**
> First-line management: Triggers - Stress, sleep change, missed meals, dehydration, hormonal, screen, alcohol; Acute migraine Rx - Ibuprofen 400-600 mg or sumatriptan nasal 10-20 mg; early use effective. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Headache in adolescents?**
> Red flags and complications: Prophylaxis - Topiramate, propranolol, amitriptyline, CBT, riboflavin 400 mg. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Headache in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
