# Safeguarding and child protection in adolescence

---

## Definition

Adolescents are vulnerable to safeguarding concerns including physical, sexual and emotional abuse, neglect, exploitation (child sexual exploitation, county lines, online exploitation), radicalisation, and harm from outside the home. The Children Acts 1989/2004 require Local Authorities to safeguard children in need and protect those at risk of significant harm. The Working Together to Safeguard Children framework sets the multi-agency approach. Adolescent safeguarding is distinct from younger children - they are more likely to experience extra-familial harm, self-neglect, exploitation and mental health crises rather than neglect or physical abuse at home.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition of harm
Significant harm = ill-treatment or impairment of health/developmental wellbeing

### Types of abuse
Physical, sexual, emotional, neglect, exploitation (CSE, criminal, online)

### Children Acts
1989/2004: duty to safeguard; Section 47 enquiry if risk of significant harm

### Working Together
Multi-agency guidance; named professionals; LADO for allegations against staff

### Section 47 enquiry
Local authority investigation of risk of significant harm

### Child protection plan
If at continuing risk; CP conference, core group, plan review

### Adolescent-specific
Extra-familial harm, exploitation, county lines, online harms, self-neglect

### CSE (child sexual exploitation)
Under-18 in sexual activity in exchange for something; coercion key

### County lines
Gangs recruiting children to carry drugs; exploitation and modern slavery

### Information sharing
Fears of breaching data protection NOT a barrier to safeguarding

- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
