# Obesity in adolescents

---

## Definition

Adolescent obesity (BMI >95th centile for age/sex; overweight 85-95th) affects 20-25% of UK adolescents and is rising globally, with major consequences for current and adult health. Causes: energy imbalance, food environment, sedentary behaviour, sleep deprivation, mental health, endocrine (rare - Cushing, hypothyroid, Prader-Willi). Complications: type 2 diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidaemia, NAFLD, PCOS, obstructive sleep apnoea, musculoskeletal, depression, low self-esteem. Management: family-based multi-component intervention (diet, physical activity, behaviour change), pharmacotherapy (orlistat >12 yrs; semaglutide emerging), bariatric surgery (severe cases).

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Obesity in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
