# Self-harm and suicide in adolescents

---

## Definition

Self-harm (non-suicidal self-injury, NSSI) is intentional self-poisoning or injury without suicidal intent; suicide is self-harm with intent to die. Both are leading causes of adolescent death globally - suicide is the 2nd-3rd leading cause of death in 15-24 year-olds. Risk factors: prior self-harm, mental illness (depression, BPD), substance use, ACEs, bullying, LGBT identity, easy access to means. Immediate management: medical stabilisation of injury, mental health assessment, safety planning, restricting means (especially ligature points, medications, firearms). Long-term: dialectical behaviour therapy (DBT) for BPD, CBT, family therapy, school-based prevention.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Self-harm and suicide in adolescents?**
> Red flags and complications: Media reporting - Avoid detail on method; do not glorify; encourage help-seeking (Papageno effect). Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

- **Mnemonic for Self-harm and suicide in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
