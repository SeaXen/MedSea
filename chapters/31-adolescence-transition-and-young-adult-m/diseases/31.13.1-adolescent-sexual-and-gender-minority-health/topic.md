# Adolescent sexual and gender minority health

---

## Definition

LGBTQ+ (lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer/questioning, +) adolescents experience unique health disparities driven by minority stress, family rejection, and discrimination. They have 2-3x higher rates of depression, anxiety, self-harm, suicide attempts; higher rates of substance use, homelessness, and victimisation. Protective factors: family acceptance, school support, affirming healthcare. Gender dysphoria requires specialist input; puberty suppression, cross-sex hormones, and surgery are staged interventions. Mental health support is core to care, not optional.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
5-10% of adolescents identify as LGBT+

### Mental health
2-3x risk of depression, anxiety, self-harm, suicide attempts

### Substance use
Higher rates of smoking, alcohol, drug use as coping

### Homelessness
20-40% of homeless youth identify as LGBT+ (family rejection)

### Family acceptance
Strongest protective factor; reduces suicide risk 8x

### Affirming care
Use chosen name/pronouns; safe clinical environment; staff training

### Gender dysphoria
Distress from incongruence; specialist CAMHS/GIDS input

### Puberty suppression
GnRH analogues (triptorelin) at Tanner 2; reversible

### Cross-sex hormones
From ~16 yrs; oestradiol for trans girls, testosterone for trans boys

### Surgery
Genital surgery usually >=18; chest surgery can be earlier

- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
