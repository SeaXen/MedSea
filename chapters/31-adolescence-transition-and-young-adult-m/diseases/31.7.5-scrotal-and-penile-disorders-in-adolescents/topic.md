# Scrotal and penile disorders in adolescents

---

## Definition

Scrotal and penile disorders in adolescents include acute (testicular torsion - surgical emergency) and chronic (varicocele, hydrocele, epididymal cyst, epididymo-orchitis) scrotal pathology, and penile conditions (phimosis, balanitis, hypospadias sequelae, Peyronie's). Testicular torsion has peak incidence at 12-18 yrs and requires exploration within 6 hrs to save the testis (90% salvage if <6 hr, <10% if >24 hr). Varicocele affects 15% of adolescent males; usually left-sided, 'bag of worms', often asymptomatic but can impair fertility. Cancer awareness: testicular cancer is the commonest solid tumour in 15-35 yr-old males; teach self-examination.

## Core Clinical Content

### Testicular torsion
Surgical emergency; sudden severe pain, absent cremasteric reflex, high testis

### Torsion window
90% salvage <6 hr; <10% salvage >24 hr

### Torsion management
Urgent surgical exploration; bilateral orchidopexy

### Varicocele
15% adolescent males; left-sided (90%); 'bag of worms'; Valsalva positive

### Varicocele management
Observe if small/asymptomatic; surgery if large, painful, or testicular atrophy

### Hydrocele
Transilluminates; usually congenital (patent processus); most resolve

### Epididymal cyst/spermatocele
Painless, transilluminates, above testis

### Epididymo-orchitis
Painful, fever, dysuria, positive Prehn's sign; sexually active - STI screen

### Phimosis
Retraction of foreskin impossible; physiological until ~6 yrs; treat if symptomatic

### Testicular cancer
Commonest solid tumour 15-35 yr; painless lump; teach self-examination
