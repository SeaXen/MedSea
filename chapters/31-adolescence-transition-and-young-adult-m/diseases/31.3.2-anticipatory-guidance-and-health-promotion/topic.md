# Anticipatory guidance and health promotion

---

## Definition

Anticipatory guidance is proactive counselling on health, development, and safety to prevent problems before they occur. For adolescents, it covers the HEEADSSS domains, plus immunisation, nutrition, physical activity, sleep, screen time, sexual health, mental health, substance use, and injury prevention. The Bright Futures (AAP) and Youre Welcome (UK DoH) frameworks set quality standards for adolescent-friendly health services. Effective delivery is brief, repeated, and embedded in routine visits. The 5 Rs (Relevance, Risk, Rewards, Roadblocks, Repetition) enhance retention.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Anticipatory guidance and health promotion** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
