# PTSD in adolescents

---

## Definition

Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) in adolescents follows exposure to actual/threatened death, serious injury, or sexual violence (directly, witnessed, or indirectly via family). Symptoms cluster in 4 groups: intrusion (flashbacks, nightmares), avoidance (of trauma reminders), negative alterations in cognition/mood (guilt, shame, dissociation), and hyperarousal (sleep, concentration, anger). Onset may be acute (<3 months) or delayed (>6 months). Prevalence 1-5% in adolescents; common after ACEs, abuse, accidents, war. Management: trauma-focused CBT (TF-CBT), EMDR for older adolescents, SSRIs (sertraline, fluoxetine); avoid debriefing immediately post-trauma. Family involvement essential. Prognosis: 50% recover within 1 year with treatment.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for PTSD in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
