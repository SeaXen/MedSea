# Respiratory Alkalosis

> [!info]
> **Respiratory Alkalosis = Primary ↓ pCO₂ (<4.5 kPa / 35 mmHg) → pH >7.45.** **Hyperventilation** → CO₂ Washout. **Acute vs Chronic Compensation** Differs. **Common in ICU** (Sepsis, Pain, Anxiety, Mechanical Ventilation).

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:

- [ ] Identify causes: anxiety, pain, sepsis, hepatic encephalopathy, salicylate, pregnancy, mechanical ventilation

## 2. Definitions & Diagnostic Criteria

| Parameter | Normal | Respiratory Alkalosis |
|-----------|--------|----------------------|
| **pH** | 7.35-7.45 | **>7.45** |
| **pCO₂** | 4.5-6.0 kPa (35-45 mmHg) | **<4.5 kPa (35 mmHg)** |
| **HCO₃⁻** | 22-26 mmol/L | **Normal (Acute) / Decreased (Chronic)** |
| **pH** | 7.35-7.45 | **>7.45** |

---

## 2. Acute vs Chronic — Compensation Rules

| Feature | **Acute Respiratory Alkalosis** | **Chronic Respiratory Alkalosis** |
|---------|--------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Onset** | Minutes-Hours | Days-Weeks |
| **Compensation** | **HCO₃⁻ ↓ 2 mmol/L per 10 mmHg pCO₂ ↓** | **HCO₃⁻ ↓ 5 mmol/L per 10 mmHg pCO₂ ↓** |
| **pH Change per 10 mmHg pCO₂ ↓** | **↑ 0.08** | **↑ 0.03** |
| **HCO₃⁻ at pCO₂ 20 mmHg** | 20-22 mmol/L | **12-14 mmol/L** |
| **Clinical Context** | Anxiety, Pain, Sepsis (Early), Salicylates, Mechanical Ventilation | **Pregnancy**, High Altitude, Chronic Liver Disease, Chronic Hypoxia |

---

## 2. Aetiology

| Category | Causes |
|----------|--------|
| **Psychogenic / Behavioural** | **Anxiety**, Panic Disorder, Hysteria, Hyperventilation Syndrome |
| **Pain / Trauma** | Severe Pain, Post-Operative, Fractures |
| **CNS** | **Stroke** (Brainstem), **Meningitis**, **Encephalitis**, Tumour, Subarachnoid Haemorrhage |
| **Pulmonary** | **PE** (Early), Pneumonia, Interstitial Lung Disease, Pneumothorax, Asthma |
| **Sepsis / SIRS** | **Early Sepsis** (Cytokine-Mediated Hyperventilation), ARDS (Early) |
| **Hepatic** | **Hepatic Encephalopathy** (Portosystemic Shunting → Ammonia → Respiratory Centre Stimulation) |
| **Drugs / Toxins** | **Salicylates** (Aspirin Overdose — **Mixed: Resp Alk + Met Acidosis**), Progesterone, Caffeine, Theophylline, Naloxone |
| **Pregnancy** | **Progesterone** → ↑ Respiratory Drive (Normal in 2nd/3rd Trimester) |
| **Mechanical Ventilation** | Excessive Rate/Tidal Volume, Inappropriate Settings |
| **Endocrine** | Thyrotoxicosis, Diabetic Ketoacidosis (Early) |

---

## 2. Acute vs Chronic — Compensation Rules

| Type | Expected HCO₃⁻ Change per 10 mmHg pCO₂ ↓ | Expected pH Change per 10 mmHg pCO₂ ↓ |
|----------------|-------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Acute** | **↓ 2 mmol/L** | **↑ 0.08** |
| **Chronic** | **↓ 5 mmol/L** | **↑ 0.03** |

### Expected HCO₃⁻ at Various pCO₂ Levels
| pCO₂ (mmHg) | Expected HCO₃⁻ (Acute) | Expected HCO₃⁻ (Chronic) |
|-------------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| 30 | 20-22 | 16-18 |
| 25 | 18-20 | 12-14 |
| 20 | 16-18 | 8-10 |

> **If Actual HCO₃⁻ < Expected for Acute** → **Concurrent Metabolic Acidosis**  
> **If Actual HCO₃⁻ > Expected for Chronic** → **Concurrent Metabolic Alkalosis**

## 3. Clinical Presentation

| Feature | Acute | Chronic |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Symptoms** | **Perioral Paraesthesia**, Carpopedal Spasm (Tetany), Dizziness, Palpitations, Anxiety | **Often Asymptomatic** (Compensated); Chronic Dyspnoea (If Hepatic/Pulmonary) |
| **Signs** | **Trousseau's/Chvostek's Signs** (Hypocalcaemia from Alkalosis), Tetany | Often Subtle (Adapted); Underlying Disease Signs |
| **ABG** | **pH >7.45**, **pCO₂ <4.5 kPa (35 mmHg)**, HCO₃⁻ Normal/Slightly ↓ | **pH >7.45**, **pCO₂ <4.5 kPa**, **HCO₃⁻ Decreased** |
| **Neurological** | Confusion, Lightheadedness, Syncope (Cerebral Vasoconstriction) | Subtle Cognitive Changes (If Hepatic Encephalopathy) |

---

## 3. Differential Diagnosis of Hypocapnia

| Condition | pCO₂ | HCO₃⁻ | Key Differentiator |
|-----------|------|-------|-------------------|
| **Acute Respiratory Alkalosis** | <4.5 kPa | Normal/Slightly ↓ | Anxiety, Pain, Sepsis, Salicylates |
| **Chronic Respiratory Alkalosis** | <4.5 kPa | **Markedly Decreased** | Pregnancy, Liver Disease, High Altitude |
| **Compensated Metabolic Acidosis** | <4.5 kPa | **Low** (Primary ↓ HCO₃⁻) | Primary Low HCO₃⁻ (Winter's Formula) |
| **Mixed: Resp Alk + Met Acidosis** | <4.5 kPa | Low | **Salicylates**, Sepsis, PE |
| **Mixed: Resp Alk + Met Alkalosis** | <4.5 kPa | High | Rare (e.g., Vomiting + Hyperventilation) |

## 3. Key Causes — Clinical Pearls

### Salicylate Toxicity (Classic Mixed Disorder)
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Pattern** | **Respiratory Alkalosis + Metabolic Acidosis** (Mixed) |
| **Mechanism** | Direct Respiratory Centre Stimulation + Uncouples Oxidative Phosphorylation → Lactic Acidosis |
| **Diagnosis** | Serum Salicylate Level; ABG: Mixed Resp Alk + Met Acidosis; Ferric Chloride Test (Urine) |
| **Management** | **IV Fluids**, **Alkalinisation** (NaHCO₃ → ↑ Renal Excretion), **Dialysis** (Severe >700 mg/L, Altered Mental Status, Renal Failure) |

### Hepatic Encephalopathy
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Mechanism** | Ammonia → Cerebral Oedema → Respiratory Centre Stimulation |
| **ABG** | **Chronic Respiratory Alkalosis** (Low pCO₂, Low HCO₃⁻) |
| **Other Features** | Asterixis, Confusion, Fetor Hepaticus, Ascites |

### Pregnancy
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Mechanism** | Progesterone → ↑ Respiratory Drive |
| **ABG** | **Compensated Chronic Respiratory Alkalosis** (pH ~7.45-7.50, pCO₂ ~27-32 mmHg, HCO₃⁻ ~18-22) |
| **Normal in 2nd/3rd Trimester** | Not Pathological |

### Mechanical Ventilation
| Scenario | Management |
|----------|------------|
| **Iatrogenic** | Reduce Respiratory Rate / Tidal Volume / Minute Ventilation |
| **Permissive Hypercapnia** | Target pH >7.20 (ARDS) / Accept pCO₂ 50-60 mmHg |

## 3. Management

### Primary Principle
| Step | Action |
|------|---------|
| **1. Treat Underlying Cause** | Anxiety (Rebreathing/Anxiolytics), Pain (Analgesia), Sepsis (Antibiotics), Salicylates (Alkalinisation/Haemodialysis), Hepatic Encephalopathy (Lactulose/Rifaximin), Adjust Ventilator |
| **2. Symptomatic Relief** | **Rebreathing (Paper Bag)** for Anxiety-Induced; Calcium Gluconate for Tetany |
| **3. Correct Hypocalcaemia** | **IV Calcium Gluconate 10% 10mL** if Tetany/Seizures (Alkalosis → ↓ Ionised Ca²⁺) |
| **4. Ventilator Adjustment** | Reduce Rate / Tidal Volume / Minute Volume; Allow Permissive Hypercapnia if Tolerated |

---

## 3. Complications & Monitoring

| Complication | Mechanism | Monitoring |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| **Cerebral Vasoconstriction** | Hypocapnia → ↓ CBF → Dizziness, Syncope, Seizures | Neurological Assessment, Continuous EEG if Seizures |
| **Hypocalcaemia** | Alkalosis → ↑ Protein Binding → ↓ Ionised Ca²⁺ → Tetany | Ionised Ca²⁺ q4-6h; Replace if Symptomatic |
| **Hypokalaemia** | Alkalosis → K⁺ Shift Into Cells | K⁺ q4-6h; Replace if <3.0 |
| **Left Shift ODC** | Alkalosis → ↑ Hb-O₂ Affinity → ↓ Tissue O₂ Delivery | Monitor Tissue Perfusion, Lactate |
| **Paradoxical CNS Acidosis** | Rapid Correction of Chronic Alk → CSF pCO₂ ↑ Faster Than Blood | Slow Weaning of Ventilator |
