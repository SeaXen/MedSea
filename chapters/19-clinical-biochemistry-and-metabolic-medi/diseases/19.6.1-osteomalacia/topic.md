# Osteomalacia (Adults) & Rickets (Children)

> [!info]
> **Osteomalacia / Rickets = Defective Mineralisation of Osteoid** (Vitamin D / Calcium / Phosphate Deficiency). **Adults = Osteomalacia; Children = Rickets** (Growth Plate Involvement). **Biochemical Hallmark: ↑ ALP, ↓ Phosphate, ↓ 25-OH Vit D, ↑ PTH (2° HPT)**.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Differentiate osteomalacia (adult) from rickets (child) and osteoporosis
- [ ] Identify causes: Vitamin D deficiency, calcium/phosphate deficiency, renal disease, drug-induced
- [ ] Recognise clinical features: bone pain, proximal myopathy, fractures, deformities (rickets)
- [ ] Apply biochemical diagnosis: ↓ PO₄, ↑ ALP, ↓ 25-OH D, ↑ PTH
- [ ] Apply management: Vitamin D replacement (loading + maintenance), calcium, phosphate if needed

---

## 2. Pathophysiology

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Core Defect** | **Defective Mineralisation** of Osteoid (Unmineralised Osteoid Accumulates) |
| **Adults** | **Osteomalacia** (Closed Growth Plates) |
| **Children** | **Rickets** (Open Growth Plates → Deformities) |
| **Key Mediator** | **Vitamin D** (↓ 1,25-(OH)₂D → ↓ Intestinal Ca²⁺/PO₄ Absorption) → ↓ Ca²⁺×PO₄ Product |

---

## 2. Aetiology — Causes of Osteomalacia / Rickets

### Vitamin D Deficiency (Most Common)
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Low Sunlight Exposure** | Housebound, Institutionalised, Dark Skin, High Latitude, Veiled Clothing |
| **Malabsorption** | Coeliac, Crohn's, Cystic Fibrosis, Bariatric Surgery, Short Bowel, Cholestatic Liver Disease |
| **Renal Disease** | CKD → ↓ 1α-Hydroxylase → ↓ 1,25-(OH)₂D |
| **Hepatic Failure** | ↓ 25-Hydroxylation |
| **Drug-Induced** | **Enzyme Inducers**: Phenytoin, Carbamazepine, Rifampicin, Isoniazid, Antiretrovirals (EFV, NVP) |

### Calcium / Phosphate Deficiency
| Deficiency | Mechanism |
|------------|-----------|
| **Calcium Deficiency** | Low Intake, Malabsorption, Hypoparathyroidism |
| **Phosphate Deficiency** | **Fanconi Syndrome**, **X-Linked Hypophosphataemic Rickets (XLH)**, **Oncogenic Osteomalacia**, **Tumour Lysis**, **Refeeding Syndrome** |

### Renal Disease
| Type | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **CKD** | ↓ 1α-Hydroxylase → ↓ 1,25-(OH)₂D; Hyperphosphataemia; ↓ Ca²⁺ Absorption |
| **Renal Tubular Disorders** | **Fanconi Syndrome** (Proximal Tubular Dysfunction → PO₄ Wasting), **XLH** (PHEX Mutation → ↑ FGF23), **Oncogenic Osteomalacia** (FGF23-Secreting Tumour) |

## 2. Clinical Features

### Adults — Osteomalacia
| Feature | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Bone Pain** | **Proximal Myopathy** (Thighs, Shoulders), Diffuse Bone Pain (Ribs, Spine, Pelvis) |
| **Fractures** | Looser's Zones (Pseudofractures), Pathological Fractures |
| **Muscular** | Proximal Myopathy (Waddling Gait, Difficulty Rising), Muscle Weakness |
| **Biochemical** | **Low Ca²⁺/Normal, ↓ Phosphate, ↑ ALP, ↓ 25-OH D, ↑ PTH** |

### Children — Rickets
| Feature | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Skeletal** | **Craniotabes** (Soft Skull), **Harrison's Groove** (Costochondral Junction), **Rachitic Rosary**, **Wrist/Ankle Widening**, Bow Legs (Genu Varum), Knock Knees (Genu Valgum) |
| **Growth** | Failure to Thrive, Delayed Walking, Stunted Growth |
| **Muscular** | Hypotonia, Proximal Myopathy, Delayed Motor Milestones |
| **Dental** | Delayed Eruption, Enamel Hypoplasia, Caries |
| **Biochemical** | **Low Ca²⁺, ↓ Phosphate, ↑ ALP, ↓ 25-OH D, ↑ PTH** |

## 2. Biochemical Diagnosis

| Test | Normal | Osteomalacia / Rickets |
|--------|--------|------------------------|
| **Serum Calcium (Corrected)** | 2.15-2.55 mmol/L | **Low / Normal** |
| **Ionised Calcium** | 1.15-1.30 mmol/L | Low / Normal |
| **Serum Phosphate** | 0.8-1.5 mmol/L | **Low** (<0.8) |
| **ALP** | 30-130 U/L | **Markedly Elevated** (Disproportionate to Other LFTs) |
| **PTH** | 1.6-6.9 pmol/L | **Elevated** (2° HPT) |
| **25-OH Vitamin D** | >50 nmol/L (20 ng/mL) | **Low** (<25 nmol/L = Deficiency) |
| **1,25-(OH)₂D** | 75-220 pmol/L | Low / Normal |
| **FGF23** | <100 RU/mL | **High** (If XLH/Oncogenic) |
| **ALP Isoenzymes** | Liver/Bone | **Bone ALP ↑** |

---

## 2. Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Ca²⁺ | PO₄ | ALP | PTH | 25-OH D | Key Differentiator |
|---------|------|-----|-----|-----|---------|-------------------|
| **Osteomalacia** | Low/Normal | **Low** | **High** | **High** | **Low** | ↓ 25-OH D, ↑ PTH |
| **Osteoporosis** | Normal | Normal | Normal/↑ | Normal | Normal | T-Score ≤-2.5, Normal PO₄/ALP |
| **Renal Osteodystrophy** | Low/Normal | **High** | High/↑ | **Very High** | Low/Normal | High Creatinine, ↑ FGF23 |
| **Hypophosphataemic Rickets (XLH)** | Normal | **Low** | High | Normal | Normal | **FGF23 ↑**, Genetic (PHEX) |
| **Oncogenic Osteomalacia** | Normal | **Low** | High | Normal | Normal | **FGF23 ↑**, Tumour (Mesenchymal) |
| **Hypophosphataemia (Other)** | Normal | **Low** | Normal/↑ | Normal/↑ | Normal | Cause-Specific (Fanconi, Antacids, Refeeding) |

## 3. Management

### Vitamin D Replacement (Core Therapy)
| Regimen | Dose | Duration |
|---------|------|----------|
| **Loading (Severe Deficiency <12 ng/mL)** | **Cholecalciferol 50,000 IU Weekly × 6-8 Weeks** | Until 25-OH D >30 ng/mL |
| **Loading (Moderate Deficiency 12-20 ng/mL)** | 50,000 IU Weekly × 4-6 Weeks | Until 25-OH D >30 ng/mL |
| **Maintenance** | **Cholecalciferol 800-2000 IU/day** (or 50,000 IU Monthly) | Lifelong if Risk Factors Persist |

### Calcium Supplementation
| Preparation | Elemental Ca²⁺ | Dose |
|-------------|----------------|------|
| **Calcium Carbonate** | 40% (400mg/tablet) | 1-2g TDS with Meals |
| **Calcium Citrate** | 21% | 1-2g TDS (Better if Achlorhydria) |
| **Target** | **1.5-2g/day Elemental** | Divided Doses |

### Phosphate Replacement (If Hypophosphataemic)
| Preparation | Dose |
|-------------|------|
| **Phosphate Sandoz** | 1-2 Tablets TDS (~16 mmol PO₄ each) |
| **Neutraphos** | 1-2 Packets QID (~8 mmol PO₄ each) |
| **IV Phosphate** (Severe <0.3) | **15-30 mmol over 6-12h** (Monitor Ca²⁺) |

### Paediatric Dosing (Rickets)
| Age | Deficiency Treatment | Maintenance |
|-----|----------------------|-------------|
| **0-1 Year** | 2,000 IU/day × 6 Weeks → 400-1,000 IU/day | 400-1,000 IU/day |
| **1-18 Years** | 50,000 IU Weekly × 6 Weeks → 600-1,000 IU/day | 600-1,000 IU/day |

## 3. Special Causes & Management

### Oncogenic Osteomalacia (Tumour-Induced)
| Feature | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Mechanism** | **FGF23-Secreting Tumour** → Renal Phosphate Wasting + ↓ 1,25-(OH)₂D |
| **Tumour Type** | **Mesenchymal** (Haemangiopericytoma, Fibrous Tumours, Osteoblastoma) |
| **Biochemical** | **↓ PO₄, ↑ FGF23, FEP >5%, Normal PTH, Low 1,25-(OH)₂D** |
| **Management** | **Tumour Resection (Curative)**; Pre-Op Phosphate + Calcitriol |

### X-Linked Hypophosphataemic Rickets (XLH)
| Feature | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Gene** | **PHEX** (X-Linked Dominant) |
| **Mechanism** | ↓ PHEX → ↑ FGF23 → Renal Phosphate Wasting + ↓ 1,25-(OH)₂D |
| **Clinical** | Rickets (Childhood), Osteomalacia (Adult), Short Stature, Dental Abscesses |
| **Management** | **Burosumab** (Anti-FGF23 mAb) **0.8mg/kg SC q2wk (Child) / 1mg/kg q4wk (Adult)**; Phosphate + Calcitriol (Traditional) |

### Renal Osteodystrophy (See Separate Note)

## 8. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Osteomalacia vs Osteoporosis** | Osteomalacia = ↓ PO₄, ↑ ALP, ↑ PTH (Mineralisation Defect); Osteoporosis = Normal PO₄/ALP/PTH (Mass Defect) |
| **Rickets vs Osteomalacia** | Rickets = Children (Open Growth Plates → Deformities); Osteomalacia = Adults |
| **Osteomalacia vs Renal Osteodystrophy** | Renal = High PO₄, High FGF23, High Creatinine; Osteomalacia = Low PO₄, Low Creatinine |
| **XLH vs Oncogenic** | XLH = Genetic (PHEX); Oncogenic = Acquired Tumour (FGF23) — Both High FGF23 |
| **Looser's Zones** | Pseudofractures — **Pathognomonic for Osteomalacia** |
| **Rachitic Rosary** | Costochondral Widening (Rickets) |
| **FGF23** | Key Mediator: XLH, Oncogenic, ADHR → ↑ FGF23 → ↓ Renal PO₄ Reabsorption |
| **Vitamin D Loading** | 50,000 IU Weekly × 6-8 Weeks (Severe Def <12 ng/mL) |
| **Maintenance** | 800-2000 IU/day Cholecalciferol (Not Calcitriol Unless CKD) |
