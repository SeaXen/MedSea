# Paget Disease of Bone (Osteitis Deformans)

> [!info]
> **Paget Disease of Bone = Focal Disorder of Bone Remodelling** with Excessive Osteoclastic Resorption → Disorganised Osteoblastic Formation. **Age >55; Male > Female; UK/Northern Europe Highest Prevalence.** **3 Phases: Lytic → Mixed → Sclerotic.** **ALP = Disease Activity Marker.** **Bisphosphonates 1st Line (Zoledronic Acid).**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise clinical presentation: bone pain, deformity, deafness, complications
- [ ] Apply radiological and biochemical diagnosis (ALP, X-Ray, Bone Scan)
- [ ] Stage disease: Lytic → Mixed → Sclerotic (Burnt-Out)
- [ ] Apply treatment: Zoledronic Acid 1st Line; Indications for treatment
- [ ] Recognise complications: OA, Fractures, Nerve Compression, High-Output HF, Osteosarcoma (<1%)

---

## 2. Epidemiology & Pathophysiology

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Incidence** | Declining; ~1-2% of >55yr in UK |
| **Age** | **>55 Years** (Rare <40) |
| **Sex** | **Male > Female** (1.5:1) |
| **Geography** | **UK/Northern Europe** Highest; African/Asian Rare |
| **Genetics** | **SQSTM1 Mutation** (~30% Familial); **Viral** (Paramyxovirus Hypothesis) |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Excessive Osteoclastic Resorption** → Compensatory Disorganised Osteoblastic Formation → **Woven Bone** (Weak, Deformed) |

### Three Phases of Paget Disease
| Phase | Histology | ALP | Clinical |
|-------|-----------|-----|----------|
| **1. Lytic (Osteolytic)** | **Osteoclasts > Osteoblasts** → Osteolysis | **↑↑↑** (Early Rise) | Often Asymptomatic; Bone Pain if Active |
| **2. Mixed (Osteolytic-Osteoblastic)** | **Osteoclasts + Osteoblasts** Active | **↑↑** (Peak) | **Bone Pain, Deformity, Widening** |
| **3. Sclerotic (Osteoblastic/Burnt-Out)** | **Osteoblasts > Osteoclasts** → Dense Sclerotic Bone | **↓** (Towards Normal) | **Deformity, Fractures, Nerve Compression** |

---

## 2. Clinical Presentation

| Feature | Frequency | Details |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Asymptomatic** | **~70%** | Incidental ALP ↑ or X-Ray Finding |
| **Bone Pain** | **30-40%** | Deep, Aching, Night Pain (Active Lytic Phase) |
| **Deformity** | **15-20%** | Bowing Tibia/Femur, Skull Enlargement (Frontal Bossing) |
| **Fractures** | **5-10%** | Insufficiency Fractures (Long Bones, Vertebrae) |
| **Osteoarthritis** | **20-30%** | Adjacent Joints (Hip, Knee) — Mechanical Stress |
| **Nerve Compression** | **5-10%** | **CN VIII Deafness** (Skull Base), Spinal Stenosis, CN VII Palsy |
| **High-Output Heart Failure** | **<1%** (Severe Polyostotic) | Arteriovenous Shunting in Vascular Pagetic Bone |
| **Osteosarcoma** | **<1%** | Malignant Transformation (Late Complication) |

---

## 2. Biochemical Markers

| Marker | Finding | Significance |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **ALP (Total)** | **Markedly ↑** (Often 3-10x ULN) | **Best Activity Marker**; Disproportionate vs Other LFTs |
| **Bone-Specific ALP (BALP)** | ↑↑ | More Specific for Bone Turnover |
| **Calcium** | Normal | Usually Normal (Unless Immobilisation/Hypercalcaemia) |
| **Phosphate** | Normal | Normal |
| **PTH** | Normal | Normal |
| **25-OH Vit D** | Normal/Low | Normal (Unless Concomitant Deficiency) |
| **Urinary Hydroxyproline / NTX / CTX** | ↑↑ | Bone Resorption Markers (If Available) |

> **ALP** is **Disproportionately Elevated** vs Other LFTs (AST, ALT, GGT, Bilirubin Normal).

---

## 2. Imaging

| Modality | Findings |
|---------|----------|
| **Plain X-Ray** | **Lytic**: Osteoporosis Circumscripta (Skull), "Flame-Shaped" Lytic Advancing Edge (Long Bones)  
**Mixed**: Coarse Trabeculae, Cortical Thickening, Bone Expansion  
**Sclerotic**: Dense Sclerosis, "Cotton Wool" Appearance (Skull), Bony Expansion |
| **Bone Scan (Tc-99m MDP)** | **Intense, Increased Uptake** (Polyostotic, Intense "Hot Spots"); **Best for Extent Assessment** |
| **MRI** | Soft Tissue Extension, Spinal Canal Stenosis, Nerve Compression |
| **CT** | Cortical Thickening, Bone Expansion, Spinal Stenosis Detail |

---

## 2. Diagnosis

### Diagnostic Criteria (Clinical + Biochemical + Radiological)
| Criterion | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Clinical** | Bone Pain, Deformity, Fracture, Deafness, Neurological Signs |
| **Biochemical** | **ALP ↑↑ (Disproportionate)**; Normal Ca²⁺, PO₄, PTH |
| **Radiological** | Lytic → Mixed → Sclerotic Changes on X-Ray/Bone Scan |

> **Bone Scan** = Best for **Extent** (Polyostotic vs Monostotic); **X-Ray** = Best for **Phase** (Lytic/Mixed/Sclerotic).

---

## 3. Indications for Treatment

| Indication | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Symptomatic Bone Pain** | Deep, Aching, Night Pain (Active Disease) |
| **Impending Fracture** | Impending Pathological Fracture (Long Bones) |
| **Neurological Complications** | Nerve Compression (CN VIII, Spinal Cord), Cranial Nerve Palsies |
| **High-Output Heart Failure** | Severe Polyostotic Disease |
| **Pre-Operative** | Surgery on Pagetic Bone (Joint Replacement, Fixation) |
| **Serum ALP >2x ULN** | Asymptomatic but High Turnover (Prevent Complications) |

---

## 3. Management

### Bisphosphonates (1st Line)
| Agent | Dose | Route | Duration | Key Points |
|-------|------|-------|----------|------------|
| **Zoledronic Acid** | **5mg IV Single Infusion** | **1st Line** (Preferred) | **Single Dose** (Long Duration) | **Most Potent**; Monitor Renal Function (eGFR ≥35) |
| **Risedronate** | **30mg OD × 2 Months** | Oral Alternative | 2-Month Course | Less Potent; Daily Upright 30min |
| **Pamidronate** | 60-90mg IV (Infusion) | If Zoledronic Unavailable | Multiple Infusions | Outdated by Zoledronic |

### Monitoring Response
| Parameter | Frequency | Target |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| **ALP** | q3-6 Months | **Normalisation** (Remission) |
| **Bone Scan** | 1-2 Years | Reduced Uptake |
| **Symptoms** | Each Visit | Pain Relief, Functional Improvement |

### Relapse / Retreatment
| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **ALP Rise >50% Above Nadir** | **Retreat with Zoledronic Acid 5mg IV** |
| **Symptomatic Relapse** | Retreat |
| **Maximum Courses** | Not Defined; Repeated Courses Safe |

---

## 3. Complications & Management

| Complication | Management |
|------------|------------|
| **Osteoarthritis** | Joint Replacement (Hip/Knee Common); Pre-Op Bisphosphonate |
| **Pathological Fracture** | Internal Fixation (IM Nailing); Post-Op Bisphosphonate |
| **Nerve Compression (CN VIII)** | **Hearing Aids** (1st Line); **Cochlear Implant** (Severe); Surgery Rare |
| **Spinal Stenosis / Cord Compression** | **Decompressive Laminectomy** ± Instrumentation; Pre-Op Bisphosphonate |
| **High-Output Heart Failure** | Diuretics, Afterload Reduction, **Bisphosphonate** (Reduces Vascular Shunt) |
| **Osteosarcoma** | **Multimodal**: Surgery (Amputation/Limb-Salvage) + Chemo (Cisplatin/Doxorubicin) |

## 8. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Paget vs Osteoporosis** | Paget = Focal, ↑ ALP, Deformity; Osteoporosis = Generalised, Normal ALP, Fracture Risk |
| **Paget vs Osteomalacia** | Paget = Normal Ca/PO₄/PTH, ↑ ALP; Osteomalacia = ↓ PO₄, ↑ ALP, ↑ PTH |
| **Lytic vs Sclerotic Phase** | Lytic = ↑↑ ALP, Expanding Lytic Edge; Sclerotic = ↓ ALP, Dense Bone |
| **ALP in Paget** | **Disproportionate ↑ vs Other LFTs** (AST/ALT/GGT Normal) |
| **Bone Scan vs X-Ray** | Bone Scan = Extent (Polyostotic); X-Ray = Phase (Lytic/Mixed/Sclerotic) |
| **Zoledronic vs Risedronate** | Zoledronic = Single IV Dose (Preferred); Risedronate = 30mg OD × 2mo (Oral) |
| **Osteosarcoma Risk** | <1% (Late); Multimodal (Surgery + Chemo) |
| **CN VIII Deafness** | Common (Skull Base); Hearing Aids / Cochlear Implant; Surgery Rare |
| **High-Output HF** | Severe Polyostotic → AV Shunting; Treat with Bisphosphonates + HF Rx |
| **Zoledronic Acid Renal** | eGFR ≥35 Required; Avoid if <35 |
