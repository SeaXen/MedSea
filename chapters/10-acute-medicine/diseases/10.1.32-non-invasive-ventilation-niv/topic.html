ACUTE MEDICINE AND CRITICAL ILLNESS
Non-Invasive Ventilation (NIV)
1 Learning Objectives
  • Know the indications and contraindications for NIV
  • Choose between CPAP and BiPAP
  • Monitor for NIV failure
  • Know when to escalate to intubation
2 Indications

Strong evidence

  • COPD exacerbation with respiratory acidosis (pH <7.35)
  • Cardiogenic pulmonary oedema
  • Immunocompromised patients with hypoxaemic respiratory failure

Other uses

  • Obesity hypoventilation syndrome
  • Weaning from invasive ventilation
3 CPAP vs BiPAP
ModeIndicationTypical Settings
-------------------------------------------------------------------
CPAPPulmonary oedema, atelectasis5–15 cmH₂O
BiPAPCOPD, hypercapnic failureIPAP 10–20, EPAP 4–8
4 Contraindications
  • Cardiac/respiratory arrest
  • GCS <8 or inability to protect airway
  • Vomiting / high aspiration risk
  • Facial trauma or burns
  • Untreated pneumothorax
5 Monitoring & Failure Criteria
  • Reassess at 1–2 hours
  • Worsening pH, rising PaCO₂, persistent hypoxia
  • Increasing respiratory rate or distress
  • Haemodynamic instability
6 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
  • Start NIV early in COPD with acidosis — do not wait for severe acidosis
  • CPAP is first choice in acute cardiogenic pulmonary oedema
  • Always have a plan for intubation if NIV fails

8 One-Page Revision Summary

NIV = CPAP or BiPAP Indicated in COPD acidosis + cardiogenic oedema Contraindicated if low GCS or aspiration risk Monitor closely — intubate if failing

9 Must Know / Should Know / Nice to Know
Must Know
  • Indications: COPD exacerbation (pH<7.35), cardiogenic pulmonary oedema, immunocompromised
  • CPAP for oedema/atelectasis (5–15 cmH₂O); BiPAP for COPD/hypercapnia (IPAP 10–20, EPAP 4–8)
  • Contraindications: arrest, GCS<8, aspiration risk, facial trauma, untreated pneumothorax
  • Monitor at 1–2h: worsening pH, rising PaCO₂, exhaustion → intubate
Should Know
  • Early NIV in COPD — don't wait for severe acidosis
  • HFNO (high-flow nasal oxygen) as alternative in some hypoxaemic failure
  • Weaning: SIMV → PS → CPAP trial
Nice to Know
  • NIV in do-not-intubate patients (palliative)
  • Home NIV for OHS/COPD
  • Helmet NIV vs mask
