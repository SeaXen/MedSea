> [!important]
> Cardiogenic shock = cardiac pump failure leading to inadequate cardiac output despite adequate preload. Mortality remains high (40–50%).

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Recognise cardiogenic shock clinically and haemodynamically
- Differentiate from other shock types
- Understand initial management (avoid excess fluid)
- Know when to use inotropes and mechanical support

## 2. Definition
Cardiac index <2.2 L/min/m² + PCWP >18 mmHg + signs of hypoperfusion (or SBP <90 mmHg with end-organ dysfunction).

## 3. Common Causes
- Acute MI (most common) — especially anterior or right ventricular
- Acute decompensated heart failure
- Myocarditis, cardiomyopathy, arrhythmia, valve rupture

## 4. Clinical Features
- Cool, clammy skin
- Raised JVP, S3 gallop, pulmonary oedema
- Hypotension + narrow pulse pressure
- Signs of congestion (crackles, oedema)

## 5. Key Investigations
- ECG, troponin, echo (bedside crucial)
- CXR (pulmonary oedema)
- ABG + lactate

## 6. Management
- **Avoid excess fluid** — cautious 250 mL boluses only if clear hypovolaemia
- **Inotropes**: Dobutamine or milrinone (if low output)
- **Vasopressors**: Noradrenaline if hypotensive
- **Revascularisation**: Urgent PCI for STEMI
- **Mechanical support**: IABP, Impella, ECMO in refractory cases

## 7. Red Flags
- Refractory hypotension
- Worsening pulmonary oedema
- Right ventricular infarction (hypotension + clear lungs + raised JVP)

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- Cardiogenic shock can occur with **normal blood pressure** initially
- Bedside echo is the most useful investigation
- Do not give large fluid boluses — it worsens pulmonary oedema

## 11. Must Know / Should Know / Nice to Know
### Must Know
- Definition: CI <2.2 + PCWP >18 + hypoperfusion
- Clinical: cool/clammy, raised JVP, S3, pulmonary oedema, hypotension
- **AVOID large fluids** — cautious 250 mL boluses only
- First-line inotrope: Dobutamine; add Noradrenaline if hypotensive
- STEMI = urgent PCI
- RV infarction: hypotension + clear lungs + raised JVP

### Should Know
- Mechanical support: IABP, Impella, ECMO
- Milrinone alternative (PDE III inhibitor) — avoid if hypotensive
- Echocardiography crucial for diagnosis

### Nice to Know
- LVAD, transplant pathways
- Cardiogenic shock registries (CSWG)
- VA-ECMO vs VV-ECMO
