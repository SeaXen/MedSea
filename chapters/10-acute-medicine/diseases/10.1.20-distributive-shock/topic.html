ACUTE MEDICINE AND CRITICAL ILLNESS
Distributive Shock
1 Learning Objectives
  • Recognise the haemodynamic profile of distributive shock
  • Differentiate aetiologies: septic, anaphylactic, neurogenic, adrenal crisis
  • Apply vasopressor strategy appropriately
  • Identify and treat the underlying cause
2 Definition

Shock caused by widespread vasodilatation → reduced systemic vascular resistance (SVR) → relative hypovolaemia despite normal absolute volume.

3 Classification
TypeMechanismKey Feature
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
**Septic**Endotoxin + cytokine stormWarm peripheries, high CO
**Anaphylactic**Mast cell degranulationRapid onset, urticaria, angioedema
**Neurogenic**Loss of sympathetic toneBradycardia + warm peripheries
**Adrenal crisis**Cortisol deficiencyHypoglycaemia, hyponatraemia
4 Haemodynamic Profile
  • High cardiac output (initially)
  • Low SVR
  • Low MAP
  • Warm, bounding peripheries (until decompensation)
5 Management
  • Fluids: large-volume crystalloids initially
  • Vasopressors: Noradrenaline first-line (except neurogenic where phenylephrine may be used)
  • Treat cause: antibiotics, adrenaline, steroids, hormone replacement
6 Red Flags
  • Refractory hypotension on high-dose vasopressors
  • Deteriorating mental status
  • Lactate rising despite resuscitation
7 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
  • Neurogenic shock: bradycardia + hypotension + warm skin (differentiates from other shocks)
  • Adrenal crisis: consider in any unexplained hypotension + hyponatraemia + hyperkalaemia
  • Anaphylaxis needs adrenaline first — fluids and vasopressors are adjuncts

10 Must Know / Should Know / Nice to Know
Must Know
  • Definition and haemodynamic profile (high CO, low SVR)
  • Four subtypes: septic, anaphylactic, neurogenic, adrenal crisis
  • Noradrenaline first-line (except neurogenic)
  • Neurogenic = bradycardia + hypotension
Should Know
  • Adrenal crisis: hypotension + hyponatraemia + hyperkalaemia
  • Anaphylaxis: adrenaline IM first, fluids second
  • Vasopressin as catecholamine-sparing add-on
Nice to Know
  • Detailed receptor mechanisms
  • Angiotensin II for refractory vasoplegia
  • ECMO in refractory distributive shock

14 Answer Key with Explanations

MCQ 4: C — Adrenaline IM first and most important. MCQ 5: B — Bradycardia + hypotension (unopposed vagal tone). MCQ 6: B — Cortisol deficiency → aldosterone deficiency → Na loss, K retention. MCQ 7: C — High CO initially (compensatory). MCQ 8: C — Low SVR (vasodilatation). MCQ 9: B — Add-on to spare noradrenaline. MCQ 10: B — Large-volume crystalloids initially.

SBA 1: C — Spinal injury + bradycardia + hypotension + warm skin = neurogenic. SBA 2: A — Adrenal crisis = fluids + hydrocortisone 100mg IV immediate. SBA 5: C — Phenylephrine (pure α₁) preferred in neurogenic to avoid tachycardia. SBA 6: C — Distributive = warm peripheries (initially). SBA 7: C — Both steroid withdrawal and stress precipitate crisis. SBA 8: B — IV adrenaline only if IM failed / cardiovascular collapse.
