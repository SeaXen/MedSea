ACUTE MEDICINE AND CRITICAL ILLNESS
Coma and Altered Consciousness
1 Learning Objectives
  • Perform rapid assessment of the unconscious patient
  • Generate a focused differential diagnosis
  • Identify and treat reversible causes immediately
  • Know when to intubate and when to image
2 Immediate Assessment (ABCDE + Glucose)
  • Airway protection (intubate if GCS ≤8)
  • Breathing and oxygenation
  • Circulation and BP
  • Disability: AVPU/GCS, pupils, glucose, seizures
  • Exposure + look for clues (rash, needle marks, trauma, odour)
3 Common Reversible Causes (AEIOU TIPS)
  • Alcohol / Anoxia
  • Epilepsy / Electrolytes / Encephalopathy
  • Insulin (hypoglycaemia)
  • Overdose / Opiates
  • Uraemia
  • Trauma / Temperature (hypo/hyperthermia)
  • Infection / Intracranial (stroke, bleed, tumour)
  • Poisoning / Psychogenic
  • Sepsis / Shock
4 Key Investigations
  • Glucose, ABG, electrolytes, LFT, U&E, FBC, toxicology screen
  • CT head (if trauma, focal signs, or no obvious cause)
  • LP (after CT if meningitis/encephalitis suspected)
5 Red Flags
  • GCS falling
  • Unequal or fixed pupils
  • Signs of raised ICP or herniation
  • Status epilepticus
6 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
  • Always check blood glucose first — it is the most rapidly reversible cause
  • Intubate early if airway at risk (GCS ≤8)
  • CT head before LP in most cases
7 Common Viva Questions
  1. Outline your approach to the unconscious patient.
  2. List reversible causes of coma.
  3. When would you perform CT before LP?

9 Must Know / Should Know / Nice to Know
Must Know
  • ABCDE + Glucose first (GCS, pupils, glucose)
  • AEIOU TIPS mnemonic for reversible causes
  • Intubate if GCS ≤8 (airway protection)
  • CT head before LP (unless clear meningitis)
  • Hypoglycaemia = most rapidly reversible cause
Should Know
  • Focal signs → urgent CT
  • Meningitis: LP after CT, but don't delay antibiotics
  • Status epilepticus: non-convulsive possible (EEG)
Nice to Know
  • Glasgow Coma Scale components (E4, V5, M6)
  • Pupillary light reflex pathways
  • ICP monitoring indications
  • Neuroprognostication after cardiac arrest
