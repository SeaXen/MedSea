ACUTE MEDICINE AND CRITICAL ILLNESS
End-of-Life Decisions in Acute Medicine
1 Learning Objectives
  • Recognise when to shift from curative to palliative goals
  • Apply legal and ethical frameworks (Mental Capacity Act, best interests)
  • Conduct effective goals-of-care conversations
  • Prescribe anticipatory medications for symptom control
2 Triggers for End-of-Life Discussion
  • Cardiac arrest with prolonged resuscitation
  • Multi-organ failure unresponsive to support
  • Advanced dementia / frailty (CFS ≥7) with acute deterioration
  • Known terminal illness with acute complication
  • Patient or family expresses wish to avoid ICU
3 Legal / Ethical Framework (UK)
  • Mental Capacity Act 2005: Assume capacity unless proven otherwise
  • Best interests decision if lacks capacity (involve family, IMCA if no family)
  • DNACPR — does not mean "do not treat"; communicate clearly
  • Advance Decision to Refuse Treatment (ADRT) — legally binding if valid and applicable
  • ReSPECT process — recommended summary plan for emergency care and treatment
4 Communication Structure (REMAP / SPIKES)
  • Review clinical situation
  • Expectations (ask what patient/family understands)
  • Map out goals and values
  • Align treatment with goals
  • Plan (including DNACPR, ceiling of care)
5 Ceiling of Care Documentation
  • Ward level only / Ward + NEWS2 trigger / HDU / Full ICU
  • DNACPR status
  • Specific treatment limits (e.g. no intubation, no vasopressors, no RRT)
  • Review date / trigger for review

7 Red Flags
  • Making DNACPR decision without discussion (human rights violation)
  • Assuming "no ICU" = "no treatment"
  • Delaying symptom control
8 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
  • DNACPR is specific to CPR — does not limit other treatment
  • Capacity is decision-specific — assess for each decision
  • Best interests = balance benefits, burdens, wishes, beliefs
  • ReSPECT form travels with patient across settings

  • DNACPR does not mean "do not treat"
  • Family cannot demand treatment that is not in best interests

16 Flashcards
  • Q: DNACPR scope

A: Applies ONLY to CPR — not to antibiotics, fluids, intubation (unless specified)

  • Q: Capacity test (MCA)

A: Understand → Retain → Weigh → Communicate (decision-specific)

  • Q: Best interests factors

A: Past wishes, beliefs, values, clinical benefits vs burdens, consult family/IMCA

  • Q: First-line anticipatory drugs

A: Morphine (pain/dyspnoea), Midazolam (agitation/seizure), Glycopyrronium (secretions), Levomepromazine (nausea/agitation)

  • Q: ADRT validity

A: Written + signed + witnessed + applicable to current situation

  • Q: ReSPECT

A: Recommended Summary Plan for Emergency Care and Treatment — travels with patient
