> [!important]
> ICU admission is **not just for dying patients** — early admission of the right patients saves lives. Use physiological criteria, not just diagnosis.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Know Level 1, 2, and 3 critical care definitions
- Apply physiological admission criteria
- Understand factors that may alter admission decisions
- Communicate effectively with ICU team

## 2. Levels of Care (ICS / FICM)
| Level | Description | Monitoring / Support |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Level 0** | Ward care | Standard monitoring |
| **Level 1** | At risk / recently stepped down | Additional monitoring (e.g. NEWS2, NIV) |
| **Level 2** | Single organ support | HDU / Level 2 bed (CPAP, vasoactive drugs, renal) |
| **Level 3** | Multi-organ support / advanced respiratory | ICU ventilator, ≥2 organ support |

## 3. Absolute Indications for Level 2/3
- **Airway**: Intubated, high-risk extubation, need for advanced airway management
- **Breathing**: Invasive ventilation, NIV failure, severe hypoxaemia/hypercapnia
- **Circulation**: Vasoactive infusions (noradrenaline, dobutamine, etc.), cardiac output monitoring
- **Neurology**: GCS <8, sedation hold impossible, ICP monitoring, status epilepticus
- **Renal**: Renal replacement therapy (CVVH, IHD)
- **Other**: Complex multi-organ support, major surgery post-op

## 4. Relative / Soft Indications
- NEWS2 ≥7 or sustained ≥5
- High-risk surgery (planned post-op HDU/ICU)
- Sepsis with organ dysfunction not responding to ward management
- Severe electrolyte/metabolic disturbances needing close monitoring
- High-risk transfers / repatriation

## 5. Factors Modifying Admission
- **Ceiling of care** / DNACPR / patient wishes
- **Frailty** (CFS >5) — may not benefit from invasive support
- **Prognosis** — irreversible underlying disease
- **Resource availability** — surge capacity

## 6. Red Flags for Deterioration (Call ICU Early)
- Rising NEWS2
- Increasing oxygen requirement
- Falling GCS
- New hypotension / lactate rise
- Oliguria despite fluids

## 7. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- NEWS2 is a **screening tool**, not a mandate — clinical context matters
- Level 2 = single organ support; Level 3 = multi-organ / invasive ventilation
- Early discussion with ICU is always better than late referral
- Document ceiling of care discussions clearly

## 9. One-Page Revision Summary
**Level 1**: Ward + extra monitoring
**Level 2**: Single organ support (HDU)
**Level 3**: Multi-organ / invasive ventilation (ICU)
Admit for: vasoactive drugs, invasive vent, RRT, GCS <8
Use NEWS2 ≥7 as trigger, but clinical judgement > score

## 10. Must Know / Should Know / Nice to Know
### Must Know
- Level 0–3 definitions (ICS/FICM)
- Absolute indications: invasive vent, vasoactives, RRT, GCS <8
- Level 2 = single organ; Level 3 = multi-organ/invasive vent
- Early ICU discussion > late referral

### Should Know
- NEWS2 triggers (≥5 review, ≥7 urgent)
- Ceiling of care / DNACPR / frailty (CFS) modify decisions
- Soft indications: high-risk surgery, sepsis not responding

### Nice to Know
- Repatriation criteria
- Surge capacity planning
- Intermediate care / HDU step-down pathways

## 13. Flashcards
- Q: Level 0/1/2/3
  A: 0=ward, 1=at risk, 2=single organ (HDU), 3=multi-organ/invasive vent (ICU)
- Q: Absolute ICU indications
  A: Invasive vent, vasoactives, RRT, GCS<8, ICP monitor, multi-organ
- Q: NEWS2 triggers
  A: ≥5 review, ≥7 urgent/critical care outreach
- Q: Frailty (CFS) and ICU
  A: CFS >5 = may not benefit, guides ceiling of care
- Q: Ceiling of care
  A: Document clearly, discuss early
- Q: Early ICU referral
  A: Always better than late
