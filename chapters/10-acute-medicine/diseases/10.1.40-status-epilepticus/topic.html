ACUTE MEDICINE AND CRITICAL ILLNESS
Status Epilepticus
1 Learning Objectives
  • Define status epilepticus
  • Apply time-based treatment protocol
  • Know first-, second-, and third-line drugs
  • Manage complications (airway, rhabdomyolysis, acidosis)
2 Definition

Continuous seizure activity >5 minutes or recurrent seizures without recovery of consciousness.

3 Time-Based Management (UK Protocol)

0–5 min: ABCDE + glucose + benzodiazepine (lorazepam 4 mg IV or midazolam 10 mg buccal) 5–20 min: Repeat benzodiazepine once 20–40 min: Phenytoin 20 mg/kg IV or levetiracetam 60 mg/kg 40–60 min: Phenobarbital or general anaesthesia (propofol/thiopental) + ICU

4 Key Investigations
  • Glucose, electrolytes, ABG, lactate, CK
  • CT/MRI brain (after stabilisation)
  • EEG (if non-convulsive status suspected)
5 Red Flags / Complications
  • Airway compromise
  • Rhabdomyolysis, hyperkalaemia, acidosis
  • Cerebral oedema, permanent neuronal damage
6 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
  • Lorazepam is preferred IV benzodiazepine
  • Treat aggressively — neuronal damage begins after 20–30 minutes
  • Always check glucose first

9 Must Know / Should Know / Nice to Know
Must Know
  • Definition: continuous seizure >5 min OR recurrent without recovery
  • Time-based protocol: 0–5 (benzo), 5–20 (repeat benzo), 20–40 (phenytoin/levetiracetam), >40 (anaesthesia+ICU)
  • First-line: lorazepam 4mg IV (or midazolam 10mg buccal/IM)
  • Always check glucose FIRST
  • Intubate if GCS ≤8 or airway compromised
Should Know
  • Phenytoin 20 mg/kg IV (max 2g) OR levetiracetam 60 mg/kg (preferred — fewer side effects)
  • Third-line: phenobarbital or anaesthetic agents (propofol/thiopental/midazolam infusion)
  • Complications: rhabdomyolysis, hyperkalaemia, acidosis, aspiration pneumonia
  • Non-convulsive status: EEG diagnosis (subtle: eye deviation, automatisms)
Nice to Know
  • Refractory status: continuous EEG monitoring, ketamine, lacosamide, magnesium
  • Febrile status epilepticus in children
  • Newer agents: brivaracetam, perampanel
  • Long-term outcomes: cognitive, epilepsy risk
