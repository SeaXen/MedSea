## Definition

**Invasive mechanical ventilation** = positive pressure ventilation delivered via **endotracheal tube** (or tracheostomy).

## Absolute Indications

### 1. Cardiopulmonary arrest
- Intubate immediately
- **D**anger, **A**irway, **R**esponse, **S**end for help (DARS)
- **C**ompressions, **A**irway, **B**reathing (CAB)

### 2. Respiratory arrest / apnoea
- **Bag-mask ventilation** initially
- **Intubate** when feasible

### 3. Severe hypoxaemia
- **PaOâ‚‚ <60 mmHg (8 kPa)** on high FiOâ‚‚ (P/F <200)
- **P/F ratio <200** with bilateral infiltrates (ARDS criteria)
- **Severe pneumonia** with rapid deterioration
- **Massive PE** with shock
- **Pulmonary oedema** unresponsive to NIV

### 4. Severe hypercapnic acidosis
- **pH <7.20** with â†‘PaCOâ‚‚
- **NIV failure** (pH <7.20 after 1â€“2 h of NIV)
- **Severe asthma** with â†‘PaCOâ‚‚ + exhaustion
- **Severe AECOPD** with exhaustion

### 5. Respiratory muscle exhaustion
- **Paradoxical breathing** (abdominal paradox)
- **Tachypnoea** >30 (sustained)
- **Use of accessory muscles** with worsening
- **Drowsiness** (hypercapnic encephalopathy)
- **Silent chest** (severe asthma)

### 6. Airway protection
- **GCS â‰¤8** (cannot protect airway)
- **Severe bulbar dysfunction** (GBS, MG, stroke)
- **Massive haemoptysis** with airway compromise
- **Upper airway obstruction** (FB, angioedema, severe stridor)

- **pH <7.20** after 1â€“2 h of NIV
- **â†‘PaCOâ‚‚ + â†‘RR + GCS â†“** despite NIV
