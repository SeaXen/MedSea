# High-flow nasal oxygen (HFNC) basics

> [!important]
> **High-flow nasal cannula (HFNC)** delivers heated, humidified O₂ at flows of 20–60 L/min with adjustable FiO₂. It provides **physiological benefits** beyond conventional O₂ (dead space washout, mild PEEP, humidification) and is first-line in **acute hypoxaemic respiratory failure** (P/F 100–300).

Related: [[Oxygen Therapy and NIV]], [[Oxygen delivery devices]], [[ABG Interpretation]], [[Respiratory Failure]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: HFNC is **first-line in acute hypoxaemic respiratory failure (P/F 100–300)** and is also useful post-extubation, in immunocompromised patients, and for palliative dyspnoea. It is **not appropriate for hypercapnic failure** (use NIV instead).

## Learning Objectives
- Define HFNC and its mechanism
- List indications and contraindications
- Set up and titrate HFNC
- Recognise when HFNC is failing
- Distinguish from NIV/CPAP

## Definition

**HFNC** = device delivering heated (37°C) and humidified (100% RH) O₂ via nasal cannula at flows >20 L/min (up to 60 L/min) with adjustable FiO₂ (0.21–1.0).

## Mechanism / Physiological Effects

| Effect | Mechanism | Benefit |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Dead space washout** | High flow flushes nasopharyngeal CO₂ | ↓PaCO₂, ↓work of breathing |
| **Mild PEEP (3–5 cmH₂O)** | Continuous flow maintains airway patency | Recruits alveoli, ↓atelectasis |
| **Stable FiO₂** | High flow matches inspiratory demand | Predictable oxygenation |
| **Humidification** | Heated, 100% RH | ↓mucosal drying, ↑mucociliary clearance, comfort |
| **Reduced WOB** | ↓work of generating inspiratory flow | Less fatigue |

## Indications

| Indication | Notes |
|------------|-------|
| **Acute hypoxaemic respiratory failure** | P/F 100–300; first-line |
| **Pneumonia** (CAP, COVID-19) | Especially in immunocompromised |
| **Post-extubation** | Reduces reintubation |
| **Pre-oxygenation** | Before intubation |
| **Palliative care** | Dyspnoea in advanced disease |
| **Bronchiolitis (paediatric)** | First-line in infants |
| **Acute heart failure** | Sometimes (if not hypercapnic) |
| **Immunocompromised** | Reduces intubation vs NIV |

## Contraindications

| Absolute | Relative |
|----------|----------|
| Need for immediate intubation | Hypercapnic respiratory failure (use NIV) |
| Recent upper airway surgery | Agitated/uncooperative patient |
| Severe facial trauma/burns | Nasal obstruction (e.g. polyps) |
| **Hypercapnic respiratory failure** (pH <7.35) — use NIV | GCS <8 (relative) |

## Setup

### Equipment
- HFNC device (e.g. AIRVO 2, Optiflow, Vapotherm)
- Nasal cannula (sized to nares)
- Tubing with humidification chamber
- Sterile water for humidifier

### Initial settings
| Parameter | Initial | Range |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Flow** | 30–40 L/min | 20–60 L/min |
| **FiO₂** | Titrate to SpO₂ | 0.21–1.0 |
| **Temperature** | 37°C (or 31°C for comfort) | 31–37°C |

### Titration
- **Start**: 30 L/min + FiO₂ to target SpO₂ 92–98%
- **Increase flow** to 40–50–60 L/min if WOB or hypoxaemia persists
- **Increase FiO₂** if SpO₂ < target
- **Monitor**: ABG within 1–2 h, then 4–6 h

## Monitoring

### Clinical
- **Respiratory rate** (key early indicator)
- **WOB** (accessory muscle use, recession)
- **SpO₂** continuously
- **Heart rate, BP**
- **Comfort** (tolerates mouth breathing)

### Lab
- **ABG within 1–2 h** of initiation
- Recheck after changes

### HFNC failure criteria
- **ROX index** = (SpO₂/FiO₂)/RR
  - ROX ≥4.88 at 2, 6, 12 h → likely success
  - ROX <3.85 → high risk of failure
- ↑RR, ↓SpO₂ despite ↑FiO₂
- Worsening ABG (↓PaO₂, ↑PaCO₂, ↓pH)
- Exhaustion, GCS ↓

## Management

### Weaning
- **Step down** when stable (SpO₂ >92% on FiO₂ <0.4, RR <25)
- Reduce FiO₂ first (to 0.4)
- Then reduce flow (to 20 L/min)
- Switch to **nasal cannula** when FiO₂ <0.4 and flow <25 L/min

### Escalation
- **HFNC failure** → **NIV** (or intubation if critical)
- Maintain airway, suction if needed
- Consider position (sitting up, prone if ARDS)
- Treat underlying cause

## Comparison with Other Devices

| Device | Flow | FiO₂ | Humidification | PEEP | Indication |
|--------|------|------|----------------|------|------------|
| **Nasal cannula** | 1–6 L | 0.24–0.44 | No | No | Stable mild hypoxaemia |
| **HFNC** | 20–60 L | 0.21–1.0 | **Yes** | **3–5 cmH₂O** | Acute hypoxaemic failure |
| **CPAP** | Variable | 0.21–1.0 | Yes (with humidifier) | **5–15 cmH₂O** | OSA, oedema |
| **BiPAP** | Variable | 0.21–1.0 | Yes | IPAP 10–20 / EPAP 4–8 | Hypercapnic failure |

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|
| **HFNC flow** | 20–60 L/min |
| **HFNC FiO₂** | 0.21–1.0 |
| **Humidification** | Heated, 100% RH (37°C) |
| **PEEP effect** | 3–5 cmH₂O |
| **First-line** | Acute hypoxaemic failure (P/F 100–300) |
| **Not for** | Hypercapnic failure (use NIV) |
| **ROX index** | (SpO₂/FiO₂)/RR; ≥4.88 success |
| **Benefits** | Dead space washout, humidification, comfort, stable FiO₂ |
| **Failure criteria** | ↑RR, ↓SpO₂, exhaustion, worsening ABG |

## MCQs (10)

1. HFNC delivers flow up to:
   A. 10 L/min
   B. 20 L/min
   C. **60 L/min**
   D. 100 L/min
   E. 200 L/min
   **Answer: C** — 20–60 L/min.

2. HFNC is first-line in:
   A. Hypercapnic respiratory failure
   B. **Acute hypoxaemic respiratory failure (P/F 100–300)**
   C. OSA
   D. Cardiac arrest
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Hypoxaemic failure.

3. HFNC temperature is typically:
   A. 25°C
   B. **37°C (with 100% humidification)**
   C. 40°C
   D. Ambient
   E. 20°C
   **Answer: B** — 37°C, 100% RH.

4. HFNC generates a PEEP of approximately:
   A. 0
   B. **3–5 cmH₂O**
   C. 10 cmH₂O
   D. 15 cmH₂O
   E. 20 cmH₂O
   **Answer: B** — Mild PEEP.

5. ROX index is calculated as:
   A. (PaO₂/FiO₂)/RR
   B. **(SpO₂/FiO₂)/RR**
   C. RR/SpO₂
   D. FiO₂/RR
   E. SpO₂/PaO₂
   **Answer: B** — (SpO₂/FiO₂)/RR.

6. ROX ≥4.88 predicts:
   A. HFNC failure
   B. **HFNC success**
   C. Need for intubation
   D. Cardiac failure
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Success.

7. HFNC is not appropriate in:
   A. Acute hypoxaemic failure
   B. Post-extubation
   C. **Hypercapnic respiratory failure (use NIV)**
   D. Bronchiolitis
   E. COVID-19 pneumonia
   **Answer: C** — Use NIV.

8. First-line in immunocompromised hypoxaemic failure:
   A. Intubate
   B. **HFNC (reduces intubation)**
   C. NIV
   D. Mask O₂
   E. Surgery
   **Answer: B** — HFNC.

9. HFNC initial flow for adult:
   A. 5 L
   B. 15 L
   C. **30–40 L/min**
   D. 80 L
   E. 5 L
   **Answer: C** — 30–40 L/min.

10. When to step down HFNC:
    A. Always
    B. **When SpO₂ >92% on FiO₂ <0.4 and RR <25**
    C. Never
    D. After 24 h
    E. Only at night
    **Answer: B** — Stable on lower support.

## Summary

HFNC delivers 20–60 L/min of heated, humidified O₂ (37°C) with adjustable FiO₂ (0.21–1.0). Benefits include dead space washout, mild PEEP (3–5 cmH₂O), stable FiO₂, comfort. First-line in acute hypoxaemic failure (P/F 100–300). Not for hypercapnic failure (use NIV). ROX index (SpO₂/FiO₂/RR) ≥4.88 predicts success.
