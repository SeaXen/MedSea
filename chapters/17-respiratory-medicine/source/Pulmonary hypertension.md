# Pulmonary hypertension

> [!important]
> **Pulmonary hypertension (PH)** is defined as **mean pulmonary artery pressure (mPAP) >20 mmHg** at rest (revised from ≥25 mmHg per ESC/ERS 2022). It comprises 5 clinical groups based on aetiology. **PAH (group 1)** and **CTEPH (group 4)** are the main subtypes managed by PH specialists.

Related: [[Pulmonary Embolism]], [[Cor pulmonale]], [[Chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension|CTEPH]], [[Right-heart strain in respiratory disease]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: Most common cause of PH is **left heart disease (group 2)**. **PAH** is rare but treatable with specific vasodilators. **CTEPH** is the only curable form (pulmonary endarterectomy). Always confirm diagnosis with **right heart catheterisation**.

## Definition

**Pulmonary hypertension** = mPAP >20 mmHg at rest (by right heart catheterisation, RHC).

### Definition evolution
- **Old**: mPAP ≥25 mmHg
- **Current (ESC/ERS 2022)**: mPAP >20 mmHg
- **Pulmonary vascular resistance (PVR)**: >2 Wood units (also revised)

## Classification (ESC/ERS 2022 — 5 groups)

### Group 1: PAH (Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension)
- Idiopathic, heritable
- Drug-induced (anorexigens, methamphetamines)
- Associated (CTD — esp. SSc, HIV, portal hypertension, congenital heart disease)
- **Vasoreactive** (5–10%): respond to CCB
- **Pulmonary veno-occlusive disease** (PVOD)

### Group 2: PH due to left heart disease
- Heart failure (HFrEF, HFpEF)
- Valvular disease (mitral stenosis/regurg, aortic stenosis)
- **Most common cause of PH**

### Group 3: PH due to lung disease/hypoxia
- COPD (most common respiratory cause)
- ILD
- OSA, OHS
- High altitude

### Group 4: PH due to pulmonary artery obstruction
- **CTEPH** (chronic thromboembolic PH)
- Pulmonary artery stenosis
- Tumour embolism

### Group 5: PH with unclear/multifactorial mechanisms
- Haematological (sickle cell, myeloproliferative)
- Systemic disorders (sarcoidosis, vasculitis)
- Metabolic (glycogen storage)
- Others

## Pathophysiology

### Mechanisms
- **Vascular remodelling** (intimal proliferation, medial hypertrophy, adventitial fibrosis)
- **Vasoconstriction** (endothelial dysfunction, ↓NO, ↑endothelin-1)
- **In situ thrombosis**
- **Vascular pruning/loss** (rarefaction)

### Right heart consequences
- **RV pressure overload** → RV dilation/hypertrophy
- **RV failure** (cor pulmonale) → ↓LV preload → ↓cardiac output
- **RV ischaemia** (↓coronary perfusion)

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- **Progressive exertional dyspnoea** (most common)
- **Fatigue**, lethargy
- **Exertional chest pain** (RV ischaemia)
- **Syncope / pre-syncope** (↓cardiac output)
- **Palpitations**
- **Peripheral oedema** (right HF)

### Signs
- **Loud P2** (most sensitive)
- **RV heave**
- **JVP ↑** (with prominent a wave)
- **Tricuspid regurgitation murmur**
- **Pulsatile liver**
- **Peripheral oedema**
- **Cyanosis**
- **Signs of underlying cause** (CREST, clubbing, etc.)

## Diagnosis

### Investigations
| Test | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **CXR** | Enlarged central pulmonary arteries, RV enlargement, pruning |
| **ECG** | RVH (R > S in V1), RBBB, right axis, P pulmonale |
| **Echo** | PAP estimate, RV dilation/dysfunction, TR velocity |
| **CTPA** | PA diameter >29 mm, RV/LV ratio, parenchymal disease |
| **V/Q scan** | **Mismatched perfusion defects** in CTEPH (vs matched or normal in PAH) |
| **PFTs** | ↓DLCO (early), restrictive/obstructive (depending on cause) |
| **ABG** | ↓PaO₂, ↓PaCO₂ (hypervent), ↑A-a |
| **6MWT** | Functional capacity, prognosis |
| **NT-proBNP** | ↑ in RV failure |
| **RHC (right heart catheterisation)** | **Gold standard** for diagnosis (mPAP >20) + vasoreactivity testing |

### Diagnostic criteria
- **Suspected**: echo PAP estimate >35 mmHg
- **Confirmed**: RHC mPAP >20 mmHg, PVR >2 WU
- **Pre-capillary PH** (PAH, CTEPH): PCWP ≤15 mmHg
- **Post-capillary PH** (left heart): PCWP >15 mmHg

## Management

### Supportive
- **O₂ therapy** (target SpO₂ >90%)
- **Diuretics** (oedema)
- **Anticoagulation** (PAH, CTEPH)
- **Avoid pregnancy** (high mortality)
- **Vaccinations** (flu, pneumococcal)
- **Rehabilitation**

### Group 1 (PAH) — specific therapy
- **Vasoreactive** (~5–10%): high-dose CCB (nifedipine, amlodipine)
- **Non-vasoreactive**: PAH-targeted therapy
  - **PDE5 inhibitors**: sildenafil, tadalafil
  - **Endothelin receptor antagonists (ERA)**: bosentan, ambrisentan, macitentan
  - **Prostacyclin analogues**: epoprostenol (IV), iloprost (inhaled), selexipag (oral)
  - **Soluble guanylate cyclase stimulator**: riociguat
- **Combination therapy** for higher-risk
- **Lung transplant** for end-stage

### Group 4 (CTEPH) — specific therapy
- **Lifelong anticoagulation**
- **Pulmonary endarterectomy** (PTE) — potentially curative
- **Riociguat** if inoperable or persistent
- **Balloon pulmonary angioplasty (BPA)** for inoperable

### Group 2 (left heart) — treat underlying
- Optimise HF Rx
- Valve surgery if applicable
- Avoid PAH-specific therapy (worsens)

### Group 3 (lung disease)
- O₂ therapy (esp. if PaO₂ <8 kPa)
- Treat underlying lung disease
- Avoid PAH-specific therapy in most cases (data limited)

## MCQs (10)

1. Definition of pulmonary hypertension (current, ESC/ERS 2022):
   A. mPAP >25 mmHg
   B. **mPAP >20 mmHg**
   C. sPAP >35 mmHg
   D. PVR >5 WU
   E. PCWP >15
   **Answer: B** — >20 mmHg.

2. Most common cause of PH:
   A. PAH
   B. **Left heart disease**
   C. COPD
   D. CTEPH
   E. Idiopathic
   **Answer: B** — Group 2 (left heart).

3. Most sensitive clinical sign of PH:
   A. JVP
   B. **Loud P2**
   C. RV heave
   D. Cyanosis
   E. Oedema
   **Answer: B** — Loud P2.

4. Gold standard for PH diagnosis:
   A. Echo
   B. **Right heart catheterisation (RHC)**
   C. CTPA
   D. V/Q scan
   D. ECG
   **Answer: B** — RHC.

5. CTEPH is potentially curable by:
   A. Lung transplant
   B. **Pulmonary endarterectomy**
   C. Riociguat
   D. Vasodilators
   E. Anticoagulation only
   **Answer: B** — PTE.

6. Vasoreactive PAH (responds to CCB) is:
   A. 50%
   B. **5–10%**
   C. 90%
   D. 0%
   E. 25%
   **Answer: B** — 5–10% only.

7. Riociguat is approved for:
   A. PAH only
   B. **PAH and CTEPH**
   C. PH due to lung disease
   D. Left heart PH
   E. Acute PE
   **Answer: B** — PAH and CTEPH.

8. Bosentan is a(n):
   A. PDE5 inhibitor
   B. **Endothelin receptor antagonist (ERA)**
   C. Prostacyclin
   D. Calcium channel blocker
   E. Diuretic
   **Answer: B** — ERA.

9. PAH-specific therapy is contraindicated in:
   A. PAH
   B. CTEPH
   C. **Left heart disease (group 2)**
   D. Vasoreactive PAH
   E. Severe PAH
   **Answer: C** — Worsens left heart PH.

10. Pregnancy in PAH:
    A. Safe
    B. **Contraindicated (high maternal mortality)**
    C. Always continue
    D. Always terminate
    E. None
    **Answer: B** — Contraindicated.

## SBA Questions (10)

1. A 40-year-old woman with SSc has progressive dyspnoea. Echo: PAP 50 mmHg, RV dilation. Next step:
   A. Anticoagulation
   B. **Right heart catheterisation to confirm PAH**
   C. O₂
   D. Surgery
   E. Discharge
   **Answer: B** — RHC to confirm.

2. A patient with confirmed PAH is vasoreactive on RHC. First-line Rx:
   A. Bosentan
   B. Sildenafil
   C. **High-dose CCB (e.g. nifedipine)**
   D. Epoprostenol
   E. None
   **Answer: C** — Vasoreactive = CCB.

3. CTEPH diagnosis is best made by:
   A. Echo
   B. **V/Q scan (mismatched perfusion defects)**
   C. CTPA
   D. RHC
   E. Spirogram
   **Answer: B** — V/Q (gold standard for CTEPH).

4. PH in severe COPD is managed by:
   A. PAH-specific Rx
   B. **Long-term O₂ + treat underlying COPD**
   C. Surgery
   D. Riociguat
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — O₂ + underlying.

5. Most common respiratory cause of PH:
   A. ILD
   B. **COPD**
   C. OSA
   D. OHS
   E. Asthma
   **Answer: B** — COPD.

6. Sildenafil in PAH works by:
   A. Endothelin antagonism
   B. **PDE5 inhibition (↑cGMP)**
   C. Prostacyclin
   D. Calcium
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — PDE5i.

7. Risk stratification in PAH includes:
   A. SpO₂ only
   B. **WHO functional class, 6MWD, BNP, echo, RHC**
   C. CXR only
   D. Spirogram only
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Multiple.

8. A patient with PAH on bosentan. Monitoring:
   A. Monthly LFTs (bosentan hepatotoxic)
   B. **Monthly LFTs + Hb + fluid status**
   C. No monitoring
   D. Annual only
   E. Just BP
   **Answer: B** — LFTs + Hb.

9. PH classification group 1 is:
   A. PH due to left heart
   B. **PAH (idiopathic, heritable, drug, associated)**
   C. PH due to lung disease
   D. CTEPH
   E. PH unclear
   **Answer: B** — Group 1 = PAH.

10. Initial diagnostic workup of suspected PH includes:
    A. Echo only
    B. **Echo, CXR, ECG, PFTs, V/Q scan, CTPA, RHC**
    C. RHC only
    D. CXR only
    E. None
    **Answer: B** — Multimodal.

## Summary

PH = mPAP >20 mmHg at rest. 5 groups: PAH (1), left heart (2, most common), lung disease (3), CTEPH (4), unclear (5). Loud P2 = key sign. RHC = gold standard. PAH Rx: vasoreactive → CCB; non-vasoreactive → PDE5i, ERA, prostacyclin, riociguat. CTEPH → PTE ± riociguat/BPA. Pregnancy contraindicated.
