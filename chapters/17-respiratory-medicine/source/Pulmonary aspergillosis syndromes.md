# Pulmonary aspergillosis syndromes

> [!important]
> *Aspergillus* causes a spectrum of pulmonary disease: **ABPA** (hypersensitivity in asthma/CF), **aspergilloma** (mycetoma in pre-existing cavity), **invasive aspergillosis** (immunocompromised), **chronic necrotising aspergillosis** (semi-invasive), and **IgE-mediated asthma**. Diagnosis and treatment differ markedly by syndrome.

Related: [[ABPA and bronchiectasis]], [[Opportunistic pulmonary infections]], [[Asthma]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: **ABPA** = asthma + ↑IgE + central bronchiectasis + HAM on CT → prednisolone + itraconazole. **Aspergilloma** = fungal ball in old TB cavity (air crescent sign) → surgery or BAE for haemoptysis. **Invasive aspergillosis** = neutropenic + halo sign → voriconazole.

## Spectrum of *Aspergillus* Disease

| Syndrome | Host | Pathology | Treatment |
|----------|------|-----------|-----------|
| **ABPA** | Asthma, CF | Hypersensitivity (Type I/III) | Steroids + itraconazole |
| **Aspergilloma** | Pre-existing cavity | Fungal ball | Observation, BAE, surgery |
| **Invasive aspergillosis** | Neutropenic | Tissue invasion | Voriconazole |
| **Chronic necrotising (semi-invasive)** | Mild immunocompromise | Slow invasion | Antifungal ± surgery |
| **IgE-mediated asthma** | Asthma | Allergy | Avoid exposure + asthma Rx |

## ABPA (Allergic Bronchopulmonary Aspergillosis)

### Definition
- **Hypersensitivity reaction** to *Aspergillus fumigatus* in **asthma** (1–2%) and **CF** (7–15%)
- Non-invasive; recurrent exacerbations

### Diagnosis (ISHAM criteria)
**Essential** (both):
- **Asthma** (or CF)
- **↑Total serum IgE >1000 IU/mL**

**Other** (≥2 of 6):
1. **Positive immediate skin-prick** to *A. fumigatus*
2. **↑Specific IgE** to *A. fumigatus**
3. **↑Serum precipitins** (IgG to *A. fumigatus*)
4. **Peripheral blood eosinophilia** >500/µL
5. **Central (proximal) bronchiectasis** on HRCT
6. **High-attenuation mucus (HAM)** on HRCT (pathognomonic)

### Treatment
- **Prednisolone** 0.5–0.75 mg/kg/day × 2 weeks → taper
- **Itraconazole** 200 mg BD as steroid-sparing
- **Omalizumab** (anti-IgE) in refractory
- Monitor IgE, eosinophils, CXR

## Aspergilloma (Mycetoma)

### Definition
- **Fungal ball** (mass of hyphae + mucus + debris) in **pre-existing lung cavity** (TB, sarcoidosis, emphysema, lung cyst)
- Non-invasive but can cause **massive haemoptysis**

### Diagnosis
- **CXR**: mobile intracavitary mass with **air crescent sign**
- **CT chest**: intracavitary mass + air crescent
- **Aspergillus precipitins** (positive in 70%)
- Aspergillus IgG ↑

### Treatment
- **Asymptomatic**: observation
- **Haemoptysis**:
  - **Bronchial artery embolisation (BAE)** for acute
  - **Surgical resection** for isolated lesion + recurrent/persistent
  - **Antifungal** (itraconazole/voriconazole) — limited role
  - **Intracavitary** amphotericin (rare)

## Invasive Aspergillosis

### Host
- **Severe neutropenia** (<500, especially <100)
- **HSCT, SOT** (lung, liver)
- **High-dose steroids**
- **Advanced AIDS** (CD4 <100)

### Diagnosis (EORTC/MSG criteria — proven/probable/possible)
- **Host** (neutropenia, transplant, steroids)
- **Clinical** (fever, symptoms, imaging)
- **Mycological** (Aspergillus culture from sputum/BAL, galactomannan, PCR)

### HRCT findings
- **Halo sign**: nodule with surrounding GGO (early; ~5 days)
- **Air crescent sign**: crescent of air around nodule (late; ~2 weeks)
- **Cavitation** (late)

### Treatment
- **Voriconazole** (first-line) 6 mg/kg IV ×2 → 4 mg/kg IV BD
- **Isavuconazole** (alternative)
- **Liposomal amphotericin B** (alternative/rescue)
- **Posaconazole** (prophylaxis in HSCT, salvage)
- **Anidulafungin** (echinocandin, salvage)
- Duration: 6–12 weeks
- **Surgery** for localised invasive disease

## Chronic Necrotising (Semi-Invasive) Aspergillosis

### Host
- **Mild immunocompromise** (COPD, diabetes, alcohol, low-dose steroids)
- Slow progression

### Features
- Chronic cough, weight loss, fatigue
- Cavitary lesion (often upper lobe)
- Fungal hyphae in tissue

### Treatment
- **Antifungal** (voriconazole or itraconazole) 3–6 months
- **Surgery** if localised

## IgE-Mediated Asthma (Aspergillus sensitisation)

- **Positive skin-prick or specific IgE** to *Aspergillus* in asthma without meeting ABPA criteria
- Treat as asthma + avoid exposure

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|
| **ABPA** | Asthma/CF + ↑IgE + central bronchiectasis + HAM |
| **ABPA Rx** | Prednisolone + itraconazole |
| **Aspergilloma** | Fungal ball in cavity; air crescent sign |
| **Aspergilloma Rx** | BAE (acute) or surgery (recurrent) |
| **Invasive Aspergillus** | Neutropenic + halo sign |
| **Invasive Rx** | Voriconazole (1st line) |
| **Air crescent sign** | Late invasive or aspergilloma |
| **Halo sign** | Early invasive (GGO around nodule) |
| **Galactomannan** | Diagnostic marker |

## MCQs (10)

1. ABPA is most commonly associated with:
   A. COPD
   B. **Asthma and CF**
   C. Lung cancer
   D. None
   E. TB
   **Answer: B** — Asthma and CF.

2. First-line treatment for ABPA:
   A. **Prednisolone + itraconazole**
   B. Voriconazole alone
   C. Surgery
   D. Theophylline
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — Steroids + itraconazole.

3. The pathognomonic HRCT finding in ABPA:
   A. Cavitation
   B. **High-attenuation mucus (HAM)**
   C. Pleural effusion
   D. Nodules
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — HAM.

4. Air crescent sign in a TB cavity suggests:
   A. Active TB
   B. **Aspergilloma**
   C. Lung cancer
   D. None
   E. Pneumonia
   **Answer: B** — Aspergilloma.

5. First-line treatment for massive haemoptysis in aspergilloma:
   A. Antifungal
   B. **Bronchial artery embolisation (BAE)**
   C. Steroids
   D. Wait
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — BAE.

6. First-line treatment for invasive aspergillosis:
   A. **Voriconazole**
   B. Fluconazole
   C. Acyclovir
   D. TMP-SMX
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — Voriconazole.

7. The halo sign on HRCT is seen in:
   A. Aspergilloma
   B. **Invasive aspergillosis (early)**
   C. TB
   D. Sarcoid
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Early invasive.

8. ABPA in CF: prevalence:
   A. 1%
   B. 5%
   C. **7–15%**
   D. 50%
   E. None
   **Answer: C** — 7–15%.

9. Serum galactomannan is a marker of:
   A. ABPA
   B. **Invasive aspergillosis**
   C. TB
   D. Sarcoidosis
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Invasive.

10. The minimum IgE for ABPA diagnosis (ISHAM):
    A. >100
    B. **>1000 IU/mL**
    C. >500
    D. >100
    E. None
    **Answer: B** — >1000.

1. A 30-year-old with asthma has ↑IgE to 3000, central bronchiectasis, HAM. Diagnosis:
   A. Asthma
   B. **ABPA**
   C. CF
   D. None
   E. Sarcoid
   **Answer: B** — ABPA.

   A. **Prednisolone (0.5 mg/kg/day) + itraconazole**
   B. Voriconazole
   C. Surgery
   D. Wait
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — Steroids + itraconazole.

3. A 60-year-old with old TB has new mass in cavity with air crescent. Best management:
   A. Antifungal
   B. **Observe if asymptomatic; BAE if haemoptysis**
   C. Steroids
   D. Surgery
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Observe/BAE.

4. Invasive aspergillosis is most common in:
   A. Asthma
   B. **Neutropenic (HSCT, leukaemia)**
   C. COPD
   D. None
   E. CF
   **Answer: B** — Neutropenic.

5. The first sign of invasive aspergillosis on CT:
   A. Cavitation
   B. **Halo sign (GGO around nodule)**
   C. Pleural effusion
   D. None
   E. Air crescent
   **Answer: B** — Halo.

6. Treatment duration for invasive aspergillosis:
   A. 1 week
   B. **6–12 weeks**
   C. 3 days
   D. 1 year
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — 6–12 weeks.

7. ABPA in CF: first-line antifungal:
   A. Voriconazole
   B. **Itraconazole**
   C. Posaconazole
   D. None
   E. Ampho B
   **Answer: B** — Itraconazole.

8. A neutropenic patient has fever + new cavitating lesion + halo sign. Treatment:
   A. Wait

9. The most useful serum marker for invasive aspergillosis:
   A. CRP

  A: Prednisolone + itraconazole.

## Summary

*Aspergillus* causes 5 main syndromes: ABPA (asthma + ↑IgE + HAM → steroids + itraconazole), aspergilloma (fungal ball → BAE/surgery), invasive (neutropenic + halo → voriconazole), chronic necrotising, IgE asthma. Diagnosis: imaging (CT, air crescent, halo, HAM), galactomannan, precipitins, skin-prick.
