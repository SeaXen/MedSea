# COPD — Last Night Before Exam

Related: [[COPD]], [[Asthma]], [[Respiratory Failure]], [[ABG Interpretation]], [[Oxygen Therapy and NIV]]

## Core Definition
- **COPD** = chronic obstructive pulmonary disease
- Persistent respiratory symptoms + **persistent airflow limitation**
- Caused by:
  - **small airway disease**
  - **emphysema**
- Usually due to **smoking** or **biomass exposure**

## Classic Clinical Picture
- Progressive **exertional dyspnea**
- Chronic cough
- Sputum production
- Wheeze
- Recurrent exacerbations
- Reduced exercise tolerance

> [!important]
> Core exam phrase: **persistent airflow limitation that is not fully reversible**.

## Key Pathophysiology
- Small airway narrowing + inflammation
- Mucus hypersecretion
- Loss of elastic recoil in emphysema
- Air trapping → **hyperinflation**
- V/Q mismatch → **hypoxemia**
- Advanced disease → **hypercapnia** and respiratory failure

## Must-Know Risk Factors
- **Smoking**
- Biomass fuel exposure
- Occupational dust/fume exposure
- Air pollution
- Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency
- Recurrent respiratory infections / poor lung growth

## Anatomy Pearls
- Major pathology is in **small airways (<2 mm)**
- Emphysema destroys **alveolar walls**
- Hyperinflation flattens the **diaphragm**
- Chronic hypoxia affects pulmonary vasculature → pulmonary hypertension / cor pulmonale

## Physiology Pearls
- Major cause of hypoxemia = **V/Q mismatch**
- Emphysema causes **↓ elastic recoil** and often **↓ DLCO**
- Air trapping causes **↑ RV, ↑ FRC, hyperinflation**
- Dynamic hyperinflation worsens during exercise and exacerbation

## Diagnosis
### Confirm with spirometry
- **Post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC <0.70**

### FEV1 severity
- **GOLD 1**: ≥80%
- **GOLD 2**: 50–79%
- **GOLD 3**: 30–49%
- **GOLD 4**: <30%

## Important Investigations
- **Spirometry** = key diagnostic test
- Chest X-ray
- **ABG** in severe exacerbation / drowsiness / low saturation
- CBC
- ECG / echo for cor pulmonale / cardiac disease
- CT chest if diagnosis unclear
- Alpha-1 antitrypsin level in early-onset / minimal-smoker emphysema

## ABG / Oxygen Pearls
### Normal values
- pH: **7.35–7.45**
- PaCO2: **35–45 mmHg**
- PaO2: **80–100 mmHg**

### COPD oxygen target
- If risk of CO2 retention: **SpO2 88–92%**

### Why too much oxygen may worsen hypercapnia
- worsened **V/Q mismatch**
- loss of hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction
- **Haldane effect**

> [!warning]
> Never write “oxygen as much as possible” in a COPD retainer. Write **controlled oxygen to 88–92%**.

## Chronic Bronchitis vs Emphysema
### Chronic bronchitis
- Productive cough dominant
- Defined as cough + sputum for **3 months in each of 2 consecutive years**

### Emphysema
- Alveolar destruction
- Dyspnea dominant
- Hyperinflation prominent
- **DLCO reduced**

## Differentials
- [[Asthma]]
- Heart failure
- Bronchiectasis
- Lung cancer
- Tuberculosis
- Interstitial lung disease

## Stable Management
- **Smoking cessation** = most important intervention
- Vaccination: influenza, pneumococcal
- SABA/SAMA for relief
- LABA or LAMA for maintenance
- LABA + LAMA if more symptomatic
- ICS only in selected cases:
  - frequent exacerbations
  - eosinophilic phenotype
  - asthma overlap
- Pulmonary rehabilitation
- Nutrition and exercise support
- LTOT if chronically hypoxemic

## LTOT Criteria
Use in stable COPD if:
- **PaO2 ≤7.3 kPa (55 mmHg)**
or
- **PaO2 7.3–8.0 kPa** with:
  - pulmonary hypertension
  - cor pulmonale/peripheral edema
  - secondary polycythemia

## Acute Exacerbation (AECOPD)
### Common triggers
- infection
- pneumonia
- air pollution
- heart failure
- PE
- pneumothorax
- poor adherence

### Treatment = O-B-S-A-N
- **O**xygen controlled (88–92%)
- **B**ronchodilators
- **S**teroids
- **A**ntibiotics if indicated
- **N**IV if acidotic hypercapnia

### Antibiotics when?
Especially if:
- increased sputum purulence
- increased sputum volume
- increased dyspnea

## NIV
### Indications
- **Acute hypercapnic respiratory acidosis**
- increased work of breathing
- respiratory muscle fatigue

### Contraindications
- inability to protect airway
- vomiting
- facial trauma
- peri-arrest state

## Red Flags / Emergencies
- Silent chest
- Exhaustion
- Confusion / drowsiness
- Cyanosis
- Worsening acidosis on ABG
- Sudden pleuritic pain → think pneumothorax
- New focal signs → think pneumonia

## Complications
- Recurrent exacerbations
- Type 1 / type 2 respiratory failure
- Pulmonary hypertension
- **Cor pulmonale**
- Pneumothorax
- Polycythemia
- Weight loss and muscle wasting
- Lung cancer association

## Top FCPS/MRCP One-Liners
- **COPD is confirmed by post-bronchodilator spirometry.**
- **FEV1/FVC <0.70** is the classic threshold.
- Main cause of hypoxemia = **V/Q mismatch**.
- Oxygen target in CO2 retainers = **88–92%**.
- **NIV** is used in acute hypercapnic acidosis.
- Smoking cessation slows decline.
- LTOT improves survival in selected chronically hypoxemic patients.

## Ultra-Short Viva Answers
### Define COPD
A chronic preventable and treatable disease characterized by persistent respiratory symptoms and persistent airflow limitation due to small airway disease and/or emphysema, usually from smoking.

### Diagnostic criterion
**Post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC <0.70**.

### Why can oxygen worsen CO2 retention?
By worsening V/Q mismatch, reducing hypoxic vasoconstriction, and through the Haldane effect.

### When do you use NIV?
In **acute hypercapnic respiratory acidosis** if no contraindication exists.

## Last 10 Things to Memorize
1. Smoking = main risk factor
2. FEV1/FVC **<0.70**
3. Chronic bronchitis = **3 months in each of 2 years**
4. Emphysema = alveolar destruction
5. Hypoxemia mainly from **V/Q mismatch**
6. O2 target = **88–92%**
7. Exacerbation: bronchodilator + steroid + antibiotics if infective
8. ABG is essential in severe exacerbation
9. NIV for **acidotic hypercapnia**
10. LTOT improves survival in severe chronic hypoxemia
