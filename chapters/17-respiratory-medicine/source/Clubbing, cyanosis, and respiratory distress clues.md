# Clubbing, cyanosis, and respiratory distress clues

> [!important]
> **Clubbing** is a key bedside sign with a specific differential (**suppurative lung disease, malignancy, cyanotic heart disease, idiopathic**). **Cyanosis** distinguishes **central (respiratory/cardiac)** from **peripheral** causes. Recognition of **respiratory distress** (use of accessory muscles, paradoxical breathing, tracheal tug) is vital for severity assessment.

Related: [[Lung Cancer]], [[Interstitial Lung Disease]], [[Bronchiectasis]], [[Acute severe breathlessness approach]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: **Clubbing + chronic lung disease = think suppurative (bronchiectasis, CF, abscess) or malignancy (lung cancer, mesothelioma)**. **Central cyanosis = hypoxaemia** (respiratory/cardiac). **Peripheral cyanosis = sluggish circulation** (cold, shock, RVF). **Respiratory distress signs (use of accessory muscles, paradoxical breathing, stridor) = severity + need for intervention**.

## Clubbing (Digital Clubbing)

### Definition
**Bulbous enlargement** of distal phalanges (fingers/toes) with loss of nail-fold angle (Lovibond's angle).

### Pathophysiology
- **Megakaryocytes** and platelet clumps **bypass pulmonary filter** (in right-to-left shunt or chronic lung disease) → lodge in distal capillaries
- Release **PDGF** and **VEGF** → connective tissue proliferation
- **Chronic hypoxaemia, inflammation** contribute

### Causes
| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Respiratory** | Bronchiectasis, CF, lung abscess, empyema, lung cancer, mesothelioma, ILD (esp. asbestosis) |
| **Cardiac** | Cyanotic CHD, IE, myxoma |
| **GI** | Cirrhosis, IBD, coeliac |
| **Endocrine** | Thyrotoxicosis |
| **Other** | Idiopathic (5–10%), hereditary, pregnancy |
| **NOT clubbing** | COPD alone, sarcoidosis, asthma (usually) |

### Examination
- **Schamroth window test**: loss of diamond-shaped window when opposing nails are apposed
- **Phalangeal depth ratio (PDR)** >1 (DTP/DIP)
- **Distal phalangeal fibrous thickening**

### Grades
| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | ↑Fluctuation, softening of nail bed |
| 2 | Loss of nail-fold angle (Lovibond) |
| 3 | ↑AP and lateral curvature of nail |
| 4 | Drumstick terminal phalanx |

### Clinical significance
- **Bilateral** (most): respiratory, cardiac, GI, idiopathic
- **Unilateral**: local vascular (AV fistula, aneurysm)
- **Painful** (hypertrophic osteoarthropathy): lung cancer, mesothelioma
- **Symmetric + rapid**: lung cancer, abscess

## Cyanosis

### Definition
**Bluish discolouration** of skin/mucosae from **desaturated Hb >5 g/dL** (capillary level).

### Central cyanosis
- **Tongue, lips, oral mucosa, conjunctiva**
- Causes:
  - **Respiratory**: hypoxaemia (pneumonia, PE, COPD, PTX, OSA, foreign body)
  - **Cardiac**: cyanotic CHD, pulmonary hypertension
  - **Haemoglobin**: methaemoglobinaemia, sulphaemoglobinaemia

### Peripheral cyanosis
- **Fingers, toes, earlobes, nose** (cold exposure)
- Causes: **cold**, shock, heart failure, Raynaud's, peripheral vascular disease
- **Normal SpO₂**

### Differential cyanosis
- **Upper body cyanotic, lower body pink** = PDA with R→L shunt (Eisenmenger)
- **Reverse differential** (lower body cyanotic) = PDA with R→L above (rare)

### Methaemoglobinaemia
- **Chocolate-brown blood** (does not change with O₂)
- Causes: dapsone, benzocaine, nitrites
- Treatment: **methylene blue** (1–2 mg/kg IV)
- SpO₂ reads ~85% regardless of actual saturation

## Respiratory Distress

### Signs of severity
| Sign | Significance |
|------|--------------|
| **Tachypnoea** (>25/min) | Most sensitive |
| **Tachycardia** | Compensation |
| **Use of accessory muscles** (SCM, scalenes) | ↑WOB |
| **Intercostal/subcostal recession** | ↑WOB |
| **Tracheal tug** | ↑WOB |
| **Paradoxical breathing** (abdominal paradox) | Diaphragmatic fatigue |
| **Nasal flaring** | Severe (esp. children) |
| **Pursed-lip breathing** | COPD (auto-PEEP) |
| **Tripoding** (leaning forward) | Severe obstructive |
| **Cyanosis** | Hypoxaemia |
| **Altered mental status** | Hypoxaemia, hypercapnia |
| **Diaphoresis** | Severe, ↑WOB |

### Pattern recognition
- **Obstructive** (asthma, COPD): prolonged expiration, wheeze, accessory muscle use, pursed-lip, tripoding
- **Restrictive** (ILD): rapid shallow breathing, dry cough
- **Central airway obstruction** (foreign body, tumour): stridor, monophonic wheeze

## Cyanotic Congenital Heart Disease (5 Ts)
- **Tetralogy of Fallot**
- **Transposition** of great arteries
- **Tricuspid atresia**
- **Truncus arteriosus**
- **Total anomalous pulmonary venous return**

## Examination Approach

### Quick respiratory assessment
1. **General**: distress, colour, position, conscious level
2. **Hands**: clubbing, cyanosis, CO₂ flap, nicotine, tar
3. **Face/neck**: JVP, trachea, stridor, lymph nodes
4. **Chest**: shape, movement, percussion, auscultation
5. **Cardiac**: apex, murmurs, S3, S4, pericardial rub
6. **Abdomen**: hepatomegaly, ascites (right HF)
7. **Limbs**: oedema, calf swelling, clubbing

## Management of Distress

### General
- **Position**: sitting up
- **O₂**: target 94–98% (88–92% if hypercapnic)
- **Reassure** (anxiety worsens)
- **Specific treatment** by cause
- **Escalate** (HDU/ITU) if severe

### Cause-specific
| Distress cause | Rx |
|----------------|-----|
| Severe asthma | SABA + ipratropium + IV hydrocortisone + IV MgSO₄ |
| AECOPD | Controlled O₂ + SABA + steroid + NIV |
| APO | O₂ + IV furosemide + GTN + NIV |
| Pneumonia | Antibiotics + O₂ + fluids |
| PE | O₂ + anticoagulation |
| PTX | Chest drain |

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|
| **Clubbing** | Lung cancer, bronchiectasis, CF, abscess, mesothelioma |
| **Not clubbing** | Asthma, sarcoid (usually) |
| **Central cyanosis** | Hypoxaemia (respiratory/cardiac) |
| **Peripheral cyanosis** | Cold, shock (normal SpO₂) |
| **Methaemoglobinaemia** | Chocolate blood, methylene blue |
| **Tachypnoea** | Most sensitive distress sign |
| **Paradoxical breathing** | Diaphragmatic fatigue |
| **Position** | Sitting up, leaning forward |

## MCQs (10)

1. The most common respiratory cause of clubbing is:
   A. COPD
   B. **Bronchiectasis (suppurative lung disease)**
   C. Asthma
   D. Sarcoidosis
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Bronchiectasis.

2. COPD alone usually:
   A. Causes clubbing
   B. **Does not cause clubbing (look for another cause)**
   C. Always
   D. None
   E. Lung cancer
   **Answer: B** — Look for other cause.

3. The most sensitive sign of respiratory distress is:
   A. Cyanosis
   B. **Tachypnoea (RR >25)**
   C. Wheeze
   D. None
   E. Confusion
   **Answer: B** — Tachypnoea.

4. Paradoxical breathing (abdominal paradox) is a sign of:
   A. Asthma
   B. **Diaphragmatic fatigue**
   C. COPD
   D. Pneumonia
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Diaphragm fatigue.

5. Central cyanosis is best detected at:
   A. Fingers
   B. **Tongue, lips, oral mucosa**
   C. Earlobes
   D. None
   E. Toes
   **Answer: B** — Tongue, lips, mucosa.

6. Peripheral cyanosis is caused by:
   A. Lung disease
   B. **Sluggish circulation (cold, shock)**
   C. Heart disease
   D. None
   E. Cyanotic CHD
   **Answer: B** — Sluggish circulation.

7. Methaemoglobinaemia is treated with:
   A. O₂
   B. **Methylene blue**
   C. Naloxone
   D. Steroids
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Methylene blue.

8. Pursed-lip breathing in COPD is for:
   A. Speech
   B. **Maintaining airway patency (auto-PEEP)**
   C. Sleep
   D. None
   E. Exercise
   **Answer: B** — Airway patency.

9. The 5 cyanotic congenital heart diseases are the 5 Ts:
   A. **Tetralogy, Transposition, Tricuspid atresia, Truncus, TAPVR**
   B. ASD, VSD, PDA, AV canal, coarctation
   C. None
   D. ToF, TGA, MVP, MR, AR
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — 5 Ts.

10. Tracheal tug is a sign of:
    A. Pneumonia
    B. **Severe airflow obstruction (↑WOB)**
    C. Heart failure
    D. None
    E. Anaemia
    **Answer: B** — Severe obstruction.

## SBA Questions (10)

1. A 50-year-old with lung cancer and painful swollen joints. Diagnosis:
   A. RA
   B. **Hypertrophic osteoarthropathy**
   C. None
   D. Gout
   E. Infection
   **Answer: B** — HPOA (paraneoplastic).

2. Cyanotic CHD with differential cyanosis (upper cyanotic, lower pink) suggests:
   A. Tetralogy of Fallot
   B. **PDA with Eisenmenger (R→L shunt)**
   C. TGA
   D. None
   E. ASD
   **Answer: B** — PDA with Eisenmenger.

3. The first sign of respiratory failure in a child with asthma:
   A. Wheeze
   B. **Silent chest (worsening)**
   C. Cyanosis
   D. None
   E. Tachycardia
   **Answer: B** — Silent chest.

4. Methaemoglobinaemia shows:
   A. Cyanosis improved with O₂
   B. **Chocolate-brown blood, no response to O₂**
   C. Normal colour
   D. None
   E. Pink
   **Answer: B** — Chocolate blood.

5. A smoker with finger clubbing. Most likely:
   A. COPD alone
   B. **Lung cancer (until proven otherwise)**
   C. Asthma
   D. None
   E. Bronchitis
   **Answer: B** — Lung cancer.

6. The Schamroth test is positive in:
   A. Normal
   B. **Clubbing (loss of nail-fold angle)**
   C. Cyanosis
   D. None
   E. Asthma
   **Answer: B** — Loss of diamond.

7. Cyanosis in the lower extremities only (reverse differential) is seen in:
   A. PDA
   B. **TGA with PDA, pulmonary hypertension, or coarctation**
   C. ToF
   D. None
   E. VSD
   **Answer: B** — TGA with coarctation or PDA.

8. The most common cause of painful clubbing:
   A. Idiopathic
   B. **Lung cancer (hypertrophic osteoarthropathy)**
   C. Bronchiectasis
   D. None
   E. CF
   **Answer: B** — Lung cancer.

9. A 60-year-old with cyanosis and normal SpO₂ (probe on finger). Cause:
   A. Hypoxemia
   B. **Peripheral cyanosis (cold, shock) or methaemoglobinaemia**
   C. None
   D. Heart
   E. Lung
   **Answer: B** — Peripheral or methaemoglobin.

10. The earliest sign of acute respiratory failure is:
    A. Cyanosis
    B. **Tachypnoea**
    C. Confusion
    D. None
    E. Wheeze
    **Answer: B** — Tachypnoea.

## Summary

Clubbing: most common respiratory cause = bronchiectasis; smoker with clubbing = lung cancer. Cyanosis: central (tongue, mucosa) = hypoxaemia; peripheral (fingers) = sluggish circulation. Methaemoglobinaemia: chocolate blood, methylene blue. Distress: tachypnoea most sensitive; paradoxical breathing = diaphragmatic fatigue. Treat underlying cause.
