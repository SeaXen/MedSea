# Pleural fluid interpretation

> [!important]
> **Pleural fluid analysis** is essential to determine the cause of an effusion. **Light's criteria** separate transudate (systemic cause) from exudate (pleural/local cause). Fluid appearance, biochemistry, microbiology, and cytology guide specific management.

Related: [[Pleural Effusion]], [[Empyema and pleural infection]], [[Malignant pleural effusion]], [[Parapneumonic effusion]], [[Pneumothorax]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: **Light's criteria**: exudate = fluid/serum protein >0.5, OR fluid/serum LDH >0.6, OR fluid LDH >2/3 upper limit of serum normal. **Exudate** = infection, malignancy, PE, autoimmune. **Transudate** = heart failure, cirrhosis, nephrotic.

## Learning Objectives
- Differentiate transudate vs exudate (Light's criteria)
- Interpret pleural fluid appearance, pH, glucose, LDH, protein
- Apply to specific disease states
- Recognise complicated parapneumonic effusion and empyema
- Choose appropriate further investigations

## Pleural Fluid Formation & Circulation

- **Starling forces**: hydrostatic pressure (capillaries) vs oncotic pressure (plasma)
- **Production**: parietal pleura (capillary filtration)
- **Absorption**: visceral pleura (lymphatics)
- **Imbalance** → effusion (↑production or ↓absorption)

## Transudate vs Exudate

### Light's criteria
**Exudate** if any ONE of:
1. **Pleural fluid protein / serum protein >0.5**
2. **Pleural fluid LDH / serum LDH >0.6**
3. **Pleural fluid LDH >2/3 upper limit of normal serum LDH**

### Transudate causes
- Heart failure (most common)
- Hepatic cirrhosis
- Nephrotic syndrome
- Hypoalbuminaemia
- Peritoneal dialysis
- Constrictive pericarditis
- Atelectasis

### Exudate causes
- **Infection**: pneumonia (parapneumonic), empyema, TB, viral
- **Malignancy**: lung, breast, lymphoma, mesothelioma
- **PE** (often exudative)
- **Autoimmune**: RA, SLE, ABPA
- **Pancreatitis** (high amylase)
- **Drugs** (nitrofurantoin, methotrexate, amiodarone)
- **Post-cardiac injury syndrome** (Dressler)
- **Chylothorax** (triglycerides)

## Pleural Fluid Analysis

### Appearance
| Appearance | Significance |
|------------|--------------|
| **Clear/straw** | Transudate, early exudate |
| **Turbid/opaque** | Infection, inflammation, chylothorax |
| **Bloody** | Malignancy, trauma, PE, post-cardiac injury |
| **Frank blood** | Haemothorax (Hct >50% blood) |
| **Purulent** | Empyema |
| **White/milky** | Chylothorax (triglycerides), pseudochylothorax (cholesterol) |
| **Food particles** | Oesophageal rupture (Boerhaave) |
| **Black** | *Aspergillus* infection |
| **Anchovy paste** | Ruptured amoebic liver abscess |
| **Brown** | Chronic rupture |
| **Green** | *Pseudomonas* |

### Biochemistry
| Test | Transudate | Exudate | Specific |
|------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **Protein** | <30 g/L | >30 g/L | Light's |
| **LDH** | <200 IU/L | >200 IU/L | Light's |
| **Protein ratio** | <0.5 | >0.5 | Light's |
| **LDH ratio** | <0.6 | >0.6 | Light's |
| **Glucose** | Normal | ↓ (infection, RA) | <3.3 mmol/L: empyema, RA, TB |
| **pH** | Normal (7.60) | ↓ | <7.2: complicated parapneumonic, empyema, oesophageal rupture, RA |
| **Amylase** | Normal | ↑ | Pancreatitis, oesophageal rupture, malignancy |
| **Triglycerides** | — | — | >1.24 mmol/L (or 110 mg/dL): chylothorax |
| **Cholesterol** | — | — | >5.18 mmol/L: pseudochylothorax |
| **Bilirubin** | — | — | Fluid/serum >0.6: complicated parapneumonic |
| **Adenosine deaminase (ADA)** | — | — | >40 U/L: TB (in lymphocyte-predominant) |

### Cell count and differential
| Cell type | Significance |
|----------|--------------|
| **Mesothelial cells** | >5%: argues against TB |
| **Lymphocytes** | TB, malignancy, sarcoidosis, chylothorax |
| **Neutrophils** | Parapneumonic, PE, pancreatitis, subphrenic abscess |
| **Eosinophils** | Air/blood in pleura, drugs, asbestos, parasitic, malignancy |
| **Mesothelial cells** | Exudate |

### pH interpretation
| pH | Significance |
|----|--------------|
| **>7.30** | Normal/exudate, non-complicated |
| **7.20–7.30** | Borderline; consider drainage |
| **<7.20** | Complicated parapneumonic effusion, empyema, oesophageal rupture, RA, TB |
| **<6.0** | Oesophageal rupture (very low) |

## Specific Conditions

### Complicated parapneumonic effusion / Empyema
**Criteria for chest drain**:
- pH <7.2
- Glucose <3.3 mmol/L
- LDH >1000 IU/L
- Frank pus (empyema)
- Positive Gram stain/culture
- Loculated

### Malignant effusion
- Cytology positive in ~60% (single sample); higher with repeat
- Lymphocyte-predominant
- ↓glucose, ↓pH
- Often massive (>1500 mL on CXR)

### Tuberculous pleural effusion
- Lymphocyte-predominant
- **↑ADA** (>40 U/L; high sensitivity/specificity)
- ↑IFN-γ
- Mesothelial cells <5% (argues against)
- Culture positive in <20%

### Chylothorax
- Triglycerides >1.24 mmol/L (>110 mg/dL) OR chylomicrons
- Causes: trauma, surgery, malignancy (lymphoma), congenital
- Milky white appearance
- **Treatment**: drainage, low-fat diet, MCT, surgery (thoracic duct ligation)

### Pseudochylothorax
- Cholesterol crystals, ↑cholesterol
- Chronic (>5 years) effusion (TB, RA)
- Treatment: drainage, decortication

### Empyema
- Frank pus, pH <7.0, glucose very low, LDH very high
- 3 stages: exudative → fibrinopurulent → organising
- Treatment: antibiotics + chest drain ± fibrinolytics ± VATS decortication

### Haemothorax
- Fluid Hct >50% of blood Hct
- Trauma, iatrogenic, malignancy, PE
- Treatment: chest drain, VATS if large/ongoing

## Diagnostic Approach

### Initial
1. **History + exam** (cause)
2. **CXR** (laterality, size, meniscus)
3. **Pleural US** (guides tap, septations)
4. **Diagnostic thoracentesis** (Light's criteria + cell count + culture + cytology + pH + glucose + LDH + protein)

### Further (if exudate and not diagnosed)
1. **CT chest** (malignancy, PE, infection)
2. **Pleural biopsy** (image-guided or medical thoracoscopy) — for TB, malignancy
3. **Thoracoscopy** (VATS or medical) — best for malignancy
4. **Indwelling pleural catheter** — for recurrent malignant

## MCQs (10)

1. Light's criteria: exudate if:
   A. Protein ratio >0.3
   B. **Any one of: protein ratio >0.5, LDH ratio >0.6, fluid LDH >2/3 ULN**
   C. LDH <200
   D. Glucose >5
   E. pH >7.4
   **Answer: B** — Any 1 of 3 criteria.

2. Most common cause of transudative effusion:
   A. Cirrhosis
   B. **Heart failure**
   C. Malignancy
   D. PE
   E. TB
   **Answer: B** — Heart failure.

3. ADA >40 U/L in lymphocyte-predominant pleural fluid suggests:
   A. Malignancy
   B. **TB**
   C. Empyema
   D. RA
   E. PE
   **Answer: B** — TB.

4. Pleural fluid pH <7.2 suggests:
   A. Heart failure
   B. **Empyema, complicated parapneumonic, oesophageal rupture, RA**
   C. TB
   D. Malignancy
   E. Viral
   **Answer: B** — Complicated effusions.

5. Chylothorax is diagnosed by:
   A. pH <7.0
   B. **Triglycerides >1.24 mmol/L**
   C. ↑LDH
   D. ↑Protein
   E. ↑WBC
   **Answer: B** — Triglycerides.

6. Pleural fluid in RA typically shows:
   A. **↓Glucose + ↓pH + high LDH**
   B. ↑Glucose
   C. ↑Triglycerides
   D. Blood
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — RA: ↓glucose, ↓pH.

7. Pleural fluid in TB is typically:
   A. Neutrophil-predominant
   B. **Lymphocyte-predominant with ↑ADA**
   C. Eosinophil-predominant
   D. Mesothelial
   E. Acellular
   **Answer: B** — TB: lymphocyte + ↑ADA.

8. Frank pus in pleural cavity = empyema; immediate treatment:
   A. Antibiotics only
   B. **Antibiotics + chest drain (large-bore)**
   C. Observation
   D. Discharge
   E. Steroids
   **Answer: B** — Drain + antibiotics.

9. Pseudochylothorax is associated with:
   A. Trauma
   B. **Chronic (>5 years) effusion (TB, RA)**
   C. Acute
   D. Infection
   E. PE
   **Answer: B** — Chronic effusion.

10. Light's criteria all 3 negative. Pleural fluid is:
    A. Exudate
    B. **Transudate**
    C. Mixed
    D. Indeterminate
    E. None
    **Answer: B** — All 3 negative = transudate.

## SBA Questions (10)

1. A 70-year-old with CHF has bilateral effusions. Pleural tap: protein ratio 0.3, LDH ratio 0.4. Type:
   A. Exudate
   B. **Transudate (CHF)**
   C. Empyema
   D. Malignancy
   E. TB
   **Answer: B** — Transudate (CHF).

2. Pleural fluid is turbid white. Diagnosis:
   A. Empyema
   B. **Chylothorax (or pseudochylothorax)**
   C. RA
   D. TB
   E. Malignancy
   **Answer: B** — Milky = chyle or pseudochyle.

3. Complicated parapneumonic effusion diagnosis:
   A. Positive culture
   B. **pH <7.2 OR glucose <3.3 OR LDH >1000 OR frank pus**
   C. Lymphocytes
   D. ↑WBC
   E. Any effusion
   **Answer: B** — pH/glucose/LDH criteria.

4. Pleural fluid amylase ↑ in:
   A. TB
   B. **Pancreatitis, oesophageal rupture, malignancy**
   C. CHF
   D. PE
   E. RA
   **Answer: B** — Pancreas, oesophagus, malignancy.

5. A 60-year-old has pleural fluid pH 6.8, glucose 2.0. Most likely:
   A. Heart failure
   B. **Empyema or oesophageal rupture**
   C. TB
   D. Malignancy
   E. Viral
   **Answer: B** — Very low pH + glucose.

6. Best next step if Light's criteria positive (exudate) but cause unclear:
   A. Discharge
   B. **CT chest +/− pleural biopsy**
   C. Antibiotics
   D. Steroids
   E. Observation
   **Answer: B** — Further imaging + biopsy.

7. Most sensitive test for TB pleural effusion:
   A. ADA
   B. **Pleural biopsy (culture/histology)**
   C. Sputum
   D. CXR
   E. Pleural fluid cytology
   **Answer: B** — Pleural biopsy is most sensitive.

8. The most common cause of malignant pleural effusion:
   A. Breast
   B. **Lung cancer**
   C. Lymphoma
   D. Mesothelioma
   E. Ovarian
   **Answer: B** — Lung most common.

9. Bilateral effusions with normal-sized heart on CXR. Cause:
   A. Heart failure
   B. **Liver cirrhosis or nephrotic syndrome**
   C. Malignancy
   D. TB
   E. PE
   **Answer: B** — Bilateral effusions with normal heart suggest non-cardiac cause.

10. Empyema at 5 days (fibrinopurulent stage). Best treatment:
    A. Antibiotics only
    B. **Antibiotics + chest drain +/− intrapleural fibrinolytics (e.g. urokinase)**
    C. Surgery
    D. Discharge
    E. Wait
    **Answer: B** — Drain + fibrinolytics.

## Summary

Pleural fluid interpretation uses Light's criteria to differentiate transudate (CHF, cirrhosis, nephrotic) from exudate (infection, malignancy, PE, autoimmune). pH <7.2, glucose <3.3, LDH >1000 indicate complicated parapneumonic effusion/empyema. ADA >40 in lymphocyte-rich fluid = TB. Triglycerides >1.24 = chylothorax. Frank pus = empyema. Further workup with CT + pleural biopsy if exudate unclear.
