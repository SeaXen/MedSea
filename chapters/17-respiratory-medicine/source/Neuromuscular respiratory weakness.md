# Neuromuscular respiratory weakness

> [!important]
> **Neuromuscular (NM) disorders** can cause **acute or chronic ventilatory failure** through weakness of respiratory muscles (diaphragm, intercostals, accessory). Common causes include **GBS, MG, ALS, muscular dystrophies, myositis**. **Serial monitoring** of vital capacity, NIF, and ABG is essential — intubate early before crisis.

Related: [[Respiratory Failure]], [[Oxygen Therapy and NIV]], [[Indications for intubation in respiratory disease]], [[Type 2 respiratory failure]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: **"20/30/40 rule"** for GBS: **VC <20 mL/kg, MIP <30 cmH₂O, MEP <40 cmH₂O** = severe respiratory failure → ICU + consider intubation. GBS patients with rapid progression should be admitted to HDU/ITU early and monitored closely. **MG crisis** = bulbar weakness + respiratory failure = plasma exchange or IVIG + ventilation.

## Definition

**Neuromuscular respiratory weakness** = respiratory failure from weakness of respiratory muscles due to NM disorders.

## Common Causes

| Acute | Subacute | Chronic |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **GBS** (Guillain-Barré) | Myasthenia gravis (crisis) | ALS |
| **Botulism** | Polymyositis/dermatomyositis | Muscular dystrophy (Duchenne) |
| **Organophosphate poisoning** | Lambert-Eaton | Myotonic dystrophy |
| **Tetanus** | Critical illness myopathy | CIDP |
| **Spinal cord injury (high)** | Myasthenia gravis | Post-polio |
| **Myasthenic crisis** | | Kyphoscoliosis-related |
| | | Pompe disease |

## Pathophysiology

### Respiratory muscle weakness
- **Diaphragm** (C3–C5) — primary inspiratory muscle
- **Intercostals, scalenes, SCM** — accessory
- **Abdominal muscles** — expiration, cough
- **Upper airway muscles** (genioglossus, etc.) — maintain patency

### Consequences
- **↓Tidal volume** → ↓minute ventilation
- **↓Cough strength** → atelectasis, infection
- **Bulbar weakness** → aspiration
- **Diaphragmatic paralysis** → orthopnoea
- **Progression** to respiratory failure

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- **Dyspnoea** (especially supine/orthopnoea)
- **Weak cough**
- **Difficulty clearing secretions**
- **Weakness** (proximal, distal, or both)
- **Bulbar symptoms** (dysphagia, dysarthria, nasal speech)
- **Fatigue, sleep disturbance**

### Signs
- **Tachypnoea** (compensation for ↓VT)
- **Tachycardia**
- **Use of accessory muscles**
- **Paradoxical breathing** (abdominal paradox — diaphragmatic fatigue)
- **Weak cough** (peak cough flow <270 L/min)
- **Bulbar weakness** (pooled secretions, weak swallow)
- **Diaphragmatic weakness** (paradoxical abdominal movement on sniffing)
- **Respiratory failure** (late)

## Diagnosis

### Bedside
- **Vital capacity (VC)**: ↓ (<20 mL/kg in adults)
- **MIP (Max Inspiratory Pressure)**: ↓ (less negative; <30 cmH₂O)
- **MEP (Max Expiratory Pressure)**: ↓ (<40 cmH₂O)
- **Sniff nasal inspiratory pressure (SNIP)**: ↓
- **Peak cough flow (PCF)**: ↓ (<270 L/min → weak cough)

### 20/30/40 rule (GBS)
- VC <20 mL/kg
- MIP <30 cmH₂O
- MEP <40 cmH₂O
- **= severe respiratory failure → ITU/intubate**

### ABG
- **↓PaO₂ + ↑PaCO₂** (hypercapnic respiratory failure)
- Initially normal; ↓pH late

### Investigations
| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **CXR** | Atelectasis, infection, normal diaphragm position |
| **Fluoroscopic sniff test** | Diaphragmatic movement (paralysis) |
| **Diaphragm US** | Thickening, excursion |
| **Nerve conduction studies** | GBS (demyelination), NMJ disorders |
| **Anti-AChR antibodies** | MG |
| **Anti-MuSK antibodies** | MG (seronegative AChR) |
| **Anti-GQ1b** | Miller Fisher syndrome |
| **CK, aldolase** | Inflammatory myopathy |
| **Spinal MRI** | Cord lesion |
| **Genetic testing** | Muscular dystrophy, ALS |

## Management

### Specific treatment (by cause)
- **GBS**: IVIG or plasma exchange (PE); NO steroids
- **MG crisis**: IVIG or plasma exchange + cholinesterase inhibitor (pyridostigmine)
- **Polymyositis/DM**: steroids, immunosuppression
- **Botulism**: antitoxin + supportive
- **OP poisoning**: atropine + pralidoxime
- **Tetanus**: metronidazole + immunoglobulin + supportive
- **Spinal cord**: supportive +/− surgery

### Respiratory support
- **Admit to HDU/ITU early** for monitoring
- **Frequent VC/NIF** (4-hourly)
- **Position**: sitting up (reduces diaphragm workload)
- **Chest physiotherapy** + assisted cough
- **NIV** for early respiratory failure (pH 7.25–7.35)
- **Intubate + ventilate** if VC <20 mL/kg, MIP <30, MEP <40, or pH <7.25
- **Tracheostomy** if prolonged ventilation expected (>7–10 days)
- **Suctioning** (cough assist device)

### Bulbar management
- **NBM (nil by mouth)** if aspiration risk
- **NG tube** for feeding
- **PEG** if prolonged
- **Speech and language therapy**

### Prevention
- **Vaccinations** (flu, pneumococcal)
- **Avoid sedatives** (worsen respiratory depression)
- **Avoid neuromuscular blocking drugs** if possible
- **Physiotherapy** (clearance, strength)

## Disease-specific pearls

### GBS
- **Post-infectious** (Campylobacter, CMV, EBV, *Mycoplasma*)
- Ascending paralysis, areflexia
- **Albuminocytological dissociation** (↑protein, normal cells in CSF)
- Anti-ganglioside antibodies
- Treatment: **IVIG or PE** (equivalent)
- Monitor in HDU/ITU (rapid progression)
- ~30% require ventilation

### MG
- **Autoimmune** (anti-AChR antibodies) → NMJ dysfunction
- **Fluctuating weakness**, worse with activity, better with rest
- **Ocular** (ptosis, diplopia) early
- **Myasthenic crisis** = severe weakness + respiratory failure
- Treatment: **pyridostigmine** (cholinesterase inhibitor), **steroids, immunosuppression**, **IVIG/PE** in crisis
- **Avoid**: aminoglycosides, fluoroquinolones, beta-blockers (worsen)
- **Lambert-Eaton**: proximal weakness, ↓with activity (paraneoplastic, often SCLC)

### ALS
- **Upper + lower motor neuron** signs
- Progressive bulbar, limb, respiratory
- **No specific treatment**; riluzole, edaravone (modest benefit)
- **NIV** prolongs survival
- **PEG** for nutrition
- Tracheostomy often needed

### Muscular dystrophy (Duchenne)
- **X-linked recessive**
- Progressive proximal weakness
- **Scoliosis + cardiomyopathy** + respiratory failure
- NIV + cough assist
- Steroids slow progression

### Polymyositis/Dermatomyositis
- Inflammatory myopathy
- **Symmetric proximal weakness**
- ↑CK, abnormal EMG, muscle biopsy diagnostic
- Treatment: **steroids**, immunosuppression

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|

| **GBS Rx** | IVIG or PE; NO steroids |
| **MG Rx** | Pyridostigmine + IVIG/PE in crisis |

## MCQs (10)

1. The 20/30/40 rule in GBS refers to:
   A. **VC <20 mL/kg, MIP <30 cmH₂O, MEP <40 cmH₂O**
   B. VC >20, MIP >30, MEP >40
   C. P/F ratio
   D. ABG
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — 20/30/40.

2. First-line treatment of GBS:
   A. **IVIG or plasma exchange**
   B. Steroids
   C. Antibiotics
   D. Aspirin
   E. Surgery
   **Answer: A** — IVIG or PE (not steroids).

3. Diaphragm is innervated by:
   A. Vagus
   B. **Phrenic nerve (C3–C5)**
   C. Intercostal nerves
   D. Sympathetic
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Phrenic.

4. MG is caused by antibodies against:
   A. **Acetylcholine receptors (postsynaptic NMJ)**
   B. Voltage-gated Ca channels
   C. Muscle
   D. Nerve
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — Anti-AChR.

5. Lambert-Eaton is associated with:
   A. Thymoma
   B. **Small cell lung cancer (paraneoplastic)**
   C. Breast
   D. Lymphoma
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — SCLC paraneoplastic.

6. GBS is associated with CSF finding:
   A. **Albuminocytological dissociation (↑protein, normal cells)**
   B. Pleocytosis
   C. ↓Glucose
   D. Blood
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — Classic.

7. A NM patient has VC 18 mL/kg, MIP 25 cmH₂O, MEP 35 cmH₂O. Action:
   A. Discharge
   B. **Admit ITU + consider intubation (20/30/40)**
   C. Wait
   D. Antibiotics
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Severe → ITU.

8. MG is treated acutely with:
   A. **Pyridostigmine + IVIG/PE in crisis**
   B. Steroids alone
   C. Antibiotics
   D. Surgery
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — Pyridostigmine ± IVIG/PE.

9. Bulbar weakness in MG requires:
   A. **NBM, NG/PEG feeding**
   B. Normal diet
   C. Fluids only
   D. Soft diet
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — NBM/NG/PEG.

10. Drugs that worsen MG include:
    A. **Aminoglycosides, fluoroquinolones, beta-blockers**
    B. Paracetamol
    C. Aspirin
    D. Steroids
    E. None
    **Answer: A** — Avoid.

## SBA Questions (10)

1. The sniff test is used to diagnose:
   A. PE
   B. **Diaphragmatic paralysis**
   C. MG
   D. GBS
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Diaphragm paralysis.

2. Peak cough flow <270 L/min indicates:
   A. Normal
   B. **Ineffective cough (aspiration risk)**
   C. ARDS
   D. Asthma
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Weak cough.

3. A 35-year-old with GBS has descending paralysis. Wait, GBS is:
   A. Descending
   B. **Ascending (but can be Miller Fisher with descending cranial nerves)**
   C. Random
   D. Pure motor
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Ascending (Miller Fisher variant: descending).

4. The best initial bedside test of respiratory muscle strength:
   A. ABG
   B. **Vital capacity (spirometry)**
   C. CXR
   D. Echo
   E. SpO₂
   **Answer: B** — VC.

5. In GBS, autonomic dysfunction may cause:
   A. Stable BP
   B. **Labile BP, bradycardia, ileus**
   C. Hyperthermia
   D. None
   E. Bradycardia only
   **Answer: B** — Autonomic dysfunction.

6. A NM patient with bulbar weakness and aspiration risk. First:
   A. **NBM, NG tube, suction, ITU**
   B. Soft diet
   C. Antibiotics
   D. Steroids
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — NBM, NG, suction.

7. The most common antecedent infection of GBS is:
   A. EBV
   B. **Campylobacter jejuni**
   C. HIV
   D. CMV
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Campylobacter.

8. ALS respiratory failure is best managed by:
   A. Steroids
   B. **NIV early + cough assist + consider tracheostomy**
   C. IVIG
   D. Antibiotics
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — NIV + supportive.

9. Duchenne muscular dystrophy respiratory failure:
   A. Steroids cure
   B. **NIV + cough assist + scoliosis surgery + cardiac management**
   C. Antibiotics
   D. IVIG
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Multidisciplinary.

10. GBS is typically treated within:
    A. 24 h of onset
    B. **As soon as possible (within 2 weeks of onset)**
    C. After 1 month
    D. Never
    E. None
    **Answer: B** — ASAP within 2 weeks.

## Summary

NM respiratory weakness → progressive respiratory failure. 20/30/40 rule (VC <20, MIP <30, MEP <40) = severe → ITU/intubate. GBS: IVIG or PE (no steroids). MG: pyridostigmine + IVIG/PE in crisis. Bulbar weakness → NBM, NG, PEG. NIV for early failure; intubate when criteria met. Monitor in HDU/ITU.
