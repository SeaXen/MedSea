# Indications for invasive ventilation in respiratory disease

> [!important]
> **Invasive mechanical ventilation** is required when non-invasive support (O₂, HFNC, NIV) is insufficient or contraindicated. The decision to intubate is based on **clinical signs + ABG + trajectory** rather than a single number. Key indications include **cardiopulmonary arrest, severe hypoxaemia, hypercapnic acidosis, exhaustion, and airway protection**.

Related: [[Respiratory Failure]], [[Oxygen Therapy and NIV]], [[NIV failure and escalation triggers]], [[ARDS]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: **GCS ≤8, intubate**. The classic indications are: (1) respiratory arrest/apnoea, (2) severe hypoxaemia (PaO₂ <60 mmHg on high FiO₂), (3) severe acidosis (pH <7.20, ↑PaCO₂), (4) respiratory muscle exhaustion, (5) airway protection (GCS <8, severe bulbar dysfunction), (6) failure of NIV.

## Learning Objectives
- List indications for invasive ventilation
- Recognise clinical signs of impending respiratory failure
- Apply ABG criteria
- Identify contraindications to NIV
- Plan intubation sequence
- Manage post-intubation

## Definition

**Invasive mechanical ventilation** = positive pressure ventilation delivered via **endotracheal tube** (or tracheostomy).

## Indications

### Absolute indications
1. **Cardiopulmonary arrest** — intubate immediately
2. **Respiratory arrest / apnoea** — bag-mask, intubate
3. **Severe hypoxaemia** — PaO₂ <60 mmHg (8 kPa) on high FiO₂ (P/F <200)
4. **Severe hypercapnic acidosis** — pH <7.20, ↑PaCO₂ despite NIV
5. **Severe respiratory muscle exhaustion** — paradoxical breathing, decreasing conscious level
6. **Airway protection** — GCS ≤8, severe bulbar dysfunction (GBS, MG, stroke)
7. **Massive haemoptysis** with airway compromise
8. **Upper airway obstruction** (FB, angioedema)
9. **Failed NIV** — pH <7.20, ↑RR, ↓GCS, exhaustion
10. **Severe shock with respiratory failure**

### Relative indications
- Impending respiratory failure (tachypnoea >35, exhaustion)
- ↓Mental status (GCS <10) with risk of aspiration
- Severe pneumonia with rapid deterioration
- ARDS with P/F <200
- Massive PE with shock
- Severe asthma not responding to therapy

### Specific disease indications
| Disease | Specific indication |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Severe asthma** | ↑PaCO₂ + exhaustion, silent chest, ↑RR |
| **COPD (AECOPD)** | NIV failure (pH <7.25), GCS ↓, shock |
| **ARDS** | P/F <200 + exhaustion |
| **Pneumonia** | Sepsis + respiratory failure, GCS ↓ |
| **PE (massive)** | Shock + respiratory failure |
| **NM weakness (GBS, MG)** | VC <20 mL/kg, GCS ↓, aspiration |

## Clinical Assessment

### Signs of impending respiratory failure
- **↑Respiratory rate** (>30/min, or paradoxically slowing)
- **Accessory muscle use** (SCM, scalenes)
- **Paradoxical breathing** (abdominal paradox — diaphragmatic fatigue)
- **Tracheal tug**
- **Cyanosis**
- **Confusion, agitation, drowsiness** (hypoxaemia/hypercapnia)
- **Haemodynamic instability** (arrhythmia, hypotension)
- **Diaphoresis**

### ABG criteria
| Parameter | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| **PaO₂** | <60 mmHg (8 kPa) on FiO₂ ≥0.5 (or P/F <200) |
| **PaCO₂** | >60 mmHg (8 kPa) with pH <7.20 |
| **pH** | <7.20 (especially rising PaCO₂) |
| **SpO₂** | <90% despite high FiO₂ |

## NIV Failure → Intubation

| Sign | Implication |
|------|-------------|
| **pH <7.20** | Severe acidosis |
| **pH falling** despite NIV | Worsening |
| **PaCO₂ rising** | Ventilatory failure |
| **RR >30** | Persisting tachypnoea |
| **GCS dropping** | Hypercapnic encephalopathy |
| **Tolerance failure** | Mask intolerance, agitation |
| **Exhaustion** | Diaphragmatic fatigue |

## Pre-Intubation Checklist

### Equipment
- Suction (working)
- Bag-valve-mask (BVM) + O₂
- Laryngoscope (Mac 3–4, Miller 2–3)
- Endotracheal tubes (7.0, 7.5, 8.0 mm)
- Bougie, stylet
- Capnography
- Monitoring (SpO₂, ECG, BP)
- Emergency drugs (succinylcholine, rocuronium, propofol, ketamine, fentanyl)

### Drugs (Rapid Sequence Induction)
- **Preoxygenation**: 100% O₂ for 3–5 min
- **Induction**: propofol 1.5–2.5 mg/kg, ketamine 1–2 mg/kg, or etomidate 0.3 mg/kg
- **Paralysis**: succinylcholine 1.5 mg/kg (rapid onset, short) or rocuronium 1.2 mg/kg (longer, but reversible with sugammadex)
- **Cricoid pressure** (controversial)

### Difficult airway predictors
- Previous difficult intubation
- Mallampati III–IV
- Limited mouth opening, neck mobility
- Obstructive sleep apnoea
- Obesity, large neck

## Post-Intubation Management

### Initial ventilator settings
| Parameter | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| **Mode** | Volume-control (AC/VC) or pressure-control (PC) |
| **Tidal volume** | 6–8 mL/kg (lung-protective; 4–6 in ARDS) |
| **RR** | 12–20/min |
| **FiO₂** | Titrate to SpO₂ 92–96% (88–92% in COPD) |
| **PEEP** | 5–10 cmH₂O (higher in ARDS) |
| **I:E ratio** | 1:2 (longer expiration in obstructive) |
| **PEEP/FiO₂ table** | ARDSnet low-tidal volume protocol |

### Monitoring
- **Capnography** (ETCO₂)
- **Pulse oximetry**
- **ABG** within 30–60 min
- **Ventilator waveforms** (pressure, flow, volume loops)
- **CXR** (post-intubation, daily)
- **Sedation score** (RASS)

### Sedation
- **Light sedation** preferred (RASS 0 to -2)
- **Propofol** (short, ↑TG with prolonged)
- **Dexmedetomidine** (less respiratory depression)
- **Midazolam** (accumulation in renal failure)
- **Fentanyl/morphine** (analgesia)

## Complications of Invasive Ventilation

| Complication | Mechanism | Management |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| **VAP** | Aspiration, colonisation | Head elevation, oral care, sedation holiday |
| **Barotrauma** | High pressures | ↓Pplat, lung-protective |
| **Volutrauma** | High volumes | ↓VT (6 mL/kg) |
| **Atelectrauma** | Repetitive collapse | PEEP |
| **O₂ toxicity** | High FiO₂ | ↓FiO₂ (target <0.6) |
| **VILI** | Multi-factorial | Lung-protective |
| **Hemodynamic compromise** | ↑Intrathoracic pressure, ↓preload | Fluids, vasopressors |
| **Aspiration** | ETT bypasses upper airway | Cuff pressure, OG tube |
| **Delirium** | ICU environment | Sleep-wake cycle, antipsychotics |
| **Critical illness myopathy** | NM blockers, steroids | Avoid excessive |
| **Tracheal stenosis** | Cuff pressure | Monitor cuff <30 cmH₂O |
| **Sinusitis** | Nasal ETT | Switch to oral |

## Weaning

### Criteria
- Underlying cause resolving
- **SpO₂ >90% on FiO₂ ≤0.4, PEEP ≤5**
- **RSBI (Rapid Shallow Breathing Index) <105** (RR/TV)
- Hemodynamic stability
- Adequate mental status
- Able to protect airway

### Spontaneous breathing trial (SBT)
- T-piece or PSV 5–8 cmH₂O for 30–120 min
- Monitor RR, SpO₂, ABG
- **Success** → extubate
- **Failure** (RR >35, SpO₂ <90, distress, ↑PaCO₂) → re-ventilate

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|
| **"GCS 8, intubate"** | Airway protection |
| **Absolute indications** | Arrest, severe hypoxaemia, pH <7.20, exhaustion, airway protection |
| **NIV failure criteria** | pH <7.20, ↑PaCO₂, exhaustion, GCS ↓ |
| **Initial ventilator settings** | VT 6–8 mL/kg, RR 12–20, PEEP 5–10, FiO₂ titrated |
| **Lung-protective ventilation** | VT 6 mL/kg, plateau <30 cmH₂O (ARDS) |
| **Capnography** | Confirms ETT placement |
| **Complications** | VAP, barotrauma, O₂ toxicity, delirium |
| **Weaning** | RSBI <105, SBT 30–120 min, extubate |

## MCQs (10)

1. The classic threshold for intubation in altered mental status is:
   A. GCS <12
   B. **GCS ≤8**
   C. GCS <10
   D. GCS <14
   E. GCS <6
   **Answer: B** — GCS ≤8, intubate.

2. Absolute indication for intubation:
   A. Mild hypoxaemia
   B. **Cardiopulmonary arrest**
   C. Mild dyspnoea
   D. Tachypnoea alone
   E. Cough
   **Answer: B** — Arrest.

3. NIV failure is indicated by:
   A. Stable pH
   B. **pH <7.20, ↑PaCO₂, exhaustion**
   C. ↓RR
   D. Improved SpO₂
   E. Comfortable patient
   **Answer: B** — Acidosis + exhaustion.

4. Initial tidal volume in ARDS (lung-protective):
   A. 12 mL/kg
   B. 10 mL/kg
   C. **6 mL/kg**
   D. 4 mL/kg
   E. 15 mL/kg
   **Answer: C** — 6 mL/kg.

5. RSBI (Rapid Shallow Breathing Index) is calculated as:
   A. RR × TV
   B. **RR / TV (litres)**
   C. TV / RR
   D. RR + TV
   E. SpO₂ / RR
   **Answer: B** — RR/TV.

6. RSBI <105 predicts:
   A. Weaning failure
   B. **Weaning success**
   C. ARDS
   D. PE
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Success.

7. Plateau pressure target in ARDS:
   A. <50 cmH₂O
   B. **<30 cmH₂O**
   C. >40 cmH₂O
   D. 60 cmH₂O
   E. No target
   **Answer: B** — <30 cmH₂O.

8. Capnography in intubation confirms:
   A. Heart rate
   B. **ETT placement in trachea (ETCO₂)**
   C. BP
   D. SpO₂
   E. RR
   **Answer: B** — ETCO₂ detection.

9. First-line drug for rapid sequence intubation in haemodynamic instability:
   A. Propofol
   B. **Ketamine (preserves BP)**
   C. Thiopentone
   D. Etomidate only
   E. Midazolam
   **Answer: B** — Ketamine.

10. Ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP) prevention includes:
    A. Supine position
    B. **Head elevation 30–45° + oral care + sedation holiday**
    C. Routine antibiotics
    D. Steroids
    E. None
    **Answer: B** — Bundle.

## Summary

Invasive ventilation is required for cardiopulmonary arrest, severe hypoxaemia (P/F <200), severe acidosis (pH <7.20), exhaustion, airway protection (GCS ≤8), NIV failure. Initial settings: VT 6–8 mL/kg, RR 12–20, PEEP 5–10. Lung-protective (VT 6 mL/kg, plateau <30 cmH₂O) in ARDS. Complications: VAP, barotrauma, O₂ toxicity, delirium. Weaning: RSBI <105, SBT 30–120 min.
