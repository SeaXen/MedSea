# Chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension (CTEPH)

> [!important]
> **CTEPH** is a form of **pulmonary hypertension** (Group 4) caused by **organised, unresolved thromboembolic obstruction** of pulmonary arteries after PE. It is the **only potentially curable form of PH** via **pulmonary endarterectomy (PTE)**. All patients with persistent dyspnoea after PE should be screened.

Related: [[Pulmonary Embolism]], [[Pulmonary hypertension]], [[Cor pulmonale]], [[D-dimer, CTPA, and Wells score framework]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: Diagnose CTEPH with **V/Q scan** (gold standard — mismatched perfusion defects) in any patient with persistent dyspnoea ≥3 months after PE despite anticoagulation. Confirm with **RHC** + **CTPA** (or DSA). Treatment: **lifelong anticoagulation + PTE if operable**; **riociguat** if inoperable/persistent; **BPA** as adjunct.

## 1. Definition

**CTEPH** = chronic, organised, thromboembolic obstruction of pulmonary arteries leading to **pre-capillary PH** (mPAP >20 mmHg, PCWP ≤15 mmHg), typically after ≥3 months of effective anticoagulation.

## 2. Pathophysiology

### Mechanism
1. **Acute PE** (often clinically silent in 25–50%)
2. **Failure of resolution** → **organised fibrotic thrombi** lining pulmonary arteries
3. **Webs, bands, intimal irregularities** in PA
4. **Distal microvascular remodelling** (secondary)
5. **↑PVR** → PH → RV failure

### Risk factors for CTEPH
- **Previous PE** (most important; 0.5–3% of PE develop CTEPH)
- **Recurrent PE**
- **Large/proximal PE**
- **Persistent PH after PE**
- **Indwelling catheters, pacemakers**
- **Thrombophilia** (especially antiphospholipid syndrome)
- **Cancer**
- **Chronic inflammation** (osteomyelitis, IBD)
- **Splenectomy**
- **High-dose thyroxine**

## 3. Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- **Progressive exertional dyspnoea** (most common)
- **Fatigue, lethargy**
- **Exercise intolerance**
- **Syncope / pre-syncope** (severe)
- **Chest pain** (RV ischaemia)
- **Haemoptysis** (rare)
- **Palpitations**

### Signs
- **Loud P2**
- **RV heave**
- **JVP ↑** (with prominent a wave)
- **Tricuspid regurgitation murmur**
- **Pulsatile liver**
- **Peripheral oedema** (late)
- **Signs of DVT** (in 30%)
- **Cyanosis**

## 4. Diagnosis

### Investigation algorithm
1. **Suspect** CTEPH in any patient with persistent dyspnoea ≥3 months after PE despite anticoagulation
2. **V/Q scan** — **gold standard screening test**; mismatched perfusion defects
3. **CTPA** (dual-energy) — confirms organised thrombi, web/band morphology
4. **RHC** — confirms PH (mPAP >20), measures PVR, assesses operability
5. **Pulmonary angiography (DSA)** — for surgical planning

### Diagnostic criteria
- mPAP >20 mmHg (RHC)
- PCWP ≤15 mmHg (pre-capillary)
- PVR >2 WU
- Mismatched perfusion defects on V/Q
- ≥3 months effective anticoagulation
- **No other explanation for PH**

### Differential diagnosis
- Idiopathic PAH
- PH due to left heart, lung disease
- Pulmonary artery sarcoma (rare)
- Fibrosing mediastinitis
- Takayasu arteritis

## 5. Management

### Anticoagulation
- **Lifelong** in all CTEPH patients
- **DOACs or warfarin** (target INR 2–3)
- Continue even after PTE

### Pulmonary endarterectomy (PTE)
- **Curative** in operable patients
- **Indication**: accessible, organised thrombi; reasonable surgical risk
- **Outcome**: significant haemodynamic improvement; 90% 3-year survival
- **Operability assessment**: multidisciplinary (surgeon + PH physician + radiologist)

### Riociguat (sGC stimulator)
- **First-line medical therapy** for inoperable or persistent post-PTE CTEPH
- Dose 0.5–2.5 mg TDS
- Improves 6MWD, PVR, WHO class

### Balloon pulmonary angioplasty (BPA)
- **Adjunct or alternative** to PTE for inoperable/distal disease
- Multidisciplinary centres
- Improves haemodynamics and symptoms

### Supportive
- **O₂** (target >90%)
- **Diuretics** (oedema)
- **Rehabilitation**
- **Vaccinations**

## 6. Prognosis
- Without treatment: progressive RV failure, 5-year survival ~30% (if mPAP >40)
- With PTE: 90% 3-year survival
- With riociguat: improved exercise capacity, PVR

## 7. Screening after PE
- Persistent dyspnoea ≥3 months after PE → echo + V/Q
- High-risk groups (antiphospholipid, recurrent PE, large clot burden) → consider echo at 3–6 months

## 9. MCQs (10)

   A. 1
   B. 2
   C. 3
   D. **4**
   E. 5
   **Answer: D** — Group 4 (PA obstruction).

   A. CTPA
   B. **V/Q scan**
   C. RHC
   D. Echo
   E. D-dimer
   **Answer: B** — V/Q scan.

3. CTEPH is potentially curable by:
   A. Lung transplant
   B. **Pulmonary endarterectomy (PTE)**
   C. Riociguat
   D. Vasodilators
   E. Anticoagulation only
   **Answer: B** — PTE.

   A. Bosentan
   B. **Riociguat**
   C. Sildenafil
   D. Epoprostenol

   A. 1 week
   B. 1 month

   **Answer: C** — ≥3 months effective anticoagulation.

   A. 50%
   B. 25%
   C. **0.5–3%**
   D. 0.01%

   B. **Soluble guanylate cyclase (sGC) stimulation**

10. BPA (balloon pulmonary angioplasty) is used in CTEPH for:

    **Answer: B** — Inoperable.
