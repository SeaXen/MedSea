# Chest X-Ray approach

> [!important]
> **CXR is the most commonly performed imaging** in respiratory medicine. Systematic interpretation (A–B–C–D–E: Airway, Bones, Cardiac, Diaphragm, Effusion, Fields, Gastric bubble, Hilar) prevents missed findings. Always compare with previous films.

Related: [[CT chest pattern approach]], [[Pneumonia]], [[Pleural Effusion]], [[Pneumothorax]], [[Lung Cancer]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: **Systematic approach** = **A-B-C-D-E-F-G-H**: **A**irway (trachea, carina), **B**ones, **C**ardiac, **D**iaphragm, **E**ffusion, **F**ields (lungs), **G**astric bubble, **H**ilar. **Always compare with prior films**. **Don't miss pneumothorax** (look for lung edge, deep sulcus sign, absence of lung markings). **Don't miss consolidation** (silhouette sign).

## 1. Definition

**Chest X-ray (CXR)** = plain radiograph of the chest; PA (posteroanterior) preferred over AP.

## 2. Standard Approach

### A–B–C–D–E–F–G–H

| Step | Component | Look for |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **A** | **A**irway | Trachea position, carina, narrowing |
| **B** | **B**ones | Ribs, clavicles, spine, fractures, lesions |
| **C** | **C**ardiac | Size (cardiothoracic ratio <0.5), shape, borders |
| **D** | **D**iaphragm | Position, shape, free air under |
| **E** | **E**ffusion (pleural) | Costophrenic angle blunting, meniscus |
| **F** | **F**ields (lungs) | Consolidation, mass, collapse, vessels |
| **H** | **H**ilar | Size, position, density, lymphadenopathy |

### Additional
- **Mediastinum**: width, position, masses
- **Soft tissues**: subcutaneous emphysema, breast shadows
- **Bones**: ribs, clavicles, scapulae, spine
- **Foreign bodies**: tubes, lines, pacemakers
- **Comparison**: previous films

## 3. Technical Assessment

### Quality
- **Rotation**: medial ends of clavicles equidistant from spinous processes
- **Inspiration**: ≥6 anterior ribs above diaphragm (good inspiration)
- **Penetration**: vertebral bodies just visible behind heart
- **Coverage**: apices to costophrenic angles

### Type
- **PA** (preferred; upright, full inspiration)
- **AP** (supine, less accurate for heart size)
- **Lateral** (localises lesions)

## 4. Common Patterns

### Pneumothorax
- **Absent lung markings** distal to visceral pleural line
- **Deep sulcus sign** (deep costophrenic angle)
- **Mediastinal shift** (tension PTX)
- **Tracheal deviation** (away, in tension)

### Pleural effusion
- **Costophrenic angle blunting** (>200 mL needed for visible blunting)
- **Meniscus sign**
- **Opacification** of hemithorax if large
- **Loculated** effusion (linear, lentiform)

### Consolidation (pneumonia)
- **Homogeneous opacification**
- **Air bronchograms**
- **Silhouette sign** (loss of normal border)
- **Lobar distribution**

### Lobar collapse
- **Direct signs**: displacement of fissures
- **Indirect signs**: ↓volume, mediastinal shift toward collapse, ↑diaphragm, rib crowding
- **Patterns**: RUL (raised horizontal fissure), LUL (veil sign), RML (blurred R heart border), LLL (sail sign, ↓L diaphragm), RLL (↓R diaphragm)

### Mass / nodule
- **Solitary pulmonary nodule** (<3 cm)
- **Mass** (>3 cm)
- **Cavitation**, **calcification**, **spiculation**
- **Comparison** with prior (stability vs growth)

### Heart failure
- **Cardiomegaly** (CT ratio >0.5 on PA)
- **Pulmonary oedema** (bat-wing, Kerley B lines, effusions)
- **Upper lobe diversion** (cephalisation)
- **Pleural effusions** (often bilateral)

### Hilar enlargement
- **Lymphadenopathy** (TB, sarcoid, lymphoma, malignancy)
- **Vascular** (PH)
- **Unilateral**: consider TB, lung cancer
- **Bilateral**: sarcoid, lymphoma, mets

## 5. Common Clinical Scenarios

### Acute dyspnoea
- **Pneumothorax** (absent markings, lung edge)
- **Pneumonia** (consolidation, air bronchograms)
- **Pulmonary oedema** (bat-wing, effusions, cardiomegaly)
- **PE** (often normal; Westermark sign, Hampton's hump)
- **Pleural effusion** (blunting, meniscus)

### Chronic cough
- **Mass, nodule** (consider lung cancer)
- **Lymphadenopathy** (TB, sarcoid, lymphoma)
- **Fibrosis** (upper: TB, sarcoid; lower: IPF)

### Haemoptysis
- **Mass, nodule** (cancer)
- **Cavitation** (TB, aspergilloma, cancer)
- **Consolidation** (pneumonia)
- **Bronchiectasis** (tramlines, ring shadows)

## 6. Silhouette Sign

### Concept
- **Loss of normal silhouette** between adjacent structures of similar density
- Localises disease

### Examples
| Lost silhouette | Lobe involved |
|----------------|----------------|
| **Right heart border** | Right middle lobe |
| **Left heart border** | Lingula |
| **Right hemidiaphragm** | Right lower lobe |
| **Left hemidiaphragm** | Left lower lobe |
| **Aortic knob** | Left upper lobe (apicoposterior) |
| **SVC** | Right upper lobe |

## 7. Red Flags on CXR
- **Pneumothorax** (especially tension)
- **Mediastinal shift** (mass effect, tension)
- **Mass or nodule** (especially >2 cm, growing)
- **New pleural effusion** (esp. unilateral)
- **Lymphadenopathy** (esp. mediastinal)
- **Wide mediastinum** (dissection, mass)
- **Pneumomediastinum** (subcutaneous emphysema)

## 8. Common Pitfalls
- **Poor inspiration** → apparent cardiomegaly
- **Rotation** → mediastinal shift
- **Skin folds, hair, clothing** → mimic PTX, mass
- **Breast shadows** → apparent opacities
- **Missed retrocardiac lesions** (LLL behind heart)
- **Missed apical lesions** (need apical views)
- **Missed subdiaphragmatic free air** (perforation)
- **Missed bone lesions** (ribs, spine)

## 9. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|
| **Approach** | A-B-C-D-E-F-G-H systematic |
| **Quality** | Rotation, inspiration, penetration |
| **PA vs AP** | PA preferred (heart size) |
| **Pneumothorax** | Absent markings, lung edge |
| **Effusion** | Blunting, meniscus |
| **Consolidation** | Air bronchograms, silhouette |
| **Collapse** | Volume loss, mediastinal shift |

## 10. MCQs (10)

1. The most systematic approach to CXR:
   A. Random
   B. **A-B-C-D-E-F-G-H (airway, bones, cardiac, diaphragm, effusion, fields, gastric, hilar)**
   C. Quick glance
   D. None
   E. Reverse
   **Answer: B** — A-B-C-D-E-F-G-H.

2. The cardiothoracic ratio on PA CXR should be:
   A. >0.5
   B. **<0.5 (normal)**
   C. >0.6
   D. 1.0
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — <0.5.

3. Loss of the right heart border silhouette suggests:
   A. LLL
   B. **Right middle lobe consolidation**
   C. RUL
   D. None
   E. LUL
   **Answer: B** — RML.

4. Pneumothorax is best seen on CXR as:
   A. **Absent lung markings + visible visceral pleural line**
   B. Cardiomegaly
   C. Effusion
   D. None
   E. Mass
   **Answer: A** — Absent markings.

5. The most reliable sign of consolidation on CXR is:
   A. Effusion
   B. **Air bronchogram**
   C. Mass
   D. None
   E. Cavitation
   **Answer: B** — Air bronchogram.

6. Bat-wing pulmonary oedema pattern on CXR is associated with:
   A. PE
   B. **Cardiogenic pulmonary oedema**
   C. Pneumonia
   D. None
   E. ARDS
   **Answer: B** — APO.

7. Hampton's hump on CXR suggests:
   A. Pneumonia
   B. **Pulmonary infarction (PE)**
   C. Mass
   D. None
   E. Effusion
   **Answer: B** — Pulmonary infarct.

8. Westermark sign on CXR suggests:
   A. Pneumonia
   B. **Pulmonary embolism (oligaemia)**
   C. Mass
   D. None
   E. Effusion
   **Answer: B** — PE.

9. Loss of the left heart border silhouette suggests:
   A. **Lingular consolidation**
   B. RML
   C. LLL
   D. None
   E. RUL
   **Answer: A** — Lingula.

10. The most common cause of unilateral white-out on CXR is:
    A. Mass
    B. **Large pleural effusion (or lobar collapse)**
    C. Pneumonia
    D. None
    E. Pneumothorax
    **Answer: B** — Effusion or collapse.

   A. **Sail sign behind heart (triangular opacity)**
   B. Mediastinal shift
   C. Mass
   D. None
   E. Cavitation
    **Answer: A** — Sail sign.

3. The most reliable sign of LUL collapse:
   A. Mass
   B. **Veil sign (hazy opacity)**
   C. Effusion
   D. None

   A. 3
   B. **6 (good inspiration)**

   A. Asthma

6. The most common cause of mediastinal widening on CXR:
   A. **Aortic aneurysm / dissection / lymphadenopathy**
   B. Asthma
   C. COPD
   D. None
   E. PE
    **Answer: A** — Aortic/lymph nodes.

7. A coin lesion on CXR is:
   A. Asthma
   B. **Solitary pulmonary nodule (<3 cm)**

   D. None

## 14. Summary

CXR: systematic A-B-C-D-E-F-G-H approach. Compare with prior. Quality: rotation, inspiration, penetration. Key patterns: PTX (absent markings), effusion (blunting), consolidation (air bronchogram), collapse (volume loss), oedema (bat-wing, cardiomegaly), mass/nodule. Silhouette sign localises disease. Don't miss PTX, retrocardiac lesions, apices.
