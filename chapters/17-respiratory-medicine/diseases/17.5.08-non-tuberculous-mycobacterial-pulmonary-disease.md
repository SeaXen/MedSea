# Non-Tuberculous Mycobacterial Pulmonary Disease (NTM-PD)

Related: [[Pulmonary tuberculosis]], [[Drug-resistant tuberculosis]], [[Bronchiectasis]], [[Lady Windermere syndrome]], [[Immunocompromised host]], [[Lung cancer]], [[Cystic fibrosis]], [[Immunosuppression]]

> [!important]
> **NTM-PD** = pulmonary disease caused by **environmental mycobacteria** (not M. tuberculosis complex). **MAC (M. avium complex)** = most common. **Key FCPS/MRCP**: **ATS/IDSA diagnostic criteria** (clinical + radiological + microbiological), **MAC** = nodular/bronchiectatic (elderly women, Lady Windermere) or fibrocavitary (smokers, men); **M. kansasii** = upper lobe cavitary (TB mimic); **M. abscessus** = rapid grower, resistant, CF/non-CF; **treatment = multi-drug, prolonged** (12mo post-culture conversion), **MDR/M. abscessus = poor prognosis**.

## Learning Objectives
- Define **NTM-PD** and distinguish from **TB** and **colonisation/contamination**
- Apply **ATS/IDSA 2007/2020 diagnostic criteria** (clinical + radiological + microbiological)
- Identify **common NTM species** and their **clinical/radiological phenotypes**
- Prescribe **standard multi-drug regimens** per species (MAC, M. kansasii, M. xenopi, M. abscessus)
- Apply **treatment monitoring** (monthly sputum cultures, 12mo post-conversion)
- Recognise **complications** (drug toxicity, relapse, drug resistance, drug interactions)
- Manage **special populations** (CF, non-CF bronchiectasis, immunosuppression, Lady Windermere)

## Definition
**Non-tuberculous mycobacterial pulmonary disease (NTM-PD)** = symptomatic pulmonary disease caused by **environmental mycobacteria** (non-tuberculous mycobacteria, NTM) meeting **ATS/IDSA diagnostic criteria**. **Not transmissible person-to-person** (environmental acquisition).

**NTM Classification (Runyon):**
| Group | Growth Rate | Examples | Pigment |
|-------|-------------|----------|---------|
| **I** (Photochromogens) | Slow | M. kansasii, M. marinum, M. szulgai | Yellow/orange in light |
| **II** (Scotochromogens) | Slow | M. scrofulaceum, M. gordonae | Pigmented in dark |
| **III** (Non-chromogens) | Slow | **M. avium complex (MAC)**, M. xenopi, M. malmoense, M. haemophilum | Non-pigmented |
| **IV** (Rapid growers) | **<7 days** | **M. abscessus**, M. fortuitum, M. chelonae | Variable |

> **FCPS/MRCP tip**: **MAC (Group III) = most common NTM-PD (80-90%)**. **M. kansasii** = TB mimic. **M. abscessus** = rapid grower, highly resistant, CF/non-CF bronchiectasis.

## ATS/IDSA Diagnostic Criteria (2007/2020) — ALL REQUIRED
| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **1. Clinical** | **Pulmonary symptoms** (cough, sputum, haemoptysis, dyspnoea, constitutional) **OR** compatible CXR/HRCT findings **AND** exclusion of other diagnoses (TB, malignancy, etc.) |
| **2. Radiological** | **CXR/HRCT** showing **nodules, bronchiectasis, cavities, consolidation, tree-in-bud** — consistent with NTM |
| **3. Microbiological** | **A.** **Positive culture from ≥2 separate expectorated sputum** (or 1 bronchial wash/lavage) **OR** **B.** **Positive culture from lung biopsy** with granulomatous inflammation **OR** **C.** Transbronchial/other biopsy with granuloma + positive culture |

> **FCPS/MRCP tip**: **Single positive sputum = colonisation/contamination**. **Need ≥2 positive sputa OR 1 BAL/biopsy**. **Exclude TB** (GeneXpert, culture).

## Common NTM Species & Clinical Phenotypes

### 1. Mycobacterium avium Complex (MAC) — **~80-90% NTM-PD**
| Phenotype | Demographics | HRCT | Clinical |
|-----------|--------------|------|----------|
| **Nodular/Bronchiectatic** | **Elderly women** (thin, non-smoker, "Lady Windermere"), **non-CF bronchiectasis**, pectus excavatum, scoliosis | **Mid/upper zone nodules + bronchiectasis** (tree-in-bud), **right middle lobe/lingula** | Chronic cough, fatigue, weight loss, low-grade fever, **no cavitation** |
| **Fibrocavitary** | **Older men**, smokers, COPD, prior TB | **Upper lobe cavities** (TB mimic), consolidation | **More severe**, haemoptysis, rapid progression, higher bacterial load |

### 2. Mycobacterium kansasii — **Second most common**
- **Phenotype**: Upper lobe cavitary disease, **TB mimic**
- **Demographics**: Men, smokers, middle-aged
- **HRCT**: Upper lobe cavities, consolidation, mediastinal lymphadenopathy
- **Treatment**: Rifampicin-based (like TB) — **high cure rate (>90%)**

### 3. Mycobacterium xenopi
- **Thermophilic** (optimal 42°C) → **hot water systems**
- **Phenotype**: Chronic cavitary/fibrotic, upper lobes
- **Demographics**: Elderly, men, comorbidities
- **Treatment**: Difficult, prolonged (clarithromycin/rifampicin/ethambutol)

### 4. Mycobacterium abscessus (Rapid Grower, Group IV)
- **Subspecies**: M. abscessus subsp. **abscessus** (erm(41) functional → inducible macrolide resistance), **massiliense** (non-functional erm(41) → macrolide susceptible), **bolletii**
- **Demographics**: **CF** (increasing), **non-CF bronchiectasis**, post-surgical, skin/soft tissue
- **HRCT**: Bronchiectasis, nodules, consolidation, cavities
- **Resistance**: **Highly drug-resistant** (erm(41) → inducible clarithromycin resistance)
- **Treatment**: **Multi-drug IV + oral** (amoxicillin-clavulanate, cefoxitin, imipenem + macrolide if susceptible) — **prolonged, toxic, poor cure rate (~30-50%)**

### 5. Mycobacterium fortuitum / chelonae (Rapid Growers)
- **Post-surgical**, post-traumatic, catheter-related
- **Pulmonary disease rare** (usually skin/soft tissue)

## Clinical Features
### Common
- **Chronic cough** (productive, often mucoid/purulent)
- **Fatigue**, weight loss, low-grade fever, night sweats
- **Dyspnoea** (progressive)
- **Haemoptysis** (fibrocavitary forms)
- **Recurrent "pneumonias"** unresponsive to standard antibiotics

### Examination
- **Crackles** (bibasal or localised)
- **Clubbing** (chronic)
- **Signs of underlying disease**: bronchiectasis, CTD, CF, immunosuppression

## Investigations
### 1. Microbiology (Diagnostic Cornerstone)
| Sample | Method | Criteria |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Sputum** (≥3 early morning) | **Culture (solid LJ + liquid MGIT)** + **AFB smear** | **≥2 positive cultures** (separate days) |
| **Bronchoscopy** (BAL, bronchial wash) | Culture | **≥1 positive culture** + compatible clinical/radiological |
| **Lung biopsy** | Culture + histology | Granulomatous inflammation + positive culture |

> **Species identification**: **MALDI-TOF**, **16S rRNA sequencing**, **hsp65 sequencing**, **whole genome sequencing** (for subspecies/ resistance)

### 2. Drug Susceptibility Testing (DST)
| Species | Recommended DST |
|---------|-----------------|
| **MAC** | **Clarithromycin** (only reliable predictor); amikacin (optional) |
| **M. kansasii** | **Rifampicin** (predictor); clarithromycin, ethambutol |
| **M. xenopi** | Clarithromycin, rifampicin, ethambutol |
| **M. abscessus** | **Clarithromycin** (inducible resistance), **amoxicillin-clavulanate**, **cefoxitin**, **imipenem**, **amikacin**, **tigecycline** |

> **Clarithromycin DST for MAC**: **MIC ≤0.25 = susceptible**; **MIC ≥0.5 = resistant**

### 3. Imaging
- **HRCT**: **Tree-in-bud** (active), **bronchiectasis**, **nodules**, **cavities**, **consolidation**
- **CXR**: Cavities (fibrocavitary), nodules, bronchiectasis

### 4. Exclusion of TB
- **GeneXpert MTB/RIF Ultra** (rapid)
- **AFB smear/culture** (differentiate M. tb complex)
- **Line probe assay** (MTBDRplus)

## ATS/IDSA Treatment Regimens (Standardised)

### 1. MAC Nodular/Bronchiectatic (Three-Drug Oral)
| Drug | Dose | Duration |
|------|------|----------|
| **Azithromycin** | 250mg daily (or 500mg 3x/week) | **12 months post-culture conversion** |
| **Ethambutol** | 15mg/kg daily (max 1200mg) | Same |
| **Rifampicin** | 10mg/kg daily (max 600mg) | Same |

> **Alternative**: **Rifabutin** (if rifampicin interactions) + **azithromycin + ethambutol**
> **Duration**: **12 months after first negative sputum culture** (total ~15–18 months)

### 2. MAC Fibrocavitary / Severe (Three-Drug IV + Oral)
| Phase | Regimen |
|-------|---------|
| **Initial (2–3 months)** | **IV Amikacin** 10–15mg/kg 3x/week **+ IV/PO Azithromycin** 250mg daily **+ PO Ethambutol** 15mg/kg daily **+ PO Rifampicin** 10mg/kg daily |
| **Continuation** | **PO Azithromycin + Ethambutol + Rifampicin** (12mo post-conversion) |

### 3. M. kansasii (Three-Drug Oral — Like TB)
| Drug | Dose | Duration |
|------|------|----------|
| **Rifampicin** | 10mg/kg (max 600mg) daily | **12 months post-conversion** |
| **Ethambutol** | 15mg/kg daily | Same |
| **Clarithromycin** 500mg BD **OR** Isoniazid 300mg daily | 500mg BD / 300mg | Same |

> **High cure rate (>90%)** with rifampicin-based regimen.

### 4. M. abscessus (Complex Multi-Drug IV + Oral)
| Phase | Regimen |
|-------|---------|
| **Initial (IV, 2–4 weeks)** | **IV Cefoxitin** 2g q6h **+ Imipenem** 500mg q6h **OR Meropenem** 1g q8h **+ Amoxicillin-clavulanate** 1.2g q6h **+ (if macrolide susceptible) Azithromycin 250mg daily** |
| **Continuation (Oral, prolonged)** | **Oral Macrolide (if susceptible) + Oral β-lactam (amox-clav) + Oral inhaled amikacin** (optional) |
| **Duration** | **12 months post-culture conversion** (often >2 years total) |

> **Key**: **Subspecies identification** (abscessus vs massiliense) determines macrolide utility. **Inducible erm(41) resistance** in M. abscessus subsp. abscessus.

### 5. M. xenopi
- **Clarithromycin 500mg BD + Rifampicin 600mg + Ethambutol 15mg/kg** ×12mo post-conversion

## Monitoring During Treatment
| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Sputum culture** (AFB + mycobacterial) | **Monthly** until 3 consecutive negative, then 3-monthly |
| **LFTs** (ALT, AST, bilirubin) | Baseline, 2-weekly ×1mo, then monthly |
| **FBC** (neutropenia — linezolid, rifampicin) | Monthly |
| **Renal function** (amikacin, tenofovir) | Monthly (weekly if on aminoglycosides) |
| **Audiometry** (amikacin, streptomycin) | Baseline, then 3-monthly |
| **Visual acuity/colour vision** (ethambutol) | Baseline, monthly |
| **ECG/QTc** (macrolides, bedaquiline) | Baseline, then as needed |
| **Weight, symptoms** | Monthly |

## Drug Interactions / Toxicity
| Drug | Key Toxicity | Key Interaction |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Rifampicin/Rifabutin** | Hepatotoxicity, orange fluids, thrombocytopenia | **Potent CYP3A4 inducer** (↓ DOACs, OCP, ART, steroids, azoles) |
| **Ethambutol** | **Optic neuritis** (colour vision, acuity) | Avoid if baseline optic neuropathy |
| **Macrolides** (clarithro/azithro) | QT prolongation, GI, hepatotoxicity | **CYP3A4 inhibitor** (↑ statins, DOACs, tacrolimus) |
| **Amikacin** | Nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity, vestibular | Loop diuretics ↑ risk |
| **Cefoxitin/Imipenem** | Renal adjust, seizures (imipenem) | — |
| **Linezolid** | Myelosuppression (thrombocytopenia), neuropathy, serotonin syndrome | SSRIs/SNRIs → serotonin syndrome |

## Special Situations

### Lady Windermere Syndrome (MAC in Non-CF Bronchiectasis)
- **Demographics**: **Elderly thin women**, non-smokers, pectus excavatum/scoliosis, mitral valve prolapse
- **HRCT**: Right middle lobe/lingula **nodules + bronchiectasis** (tree-in-bud)
- **Management**: Standard 3-drug MAC regimen; **avoid unnecessary treatment if asymptomatic**

### Cystic Fibrosis
- **M. abscessus** increasing; **M. avium**, **M. kansasii** also
- **Screening**: Annual sputum cultures
- **M. abscessus**: Multi-drug IV + inhaled amikacin, consider lung transplant implications

### Immunosuppressed (HIV, Transplant, Biologics)
- **Disseminated disease** more common
- **MAC** most common (CD4 <50)
- **Prophylaxis**: Azithromycin 1200mg weekly (CD4 <50) or clarithromycin 500mg BD
- **Treatment**: Same regimens, adjust for drug interactions (rifampicin + ART)

## Complications
- **Drug toxicity** (hepatotoxicity, optic neuritis, QT prolongation, nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity)
- **Drug interactions** (rifampicin induction, macrolide inhibition)
- **Relapse** (5–20% after completion)
- **Acquired drug resistance** (especially macrolides in MAC, inducible in M. abscessus)
- **Progression to fibrosis/cavities**
- **Massive haemoptysis** (cavitary forms)

## Red Flags / Emergencies
- **Massive haemoptysis** → BAE
- **Acute respiratory failure** → ICU, broad coverage, steroids if immune reconstitution
- **Severe drug reaction** (SJS, DRESS, hepatotoxicity) → stop all, ICU
- **Acquired macrolide resistance** (MAC) → salvage regimen (clofazimine, bedaquiline, linezolid, inhaled amikacin)

## Prognosis
| Species/Phenotype | Cure Rate | 5-Yr Survival |
|-------------------|-----------|---------------|
| **MAC nodular/bronchiectatic** | **80–90%** | Good (but relapse 10–20%) |
| **MAC fibrocavitary** | 50–70% | Variable |
| **M. kansasii** | **>90%** | Excellent |
| **M. xenopi** | 40–60% | Moderate |
| **M. abscessus** | **30–50%** | Poor (high relapse, resistance) |
| **Rapid growers (fortuitum/chelonae)** | **80–90%** (skin/soft tissue) | Good |

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
1. **NTM-PD** = environmental mycobacteria, **not person-to-person**, **ATS/IDSA criteria** (clinical + radiological + ≥2 sputum cultures)
2. **MAC = 80-90% NTM-PD**; **nodular/bronchiectatic** (elderly women, Lady Windermere) vs **fibrocavitary** (smoking men)
3. **M. kansasii** = TB mimic (upper lobe cavities), **rifampicin-based regimen = >90% cure**
4. **M. abscessus** = rapid grower, **inducible macrolide resistance** (erm(41)), **poor cure rate (30-50%)**
5. **Diagnosis**: **≥2 positive sputum cultures** OR 1 BAL/biopsy; **exclude TB**
5. **MAC treatment**: **Azithromycin + Ethambutol + Rifampicin** (3-drug) ×12mo post-conversion
6. **M. kansasii**: **Rifampicin + Ethambutol + Clarithromycin** (like TB) ×12mo post-conversion
6. **M. abscessus**: **Inducible macrolide resistance (erm(41))**, multi-drug IV+oral, **cure ~30-50%**
7. **Treatment duration**: **12 months post-culture conversion** (all species)
8. **Monitoring**: Monthly sputum cultures, LFTs, ophthalmology (ethambutol), audiometry (aminoglycosides)
9. **Lady Windermere**: Elderly thin women, MAC, RML/lingula bronchiectasis
10. **Drug interactions**: Rifampicin = CYP3A4 inducer; Macrolides = CYP3A4 inhibitor

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- **Single positive sputum = NTM-PD** — WRONG (colonisation/contamination; need ≥2)
- **All NTM treated same** — WRONG (species-specific regimens)
- **MAC = always treat** — NO (asymptomatic, stable imaging may observe)
- **M. kansasii = treat like TB** — YES (rifampicin-based, high cure)
- **M. abscessus = macrolide works** — WRONG (inducible resistance via erm(41))
- **NTM = contagious** — WRONG (environmental, not person-to-person)
- **Single positive culture = treat** — WRONG (colonisation common)
- **MAC = always cavitary** — NO (nodular/bronchiectatic more common)

## Mnemonics
- **NTM GROUPS**: **I** Photochromogens (kansasii), **II** Scotochromogens (scrofulaceum), **III** Non-chromogens (**MAC**), **IV** Rapid (abscessus)
- **MAC PHENOTYPES**: **N**odular/**B**ronchiectatic = **L**ady **W**indermere; **F**ibro**C**avitary = **S**moking **M**en
- **M. ABSCESSUS**: **M**assiliense = **M**acrolide **S**usceptible; **A**bscessus = **A**bsolute **R**esistance (erm(41))
- **NTM TREATMENT**: **M**AC = **A**zithro + **E**thambutol + **R**ifampicin; **K**ansasii = **R**ifampicin + **E**thambutol + **C**larithro; **A**BSCESSUS = **C**efoxitin + **I**mipenem + **C**lavulanate + **A**zithro (if susceptible)
- **LADY WINDERMERE**: **L**ady = **E**lderly **W**omen, **M**AC, **R**ML/**L**ingula, **N**on-CF **B**ronchiectasis

## Summary
**NTM-PD** = pulmonary disease from **environmental mycobacteria** (not M. tb complex), diagnosed by **ATS/IDSA criteria** (clinical + radiological + **≥2 sputum cultures** OR 1 BAL/biopsy, **exclude TB**). **MAC = 80-90%**, two phenotypes: **nodular/bronchiectatic** (elderly thin women, **Lady Windermere**) and **fibrocavitary** (smoking men). **M. kansasii** = **TB mimic** (upper lobe cavities), **rifampicin-based regimen >90% cure**. **M. abscessus** = rapid grower, **inducible macrolide resistance (erm(41))**, **poor cure (30-50%)**. **Treatment**: **MAC** = azithromycin + ethambutol + rifampicin; **M. kansasii** = rifampicin + ethambutol + clarithromycin; **M. abscessus** = multi-drug IV (cefoxitin, imipenem, amox-clav) ± macrolide if susceptible. **Duration = 12 months post-culture conversion** for all. **Monitoring**: monthly sputum, LFT, vision (ethambutol), hearing (amikacin). **Drug interactions**: rifampicin = CYP3A4 inducer; macrolides = CYP3A4 inhibitor.
