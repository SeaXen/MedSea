# Chronic cough approach

> [!important]
> **Chronic cough** (≥8 weeks) requires systematic evaluation. The most common causes are **asthma, upper airway cough syndrome (UACS/post-nasal drip), GERD, ACE inhibitors, and smoking**. Initial workup: history, exam, CXR, spirometry, empirical trials. Most cases resolve with targeted treatment.

Related: [[Asthma]], [[Upper airway obstruction and stridor]], [[Hemoptysis]], [[Pneumonia]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: Chronic cough = **think upper airway, asthma, GERD, ACE-i, smoking**. Most common cause: UACS (post-nasal drip). **Stop ACE-inhibitor** in any chronic cough. **Trial of PPI** for GERD-related cough. **CXR + spirometry** in all.

## 1. Definition

**Chronic cough** = cough lasting **>8 weeks**.

### Subacute
- 3–8 weeks
- Often post-infectious

### Acute
- <3 weeks
- Usually viral, self-limiting

## 2. Aetiology

### Most common causes (90% of cases)
| Cause | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| **Upper airway cough syndrome (UACS) / Post-nasal drip** | Allergic, non-allergic, chronic sinusitis |
| **Asthma** (cough-variant) | Variable; may be only symptom |
| **GERD** | Laryngopharyngeal reflux, often silent |
| **ACE inhibitors** | Within hours-weeks of starting; resolve after stopping (weeks) |
| **Smoking** | Chronic irritation |

### Other causes
- **COPD** (chronic bronchitis)
- **Bronchiectasis**
- **Lung cancer** (must exclude)
- **Interstitial lung disease** (ILD)
- **TB**
- **Pertussis** (post-infectious cough)
- **Eosinophilic bronchitis**
- **Bronchiolitis**
- **Laryngeal dysfunction** (VCD)
- **Post-viral cough**
- **Mediastinal mass**
- **Pleural disease**
- **Cardiac** (heart failure, pericardial)
- **Somatic cough syndrome** (psychogenic)
- **Foreign body** (especially in children)

## 3. Pathophysiology

### Cough reflex
- **Afferents**: vagus (larynx, trachea, bronchi, oesophagus, ear)
- **Centre**: brainstem
- **Effector**: diaphragm, intercostals, larynx
- **Sensory receptors**: rapidly adapting (RARs), C-fibres (TRPV1, TRPA1)
- Modulated by cortical input

### Hypersensitivity
- After viral infection, allergens, irritants
- ↑Cough reflex sensitivity (upregulation of receptors)

## 4. Clinical Features

### History
- **Duration**, pattern (day/night, with meals, position)
- **Triggers** (allergens, smoke, food, cold, talking, laughing)
- **Sputum** (purulent, blood, post-nasal)
- **Associated symptoms** (wheeze, dyspnoea, heartburn, nasal symptoms, throat clearing)
- **Drugs** (ACE-i, beta-blockers, NSAIDs)
- **Smoking**
- **Occupation**, hobbies
- **Cardiac/respiratory history**
- **GERD symptoms** (heartburn, sour taste, worse lying)

### Examination
- **Upper airway**: rhinitis, polyps, post-nasal drip
- **Lungs**: wheeze, crackles, monophonic wheeze (focal)
- **Cardiac**: heart failure signs
- **Ear**: foreign body, wax (Arnold's reflex)

## 5. Diagnosis

### Investigations
| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **CXR** | Exclude mass, infection, ILD, heart failure |
| **Spirometry + BD reversibility** | Asthma (often normal between episodes) |
| **FeNO** | Eosinophilic airway disease |
| **CT chest** | If CXR abnormal or high suspicion |
| **Sputum** | Cytology, AFB, culture |
| **Bronchoscopy** | If mass suspected, atypical, no response to trials |
| **CT sinuses** | Sinusitis, UACS |
| **24-hr pH/impedance** | GERD (atypical) |
| **Methacholine challenge** | Asthma (if normal spirometry) |

### Empirical treatment trials
- **Stop ACE-inhibitor** (if applicable) — 4 weeks
- **Antihistamine + decongestant** (UACS trial) — 2–4 weeks
- **Inhaled corticosteroid** (asthma/cough-variant) — 2–4 weeks
- **PPI** (GERD trial) — 8–12 weeks (double dose AM + PM)
- **Stop smoking**

## 6. Management

### Treat underlying cause
- **UACS**: intranasal corticosteroids + antihistamines +/− saline irrigation
- **Asthma**: ICS ± LABA; LTRA
- **GERD**: PPI, lifestyle (weight loss, head of bed elevated, avoid late meals)
- **ACE-i**: stop, switch to ARB
- **Smoking**: cessation
- **Pertussis**: macrolide (if acute)
- **Eosinophilic bronchitis**: ICS
- **Lung cancer**: oncology referral
- **Vocal cord dysfunction**: speech therapy

### Symptomatic treatment
- **Antitussives** (if cause untreatable):
  - **Dextromethorphan**, **codeine**, **pholcodine**
  - **Morphine** (severe chronic cough, palliative)
- **Mucolytics** (guaifenesin)
- **Speech therapy** (chronic refractory)
- **Gabapentin** (neuropathic cough)
- **Local anaesthetics** (nebulised lidocaine for intractable)

## 7. Red flags
- Haemoptysis
- Weight loss
- Smoking history
- Dyspnoea
- Abnormal CXR
- Hoarseness (laryngeal)
- Recurrent pneumonia

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|
| **Definition** | >8 weeks |
| **Most common** | UACS (post-nasal drip) |
| **Other common** | Asthma, GERD, ACE-i, smoking |
| **First steps** | Stop ACE-i, CXR, spirometry |
| **UACS Rx** | Antihistamine + INCS |
| **Asthma Rx** | ICS |

   C. GERD
   D. ACE-i
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — UACS.

2. Definition of chronic cough:
   A. >3 weeks
   B. **>8 weeks**
   C. >12 weeks
   D. >6 months
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — >8 weeks.

3. First-line investigation in chronic cough:
   A. CT chest
   B. **CXR + spirometry**
   C. Bronchoscopy
   D. MRI
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — CXR + spirometry.

4. The most common drug causing chronic cough is:
   A. Aspirin
   B. **ACE inhibitor**
   C. Beta-blocker
   D. Statin
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — ACE-i.

5. Treatment of UACS-related cough:
   A. **Antihistamine + intranasal corticosteroid**
   B. Antibiotics
   C. Steroids systemic
   D. Surgery
   E. None
   **Answer: A** — Antihist + INCS.

6. The trial duration for empirical PPI in chronic cough:
   A. 1 week
   B. 4 weeks
   C. **8–12 weeks (double dose)**
   D. 6 months
   E. None
   **Answer: C** — 8–12 weeks.

7. GERD is associated with cough via:
   A. Aspiration only
   B. **Laryngopharyngeal reflux, vagal reflex**
   C. Allergy
   D. None
   E. Smoking
   **Answer: B** — LPR + vagal reflex.

   A. Discharge

   C. Wait
   D. Antibiotics

9. The most useful diagnostic for asthma in chronic cough:
   A. CXR
   B. **Spirometry + BD reversibility (or methacholine challenge)**
   C. CT
   D. ABG
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Spirometry + reversibility.

10. The least likely cause of chronic cough is:
    A. UACS
    B. Asthma
    C. GERD
    D. ACE-i
    E. **Bacterial pneumonia (acute, not chronic)**
    **Answer: E** — Acute, not chronic.

## 13. Summary

Chronic cough >8 weeks. Most common: UACS (post-nasal drip). Other: asthma, GERD, ACE-i, smoking. First step: stop ACE-i, CXR + spirometry. Empirical trials: UACS (INCS + antihist), asthma (ICS), GERD (PPI 8–12 weeks). Red flag: CXR abnormal, haemoptysis, weight loss → workup for cancer.
