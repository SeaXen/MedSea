# Chest wall restriction and kyphoscoliosis

> [!important]
> **Chest wall disorders** (kyphoscoliosis, ankylosing spondylitis, flail chest, obesity hypoventilation) cause **restrictive ventilatory impairment** and can lead to **chronic hypercapnic respiratory failure** (Type 2). Recognition and treatment with **NIV support** can dramatically improve quality of life and survival.

Related: [[Respiratory Failure]], [[Oxygen Therapy and NIV]], [[Neuromuscular respiratory weakness]], [[Type 2 respiratory failure]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: Kyphoscoliosis is the **most common non-NM cause** of chronic respiratory failure. **OHS** is the most common cause of chronic Type 2 RF (BMI >30 + daytime hypercapnia). Treatment: **NIV/BiPAP** + weight loss (OHS) +/− surgical correction (scoliosis).

## 1. Definition

**Chest wall restriction** = reduction in chest wall compliance and/or thoracic volume, leading to **restrictive ventilatory impairment** and often chronic respiratory failure.

## 2. Common Causes

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Skeletal** | Kyphoscoliosis, ankylosing spondylitis, pectus excavatum, pectus carinatum |
| **Connective tissue** | Marfan, Ehlers-Danlos |
| **Trauma** | Flail chest (multiple rib fractures) |
| **Obesity** | Obesity hypoventilation syndrome (OHS) |
| **Pleural** | Fibrothorax (trapped lung) |
| **Postsurgical** | Thoracoplasty, pneumonectomy |
| **Ankylosing spondylitis** | ↓Chest expansion |

## 3. Pathophysiology

### Kyphoscoliosis
- **Cobb angle >70°** = significant respiratory compromise
- **Lateral curvature + vertebral rotation** → ↓thoracic volume
- **↓Compliance** of chest wall
- **Diaphragm flattened** and inefficient
- **V/Q mismatch** (compression of lung parenchyma)
- **↓Ventilatory response** to CO₂
- Progressive respiratory failure (Type 2)

### OHS
- **BMI >30** + daytime hypocapnia (PaCO₂ >6 kPa)
- **Obesity → ↓FRC, ↓ERV, ↓compliance** (chest wall and lung)
- **↑WOB** + ↓respiratory muscle efficiency
- **Central hypoventilation** (blunted response to CO₂)
- Often coexists with OSA (overlap syndrome)

### Flail chest
- ≥3 consecutive ribs fractured in ≥2 places
- Paradoxical chest movement (inward on inspiration, outward on expiration)
- Underlying lung contusion
- Hypoventilation

## 4. Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- **Progressive dyspnoea** (especially supine in OHS)
- **Daytime sleepiness** (OHS, hypoventilation)
- **Snoring, witnessed apnoea** (OHS + OSA)
- **Fatigue**
- **Cough** (weak in OHS)
- **Chest pain** (if fracture)

### Signs
- **Visible deformity** (scoliosis, pectus)
- **↓Chest expansion** (<2.5 cm = severe restriction)
- **Rapid, shallow breathing**
- **Paradoxical breathing** (flail chest, OHS)
- **Barrel chest** (OHS)
- **Cyanosis** (severe)
- **Signs of right heart failure** (cor pulmonale)

## 5. Diagnosis

### Spirometry
- **Restrictive pattern**: ↓FVC, ↓TLC, FEV₁/FVC normal or ↑
- **↓DLCO** in OHS
- ↓Chest expansion

### ABG
- **↓PaO₂ + ↑PaCO₂** (chronic Type 2)
- Compensated (HCO₃⁻ ↑)
- ↑Bicarbonate in chronic

### CXR
- Scoliosis (Cobb angle)
- Pleural thickening (fibrothorax)
- Diaphragm position
- Cardiomegaly (cor pulmonale)

### Sleep study (polysomnography)
- **OHS**: obesity + hypoventilation (CO₂ rises in sleep)
- Overlap with OSA
- AHI + hypoventilation

### Other
- **Echo**: RV dysfunction (cor pulmonale)
- **6MWT**: functional capacity
- **Bicarbonate** as screening for chronic hypoventilation (HCO₃⁻ >27 in OHS)

## 6. Management

### Kyphoscoliosis
- **Bracing** (adolescent)
- **Surgical correction** (severe, progressive)
- **Pulmonary rehabilitation**
- **LTOT** (PaO₂ <7.3 or <8 with cor pulmonale)
- **NIV** (BiPAP) for chronic respiratory failure
- **Cough assist** (weak cough)
- **Vaccinations**

### OHS
- **Weight loss** (most important long-term)
- **Bariatric surgery** (if BMI >40 or >35 with comorbidities)
- **PAP therapy** (CPAP for OSA-predominant; BiPAP for hypoventilation)
- **LTOT** if needed
- **Avoid** respiratory depressants (opioids, benzodiazepines)
- **Tracheostomy** in selected severe cases

### Flail chest
- **Analgesia** (epidural, intercostal block) — critical
- **O₂ + humidification**
- **Physiotherapy** (clearance)
- **NIV/CPAP** (pneumatic stabilisation)
- **Intubation + ventilation** if severe (V/Q mismatch, fatigue)
- **Surgical rib fixation** (selected cases)

### Ankylosing spondylitis
- **Pulmonary rehab**
- **NSAIDs + biologics** (anti-TNF, anti-IL17) for AS
- **NIV** if respiratory failure
- Treat cardiac involvement (aortitis, conduction defects)

### General
- **Vaccinations** (flu, pneumococcal, COVID-19)
- **Smoking cessation**
- **Avoid sedatives**
- **Annual review** with PFTs + ABG

## 7. Complications
- **Cor pulmonale**
- **Respiratory failure** (Type 2)
- **Pneumonia** (recurrent, due to weak cough)
- **Polycythaemia**
- **Cardiac arrhythmias**
- **Death** (advanced)

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|
| **Kyphoscoliosis** | Cobb >70° = significant |
| **OHS** | BMI >30 + daytime PaCO₂ >6 kPa |
| **Flail chest** | ≥3 ribs, ≥2 places |
| **OHS Rx** | Weight loss + PAP (CPAP/BiPAP) |
| **Kyphoscoliosis Rx** | Bracing, surgery, NIV, LTOT |

## 13. Summary

Chest wall disorders cause restrictive PFT and often chronic Type 2 respiratory failure. Kyphoscoliosis: Cobb >70°. OHS: BMI >30 + daytime hypercapnia (Rx: weight loss + PAP). Flail chest: ≥3 ribs, ≥2 places (Rx: analgesia + ventilation). All require vaccinations and rehabilitation.
