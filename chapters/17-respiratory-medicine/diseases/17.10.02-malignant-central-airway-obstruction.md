# Malignant central airway obstruction

> [!important]
> **Malignant central airway obstruction (CAO)** is a complication of **lung cancer, metastatic disease, or airway tumours** causing significant airway narrowing. It presents with **dyspnoea, stridor, monophonic wheeze, post-obstructive pneumonia**. Treatment is **urgent airway intervention** (rigid bronchoscopy with stenting, laser, debulking) plus tumour-directed therapy.

Related: [[Lung Cancer]], [[Superior vena cava obstruction|SVC obstruction]], [[Pancoast tumor and local invasion syndromes|Pancoast]], [[Paraneoplastic pulmonary syndromes|Paraneoplastic]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: **Malignant CAO = oncologic emergency**. Most common cause: **lung cancer** (esp. squamous, small cell). Treatment: **rigid bronchoscopy + stenting +/− laser/debulking + tumour Rx** (RT, chemo, immunotherapy). Always consider in cancer patient with new dyspnoea, stridor, or recurrent post-obstructive pneumonia.

## 1. Definition

**Malignant central airway obstruction (CAO)** = significant narrowing of the **trachea, main bronchi, or lobar bronchi** by **malignant tumour** (primary or metastatic).

### Severity
- **<50% narrowing**: usually asymptomatic
- **50–70%**: symptomatic with exertion
- **>70%**: severe, often at rest
- **Critical**: near-complete, life-threatening

## 2. Aetiology

### Primary lung cancer (most common)
- **Squamous cell carcinoma** (most common — central, endobronchial)
- **Small cell lung cancer** (central)
- **Adenocarcinoma** (less common — usually peripheral)
- **Carcinoid** (endobronchial, young patients)
- **Adenoid cystic carcinoma**, **mucoepidermoid** (rare, central)

### Metastatic / other
- **Renal cell carcinoma** (endobronchial mets)
- **Breast, colon, melanoma** (rare)
- **Thyroid carcinoma** (extension)
- **Lymphoma** (mediastinal)
- **Carcinoid** (low-grade, central)
- **Kaposi sarcoma** (HIV)
- **Mucoepidermoid** (rare)

## 3. Pathophysiology

### Mechanism of obstruction
- **Endobronchial tumour** (most common)
- **Extrinsic compression** (mediastinal mass, lymph nodes)
- **Mixed**
- **Mucus plugging** (above obstruction)

### Consequences
- **Dyspnoea** (obstruction)
- **Post-obstructive pneumonia** (recurrent, same lobe)
- **Atelectasis** (distal collapse)
- **Haemoptysis**
- **Stridor** (upper trachea)
- **Respiratory failure** (severe)
- **Death** (untreated)

## 4. Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- **Dyspnoea** (progressive, often acute exacerbation)
- **Stridor** (tracheal obstruction)
- **Wheeze** (monophonic, fixed, localised)
- **Stridor worse with supine**
- **Cough** (irritation)
- **Haemoptysis** (mucosal erosion)
- **Recurrent pneumonia** (same lobe)
- **Fever** (post-obstructive pneumonia)

### Signs
- **Stridor** (audible)
- **Monophonic wheeze** (fixed, not responsive to bronchodilators)
- **↓Breath sounds** distally
- **Cyanosis** (severe)
- **Use of accessory muscles**
- **Respiratory failure** (severe)

## 5. Diagnosis

### Investigations
| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **CXR** | Atelectasis, mass, mediastinal widening |
| **CT chest** | **Gold standard** — extent, location, severity |
| **Bronchoscopy** (rigid preferred) | Visualise, biopsy, **therapeutic** (stent, debulk) |
| **Spirometry** | Flow-volume loop (variable intrathoracic obstruction) |
| **PET-CT** | Staging |
| **Biopsy** | Histology + molecular |

### Flow-volume loop
- **Variable intrathoracic obstruction** (main bronchus): flattened expiratory limb
- **Variable extrathoracic** (trachea): flattened inspiratory limb
- **Fixed**: both limbs flattened

## 6. Management

### Emergency airway intervention
- **Rigid bronchoscopy** (gold standard for intervention)
  - **Mechanical debulking** (core-out)
  - **Laser** (Nd:YAG)
  - **Electrocautery / argon plasma coagulation (APC)**
  - **Cryotherapy**
  - **Stenting** (silicone or metallic)
  - **Photodynamic therapy**
- **Balloon dilatation** (temporary)

### Tumour-directed therapy
- **External beam radiotherapy** (most common)
- **Brachytherapy** (endobronchial)
- **Chemotherapy** (SCLC, sensitive NSCLC)
- **Targeted therapy** (EGFR, ALK)
- **Immunotherapy** (PD-1/PD-L1)

### Supportive
- **O₂** (target SpO₂ 94–98%)
- **Corticosteroids** (reduce oedema around tumour)
- **Antibiotics** (post-obstructive pneumonia)
- **Humidification**
- **Pulmonary rehabilitation**

### Specific by histology
| Tumour | Treatment |
|--------|-----------|
| **SCLC** | Chemo (cisplatin + etoposide), RT |
| **NSCLC squamous** | RT +/− chemo, immunotherapy |
| **Carcinoid** | Surgical resection (preferred), bronchoscopic if surgical not possible |
| **Adenoid cystic** | Surgery + RT |
| **Metastatic** | Treat primary +/− local Rx |
