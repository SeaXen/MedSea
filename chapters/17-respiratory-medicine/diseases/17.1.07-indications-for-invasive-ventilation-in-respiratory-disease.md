# Indications for invasive ventilation in respiratory disease

> [!important]
> **Invasive mechanical ventilation** is required when non-invasive support (O₂, HFNC, NIV) is insufficient or contraindicated. Key indications: **cardiopulmonary arrest, severe hypoxaemia (PaO₂ <60 mmHg), severe acidosis (pH <7.20), respiratory muscle exhaustion, airway protection (GCS <8), NIV failure**. Decision based on clinical + ABG + trajectory.

Related: [[Oxygen Therapy and NIV]], [[NIV failure and escalation triggers]], [[Respiratory Failure]], [[ARDS]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: **"GCS ≤8, intubate"**. Classic indications: (1) respiratory arrest/apnoea, (2) severe hypoxaemia (PaO₂ <60 on high FiO₂), (3) severe acidosis (pH <7.20, ↑PaCO₂), (4) respiratory muscle exhaustion, (5) airway protection, (6) NIV failure. **Don't delay intubation** in deteriorating patients.

## 1. Definition

**Invasive mechanical ventilation** = positive pressure ventilation delivered via **endotracheal tube** (or tracheostomy).

## 2. Absolute Indications

### 1. Cardiopulmonary arrest
- Intubate immediately
- **D**anger, **A**irway, **R**esponse, **S**end for help (DARS)
- **C**ompressions, **A**irway, **B**reathing (CAB)

### 2. Respiratory arrest / apnoea
- **Bag-mask ventilation** initially
- **Intubate** when feasible

### 3. Severe hypoxaemia
- **PaO₂ <60 mmHg (8 kPa)** on high FiO₂ (P/F <200)
- **P/F ratio <200** with bilateral infiltrates (ARDS criteria)
- **Severe pneumonia** with rapid deterioration
- **Massive PE** with shock
- **Pulmonary oedema** unresponsive to NIV

### 4. Severe hypercapnic acidosis
- **pH <7.20** with ↑PaCO₂
- **NIV failure** (pH <7.20 after 1–2 h of NIV)
- **Severe asthma** with ↑PaCO₂ + exhaustion
- **Severe AECOPD** with exhaustion

### 5. Respiratory muscle exhaustion
- **Paradoxical breathing** (abdominal paradox)
- **Tachypnoea** >30 (sustained)
- **Use of accessory muscles** with worsening
- **Drowsiness** (hypercapnic encephalopathy)
- **Silent chest** (severe asthma)

### 6. Airway protection
- **GCS ≤8** (cannot protect airway)
- **Severe bulbar dysfunction** (GBS, MG, stroke)
- **Massive haemoptysis** with airway compromise
- **Upper airway obstruction** (FB, angioedema, severe stridor)

### 7. NIV failure
- **pH <7.20** after 1–2 h of NIV
- **↑PaCO₂ + ↑RR + GCS ↓** despite NIV
- **Mask intolerance**
- **Exhaustion**

## 3. Relative Indications
- Impending respiratory failure (tachypnoea >35, exhaustion)
- ↓Mental status (GCS <10) with risk of aspiration
- Severe pneumonia with rapid deterioration
- ARDS with P/F <200
- Massive PE with shock
- Severe asthma not responding to therapy

## 4. Specific Disease Indications

| Disease | Specific indication |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Severe asthma** | ↑PaCO₂ + exhaustion, silent chest, ↑RR |
| **COPD (AECOPD)** | NIV failure (pH <7.25), GCS ↓, shock |
| **ARDS** | P/F <200 + exhaustion |
| **Pneumonia** | Sepsis + respiratory failure, GCS ↓ |
| **PE (massive)** | Shock + respiratory failure |
| **NM weakness (GBS, MG)** | VC <20 mL/kg, GCS ↓, aspiration |
| **APO** | Cardiogenic shock, severe acidosis, exhaustion |

## 5. Clinical Assessment

### Signs of impending respiratory failure
- **↑Respiratory rate** (>30/min, or paradoxically slowing)
- **Accessory muscle use** (SCM, scalenes)
- **Paradoxical breathing** (abdominal paradox — diaphragmatic fatigue)
- **Tracheal tug**
- **Cyanosis**
- **Confusion, agitation, drowsiness** (hypoxaemia/hypercapnia)
- **Diaphoresis**

### ABG criteria
| Parameter | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| **PaO₂** | <60 mmHg (8 kPa) on FiO₂ ≥0.5 (or P/F <200) |
| **PaCO₂** | >60 mmHg (8 kPa) with pH <7.20 |
| **pH** | <7.20 (especially rising PaCO₂) |
| **SpO₂** | <90% despite high FiO₂ |

## 6. Pre-Intubation Checklist

### Equipment
- Suction (working)
- Bag-valve-mask (BVM) + O₂
- Laryngoscope (Mac 3–4, Miller 2–3)
- Endotracheal tubes (7.0, 7.5, 8.0 mm)
- Bougie, stylet
- Capnography
- Monitoring (SpO₂, ECG, BP)
- Emergency drugs (succinylcholine, rocuronium, propofol, ketamine, fentanyl)

### Drugs (Rapid Sequence Induction)
- **Preoxygenation**: 100% O₂ for 3–5 min
- **Induction**: propofol 1.5–2.5 mg/kg, ketamine 1–2 mg/kg, or etomidate 0.3 mg/kg
- **Paralysis**: succinylcholine 1.5 mg/kg (rapid onset, short) or rocuronium 1.2 mg/kg (longer, reversible with sugammadex)
- **Cricoid pressure** (controversial)

### Difficult airway predictors
- Previous difficult intubation
- Mallampati III–IV
- Limited mouth opening, neck mobility
- Obstructive sleep apnoea
- Obesity, large neck

## 7. Post-Intubation Management

### Initial ventilator settings
| Parameter | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| **Mode** | Volume-control (AC/VC) or pressure-control (PC) |
| **Tidal volume** | 6–8 mL/kg (lung-protective; 4–6 in ARDS) |
| **RR** | 12–20/min |
| **FiO₂** | Titrate to SpO₂ 92–96% (88–92% in COPD) |
| **PEEP** | 5–10 cmH₂O (higher in ARDS) |
| **I:E ratio** | 1:2 (longer expiration in obstructive) |

### Monitoring
- **Capnography** (ETCO₂)
- **Pulse oximetry**
- **ABG** within 30–60 min
- **Ventilator waveforms** (pressure, flow, volume loops)
- **CXR** (post-intubation, daily)
- **Sedation score** (RASS)

### Sedation
- **Light sedation** preferred (RASS 0 to -2)
- **Propofol** (short, ↑TG with prolonged)
- **Dexmedetomidine** (less respiratory depression)
- **Midazolam** (accumulation in renal failure)
- **Fentanyl/morphine** (analgesia)

## 8. Complications of Invasive Ventilation

| Complication | Mechanism | Management |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| **VAP** | Aspiration, colonisation | Head elevation, oral care, sedation holiday |
| **Barotrauma** | High pressures | ↓Pplat, lung-protective |
| **Volutrauma** | High volumes | ↓VT (6 mL/kg) |
| **Atelectrauma** | Repetitive collapse | PEEP |
| **O₂ toxicity** | High FiO₂ | ↓FiO₂ (target <0.6) |
| **VILI** | Multi-factorial | Lung-protective |
| **Hemodynamic compromise** | ↑Intrathoracic pressure, ↓preload | Fluids, vasopressors |
| **Aspiration** | ETT bypasses upper airway | Cuff pressure, OG tube |
| **Delirium** | ICU environment | Sleep-wake cycle, antipsychotics |
| **Tracheal stenosis** | Cuff pressure | Monitor cuff <30 cmH₂O |

## 9. Weaning

### Criteria
- Underlying cause resolving
- **SpO₂ >90% on FiO₂ ≤0.4, PEEP ≤5**
- **RSBI (Rapid Shallow Breathing Index) <105** (RR/TV)
- Hemodynamic stability
- Adequate mental status
- Able to protect airway

### Spontaneous breathing trial (SBT)
- T-piece or PSV 5–8 cmH₂O for 30–120 min
- Monitor RR, SpO₂, ABG
- **Success** → extubate
- **Failure** (RR >35, SpO₂ <90, distress, ↑PaCO₂) → re-ventilate

## 10. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|
| **"GCS 8, intubate"** | Airway protection |
| **Absolute indications** | Arrest, severe hypoxaemia, pH <7.20, exhaustion, airway protection |
| **NIV failure criteria** | pH <7.20, ↑PaCO₂, exhaustion, GCS ↓ |
| **Initial ventilator settings** | VT 6–8 mL/kg, RR 12–20, PEEP 5–10, FiO₂ titrated |
| **Lung-protective ventilation** | VT 6 mL/kg, plateau <30 cmH₂O (ARDS) |
| **Capnography** | Confirms ETT placement |
| **Complications** | VAP, barotrauma, O₂ toxicity, delirium |
| **Weaning** | RSBI <105, SBT 30–120 min, extubate |

   A. Weaning failure

## 15. Summary

Invasive ventilation is required for cardiopulmonary arrest, severe hypoxaemia (P/F <200), severe acidosis (pH <7.20), exhaustion, airway protection (GCS ≤8), NIV failure. Initial settings: VT 6–8 mL/kg, RR 12–20, PEEP 5–10. Lung-protective (VT 6 mL/kg, plateau <30 cmH₂O) in ARDS. Complications: VAP, barotrauma, O₂ toxicity, delirium. Weaning: RSBI <105, SBT 30–120 min.
