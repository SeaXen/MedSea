# Bronchial Carcinoid Tumor

Related: [[Thoracic Malignancy]], [[Lung cancer]], [[Carcinoid syndrome]], [[MEN1]], [[SCLC]], [[Pulmonary neuroendocrine tumours]], [[Post-obstructive pneumonia]]

> [!important]
> **Bronchial carcinoid tumor** = **well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumour (NET)** of bronchial origin. **Low-grade (typical)** vs **intermediate-grade (atypical)**. **Key FCPS/MRCP**: **Young adults**, **central/endobronchial**, **cough, haemoptysis, wheeze, post-obstructive pneumonia**, **carcinoid syndrome rare (<5%)**, **MEN1 association**, **Octreotide scan / Ga-68 DOTATATE PET**, **Surgical resection curative** (typical), **Atypical = higher malignant potential**, **Carcinoid syndrome = flushing, diarrhoea, valvular heart disease**.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Define **bronchial carcinoid** and classify as **typical vs atypical** (WHO)
- Recognise **clinical presentations** (obstructive symptoms, carcinoid syndrome, incidental)
- Apply **diagnostic imaging** (CT, Octreotide scan, Ga-68 DOTATATE PET, bronchoscopy)
- Interpret **biochemistry** (serum chromogranin A, 5-HIAA, serotonin)
- Apply **management** (surgical resection = cornerstone, lymph node dissection, adjuvant therapy for atypical)
- Recognise **carcinoid syndrome** management (octreotide, telotristat, avoid triggers)
- Screen for **MEN1** (pituitary, parathyroid, pancreatic NETs)

## 2. Definition
**Bronchial carcinoid tumor** = **well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumour (NET)** arising from **bronchial Kulchitsky (neuroendocrine) cells**. **Part of pulmonary neuroendocrine tumour spectrum** along with **small cell lung cancer (SCLC)** and **large cell neuroendocrine carcinoma (LCNEC)**.

> **FCPS/MRCP tip**: **Carcinoids are NETs**. **Typical = low-grade (G1), Atypical = intermediate-grade (G2)**. **Not small cell (high-grade G3)**.

## 3. Classification (WHO 2021)
| Type | Grade | Mitoses (/2mm²) | Necrosis | Ki-67 | Metastatic Potential |
|------|-------|-----------------|----------|-------|---------------------|
| **Typical carcinoid (TC)** | **G1 (Low)** | **<2** | **Absent** | **<5%** | **Low (~10-15%)** |
| **Atypical carcinoid (AC)** | **G2 (Intermediate)** | **2–10** | **Focal (punctate)** | **5–20%** | **Moderate (~30-50%)** |

> **FCPS/MRCP tip**: **Typical** = <2 mitoses, no necrosis. **Atypical** = 2-10 mitoses and/or focal necrosis. **Ki-67 not required for grading but supportive**.

### Comparison with Other Pulmonary NETs
| Tumour | Grade | Mitoses | Differentiation | Prognosis |
|--------|-------|---------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Typical carcinoid** | G1 | <2 | Well-differentiated | Excellent (5-yr OS >90%) |
| **Atypical carcinoid** | G2 | 2-10 | Well-differentiated | Good (5-yr OS 60-80%) |
| **Large cell NE carcinoma (LCNEC)** | G3 | >10 | Poorly differentiated | Poor |
| **Small cell lung cancer (SCLC)** | G3 | High | Poorly differentiated | Poor |

## 4. Epidemiology & Risk Factors
- **Age**: **Young adults (30-50y)**, average ~45y (younger than NSCLC)
- **Sex**: Female ≈ Male (slight female predominance)
- **Smoking**: NOT a major risk factor (unlike NSCLC/SCLC)
- **MEN1**: **~5-10% of bronchial carcinoids** (assess for hyperparathyroidism, pituitary adenoma, pancreatic NETs)
- **Family history**: Rare, but MEN1 predisposes

## 5. Clinical Features
### 1. Obstructive Symptoms (Most Common, ~70-80%)
- **Cough** (persistent, often dry or mucoid)
- **Haemoptysis** (streaky to massive, from friable vascular tumour)
- **Wheeze** (monophonic, fixed, unilateral — "carcinoid wheeze")
- **Recurrent post-obstructive pneumonia** (same lobe)
- **Dyspnoea** (if significant obstruction)

### 2. Carcinoid Syndrome (Rare, <5% of Bronchial Carcinoids)
- **Flushing** (face, neck, upper chest — dry, not sweating)
- **Diarrhoea** (watery, secretory)
- **Bronchospasm** (wheeze)
- **Valvular heart disease** (right-sided: tricuspid regurgitation, pulmonary stenosis)
- **Carcinoid crisis** (severe hypotension, bronchospasm, flushing — perioperative stress)

> **FCPS/MRCP tip**: **Carcinoid syndrome = hepatic metastases** (liver bypasses first-pass metabolism of 5-HT). **Bronchial carcinoids drain to systemic circulation directly** → syndrome can occur WITHOUT liver mets (unlike GI carcinoids).

### 3. Cushing Syndrome (Ectopic ACTH)
- **Rapid onset**, hypokalaemia, metabolic alkalosis, hypertension, hyperglycaemia
- **More common with atypical carcinoids**

### 4. Incidental Finding
- **Asymptomatic nodule** on CXR/CT for other reasons

## 6. Investigations
### 1. Imaging
| Modality | Findings |
|----------|----------|
| **CXR** | **Central nodule/mass** (often hilar/perihilar), **post-obstructive collapse/consolidation**, calcifications (10-20%) |
| **CT Chest** | **Well-defined central mass**, **enhancement**, calcification, lymph nodes, distal collapse |
| **Octreotide Scan (Somatostatin Receptor Scintigraphy)** | **Positive** (SSTR2+), whole-body staging |
| **Ga-68 DOTATATE PET/CT** | **Superior sensitivity/specificity** (gold standard for NET staging) |
| **FDG PET/CT** | Variable (typical carcinoids often FDG-negative; atypical may be positive) |

### 2. Bronchoscopy
- **Endobronchial mass** (vascular, friable, "cherry-red")
- **Biopsy** — **diagnostic** (histology + IHC)
- **Assess resectability** (airway involvement, carinal distance)

### 3. Biochemistry (Neuroendocrine Markers)
| Marker | Significance |
|--------|--------------|
| **Serum Chromogranin A** | **General NET marker**, elevated in carcinoids (sensitivity ~80%) |
| **24h Urinary 5-HIAA** | **Serotonin metabolite**, elevated in carcinoid syndrome (specificity high) |
| **Serum Serotonin** | Direct measurement |
| **Neuron-specific Enolase (NSE)** | Non-specific, may be elevated |

### 4. Histology & IHC (Diagnostic Gold Standard)
| Feature | Typical | Atypical |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Morphology** | Organoid nesting, trabecular, rosettes | Similar + mitoses/necrosis |
| **IHC** | **Synaptophysin+, Chromogranin A+, CD56+**, TTF-1-, p40- | Same |
| **Ki-67** | <5% | 5-20% |
| **Mitoses (/2mm²)** | <2 | 2-10 |
| **Necrosis** | Absent | Focal (punctate) |

### 5. Staging (TNM 8th Edition — same as NSCLC)
| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **I** | Tumour ≤3cm, no nodes/mets |
| **II** | >3cm or nodal (N1) |
| **III** | N2/N3 or T3/T4 |
| **IV** | Distant mets |
