# Bronchiolitis and Obliterative Bronchiolitis

Related: [[Bronchiectasis]], [[COPD]], [[ILD/Hypersensitivity pneumonitis|Hypersensitivity pneumonitis]], [[Transplant/Bronchiolitis obliterans syndrome|Bronchiolitis obliterans syndrome]], [[Airway Diseases/Other obstructive and small-airway disorders|Other obstructive and small-airway disorders]]

> [!important]
> **Bronchiolitis** = inflammation of bronchioles (<2mm). **Obliterative bronchiolitis (OB)** = fibrotic obliteration of bronchiolar lumen → **fixed airflow obstruction**. **Post-infectious** (adenovirus, RSV), **post-transplant (BOS)**, **connective tissue disease**, **inhalational injury**, **drug-induced**. **Key FCPS/MRCP**: distinction from asthma/COPD, HRCT mosaic attenuation, constrictive vs proliferative, post-transplant BOS, management.

## Learning Objectives
- Define bronchiolitis vs obliterative bronchiolitis (constrictive vs proliferative)
- Identify aetiologies (post-infectious, post-transplant, CTD, inhalational, drugs)
- Apply HRCT findings (mosaic attenuation, air trapping, tree-in-bud)
- Differentiate from asthma, COPD, bronchiectasis, HP
- Manage based on aetiology (steroids, immunosuppression, transplant)

## Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Bronchiolitis** | Inflammation of **bronchioles** (airways <2mm diameter, no cartilage) |
| **Acute bronchiolitis** | Viral (RSV, adenovirus) in infants — usually self-limiting |
| **Obliterative bronchiolitis (OB)** | **Fibrotic obliteration** of bronchiolar lumen → **fixed, irreversible airflow obstruction** |
| **Constrictive bronchiolitis (CB)** | **Concentric fibrosis** → luminal narrowing (most common OB pattern) |
| **Proliferative bronchiolitis (PB)** | **Intraluminal polyps** (Masson bodies) organising pneumonia pattern (COP/BOOP) |

> **FCPS/MRCP tip**: **OB = constrictive (fixed obstruction) vs PB = proliferative (organising pneumonia/COP)**. **Post-transplant OB = Bronchiolitis Obliterans Syndrome (BOS)**.

## Classification by Aetiology

| Category | Causes | Key Features |
|----------|--------|--------------|
| **Post-infectious** | Adenovirus (esp. serotype 21), RSV, influenza, parainfluenza, mycoplasma, measles | Children; often unilateral (Swyer-James/MacLeod syndrome); bronchiectasis |
| **Post-transplant** | **Lung (BOS)**, HSCT (GVHD), solid organ | BOS = leading cause of late mortality post-lung transplant |
| **Connective Tissue Disease** | **RA**, Sjögren's, SLE, systemic sclerosis, MCTD | Often with ILD (NSIP/UIP); CB common |
| **Inhalational / Toxic** | **Nitrogen dioxide** (silo filler's disease), chlorine, ammonia, sulfur dioxide, welding fumes, popcorn worker's lung (diacetyl) | Acute exposure → delayed OB (2-6 weeks) |
| **Drug-Induced** | Penicillamine, gold, sulfasalazine, carbamazepine, 5-FU, bleomycin | Subacute onset; may improve on withdrawal |
| **Idiopathic** | **Cryptogenic constrictive bronchiolitis** | Rare; diagnosis of exclusion |
| **Post-radiation** | Thoracic RT | Fibrotic airway narrowing |

## Pathological Patterns

| Pattern | Histology | Causes |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Constrictive (CB)** | **Concentric fibrosis** → luminal narrowing/obliteration; **no intraluminal polyps** | Post-infectious, post-transplant (BOS), CTD, inhalational, drugs, idiopathic |
| **Proliferative (PB) / BOOP / COP** | **Intraluminal buds of granulation tissue (Masson bodies)** extending into alveoli | Cryptogenic organising pneumonia (COP), CTD, post-infectious, drugs |

> **Key distinction**: **CB = fixed obstruction (irreversible)**; **PB = organising pneumonia (responsive to steroids)**.

## Clinical Features

| Presentation | Typical Features |
|--------------|------------------|
| **Dyspnoea** | Progressive, exertional (often out of proportion to spirometry) |
| **Cough** | Dry or minimally productive |
| **Wheeze** | May be present (fixed wheeze) |
| **Exercise limitation** | Disproportionate to FEV₁ |
| **Crackles** | Early inspiratory (if associated ILD) |
| **Signs of cause** | CTD features, transplant history, exposure history |

### Syndrome-Specific
| Syndrome | Key Features |
|----------|--------------|
| **Post-infectious (Swyer-James/MacLeod)** | Unilateral hyperlucent lung, bronchiectasis, recurrent infections; often post-adenovirus |
| **Post-transplant BOS** | Progressive FEV₁ decline >20% from baseline; FEV₁/FVC <0.7; no other cause |
| **CTD-associated** | RA/Sjögren's/SLE; often with NSIP/UIP pattern ILD |
| **Inhalational (diacetyl)** | "Popcorn worker's lung" — fixed obstruction, popcorn factory workers |
| **Penicillamine/Gold** | Rheumatoid arthritis treatment; subacute onset |

## Investigations

### Pulmonary Function Tests
| Pattern | Findings |
|---------|----------|
| **Spirometry** | **Irreversible obstructive** (FEV₁/FVC <0.7, no reversibility) |
| **Lung volumes** | **Air trapping**: ↑ RV, ↑ RV/TLC |
| **DLCO** | Usually **preserved** (unless concomitant ILD/emphysema) |
| **Flow-volume loop** | **Concave expiratory limb** (fixed obstruction); plateau |

### HRCT (Diagnostic Gold Standard)
| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Mosaic attenuation** | **Patchy lucency** (air trapping) + dense areas = **hallmark** |
| **Expiratory air trapping** | **Lucent areas persist on expiration** (key diagnostic) |
| **Tree-in-bud** | Centrilobular nodules (if infectious/inflammatory component) |
| **Bronchial wall thickening** | Non-specific |
| **Bronchiectasis** | May coexist (post-infectious) |
| **Centrilobular emphysema** | Differential (smoking) |

> **HRCT Protocol**: **Inspiratory + Expiratory** slices — air trapping only visible on expiration!

### Bronchoscopy
- Usually **normal** airways (small airway disease)
- **BAL**: neutrophilia (if neutrophilic inflammation); exclude infection
- **Transbronchial biopsy**: limited yield (small airways); may show fibrosis

### Pulmonary Function — Serial Monitoring
- **BOS grading** (post-transplant): FEV₁ decline from baseline
  - BOS 0-p: FEV₁ 81-90% + FEF₂₅-₇₅ <75%
  - BOS 1: FEV₁ 66-80%
  - BOS 2: FEV₁ 51-65%
  - BOS 3: FEV₁ ≤50%

## Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Differentiating Features |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **COPD** | Smoking history, centriacinar emphysema, reversibility, DLCO ↓ |
| **Asthma** | Reversible obstruction, eosinophilia, variability, atopy |
| **Bronchiectasis** | Productive cough, HRCT: dilated bronchi (signet ring), mucus plugging |
| **IPF/UIP/NSIP** | Restrictive + HRCT honeycombing/ground-glass (ILD dominant) |
| **HP** | Exposure history, centrilobular nodules, mosaic attenuation + ground-glass |
| **Eosinophilic granulomatosis (EGPA)** | ANCA+, eosinophilia, vasculitis features |
| **Swyer-James (unilateral)** | Unilateral hyperlucent lung, post-infectious |

## Management by Aetiology

### General Principles
- **Remove exposure** (inhalational, drug)
- **Treat underlying cause** (infection, CTD, GVHD)
- **Bronchodilators** (LAMA/LABA) — symptomatic relief only
- **Corticosteroids** — **proliferative (PB/COP) responsive**; **constrictive (CB) usually refractory**

### Aetiology-Specific

| Aetiology | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| **Post-infectious** | Supportive; bronchodilators; macrolides if neutrophilic; surgery if localised (Swyer-James) |
| **Post-transplant (BOS)** | **Augment immunosuppression** (tacrolimus ↑, add sirolimus/everolimus); **azithromycin 250mg 3x/wk** (↓ decline); **Montelukast**; **retransplant** if end-stage |
| **CTD-associated** | **High-dose steroids** (1mg/kg) → taper; **immunosuppressants** (MMF, azathioprine, rituximab); treat underlying CTD |
| **Inhalational** | **Remove exposure**; steroids (limited evidence); bronchodilators; lung transplant if end-stage |
| **Drug-induced** | **Stop drug**; steroids if proliferative; may be irreversible if constrictive |
| **Rheumatoid / Sjögren's** | Treat underlying CTD (methotrexate, biologics) + bronchodilators |
| **Penicillamine/Gold** | Stop drug; steroids may help if proliferative |

### 1. Bronchodilators (All Types)
- **LAMA** (tiotropium, glycopyrronium) — first-line
- **LABA** (formoterol, salmeterol) — add if needed
- **SABA** — rescue

### 2. Corticosteroids
| Pattern | Response |
|---------|----------|
| **Proliferative (PB/COP)** | **Excellent** — prednisolone 0.5-1mg/kg → taper |
| **Constrictive (CB)** | **Poor** — may slow progression; high-dose trial sometimes tried |

### 3. Immunosuppressants (CTD / Refractory)
- **Azathioprine**, **MMF**, **rituximab**, **cyclophosphamide** (severe)
- **Calcineurin inhibitors** (tacrolimus) — post-transplant
- **Azithromycin** 250mg 3x/wk — BOS (neuroimmunomodulation)

### 4. Specific Therapies
| Aetiology | Specific Treatment |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Post-transplant BOS** | ↑ tacrolimus, add sirolimus/everolimus; **azithromycin 250mg 3x/wk**; montelukast; consider retransplant |
| **Azithromycin** | 250mg 3x/wk (anti-inflammatory, ↓ BOS progression) |
| **Montelukast** | Leukotriene antagonist — BOS, aspirin-exacerbated |

### 5. Lung Transplantation
- **Indication**: end-stage BOS, refractory CB, progressive despite immunosuppression
- **BOS**: transplant-free survival ~3-5 yrs from onset

## Prognosis
| Type | Prognosis |
|------|-----------|
| **Proliferative (PB/COP)** | **Good** — responsive to steroids; may relapse |
| **Constrictive (CB)** | **Poor** — progressive, often fatal in 5-10 yrs without transplant |
| **Post-transplant BOS** | Survival ~3-5 years from diagnosis; leading cause of death >1yr post-transplant |
| **CTD-associated** | Variable; depends on CTD control + OB severity |
| **Inhalational (diacetyl)** | Progressive if exposure continues; may stabilise on removal |

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
1. **Obliterative bronchiolitis** = fibrotic airway obliteration → **fixed obstructive defect** (irreversible)
2. **Constrictive (CB)** = concentric fibrosis (fixed obstruction); **Proliferative (PB/BOOP/COP)** = intraluminal polyps (steroid-responsive)
3. **HRCT hallmark**: **mosaic attenuation + expiratory air trapping** (diagnostic)
4. **BOS (post-transplant)** = FEV₁ decline >20% baseline; azithromycin 250mg 3x/wk slows progression
4. **CTD-associated**: RA/Sjögren's/SLE — treat underlying CTD + immunosuppressants
5. **Inhalational**: diacetyl (popcorn lung), NO₂ (silo filler's) — remove exposure
5. **Post-infectious**: adenovirus → Swyer-James (unilateral hyperlucent lung)
6. **Drug-induced**: penicillamine, gold, 5-FU — stop drug; CB often irreversible
7. **HRCT**: mosaic attenuation + **expiratory air trapping** (diagnostic)
8. **Spirometry**: irreversible obstruction + air trapping (↑ RV, RV/TLC)
9. **DLCO usually preserved** in pure OB (vs COPD/ILD)
10. **Constrictive = irreversible; Proliferative = steroid-responsive**

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- **OB ≠ asthma/COPD**: fixed obstruction, **no reversibility**, DLCO preserved
- **Constrictive ≠ Proliferative**: CB = fibrosis (irreversible); PB = organising pneumonia (steroid responsive)
- **BOS** = post-transplant OB; **diagnosis of exclusion** (FEV₁ decline >20% + no infection/rejection)
- **Swyer-James** = unilateral hyperlucent lung post-adenovirus
- **HRCT expiration essential** for air trapping (inspiratory alone misses it)
- **DLCO preserved** in pure OB (vs COPD = ↓ DLCO)
- **Azithromycin** = evidence in BOS (neuroimmunomodulation), not other OB types
- **Penicillamine/Gold** = drug-induced CB, often irreversible

## Mnemonics
- **OB TYPES**: **C**onstrictive = **C**oncentric **F**ibrosis (Irreversible); **P**roliferative = **P**olyps **M**asson (Steroid responsive)
- **OB CAUSES**: **P**ost-infectious, **P**ost-transplant, **C**TD, **I**nhalational, **D**rugs, **I**diopathic
- **HRCT FINDINGS**: **M**osaic **A**ttenuation + **E**xpiratory **A**ir **T**rapping = **OB**
- **BOS**: **B**ronchiolitis **O**bliterans **S**yndrome = **F**EV₁ **D**ecline >20% **P**ost-transplant
- **CONSTRICTIVE vs PROLIFERATIVE**: **C**onstrictive = **C**oncentric **F**ibrosis (No steroids); **P**roliferative = **P**olyps (Steroids work)
