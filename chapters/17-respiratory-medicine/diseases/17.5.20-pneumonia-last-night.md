# Pneumonia — Last Night Before Exam

Related: [[Pneumonia]], [[Respiratory Failure]], [[Pleural Effusion]], [[Tuberculosis]], [[ABG Interpretation]], [[Chest X-Ray Approach]]

## 1. Core Definition
- **Pneumonia** = acute infection of **lung parenchyma/alveoli**
- Causes:
  - alveolar inflammation
  - exudation/consolidation
  - impaired gas exchange
  - systemic illness

> [!important]
> Exam trigger phrase: **fever + cough + focal chest signs + CXR infiltrate/consolidation**.

## 2. Classic Clinical Picture
- Fever
- Cough
- Purulent sputum
- Dyspnea
- Pleuritic chest pain
- Malaise
- Confusion in elderly may be presenting feature

## 3. Key Pathophysiology
- Organisms reach distal air spaces
- Alveoli fill with inflammatory exudate
- Consolidation develops
- Ventilation falls in affected segments
- **V/Q mismatch / shunt** causes hypoxemia
- Severe disease may cause sepsis and respiratory failure

## 4. Anatomy Pearls
- Disease mainly affects **alveoli and distal air spaces**
- Type I pneumocytes = gas exchange surface
- Type II pneumocytes = surfactant + repair
- Pleural involvement may cause:
  - pleuritic pain
  - parapneumonic effusion
  - empyema

## 5. Physiology Pearls
- Main oxygenation problem = **alveolar filling → V/Q mismatch/shunt**
- Compliance falls → increased work of breathing
- Early severe disease: low PaO2, often low/normal PaCO2
- **Rising PaCO2 = fatigue / impending failure**

## 6. Classification You Must Know
- **CAP** = community-acquired pneumonia
- **HAP** = hospital-acquired pneumonia
- **Aspiration pneumonia**
- **Typical** vs **atypical** pneumonia
- **Lobar pneumonia** vs **bronchopneumonia**

## 7. Common Causes
### CAP
- **Streptococcus pneumoniae** — most classic
- Haemophilus influenzae
- Staphylococcus aureus
- Moraxella catarrhalis

### Atypicals
- Mycoplasma pneumoniae
- Chlamydophila pneumoniae
- Legionella pneumophila

### Viral
- Influenza
- RSV
- SARS-CoV-2 and others

### Aspiration
- mixed flora including anaerobic/oral organisms depending on context

## 8. Risk Factors
- Elderly age
- Smoking
- COPD / asthma / chronic lung disease
- Diabetes
- Alcohol misuse
- Immunosuppression
- Dysphagia / stroke / aspiration risk
- Recent viral illness
- Hospital stay / ventilation for HAP/VAP

## 9. Signs of Consolidation
- **Dull percussion note**
- **Bronchial breathing**
- **Increased vocal resonance**
- Crackles/crepitations
- Reduced chest expansion locally

## 10. Investigation of Choice
- **Chest X-ray** = key imaging test

### CXR may show
- lobar consolidation
- patchy bronchopneumonia
- interstitial/atypical pattern
- pleural effusion
- cavitation/abscess in complicated disease

## 11. Other Important Investigations
- **Pulse oximetry**
- **ABG** if severe / hypoxemic / drowsy / distressed
- CBC
- Urea/electrolytes/creatinine
- CRP
- Blood cultures in severe disease/sepsis
- Sputum culture if productive and significant disease
- Pleural ultrasound/CT if complication suspected

## 12. CURB-65
- **C**onfusion
- **U**rea >7 mmol/L
- **R**R ≥30/min
- **B**P: systolic <90 or diastolic ≤60
- age **65**+

### Quick interpretation
- **0–1**: often low risk
- **2**: consider admission
- **3–5**: severe pneumonia / urgent admission / possible ICU review

> [!warning]
> CURB-65 supports decisions but does **not** replace clinical judgment.

## 13. Typical vs Atypical Pneumonia
### Typical
- abrupt onset
- productive cough
- focal consolidation
- lobar pattern common

### Atypical
- more gradual
- dry cough
- headache/myalgia
- patchy/interstitial changes

## 14. Differential Diagnosis
- Acute bronchitis
- Pulmonary edema
- [[Tuberculosis]]
- Pulmonary embolism
- Lung cancer / post-obstructive change
- [[COPD]] exacerbation
- Lung abscess

## 15. Management Principles
- Assess severity first
- Give oxygen if hypoxemic
- Start **empiric antibiotics early**
- Hydration / analgesia / antipyretics
- Treat sepsis if present
- Search for complications

## 16. Severe Pneumonia / Sepsis Clues
- RR ≥30/min
- Hypoxemia / cyanosis
- Hypotension
- Confusion
- Poor oral intake
- Multilobar disease
- Rising PaCO2 / exhaustion

## 17. Aspiration Pneumonia Clues
- Stroke
- Dysphagia
- Reduced consciousness
- Seizure/intoxication
- Dependent-lobe infiltrates

## 18. Pleural Complications
- **Parapneumonic effusion**
- **Empyema**

### Important management pearl
- Significant infected pleural fluid may need **diagnostic aspiration** and **drainage**, not antibiotics alone.

## 19. Red Flags / Emergencies
- Respiratory distress
- Hypoxemia
- Shock / hypotension
- Confusion / drowsiness
- Rising PaCO2
- Sepsis
- Empyema
- Failure to improve on treatment

## 20. Complications
- Respiratory failure
- Sepsis / septic shock
- Pleural effusion
- Empyema
- Lung abscess
- ARDS
- Delirium in elderly
- Death

## 21. Top FCPS/MRCP One-Liners
- Pneumonia is infection of **alveoli/lung parenchyma**.
- Main imaging test = **chest X-ray**.
- Main oxygenation problem = **V/Q mismatch/shunt**.
- Classical signs of consolidation = **dullness, bronchial breathing, increased vocal resonance**.
- **Streptococcus pneumoniae** is the classic CAP organism.
- Use **CURB-65 + clinical judgment**.
- Watch for **effusion/empyema, sepsis, respiratory failure, ARDS**.

## 22. Ultra-Short Viva Answers
### Define pneumonia
Acute infection of the lung parenchyma, especially alveoli, causing inflammatory exudation, consolidation, impaired gas exchange, and systemic illness.

### Why does pneumonia cause hypoxemia?
Because alveolar filling reduces ventilation to perfused lung units, causing **V/Q mismatch and shunt**.

### Best imaging test
**Chest X-ray**.

### What is the most classical CAP organism?
**Streptococcus pneumoniae**.

## 23. Last 10 Things to Memorize
1. Alveolar infection = pneumonia
2. Fever + cough + focal signs + CXR infiltrate
3. Classic organism = **S. pneumoniae**
4. Consolidation signs = dullness + bronchial breathing + ↑ vocal resonance
5. Hypoxemia from **V/Q mismatch/shunt**
6. CURB-65 must be known
7. Start antibiotics early
8. Severe disease can cause **sepsis**
9. Pleural complication = **empyema**
10. Non-resolving pneumonia → think TB, abscess, malignancy, resistant organism
