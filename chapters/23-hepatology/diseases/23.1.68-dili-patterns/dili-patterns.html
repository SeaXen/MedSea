2 DILI Patterns in Jaundice 
flowchart TD
    A[Suspected DILI in Jaundiced Patient] --> B{Calculate R Ratio}
    B -->|R > 5| C[Hepatocellular Pattern]
    B -->|R < 2| D[Cholestatic Pattern]
    B -->|R 2-5| E[Mixed Pattern]
R Ratio = (ALT/ULN_ALT) ÷ (ALP/ULN_ALP) — Use ULN-corrected values

7 RUCAM (Causality Assessment) 
flowchart TD
    A[Time to Onset] --> B{5-90 days from drug start?}
    B -->|Yes| C[+2]
    B -->|No| D[+1]
    A --> E{Dechallenge}
    E -->|ALT ↓50% in 8 days| F[+3]
    E -->|ALT ↓50% in 30 days| G[+2]
    E -->|No info| H[0]
    E -->|No decrease| I[-2]
    A --> J{Risk Factors}
    J -->|Alcohol/Age>55| K[+1]
    J -->|No| L[0]
    A --> M{Concomitant Drugs}
    M -->|None| N[0]
    M -->|Possible| O[-1]
    M -->|Probable| P[-2]
    A --> Q{Exclusion of Other Causes}
    Q -->|All ruled out| R[+2]
    Q -->|5/6 ruled out| S[+1]
    Q -->|<5 ruled out| T[-1]
    Q -->|Alternative probable| U[-3]
    A --> V{Rechallenge}
    V -->|ALT doubles| W[+3]
    V -->|Clinical only| X[+1]
    V -->|Negative| Y[-2]
    V -->|Not done| Z[0]

9 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
ConceptKey Points
---------------------
**Hepatocellular**R>5, ALT↑↑↑ — Paracetamol, INH, Anticonvulsants
**Cholestatic**R<2, ALP↑↑ — Amox-Clav, Flucloxacillin, OCP, Chlorpromazine
**Mixed**R 2-5 — Rifampicin, Cotrimoxazole, Tricyclics
**Paracetamol**ALT >5000, NAC within 8h
**Amox-Clav**Cholestatic, Male >55, Post-course onset
**Flucloxacillin**Cholestatic, >55y, >14d use
**RUCAM**≥9 Definite, 6-8 Probable, 3-5 Possible
**Latency**Paracetamol: hours; Others: weeks-months

11 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
DILI vs AIHAIH: IgG↑, AutoAbs+, Responds to steroids; DILI: Temporal drug relation, RUCAM, No AutoAbs
DILI vs ViralViral: Serology+, No drug temporal relation; DILI: Drug temporal relation
Amox-Clav vs FlucloxacillinBoth cholestatic; Amox-Clav: Male>55, Post-course; Fluclox: >55, >14d use
INH vs RifampicinINH = Hepatocellular; Rifampicin = Cholestatic
RUCAM scoringTime onset, Dechallenge, Risk factors, Concomitant drugs, Exclusion, Rechallenge
Paracetamol vs Other DILIParacetamol: ALT>5000, NAC urgent; Others: Variable ALT, Stop drug first
