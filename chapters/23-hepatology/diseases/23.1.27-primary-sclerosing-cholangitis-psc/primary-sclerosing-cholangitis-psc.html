2 Definition & Epidemiology
FeaturePSC
------------
**Definition**Chronic cholestatic liver disease with **fibrosis and stricturing of intra- and extrahepatic bile ducts**
**Demographics****Men 60-70%**, Age 30-40 at Diagnosis
**IBD Association****70-80% Have IBD** (Ulcerative Colitis > Crohn's); **IBD May Precede or Follow PSC**
**Autoantibodies****p-ANCA 65-85%** (Not Diagnostic); ANA/SMA Variable; **AMA Negative**
**Progression**Variable; Median to Transplant/Death ~12-18 Years
3 Diagnostic Criteria 
flowchart TD
    A[Suspect PSC: Young Man, IBD, ↑ALP, Cholestatic Picture] --> B{MRCP (Non-Invasive)}
    B -->|Beading: Multifocal Strictures + Dilatations| C[Diagnose PSC]
    B -->|Equivocal / Pre-MRCP Era| D[ERCP / Percutaneous Cholangiography]
    D -->|Classic Beading| C
    D -->|Dominant Stricture| E[Brush Cytology for CCA]
    B -->|No Biliary Changes| F[Exclude: Small-Duct PSC, IgG4-SC, Secondary Causes]
    F --> G{Liver Biopsy}
    G -->|Onion-Skin Fibrosis, Obliterative Fibrosis| H[Small-Duct PSC]
    G -->|IgG4+ Plasma Cells| I[IgG4-SC]
Diagnostic Requirements
  1. Cholestatic LFTs (ALP >1.5×ULN for >6 Months)
  2. Imaging: MRCP Showing Multifocal Strictures + Dilatations ("Beading")
  • Sensitivity 85-90%, Specificity >95%
  1. Exclusions: Secondary Sclerosing Cholangitis (Stones, Surgery, Ischaemia, IgG4-SC)
  2. Liver Biopsy: Only if Small-Duct PSC Suspected (Normal MRCP) or IgG4-SC
4 MRCP: The "Beading" Appearance
FeatureDescription
----------------------
**Multifocal Strictures**Short, Concentric Narrowing
**Segmental Dilatations**Between Strictures → **"Beads on a String"**
**Involvement****Both Intra- and Extrahepatic** (vs PBC: Intrahepatic Only)
**Sensitivity**85-90% (Better for Larger Ducts)
**Small-Duct PSC**Normal MRCP — Needs Biopsy
FCPS/MRCP: MRCP is First-Line Diagnostic — ERCP Only for Therapy (Stenting, Brushing)
5 IBD Association
AspectDetail
----------------
**Prevalence**70-80% PSC Have IBD; 3-5% UC Have PSC
**IBD Type****Ulcerative Colitis > Crohn's** (UC 90% of IBD in PSC)
**IBD Phenotype****Proctitis/Left-Sided**; Often **Quiescent**; "Backwash Ileitis"
**Chronology**IBD Can **Precede, Coincide, or Follow** PSC
**Colectomy**Does NOT Halt PSC Progression (But Reduces Colon Cancer Risk)
**Surveillance****Annual Colonoscopy** if IBD ≥8 Years (Chromoendoscopy Preferred)
6 Cholangiocarcinoma (CCA) Risk & Surveillance
Risk FactorMagnitude
------------------------
**PSC Itself****Lifetime Risk 10-20%**
**Duration**Increases with Time
**Dominant Stricture**Higher Risk
**Age >50**Higher Risk
Surveillance Protocol (Controversial — No Survival Benefit Proven)
ModalityIntervalNotes
---------------------------
**MRI/MRCP****Annual**Detects Mass, Ductal Dilatation
**CA 19-9****6-12 Monthly**Cut-off >100-200 U/mL (Low Specificity)
**ERCP + Brushing**If Dominant Stricture / MassSensitivity 30-50% Per Session; Repeat ×3
No Consensus on Survival Benefit — But Standard Practice in Most Centres
7 Dominant Stricture Management 
flowchart TD
    A[Dominant Stricture on MRCP/ERCP] --> B{Symptoms / Biochem Deterioration?}
    B -->|Yes| C[ERCP: Balloon Dilatation ± Stent]
    B -->|No| D[Observe / Repeat MRCP 6-12m]
    C --> E{Brush Cytology}
    E -->|CCA| F[CCA Staging / MDT / Transplant Eval]
    E -->|Negative| G[Short-Term Stent (If Needed) -> Remove 4-6w]
    G --> H[Dilatation Alone Preferred Over Stenting]
  • Balloon Dilatation Preferred Over Stenting (Less Cholangitis)
  • Short-Term Stent (4-6 Weeks) If Dilatation Alone Insufficient
  • Avoid Long-Term Stents → Biofilm, Cholangitis
8 Medical Management
DrugRoleEvidence
----------------------
**UDCA****High-Dose (28-30 mg/kg) HISTORICAL** — **NOT Recommended** (RCT Showed Harm)Standard Dose (13-15 mg/kg) May Improve Biochem But **No Survival Benefit**
**Obeticholic Acid**Phase 3 Trial OngoingNot Approved
**Vancomycin**Pediatric/Small-Duct PSC — ExperimentalNot Standard
**Immunosuppression**No Role (Except AIH-PSC Overlap)—
No Medical Therapy Improves Survival — UDCA at Standard Dose Optional for Biochem
9 Complications & Management
ComplicationManagement
--------------------------
**Recurrent Cholangitis**ERCP for Dominant Stricture; Prophylactic Antibiotics (Controversial)
**Cirrhosis / Portal Hypertension**Variceal Screening, HCC Surveillance, Transplant Eval
**Fat-Soluble Vitamin Deficiency**Check A, D, E, K Annually; Replace
**Osteoporosis**DEXA; Calcium/Vit D; Bisphosphonate If Indicated
**Cholangiocarcinoma**MDT; Surgery If Early (Rare); Transplant (Neoadjuvant Protocol - Mayo)
10 Liver Transplantation
IndicationDetail
--------------------
**Decompensated Cirrhosis**Standard MELD
**Recurrent Cholangitis**Quality of Life
**CCA <3cm (No Nodes/Mets)****Mayo Protocol**: Neoadjuvant Chemoradiation → Transplant (Excellent Outcomes)
**Refractory Pruritus**Rare Indication

Recurrence Post-Transplant: 20-30% at 5-10 Years (Diagnostic Criteria Same)

11 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
ConceptKey Points
---------------------
**Demographics**Men 60-70%, Age 30-40
**IBD****70-80%** (UC > Crohn's)
**Diagnosis****MRCP Beading** (Multifocal Strictures + Dilatations)
**Autoantibodies**p-ANCA 65-85%; AMA Negative
**Small-Duct PSC**Normal MRCP + Biopsy (Onion-Skin Fibrosis)
**CCA Risk****10-20% Lifetime** — Surveillance MRI + CA19-9
**Dominant Stricture**Balloon Dilatation ± Short Stent
**UDCA****High-Dose HARMFUL**; Standard Dose Optional
**Transplant**Decompensation, Recurrent Cholangitis, Early CCA (Mayo Protocol)
**Colonoscopy**Annual If IBD ≥8 Years

13 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
PSC vs PBCPSC: Men, IBD, MRCP Beading (Intra+Extrahepatic), AMA-, p-ANCA+; PBC: Women, AMA+, Intrahepatic Only, No IBD
MRCP vs ERCP**MRCP Diagnostic**; ERCP Therapeutic (Dilatation, Brushing, Stenting)
UDCA in PSC**High-Dose (28-30 mg/kg) HARMFUL** (Increased Death/Transplant); Standard Dose Optional Only
Small-Duct PSCNormal MRCP + Biopsy → "Onion-Skin" Fibrosis → Similar Prognosis
CCA Surveillance**Annual MRI/MRCP + 6-12m CA19-9** — No Proven Survival Benefit But Standard
p-ANCA**65-85% PSC Positive** — Not Diagnostic; Also in UC, AIH
