## 3. Pharmacokinetic Changes in Ageing

| Parameter | Change | Clinical Implication |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| **Absorption** | ↓ Gastric Acid, ↓ GI Motility, ↓ Splachnic Blood Flow | Variable (Delayed/Reduced Peak) |
| **Distribution** | ↓ Total Body Water, ↑ Body Fat, ↓ Albumin | ↑ Vd for Lipophilic Drugs; ↑ Free Drug (Low Albumin) |
| **Metabolism** | ↓ Hepatic Mass, ↓ Blood Flow, ↓ CYP450 Activity | **↓ Clearance (Phase I)**; ↑ Half-Life; Phase II (Glucuronidation) Preserved |
| **Excretion** | ↓ Renal Function (Parallel) | ↓ Renal Clearance; Dose Adjust Often Needed |

> **Key**: **Phase I Oxidation (CYP450) ↓↓**; **Phase II Conjugation → Relatively Preserved**

## 4. High-Risk Drugs in the Elderly

| Drug Class | Specific Drugs | Hepatic Risk in Elderly |
|------------|----------------|------------------------|
| **Anticoagulants** | Warfarin, DOACs | **↑ Bleeding Risk** (↓ Albumin → ↑ Free Warfarin; ↓ Clearance) |
| **Antibiotics** | Flucloxacillin, Co-trimoxazole, Macrolides | **Cholestatic DILI** (Flucloxacillin >55y); QT Prolongation |
| **Antiepileptics** | Phenytoin, Valproate, Carbamazepine | **Enzyme Induction + Narrow Therapeutic Index**; ↓ Clearance |
| **NSAIDs** | Diclofenac, Ibuprofen | **↑ GI Bleed, Renal Impairment, Sodium Retention** |
| **Statins** | Simvastatin, Atorvastatin | ↑ Transaminases (Dose-Dependent); ↓ Clearance |
| **Amiodarone** | Amiodarone | **Phospholipidosis**; Long Half-Life (100d) → Accumulation |
| **Methotrexate** | Methotrexate | **Hepatotoxicity/Fibrosis**; ↓ Renal Clearance → Accumulation |
| **Antipsychotics** | Clozapine, Olanzapine | ↑ Transaminases; Metabolic Syndrome |

## 5. Altered Presentation of Liver Disease

| Condition | Typical Presentation | **Elderly Presentation** |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| **Acute Viral Hepatitis** | Jaundice, Fever, RUQ Pain | **Aniicteric, Confusion, Falls, Anorexia, Fatigue** |
| **Acute Cholecystitis** | RUQ Pain, Fever, Murphy's Sign | **Vague Abdominal Pain, Confusion, No Fever, No Murphy's** |
| **Ascites** | Abdominal Distension | **Confused with Obesity, Heart Failure, Constipation** |
| **Hepatic Encephalopathy** | Asterixis, Confusion, Coma | **Subtle: Personality Change, Sleep Reversal, Falls, Incontinence** |
| **HCC** | RUQ Pain, Weight Loss | **Incidental on Imaging, Weight Loss Attributed to Age** |
| **DILI** | Jaundice, Nausea, Rash | **Falls, Confusion, Functional Decline, Anorexia** |

> **FCPS/MRCP**: **Jaundice Often Absent**; **Confusion/Falls = Think Liver** in Elderly

## 6. Common Liver Conditions in the Elderly

### 1. NAFLD/NASH
- **High Prevalence** (Metabolic Syndrome Common)
- **Often Silent** → Detected Incidentally
- **Higher Fibrosis Progression Rate** vs Younger

### 2. Alcoholic Liver Disease
- **Increasing Prevalence** (Retirement, Bereavement, Isolation)
- **"Hidden" Alcohol Use** (Under-Reported)
- **Higher Complication Rates** (Frailty, Comorbidities)

### 3. Drug-Induced Liver Injury (DILI)
- **Polypharmacy** (Average >5 Drugs) → ↑ Risk
- **Age-Related ↓ Clearance** → ↑ Drug Levels
- **Common Culprits**: Antibiotics, Statins, Antiepileptics, Amiodarone, Methotrexate

### 4. Acute Cholecystitis / Cholangitis
- **Atypical Presentation** (No Fever, No Murphy's, Confusion)
- **Higher Complication Rates** (Perforation, Sepsis)
- **Early Surgery Beneficial** (If Fit)

### 5. Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)
- **Increasing Incidence** (Age >60)
- **Often Diagnosed Late** (Attributed to Age)
- **Surveillance if Cirrhosis/F3 Fibrosis** (Same Guidelines)

## 7. Drug Prescribing Principles

### Beers Criteria (Hepatic Focus)

| Drug | Recommendation |
|------|----------------|
| **Amiodarone** | Avoid First-Line (High Hepatotoxicity, Long t½) |
| **Methotrexate** | **Contraindicated** if Significant Liver Disease |
| **Valproate** | Caution (Hyperammonaemia, Hepatotoxicity) |
| **High-Dose Acetaminophen** | **Max 3g/day** (vs 4g Standard) |
| **Nitrofurantoin** | Avoid Long-Term (Chronic Hepatitis Risk) |
| **Isoniazid** | Age >35 → ↑ Hepatotoxicity Risk; Add Pyridoxine |

### Dosing Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Start Low, Go Slow** | 25-50% of Adult Dose, Titrate Slowly |
| **Renal Adjustment First** | Use Cockcroft-Gault (eGFR) |
| **Avoid Polypharmacy** | Review at Every Visit |
| **Monitor LFTs** | Baseline, 2-4 Weeks, Then 3-Monthly |
| **Albumin-Corrected Dosing** | For Highly Protein-Bound Drugs (Warfarin, Phenytoin) |

## 8. Geriatric Assessment in Liver Disease

| Domain | Tool | Relevance |
|--------|------|-----------|
| **Frailty** | Clinical Frailty Scale (CFS) | Predicts Surgical Outcomes, Mortality |
| **Cognition** | MoCA / MMSE | Detects HE, Capacity for Consent |
| **Nutrition** | MNA, BMI, Handgrip | Sarcopenia = Poor Outcomes |
| **Functional** | ADL/IADL | Determines Care Needs |
| **Polypharmacy** | Medication Review (STOPP/START) | Reduces DILI Risk |

## 9. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Concept | Key Points |
|---------|------------|
| **Liver Volume** | ↓ 20-30% by Age 80 |
| **Blood Flow** | ↓ 35-40% |
| **Phase I Metabolism** | **Markedly Reduced** (CYP450) |
| **Phase II Metabolism** | **Relatively Preserved** (Glucuronidation) |
| **Drug Sensitivity** | **↑ Sensitivity to Warfarin, Benzodiazepines, Digoxin** |
| **Jaundice in Elderly** | **Often Absent** (Conjugated Bilirubin >40% Have No Jaundice) |
| **HE Presentation** | **Confusion, Falls, Sleep Reversal** (Not Asterixis) |
| **DILI Risk** | **Polypharmacy + ↓ Clearance** = High Risk |
| **Cholecystitis** | **No Fever, No Murphy's** → Confusion, Vague Pain |
| **Drug Dosing** | **Start Low, Go Slow**; Renal Adjust First |

## 11. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Phase I vs Phase II | **Phase I (Oxidation) ↓↓**; Phase II (Conjugation) Preserved |
| Warfarin in Elderly | **More Sensitive** (↓ Albumin → ↑ Free Drug; ↓ Clearance) |
| Benzodiazepines in Elderly | **More Sensitive** (Lipophilic, ↑ Half-Life, ↑ CNS Effects) |
| Jaundice Absent | **Conjugated Bilirubin Not Always Visible** (Skin Thickness, Anaemia) |
| HE in Elderly | **Confusion/Falls/Sleep Reversal** — Not Classic Asterixis/Coma |
| DILI Detection | **LFTs Often Normal** (Baseline Low); Watch Functional Decline |
| Polypharmacy | >5 Drugs → ↑↑ DILI Risk; Review at Every Visit |
| Dosing Principle | **Start Low (25-50%), Go Slow**; Renal Adjust First |
