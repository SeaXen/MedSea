> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP Priority: HIGH**
> **Variceal Bleed = Portal hypertension complication** — **TG18 protocol**: **Terlipressin + Ceftriaxone + Endoscopy ≤12h → EVL**; **Secondary prophylaxis = NSBB + EVL**; **Gastric varices = cyanoacrylate glue**; **TIPS** for refractory bleeding.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Apply **TG18 acute variceal bleed protocol**: vasoactive + antibiotic + endoscopy ≤12h
- [ ] Perform **EVL** for oesophageal varices, **glue injection** for gastric varices
- [ ] Apply **primary prophylaxis**: NSBB or EVL for medium/large varices
- [ ] Apply **secondary prophylaxis**: **NSBB + EVL combined**
- [ ] Indicate **TIPS** for refractory bleeding (early TIPS for Child B/C, HVPG>20)

## 1. Acute Variceal Bleed Protocol (TG18)

### Immediate Management (0-6 hours)
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Suspected Variceal Bleed] --> B[**Resuscitation**: Airway, 2 large-bore IV, Bloods, Crossmatch 4-6 units]
    B --> C[**Vasoactive Drug ASAP**: **Terlipressin 2mg IV q4h** OR **Octreotide 50mcg bolus + 50mcg/h infusion**]
    C --> D[**Antibiotic Prophylaxis**: **Ceftriaxone 1g IV OD ×7 days**]
    D --> E[**Endoscopy within 12h** (ideally <6h if active bleed)]
    E --> F{Endoscopic Findings}
    F -->|Oesophageal Varices| G[**EVL (Band Ligation) ± Sclerotherapy**]
    F -->|Gastric Varices| H[**Cyanoacrylate Glue Injection**]
    F -->|No Varices| I[Manage accordingly]
```

### Vasoactive Drugs
| Drug | Dose | Advantage |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Terlipressin** | 2mg IV q4h | **First-line**; reduces portal pressure, splanchnic vasoconstriction |
| **Octreotide** | 50mcg bolus + 50mcg/h infusion | Alternative (less ischaemic side effects) |
| **Somatostatin** | 250mcg bolus + 250mcg/h | Alternative |

### Antibiotic Prophylaxis
| Drug | Dose | Duration |
|------|------|----------|
| **Ceftriaxone** | 1g IV OD | **7 days** (reduces infection, rebleeding, mortality) |
| **Alternative** | Norfloxacin 400mg BD PO | If ceftriaxone contraindicated |

---

## 2. Endoscopic Therapy

### Oesophageal Varices
| Procedure | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **EVL (Band Ligation)** | **Gold standard** — place bands on varices, repeat q2-4wks until eradication |
| **Sclerotherapy** | Sodium tetradecyl sulfate / ethanolamine — **if EVL not feasible** |

### Gastric Varices (GOV1, GOV2, IGV1, IGV2)
| Type | Preferred Treatment |
|------|---------------------|
| **GOV1** (continuation of oesophageal) | **EVL** (similar to oesophageal) |
| **GOV2** (fundal, extending along greater curvature) | **Cyanoacrylate glue injection** (N-butyl-2-cyanoacrylate + lipiodol) |
| **IGV1** (isolated fundal) | **Glue injection** |
| **IGV2** (isolated elsewhere) | **Glue injection** |

> **Glue injection**: N-butyl-2-cyanoacrylate + lipiodol (1:1 to 1:3) — **risk of systemic embolisation** (embolise via gastric veins)

---

## 3. Primary Prophylaxis (No Prior Bleed)

| Patient | Recommendation | Evidence |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| **Medium/Large varices (F2/F3)** | **NSBB (propranolol/nadolol) titrated to HR 55-60 bpm or ↓25%** OR **EVL q2wks** | NSBB = EVL for bleed prevention; NSBB also ↓ ascites/HE |
| **Small varices (F1) + red wale signs / Child C** | **NSBB** | High-risk small varices |
| **Small varices, no red signs, Child A/B** | **Surveillance endoscopy q2-3yrs** | Low bleed risk |

### NSBB Dosing & Titration
| Drug | Starting Dose | Target | Contraindications |
|------|---------------|--------|-------------------|
| **Propranolol** | 20mg BD → titrate | **HR 55-60 bpm** or **↓25% from baseline** | Asthma, severe COPD, HR<50, SBP<90, AV block |
| **Nadolol** | 20mg OD → titrate | **HR 55-60 bpm** | Same as propranolol |

---

## 4. Secondary Prophylaxis (Post-Bleed)

| Strategy | Regimen |
|----------|---------|
| **Combined (Preferred)** | **NSBB + EVL q2-4wks until eradication** |
| **Alternative** | **EVL alone** (if NSBB contraindicated) |
| **HVPG-guided** (if available) | Target **HVPG <12 mmHg** or **≥20% reduction** from baseline |

> **NSBB + EVL combined** superior to either alone for preventing rebleeding and improving survival.

---

## 4. Refractory Bleeding & TIPS

### Early TIPS Indications (within 72h)
| Criteria | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Child-Pugh B/C** (Child B with active bleed, Child C <14) | **Early TIPS within 72h** (reduces rebleeding, mortality) |
| **HVPG >20 mmHg** (if measured) | **Early TIPS** |
| **Failure of endoscopic/medical therapy** | **Rescue TIPS** |

### TIPS Complications
| Complication | Management |
|--------------|------------|
| **New/worsening HE** | Lactulose, rifaximin; consider TIPS revision/reduction |
| **Stent stenosis/occlusion** | Surveillance Doppler US q3-6mo; re-intervention |
| **Renal dysfunction** | Monitor creatinine; adjust diuretics |

---

## 3. Primary vs Secondary Prophylaxis Comparison

| Aspect | Primary Prophylaxis | Secondary Prophylaxis |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Indication** | No prior bleed, medium/large varices | Post-variceal bleed |
| **Preferred** | **NSBB or EVL** (either) | **NSBB + EVL combined** |
| **NSBB Target** | HR 55-60 bpm / ↓25% | Same |
| **EVL Schedule** | q2-4wks until eradication | q2-4wks until eradication |
| **Duration** | Indefinite | Lifelong |

## 6. Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)

| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Acute Variceal Bleed — Immediate Management (TG18)?** | **1. Resuscitation**, **2. Terlipressin 2mg IV q4h (or Octreotide)**, **3. Ceftriaxone 1g IV OD**, **4. Endoscopy ≤12h → EVL**. |
| **Terlipressin vs Octreotide?** | **Terlipressin**: First-line, reduces portal pressure via V1 receptors; **Octreotide**: Alternative, fewer ischaemic side effects, shorter half-life. |
| **Ceftriaxone in Variceal Bleed — Dose, Duration, Why?** | **1g IV OD ×7 days**; reduces bacterial infections, rebleeding, mortality (RCT evidence). |
| **Oesophageal vs Gastric Varices — Endoscopic Treatment?** | **Oesophageal: EVL (band ligation)**; **Gastric: Cyanoacrylate glue injection** (GOV2/IGV1/IGV2). |
| **Primary Prophylaxis — NSBB vs EVL?** | **Equal efficacy for bleed prevention**; **NSBB** also reduces ascites/HE; **EVL** if NSBB contraindicated/intolerant. |
| **Secondary Prophylaxis — Gold Standard?** | **NSBB + EVL combined** (superior to either alone for rebleeding prevention and survival). |
| **Early TIPS — Indications, Timing?** | **Child B (active bleed) / Child C (<14)**, **HVPG >20 mmHg**, **Refractory bleed** → **TIPS within 72h**. |
| **HVPG — Target for Secondary Prophylaxis?** | **<12 mmHg** or **≥20% reduction** from baseline. |
| **Gastric Varices — GOV1 vs GOV2 Treatment?** | **GOV1**: EVL (like oesophageal); **GOV2/IGV1/IGV2**: Cyanoacrylate glue injection. |
| **NSBB Dosing — Target, Contraindications?** | **Propranolol/Nadolol titrate to HR 55-60 bpm or ↓25%**; **Contraindicated**: Asthma, severe COPD, HR<50, SBP<90, AV block. |

## 6. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Primary vs Secondary Prophylaxis** | **Primary**: No prior bleed, medium/large varices → NSBB OR EVL; **Secondary**: Post-bleed → **NSBB + EVL combined** |
| **NSBB vs EVL (Primary)** | **Equivalent** for bleed prevention; **NSBB** also ↓ ascites/HE; **EVL** if NSBB contraindicated |
| **EVL vs Sclerotherapy** | **EVL superior**: fewer complications, less pain, faster eradication; Sclerotherapy = backup |
| **Terlipressin vs Octreotide** | **Terlipressin**: First-line, potent splanchnic vasoconstriction; **Octreotide**: Alternative, fewer side effects |
| **Early vs Rescue TIPS** | **Early TIPS**: Planned within 72h for Child B/C, HVPG>20; **Rescue TIPS**: Failed endoscopy/medical therapy |
| **Cyanoacrylate Glue — Risks** | **Systemic embolisation** (via gastric veins → pulmonary, cerebral); use lipiodol mixture, small volumes |

**Mnemonic: VARICEAL-BLEED**
- **V**asoactives: **Terlipressin 2mg q4h** (1st line), Octreotide alternative
- **A**ntibiotics: **Ceftriaxone 1g OD ×7d** (reduces infection/rebleed/mortality)
- **R**escue TIPS: **Child B/C, HVPG>20, Refractory → Early TIPS ≤72h**
- **I**ntravenous: **Terlipressin 2mg q4h** (or Octreotide 50mcg bolus+50/h)
- **C**ombo Prophylaxis: **Secondary = NSBB + EVL** (Gold Standard)
- **E**ndoscopy: **≤12h (Ideally <6h)** — EVL (Oesophageal), Glue (Gastric GOV2/IGV)
- **A**lvarado? No — **ABCD2** not for varices; **Child-Pugh** for TIPS
- **L**igation: **EVL q2-4wks until eradication** (Primary/Secondary)
- **B**leeding Control: **Terlipressin + Ceftriaxone + Endoscopy ≤12h**
- **E**arly TIPS: **Child B/C, HVPG>20, ≤72h**
- **E**VL vs Sclerotherapy: **EVL superior** (fewer complications, faster eradication)
- **A**lternative Vasoactive: **Octreotide 50mcg bolus + 50mcg/h**
- **D**APT not for varices: **Antiplatelet not used** (NSBB for portal hypertension)
