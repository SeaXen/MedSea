# Biliary Tract Disease

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP Priority: HIGH**
> **Biliary Tract Disease = Gallstone disease (colic, cholecystitis, choledocholithiasis, cholangitis, pancreatitis) + Biliary strictures + Biliary tumours** — **Acute cholecystitis** (Tokyo Guidelines), **Choledocholithiasis** (ERCP indications), **Ascending cholangitis** (Charcot's triad, Reynolds' pentad, Tokyo Guidelines), **Gallstone pancreatitis**, **Mirizzi syndrome**, **Biliary strictures** (benign vs malignant), **Biliary tumours** (cholangiocarcinoma types, gallbladder cancer, ampullary).

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Diagnose and manage **gallstone disease**: asymptomatic, biliary colic, acute cholecystitis (Tokyo Guidelines), Mirizzi syndrome
- [ ] Manage **choledocholithiasis** and **ascending cholangitis** (Charcot's triad, Reynolds' pentad, Tokyo Guidelines, ERCP timing)
- [ ] Apply **gallstone pancreatitis** management (early ERCP if cholangitis/severe)
- [ ] Differentiate **biliary strictures** (benign vs malignant) and manage
- [ ] Recognise **biliary tract tumours**: cholangiocarcinoma (intra/extra/hilar), gallbladder cancer, ampullary carcinoma

## 1. Gallstone Disease

### Spectrum
| Condition | Features | Management |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **Asymptomatic Gallstones** | Incidental on imaging | **Observe** (no prophylactic cholecystectomy) |
| **Biliary Colic** | **Episodic RUQ pain**, post-prandial, 30min-6h, no fever/LFT abnormality | **Elective cholecystectomy** |
| **Acute Cholecystitis** | **RUQ pain + fever + Murphy's sign + leukocytosis**; US: wall thickening >3mm, pericholecystic fluid | **Tokyo Guidelines**; **Early cholecystectomy (≤72h)** |
| **Chronic Cholecystitis** | Recurrent colic, thickened wall on US | **Elective cholecystectomy** |

### Tokyo Guidelines 2018 (TG18) — Acute Cholecystitis

| Severity | Criteria | Management |
|----------|----------|------------|
| **Grade I (Mild)** | No organ dysfunction, no extensive inflammation | **Early cholecystectomy (≤72h)** |
| **Grade II (Moderate)** | **Any**: WBC >18k, palpable GB, disease >72h, Marked local inflammation | **Early cholecystectomy (≤72h)**; consider PTGBD if high surgical risk |
| **Grade III (Severe)** | **Organ dysfunction** (cardiovascular, neurological, respiratory, renal, hepatic, haematological) | **Urgent biliary drainage (PTGBD/ERCP)** → delayed cholecystectomy |

### Mirizzi Syndrome
| Type | Mechanism | Management |
|------|-----------|------------|
| **Type I** | Stone impacted in Hartmann's pouch → extrinsic compression of CHD | **Cholecystectomy + CBD exploration** (lap/open) |
| **Type II** | Cholecystocholedochal fistula (erosion into CBD) | **Cholecystectomy + fistula repair + T-tube** |
| **Type III** | Fistula involving CHD proper | **Biliary reconstruction (hepaticojejunostomy)** |
| **Type IV** | Complete destruction of CHD wall | **Hepaticojejunostomy** |

## 2. Choledocholithiasis

### Presentation & Diagnosis
| Feature | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| **Symptoms** | RUQ pain, jaundice, cholangitis, pancreatitis |
| **LFTs** | **Cholestatic pattern** (ALP ↑↑, GGT ↑↑, bilirubin ↑) |
| **Imaging** | **MRCP** (non-invasive, 95% sensitivity) / **ERCP** (therapeutic) / **EUS** (if MRCP equivocal) |
| **Predictors** (ASGE) | **High probability**: CBD stone on imaging, cholangitis, bilirubin >4.3 mg/dL (73 µmol/L), dilated CBD >6mm + stones |

### ERCP Indications (ASGE)
| Priority | Indication |
|----------|------------|
| **Emergent (<24h)** | **Ascending cholangitis**, **Gallstone pancreatitis with cholangitis** |
| **Urgent (24-72h)** | **Gallstone pancreatitis (no cholangitis)**, **Choledocholithiasis with symptoms** |
| **Elective** | **Asymptomatic CBD stone** (incidental), **Biliary strictures** |

## 3. Ascending Cholangitis

### Charcot's Triad vs Reynolds' Pentad
| Component | Charcot's Triad | Reynolds' Pentad (Severe) |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **Fever** | ✓ | ✓ |
| **RUQ Pain** | ✓ | ✓ |
| **Jaundice** | ✓ | ✓ |
| **Altered Mental Status** | | ✓ |
| **Hypotension/Shock** | | ✓ |

> **Charcot's Triad** = **~50-70% sensitive**; **Reynolds' Pentad** = **<10% sensitive** but specific for severe cholangitis

### Tokyo Guidelines 2018 (TG18) — Acute Cholangitis

| Severity | Criteria | Management |
|----------|----------|------------|
| **Grade I (Mild)** | No organ dysfunction | **Antibiotics + Elective ERCP (24-48h)** |
| **Grade II (Moderate)** | **Any**: WBC >12k/<4k, Temp >39°C, Age >75, Hyperbilirubinemia >5mg/dL, Hypoalbuminemia <25g/L | **Antibiotics + Early ERCP (<24h)** |
| **Grade III (Severe)** | **Organ dysfunction**: CV (pressor), Neurological (GCS<14), Respiratory (PaO2/FiO2<300), Renal (Cr>2), Hepatic (INR>1.5), Haematological (Plt<100k) | **Resuscitation → Antibiotics → Emergency ERCP/PTBD (<12h)** |

### Acute Cholangitis Management Algorithm

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Suspected Cholangitis] --> B[Resuscitation: Fluids, Bloods, Blood cultures]
    B --> C[**IV Antibiotics IMMEDIATELY**]
    C --> D{Severity (TG18)}
    D -->|Grade I| E[Elective ERCP 24-48h]
    D -->|Grade II| F[**Early ERCP <24h**]
    D -->|Grade III| G[**ICU + Emergency ERCP/PTBD <12h**]
    C --> H[**Antibiotic Choice**: Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV q6h OR Ceftriaxone 2g q12h + Metronidazole 500mg q8h]
    F --> I[ERCP: Sphincterotomy + Stone extraction ± Stent]
    G --> I
    E --> I
```

### Antibiotic Regimens

| Severity | Regimen |
|----------|---------|
| **Mild/Moderate** | **Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV q6h** OR **Ceftriaxone 2g IV q12h + Metronidazole 500mg IV q8h** |
| **Severe/Septic Shock** | **Meropenem 1g IV q8h** OR **Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g q6h + Vancomycin** (if MRSA risk) |
| **Duration** | **5-7 days** (step-down to oral if improving) |

## 4. Gallstone Pancreatitis

### Diagnosis
| Criterion | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| **Clinical** | Epigastric pain radiating to back, nausea/vomiting |
| **Lipase/Amylase** | **>3× ULN** |
| **Imaging** | **CT abdomen** (revised Atlanta classification); **US** for gallstones; **MRCP** for CBD stones |

### Management

| Severity (Atlanta) | Management |
|--------------------|------------|
| **Mild** | Conservative (fluids, analgesia, early feeding) + **Cholecystectomy same admission** |
| **Moderate** | ICU if needed, fluids, monitor for complications |
| **Severe** | ICU, organ support, consider necrosectomy if infected necrosis |

### ERCP Timing in Gallstone Pancreatitis

| Scenario | ERCP Timing |
|----------|-------------|
| **Concurrent Cholangitis** | **Emergent (<24h)** |
| **Severe Pancreatitis + CBD Stone** | **Early (<24-48h)** |
| **Mild/Moderate, No Cholangitis** | **No routine ERCP**; **Cholecystectomy same admission** |
| **CBD Stone on Imaging, No Pancreatitis** | **Elective ERCP** before cholecystectomy |

## 4. Biliary Strictures

### Classification
| Type | Causes | Features |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Benign** | **Post-cholecystectomy (iatrogenic)**, **Post-ERCP**, **Primary sclerosing cholangitis**, **Chronic pancreatitis**, **Ischemic (hepatic artery thrombosis)**, **Trauma**, **Radiation** | Usually single, proximal to injury site |
| **Malignant** | **Cholangiocarcinoma**, **Pancreatic cancer**, **Gallbladder cancer**, **Ampullary carcinoma**, **Metastatic nodes** | Progressive, weight loss, CA19-9 ↑, irregular stricture on imaging |

### Diagnostic Workup
| Step | Investigation |
|------|---------------|
| **1. LFTs** | Cholestatic pattern (ALP ↑↑, GGT ↑↑) |
| **2. Imaging** | **MRCP** (morphology, location, length); **CT CAP** (malignancy staging) |
| **3. ERCP** | **Direct cholangiography**, brushing cytology, **stent/dilation** (therapeutic) |
| **EUS** | If mass suspected (FNA) |
| **CA19-9** | Elevated in malignant (but also benign cholangitis) |

### Management
| Type | Management |
|------|------------|
| **Benign (Post-cholecystectomy)** | **ERCP: Balloon dilation + Stenting (plastic ×3-6mo)**; **Surgical hepaticojejunostomy** (if failed ERCP, long stricture) |
| **PSC** | **UDCA** (not proven), **ERCP for dominant strictures**, **CCA surveillance** |
| **Malignant** | **Palliative stenting (metal stent)**; **Surgical resection** (if resectable); **Chemotherapy** (if advanced) |

## 4. Biliary Tract Tumours

### Cholangiocarcinoma (CCA)

| Type | Location | Incidence | Key Features |
|------|----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Intrahepatic (iCCA)** | Liver parenchyma (proximal 2nd order ducts) | 10-15% | Mass-forming, periductal infiltrating, intraductal growth; **CA19-9 ↑** |
| **Perihilar (pCCA / Klatskin)** | **Confluence of RHD/LHD** | **50-60%** | **Bismuth-Corlette Type I-IV**, periductal infiltrating; **Jaundice early** |
| **Distal (dCCA)** | **Distal CBD** (below cystic duct) | 20-30% | **Obstructive jaundice**, pancreatic head involvement |

### Staging & Treatment

| Type | Resectable | Unresectable/Metastatic |
|------|------------|-------------------------|
| **Intrahepatic** | **Hepatectomy + LND** (R0) | **GemCis** (Gemcitabine + Cisplatin) ± Immunotherapy |
| **Perihilar (Klatskin)** | **Major hepatectomy + caudate lobectomy + RHD/LHD resection + hepaticojejunostomy** (Type I-IV) | **GemCis** + Radiotherapy ± Immunotherapy |
| **Distal** | **Whipple (PD) / Pylorus-preserving PD** | **GemCis** |

### Bismuth-Corlette Classification (Perihilar CCA)

| Type | Level of Obstruction |
|------|---------------------|
| **Type I** | Below confluence (common hepatic duct) |
| **Type II** | **At confluence** (RHD/LHD confluence) |
| **Type IIIa** | Confluence + **RHD** |
| **Type IIIb** | Confluence + **LHD** |
| **Type IV** | **Bilateral** (RHD + LHD both involved) |

## 4. Gallbladder Cancer

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Incidence** | Rare; **6th most common GI cancer**; **Female > Male** |
| **Risk Factors** | **Gallstones** (70-90%), **Porcelain GB**, **Chronic typhoid carrier**, **Congenital anomalies**, **Obesity**, **Age >65** |
| **Presentation** | **Incidental** (on cholecystectomy for stones), **RUQ pain, jaundice, weight loss**, **Palpable mass** |
| **Staging (TNM)** | **T1**: Mucosa/submucosa; **T2**: Muscularis; **T3**: Perimuscular/serosa; **T4**: Adjacent organ invasion |
| **Treatment** | **T1a (mucosa)**: Simple cholecystectomy; **T1b (submucosa)**: Extended cholecystectomy + LND; **T2+**: Radical cholecystectomy (segments IVb/V + LND); **Unresectable**: GemCis |

---

## 4. Ampullary Carcinoma

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Location** | **Ampulla of Vater** (within 2cm) |
| **Presentation** | **Early jaundice** (obstructive), **Pancreatitis**, **Occult GI bleed** |
| **Treatment** | **Whipple (PD) / PPPD** (standard); Local excision if Tis/T1 small |
| **Prognosis** | Better than pancreatic/CCA (5-yr OS 30-50% if resected) |

## 5. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **Asymptomatic Gallstones** | **Observe** (no cholecystectomy) |
| **Biliary Colic** | RUQ pain, post-prandial, no fever/LFT abnormality → **Elective cholecystectomy** |
| **Acute Cholecystitis** | **Tokyo Guidelines**: Grade I/II → **Early cholecystectomy ≤72h**; Grade III → PTGBD → delayed |
| **Mirizzi Syndrome** | Stone in Hartmann's pouch → CHD compression; Type I/II/III/IV; Cholecystectomy + repair |
| **Choledocholithiasis** | **ASGE High probability**: CBD stone on imaging, cholangitis, bilirubin >4.3, dilated CBD>6mm+stones |
| **Acute Cholangitis** | **Charcot's Triad** (fever, pain, jaundice); **Reynolds' Pentad** (add AMS, hypotension); **TG18 Grade III → Emergency ERCP <12h** |
| **Antibiotics** | **Piptaz 4.5g q6h** or **Ceftriaxone 2g + Metronidazole** (Grade I/II); **Meropenem** (Grade III) |
| **Gallstone Pancreatitis** | **Cholecystectomy same admission** (mild); **ERCP if cholangitis/severe** |
| **Biliary Strictures** | Benign (post-cholecystectomy, PSC) vs Malignant (CCA, pancreas, GB); **ERCP stent/dilation** |
| **Cholangiocarcinoma** | **Perihilar (Klatskin) 50-60%**; Bismuth Type I-IV; **GemCis** 1L systemic |
| **Gallbladder Cancer** | **T1a**: Simple cholecystectomy; **T1b+**: Radical cholecystectomy + LND |
| **Ampullary Cancer** | Early jaundice; **Whipple/PPPD**; Better prognosis than pancreatic/CCA |

| **Mirizzi Syndrome — Types I-IV?** | **I**: CHD compression; **II**: Cholecystocholedochal fistula; **III**: Fistula into CHD proper; **IV**: CHD wall destruction. |

| **Cholangitis — Charcot's Triad vs Reynolds' Pentad?** | **Triad**: Fever, RUQ pain, Jaundice; **Pentad**: + AMS + Hypotension. |

## 6. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Acute Cholecystitis vs Cholangitis** | **Cholecystitis**: GB wall thickening, Murphy's sign, no dilated CBD; **Cholangitis**: Fever + Jaundice + RUQ pain, dilated CBD, sepsis |
| **TG18 Cholecystitis vs Cholangitis Grades** | **Cholecystitis Grade III**: Organ dysfunction; **Cholangitis Grade III**: Organ dysfunction — both need urgent drainage |
| **Mirizzi Type I vs II** | **I**: Extrinsic compression by stone; **II**: Fistula into CBD |
| **Choledocholithiasis vs Cholangitis** | **Choledocholithiasis**: Stone in CBD (may be asymptomatic); **Cholangitis**: Infection + obstruction = fever + jaundice + pain |
| **TG18 Cholangitis Grade I vs II vs III** | **I**: No organ dysfunction; **II**: 1 organ dysfunction marker; **III**: ≥1 organ failure |
| **PTC vs ERCP for Drainage** | **PTC**: Proximal obstruction, failed ERCP, altered anatomy; **ERCP**: First-line, therapeutic (sphincterotomy, stent) |
| **Perihilar CCA Bismuth Types** | **I**: Below confluence; **II**: At confluence; **IIIa/b**: +RHD/LHD; **IV**: Bilateral |
| **Gallbladder T1a vs T1b** | **T1a**: Mucosa only → simple cholecystectomy; **T1b**: Submucosa → extended cholecystectomy + LND |

**Mnemonic: BILIARY-TRACT**
- **B**iliary Colic: **RUQ Pain, Post-Prandial, No Fever/LFT Abnormality → Elective Cholecystectomy**
- **I**nflamed GB: **Tokyo Guidelines I/II → Early Cholecystectomy ≤72h; Grade III → PTGBD**
- **L**ithiasis CBD: **ASGE High Prob: Stone on Imaging, Cholangitis, Bilirubin>4.3, CBD>6mm+Stones**
- **I**nfected Bile: **Charcot's Triad (Fever, Pain, Jaundice); Reynolds' Pentad (+AMS, Hypotension)**
- **A**scending Cholangitis: **TG18 I: Elective ERCP 24-48h; II: Early ERCP <24h; III: ICU + Emergency ERCP <12h**
- **R**ed Flags Pancreatitis: **Gallstone Etiology → Cholecystectomy Same Admission; ERCP if Cholangitis/Severe**
- **Y**ellow Bile Strictures: **Benign (Post-Chole, PSC) vs Malignant (CCA, Pancreas); ERCP Stent/Metal Stent**
- **T**umours: **CCA (Perihilar 50-60%, Klatskin Bismuth I-IV, GemCis); GB Ca (T1a Simple, T1b+ Radical); Ampullary (Whipple, Better Prognosis)**
- **R**isk Factors: **Gallstones (CCA/GB Ca), PSC (CCA), Porcelain GB (GB Ca)**
- **A**mpullary: **Early Jaundice, Whipple, Better Prognosis than Pancreatic/CCA**
- **C**holedocholithiasis: **ERCP Sphincterotomy + Stone Extraction ± Stent**
- **T**okyo Guidelines: **Cholecystitis I/II Early Cholecystectomy ≤72h; III PTGBD**
