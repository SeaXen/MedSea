HEPATOLOGY
Hepatopulmonary Syndrome & Portopulmonary Hypertension
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Diagnose HPS: liver disease + PaO₂ <80mmHg + positive contrast echo (delayed bubbles)
  • [ ] Diagnose PoPH: RHC — mPAP >25mmHg, PVR >240 dyn·s·cm⁻⁵, PAWP ≤15mmHg
  • [ ] Differentiate HPS vs PoPH pathophysiology and management
  • [ ] Select treatment: HPS = liver transplant; PoPH = vasodilators bridge to transplant
  • [ ] Recognise clinical features: platypnoea, orthodeoxia (HPS); dyspnoea, syncope (PoPH)
1 Hepatopulmonary Syndrome (HPS)
Definition (Triad Required)
ComponentCriteria
---------------------
**1. Liver Disease**Cirrhosis or portal hypertension (any aetiology)
**2. Hypoxaemia****PaO₂ <80 mmHg** (or A-a gradient ≥15mmHg age-adjusted)
**3. Intrapulmonary Vasodilation****Positive contrast Echocardiography** (delayed bubbles 3-6 cardiac cycles)
Clinical Features
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Platypnoea****Dyspnoea worse upright, better supine** (gravity-dependent shunting)
**Orthodeoxia****PaO₂ drops >5mmHg or SpO₂ drops >5% on sitting/standing**
**Spider Naevi, Clubbing**Common in chronic liver disease
**Cyanosis**Late sign
Diagnosis
TestFinding
---------------
**Contrast Echocardiography****Delayed bubbles in left atrium (3-6 cardiac cycles after RA)** — hallmark
**ABG****PaO₂ <80mmHg** (room air); **A-a gradient ≥15mmHg** (age-adjusted: <20 + age/4)
**Macroaggregated Albumin (MAA) Scan****Brain uptake >6%** (quantitative, >6% = significant shunting)
**Pulmonary Function****↓DLCO**, normal spirometry
**Chest X-ray/CT**Normal or bibasal reticular changes
Differential: HPS vs PoPH
FeatureHPSPoPH
-------------------
**Pathophysiology**Intrapulmonary vasodilationPulmonary vasoconstriction/remodelling
**PaO₂****Low** (<80mmHg)Normal or mildly low
**Contrast Echo****Delayed bubbles (3-6 cycles)**Normal or early bubbles
**Pulmonary Pressures**Normal/low**mPAP >25, PVR >240**
**Platypnoea/Orthodeoxia****Present (hallmark)**Absent
**Treatment****Liver Transplant (curative)**Vasodilators → Bridge to transplant
2 Portopulmonary Hypertension (PoPH)
Diagnostic Criteria (RHC Required)
ParameterCut-off
--------------------
**mPAP****>25 mmHg** (mean Pulmonary Artery Pressure)
**PVR****>240 dyn·s·cm⁻⁵** (Pulmonary Vascular Resistance)
**PAWP****≤15 mmHg** (Pulmonary Artery Wedge Pressure)
**CO**Normal or high (vs left heart disease)
**Portal Hypertension**Cirrhosis or non-cirrhotic PH
Clinical Features
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Dyspnoea**Exertional, progressive
**Syncope**Exertional (reduced CO)
**Signs****Loud P2, TR murmur, RV heave, elevated JVP, peripheral oedema**
**Syncope on Exertion**Ominous sign (fixed CO)
3 Diagnosis Algorithm 
flowchart TD
    A[Liver Disease + Dyspnoea] --> B{Contrast Echo}
    B -->|Delayed Bubbles (3-6 cycles)| C[HPS Confirmed]
    B -->|Normal / Early Bubbles| D[RHC If PoPH Suspected]
    D --> E{mPAP>25, PVR>240, PAWP≤15}
    E -->|Yes| F[PoPH Confirmed]
    E -->|No| G[Other Causes: COPD, ILD, Left Heart Disease]
    C --> H[ABG: PaO2<80, A-a gradient≥15]
    C --> I[Liver Transplant Evaluation]
    F --> J[Vasodilators + Transplant Assessment]
4 Management
Hepatopulmonary Syndrome (HPS)
TreatmentDetails
------------------
**Liver Transplantation****Only curative treatment** — resolution of HPS in 80-90%
**Supplemental O₂**Symptomatic relief (does not alter disease)
**TIPS****Contraindicated** (worsens shunting, may worsen hypoxaemia)
**Post-Transplant**PaO₂ normalises over weeks-months
Transplant Listing: HPS with PaO₂ <60mmHg = MELD exception (MELD 22 in UK); PaO₂ <50mmHg = higher priority
Portopulmonary Hypertension (PoPH)
TreatmentDetails
------------------
**Pulmonary Vasodilators** (Bridge to Transplant)**Epoprostenol IV** (prostacyclin), **Bosentan** (ERA), **Sildenafil/Tadalafil** (PDE5i)
**Target****mPAP <35mmHg, PVR <400** for transplant eligibility
**Anticoagulation**Consider (venous thrombosis risk) — controversial
**TIPS****Contraindicated** (worsens PoPH)
**Liver Transplant****Definitive** if mPAP <35mmHg on vasodilators; **Contraindicated** if mPAP >35mmHg unresponsive
4 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
TopicKey Points
-------------------
**HPS Triad**Liver disease + **PaO₂ <80mmHg** + **Contrast echo delayed bubbles (3-6 cycles)**
**HPS Hallmark****Platypnoea + Orthodeoxia** (worse upright, better supine)
**HPS Diagnosis**Contrast echo delayed bubbles (3-6 cycles) + PaO₂ <80mmHg + liver disease
**HPS Treatment****Liver Transplant (curative)**; TIPS contraindicated; O₂ symptomatic
**PoPH Diagnosis**RHC: **mPAP >25, PVR >240, PAWP ≤15** + portal hypertension
**PoPH Treatment****Vasodilators (Epoprostenol, Bosentan, Sildenafil) → Bridge to Transplant**
**PoPH Transplant Eligibility****mPAP <35mmHg on vasodilators** = eligible; **mPAP >35 unresponsive** = contraindicated
**HPS vs PoPH****HPS**: Vasodilation, hypoxaemia, delayed bubbles, transplant curative; **PoPH**: Vasoconstriction, high PVR, vasodilators bridge to transplant
**TIPS****Contraindicated in both** (HPS: worsens shunting; PoPH: worsens pressure)
5 Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)
QuestionExpected Answer
---------------------------
**HPS Diagnostic Triad?****1. Liver disease, 2. PaO₂ <80mmHg, 3. Intrapulmonary vasodilation (Contrast echo delayed bubbles 3-6 cycles)**.
**Contrast Echo in HPS — Timing of Bubbles?****Delayed bubbles appearing 3-6 cardiac cycles after right atrial opacification** (vs immediate in cirrhosis without HPS).
**Platypnoea & Orthodeoxia — Definition, Significance?****Platypnoea**: Dyspnoea worse upright; **Orthodeoxia**: PaO₂ drop >5mmHg or SpO₂ drop >5% on sitting. **Hallmark of HPS** (gravity-dependent shunting).
**HPS vs PoPH — Pathophysiology Difference?****HPS**: Pulmonary vasodilation → shunting → hypoxaemia; **PoPH**: Pulmonary vasoconstriction/remodelling → high PVR → right heart failure.
**PoPH Diagnosis — RHC Criteria?****mPAP >25mmHg, PVR >240 dyn·s·cm⁻⁵, PAWP ≤15mmHg** + portal hypertension.
**HPS Treatment — Is TIPS Indicated?****No — TIPS contraindicated** (worsens intrapulmonary shunting); **Liver transplant only curative**.
**PoPH — Transplant Eligibility Criteria?****mPAP <35mmHg on vasodilators** = eligible; **mPAP >35mmHg unresponsive** = contraindicated.
**PoPH Vasodilators — First-Line Options?****Epoprostenol IV** (prostacyclin), **Bosentan** (ERA), **Sildenafil/Tadalafil** (PDE5i).
**Contrast Echo — HPS vs Normal Cirrhosis?****Normal cirrhosis**: Bubbles in LA within 3 cycles (intracardiac shunt); **HPS**: Bubbles appear 3-6 cycles later (pulmonary transit).
**HPS Transplant Priority — MELD Exception?****PaO₂ <60mmHg = MELD 22 exception** (UK); PaO₂ <50mmHg = higher priority.
7 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**HPS vs PoPH****HPS**: Vasodilation → hypoxaemia, delayed bubbles, orthodeoxia; **PoPH**: Vasoconstriction → high PVR, high mPAP, RHC diagnosis
**Contrast Echo — Delayed vs Immediate****Immediate (<3 cycles)**: Intracardiac shunt (normal in cirrhosis); **Delayed (3-6 cycles)**: Intrapulmonary shunting = HPS
**HPS vs PoPH — TIPS****TIPS Contraindicated in BOTH**; HPS: worsens shunting; PoPH: worsens pulmonary hypertension
**Orthodeoxia vs Platypnoea****Platypnoea**: Dyspnoea worse upright; **Orthodeoxia**: PaO₂ drops >5mmHg upright; **Both = HPS hallmark**
**PoPH vs Pulmonary Hypertension (Group 1)****PoPH**: Portal hypertension + RHC criteria; **PAH**: No liver disease, different treatment algorithms
**Contrast Echo — Intracardiac vs Intrapulmonary****<3 cycles**: Intracardiac; **3-6 cycles**: Intrapulmonary (HPS)

Mnemonic: HPS-POPH

  • HPS: Hypoxaemia, Platypnoea, Orthodeoxia, Shunting (delayed bubbles)
  • Portal: Underlying liver disease / portal hypertension
  • Shunt: Intrapulmonary vasodilation → delayed bubbles (3-6 cycles)
  • PoPH: Pulmonary Hypertension (mPAP>25, PVR>240, PAWP≤15)
  • Only Cure HPS: Liver Transplant (TIPS contraindicated)
  • Pulmonary Vasodilators: Epoprostenol, Bosentan, Sildenafil → Bridge to Transplant
  • Hypoxaemia: PaO₂<80mmHg, A-a gradient≥15
  • Platypnoea/Orthodeoxia: HPS Hallmark (Gravity-Dependent Shunting)
