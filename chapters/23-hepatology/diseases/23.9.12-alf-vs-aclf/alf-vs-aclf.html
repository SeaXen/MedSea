3 Diagnostic Criteria Comparison
Acute Liver Failure (ALF)
All 3 Required:
  1. INR ≥1.5
  2. Encephalopathy (any grade)
  3. No pre-existing liver disease AND Illness duration <26 weeks

#### ALF Subtypes (by Time to Encephalopathy) <table class="ctable"><tr><th>Subtype</th><th>Onset to Encephalopathy</th><th>Prognosis</th></tr><tr><td>---------</td><td>------------------------</td><td>-----------</td></tr><tr><td>Hyperacute</td><td><7 days</td><td>Best (Spontaneous recovery 40-50%)</td></tr><tr><td>Acute</td><td>8-28 days</td><td>Intermediate</td></tr><tr><td>Subacute</td><td>29 days - 26 weeks</td><td>Worst (Less regeneration time)</td></tr></table>

Acute-on-Chronic Liver Failure (ACLF) — EASL-CLIF (CANONIC)
All 3 Required:
  1. Cirrhosis (previously diagnosed or diagnosed at presentation)
  2. Acute decompensation (Ascites, GI bleed, HE, Infection)
  3. Organ failure(s) per CLIF-SOFA

12 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
ALF vs ACLF**ALF: NO prior liver disease**; **ACLF: Cirrhosis + Acute Decompensation + Organ Failure**
ALF vs Severe Acute HepatitisALF = INR ≥1.5 + Encephalopathy; Severe Hepatitis = INR ≥1.5 NO Encephalopathy
ALF SubtypesTime to Encephalopathy: Hyperacute <7d (Best), Acute 8-28d, Subacute 29d-26w (Worst)
ACLF-1 vs No ACLFACLF-1 = 1 Organ Failure (or Kidney + Mild Other); No ACLF = 0 Organ Failures
CLIF-SOFA vs SOFACLIF-SOFA: Liver replaces Platelets; Bilirubin ≥12 mg/dL = Liver Failure
King's College vs CLIF-CKCC: Binary (Refer/Don't); CLIF-C: Continuous Score (0-100), Dynamic
Kidney OF in CLIF-SOFACr ≥2.0 OR RRT — Lower threshold than KDIGO Stage 3 (Cr≥3×Baseline or ≥4.0)
