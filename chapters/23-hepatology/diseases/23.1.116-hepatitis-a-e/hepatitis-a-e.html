SEA KNOWLEDGE STUDY NOTES
HEPATOLOGY
Hepatitis A & E
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Diagnose Hepatitis A: IgM anti-HAV, self-limiting, faecal-oral
  • [ ] Diagnose Hepatitis E: IgM anti-HEV, high mortality in pregnancy, chronic in immunosuppressed
  • [ ] Manage acute HAV/HEV: supportive, avoid hepatotoxins
  • [ ] Recognise HEV in pregnancy (high mortality) and chronic HEV in immunosuppressed
  • [ ] Apply prevention: HAV vaccine, hygiene, ribavirin for chronic HEV
1 Hepatitis A (HAV)
Epidemiology & Transmission
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Transmission****Faecal-oral** (contaminated food/water, person-to-person)
**Incubation****15-50 days** (average 28 days)
**Endemicity**High in developing countries; travel-related in developed
**Vaccine****Inactivated HAV vaccine** — 2 doses (0, 6-12 months); **>95% efficacy**
Clinical Course
PhaseFeatures
-----------------
**Incubation**Asymptomatic
**Prodromal**Fatigue, nausea, anorexia, low-grade fever, RUQ discomfort
**Icteric****Jaundice**, dark urine, pale stools, hepatomegaly, **ALT/AST ↑↑ (1000-3000)**
**Convalescent**Gradual resolution over **4-8 weeks**
Diagnosis
TestFinding
---------------
**IgM anti-HAV****Diagnostic** (appears early, persists 3-6 months)
**IgG anti-HAV****Past infection/immunity** (persists lifelong)
**LFTs****ALT/AST ↑↑ (1000-3000)**, bilirubin ↑, ALP normal/mildly ↑
Management
AspectRecommendation
------------------------
**Treatment****Supportive only** — rest, hydration, avoid hepatotoxins (alcohol, paracetamol)
**Isolation****Enteric precautions** 1 week after jaundice onset
**Post-Exposure Prophylaxis****HAV vaccine + IgG (if >40 or immunocompromised)** within 2 weeks
**Vaccination****2 doses (0, 6-12mo)** — pre-travel, high-risk groups, chronic liver disease
Complications (Rare)
  • Acute Liver Failure (<0.5%, higher in >50, pre-existing liver disease)
  • Relapsing Hepatitis A (3-10%, typically 1-3 months later)
  • Cholestatic Hepatitis A (prolonged jaundice, pruritus)
2 Hepatitis E (HEV)
Epidemiology & Transmission
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Transmission****Faecal-oral** (contaminated water, undercooked pork/game), zoonotic (genotype 3/4)
**Genotypes****1 & 2**: Human only, faecal-oral waterborne (Asia, Africa); **3 & 4**: Zoonotic (pigs, deer), foodborne (developed countries)
**Incubation****15-60 days** (average 40 days)
Clinical Features by Genotype
GenotypePopulationCourseKey Features
--------------------------------------------
**1 & 2**Endemic areas (Asia, Africa)**Acute self-limiting**Large outbreaks, waterborne
**3 & 4**Developed countries**Acute** (usually) **or Chronic** (immunosuppressed)Foodborne (pork, game), autochthonous
Clinical Features
PhaseFeatures
-----------------
**Acute HEV**Similar to HAV — jaundice, fatigue, nausea, RUQ pain, **ALT/AST ↑↑**
**Pregnancy (Genotype 1)****Severe** — **fulminant hepatic failure in 3rd trimester**, **mortality 15-25%**
**Chronic HEV****Immunosuppressed** (transplant, HIV, chemo) — **persistent viraemia >3 months** → cirrhosis
Diagnosis
TestFinding
---------------
**IgM anti-HEV****Acute infection** (appears early, persists 3-6 months)
**IgG anti-HEV**Past infection
**HEV RNA (PCR)****Confirmatory**, quantitative (viral load), essential for chronic HEV diagnosis
**LFTs****ALT/AST ↑↑** (similar to HAV)
3 Special Situations
HEV in Pregnancy (Genotype 1)
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Timing****3rd trimester** (highest risk)
**Severity****Fulminant hepatic failure** in 10-25%
**Mortality****15-25%** (vs <1% in non-pregnant)
**Mechanism**Hormonal/immune modulation → ↑ viral replication
**Vertical Transmission****~30-50%** — high risk of preterm labour, neonatal HEV
**Treatment****Supportive only** — **Ribavirin CONTRAINDICATED** (teratogenic)
Chronic HEV in Immunosuppressed
PopulationManagement
------------------------
**Transplant recipients****Reduce immunosuppression** (if possible) → **Ribavirin 600-800mg/day ×3 months**
**HIV (CD4<200)****Ribavirin** (adjust for renal function)
**Haematologic malignancy****Ribavirin** (dose adjust for Hb)
**Duration****3 months** (extend if persistent viraemia)
4 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
TopicKey Points
-------------------
**HAV Transmission****Faecal-oral** (food, water, person-to-person)
**HAV Diagnosis****IgM anti-HAV** (diagnostic), IgG = immunity
**HAV Course****Self-limiting** (4-8 weeks), **no chronicity**, vaccine preventable
**HAV ALF**<0.5% (higher in >50, pre-existing liver disease)
**HEV Genotypes****1&2**: Human, waterborne, endemic; **3&4**: Zoonotic (pork), foodborne
**HEV in Pregnancy****Genotype 1, 3rd trimester** → **Fulminant failure, mortality 15-25%**; **Ribavirin CONTRAINDICATED**
**HEV Chronic****Immunosuppressed** (transplant, HIV) → **Ribavirin 600-800mg ×3mo**
**HEV Diagnosis****IgM anti-HEV** (acute); **HEV RNA** (chronic, quantitative)
**Prevention HAV****Vaccine 2 doses (0, 6-12mo)** — pre-travel, chronic liver disease
**Ribavirin in HEV****Chronic HEV only** (600-800mg/day ×3mo); **CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy**
5 Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)
QuestionExpected Answer
---------------------------
**HAV Diagnosis — Serology?****IgM anti-HAV = acute**; **IgG anti-HAV = immunity**; HAV RNA not routinely needed.
**HEV in Pregnancy — Genotype, Mortality, Treatment?****Genotype 1, 3rd trimester**; **mortality 15-25%**; **Ribavirin CONTRAINDICATED** (teratogenic); supportive only.
**HEV Chronic — Who, Treatment?****Immunosuppressed (transplant, HIV, chemo)**; **Ribavirin 600-800mg/day ×3 months** (dose adjust for renal/Hb).
**HAV vs HEV — Transmission, Chronicity?****Both faecal-oral**; **HAV: never chronic, vaccine available**; **HEV: genotypes 3/4 can be chronic in immunosuppressed, no vaccine (UK)**.
**HEV Genotypes — 1/2 vs 3/4?****1&2**: Human, waterborne, endemic (Asia/Africa); **3&4**: Zoonotic (pork/game), foodborne, autochthonous.
**HAV Vaccine — Schedule, Target Groups?****2 doses (0, 6-12 months)**; **pre-travel, chronic liver disease, MSM, PWID, sewage workers**.
**HEV in Immunosuppressed — Diagnosis, Treatment?****HEV RNA PCR (quantitative) >3 months = chronic**; **Ribavirin 600-800mg/day ×3mo** (reduce immunosuppression if possible).
**HEV Ribavirin — Contraindication?****Pregnancy** — **Category X (teratogenic)**; use only in non-pregnant chronic HEV.
**HEV vs HAV — Vaccine?****HAV: Vaccine available (2 doses)**; **HEV: No licensed vaccine in UK** (China has vaccine).
**Acute Viral Hepatitis — HAV vs HEV vs HBV vs HCV — Key Serology?****HAV**: IgM anti-HAV; **HEV**: IgM anti-HEV; **HBV**: HBsAg, IgM anti-HBc; **HCV**: HCV RNA, anti-HCV.
6 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**HAV vs HEV — Chronicity****HAV: Never chronic**; **HEV: Genotype 3/4 can be chronic in immunosuppressed**
**HEV in Pregnancy****Genotype 1, 3rd trimester**; **mortality 15-25%**; **Ribavirin CONTRAINDICATED**
**HEV Chronic****Only in immunosuppressed** (transplant, HIV, chemo); **not in immunocompetent**
**Ribavirin in HEV****Chronic HEV only**: 600-800mg/day ×3mo; **Contraindicated in pregnancy** (Category X)
**HAV Vaccine****2 doses (0, 6-12mo)**; **HEV: No licensed vaccine in UK** (China has vaccine)
**HEV in Pregnancy — Why Severe?**Hormonal/immune modulation → ↑ viral replication; **Genotype 1 most virulent**
**HEV Genotype 1 vs 3****Genotype 1**: Human, waterborne, severe in pregnancy; **Genotype 3**: Zoonotic (pork), foodborne, chronic in immunosuppressed

Mnemonic: HEPATITIS-A-E

  • Hepatitis A: Faecal-oral, IgM anti-HAV, Self-limiting, Vaccine (2 doses), No chronicity
  • Entéric Transmission: Both faecal-oral (water, food, person-to-person)
  • Pregnancy HEV: Genotype 1, 3rd Trimester, Mortality 15-25%, NO Ribavirin
  • Acute Only HAV: Never Chronic (HEV 3/4 can be chronic in immunosuppressed)
  • Target Groups HAV Vaccine: Travellers, Chronic Liver Disease, MSM, PWID, Sewage Workers
  • Immunosuppressed HEV: Chronic HEV → Ribavirin 600-800mg ×3mo (Adjust Renal/Hb)
  • Travel: HAV Vaccine Pre-Travel (Endemic Areas)
  • Immunoglobulin: HAV Post-Exposure: IgG + Vaccine if >40/Immunocompromised
  • Self-Limiting: HAV 4-8 weeks, HEV Acute 4-8wks (Except Chronic in IS)
  • EV Genotypes: 1&2 Human (Waterborne, Endemic); 3&4 Zoonotic (Pork, Foodborne)
  • Hyperacute HEV in Pregnancy: 3rd Trimester, Genotype 1, 15-25% Mortality
