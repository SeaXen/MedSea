# Post-SVR Follow-Up in HCV

## Learning Objectives
- [ ] Define SVR12 and confirm cure
- [ ] Implement post-SVR monitoring schedule
- [ ] Apply HCC surveillance based on pre-treatment fibrosis
- [ ] Assess reinfection risk and prevention
- [ ] Manage extrahepatic manifestations post-SVR
- [ ] Identify FCPS/MRCP high-yield long-term management

---

## SVR12: The Definition of Cure

| Endpoint | Definition | Significance |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| **SVR12** | HCV RNA Undetectable **12 Weeks Post-Treatment** | **>99% Durability = CURE** |
| **SVR24** | HCV RNA Undetectable 24 Weeks Post-Treatment | Historical Gold Standard; Now Equivalent to SVR12 |

> **FCPS/MRCP**: **SVR12 = CURE** — No Need for SVR24 in DAA Era

---

## Post-SVR Monitoring Schedule

| Timepoint | Assessment | Action |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| **SVR12 (Week 12 Post-Tx)** | **HCV RNA Quantitative (Primary Endpoint)** | If Undetectable = **CURE**; If Detectable → **Treatment Failure** |
| **Week 24 Post-Tx (Optional)** | HCV RNA, LFTs | Confirm Durability (Optional) |
| **6 Months Post-SVR** | LFTs (ALT, AST, Bilirubin, ALP, GGT) | Baseline for Long-Term Monitoring |
| **Annually (If No Cirrhosis)** | LFTs, HCV Antibody (Optional) | Monitor Liver Health |
| **6-Monthly (If Cirrhosis Pre-Tx)** | **US ± AFP (HCC Surveillance)** | **Lifelong** |
| **Annual (If F3 Fibrosis Pre-Tx)** | US ± AFP (Consider) | Risk Reduction but Not Eliminated |

## HCC Surveillance Post-SVR

| Pre-Treatment Fibrosis | HCC Risk Post-SVR | Surveillance |
|------------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **F0-F1 (No/Minimal Fibrosis)** | **Near Zero** | **No Routine HCC Surveillance** (unless other risk factors) |
| **F2 (Significant Fibrosis)** | Low | **Consider** US ± AFP 6-monthly (Guideline Variation) |
| **F3 (Advanced Fibrosis)** | **Reduced but Persistent** | **Yes** — 6-Monthly US ± AFP (Some Guidelines) |
| **F4 (Cirrhosis)** | **Significant (1-3%/Year)** | **MANDATORY** — **Lifelong 6-Monthly US ± AFP** |

> **FCPS/MRCP**: **Cirrhosis = Lifelong HCC Surveillance** Even After SVR12

### Why HCC Risk Persists in Cirrhosis
- **Irreversible Architectural Distortion** (Nodules, Fibrosis)
- **Epigenetic Changes** in Hepatocytes
- **Ongoing Inflammation** (Low-Grade)
- **Co-factors**: Alcohol, Obesity, Diabetes, HBV Coinfection

---

## Reinfection vs Late Relapse

| Feature | **Reinfection** | **Late Relapse** |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Timing** | >12 Weeks Post-SVR (Usually >6 Months) | >12 Weeks Post-SVR (Rare) |
| **Viral Strain** | **Different Genotype/Subtype/Quasispecies** | **Same Strain** as Pre-Treatment |
| **Risk Groups** | **PWID, MSM, Haemodialysis, Prison, Unsafe Medical** | **Very Rare (<1%)** |
| **Management** | **Retreat as New Infection** (Same Regimens Work) | **Retreat** (Same Regimens Work) |

> **Key**: **Reinfection ≠ Treatment Failure** — New Exposure

---

## Reinfection Prevention & Monitoring

| Risk Group | Monitoring | Prevention |
|------------|------------|------------|
| **People Who Inject Drugs (PWID)** | **HCV RNA q6-12mo** | Harm Reduction (NSP, OST), Education |
| **Men Who Have Sex with Men (MSM)** | HCV RNA q12mo | Condom Use, Regular Testing |
| **Haemodialysis Patients** | Monthly ALT, q6mo HCV RNA | Infection Control, Machine Dedication |
| **Prisoners** | Annual HCV RNA | Harm Reduction, Education |
| **High-Risk Sexual Practices** | q12mo HCV RNA | Condom Use, Partner Testing |

## Extrahepatic Manifestations Post-SVR

| Manifestation | Post-SVR Course | Management |
|---------------|----------------|------------|
| **Cryoglobulinaemic Vasculitis** | **Improves/Resolves** in Majority | Monitor Rheumatology; Rituximab if Persistent |
| **Membranoproliferative GN** | Proteinuria Improves | Nephrology Follow-up |
| **Porphyria Cutanea Tarda** | Improves with SVR | Phlebotomy, Sun Protection |
| **Lichen Planus** | Improves | Dermatology Follow-up |
| **Sjögren's Syndrome** | Variable | Rheumatology |
| **B-cell Lymphoma** | Risk Reduced but Not Eliminated | Surveillance (If Pre-Existing) |

> **Key**: **SVR Reduces but Does Not Eliminate** Extrahepatic Risk

---

## Metabolic Outcomes Post-SVR

| Parameter | Change Post-SVR | Clinical Significance |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Insulin Resistance / HOMA-IR** | **Improves** | Reduced Diabetes Risk |
| **Steatosis** | **Improves/Resolves** | NASH Risk Reduction |
| **Lipid Profile** | TC, LDL, TG **Increase** (Paradoxical) | Monitor Cardiovascular Risk |
| **Body Weight** | May Increase | Lifestyle Counselling |

## Special Populations Post-SVR

### Cirrhosis (F4)
| Surveillance | Frequency | Duration |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| **HCC: US ± AFP** | **6-Monthly** | **Lifelong** |
| **Varices: Endoscopy** | 1-3 Yearly | If No Varices on Eradication |
| **LFTs / Platelets / INR** | 6-12 Monthly | Lifelong |
| **Nutrition / Bone Health** | Annual | DEXA if Indicated |

### Post-Liver Transplant + SVR
| Surveillance | Frequency |
|--------------|-----------|
| HCV RNA | Month 1, 3, 6, 12, then Annually |
| LFTs | Monthly ×3, then 3-Monthly |
| Rejection / CNI Toxicity | Per Protocol |
| Reinfection Risk | Higher if Immunosuppressed |

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Concept | Key Points |
|---------|------------|
| **SVR12** | **HCV RNA Undetectable 12w Post-Tx = CURE** (>99% Durability) |
| **No SVR24 Needed** | SVR12 Sufficient |
| **HCC Surveillance** | **Cirrhosis (F4): Lifelong 6m US ± AFP**; F3: Consider; F0-F2: Not Routine |
| **Reinfection** | New Exposure (Different Strain) — **Retreat as New Infection** |
| **High-Risk Reinfection** | PWID, MSM, Dialysis, Prison — RNA q6-12mo |
| **Extrahepatic** | Improve Post-SVR (Vasculitis, PCT, Lichen Planus) — Monitor |
| **Metabolic** | Insulin Resistance Improves; Lipids ↑ Paradoxically |
| **Cirrhosis Post-SVR** | **Lifelong HCC Surveillance + Variceal Surveillance** |

## Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| SVR12 vs SVR24 | **SVR12 = Cure**; SVR24 Historical; 99.5% Concordance |
| Reinfection vs Relapse | **Reinfection = New Exposure (Different Strain)**; Relapse = Same Strain (Rare >12w) |
| HCC Risk F4 Post-SVR | **Persists** (1-3%/yr) — **Lifelong Surveillance** |
| F3 Post-SVR | Guidelines Vary — Many Recommend 6m US |
| Lipids Post-SVR | **Paradoxical Increase** (TC, LDL, TG) — Monitor CV Risk |
| Reinfection in PWID | **High** (5-20%/yr) — RNA q6-12mo |
| Cryoglobulinaemia | **Improves Post-SVR** — Rituximab if Persistent |
| SVR12 Durability | **>99%** — Late Relapse <1% |
