2 Definition & Genetics
FeatureAlpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency
----------------------------------------
**Gene****SERPINA1** (chromosome 14)
**Inheritance****Autosomal co-dominant**
**Protein**Alpha-1 antitrypsin (A1AT) — **serine protease inhibitor** (mainly neutrophil elastase)
**Defect**Mutant protein **misfolds → polymerizes in hepatocytes** → **retention in ER** → liver injury + **low circulating levels** → unopposed elastase → lung destruction
**Prevalence**1:2,000-3,000 (Northern European); Carrier 1:25
3 Phenotypes (Alleles)
AlleleLevelClinical Significance
-------------------------------------
**M (Normal)**100%Normal
**Z (Glu342Lys)****10-15%****Severe deficiency**; Polymerizes in liver
**S (Glu264Val)**60%Mild deficiency; rarely causes disease alone
**Null**0%No protein; lung disease only (no liver accumulation)

4 Clinical Manifestations
Liver Disease
PresentationAgeFeatures
-----------------------------
**Neonatal Hepatitis**0-12 monthsJaundice, hepatomegaly, acholic stools; **10-20% of PiZZ**
**Childhood Chronic Liver Disease**5-15 yearsHepatomegaly, elevated LFTs, portal hypertension
**Adult Cirrhosis**30-50 years**20-30% of PiZZ develop cirrhosis**; HCC risk
**Adult HCC**50+ yearsUsually on background of cirrhosis
Liver disease = gain-of-toxic-function (polymer accumulation)
Lung Disease
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Emphysema****Panacinar** (basal predominant) — vs centriacinar (smoking)
**Onset**30-40 years (non-smokers); 20-30 years (smokers)
**Smoking****Accelerates by 10-20 years** — absolute contraindication
**Bronchiectasis**Common
Lung disease = loss-of-function (low A1AT → unopposed elastase)

Diagnostic Criteria
TestDeficiency Threshold
---------------------------
**Serum A1AT Level****<11 μM** (or **<50-60 mg/dL**)
**Phenotyping (IEF)****PiZZ, PiSZ, PiNull**
**Genotyping****Z allele (Glu342Lys)** — targeted PCR
Liver Biopsy (If Needed)
  • PAS-positive, diastase-resistant globules in periportal hepatocytes
  • Electron microscopy: ER dilatation with polymer aggregates
6 Management
Liver Disease
StageManagement
-------------------
**Asymptomatic / Mild**Surveillance: LFTs 6-12 monthly, US 6-12 monthly, FibroScan
**Cirrhosis****Standard cirrhosis care** (variceal screening, HCC surveillance 6m US ± AFP)
**Decompensated / HCC****Liver Transplant** (cures metabolic defect — donor A1AT normal)
**Augmentation Therapy****NOT for liver disease** (does not prevent polymer accumulation)
Lung Disease
InterventionDetail
----------------------
**Smoking Cessation****Absolute** — most critical intervention
**Augmentation Therapy****IV A1AT (purified human)** 60 mg/kg weekly — **slows FEV1 decline**
**Indication****PiZZ or PiNull with FEV1 35-65% predicted**
**Bronchodilators**LABA/LAMA for airflow limitation
**Vaccinations**Pneumococcal, Influenza, COVID
**Lung Transplant**End-stage disease
Other
  • Avoid hepatotoxins (alcohol, unnecessary drugs)
  • Vaccinate: Hep A, Hep B
  • Genetic counselling for family
7 Family Screening
RelativeTesting
-------------------
**First-degree****Serum A1AT level + Genotyping**
**PiZZ Proband**Siblings: 25% PiZZ; Parents: Obligate carriers
**Partner**Test to determine offspring risk
8 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
ConceptKey Points
---------------------
**Gene**SERPINA1 (autosomal co-dominant)
**Classic severe****PiZZ** (10-15% level)
**Liver disease**Gain-of-toxic-function (polymer retention → neonatal hepatitis, cirrhosis, HCC)
**Lung disease**Loss-of-function (low A1AT → panacinar emphysema, basal)
**Diagnosis****Serum A1AT <11 μM** → **Phenotype/Genotype (PiZZ)**
**Biopsy****PAS+ diastase-resistant globules** in periportal hepatocytes
**Augmentation****IV A1AT 60mg/kg weekly** — for LUNG only (FEV1 35-65%)
**Liver transplant**Cures metabolic defect (donor liver makes normal A1AT)
**Smoking****Absolute contraindication** — accelerates emphysema 10-20y
**Neonatal hepatitis**10-20% of PiZZ present in infancy

10 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
Liver vs Lung mechanismLiver = **toxic gain-of-function** (polymers); Lung = **loss-of-function** (low anti-elastase)
Augmentation therapy**For LUNG only** — does not reach hepatocytes in meaningful amounts
PiZZ vs PiNullPiZZ: liver + lung; PiNull: lung ONLY (no polymer → no liver disease)
Neonatal hepatitis**10-20% of PiZZ** — may resolve or progress to cirrhosis
Panacinar vs Centriacinar emphysema**A1AT = Panacinar (basal)**; Smoking = Centriacinar (upper)
A1AT level units**μM** (SI) or **mg/dL**; <11 μM ≈ <50-60 mg/dL
Heterozygote PiMZ**Carrier** — slightly ↑ liver risk if other hits; no lung disease
Co-dominantBoth alleles expressed — MZ = 50-60% level
