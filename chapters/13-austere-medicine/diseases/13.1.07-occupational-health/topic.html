2 📖 Principles of Occupational Medicine
Hierarchy of Controls (Most to Least Effective)
LevelControl MeasureExamples
---------------------------------
**1. Elimination****Remove hazard completely**Substitute hazardous chemical, Automate process
**2. Substitution****Replace with less hazardous**Water-based paint instead of solvent-based
**3. Engineering Controls****Isolate people from hazard**Local exhaust ventilation, Machine guarding, Enclosure
**4. Administrative Controls****Change how people work**Job rotation, Training, Work schedules, Signage
**5. PPE****Protect worker with equipment**Respirators, Gloves, Goggles, Hearing protection
[!tip] Hierarchy = ELIMINATE → SUBSTITUTE → ENGINEER → ADMIN → PPE. Higher Levels = More Effective & Reliable.

7 📖 Noise-Induced Hearing Loss (NIHL)
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Pathophysiology****Noise → Hair Cell Damage (Cochlea) → Permanent Threshold Shift**
**Audiometry****Notch at 4-6 kHz** (Classic), **Progressive to Speech Frequencies**
**Diagnosis****Bilateral Symmetric SNHL**, **Noise Exposure History**, **Audiogram Notch**
**Prevention****Hierarchy of Controls**: **Eliminate → Engineering (Enclosure, Silencers) → Administrative (Rotation, Time Limits) → Hearing Protection (Earplugs/Muffs)**
**Audiometric Monitoring****Baseline + Periodic (Annual/Biennial)**, **STS (Standard Threshold Shift) = 10dB Shift at 2/3/4 kHz**
**Regulations****Action Level: 80 dB(A)**, **Limit Value: 85 dB(A) (8h TWA)**, **Peak: 137 dB(C)**
[!warning] NIHL = Permanent, Irreversible, Bilateral, 4-6 kHz Notch. Prevention = Hierarchy of Controls. Audiometry = Surveillance Tool.

12 🛡️ Risk Assessment — COSHH Approach
StepAction
--------------
**1. Identify Hazards****Inventory Substances**, **Review SDS**, **Walkthrough**, **Task Analysis**
**2. Assess Risk****Likelihood × Severity**, **Who is Exposed?**, **Duration/Frequency**, **Existing Controls**
**3. Control Measures****Hierarchy of Controls (Eliminate → Substitute → Engineering → Admin → PPE)**
**4. Implement Controls****Engineering (LEV, Enclosure), Admin (Rotation, Training), PPE (Fit-Tested)**
**5. Monitor & Review****Exposure Monitoring (Air, Biological), Health Surveillance, Incident Review, Annual Review**

14 ❓ Viva Questions
  1. What is the hierarchy of controls in occupational health?
  • Eliminate → Substitute → Engineering → Administrative → PPE (Most to Least Effective)
  1. What are the radiographic features of Silicosis?
  • Small Rounded Opacities (Simple), Progressive Massive Fibrosis (PMF), Eggshell Calcification of Hilar Nodes.
  1. How do you differentiate Occupational Asthma from Work-Exacerbated Asthma?
  • OA = New Asthma Caused by Work (Latent Period, Sensitiser/Irritant); WEA = Pre-existing Asthma Worsened by Work.
  1. What is the classic radiographic finding in Asbestosis?
  • Pleural Plaques (Calcified, Diaphragm), Basal Interstitial Fibrosis, Honeycombing (Late).
  1. What is the diagnostic criteria for Hand-Arm Vibration Syndrome (HAVS)?
  • Vibration White Finger (Raynaud's) + Sensorineural Symptoms (Numbness/Tingling), Stockholm Workshop Scale, Exposure History to Vibrating Tools.
  1. What is the characteristic audiometric finding in Noise-Induced Hearing Loss?
  • Bilateral Symmetric Sensorineural Hearing Loss with Notch at 4-6 kHz.
  1. What is the relationship between Asbestos and Mesothelioma?
  • Asbestos = Only Known Cause of Mesothelioma (Signature Cancer), All Types Carcinogenic, Latency 20-40 Years.
  1. What is the management of Hand-Arm Vibration Syndrome?
  • Stop Exposure, Warmth, Smoking Cessation, Nifedipine (If Severe), Avoid Cold/Vibration.
  1. What are the common causes of Occupational Contact Dermatitis?
  • Allergic: Nickel, Chromate (Cement), Epoxy, Rubber Accelerators, Formaldehyde; Irritant: Wet Work, Solvents, Cutting Oils, Acids/Alkalis.
  1. What are the criteria for diagnosing Occupational Asthma?
  • Work-Related Symptoms + Objective Evidence (PEF Variability >20%, Specific IgE, Specific Inhalation Challenge).
  1. What are the health surveillance requirements for Lead workers?
  • Blood Lead Level 3-6 Monthly, Action Level >20 µg/dL, Suspension >50 µg/dL, ZPP Monitoring.
  1. What is the difference between Occupational Asthma and RADS?
  • OA = Latent Period, Sensitiser; RADS = Single High Exposure → Immediate Onset, No Latency, Persistent Asthma.
  1. What are the prescribed diseases for Workers' Compensation (UK)?
  • Mesothelioma, Asbestosis, Silicosis, CWP, Byssinosis, Occupational Asthma, NIHL, HAVS, WRULDs, Contact Dermatitis, Occupational Cancers, Infectious Diseases (Lab/Animal Workers).
