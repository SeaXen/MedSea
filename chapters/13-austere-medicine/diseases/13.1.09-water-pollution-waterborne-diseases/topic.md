## 5. 📖 Key Waterborne Disease Profiles

### Cholera (*Vibrio cholerae* O1/O139)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Transmission** | **Water/Food** contaminated with faeces |
| **Incubation** | **2 Hours – 5 Days** |
| **Clinical** | **Painless Rice-Water Diarrhoea**, **Vomiting**, **Rapid Dehydration**, **Rice-Water Stools**, **No Fever** |
| **Complications** | **Severe Dehydration**, **Hypovolaemic Shock**, **Acute Renal Failure**, **Hypokalaemia** |
| **Diagnosis** | **Stool Culture (TCBS)**, **Rapid Dipstick (VC-O1/O139)**, **PCR** |
| **Treatment** | **ORT (WHO-ORS)** → **IV Fluids (Ringer's Lactate) if Severe**, **Doxycycline 300mg Single Dose / Azithromycin 1g Single Dose / Ciprofloxacin** |
| **Control** | **WASH**, **Oral Cholera Vaccine (OCV) - Killed Whole Cell (Shanchol/Euvichol)**, **Sanitation** |

### Typhoid Fever (*Salmonella Typhi*)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Transmission** | **Water/Food** contaminated by chronic carriers |
| **Incubation** | **1-3 Weeks** |
| **Clinical** | **Step-Ladder Fever**, **Relative Bradycardia**, **Rose Spots**, **Hepatosplenomegaly**, **Leukopenia** |
| **Complications** | **Intestinal Perforation**, **Haemorrhage**, **Encephalopathy**, **Relapse** |
| **Diagnosis** | **Blood Culture (Gold Standard)**, **Widal Test (Low Sens/Spec)**, **PCR** |
| **Treatment** | **Ceftriaxone 2g IV BD / Azithromycin 1g OD × 7d** (Resistance Rising) |

### Hepatitis A & E

| Feature | **Hepatitis A** | **Hepatitis E** |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| **Transmission** | **Faecal-Oral (Water, Food, Person-to-Person)** | **Water (Genotype 1/2), Zoonotic (Genotype 3/4 - Pork/Game)** |
| **Incubation** | **15-50 Days** | **15-60 Days** |
| **Clinical** | **Acute Hepatitis, Jaundice, Self-Limited** | **Acute Hepatitis, **High Mortality in Pregnancy (20-25%)**, Chronic in Immunocompromised** |
| **Diagnosis** | **IgM Anti-HAV** | **IgM Anti-HEV, RNA PCR** |
| **Prevention** | **Hep A Vaccine (2 Doses)**, **Hygiene, Safe Water** | **Hygiene, Safe Water, Cook Pork Thoroughly (Genotype 3/4)** |

### Protozoal Parasites

| Parasite | Key Features | Diagnosis | Treatment |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Giardia lamblia** | **Diarrhoea, Steatorrhoea, Bloating, Malabsorption** | **Stool Antigen ELISA (Gold Standard), PCR** | **Metronidazole 400mg TDS × 5-7d / Tinidazole 2g Single Dose** |
| **Cryptosporidium** | **Watery Diarrhoea**, **Immunocompromised: Severe/Chronic**, **Chlorine Resistant** | **Stool Antigen ELISA, PCR, Modified Acid-Fast Stain** | **Nitazoxanide 500mg BD × 3d**, **Supportive (Immunocompetent)** |
| **Cyclospora** | **Prolonged Watery Diarrhoea**, **Fatigue, Anorexia** | **Modified Acid-Fast Stain (Autofluorescence), PCR** | **TMP-SMX (Bactrim) DS BD × 7-10d** |

## 6. 📖 Water Treatment — Multi-Barrier Approach

### Conventional Treatment Train

| Step | Process | Purpose | Key Parameters |
|------|---------|---------|----------------|
| **1. Screening** | **Screens / Microstrainers** | Remove Large Debris | — |
| **2. Coagulation / Flocculation** | **Coagulants**: Alum, Ferric Chloride, PACl | Destabilise Colloids, Form Flocs | **pH, Dose, Rapid Mix** |
| **3. Sedimentation / Clarification** | **Settling Tanks / Lamella Clarifiers / DAF** | Remove Flocs | **Detention Time, Surface Loading Rate** |
| **4. Filtration** | **Rapid Sand / Dual Media / Membrane (UF/MF/RO)** | Remove Particles, Microbes | **Turbidity <0.1-0.3 NTU**, **LRV** |
| **5. Disinfection** | **Chlorination (Primary)**, **Ozone, UV, Chloramines** | **Pathogen Inactivation** | **CT Value, Residual** |
| **6. pH Correction / Fluoridation** | **Lime / Soda Ash / CO2**, **Fluoride** | **Corrosion Control, Dental Health** | **pH 7.5-8.5, F⁻ 0.7-1.0 mg/L** |

### Disinfection Methods

| Method | Mechanism | Advantages | Limitations |
|--------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| **Chlorination (Cl₂, NaOCl, Ca(OCl)₂)** | **Oxidation**, HOCl/OCl⁻ | **Residual Protection**, Low Cost, Established | **DBPs (THMs, HAAs)**, Taste/Odour, Less Effective on Protozoa Cysts |
| **Chloramination (NH₂Cl)** | Chlorine + Ammonia | **Longer Lasting Residual**, Less DBPs | **Weaker Disinfectant**, Nitrification, Lead Solubility |
| **Ozonation (O₃)** | **Strong Oxidation** | **Strongest Disinfectant**, No Residual, Removes Taste/Odour/Colour | **No Residual**, Bryate Formation, High Cost, Complex |
| **UV Irradiation (254 nm)** | **DNA Damage** | **No Chemicals/DBPs**, Effective on Protozoa (Crypto/Giardia) | **No Residual**, Turbidity Dependent, No Residual Protection |
| **Chlorine Dioxide (ClO₂)** | Oxidation | **Effective on Biofilm**, Less THMs | **Chlorite/Chlorate Byproducts, Unstable** |

> [!tip] **Multi-Barrier = Best Protection**. **Filtration + Chlorination = Standard**. **UV/Ozone = Protozoa (Crypto/Giardia)**. **Chlorine = Residual + Cost-Effective**.

## 8. 📖 Water Safety Plans (WSP) — WHO Framework

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[**WSP Development**] --> B[**Team Assembly** (Multi-disciplinary)]
    B --> C[**System Description** (Source → Consumer)]
    C --> D[**Hazard Identification & Risk Assessment**]
    D --> E[**Critical Control Points (CCPs)**]
    E --> F[**Operational Monitoring** (CCP Parameters)]
    F --> G[**Corrective Actions** (Deviation Response)]
    G --> H[**Verification** (Audit, Water Quality Monitoring)]
    H --> I[**Documentation & Communication**]
    I --> J[**Review & Improvement** (Periodic, Incident-Triggered)]
```

### Key Components

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **System Assessment** | **Source Water Quality**, **Treatment Processes**, **Distribution System**, **Consumer Points** |
| **Hazard Identification** | **Microbiological (Pathogens)**, **Chemical (Priority Chemicals)**, **Physical/Radiological** |
| **Risk Assessment** | **Likelihood × Severity** → **Risk Matrix** |
| **CCPs (Critical Control Points)** | **Points Where Control is Essential** (e.g., Disinfection CT, Filter Turbidity, pH) |
| **Operational Monitoring** | **Real-Time/Online** at CCPs (Turbidity, Chlorine Residual, pH, Flow) |
| **Corrective Actions** | **Pre-Defined Actions** if CCP Limits Exceeded |
| **Verification** | **Water Quality Monitoring**, **Audits**, **Consumer Complaints** |
| **Management Procedures** | **Documentation, Training, Emergency Response, Record Keeping** |

> [!tip] **WSP = Proactive, Risk-Based, Multi-Barrier**. **CCPs = Points Where Loss of Control = Unacceptable Risk**.

## 11. ❓ Viva Questions

1. **What is the indicator organism for faecal contamination of water?**
   - **E. coli / Thermotolerant Coliforms** (Primary); **Enterococci** (Marine/Recreational)

2. **What is the treatment of choice for Cholera?**
   - **ORT (WHO-ORS) First**, **IV Ringer's Lactate if Severe**, **Doxycycline 300mg Single Dose** or **Azithromycin 1g Single Dose**.

3. **What is the treatment for Typhoid Fever?**
   - **Ceftriaxone 2g IV BD** or **Azithromycin 1g OD × 7 Days**.

4. **How is Cryptosporidium resistant to chlorine?**
   - **Oocysts Have Thick Wall**, **Highly Chlorine-Resistant**, **UV/Ozone Effective**.

5. **What is the standard water treatment train?**
   - **Screening → Coagulation/Flocculation → Sedimentation → Filtration → Disinfection (Chlorination) → pH Correction**.

6. **What is the CT value in water disinfection?**
   - **CT = Concentration (mg/L) × Contact Time (min)**; **Measure of Disinfection Efficacy**.

5. **What is the WHO guideline value for Arsenic in drinking water?**
   - **10 µg/L**.

6. **What is the point-of-use treatment for emergency water purification?**
   - **Boiling (1 min)**, **Chlorine Tablets**, **SODIS (Clear Bottle + Sun 6h)**, **Ceramic Filter**, **Coagulant/Disinfectant Sachets**.

6. **What is a Water Safety Plan (WSP) and its key components?**
   - **Risk-Based Management**: System Assessment → Hazard ID → CCPs → Operational Monitoring → Corrective Actions → Verification → Management Procedures.

7. **How do Cryptosporidium oocysts resist chlorination?**
   - **Thick Wall, Low Metabolic Rate**, **CT Value Required > Standard Chlorination Provides**.

8. **What is the treatment for Typhoid Fever?**
   - **Ceftriaxone 2g IV BD** or **Azithromycin 1g OD × 7 Days**.

9. **What is the WHO guideline for E. coli in drinking water?**
   - **0 CFU/100mL (Must Not Be Detected in 100mL Sample)**.

10. **What is the arsenic guideline value and its main health effect?**
    - **10 µg/L**, **Skin Lesions, Cancer (Skin/Lung/Bladder), CVD, Diabetes, Neurodevelopmental**.
