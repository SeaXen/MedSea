## 1. 🎯 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Identify **Bartonella species**: *B. henselae* (cat-scratch), *B. quintana* (trench fever, endocarditis), *B. bacilliformis* (Carrion's disease)
- [ ] Recognise **Cat-Scratch Disease**: Subacute regional lymphadenopathy after cat scratch/bite
- [ ] Recognise **Bacillary Angiomatosis/Peliosis**: Vascular proliferative lesions in immunocompromised (HIV)
- [ ] Diagnose **Culture-negative endocarditis**: Bartonella as key cause (with Coxiella, Tropheryma, HACEK)
- [ ] Manage: **Azithromycin** (cat-scratch), **Doxycycline + Gentamicin** (endocarditis), **Erythromycin/Doxycycline** (bacillary angiomatosis)

## 2. 📚 Bartonella Species & Clinical Syndromes

| Species | Disease | Transmission | Population |
|---------|---------|--------------|------------|
| **B. henselae** | Cat-scratch disease, bacillary angiomatosis, peliosis hepatis, endocarditis | Cat scratch/bite (fleas), cat fleas | Healthy (CSD), HIV/CD4<100 (BA) |
| **B. quintana** | Trench fever, bacillary angiomatosis, endocarditis, chronic bacteraemia | Human body louse | Homeless, alcoholics, HIV |
| **B. bacilliformis** | Carrion's disease (Oroya fever, Verruga peruana) | Sand fly (*Lutzomyia*) | Andes mountains (Peru, Ecuador, Colombia) |

---

## 3. 🩺 Clinical Features

### 1. Cat-Scratch Disease (*B. henselae*)
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Incubation** | 3-14 days |
| **Primary lesion** | Papule/pustule at scratch site (50-90%) |
| **Lymphadenopathy** | **Unilateral, regional**, tender, 1-5 cm (axillary, epitrochlear, cervical); suppurates in 10-30% |
| **Systemic** | Low-grade fever, malaise, anorexia |
| **Atypical** | Parinaud's oculoglandular syndrome, neuroretinitis, encephalitis, hepatitis, osteomyelitis |
| **Course** | Self-limited (2-4 months); immunocompetent |

### 2. Bacillary Angiomatosis / Peliosis Hepatis (HIV, CD4<100)
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Cutaneous BA** | Red-purple papules/nodules, friable, bleed easily; mimic Kaposi sarcoma |
| **Visceral BA** | **Peliosis hepatis** (blood-filled cystic spaces in liver), spleen, nodes, bone |
| **Symptoms** | Fever, weight loss, abdominal pain, hepatosplenomegaly |
| **DDx** | Kaposi sarcoma, pyogenic granuloma, melanoma |

### 3. Culture-Negative Endocarditis
- **B. henselae**, **B. quintana** → **Top causes** of culture-negative IE (with Coxiella, Tropheryma whipplei, HACEK)
- **Risk**: Homeless, alcoholics, cat exposure, HIV, valvular disease
- **Features**: Subacute, large vegetations, embolisation, negative blood cultures (standard media)

### 4. Trench Fever (*B. quintana*)
- **5-day fever** (quintan fever), headache, shin pain, rash, relapsing
- **Vector**: Body louse; associated with homelessness, poor hygiene

### 5. Carrion's Disease (*B. bacilliformis*)
- **Oroya fever** (acute): Severe haemolytic anaemia, fever, high mortality (40-85% untreated)
- **Verruga peruana** (chronic): Vascular skin nodules (angiomatous), benign

## 4. 🔬 Diagnosis

| Test | Utility |
|------|---------|
| **Serology (IFA/IgG)** | **First-line**: *B. henselae/quintana*; titre ≥1:64 supportive, ≥1:256 strong evidence; cross-reactivity between species |
| **PCR** (tissue, blood, valve) | **Definitive**: High sensitivity/specificity; species differentiation |
| **Culture** | Difficult: Requires **BACTEC** or **chocolate agar + 5% CO2, 2-6 weeks**; lysis-centrifugation (Isolator) improves yield |
| **Histopathology (Warthin-Starry)** | Silver stain shows **clumps of bacilli** in vascular lesions (BA, peliosis) |
| **Echocardiography** | Valve vegetations (endocarditis) |

> [!tip] **Exam Pearl**: Culture-negative endocarditis + cat exposure/homeless → **Bartonella serology + PCR valve tissue**. Warthin-Starry stain for BA.

---

## 5. 💊 Management

| Syndrome | Regimen | Duration |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **Cat-scratch disease** (mild) | **Supportive** (analgesia, warm compresses); **Azithromycin 500mg day 1, then 250mg daily × 4d** if severe/suppurative | 5 days |
| **Cat-scratch disease** (complicated: neuroretinitis, encephalitis, hepatitis) | **Doxycycline 100mg bd ± Rifampin 300mg bd** | 4-6 weeks |
| **Bacillary angiomatosis** (HIV) | **Doxycycline 100mg bd** OR **Erythromycin 500mg q6h** | **3 months** (until immune reconstitution) |
| **Peliosis hepatis** | **Doxycycline 100mg bd + Rifampin 300mg bd** | 4-6 months |
| **Endocarditis** (native valve) | **Doxycycline 100mg bd + Gentamicin 3mg/kg IV od** | 6 weeks (gentamicin 2 weeks) |
| **Endocarditis** (prosthetic valve) | **Doxycycline 100mg bd + Gentamicin 3mg/kg IV od + Rifampin 300mg bd** | 6 weeks (gentamicin 2 weeks) |
| **Trench fever** | **Doxycycline 100mg bd** OR **Azithromycin 500mg od** | 7-14 days |
| **Carrion's disease (Oroya fever)** | **Ciprofloxacin 500mg bd** OR **Chloramphenicol 50mg/kg/day IV** + **blood transfusion** | 7-10 days |
| **Verruga peruana** | **Azithromycin 500mg od** OR **Doxycycline 100mg bd** | 7-10 days |

> [!warning] **Pregnancy**: Avoid doxycycline/gentamicin → use **Azithromycin** or **Erythromycin**

## 8. 🧠 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Bartonella vs Toxoplasma lymphadenopathy** | Bartonella: cat exposure, regional nodes, suppuration; Toxoplasma: diffuse, often posterior cervical, no cat scratch history |
| **Bacillary angiomatosis vs Kaposi sarcoma** | BA: HIV/CD4<100, Bartonella PCR+, Warthin-Starry+, responds to doxycycline; KS: HHV-8, spindle cells, no antibiotic response |
| **Bartonella vs HACEK endocarditis** | Both culture-negative; Bartonella: cat/louse exposure, homeless, HIV; HACEK: dental procedures, IVDU, native valves |

**Mnemonic**: **"BARTON"** → **B**acillary angiomatosis (HIV), **A**ngiomatosis/peliosis, **R**egional lymphadenopathy (cat-scratch), **T**rench fever (*B. quintana*), **O**roya fever/Verruga peruana (*B. bacilliformis*), **N**egative cultures (endocarditis)
