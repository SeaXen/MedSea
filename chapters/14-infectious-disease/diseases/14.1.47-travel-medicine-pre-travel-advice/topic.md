## 2. 📖 Pre-Travel Consultation Framework

### Consultation Timeline

| Time Before Travel | Action |
|-------------------|--------|
| **6-8 Weeks** | **Ideal** — Vaccines (Multi-dose), Malaria Prophylaxis Start, Insurance |
| **4-6 Weeks** | **Minimum** — Most Vaccines, Malaria Prophylaxis Start |
| **2-4 Weeks** | **Accelerated** — Single-Dose Vaccines, Rapid Schedules |
| **<2 Weeks** | **Limited** — Accelerated Schedules, Standby Treatment, Risk Avoidance |

### Structured Consultation (The "5 Ws")

| Component | Key Questions |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Who** | Age, Medical Conditions, Medications, Allergies, Pregnancy, Immunosuppression |
| **Where** | Countries, Regions, Urban/Rural, Altitude, Season |
| **When** | Dates, Duration, Season (Transmission Seasons) |
| **What** | Activities (Adventure, Water, Animal Contact, Medical Tourism, Sexual) |
| **Why** | Tourism, Business, VFR (Visiting Friends/Relatives), Medical Tourism, Humanitarian |

## 3. 💉 Vaccination — Routine, Required, Recommended

### Routine (Update Regardless of Travel)

| Vaccine | Schedule | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| **Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis (DTaP/Tdap)** | **Booster Every 10 Years** | Tdap Once, Then Td |
| **Measles, Mumps, Rubella (MMR)** | **2 Doses** (If Born After 1970, No Evidence of Immunity) | Live — Contraindicated in Immunocompromised/Pregnancy |
| **Varicella** | **2 Doses** (If No History/Immunity) | Live |
| **Influenza** | **Annual** | **Strongly Recommended** for All Travellers |
| **Pneumococcal (PCV20/PPSV23)** | **Age ≥65 / Risk Groups** | |
| **COVID-19** | **Per Current Guidelines** | |

### Required (International Health Regulations)

| Vaccine | Requirement | Certificate |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Yellow Fever** | **Endemic Countries** (Africa, South America) | **ICVP** (International Certificate of Vaccination/Prophylaxis) — **Valid 10 Days Post-Vaccine, Lifelong** |
| **Polio (IPV)** | **Endemic Countries** (Afghanistan, Pakistan, Outbreak Areas) | **ICVP** (If Stay >4 Weeks) |
| **Meningococcal (ACWY)** | **Saudi Arabia (Hajj/Umrah)** | **ICVP** (Quadrivalent Conjugate) |

### Recommended by Destination/Activity

| Vaccine | Indication | Schedule |
|---------|------------|----------|
| **Hepatitis A** | **All Travellers to Endemic Areas** | **2 Doses** (0, 6-12 Months) — **Single Dose Gives 1yr Protection** |
| **Typhoid** | **Endemic Areas (South Asia, Africa, Latin America)** | **Vi Polysaccharide (Single Dose, 2-3yr)** OR **Ty21a Oral (3 Doses, 5-7yr)** |
| **Hepatitis B** | **Long Stay, Medical/Dental Work, Sexual Contact, Healthcare Workers** | **3 Doses (0, 1, 6 Months)** OR **Accelerated (0, 1, 2, 12 Months)** |
| **Rabies** | **High-Risk Activities (Caving, Animal Work), Long Stay Rural, Children** | **Pre-Exposure: 3 Doses (0, 7, 21-28 Days)** → **Post-Exposure: 4 Doses (0, 3, 7, 14) + RIG** |
| **Japanese Encephalitis (JE)** | **Rural Asia (Rice Paddies, Monsoon), Long Stay >1 Month** | **IXIARO: 2 Doses (0, 28 Days)** |
| **Meningococcal (ACWY)** | **African Meningitis Belt (Dec-Jun), Hajj/Umrah** | **Conjugate ACWY Single Dose** |
| **Cholera (Oral)** | **Humanitarian Workers, Outbreak Areas** | **2 Doses (1-6 Weeks Apart)** |
| **Tick-Borne Encephalitis (TBE)** | **Endemic Europe/Asia (Forest/Hiking, Spring-Autumn)** | **3 Doses (0, 1-3 Months, 5-12 Months)** |
| **Yellow Fever** | **Endemic Africa/South America** | **Single Dose, Lifelong** (ICVP Valid 10 Days Post-Vax) |

> [!tip] **Live Vaccines (MMR, Varicella, Yellow Fever, Oral Ty21a, BCG, LAIV) Contraindicated in Immunocompromised/Pregnancy**. **Inactivated Safe**.

## 4. 🦟 Malaria Prophylaxis

### Geographic Resistance & Drug Choice

| Region | Predominant Species | Resistance Pattern | 1st Line Prophylaxis |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Sub-Saharan Africa** | **P. falciparum** (Dominant) | **Chloroquine Resistance Universal**; Mefloquine Resistance (Border Areas) | **Atovaquone-Proguanil** / **Doxycycline** / **Mefloquine** |
| **South Asia (India, SE Asia)** | **P. vivax > P. falciparum** | **Chloroquine Resistance (P. falciparum)**; **P. vivax Chloroquine Sensitive** | **Atovaquone-Proguanil** / **Doxycycline** |
| **South America** | **P. vivax > P. falciparum** | **Chloroquine Resistance (P. falciparum)** | **Atovaquone-Proguanil** / **Doxycycline** |
| **Central America / Caribbean** | **P. vivax** | **Chloroquine Sensitive (P. vivax)** | **Chloroquine** (If P. vivax Only) / Atovaquone-Proguanil |
| **Middle East** | **P. vivax** | **Chloroquine Sensitive** | **Chloroquine** |
| **Oceania (PNG, Solomon)** | **P. falciparum, P. vivax** | **Chloroquine Resistant (Both)** | **Atovaquone-Proguanil / Doxycycline** |

### Drug Regimens (Adults)

| Drug | Dose (Adult) | Schedule | Key Points |
|------|--------------|----------|------------|
| **Atovaquone-Proguanil (Malarone)** | **250/100mg OD** | **Start 1-2 Days Before → Daily During → 7 Days After Return** | **1st Line** (Well Tolerated, Short Post-Travel, Paediatric Formulation) |
| **Doxycycline** | **100mg OD** | **Start 1-2 Days Before → Daily During → 28 Days After Return** | **Cheap**, **Photosensitivity**, **Oesophagitis** (Take with Food/Water, Upright) |
| **Mefloquine (Lariam)** | **250mg Weekly** | **Start 2-3 Weeks Before → Weekly During → 4 Weeks After Return** | **Neuropsychiatric SE** (Contraindicated: Psych History, Seizures, Cardiac Conduction) |
| **Chloroquine** | **300mg Base Weekly** (5mg/kg) | **Start 1 Week Before → Weekly During → 4 Weeks After** | **Only Chloroquine-Sensitive Areas** (P. vivax/ovale) |
| **Tafenoquine (Kozenis)** | **200mg Single Dose** | **Start 3 Days Before → Single Dose After Return** | **P. vivax/ovale Radical Cure** (G6PD Test Required!) |

> [!warning] **Mefloquine Contraindicated**: **Psychiatric History, Seizures, Cardiac Conduction Defects**. **Tafenoquine: G6PD Testing Mandatory** (Haemolysis Risk). **Doxycycline: Photosensitivity, Oesophagitis**.

### Duration & Timing

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| **Pre-Travel** | **Atovaquone-Proguanil/Doxycycline: 1-2 Days Before**; **Mefloquine: 2-3 Weeks Before**; **Chloroquine: 1 Week Before** |
| **During Travel** | **Daily (Atovaquone/Doxy) / Weekly (Mefloquine/Chloroquine)** |
| **Post-Travel** | **Atovaquone-Proguanil/Doxycycline: 7 Days**; **Mefloquine/Chloroquine: 4 Weeks** |

## 5. 💩 Travellers' Diarrhoea (TD)

### Definition & Aetiology

| Severity | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **Mild** | **1-2 Loose Stools/24h**, No Distress |
| **Moderate** | **≥3 Loose Stools/24h** + **Cramps/Nausea/Fever** |
| **Severe** | **≥6 Loose Stools/24h** + **Fever/Blood/Severe Dehydration** |

| Aetiology | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **ETEC (Enterotoxigenic E. coli)** | **60-80%** |
| **Campylobacter** | **10-20%** |
| **Shigella** | **5-10%** |
| **Salmonella** | **5-10%** |
| **Viral (Norovirus, Rotavirus)** | **10-20%** |
| **Parasites (Giardia, Crypto, Cyclospora)** | **<5% (Usually >14 Days)** |

### Management

| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| **Mild** | **Oral Rehydration (ORS)**, **Loperamide 4mg Stat → 2mg After Each Loose Stool (Max 8mg/24h)**, **Bismuth Subsalicylate** |
| **Moderate** | **Antibiotic + Loperamide** → **Azithromycin 500mg OD × 3 Days** (1st Line) OR **Ciprofloxacin 500mg BD × 3 Days** (If Susceptible) |
| **Severe** | **IV Fluids**, **IV Antibiotics** (Ceftriaxone/Azithromycin), **Monitor Electrolytes** |

> [!warning] **Loperamide Contraindicated**: **Bloody Diarrhoea, High Fever, Suspected Shigella/Campylobacter** (Risk Toxic Megacolon). **Fluoroquinolone Resistance Rising (SE Asia) → Azithromycin Preferred**.

## 6. 🏔️ Altitude Illness

### Definitions & Presentation

| Syndrome | Altitude | Features |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **AMS (Acute Mountain Sickness)** | **>2500m** | **Headache + ≥1: Anorexia, Nausea, Insomnia, Fatigue, Dizziness** |
| **HACE (High Altitude Cerebral Edema)** | **>3000m** | **AMS + Ataxia, Confusion, Altered Consciousness** → **Life-Threatening** |
| **HAPE (High Altitude Pulmonary Edema)** | **>2500m** | **Dyspnoea at Rest, Cough (Pink Frothy Sputum), Cyanosis, Tachycardia** → **Life-Threatening** |

### Prevention & Treatment

| Strategy | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Gradual Ascent** | **<500m/Day Sleep Altitude Gain Above 3000m**, **Rest Day Every 3-4 Days** |
| **Acetazolamide (Prophylaxis)** | **125mg BD PO** (Start 24h Before Ascent, Continue 2 Days at Max Altitude) |
| **Acetazolamide (Treatment AMS)** | **250mg BD** (Until Symptoms Resolve) |
| **Dexamethasone (HACE)** | **8mg IV/PO Stat, Then 4mg Q6H** + **Immediate Descent** |
| **Nifedipine (HAPE)** | **30mg MR BD PO** + **Oxygen, Descent** |
| **Oxygen / Descent** | **Immediate Descent = Definitive Treatment** for HACE/HAPE |

> [!warning] **Descent = Definitive Treatment for HACE/HAPE**. **Do Not Delay**. **Acetazolamide = Prophylaxis + AMS Treatment**.

## 7. 🛡️ Special Populations

| Population | Key Considerations |
|------------|---------------------|
| **Pregnancy** | **Live Vaccines Contraindicated**, **Malaria Risk ↑** (Severe), **Mefloquine 2nd/3rd Trimester Only**, **Doxycycline Contraindicated**, **TD: Azithromycin Preferred** |
| **Children** | **Weight-Based Dosing**, **Live Vaccines Contraindicated <12mo (MMR) / <6mo (Yellow Fever)**, **Mefloquine <5kg Contraindicated**, **Doxycycline <8y Contraindicated** |
| **Elderly** | **Comorbidities, Polypharmacy, ↑ Bleeding Risk (Anticoagulants), ↑ Fall Risk**, **Influenza/Pneumococcal Vaccines Critical** |
| **Immunocompromised** | **Live Vaccines Contraindicated**, **Inactivated Safe**, **Higher Infection Risk**, **Prophylaxis (PJP, Fungal, CMV)**, **Reduced Vaccine Response** |
| **Chronic Disease (Cardiac/Pulmonary/Renal/Diabetes)** | **Medication Adjustments**, **Vaccines Priority**, **High Altitude Risk Assessment**, **Insulin Adjustment at Altitude** |

## 10. ❓ Viva Questions

1. **What is the recommended timing for a pre-travel consultation?**
   - **Ideally 6-8 weeks before travel**; **Minimum 4-6 weeks** for vaccines/prophylaxis.

2. **Which vaccine is required under International Health Regulations?**
   - **Yellow Fever** (Endemic Countries) — **ICVP Valid 10 Days Post-Vaccine, Lifelong**.

3. **What is the first-line malaria prophylaxis for Sub-Saharan Africa?**
   - **Atovaquone-Proguanil (Malarone)** — Daily, Start 1-2 Days Before, Continue 7 Days After Return.

4. **Which antimalarial is contraindicated in psychiatric history?**
   - **Mefloquine** (Risk of Neuropsychiatric Side Effects: Anxiety, Depression, Psychosis, Seizures).

5. **What is the first-line treatment for travellers' diarrhoea?**
   - **Azithromycin 500mg OD × 3 Days** + **Loperamide** (Avoid if Bloody/Fever).

6. **How do you prevent altitude sickness?**
   - **Gradual Ascent (<500m/Day Sleep Altitude Gain >3000m)**, **Acetazolamide 125mg BD Prophylaxis** (Start 24h Before Ascent).

6. **What is the treatment for HACE?**
   - **Dexamethasone 8mg IV/PO Stat → 4mg Q6H + Immediate Descent + Oxygen**.

7. **What is the drug of choice for malaria prophylaxis in pregnancy?**
   - **Mefloquine (2nd/3rd Trimester Only)**; **Doxycycline Contraindicated**; **Atovaquone-Proguanil Limited Data**.

8. **Which vaccine is contraindicated in immunocompromised patients?**
   - **Live Vaccines** (MMR, Varicella, Yellow Fever, Oral Typhoid, BCG, LAIV).

9. **What is the schedule for Rabies Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis?**
   - **3 Doses: Day 0, 7, 21-28** (Intradermal 0.1ml or IM 1ml).

10. **What is the first-line treatment for travellers' diarrhoea?**
    - **Azithromycin 500mg OD × 3 Days + Loperamide**; **Fluoroquinolones (Ciprofloxacin) 2nd Line** (Resistance Rising).

## 11. 🧠 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Mefloquine vs Doxycycline** | **Mefloquine = Weekly, Neuropsychiatric SE**; **Doxycycline = Daily, Photosensitivity, Oesophagitis** |
| **Atovaquone-Proguanil vs Doxycycline** | **A-P: 1-2d Before, 7d After, Expensive**; **Doxy: 1-2d Before, 28d After, Cheap, Photosensitivity** |
| **Mefloquine in Pregnancy** | **Contraindicated 1st Trimester**; **Safe 2nd/3rd Trimester** |
| **Acetazolamide Prophylaxis vs Treatment** | **Prophylaxis: 125mg BD**; **Treatment: 250mg BD** |
| **HACE vs HAPE** | **HACE = Cerebral (Ataxia, Confusion)**; **HAPE = Pulmonary (Dyspnoea, Frothy Sputum)** |
| **Live vs Inactivated Vaccines** | **Live: MMR, Varicella, Yellow Fever, Oral Ty21a, BCG, LAIV — Contra in Immunocompromised/Pregnant** |

| Mnemonic | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **"Malaria: A-P = Daily, 7d After; Doxy = Daily, 28d After; Meflo = Weekly, 4w After"** | Prophylaxis Duration |
| **"Yellow Fever = ICVP = 10 Days Valid"** | YF Certificate |
| **"TD = Azithro + Loperamide (No Blood/Fever)"** | Travellers' Diarrhoea |
| **"HACE = Head (Brain), HAPE = Lungs"** | Altitude Illness |
| **"Gradual Ascent = <500m/Day Sleep Gain >3000m"** | Prevention |
| **"Pregnancy = No Live Vax, No Doxy, Meflo 2nd/3rd Tri Only"** | Pregnancy Restrictions |
