## 1. 🎯 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Distinguish **Louse-borne** (*B. recurrentis*) vs **Tick-borne** (*B. duttonii, B. hermsii*) relapsing fever
- [ ] Recognise clinical pattern: **Recurrent febrile episodes** (3-5 days) separated by afebrile periods
- [ ] Diagnose: **Peripheral blood smear** (Giemsa/Wright stain) showing spirochaetes during febrile episode
- [ ] Manage: **Doxycycline** or **Tetracycline** (single dose for louse-borne); avoid in pregnancy
- [ ] Recognise and manage **Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction** (anticipate with first dose)

---

## 2. 📚 Epidemiology & Transmission

| Feature | Louse-borne (Epidemic) | Tick-borne (Endemic) |
|---------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **Agent** | *Borrelia recurrentis* | *B. duttonii* (Africa), *B. hermsii* (USA), *B. miyamotoi* |
| **Vector** | Human body louse (*Pediculus humanus*) | Soft ticks (*Ornithodoros* spp.) |
| **Reservoir** | Humans only | Rodents, small mammals |
| **Geography** | Horn of Africa, refugee camps | Africa, Americas, Asia, Europe (focal) |
| **Transmission** | Louse crushed → hemolymph enters skin/mucosa | Tick bite (often painless, nocturnal) |
| **Risk factors** | Overcrowding, poor hygiene, displacement | Cave exposure, rodent contact, travel |

---

## 3. 🦠 Pathophysiology

- **Antigenic variation**: Borrelia changes surface protein (Vmp - variable major protein) → immune evasion → recurrent bacteraemia
- **Cycles**: Each febrile episode corresponds to a new antigenic variant
- **Louse-borne**: More severe, higher mortality (10-40% untreated); single episode typical
- **Tick-borne**: Milder, multiple relapses (3-10 episodes); lower mortality

---

## 4. 🩺 Clinical Features

### Typical Episode
| Phase | Features |
|-------|----------|
| **Incubation** | 4-18 days (average 7) |
| **Febrile phase** (3-5 days) | High fever (39-41°C), chills, headache, myalgia, arthralgia, nausea, vomiting |
| **Crisis** | Rigors → profuse sweating → temperature drop (risk of hypotension, shock) |
| **Afebrile period** | 4-7 days (louse-borne: longer, often single episode; tick-borne: shorter, multiple relapses) |

### Complications
- **CNS involvement**: Meningitis, cranial nerve palsies, coma (more common in louse-borne)
- **Hepatitis**, **myocarditis**, **ARDS**, **DIC**
- **Pregnancy**: High risk of abortion, preterm labour, neonatal infection
- **Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction**: Within 2-4 hours of first antibiotic dose (fever, rigors, hypotension, worsening)

---

## 5. 🔬 Diagnosis

| Test | Findings | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Peripheral blood smear** (Giemsa/Wright) | **Spirochaetes** visible during febrile episode (10-100/field) | **Gold standard**; sensitivity 70-90% in febrile phase |
| **Dark-field microscopy** | Motile spirochaetes | Higher sensitivity, needs fresh sample |
| **PCR** (blood, CSF) | *Borrelia* DNA | Highly sensitive/specific; not widely available |
| **Serology** (IFA, ELISA) | Rising titres | Retrospective; cross-reacts with Lyme |
| **QBC** (Quantitative Buffy Coat) | Concentrated spirochaetes | Higher yield than thin smear |

> [!tip] **Exam Pearl**: Best diagnostic yield = **thick/thin blood smear during febrile spike**. If negative, repeat in 24h.

---

## 6. 💊 Management

### First-line
| Scenario | Regimen |
|----------|---------|
| **Louse-borne** (adults/children >8yr) | **Doxycycline 200 mg PO single dose** OR **Tetracycline 500 mg PO q6h × 2 doses** |
| **Tick-borne** | **Doxycycline 100 mg PO bd × 7-10 days** |
| **Pregnancy** | **Erythromycin 500 mg PO q6h × 7 days** (avoid tetracyclines) |
| **CNS involvement** | **Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q12h × 10-14 days** + consider dexamethasone |

### Jarisch-Herxheimer Reaction Management
- **Anticipate**: Pre-hydrate, monitor vitals
- **Treat**: **Fluid resuscitation**, **vasopressors** if hypotensive, **antipyretics**
- **Steroids**: Consider **dexamethasone 10 mg IV** *before* first dose in high-risk (CNS disease, pregnancy)

## 9. 🧠 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Relapsing fever vs Malaria** | Relapsing fever: spirochaetes on smear, JHR with abx, lice/ticks vectors; Malaria: parasites on smear, no JHR, mosquito vector |
| **Relapsing fever vs Typhoid** | Typhoid: step-ladder fever, relative bradycardia, rose spots, blood culture +ve; Relapsing fever: recurrent spikes, JHR, smear +ve |
| **Louse-borne vs Tick-borne** | Louse: epidemic, humans only reservoir, severe, single episode; Tick: endemic, rodent reservoir, milder, multiple relapses |

**Mnemonic**: **"RELAPSE"** → **R**ecurrent fever, **E**pidemic (louse) / **E**ndemic (tick), **L**ouse/*Ornithodoros* vector, **A**ntigenic variation, **P**eripheral smear (Giemsa), **S**ingle dose doxy (louse), **E**rythromycin in pregnancy
