SEA KNOWLEDGE STUDY NOTES
INFECTIOUS DISEASE
Toxoplasmosis
1 🎯 Learning Objectives
  • [ ] Identify Toxoplasma gondii: obligate intracellular protozoan; definitive host = cat
  • [ ] Recognise Acute toxoplasmosis (immunocompetent): Asymptomatic (80%) or mononucleosis-like (lymphadenopathy, fever)
  • [ ] Recognise Cerebral toxoplasmosis (HIV, CD4<100): Headache, focal deficit, seizures, multiple ring-enhancing lesions
  • [ ] Recognise Ocular toxoplasmosis: Chorioretinitis (posterior pole, focal necrotising retinitis)
  • [ ] Recognise Congenital toxoplasmosis: Classic triad (chorioretinitis, hydrocephalus, intracranial calcifications)
  • [ ] Diagnose: IgG/IgM serology (Sabin-Feldman dye test = gold), PCR (CSF, blood, aqueous), CT/MRI brain, fundoscopy
  • [ ] Manage: Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin × 6 weeks (acute), then ½ dose (suppression)
  • [ ] Prophylaxis: TMP-SMX when CD4<100 + IgG+ (covers both PCP and Toxo)
2 📚 Microbiology
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Organism***Toxoplasma gondii* (obligate intracellular apicomplexan protozoan)
**Definitive host****Cat** (felids) — sexual reproduction in enterocytes → oocysts shed in faeces
**Intermediate hosts**Humans, rodents, livestock, birds — asexual reproduction in tissues (cysts)
**Transmission****Ingestion of oocysts** (contaminated food/water/soil/cat litter), **ingestion of tissue cysts** (undercooked meat), **transplacental**, **organ transplant**, **rare person-to-person**
**Forms****Tachyzoite** (rapidly dividing, acute), **Bradyzoite** (slow, tissue cyst, chronic/latent)
3 🩺 Clinical Syndromes
1. Immunocompetent Primary Infection
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Asymptomatic**~80%
**Symptomatic**Mononucleosis-like: cervical **lymphadenopathy** (KEY), fever, malaise, sore throat, hepatosplenomegaly, atypical lymphocytes
**Course**Self-limited (weeks-months); lymphadenopathy may persist; chorioretinitis may develop later
**Rare**Pneumonitis, myocarditis, encephalitis, hepatitis
2. Cerebral Toxoplasmosis (HIV, CD4<100)
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Presentation**Subacute (days-weeks): headache, confusion, fever, **focal neurological deficit** (hemiparesis, aphasia), **seizures** (30%), ataxia
**Imaging****Multiple ring-enhancing lesions** (basal ganglia, corticomedullary junction) with **surrounding oedema** ("eccentric target sign" pathognomonic but rare); solitary lesion possible (15%)
**CSF**Mild pleocytosis, ↑protein, normal/low glucose; **PCR** (specificity >95%, sensitivity 50%)
**Differential****Primary CNS lymphoma** (solitary, periventricular, EBV+ in CSF), brain abscess, TB, PML
**Therapeutic trial**Empirical anti-toxo × 2 weeks; **clinical + radiological improvement** at 2-3 weeks confirms diagnosis
3. Ocular Toxoplasmosis
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Presentation**Floaters, blurred vision, scotoma, photophobia; **focal necrotising retinitis** (yellow-white "headlight in fog" lesion)
**Location****Posterior pole**, adjacent to old chorioretinal scar (recurrent)
**Complications**Macular scar, retinal detachment, optic neuritis, cataract
4. Congenital Toxoplasmosis
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Maternal**Primary infection during pregnancy (reactivation usually not transmitted if immunocompetent)
**Classic triad****Chorioretinitis** + **Hydrocephalus** + **Intracranial calcifications (diffuse)**
**Other**Microcephaly, seizures, psychomotor retardation, hepatosplenomegaly, jaundice, thrombocytopenia
**Asymptomatic at birth**70-90%; develop chorioretinitis or learning disabilities later
**Severity****Worse with first-trimester infection**; later pregnancy = less severe fetal disease but higher transmission
5. Other
  • Lymphadenopathy (cervical, occipital) — most common presentation
  • Myocarditis, pneumonitis (immunocompromised)
4 🔬 Diagnosis
TestSensitivity/SpecificityNotes
-------------------------------------
**Sabin-Feldman dye test** (IgG)Gold standard serology (reference labs only)Live tachyzoites; patient's antibodies lyse; dye doesn't penetrate
**ELISA IgG**High sensitivity/specificityStandard screening; seroprevalence 30-70% globally
**ELISA IgM**Recent infection (rises early, falls in months)False +ve possible; **confirm with IgG avidity**
**IgG avidity****Low avidity = recent (<3-4 months)**; high avidity = old (>4 months)Critical in pregnancy
**PCR (CSF, blood, aqueous, BAL)**High specificity; variable sensitivityCSF PCR for cerebral toxo (specificity >95%, sensitivity 50%)
**Brain CT/MRI**Multiple ring-enhancing lesions + oedema"Eccentric target sign" (asymmetric ring enhancement)
**Brain biopsy**Definitive; rarely neededIf no response to empirical Rx → consider biopsy (PCNSL?)
**Fundoscopy**Chorioretinitis (active vs scar)All HIV with visual symptoms
**Antenatal**Amniocentesis + PCR (≥18w gestation)Confirms fetal infection
[!tip] Exam Pearl: HIV with multiple ring-enhancing brain lesions = treat as toxoplasmosis empirically. If no response in 2 weeks → biopsy (think PCNSL, EBV+).
5 💊 Management
Cerebral Toxoplasmosis (HIV)
PhaseRegimenDuration
--------------------------
**Acute (induction)****Pyrimethamine 200 mg loading → 50-75 mg/day** + **Sulfadiazine 1-1.5 g q6h** + **Leucovorin (folinic acid) 10-25 mg/day****≥6 weeks** (until clinical/radiological response)
**Suppression (secondary)****Pyrimethamine 25-50 mg/day** + **Sulfadiazine 500 mg q6h** + **Leucovorin 10-25 mg/day**Until CD4 >200 ×3-6 months on ART
Alternative (Sulfa Allergy / Intolerance)
DrugDoseNotes
-------------------
**Pyrimethamine + Clindamycin**Pyrimethamine 200mg loading → 50-75 mg/day + Clindamycin 600 mg q6h IV/POAdd Leucovorin; alternative to sulfadiazine
**Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX)**5 mg TMP/kg q12hLess effective than pyrimethamine regimen; option in resource-limited
**Atovaquone + Pyrimethamine**Atovaquone 1500 mg bd + Pyrimethamine 50-75 mg/daySalvage; Leucovorin
[!critical] ALWAYS add Leucovorin (folinic acid) with pyrimethamine — prevents bone marrow suppression (folate antagonist).
Other Forms
SyndromeTreatment
---------------------
**Acute toxoplasmosis (immunocompetent)**Usually self-limited; treat if severe: **Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin × 2-4 weeks**
**Ocular toxoplasmosis****Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin × 4-6 weeks** + **Prednisolone** (if vitritis/macular involvement); intravitreal clindamycin/dexamethasone for severe
**Pregnancy (acute, <18w)****Spiramycin 3 g/day** (until amniocentesis); if fetal infection confirmed → Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin (after 1st trimester)
**Pregnancy (acute, ≥18w)****Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin** directly
**Congenital toxoplasmosis****Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin × 12 months**
6 🛡️ Prophylaxis
IndicationRegimenNotes
----------------------------
**HIV CD4 <100 + Toxo IgG+****TMP-SMX DS (160/800 mg) PO daily**Same drug covers PCP (CD4<200); doubles as primary prophylaxis
**Alternative (sulfa allergy)****Dapsone 200 mg weekly + Pyrimethamine 75 mg weekly + Leucovorin 25 mg weekly**OR Atovaquone ± Pyrimethamine
**Discontinue**CD4 >200 ×3 months on ART

9 🧠 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**Toxoplasma vs CMV retinitis (HIV)**Toxo: **Posterior pole**, focal, adjacent to old scar, "headlight in fog"; CMV: **Perivascular**, diffuse, haemorrhagic ("pizza-pie" / "ketchup-cottage cheese")
**Toxoplasma vs PCNSL (HIV)**Toxo: **Multiple** lesions, basal ganglia, oedema, **Toxo IgG+**; PCNSL: **Solitary** (often), periventricular, **EBV+ in CSF**, homogeneously enhancing
**Pyrimethamine vs Spiramycin in pregnancy**Spiramycin: **Early pregnancy** (prevention of transmission, no fetal treatment); Pyrimethamine+Sulfadiazine: **After 1st trimester** (fetal treatment, teratogenic early)
**Sabin-Feldman test vs ELISA**Sabin-Feldman: **Reference** (gold, live tachyzoites); ELISA IgG: **Routine screening**
**TMP-SMX prophylaxis covers both PCP and Toxo****YES** — same drug (DS daily) covers both; no need to add when CD4 crosses <100 if already on for PCP

Mnemonic: "TOXOPLASMA" → Tissue cysts (meat), Oocysts (cat faeces), X-linked? No — autosomal, Obligate intracellular, Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin, AIDS (CD4<100), Sabin-Feldman, Multiple ring lesions, Ante-natal (spiramycin)

Mnemonic - Cerebral toxo Rx: "PSL" → Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin (folinic acid)

Mnemonic - Congenital triad: "CHIC" → Chorioretinitis, Hydrocephalus, Intracranial calcifications (diffuse)
