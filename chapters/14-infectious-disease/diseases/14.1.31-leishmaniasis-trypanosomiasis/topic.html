1 🎯 Learning Objectives
  • [ ] Classify Leishmania Species: L. donovani/infantum (Visceral), L. tropica/major/ethiopica (Cutaneous), L. braziliensis (Mucocutaneous)
  • [ ] Recognise Visceral Leishmaniasis (VL/Kala-azar): Fever, Splenomegaly, Pancytopenia, Weight Loss, Hypergammaglobulinaemia
  • [ ] Recognise Cutaneous (CL) & Mucocutaneous (ML): Ulcers, Mucosal Destruction (Nose/Pharynx)
  • [ ] Diagnose Leishmaniasis: rK39 Dipstick (VL), Smear/PCR/Culture (CL/ML), MAT/ELISA (Serology)
  • [ ] Manage Leishmaniasis: Liposomal Amphotericin B (VL), Miltefosine (VL/CL), Amphotericin B (ML), Antimonials (Historical)
  • [ ] Classify Trypanosomes: T. brucei gambiense/rhodesiense (African Sleeping Sickness), T. cruzi (Chagas Disease)
  • [ ] Recognise African Trypanosomiasis: Stage 1 (Haemo-lymphatic) vs Stage 2 (Meningo-encephalitic)
  • [ ] Recognise Chagas Disease: Acute (Romaña Sign), Chronic (Cardiac, GI Megasyndromes)
  • [ ] Manage Trypanosomiasis: Suramin/Pentamidine (Stage 1), Eflornithine/Nifurtimox (Stage 2), Benznidazole/Nifurtimox (Chagas)

3 📖 Visceral Leishmaniasis (VL / Kala-azar)
Clinical Features
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Classic Triad****Fever** (Intermittent, Double Quotidian), **Massive Splenomegaly**, **Pancytopenia**
**Other****Weight Loss**, **Wasting**, **Hypergammaglobulinaemia** (↑ IgG/IgM), **Liver Enlargement**, **Lymphadenopathy** (African VL), **Skin Changes** (Hyperpigmentation/Kala-azar = "Black Fever")
**Complications****Secondary Infections** (TB, Pneumonia), **Bleeding** (Thrombocytopenia), **Renal Failure**, **PKDL** (Post-Treatment)
Diagnosis
TestSensitivity/SpecificityRole
------------------------------------
**rK39 Dipstick** (Rapid)**Sens 95-98%, Spec 90-95%****Field-Friendly, Rapid Screening (VL)**
**Serology (DAT, ELISA, IFAT)**High Sens/Spec**Confirmatory**, **Antibody Persists**
**Parasite Demonstration****Splenic Aspiration > BM > Lymph Node****Gold Standard** (Microscopy: LD Bodies in Macrophages)
**PCR****High Sens/Spec****Species ID, Monitoring**
**Aldehyde Test (Formal Gel)**Low SensitivityHistorical
Treatment (VL)
Region / Scenario1st LineAlternativeDuration
----------------------------------------------------
**Indian Subcontinent (L. donovani)****LAmB 10mg/kg Single Dose** (or 5mg/kg Day 1-5)**Miltefosine 50mg BD (Weight-Based) × 28d****Single Dose LAmB**
**East Africa (L. donovani)****LAmB 3-5mg/kg/day × 5-10d****SSG+PM (Sodium Stibogluconate + Paromomycin) × 17d****5-10 Days LAmB**
**Mediterranean/Latin America (L. infantum)****LAmB 3-5mg/kg/day × 5-10d****Miltefosine****5-10 Days**

[!warning] Miltefosine = Teratogenic (Contraindicated in Pregnancy). SSG = Cardiotoxicity/Pancreatitis (Monitor ECG/Amylase). LAmB = Preferred (Safety, Efficacy).

FeatureOld World (L. tropica, L. major)New World (L. mexicana, L. braziliensis)

Mucocutaneous Leishmaniasis (ML / Espundia)
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Agent****L. braziliensis** (Predominant), *L. panamensis*, *L. guyanensis*
**Pathogenesis****Metastatic Spread** from Primary Cutaneous Lesion → Mucosa (Nose, Pharynx, Larynx)
**Presentation****Nose** (Epistaxis, Septal Perforation, Saddle Nose), **Pharynx/Larynx** (Dysphagia, Hoarseness, Airway Obstruction)
**Timing****Months-Years** After Primary Cutaneous Lesion Heals

Form1st LineAlternative
-----------------------------
**CL (Old World)****Intral Lesional Sodium Stibogluconate** / **Cryotherapy** / **Topical Paromomycin** / **Thermotherapy****Systemic: Miltefosine / LAmB / Pentavalent Antimonials**

**ML****LAmB 3-5mg/kg/day × 20-30d****Pentavalent Antimonials × 30d**

African Trypanosomiasis (Sleeping Sickness)
Feature*T. b. gambiense* (West/Central Africa)*T. b. rhodesiense* (East/Southern Africa)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
**Epidemiology****Chronic** (98% Cases), **Anthroponotic****Acute** (Weeks), **Zoonotic** (Cattle/Game)
**Vector***Glossina palpalis* (Riverine)*Glossina morsitans* (Savannah)

[!tip] Winterbottom's Sign = Posterior Cervical Lymphadenopathy = Pathognomonic for T.b. gambiense.

Treatment (African Trypanosomiasis)
Stage*T. b. gambiense**T. b. rhodesiense*
------------------------------------------------
**Stage 1****Fexinidazole 1800mg OD × 10 Days** (Oral, All Stages) **OR** Pentamidine 4mg/kg IM × 7-10d**Suramin 20mg/kg IV Days 1,3,7,14,21** (Test Dose First)

6 📖 Chagas Disease (American Trypanosomiasis)
Clinical Phases
PhaseFeatures
-----------------
**Acute (Weeks 1-8)****Romaña's Sign** (Unilateral Periorbital Oedema + Conjunctivitis), **Chagoma** (Inoculation Site), Fever, Lymphadenopathy, Hepatosplenomegaly, **Myocarditis** (Arrhythmia, Heart Failure), **Meningoencephalitis** (Rare)
**Indeterminate (Latent)****Asymptomatic**, **Positive Serology**, **Can Last Decades**
**Chronic (10-30 Years Later)****Cardiac** (Cardiomyopathy, Arrhythmias, Heart Failure, Thromboembolism, Sudden Death), **GI** (Megaesophagus, Megacolon), **Neurological**
Diagnosis
PhaseTest
-------------
**Acute****Microscopy (Blood - Motile Trypomastigotes)**, **PCR** (High Sens), **Antigen Detection**
**Chronic****Serology** (IgG ELISA/IHA/IIF — **Two Tests Required**), **PCR** (Low Sensitivity in Chronic)
Treatment
PhaseRegimen
----------------
**Acute / Congenital / Reactivation****Benznidazole 5-7mg/kg/day BD × 60 Days** (1st Line) **OR Nifurtimox 8-10mg/kg/day BD × 60-90 Days**
**Chronic (Indeterminate/Early Cardiac)****Benznidazole 5-7mg/kg/day BD × 60 Days** (Benefit Controversial)
**Advanced Cardiac/GI****Supportive** (Heart Failure Rx, Pacemaker, Surgery for Megaesophagus/Colon)
[!warning] Benznidazole = Teratogenic (Contraindicated in Pregnancy). Nifurtimox = Neurotoxicity/Psychosis. Both Require 60 Days.

8 ❓ Viva Questions
  1. What is the classic presentation of Visceral Leishmaniasis (Kala-azar)?
  • Fever + Massive Splenomegaly + Pancytopenia + Hypergammaglobulinaemia.
  1. What is the rapid diagnostic test for Visceral Leishmaniasis?
  • rK39 Dipstick (Sensitivity 95-98%, Specificity 90-95%).
  1. What is the first-line treatment for Visceral Leishmaniasis in the Indian Subcontinent?
  • Single Dose Liposomal Amphotericin B 10mg/kg.
  1. What is the difference between Old World and New World Cutaneous Leishmaniasis?
  • Old World (L. tropica/major): Self-Limiting, Single Ulcer; New World (L. braziliensis/mexicana): Larger, Multiple, Higher ML Risk.
  1. What is Mucocutaneous Leishmaniasis and which species causes it?
  • Mucosal Destruction (Nose/Pharynx/Larynx) = Espundia, caused by L. braziliensis (New World).
  1. What is Winterbottom's Sign and which disease is it associated with?
  • Posterior Cervical Lymphadenopathy = Pathognomonic for T. b. gambiense (African Trypanosomiasis).
  1. How do you distinguish Stage 1 from Stage 2 African Trypanosomiasis?
  • Stage 1: CSF WBC ≤5, No Trypanosomes in CSF, No Neuro Signs; Stage 2: CSF WBC >5, Trypanosomes in CSF, Neuro Signs Present.
  1. What is the treatment for Stage 2 T. b. gambiense?
  • Fexinidazole 1800mg OD × 10 Days OR NECT (Nifurtimox + Eflornithine).
  1. What is Romaña's Sign in Chagas Disease?
  • Unilateral Periorbital Oedema + Conjunctivitis at Inoculation Site (Acute Phase).
  1. What is the treatment for Acute Chagas Disease?
  • Benznidazole 5-7mg/kg/day BD × 60 Days (1st Line) or Nifurtimox 8-10mg/kg/day BD × 60-90d.
  1. What are the chronic manifestations of Chagas Disease?
  • Cardiac (Cardiomyopathy, Arrhythmias, Heart Failure, Sudden Death) + GI (Megaesophagus, Megacolon).
  1. How do you diagnose Chronic Chagas Disease?
  • Two Serological Tests Positive (ELISA + IHA/IIF) AND/OR PCR (Low Sensitivity in Chronic).
