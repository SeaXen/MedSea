## 1. 🎯 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Classify **Rickettsial Diseases**: **Spotted Fever Group** (RMSF, Mediterranean Spotted Fever, African Tick Typhus) vs **Typhus Group** (Epidemic, Murine) vs **Scrub Typhus** (Orientia tsutsugamushi)
- [ ] Recognise **Clinical Presentation**: Fever, Headache, Rash, **Eschar (Tache Noire)** — Pathognomonic for Spotted Fever/Scrub Typhus
- [ ] Diagnose: **IFA** (Gold Standard), **PCR** (Early), **Weil-Felix** (Historical/Low Sensitivity), **PCR/Immunohistochemistry** (Tissue)
- [ ] Manage: **Doxycycline 100mg BD PO** (1st Line All Rickettsial), **Chloramphenicol** (Pregnancy/Children), **Ceftriaxone** (CNS)
- [ ] Recognise **Complications**: Vasculitis, Encephalitis, Renal Failure, ARDS, Multi-Organ Failure
- [ ] Distinguish **Spotted Fever** (Eschar + Maculopapular Rash) vs **Typhus** (No Eschar, Central Rash) vs **Scrub Typhus** (Eschar + Lymphadenopathy)

## 2. 📖 Classification & Key Species

| Group | Representative Species | Disease | Vector | Geography |
|-------|----------------------|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Spotted Fever Group (SFG)** | *R. rickettsii* | **Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever (RMSF)** | *Dermacentor* Tick | Americas (USA, Mexico, Brazil) |
| | *R. conorii* | **Mediterranean Spotted Fever (MSF)** | *Rhipicephalus* Tick | Mediterranean, Africa, India |
| | *R. africae* | **African Tick Bite Fever (ATBF)** | *Amblyomma* Tick | Sub-Saharan Africa |
| | *R. japonica* | **Japanese Spotted Fever** | *Haemaphysalis* Tick | Japan |
| | *R. honei* | **Flinders Island Spotted Fever** | *Bothriocroton* Tick | Australia |
| **Typhus Group** | *R. prowazekii* | **Epidemic Typhus** (Louse-Borne) | *Pediculus humanus* (Body Louse) | Crowded/Displaced Populations, Cold Climate |
| | *R. typhi* | **Murine (Endemic) Typhus** (Flea-Borne) | *Xenopsylla* Flea (Rat) | Worldwide (Tropical/Subtropical) |
| **Scrub Typhus Group** | *Orientia tsutsugamushi* | **Scrub Typhus** (Tsutsugamushi Disease) | *Leptotrombidium* Mite (Chigger) | **Asia-Pacific** (Tsutsugamushi Triangle) |
| **Other** | *Anaplasma phagocytophilum* | **Human Granulocytic Anaplasmosis (HGA)** | *Ixodes* Tick | USA, Europe, Asia |
| | *Ehrlichia chaffeensis* | **Human Monocytic Ehrlichiosis (HME)** | *Amblyomma* Tick | USA (SE/South-Central) |
| | *Ehrlichia ewingii* | **E. ewingii Infection** | *Amblyomma* Tick | USA (SE) |

> [!tip] **Key Differentiator: Eschar (Tache Noire)** = **Present in Spotted Fever Group & Scrub Typhus**; **Absent in Typhus Group** (Epidemic/Murine).

## 3. 📖 Clinical Syndromes

### Spotted Fever Group (SFG)

| Disease | Vector | Geography | Key Features |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever (RMSF)** | *Dermacentor* Tick (Dog Tick, Wood Tick) | **USA (NC, OK, AR, TN, MO, AR)**, Mexico, Brazil, Canada | **High Fever, Headache, Myalgia**, **Centrifugal Rash** (Wrists/Ankles → Centripetal), **Palmoplantar Involvement**, **NO Eschar** (Usually), **High Mortality if Untreated** |
| **Mediterranean Spotted Fever (MSF)** | *Rhipicephalus sanguineus* (Brown Dog Tick) | Mediterranean, Africa, India, Black Sea | **Eschar (Tache Noire) @ Bite Site**, **Maculopapular Rash**, **Fever, Headache**, **Benign Course** (Usually) |
| **African Tick Bite Fever (ATBF)** | *Amblyocroton* Tick | Sub-Saharan Africa, Caribbean | **Multiple Eschars**, **Regional Lymphadenopathy**, **Mild Fever**, **Self-Limited** |

### Typhus Group

| Disease | Vector | Geography | Key Features |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Epidemic Typhus** | Human Body Louse (*Pediculus humanus corporis*) | **Crowded/Displaced Populations**, Cold Climate, War, Famine | **High Fever, Headache, Rash (Centripetal → Trunk → Extremities)**, **NO Eschar**, **Neurological (Confusion, Delirium)**, **High Mortality if Untreated** |
| **Murine (Endemic) Typhus** | Rat Flea (*Xenopsylla cheopis*) | Worldwide (Tropical/Subtropical — Port Cities) | **Fever, Headache, Rash (Trunk → Extremities)**, **NO Eschar**, Milder than Epidemic |

### Scrub Typhus

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Agent** | *Orientia tsutsugamushi* (Mite/Chigger — *Leptotrombidium*) |
| **Geography** | **Tsutsugamushi Triangle** (Asia-Pacific: Japan, China, Korea, SE Asia, India, Pakistan, N. Australia) |
| **Vector** | **Chigger (Larval Mite)** — Bite in **Sweaty Areas** (Axilla, Groin, Belt Line) |
| **Key Feature** | **Eschar** at Bite Site (Painless, 5-10mm, Necrotic Centre, Black Crust) + **Regional Lymphadenopathy** |
| **Clinical** | Fever, Headache, Maculopapular Rash (Centrifugal), **Lymphadenopathy (Generalised)**, **Hepatosplenomegaly**, **Interstitial Pneumonitis**, **Meningoencephalitis** |

## 4. 🔬 Diagnostic Workup

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Fever + Rash + Exposure History] --> B{**Eschar Present?**}
    B -->|Yes| C[**Spotted Fever Group / Scrub Typhus**]
    B -->|No| D[**Typhus Group / Other**]
    C --> E[**Geography + Vector**]
    E -->|Asia-Pacific + Mite| F[**Scrub Typhus** (Eschar + Lymphadenopathy)]
    E -->|Mediterranean/Africa + Tick| G[**MSF / ATBF**]
    E -->|USA + Dog Tick| H[**RMSF**]
    D --> I[**Typhus Group**]
    I -->|Louse + Crowded| J[**Epidemic Typhus**]
    I -->|Flea + Rat| K[**Murine Typhus**]
```

### Key Investigations

| Test | Role | Timing | Sensitivity/Specificity |
|------|------|--------|------------------------|
| **IFA (Immunofluorescence Assay)** | **Gold Standard** (IgG/IgM) | **Convalescent (Day 10-14)**; **Paired Sera 2-4w Apart** | **High Sens/Spec** |
| **PCR** | **Early Diagnosis** (Blood/ESCHAR Swab) | **Acute Phase (Day 1-10)** | High Sens/Spec (ESCHAR > Blood) |
| **Weil-Felix Test** | **Historical** (OX19, OX2, OXK) | **Week 2** | **Low Sens/Spec** (OBsolete) |
| **Immunohistochemistry** | **Tissue Biopsy** (Eschar/Skin) | Any Time | High Specificity |
| **Culture** | Reference Only | Weeks | Low Sensitivity, Biosafety Level 3 |

### Weil-Felix Test (Historical/Limited Use)

| Antigen | Reactivity | Disease |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **OX19** | Positive | **Epidemic/Murine Typhus**, **Spotted Fever (RMSF, MSF)** |
| **OX2** | Positive | **Spotted Fever (RMSF, MSF)** |
| **OXK** | Positive | **Scrub Typhus** |

> [!warning] **Weil-Felix = Obsolete, Low Sensitivity/Specificity**. **Use IFA/PCR**. Kept for Resource-Limited Settings.

## 5. 💊 Management

### Antibiotic Therapy

| Disease / Severity | 1st Line | Alternative (Pregnancy/Children) | Duration |
|--------------------|----------|----------------------------------|----------|
| **All Rickettsial (Spotted Fever, Typhus, Scrub Typhus)** | **Doxycycline 100mg PO BD** | **Chloramphenicol 500mg QID** (Pregnancy/Children <8y) | **7-14 Days** (Until Afebrile 48-72h) |
| **Severe/RMSF/Scrub Typhus with Complications** | **Doxycycline 100mg IV/PO BD** + **Chloramphenicol** (If CNS) | **Chloramphenicol 500mg IV QID** | **7-14 Days** |
| **Epidemic Typhus (Severe)** | **Doxycycline 200mg IV/PO Day 1, then 100mg BD** | **Chloramphenicol 500mg IV QID** | **10-14 Days** |
| **Anaplasmosis / Ehrlichiosis** | **Doxycycline 100mg BD PO/IV** | **Rifampin 600mg BD** (Pregnancy) | **7-14 Days** |

> [!warning] **Doxycycline = 1st Line for ALL Rickettsial Diseases**. **Chloramphenicol = Pregnancy/Children <8y** (Bone Marrow Toxicity Risk). **Fluoroquinolones NOT Reliable**.

## 6. 🩺 Clinical Syndromes & Complications

| Syndrome | Key Features | Complications |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **RMSF** | Fever, Headache, Myalgia, **Centripetal Rash** (Wrists/Ankles → Trunk → Palms/Soles), **No Eschar** (Usually) | **Vasculitis → Encephalitis, Renal Failure, ARDS, DIC, Myocarditis** |
| **MSF** | **Eschar (Tache Noire)**, Maculopapular Rash, Fever, Headache | **Usually Self-Limited**, Rare Severe |
| **ATBF** | **Multiple Eschars**, Regional Lymphadenopathy, Mild | **Generally Benign** |
| **Epidemic Typhus** | High Fever, Headache, **Central Rash (Trunk→Extremities)**, **NO ESCHAR**, Neurological | **Gangrene, Myocarditis, Renal Failure, Encephalitis** |
| **Murine Typhus** | Fever, Headache, Rash (Trunk→Extremities), **NO ESCHAR** | **Milder**, Rare Complications |
| **Scrub Typhus** | **Eschar + Lymphadenopathy**, Fever, Rash, Cough, Hepatosplenomegaly | **Interstitial Pneumonitis, Meningoencephalitis, ARDS, Myocarditis, ARF** |

## 8. 💊 Treatment Summary

| Scenario | Regimen |
|---------|---------|
| **All Rickettsial (Outpatient/Mild)** | **Doxycycline 100mg PO BD × 7-14 Days** (Until Afebrile 48h + Clinical Improvement) |
| **Severe / Hospitalised** | **Doxycycline 100mg IV/PO BD** + **Supportive Care**; **Chloramphenicol if Pregnant/Child** |
| **CNS Involvement** | **Doxycycline IV/PO** (Good CNS Penetration) + **Ceftriaxone** (If Meningitis Suspected) |
| **Pregnancy / Children <8y** | **Chloramphenicol 500mg QID PO/IV** (Alternative: Azithromycin — Limited Data) |

> [!warning] **Doxycycline = 1st Line for ALL**. **Chloramphenicol = Pregnancy/Children <8y** (Grey Baby Syndrome Risk). **Fluoroquinolones NOT Recommended** (Variable Efficacy).

## 9. 🔄 Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Features |
|---------|--------------------------|
| **RMSF vs Meningococcemia** | **RMSF: Rash Peripheral → Central, No Meningismus Initially**; **Meningococcemia: Petechial/Purpuric, Rapid, Meningismus** |
| **RMSF vs Dengue** | **RMSF: Rash Peripheral→Central, No Thrombocytopenia Initially**; **Dengue: Thrombocytopenia, Plasma Leakage, NS1+** |
| **Scrub Typhus vs Typhoid** | **Scrub: Eschar + Lymphadenopathy + Doxycycline**; **Typhoid: Relative Bradycardia, Blood Culture+, Ceftriaxone** |
| **Epidemic Typhus vs Murine Typhus** | **Epidemic = Louse, Crowded, Severe, Neurological**; **Murine = Flea, Rat, Milder** |
| **Scrub Typhus vs Leptospirosis** | **Scrub: Eschar + Lymphadenopathy**; **Lepto: Conjunctival Suffusion, Calf Myalgia, Renal Failure, Pulm Haemorrhage** |
| **Rickettsial vs Leptospirosis** | **Rickettsial: Eschar (SFG/Scrub), No Conjunctival Suffusion**; **Lepto: Conjunctival Suffusion, Calf Myalgia, Pulm Haemorrhage** |

## 11. ❓ Viva Questions

1. **What is the unifying treatment for all Rickettsial infections?**
   - **Doxycycline 100mg BD PO/IV** — 1st Line for ALL Rickettsial diseases.

2. **How do you distinguish Spotted Fever Group from Typhus Group clinically?**
   - **Spotted Fever Group = Eschar (Tache Noire) Present**; **Typhus Group = No Eschar**.

2. **What is the classic triad of Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever?**
   - **Fever, Headache, Rash** (Centrifugal: Wrists/Ankles → Trunk → Palms/Soles); **NO ESCHAR** typically.

3. **What is the characteristic feature of Scrub Typhus?**
   - **Eschar (Tache Noire) at Bite Site + Generalised Lymphadenopathy + Interstitial Pneumonitis**.

3. **How do you differentiate Mediterranean Spotted Fever from RMSF?**
   - **MSF: Eschar Present (Tache Noire), Benign Course, Mediterranean**; **RMSF: No Eschar Usually, Severe, Americas**.

4. **What is the treatment for a pregnant woman with Scrub Typhus?**
   - **Chloramphenicol 500mg QID PO/IV** (Doxycycline Contraindicated), **Azithromycin Alternative (Limited Data)**.

5. **What is the Weil-Felix test and its limitations?**
   - **Agglutination Test** using *Proteus* Antigens (OX19, OX2, OXK); **Low Sensitivity/Specificity** (Obsolete); **Used Only in Resource-Limited Settings**.

6. **How do you diagnose Rickettsial infection?**
   - **IFA (Gold Standard)**: IgG/IgM Seroconversion (Paired Sera 2-4 Weeks); **PCR** (Blood/Eschar Swab, Early); **Immunohistochemistry** (Tissue Biopsy).

7. **What is the drug of choice for all Rickettsial infections?**
   - **Doxycycline 100mg PO/IV BD** (7-14 Days until Afebrile 48h).

8. **What is the significance of an eschar in a febrile patient?**
   - **Pathognomonic for Spotted Fever Group Rickettsiosis or Scrub Typhus**; **Absent in Typhus Group**.

9. **How do you manage Scrub Typhus with pneumonitis?**
   - **Doxycycline 100mg BD IV/PO + Supportive** (Oxygen, Ventilation if Severe); **Chloramphenicol if Pregnancy**.

10. **Differentiate Epidemic Typhus from Murine Typhus.**
    - **Epidemic: Louse-Borne, Crowded/Displaced, Severe, Neurological Involvement, NO ESCHAR**; **Murine: Flea-Borne (Rat), Mild, NO ESCHAR, Worldwide Ports**.
