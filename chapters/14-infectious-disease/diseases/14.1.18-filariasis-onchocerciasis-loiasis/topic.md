## 1. 🎯 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Classify **filarial parasites** by niche: lymphatic (*W. bancrofti, B. malayi, B. timori*), subcutaneous (*O. volvulus, L. loa, M. perstans, M. ozzardi*), body cavity (*M. perstans*)
- [ ] Recognise **Lymphatic filariasis**: Asymptomatic microfilaraemia → acute adenolymphangitis → chronic lymphoedema/elephantiasis/hydrocele
- [ ] Recognise **Onchocerciasis**: Skin (onchocercoma, lichenified dermatitis), eye (river blindness - corneal opacity, chorioretinitis)
- [ ] Recognise **Loiasis**: Calabar swellings, subconjunctival worm migration, high microfilariae → encephalopathy risk with DEC
- [ ] Diagnose: **Nocturnal/peri-diurnal blood smear** (microfilariae), **skin snip** (onchocerciasis), **serology/antigen (Og4C3)**, PCR
- [ ] Manage: **DEC** (lymphatic, loiasis - *with caution*), **Ivermectin** (onchocerciasis, lymphatic - *not loiasis*), **Albendazole** (adjunct)

## 3. 🩺 1. Lymphatic Filariasis (*W. bancrofti, B. malayi, B. timori*)

### Pathogenesis
- Adult worms in lymphatics → **lymphatic dilation, inflammation, fibrosis** → obstruction
- **Immune response** to microfilariae/adults → recurrent acute attacks

### Clinical Stages
| Stage | Features |
|-------|----------|
| **Asymptomatic microfilaraemia** | Microfilariae in blood (nocturnal periodicity - *W. bancrofti*); hidden lung/kidney damage (proteinuria, haematuria) |
| **Acute** | **Acute adenolymphangitis (ADL)**: Fever, painful lymphadenitis, lymphangitis (retrograde), funiculitis, epididymitis; **bacterial superinfection** common |
| **Chronic** | **Lymphoedema** (grades 1-7), **elephantiasis** (leg, scrotum, vulva, breast), **hydrocele** (most common in men), chyluria, chylous ascites |

### Tropical Pulmonary Eosinophilia (TPE)
- **Hyperimmune response** to microfilariae in lungs
- **Symptoms**: Nocturnal cough, wheeze, dyspnoea, weight loss, fever
- **Labs**: **Marked eosinophilia (>3000/μL)**, high IgE, high antifilarial titres
- **CXR**: Miliary/reticulonodular infiltrates
- **Treatment**: **DEC 6mg/kg/day × 14-21 days** (dramatic response)

## 4. 🩺 2. Onchocerciasis — *Onchocerca volvulus* (River Blindness)

### Transmission
- **Black fly** (*Simulium*) breeds in **fast-flowing rivers** → "river blindness"

### Clinical Features
| System | Features |
|--------|----------|
| **Skin** | **Onchocercomas** (subcutaneous nodules over bony prominences), **pruritus**, **lichenified dermatitis** ("leopard skin", "lizard skin"), **depigmentation** ("leopard skin"), atrophy, hanging groin |
| **Eye** | **River blindness**: Punctate keratitis → sclerosing keratitis → **corneal opacity**, iridocyclitis, glaucoma, chorioretinitis, optic atrophy → **blindness** |
| **Systemic** | Lymphadenopathy, low-grade fever |

### Classification of Skin Disease
- **Acute papular onchodermatitis** (pruritic papules)
- **Chronic papular onchodermatitis** (hyperpigmented)
- **Lichenified onchodermatitis** (thickened, "elephant skin")
- **Atrophy** ("cigarette paper skin")
- **Depigmentation** ("leopard skin")

## 5. 🩺 3. Loiasis — *Loa loa* (African Eye Worm)

### Transmission
- **Deer fly / Mango fly** (*Chrysops*) bites during day in rainforest

### Clinical Features
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Calabar swellings** | Transient, non-painful, non-pruritic **angioedema** (hand, wrist, face); last 1-3 days; allergic response to migrating adult |
| **Subconjunctival worm** | **Adult worm migrates across eye** (visible, 3-15 min); "African eye worm"; no permanent damage |
| **Microfilaraemia** | **Diurnal periodicity** (peak 10am-2pm); can be **very high** (>50,000/mL) |
| **Complications** | Encephalopathy (with DEC if high microfilariae), nephropathy, endomyocardial fibrosis |

### Loa Loa Encephalopathy Risk
- **High microfilarial density** (>8,000-30,000/mL) + **DEC/ivermectin** → **fatal encephalopathy**
- **Screen before MDA** in co-endemic areas (RAPLOA - rapid assessment)

## 6. 🔬 Diagnosis

| Test | Lymphatic Filariasis | Onchocerciasis | Loiasis |
|------|---------------------|----------------|---------|
| **Blood smear (microfilariae)** | **Nocturnal** (peak midnight); *W. bancrofti* = sheath + nuclei to tip; *Brugia* = sheath, 2 terminal nuclei | Not useful | **Diurnal** (peak 10am-2pm); **sheath + nuclei to tip**; high density |
| **Skin snip** | - | **Gold standard**: Microfilariae emerge in saline; PCR | - |
| **Antigen test (Og4C3 / Wb123)** | **Rapid ICT card** (sensitivity >90%); detects adult worm antigen | - | - |
| **Serology (ELISA/IFA)** | High titre in TPE | Supportive | Supportive |
| **PCR** | Species ID | Species ID | Species ID |
| **Ultrasound** | **Filarial dance sign** (adult worms in lymphatics/scrotum) | Nodules | - |
| **Slit lamp** | - | Microfilariae in anterior chamber | **Adult worm in subconjunctiva** |

> [!tip] **Exam Pearl**: *W. bancrofti* microfilariae = **sheath + nuclei extend to tip**; *Brugia* = **sheath + 2 terminal nuclei distinct**; *Loa loa* = **sheath + nuclei to tip + diurnal**.

## 7. 💊 Management

### Lymphatic Filariasis
| Scenario | Regimen |
|----------|---------|
| **Individual treatment** | **DEC 6 mg/kg/day × 12 days** (or single dose 6mg/kg + Albendazole 400mg) |
| **Mass Drug Administration (MDA)** | **Ivermectin 200 μg/kg + Albendazole 400mg** (annual) OR **DEC + Albendazole** (where onchocerciasis/loiasis not co-endemic) |
| **Hydrocele** | **Surgery** (hydrocelectomy) |
| **Lymphoedema / Elephantiasis** | **Hygiene** (wash, elevate, exercise, skin care), **antibiotics** for ADL, **compression**, surgery (debulking) |
| **TPE** | **DEC 6 mg/kg/day × 14-21 days** + steroids if severe |

### Onchocerciasis
| Scenario | Regimen |
|----------|---------|
| **Individual / MDA** | **Ivermectin 150-200 μg/kg single dose** (repeat **every 6-12 months** for 10-15 years) |
| **Adjunct** | **Doxycycline 100mg bd × 6 weeks** (targets *Wolbachia* endosymbiont → macrofilaricidal) |
| **Note** | **DEC contraindicated** (severe Mazzotti reaction) |

### Loiasis
| Scenario | Regimen |
|----------|---------|
| **Low microfilaraemia** (<8,000/mL) | **DEC 6 mg/kg/day × 21 days** (or 8-10 mg/kg/day × 3 weeks) |
| **High microfilaraemia** (>8,000/mL) | **Apheresis** to reduce load → **Albendazole 400mg bd × 3 weeks** → then **DEC** with steroids/antihistamines |
| **Ivermectin** | **CONTRAINDICATED** if high microfilaraemia (encephalopathy risk) |
| **Eye worm** | **Manual removal** (slit lamp + forceps) |

## 8. 📋 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **Vector** | *W. bancrofti*: *Culex* mosquito; *Onchocerca*: *Simulium* (black fly, rivers); *Loa loa*: *Chrysops* (deer fly, day) |
| **Periodicity** | *W. bancrofti*: **Nocturnal**; *Loa loa*: **Diurnal**; *Brugia*: Nocturnal/subperiodic |
| **Microfilariae ID** | *Wuchereria*: Sheath, nuclei to tip; *Brugia*: Sheath, 2 terminal nuclei; *Loa*: Sheath, nuclei to tip |
| **Skin snip** | Onchocerciasis gold standard |
| **Antigen test** | Og4C3/Wb123 ICT card = lymphatic filariasis (adult worm antigen) |
| **DEC vs Ivermectin** | DEC: Lymphatic, Loiasis; Ivermectin: Onchocerciasis, Lymphatic (MDA); **Both contraindicated in high Loa loa** |
| **TPE** | Nocturnal cough/wheeze, **eosinophilia >3000**, high IgE, DEC responsive |
| **River blindness** | Corneal opacity → blindness; ivermectin q6-12mo |

## 10. 🧠 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **DEC vs Ivermectin indications** | DEC: Lymphatic, Loiasis; Ivermectin: Onchocerciasis, Lymphatic (MDA); **Never ivermectin in high Loa loa** |
| **Nocturnal vs Diurnal periodicity** | *Wuchereria/Brugia*: Nocturnal (mosquito vectors bite night); *Loa loa*: Diurnal (*Chrysops* bites day) |
| **Onchocerciasis vs Loiasis eye involvement** | Onchocerciasis: **Microfilariae** in anterior chamber → corneal opacity/blindness; Loiasis: **Adult worm** migrates across conjunctiva (transient, no blindness) |
| **TPE vs Asthma** | TPE: **Eosinophilia >3000**, high antifilarial IgE, nocturnal cough, responds to DEC; Asthma: Variable eosinophilia, atopy, bronchodilator response |

**Mnemonic - Lymphatic Filariasis**: **"WUCHERERIA"** → **W**uchereria, **U**rban/rural *Culex*, **C**hronic elephantiasis, **H**ydrocele, **E**lephantiasis, **R**ecurrent ADL, **E**osinophilia (TPE), **R**iver/coastal, **I**vermectin/DEC/Alb, **A**ntigen Og4C3

**Mnemonic - Onchocerciasis**: **"ONCHO"** → **O**nchocerca, **N**odules (onchocercomas), **C**orneal opacity (river blindness), **H**anging groin/leopard skin, **O**nchocercoma, **I**vermectin q6-12mo, **S**imulium (black fly), **R**iver breeding

**Mnemonic - Loiasis**: **"LOA"** → **L**oa loa, **O**cular worm (subconjunctival), **A**ngioedema (Calabar), **C**hrysops (deer fly), **D**iurnal periodicity, **E**ncephalopathy risk (DEC), **N**o ivermectin
