> [!important]
> **Malaria** = protozoal infection by *Plasmodium* spp. **Medical emergency** — *P. falciparum* can kill within 24–48h. **Diagnose with thick/thin blood films + RDT**; **treat urgently** with ACT (artemisinin-based combination therapy). **Severe malaria** = parenteral artesunate.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Identify clinical features of uncomplicated and severe malaria
- Differentiate *Plasmodium* species by blood film and clinical features
- Apply diagnostic algorithms (microscopy + RDT)
- Treat uncomplicated and severe malaria per WHO/UK guidelines
- Manage chemoprophylaxis and special populations (pregnancy, children)

## 2. Plasmodium Species
| Species | Geography | Severity | Key Features |
|---------|-----------|----------|--------------|
| **P. falciparum** | Africa, SE Asia, S America, Haiti | **Most severe**; cerebral, severe anaemia, acidosis, renal | **Ring forms only** in peripheral blood; sequestration in deep vessels |
| **P. vivax** | Asia, Latin America, Horn of Africa | Relapsing (hypnozoites); rarely severe | **Schüffner's dots**; larger rings; amoeboid trophozoites |
| **P. ovale** | West Africa, Pacific | Relapsing (hypnozoites); milder | **Schüffner's dots**; oval-infected RBC; fimbriated |
| **P. malariae** | Worldwide (patchy) | Chronic; quartan fever (72h); nephrotic syndrome | **Band forms**; rosettes; no hypnozoites |
| **P. knowlesi** | SE Asia (Borneo) | Zoonotic (macaques); can be severe/fatal | **All stages** in peripheral blood; daily fever (24h cycle) |

## 3. Clinical Features

### Uncomplicated Malaria
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Fever** | Paroxysmal (tertian 48h: vivax/ovale/falciparum; quartan 72h: malariae) or continuous |
| **Rigors** | Cold phase → hot phase → sweating |
| **Headache, myalgia, arthralgia** | Common |
| **Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea** | Frequent |
| **Anaemia** | Haemolysis + suppressed erythropoiesis |
| **Splenomegaly** | Common (palpable by day 7) |
| **Mild jaundice** | Haemolysis + hepatic dysfunction |

### Severe Malaria (WHO Criteria) — *P. falciparum* predominantly
| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Impaired consciousness / cerebral malaria** | GCS <11 (adults) / Blantyre <3 (children) |
| **Severe anaemia** | Hb <70 g/L (or <50 in children) + parasites |
| **Acidosis** | Bicarbonate <15 mmol/L or lactate ≥5 mmol/L |
| **Hypoglycaemia** | Blood glucose <2.2 mmol/L |
| **Renal impairment** | Creatinine >265 µmol/L (or >3 mg/dL) |
| **Pulmonary oedema / ARDS** | Radiological + hypoxaemia |
| **Significant bleeding / DIC** | Platelets <50, PT prolonged |
| **Shock** | SBP <80 (adults) / <50 (children) |
| **Parasitaemia** | >10% (or >250,000/µL in low transmission) |
| **Hyperparasitaemia** | >20% (>500,000/µL) |

> **Cerebral malaria** = unrousable coma + falciparum + no other cause
> **Blackwater fever** = massive intravascular haemolysis + haemoglobinuria + renal failure (falciparum + G6PD deficiency + oxidant drugs)

## 4. Diagnosis

### Gold Standard: Microscopy (Thick + Thin Blood Films)
| Film | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Thick film** | **Screening** (concentrated, 20–30x sensitivity); detect low parasitaemia; count parasitaemia |
| **Thin film** | **Species identification** (morphology); quantify % parasitaemia |

> **3 negative films 12–24h apart** to exclude malaria (if high suspicion, continue)

### Rapid Diagnostic Tests (RDTs)
| Target | Sensitivity | Specificity | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| **HRP2 (falciparum-specific)** | High (>95%) | High | Persists weeks post-treatment (false +) |
| **pLDH (pan-specific or species-specific)** | High | High | Clears rapidly post-treatment |
| **Aldolase (pan-specific)** | Moderate | High | Less common |

> **RDT + microscopy** = ideal; **RDT negative + high suspicion** → repeat microscopy

### Parasitaemia Quantification
| Method | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| **Thin film** | % parasitised RBCs = (parasitised RBCs / total RBCs) × 100 |
| **Thick film** | Parasites/µL = (parasites counted / WBC counted) × patient WBC count |

## 5. Treatment

### Uncomplicated *P. falciparum* (WHO/UK Guidelines)
| Regimen | Dose | Duration | Notes |
|---------|------|----------|-------|
| **Artemether-Lumefantrine (AL)** | 20/120mg: 4 tabs BD (weight-based) | 3 days | **1st-line UK/WHO**; take with fat |
| **Dihydroartemisinin-Piperaquine (DHA-PPQ)** | 40/320mg: 3 tabs OD | 3 days | Alternative; long half-life (piperaquine) |
| **Artesunate-Amodiaquine (AS-AQ)** | Weight-based | 3 days | Africa preferred; not UK 1st-line |
| **Artesunate-Mefloquine (AS-MQ)** | Weight-based | 3 days | SE Asia; neuropsychiatric AE (mefloquine) |

> **ACT = Artemisinin + Partner drug** (artemisinin rapidly reduces biomass; partner eliminates remainder)
> **Take with fatty food** (lumefantrine, piperaquine absorption ↑)
> **Avoid artemisinin monotherapy** (resistance)

### Uncomplicated Non-Falciparum (*P. vivax, P. ovale, P. malariae*)
| Species | Acute Treatment | Radical Cure (Hypnozoites) |
|---------|----------------|----------------------------|
| **P. vivax / P. ovale** | **Chloroquine** (if sensitive) OR **ACT** (if resistant) | **Primaquine 15mg OD × 14d** (G6PD test mandatory!) |
| **P. malariae** | **Chloroquine** (usually sensitive) | **Not needed** (no hypnozoites) |
| **P. knowlesi** | **ACT** (artemether-lumefantrine) | **Primaquine not needed** (no hypnozoites) |

> **Primaquine**: **G6PD testing MANDATORY** (haemolysis risk); 15mg OD × 14d (adults); 0.5mg/kg/d in children
> **G6PD deficiency**: Weekly primaquine 0.75mg/kg × 8w (supervised)

### Chloroquine Dose (if sensitive)
| Drug | Dose |
|------|------|
| **Chloroquine base** | 10mg/kg stat → 5mg/kg at 6h, 24h, 48h (total 25mg/kg over 3 days) |

### Severe Malaria (*P. falciparum*) — **Medical Emergency**
| Drug | Dose | Route | Notes |
|------|------|-------|-------|
| **Artesunate** | **2.4mg/kg IV/IM** at 0, 12, 24h then daily | **IV preferred** | **1st-line WHO/UK**; continue 24h after clinical improvement → switch oral ACT × 3d total |
| **Quinine** (if artesunate unavailable) | 20mg/kg loading IV over 4h → 10mg/kg q8h | IV | Cardiotoxicity (QT); monitor ECG, glucose; switch oral when improving |

> **Artesunate superior to quinine** (↓ mortality 22–35%, fewer side effects)
> **Parenteral for min 24h** → switch to full oral ACT course

### Adjunctive Management of Severe Malaria
| Complication | Management |
|--------------|------------|
| **Cerebral malaria** | Airways, anticonvulsants (levetiracetam), mannitol if raised ICP, avoid steroids |
| **Severe anaemia** | Transfuse Hb <70 (or <50 children); avoid overload |
| **Acidosis** | Bicarbonate if pH <7.1; ventilatory support |
| **Renal failure** | Fluid balance, RRT if indicated |
| **Hypoglycaemia** | 10% dextrose bolus + infusion; monitor glucose q4–6h |
| **Pulmonary oedema** | Fluid restriction, diuretics, CPAP/ventilation |
| **DIC** | FFP, platelets, cryoprecipitate |

## 6. Chemoprophylaxis (Travellers)
| Drug | Dose | Start/Stop | Indications |
|------|------|------------|-------------|
| **Atovaquone-Proguanil (Malarone)** | 250/100mg OD | 1–2d before → 7d after | **1st-line** (well-tolerated, short post-travel) |
| **Doxycycline** | 100mg OD | 1–2d before → 4w after | Cheap; photosensitivity, GI, oesophagitis |
| **Mefloquine** | 250mg weekly | 2–3w before → 4w after | Neuropsychiatric AE (contraindicated if hx); long half-life |
| **Chloroquine + Proguanil** | Chloroquine weekly + Proguanil daily | 1w before → 4w after | Limited areas (chloroquine-sensitive only) |

> **No prophylaxis 100% effective** — bite avoidance essential (DEET, nets, clothing)
> **Standby emergency treatment (SBET)**: Carry AL for self-treatment if medical care >24h away

## 7. Malaria in Special Populations

### Pregnancy
| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Risk** | ↑ severity (falciparum); ↑ maternal/fetal mortality; placental malaria → LBW, stillbirth |
| **Treatment (uncomplicated)** | **2nd/3rd tri: ACT (AL preferred)**; **1st tri: Quinine + Clindamycin** (artemisinin safety uncertain) |
| **Treatment (severe)** | **Artesunate IV** all trimesters (life-saving) |
| **Prophylaxis** | **Mefloquine** (2nd/3rd tri); **Atovaquone-Proguanil** (2nd/3rd); **Doxycycline CONTRAINDICATED**; Chloroquine + Proguanil + Folic acid |

### Children
| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Weight-based dosing** | Essential (artesunate 2.4mg/kg AL weight-based) |
| **Severe malaria** | Artesunate IV 2.4mg/kg (same as adults); rectal artesunate if IV unavailable |
| **Primaquine** | G6PD test mandatory; 0.5mg/kg/d × 14d |

### HIV Co-infection
| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Severity** | ↑ parasitaemia, ↑ severe malaria if CD4 low |
| **Treatment** | Same ACT (check interactions: efavirenz ↓ lumefantrine; DTG + AL OK) |
| **Prophylaxis** | Atovaquone-proguanil OK with ART; Mefloquine + EFV = ↓ mefloquine levels |

## 8. UK Imported Malaria Guidelines (Key Points)
- **All fever in returned traveller from endemic area** = malaria until proven otherwise
- **3 blood films 12–24h apart** before excluding
- **RDT + microscopy** = standard
- **Artesunate IV** = 1st-line severe malaria
- **AL (artemether-lumefantrine)** = 1st-line uncomplicated falciparum
- **Primaquine 15mg OD × 14d** for vivax/ovale (G6PD test!)
- **Notify Public Health** (statutory)

## 9. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- **Falciparum** = cerebral, severe anaemia, acidosis, renal; **ring forms only**
- **Vivax/Ovale** = relapsing (hypnozoites), **primaquine 14d** (G6PD test!)
- **Severe malaria** = artesunate IV 2.4mg/kg (not quinine)
- **Uncomplicated falciparum** = AL (artemether-lumefantrine) 3d
- **Chemoprophylaxis**: Atovaquone-proguanil 1st-line; doxycycline; mefloquine (neuropsychiatric)
- **Cerebral malaria** = GCS <11 + falciparum; artesunate + supportive
- **Blackwater fever** = massive haemolysis + Hburia + renal failure (G6PD + falciparum)
- **P. knowlesi** = daily fever (24h), SE Asia, can be severe

## 11. Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- **Quinine no longer 1st-line for severe malaria** — artesunate IV is superior
- **Primaquine = G6PD test mandatory** (haemolysis)
- **Artesunate IV > quinine** (mortality benefit)
- **Cerebral malaria** = treat with artesunate, NOT steroids
- **Chloroquine resistance** = widespread (P. falciparum, P. vivax in many areas)
- **RDT HRP2 persists** weeks post-treatment = false positive
- **RDT negative ≠ no malaria** if high suspicion → microscopy
- **ACT = artemisinin + partner** (never use artemisinin alone)
