1 🎯 Learning Objectives
  • [ ] Identify Dimorphic Fungi: Mould at 25°C / Yeast at 37°C — Histoplasma, Coccidioides, Blastomyces, Paracoccidioides, Talaromyces (Penicillium)
  • [ ] Recognise Endemic Geography: Histoplasma (Ohio/Mississippi River Valleys), Coccidioides (SW USA, N Mexico), Blastomyces (Great Lakes, SE USA), Paracoccidioides (Latin America), Talaromyces (SE Asia)
  • [ ] Recognise Clinical Presentations: Acute/Subacute Pneumonia, Chronic Pulmonary, Disseminated (Skin, Bone, CNS, Adrenal), Immunocompromised
  • [ ] Diagnose: Culture (Gold Standard), Antigen Detection (Urine/Blood/CSF), Serology (CF, ID, EIA), PCR, Histopathology (Yeast in Macrophages)
  • [ ] Manage: Itraconazole (Mild-Mod), Amphotericin B (Severe), Lifelong Suppression (Immunosuppressed), Azoles (Itraconazole, Voriconazole, Posaconazole)
2 📖 General Features of Dimorphic Fungi
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Dimorphism****Mould at 25°C (Environment)** → **Yeast at 37°C (Host)** — **Thermal Dimorphism**
**Infection Route****Inhalation** of Conidia → **Primary Pulmonary** → **Haematogenous Dissemination**
**Risk Factors****Immunocompromise** (HIV/CD4<150, Transplant, TNF-α Inhibitors, Steroids), **Occupational/Recreational Exposure** (Caves, Soil, Construction)
**Endemicity****Geographically Restricted** (See Table Below)
**Incubation****1-3 Weeks** (Acute), **Months-Years** (Reactivation)
PathogenEndemic RegionVector/Source
----------------------------------------
**Histoplasma capsulatum****Ohio/Mississippi River Valleys** (USA), Central/South America, Africa, SE Asia**Bird/Bat Guano** (Caves, Chicken Coops, Old Buildings)
**Coccidioides immitis/posadasii****SW USA (CA, AZ, NV, NM, TX), N Mexico****Soil** (Desert, Dust Storms, Construction, Archaeology)
**Blastomyces dermatitidis****Great Lakes, SE USA, Ohio/Mississippi River Valleys, Canada (Ontario, Manitoba)****Moist Soil, Decaying Wood** (Riverbanks, Lakes, Excavation)
**Paracoccidioides brasiliensis/lutzii****Latin America** (Brazil, Colombia, Venezuela, Argentina)**Soil** (Rural, Agricultural)
**Talaromyces (Penicillium) marneffei****SE Asia** (Thailand, Vietnam, Hong Kong, Taiwan, NE India)**Bamboo Rats, Soil** (Immunocompromised Hosts)
[!tip] Endemic Mycoses = Dimorphic Fungi (Mould 25°C → Yeast 37°C). Inhalation → Primary Pulmonary → Dissemination. Geography = Diagnosis Clue.
3 📖 Specific Endemic Mycoses
1. Histoplasmosis (*Histoplasma capsulatum*)
AspectDetails
-----------------
**Endemic Areas****Ohio/Mississippi River Valleys (USA)**, Central/South America, Africa, SE Asia
**Exposure****Bird/Bat Guano** (Caves, Chicken Coops, Old Buildings, Bridge Demolition)
**Clinical Forms****Acute Pulmonary** (Self-Limited), **Chronic Pulmonary** (Cavitary, COPD-like), **Disseminated** (Immunocompromised — Adrenal, Skin, CNS, Mucosal), **Mediastinal Fibrosis** (Broncholithiasis, SVCO)
**Acute Pulmonary**Flu-like, Cough, Fever, Chest Pain, Hilar Lymphadenopathy, **Self-Limited**
**Chronic Pulmonary****Cavitary Apollo Upper Lobes**, **Cough, Haemoptysis, Weight Loss**, Mimics TB
**Disseminated****HIV/CD4<150**, **Adrenal Insufficiency (Addison's)**, **Oral/NG Ulcers**, **Hepatosplenomegaly**, **CNS**

#### Diagnosis

TestSensitivity/SpecificityRole
------------------------------------
**Urine/Serum Histoplasma Antigen****High Sens/Spec (90%+)****Rapid, First-Line**
**Culture (Gold Standard)****Definitive****Slow (2-4 Weeks), BSL-3**
**Serology (CF, ID, EIA)**Antibody DetectionSupportive
**Histopathology****Yeast in Macrophages** (Small, Oval, Intracellular)**Diagnostic** (Biopsy)
**PCR****Rapid**Emerging

#### Treatment

Syndrome1st LineDuration
------------------------------
**Acute Pulmonary (Mild)****Observe** (Self-Limited) / **Itraconazole 200mg BD** if Prolonged >1 Month6-12 Weeks
**Chronic Pulmonary****Itraconazole 200mg BD****12-24 Months**
**Disseminated / CNS****LAmB 3-5mg/kg/day IV ×2w** → **Itraconazole 200mg BD****12 Months** (Lifelong if Immunosuppressed)
**Mediastinal Fibrosis****No Antifungal** → **Surgery/Stents** if SVCO—
2. Coccidioidomycosis (Valley Fever)
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Agent***Coccidioides immitis* (CA), *C. posadasii* (AZ, MX, Rest)
**Endemic****SW USA (CA, AZ, NV, NM, UT, TX), N Mexico**
**Exposure****Soil Dust** (Construction, Archaeology, Dust Storms, Military, Agriculture)
**Clinical****60% Asymptomatic**; **Acute Pneumonia (Valley Fever)**: Fever, Cough, Fatigue, **Erythema Nodosum** (Erythema Multiforme), **Arthralgia** ("Desert Rheumatism"), **Chronic Cavitary**, **Disseminated** (Meningitis, Skin, Bone, Joints)
**Risk Factors****Immunocompromised**, **Pregnancy (3rd Trimester)**, **African/Filipino Ancestry** (↑ Dissemination)

#### Diagnosis & Treatment

TestUtility
---------------
**Serology (CF, ID, EIA)****IgM (Early), IgG (Late), CF Titer (Prognosis)**
**Culture****Definitive** (BSL-3)
**Antigen (Urine/Serum)**Available
Syndrome1st LineDuration
------------------------------
**Primary Pulmonary (Mild)****Observe** / **Fluconazole 400mg OD**3-6 Months
**Chronic Pulmonary / Disseminated****Fluconazole 400-800mg OD** / **Itraconazole 200mg BD****12+ Months**
**Meningitis****Fluconazole 800-1200mg OD** + **Intrathecal Amphotericin** (Refractory)**Lifelong**
[!warning] Coccidioidal Meningitis = Lifelong Suppression (Fluconazole 800-1200mg OD). Relapse Common if Stopped.
3. Blastomycosis
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Agent***Blastomyces dermatitidis*
**Endemic****Great Lakes, SE USA, Ohio/Mississippi River Valleys**, Canada (ON, MB)
**Exposure****Moist Soil, Decaying Wood** (Riverbanks, Lakes, Excavation, Construction)
**Clinical****Acute Pneumonia** (Flu-like), **Chronic Pneumonia** (Cavitary, Mimics TB), **Disseminated** (Skin: Verrucous Ulcers, **Bone (Osteomyelitis)**, **Prostatitis**, **CNS**)
**Skin Lesions****Verrucous, Ulcerative, Crusted** (Face, Extremities) — **"Blastomycotic Dermatitis"**

#### Diagnosis & Treatment

TestUtility
---------------
**Culture****Gold Standard** (Yeast at 37°C, Broad-Based Budding)
**Antigen (Urine/Serum)****High Sens/Spec** (Cross-Reacts with Histoplasma)
**KOH/Histology****Broad-Based Budding Yeast (8-15µm), Thick Wall**
Syndrome1st LineDuration
------------------------------
**Pulmonary (Mild/Moderate)****Itraconazole 200mg BD****6-12 Months**
**Severe / CNS / Disseminated****LAmB 3-5mg/kg/day ×2w** → **Itraconazole 200mg BD****6-12 Months**
**CNS****LAmB 5mg/kg/day ×4-6w** → **Fluconazole 800mg OD****Lifelong**
[!warning] Blastomycosis = Broad-Based Budding Yeast (8-15µm) on KOH/Histology. Skin Lesions = Verrucous Ulcers. Prostatitis Common in Men.
4. Paracoccidioidomycosis
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Agent***Paracoccidioides brasiliensis* / *P. lutzii*
**Endemic****Latin America** (Brazil, Colombia, Venezuela, Argentina, Ecuador)
**Clinical****Acute/Subacute (Juvenile)**: Fever, Lymphadenopathy, Hepatosplenomegaly; **Chronic Adult (Adult)**: **Pulmonary (Cavitary)**, **Mucosal (Oral/Pharyngeal — "Mulberry" Ulcers)**, **Adrenal Insufficiency**, **Lymphadenopathy**
**Microscopy****"Pilot's Wheel" / "Mariner's Wheel"** Yeast (Multiple Budding)
SyndromeTreatment
---------------------
**Acute/Subacute****Amphotericin B** → **Itraconazole 200mg BD × 6-12 Months**
**Chronic****Itraconazole 200mg BD** × **6-18 Months**
5. Talaromycosis (Penicilliosis marneffei)
FeatureDetails
------------------
**Agent***Talaromyces (Penicillium) marneffei*
**Endemic****SE Asia** (Thailand, Vietnam, Hong Kong, Taiwan, NE India)
**Host****HIV/AIDS (CD4<100)** — **AIDS-Defining Illness**
**Clinical****Fever, Weight Loss, Anaemia**, **Skin Lesions (Umbilicated Papules, Central Necrosis)**, **Lymphadenopathy**, **Hepatosplenomegaly**, **Respiratory**, **GI**
**Diagnosis****Culture (Red Colony, Soluble Red Pigment)**, **PCR**, **Histology (Yeast in Macrophages, Transverse Septa)**
**Treatment****LAmB 3-5mg/kg/day IV ×2w** → **Itraconazole 200mg BD ×10 Weeks** (Secondary Prophylaxis: **Itraconazole 200mg OD** until Immune Reconstitution)

5 ❓ Viva Questions
  1. What is the endemic region for Histoplasmosis?
  • Ohio and Mississippi River Valleys (USA), also Central/South America, Africa, SE Asia.
  1. What is the classic radiological finding in Chronic Pulmonary Histoplasmosis?
  • Cavitary Lesions in Upper Lobes (Mimics TB).
  1. What is the diagnostic test of choice for Histoplasmosis?
  • Urine/Serum Histoplasma Antigen (High Sens/Spec, Rapid); Culture = Gold Standard.
  1. What is the endemic region for Coccidioidomycosis?
  • SW USA (CA, AZ, NV, NM, UT, TX) + Northern Mexico.
  1. What is the classic triad of Coccidioidomycosis ("Valley Fever")?
  • Fever, Cough, Erythema Nodosum (Plus Arthralgia = "Desert Rheumatism").
  1. What is the treatment for Coccidioidal Meningitis?
  • Fluconazole 800-1200mg OD Lifelong (Relapse Common if Stopped).
  1. What is the characteristic microscopic appearance of Blastomyces?
  • Broad-Based Budding Yeast (8-15µm), Thick Double Wall ("Broad-Based Budding").
  1. What is the endemic area for Blastomycosis?
  • Great Lakes, SE USA, Ohio/Mississippi River Valleys, Canada (Ontario, Manitoba).
  1. What is the characteristic skin lesion of Blastomycosis?
  • Verrucous, Ulcerated, Crusted ("Blastomycotic Dermatitis").
  1. Describe Paracoccidioidomycosis microscopy.
  • "Pilot's Wheel" / "Mariner's Wheel" Yeast (Multiple Budding Daughter Cells Around Mother Cell).
  1. What is the treatment for Paracoccidioidomycosis?
  • Itraconazole 200mg BD × 6-18 Months (Chronic); Amphotericin B → Itraconazole (Acute).
  1. Where is Talaromyces marneffei endemic and who does it affect?
  • SE Asia (Thailand, Vietnam, Hong Kong, Taiwan, NE India); HIV/AIDS (CD4<100) — AIDS-Defining.
  1. Differentiate Histoplasmosis from Blastomycosis on microscopy.
  • Histoplasma: Small Yeast (2-4µm) Inside Macrophages; Blastomyces: Large Yeast (8-15µm), Broad-Based Budding, Thick Wall, Extracellular.
6 🧠 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**Histoplasma vs Blastomyces****Histoplasma: Small Intracellular Yeast (2-4µm) in Macrophages**; **Blastomyces: Large Extracellular Yeast (8-15µm), Broad-Based Budding**
**Coccidioides vs Histoplasma****Cocci: SW USA, Erythema Nodosum, Arthralgia, CF Serology**; **Histo: Ohio/Mississippi Valleys, Bat Guano, Urine Antigen**
**Blastomyces vs Paracoccidioides****Blasto: Broad-Based Budding Yeast (8-15µm)**; **Paracoccidioides: "Pilot's Wheel" Multiple Budding**
**Talaromyces vs Penicillium****T. marneffei = Former Penicillium marneffei**; **Red Pigment, Transverse Septa, SE Asia + HIV**
**Itraconazole vs Fluconazole****Itraconazole = 1st Line for Most Endemic Mycoses**; **Fluconazole = Coccidioidal Meningitis, Maintenance**
MnemonicMeaning
-------------------
**"Histo = Ohio/Mississippi + Bat Guano + Urine Antigen"**Histoplasma

**"Blasto = Great Lakes + Broad-Based Budding + Skin Ulcers"**Blastomyces
**"Paracoccidioides = Latin America + Pilot's Wheel + Mucosal Ulcers"**Paracoccidioides
**"Talaromyces = SE Asia + HIV + Red Pigment + Umbilicated Papules"**Talaromycosis
