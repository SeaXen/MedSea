3 📖 Maternal Infections in Pregnancy — Key Management
Syphilis in Pregnancy
StageTreatment
------------------
**Primary, Secondary, Early Latent (<1 Year)****Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Single Dose**
**Late Latent (>1 Year) / Tertiary****Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3 Doses**
**Penicillin Allergy****Desensitisation → Penicillin** (Doxycycline/Azithromycin NOT Effective for Congenital Prevention)
**Neonatal Assessment****Physical Exam, VDRL/RPR, CSF (If Mother Untreated/Inadequate Rx/Late Rx/Clinical Signs)**, **Long Bone X-Ray**
[!warning] Penicillin = Only Reliable Prevention of Congenital Syphilis. Doxycycline/Azithromycin Do NOT Prevent Congenital Syphilis. Penicillin Allergy → Desensitisation.
HIV in Pregnancy
AspectManagement
--------------------
**ART Goal****Viral Suppression (VL <50 copies/mL) by Delivery**
**ART Regimen****Dolutegravir + TDF/FTC** (Preferred); **Efavirenz** (Avoid 1st Trimester Historically, Now OK)
**Viral Load Monitoring****Booking, 36wks, Delivery**
**Delivery****Viral Load <50 → Vaginal OK**; **Viral Load 50-399 → Elective C-Section 38wks**; **Viral Load >400 → Emergency C-Section**
**Neonatal Prophylaxis****Zidovudine 4mg/kg BD × 4 Weeks** (If Maternal VL <50) / **Zidovudine + Nevirapine + Lamivudine** (High Risk)
**Breastfeeding****Contraindicated in High-Income Settings** (Formula Feed); **Low-Income: Exclusive BF + Maternal ART + Infant Prophylaxis**
Hepatitis B in Pregnancy
ScenarioManagement
----------------------
**HBsAg+ Mother****HBIG 100IU IM + HBV Vaccine 10µg IM** to Infant **Within 12h of Birth**
**High Infectivity (HBeAg+, VL >200,000 IU/mL)****Tenofovir 300mg OD From 28-32 Weeks** → **Stop at Delivery/Postpartum**
**Breastfeeding****Safe** (If Infant Received HBIG + Vaccine)
**Infant Schedule****HBIG + Vaccine at Birth → Vaccine at 1, 6 Months**
Hepatitis C in Pregnancy
AspectManagement
--------------------
**Vertical Transmission****~5-6%** (Higher if HIV Co-infection, High VL)
**Treatment****DAA Deferred Until Postpartum** (Safety Data Limited in Pregnancy)
**Breastfeeding****Safe** (If Nipples Not Cracked/Bleeding)
**Infant Testing****HCV RNA at 2-6 Months** or **Anti-HCV at 18 Months**
GBS (Group B Streptococcus) Prevention
StrategyDetails
-----------------
**Screening****Rectovaginal Swab at 36-37 Weeks** (Culture or PCR)
**IAP Indication****GBS Positive**, **Previous GBS Neonate**, **Bacteriuria in Pregnancy**, **Unknown Status + Preterm Labour/Rupture/≥18h Rupture/Fever**
**IAP Regimen****Penicillin G 5MU IV Load → 2.5-3MU Q4H** (Until Delivery)
**Penicillin Allergy****Cefazolin 2g IV Load → 1g Q8H** (If No Anaphylaxis); **Clindamycin/Vancomycin** (If High Risk Anaphylaxis)
**Duration****Until Delivery** (≥4h Pre-Delivery Optimal)

5 📖 Congenital Infections — Specific Management
Congenital CMV (cCMV)
AspectDetails
-----------------
**Epidemiology****Most Common Congenital Infection** (~0.5-1% Live Births); **Symptomatic ~10-15%**
**Transmission****Primary Maternal Infection (30-40% Transmission)**; **Reactivation/Reinfection (<2%)**
**Clinical****Asymptomatic (85-90%)** vs **Symptomatic (10-15%)**: Petechiae, Hepatosplenomegaly, Jaundice, Microcephaly, **Sensorineural Hearing Loss (Most Common Sequela)**, Chorioretinitis, Neurodevelopmental Delay
**Diagnosis****Urine/Saliva PCR (Gold Standard, <21 Days)**, **CMV IgM**, **Culture**
**Treatment****Symptomatic: Valganciclovir 16mg/kg BD PO × 6 Months** (Improves Hearing/Neurodevelopment)
**Follow-Up****Audiology (Every 6mo × 3yr)**, **Developmental Assessment**, **Ophthalmology**
Neonatal HSV
TypeFeaturesTreatment
---------------------------
**SEM (Skin/Eye/Mouth)****Vesicles (45%)**, Conjunctivitis, Oral Lesions**Acyclovir 20mg/kg IV Q8H × 14 Days**
**CNS****Seizures, Lethargy, CSF Pleocytosis****Acyclovir 20mg/kg IV Q8H × 21 Days**
**Disseminated****Multi-Organ (Liver, Lung, CNS, DIC)****Acyclovir 20mg/kg IV Q8H × 21 Days**
[!warning] Neonatal HSV = Acyclovir 20mg/kg IV Q8H; CNS/Disseminated = 21 Days, SEM = 14 Days. CSF PCR = Gold Standard.
Congenital Syphilis
ScenarioManagement
----------------------
**Maternal Treatment Adequate****Baby: Benzathine Penicillin G 50,000U/kg IM Single Dose** (If Asymptomatic, Normal Exam, Normal CSF/Long Bones)
**Maternal Treatment Inadequate/Unknown****Baby: Aqueous Penicillin G 50,000U/kg IV Q12H × 10-14 Days**
**CSF Abnormal / Clinical Signs****Aqueous Penicillin G 50,000U/kg IV Q12H × 10-14 Days**

InfectionBreastfeeding Recommendation
-----------------------------------------
**HIV****Contraindicated (High-Income)**; **Low-Income: Exclusive BF + Maternal ART + Infant Prophylaxis**
**HBV****Safe** (After HBIG + Vaccine to Infant)
**HCV****Safe** (If Nipples Intact)
**CMV****Safe** (If Term Infant); **Preterm <32w/1500g: Pasteurised Donor Milk / Freeze-Thaw**
**HSV****Safe** (If No Breast Lesions); **Avoid if Active Lesion on Breast**

**Malaria****Safe** (If Mother Treated)
**Zika****Safe** (No Live Virus in Breast Milk)

8 ❓ Viva Questions
  1. What is the most common congenital infection?
  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV).
  1. What is the treatment for congenital syphilis in the neonate?
  • Aqueous Penicillin G 50,000U/kg IV Q12H × 10-14 Days (If Maternal Treatment Inadequate/CSF Abnormal).
  1. How do you prevent mother-to-child transmission of HIV?
  • ART to Achieve VL<50 by Delivery, Elective C-Section if VL>400, Neonatal Zidovudine 4mg/kg BD × 4 Weeks, Avoid Breastfeeding (High-Income).
  1. How do you prevent GBS neonatal sepsis?
  • Screen at 36-37 Weeks (Rectovaginal Swab) → Penicillin G 5MU IV Load → 2.5-3MU Q4H Until Delivery if Positive.
  1. What is the management of a neonate born to HBsAg+ mother?
  • HBIG 100IU IM + HBV Vaccine 10µg IM Within 12 Hours of Birth; Complete Vaccine Series.
  1. What is the treatment for neonatal HSV infection?
  • Acyclovir 20mg/kg IV Q8H; 14 Days (SEM), 21 Days (CNS/Disseminated).
  1. What is the risk of vertical transmission of Hepatitis C?
  • ~5-6% (Higher if HIV Co-infection or High Viral Load).
  1. How do you diagnose congenital CMV?
  • Urine or Saliva PCR (Gold Standard) Within 21 Days of Life; Culture (Gold Standard); IgM Less Reliable.
  1. What is the management of congenital toxoplasmosis?
  • Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Folinic Acid × 12 Months.
  1. When do you give HBIG + Hepatitis B vaccine to a neonate?
  • Within 12 Hours of Birth (HBsAg+ Mother).
  1. What is the empirical antibiotic regimen for early-onset neonatal sepsis?
  • Ampicillin 50mg/kg Q12H + Gentamicin 4-5mg/kg OD (Term) — Adjust for Preterm.
  1. How do you prevent mother-to-child transmission of Hepatitis B?
  • HBIG + Vaccine at Birth → Vaccine at 1 & 6 Months; Tenofovir 300mg OD from 28-32 Weeks if Viral Load >200,000 IU/mL.
  1. What is the treatment for congenital toxoplasmosis?
  • Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Folinic Acid × 12 Months.

**CMV vs Rubella****CMV = Most Common, Hearing Loss**; **Rubella = Cataracts, Deafness, PDA**

**CMV vs HSV Neonatal****CMV: Hearing Loss, Microcephaly**; **HSV: Vesicles, CNS/Disseminated, Acyclovir**
