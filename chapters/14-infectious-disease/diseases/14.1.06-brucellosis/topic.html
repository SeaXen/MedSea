SEA KNOWLEDGE STUDY NOTES
INFECTIOUS DISEASE
Brucellosis
1 Learning Objectives
  • Recognise clinical presentations (acute, subacute, chronic, focal)
  • Apply diagnostic criteria (culture, serology, PCR)
  • Select appropriate combination therapy by disease form
  • Manage complications (endocarditis, neurobrucellosis, osteoarticular)
  • Apply prevention (pasteurisation, animal vaccination, occupational hygiene)
2 Epidemiology & Transmission
AspectDetails
-----------------
**Agents***Brucella* spp.: **B. melitensis** (goats/sheep — most virulent), **B. abortus** (cattle), **B. suis** (pigs), **B. canis** (dogs)
**Reservoirs**Domestic livestock (sheep, goats, cattle, pigs); wildlife
**Transmission****Unpasteurised dairy** (milk, cheese, butter); **Direct contact** with infected tissues/fluids (placenta, fetus, blood); **Aerosol inhalation** (lab, abattoir); **Rare**: human-to-human (sexual, breastfeeding, transplant)
**Risk Groups**Farmers, veterinarians, abattoir workers, lab workers, travellers consuming unpasteurised dairy
**Geography**Mediterranean, Middle East, Central Asia, Latin America, India (endemic)
3 Clinical Manifestations
FormFeatures
----------------
**Acute/Subacute****Undulant fever** (spiking fever, remission, recurrence), **arthralgia/myalgia**, headache, sweats, fatigue, hepatosplenomegaly
**Focal/Complicated****Osteoarticular** (sacroiliitis, spondylitis, peripheral arthritis — 20–60%); **Endocarditis** (aortic valve, high mortality); **Neurobrucellosis** (meningoencephalitis, radiculopathy); **Genitourinary** (orchitis, epididymitis); **Hepatic/Splenic** (abscesses)
**Chronic**Persistent fatigue, depression, arthralgia >1 year; often misdiagnosed as CFS
[!key] Osteoarticular = most common focal (20–60%); sacroiliitis characteristic. Endocarditis = most serious (aortic valve, culture-negative). Neurobrucellosis = meningitic/encephalitic; CSF lymphocytic, ↑ protein, low glucose.

TestSensitivity/SpecificityTimingNotes
---------------------------------------------
**Blood Culture****Gold standard**; 50–70% (acute), <30% (chronic)Weeks 1–3**Castaneda biphasic media**; slow (3–7 days); BSL-3
**Bone Marrow Culture****90%**Any timeHighest yield if antibiotics given
**Standard Agglutination Test (SAT)**80–95% / 95%Week 2+**≥1:160 = positive**; ≥1:320 = active; prozone phenomenon
**Coombs Anti-Brucella**Detects incomplete (IgG) antibodiesChronic/relapseHigher titre than SAT
**Brucella IgG/IgM ELISA**High sensitivityWeek 1+IgM = acute; IgG = past/chronic; **Lack of standardisation**
**Brucellacapt (Lateral Flow)**High sensitivity/specificityAcute/chronicDetects IgG+IgM+IgA; quantitative
**PCR (Blood/Tissue)**High sensitivityEarlyRapid; not universally available
**CSF Analysis (Neuro)**Lymphocytic, ↑ protein, low glucoseNeurobrucellosisPCR + culture + serology
[!key] Culture = gold standard but slow; SAT ≥1:160 + compatible clinical = probable; SAT ≥1:320 + clinical = confirmed. Bone marrow culture > blood culture. PCR = rapid adjunct.
5 Treatment
Acute/Uncomplicated Brucellosis (Adults)
RegimenDoseDurationNotes
--------------------------------
**Doxycycline + Rifampicin****Doxycycline 100mg PO 12h + Rifampicin 600mg PO OD****6 weeks****1st-line (WHO)**; reload if relapse
**Alternative (pregnancy/children <8)****TMP-SMX 160/800mg 12h + Rifampicin 600mg OD**6 weeksDoxycycline contraindicated
Complicated/Focal Brucellosis
SyndromeRegimenDuration
-----------------------------
**Endocarditis****Doxycycline 100mg 12h + Rifampicin 600mg OD + Gentamicin 5mg/kg IV OD** (first 2w)**3–6 months** (valve replacement often needed)
**Neurobrucellosis****Doxycycline 100mg 12h + Rifampicin 600mg OD + TMP-SMX DS 12h****3–6 months**
**Osteoarticular (Sacroiliitis/Spondylitis)****Doxycycline 100mg 12h + Rifampicin 600mg OD****3–6 months**
**CNS/Endocarditis (Doxycycline contraindicated)****TMP-SMX DS 12h + Rifampicin 600mg OD + Gentamicin** (2w)3–6 months
[!key] Doxycycline + Rifampicin ×6w = standard for uncomplicated. Endocarditis/CNS = add aminoglycoside (first 2w) + prolong to 3–6m. Relapse 5–15% — retreat with same or alternative regimen.
Pregnancy/Children
PopulationRegimen
---------------------
**Pregnancy****TMP-SMX DS 12h + Rifampicin 600mg OD** (avoid doxycycline)
**Children <8 years****TMP-SMX 8mg/kg TMP 12h + Rifampicin 15mg/kg OD**
**Children ≥8 years****Doxycycline 2.2mg/kg 12h + Rifampicin 15mg/kg OD**
6 Complications
ComplicationFrequencyManagement
-------------------------------------
**Osteoarticular**20–60%Doxy + Rifampicin ×3–6m; sacroiliitis → MRI; joint aspiration if septic
**Endocarditis**1–2%**Doxy + Rifampicin + Gentamicin (2w) ×3–6m**; valve replacement often needed
**Neurobrucellosis**<5%**Doxy + Rifampicin + TMP-SMX ×3–6m**; steroids if meningoencephalitis
**Genitourinary**2–20% (orchitis/epididymitis)Standard regimen; scrotal support
**Hepatosplenic Abscess**RareDrainage + prolonged antibiotics
**Relapse**5–15%Retreat with same/alternative regimen
7 Prevention
MeasureDetails
------------------
**Pasteurisation****Milk, cheese, dairy products** — most effective
**Animal Vaccination****RB51 (cattle), Rev-1 (sheep/goats)** — reduces animal prevalence
**Occupational Hygiene**PPE (gloves, goggles, N95) for high-risk workers; lab safety (BSL-3)
**Food Safety****Avoid unpasteurised dairy** (raw milk, soft cheese); cook meat thoroughly
**Animal Control**Test-and-slaughter; quarantine; movement restrictions
8 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
  • Brucellosis: zoonosis from unpasteurised dairy/animal contact; B. melitensis (goats) most virulent
  • Undulant fever, arthralgia, hepatosplenomegaly; sacroiliitis (characteristic)
  • Diagnosis: Blood/bone marrow culture (gold); SAT ≥1:160; Brucella IgG/IgM ELISA
  • Acute treatment: Doxycycline 100mg 12h + Rifampicin 600mg OD ×6w
  • Endocarditis/Neuro/CNS: Doxy + Rifampicin + Gentamicin (2w) ×3–6m
  • Pregnancy/children <8: TMP-SMX + Rifampicin (doxy contraindicated)
  • Relapse 5–15%; retreat with same/alternative regimen
  • Prevention: pasteurisation, animal vaccination (RB51, Rev-1)
9 Common Viva Questions
  1. **Most common Brucella species causing human disease?** B. melitensis (goats/sheep) — most virulent.
  2. Classic clinical triad of brucellosis? Undulant fever, arthralgia, hepatosplenomegaly.
  3. Most common focal complication? Osteoarticular (sacroiliitis, spondylitis) — 20–60%.
  4. Diagnostic gold standard? Blood/bone marrow culture (bone marrow > blood).
  5. Standard agglutination test (SAT) cut-off? ≥1:160 positive; ≥1:320 active disease.
  6. Treatment for uncomplicated brucellosis? Doxycycline 100mg 12h + Rifampicin 600mg OD ×6w.
  7. Treatment for brucellar endocarditis? Doxycycline + Rifampicin + Gentamicin (first 2w) ×3–6m.
  8. Treatment in pregnancy/children? TMP-SMX + Rifampicin (doxycycline contraindicated).
  9. Relapse rate? 5–15%; retreat with same/alternative regimen.
  10. Prevention? Pasteurisation of dairy; animal vaccination (RB51, Rev-1).
10 Common Confusions / Exam Traps
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
SAT negative = no brucellosis**Prozone phenomenon** (high antibody = false low); do serial dilutions / Coombs
Doxycycline safe in pregnancy**CONTRAINDICATED** (bone/teeth); use TMP-SMX + Rifampicin
Rifampicin monotherapy**Never** — rapid resistance; always combine
Doxycycline + Rifampicin = 2 weeks**6 weeks minimum** for uncomplicated; 3–6m for focal
Brucella endocarditis = same as other culture-negative**Aortic valve predominant; high mortality; needs prolonged combo + surgery**
SAT single titre diagnostic**Need rising titre (4-fold) or ≥1:320 + clinical**
Doxycycline alone sufficient**Monotherapy = relapse/resistance; always combine with Rifampicin**
Chronic fatigue = brucellosis**Post-brucellosis fatigue syndrome = exclusion diagnosis**
