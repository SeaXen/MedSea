Viral Hepatitis
1 Learning Objectives
  • Differentiate hepatitis viruses by transmission, clinical course, and chronicity
  • Interpret serology and molecular markers for each hepatitis virus
  • Stage Hepatitis B and C (fibrosis, cirrhosis, HCC risk)
  • Select and monitor antiviral therapy for Hepatitis B and C
  • Manage complications (cirrhosis, HCC, reactivation)
  • Apply vaccination and prevention strategies
  • Manage special populations (pregnancy, HIV coinfection, immunosuppression, transplant)

3 Hepatitis A (HAV)
AspectDetails
-----------------
**Transmission**Faecal-oral (contaminated food/water, person-to-person); shellfish; outbreaks in institutions
**Risk Groups**Travellers to endemic areas, MSM, PWID, household contacts, institutionalised
**Clinical****Acute self-limiting**; prodrome (fever, malaise, anorexia, nausea) → jaundice, dark urine, pale stools; **no chronic infection**; fulminant rare (<0.5%)
**Diagnosis****Anti-HAV IgM** (acute); Anti-HAV IgG (past immunity); HAV RNA (research)
**Management**Supportive; **no specific antiviral**; hospitalise if severe/fulminant; avoid hepatotoxins
**Prevention****Hep A vaccine (inactivated)**: 2 doses (0, 6–12m); **Post-exposure**: vaccine ± IgG (within 2w); hygiene
**Special****Pregnancy**: increased severity in 3rd trimester; vaccine safe

Serology Interpretation
MarkerMeaning
-----------------
**HBsAg**Current infection (acute or chronic)
**Anti-HBs**Immunity (vaccine or resolved)
**Anti-HBc IgM**Acute/recent infection (<6m)
**Anti-HBc IgG**Past or chronic infection
**HBeAg**High replicative, high infectivity
**Anti-HBe**Lower replicative (usually)
**HBV DNA**Viral load (quantifies replication)
Fibrosis Staging & HCC Surveillance
ToolIndication
------------------
**Transient Elastography (FibroScan)**Non-invasive fibrosis staging (kPa)
**APRI / FIB-4**Serum-based fibrosis scores
**Liver Biopsy**Gold standard (if noninvasive discordant)
**HCC Surveillance****6-monthly US ± AFP** if cirrhosis OR high-risk chronic HBV (Asian male >40, female >50, family HCC, cirrhosis)
HBV Treatment Indications (EASL/AASLD/APASL)
IndicationCriteria
----------------------
**Immune Active (HBeAg+)**HBV DNA >20,000 IU/mL + ALT >2×ULN (≥2× over 3–6m)
**HBeAg-Negative Chronic Hepatitis**HBV DNA >2,000 IU/mL + ALT >ULN (≥2× over 3–6m)
**Cirrhosis (Compensated)****Any detectable HBV DNA**
**Decompensated Cirrhosis**Any detectable HBV DNA
**Reactivation Risk**Immunosuppression/chemo (prophylactic NUCs)

Special HBV Situations
SituationManagement
-----------------------
**Pregnancy****TDF/TAF safe**; avoid ETV (limited data); **TDF 3rd trimester if HBV DNA >200,000** (reduce vertical transmission); infant HBIG + vaccine 12h
**HIV Coinfection****TDF/TAF + 3TC/FTC** covers both; DTG preferred; avoid 3TC monotherapy
**Immunosuppression/Chemo****Prophylactic NUCs (TDF/TAF/ETV)** if HBsAg+ or anti-HBc+ (reactivation risk)
**HBV Reactivation****NUCs immediately**; monitor HBV DNA; continue ≥12m post-immunosuppression
**Hep D Coinfection****PegIFN-α 48w** only effective therapy; NUCs alone insufficient
5 Hepatitis C (HCV)
Natural History
StageOutcome
----------------
**Acute HCV**15–25% spontaneous clearance; 75–85% chronic
**Chronic HCV**20–30% cirrhosis over 20–30y; risk factors: age >50, male, alcohol, HIV, obesity, steatosis
**Cirrhosis**Decompensation (ascites, varices, encephalopathy), HCC (1–4%/yr)

HCV Treatment — Key Principles
AspectDetail
----------------
**SVR Definition**HCV RNA undetectable **12 weeks post-treatment (SVR12)** = cure
**Pre-treatment**Genotype, Fibrosis (FibroScan/APRI/FIB-4), NS5A RAS testing (GT3/1a if cirrhosis), Drug interactions, Renal function
**Monitoring**HCV RNA at week 4 (rapid virological response), end of treatment, SVR12
**Retreatment Failure****GLE/PIB + SOF + RBV** (16–24w) OR **SOF/VEL/VOX (Vosevi)** 12w
Special HCV Situations
SituationManagement
-----------------------
**HIV Coinfection**Same DAA regimens; check interactions (DTG/GLE/PIB OK); same SVR
**Renal Impairment (CrCl<30)****GLE/PIB** (no renal adjust) OR **SOF/VEL** (avoid SOF if <30); **Grazoprevir/Elbasvir** alternative
**Decompensated Cirrhosis** (Child-Pugh B/C)**SOF/VEL + RBV** 12w (Child-Pugh B); avoid protease inhibitors (GLE/PIB contraindicated)
**Post-Liver Transplant****SOF/VEL** 12w (preferred); GLE/PIB alternative; monitor interactions with CNI
**Acute HCV****DAA if persists >12w**; treat as chronic
6 Hepatitis D (HDV)
AspectDetails
-----------------
**Defective Virus**Requires HBsAg for assembly/transmission
**Transmission**Co-infection (simultaneous HBV+HDV) or superinfection (HDV on chronic HBV)
**Clinical****Superinfection = more severe**; rapid cirrhosis, high HCC risk
**Diagnosis****Anti-HDV IgM/IgG**; **HDV RNA** (quantitative)
**Treatment****PegIFN-α 48 weeks** (only effective therapy); **SVR ~25%**; NUCs alone insufficient
**New Therapies****Bulevirtide (entry inhibitor)** — approved EU; **Lonafarnib** (assembly inhibitor) in trials
7 Hepatitis E (HEV)
AspectDetails
-----------------
**Genotypes****GT1/2** (human, faecal-oral, waterborne, endemic Asia/Africa); **GT3/4** (zoonotic, pigs, undercooked pork, Europe/USA)
**Clinical****Acute self-limiting** (GT1/2); **GT3/4 = chronic in immunosuppressed** (transplant, HIV, chemo)
**Severe in Pregnancy****GT1: 20–30% mortality in 3rd trimester** (fulminant hepatic failure)
**Diagnosis****Anti-HEV IgM** (acute); **HEV RNA** (blood/stool); Anti-HEV IgG (past)
**Treatment****Acute: supportive**; **Chronic (GT3/4 in immunosuppressed): Ribavirin 600mg OD ×3m** (reduce immunosuppression); **PegIFN-α** if ribavirin fails
8 Reactivation & Special Populations
HBV Reactivation
RiskProphylaxis
-------------------
**HBsAg+** (any immunosuppression)**TDF/TAF/ETV prophylaxis** (start before/during, continue ≥12m post)
**HBsAg−/anti-HBc+** (rituximab, high-dose steroids, HSCT)**TDF/TAF/ETV prophylaxis** or close monitoring (HBV DNA q1–3m)
**Resolved HBV (anti-HBc+ only)**Low risk; monitor HBV DNA if high-risk (rituximab, HSCT, alemtuzumab)
HCV Reinfection
RiskManagement
------------------
**Post-SVR reinfection**Re-treat with DAA; same SVR rates; harm reduction (PWID: OAT, needle exchange)
9 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
  • HAV/HEV = faecal-oral, acute only; HBV/HCV/HDV = blood-borne, chronic risk
  • HBV: immune tolerant → immune active → inactive carrier → HBeAg-negative CHB
  • HBV treatment: TDF/TAF/ETV 1st-line (high barrier); lifelong usually; stop if HBeAg seroconversion + 12m consolidation
  • HCV: DAA cure >95%; GLE/PIB pan-genotypic 8w (non-cirr) / 12w (cirr); GT3 cirrhosis = 16w SOF/VEL or 12w GLE/PIB
  • HDV: requires HBsAg; superinfection worse; PegIFN only effective therapy; bulevirtide emerging
  • HEV: GT1/2 acute; GT3/4 chronic in immunocompromised; pregnancy GT1 = 20-30% mortality; chronic = ribavirin
  • HBV reactivation: HBsAg+ = prophylactic NUCs; anti-HBc+ (rituximab/HSCT) = prophylactic NUCs
  • Pregnancy: HAV/HEV severe in 3rd tri; HBV TDF 3rd tri if DNA>200k; HCV treat postpartum; HEV severe in pregnancy
  • HCC surveillance: 6-monthly US ± AFP if cirrhosis or high-risk chronic HBV
10 Common Viva Questions
  1. HBV serology interpretation? HBsAg = infection; Anti-HBs = immunity; Anti-HBc IgM = acute; Anti-HBc IgG = past/chronic; HBeAg = high replication.
  2. HBV treatment indications? Immune active (DNA>20k + ALT>2×ULN), HBeAg-neg CHB (DNA>2k + ALT>ULN), cirrhosis (any DNA).
  3. HCV treatment — pan-genotypic regimen? GLE/PIB 300/120mg OD ×8w (non-cirr) / 12w (cirr); SOF/VEL alternative.
  4. HCV GT3 cirrhosis treatment? SOF/VEL 16w OR GLE/PIB 12w (with RBV if prior treatment failure).
  5. HDV treatment? PegIFN-α 48w only proven; bulevirtide emerging; NUCs alone insufficient.
  6. HEV in pregnancy? GT1 3rd trimester = 20-30% mortality; supportive only.
