## 7.1 STI Surveillance

### Types of STI Surveillance

| Type | Purpose | Methods | Examples |
|------|---------|---------|----------|
| **Case-Based Notification** | **Mandatory Reporting** of Diagnosed Cases (Legal Requirement) | **Statutory Notification** (Lab/Clinician → Public Health) | **Syphilis, Gonorrhoea, Chlamydia, HIV, Hepatitis B/C, Chancroid, LGV, Mpox** |
| **Sentinel Surveillance** | **Detailed Data** from Selected Sites (Representative) | **Designated STI Clinics/GUM** → Enhanced Data (Demographics, Risk, AMR) | **Gonorrhoea AMR (WHO GASP)**, **Chlamydia Positivity**, **Syphilis Stage Distribution** |
| **Syndromic Surveillance** | **Early Warning** (Pre-Lab) | **Symptom-Based (UD, VD, GUD, PID)** from Primary Care/ED | **Outbreak Detection (Shigella, Mpox, LGV Clusters)** |
| **AMR Surveillance (WHO GASP)** | **Monitor Resistance Trends** | **Culture + AST from Sentinel Sites** (National/Regional) | **Gonorrhoea (Ceftriaxone, Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin, Cefixime)** |
| **Behavioural Surveillance (IBBS)** | **Risk Behaviours & Determinants** | **Integrated Bio-Behavioural Surveys (IBBS)** in Key Populations | **MSM, Sex Workers, PWID, Prisoners, Migrants** |
| **Molecular Surveillance** | **Transmission Dynamics, Clusters** | **Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS), MLST, SNP Analysis** | **Gonorrhoea (NG-MAST), Syphilis (TPA), HIV, HCV, Mpox Clades** |

### Key Surveillance Indicators (WHO/Global Health Sector Strategy)

| Indicator | Target (2030) |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Incidence of Syphilis (Maternal)** | **<50 Cases per 100,000 Live Births** (Elimination) |
| **Incidence of Gonorrhoea** | **90% Reduction** (vs 2018 Baseline) |
| **Incidence of Chlamydia** | **90% Reduction** (vs 2018 Baseline) |
| **Hepatitis B Surface Antigen (Children <5)** | **<0.1%** (Elimination) |
| **HPV Vaccination Coverage (Girls 15y)** | **>90%** |
| **STI Service Coverage (Key Populations)** | **>90% Access** |
| **AMR: Ceftriaxone-Resistant Gonorrhoea** | **<5% of Isolates** |

## 7.2 Partner Notification (PN)

### PN Strategies

| Strategy | Description | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|----------|-------------|------------|---------------|
| **Patient Referral (Self-Referral)** | Index Patient Informs Partners | **Empowers Patient, Preserves Autonomy, Lower Cost** | **Low Completion**, Relies on Patient Motivation/Skill |
| **Provider Referral (Health Dept Notification)** | Health Staff Notify Partners (Confidential) | **High Completion, Professional, Confidential** | **Resource-Intensive**, Requires Infrastructure |
| **Contract Referral (Conditional)** | Patient Agrees to Notify Within Timeframe (e.g., 1-2 Weeks), Else Provider Does | **Balances Autonomy & Assurance** | **Complex, Requires Follow-Up** |
| **Expedited Partner Therapy (EPT)** | **Patient Delivers Medication/Prescription to Partners** (No Clinical Assessment) | **Rapid, High Coverage**, Overcomes Barriers | **Legal Barriers**, Allergy/Adverse Event Risk, No Clinical Assessment |

### PN Effectiveness & Coverage

| Strategy | Partner Treatment Rate | Resources Required |
|----------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **Patient Referral** | 30-50% | Low |
| **Provider Referral** | 70-90% | High (Staff, Database) |
| **Contract Referral** | 50-70% | Moderate |
| **EPT** | **70-90%** (Where Legal) | Moderate (Medication Supply) |

> **Evidence**: **Provider Referral + EPT = Highest Partner Treatment Rates**; **Combination Approaches Optimal**

### Lookback Periods (Timeframe for Partner Notification)

| Infection | Lookback Period |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Gonorrhoea / Chlamydia / Trichomonas** | **60 Days** Prior to Symptom Onset/Diagnosis |
| **Syphilis (Primary)** | **3 Months + Duration of Symptoms** |
| **Syphilis (Secondary)** | **6 Months + Duration of Symptoms** |
| **Syphilis (Early Latent <1 Year)** | **1 Year** |
| **Syphilis (Late Latent/Unknown)** | **Long-Term Partners (No Fixed Limit)** |
| **HSV / HPV** | **60 Days** (If Symptomatic Episode) |
| **Hepatitis B / C** | **6 Months** (Sexual Partners) |
| **Mpox** | **21 Days** Prior to Symptom Onset |
| **HIV** | **Since Last Negative Test or Indefinite** (All Sexual/Needle-Sharing Partners) |

### EPT (Expedited Partner Therapy) — Current Status

| Infection | EPT Regimen | Legal Status (UK/US/WHO) |
|-----------|-------------|--------------------------|
| **Gonorrhoea** | **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Azithromycin 1g PO** (Requires Injection) | **Complex** (Injection Required); **Provider Referral Preferred** |
| **Chlamydia** | **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7d** or **Azithromycin 1g Stat** | **Legal in Many Jurisdictions** (Oral, Simple) |
| **Trichomoniasis** | **Metronidazole 2g Stat** / **Tinidazole 2g Stat** | **Legal Where Permitted** |
| **Syphilis** | **Not Suitable for EPT** (Injection, Staging, CSF, Follow-Up Required) | **Not Recommended** |
| **Mpox** | **MVA-BN Vaccine (2 Doses) + Tecovirimat if High-Risk** | **Not Standard EPT** (Vaccine = PrEP/PEP) |

## 7.3 STI Vaccination Programmes

### HPV Vaccination Programme

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Vaccine** | **9-Valent (Gardasil 9): 6, 11, 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58** |
| **Target Population** | **Girls (Primary): Age 9-14** (Pre-Sexual Debut); **Boys: Increasingly Included** |
| **Schedule** | **<15 Years: 2 Doses (0, 6-12 Months)**; **≥15 Years: 3 Doses (0, 2, 6 Months)** |
| **Catch-Up** | **Females ≤26 Years, Males ≤21 Years** (MSM/Immunocompromised ≤26 Years) |
| **Shared Clinical Decision** | **Ages 27-45 Years** (Individual Benefit Assessment) |
| **HIV/Immunocompromised** | **3 Doses (0, 2, 6 Months) Regardless of Age** |
| **Delivery Platform** | **School-Based (Primary)** + **Primary Care/Catch-Up Clinics** |
| **Impact Targets (WHO 2030)** | **90% Coverage (Girls 15y)**; **Screening 70% (35y & 45y)**; **Treatment 90% CIN** |

### Hepatitis B Vaccination Programme

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Universal Infant** | **Birth Dose (Within 24h) + 1, 6 Months** (or 6, 10, 14 Weeks EPI) |
| **Adult Risk Groups** | **MSM, Sex Workers, PWID, HIV, CKD, Healthcare Workers, Prisoners, Partners of HBsAg+, Travelers to Endemic Areas** |
| **Schedule** | **0, 1, 6 Months** (or Accelerated 0, 1, 2, 12 Months) |
| **Non-Responders (Anti-HBs <10 mIU/mL)** | **Repeat Series (Double Dose/Accelerated)** → **Recheck Anti-HBs 1-2 Months Post** |
| **Pregnancy** | **Screen All (HBsAg)**; **Infant: HBIG + Vaccine (Birth) if Mother HBsAg+** |
| **Impact Target** | **<0.1% HBsAg in Children <5 Years (Elimination)** |

### Mpox Vaccination Programme

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Vaccine** | **MVA-BN (JYNNEOS/IMVANEX/IMVAMUNE) — Non-Replicating** |
| **Schedule** | **2 Doses (28 Days Apart)**; **Booster if Ongoing Risk** |
| **Priority Groups (PrEP)** | **MSM (High Risk: Multiple Partners, PrEP Users, Recent STIs)**, **Sex Workers**, **Lab Workers (Orthopoxvirus)**, **Healthcare Workers (High-Risk Exposure)** |
| **PEP** | **MVA-BN ≤4 Days Post-Exposure (Up to 14 Days) + Tecovirimat if High-Risk Individual** |
| **Special Populations** | **HIV (Safe, 2 Doses), Pregnancy (Safe), Immunocompromised (Safe); ACAM2000 Contraindicated** |

### Hepatitis A Vaccination (Relevant to MSM/Travel)

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Risk Groups** | **MSM, PWID, Travellers to Endemic Areas, Chronic Liver Disease, Occupational** |
| **Schedule** | **2 Doses (0, 6-12 Months)**; **Combined Hep A+B (Twinrix): 0, 1, 6 Months** |
| **Co-administration** | **Safe with All Other Vaccines** |

## 7.4 Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) in STIs — Control Strategies

### Gonorrhoea AMR — Global Threat (WHO Priority 2)

| Antimicrobial | Resistance Mechanism | Global Status (2023) | Control Strategy |
|---------------|---------------------|----------------------|------------------|
| **Ciprofloxacin** | **gyrA/parC Mutations** | **>50% Global Resistance** | **Do Not Use Empirically** |
| **Azithromycin** | **23S rRNA Mutations, Efflux (MtrCDE)** | **Rising (MIC Creep)** | **Adjunct Only (Not Monotherapy)** |
| **Cefixime** | **penA Mosaic, porB** | **Declining Susceptibility** | **Not Preferred for Pharyngeal** |
| **Ceftriaxone** | **penA Mosaic (XXXIV, XIX), porB, mtrR** | **Emerging (MIC >0.125 mg/L)** | **Dual Therapy, Higher Dose (1g IM), Surveillance** |
| **Spectinomycin** | **rpsE Mutation** | **Low (Rarely Used)** | **Alternative (If Available)** |

### WHO GASP (Gonococcal Antimicrobial Surveillance Programme)

| Component | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Sentinel Sites** | **Representative Geographic/Population Coverage** |
| **Culture + AST** | **Ceftriaxone, Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin, Cefixime, Spectinomycin** |
| **Molecular Surveillance** | **penA, gyrA/parC, 23S rRNA, mtrR, porB** (Track Resistance Determinants) |
| **Data Sharing** | **WHO GASP Database, National Reports, Regional Networks** |
| **Early Warning** | **Ceftriaxone MIC >0.06 mg/L → Enhanced Surveillance** |

### M. genitalium AMR

| Resistance | Mechanism | Management |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| **Macrolide (Azithromycin)** | **23S rRNA A2058/2059 Mutations** | **Moxifloxacin 400mg OD × 7-14d** (Preferred if Resistance Confirmed/High Prevalence) |
| **Fluoroquinolone (Moxifloxacin)** | **parC (S83I), gyrA (S83I) Mutations** | **Pristinamycin 1g QID × 10-14d** (If Available) |
| **Dual Resistance (Macrolide + Fluoroquinolone)** | **Both Mechanisms** | **Pristinamycin** (If Available) → **Specialist Referral** |

### AMR Control Strategies (WHO Global Action Plan)

| Strategy | Actions |
|----------|---------|
| **Surveillance** | **WHO GASP Expansion**, Molecular Tracking, Data Sharing |
| **Antimicrobial Stewardship** | **Dual Therapy (GC), Resistance-Guided Therapy (MG), Avoid Monotherapy** |
| **Treatment Guidelines** | **Regular Updates Based on AMR Data**, Resistance-Guided Algorithms |
| **New Drug Development** | **Zoliflodacin (Spiropyrimidinetrione), Gepotidacin (Topoisomerase Inhibitor)** — **Phase 3 Trials** |
| **Diagnostics** | **Rapid POC AMR Testing (NAAT + Resistance Markers)** |
| **Vaccines** | **Gonorrhoea Vaccine Candidates (NgOMP, rPorB, etc.) — Phase 1/2** |

## 7.5 STI Prevention & Control Interventions

### Biomedical Interventions

| Intervention | Effectiveness | Target Population |
|--------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Male Condoms (Latex/Polyurethane)** | **85-98% (Consistent Correct Use)** | **All Sexually Active** |
| **Female Condoms** | **79-95%** | **Women (Female-Controlled)** |
| **PrEP (TDF/FTC Daily / Event-Driven / CAB-LA)** | **>99% HIV Prevention** | **High-Risk (MSM, Serodiscordant, Sex Workers, PWID)** |
| **Doxy-PEP (200mg ≤72h Post-Sex)** | **GC ↓55%, CT ↓88%, Syphilis ↓87%** (MSM/Trans Women on PrEP/HIV) | **MSM/Trans Women on PrEP/HIV** |
| **HPV Vaccine (9vHPV)** | **>90% Vaccine-Type HPV/CIN/Warts** | **Girls <15y (2-Dose), Catch-Up ≤26y** |
| **HBV Vaccine** | **>95% Protection** | **Universal Birth + Risk Adults** |
| **Mpox Vaccine (MVA-BN)** | **~85% Protection** | **High-Risk MSM, Sex Workers, Lab/HCW** |
| **Hepatitis A Vaccine** | **>95%** | **MSM, PWID, Travellers, Chronic Liver Disease** |

### Behavioural Interventions

| Intervention | Evidence | Delivery |
|--------------|----------|----------|
| **Comprehensive Sexuality Education (CSE)** | **Delays Sexual Debut, ↑ Condom Use, ↓ Partners** | **School-Based (Age-Appropriate), Community** |
| **Brief Risk Reduction Counselling** | **Modest STI Reduction** | **Clinical Settings (STI/Contraception/PrEP Visits)** |
| **Motivational Interviewing (MI)** | **Modest Behaviour Change** | **Trained Counsellors, Peer Educators** |
| **Peer Education/Navigation** | **High Acceptability in Key Populations** | **Peer-Led (MSM, Sex Workers, PWID, Transgender)** |
| **Digital Interventions (Apps, SMS, Web)** | **Modest, Scalable** | **Apps (PrEP Reminders, STI Testing Reminders, Partner Notification)** |

### Structural Interventions

| Intervention | Impact | Example |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| **Decriminalisation of Sex Work** | **↓ Violence, ↑ Condom Use, ↑ Health Access** | **New Zealand Model (Decriminalised 2003)** |
| **Stigma Reduction** | **↑ Testing, ↑ Treatment Adherence, ↓ Discrimination** | **Anti-Stigma Campaigns, Health Worker Training** |
| **Gender Equality / IPV Prevention** | **↓ Sexual Violence, ↑ Women's Autonomy** | **Legal reform, Community Mobilisation** |
| **Universal Health Coverage (UHC)** | **↑ Access to STI Services, ↓ Financial Barriers** | **Free STI Testing/Treatment, Free Vaccines** |
| **Harm Reduction (PWID)** | **↓ HIV/HCV Transmission** | **OAT (Methadone/Buprenorphine), Needle/Syringe Programmes** |

## 7.6 STI Service Delivery Models

### Service Delivery Models

| Model | Features | Advantages | Challenges |
|-------|----------|------------|------------|
| **Specialised STI/GUM Clinics** | **Expertise, Full Diagnostics, Partner Notification, Complex Cases** | **High Quality, Specialist Skills** | **Stigma, Access Barriers, Cost** |
| **Integrated STI/HIV/SRH** | **One-Stop Shop (HIV, STI, Contraception, Cervical Screen, PrEP, Gender-Affirming)** | **Efficiency, Person-Centred, Reduced Stigma, Retention** | **Complex Coordination, Staff Training** |
| **Primary Care / General Practice** | **Accessible, Familiar, Opportunistic Screening** | **High Access, Low Stigma** | **Variable Expertise, Time Constraints** |
| **Community / Outreach** | **Mobile Vans, Pop-Up, Peer-Led, Key Population-Focused** | **Reaches Marginalised, Low Threshold** | **Limited Diagnostics, Sustainability** |
| **Digital / Self-Sampling** | **Online Ordering, Home Self-Sampling (Vaginal/Rectal/Pharyngeal/Urine), Postal Return, Results by Text/App** | **High Acceptability, Privacy, Reaches Youth/MSM** | **Lab Capacity, Clinical Follow-Up Pathways, Digital Divide** |
| **Pharmacy-Led** | **EPT, Condoms, Vaccines, Self-Test Kits, Counselling** | **High Access, Extended Hours** | **Limited Clinical Assessment** |

### Digital Health Innovations

| Innovation | Application | Evidence |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| **Online STI Testing** | **Order Kit → Self-Sample → Post → Results by Text/Email** | **High Uptake (MSM, Youth), Equivalent Positivity to Clinic** |
| **Partner Notification Apps** | **Anonymous/Confidentia l Partner Notification via SMS/Email** | **Increases PN Completion, Reduces Stigma** |
| **PrEP/STI Reminder Apps** | **Medication Reminders, Appointment Reminders, Test Reminders** | **Improves Adherence, Retention** |
| **Telemedicine (Video/Phone)** | **STI Consultations, Results, Prescriptions** | **Non-Inferior for Uncomplicated STIs, High Satisfaction** |
| **AI Triage/Chatbots** | **Symptom Checker, Risk Assessment, Signposting** | **Early Stage, Potential for Triage** |

## 7.8 STI in Pregnancy & Neonatal Prevention

### Antenatal STI Screening (Universal)

| Test | Timing | Repeat |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Syphilis (RPR/VDRL + TPPA)** | **Booking (First Trimester)** | **28-32 Weeks + Delivery (High Risk/High Prevalence)** |
| **Hepatitis B (HBsAg)** | **Booking** | **If Negative, No Repeat (Unless Risk)** |
| **Hepatitis C (Anti-HCV)** | **Booking** | **If Risk Factors** |
| **HIV** | **Booking** | **28-32 Weeks + Delivery (High Prevalence)** |
| **Gonorrhoea/Chlamydia** | **If <25y or Risk Factors** | **28-32 Weeks (If Risk)** |
| **HSV** | **If Lesions/History** | **36 Weeks Onwards (Suppressive Therapy Decision)** |

### Neonatal Prevention Package

| STI | Intervention |
|-----|--------------|
| **Syphilis** | **Maternal BPC ≥30d Before Delivery** → **Infant: Observe (If Adequate) / Penicillin G IV 10d (If Inadequate/Uncertain)** |
| **Gonorrhoea** | **Infant: Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg IM/IV Single Dose (If Maternal GC+ / Untreated)** |
| **Chlamydia** | **Infant: Azithromycin 20mg/kg OD × 3d (If Maternal CT+ / Untreated)** |
| **HSV** | **Maternal Suppression (Acyclovir 400mg TDS 36w→Delivery)**; **Cesarean if Active Lesions/Prodrome at Labour** |
| **HBV** | **Infant: HBIG (100 IU) + HBV Vaccine (0, 1, 6 Months) Within 24h** |
| **HCV** | **No Proven Prevention**; **Breastfeeding Safe (If Nipples Intact)** |
| **HIV** | **Maternal ART (VL<50); Infant AZT ± NVP (4-6 Weeks); Cesarean if VL>1000; No Breastfeeding (Resource-Dependent)** |
