1️⃣ Hepatitis B (HBV)
Epidemiology & Sexual Transmission
ParameterDetail
-------------------
**Global Prevalence****296 Million Chronic** (2019); **1.5 Million New Infections/Year**
**Sexual Transmission Efficiency****High** (100x HIV, 10x HCV); **MSM, Multiple Partners, Sex Workers = High Risk**
**Other Routes**Perinatal (Vertical), Percutaneous (Needles, Tattoo, medical), Horizontal (Household)
**Incubation****60-150 Days** (Average 90 Days)
HBV Serology — Interpretation Masterclass
MarkerSignificanceInterpretation
--------------------------------------
**HBsAg****Surface Antigen****Current Infection (Acute or Chronic)**; **Positive >6 Months = Chronic**
**Anti-HBs****Surface Antibody****Immunity** (Vaccination or Resolved Infection); **>10 mIU/mL = Protected**
**Anti-HBc Total****Core Antibody (IgG+IgM)****Past or Current Infection** (Never Positive from Vaccine Alone)
**Anti-HBc IgM****Core IgM Antibody****Acute/Recent Infection** (<6 Months); **Window Period Marker**
**HBeAg****e Antigen****High Replicative State**, High Infectivity
**Anti-HBe****e Antibody****Lower Replication**, Seroconversion (Usually Good Prognosis)
**HBV DNA****Viral Load****Quantitative Replication**; **Guides Treatment, Monitors Response**

3️⃣ Hepatitis B — Treatment (Nucleos(t)ide Analogues)
First-Line NUCs (Potent, High Barrier to Resistance)

Treatment Duration & Stopping Rules
ScenarioDurationStopping Criteria
---------------------------------------
**HBeAg +ve Chronic Hepatitis****Until HBeAg Seroconversion + 12 Months Consolidation****HBeAg Seroconversion + Anti-HBe + HBV DNA Undetectable × 12 Months**
**HBeAg -ve Chronic Hepatitis****Long-Term (Indefinite)****HBsAg Loss (Rare)**; **Consider Stop if HBsAg Loss + 12 Months**
**Cirrhosis****Lifelong****Do Not Stop** (Risk of Decompensation)
**Pregnancy (TDF/TAF/ETV)****Throughout Pregnancy + 3-12 Months Postpartum****Prevent Vertical Transmission**
3️⃣ Hepatitis B — Special Situations
HBV in Pregnancy
AspectManagement
--------------------
**Screening****Universal HBsAg at Booking** (All Pregnant Women)
**High Viral Load (>200,000 IU/mL)****TAF/TDF/ETV from 28-32 Weeks → 3-12 Months Postpartum** (Prevent Vertical Transmission)
**HBsAg +ve Mother****Infant: HBIG (100 IU IM) + HBV Vaccine (0, 1, 6 Months) Within 24 Hours of Birth**
**Breastfeeding****Safe** (If Infant Received HBIG + Vaccine)
**Antivirals in Pregnancy****TAF/TDF/ETV Safe** (Preferred: TAF/TDF); **Start 28-32 Weeks if HBV DNA >200,000 IU/mL**
HBV/HCV Co-infection with HIV
AspectManagement
--------------------
**ART Regimen****TDF/TAF + 3TC/FTC (Backbone Covers HBV)**
**HBV Mono-infected on NUCs****Continue NUCs** (Do Not Stop — Risk of Flare)
**Monitoring****HBV DNA, ALT q3-6 Months**; **HBsAg q6-12 Months**
**Hepatitis Flare****ART Initiation (IRIS)** or **NUC Stoppage** — **Manage Expectantly/Restart NUC**

4️⃣ Hepatitis C (HCV)
Epidemiology & Sexual Transmission
ParameterDetail
-------------------
**Global Prevalence****58 Million Chronic** (2019); **1.5 Million New Infections/Year**
**Sexual Transmission Efficiency****Low** (Overall); **Higher in MSM (HIV+, Chemsex, Rough Sex, Mucosal Trauma)**
**Primary Route****Percutaneous (PWID, Unsafe Medical, Transfusion Pre-1992)**
**Incubation****2 Weeks – 6 Months** (Average 6-9 Weeks)

**Cirrhosis****15-30% over 20-30 Years**Accelerated by Alcohol, HIV, Age, Male, Obesity
**HCC****1-4%/Year (Cirrhosis)**Surveillance Required

5️⃣ HCV — Special Situations
HCV in Pregnancy
AspectManagement
--------------------
**Screening****Universal Anti-HCV at Booking** (Recommended by WHO/CDC)
**DAA Therapy****Contraindicated in Pregnancy** (No Safety Data); **Defer Until Postpartum**
**Vertical Transmission****~5-6%** (Higher if HIV Co-infection, High Viral Load); **No Proven Intervention**
**Breastfeeding****Safe** (If Nipples Intact); **Avoid if Cracked/Bleeding Nipples**
**Infant Testing****HCV RNA at 2-6 Months** (Anti-HCV Passive Maternal Transfer); **Confirm Chronic at 18 Months**
HCV/HIV Co-infection
AspectManagement
--------------------
**DAA Therapy****Same Regimens/Durations as Mono-Infected** (Check Drug Interactions with ART)
**Key Interactions****Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir ↔ Rifampicin (Contraindicated); Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir ↔ Certain ART (Check Liverpool Database)**
**ART Regimen****Integrase Inhibitor Based Preferred** (DTG, BIC, RAL) — Fewest DAA Interactions
**Monitoring****SVR12 = Cure**; **HCV Reinfection Risk Higher (Continue Risk Reduction)**
HCV in Renal Impairment
eGFRPreferred Regimen
-------------------------
**≥30**Any Pan-Genotypic
**<30 / Dialysis****Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir** (Renal Safe — No Dose Adjustment)
5️⃣ HCV Sexual Transmission & Prevention
AspectDetail
----------------
**Sexual Transmission****Low Overall**; **Higher: MSM (HIV+, Chemsex, Group Sex, Mucosal Trauma), Multiple Partners, Co-existing STIs**
**Prevention****Condoms (Reduce Risk)**, **Treat HCV (SVR = No Transmission)**, **Harm Reduction (PWID)**
**PEP for HCV****No Standard PEP** (No Proven Post-Exposure Antiviral); **Early Detection → Early Treatment**
**Reinfection****Possible After SVR** (Continued Risk Behaviours → Reinfection Rate 1-5%/Year)
3 ⚡ FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
TopicKey Points
-------------------
**HBV Serology****HBsAg = Current Infection; Anti-HBs = Immunity; Anti-HBc = Past/Current; Anti-HBc IgM = Acute; HBeAg = High Replication; HBV DNA = Quantification**
**HBV Phases**Immune Tolerant (HBeAg+, High DNA, Normal ALT) → Immune Active (HBeAg+, High DNA, ↑ALT) → Inactive Carrier (HBeAg-, Low DNA, Normal ALT) → HBeAg-Negative CHB (HBeAg-, ↑DNA, ↑ALT)
**HBV Treatment****TAF > TDF > ETV** (High Barrier); **HBeAg+ → Seroconversion + 12m Consolidation**; **HBeAg- → Long-Term/Indefinite**; **Cirrhosis = Lifelong**
**HBV Vaccination****Birth, 1, 6m (Infants)**; **Risk Adults: 0,1,6m**; **PEP: HBIG + Vaccine (≤24h/7d)**
**HCV Natural History****15-45% Spontaneous Clearance; 55-85% Chronic → Cirrhosis (15-30% in 20-30y) → HCC**
**HCV Diagnosis****Anti-HCV + HCV RNA (Quantitative PCR) = Chronic**; **Genotype Optional (Pan-Genotypic DAAs)**
**HCV DAA Therapy****Pan-Genotypic: SOF/VEL 12w, GLE/PIB 8w (Non-Cirrhotics), SOF/VEL/VOX 12w (Retreatment)**; **SVR12 = Cure (>95%)**
**HCV in Pregnancy****Screen at Booking; DAA Contraindicated; Defer to Postpartum; Vertical Transmission ~5-6%**
**HBV/HCV in HIV****TDF/TAF+3TC/FTC Covers HBV**; **HCV DAA Same Regimens (Check Interactions)**
**PEP****HBV: HBIG + Vaccine (≤24h); HCV: No PEP (Early Detection/Treatment)**
**Vaccination****HBV Universal (Birth, 1, 6m); Risk Adults 0,1,6m; No HCV Vaccine**

5 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**"HBV and HCV both have vaccines"****NO.** **HBV = Vaccine Available**; **HCV = NO Vaccine**
**"HBeAg +ve = Always Treat"****NO.** **Immune Tolerant (HBeAg+, High DNA, Normal ALT) = Monitor, NOT Treat**
**"HCV Genotype Matters for Current DAAs"****NO.** **Pan-Genotypic DAAs (GLE/PIB, SOF/VEL) Cover All Genotypes 1-6**; **Genotyping Optional**
**"HCV Vaccine Exists"****NO.** **NO HCV Vaccine** (High Genetic Diversity); **Only HBV Vaccine**
**"HCV Spontaneous Clearance = Immunity"****NO.** **Reinfection Possible** (No Protective Immunity); **Risk Reduction Still Needed After SVR**
**"HBV Vaccine = Lifelong Immunity"****Mostly Yes**; **Booster Not Routine**; **Anti-HBs >10 = Protected**; **Non-Responders: Repeat Series**
**"HCV PEP = Give DAAs"****NO.** **No HCV PEP**; **Early Detection (RNA at 4-12 Weeks) → Early Treatment**
**"All Chronic HBV Need Treatment"****NO.** **Immune Tolerant & Inactive Carrier = Monitor Only**
**"HCV Genotype 3 = Hardest to Treat"****Historical**; **Current Pan-Genotypic DAAs (GLE/PIB, SOF/VEL) Achieve >95% SVR in GT3**
**"HBV = Sexual, HCV = Blood Only"****NO.** **HBV = Highly Sexual (100x HIV)**; **HCV = Low Sexual Overall, BUT Higher in MSM (HIV+, Chemsex, Trauma)**

  • HBV Serology: HBsAg = Current Infection; Anti-HBs = Immunity; Anti-HBc = Past/Current; Anti-HBc IgM = Acute; HBeAg = High Replication; HBV DNA = Quantification
  • HBV Phases: Immune Tolerant (HBeAg+, High DNA, Normal ALT) → NO TREATMENT; Immune Active (HBeAg+, ↑ALT, High DNA) → TREAT; Inactive Carrier (HBeAg-, Low DNA, Normal ALT) → MONITOR; HBeAg-Negative CHB (HBeAg-, ↑DNA >2000, ↑ALT) → TREAT
  • HBV Treatment: TAF > TDF > ETV (High Barrier); HBeAg+ → Seroconversion + 12m Consolidation; HBeAg- → Long-Term/Indefinite; Cirrhosis = LIFELONG
  • HBV Vaccination: Universal (Birth, 1, 6m); Risk Adults (0,1,6m); PEP: HBIG + Vaccine (≤24h/7d)
  • HCV Natural History: 15-45% Spontaneous Clearance; 55-85% Chronic → Cirrhosis (15-30% in 20-30y) → HCC
  • HCV Diagnosis: Anti-HCV + HCV RNA (Positive) = Chronic Infection; Genotype Optional (Pan-Genotypic DAAs)
  • HCV DAA Therapy: Pan-Genotypic: SOF/VEL 12w (All); GLE/PIB 8w (Non-Cirrhotic) / 12w (Cirrhotic); SOF/VEL/VOX 12w (Retreatment); SVR12 = CURE (>95%)
  • HCV in Pregnancy: Screen at Booking; DAA Contraindicated → Defer Postpartum; Vertical Transmission ~5-6%; Breastfeeding Safe (If Nipples Intact)
  • HBV/HCV in HIV: HBV: TDF/TAF+3TC/FTC in ART (Covers HBV); Continue NUCs; HCV: Same DAA Regimens (Check Drug Interactions)
  • PEP: HBV: HBIG + Vaccine (Within 24h, Up to 7 Days); HCV: No PEP (Early RNA Testing → Early Treatment)
  • Sexual Transmission: HBV = Highly Efficient (100x HIV); HCV = Low Overall, Higher in MSM (HIV+, Chemsex, Trauma)
11 ❓ MCQs (10)
  1. HBV serology — marker of acute infection?

A. HBsAg B. Anti-HBc IgM C. Anti-HBs D. HBeAg Answer: B. Anti-HBc IgM = Acute/Recent Infection.

  1. HBV phase — immune tolerant phase characteristics?

A. HBeAg-, Low DNA, Normal ALT B. HBeAg+, High DNA, Normal ALT C. HBeAg-, High DNA, Elevated ALT D. HBsAg-, Anti-HBs+ Answer: B. Immune Tolerant = HBeAg+, High DNA (>10⁷), Normal ALT, No Treatment.

  1. First-line HBV antiviral — preferred agent?

A. Lamivudine B. Tenofovir Alafenamide (TAF) C. Adefovir D. Telbivudine Answer: B. TAF > TDF > ETV (Highest Barrier, Renal/Bone Safety).

  1. HCV diagnosis — confirmatory test for chronic infection?

A. Anti-HCV B. HCV RNA (Quantitative PCR) C. Anti-HCV IgM D. Liver Biopsy Answer: B. HCV RNA (Quantitative PCR) Positive = Chronic Infection.

  1. Pan-genotypic DAA regimen for treatment-naive non-cirrhotic?

A. SOF/VEL 8 Weeks B. GLE/PIB 8 Weeks (Preferred) C. SOF/VEL/VOX 12 Weeks D. SOF/VEL 24 Weeks Answer: B. GLE/PIB 8 Weeks (Shortest, Renal Safe, Pan-Genotypic).

  1. HCV SVR definition?

A. RNA Negative at End of Treatment B. RNA Negative at 12 Weeks Post-Treatment (SVR12) C. RNA Negative at 24 Weeks D. Antibody Negative Answer: B. SVR12 = HCV RNA Undetectable 12 Weeks Post-Treatment = Cure (>95%).

  1. HCV in pregnancy — DAA therapy?

A. Safe, Recommended B. Contraindicated → Defer Until Postpartum C. Only 2nd Trimester D. Only Genotype 1 Answer: B. DAA Contraindicated in Pregnancy → Defer Until Postpartum.

  1. HBV in pregnancy — infant prophylaxis?

A. HBV Vaccine Only B. HBIG (100 IU) + HBV Vaccine Within 24 Hours of Birth C. HBIG Only D. No Prophylaxis Needed Answer: B. HBIG + HBV Vaccine Within 24 Hours of Birth (Standard of Care).

  1. HBV/HCV PEP for needlestick from HBsAg+ source — unvaccinated HCW?

A. HBV Vaccine Only B. HBIG (0.06 mL/kg IM) + HBV Vaccine (0, 1, 6 Months) Within 24h C. HBIG Only D. No PEP Needed Answer: B. HBIG + HBV Vaccine Series (Within 24h Ideally, Up to 7 Days).

  1. Pan-genotypic DAA for treatment-experienced (retreatment)?

A. SOF/VEL 12 Weeks B. GLE/PIB 8 Weeks C. SOF/VEL/VOX 12 Weeks D. SOF/VEL 24 Weeks Answer: C. SOF/VEL/VOX (Vosevi) 12 Weeks = Retreatment Regimen.

A. SOF/VEL 12 Weeks B. GLE/PIB 8 Weeks C. SOF/VEL/VOX 12 Weeks D. SOF/VEL 24 Weeks Answer: B. GLE/PIB 8 Weeks (Shortest, Renal Safe, Pan-Genotypic).
