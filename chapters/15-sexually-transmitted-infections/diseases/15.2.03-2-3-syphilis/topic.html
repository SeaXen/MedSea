1️⃣ Syphilis Staging — Clinical Features
Primary Syphilis
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Incubation****10-90 Days** (Median 21 Days)
**Chancre****Painless**, **Indurated**, **Clean Base**, **Well-Defined Margins**, **Single** (Multiple in HIV), **Heals Spontaneously in 3-6 Weeks**
**Lymphadenopathy****Regional**, **Non-Tender**, **Rubbery**, **Non-Suppurative**
**Serology****RPR/VDRL Negative Early** (Seroconversion 1-4 Weeks Post-Chancre); **TPPA/FTA-ABS Positive Earlier**
**Duration****3-6 Weeks** Untreated → Heals Spontaneously
Secondary Syphilis
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Timing****4-10 Weeks** Post-Chancre (Overlap Possible)
**Rash****Maculopapular**, **Palms & Soles** (Pathognomonic), **Trunk, Extremities, Face**, **Non-Pruritic**, **Copper-Coloured**
**Mucous Membranes****Condylomata Lata** (Moist, Flat, Infectious Papules — Perineum/Anal), **Mucous Patches** (Oral/Genital), **Snail-Track Ulcers**
**Systemic****Generalised Lymphadenopathy** (Rubbery, Non-Tender), Fever, Malaise, Headache, Pharyngitis, Hepatosplenomegaly, Iritis, Nephritis
**Other****Alopecia** (Moth-Eaten), **Periostitis**, **Glomerulonephritis**
**Duration****2-6 Weeks** (Waxing/Waning), Resolution → Latent
Latent Syphilis
StageDefinitionSerologyInfectivityDuration
----------------------------------------------------
**Early Latent****<1 Year** Post-Infection (or <2 Years CDC)**RPR/VDRL High Titre** (≥1:16), TPPA+**Infectious** (Early Relapse to Secondary)Variable
**Late Latent****>1 Year** Post-Infection (or >2 Years CDC)**RPR/VDRL Low/Declining**, TPPA+**Non-Infectious** (Sexual)Years-Decades
Key Distinction: Early Latent = <1 Year (or <2 Years CDC); Late Latent = >1 Year (Duration Unknown → Assume Late Latent)
Tertiary Syphilis (Late Manifestations)
TypeFeaturesTiming
------------------------
**Gummatous****Gummas** (Granulomatous, Necrotic Lesions) — Skin (Ulcers), Bone (Osteitis), Liver (Hepatitis), Testes, Brain, Heart; Slowly Progressive, Scarring**3-30 Years**
**Cardiovascular****Aortitis** (Ascending Aorta), **Aortic Regurgitation**, **Aortic Aneurysm** (Saccular, Ascending), **Coronary Ostial Stenosis** → Angina/MI**10-30 Years**
**Neurosyphilis**See Below**Any Time** (Can Occur Early)
Neurosyphilis (Neurological Syphilis)
SyndromeFeaturesCSF Findings
---------------------------------
**Asymptomatic**Normal Neurological Exam**CSF Abnormalities** (↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, +VDRL)
**Meningeal**Headache, Nuchal Rigidity, Cranial Nerve Palsies (III, VI, VII), Photophobia↑ Lymphocytes, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve**
**Meningovascular****Stroke (Ischaemic) in Young**, Cranial Nerve Palsies, Seizures, Hemiparesis, Aphasia↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve**
**General Paresis (Dementia Paralytica)****Personality Change**, **Cognitive Decline**, **Memory Loss**, **Delusions**, **Apathy**, **Argyll Robertson Pupils** (Accommodate, Not React to Light)↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve**
**Tabes Dorsalis****Lightning Pains** (Lancinating Leg Pains), **Sensory Ataxia**, **Loss of Proprioception/Vibration**, **Argyll Robertson Pupils**, **Absent Reflexes**, **Charcot Joints**↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve**
**Ocular Syphilis**Uveitis, Optic Neuritis, Retinitis, Interstitial Keratitis↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve**
**Otic Syphilis**Sensorineural Hearing Loss, Tinnitus, Vertigo↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve**
Key: Neurosyphilis Can Occur at ANY STAGE (Even Primary/Secondary); CSF Examination Mandatory if Neurological Signs, HIV+, Treatment Failure, Late Latent/Tertiary
1️⃣ Congenital Syphilis
Transmission & Risk
FactorRisk
--------------
**Transplacental****Any Stage of Pregnancy** (Highest 2nd/3rd Trimester)
**Maternal Stage**Primary/Secondary: **70-100%**; Early Latent: **40%**, Late Latent: **10%**; Treated: **<1-2%** if Adequate Treatment ≥30 Days Before Delivery
**Timing****No Barrier** After 16-18 Weeks Gestation; Treponemes Cross Placenta Freely
Clinical Classification
CategoryFeatures
-------------------
**Early Congenital (<2 Years)****Snuffles** (Bloody, Mucopurulent Rhinitis), **Rash** (Maculopapular, Palms/Soles, Vesiculobullous), **Hepatosplenomegaly**, **Jaundice**, **Pneumonitis**, **Osteochondritis** (Symmetric Long Bone Lesions), **Pseudoparalysis** (Parrot's — Painful Limb Movement), **Lymphadenopathy**, **Nephrotic Syndrome**, **Thrombocytopenia**
**Late Congenital (>2 Years)****Hutchinson's Triad**: **Hutchinson's Teeth** (Notched, Peg-Shaped Incisors), **Interstitial Keratitis**, **8th Nerve Deafness**; **Other**: Saddle Nose, Saber Shins, Clutton's Joints (Knee Effusions), Mulberry Molars, Rhagades

2️⃣ Syphilis Serology — Interpretation Mastery
Test Types
CategoryTestsPrincipleCharacteristics
---------------------------------------------
**Non-Treponemal (NTT)****RPR** (Rapid Plasma Reagin), **VDRL** (Venereal Disease Research Lab)Detect **Anti-Cardiolipin (Lipoidal) Antibodies****Quantitative (Titre)**, **Sensitive for Active Disease**, **Correlates with Activity**, **Prozone Phenomenon** (False -ve at High Titre), **Biological False Positives (BFP)** (Autoimmune, Viral, Pregnancy, Age)
**Treponemal (TT)****TPPA** (Treponema pallidum Particle Agglutination), **FTA-ABS** (Fluorescent Treponemal Antibody Absorption), **EIA/CIA** (Enzyme/Chemiluminescence Immunoassay), **Western Blot**Detect **Anti-T. pallidum Specific Antibodies****Qualitative (Positive/Negative)**, **Highly Specific**, **Lifelong Positivity** (Even After Cure), **Does Not Correlate with Activity**
Testing Algorithms
AlgorithmSequenceAdvantage
--------------------------------
**Traditional****NTT (RPR/VDRL) → If Positive → TT (TPPA/FTA-ABS)**Cheaper, Detects Active Disease First, Quantifies Activity
**Reverse (CDC/WHO Recommended)****TT (EIA/CIA/TPPA) → If Positive → NTT (RPR/VDRL) for Titres**Higher Sensitivity (Detects Early Primary, Latent, Treated), Automated, High-Throughput
Serology Interpretation Matrix
Clinical StageNTT (RPR/VDRL)TT (TPPA/FTA-ABS)Notes
----------------------------------------------------------
**Primary****Negative → Positive (Seroconversion 1-4w)****Positive Early****TT Positive Before NTT**; Repeat NTT if Clinical Suspicion
**Secondary****High Titre** (1:64 to 1:1024)**Positive**Peak NTT Activity
**Early Latent****Positive (Moderate-High)****Positive**High Relapse Risk
**Late Latent****Low/Declining Titre** (<1:16) or **Negative****Positive**NTT May Be Negative
**Tertiary (Gumma/CV)****Often Negative****Positive**NTT Insensitive in Late Disease
**Neurosyphilis****CSF-VDRL +ve** (Specific)CSF TPPA/FTA-ABS**CSF-VDRL = Diagnostic** (High Specificity, Low Sensitivity)
**Congenital****Neonatal Titre ≥4x Maternal****Neonatal Positive** (Maternal IgG Transfer = Positive)**IgM FTA-ABS / PCR** = Active Infection; **Maternal IgG** = Passive Transfer
**Treated (Cured)****Titre Decline ≥4-Fold (2 Dilutions)****Remains Positive (Lifelong)****Serofast** (Persistent Low Titre) Common; **NTT Negative = Cure**
**Re-infection****Titre Rise ≥4-Fold (2 Dilutions)****Remains Positive**Distinguished from Serofast
Key Concepts: - Seroconversion = NTT Negative → Positive - Serofast = Persistent Low NTT Titre After Treatment (<1:4) — Not Treatment Failure - Prozone Phenomenon = False Negative NTT at Very High Titre → Dilute Serum - Biological False Positive (BFP) = Positive NTT, Negative TT — Autoimmune, Viral, Pregnancy, Age
Treatment Response & Follow-Up
ParameterExpected Response
------------------------------
**NTT Titre (RPR/VDRL)****≥4-Fold Decline (2 Dilutions)** by **6 Months (1°/2°)**, **12-24 Months (Latent/Tertiary)**
**Serofast**Persistent Low Titre (<1:4) After Adequate Treatment — **Not Failure**
**Treatment Failure****Titre Rise ≥4-Fold** (Re-infection/Failure) or **No Decline by 6-12 Months**
**Re-infection****Titre Rise ≥4-Fold** (After Prior Decline) — Treat Again

2️⃣ Treatment — Stage-Specific Penicillin Regimens
Penicillin G — The Only Proven Curative Agent
StageRegimenDose & RouteDurationAlternative (Penicillin Allergy)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
**Primary / Secondary / Early Latent****Benzathine Penicillin G****2.4 Million Units IM Single Dose****Single Dose****Desensitise → Penicillin** (Preferred); **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 14d** (Non-Pregnant); **Ceftriaxone 1g IM/IV Daily × 10-14d** (Limited Data)
**Late Latent / Tertiary (Gummatous, Cardiovascular)****Benzathine Penicillin G****2.4 Million Units IM Weekly × 3 Doses** (Total 7.2 MU)**3 Weekly Doses****Desensitise → Penicillin**; **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 28d** (Non-Pregnant); **Ceftriaxone 1-2g IV Daily × 14d**
**Neurosyphilis (All Forms)****Aqueous Penicillin G IV****18-24 Million Units/Day** (3-4 MU q4h) **OR 18-24 MU Continuous Infusion****10-14 Days****Desensitise → Penicillin G IV**; **Ceftriaxone 2g IV Daily × 14d** (Alternative)
**Congenital Syphilis****Aqueous Penicillin G IV****50,000 Units/kg/dose q12h (First 7 Days) → q8h (Day 8-10)****10 Days Total****Desensitise → Penicillin** (Only Proven); **Procaine Penicillin 50,000 U/kg IM Daily × 10d** (If IV Access Difficult)
Critical: Penicillin G is the ONLY PROVEN Curative Agent for Syphilis in Pregnancy and Neurosyphilis; No Oral Alternatives Proven for Neurosyphilis/Congenital
Penicillin Allergy Management
ApproachDetail
------------------
**Desensitisation****Preferred for ALL Stages** (Especially Pregnancy, Neurosyphilis, Congenital); **Oral/IV Protocols** (Gradual Dose Escalation Over Hours); **Specialist Allergist/ID Supervision**
**Alternative (Non-Pregnant, Non-Neurosyphilis)****Doxycycline 100mg BD** — Primary/Secondary/Early Latent: **14 Days**; Late Latent/Tertiary: **28 Days**
**Alternative (Pregnancy)****NO PROVEN ORAL ALTERNATIVE** — **Desensitisation Mandatory**
**Ceftriaxone****1-2g IV/IM Daily × 10-14 Days** (Limited Data, Not 1st Line); **Not for Neurosyphilis/Congenital as 1st Choice**
**Azithromycin****NOT RECOMMENDED** (Resistance Documented, Treatment Failures)
Jarisch-Herxheimer Reaction (JHR)
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Mechanism****Massive Treponemal Lysis** → Release of Lipoproteins → Cytokine Storm (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-8)
**Incidence****~50% Secondary**, 10-25% Primary, <10% Latent; **Higher in HIV+**
**Timing****2-12 Hours Post-Treatment** (Peak 4-6h), Resolves in 12-24h
**Symptoms**Fever, Chills, Rigors, Headache, Myalgia, **Worsening of Rash**, Hypotension, Tachycardia
**Management****Supportive**: Antipyretics (Paracetamol), Fluids, **Do NOT Delay/Stop Penicillin**; **Prednisolone Prophylaxis** (1mg/kg Pre-Treatment) Optional in Severe Cases
**Differential**Anaphylaxis (Urticaria, Angioedema, Wheeze, Stridor — Immediate <1h) — **Treat as Anaphylaxis**

CSF Diagnostic Criteria for Neurosyphilis
FindingSignificance
----------------------

4️⃣ Special Situations & Complications
Syphilis in HIV Infection
AspectImpact
----------------
**Serology****Atypical**: Lower NTT Titres, Delayed Seroconversion, False-Negative NTT (Prozone), BFP More Common
**Clinical****More Severe**: Multiple Chancres, Rapid Progression, Neurosyphilis More Common/At Earlier Stage
**CSF Examination****Mandatory at All Stages** (Higher Neurosyphilis Risk)
**Treatment****Same Penicillin Regimens**; **Close Follow-Up** (NTT Monthly × 3m, Then 3-Monthly × 2y); **CSF at 6m if Neurosyphilis**
**JHR****More Frequent/Severe** (Cytokine Dysregulation)
Treatment Failure & Re-infection
ScenarioDefinitionManagement
----------------------------------
**Treatment Failure****NTT Titre No 4-Fold Decline by 6-12 Months** (or Rise)**CSF Examination Mandatory**; **Re-treat As Per Stage** (Usually Late Latent Regimen: BPC 2.4MU Weekly × 3)
**Re-infection****NTT Titre Rise ≥4-Fold After Previous Decline****Treat as New Infection** (Stage-Appropriate Regimen); **Partner Notification**
**Serofast****Persistent Low NTT Titre (<1:4) After Adequate Treatment****Not Failure** — Monitor, No Re-Treatment Needed

5 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**"Syphilis = Only Genital Disease"****NO.** Systemic: Skin, Mucous Membranes, Bone, Liver, CV, CNS, Eyes, Ears, Placenta
**"RPR Positive = Active Syphilis"****NO.** RPR = Quantitative Activity Marker; **Can Be BFP, Prozone, Serofast, Persist After Cure**; **TT (TPPA) = Lifelong Positive**
**"Late Latent = Not Infectious"****YES (Sexually)**; **But Can Transmit Vertically (Pregnancy)**; **Treat as Infectious for Partner Notification**
**"Neurosyphilis = Only Late Stage"****NO.** Can Occur **At ANY Stage** (Even Primary/Secondary); **HIV Increases Early Risk**
**"Penicillin Allergy = Use Doxycycline"****NO.** **Pregnancy/Neurosyphilis/Congenital: NO PROVEN ALTERNATIVE** — **Desensitise → Penicillin Mandatory**
**"Ceftriaxone = Alternative to Penicillin for Neurosyphilis"****NO.** **Limited Data, Not 1st Line**; **Penicillin G IV Only Proven**; **Ceftriaxole = Alternative if Desensitisation Absolutely Impossible**
**"RPR Negative = No Syphilis"****NO.** **Primary: RPR Negative Early (Seroconversion 1-4w)**; **Late Latent/Tertiary: RPR Can Be Negative**; **Prozone = False Negative at High Titre**
**"All Syphilis Stages Same Penicillin Dose"****NO.** **Early Stages = Single Dose BPC**; **Late Stages = 3 Weekly Doses BPC**; **Neurosyphilis/Congenital = High-Dose IV Pen G**
**"Serofast = Treatment Failure"****NO.** **Persistent Low Titre (<1:4) After Adequate Treatment** — **Not Failure, No Re-Treatment**
**"RPR Titre 4-Fold Decline = Cure at 3 Months"****NO.** **1°/2°: 6 Months**; **Latent/Tertiary: 12-24 Months**; **If No Decline by Expected Time → CSF/LP**
Mnemonic: SYPHILIS MASTER SEROLOGY Syphilis Stages: 1° (Chancre) → 2° (Rash/Palms-Soles/Condylomata Lata) → Early Latent (<1y) → Late Latent (>1y) → Tertiary (Gumma/Aortitis/Neurosyphilis) Y (Why Penicillin?): Only Proven Cure — 1°/2°/Early Latent: BPC 2.4MU IM Single; Late Latent/Tertiary: BPC 2.4MU IM × 3 Weekly; Neurosyphilis: Pen G IV 18-24MU/d × 10-14d; Congenital: Pen G IV 50k/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d Penicillin Allergy: Desensitise → Penicillin (Preferred, Mandatory Pregnancy/Neuro/Congenital); Doxy 14-28d (Non-Pregnant/Non-Neurosyphilis) HIV Co-infection: Atypical Serology, More Severe, Neurosyphilis Common → LP Mandatory All Stages In Pregnancy: Screen All (Booking); Positive → BPC Stage-Based; Treat ≥30d Before Delivery; Allergy → Desensitise Latent Syphilis: Early (<1y) = BPC Single; Late (>1y/Unknown) = BPC × 3 Weekly Interpretation Serology: NTT (RPR/VDRL) = Quantitative, Activity; TT (TPPA/FTA-ABS) = Qualitative, Lifelong Serology Algorithms: Traditional (NTT→TT) vs Reverse (TT→NTT) — Reverse Preferred (WHO/CDC) Congenital: Maternal BPC ≥30d Before Delivery = Prevention; Neonatal Pen G IV 50k/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d; Maternal Adequate + Declining = Observe Jarisch-Herxheimer: 2-12h Post-Tx (Fever, Chills, Worsening Rash) — Supportive, Do NOT Stop Penicillin Antimicrobial: Penicillin G ONLY Proven; No Oral Alternatives for Neuro/Congenital/Pregnancy Lumbar Puncture: Indicated: Neuro Symptoms, HIV All Stages, Treatment Failure, Late Latent/Tertiary, Congenital Treatment Failure: No 4-Fold NTT Decline by 6-12m → LP Mandatory; Re-treat as Late Latent (BPC × 3) Re-infection: NTT Rise ≥4-Fold After Prior Decline → Treat as New Infection Serofast: Persistent Low NTT (<1:4) After Adequate Tx — Not Failure, Monitor Syphilis Congenital: Maternal Treatment ≥30d Before Delivery = Prevention

10 📌 Summary
  • Staging: 1° Chancre (Painless, Indurated), 2° Rash (Palms/Soles), Condylomata Lata, Mucous Patches, Early Latent (<1y), Late Latent (>1y), Tertiary (Gumma, Aortitis, Neurosyphilis)
  • Serology: NTT (RPR/VDRL) = Quantitative, Activity; TT (TPPA/FTA-ABS) = Qualitative, Lifelong — Reverse Algorithm (TT→NTT) Preferred
  • Interpretation: 1°: NTT Negative→Positive; 2°: High Titre NTT; Latent: Low/Declining NTT; Tertiary: NTT Often Negative
  • Treatment: Penicillin G ONLY Proven Cure — 1°/2°/Early Latent: BPC 2.4MU IM Single; Late Latent/Tertiary: BPC 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3; Neurosyphilis: Pen G IV 18-24MU/d × 10-14d; Congenital: Pen G IV 50,000U/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d (Total 10d)
  • Penicillin Allergy: Desensitise → Penicillin (Mandatory in Pregnancy, Neurosyphilis, Congenital); Doxycycline 100mg BD × 14-28d (Non-Pregnant, Non-Neurosyphilis Only)
  • Neurosyphilis: CSF VDRL Positive (Specific, Low Sensitivity); Pen G IV 18-24MU/d × 10-14d; CSF Follow-Up at 6 Months
  • Congenital Prevention: Maternal BPC ≥30 Days Before Delivery; Neonatal Pen G IV 10 Days if Proven/Probable
  • JHR: 2-12 Hours Post-Treatment — Fever, Chills, Worsening Rash — Supportive, Do NOT Stop Penicillin
  • Syphilis in HIV: Atypical Serology, More Severe, Neurosyphilis Common — LP Mandatory at All Stages
  • Screening: Pregnancy (Booking, Universal); MSM (Annual); High Risk (Repeat 28-32w, Delivery)
11 ❓ MCQs (10)
  1. Primary syphilis — characteristic chancre features?

A. Painful, Purulent, Multiple B. Painless, Indurated, Clean Base, Non-Tender Lymphadenopathy C. Painful, Soft, Undermined Edges D. Multiple, Tender, Suppurative Answer: B. Painless, Indurated, Clean Base, Non-Tender Regional Lymphadenopathy.

  1. Syphilis serology — which test is quantitative and correlates with disease activity?

A. TPPA B. FTA-ABS C. RPR / VDRL (Non-Treponemal) D. Western Blot Answer: C. NTT (RPR/VDRL) = Quantitative, Activity Marker; TT = Qualitative, Lifelong.

  1. Late latent syphilis definition & treatment?

A. <1 Year, BPC Single Dose B. >1 Year (or Unknown), BPC 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3 Doses C. >2 Years, No Treatment D. <1 Year, BPC × 3 Weekly Answer: B. Late Latent = >1 Year (or Unknown Duration) → Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3 Doses.

  1. Neurosyphilis — most specific CSF test?

A. CSF FTA-ABS B. CSF Protein C. CSF VDRL D. CSF WBC Count Answer: C. CSF VDRL = Highly Specific (~99%), Low Sensitivity; Diagnostic if Positive.

  1. Syphilis in pregnancy — prevention of congenital syphilis?

A. Treat at Delivery B. Treat ≥30 Days Before Delivery (Stage-Appropriate Penicillin) C. Treat at 36 Weeks Only D. No Treatment Needed if RPR Low Answer: B. Maternal Penicillin Treatment ≥30 Days Before Delivery = Prevention (<1-2% Transmission).

  1. Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction — timing and management?

A. Immediate (<1h), Stop Penicillin B. 2-12 Hours Post-Treatment, Supportive (Do NOT Stop Penicillin) C. 24-48 Hours, Steroids D. 1 Week, Antihistamines Answer: B. 2-12 Hours Post-Treatment (Fever, Chills, Worsening Rash) → Supportive, Do NOT Stop Penicillin.

  1. Penicillin allergy in neurosyphilis — correct management?

A. Ceftriaxone 2g IV Daily B. Desensitisation → Penicillin G IV (Only Proven Cure) C. Doxycycline 100mg BD D. Azithromycin Answer: B. Desensitisation → Penicillin G IV Mandatory (Only Proven Cure for Neurosyphilis/Congenital/Pregnancy).

  1. Neonatal congenital syphilis — proven/probable case treatment?

A. Procaine Penicillin IM 10d B. Aqueous Penicillin G IV 50,000 U/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d (Total 10d) C. Ceftriaxone IV D. Azithromycin Answer: B. Aqueous Penicillin G IV 50,000 U/kg/dose q12h (Days 1-7) → q8h (Days 8-10) = Total 10 Days.

  1. Syphilis screening in pregnancy — when?

A. 36 Weeks Only B. Booking (First Trimester) — Universal; Repeat 28-32w & Delivery if High Risk C. Only If Symptoms D. 20 Weeks Only Answer: B. Universal at Booking; Repeat at 28-32 Weeks & Delivery if High Risk/High Prevalence.

  1. Biologic false positive (BFP) RPR — common causes?

A. HIV, Syphilis B. Autoimmune (SLE, APS), Viral (EBV, CMV), Pregnancy, Age, TB, Leprosy, Drugs C. Recent Vaccination D. Hepatitis Only Answer: B. Autoimmune (SLE, APS), Viral Infections (EBV, CMV, Hepatitis), Pregnancy, Age, TB, Drugs.

  1. Syphilis treatment failure — definition?

A. RPR Negative at 6 Months B. No 4-Fold Decline in RPR by 6-12 Months (or Rise) C. Persistent Low RPR D. TT Becomes Negative Answer: B. No 4-Fold Decline in RPR by 6-12 Months (or Titre Rise) = Treatment Failure; CSF Mandatory.
