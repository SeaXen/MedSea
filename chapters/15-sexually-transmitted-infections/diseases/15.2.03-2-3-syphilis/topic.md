## 2. 🧠 Core Concept: Syphilis Natural History & Staging

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Treponema pallidum Infection] --> B[Primary Syphilis<br/>Chancre (Painless, Indurated, Clean Base)<br/>Regional Lymphadenopathy (Non-Tender) <br/>3w-90d Post-Exposure]
    B --> C[Secondary Syphilis<br/>Systemic Dissemination<br/>Rash (Palms/Soles, Maculopapular)<br/>Condylomata Lata, Mucous Patches<br/>Generalised Lymphadenopathy<br/>Constitutional Symptoms<br/>4-10w Post-Chancre]
    C --> D{Latent Syphilis}
    D -->|<1 Year| E[Early Latent<br/>Seroreversion Risk High<br/>Asymptomatic, Seropositive]
    D -->|>1 Year / Unknown| F[Late Latent<br/>Low Infectivity<br/>Asymptomatic, Seropositive]
    E & F --> G[Tertiary Syphilis (Gummatous, Cardiovascular, Neurosyphilis)]
    G --> G1[Gummatous: Granulomatous Lesions (Skin, Bone, Liver)]
    G --> G2[Cardiovascular: Aortitis, Aortic Regurgitation, Aneurysm]
    G --> G3[Neurosyphilis: Meningovascular, General Paresis, Tabes Dorsalis]
    A --> H[Congenital Syphilis<br/>Transplacental Transmission<br/>Early (<2y) / Late (>2y) Manifestations]
```

## 1️⃣ Syphilis Staging — Clinical Features

### Primary Syphilis

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Incubation** | **10-90 Days** (Median 21 Days) |
| **Chancre** | **Painless**, **Indurated**, **Clean Base**, **Well-Defined Margins**, **Single** (Multiple in HIV), **Heals Spontaneously in 3-6 Weeks** |
| **Lymphadenopathy** | **Regional**, **Non-Tender**, **Rubbery**, **Non-Suppurative** |
| **Serology** | **RPR/VDRL Negative Early** (Seroconversion 1-4 Weeks Post-Chancre); **TPPA/FTA-ABS Positive Earlier** |
| **Duration** | **3-6 Weeks** Untreated → Heals Spontaneously |

### Secondary Syphilis

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Timing** | **4-10 Weeks** Post-Chancre (Overlap Possible) |
| **Rash** | **Maculopapular**, **Palms & Soles** (Pathognomonic), **Trunk, Extremities, Face**, **Non-Pruritic**, **Copper-Coloured** |
| **Mucous Membranes** | **Condylomata Lata** (Moist, Flat, Infectious Papules — Perineum/Anal), **Mucous Patches** (Oral/Genital), **Snail-Track Ulcers** |
| **Systemic** | **Generalised Lymphadenopathy** (Rubbery, Non-Tender), Fever, Malaise, Headache, Pharyngitis, Hepatosplenomegaly, Iritis, Nephritis |
| **Other** | **Alopecia** (Moth-Eaten), **Periostitis**, **Glomerulonephritis** |
| **Duration** | **2-6 Weeks** (Waxing/Waning), Resolution → Latent |

### Latent Syphilis

| Stage | Definition | Serology | Infectivity | Duration |
|-------|------------|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Early Latent** | **<1 Year** Post-Infection (or <2 Years CDC) | **RPR/VDRL High Titre** (≥1:16), TPPA+ | **Infectious** (Early Relapse to Secondary) | Variable |
| **Late Latent** | **>1 Year** Post-Infection (or >2 Years CDC) | **RPR/VDRL Low/Declining**, TPPA+ | **Non-Infectious** (Sexual) | Years-Decades |

> **Key Distinction**: **Early Latent = <1 Year** (or <2 Years CDC); **Late Latent = >1 Year** (Duration Unknown → Assume Late Latent)

### Tertiary Syphilis (Late Manifestations)

| Type | Features | Timing |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Gummatous** | **Gummas** (Granulomatous, Necrotic Lesions) — Skin (Ulcers), Bone (Osteitis), Liver (Hepatitis), Testes, Brain, Heart; Slowly Progressive, Scarring | **3-30 Years** |
| **Cardiovascular** | **Aortitis** (Ascending Aorta), **Aortic Regurgitation**, **Aortic Aneurysm** (Saccular, Ascending), **Coronary Ostial Stenosis** → Angina/MI | **10-30 Years** |
| **Neurosyphilis** | See Below | **Any Time** (Can Occur Early) |

### Neurosyphilis (Neurological Syphilis)

| Syndrome | Features | CSF Findings |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| **Asymptomatic** | Normal Neurological Exam | **CSF Abnormalities** (↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, +VDRL) |
| **Meningeal** | Headache, Nuchal Rigidity, Cranial Nerve Palsies (III, VI, VII), Photophobia | ↑ Lymphocytes, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve** |
| **Meningovascular** | **Stroke (Ischaemic) in Young**, Cranial Nerve Palsies, Seizures, Hemiparesis, Aphasia | ↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve** |
| **General Paresis (Dementia Paralytica)** | **Personality Change**, **Cognitive Decline**, **Memory Loss**, **Delusions**, **Apathy**, **Argyll Robertson Pupils** (Accommodate, Not React to Light) | ↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve** |
| **Tabes Dorsalis** | **Lightning Pains** (Lancinating Leg Pains), **Sensory Ataxia**, **Loss of Proprioception/Vibration**, **Argyll Robertson Pupils**, **Absent Reflexes**, **Charcot Joints** | ↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve** |
| **Ocular Syphilis** | Uveitis, Optic Neuritis, Retinitis, Interstitial Keratitis | ↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve** |
| **Otic Syphilis** | Sensorineural Hearing Loss, Tinnitus, Vertigo | ↑ WBC, ↑ Protein, **CSF-VDRL +ve** |

> **Key**: **Neurosyphilis Can Occur at ANY STAGE** (Even Primary/Secondary); **CSF Examination Mandatory** if Neurological Signs, HIV+, Treatment Failure, Late Latent/Tertiary

## 1️⃣ Congenital Syphilis

### Transmission & Risk

| Factor | Risk |
|--------|------|
| **Transplacental** | **Any Stage of Pregnancy** (Highest 2nd/3rd Trimester) |
| **Maternal Stage** | Primary/Secondary: **70-100%**; Early Latent: **40%**, Late Latent: **10%**; Treated: **<1-2%** if Adequate Treatment ≥30 Days Before Delivery |
| **Timing** | **No Barrier** After 16-18 Weeks Gestation; Treponemes Cross Placenta Freely |

### Clinical Classification

| Category | Features |
|---------|----------|
| **Early Congenital (<2 Years)** | **Snuffles** (Bloody, Mucopurulent Rhinitis), **Rash** (Maculopapular, Palms/Soles, Vesiculobullous), **Hepatosplenomegaly**, **Jaundice**, **Pneumonitis**, **Osteochondritis** (Symmetric Long Bone Lesions), **Pseudoparalysis** (Parrot's — Painful Limb Movement), **Lymphadenopathy**, **Nephrotic Syndrome**, **Thrombocytopenia** |
| **Late Congenital (>2 Years)** | **Hutchinson's Triad**: **Hutchinson's Teeth** (Notched, Peg-Shaped Incisors), **Interstitial Keratitis**, **8th Nerve Deafness**; **Other**: Saddle Nose, Saber Shins, Clutton's Joints (Knee Effusions), Mulberry Molars, Rhagades |

### Diagnosis — Neonatal Evaluation

| Investigation | Indication | Interpretation |
|--------------|------------|----------------|
| **Maternal Serology** | All Pregnant Women | RPR/VDRL + TPPA (Stage Maternal Infection) |
| **Neonatal RPR/VDRL** | Compared to Maternal | **Neonatal Titre ≥4x Maternal → Probable/Congenital**; Same/Declining → Monitor |
| **Treponemal Test (TPPA/FTA-ABS)** | Confirmatory | **Positive = Exposed** (Maternal IgG Transfer); **IgM FTA-ABS / PCR / Dark Field** = Active Infection |
| **CSF Examination** | All Probable/Confirmed Cases | **VDRL, WBC, Protein** — Neurosyphilis if Abnormal |
| **Long Bone X-Rays** | Probable/Confirmed | **Osteochondritis** (Metaphyseal Lucencies, Periostitis) |
| **CBC, LFT, Coagulation** | Baseline | Thrombocytopenia, Anaemia, Elevated LFTs |
| **Dark Field / PCR (Lesion Fluid)** | If Lesions Present | **Direct Detection** of T. pallidum |

## 2️⃣ Syphilis Serology — Interpretation Mastery

### Test Types

| Category | Tests | Principle | Characteristics |
|----------|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Non-Treponemal (NTT)** | **RPR** (Rapid Plasma Reagin), **VDRL** (Venereal Disease Research Lab) | Detect **Anti-Cardiolipin (Lipoidal) Antibodies** | **Quantitative (Titre)**, **Sensitive for Active Disease**, **Correlates with Activity**, **Prozone Phenomenon** (False -ve at High Titre), **Biological False Positives (BFP)** (Autoimmune, Viral, Pregnancy, Age) |
| **Treponemal (TT)** | **TPPA** (Treponema pallidum Particle Agglutination), **FTA-ABS** (Fluorescent Treponemal Antibody Absorption), **EIA/CIA** (Enzyme/Chemiluminescence Immunoassay), **Western Blot** | Detect **Anti-T. pallidum Specific Antibodies** | **Qualitative (Positive/Negative)**, **Highly Specific**, **Lifelong Positivity** (Even After Cure), **Does Not Correlate with Activity** |

### Testing Algorithms

| Algorithm | Sequence | Advantage |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Traditional** | **NTT (RPR/VDRL) → If Positive → TT (TPPA/FTA-ABS)** | Cheaper, Detects Active Disease First, Quantifies Activity |
| **Reverse (CDC/WHO Recommended)** | **TT (EIA/CIA/TPPA) → If Positive → NTT (RPR/VDRL) for Titres** | Higher Sensitivity (Detects Early Primary, Latent, Treated), Automated, High-Throughput |

### Serology Interpretation Matrix

| Clinical Stage | NTT (RPR/VDRL) | TT (TPPA/FTA-ABS) | Notes |
|----------------|----------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Primary** | **Negative → Positive (Seroconversion 1-4w)** | **Positive Early** | **TT Positive Before NTT**; Repeat NTT if Clinical Suspicion |
| **Secondary** | **High Titre** (1:64 to 1:1024) | **Positive** | Peak NTT Activity |
| **Early Latent** | **Positive (Moderate-High)** | **Positive** | High Relapse Risk |
| **Late Latent** | **Low/Declining Titre** (<1:16) or **Negative** | **Positive** | NTT May Be Negative |
| **Tertiary (Gumma/CV)** | **Often Negative** | **Positive** | NTT Insensitive in Late Disease |
| **Neurosyphilis** | **CSF-VDRL +ve** (Specific) | CSF TPPA/FTA-ABS | **CSF-VDRL = Diagnostic** (High Specificity, Low Sensitivity) |
| **Congenital** | **Neonatal Titre ≥4x Maternal** | **Neonatal Positive** (Maternal IgG Transfer = Positive) | **IgM FTA-ABS / PCR** = Active Infection; **Maternal IgG** = Passive Transfer |
| **Treated (Cured)** | **Titre Decline ≥4-Fold (2 Dilutions)** | **Remains Positive (Lifelong)** | **Serofast** (Persistent Low Titre) Common; **NTT Negative = Cure** |
| **Re-infection** | **Titre Rise ≥4-Fold (2 Dilutions)** | **Remains Positive** | Distinguished from Serofast |

> **Key Concepts**:
> - **Seroconversion** = NTT Negative → Positive
> - **Serofast** = Persistent Low NTT Titre After Treatment (<1:4) — Not Treatment Failure
> - **Prozone Phenomenon** = False Negative NTT at Very High Titre → Dilute Serum
> - **Biological False Positive (BFP)** = Positive NTT, Negative TT — Autoimmune, Viral, Pregnancy, Age

### Treatment Response & Follow-Up

| Parameter | Expected Response |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **NTT Titre (RPR/VDRL)** | **≥4-Fold Decline (2 Dilutions)** by **6 Months (1°/2°)**, **12-24 Months (Latent/Tertiary)** |
| **Serofast** | Persistent Low Titre (<1:4) After Adequate Treatment — **Not Failure** |
| **Treatment Failure** | **Titre Rise ≥4-Fold** (Re-infection/Failure) or **No Decline by 6-12 Months** |
| **Re-infection** | **Titre Rise ≥4-Fold** (After Prior Decline) — Treat Again |

### CSF Analysis for Neurosyphilis

| Parameter | Normal | Neurosyphilis |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| **WBC (Lymphocytes)** | <5/μL | **>5-20/μL** (Mild-Moderate) |
| **Protein** | <45 mg/dL | **>45 mg/dL** |
| **CSF-VDRL** | Negative | **Positive (Specific, Low Sensitivity ~50-70%)** |
| **CSF FTA-ABS/TPPA** | Negative | Positive (High Sensitivity, Low Specificity) |
| **Glucose** | Normal | Normal or Low |

> **Diagnosis of Neurosyphilis**: **Reactive CSF-VDRL + Clinical/CSF Abnormalities** (No Single Test Perfect)

## 2️⃣ Treatment — Stage-Specific Penicillin Regimens

### Penicillin G — The Only Proven Curative Agent

| Stage | Regimen | Dose & Route | Duration | Alternative (Penicillin Allergy) |
|-------|---------|--------------|----------|----------------------------------|
| **Primary / Secondary / Early Latent** | **Benzathine Penicillin G** | **2.4 Million Units IM Single Dose** | **Single Dose** | **Desensitise → Penicillin** (Preferred); **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 14d** (Non-Pregnant); **Ceftriaxone 1g IM/IV Daily × 10-14d** (Limited Data) |
| **Late Latent / Tertiary (Gummatous, Cardiovascular)** | **Benzathine Penicillin G** | **2.4 Million Units IM Weekly × 3 Doses** (Total 7.2 MU) | **3 Weekly Doses** | **Desensitise → Penicillin**; **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 28d** (Non-Pregnant); **Ceftriaxone 1-2g IV Daily × 14d** |
| **Neurosyphilis (All Forms)** | **Aqueous Penicillin G IV** | **18-24 Million Units/Day** (3-4 MU q4h) **OR 18-24 MU Continuous Infusion** | **10-14 Days** | **Desensitise → Penicillin G IV**; **Ceftriaxone 2g IV Daily × 14d** (Alternative) |
| **Congenital Syphilis** | **Aqueous Penicillin G IV** | **50,000 Units/kg/dose q12h (First 7 Days) → q8h (Day 8-10)** | **10 Days Total** | **Desensitise → Penicillin** (Only Proven); **Procaine Penicillin 50,000 U/kg IM Daily × 10d** (If IV Access Difficult) |

> **Critical**: **Penicillin G is the ONLY PROVEN Curative Agent for Syphilis in Pregnancy and Neurosyphilis; No Oral Alternatives Proven for Neurosyphilis/Congenital**

### Penicillin Allergy Management

| Approach | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Desensitisation** | **Preferred for ALL Stages** (Especially Pregnancy, Neurosyphilis, Congenital); **Oral/IV Protocols** (Gradual Dose Escalation Over Hours); **Specialist Allergist/ID Supervision** |
| **Alternative (Non-Pregnant, Non-Neurosyphilis)** | **Doxycycline 100mg BD** — Primary/Secondary/Early Latent: **14 Days**; Late Latent/Tertiary: **28 Days** |
| **Alternative (Pregnancy)** | **NO PROVEN ORAL ALTERNATIVE** — **Desensitisation Mandatory** |
| **Ceftriaxone** | **1-2g IV/IM Daily × 10-14 Days** (Limited Data, Not 1st Line); **Not for Neurosyphilis/Congenital as 1st Choice** |
| **Azithromycin** | **NOT RECOMMENDED** (Resistance Documented, Treatment Failures) |

### Jarisch-Herxheimer Reaction (JHR)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Mechanism** | **Massive Treponemal Lysis** → Release of Lipoproteins → Cytokine Storm (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-8) |
| **Incidence** | **~50% Secondary**, 10-25% Primary, <10% Latent; **Higher in HIV+** |
| **Timing** | **2-12 Hours Post-Treatment** (Peak 4-6h), Resolves in 12-24h |
| **Symptoms** | Fever, Chills, Rigors, Headache, Myalgia, **Worsening of Rash**, Hypotension, Tachycardia |
| **Management** | **Supportive**: Antipyretics (Paracetamol), Fluids, **Do NOT Delay/Stop Penicillin**; **Prednisolone Prophylaxis** (1mg/kg Pre-Treatment) Optional in Severe Cases |
| **Differential** | Anaphylaxis (Urticaria, Angioedema, Wheeze, Stridor — Immediate <1h) — **Treat as Anaphylaxis** |

## 2️⃣ Neurosyphilis — Detailed Management

### Indications for CSF Examination (Lumbar Puncture)

| Indication | Mandatory / Recommended |
|------------|--------------------------|
| **Neurological Symptoms** | **Mandatory** (Headache, Cranial Nerve Palsy, Sensory/Motor Deficits, Cognitive Changes) |
| **HIV Infection** | **Mandatory** (All Stages — Higher Neurosyphilis Risk, Atypical Serology) |
| **Treatment Failure** | **Mandatory** (Titre Rise/No Decline) |
| **Late Latent / Tertiary Syphilis** | **Recommended** (If Duration Unknown) |
| **Congenital Syphilis** | **Mandatory** (All Probable/Confirmed Cases) |
| **Ocular/Otic Syphilis** | **Mandatory** |

### CSF Diagnostic Criteria for Neurosyphilis

| Finding | Significance |
|---------|-------------|
| **CSF-VDRL Reactive** | **Highly Specific (~99%)**, Low Sensitivity (~50-70%) — **Diagnostic if Positive** |
| **CSF WBC >5/μL (Lymphocytes)** | **Supportive** (Mild Pleocytosis) |
| **CSF Protein >45 mg/dL** | **Supportive** |
| **CSF FTA-ABS/TPPA Reactive** | **High Sensitivity (~90%)**, Low Specificity — **Negative Rules Out** |
| **CSF PCR (T. pallidum)** | Emerging, High Sensitivity/Specificity — Not Widely Available |

> **Diagnosis**: **Reactive CSF-VDRL + Clinical/CSF Abnormalities** = Definite Neurosyphilis; **Clinical + CSF Abnormalities + Reactive Serology** = Probable Neurosyphilis

### Neurosyphilis Treatment Algorithm

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Neurosyphilis Diagnosed] --> B{Penicillin Allergy?}
    B -->|No| C[Aqueous Penicillin G IV<br/>18-24 MU/Day ÷ 4h × 10-14d<br/>(or Continuous Infusion)]
    B -->|Yes| D{Desensitisation Possible?}
    D -->|Yes| C
    D -->|No| E[Ceftriaxone 2g IV Daily × 14d<br/>(Alternative — Limited Data)]
    C & E --> F[Follow-Up: CSF at 6m<br/>If CSF Abnormal Persists → Re-treat]
    F --> G[If HIV+: Monitor Closer<br/>CSF at 3m, 6m, 12m]
```

## 2️⃣ Syphilis in Pregnancy & Congenital Prevention

### Maternal Screening & Staging

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Booking (First Trimester)** | **Universal Screening: RPR/VDRL + TPPA** (Reverse Algorithm Preferred) |
| **High Risk / High Prevalence Areas** | **Repeat at 28-32 Weeks & Delivery** |
| **Positive Screen** | **Stage Maternal Infection** (Clinical, Serology, CSF if Indicated) |

### Treatment in Pregnancy

| Maternal Stage | Regimen | Comments |
|----------------|---------|----------|
| **Primary / Secondary / Early Latent** | **Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Single Dose** | **Only Proven Treatment**; **Desensitise if Allergic**; **Treat ≥30 Days Before Delivery** |
| **Late Latent / Unknown Duration** | **Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3 Doses** | **Treat ≥30 Days Before Delivery** |
| **Neurosyphilis** | **Penicillin G IV 18-24 MU/Day × 10-14 Days** | **Desensitise if Allergic** |
| **Penicillin Allergy** | **Desensitisation → Penicillin** (Mandatory — No Proven Alternative) | **Doxycycline/Tetracycline Contraindicated** (Teratogenic) |

> **Critical**: **Adequate Maternal Treatment ≥30 Days Before Delivery = <1-2% Congenital Transmission**; **Untreated/Inadequate = 40-100% Transmission (Stage-Dependent)**

### Congenital Syphilis — Neonatal Evaluation & Management

#### Classification & Management Algorithm

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Maternal Syphilis Positive] --> B{Infant Evaluation}
    B --> C[Neonatal RPR/VDRL Titre<br/>Compared to Maternal]
    C --> D{Neonatal Titre ≥4x Maternal?}
    D -->|Yes| E[**Probable/Confirmed Congenital Syphilis**<br/>→ Full Workup (CSF, X-Ray, CBC, LFT)<br/>→ **Penicillin G IV 50,000 U/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d (Total 10d)**]
    D -->|No| F{Abnormal Clinical/X-Ray/CSF?}
    F -->|Yes| E
    F -->|No| G{Maternal Treatment Adequate?<br/>(≥30d Before Delivery, 4-Fold Decline)}
    G -->|Yes| H[**Unlikely Congenital**<br/>→ Observe, Serial RPR (Monthly × 3m)<br/>→ Ensure Titres Decline]
    G -->|No/Unknown| I[**Consider Treatment**<br/>→ Procaine Penicillin 50,000 U/kg IM Daily × 10d<br/>(If Follow-Up Uncertain)]
```

#### Neonatal Treatment Regimens

| Scenario | Regimen |
|----------|---------|
| **Proven/Probable Congenital (Abnormal CSF/X-Ray/Clinical/Neonatal Titre ≥4x Maternal)** | **Aqueous Penicillin G IV 50,000 Units/kg/dose q12h (Days 1-7) → q8h (Days 8-10)** — **Total 10 Days** |
| **Possible Congenital (Maternal Untreated/Inadequate, Normal Infant Workup)** | **Procaine Penicillin G 50,000 Units/kg IM Daily × 10 Days** (If IV Access Difficult / Follow-Up Uncertain) |
| **Unlikely Congenital (Maternal Adequately Treated, Normal Infant)** | **Observation Only** — Serial RPR/VDRL Monthly × 3 Months (Ensure Decline) |

## 4️⃣ Special Situations & Complications

### Syphilis in HIV Infection

| Aspect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Serology** | **Atypical**: Lower NTT Titres, Delayed Seroconversion, False-Negative NTT (Prozone), BFP More Common |
| **Clinical** | **More Severe**: Multiple Chancres, Rapid Progression, Neurosyphilis More Common/At Earlier Stage |
| **CSF Examination** | **Mandatory at All Stages** (Higher Neurosyphilis Risk) |
| **Treatment** | **Same Penicillin Regimens**; **Close Follow-Up** (NTT Monthly × 3m, Then 3-Monthly × 2y); **CSF at 6m if Neurosyphilis** |
| **JHR** | **More Frequent/Severe** (Cytokine Dysregulation) |

### Treatment Failure & Re-infection

| Scenario | Definition | Management |
|----------|------------|------------|
| **Treatment Failure** | **NTT Titre No 4-Fold Decline by 6-12 Months** (or Rise) | **CSF Examination Mandatory**; **Re-treat As Per Stage** (Usually Late Latent Regimen: BPC 2.4MU Weekly × 3) |
| **Re-infection** | **NTT Titre Rise ≥4-Fold After Previous Decline** | **Treat as New Infection** (Stage-Appropriate Regimen); **Partner Notification** |
| **Serofast** | **Persistent Low NTT Titre (<1:4) After Adequate Treatment** | **Not Failure** — Monitor, No Re-Treatment Needed |

## 3. ⚡ FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **Staging** | **1°: Chancre (Painless, Indurated)**, **2°: Rash (Palms/Soles), Condylomata Lata, Mucous Patches**, **Early Latent (<1y)**, **Late Latent (>1y)**, **Tertiary: Gumma, CV (Aortitis), Neurosyphilis** |
| **Serology** | **NTT (RPR/VDRL): Quantitative, Activity; TT (TPPA/FTA-ABS): Qualitative, Lifelong** — **Reverse Algorithm (TT→NTT) Preferred** |
| **Serology Interpretation** | **Primary: NTT -ve → +ve (Seroconversion); 2°: High NTT (+ve TT); Latent: Low/Declining NTT (+ve TT); Tertiary: NTT Often -ve (+ve TT)** |
| **Treatment** | **Penicillin G ONLY Proven Cure**; **1°/2°/Early Latent: BPC 2.4MU IM Single**; **Late Latent/Tertiary: BPC 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3**; **Neurosyphilis: Penicillin G IV 18-24MU/d × 10-14d**; **Congenital: Pen G IV 50,000U/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d** |
| **Penicillin Allergy** | **Desensitisation → Penicillin (Preferred, Mandatory in Pregnancy/Neurosyphilis/Congenital)**; **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 14-28d (Non-Pregnant, Non-Neurosyphilis)** |
| **Neurosyphilis** | **CSF VDRL +ve (Specific)**; **Penicillin G IV 18-24MU/d × 10-14d**; **CSF F/U at 6m** |

## 5. 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"Syphilis = Only Genital Disease"** | **NO.** Systemic: Skin, Mucous Membranes, Bone, Liver, CV, CNS, Eyes, Ears, Placenta |
| **"RPR Positive = Active Syphilis"** | **NO.** RPR = Quantitative Activity Marker; **Can Be BFP, Prozone, Serofast, Persist After Cure**; **TT (TPPA) = Lifelong Positive** |
| **"Late Latent = Not Infectious"** | **YES (Sexually)**; **But Can Transmit Vertically (Pregnancy)**; **Treat as Infectious for Partner Notification** |
| **"Neurosyphilis = Only Late Stage"** | **NO.** Can Occur **At ANY Stage** (Even Primary/Secondary); **HIV Increases Early Risk** |
| **"Penicillin Allergy = Use Doxycycline"** | **NO.** **Pregnancy/Neurosyphilis/Congenital: NO PROVEN ALTERNATIVE** — **Desensitise → Penicillin Mandatory** |
| **"Ceftriaxone = Alternative to Penicillin for Neurosyphilis"** | **NO.** **Limited Data, Not 1st Line**; **Penicillin G IV Only Proven**; **Ceftriaxole = Alternative if Desensitisation Absolutely Impossible** |
| **"RPR Negative = No Syphilis"** | **NO.** **Primary: RPR Negative Early (Seroconversion 1-4w)**; **Late Latent/Tertiary: RPR Can Be Negative**; **Prozone = False Negative at High Titre** |
| **"All Syphilis Stages Same Penicillin Dose"** | **NO.** **Early Stages = Single Dose BPC**; **Late Stages = 3 Weekly Doses BPC**; **Neurosyphilis/Congenital = High-Dose IV Pen G** |
| **"Serofast = Treatment Failure"** | **NO.** **Persistent Low Titre (<1:4) After Adequate Treatment** — **Not Failure, No Re-Treatment** |
| **"RPR Titre 4-Fold Decline = Cure at 3 Months"** | **NO.** **1°/2°: 6 Months**; **Latent/Tertiary: 12-24 Months**; **If No Decline by Expected Time → CSF/LP** |

> **Mnemonic: SYPHILIS MASTER SEROLOGY**  
> **S**yphilis Stages: **1° (Chancre) → 2° (Rash/Palms-Soles/Condylomata Lata) → Early Latent (<1y) → Late Latent (>1y) → Tertiary (Gumma/Aortitis/Neurosyphilis)**  
> **Y** (Why Penicillin?): **Only Proven Cure** — **1°/2°/Early Latent: BPC 2.4MU IM Single**; **Late Latent/Tertiary: BPC 2.4MU IM × 3 Weekly**; **Neurosyphilis: Pen G IV 18-24MU/d × 10-14d**; **Congenital: Pen G IV 50k/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d**  
> **P**enicillin Allergy: **Desensitise → Penicillin (Preferred, Mandatory Pregnancy/Neuro/Congenital)**; **Doxy 14-28d (Non-Pregnant/Non-Neurosyphilis)**  
> **H**IV Co-infection: **Atypical Serology, More Severe, Neurosyphilis Common → LP Mandatory All Stages**  
> **I**n Pregnancy: **Screen All (Booking)**; **Positive → BPC Stage-Based**; **Treat ≥30d Before Delivery**; **Allergy → Desensitise**  
> **L**atent Syphilis: **Early (<1y) = BPC Single**; **Late (>1y/Unknown) = BPC × 3 Weekly**  
> **I**nterpretation Serology: **NTT (RPR/VDRL) = Quantitative, Activity; TT (TPPA/FTA-ABS) = Qualitative, Lifelong**  
> **S**erology Algorithms: **Traditional (NTT→TT) vs Reverse (TT→NTT) — Reverse Preferred (WHO/CDC)**  
> **C**ongenital: **Maternal BPC ≥30d Before Delivery = Prevention**; **Neonatal Pen G IV 50k/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d**; **Maternal Adequate + Declining = Observe**  
> **J**arisch-Herxheimer: **2-12h Post-Tx (Fever, Chills, Worsening Rash)** — **Supportive, Do NOT Stop Penicillin**  
> **A**ntimicrobial: **Penicillin G ONLY Proven**; **No Oral Alternatives for Neuro/Congenital/Pregnancy**  
> **L**umbar Puncture: **Indicated: Neuro Symptoms, HIV All Stages, Treatment Failure, Late Latent/Tertiary, Congenital**  
> **T**reatment Failure: **No 4-Fold NTT Decline by 6-12m → LP Mandatory**; **Re-treat as Late Latent (BPC × 3)**  
> **R**e-infection: **NTT Rise ≥4-Fold After Prior Decline** → Treat as New Infection  
> **S**erofast: **Persistent Low NTT (<1:4) After Adequate Tx** — Not Failure, Monitor  
> **S**yphilis Congenital: **Maternal Treatment ≥30d Before Delivery = Prevention**

## 10. 📌 Summary
- **Staging**: **1° Chancre (Painless, Indurated)**, **2° Rash (Palms/Soles), Condylomata Lata, Mucous Patches**, **Early Latent (<1y)**, **Late Latent (>1y)**, **Tertiary (Gumma, Aortitis, Neurosyphilis)**
- **Serology**: **NTT (RPR/VDRL) = Quantitative, Activity**; **TT (TPPA/FTA-ABS) = Qualitative, Lifelong** — **Reverse Algorithm (TT→NTT) Preferred**
- **Interpretation**: **1°: NTT Negative→Positive**; **2°: High Titre NTT**; **Latent: Low/Declining NTT**; **Tertiary: NTT Often Negative**
- **Treatment**: **Penicillin G ONLY Proven Cure** — **1°/2°/Early Latent: BPC 2.4MU IM Single**; **Late Latent/Tertiary: BPC 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3**; **Neurosyphilis: Pen G IV 18-24MU/d × 10-14d**; **Congenital: Pen G IV 50,000U/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d (Total 10d)**
- **Penicillin Allergy**: **Desensitise → Penicillin (Mandatory in Pregnancy, Neurosyphilis, Congenital)**; **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 14-28d (Non-Pregnant, Non-Neurosyphilis Only)**
- **Neurosyphilis**: **CSF VDRL Positive (Specific, Low Sensitivity)**; **Pen G IV 18-24MU/d × 10-14d**; **CSF Follow-Up at 6 Months**
- **Congenital Prevention**: **Maternal BPC ≥30 Days Before Delivery**; **Neonatal Pen G IV 10 Days** if Proven/Probable
- **JHR**: **2-12 Hours Post-Treatment** — Fever, Chills, Worsening Rash — **Supportive, Do NOT Stop Penicillin**
- **Syphilis in HIV**: **Atypical Serology, More Severe, Neurosyphilis Common** — **LP Mandatory at All Stages**
- **Screening**: **Pregnancy (Booking, Universal)**; **MSM (Annual)**; **High Risk (Repeat 28-32w, Delivery)**

## 11. ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **Primary syphilis — characteristic chancre features?**  
   A. Painful, Purulent, Multiple  B. **Painless, Indurated, Clean Base, Non-Tender Lymphadenopathy**  C. Painful, Soft, Undermined Edges  D. Multiple, Tender, Suppurative  
   *Answer: B. Painless, Indurated, Clean Base, Non-Tender Regional Lymphadenopathy.*

2. **Syphilis serology — which test is quantitative and correlates with disease activity?**  
   A. TPPA  B. FTA-ABS  C. **RPR / VDRL (Non-Treponemal)**  D. Western Blot  
   *Answer: C. NTT (RPR/VDRL) = Quantitative, Activity Marker; TT = Qualitative, Lifelong.*

3. **Late latent syphilis definition & treatment?**  
   A. <1 Year, BPC Single Dose  B. **>1 Year (or Unknown), BPC 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3 Doses**  C. >2 Years, No Treatment  D. <1 Year, BPC × 3 Weekly  
   *Answer: B. Late Latent = >1 Year (or Unknown Duration) → Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3 Doses.*

4. **Neurosyphilis — most specific CSF test?**  
   A. CSF FTA-ABS  B. CSF Protein  C. **CSF VDRL**  D. CSF WBC Count  
   *Answer: C. CSF VDRL = Highly Specific (~99%), Low Sensitivity; Diagnostic if Positive.*

5. **Syphilis in pregnancy — prevention of congenital syphilis?**  
   A. Treat at Delivery  B. **Treat ≥30 Days Before Delivery (Stage-Appropriate Penicillin)**  C. Treat at 36 Weeks Only  D. No Treatment Needed if RPR Low  
   *Answer: B. Maternal Penicillin Treatment ≥30 Days Before Delivery = Prevention (<1-2% Transmission).*

6. **Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction — timing and management?**  
   A. Immediate (<1h), Stop Penicillin  B. **2-12 Hours Post-Treatment, Supportive (Do NOT Stop Penicillin)**  C. 24-48 Hours, Steroids  D. 1 Week, Antihistamines  
   *Answer: B. 2-12 Hours Post-Treatment (Fever, Chills, Worsening Rash) → Supportive, Do NOT Stop Penicillin.*

7. **Penicillin allergy in neurosyphilis — correct management?**  
   A. Ceftriaxone 2g IV Daily  B. **Desensitisation → Penicillin G IV (Only Proven Cure)**  C. Doxycycline 100mg BD  D. Azithromycin  
   *Answer: B. Desensitisation → Penicillin G IV Mandatory (Only Proven Cure for Neurosyphilis/Congenital/Pregnancy).*

8. **Neonatal congenital syphilis — proven/probable case treatment?**  
   A. Procaine Penicillin IM 10d  B. **Aqueous Penicillin G IV 50,000 U/kg q12h × 7d → q8h × 3d (Total 10d)**  C. Ceftriaxone IV  D. Azithromycin  
   *Answer: B. Aqueous Penicillin G IV 50,000 U/kg/dose q12h (Days 1-7) → q8h (Days 8-10) = Total 10 Days.*

9. **Syphilis screening in pregnancy — when?**  
   A. 36 Weeks Only  B. **Booking (First Trimester) — Universal; Repeat 28-32w & Delivery if High Risk**  C. Only If Symptoms  D. 20 Weeks Only  
   *Answer: B. Universal at Booking; Repeat at 28-32 Weeks & Delivery if High Risk/High Prevalence.*

9. **Biologic false positive (BFP) RPR — common causes?**  
   A. HIV, Syphilis  B. **Autoimmune (SLE, APS), Viral (EBV, CMV), Pregnancy, Age, TB, Leprosy, Drugs**  C. Recent Vaccination  D. Hepatitis Only  
   *Answer: B. Autoimmune (SLE, APS), Viral Infections (EBV, CMV, Hepatitis), Pregnancy, Age, TB, Drugs.*

10. **Syphilis treatment failure — definition?**  
    A. RPR Negative at 6 Months  B. **No 4-Fold Decline in RPR by 6-12 Months (or Rise)**  C. Persistent Low RPR  D. TT Becomes Negative  
    *Answer: B. No 4-Fold Decline in RPR by 6-12 Months (or Titre Rise) = Treatment Failure; CSF Mandatory.*
