1️⃣ Gonorrhoea — Clinical Manifestations
Epidemiology
ParameterDetail
-----------------
**Global Incidence****~82 Million** New Cases/Year (WHO 2023)
**Prevalence****0.9%** (Women 15-49), **0.7%** (Men 15-49)
**Age Peak****15-24 Years** (Highest Incidence)
**Asymptomatic Rate****10-20% Men**, **50-80% Women**, **>90% Pharyngeal/Rectal**
**Transmission**Sexual (Vaginal, Anal, Oral), Vertical (Mother→Child)
**Co-infection****10-40%** with Chlamydia; **HIV** Co-infection ↑ Risk
**AMR Threat****WHO Priority 2 (High)** — Ceftriaxone Resistance Emerging Globally
Clinical Presentation by Site
SiteSymptomaticKey FeaturesComplications
------------------------------------------------
**Male Urethra****80-90%****Urethritis**: Dysuria, **Purulent Urethral Discharge**, Meatal Erythema/Edema**Epididymo-orchitis**, Stricture, DGI
**Female Cervix****30-50%****Cervicitis**: Mucopurulent Discharge, Ectopy, Friability, ICB/IM Bleeding**PID**, Infertility, Ectopic, Chronic Pelvic Pain
**Rectal****Often Asymptomatic** (>90%)Proctitis: Mucus, Blood, Tenesmus, Pain (If Symptomatic)**Proctitis**, Strictures, Anal Fissures
**Pharyngeal****Rarely Symptomatic** (>90% Asymptomatic)Sore Throat, Exudate (Rare)**Reservoir for AMR**, Transmission
**Conjunctival****Acute****Hyperacute Conjunctivitis**: Copious Purulent Discharge, Eyelid Edema, Chemosis**Corneal Ulceration**, Perforation, Blindness
**DGI****0.5-3%****Arthritis-Dermatitis Syndrome** (Migratory Polyarthralgia, Tenosynovitis, Pustular Rash) → **Septic Arthritis** (Monoarticular)**Septic Arthritis**, Endocarditis, Meningitis
1️⃣ Disseminated Gonococcal Infection (DGI)
Pathophysiology
MechanismDetail
-------------------
**Bacteremia**Gonococci Enter Bloodstream → Haematogenous Spread
**Immune Complexes**IgG-Gonococcal Antigen Complexes → Vasculitis, Dermatitis
**Direct Invasion**Joints, Skin, Tendons, Pericardium, Meninges
Clinical Presentation
SyndromeFeaturesPrevalence
--------------------------------
**Arthritis-Dermatitis Syndrome**Fever, **Migratory Polyarthralgia**, **Tenosynovitis** (Wrist/Ankle), **Pustular Rash** (Vesiculopustular, Acral/Distal Extremities), **Palpable Purpura****~60%**
**Septic Arthritis****Monoarticular** (Knee > Wrist > Ankle), Severe Pain, Effusion, Fever, **No Rash****~40%**
**Other**Endocarditis, Meningitis, Hepatitis, MyocarditisRare
Key: DGI = Bacteremia + Arthritis/Dermatitis; Blood Culture Often Negative; Joint Fluid Culture + NAAT for Diagnosis
Diagnosis
TestUtility
---------------
**Joint Fluid (NAAT + Culture + AST)****Gold Standard** (Crucial for AST)
**Blood Culture**Often Negative (<30% Positive)
**Mucosal Sites (NAAT + Culture)**Urethral, Cervical, Pharyngeal, Rectal — Identify Source
**Skin Lesion Biopsy/NAAT**Vesiculopustular Lesions
Treatment (DGI)
RegimenDose
---------------
**Ceftriaxone****1g IV/IM q24h** (or q12h if Severe) × **7 Days** (Switch to Oral When Improving)
**Alternative** (If Ceftriaxone Allergy)**Gentamicin 240mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 2g PO Stat** (Or Azithro 1g Weekly × 2)
**Plus****Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7d** (Co-infection CT Cover)
**Joint Management**Aspiration (Repeated if Needed), Immobilisation, Physiotherapy
2️⃣ Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) — The Global Crisis
Resistance Mechanisms & Surveillance
AntimicrobialResistance MechanismGlobal Status (WHO GASP 2023)
------------------------------------------------------------------
**Penicillin****penA** (PBP2 Mutation), **TEM-1 Plasmid (Penicillinase)****High Resistance Global** (Historically 1st Drug)
**Tetracycline****TetM Plasmid (Ribosomal Protection)**, Mutations**High Resistance**
**Ciprofloxacin (Fluoroquinolones)****gyrA/parC Mutations** (DNA Gyrase/Topoisomerase IV)**High Resistance >50% Global**; **Do Not Use Empirically**
**Azithromycin (Macrolides)****23S rRNA Mutations**, **Efflux Pumps (MtrCDE)**, **erm****Rising Resistance** (MIC Creep); Not Monotherapy
**Cefixime (Oral Cephalosporins)****penA Mutations**, Mosaic penA**Declining Susceptibility**; Not Preferred for Pharyngeal
**Ceftriaxone (Injectable Cephalosporins)****penA Mutations (Mosaic penA XXXIV)**, **porB**, **mtrR****Emerging Resistance** (MIC Creep, Ceftriaxone-R Strains Reported)
WHO GASP (Gonococcal Antimicrobial Surveillance Programme)
ComponentAction
------------------
**Sentinel Surveillance**Sentinel Sites, Representative Sampling (Geographic, Population)
**AMR Testing**Culture + AST (Ceftriaxone, Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin, Cefixime, Spectinomycin)
**Molecular Surveillance****penA** (Ceftriaxone), **gyrA/parC** (Ciprofloxacin), **23S rRNA** (Azithromycin), **mtrR** (Efflux)
**Data Sharing**WHO GASP Database, National AMR Surveillance, AMR Action Plans
Ceftriaxone Resistance — Critical Threat
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Mechanism****penA Mosaic Mutations** (XXXIV, XIX, etc.), **porB**, **mtrR** Efflux, **pilQ**
**Resistance Level****MIC >0.125 mg/L** (EUCAST/CLSI Breakpoints)
**Geographic Spread****Japan, China, Europe, Australia, USA** — Global Spread
**Response****Dual Therapy**, **Higher Dose Ceftriaxone**, **Alternative Regimens**, **Spectinomycin** (If Available)

Gonorrhoea in Pregnancy
RegimenSafety
-----------------
**Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat****Safe in All Trimesters** (Ceftriaxone B, Azithro B)

3️⃣ Disseminated Gonococcal Infection (DGI) — Treatment
PhaseRegimen
----------------
**Initial (Parenteral)****Ceftriaxone 1g IV/IM q24h** (or q12h if Severe) × **7 Days**
**Step-Down (Oral)****Cefixime 400mg BD PO** (When Clinical Improvement, Total 7 Days)
**Ceftriaxone Allergy****Gentamicin 240mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 2g PO Stat** (Then Azithro 1g Weekly × 2)
**Co-Infection Cover****Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7d** (Added for CT, MG)
**Joint Management****Repeated Aspiration**, Immobilisation, Physiotherapy
3️⃣ Neonatal Gonorrhoea
Ophthalmia Neonatorum
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Onset****Day 2-5** Post-Partum (Incubation 2-5 Days) — **Earlier than Chlamydia**
**Clinical****Hyperacute Conjunctivitis**: **Copious Purulent Discharge**, **Marked Eyelid Edema**, **Chemosis**, **Periorbital Cellulitis**
**Complications****Corneal Ulceration**, **Perforation**, **Blindness** (Rapid Onset, Hours)
**Diagnosis****Gram Stain** (Gram-Negative Intracellular Diplococci — **Presumptive**), **NAAT**, **Culture + AST**
**Treatment****Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg (Max 125mg) IM/IV Single Dose** + **Saline Irrigation**
**Prophylaxis****Erythromycin/Tetracycline Ointment** at Birth (Prevents GC/CT)
Neonatal Scalp Abscess
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Cause****Fetal Scalp Electrode** or **Forceps/Vacuum Trauma** → Gonococcal Inoculation
**Clinical****Abscess** (Fluctuant, Purulent), Cellulitis
**Treatment****Incision & Drainage** + **Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg IM/IV × 7 Days**
Neonatal DGI
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Onset****Day 7-14** Post-Partum
**Clinical**Sepsis, Arthritis (Septic), Meningitis, Dermatitis
**Treatment****Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg IV/IM q24h × 7-10 Days** + Supportive

  1. Current first-line treatment for uncomplicated genital gonorrhoea?

A. Ciprofloxacin 500mg BD × 7d B. Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat C. Azithromycin 1g Stat D. Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat Answer: B. Dual Therapy Mandatory (Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin).

  1. Pharyngeal gonorrhoea treatment differs from genital by:

A. Longer Duration B. Higher Ceftriaxone Dose (1g IM vs 500mg IM) C. Different Antibiotic D. Oral Ceftriaxone Answer: B. Pharyngeal: Ceftriaxone 1g IM (Higher Dose) + Mandatory TOC at 2 Weeks.

  1. Ciprofloxacin for gonorrhoea — current recommendation?

A. 1st Line B. Do Not Use — >50% Global Resistance C. Alternative if Allergic D. Use with Azithromycin Answer: B. >50% Global Resistance — Do Not Use Empirically; Only If AST Confirms Susceptibility.

  1. Disseminated Gonococcal Infection — classic clinical presentation?

  1. Neonatal gonococcal ophthalmia — typical onset vs chlamydia?

A. Same Onset B. GC: Day 2-5 (Hyperacute); CT: Day 5-14 (Mucopurulent) C. GC Later D. CT Earlier Answer: B. GC: Day 2-5 (Hyperacute, Copious Purulent); CT: Day 5-14 (Mucopurulent, Milder).

  1. Gonorrhoea test of cure — timing for pharyngeal infection?

A. 1 Week B. 2 Weeks (Mandatory) C. 4 Weeks D. 8 Weeks Answer: B. 2 Weeks (Mandatory for Pharyngeal GC) — Higher Failure Rate.

  1. Gonorrhoea in pregnancy — safe treatment?

A. Ciprofloxacin 500mg BD B. Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Azithromycin 1g PO C. Doxycycline 100mg BD D. Gentamicin IM Answer: B. Dual Therapy Safe in Pregnancy; Doxy/Cipro/Genta Contraindicated.

  1. Ceftriaxone resistance in gonorrhoea — current status?

A. Non-Existent B. Emerging (MIC >0.125 mg/L), penA Mosaic Mutations C. Widespread >50% D. Only in Japan Answer: B. Emerging Globally (MIC >0.125 mg/L); penA Mosaic Mutations; Dual Therapy, Higher Dose, Alternatives.

  1. Gonorrhoea test of cure — minimum time after treatment?

A. 3 Days B. 2 Weeks C. 4 Weeks D. 8 Weeks Answer: B. 2 Weeks (GC TOC); CT = 4 Weeks.

  1. Partner notification for gonorrhoea — EPT complexity?
