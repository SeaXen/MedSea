## 2️⃣ STI Screening in HIV — Enhanced Protocols

### Screening Frequency (BHIVA/EACS/WHO)

| Population | Screening Interval | Tests |
|------------|-------------------|-------|
| **All HIV+** | **At Diagnosis + Annually** | **Syphilis (RPR/VDRL+TPPA), HCV (Anti-HCV+RNA), HBV (HBsAg/Anti-HBs), HSV (Clinical), Chlamydia/Gonorrhoea (NAAT 3-Site if MSM)** |
| **MSM** | **Every 3-6 Months** | **3-Site NAAT (Urethral, Pharyngeal, Rectal) GC/CT**; **Syphilis (RPR/VDRL+TPPA) Every 3-6m**; **HCV RNA Annually** |
| **Sex Workers** | **Every 3 Months** | **Full STI Screen (GC/CT 3-Site, Syphilis, HIV, HBV, HCV, HSV, Trichomonas)** |
| **Pregnant HIV+** | **Booking + 28-32w + Delivery** | **Syphilis, HBV, HCV, HIV VL, GC/CT (If Risk), HSV (If Lesions)** |
| **PrEP Users** | **Every 3 Months** | **HIV Ag/Ab, Syphilis, GC/CT (3-Site if MSM), HCV (If Risk), Renal Function** |

> **Key**: **3-Site Testing (Urethral, Pharyngeal, Rectal) for GC/CT in MSM**; **Syphilis Every 3-6m in MSM**; **HCV RNA Annually if Risk**

## 3️⃣ STI Co-infection Management in HIV

### Syphilis in HIV

| Aspect | HIV-Specific Management |
|--------|-------------------------|
| **Serology** | **Atypical**: Lower RPR Titres, Delayed Seroconversion, **Prozone Phenomenon**, **Biologic False Positives (BFP) More Common**, **IgM Less Reliable** |
| **Clinical** | **More Severe**: Multiple Chancres, Rapid Progression, **Neurosyphilis More Common/At Earlier Stage**, Ocular Syphilis, Relapse After Treatment |
| **CSF Examination** | **Mandatory at ALL Stages** (Higher Neurosyphilis Risk, Atypical CSF) |
| **Treatment** | **Same Penicillin Regimens**; **Longer Follow-Up (RPR 3, 6, 12, 24 Months)**; **CSF at 6 Months if Neurosyphilis** |
| **Jarisch-Herxheimer** | **More Frequent/Severe** (Cytokine Dysregulation) — **Prednisolone Prophylaxis Considered** |
| **Treatment Failure** | **More Common** — **CSF Mandatory if RPR No 4-Fold Decline by 12 Months** |

### HSV in HIV

| Aspect | HIV-Specific Management |
|--------|-------------------------|
| **Clinical** | **More Severe/Extensive/Chronic**: Deep Ulcers, Mucosal Involvement, Perianal/Rectal, Disseminated, Oesophagitis, Encephalitis |
| **Recurrences** | **More Frequent, Prolonged, Severe** |
| **Resistance** | **TK Mutations More Common** → **Acyclovir/Valacyclovir/Famciclovir Cross-Resistance** |
| **Treatment** | **Episodic: Acyclovir 400mg 5x Daily × 7-10d** (Higher Dose/Longer); **Suppressive: Valacyclovir 500mg BD** (Preferred); **Resistant → Foscarnet IV 40mg/kg q8h** |
| **Suppressive Therapy** | **Indicated for All HIV+ with HSV-2** (Reduces Recurrences, Shedding, HIV Transmission); **Valacyclovir 500mg BD** |

### HPV in HIV

| Aspect | HIV-Specific Management |
|--------|-------------------------|
| **Cervical/Anal Disease** | **Higher Prevalence, Multiple Types, ↑ Persistence, Faster CIN2+ Progression** |
| **Screening** | **Cervical: Annual Cytology + HPV (vs 3-5y General)**; **Anal: Annual Cytology + HPV (MSM/HIV+)** |
| **Vaccination** | **9vHPV: 3 Doses (0, 2, 6m) Regardless of Age**; **Catch-Up to Age 26 (MSM to 26)** |
| **Treatment** | **CIN: Same Excision (LLETZ)**; **Warts: More Extensive/Refractory → Surgical Preferred**; **Recurrence Higher** |

### HCV in HIV

| Aspect | HIV-Specific Management |
|--------|-------------------------|
| **DAA Therapy** | **Same Regimens/Durations as Mono-Infected** (SOF/VEL 12w, GLE/PIB 8w/12w, SOF/VEL/VOX 12w Retreatment) |
| **SVR Rates** | **>95%** (Same as Mono-Infected with Modern DAAs) |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Critical**: **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir ↔ Rifampicin (Contraindicated)**; **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir ↔ Certain ART (Check Liverpool Database)** |
| **ART Regimen Choice** | **Integrase Inhibitor Based (DTG, BIC, RAL) Preferred** (Fewest DAA Interactions) |
| **Reinfection** | **Higher Risk (Continued Risk Behaviours)** — **Annual HCV RNA Screen** |

### Mpox in HIV

| Aspect | HIV-Specific Management |
|--------|-------------------------|
| **Severity** | **CD4<200 = Severe, Prolonged, Disseminated, Higher Mortality** |
| **Antiviral** | **Tecovirimat Indicated (CD4<200/Severe)**; **Drug Interactions (Tecovirimat ↔ CYP3A4 Inducers/Inhibitors — Rifampicin, EFV, Boosted PIs)** |
| **Vaccination** | **MVA-BN Safe (2 Doses)** — **Prioritise HIV+ (CD4<200)** |
| **ART** | **Continue/Initiate ART** (Immune Reconstitution Critical) |

## 3️⃣ ART & STI Drug Interactions — Critical Reference

### Key Interaction Principles

| Mechanism | ART Classes Affected | STI Drugs Affected | Clinical Significance |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **CYP3A4 Inhibition** | **Boosted PIs (DRV/r, ATV/r, LPV/r)**, **Cobicistat** | **Tecovirimat, DAA (GLE/PIB, SOF/VEL), Azole Antifungals, Azithromycin** | **↑ STI Drug Levels → Toxicity** or **↑ ART Levels → Toxicity** |
| **CYP3A4 Induction** | **EFV, NVP, ETV, RPV, Rifampicin (TB Tx)** | **Tecovirimat, DAA (GLE/PIB, SOF/VEL), Azole Antifungals** | **↓ STI Drug Levels → Treatment Failure** |
| **Renal Tubular Secretion** | **TDF, TAF, 3TC, FTC** | **Acyclovir, Valacyclovir, Ganciclovir, Valganciclovir, Tenofovir** | **↑ Renal Toxicity** (Monitor eGFR) |
| **Bone Marrow Suppression** | **ZDV, TMP-SMX** | **Ganciclovir, Valganciclovir** | **↑ Neutropenia/Anemia** |

### High-Risk Interaction Pairs

| ART | STI Drug | Interaction | Management |
|-----|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Boosted PIs (DRV/r, ATV/r)** | **Tecovirimat** | **↑ Tecovirimat (CYP3A4 Inhibition)** | **Monitor Tecovirimat Toxicity; Consider Dose Reduction** |
| **EFV/NVP** | **Tecovirimat** | **↓ Tecovirimat (CYP3A4 Induction)** | **Avoid Combination; Use Alternative Antiviral** |
| **Boosted PIs** | **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir** | **↑ DAA Levels** | **Avoid; Use SOF/VEL Instead** |
| **DTG/BIC/RAL** | **Most STI Drugs** | **Minimal Interactions** | **Preferred ART for STI Co-treatment** |
| **Rifampicin (TB Tx)** | **All DAAs, Tecovirimat, Azole Antifungals** | **Strong Induction → Treatment Failure** | **Avoid; Use Rifabutin or Alternative TB Regimen** |

> **Key**: **DTG/BIC/RAL-Based ART = Fewest STI Drug Interactions**; **Always Check Liverpool HIV Drug Interactions Database**

## 4️⃣ Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome (IRIS) — STI-Related

### IRIS Definition
> **Paradoxical Worsening or New Onset of Symptoms** after ART Initiation due to **Immune Reconstitution** unmasking subclinical infection or exaggerated inflammatory response.

### STI-Associated IRIS

| STI | IRIS Presentation | Timing | Management |
|-----|-------------------|--------|------------|
| **M. tuberculosis (TB-IRIS)** | Fevers, Lymphadenopathy, Worsening CXR, New Lesions | **2-8 Weeks Post-ART** | **Continue ART + TB Tx**; **Steroids (Prednisolone 1.5mg/kg) if Severe** |
| **Herpes Simplex (HSV-IRIS)** | Severe Mucocutaneous Ulcers, Oesophagitis, Encephalitis | **2-12 Weeks** | **Continue ART + Antivirals (Higher Dose/Longer)**; **Steroids if Severe** |
| **Varicella Zoster (VZV-IRIS)** | Disseminated Shingles, Meningitis | **4-12 Weeks** | **IV Acyclovir/Foscarnet + Steroids** |
| **Cryptococcal Meningitis (CM-IRIS)** | Worsening Headache, ICP ↑, Neurological Decline | **2-10 Weeks** | **ART Delay if Possible; Steroids + Amphotericin/Flucytosine** |
| **Syphilis (Syphilis-IRIS)** | Worsening RPR, New Lesions, Neurosyphilis Flare | **4-12 Weeks** | **Continue ART + Penicillin; Steroids if Neurosyphilis Flare** |
| **HPV (Warts/CIN-IRIS)** | Rapid Wart Growth, New CIN Lesions | **3-12 Months** | **Standard Treatment (Excision/Imiquimod); Continue ART** |
| **HCV (Hepatitis Flare)** | ALT Flare >5x ULN Post-ART | **4-12 Weeks** | **Monitor; Continue ART + DAA if Indicated**; **Steroids Rarely Needed** |

### IRIS Diagnostic Criteria (General)
1. **New/Worsening Symptoms** after ART Initiation
2. **Temporal Association** (Typically 2-12 Weeks Post-ART)
3. **CD4 Rise / HIV VL Drop** (Immune Reconstitution)
4. **Exclusion of Drug Toxicity, Treatment Failure, New Infection**

### IRIS Management Principles
1. **Continue ART** (Except Life-Threatening CM-IRIS → Delay ART if Possible)
2. **Treat Underlying Infection** (Appropriate Antimicrobials)
3. **Anti-Inflammatory** (Corticosteroids if Severe/Organ-Threatening)
4. **Monitor** (Clinical, Lab, Imaging)

## 4️⃣ PrEP/PEP Impact on STI Epidemiology

### PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis) for HIV
| Regimen | Efficacy | STI Impact |
|---------|----------|------------|
| **TDF/FTC Daily** | **>99%** (Adherent) | **↑ STI Incidence** (Risk Compensation: ↓ Condom Use, ↑ Partners) |
| **Event-Driven (2-1-1)** | **High (MSM)** | Similar Risk Compensation |
| **Cabotegravir LA (Bimonthly IM)** | **>99%** | **STI Monitoring Critical** (No Daily Pill Reminder) |

### PrEP & STI Monitoring
| Recommendation | Frequency |
|----------------|-----------|
| **HIV Testing** | **Every 3 Months** |
| **Syphilis (RPR/VDRL+TPPA)** | **Every 3 Months** |
| **GC/CT (3-Site if MSM)** | **Every 3 Months** |
| **HCV RNA** | **Annually (If Risk)** |
| **Renal Function (eGFR)** | **Every 6 Months (TDF/FTC)** |
| **STI Counselling** | **Every Visit** (Condoms, Vaccination, Doxy-PEP) |

### Doxy-PEP (Post-Exposure Doxycycline for Bacterial STIs)
| Regimen | Efficacy | Population |
|---------|----------|------------|
| **Doxycycline 200mg Single Dose Within 72h Post-Sex** | **GC ↓55%, CT ↓88%, Syphilis ↓87%** (DoxyPEP Trial) | **MSM, Trans Women on PrEP or Living with HIV** |
| **Caveats** | **Tetracycline Resistance (M. genitalium, Commensals)**, **GI Side Effects**, **Photosensitivity** | **Not Standard of Care Yet (Guidelines Emerging)** |

## 4️⃣ STI Management in Specific HIV Clinical Scenarios

### Acute HIV Infection (Primary HIV)
| STI Screening | Timing |
|--------------|--------|
| **Full STI Screen** | **At HIV Diagnosis** (Syphilis, HCV, HBV, HSV, GC/CT 3-Site, Trichomonas, HPV) |
| **HCV RNA** | **At Diagnosis + 6 Months** (Acute HCV Co-infection Possible) |
| **Syphilis RPR/VDRL** | **At Diagnosis + 3 Months** (Window Period) |

### Advanced HIV (CD4<200)
| STI | Management Priority |
|-----|---------------------|
| **OIs (PCP, Toxo, Crypto, MAC)** | **Priority 1** (Life-Threatening) |
| **Syphilis** | **CSF Mandatory, Penicillin, Monitor RPR Monthly × 3** |
| **HSV** | **Suppressive Valacyclovir 500mg BD** (Reduce Reactivation) |
| **CMV** | **Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir (Retinitis/Colitis)** |
| **Mpox** | **Tecovirimat Indicated** |
| **Cryptosporidium/Microsporidia** | **ART = Definitive Therapy; Nitazoxanide/Albendazole Adjunct** |

## 5️⃣ STI & ART Drug-Drug Interaction Quick Reference

| STI Drug | Key ART Interactions | Monitoring |
|----------|---------------------|------------|
| **Tecovirimat** | **↑ with CYP3A4 Inhibitors (Boosted PIs, Cobicistat); ↓ with CYP3A4 Inducers (EFV, Rifampicin)** | LFT, Tecovirimat Level if Available |
| **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir** | **Contraindicated with Rifampicin; ↑ with Boosted PIs** | LFT, HCV RNA |
| **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir** | **↓ with EFV/Rifampicin; Few Interactions with INSTI** | HCV RNA, Renal Function (SOF) |
| **Acyclovir/Valacyclovir** | **↑ Renal Toxicity with TDF/TAF** | Renal Function (eGFR) |
| **Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir** | **↑ Myelosuppression with ZDV/TMP-SMX** | CBC, Renal Function |
| **Foscarnet** | **↑ Renal Toxicity with TDF/TAF, Aminoglycosides** | Renal Function, Electrolytes (Ca, Mg, K, P) |
| **Azithromycin** | **QT Prolongation with Certain ART (RPV, LPV/r)** | ECG if Risk Factors |
| **Fluconazole** | **↑ Levels with Boosted PIs; CYP3A4 Inhibition** | LFT, Drug Levels if Available |

> **Rule of Thumb**: **INSTI-Based ART (DTG, BIC, RAL) = Fewest STI Drug Interactions**; **Always Check Liverpool HIV Drug Interactions Database Before Co-Prescribing**

## 5. 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"HIV = Always Worse STI Outcomes"** | **Mostly True**, but **ART Restores Immunity** → **IRIS = Paradoxical Worsening** (Not Treatment Failure) |
| **"HIV = Worse Syphilis Serology"** | **Yes (Atypical)** but **Penicillin Still Works**; **CSF Mandatory All Stages** |
| **"HIV = Worse HSV, No Suppressive Therapy"** | **NO.** **Suppressive Valacyclovir 500mg BD Indicated for ALL HIV+ with HSV-2** |
| **"HCV DAA = Different in HIV"** | **NO.** **Same Regimens/Durations**; **Just Check ART Interactions (Liverpool DB)** |
| **"IRIS = Treatment Failure"** | **NO.** **Paradoxical Immune Reconstitution** → **Continue ART + Treat Infection + Steroids if Severe** |
| **"IRIS = Stop ART"** | **NO.** **Continue ART (Except Life-Threatening CM-IRIS Where Delay May Be Needed)** |
| **"PrEP = No STI Risk"** | **NO.** **Risk Compensation → ↑ STIs (GC/CT/Syphilis ↑ in PrEP Users)** |
| **"Doxy-PEP = Standard of Care"** | **NO.** **Emerging Evidence (DoxyPEP Trial); Guidelines Emerging; Resistance Concerns (M. genitalium)** |
| **"All ART = Same STI Interactions"** | **NO.** **INSTI (DTG/BIC/RAL) = Fewest Interactions**; **Boosted PIs = Most Interactions** |
| **"Mpox = No Vaccine for HIV+"** | **NO.** **MVA-BN Safe (2 Doses) for HIV+ (Even CD4<200); ACAM2000 Contraindicated** |

> **Mnemonic: HIV-STI SYNERGY MASTER**  
> **H**IV-STI Synergy: **Biological (Mucosal Disruption, Immunosuppression) + Epidemiological (Shared Networks)**  
> **I**n HIV: **STIs More Severe, Atypical, Faster Progression, Higher Reactivation**  
> **V**iral STIs in HIV: **HSV (Severe, Suppressive Valacyclovir 500mg BD), HPV (Annual Anal Cyto+HPV), HCV (DAA Same Regimens, Check Interactions)**  
> **S**yphilis in HIV: **Atypical Serology (Low RPR, Prozone, BFP), Neurosyphilis Common → LP Mandatory All Stages**  
> **I**RIS: **Paradoxical Worsening Post-ART (TB, HSV, Syphilis, Crypto, CMV, HCV Flare)** → **Continue ART + Antimicrobials + Steroids if Severe**  
> **D**rug Interactions: **Tecovirimat ↔ CYP3A4 (PIs ↑, EFV ↓); DAAs ↔ Rifampicin (Contra); INSTI = Fewest Interactions**  
> **P**rEP & STIs: **Risk Compensation → ↑ STIs; 3m Screen (HIV, Syphilis, GC/CT 3-Site, HCV); Doxy-PEP Emerging**  
> **S**creening in HIV: **MSM: 3-Site GC/CT q3-6m, Syphilis q3-6m, HCV RNA Annually**  
> **Y** (Why Integrated Services?): **Concentric Epidemics, Shared Risk, Efficiency, Better Outcomes**  
> **N**eurosyphilis HIV: **LP Mandatory All Stages; Penicillin G IV 18-24MU/d × 10-14d**  
> **E**PI/PEP: **Doxy-PEP (200mg ≤72h Post-Sex) → GC↓55%, CT↓88%, Syphilis↓87% (MSM/Trans Women on PrEP/HIV)**  
> **R**efrence: **Liverpool HIV Drug Interactions Database = Gold Standard for ART-STI Co-Prescribing Check**  
> **G**uidelines: **WHO/BHIVA/EACS/CDC → Integrated STI/HIV Services, Combined Screening, Shared Decision-Making**

## 10. 📌 Summary
- **HIV-STI Synergy**: **Biological (Mucosal Disruption, Immunosuppression) + Epidemiological (Shared Risk Networks)** → **↑ Acquisition, Severity, Reactivation**
- **Syphilis in HIV**: **Atypical Serology (Low RPR, Prozone, BFP)**, **Neurosyphilis More Common** → **LP Mandatory All Stages**; **Same Penicillin Regimens**, **Longer Follow-Up (RPR Monthly × 3, CSF at 6m)**
- **HSV in HIV**: **Severe, Extensive, Recurrent, TK Resistance** → **Suppressive Valacyclovir 500mg BD Indicated for ALL HIV+**
- **HPV in HIV**: **↑ Persistence, CIN2+, Anal Cancer** → **Annual Anal Cytology+HPV (MSM/HIV+), 3-Dose 9vHPV Any Age**
- **HCV in HIV**: **DAA Same Regimens** (SOF/VEL 12w, GLE/PIB 8w/12w, SOF/VEL/VOX 12w) — **Check ART Interactions (Liverpool Database)**
- **Mpox in HIV**: **CD4<200 = Severe** → **Tecovirimat Indicated**; **MVA-BN Safe (2 Doses)**; **ART Continue**
- **IRIS**: **Paradoxical Worsening Post-ART** (TB, HSV, Syphilis, CM, Crypto, HCV Flare) → **Continue ART + Treat Infection + Steroids if Severe/Organ-Threatening**
- **Drug Interactions**: **Tecovirimat ↔ CYP3A4 (Boosted PIs ↑, EFV ↓); DAAs ↔ Rifampicin (Contraindicated); INSTI (DTG/BIC/RAL) = Fewest Interactions** — **Always Check Liverpool HIV Drug Interactions Database**
- **PrEP & STIs**: **Risk Compensation → ↑ STI Incidence**; **3-Monthly Screening (HIV, Syphilis, GC/CT 3-Site, HCV RNA)**; **Doxy-PEP Emerging (GC↓55%, CT↓88%, Syphilis↓87%)**
- **Screening in HIV**: **MSM: 3-Site GC/CT q3-6m, Syphilis q3-6m, HCV RNA Annually**; **Pregnant: Booking + 28-32w + Delivery**; **All HIV+: Annual Full STI Screen**
