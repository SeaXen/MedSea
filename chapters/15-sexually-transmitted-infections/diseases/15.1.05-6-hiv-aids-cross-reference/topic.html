3️⃣ STI Co-infection Management in HIV
Syphilis in HIV
AspectHIV-Specific Management
---------------------------------
**Serology****Atypical**: Lower RPR Titres, Delayed Seroconversion, **Prozone Phenomenon**, **Biologic False Positives (BFP) More Common**, **IgM Less Reliable**
**Clinical****More Severe**: Multiple Chancres, Rapid Progression, **Neurosyphilis More Common/At Earlier Stage**, Ocular Syphilis, Relapse After Treatment
**CSF Examination****Mandatory at ALL Stages** (Higher Neurosyphilis Risk, Atypical CSF)
**Treatment****Same Penicillin Regimens**; **Longer Follow-Up (RPR 3, 6, 12, 24 Months)**; **CSF at 6 Months if Neurosyphilis**
**Jarisch-Herxheimer****More Frequent/Severe** (Cytokine Dysregulation) — **Prednisolone Prophylaxis Considered**
**Treatment Failure****More Common** — **CSF Mandatory if RPR No 4-Fold Decline by 12 Months**
HSV in HIV
AspectHIV-Specific Management
---------------------------------
**Clinical****More Severe/Extensive/Chronic**: Deep Ulcers, Mucosal Involvement, Perianal/Rectal, Disseminated, Oesophagitis, Encephalitis
**Recurrences****More Frequent, Prolonged, Severe**
**Resistance****TK Mutations More Common** → **Acyclovir/Valacyclovir/Famciclovir Cross-Resistance**
**Treatment****Episodic: Acyclovir 400mg 5x Daily × 7-10d** (Higher Dose/Longer); **Suppressive: Valacyclovir 500mg BD** (Preferred); **Resistant → Foscarnet IV 40mg/kg q8h**
**Suppressive Therapy****Indicated for All HIV+ with HSV-2** (Reduces Recurrences, Shedding, HIV Transmission); **Valacyclovir 500mg BD**
HPV in HIV
AspectHIV-Specific Management
---------------------------------
**Cervical/Anal Disease****Higher Prevalence, Multiple Types, ↑ Persistence, Faster CIN2+ Progression**
**Screening****Cervical: Annual Cytology + HPV (vs 3-5y General)**; **Anal: Annual Cytology + HPV (MSM/HIV+)**
**Vaccination****9vHPV: 3 Doses (0, 2, 6m) Regardless of Age**; **Catch-Up to Age 26 (MSM to 26)**
**Treatment****CIN: Same Excision (LLETZ)**; **Warts: More Extensive/Refractory → Surgical Preferred**; **Recurrence Higher**
HCV in HIV
AspectHIV-Specific Management
---------------------------------
**DAA Therapy****Same Regimens/Durations as Mono-Infected** (SOF/VEL 12w, GLE/PIB 8w/12w, SOF/VEL/VOX 12w Retreatment)
**SVR Rates****>95%** (Same as Mono-Infected with Modern DAAs)
**Drug Interactions****Critical**: **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir ↔ Rifampicin (Contraindicated)**; **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir ↔ Certain ART (Check Liverpool Database)**
**ART Regimen Choice****Integrase Inhibitor Based (DTG, BIC, RAL) Preferred** (Fewest DAA Interactions)
**Reinfection****Higher Risk (Continued Risk Behaviours)** — **Annual HCV RNA Screen**
Mpox in HIV
AspectHIV-Specific Management
---------------------------------
**Severity****CD4<200 = Severe, Prolonged, Disseminated, Higher Mortality**
**Antiviral****Tecovirimat Indicated (CD4<200/Severe)**; **Drug Interactions (Tecovirimat ↔ CYP3A4 Inducers/Inhibitors — Rifampicin, EFV, Boosted PIs)**
**Vaccination****MVA-BN Safe (2 Doses)** — **Prioritise HIV+ (CD4<200)**
**ART****Continue/Initiate ART** (Immune Reconstitution Critical)

High-Risk Interaction Pairs
ARTSTI DrugInteractionManagement
----------------------------------------

**EFV/NVP****Tecovirimat****↓ Tecovirimat (CYP3A4 Induction)****Avoid Combination; Use Alternative Antiviral**

4️⃣ Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome (IRIS) — STI-Related
IRIS Definition
Paradoxical Worsening or New Onset of Symptoms after ART Initiation due to Immune Reconstitution unmasking subclinical infection or exaggerated inflammatory response.
STI-Associated IRIS
STIIRIS PresentationTimingManagement
--------------------------------------------
**M. tuberculosis (TB-IRIS)**Fevers, Lymphadenopathy, Worsening CXR, New Lesions**2-8 Weeks Post-ART****Continue ART + TB Tx**; **Steroids (Prednisolone 1.5mg/kg) if Severe**
**Herpes Simplex (HSV-IRIS)**Severe Mucocutaneous Ulcers, Oesophagitis, Encephalitis**2-12 Weeks****Continue ART + Antivirals (Higher Dose/Longer)**; **Steroids if Severe**
**Varicella Zoster (VZV-IRIS)**Disseminated Shingles, Meningitis**4-12 Weeks****IV Acyclovir/Foscarnet + Steroids**
**Cryptococcal Meningitis (CM-IRIS)**Worsening Headache, ICP ↑, Neurological Decline**2-10 Weeks****ART Delay if Possible; Steroids + Amphotericin/Flucytosine**
**Syphilis (Syphilis-IRIS)**Worsening RPR, New Lesions, Neurosyphilis Flare**4-12 Weeks****Continue ART + Penicillin; Steroids if Neurosyphilis Flare**
**HPV (Warts/CIN-IRIS)**Rapid Wart Growth, New CIN Lesions**3-12 Months****Standard Treatment (Excision/Imiquimod); Continue ART**
**HCV (Hepatitis Flare)**ALT Flare >5x ULN Post-ART**4-12 Weeks****Monitor; Continue ART + DAA if Indicated**; **Steroids Rarely Needed**
IRIS Diagnostic Criteria (General)
  1. New/Worsening Symptoms after ART Initiation
  2. Temporal Association (Typically 2-12 Weeks Post-ART)
  3. CD4 Rise / HIV VL Drop (Immune Reconstitution)
  4. Exclusion of Drug Toxicity, Treatment Failure, New Infection
IRIS Management Principles
  1. Continue ART (Except Life-Threatening CM-IRIS → Delay ART if Possible)
  2. Treat Underlying Infection (Appropriate Antimicrobials)
  3. Anti-Inflammatory (Corticosteroids if Severe/Organ-Threatening)
  4. Monitor (Clinical, Lab, Imaging)

--------------------------

  • Syphilis in HIV: Atypical Serology (Low RPR, Prozone, BFP), Neurosyphilis More Common → LP Mandatory All Stages; Same Penicillin Regimens, Longer Follow-Up (RPR Monthly × 3, CSF at 6m)

  • HPV in HIV: ↑ Persistence, CIN2+, Anal Cancer → Annual Anal Cytology+HPV (MSM/HIV+), 3-Dose 9vHPV Any Age
  • HCV in HIV: DAA Same Regimens (SOF/VEL 12w, GLE/PIB 8w/12w, SOF/VEL/VOX 12w) — Check ART Interactions (Liverpool Database)
  • Mpox in HIV: CD4<200 = Severe → Tecovirimat Indicated; MVA-BN Safe (2 Doses); ART Continue
  • IRIS: Paradoxical Worsening Post-ART (TB, HSV, Syphilis, CM, Crypto, HCV Flare) → Continue ART + Treat Infection + Steroids if Severe/Organ-Threatening
  • Drug Interactions: Tecovirimat ↔ CYP3A4 (Boosted PIs ↑, EFV ↓); DAAs ↔ Rifampicin (Contraindicated); INSTI (DTG/BIC/RAL) = Fewest Interactions — Always Check Liverpool HIV Drug Interactions Database
  • PrEP & STIs: Risk Compensation → ↑ STI Incidence; 3-Monthly Screening (HIV, Syphilis, GC/CT 3-Site, HCV RNA); Doxy-PEP Emerging (GC↓55%, CT↓88%, Syphilis↓87%)
  • Screening in HIV: MSM: 3-Site GC/CT q3-6m, Syphilis q3-6m, HCV RNA Annually; Pregnant: Booking + 28-32w + Delivery; All HIV+: Annual Full STI Screen
11 ❓ MCQs (10)
  1. Syphilis in HIV — serological hallmark?

A. High RPR Titres B. Atypical: Low RPR, Prozone, Biologic False Positives C. Rapid Seroconversion D. Negative TPPA Answer: B. Atypical Serology: Low/Erratic RPR, Prozone Effect, More BFP, Delayed Seroconversion.

  1. Neurosyphilis in HIV — CSF examination indication?

A. Only if Neurological Symptoms B. Mandatory at ALL Stages C. Only Late Latent D. Only if RPR >1:32 Answer: B. LP Mandatory at ALL Stages in HIV (Higher Neurosyphilis Risk, Atypical Presentation).

  1. HSV suppressive therapy in HIV — indication?

A. Only if ≥10 Recurrences/Year B. All HIV+ with HSV-2 C. Only if CD4<200 D. Not Recommended Answer: B. Suppressive Valacyclovir 500mg BD Indicated for ALL HIV+ with HSV-2 (Reduces Shedding, Recurrence, HIV Transmission).

  1. HCV DAA regimen in HIV — same as mono-infected?

A. No, Different B. Yes, Same Regimens/Durations (Check ART Interactions) C. Only IFN-Based D. Only Ribavirin-Based Answer: B. Same Pan-Genotypic Regimens (SOF/VEL 12w, GLE/PIB 8w/12w, SOF/VEL/VOX 12w); Check ART Interactions (Liverpool DB).

  1. Mpox in HIV — tecovirimat indication?

A. All HIV+ B. CD4<200 or Severe Disease C. Only CD4<50 D. Never Answer: B. CD4<200 or Severe Disease → Tecovirimat 600mg BD × 14d (Check CYP3A4 Interactions).

  1. IRIS definition?

A. Treatment Failure B. Paradoxical Worsening/Unmasking Post-ART Due to Immune Reconstitution C. Drug Toxicity D. New Infection Answer: B. Paradoxical Worsening/Unmasking Within Weeks of ART Initiation Due to Immune Reconstitution.

  1. IRIS management — key principle?

A. Stop ART B. Continue ART + Treat Underlying Infection + Steroids if Severe/Organ-Threatening C. Stop ART + Treat D. Reduce ART Dose Answer: B. Continue ART (Except Life-Threatening CM-IRIS) + Treat Infection + Steroids if Severe.

  1. PrEP & STI monitoring — recommended frequency for MSM?

A. Annually B. Every 3 Months (HIV, Syphilis, GC/CT 3-Site, HCV RNA Annually, Renal q6m) C. Every 6 Months D. Only at Initiation Answer: B. Every 3 Months (HIV Ag/Ab, Syphilis RPR/VDRL+TPPA, GC/CT 3-Site NAAT, HCV RNA Annually, Renal q6m).

  1. Tecovirimat ART interaction — boosted PIs?

A. ↓ Tecovirimat B. ↑ Tecovirimat (CYP3A4 Inhibition) C. No Interaction D. Contraindicated Answer: B. Boosted PIs (CYP3A4 Inhibitors) → ↑ Tecovirimat Levels; Monitor Toxicity/Dose Adjust.

  1. Doxy-PEP — efficacy for bacterial STIs?

A. GC ↓90%, CT ↓90%, Syphilis ↓90% B. GC ↓55%, CT ↓88%, Syphilis ↓87% (DoxyPEP Trial) C. No Significant Reduction D. Only Syphilis Reduced Answer: B. DoxyPEP Trial: GC ↓55%, CT ↓88%, Syphilis ↓87% (MSM/Trans Women on PrEP/HIV).

12 📋 SBAs (10)
  1. HIV+ MSM, CD4 350, on TDF/FTC/DTG PrEP, diagnosed with Mpox. Management?

A. Tecovirimat 600mg BD × 14d B. Supportive Care + Isolation (Mild, CD4>200) C. MVA-BN Vaccine Only D. Ceftriaxone Answer: B. Mild Mpox (CD4>200) → Supportive Care + Isolation; Tecovirimat Only if Severe/High-Risk.

  1. HIV+ patient on ART 4 weeks develops fever, worsening RPR, new rash. Diagnosis?

A. Drug Rash B. Syphilis IRIS C. Acute HIV Seroconversion D. Secondary Syphilis Answer: B. Syphilis IRIS (Paradoxical Worsening 2-12w Post-ART); Continue ART + Penicillin + Consider Steroids.

  1. HIV+ patient with HSV-2, 8 recurrences/year. Suppressive therapy?

A. Acyclovir 400mg BD B. Valacyclovir 500mg OD (or BD) C. No Suppression D. Foscarnet Answer: B. Valacyclovir 500mg OD (Preferred) or Acyclovir 400mg BD; Indicated for ALL HIV+ with HSV-2.

  1. HIV+ patient on efavirenz-based ART needs HCV DAA. Preferred regimen?

A. Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir B. Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir (Fewer Interactions with EFV) C. SOF/VEL/VOX D. Interferon+Ribavirin Answer: B. SOF/VEL Preferred with EFV (GLE/PIB Contraindicated with Rifampicin, EFV Significant Interaction).

  1. HIV+ MSM on PrEP, 3-month STI screen. Tests?

A. HIV Only B. HIV + Syphilis C. HIV + Syphilis + 3-Site GC/CT NAAT + HCV RNA (Annually) D. HIV + HCV Only Answer: C. 3-Site GC/CT + Syphilis + HCV RNA (Annually) + Renal Function q6m.
