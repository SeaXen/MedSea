## 📝 Exam Answer Templates

### Viva: "Describe the stages of syphilis and their management."
1. **Primary**: Chancre (Painless, Indurated), Inguinal Lymphadenopathy — **Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Single Dose**
2. **Secondary**: Rash (Palms/Soles), Condylomata Lata, Mucous Patches, Generalised Lymphadenopathy — **Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Single Dose**
3. **Early Latent** (<1y) — Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Single Dose
4. **Late Latent** (>1y or Unknown) — **Benzathine Penicillin G 2.4MU IM Weekly × 3 Doses**
5. **Tertiary** (Gummatous, Cardiovascular, Neurosyphilis) — **Penicillin G IV 18-24MU Daily × 14-21 Days**
6. **Neurosyphilis** — **Penicillin G IV 18-24MU Daily × 14 Days** (+ Probenecid)
7. **Congenital** — Penicillin G IV/Aqueous 50,000U/kg/dose q12h × 10 Days (or 50,000U/kg/dose q12h × 10 Days)
7. **Penicillin Allergy** — Desensitisation, then Penicillin; Ceftriaxone Alternative (Limited Data)

### Viva: "Describe the syndromic management of urethral discharge."
1. **Assess** — History, Examination (Urethral Discharge, Meatal Redness)
2. **Investigate** — NAAT for GC/CT (First-Line), Gram Stain (Gram-Negative Diplococci = GC)
2. **Treat Syndromically** — Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat + **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7 Days** (Covers GC + CT)
3. **Partner Notification** — All Partners Last 60 Days (EPT if Available)
9. **Follow-Up** — Test of Cure (NAAT at 2-4 Weeks for GC), Partner Treatment Verification

### Viva: "Describe the management of gonorrhoea with antimicrobial resistance."
1. **First-Line** — **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat** (Dual Therapy)
2. **Ceftriaxone Resistance** — **Ceftriaxone 1g IM + Azithromycin 2g** (If Susceptible), **Gentamicin 240mg IM + Azithromycin 2g** (Alternative)
4. **Pharyngeal Gonorrhoea** — Higher Dose Ceftriaxone (1g IM), Test of Cure Mandatory (NAAT at 2 Weeks)
4. **Test of Cure** — Mandatory for Pharyngeal, 2 Weeks Post-Treatment (NAAT)
4. **Partner Notification** — All Partners Last 60 Days, EPT if Available
