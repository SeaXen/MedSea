# Painful diabetic neuropathy

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Neuropathic Pain**

## 1. Definition

Painful diabetic neuropathy (PDN) is a common complication of diabetes (affects ~25% of diabetic patients with neuropathy), typically distal symmetric sensorimotor polyneuropathy with burning, electric, pins-and-needles pain in feet (worse at night). Risk factors: poor glycaemic control, duration of diabetes, older age, smoking, alcohol. Treatment: NICE-recommended first-line: TCAs (amitriptyline), SNRIs (duloxetine — first-line in many guidelines), gabapentinoids (gabapentin, pregabalin). Prevention: optimal glycaemic control, lifestyle. Second-line: tramadol, capsaicin, lidocaine. Third-line: neuromodulation (spinal cord stimulation).

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Painful diabetic neuropathy affects 30% of patients with diabetes mellitus, often as distal symmetric polyneuropathy (DSPN) with burning, shooting, electric, pins-and-needles in stocking-and-glove distribution (feet first, hands later). Worse at night. Associated with autonomic features (orthostatic hypotension, gastroparesis, bladder/bowel dysfunction), and other complications (retinopathy, nephropathy). Painful in only 10-20% of all diabetic neuropathy, but disabling. Risk factors: duration of diabetes, poor glycaemic control, obesity, smoking, hypertension.


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
HbA1c (control), B12 (exclude deficiency, especially on metformin), folate, renal function (U&E), TFT, autoimmune screen, paraprotein (exclude other causes), nerve conduction studies (large fibre, axonal sensory), MRI spine (if asymmetric/radicular to exclude compression), skin biopsy (small fibre neuropathy — IENFD).

### Management
Optimise glycaemic control (HbA1c <7%; intensive control reduces progression). First-line: duloxetine 60-120mg/day (NICE, EMA-approved for diabetic neuropathy), gabapentin, pregabalin, amitriptyline. Combination therapy if inadequate. Topical: lidocaine 5% patch, capsaicin 8% patch. Tramadol, strong opioids (third-line). Non-pharm: TENS, foot care, psychological support. Treat autonomic features (orthostatic hypotension, gastroparesis).

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line systemic for painful diabetic neuropathy? A: Duloxetine (60-120mg/day), gabapentin, pregabalin, amitriptyline.

2. 2. Q: Why duloxetine? A: SNRI; FDA/EMA approved; addresses both pain and depression (common comorbidity).

3. 3. Q: Prevention? A: Intensive glycaemic control (HbA1c <7%); lifestyle; early screening.

4. 4. Q: Autonomic features? A: Orthostatic hypotension, gastroparesis, bladder/bowel dysfunction, sweating, erectile dysfunction.

5. 5. Q: Distinction from B12 neuropathy? A: B12: subacute combined degeneration (posterior column + corticospinal + peripheral), macrocytosis; DM: stocking-glove, no dorsal column signs, normal MCV.
