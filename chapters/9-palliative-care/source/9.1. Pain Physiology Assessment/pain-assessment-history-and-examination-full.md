# Pain assessment history and examination

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Pain Physiology & Assessment**

## 1. Definition

Pain assessment in palliative care requires comprehensive history (SOCRATES + oncology-specific: site, severity, character, timing, aggravating/relieving, previous treatment, impact on function/mood/sleep) and examination. Multidimensional tools: Brief Pain Inventory (BPI), McGill Pain Questionnaire. Pain in non-communicating patients: PAINAD, Doloplus-2, FLACC (for children). Assessment must consider total pain (physical, emotional, social, spiritual).

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Pain history uses SOCRATES (Site, Onset, Character, Radiation, Associations, Timing, Exacerbating/relieving, Severity) plus cancer-specific (incident, breakthrough, end-of-dose failure). Functional impact: activities of daily living, work, sleep, mood, relationships. Psychosocial: beliefs, fears, cultural, spiritual. Examination: observation (posture, facial expression, gait, skin changes), palpation (tenderness, trigger points, allodynia), percussion, range of motion, neurological (sensation, motor, reflexes), musculoskeletal, abdominal. Severity by patient report on 0-10 scale. Impact: BPI, QoL.


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Pain assessment tools: NRS, VAS, VRS, McGill, BPI, PAIN-AD (dementia), FLACC (non-verbal children), PPP (paediatric). Disease-specific: imaging, bloods, neurophysiology. Functional: Barthel Index, Karnofsky Performance Status, ECOG.

### Management
Comprehensive assessment is the foundation of effective pain management: takes time, builds trust, identifies cause. Use validated tools. Address psychosocial and spiritual (total pain). Reassess regularly and document.

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: What is SOCRATES for pain history? A: Site, Onset, Character, Radiation, Associations, Timing, Exacerbating/relieving, Severity.

2. 2. Q: Best tool for non-communicating patient? A: PAIN-AD (Pain in Advanced Dementia) or FLACC (children).

3. 3. Q: Components of total pain? A: Physical + psychological + social + spiritual.

4. 4. Q: Why use validated pain tools? A: Standardised, comparable, sensitive to change, audit.

5. 5. Q: Cancer pain sub-types? A: Background, breakthrough, incident, end-of-dose failure.


## 7. Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **SOCRATES** = Site, Onset, Character, Radiation, Associations, Time course, Exacerbating/relieving, Severity
