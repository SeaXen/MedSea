# Cancer pain syndromes summary

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Cancer Pain Syndromes**

## 1. Definition

Cancer pain syndromes can be categorised by mechanism: nociceptive (somatic, visceral), neuropathic (plexus infiltration, MSCC, PHN, chemotherapy-induced), mixed (most common), incident (movement-triggered), breakthrough (spontaneous, end-of-dose). Common cancer pain syndromes: bone metastases (incident pain, NSAID+bisphosphonates+RT), pancreatic cancer (visceral, coeliac plexus), MSCC (emergency, dexamethasone), brachial/lumbosacral plexopathy (neuropathic), malignant bowel obstruction, post-radiation (radiation-induced, MISP), chemotherapy-induced neuropathy, malignant wound. Stepwise approach: WHO ladder + adjuvant, opioid rotation, breakthrough dose, interventional (nerve blocks, IT analgesia), palliative RT, palliative sedation.

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Cancer pain syndromes are classified by mechanism and site: nociceptive (somatic — bone, soft tissue; visceral — organ capsular, hollow viscus), neuropathic (nerve compression, infiltration, post-treatment), incident (movement-related, e.g., pathological fracture), breakthrough (transient exacerbation on stable background). Common syndromes: bone metastases (60-80%), neuropathic (30-40% — plexus infiltration, post-radiation, post-chemo), visceral (pancreatic, hepatic, peritoneal), incident (pathological fracture, movement).


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Comprehensive pain assessment: site, character, mechanism (nociceptive vs neuropathic), triggers, temporal pattern, associated features. Disease-specific imaging, biomarkers. Multidisciplinary team assessment.

### Management
Total pain concept: physical + psychological + social + spiritual. WHO ladder + adjuvants (gabapentin for neuropathic, bisphosphonates for bone, anticholinergics for colic). Disease-modifying: surgery, chemotherapy, radiotherapy. Interventional: nerve blocks, intrathecal pump, spinal cord stimulation. Non-pharm: TENS, CBT, physiotherapy, complementary therapy. Psychosocial and spiritual support.

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Most common cancer pain syndrome? A: Bone metastases (60-80%); neuropathic 30-40%.

2. 2. Q: 4 main mechanisms of cancer pain? A: Nociceptive (somatic, visceral), neuropathic, mixed, incident.

3. 3. Q: Most common cancers with bone mets? A: Breast, prostate, lung, kidney, thyroid, myeloma.

4. 4. Q: Cancer-related neuropathic pain causes? A: Tumour compression/infiltration, surgery, chemotherapy (vinca, taxane, platinum), radiotherapy.

5. 5. Q: WHO ladder? A: Mild → paracetamol ± NSAID; Moderate → weak opioid (codeine, tramadol); Severe → strong opioid (morphine, oxycodone, fentanyl).
