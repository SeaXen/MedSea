CHAPTER 9 PALLIATIVE AND SUPPORTIVE CARE / 9.5. CANCER PAIN SYNDROMES
Patient Controlled Analgesia Pca Full
1 Definition

Patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) is a method of pain control allowing the patient to self-administer boluses of analgesic (typically IV or SC) within preset limits, using a programmable pump. Used for severe acute postoperative pain, trauma, burn, acute crisis in palliative care, labour. Drugs: morphine, fentanyl, hydromorphone, oxycodone. Settings: bolus dose (e.g., morphine 1-2mg), lockout interval (5-10 min), 4-hour limit (optional, safety), background infusion (controversial for opioid-naive; useful for chronic pain/cancer). Advantages: patient autonomy, on-demand analgesia, reduced anxiety, less time to peak effect, lower total opioid use. Safe with appropriate monitoring (respiratory rate, sedation, oxygen). Contraindications: confused, unable to use pump, severe respiratory disease (cautious). PCA is not for end-of-life (use CSCI).

3 Investigation and Management Summary
Investigations

Pain assessment, opioid tolerance (if cancer pain), sedation (Pasero scale), respiratory rate (every 1-2h initially), oxygen saturation, blood pressure. Patient education (how to use pump). Lockout, bolus, 4-hour limit, background infusion parameters. Total opioid use (24h), effectiveness. Complications: respiratory depression, sedation, opioid side effects, equipment failure, programming error.

Management

Indication: severe acute pain (post-operative, trauma, labour, cancer crisis) with conscious patient able to use pump. Settings: bolus dose (morphine 1-2mg IV, fentanyl 25-50mcg IV), lockout interval (5-10 min), 4-hour limit (optional, safety), background infusion (controversial for opioid-naive; useful for chronic pain/cancer — e.g., morphine 1-2mg/h). Monitoring: RR, sedation, pain, total use. Side effect management: laxative, antiemetic, naloxone for RD (3-5mcg IV, titrate). PCA is NOT for end-of-life (use CSCI). Contraindications: confused, unable to use pump, severe respiratory disease (cautious).
