# Coeliac plexus block (CPB)

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Cancer Pain Syndromes

## 1. Definition

Coeliac plexus block (CPB) is an interventional technique for visceral pain from upper abdominal organs (pancreas, liver, gallbladder, spleen, proximal small bowel). The coeliac plexus is a network of sympathetic nerves in the retroperitoneum at the level of the celiac trunk. Block: needle inserted posteriorly (posterior approach) under CT/ultrasound guidance, or via EUS guidance; injectate: local anaesthetic + corticosteroid (block, diagnostic and short-term) or absolute alcohol/phenol (neurolysis, longer-lasting). Indications: pancreatic cancer pain (most common, 70-90% effective for visceral component), chronic pancreatitis, intra-abdominal malignancy. Side effects: hypotension (sympathetic block), diarrhoea (loss of sympathetic tone), back pain, paraplegia (rare, anterior spinal artery injury), pneumothorax, infection. Effective, well-tolerated, opioid-sparing.

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Coeliac plexus block (CPB) is an interventional technique for visceral pain from upper abdominal organs (pancreas, liver, gallbladder, spleen, proximal small bowel). The coeliac plexus is a sympathetic network in the retroperitoneum at the level of the celiac trunk (T12-L1). Indications: pancreatic cancer pain (most common, 70-90% effective for visceral component), chronic pancreatitis, intra-abdominal malignancy. Side effects: hypotension (sympathetic block, common), diarrhoea (loss of sympathetic tone), back pain, paraplegia (rare, anterior spinal artery injury), pneumothorax, infection.


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Indication: pancreatic or upper abdominal cancer pain, refractory to systemic opioid. Imaging: CT, ultrasound, or EUS for guidance. Coagulation: PT, aPTT, platelets (stop anticoagulation). Renal function: avoid dehydration; pre-hydrate. Pain assessment, bowel function, BP monitoring.

### Management
Procedure: CT/USS-guided posterior or EUS-guided anterior approach; needle to celiac plexus; inject LA + corticosteroid (block) or alcohol/phenol (neurolysis). Pre: IV hydration, monitoring, consent. During: BP, pain, comfort. Post: monitor BP, pain, bowel function, neurological signs. Side effects: hypotension (IV fluids, vasopressor), diarrhoea (loperamide, antispasmodic), back pain. Complications: paraplegia (urgent decompression, MRI), pneumothorax, infection. Effective for visceral pain 70-90%; less for somatic (consider other blocks).
