# Gabapentin and pregabalin

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Adjuvant Analgesics**

## 1. Definition

Gabapentinoids (gabapentin, pregabalin) are α2δ calcium channel ligands and first-line adjuvant analgesics for neuropathic pain. Mechanism: bind α2δ subunit of voltage-gated calcium channels in dorsal horn, reducing neurotransmitter release. Gabapentin: 100-300mg TDS, titrate up to 3600mg/day; renally excreted (dose reduction). Pregabalin: 75mg BD, titrate up to 600mg/day; linear PK, more predictable. Side effects: sedation, dizziness, peripheral oedema, ataxia, weight gain, mood changes. Schedule 3 (pregabalin) / Schedule 5 (gabapentin) controlled drugs. First-line for neuropathic pain (NICE).

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Gabapentin and pregabalin are α2δ subunit ligands of voltage-gated calcium channels, reducing excitatory neurotransmitter release (glutamate, substance P). Effective for neuropathic pain (PHN, diabetic neuropathy, central post-stroke pain, trigeminal neuralgia, fibromyalgia). Gabapentin: 100-300mg nocte, titrate to 300-600mg TDS (max 3600mg/day), renally excreted. Pregabalin: 25-75mg BD, titrate to 150-300mg BD (max 600mg/day). Side effects: sedation, dizziness, ataxia, peripheral oedema, weight gain, cognitive impairment, abuse potential (pregabalin Schedule 5 in UK).


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Renal function (dose reduction in eGFR<30), monitor for sedation, ataxia, especially in elderly. Baseline and follow-up mood (depression).

### Management
Slow titration (start low, increase every 3-7 days). Take with food. Counsel on driving (sedation). Avoid abrupt withdrawal (anxiety, insomnia, seizures, N&V). Combination with TCA or SNRI for refractory neuropathic pain (synergistic).

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Mechanism of gabapentin/pregabalin? A: α2δ ligand of voltage-gated Ca channels; reduces neurotransmitter release.

2. 2. Q: First-line for what? A: Neuropathic pain (PHN, diabetic neuropathy, central pain, trigeminal neuralgia).

3. 3. Q: Difference between them? A: Pregabalin has higher bioavailability (>90% vs 60%), faster titration, abuse potential.

4. 4. Q: Maximum dose gabapentin? A: 3600mg/day (renally adjusted).

5. 5. Q: Side effects? A: Sedation, dizziness, ataxia, oedema, weight gain, cognitive impairment.
