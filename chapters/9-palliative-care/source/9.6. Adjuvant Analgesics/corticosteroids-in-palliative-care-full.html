CHAPTER 9 PALLIATIVE AND SUPPORTIVE CARE / 9.6. ADJUVANT ANALGESICS
Corticosteroids In Palliative Care Full
1 Definition

Corticosteroids (dexamethasone most commonly) are used in palliative care for multiple indications: pain (nerve compression, bone pain, raised ICP), anorexia-cachexia, dyspnoea (lymphangitic carcinomatosis, SVCO), nausea (raised ICP, chemical), prevention of cisplatin-induced N&V, stridor (lymphoma). Dose: 2-16mg/day (low-dose for anorexia, high-dose for raised ICP, MSCC). Side effects: hyperglycaemia, mood changes (euphoria, insomnia), Cushing's, osteoporosis, infection, gastric irritation (PPI cover), proximal myopathy. Avoid in: systemic infection (unless treated), uncontrolled diabetes.

2 Clinical Features and Presentation

Corticosteroids (dexamethasone, prednisolone) are used in palliative care for multiple indications: pain (bone metastases, raised ICP, neuropathic from tumour compression, lymphoedema), nausea (raised ICP, bowel obstruction), anorexia-cachexia, dyspnoea (lymphangitis, SVC), spinal cord compression (high dose to reduce oedema), cerebral oedema. Dexamethasone preferred (less mineralocorticoid, longer duration). Dose: low (2-4mg/day) for anorexia, moderate (4-8mg) for pain/N&V, high (8-16mg) for MSCC, very high (16-24mg) for cerebral oedema, single dose 10-16mg then 4mg q6h IV for MSCC emergency. Side effects: hyperglycaemia, insomnia, agitation/euphoria, weight gain/appetite, peptic ulcer, infection, adrenal suppression, osteoporosis, myopathy, skin atrophy, mood changes.

3 Investigation and Management Summary
Investigations

Fasting glucose, electrolytes, consider H. pylori. Monitor BP, weight, mood. Avoid long-term use; consider PPI cover.

Management

Lowest effective dose, shortest time. Once daily morning dosing. PPI cover if risk of GI. Avoid in: peptic ulcer (unless healed, PPI cover), uncontrolled diabetes (caution), active infection, severe osteoporosis. Taper if >2 weeks use to avoid adrenal suppression. Monitor for side effects.

6 Viva Questions
  1. 1. Q: Why dexamethasone vs prednisolone? A: Less mineralocorticoid effect, longer duration, less fluid retention.
  1. 2. Q: Indications in pall care? A: Pain (bone, neuropathic, raised ICP), N&V, anorexia, MSCC, cerebral oedema, SVC, dyspnoea.
  1. 3. Q: MSCC dose? A: 10-16mg IV bolus then 4mg q6h.
  1. 4. Q: Side effects? A: Hyperglycaemia, insomnia, mood, infection, osteoporosis, adrenal suppression, GI ulcer.
  1. 5. Q: Why taper? A: Adrenal suppression (HPA axis) after >2 weeks.
