# Carbamazepine and oxcarbazepine

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Adjuvant Analgesics**

## 1. Definition

Carbamazepine (and oxcarbazepine) are anticonvulsants used as first-line for trigeminal neuralgia. Mechanism: voltage-gated sodium channel blockade. Dose: carbamazepine 100-200mg BD, titrate to 400-800mg BD; oxcarbazepine 300mg BD, titrate to 600-1200mg BD. Side effects: drowsiness, ataxia, diplopia, hyponatraemia (oxcarbazepine, especially elderly), agranulocytosis (rare — FBC monitoring), aplastic anaemia, Stevens-Johnson syndrome (HLA-B*15:02 in Asian populations, FDA black box warning), CYP450 inducer (drug interactions). Contraindicated in: AV block, porphyria, MAOI.

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Carbamazepine and oxcarbazepine are voltage-gated sodium channel blockers, first-line for trigeminal neuralgia (carbamazepine effective in 70% initially, oxcarbazepine similar efficacy, better tolerated). Also effective for glossopharyngeal neuralgia, paroxysmal symptoms in MS. Carbamazepine: 100-200mg BD, titrate to 400-1200mg/day. Oxcarbazepine: 300-600mg BD. Side effects: dizziness, ataxia, sedation, diplopia, hyponatraemia (more with oxcarbazepine, especially in elderly), agranulocytosis, aplastic anaemia, severe dermatological reactions (SJS/TEN, especially HLA-B*15:02 in Asian populations — screen before), hepatic, induces CYP450 (carbamazepine, not oxcarbazepine).


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
FBC, LFT, U&E (sodium — SIADH), plasma levels (carbamazepine therapeutic 4-12mg/L), HLA-B*15:02 in Asian populations (before carbamazepine), ECG in cardiac history. Monitor for skin reactions.

### Management
Start low, titrate slowly (1-2 weekly). Take with food. Counsel on signs of bone marrow suppression (fever, sore throat, bruising). Avoid in: AV block, porphyria, severe hepatic disease, concurrent MAOI. Oxcarbazepine advantages: less CYP450 induction, no epoxide metabolite (less interaction with other drugs).

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line for trigeminal neuralgia? A: Carbamazepine (oxcarbazepine similar, better tolerated).

2. 2. Q: HLA testing for carbamazepine? A: HLA-B*15:02 in Asian populations (Han Chinese, Thai, Indian) due to SJS/TEN risk.

3. 3. Q: Carbamazepine vs oxcarbazepine? A: Oxcarbazepine better tolerated, less CYP450 induction, no epoxide metabolite; carbamazepine more potent.

4. 4. Q: Why hyponatraemia? A: SIADH (more with oxcarbazepine); monitor sodium, especially elderly.

5. 5. Q: Warning signs? A: Fever, sore throat, mouth ulcers (bone marrow suppression); rash (SJS/TEN); jaundice (hepatic).
