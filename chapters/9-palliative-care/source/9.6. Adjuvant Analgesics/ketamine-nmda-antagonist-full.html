CHAPTER 9 PALLIATIVE AND SUPPORTIVE CARE / 9.6. ADJUVANT ANALGESICS
Ketamine Nmda Antagonist Full
1 Definition

Ketamine is an NMDA receptor antagonist used for refractory neuropathic cancer pain, opioid-sparing, and palliative sedation. Mechanism: NMDA receptor antagonism (central sensitisation), opioid receptor interaction, sodium channel blockade. Routes: oral, SC, IV, intranasal, topical. Dose: oral 10-25mg TDS (titrate); SC infusion 0.1-0.5 mg/kg/hr; IV 0.1-0.5 mg/kg/hr. Side effects: dissociation, hallucination, sedation, hypertension, salivation, urinary symptoms; monitor LFT. At subanaesthetic doses for analgesia. Limited evidence; specialist use.

2 Clinical Features and Presentation

Ketamine is a non-competitive NMDA receptor antagonist, used for refractory neuropathic pain (CRPS, post-herpetic, central, cancer pain with neuropathic component) and opioid-resistant pain. Low-dose ketamine 0.1-0.3mg/kg IV/SC, or 100-500mg/24h CSCI. Oral, SC, IV, intranasal. Side effects: dissociation, hallucinations, dysphoria (more with higher doses), sedation, hypertension, increased secretions, nausea, dizziness. Use with caution in: cardiovascular disease (HTN, ischaemia), schizophrenia, severe hepatic disease.

3 Investigation and Management Summary
Investigations

ECG, BP, psychiatric history (psychosis), substance use, hepatic function. Monitor during administration: BP, HR, consciousness, emergence phenomena.

Management

Start low (e.g., 10-20mg SC/PO TDS), titrate. Add benzodiazepine (midazolam) to reduce dysphoria. Specialist use, often in inpatient/palliative care. Continue if benefit; discontinue if no response. Use with caution in substance use (abuse potential).
