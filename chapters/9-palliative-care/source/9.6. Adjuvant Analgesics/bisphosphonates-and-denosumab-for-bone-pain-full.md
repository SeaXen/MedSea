# Bisphosphonates and denosumab for bone pain

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Adjuvant Analgesics**

## 1. Definition

Bisphosphonates (e.g., zoledronic acid 4mg IV q3-4w) and denosumab (120mg SC q4w) are antiresorptive agents used to prevent skeletal-related events (SRE) in bone metastases and multiple myeloma, and to reduce bone pain. Mechanism: bisphosphonates inhibit osteoclast activity (bind to bone, taken up by osteoclasts); denosumab is anti-RANKL monoclonal antibody preventing osteoclast formation/activation. Efficacy: delay SRE (pathological fracture, spinal cord compression, hypercalcaemia, radiation/surgery), reduce bone pain. Side effects: hypocalcaemia (correct vitamin D, Ca), renal toxicity (zoledronic acid), osteonecrosis of jaw (ONJ — good dental hygiene), atypical femoral fractures (denosumab), flu-like symptoms (first dose).

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Bisphosphonates (zoledronic acid 4mg IV monthly, pamidronate 90mg IV monthly, ibandronate 6mg IV monthly) and denosumab (120mg SC monthly) inhibit osteoclast-mediated bone resorption, reducing skeletal-related events (SREs — fracture, hypercalcaemia, spinal cord compression, need for RT/surgery) and pain in bone metastases (breast, prostate, lung, kidney, thyroid, myeloma). Onset of pain relief: 1-2 weeks; full effect 4-12 weeks. Side effects: flu-like symptoms (acute phase reaction with first dose, especially zoledronic acid), hypocalcaemia, renal toxicity (zoledronic acid — check eGFR, monitor), osteonecrosis of the jaw (ONJ — 1-2%, risk with dental procedures, poor oral hygiene), atypical femoral fractures (long-term).


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Baseline: dental review (mandatory before), calcium, phosphate, vitamin D, renal function, FBC, bone scan. Monitor: calcium, renal function, dental symptoms. Stop if severe hypocalcaemia, ONJ, atypical fracture.

### Management
Denosumab preferred in renal impairment (no nephrotoxicity). Zoledronic acid contraindicated if eGFR<30. Monitor renal function. Dental hygiene. Calcium and vitamin D supplementation. Treat hypocalcaemia (calcium IV/oral, vitamin D). Onset of action 1-2 weeks.

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Difference bisphosphonate vs denosumab? A: Bisphosphonate incorporates into bone, long-lasting; denosumab is monoclonal Ab against RANKL, no renal toxicity, reversibly stops osteoclasts.

2. 2. Q: ONJ risk? A: 1-2%; risk factors: dental procedures, poor oral hygiene, chemotherapy, steroids; need baseline dental review and ongoing dental care.

3. 3. Q: Renal dose adjustment? A: Zoledronic acid: avoid eGFR<30; pamidronate: 90→60mg if mild impairment; denosumab: no adjustment (safer in renal failure).

4. 4. Q: How long for pain relief? A: 1-2 weeks onset, full effect 4-12 weeks.

5. 5. Q: Hypocalcaemia risk? A: Yes; check baseline calcium, vitamin D; supplement if needed.
