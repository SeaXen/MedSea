# TCAs as analgesics

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Adjuvant Analgesics**

## 1. Definition

Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs) are adjuvant analgesics used for neuropathic pain (first-line per NICE). Mechanism: serotonin and noradrenaline reuptake inhibition (SNRI-like), sodium channel blockade, NMDA receptor antagonism. Examples: amitriptyline (most used, sedating), nortriptyline (less sedating), imipramine, desipramine. Dose: 10-25mg nocte (amitriptyline), titrate up to 75-150mg. Onset 1-2 weeks. Side effects: anticholinergic (dry mouth, urinary retention, constipation, blurred vision), sedation, cardiac (QTc, conduction — overdose toxic), orthostatic hypotension. Contraindicated in: recent MI, arrhythmias, narrow-angle glaucoma, urinary retention.

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs — amitriptyline, nortriptyline, imipramine, desipramine) have analgesic efficacy in neuropathic pain at lower doses than antidepressant. Mechanism: serotonin/NA reuptake inhibition, sodium channel blockade, NMDA antagonism, opioid receptor modulation. First-line for neuropathic pain (NICE). Dose: amitriptyline 10-25mg nocte, titrate to 25-150mg nocte. Side effects: anticholinergic (dry mouth, constipation, urinary retention, blurred vision, tachycardia), sedation, orthostatic hypotension, weight gain, cardiac (QRS widening, arrhythmias — caution in elderly, IHD, conduction disease), seizure threshold lowering (high doses), suicide risk in overdose.


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
ECG before (QTc, conduction disease), especially in elderly or cardiac history. Renal, liver function. Lying/standing BP. Monitor for anticholinergic side effects.

### Management
Start low (10-25mg amitriptyline in elderly, 25-50mg in adults), titrate by 10-25mg weekly to 25-150mg nocte. Take at bedtime. Avoid in: elderly with falls, cardiac disease (recent MI, arrhythmia), narrow-angle glaucoma, urinary retention, mania, severe liver disease, MAOI use (washout 14 days). Counsel on overdose risk.

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Why amitriptyline for neuropathic pain? A: TCA, multiple mechanisms (5HT/NA reuptake, Na channel, NMDA), effective at low doses.

2. 2. Q: Why elderly avoid? A: Anticholinergic side effects, falls, orthostatic hypotension, cardiac conduction.

3. 3. Q: First-line with gabapentin? A: Yes, both NICE first-line; combination if inadequate.

4. 4. Q: Best time to take? A: Bedtime (sedating, helps sleep).

5. 5. Q: Contraindication? A: Recent MI, conduction disease, narrow-angle glaucoma, urinary retention, MAOI use.
