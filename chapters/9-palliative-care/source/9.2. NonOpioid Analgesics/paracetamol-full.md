# Paracetamol

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Opioid Analgesics**

## 1. Definition

Paracetamol (acetaminophen) is a non-opioid analgesic and antipyretic. Mechanism: weak central COX inhibition (COX-1, COX-2), endocannabinoid system activation, TRPA1 ion channel inhibition. Effective for mild-moderate pain and fever. Dose: 500-1000mg q4-6h, max 4g/day (2g in liver disease, alcohol). Safe in pregnancy, paediatric, GI/renal/CV (vs NSAIDs). Hepatotoxicity in overdose: hepatocellular necrosis via toxic metabolite NAPQI (glucuronidation/sulfation exhausted); antidote: N-acetylcysteine (NAC) within 8-10h.

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Paracetamol (acetaminophen) is a non-opioid analgesic and antipyretic, used for mild-moderate pain and as adjunct in WHO ladder. Mechanism: central COX inhibition (COX-3, less peripheral), serotonergic pathways, endocannabinoid reuptake inhibition. Dose: 500-1000mg q4-6h, max 4g/day (UK), 3g/day if frail/elderly/hepatic disease. Bioavailability ~70%. Onset 30-60min, duration 4-6h. Safe in pregnancy, children, hepatic metabolism (CYP2E1, glucuronidation).


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
LFT (baseline in chronic use, suspected toxicity), INR (toxicity), paracetamol level at 4h post-ingestion (Rumack-Matthew nomogram for toxicity). Safe at therapeutic doses. No monitoring needed short-term.

### Management
First-line mild-moderate pain, adjuvant to opioids (spares opioid dose, reduces opioid side effects), antipyretic, safe in pregnancy. Caution: chronic alcohol use (induces CYP2E1, toxic metabolite NAPQI accumulates), malnutrition, dehydration, hepatic disease. Toxicity: nausea/vomiting at 24h, RUQ pain at 48h, hepatic failure at 72-96h; treat with N-acetylcysteine (NAC) within 8-10h.

## 5. Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| NSAIDs | Anti-inflammatory; more GI/renal/CV side effects |
| Opioids | Stronger but more side effects; constipation, N&V, RD |



## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Max paracetamol dose? A: 4g/day (UK), 3g/day in frail/elderly/hepatic disease.

2. 2. Q: Mechanism? A: Central COX inhibition (COX-3), serotonergic, endocannabinoid.

3. 3. Q: Antidote for toxicity? A: N-acetylcysteine (NAC), most effective within 8-10h of overdose.

4. 4. Q: Safe in pregnancy? A: Yes (category A).

5. 5. Q: Toxic metabolite? A: NAPQI (N-acetyl-p-benzoquinone imine), via CYP2E1, accumulates in overdose and glutathione depletion.
