# Hydromorphone

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Opioid Analgesics**

## 1. Definition

Hydromorphone is a strong semi-synthetic opioid agonist, 5-7x more potent than oral morphine (oral morphine 30mg = oral hydromorphone 5-6mg = parenteral hydromorphone 1.5-2mg), alternative strong opioid (WHO step 3) for cancer pain. Pure μ-receptor agonist. Available: oral immediate release (Palladone IR), oral modified release (Palladone SR), injection (SC, IV, IM), suppositories. Indications: alternative to morphine (alternative side effects, renal failure — no active metabolites, better tolerated), opioid rotation when morphine not tolerated (less histamine, less hallucinations, less nausea for some). Cautions: similar to morphine — opioid-naïve caution, history of substance use, elderly. Side effects: similar to morphine (constipation, N&V, sedation, respiratory depression, pruritus), but less histamine release (less pruritus), less active metabolites (safer in renal failure). Dosing: opioid-naïve 1-2mg PO q4-6h IR; MR 4-8mg OD. Equianalgesia: PO morphine 30mg = PO hydromorphone 6mg = IV hydromorphone 2mg = SC hydromorphone 3mg.

**Key clinical pearls:**

- Pure μ-agonist
- Less histamine than morphine
- No active metabolites (renal safe)
- Better tolerated for some


## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Hydromorphone is a strong opioid (WHO step 3) for severe cancer pain, alternative to morphine. Semi-synthetic, μ-agonist. 5-7x more potent than morphine. Available: IR (Palladone IR 1.3/2.6mg), MR (Palladone SR 2/4/8/16/24mg), injection (SC, IV, IM), suppositories. Side effects: similar to morphine, but less histamine (less pruritus), less neuroexcitatory metabolites (safer in renal failure). No active metabolites.


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Pain assessment, renal function (safer than morphine, dose reduction only in severe failure), liver function, sedation, bowel function.

### Management
Start: opioid-naïve 1.3mg q4-6h IR or 4mg 24-hourly MR. Titrate. Equianalgesia: PO morphine 30mg = PO hydromorphone 6mg = IV/SC hydromorphone 2mg. Good alternative for patients with renal impairment or morphine intolerance.

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Hydromorphone vs morphine potency? A: 5-7x more potent (PO 6mg = PO morphine 30mg).

2. 2. Q: Why use instead of morphine? A: No active metabolites (safer in renal failure), less histamine (less pruritus), alternative if morphine side effects.

3. 3. Q: Available routes? A: PO (IR, MR), SC, IV, IM, PR.

4. 4. Q: When is hydromorphone preferred? A: Renal failure, morphine intolerance (hallucinations, severe N&V), high opioid doses.

5. 5. Q: Equianalgesia? A: PO morphine 30mg = PO hydromorphone 6mg (5:1) = IV/SC hydromorphone 2mg (15:1).
