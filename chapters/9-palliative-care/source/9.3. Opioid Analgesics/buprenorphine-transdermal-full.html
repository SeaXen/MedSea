CHAPTER 9 PALLIATIVE AND SUPPORTIVE CARE / 9.3. OPIOID ANALGESICS
Buprenorphine Transdermal Full
1 Definition

Buprenorphine is a partial μ-agonist (with ceiling effect for respiratory depression) and κ-antagonist. Transdermal patch q7d. Ceiling for respiratory depression (analgesic ceiling debated). Available: sublingual (Temgesic, Subutex), patch (Butrans, Transtec), buccal film (Belbuca), implant. Equianalgesia: patch 5-20 mcg/hr = 10-35mg oral morphine/24h. Renally safe (hepatic metabolism, mostly faecal). Useful in elderly, renal impairment, opioid rotation. Less constipation.

2 Clinical Features and Presentation

Buprenorphine is a partial μ-agonist and κ-antagonist, available as transdermal patch (Butec, BuTrans, Transtec 5/10/15/20/35/52.5/70 mcg/hr, weekly or 96h depending on brand), sublingual (Subutex, Suboxone with naloxone for addiction), injection. Ceiling effect for respiratory depression at higher doses (analgesic ceiling controversial). Long duration (96h patch, 7-day patch). Safe in renal failure (no active metabolites, hepatic clearance).

3 Investigation and Management Summary
Investigations

Pain assessment (opioid-tolerant), renal function (safe), liver function (metabolised by CYP3A4; caution with inhibitors), respiratory, sedation, patch site.

Management

Transdermal: opioid-tolerant ≥80-120mg PO morphine/day for 7-day patch; convert 0.8-1 mcg/hr = 30mg PO morphine/day. Apply on clean, hairless skin, rotate site. Once weekly or every 96h depending on brand. Breakthrough: 1/6 of total daily dose as IR opioid. May precipitate withdrawal if switched from full agonist in opioid-tolerant (low dose — partial agonist effect).

6 Viva Questions
  1. 1. Q: Buprenorphine classification? A: Partial μ-agonist + κ-antagonist.
  1. 2. Q: Why preferred in renal failure? A: No active metabolites, hepatic clearance, safe.
  1. 3. Q: Patch frequency? A: Every 96h (BuTrans) or 7 days (BuTrans weekly).
  1. 4. Q: Ceiling for analgesia? A: Controversial; clear ceiling for respiratory depression at high doses.
  1. 5. Q: When NOT to use? A: Acute severe pain, severe respiratory depression, severe asthma, concurrent MAOI.
