# Fentanyl (transdermal and other routes)

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Opioid Analgesics**

## 1. Definition

Fentanyl is a strong synthetic opioid (μ-agonist), 50-100x more potent than morphine. Available: transdermal patch (q72h, for stable pain), sublingual (Abstral, Recivit), buccal (Effentora), intranasal (Instanyl, PecFent), lozenge (Actiq), SC, IV, epidural, intrathecal. Renally safe (no active metabolites). Patches: 12-25 mcg/hr for 30-60mg oral morphine/24h; takes 24-72h to reach steady state. For stable pain only (not for opioid titration). Less constipation, but may need higher breakthrough doses. Heat increases absorption (fever, hot bath).

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Fentanyl is a strong opioid (WHO step 3) with transdermal, buccal, sublingual, intranasal, SC, IV formulations. Transdermal patch (Durogesic DTrans, generic): 12.5/25/50/75/100 mcg/hr, applied every 72h, takes 24-72h to reach steady state, lasts 72h after removal (subcutaneous depot). Buccal/sublingual (Effentora, Abstral, Actiq lozenge) for breakthrough cancer pain (rapid onset 5-15 min). Highly lipophilic, 100x more potent than morphine. No active metabolites, no histamine, safe in renal failure.


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Pain assessment (must be opioid-tolerant before transdermal, ≥60mg oral morphine/day for at least 1 week), renal function (safer), liver function (caution in severe), temperature (fever increases absorption), site of patch (rotate, avoid damaged skin, hairless, clean), respiratory rate, sedation.

### Management
Transdermal: opioid-tolerant ≥60mg PO morphine/day; convert using 12.5mcg/hr = 30-60mg PO morphine/day (e.g., 25mcg/hr = 60-90mg, 50mcg/hr = 120-180mg). Apply on dry, hairless skin (chest, back, upper arm); rotate site every 72h. Breakthrough: 1/6 of total daily oral morphine equivalent as IR opioid. Buccal/sublingual for breakthrough: titration starting low. Heat increases absorption (fever, hot bath, heating pad).

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: When to use transdermal fentanyl? A: Opioid-tolerant, stable pain, oral not possible (vomiting, dysphagia), renal failure, compliance.

2. 2. Q: Patch onset/duration? A: Onset 24-72h (subcutaneous depot); apply every 72h; lasts 72h after removal.

3. 3. Q: Breakthrough on fentanyl patch? A: IR opioid, ~1/6 of total daily dose, q1h prn (separate from patch).

4. 4. Q: Why is fentanyl renally safe? A: No active metabolites (metabolised in liver by CYP3A4 to inactive metabolites).

5. 5. Q: Why avoid in opioid-naïve? A: Risk of severe respiratory depression; transdermal not titratable for acute pain.
