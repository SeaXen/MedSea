# Morphine

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Opioid Analgesics**

## 1. Definition

Morphine is the gold-standard strong opioid for moderate-severe cancer pain (WHO step 3). Pure μ-receptor agonist. Available: oral immediate release (Sevredol, Oramorph), oral modified release (MST Continus, Zomorph), SC, IV, IM, rectal, intrathecal. Dose: 5-10mg q4h IR (opioid-naïve); MR 30mg BD (typical starting total daily dose). Equianalgesia: oral 30mg = SC 15mg = IV 10mg. Active metabolites (M3G, M6G) accumulate in renal failure (use fentanyl/buprenorphine instead). Side effects: constipation, N&V, sedation, respiratory depression (late), pruritus, OIH.

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Morphine is the gold-standard strong opioid (WHO step 3) for cancer pain and severe chronic pain. Pure μ-agonist. Available: oral IR (Sevredol, Oramorph 5/10/20mg/1mL), oral MR (MST Continus 5/10/15/30/60/100/200mg, Zomorph 10/30/60/100/200mg, both 12-hourly), SC, IV, IM, PR, intrathecal, epidural. Bioavailability PO 30%, SC 100%, IV 100%. Metabolites: M3G (neuroexcitatory), M6G (active, 2x potency), accumulate in renal failure. Equianalgesia: PO 30mg = SC 15mg = IV 10mg. Side effects: constipation, N&V, sedation, respiratory depression (late sign), pruritus, urinary retention, OIH.


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Pain assessment, sedation score, respiratory rate, bowel function, baseline renal (switch to fentanyl/buprenorphine if eGFR<30), liver function. No routine monitoring in stable patients. Morphine levels not clinically used.

### Management
Start: opioid-naïve 5-10mg PO q4h IR or 30mg MR 12-hourly; switch from weak opioid (codeine 240mg/day = morphine 30mg/day). Titrate by 25-50% every 24-48h. Co-prescribe stimulant laxative (senna) + softener (docusate). Antiemetic prn first 1-2 weeks. Breakthrough: 1/6 of total daily dose, q1h prn. Side effect management: constipation (laxatives), N&V (antiemetic), sedation (reduce dose), respiratory depression (naloxone 3-5mcg IV, repeat).

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Equianalgesia PO:SC:IV for morphine? A: 30:15:10 mg (3:1.5:1).

2. 2. Q: Active metabolites of morphine? A: M6G (active, 2x potency); M3G (neuroexcitatory); both accumulate in renal failure.

3. 3. Q: Why avoid in renal failure? A: M3G/M6G accumulation → prolonged effect, neuroexcitatory, N&V; switch to fentanyl or buprenorphine.

4. 4. Q: Breakthrough dose? A: 1/6 of total daily dose, q1h prn.

5. 5. Q: First sign of opioid toxicity? A: Sedation, then respiratory depression (late).
