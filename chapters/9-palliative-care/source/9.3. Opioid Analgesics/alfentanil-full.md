# Alfentanil

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

## 1. Definition

Alfentanil is a short-acting, potent synthetic opioid (μ-agonist), 10-25x more potent than morphine IV. Rapid onset (1-2 min IV, 5-10 min sublingual/buccal), short duration (10-15 min IV, 30-60 min sublingual). Available: IV (anesthesia), sublingual/buccal (Rapinyl — breakthrough cancer pain). Similar to fentanyl but shorter half-life. Side effects: similar to opioids (N&V, constipation, sedation, respiratory depression, pruritus). Used for breakthrough cancer pain (sublingual/buccal) and procedural analgesia/anesthesia (IV). Rapid onset makes it useful for incident and breakthrough pain.

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Alfentanil is a short-acting synthetic opioid (μ-agonist), 10-25x more potent than IV morphine, used for breakthrough cancer pain (sublingual/buccal) and procedural analgesia. Rapid onset (1-2 min IV, 5-10 min sublingual/buccal) and short duration (10-15 min IV, 30-60 min sublingual) make it useful for incident and breakthrough pain. Side effects: N&V, constipation (chronic use), sedation, respiratory depression (dose-related), pruritus, chest wall rigidity (very high dose), bradycardia, hypotension. UK: Rapinyl (sublingual) approved for breakthrough cancer pain in opioid-tolerant patients (≥60mg oral morphine/day).


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Pain assessment, opioid tolerance check, baseline pain scores, breakthrough frequency and characteristics, sedation scale, respiratory rate, drug review (interactions with CYP3A4 inhibitors — azoles, macrolides, protease inhibitors — increase levels).

### Management
Indication: breakthrough cancer pain in opioid-tolerant. Dose: titrate from 100mcg sublingual/buccal, increase per breakthrough episode; max per episode (typically 1-2 doses, 1+ hour apart). Avoid with CYP3A4 inhibitors. Side effect management: laxative, antiemetic, sedation monitoring, naloxone for overdose. Withdraw gradually after chronic use.

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Onset of alfentanil? A: IV 1-2 min; sublingual/buccal 5-10 min.

2. 2. Q: Opioid tolerance for breakthrough? A: ≥60mg oral morphine/day for at least 1 week.

3. 3. Q: Vs fentanyl? A: Shorter half-life, faster onset, less potent (10-25x morphine vs fentanyl 100x morphine).

4. 4. Q: Why used for breakthrough? A: Rapid onset, short duration — ideal for breakthrough which is typically rapid onset, short-lived.

5. 5. Q: Drug interactions? A: CYP3A4 inhibitors (azoles, macrolides, protease inhibitors) increase levels; benzodiazepines increase sedation.
