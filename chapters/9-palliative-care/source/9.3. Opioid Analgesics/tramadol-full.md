# Tramadol

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Opioid Analgesics**

## 1. Definition

Tramadol is a weak opioid (μ-receptor agonist) with additional SNRI activity (serotonin and noradrenaline reuptake inhibition), making it useful for mixed and neuropathic pain. Dose: 50-100mg q6h, max 400mg/day (or 300mg in elderly). Avoid in epilepsy (lowers seizure threshold), MAOI (serotonin syndrome), and severe asthma. Often combined with paracetamol. Side effects: N&V, dizziness, seizures, serotonin syndrome with SSRIs/MAOIs. Less constipation than other opioids.

## 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Tramadol is a weak opioid (WHO step 2) with dual mechanism: μ-agonist + serotonin and noradrenaline reuptake inhibition (SNRI). Effective for nociceptive and neuropathic pain. Equianalgesia: tramadol 100mg PO = morphine 10mg PO (10:1). Side effects: constipation (less than morphine), N&V, sedation, dizziness, seizure threshold lowering, serotonin syndrome (with SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, TCAs, linezolid, triptans). Caution: renal failure (reduce dose, accumulate), hepatic failure. Available: IR (50mg), MR (100/150/200/300mg).


## 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Pain assessment, renal function (dose reduction in eGFR<30), liver function (dose reduction in cirrhosis), monitoring for serotonin syndrome (mental status, autonomic instability, hyperreflexia, clonus).

### Management
Start 50mg q6h IR or 100mg BD MR. Titrate by 50-100mg/day. Maximum 400mg/day (300mg in elderly). Reduce dose in renal/hepatic impairment. Avoid with SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, MAOIs (serotonin syndrome risk). Caution with epilepsy (lowers seizure threshold). Withdraw gradually (N&V, anxiety, sweating).

## 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Tramadol mechanism? A: μ-agonist + serotonin/noradrenaline reuptake inhibition (dual).

2. 2. Q: Equianalgesia tramadol:morphine? A: 10:1 (100mg tramadol = 10mg morphine).

3. 3. Q: Serotonin syndrome risk? A: Yes, with SSRIs/SNRIs/TCAs/MAOIs (excess serotonergic activity).

4. 4. Q: Side effect vs morphine? A: Less constipation, more dizziness, seizure threshold lowering.

5. 5. Q: Maximum dose? A: 400mg/day (300mg in elderly).
