# Symptom control in last days of life

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — End of Life Care

## Definition

Symptom control in the last days of life is the active management of pain, dyspnoea, nausea/vomiting, respiratory secretions (death rattle), delirium, terminal agitation, pruritus, mouth care, and family support. Most patients will have multiple symptoms. Key principles: anticipatory prescribing (just in case), regular review, simple regimens, common routes (PO, SC, CSCI, PR, buccal), avoid polypharmacy, address family/carer concerns, keep patient clean, comfortable, and dignified. Mouth care: sips, ice chips, lip balm, artificial saliva spray, treat oral candida. Respiratory secretions (death rattle): noisy breathing from pooled secretions; treat with anticholinergics (glycopyrronium 200-400µg SC, hyoscine butylbromide 20mg SC).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Most dying patients have multiple symptoms requiring active management: pain, dyspnoea, N&V, respiratory secretions (death rattle), delirium (terminal agitation), terminal agitation, pruritus, mouth care, family support.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Minimal (focus on comfort); identify and treat reversible.

### Management
Anticipatory prescribing ('just in case' box: opioid, midazolam, levomepromazine, haloperidol, glycopyrronium). Pain: regular opioid (PO or SC/CSCI), breakthrough plan. Dyspnoea: opioid, benzodiazepine, oxygen, positioning, airflow. N&V: levomepromazine, haloperidol, cyclizine, metoclopramide, ondansetron. Death rattle: glycopyrronium 200-400mcg SC, hyoscine butylbromide 20mg SC. Delirium: haloperidol, levomepromazine. Agitation: midazolam (consider palliative sedation). Pruritus: antihistamine, opioid switch. Mouth care: sips, ice chips, lip balm, treat Candida. Family: information, support, involvement, after-death.

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Anticipatory prescribing? A: 'Just in case' box: opioid, midazolam, antiemetic, glycopyrronium.

2. 2. Q: Death rattle? A: Glycopyrronium 200-400mcg SC, hyoscine butylbromide 20mg SC.

3. 3. Q: Delirium at end of life? A: Haloperidol 1-3mg SC; levomepromazine; family education, comfort care.

4. 4. Q: Mouth care? A: Sips, ice chips, lip balm, artificial saliva, treat Candida.

5. 5. Q: Family support? A: Information, involvement, support, after-death (sympathy, follow-up).
