# Terminal agitation and palliative sedation

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — End of Life Care

## Definition

Terminal agitation is restlessness, confusion, and distress in the last days of life, often due to delirium, pain, urinary retention, constipation, metabolic disturbance, drug toxicity, hypoxia, anxiety. Reversible causes should be sought and treated. If refractory, palliative sedation may be considered — the monitored use of medications to reduce consciousness for refractory symptoms at the end of life, with the intention of providing relief, not hastening death. Common indications: intractable pain, breathlessness, delirium, seizures, psychological/spiritual distress. Drugs: midazolam (5-10mg SC stat, then CSCI 10-60mg/24h), levomepromazine, haloperidol, phenobarbital, propofol. Ethics: doctrine of double effect, MDT involvement, family consent (in some settings), review by senior clinician.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Terminal agitation is restlessness, confusion, distress in last days. Causes: delirium (metabolic, drugs, infection, hypoxia), pain, urinary retention, constipation, drug withdrawal, dyspnoea, anxiety, existential distress, raised ICP, terminal organ failure. Reversible causes should be sought. If refractory, palliative sedation (monitored use of medications to reduce consciousness for refractory symptoms at end of life, with intention of providing relief, not hastening death).


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Clinical, identify reversible causes (drugs, pain, retention, metabolic, hypoxia), bloods if appropriate.

### Management
Identify and treat reversible. Non-pharm: calm environment, family presence, music, massage. Pharm: midazolam 5-10mg SC stat then CSCI 10-60mg/24h (first-line); levomepromazine 12.5-50mg SC/CSCI (second-line, broader, more sedating); haloperidol 1-3mg SC (delirium, hallucinations); opioids if pain. Palliative sedation (refractory): midazolam CSCI titrated, with consent, MDT, documentation, double-effect principle. Refractory: propofol, phenobarbital. Ethics: doctrine of double effect (intention relief, not death), MDT, family, senior review.

## Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Delirium (terminal) | Hallucinations, fluctuating |
| Pain | Vocalises, guards |
| Urinary retention | Distended bladder |
| Dyspnoea | Tachypnoeic |



## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line for terminal agitation? A: Midazolam 5-10mg SC stat then CSCI 10-60mg/24h.

2. 2. Q: Palliative sedation principle? A: Midazolam CSCI for refractory symptoms; double effect; consent; MDT.

3. 3. Q: Doctrine of double effect? A: Two effects: good (relief) and bad (potential harm/death); intention is good; not the means to bad.

4. 4. Q: Reversible causes? A: Pain, urinary retention, constipation, drugs, metabolic, hypoxia, anxiety, dyspnoea.

5. 5. Q: MDT involvement? A: Senior clinician, nurse, family, ethics; documentation of decision and consent.
