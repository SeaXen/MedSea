> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Cancer Pain Syndromes**

## 1. 1. Definition

Neuropathic cancer pain in cancer arises from direct nerve compression, infiltration, or damage by tumour, often involving brachial plexus (Pancoast, breast), lumbosacral plexus (pelvic cancers — cervical, prostate, colorectal), or peripheral nerves. Features: burning, electric, shooting, allodynia in nerve distribution. Often combined with somatic pain. Treatment: WHO ladder + adjuvant (TCAs, gabapentin, pregabalin — first-line for neuropathic); corticosteroids (dexamethasone) for compression; radiotherapy for tumour shrinkage; chemotherapy; nerve blocks; intrathecal analgesia.

## 2. 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Neuropathic cancer pain from nerve infiltration/compression is common (plexus — brachial or lumbosacral; spinal cord compression; peripheral nerves). Brachial plexus infiltration: lung apex (Pancoast), breast, lymphoma. Lumbosacral plexus: colorectal, gynaecological, retroperitoneal tumours. Features: burning, shooting, electric, lancinating in nerve distribution, with sensory changes, motor weakness, autonomic changes, allodynia. Often severe, with neuropathic components poorly responsive to opioids alone.


## 3. 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### 1. Investigations
MRI (brachial/lumbosacral plexus, spine) to identify cause; nerve conduction studies; PET-CT to assess disease activity. Pain assessment, function.

### 2. Management
WHO ladder + adjuvants (gabapentin, pregabalin, amitriptyline, duloxetine). Opioid rotation if inadequate. Radiotherapy for radiosensitive tumours (lymphoma, breast, small cell lung) — often gives rapid relief. Chemotherapy if appropriate. Interventional: nerve blocks (brachial plexus block), intrathecal pump, spinal cord stimulation. Surgery for decompression if acute compression (e.g., spinal cord).

## 6. 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Most common cause of brachial plexopathy in cancer? A: Pancoast tumour (lung apex), breast cancer, lymphoma.

2. 2. Q: First-line adjuvant for neuropathic cancer pain? A: Gabapentin, pregabalin, amitriptyline, or duloxetine.

3. 3. Q: Interventional option? A: Brachial plexus block, intrathecal pump, spinal cord stimulation.

4. 4. Q: When is radiotherapy useful? A: Radiosensitive tumours (lymphoma, breast, small cell lung); often rapid relief.

5. 5. Q: Lumbosacral plexopathy causes? A: Colorectal, gynaecological (cervix, ovary), retroperitoneal, lymphomas.
