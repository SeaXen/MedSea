# Advance care planning (ACP)

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — End of Life Care

## Definition

Advance care planning (ACP) is a process of discussion between a patient, family/carers, and healthcare professionals about future care, particularly regarding end-of-life preferences. Components: ADRT (Advance Decision to Refuse Treatment) — legally binding, specifies treatments the patient would refuse in specific circumstances (e.g., CPR, ventilation, dialysis); lasting power of attorney (LPA) — legally designates a person to make decisions on behalf of the patient; values and preferences (place of care, level of intervention, resuscitation, organ donation, spiritual care). Documentation: written, dated, signed, witnessed, shared with relevant professionals (GP, hospital, ambulance, care home). Important: start early, review, address capacity, respect cultural/spiritual wishes, support family. MDT involvement.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Capacity assessment (essential before ACP); mental capacity 4-criteria test.

### Management
Start early, review, address capacity, respect cultural/spiritual wishes, family support, MDT involvement. ACP is a process, not an event. Capacity: must be assessed; if lacking, best-interests decision. ADRT: legally binding if valid and applicable. LPA: designate decision-maker for when capacity lacking. DNACPR: specific decision (not 'no treatment'). Family involvement per patient wishes. Documentation shared with relevant professionals (GP, hospital, ambulance, care home).
