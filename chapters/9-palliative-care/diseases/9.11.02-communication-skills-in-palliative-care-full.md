# Communication skills in palliative care

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Communication and Ethics

## Definition

Communication in palliative care is essential for all aspects of care — breaking bad news, advance care planning, family meetings, conflict resolution, treatment decisions, and supporting grief. Key skills: active listening, open questions, silence, empathy (name, validate, support), summarising, shared decision-making, managing emotions, dealing with denial, anger, distress, hope vs realism. Frameworks: SPIKES (breaking bad news), BREAKS, Calman gap, family meeting structure (preparation, introductions, agenda, discussion, decision, summary, follow-up). Cultural/spiritual awareness. Documentation. Training: role play, video, mentorship.
