# Care of the family in palliative care

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Communication and Ethics

## Definition

Care of the family in palliative care is the support of relatives and loved ones throughout the illness, at the time of death, and in bereavement. Components: information (clear, honest, regular), involvement (in care, decisions per patient wishes), support (emotional, practical, financial — social worker), anticipatory grief, support at death, bereavement (sympathy, follow-up). Carer assessment: needs, stress, sleep, finances, employment, own health. Risk factors for complicated bereavement: pre-death conflict, multiple losses, low social support, financial strain, mental illness. After death: practical advice (registration, funeral), grief support, follow-up, referral if needed.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
None (carer assessment).

### Management
Information: clear, honest, regular, empathic. Involvement: in care, decisions (per patient wishes). Support: emotional, practical, financial (social worker, benefits). Anticipatory grief: pre-death; in patient and family. At death: support, information, follow-up. After death: registration, funeral, bereavement, follow-up. Carer assessment: needs, stress, sleep, finances, employment, own health. Risk factors for complicated bereavement: pre-death conflict, multiple losses, low social support, financial strain, mental illness. Children: special needs, age-appropriate support. Documentation: family, contacts, support provided.
