# DNACPR and treatment escalation decisions

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — End of Life Care

## Definition

DNACPR (Do Not Attempt Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) and treatment escalation plans (TEP) are decisions about appropriate level of intervention in deteriorating patients, balancing benefits, burdens, and patient preferences. CPR: chest compressions, ventilation, defibrillation, adrenaline; success rate in advanced illness <1-5%; can cause harm. Indications for DNACPR: patient wishes, futility (e.g., advanced cancer, end-stage organ failure), no benefit, quality of life. Decision: MDT discussion, patient (if capacity) and family involvement, documented, shared with relevant services. Treatment escalation: ward-based care, high-dependency, ICU, palliative. Important: do not equate DNACPR with no treatment — patient may still receive full active treatment except for CPR.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
MDT discussion, patient (if capacity) and family, documentation, shared with services.

### Management
Indications for DNACPR: patient wishes, futility (e.g., advanced cancer, end-stage organ failure), no benefit, quality of life. Decision: MDT, patient (if capacity), family, documented, shared. TEP: ward-based care, high-dependency, ICU, palliative. DNACPR not 'do not treat' — patient may receive full active treatment except for CPR. Communication: sensitive, honest, respect denial, support family. Documentation: form, electronic record, share with ambulance, out-of-hours. Review if circumstances change.

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Success of CPR in advanced illness? A: <1-5% (often <1% in advanced cancer).

2. 2. Q: Indications for DNACPR? A: Patient wishes, futility, no benefit, quality of life.

3. 3. Q: Decision process? A: MDT, patient (if capacity), family, documented, shared.

4. 4. Q: DNACPR vs treatment? A: DNACPR is specific decision (no CPR); patient may still receive full active treatment otherwise.

5. 5. Q: Documentation? A: DNACPR form, electronic record, share with ambulance, out-of-hours, care home.
