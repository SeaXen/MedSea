# Terminal agitation and restlessness

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care**

## Definition

Terminal agitation/restlessness is a common, distressing symptom in the last days of life, with motor agitation, confusion, moaning, calling out, picking at bedclothes. Causes: terminal delirium (most common), pain, urinary retention, constipation, hypoxia, hypercalcaemia, drug withdrawal, anxiety, spiritual distress, terminal hepatic/renal failure. Treatment: identify and treat reversible causes; non-pharmacological (reassurance, family, familiar environment, music, dim light); pharmacological (midazolam 2.5-5mg SC PRN, or continuous SC infusion 10-30mg/24h; haloperidol 1-2.5mg SC PRN; levomepromazine 6.25-12.5mg SC PRN; phenobarbital 100-200mg IM PRN if refractory). Consider palliative sedation if refractory.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Terminal agitation is restlessness, confusion, distress in last days of life. Causes: delirium (metabolic, drugs, infection, hypoxia), pain, urinary retention, constipation, drug withdrawal, dyspnoea, anxiety, existential distress, raised ICP, terminal organ failure. Reversible causes should be sought and treated. If refractory, palliative sedation may be considered.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Clinical assessment, identify reversible causes (drugs, pain, retention, metabolic, hypoxia), bloods if appropriate (often not at end of life), family discussion.

### Management
Identify and treat reversible. Non-pharm: calm environment, family presence, music, massage, low light, familiar items. Pharmacological: midazolam 2.5-5mg SC prn (first-line), CSCI 10-30mg/24h (refractory); haloperidol 1-3mg SC for delirium; levomepromazine 12.5-50mg SC/CSCI for refractory; opioids if pain. Palliative sedation for refractory: midazolam CSCI titrated, with consent, MDT, documentation, double-effect principle.

## Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Delirium (terminal) | Hallucinations, fluctuating |
| Pain | Vocalises, guards |
| Urinary retention | Distended bladder |
| Dyspnoea | Tachypnoeic, hypoxic |



## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line for terminal agitation? A: Midazolam 2.5-5mg SC prn; CSCI 10-30mg/24h for refractory.

2. 2. Q: Reversible causes? A: Pain, urinary retention, constipation, drugs, metabolic, hypoxia, anxiety, dyspnoea.

3. 3. Q: Palliative sedation principle? A: Midazolam CSCI for refractory symptoms; double effect; consent; MDT.

4. 4. Q: Haloperidol vs midazolam? A: Haloperidol for delirium (psychosis, hallucinations); midazolam for anxiety, agitation, sedation.

5. 5. Q: Levomepromazine? A: Antipsychotic + sedative + antiemetic; for refractory; broad-spectrum.
