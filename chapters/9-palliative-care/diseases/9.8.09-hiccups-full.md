# Hiccups

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care**

## Definition

Hiccups (singultus) are involuntary, intermittent spasms of the diaphragm, often self-limiting but in palliative care can be persistent (>48h) and distressing. Causes: gastric distension (reflux, overeating), metabolic (uraemia, hyponatraemia, hypocalcaemia, hypokalaemia), CNS (stroke, tumour, infection), diaphragmatic irritation (tumour, pericarditis, pneumonia, abdominal distension), drugs (dexamethasone, benzodiazepines, opioids, dopamine agonists). Investigation: history, examination, bloods (U&E, calcium), CXR, abdominal imaging if indicated. Management: simple measures (sip water, paper bag rebreathing, Valsalva, swallow dry sugar, vagal stimulation), gabapentin (first-line for persistent, starting 100mg TDS titrated to 300-600mg TDS), baclofen 5-10mg TDS, metoclopramide 10mg TDS, haloperidol 1-3mg nocte, midazolam CSCI 10-30mg/24h for refractory, chlorpromazine, nifedipine; for intractable: anaesthetic block (phrenic nerve).

**Key clinical pearls:**

- Gabapentin 100-300mg TDS first-line
- Baclofen 5-10mg TDS
- Treat underlying cause
- Lasts >48h is chronic
