> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Adjuvant Analgesics**

## 1. 1. Definition

Serotonin-noradrenaline reuptake inhibitors (SNRIs) are adjuvant analgesics for neuropathic pain (first-line per NICE). Examples: duloxetine (60mg/day), venlafaxine (75-225mg/day). Duloxetine is first-line specifically for painful diabetic neuropathy. Mechanism: serotonin and noradrenaline reuptake inhibition, enhancing descending inhibitory pathways. Side effects: nausea (most common, take with food), dry mouth, dizziness, sexual dysfunction, sweating, hyponatraemia (SIADH in elderly), hypertension (venlafaxine high doses), serotonin syndrome with MAOIs, withdrawal on stopping. Avoid in: uncontrolled hypertension, MAOI, severe liver disease, severe renal disease.

## 2. 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Serotonin-noradrenaline reuptake inhibitors (SNRIs — duloxetine, venlafaxine, milnacipran) are first-line for neuropathic pain (NICE), especially duloxetine for painful diabetic neuropathy (FDA/EMA-approved). Duloxetine 60-120mg/day; venlafaxine 75-225mg/day (MR). Effective for diabetic neuropathy, fibromyalgia, chronic musculoskeletal pain. Side effects: nausea, dry mouth, constipation, sweating, sexual dysfunction, hypertension (venlafaxine, especially high dose), hepatotoxicity (duloxetine — avoid in liver disease), withdrawal syndrome (gradual taper).


## 3. 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### 1. Investigations
LFT (duloxetine), BP (venlafaxine), renal function. Monitor for serotonin syndrome (with tramadol, TCAs, MAOIs, triptans).

### 2. Management
Start duloxetine 30mg/day, titrate to 60-120mg/day. Take with food. Avoid in: liver disease, severe renal failure, uncontrolled hypertension, MAOI use. Counsel on withdrawal (gradual taper, never abrupt).

## 6. 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line SNRI for neuropathic pain? A: Duloxetine (FDA/EMA-approved for diabetic neuropathy).

2. 2. Q: Why duloxetine in diabetic neuropathy? A: SNRI; treats both pain and depression (common comorbidity).

3. 3. Q: Risk vs TCA? A: Less sedation, no anticholinergic, no cardiac conduction effects.

4. 4. Q: Side effects? A: Nausea, dry mouth, sexual dysfunction, hypertension, hepatotoxicity (duloxetine).

5. 5. Q: Serotonin syndrome risk? A: Yes, with tramadol, SSRIs, TCAs, MAOIs, triptans.
