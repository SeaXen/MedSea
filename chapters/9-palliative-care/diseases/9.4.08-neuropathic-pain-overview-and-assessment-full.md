# Neuropathic pain overview and assessment

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Neuropathic Pain**

## Definition

Neuropathic pain is pain from a lesion or disease of the somatosensory system (IASP definition). Affects 7-10% of population. Features: burning, electric, shooting, pins-and-needles, numbness, allodynia, hyperalgesia. Causes: peripheral (diabetic neuropathy, post-herpetic neuralgia, trigeminal neuralgia, nerve injury, cancer infiltration), central (post-stroke, spinal cord injury, MS). Screening tools: LANSS, DN4, painDETECT. Treatment: NICE first-line (TCAs, duloxetine, gabapentin, pregabalin); second-line (tramadol, capsaicin, lidocaine); third-line (strong opioids, neuromodulation).

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
LANSS, DN4, painDETECT, QST, MRI neuroaxis, NCS/EMG, bloods (B12, folate, glucose, autoimmune, paraneoplastic).

### Management
First-line: gabapentin 300-3600mg/day, pregabalin 150-600mg/day, duloxetine 60-120mg/day, amitriptyline 25-150mg. Second-line: combination, tramadol, topical lidocaine 5%, capsaicin 8%. Interventional: SCS, PNS, nerve blocks.

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: LANSS score? A: ≥12 = neuropathic pain.

2. 2. Q: DN4 score? A: ≥4 = neuropathic pain.

3. 3. Q: First-line treatments? A: Gabapentin, pregabalin, duloxetine, amitriptyline.

4. 4. Q: Spinal cord stimulation indication? A: Failed back surgery syndrome, refractory CRPS, neuropathic pain.

5. 5. Q: Capsaicin 8% patch? A: Single 60min application, effective for PHN and diabetic neuropathy.
