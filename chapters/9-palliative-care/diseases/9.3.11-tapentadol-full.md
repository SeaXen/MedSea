> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Opioid Analgesics**

## 1. 1. Definition

Tapentadol is a unique dual-mechanism analgesic: μ-opioid receptor agonist (MOR, 18x weaker than morphine) AND noradrenaline reuptake inhibitor (NRI), giving analgesia for both nociceptive and neuropathic pain. Available: oral immediate release (Palexia IR 50/75/100mg), oral modified release (Palexia SR 50-250mg BD). Indications: moderate-severe acute and chronic pain, including neuropathic pain (diabetic neuropathy, low back pain with neuropathic component). Dose: IR 50-100mg q4-6h (max 600mg/day); SR 50-250mg BD. Side effects: similar to opioids (N&V, constipation, dizziness, somnolence) but less than equianalgesic morphine; lower abuse potential than pure opioids; serotonin syndrome risk (with SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, TCAs). Equianalgesia: 50mg tapentadol ≈ 50mg tramadol ≈ 10mg morphine (approximate).

**Key clinical pearls:**

- Dual mechanism: MOR + NRI
- Neuropathic + nociceptive
- Lower abuse potential
- Serotonin syndrome risk


## 2. 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Tapentadol is a strong opioid (also approved for moderate-severe pain) with dual mechanism: μ-agonist (18x weaker than morphine) + noradrenaline reuptake inhibitor (NRI). Effective for nociceptive and neuropathic pain. Available: IR (Palexia IR 50/75/100mg, q4-6h, max 600mg/day), MR (Palexia SR 50/75/100/150/200/250mg, BD). Side effects: N&V, constipation, dizziness, somnolence (less than equianalgesic morphine), lower abuse potential, less respiratory depression. Serotonin syndrome risk (with SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, MAOIs, triptans, linezolid).


## 3. 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### 1. Investigations
Pain assessment, renal function (reduce dose in severe impairment), liver function (avoid in severe), monitoring for serotonin syndrome, no ECG required (unlike methadone).

### 2. Management
Start IR 50mg q4-6h, max 600mg/day; MR 50mg BD, titrate to max 250mg BD. Equianalgesia: tapentadol 50mg ≈ tramadol 50mg ≈ morphine 10mg. Avoid with MAOIs (serotonin syndrome, severe reaction including death), SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs (use with caution).

## 6. 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Tapentadol mechanism? A: μ-agonist (weak) + NRI (dual).

2. 2. Q: Difference from tramadol? A: No serotonin effect (only NRI, no 5HT reuptake), so less seizure risk and serotonin syndrome.

3. 3. Q: Serotonin syndrome risk? A: Yes, with SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, MAOIs, triptans, linezolid.

4. 4. Q: Abuse potential vs morphine? A: Lower.

5. 5. Q: Maximum dose? A: IR 600mg/day, MR 500mg/day (250mg BD).
