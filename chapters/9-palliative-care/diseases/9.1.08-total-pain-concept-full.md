> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Pain Physiology & Assessment**

## 1. 1. Definition

Total pain (Cicely Saunders) is the recognition that pain in advanced disease is not just physical but encompasses emotional, social, spiritual, and existential suffering. Components: physical (disease, treatment), emotional (depression, anxiety, fear), social (family, isolation, finances), spiritual (meaning, faith, hope), existential (loss, death). Assessment requires a multidisciplinary team. Treatment requires addressing all dimensions — pharmacological (analgesics) AND non-pharmacological (counselling, spiritual care, family support).

## 2. 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Total pain concept (Cicely Saunders, founder of modern palliative care) recognises that pain has multiple components: physical (tissue damage, nerve, organ), psychological (anxiety, depression, fear, anger), social (loss of role, financial, isolation, family conflict), spiritual (existential distress, loss of meaning, fear of death). All components interact. Clinical features: complex pain, refractory to opioids alone, requires multimodal team, family involvement, addressing non-physical components is essential.


## 3. 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### 1. Investigations
Holistic assessment: physical, psychological, social, spiritual. Use total pain framework. Tools: HADS, ESAS, FICA, HOPE, PHQ-9, GAD-7. Multidisciplinary team assessment.

### 2. Management
Multimodal, multidisciplinary: physical (analgesia, adjuvants, interventional); psychological (CBT, counselling, relaxation, mindfulness); social (social work, family meetings, financial support, carer support); spiritual (chaplain, faith community, existential exploration). Family involvement. Address fears and concerns. Listen and validate.

## 6. 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: What is the total pain concept? A: Pain with physical, psychological, social, and spiritual components, all interacting (Cicely Saunders).

2. 2. Q: Why address all components? A: Pain in one component amplifies others; multimodal approach is more effective than opioids alone.

3. 3. Q: Who is Cicely Saunders? A: Founder of modern palliative care, St Christopher's Hospice (1967), developed total pain concept.

4. 4. Q: Example of social pain? A: Loss of role, financial worries, isolation, family conflict.

5. 5. Q: Example of spiritual pain? A: Loss of meaning, fear of death, existential distress, loss of faith.
