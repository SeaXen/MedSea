# Shared decision-making

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Communication and Ethics

## Definition

Shared decision-making (SDM) is a collaborative process in which patient and clinician work together to make decisions about care, considering the patient's values, preferences, and goals, alongside the clinical evidence. Components: information exchange (clear, balanced, evidence-based), deliberation (patient preferences, goals, values), decision-making (joint), implementation. Supported by decision aids, patient information leaflets. Important: assess capacity, respect cultural values, address family concerns, document, support, review. In palliative care: essential for advance care planning, treatment escalation, place of care, end-of-life decisions.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
None.

### Management
Information exchange: clear, balanced, evidence-based. Deliberation: patient preferences, goals, values. Joint decision: shared. Implementation: plan, follow-up. Patient: values, preferences, goals, capacity. Clinician: evidence, options, recommendations. Capacity: must be assessed; if lacking, best-interests. Decision aids: support information, options, outcomes. Palliative care: ACP, escalation, place of care, end-of-life. Family: involve per patient wishes. Documentation: decision, reasoning.
