# Superior vena cava obstruction (SVCO)

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Palliative Care Emergencies

## Definition

Superior vena cava obstruction (SVCO) is a clinical syndrome from obstruction of blood flow through the SVC, most commonly due to malignancy (lung cancer 70-80% — small cell, squamous; lymphoma; breast; metastatic). Clinical features: facial/neck/arm swelling (especially morning), plethora (redness), distended neck and chest wall veins, dyspnoea, cough, orthopnoea, headache worse on stooping/bending, intracranial symptoms (confusion, seizures, visual). Onset: gradual (weeks). Subacute onset, no history of malignancy, no obvious cause → urgent imaging to identify cause. Diagnosis: CXR, CT with contrast (most sensitive), bronchoscopy/biopsy for histology. Treatment: general measures (elevate head, oxygen, steroids — dexamethasone 4mg q6h); definitive: chemotherapy (small cell lung cancer, lymphoma — rapidly responsive), radiotherapy; endovascular stenting (rapid, effective, palliative).

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
CXR (widened mediastinum, mass), CT chest with contrast (most sensitive — obstruction site, cause, collateral vessels); tissue biopsy (bronchoscopy, mediastinoscopy, image-guided).

### Management
General: elevate head, oxygen, dexamethasone 4mg q6h. Definitive treatment of cause: SCLC/lymphoma: chemotherapy (rapid response); radiotherapy. Endovascular SVC stenting: rapid palliative relief (24-48h) for severe SVCO or when other options limited.
