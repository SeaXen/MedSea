# Anxiety in palliative care

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care**

## Definition

Anxiety is common in palliative care, especially with advanced disease, facing death, uncontrolled symptoms, and existential distress. Features: excessive worry, fear, restlessness, autonomic arousal, panic. Causes: existential (death, loss of control), physical (uncontrolled pain, dyspnoea, N&V), drugs (corticosteroids, withdrawal), psychiatric (pre-existing anxiety). Treatment: address causes (existential, spiritual, social); psychological (CBT, mindfulness, relaxation, counselling); pharmacological (short-term benzodiazepines: lorazepam 0.5-1mg, midazolam 2-5mg SC, diazepam; SSRI/SNRI for chronic).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Anxiety in palliative care is common (30-50%), persistent worry, fear, restlessness, panic, autonomic symptoms (tachycardia, sweating, hyperventilation), sleep disturbance, muscle tension. Causes: situational (cancer diagnosis, death anxiety, separation), medical (hyperthyroid, PE, heart failure, hypoxia, sepsis, drug withdrawal, drug-induced — steroids, stimulants), psychiatric (anxiety disorder, depression). Often comorbid with pain, depression, dyspnoea.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
History, HADS or GAD-7, medical evaluation (oxygen saturation, TFT, drug review, ABG if indicated).

### Management
Non-pharm: CBT, relaxation, mindfulness, breathing techniques, massage, music, spiritual support, family involvement, environmental modification. Pharmacological: SSRIs (long-term, generalised anxiety disorder — 4-6 weeks onset), short-acting benzodiazepines (acute symptoms, anticipatory anxiety, procedure anxiety): lorazepam 0.5-1mg SL/PO, diazepam 2-5mg PO, midazolam 2-5mg SC prn or CSCI; beta-blockers (autonomic symptoms); atypical antipsychotics if refractory; gabapentin/pregabalin (anxiety, also pain).

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line for generalised anxiety? A: SSRI (escitalopram, sertraline) ± short-acting benzodiazepine for acute symptoms.

2. 2. Q: First-line for acute anxiety in palliative care? A: Short-acting benzodiazepine (lorazepam 0.5-1mg SL, midazolam 2-5mg SC, diazepam 2-5mg PO).

3. 3. Q: Anxiety at end of life? A: Midazolam CSCI 5-20mg/24h.

4. 4. Q: Beta-blocker in anxiety? A: For autonomic symptoms (tachycardia, tremor) in performance anxiety or specific situations.

5. 5. Q: Common causes in palliative care? A: Cancer-related fear, death anxiety, uncontrolled symptoms, drug-induced, withdrawal, hypoxia, hyperthyroid.
