# Acute dyspnoea in palliative care

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Palliative Care Emergencies

## Definition

Acute dyspnoea (breathlessness) is a common palliative care emergency in advanced cancer, end-stage cardiac/respiratory disease. Causes: tumour (pleural effusion, bronchial obstruction, lymphangitis, SVC), cardiac failure, infection (pneumonia, COPD exacerbation), anaemia, PE, anxiety, ascites. Management: opioids (oral morphine 2.5-5mg q4h or SC diamorphine 1-2mg prn or CSCI), benzodiazepines for anxiety (midazolam 5-10mg SC prn or CSCI, or diazepam 5-10mg PO/rectal), oxygen if hypoxic, positioning, airflow (open window, fan), positioning, treat reversible (infection, effusion drainage, blood transfusion, stenting). Non-pharmacological: breathing techniques, relaxation, family support.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Acute dyspnoea (breathlessness) is common in advanced cancer, end-stage cardiac/respiratory disease. Causes: tumour (pleural effusion, bronchial obstruction, lymphangitis, SVC), cardiac failure, infection (pneumonia, COPD exacerbation), anaemia, PE, anxiety, ascites, fluid overload.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Cause: clinical, CXR, oxygen saturation, BNP, bloods (FBC, U&E, ABG if indicated), ECG, sputum culture if infection.

### Management
Treat reversible (infection, effusion drainage, anaemia, bronchospasm). Opioids (oral morphine 2.5-5mg q4h, SC diamorphine 1-2mg prn, CSCI for persistent). Benzodiazepines for anxiety (midazolam 5-10mg SC prn or CSCI, diazepam 5-10mg PO). Oxygen if hypoxic (SpO2 <88%, or symptomatic hypoxia). Positioning (sitting up, lean forward). Airflow (fan, open window, even if not hypoxic). Non-pharm: breathing techniques, relaxation, family support, calm environment.

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Pulmonary | Cancer, COPD, infection, PE, effusion, SVC |
| Cardiac | CCF, anaemia, PE |
| Metabolic | Acidosis, anaemia |
| Anxiety | Often comorbid |



## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line drug? A: Opioid (morphine 2.5-5mg q4h or SC diamorphine 1-2mg prn).

2. 2. Q: Second-line/anxiety? A: Benzodiazepine (midazolam 5-10mg SC, diazepam 5-10mg PO).

3. 3. Q: When oxygen? A: Hypoxic (SpO2 <88%) or symptomatic hypoxia; no evidence for non-hypoxic.

4. 4. Q: Non-pharm? A: Positioning, fan, open window, breathing techniques, relaxation, family support.

5. 5. Q: Treat reversible? A: Infection (ABx), effusion (drain), anaemia (transfuse), bronchospasm (salbutamol), PE (anticoagulation).


## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **Dyspnoea** = Opioids + benzodiazepines + oxygen + airflow + position + treat cause
