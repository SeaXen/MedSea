# Nausea and vomiting in palliative care

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care**

## Definition

Nausea and vomiting (N&V) in palliative care affects 30-60% of patients with advanced cancer. Pathophysiology: 4 main pathways (CTZ via 5-HT3/D2 in floor of 4th ventricle, vestibular apparatus via H1/M1, gut vagal afferents via 5-HT3, higher centres via cortex). Causes: metabolic (hypercalcaemia, uraemia, hyponatraemia), drugs (opioids, chemotherapy, antibiotics), mechanical (gastric stasis, bowel obstruction, raised ICP), vestibular, anxiety. Antiemetic choice by receptor/cause: haloperidol (D2 — chemical/CTZ), cyclizine (H1/M1 — vestibular/gut), ondansetron (5-HT3 — chemo/post-op/gut), metoclopramide (D2/5-HT4 — gastric stasis), levomepromazine (broad-spectrum), dexamethasone (raised ICP, multi-action), hyoscine (gut motility, secretions).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Nausea and vomiting (N&V) affects 30-60% of advanced cancer. 4 pathways: CTZ (5-HT3, D2, NK1), vestibular (H1, M1), gut vagal afferents (5-HT3, D2), higher centres (cortex, anxiety). Causes: metabolic (Ca, urea, Na), drugs (opioids, chemo, antibiotics, NSAIDs), GI (gastric stasis, obstruction, constipation), vestibular, raised ICP, anxiety. Antiemetic by receptor: haloperidol D2, cyclizine H1/M1, ondansetron 5-HT3, metoclopramide D2/5-HT4, levomepromazine broad, dexamethasone ICP, hyoscine gut motility.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
History (timing, triggers, content, associations), examination (hydration, abdomen, fundoscopy for papilloedema), bloods (U&E, Ca, LFT, glucose), abdominal X-ray (obstruction), drug review, constipation check.

### Management
Identify cause. Receptor-targeted antiemetic. First-line: haloperidol 0.5-1.5mg nocte (chemical/CTZ) or metoclopramide 10mg TDS (gastric stasis). Second-line: cyclizine 50mg TDS (vestibular/gut), ondansetron 8mg TDS (chemo/post-op/gut), levomepromazine 6.25mg TDS (broad-spectrum, last-line). Refractory: combination, levomepromazine, dexamethasone (4-8mg), CSCI, nasogastric decompression, palliative stenting.

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: 4 main pathways of N&V? A: CTZ (5-HT3, D2), vestibular (H1, M1), gut vagal afferents (5-HT3, D2), higher centres (cortex).

2. 2. Q: Antiemetic receptor targets? A: Haloperidol D2; cyclizine H1/M1; ondansetron 5-HT3; metoclopramide D2/5-HT4; levomepromazine broad.

3. 3. Q: First-line for opioid-induced N&V? A: Haloperidol (D2) or metoclopramide (D2/5-HT4).

4. 4. Q: Refractory N&V in palliative care? A: Combination of above + levomepromazine 6.25mg, dexamethasone, CSCI.

5. 5. Q: Bowel obstruction management? A: Cyclizine, haloperidol, dexamethasone, hyoscine (anticholinergic, antisecretory), octreotide; avoid metoclopramide (prokinetic, contraindicated).
