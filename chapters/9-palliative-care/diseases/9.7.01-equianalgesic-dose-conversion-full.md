# Equianalgesic dose conversion

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Opioid Management**

## Definition

Equianalgesic dose conversion is the calculation of equivalent analgesic doses between different opioids and routes, used for opioid rotation and switching. Oral morphine 30mg = oral oxycodone 20mg = oral hydromorphone 8mg = oral tramadol 150mg (4-hourly) = oral codeine 200mg (less reliable) = SC morphine 15mg = IV morphine 10mg = transdermal fentanyl 12-25 mcg/hr (based on 24h morphine 30-60mg) = transdermal buprenorphine 5-20 mcg/hr (based on 24h morphine 10-35mg). For most opioids, oral to SC ratio is 2:1 (PO:SC) and PO to IV is 3:1. For rotation, reduce calculated dose by 25-30% for incomplete cross-tolerance.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Equianalgesic tables allow conversion between different opioid analgesics, useful in opioid rotation. Approximate oral morphine equivalent (OME) doses: morphine PO 30mg/day = oxycodone PO 20mg/day = hydromorphone PO 6mg/day = tramadol PO 300mg/day = codeine PO 240mg/day = fentanyl 25mcg/hr patch = buprenorphine 0.8mcg/hr patch. Reduce new dose by 25-50% (incomplete cross-tolerance).


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Document current opioid and dose, calculate OME, choose target opioid, calculate new dose, reduce 25-50%, monitor.

### Management
Use validated conversion tools. Apply 25-50% reduction for incomplete cross-tolerance. Round to available formulations. Monitor for withdrawal (if reducing) or overdose (if too high). Breakthrough dose = 1/6 of total daily dose.

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Equianalgesic oral morphine 30mg = ? A: Oxycodone 20mg, hydromorphone 6mg, fentanyl 25mcg/hr, buprenorphine 0.8mcg/hr.

2. 2. Q: Why reduce new dose by 25-50%? A: Incomplete cross-tolerance (less analgesic effect with same dose, more side effects).

3. 3. Q: Breakthrough dose? A: 1/6 of total daily dose.

4. 4. Q: Common errors in conversion? A: Forgetting to reduce, miscalculating, missing breakthrough.

5. 5. Q: Methadone conversion? A: Specialist only; complex due to NMDA and SNRI effects, variable half-life.
