# Syringe drivers (CSCI)

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — End of Life Care

## Definition

Continuous subcutaneous infusion (CSCI, 'syringe driver') is a portable pump delivering subcutaneous infusion over 24 hours, used in palliative care when oral route is not possible (dysphagia, vomiting, weakness, intestinal obstruction). Drugs commonly used: morphine/diamorphine, midazolam, haloperidol, levomepromazine, cyclizine, glycopyrronium, hyoscine butylbromide, dexamethasone, metoclopramide, ondansetron. Compatibility: limited — usually 2-3 drugs in one syringe; check compatibility tables (most common are diamorphine + midazolam + glycopyrronium, or diamorphine + haloperidol + midazolam). Site: anterior abdominal wall, thigh, upper arm; rotate every 3-5 days; can use chest if edematous. Volume: usually 10-20mL over 24h.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

CSCI (continuous subcutaneous infusion, 'syringe driver') is portable pump delivering SC infusion over 24h, used in palliative care when oral route not possible. Drugs commonly used: morphine/diamorphine, midazolam, haloperidol, levomepromazine, cyclizine, glycopyrronium, hyoscine butylbromide, dexamethasone, metoclopramide, ondansetron.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Compatibility (check tables; usually 2-3 drugs in one syringe); site (anterior abdominal wall, thigh, upper arm; rotate 3-5 days); volume (10-20mL over 24h).

### Management
Compatibility: e.g., diamorphine + midazolam + glycopyrronium. Site: anterior abdominal wall, thigh, upper arm; rotate every 3-5 days. Volume: 10-20mL over 24h. Diamorphine: more water-soluble than morphine, preferred for SC. Glycopyrronium: anticholinergic, no CNS penetration, no sedation. Site reaction: common; rotate; consider alternative drug or route. Syringe driver for symptom control, not for hydration.
