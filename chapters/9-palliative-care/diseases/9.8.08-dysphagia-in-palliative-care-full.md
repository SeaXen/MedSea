# Dysphagia in palliative care

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care

## Definition

Dysphagia (difficulty swallowing) is a common, distressing symptom in palliative care, often in head and neck cancer, oesophageal cancer, neurological disease (motor neurone disease, stroke, Parkinson's, dementia), and advanced debility. Causes: mechanical (tumour obstruction, stricture), neurological (bulbar palsy, neuromuscular), functional (weakness, cognitive, debility). Assessment: clinical, swallow assessment by speech and language therapist, video fluoroscopy if needed. Management: treat cause if possible (stent for oesophageal obstruction, radiotherapy, dilation), modify diet (soft, puréed, thickened fluids), feeding tubes (NG, PEG) — careful ethical consideration, mouth care, posture, calm environment. Consider advance care planning: eating for pleasure vs nutrition, comfort feeding, withdrawing artificial nutrition.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Dysphagia (difficulty swallowing) in palliative care is distressing, common in head and neck cancer, oesophageal cancer, neurological disease (MND, stroke, Parkinson's, dementia), advanced debility. Causes: mechanical (tumour obstruction, stricture), neurological (bulbar palsy, neuromuscular), functional (weakness, cognitive, debility). Aspiration risk: pneumonia, choking.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Clinical, SALT swallow assessment, video fluoroscopy if needed.

### Management
Treat cause if possible (stent for oesophageal obstruction, radiotherapy, dilation), modify diet (soft, puréed, thickened fluids, IDDSI levels), feeding tubes (NG, PEG) — careful ethical consideration, mouth care, posture, calm environment. Consider advance care planning: eating for pleasure vs nutrition, comfort feeding, withdrawing artificial nutrition (ethically equivalent to withholding).

## Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Odynophagia | Painful swallow |
| Globus | Sensation without dysphagia |
| Mechanical vs neurological | SALT can differentiate |



## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line? A: SALT assessment + modify diet (soft, puréed, thickened fluids).

2. 2. Q: Stent indication? A: Oesophageal obstruction (tumour, stricture); rapid relief, alternative to PEG.

3. 3. Q: PEG considerations? A: Ethics (prolonging life vs quality), advance care plan, patient wishes, family.

4. 4. Q: Withdrawing artificial nutrition? A: Ethically equivalent to withholding; comfort care at end of life; requires MDT and family discussion.

5. 5. Q: Aspiration risk? A: Pneumonia, choking, choking on secretions; treat infection; comfort.


## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **Dysphagia in pall care** = SALT + modify diet + treat cause + ethics
