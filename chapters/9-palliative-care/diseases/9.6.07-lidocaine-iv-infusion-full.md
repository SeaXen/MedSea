## 1. 1. Definition

Lidocaine IV infusion is a parenteral adjuvant analgesic for refractory neuropathic pain (PHN, diabetic neuropathy, post-surgical, central pain, cancer pain). Mechanism: sodium channel blockade, anti-inflammatory, NMDA effects. Dose: 1-5mg/kg IV bolus then 1-3mg/kg/h infusion over 1-7 days (often 4-6h for trial). Contraindications: severe hepatic/renal failure, heart block, severe heart failure, epilepsy, pregnancy. Monitoring: ECG (QT, PR, QRS), BP, heart rate, neurological status (perioral numbness, tinnitus, seizures = toxicity). Side effects: CNS (perioral numbness, tinnitus, dizziness, tremor, seizures), cardiac (arrhythmias, heart block, hypotension), methaemoglobinaemia. Often used as inpatient trial to predict response; outpatient continuous infusion in select patients.

## 2. 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Lidocaine IV infusion is a parenteral adjuvant analgesic for refractory neuropathic pain (PHN, diabetic neuropathy, post-surgical, central pain, cancer pain). Mechanism: sodium channel blockade (NaV 1.7, 1.8, 1.9 in pain pathways), anti-inflammatory, NMDA effects. Dose: 1-5mg/kg IV bolus then 1-3mg/kg/h infusion over 1-7 days (often 4-6h for trial). Contraindications: severe hepatic/renal failure, heart block, severe heart failure, epilepsy, pregnancy. Side effects: CNS (perioral numbness, tinnitus, dizziness, tremor, seizures), cardiac (arrhythmias, heart block, hypotension, bradycardia), methaemoglobinaemia (rare). Monitoring: ECG (QT, PR, QRS), BP, heart rate, neurological status. Often used as inpatient trial to predict response.


## 3. 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### 1. Investigations
ECG (QT, PR, QRS) before and during infusion. Renal, hepatic function. Pain assessment, baseline, during, after. Drug levels (rarely used clinically). Methaemoglobin if cyanosis. Adverse effect monitoring: perioral numbness, tinnitus (early), tremor, seizures (toxicity).

### 2. Management
Indication: refractory neuropathic pain. Trial: often inpatient 4-6h; 1-3mg/kg IV infusion; assess pain relief. Continued: if response, consider CSCI lidocaine or repeat courses. Monitoring: ECG, BP, neurological status. Contraindications: severe hepatic/renal failure, heart block, heart failure, epilepsy. Side effects: perioral numbness, tinnitus (stop or reduce), dizziness, tremor, seizures (stop, supportive), cardiac (arrhythmias, hypotension). Combined with systemic opioid and adjuvant. Outpatient continuous infusion in select patients (rare, specialist).
