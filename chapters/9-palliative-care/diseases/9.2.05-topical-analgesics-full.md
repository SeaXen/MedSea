> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Opioid Analgesics**

## 1. 1. Definition

Topical analgesics are applied directly to the skin for localised pain, avoiding systemic side effects. Classes: topical NSAIDs (diclofenac gel, ibuprofen gel — for soft tissue/joint pain), topical capsaicin (0.025-0.075% cream, 8% patch for PHN — depletes substance P), topical lidocaine (5% patch/cream for PHN, neuropathic), topical salicylates (methyl salicylate). Useful for osteoarthritis (knee, hand), neuropathic pain (PHN), and patients who can't tolerate systemic NSAIDs.

## 2. 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

Topical analgesics: capsaicin (TRPV1 agonist, depletes substance P, effective for post-herpetic neuralgia, diabetic neuropathy, osteoarthritis; 0.025% cream BD-TDS, 8% patch single application); lidocaine 5% patch (sodium channel blocker, neuropathic pain, especially localised; max 3 patches 12h/day); topical NSAIDs (diclofenac 1-3% gel, ibuprofen gel; effective for soft tissue/joint, less systemic side effects). Useful when systemic NSAIDs contraindicated (CKD, GI risk, elderly), or in localised neuropathic pain.


## 3. 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### 1. Investigations
Localised pain assessment, photosensitivity, pre-application skin integrity. No specific investigations. EMLA/lidocaine toxicity in overdose.

### 2. Management
Apply to intact skin, avoid broken skin, eyes, mucosa. Capsaicin: pre-treat with topical anaesthetic; 8% patch requires pre-treatment due to burning. Lidocaine 5% patch: max 3 patches 12h/day, no other local anaesthetic, caution with antiarrhythmic drugs. Topical NSAID: thin layer, avoid occlusion.

## 5. 5. Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Capsaicin vs lidocaine | Capsaicin = TRPV1, substance P depletion; lidocaine = Na+ channel blocker |
| Topical vs systemic NSAID | Less systemic effects; localised only |



## 6. 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Capsaicin mechanism? A: TRPV1 agonist, depletes substance P from sensory neurons.

2. 2. Q: Capsaicin 8% patch indication? A: Post-herpetic neuralgia; single application 60min; needs pre-treatment.

3. 3. Q: Lidocaine 5% patch indication? A: Localised neuropathic pain (PHN); max 3 patches 12h/day.

4. 4. Q: Topical NSAID vs systemic? A: Less systemic side effects, effective for localised soft tissue pain.

5. 5. Q: Common side effect of capsaicin? A: Burning sensation on application (TRPV1 activation); reduced with repeated use.
