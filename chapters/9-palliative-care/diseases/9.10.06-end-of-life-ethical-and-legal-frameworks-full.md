# End-of-life ethical and legal frameworks

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — End of Life Care

## Definition

End-of-life ethical and legal frameworks guide decisions about withdrawing/withholding treatment, palliative sedation, euthanasia, and physician-assisted suicide. UK legal framework: Mental Capacity Act 2005 (capacity assessment, best interests, ADRT, lasting power of attorney), Suicide Act 1961 (assisting suicide is illegal in UK, but prosecution rarely pursued for compassionate cases), common law on withdrawing/withholding treatment (ethically and legally equivalent). International: euthanasia legal in Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, Colombia, Canada (MAID), Western Australia, Spain, New Zealand; PAS legal in some US states (Oregon, Washington, California, etc.), Switzerland (PASS), Germany. Medical consensus: palliative care is distinct from euthanasia/PAS; palliative sedation with appropriate intent is not euthanasia.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
None (clinical/legal).

### Management
UK: Mental Capacity Act 2005, Suicide Act 1961, common law. International: euthanasia legal in Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, Colombia, Canada (MAID), Western Australia, Spain, New Zealand; PAS legal in some US states, Switzerland, Germany. Palliative care is distinct from euthanasia/PAS. Capacity: assessment; if lacking, best-interests decision. ADRT: legally binding if valid and applicable. LPA: designate decision-maker for when capacity lacking. MDT: ethics committee, senior review, family, documentation.
