POISONING
Mushroom Poisoning
1 Learning Objectives
  • Classify mushroom poisoning by time to onset
  • Recognize Amanita phalloides (death cap) syndrome
  • Apply specific antidotes (silibinin, penicillin G, NAC)
  • Identify transplant criteria
  • Differentiate other toxic mushrooms (Gyromitra, Cortinarius, muscarine, psilocybin)
2 Definition

Mushroom poisoning = toxicity from ingestion of poisonous fungi causing gastrointestinal, hepatic, renal, neurological, and muscarinic syndromes depending on toxin.

3 Core Physiology
  • Classification by onset:
  • Early (< 6h): GI irritants (mild, self-limiting)
  • Delayed (> 6h, typically 6-24h): hepatotoxic (Amanita), nephrotoxic (Cortinarius), neurotoxic (Gyromitra, muscarine, psilocybin)
  • Amanita phalloides (Death Cap):
  • Amatoxins (α-amanitin): inhibit RNA polymerase II → block mRNA synthesis → cell death (liver, kidney, intestine)
  • Phallotoxins: GI irritation (not absorbed orally)
  • Lethal dose: ~0.1 mg/kg (1 cap = fatal)
  • Gyromitra (False Morel): monomethylhydrazine (MMH) → inhibits GABA synthesis (pyridoxine antagonist) → seizures; also hepatotoxic
  • Cortinarius: orellanine → interstitial nephritis → delayed renal failure (3-14 days)
  • Muscarine (Inocybe, Clitocybe): pure muscarinic agonist → SLUDGE (self-limiting)
  • Psilocybin (Magic mushrooms): 5-HT₂ₐ agonist → hallucinations, tachycardia, hypertension (self-limiting)
4 Clinical Features (By Syndrome)
1. Early GI Irritants (< 6h) — Most Common, Mild
  • Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain
  • Self-limiting (24-48h)
  • Examples: Chlorophyllum molybdites, Boletus, Omphalotus
2. Amanita phalloides (Death Cap) — **DEADLY**
PhaseTimeFeatures
-----------------------
**Latent**6-24h (6-12h typical)**Asymptomatic** — **"deceptive recovery"**
**GI**6-24hSevere vomiting, watery diarrhea (cholera-like), abdominal pain, dehydration
**Recovery**24-48h**Apparent improvement** — **false recovery**
**Hepatic**48-96h**Acute liver failure**: jaundice, coagulopathy (INR↑), encephalopathy, hypoglycemia, metabolic acidosis, AKI, DIC, death
3. Gyromitra (False Morel) — Neurotoxic + Hepatotoxic
  • Onset: 6-12h
  • GI: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
  • Neurological: seizures (pyridoxine-responsive), coma, cerebellar signs
  • Hepatic: hepatic necrosis (less consistent than Amanita)
4. Cortinarius (Webcaps) — Nephrotoxic
  • Onset: 3-14 days (delayed)
  • Early: mild GI symptoms
  • Late: oliguric/anuric renal failure (interstitial nephritis), proteinuria, hematuria
  • Non-renal: rare hepatic/CNS
5. Muscarinic (Inocybe, Clitocybe) — SLUDGE
  • Onset: 30 min - 2h
  • SLUDGE: Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Defecation, GI cramps, Emesis
  • Miosis, bradycardia, bronchospasm
  • Self-limiting (6-8h), atropine if severe
6. Psilocybin (Magic Mushrooms) — Hallucinogenic
  • Onset: 30 min - 1h
  • Hallucinations (visual, auditory), euphoria, anxiety, tachycardia, hypertension, mydriasis
  • Self-limiting (4-6h), supportive care
5 Differential Diagnosis
  • Amanita vs other delayed: Amanita = hepatic failure; Cortinarius = renal failure; Gyromitra = seizures
  • Acetaminophen: similar hepatic failure but history of ingestion
  • Viral hepatitis: prodrome, risk factors, serology
  • Ischemic hepatitis: shock, rapid AST/ALT rise/fall
  • Organophosphate: muscarinic + nicotinic + CNS
6 Investigations
  • Mushroom identification (mycologist if possible) — critical
  • LFTs, INR, bilirubin, glucose (serial q6-12h for Amanita)
  • Renal function: creatinine, urea, electrolytes
  • ABG/VBG: pH, lactate, glucose
  • CK: rhabdo (Gyromitra seizures)
  • Coagulation: DIC
  • Amatoxin level (blood/urine — specialized lab, not routine)
  • Paracetamol level (always — co-ingestion or DDx)
  • Pregnancy test (females childbearing age)
7 Management
1. General
  • Activated charcoal: 1 g/kg ASAP (ideally < 1-2h, but up to 6-12h for amatoxins — enterohepatic recirculation)
  • Whole bowel irrigation: consider for large ingestion
  • IV fluids: aggressive hydration, correct electrolytes
  • Monitor: LFTs, INR, glucose q6-12h, renal function, CK
2. Amanita phalloides — **SPECIFIC ANTIDOTES**
AntidoteDoseDurationNotes
---------------------------------
**Silibinin (IV)****5 mg/kg load** over 1h, then **20 mg/kg/day continuous infusion**3-5 days**Preferred**, inhibits amatoxin uptake by hepatocytes
**Penicillin G (High-dose)****300,000-400,000 units/kg/day** IV divided q4-6h (max 20-40M units/day)3-5 daysCompetes for OATP transporter
**N-acetylcysteine (NAC)**Standard IV protocol (SNAP 2-bag)20h+Antioxidant, glutathione precursor
**Vitamin C**1 g IV q6hAdjunctAntioxidant
**Thioctic acid (α-lipoic acid)**300-600 mg IV q6hAdjunctAntioxidant

Silibinin NOT available → use Penicillin G + NAC + supportive

3. Gyromitra
  • Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6): 25 mg/kg IV (max 2-5 g) over 30 min, repeat q4-6h for seizures
  • Benzodiazepines for seizures
  • NAC for hepatic protection
4. Cortinarius
  • Supportive: hydration, electrolyte management
  • Renal replacement for AKI (often needed)
  • No specific antidote
5. Muscarinic
  • Atropine 0.5-1 mg IV q5-10 min till secretions dry/HR > 80
  • Usually self-limiting
6. Psilocybin
  • Supportive: quiet environment, benzodiazepines for agitation/anxiety
  • Self-limiting
7. Transplant Criteria (Amanita) — **King's College + Lactate**
  • Any ONE:
  • Arterial pH < 7.30 (after resuscitation) OR
  • ALL THREE: INR > 6.5, Creatinine > 300 µmol/L, Grade III/IV encephalopathy PLUS
  • Lactate > 3.5 mmol/L (admission) or > 3.0 (post-resuscitation)
  • Early referral to transplant center (within 24h of ALF diagnosis)
8. Enhanced Elimination
  • MDAC: may help (enterohepatic recirculation of amatoxins)
  • Hemodialysis/hemoperfusion: limited (high protein binding) — consider early
  • Plasma exchange (TPE): removes amatoxins (protein-bound), early use recommended in severe cases
8 Complications
  • Acute liver failure (Amanita) → death or transplant
  • AKI (Amanita, Cortinarius, Gyromitra)
  • DIC
  • Seizures (Gyromitra)
  • Encephalopathy
  • Death (Amanita: 10-30% with treatment, higher without)
9 Prognosis
  • Amanita: 10-30% mortality with aggressive treatment; early silibinin + transplant referral improves survival
  • Cortinarius: renal recovery often incomplete (CKD)
  • Gyromitra: good if seizures controlled; hepatic failure possible
  • Muscarinic/Psilocybin: excellent
10 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
  1. Onset timing = classification: < 6h = GI irritant; > 6h = toxic (Amanita, Cortinarius, Gyromitra)
  2. Amanita phalloides: amatoxins inhibit RNA polymerase II → latent 6-24h → GI → false recovery → hepatic failure
  3. "Deceptive recovery" = hallmark of Amanita (patient feels better at 24-48h before liver failure)
  4. Silibinin 5mg/kg load + 20mg/kg/day infusion = preferred antidote
  5. Penicillin G 300-400K units/kg/day + NAC = alternatives/adjuncts
  6. Transplant criteria: King's College + lactate > 3.5
  7. Gyromitra: monomethylhydrazine → seizures (pyridoxine responsive) + hepatic
  8. Cortinarius: orellanine → delayed renal failure (3-14 days)
  9. Muscarinic: SLUDGE, atropine, self-limiting
  10. Psilocybin: hallucinations, tachycardia, self-limiting

12 Common Confusions / Exam Traps
  • All mushrooms = same → NO, onset timing defines toxicity
  • Amanita = immediate GI then liver → NO, latent 6-24h → GI → FALSE RECOVERY → liver failure
  • Silibinin = oral → NO, IV only (IV silibinin = silibinin dihemisuccinate)
  • Penicillin G = standard dose → NO, HIGH DOSE 300-400K units/kg/day
  • Cortinarius = hepatic → NO, renal (orellanine)
  • Gyromitra = only hepatic → NO, neuro (seizures) + hepatic
  • Early charcoal useless for delayed → NO, charcoal up to 12h for amatoxins (enterohepatic recirculation)
13 Mnemonics
  • MUSHROOM ONSET: Early (<6h) = GI; Delayed (>6h) = Liver/Kidney/Brain
  • AMANITA PHASES: Latent 6-24h → GI → False Recovery → Hepatic Failure
  • DECEPTIVE RECOVERY: Deceptive Recovery = Amanita Hallmark
  • SILIBININ: 5 mg/kg load, 20 mg/kg/day infusion (IV)
  • PENICILLIN G: 300-400K units/kg/day (HIGH DOSE)
  • CORTINARIUS: Cortinarius = Kidney (orellanine) → Renal failure 3-14 days
  • GYROMITRA: Gyromitra = Seizures + Hepatic → Pyridoxine
  • TRANSPLANT: pH<7.3 OR INR>6.5+Cr>300+Enceph + Lactate>3.5
