> [!tip]
> **Iron = corrosive + metabolic toxin**. **5 stages**: GI (0-6h) → latent (6-24h) → shock/metabolic (12-48h) → hepatic (2-4d) → GI scarring (weeks). **Vin rosé urine** = deferoxamine-iron complex. **Deferoxamine** if systemic toxicity or level > 500 µg/dL. **Radiopaque pills on AXR**. Key FCPS/MRCP: elemental iron dose calculation (ferrous sulfate 20%, fumarate 33%, gluconate 12%); deferoxamine infusion 15 mg/kg/hr (max 80 mg/kg/24h); vin rosé urine; radiopaque tablets; stages.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Calculate elemental iron dose from preparation
- Recognize 5 clinical stages
- Apply deferoxamine indications and dosing
- Interpret radiopaque pills on abdominal X-ray
- Manage metabolic acidosis, shock, coagulopathy

## 2. Definition
Iron poisoning = toxicity from elemental iron causing **direct GI corrosion** and **systemic metabolic toxicity** (mitochondrial dysfunction, vascular injury, hepatic necrosis).

## 3. Core Physiology
- **Mechanism**:
  1. **Local corrosion**: Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ → GI mucosal necrosis, hemorrhage, stricture
  2. **Systemic**: free iron → **mitochondrial uncoupling** → lactic acidosis; **endothelial damage** → vasodilation, capillary leak, shock; **hepatic necrosis**; **coagulopathy**
- **Preparations & Elemental Iron %**:
  - Ferrous sulfate: **20%** (60 mg tablet = 12 mg elemental)
  - Ferrous fumarate: **33%** (200 mg tablet = 66 mg elemental)
  - Ferrous gluconate: **12%** (300 mg tablet = 36 mg elemental)
  - Ferrous succinate: **35%**
  - Carbonyl iron: **100%** (pure elemental)
- **Toxic dose**: **> 40 mg/kg elemental iron** (moderate); **> 60 mg/kg** (severe)
- **Absorption**: saturable, delayed by food/antacids; peak 2-4h (longer with enteric-coated)

## 4. Clinical Features (Five Stages)

| Stage | Time | Features |
|-------|------|----------|
| **I: GI** | 0-6h | **Vomiting (hematemesis), abdominal pain, diarrhea, hematochezia**, GI bleeding, lethargy |
| **II: Latent** | 6-24h | **Apparent improvement** (deceptive), but ongoing absorption |
| **III: Shock/Metabolic** | 12-48h | **Metabolic acidosis, shock, lethargy/coma, seizures, coagulopathy, hyperglycemia, fever** |
| **IV: Hepatic** | 2-4d | **Hepatic failure** (AST/ALT ↑↑, INR ↑, bilirubin ↑, hypoglycemia, encephalopathy) |
| **V: GI Scarring** | 2-6 weeks | **Gastric outlet obstruction, pyloric stenosis, esophageal strictures** |

## 5. Differential Diagnosis
- **Caustic ingestion**: oral burns, drooling, no metabolic acidosis early
- **Salicylate**: tinnitus, respiratory alkalosis + metabolic acidosis
- **Methanol/EG**: osmolar gap, specific metabolites
- **Sepsis**: fever, shock, but no iron pills on AXR

## 6. Investigations
- **Serum iron level** — drawn **4-6h post-ingestion** (peak absorption); **> 300 µg/dL (53 µmol/L)** = significant; **> 500 µg/dL (90 µmol/L)** = severe (treat)
- **TIBC** (total iron binding capacity) — **free iron = serum iron - TIBC** (if > 0, toxic)
- **ABG/VBG** — metabolic acidosis, lactate
- **Electrolytes, glucose, renal, LFTs, coagulation, CK**
- **Abdominal X-ray** — **radiopaque tablets** visible (iron is radiopaque) — **quantitate pill count**
- **Urine color** — **"vin rosé" (pink/red)** = deferoxamine-iron complex (after deferoxamine)
- **Pregnancy test** (females)

## 7. Management

### 1. Resuscitation (ABCDE)
- **Airway**: protect if vomiting blood, GCS < 8
- **Breathing**: O₂, ventilation if needed
- **Circulation**: **aggressive IV fluids** for shock (capillary leak); vasopressors (NE) if refractory
- **Correct acidosis**: bicarbonate for pH < 7.2
- **Correct coagulopathy**: FFP, platelets, vitamin K if bleeding

### 2. Decontamination
- **Whole Bowel Irrigation (WBI)** — **INDICATED** for iron (radiopaque, sustained-release, not adsorbed by charcoal)
  - **Polyethylene glycol (PEG) 1-2 L/hr** via NG tube until clear effluent
  - **Airway must be protected**
- **Activated charcoal**: **NOT effective** (does not adsorb iron)
- **Gastric lavage**: **contraindicated** (corrosive, perforation risk)
- **Emesis**: contraindicated

### 3. Deferoxamine (Desferrioxamine) — **SPECIFIC ANTIDOTE**
- **Mechanism**: chelates free iron → **ferrioxamine** (water-soluble, renally excreted)
- **Indications** (Any One):
  - **Systemic toxicity** (metabolic acidosis, shock, lethargy, coma, seizures)
  - **Serum iron > 500 µg/dL (90 µmol/L)**
  - **Vomiting/hematemesis** + radiopaque pills on AXR
  - **Ingestion > 60 mg/kg elemental iron**
- **Dose**: **IV infusion 15 mg/kg/hr** (max **80 mg/kg/24h**)
  - Typical: 1 g in 100 mL NS/D5W at 15 mg/kg/hr
  - **Do NOT bolus** (hypotension, anaphylaxis)
- **Duration**: until **clinical improvement + iron level falling + urine color normal**
  - Usually 12-24h, may need longer
- **"Vin Rosé" Urine** — **pink/red urine** = ferrioxamine complex excreted — **confirms chelation working**
- **Adverse**: hypotension (rapid infusion), ARDS (prolonged > 24h high dose), Yersinia sepsis (iron-rich environment), allergic

### 4. Enhanced Elimination
- **Hemodialysis**: **NOT effective** for iron (ferrioxamine not well dialyzed)
- **Hemoperfusion**: not routinely used

### 5. Surgical
- **Endoscopy** (Stage I): assess GI corrosion (grade 1-3) — **avoid if perforation suspected**
- **Stricture dilation/surgery** (Stage V): delayed (weeks-months)

## 8. Complications
- GI perforation, hemorrhage
- Shock, multi-organ failure
- Hepatic failure
- Coagulopathy, DIC
- GI strictures (late)
- Yersinia sepsis (with deferoxamine)

## 9. Prognosis
- **Good if deferoxamine started early** (Stage I/II)
- **Mortality**: < 1% if treated; 10-20% if Stage III shock
- **Late strictures** in 10-20% of significant ingestions

## 10. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
1. **Elemental iron calculation**: sulfate 20%, fumarate 33%, gluconate 12%
2. **Toxic dose**: > 40 mg/kg elemental (moderate); > 60 mg/kg (severe)
3. **5 Stages**: GI → Latent (deceptive!) → Shock/Metabolic → Hepatic → GI Scarring
4. **Serum iron > 500 µg/dL** = treat with deferoxamine
5. **Deferoxamine 15 mg/kg/hr IV infusion** (max 80 mg/kg/24h) — **NO bolus**
5. **"Vin rosé" urine** = deferoxamine-iron complex (ferrioxamine)
6. **Abdominal X-ray**: radiopaque tablets visible — count pills
7. **WBI indicated** (PEG 1-2 L/hr) — charcoal NOT effective
8. **Latent phase** = deceptive improvement, ongoing absorption
9. **Hepatic failure** Stage IV (2-4d)
10. **Pill count on AXR** correlates with severity

## 12. Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- **Charcoal for iron** → NO, doesn't adsorb
- **Gastric lavage** → NO, corrosive risk
- **Deferoxamine bolus** → NO, hypotension/anaphylaxis
- **Serum iron at presentation** → may be low (not yet absorbed) — repeat at 4-6h
- **TIBC** — use transferrin saturation > 50% or free iron calculation
- **Latent phase** = NOT recovery — ongoing absorption
- **Vin rosé urine** = AFTER deferoxamine, not before
- **Radiopaque pills** = only iron and few others (Ca, heavy metals)
