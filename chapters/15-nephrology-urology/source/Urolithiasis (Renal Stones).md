# Urolithiasis (Renal Stones)

**Related:** [[Urinary Tract Obstruction]], [[Investigation of Renal and Urinary Tract Disease]], [[Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)]], [[Functional Anatomy and Physiology of the Kidney and Urinary Tract]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **Renal stones = crystalline deposits in urinary tract. Lifetime risk ~10–15%. Peak 30–50yrs, M>F. Types: Calcium oxalate (80%), Calcium phosphate (15%), Uric acid (10%), Struvite (10–15%), Cystine (<1%). Risk: low fluid, high salt/animal protein, hypercalciuria, hyperuricosuria, low citrate, obesity, DM. Management: acute colic (NSAIDs, alpha-blocker), definitive (ESWL, URS, PCNL), prevention (fluids, diet, metabolic workup).**

## Stone Classification

| Type | % | Composition | Risk Factors | Urine pH |
|------|---|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **Calcium Oxalate (CaOx)** | **80%** | CaC₂O₄ (monohydrate/dihydrate) | Hypercalciuria, hyperoxaluria, hypocitraturia, low volume | Acidic/neutral |
| **Calcium Phosphate (CaP)** | 15% | CaHPO₄, Ca₃(PO₄)₂ (brushite, apatite) | Incomplete RTA, high urine pH, hypercalciuria | **Alkaline (>6.5)** |
| **Uric Acid** | 10% | Uric acid | **Low urine pH (<5.5)**, hyperuricosuria, gout, myeloproliferative, chemotherapy | **Acidic (<5.5)** |
| **Struvite (Magnesium Ammonium Phosphate)** | 10–15% | MgNH₄PO₄·6H₂O | **UTI with urease-producers** (Proteus, Klebsiella, Pseudomonas); staghorn calculi | Alkaline |
| **Cystine** | <1% | Cystine (amino acid) | **Autosomal recessive** (SLC3A1, SLC7A9); cystinuria | Acidic |
| **Drug-Induced** | Rare | Indinavir, atazanavir, triamterene, sulfa, aciclovir, ceftriaxone | Medication use | Variable |

## Pathophysiology

| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Supersaturation** | [Ion product] > [Solubility product] → crystallisation |
| **Promoters** | High Ca, oxalate, urate, low citrate, low volume, low pH (uric acid), high pH (CaP/struvite) |
| **Inhibitors** | **Citrate** (chelates Ca), **Magnesium**, **Pyrophosphate**, **Tamm-Horsfall protein**, **Nephrocalcin** |
| **Randall's Plaque** | Suburothelial CaP deposits → nidus for CaOx stones |

## Clinical Presentation

| Syndrome | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Renal Colic** | **Sudden severe loin → groin pain**, radiating to testis/labia; nausea/vomiting; restlessness (vs peritonitis: still); haematuria (85%) |
| **Asymptomatic / Incidental** | Found on imaging for other reasons |
| **UTI / Pyelonephritis** | Struvite stones, infected obstruction |
| **Obstructive AKI** | Bilateral/solitary kidney stone → anuria, AKI |
| **Chronic CKD** | Large staghorn, bilateral stones, recurrent obstruction |

## Diagnostic Workup

### Imaging (Acute)
| Modality | Role | Sensitivity/Specificity |
|----------|------|------------------------|
| **CT KUB (Non-contrast)** | **Gold standard** | **>95% / >95%**; stone size, location, density (HU), hydronephrosis, alternative dx |
| **US KUB** | First-line (pregnancy, children, radiation avoidance) | 60–80% for stones; good for hydronephrosis |
| **Plain X-ray (KUB)** | Radio-opaque stones only (CaOx, CaP, Struvite); misses uric acid, cystine, indinavir | Low |
| **IVU / CT Urogram** | Anatomy, function (excretory phase); pre-surgery planning | Historical |

### Stone Density (Hounsfield Units on CT)
| Stone Type | HU Range |
|------------|----------|
| **Calcium Oxalate/Phosphate** | **>400–1000+** (dense) |
| **Struvite** | 300–500 |
| **Cystine** | 200–400 |
| **Uric Acid** | **<400 (200–400)** — radiolucent on X-ray |
| **Drug (Indinavir)** | <200 (often invisible) |

---

## Metabolic Evaluation (Indicated If)

| Indication | Tests |
|------------|-------|
| **Recurrent stones** (≥2) | 2× 24h urine: volume, pH, Ca, oxalate, urate, citrate, Na, K, Mg, creatinine |
| **High-risk** (solitary kidney, transplant, gastric bypass, bowel disease) | Blood: Ca, PO4, urate, bicarbonate, PTH, Vitamin D; Stone analysis (FTIR) |
| **Paediatric / Young onset** | Full metabolic workup |
| **Atypical stone type** (uric acid, cystine, struvite, brushite) | Targeted workup |

> [!key]
> **24h urine**: Volume >2.5L/day target; Ca <7.5mmol/day (M) / <6.2 (F); Oxalate <0.5mmol/day; Citrate >3mmol/day; Urate <3.5mmol/day; pH 6.0–6.5 (uric acid target >6.5).

---

## Acute Management (Renal Colic)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. Analgesia** | **NSAID 1st line** (Diclofenac 75mg IM/PR; Ibuprofen 400–600mg PO) — **superior to opioids**, less nausea; Opioid (morphine) if NSAID contraindicated |
| **2. Antiemetic** | Metoclopramide / ondansetron |
| **3. Hydration** | **Oral >2.5L/day** (IV if vomiting); **do NOT overhydrate** acutely (↑ intraluminal pressure) |
| **4. Medical Expulsive Therapy (MET)** | **Tamsulosin 400mcg OD** (alpha-blocker) for **distal ureteric stones <10mm** — ↑ spontaneous passage, ↓ time, ↓ analgesia |
| **5. Admission Indicators** | Infection (fever, sepsis), solitary kidney, bilateral obstruction, intractable pain/vomiting, AKI, pregnancy |

---

## Definitive Stone Management

| Stone Factor | 1st Line | Alternatives |
|--------------|----------|--------------|
| **<10mm, distal ureter** | **MET (tamsulosin)** + analgesia | ESWL, URS |
| **<10mm, proximal ureter** | **ESWL** | URS |
| **10–20mm, renal/upper ureter** | **ESWL** (if favourable: <1000 HU, <10mm, lower pole <30°) | **URS** (flexible), PCNL |
| **>20mm / Staghorn / Complex** | **PCNL** | Staged URS, ESWL (adjunct) |
| **Lower pole 10–20mm** | **URS** (flexible) | ESWL (if <10mm, favourable anatomy) |
| **Uric Acid Stones** | **Alkalinisation** (K-citrate, target urine pH 6.5–7.0) → dissolution | ESWL, URS |
| **Cystine Stones** | High fluid (>3L), alkalinisation (pH >7.5), thiol drugs (D-penicillamine, tiopronin) | ESWL (poor fragmentation), URS, PCNL |
| **Struvite (Infection)** | **Complete surgical removal (PCNL/URS) + culture-directed antibiotics** | ESWL alone contraindicated (residual fragments = recurrent infection) |

---

## Prevention (All Stone Formers)

| Measure | Target |
|---------|--------|
| **Fluid Intake** | **>2.5–3L/day** (urine output >2–2.5L) — single most effective |
| **Diet** | Normal Ca (1000–1200mg) — **do NOT restrict**; Low salt (<5g/day); Low animal protein; High fruit/veg (citrate); Avoid excess oxalate (spinach, nuts, tea) if hyperoxaluria |
| **Urine pH** | CaOx/CaP: 6.0–6.5; Uric acid: **>6.5** (alkalinisation); Cystine: **>7.5** |
| **Pharmacological** | **Thiazide** (hypercalciuria); **Potassium citrate** (hypocitraturia, uric acid, cystine); **Allopurinol/febuxostat** (hyperuricosuria/gout); **D-penicillamine/tiopronin** (cystinuria) |

---

## Special Populations

| Population | Considerations |
|------------|----------------|
| **Pregnancy** | US only (no CT); conservative (stent/nephrostomy if obstruction); avoid NSAIDs 3rd trimester; MET controversial |
| **Paediatric** | US first; metabolic workup mandatory (cystinuria, hypercalciuria, RTA); ESWL/URS preferred |
| **Obesity / BMI >30** | CT KUB still gold; PCNL technically harder; ESWL less effective (skin-to-stone distance >10cm) |
| **Anticoagulation** | Hold for surgery; ESWL/URS possible with bridging; stent if urgent |
| **Solitary Kidney / Transplant** | Lower threshold for intervention; stent/nephrostomy for obstruction; MET cautious |

---

## Complications

| Complication | Management |
|--------------|------------|
| **Infected Obstruction (Pyonephrosis)** | **Emergency decompression** (nephrostomy/stent) + IV antibiotics |
| **Ureteric Injury (iatrogenic)** | Stent 4–6 weeks; ureteric reimplantation if complete |
| **Steinstrasse** (stone street) | After ESWL; stent, URS for large fragments |
| **Recurrence** | 50% at 5 years, 70% at 10 years without prevention |

## High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **Calcium oxalate = commonest (80%)**
> - **Risk**: low fluid, high salt/protein, hypercalciuria, hypocitraturia, obesity, DM
> - **Renal colic**: sudden loin→groin pain, restlessness, haematuria; **NSAID 1st line** (diclofenac IM)
> - **CT KUB = gold standard** (size, location, HU, hydronephrosis)
> - **Uric acid stones: radiolucent on X-ray, low HU (<400), low pH (<5.5)** → alkalinisation dissolves
> - **Struvite = UTI with urease-producers (Proteus); staghorn; complete removal + antibiotics**
> - **Cystine = genetic (AR); high fluid, alkalinisation pH >7.5, thiol drugs**
> - **MET**: tamsulosin 400mcg for distal ureteric <10mm
> - **ESWL**: <10mm proximal, favourable anatomy; PCNL >20mm/staghorn
> - **Prevention**: >2.5L urine/day, normal Ca diet, low salt, potassium citrate (hypocitraturia), thiazide (hypercalciuria)
> - **24h urine metabolic workup**: recurrent, high-risk, young, atypical stones

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **Low Ca diet prevents stones** | **Normal Ca (1000–1200mg) protects** (binds oxalate in gut); low Ca ↑ oxalate absorption |
| **Uric acid stones = radiopaque** | **Radiolucent on X-ray**; low HU on CT |
| **ESWL for all stones** | **PCNL for >20mm/staghorn**; ESWL fails for cystine, brushite, dense stones |
| **MET for all stones** | **Distal ureteric <10mm only** |
| **Struvite = treat with antibiotics alone** | **Complete surgical removal required** (residual = recurrent infection) |
| **Cystine stones fragment well with ESWL** | **Poor fragmentation**; URS/PCNL preferred |
| **24h urine = single collection** | **Need 2 collections** (variability) |
| **Citrate only for hypocitraturia** | Also for uric acid (alkalinisation) and cystine |
| **All stones need surgery** | Many <5mm pass spontaneously; MET helps |
| **Overhydration helps acute colic** | **Avoid IV overhydration** (↑ pressure); oral 2.5L/day |

## Summary

**Urolithiasis** is a **Must Know 🔴** topic.
**Key takeaway:** **CaOx 80%** commonest. Risk: low fluid, high salt/protein, hypercalciuria, hypocitraturia. **Renal colic**: loin→groin, restlessness; **NSAID 1st line** (diclofenac IM). **CT KUB gold standard** (size, HU, hydronephrosis). **Uric acid**: radiolucent, low HU <400, pH <5.5 → alkalinisation dissolves. **Struvite**: UTI + urease (Proteus), staghorn; **complete removal + antibiotics**. **Cystine**: AR, hexagonal crystals; fluid >3L, pH >7.5, thiol drugs. **MET**: tamsulosin distal <10mm. **ESWL**: <10mm proximal favourable. **PCNL**: >20mm/staghorn. **Prevention**: >2.5L urine/day, normal Ca, low salt, citrate, thiazide. **Metabolic workup**: recurrent, high-risk, young, atypical stones.
**Exam focus:** Stone types, renal colic management, CT KUB, MET, ESWL/URS/PCNL indications, uric acid alkalinisation, struvite complete removal, cystine genetics, prevention, metabolic workup.
**Clinical relevance:** High recurrence (50% at 5yrs); dietary/fluid modification + targeted pharmacotherapy dramatically reduce recurrence; CT KUB avoids radiation of IVU.
