# Neurogenic Bladder

**Related:** [[Urinary Tract Infection (UTI, Pyelonephritis, Prostatitis, Catheter-Associated)]], [[Urinary Incontinence]], [[Urinary Tract Obstruction]], [[Spinal Cord Injury]], [[Neurology]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **Neurogenic bladder = bladder dysfunction due to CNS/PNS lesions. Classification by lesion level: Suprasacral (UMN) = detrusor overactivity + DSD; Sacral/Infrasacral (LMN) = areflexia/underactivity. Key: Urodynamics (pressure-flow) essential. Complications: UTI, stones, hydronephrosis, VUR, renal failure. Management: CIC, anticholinergics, botox, augmentation.**

---

## Learning Objectives
- Classify neurogenic bladder by lesion level (UMN vs LMN)
- Apply urodynamic interpretation (compliance, DSD, detrusor pressure)

- Apply to FCPS/MRCP spinal cord injury / myelomeningocele vignettes

## Classification by Lesion Level

| Lesion Level | Type | Bladder Behaviour | Sphincter | Urodynamics |
|--------------|------|-------------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Suprasacral** (UMN) | **Hyperreflexic** (Overactive) | **Detrusor overactivity** (involuntary contractions) | **DSD** (Detrusor-Sphincter Dyssynergia) — external sphincter contracts during detrusor contraction | High detrusor pressure, low compliance, DSD |
| **Sacral/Infrasacral** (LMN) | **Areflexic** (Underactive) | **Areflexia / Acontractility** — no detrusor contraction | **Flaccid** (no voluntary control) | Low detrusor pressure, high capacity, no DSD |
| **Conus Medullaris** | Mixed | Variable (often areflexic detrusor + DSD possible) | Mixed | Mixed |

> **UMN (Suprasacral)** = spastic bladder + DSD → high pressure → **upper tract risk**.
> **LMN (Sacral)** = flaccid bladder → retention → overflow incontinence + UTI.

---

## Common Aetiologies
| Level | Causes |
|-------|--------|
| **Suprasacral (UMN)** | Spinal cord injury (T12/L1), Multiple sclerosis, Transverse myelitis, Spinal tumours, Cerebral palsy, Stroke, Parkinson's |
| **Sacral/Infrasacral (LMN)** | Cauda equina syndrome, Sacral tumours, Spina bifida/Myelomeningocele, Diabetic neuropathy, Pelvic surgery (rectal/prostate), Herpes zoster (S2–S4) |

---

## Clinical Presentation

### Suprasacral (UMN) — Hyperreflexic
| Symptom | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Urgency, frequency, urge incontinence** | Detrusor overactivity |
| **Incomplete emptying, retention** | DSD → functional obstruction |
| **Recurrent UTIs** | Stasis, high pressure |
| **Autonomic dysreflexia** (if lesion >T6) | Bladder distension → massive sympathetic surge → hypertension, bradycardia, headache |

### Sacral/Infrasacral (LMN) — Areflexic
| Symptom | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Overflow incontinence** | Acontractile detrusor → chronic retention → overflow |
| **Stress incontinence** | Flaccid sphincter + abdominal pressure |
| **Recurrent UTIs** | Stasis, incomplete emptying |
| **Saddle anaesthesia, lax anus** | Sacral root involvement |

---

## Urodynamics (Essential for Diagnosis & Management)

| Parameter | UMN (Suprasacral) | LMN (Sacral) |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Detrusor activity** | **Overactive** (involuntary contractions) | **Areflexic / Underactive** |
| **Compliance** | **Low** (stiff bladder) | High (flaccid) |
| **Detrusor pressure** | **High** (>40 cmH₂O risk) | Low |
| **DSD** | **Present** (EMG activity during contraction) | Absent |
| **Sphincter tone** | Hypertonic | Flaccid |
| **Bladder capacity** | Reduced | Increased |
| **Maximum flow rate** | Low (obstructed by DSD) | Low (no contractility) |

> **Detrusor leak point pressure (DLPP) >40 cmH₂O** = risk of upper tract damage (hydronephrosis, VUR).

---

## Diagnostic Workup
| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Urodynamics (video-urodynamics preferred)** | **Gold standard** — confirms type, measures pressures, DSD, compliance |
| **Renal US** | Screen for hydronephrosis, cortical thinning, stones |
| **DMSA scan** | Renal scarring (reflux nephropathy) |
| **VCUG** | Vesicoureteric reflux (VUR) grading |
| **Post-void residual (PVR)** | Quantifies retention |
| **Cystoscopy** | Anatomy, stones, bladder trabeculation |
| **MRI Spine** | Identifies lesion level if unknown |

---

## Management Principles

### 1. Clean Intermittent Catheterisation (CIC) — **Cornerstone**
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Indication** | PVR >100–150mL, inadequate emptying, high detrusor pressure |
| **Frequency** | q4–6h (target PVR <100mL) |
| **Technique** | Sterile vs clean (clean acceptable for chronic); hydrophilic catheters |
| **Complications** | UTI (most common), urethral stricture, false passages, haematuria |
| **Teaching** | Patient/caregiver training essential; lifelong in most |

> **CIC + Anticholinergic** = standard for UMN to protect upper tracts.

---

### 2. Pharmacotherapy
| Drug Class | Indication | Details |
|------------|------------|---------|
| **Anticholinergics** (Oxybutynin, Tolterodine, Solifenacin, Darifenacin, Fesoterodine) | **UMN** — detrusor overactivity, low compliance | Oral/Transdermal; side effects: dry mouth, constipation, cognitive (elderly), retention |
| **β₃-Agonist** (Mirabegron) | UMN (add-on or alternative) | Relax detrusor via β₃; fewer anticholinergic SEs |
| **α-Blockers** (Tamsulosin, Alfuzosin) | **DSD** (UMN) — ↓ sphincter resistance | Helps emptying with CIC |
| **Botulinum Toxin A** (OnabotulinumtoxinA) | Refractory UMN | 100–200U intradetrusor q6–12mo; may need CIC post-injection |
| **Baclofen (intrathecal)** | Severe spasticity/DSD | Intrathecal pump for spasticity + bladder |

---

### 3. Surgical Options (Refractory Cases)
| Procedure | Indication | Details |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Augmentation Cystoplasty** (ileum/colon/ileocaecal) | Low compliance, high pressure refractory to meds | ↑ capacity, ↓ pressure; CIC mandatory lifelong; risks: stones, mucus, metabolic acidosis, malignancy (long-term) |
| **Urinary Diversion** (ileal conduit, continent pouch) | Failed augmentation, patient preference | Stoma or catheterisable channel (Mitrofanoff) |
| **Sacral Neuromodulation** | Non-neurogenic + selected neurogenic | Not first-line for neurogenic; limited evidence |
| **Sphincterotomy** (endoscopic) | Male UMN with DSD refractory | ↓ outlet resistance; incontinence → condom drainage |
| **Artificial Urinary Sphincter (AUS)** | Stress incontinence (LMN) | Gold standard for sphincteric incontinence; revision rate high |

---

## Upper Tract Surveillance (Critical)
| Parameter | Frequency | Action Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|------------------|
| **Renal US** | q6–12mo | New hydronephrosis → investigate |
| **Serum Creatinine / eGFR** | q6–12mo | Rising → urgent urodynamics |
| **Urodynamics** | q1–2yr (or change) | DLPP >40 cmH₂O → intervene |
| **DMSA** | If VUTI / hydronephrosis | Scarring → aggressive management |

> **Goal**: Keep detrusor pressure <40 cmH₂O, compliance >20 mL/cmH₂O, PVR <100mL.

---

## Complications
| Complication | Prevention |
|--------------|------------|
| **UTI** | CIC technique, cranberry/methenamine, prophylactic abx (selected) |
| **Bladder Stones** | Hydration, CIC, augmentation surveillance |
| **Hydronephrosis / VUR** | Low detrusor pressure (anticholinergics, botox, augmentation) |
| **Renal Failure** | Early detection + aggressive pressure management |
| **Bladder Cancer** (↑ risk in augmentation) | Annual cystoscopy + cytology 10yr post-augmentation |

## Special Populations

### Spina Bifida / Myelomeningocele
- **LMN pattern** (sacral agenesis) — areflexic bladder + flaccid sphincter
- **Early CIC** (from infancy) + anticholinergics if high pressure
- **Lifelong surveillance** — renal US q6mo, urodynamics q1–2yr

### Spinal Cord Injury (SCI)
- **Acute phase** (spinal shock) — areflexic bladder → indwelling catheter
- **Recovery phase** (weeks-months) — UMN pattern emerges → transition to CIC
- **Autonomic dysreflexia** risk if lesion >T6 — bladder/bowel distension triggers

### Multiple Sclerosis
- Variable (UMN common) — fluctuating course
- Urodynamics at baseline + q1–2yr or symptom change

## High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **Neurogenic bladder** = bladder dysfunction from CNS/PNS lesion.
> - **UMN (Suprasacral)** = detrusor overactivity + **DSD** → high pressure → **upper tract risk**.
> - **LMN (Sacral)** = areflexic/underactive + flaccid sphincter → overflow incontinence.
> - **Urodynamics** = gold standard (compliance, DSD, detrusor pressure, DLPP).
> - **DLPP >40 cmH₂O** = danger threshold for upper tracts.
> - **CIC** = cornerstone of management (q4–6h, target PVR <100mL).
> - **UMN**: Anticholinergics + CIC ± Botox ± α-blocker for DSD.
> - **LMN**: CIC ± α-blocker; AUS for stress incontinence.
> - **Augmentation cystoplasty** = refractory low compliance; CIC lifelong; metabolic acidosis, stones, malignancy risk.
> - **Surveillance**: US q6–12mo, urodynamics q1–2yr, DMSA if VUTI.
> - **Autonomic dysreflexia** (SCI >T6): bladder distension → hypertensive crisis.

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **All neurogenic bladder = UMN** | **Sacral/cauda equina = LMN** (areflexic, overflow) |
| **DSD = sphincter relaxes during voiding** | **DSD = sphincter CONTRACTS during detrusor contraction** (dyssynergia) |
| **CIC not needed if continent** | **PVR >150mL or high detrusor pressure = CIC needed** even if continent |
| **Anticholinergics for all** | **LMN (areflexic) = anticholinergics WORSEN retention** |
| **Augmentation = no more CIC** | **CIC MANDATORY lifelong after augmentation** (mucus, incomplete emptying) |
| **β₃-agonist = same as anticholinergic** | **Mirabegron** = different mechanism (β₃), fewer cognitive/dry mouth SEs |
| **α-blocker = for DSD in UMN** | Yes — tamsulosin ↓ sphincter tone → helps emptying with CIC |
| **Autonomic dysreflexia = any SCI** | **Only lesions >T6** (T6 and above) |
| **Urodynamics = one-time test** | **Repeat q1–2yr or with symptom change** |

## Summary
**Neurogenic Bladder** is a **Must Know 🔴** urology/neurology topic.  
**Classification**: **UMN (Suprasacral)** = detrusor overactivity + **DSD** (sphincter contracts during voiding) → high pressure; **LMN (Sacral/Infrasacral)** = areflexic/underactive + flaccid sphincter → overflow.  
**Urodynamics**: Gold standard. **DLPP >40 cmH₂O** = upper tract danger.  
**Management**: **CIC q4–6h (PVR <100mL)** = cornerstone. **UMN**: CIC + anticholinergics/β₃-agonist ± Botox ± α-blocker (tamsulosin for DSD). **LMN**: CIC ± AUS for stress incontinence.  
**Refractory**: **Augmentation cystoplasty** (ileum/colon) → ↑ capacity, ↓ pressure; **lifelong CIC mandatory**.  
**Surveillance**: US q6–12mo, urodynamics q1–2yr, creatinine q6–12mo, DMSA if VUTI.  
**Autonomic dysreflexia**: Lesion >T6, triggered by bladder/bowel distension → hypertensive crisis; **immediate catheterisation** first step.  
**Exam focus**: UMN vs LMN, DSD, DLPP, CIC, anticholinergics UMN only, augmentation + CIC, autonomic dysreflexia >T6.
