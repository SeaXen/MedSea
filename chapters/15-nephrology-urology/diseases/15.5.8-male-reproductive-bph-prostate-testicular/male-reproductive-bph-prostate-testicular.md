# Male Reproductive Tract Disorders (BPH, Prostate Cancer, Testicular Cancer)

**Related:** [[Urinary Incontinence]], [[Urinary Tract Obstruction]], [[Urological Malignancies (RCC, Bladder Cancer, Urothelial Cancer)]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **BPH = commonest benign tumour in men; Prostate cancer = commonest male cancer; Testicular cancer = commonest solid cancer in young men (15–35). Key: BPH = LUTS, PSA screening controversial; Prostate Ca = Gleason grading, TNM staging; Testicular Ca = germ cell (seminoma vs non-seminoma), tumour markers (AFP, β-hCG, LDH).**

## Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)

### Epidemiology & Pathophysiology
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Prevalence** | Histological: 50% at 50yo, 80% at 80yo; Clinical: 25–50% symptomatic |
| **Pathophysiology** | Stromal + glandular proliferation in **transition zone** → urethral compression → BOO |
| **Risk Factors** | Age, family history, metabolic syndrome, obesity, sedentary lifestyle |

### Clinical Presentation (LUTS)
| Storage Symptoms | Voiding Symptoms | Post-Micturition |
|------------------|------------------|------------------|
| Frequency | Hesitancy | Post-void dribbling |
| Urgency | Weak stream | Sensation of incomplete emptying |
| Nocturia | Intermittency | |
| Urge incontinence | Straining | |
| | Terminal dribbling | |

> [!key]
> **IPSS (International Prostate Symptom Score): 0–7 mild, 8–19 moderate, 20–35 severe.** QoL question separate (0–6).

### Assessment
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **History** | IPSS, voiding diary, QoL, sexual function (IIEF), medical history, medications (α-blockers, anticholinergics, diuretics) |
| **Examination** | **DRE: enlarged, smooth, firm, non-tender** (median sulcus obliterated); abdominal (distended bladder) |
| **Investigations** | **PSA** (age-adjusted: <3.5 at 50–59, <4.5 at 60–69, <6.5 at 70+), urinalysis, creatinine, eGFR, glucose, **Uroflowmetry (Qmax <15 mL/s = obstruction)**, PVR US |
| **Urodynamics** | Pressure-flow study if diagnosis unclear, prior surgery, neurogenic |

### Management

#### Conservative (Mild IPSS <8, bother low)
| Measure | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Lifestyle** | Fluid modification (reduce evening), reduce caffeine/alcohol, treat constipation, avoid anticholinergics/decongestants, timed voiding, double voiding |
| **Watchful Waiting** | Annual IPSS, DRE, PSA, PVR |

#### Medical (Moderate-Severe IPSS ≥8, bother high)
| Drug Class | Agents | Mechanism | Notes |
|------------|--------|-----------|-------|
| **α-Blockers** | **Tamsulosin** 0.4mg OD, Alfuzosin 10mg OD, Silodosin 8mg OD, Doxazosin 4mg OD, Terazosin | **α1A antagonism → prostate/urethral relaxation** | **1st line**; rapid onset (days); side effects: dizziness, retrograde ejaculation (tamsulosin/silodosin), hypotension (doxazosin/terazosin) |
| **5-ARIs** | **Finasteride** 5mg OD, Dutasteride 0.5mg OD | **↓ DHT → prostate volume ↓ 20–30%** | **Prostate >30–40g or PSA >1.5**; slow onset (6–12mo); reduces AUR/surgery risk; side effects: sexual dysfunction, gynaecomastia |
| **Combination** | α-blocker + 5-ARI (e.g., tamsulosin + dutasteride) | **Synergistic** | **MTOPS/CombAT trials**: superior to monotherapy for progression; larger prostates |
| **Phosphodiesterase-5 Inhibitor** | Tadalafil 5mg OD | Smooth muscle relaxation | **LUTS + ED**; alternative if α-blocker contraindicated |
| **Anticholinergics/β3-Agonists** | Add-on for storage symptoms (OAB) | If persistent storage symptoms after α-blocker | Caution if high PVR |

#### Surgical (Failed Medical, Complications, Patient Choice)
| Indication | Procedure |
|------------|-----------|
| **AUR (acute urinary retention)** | Trial without catheter (TWOC) after α-blocker; if fails → surgery |
| **Recurrent UTI / Haematuria / Bladder stones / Renal impairment** | Surgery |
| **Patient choice** | After informed discussion |

| Procedure | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **TURP** (Transurethral Resection) | **Gold standard** <80–100g; monopolar/bipolar; 85–90% symptom improvement |
| **HoLEP** (Holmium Laser Enucleation) | **Any size**; less bleeding, shorter catheter/stay; equivalent to TURP; **>100g preferred** |
| **PVP** (Photoselective Vaporisation) | GreenLight laser; <80g; anticoagulation safe |
| **Rezum** (Water Vapor Thermal) | Minimally invasive; office-based; preserves ejaculation |
| **UroLift** (Prostatic Urethral Lift) | Mechanical; preserves ejaculation; <70g; less durable |
| **Simple Prostatectomy** (Open/Robotic) | **>100–150g**; high morbidity; rarely needed now |

> [!key]
> **TURP = gold standard <80–100g; HoLEP = any size, >100g preferred.** Bipolar TURP = saline, less TUR syndrome.

### Complications
| Complication | Management |
|--------------|------------|
| **TUR Syndrome** (hyponatraemia) | Glycine absorption → limit resection time, bipolar saline; treat hyponatraemia |
| **Retrograde Ejaculation** | ~70% TURP; α-blockers also; counsel pre-op |
| **Erectile Dysfunction** | 5–10% TURP; nerve-sparing techniques |
| **Stricture** | Urethral dilation, urethrotomy |
| **Incontinence** | Rare (<1%); usually transient; AUS if persistent |

## Prostate Cancer

### Epidemiology & Risk Factors
| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Incidence** | **Commonest male cancer** (UK/US); >50yo; Black > White > Asian |
| **Risk** | Age, family history (2–3x if 1st degree), Black ethnicity, BRCA2, Lynch syndrome, diet (high fat, low veg) |
| **Screening** | **PSA controversial** — ERSPC (↓ mortality 21%), PLCO (no benefit); **Shared decision-making** (age 50–69, life expectancy >10yr) |

### Diagnosis
| Step | Method |
|------|--------|
| **PSA** | >3–4 ng/mL (age-adjusted); **free:total PSA ratio <10–15% ↑ specificity** |
| **DRE** | Nodule, induration, asymmetry (25% Ca with normal PSA) |
| **MRI (mpMRI)** | **PI-RADS 1–5**; PI-RADS 3–5 → targeted biopsy |
| **Biopsy** | **Transperineal (preferred, lower sepsis) or TRUS-guided**; systematic + targeted |
| **Pathology** | **Gleason Grade Group 1–5** (see below) |

### Gleason Grading (2014 ISUP)
| Grade Group | Gleason Score | Pattern | Prognosis |
|-------------|---------------|---------|-----------|
| **1** | 3+3=6 | Only pattern 3 | **Very low risk** |
| **2** | 3+4=7 | Predominant 3, minor 4 | Low-intermediate |
| **3** | 4+3=7 | Predominant 4, minor 3 | Intermediate |
| **4** | 8 (4+4, 3+5, 5+3) | Pattern 4/5 | High |
| **5** | 9–10 (4+5, 5+4, 5+5) | Pattern 5 | Very high |

### Staging (TNM AJCC 8th)
| T Stage | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| **T1** | Clinically inapparent (T1a/b TURP incidental, T1c needle biopsy) |
| **T2** | Confined to prostate (T2a ≤half lobe, T2b >half lobe, T2c both lobes) |
| **T3** | Extraprostatic extension (T3a microscopic, T3b seminal vesicle invasion) |
| **T4** | Adjacent structures (bladder neck, rectum, levator, pelvic wall) |
| **N1** | Regional nodes |
| **M1** | Distant mets (M1a nodes, M1b bone, M1c other) |

### Risk Stratification (Localised)
| Risk | Criteria |
|------|----------|
| **Very Low** | T1c, GG1, PSA <10, <3 cores, ≤50% core involvement, PSA density <0.15 |
| **Low** | T1–T2a, GG1, PSA <10 |
| **Intermediate** | **Favourable**: T2b–T2c, GG2, or PSA 10–20; **Unfavourable**: GG3, or >1 intermediate factor |
| **High** | T3a, GG4–5, PSA >20 |
| **Very High** | T3b–T4, GG5, >1 high-risk feature |

### Management by Risk

#### Localised
| Risk | Options |
|------|---------|
| **Very Low / Low** | **Active Surveillance** (AS) preferred; Radical prostatectomy (RP), RT (EBRT/brachytherapy) |
| **Intermediate Favourable** | RP, **RT + ADT (4–6mo)**, AS (selected low-volume GG2) |
| **Intermediate Unfavourable** | RP, **RT + ADT (4–6mo)** |
| **High / Very High** | **RP + PLND**, **RT + ADT (18–36mo)** |

#### Advanced
| Stage | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| **Locally Advanced (T3–T4, N0)** | RT + long-term ADT (18–36mo) ± abiraterone/apalutamide/docetaxel (STAMPEDE) |
| **Node-Positive (N1)** | **ADT + abiraterone/apalutamide/docetaxel**; RT to prostate ± nodes |
| **Metastatic Hormone-Sensitive (mHSPC)** | **ADT + novel hormonal (abiraterone, apalutamide, enzalutamide, darolutamide) OR docetaxel** |
| **Castration-Resistant (mCRPC)** | Sequential: novel hormonal → other novel hormonal → docetaxel/cabazitaxel → Lu-177 PSMA → radium-223 (bone-only) |

> [!key]
> **ADT = GnRH agonist (leuprolide, goserelin) or antagonist (degarelix, relugolix).** **Anti-androgens** (bicalutamide) for flare prevention. **PSA nadir <0.1** post-RT/RP = good.

### Follow-Up (Post-Treatment)
| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **PSA** | 3mo × 2yr, 6mo × 5yr, then annually |
| **Imaging** | Restaging if biochemical recurrence (PSA rise ≥0.2 post-RP; nadir +2 post-RT) |
| **Bone Health** | Denosumab/zoledronic acid (on ADT); Ca/Vit D |

## Testicular Cancer

### Epidemiology & Risk Factors
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Incidence** | **Commonest solid cancer in men 15–35yo**; rare >50 |
| **Risk** | **Cryptorchidism** (3–5x, even if orchidopexy), family history, prior testis Ca, GCNIS, White ethnicity |
| **GCNIS** | Germ Cell Neoplasia In Situ (pre-invasive); contralateral biopsy if risk factors |

### Histology (WHO)
| Type | % | Tumour Markers | Radiosensitivity |
|------|---|----------------|------------------|
| **Seminoma** | **50–60%** | **β-hCG ↑ (10–20%), LDH ↑, AFP NEVER** | **Highly radiosensitive** |
| **Non-Seminoma (NSGCT)** | 40–50% | **AFP ↑, β-hCG ↑, LDH ↑** | Radioresistant |
| **Mixed** | — | Treat as **non-seminoma** | Treat as non-seminoma |

#### Non-Seminoma Subtypes
| Subtype | Features |
|---------|----------|
| **Embryonal Carcinoma** | Most aggressive; AFP/β-hCG variable |
| **Yolk Sac Tumour** | **AFP ↑↑**; children |
| **Choriocarcinoma** | **β-hCG ↑↑↑**; highly vascular, early mets |
| **Teratoma** | Mature/immature; markers normal; chemo-resistant |
| **Somatic Malignancy** | Sarcoma, carcinoma in teratoma |

### Tumour Markers (Essential)
| Marker | Half-Life | Seminoma | Non-Seminoma | Use |
|--------|-----------|----------|--------------|-----|
| **AFP** | 5–7 days | **Never elevated** | **Elevated (yolk sac, embryonal)** | Diagnosis, staging, monitoring |
| **β-hCG** | 24–36 hours | **Mildly elevated (10–20%)** | **Elevated (choriocarcinoma, embryonal)** | Diagnosis, staging, monitoring |
| **LDH** | — | Elevated (bulk) | Elevated (bulk) | Prognostic (IGCCCG) |

> [!key]
> **AFP NEVER elevated in pure seminoma.** If AFP ↑ = non-seminoma component (mixed) → treat as non-seminoma.

### Staging (TNM + S for Markers)
| Stage | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **pTis** | GCNIS |
| **pT1** | Tumour confined to testis/epididymis (no vascular/lymphatic invasion) |
| **pT2** | Vascular/lymphatic invasion or tunica vaginalis |
| **pT3** | Spermatic cord invasion |
| **pT4** | Scrotal invasion |
| **N** | Retroperitoneal nodes (pelvic nodes rare) |
| **M** | Distant (M1a non-regional nodes/lung; M1b other) |
| **S** | Marker level (S0 normal, S1 mild, S2 moderate, S3 high) |

### IGCCCG Prognostic Groups (Metastatic)
| Group | Seminoma | Non-Seminoma |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **Good** | Any primary, no non-pulmonary visceral mets, **normal AFP** | Testis/retroperitoneal primary, **no non-pulmonary visceral mets, AFP <1000, β-hCG <5000, LDH <1.5×ULN** |
| **Intermediate** | Any primary, non-pulmonary visceral mets, **normal AFP** | Testis/retro, no non-pulm visceral, **AFP 1000–10000, β-hCG 5000–50000, LDH 1.5–10×ULN** |
| **Poor** | **None** (seminoma never poor) | Mediastinal primary, or non-pulmonary visceral mets, or **AFP >10000, β-hCG >50000, LDH >10×ULN** |

### Management

#### Primary Treatment (All Stages)
| Step | Seminoma | Non-Seminoma |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **1. Radical Inguinal Orchiectomy** | **Standard** (high inguinal, cord ligation); **NO trans-scrotal** | **Standard**; testis-sparing if bilateral/solitary + small |

#### Stage I
| Seminoma | Non-Seminoma |
|----------|--------------|
| **Surveillance** (preferred; 80% no relapse) | **Surveillance** (preferred low-risk pT1; 70% no relapse) |
| **Adjuvant Carboplatin** (AUC 7 × 1–2 cycles) | **Adjuvant BEP × 1 cycle** (pT2–4, LVI+) |
| **Adjuvant RT** (para-aortic ± iliac; 20–30Gy) — declining use | **RPLND** (nerve-sparing; staging/therapeutic) |

#### Stage II (Nodal)
| Seminoma | Non-Seminoma |
|----------|--------------|
| **IIA/IIB (<3cm): RT** (para-aortic ± pelvic; 30–36Gy) | **IIA/IIB: RPLND** (nerve-sparing) or **BEP × 3 cycles** |
| **IIC/III (>3cm): Chemo** (BEP × 3–4, or EP × 4) | **IIC/III: BEP × 3–4** (or VIP) |

#### Stage III (Metastatic)
| Seminoma | Non-Seminoma |
|----------|--------------|
| **Good/Intermediate: BEP × 3–4** (or EP × 4) | **Good: BEP × 3**; **Intermediate/Poor: BEP × 4** (or VIP/HDCT) |
| **Residual mass:** PET-CT (if >3cm) → observe if negative; resect if positive | **Residual mass:** **RPLND** (residual >1cm or markers normalised + mass) |

#### Chemotherapy Regimens
| Regimen | Composition |
|---------|-------------|
| **BEP** | Bleomycin 30U D1,8,15 + Etoposide 100mg/m² D1–5 + Cisplatin 20mg/m² D1–5 q3wk |
| **EP** | Etoposide + Cisplatin (if bleomycin contraindicated) |
| **VIP** | Etoposide + Ifosfamide + Cisplatin (salvage/poor risk) |
| **TIP** | Paclitaxel + Ifosfamide + Cisplatin (salvage) |
| **HDCT + ASCT** | High-dose carboplatin/etoposide + auto-SCT (relapsed/refractory) |

#### Post-Chemo Residual Mass
| Seminoma | Non-Seminoma |
|----------|--------------|
| **Residual >3cm: PET-CT** (6–8wk post-chemo) | **Residual >1cm: RPLND** (mandatory if markers normalised) |
| PET+ve → salvage chemo/resection | RPLND → pathology: necrosis (70%), teratoma (20%), viable (10%) |
| PET-ve → observe | Teratoma = chemotherapy-resistant; viable = needs further chemo |

## High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **BPH: transition zone enlargement → LUTS; IPSS scoring; DRE = smooth, enlarged**
> - **BPH Medical: α-blockers (tamsulosin) 1st line; 5-ARI (finasteride) if prostate >30–40g/PSA >1.5**
> - **BPH Surgical: TURP gold standard <80–100g; HoLEP any size/>100g**
> - **Prostate Ca: commonest male cancer; Gleason Grade Groups 1–5; PI-RADS MRI → targeted biopsy**
> - **Prostate Ca Risk: Very Low/Low → Active Surveillance; Intermediate → RP/RT+ADT; High → RP+PLND/RT+ADT**
> - **Metastatic HSPC: ADT + novel hormonal (abi/apa/enz/daro) OR docetaxel**
> - **Testicular Ca: 15–35yo commonest solid cancer; Seminoma 50–60% (radiosensitive, β-hCG↑, AFP never); NSGCT 40–50% (AFP↑, β-hCG↑)**
> - **Orchiectomy: high inguinal, NO trans-scrotal; sentinel node not routine**
> - **Stage I Seminoma: Surveillance > Carboplatin > RT; NSGCT: Surveillance > BEP×1 > RPLND**
> - **Metastatic: Good/Int seminoma = BEP×3–4; NSGCT Good = BEP×3, Int/Poor = BEP×4**
> - **Residual mass: Seminoma >3cm PET-CT; NSGCT >1cm RPLND**
> - **Tumour markers: AFP never in pure seminoma; β-hCG in both; LDH prognostic**

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **PSA = prostate cancer** | PSA ↑ in BPH, prostatitis, retention, instrumentation; **free:total ratio <10% ↑ specificity** |
| **All prostate Ca = surgery** | **Low/very low risk = Active Surveillance** (preferred) |
| **Gleason 3+4 = 4+3** | **Grade Group 2 (3+4) vs 3 (4+3)** — different prognosis |
| **Testicular Ca = trans-scrotal biopsy** | **NO trans-scrotal** (scrotal violation → upstage); **High inguinal orchiectomy** |
| **Seminoma = AFP elevated** | **AFP NEVER in pure seminoma**; if AFP ↑ = non-seminoma component |
| **All Stage I = chemo** | **Surveillance preferred** (seminoma 80% cure, NSGCT 70% cure pT1) |
| **Seminoma poor risk** | **Seminoma NEVER poor risk** (IGCCCG) |
| **Residual mass seminoma = RPLND** | **Seminoma residual >3cm = PET-CT**; NSGCT >1cm = RPLND |
| **BEP = 3 cycles always** | **Good risk NSGCT = BEP×3; Int/Poor = BEP×4** |
| **Teratoma = chemo-sensitive** | **Teratoma = CHEMO-RESISTANT** → surgery (RPLND) |
| **GCNIS = biopsy routine** | **Contralateral biopsy only if risk factors** (cryptorchidism, atrophy, infertility) |

## Mnemonics

- **BPH Zone**: **T**ransition **Z**one = **TZ**
- **LUTS**: **S**torage (freq, urg, noct), **V**oiding (hesit, weak, intermit), **P**ost-mict (dribble, incomplete) = **SVP**
- **IPSS**: **0–7 Mild**, **8–19 Mod**, **20–35 Sev** = **0-7-19-35**
- **BPH Meds**: **α**lpha-blockers (fast), **5-ARI** (slow, big prostate) = **α5**
- **TURP**: **T**UR **S**yndrome = hyponatraemia (glycine); **B**ipolar = saline
- **Prostate Ca Grade**: **GG1=6**, **GG2=3+4**, **GG3=4+3**, **GG4=8**, **GG5=9-10** = **1-2-3-4-5**
- **Prostate Ca Risk**: **V**ery **L**ow, **L**ow, **I**ntermed, **H**igh, **V**ery **H**igh = **VLIHVH**
- **Testicular Ca Age**: **1**5–35 = **135**
- **Seminoma Markers**: **A**FP **N**ever, **β**-**hCG** sometimes, **LDH** bulk = **ANβL**
- **Orchiectomy**: **H**igh **I**nguinal, **N**O **T**rans-scrotal = **HINT**
- **IGCCCG Good NSGCT**: **A**FP <1000, **β**-**hCG** <5000, **L**DH <1.5×ULN = **AβL**
- **Residual Mass**: **S**eminoma >3cm **P**ET; **N**SGCT >1cm **R**PLND = **SP-NR**

## Summary

**Male Reproductive Tract Disorders** is a **Must Know 🔴** topic.
**Key takeaway:** **BPH**: transition zone → LUTS; IPSS; **tamsulosin 1st line**; finasteride if >30–40g/PSA>1.5; **TURP <80–100g gold, HoLEP >100g**. **Prostate Ca**: Gleason Grade Groups 1–5; **Risk: VLow/Low → AS; Intermediate → RP/RT+ADT; High → RP+PLND/RT+ADT(18–36mo)**; **mHSPC: ADT + novel hormonal (abiraterone/apalutamide/enzalutamide/darolutamide) OR docetaxel**. **Testicular Ca**: 15–35yo; **Seminoma 50–60% (AFP NEVER, radiosensitive); NSGCT 40–50% (AFP↑)**; **High inguinal orchiectomy (NO trans-scrotal)**; **Stage I: Surveillance > Carboplatin (sem) / BEP×1/RPLND (NSGCT)**; **Metastatic: IGCCCG Good/Int seminoma BEP×3–4; NSGCT Good BEP×3, Int/Poor BEP×4**; **Residual: Seminoma >3cm PET-CT; NSGCT >1cm RPLND**; **Teratoma = chemo-resistant**.
**Exam focus:** BPH management algorithm, prostate cancer Gleason/Grade Groups/risk stratification/AS, mHSPC treatment, testicular cancer age/histology/markers, orchiectomy approach, stage I management, IGCCCG groups, BEP cycles, residual mass protocols.
**Clinical relevance:** Active surveillance avoids overtreatment in prostate cancer; testicular cancer highly curable with multidisciplinary approach; BPH very common, stepwise management improves QoL.
