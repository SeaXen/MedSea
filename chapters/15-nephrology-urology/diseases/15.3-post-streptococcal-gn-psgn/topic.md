# Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis (PSGN)

<callout icon="🩺" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis (PSGN) — Nephrology & Urology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

**Related:** [[Glomerular Diseases — Overview and Classification]], [[Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)]], [[MPGN and C3 Glomerulopathy (DDD, C3GN)]], [[Anti-GBM Disease (Goodpasture Syndrome)]], [[Infective Endocarditis]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **PSGN = immune-complex GN following group A β-haemolytic Streptococcus (GAS) infection (pharyngitis/impetigo). Peak 1–3 weeks post-infection. Children 5–15yr. Nephritic syndrome: haematuria (cola-coloured urine), RBC casts, hypertension, oliguria, periorbital oedema. Low C3, normal C4. ASO titre ↑. Subepithelial "humps" on EM. Self-limited (95% childhood recovery). Adults: worse prognosis, ↑ CKD risk.**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Recognise classic post-strep presentation (nephritic syndrome 1–3 wks post-GAS)
- Interpret serology (ASO, anti-DNase B, C3, C4)
- Differentiate from other nephritic causes (IgA, anti-GBM, ANCA, lupus, C3G)
- Manage supportively (BP control, volume, antibiotics for residual infection)
- Identify indications for biopsy (atypical features, adult, persistent)
- Counsel on prognosis (childhood vs adult)

---

## 2. Epidemiology & Aetiology
| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Organism** | **Group A β-haemolytic Streptococcus** (GAS) — M-types 1, 2, 4, 12, 25, 49 (nephritogenic) |
| **Infection site** | Pharyngitis (most common) or **Impetigo** (skin) — M-types 49, 55, 57, 59, 60 |
| **Latency** | **1–3 weeks** post-pharyngitis; **3–6 weeks** post-impetigo |
| **Age** | **Children 5–15 years** (peak); rare <3yr, >40yr |
| **Sex** | Male = Female |
| **Geography** | Worldwide; ↓ in developed (antibiotics), still common in developing |

---

## 3. Pathogenesis
- **Immune complexes** (streptococcal antigen + antibody) deposit in **subepithelial space** of GBM
- **Antigens**: Streptococcal pyrogenic exotoxin B (SpeB), nephritis-associated plasmin receptor (NAPlr), streptococcal DNAase B
- **Complement**: **Alternative pathway** activation → **Low C3, Normal C4**
- **EM**: **Subepithelial "humps"** (large, dome-shaped deposits on epithelial side of GBM)

---

## 4. Clinical Presentation
| Feature | Typical (Child) | Atypical / Adult |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Gross haematuria** | **Coca-cola / tea-coloured urine** (90%) | Microscopic may predominate |
| **Periorbital oedema** | Morning, improves by evening | May be anasarca |
| **Hypertension** | 60–80% (volume + RAAS) | Often severe |
| **Oliguria/AKI** | Mild-moderate (20–30%) | More severe, dialysis may be needed |
| **Flank pain** | Rare | Possible |
| **Fever** | Low-grade (if recent infection) | Uncommon |
| **ASO titre** | **↑ (peaks 3–5 wks)** | May be negative (impetigo) |

---

## 5. Laboratory Findings
| Test | PSGN Typical | Differential Clues |
|------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Urine** | Haematuria (RBC >50/HPF), **RBC casts**, proteinuria (subnephrotic 0.5–3g/day) | RBC casts = glomerular |
| **Serum C3** | **Low (85–90%)** — normalises 6–8 wks | **Persistently low → C3G, MPGN, SLE, endocarditis** |
| **Serum C4** | **Normal** | Low C4 = classical pathway (SLE, cryo, MPGN) |
| **ASO titre** | **↑ >200 IU/mL (or rising)** — peaks 3–5 wks | Impetigo: ASO may be low → **Anti-DNase B** better |
| **Anti-DNase B** | ↑ (more sensitive for skin infection) | Use if ASO negative, impetigo history |
| **Anti-streptolysin O (ASO)** | Not streptolysin O specific | — |
| **Throat/skin culture** | May be negative (latent phase) | Confirms recent GAS if positive |
| **CBC** | Normal or leukocytosis | — |

> **C3 normalisation** by 8 weeks = typical PSGN. **Persistent low C3 >12 weeks** → consider C3G, MPGN, SLE, infective endocarditis.

---

## 6. Differential Diagnosis (Nephritic Syndrome)
| Condition | C3 | C4 | Key Differentiator |
|-----------|----|----|-------------------|
| **PSGN** | **Low** | **Normal** | Recent GAS, ASO↑, humps EM, self-limited |
| **IgA Nephropathy** | Normal | Normal | **Coincident with URTI** (not 1–3 wks after), no ASO rise |
| **Anti-GBM** | Normal/Low | Normal | **Linear IgG**, anti-GBM+, pulmonary bleed |
| **ANCA Vasculitis** | Normal | Normal | **p-ANCA/c-ANCA**, pauci-immune, no ASO |
| **Lupus Nephritis** | Low | **Low** | **Full-house IF**, ANA/dsDNA+, systemic features |
| **C3G (DDD/C3GN)** | Low | Normal | **C3 dominant IF**, C3NeF+, **persistent low C3 >12 wks** |
| **Infective Endocarditis** | Low | Low/Normal | **Blood cultures +**, murmur, embolic phenomena |
| **Cryoglobulinaemia** | Low | **Low** | Cryoglobulins+, HCV+, low C4 |

---

## 7. Indications for Renal Biopsy
| Indication | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| **Adult >40 years** | Higher malignancy/vasculitis risk; PSGN rare |
| **Atypical features** | Nephrotic-range proteinuria, severe AKI, persistent low C3 >12 wks |
| **Systemic symptoms** | Fever, weight loss, rash, arthralgia (suggest SLE, vasculitis, IE) |
| **Persistent proteinuria/haematuria** | >6 months (risk of chronic GN) |
| **Uncertain diagnosis** | Differentiate from IgA, C3G, lupus, anti-GBM |

> **Children with classic presentation**: **Biopsy NOT routinely needed**.

---

## 8. Management

### Supportive (Mainstay)
| Issue | Management |
|-------|------------|
| **Hypertension** | **ACEi/ARB** (if no hyperkalaemia/AKI contraindication), CCB, diuretics; target <130/80 |
| **Volume overload** | **Loop diuretic** (furosemide) ± fluid restriction |
| **AKI** | Conservative — avoid nephrotoxins, monitor electrolytes, dialysis if severe (rare in kids) |
| **Residual GAS infection** | **Penicillin V 500mg QID × 10 days** (or Amoxicillin) — eradicates carriage, prevents spread |
| **Contact prophylaxis** | Household contacts: throat culture → treat if GAS+ |

### Immunosuppression
| Situation | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| **Typical childhood PSGN** | **NOT INDICATED** — self-limited |
| **Adult with severe disease** | Consider **steroids ± cyclophosphamide** if crescentic >50% / rapidly progressive |
| **Persistent low C3 + proteinuria >3.5g** | Evaluate for C3G/MPGN — treat accordingly |

---

## 9. Prognosis

### Children (95% complete recovery)
| Outcome | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|
| **Complete resolution** | **95%** (proteinuria/haematuria resolve by 1–2 yrs) |
| **Persistent microscopic haematuria** | 5–10% (benign) |
| **Proteinuria >1yr** | 2–5% |
| **CKD/ESRD** | **<1%** |

### Adults (Worse)
| Outcome | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|
| **Complete resolution** | 50–60% |
| **Persistent proteinuria** | 30–40% |
| **CKD/ESRD** | **10–20%** |
| **Recurrence** | Rare (but may indicate C3G/mutation) |

> **Risk factors for poor outcome**: Age >40, nephrotic syndrome, severe AKI (dialysis), crescents >50%, persistent low C3 >12 wks.

---

## 10. Complications
| Complication | Management |
|--------------|------------|
| **Hypertensive encephalopathy** | IV labetalol/nicardipine, ICU |
| **Acute pulmonary oedema** | IV furosemide, nitrates, NIPPV |
| **AKI requiring dialysis** | Temporary (usually) — supportive |
| **Post-strep reactive arthritis** | NSAIDs, self-limited |
| **Rheumatic fever** | Separate entity (Jones criteria) — not caused by PSGN |

---

## 11. High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **PSGN**: Nephritic syndrome 1–3 wks post-GAS pharyngitis/impetigo. Children 5–15yr.
> - **Classic**: Cola-coloured urine, periorbital oedema, hypertension, RBC casts.
> - **Labs**: **Low C3, Normal C4**, **ASO titre ↑** (peaks 3–5 wks), Anti-DNase B for impetigo.
> - **EM**: **Subepithelial "humps"** (pathognomonic).
> - **C3 normalises by 8 wks** — persistent low C3 >12 wks = NOT PSGN (think C3G, MPGN, SLE, IE).
> - **Management**: Supportive (BP, volume, antibiotics for carriage). **No steroids in typical childhood PSGN**.
> - **Prognosis**: Children 95% recovery; Adults 10–20% ESRD.
> - **Biopsy NOT needed** in classic childhood presentation.
> - **Differential**: IgA (synpharyngitic), anti-GBM, ANCA, lupus, C3G, IE.

## 12. Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **PSGN = immediate post-infection** | **Latency 1–3 wks** (pharyngitis), 3–6 wks (impetigo) |
| **ASO always positive** | **Impetigo: ASO often negative** → use **Anti-DNase B** |
| **Low C3 + Low C4 = PSGN** | **PSGN = Low C3 + Normal C4**. Low C4 = SLE, cryo, IE |
| **Steroids help childhood PSGN** | **No steroids** — self-limited, supportive only |
| **C3 stays low forever in PSGN** | **C3 normalises by 6–8 wks** — persistent low C3 = C3G/MPGN/SLE/IE |
| **Adults recover like children** | Adults: **10–20% ESRD**, higher persistent proteinuria |
| **Biopsy all nephritic syndrome** | **Childhood classic PSGN: NO biopsy needed** |
| **PSGN causes rheumatic fever** | Separate entity — PSGN ≠ rheumatic fever (different M-types, different immune response) |
| **Impetigo PSGN = throat PSGN** | Different M-types (49, 55–60), longer latency, ASO may be negative |
| **ASO titre = single value diagnostic** | **Rising titre** (convalescent > acute) more diagnostic than single value |

- **PSGN complement**: **C3 Low, C4 Normal** = **C3L C4N**

- **ASO alternative**: **Anti-DNase B** for **B**ody (skin/impetigo)

## 22. Summary
**Post-Streptococcal Glomerulonephritis** is a **Must Know 🔴** topic.  
**Aetiology**: GAS infection (pharyngitis/impetigo) → immune complexes (subepithelial) → nephritic syndrome after 1–3 wks (pharyngitis) or 3–6 wks (impetigo).  
**Clinical**: Cola-coloured urine, periorbital oedema, hypertension, RBC casts. Children 5–15yr.  
**Labs**: **Low C3, Normal C4** (alternative pathway), **ASO ↑** (peaks 3–5 wks), **Anti-DNase B for impetigo**. **EM: Subepithelial humps**.  
**C3 normalises by 8 weeks** — persistent low C3 >12 wks = C3G/MPGN/SLE/IE.  
**Management**: Supportive (BP control, diuretics, Penicillin V × 10 days for carriage). **NO steroids in typical childhood PSGN**.  
**Prognosis**: Children 95% recovery; Adults 10–20% ESRD (worse if crescents >50%, nephrotic, severe AKI).  
**Biopsy**: Not routine in classic childhood; indicated if adult >40, atypical, persistent low C3, nephrotic.  
**Exam focus**: Latency, complement profile, ASO/anti-DNase B, humps EM, supportive Rx, C3 timeline, child vs adult prognosis, biopsy indications.
