# Diabetic Nephropathy (Diabetic Kidney Disease)

**Related:** [[Glomerular Diseases — Overview and Classification]], [[Secondary Glomerulonephritides — Lupus Nephritis]], [[Secondary Glomerulonephritides — ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (GPA, MPA, EGPA)]], [[Secondary Glomerulonephritides — Amyloidosis]], [[Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **Diabetic Nephropathy = commonest cause of ESRD worldwide (~40–50%). Type 1: microalbuminuria → macroalbuminuria → declining GFR. Type 2: more variable, often CKD with albuminuria. Key: early detection (UACR), glycaemic control, BP control (ACEi/ARB), SGLT2i (game-changer), GLP-1 RA. Staging: KDIGO GFR + albuminuria categories.**

---

## Learning Objectives
- Define diabetic kidney disease (DKD) and staging (KDIGO)
- Recognise natural history (hyperfiltration → microalbuminuria → macroalbuminuria → ESRD)
- Apply comprehensive management (glycaemia, BP, RAAS blockade, SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA)
- Differentiate DKD from non-diabetic kidney disease in diabetes

---

## Epidemiology & Significance

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Prevalence** | ~40% of T1DM, ~20–30% of T2DM develop DKD |
| **ESRD Cause** | **Commonest cause of ESRD worldwide (~40–50%)** |
| **Type 1 vs Type 2** | T1DM: more predictable progression; T2DM: heterogeneous, older, more comorbidities |
| **Risk Factors** | Poor glycaemic control, hypertension, smoking, dyslipidaemia, family history, ethnicity (South Asian, African, Hispanic higher risk) |

---

## Pathogenesis

| Mechanism | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Haemodynamic** | Hyperfiltration → glomerular hypertension → sclerosis (early) |
| **Metabolic** | Advanced glycation end-products (AGEs), PKC activation, polyol pathway, oxidative stress |
| **Inflammatory** | Cytokines (TGF-β, VEGF), macrophage infiltration |
| **Structural** | GBM thickening, mesangial expansion (Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules), arteriolar hyalinosis (afferent + efferent), tubulointerstitial fibrosis |

---

## Natural History (Mogensen Stages — Type 1)

| Stage | GFR | Albuminuria | Pathology |
|-------|-----|-------------|-----------|
| **1: Hyperfiltration** | ↑↑ (130–150) | Normal | Reversible with glycaemic control |
| **2: Silent** | Normal/High | Normal | GBM thickening, mesangial expansion |
| **3: Incipient (Microalbuminuria)** | Normal | **30–300 mg/day (UACR 3–30 mg/mmol)** | Reversible with ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i |
| **4: Overt (Macroalbuminuria)** | Declining | **>300 mg/day (UACR >30 mg/mmol)** | Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules, progressive |
| **5: ESRD** | <15 | Variable | Global glomerulosclerosis, fibrosis |

> [!key]
> **Type 2 DM: no clear stages; often CKD with albuminuria at diagnosis; screen annually from diagnosis.**

---

## Screening & Diagnosis

| Test | Frequency | Threshold |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **UACR (Urine Albumin:Creatinine Ratio)** | **Annually from diagnosis (T2DM); 5yr post-diagnosis (T1DM)** | **Microalbuminuria: 3–30 mg/mmol (30–300 mg/day)**; **Macroalbuminuria: >30 mg/mmol (>300 mg/day)** |
| **eGFR** | Annually (more often if CKD) | KDIGO categories G1–G5 |
| **Confirm** | **2 of 3 specimens in 3–6 months** (exclude transient causes: UTI, fever, exercise, HF, menstruation) | |

### KDIGO Staging (GFR + Albuminuria)

| GFR Category | G1 (≥90) | G2 (60–89) | G3a (45–59) | G3b (30–44) | G4 (15–29) | G5 (<15) |
|--------------|----------|------------|-------------|-------------|------------|----------|
| **A1 (<3 mg/mmol)** | Low | Low | Moderate | High | Very High | Very High |
| **A2 (3–30 mg/mmol)** | Low | Moderate | **High** | **Very High** | Very High | Very High |
| **A3 (>30 mg/mmol)** | Moderate | **High** | **Very High** | **Very High** | Very High | Very High |

## Differential: DKD vs Non-Diabetic Kidney Disease in Diabetes

| Feature | **Typical DKD** | **Suggests Non-Diabetic Kidney Disease** |
|---------|-----------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Retinopathy** | **Present** (especially T1DM) | Absent (esp. T1DM) |
| **Albuminuria Onset** | Gradual, progressive | Sudden, nephrotic range |
| **Haematuria** | Absent/minimal | **Active sediment (RBC casts)** |
| **Renal Size** | Normal/large early; small late | Small (chronic) or large (infiltrative) |
| **Progression** | Gradual GFR decline | Rapid decline |
| **Serology** | Negative | ANA, ANCA, complement, paraprotein +ve |
| **Biopsy Indication** | Atypical features | Unexplained AKI, nephrotic syndrome, haematuria, no retinopathy (T1DM) |

> [!key]
> **No retinopathy + T1DM = biopsy strongly considered** (DKD unlikely without retinopathy).

## Management — Multifactorial Intervention

### 1. Glycaemic Control
| Target | Agent Preference |
|--------|------------------|
| **HbA1c ~7% (53 mmol/mol)** | **SGLT2i (1st line if eGFR ≥20)** → **GLP-1 RA** → Metformin (if eGFR ≥30) → DPP-4i → Insulin |
| **Individualise** | Avoid hypoglycaemia (esp. elderly, CKD G4–5) |

| Agent | Renal Benefit | eGFR Cutoff |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| **SGLT2i** (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin, canagliflozin) | **↓ ESRD, ↓ CV death, ↓ HF** (CREDENCE, DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY) | **≥20** (initiate); continue to dialysis |
| **GLP-1 RA** (semaglutide, liraglutide, dulaglutide) | ↓ Albuminuria, ↓ CV events | No lower limit (dose adjust) |
| **Metformin** | Neutral | ≥30 (caution 30–45); contraindicated <30 |
| **Insulin** | Neutral | All stages (dose reduce in CKD) |
| **DPP-4i** (linagliptin safe) | Neutral | All stages |

### 2. Blood Pressure & RAAS Blockade
| Target | Agent |
|--------|-------|
| **<130/80** (KDIGO 2024) | **ACEi/ARB (1st line)** — max tolerated dose |
| **Add-on** | Thiazide-like (G1–G3), loop diuretic (G4–5), CCB, β-blocker |
| **Dual RAAS Blockade** | **Contraindicated** (ACEi + ARB = ↑ hyperkalaemia, AKI) |

> [!key]
> **ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i = cornerstone of DKD management.** Dual RAAS blockade prohibited.

### 3. Lipid Management
| Agent | Indication |
|-------|------------|
| **Statin** | All DKD ≥50yo or CVD risk >10% (or all CKD per some guidelines) |
| **Ezetimibe** | If statin intolerant or LDL not at target |

### 4. Lifestyle
| Intervention | Target |
|--------------|--------|
| **Salt** | <2g Na/day (5g salt) |
| **Protein** | 0.8 g/kg/day (avoid >1.3) |
| **Weight** | BMI <25 |
| **Smoking** | Cessation |
| **Exercise** | 150 min/week moderate |

## SGLT2 Inhibitors — Landmark Trials

| Trial | Population | Key Result |
|-------|------------|------------|
| **CREDENCE** | T2DM + DKD (UACR 300–5000, eGFR 30–90) | Canagliflozin: **↓ 30% ESRD/CV death/renal death** |
| **DAPA-CKD** | CKD (incl. non-diabetic) (UACR 200–5000, eGFR 25–75) | Dapagliflozin: **↓ 39% ESRD/death** |
| **EMPA-KIDNEY** | CKD (incl. non-diabetic, low albuminuria) (eGFR 20–45 or 45–90 + UACR ≥200) | Empagliflozin: **↓ 28% ESRD/CV death** |

> [!key]
> **SGLT2i benefit extends to non-diabetic CKD** (DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY). Initiate if eGFR ≥20.

---

## Finerenone (Non-Steroidal MRA)

| Trial | Population | Result |
|-------|------------|--------|
| **FIDELIO-DKD** | T2DM + DKD (UACR 30–5000, eGFR 25–60) + ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i | **↓ 18% ESRD/CV death** |
| **FIGARO-DKD** | Higher eGFR (≥60) | **↓ 13% CV events** |

| Use | Add to ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i if eGFR ≥25, K+ ≤4.8, albuminuria persists |
|-----|------------------------------------------------------------------------|

---

## Advanced DKD — Renal Replacement

| Stage | Preparation |
|-------|-------------|
| **G4 (eGFR 15–29)** | **Refer nephrology**; AV fistula planning; transplant assessment; conservative care discussion |
| **G5 (eGFR <15)** | **Dialysis** (HD/PD) or **Transplant** (pre-emptive ideal); SGLT2i continue if tolerated |

## High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **DKD = commonest cause ESRD worldwide (~40–50%)**
> - **Screen**: UACR annually (T2DM from diagnosis; T1DM 5yr post-diagnosis)
> - **Microalbuminuria**: UACR 3–30 mg/mmol (30–300 mg/day)
> - **Macroalbuminuria**: UACR >30 mg/mmol (>300 mg/day)
> - **No retinopathy + T1DM = suspect non-diabetic kidney disease**
> - **BP target <130/80**; **ACEi/ARB 1st line**; **dual RAAS blockade contraindicated**
> - **SGLT2i = game-changer** (CREDENCE, DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY) — **initiate eGFR ≥20**
> - **GLP-1 RA** add-on for CV/renal benefit
> - **Finerenone** add-on if albuminuria persists on ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i
> - **G4: refer nephrology** (AV fistula, transplant); **G5: RRT**
> - **Metformin**: safe ≥30; caution 30–45; stop <30

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **Microalbuminuria = 30–300 mg/day** | **Also UACR 3–30 mg/mmol** (both used) |
| **ACEi + ARB = better** | **Contraindicated** (ONTARGET, VA NEPHRON: ↑ hyperkalaemia, AKI) |
| **SGLT2i only for diabetes** | **Benefit in non-diabetic CKD** (DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY) |
| **SGLT2i stop at eGFR <30** | **Initiate ≥20; continue to dialysis** |
| **All diabetes + CKD = DKD** | **Rule out non-diabetic**: no retinopathy (T1DM), haematuria, rapid decline, nephrotic syndrome |
| **Finerenone = spironolactone** | **Non-steroidal MRA**; less hyperkalaemia, no anti-androgen effects |
| **GLP-1 RA only for diabetes** | **CV/renal benefit in DKD**; use regardless of HbA1c if indicated |
| **Kimmelstiel-Wilson = only DKD** | Also in other diseases (rare), but pathognomonic for DKD |

## Mnemonics

- **DKD Screening**: **U**ACR **A**nnually from **D**iagnosis (T2DM) = **UAD**
- **Albuminuria**: **M**icro = **3–30**; **M**acro = **>30** = **M3/M30**
- **Retinopathy**: **T1DM no retinopathy = biopsy** = **T1-NR-BX**
- **BP**: **<130/80** = **130/80**
- **ACEi/ARB**: **D**ual **B**lockade **C**ontraindicated = **DBC**
- **SGLT2i**: **C**REDENCE, **D**APA-CKD, **E**MPA-KIDNEY = **CDE**
- **SGLT2i eGFR**: **Initiate ≥20, continue to dialysis** = **20-dialysis**
- **KDIGO**: **G**FR + **A**lbuminuria = **GA** matrix

## Summary

**Diabetic Nephropathy** is a **Must Know 🔴** topic.
**Key takeaway:** Commonest ESRD cause. Screen: UACR annually (T2DM from diagnosis, T1DM 5yr post). Micro: 3–30 mg/mmol; Macro: >30 mg/mmol. KDIGO G+A matrix for risk. **No retinopathy + T1DM = biopsy**. BP <130/80; **ACEi/ARB 1st line; NO dual blockade**. **SGLT2i game-changer** (CREDENCE/DAPA-CKD/EMPA-KIDNEY) — **initiate eGFR ≥20, continue to dialysis**. GLP-1 RA add-on. **Finerenone** add-on if albuminuria persists. Metformin safe ≥30. G4: refer nephrology (AV fistula, transplant); G5: RRT.
**Exam focus:** Screening, albuminuria thresholds, KDIGO staging, DKD vs non-diabetic, ACEi/ARB, SGLT2i trials/cutoffs, dual RAAS contraindicated, finerenone/GLP-1 RA, referral criteria.
**Clinical relevance:** Early SGLT2i + ACEi/ARB dramatically slows progression; comprehensive multifactorial management reduces CV/renal events.
