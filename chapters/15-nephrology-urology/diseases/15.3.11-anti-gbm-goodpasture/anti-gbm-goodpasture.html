NEPHROLOGY & UROLOGY
Anti-GBM Disease (Goodpasture Syndrome)
1 Learning Objectives
  • Recognise clinical presentation (RPGN ± pulmonary haemorrhage)
  • Interpret anti-GBM antibody testing and renal biopsy (linear IgG)
  • Apply emergency management (plasma exchange + cyclophosphamide + steroids)
  • Understand prognosis factors (dialysis dependence, pulmonary haemorrhage)
  • Differentiate from ANCA vasculitis (double-positive overlap)
  • Apply to FCPS/MRCP emergency vignettes
2 Pathogenesis
ComponentDetail
-------------------
**Target antigen****α3(IV)NC1 collagen** (non-collagenous domain of α3 chain of type IV collagen)
**Antibody**IgG (mostly IgG1, IgG3) — anti-GBM
**Location****GBM** (renal) + **Alveolar basement membrane** (pulmonary)
**HLA association****HLA-DR15 (DRB1*1501)** — strong (88% vs 25% controls)
**Triggers**Hydrocarbon exposure, smoking, viral infections (influenza), cocaine, HLA-DR15
Mechanism: Anti-GBM IgG binds GBM → complement activation (C5b-9) + neutrophil recruitment → crescent formation. Same antigen in alveoli → pulmonary capillaritis → haemorrhage.
3 Clinical Presentation
Renal (100%)
FeatureFrequency
--------------------
**RPGN** (AKI, haematuria, RBC casts, proteinuria)100%
**Hypertension**60–80%
**Oliguria/anuria**If advanced
**Subnephrotic proteinuria**<3.5g/day typical
Pulmonary (40–60% = Goodpasture's Syndrome)
FeatureFrequency
--------------------
**Haemoptysis**70–90% of pulmonary involvement
**Dyspnoea**Common
**Iron deficiency anaemia**Chronic occult blood loss
**CXR/CT**Bilateral diffuse alveolar infiltrates (flitting)
Smoking/hydrocarbon exposure ↑ pulmonary involvement risk.
4 Diagnosis
Serology
TestSensitivitySpecificityRole
--------------------------------------

Anti-GBM titre correlates with disease activity. Falling titre = response to treatment.

FindingDescription

Differential on IF: Linear IgG also in diabetic nephropathy (non-specific, weak), fibrillary GN (smudgy), amyloid (kappa/lambda).

6 Management (EMERGENCY)
Indications for Urgent Treatment
  • Anti-GBM positive + RPGN (creatinine rising, crescents on biopsy)
  • Pulmonary haemorrhage (life-threatening)
Standard Regimen (KDIGO/ERA-EDTA)
ComponentDose/DurationRole
--------------------------------
**Plasma Exchange (PLEX)****1–1.5 plasma vol daily × 14 days** (or until anti-GBM negative)Removes circulating anti-GBM antibody
**Cyclophosphamide (CyC)****IV 500mg/m² q2wk × 3mo** (or oral 2mg/kg/day × 3mo)Stops new antibody production
**Corticosteroids****Methylprednisolone 500–1000mg IV × 3 days** → Prednisolone 1mg/kg/day (taper 3–6mo)Anti-inflammatory, ↓ cytokine storm
PLEX: Start immediately — do not wait for biopsy if clinical picture clear. Use 5% albumin or FFP replacement.
Pulmonary Haemorrhage Additions
InterventionIndication
--------------------------
**Intubation/ventilation**Massive haemorrhage, hypoxia
**Recombinant Factor VIIa**Refractory bleeding (case reports)
**Tranexamic acid**Adjunct (avoid if thrombotic risk)
Monitoring
ParameterFrequency
----------------------
**Anti-GBM titre**Weekly during PLEX, then monthly × 6mo, then q3mo
**Renal function**Daily during acute, then weekly
**Hb/iron studies**Weekly (pulmonary bleed)
**ANCA**If double-positive, monitor q3mo
7 Prognosis
FactorGood PrognosisPoor Prognosis
----------------------------------------

Relapse
  • Anti-GBM alone: Very rare (<5%) once antibody cleared.
  • Double-positive: Relapse rate ~20–30% (ANCA component) — consider maintenance azathioprine/rituximab.
8 High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points
[!important] - Anti-GBM disease: Anti-α3(IV)NC1 collagen IgG → linear IgG on IF. - Goodpasture's syndrome = Anti-GBM + pulmonary haemorrhage (40–60%). - HLA-DR15 strong association. - Emergency: PLEX (daily × 14 days) + IV CyC + IV methylprednisolone → START IMMEDIATELY. - Dialysis-dependent at presentation → poor renal recovery (<10%). - Check ANCA — 20–30% double-positive (p-ANCA/MPO) → worse prognosis, ↑ relapse. - Anti-GBM titre monitors disease activity. - Relapse rare in pure anti-GBM; common in double-positive. - Differential linear IgG: Diabetic nephropathy (weak, non-specific), fibrillary GN, amyloid.
9 Common Confusions / Exam Traps
TrapCorrection
------------------
**Linear IgG = always anti-GBM**Also in **diabetic nephropathy** (weak, non-specific), fibrillary GN, amyloid
**Anti-GBM = no ANCA****20–30% double-positive** (p-ANCA/MPO) — worse prognosis
**Wait for biopsy before treatment****NO — treat immediately** if clinical + serology fits (PLEX life-saving)
**PLEX = only for pulmonary bleed**PLEX for **ALL** anti-GBM RPGN (removes antibody)
**Good prognosis if creatinine normal****Creatinine at presentation** is key — >500 = dialysis likely permanent
**Maintenance immunosuppression always needed**Pure anti-GBM: **no maintenance** (relapse rare); Double-positive: consider maintenance
**Anti-GBM IFA = ELISA**ELISA = quantitative, diagnostic standard; IFA = confirmatory
**Children never get anti-GBM**Rare but occurs (paediatric RPGN differential)
**Pulmonary haemorrhage = always Goodpasture's**Also in ANCA vasculitis, SLE, cryoglobulinaemia, anticoagulation
10 Mnemonics
  • Anti-GBM target: α3(IV)NC1 = α3 chain, IV collagen, Non-Collagenous 1 domain
  • Goodpasture's triad: RPGN + Pulmonary haemorrhage + Anti-GBM = RPA
  • HLA: DR15 = DRives 15% of controls vs 88% patients
  • Treatment: PLEX + CyC + Steroids = PCS (start IMMEDIATELY)
  • Prognosis: Creatinine >500 = Chronic Dialysis

19 Summary

Anti-GBM Disease is a Must Know 🔴 emergency topic. Pathogenesis: IgG against α3(IV)NC1 collagen → linear IgG on IF. HLA-DR15. Clinical: RPGN (100%) ± pulmonary haemorrhage (40–60% = Goodpasture's). Diagnosis: Anti-GBM ELISA (quantitative) + always check ANCA (20–30% double-positive). Biopsy: crescents >50%, linear IgG IF, no deposits EM. Emergency Rx: PLEX (daily × 14d) + IV CyC + IV Methylpred → oral taper. Start immediately — do not wait for biopsy. Prognosis: Dialysis-dependent at presentation → <10% renal recovery. Cr <300 = good recovery. Double-positive (20–30%): p-ANCA/MPO, worse prognosis, ↑ relapse → maintenance immunosuppression. Monitoring: Anti-GBM titre weekly → monthly → q3mo. Exam focus: Linear IgG, emergency PLEX+CyC+steroids, creatinine prognosis, double-positive ANCA, Goodpasture's definition, relapse patterns.
