# Rapidly Progressive Glomerulonephritis (RPGN)

<callout icon="🩺" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Rapidly Progressive Glomerulonephritis (RPGN) — Nephrology & Urology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

**Related:** [[Glomerular Diseases — Overview and Classification]], [[Anti-GBM Disease (Goodpasture Syndrome)]], [[MPGN and C3 Glomerulopathy (DDD, C3GN)]], [[Cryoglobulinaemic Glomerulonephritis]], [[ANCA-Associated Vasculitis (GPA, MPA, EGPA)]], [[Lupus Nephritis]], [[Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **RPGN = clinical syndrome of rapid loss of renal function (days–weeks) with glomerular disease. Pathology: Crescentic GN (>50% crescents). Three aetiological categories: Type I (Anti-GBM), Type II (Immune-complex), Type III (Pauci-immune/ANCA). Emergency: treat based on serology (Anti-GBM, ANCA, ANA, Cryo, Complement). Pulse steroids + cyclophosphamide/rituximab ± plasma exchange.**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Define RPGN (rapid AKI + active sediment + crescents)
- Classify by aetiology (Anti-GBM, Immune-complex, Pauci-immune)
- Recognise clinical presentations and serological patterns
- Apply urgent diagnostic workup (serology, biopsy)
- Manage emergently based on category (pulse steroids + CyC/rituximab ± PLEX)
- Differentiate from other causes of rapid AKI
- Apply to FCPS/MRCP emergency nephrology vignettes

---

## 2. Definition & Clinical Presentation
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Definition** | **Rapid loss of GFR** (50% decline in days–weeks) + **active urinary sediment** (haematuria, RBC casts, proteinuria) |
| **Pathology** | **Crescentic GN** — cellular crescents in **>50% glomeruli** |
| **Timecourse** | Days to weeks (vs CKD = months–years) |
| **Presentation** | AKI, haematuria (microscopic/macroscopic), proteinuria (subnephrotic), hypertension, oedema, ± pulmonary haemorrhage |

---

## 3. Aetiological Classification (Crucial for Management)

| Category | Mechanism | Serology | Examples |
|----------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Type I: Anti-GBM** | Anti-α3(IV)NC1 collagen IgG | **Anti-GBM+**, ANCA− | **Anti-GBM disease** (Goodpasture's if pulmonary) |
| **Type II: Immune-Complex** | Immune complex deposition | **Low C3/C4**, ANA/dsDNA+, Cryo+, ANCA− | **Lupus nephritis (III/IV), Post-strep, Cryoglobulinaemia, IE, IgA (rare), MPGN** |
| **Type III: Pauci-immune** | **ANCA-mediated** (neutrophil activation) | **ANCA+ (p-ANCA/MPO or c-ANCA/PR3)**, C3/C4 normal | **GPA, MPA, EGPA, Renal-limited vasculitis** |

> **Double-positive** (Anti-GBM + ANCA) = 20–30% of Type I — treat as both.

---

## 4. Serological Workup (Order STAT)
| Test | Type I | Type II | Type III |
|------|--------|---------|----------|
| **Anti-GBM** | **Positive** | Negative | Negative |
| **ANCA (MPO/PR3)** | Negative (± double-positive) | Negative | **Positive** |
| **C3, C4** | Normal/Low C3 | **Low C3 ± Low C4** | Normal |
| **ANA, dsDNA** | Negative | **Positive (SLE)** | Negative |
| **Cryoglobulins** | Negative | **Positive (Type II/III)** | Negative |
| **Hepatitis B/C, HIV** | Negative | **Positive (HCV, HBV)** | Negative |

---

## 5. Clinical Differentiation

| Feature | Anti-GBM (Type I) | Immune-Complex (Type II) | Pauci-immune/ANCA (Type III) |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Age** | Bimodal (20–30, 60–70) | Variable (SLE: young ♀; Post-strep: children) | Older (>50) |
| **Pulmonary haemorrhage** | **Common (40–60%)** | Rare (SLE, cryo) | **Common in GPA/MPA** |
| **Anti-GBM** | **Positive** | Negative | Negative |
| **ANCA** | Negative* | Negative | **Positive** |
| **Complement** | Normal | **Low C3/C4** | Normal |
| **Biopsy IF** | **Linear IgG** | Granular ("full-house" SLE) | **Pauci-immune** (scant) |
| **Double-positive** | 20–30% ANCA+ | — | 20–30% Anti-GBM+ |

> **Always check both Anti-GBM and ANCA** in RPGN — double-positive changes management.

---

## 6. Emergency Management (Start IMMEDIATELY)

### General Principles
- **Do NOT wait for biopsy** if clinical + serology clear
- **Pulse IV Methylprednisolone 500–1000mg × 3 days** → Oral Prednisolone 1mg/kg/day
- **Cyclophosphamide** (IV 500mg/m² q2wk × 3mo OR oral 2mg/kg/day × 3mo)
- **Plasma Exchange (PLEX)** based on category

---

### Category-Specific Regimens

#### Type I: Anti-GBM
| Component | Regimen |
|-----------|---------|
| **PLEX** | **Daily × 14 days** (or until anti-GBM negative) — **MANDATORY** |
| **Cyclophosphamide** | IV 500mg/m² q2wk × 3mo |
| **Steroids** | Pulse → Oral taper 3–6mo |
| **Rituximab** | Alternative to CyC (especially young women — fertility) |

> **Anti-GBM**: PLEX = life-saving (removes antibody). Dialysis-dependent at presentation → <10% renal recovery.

#### Type II: Immune-Complex (SLE, Cryo, Post-strep, IE)
| Aetiology | Regimen |
|-----------|---------|
| **SLE (Class III/IV)** | Pulse steroids → **MMF 2–3g/day OR IV CyC 500mg/m² q2wk** + steroids |
| **Cryoglobulinaemia** | **PLEX + Rituximab + Steroids** (if severe/RPGN) |
| **Post-strep** | Usually self-limited; supportive; steroids if crescentic >50% |
| **Infective Endocarditis** | **Antibiotics** (surgery if needed); immunosuppression contraindicated |
| **IgA Nephropathy** | Rarely RPGN; if so: steroids ± CyC |

> **Identify and treat infection first** (IE, post-strep) — immunosuppression can worsen.

#### Type III: Pauci-immune (ANCA Vasculitis — GPA, MPA, EGPA)
| Component | Regimen |
|-----------|---------|
| **PLEX** | **If Cr >500 μmol/L or pulmonary haemorrhage** (PEXIVAS trial: PLEX not routine for all) |
| **Induction** | **Rituximab 375mg/m² weekly × 4** (preferred) **OR IV CyC 500mg/m² q2wk × 3mo** |
| **Steroids** | Pulse → Oral taper (RAVE/PEXIVAS: lower cumulative steroid better) |
| **Maintenance** | **Rituximab q6mo OR Azathioprine/MMf** (MAINRITSAN: rituximab superior) |

> **ANCA RPGN**: Rituximab non-inferior to CyC (RAVE); preferred for relapsing/young women.

---

## 7. PLEX Indications Summary
| Category | PLEX Indication |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Anti-GBM** | **ALWAYS** (daily × 14d) |
| **ANCA (Type III)** | Cr >500 (5.7 mg/dL) **OR** Pulmonary haemorrhage |
| **Cryoglobulinaemia** | RPGN, alveolar haemorrhage, severe neuropathy |
| **SLE** | Not routine (consider if TMA, catastrophic APS) |
| **TMA/HUS** | Daily (see TMA topic) |

---

## 8. Prognosis by Category
| Category | Renal Recovery (if treated early) | Relapse Risk |
|----------|-----------------------------------|--------------|
| **Anti-GBM** | Cr <300: 70–80%; Dialysis-dependent: **<10%** | Rare (<5%) once Ab cleared |
| **SLE** | Good with early tx (MMF/CyC) | Moderate (30–40%) |
| **ANCA** | Cr <300: 70–80% | **High (30–50%)** — maintenance needed |
| **Cryoglobulinaemia** | Variable (HCV cure improves) | High if HCV persists |

---

## 9. High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **RPGN** = rapid AKI + active sediment + **crescents >50%**.
> - **Three categories**: Anti-GBM (Type I), Immune-complex (Type II), Pauci-immune/ANCA (Type III).
> - **Emergency workup**: Anti-GBM, ANCA (MPO/PR3), ANA/dsDNA, C3/C4, Cryoglobulins, Hepatitis.
> - **Anti-GBM**: Linear IgG, pulmonary haemorrhage common, **PLEX mandatory × 14d**.
> - **ANCA vasculitis**: Rituximab preferred induction (non-inferior to CyC, fertility-sparing).
> - **Double-positive** (Anti-GBM + ANCA): 20–30% — treat as both.
> - **PLEX**: Anti-GBM = always; ANCA = if Cr >500 or pulmonary bleed.
> - **SLE RPGN**: MMF or CyC + steroids (not PLEX routine).
> - **Prognosis**: Dialysis-dependent Anti-GBM → <10% recovery; ANCA high relapse → maintenance.

## 10. Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **RPGN = always ANCA** | Only **Type III** = ANCA; Type I = Anti-GBM; Type II = Immune-complex |
| **PLEX for all RPGN** | **Anti-GBM = always**; **ANCA = only if Cr >500 or pulmonary bleed** |
| **Rituximab for all** | Anti-GBM: CyC standard (rituximab alternative); ANCA: rituximab preferred; SLE: MMF/CyC |
| **Crescents = RPGN** | **>50% crescents = RPGN**; <50% = crescentic GN but not RPGN clinically |
| **Anti-GBM = no ANCA** | **20–30% double-positive** — worse prognosis, ↑ relapse |
| **Wait for biopsy before treatment** | **NO — treat emergently on serology**; biopsy confirms |
| **SLE RPGN = PLEX** | PLEX not routine for SLE; MMF/CyC + steroids first |
| **All pulmonary haemorrhage = Goodpasture's** | Also ANCA vasculitis, SLE, cryoglobulinaemia, catastrophic APS |
| **ANCA types** | **p-ANCA/MPO** = MPA/EGPA/renal-limited; **c-ANCA/PR3** = GPA |
| **Post-strep = RPGN rare** | Post-strep usually self-limited; RPGN rare, steroids if crescents >50% |

## 11. Mnemonics
- **RPGN Types**: **I** = **I**mmune **A**nti-GBM; **II** = **I**mmune **C**omplex; **III** = **P**auci-immune **A**NCA = **ICAP**
- **Anti-GBM**: **L**inear **I**gG + **P**ulmonary = **LIP**
- **ANCA**: **P**R3 = **P**R3 **G**PA; **M**PO = **M**PA/**E**GPA
- **PLEX**: **A**nti-GBM = **A**lways; **A**NCA = **A**lways if Cr>500/Pulmonary
- **Double-positive**: **A**nti-GBM + **A**NCA = **A**wful (worse prognosis)

## 20. Summary
**Rapidly Progressive Glomerulonephritis (RPGN)** is a **Must Know 🔴** emergency nephrology topic.  
**Definition**: Rapid AKI (days–weeks) + active sediment + **crescents >50%**.  
**Three Categories**: **Type I** = Anti-GBM (linear IgG); **Type II** = Immune-complex (SLE, Cryo, Post-strep, IE); **Type III** = Pauci-immune/ANCA (GPA, MPA, EGPA).  
**Emergency Workup**: Anti-GBM, ANCA (MPO/PR3), ANA/dsDNA, C3/C4, Cryoglobulins, Hepatitis.  
**Management**: **Anti-GBM**: **PLEX daily × 14d** + CyC + Steroids (mandatory). **ANCA**: Rituximab preferred + Steroids; **PLEX if Cr >500 or pulmonary bleed** (PEXIVAS). **SLE**: MMF/CyC + steroids (PLEX not routine). **Cryo**: PLEX + Rituximab + Steroids. **Double-positive (20–30%)**: Treat as both.  
**Prognosis**: Dialysis-dependent Anti-GBM <10% recovery; ANCA high relapse → maintenance rituximab.  
**Exam focus**: 3 types, serology, PLEX indications, rituximab vs CyC, double-positive, SLE vs ANCA management, crescent threshold.
