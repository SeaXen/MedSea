**Related:** [[Glomerular Diseases — Overview and Classification]], [[Primary Glomerulonephritides — IgA Nephropathy (Berger's Disease)]], [[Primary Glomerulonephritides — FSGS]], [[Primary Glomerulonephritides — Minimal Change Disease]], [[Secondary Glomerulonephritides — Lupus Nephritis]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **Membranous Nephropathy = commonest cause of nephrotic syndrome in adults (non-diabetic). Subepithelial immune complexes → complement-mediated podocyte injury. Primary (anti-PLA2R/THSD7A) vs Secondary (SLE, malignancy, infections, drugs). Spontaneous remission ~30%. Management: supportive (ACEi/ARB, SGLT2i) + immunosuppression if high-risk.**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Distinguish primary vs secondary membranous nephropathy
- Interpret anti-PLA2R/THSD7A serology and biopsy stages
- Apply KDIGO risk stratification (low/medium/high) for immunosuppression
- Manage nephrotic complications (thrombosis, infection, lipids)

---

## 2. Epidemiology & Classification

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Epidemiology** | Commonest cause of **adult nephrotic syndrome** (non-diabetic); peak 50–60yo; M>F |
| **Primary (Idiopathic)** | ~70–80%; **anti-PLA2R+ (70–80%)**, **anti-THSD7A+ (2–5%)**, double-negative (15–20%) |
| **Secondary** | ~20–30%; SLE (Class V), malignancy (solid tumours), infections (HBV, HCV, syphilis), drugs (NSAIDs, gold, penicillamine), sarcoidosis |

---

## 3. Pathogenesis

| Mechanism | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Primary** | **Podocyte antigens**: PLA2R (phospholipase A2 receptor), THSD7A (thrombospondin type-1 domain-containing 7A) → autoantibodies (IgG4 dominant) → in situ immune complex formation in subepithelial space |
| **Secondary** | Exogenous antigens (malignancy, drugs, infections) or immune dysregulation (SLE) |
| **Complement** | Membrane attack complex (C5b-9) → podocyte injury → proteinuria |
| **IgG Subclass** | **IgG4 dominant** in primary MN (vs IgG1/3 in secondary) |

---

## 4. Clinical Presentation

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Nephrotic Syndrome** | ~80%: heavy proteinuria (>3.5g/day), hypoalbuminaemia, oedema, hyperlipidaemia |
| **Asymptomatic Proteinuria** | Incidental finding; subnephrotic range |
| **Renal Impairment** | ~20–30% at presentation (usually chronic) |
| **Thrombotic Complications** | **Renal vein thrombosis** (20–40%), PE, DVT — hypercoagulable state |
| **Extrarenal (Secondary)** | SLE features, malignancy symptoms, infection signs |

---

## 5. Histopathology (Biopsy)

| Modality | Findings |
|----------|----------|
| **Light Microscopy** | **Diffuse GBM thickening** (spikes on Jones/PAS); no hypercellularity; advanced = sclerosis |
| **Immunofluorescence** | **Granular capillary wall deposits**: IgG (dominant), C3; **IgG4 predominant** in primary |
| **Electron Microscopy** | **Subepithelial electron-dense deposits** (subepithelial "humps"); corresponding GBM spikes; podocyte foot process effacement |

---

## 6. Staging (Ehrenreich-Churg)

| Stage | EM Findings | Prognosis |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Stage I** | Small, sparse subepithelial deposits; no spikes | Best |
| **Stage II** | Larger, confluent deposits; early spikes | Good |
| **Stage III** | Deposits incorporated into GBM; prominent spikes | Intermediate |
| **Stage IV** | Advanced GBM thickening, sclerosis, few deposits | Worst (chronic) |

> [!key]
> **Stage I–II better response to immunosuppression; Stage III–IV more chronic, less responsive.**

---

## 7. Serology & Biomarkers

| Marker | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| **Anti-PLA2R Ab** | **Highly specific (90–95%)** for primary MN; titre correlates with disease activity; serial monitoring guides therapy |
| **Anti-THSD7A Ab** | ~2–5% primary MN; associated with malignancy |
| **Complement (C3, C4)** | Usually normal (vs low in SLE, post-infectious, C3G) |
| **ANA, anti-dsDNA** | Rule out SLE (Class V lupus) |
| **Malignancy screen** | CT chest/abdomen/pelvis if >60yo, anti-THSD7A+, refractory |

---

## 8. KDIGO Risk Stratification & Management

| Risk | Criteria | 5-yr ESRD Risk | Management |
|------|----------|----------------|------------|
| **Low** | Persistent proteinuria **<4g/day**, **normal eGFR** | <5% | **Supportive only**: ACEi/ARB, SGLT2i, statin, anticoagulation if indicated, monitor q3–6mo |
| **Medium** | Proteinuria **4–8g/day** OR **eGFR <60** | 5–15% | **Consider immunosuppression** if persistent >6mo supportive; shared decision-making |
| **High** | Proteinuria **>8g/day** OR **eGFR <40** | >15% | **Immunosuppression recommended** (see below) |

---

## 9. Immunosuppression Regimens (Primary MN)

| Regimen | Induction | Maintenance | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Rituximab (Preferred)** | **375mg/m² weekly × 4** (or 1g × 2 doses 2wks apart) | **Repeat if relapse** (B-cell driven); monitor CD19+ | **MENTOR trial**: non-inferior to CYC, safer; first-line per KDIGO 2024 |
| **Cyclophosphamide + Steroids (Ponticelli)** | CYC 0.5–1g/m² IV monthly × 6mo (alt monthly oral) + pred 0.5mg/kg alt days | None (6mo total) | **Classic regimen**; effective but higher toxicity (gonadal, bladder, infection) |
| **CNIs (Tacrolimus/Cyclosporine) + Steroids** | Tac 0.05–0.1mg/kg/day + pred | Taper to lowest effective dose | High relapse rate after stopping; nephrotoxicity risk |
| **Anti-PLA2R-guided** | Treat to **anti-PLA2R negativity** | Monitor titres q3mo; retreat if rising | Personalised approach; correlate with proteinuria |

---

## 10. Supportive Care (All Patients)

| Intervention | Target |
|--------------|--------|
| **ACEi/ARB** | Max tolerated; target proteinuria reduction >50% |
| **SGLT2 Inhibitor** | Dapagliflozin/empagliflozin (eGFR ≥20); reduces progression |
| **BP Control** | <130/80 |
| **Statins** | All nephrotic adults (CVD risk) |
| **Anticoagulation** | **Prophylactic** if serum albumin <20g/L + additional risk factors (immobility, surgery, prior VTE); **Therapeutic** if VTE/renal vein thrombosis |
| **Infection Prophylaxis** | Pneumococcal, influenza, COVID vaccines; consider varicella if immunosuppressed |
| **Diet** | Low salt, moderate protein |

---

## 11. Secondary MN — Key Associations

| Cause | Key Features |
|-------|-------------|
| **SLE (Class V)** | **High ANA/dsDNA**, low C3/C4, **full-house IF** (IgG, IgM, IgA, C3, C1q); often mixed Class III/IV + V |
| **Malignancy** | Solid tumours (lung, colon, breast, prostate); older age; **anti-THSD7A+**; remission with cancer treatment |
| **Infections** | **HBV** (children, HBeAg+), **HCV**, syphilis, malaria; treat infection → remission |
| **Drugs** | NSAIDs, gold, penicillamine, captopril, tiopronin; stop drug → remission |
| **Sarcoidosis** | Granulomatous disease; ACE elevated |

## 12. High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **Commonest cause adult nephrotic syndrome (non-diabetic)** → Membranous Nephropathy
> - **Primary**: anti-PLA2R+ (70–80%), anti-THSD7A+ (2–5%)
> - **Biopsy**: Diffuse GBM thickening + **spikes** (Jones); **subepithelial deposits** on EM; **IgG4 dominant** on IF
> - **Staging**: Ehrenreich-Churg I–IV; early stages = better immunosuppression response
> - **KDIGO Risk**: Low (<4g protein, normal eGFR) → supportive; High (>8g or eGFR <40) → immunosuppress
> - **First-line immunosuppression**: **Rituximab** (MENTOR trial) — safer than CYC
> - **Ponticelli**: CYC + steroids (alternating monthly × 6mo) — classic but toxic
> - **Secondary MN screen**: ANA, dsDNA, C3/C4, hepatitis serology, malignancy screen (if >60)
> - **Renal vein thrombosis**: 20–40% risk; prophylactic anticoagulation if albumin <20g/L + risk factors
> - **Spontaneous remission**: ~30% (esp. low-risk)

## 13. Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **All MN = primary** | 20–30% secondary; always screen (ANA, hepatitis, malignancy if >60) |
| **Anti-PLA2R = 100% specific** | 90–95% specific; false + in secondary (rare) |
| **Subepithelial deposits = only MN** | Also post-strep (humps), lupus Class V, infection-related GN |
| **IgG4 dominant = primary** | IgG4 dominant in primary; IgG1/3 in secondary (e.g., SLE) |
| **Rituximab = only for relapse** | Now first-line induction per KDIGO 2024 (MENTOR trial) |
| **All need immunosuppression** | Low-risk: supportive only; spontaneous remission 30% |
| **Anticoagulation for everyone** | Only if albumin <20g/L + risk factors (or therapeutic if VTE) |
| **SLE Class V = primary MN** | SLE = full-house IF, low complement, positive ANA/dsDNA |

## 23. Summary

**Membranous Nephropathy** is a **Must Know 🔴** topic.
**Key takeaway:** Commonest adult nephrotic (non-diabetic). Primary = anti-PLA2R+ (70–80%), anti-THSD7A+ (malignancy). Biopsy: GBM thickening + spikes (LM), granular capillary wall IgG4 (IF), subepithelial deposits (EM). KDIGO risk: Low (<4g, normal eGFR) → supportive; High (>8g or eGFR<40) → immunosuppress. **Rituximab first-line** (MENTOR). Secondary screen: ANA, hepatitis, malignancy (>60). Renal vein thrombosis: 20–40%; prophylactic anticoagulation if albumin <20 + risk factors.
**Exam focus:** Primary vs secondary, anti-PLA2R, biopsy findings, KDIGO risk, Rituximab vs Ponticelli, complications.
**Clinical relevance:** Early rituximab improves remission rates; screening avoids missing secondary causes.
