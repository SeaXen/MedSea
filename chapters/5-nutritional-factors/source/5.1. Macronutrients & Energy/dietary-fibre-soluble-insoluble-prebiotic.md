# Dietary Fibre- Soluble, Insoluble & Prebiotic

**Related:** [[Nutritional Factors in Disease MOC]], [[Davidson Chapter 22 - Nutritional Factors in Disease Hierarchy]], [[../00_Index/Medicine MOC|Medicine MOC]]

> [!important]
> **Dietary fibre = plant polysaccharides + lignin resistant to human small intestinal digestion; fermented in colon; recommended 30 g/day (UK) / 25–38 g/day (IOM); average intake 15–18 g/day.**

## 1. Learning Objectives

## 2. Definitions / Key Concepts

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Dietary Fibre** | Edible plant parts / analogous carbohydrates resistant to digestion/absorption in small intestine; complete/partial fermentation in large intestine |
| **Soluble Fibre** | Dissolves in water → viscous gel; fermentable; slows gastric emptying, ↓glucose/lipid absorption; β-glucan (oats/barley), psyllium, pectin, guar gum, FOS/GOS/inulin |
| **Insoluble Fibre** | Does not dissolve; low fermentability; adds bulk, accelerates transit; cellulose, hemicellulose, lignin (wheat bran, whole grains, vegetable skins) |
| **Functional Fibre** | Isolated/extracted non-digestible carbs with beneficial physiological effect (e.g., inulin, polydextrose, resistant maltodextrin) |
| **Total Fibre** | Dietary Fibre + Functional Fibre (IOM definition) |
| **Fermentability** | Degree colonic bacteria degrade fibre → SCFA (acetate, propionate, butyrate); high (pectin, inulin), medium (β-glucan), low (cellulose, lignin) |
| **Viscosity** | Gel-forming capacity; correlates with metabolic benefits (↓postprandial glucose, ↓LDL-C); high: psyllium, β-glucan, guar; low: wheat bran |
| **Prebiotic** | Substrate selectively utilised by host microorganisms conferring health benefit (FOS, GOS, inulin, lactulose); ↑Bifidobacteria/Lactobacilli |
| **Synbiotic** | Probiotic + prebiotic combination (e.g., yogurt + inulin) |
| **Resistant Starch (RS)** | Starch escaping small intestinal digestion: RS1 (physically inaccessible), RS2 (raw granules), RS3 (retrograded), RS4 (chemically modified); acts as fermentable fibre |
| **SCFA** | Short-chain fatty acids (acetate C2, propionate C3, butyrate C4); butyrate = colonocyte fuel (70% energy), anti-inflammatory, ↑colon blood flow; propionate → hepatic gluconeogenesis inhibition; acetate → peripheral energy |
| **FODMAP** | Fermentable Oligo-, Di-, Monosaccharides And Polyols; poorly absorbed, rapidly fermented → gas/bloating; restrict in IBS; many fibres are high-FODMAP (inulin, FOS, wheat fructans) |
| **Diverticular Disease** | Diverticulosis (asymptomatic pouches) → diverticulitis (inflammation); fibre ↑stool bulk → ↓intraluminal pressure → prevention |
| **Bristol Stool Scale** | Type 1–2 constipation, 3–4 normal, 5–7 diarrhoea; fibre aims for Type 4 (smooth, soft sausage) |

## 3. Core Content

### Section 1: Classification of Dietary Fibre
**By Solubility:**
- **Soluble viscous (gel-forming → metabolic benefits):** β-glucan (oats, barley), psyllium (Plantago ovata husk), pectin (citrus, apple), guar gum, glucomannan (konjac), alginates
- **Soluble non-viscous (highly fermentable → prebiotic):** Inulin (chicory root), FOS (fructooligosaccharides), GOS (galactooligosaccharides), lactulose, resistant starch
- **Insoluble (bulk-forming → laxation):** Cellulose, hemicellulose (arabinoxylan), lignin (wheat bran, whole grains, nuts, seeds, vegetable skins)

**By Fermentability (CODEX):**
- **High:** Inulin, FOS, GOS, pectin, resistant starch, lactulose → high SCFA, gas
- **Moderate:** β-glucan, psyllium, guar gum, arabinoxylan
- **Low:** Cellulose, lignin, wheat bran → minimal gas, primary laxation

**By Physiological Effect (EFSA health claims):**
- **Cholesterol-lowering:** β-glucan ≥3 g/day, psyllium ≥7 g/day, guar gum
- **Glycaemic control:** Viscous fibres (psyllium, β-glucan, guar, konjac) reduce postprandial glucose
- **Laxation:** Wheat bran (≥10 g), psyllium, cellulose, resistant starch
- **Prebiotic:** Inulin, FOS, GOS (≥3–5 g/day) → ↑Bifidobacteria

### Section 2: Physiological Mechanisms
**Upper GI:**
- **Gastric emptying:** Viscous fibres ↑gastric volume, ↓emptying rate → satiety, ↓postprandial glucose peak
- **Nutrient absorption:** Viscous gel ↓diffusion of glucose/cholesterol/bile acids across unstirred water layer → ↓absorption
- **Bile acid binding:** Fibres (esp. psyllium, β-glucan) bind bile acids in ileum → faecal excretion → hepatic cholesterol → bile acids → ↓serum LDL-C (1 mmol/L bile acid loss ≈ 0.5 mmol/L LDL-C ↓)

**Lower GI (Colon):**
- **Fermentation:** Anaerobic bacteria (Bacteroidetes, Firmicutes) hydrolyse fibre → SCFA + gases (H₂, CO₂, CH₄)
- **SCFA effects:** Butyrate (colonocyte energy, HDAC inhibition → anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory), Propionate (hepatic cholesterol synthesis inhibition ↓HMG-CoA reductase), Acetate (peripheral energy, lipogenesis)
- **Microbiome modulation:** Prebiotics selectively ↑Bifidobacterium, Lactobacillus → ↓pathogens, ↑mucin, ↑IgA, ↓pH (inhibits C. difficile)
- **Stool bulk:** Insoluble fibre + bacterial mass + water retention → ↑stool weight, ↓transit time, ↓intraluminal pressure

**Systemic:**
- **CVD:** Meta-analyses: +7 g/day fibre → ↓CVD 9%, ↓CHD 9%, ↓stroke 7% (viscous predominant)
- **T2DM:** +10 g/day cereal fibre → ↓T2DM 25%; viscous fibre improves HbA1c –0.5%
- **Colorectal Cancer:** WCRF: ≥90 g/day whole grains (≈30 g fibre) → ↓CRC 17%; butyrate → apoptosis, ↓proliferation
- **Weight:** High fibre diets ↓energy density, ↑satiety (GLP-1, PYY), ↓weight gain

### Section 4: Prebiotics & Synbiotics
**Definition (ISAPP 2017):** "Selectively utilised by host microorganisms conferring health benefit" — must resist gastric acid/enzymes, fermented by colonic microbiota, selective for beneficial genera.
**Established prebiotics:**
- **Inulin-type fructans:** Inulin (DP 2–60), oligofructose/FOS (DP 2–10); chicory root, Jerusalem artichoke, onion, garlic, wheat, banana
- **GOS:** From lactose (β-galactosidase); human milk oligosaccharide analogue; infant formula
- **Lactulose:** Synthetic (galactose+fructose); osmotic laxative + prebiotic; hepatic encephalopathy
**Candidate prebiotics:** Polydextrose, resistant starch, pectin, arabinoxylan, β-glucan, polyphenols (cocoa, tea)
**Synbiotics:** Probiotic + prebiotic (e.g., Bifidobacterium + inulin); synergistic survival/colonisation
**Health claims (EFSA/ISAPP):** ↑Bifidobacteria (✓), ↑Ca/Mg absorption (✓ inulin), ↓constipation (✓ lactulose/GOS), ↓atopic dermatitis (✗ inconsistent), ↓traveller's diarrhoea (✗)

### Section 5: Clinical Applications & Disease-Specific Recommendations

| Condition | Fibre Recommendation | Rationale / Evidence |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Constipation** (functional) | **Psyllium 10–15 g/day** or **wheat bran 10–20 g/day** + fluids 2–3 L | Psyllium > bran for stool frequency/consistency (RCT); gradual titration to avoid bloating |
| **IBS-C** | **Soluble: psyllium 5–10 g, oat β-glucan** | INSOLUBLE (wheat bran) WORSENS symptoms; Low FODMAP fibre (psyllium, oat, linseed) |
| **IBS-D** | **Low FODMAP** + **psyllium** (gel-forming) | Avoid inulin, FOS, GOS, wheat bran, resistant starch (rapid fermentation → gas) |
| **Diverticular disease** | **High fibre 30+ g/day** (insoluble > soluble) | EPIC: >25 g/day ↓diverticulitis 41%; prevents progression diverticulosis→diverticulitis |
| **IBD (Crohn's/UC remission)** | **Low residue during flare**; **Soluble (psyllium, oat) in remission** | Insoluble may obstruct strictures; UC: psyllium = mesalamine for maintenance (RCT) |
| **Post-op ileus / ERAS** | **Early enteral + soluble fibre (guar/pectin)** | Gum arabic/pectin ↓post-op ileus duration; avoid bulk if anastomosis fresh |
| **Radiation enteritis** | **Low fibre during acute**; **Soluble (psyllium) chronic** | Chronic radiation → stricture risk; psyllium improves stool form without obstruction |
| **Diabetes (T2DM)** | **Viscous soluble 10–15 g/day** (psyllium, β-glucan, guar, konjac) | HbA1c ↓0.5%, postprandial glucose ↓20–30%; add to metformin/lifestyle |
| **Hypercholesterolaemia** | **β-glucan 3 g (oats/barley) OR psyllium 7–10 g/day** | LDL-C ↓5–15%; adjunct to statin (portfolio diet) |
| **CKD (non-dialysis)** | **Moderate fibre 20–25 g** + **prebiotic (inulin/FOS)** | SCFA ↓uraemic toxins (indoxyl sulphate, p-cresyl sulphate); avoid high K fruit/veg if hyperK |
| **Hepatic encephalopathy** | **Lactulose 30–45 mL/day** (titrate 2–3 softstools) | ↓colonic pH → ↓NH₃ absorption → ↓neurotoxicity; prebiotic effect |
| **Critical care (EN)** | **Fibre-enriched formula (10–15 g/L)** if tolerating EN | ↓diarrhoea, ↑SCFA, ↓ventilator days (meta-analysis); avoid if high gastric residuals |

### Section 6: Adverse Effects & Contraindications
**Common:** Bloating, flatulence, abdominal distension (rapid titration, high fermentability); manage: **start low, go slow** (5 g increments/week), adequate fluids (2–3 L/day).
**Phytobezoar:** Persimmon, cellulose, pumpkin; ↑risk with gastrectomy, hypomotility.
**Mineral binding:** Phytate (whole grains, legumes) binds Fe, Zn, Ca, Mg → ↓absorption 10–40%; soaking/sprouting/fermenting degrades phytate.
**Drug interactions:** Psyllium binds digoxin, lithium, carbamazepine, metformin, thyroxine → separate ≥2 hours.
**Contraindications:** Suspected bowel obstruction, strictures (Crohn's), acute diverticulitis, post-colonic anastomosis (<7 days), severe dysphagia.

### Section 8: Labelling & Food Sources
**UK label:** "High fibre" ≥6 g/100 g; "Source of fibre" ≥3 g/100 g.
**Food sources (per typical portion):**
- **Very high (>6 g):** Bran cereals (All-Bran 10 g/40 g), chia seeds (10 g/25 g), lentils (8 g/100 g cooked), baked beans (7 g/200 g), pear with skin (6 g)
- **High (3–6 g):** Oats (4 g/40 g), wholemeal bread (3 g/slice), broccoli (3 g/80 g), sweet potato skin (4 g), almonds (3 g/30 g)
- **Moderate (1–3 g):** Brown rice (2 g/100 g), banana (2 g), apple with skin (2 g), carrots (2 g/80 g)
**Processing effect:** Milling → removes bran (↓fibre 70%); cooking → minor loss; juicing → removes insoluble fibre (pulp).

## 6. Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| Wheat bran good for all constipation | **Worse in IBS, opioid constipation, slow transit**; psyllium safer (gel-forming, less gas) |
| All fibre = prebiotic | **Only selectively fermented fibres (FOS/GOS/inulin/lactulose/RS) = prebiotic**; cellulose/lignin not |
| Inulin good for gut health universally | **High FODMAP → bloats IBS**; use psyllium/oat β-glucan instead in IBS |
| Phytate only in whole grains | **Legumes, nuts, seeds also high**; soaking/sprouting/fermenting ↓phytate 50–90% |
| Psyllium = bran | **Psyllium: soluble, viscous, gel, low gas**; Bran: insoluble, bulk, high phytate, gas |
| Low residue = low fibre | **Low residue = low stool output (↓insoluble, ↓raw veg); soluble fibre (psyllium) often allowed** |
| Fibre always lowers cholesterol | **Only viscous soluble (β-glucan, psyllium, pectin, guar)**; insoluble (wheat bran) minimal effect |
| Resistant starch = starch | **Acts as fermentable fibre**; butyrate producer; cooled potato/pasta/rice > hot |
| Prebiotic = probiotic | **Prebiotic = food for bacteria**; Probiotic = live bacteria; Synbiotic = both |
| High fibre = low calorie | **Fibre 2 kcal/g (fermentation → SCFA)**; not zero; whole foods lower energy density |

## 7. Mnemonics

- **Fibre types:** **SIV** = **S**oluble **I**nsoluble **V**iscous **F**ermentable (classification axes)
- **Soluble metabolic effects:** **GEL** = **G**astric emptying ↓, **E**nteral absorption ↓ (glucose/cholesterol), **L**DL-C ↓
- **SCFA:** **ABC** = **A**cetate (peripheral), **B**utyrate (colonocyte fuel, anti-cancer), **C** (Propionate) hepatic cholesterol ↓
- **Prebiotics:** **FIL** = **F**OS, **I**nulin, **L**actulose (+ GOS, RS)
- **IBS fibre:** **PSYLLIUM YES, BRAN NO** (soluble gel vs insoluble bulk/gas)
- **Diverticular:** **FIBRE PREVENTS** → bulk ↓pressure → ↓diverticula formation → ↓diverticulitis
- **Drug binding:** **PSYLLIUM BINDS** → separate 2h: Digoxin, Lithium, Carbamazepine, Metformin, Thyroxine
- **Butyrate:** **BUTYRATE = B**owel fuel, **U**nicancer, **T**ight junctions, **Y**inflammation ↓, **R**esolution, **A**poptosis, **T**ransporter (MCT1), **E**pigenetics (HDACi)
- **FODMAP fibre:** **FOOD** = **F**ructans (wheat, onion), **O**ligosaccharides (GOS/FOS), **O**ligos (lactose), **D**isaccharides (isomaltose), **M**onos (fructose), **A**nd **P**olyols (sorbitol, mannitol)

### Short Note (7 min)
**Topic:** "Prebiotics"
- ISAPP definition: "Substrate selectively utilised by host microorganisms conferring health benefit"
- Established: Inulin, FOS, GOS, lactulose; candidate: RS, polyphenols
- Mechanisms: ↓pH → ↓pathogens; ↑Bifidobacterium/Lactobacillus; ↑SCFA (butyrate); ↑mineral absorption
- Clinical: constipation (GOS/lactulose), hepatic encephalopathy (lactulose), infant formula (GOS), IBD (inconsistent)

### Viva Answer (3 min)
**Q:** "Why is wheat bran bad for IBS?"
"A: Wheat bran is **insoluble, poorly fermentable, high phytate** — increases stool bulk but **does not form gel**, worsens **bloating/distension** in visceral hypersensitivity, and **rapid transit → diarrhoea**. **Use psyllium or oat β-glucan**: soluble, viscous gel, normalises stool form, low gas, prebiotic effect."

## 18. Answer Key with Explanations

### MCQs
1. **B** — CODEX: carbohydrate polymers ≥10 monomeric units resistant to hydrolysis by endogenous small intestinal enzymes; includes resistant starch, NDO.
2. **C** — Psyllium = soluble, highly viscous, gel-forming; wheat bran/cellulose/lignin = insoluble; RS2 = insoluble granules (fermentable but not gel).
3. **B** — ISAPP 2017 update: "substrate selectively utilised..." Probiotic = live microbes; synbiotic = probiotic + prebiotic.
4. **B** — Inulin/FOS/GOS/lactulose are established; polydextrose/arabinoxylan/β-glucan/pectin are candidate.
5. **B** — IBS-C: soluble (psyllium/oat) gel normalises stool; bran insoluble worsens bloating; inulin/FOS high FODMAP.
6. **C** — SACN 2015: 30 g/day for adults (previously 18 g NSP).
7. **B** — Bile acid binding in terminal ileum → enterohepatic interruption → hepatic conversion cholesterol→bile acids → LDL-R upregulation → ↑LDL clearance.
8. **C** — Butyrate = major colonocyte fuel (β-oxidation), HDAC inhibition → apoptosis/anti-inflammatory, tight junction integrity.
9. **B** — Acute diverticulitis/strictures/anastomosis: insoluble bulk = obstruction risk; soluble (psyllium) often tolerated.
10. **B** — Psyllium forms gel → binds drugs in GI lumen; separate ≥2h for narrow TI drugs (digoxin, lithium, carbamazepine, thyroxine, metformin).

### SBAs
1. **C** — IBS-D: bran/inulin/FOS/GOS = high FODMAP → rapid fermentation → gas/bloating; psyllium low FODMAP, gel-forming, normalises stool.
2. **B** — T2DM: viscous soluble (psyllium, β-glucan, guar) with meals ↓postprandial glucose; wheat bran insoluble = no metabolic benefit.

## 19. Summary

**Dietary Fibre** is a **Must Know 🔴** topic for FCPS/MRCP.
