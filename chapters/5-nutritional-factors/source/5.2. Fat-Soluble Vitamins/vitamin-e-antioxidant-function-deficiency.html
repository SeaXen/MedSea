2 Definitions / Key Concepts
TermDefinition
------------------
**Tocopherols**Saturated side chain (phytyl); α, β, γ, δ differ in methyl groups on chromanol ring
**Tocotrienols**Unsaturated side chain (3 double bonds); α, β, γ, δ; more potent antioxidant (membrane mobility)
**α-TTP (α-Tocopherol Transfer Protein)**Hepatic protein; binds α-tocopherol preferentially (vs γ/δ) → incorporation into VLDL → plasma; TTPA gene mutation → isolated vit E deficiency
**RRR-α-Tocopherol**Natural stereoisomer (2R,4'R,8'R); "d-α-tocopherol"; 100% bioactivity; 1 mg = 1.49 IU
**All-rac-α-Tocopherol**Synthetic mixture of 8 stereoisomers; "dl-α-tocopherol"; ~50% bioactivity; 1 mg = 2.22 IU (but only 1/8 is RRR)
**IU Conversion**1 mg RRR-α-tocopherol = 1.49 IU; 1 mg all-rac = 2.22 IU (label); but effective bioactivity ratio ~2:1
**Lipid Peroxidation**Free radical chain: Initiation (LH → L•) → Propagation (L• + O₂ → LOO• → LOOH + L•) → Termination
**Antioxidant Cycle**LOO• + α-TOH → LOOH + α-TO• (tocopheroxyl radical); α-TO• + Ascorbate/GSH → α-TOH (recycled)
**α-Tocopherol**Major membrane antioxidant; 1 molecule/200–500 phospholipids; protects PUFA in lipoprotein/membrane
**γ-Tocopherol**Major form in US diet (soybean/corn oil); traps reactive nitrogen species (RNS); ↑COX-2 inhibition
**Abetalipoproteinaemia**MTTP mutation (AR) → no microsomal triglyceride transfer protein → no chylomicron/VLDL assembly → fat malabsorption + vit A/D/E/K deficiency; acanthocytosis, retinitis pigmentosa, neuropathy
**Isolated Vit E Deficiency (AVED)**TTPA mutation (AR, chromosome 8) → ↓α-TTP → α-tocopherol not secreted into VLDL → plasma vit E <5% normal; onset 5–15y; responds to high-dose oral vit E (800–1200 IU/day)
**Preterm Haemolytic Anaemia**Low vit E stores (transplacental transfer late 3rd trimester) + high O₂ + iron → lipid peroxidation of RBC membrane → haemolysis; prevented by vit E supplementation
**SELECT Trial**Selenium + Vitamin E Cancer Prevention Trial; 35,000 men; vit E 400 IU/day + Se 200 µg → ↑prostate cancer HR 1.17 (significant); stopped early
**Meta-Analysis (Miller 2005)**19 trials, 136,000 pts; vit E ≥400 IU/day → ↑all-cause mortality (RR 1.04, p=0.04); dose-response >150 IU/day
**HDL & Statins**HDL-C ↑ with niacin + statin; adding vit E (along with vit C, β-carotene) → blunts HDL₂ rise (ARBITER, HATS)
3 Core Content
Section 1: Chemistry, Isoforms & Bioavailability

8 natural isoforms: 4 tocopherols (saturated phytyl tail) + 4 tocotrienols (unsaturated isoprenoid tail with 3 double bonds). Each has α, β, γ, δ forms based on methyl substitution on chromanol ring (α = 3 methyls, β/γ = 2, δ = 1). Biological potency: α-tocopherol > β-tocopherol > γ-tocopherol > δ-tocopherol (α-TTP affinity). Tocotrienols > tocopherols antioxidant capacity (unsaturated tail → better membrane penetration). Absorption: Like other fat-soluble vitamins — mixed micelles → enterocyte → chylomicron secretion → lymph. α-TTP in liver selectively transfers α-tocopherol to VLDL for plasma distribution; γ/δ-tocopherols metabolised by CYP4F2 → excreted in bile/urine. Plasma levels: α-tocopherol 5–20 µg/mL (11.6–46 µmol/L); γ-tocopherol 1–5 µg/mL (varies by diet). Bioavailability: Natural RRR-α-tocopherol (d-α-tocopherol) 2x synthetic all-rac-α-tocopherol (dl-α-tocopherol). Ester forms (acetate, succinate) hydrolysed in gut → free tocopherol.

Section 2: Antioxidant Mechanism

Lipid peroxidation chain reaction:

  1. Initiation: ROS (OH•, ROO•) abstract H from lipid (LH) → lipid radical (L•)
  2. Propagation: L• + O₂ → LOO• (lipid peroxyl radical) → LOO• + LH → LOOH (lipid hydroperoxide) + L• (chain continues)
  3. Termination: Radical-radical reactions or antioxidant intervention

Vitamin E action (chain-breaking):

  • LOO• + α-TOH → LOOH + α-TO• (tocopheroxyl radical)
  • α-TO• relatively unreactive (resonance-stabilised) → chain terminated
  • Recycling: α-TO• + Ascorbate (vit C) → α-TOH + Ascorbyl radical → GSH/NADPH → Ascorbate
  • Synergy: Vit E (lipid phase) + Vit C (aqueous phase) + GSH (intracellular) = antioxidant network

Non-antioxidant functions: Gene expression (↓CD36 scavenger receptor, ↓VCAM-1), inhibition of PKC (smooth muscle proliferation), platelet aggregation inhibition (↓TXA₂, ↑prostacyclin), immune modulation (↑IL-2, NK activity), regulation of HMG-CoA reductase.

Section 3: Requirements & Sources

RDA (IOM 2000): 15 mg/day (22.4 IU) α-tocopherol for adults; based on plasma α-tocopherol 12 µmol/L at intake 12 mg/day. UL: 1,000 mg/day (1,500 IU natural / 1,100 IU synthetic) — based on bleeding risk. Dietary sources (α-tocopherol mg/serving):

  • Wheat germ oil (1 Tbsp = 20 mg), Sunflower oil (1 Tbsp = 5.6 mg), Almonds (1 oz = 7.3 mg), Sunflower seeds (1 oz = 7.4 mg), Peanut butter (2 Tbsp = 2.9 mg), Spinach (½ cup cooked = 1.9 mg), Avocado (½ = 2.1 mg)
  • γ-Tocopherol dominant in US diet: Soybean oil, corn oil, canola oil (major contributors)

Deficiency risk: Extremely rare in healthy adults (large body stores, efficient recycling). Only in fat malabsorption or genetic defects.

Section 4: Deficiency — Causes & Clinical Features
CauseMechanism
------------------
**Cystic fibrosis**Pancreatic insufficiency → fat malabsorption
**Cholestatic liver disease**Biliary atresia, PSC, Alagille → ↓bile salts → fat malabsorption
**Abetalipoproteinaemia (Bassen-Kornzweig)**MTTP mutation → no apoB → no chylomicron/VLDL → fat malabsorption + all fat-soluble vit deficiency
**Isolated Vitamin E Deficiency (AVED)**TTPA mutation → ↓α-TTP → α-toc not secreted in VLDL; plasma α-toc <5% normal
**Short bowel / intestinal resection**↓Surface area, ↓bile salt pool
**CRF / dialysis**Losses, dietary restriction, oxidative stress
**Genetic (CYP4F2, SCARB1)**Rare variants affecting metabolism/transport

Clinical Triad (Neurological):

  1. Spinocerebellar ataxia — gait instability, dysmetria, intention tremor, dysarthria, nystagmus; posterior column + cerebellar degeneration
  2. Peripheral neuropathy — axonal sensorimotor; loss of vibration/position sense; areflexia; distal weakness
  3. Pigmented retinopathy — granular retinal pigment epithelium changes; night blindness; "retinitis pigmentosa-like"

Additional: Haemolytic anaemia (preterm infants), immune impairment, muscle weakness, cognitive decline (late).

Abetalipoproteinaemia (MTTP, AR): Onset infancy — steatorrhoea, failure to thrive, acanthocytosis (spur cells), retinitis pigmentosa, spinocerebellar degeneration, coagulopathy (vit K deficiency). Landmark: Absent apoB, LDL, VLDL; cholesterol <50 mg/dL; triglycerides <20 mg/dL. Treatment: High-dose fat-soluble vitamins (vit E 100–200 IU/kg/day), medium-chain triglycerides, fat restriction.

AVED (TTPA, AR): Onset 5–15 years — progressive ataxia, areflexia, proprioception loss, retinopathy; plasma α-toc <5% normal with normal lipids/lipoproteins. Treatment: High-dose oral α-tocopherol 800–1200 IU/day (RRR) → halts progression. Genetic testing confirmatory.

Preterm Infants: Low vit E stores (transplacental transfer peaks 32–36w), high O₂ exposure, iron catalysis → lipid peroxidation of RBC membranes → haemolytic anaemia. Prevention: Vit E 10–20 IU/kg/day in TPN/formula (modern formulas supplemented).

Section 5: Toxicity & High-Dose Risks

UL: 1000 mg/day (1500 IU natural, 1100 IU synthetic) — based on bleeding tendency (antiplatelet effect). Acute toxicity: Rare; very high doses (>10,000 IU) → nausea, diarrhoea, fatigue, weakness, blurred vision. Chronic high-dose risks (Epidemiological):

  • All-cause mortality ↑: Miller et al. 2005 meta-analysis (19 RCTs, 136,000 pts): vit E ≥400 IU/day → ↑all-cause mortality (RR 1.04, 95% CI 1.01–1.07); dose-response >150 IU/day.
  • Haemorrhagic stroke ↑: HPFS/WHS: vit E 600 IU q2d → ↑haemorrhagic stroke (RR 1.22); no benefit for ischaemic stroke.
  • Prostate cancer ↑: SELECT trial (35,533 men): vit E 400 IU/day + Se 200 µg/day → ↑prostate cancer HR 1.17 (99% CI 1.004–1.36); vit E alone also ↑risk; stopped early 2008.
  • Heart failure ↑: HOPE-TOO, GISSI-Prevenzione: vit E 400 IU/day → ↑HF hospitalisation.
  • All-cause mortality meta-analysis: Cochrane 2018 (78 RCTs, 299,000): vit E ↑mortality (RR 1.03); no cardiovascular benefit.

Mechanism of harm: High-dose α-tocopherol → ↓γ-tocopherol (competition for α-TTP) → loss of γ-tocopherol's unique RNS trapping & anti-inflammatory effects; pro-oxidant at high concentrations (tocopheroxyl radical not recycled); interference with vitamin K (↑bleeding); modulation of CYP450, signal transduction.

Viva Answer (3 min)

Q: "Why is vitamin E supplementation not recommended for cardiovascular prevention?" "A: No benefit, possible harm. Large RCTs (HOPE-TOO, GISSI-P, PHS II) and meta-analyses (Cochrane 2018, 78 RCTs, 299k pts) show no reduction in CVD events; Cochrane: vit E ↑all-cause mortality RR 1.03. 17 Flashcards
  • Q: 8 vitamin E isoforms

A: 4 tocopherols (α,β,γ,δ) + 4 tocotrienols (α,β,γ,δ)

  • Q: α-TTP function

A: Hepatic protein; binds α-tocopherol preferentially → incorporates into VLDL for plasma distribution

  • Q: Antioxidant mechanism

A: LOO• + α-TOH → LOOH + α-TO• (tocopheroxyl radical); α-TO• + Vit C/GSH → α-TOH (recycled)

  • Q: Deficiency ONLY in

A: Fat malabsorption syndromes (CF, cholestasis, abetalipo, AVED, preterm)

  • Q: Neuro triad

A: Spinocerebellar ataxia, Peripheral neuropathy (axonal sensory), Pigmented retinopathy

  • Q: Abetalipoproteinaemia

A: MTTP mutation → no MTP → no chylomicron/VLDL → absent apoB, LDL, VLDL; acanthocytes, retinitis, coagulopathy

  • Q: AVED (TTPA)

A: Normal lipids/lipoproteins; plasma α-toc <5% normal; Tx: RRR-α-toc 800–1200 IU/day lifelong

  • Q: Preterm haemolytic anaemia

A: Low vit E stores + high O₂ + iron → lipid peroxidation of RBC membranes; Tx: 10–20 IU/kg/day

  • Q: SELECT trial

A: 35,533 men; vit E 400 IU/day + Se 200 µg → ↑prostate cancer HR 1.17; stopped early

  • Q: Meta-analysis >400 IU/day

A: ↑All-cause mortality (RR 1.03–1.04); no CVD benefit (Cochrane 2018, 78 RCTs, 299k)

  • Q: Warfarin interaction

A: ↑INR/bleeding risk; antiplatelet effect (↓TXA₂, ↑PGI₂)

  • Q: Statins + Niacin + Antioxidants

A: ARBITER/HATS: vit E/C/β-carotene blunt HDL₂ rise from niacin+statin

  • Q: UL & bioactivity

A: UL 1000 mg (1500 IU natural); RRR-α-toc 2x bioactivity of all-rac

  • Q: γ-tocopherol

A: Major dietary form (soybean/corn oil); traps RNS (NO₂•, ONOO⁻); anti-inflammatory

  • Q: Synthetic vs natural

A: All-rac (dl-α-toc) = 8 stereoisomers; only 1/8 is RRR; 2x dose needed for same bioactivity

19 Summary

Vitamin E is a Must Know 🔴 topic for FCPS/MRCP. Key takeaway: 8 isoforms; α-tocopherol biologically active (α-TTP retention); chain-breaking antioxidant (LOO• + α-TOH → recycled by vit C/GSH); deficiency ONLY in fat malabsorption (CF, cholestasis, abetalipoproteinaemia, AVED, preterm); neurological triad (ataxia, neuropathy, retinopathy); abetalipoproteinaemia (MTTP, no apoB) vs AVED (TTPA, normal lipids); AVED treated with high-dose RRR-α-tocopherol 800–1200 IU/day.
