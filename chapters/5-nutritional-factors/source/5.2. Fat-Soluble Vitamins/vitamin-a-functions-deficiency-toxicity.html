2 Definitions / Key Concepts
TermDefinition
------------------
**Retinol**Preformed vitamin A (alcohol); transport form in blood (bound to RBP + transthyretin)
**Retinal**Aldehyde; visual chromophore (11-cis-retinal + opsin = rhodopsin)
**Retinoic Acid**Acid; nuclear receptor ligand (RAR/RXR) → gene transcription (growth, differentiation)
**β-Carotene**Provitamin A carotenoid (2 retinal equivalents); plant source; cleavage by BCO1
**RAE (Retinol Activity Equivalent)**1 µg retinol = 1 µg RAE = 12 µg β-carotene = 24 µg α-carotene/β-cryptoxanthin
**RBP**Retinol-binding protein; transports retinol in 1:1 complex with transthyretin
**BCO1**β-carotene 15,15'-oxygenase; cleaves β-carotene → 2 retinal; polymorphic (↓conversion)
**Rhodopsin**Visual pigment (rods); 11-cis-retinal + opsin; light → all-trans-retinal → neural signal
**Night Blindness (X1A)**Inability to see in dim light; earliest sign; impaired rod function
**Bitot's Spots (X1B)**Triangular foamy deposits on bulbar conjunctiva (temporal); keratinised epithelium
**Conjunctival Xerosis (X1B)**Dry, lusterless conjunctiva; loss of goblet cells; keratinisation
**Corneal Xerosis (X2)**Dry, hazy cornea; loss of lustre; precedes ulceration
**Keratomalacia (X3A/X3B)**Corneal softening/liquefaction; X3A <1/3, X3B >1/3; ophthalmic emergency
**Follicular Hyperkeratosis**Phrynoderma; horny plugs in hair follicles (extensor surfaces); vitamin A deficiency
**Hypervitaminosis A (Acute)**Single massive dose (>100x RDA): nausea, vomiting, headache, bulging fontanelle (infants)
**Hypervitaminosis A (Chronic)**Prolonged excess (>10x RDA months): dry skin, alopecia, hepatomegaly, hyperostosis, teratogenic
**Pseudotumor Cerebri (PIVS)**↑ICP without mass lesion; papilloedema, headache, VI nerve palsy; vit A / tetracycline / withdrawal
**Carotenoderma**Yellow-orange skin (palms/soles, nasolabial folds); NO scleral icterus; β-carotene excess
**Isotretinoin (13-cis-retinoic acid)**Severe acne; teratogenic Category X; requires pregnancy prevention programme (iPledge); side effects: cheilitis, dry skin, ↑TG, ↑LFT, mood changes
**WHO X Classification**X1A night blindness, X1B Bitot's/conjunctival xerosis, X2 corneal xerosis, X3A keratomalacia <1/3, X3B >1/3, XS corneal scar
3 Core Content
Section 1: Vitamin A Forms, Absorption & Transport

Forms: Preformed (retinyl esters: animal liver, fish oil, dairy, eggs) + Provitamin A carotenoids (β-carotene, α-carotene, β-cryptoxanthin: carrots, sweet potato, spinach, mango, papaya). Absorption: Retinyl esters → pancreatic lipase/intestinal esterases → retinol → mixed micelles → enterocyte → re-esterified → chylomicron → lymph. Carotenoids → passive diffusion → enterocyte → BCO1 cleavage → retinal → retinol (less efficient, variable). Transport: Chylomicron remnant uptake by liver → stored as retinyl esters (stellate cells) → mobilised → retinol + RBP + transthyretin → systemic delivery → STRA6 receptor uptake. Storage: Liver (80–90% total body); normal liver reserve = 100–300 µg/g; deficiency when <20 µg/g (≈0.7 µmol/L plasma retinol). Excretion: Bile (retinol glucuronide) → faeces; urine (polar metabolites).

Section 2: Visual Cycle & Epithelial Function

Rhodopsin cycle (rods): Dark: 11-cis-retinal + opsin → rhodopsin (λmax 498 nm). Photon → 11-cis → all-trans retinal → metarhodopsin II → activates transducin (Gt) → activates cGMP phosphodiesterase → cGMP hydrolysed → cGMP-gated Na⁺ channels close → hyperpolarisation → ↓glutamate release → bipolar cell signal. Regeneration: all-trans-retinal → all-trans-retinol (RDH) → transported to RPE → isomerised to 11-cis-retinol (RPE65) → oxidised to 11-cis-retinal (RDH) → back to photoreceptor. Epithelial differentiation: Retinoic acid → binds RAR/RXR nuclear receptors → regulates HOX genes, growth factors → maintains columnar epithelium, goblet cells, mucus secretion. Deficiency → squamous metaplasia (respiratory, GI, GU tracts) → ↑infection risk.

Section 3: Immune Function & Measles

Mechanism: Retinoic acid → ↑T-cell proliferation, Th2 differentiation, IgA secretion, neutrophil function, macrophage phagocytosis, NK activity. Measles mortality: WHO: vitamin A supplementation (200,000 IU day 1 + day 2) ↓measles mortality 50% in children >12m; ↓diarrhoea mortality 33%. Infection cycle: Infection → ↓retinol (acute phase: ↓RBP synthesis, ↓retinol mobilisation) → ↓immune function → worse infection.

Section 5: WHO Prevention & Treatment

Prophylaxis (universal in high-prevalence areas):

  • Infants 6–12m: 100,000 IU (30 mg RE) oral q4–6m
  • Children 12–59m: 200,000 IU (60 mg RE) oral q4–6m
  • Postpartum women: 200,000 IU single dose within 8 weeks delivery
  • Pregnant women: NOT high-dose (teratogenic); max 10,000 IU/day or 25,000 IU/week

Treatment (active xerophthalmia):

  • Day 1: 200,000 IU (6–12m: 100,000 IU) oral
  • Day 2: Repeat same dose
  • Day 14: Repeat same dose
  • Keratomalacia: Topical antibiotics (prevent secondary infection) + eye padding; corneal transplant if perforated

TypeDoseFeaturesManagement

**Acute**>100x RDA single dose (>300,000 IU adult)Nausea, vomiting, severe headache, drowsiness, bulging fontanelle (infants), diplopia (VI palsy)Supportive; stop vitamin A; mannitol for ↑ICP
**Chronic**>10x RDA months (>25,000 IU/day)Dry scaly skin, cheilitis, alopecia, hepatomegaly, splenomegaly, hyperostosis (long bones), arthralgia, ↑ICP (PIVS), teratogenicityStop vitamin A; monitor LFT, lipids, bone density; PIVS: acetazolamide, LP

Teratogenicity: Retinoic acid → HOX gene dysregulation → neural crest defects (craniofacial, cardiac, thymic, CNS); Critical window: weeks 3–8 gestation. Isotretinoin (13-cis-RA): Category X; strict contraception (iPledge); avoid pregnancy 1 month before/during/1 month after.

Section 7: β-Carotene & Carotenoids
  • Provitamin A: β-carotene (2 retinal), α-carotene, β-cryptoxanthin (1 retinal each); lycopene/lutein/zeaxanthin = no vitamin A activity.
  • Absorption: Fat-dependent (3–5 g fat/meal); food matrix (cooking ↑bioavailability); BCO1 genetic variation (↓conversion 50% in some).
  • Carotenoderma: Yellow-orange palms/soles, nasolabial folds; sclerae WHITE; harmless; reversible on stopping.
  • CARET/ATBC trials: High-dose β-carotene (20–30 mg/day) + smokers → ↑lung cancer mortality; avoid in smokers/asbestos workers.

Long Answer / Essay (20 min)

Topic: "Vitamin A: metabolism, deficiency manifestations, and management"

  • Forms: retinol, retinal, retinoic acid, β-carotene; absorption, transport (RBP), storage
  • Visual cycle: 11-cis-retinal + opsin → rhodopsin; light → all-trans → hyperpolarisation; regeneration
  • Epithelial differentiation: retinoic acid → RAR/RXR → HOX genes
  • Deficiency: WHO classification (X1A night blindness → X3B keratomalacia → XS blindness); systemic (phrynoderma, growth, immunity)
  • WHO prevention: 100k IU (6–12m), 200k IU (12–59m) q4–6m; treatment day 1, 2, 14
  • Toxicity: acute (nausea, bulging fontanelle), chronic (hepatomegaly, hyperostosis, PIVS), teratogenicity (isotretinoin Cat X, HOX genes, weeks 3–8)
  • β-carotene: carotenoderma (harmless), smokers ↑lung cancer
Short Note (7 min)

Topic: "Xerophthalmia classification and management"

  • X1A: Night blindness (impaired dark adaptation)
  • X1B: Bitot's spots (foamy temporal conjunctiva) + conjunctival xerosis
  • X2: Corneal xerosis (dull, hazy cornea)
  • X3A: Keratomalacia <1/3 cornea; X3B: >1/3 (perforation risk)
  • XS: Corneal scar (blindness)
  • Treatment: 200k IU (100k if 6–12m) Day 1, Day 2, Day 14; topical antibiotics, eye padding for keratomalacia

19 Summary

Vitamin A is a Must Know 🔴 topic for FCPS/MRCP. Key takeaway: Vitamin A (retinol/retinal/retinoic acid/β-carotene) essential for vision (rhodopsin cycle), epithelial integrity (retinoic acid → RAR/RXR → HOX), immunity. Toxicity: chronic (hepatomegaly, hyperostosis, PIVS), teratogenic (isotretinoin Cat X, HOX genes, weeks 3–8). β-carotene: carotenoderma only; smokers ↑lung cancer. Exam focus: Visual cycle, WHO classification/treatment, teratogenicity window, toxicity triad, RAE conversions. Clinical relevance: Measles mortality reduction; VAD prevention in preschool children; isotretinoin counselling; CF fat-soluble vitamin supplementation.
