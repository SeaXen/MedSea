# Adjustment disorder and acute stress reaction

---

## Definition

Adjustment disorder is a maladaptive reaction to an identifiable stressor occurring within 3 months, with symptoms that do not meet criteria for another mental disorder and resolve within 6 months. Acute stress reaction (formerly acute stress disorder in DSM-IV) is a transient disturbance following trauma, lasting hours to days.

## Core Clinical Content

### Adjustment disorder criteria
Symptoms within 3 months of stressor; not meeting criteria for another disorder; resolves within 6 months

### Adjustment disorder subtypes
Depressed mood, anxiety, mixed, disturbance of conduct, mixed disturbance

### Adjustment disorder prevalence
Common; associated with life events (bereavement, divorce, illness, job loss)

### Adjustment disorder treatment
Brief psychotherapy (CBT, supportive); usually self-limiting

### Adjustment disorder vs bereavement
Bereavement: normal grief, not disorder; AD: symptoms disproportionate, impairs function

### Adjustment disorder vs MDD
MDD: meets full criteria; AD: subsyndromal, tied to stressor

### Acute stress reaction (ICD-11)
Transient; days; mixed anxiety-depression; dissociative symptoms

### Acute stress disorder (DSM-5)
≥3 days to 1 month; trauma-related; similar to PTSD but shorter

### Risk of PTSD
Acute stress disorder 50% progress to PTSD

### Treatment
Brief CBT; watchful waiting; trauma-focused therapy if persistent
