# Illness anxiety disorder

---

## Definition

Illness anxiety disorder (formerly hypochondriasis) is characterised by preoccupation with having or acquiring a serious illness, with high health anxiety, despite minimal or no somatic symptoms. Patients have excessive health-related behaviours (seeking reassurance, checking body) or maladaptive avoidance.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
Preoccupation with having/acquiring serious illness; somatic symptoms not present or mild; high anxiety; excessive health-related behaviours; ≥6 months

### Distinguish from SSD
IAD: minimal somatic symptoms but high anxiety; SSD: real distressing symptoms + excessive reaction

### Distinguish from OCD
IAD: focus on having illness; OCD: focus on contamination/harm regardless of health

### Distinguish from somatic delusion
IAD: recognise fear as excessive (insight preserved); somatic delusion: fixed, unshakeable belief

### Prevalence
1-2% of general population

### Comorbidity
Anxiety, depression, OCD, personality disorders

### Behaviour patterns
Reassurance seeking OR avoidance (cancel appointments, avoid news)

### Treatment
CBT (first-line); SSRI; minimise investigations/reassurance-seeking

### Reassurance trap
Brief reassurance helps; extensive reassurance-seeking reinforces anxiety

### Prognosis
Chronic, fluctuating; can improve with treatment
