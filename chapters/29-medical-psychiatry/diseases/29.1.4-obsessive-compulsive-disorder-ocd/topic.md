# Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)

---

## Definition

Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is characterised by obsessions (recurrent, intrusive, distressing thoughts/images) and/or compulsions (repetitive behaviours or mental acts performed to reduce anxiety), causing significant impairment.

## Core Clinical Content

### Obsessions
Recurrent, intrusive, unwanted thoughts/images; recognised as own (ego-dystonic)

### Compulsions
Repetitive behaviours or mental acts; aimed at reducing anxiety

### Common obsessions
Contamination, harm, symmetry, sexual, religious, somatic

### Common compulsions
Washing, checking, counting, ordering, repeating, mental rituals

### Insight
Usually preserved (ego-dystonic)

### Prevalence
Lifetime ~2-3%; equal M:F; onset usually in adolescence/young adulthood

### Comorbidity
Depression (60%), tic disorders, anxiety, OCPD (different condition)

### First-line treatment
SSRI (higher dose than for depression) + CBT (ERP — exposure and response prevention)

### SSRI dose
Often need higher doses (e.g., fluoxetine 60-80 mg) for OCD

### Onset of action
Slower than for depression (8-12 weeks for full effect)
