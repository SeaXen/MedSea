# Depression in medical illness

---

## Definition

Depression comorbid with medical illness is common, often under-recognised, and worsens medical outcomes. It occurs in 20-40% of patients with chronic medical conditions and requires active screening and treatment.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence in medical illness
20-40% in chronic conditions (cancer, stroke, MI, diabetes)

### Post-stroke depression
~30-40%; peaks 3-6 months post-stroke; often missed

### Post-MI depression
~20%; increases mortality and reinfarction risk

### Cancer-related depression
Common in pancreatic, lung, breast; requires active screening

### Parkinson's disease
Depression in 40-50%; may precede motor symptoms

### Hypothyroidism
Common cause of depressive symptoms; check TFTs

### Diagnosis in medical illness
Same criteria; somatic symptoms less specific in medical patients

### Treatment
SSRI first-line; mirtazapine useful with insomnia/appetite loss

### Drug interactions
SSRIs + tramadol (serotonin syndrome), SSRIs + warfarin (bleeding)

### Bereavement-related depression
Distinguish normal grief from MDD; treat if severe/prolonged
