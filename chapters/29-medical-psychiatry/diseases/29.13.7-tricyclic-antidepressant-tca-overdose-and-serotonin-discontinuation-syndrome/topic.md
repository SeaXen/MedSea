# Tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) overdose and serotonin discontinuation syndrome

---

## Definition

TCA overdose is a life-threatening emergency with anticholinergic, cardiovascular, and CNS toxicity. Serotonin discontinuation syndrome is a transient syndrome occurring after abrupt cessation of serotonergic antidepressants, characterised by a constellation of symptoms.

## Core Clinical Content

### TCA toxicity triad
Anticholinergic, cardiovascular (arrhythmia, hypotension), CNS (seizures, coma)

### TCA cardiovascular toxicity
Sodium channel blockade → QRS widening, VT, torsades; hypotension

### TCA overdose treatment
Sodium bicarbonate for QRS >100 ms; IV fluids; activated charcoal if <2 h; supportive

### TCA lethal dose
~15-20 mg/kg (e.g., 1-1.5 g for adult); 30 mg/kg is often fatal

### TCA and serotonin syndrome
TCAs have serotonergic activity; combined with MAOI/SSRI → risk

### Serotonin discontinuation syndrome
Occurs after abrupt stop of SSRI/SNRI; dizziness, nausea, paresthesia, 'brain zaps'

### Discontinuation timing
Short-half-life SSRIs (paroxetine) worse; fluoxetine less (long half-life)

### Discontinuation treatment
Reintroduce antidepressant; taper slowly over weeks-months

### Differentiating discontinuation from relapse
Discontinuation: rapid onset, somatic symptoms, brief; relapse: gradual onset, original symptoms return

### Discontinuation prevention
Always taper antidepressants gradually; longer taper for short half-life drugs
