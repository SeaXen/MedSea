# Phobias (specific, social, agoraphobia)

---

## Definition

Phobias are anxiety disorders characterised by excessive, persistent, irrational fear of a specific object, situation, or activity, leading to avoidance and significant functional impairment.

## Core Clinical Content

### Specific phobia
Fear of specific object/animal/situation (spiders, heights, blood, flying)

### Types of specific phobia
Animal, natural environment, blood-injection-injury, situational, other

### Social anxiety disorder
Fear of social situations where scrutiny is possible; performance vs generalised

### Agoraphobia
Fear of ≥2 of: public transport, open spaces, enclosed places, queues, crowds, going out alone

### Prevalence
Specific phobia 7-9%, social anxiety 7%, agoraphobia 1.7%

### Treatment of specific phobia
Exposure therapy (gold standard); CBT; SSRI/SNRI for severe

### Treatment of social anxiety
CBT (exposure), SSRI (sertraline, paroxetine), SNRI (venlafaxine)

### Beta-blockers
For performance-only social anxiety (propranolol before event)

### Course
Usually chronic if untreated; specific phobias often start in childhood

### Insight
Usually preserved; patient recognises fear as excessive
