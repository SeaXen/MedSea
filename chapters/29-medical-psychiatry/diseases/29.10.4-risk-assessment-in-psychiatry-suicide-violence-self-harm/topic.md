# Risk assessment in psychiatry (suicide, violence, self-harm)

---

## Definition

Psychiatric risk assessment is the systematic evaluation of a patient's risk of suicide, self-harm, violence to others, self-neglect, and vulnerability, used to guide management decisions including hospitalisation and supervision.

## Core Clinical Content

### Suicide risk factors
Previous attempt, mental illness (depression, schizophrenia), substance use, social isolation, chronic pain, terminal illness, access to means

### Suicide protective factors
Social support, family, reasons for living, religious beliefs, treatment engagement

### SAD PERSONS scale
Sex, Age, Depression, Previous attempt, Ethanol, Rationality loss, Social support lacking, Organised plan, No spouse, Sickness

### High-risk features
Active suicidal ideation with plan and means, prior attempt, intent

### Ask directly about suicide
Does NOT increase risk; can decrease; always ask

### Violence risk factors
Past violence, substance use, paranoid ideation, command hallucinations, personality disorder

### HCR-20
Historical, Clinical, Risk Management violence risk assessment tool

### Self-harm
Distinguish from suicide attempt; often relieves tension, no intent to die

### Risk management
Remove means, supervised care, hospitalisation if high risk, crisis plan

### Documentation
Document risk assessment, plan, discussion with patient and family
