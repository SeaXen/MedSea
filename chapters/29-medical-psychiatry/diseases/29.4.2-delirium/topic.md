# Delirium

---

## Definition

Delirium (acute confusional state) is an acute, fluctuating disturbance of attention, awareness, and cognition, usually caused by an underlying medical condition, substance, or medication, with onset over hours to days.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
Disturbance of attention, awareness, cognition; acute onset (hours-days); fluctuation; evidence of physiological cause

### Prevalence in hospital
10-30% of medical inpatients; 50% in ICU; 50% post-hip fracture

### Key features
Acute onset, fluctuating course, inattention, disorganised thinking, altered consciousness

### Hyperactive vs hypoactive
Hyperactive: agitation, hallucinations; Hypoactive: withdrawn, sleepy (more common, often missed)

### Causes (DIMES)
Drugs, Infection, Metabolic, Environmental/Emotional, Structural (stroke, bleed)

### Predisposing factors
Dementia, advanced age, polypharmacy, sensory impairment, frailty

### Investigations
Bloods (FBC, U&E, Ca, glucose, LFT, TSH, B12), sepsis screen, ECG, CXR, CT head if indicated

### Treatment
Treat underlying cause; reorient; avoid restraints; antipsychotic if severely agitated

### Antipsychotic choice
Haloperidol 0.5-1 mg or olanzapine; lowest dose, shortest time

### Prognosis
Associated with increased mortality, prolonged hospital stay, increased dementia risk
