# Adult ADHD and tic disorders

---

## Definition

Adult attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder persisting into adulthood, characterised by inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity. Tic disorders, including Tourette syndrome, are characterised by sudden, rapid, recurrent motor movements or vocalisations.

## Core Clinical Content

### Adult ADHD prevalence
~3-5% of adults; 50% of childhood ADHD persists into adulthood

### ADHD triad
Inattention, hyperactivity, impulsivity; ICD-11 also includes emotional dysregulation

### Adult presentation
Inattention predominates; hyperactivity less prominent; emotional dysregulation

### Comorbidities
Anxiety, depression, substance use, OCD, personality disorders, sleep disorders

### Diagnosis
Clinical; DIVA-5 or ACE+; collateral history important; consider QB tests

### Treatment
Stimulants (methylphenidate, lisdexamfetamine) first-line; atomoxetine (non-stimulant)

### Stimulant side effects
Appetite ↓, weight loss, insomnia, ↑ HR/BP, tics; abuse potential

### Atomoxetine
Non-stimulant; less effective; useful in substance use comorbidity

### Tourette syndrome
Multiple motor + ≥1 vocal tics; >1 year; onset <18; ADHD 50%

### Tourette treatment
Behavioural therapy first; α2 agonists (clonidine, guanfacine); antipsychotics for severe
