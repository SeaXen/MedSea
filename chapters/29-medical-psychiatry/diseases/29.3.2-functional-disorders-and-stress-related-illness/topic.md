# Functional disorders and stress-related illness

---

## Definition

Functional disorders (medically unexplained symptoms) are conditions with distressing symptoms not explained by organic disease, often triggered or exacerbated by stress, with significant functional impact. They are managed with the biopsychosocial model and avoiding excessive investigation.

## Core Clinical Content

### Functional disorders
Symptoms not explained by organic disease; real, not 'faked'

### Common functional disorders
Fibromyalgia, IBS, chronic fatigue syndrome, functional dyspepsia, non-cardiac chest pain

### Fibromyalgia
Widespread musculoskeletal pain, fatigue, sleep disturbance, tender points

### Fibromyalgia treatment
Exercise (most effective), CBT, duloxetine, pregabalin; multidisciplinary

### Chronic fatigue syndrome (CFS/ME)
Severe fatigue >6 months, post-exertional malaise, unrefreshing sleep, cognitive dysfunction

### CFS/ME management
Pacing, activity management, CBT, supportive care; avoid excessive rest

### Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)
Abdominal pain, altered bowel habit; consider as functional if no organic cause

### Functional neurological disorder (FND)
See Conversion disorder (separate file)

### Stress-related illness
Adjustment disorder, acute stress reaction; often self-limiting

### Medically unexplained symptoms (MUS)
20-30% of primary care consultations; avoid dismissive 'all in the head' approach
