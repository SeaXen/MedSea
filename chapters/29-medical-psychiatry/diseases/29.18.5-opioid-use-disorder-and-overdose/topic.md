# Opioid use disorder and overdose

---

## Definition

Opioid use disorder is a chronic relapsing condition characterised by problematic opioid use, with opioid overdose being a life-threatening emergency requiring urgent reversal with naloxone.

## Core Clinical Content

### Common opioids
Heroin, morphine, oxycodone, fentanyl, methadone, codeine, tramadol

### Overdose triad
Respiratory depression, pinpoint pupils, decreased consciousness (NOT miosis alone)

### Other overdose signs
Hypoxia, bradycardia, hypotension, hypothermia

### Naloxone
μ-opioid antagonist; rapid onset (1-2 min); short-acting (30-90 min)

### Repeat naloxone
May need repeated doses as naloxone wears off before opioid

### Naloxone administration
IV/IM/SC/intranasal; start at 0.4-2 mg, titrate to respiratory effort

### Duration monitoring
At least 4-6 hours after last naloxone dose (longer for long-acting opioids)

### Methadone
Long-acting opioid agonist; risk of QTc prolongation; used in maintenance therapy

### Buprenorphine
Partial μ-agonist; ceiling effect (safer); used in maintenance therapy

### Withdrawal
Yawning, lacrimation, piloerection, mydriasis, GI upset, myalgia; not life-threatening
