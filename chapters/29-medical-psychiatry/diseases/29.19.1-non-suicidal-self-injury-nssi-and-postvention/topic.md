# Non-suicidal self-injury (NSSI) and postvention

---

## Definition

Non-suicidal self-injury (NSSI) is intentional self-damage (cutting, burning, hitting) without suicidal intent, often used for emotional regulation. Postvention refers to interventions after a suicide to support affected individuals and prevent further suicides.

## Core Clinical Content

### NSSI definition
Intentional self-damage without suicidal intent; ≥5 days in past year for DSM-5 diagnosis

### NSSI prevalence
17% in adolescents; 5% in adults; more common in females

### Common methods
Cutting, burning, scratching, hitting self

### Functions of NSSI
Affect regulation, self-punishment, anti-dissociation, interpersonal communication

### Risk factors
Childhood trauma, BPD, depression, eating disorders, substance use

### Differential from suicide attempt
NSSI: no intent to die; often for relief; usually superficial

### Differential from BD
NSSI: not suicidal; BD: suicidal; can co-occur

### Management
DBT (first-line for NSSI/BPD), CBT, addressing underlying distress

### Postvention
Support for bereaved after suicide; psychoeducation; reducing contagion

### Papageno effect vs Werther effect
Papageno: protective media (recovery stories); Werther: imitative suicide after media coverage
