# Factitious disorder and malingering

---

## Definition

Factitious disorder is the intentional production of symptoms to assume the sick role (no external gain). Malingering is the intentional production of symptoms for external gain (avoiding work, obtaining drugs, financial compensation). Both differ from somatic symptom disorder (unconscious).

## Core Clinical Content

### Factitious disorder criteria
Falsification of signs/symptoms, presents self as ill, deceptive behaviour, no external incentives

### Munchausen syndrome
Severe chronic form of factitious disorder; wandering, dramatic stories, multiple scars

### Malingering
Intentional production for external gain (work avoidance, drugs, money)

### Malingering — DSM-5
Not a psychiatric diagnosis; listed as 'Other conditions for clinical focus'

### Distinguish from SSD
SSD: unconscious; factitious: conscious (no external gain); malingering: conscious (external gain)

### Factitious disorder imposed on another (formerly Munchausen by proxy)
Caregiver produces illness in child; child protection issue

### Management
Direct but non-confrontational discussion; avoid unnecessary investigations; address underlying needs

### Prognosis
Poor; chronic, recurrent; difficult to treat

### Legal aspects
Factitious disorder imposed on another = criminal offence; mandatory reporting

### Sick role
Primary motivation in factitious disorder (no external gain)
