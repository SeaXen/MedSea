# Bulimia nervosa

---

## Definition

Bulimia nervosa is an eating disorder characterised by recurrent episodes of binge eating followed by compensatory behaviours (vomiting, laxatives, excessive exercise) to prevent weight gain, occurring at least weekly for 3 months, with self-evaluation unduly influenced by body shape/weight.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
Recurrent binge eating + compensatory behaviours ≥1/week × 3 months; self-evaluation unduly influenced by body shape

### Prevalence
1-2% of females; onset late teens/early 20s

### Body weight
Usually normal or slightly above (unlike anorexia)

### Binge eating
Eating large amount in discrete period (2 h) with sense of loss of control

### Compensatory behaviours
Self-induced vomiting, laxatives, diuretics, fasting, excessive exercise

### Physical signs
Dental enamel erosion, parotid enlargement, calluses on knuckles (Russell's sign), electrolyte disturbance

### Hypokalaemia
Common due to vomiting/laxative abuse; risk of arrhythmia

### Differential
Anorexia binge-purge type, binge eating disorder, MDD

### Treatment
CBT (first-line); SSRI (fluoxetine 60 mg); no medication licensed specifically

### Prognosis
Better than anorexia; 70% recover with treatment
