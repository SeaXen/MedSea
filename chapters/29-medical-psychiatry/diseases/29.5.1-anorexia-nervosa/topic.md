# Anorexia nervosa

---

## Definition

Anorexia nervosa is an eating disorder characterised by restriction of energy intake, intense fear of gaining weight, and disturbance in self-perceived body weight/shape, with significantly low body weight. It often begins in adolescence and is more common in females.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
Restriction of energy intake, intense fear of gaining weight, body image disturbance, significantly low weight

### Types
Restricting type (no binge/purge) and binge-eating/purging type

### Prevalence
0.5-1% of females; rare in males; onset 13-20 years

### BMI categories (DSM-5)
Mild ≥17, moderate 16-16.99, severe 15-15.99, extreme <15

### Physical signs
Lanugo, bradycardia, hypotension, hypothermia, amenorrhoea, osteoporosis

### Investigations
FBC, U&E, Ca/Mg/Phos, LFTs, TFTs, ECG, DEXA for bone density

### Hypokalaemia
Common (especially purging type); risk of cardiac arrhythmia

### Refeeding syndrome
Hypophosphataemia, hypokalaemia, hypomagnesaemia, fluid overload with reintroduction of nutrition

### Refeeding prevention
Start slowly (10-20 kcal/kg/day), supplement thiamine, monitor electrolytes

### Treatment
Medical stabilisation + nutritional rehabilitation + psychotherapy (FBT for adolescents, CBT)
