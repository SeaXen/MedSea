# Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)

---

## Definition

Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) develops after exposure to a traumatic event, characterised by re-experiencing, avoidance, negative cognitions/mood, and hyperarousal, lasting >1 month.

## Core Clinical Content

### Trauma criteria
Exposure to actual/threatened death, serious injury, or sexual violence

### DSM-5 symptom clusters
Intrusion (re-experiencing), Avoidance, Negative alterations in cognition/mood, Hyperarousal

### Duration
≥1 month (if <3 months = acute; >3 months = chronic)

### Complex PTSD
PTSD + affect dysregulation, negative self-concept, relationship difficulties

### Risk factors
Severity of trauma, prior trauma, lack of support, female sex

### First-line trauma-focused therapy
TF-CBT, EMDR (eye movement desensitisation and reprocessing)

### First-line medication
SSRI (sertraline, paroxetine, fluoxetine) — FDA approved

### Prazosin
For trauma-related nightmares (off-label)

### Acute stress disorder
Lasts 3 days to 1 month; same symptoms as PTSD; if >1 month → PTSD

### Adjustment disorder
Milder, more common, develops within 3 months of stressor
