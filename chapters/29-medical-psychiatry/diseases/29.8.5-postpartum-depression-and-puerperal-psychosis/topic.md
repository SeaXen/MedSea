# Postpartum depression and puerperal psychosis

---

## Definition

Postpartum depression is a major depressive episode occurring within 4 weeks (DSM-5) or up to 12 months of delivery. Puerperal psychosis is a severe postpartum psychiatric emergency with mania, depression, and psychosis, typically presenting within 2 weeks of delivery.

## Core Clinical Content

### Baby blues
Mild mood lability, tearful, 3-5 days postpartum, resolves by 10-14 days

### Postpartum depression
Major depression within 4 weeks (DSM-5) or up to 12 months; 10-15% of mothers

### Risk factors
Previous postpartum depression, family history, perinatal complications, lack of support

### Puerperal psychosis
Psychiatric emergency; onset within 2 weeks; risk of infanticide

### Puerperal psychosis features
Mania, depression, psychosis, disorganised behaviour, delirium-like features

### Treatment of postpartum depression
SSRI (sertraline, sertraline safe in breastfeeding); CBT; ECT for severe

### Treatment of puerperal psychosis
Urgent admission, antipsychotic, mood stabiliser; mother-baby unit if available

### Breastfeeding considerations
Sertraline preferred (low levels in breast milk); avoid lithium, benzodiazepines

### Suicide risk in postpartum
Suicide is a leading cause of maternal death in first year

### Infanticide risk
Puerperal psychosis with command hallucinations or severe depression; urgent assessment
