# Benzodiazepine and Z-drug use disorder

---

## Definition

Benzodiazepine and Z-drug use disorder is a chronic condition characterised by problematic use of these sedative-hypnotic agents, with significant risk of dependence and withdrawal that can be life-threatening.

## Core Clinical Content

### Mechanism
Benzodiazepines: ↑ GABA-A; Z-drugs: similar but more selective for α1 subunit

### Common drugs
Benzodiazepines (diazepam, lorazepam, temazepam); Z-drugs (zolpidem, zopiclone, zaleplon)

### Indication for long-term use
Limited; only for refractory epilepsy, severe anxiety unresponsive to other treatments

### Tolerance
Develops over weeks to months

### Withdrawal symptoms
Rebound anxiety, insomnia, tremor, autonomic hyperactivity, perceptual disturbance

### Severe withdrawal
Seizures, delirium, similar to alcohol withdrawal

### Withdrawal timing
Short-acting (lorazepam): 1-2 days onset; long-acting (diazepam): 5-7 days onset

### Withdrawal management
Convert to long-acting (diazepam), then taper slowly (5-10% every 1-2 weeks)

### Elderly
Increased sensitivity, falls, cognitive impairment; AVOID long-term

### Overdose
Reversed with flumazenil (cautiously — risk of seizures in dependent users)
