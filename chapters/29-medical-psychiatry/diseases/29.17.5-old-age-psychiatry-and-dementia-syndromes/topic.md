# Old age psychiatry and dementia syndromes

---

## Definition

Old age psychiatry addresses mental health in older adults, including dementia, late-life depression, anxiety, psychosis, and behavioural and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD). Comorbid physical illness and polypharmacy are common considerations.

## Core Clinical Content

### Late-life depression
Common but underdiagnosed; often somatic; may present as pseudodementia

### Pseudodementia
Cognitive impairment from depression; reversible; differs from dementia (depressed says 'I don't know')

### BPSD
Behavioural and psychological symptoms of dementia; non-cognitive symptoms

### BPSD features
Agitation, aggression, psychosis, depression, wandering, sleep disturbance

### BPSD non-pharmacological
Person-centred care, environment, activities, communication strategies

### BPSD pharmacological
Antipsychotic (risperidone, haloperidol) only if severe/aggressive; risk of cerebrovascular events

### Antipsychotic in dementia
↑ Mortality, ↑ CVA risk; only use after non-pharmacological, shortest time, regular review

### Depression in elderly
SSRI first-line; avoid TCAs (anticholinergic, falls); longer time to response

### Sleep in elderly
Reduced need, fragmented; non-pharmacological first; melatonin may help

### Polypharmacy
Common; review regularly; anticholinergic burden affects cognition
