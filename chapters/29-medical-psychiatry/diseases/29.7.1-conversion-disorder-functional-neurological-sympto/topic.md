# Conversion disorder (functional neurological symptom disorder)

---

## Definition

Conversion disorder (functional neurological symptom disorder) is characterised by one or more symptoms of altered voluntary motor or sensory function incompatible with recognised neurological/medical conditions, not better explained by another disorder, causing significant distress or impairment.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
≥1 symptom of altered voluntary motor/sensory function; clinical findings provide evidence of incompatibility; symptom not better explained by another disorder; causes distress/impairment

### Common presentations
Weakness, paralysis, tremor, dystonia, gait disorder, seizures (PNES), blindness, deafness, anaesthesia

### La belle indifférence
Lack of concern about symptoms (NOT specific or required for diagnosis)

### Prevalence
~5 per 100,000; more common in women; onset in young adulthood

### Positive signs
Hoover's sign (hip extension weakness), tremor entrainment, give-way weakness

### Hoover's sign
Hip extension weakness corrects with contralateral hip flexion against resistance

### PNES (psychogenic non-epileptic seizures)
Resemble epilepsy but no EEG changes; prolonged, eyes closed, waxing/waning

### Risk factors
Recent stress, prior trauma, childhood abuse, female sex

### Treatment
Clear positive diagnosis, physiotherapy, CBT, address comorbidities

### Prognosis
Initial improvement in 50%; long-term symptoms persist in many
