# Bipolar disorder type I and II

---

## Definition

Bipolar disorder is a recurrent mood disorder characterised by episodes of mania (type I) or hypomania (type II) alternating with depressive episodes. The lifetime prevalence is ~2-3%.

## Core Clinical Content

### Bipolar I
Mania (≥1 week, hospitalisation may be needed) + depressive episodes

### Bipolar II
Hypomania (≥4 days) + major depression; never full mania

### Mania symptoms (DIG FAST)
Distractibility, Indiscretion, Grandiosity, Flight of ideas, Activity increase, Sleep deficit, Talkativeness

### Mania duration
≥1 week (or any duration if hospitalisation needed)

### Hypomania
Similar to mania but <1 week, no marked functional impairment, no psychosis

### Mixed features
Mania + depression simultaneously; high suicide risk

### First-line for acute mania
Lithium, valproate, or atypical antipsychotic (olanzapine, risperidone, quetiapine)

### Long-term prophylaxis
Lithium (gold standard, also ↓ suicide), valproate, lamotrigine

### Lithium monitoring
TFTs (hypothyroid), U&E (renal), Ca (hyperparathyroidism), levels every 3-6 months

### Antidepressant caution
Can trigger mania; use with mood stabiliser in bipolar

- **'DIG FAST'** for mania: Distractibility, Indiscretion, Grandiosity, Flight of ideas, Activity, Sleep, Talkativeness
- **'ABCDEFGHI'** for MSE: Appearance, Behaviour, Speech, Mood, Affect, Thought form, Thought content, Perception, Cognition, Insight (with Risk)
