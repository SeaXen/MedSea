# Obstructive sleep apnoea and narcolepsy

---

## Definition

Obstructive sleep apnoea (OSA) is a sleep-related breathing disorder characterised by repetitive upper airway collapse during sleep causing apnoeas/hypopnoeas, oxygen desaturation, and sleep fragmentation. Narcolepsy is a central hypersomnolence disorder with excessive daytime sleepiness, cataplexy, sleep paralysis, and hypnagogic hallucinations.

## Core Clinical Content

### OSA definition
Repetitive upper airway collapse during sleep with apnoea/hypopnoea

### OSA risk factors
Obesity, male, age, large neck, alcohol, sedative use, anatomical (enlarged tonsils)

### OSA symptoms
Snoring, witnessed apnoeas, daytime sleepiness, morning headache, poor concentration

### OSA diagnosis
Polysomnography; AHI ≥5 (mild), ≥15 (moderate), ≥30 (severe)

### OSA complications
Hypertension, MI, stroke, accidents, type 2 diabetes, depression

### OSA treatment
CPAP first-line; weight loss; positional therapy; mandibular advancement device; surgery (UPPP)

### Narcolepsy tetrad
Excessive daytime sleepiness, cataplexy, sleep paralysis, hypnagogic hallucinations

### Narcolepsy type 1
With cataplexy (loss of muscle tone with emotion); low hypocretin

### Narcolepsy type 2
Without cataplexy

### Narcolepsy treatment
Modafinil (first-line); sodium oxybate (for cataplexy); modafinil, methylphenidate
