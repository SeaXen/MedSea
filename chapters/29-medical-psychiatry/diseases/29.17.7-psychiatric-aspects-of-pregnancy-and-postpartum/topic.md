# Psychiatric aspects of pregnancy and postpartum

---

## Definition

Perinatal psychiatry addresses mental health conditions occurring during pregnancy (antenatal) and up to one year postpartum (postnatal). Conditions include baby blues, postpartum depression, puerperal psychosis, and pre-existing psychiatric conditions in pregnancy.

## Core Clinical Content

### Antenatal depression
10-15% of pregnancies; often underdiagnosed; risk factors: previous depression, lack of support

### Baby blues
50-80%; 3-5 days postpartum; mild; resolves by 10-14 days

### Postpartum depression
10-15%; within 4 weeks (DSM-5) or up to 12 months; same criteria as MDD

### Puerperal psychosis
1-2 per 1000; psychiatric emergency; within 2 weeks; high suicide/infanticide risk

### Treatment in pregnancy
SSRI (sertraline preferred); avoid paroxetine (cardiac malformations); ECT if severe

### Treatment in breastfeeding
Sertraline preferred (lowest infant levels); avoid lithium, benzodiazepines, lamotrigine

### Antidepressant in pregnancy
Risks: persistent pulmonary hypertension of newborn, neonatal adaptation syndrome

### Valproate in pregnancy
HIGHLY teratogenic (neural tube defects); AVOID in women of childbearing age

### Lithium in pregnancy
Ebstein anomaly (cardiac malformation); avoid in 1st trimester; use cautiously

### Perinatal mental health team
Joint obstetric-psychiatric care; mother-baby unit if needed
