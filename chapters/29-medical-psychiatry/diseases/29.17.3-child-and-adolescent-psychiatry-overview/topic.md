# Child and adolescent psychiatry overview

---

## Definition

Child and adolescent psychiatry addresses mental health conditions in children and adolescents, with disorders often presenting differently from adults and requiring developmentally appropriate assessment and treatment.

## Core Clinical Content

### ADHD
Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder; 5% children; inattentive/hyperactive/combined; stimulants first-line

### Autism spectrum disorder (ASD)
Persistent deficits in social communication + restricted/repetitive behaviours; early onset

### Conduct disorder
Repetitive violation of others' rights/age-appropriate norms; <18 years; precursor to ASPD

### Oppositional defiant disorder (ODD)
Angry/irritable mood, argumentative, vindictive behaviour; less severe than CD

### Tourette syndrome
Multiple motor + ≥1 vocal tics; >1 year; onset <18; ADHD comorbidity 50%

### Specific learning disorder
Persistent difficulties in reading (dyslexia), writing, mathematics

### Enuresis
Involuntary urination ≥2/week in ≥5-year-old; primary vs secondary; enuresis alarm first-line

### Encopresis
Faecal incontinence; usually functional; consider abuse if secondary

### Adolescent depression
Often masked (irritability, somatic); ↑ suicide risk; SSRIs (fluoxetine approved)

### Eating disorders in adolescents
Family-based treatment (FBT) first-line; medical stabilisation priority
