# Alcohol use disorder

---

## Definition

Alcohol use disorder (AUD) is a chronic, relapsing condition characterised by problematic alcohol use causing significant impairment or distress, with ≥2 of 11 DSM-5 criteria over 12 months.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
≥2 of 11 in 12 months (tolerance, withdrawal, larger amounts, unsuccessful cut-down, time spent, role impairment, social impairment, hazardous use, use despite problems, craving)

### Screening tools
AUDIT (10-item), CAGE (4-item)

### CAGE
Cut down, Annoyed, Guilty, Eye-opener

### UK alcohol limits
≤14 units/week for both M and F (revised 2016)

### Complications
Hepatic, neurological (Wernicke, Korsakoff), cardiac, pancreatitis, malignancy

### Wernicke's encephalopathy
Triad: confusion, ataxia, ophthalmoplegia

### Korsakoff syndrome
Anterograde amnesia, confabulation, irreversible

### Treatment of dependence
Detoxification, relapse prevention (naltrexone, acamprosate, disulfiram)

### Naltrexone
μ-opioid antagonist; reduces craving; monitor LFTs

### Acamprosate
NMDA antagonist; reduces craving; safe in liver disease
