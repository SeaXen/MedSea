# Bereavement, grief, and adjustment disorders

---

## Definition

Bereavement is the loss of a loved one through death. Grief is the normal emotional response to loss. Adjustment disorder is a maladaptive response to an identifiable stressor occurring within 3 months, with symptoms that resolve within 6 months of stressor removal.

## Core Clinical Content

### Normal grief stages (Kübler-Ross)
Denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance (note: not linear)

### Worden's tasks of mourning
Accept reality, work through pain, adjust to life without deceased, find lasting connection

### Anticipatory grief
Before the loss; common in terminal illness

### Prolonged grief disorder (PGD)
Grief >12 months; yearning for deceased; functional impairment; new DSM-5 diagnosis

### Complicated grief
Severe, prolonged grief; may need intervention

### Bereavement vs MDD
MDD: pervasive depression, anhedonia, suicidal ideation, low self-esteem; grief: waves, focused on deceased

### Adjustment disorder subtypes
Depressed mood, anxiety, mixed, disturbance of conduct

### Adjustment disorder criteria
Symptoms within 3 months of stressor; not meeting criteria for another disorder; resolves within 6 months of stressor removal

### Treatment of complicated grief
CBT, complicated grief therapy, support groups; avoid routine antidepressants

### Pharmacological considerations
AVOID benzodiazepines; short-term hypnotics for sleep; SSRI for comorbid depression
