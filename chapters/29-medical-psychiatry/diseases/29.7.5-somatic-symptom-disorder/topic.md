# Somatic symptom disorder

---

## Definition

Somatic symptom disorder (formerly somatisation disorder / somatoform disorder) is characterised by one or more distressing somatic symptoms with excessive thoughts, feelings, or behaviours related to the symptoms, persisting >6 months.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
≥1 distressing somatic symptom; excessive thoughts/feelings/behaviours; ≥6 months

### Prevalence
5-7% of general population; more common in women; often presents in primary care

### Common symptoms
Pain, fatigue, GI, neurological, sexual — often multiple

### Key feature
Distress disproportionate to objective findings; NOT a 'malingered' condition

### Distinguish from factitious/malingering
SSD: genuine symptoms, not consciously produced; factitious: conscious production; malingering: conscious production for external gain

### Comorbidity
Depression, anxiety, other medical conditions (sometimes)

### Management principles
Validate symptoms; regular short appointments; avoid unnecessary investigations

### Treatment
CBT (first-line); SSRI for comorbid depression; address health anxiety

### Prognosis
Chronic, fluctuating; early treatment improves outcome

### Avoid
Dismissing as 'all in the head'; excessive investigations reinforce illness behaviour
