# Anxiolytics and hypnotics (benzodiazepines, Z-drugs, buspirone)

---

## Definition

Anxiolytics and hypnotics are medications used to treat anxiety and insomnia. The main classes are benzodiazepines, Z-drugs (non-benzodiazepine hypnotics), buspirone, and beta-blockers (for performance anxiety).

## Core Clinical Content

### Benzodiazepine mechanism
↑ GABA-A receptor activity (positive allosteric modulator)

### Benzodiazepine examples
Diazepam (long-acting), lorazepam (intermediate), temazepam (short-acting)

### Benzodiazepine uses
Anxiety, insomnia, alcohol withdrawal, status epilepticus, procedural sedation

### Benzodiazepine side effects
Sedation, dependence, tolerance, falls, respiratory depression (with opioids)

### Benzodiazepine dependence
Develops within weeks; withdrawal can be life-threatening (seizures)

### Benzodiazepine withdrawal
Taper slowly (5-10% every 1-2 weeks); convert to long-acting (diazepam) first

### Z-drugs
Zolpidem, zopiclone, zaleplon; non-benzodiazepine hypnotics; same risks

### Buspirone
5-HT1A partial agonist; anxiolytic; non-sedating; no dependence; takes 2-4 weeks

### Pregabalin
α2δ ligand; reduces glutamate; anxiolytic (GAD), neuropathic pain, epilepsy

### Beta-blockers (propranolol)
For performance anxiety (blocks peripheral symptoms); not for GAD
