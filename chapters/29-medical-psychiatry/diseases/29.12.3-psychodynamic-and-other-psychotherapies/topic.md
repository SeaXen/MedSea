# Psychodynamic and other psychotherapies

---

## Definition

Psychodynamic psychotherapy explores unconscious conflicts, early life experiences, and the therapeutic relationship to promote insight and change. Other psychotherapies include interpersonal therapy (IPT), family therapy, and group therapy.

## Core Clinical Content

### Psychodynamic therapy
Explores unconscious conflicts, transference, defence mechanisms; longer-term

### Defence mechanisms
Denial, projection, displacement, sublimation, rationalisation, reaction formation, repression

### Transference
Patient's feelings about therapist (from past relationships)

### Countertransference
Therapist's emotional response to patient

### Psychodynamic therapy uses
Personality disorders, chronic depression, anxiety, eating disorders

### Interpersonal therapy (IPT)
Time-limited (12-16 sessions); focuses on interpersonal problems; for depression

### IPT focus areas
Grief, role disputes, role transitions, interpersonal deficits

### Family therapy
Treats family as a system; for schizophrenia, eating disorders, family conflict

### Group therapy
Therapeutic factors: universality, altruism, instillation of hope, catharsis

### Supportive psychotherapy
Active listening, empathy, reassurance; for chronic conditions, crisis
