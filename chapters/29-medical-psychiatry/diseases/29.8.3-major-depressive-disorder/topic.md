# Major depressive disorder

---

## Definition

Major depressive disorder (MDD) is a mood disorder characterised by persistent low mood and/or anhedonia for at least 2 weeks, with associated cognitive, behavioural, and somatic symptoms causing significant functional impairment.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria (SIG E CAPS)
Sleep, Interest loss, Guilt, Energy loss, Concentration, Appetite change, Psychomotor changes, Suicidal ideation + depressed mood or anhedonia ≥2 weeks

### At least 5 of 9 symptoms
Must include either depressed mood OR anhedonia

### Duration
≥2 weeks (usually much longer)

### Prevalence
Lifetime ~15-20%; 2x more common in women

### Risk factors
Previous episode, family history, chronic illness, substance use, stress, loss

### Suicide risk
~15% of severe MDD; highest in first month of treatment

### First-line treatment
SSRI or SNRI + CBT or IPT (combination > monotherapy)

### Duration of treatment
Continue for 6-12 months after remission (first episode); longer for recurrent

### ECT
For severe, treatment-resistant, psychotic, or life-threatening depression

### Antidepressant onset
2-4 weeks for full effect; assess at 4-6 weeks
