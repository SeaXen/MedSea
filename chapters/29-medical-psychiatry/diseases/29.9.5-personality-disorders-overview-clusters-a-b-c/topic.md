# Personality disorders overview (clusters A, B, C)

---

## Definition

Personality disorders are enduring, inflexible, maladaptive patterns of inner experience and behaviour that deviate from cultural expectations, causing distress or impairment. They are classified into three clusters: A (odd/eccentric), B (dramatic/erratic), and C (anxious/fearful).

## Core Clinical Content

### Cluster A — odd/eccentric
Paranoid, Schizoid, Schizotypal

### Cluster B — dramatic/erratic
Antisocial, Borderline, Histrionic, Narcissistic

### Cluster C — anxious/fearful
Avoidant, Dependent, Obsessive-compulsive

### General criteria
Enduring pattern, deviates from cultural norms, pervasive, inflexibility, onset by adolescence/adulthood

### Prevalence
10-13% of general population; cluster C most common

### Comorbidity
High with mood, anxiety, substance use, eating disorders

### Treatment
Long-term psychotherapy (DBT for BPD, mentalisation-based, schema therapy); medication for comorbidities

### Prognosis
Tends to improve with age; some evidence of neuroplasticity

### Personality change
Distinguish from personality disorder (temporary) vs PD (enduring)

### Cluster A vs schizophrenia
PD: eccentric but reality testing preserved; schizophrenia: frank psychosis
