# Capacity assessment

---

## Definition

Capacity (or competence) is the ability to understand, retain, weigh, and communicate a decision about a specific medical intervention at a particular time. It is decision-specific and time-specific.

## Core Clinical Content

### Four criteria of capacity (UK MCA 2005)
Understand, Retain, Weigh, Communicate

### Capacity is decision-specific
Can have capacity for one decision but not another

### Capacity is time-specific
Can fluctuate; review when condition changes

### Presumption of capacity
Adults presumed to have capacity unless assessed otherwise

### Who assesses capacity?
Treating clinician (not a psychiatrist necessarily)

### Two-stage test
(1) Diagnostic — does the person have impairment of mind/brain? (2) Functional — does the impairment affect capacity for this decision?

### Common scenarios
Refusal of treatment, advance decisions, research participation, financial decisions

### If lacks capacity
Best interests decision, advance decision, IMCA, court of protection

### Communication aids
Use interpreters, sign language, simple language, visual aids

### Adults with capacity can make unwise decisions
Capacity ≠ 'right' decision
