# Delusional disorder

---

## Definition

Delusional disorder is a psychiatric condition characterised by one or more non-bizarre delusions (involving real-life situations that could occur in real life, e.g., persecution, infidelity, illness) lasting ≥1 month, with functioning relatively preserved outside the delusional theme.

## Core Clinical Content

### Key feature
Non-bizarre delusions (could occur in real life)

### Duration
≥1 month

### Types
Persecutory, jealous (Othello), erotomanic (de Clerambault), somatic, grandiose, mixed

### Erotomanic
False belief that someone (usually higher status) is in love with them

### Othello syndrome
Pathological jealousy; belief partner is unfaithful; can lead to violence

### Somatic
Belief of having a disease (e.g., parasites, cancer); may seek multiple opinions

### Functioning
Relatively preserved outside delusional theme

### Behaviour
Generally normal except for actions based on delusion

### Treatment
Antipsychotic (poor response); psychotherapy; management of behaviour

### Prognosis
Often chronic; insight often limited
