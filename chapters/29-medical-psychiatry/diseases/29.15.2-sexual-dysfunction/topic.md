# Sexual dysfunction

---

## Definition

Sexual dysfunctions are a group of disorders characterised by clinically significant disturbance in sexual response or experience, including desire, arousal, orgasm, and pain disorders, causing marked distress.

## Core Clinical Content

### Types
Desire (low), arousal (erectile, female), orgasm (premature, delayed, anorgasmia), pain (dyspareunia, vaginismus)

### Erectile dysfunction (ED)
Inability to achieve/maintain erection; organic vs psychogenic causes

### ED organic causes
Vascular (atherosclerosis), diabetes, hormonal, neurological, medications, alcohol

### ED psychogenic causes
Anxiety (performance), depression, relationship problems, stress

### ED investigations
Testosterone, prolactin, glucose, lipids, TSH; nocturnal penile tumescence test

### ED treatment
PDE5 inhibitors (sildenafil, tadalafil) first-line; vacuum device; intracavernosal injection; implant

### Premature ejaculation
Ejaculation sooner than desired; behavioural therapy, dapoxetine (SSRI)

### Vaginismus
Involuntary vaginal muscle contraction preventing penetration; pelvic floor therapy, CBT

### Anorgasmia in women
Often psychological; ensure adequate stimulation, address anxiety

### Sexual dysfunction in psychiatric illness
Common in depression, anxiety, PTSD; screen routinely
