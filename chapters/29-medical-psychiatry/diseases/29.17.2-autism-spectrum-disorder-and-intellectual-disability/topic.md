# Autism spectrum disorder and intellectual disability

---

## Definition

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterised by persistent deficits in social communication and interaction plus restricted, repetitive patterns of behaviour, interests, or activities. Intellectual disability (ID) is characterised by significant limitations in intellectual functioning and adaptive behaviour, with onset in the developmental period.

## Core Clinical Content

### ASD prevalence
~1-2% of children; 4:1 male:female ratio

### ASD triad (DSM-5)
Persistent deficits in: (1) social communication/interaction, (2) restricted/repetitive behaviours, (3) early onset

### ASD onset
Recognisable in early childhood; some regression in language/social skills

### ASD severity levels
Level 1: requiring support; Level 2: substantial support; Level 3: very substantial support

### ASD comorbidities
Intellectual disability (~30%), ADHD, anxiety, depression, OCD, tics, epilepsy

### ASD causes
Mostly genetic; specific genes (SHANK3, CHD8, FMR1); environmental (advanced parental age)

### ASD management
Early intervention; ABA (applied behaviour analysis); speech therapy; OT; school support; SSRI for OCD/anxiety

### ASD pharmacotherapy
No curative medication; antipsychotic (risperidone, aripiprazole) for irritability; melatonin for sleep

### Intellectual disability (ID)
IQ <70; deficits in adaptive functioning; onset <18 years

### ID severity
Mild (IQ 50-69), moderate (35-49), severe (20-34), profound (<20)

**Q3. How is Autism spectrum disorder and intellectual disability diagnosed?**
> Based on DSM-5/ICD-11 criteria, clinical assessment, and appropriate investigations.

> Combination of pharmacological and psychological interventions per evidence-based guidelines.
