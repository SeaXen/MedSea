# Frontotemporal dementia and other dementias

---

## Definition

Frontotemporal dementia (FTD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder primarily affecting the frontal and temporal lobes, with early personality, behaviour, or language changes (rather than memory), often with onset in middle age (45-65 years).

## Core Clinical Content

### FTD prevalence
5-10% of all dementia; common cause of young-onset dementia (<65)

### FTD subtypes
Behavioural variant (bvFTD) and primary progressive aphasia (PPA)

### Behavioural variant (bvFTD)
Early disinhibition, apathy, loss of empathy, compulsive behaviours, hyperorality

### PPA subtypes
Non-fluent (grammar), semantic (word meaning), logopenic (word retrieval)

### Pick disease
FTD with Pick bodies (tau-positive intraneuronal inclusions)

### Pathology
Tau or TDP-43 inclusions; frontotemporal atrophy on imaging

### Imaging
MRI: focal frontal and/or temporal atrophy; 'knife-edge' gyri

### Genetics
30% familial; MAPT, GRN, C9ORF72 mutations

### Treatment
No disease-modifying therapy; SSRI for behavioural symptoms; AVOID antipsychotics

### Prognosis
Progressive; 6-11 years from diagnosis; younger onset often more rapid
