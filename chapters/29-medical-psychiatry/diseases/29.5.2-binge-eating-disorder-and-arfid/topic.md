# Binge eating disorder and ARFID

---

## Definition

Binge eating disorder (BED) is an eating disorder characterised by recurrent binge eating episodes WITHOUT compensatory behaviours, causing marked distress. ARFID (avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder) is avoidance of food without weight/shape concerns, often in children.

## Core Clinical Content

### BED criteria
Recurrent binge eating (≥1/week × 3 months) with distress, NO compensatory behaviours

### BED prevalence
Most common eating disorder (~2-3%); affects all genders; associated with obesity

### BED features
Eating rapidly, eating when not hungry, eating alone, feeling guilty after

### BED vs bulimia
BED: no compensatory behaviours; bulimia: recurrent purging/fasting/exercise

### BED treatment
CBT (first-line), interpersonal therapy; lisdexamfetamine (FDA approved)

### ARFID
Avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder; food avoidance WITHOUT body image concerns

### ARFID features
Sensory sensitivity, fear of choking/vomiting, lack of interest in eating

### ARFID demographics
Children/adolescents; equal M:F; associated with autism, anxiety

### ARFID complications
Weight loss, nutritional deficiencies, dependence on supplements

### ARFID treatment
Family-based, CBT-AR, exposure therapy; medical/nutritional support
