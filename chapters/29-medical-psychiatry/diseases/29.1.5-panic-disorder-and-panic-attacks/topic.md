# Panic disorder and panic attacks

---

## Definition

Panic disorder is characterised by recurrent, unexpected panic attacks (discrete episodes of intense fear with physical/cognitive symptoms) followed by persistent concern about further attacks, leading to behavioural change (agoraphobia).

## Core Clinical Content

### Panic attack
Discrete period of intense fear, ≥4 of 13 symptoms, peaks within minutes

### Panic attack symptoms
Palpitations, sweating, trembling, dyspnoea, choking, chest pain, nausea, dizziness, derealisation, paraesthesia, chills, fear of dying/losing control

### Duration
Typically 5-30 min, peaks within 10 min

### Panic disorder
Recurrent unexpected attacks + persistent concern (≥1 month)

### Agoraphobia
Fear of places where escape might be difficult; often develops with panic

### Differential
MI, PE, hyperthyroidism, arrhythmias, phaeochromocytoma

### First-line treatment
SSRI + CBT (exposure therapy)

### Acute treatment
Benzodiazepine (short-acting) for acute attack

### Hyperventilation
Often present; breathing exercises can help

### Prognosis
Chronic, fluctuating; good response to treatment; 50-70% recover with treatment
