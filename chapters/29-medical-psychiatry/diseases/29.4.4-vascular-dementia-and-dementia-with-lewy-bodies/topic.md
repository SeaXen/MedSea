# Vascular dementia and dementia with Lewy bodies

---

## Definition

Vascular dementia (VaD) is cognitive decline due to cerebrovascular disease (multi-infarct, strategic infarct, small vessel disease). Dementia with Lewy bodies (DLB) is a synucleinopathy with fluctuating cognition, visual hallucinations, and parkinsonism.

## Core Clinical Content

### Vascular dementia — second most common
15-25% of dementia; preventable via vascular risk factor control

### VaD features
Stepwise decline, focal neurological signs, early executive dysfunction, gait disturbance

### VaD risk factors
Hypertension, diabetes, smoking, hyperlipidaemia, atrial fibrillation

### VaD prevention
BP control, antiplatelet, statin, lifestyle modifications

### DLB prevalence
10-15% of dementia; male predominance

### DLB core features
Fluctuating cognition, visual hallucinations, parkinsonism, REM sleep behaviour disorder

### DLB supportive features
Severe neuroleptic sensitivity, autonomic dysfunction, hyposmia, depression

### DLB vs Parkinson's dementia
DLB: dementia within 1 year of parkinsonism; PDD: parkinsonism precedes dementia by >1 year

### DLB treatment
Cholinesterase inhibitors (rivastigmine); AVOID antipsychotics (severe sensitivity)

### Prognosis
VaD: variable, stepwise; DLB: progressive, falls, fluctuating course
