# Alzheimer's disease and other dementias

---

## Definition

Alzheimer's disease (AD) is the most common form of dementia (60-70%), characterised by progressive memory impairment, other cognitive deficits, and behavioural changes, with characteristic neuropathology of amyloid plaques and neurofibrillary tangles.

## Core Clinical Content

### Dementia definition
Acquired cognitive decline in ≥2 domains severe enough to impair function

### Alzheimer's prevalence
60-70% of all dementia; 10% of >65 years; 30% of >85 years

### Pathology
Amyloid (Aβ) plaques + neurofibrillary tangles (hyperphosphorylated tau) + cortical atrophy

### Risk factors
Age, APOE4, family history, low education, cardiovascular risk factors

### Early symptoms
Episodic memory loss (recent events), disorientation to time, word-finding difficulty

### Late symptoms
Personality change, behavioural disturbance, motor symptoms, incontinence

### Investigations
MMSE/MoCA, bloods to exclude reversible causes, CT/MRI (atrophy), FDG-PET or CSF biomarkers

### Treatment
Cholinesterase inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine); memantine for moderate-severe

### Memantine
NMDA antagonist; for moderate-severe AD; monotherapy or add-on

### Prognosis
Progressive; 3-10 years from diagnosis; common cause of death
