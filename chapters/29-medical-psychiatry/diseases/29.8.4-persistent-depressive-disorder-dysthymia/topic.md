# Persistent depressive disorder (dysthymia)

---

## Definition

Persistent depressive disorder (formerly dysthymia) is a chronic form of depression with depressed mood for most of the day, more days than not, for at least 2 years (1 year in children/adolescents).

## Core Clinical Content

### Duration
≥2 years (1 year in children/adolescents)

### Symptoms
Same as MDD but fewer symptoms (≥2 of 6: poor appetite, sleep disturbance, low energy, low self-esteem, poor concentration, hopelessness)

### Course
Chronic, often lifelong; less acute episodes than MDD

### Prevalence
2-3% of adults; 1.5-3x more common in women

### Comorbidity
High comorbidity with anxiety, substance use, personality disorders

### Differential
MDD (episodic), cyclothymia, depression secondary to medical condition

### Treatment
Same as MDD: SSRI/SNRI + psychotherapy; longer-term maintenance

### Response rate
Lower than MDD; chronic course limits response

### Risk of MDD
May develop superimposed MDD ('double depression')

### Function
Significant functional impairment despite mild symptoms
