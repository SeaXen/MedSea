# Cluster C personality disorders (avoidant, dependent, OCPD)

---

## Definition

Cluster C personality disorders (avoidant, dependent, obsessive-compulsive) are characterised by anxiety, fear, and avoidance. They are the most common personality disorders and often present in general medical settings.

## Core Clinical Content

### Cluster C
Anxious/fearful; 'worried/wet'

### Avoidant PD
Social inhibition, feelings of inadequacy, hypersensitivity to criticism; wants but fears relationships

### Avoidant vs schizoid
Avoidant: wants relationships; schizoid: doesn't want them

### Avoidant vs social anxiety
PD: pervasive, ego-syntonic lifelong; SAD: episodic, ego-dystonic

### Dependent PD
Pervasive need to be taken care of; submissive, clinging; fear of separation

### Dependent PD risk
Higher risk of depression, abuse, somatic symptoms

### Dependent vs dependent traits
PD: pervasive, rigid, maladaptive; traits: situational

### OCPD
Preoccupation with order, perfectionism, control; ego-syntonic

### OCPD vs OCD
OCPD: personality (egosyntonic); OCD: ego-dystonic obsessions/compulsions; often comorbid

### Cluster C treatment
Psychotherapy (CBT, schema therapy, supportive); SSRIs for anxiety/depression; longer-term
