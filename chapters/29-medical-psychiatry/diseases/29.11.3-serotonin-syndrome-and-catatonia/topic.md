# Serotonin syndrome and catatonia

---

## Definition

Serotonin syndrome is a potentially life-threatening condition caused by excess serotonergic activity, characterised by cognitive, autonomic, and somatic features. Catatonia is a neuropsychiatric syndrome of psychomotor dysregulation with multiple subtypes.

## Core Clinical Content

### Serotonin syndrome triad
Cognitive (agitation, confusion), autonomic (fever, tachycardia, hypertension), somatic (clonus, hyperreflexia, tremor)

### Cause
Serotonergic drugs (SSRIs + MAOIs, tramadol, linezolid, St John's Wort, triptans)

### Onset
Within hours of serotonergic drug change

### Clonus
Inducible, spontaneous, ocular — hallmark of serotonin syndrome

### Treatment
STOP serotonergic drugs; supportive care; cyproheptadine (5-HT2A antagonist)

### Cyproheptadine
5-HT2A antagonist; for moderate-severe cases

### Catatonia features
Mutism, stupor, negativism, waxy flexibility, echolalia, echopraxia, posturing

### Catatonia subtypes
Retarded (withdrawn), excited, malignant (with autonomic instability)

### Catatonia causes
Mood disorders (esp. bipolar), schizophrenia, medical, drug-induced

### Lorazepam challenge
1-2 mg IV/IM lorazepam; rapid response confirms catatonia; ECT for refractory
