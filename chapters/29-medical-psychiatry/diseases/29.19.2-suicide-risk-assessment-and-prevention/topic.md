# Suicide risk assessment and prevention

---

## Definition

Suicide is the act of intentionally ending one's own life. Suicide risk assessment is a structured evaluation of factors that increase or decrease suicide risk, used to guide safety planning and intervention. Self-harm (non-suicidal self-injury, NSSI) is intentional self-damage without suicidal intent.

## Core Clinical Content

### Suicide epidemiology
~700,000 deaths/year worldwide; 1 every 40 seconds; leading cause of death in young adults

### Risk factors (SAD PERSONS)
Sex (M), Age (extremes), Depression, Previous attempt, Ethanol, Rationality loss, Social support lacking, Organised plan, No spouse, Sickness

### Strongest risk factor
Previous suicide attempt (40x increased risk)

### Protective factors
Family support, religious beliefs, engagement with treatment, reasons for living, access to care

### Warning signs
Talking about wanting to die, looking for means, withdrawal, mood changes, giving possessions

### Direct questioning
Asking about suicide does NOT increase risk; can decrease it

### Suicide plan
Specificity of plan, availability of means, timeframe all increase risk

### Self-harm (NSSI)
Intentional self-injury without suicidal intent; often for emotional regulation

### Treatment
Safety plan, remove means, CBT-DBT, medications, hospitalisation if high risk

### Means restriction
Effective prevention strategy (e.g., bridge barriers, medication blister packs)
