# Insomnia disorder

---

## Definition

Insomnia disorder is a sleep-wake disorder characterised by dissatisfaction with sleep quantity or quality, with difficulty initiating sleep, maintaining sleep, or early morning awakening, causing significant daytime impairment, occurring ≥3 nights/week for ≥3 months.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
Dissatisfaction with sleep quantity/quality; ≥3 nights/week for ≥3 months; daytime impairment; not better explained by another condition

### Types
Sleep-onset, sleep-maintenance, early-morning awakening, mixed

### Prevalence
Common (~30% adults have symptoms; 10% chronic)

### Causes
Stress, medical conditions, psychiatric disorders, medications, substance use, poor sleep hygiene

### 3P model
Predisposing, Precipitating, Perpetuating factors

### Sleep hygiene
Regular schedule, dark/cool room, no caffeine/alcohol/exercise before bed, no screens

### CBT-I
Cognitive behavioural therapy for insomnia; first-line; lasting benefit

### CBT-I components
Stimulus control, sleep restriction, relaxation, cognitive therapy, sleep hygiene

### Pharmacotherapy
Short-term only: Z-drugs (zolpidem, zopiclone) or melatonin; AVOID long-term benzodiazepines

### Dependence risk
High with benzodiazepines and Z-drugs; reserved for short-term use
