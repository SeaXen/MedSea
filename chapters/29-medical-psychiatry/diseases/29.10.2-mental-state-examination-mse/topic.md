# Mental state examination (MSE)

---

## Definition

The mental state examination (MSE) is a structured assessment of a patient's current mental state, organised by Appearance, Behaviour, Speech, Mood, Affect, Thought, Perception, Cognition, Insight, and Risk.

## Core Clinical Content

### MSE components
Appearance, Behaviour, Speech, Mood, Affect, Thought form/content, Perception, Cognition, Insight, Risk

### Appearance
Dress, grooming, hygiene, apparent age, distinguishing features

### Behaviour
Eye contact, rapport, psychomotor activity (retardation, agitation), abnormal movements

### Speech
Rate, rhythm, volume, quantity, spontaneity, latency, abnormalities (pressure, mute)

### Mood
Subjective emotional state (euthymic, depressed, elevated, anxious, irritable)

### Affect
Observed emotional expression (broad, restricted, blunted, flat, labile, congruent)

### Thought form
Linear, circumstantial, tangential, flight of ideas, loosening of associations, neologisms

### Thought content
Preoccupations, obsessions, phobias, delusions, suicidal/homicidal ideation

### Perception
Illusions, hallucinations (auditory, visual, olfactory, gustatory, tactile)

### Cognition
Orientation, attention, concentration, memory (immediate, recent, remote)

### Insight
Patient's understanding of their condition; levels 0-3 (none, partial, full)
