# Chronic pain and medically unexplained pain

---

## Definition

Chronic pain is pain persisting >3 months, often with limited response to standard treatments. It involves complex interactions between physical, psychological, and social factors (biopsychosocial model). Medically unexplained pain (somatic symptom disorder with predominant pain) overlaps with this.

## Core Clinical Content

### Chronic pain definition
Pain persisting >3 months; >normal healing time

### Biopsychosocial model
Pain = interaction of biological, psychological (anxiety, depression, catastrophising), social factors

### Yellow flags
Psychosocial risk factors for chronicity: beliefs, emotions, behaviours, social context

### Depression in chronic pain
30-50% comorbidity; bidirectional relationship; under-recognised

### Catastrophising
Negative cognitive style ('this is terrible, will never end'); predicts pain outcomes

### Opioid use
Limited benefit beyond 4-6 weeks; high risk dependence; AVOID long-term

### Gabapentinoids
Gabapentin, pregabalin; for neuropathic pain; risk of dependence, sedation

### Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs)
Amitriptyline, nortriptyline; for neuropathic pain, chronic headache; start low

### SNRIs
Duloxetine; for neuropathic pain (diabetic), fibromyalgia, chronic musculoskeletal pain

### Multidisciplinary pain management
Combination of medication, physiotherapy, CBT, occupational therapy, self-management
