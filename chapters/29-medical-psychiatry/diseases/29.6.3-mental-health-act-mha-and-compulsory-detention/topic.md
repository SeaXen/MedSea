# Mental Health Act (MHA) and compulsory detention

---

## Definition

The Mental Health Act (MHA) 1983 (amended 2007) provides the legal framework for the assessment, treatment, and detention of people with mental disorders in England and Wales. It allows compulsory treatment for patients who lack capacity or pose a risk to themselves or others.

## Core Clinical Content

### MHA criteria for detention
Mental disorder of nature/degree warranting detention; appropriate treatment available; risk to self/others

### Section 2 (MHA)
Admission for assessment; up to 28 days; AMHP + 2 doctors (one Section 12 approved)

### Section 3 (MHA)
Admission for treatment; up to 6 months (renewable)

### Section 4 (MHA)
Emergency admission; up to 72 hours; 1 doctor + AMHP (when Section 12 doctor not available)

### Section 5(2) (MHA)
Doctor's holding power; up to 72 hours for already admitted patients

### Section 135 (MHA)
Warrant to enter premises and remove person to place of safety

### Section 136 (MHA)
Police power to remove person from public place to place of safety; up to 24 h

### Nearest Relative
Defined by Act; can be displaced by court; has specific rights (request assessment, discharge)

### Mental Health Act Tribunals
Patients detained under Section 2/3 can apply for tribunal hearing

### Consent to treatment (Part IV)
Section 57 (ECT, neurosurgery) requires patient + SOAD consent; Section 58 (3 months) for detained patients
