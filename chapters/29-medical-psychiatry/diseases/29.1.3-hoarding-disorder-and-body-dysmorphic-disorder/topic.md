# Hoarding disorder and body dysmorphic disorder

---

## Definition

Hoarding disorder is characterised by persistent difficulty discarding or parting with possessions regardless of value, due to perceived need to save and distress associated with discarding. Body dysmorphic disorder (BDD) is preoccupation with perceived defects in physical appearance that are not observable or appear slight, causing significant distress.

## Core Clinical Content

### Hoarding disorder DSM-5
Persistent difficulty discarding possessions; cluttered living space; distress/impairment

### Hoarding prevalence
~2-6%; increases with age; more common in older adults

### Hoarding vs OCD
Hoarding: ego-syntonic, no obsessions; OCD: ego-dystonic, obsessions present

### Hoarding vs collecting
Collectors: organised, valued, displayed; hoarders: cluttered, distress, impairment

### Hoarding treatment
CBT (most evidence-based); decluttering; motivational interviewing; SSRIs sometimes

### BDD definition
Preoccupation with perceived physical defects not visible to others; significant distress

### BDD prevalence
~2%; often begins in adolescence; equal M:F

### BDD common concerns
Skin, hair, nose, body shape, symmetry; mirror checking, camouflaging

### BDD comorbidity
Depression (60-80%), OCD (30%), social anxiety, eating disorders

### BDD treatment
SSRI (fluoxetine, fluvoxamine); CBT (ERP + cognitive restructuring); clomipramine
