# Gender dysphoria and paraphilias

---

## Definition

Gender dysphoria is distress caused by a marked incongruence between experienced/expressed gender and assigned gender, lasting ≥6 months. Paraphilias are persistent, intense, atypical sexual interests; paraphilic disorders cause harm/distress.

## Core Clinical Content

### Gender dysphoria definition
Distress from incongruence between experienced gender and assigned gender; ≥6 months

### Gender dysphoria onset
Often childhood; some present in adolescence/adulthood

### GD treatment in children
Reversible interventions (puberty blockers at Tanner stage 2); supportive care

### GD treatment in adults
Affirming care; hormones, surgery, psychological support; no conversion therapy

### WPATH standards
International guidelines for transgender health; individualised, multi-disciplinary

### Paraphilias vs disorders
Paraphilia: atypical interest; paraphilic disorder: causes harm/distress/impairment

### Common paraphilias
Paedophilia, exhibitionism, voyeurism, fetishism, frotteurism, sexual masochism/sadism

### Paraphilic disorder assessment
Risk assessment (especially for child-related interests); treatment often court-mandated

### Treatment
CBT, relapse prevention; anti-androgens or GnRH agonists for severe paraphilias

### Co-occurring conditions
Substance use, depression, personality disorders, other paraphilias
