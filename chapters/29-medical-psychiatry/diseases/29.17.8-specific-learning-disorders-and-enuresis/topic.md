# Specific learning disorders and enuresis

---

## Definition

Specific learning disorders are neurodevelopmental disorders characterised by persistent difficulties in learning academic skills, with onset in school age. Enuresis is involuntary urination in a child ≥5 years old; encopresis is involuntary faecal soiling. Both are common in childhood and often have psychological components.

## Core Clinical Content

### Specific learning disorder types
Dyslexia (reading), dyscalculia (maths), dysgraphia (writing)

### Dyslexia prevalence
~5-10% of school-age children; M:F 2:1; phonological processing deficit

### Dyslexia features
Letter/word reversal, slow reading, poor spelling, phoneme awareness problems

### Dyscalculia prevalence
~3-6%; difficulty with numbers, arithmetic, mathematical reasoning

### Dyslexia diagnosis
Achievement below expected for age + IQ + education; psychometric testing

### Dyslexia management
Phonics-based reading instruction; educational support; screen for comorbidities (ADHD, anxiety)

### Enuresis definition
Involuntary urination ≥2/week in ≥5-year-old; ≥3 months

### Enuresis types
Primary (never dry) vs secondary (dry then relapses); monosymptomatic vs non-monosymptomatic

### Enuresis causes
Genetic, maturational delay, small bladder, ↑ ADH, psychological, UTI, sleep apnoea

### Enuresis treatment
Enuresis alarm (first-line, most effective); desmopressin; reward systems; address comorbidities
