# Generalised anxiety disorder (GAD)

---

## Definition

Generalised anxiety disorder (GAD) is characterised by excessive, uncontrollable worry about multiple events/activities for at least 6 months, accompanied by physical symptoms of anxiety (restlessness, fatigue, concentration difficulty, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance).

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
Excessive anxiety/worry ≥6 months, ≥3 of 6 associated symptoms

### Associated symptoms
Restlessness, fatigue, concentration difficulty, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance

### Prevalence
Lifetime ~5-6%; 2x more common in women; median onset 30 years

### Comorbidity
Depression (60%), other anxiety disorders, substance use

### First-line treatment
SSRI/SNRI + CBT

### Short-term anxiolytic
Benzodiazepine (max 2-4 weeks) for severe acute anxiety

### Pregabalin
Alternative to benzodiazepine; reduced abuse potential

### Buspirone
5-HT1A partial agonist; non-sedating, no dependence; takes 2-4 weeks

### Cognitive behaviour therapy (CBT)
As effective as medication; lasting benefits

### Course
Chronic, fluctuating; tends to improve with age
