## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Incidence** | 5-15% on PI-based ART develop diabetes |
| **Risk** | Older PIs (indinavir, lopinavir) > newer PIs (atazanavir, darunavir) |
| **Mechanism** | Insulin resistance, lipodystrophy, mitochondrial toxicity |

## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading

### PI Generations & Metabolic Risk
| Generation | Agents | Metabolic Risk |
|------------|--------|----------------|
| **1st Gen** | Indinavir, Saquinavir, Nelfinavir | High (IR, lipodystrophy, dyslipidaemia) |

| **3rd Gen** | Darunavir/r, Tipranavir/r | Lower (darunavir best) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **T2DM** | No ART; obesity primary |
| **NRTI lipodystrophy** | Stavudine/ZDV -> lipoatrophy |
| **Metabolic syndrome** | No ART; central obesity |

## 7. Management

### ART Optimisation
| Strategy | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Switch PI** | To newer PI (darunavir) or INSTI (DTG/BIC) if suppressed |
| **Metabolic Rx** | Metformin 1st line for IR; SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA if T2DM |
| **Lipids** | Statin (atorvastatin ≤20mg with PI); fibrate if TG>5 |

### Lipodystrophy Management
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **Lipoatrophy** | Avoid stavudine/ZDV; switch to TAF/ABC; tesamorelin if HIV |
| **Lipohypertrophy** | Weight loss; switch ART; surgical (liposuction) |

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **Older PI** | Indinavir, lopinavir -> high IR, lipodystrophy, dyslipidaemia |
| **Newer PI** | Atazanavir, darunavir -> lower metabolic risk |
| **Darunavir** | Best metabolic profile among PIs |
| **Statin + PI** | Atorvastatin ≤20mg; rosuvastatin 10mg; avoid simvastatin |
| **Metformin** | 1st line for IR |

## 9. Viva Questions
| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Which protease inhibitor has least metabolic risk?** | **Darunavir** (best metabolic profile among PIs) |
| **Which PI causes most lipodystrophy?** | Older PIs (indinavir, lopinavir, nelfinavir) |
| **How do you manage PI-induced insulin resistance?** | Switch to newer PI (darunavir) or INSTI; metformin 1st line |
| **What is the statin interaction with PIs?** | CYP3A4 inhibition -> ↑statin levels; atorvastatin ≤20mg, rosuvastatin 10mg |
| **Which NRTI causes lipoatrophy?** | Stavudine, zidovudine (older) |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **All PIs = same metabolic risk?** | NO - older (indinavir, lopinavir) > newer (atazanavir, darunavir) |
| **PI + statin = always contraindicated?** | NO - dose adjust statin; atorvastatin ≤20mg with boosted PI |
| **Protease inhibitors cause direct beta-cell damage?** | No - mainly insulin resistance + lipodystrophy |

**Mnemonic: PI-METABOLIC-RISK**
- **P**rotease inhibitors: older > newer metabolic risk
- **I**ndinavir: 1st gen, high IR + lipodystrophy
- **L**opinavir: 2nd gen, moderate-high
- **A**tazanavir: 2nd gen, better than lopinavir
- **D**arunavir: **best metabolic profile** (3rd gen)
- **T**iptrnavir: 3rd gen, similar to darunavir
- **E**volving: newer PIs better
- **M**etabolic: IR, lipodystrophy, dyslipidaemia
- **E**xtreme: 1st gen (indinavir, nelfinavir, saquinavir)
- **T**AF vs TDF: TAF > TDF weight gain
- **A**TV/r: atazanavir/r better than LPV/r
- **B**oosted PI: all affect statin (CYP3A4)
- **A**TV: less dyslipidaemia than LPV
- **B**est PI: darunavir (metabolic)
- **C**ombination: PI + NRTI = additive metabolic risk
- **R**osuvastatin: best with PI (≤10mg)
- **A**void: simvastatin with PI
- **V**iral load: suppressed before switch
- **I**NSTI: DTG/BIC better metabolic than PI
- **S**witch: PI -> INSTI if metabolic issues
- **K**now: darunavir = metabolic winner**</think>
