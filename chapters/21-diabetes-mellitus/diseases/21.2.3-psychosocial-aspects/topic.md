# Psychosocial Aspects of Diabetes

> [!info]
> **Psychosocial comorbidity is the rule, not exception** — Depression 2–3×, anxiety 20%, diabetes distress 30–45%, eating disorders ↑; all worsen glycaemic control and outcomes.

---

## 2. Prevalence (Meta-Analyses)

| Condition | T1DM | T2DM | General Population | Relative Risk |
|-----------|------|------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Major Depressive Disorder** | 12–18% | 10–15% | 5–7% | **2–3×** |
| **Depressive symptoms** (subthreshold) | 25–30% | 20–25% | 10–15% | 2× |
| **Anxiety disorders** | 15–20% | 14–18% | 7–10% | **2×** |
| **Diabetes distress** | **30–45%** | **30–40%** | N/A | — |
| **Eating disorders** | 2–4× (esp. women) | ↑ binge eating | 1–3% | **2–4×** |

## 3. Diabetes Distress vs Depression — Critical Distinction

| Feature | **Diabetes Distress** | **Major Depression** |
|---------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Definition** | Emotional burden of self-management demands | Pervasive low mood, anhedonia, neurovegetative symptoms |
| **Scope** | Diabetes-specific | Global |
| **Prevalence** | **30–45%** (much higher) | 10–18% |
| **Duration** | Fluctuates with diabetes demands | ≥2 weeks persistent |
| **Treatment response** | **Diabetes education, peer support, CBT-DM** | Antidepressants + CBT |
| **Screening tool** | **DDS-17 / PAID** / PHQ-9 | PHQ-9 |

**Key**: Diabetes distress responds to **diabetes-specific interventions** (structured education, CGM, peer support), not antidepressants.

---

## 4. Screening Tools (NICE/ADA Recommended)

| Tool | Target | Threshold | Time |
|------|--------|-----------|------|
| **PHQ-9** | Depression | ≥10 (moderate); ≥15 (severe) | 2 min |
| **GAD-7** | Anxiety | ≥10 | 2 min |
| **DDS-17** (Diabetes Distress Scale) | Diabetes distress | Mean ≥2 (moderate); ≥3 (high) | 3 min |
| **PAID** (Problem Areas in Diabetes) | Diabetes distress | ≥40 (moderate) | 2 min |
| **WHO-5** | Well-being | ≤50 (poor) | 1 min |
| **SCOFF** / **ESP** | Eating disorders | ≥2 / ≥1 | 1 min |

**When to screen**: At diagnosis, annually, when complications arise, if poor engagement, HbA1c not improving despite treatment.

---

## 5. Bidirectional Relationship with Glycaemic Control

```
Depression/Distress
       ↓
Poor self-care (missed doses, diet, monitoring, appointments)
       ↓
Hyperglycaemia → Complications → More distress
       ↑
Complications / hypoglycaemia fear → More distress
```

**Evidence**:
- Depression → 1.5–2× higher HbA1c
- Depression → 1.5–2× higher complication rates (retinopathy, CVD, mortality)
- Treating depression → modest HbA1c improvement (~0.3–0.5%)

## 6. Eating Disorders in Diabetes

| Disorder | T1DM Prevalence | T2DM Prevalence | Key Features |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Diabulimia** (insulin omission for weight loss) | **Highest risk** (women 15–30y) | Rare | HbA1c very high, recurrent DKA, normal/low BMI |
| **Binge eating (BED)** | 5–10% | **15–25%** | Weight gain, guilt, no compensatory behaviour |
| **Bulimia nervosa** | ↑ vs general | ↑ | Binge + purge (vomiting, laxatives, insulin omission) |
| **Anorexia nervosa** | Rare in established T1DM | Rare | Severe insulin restriction |

**Screening**: SCOFF questionnaire; ask about insulin omission, weight/shape concerns.

---

## 7. Hypoglycaemia Fear / Avoidance

| Behaviour | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Overtreatment** (running high to avoid hypos) | HbA1c ↑, long-term complications |
| **Reduced activity/exercise** | Fitness loss, weight gain, IR |
| **Sleep disruption** (nocturnal checking) | Fatigue, distress |
| **Avoidance of driving/travel** | Social isolation |

**Management**: CGM with alarms, structured education (DAFNE), psychological support.

---

## 8. Management Principles

### Stepped Care Model (NICE/ADA)

| Step | Intervention | For |
|------|--------------|-----|
| **1. Universal** | Structured education (DAFNE/DESMOND), peer support, diabetes distress screening (DDS/PAID) | All |
| **2. Mild distress/low mood** | Guided self-help (CBT-based apps, SilverCloud), diabetes-focused CBT (CBT-DM), DSN support | DDS 2–3, PHQ-9 <10 |
| **3. Moderate depression/anxiety** | **High-intensity CBT** (IAPT/psychology), **antidepressant** (SSRI 1st line), consider diabetes psychology | PHQ-9 10–19, GAD-7 ≥10 |
| **4. Severe/complex** | Psychiatry referral, combined psychotherapy + medication, crisis planning | PHQ-9 ≥20, suicide risk, eating disorder, treatment-resistant |

**Antidepressant choice in diabetes**:
| Drug | Glycaemic Effect | Weight | Notes |
|------|------------------|--------|-------|
| **SSRIs (sertraline, citalopram, fluoxetine)** | Neutral/slight ↓ HbA1c | Neutral/slight ↓ | **1st line** |
| **SNRIs (duloxetine, venlafaxine)** | Neutral | Neutral | Also for neuropathic pain |
| **Mirtazapine** | ↑ HbA1c | **↑ Weight** | Avoid if obese |
| **TCAs** | ↑ HbA1c | ↑↑ Weight | Anticholinergic; avoid |
| **Bupropion** | Neutral/↓ | ↓ Weight | Lower seizure threshold (hypoglycaemia risk) |

## 9. Special Populations

| Population | Specific Issues |
|------------|-----------------|
| **Young adults (18–30)** | Transition gap; high distress; eating disorders; "diabetes burnout" |
| **Older adults** | Depression often missed (attributed to aging); polypharmacy; cognitive decline; isolation |
| **Pregnancy (GDM/pre-existing)** | Anxiety high; perinatal depression screen (EPDS); body image |
| **Ethnic minorities** | Cultural stigma; language barriers; different illness beliefs; lower access to psychology |
| **Learning disabilities** | Diagnostic overshadowing; adapted tools needed |
