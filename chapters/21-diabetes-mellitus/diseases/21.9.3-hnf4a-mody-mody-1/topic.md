# HNF4A-MODY (MODY 1)

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise HNF4A-MODY clinical features (macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycaemia)
- [ ] Explain sulfonylurea responsiveness
- [ ] Differentiate from HNF1A-MODY and other MODY types

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Gene** | HNF4A (hepatocyte nuclear factor 4-alpha) |
| **Chromosome** | 20q13.1 |
| **Prevalence** | ~5-10% of MODY |
| **Inheritance** | Autosomal dominant |

## 3. Clinical Features / Presentation
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Onset** | Childhood/adolescence |
| **Birth weight** | **Macrosomia** (>4kg) |
| **Neonatal** | **Transient neonatal hypoglycaemia** (due to hyperinsulinaemia) |
| **Progression** | Progressive beta-cell dysfunction (similar to HNF1A) |
| **Autoantibodies** | Negative |
| **C-peptide** | Preserved |
| **Lipids** | Low HDL, raised ApoAII |

## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading
| Feature | HNF4A-MODY | HNF1A-MODY | GCK-MODY |
|---------|------------|------------|----------|
| **Birth** | Macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycaemia | Normal | Normal |
| **SU response** | **Excellent** | **Excellent** | None |
| **Lipids** | Low HDL, high ApoAII | Low HDL | Normal |
| **Autoantibodies** | Negative | Negative | Negative |

## 5. Diagnosis & Investigations
| Test | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **Genetic testing** | HNF4A mutation |
| **Autoantibodies** | Negative |
| **C-peptide** | Preserved |
| **Lipids** | Low HDL, raised ApoAII |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **HNF1A-MODY** | No macrosomia/neonatal hypoglycaemia; low CRP; low renal threshold |
| **T1DM** | Autoantibodies +, low C-peptide |
| **T2DM** | Obese, older, insulin resistance |
| **Beckwith-Wiedemann** | Macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycaemia, macrosomia, hemihypertrophy |

## 7. Management
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Sulfonylurea** | **Excellent response** (gliclazide MR 1st line) |
| **Monitoring** | HbA1c 6-monthly; lipids annual |
| **Genetic counselling** | Autosomal dominant; 50% offspring risk |

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **Key features** | Macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycaemia, SU responsive |
| **Lipids** | Low HDL, raised ApoAII |
| **Sulfonylurea** | **Excellent response** (like HNF1A) |
| **Genetics** | HNF4A (20q13); autosomal dominant |

## 9. Viva Questions
| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **What distinguishes HNF4A-MODY from HNF1A-MODY?** | Macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycaemia, low HDL, raised ApoAII |
| **What is the sulfonylurea response in HNF4A-MODY?** | Excellent (similar to HNF1A) |
| **What is the inheritance pattern?** | Autosomal dominant (HNF4A, 20q13) |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **HNF4A = HNF1A?** | Both SU responsive but HNF4A has macrosomia/neonatal hypoglycaemia |
| **All MODY need insulin?** | NO - HNF1A/4A = SU; GCK = none; HNF1B = insulin |

**Mnemonic: HNF4A-MODY-MACRO**
