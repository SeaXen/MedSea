# Amputation Prevention and Multidisciplinary Care

> [!info]
> **85% of diabetes-related amputations are preventable** — MDT care reduces major amputation rate **50–80%**; key pillars: early recognition, offloading, infection control, revascularisation, MDT coordination.

## 2. Epidemiology & Impact

| Statistic | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Lifetime DFU risk** | 19–34% |
| **DFU preceding amputation** | **85%** |
| **Annual amputation rate (diabetes)** | 0.5–1.0% (vs 0.02% non-diabetes) |
| **Major amputation (above ankle)** | 20–30% of all amputations |
| **5-year mortality post-major amputation** | **50–70%** (≈ aggressive cancer) |
| **MDT reduces major amputation** | **50–80%** (multiple studies) |
| **Cost: amputation vs MDT** | Amputation £15,000–25,000 + £5,000/yr; MDT saves £££ |

---

## 3. "Toe and Flow" — Two Pillars of Salvage

| Pillar | Action | Urgency |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **TOE** (Wound) | Offloading (TCC), debridement, infection control, wound care | **Days** |
| **FLOW** (Vascular) | Assessment → revascularisation (endovascular/bypass) | **Hours–Days** |

**Neither alone suffices** — revascularisation without offloading fails; offloading without flow fails.

---

## 4. Urgent MDT Referral Criteria (NICE NG19 / IWGDF)

**Refer within 24–48 hours if ANY**:
- Active foot ulcer (any grade)
- **Spreading infection** (erythema >2cm, lymphangitis, systemic signs)
- **Critical limb ischaemia** (rest pain, tissue loss, ABI <0.5, TBI <0.25)
- **Active Charcot** (hot, swollen, deformed foot)
- **Deep abscess / necrotising fasciitis** (surgical emergency)
- **Gangrene** (wet or dry)

**MDT must include**: Diabetologist + Podiatrist + Vascular Surgeon + Orthotist + Microbiologist/ID.

## 5. MDT Structure & Pathways

### Core MDT Members
| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Diabetologist** | Medical lead; glycaemic control; comorbidities; antibiotics |
| **Podiatrist** | Wound assessment; debridement; offloading (TCC); footwear |
| **Vascular Surgeon** | Revascularisation (endovascular/open); amputation surgery |
| **Orthotist** | Custom footwear; insoles; TCC application; orthoses |
| **Microbiologist/ID** | Antibiotic stewardship; culture interpretation |
| **Orthopaedic Surgeon** | Bony surgery; Charcot reconstruction; amputation |
| **Tissue Viability Nurse** | Complex wound dressings; NPWT |
| **Radiologist** | Imaging (Duplex, CTA, MRI, plain films) |
| **Plastic Surgeon** | Soft tissue coverage; flaps |
| **Rehab/Prosthetist** | Post-amputation rehabilitation |

### Pathway Tiers
| Tier | Population | Frequency | Team |
|------|------------|-----------|------|
| **Community** | Risk 0–1 | Annual screen | GP, Practice Nurse, Community Podiatry |
| **Specialist Foot Protection** | Risk 2 | 3–6 monthly | Podiatry-led MDT (diabetologist access) |
| **Multidisciplinary Foot Clinic** | Risk 3, active disease | 1–3 monthly / urgent | Full MDT (all core members) |

## 6. Amputation Levels & Indications

| Level | Indication | Functional Outcome |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| **Toe (Digital)** | Localised gangrene/ulcer; good perfusion | Excellent; minimal gait change |
| **Ray (Toe + Metatarsal)** | Forefoot ulcer/gangrene extending to metatarsal | Good; forefoot loading altered |
| **Transmetatarsal (TMA)** | Forefoot loss; viable heel pad | Good with custom shoe; equinus risk |
| **Lisfranc / Chopart** | Midfoot loss | Fair; requires AFO/orthosis |
| **Below-Knee (BKA)** | Non-salvageable foot; adequate popliteal flow | **Best major amputation** for prosthesis |
| **Through-Knee (TKA)** | Knee joint preserved | Good prosthetic fit; cosmesis |
| **Above-Knee (AKA)** | Popliteal disease; proximal infection | Higher energy cost; harder rehab |

**Decision principle**: **Most distal level with viable tissue + adequate perfusion**.

## 7. Pre-Amputation Optimisation

| Action | Timing |
|--------|--------|
| **Glycaemic optimisation** | HbA1c <64 (8.0%) if possible |
| **Nutrition** | Protein 1.5g/kg; vitamin C, zinc; correct malnutrition |
| **Smoking cessation** | **Mandatory** — 6 weeks pre-op ideal |
| **Vascular imaging** | Duplex/CTA — define amputation level |
| **Prosthetist review** | Pre-op — discuss expectations, phantom pain |
| **Psychosocial** | Counselling; peer support; body image |
| **Antibiotics** | Perioperative per infection grade |

---

## 8. Post-Amputation Rehabilitation

| Phase | Timeline | Goals |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Acute** | 0–2 weeks | Wound healing; pain control; oedema management (shrinker sock); mobility (wheelchair) |
| **Pre-prosthetic** | 2–6 weeks | Stump shaping; strengthening; balance; desensitisation; **phantom pain** management (gabapentin, mirror therapy) |
| **Prosthetic training** | 6–12 weeks | Cast/measuring; fitting; gait training; stairs; community reintegration |
| **Lifelong** | Ongoing | Annual prosthesis review; skin care; contralateral foot protection (Risk 3); vocational support |

## 9. Quality Indicators (NICE QS6 / National Diabetes Foot Audit)

| Indicator | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| **MDT referral within 24h** (active disease) | >90% |
| **Major amputation rate** | <0.5/1000 diabetes population |
| **Minor:Major amputation ratio** | >1:1 (ideally >2:1) |
| **Revascularisation before major amputation** | >80% |
| **TCC use for plantar ulcers** | >70% eligible |
| **Foot risk documented** | 100% |
