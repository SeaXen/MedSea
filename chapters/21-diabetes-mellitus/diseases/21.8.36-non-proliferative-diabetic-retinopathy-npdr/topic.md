# Non-Proliferative Diabetic Retinopathy (NPDR)

> [!info]
> **NPDR = R1 (mild/background) + R2 (pre-proliferative)**; microvascular leakage → haemorrhages, exudates, ischaemia; **4-2-1 rule** defines severe NPDR (R2); progression to PDR driven by ischaemia/VEGF; systemic optimisation is mainstay.

## 2. Classification (ETDRS / ENDESP)

| Grade | Name | Lesions | Progression Risk to PDR (5y) |
|-------|------|---------|------------------------------|
| **R1** | **Mild / Background NPDR** | **≥1 Microaneurysm** ± dot/blot haemorrhages ± hard exudates <1 DD from fovea | **Low (~5%)** |
| **R2** | **Moderate/Severe / Pre-proliferative NPDR** | **Venous beading**, **IRMA**, **multiple haemorrhages** (>4 quadrants), **cotton wool spots** | **Moderate-High (~15–25%)** |

---

## 3. 4-2-1 Rule (Severe NPDR / R2 Criteria)

**Any ONE of the following** in **≥1 quadrant** = Severe NPDR (ETDRS Level 53):
- **4** quadrants of **haemorrhages/microaneurysms**
- **2** quadrants of **venous beading**
- **1** quadrant of **IRMA**

**Very Severe NPDR** (ETDRS Level 61): **2+ of the above** — highest risk of PDR.

---

## 4. Pathophysiology

| Process | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Pericyte loss** | Early hyperglycaemia → pericyte apoptosis → capillary wall weakening → **microaneurysms** |
| **Endothelial damage** | ↑ VEGF, PKC, AGEs, oxidative stress → **vascular leakage** → haemorrhages, exudates |
| **Capillary non-perfusion** | Microthrombi, endothelial loss → **ischaemia** → **cotton wool spots** (axonal stasis), **venous beading**, **IRMA** (shunt vessels) |
| **VEGF upregulation** | Ischaemia → HIF-1α → VEGF → **vascular leakage** (DMO) + **neovascularisation** (PDR) |

---

## 5. Clinical Features

| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Microaneurysms** | Earliest lesion; saccular capillary outpouching |
| **Dot/blot haemorrhages** | Intraretinal; deep retinal layers |
| **Flame haemorrhages** | Superficial (nerve fibre layer) — more hypertensive |
| **Hard exudates** | Lipid leakage; circinate rings if focal |
| **Soft exudates (CWS)** | Ischaemic axonal stasis; pre-proliferative sign |
| **Venous beading** | Venous wall irregularity; ischaemia marker |
| **IRMA** | Intraretinal shunts (arterio-venous); pre-proliferative |
| **Venous loops/reduplication** | Advanced venous remodelling |

---

## 6. Risk Factors for Progression

| Modifiable | Non-Modifiable |
|------------|----------------|
| **HbA1c** (each 1% ↑ = 30–40% ↑ progression) | **Duration** of diabetes |
| **Hypertension** (SBP >140) | **Puberty** (accelerates) |
| **Dyslipidaemia** (↑ TG, ↓ HDL) | **Pregnancy** (↑ progression 2–3×) |
| **Anaemia** (Hb <11) | **Genetics** (familial clustering) |
| **Smoking** | **Ethnicity** (South Asian, Black higher) |
| **CKD** (eGFR <60) | |

**DCCT/EDIC**: Intensive control (HbA1c ~7% vs 9%) → **76% ↓ onset, 54% ↓ progression**; legacy effect persists 30y.

---

## 7. Management

### Systemic Optimisation (Mainstay)
| Target | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| **HbA1c <53 mmol/mol (7.0%)** | DCCT/UKPDS: ↓ onset & progression |
| **BP <130/80** | UKPDS: tight BP ↓ progression 34% |
| **Lipids** — Statins ± Fenofibrate | ACCORD-Eye: fenofibrate + statin ↓ progression 40% (especially mild NPDR) |
| **SGLT2i / GLP-1 RA** | Emerging retinal benefit (post-hoc) |

### Ophthalmology
- **No laser/anti-VEGF for NPDR alone** (unless CSME/DMO)
- **Pan-retinal photocoagulation (PRP)** — **not indicated** for NPDR (ETDRS: PRP increases DMO risk without benefit in NPDR)
- **Monitor**: R1 annual; R2 6–12 monthly (ophthalmology)

---

## 8. Diabetic Macular Oedema (DMO) in NPDR
- Can occur at **any NPDR stage** (even R1)
- **CSME** → refer for **anti-VEGF** (first-line) ± laser
- **Screen**: OCT at each visit if exudates near fovea

---

## 9. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **R1 (Background)** | Microaneurysms ± dot/blot haemorrhages |
| **R2 (Pre-proliferative)** | Venous beading, IRMA, CWS, multiple haemorrhages |
| **4-2-1 rule** | 4 quadrants haemorrhages OR 2 quadrants venous beading OR 1 quadrant IRMA = severe NPDR |
| **Progression R1→PDR** | ~5% at 5y |
| **Progression R2→PDR** | ~15–25% at 5y |
| **DCCT/EDIC legacy** | Early intensive control → 30y benefit |
| **Fenofibrate** | ACCORD-Eye: ↓ progression 40% (mild NPDR) |
| **PRP in NPDR** | **Not indicated** (increases DMO risk) |
| **Pregnancy** | ↑ NPDR progression 2–3× — screen each trimester |
| **Referral R2** | Ophthalmology 6–12 weeks |
