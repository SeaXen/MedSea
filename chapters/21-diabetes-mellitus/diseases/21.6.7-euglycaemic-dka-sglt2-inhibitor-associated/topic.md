# Euglycaemic DKA (SGLT2 Inhibitor Associated)

> [!info]
> **Euglycaemic DKA (euDKA) = DKA with blood glucose <14 mmol/L** (typically 8–13 mmol/L); **SGLT2i major risk factor**; same acidosis/ketosis as typical DKA but delayed recognition; **stop SGLT2i immediately**; dextrose infusion earlier; same insulin/fluid/K+ management.

## 2. Definition & Diagnostic Criteria

**Euglycaemic DKA** = Triad of:
1. **Blood glucose <14 mmol/L** (often **8–13 mmol/L**; rarely <8)
2. **Ketonaemia** (β-hydroxybutyrate **≥3 mmol/L**) OR **ketonuria** (+++)
3. **Metabolic acidosis**: pH <7.3, bicarbonate <15 mmol/L, anion gap >10

**Contrast with typical DKA**: Glucose **>14 mmol/L** (usually >20)

---

## 3. SGLT2 Inhibitors — Mechanism of euDKA Risk

| Mechanism | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Glycosuria** | ↓ Glucose reabsorption → ↓ glucose availability → ↓ insulin secretion |
| **Glucagon ↑** | Low glucose + low insulin → ↑ glucagon → ↑ lipolysis + ketogenesis |
| **Volume depletion** | Osmotic diuresis → ↑ catecholamines, cortisol → ↑ lipolysis |
| **Renal ketone handling** | SGLT2i may ↓ renal ketone clearance |

**Net effect**: **Insopenia + glucagon excess + volume depletion** → accelerated ketogenesis despite "normal" glucose.

---

## 4. Precipitating Factors (Often Multifactorial)

| Category | Triggers |
|----------|----------|
| **SGLT2i use** | All agents (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin, canagliflozin, ertugliflozin) |
| **Starvation / Low carb** | **Low calorie, ketogenic diet, intermittent fasting, Ramadan** |
| **Acute illness** | Infection, gastroenteritis, MI, stroke, pancreatitis |
| **Surgery / Procedures** | **Pre-op fasting + SGLT2i continued** → high risk |
| **Alcohol excess** | Impairs gluconeogenesis; starvation ketosis |
| **Pregnancy** | Physiological ketogenesis + SGLT2i (contraindicated) |
| **Insulin reduction** | Pump failure, missed doses, dose reduction |
| **Cocaine / stimulant use** | Catecholamine surge |

**Key**: **SGLT2i + any stressor** = euDKA risk.

---

## 5. Clinical Presentation

| Feature | Typical DKA | Euglycaemic DKA |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Glucose** | >14 (usually >20) | **<14 (8–13 typical)** |
| **Symptoms** | Polyuria, polydipsia, dehydration | **Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, malaise** (less dehydration) |
| **Ketones** | High | **High (often higher relative to glucose)** |
| **pH/Bicarb** | Severe acidosis | **Similar severity** |
| **Recognition** | **Easier** (high glucose prompts check) | **Delayed** (glucose "reassuring") |

**Diagnostic pitfall**: Glucose 9 mmol/L → "not DKA" → **miss ketones/acidosis**.

---

## 6. Diagnosis — High Index of Suspicion

**Check ketones (blood β-hydroxybutyrate) in ANY unwell patient on SGLT2i**, regardless of glucose.

| Test | euDKA Finding |
|------|---------------|
| **Blood glucose** | 8–13 mmol/L |
| **β-hydroxybutyrate** | **≥3 mmol/L** |
| **Venous pH** | <7.3 |
| **Bicarbonate** | <15 mmol/L |
| **Anion gap** | >10 |
| **HbA1c** | Often **normal/near-normal** (euDKA can occur at any HbA1c) |

---

## 7. Management — Modifications from Typical DKA

| Step | Typical DKA | Euglycaemic DKA |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **1. STOP SGLT2i** | N/A | **IMMEDIATELY** (do not restart until resolved + review) |
| **2. IV Fluids** | 0.9% NaCl boluses | **Same**; volume depletion often prominent |
| **3. Insulin** | Fixed-rate IV insulin (0.1u/kg/h) | **Same rate** (0.05–0.1u/kg/h) |
| **4. Dextrose** | Start when glucose <14 | **START EARLIER** (when glucose <11–12) — **5–10% dextrose** |
| **5. Potassium** | Protocol-based | **Same protocol** (monitor 1–2h) |
| **6. Bicarbonate** | Not routine | **Same** (pH <6.9) |
| **7. Monitoring** | Glucose, ketones, pH, K+ q1–2h | **Glucose + ketones + pH + K+ q1–2h** |

**Key difference**: **Dextrose infusion starts at higher glucose threshold** to allow continued insulin for ketone suppression without hypoglycaemia.

---

## 8. Sickle Day Rules for SGLT2i Patients

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Acute illness** (fever, infection, vomiting, diarrhoea) | **STOP SGLT2i**; check ketones; hydrate; seek medical review if ketones >0.6 or unwell |
| **Pre-surgery/procedure** | **STOP SGLT2i 3 days before** (elective); emergency → stop immediately |
| **Fasting** (religious, medical) | **STOP SGLT2i**; discuss with diabetes team |
| **Low carb/ketogenic diet** | **Avoid** on SGLT2i; if essential → stop SGLT2i |
| **Pregnancy** | **Contraindicated** — stop immediately, switch to insulin |

---

## 9. Outcomes & Prognosis

| Aspect | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Mortality** | Similar to typical DKA (~1–2% in-hospital) |
| **ICU admission** | Similar |
| **Length of stay** | Slightly longer (delayed diagnosis) |
| **Recurrence** | **High if SGLT2i restarted without counselling** |
| **Long-term** | No specific renal/cardiac sequelae beyond DKA itself |
