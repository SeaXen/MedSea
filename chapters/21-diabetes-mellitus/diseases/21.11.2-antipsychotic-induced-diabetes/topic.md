# Antipsychotic-induced diabetes

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise antipsychotic-induced metabolic syndrome
- [ ] Rank antipsychotics by diabetes risk
- [ ] Apply monitoring and management protocols
- [ ] Apply switching strategies

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Incidence** | 10-30% develop glucose dysregulation |
| **Risk hierarchy** | Olanzapine ≈ Clozapine > Risperidone > Aripiprazole > Lurasidone > Ziprasidone |
| **Mechanism** | Weight gain, insulin resistance, direct beta-cell toxicity (clozapine/olanzapine) |

## 3. Clinical Features / Presentation
| Feature | Presentation |
|---------|--------------|
| **Weight gain** | 5-15kg (clozapine/olanzapine highest) |
| **Dyslipidaemia** | ↑TG, ↓HDL, ↑LDL |
| **Glucose** | Impaired fasting glucose -> diabetes |
| **Onset** | 3-12 months (can be rapid with clozapine/olanzapine) |

## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading

### Antipsychotic Diabetes Risk
| Risk | Agents |
|------|--------|
| **High** | Clozapine, Olanzapine |
| **Moderate** | Risperidone, Quetiapine, Paliperidone |
| **Low** | Aripiprazole, Lurasidone, Ziprasidone, Amisulpride |
| **Lowest** | Haloperidol, Chlorpromazine (low dose) |

## 5. Diagnosis & Investigations
| Test | Frequency |
|------|-----------|

| **Fasting glucose/HbA1c** | Baseline, 12 weeks, annually |
| **Lipid profile** | Baseline, 12 weeks, annually |
| **Prolactin** | If on risperidone/paliperidone |

## 7. Management

### Prevention & Monitoring
| Strategy | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Baseline** | Weight, BMI, glucose, lipids, waist circumference |
| **Monitoring** | Weight q4wk x12wk; glucose/lipids 12wk then annually |
| **Switch** | If metabolic adverse effects; switch to lower risk agent |
| **Dose reduction** | Minimum effective dose |

### Treatment of Established Diabetes
| Approach | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Lifestyle** | Diet, exercise, weight management |
| **Metformin** | 1st line (weight neutral/loss; improves insulin sensitivity) |
| **GLP-1 RA/SGLT2i** | Weight loss + glucose control |
| **Switch antipsychotic** | High risk -> low risk (if psychiatric stable) |

### High-Risk Switching
| From | To |
|------|----|
| Clozapine/Olanzapine | Aripiprazole/Lurasidone/Ziprasidone |
| Risperidone | Aripiprazole/Lurasidone |

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **Highest risk** | Clozapine = Olanzapine > Risperidone > Aripiprazole/Lurasidone |
| **Mechanism** | Weight gain -> insulin resistance; direct beta-cell toxicity (clozapine/olanzapine) |
| **Monitoring** | Weight q4wk x12wk; glucose/lipids 12wk, annually |
| **First-line Rx** | Metformin (weight neutral/loss); GLP-1 RA/SGLT2i if T2DM |
| **Switch strategy** | High risk -> low risk if psychiatric stable |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **All antipsychotics = same risk?** | NO - Clozapine/Olanzapine >> Risperidone > Aripiprazole/Lurasidone/Ziprasidone |
| **Aripiprazole = no risk?** | LOW risk but not zero; metabolic neutral |
| **Weight gain = only mechanism?** | NO - direct beta-cell toxicity also (clozapine/olanzapine) |
