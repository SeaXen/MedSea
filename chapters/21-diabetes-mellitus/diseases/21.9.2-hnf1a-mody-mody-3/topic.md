## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **T1DM** | Autoantibodies +, low C-peptide, DKA |
| **T2DM** | Obese, insulin resistance, negative Abs |
| **GCK-MODY** | Mild fasting hyperglycaemia, no treatment needed |
| **HNF4A-MODY** | Macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycaemia, SU responsive |
| **Type 3c** | Exocrine insufficiency, pancreatic pathology |

## 7. Management

### Sulfonylurea Therapy (1st Line)
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Drug** | Gliclazide MR (preferred) |
| **Starting dose** | 30-60mg OD |
| **Titration** | Up to 120mg OD (max) |
| **Response** | **Excellent** (>90% achieve target); often allows insulin discontinuation |
| **Mechanism** | HNF1A regulates KATP channel expression; SU closes residual channels |

### Monitoring
| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **HbA1c** | 6-monthly |
| **Renal function** | Annual (monitor for diabetic nephropathy) |
| **Lipids** | Annual |
| **Renal threshold** | Monitor for glycosuria at normal glucose |

### Genetic Counselling
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Inheritance** | Autosomal dominant (50% offspring risk) |
| **Prenatal** | Available if mutation known |
| **Family screening** | Offer testing to 1st degree relatives |
