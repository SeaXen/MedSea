# Albuminuria categories (A1/A2/A3)

## 1. Learning Objectives
- [ ] Define ACR categories (A1, A2, A3) and their clinical significance
- [ ] Apply KDIGO staging (GFR × Albuminuria) for prognosis
- [ ] Interpret ACR trends for treatment response
- [ ] Recognise when to confirm ACR (2/3 samples in 3–6 months)

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **ACR (Albumin:Creatinine Ratio)** | Spot urine albumin (mg) / creatinine (mmol) — preferred over 24h urine |
| **Units** | mg/mmol (UK) or mg/g (US); 1 mg/mmol ≈ 8.8 mg/g |
| **Timing** | Early morning sample; avoid exercise, UTI, fever, menstruation, heart failure |

## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading

### Albuminuria Categories (KDIGO 2012/2022)
| Category | ACR (mg/mmol) | ACR (mg/g) | Terminology | Clinical Significance |
|----------|---------------|------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **A1** | **<3** | <30 | Normal / mildly increased | Low risk; annual monitoring |
| **A2** | **3–30** | 30–300 | Moderately increased (microalbuminuria) | **Early DKD**; CV risk ↑; ACEi/ARB indicated |
| **A3** | **>30** | >300 | Severely increased (macroalbuminuria) | **Overt DKD**; rapid progression; MDT |

### KDIGO Heat Map Integration
| GFR Category | A1 (<3) | A2 (3–30) | A3 (>30) |
|--------------|---------|-----------|----------|
| **G1 (≥90)** | Low risk 🟢 | Moderate 🟡 | High 🟠 |
| **G2 (60–89)** | Low 🟢 | Moderate 🟡 | High 🟠 |
| **G3a (45–59)** | Moderate 🟡 | High 🟠 | Very High 🔴 |
| **G3b (30–44)** | High 🟠 | Very High 🔴 | Very High 🔴 |
| **G4 (15–29)** | Very High 🔴 | Very High 🔴 | Very High 🔴 |
| **G5 (<15)** | Very High 🔴 | Very High 🔴 | Very High 🔴 |

## 5. Diagnosis & Investigations
| Investigation | Role | Details |
|---------------|------|---------|
| **Spot urine ACR** | Primary test | Early morning; confirm with 2/3 positive in 3–6 months |
| **Urine albumin only** | If creatinine unreliable | Albumin concentration >20 mg/L ≈ A2 |
| **eGFR (CKD-EPI)** | Required for staging | Paired with ACR for KDIGO heat map |
| **PCR (Protein:Creatinine)** | If non-albumin proteinuria suspected | PCR >50 mg/mmol ≈ A3; ACR preferred for DKD |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | ACR Pattern | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------------|
| **DKD** | Persistent A2/A3 + DM >10y/retinopathy | Gradual progression; retinopathy usually present |
| **Hypertensive nephrosclerosis** | A1 or low A2 | Long-standing HTN; no retinopathy; bland sediment |
| **Glomerulonephritis** | Variable (often A3) | Haematuria, RBC casts; systemic features |
| **Amyloidosis** | A3 (nephrotic) | Low albumin, systemic involvement; Congo red +ve |
| **Multiple myeloma** | Bence Jones proteinuria | PCR high, ACR normal; urine protein electrophoresis |

## 7. Management by ACR Category
| Category | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **A1 (<3)** | Annual ACR/eGFR; optimise HbA1c/BP/lipids |
| **A2 (3–30)** | **ACEi/ARB max tolerated** (↓progression 30–40%); SGLT2i if eGFR≥20; target ACR <3 or ↓30% |
| **A3 (>30)** | **ACEi/ARB max + SGLT2i (eGFR≥20) + Finerenone (eGFR≥25, K+≤4.8)**; nephrology referral; proteinuria target ↓30% |

### Monitoring Frequency by KDIGO Category
| Risk (Colour) | ACR Frequency | eGFR Frequency |
|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Green (Low)** | Annual | Annual |
| **Yellow (Moderate)** | 6-monthly | 6-monthly |
| **Orange (High)** | 3–6 monthly | 3–6 monthly |
| **Red (Very High)** | 1–3 monthly | 1–3 monthly |

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **ACR categories** | A1 <3; A2 3–30 (micro); A3 >30 (macro) mg/mmol |
| **Confirmation** | 2/3 positive in 3–6 months (early morning) |
| **A2 (microalbuminuria)** | **Start ACEi/ARB** (max dose); add SGLT2i if eGFR≥20 |
| **A3 (macroalbuminuria)** | ACEi/ARB max + SGLT2i + Finerenone (eGFR≥25, K+≤4.8); nephrology |
| **KDIGO heat map** | G3aA2 = moderate; G3bA3 = very high risk |
| **Proteinuria target** | ↓ACR ≥30% at 3–6mo; ultimate <3 mg/mmol |

## 9. Viva Questions
| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **What are the ACR categories for albuminuria?** | A1: <3 mg/mmol; A2: 3–30 mg/mmol (microalbuminuria); A3: >30 mg/mmol (macroalbuminuria) |
| **When do you start ACEi/ARB in diabetes?** | **ACR ≥3 mg/mmol (A2/A3)** — max tolerated dose; target ↓ACR ≥30% or <3 mg/mmol |
| **What is the KDIGO heat map?** | GFR G1–5 × Albuminuria A1–3; colour codes risk: Green=low, Yellow=moderate, Orange=high, Red=very high |
| **How do you confirm microalbuminuria?** | 2/3 early morning spot urine ACR ≥3 mg/mmol in 3–6 months |
| **What is the proteinuria reduction target?** | ↓ACR ≥30% at 3–6 months; ideal <3 mg/mmol |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **ACR vs PCR?** | ACR = albumin-specific (preferred for DKD); PCR = total protein (if non-albumin proteinuria) |
| **A2 = microalbuminuria?** | YES — historical term; KDIGO uses A2 (3–30 mg/mmol) |
| **Single ACR diagnostic?** | NO — biological variability high; need 2/3 samples |

**Mnemonic: ACR-KDIGO**
- **A**CR: spot urine albumin/creatinine (early morning)
- **C**ategories: A1 <3, A2 3-30, A3 >30 mg/mmol
- **R**epeat: 2/3 in 3-6mo to confirm
- **K**DIGO: G1-5 × A1-3 heat map
- **D**KD: A2 = start ACEi/ARB; A3 = ACEi+SGLT2i+Finerenone
- **I**deal target: ACR <3 or ↓30%
- **G**FR categories: G1 ≥90, G2 60-89, G3a 45-59, G3b 30-44, G4 15-29, G5 <15
- **O**nly early morning sample; avoid exercise/UTI/fever
