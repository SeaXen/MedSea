## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:

## 7. Management

### Prevention
| Strategy | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **CNI choice** | Ciclosporin if high NODAT risk; minimise tacrolimus dose |
| **Steroid minimisation** | Early steroid withdrawal if possible |
| **Weight management** | Pre-transplant weight loss |
| **Screening** | HbA1c pre-transplant; annual post-transplant |

### Management of Established NODAT
| Approach | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **CNI adjustment** | Reduce tacrolimus; switch to ciclosporin if high risk |
| **Metformin** | If eGFR >=30; 1st line |
| **SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA** | If eGFR permits; cardiorenal benefit |
| **Insulin** | If oral agents fail; flexible dosing |
| **Sulfonylureas** | Avoid (hypo risk with CNI) |

### CNI Dose Reduction/Switch
| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Tacrolimus high levels** | Reduce dose |
| **Persistent NODAT** | Switch tacrolimus -> ciclosporin |
| **Severe NODAT** | Consider belatacept (CNIs-sparing) |

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **NODAT** | 10-30% post-transplant; tacrolimus > ciclosporin (2-3x) |
| **Mechanism** | Tacrolimus: beta-cell toxicity + IR; ciclosporin: mainly IR |
| **Risk factors** | Tacrolimus, age>40, obesity, HCV, FH, steroids |
| **Monitoring** | Glucose weekly x1mo, then monthly x3mo, then 3-monthly |
| **Management** | Minimise tacrolimus; switch to ciclosporin; metformin/SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA |
| **Switch** | Tacrolimus -> ciclosporin if NODAT persistent |

## 9. Viva Questions
| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Which calcineurin inhibitor has higher NODAT risk?** | Tacrolimus (2-3x higher than ciclosporin) |
| **What is the mechanism of tacrolimus-induced diabetes?** | Beta-cell toxicity (calcineurin in beta-cells) + insulin resistance |
| **How does ciclosporin cause diabetes?** | Mainly insulin resistance; less beta-cell toxicity |
| **How do you manage tacrolimus-induced NODAT?** | Reduce tacrolimus dose; switch to ciclosporin; start metformin/SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA |
| **Can NODAT be reversed by switching CNI?** | Ciclosporin may improve; tacrolimus beta-cell toxicity often persistent |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **All NODAT = tacrolimus?** | Ciclosporin also causes but lower risk |
| **NODAT = T2DM?** | Similar but CNI-specific mechanisms; may improve with CNI adjustment |
| **All transplant patients get NODAT?** | 10-30%; risk stratification essential |
