## High-Yield Exam Points (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Facts |
|-------|-----------|
| **MODY red flags** | Age <25, non-obese, autosomal dominant (3 generations), negative autoantibodies, C-peptide preserved |
| **GCK-MODY (MODY2)** | Glucokinase defect · Mild fasting hyperglycaemia (5.5-8.0) · No treatment needed · No complications |
| **HNF1A-MODY (MODY3)** | Progressive β-cell defect · **Sulfonylurea responsive** (excellent response) · Low renal threshold |
| **HNF4A-MODY (MODY1)** | Similar to HNF1A · Macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycaemia · Sulfonylurea responsive |
| **MODY testing** | Cost-effective if probability >6% (calculator: MODY probability calculator) · Panel vs targeted |
| **Neonatal diabetes** | <6 months age · KCNJ11 (Kir6.2), ABCC8 (SUR1) = 40-50% · **Sulfonylurea responsive** (90% switch) |
| **Transient vs permanent** | Transient: remission by 18mo, relapse later · Permanent: lifelong · 6q24 imprinting = transient |
| **Mitochondrial diabetes** | m.3243A>G (MELAS/MIDD) · Maternal inheritance · Sensorineural hearing loss, CPEO, cardiomyopathy |
| **Wolfram syndrome** | WFS1 gene · DIABETES INSIPIDUS, DIABETES MELLITUS, OPTIC ATROPHY, DEAFNESS (DIDMOAD) |
| **Lipodystrophy** | Generalised (CGL) or partial (FPLD) · Severe insulin resistance · Leptin replacement (metreleptin) |
