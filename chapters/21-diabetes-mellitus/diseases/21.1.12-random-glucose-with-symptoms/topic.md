# Random Glucose with Symptoms

> [!info]
> **Random plasma glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L (200 mg/dL) + classic hyperglycaemia symptoms = Diabetes** — No confirmatory repeat required per ADA/WHO.

## 2. Diagnostic Criteria

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Random plasma glucose** | **≥11.1 mmol/L (200 mg/dL)** — any time of day, regardless of last meal |
| **Symptoms** | **Classic hyperglycaemia triad**: Polyuria + Polydipsia + Weight loss (± polyphagia, blurred vision, fatigue) |
| **Confirmatory repeat** | **NOT REQUIRED** if both criteria met unequivocally |

**ADA 2024 / WHO 2006**: "In a patient with classic symptoms of hyperglycaemia or hyperglycaemic crisis, a random plasma glucose ≥11.1 mmol/L (200 mg/dL) confirms diabetes."

---

## 3. Classic Symptoms (Must Have)

| Symptom | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| **Polyuria** | Osmotic diuresis from glycosuria (> renal threshold ~10 mmol/L) |
| **Polydipsia** | Thirst from hyperosmolality + volume depletion |
| **Weight loss** | Catabolism (lipolysis/proteolysis) from insulin deficiency ± glycosuria calorie loss |
| **Polyphagia** | Cellular starvation despite hyperglycaemia |
| **Blurred vision** | Osmotic lens swelling |
| **Fatigue** | Impaired glucose utilisation |

**All three** (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss) = classic triad. Two of three often sufficient.

---

## 4. When Repeat Confirmation IS Still Needed

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **No symptoms** (asymptomatic screening) | Repeat on separate day (FPG, HbA1c, or OGTT) |
| **Equivocal symptoms** (e.g. only fatigue) | Confirm with second test |
| **Random glucose 11.1–13.8 mmol/L** (mild) | Consider confirmatory FPG/HbA1c next day |
| **Stress hyperglycaemia** (acute illness, MI, stroke, steroids) | **Do not diagnose** — repeat when stable |
| **Children** | Often require confirmatory test per ISPAD |

---

## 5. Stress Hyperglycaemia — Key Differential

| Feature | Stress Hyperglycaemia | New-Onset Diabetes |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Context** | Acute illness, trauma, surgery, steroids | Often subacute, outpatient |
| **Glucose** | Usually <15 mmol/L | Often >15, can be >30 |
| **Ketones** | Usually negative/trace | Often positive (if T1DM) |
| **HbA1c** | Normal or mildly elevated (reflects chronic) | Usually elevated (>48 mmol/mol) |
| **Resolution** | Resolves with illness recovery | Persists |
| **Insulin need** | Transient (sliding scale) | Ongoing |

**Rule**: Never diagnose diabetes based on glucose during acute illness without follow-up.

---

## 6. Application in Acute Presentations

**DKA**: Random glucose usually >14 mmol/L (often 20–40) + ketosis + acidosis → diagnosis clear
**HHS**: Random glucose >30 mmol/L (often 40–60) + severe dehydration + no significant ketosis
**New T1DM in child**: Polyuria/polydipsia/weight loss + random glucose ≥11.1 → diagnostic
**Steroid-induced**: May meet criteria during treatment; classify as **drug-induced diabetes** if persistent

---

## 7. Comparison of Diagnostic Criteria

| Test | Threshold | Symptoms Needed? | Repeat Needed? |
|------|-----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Random + symptoms** | ≥11.1 mmol/L | **Yes (classic)** | **No** |
| **FPG** | ≥7.0 mmol/L | No | Yes (separate day) |
| **HbA1c** | ≥48 mmol/mol (6.5%) | No | Yes (separate day) |
| **OGTT 2-hr** | ≥11.1 mmol/L | No | Yes (separate day) |

---

## 9. Management After Diagnosis

1. **Type clarification**: Autoantibodies (GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, IAA) if T1DM suspected
2. **Ketone check**: If T1DM or glucose >15 — check β-hydroxybutyrate
3. **Baseline**: HbA1c, renal function, lipids, LFTs, urine ACR
4. **Education**: Sick day rules, hypoglycaemia awareness, DVLA notification (if on insulin)
5. **Referral**: DSN, dietitian, structured education (DAFNE/DESMOND)
