# Insulin resistance

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Define insulin resistance and its molecular mechanisms
- [ ] Explain tissue-specific insulin resistance (liver, muscle, adipose)
- [ ] Identify mediators: FFA, inflammation, adipokines, ceramides
- [ ] Apply HOMA-IR and other IR indices

---

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology

| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Definition** | Impaired biological response to physiological insulin concentrations |
| **Prevalence** | ~50% adults have some degree; central to T2DM, MetS, CVD |
| **Tissue Specificity** | Liver (HGP), Muscle (glucose disposal), Adipose (lipolysis) |

## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading

### Molecular Mechanisms of Insulin Resistance

| Level | Mechanism | Key Mediators |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **Receptor** | Decreased insulin receptor tyrosine kinase activity | Serine phosphorylation (IRS-1) |
| **Post-receptor** | Impaired PI3K-Akt signalling | PKC-theta, JNK, IKK-beta |
| **Metabolic** | Lipotoxicity, glucotoxicity | Ceramides, DAG, ROS, ER stress |
| **Inflammatory** | Adipose tissue inflammation | TNF-alpha, IL-6, MCP-1, leptin/down adiponectin |
| **Ectopic fat** | Liver, muscle, pancreas fat deposition | Hepatic steatosis, myosteatosis |

### Tissue-Specific Effects

| Tissue | Normal Insulin Action | Insulin Resistance Effect |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **Liver** | Decreased HGP (glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis) | Increased HGP (fasting hyperglycaemia) |
| **Muscle** | Increased glucose uptake (GLUT4) | Decreased glucose disposal (post-prandial hyperglycaemia) |
| **Adipose** | Decreased lipolysis, increased lipogenesis | Increased FFA, decreased adiponectin, increased leptin |

### Surrogate Indices
| Index | Formula | Cut-off (IR) |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| **HOMA-IR** | (Fasting Insulin µU/mL x Fasting Glucose mmol/L) / 22.5 | >2.5 (varies by population) |
| **QUICKI** | 1 / (log(Insulin) + log(Glucose)) | <0.33 |
| **Matsuda Index** | OGTT-derived | <4.0 |

---

## 5. Diagnosis & Investigations
| Test | Role |
|------|------|
| **Fasting insulin + glucose** | HOMA-IR calculation |
| **OGTT with insulin** | Matsuda index, beta-cell function |
| **Hyperinsulinaemic-euglycaemic clamp** | Gold standard (research only) |
| **Adiponectin/leptin** | Adipokine profile |

---

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Insulin receptor mutations** | Severe IR, acanthosis nigricans, hyperinsulinaemia (Donohue syndrome) |
| **Lipodystrophy** | Generalised/partial fat loss, severe IR, high triglycerides |
| **Cushing/Acromegaly** | Hormone excess causing secondary IR |

---

## 7. Management
| Strategy | Mechanism |
|----------|-----------|
| **Weight loss** | Decreased ectopic fat, decreased inflammation, increased adiponectin |
| **Exercise** | Increased GLUT4 translocation (AMPK), increased muscle insulin sensitivity |
| **Metformin** | Increased AMPK, decreased HGP |
| **TZDs (pioglitazone)** | PPAR-gamma agonist -> increased adiponectin, decreased inflammation |
| **SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA** | Weight loss + direct metabolic effects |

## 9. Viva Questions
| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **What is the molecular mechanism of insulin resistance?** | Serine phosphorylation of IRS-1 -> blocks PI3K-Akt signalling; mediated by PKC, JNK, IKK-beta, ceramides, inflammation |
| **How does lipotoxicity cause insulin resistance?** | Ectopic fat -> ceramides/DAG -> PKC/JNK activation -> IRS-1 serine phosphorylation -> blocks insulin signalling |
| **What is the role of adipose tissue inflammation?** | TNF-alpha, IL-6, MCP-1 from adipose macrophages -> systemic inflammation -> serine phosphorylation of IRS-1; decreased adiponectin |
| **How do you measure insulin resistance clinically?** | HOMA-IR = (Fasting insulin µU/mL x Fasting glucose mmol/L) / 22.5; >2.5 suggests IR |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **IR = T2DM?** | IR necessary but not sufficient; beta-cell failure also required |
| **All obese = IR?** | Metabolically healthy obesity exists; visceral fat more important than BMI |

**Mnemonic: IR-INSULIN**
- **I**nsulin resistance: impaired response to physiological insulin
- **R**eceptor: normal binding, post-receptor defect (PI3K-Akt)
- **I**RS-1: serine phosphorylation blocks signalling
- **N**F-kappaB: inflammation pathway (TNF-alpha, IL-6)
- **S**erine phosphorylation: IRS-1 inactivation
- **U**ntoward lipids: ceramides, DAG -> PKC/JNK
- **L**ipotoxicity: ectopic fat in liver/muscle/pancreas
- **I**nflammation: TNF-alpha, IL-6, decreased adiponectin
- **N**on-receptor: post-receptor defect predominates
