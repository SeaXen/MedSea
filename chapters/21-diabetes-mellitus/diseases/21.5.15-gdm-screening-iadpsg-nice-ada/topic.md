# GDM Screening (IADPSG, NICE, ADA)

> [!info]
> **Two major approaches**: **IADPSG/ADA/WHO** = Universal one-step 75g OGTT; **NICE** = Selective two-step (risk factors → OGTT). Different thresholds → different prevalence.

---

## 2. IADPSG / ADA / WHO — Universal One-Step (75g OGTT)

### Strategy
- **All pregnant women** without known diabetes
- **Timing**: 24–28 weeks gestation
- **Test**: 75g OGTT (fasting, 1h, 2h) after 8h fast
- **Diagnosis**: **Any ONE value** meets threshold

### Diagnostic Thresholds (HAPO-Based)

| Time Point | Threshold | Rationale |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Fasting** | **≥5.1 mmol/L (92 mg/dL)** | OR 1.75 for birth weight >90th %ile, C-section, neonatal hypoglycaemia |
| **1-hour** | **≥10.0 mmol/L (180 mg/dL)** | Unique to IADPSG; strong predictor |
| **2-hour** | **≥8.5 mmol/L (153 mg/dL)** | |

### Early Pregnancy (<24 weeks) — Overt Diabetes
If tested early (high risk), use **non-pregnant criteria**:
- FPG ≥7.0 mmol/L
- HbA1c ≥48 mmol/mol (6.5%)
- Random ≥11.1 mmol/L + symptoms
→ Diagnose as **overt/pre-existing diabetes**, NOT GDM

---

## 3. NICE NG3 — Selective Two-Step

### Step 1: Risk Assessment at Booking (10–12 weeks)
**Risk factors for GDM**:
- BMI ≥30 kg/m²
- Previous macrosomic baby (≥4.5kg or >90th %ile)
- **Previous GDM**
- Family history T2DM (1st degree relative)
- Ethnicity: South Asian, Black Caribbean, Middle Eastern (high prevalence)
- Age ≥40 years
- PCOS
- Antipsychotic medication

### Step 2: OGTT for Those with Risk Factors
- **Timing**: 24–28 weeks (if previous GDM: also at booking)
- **Test**: 75g OGTT (fasting + 2h only — **no 1-hour**)

### NICE Diagnostic Thresholds

| Time Point | Threshold |
|------------|-----------|
| **Fasting** | **≥5.6 mmol/L** |
| **2-hour** | **≥7.8 mmol/L** |

*Note: No 1-hour threshold in NICE.*

## 4. Comparison Table

| Feature | **IADPSG / ADA / WHO** | **NICE** |
|---------|------------------------|----------|
| **Approach** | Universal one-step | Selective two-step |
| **Screen ALL** | **Yes** (24–28w) | **No** — only if risk factors |
| **OGTT Load** | 75g | 75g |
| **Time Points** | Fasting, 1h, 2h | Fasting, 2h (no 1h) |
| **Fasting Threshold** | **5.1 mmol/L** | **5.6 mmol/L** |
| **1-hour Threshold** | **10.0 mmol/L** | Not used |
| **2-hour Threshold** | **8.5 mmol/L** | **7.8 mmol/L** |
| **Diagnosis Rule** | **Any ONE** abnormal | **Either** fasting OR 2h |
| **GDM Prevalence** | **15–25%** (high) | **5–10%** (lower) |
| **Evidence Base** | HAPO (continuous risk) | Health economic modelling |
| **Cost** | Higher (more tests, more GDM) | Lower |

## 5. Risk Factors for Early Screening (Both Guidelines)

| Risk Factor | NICE: Test at Booking | IADPSG/ADA: Test Early (<24w) |
|-------------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Previous GDM** | **Yes** (OGTT at booking + 24–28w) | Yes (overt diabetes criteria) |
| **Glycosuria** (≥2+ on 2 occasions) | Yes | Yes |
| **BMI ≥30** | Yes (at 24–28w) | Consider early |
| **PCOS** | Yes | Consider early |
| **FHx T2DM (1st degree)** | Yes | Consider early |
| **High-risk ethnicity** | Yes | Consider early |
| **Age ≥40** | Yes | Consider early |
| **Previous macrosomia** | Yes | Consider early |

---

## 6. ADA 2024 Specifics

| Population | Screening |
|------------|-----------|
| **All pregnant** | 75g OGTT 24–28w (IADPSG thresholds) |
| **High risk** (BMI ≥25 + 1 risk factor) | Test at first prenatal visit |
| **Previous GDM** | Early OGTT + repeat 24–28w if normal |
| **Diagnosis early (<24w)** | Use non-pregnant criteria → overt diabetes |

**ADA also endorses** HbA1c ≥39 mmol/mol (5.7%) at first visit as high-risk flag (but not diagnostic).

---

## 7. Controversies & Debate

| Issue | IADPSG/ADA View | NICE View |
|-------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Universal vs selective** | Universal — misses fewer cases; HAPO shows continuous risk | Selective — cost-effective; treats those most likely to benefit |
| **1-hour value** | Adds predictive power; identifies more | No evidence it improves outcomes beyond fasting+2h |
| **Lower thresholds** | Captures milder hyperglycaemia with risk | May over-medicalise; resource implications |
| **Cost-effectiveness** | Long-term offspring benefits (epigenetic) | Short-term NHS cost per QALY favourable for selective |

**UK Practice**: NICE is mandated; IADPSG used in some academic centres, research, and for comparison with international literature.

---

## 8. Practical Algorithm (NICE-Based)

```
Booking (10-12w)
  ├─ Risk factors? → OGTT now
  │    ├─ GDM → Manage
  │    └─ Normal → Repeat OGTT 24-28w
  ├─ Previous GDM? → OGTT now + repeat 24-28w
  └─ No risk factors → No routine OGTT
       (but check glycosuria at each visit)
```

**Glycosuria**: ≥2+ on 2 occasions → OGTT (renal threshold lowered in pregnancy).

## 9. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **IADPSG fasting threshold** | **5.1 mmol/L** (lower than NICE 5.6) |
| **IADPSG 1-hour threshold** | **10.0 mmol/L** (unique to IADPSG) |
| **IADPSG 2-hour threshold** | **8.5 mmol/L** (lower than NICE 7.8) |
| **NICE screening** | **Risk-factor based** — not universal |
| **NICE OGTT** | Fasting + 2h only (no 1h) |
| **Previous GDM** | OGTT at booking **+** repeat 24–28w |
| **Overt diabetes in pregnancy** | FPG ≥7.0 / HbA1c ≥48 at booking → pre-existing, not GDM |
| **HAPO study** | Basis for IADPSG — continuous risk, no clear threshold |
| **Prevalence difference** | IADPSG 15–25%; NICE 5–10% |
| **Cost-effectiveness** | NICE selective approach favoured in UK health economic models |
