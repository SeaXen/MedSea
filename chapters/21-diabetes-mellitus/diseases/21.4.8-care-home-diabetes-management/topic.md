## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading

### Individualised HbA1c Targets (Care Home)
| Frailty/Dependency | HbA1c Target | Rationale |
|--------------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Independent** | <58 mmol/mol (7.5%) | Standard target |
| **Mild dependency** | <64 mmol/mol (8.0%) | Balance control vs hypo risk |
| **Severe dependency / Dementia** | <69 mmol/mol (8.5%) | Avoid hypo; symptom control |
| **End of life / Palliative** | **No target** | Symptom control only; avoid hypo |

## 7. Management

### Care Plan Components
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[New Care Home Resident with DM] --> B[Individualised Care Plan (GP + DSN + Care Home)]
    B --> C[HbA1c Target per frailty/dependency]
    C --> D[Insulin Regimen: Basal + CHO-matched bolus]
    D --> E[Avoid Sliding Scale]
    E --> F[Hypoglycaemia Protocol (glucagon, IV access, 111/999)]
    F --> G[Staff Training: hypo recognition, glucagon, glucose gel]
    G --> H[Regular Review: 6-monthly HbA1c; annual specialist review]
```

### Insulin Regimen (Avoid Sliding Scale)
| Approach | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Basal insulin** | Glargine U-300 / Degludec OD (stable, predictable) |
| **Bolus insulin** | CHO-matched (fixed ratio e.g. 1 unit per 10-15g CHO) |
| **Avoid** | Sliding scale (reactive, erratic, hypo risk) |
| **Prefilled pens** | Reduce dosing errors; carer-friendly |

### Hypoglycaemia Protocol
| Level | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **Alert (<3.9)** | Glucose gel 15-20g; recheck 15min; notify GP/DSN |
| **Clinical (<3.0)** | Glucose gel + carer observation; notify GP/DSN |
| **Severe (unconscious)** | Glucagon 1mg IM/SC (carer trained); 999 if no response 15min; IV dextrose if paramedic |

### Staff Training Essentials
| Topic | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|
| **Hypo recognition** | Atypical signs (confusion, agitation, falls) |
| **Glucagon administration** | IM/SC 1mg; nasal 3mg; annual competency |
| **Glucose gel** | 15-20g buccal; technique |
| **Glucose monitoring** | SMBG/CGM technique; recording |
| **Insulin pens** | Priming, dialling, injection technique |
| **Foot checks** | Daily visual; annual specialist |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Sliding scale = convenient?** | NO - reactive, erratic, increases hypo; basal + CHO-matched bolus is proactive |
| **HbA1c <53 for all?** | NO - care home residents frail; targets relaxed to avoid hypo |
| **Insulin stops in palliative?** | NO - continue basal to avoid symptomatic hyperglycaemia; stop bolus |

**Mnemonic: CARE-HOME-DM**
- **C**are home: 25-30% have DM; frail, polypharmacy
- **A**void sliding scale: reactive, erratic, hypo risk
- **R**esident-specific: HbA1c targets by dependency (58/64/69/none)
- **E**ducation: staff training (hypo, glucagon, glucose gel, pens, feet)
- **H**ypo: atypical (confusion, falls); glucagon 1mg IM/SC carer-trained
- **O**ther meds: review polypharmacy; stop SU; simplify insulin
- **M**DT: GP, DSN, care manager, dietitian, podiatrist
- **E**xpect: 2-3x mortality vs community elderly DM
- **D**ementia: <69 HbA1c target; carer-dependent recognition
- **M**onitoring: 6-monthly HbA1c; daily foot check; annual specialist
- **N**o sliding scale: basal + CHO-matched bolus only
