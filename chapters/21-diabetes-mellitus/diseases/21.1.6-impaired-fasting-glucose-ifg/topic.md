# Impaired Fasting Glucose (IFG)

> [!info]
> **FPG 6.1–6.9 mmol/L (110–125 mg/dL)** — Isolated IFG reflects hepatic insulin resistance; ~5–10%/year progression to T2DM.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] State diagnostic criteria for IFG (ADA vs WHO differences)
- [ ] Explain pathophysiology: hepatic insulin resistance vs muscle
- [ ] Quantify progression risk to T2DM
- [ ] Apply screening intervals and lifestyle intervention evidence
- [ ] Recognise when to do OGTT (IFG + risk factors)

---

## 2. Diagnostic Criteria

| Guideline | FPG Range | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **ADA 2024** | 5.6–6.9 mmol/L (100–125 mg/dL) | Lower cut-off captures more at-risk |
| **WHO 1999/2006** | 6.1–6.9 mmol/L (110–125 mg/dL) | Higher cut-off, more specific |
| **NICE CG12** | 6.1–6.9 mmol/L | Aligns with WHO |

**Key difference**: ADA uses 5.6 mmol/L (100 mg/dL) lower threshold; WHO/NICE use 6.1 mmol/L (110 mg/dL). Both require **confirmatory repeat test** unless symptomatic hyperglycaemia.

---

## 3. Pathophysiology

**Isolated IFG** = **Hepatic insulin resistance** predominates:
- Fasting hyperglycaemia from ↑ hepatic glucose output (HGO)
- Normal muscle glucose disposal → normal 2-hr OGTT
- Early β-cell compensation maintains post-prandial glucose

**Isolated IGT** = **Muscle insulin resistance** predominates:
- Normal fasting (hepatic insulin sensitivity preserved)
- Impaired post-prandial disposal → ↑ 2-hr glucose

**Combined IFG+IGT** = Highest progression risk (dual defect).

---

## 4. Progression Risk

| Category | Annual Progression to T2DM | 5-Year Risk |
|----------|----------------------------|-------------|
| **Normal (FPG <5.6, 2h <7.8)** | <1% | <5% |
| **IFG alone (WHO)** | 4–6% | 15–25% |
| **IGT alone** | 5–8% | 20–35% |
| **IFG + IGT** | 10–15% | 40–60% |
| **HbA1c 39–47 mmol/mol (5.7–6.4%)** | ~5–8% | 25–35% |

**Risk modifiers**: BMI ≥30, family history, GDM history, PCOS, ethnicity (South Asian, African-Caribbean), hypertension, dyslipidaemia.

---

## 5. Screening & Diagnosis

**Who to screen** (ADA/NICE):
- Age ≥45 years (or ≥25 if South Asian/other high-risk ethnicity)
- BMI ≥25 (≥23 Asian) + ≥1 risk factor
- GDM history
- PCOS, acanthosis nigricans, HIV, antipsychotics

**Tests**:
| Test | Cut-off | Advantages | Limitations |
|------|---------|------------|-------------|
| **FPG** | 5.6–6.9 (ADA) / 6.1–6.9 (WHO) | Cheap, convenient, reproducible | Misses isolated IGT; stress hyperglycaemia |
| **HbA1c** | 39–47 mmol/mol (5.7–6.4%) | No fasting, stable | Misses IFG/IGT; unreliable in anaemia, Hb variants, CKD |
| **OGTT** | 2-hr 7.8–11.0 mmol/L | Gold standard for IGT | Inconvenient, poor reproducibility (CV 15%) |

**Diagnostic algorithm**:
```
Screen FPG or HbA1c
  ├─ Normal → Repeat 3-yearly
  ├─ IFG (5.6–6.9) → Do OGTT to exclude IGT/diabetes
  ├─ HbA1c 39–47 → Confirm with FPG/OGTT
  └─ Diabetes range → Confirm second test
```

---

## 6. Management — Lifestyle First

**Evidence**: DPP, Finnish DPS, Da Qing all show **~58% risk reduction** with intensive lifestyle.

| Target | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| **Weight** | 5–7% body weight loss |
| **Diet** | Reduced calorie, low fat, high fibre (Mediterranean/DASH) |
| **Activity** | ≥150 min/week moderate (e.g. brisk walking) |
| **Behaviour** | Structured programme (DPP curriculum, DESMOND) |

**Metformin consideration** (ADA):
- Age 25–59, BMI ≥30, FPG ≥6.1, HbA1c ≥42 mmol/mol (6.0%)
- Women with prior GDM
- Reduces progression ~31% (vs 58% lifestyle)

---

## 7. Follow-up Intervals

| Finding | Repeat Testing |
|---------|----------------|
| Normal | 3 years |
| IFG/IGT/HbA1c 39–47 | **Annually** |
| Previous GDM | 6–12 weeks postpartum, then 1–3 yearly |

---

## 8. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **ADA vs WHO IFG cut-off** | ADA 5.6 (100), WHO 6.1 (110) mmol/L — know both |
| **IFG vs IGT pathophysiology** | IFG = hepatic IR; IGT = muscle IR |
| **Progression risk** | Combined IFG+IGT > IGT alone > IFG alone |
| **Metformin indication** | Age 25–59, BMI ≥30, high-risk IFG |
| **Screening interval IFG** | Annual (not 3-yearly) |
| **OGTT indication** | IFG on FPG — to rule out IGT/diabetes |

---

## 9. Differential Diagnosis
- **Stress hyperglycaemia** (acute illness, steroids) — repeat when well
- **Early T2DM** — HbA1c ≥48 or 2-hr ≥11.1
- **MODY (GCK)** — Mild fasting hyperglycaemia 5.5–8.0, stable, family history, non-progressive
- **Type 1 diabetes (early)** — Weight loss, ketosis, autoantibodies+

---

## 10. Complications if Untreated
- Micro/macrovascular risk starts in prediabetes (DECODE, DECODA studies)
- Retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy prevalence ↑ even at IFG/IGT
- CVD risk ~1.5–2× normal
