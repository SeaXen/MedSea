## 1. Definition & Exam Relevance
**Time in Range (TIR)** = percentage of time glucose spends within target range (3.9–10 mmol/L) as measured by CGM.  
**FCPS/MRCP relevance**: Emerging standard for glycaemic assessment; ATTD consensus; correlates with complications; exam questions on targets, interpretation, relationship to HbA1c.

---

## 2. Standard CGM Metrics (ATTD 2019 Consensus)
| Metric | Range | Target (T1DM / T2DM) | Clinical Significance |
|--------|-------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **TIR** | 3.9–10 mmol/L | >70% / >70% | Primary efficacy endpoint |
| **TAR (Level 1)** | >10–13.9 mmol/L | <25% / <25% | Hyperglycaemia exposure |
| **TAR (Level 2)** | >13.9 mmol/L | <5% / <5% | Severe hyperglycaemia |
| **TBR (Level 1)** | 3.0–3.9 mmol/L | <4% / <4% | Hypoglycaemia alert |
| **TBR (Level 2)** | <3.0 mmol/L | <1% / <1% | Severe hypoglycaemia |
| **CV (Coefficient of Variation)** | SD/Mean × 100 | <36% / <36% | Glycaemic variability |

**Pregnancy targets (T1DM)**: TIR >70% (3.5–7.8 mmol/L), TBR <4% (<3.5 mmol/L), CV <36%

---

## 3. TIR ↔ HbA1c Relationship (Derived from DCCT/ADAG)
| TIR | Estimated HbA1c | Category |
|-----|----------------|----------|
| >70% | <53 mmol/mol (<7.0%) | **Target** |
| 60–70% | 53–58 mmol/mol (7.0–7.5%) | **Suboptimal** |
| 50–60% | 58–64 mmol/mol (7.5–8.0%) | **Inadequate** |
| <50% | >64 mmol/mol (>8.0%) | **Poor** |

**Formula (approximate)**: HbA1c (mmol/mol) ≈ 77.6 – 0.46 × TIR(%)  
**GMI (Glucose Management Indicator)**: Calculated from mean CGM glucose; correlates with lab HbA1c.

---

## 4. Clinical Interpretation Framework
### Step 1: Assess Hypoglycaemia (Safety First)
- TBR Level 2 >1% → **immediate action** (basal reduction, education, CGM alarms)
- TBR Level 1 >4% → **review patterns** (nocturnal, post-exercise, alcohol)

### Step 2: Assess Hyperglycaemia (Efficacy)
- TAR Level 2 >5% → **intensify therapy** (bolus, basal, lifestyle)
- TAR Level 1 >25% → **review carbohydrate counting, ICR, meal timing**

### Step 3: Assess Variability
- CV >36% → **unstable control** → review injection technique, site rotation, insulin absorption, CGM artefacts

### Step 4: Assess TIR
- TIR <70% → **comprehensive regimen review** (basal testing, ICR, ISF, lifestyle)

---

## 5. AGP (Ambulatory Glucose Profile) Interpretation
| AGP Feature | What to Look For | Action |
|-------------|------------------|--------|
| **Median line** | Central tendency | Shift up/down = basal adjustment |
| **25–75th percentile (IQR)** | Day-to-day variability | Wide = erratic lifestyle/insulin |
| **5–95th percentile** | Extremes | Outliers = specific events (missed bolus, exercise) |
| **Time blocks** | Overnight, morning, afternoon, evening | Targeted adjustments per block |

**Overnight (00:00–06:00)**: Most stable; reflects basal insulin adequacy.

---

## 6. Data Sufficiency Requirements
| Requirement | Minimum |
|-------------|---------|
| **Sensor wear** | >70% of time (14-day period → >10 days) |
| **Readings per day** | >70% of expected (288/day for 5-min → >200/day) |
| **Report period** | 14 days ideal; 10 days minimum |

**Insufficient data**: Do not make major regimen changes; optimize wear first.

---

## 7. Special Populations — Target Adjustments
| Population | TIR Target | TBR Target | Range |
|------------|------------|------------|-------|
| **Frail elderly / limited life expectancy** | >50% | <1% (L2) | 5.6–11.1 mmol/L |
| **High hypoglycaemia risk / impaired awareness** | >50% | <1% (L2) | 5.6–11.1 mmol/L |
| **Pregnancy (T1DM)** | >70% | <4% (L1) | 3.5–7.8 mmol/L |
| **Children <6 years** | >60% | <3% (L1) | 3.9–10 mmol/L |
| **Children 6–18 years** | >65% | <3% (L1) | 3.9–10 mmol/L |

---

## 8. FCPS/MRCP Exam Pearls
- **TIR >70% = HbA1c ~53 mmol/mol (7.0%)** — memorize this equivalence
- **Each 5% increase in TIR ≈ 5 mmol/mol (0.5%) HbA1c reduction**
- **CV >36%** = high variability → independently predicts complications
- **TIR vs HbA1c**: TIR captures hypoglycaemia & variability; HbA1c does not
- **ATTD 2019 consensus**: TIR primary outcome for clinical trials
- **GMI vs HbA1c**: GMI calculated from mean CGM glucose; discordance → haemoglobinopathy, anaemia, CGM artefact
- **Data sufficiency**: 14 days, >70% wear — standard exam question
