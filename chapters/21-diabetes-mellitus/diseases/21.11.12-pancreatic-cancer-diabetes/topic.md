## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Definition** | New-onset diabetes as paraneoplastic manifestation of pancreatic adenocarcinoma |
| **Incidence** | 1% of new-onset DM >50y = pancreatic cancer; 25% of pancreatic cancer present with DM |
| **Timing** | Diabetes precedes cancer diagnosis by 6-36 months (median 12) |
| **Mechanism** | Tumour factors (adrenomedullin, SST, inflammatory cytokines) -> beta-cell dysfunction |

## 5. Diagnosis & Investigations
| Test | Role |
|------|------|
| **CT pancreas protocol** | Gold standard (arterial/venous phase); mass, duct dilatation, mets |
| **CA19-9** | Tumour marker (sensitivity 80%; specificity 90%); >37 U/mL |
| **MRCP** | If CT equivocal; ductal anatomy |
| **EUS-FNA** | Tissue diagnosis if resectable/biopsy needed |
| **HbA1c/glucose** | Monitor diabetes |
| **Exocrine function** | Faecal elastase (often normal unless duct obstruction) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **T2DM** | Overweight, gradual onset, FH+, no weight loss |
| **Chronic pancreatitis DM** | Exocrine insufficiency, calcification, alcohol history |
| **Type 3c (other)** | Chronic pancreatitis, cystic fibrosis, post-pancreatectomy |
| **Cushing/acromegaly** | Hormone excess features |

## 7. Management

### Urgent Referral
| Action | Timeline |
|--------|----------|
| **CT pancreas protocol** | Within 2 weeks (NICE) |
| **CA19-9** | Baseline |
| **MDT referral** | Pancreatic cancer MDT |

### Diabetes Management
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Insulin** | Preferred (flexible, no hypoglycaemia if dosed correctly) |
| **Steroids** | If on chemo; adjust insulin |
| **SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA** | Caution (weight loss, GI); consider if CVD/CKD |
| **Surgery (Whipple)** | Diabetes may persist/resolve; insulin perioperative |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **All new DM >50 = cancer?** | NO - 1% risk; but must exclude |
| **CA19-9 = diagnostic?** | NO - not sensitive/specific enough alone; needs imaging |
| **Diabetes resolves after Whipple?** | Often persists (beta-cell loss); may improve if insulin resistance from cancer factors |
