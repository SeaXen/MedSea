## 1. Definition & Exam Relevance
**IA-2 autoantibodies** = antibodies against **insulinoma-associated antigen-2** (tyrosine phosphatase-like protein, ICA512).  
**FCPS/MRCP relevance**: Part of T1DM autoantibody panel; exam questions on sensitivity, specificity, predictive value, distinction from GAD65.

---

## 2. Antigen Characteristics
| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Full name** | Insulinoma-Associated Antigen 2 (IA-2) / ICA512 / PTPRN |
| **Location** | Neuroendocrine secretory granules (pancreatic β-cells, adrenal medulla, brain) |
| **Structure** | Transmembrane receptor-like protein tyrosine phosphatase (inactive catalytic domain) |
| **Molecular weight** | ~105 kDa (intracellular domain used in assays) |
| **Function** | Dense core vesicle trafficking, insulin secretion modulation |

---

## 3. Clinical Performance
| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Sensitivity (new-onset T1DM)** | 50–70% | Lower than GAD65 (~70–80%) |
| **Specificity** | >95% | Very high — rare in healthy controls |
| **Positive Predictive Value** | High | Especially with multiple antibodies |
| **Age dependence** | Higher in children/adolescents | Less common in adult-onset (LADA) |
| **Persistence** | Declines over years | Often becomes negative after 5–10 years |

**Key point**: IA-2 is **more specific but less sensitive** than GAD65.

---

## 4. Comparison with Other Autoantibodies
| Feature | GAD65 | IA-2 | IAA | ZnT8 |
|---------|-------|------|-----|------|
| **Sensitivity (T1DM)** | 70–80% | 50–70% | 40–60% (children) | 60–80% |
| **Specificity** | 95–98% | **>98%** | 90–95% | 95–98% |
| **LADA positivity** | **High (70–80%)** | Low (20–30%) | Low | Moderate (40–50%) |
| **Age preference** | All ages | **Children > Adults** | Young children | All ages |
| **Persistence** | Years–decades | Declines | Declines (esp. if insulin started) | Persists |
| **Assay type** | ELISA/RBA | ELISA/RBA | RBA (insulin binding) | ELISA |

---

## 5. Predictive Value for T1DM
| Antibody Profile | 5-Year Risk | 10-Year Risk |
|------------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Single Ab (any)** | ~10–15% | ~20–30% |
| **IA-2 alone** | ~20% | ~35% |
| **GAD65 alone** | ~15% | ~25% |
| **Two Abs (any combo)** | **~40–60%** | **~60–80%** |
| **Three+ Abs** | **>80%** | **>90%** |
| **IA-2 + GAD65 + ZnT8** | **>90%** | **>95%** |

**IA-2 positivity strongly predicts rapid progression** to insulin requirement.

---

## 6. Clinical Utility
| Scenario | Role of IA-2 |
|----------|--------------|
| **New-onset diabetes (adult)** | Part of panel to confirm T1DM vs T2DM |
| **LADA diagnosis** | Low sensitivity — **GAD65 preferred** for LADA |
| **Paediatric T1DM** | High sensitivity in children; adds to multi-ab panel |
| **Research / Prevention trials** | Enrichment for high-risk (multiple Abs) |
| **Post-transplant / Monogenic** | Negative IA-2 supports non-autoimmune aetiology |
| **Recurrence post-pancreas transplant** | Rising IA-2 may predict recurrence |

---

## 7. Assay Methodology
| Method | Principle | Advantage |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Radiobinding Assay (RBA)** | Radiolabelled IA-2 + patient serum + precipitation | Gold standard; high sensitivity |
| **ELISA** | Recombinant IA-2 (intracellular domain) coated plate | High throughput; automated; no radioactivity |
| **Luciferase Immunoprecipitation (LIPS)** | Luciferase-tagged IA-2 + luminescence | High sensitivity; wide dynamic range |

**Standardization**: WHO International Standard (NIBSC 05/210) for IA-2 autoantibodies; expressed in **U/mL** (units/mL).

## 8. Interpretation Algorithm
```
New diabetes diagnosis → autoantibody panel
    ↓
IA-2 POSITIVE
    ├─ + GAD65 + → **T1DM confirmed** (high specificity)
    ├─ + ZnT8 + → **T1DM confirmed** (high specificity)
    ├─ + IAA + → **T1DM confirmed** (if insulin-naïve)
    └─ IA-2 ONLY → **T1DM likely** (high specificity); check clinical context
        • Adult, lean, ketotic → T1DM
        • Adult, overweight, non-ketotic → consider LADA (check GAD65)
```

## 9. FCPS/MRCP Exam Pearls
- **IA-2 = ICA512 = tyrosine phosphatase** — same antigen
- **Specificity >98%** — highest among T1DM autoantibodies
- **Sensitivity 50–70%** — lower than GAD65; **misses some T1DM** if used alone
- **Children > Adults** — more common in paediatric T1DM
- **LADA**: GAD65 is the marker; IA-2 low yield
- **Multiple antibodies (>2)** = high predictive value (>80% 10-yr risk)
- **Declines over time** — may become negative 5–10y post-diagnosis
- **Not insulin-dependent** — unlike IAA, not affected by exogenous insulin
- **Assay**: ELISA (routine), RBA (reference), LIPS (research)
