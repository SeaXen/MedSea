# Neuropathy Screening (10g Monofilament, Vibration, ANNS)

> [!info]
> **NICE 9 Care Process #8**: Annual foot examination with **10g monofilament, vibration perception, ankle reflexes**; **ANNS/MNSI** questionnaires; **LOPS** = monofilament -ve at ≥1 site; risk stratification per IWGDF.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Perform annual neuropathy screen per NICE
- [ ] Apply 10g monofilament technique (3 sites/foot)
- [ ] Assess vibration perception (128Hz tuning fork)
- [ ] Use ANNS and MNSI questionnaires
- [ ] Stratify risk (IWGDF 0–3) and refer appropriately

---

## 2. Annual Screen Components (NICE 9 Care Process)

| Test | Method | Abnormal Threshold |
|------|--------|---------------------|
| **10g Monofilament** | Apply to **3 sites per foot** (plantar hallux, 1st & 5th metatarsal heads); buckle, hold 1s, release | **Negative at ≥1 of 6 sites** = **LOPS** (Loss of Protective Sensation) |
| **Vibration Perception** | **128Hz tuning fork** on **hallux IP joint** and **medial malleolus**; compare to examiner | **Reduced vs examiner** or **duration <10s** |
| **Ankle Reflexes** | Tap Achilles tendon with knee flexed | **Absent** |

**LOPS** = Loss of Protective Sensation — defines IWGDF Risk 2+.

---

## 3. Monofilament Technique (Semmes-Weinstein 10g)

### Procedure
1. **Site selection**: Plantar hallux, 1st metatarsal head, 5th metatarsal head (3 sites/foot)
2. **Application**: Touch skin perpendicularly → **buckle** → hold **1 second** → release
3. **Patient response**: "Yes" when felt; eyes closed
4. **Repeat** each site **once** (do not repeat if felt)
5. **Record**: Positive (felt) / Negative (not felt) per site

### Interpretation
| Result | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **All 6 sites positive** | Normal protective sensation |
| **≥1 site negative** | **LOPS** = IWGDF Risk 2 (if pulses present) or Risk 3 (if absent pulses/prior ulcer) |

**Key**: Do **not** test over callus/ulcer; avoid callused areas.

---

## 4. Vibration Perception (128Hz Tuning Fork)

### Procedure
1. Strike fork on rubber surface
2. Place stem on **hallux IP joint** (distal) → move to **medial malleolus** if not felt
3. Ask patient to say "now" when vibration stops
4. **Examiner feels fork** simultaneously — compare duration

### Interpretation
| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Felt at IP joint, duration similar to examiner** | Normal |
| **Felt at IP but duration ↓** | Early impairment |
| **Not felt at IP, felt at malleolus** | Moderate impairment |
| **Not felt at malleolus** | Severe impairment |

**Quantitative**: Biothesiometer / Neurothesiometer — voltage threshold >25V = abnormal.

---

## 5. Questionnaires

### ANNS (Annual Neuropathy Symptom Score) — **4 Questions**
| Question | Score if Yes |
|----------|--------------|
| 1. Do you have **pain** in legs/feet? | 1 |
| 2. Do you have **numbness** in legs/feet? | 1 |
| 3. Do you have **tingling** in legs/feet? | 1 |
| 4. Do symptoms **worsen at night**? | 1 |
| **Total ≥1** | **Positive screen** |

**Advantages**: Quick, patient-reported, identifies painful DSPN.

### MNSI (Michigan Neuropathy Screening Instrument) — **15-item Questionnaire + Exam**
| Component | Abnormal Threshold |
|-----------|--------------------|
| **Questionnaire** (15 yes/no) | **≥7/15** |
| **Exam** (inspection, monofilament, vibration, reflexes) | **≥2.5/10** |

**More comprehensive** than ANNS; used in research and some clinics.

---

## 6. Risk Stratification (IWGDF) — Links to Foot Risk

| Neuropathy Finding | Vascular Status | IWGDF Risk | Action |
|-------------------|-----------------|------------|--------|
| **No LOPS, normal vibration** | Pulses present | **Risk 0** | Annual screen |
| **LOPS only** (monofilament -ve) | Pulses present | **Risk 1** | Annual + podiatry nail care |
| **LOPS + PAD** (absent pulses) | **Absent pulses** | **Risk 2** | **3–6m podiatry MDT**; footwear |
| **LOPS + deformity** | Pulses present | **Risk 2** | **3–6m podiatry MDT**; orthotics |
| **Prior ulcer/amputation** | Any | **Risk 3** | **1–3m specialist MDT** |
| **Active ulcer/infection/Charcot** | Any | **Active Disease** | **Urgent MDT 24–48h** |

---

## 7. Electrodiagnostics (NCS) — Not Routine

| Indication | Purpose |
|------------|---------|
| **Atypical features** (asymmetric, motor-predominant, rapid progression) | Rule out CIDP, radiculopathy, vasculitis |
| **Diagnostic uncertainty** | Confirm axonal vs demyelinating |
| **Research / trials** | Objective endpoint |
| **Medicolegal** | Documentation |

**Typical DSPN NCS**: Axonal sensorimotor — ↓ SNAP/CMAP amplitudes, normal/ mildly slowed velocities.

---

## 8. Screening Intervals

| Population | Frequency |
|------------|-----------|
| **All diabetes ≥12y** | **Annual** (NICE) |
| **Risk 2 (LOPS + PAD/deformity)** | **3–6 months** (podiatry) |
| **Risk 3 (prior ulcer/amputation)** | **1–3 months** (specialist MDT) |
| **Pregnancy (pre-existing DM)** | Each trimester (foot risk increases) |
| **Post-foot surgery** | Per surgical protocol |

## 9. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **Monofilament sites** | 3 per foot: hallux, 1st & 5th met heads |
| **LOPS definition** | Monofilament -ve at **≥1 of 6 sites** |
| **Vibration test** | 128Hz fork on hallux IP joint → malleolus |
| **Ankle reflex** | Absent = neuropathy sign |
| **ANNS** | 4 questions; ≥1 yes = positive |
| **MNSI** | Questionnaire ≥7/15; Exam ≥2.5/10 |
| **IWGDF Risk 0** | No LOPS, pulses present |
| **IWGDF Risk 1** | LOPS only, pulses present |
| **IWGDF Risk 2** | LOPS + (PAD or deformity) |
| **IWGDF Risk 3** | Prior ulcer/amputation, active Charcot, critical ischaemia |
| **NCS** | Not routine; atypical features only |
