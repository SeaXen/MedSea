## 1. Definition & Exam Relevance
**Insulin Autoantibodies (IAA)** = antibodies against **native human insulin**.  
**Critical distinction**: IAA = autoantibodies (pre-insulin therapy); **IAA** vs **Insulin Antibodies (IA)** = antibodies induced by exogenous insulin therapy.  
**FCPS/MRCP relevance**: Only valid **before insulin initiation**; exam questions on timing, assay interference, paediatric sensitivity.

---

## 2. Key Concept: IAA vs Insulin Antibodies (IA)
| Feature | IAA (Autoantibodies) | IA (Insulin Antibodies) |
|---------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Origin** | Autoimmune (pre-insulin) | Induced by exogenous insulin |
| **Timing** | **Present at diagnosis, pre-treatment** | Develop **weeks–months after insulin start** |
| **Clinical significance** | Marker of autoimmune β-cell destruction | Cause of insulin resistance, erratic absorption |
| **Assay interference** | Must test **before insulin exposure** | Always positive on insulin therapy |
| **Disappearance** | Declines post-diagnosis | Persists while on insulin |

**Exam rule**: IAA **must be measured before first insulin dose** (or within 2 weeks). After insulin therapy → measures IA, not IAA.

---

## 3. Clinical Performance
| Metric | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Sensitivity (new T1DM)** | 40–60% (children) | **Highest in young children** (<5y: 60–70%) |
| **Sensitivity (adult/LADA)** | 20–30% | Low — **not useful for LADA** |
| **Specificity** | 90–95% | Lower than GAD65/IA-2/ZnT8 |
| **Age dependence** | **Strong inverse** with age | <5y: ~70%; 10–14y: ~50%; adult: <30% |
| **Persistence** | Declines rapidly after insulin start | Half-life ~6 months post-insulin |

## 4. Comparison with Other Autoantibodies
| Feature | GAD65 | IA-2 | **IAA** | ZnT8 |
|---------|-------|------|--------|------|
| **Sensitivity (children)** | 70–80% | 50–70% | **60–70% (<5y)** | 60–80% |
| **Sensitivity (adult)** | 70–80% | 30–50% | 20–30% | 50–60% |
| **Specificity** | 95–98% | >98% | 90–95% | 95–98% |
| **Pre-insulin requirement** | No | No | **YES (critical)** | No |
| **LADA utility** | **High** | Low | **Low** | Moderate |
| **Assay type** | ELISA/RBA | ELISA/RBA | **RBA (radiolabelled insulin)** | ELISA |

---

## 5. Assay Methodology — Radiobinding Assay (RBA)
| Step | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Tracer** | ¹²⁵I-labelled human insulin (A14 Tyr or B25 Tyr) |
| **Incubation** | Patient serum + tracer + excess unlabelled insulin (competition) |
| **Separation** | Protein A/G precipitation or PEG precipitation |
| **Readout** | Gamma counter; % bound = IAA level |
| **Units** | **nU/mL** (nano-units/mL) or **JDF units** |
| **Standardization** | WHO Standard (NIBSC 83/500) |

**False positives**: **Proinsulin cross-reactivity** (some assays); **insulin therapy** (exogenous insulin → IA, not IAA).

---

## 6. Clinical Utility & Indications
| Scenario | IAA Role |
|----------|----------|
| **Child <10y, new diabetes** | **High yield** — 60–70% positive; confirms T1DM |
| **Adult new diabetes** | Low yield (<30%) — prefer GAD65 + IA-2 + ZnT8 |
| **LADA diagnosis** | **Not recommended** — low sensitivity |
| **Pre-symptomatic screening (trial)** | Part of multiple-Ab panel (DPT-1, TrialNet) |
| **Monogenic diabetes exclusion** | Negative panel supports monogenic (but not definitive) |
| **Post-transplant recurrence** | Not used (on insulin → IA positive) |

---

## 7. Interpretation Algorithm
```
New diabetes, insulin-naïve
    ↓
Measure IAA (with GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8)
    ↓
IAA POSITIVE
    ├─ Child <10y + other Abs → **T1DM confirmed**
    ├─ Adult + GAD65/IA-2/ZnT8 + → **T1DM/LADA confirmed**
    └─ Isolated IAA + adult → **Low specificity**; consider assay artefact, repeat
IAA NEGATIVE
    ├─ Child + GAD65/IA-2/ZnT8 + → T1DM (IAA absent in ~40%)
    ├─ Adult + GAD65 + → LADA likely
    └─ All Abs negative → Consider T2DM, monogenic, secondary
```

---

## 8. Special Considerations
| Situation | Impact on IAA |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Exogenous insulin (any)** | **Invalidates IAA** → measures insulin antibodies (IA) |
| **Porcine/bovine insulin (historical)** | Higher immunogenicity → more IA |
| **Insulin analogues (lispro, aspart, glargine)** | Lower immunogenicity but still induce IA |
| **High proinsulin (insulinoma)** | Cross-reactivity in some assays → false positive |
| **Pregnancy** | Transient IAA possible; unclear significance |

---

## 9. FCPS/MRCP Exam Pearls
- **IAA = pre-insulin only** — **most tested fact**: "Patient on insulin for 3 months, IAA positive" → this is **insulin antibodies (IA)**, not IAA
- **Highest sensitivity in young children** (<5 years) — paediatric preference
- **Low sensitivity in adults/LADA** — don't use for LADA screen (use GAD65)
- **Assay = RBA (radiobinding)** — uses radiolabelled insulin
- **Specificity 90–95%** — lower than GAD65/IA-2/ZnT8
- **Declines after insulin start** — half-life ~6 months
- **Multiple Abs**: IAA + GAD65 + IA-2 = very high predictive value
- **Not affected by insulin type** for IAA (but insulin therapy induces IA)
