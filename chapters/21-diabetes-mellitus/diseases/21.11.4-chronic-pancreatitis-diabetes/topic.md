# Chronic pancreatitis diabetes

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Diagnose type 3c diabetes from chronic pancreatitis
- [ ] Recognise exocrine insufficiency features
- [ ] Apply management: pancreatic enzymes, insulin, nutritional support
- [ ] Differentiate from T1DM, T2DM, other secondary DM

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Definition** | Diabetes secondary to chronic pancreatitis with exocrine insufficiency |
| **Incidence** | 50% of CP develop diabetes at 20 years |
| **Mechanism** | Pancreatic destruction -> loss of islets + exocrine tissue |
| **Aetiology** | Alcohol (70%), smoking, genetic (PRSS1, SPINK1, CFTR), idiopathic |

## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading
| Stage | Features |
|-------|----------|
| **Early** | Recurrent pancreatitis; normal glucose |
| **Developing** | Impaired glucose tolerance; mild exocrine insufficiency |
| **Established** | Diabetes + exocrine insufficiency (faecal elastase <200) |
| **Advanced** | Brittle diabetes; severe exocrine insufficiency; malnutrition |

## 5. Diagnosis & Investigations
| Test | Type 3c | T1DM | T2DM |
|------|---------|------|------|
| **Faecal elastase** | **<200 mcg/g** | Normal | Normal |
| **CT/MRI pancreas** | Calcification, atrophy, duct dilatation | Normal | Normal |
| **Autoantibodies** | Negative | Positive (2+) | Negative |
| **C-peptide** | Low | Low/absent | Normal/high |
| **Glucagon** | Low | Low | Normal/high |
| **HbA1c** | Often elevated | Elevated | Elevated |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **T1DM** | Autoantibodies +, low C-peptide, no exocrine insufficiency |
| **T2DM** | Obese, insulin resistance, negative Abs, normal exocrine |
| **Pancreatic cancer DM** | New-onset >50y, weight loss, imaging mass |
| **Cystic fibrosis DM** | CFTR mutation; respiratory/GI symptoms |

## 7. Management

### Exocrine Replacement (Essential)
| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Pancreatic enzymes** | **Enteric-coated microspheres**; 25,000-50,000 U lipase/meal; with snacks |
| **Dosing** | 50,000 U lipase main meal; 25,000 U snack; titrate to symptoms |
| **Acid suppression** | PPI (improves enzyme efficacy) |
| **Monitoring** | Stool frequency/consistency; weight; fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) |

### Diabetes Management
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Insulin** | **Preferred** (flexible, physiologic); high doses often needed due to ↓glucagon |
| **SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA** | Caution (weight loss, GI effects); consider if CVD/CKD |
| **Sulfonylureas** | Avoid (hypo risk with erratic absorption) |
| **Metformin** | If eGFR allows; weight neutral |
| **Glucagon** | **Ineffective** (alpha cell loss); use IV dextrose for severe hypo |

### Brittle Diabetes Management
| Strategy | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **CSII** | Preferred for brittle control; predictive low suspend |
| **CGM** | Essential; predictive alerts |
| **Hypoglycaemia** | High risk (↓glucagon, ↓counter-regulation); glucagon **ineffective** |
| **Glucagon** | **Ineffective** (alpha cell loss); use IV dextrose |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Type 3c = T2DM?** | NO - exocrine insufficiency, negative Abs, low glucagon |
| **Metformin in type 3c?** | If eGFR allows; but insulin preferred for flexibility |
| **Glucagon for hypo?** | INEFFECTIVE - alpha cell loss; use IV dextrose |
