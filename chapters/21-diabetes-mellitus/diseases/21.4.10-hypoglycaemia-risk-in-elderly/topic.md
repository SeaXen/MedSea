## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading

### Risk Factors in Elderly
| Category | Risk Factors |
|----------|--------------|
| **Renal** | eGFR < 45 (↓ insulin clearance); dialysis (glucose-free dialysate) |
| **Medications** | Insulin, sulfonylureas, beta-blockers (mask symptoms), ACEi/ARB |
| **Cognitive** | Dementia, MCI (impaired recognition/reporting) |
| **Physiological** | ↓ counter-regulation (glucagon/epinephrine); ↓ renal gluconeogenesis |
| **Social** | Living alone; erratic meals; carer-dependent; alcohol |
| **Comorbidities** | CKD, HF, liver disease; malnutrition; polypharmacy |

### Severity Grading (ADA)
| Level | Glucose | Clinical |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **1 (Alert)** | < 3.9 mmol/L | Self-treat |
| **2 (Clinical)** | < 3.0 mmol/L | Impaired judgement; self-treat possible |
| **3 (Severe)** | Any (requires help) | Unconscious, seizure, needs 3rd party |

## 5. Diagnosis & Investigations
| Tool | Role |
|------|-------|
| **CGM** | Gold standard detection (nocturnal, asymptomatic); predictive alerts |
| **SMBG** | 4-6x/day if insulin/SU; pre-meal, bedtime, 3am |
| **HbA1c** | Falsely LOW in CKD/ESRD (↓RBC lifespan); use fructosamine/GA |
| **Renal function** | eGFR; adjust insulin/SU doses |
| **Cognitive screen** | MoCA/MoCA; identify impaired recognition |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Falls** | Hypoglycaemia = common cause; check glucose |
| **Confusion/delirium** | Check glucose first (reversible cause) |
| **Stroke/TIA** | Focal neurology; glucose may be high/low |
| **Sepsis** | Fever, tachycardia; glucose variable |
| **Medication toxicity** | Digoxin, opioids, benzodiazepines |

## 7. Management

### Prevention Strategies
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Elderly DM] --> B[Assess hypo risk factors]
    B --> C{On SU/insulin?}
    C -->|Yes| D[Review doses; STOP SU; reduce insulin]
    C -->|No| E[Review other meds (beta-blockers, ACEi)]
    D --> F[Simplify regimen: once-daily basal; stop SU]
    F --> G[Individualised HbA1c target per frailty]
    G --> H[CGM with predictive alerts]
    H --> I[Carer education: hypo recognition, glucagon]
    I --> J[Regular review: 3-monthly HbA1c; CGM review]
```

### Prevention Strategies by Domain
| Domain | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| **Medication** | STOP sulfonylureas; reduce insulin 20-50%; once-daily basal; stop bolus |
| **Targets** | Relax HbA1c per frailty (fit 53, mild 58, mod 64, sev 69 mmol/mol) |
| **Technology** | CGM with predictive alerts (low glucose suspend if pump); SMBG 4x/day if no CGM |
| **Renal** | Insulin dose ↓ 25-50% if eGFR < 45; stop SU if eGFR < 45 |
| **Cognitive** | Carer-administered insulin; CGM with remote monitoring; simplified regimen |
| **Social** | Regular meals; bedtime snack; alcohol moderation; glucagon available |

### Nocturnal Hypoglycaemia Prevention
| Strategy | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Bedtime snack** | 15-20g complex carb if bedtime glucose < 7 mmol/L |
| **Basal insulin** | Reduce evening dose; switch to degludec/U-300 (stable) |
| **CGM alerts** | Low glucose suspend (pump); predictive alerts (CGM) |
| **Bedtime glucose** | Target 7-10 mmol/L at bedtime |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Elderly = same hypo symptoms?** | NO - autonomic blunted; neuroglycopenic (confusion, falls) predominant |
| **HbA1c reliable in elderly CKD?** | NO - falsely LOW (↓ RBC lifespan, ESA); use fructosamine/GA |
| **Glucagon in elderly?** | Works if glycogen present; ineffective in starvation/alcohol/liver failure |

**Mnemonic: HYPO-ELDERLY-RISK**
- **H**ypoglycaemia: 5x risk in frail; 2x in >75y
- **Y**ield: atypical presentation (confusion, falls, not tremor)
- **P**olypharmacy: SU + insulin + beta-blockers = highest risk
- **O**ld age: blunted counter-regulation (glucagon/epinephrine down)
- **E**lderly CKD: insulin clearance down; reduce dose 25-50%
- **L**iving alone: no one to recognize/help
- **D**ementia: cognitive impairment = can't recognize/report
- **E**lderly frail: Rockwood >=5 = 5x hypo risk
- **R**enal: eGFR<45 -> insulin reduce 25-50%; stop SU
- **L**ow glucose at night: bedtime 7-10; CGM alerts; snack
- **Y**ear: annual hypoglycaemia review
- **R**enal: fructosamine/GA if HbA1c unreliable
- **I**nsulin: reduce 25-50% in CKD; stop bolus; simplify basal
- **S**top sulfonylureas **FIRST** (highest risk)
- **K**now: glucagon ineffective if glycogen depleted (starvation/alcohol)</think>
