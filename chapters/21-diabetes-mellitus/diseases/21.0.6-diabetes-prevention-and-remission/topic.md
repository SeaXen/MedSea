## High-Yield Exam Points (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Facts |
|-------|-----------|
| **Teplizumab** | Anti-CD3 monoclonal antibody · Delays stage 3 T1D by ~2 years in stage 2 (≥2 autoantibodies + dysglycaemia) · FDA approved |
| **T1D immunotherapy** | Abatacept, rituximab, alefacept, verapamil, IL-2, ATG/G-CSF · Mostly delay, not prevent |
| **DPP (US)** | Lifestyle 58% reduction · Metformin 31% reduction · Weight loss 7% + 150min/wk activity |
| **Da Qing (China)** | Diet, exercise, diet+exercise · 43% reduction at 20y · CVD/mortality benefit |
| **Finnish DPS** | Intensive lifestyle · 58% reduction · Weight loss, fibre, fat reduction, exercise |
| **Metformin prevention** | BMI ≥35, age <60, women with GDM history · Less effective than lifestyle |
| **DiRECT trial** | Low-calorie diet (825-853 kcal/d) 12-20wk → food reintroduction → maintenance · 46% remission at 1y, 36% at 2y |
| **Remission criteria** | HbA1c <48mmol/mol (6.5%) off all glucose-lowering meds ≥3 months · ADA/Diabetes UK consensus |
| **Low-calorie diet** | Formula meal replacements (soups/shakes) · 15kg mean weight loss → remission |
| **Maintenance** | Continued weight maintenance, physical activity, regular monitoring · Relapse if weight regain |
