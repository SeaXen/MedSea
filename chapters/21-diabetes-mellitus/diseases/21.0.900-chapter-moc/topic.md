## Study Plan / Topic Queue

### Core High-Yield Topics (FCPS/MRCP Priority)

- [ ] **Classification & Diagnostic Criteria** — ADA/WHO/EASD criteria, HbA1c vs glucose, prediabetes categories, monogenic/secondary diabetes red flags
- [ ] **Type 1 Diabetes Pathophysiology** — Autoimmune destruction, staging (1-3), autoantibodies (GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, IAA), LADA, genetic susceptibility (HLA-DR3/DR4)
- [ ] **Type 2 Diabetes Pathophysiology** — Insulin resistance, beta-cell failure, incretin defect, lipotoxicity/glucotoxicity, TCF7L2 and polygenic risk
- [ ] **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)** — Diagnostic criteria (glucose, ketones, pH, bicarbonate), severity grading, management protocol (fluids, insulin, K+, monitoring), cerebral oedema, euglycaemic DKA (SGLT2i)
- [ ] **Hyperosmolar Hyperglycaemic State (HHS)** — Diagnostic criteria, HHS vs DKA differentiation, management (lower insulin, higher fluids), mortality predictors
- [ ] **Hypoglycaemia** — Classification (level 1/2/3), glucose counter-regulation failure, hypoglycaemia unawareness, glucagon (IM/IN/auto-injector), hospital insulin errors
- [ ] **Type 1 Insulin Therapy** — Basal-bolus principles, insulin analogues (rapid: lispro/aspart/glulisine, long: glargine U-100/U-300, degludec, ultra-long), CSII/pump therapy, carbohydrate counting, CGM-guided dosing
- [ ] **Type 2 Treatment Algorithm** — ADA/EASD 2023+ consensus, NICE NG28, CVOT-guided (SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA), CKD/HF-guided therapy, metabolic surgery indications
- [ ] **Oral Glucose-Lowering Agents** — Metformin (contraindications, B12, lactic acidosis), sulfonylureas (hypoglycaemia, weight), DPP-4i (neutral), SGLT2i (CV/renal benefit, genital infections, euglycaemic DKA), TZDs (fluid retention, fracture, bladder cancer), alpha-glucosidase, meglitinides
- [ ] **Injectable Non-Insulin Therapy** — GLP-1 RA (semaglutide, liraglutide, dulaglutide, CV benefit, weight loss, pancreatitis risk), dual GIP/GLP-1 (tirzepatide), amylin analogues
- [ ] **Insulin in Type 2 Diabetes** — Basal initiation, basal-bolus intensification, pre-mixed, concentrated insulins (U-200/U-300/U-500), degludec vs glargine U-300
- [ ] **Diabetic Retinopathy** — NPDR vs PDR, ETDRS grading, diabetic macular oedema, screening intervals, treatment (laser, anti-VEGF: ranibizumab/aflibercept, vitrectomy)
- [ ] **Diabetic Kidney Disease (DKD)** — KDIGO staging (GFR + albuminuria), ACR categories, GFR categories, management (ACEi/ARB max dose, SGLT2i, finerenone, GLP-1 RA), proteinuria reduction targets
- [ ] **Diabetic Neuropathy** — DSPN (stocking-glove), autonomic (cardiovascular: HRV, orthostatic; GI: gastroparesis; GU: ED, bladder), screening (10g monofilament, vibration, ANNS), neuropathic pain (duloxetine, pregabalin, gabapentin, amitriptyline)
- [ ] **Diabetic Foot Disease** — IWGDF/Scottish risk classification (low/moderate/high/active), Wagner/University of Texas DFU classification, offloading (TCC gold standard), infection (PEDIS), revascularisation, Charcot neuroarthropathy (Eichenholtz), MDT amputation prevention
- [ ] **Cardiovascular Disease in Diabetes** — Diabetes as CV risk equivalent, QRISK3/ASCVD Pooled Cohort, statin intensity (high-intensity atorvastatin 40-80mg), BP targets (<130/80 ADA/ESC, <140/90 NICE), aspirin primary prevention (10-year risk >10%), SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA for CV risk reduction
- [ ] **Heart Failure in Diabetes** — HFrEF vs HFpEF, SGLT2i (DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced/Preserved, DELIVER), GLP-1 RA (FIGHT, STEP-HFpEF), diagnostic challenges (diastolic dysfunction)
- [ ] **Diabetes in Pregnancy** — GDM screening (IADPSG 75g OGTT, NICE risk factors + OGTT), targets (FBG <5.3, 1h <7.8, 2h <6.4 mmol/L), management (diet → metformin → insulin), pre-existing DM (pre-conception HbA1c <48mmol/mol, folic acid 5mg), postpartum OGTT 6-13 weeks
- [ ] **Diabetes in Elderly** — Frailty-guided HbA1c targets (Fit: <53, Mild frailty: <58, Moderate/severe: <64), deintensification/deprescribing, hypoglycaemia avoidance, care home management
- [ ] **Diabetes in CKD/Dialysis** — HbA1c pitfalls (falsely low in CKD/ESRD, use fructosamine/GA), drug dosing adjustments (metformin eGFR cut-offs, SGLT2i eGFR thresholds, GLP-1 RA safe, insulin dose reduction), dialysis glucose management
- [ ] **Monogenic Diabetes** — MODY (HNF1A MODY3: sulfonylurea responsive, GCK MODY2: no treatment, HNF4A MODY1), neonatal diabetes (KCNJ11/ABCC8: sulfonylurea responsive), mitochondrial diabetes (m.3243A>G), genetic testing indications
- [ ] **Secondary Diabetes** — Type 3c (pancreatic: chronic pancreatitis, cancer, post-pancreatectomy, CF), endocrine (Cushing, acromegaly, thyrotoxicosis, phaeochromocytoma, glucagonoma), drug-induced (steroids, antipsychotics, calcineurin inhibitors, ICI, protease inhibitors, thiazides)
- [ ] **Type 2 Diabetes Prevention & Remission** — DPP (lifestyle 58%, metformin 31%), Da Qing, Finnish DPS, DiRECT trial (46% remission at 1 year, 36% at 2 years), remission criteria (HbA1c <48mmol/mol off glucose-lowering meds x3 months), maintenance
- [ ] **Diabetes Technology** — rtCGM vs isCGM, CGM metrics (TIR >70%, TBR <4%, TAR <25%, GMI, CV <36%), hybrid closed-loop (670G, 780G, CamAPS FX, Loop), connected pens, digital platforms
- [ ] **Monitoring & Quality of Care** — Individualised HbA1c targets, annual review (retinopathy, nephropathy ACR/eGFR, neuropathy/foot, CV risk), structured education (DAFNE type 1, DESMOND/X-PERT type 2), psychosocial (diabetes distress, depression, eating disorders/diabulimia)

## Key Algorithms & Scoring Systems (Memorize for Viva)

| Algorithm/Score | Use Case | Key Cut-offs |
|----------------|----------|--------------|
| **ADA/WHO Diagnostic Criteria** | Diabetes diagnosis | FPG ≥7.0, 2h-OGTT ≥11.1, HbA1c ≥48mmol/mol (6.5%), random ≥11.1 + symptoms |
| **Prediabetes Criteria** | Risk stratification | IFG 6.1-6.9, IGT 7.8-11.0, HbA1c 39-47mmol/mol (5.7-6.4%) |
| **DKA Severity** | DKA triage | Mild: pH 7.25-7.30, K+ >3.5; Moderate: pH 7.0-7.24, K+ 3.0-3.5; Severe: pH <7.0, K+ <3.0 |
| **HHS Criteria** | HHS diagnosis | Glucose >30mmol/L, osmolality >320mOsm/kg, pH >7.3, bicarbonate >18, no significant ketonaemia |
| **KDIGO DKD Staging** | DKD prognosis | G1-G5 (GFR) × A1-A3 (ACR); e.g., G3aA2 = moderate risk |
| **ETDRS Retinopathy Severity** | Retinopathy grading | 10-12: no DR; 20-35: mild/mod NPDR; 43-53: severe NPDR; 61+: PDR |
| **IWGDF Foot Risk** | Foot risk stratification | 0: no LOPS/PAD; 1: LOPS; 2: LOPS + PAD/deformity; 3: history ulcer/amputation |
| **Wagner DFU Classification** | Ulcer depth | 0: pre-ulcer; 1: superficial; 2: deep to tendon; 3: bone/abscess; 4: forefoot gangrene; 5: whole foot gangrene |
| **University of Texas DFU** | Ulcer depth + infection + ischaemia | Grade 0-3 × Stage A-D (A=clean, B=infected, C=ischaemic, D=infected+ischaemic) |
| **CHA₂DS₂-VASc** | AF stroke risk (often asked in DM) | CHF, HTN, Age≥75, DM, Stroke/TIA, Vascular, Age 65-74, Sex |
| **ADA/EASD 2023 Algorithm** | T2DM treatment selection | ASCVD/HF/CKD → SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA; no CV → metformin + lifestyle → add based on comorbidities |
| **NICE NG28 T2DM Pathway** | UK T2DM algorithm | Metformin → dual → triple → insulin; SGLT2i if ASCVD/HF/CKD |
| **CGM Targets (ATTD)** | CGM interpretation | TIR 70-180mg/dL >70%; TBR <54mg/dL <4%; TAR >180mg/dL <25%; CV <36% |
| **Diabetes Remission Criteria** | Remission definition | HbA1c <48mmol/mol (6.5%) off all glucose-lowering meds ≥3 months |
| **Folic Acid in Pre-existing DM Pregnancy** | Neural tube defect prevention | 5mg daily from pre-conception to 12 weeks gestation |

## High-Yield Viva Topics (FCPS/MRCP)

### Must-Know Definitions

- [ ] Diabetes mellitus (ADA/WHO) vs impaired glucose regulation
- [ ] DKA vs HHS vs mixed DKA-HHS
- [ ] DKA severity grading (mild/moderate/severe)
- [ ] DKD staging (KDIGO G1-G5 × A1-A3)
- [ ] Retinopathy: NPDR vs PDR vs DMO
- [ ] Foot risk: IWGDF 0-3 vs Wagner 0-5 vs UT classification
- [ ] Hypoglycaemia levels (Level 1: <3.9, Level 2: <3.0, Level 3: severe requiring assistance)
- [ ] Diabetes remission (ADA/Diabetes UK consensus)
- [ ] LADA vs classic type 1 vs type 2
- [ ] MODY vs neonatal diabetes vs type 1 vs type 2

### Must-Know Doses/Durations

- [ ] DKA: 0.9% NaCl 1L/hr initially then adjusted; fixed-rate insulin infusion 0.1U/kg/hr; K+ replacement (40mmol/L if K+ 3.5-5.5, 60mmol/L if <3.5, hold if >5.5); bicarbonate if pH <7.0
- [ ] HHS: 0.9% NaCl 1L/hr (lower insulin: 0.05U/kg/hr); monitor osmolality drop <3mOsm/kg/hr
- [ ] Hypoglycaemia: Glucagon 1mg IM/SC (adult), 0.5mg <25kg/child; nasal glucagon 3mg; oral carbs 15-20g if conscious
- [ ] Metformin: Start 500mg OD, titrate to 2g/day max; hold if eGFR <30, caution 30-45
- [ ] SGLT2i: Empagliflozin 10/25mg, dapagliflozin 10mg, canagliflozin 100/300mg; eGFR cut-offs (initiate ≥45/30, continue ≥20/15)
- [ ] GLP-1 RA: Semaglutide SC 0.25→0.5→1mg weekly; oral 3→7→14mg daily; liraglutide 0.6→1.2→1.8mg daily; dulaglutide 0.75/1.5mg weekly
- [ ] Tirzepatide: 2.5→5→7.5→10→12.5→15mg weekly
- [ ] Insulin analogues: Rapid (lispro/aspart/glulisine) 0-15min pre-meal; Long (glargine U-100/U-300, degludec) once daily; Pre-mixed BD
- [ ] Folic acid 5mg OD pre-conception to 12 weeks in pre-existing DM pregnancy
- [ ] Aspirin 75mg OD primary prevention if 10-year ASCVD risk >10% (ADA) or age >50 with risk factor (ESC)
- [ ] Statin: Atorvastatin 40-80mg (high-intensity) for primary prevention age 40-75 with DM

## 6. Chapter-Level SBAs (Single Best Answer)

> These SBAs test application of **Diabetes Mellitus**-specific scoring systems and clinical decision algorithms.

**SBA1.** A patient with diabetes diagnosis requires risk stratification using **ADA/WHO Diagnostic Criteria**. What is the clinical significance of the threshold value?
A. ADA/WHO Diagnostic Criteria < threshold = no clinical action needed
B. **ADA/WHO Diagnostic Criteria ≥ threshold = FPG ≥7.0, 2h-OGTT ≥11.1, HbA1c ≥48mmol/mol (6.5%), random ≥11.1 + symptoms**
C. ADA/WHO Diagnostic Criteria score is purely prognostic without therapeutic implications
D. ADA/WHO Diagnostic Criteria should only be calculated by specialists
E. ADA/WHO Diagnostic Criteria replaces clinical judgement entirely

> **Answer:** B
> **Rationale:** ADA/WHO Diagnostic Criteria thresholds guide clinical decisions: FPG ≥7.0, 2h-OGTT ≥11.1, HbA1c ≥48mmol/mol (6.5%), random ≥11.1 + symptoms.

**SBA2.** A patient with risk stratification requires risk stratification using **Prediabetes Criteria**. What is the clinical significance of the threshold value?

B. **Prediabetes Criteria ≥ threshold = IFG 6.1-6.9, IGT 7.8-11.0, HbA1c 39-47mmol/mol (5.7-6.4%)**
C. Prediabetes Criteria score is purely prognostic without therapeutic implications
D. Prediabetes Criteria should only be calculated by specialists
E. Prediabetes Criteria replaces clinical judgement entirely

> **Answer:** B
> **Rationale:** Prediabetes Criteria thresholds guide clinical decisions: IFG 6.1-6.9, IGT 7.8-11.0, HbA1c 39-47mmol/mol (5.7-6.4%).

**SBA3.** A patient with dka triage requires risk stratification using **DKA Severity**. What is the clinical significance of the threshold value?
