# KCNJ11 and ABCC8 neonatal diabetes

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise neonatal diabetes (<6 months) due to KCNJ11/ABCC8
- [ ] Explain sulfonylurea responsiveness mechanism
- [ ] Apply genetic testing and management
- [ ] Recognise DEND/iDEND syndromes

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Definition** | Diabetes diagnosed <6 months age |
| **Genetics** | Activating mutations in KCNJ11 (Kir6.2) or ABCC8 (SUR1) |
| **Incidence** | ~1:100,000-500,000 live births; 40-50% of neonatal DM |
| **Mechanism** | Mutated KATP channel fails to close -> ↓insulin secretion |

## 3. Clinical Features / Presentation
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Age** | <6 months (often <1 month) |
| **Presentation** | Hyperglycaemia, dehydration, ketoacidosis |
| **Birth weight** | Often low (reduced fetal insulin -> ↓growth) |
| **Neurological** | DEND (developmental delay, epilepsy, neonatal diabetes) |
| **Growth** | Failure to thrive |

## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading

### Neurological Spectrum
| Syndrome | Features | Mutation Severity |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Permanent Neonatal Diabetes (PNDM)** | Diabetes only | Mild |
| **iDEND** | Diabetes + developmental delay | Moderate |
| **DEND** | Developmental delay, epilepsy, neonatal diabetes | Severe |

## 5. Diagnosis & Investigations
| Test | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **Genetic testing** | KCNJ11 or ABCC8 activating mutation |
| **Autoantibodies** | Negative |
| **C-peptide** | Low/undetectable |
| **CT/MRI brain** | Normal or subtle changes (DEND) |
| **EEG** | Epileptiform activity (DEND/iDEND) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **6q24 TNDM** | Remission by 18mo; imprinting defect |
| **INS mutation** | No SU response; insulin only |
| **GCK-MODY** | Mild fasting hyperglycaemia; no treatment |
| **Transient neonatal DM** | 6q24 imprinting; remission by 18mo |

## 7. Management

### Sulfonylurea Switch (90% Success)
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Drug** | Gliclazide MR (tolbutamide if <10kg) |
| **Starting dose** | 0.5-1.0 mg/kg/day |
| **Titration** | Increase weekly to max 2-3 mg/kg/day |
| **Monitoring** | Pre-prandial glucose; HbA1c 3-monthly; neuro assessment |
| **Insulin taper** | Gradual over 2-4 weeks as SU established |

### DEND/iDEND Specific
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **Epilepsy** | Anticonvulsants (sodium channel blockers may help) |
| **Developmental** | Early intervention; physiotherapy, speech therapy |

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **Genes** | KCNJ11 (Kir6.2), ABCC8 (SUR1) - KATP channel subunits |
| **PNDM** | Diabetes <6mo; no remission |
| **TNDM** | 6q24 imprinting; remission by 18mo, relapse later |
| **Sulfonylurea** | **90% switch successfully** (KCNJ11/ABCC8) |
| **DEND/iDEND** | Developmental delay, epilepsy; higher SU doses needed |
| **Mechanism** | Mutated KATP stays open -> ↓insulin; SU closes channel |

## 9. Viva Questions
| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **What genes cause neonatal diabetes?** | KCNJ11 (Kir6.2), ABCC8 (SUR1) - KATP channel subunits |
| **What is the sulfonylurea response rate?** | ~90% |
| **What is DEND syndrome?** | Developmental delay, Epilepsy, Neonatal Diabetes |
| **How do you switch from insulin to sulfonylurea?** | Gradual taper over 2-4 weeks; SU gliclazide 0.5-1mg/kg/day |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **All neonatal DM = KCNJ11/ABCC8?** | NO - 6q24 TNDM, INS, GCK, PDX1 also |
| **All KCNJ11 = PNDM?** | Some TNDM also have KCNJ11 mutations |
| **Sulfonylurea = cure?** | No - life-long treatment; dose may need increase with age |
