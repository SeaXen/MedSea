# Increased HbA1c Risk Category (Prediabetes by HbA1c)

> [!info]
> **HbA1c 39–47 mmol/mol (5.7–6.4%)** — ADA prediabetes category; convenient but misses isolated IFG/IGT; unreliable in haemoglobinopathies, anaemia, CKD, pregnancy.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:

## 2. Diagnostic Cut-offs

| Organisation | Diabetes | Prediabetes / High Risk | Normal |
|--------------|----------|-------------------------|--------|
| **ADA 2024** | ≥48 mmol/mol (≥6.5%) | **39–47 mmol/mol (5.7–6.4%)** | <39 (<5.7%) |
| **NICE NG28** | ≥48 mmol/mol (≥6.5%) | **42–47 mmol/mol (6.0–6.4%)** | <42 (<6.0%) |
| **WHO** | ≥48 mmol/mol | Not endorsed for diagnosis | — |
| **Diabetes UK** | ≥48 mmol/mol | **42–47 mmol/mol** (high risk) | <42 |

**Key difference**: ADA uses 39 mmol/mol (5.7%) lower threshold; NICE/Diabetes UK use 42 mmol/mol (6.0%).

---

## 3. Conditions Affecting HbA1c Reliability

| Condition | Effect on HbA1c | Mechanism | Alternative Test |
|-----------|----------------|-----------|------------------|
| **Haemoglobinopathies (HbS, HbC, HbE, thalassaemia)** | **Falsely LOW** | Reduced RBC lifespan / variant Hb | FPG, OGTT, fructosamine |
| **Iron deficiency anaemia** | **Falsely HIGH** | ↑ RBC lifespan, altered glycation | FPG, OGTT (treat iron first) |
| **Haemolytic anaemia** | **Falsely LOW** | ↓ RBC lifespan | FPG, OGTT |
| **Recent blood loss/transfusion** | **Falsely LOW** | ↓ Mean RBC age | FPG, OGTT (wait 3 months) |
| **CKD (esp. dialysis)** | **Falsely LOW** | ↓ RBC lifespan, ESA use | FPG, OGTT, fructosamine |
| **Pregnancy (2nd/3rd trimester)** | **Falsely LOW** | ↑ RBC turnover, haemodilution | OGTT (diagnostic standard) |
| **High-dose vitamin C/E** | **Falsely LOW** | Antioxidant effect on glycation | FPG, OGTT |
| **Opioids, high-dose aspirin** | **Falsely HIGH** | Chemical modification | FPG, OGTT |
| **Splenectomy** | **Falsely HIGH** | ↑ RBC lifespan | FPG, OGTT |

---

## 4. Ethnic Differences

- **African-Caribbean / Black**: HbA1c ~0.3–0.4% (3–4 mmol/mol) **higher** at same mean glucose
- **South Asian**: Slightly higher at same glucose
- **Implication**: Same HbA1c cut-off may over-diagnose prediabetes in Black populations; consider FPG/OGTT confirmation

---

## 5. HbA1c vs FPG/OGTT Discordance

| Scenario | Interpretation | Action |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| **HbA1c 39–47, FPG normal** | Isolated HbA1c elevation | Repeat HbA1c; check for falsely high causes (IDA, splenectomy); do OGTT |
| **HbA1c normal, FPG 5.6–6.9** | Isolated IFG | Do OGTT to exclude IGT |
| **HbA1c normal, 2-hr 7.8–11.0** | Isolated IGT | Confirm with repeat OGTT |
| **All three discordant** | Analytical/pre-analytical issue | Repeat all; check RBC indices, haemoglobinopathy screen |

**Concordance**: Only ~50–70% agreement between HbA1c and FPG/OGTT for prediabetes.

---

## 6. Progression Risk

| HbA1c Range | 5-Year T2DM Risk | CVD Risk |
|-------------|------------------|----------|
| <39 (<5.7%) | <5% | Baseline |
| **39–41 (5.7–5.9%)** | 9–15% | 1.2× |
| **42–47 (6.0–6.4%)** | 25–50% | 1.5–2.0× |
| ≥48 (≥6.5%) | Diabetes | 2–4× |

---

## 7. Screening & Follow-up

**Who to screen**: Same as IFG/IGT (age ≥45, BMI ≥25 + risk factors, etc.)

**Intervals**:
| Result | Repeat Interval |
|--------|-----------------|
| Normal (<39 ADA, <42 NICE) | 3 years |
| **39–47 (ADA) / 42–47 (NICE)** | **Annually** |
| Previous GDM | 1–3 yearly |

---

## 8. Management

**Lifestyle** (same evidence as IFG/IGT):
- DPP, DPS, Da Qing all included HbA1c-defined prediabetes subsets
- Weight loss 5–7%, 150 min/week activity, Mediterranean/DASH diet

**Metformin** (ADA):
- HbA1c ≥42 mmol/mol (6.0%), BMI ≥30, age 25–59
- Prior GDM
- Not NICE-recommended in UK

| Topic | Key Point |

| **ADA vs NICE HbA1c prediabetes** | ADA 39–47 (5.7–6.4%), NICE 42–47 (6.0–6.4%) |

| **Falsely HIGH HbA1c** | Iron deficiency, splenectomy, opioids |
| **Pregnancy** | **OGTT only** — HbA1c not diagnostic |
| **Anaemia** | Treat iron deficiency first, then recheck HbA1c |
| **Ethnicity** | Black African-Caribbean: HbA1c reads higher at same glucose |

---

## 10. When HbA1c Cannot Be Used (Must Use FPG/OGTT)

1. **Pregnancy** (all trimesters for diagnosis; 1st trimester screening only)
2. **Haemoglobinopathies** (HbSS, HbSC, thalassaemia major)
3. **Significant anaemia** (Hb <100 g/L) — treat first
4. **CKD stage 4–5 / dialysis**
5. **Recent blood transfusion / haemorrhage** (≤3 months)
6. **Children and adolescents** (limited validation)
7. **Acute illness / stress hyperglycaemia**
