# Aspirin Primary Prevention in Diabetes

> [!info]
> **ASCEND trial (2018)**: Aspirin 100mg ↓ serious vascular events 12% (HR 0.88) but ↑ major bleeding 29% (HR 1.29) → **net clinical benefit marginal**; NICE: do not offer routinely; ADA: consider if high CVD risk + low bleeding risk.

## 2. Key Trial Evidence

| Trial | Population | Aspirin Dose | CV Outcome | Bleeding Outcome | Conclusion |
|-------|------------|--------------|------------|------------------|------------|
| **ASCEND** (2018) | **15,480 T2DM, no CVD** | 100mg | **↓ 12%** serious vascular events (8.5% vs 9.6%; HR 0.88) | **↑ 29%** major bleeding (4.1% vs 3.2%; HR 1.29) | Net benefit marginal |
| **ASPREE** (2018) | 19,114 **>70y** (no CVD/dementia) | 100mg | No benefit (HR 0.95) | **↑ 38%** major bleeding | Harm > benefit in elderly |
| **ARRIVE** (2018) | 12,546 **moderate risk** (no diabetes) | 100mg | No benefit (HR 0.96) | ↑ bleeding | No role in moderate risk |
| **ACCEPT-D** (2020) | 5,183 **T1DM/T2DM, no CVD** | 100mg | ↓ CV events (HR 0.82) | ↑ major bleeding (HR 1.45) | Similar to ASCEND |

**Meta-analyses**: ~10% CV reduction, ~30% major bleeding increase → NNT ~100–200 for CV benefit, NNH ~100–200 for major bleed.

## 3. Guideline Recommendations

### NICE NG28 / CG181 (UK) — **Do Not Offer Routinely**
- **Do not offer** aspirin for primary prevention in T2DM
- **Consider** only if **high CVD risk** (QRISK3 ≥10%) **AND** low bleeding risk — shared decision
- **Review annually** — stop if bleeding risk increases

### ADA Standards of Care 2024 — **Consider Selectively**
- **Consider** aspirin (75–162mg) for primary prevention in:
  - **High CVD risk** (≥10% 10-year ASCVD risk) **AND**
  - **Low bleeding risk** (no GI bleed, no anticoagulant, age <70)
- **Not recommended** for:
  - Low CVD risk (<10%)
  - Age >70 (ASPREE harm)
  - High bleeding risk

### ESC 2021 CVD Prevention — **Individualised**
- **Class IIb, Level B**: May be considered in **very high risk** diabetes (target organ damage, diabetes >20y, CKD)
- **Bleeding risk assessment mandatory** (HAS-BLED)
- **Contraindicated** if HAS-BLED ≥3

## 4. Bleeding Risk Assessment — HAS-BLED

| Letter | Risk Factor | Points |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **H** | Hypertension (uncontrolled, SBP >160) | 1 |
| **A** | Abnormal renal/liver function (eGFR <60, cirrhosis) | 1 each |
| **S** | Stroke (prior) | 1 |
| **B** | Bleeding history or predisposition (anaemia, GI bleed) | 1 |
| **L** | Labile INRs (if on warfarin) | 1 |
| **E** | Elderly (age ≥65) | 1 |
| **D** | Drugs (NSAIDs, antiplatelets, anticoagulants) / Alcohol (≥8 units/week) | 1 each |

**Score Interpretation**:
- **0–1**: Low bleeding risk → aspirin reasonable if CV benefit
- **2**: Moderate → caution, shared decision
- **≥3**: **High** → avoid aspirin for primary prevention

---

## 5. Primary vs Secondary Prevention — Critical Distinction

| Aspect | **Primary Prevention** | **Secondary Prevention** |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Definition** | No prior CVD event | Prior MI, stroke, PAD, revascularisation, ACS |
| **Aspirin benefit** | Marginal (NNT 100–200) | **Clear** (NNT ~50) |
| **Guideline consensus** | **Controversial / Selective** | **Mandatory** (unless contraindicated) |
| **Dose** | 75–100mg | 75–100mg (75mg UK) |
| **Duration** | Review annually | **Lifelong** |

**In diabetes**: Prior CVD = secondary prevention → **aspirin lifelong** (unless high bleed risk).

## 6. Practical Decision Framework

```
Patient with diabetes, NO prior CVD
    │
    ├─► Age >70? → NO aspirin (ASPREE harm)
    │
    ├─► Calculate 10-year risk (QRISK3 / ASCVD)
    │     ├─► <10% / <7.5% → NO aspirin
    │     │
    │     └─► ≥10% / ≥7.5% → Assess bleeding (HAS-BLED)
    │           ├─► HAS-BLED ≥3 → NO aspirin
    │           │
    │           └─► HAS-BLED 0–2 → Shared decision:
    │                 • CV benefit: ~1 event prevented per 100–200 treated/year
    │                 • Bleed harm: ~1 major bleed per 100–200 treated/year
    │                 • Patient values, preferences
    │                 • Document discussion
    │
    └─► If started: Review annually — stop if bleed, age >70, risk changes
```

## 7. Special Situations

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **T1DM, age <40, no CVD, no risk factors** | No aspirin |
| **T1DM, age >40 OR >10y duration** | Consider per primary prevention framework |
| **CKD G3–G5** | ↑ Bleeding risk (uraemic platelet dysfunction) → caution |
| **On anticoagulant (AF, VTE)** | **No aspirin** (dual therapy ↑ bleed without CV benefit) |
| **Post-bariatric surgery** | No specific data — follow primary prevention |
| **Pregnancy** | Low-dose aspirin 75–150mg from 12w for **pre-eclampsia prevention** (not CVD) |
