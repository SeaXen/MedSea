# Immune checkpoint inhibitor diabetes

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise ICI-induced autoimmune diabetes (fulminant T1DM)
- [ ] Apply diagnostic criteria (autoantibodies, acute onset, DKA)
- [ ] Manage acute presentation (DKA, insulin)
- [ ] Apply long-term management

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Incidence** | 0.5-1.5% on anti-PD-1/PD-L1; higher with combination |
| **Onset** | Median 6-12 weeks; can occur anytime |
| **Mechanism** | Anti-PD-1/PD-L1 -> loss of peripheral tolerance -> autoimmune beta-cell destruction |
| **Phenotype** | Fulminant T1DM; DKA at presentation common (60-80%) |

## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading
| Feature | ICI-Diabetes |
|--------|--------------|
| **Mechanism** | Immune checkpoint blockade -> loss of tolerance -> autoimmune beta-cell destruction |
| **Agents** | Anti-PD-1 (pembrolizumab, nivolumab), Anti-PD-L1 (atezolizumab, durvalumab, avelumab) |
| **Onset** | Weeks-months after ICI initiation |
| **DKA** | Common at presentation (60-80%) |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **Classic T1DM** | Slower onset; autoantibodies positive; younger age |
| **Type 2DM** | No DKA; autoantibodies negative |
| **Acute pancreatitis** | Abdominal pain; amylase/lipase elevated; no autoantibodies |
| **Pancreatic cancer DM** | Weight loss; imaging mass; older |

## 7. Management

### Acute DKA
| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1** | Standard DKA protocol (fluids, FRII, K+, monitor) |
| **2** | **Continue ICI controversy** (generally hold; oncologist decision) |
| **3** | High-dose steroids? (Not routinely; may worsen hyperglycaemia) |

### Long-term Management
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **Insulin** | **Lifelong** (fulminant beta-cell destruction) |
| **Regimen** | Basal-bolus or CSII |
| **ICI continuation** | Multidisciplinary decision (oncologist + endocrinologist) |
| **Steroids** | Generally avoided (worsens hyperglycaemia) |
| **Monitoring** | Standard T1DM follow-up |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **ICI diabetes = T1DM?** | Similar but: older age, acute onset, DKA common, autoantibodies often negative |
| **Continue ICI after diabetes?** | Multidisciplinary decision; often held during DKA; may continue if cancer benefit > risk |
| **Steroids for ICI diabetes?** | Generally avoided (worsens hyperglycaemia); different from ICI-colitis/hepatitis |
