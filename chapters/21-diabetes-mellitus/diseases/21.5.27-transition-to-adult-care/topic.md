## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Definition** | Planned transfer from paediatric to adult diabetes services |
| **Age** | 16-18 years (individualised) |
| **Gap Risk** | 30-60% loss to follow-up during transition |
| **Outcome** | Deterioration in HbA1c, increased DKA, complications |

### NICE NG18 / SIGN 154 Recommendations
| Recommendation | Detail |
|----------------|--------|
| **Age** | Start preparation at 14-16; transfer 16-18 |
| **Joint clinics** | Minimum 2-3 joint paediatric-adult appointments |
| **Named worker** | Transition coordinator/nurse |
| **DAFNE-Youth** | Structured education for young adults |
| **Psychology** | Screen for eating disorders, depression, distress |

## 7. Management

### Transition Pathway
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Age 14-15] --> B[Start transition planning]
    B --> C[Assess readiness checklist]
    C --> D[Named transition coordinator]
    D --> E[Joint paediatric-adult clinics (2-3 min)]
    E --> F[DAFNE-Youth / structured education]
    F --> G[Psychology screening: eating disorders, depression]
    G --> H[Discuss: driving, alcohol, contraception, alcohol]
    H --> I[Transfer at 16-18y (individualised)]
    I --> J[Adult service: named consultant, 3mo follow-up]
```

### Key Discussion Topics
| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **Adherence** | CGM/SMBG, insulin omission, bolus skipping |
| **Eating disorders** | Diabulimia (insulin omission for weight loss); screen DEPS-R |
| **Contraception** | Pre-conception counselling; folic acid 5mg if T1DM/T2DM |
| **Alcohol** | Hypoglycaemia risk; carb content; glucagon ineffective if drunk |
| **Driving** | DVLA rules: hypo awareness, 3 monthly reviews if on insulin |
| **Employment** | Shift work, safety-critical jobs, rights (Equality Act) |

### Psychological Support
| Issue | Screening/Management |
|-------|---------------------|
| **Diabetes distress** | PAID/DDS; education, shared decision-making |
| **Depression** | PHQ-9; SSRIs 1st line; collaborative care |
| **Eating disorders** | DEPS-R >=20; diabulimia; MDT (psych, diabetologist, dietitian) |
| **Anxiety** | GAD-7; CBT; mindfulness |

## 9. Viva Questions
| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **What is the recommended age for transition to adult diabetes services?** | 16-18 years (individualised); preparation starts 14-16 years |
| **What are the key components of a transition programme?** | Joint clinics (2-3), named coordinator, DAFNE-Youth, psychology screening |
| **What psychological issues should be screened during transition?** | Eating disorders (DEPS-R >=20), depression (PHQ-9), diabetes distress (PAID) |
| **What are the DVLA rules for driving with T1DM?** | Must have hypo awareness; no severe hypo in last 12 months; 3-monthly reviews if on insulin |
| **What is diabulimia?** | Insulin omission for weight loss; recurrent DKA; high mortality; MDT management |
| **What is the role of a transition coordinator?** | Ensure continuity; coordinate joint clinics; track attendance; support young person |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Transition = discharge?** | NO - transfer of care with continuity; not discharge |
| **All 16-year-olds transfer?** | NO - individualised; some may stay paediatric until 18 |
| **Adult services = paediatric services?** | NO - different model; young person must self-advocate |

**Mnemonic: TRANSITION-DM**
- **T**ransition: 16-18y (prep 14-16)
- **R**eadiness: checklist (self-mgmt, knowledge, psychosocial)
- **A**dolescent: joint clinics (2-3 paeds/adult)
- **N**amed coordinator: tracks, supports, prevents loss to follow-up
- **S**tructured education: DAFNE-Youth
- **I**ndependence: driving, alcohol, contraception, employment
- **T**herapy: psychology screen (eating disorders, depression, distress)
- **I**nsulin: self-management (CGM, pump, sick day rules)
- **O**utreach: named coordinator prevents loss to follow-up
- **N**ext: adult service 3mo follow-up
