# Transient vs permanent neonatal diabetes

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Differentiate transient (TNDM) vs permanent (PNDM) neonatal diabetes
- [ ] Recognise 6q24 imprinting defect in TNDM
- [ ] Apply management differences
- [ ] Counsel on relapse risk

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology
| Feature | Transient (TNDM) | Permanent (PNDM) |
|--------|------------------|------------------|
| **Definition** | Remission by 18 months | Lifelong |
| **Proportion** | ~50% of neonatal DM | ~50% of neonatal DM |
| **Genetics** | 6q24 imprinting (70%); ZFP57, HYMAI | KCNJ11, ABCC8, INS, GCK, PDX1, PTF1A |
| **Remission** | Median 12 weeks (range 2w-18mo) | Never |
| **Relapse** | Adolescence/early adulthood (50%) | N/A |

## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading

### TNDM Genetic Subtypes
| Subtype | Gene/Region | Mechanism | Proportion |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------------|
| **TNDM1** | 6q24 (PLAGL1/HYMAI) | Imprinting defect (paternal UPD/duplication) | 70% |
| **TNDM2** | 6q24 (ZFP57) | ZFP57 mutation -> imprinting loss | Rare |
| **TNDM3** | KCNJ11/ABCC8 | Activating mutations (milder) | 10-20% |

## 5. Diagnosis & Investigations
| Test | TNDM | PNDM |
|------|------|------|
| **Genetic testing** | 6q24 methylation analysis | KCNJ11, ABCC8, INS, GCK, PDX1, PTF1A, EIF2AK3 |
| **Methylation assay** | Hypomethylation at 6q24 | Normal |
| **C-peptide** | Low at dx; recovers at remission | Persistently low |

## 6. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| **PNDM** | Never remits; KCNJ11/ABCC8/INS |
| **Syndromic** | Wolcott-Rallison (EIF2AK3), IPEX (FOXP3), Wolfram |

## 7. Management

### TNDM
| Phase | Management |
|-------|------------|
| **Acute** | Insulin (usually low dose; ~0.3 U/kg/day) |
| **Remission** | Stop insulin; monitor glucose weekly x 3mo |
| **Surveillance** | Annual OGTT/HbA1c from age 10; relapse risk 50% |

### PNDM
| Gene | Management |
|------|------------|
| **KCNJ11/ABCC8** | **Sulfonylurea 90% switch** |
| **INS/GCK/PDX1/PTF1A** | Insulin lifelong |
| **EIF2AK3 (Wolcott-Rallison)** | Insulin; multi-system management |

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **TNDM** | 6q24 imprinting (70%); remission by 18mo; relapse 50% in adulthood |
| **PNDM** | KCNJ11/ABCC8 (90% SU switch); INS/GCK/PDX1 = insulin |
| **6q24** | Paternal UPD/duplication; HYMAI/PLAGL1 overexpression |
| **Relapse** | TNDM relapse ~50% in adolescence/young adulthood |
| **Birth weight** | Low in both (IUGR) |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **All TNDM = 6q24?** | 70%; KCNJ11/ABCC8 also cause TNDM |
| **Relapse = new diabetes?** | Same genetic defect; beta-cell failure recurs |
| **TNDM = always transient?** | 50% relapse later; not "cured" |
