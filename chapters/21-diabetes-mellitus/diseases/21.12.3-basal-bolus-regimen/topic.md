## 1. Definition & Exam Relevance
**Basal-Bolus Regimen (MDI)** = Multiple Daily Injections: once/twice-daily long-acting basal insulin + rapid-acting bolus insulin with each meal.  
**Gold standard** for T1DM not on pump therapy.  
**FCPS/MRCP relevance**: Core management; exam questions on dose calculation, titration, troubleshooting, conversion to/from pump.

---

## 2. Regimen Structure
| Component | Insulin Type | Frequency | Timing | Proportion of TDD |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| **Basal** | Glargine U100/U300, Detemir, Degludec | Once daily (Degludec/U300) or BID (Detemir/Glargine U100 in some) | Fixed time (e.g., 22:00) | 40–50% |
| **Bolus (Breakfast)** | Lispro, Aspart, Fiasp, Lyumjev, Glulisine | With meal | 15 min before (std) / at meal (ultra-rapid) | 20–25% |
| **Bolus (Lunch)** | Same | With meal | 15 min before / at meal | 15–20% |
| **Bolus (Dinner)** | Same | With meal | 15 min before / at meal | 15–20% |
| **Correction** | Same | As needed | With meals or separate | Variable |

**Total Daily Dose (TDD)**: 0.4–0.5 units/kg/day (new onset: 0.2–0.3 u/kg during honeymoon).

---

## 3. Starting Dose Calculation (New Diagnosis / Conversion)
### Step 1: Estimate TDD
- **Weight-based**: 0.4–0.5 units/kg/day
- **From previous regimen**: Sum all insulin units in 24h

### Step 2: Split Basal : Bolus
| Approach | Basal % | Bolus % |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Standard** | 50% | 50% |
| **Conservative (elderly, renal, hypoglycaemia risk)** | 60% | 40% |
| **Low-carb diet** | 60–70% | 30–40% |

### Step 3: Distribute Bolus Across Meals
| Method | Calculation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Equal split** | Total bolus ÷ 3 (or ÷ 4 if snack) |
| **Carb-proportional** | Based on typical carb intake per meal |
| **DAFNE standard** | Breakfast 30%, Lunch 25%, Dinner 30%, Supper 15% |

### Step 4: Set ICR & ISF
- **ICR**: 500 ÷ TDD (adjust per meal: breakfast tighter)
- **ISF**: 100 ÷ TDD (target correction to 5.5–6.0 mmol/L)

---

## 4. Basal Insulin Titration Protocol
| Target Fasting Glucose | Action |
|------------------------|--------|
| **<4 mmol/L** | ↓ Basal by 10–20% (or 2–4 units) |
| **4–7 mmol/L** | **No change** (target achieved) |
| **7–10 mmol/L** | ↑ Basal by 10% (or 2–4 units) |
| **>10 mmol/L** | ↑ Basal by 20% (or 4–6 units); check adherence, 3am glucose |

**Frequency**: Titrate **every 3–7 days**; allow 3–4 days for glargine U300/degludec steady state.

**3am Check**: If fasting high but 3am low (<3.9) → **Somogyi** → ↓ basal; if 3am high → **dawn/insufficient basal** → ↑ basal.

---

## 5. Bolus Titration (Pattern Management)
| Pattern | Adjustment |
|---------|------------|
| **Post-breakfast >9 mmol/L consistently** | ↑ Breakfast ICR (1:12 → 1:10) |
| **Post-lunch >9 mmol/L consistently** | ↑ Lunch ICR |
| **Post-dinner >9 mmol/L consistently** | ↑ Dinner ICR |
| **Pre-lunch low <4 mmol/L** | ↓ Breakfast ICR or ↓ breakfast carbs |
| **Pre-dinner low <4 mmol/L** | ↓ Lunch ICR |
| **Bedtime low <4 mmol/L** | ↓ Dinner ICR or ↓ bedtime basal |
| **Pre-breakfast high, post-breakfast OK** | ↑ Basal (not breakfast bolus) |

**Review frequency**: Weekly initially; then monthly once stable.

---

## 6. Basal Insulin Choice — Decision Algorithm
```
NEW T1DM START
    ↓
Prefer DEGLUDEC (Tresiba) — lowest variability, flexible timing, least nocturnal hypo
    ↓
If cost/access issue → GLARGINE U100 (biosimilar Abasaglar/Semglee)
    ↓
If high dose (>80u) / volume issue → GLARGINE U300 (Toujeo) or DEGLUDEC U200
    ↓
If weight gain concern → DETEMIR (Levemir) — but BID often needed
    ↓
Avoid NPH — higher variability, peak → nocturnal hypo
```

## 7. Sick Day Rules (Basal-Bolus)
| Principle | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Never stop basal insulin** | Continue full basal dose (or ↑ 20% if ketotic) |
| **Bolus for carbs eaten** | Use usual ICR; if not eating, give correction only |
| **Check ketones** | If BG >13.9 mmol/L or symptomatic → blood ketones q4h |
| **Hydration** | 150–250 mL/hour sugar-free fluids |
| **Seek help** | Ketones >1.5 mmol/L + unable to keep fluids / vomiting → A&E for IV fluids/insulin |

**Correction scale for illness** (if ketones negative):
- BG 11–17 mmol/L: 10% TDD as correction
- BG 17–22 mmol/L: 15% TDD
- BG >22 mmol/L: 20% TDD + seek medical review

---

## 8. Conversion: Basal-Bolus ↔ Pump (CSII)
| Direction | Calculation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **MDI → Pump** | TDD × 0.8 (20% reduction) → 50% basal rate, 50% bolus |
| **Pump → MDI** | Total daily basal (from pump) → basal analogue; bolus doses → ICR/ISF |
| **Basal rate → Glargine U100** | 24h basal units × 1.0–1.1 |
| **Basal rate → Degludec** | 24h basal units × 0.9–1.0 |

---

## 9. FCPS/MRCP Exam Pearls
- **TDD 0.4–0.5 u/kg/day** — starting estimate for fresh T1DM
- **Basal 50% / Bolus 50%** — standard split
- **Titrate basal to fasting 4–7 mmol/L** — every 3–7 days
- **3am check distinguishes Somogyi vs Dawn** — critical concept
- **Degludec preferred first-line** (NICE/ADA) — flexibility, low hypo, low variability
- **Sick days: NEVER stop basal** — exam favorite
- **MDI→Pump: reduce TDD by 20%** (pump more efficient)
- **Bioequivalence**: Glargine biosimilars (Abasaglar/Semglee) = Lantus; switch unit-for-unit
