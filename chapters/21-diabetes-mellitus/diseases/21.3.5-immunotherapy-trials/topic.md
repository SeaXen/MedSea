## 4. Classification / Staging / Grading

### Key Immunotherapy Trials
| Trial | Agent | Population | Outcome |
|-------|-------|------------|---------|
| **TN-10 (Teplizumab)** | Anti-CD3 | Stage 2 (2+Ab + dysglycaemia) | **Delayed Stage 3 by ~2 years** (HR 0.48) |
| **Abatacept** | CTLA4-Ig | Stage 1-2 | Modest C-peptide preservation |
| **Rituximab** | Anti-CD20 | New-onset (Stage 3) | Preserved C-peptide at 1 year |
| **Verapamil** | Calcium channel blocker | New-onset | Preserved beta-cell function |
| **IL-2 (low dose)** | IL-2 | Stage 1-2 | ↑Tregs, preserved C-peptide |
| **ATG/G-CSF** | ATG + G-CSF | New-onset | Preserved C-peptide at 2 years |
| **Alefacept** | Anti-CD2 | New-onset | Preserved C-peptide |

## 7. Management

### Trial Eligibility (General)
| Criterion | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Autoantibodies** | 2+ (GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, IAA) |
| **Stage** | 1 (normoglycaemia), 2 (dysglycaemia), or new-onset |
| **Age** | Usually 8-45 years |
| **Exclusion** | Active infection, immunosuppression, pregnancy |

## 9. Viva Questions
| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **What immunotherapies have shown benefit in T1DM?** | Teplizumab (approved), abatacept, rituximab, verapamil, IL-2, ATG/G-CSF |
| **What is the difference between prevention and preservation trials?** | Prevention = Stage 1-2 (pre-symptomatic); Preservation = Stage 3 (new-onset) |
| **What was the outcome of rituximab trial?** | Preserved C-peptide at 1 year in new-onset T1DM |
| **What is the mechanism of teplizumab?** | Anti-CD3 mAb -> partial T-cell agonism -> modulates immune response |
| **Are any immunotherapies approved for T1DM prevention?** | **Teplizumab only** (FDA 2022, Stage 2, age ≥8) |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Immunotherapy = cure?** | NO - delay/preservation only; no cure yet |
| **All trials = success?** | NO - many failed (oral insulin, nasal insulin, GAD-alum) |
| **Teplizumab = only approved?** | YES - as of 2024, only FDA-approved prevention therapy |

**Mnemonic: IMMUNO-T1DM-TRIALS**
- **I**mmunotherapy: T1DM prevention/preservation
- **M**echanism: modulate immune system (T-cells, B-cells, cytokines)
- **M**ultiple agents: teplizumab, abatacept, rituximab, verapamil, IL-2, ATG
- **U**nique: teplizumab = only approved (FDA 2022)
- **N**ew-onset: preservation trials (rituximab, abatacept, verapamil)
- **O**nly teplizumab approved for prevention (Stage 2)
- **T**eplizumab: anti-CD3, delays Stage 3 ~2 years
- **R**ituximab: anti-CD20, new-onset preservation
- **I**nterleukin-2: low dose -> Tregs
- **A**batacept: CTLA4-Ig, blocks T-cell activation
- **V**erapamil: calcium channel blocker, new-onset
- **A**TG/G-CSF: new-onset preservation
- **L**imits: delay/preserve, not cure
- **S**creening: relatives (TrialNet), general (Fr1da, ASK)
- **T**rialNet: relatives of T1DM
- **R**esults: modest; delay 1-2 years
- **I**mmunotherapy: not for everyone
- **A**dverse: lymphopenia, infections, CRS
- **L**ong-term: unknown durability
