# Post-Surgery Diabetes Management

> [!info]
> **Post-bariatric glucose management**: IV insulin → SC 50–70% dose reduction within days; **rapid remission** (incretin effect); **dumping syndrome** (early/late); **nutritional deficiencies** (B12, iron, Ca, D, thiamine); **lifelong supplementation + monitoring**; contraception 12–18m; pregnancy planning.

## 2. Immediate Postoperative Glucose Management (Days 0–30)

### Day 0–1 (ICU/HDU)
| Situation | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| **NPO / IV fluids** | **IV insulin infusion** (sliding scale) target 6–10 mmol/L |
| **Regular insulin drip** | 0.05–0.1u/kg/h; adjust q1–2h |
| **Existing basal insulin** | **STOP** — IV insulin replaces |

### Day 1–2 (Ward, Starting Oral Intake)
| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. Stop IV insulin** | When tolerating oral fluids/protein shakes |
| **2. Start SC insulin** | **50–70% of pre-op total daily dose** (incretin effect + caloric restriction) |
| **3. Regimen** | Basal (glargine/detemir/degludec) + **minimal/rapid correction** only |
| **4. Stop oral glucose meds** | **Sulfonylurea, SGLT2i, DPP-4i, GLP-1 RA, TZD** → all STOP |
| **5. Metformin** | **Continue if eGFR ≥30** (adjust for weight loss) |

### Day 3–7
| Glucose Pattern | Action |
|-----------------|--------|
| **Normoglycaemia (4–7)** | **Stop ALL glucose meds** (assess remission) |
| **Mild hyperglycaemia (7–10)** | Low-dose basal only (e.g., 5–10u); recheck daily |
| **Hypoglycaemia (<4)** | **Reduce/stop insulin**; carb snack; reassess dose |

**Key**: **Rapid remission** common — 50–70% off all meds by discharge.

## 3. Dumping Syndrome — Early vs Late

| Feature | **Early Dumping** (15–30min post-meal) | **Late Dumping** (1–3h post-meal) |
|---------|----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Mechanism** | Rapid fluid shift → hypovolaemia | **Reactive hypoglycaemia** (hyperinsulinaemia) |
| **Symptoms** | Nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, cramps, flushing, tachycardia, dizziness, syncope | Sweating, tremor, palpitations, hunger, confusion, neuroglycopenia |
| **Trigger** | **Hyperosmolar** foods (sugar, simple carbs), large volumes | High glycaemic carbs → rapid absorption → insulin surge |
| **Diagnosis** | Clinical (Symptom score: Sigstad >7) | **CGM/glucose <3.5 at 1–3h** post-meal |
| **Management** | **Diet**: small frequent meals, low sugar, low GI, fluid separate from food (30min), protein/fat with carbs, lie down post-meal | **Diet**: complex carbs, protein/fat each meal, avoid simple sugars; **Acarbose** 50mg TDS (delays absorption); **Octreotide** (refractory) |

## 4. Nutritional Deficiencies — Lifelong Monitoring

| Nutrient | Deficiency Risk | Monitoring | Supplementation |
|----------|-----------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Vitamin B12** | **High** (ileal bypass, ↓ intrinsic factor + acid) | **Annual** (B12, MMA, homocysteine) | **IM 1mg 3-monthly** OR oral 1mg daily |
| **Iron** | **High** (duodenal bypass, ↓ acid, menstrual loss) | **6-monthly** (ferritin, Tsat, Hb) | **Oral 100–200mg elemental** ± IV if severe |
| **Calcium / Vit D** | **High** (bypass, ↓ absorption, bone loss) | **Annual** (Ca, PO4, ALP, PTH, 25-OH-D, DEXA q2y) | **Ca 1200–1500mg + Vit D 2000–4000 IU** daily |
| **Thiamine (B1)** | **Critical** (Wernicke's risk — rapid weight loss, vomiting) | **Annual** (clinical; erythrocyte transketolase if indicated) | **Prophylactic 100mg daily** post-op; **IV 500mg TDS** if suspected Wernicke's |
| **Folate** | Moderate | Annual | 1mg daily (in multivitamin) |
| **Zinc / Copper / Selenium** | Moderate (malabsorptive procedures) | Annual | In bariatric multivitamin |
| **Vitamin A / K** | Low (DS/BPD) | If DS/BPD | As per protocol |

## 5. Long-Term Follow-Up Schedule

| Timepoint | Assessments |
|-----------|-------------|
| **6 weeks** | Wound, diet advancement, glucose, B12, iron, folate, D, Ca, PTH, thiamine |
| **3 months** | Full bloods, HbA1c, weight, diet adherence, medication review, supplementation check |
| **6 months** | Full nutritional panel, DEXA (baseline), weight trajectory |
| **Annual (lifelong)** | **CBC, U&E, LFT, iron studies, B12, folate, Vit D, Ca, PTH, Mg, Zn, Cu, Se, HbA1c, weight, DEXA q2y**, endoscopy if symptoms |

**Supplementation (Lifelong, all procedures)**:
- **Bariatric multivitamin** (2/day)
- **B12**: IM 1mg 3-monthly OR oral 1mg daily
- **Iron**: 100–200mg elemental daily (adjust by ferritin)
- **Calcium citrate** 1200–1500mg + **Vit D3** 2000–4000 IU daily
- **Thiamine** 100mg daily

## 6. Specific Post-Surgical Issues

### Hypoglycaemia (Late Dumping / Reactive)
- **CGM essential** for detection
- **Diet**: complex carbs, protein/fat each meal, avoid simple sugars
- **Acarbose** 50–100mg TDS with meals
- **Diazoxide / Octreotide** (refractory, specialist)

### Bone Health
- **DEXA at baseline, 2y, then q2–3y**
- **High fracture risk** (↓ weight-bearing, ↓ Ca/VitD, ↑ bone turnover)
- **Bisphosphonate** if T-score <−2.5 or fracture

### Anaemia
- **Multifactorial**: Iron, B12, folate, copper, chronic inflammation
- **Treat specific deficiency**; IV iron if oral fails/intolerant

### Nephrolithiasis
- ↑ Oxalate absorption (fat malabsorption) → **calcium oxalate stones**
- **High fluid intake** (2–3L/day); **citrate** (potassium citrate); low oxalate diet

## 7. Contraception & Pregnancy

| Recommendation | Detail |
|----------------|--------|
| **Contraception** | **Effective required 12–18 months** post-op (rapid fertility return) |
| **Preferred** | **LARC** (IUD, implant); avoid oral (malabsorption risk) |
| **Pregnancy planning** | **Wait 12–18 months** (weight stabilisation, nutrition optimisation) |
| **Pre-conception** | Nutritional optimisation (B12, iron, folate 5mg, Ca, D); HbA1c <48; stop teratogens |
| **During pregnancy** | **Specialist obstetric + bariatric team**; increased nutritional monitoring; adjusted supplementation |
