## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Focus
- **OA vs inflammatory**: mechanical pain, crepitus, bony swelling (Heberden's/Bouchard's), Kellgren-Lawrence grading
- **Hand OA**: DIP/PIP (nodal) vs MCP (not OA), squaring thumb base
- **Red flags**: inflammatory features, systemic symptoms, young age → think inflammatory/secondary
- **Erosive OA**: DIP/PIP erosions + inflammation, 'gull-wing' appearance, postmenopausal women
- **Charcot**: neuropathy + trauma, rocker-bottom foot, acute = hot/swollen = mimic septic
