Chapter 3: Clinical Genetics

├── 1. FUNDAMENTALS OF MEDICAL GENETICS
│   ├── 1.1 DNA, Genes & Genome Organisation
│   │   ├── DNA structure, replication, transcription, translation
│   │   ├── Gene structure (exons, introns, promoters, enhancers)
│   │   ├── Human genome organisation (nuclear, mitochondrial)
│   │   ├── Gene nomenclature (HGNC), databases (OMIM, GeneReviews, ClinVar)
│   │   └── Central dogma, splicing, post-translational modification
│   ├── 1.2 Chromosome Structure & Analysis
│   │   ├── Chromosome morphology (metacentric, submetacentric, acrocentric)
│   │   ├── Karyotyping (G-banding, ISCN nomenclature)
│   │   ├── FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridisation)
│   │   ├── Chromosomal microarray (aCGH, SNP array)
│   │   └── Next-generation sequencing (NGS): panels, exome, genome
│   ├── 1.3 Cell Division & Genetic Variation
│   │   ├── Mitosis vs Meiosis (recombination, independent assortment)
│   │   ├── Non-disjunction → Aneuploidy
│   │   ├── Structural variants (deletion, duplication, inversion, translocation)
│   │   ├── Copy number variants (CNVs) vs Single nucleotide variants (SNVs)
│   │   └── Mutation types (missense, nonsense, frameshift, splice, CNV)
│   └── 1.4 Population Genetics
│       ├── Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium (p² + 2pq + q² = 1)
│       ├── Allele frequencies, Carrier frequencies (2pq)
│       ├── Founder effect, Genetic drift, Bottleneck
│       ├── Consanguinity → Autosomal recessive risk (F coefficient)
│       └── Linkage disequilibrium, Haplotypes
│
├── 2. PATTERNS OF INHERITANCE
│   ├── 2.1 Mendelian Inheritance
│   │   ├── Autosomal Dominant (AD)
│   │   │   ├── Features: Vertical transmission, 50% risk, variable expressivity, penetrance
│   │   │   ├── Mechanisms: Haploinsufficiency, Dominant negative, Gain of function
│   │   │   ├── Examples: Huntington disease, Neurofibromatosis type 1, Marfan, ADPKD
│   │   │   └── Key concepts: Anticipation, Mosaicism, Germline vs Somatic
│   │   ├── Autosomal Recessive (AR)
│   │   │   ├── Features: Horizontal, 25% risk, carriers asymptomatic, consanguinity
│   │   │   ├── Examples: Cystic fibrosis, Sickle cell, Thalassaemia, PKU, Tay-Sachs
│   │   │   ├── Carrier screening, Founder mutations
│   │   │   └── Pseudodominance, Compound heterozygosity
│   │   ├── X-Linked Recessive (XLR)
│   │   │   ├── Features: Males affected, carrier females, no male-to-male
│   │   │   ├── Examples: Duchenne/Becker MD, Haemophilia A/B, G6PD deficiency
│   │   │   ├── X-inactivation (Lyonisation) → Carrier manifestations
│   │   │   └── Skewed X-inactivation
│   │   ├── X-Linked Dominant (XLD)
│   │   │   ├── Features: Both sexes, affected males→all daughters, affected females→50% offspring
│   │   │   ├── Examples: Rett syndrome, Fragile X (premutation), Incontinentia pigmenti
│   │   │   └── Male lethality (e.g., Incontinentia pigmenti)
│   │   └── Y-Linked
│   │       ├── Features: Males only, father-to-son
│   │       └── Examples: SRY, Azoospermia factors
│   ├── 2.2 Non-Mendelian Inheritance
│   │   ├── Mitochondrial Inheritance
│   │   │   ├── Maternal transmission, Heteroplasmy, Threshold effect
│   │   │   ├── Examples: MELAS, MERRF, LHON, Kearns-Sayre, mtDNA depletion
│   │   │   └── Bottleneck effect, Mitotic segregation
│   │   ├── Genomic Imprinting
│   │   │   ├── Parent-of-origin expression, DMRs, CTCF
│   │   │   ├── Disorders: Prader-Willi, Angelman, Beckwith-Wiedemann, Silver-Russell
│   │   │   └── Uniparental disomy (UPD)
│   │   ├── Trinucleotide Repeat Disorders (Anticipation)
│   │   │   ├── Mechanism: Slippage, expansion, toxic RNA/protein
│   │   │   ├── Examples: Huntington, Fragile X, Myotonic dystrophy, SCA, Friedreich ataxia
│   │   │   └── Premutation vs Full mutation
│   │   ├── Mosaicism & Chimerism
│   │   │   ├── Post-zygotic mutation, Gonadal vs Somatic mosaicism
│   │   │   └── Clinical significance: Segmental disorders, Recurrence risk
│   │   └── Uniparental Disomy (UPD)
│   │       ├── Iso/UPD, Heterodisomy vs Isodisomy
│   │       └── Imprinting disorders due to UPD (e.g., UPD15 → PWS/AS)
│   └── 2.3 Multifactorial / Complex Inheritance
│       ├── Polygenic + Environmental (Threshold model)
│       ├── Heritability (h²), Recurrence risk (λs)
│       ├── Examples: Diabetes mellitus, Hypertension, Cleft lip, Neural tube defects
│       ├── GWAS, Polygenic risk scores (PRS)
│       └── Gene-environment interaction
│
├── 3. CHROMOSOMAL DISORDERS
│   ├── 3.1 Aneuploidies (Autosomal)
│   │   ├── Down Syndrome (Trisomy 21) — Features, Mosaic, Translocation (Robertsonian)
│   │   ├── Edwards Syndrome (Trisomy 18)
│   │   ├── Patau Syndrome (Trisomy 13)
│   │   └── Rare trisomies (9, 16, 22) — Mosaicism, Confined placental
│   ├── 3.2 Sex Chromosome Aneuploidies
│   │   ├── Turner Syndrome (45,X) — Mosaicism, 45,X/46,XX
│   │   ├── Klinefelter Syndrome (47,XXY) — Variants (48,XXXY, 49,XXXXY)
│   │   ├── Triple X Syndrome (47,XXX)
│   │   ├── XYY Syndrome (47,XYY)
│   │   └── Other variants (XXYY, XXXXY)
│   ├── 3.3 Structural Chromosomal Rearrangements
│   │   ├── Balanced Translocation (Reciprocal, Robertsonian) → Carrier risk
│   │   ├── Unbalanced Translocation → Partial aneuploidy
│   │   ├── Inversions (Paracentric, Pericentric) → Recombinant risk
│   │   ├── Deletions (Microdeletion syndromes)
│   │   │   ├── 22q11.2 Deletion (DiGeorge/VCFS), 1p36, 5p (Cri-du-chat)
│   │   │   ├── 7q11.23 (Williams), 15q11-13 (PWS/AS), 17p13.1 (Miller-Dieker)
│   │   │   └── Microdeletion → Recurrent vs Non-recurrent
│   │   ├── Duplications (e.g., 17p12 → CMT1A, 22q11.2 dup)
│   │   ├── Ring Chromosomes, Isochromosomes, Dicentric
│   │   └── Chromothripsis, Chromoplexy
│   └── 3.4 Chromosomal Instability Syndromes
│       ├── Fanconi Anaemia, Bloom Syndrome, Ataxia-Telangiectasia
│       ├── Nijmegen Breakage Syndrome, Werner Syndrome
│       └── DNA repair defects → Cancer predisposition
│
├── 4. SINGLE GENE DISORDERS (MONOGENIC)
│   ├── 4.1 Autosomal Dominant Disorders
│   │   ├── Neurofibromatosis Type 1 (NF1) — Legius syndrome differential
│   │   ├── Marfan Syndrome (FBN1) — Loeys-Dietz differential
│   │   ├── Huntington Disease (HTT) — Genetic testing, Predictive testing
│   │   ├── Adult Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD1, PKD2)
│   │   ├── Familial Hypercholesterolaemia (LDLR, APOB, PCSK9)
│   │   ├── Hereditary Haemochromatosis (HFE) — vs Non-HFE
│   │   ├── Noonan Syndrome (PTPN11, SOS1, RAF1) — RASopathies
│   │   ├── Achondroplasia (FGFR3) — Allelic with Hypochondroplasia/Thanatophoric
│   │   ├── Familial Adenomatous Polyposis (APC) — Gardner, Turcot
│   │   ├── Tuberous Sclerosis (TSC1, TSC2) — mTOR pathway
│   │   ├── Von Hippel-Lindau (VHL) — HIF pathway
│   │   ├── Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia (MEN1, MEN2A/2B)
│   │   ├── Hereditary Spherocytosis (ANK1, SPTB, SLC4A1, EPB42)
│   │   └── Porphyrias (Acute intermittent, Variegate, etc.)
│   ├── 4.2 Autosomal Recessive Disorders
│   │   ├── Cystic Fibrosis (CFTR) — F508del, Genetic counselling, CFTR modulators
│   │   ├── Haemoglobinopathies: Sickle Cell, β-Thalassaemia, α-Thalassaemia
│   │   ├── Phenylketonuria (PAH) — Newborn screening, Diet, Sapropterin
│   │   ├── Tay-Sachs, Gaucher, Niemann-Pick (LSDs)
│   │   ├── Wilson Disease (ATP7B) — Copper metabolism
│   │   ├── Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (SERPINA1) — Z allele, Lung/Liver
│   │   ├── Ataxia-Telangiectasia (ATM) — Radiosensitivity, Cancer risk
│   │   ├── Friedrich Ataxia (FXN) — GAA repeat, Cardiac involvement
│   │   ├── Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMN1) — SMN2 copy number, Therapy
│   │   ├── Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (CYP21A2) — 21-hydroxylase deficiency
│   │   └── Alkaptonuria, Homocystinuria, MSUD
│   ├── 4.3 X-Linked Disorders
│   │   ├── Duchenne / Becker Muscular Dystrophy (DMD) — Exon deletions, exon skipping
│   │   ├── Haemophilia A (F8) / B (F9) — Inhibitor development
│   │   ├── G6PD Deficiency — Variants, Favism, Neonatal jaundice, Drug triggers
│   │   ├── Fabry Disease (GLA) — α-galactosidase, Enzyme replacement
│   │   ├── Hunter Syndrome (MPS II, IDS)
│   │   ├── Adrenoleukodystrophy (ABCD1) — VLCFA, Stem cell transplant
│   │   ├── Fragile X Syndrome (FMR1) — CGG repeats, Premutation carriers, FXPOI, FXTAS
│   │   ├── Incontinentia Pigmenti (IKBKG) — Male lethality
│   │   ├── X-linked Lymphoproliferative Disease (SH2D1A)
│   │   └── Wiskott-Aldrich (WAS) — Thrombocytopenia, Immunodeficiency
│   ├── 4.4 Mitochondrial Disorders
│   │   ├── MELAS (m.3243A>G) — Stroke-like episodes, Diabetes, Deafness
│   │   ├── MERRF (m.8344A>G) — Myoclonus, Epilepsy, Ataxia
│   │   ├── LHON (m.11778G>A, m.14484T>C, m.3460G>A) — Optic neuropathy
│   │   ├── Kearns-Sayre Syndrome (Large mtDNA deletions) — CPEO, Heart block
│   │   ├── NARP / Leigh Syndrome (m.8993T>G/C) — Neurodegeneration
│   │   ├── MNGIE (TYMP) — GI dysmotility, Leukoencephalopathy
│   │   ├── mtDNA Depletion Syndromes (POLG, MPV17, DGUOK)
│   │   └── Nuclear-encoded mitochondrial disorders (e.g., FRDA, SURF1)
│   └── 4.5 Imprinting Disorders & UPD
│       ├── Prader-Willi Syndrome (15q11-13 paternal deletion/UPDmat)
│       ├── Angelman Syndrome (15q11-13 maternal deletion/UPDpat)
│       ├── Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome (11p15.5) — IC1/IC2, Mosaicism
│       ├── Silver-Russell Syndrome (11p15.5, H19/IGF2 hypomethylation)
│       ├── Transient Neonatal Diabetes (6q24) — PLAGL1/HYMAI
│       └── UPD disorders: UPD7 (SRS), UPD14 (Temple/Kagami-Ogata)
│
├── 5. GENETIC TESTING & DIAGNOSTIC TECHNOLOGIES
│   ├── 5.1 Cytogenetics
│   │   ├── Karyotyping (550-band vs 850-band), Resolution limits
│   │   ├── FISH (Locus-specific, Centromeric, Whole chromosome paint)
│   │   └── Chromosomal Microarray (aCGH, SNP array) — CNV detection, LOH, UPD
│   ├── 5.2 Molecular Genetics
│   │   ├── Sanger Sequencing (Gold standard for known variants)
│   │   ├── MLPA (Multiplex Ligation-dependent Probe Amplification) — CNV, Exon deletion
│   │   ├── PCR-based (Allele-specific, qPCR, Digital PCR)
│   │   ├── Methylation Analysis (MS-PCR, Pyrosequencing) — Imprinting disorders
│   │   ├── Repeat-Primed PCR — Trinucleotide repeat expansion
│   │   └── RNA Analysis (Splicing variants)
│   ├── 5.3 Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)
│   │   ├── Gene Panels (Targeted, Clinical exome) — Depth, Coverage, Variant classification
│   │   ├── Whole Exome Sequencing (WES) — Coding regions, 85% variants
│   │   ├── Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS) — Non-coding, Structural variants
│   │   ├── Bioinformatics Pipeline (Alignment, Variant calling, Annotation, Filtering)
│   │   ├── Variant Classification (ACMG/AMP 5-tier: Pathogenic, LP, VUS, LB, B)
│   │   ├── Incidental/Secondary Findings (ACMG SF v3.1 list)
│   │   └── Limitations (Regulatory, Deep intronic, CNV sensitivity, Mosaicism)
│   ├── 5.4 Prenatal & Preimplantation Genetic Testing
│   │   ├── Prenatal Screening: Combined test (NT + PAPP-A + β-hCG), NIPT (cfDNA)
│   │   ├── Invasive Testing: CVS (11-14w), Amniocentesis (15-20w), Cordocentesis
│   │   ├── Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT-A, PGT-M, PGT-SR)
│   │   ├── Rapid Aneuploidy Testing (QF-PCR, FISH, BoBs)
│   │   └── Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing (NIPT) — cfDNA, Sensitivity/Specificity, Limitations
│   └── 5.5 Genetic Counselling
│       ├── Principles: Non-directive, Autonomy, Confidentiality, Non-discrimination
│       ├── Risk Calculation (Bayesian, Conditional probability)
│       ├── Pre-test & Post-test Counselling
│       ├── Predictive Testing (Huntington, Cancer predisposition) — Protocols, Implications
│       ├── Carrier Screening (Population-based, Ethnicity-based, Expanded panels)
│       ├── Cascade Testing (Family tracing)
│       └── Psychosocial Support, Decision-making, Reproductive options
│
├── 6. CANCER GENETICS
│   ├── 6.1 Hereditary Cancer Syndromes
│   │   ├── Hereditary Breast & Ovarian Cancer (HBOC) — BRCA1/2, PALB2, CHEK2, ATM
│   │   ├── Lynch Syndrome (LS) — MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2, EPCAM (MMR genes)
│   │   ├── Familial Adenomatous Polyposis (FAP, APC) — Gardner, Turcot, Attenuated FAP
│   │   ├── MUTYH-Associated Polyposis (MAP) — Base excision repair
│   │   ├── Li-Fraumeni Syndrome (TP53) — Core cancers, Surveillance
│   │   ├── Cowden Syndrome (PTEN) — PTEN hamartoma tumour syndrome
│   │   ├── Von Hippel-Lindau (VHL) — RCC, Phaeochromocytoma, Haemangioblastoma
│   │   ├── Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia (MEN1, MEN2A/2B) — RET, MEN1
│   │   ├── Hereditary Diffuse Gastric Cancer (CDH1) — E-cadherin
│   │   ├── Peutz-Jeghers (STK11) — Hamartomatous polyps, Mucocutaneous pigmentation
│   │   ├── Juvenile Polyposis (SMAD4, BMPR1A)
│   │   ├── Familial Melanoma (CDKN2A, CDK4)
│   │   └── Renal Cancer Syndromes (VHL, HLRCC, BHD, TSC)
│   ├── 6.2 Tumour Genetics
│   │   ├── Oncogenes vs Tumour Suppressor Genes (Two-hit hypothesis)
│   │   ├── Driver vs Passenger Mutations
│   │   ├── Tumour Mutational Burden (TMB), Microsatellite Instability (MSI)
│   │   ├── Homologous Recombination Deficiency (HRD), BRCAness
│   │   ├── Circulating Tumour DNA (ctDNA), Liquid Biopsy
│   │   └── Targeted Therapy Biomarkers (EGFR, ALK, ROS1, BRAF, KRAS, HER2)
│   └── 6.3 Genetic Testing in Oncology
│       ├── Germline vs Somatic Testing
│       ├── Testing Criteria (NCCN, NHS England Genomic Medicine Service)
│       ├── Panel Testing vs Exome/Genome
│       ├── Variant Interpretation (Oncogenicity, ACMG/AMP, ClinGen)
│       ├── Therapeutic Implications (PARPi, Immunotherapy, Targeted agents)
│       └── Surveillance Protocols (e.g., Manchester scoring, NICE guidelines)
│
├── 7. PHARMACOGENETICS
│   ├── 7.1 Drug Metabolism Genes (Cytochrome P450)
│   │   ├── CYP2C19 (Clopidogrel, PPIs), CYP2D6 (Codeine, Tamoxifen, Antidepressants)
│   │   ├── CYP2C9 (Warfarin, Phenytoin), CYP3A4/5 (Tacrolimus, Statins)
│   │   └── NAT2 (Isoniazid), TPMT (Azathioprine, Mercaptopurine)
│   ├── 7.2 Drug Target & Transport Genes
│   │   ├── VKORC1 (Warfarin), SLCO1B1 (Statins), ABCB1 (MDR1)
│   │   ├── HLA Alleles (Carbamazepine HLA-B*15:02, Abacavir HLA-B*57:01)
│   │   ├── DPYD (5-FU), UGT1A1 (Irinotecan), NUDT15 (Thiopurines)
│   └── 7.3 Clinical Implementation
│       ├── CPIC/DPWG Guidelines, NHS GMS DPYD, TPMT, CYP2C19 testing
│       ├── Pre-emptive vs Reactive Testing
│       ├── Clinical Decision Support (CDS), EHR Integration
│       └── Ethical: Equity, Access, Data sharing
│
├── 8. POPULATION SCREENING & NEWBORN SCREENING
│   ├── 8.1 Newborn Blood Spot Screening (UK Programme)
│   │   ├── Conditions: PKU, CHT, CF, SCD, MCADD, MSUD, IVA, GA1, HCU
│   │   ├── Tandem Mass Spectrometry (Acylcarnitines, Amino acids)
│   │   ├── Screening Criteria (Wilson & Jungner), Ethics, Consent
│   └── 8.2 Population Carrier Screening
│       ├── Ethnicity-based (e.g., Ashkenazi Jewish panel, Thalassaemia in Mediterranean)
│       ├── Expanded Panels (Hundreds of conditions)
│       ├── Reproductive Counselling, IVF/PGT-M
│       └── Ethics: Autonomy, Stigma, Equitable access
│
├── 9. ETHICAL, LEGAL & SOCIAL ISSUES (ELSI)
│   ├── 9.1 Genetic Discrimination (Insurance, Employment — GINA, UK Equality Act)
│   ├── 9.2 Privacy & Confidentiality (Familial Implications, Duty to Warn)

└── 10. GENETICS IN CLINICAL PRACTICE (SYSTEM-BASED)
