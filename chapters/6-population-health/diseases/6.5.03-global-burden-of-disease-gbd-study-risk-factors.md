## 5. 5. Diagnosis & Investigations (Attribution Methods)

**Population Attributable Fraction (PAF):**
```
PAF = Pₑ × (RR - 1) / [1 + Pₑ × (RR - 1)]
```
Where Pₑ = prevalence of exposure, RR = relative risk.

**Comparative Risk Assessment (CRA) Steps:**
1. Identify risk-outcome pairs (evidence review)
2. Estimate exposure distribution (population-level)
3. Estimate relative risks (meta-analyses)
4. Calculate theoretical minimum risk exposure level (TMREL)
5. Compute PAF and attributable burden (DALYs, deaths)
6. Account for mediation (e.g., BMI → SBP → CVD)

**Mermaid: Risk Attribution Pathway**
```mermaid
flowchart LR
    A[Risk Factor\nExposure] --> B[Relative Risk\nfrom Meta-Analysis]
    C[Exposure\nDistribution] --> D[Population Attributable\nFraction (PAF)]
    B --> D
    D --> E[Attributable\nDALYs/Deaths]
    E --> F[Policy Priority\nSetting]
    style F fill:#bfb,stroke:#333
```

| Risk | TMREL |
|------|-------|
| **Tobacco** | Zero |
| **Alcohol** | Zero (GBD 2020); previously 0-1 drink/day |

| **BMI** | 20-25 kg/m² |

## 6. 6. Differential Diagnosis (GBD Confusions)

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **YLL vs YLD** | YLL = fatal burden (mortality). YLD = non-fatal burden (morbidity). NCDs = both; Injuries = mostly YLL; Mental/MSK = mostly YLD. |
| **Attributable vs Joint PAF** | Attributable = single risk factor. Joint PAF < sum of individual PAFs (risks overlap/mediation). GBD reports joint for risk clusters. |
| **TMREL Changes** | TMREL evolves with evidence (e.g., alcohol → zero; SBP lower). Affects trend comparability. |
| **Age-Standardised vs All-Age** | Age-standardised removes demographic effects (aging/pop growth). All-age = actual burden for planning. |
| **GBD vs National Estimates** | GBD uses standard methods for comparability. National estimates may use local data, different methods. |

## 7. 7. Management (Policy Application & SDGs)

**GBD for Policy:**
- **Priority Setting**: Rank risks by attributable DALYs → "Best Buys"
- **Resource Allocation**: Cost-effectiveness of targeting top risks
- **Progress Tracking**: SDG 3.4 (NCD mortality 1/3 reduction), 3.3 (end epidemics), 3.6 (RTA 1/2)
- **Equity Analysis**: Subnational, socioeconomic disparities (concentration index)
- **Forecasting**: Alternative scenarios (SDG pace, compounding risks)

**SDG 3 Targets Tracked by GBD:**
| Target | Indicator | GBD Metric |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **3.1 Maternal Mortality** | MMR | Maternal deaths/100K live births |
| **3.2 Child Mortality** | U5MR, NMR | Deaths <5y, <28d per 1000 |
| **3.3 Communicable** | HIV, TB, Malaria, NTDs, Hepatitis | Incidence, mortality, DALYs |
| **3.4 NCD Mortality** | Premature NCD mortality (30-70y) | Probability of dying 30-70 from NCD |
| **3.4 Mental Health** | Suicide mortality | Suicide rate |
| **3.5 Substance** | Alcohol, treatment coverage | Alcohol per capita, AUD treatment |
| **3.6 RTA** | RTA deaths | RTA deaths/100K |
| **3.7 Sexual/Reproductive** | Coverage, adolescent birth | Contraceptive prevalence, ABR |
| **3.8 UHC** | Service coverage index | UHC SCI (tracer interventions) |
| **3.9 Environment** | Air pollution, WASH mortality | PM2.5 attributable, unsafe WASH |

## 9. 9. Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)

| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **What is the GBD study? Who leads it?** | Global Burden of Diseases, Injuries, Risk Factors Study. Led by IHME (University of Washington) with >9000 collaborators. |
| **What are the two main burden metrics?** | YLL (Years of Life Lost - mortality) and YLD (Years Lived with Disability - morbidity). DALY = YLL + YLD. |
| **Top 3 global risk factors for DALYs (GBD 2019)?** | High systolic blood pressure, Tobacco, High fasting plasma glucose (FPG). |
| **What is the risk factor hierarchy in GBD?** | Level 1: Behavioural (tobacco, diet, alcohol). Level 2: Metabolic (SBP, BMI, FPG, LDL). Level 3: Environmental/Occupational (air pollution, WASH, occupational). Level 4: Child/Maternal. |
| **What is PAF? How calculated?** | Population Attributable Fraction = proportion of disease burden due to a risk factor. PAF = Pₑ(RR-1)/[1+Pₑ(RR-1)]. |
| **What is TMREL? Give examples.** | Theoretical Minimum Risk Exposure Level. Tobacco=zero, SBP=110-115, BMI=20-25, Alcohol=zero (2020), PM2.5=2.4-5.9 μg/m³. |
| **How does GBD track SDG 3.4 (NCD mortality)?** | Probability of dying between 30-70 from cardiovascular, cancer, diabetes, chronic respiratory. Target: reduce by 1/3 by 2030. |
| **Why is joint PAF less than sum of individual PAFs?** | Risk factors overlap (e.g., high BMI causes high SBP and high FPG). Mediation and correlation mean effects are not independent. |
| **Difference between age-standardised and all-age rates?** | Age-standardised removes demographic change (aging, population growth) for trend comparison. All-age = actual burden for health service planning. |
| **GBD 2019: Top cause of YLD?** | Low back pain. Depression #2, Headache #3, Anxiety #4, Neck pain #5. |

## 10. 10. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **GBD vs WHO Estimates** | GBD = standardised, comparable, annual. WHO = official stats for member states, may differ in methods. |
| **Risk-Outcome Pairs** | GBD only includes pairs with "convincing" or "probable" evidence (WCRF/IARC criteria). |
| **Counterfactual** | TMREL = counterfactual exposure distribution (what if everyone at minimum risk?). |
| **Disability Weights** | Derived from population surveys (paired comparison). 235 health states in GBD 2019. |

**Mnemonic: GBD METRICS (DYH)**
- **D**ALY = **Y**LL + **Y**LD
- **H**ALE = Healthy Life Expectancy

**Mnemonic: TOP RISKS 2019 (SBF-BAP)**
- **S**BP (High systolic BP)
- **B**MI (High BMI)
- **F**PG (High fasting glucose)
- **B**acco (Tobacco)
- **A**lcohol
- **P**M2.5 (Air pollution)

**Mnemonic: RISK HIERARCHY (BMEC)**
- **B**ehavioural (Level 1)
- **M**etabolic (Level 2)
- **E**nvironmental/Occupational (Level 3)
- **C**hild/Maternal (Level 4)

**Mnemonic: SDG 3 TARGETS (3.1-3.9)**
- **3.1** Maternal
- **3.2** Child
- **3.3** Communicable
- **3.4** NCD + Mental
- **3.5** Substance
- **3.6** RTA
- **3.7** Sexual/Reproductive
- **3.8** UHC
- **3.9** Environment

**Mnemonic: PAF FORMULA**
- **P**revalence × **(RR - 1)**
- **D**ivided by **[1 + P(RR-1)]**
