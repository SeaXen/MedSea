# Respiratory Disease Epidemiology (COPD, Asthma, Pneumonia, Influenza)

> [!info]
> **Respiratory diseases = Major global health burden.** **COPD = 3rd leading cause of death globally.** **Asthma = Most common chronic disease in children.** **Pneumonia = Leading infectious cause of death.** **Influenza/COVID = Pandemic potential.** **Air pollution & smoking = Major shared risk factors.**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Describe global and UK burden of COPD, asthma, pneumonia, influenza
- [ ] Apply key epidemiological measures (prevalence, incidence, mortality, DALYs)
- [ ] Identify risk factors: smoking, air pollution, occupational, infections, genetics
- [ ] Apply prevention strategies: SECURE for COPD, vaccination, air quality, smoking cessation

## 2. Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

### 2.1 Burden

| Metric | Global (2019) | UK |
|--------|---------------|-----|
| **Prevalence** | ~380 million (2019) | **1.2M diagnosed** (2M+ undiagnosed) |
| **Deaths/year** | **3.2M** (3rd leading cause) | **~30,000** (5% of deaths) |
| **DALYs** | ~74 million | ~1.2M DALYs |
| **Cost** | >$2 trillion global (2010) | **NHS £1.9B/year** |

### 2.2 Risk Factors

| Category | Factors | Population Attributable Fraction (PAF) |
|----------|---------|----------------------------------------|
| **Smoking** | **Active** (>80% COPD), passive | **~70–80%** |
| **Air Pollution** | **Ambient PM2.5** (outdoor), Household (biomass fuel) | **~15–20%** |
| **Occupational** | Dusts (silica, coal, cotton), fumes (welding), gases (cadmium) | **~15%** |
| **Genetics** | **Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency** (PiZZ) | 1–2% of COPD |
| **Early Life** | Low birth weight, childhood infections, maternal smoking | Early life programming |
| **Ageing** | Lung function declines with age | Accelerated in smokers |

### 2.3 SECURE Strategy (COPD Management Framework)

| Letter | Component | Key Actions |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **S** | **Smoking Cessation** | **Most effective intervention**; NRT, varenicline, behavioural support |
| **E** | **Exercise** (Pulmonary Rehabilitation) | **Gold standard**; improves exercise capacity, QoL, reduces admissions |
| **C** | **Comorbidity Management** | Cardiovascular, osteoporosis, anxiety/depression, diabetes, GERD |
| **U** | **Understand Medication** | Inhaler technique, adherence, stepping up/down (ICS/LABA/LAMA) |
| **R** | **Rescue Pack / Rescue Plan** | Antibiotics + steroids at home; action plan |
| **E** | **Exacerbation Prevention** | Vaccines (flu, pneumococcal, COVID), self-management plan |

## 3. Asthma

### 2.1 Burden

| Metric | Global | UK |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **Prevalence** | **~260 million** (2019) | **~5.4M** (8% adults, 9% children) |
| **Deaths/year** | ~455,000 | ~1,400 (preventable) |
| **DALYs** | ~22M | ~600,000 DALYs |
| **Childhood Onset** | **Most common chronic disease in children** | 1 in 11 children |

### 2.2 Risk Factors

| Category | Factors |
|----------|---------|
| **Genetic** | Atopy (family history), IL-4/IL-13 pathways, filaggrin mutations |
| **Environmental** | **Allergens** (house dust mite, pet dander, pollen, mould), **Air pollution** (PM2.5, NO₂, O₃), **Tobacco smoke** (prenatal + passive) |
| **Infections** | **RSV bronchiolitis** (early life), viral exacerbations (rhinovirus) |
| **Occupational** | **~15% adult asthma**; isocyanates, flour, latex, animals, wood dust |
| **Obesity** | Mechanical + inflammatory (adipokines) |
| **Early Life** | Antibiotic use, C-section, low microbial diversity (hygiene hypothesis) |

### 2.3 Asthma Epidemiology by Age

| Age Group | Pattern |
|-----------|---------|
| **Childhood** | Male > Female; atopic; viral triggers; remission possible in adolescence |
| **Adult-Onset** | Female > Male; often non-atopic; more severe; occupational triggers |
| **Elderly** | Underdiagnosed; overlap with COPD (ACOS); polypharmacy |

## 4. Pneumonia

### 2.1 Burden

| Metric | Global | UK |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **Incidence** | ~450M cases/yr (LRTI) | ~220,000 hospital admissions/yr |
| **Deaths** | **~2.5M** (leading infectious killer) | **~30,000/yr** (6th leading cause) |
| **High-Risk Groups** | <5y, >65y, immunocompromised, chronic disease | Age >65 = 10× risk |

### 2.2 Aetiology

| Setting | Common Pathogens |
|---------|------------------|
| **CAP (Community-Acquired)** | *S. pneumoniae* (most common), *H. influenzae*, *M. pneumoniae*, *C. pneumoniae*, *Legionella*, Viruses (Flu, RSV, COVID) |
| **HAP (Hospital-Acquired)** | *S. aureus* (MRSA), *P. aeruginosa*, *Klebsiella*, *Acinetobacter* |
| **VAP** | *P. aeruginosa*, *S. aureus*, *Enterobacteriaceae* |
| **Aspiration** | Anaerobes, *S. pneumoniae*, *S. aureus* |

### 2.3 Prevention

| Strategy | Intervention |
|----------|--------------|
| **Vaccination** | **Pneumococcal** (PCV13/PCV20 kids; PPV23/PCV20 adults >65/risk), **Influenza** (annual), **Hib**, **COVID-19** |
| **Smoking Cessation** | Major risk reduction |
| **Aspiration Prevention** | HOB elevation, oral care, dysphagia management |
| **Aspiration Pneumonia** | HOB elevation, oral care, dysphagia screening |
| **HAP/VAP Prevention** | HOB 30°, sedation holiday, oral chlorhexidine, subglottic suction |

## 5. Influenza

### 3.1 Epidemiology

| Parameter | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Seasonality** | **Winter** (Northern Hemisphere: Dec–Mar); Year-round tropics |
| **Attack Rate** | 5–10% adults; 20–30% children annually |
| **Burden** | **3–5M severe cases**, 290–650k deaths/yr globally; UK ~10–20k deaths/yr |
| **High-Risk** | >65y, <5y, pregnant, chronic disease, immunosuppressed, care home residents |

### 3.2 Virus Types & Evolution

| Type | Characteristics |
|------|-----------------|
| **Influenza A** | **Pandemic potential**; H1N1, H3N2 subtypes; antigenic drift/shift |
| **Influenza B** | Seasonal; two lineages (Victoria, Yamagata); less severe than A |
| **Influenza C** | Mild; sporadic |
| **Influenza D** | Cattle; not human pathogen |

| Evolution Mechanism | Description |
|---------------------|-------------|
| **Antigenic Drift** | Point mutations in HA/NA → seasonal epidemics |
| **Antigenic Shift** | Reassortment → new HA/NA subtype → **Pandemic** |

### 3.3 Vaccination

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Composition** | Updated biannually (WHO GISRS); Trivalent/Quadrivalent (A/H1N1, A/H3N2, B/Victoria, B/Yamagata) |
| **Effectiveness** | **40–60%** (varies by match, age, immune status) |
| **Target Groups (UK)** | >65y, <5y, pregnant, chronic disease, immunosuppressed, care home residents, HCWs, carers |
| **Vaccine Types** | Inactivated (IIV), Live Attenuated (LAIV, nasal, children), Recombinant (Flublok), Adjuvanted (aIIV, >65) |
| **Effectiveness** | **40–60%** overall; lower in elderly; reduces severity even if infection occurs |

|--------|------------|

## 7. Air Pollution & Respiratory Health

| Pollutant | Sources | Respiratory Effects | Cardiovascular |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|----------------|
| **PM2.5** | Combustion (traffic, industry, domestic) | COPD exacerbation, asthma, lung cancer, reduced lung growth | MI, stroke, HF, arrhythmia |
| **PM10** | Dust, construction, roads | Chronic bronchitis, reduced lung function | |
| **NO₂** | Traffic, gas cooking | Asthma exacerbation, airway hyperresponsiveness | |
| **O₃** | Photochemical (NOx + VOCs) | Airway inflammation, reduced lung function | |
| **SO₂** | Coal, shipping | Bronchoconstriction (asthma) | |
| **Indoor** | Biomass fuel (3B people), radon, VOCs | COPD (women in LMICs), lung cancer, ALRI | |

| Metric | WHO Air Quality Guidelines (2021) |
|----------|-----------------------------------|
| **PM2.5 Annual** | **5 µg/m³** |
| **PM2.5 24-hr** | 15 µg/m³ |
| **PM10 Annual** | 15 µg/m³ |
| **NO₂ Annual** | 10 µg/m³ |
| **O₃ 8-hr** | 100 µg/m³ |

## 10. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **COPD vs Asthma** | COPD = irreversible, smoking; Asthma = reversible, atopic, variable |
| **COPD vs Asthma Exacerbation** | COPD: ↑ dyspnoea, sputum volume/purulence; Asthma: wheeze, PEF drop |
| **CAP vs HAP vs VAP** | CAP = community; HAP = >48h hospital; VAP = >48h ventilator |
| **Influenza A vs B** | A = pandemic potential (drift/shift); B = seasonal, two lineages |
| **Antigenic Drift vs Shift** | Drift = point mutations (seasonal); Shift = reassortment (pandemic) |
| **COPD Exacerbation** | Anthonisen: ↑ Dyspnoea, ↑ Sputum volume, ↑ Purulence (2/3 = treat) |
| **Asthma Severity** | Life-threatening: PEF <33%, SpO₂ <92%, silent chest, cyanosis |
| **Pneumonia CURB-65** | Confusion, Urea>7, RR≥30, BP<90/60, age≥65 (Score ≥2 = admit) |
| **COPD vs Asthma** | COPD = fixed obstruction, smoking; Asthma = reversible, variable, atopic |
| **Pneumonia Types** | CAP (community), HAP (hospital >48h), VAP (ventilator >48h) |
| **Influenza Vaccine** | Annual; Quadri/Trivalent; LAIV (kids); aIIV (elderly) |
| **COPD Management** | SECURE: Smoking, Exercise, Comorbidities, Understand meds, Rescue, Exacerbation prevention |
| **Air Pollution PM2.5** | WHO 5 µg/m³ annual; Major COPD/asthma/lung cancer risk |
| **Occupational Asthma** | 15% adult asthma; Isocyanates, flour, latex, animals |
| **Long COVID** | Symptoms >12 weeks; Multisystem; 10–20% infected |

## 13. Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)

| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Global COPD burden and top risk factor?** | ~3.2M deaths/year (3rd leading cause globally). Smoking 70-80% PAF. ~1.2M diagnosed in UK. |
| **Asthma prevalence and key risks?** | ~262M global; ~5.4M UK (8% adults, 9% children). Atopy 30-50%, air pollution, occupational 15%, obesity. |
| **Anthonisen criteria for COPD exacerbation?** | 3 cardinal symptoms: ↑ Dyspnoea, ↑ Sputum volume, ↑ Sputum purulence. 2/3 = treat with antibiotics ± steroids. |
| **CURB-65 score for pneumonia?** | **C**onfusion, **U**rea >7, **R**R ≥30, **B**P <90/60, **A**ge ≥65. Score 0-1: outpatient; 2: admit; 3-5: severe/admit. |
| **Pneumonia pathogens - CAP vs HAP/VAP?** | CAP: S. pneumoniae > H. influenzae > Mycoplasma > Viruses. HAP/VAP: S. aureus, Pseudomonas, Klebsiella. |
| **COPD vs Asthma spirometry?** | Both: FEV1/FVC <0.7 obstruction. COPD: limited reversibility (<12%/200ml post-BD). Asthma: significant reversibility (>12%/200ml), variable. |
| **Influenza A vs B - which causes pandemics?** | Influenza A: pandemic potential (antigenic shift = HA/NA reassortment). Influenza B: seasonal, two lineages (Victoria, Yamagata). |
| **Antigenic drift vs shift?** | Drift: point mutations in HA/NA (seasonal epidemics, annual vaccine). Shift: reassortment of HA/NA (pandemics every 10-40 years). |
| **Air pollution - main culprit for respiratory disease?** | PM2.5 (fine particulate matter). WHO guideline 5 μg/m³ annual mean. Major risk for COPD, asthma, lung cancer, CVD. |
| **Occupational asthma - commonest causes?** | Isocyanates (paint, foam), flour (bakers), animal dander (vets), latex (HCW), wood dust, metal salts. ~15% of adult asthma. |
