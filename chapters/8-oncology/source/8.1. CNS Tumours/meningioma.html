CHAPTER 8 ONCOLOGY / 8.1. CNS TUMOURS
Meningioma
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Apply WHO CNS5 Grading (Grade 1, 2, 3) based on Mitoses, Brain Invasion, Criteria
  • [ ] Perform Simpson Resection Grading and Target Grade I (Complete + Dura/Bone)
  • [ ] Indicate Adjuvant RT for Grade 2/3, Subtotal Resection, Recurrence
  • [ ] Choose SRS vs FSRT for Residual/Recurrent Small Lesions
  • [ ] Recognise Molecular Subgroups: NF2 (Merlin), TRAF7, KLF4, AKT1, SMO, PIK3CA
  • [ ] Manage Skull Base / Optic Nerve Sheath Meningiomas (Multidisciplinary)
  • [ ] Counsel on Surveillance and Recurrence Risk by Grade/Simpson/Extent
2 Definition & Epidemiology
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Definition**Tumour of **Arachnoid Cap Cells** (Meninges); **Extra-axial**, **Dural-Based**
**Incidence****Most Common Primary CNS Tumour (30-40%)**: ~8-10/100,000/year
**Prevalence****Female Predominance (2:1)**; Hormone Receptor Positive (PR+)
**Peak Age****50-70 years**
**Risk Factors****Ionising Radiation** (Prior RT, Tinea Capitis), **Neurofibromatosis Type 2 (NF2)**, **Hormonal** (Oral Contraceptives, HRT, Pregnancy Growth), **Obesity**
3 WHO CNS5 Classification (Grades 1-3)
GradeCriteria (Any One Sufficient)Frequency10-yr PFS
------------------------------------------------------------
**Grade 1 (Benign)****<4 Mitoses/10 HPF**, **No Brain Invasion**, **None of Grade 2/3 Criteria****80-85%****>90%**
**Grade 2 (Atypical)****4-19 Mitoses/10 HPF** **OR** **Brain Invasion** **OR** **≥3 of: High Cellularity, Small Cells, Prominent Nucleoli, Sheeting, Necrosis****15-18%****~60-70%**
**Grade 3 (Anaplastic/Malignant)****≥20 Mitoses/10 HPF** **OR** **Frank Anaplasia (Carcinoma/Sarcoma/Melanoma-like)****1-3%****~30-40%**
Note: Brain Invasion = Irregular Infiltration of Brain Parenchyma (Not Just Abutment)

5 Clinical Features
FeatureDescription
----------------------
**Asymptomatic****Incidental (20-30%)** on Imaging
**Headache**Non-Specific, Progressive
**Seizures****20-30%** (Convexity Lesions)
**Focal Deficits**Motor, Sensory, Cranial Nerve (Depends on Location)

7 Simpson Resection Grades (Extent of Resection)
GradeDescription10-yr Recurrence
--------------------------------------
**Grade I****Complete Removal** of Tumour, **Dura**, **Abnormal Bone****9%**
**Grade II**Complete Tumour Removal + **Coagulation of Dural Attachment****19%**
**Grade III**Complete Tumour Removal **WITHOUT** Dural Coagulation/Resection**29%**
**Grade IV****Subtotal Resection****40%**
**Grade V****Biopsy Only****~100%**
Goal: Simpson Grade I (or II) for Grade 1; Grade I Mandatory for Grade 2/3

ScenarioManagement
----------------------

13 Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)
QuestionExpected Answer
---------------------------
**55F, Incidental 2cm Convexity Meningioma, Asymptomatic. Management?****Observation + Serial MRI (q6-12mo)** — **Indications for Treatment: Growth, Symptoms, Hydrocephalus**.
**Same Patient, Tumour Grows to 3.5cm at 18mo. Surgery — Target Simpson Grade?****Simpson Grade I (Complete Tumour + Dura + Abnormal Bone)** — **10-yr Recurrence 9%**.
**Simpson Grade I vs II — Difference?****Grade I: Tumour + Dura + Bone**; **Grade II: Tumour + Coagulated Dura (No Bone Resection)**; **Recurrence: 9% vs 19%**.
**Grade 2 Meningioma, Simpson GTR — Adjuvant?****Adjuvant RT (54-60Gy/30fx)** — **Standard** (Reduces Recurrence from ~40% to ~15%).
**Grade 2 Meningioma, STR — Management?****Adjuvant RT (54-60Gy) Mandatory** — **SRS/FSRT for Small Residual**.
**Skull Base Meningioma (Cavernous Sinus) — Primary Treatment?****SRS/FSRT** — **Surgery High Morbidity (Cranial Nerves, ICA)**; **Surgery for Decompression if Mass Effect**.
**Optic Nerve Sheath Meningioma — Vision Loss, Primary?****SRS/FSRT** — **Surgery = Blindness Risk**, **RT Preserves Vision**.
**NF2 Mutation — Associated Tumours?****Meningioma (Multiple), Vestibular Schwannoma (Bilateral = NF2), Ependymoma, Cataract**.
**SRS vs FSRT — Indications?****SRS: Single Fraction, <3cm, Away from Optic Apparatus**; **FSRT: >3cm, Near Optic Nerve/Chiasm, Hypofractionated (5-25fx)**.
**Recurrence Surveillance Grade 1 vs 2?****Gr1: MRI q6-12mo ×5yr**; **Gr2: MRI q6mo ×3yr, then q12mo ×2yr**.
14 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**Grade 1 vs 2 — Mitoses Threshold****Grade 1: <4/10 HPF**; **Grade 2: 4-19/10 HPF**; **Grade 3: ≥20/10 HPF**
**Brain Invasion vs Abutment****Invasion = Irregular Infiltration into Parenchyma (Grade 2)**; **Abutment = Clear Plane (Grade 1)**
**Simpson Grade 0?****Not in Original Classification**; Some Add **Grade 0 = GTR + Dura + Bone + Venous Sinus Reconstruction**
**SRS vs FSRT for Optic Apparatus****SRS Contraindicated if Within 2-3mm of Optic Nerve/Chiasm**; **FSRT (5-25fx) Spares Optic Structures**
**PR Status****PR+ (80%)**, **Hormone Sensitive** (Growth in Pregnancy); **PR- = Worse Prognosis**
**NF2 vs Non-NF2****NF2: Fibrous/Transitional, Spinal, Parasagittal**; **Non-NF2: Secretory, Meningothelial, Angiomatous, Higher Grade**

Mnemonic: MENINGIOMA

  • Most Common Primary CNS Tumour (30-40%)
  • Epithelial (Meningothelial), Fibrous, Transitional, Psammoma Bodies
  • NF2 Mutation (50%, Merlin, 22q), TRAF7 (20%)
  • Incidental 20-30%, Female 2:1, PR+ (Hormone Sensitive)
  • New WHO Grades: Gr1 (<4 Mitoses), Gr2 (4-19/Brain Invasion), Gr3 (≥20/Anaplasia)
  • Grade 1: Obs/Surgery; Gr2: RT Adjuvant; Gr3: Mandatory RT
  • Invasion: Brain Invasion = Gr2 (NOT Abutment)
  • Operation: Simpson Grade I (Best): Tumour + Dura + Bone
  • MRI: Homogeneous Enhancement, Dural Tail, Broad Base, Hyperostosis
  • Optic Sheath: SRS/FSRT (Surgery = Blindness)
  • Angiomatous/Clear Cell/Chordoid: Higher Grade Potential
