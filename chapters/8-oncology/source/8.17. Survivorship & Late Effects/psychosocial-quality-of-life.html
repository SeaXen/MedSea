SEA KNOWLEDGE STUDY NOTES
CHAPTER 8 ONCOLOGY / 8.17. SURVIVORSHIP & LATE EFFECTS
Psychosocial Quality Of Life
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Screen for distress, depression, anxiety using validated tools
  • [ ] Assess fear of recurrence and its impact on QOL
  • [ ] Evaluate body image, sexual function, cognitive function in survivors
  • [ ] Screen for financial toxicity and return-to-work barriers
  • [ ] Apply evidence-based interventions: CBT, ACT, Mindfulness, Exercise, Support Groups
  • [ ] Prescribe pharmacotherapy for depression/anxiety when indicated
  • [ ] Address return-to-work, financial toxicity, social isolation
2 Distress Screening
NCCN Distress Thermometer (DT)
ToolFormatCutoffAction
------------------------------
**Distress Thermometer (DT)****0-10 Visual Analogue Scale** + **Problem List** (Practical, Family, Emotional, Spiritual, Physical)**≥4** = **Significant Distress** → **Referral to Psychosocial Services****Screen at Diagnosis, Key Transitions, Routine Intervals**
Problem List Domains
DomainExamples
------------------
**Practical**Housing, Insurance, Transportation, Work/School, Childcare
**Family**Partner, Children, Caregiver Strain, Communication
**Emotional**Depression, Anxiety, Fear, Anger, Guilt, Loss of Control
**Spiritual/Religious**Meaning, Faith, Existential Distress
**Physical**Pain, Fatigue, Sleep, Appetite, Nausea, Constipation, Neuropathy, Sexual, Concentration/Memory
3 Depression & Anxiety Screening
Validated Tools
ToolItemsCutoffPopulation
---------------------------------
**HADS (Hospital Anxiety and Depression Scale)****14 Items (7 Anxiety, 7 Depression)****≥8** (Subthreshold), **≥11** (Probable)**Validated in Cancer**, **No Somatic Items**
**PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9)****9 Items (DSM-5 Criteria)****≥10** (Moderate), **≥15** (Moderately Severe), **≥20** (Severe)**Widely Used**, **Tracks Severity**
**GAD-7 (Generalised Anxiety Disorder-7)****7 Items****≥10** (Moderate), **≥15** (Severe)**Specific for Anxiety**
**PHQ-2 / GAD-2****2 Items Each****≥3** (Screen Positive)**Ultra-Brief**, **Rule-In**
Clinical Approach
StepAction
--------------
**1. Screen****DT ≥4 OR PHQ-2 ≥3 OR GAD-2 ≥3** → **Full Assessment**
**2. Assess Severity****PHQ-9, GAD-7, HADS**
**3. Risk Assessment****Suicidality (PHQ-9 Item 9), Self-Harm, Psychosis**
**4. Treat/Refer****Mild: Watchful Waiting + Psychoeducation**; **Moderate: CBT/SSRIs**; **Severe: Psychiatry Referral + Pharmacotherapy**
4 Fear of Cancer Recurrence (FCR)
Definition & Prevalence
AspectDetail
----------------
**Definition****Fear, Worry, Concern** about Cancer Returning/Progressing
**Prevalence****30-70%** of Survivors (Highest in First 2-3 Years)
**Impact****Reduced QOL**, **Avoidance of Follow-Up**, **Excessive Body Checking**, **Healthcare Overutilisation**
Assessment Tools
ToolItemsCutoff
---------------------
**FCRI (Fear of Cancer Recurrence Inventory)****42 Items (7 Subscales)****≥13 (Short Form)** / **≥34 (Full)** = Clinical FCR
**CARS (Cancer Recurrence Worry Scale)****4 Items****≥8** = High FCR
**Single Item****"How fearful are you of recurrence?"** (0-10)**≥4** = Clinically Significant
Management
ApproachIntervention
------------------------
**Psychoeducation****Normalise Fear**, **Provide Accurate Recurrence Statistics**, **Differentiate Awareness from Fear**
**CBT/Metacognitive Therapy****Identify/Challenge Catastrophic Thoughts**, **Behavioural Experiments** (Reduce Checking)
**Mindfulness/ACT****Acceptance of Uncertainty**, **Values-Based Action**
**Exposure Therapy****Gradual Exposure to Avoided Stimuli** (Follow-Up Visits, Body Scans)
**Psychoeducation + CBT****Most Evidence-Based**, **Reduces FCR Severity**

**Radiation Changes****Fibrosis, Pigmentation, Telangiectasia, Breast Shrinkage****Patient-Reported Outcome Measures (PROMs)**
**Alopecia****Chemotherapy, Cranial RT****Distress Thermometer**

Sexual Dysfunction
PopulationCommon IssuesAssessment
---------------------------------------

**Men****Erectile Dysfunction (Prostate Surgery/RT/ADT), Low Libido, Ejaculatory Changes, Body Image****IIEF (International Index of Erectile Function)**, **SHIM**
Management
IssueIntervention
---------------------
**Vaginal Dryness/Dyspareunia****Vaginal Moisturisers (Regular), Lubricants (During), Topical Oestrogen (If Safe)**, **Dilators, Pelvic Floor PT**
**Erectile Dysfunction****PDE5 Inhibitors (Sildenafil, Tadalafil)**, **Vacuum Device, Penile Injections, Implant**, **Penile Rehab (Early Post-Prostatectomy)**

6 Cognitive Impairment ("Chemo Brain")
Definition & Prevalence
FeatureDetail
----------------
**Definition****Subjective/Objective Cognitive Decline** (Memory, Attention, Executive Function, Processing Speed)
**Prevalence****15-75%** (Varies by Assessment, Timing, Population)
**Risk Factors****Chemotherapy (Anthracyclines, Alkylators, Taxanes, Platinum), Cranial RT, Hormonal Therapy, Age, Pre-Existing Cognitive Impairment, APOE ε4, Fatigue, Depression, Anxiety**
Affected Domains
DomainExamples
------------------
**Executive Function****Planning, Organising, Multitasking, Decision-Making**
**Memory****Working Memory, Verbal/Visual Recall**
**Attention/Processing Speed****Sustained Attention, Reaction Time, Mental Flexibility**
**Language****Word-Finding, Verbal Fluency**
Assessment
ToolDomainFormat
----------------------
**FACT-Cog****Self-Report****PROM**
**EORTC QLQ-C30 + QLQ-CIPN20****Self-Report + Neuropathy****PROM**
**MoCA (Montreal Cognitive Assessment)****Objective Screening****30-Point, 10-Min**
**Formal Neuropsychological Battery****Comprehensive****Gold Standard** (Time-Intensive)
Management
ApproachIntervention
------------------------
**Cognitive Rehabilitation****Computerised Training (Cogmed, Lumosity)**, **Strategy Training (Compensatory Strategies)**
**Physical Exercise****Aerobic + Resistance (3x/week, 150min/week)** — **Strongest Evidence**
**Pharmacological****Methylphenidate 5-20mg/d** (Psychostimulant), **Modafinil 100-200mg** (Limited Evidence), **Donepezil** (No Benefit in RCTs)
**Compensatory Strategies****Planners, Reminders, Chunking, Prioritisation, Environmental Modification**
**Treat Reversible Causes****Anaemia, Thyroid, B12/Folate, Depression, Anxiety, Sleep Apnoea, Medication Review**

Assessment
ToolFormat
--------------

**EORTC QLQ-FA12****12 Items, Fatigue Module**

ModalityIntervention

Common Issues
IssuePrevalenceCauses
---------------------------
**Insomnia****30-50%****Pain, Anxiety, Depression, Steroids, Hormonal Therapy, Night Sweats, Nocturia**
**Circadian Disruption****Common****Hospitalisation, Chemo Cycles, Reduced Light Exposure**
**Sleep Apnoea****Higher in Cancer****Obesity, Opioids, Steroids, Head/Neck RT**
Management
ApproachIntervention
------------------------
**CBT-I (Gold Standard)****Sleep Restriction, Stimulus Control, Sleep Hygiene, Cognitive Restructuring**
**Pharmacological****Short-Term Hypnotics (Zolpidem 5-10mg, Zopiclone 3.75-7.5mg)**, **Melatonin 2-5mg**, **Trazodone 25-50mg ON**, **Mirtazapine 15-30mg ON**

**Treat Underlying****Pain, Anxiety, Depression, Sleep Apnoea (CPAP), Steroids (Minimise/Taper)**
9 Financial Toxicity
Definition & Impact
AspectDetail
----------------
**Definition****Patient-Level Financial Hardship** from Cancer Care Costs
**Components****Out-of-Pocket (OOP) Costs, Lost Income, Insurance Gaps, Asset Depletion**
**Risk Factors****Young Age, Low Income, Uninsured/Underinsured, Advanced Stage, Intensive Treatment, Long Duration**
**Consequences****Treatment Non-Adherence, Delayed Care, Bankruptcy, Reduced QOL, Mortality ↑**
Screening & Management
ToolDescription
-------------------
**COST (Comprehensive Score for Financial Toxicity)****11-Item PROM, Validated**
**NCCN Distress Thermometer****Financial Problem List Item**
ManagementAction
--------------------

**Insurance Optimisation****Appeal Denials, Co-Pay Assistance, Foundation Grants, Pharma Patient Assistance**
**Income Support****Disability Benefits, Sick Pay, Charity Grants**
**Employment Rights****Flexible Working, Phased Return, Legal Protections (Equality Act 2010/ADA)**

14 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**HADS vs PHQ-9 vs GAD-7****HADS**: No Somatic Items (Valid in Cancer), **Anxiety + Depression**; **PHQ-9**: Depression Only, DSM-5; **GAD-7**: Anxiety Only
**FCR vs Normal Concern****FCR**: **Disproportionate, Persistent, Impairs Function**, **Avoidance/Checking Behaviours**; **Normal: Transient, Proportional**
**Chemo Brain vs Dementia****Chemo Brain**: Subtle, Often Improves, Functional; **Dementia**: Progressive, Impairs ADLs, Structural Brain Changes
**CRF vs Normal Fatigue****CRF**: **Disproportionate to Activity**, **Not Relieved by Rest**, **Persistent (>6mo)**
**Insomnia vs Sleep Deprivation****Insomnia**: **Opportunity to Sleep but Can't**; **Deprivation: No Opportunity**
**Financial Toxicity vs Cost****Toxicity**: **Patient Harm (Non-Adherence, Bankruptcy, Distress)**; **Cost: Dollar Amount**
**RTW Phased vs Gradual****Phased**: Structured Plan (Hours/Duties); **Gradual: Informal**

Mnemonic: PSYCHOSOCIAL-QOL

  • Psychosocial: Core of Survivorship
  • Screening: DT ≥4, HADS, PHQ-9, GAD-7, FCRI
  • Y fear: Fear of Recurrence (30-70%), CBT, Mindfulness, ACT
  • Cognitive: "Chemo Brain" (Executive, Memory, Speed), Exercise Best
  • Heart: Body Image (Surgery/RT/Stoma/Alopecia), BIS/BSQ
  • Opportunity: Sexual Health (Women: Moisturise/Dilators, Men: PDE5i)
  • Sleep: CBT-I (Gold Standard), Short-Term Hypnotics
  • Only Fatigue: CRF (Disproportionate, Persistent), Exercise Best
  • Cognitive: Compensatory Strategies (Planners, Chunking)
  • Insomnia: CBT-I (Gold Std), Melatonin, Short-Term Hypnotics
  • Anxiety/Depression: HADS/PHQ-9/GAD-7, CBT, SSRIs/SNRIs
  • Legal/Work: RTW (Phased, Accommodations, Equality Act/ADA)
  • Quality of Life: FACT-G, EORTC QLQ-C30, WHOQOL
  • Out-of-Pocket: Financial Toxicity (COST Tool, Navigation)
  • Loneliness/Isolation: Support Groups, Caregiver Support (Zarit)
