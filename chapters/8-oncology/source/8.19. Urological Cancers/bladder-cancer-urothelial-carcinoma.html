CHAPTER 8 ONCOLOGY / 8.19. UROLOGICAL CANCERS
Bladder Cancer Urothelial Carcinoma
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Distinguish NMIBC (Ta/Tis/T1) vs MIBC (T2-4) management pathways
  • [ ] Apply EAU/EORTC risk tables for NMIBC recurrence/progression
  • [ ] Define BCG regimen: Induction (6 weekly) + Maintenance (SWOG 8507: 3 weekly at 3,6,12,18,24,30,36mo)
  • [ ] Recognise BCG-unresponsive definition and treatment options (ICI, Enfortumab, Cystectomy)
  • [ ] Sequence MIBC: Neoadjuvant chemo (GC preferred) → Radical Cystectomy + Extended PLND → Adjuvant Nivolumab (CheckMate 274)
  • [ ] Apply Galsky/Bellmunt criteria for cisplatin eligibility
  • [ ] Sequence Metastatic: 1L GC/DD-MVAC → Maintenance Avelumab; Cisplatin-ineligible: ICI/GemCarbo → 2L Enfortumab vedotin / Erdafitinib (FGFR3)
2 Definition & Epidemiology
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Definition**Malignant tumour of urothelium; **Urothelial carcinoma (UC) = 90%**; Variants: Squamous, Glandular, Micropapillary, Nested, Plasmacytoid, Sarcomatoid
**Incidence**UK: ~10,300/year; 4th most common (M), 11th (F); M:F = 3-4:1
**Prevalence**5-year OS: NMIBC ~80-90%, MIBC ~50%, Metastatic ~10%
**Peak Age**70-80 years
**Sex Ratio**Male 3-4:1
**Risk Factors****Smoking** (↑3-4x, 50% attributable), **Occupational**: Aromatic amines (dyes, rubber, leather), **Aristolochic acid** (Chinese herbs), **Chronic inflammation**: Schistosoma haematobium (squamous), Indwelling catheters, Stones; **Prior Cyclophosphamide** (acrolein), **Pelvic RT**; **Lynch Syndrome** (MSH2/MSH6)

4 Clinical Features
FeatureDescription
----------------------
**Painless Haematuria****Most common (80-90%)**; Macroscopic or Microscopic; **Never ignore** — 2-week rule
**Irritative Voiding**Dysuria, frequency, urgency (CIS, trigone tumours)
**Obstructive Symptoms**Advanced: Flank pain (ureteric obstruction), renal failure
**Metastatic**Bone pain, lymphadenopathy, weight loss, cough (lung mets)
**Palpable Mass**Advanced MIBC
5 Staging & Classification
SystemDetail
----------------
**TNM 8th Edition****Ta**: Non-invasive papillary; **Tis**: CIS (flat high-grade); **T1**: Lamina propria; **T2**: Muscularis propria (T2a inner half, T2b outer half); **T3**: Perivesical fat (T3a micro, T3b macro); **T4**: Adjacent organs (prostate, uterus, vagina, pelvic wall)
**N Stage**N1: Single regional node; N2: Multiple regional; N3: Common iliac nodes
**M Stage**M1a: Non-regional nodes; M1b: Distant mets
**Grade (WHO 2016/2022)****Low-grade** (PUNLMP → LGUC); **High-grade** (HGUC) — **Replaces 1973 Grade 1/2/3**
**Risk Stratification (EAU/EORTC)****Low**: Single, primary, Ta, LG, <3cm, no CIS
**Intermediate**: Not low, not high (multiple, recurrent, >3cm, Ta LG, T1 LG)
**High**: **Any T1 HG, Any CIS, Any Ta HG, Multiple/large/recurrent HG**
6 Diagnosis & Investigations
InvestigationRoleKey Findings
-----------------------------------
**Cystoscopy****Gold standard** — Visualisation, TURBTWhite light + **Narrow Band Imaging (NBI) / Blue Light (Cysview)** for CIS detection
**TURBT****Diagnosis + Staging + Treatment****Must include detrusor muscle** (except Ta LG); Re-TURBT if T1 or incomplete
**Urine Cytology**Adjunct for CIS/High-gradeSensitivity: CIS 80%, HG 50%, LG <10%
**Urine Biomarkers**NMP22, UroVysion FISH, CxbladderNot standard; Adjunct for surveillance
**CT Urogram / MRI**Staging (MIBC/Metastatic), Upper tract**CTU**: Excretory phase for urothelial enhancement; **MRI**: Local staging if CT equivocal
**Chest CT**Pulmonary metsStandard for MIBC/Metastatic
**Bone Scan / PET-CT**Symptomatic bone; PET for equivocalNot routine for NMIBC
**Biomarkers (Metastatic)****FGFR3 mut/fusion**, **PD-L1**, **TMB**, **MSI**, **HER2**Guide targeted/ICI therapy
7 Differential Diagnosis
ConditionDistinguishing Features
------------------------------------
**UTI**Dysuria, frequency, pyuria, culture +ve; Haematuria resolves with antibiotics
**Bladder Stone**Irritative symptoms, haematuria; CT: Calculus
**Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia**Obstructive symptoms, microscopic haematuria; No mass on cystoscopy
**Prostate Cancer**Elevated PSA, prostate nodules on DRE; Cystoscopy may show prostatic urethral invasion
**Upper Tract Urothelial Carcinoma (UTUC)**Flank pain, haematuria; CTU shows ureteric/renal pelvis mass; **Same biology as bladder UC**
**Schistosomiasis**Endemic areas; Squamous differentiation; Calcified bladder wall
**Radiation Cystitis**Prior pelvic RT; Telangiectasia on cystoscopy; No mass

BCG-Unresponsive Definition (FDA/EAU):

  • CIS: Persistent/recurrent CIS at 6mo (after adequate BCG: ≥5 induction + ≥2 maintenance OR ≥2 induction + ≥1 maintenance)
  • Ta/T1 HG: Recurrence at 6mo (after adequate BCG)
  • T1 HG: Any recurrence at 3mo

10 Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)
QuestionExpected Answer
---------------------------
**65M, painless macroscopic haematuria. Cystoscopy: solitary 2cm papillary tumour. TURBT: Ta Low-grade. Management?****Low-risk NMIBC** → **Single post-TURBT chemo instillation** (MMC/Epirubicin/Gem) within 24h → **Surveillance**: Cystoscopy 3mo, then q6-12mo.
**Same patient but T1 High-grade. Management?****High-risk NMIBC** → **Re-TURBT** (mandatory) → If residual T1 → **Radical Cystectomy**; If Ta/Tis → **BCG Induction + Maintenance (SWOG 8507)**.
**What is BCG maintenance schedule (SWOG 8507)?****3 weekly instillations at 3, 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36 months** (Total 21 maintenance + 6 induction = 27 instillations).
**Define BCG-unresponsive NMIBC.****CIS**: Persistent/recurrent at 6mo after adequate BCG (≥5 ind + ≥2 maint OR ≥2 ind + ≥1 maint).
**Ta/T1 HG**: Recurrence at 6mo after adequate BCG.
**T1 HG**: Any recurrence at 3mo.
**MIBC — neoadjuvant regimen of choice?****Gemcitabine + Cisplatin (GC)** ×3-4 cycles (preferred over DD-MVAC for tolerability).
**Adjuvant nivolumab for MIBC — who? Trial?****CheckMate 274**: **ypT1-4 or ypN+** after neoadjuvant chemo + radical cystectomy → **Nivolumab 240mg q2w ×1 year** (DFS benefit HR 0.70).
**Cisplatin-ineligible metastatic — 1L options?****Pembrolizumab** (KEYNOTE-052) / **Atezolizumab** (IMvigor210) if **PD-L1 CPS≥10 or IC2/3**; **Gemcitabine + Carboplatin** if PD-L1 low/unfit for ICI.
**JAVELIN Bladder 100 — maintenance what?****Avelumab 10mg/kg q2w** after **4-6 cycles 1L platinum chemo without progression** → **OS HR 0.69**.
**Enfortumab vedotin — target, indication, trial?****Anti-Nectin-4 ADC**; **EV-301**: Post-PD-1 + Platinum → **OS HR 0.70**; Dose 1.25mg/kg d1,8,15 q4w.
**Erdafitinib — target, indication, monitoring?****FGFR3 mut/fusion**; **BLC2001**: Post-Platinum; **Dose 8mg/d** (↑ to 9mg if serum phosphate <5.5 mg/dL); **Monitor phosphate, ocular toxicity (CSR)**.
11 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**NMIBC vs MIBC — T1 is NMIBC****T1 = Lamina propria** (NMIBC); **T2 = Muscularis propria** (MIBC) — Critical distinction
**BCG Induction vs Maintenance****Induction**: 6 weekly; **Maintenance**: 3 weekly at 3,6,12,18,24,30,36mo (SWOG 8507) — **Total 27**
**BCG-Unresponsive vs BCG-Relapsing****Unresponsive** = High-risk recurrence after **adequate** BCG → Cystectomy/ICI; **Relapsing** = Recurrence after **inadequate** BCG → Can retry BCG
**GC vs DD-MVAC****GC**: q3w, less toxic, **preferred**; **DD-MVAC**: q2w + G-CSF, higher pCR, more toxic
**Adjuvant Nivo vs ICI in Metastatic****Adjuvant Nivo**: Post-cystectomy for **ypT1-4/ypN+** (CheckMate 274); **Metastatic ICI**: 1L cisplatin-ineligible or Maintenance Avelumab
**FGFR3 mut vs FGFR3 fusion**Both sensitive to **Erdafitinib**; Fusion more common in upper tract

Mnemonic: BLADDER

  • BCG: Induction 6wk → Maintenance 3wk at 3,6,12,18,24,30,36mo
  • Low/Int/High: EAU/EORTC risk tables
  • Adequate BCG: ≥5 ind + ≥2 maint (or ≥2 ind + ≥1 maint)
  • Detrusor muscle in TURBT (mandatory for T1)
  • DD-MVAC vs GC: GC preferred neoadjuvant
  • Enfortumab: Nectin-4 ADC (2L post-PD-1+Plat)
  • Radical Cystectomy: Extended PLND + Diversion

19 Flashcards

Q1: Haematuria? A1: Painless macroscopic haematuria = 2-week rule cystoscopy

Q2: NMIBC Risk? A2: EAU/EORTC: Low / Intermediate / High (based on grade, stage, size, number, recurrence, CIS)

Q3: BCG Regimen? A3: Induction 6 weekly → Maintenance SWOG 8507: 3 weekly at 3,6,12,18,24,30,36mo (Total 27)

Q4: BCG-Unresponsive? A4: CIS recurrence at 6mo / Ta/T1 HG at 6mo / T1 HG at 3mo after adequate BCG → Cystectomy or ICI/Enfortumab

Q5: Re-TURBT? A5: Mandatory for T1 (10-30% upstaging to T2) and incomplete resection

Q6: MIBC? A6: Neoadjuvant Cisplatin (GC preferred) → Radical Cystectomy + Extended PLND → Adjuvant Nivo if ypT1-4/ypN+

Q7: Cisplatin Eligibility? A7: Galsky: GFR<60, ECOG≥2, Neuropathy≥2, Hearing≥2, HF Class III/IV

Q8: Metastatic 1L? A8: Cisplatin-eligible: GC/DD-MVAC → Maintenance Avelumab (JAVELIN Bladder 100)
