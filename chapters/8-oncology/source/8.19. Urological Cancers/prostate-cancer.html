Prostate Cancer
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Apply NCCN/EAU risk stratification for localised disease
  • [ ] Select Active Surveillance criteria (Very Low/Low risk)
  • [ ] Compare Radical Prostatectomy vs Radiotherapy (EBRT + Brachy boost) for intermediate/high risk
  • [ ] Sequence mHSPC: ADT + ARSI (Abiraterone/Enzalutamide/Apalutamide/Darolutamide) or Docetaxel
  • [ ] Sequence mCRPC: ARSI → ARSI switch, Docetaxel/Cabazitaxel, PARPi (BRCA), Radium-223, PSMA-Lu177
  • [ ] Apply Galsky criteria for docetaxel eligibility in mCRPC
  • [ ] Interpret PSMA-PET for staging, oligometastatic directed therapy, Lu177 eligibility
2 Definition & Epidemiology
FeatureDetail
-----------------
**Definition**Malignant tumour of prostate epithelium; **Acinar Adenocarcinoma >95%**; Variants: Ductal, Small cell, Neuroendocrine, Squamous
**Incidence**UK: ~52,000/year; **Most common male cancer**; 2nd cause of cancer death (M)
**Prevalence**5-year OS ~95% (localised), ~30% (metastatic)
**Peak Age**65-75 years; Rare <50
**Sex Ratio**Male only
**Risk Factors****Age**, **Family history** (↑2-3x if 1st degree), **BRCA2** (↑5-8x, earlier onset), **BRCA1**, **HOXB13**, **Lynch**, **African ancestry** (↑ incidence & mortality), **Obesity** (aggressive disease), **Diet** (high dairy, processed meat)

4 Clinical Features
FeatureDescription
----------------------
**Localised**Often **asymptomatic**; Detected on PSA screening; **LUTS** (frequency, nocturia, weak stream) — usually BPH
**Locally Advanced**Urinary obstruction, haematuria, bladder outlet obstruction
**Metastatic****Bone pain** (axial skeleton, pelvis, femur), Spinal cord compression, Pathological fractures, Lymphadenopathy, Visceral mets (late)
**Paraneoplastic**Rare; Hypertrophic pulmonary osteoarthropathy (HPOA)
5 Staging & Classification
SystemDetail
----------------
**TNM 8th Edition**T1: Incidental (T1a ≤5%, T1b >5%, T1c needle bx); T2: Organ-confined (T2a ≤1/2 lobe, T2b >1/2 lobe, T2c bilateral); T3: Extracapsular (T3a ECE, T3b SVI); T4: Adjacent organs
**N Stage**N1: Regional nodes
**M Stage**M1a: Non-regional nodes; M1b: Bone; M1c: Visceral
**Grade Groups (ISUP 2014)****GG1**: Gleason 3+3=6; **GG2**: 3+4=7; **GG3**: 4+3=7; **GG4**: 4+4=8, 3+5=8, 5+3=8; **GG5**: 4+5=9, 5+4=9, 5+5=10
Risk Stratification
SystemCategories

**Low**: T1-T2a, GG1, PSA<10

7 Differential Diagnosis
ConditionDistinguishing Features
------------------------------------
**Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH)**LUTS, symmetrically enlarged prostate, PSA ↑ (usually <10), **No nodules on DRE**
**Prostatitis**Acute: Fever, dysuria, tender prostate; Chronic: Pelvic pain, PSA ↑; **Resolves with antibiotics**
**Neuroendocrine Prostate Cancer**Rapid progression, **low PSA**, visceral mets, elevated NSE/Chromogranin; **RB1/TP53 loss**
**Small Cell Carcinoma**Aggressive, paraneoplastic (SIADH, Lambert-Eaton), **PSA often low**, Platinum+Etoposide
**Metastasis to Prostate**Rare; Bladder, RCC, Lung, Melanoma; History of primary

Active Surveillance Criteria (Strict):

  • NCCN Very Low/Low; EAU Low
  • Life expectancy >10 years
  • Compliance with protocol
mHSPC (Metastatic Hormone-Sensitive)

``mermaid flowchart TD A[mHSPC Diagnosis] --> B[ADT (GnRH Agonist/Antagonist)<br/>+ ARSI or Docetaxel] B --> B1[Abiraterone + Prednisone (LATITUDE, STAMPEDE)<br/>Preferred if high-volume/symptomatic] B --> B2[Enzalutamide (ENZAMET, ARCHES, STAMPEDE)<br/>Preferred if low-volume/asymptomatic, no seizure hx] B --> B3[Apalutamide (TITAN)<br/>Crosses BBB less] B --> B4[Darolutamide (ARASENS)<br/>No seizure risk, minimal drug interactions (CYP3A4)] B --> B5[Docetaxel 75mg/m² q3w ×6 cycles (STAMPEDE, CHAARTED, GETUG-AFU 15)<br/>If high-volume, fit, rapid progression] B1 --> C[Bone Health: Denosumab/Zoledronic Acid<br/>if ADT >1yr or osteopenia] B2 --> C B3 --> C B4 --> C B5 --> C ``

mCRPC (Metastatic Castration-Resistant)

``mermaid flowchart TD A[mCRPC: PSA rise + Castrate Testosterone (<50ng/dL)] --> B{Prior Therapy} B -->|ADT only (mHSPC not treated)| C[ARSI: Abiraterone / Enzalutamide / Apalutamide / Darolutamide] B -->|Post-ARSI (Abira or Enza)| D[Switch ARSI<br/>Abira → Enza/Apal/Daro (or vice versa)<br/>Cross-resistance ~30-50%] B -->|Post-ARSI + Chemo| E[Sequencing Options] C --> E D --> E E --> E1[Docetaxel 75mg/m² q3w ×10 cycles (TAX327, SWOG 9916)<br/>If fit, visceral mets, rapid progression] E --> E2[Cabazitaxel 25mg/m² q3w (TROPIC, PROSELICA)<br/>Post-Docetaxel; 20mg/m² non-inferior, less toxic] E --> E3[PARP Inhibitor<br/>Olaparib (PROfound) / Talazoparib (TALAPRO-2)<br/>BRCA1/2, ATM, PALB2 (HRD)] E --> E4[Radium-223 (ALSYMPCA)<br/>Bone-only mets, symptomatic, no visceral<br/>55 kBq/kg q4w ×6 cycles; No concurrent ARSI (ERA-223 ↑ fractures)] E --> E5[PSMA-Lutetium-177 (VISION)<br/>PSMA-PET positive (Ga-68/F-18)<br/>7.4 GBq q6wk ×6 cycles; Post-ARSI + Taxane] E --> E6[Immunotherapy<br/>Pembrolizumab if MSI-H/dMMR<br/>Ipilimumab+Nivo — not standard] E1 --> F[Further Sequencing: ARSI switch → Taxane → PARPi → Ra-223 → Lu-177 → Trial] E2 --> F E3 --> F E4 --> F E5 --> F ``

Oligometastatic Disease (≤3-5 mets)
  • PSMA-PET directed: SBRT to all mets + ADT ± ARSI (STOMP, ORIOLE)
  • Defer systemic therapy if asymptomatic

10 Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)
QuestionExpected Answer
---------------------------
**65M, PSA 6, MRI PI-RADS 3, bx: GG1 (3+3=6) in 1/12 cores. Management?****Active Surveillance** (Very Low/Low risk): PSA q3-6mo, DRE q12mo, mpMRI q1-2yr, Re-bx q3yr. Triggers: GG≥2, >2 cores +, PSA DT<3yr.
**70M, cT2c, GG4 (4+4=8), PSA 25. Localised treatment options?****High risk**: **Radical Prostatectomy + Extended PLND** OR **EBRT + Brachy Boost + ADT 18-36mo** (long-course). Surgery preferred if <70, fit.
**mHSPC — high-volume (CHAARTED: visceral or ≥4 bone mets with ≥1 beyond axial). 1L?****ADT + Abiraterone** (LATITUDE/STAMPEDE) **OR** **ADT + Docetaxel** (CHAARTED/STAMPEDE). **ARASENS**: ADT + Docetaxel + Darolutamide (triple therapy).
**mHSPC — low-volume, asymptomatic. 1L?****ADT + Enzalutamide** (ENZAMET/ARCHES) **OR** **ADT + Apalutamide** (TITAN) **OR** **ADT + Darolutamide** (ARASENS — all comers).
**mCRPC post-Abira — next?****Enzalutamide/Apalutamide/Darolutamide** (switch ARSI) **OR** **Docetaxel** (if fit, visceral, rapid progression).
**PARPi in mCRPC — indications, trials?****Olaparib** (PROfound) / **Talazoparib** (TALAPRO-2) → **BRCA1/2, ATM, PALB2** (HRD). Not for all-comers.
**Radium-223 — indication, contraindication?****Bone-only mCRPC, symptomatic, no visceral mets**; **Contraindicated with concurrent ARSI** (ERA-223: ↑ fractures, no OS benefit).
**PSMA-Lu177 — eligibility, trial?****VISION**: **PSMA-PET+** (Ga-68/F-18), **Post-ARSI + Taxane** → 7.4 GBq q6wk ×6 → **OS HR 0.62**.
**PSMA-PET — when indicated?****Staging**: High-risk localised (NCCN High/Very High); **Restaging**: BCR (PSA>0.2 post-RP, PSA>2 post-RT); **Lu177 eligibility**.
**Denosumab vs Zoledronic Acid on ADT?**Both reduce fractures; **Denosumab 60mg SC q6mo** (no renal adjustment); **ZA 4mg IV q3-6mo** (renal adjust).
11 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**Gleason vs Grade Groups****GG1=3+3=6**, **GG2=3+4=7**, **GG3=4+3=7**, **GG4=8 (4+4, 3+5, 5+3)**, **GG5=9-10**
**Abiraterone vs Enzalutamide — when which?****Abira**: High-volume, symptomatic (LATITUDE); **Enza**: Low-volume, asymptomatic, **no seizure hx**; **Apa**: Similar to Enza; **Daro**: No seizure risk, **no CYP3A4 interaction** (ARASENS)
**ARSI switch — efficacy?****Cross-resistance ~30-50%**; Response to 2nd ARSI lower than 1st
**Radium-223 + ARSI****CONTRAINDICATED** (ERA-223: ↑ fractures, no OS benefit); Sequential OK
**PSMA-PET vs Bone Scan****PSMA-PET superior**: Higher sensitivity/specificity; Detects nodal/visceral; **Standard for high-risk staging & BCR**
**Oligometastatic definition****≤3-5 mets** on PSMA-PET; **SBRT to all mets + ADT** (STOMP, ORIOLE)

Mnemonic: PROSTATE

  • PSA + DRE + mpMRI (PI-RADS) → Diagnosis
  • Risk: NCCN (VLow/Low/IntF/IntU/High/VHigh) / EAU (Low/Int/High)
  • Oligometastatic: ≤5 mets → SBRT + ADT
  • Screening: Age 50-70 (earlier FH/Black)
  • Treatment Localised: AS (VLow/Low) / RP / RT±ADT (Int/High)
  • ADT + ARSI (Abira/Enza/Apa/Daro) or Docetaxel = mHSPC 1L
  • Taxane (Docetaxel → Cabazitaxel) = mCRPC chemo
  • ENHANCE: PARPi (BRCA), Ra-223 (bone), Lu-177 (PSMA+)

19 Flashcards

Q1: Screening? A1: PSA + DRE; Age 50-70 (earlier if FH/Black); MRI before biopsy (PI-RADS)

Q2: Grade Groups? A2: GG1 (3+3=6) to GG5 (9-10); Replaces Gleason 6-10

Q3: Risk Stratification? A3: NCCN: Very Low/Low/Int Fav/Int Unfav/High/Very High; EAU: Low/Int/High

Q4: Active Surveillance? A4: Very Low/Low risk; Triggers: GG≥2, PSA DT<3yr, volume↑, >2 cores +

Q5: Localised Rx? A5: Int Fav: AS / RP / RT+ADT; Int Unfav/High: RP+PLND / RT+Brachy+ADT 4-6mo / RT+ADT 18-36mo

Q6: mHSPC? A6: ADT + ARSI (Abira/Enza/Apa/Daro) OR Docetaxel; STAMPEDE: Abira+ADT ↓mortality 37%; ARASENS: Daro+Doc+ADT

Q7: mCRPC Sequence? A7: 1st ARSI → 2nd ARSI switch → Docetaxel → Cabazitaxel → PARPi (BRCA) → Ra-223 (bone-only) → Lu-177 (PSMA+)

Q8: PARPi? A8: Olaparib / Talazoparib for BRCA1/2, ATM, PALB2 (PROfound, TALAPRO-2)
