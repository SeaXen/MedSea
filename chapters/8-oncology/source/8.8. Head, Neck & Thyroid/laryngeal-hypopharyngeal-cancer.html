CHAPTER 8 ONCOLOGY / 8.8. HEAD, NECK & THYROID
Laryngeal Hypopharyngeal Cancer
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Distinguish Glottic vs Supraglottic vs Subglottic by anatomy, presentation, lymphatic drainage, prognosis
  • [ ] Apply AJCC 8th Staging for Larynx and Hypopharynx
  • [ ] Select primary RT for Early Glottic (T1-2 N0)
  • [ ] Indicate CCRT (Cisplatin) for Larynx Preservation in Locally Advanced (RTOG 91-11)
  • [ ] Compare Surgery vs RT for Early Supraglottic
  • [ ] Manage Hypopharyngeal Cancer (CCRT vs Total Pharyngolaryngectomy)
  • [ ] Describe Voice Rehabilitation: TEP (Tracheoesophageal Puncture) — Gold Standard
  • [ ] Recognise RTOG 91-11 and GORTEC 2000 trial results

3 Management
Early Glottic (T1-2 N0)

``mermaid flowchart TD A[Early Glottic T1-2 N0] --> B[Primary Radiotherapy (Standard)] B --> B1[IMRT/VMAT 63-66Gy/28-33fx (T1); 66-70Gy/33-35fx (T2)] B --> B2[Voice Preservation: Excellent (>90% good voice)] B --> B3[Surgery (Cordectomy/Endoscopic) — Alternative<br/>If RT contraindicated/Failed RT/Preferred by patient<br/>TORS/Transoral Laser Microsurgery (TLM)] B1 --> C[Surveillance: Laryngoscopy q3mo×2yr, q6mo×3yr] ``

Early Supraglottic (T1-2 N0)

``mermaid flowchart TD A[Early Supraglottic T1-2 N0] --> B[Option 1: Surgery] B --> B1[Supraglottic Laryngectomy (HEMI/Horizontal)<br/>Preserves Voice (No TEP needed)<br/>Oncologic Equivalent to RT] B --> B2[Transoral Laser (TLM) / TORS<br/>Minimally invasive] B --> C[Option 2: Primary RT<br/>66-70Gy/33-35fx (Higher dose than Glottic)<br/>Elective Neck RT (II-III) for N0 (Rich lymphatics)] C --> D[Surveillance] B2 --> D ``

Locally Advanced Laryngeal (T3-4, N+)

``mermaid flowchart TD A[Locally Advanced Laryngeal (T3-4, N+)] --> B[Larynx Preservation Strategy] B --> B1[CCRT: Cisplatin 100mg/m² q3w ×3 + IMRT 70Gy/35fx<br/>RTOG 91-11: CCRT > Induction+RT > RT alone (Larynx Preservation 88% vs 75% vs 70%)] B --> B2[Induction Chemo (TPF) → Response → RT (GORTEC 2000 — Inferior to CCRT for Preservation)] B1 --> C[Response Assessment (Laryngoscopy + PET-CT at 12wk)] B2 --> C C -->|CR| D[Surveillance] C -->|Residual / Recurrence| E[Salvage Total Laryngectomy + Neck Dissection] ``

Total Laryngectomy (Salvage / Primary T4b)
IndicationDetails
---------------------
**Salvage**CCRT failure, Recurrence, Persistent disease
**Primary**T4b (Prevertebral/Carotid), Extensive cartilage destruction
**Procedure****Total Laryngectomy + Bilateral Neck Dissection (I-V)** + **Tracheostomy (Permanent)**
**Voice Rehab****TEP (Tracheoesophageal Puncture)** — **Primary (at laryngectomy) or Secondary** — **Blom-Singer / Provox** — **Gold Standard**
**Alternatives****Electrolarynx**, **Oesophageal Speech**

6 Voice Rehabilitation
MethodDescriptionProsCons
---------------------------------
**TEP (Tracheoesophageal Puncture)****Primary (at Laryngectomy) or Secondary**; **Prosthesis (Blom-Singer/Provox)** shunt air from Trachea → Oesophagus**Best Voice Quality**, Hands-free (hands-free valves), **Gold Standard****Prosthesis Maintenance**, Leakage, Candida, Stenosis
**Electrolarynx****Handheld Device**; Vibrates neck/cheek → Sound articulated by mouth**Immediate**, No Surgery, Low Cost**Monotone**, Handheld, Stigma
**Oesophageal Speech****Air Swallowed → Oesophagus → Released** → Vibrations → Articulated**No Device**, Hands-free**Difficult to Learn**, Limited Duration/Volume, Low Success Rate
7 Key Trials
TrialDesignKey Result
---------------------------
**RTOG 91-11****CCRT (Cisplatin+RT) vs Induction (Cis/5-FU)→RT vs RT Alone****CCRT: Best Larynx Preservation (88% @2yr) & Loco-Regional Control**; **Induction+RT: No Survival Benefit over RT**
**GORTEC 2000****Induction (TPF) → RT vs CCRT****CCRT Non-Inferior**; **Induction → More Toxicity, Delayed RT**
**VA Larynx Study****Induction Cis/5-FU → RT vs Surgery+RT****Induction → 64% Larynx Preservation, Equivalent Survival** — **Started Organ Preservation**

9 Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)
QuestionExpected Answer
---------------------------
**60M, Hoarseness 3 months, T1a Glottic SCC. Treatment?****Primary RT (IMRT/VMAT) 63-66Gy/28-33fx** — **Standard for Early Glottic**; Voice Preservation Excellent.
**55F, T2 Supraglottic SCC, N0. Options?****1) Supraglottic Laryngectomy (HEMI/Horizontal) + Neck Dissection** (Preserves Voice) **OR 2) Primary RT 66-70Gy + Elective Neck RT (II-III)**.
**RTOG 91-11 — Three Arms, Result?****Arm 1: CCRT (Cisplatin+RT)**, **Arm 2: Induction Cis/5-FU → RT**, **Arm 3: RT Alone** → **CCRT Best Larynx Preservation (88%) & LRC**; **Induction No Survival Benefit**.
**T3 Glottic, N1 — Organ Preservation?****CCRT: Cisplatin 100mg/m² q3w ×3 + IMRT 70Gy/35fx** — **RTOG 91-11**; **Salvage Total Laryngectomy if Residual/Recurrence**.
**Total Laryngectomy — Voice Rehab Options?****1) TEP (Tracheoesophageal Puncture) — Gold Standard**; **2) Electrolarynx**; **3) Oesophageal Speech**.
**Hypopharyngeal Cancer — Most Common Subsite?****Pyriform Sinus (70%)** — **Late Presentation, Early Bilateral Nodal Spread**.
**Hypopharynx Locally Advanced — Standard?****CCRT (Cisplatin+RT) for Organ Preservation** (RTOG 91-11 Principles) **OR Total Pharyngolaryngectomy + Laryngectomy + Free Flap**.
**Supraglottic vs Glottic Lymphatics?****Supraglottic: Rich (Pre-epiglottic, Level II/III), Early Nodal Mets (30-40% at dx)**; **Glottic: Sparse, Late Nodal Mets (<10% T1-2)**.
**TEP — Primary vs Secondary?****Primary: At Time of Laryngectomy**; **Secondary: Months/Years Later** (after healing, RT completed).
**Post-Laryngectomy Stoma Care?****Humidification**, **Stoma Cleaning**, **Heat-Moisture Exchanger (HME)**, **Emergency Airway Access (Patient carries card)**.
10 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**Glottic vs Supraglottic RT Dose****Glottic T1: 63-66Gy**; **Glottic T2: 66-70Gy**; **Supraglottic: 66-70Gy + Elective Neck RT**
**CCRT vs Induction+RT****RTOG 91-11: CCRT Superior** (Larynx Preservation, LRC); **GORTEC 2000: CCRT Non-Inferior to Induction+RT, Less Toxic**
**Laryngeal vs Hypopharyngeal Staging****Laryngeal: T1-2 by Cord Mobility/Invasion**; **Hypopharyngeal: T1-2 by Size/Subsites, T3 by Laryngeal Invasion/Size>4cm**
**Organ Preservation Criteria****T3-4, N0-2, Resectable, Patient Fit for Cisplatin, No Cartilage Invasion (T4a relative)**; **T4b = Surgery**
**TEP Complications****Prosthesis Leakage**, **Candida**, **Granulation**, **Stenosis**, **Aspiration** — **Regular SLP Follow-up**
**Supraglottic Laryngectomy vs Total****Supraglottic: Preserves True Cords (Voice Intact), Removes Supraglottis**; **Total: Removes All Larynx, Permanent Tracheostomy**

Mnemonic: LARYNX-HYPO

  • Laryngeal: Glottic 60% (Best), Supraglottic 35%, Subglottic 5% (Worst)

  • RTOG 91-11: CCRT > Induction+RT > RT (Larynx Preservation)
  • Yoice Rehab: TEP (Gold Standard) > Electrolarynx > Oesophageal Speech
  • Neck: Supraglottic Rich (Early N+), Glottic Sparse (Late N+)

  • Hypopharynx: Pyriform 70%, Late, Early N+ (Bilateral)
  • Ypopharynx Tx: CCRT (Organ Pres) OR Total Pharyngolaryngectomy + Laryngectomy
