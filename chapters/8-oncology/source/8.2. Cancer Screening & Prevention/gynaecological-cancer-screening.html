CHAPTER 8 ONCOLOGY / 8.2. CANCER SCREENING & PREVENTION
Gynaecological Cancer Screening
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Describe which gynaecological cancers have population screening (Cervical only)
  • [ ] Apply high-risk surveillance for ovarian, endometrial, vulval cancers
  • [ ] Interpret UKCTOCS trial results for ovarian screening
  • [ ] Apply high-risk surveillance protocols for Lynch, BRCA, Obesity, Tamoxifen, Lichen Sclerosus
  • [ ] Counsel patients on screening vs surveillance distinction
2 Cervical Cancer Screening — Only Organised Programme
ParameterDetail
-------------------
**Programme****NHS Cervical Screening Programme (NHS CSP)**
**Age Range****25-64 years**
**Interval****25-49: 3 years**, **50-64: 5 years**
**Primary Test****HPV Primary Screening** (hrHPV PCR)
**Triage****HPV+ → Cytology (LBC)**; **Cytology+ → Colposcopy**; **HPV- → Routine Recall**
**HPV Vaccination****9-valent (6,11,16,18,31,33,45,52,58)** → **~90% prevention**
**Mortality Reduction****~70%** since programme inception
Only Organised Gynaecological Cancer Screening Programme in UK
3 Ovarian Cancer — No Population Screening
AspectDetail
---------------
**Population Screening****Not Recommended** (UKCTOCS Trial: No Mortality Benefit)
**UKCTOCS Trial****200,000 women, Multimodal (CA125 + TVUS) vs US vs Control** → **No Mortality Benefit**
**High-Risk Surveillance****BRCA1/2**, **Lynch**, **Strong FH** → **CA125 + TVUS q6-12mo**
**Ovarian Cancer****CA125 + TVUS q6-12mo** (High-Risk Only)
**Genetic Counselling****BRCA1/2, Lynch** → **Cascade Testing, RRSO**
UKCTOCS Trial Summary
FeatureDetail
----------------
**Design****RCT, 200,000 women**, Multimodal (CA125+TVUS) vs US vs Control
**Primary Outcome****Ovarian Cancer Mortality**
**Result****No Significant Mortality Reduction** (HR 0.93, NS)
**Mode Shift****Earlier Stage Detection** (Stage I/II ↑) but **No Mortality Benefit**
**Conclusion****No Population Screening Recommended**
4 Endometrial Cancer — High-Risk Surveillance
Risk FactorSurveillance
---------------------------
**Lynch Syndrome****Annual Endometrial Sampling / TVUS** (From 30-35yr)
**Obesity (BMI >30)****Annual Endometrial Sampling / TVUS** (From 35-40yr)
**Tamoxifen Use****Annual TVUS** (During + 2-5yr Post-Treatment)
**Unopposed Oestrogen** (PCOS, HRT)**Consider Annual Surveillance**
**Genetic Testing****If <50yr, FH, Obesity** → **Lynch/PTEN Testing**
Lynch Syndrome Gynaecological Surveillance
SurveillanceFrequencyStart Age
------------------------------------
**Endometrial Sampling / TVUS****Annual****30-35 years**
**Ovarian Surveillance****CA125 + TVUS q6-12mo****30-35 years**
**Gastric/SB/Urinary****OGD q2-3yr, Urinalysis****25-30 years**
5 Vulval & Vaginal Cancer — High-Risk Surveillance
ConditionSurveillance
-----------------------
**Lichen Sclerosus (LS)****Annual Vulval Exam**, **Biopsy if Change**, **Topical Steroids**
**HPV Infection** (VIN)**Colposcopy + Biopsy**, **Vaccination Preventive**
**Immunosuppression** (HIV, Post-Tx)**Annual Vulval Exam**, **Colposcopy if Abnormal**
**Prior Cervical/Vaginal CIN/CA****Annual Vulval Exam + Colposcopy**
**VIN (Vulval Intraepithelial Neoplasia)****Surveillance q6-12mo**, **Treatment if Progression**

7 Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)
QuestionExpected Answer
---------------------------
**Gynaecological Cancers — Which Has Population Screening?****Cervical Only (NHS CSP 25-64, HPV Primary 3/5yr)**.
**Ovarian Cancer Screening — UKCTOCS Result?****No Mortality Benefit** (HR 0.93, NS); **Earlier Stage Detection but No Mortality Reduction** → **No Population Screening**.
**Ovarian High-Risk Surveillance — Who, What?****BRCA1/2, Lynch, Strong FH** → **CA125 + TVUS q6-12mo**.
**Endometrial High-Risk — Who, What?****Lynch (Annual Endo Sampling/TVUS)**, **Obesity BMI>30 (Annual TVUS)**, **Tamoxifen (Annual TVUS during + 2-5yr post)**.
**Vulval Cancer — High-Risk Groups, Surveillance?****LS (Annual Vulval Exam), HPV/VIN (Colposcopy), Immunosuppression (Annual)**.
**UKCTOCS — Design, Result, Conclusion?****RCT 200k, Multimodal (CA125+TVUS) vs US vs Control; No Mortality Benefit; Earlier Stage Shift → No Population Screening**.
**Lynch Syndrome Gynaecological Surveillance?****Endometrial Sampling/TVUS Annual (30-35+), CA125+TVUS q6-12mo (Ovarian), OGD q2-3yr, Gynae Surveillance**.
**Tamoxifen & Endometrial Cancer — Surveillance?****Annual TVUS During + 2-5yr Post-Treatment** (Risk ~2-3x).
8 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**Why No Ovarian Screening?****UKCTOCS: No Mortality Benefit** (Earlier Stage Shift ≠ Mortality Reduction); **CA125/TVUS Lack Specificity/Sensitivity for Population Screening**
**Cervical vs Ovarian Screening****Cervical: Organised Programme (HPV Primary)**; **Ovarian: No Programme (UKCTOCS Negative)**
**Endometrial vs Ovarian Surveillance****Endometrial: High-Risk (Lynch, Obesity, Tamoxifen) → Annual Sampling/TVUS**; **Ovarian: High-Risk (BRCA/Lynch) → CA125+TVUS**
**UKCTOCS — Why No Benefit?****Lead Time Bias** (Earlier Detection ≠ Mortality ↓); **No Stage Migration to Curable Disease**; **False Positives → Unnecessary Surgery**
**Vulval Cancer — Lichen Sclerosus Link****LS → dVIN → Differentiated VIN → Vulval SCC**; **Annual Vulval Exam Essential**

Mnemonic: GYNAE-SCREEN

  • Gynaecological: Only Cervical Has Population Programme
  • Yearly Cervical: NHS CSP 25-64, HPV Primary, 3/5yr
  • No Ovarian Screening: UKCTOCS Negative (No Mortality Benefit)
  • Advanced Ovarian Risk: BRCA/Lynch/FH → CA125+TVUS q6-12mo
  • Endometrial Risk: Lynch, Obesity, Tamoxifen → Annual TVUS/Sampling
  • Screening Only Cervical: NHS CSP 25-64
  • Cervical: HPV Primary, 3/5yr Interval
  • Recruitment: Cervical Only Organised Programme
  • Endometrial: Lynch/Obese/Tamoxifen → Annual
  • Early Detection Only Cervical
  • No Ovarian: UKCTOCS Negative
  • Ovarian High-Risk: BRCA/Lynch → CA125+TVUS
