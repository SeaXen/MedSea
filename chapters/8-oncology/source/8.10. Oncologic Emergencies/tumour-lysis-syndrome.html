CHAPTER 8 ONCOLOGY / 8.10. ONCOLOGIC EMERGENCIES
Tumour Lysis Syndrome
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Apply Cairo-Bishop Criteria for Laboratory and Clinical TLS diagnosis
  • [ ] Stratify TLS Risk (High, Intermediate, Low) by tumour type
  • [ ] Prescribe Rasburicase (High Risk/Established) vs Allopurinol (Prophylaxis)
  • [ ] Implement Aggressive Hydration (3L/m²/d)
  • [ ] Monitor Electrolytes, Uric Acid, Renal Function, ECG q4-6h
  • [ ] Manage Complications: Hyperkalaemia, Hypocalcaemia, AKI, Arrhythmias
  • [ ] Recognise G6PD Deficiency contraindication for Rasburicase
2 Definition & Pathophysiology
EventMechanism
------------------
**Massive Tumour Cell Lysis**Spontaneous or Post-Chemotherapy → Intracellular Contents Released
**Potassium Release****Intracellular K⁺ (150 mmol/L)** → Hyperkalaemia
**Phosphate Release****Intracellular Phosphate** → Hyperphosphataemia
**Nucleic Acid Catabolism****Purines → Uric Acid** (via Xanthine Oxidase) → Hyperuricaemia
**Calcium Phosphate Precipitation****Hyperphosphataemia + Hypocalcaemia** → CaPO₄ Crystals in Renal Tubules
**Uric Acid Crystallisation****Low Solubility at pH<5.5** → Renal Tubular Obstruction → AKI
3 Cairo-Bishop Criteria (2004)
Laboratory TLS (≥2 of the following within 3 days pre- / 7 days post-chemotherapy)
ParameterThreshold
----------------------
**Uric Acid****≥476 μmol/L (8 mg/dL)**
**Potassium****≥6.0 mmol/L**
**Phosphate****≥2.1 mmol/L (6.5 mg/dL)** (Adults) / **≥2.9 mmol/L** (Children)
**Calcium****≤1.75 mmol/L (7 mg/dL)**
Clinical TLS (Laboratory TLS + ≥1 of)
ComplicationDefinition
--------------------------
**Renal**Creatinine >1.5x ULN
**Cardiac**Arrhythmia / Sudden Death
**Neurological**Seizure
**Fatal**Death
4 Risk Stratification
Risk GroupTumour TypesTLS IncidenceProphylaxis
------------------------------------------------------
**High****Burkitt Lymphoma**, **ALL (WBC >50-100×10⁹/L)**, **AML (WBC >50×10⁹/L)**, **High-Grade NHL (Lymphoblastic, DLBCL High Tumour Burden)**, **SCLC****5-25%****Rasburicase + Aggressive Hydration**
**Intermediate****DLBCL (Bulky)**, **Other NHL**, **Multiple Myeloma**, **Acute Leukaemias (Lower WBC)**, **Solid Tumours (High Turnover)****1-5%****Allopurinol + Hydration**
**Low****Solid Tumours (Low Turnover)**, **Indolent NHL (CLL, Follicular)**, **CML Chronic Phase****<1%****Hydration + Allopurinol PRN**
5 Management Algorithm

``mermaid flowchart TD A[TLS Suspected / High Risk] --> B[Aggressive Hydration: 3L/m²/d IV 0.9% Saline (Target Urine >100mL/h)] B --> C[Rasburicase 0.2mg/kg IV Daily (Until Uric Acid Normal) — Gold Standard] C --> D[Allopurinol 300mg OD (If Rasburicase Unavailable / Prophylaxis)] D --> E[Monitor: Uric Acid, K, Phos, Ca, Creatinine, ECG q4-6h] E --> F{Uric Acid / Electrolytes} F -->|K ≥6.0 / ECG Changes| G[Calcium Gluconate 10mL 10% IV, Insulin/Dextrose, Salbutamol, Dialysis if Refractory] F -->|Phos ≥2.1 / Ca ≤1.75| H[Phosphate Binders (Sevelamer), Calcium Supplement (If Hypocalcaemic)] F -->|AKI / Oliguria| I[Renal Replacement Therapy (Dialysis)] ``

6 Rasburicase vs Allopurinol
Feature**Rasburicase****Allopurinol**
-------------------------------------------
**Mechanism****Recombinant Urate Oxidase** → Converts Uric Acid → **Allantoin** (5-10x More Soluble)**Xanthine Oxidase Inhibitor** → Prevents Uric Acid Formation
**Onset****Hours** (Rapid Uric Acid Reduction)**Days** (Prevents New Formation Only)
**Indication****Established TLS / High Risk****Prophylaxis / Intermediate Risk**
**Contraindication****G6PD Deficiency** (Haemolysis, Methaemoglobinaemia)**Hypersensitivity** (Stevens-Johnson, DRESS)
**Cost****High****Low**
**Dose****0.2mg/kg IV Daily** (1-5 Days)**300mg OD** (Renal Adjust if CrCl<30)
**Monitoring****Uric Acid q4-6h**, **Methaemoglobin****Uric Acid Daily**, **LFTs**
7 G6PD Deficiency & Rasburicase
PopulationG6PD Deficiency PrevalenceAction
-----------------------------------------------
**African, Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, Asian****5-25%****Screen Before Rasburicase** if High Prevalence
**Test****Quantitative G6PD Assay** (Not Qualitative)**If Deficient: Avoid Rasburicase, Use Allopurinol**
8 Complication Management
Hyperkalaemia (K⁺ ≥6.0 mmol/L or ECG Changes)
StepTreatment
-----------------
**1. Cardiac Stabilisation****Calcium Gluconate 10mL 10% IV** (Over 5-10min)
**2. Shift K⁺ Intracellular****Insulin 10U + Dextrose 50mL 50% IV**; **Salbutamol 5mg Nebulised**
**3. Enhance Excretion****Furosemide 40-80mg IV** (If Urine Output Adequate); **Sodium Polystyrene Sulfonate**
**4. Definitive****Dialysis** (Refractory, Severe AKI, ECG Changes Persist)
Hypocalcaemia (Corrected Ca ≤1.75 mmol/L)
ScenarioTreatment
---------------------
**Symptomatic (Tetany, Seizures, QT Prolongation)****Calcium Gluconate 10mL 10% IV** (Repeat PRN)
**Asymptomatic****Oral Calcium + Vitamin D**; **Avoid IV if Phosphate High** (Risk CaPO₄ Precipitation)
Acute Kidney Injury
Indication for DialysisModality
-----------------------------------
**Refractory Hyperkalaemia****Intermittent HD / CRRT**
**Severe Metabolic Acidosis****CRRT Preferred**
**Fluid Overload / Pulmonary Oedema****CRRT / HD**
**Uric Acid >1000 μmol/L Refractory****HD**

10 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary
TopicKey Points
-------------------
**Cairo-Bishop Lab TLS****≥2 of**: UA ≥476, K≥6.0, Phos≥2.1, Ca≤1.75 (within 3d pre/7d post chemo)
**Clinical TLS**Lab TLS + Creatinine>1.5xULN, Arrhythmia, Seizure, Death
**Risk Groups****High**: Burkitt, ALL/AML (High WBC), High-Grade NHL; **Intermediate**: DLBCL, Myeloma; **Low**: Indolent
**Hydration****3L/m²/d 0.9% Saline**, Urine >100mL/h, Alkalisation (Not Routine with Rasburicase)
**Rasburicase****0.2mg/kg IV Daily** (GOLD Standard), **G6PD Contraindicated**, Until UA Normal
**Allopurinol****300mg OD** (Prophylaxis/Intermediate), Xanthine Oxidase Inhibitor
**Hyperkalaemia****Ca Gluconate → Insulin/Dextrose → Salbutamol → Dialysis**
**Hypocalcaemia****Do NOT Correct Aggressively** (CaPO₄ Precipitation Risk); Symptomatic → Ca Gluconate
**G6PD****Screen if High Prevalence**; **Contraindicated with Rasburicase**
**TLS Prevention****Hydration + Rasburicase (High Risk) / Allopurinol (Intermediate)**
11 Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)
QuestionExpected Answer
---------------------------
**Cairo-Bishop Laboratory TLS Criteria?****≥2 of: UA ≥476 μmol/L, K≥6.0, Phos≥2.1, Ca≤1.75** within 3d pre / 7d post chemo.
**Clinical TLS Definition?****Lab TLS + ≥1: Creatinine>1.5xULN, Arrhythmia, Seizure, Death**.
**High Risk Tumours for TLS?****Burkitt, ALL (WBC>50-100), AML (WBC>50), High-Grade NHL, SCLC**.
**Rasburicase vs Allopurinol — When Which?****Rasburicase: High Risk/Established TLS (Rapid UA↓)**; **Allopurinol: Prophylaxis/Intermediate Risk**.
**Rasburicase Contraindication?****G6PD Deficiency** (Haemolysis, Methaemoglobinaemia).
**Rasburicase Dose, Duration?****0.2mg/kg IV Daily** until Uric Acid Normal (Typically 1-5 Days).
**Hyperkalaemia in TLS — Immediate Management?****Calcium Gluconate 10mL 10% IV → Insulin/Dextrose → Salbutamol Neb → Dialysis if Refractory**.
**Hypocalcaemia in TLS — Why Not Aggressively Correct?****CaPO₄ Precipitation Risk** (Worsens Nephropathy); **Only Correct If Symptomatic**.
**G6PD Deficiency — Rasburicase Action?****Contraindicated** → **Use Allopurinol**; **Screen if High Prevalence Population**.
**TLS Prevention — Intermediate Risk?****Allopurinol 300mg OD + Hydration 3L/m²/d**.
12 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**Rasburicase vs Allopurinol****Rasburicase**: Degrades Existing UA (Urate Oxidase); **Allopurinol**: Prevents New UA (XO Inhibitor)
**Lab TLS vs Clinical TLS****Lab**: ≥2 Biochemical Abnormalities; **Clinical**: Lab TLS + Organ Dysfunction
**Rasburicase in G6PD****Contraindicated** (Haemolysis); **Allopurinol Safe**
**Phosphate in TLS****↑** (Intracellular Release) → **Hypocalcaemia** (CaPO₄ Precipitation)
**Hypocalcaemia Correction****Do Not Overcorrect** → Risk CaPO₄ Precipitation; Only Symptomatic
**TLS vs Hypercalcaemia — Phosphate****TLS: Phos ↑**; **Hypercalcaemia: Phos Normal/↓** (PTHrP → Phos ↓)
**Allopurinol in Renal Impairment****Dose Reduce** (CrCl<30: 100mg OD/Alternate Day); **Rasburicase: No Renal Adjustment**

Mnemonic: TLS-CAIRO-BISHOP

  • Tumour Lysis Syndrome
  • CAIRO: Ca (Hypocalcaemia ≤1.75), I (UA ≥476), R (K≥6.0), O (Phos ≥2.1)
  • Bishop: Clinical = Lab + Organ Dysfunction (Creatinine, Arrhythmia, Seizure, Death)
  • Risk: Burkitt, ALL, AML (High WBC), High-Grade NHL
  • Allopurinol: Prophylaxis/Intermediate (XO Inhibitor)
  • Screen G6PD: Rasburicase Contraindicated
  • Hydration: 3L/m²/d Saline, Urine >100mL/h
  • Immediate: Rasburicase 0.2mg/kg IV (Urate Oxidase → Allantoin)
  • Organ Dysfunction: AKI, Arrhythmia, Seizure
  • Phosphate: ↑ → Hypocalcaemia (CaPO₄ Precipitation)
