# Pathological Fractures in Malignancy

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP Priority: HIGH**
> **Pathological Fracture = Fracture Through Bone Weakened by Tumour**; **Common Primaries**: Breast, Prostate, Lung, Renal, Myeloma; **Mirels Score** (Site, Pain, Lesion Type, Size) → **≥9 = Fixation**, **7-8 = Consider**, **≤6 = Non-Operative**; **Management**: **Impending/Actual** → Orthopaedic Fixation + RT, Medical (Bisphosphonates/Denosumab), Radiotherapy; **Spinal** → MSCC Protocol.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Define **pathological fracture** and identify **common primary tumours**
- [ ] Apply **Mirels Score** for surgical decision-making
- [ ] Manage **impending vs actual** pathological fractures
- [ ] Apply **surgical vs non-operative** management principles
- [ ] Integrate **radiotherapy, bisphosphonates, denosumab** in management

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Definition** | Fracture through bone weakened by metastatic tumour or primary bone tumour |
| **Incidence** | **8-30% of bone metastases** develop pathological fracture |
| **Common Primaries** | **Breast (20-25%)**, **Prostate (15-20%)**, **Lung (15-20%)**, **Renal (10%)**, **Myeloma (10-15%)**, **Thyroid, Breast, Lung, Kidney** |
| **Common Sites** | **Femur (50%)**, **Humerus (15%)**, **Tibia, Vertebrae, Pelvis** |
| **Risk Factors** | **Lytic Lesions**, **Large Size (>2.5cm)**, **Cortical Destruction >50%**, **Weight-Bearing Bone** |

---

## 3. Mirels Scoring System

| Parameter | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Site** | Upper Limb | Lower Limb (Non-Weight Bearing) | **Lower Limb (Weight Bearing: Femur, Tibia)** |
| **Pain** | Mild | Moderate | **Severe/Functional** |
| **Lesion Type** | **Blastic** | **Mixed** | **Lytic** |
| **Size** | **<1/3 Cortex** | **1/3 - 2/3 Cortex** | **>2/3 Cortex** |

| Total Score | Management |
|-------------|------------|
| **≤6** | **Non-Operative** (Radiotherapy, Bisphosphonates, Protected Weight Bearing) |
| **7-8** | **Consider Fixation** (Multidisciplinary Decision) |
| **≥9** | **Prophylactic Fixation** (Surgery) |

> **Score ≥9 = High Fracture Risk → Prophylactic Fixation Recommended**

---

## 4. Impending vs Actual Pathological Fracture

| Feature | **Impending Fracture** | **Actual Pathological Fracture** |
|----------|------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Definition** | **Cortical breach imminent** (Pain, Cortical disruption on imaging) | **Complete Cortical Disruption** with displacement |
| **Imaging** | **CT/MRI**: Cortical thinning, <1mm cortical thickness, Periosteal reaction | **X-ray/CT**: Fracture Line, Displacement, Soft Tissue Swelling |
| **Mirels Score** | **7-12** (High Risk) | **N/A (Already Fractured)** |
| **Management** | **Prophylactic Fixation + RT** | **Surgical Fixation + RT** |

## 5. Management Algorithm

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Suspected Pathological Fracture] --> B[**Mirels Score Assessment**]
    B --> C{Score}
    C -->|**≤6**| D[**Non-Operative**]
    D --> D1[**Radiotherapy** (8Gy×1 or 20Gy/5fx or 30Gy/10fx)]
    D --> D2[**Bisphosphonate/Denosumab**]
    D --> D3[**Analgesia, Protected WB**]
    C -->|**7-8**| E[**MDT Discussion**]
    E --> E1[**Consider Fixation** if Progressive Pain/Enlarging Lesion]
    E --> E2[**RT + Medical Management**]
    C -->|**≥9**| F[**Prophylactic Fixation**]
    F --> F1[**Intramedullary Nail (Femur/Tibia)**]
    F --> F2[**Plate/Screw (Humerus)**]
    F --> F3[**Endoprosthesis (Proximal Femur/Humerus)**]
    F --> F4[**Post-Op RT** (20Gy/5fx or 30Gy/10fx)]
```

## 6. Surgical Options by Site

| Site | Preferred Fixation | Notes |
|------|-------------------|-------|
| **Femur** | **Intramedullary Nail** (Long, Cephalomedullary) | **Long-stem if Proximal** |
| **Humerus** | **Plate/Screw** OR **Intramedullary Nail** | **Locking Plate** (Better for Metastatic) |
| **Tibia** | **Intramedullary Nail** | **Long Segment** |
| **Proximal Femur** | **Bipolar Hemiarthroplasty / Endoprosthesis** | **If Subtrochanteric/Neck, Poor Bone Quality** |
| **Vertebra** | **Kyphoplasty/Vertebroplasty** OR **Spinal Instrumentation** | **See MSCC Protocol** |

---

## 7. Adjunctive Medical Management

| Therapy | Dose/Regimen | Role |
|-------|--------------|------|
| **Bisphosphonates** | **Zoledronic Acid 4mg IV q4wk** | **Reduce SREs, Pain,hypercalcaemia** |
| **Denosumab** | **120mg SC q4wk** (Loading d1,8,15,29) | **Superior to ZA** (SRE Delay), No Renal Adjust |
| **Radiotherapy** | **8Gy ×1 / 20Gy/5fx / 30Gy/10fx** | **Pain Control, Local Control, Post-Op** |
| **Analgesia** | **WHO Ladder** (Opioids, Adjuvants) | **Stepwise** |

## 8. Spinal Pathological Fractures — MSCC Protocol

> **See MSCC Note for Full Details**

| Scenario | Management |
|----------|-----------|
| **Ambulatory / No Cord Compression** | **RT 20Gy/5fx** (Within 24h) |
| **Non-Ambulatory <48h** | **Emergency Decompression + Stabilisation + RT** |
| **Non-Ambulatory >48h / Complete Paraplegia** | **RT Alone** (Surgery Futile) |
| **Dexamethasone** | **16mg IV Stat → 4mg q6h** (Urgent) |

## 11. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Mirels Score Components** | **Site (Lower Limb Weight-Bearing = 3), Pain (Severe = 3), Lytic (3), Size >2/3 Cortex (3)** |
| **Impending vs Actual Fracture** | **Impending**: Cortical Breach Imminent (Pain, Imaging); **Actual**: Complete Fracture, Displacement |
| **Mirels 7-8 — Fix or Not?** | **MDT Decision** — Consider Fixation if Progressive Symptoms/Lesion |
| **Denosumab vs Zoledronic Acid** | **Denosumab**: No Renal Adjust, Slightly Superior SRE Delay; **Zoledronic**: Renal Adjust, Lower Cost |
| **RT Fractionation** | **8Gy ×1** (Palliation); **20Gy/5fx or 30Gy/10fx** (Post-Op, Impending Fracture) |
| **MSCC vs Pathological Fracture** | **MSCC**: Cord Compression (Neuro Deficit); **Path Fx**: Bone Break (Pain, Mechanical Instability) |

**Mnemonic: PATH-FRACTURE**
- **P**athological: **Tumour Weakens Bone** → Fracture
- **A**natomy: **Femur (50%), Humerus (15%), Tibia, Vertebrae**
- **T**umours: **Breast, Prostate, Lung, Renal, Myeloma**
- **H**igh Risk: **Lytic, >2/3 Cortex, Weight-Bearing, Severe Pain**
- **F**racture Types: **Impending (Imminent) vs Actual (Complete)**
- **R**isk Score: **Mirels (Site, Pain, Lytic, Size)** → **≥9 = Fix**
- **A**djunctive: **RT (8Gy×1/20Gy/5fx), Bisphosphonates, Denosumab**
- **C**ommon Primaries: **Breast, Prostate, Lung, Renal, Myeloma**
- **T**reatment Fixation: **IM Nail (Femur/Tibia), Plate (Humerus), Endoprosthesis (Hip)**
- **U**rgent Spinal: **MSCC Protocol (Dex 16mg → MRI → RT/Surgery)**
- **R**adiotherapy: **8Gy×1 (Palliation), 20Gy/5fx (Post-Fix/Impending)**
- **E**lementary: **Mirels ≥9 = Fix, 7-8 MDT, ≤6 Non-Op**
- **S**urgical: **IM Nail (Femur/Tibia), Plate (Humerus), Endoprosthesis (Hip)**
