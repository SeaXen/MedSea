SEA KNOWLEDGE STUDY NOTES
CHAPTER 8 ONCOLOGY / 8.13. PARANEOPLASTIC SYNDROMES
Paraneoplastic Syndromes
1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this note you should be able to:

  • [ ] Classify Paraneoplastic Syndromes by System (Endocrine, Neurological, Dermatological, Haematological, Rheumatological, Renal)
  • [ ] Recognise Endocrine Syndromes: SIADH (SCLC), Ectopic ACTH (SCLC, Carcinoid), Hypercalcaemia/PTHrP (SCC, Breast), Hypoglycaemia/IGF-2 (Mesenchymal), Carcinoid/Serotonin (NET)
  • [ ] Identify Neurological Syndromes and their Onconeural Antibodies: Lambert-Eaton/VGCC (SCLC), PCD/CDR2 (Gynae), Anti-Hu (SCLC), Anti-Yo (Gynae), Anti-Ri (Breast), Anti-Ma2 (Testicular), Anti-CV2/CRMP5 (SCLC), Anti-NMDA (Ovarian Teratoma)
  • [ ] Recognise Dermatological Signs: Acanthosis Nigricans, Dermatomyositis, Sweet's Syndrome, Bazex, Leser-Trélat
  • [ ] Identify Haematological Syndromes: TTP, DIC, Erythrocytosis, Trousseau's
  • [ ] Link Specific Antibodies to Associated Cancers for Targeted Investigation

**Sweet's vs Erythema Nodosum****Sweet's: Neutrophilia, Fever, Plaques (Neutrophils), AML/MDS**; **EN: Tender Nodules (Septal Panniculitis), TB, Sarcoid, Strep, Drugs**

Mnemonic: PARANEOPLASTIC

  • Paraneoplastic: Remote Effects, Not Direct Invasion
  • Anibody-Mediated: Onconeural (Hu, Yo, Ri, Ma2, CV2, NMDA, VGCC, Amphiphysin, GAD, LGI1, CASPR2)
  • Remote Effects: Hormonal (SIADH, ACTH, PTHrP, IGF-2, Serotonin)
  • Anibody-Neurological: Hu (SCLC, Encephalomyelitis), Yo (Gynae, PCD), Ri (Breast, Opsoclonus), Ma2 (Testicular, Limbic), CV2 (SCLC), NMDA (Ovarian Teratoma)
  • Neuro Lambert-Eaton: VGCC Ab, SCLC, Facilitation, 3,4-DAP
  • Endocrine: SIADH (SCLC), ACTH (SCLC/Carcinoid), PTHrP (SCC/Breast), IGF-2 (Mesenchymal), Carcinoid (NET)
  • Onconeural Paraneoplastic: PCD (Anti-Yo), LEMS (VGCC), Encephalitis (Hu, Ma2, NMDA), Neuropathy (Hu, CV2)
  • Lambert-Eaton: VGCC, Facilitation, SCLC, 3,4-DAP
  • Acanthosis Nigricans: Gastric, Lung, Velvety Hyperpigmentation
  • Autoimmune Skin: Dermatomyositis (Ovarian/Lung), Sweet's (AML), Bazex (H&N), Leser-Trélat (Gastric)
  • Autoimmune Syndromes: TTP (ADAMTS13, PLEX), DIC (APL, Adeno), Trousseau's (Mucinous Adeno)
  • TTP: ADAMTS13 Def, MAHA, Thrombocytopenia, PLEX + Steroids
  • Immunotherapy: IVIG, PLEX, Rituximab, Steroids, Cyclophosphamide
  • Cancer Search: CT/PET-CT, Tumour Markers, Onconeural Panel
  • Targeted Treatment: Treat Cancer = Best for Paraneoplastic

1. A 55-year-old presents with classic features. MDT discussion recommends:

  • A. SIADH
  • B. SIADH (less specific)
  • C. Empirical broad approach
  • D. No intervention required
  • Answer: A — first-line: SIADH

2. On staging workup, the patient is found to be [Stage X]. Best management is:

  • A. LEMS
  • B. LEMS (less specific)
  • C. Empirical broad approach
  • D. No intervention required
  • Answer: A — stage-specific: LEMS

3. Following first-line treatment, the patient develops [complication]. Best next step:

  • A. Acanthosis Nigricans
  • B. **Acanthosis (less specific)
  • C. Empirical broad approach
  • D. No intervention required
  • Answer: A — complication: Acanthosis Nigricans

4. The patient asks about prognosis. Most appropriate response based on:

  • A. TTP
  • B. TTP (less specific)
  • C. Empirical broad approach
  • D. No intervention required
  • Answer: A — prognosis: TTP

5. A 65-year-old with relevant risk factors should be screened with:

  • A. HPOA
  • B. HPOA (less specific)
  • C. Empirical broad approach
  • D. No intervention required
  • Answer: A — screening: HPOA

6. A clinician encounters a patient with this presentation. Best approach:

  • A. Ectopic ACTH / Cushing's: Ectopic ACTH (PRO-ACTH/POMC)
  • B. Watch and wait approach
  • C. Empirical broad treatment
  • D. No intervention
  • Answer: A — Ectopic ACTH / Cushing's: Ectopic ACTH (PRO-ACTH/POMC)

7. On further evaluation, the finding is confirmed. Most appropriate next step:

  • A. Hypercalcaemia: PTHrP (Humoral), Osteolytic Mets, Vit D (Lymphoma)
  • B. Watch and wait approach
  • C. Empirical broad treatment
  • D. No intervention
  • Answer: A — Hypercalcaemia: PTHrP (Humoral), Osteolytic Mets, Vit D (Lymphoma)

8. The patient asks about management options. Best evidence-based response:

  • A. Hypoglycaemia: IGF-2 (Big IGF-2), Insulinoma, Glycogenolysis
  • B. Watch and wait approach
  • C. Empirical broad treatment
  • D. No intervention
  • Answer: A — Hypoglycaemia: IGF-2 (Big IGF-2), Insulinoma, Glycogenolysis

9. Treatment is initiated. Most important monitoring parameter:

  • A. Carcinoid Syndrome: Serotonin, Kallikrein, Bradykinin, Prostaglandins
  • B. Watch and wait approach
  • C. Empirical broad treatment
  • D. No intervention
  • Answer: A — Carcinoid Syndrome: Serotonin, Kallikrein, Bradykinin, Prostaglandins

10. Follow-up assessment reveals the following. Best next step:

  • A. Hyponatraemia (CSW): Cerebral Salt Wasting (Brain Natriuretic Peptide)
  • B. Watch and wait approach
  • C. Empirical broad treatment
  • D. No intervention
  • Answer: A — Hyponatraemia (CSW): Cerebral Salt Wasting (Brain Natriuretic Peptide)
