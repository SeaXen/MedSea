## 📖 DOAC Comparison

| Feature | **Rivaroxaban** | **Apixaban** | **Dabigatran** | **Edoxaban** |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|----------------|--------------|
| **Mechanism** | **Direct Factor Xa Inhibitor** | **Direct Factor Xa Inhibitor** | **Direct Thrombin (IIa) Inhibitor** | **Direct Factor Xa Inhibitor** |
| **Bioavailability** | ~66% (With Food) | ~50% | ~6.5% (Prodrug) | ~62% |
| **Half-life** | 5-9 h (Young), 11-13 h (Elderly) | 12 h | 12-17 h | 10-14 h |
| **Renal Clearance** | **66%** | **27%** | **80%** | **50%** |
| **Hepatic Metabolism** | CYP3A4, CYP2J2 | CYP3A4 | Minimal (Esterase) | CYP3A4 |
| **P-gp Substrate** | **Yes** | **Yes** | **Yes** | **Yes** |
| **Food Effect** | **↑ Absorption (With Food)** | Minimal | Food ↑ Absorption 2x | Minimal |

## 💊 Dosing Regimens

### Atrial Fibrillation (Stroke Prevention)

| Agent | Standard Dose | **Renal Impairment** (CrCl) | **Low Weight / Elderly** |
|-------|---------------|----------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Rivaroxaban** | **20 mg OD** (With Evening Meal) | **15 mg OD** if CrCl 15-49 mL/min | **15 mg OD** if Weight ≤60 kg or Age ≥80 |
| **Apixaban** | **5 mg BD** | **2.5 mg BD** if **≥2 Criteria**: Age ≥80, Weight ≤60 kg, CrCl ≤1.5 mg/dL | **2.5 mg BD** if **≥2 Criteria** |
| **Dabigatran** | **150 mg BD** (110 mg BD if Age ≥80 or High Bleed Risk) | **110 mg BD** if CrCl 30-49 mL/min; **Contraindicated <30** | **110 mg BD** if Age ≥80 |
| **Edoxaban** | **60 mg OD** | **30 mg OD** if CrCl 15-50, Weight ≤60 kg, or Concomitant P-gp Inhibitors | **30 mg OD** if **≥1 Criterion** |

### VTE Treatment (DVT/PE)

| Agent | Acute Phase | Maintenance |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Rivaroxaban** | **15 mg BD × 21 days** (With Food) | **20 mg OD** (With Evening Meal) |
| **Apixaban** | **10 mg BD × 7 days** | **5 mg BD** |
| **Dabigatran** | **LMWH 5 days Overlap** → **150 mg BD** (110 mg BD if High Bleed Risk) | Same |
| **Edoxaban** | **LMWH 5 days Overlap** → **60 mg OD** (30 mg OD if Adjusted) | Same |

### Post-Orthopaedic Surgery Prophylaxis

| Surgery | Agent & Dose | Duration |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| **Hip Replacement** | **Rivaroxaban 10 mg OD** / **Apixaban 2.5 mg BD** / **Dabigatran 220 mg OD** | **35 days** |
| **Knee Replacement** | **Rivaroxaban 10 mg OD** / **Apixaban 2.5 mg BD** / **Dabigatran 150 mg OD** | **10-14 days** |

|-------|--------------|----------------|----------------|-------------------------|------------------------|

## 🔄 Reversal Agents

| Agent | Reversal Agent | Dose | Indication |
|-------|----------------|------|------------|
| **Dabigatran** | **Idarucizumab (Praxbind)** | **5 g IV** (2 × 2.5 g vials, 5 min apart) | **Life-threatening Bleeding / Emergency Surgery** |
| **Rivaroxaban / Apixaban / Edoxaban** | **Andexanet Alfa (Ondexxya)** | **Bolus + 2h Infusion** (Low/High Dose per Agent) | **Life-threatening Bleeding / Emergency Surgery** |
| **All DOACs** | **4-Factor PCC** (Kcentra) | **25-50 IU/kg** | **If Specific Reversal Unavailable** |
| **All DOACs** | **Activated Charcoal** | **50 g PO** | **Recent Ingestion (<2-4h)** |

| Reversal Agent | Dosing Details |
|----------------|----------------|
| **Idarucizumab** | **5 g IV** (2 × 2.5 g/50mL) over 5-10 min; **Repeat 2.5g if Rebound** |
| **Andexanet Alfa (Low Dose)** | **Rivaroxaban ≤10mg / Apixaban ≤5mg**: **400mg Bolus + 480mg/2h Infusion** |
| **Andexanet Alfa (High Dose)** | **Rivaroxaban >10mg / Apixaban >5mg / Edoxaban**: **800mg Bolus + 960mg/2h Infusion** |

> [!warning] **Andexanet Alfa**: **Rebound Anticoagulation** Risk → **Restart Anticoagulation ASAP** if Indicated. **Idarucizumab**: **Only for Dabigatran**.

## 🏥 Peri-procedural Management

| Procedure Bleeding Risk | DOAC Hold | Restart |
|------------------------|-----------|---------|
| **Low** (Dental, Cataract, Superficial) | **Skip Morning Dose** (If OD) / **Skip 1 Dose** (If BD) | **Next Scheduled Dose** |
| **Moderate** (Endoscopy w/ Biopsy, Minor Surgery) | **Skip 24h** (OD) / **Skip 2 Doses** (BD) | **6-24h Post-op** (If Haemostasis Secure) |
| **High** (Major Surgery, Neurosurgery, Spinal, Cardiac) | **Skip 48h** (OD) / **Skip 4 Doses (48h)** (BD) | **48-72h Post-op** (If Haemostasis Secure) |

| Renal Function | Rivaroxaban/Edoxaban Hold | Apixaban Hold | Dabigatran Hold |
|----------------|---------------------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Normal** | 24h | 24h | 24-48h |
| **CrCl 30-50** | 24h | 24h | 48h |
| **CrCl <30** | **Contraindicated** (Rivaroxaban/Edoxaban) | 48h | **Contraindicated** |

> [!warning] **No Routine Bridging** with LMWH for DOACs (Unlike Warfarin). **Restart Based on Bleeding Risk** not INR.

## 🩸 Bleeding Management

| Severity | Management |
|----------|------------|
| **Minor** (Epistaxis, Bruising) | **Hold 1-2 Doses**, **Local Measures**, **Restart when Stable** |
| **Moderate** (Macroscopic Haematuria, GI Bleed Stable) | **Hold DOAC**, **IV Fluids**, **Transfuse if Hb <70**, **Consider Reversal** |
| **Major/Life-threatening** (ICH, Massive GI Bleed, Haemodynamic Instability) | **STOP DOAC**, **Call Haematology**, **Specific Reversal Agent + PCC**, **Surgical/IR Intervention**, **ICU** |

## 🔄 Switching Anticoagulants

| Switch | Protocol |
|--------|----------|
| **Warfarin → DOAC** | **Stop Warfarin**, **Start DOAC when INR <2.0** (Apixaban/Dabigatran) or **INR <2.5** (Rivaroxaban/Edoxaban) |
| **DOAC → Warfarin** | **Start Warfarin + Continue DOAC** until **INR ≥2.0 for 2 Days**, Then Stop DOAC |
| **DOAC → DOAC** | **Stop DOAC 1**, **Start DOAC 2 at Next Scheduled Dose** |
| **LMWH → DOAC** | **Give DOAC at Time of Next LMWH Dose** |
| **DOAC → LMWH** | **Give LMWH at Time of Next DOAC Dose** |

## 🤰 DOACs in Special Populations

| Population | Recommendation |
|------------|----------------|
| **Elderly (≥80)** | **Apixaban 2.5mg BD** (Preferred), **Rivaroxaban 15mg OD**, **Dabigatran 110mg BD**, **Edoxaban 30mg OD** |
| **Renal Impairment** | **Apixaban Preferred** (2.5mg BD if CrCl 15-29); **Avoid Dabigatran/Edoxaban/Rivaroxaban if CrCl<15** |
| **Obesity (BMI >40 / Weight >120kg)** | Standard Dose OK (Apixaban/Rivaroxaban); **Avoid Dabigatran** (Limited Data) |
| **Cancer (VTE)** | **LMWH Preferred** (CLOT Trial); **DOACs Non-Inferior** (SELECT-D, CARAVAGGIO) - **Apixaban Preferred** |
| **Pregnancy** | **CONTRAINDICATED** (All DOACs) → **LMWH Throughout** |
| **Lactation** | **Avoid** (Limited Data) → **LMWH Preferred** |
| **Mechanical Heart Valve** | **CONTRAINDICATED** (RE-ALIGN Trial Harm) → **Warfarin Only** |
| **Antiphospholipid Syndrome** | **DOACs Contraindicated in Triple Positive** (TRAPS, ASTRO-APS) → **Warfarin INR 3-4** |

## ❓ Viva Questions

1. **Which DOAC is preferred in a patient with severe renal impairment (CrCl 20 mL/min)?**
   - **Apixaban 2.5 mg BD** (Only DOAC licensed down to CrCl 15 mL/min)

2. **How do you reverse dabigatran in life-threatening bleeding?**
   - **Idarucizumab 5g IV (2 × 2.5g vials) over 5-10 minutes**

3. **What is the antidote for rivaroxaban/apixaban/edoxaban?**
   - **Andexanet alfa** (Factor Xa inhibitor antidote); **4-factor PCC if unavailable**

4. **When should you hold DOACs before elective surgery?**
   - **Low Risk: Skip 1 dose**; **High Risk: 48h (Factor Xa) / 48-72h (Dabigatran)**

5. **Can DOACs be used in mechanical heart valves?**
   - **NO - CONTRAINDICATED** (RE-ALIGN trial showed excess thromboembolic events with dabigatran)

6. **How do you switch from warfarin to apixaban?**
   - **Stop warfarin, Start apixaban when INR <2.0**

6. **Which DOAC is safest in dialysis patients?**
   - **Apixaban 2.5mg BD** (Only DOAC with dialysis data; **Off-label but Used**)

7. **How do you manage peri-procedural anticoagulation for a patient on dabigatran 150mg BD for AF undergoing colonoscopy with polypectomy?**
   - **Hold Dabigatran 48h Pre-procedure** (CrCl >50); **Restart 6-24h Post-procedure if Haemostasis Secure**

7. **What is the reversal agent for factor Xa inhibitors and its dosing?**
   - **Andexanet Alfa**: **Low Dose (Riva ≤10mg/Apx ≤5mg): 400mg Bolus + 480mg/2h Infusion**; **High Dose: 800mg Bolus + 960mg/2h Infusion**

8. **When can you restart DOAC after major surgery?**
   - **48-72h Post-op** if Haemostasis Secure; **Earlier for Low Bleed Risk**

9. **Why is apixaban preferred over other DOACs in CKD?**
   - **Lowest Renal Clearance (27%)**, **Licensed down to CrCl 15**, **Consistent Pharmacokinetics**

10. **How do you switch from rivaroxaban to warfarin?**
    - **Start Warfarin + Continue Rivaroxaban until INR ≥2.0 for 2 consecutive days**, Then Stop Rivaroxaban
