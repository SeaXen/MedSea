# Renal Anaemia (Anaemia of Chronic Kidney Disease)

> [!info] **Davidson Ch 25 Alignment**: Anaemia and Red Cell Disorders → Renal Anaemia
> **FCPS/MRCP Focus**: EPO deficiency, HIF-PH inhibitors (roxadustat), IV iron, target Hb 10-11 g/dL, ESA resistance, cardiovascular risk

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Define renal anaemia: **Normocytic normochromic anaemia** due to **EPO deficiency** + **iron restriction** + **uraemic inhibitors** + **blood loss**
- [ ] Diagnose: **CKD (eGFR <60)**, Hb <13 (men) / <12 (women), **Exclude other causes** (iron, B12, folate, haemolysis, bleeding)
- [ ] Manage **Iron Therapy**: **IV iron preferred** in CKD/ESRD (hepcidin blocks oral); **TSAT >20%, Ferritin >100**
- [ ] Use **ESA (Epoetin/DA/darbepoetin)**: Target **Hb 10-11 g/dL**; **AVOID >11.5** (↑ stroke/thrombosis/CV death)
- [ ] Apply **HIF-PH Inhibitors (Roxadustat, Daprodustat, Vadadustat)**: Oral, ↑ endogenous EPO, ↑ iron mobilisation
- [ ] Recognise **ESA Resistance**: Iron deficiency, inflammation, hyperparathyroidism, blood loss, malignancy
- [ ] Manage **Pre-dialysis vs Dialysis vs Post-transplant** differences

---

## 📖 Definition & Pathophysiology

| Mechanism | Contribution |
|-----------|--------------|
| **EPO Deficiency** | **Primary driver** – peritubular fibroblasts fail to produce EPO as GFR declines |
| **Iron Restriction** | **Functional (hepcidin↑ blocks absorption/release)** + **Absolute (blood loss, dialysis)** |
| **Uraemic Toxins** | Inhibit erythropoiesis, shorten RBC survival |
| **Chronic Inflammation** | ↑ Hepcidin, ↓ EPO response, ↓ iron availability |
| **Blood Loss** | Dialysis circuit, GI angiodysplasia, platelet dysfunction |
| **Folate/B12 Deficiency** | Dialysis losses, poor intake |

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP**: **Renal anaemia = EPO deficiency + functional iron deficiency (hepcidin)**. **Normocytic normochromic**. **IV iron > oral in CKD/ESRD**. **Target Hb 10-11 g/dL (NOT >11.5)**.


## 🔬 Diagnostic Workup

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[CKD Patient with Anaemia] --> B[CBC: Hb <13 (M) / <12 (F) for CKD G3+]
    B --> C[**Iron Studies: Ferritin, TSAT**]
    C --> D[**Reticulocyte Count** (Low/Inappropriately normal)]
    D --> E[**Exclude Other Causes**]
    E --> F1[B12/Folate]
    E --> F2[Haemolysis Screen (LDH, Haptoglobin, Bilirubin)]
    E --> F3[Occult GI Blood Loss]
    E --> F4[Hyperparathyroidism (iPTH)]
    E --> F5[Nutritional Deficiencies]
    F1 & F2 & F3 & F4 & F5 --> G[**Diagnosis: Renal Anaemia** if CKD + No Other Cause]
    G --> H[**Assess Iron Status: TSAT, Ferritin**]
    H --> I[Guide Iron + ESA/HIF-PH Therapy]
```

### Key Investigations

| Test | Target/Interpretation |
|------|----------------------|
| **Hb** | <13 (men) / <12 (women) = Anaemia in CKD G3+ |
| **Ferritin** | **>100 ng/mL** (CKD/ESRD); >500 → assess inflammation |
| **TSAT** | **>20%** (functional iron sufficiency) |
| **CRP** | If high → ferritin unreliable (acute phase reactant) |
| **Reticulocytes** | Low/inappropriately normal (↓ production) |
| **iPTH** | If high → assess for aluminium toxicity (historical) / bone disease |
| **EPO Level** | Not routinely measured (low in CKD, but not diagnostic) |

## 💊 Management

### 1. Iron Therapy – **Foundation**

| Route | Preference | Target |
|-------|------------|--------|
| **IV Iron** | **Preferred in CKD G4-5 / Dialysis** (bypasses hepcidin block) | **Ferritin >100, TSAT >20%** |
| **Oral Iron** | CKD G1-3 (if compliant, no inflammation) | Same targets; often fails due to hepcidin |

**IV Iron Formulations**: Iron sucrose (Venofer), Ferric carboxymaltose (Ferinject), Ferric derisomaltose (Monofer), Iron isomaltoside

> [!tip] **IV iron repletes stores rapidly, improves ESA response, may avoid ESA**. **Avoid if active infection**.

### 2. Erythropoiesis-Stimulating Agents (ESA)

| Agent | Type | Dosing |
|-------|------|--------|
| **Epoetin alfa/beta** | Short-acting | 50-100 IU/kg SC/IV × 1-3/week |
| **Darbepoetin alfa** | Long-acting (DA) | **0.45 µg/kg SC/IV q2-4wk** (preferred for convenience) |
| **Methoxy PEG-Epoetin beta** | Ultra-long-acting | 0.6 µg/kg SC q2-4wk |

**Target Hb**: **10-11 g/dL** (KDIGO)
- **AVOID Hb >11.5 g/dL** (CHOIR, CREATE, TREAT trials: ↑ stroke, thrombosis, CV death, tumour progression)
- **Dose adjust**: Hb rise >1 g/dL in 2wk → ↓ dose 25%; Hb >11.5 → hold

### 3. HIF-PH Inhibitors (Oral, Newer)

| Agent | Mechanism | Advantages |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **Roxadustat** | **HIF-PH inhibition** → ↑ endogenous EPO transcription + ↑ iron absorption (↓ hepcidin) + ↑ HIF target genes | **Oral**, **↑ iron mobilisation**, **works in inflammation**, **no cold chain** |
| **Daprodustat** | Similar | Oral, once daily |
| **Vadadustat** | Similar | Oral, once daily |

**Indication**: CKD G3-5 (dialysis & non-dialysis); **Contraindicated**: Active malignancy, uncontrolled HTN, recent stroke/MI

## 🩺 ESA Resistance – **Common & Important**

**Definition**: Failure to achieve target Hb despite **Epoetin >300 IU/kg/wk** OR **Darbepoetin >1.5 µg/kg/wk**

| Cause | Evaluation | Management |
|-------|------------|------------|
| **Iron Deficiency (Absolute/Functional)** | **Most common**; Ferritin <100 or TSAT <20% | **IV Iron repletion** |
| **Inflammation/Infection** | ↑ CRP, ↑ Hepcidin | Treat source; IV iron; HIF-PH inhibitor |
| **Secondary Hyperparathyroidism** | iPTH >500 pg/mL | Cinacalcet, parathyroidectomy |
| **Occult Blood Loss** | GI (angiodysplasia), dialysis circuit | Endoscopy, optimise dialysis |
| **Malignancy** | Screen age-appropriate | Treat malignancy |
| **Aluminium Toxicity** | Historical (dialysis fluid) | Not current |
| **Folate/B12 Deficiency** | Levels | Replace |
| **Pure Red Cell Aplasia (Anti-EPO Ab)** | Very rare (epoetin) | Stop ESA, immunosuppression |

## 🎯 Target Hb & Monitoring (KDIGO)

| Population | Target Hb | ESA Initiation Threshold |
|------------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **CKD Non-Dialysis** | **10-11 g/dL** | Hb <10 g/dL (symptomatic) |
| **Dialysis (HD/PD)** | **10-11 g/dL** | Hb <10 g/dL |
| **Transplant** | **10-11 g/dL** | If anaemic post-transplant |

**Monitoring**: Hb **q2wk** during titration; **q4wk** at stable dose; **Iron studies q1-3mo**

## 🔄 Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Anaemia of Chronic Disease** | Similar mechanism (hepcidin); **No CKD** or CKD not primary driver |
| **Iron Deficiency Anaemia** | **Low Ferritin, Low TSAT, Microcytic**; Renal = normocytic |
| **B12/Folate Deficiency** | **Macrocytic**, specific levels low |
| **Myelodysplasia** | Dysplasia, cytogenetics, not explained by CKD alone |
| **Haemolysis** | ↑ LDH, ↓ Haptoglobin, ↑ Reticulocytes |

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **Mechanism** | **EPO deficiency + Hepcidin-mediated functional iron deficiency** |
| **Diagnosis** | CKD + Anaemia + Exclude other causes |
| **Iron** | **IV Iron preferred (CKD G4-5/ESRD)**; **Ferritin >100, TSAT >20%** |
| **ESA** | **Darbepoetin preferred (q2-4wk)**; **Target Hb 10-11 g/dL** |
| **Hb Target** | **DO NOT EXCEED 11.5 g/dL** (↑ CV events, stroke, death) |
| **ESA Resistance** | **Iron deficiency #1 cause** → IV iron; then inflammation, iPTH, blood loss |
| **HIF-PH Inhibitors** | **Roxadustat/Daprodustat/Vadadustat** – oral, ↑ EPO + ↓ hepcidin + ↑ iron |
| **Pre-dialysis** | Correct iron first; ESA if Hb <10 symptomatic |
| **Dialysis** | IV iron routine; ESA/HIF-PH per protocol |
| **Transplant** | Anaemia common early (immunosuppression); usually resolves by 6-12mo |

## ❓ Viva Questions

1. **What is the primary mechanism of anaemia in CKD?**
   - **EPO deficiency** (peritubular fibroblast loss) + **Functional iron deficiency** (hepcidin↑ blocks absorption/mobilisation)

2. **What are the KDIGO target Hb and upper limit for ESA therapy?**
   - **Target 10-11 g/dL**; **Upper limit 11.5 g/dL** (avoid – ↑ stroke/thrombosis/CV death)

3. **Why is IV iron preferred over oral in dialysis patients?**
   - **Hepcidin blocks oral absorption**; IV bypasses gut, rapidly repletes stores, improves ESA response

4. **What is the most common cause of ESA resistance?**
   - **Iron deficiency** (absolute or functional) – check Ferritin <100, TSAT <20%

5. **How do HIF-PH inhibitors (roxadustat) work?**
   - **Inhibit HIF prolyl hydroxylase** → stabilise HIF → **↑ endogenous EPO + ↓ hepcidin + ↑ iron absorption**

6. **When do you initiate ESA in CKD non-dialysis?**
   - **Hb <10 g/dL with symptoms** (after iron repletion); individualise

7. **What are the contraindications to HIF-PH inhibitors?**
   - Active malignancy, uncontrolled hypertension, recent stroke/MI/VTE

8. **How does secondary hyperparathyroidism cause ESA resistance?**
   - **Bone marrow fibrosis** (osteitis fibrosa) → impaired erythropoiesis; treat with cinacalcet/parathyroidectomy

9. **Differentiate renal anaemia from anaemia of chronic disease.**
   - **Renal = CKD-driven, EPO deficient**; **ACD = Inflammation-driven, EPO relatively preserved**

10. **What monitoring is required on ESA therapy?**
    - **Hb q2wk (titration) → q4wk (stable)**; **Iron studies q1-3mo**; **BP, vascular access**

## 🧠 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Renal Anaemia vs ACD** | **Renal = EPO deficient**; **ACD = EPO relatively normal but blunted response** |
| **IV vs Oral Iron** | **CKD G4-5/ESRD = IV** (hepcidin blocks oral); **CKD G1-3 = Oral trial** |
| **Hb Target** | **10-11 g/dL**; **>11.5 = HARM** (CHOIR, CREATE, TREAT) |
| **ESA Resistance** | **Iron #1**; then inflammation, iPTH, blood loss, malignancy |
| **HIF-PH Inhibitors** | **Oral**, **↑ EPO + ↓ Hepcidin + ↑ Iron** – different from ESA |

| Mnemonic | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **"Renal = EPO Down, Hepcidin Up"** | Dual mechanism |
| **"IV Iron in ESRD"** | Bypass hepcidin |
| **"Hb 10-11, Stop 11.5"** | Target & ceiling |
| **"ESA Resist = Iron First"** | Commonest cause |
| **"HIF = Oral, EPO + Iron"** | HIF-PH inhibitor benefit |
