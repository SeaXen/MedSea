# Haemophilia A & B

## Learning Objectives
- [ ] Differentiate Haemophilia A (FVIII deficiency) from Haemophilia B (FIX deficiency)

---

## Definition & Epidemiology

| Feature | Haemophilia A | Haemophilia B |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| **Deficiency** | **Factor VIII** | **Factor IX** |
| **Inheritance** | **X-Linked Recessive** | **X-Linked Recessive** |
| **Incidence** | **1 in 5,000 males** | **1 in 25,000 males** |
| **Gene** | **F8** (Xq28) | **FIX** (Xq27) |
| **Prevalence** | **~80% of Haemophilia** | ~15-20% |

> **FCPS/MRCP**: **Haemophilia A is 4-5x more common than Haemophilia B**. X-linked recessive — Males affected, females carriers.

---

## Classification by Severity

| Severity | Factor Level (% Normal) | Clinical Phenotype | Annual Bleeds |
|----------|------------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Severe** | **<1%** (<0.01 IU/mL) | Spontaneous Joint/Muscle Bleeds | 20-50 |
| **Moderate** | **1-5%** (0.01-0.05 IU/mL) | Bleeds after Minor Trauma | 1-5 |
| **Mild** | **5-40%** (0.05-0.4 IU/mL) | Bleeding after Major Trauma/Surgery | Rare |

> **FCPS/MRCP**: **Severity = Factor Level**. Severe = <1% = Prophylaxis Indicated.

---

## Clinical Presentation by Severity

| Severity | Typical Presentation |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Severe** | Spontaneous Haemarthroses (Knees, Elbows, Ankles), Muscle Haematomas, Neonatal Cephalhaematoma, Prolonged Bleeding Post-Circumcision |
| **Moderate** | Bleeding After Minor Trauma/Surgery, Occasional Spontaneous Joint Bleeds |
| **Mild** | Bleeding Only After Major Trauma/Surgery/Dental Extraction |

> **FCPS/MRCP**: **Target Joints** = Recurrent Haemarthroses → Synovial Hypertrophy → Chronic Arthropathy. **Knees, Elbows, Ankles** most common.

## Inheritance & Genetics

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Haemophilia] --> B[X-Linked Recessive]
    B --> C[F8 Gene (Xq28) → Haemophilia A]
    B --> D[FIX Gene (Xq27) → Haemophilia B]
    C --> E[Inversions (Intron 22/1) = Severe HA]
    D --> F[Point Mutations = Mild/Moderate]
```

| Feature | Haemophilia A | Haemophilia B |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| **Gene** | **F8** (Xq28) | **FIX** (Xq27) |
| **Severe Mutation** | **Intron 22 Inversion** (~50%) / Intron 1 Inversion (~5%) | **Point Mutations** (Rare Inversions) |
| **Carrier Females** | **Symptomatic Carriers** Possible (Lyonization) | Same |
| **New Mutations** | **~30%** (No Family History) | ~30% |

## Clinical Features by Factor Level

| Factor Level | Clinical Picture |
|--------------|------------------|
| **<1% (<0.01 IU/mL)** | **Spontaneous Haemarthroses**, Muscle Bleeds, Life-threatening (ICH, Retroperitoneal) |
| **1-5%** | Trauma-Induced Bleeds, Occasional Spontaneous Joint Bleeds |
| **5-40%** | **Post-Surgical/Trauma Bleeding**, Baseline Asymptomatic |

---

## Diagnostic Assessment

### Laboratory Diagnosis

| Test | Normal | Haemophilia A | Haemophilia B |
|------|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **PT** | Normal | Normal | Normal |
| **aPTT** | Normal | **Prolonged** | **Prolonged** |
| **Factor VIII:C** | 50-150% | **Low** | **Normal** |
| **Factor IX:C** | 50-150% | Normal | **Low** |
| **vWF:Ag** | 50-150% | Normal/↓ (Type 2N) | Normal |
| **Bleeding Time** | Normal | Normal/↑ | Normal |

### Mixing Studies
| Result | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| **aPTT Corrects** | Factor Deficiency (Haemophilia) |
| **aPTT Does NOT Correct** | Inhibitor (Antibody) |

> **FCPS/MRCP**: **Prolonged aPTT + Normal PT = Intrinsic Pathway Defect**. **Mixing Study Corrects = Factor Deficiency; Fails = Inhibitor**.

---

## Severity Classification & Management Approach

| Severity | FVIII/FIX Level | Management Strategy |
|----------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Severe (<1%)** | <1% | **Primary Prophylaxis** (Standard of Care) |
| **Moderate (1-5%)** | 1-5% | **Prophylaxis or On-Demand** (Individualised) |
| **Mild (5-40%)** | 5-40% | **On-Demand** Treatment |

---

## Treatment: Factor Replacement

### Factor Concentrates
| Product | Haemophilia A | Haemophilia B |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **Plasma-Derived** | FVIII Concentrate | FIX Concentrate |
| **Recombinant (rFVIII/rFIX)** | **Standard (Preferred)** | **Standard (Preferred)** |
| **Extended Half-Life (EHL)** | **Elocta® (rFVIII-Fc), Adynovi®, Jivi®** | **Alprolix®, Idelvion®, Rebinyn®** |

> **FCPS/MRCP**: **Recombinant Factor = Standard of Care** (No Viral Transmission Risk). **EHL = Less Frequent Dosing**.

---

## Dosing Calculations

### FVIII Dose (Haemophilia A)
```
Dose (IU/kg) = [Desired % Rise - Current %] / Recovery
Recovery = 2 IU/dL per IU/kg (2% per IU/kg)

Dose (IU) = Weight (kg) × Desired Rise (%) / 2
```

| Situation | Target FVIII Level |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Minor Bleed** | **30-50%** |
| **Major Bleed / Surgery** | **80-100%** |
| **Life-Threatening (ICH)** | **100%** |

### FIX Dose (Haemophilia B)
```
Recovery = 1 IU/dL per IU/kg (1% per IU/kg) — Half of FVIII

Dose (IU) = Weight (kg) × Desired Rise (%) / 1
```

## Prophylaxis Regimens

### Standard Half-Life (SHL)
| Regimen | FVIII Dose | FIX Dose | Frequency |
|---------|------------|----------|-----------|
| **Standard** | 25-40 IU/kg | 40-60 IU/kg | **3x/Week (FVIII) / 2x/Week (FIX)** |
| **PK-Guided** | PK Tailored | PK Tailored | Individualised |

### Extended Half-Life (EHL)
| Product | FVIII | FIX |
|---------|-------|-----|
| **Brand** | Elocta® (rFVIII-Fc), Adynovi®, Jivi® | Alprolix®, Idelvion®, Rebinyn® |
| **Half-Life** | **~1.5-1.9x SHL** | **~5x SHL** |
| **Dosing Frequency** | **2x/Week** | **Weekly / Biweekly** |

> **FCPS/MRCP**: **EHL = Less Frequent Injections, Better Adherence, Better Joint Outcomes**.

---

## Inhibitor Development

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Incidence** | **HA: 20-30%** (Severe); **HB: 2-5%** |
| **Risk Factors** | Severe HA, Family History, Black Race, Intensive Early Treatment, High-Dose FVIII |
| **Diagnosis** | **Bethesda Assay** (Inhibitor Titre in BU/mL) |
| **Low Titre (<5 BU)** | Bypass Often Works, ITI Possible |
| **High Titre (≥5 BU)** | **Bypass Agents Required** |

### Inhibitor Management

| Scenario | Management |
|---------|------------|
| **Low Titre (<5 BU)** | **High-Dose FVIII** (May Overcome) / **ITI** (Immune Tolerance Induction) |
| **High Titre (≥5 BU)** | **Bypass Agents**: **rFVIIa (NovoSeven®)** OR **aPCC (FEIBA®)** |

### Bypass Agents

| Agent | Dose |
|-------|------|
| **rFVIIa (NovoSeven®)** | **90 µg/kg q2-3h** (Until Haemostasis) |
| **aPCC (FEIBA®)** | **50-100 U/kg q8-12h** (Max 200 U/kg/day) |

> **FEIBA**: Max **200 U/kg/day** (Thrombosis Risk).

---

## Prophylaxis Protocols

### Primary Prophylaxis (Start by Age 2-3 in Severe)
| Regimen | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Standard Half-Life FVIII** | 25-40 IU/kg **3x/Week** |
| **EHL FVIII** | 20-50 IU/kg **2x/Week** |
| **FIX (SHL)** | 40-60 IU/kg **2x/Week** |
| **FIX (EHL)** | 40-80 IU/kg **Weekly/Biweekly** |

> **Goal**: Maintain Trough FVIII >1% (Severe) / >2-3% (Moderate) — Prevent Joint Bleeds.

---

## Acute Bleed Management

| Bleed Type | Factor Target | Duration |
|------------|---------------|----------|
| **Early Haemarthrosis** | 30-50% | 1-2 Days |
| **Established Haemarthrosis** | 50-80% | 3-5 Days |
| **Muscle Haematoma** | 50-80% | 3-5 Days |
| **Life-Threatening (ICH, Retroperitoneal, Airway)** | **100%** | 7-14 Days |
| **Major Surgery** | **80-100%** | 7-14 Days |

---

## Target Joint Management

| Stage | Features | Management |
|-------|----------|------------|
| **Early** (1-3 bleeds/6mo) | Recurrent Haemarthroses | **Intensify Prophylaxis**, Physiotherapy |
| **Established** (≥4 bleeds/6mo) | Synovial Hypertrophy | **Synovectomy** (Arthroscopic/Radioisotope) + Factor Cover |
| **End-Stage** | Fixed Contracture, Arthritis | Orthopaedic Surgery (Synovectomy, Arthroplasty) |

> **Target Joint**: **≥4 Bleeds in 6 Months** in Same Joint = Target Joint.

---

## Surgical Prophylaxis

| Surgery Type | FVIII Target | FIX Target | Duration |
|------------|--------------|------------|----------|
| **Minor (Dental, Biopsy)** | 30-50% | 30-50% | 1-3 Days |
| **Major (Abdominal, Orthopedic)** | **80-100%** | 80-100% | 7-14 Days |
| **Neurosurgery/Cardiac** | **100%** | 100% | 10-14 Days |

> **Continuous Infusion** often used for Major Surgery (Steady Levels).

---

## Carrier Females

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Bleeding Risk** | **Symptomatic Carriers** if FVIII/FIX <40% (Lyonization) |
| **Management** | Treat as Mild Haemophilia (DDAVP, Factor Concentrate) |
| **Pregnancy** | Levels Rise (FVIII ↑↑, FIX ↑); Monitor Postpartum Drop |

## Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| FVIII vs FIX Recovery | **FVIII: 2 IU/dL per IU/kg**; **FIX: 1 IU/dL per IU/kg** (FIX Half) |
| Haemophilia A vs B | **FVIII vs FIX**; HA 4-5x More Common |
| Severe vs Moderate | **Severe <1%** (Spontaneous Bleeds); **Moderate 1-5%** (Trauma Bleeds) |
| Prophylaxis vs On-Demand | **Prophylaxis = Prevent Bleeds**; **On-Demand = Treat Bleeds** |
| rFVIIa vs FEIBA | **rFVIIa = Recombinant FVIIa**; **FEIBA = aPCC (Contains FII, FIX, FX)** |
| FVIII Half-Life | **SHL ~12h**; **EHL ~18-24h** |
| FIX Half-Life | **SHL ~18h**; **EHL ~80-100h** |
