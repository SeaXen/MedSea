# Renal Artery Stenosis (RAS)

**Related:** [[Vascular Diseases of the Kidney — Thrombotic Microangiopathies (HUS, TTP)]], [[Vascular Diseases of the Kidney — Atheroembolic Disease]], [[Vascular Diseases of the Kidney — Renal Vein Thrombosis]], [[Hypertension]], [[Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **RAS = narrowing of renal artery → renal hypoperfusion → RAAS activation → hypertension. Atherosclerotic (90%): older, smokers, CVD, diffuse atherosclerosis. FMD (10%): younger women, "string of beads". Clinical: resistant/flash pulmonary oedema, asymmetric kidneys, bruits. Gold standard: catheter angiography; screening: duplex US, CTA, MRA. Revascularisation: PTAS for FMD; atherosclerotic: medical therapy first (CORAL trial), PTAS for flash pulmonary oedema/refractory HTN.**

---

## Learning Objectives
- Distinguish atherosclerotic RAS vs fibromuscular dysplasia (FMD)
- Recognise clinical presentations (resistant HTN, flash pulmonary oedema, CKD)
- Apply diagnostic algorithm (duplex US → CTA/MRA → catheter angiography)
- Understand revascularisation indications (CORAL, ASTRAL trials)
- Manage medically (ACEi/ARB caution, statin, antiplatelet)

---

## Aetiology & Classification

| Type | % | Demographics | Pathology | Location |
|------|---|--------------|-----------|----------|
| **Atherosclerotic (ARAS)** | **90%** | >50yo, M>F, smokers, CVD, diabetes, dyslipidaemia | Atheromatous plaque | **Ostium/proximal 1/3** (commonest); can be distal |
| **Fibromuscular Dysplasia (FMD)** | **10%** | **Young women (15–50yo)**, F:M 9:1 | **Medial fibroplasia** ("string of beads") | **Mid/distal renal artery** (sparing ostium) |
| **Others** | <1% | | Vasculitis (Takayasu, PAN), neurofibromatosis, external compression, radiation | Variable |

---

## Pathophysiology

| Mechanism | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Renal artery narrowing** | ↓ Renal perfusion pressure |
| **RAAS activation** | ↑ Renin → ↑ Angiotensin II → ↑ Aldosterone |
| **Systemic effects** | Vasoconstriction, sodium retention → **hypertension** |
| **Contralateral kidney** | Pressure natriuresis → volume expansion → worsens HTN |
| **Chronic ischaemia** | Tubulointerstitial fibrosis → **atrophic kidney** (if bilateral/severe) |

---

## Clinical Presentation

| Feature | Atherosclerotic RAS | FMD |
|---------|---------------------|-----|
| **Age** | >50 years | **15–50 years** |
| **Sex** | M > F | **F > M (9:1)** |
| **Hypertension** | Resistant, sudden onset/worsening, malignant | Resistant, often younger onset |
| **Flash Pulmonary Oedema** | **Classic** (bilateral RAS or solitary kidney) | Rare |
| **Asymmetric Kidneys** | >1.5 cm size difference | Possible |
| **Abdominal Bruit** | ~50% (systolic-diastolic) | ~50% |
| **Peripheral Vascular Disease** | Common (carotid, femoral, coronary) | Absent |
| **Renal Function** | CKD, often asymmetric | Usually preserved |
| **Associated Features** | CVD, diabetes, smoking | **None** (isolated) |

> [!key]
> **Flash pulmonary oedema + resistant HTN + preserved LV function = think bilateral RAS (or solitary kidney RAS).**

## Diagnostic Algorithm

### Screening (Non-invasive)
| Test | Sensitivity/Specificity | Role |
|------|------------------------|------|
| **Duplex US** | Sens 85–90%, Spec 90–95% (operator-dependent) | **First-line screening**; measures PSV, RAR, resistive index |
| **CT Angiography** | Sens 95–99%, Spec 95% | **Anatomical detail**; contrast risk (avoid if CKD) |
| **MR Angiography** | Sens 90–95%, Spec 90–95% | **No radiation**; gadolinium risk (NSF if eGFR <30); overestimates stenosis |
| **Captopril Renography** | Sens 80–90%, Spec 85–90% | Functional significance; less used now |

### Duplex US Criteria (Renal Artery)
| Parameter | Threshold for >60% Stenosis |
|-----------|-----------------------------|
| **Peak Systolic Velocity (PSV)** | **>180–200 cm/s** |
| **Renal-Aortic Ratio (RAR)** | **>3.5** (Renal PSV / Aortic PSV) |
| **Resistive Index (RI)** | **>0.80** (predicts poor recovery post-revascularisation) |

### Gold Standard
| Test | Role |
|------|------|
| **Catheter Angiography** | **Gold standard** (anatomical + physiological); pressure gradient measurement; allows intervention |

## Indications for Revascularisation (PTAS / Surgery)

| Guideline | Indications |
|-----------|-------------|
| **KDIGO/ESC/ACC** | 1. **Flash pulmonary oedema** (bilateral RAS or solitary kidney) |
| | 2. **Refractory hypertension** despite ≥3 agents (incl. diuretic) |
| | 3. **Progressive CKD** with unilateral/bilateral RAS (no other cause) |
| | 4. **Recurrent acute kidney injury** (esp. with ACEi/ARB) |
| **NOT Indicated** (CORAL, ASTRAL trials) | Asymptomatic RAS; stable CKD; HTN controlled on meds |

> [!key]
> **CORAL trial (2014)**: PTAS + medical therapy vs medical therapy alone in ARAS — **NO difference** in CV/renal outcomes.
> **ASTRAL trial (2009)**: Revascularisation + medical vs medical alone — **NO benefit**, more complications.
> **FMD**: PTAS **highly effective** (cure/improvement >80%); first-line for symptomatic FMD.

---

## Revascularisation: PTAS vs Surgery

| Modality | Indication | Outcome |
|----------|------------|---------|
| **PTAS (Percutaneous Transluminal Angioplasty + Stent)** | **ARAS**: ostial/proximal lesions (stent required) | Technical success >95%; restenosis 10–20% |
| **PTAS (Balloon only)** | **FMD**: mid/distal lesions (no stent usually) | **Cure/improvement >80%**; low restenosis |
| **Surgical Bypass/Endarterectomy** | Complex anatomy, failed PTAS, aortic aneurysm repair | Higher morbidity; durable |

---

## Medical Management (All RAS)

| Intervention | Target |
|--------------|--------|
| **BP Control** | <130/80 (KDIGO) |
| **ACEi/ARB** | **Caution**: can precipitate AKI in bilateral RAS/solitary kidney; **monitor Cr/K+ closely**; if Cr ↑ >30% or K+ >5.5 → stop/reduce |
| **Statin** | High-intensity (all ARAS) |
| **Antiplatelet** | Aspirin 75mg (ARAS) |
| **Smoking Cessation** | Mandatory |
| **Diabetes/Lipid Control** | Standard targets |
| **Diuretic** | Thiazide (if eGFR >30); loop (if eGFR <30) — avoid volume depletion |

---

## ACEi/ARB in RAS — Special Considerations

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Unilateral RAS, normal contralateral** | ACEi/ARB **safe**; monitor Cr (↑ up to 30% acceptable) |
| **Bilateral RAS or Solitary Kidney RAS** | ACEi/ARB **high risk AKI**; avoid or use with extreme caution + close monitoring |
| **Cr rise >30% or K+ >5.5** | Stop ACEi/ARB |
| **Flash pulmonary oedema** | ACEi/ARB **beneficial** post-revascularisation |

## High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **RAS = 90% atherosclerotic (older, CVD), 10% FMD (young women, "string of beads")**
> - **ARAS: ostial/proximal; FMD: mid/distal ("string of beads")**
> - **Clinical**: Resistant HTN, **flash pulmonary oedema** (bilateral/solitary kidney), asymmetric kidneys, bruits
> - **Screening**: Duplex US (PSV >180–200, RAR >3.5); CTA/MRA for anatomy
> - **Gold standard**: Catheter angiography (allows PTAS)
> - **Revascularisation INDICATED**: Flash pulmonary oedema, refractory HTN (≥3 drugs), progressive CKD, recurrent AKI
> - **Revascularisation NOT indicated**: Asymptomatic, controlled HTN, stable CKD (CORAL, ASTRAL)
> - **FMD**: PTAS balloon only = >80% cure; ARAS: PTAS + stent
> - **ACEi/ARB**: Caution in bilateral/solitary kidney; monitor Cr/K+
> - **Medical therapy**: Statin, aspirin, BP control, smoking cessation

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **All RAS needs stenting** | Only symptomatic (flash oedema, refractory HTN, progressive CKD); CORAL/ASTRAL negative |
| **ACEi/ARB contraindicated in all RAS** | Only bilateral/solitary kidney high risk; unilateral = safe with monitoring |
| **FMD = atherosclerotic** | FMD = young women, "string of beads", medial fibroplasia, mid/distal |
| **Duplex US = gold standard** | Catheter angiography = gold standard; duplex = screening |
| **Flash pulmonary oedema = only heart failure** | **Think bilateral RAS** (preserved LV function) |
| **FMD needs stent** | FMD = balloon angioplasty only (no stent usually) |
| **ARAS = always ostial** | Ostial/proximal most common but can be distal |
| **RI >0.80 = good recovery** | **RI >0.80 = POOR recovery** post-revascularisation |

## Summary

**Renal Artery Stenosis** is a **Must Know 🔴** topic.
**Key takeaway:** 90% ARAS (older, ostial/proximal, CVD), 10% FMD (young women, "string of beads" mid/distal). Clinical: resistant HTN, **flash pulmonary oedema** (bilateral/solitary kidney), asymmetric kidneys, bruits. **Duplex: PSV >180–200, RAR >3.5, RI >0.80 (poor recovery).** Gold standard: catheter angiography. **Revascularisation INDICATED: flash PO, refractory HTN, progressive CKD, recurrent AKI.** CORAL/ASTRAL: NO benefit for asymptomatic/controlled. **FMD: balloon only >80% cure; ARAS: PTAS + stent.** ACEi/ARB: caution bilateral/solitary kidney. Medical: statin, aspirin, BP <130/80, smoking cessation.
**Exam focus:** ARAS vs FMD, flash PO, duplex criteria, revascularisation indications, CORAL/ASTRAL, FMD vs ARAS treatment, ACEi caution, gold standard.
**Clinical relevance:** Avoid unnecessary stenting; medical therapy first for most ARAS; FMD highly curable with balloon.
