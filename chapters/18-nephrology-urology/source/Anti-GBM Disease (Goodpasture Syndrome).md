# Anti-GBM Disease (Goodpasture's Syndrome)

**Related:** [[Glomerular Diseases — Overview and Classification]], [[MPGN and C3 Glomerulopathy (DDD, C3GN)]], [[Cryoglobulinaemic Glomerulonephritis]], [[Rapidly Progressive Glomerulonephritis (RPGN)]], [[Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **Anti-GBM disease = antibodies against α3 chain of type IV collagen (NC1 domain) in GBM & alveolar basement membrane. 40–60% have pulmonary haemorrhage (Goodpasture's syndrome). Linear IgG on IF (pathognomonic). RPGN with crescents >50%. Peak incidence: young men (20–30) & older adults (60–70). HLA-DR15 association.**

---

## Learning Objectives
- Recognise clinical presentation (RPGN ± pulmonary haemorrhage)
- Interpret anti-GBM antibody testing and renal biopsy (linear IgG)
- Apply emergency management (plasma exchange + cyclophosphamide + steroids)
- Understand prognosis factors (dialysis dependence, pulmonary haemorrhage)
- Differentiate from ANCA vasculitis (double-positive overlap)
- Apply to FCPS/MRCP emergency vignettes

## Pathogenesis
| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Target antigen** | **α3(IV)NC1 collagen** (non-collagenous domain of α3 chain of type IV collagen) |
| **Antibody** | IgG (mostly IgG1, IgG3) — anti-GBM |
| **Location** | **GBM** (renal) + **Alveolar basement membrane** (pulmonary) |
| **HLA association** | **HLA-DR15 (DRB1*1501)** — strong (88% vs 25% controls) |
| **Triggers** | Hydrocarbon exposure, smoking, viral infections (influenza), cocaine, HLA-DR15 |

> **Mechanism**: Anti-GBM IgG binds GBM → complement activation (C5b-9) + neutrophil recruitment → crescent formation. Same antigen in alveoli → pulmonary capillaritis → haemorrhage.

---

## Clinical Presentation

### Renal (100%)
| Feature | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|
| **RPGN** (AKI, haematuria, RBC casts, proteinuria) | 100% |
| **Hypertension** | 60–80% |
| **Oliguria/anuria** | If advanced |
| **Subnephrotic proteinuria** | <3.5g/day typical |

### Pulmonary (40–60% = Goodpasture's Syndrome)
| Feature | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|
| **Haemoptysis** | 70–90% of pulmonary involvement |
| **Dyspnoea** | Common |
| **Iron deficiency anaemia** | Chronic occult blood loss |
| **CXR/CT** | Bilateral diffuse alveolar infiltrates (flitting) |

> **Smoking/hydrocarbon exposure** ↑ pulmonary involvement risk.

---

## Diagnosis

### Serology
| Test | Sensitivity | Specificity | Role |
|------|-------------|-------------|------|
| **Anti-GBM ELISA** | 90–95% | 95–100% | **Diagnostic** (quantitative — monitors disease) |
| **Anti-GBM IIF** | Lower | High | Confirmatory |
| **ANCA (p-ANCA/MPO, c-ANCA/PR3)** | — | — | **Check for double-positive** (20–30%) |

> **Anti-GBM titre** correlates with disease activity. Falling titre = response to treatment.

### Renal Biopsy (If needed — often diagnosed on serology + clinical)
| Finding | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **LM** | **Crescentic GN** — cellular crescents >50% (RPGN) |
| **IF** | **Linear IgG** along GBM (pathognomonic) — ± C3 linear |
| **EM** | No immune deposits (pauci-immune by EM) — GBM disruption |

> **Differential on IF**: Linear IgG also in **diabetic nephropathy** (non-specific, weak), **fibrillary GN** (smudgy), **amyloid** (kappa/lambda).

---

## Double-Positive Anti-GBM + ANCA (20–30%)
| Feature | Anti-GBM alone | Double-positive (Anti-GBM + ANCA) |
|---------|----------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Age** | Bimodal (20–30, 60–70) | Older (mean 60+) |
| **ANCA type** | — | **p-ANCA/MPO** (90%) |
| **Pulmonary haemorrhage** | 40–60% | ↑ (60–80%) |
| **Renal survival** | Better if treated early | Worse |
| **Relapse** | Rare | ↑ (ANCA component) |
| **Treatment** | PLEX + CyC + Steroids | **Same + consider maintenance** (azathioprine/rituximab) |

> **Always check ANCA** in anti-GBM disease. Double-positive = vasculitis overlap.

---

## Management (EMERGENCY)

### Indications for Urgent Treatment
- **Anti-GBM positive + RPGN** (creatinine rising, crescents on biopsy)
- **Pulmonary haemorrhage** (life-threatening)

### Standard Regimen (KDIGO/ERA-EDTA)
| Component | Dose/Duration | Role |
|-----------|---------------|------|
| **Plasma Exchange (PLEX)** | **1–1.5 plasma vol daily × 14 days** (or until anti-GBM negative) | Removes circulating anti-GBM antibody |
| **Cyclophosphamide (CyC)** | **IV 500mg/m² q2wk × 3mo** (or oral 2mg/kg/day × 3mo) | Stops new antibody production |
| **Corticosteroids** | **Methylprednisolone 500–1000mg IV × 3 days** → Prednisolone 1mg/kg/day (taper 3–6mo) | Anti-inflammatory, ↓ cytokine storm |

> **PLEX**: Start **immediately** — do not wait for biopsy if clinical picture clear. Use 5% albumin or FFP replacement.

### Pulmonary Haemorrhage Additions
| Intervention | Indication |
|--------------|------------|
| **Intubation/ventilation** | Massive haemorrhage, hypoxia |
| **Recombinant Factor VIIa** | Refractory bleeding (case reports) |
| **Tranexamic acid** | Adjunct (avoid if thrombotic risk) |

### Monitoring
| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Anti-GBM titre** | Weekly during PLEX, then monthly × 6mo, then q3mo |
| **Renal function** | Daily during acute, then weekly |
| **Hb/iron studies** | Weekly (pulmonary bleed) |
| **ANCA** | If double-positive, monitor q3mo |

## Prognosis

| Factor | Good Prognosis | Poor Prognosis |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **Serum Creatinine at presentation** | <300 μmol/L | **>500–600 μmol/L / dialysis-dependent** |
| **% Crescents on biopsy** | <50% | **>75–80%** |
| **Pulmonary haemorrhage** | Absent | Present (↑ early mortality) |
| **Anti-GBM titre** | Low | High |
| **Double-positive ANCA** | — | Worse renal survival, ↑ relapse |

> **Dialysis dependence at presentation** → **<10% renal recovery**. Early treatment (Cr <300) → 70–80% renal survival at 1yr.

### Relapse
- **Anti-GBM alone**: Very rare (<5%) once antibody cleared.
- **Double-positive**: Relapse rate ~20–30% (ANCA component) — consider maintenance azathioprine/rituximab.

## High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **Anti-GBM disease**: Anti-α3(IV)NC1 collagen IgG → linear IgG on IF.
> - **Goodpasture's syndrome** = Anti-GBM + pulmonary haemorrhage (40–60%).
> - **HLA-DR15** strong association.
> - **Emergency**: PLEX (daily × 14 days) + IV CyC + IV methylprednisolone → **START IMMEDIATELY**.
> - **Dialysis-dependent at presentation** → poor renal recovery (<10%).
> - **Check ANCA** — 20–30% double-positive (p-ANCA/MPO) → worse prognosis, ↑ relapse.
> - **Anti-GBM titre** monitors disease activity.
> - **Relapse rare** in pure anti-GBM; common in double-positive.
> - **Differential linear IgG**: Diabetic nephropathy (weak, non-specific), fibrillary GN, amyloid.

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **Linear IgG = always anti-GBM** | Also in **diabetic nephropathy** (weak, non-specific), fibrillary GN, amyloid |
| **Anti-GBM = no ANCA** | **20–30% double-positive** (p-ANCA/MPO) — worse prognosis |
| **Wait for biopsy before treatment** | **NO — treat immediately** if clinical + serology fits (PLEX life-saving) |
| **PLEX = only for pulmonary bleed** | PLEX for **ALL** anti-GBM RPGN (removes antibody) |
| **Good prognosis if creatinine normal** | **Creatinine at presentation** is key — >500 = dialysis likely permanent |
| **Maintenance immunosuppression always needed** | Pure anti-GBM: **no maintenance** (relapse rare); Double-positive: consider maintenance |
| **Anti-GBM IFA = ELISA** | ELISA = quantitative, diagnostic standard; IFA = confirmatory |
| **Children never get anti-GBM** | Rare but occurs (paediatric RPGN differential) |
| **Pulmonary haemorrhage = always Goodpasture's** | Also in ANCA vasculitis, SLE, cryoglobulinaemia, anticoagulation |

## Summary
**Anti-GBM Disease** is a **Must Know 🔴** emergency topic.  
**Pathogenesis**: IgG against α3(IV)NC1 collagen → linear IgG on IF. HLA-DR15.  
**Clinical**: RPGN (100%) ± pulmonary haemorrhage (40–60% = Goodpasture's).  
**Diagnosis**: Anti-GBM ELISA (quantitative) + **always check ANCA** (20–30% double-positive). Biopsy: crescents >50%, linear IgG IF, no deposits EM.  
**Emergency Rx**: **PLEX (daily × 14d) + IV CyC + IV Methylpred → oral taper**. Start immediately — do not wait for biopsy.  
**Prognosis**: Dialysis-dependent at presentation → <10% renal recovery. Cr <300 = good recovery.  
**Double-positive (20–30%)**: p-ANCA/MPO, worse prognosis, ↑ relapse → maintenance immunosuppression.  
**Monitoring**: Anti-GBM titre weekly → monthly → q3mo.  
**Exam focus**: Linear IgG, emergency PLEX+CyC+steroids, creatinine prognosis, double-positive ANCA, Goodpasture's definition, relapse patterns.
