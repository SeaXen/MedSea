# ADTKD & Other Inherited Tubulointerstitial Diseases

**Related:** [[Polycystic Kidney Disease (ADPKD, ARPKD)]], [[Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)]], [[Renal Tubular Acidosis (RTA Types 1, 2, 4)]], [[Bartter and Gitelman Syndromes]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **ADTKD = Autosomal Dominant Tubulointerstitial Kidney Disease. Bland urine sediment (no protein/haematuria), progressive CKD, normal-sized kidneys. Four main genes: UMOD (gout), MUC1, REN (anaemia), HNF1B (MODY5). Formerly "Medullary Cystic Kidney Disease (MCKD)" & "Familial Juvenile Hyperuricaemic Nephropathy (FJHN)".**

---

## Learning Objectives
- Differentiate ADTKD subtypes (UMOD, MUC1, REN, HNF1B, SEC61A1)
- Recognise clinical clues (gout, anaemia, MODY, bland urine)
- Apply genetic testing indications
- Distinguish from ADPKD (no massive cysts) and other inherited diseases
- Manage conservatively (uric acid, BP, CKD progression, transplant counselling)

## ADTKD Subtypes Comparison

| Gene | Protein | Old Name | Key Features | Extrarenal |
|------|---------|----------|--------------|------------|
| **UMOD** | Uromodulin (Tamm-Horsfall) | FJHN / MCKD2 | **Early-onset gout** (teens-30s), hyperuricaemia, CKD, bland urine | — |
| **MUC1** | Mucin-1 | MCKD1 | CKD, **no gout**, bland urine, indistinguishable from UMOD clinically | — |
| **REN** | Renin | — | **Childhood anaemia**, hypotension, hyperkalaemia, CKD | — |
| **HNF1B** | Transcription factor | MODY5 / RCAD | **MODY5 diabetes**, renal cysts, genital anomalies, hyperuricaemia | Pancreas, liver, GU tract |
| **SEC61A1** | Translocon subunit | — | CKD, anaemia, neurodevelopmental delay | Brain |

## ADTKD-UMOD (Uromodulin-Associated)

### Genetics
- **UMOD** (chr16p12.3) → **Uromodulin** (Tamm-Horsfall protein) — most abundant urinary protein
- **Autosomal dominant**, high penetrance
- Mutations → misfolding → ER retention → tubular cell stress → interstitial fibrosis

### Clinical Features
| Feature | Typical |
|---------|---------|
| **Gout** | **Onset teens-30s** (often first manifestation), tophi may develop |
| **Hyperuricaemia** | Marked (↓ fractional excretion of urate) |
| **CKD** | Slow progression, ESRD 40–70yr |
| **Urine** | **Bland** (no proteinuria, no haematuria) |
| **Kidneys** | Normal or slightly small (no massive cysts like ADPKD) |
| **Family history** | Strong, vertical transmission |

> **Uromodulin** normally inhibits NKCC2 in TAL → mutations → ↑ urate reabsorption → hyperuricaemia/gout.

---

## ADTKD-MUC1 (Mucin-1-Associated)

### Genetics
- **MUC1** (chr1q22) → Mucin-1 (transmembrane glycoprotein)
- **Cytosine insertion** in VNTR → frameshift → toxic frameshift protein
- Autosomal dominant

### Clinical Features
| Feature | Typical |
|---------|---------|
| **Gout** | **Absent** (key distinguisher from UMOD) |
| **Hyperuricaemia** | Mild/absent |
| **CKD** | Similar progression to UMOD |
| **Urine** | Bland |
| **Diagnosis** | **Genetic testing required** (no commercial protein assay) |

> **No gout** = think MUC1. If gout present = think UMOD.

---

## ADTKD-REN (Renin-Associated)

### Genetics
- **REN** (chr1q32) → Renin (rate-limiting RAAS enzyme)
- Autosomal dominant

### Clinical Features
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Anaemia** | **Childhood-onset** (↓ EPO from ↓ renin → ↓ Ang II → ↓ EPO stim) |
| **Hypotension** | Low BP (↓ Ang II) |
| **Hyperkalaemia** | ↓ Aldo (↓ renin → ↓ Ang II → ↓ aldo) |
| **CKD** | Progressive, ESRD 40–60yr |
| **Hyperuricaemia/Gout** | May occur |

> **Childhood anaemia + hypotension + hyperkalaemia + CKD** = ADTKD-REN.

---

## ADTKD-HNF1B (HNF1B-Associated = MODY5 / RCAD)

### Genetics
- **HNF1B** (chr17q12) → Hepatocyte Nuclear Factor 1β (transcription factor)
- Autosomal dominant; **de novo** mutations common (~50%)

### Clinical Features (RCAD = Renal Cysts And Diabetes)
| System | Manifestation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Renal** | **Cysts** (variable, often cortical), hypoplastic kidneys, single kidney, CAKUT |
| **Pancreas** | **MODY5 diabetes** (early-onset, insulin-deficient, not insulin-resistant) |
| **Genital** | **Müllerian aplasia** (absent uterus/fallopian tubes in females), hypospadias |
| **Liver** | Elevated LFTs, hepatic cysts |
| **Other** | Hypomagnesaemia (renal Mg wasting), hyperuricaemia, gout |

> **MODY5 + renal cysts + genital anomalies** = HNF1B. Most pleiotropic ADTKD.

---

## ADTKD-SEC61A1

- **SEC61A1** (chr3q24) → Translocon subunit
- CKD, anaemia, **neurodevelopmental delay**, intellectual disability
- Rare

---

## Diagnostic Approach

| Step | Test | Finding |
|------|------|---------|
| 1 | **Urinalysis** | **Bland** (no protein, no haematuria) — key clue |
| 2 | **Serum urate** | ↑ in UMOD, HNF1B |
| 3 | **Kidney US** | Normal/small kidneys, **cortical cysts** (HNF1B), **no massive cysts** (vs ADPKD) |
| 4 | **Family history** | Autosomal dominant vertical transmission |
| 5 | **Genetic panel** | UMOD, MUC1, REN, HNF1B, SEC61A1 |

> **ADTKD vs ADPKD**: ADTKD = bland urine, no massive cysts, normal kidneys; ADPKD = massive cysts, haematuria/proteinuria, enlarged kidneys.

---

## Management

| Aspect | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| **BP control** | ACEi/ARB (renoprotective) |
| **Hyperuricaemia/Gout (UMOD/HNF1B)** | Allopurinol/febuxostat (target urate <360 μmol/L) |
| **Anaemia (REN)** | ESA if symptomatic; avoid NSAIDs |
| **Diabetes (HNF1B)** | Insulin early (not insulin-resistant) |
| **Genital (HNF1B)** | Gynaecology referral (Müllerian anomalies) |
| **CKD progression** | SGLT2i (DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY), standard CKD care |
| **Transplant** | Excellent outcomes; **recurrence rare** (genetic, not in donor kidney) |
| **Genetic counselling** | 50% transmission; prenatal/preimplantation available |

## High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **ADTKD** = bland urine + CKD + autosomal dominant + normal kidneys.
> - **UMOD** = **gout** (early) + hyperuricaemia; **MUC1** = **no gout**.
> - **REN** = childhood **anaemia + hypotension + hyperkalaemia**.
> - **HNF1B** = **MODY5 diabetes + renal cysts + genital anomalies** (Müllerian aplasia).
> - **No massive cysts** = distinguishes from ADPKD.
> - **Genetic testing** confirms; transplant curative (no recurrence).
> - **Former names**: MCKD (medullary cystic kidney disease), FJHN (familial juvenile hyperuricaemic nephropathy) = now ADTKD-UMOD/MUC1.

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **ADTKD = ADPKD** | ADTKD: bland urine, normal kidneys, no massive cysts |
| **All ADTKD have gout** | **Only UMOD & HNF1B** have gout; **MUC1 = no gout** |
| **REN = hypertension** | **REN = HYPOTENSION** (low renin → low Ang II) |
| **HNF1B = only diabetes** | **Multi-system**: renal cysts, genital (Müllerian), liver, Mg wasting |
| **Medullary cysts on US = ADTKD** | Medullary cysts may be absent; **cortical cysts in HNF1B** |
| **ADTKD recurs in transplant** | **No recurrence** (genetic defect in native tissue, not donor) |
| **All gout + CKD = UMOD** | Also HNF1B, classic gout, Lesch-Nyhan, tumour lysis |
| **Bland urine = no kidney disease** | ADTKD = **bland urine BUT progressive CKD** |

## Mnemonics
- **ADTKD genes**: **U**MOD = **U**ric acid (gout); **M**UC1 = **M**inus gout; **R**EN = **R**ed cells low (anaemia); **H**NF1B = **H**yperglycaemia (MODY) + **H**ysterectomy (Müllerian); **S**EC61 = **S**low development
- **ADTKD vs ADPKD**: **A**DTKD = **A**lmost **N**ormal kidneys, **B**land urine; **A**DPKD = **A**ll **C**ysts, **H**aematuria
- **UMOD gout**: **U**MOD = **U**rate **M**etabolism **O**verload → **D**istal **K**idney **D**efect

## Summary
**ADTKD & Other Inherited Tubulointerstitial Diseases** is a **Must Know 🔴** topic.  
**Definition**: Autosomal dominant, bland urine, progressive CKD, normal/small kidneys (no massive cysts).  
**Subtypes**: **UMOD** = gout/hyperuricaemia (FJHN); **MUC1** = no gout (MCKD1); **REN** = anaemia/hypotension/hyperK; **HNF1B** = MODY5 + renal cysts + genital anomalies; **SEC61A1** = CKD + neuro delay.  
**Diagnosis**: Bland urine (key), US (normal kidneys ± cortical cysts), genetic panel.  
**Management**: ACEi/ARB, allopurinol (UMOD/HNF1B), ESA (REN), insulin (HNF1B), transplant curative.  
**Vs ADPKD**: ADTKD = bland urine, normal kidneys; ADPKD = massive cysts, haematuria.  
**Exam focus**: Bland urine clue, UMOD vs MUC1 (gout), REN triad, HNF1B multi-system, transplant no recurrence.
