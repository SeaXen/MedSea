# Testicular Torsion

<callout icon="🩺" color="red_bg">

**Related:** [[Male Reproductive Tract Disorders (BPH, Prostate Cancer, Testicular Cancer)]], [[Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)]], [[Scrotal Pain]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **Testicular torsion = twisting of spermatic cord → venous occlusion → arterial occlusion → testicular ischaemia. Urological EMERGENCY. Time is testis: salvage rate ~90% at <6hr, ~50% at 6–12hr, <10% at >24hr. Bell-clapper deformity (absent gubernaculum attachment) = anatomical predisposition. Diagnosis: clinical (sudden pain, high-riding testis, absent cremasteric reflex) → immediate surgical exploration. Doppler US if equivocal. Detorsion + orchiopexy (bilateral).**

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Recognise classic presentation (sudden scrotal pain, nausea, high-riding testis, absent cremasteric reflex)
- Apply time-critical management (exploration within 6 hours)
- Differentiate from torsion of appendix testis, epididymo-orchitis, incarcerated hernia
- Apply Doppler US criteria (absent flow = torsion)
- Perform surgical detorsion + bilateral orchiopexy
- Counsel on salvage rates and fertility implications

---

## 2. Pathophysiology
| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Mechanism** | Spermatic cord rotation → **venous occlusion** → oedema → **arterial occlusion** → ischaemia → infarction |
| **Anatomical predisposition** | **Bell-clapper deformity** — tunica vaginalis inserts high on testis → testis hangs free → can rotate |
| **Degree** | 180° (incomplete), 360° (complete), 720° (multiple) — >360° = complete arterial occlusion |
| **Age peaks** | **Neonatal** (prenatal/perinatal) & **Adolescent (12–18yr)** — bimodal |

---

## 3. Clinical Presentation

### Classic (Adolescent)
| Feature | Typical |
|---------|---------|
| **Sudden severe scrotal pain** | Often wakes from sleep; may radiate to groin/abdomen |
| **Nausea / Vomiting** | 50–70% (visceral referral) |
| **High-riding testis** | Testis pulled up by shortened cord |
| **Horizontal lie** | Testis rotates to transverse position |
| **Absent cremasteric reflex** | **Most sensitive sign** (ipsilateral) |
| **Swollen, tender testis** | Entire testis tender (vs epididymitis = epididymis only) |
| **No relief with elevation** | Negative Prehn's sign |

### Neonatal
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Prenatal/Perinatal** | Often irreversible at birth (dark, firm testis) |
| **Presentation** | Scrotal discolouration (blue/black), firm mass, absent cremasteric |
| **Contralateral** | High risk of asynchronous torsion — **bilateral fixation needed** |

---

## 4. Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Pain Onset | Key Features | Doppler |
|-----------|------------|--------------|---------|
| **Testicular Torsion** | **Sudden, severe** | High-riding, horizontal, absent cremasteric, no Prehn's | **Absent flow** |
| **Torsion of Appendix Testis** | Sudden | **Blue dot sign** (tender nodule at upper pole), normal testis lie | Normal testicular flow |
| **Epididymo-orchitis** | **Gradual** (hours–days) | Fever, dysuria, pyuria, tender epididymis ± testis, **Prehn's sign +** (relief with elevation) | **Increased flow** |
| **Incarcerated Inguinal Hernia** | Sudden | Groin mass, irreducible, bowel obstruction signs | N/A |
| **Idiopathic Scrotal Oedema** | Acute | Painless, bilateral scrotal wall oedema, normal testes | Normal |

---

## 5. Diagnostic Workup

### Clinical Diagnosis (Time-Critical)
> **If clinical suspicion high → IMMEDIATE SURGICAL EXPLORATION. Do NOT delay for imaging.**

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Classic presentation** (sudden pain, absent cremasteric, high-riding) | **Straight to OR** |
| **Equivocal / Atypical** | **Doppler US stat** (if available within 30–60 min) |

### Doppler Ultrasound (If Needed)
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| **Absent intratesticular flow** | **Torsion** (sensitivity 85–95%, specificity 95%) |
| **Reduced flow** | Intermittent/partial torsion |
| **Normal/Increased flow** | Epididymo-orchitis, appendix testis torsion |
| **"Whirlpool sign"** | Twisted spermatic cord (specific) |

> **False negatives**: Intermittent torsion, early torsion with partial flow.

---

## 6. Management — **TIME IS TESTIS**

### Salvage Rate by Time
| Time to Detorsion | Salvage Rate |
|-------------------|--------------|
| **<6 hours** | **90–100%** |
| **6–12 hours** | **50–60%** |
| **12–24 hours** | **10–20%** |
| **>24 hours** | **<10%** (orchiectomy likely) |

> **Every hour counts.** 6-hour golden window.

---

### Surgical Exploration (Standard)
| Step | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Incision** | Transverse scrotal or inguinal (if equivocal) |
| **Detorsion** | Rotate testis **counterclockwise** (medial to lateral) — usually 2–3 full turns |
| **Viability assessment** | Wait 10–15 min: colour change (pink), bleeding from tunica albuginea |
| **Orchiopexy** | **Bilateral** (non-absorbable sutures, 3-point fixation) — prevents recurrence |
| **Orchiectomy** | If non-viable (black, no bleeding after detorsion) — place prosthesis if desired |

> **Always fix contralateral testis** — bell-clapper deformity is bilateral.

---

### Neonatal Torsion
| Scenario | Management |
|----------|------------|
| **Prenatal (in utero)** | Usually non-salvageable; observe contralateral |
| **Perinatal (at birth)** | **Urgent exploration** if viable; contralateral fixation mandatory |
| **Asynchronous torsion** | Contralateral fixation at same time |

---

## 7. Post-Operative Care
| Aspect | Protocol |
|--------|----------|
| **Pain control** | Regular analgesia (NSAIDs + opioids PRN) |
| **Activity** | Scrotal support, avoid strenuous activity 2–4 weeks |
| **Follow-up** | Clinical exam at 2 weeks, 3 months (atrophy check) |
| **Fertility counselling** | Unilateral torsion → usually preserved fertility; semen analysis at 6–12mo if concerned |

---

## 8. Complications
| Complication | Prevention/Management |
|--------------|----------------------|
| **Testicular atrophy** | Early exploration (<6hr); assess size at follow-up |
| **Infertility** | Autoimmune anti-sperm antibodies possible; semen analysis |
| **Cosmetic/psychological** | Testicular prosthesis if orchiectomy |
| **Recurrence** | Bilateral fixation prevents |

## 9. High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **Testicular torsion** = urological emergency — **time is testis**.
> - **Classic**: Sudden scrotal pain, nausea, **high-riding testis**, **horizontal lie**, **absent cremasteric reflex**.
> - **Bell-clapper deformity** (absent gubernaculum) = bilateral predisposition.
> - **Salvage**: <6hr = 90%, 6–12hr = 50%, >24hr = <10%.
> - **Diagnosis**: Clinical → **immediate OR**; Doppler US only if equivocal (absent flow = torsion).
> - **Management**: Surgical detorsion (counterclockwise) + **bilateral orchiopexy**.
> - **Orchiectomy** if non-viable (black, no bleeding after 15min).
> - **Differential**: Appendix testis torsion (blue dot), epididymo-orchitis (gradual, fever, Prehn's +), incarcerated hernia.
> - **Neonatal**: Often prenatal/perinatal; contralateral fixation mandatory.

## 10. Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **Order Doppler first** | **High suspicion → OR immediately**; Doppler only if equivocal |
| **Pain relief with elevation = torsion** | **Prehn's sign NEGATIVE in torsion** (pain NOT relieved); +ve in epididymitis |
| **Cremasteric reflex present = no torsion** | **Absent cremasteric = torsion** (but can be absent in other conditions) |
| **Blue dot = torsion** | **Blue dot = appendix testis torsion**; whole testis not tender |
| **Unilateral fixation enough** | **Bilateral orchiopexy mandatory** — bell-clapper is bilateral |
| **Neonatal = wait and see** | **Urgent exploration if viable**; high asynchronous torsion risk |
| **Doppler rules out torsion** | **False negatives** in intermittent/early torsion |
| **Torsion = always 360°** | Can be 180° (incomplete), 720° (multiple) |
| **Orchiectomy always needed if >24hr** | **Assess viability intraoperatively** — some recover even late |
| **Fertility always lost** | Unilateral torsion → usually normal fertility; semen analysis if concerned |

## 20. Summary
**Testicular Torsion** is a **Must Know 🔴** urological emergency.  
**Pathophysiology**: Spermatic cord twist → venous → arterial occlusion → ischaemia. **Bell-clapper deformity** (absent gubernaculum) = bilateral predisposition.  
**Clinical**: Sudden severe scrotal pain, nausea, **high-riding testis**, **horizontal lie**, **absent cremasteric reflex**, **negative Prehn's sign**.  
**Time-critical**: **<6hr = 90% salvage**; 6–12hr = 50–60%; >24hr = <10%.  
**Diagnosis**: **Clinical → immediate OR**. Doppler US only if equivocal (absent flow = torsion).  
**Management**: Surgical exploration → detorsion (counterclockwise) → viability assessment → **bilateral orchiopexy**. Orchiectomy if non-viable.  
**Differential**: Appendix testis torsion (blue dot, localised), Epididymo-orchitis (gradual, fever, Prehn's +, pyuria), Incarcerated hernia.  
**Neonatal**: Prenatal often non-salvageable; perinatal viable → urgent exploration + contralateral fixation.  
**Exam focus**: Time window, absent cremasteric, bell-clapper bilateral, detorsion counterclockwise, bilateral fixation, blue dot differential, Prehn's sign negative.
