NEPHROLOGY & UROLOGY
Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)
1 Learning Objectives
  • Define AKI using KDIGO criteria
  • Classify AKI aetiology (pre-renal, intrinsic, post-renal)
  • Recognise clinical presentations and diagnostic approach
  • Apply management principles (fluid resuscitation, nephrotoxin avoidance, RRT indications)
  • Manage AKI complications (hyperkalaemia, acidosis, fluid overload)
2 KDIGO Definition & Staging
StageCreatinine CriteriaUrine Output Criteria
--------------------------------------------------
**Stage 1**↑Cr ≥26.5 µmol/L in 48h **OR** ↑Cr 1.5–1.9× baseline**UO <0.5 mL/kg/h × 6–12h**
**Stage 2**↑Cr 2.0–2.9× baseline**UO <0.5 mL/kg/h × ≥12h**
**Stage 3**↑Cr ≥3.0× baseline **OR** ↑Cr ≥354 µmol/L **OR** RRT initiated**UO <0.3 mL/kg/h × 24h OR Anuria × 12h**
[!key] AKI = acute rise in Cr OR fall in UO. Stage by worst creatinine OR urine output criterion.
3 Aetiology Classification
CategoryExamplesKey Clues
-------------------------------
**Pre-renal** (↓ renal perfusion)Hypovolaemia (haemorrhage, dehydration, burns), ↓ CO (heart failure, sepsis), renal vasoconstriction (NSAIDs, ACEi/ARB, hepatorenal), renal artery stenosisBUN:Cr >20, FeNa <1%, Uosm >500, Uosm > plasma, FeUrea <35%
**Intrinsic Renal****ATN** (ischaemia, nephrotoxins: aminoglycosides, contrast, cisplatin, myoglobin, HUS/TTP)FeNa >2% (ATN), muddy brown casts, FeNa <1% (contrast, sepsis, rhabdo, glomerulonephritis)
**Glomerulonephritis** (RPGN, IgA, anti-GBM, lupus, post-strep)Haematuria, RBC casts, proteinuria, hypertension, oedema
**Acute Interstitial Nephritis (AIN)**Drug-induced (NSAIDs, antibiotics, PPI), fever, rash, eosinophilia, WBC casts, eosinophiluria
**Vascular** (renal artery thrombosis, atheroemboli, TMA/HUS/TTP, malignant HTN)Flank pain, livedo reticularis, schistocytes, hypertension
**Post-renal (Obstruction)**Stones, BPH, tumours, strictures, retroperitoneal fibrosis, neurogenic bladderHydronephrosis on US, palpable bladder, anuria/fluctuating output
4 Clinical Assessment
History
DomainKey Questions
-----------------------
**Onset**Acute vs chronic progression
**Urine Output**Oliguria (<400 mL/d), anuria (<100 mL/d), polyuria (recovery phase)
**Symptoms**Nausea, vomiting, confusion (uraemia), dyspnoea (fluid overload), flank pain (obstruction)
**Drug History****Nephrotoxins**: NSAIDs, ACEi/ARB, aminoglycosides, contrast, cisplatin, PPI, diuretics
**Medical History**Heart failure, liver disease, sepsis, diabetes, hypertension, malignancy
**Exposures**Contrast (recent CT), antibiotics (aminoglycosides), chemotherapy
Examination
SystemKey Findings
----------------------
**Volume Status**JVP, skin turgor, mucous membranes, peripheral oedema, JVP
**Vitals**BP (hypotension = pre-renal; hypertension = intrinsic/GN), HR, temp
**Abdomen**Palpable bladder (retention), flank tenderness (pyelo, colic), renal masses
**Skin**Rash (AIN, vasculitis), purpura (vasculitis/TTP), urticaria (AIN)
**Neuro**Asterixis (uraemic encephalopathy), confusion
5 Diagnostic Approach
Blood Tests
TestPurpose
----------------
**U&E, Cr, eGFR**Baseline, staging
**VBG/ABG**Acid-base, lactate, K+
**FBC, CRP**Infection, inflammation, eosinophilia (AIN)
**Ca, PO4, Mg, Urate**Tumour lysis, rhabdo, AKI complications
**CK**Rhabdomyolysis
**Uric Acid**Tumour lysis, rhabdo
**Coagulation**DIC, TTP/HUS
**Blood Cultures**Sepsis
**Autoantibodies**ANA, ANCA, anti-GBM, complement (if GN suspected)
Urine Tests
TestInterpretation
-----------------------
**Urine Dipstick**Protein, blood, leucocytes, nitrites
**Microscopy**RBC casts = GN; WBC casts = pyelo/AIN; muddy brown = ATN; eosinophiluria = AIN
**FeNa / FeUrea****FeNa <1% = pre-renal**; **FeNa >2% = ATN** (unreliable on diuretics); FeUrea <35% = pre-renal
FormulaCalculation
----------------------
**FeNa**(U_Na × P_Cr) / (P_Na × U_Cr) × 100%
**FeUrea**(U_Urea × P_Cr) / (P_Urea × U_Cr) × 100%
Imaging
ModalityIndication
----------------------
**US KUB**First-line; hydronephrosis, kidney size, cysts, stones, bladder volume
**CT KUB**Stones (gold standard)
**CT Urogram**Haematuria, masses, complex obstruction
**Bladder Scan**Post-void residual (retention)
6 Management Principles
1. Resuscitation (First 6 Hours)
StepAction
--------------
**A**Airway, high-flow O2 if hypoxic
**B**Assess breathing, intubate if needed
**C****Fluid resuscitation**: 500 mL crystalloid bolus, reassess; target MAP >65
**D**Neurological assessment (GCS, asterixis)
**E**Exposure, monitor temperature
2. Identify & Treat Cause
CategoryAction
------------------
**Pre-renal**Fluid resuscitation (crystalloid 500 mL boluses), stop diuretics/ACEi/ARB/NSAIDs
**ATN**Stop nephrotoxins (NSAIDs, ACEi/ARB, aminoglycosides, contrast); maintain euvolaemia
**GN**Immunosuppression (steroids ± cyclophosphamide/rituximab) per histology
**AIN**Stop offending drug; steroids if severe (pred 1 mg/kg)
**Obstruction****Urgent decompression**: Foley catheter, nephrostomy, stent
**Vascular**Anticoagulation (renal vein thrombosis), plasma exchange (TTP/HUS), BP control
3. Supportive Management
ComplicationManagement
--------------------------
**Hyperkalaemia****>6.5 or ECG changes**: Ca gluconate 10 mL 10%, insulin/dextrose, salbutamol, kayexalate, dialysis
**Metabolic Acidosis**HCO3- <15 or pH <7.2: IV bicarbonate (target pH >7.2)
**Fluid Overload**Diuretics (furosemide IV), fluid restriction, dialysis if refractory
**Uraemic Complications**Pericarditis, encephalopathy, bleeding (DDAVP, platelets), neuropathy
4. Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT) Indications
IndicationAEIOU Mnemonic
----------------------------
**A****Acidosis** (pH <7.2, HCO3 <12)
**E****Electrolytes** (K+ >6.5, refractory)
**I****Ingestion** (toxic alcohols, lithium, aspirin, metformin)
**O****Overload** (pulmonary oedema refractory to diuretics)
**U****Uraemia** (pericarditis, encephalopathy, neuropathy, bleeding)
ModalityIndication
----------------------
**IHD**Haemodynamically stable, hyperkalaemia, overdose
**CVVH/CVVHDF**Haemodynamically unstable, fluid overload, sepsis
**PD**Haemodynamically stable, no vascular access, resource-limited
7 Special AKI Syndromes
SyndromeKey FeaturesManagement
-----------------------------------
**Contrast-Induced AKI**↑Cr 48–72h post-contrast; risk: CKD, DM, HF, age >70, volume depletionHydration (IV 0.9% saline 1 mL/kg/h 12h pre/post); NAC controversial; avoid NSAIDs
**Rhabdomyolysis**CK >5000 (or >5× ULN), myoglobinuria, tea-coloured urine, hyperkalaemia, hyperphosphataemiaAggressive IV fluids (target UO 200–300 mL/h), alkalinisation (target urine pH >6.5), avoid myoglobin precipitation
**Tumour Lysis Syndrome**TLS: K+, PO4, Uric acid ↑, Ca ↓; Cairo-Bishop criteriaHydration, allopurinol/rasburicase, monitor K+/PO4/Ca, dialysis if severe
**Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS)**Cirrhosis + ascites + renal failure (no other cause); HRS-1 (rapid), HRS-2 (slow)Terlipressin + albumin; TIPS; liver transplant evaluation
**Post-Obstructive Diuresis**Polyuria after relief of obstructionReplace urine output mL-for-mL with 0.45% saline + KCl; monitor electrolytes
8 High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points
[!important] - KDIGO AKI: Cr ↑26.5 in 48h OR 1.5× baseline in 7d OR UO <0.5 mL/kg/h × 6h - Staging: Stage 1 (1.5–1.9× Cr), Stage 2 (2–2.9×), Stage 3 (3×, RRT, Cr >354) - Pre-renal: FeNa <1%, FeUrea <35% - ATN: FeNa >2%, muddy brown casts - AIN: Fever, rash, eosinophilia, WBC casts, eosinophiluria - Obstruction: Hydronephrosis on US; relieve ASAP (catheter, nephrostomy, stent) - AEIOU for RRT: Acidosis, Electrolytes, Ingestion, Overload, Uraemia - Contrast nephropathy: Hydration, stop nephrotoxins, NAC controversial - Rhabdo: CK >5000, tea urine, aggressive fluids + alkalinisation - TLS: K+, PO4, UA up; Ca down; rasburicase, hydration
9 Common Confusions / Exam Traps
TrapCorrection
------------------
**AKI = only Cr rise**Also UO criteria (KDIGO)
**FeNa <1% = always pre-renal**Invalid on diuretics; use FeUrea (<35%)
**FeNa >2% = always ATN**Also in GN, obstruction, sepsis, contrast; use FeUrea
**Oliguria = AKI**Oliguria = <400 mL/d; AKI needs KDIGO criteria
**AKI = always reversible**Can progress to CKD/ESRD if severe/prolonged
**Contrast nephropathy = immediate**Peaks at 48–72h post-contrast
**Urine output = only AKI marker**AKI = Cr rise OR UO decline
**All AKI needs dialysis**Only AEIOU indications
10 Mnemonics
  • AKI Definition: Cr ↑26.5 in 48h OR 1.5× baseline in 7d OR UO <0.5 × 6h = C-Or-U
  • AKI Stages: 1 = 1.5–1.9×; 2 = 2–2.9×; 3 = 3× or RRT = 123
  • AEIOU RRT: Acidosis, Electrolytes (K+), Ingestion, Overload, Uraemia = AEIOU
  • Pre-renal vs ATN: FeNa <1% = Pre, >2% = ATN = FeNa 1 = Pre, 2 = ATN
  • AIN triad: Fever, Rash, Eosinophilia = FRE
  • Obstruction relief: Foley, Nephrostomy, Stent = FNS
  • AKI causes: Pre-renal, Intrinsic, Post-renal = PIP

19 Summary

Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) is a Must Know 🔴 topic. Key takeaway: KDIGO = Cr ↑26.5/48h OR 1.5× baseline OR UO <0.5 mL/kg/h × 6h. Staging: 1.5–1.9× (1), 2–2.9× (2), ≥3×/RRT/Cr>354 (3). Pre-renal: FeNa <1%, FeUrea <35%; ATN: FeNa >2%, muddy brown casts. AIN: fever/rash/eosinophilia/WBC casts. AEIOU for RRT. Contrast nephropathy peaks 48–72h; prevent with IV fluids. Rhabdo: CK>5000, fluids + alkalinisation. Obstruction: US → decompress (Foley/nephrostomy/stent). Exam focus: KDIGO criteria/staging, FeNa/FeUrea interpretation, cast identification, AEIOU, contrast nephropathy, rhabdo/TLS management, obstruction relief. Clinical relevance: Early recognition, timely intervention, RRT indications, prevention strategies.
