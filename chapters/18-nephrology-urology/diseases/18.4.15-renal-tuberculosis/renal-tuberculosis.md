> [!important]
> **Renal TB = haematogenous spread of Mycobacterium tuberculosis to kidney (usually from pulmonary focus). "Putty kidney" = end-stage calcified caseous necrosis. Sterile pyuria is hallmark. Diagnosis: urine AFB/culture/PCR, imaging. Treatment: Standard HRZE × 2mo → HR × 4–7mo (total 6–9mo). May need nephrectomy for non-functioning kidney.**

## 2. Epidemiology & Pathogenesis
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Route** | Haematogenous (from pulmonary TB focus — often silent/old) |
| **Incidence** | ~5–10% of TB patients have renal involvement; 30% of extrapulmonary TB |
| **Age** | Young adults (20–40yr); reactivation in elderly/immunocompromised |
| **HIV co-infection** | ↑ risk, atypical presentation, disseminated |
| **Pathology** | Caseating granulomas → papillary necrosis → strictures → calcified "putty kidney" |

---

## 3. Clinical Presentation
| Symptom/Sign | Frequency | Notes |
|--------------|-----------|-------|
| **Sterile pyuria** | **90–95%** | **Hallmark** — WBC in urine, no growth on routine culture |
| **Flank pain** | 50–70% | Dull, persistent |
| **Frequency/Dysuria** | 40–60% | Bladder involvement (TB cystitis) |
| **Haematuria** | 30–40% | Microscopic > macroscopic |
| **Constitutional** | Variable | Fever, night sweats, weight loss (may be absent) |
| **Palpable kidney** | Rare | Enlarged early; small/contracted late |

> **Sterile pyuria + flank pain + constitutional symptoms** = Think Renal TB.

---

## 4. Differential: Sterile Pyuria
| Cause | Key Differentiator |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Renal TB** | AFB culture/PCR+, radiographic calcifications, strictures |
| **AIN** | Drug history, eosinophiluria, normal imaging |
| **Stones** | Colic, CT shows calculi |
| **Vasculitis** | ANCA+, systemic features, RPGN |
| **Prostatitis** | Expressed prostatic secretions WBC |
| **Urethritis** | Gonorrhoea/Chlamydia PCR, sexual history |
| **Bladder cancer** | Cystoscopy + biopsy, older age |
| **Interstitial nephritis (Sjögren's, etc.)** | Autoimmune markers, tubular dysfunction |
| **Partial UTI treatment** | Recent antibiotics, repeat culture |

## 5. Diagnostic Workup

### Urine Studies
| Test | Sensitivity/Specificity | Role |
|------|------------------------|------|
| **Urine AFB smear (ZN stain)** | Low sensitivity (~20%) | Rapid, screen |
| **Urine AFB culture (LJ/MGIT)** | **Gold standard** (80–90%) | Definitive; 4–8 weeks (MGIT 2–3 weeks) |
| **Urine PCR (Xpert MTB/RIF Ultra)** | **High (>90%)** | Rapid (hours); detects rifampicin resistance |
| **3 Early morning specimens** | Increases yield | Standard recommendation |

### Imaging
| Modality | Findings |
|----------|----------|
| **IVU / CTU** | **Calyceal distortion**, strictures, "moth-eaten" calyces, papillary necrosis, **calcifications** ("putty kidney") |
| **Ultrasound** | Hydronephrosis, calcifications, small kidney |
| **CT** | Parenchymal calcifications, strictures, lymphadenopathy |

### Cystoscopy
| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Ulcerative cystitis** (hunters' ulcers) | Bladder TB |
| **Strictures** (ureteric, urethral) | Fibrotic sequelae |
| **Biopsy** | Caseating granulomas, AFB |

---

## 6. Treatment (Standard Anti-TB Regimen)

### Drug-Sensitive TB
| Phase | Drugs | Duration | Notes |
|-------|-------|----------|-------|
| **Intensive** | **HRZE** (Isoniazid, Rifampicin, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol) | **2 months** | Pyridoxine 10–25mg/day with INH |
| **Continuation** | **HR** (Isoniazid, Rifampicin) | **4–7 months** | **Total 6–9 months** (renal TB often needs 9mo) |

> **Renal TB**: Extend continuation to 7 months (total 9 months) due to poor drug penetration in caseous lesions.

### Drug Dosing (Renal Impairment)
| Drug | Normal Dose | eGFR <30 Adjustment |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Isoniazid (H)** | 5 mg/kg (max 300mg) | No adjustment (hepatic metabolism) |
| **Rifampicin (R)** | 10 mg/kg (max 600mg) | No adjustment |
| **Pyrazinamide (Z)** | 25 mg/kg (max 2g) | **Reduce frequency** (25 mg/kg 3x/week) |
| **Ethambutol (E)** | 15 mg/kg (max 1.2g) | **Avoid if <15** (dose reduce 15mg/kg 3x/week) |

> **Ethambutol**: Monitor visual acuity/colour vision monthly.

### MDR-TB Renal
- Individualised regimen per DST (bedaquiline, linezolid, levofloxacin, clofazimine, cycloserine)
- Minimum 18–24 months total
- Expert consultation mandatory

---

## 7. Complications & Sequelae
| Complication | Management |
|--------------|------------|
| **Ureteric strictures** | Endoscopic dilatation, stenting, ureteric reimplantation |
| **Bladder fibrosis/contracture** | Augmentation cystoplasty |
| **Non-functioning kidney** | Nephrectomy (if hypertension, recurrent infection, pain) |
| **Amyloidosis (AA)** | Chronic inflammation → renal AA amyloidosis |
| **Renal failure** | Dialysis/transplant; TB prophylaxis if latent |

> **Nephrectomy**: Only if kidney non-functioning (<10% function) AND causing symptoms (pain, infection, hypertension).

---

## 8. Renal TB in Special Populations

### HIV Co-infection
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Presentation** | More disseminated, atypical (may lack sterile pyuria) |
| **Diagnosis** | Xpert MTB/RIF Ultra preferred (higher sensitivity in HIV) |
| **ART interaction** | Rifampicin ↓ PI/NNRTI levels → use rifabutin or adjust ART |
| **IRIS** | Immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome on starting ART |

### Transplant Recipients
- Reactivation risk on immunosuppression
- Prophylaxis (isoniazid 300mg/day × 6–9mo) if latent TB (IGRA/TST+)

## 9. High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **Renal TB** = haematogenous spread from pulmonary focus.
> - **Sterile pyuria** = hallmark (90–95%).
> - **Diagnosis**: Urine AFB culture (gold standard) / Xpert MTB/RIF Ultra (rapid).
> - **Imaging**: IVU/CTU → calyceal distortion, strictures, calcifications ("putty kidney").
> - **Treatment**: HRZE × 2mo → HR × 7mo (**total 9 months** for renal TB).
> - **Ethambutol** dose adjust in CKD; monitor vision.
> - **Complications**: Ureteric strictures, bladder fibrosis, non-functioning kidney → nephrectomy.
> - **HIV**: ↑ disseminated, rifampicin-ART interactions (use rifabutin), IRIS risk.
> - **Differential sterile pyuria**: TB, AIN, stones, vasculitis, bladder cancer, prostatitis.

## 10. Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **Sterile pyuria = TB** | Also AIN, stones, vasculitis, bladder cancer, prostatitis, partially treated UTI |
| **Urine AFB smear = diagnostic** | Sensitivity only ~20%; need **culture/PCR** |
| **6 months treatment enough** | **Renal TB needs 9 months** (2HRZE + 7HR) |
| **Ethambutol safe in all CKD** | **Avoid if eGFR <15**; reduce frequency if <30 |
| **Nephrectomy for all TB kidneys** | Only if **non-functioning (<10%) + symptomatic** |
| **TB only in active pulmonary** | Often **old/healed pulmonary focus** (silent) |
| **HIV TB = typical presentation** | Often **atypical, disseminated, smear-negative** |
| **Rifampicin with all ART** | Rifampicin ↓ PI/NNRTI → **switch to rifabutin** or adjust ART |

## 19. Answer Key with Explanations

### MCQs
1. **B** — Sterile pyuria (WBC in urine, no growth on routine culture) is the hallmark of renal TB.
2. **B** — Urine AFB culture (Löwenstein-Jensen or MGIT) = gold standard. Xpert PCR rapid but culture definitive.
3. **B** — Renal TB: 2HRZE + 7HR = 9 months total (longer than pulmonary 6mo due to poor drug penetration in caseous lesions).
4. **B** — "Putty kidney" = calcified caseous necrotic renal parenchyma (end-stage).
5. **B** — Ethambutol: avoid if eGFR <15; reduce to 15mg/kg 3x/week if eGFR 15–30.
6. **B** — Ethambutol toxicity = optic neuritis → monitor visual acuity & colour vision monthly.
7. **B** — Rifampicin potent CYP3A4 inducer → ↓ PI/NNRTI levels. Use rifabutin (less induction) with dose-adjusted ART.
8. **B** — Nephrectomy only if kidney non-functioning (<10% split function) AND symptomatic (recurrent infection, pain, hypertension).
9. **B** — Xpert MTB/RIF Ultra = rapid PCR (hours), detects M. tuberculosis + rifampicin resistance (rpoB mutations).
10. **D** — Sterile pyuria = no growth on routine culture. Untreated UTI grows organisms → not "sterile".

### SBAs
1. **B** — High suspicion + sterile pyuria + IVU findings = start empiric HRZE immediately; don't wait 4–8 weeks for culture.
2. **B** — Ethambutol optic neuropathy: stop E, continue HRZ; vision often recovers if caught early.
3. **B** — Rifampicin with efavirenz: standard is rifabutin + efavirenz 800mg (or dolutegravir 50mg BD without rifampicin interaction).
4. **B** — Non-functioning kidney (<10%) + recurrent UTIs = nephrectomy indicated after adequate anti-TB.
5. **B** — Ureteric strictures: urgent drainage (stent/nephrostomy) + continue anti-TB; surgery after treatment.
6. **B** — Transplant + latent TB (IGRA+): INH 300mg + pyridoxine × 6–9 months prophylaxis.
7. **B** — Drug-induced hepatitis: stop hepatotoxic drugs (H, Z); continue R, E; reintroduce H then Z sequentially.
8. **B** — MDR-TB: individualised regimen per DST with ≥4 effective drugs (bedaquiline, linezolid, fluoroquinolone, clofazimine, cycloserine).
9. **B** — IRIS: paradoxical worsening 2–8 weeks after ART start in advanced HIV; treat with steroids if severe, continue ART + anti-TB.
10. **B** — Asymptomatic non-functioning kidney on treatment: observe; nephrectomy only if complications develop.

## 20. Summary
**Renal Tuberculosis** is a **Must Know 🔴** infectious/urology topic.  
**Pathogenesis**: Haematogenous spread from pulmonary TB (often silent/old focus).  
**Clinical**: **Sterile pyuria (90–95%)**, flank pain, frequency/dysuria, constitutional symptoms.  
**Diagnosis**: **Urine AFB culture (gold standard)**, **Xpert MTB/RIF Ultra (rapid + RIF resistance)**, **IVU/CTU** (calyceal distortion, strictures, **"putty kidney"** calcifications), cystoscopy (ulcerative cystitis).  
**Treatment**: **2HRZE + 7HR = 9 months total**. Pyridoxine with INH. Ethambutol dose adjust in CKD + monthly vision monitoring.  
**Complications**: Ureteric strictures, bladder fibrosis/contracture, non-functioning kidney → **nephrectomy if <10% function + symptomatic**.  
**HIV**: Atypical disseminated; **rifabutin for ART interactions**; IRIS risk on starting ART.  
**Exam focus**: Sterile pyuria, 9-month regimen, putty kidney, ethambutol vision monitoring, HIV ART interactions, nephrectomy criteria.
