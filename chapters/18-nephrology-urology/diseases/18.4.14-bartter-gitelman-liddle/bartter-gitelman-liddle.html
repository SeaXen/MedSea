Bartter and Gitelman Syndromes (plus Liddle)
1 Learning Objectives
  • Differentiate Bartter vs Gitelman vs Liddle by site, labs, and clinical features
  • Apply genetic classification (Bartter Types 1–5, Gitelman, Liddle)
  • Diagnose using urine electrolytes, renin/aldosterone, genetic testing
  • Manage each syndrome (NSAIDs/K+ for Bartter, Mg/K+ for Gitelman, amiloride for Liddle)

All Bartter types = TAL salt wasting → ↑ prostaglandin E2 → polyuria, polyhydramnios.
Pathophysiology (TAL Defect)
  • TAL = Na⁺-K⁺-2Cl⁻ cotransporter (NKCC2) + ROMK + ClC-Kb/Barttin
  • Defect → ↓ NaCl reabsorption → salt wasting → volume depletion → ↑ renin/aldo
  • Consequences: Polyuria (↓ medullary gradient), hypokalaemia, metabolic alkalosis, hypercalciuria → nephrocalcinosis

3 Gitelman Syndrome
Genetics
  • SLC12A3 (chr16q13) → NCC (Na-Cl cotransporter) in DCT
  • Autosomal recessive
  • Prevalence ~1:40,000 (higher in East Asia)
Pathophysiology (DCT Defect)
  • NCC loss-of-function → ↓ NaCl reabsorption in DCT → mild volume depletion → ↑ renin/aldo
  • Paradoxical Mg2+ wasting (DCT = major Mg reabsorption site via TRPM6)
  • Hypocalciuria (↑ proximal Ca2+ reabsorption due to volume depletion)
Clinical Presentation

**BP****Normal/Low**
**Growth**Normal (vs Bartter failure to thrive)
**Nephrocalcinosis****Absent** (hypocalciuria)
**Chondrocalcinosis**Adult: CPPD deposition (pseudogout)

4 Liddle Syndrome (Pseudohyperaldosteronism)
Genetics
  • SCNN1B or SCNN1G (chr16p12) → ENaC β/γ subunits gain-of-function
  • Autosomal dominant
  • Constitutive ENaC activation → Na+ reabsorption independent of aldosterone
Pathophysiology
  • ENaC always open → ↑ Na+ reabsorption in CD → volume expansion → hypertension
  • ↓ Renin, ↓ Aldosterone (suppressed)
  • ↑ K+ & H+ secretion → hypokalaemia, metabolic alkalosis

6 High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points
[!important] - Bartter = TAL defect, antenatal polyhydramnios, nephrocalcinosis, indomethacin. - Gitelman = DCT defect, hypomagnesaemia + hypocalciuria, later onset, Mg²⁺ + amiloride. - Liddle = ENaC gain-of-function, hypertension + hypokalaemia + LOW renin/aldo, amiloride (not spiro). - Bartter vs Gitelman: Nephrocalcinosis (Bartter YES, Gitelman NO); Hypomagnesaemia (Bartter NO, Gitelman YES); Hypocalciuria (Bartter NO, Gitelman YES). - Genetics: Bartter Types 1–5 (NKCC2, ROMK, ClC-Kb, Barttin, MAGE-D2); Gitelman = NCC (SLC12A3); Liddle = ENaC (SCNN1B/G). - Urinary Ca2+ is the key discriminator: high in Bartter, low in Gitelman.

8 Mnemonics
  • Bartter B's: Bartter = Baby (antenatal), Big Ca²⁺ (nephrocalcinosis), Bad growth, Block NKCC2/ROMK
  • Gitelman G's: Gitelman = Grown-up (adult), Granny (hypomagnesaemia), Generous Ca²⁺ reabs (hypocalciuria)
  • Liddle L's: Liddle = Low renin/aldo, Lefty (hypertension), Locks ENaC open
  • Site mnemonic: Bartter = Base of loop (TAL); Gitelman = Goes distal (DCT); Liddle = Last segment (CD)
  • Rx: Bartter = Indomethacin; Gitelman = Mg + Amiloride; Liddle = Amiloride

17 Summary

Bartter, Gitelman & Liddle Syndromes are Must Know 🔴 tubulopathy topics. Bartter: TAL defect (NKCC2/ROMK/ClC-Kb/Barttin/MAGED2), AR (Types 1–4), X-linked (Type 5). Antenatal polyhydramnios, FTT, nephrocalcinosis (hypercalciuria), high renin/aldo. Rx: Indomethacin + K+. Gitelman: DCT defect (NCC/SLC12A3), AR. Adolescent/adult, hypomagnesaemia + hypocalciuria (hallmark), NO nephrocalcinosis, high renin/aldo. Rx: Mg²⁺ + Amiloride + K+. Liddle: CD ENaC gain-of-function (SCNN1B/G), AD. Hypertension, hypokalaemia, alkalosis, LOW renin/aldo. Rx: Amiloride (NOT spiro). Key discriminators: Urine Ca²⁺ (Bartter HIGH, Gitelman LOW); BP (Liddle HIGH, others LOW); Mg²⁺ (Gitelman LOW); Renin/aldo (Liddle LOW, others HIGH). Genetics: Bartter 1–5 (NKCC2, ROMK, ClC-Kb, Barttin, MAGED2); Gitelman (NCC); Liddle (ENaC). Exam focus: Site, age, nephrocalcinosis, Mg/Ca urine, BP, renin/aldo, specific Rx.
