Urolithiasis (Renal Stones)
1 Learning Objectives
  • Classify renal stones by composition and aetiology
  • Apply diagnostic workup (imaging, metabolic evaluation)
  • Manage acute renal colic and stone disease
  • Apply preventive strategies (dietary, pharmacological)
  • Apply to FCPS/MRCP clinical scenarios

3 Pathophysiology
FactorMechanism
-------------------
**Supersaturation**[Ion product] > [Solubility product] → crystallisation
**Promoters**High Ca, oxalate, urate, low citrate, low volume, low pH (uric acid), high pH (CaP/struvite)
**Inhibitors****Citrate** (chelates Ca), **Magnesium**, **Pyrophosphate**, **Tamm-Horsfall protein**, **Nephrocalcin**
**Randall's Plaque**Suburothelial CaP deposits → nidus for CaOx stones
4 Clinical Presentation
SyndromeFeatures
--------------------
**Renal Colic****Sudden severe loin → groin pain**, radiating to testis/labia; nausea/vomiting; restlessness (vs peritonitis: still); haematuria (85%)
**Asymptomatic / Incidental**Found on imaging for other reasons
**UTI / Pyelonephritis**Struvite stones, infected obstruction
**Obstructive AKI**Bilateral/solitary kidney stone → anuria, AKI
**Chronic CKD**Large staghorn, bilateral stones, recurrent obstruction

6 Metabolic Evaluation (Indicated If)
IndicationTests
-------------------
**Recurrent stones** (≥2)2× 24h urine: volume, pH, Ca, oxalate, urate, citrate, Na, K, Mg, creatinine
**High-risk** (solitary kidney, transplant, gastric bypass, bowel disease)Blood: Ca, PO4, urate, bicarbonate, PTH, Vitamin D; Stone analysis (FTIR)
**Paediatric / Young onset**Full metabolic workup
**Atypical stone type** (uric acid, cystine, struvite, brushite)Targeted workup
[!key] 24h urine: Volume >2.5L/day target; Ca <7.5mmol/day (M) / <6.2 (F); Oxalate <0.5mmol/day; Citrate >3mmol/day; Urate <3.5mmol/day; pH 6.0–6.5 (uric acid target >6.5).
7 Acute Management (Renal Colic)
StepAction
--------------
**1. Analgesia****NSAID 1st line** (Diclofenac 75mg IM/PR; Ibuprofen 400–600mg PO) — **superior to opioids**, less nausea; Opioid (morphine) if NSAID contraindicated
**2. Antiemetic**Metoclopramide / ondansetron
**3. Hydration****Oral >2.5L/day** (IV if vomiting); **do NOT overhydrate** acutely (↑ intraluminal pressure)
**4. Medical Expulsive Therapy (MET)****Tamsulosin 400mcg OD** (alpha-blocker) for **distal ureteric stones <10mm** — ↑ spontaneous passage, ↓ time, ↓ analgesia
**5. Admission Indicators**Infection (fever, sepsis), solitary kidney, bilateral obstruction, intractable pain/vomiting, AKI, pregnancy
8 Definitive Stone Management
Stone Factor1st LineAlternatives
--------------------------------------
**<10mm, distal ureter****MET (tamsulosin)** + analgesiaESWL, URS
**<10mm, proximal ureter****ESWL**URS
**10–20mm, renal/upper ureter****ESWL** (if favourable: <1000 HU, <10mm, lower pole <30°)**URS** (flexible), PCNL
**>20mm / Staghorn / Complex****PCNL**Staged URS, ESWL (adjunct)
**Lower pole 10–20mm****URS** (flexible)ESWL (if <10mm, favourable anatomy)
**Uric Acid Stones****Alkalinisation** (K-citrate, target urine pH 6.5–7.0) → dissolutionESWL, URS
**Cystine Stones**High fluid (>3L), alkalinisation (pH >7.5), thiol drugs (D-penicillamine, tiopronin)ESWL (poor fragmentation), URS, PCNL
**Struvite (Infection)****Complete surgical removal (PCNL/URS) + culture-directed antibiotics**ESWL alone contraindicated (residual fragments = recurrent infection)
9 Prevention (All Stone Formers)
MeasureTarget
-----------------
**Fluid Intake****>2.5–3L/day** (urine output >2–2.5L) — single most effective
**Diet**Normal Ca (1000–1200mg) — **do NOT restrict**; Low salt (<5g/day); Low animal protein; High fruit/veg (citrate); Avoid excess oxalate (spinach, nuts, tea) if hyperoxaluria
**Urine pH**CaOx/CaP: 6.0–6.5; Uric acid: **>6.5** (alkalinisation); Cystine: **>7.5**
**Pharmacological****Thiazide** (hypercalciuria); **Potassium citrate** (hypocitraturia, uric acid, cystine); **Allopurinol/febuxostat** (hyperuricosuria/gout); **D-penicillamine/tiopronin** (cystinuria)
10 Special Populations
PopulationConsiderations
----------------------------
**Pregnancy**US only (no CT); conservative (stent/nephrostomy if obstruction); avoid NSAIDs 3rd trimester; MET controversial
**Paediatric**US first; metabolic workup mandatory (cystinuria, hypercalciuria, RTA); ESWL/URS preferred
**Obesity / BMI >30**CT KUB still gold; PCNL technically harder; ESWL less effective (skin-to-stone distance >10cm)
**Anticoagulation**Hold for surgery; ESWL/URS possible with bridging; stent if urgent
**Solitary Kidney / Transplant**Lower threshold for intervention; stent/nephrostomy for obstruction; MET cautious
11 Complications
ComplicationManagement
--------------------------
**Infected Obstruction (Pyonephrosis)****Emergency decompression** (nephrostomy/stent) + IV antibiotics
**Ureteric Injury (iatrogenic)**Stent 4–6 weeks; ureteric reimplantation if complete
**Steinstrasse** (stone street)After ESWL; stent, URS for large fragments
**Recurrence**50% at 5 years, 70% at 10 years without prevention
12 High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points
[!important] - Calcium oxalate = commonest (80%) - Risk: low fluid, high salt/protein, hypercalciuria, hypocitraturia, obesity, DM - Renal colic: sudden loin→groin pain, restlessness, haematuria; NSAID 1st line (diclofenac IM) - CT KUB = gold standard (size, location, HU, hydronephrosis) - Uric acid stones: radiolucent on X-ray, low HU (<400), low pH (<5.5) → alkalinisation dissolves - Struvite = UTI with urease-producers (Proteus); staghorn; complete removal + antibiotics - Cystine = genetic (AR); high fluid, alkalinisation pH >7.5, thiol drugs - MET: tamsulosin 400mcg for distal ureteric <10mm - ESWL: <10mm proximal, favourable anatomy; PCNL >20mm/staghorn - Prevention: >2.5L urine/day, normal Ca diet, low salt, potassium citrate (hypocitraturia), thiazide (hypercalciuria) - 24h urine metabolic workup: recurrent, high-risk, young, atypical stones
13 Common Confusions / Exam Traps
TrapCorrection
------------------
**Low Ca diet prevents stones****Normal Ca (1000–1200mg) protects** (binds oxalate in gut); low Ca ↑ oxalate absorption
**Uric acid stones = radiopaque****Radiolucent on X-ray**; low HU on CT
**ESWL for all stones****PCNL for >20mm/staghorn**; ESWL fails for cystine, brushite, dense stones
**MET for all stones****Distal ureteric <10mm only**
**Struvite = treat with antibiotics alone****Complete surgical removal required** (residual = recurrent infection)
**Cystine stones fragment well with ESWL****Poor fragmentation**; URS/PCNL preferred
**24h urine = single collection****Need 2 collections** (variability)
**Citrate only for hypocitraturia**Also for uric acid (alkalinisation) and cystine
**All stones need surgery**Many <5mm pass spontaneously; MET helps
**Overhydration helps acute colic****Avoid IV overhydration** (↑ pressure); oral 2.5L/day

23 Summary

Urolithiasis is a Must Know 🔴 topic. Key takeaway: CaOx 80% commonest. Risk: low fluid, high salt/protein, hypercalciuria, hypocitraturia. Renal colic: loin→groin, restlessness; NSAID 1st line (diclofenac IM). CT KUB gold standard (size, HU, hydronephrosis). Uric acid: radiolucent, low HU <400, pH <5.5 → alkalinisation dissolves. Struvite: UTI + urease (Proteus), staghorn; complete removal + antibiotics. Cystine: AR, hexagonal crystals; fluid >3L, pH >7.5, thiol drugs. MET: tamsulosin distal <10mm. ESWL: <10mm proximal favourable. PCNL: >20mm/staghorn. Prevention: >2.5L urine/day, normal Ca, low salt, citrate, thiazide. Metabolic workup: recurrent, high-risk, young, atypical stones. Exam focus: Stone types, renal colic management, CT KUB, MET, ESWL/URS/PCNL indications, uric acid alkalinisation, struvite complete removal, cystine genetics, prevention, metabolic workup. Clinical relevance: High recurrence (50% at 5yrs); dietary/fluid modification + targeted pharmacotherapy dramatically reduce recurrence; CT KUB avoids radiation of IVU.
