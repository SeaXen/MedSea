# TDM: Immunosuppressants (Tacrolimus, Cyclosporine, Sirolimus)

**Parent Topic:** [Therapeutic Drug Monitoring](../../Therapeutic%20Drug%20Monitoring.md) → [Clinical Therapeutics Overview](../../Clinical%20Therapeutics%20and%20Good%20Prescribing%20MOC.md)
**Status:** `full-fcps-mrcp-note`
**Priority:** ⭐⭐⭐ HIGHEST (FCPS/MRCP — narrow TI, CYP3A4/P-gp interactions, trough vs C2 monitoring, rejection vs toxicity)
**Source:** Davidson 24th Ed Ch 2; BNF; KDIGO Guidelines; BST Guidelines; Maudsley; Sanford Guide; Transplant TDM literature

## 🎯 Learning Objectives
- [ ] Understand **CNI (Calcineurin Inhibitor) PK**: narrow TI, CYP3A4/P-gp metabolism, intra-patient variability
- [ ] Apply **therapeutic ranges**: Tacrolimus vs Cyclosporine (trough vs C2), Induction vs Maintenance
- [ ] Time **sampling correctly**: Trough (C0) standard; C2 for Cyclosporine (better AUC correlation)
- [ ] Adjust for **drug interactions**: CYP3A4 inhibitors (↑ levels), inducers (↓ levels)
- [ ] Recognise **CNI toxicity**: Nephrotoxicity, neurotoxicity (tremor, seizures, PRES), diabetes, hypertension, hyperlipidaemia
- [ ] Manage **Sirolimus/Everolimus**: mTOR inhibitors, different monitoring (trough), wound healing, pneumonitis
- [ ] Apply **conversion** between CNIs
- [ ] Answer viva: "Tacrolimus vs Cyclosporine monitoring" and "CNI interactions with clarithromycin/rifampicin"

## 🧠 Core Concept: Immunosuppressant Pharmacokinetics

| Property | Tacrolimus | Cyclosporine | Sirolimus / Everolimus |
|----------|------------|--------------|------------------------|
| **Class** | Calcineurin Inhibitor (CNI) | Calcineurin Inhibitor (CNI) | mTOR Inhibitor |
| **Mechanism** | FKBP12 → Calcineurin inhibition → ↓ IL-2 | Cyclophilin → Calcineurin inhibition → ↓ IL-2 | FKBP12 → mTOR inhibition → ↓ T-cell proliferation |
| **Metabolism** | **CYP3A4/5** (gut + liver) | **CYP3A4/5** (gut + liver) | **CYP3A4** (gut + liver) |
| **Transporter** | **P-glycoprotein (P-gp)** | **P-glycoprotein (P-gp)** | **P-gp** |
| **Protein binding** | 99% (albumin, α1-acid glycoprotein) | 90% (lipoproteins) | 92% |
| **Blood:Plasma ratio** | ~15–25 (high in RBCs) | ~2 (variable) | ~30–40 |
| **Half-life** | 12–24h | 6–12h | Sirolimus: 60h; Everolimus: 30h |
| **Therapeutic index** | **Very narrow** | **Narrow** | Narrow |
| **Key toxicity** | Nephro, Neuro, Diabetes, HTN | Nephro, HTN, Hyperlipidaemia, Hirsutism | Thrombocytopenia, Pneumonitis, Wound healing, Hyperlipidaemia |

> **Key Principle:** *All three are **CYP3A4 + P-gp substrates**. **Inhibitors ↑ levels (toxicity)**, **Inducers ↓ levels (rejection)**. **Whole blood** monitoring (not plasma/serum) due to RBC partitioning.*

## 1️⃣ Tacrolimus (FK506 / Prograf® / Advagraf® / Envarsus®)

### Therapeutic Ranges (Whole Blood Trough, C0) — KDIGO/BST

| Phase | Target Range (μg/L = ng/mL) |
|-------|-----------------------------|
| **Induction (0–3 months)** | **10–15** (kidney), **15–20** (liver/heart/lung) |
| **Maintenance (3–12 months)** | **5–10** (kidney), **10–15** (liver/heart/lung) |
| **Long-term (>1 year)** | **3–8** (kidney), **5–10** (liver/heart/lung) |
| **Minimisation (with MMF/mTOR)** | **3–7** |

> **Viva Key:** *Tacrolimus trough **higher in induction**, **lower in maintenance**, **lowest in minimisation**. Liver/heart/lung targets higher than kidney.*

### Formulations & Dosing
| Formulation | Dosing | Conversion |
|-------------|--------|------------|
| **Prograf® (IR)** | BD (0.1–0.2 mg/kg/day) | Reference |
| **Advagraf® (PR)** | OD (same total daily dose) | 1:1 total daily dose |
| **Envarsus® (ER)** | OD (lower dose) | **Envarsus 0.7mg ≈ Advagraf 1mg ≈ Prograf 1mg** |

### Sampling Time
- **Trough (C0)**: **Pre-dose** (12h post-BD, 24h post-OD)
- **Consistent timing** critical (food, other drugs)
- **Steady state**: 3–5 days (5 half-lives)

## 2️⃣ Cyclosporine (Neoral® / Sandimmune®)

### Therapeutic Ranges (Whole Blood)

| Monitoring | Target Range (μg/L) |
|------------|---------------------|
| **Trough (C0)** | **Induction: 200–300**; Maintenance: **100–200**; Long-term: **75–150** |
| **C2 (2h post-dose)** | **Induction: 800–1200**; Maintenance: **400–800**; Long-term: **300–600** |

> **C2 vs C0**: *C2 (2h post-dose) **better correlates with AUC** and clinical outcomes. **Preferred for Cyclosporine (Neoral®)**. C0 acceptable if C2 not feasible.*

### Formulations
| Formulation | Bioavailability | Notes |
|-------------|-----------------|-------|
| **Neoral® (Microemulsion)** | ~30–50% (more consistent) | **Standard**; BD dosing |
| **Sandimmune® (Oil-based)** | ~20–30% (variable) | **Avoid interchange**; not bioequivalent |

### Sampling Time
- **C0**: Pre-dose (12h post-BD)
- **C2**: **Exactly 2h post-dose** (critical timing)
- **Steady state**: 3–5 days

## 3️⃣ Sirolimus (Rapamune®) / Everolimus (Certican® / Afinitor®)

### Therapeutic Ranges (Whole Blood Trough)

| Drug | Induction | Maintenance | Minimisation (with CNI reduction) |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Sirolimus** | **12–20** | **5–12** | **4–10** (with low-dose CNI) |
| **Everolimus** | **6–10** | **3–8** | **3–6** (with reduced CNI) |

> **Key:** *mTOR inhibitors used **with reduced-dose CNI** (minimisation) or **CNI-free** (if nephrotoxicity). **Higher targets with CNI-free**.*

### Key Characteristics
| Feature | Sirolimus | Everolimus |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Half-life** | ~60h | ~30h |
| **Dosing** | OD (loading 6–15mg, then 2–5mg) | BD (0.75–1.5mg) |
| **Steady state** | 5–7 days | 3–4 days |
| **Monitoring** | Trough (C0) | Trough (C0) |

### Specific Toxicities
| Toxicity | Sirolimus | Everolimus |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Wound healing impairment** | **High** (hold peri-op) | Moderate |
| **Pneumonitis** | **~10–15%** (interstitial) | ~5–10% |
| **Thrombocytopenia** | Common | Common |
| **Hyperlipidaemia** | **Severe** (↑ TG, cholesterol) | Moderate |
| **Proteinuria** | **Can worsen** | Can worsen |
| **Oral ulcers** | Common | Common |

## 4️⃣ Major Drug Interactions — CYP3A4 / P-gp

### CYP3A4 / P-gp Inhibitors (↑ Levels → TOXICITY)

| Drug | Effect on CNI/mTOR | Magnitude | Management |
|------|-------------------|-----------|------------|
| **Clarithromycin / Erythromycin** | ↑↑ Tacrolimus, Cyclosporine, Sirolimus | **5–10x** | **Avoid**; use azithromycin (no CYP3A4 inhibition) |
| **Azole antifungals** (Fluconazole, Itraconazole, Voriconazole, Posaconazole) | ↑↑ All | **3–10x** | **Reduce dose 50–75%**; monitor levels q2–3d |
| **Protease inhibitors** (Ritonavir, Atazanavir) | ↑↑ All | **5–20x** | **Avoid / specialist only** |
| **Diltiazem / Verapamil** | ↑ Tacrolimus, Cyclosporine | **2–4x** | Reduce dose 50%; monitor |
| **Grapefruit juice** | ↑ All | **2–3x** | **Avoid** |
| **Amiodarone** | ↑ Tacrolimus | **2–3x** | Reduce dose; monitor |
| **Rifampicin** | **↓↓ All** (inducer) | **90% ↓** | **Avoid**; if essential → increase dose 3–5x + close monitoring |
| **Carbamazepine / Phenytoin / Phenobarbital** | ↓↓ All | **50–80% ↓** | Avoid; use alternative anticonvulsant (levetiracetam) |
| **St John's Wort** | ↓ All | **30–50% ↓** | Avoid |

> **Viva Key:** *Macrolides (clarithromycin) + Tacrolimus = **classic interaction** → **acute nephrotoxicity, neurotoxicity**. **Use azithromycin instead**. Azoles → **reduce CNI dose 50–75%**. Rifampicin → **avoid (rejector risk)**.*

### Other Interactions
| Interaction | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| **ACEi/ARB + CNI** | ↑ Nephrotoxicity risk (additive) |
| **NSAIDs + CNI** | ↓ GFR + ↑ Nephrotoxicity |
| **Statins + CNI/mTOR** | ↑ Myopathy risk (esp. simvastatin/atorvastatin with sirolimus) |
| **Potassium-sparing diuretics + Tacrolimus** | ↑ Hyperkalaemia risk |
| **IVIG + Tacrolimus** | ↑ Neurotoxicity (PRES) |

## 5️⃣ CNI Toxicity — Recognition & Management

### Tacrolimus vs Cyclosporine Toxicity Profile

| Toxicity | Tacrolimus | Cyclosporine |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| **Nephrotoxicity** | **Acute (vasoconstriction) + Chronic (arteriolar hyalinosis)** | Similar (more chronic) |
| **Neurotoxicity** | **Tremor, headache, insomnia, PRES, seizures** (more common) | Less common |
| **Diabetes (NODAT)** | **High risk** (↑ 10–20% kidney Tx) | Lower risk |
| **Hypertension** | Common | **More common/severe** |
| **Hyperlipidaemia** | Mild | **Marked** (↑ TG, cholesterol) |
| **Hirsutism / Gingival hyperplasia** | Rare | **Common** |
| **Electrolytes** | **Hyperkalaemia, Hypomagnesaemia** | Hyperkalaemia |

### Nephrotoxicity — Acute vs Chronic
| Type | Features | Management |
|------|----------|------------|
| **Acute (Functional)** | Reversible afferent arteriolar vasoconstriction; ↑ Cr, ↓ UOP | **Reduce dose**; ensure euvolaemia; avoid NSAIDs/ACEi; IV fluids |
| **Chronic (Structural)** | Arteriolar hyalinosis, interstitial fibrosis; **irreversible** | **CNI minimisation/withdrawal**; convert to mTOR/belatacept |

### Neurotoxicity — PRES (Posterior Reversible Encephalopathy Syndrome)
| Trigger | High tacrolimus level, hypertension, IVIG, sepsis |
|---------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Features | Headache, visual disturbance, seizures, confusion, cortical blindness |
| Management | **Stop/Reduce tacrolimus**; control BP; anticonvulsants; usually reversible |

## 6️⃣ CNI Conversion / Minimisation Strategies

### Tacrolimus ↔ Cyclosporine Conversion
| Direction | Ratio | Method |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Tac → CyA** | **1:5 to 1:7** (Tac 1mg ≈ CyA 5–7mg) | Switch at same time; monitor C0/C2 at 24h, 48h, 1w |
| **CyA → Tac** | **5:1 to 7:1** | Same; monitor closely |

> **Principle:** *Convert **total daily dose** using ratio. **Monitor levels daily × 3–5 days**.*

### CNI Minimisation / Withdrawal (for Nephrotoxicity)
| Strategy | Regimen |
|----------|---------|
| **CNI → mTOR** | Reduce CNI by 50% → start sirolimus/everolimus → taper CNI over 4–8w |
| **CNI-free** | Stop CNI → sirolimus/everolimus + MMF + steroids (selected patients) |
| **CNI → Belatacept** | IV monthly (EBV+ only); CNI-free; better renal function |

## 7️⃣ Monitoring Schedule (KDIGO/BST)

| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Tacrolimus/Cyclosporine level** | **Daily × 1w** post-Tx/change → **2–3x/wk × 1m** → **weekly × 3m** → **monthly × 1y** → **3-monthly** |
| **Sirolimus/Everolimus level** | **Weekly × 1m** → **monthly × 3m** → **3-monthly** |
| **Renal function (Cr, eGFR)** | Daily (early) → weekly → monthly → 3-monthly |
| **Fasting lipids** | Baseline → 3-monthly (mTOR: monthly × 3m) |
| **Glucose / HbA1c** | Baseline → 3-monthly (Tac: monthly × 3m) |
| **BP** | Each visit |
| **Mg²⁺, K⁺** | Monthly × 3m → 3-monthly |
| **LFT** | Monthly × 3m → 3-monthly |
| **FBC** | Monthly × 3m → 3-monthly (mTOR: thrombocytopenia) |

## 8️⃣ Special Populations

### Pregnancy (Transplant Recipients)
| Drug | Safety | Monitoring |
|------|--------|------------|
| **Tacrolimus** | **Preferred CNI** (more data); ↑ clearance in pregnancy | **Levels q2w**; dose ↑ 20–50% |
| **Cyclosporine** | Alternative; ↑ HTN risk | Levels q2w |
| **Sirolimus/Everolimus** | **Contraindicated** (teratogenic in animals) | **Stop pre-conception** |
| **MMF/Mycophenolate** | **Contraindicated** (teratogenic) | **Switch to Azathioprine pre-conception** |

### Paediatrics
- **Higher clearance** → higher mg/kg doses
- **Tacrolimus**: 0.1–0.3 mg/kg/day BD
- **Growth monitoring** (steroids, CNI)

### Hepatic Impairment
- **Tacrolimus**: ↓ Clearance → **reduce dose**; monitor levels daily
- **Cyclosporine**: ↓ Clearance → reduce dose
- **Sirolimus**: ↑↑ Levels → **avoid severe hepatic impairment**

## 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"Tacrolimus and Cyclosporine same monitoring"** | **NO.** Tacrolimus = **trough (C0)** only. Cyclosporine = **C2 preferred** (2h post-dose). |
| **"C2 = 2h post ANY formulation"** | **C2 validated for Neoral® (microemulsion)**. Sandimmune® not suitable for C2. |
| **"Sirolimus = CNI-sparing = no CNI needed"** | **Usually with REDUCED CNI** (minimisation). CNI-free only in selected patients (nephrotoxicity). |
| **"Azithromycin interacts like clarithromycin"** | **NO.** **Azithromycin = NO CYP3A4 inhibition** → safe with CNIs. Preferred macrolide. |
| **"CNI levels = plasma/serum"** | **NO.** **Whole blood** (EDTA) — CNIs partition into RBCs. Plasma/serum levels meaningless. |
| **"Tremor = always tacrolimus toxicity"** | **Tremor common at therapeutic levels**. **Coarse tremor + hyperreflexia + confusion = toxicity**. |
| **"Wound healing only with sirolimus"** | **Everolimus also impairs** (less). CNIs also impair but less. **Hold mTOR peri-op**. |
| **"Cyclosporine gingival hyperplasia = poor hygiene"** | **Cyclosporine-specific** (not tacrolimus). Good hygiene helps but not preventable. |

> **Mnemonic: IMMUNOSUPPRESSANT TDM**  
> **I**mmunosuppressants: **Tacrolimus, Cyclosporine, Sirolimus, Everolimus**  
> **M**onitoring: **Whole blood** (not plasma) — RBC partitioning critical  
> **M**etabolism: **CYP3A4 + P-gp** for ALL — **Inhibitors ↑ (toxicity), Inducers ↓ (rejection)**  
> **U**niversal interactions: **Clarithromycin (avoid → azithromycin), Azoles (↓ dose 50-75%), Rifampicin (avoid)**  
> **N**ephrotoxicity: **Acute (reversible) vs Chronic (irreversible → CNI minimisation/mTOR switch)**  
> **O**ptimal ranges: **Tac C0: Ind 10-15, Maint 5-10; CyA C2: Ind 800-1200, Maint 400-800**  
> **S**irolimus: **mTOR**; **Wound healing, Pneumonitis, Thrombocytopenia, Hyperlipidaemia**  
> **U**nique toxicities: **Tac = Neuro (tremor/PRES), NODAT; CyA = HTN, Lipids, Hirsutism**  
> **P**regnancy: **Tac/CyA OK (levels q2w); Sirolimus/Everolimus/MMF CONTRAINDICATED**  
> **P**ERI-OP: **Hold mTOR 1w pre / 2w post** (wound healing)  
> **R**efraction: **CyA ↔ Tac 5:1 to 7:1**; daily levels × 3-5d  
> **E**verolimus: **t½ 30h (vs Siro 60h)**; BD dosing; lower pneumonitis  
> **S**ampling: **Tac/CyA/Siro/Evero = Trough (C0) pre-dose**; **CyA C2 = 2h post (better AUC)**  
> **S**tatin interaction: **mTOR + Statin = ↑ Myopathy** (simva/atorva worst)  
> **A**zoles: **Fluconazole/Itraconazole/Voriconazole/Posaconazole** all ↑↑ CNI/mTOR  
> **N**ODAT: **Tacrolimus > Cyclosporine** (insulinopenia + insulin resistance)  
> **N**euro: **Tac = Tremor → PRES → Seizures**; CyA = less neuro  
> **T**herapeutic drug monitoring: **MANDATORY** for all — narrow TI, high variability  
> **D**ose adjustment: **Small changes (25-50%)**; re-level 24-48h  
> **M**inimisation: **CNI → mTOR** for chronic nephrotoxicity; Belatacept alternative

## 📌 Summary
- **Whole blood monitoring** for all (RBC partitioning)
- **Tacrolimus**: C0 trough; Induction 10–15, Maintenance 5–10, Long-term 3–8 μg/L
- **Cyclosporine**: **C2 (2h post) preferred**; Induction 800–1200, Maintenance 400–800; C0 Ind 200–300, Maint 100–200
- **Sirolimus/Everolimus**: mTOR inhibitors; C0 trough; Siro Ind 12–20, Maint 5–12; Ever Ind 6–10, Maint 3–8
- **CYP3A4/P-gp interactions**: **Clarithromycin/erythromycin → avoid (use azithromycin)**; **Azoles → reduce dose 50–75%**; **Rifampicin/carbamazepine/phenytoin → avoid (rejection risk)**
- **Toxicity**: Tacrolimus = Neuro (tremor, PRES), NODAT; Cyclosporine = HTN, Hyperlipidaemia, Hirsutism; mTOR = Wound healing, Pneumonitis, Thrombocytopenia, Hyperlipidaemia
- **Pregnancy**: Tacrolimus/Cyclosporine OK (levels q2w); **Sirolimus/Everolimus/MMF contraindicated**
- **Peri-op**: **Hold mTOR 1 week pre / 2 weeks post** major surgery
- **Conversion**: CyA:Tac ≈ 5:1 to 7:1; CNI minimisation with mTOR for chronic nephrotoxicity

## ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **Preferred sampling time for Cyclosporine (Neoral®) monitoring:**  
   A. Trough (C0) only  B. **C2 (2h post-dose)**  C. Peak (1h)  D. Random  
   *Answer: B. C2 better correlates with AUC and outcomes for Cyclosporine microemulsion.*

2. **Tacrolimus therapeutic trough for kidney transplant maintenance:**  
   A. 15–20 μg/L  B. **5–10 μg/L**  C. 20–30 μg/L  D. 0.5–1.0 μg/L

   *Answer: B. mTOR inhibitors impair wound healing → hold 1 week before and 2 weeks after major surgery.*

   A. 1:1  B. 2:1  C. **5:1 to 7:1**  D. 10:1  
   *Answer: C. CyA 100mg ≈ Tac 15–20mg (5:1 to 7:1). Monitor levels daily × 3–5 days.*

   A. ↑ Levels  B. **↓↓ Levels (90%)**  C. No effect  D. Variable
