# Prescribing in Pregnancy & Lactation

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP Priority: HIGH**
> **Obstetric medicine staple.** FDA categories (historical), UK narrative labelling, Key teratogens, Safe drug choices by indication, Lactation compatibility (LactMed/Hale's).
> Viva: *"Woman on warfarin for mechanical mitral valve — how do you manage anticoagulation in pregnancy?"*

---

## 1. Pregnancy Categorisation Systems

### FDA Categories (Phased Out 2015 — Still in Exam Questions)
| Category | Definition | Examples |
|----------|------------|----------|
| **A** | Adequate, well-controlled studies in pregnant women — **No risk** | Levothyroxine, Folic acid, Pyridoxine, Insulin (human) |
| **B** | Animal studies no risk; No adequate human studies **OR** Animal risk but human studies no risk | Penicillins, Cephalosporins, **Metformin**, **Insulin**, Paracetamol, Heparin/LMWH |
| **C** | Animal studies show risk; No adequate human studies **OR** No animal/human data | **Most SSRIs**, Most antihypertensives (except ACEi/ARB), Corticosteroids, Anticonvulsants (except Valproate/Carbamazepine), Theophylline |
| **D** | Positive evidence of human fetal risk; Benefits may outweigh risks | **Lithium**, **Valproate**, **Carbamazepine**, Phenytoin, **Warfarin**, **ACEi/ARB**, **DOACs**, **Statins**, **Thiazides** |
| **X** | Contraindicated; Risk > Benefit | **Isotretinoin**, **Thalidomide**, **Methotrexate**, **Ribavirin**, **Mycophenolate**, **Misoprostol**, **Finasteride** |

### UK/EU System (Current — SmPC Section 4.6)
| Label | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Contraindicated** | Do not use |
| **Not recommended** | Avoid unless clearly necessary |
| **Use only if clearly needed** | Benefit > Risk; Specialist supervision |
| **May be used** | Sufficient safety data |

## 2. Critical Periods of Teratogenicity

| Organ System | Critical Period (Post-Conception) | Key Teratogens |
|--------------|-----------------------------------|----------------|
| **Neural Tube** | Days 18-28 (Week 3-4) | **Valproate**, Carbamazepine, Folate antagonists (Methotrexate, TMP-SMX) |
| **Heart** | Days 20-50 (Week 3-7) | **Lithium** (Ebstein's), Thalidomide, ACEi/ARB, Retinoids |
| **Limbs** | Days 24-46 (Week 4-7) | **Thalidomide**, Valproate, Warfarin (Chondrodysplasia) |
| **Eyes** | Days 21-42 (Week 3-6) | Retinoids, Thalidomide |
| **Palate** | Days 42-56 (Week 6-8) | Phenytoin, Corticosteroids (high dose), Valproate |
| **External Genitalia** | Weeks 7-12 | Androgens, Progestins, Phenytoin |
| **Brain (Functional)** | **Throughout** (esp. 2nd/3rd Trimester) | **Valproate** (Autism/ID), Alcohol, SSRIs (Neonatal adaptation), Antipsychotics |

> **Pearl:** **Folic acid 5mg daily** from pre-conception to 12 weeks for **ALL women on antiepileptics** (Reduces NTD risk)

## 3. Major Teratogens — "Must Avoid" in Pregnancy

| Drug | Teratogenic Effect | Critical Period | Category |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Valproate** | **Neural tube defects (1-2%), Craniofacial, Limb, Cardiac, Developmental delay, Autism (↑↑ vs other AEDs)** | Neural tube (Wk 3-4), Throughout (Neurodev) | **X / Contraindicated** |
| **Carbamazepine** | Neural tube defects, Craniofacial, Growth retardation | Neural tube (Wk 3-4) | D |
| **Lamotrigine** | Cleft palate (Low risk vs older AEDs) | Palate (Wk 6-8) | C (Preferred if needed) |
| **Lithium** | **Ebstein's anomaly** (Tricuspid valve), Cardiac | Heart (Wk 3-7) | D |
| **Warfarin** | **Warfarin embryopathy** (Chondrodysplasia punctata, Nasal hypoplasia, Limb hypoplasia) — 1st Tri; **CNS bleeding** — 2nd/3rd Tri | Limbs (Wk 4-7), CNS (Throughout) | X / Contraindicated 1st Tri |
| **ACEi / ARB** | **Fetal renal dysplasia, Oligohydramnios, Pulmonary hypoplasia, Skull ossification defects** | 2nd/3rd Trimester (Renal) | D / Contraindicated 2nd/3rd |
| **Isotretinoin** | **Severe CNS, Cardiac, Thymic, Craniofacial defects** | Multiple (Throughout) | X / Contraindicated |
| **Methotrexate** | Fetal death, Craniofacial, Limb defects (Folic acid antagonist) | Neural tube, Limbs (Wk 3-8) | X / Contraindicated |
| **Thalidomide** | **Phocomelia** (Limb reduction), Ear/eye defects | Limbs (Wk 4-7) | X / Contraindicated |
| **Mycophenolate** | Ear malformations, Cleft palate, Limb defects, Microphthalmia | Multiple | X / Contraindicated |
| **Statins** | Animal teratogenicity; Theoretical cholesterol synthesis disruption | Throughout | X / Contraindicated |
| **Ribavirin** | Teratogenic in all animal species | Throughout | X / Contraindicated |
| **Tetracyclines** | Dental discoloration, Enamel hypoplasia, Bone growth inhibition | 2nd/3rd Trimester | D |

## 4. Safe Drug Choices by Indication in Pregnancy

| Indication | 1st Line (Safe) | 2nd Line / Caution | Avoid |
|------------|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Hypertension** | **Labetalol**, **Methyldopa**, **Nifedipine MR** | Hydralazine (IV acute) | **ACEi, ARB, Diuretics (↓ placental perfusion), Spironolactone, Clonidine** |
| **Anticoagulation** | **LMWH (Enoxaparin)** — All trimesters | **Warfarin** 2nd/3rd Tri ONLY if mechanical mitral valve (Target INR 2.5-3.5) | **Warfarin 1st Tri**, **DOACs (All trimesters)**, Fondaparinux |
| **Diabetes** | **Insulin** (Gold standard) | **Metformin** (Growing evidence, 2nd line) | **SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA, DPP-4i, Sulfonylureas (Glibenclamide crosses placenta)** |
| **Epilepsy** | **Lamotrigine**, **Levetiracetam** (Lowest teratogenicity) | Carbamazepine (If only option + Folic acid 5mg), Oxcarbazepine | **Valproate (Contraindicated)**, Phenytoin, Topiramate, Vigabatrin |
| **Depression/Anxiety** | **Sertraline**, **Fluoxetine**, **Citalopram** (SSRIs preferred) | SNRIs (Venlafaxine, Duloxetine), Mirtazapine | **Paroxetine** (Cardiac septal defects), **MAOIs**, TCAs (Anticholinergic) |
| **Bipolar** | **Lamotrigine** (if depressive pole), **Quetiapine** | Lithium (If essential — Specialist, Lowest dose, Monitor levels, Echo at 20wk) | **Valproate (Contraindicated)**, Carbamazepine |
| **Asthma** | **Continue ALL inhalers** (ICS, LABA, SABA, LTRA) | Theophylline (Monitor levels) | — (Uncontrolled asthma > Drug risk) |
| **Thyroid** | **Levothyroxine** (Increase dose 25-50% in 1st Tri), **Carbimazole** (1st Tri) → **PTU** (2nd/3rd Tri) | — | **Radioiodine** |
| **Pain** | **Paracetamol** (1st line, all trimesters) | **Opioids** (Short-term, lowest dose; Neonatal withdrawal risk) | **NSAIDs** (Avoid 3rd Tri — Premature ductal closure, Oligohydramnios) |
| **Nausea/Vomiting** | **Doxylamine + Pyridoxine** (1st line, licensed), **Metoclopramide**, **Ondansetron** (2nd line) | **Cyclizine**, **Promethazine** | **Corticosteroids** (Unless severe HG) |
| **UTI** | **Nitrofurantoin** (Avoid term — Neonatal haemolysis), **Amoxicillin**, **Cefalexin** | **TMP-SMX** (Avoid 1st Tri — Folate antagonist; Avoid term — Kernicterus) | **Fluoroquinolones**, **Tetracyclines** |

## 5. Lactation Safety (Breastfeeding)

### Hale's Lactation Risk Categories (L1-L5)
| Category | Definition | Action |
|----------|------------|--------|
| **L1 (Safest)** | No reports of adverse effects; Controlled studies show no risk | **Compatible** |
| **L2 (Safer)** | Limited studies; No adverse effects reported | **Compatible** |
| **L3 (Moderate)** | No controlled studies; Possible risk; Minimal non-threatening effects | **Use with caution** / Monitor infant |
| **L4 (Potentially Hazardous)** | Evidence of risk; May be acceptable if benefit > risk | **Avoid if alternative exists** |
| **L5 (Contraindicated)** | Significant risk documented; Contraindicated | **Contraindicated** |

### Key Principles
- **Relative Infant Dose (RID)** = (Infant dose via milk mg/kg/day) ÷ (Maternal dose mg/kg/day) × 100
- **RID <10%** = Generally compatible
- **Milk:Plasma (M/P) ratio** <1 = Low transfer; >1 = High transfer
- **Oral bioavailability** of drug in infant matters (e.g., Heparin, Insulin = Not absorbed orally = Safe)

### Common Drugs in Lactation

| Drug | Hale Category | RID | Compatible? | Notes |
|------|---------------|-----|-------------|-------|
| **Paracetamol** | L1 | <1% | **Yes** | Preferred analgesic |
| **Ibuprofen** | L1 | 0.06% | **Yes** | Preferred NSAID |
| **Naproxen** | L3 | 1-3% | **Caution** | Long half-life; Prefer Ibuprofen |
| **Codeine** | **L3/L4** | Variable | **Avoid** | **CYP2D6 Ultra-rapid metabolisers → Morphine toxicity in infant** |
| **Tramadol** | L3 | 2-3% | Caution | Monitor infant sedation |
| **Morphine** | L2/L3 | 2-3% | **Yes (Short-term)** | Monitor infant sedation, resp depression |
| **SSRIs (Sertraline, Paroxetine)** | L2/L3 | 0.5-3% | **Yes (Sertraline preferred)** | Monitor infant irritability, feeding |
| **Lamotrigine** | L3 | 9-18% | **Caution** | Monitor infant rash, drowsiness; Levels ↑ in pregnancy |
| **Levetiracetam** | L3 | 3-5% | **Yes** | Preferred AED |
| **Valproate** | L2 | 1-3% | **Yes BUT** | Teratogen in pregnancy; Monitor infant LFTs, platelets |
| **Warfarin** | L1 | Not detected | **Yes** | Does not enter milk (High protein binding) |
| **LMWH** | L1 | Not absorbed orally | **Yes** | Preferred anticoagulant |
| **DOACs** | L3/L4 | Unknown | **Avoid** | No safety data |
| **ACEi/ARB** | L3/L4 | Low | **Avoid in newborn** (Renal risk) | OK if infant >1 month |
| **Metformin** | L2 | 0.2-0.7% | **Yes** | Increasingly used |
| **Insulin** | L1 | Not absorbed orally | **Yes** | Safe |
| **Prednisolone** | L2 | <0.1% | **Yes** | Low milk transfer |
| **Hydroxychloroquine** | L2 | 2-4% | **Yes** | Continue if needed for SLE |
| **Azathioprine** | L3 | 1-2% | **Yes** | Monitor infant FBC |
| **Mycophenolate** | **L5** | — | **Contraindicated** | Teratogen/Immunosuppression |
| **Amiodarone** | **L5** | High (Iodine) | **Contraindicated** | Infant thyroid dysfunction |
| **Lithium** | **L5** | High | **Contraindicated** | Infant toxicity (Renal, Thyroid, CNS) |

## 6. High-Yield Clinical Scenarios (Viva Favourites)

### Valproate in Women of Childbearing Age
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Woman on Valproate] --> B{Planning Pregnancy?}
    B -->|Yes| C[**STOP Valproate Pre-conception**]
    C --> D[Switch to Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam]
    D --> E[Folic Acid 5mg Daily]
    E --> F[Valproate Pregnancy Prevention Programme (PPP)]
    B -->|No| G[Enroll in PPP: Annual review, Contraception, Risk acknowledgement]
```

**Valproate PPP (MHRA):** Annual specialist review, Highly effective contraception (LARC preferred), Signed risk acknowledgement form, Patient card.

### Warfarin in Pregnancy (Mechanical Valve)
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Mechanical Valve on Warfarin] --> B{Pregnancy Confirmed}
    B --> C[**STOP Warfarin Immediately (1st Tri)**]
    C --> D[**Switch to Therapeutic LMWH** (Enoxaparin 1mg/kg BD SC)]
    D --> E[Anti-Xa Monitoring (Target 0.8-1.2 IU/mL 4h post-dose)]
    E --> F[**Weekly Anti-Xa** + Renal function]
    F --> G[Restart Warfarin 2nd Tri IF Mechanical MITRAL valve] 
    G --> H[INR Target 2.5-3.5; Close monitoring]
    H --> I[**Stop Warfarin 36wks** → LMWH → Delivery → Restart Warfarin + LMWH overlap]
```

### ACEi/ARB Exposure in 1st Trimester
- **1st Trimester:** No strong evidence of teratogenicity (Renal effects 2nd/3rd tri)
- **Action:** **Stop immediately on confirmation**, Switch to Labetalol/Methyldopa/Nifedipine
- **Counselling:** Low absolute risk if brief 1st tri exposure; No termination indicated

## 8. Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)

| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **28yo woman on sodium valproate 1g/day for epilepsy planning pregnancy. Management?** | **Contraindicated in pregnancy** (NTD 1-2%, Autism/ID). **Enrol in Valproate PPP**. **Switch pre-conception** to Lamotrigine (slow titration) or Levetiracetam. **Folic acid 5mg daily** pre-conception to 12wk. Annual specialist review, Effective contraception until switched. |
| **Mechanical mitral valve on warfarin — antenatal anticoagulation?** | **1st Trimester:** Stop warfarin → **Therapeutic LMWH (Enoxaparin 1mg/kg BD)** + Anti-Xa monitoring weekly. **2nd/3rd Trimester:** Restart warfarin (Target INR 2.5-3.5) with LMWH overlap until INR therapeutic. **36 weeks:** Stop warfarin → LMWH until delivery. Postpartum: Restart warfarin + LMWH overlap. |
| **ACEi in 1st trimester — teratogenic?** | **No strong evidence** for 1st trimester teratogenicity (Renal effects 2nd/3rd tri). **Stop immediately on confirmation**, Switch to Labetalol/Methyldopa/Nifedipine. No termination indicated for brief exposure. |
| **Woman on lithium for bipolar disorder becomes pregnant. Management?** | **Ebstein's anomaly risk (1st tri)**. If mild: **Stop lithium** (Risk relapse vs teratogenicity). If severe (essential): **Lowest effective dose**, **Level monitoring q4wk (1st tri), q2wk (2nd/3rd)**, **Fetal echo at 20wk**, Monitor neonatal lithium levels, thyroid, renal function. Avoid breastfeeding. |
| **Safe antidepressant in pregnancy?** | **Sertraline, Fluoxetine, Citalopram** (SSRIs preferred). **Paroxetine avoid** (Cardiac septal defects). Neonatal adaptation syndrome (Jitteriness, IRDS, feeding issues) — Usually self-limiting. |
| **Which antihypertensives are safe in pregnancy?** | **1st Line:** Labetalol, Methyldopa, Nifedipine MR. **Avoid:** ACEi, ARB, Diuretics (↓ placental perfusion), Spironolactone (Anti-androgenic), Clonidine (Rebound HTN). |
| **Anticoagulation for VTE in pregnancy?** | **Therapeutic LMWH (Enoxaparin 1.5mg/kg OD or 1mg/kg BD)** entire pregnancy + 6 weeks postpartum. **Anti-Xa monitoring** (Target 0.8-1.2 IU/mL 4h post-dose). Warfarin/DOACs contraindicated. |
| **Codeine in breastfeeding — safe?** | **NO — Avoid**. **CYP2D6 Ultra-rapid metabolisers** → ↑ Morphine in milk → Infant respiratory depression, sedation, death. Use **Paracetamol + Ibuprofen** instead. |
| **Lamotrigine in pregnancy — dose adjustment?** | **Levels DROP 50-60% in pregnancy** (↑ Clearance, ↑ Volume). **Monitor levels q4wk**; Increase dose to maintain pre-pregnancy levels. **Folic acid 5mg daily**. Postpartum: Reduce dose rapidly (Levels surge). |
| **DOACs in pregnancy — contraindicated because?** | **No human safety data; Animal studies show fetal bleeding/placental haemorrhage**; Excreted in milk (Unknown infant exposure); **Use LMWH instead**. |

## 9. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **FDA Category C = Safe or Unsafe?** | **Uncertain** — Animal risk, no human data. Many C drugs used (SSRIs, Steroids) — Individual risk-benefit. |
| **Warfarin 1st vs 2nd/3rd Trimester** | **1st Tri: Embryopathy** (Bone, Nose, Limbs); **2nd/3rd: CNS Bleeding** (Intracranial haemorrhage) — Different mechanisms |
| **ACEi teratogenicity timing** | **2nd/3rd Trimester only** (Fetal renal development); 1st Tri = No proven risk |
| **Valproate vs Lamotrigine** | **Valproate = Contraindicated** (High NTD + Neurodev); **Lamotrigine = Preferred** (Lowest risk, but levels drop in pregnancy) |
| **Codeine breastfeeding** | **Avoid** — CYP2D6 Ultra-rapid metabolisers produce toxic morphine levels in milk |
| **Lamotrigine levels in pregnancy** | **↓ 50-60%** (Induced glucuronidation, ↑ Vd) → **Monitor q4wk, Increase dose**; Postpartum → **Rapidly reduce** (Levels rebound) |
| **Steroids in pregnancy** | **OK (Category C)** — Prednisolone/Prednisone inactivated by placental 11β-HSD2; Betamethasone/Dexamethasone cross placenta (For fetal lung maturity) |
| **Metformin in pregnancy** | **Increasingly used** (PCOS, GDM, T2DM) — **Category B**; Crosses placenta but no teratogenicity; Long-term follow-up reassuring |

**Mnemonic: PREGNANT-SAFE**
- **P**aracetamol (Analgesia)
- **R** (ACEi/**ARB** — **Avoid 2nd/3rd**)
- **E**noxaparin (LMWH — **All trimesters**)
- **G**estational DM → **Insulin/Metformin**
- **N**TD prevention → **Folic Acid 5mg** (AEDs)
- **A**ntiepileptic → **Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam**
- **N**SAIDs — **Avoid 3rd Tri**
- **T**eratogens → **Valproate, Warfarin 1st, ACEi, Lithium, Isotretinoin, Methotrexate, Thalidomide, Mycophenolate, Statins**
- **S**SRIs → **Sertraline/Fluoxetine** (Paroxetine avoid)
- **A**ntipsychotic → **Quetiapine/Lamotrigine** (Valproate X)
- **F**eeding → **Hale L1/L2 OK; Codeine X; Lithium/Amiodarone/Mycophenolate X**
- **E**thy Estrogens (OCP) — Thrombosis risk

## 13. Exam Answer Modes

### Long Answer Skeleton
1. Identify teratogen risk of current drug
2. Cite FDA/UK category & critical period
3. Recommend safer alternative per indication
4. Address folic acid, monitoring, PPP if valproate
5. Plan for labour/delivery/postpartum (Anticoagulation, Breastfeeding)

### Short Note Skeleton
- **FDA:** A/B=Safe, C=Risk unknown, D=Risk+Benefit, X=Contraindicated
- **Teratogens:** Valproate (NTD+Neurodev), Warfarin 1st (Embryopathy), ACEi 2nd/3rd (Renal), Lithium (Ebstein), Isotretinoin (Multi), Methotrexate/Thalidomide/Myco/Statins
- **Safe:** HTN→Labetalol/Methyldopa/Nifedipine; Anticoag→LMWH; DM→Insulin/Metformin; Epilepsy→Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam+FA5mg; Depression→Sertraline
- **Lactation:** Hale L1/L2 OK; L3 Caution; L4/L5 Avoid; Codeine X; Lithium/Amiodarone/Myco X

### Viva One-Liners
- "Valproate = Contraindicated in pregnancy — NTD 1-2%, Autism/ID; PPP mandatory; Switch to Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam + FA 5mg"
- "Warfarin 1st tri = Chondrodysplasia; 2nd/3rd = CNS bleed; Mech mitral = LMWH 1st, Warfarin 2nd/3rd"
- "ACEi/ARB = Contraindicated 2nd/3rd tri — Fetal renal dysplasia, Oligohydramnios"
- "LMWH = Anticoagulant of choice in pregnancy — All trimesters, Anti-Xa monitoring"
- "Codeine breastfeeding = Avoid — CYP2D6 ultra-rapid → Infant morphine toxicity"
- "Lamotrigine pregnancy = Levels drop 50% → Monitor q4wk, Increase dose; Postpartum rapidly reduce"

### Ward-Case Discussion Points
- Pre-conception clinic: **Review ALL drugs** — Stop teratogens (Valproate, Warfarin, ACEi, Statins, Isotretinoin, Methotrexate, Mycophenolate)
- Valproate PPP: **Annual review, LARC contraception, Signed risk form**
- Pregnancy booking: **Folic acid 5mg if on AEDs**; **LMWH if high VTE risk**
- Antenatal: **Stop NSAIDs 3rd tri**; **Monitor Lamotrigine levels q4wk**
- Delivery: **Stop LMWH 24h before elective LSCS/Induction**; **Restart 6-12h post-delivery if haemostasis**
- Postpartum: **Breastfeeding support** — Hale L1/L2 drugs compatible; **Restart teratogens if needed**

## Summary
Pregnancy prescribing uses **FDA categories (historical) or UK SmPC narrative labelling**. **Critical periods**: Neural tube (Wk 3-4), Heart (Wk 3-7), Limbs (Wk 4-7), Palate (Wk 6-8), Brain (Throughout). **Major teratogens (Avoid)**: Valproate (NTD 1-2%, Neurodev), Warfarin 1st tri (Chondrodysplasia), ACEi/ARB 2nd/3rd (Renal dysplasia), Lithium (Ebstein's), Isotretinoin (Multi-organ), Methotrexate/Thalidomide/Mycophenolate/Statins (Category X). **Safe choices**: HTN→Labetalol/Methyldopa/Nifedipine; Anticoag→LMWH all trimesters; DM→Insulin/Metformin; Epilepsy→Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam + FA 5mg; Depression→Sertraline/Fluoxetine. **Lactation**: Hale L1/L2 compatible; Codeine avoid (CYP2D6 ultra-rapid); Lithium/Amiodarone/Mycophenolate contraindicated.

## Answer Key with Explanations

### MCQs
1. **B** — Isotretinoin = Category X (Contraindicated); Metformin = B; Sertraline = C; Labetalol = C
2. **B** — Neural tube closes by Day 28 (Week 4)
3. **B** — Warfarin 1st tri = Warfarin embryopathy (Chondrodysplasia punctata, Nasal hypoplasia); 2nd/3rd = CNS bleeding
4. **B** — ACEi/ARB affect fetal renal development (2nd/3rd trimester)
5. **C** — Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam = Lowest teratogenicity; Valproate = Contraindicated
6. **B** — LMWH (Enoxaparin) = Standard for VTE treatment/prophylaxis in pregnancy; Anti-Xa monitoring
7. **B** — CYP2D6 ultra-rapid metabolisers convert codeine → morphine rapidly → Toxic levels in breast milk
8. **B** — Lamotrigine clearance ↑ in pregnancy (Induced UGT1A4, ↑ Vd) → Levels drop 50-60%
9. **C** — Labetalol/Methyldopa/Nifedipine MR = 1st line; ACEi/ARB/Diuretics/Spironolactone = Avoid
10. **B** — Valproate PPP = Annual specialist review + Highly effective contraception (LARC) + Signed risk acknowledgement form

### SBAs
1. **B** — Valproate contraindicated at any gestation; Immediate switch to safer AED + High-dose folic acid
2. **B** — Mechanical aortic: LMWH 1st tri, Warfarin 2nd/3rd (INR 2.5-3.5); Mitral: Warfarin throughout in some guidelines (Higher thrombotic risk)
3. **B** — Lithium = Ebstein's anomaly (Tricuspid valve dysplasia) in 1st trimester
4. **C** — ACEi contraindicated 2nd/3rd trimester (Fetal renal dysplasia)
5. **C** — Codeine avoid in breastfeeding (CYP2D6 ultra-rapid metabolisers → Infant morphine toxicity)
6. **B** — Isotretinoin = Category X; Severe multi-organ malformations (CNS, Cardiac, Thymus, Craniofacial)
7. **B** — Lamotrigine levels drop 50-60% in pregnancy → Monitor q4wk, Titrate up; Postpartum rapidly reduce
8. **B** — Sertraline = Most data, favourable profile; Fluoxetine also OK; Paroxetine avoid
9. **B** — Mechanical mitral valve 1st tri = **Therapeutic LMWH** (Warfarin embryopathy risk); 2nd/3rd = Warfarin
10. **D** — Mycophenolate = Category X (Contraindicated) — Ear, Palate, Limb, Eye defects
