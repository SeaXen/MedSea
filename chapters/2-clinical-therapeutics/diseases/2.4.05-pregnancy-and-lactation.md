## 2. 2. 🧠 Core Concept: PK/PD Changes in Pregnancy

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Pregnancy Physiological Changes] --> B[PK Changes]
    A --> C[PD Changes]
    
    B --> B1[↑ Plasma volume 40–50%
↓ Albumin → ↑ Vd (hydrophilic)
↑ Free fraction (protein-bound)]
    B --> B2[↑ GFR 50% → ↑ Renal clearance
↑ Creatinine clearance
↓ Drug levels: penicillins, atenolol, digoxin, lithium, gabapentin]
    B --> B3[↑ Hepatic blood flow
↑ CYP3A4, CYP2D6, CYP2C9
↑ UGT (glucuronidation)
↑ Clearance: lamotrigine, phenytoin, carbamazepine, methadone, buprenorphine]
    B --> B4[↓ Gastric motility / ↑ pH
↓ Absorption (some drugs)
↑ Bioavailability (others)]
    B --> B5[↑ Fat mass → ↑ Vd (lipophilic)
↑ Progesterone → ↓ GI transit]
    
    C --> C1[↓ Sensitivity to warfarin (↑ clotting factors)]
    C --> C2[↑ Insulin resistance → ↑ insulin requirement]
    C --> C3[↓ BP (vasodilation) → ↓ antihypertensive requirement 1st/2nd tri]
```

### 1. Critical Periods for Teratogenicity

| Period | Weeks | Sensitivity | Examples |
|--------|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Pre-implantation** | 0–2 (fertilisation to implantation) | **All-or-nothing** (embryo dies or survives unharmed) | High dose radiation, chemotherapy |
| **Organogenesis** | **3–8 weeks** (from LMP: 5–10 weeks) | **HIGHEST** — structural malformations | Thalidomide (limb), Isotretinoin (CNS/heart), Valproate (NTD), Warfarin (chondrodysplasia), ACEi (renal), Methotrexate (multiple) |
| **Fetal period** | 9 weeks – term | Growth restriction, functional deficits, neonatal toxicity | NSAIDs (premature ductus closure), ACEi (renal dysplasia), SSRIs (PPHN), Benzos (floppy infant), Opioids (NAS) |

> **Viva Key:** *Organogenesis 3–8 weeks post-conception = 5–10 weeks LMP. This is when structural teratogens act. After 8 weeks = functional/growth effects.*

## 3. 3. ️⃣ FDA Pregnancy Categories (Historical) & PLLR (Current)

### 1. Old FDA Categories (Still Widely Referenced in Exams)

| Category | Definition | Examples |
|----------|------------|----------|
| **A** | Adequate human studies show **no risk** | Levothyroxine, Folic acid, Pyridoxine, Insulin (human) |
| **B** | Animal studies no risk, **no human studies** OR animal risk but **human studies no risk** | Amoxicillin, Cephalosporins, Metformin, Paracetamol, Metoprolol, Heparin, LMWH |
| **C** | **Animal studies show risk**, no human studies OR no animal/human data | Fluoxetine, Sertraline, Amlodipine, Furosemide, Prednisolone, Codeine, Tramadol |
| **D** | **Human evidence of risk**, but benefits may outweigh (life-threatening) | **Valproate, Carbamazepine, Phenytoin, Warfarin, ACEi, ARB, DOACs, Statins, Tetracyclines, Sulfonamides (3rd tri), Lithium** |
| **X** | **Contraindicated** — risk > benefit | **Isotretinoin, Thalidomide, Misoprostol, Methotrexate, Ribavirin, Statins (some), Estradiol (high dose), Finasteride** |

> **Exam Note:** FCPS/MRCP still use old FDA categories. Know **D vs X**: D = may use if life-threatening (e.g., warfarin for mechanical valve); X = never use (e.g., isotretinoin, thalidomide).

### 2. New FDA PLLR (Pregnancy and Lactation Labelling Rule) — 2015+

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Pregnancy** | Risk summary, clinical considerations, data (human/animal) |
| **Lactation** | Drug in milk, infant effects, milk production effects |
| **Females/Males of Reproductive Potential** | Contraception, infertility, pregnancy testing |

**No letter categories** — narrative-based. But exam questions still use A/B/C/D/X.

## 4. 4. ️⃣ Major Teratogens (Contraindicated / Avoid) — HIGH YIELD

### 1. Absolute Contraindications (FDA X / Category D with No Alternative)

| Drug | Teratogenic Effect | Critical Period |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Isotretinoin** | **CNS (hydrocephalus, microcephaly), cardiac, thymic, craniofacial** | 3–8 weeks |
| **Thalidomide** | **Phocomelia** (limb reduction), ear/eye/heart defects | 4–7 weeks (limb bud) |
| **Methotrexate** | **Multiple**: craniofacial, limb, CNS, cardiac (methotrexate syndrome) | 6–8 weeks |
| **Ribavirin** | Multiple congenital anomalies | Throughout |
| **Mycophenolate** | **Ear, eye, limb, heart, oesophageal atresia** | Throughout |
| **Finasteride / Dutasteride** | **Hypospadias, ambiguous genitalia** (male fetus) | 8–14 weeks (genital differentiation) |

### 2. Major Teratogens (Category D — Use Only If No Alternative)

| Drug | Teratogenic Effect | Management in Pregnancy |
|------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Valproate** | **Neural tube defects (1–2%),** cardiac, craniofacial, developmental delay, autism (↑ dose) | **AVOID**; switch pre-conception to lamotrigine/levetiracetam; if pregnant: folic acid 5mg, lowest effective dose, monitor levels |
| **Carbamazepine** | **Neural tube defects (0.5–1%),** craniofacial, cardiac, hypospadias | Folic acid 5mg; lowest dose; TDM; consider switch to lamotrigine/levetiracetam |
| **Phenytoin** | **Fetal hydantoin syndrome:** craniofacial, limb, cardiac, NTD, growth restriction | Folic acid 5mg; TDM (FREE level); avoid if possible |
| **Warfarin** | **Warfarin embryopathy (6–12%):** chondrodysplasia punctata (stippled epiphyses), nasal hypoplasia, CNS, eye; **Fetal intracranial haemorrhage** | **Switch to LMWH** pre-conception or ASAP; if mechanical mitral valve: LMWH 1st tri, warfarin 2nd/3rd tri with close INR, switch back LMWH 36w |
| **ACEi / ARB** | **Fetal renal dysplasia, oligohydramnios, pulmonary hypoplasia, skull hypoplasia (2nd/3rd tri)** | **STOP immediately** if pregnancy confirmed; switch to methyldopa/labetalol/nifedipine |
| **DOACs** | Limited data; animal fetal bleeding | **Contraindicated**; switch to LMWH |
| **Statins** | Theoretical (cholesterol needed for fetal development); limited human data | **Contraindicated**; stop pre-conception |
| **Lithium** | **Ebstein's anomaly (0.1–0.2%)** — tricuspid valve dysplasia; neonatal toxicity (floppy, hypothyroidism, diabetes insipidus) | **Avoid 1st tri if possible**; if essential: lowest dose, folic acid, fetal echo 18–20w, monitor maternal levels |
| **Tetracyclines** | **Tooth discoloration, enamel hypoplasia, bone growth inhibition** (2nd/3rd tri) | **Avoid after 12 weeks**; doxycycline short course <1st tri if essential |
| **Sulfonamides / TMP-SMX** | **Kernicterus** (displace bilirubin) — 3rd trimester; NTD risk (folate antagonism) 1st tri | **Avoid 1st tri (folate antagonism)** and **3rd tri (kernicterus)** |
| **Fluoroquinolones** | **Cartilage damage** (animal) — arthropathy | **Avoid**; use alternatives |
| **Aminoglycosides** | **Ototoxicity** (vestibular > auditory) — 8th cranial nerve | **Avoid if possible**; if essential: monitor hearing, shortest course |

## 5. 5. ️⃣ Safe Drugs in Pregnancy (Common Indications)

### 1. Hypertension

| Preferred | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|
| **Methyldopa** (1st line, most evidence) | ACEi / ARB (D) |
| **Labetalol** (1st line, β+α) | Thiazides (C — reduce placental perfusion) |
| **Nifedipine MR** (CCB, 2nd line) | Spironolactone (anti-androgen) |
| **Hydralazine** (acute severe) | Atenolol (↓ fetal growth — avoid 1st line) |
| **Metoprolol** (if β-blocker needed) | |

> **Viva Key:** *Methyldopa & Labetalol = 1st line. Nifedipine = 2nd line. ACEi/ARB = STOP immediately (renal dysplasia). Thiazides = reduce placental perfusion.*

### 2. Anticoagulation (AF, VTE, Mechanical Valve)

| Indication | Regimen |
|------------|---------|
| **VTE treatment / prophylaxis** | **LMWH (enoxaparin/dalteparin)** throughout — **preferred** (no placental transfer, no fetal bleeding) |
| **AF (CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥2)** | **LMWH** throughout; or warfarin 2nd/3rd tri if high risk |
| **Mechanical mitral valve** | **LMWH 1st trimester**, Warfarin 2nd/3rd (if INR control good), **LMWH last 4 weeks** |
| **Mechanical aortic valve** | LMWH throughout (or warfarin 2nd/3rd if high thromboembolic risk) |
| **Warfarin dose >5mg/day** | Consider LMWH throughout (↑ embryopathy risk) |

### 3. Epilepsy

| Preferred | Avoid / Caution |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Lamotrigine** (↑ clearance in pregnancy → TDM dose ↑) | **Valproate** (D — NTD, neurodevelopmental) |
| **Levetiracetam** (↑ clearance → TDM dose ↑) | **Carbamazepine** (D — NTD, enzyme induction → vit K deficiency) |
| **Topiramate** (C — cleft lip/palate, growth restriction) | **Phenytoin** (D — fetal hydantoin syndrome) |
| **Folic acid 5mg daily** pre-conception & 1st tri for ALL | **Phenobarbital** (D — similar to phenytoin) |
| **Vitamin K 10mg IM at birth** (if on enzyme inducers) | |

### 4. Diabetes

| Preferred | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|
| **Insulin** (human, aspart, lispro, detemir, glargine) — **Gold standard** | **Metformin** (B — but crosses placenta; used in PCOS/GDM but insulin preferred for T1DM/T2DM) |
| **Metformin** (B — increasingly used in GDM, PCOS) | **Sulfonylureas** (C/D — glibenclamide crosses placenta; gliclazide less) |
| | **SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA, DPP-4, Pioglitazone** — insufficient data (avoid) |

### 5. Asthma

| Preferred | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|
| **SABA (salbutamol)** — reliever | **None** — uncontrolled asthma > drug risk |
| **ICS (budesonide > beclomethasone > fluticasone)** — controller | |
| **LABA (salmeterol/formoterol)** — add-on | |
| **LTRA (montelukast)** — add-on | |
| **Theophylline** — narrow TI, monitor | |
| **Oral steroids** — if severe (prednisolone) | |

### 6. Depression / Anxiety

| Preferred | Avoid / Caution |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Sertraline** (SSRI, most data) | **Paroxetine** (D — cardiac malformations) |
| **Fluoxetine** (SSRI, long half-life) | **Venlafaxine** (C — hypertension, neonatal withdrawal) |
| **Citalopram/Escitalopram** (C — QT) | **TCAs** (C — anticholinergic, cardiac) |
| **Mirtazapine** (C — limited data) | **MAOIs** — avoid |
| **CBT / psychological therapy** — 1st line mild-moderate | **Benzodiazepines** (D — floppy infant, withdrawal, cleft palate?) |

> **Neonatal Adaptation Syndrome (SSRI 3rd tri):** Jitteriness, irritability, feeding difficulty, respiratory distress, hypoglycaemia — self-limiting 2–14 days. **Not** neonatal withdrawal.

### 7. Infections — Antibiotics

| Safe (B) | Caution (C) | Avoid (D/X) |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Penicillins (amoxicillin, flucloxacillin, co-amoxiclav) | Macrolides (erythromycin, clarithromycin) — QT, hepatotoxicity | Tetracyclines (D) |
| Cephalosporins (cefalexin, cefuroxime, ceftriaxone) | Metronidazole (C — avoid 1st tri if possible; short course OK) | Fluoroquinolones (C/D) |
| Carbapenems (meropenem) | Nitrofurantoin (C — avoid near term: haemolytic anaemia G6PD) | Aminoglycosides (D — ototoxicity) |
| Azithromycin (B — preferred macrolide) | | Sulfonamides/TMP-SMX (D 1st/3rd tri) |
| Clindamycin (B) | | Rifampicin (C — hepatotoxicity, enzyme induction) |
| Vancomycin (B) | | |

## 6. 6. ️⃣ Lactation Prescribing

### 1. Lactation Risk Categories (Hale's Medication & Mothers' Milk)

| Category | Definition | Examples |
|----------|------------|----------|
| **L1 (Safest)** | No risk in controlled studies | Paracetamol, Ibuprofen, Penicillins, Cephalosporins, Insulin, Heparin, LMWH, Levothyroxine, Folic acid |
| **L2 (Safer)** | Limited studies, no adverse effects | Metformin, Sertraline, Labetalol, Nifedipine, Atenolol, Metoprolol, Azithromycin, Clarithromycin |
| **L3 (Moderate Risk)** | No controlled studies, potential risk | Fluoxetine, Citalopram, Venlafaxine, Carbamazepine, Phenytoin, Valproate (monitor infant), Warfarin, Prednisolone, Codeine |
| **L4 (Possibly Hazardous)** | Evidence of risk | **Diazepam** (sedation), **Lithium** (↑ infant levels), **Amiodarone** (iodine), **Gold**, **Methotrexate** |
| **L5 (Contraindicated)** | Significant risk | **Cocaine, Heroin, Radioactives, Cancer chemo, Ergotamine, Bromocriptine (suppresses lactation)** |

### 2. Infant Dose via Milk — Relative Infant Dose (RID)
```
RID (%) = (Infant dose via milk / Maternal weight-adjusted dose) × 100
```
- **RID <10%** = Generally safe (most drugs)
- **RID >10%** = Caution (lithium ~50%, diazepam ~10%, amiodarone high iodine)
- **Premature/neonate** — lower threshold (immature metabolism)

### 3. Drugs to AVOID in Breastfeeding

| Drug | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Lithium** | Excreted in milk (RID ~50%); infant toxicity (hypotonia, hypothyroidism, renal) |
| **Amiodarone** | High iodine in milk → infant thyroid dysfunction |
| **Diazepam / Long-acting benzos** | Sedation, poor feeding, weight loss (accumulation) |
| **Codeine** | Ultra-rapid metabolisers (CYP2D6) → morphine → infant respiratory depression |
| **Aspirin** | Reye's syndrome risk (viral illness) |
| **Radioisotopes / Chemo** | Direct toxicity |
| **Ergotamine / Bromocriptine / Cabergoline** | Suppress lactation |
| **High-dose iodine** | Infant thyroid suppression |

### 4. Preferred Alternatives for Breastfeeding

| Condition | Avoid | Preferred |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Analgesia | Codeine, Aspirin | **Paracetamol, Ibuprofen** (L1) |
| Depression | Fluoxetine (long half-life) | **Sertraline, Paroxetine** (low milk transfer) |
| Anticoagulation | Warfarin (monitor infant INR) | **LMWH, Heparin** (no milk transfer) |
| Hypertension | Atenolol (high milk transfer) | **Labetalol, Nifedipine, Methyldopa** |
| Epilepsy | Valproate (monitor infant LFTs/blood) | **Lamotrigine, Levetiracetam** (monitor infant levels) |
| Contraception | Combined OCP (↓ milk supply) | **Progesterone-only (POP, implant, IUS), Copper IUD** |

## 7. 7. ️⃣ Pre-Conception Prescribing

### 1. For All Women of Childbearing Potential

| Action | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Medication review** | Apply STOPP/START; identify teratogens (D/X) |
| **Folic acid 400μg daily** | **5mg if**: epilepsy (AEDs), diabetes, BMI >30, previous NTD, family history, on folate antagonists (methotrexate, sulfonamides, TMP-SMX) |
| **Switch teratogens pre-conception** | Valproate → lamotrigine/levetiracetam; Warfarin → LMWH; ACEi/ARB → methyldopa/labetalol; Statins → stop; Isotretinoin → stop 1 month before |
| **Rubella/Varicella immunity** | Vaccinate if non-immune (live vaccines — avoid pregnancy 1 month) |
| **Thyroid** | TSH <2.5 mU/L pre-conception; levothyroxine dose ↑ 25–30% in pregnancy |
| **Diabetes** | HbA1c <48 mmol/mol (6.5%) pre-conception; insulin optimisation |
| **Epilepsy** | Monotherapy if possible; lowest effective dose; folic acid 5mg; TDM baseline |

## 8. 8. ️⃣ Specific High-Yield Clinical Scenarios

### 1. Scenario 1: Woman on Valproate for Epilepsy Becomes Pregnant
1. **Do NOT stop abruptly** — seizure risk > teratogen risk
2. **Urgent neurology review** — consider switch to lamotrigine/levetiracetam (slow cross-taper)
3. **Folic acid 5mg daily** immediately
4. **TDM** — valproate levels (↑ clearance in pregnancy)
5. **Fetal anomaly scan** (18–20w) + fetal echo
6. **Vitamin K 10mg IM** at birth (enzyme induction → vit K deficiency)

### 2. Scenario 2: Woman on Warfarin for Mechanical Mitral Valve Plans Pregnancy
1. **Pre-conception**: Switch to **therapeutic LMWH** (enoxaparin 1mg/kg BD)
2. **If pregnant on warfarin**: **Switch to LMWH immediately** (1st tri = highest embryopathy risk)
3. **2nd/3rd trimester**: Can continue LMWH OR switch to warfarin (if INR stable, dose ≤5mg) with **close INR monitoring**
4. **36 weeks**: Switch back to LMWH (reduce intracranial haemorrhage risk at delivery)
5. **Postpartum**: Restart warfarin + LMWH bridging

### 3. Scenario 3: Hypertensive Woman Becomes Pregnant on Ramipril
1. **STOP ACEi immediately** (renal dysplasia risk 2nd/3rd tri)
2. **Switch to methyldopa or labetalol** (1st line)
3. **Monitor BP weekly**; target <140/90 (or <135/85 if end-organ damage)
4. **Aspirin 75–150mg OD from 12 weeks** (pre-eclampsia prophylaxis if risk factors)

### 4. Scenario 4: Breastfeeding Mother Needs Antidepressant
1. **Sertraline** — lowest milk transfer (RID <1%), most safety data
2. **Paroxetine** — low milk transfer, short half-life
3. **Avoid fluoxetine** — long half-life, active metabolite, higher infant levels
4. **Monitor infant**: drowsiness, feeding, weight gain

## 11. 11. 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"Category B = safe"** | B = **animal studies no risk, no adequate human studies** OR animal risk but human studies no risk. "Safe" based on indirect evidence. |
| **"Category C = unsafe"** | C = **animal risk, no human data** OR no data. Many C drugs used if benefit > risk (e.g., fluoxetine, amlodipine). |
| **"All statins Category X"** | Some statins Category X (simvastatin, atorvastatin), some Category D. **All contraindicated in pregnancy** — cholesterol needed for fetal development. |
| **"Warfarin safe in 2nd/3rd trimester"** | Warfarin = **CNS defects (microcephaly, Dandy-Walker), fetal ICH** throughout. 1st tri = embryopathy. 2nd/3rd = CNS/bleeding. **Only use if mechanical mitral valve + dose ≤5mg + good INR control.** |
| **"Metformin contraindicated in pregnancy"** | **Category B** — NOT contraindicated. Used in GDM, PCOS. Crosses placenta but no teratogenicity. Insulin preferred for T1DM/T2DM. |
| **"Breastfeeding safe for all drugs"** | **No** — lithium, amiodarone, diazepam, codeine, aspirin, chemo, radioisotopes contraindicated. Check Hale's L1–L5. |
| **"Combined OCP OK in breastfeeding"** | **Oestrogen ↓ milk supply**. Use progesterone-only (POP, implant, IUS) or copper IUD. |
| **"Tetracyclines OK in 1st trimester"** | **Avoid ALL trimesters** — tooth/bone effects from 2nd tri, but also hepatotoxicity maternal. Doxycycline short course <1st tri if essential (e.g., rickettsial). |
| **"Vaccines contraindicated in pregnancy"** | **Inactivated vaccines SAFE & RECOMMENDED** (flu, pertussis/Tdap, COVID). **Live vaccines contraindicated** (MMR, varicella, yellow fever, BCG, LAIV). |

> **R**eview meds pre-conception: Switch D/X teratogens  
> **E**pilepsy: **Valproate AVOID** → Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam; Folic acid **5mg**  
> **G**estational HTN: **Methyldopa/Labetalol 1st line**; Nifedipine 2nd; **ACEi/ARB STOP**  
> **N**o NSAIDs 3rd tri (ductus closure); No tetracyclines (teeth); No fluoroquinolones (cartilage)

> **A**void L4/L5: Lithium, Amiodarone, Diazepam, Codeine, Aspirin, Combined OCP  
> **C**onception: Folic acid 400μg (5mg if AED/DM/BMI>30/NTD hx); Rubella/varicella immunity; TSH<2.5; HbA1c<48  
> **T**DM in pregnancy: Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam/Valproate/CBZ/PHT ↑ clearance → TDM dose ↑  
> **I**nsulin: Gold standard DM; Metformin B (GDM/PCOS); Avoid SGLT2i/GLP-1/DPP-4/Pio  
> **N**eonatal Vit K: 10mg IM if mother on enzyme inducers (CBZ, PHT, PB, Rifampicin)  
> **G**uidelines: BNF, UKTIS, LactMed, RCOG, FDA PLLR

## 16. 16. 📌 Summary
- **Critical period**: Organogenesis 3–8 weeks (5–10 weeks LMP) = highest structural teratogen risk
- **FDA Categories**: A (safe), B (animal safe), C (animal risk), D (human risk, may use if benefit>risk), X (contraindicated)
- **Category X (avoid always)**: Isotretinoin, Thalidomide, Methotrexate, Ribavirin, Mycophenolate, Finasteride
- **Category D (major teratogens)**: Valproate (NTD, neuro), Carbamazepine/Phenytoin (NTD, syndromes), Warfarin (chondrodysplasia, ICH), ACEi/ARB (renal dysplasia), DOACs, Statins, Lithium (Ebstein's), Tetracyclines (teeth/bone), Sulfonamides (kernicterus 3rd tri), Fluoroquinolones (cartilage), Aminoglycosides (ototoxicity)
- **HTN**: Methyldopa/Labetalol 1st line; Nifedipine 2nd; ACEi/ARB/Thiazides/Atenolol avoid
- **Anticoagulation**: LMWH throughout for VTE/AF; Mechanical valve: LMWH 1st tri, Warfarin 2nd/3rd (if ≤5mg), LMWH 36w
- **Epilepsy**: Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam preferred; Valproate avoid; Folic acid 5mg; TDM (↑ clearance); Vit K at birth if enzyme inducers
- **Diabetes**: Insulin gold standard; Metformin B (GDM); Others avoid
- **Depression**: Sertraline preferred; Paroxetine avoid (cardiac); Neonatal adaptation syndrome (self-limiting)
- **Antibiotics**: Safe — Penicillins, Cephalosporins, Carbapenems, Azithromycin, Clindamycin, Vancomycin; Avoid — Tetracyclines, Fluoroquinolones, Aminoglycosides, Sulfonamides
- **Lactation**: L1/L2 safe (paracetamol, ibuprofen, penicillins, sertraline, labetalol, LMWH); Avoid L4/L5: Lithium, Amiodarone, Diazepam, Codeine, Aspirin, Combined OCP
- **Pre-conception**: Folic acid 400μg (5mg high risk); Switch teratogens; Immunity; TSH<2.5; HbA1c<48

## 17. 17. ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **Critical period for structural teratogenicity:**  
   A. 0–2 weeks  B. **3–8 weeks**  C. 9–12 weeks  D. 2nd trimester  
   *Answer: B. Organogenesis 3–8 weeks post-conception (5–10 weeks LMP).*

2. **FDA Category X drug (contraindicated in pregnancy):**  
   A. Warfarin  B. Valproate  C. **Isotretinoin**  D. Lithium  
   *Answer: C. Isotretinoin = Category X. Warfarin/Valproate/Lithium = Category D.*

3. **First-line antihypertensive in pregnancy:**  
   A. Ramipril  B. **Methyldopa**  C. Atenolol  D. Hydrochlorothiazide  
   *Answer: B. Methyldopa (most evidence) and Labetalol are 1st line. ACEi/ARB contraindicated.*

4. **Anticoagulation for mechanical mitral valve in pregnancy:**  
   A. Warfarin throughout  B. DOAC throughout  C. **LMWH 1st tri, Warfarin 2nd/3rd (if dose≤5mg), LMWH 36w**  D. Aspirin only  
   *Answer: C. Standard regimen. Or LMWH throughout.*

5. **Preferred antiepileptic in pregnancy:**  
   A. Valproate  B. Carbamazepine  C. **Lamotrigine**  D. Phenytoin  
   *Answer: C. Lamotrigine (and Levetiracetam) preferred. Valproate/CBZ/PHT = Category D teratogens.*

6. **Folic acid dose for woman on antiepileptics planning pregnancy:**  
   A. 400μg  B. **5mg**  C. 1mg  D. 10mg  
   *Answer: B. 5mg daily for high-risk (AEDs, diabetes, BMI>30, previous NTD, family history, folate antagonists).*

7. **SSRI with most safety data in pregnancy:**  
   A. Paroxetine  B. Fluoxetine  C. **Sertraline**  D. Citalopram  
   *Answer: C. Sertraline preferred. Paroxetine = Category D (cardiac malformations).*

8. **Antibiotic contraindicated in pregnancy (Category D):**  
   A. Amoxicillin  B. Cephalexin  C. **Doxycycline**  D. Azithromycin  
   *Answer: C. Tetracyclines = Category D (tooth discoloration, bone growth inhibition).*

9. **Drug to AVOID in breastfeeding (Hale L4/L5):**  
   A. Paracetamol  B. Ibuprofen  C. **Lithium**  D. Sertraline  
   *Answer: C. Lithium = L4 (high milk transfer, infant toxicity). Paracetamol/Ibuprofen/Sertraline = L1/L2.*

10. **Neonatal adaptation syndrome caused by:**  
    A. Benzodiazepines  B. **SSRIs (3rd trimester)**  C. Opioids  D. Beta-blockers  
    *Answer: B. SSRIs in 3rd trimester → jitteriness, irritability, feeding difficulty, respiratory distress, hypoglycaemia. Self-limiting 2–14 days.*

## 18. 18. 📋 SBAs (10)

1. **32F with mechanical mitral valve (ON-X) on warfarin 4mg/day plans pregnancy. Best management?**  
   A. Continue warfarin throughout  B. **Switch to therapeutic LMWH pre-conception**  C. Switch to apixaban  D. Stop anticoagulation  
   *Answer: B. Pre-conception switch to LMWH. Warfarin embryopathy risk 6–12% in 1st trimester.*

2. **26F on valproate 1000mg for juvenile myoclonic epilepsy discovers she's 6 weeks pregnant. Immediate action?**  
   A. Stop valproate immediately  B. **Continue valproate; urgent neurology referral; start folic acid 5mg**  C. Switch to carbamazepine  D. Reduce valproate by 50%  
   *Answer: B. Do NOT stop abruptly (seizure risk). Urgent specialist review for cross-taper to LTG/LEV. Folic acid 5mg immediately.*

3. **Pregnant woman at 28 weeks with UTI. E. coli sensitive to all. Best antibiotic?**  
   A. Ciprofloxacin  B. **Cefalexin**  C. Nitrofurantoin  D. TMP-SMX  
   *Answer: B. Cephalosporins (cefalexin) = Category B, safe. Ciprofloxacin = avoid (cartilage). Nitrofurantoin = avoid near term (haemolysis). TMP-SMX = avoid 3rd tri (kernicterus).*

4. **Breastfeeding mother needs analgesia for mastitis. Safe choice?**  
   A. Codeine  B. Aspirin  C. **Ibuprofen**  D. Diazepam  
   *Answer: C. Ibuprofen = L1 (safe). Codeine = risk respiratory depression. Aspirin = Reye's. Diazepam = sedation.*

5. **Woman on lithium 600mg for bipolar disorder becomes pregnant. Fetal risk?**  
   A. Neural tube defects  B. **Ebstein's anomaly (tricuspid dysplasia)**  C. Limb reduction  D. Renal dysplasia  
   *Answer: B. Lithium = Ebstein's anomaly (0.1–0.2%). Valproate/CBZ/PHT = NTD. Warfarin = chondrodysplasia. ACEi = renal dysplasia.*

6. **Folic acid 5mg indicated in all EXCEPT:**  
   A. Epilepsy on carbamazepine  B. BMI 32  C. **Previous uncomplicated pregnancy**  D. Diabetes  
   *Answer: C. High-risk indications: AEDs, diabetes, BMI>30, previous NTD, family history, folate antagonists. Previous uncomplicated pregnancy = standard 400μg.*

7. **ACE inhibitor fetopathy features:**  
   A. Chondrodysplasia punctata  B. **Renal dysplasia, oligohydramnios, pulmonary hypoplasia**  C. Ebstein's anomaly  D. Neural tube defects  
   *Answer: B. ACEi/ARB 2nd/3rd tri → fetal renal dysplasia → oligohydramnios → pulmonary hypoplasia, skull hypoplasia.*

8. **Lamotrigine in pregnancy — dose adjustment?**  
   A. No change  B. **Increase dose (↑ clearance 2–3x); TDM guided**  C. Decrease dose  D. Stop  
   *Answer: B. Lamotrigine clearance ↑ 2–3x in pregnancy (↑ UGT). TDM essential; dose often doubled.*

9. **Combined oral contraceptive in breastfeeding — effect?**  
   A. Increases milk supply  B. **Decreases milk supply (oestrogen)**  C. No effect  D. Causes infant thrombosis  
   *Answer: B. Oestrogen suppresses prolactin → ↓ milk supply. Use progesterone-only methods.*

10. **Warfarin embryopathy — characteristic feature:**  
    A. **Chondrodysplasia punctata (stippled epiphyses)**  B. Ebstein's anomaly  C. Neural tube defects  D. Renal dysplasia  
    *Answer: A. Warfarin embryopathy = chondrodysplasia punctata, nasal hypoplasia, CNS/eye defects. Ebstein's = lithium. NTD = valproate/CBZ/PHT. Renal dysplasia = ACEi.*
