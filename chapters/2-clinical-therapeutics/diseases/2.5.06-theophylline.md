**Status:** `full-fcps-mrcp-note`
**Priority:** ⭐⭐ HIGH (FCPS/MRCP — narrow TI for asthma/COPD, CYP1A2 metabolism, smoking/age/drug interactions, toxicity: seizures/arrhythmias)

## 🧠 Core Concept: Theophylline Pharmacokinetics

### Key PK Properties

| Property | Value | Clinical Implication |
|----------|-------|---------------------|
| **Therapeutic index** | **Narrow** (toxic >20 mg/L) | TDM essential |
| **Absorption (oral)** | 100% (IR); SR variable (food effects) | Take on empty stomach (IR); SR with/without food per brand |
| **Distribution** | Vd 0.45–0.5 L/kg (TBW) | Dose by ideal/actual BW |
| **Protein binding** | **Low (40–60%)** | Minimal displacement interactions |
| **Metabolism** | **Hepatic: CYP1A2 (major), CYP2E1, CYP3A4** | **Major interaction target**; saturable at high doses |
| **Elimination** | Renal ~10% unchanged; hepatic ~90% | Renal impairment → minor dose reduction |
| **Half-life** | **Highly variable**: 3–9h (adult non-smoker); 2–3h (smoker); 24h+ (HF/liver disease) | Clearance = primary determinant |
| **Clearance** | **Clearance-based dosing** (mL/kg/h) | **Not dose-proportional** at high levels |

> **Key Principle:** *Theophylline clearance is **CYP1A2-dependent** and highly variable. **Smoking induces** (↓ t½ to 2–3h). **Heart failure, liver disease, elderly, CYP1A2 inhibitors** ↓ clearance (↑ t½ to 24h+).*

## 1️⃣ Therapeutic Ranges

| Indication | Target Range (mg/L = μg/mL) | Notes |
|------------|-----------------------------|-------|
| **Acute Asthma** | **10–20** | Higher for severe exacerbation |
| **Chronic Asthma** | **10–20** | Symptom control |
| **COPD (stable)** | **10–20** (some guidelines **5–10**) | Lower for maintenance; ↑ toxicity risk in elderly |
| **Apnoea of Prematurity** | **6–13** | Neonatal dosing different |
| **Toxic Threshold** | **>20** | Adverse effects increase sharply |

> **Viva Key:** *Standard therapeutic range **10–20 mg/L**. **COPD maintenance often 5–10** (lower efficacy needed, ↑ toxicity risk in elderly). Toxic >20.*

## 2️⃣ Clearance-Based Dosing —(Key to Theophylline)

### Clearance Estimates by Population

| Population | Clearance (mL/kg/h) | Typical Dose (mg/kg/day) | Half-life |
|------------|---------------------|--------------------------|-----------|
| **Adult non-smoker** | **0.7** | 0.7 × 15 = **~10.5 mg/kg/day** | 6–9h |
| **Adult smoker** | **1.5** | 1.5 × 15 = **~22.5 mg/kg/day** | 2–3h |
| **Elderly (>65)** | **0.4–0.5** | 0.5 × 15 = **~7.5 mg/kg/day** | 8–12h |
| **Heart Failure (NYHA III/IV)** | **0.3–0.4** | 0.35 × 15 = **~5 mg/kg/day** | 12–24h |
| **Liver Disease (cirrhosis)** | **0.3–0.4** | 0.35 × 15 = **~5 mg/kg/day** | 12–24h |
| **Viral illness (children)** | **0.3–0.5** | Reduced | Prolonged |
| **Neonate (preterm)** | **0.05–0.1** | 0.08 × 15 = **~1.2 mg/kg/day** | 20–30h |

### Dosing Formula (Steady-State Maintenance)
```
Dose (mg/day) = Target Css (mg/L) × Clearance (mL/kg/h) × Weight (kg) × 24 / 1000
```
**Simplified**: For target 15 mg/L:
```
Dose (mg/kg/day) ≈ Clearance (mL/kg/h) × 15 × 24 / 1000 ≈ Clearance × 0.36
```

### Practical Starting Doses (mg/kg/day) — IR vs SR

| Population | Aminophylline IV (loading) | Oral IR (BD) | Oral SR (OD/BD) |
|------------|----------------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Adult non-smoker** | 5 mg/kg over 20 min | 300 mg BD | 400 mg BD |
| **Adult smoker** | 5 mg/kg over 20 min | 400 mg BD | 600 mg BD |
| **Elderly / HF / Liver disease** | **2.5 mg/kg** over 30 min | 200 mg BD | 200–300 mg BD |
| **Paediatric >1yr** | 5 mg/kg | 12–16 mg/kg/day | 12–16 mg/kg/day |

> **Loading dose (IV aminophylline)**: 5 mg/kg (non-smoker) / 2.5 mg/kg (elderly/HF/liver) over 20–30 min. **No loading if on oral theophylline** (risk of toxicity).

## 3️⃣ Sampling Time & Steady State

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Steady state** | **5 half-lives** = 24–48h (adult); 3–5 days (elderly/HF) |
| **Sampling time** | **Trough (pre-dose)** for BD dosing; **pre-dose** for OD SR |
| **When to check** | After 3 days at stable dose (or 24h post-IV load) |
| **Repeat after dose change** | 3–5 days (5 half-lives at new clearance) |

> **Do NOT** check random level. **Trough = pre-dose** (just before next dose).

## 4️⃣ Major Drug Interactions — CYP1A2 Focus

### CYP1A2 Inhibitors (↑ Theophylline Levels → Toxicity)

| Drug | Effect on Clearance | Dose Adjustment |
|------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Fluvoxamine** | ↓ **80–90%** (strongest) | **Avoid**; if essential → ↓ theophylline **75–90%** |
| **Ciprofloxacin** | ↓ **30–50%** | **Halve dose**; monitor level |
| **Enoxacin / Norfloxacin** | ↓ 30–50% | Similar to ciprofloxacin |
| **Macrolides** (clarithromycin, erythromycin) | ↓ 20–30% | Monitor level; consider alternative |
| **Cimetidine** | ↓ 20–30% | Monitor; ranitidine/famotidine preferred |
| **Allopurinol** | ↓ 20–30% | Monitor |
| **Oral contraceptives** | ↓ 20–30% | Monitor |
| **Ticlopidine** | ↓ 20–30% | Monitor |
| **Interferon-α** | ↓ 30–50% | Halve dose |

### CYP1A2 Inducers (↓ Theophylline Levels → Loss of Control)

| Drug | Effect on Clearance | Dose Adjustment |
|------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Smoking (cigarettes)** | ↑ **50–100%** (2x) | **Double dose**; monitor level |
| **Marijuana** | ↑ Similar to tobacco | Monitor |
| **Rifampicin** | ↑ 50–100% | Double dose; monitor |
| **Carbamazepine / Phenytoin / Phenobarbital** | ↑ 50–100% | Double dose; monitor |
| **St John's Wort** | ↑ 30–50% | Increase; monitor |
| **Charcoal-broiled food** (chronic) | ↑ Mild | Monitor |

### Other Interactions
| Drug | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| **Adenosine** | Theophylline **antagonises** adenosine (↑ dose needed for SVT) |
| **Beta-blockers** | Non-selective ↑ theophylline clearance slightly; theophylline ↓ beta-blocker effect |
| **Loop diuretics** | ↑ Hypokalaemia risk (theophylline ↓ K⁺) |
| **Corticosteroids** | Additive hypokalaemia; theophylline may ↑ steroid clearance |

## 5️⃣ Toxicity — Concentration-Dependent

| Level (mg/L) | Clinical Features |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **10–20** (Therapeutic) | Usually asymptomatic; mild GI upset, insomnia, tachycardia |
| **20–30** (Mild Toxic) | **Nausea, vomiting, tremor, agitation, tachycardia, hypokalaemia, hyperglycaemia** |
| **30–40** (Moderate Toxic) | **Seizures (tonic-clonic), ventricular arrhythmias, severe hypokalaemia, metabolic acidosis** |
| **>40** (Severe Toxic) | **Status epilepticus, VF, hypotension, rhabdomyolysis, death** |

### Chronic Toxicity (Levels 15–25 in elderly/sensitive)
- Insomnia, personality change, GI reflux, tremor, weight loss

### Key Toxicity Risk Factors
| Factor | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Elderly** | ↓ Clearance, ↑ sensitivity (CNS, cardiac) |
| **Heart Failure / Liver Disease** | ↓ Clearance dramatically |
| **Viral Illness** (esp. children) | ↓ Clearance |
| **CYP1A2 Inhibitors** | Drug interactions (see above) |
| **Smoking Cessation** | Clearance ↓ 50% over 1 week → toxicity if dose not reduced |

> **Viva Key:** *Theophylline toxicity = **GI (nausea), CNS (tremor, seizures), Cardiac (arrhythmias), Metabolic (hypokalaemia, hyperglycaemia)**. **Seizures at >30 mg/L often refractory to benzodiazepines** (adenosine antagonism).*

## 6️⃣ Management of Toxicity

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Theophylline Toxicity Suspected] --> B{Level & Severity}
    B -->|Level 20-30, Mild| C[Hold dose<br>Hydrate<br>Correct K⁺/Mg²⁺<br>Repeat level 24h]
    B -->|Level 30-40 / Seizures / Arrhythmias| D[**STOP theophylline**<br>**Activated charcoal** (if recent, MDAC)<br>IV fluids + KCl (hypokalaemia)<br>**Seizures: Benzodiazepines** (may be refractory)<br>**Arrhythmias: Standard ACLS**<br>**NO adenosine** (antagonised)<br>**NO beta-blockers** (worsen bronchospasm)<br>Consider **haemodialysis/haemoperfusion** if >40 or severe]
    B -->|Level >40 / Life-threatening| E[ICU<br>**Haemodialysis/haemoperfusion** (theophylline dialysable: low protein binding, small Vd)<br>Supportive care]
```

### Haemodialysis Indications
- Level **>40 mg/L** (acute)
- Level **>30 mg/L** with seizures/arrhythmias
- Severe symptoms unresponsive to supportive care
- **Theophylline is dialysable** (Vd 0.5 L/kg, 40% protein binding, low MW) — **HD effective**

## 7️⃣ Special Populations

### Elderly (>65)
- **Clearance ↓ 30–50%** (0.4–0.5 mL/kg/h)
- **Target 5–10 mg/L** for COPD maintenance
- **Start low**: 200 mg BD SR (or 300 mg OD SR)
- **Monitor**: Level at 3 days, then 3-monthly; renal function; drug interactions

### Heart Failure (NYHA III/IV)
- **Clearance ↓ 50–70%** (0.3–0.4 mL/kg/h) — ↓ hepatic perfusion
- **IV loading dose: 2.5 mg/kg** (not 5 mg/kg)
- **Maintenance: 200–300 mg/day** max
- **Monitor closely**: Daily levels if acute; renal function

### Liver Disease (Cirrhosis)
- **Clearance ↓ 50–70%** (0.3–0.4 mL/kg/h)
- Avoid if possible; if essential → **low dose, frequent levels**

### Pregnancy
- **Clearance ↑** (especially 3rd trimester) — may need ↑ dose
- **Category C** (crosses placenta; neonatal irritability, tachycardia at high levels)
- **Monitor levels monthly**; adjust dose

### Smoking
- **Current smoker**: Clearance ~1.5 mL/kg/h (2x non-smoker)
- **Ex-smoker (>1 week)**: Clearance returns to non-smoker over 1 week
- **Dose adjustment essential** on starting/stopping smoking

### Paediatrics
- **Children 1–9yr**: Higher clearance (1–1.5 mL/kg/h) → higher mg/kg doses
- **Viral illness**: Clearance ↓ dramatically → toxicity risk
- **Apnoea of prematurity**: Different dosing (see neonatal formularies)

## 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"Theophylline has wide therapeutic index"** | **NO.** **Narrow** — toxic >20 mg/L. TDM mandatory. |
| **"Peak level is best for monitoring"** | **NO.** **Trough (pre-dose)** at steady state. Peak varies with absorption. |
| **"Smoking only affects dose slightly"** | **NO.** **Smoking DOUBLES clearance** → dose must double. Smoking cessation → 50% dose reduction. |
| **"Ciprofloxacin safe with theophylline"** | **NO.** **Ciprofloxacin ↓ clearance 30–50%** → **halve theophylline dose**. Fluvoxamine ↓ 80–90% (avoid). |
| **"Theophylline seizures respond to diazepam"** | **Often REFRACTORY** (adenosine antagonism). May need propofol, phenobarbital, anaesthesia. |
| **"Beta-blockers safe with theophylline"** | Non-selective beta-blockers ↑ theophylline clearance slightly; theophylline ↓ beta-blocker effect. Avoid non-selective in asthma anyway. |
| **"Theophylline not dialysable"** | **YES dialysable** — low protein binding (40%), small Vd (0.5 L/kg), low MW. HD effective. |

> **Mnemonic: THEOPHYLLINE TDM**  
> **T**herapeutic: **10-20 mg/L** (asthma); **5-10** (COPD/elderly)  
> **H**alf-life: **Variable** — 3-9h (adult), 2-3h (smoker), 24h+ (HF/liver/elderly)  
> **E**nzyme: **CYP1A2** (major) — **Inhibitors ↑ levels, Inducers ↓ levels**  
> **O**ral: IR BD, SR BD; IV = aminophylline (loading 5mg/kg, 2.5mg/kg HF/elderly)  
> **P**eak NOT used — **Trough (pre-dose) at steady state (3-5 days)**  
> **H**ypokalaemia + **Hyperglycaemia** = metabolic toxicity  
> **Y**oung children: viral illness ↓ clearance → toxicity risk  
> **L**oading dose: **No load if on oral**; IV load 5mg/kg (2.5mg/kg HF/elderly)  
> **L**iver/HF/elderly: **Clearance 0.35 mL/kg/h** → dose ~5mg/kg/day; target 5-10  
> **I**nteractions: **Ciprofloxacin (halve dose), Fluvoxamine (avoid), Smoking (double dose)**  
> **N**euro: **Tremor → Seizures (>30)** — **refractory to benzos** (adenosine antagonism)  
> **E**x-smoker: **Clearance drops 50% in 1 week** → reduce dose 50%  
> **N**o adenosine for SVT on theophylline (antagonised)  
> **D**ialysis: **Effective** (low protein binding, small Vd) — >40 or severe neuro/cardiac  
> **M**onitoring: **Level 3-monthly stable**; after any interaction/illness/smoking change  
> **I**V aminophylline: **Monitor HR, ECG, K+**; extravasation → necrosis  
> **N**arrow TI — **TDM mandatory** for all indications  
> **E**lderly/COPD: **Lower target (5-10)** — less efficacy needed, more toxicity risk

## 📌 Summary
- **Therapeutic range**: **10–20 mg/L** (asthma/acute); **5–10 mg/L** (COPD maintenance, elderly)
- **Sampling**: **Trough (pre-dose)** at steady state (3 days adult; 5 days elderly/HF)
- **Clearance-based dosing**: Non-smoker **0.7**, Smoker **1.5**, Elderly/HF/Liver **0.35** mL/kg/h
- **CYP1A2 interactions**: **Inhibitors** — Fluvoxamine (avoid, ↓90%), **Ciprofloxacin (halve dose, ↓30–50%)**, macrolides, cimetidine, OCPs. **Inducers** — **Smoking (2x dose)**, rifampicin, anticonvulsants
- **Smoking cessation**: Clearance ↓ 50% over 1 week → **reduce theophylline 50%**
- **Toxicity**: GI (nausea), CNS (tremor → **seizures >30, refractory to benzos**), Cardiac (arrhythmias), Metabolic (hypokalaemia, hyperglycaemia)
- **Adenosine antagonism** — higher doses needed for SVT; avoid adenosine in theophylline toxicity
- **Special pops**: Elderly/HF/Liver — low dose, target 5–10; Pregnancy — clearance ↑; Children — viral illness ↓ clearance
- **Haemodialysis**: **Effective** (low protein binding, small Vd) — indicated level >40 or severe neuro/cardiac toxicity

## ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **Therapeutic theophylline range for acute asthma:**  
   A. 5–10 mg/L  B. **10–20 mg/L**  C. 20–30 mg/L  D. 2–5 mg/L  
   *Answer: B. 10–20 mg/L for asthma/acute. 5–10 for COPD maintenance/elderly.*

2. **Ciprofloxacin effect on theophylline:**  
   A. ↑ Clearance  B. **↓ Clearance 30–50%**  C. No effect  D. ↓ Clearance 80–90%  
   *Answer: B. Ciprofloxacin = CYP1A2 inhibitor → ↓ theophylline clearance 30–50%. Halve dose.*

3. **Theophylline clearance in adult cigarette smoker:**  
   A. 0.35 mL/kg/h  B. **1.5 mL/kg/h**  C. 0.7 mL/kg/h  D. 0.1 mL/kg/h  
   *Answer: B. Smoking induces CYP1A2 → clearance ~1.5 (2x non-smoker 0.7).*

4. **Theophylline seizure characteristic:**  
   A. Responds well to lorazepam  B. **Often refractory to benzodiazepines**  C. Only at >50 mg/L  D. Focal only  
   *Answer: B. Theophylline seizures often refractory to benzodiazepines (adenosine receptor antagonism).*

5. **Patient on theophylline stops smoking. Dose adjustment?**  
   A. Increase 50%  B. **Decrease 50%**  C. No change  D. Double dose  
   *Answer: B. Smoking cessation → CYP1A2 induction lost → clearance ↓ 50% over 1 week → reduce dose 50%.*

6. **Theophylline in decompensated heart failure — clearance:**  
   A. 1.5 mL/kg/h  B. 0.7 mL/kg/h  C. **0.3–0.4 mL/kg/h**  D. 2.0 mL/kg/h  
   *Answer: C. HF ↓ hepatic perfusion → clearance ↓ 50–70% (0.3–0.4 mL/kg/h).*

7. **Adenosine for SVT in patient on theophylline:**  
   A. Standard dose  B. **Higher doses needed (antagonised)**  C. Contraindicated  D. More effective  
   *Answer: B. Theophylline = adenosine receptor antagonist → antagonises adenosine effect.*

8. **Haemodialysis for theophylline removal — effective because:**  
   A. High protein binding  B. Large Vd  C. **Low protein binding, small Vd, low MW**  D. High lipid solubility  
   *Answer: C. Theophylline: 40% protein binding, Vd 0.5 L/kg, MW 180 — dialysable.*

9. **Fluvoxamine + theophylline — management:**  
   A. Halve theophylline  B. **Avoid combination**  C. No change  D. Double theophylline
