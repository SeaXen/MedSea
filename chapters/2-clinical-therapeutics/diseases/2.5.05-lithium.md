## 🧠 Core Concept: Lithium Pharmacokinetics

### Key PK Properties

| Property | Value | Clinical Implication |
|----------|-------|---------------------|
| **Therapeutic index** | **Very narrow** (toxic >1.0–1.2) | TDM mandatory |
| **Absorption** | ~100% (oral); slow-release = smoother levels | SR preferred for adherence/less peaks |
| **Distribution** | Vd ~0.6–0.9 L/kg (total body water) | ↑ in dehydration, elderly; not dialysable efficiently |
| **Protein binding** | None | No displacement interactions |
| **Metabolism** | **None** (excreted unchanged) | No CYP interactions |
| **Elimination** | **100% renal** (glomerular filtration + proximal reabsorption) | **CrCl = clearance**; Na⁺/volume status affects reabsorption |
| **Half-life** | **18–36h** (normal renal); **40–60h** (elderly/CKD) | Steady state: 4–7 days |
| **Clearance** | ~20–30% of CrCl (proximal reabsorption ~70–80%) | Parallels renal function |

> **Key Principle:** *Lithium is handled like sodium — proximal tubular reabsorption. **Volume depletion → ↑ reabsorption → ↑ levels**. **NSAIDs/ACEi/ARB/diuretics → ↓ renal perfusion → ↑ reabsorption → toxicity**.*

## 1️⃣ Therapeutic Ranges (Serum Lithium)

| Indication | Target Range (mmol/L) | Notes |
|------------|----------------------|-------|
| **Acute Mania** | **0.6–0.8** (some guidelines 0.8–1.0) | Higher end for acute control |
| **Maintenance / Prophylaxis** | **0.4–0.6** (some 0.4–0.8) | Lower = less toxicity, still effective |
| **Rapid Cycling / Refractory** | 0.6–0.8 | May need higher |
| **Elderly / Renal Impairment** | **0.4–0.6** (lower end) | ↑ Sensitivity, ↓ clearance |

> **Viva Key:** *Modern targets are **lower** than historical. Maintenance **0.4–0.6 mmol/L** is standard. Acute mania **0.6–0.8** (not routinely >1.0). Toxic >1.0–1.2.*

## 2️⃣ Sampling Time — CRITICAL

| Preparation | Sampling Time |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Standard release** (Camcolit, Priadel liquid) | **12h post-dose** (trough) |
| **Slow-release** (Priadel SR, Liskonum) | **12h post-dose** (trough) — same |
| **Why 12h?** | Lithium t½ ~24h → 12h = consistent trough; avoids peak variability |

> **Never** check random level. **Must be 12h post-dose** (±1h). If timing wrong → **repeat**. Document exact dose time and sample time.

## 3️⃣ Dosing & Renal Adjustment

### Initiation (Lithium-Naive)
| Step | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Baseline** | U&E, Cr, eGFR, TFT, Ca²⁺, TSH, ECG (>50 or cardiac hx), BMI, pregnancy test |
| **Starting dose** | **200–400mg daily** (SR preferred) or 200mg BD (standard) |
| **Titration** | Increase by 200–400mg weekly based on level |
| **Target level** | Check at **1 week** (steady state ~5 days), then weekly until stable |

### Renal Impairment — Dose Adjustment

| eGFR / CrCl (mL/min) | Dose Adjustment | Monitoring |
|----------------------|-----------------|------------|
| **>60** | Standard dosing | Level 12h post-dose weekly × 4, then 3-monthly |
| **45–60** | **Reduce dose ~25%** | Level weekly × 4, then 2-monthly; U&E 3-monthly |
| **30–45** | **Reduce dose ~50%** | Level 2-weekly × 4, then monthly; U&E 2-monthly |
| **15–30** | **Reduce dose ~75%** (100–200mg daily) | Level weekly; U&E monthly; consider specialist |
| **<15** | **Avoid if possible**; if essential → **minimal dose** (100mg daily/alternate) | Level weekly; U&E 2-weekly; nephrology input |

> **Rule of Thumb:** *Lithium clearance ≈ 20–30% of CrCl. Dose ∝ CrCl. **Halve dose if CrCl halved**.*

### Obesity
- **Vd ↑** (total body water) → loading may need higher
- **But clearance ∝ CrCl** (not weight) → maintenance by renal function
- Use **ideal body weight** for dose calculation if obese

## 4️⃣ Drug Interactions — Major (↑ Lithium Levels → Toxicity)

| Drug Class | Mechanism | Magnitude | Management |
|------------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Thiazide diuretics** | ↓ Na⁺ → ↑ proximal Li⁺ reabsorption | **↑ Level 25–50%** (most common serious interaction) | **Avoid if possible**; if essential → halve Li⁺ dose; monitor level weekly × 4 |
| **Loop diuretics** | Less effect (distal) but volume depletion | **↑ Level 10–20%** | Monitor; reduce dose if needed |
| **ACE inhibitors** | ↓ GFR + ↑ proximal reabsorption | **↑ Level 20–50%** | **Avoid if possible**; if essential → halve Li⁺ dose; monitor |
| **ARBs** | Similar to ACEi | **↑ Level 20–40%** | Same as ACEi |
| **NSAIDs** | ↓ Prostaglandins → ↓ GFR + ↑ reabsorption | **↑ Level 20–60%** | **Avoid chronic use**; if essential → monitor level |
| **COX-2 inhibitors** | Similar to NSAIDs | Moderate | Monitor |
| **Potassium-sparing diuretics** (amiloride, triamterene) | Minimal effect on Li⁺ | Low | Preferred over thiazides if diuretic needed |
| **Tetracyclines** | May ↑ levels | Mild | Monitor |
| **Metronidazole** | Possible ↑ levels | Mild | Monitor |

> **Viva Key:** *Thiazide + lithium = **classic interaction**. **Halve lithium dose** and monitor level weekly. ACEi/ARB + lithium = similar. NSAIDs + lithium = avoid chronic.*

### Interactions Decreasing Levels (Rare)
| Drug | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Theophylline** | ↑ Renal clearance (mild) |
| **Caffeine** | ↑ Renal clearance (mild) |
| **Sodium bicarbonate** | ↑ Urinary pH → ↑ excretion |

## 5️⃣ Toxicity — Acute vs Chronic

### Acute Toxicity (Overdose / Rapid Accumulation)

| Level | Features |
|-------|----------|
| **1.5–2.0** | GI (nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea), tremor, confusion, ataxia |
| **2.0–2.5** | **Neurologic**: coarse tremor, hyperreflexia, clonus, seizures, **encephalopathy**, coma |
| **>2.5–3.0** | **Life-threatening**: seizures, status epilepticus, respiratory failure, **permanent neuro damage** |

### Chronic Toxicity (Therapeutic Levels but Accumulation / Sensitivity)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Neurologic** | **Fine tremor** (most common), cognitive slowing, memory impairment |
| **Renal** | **Nephrogenic Diabetes Insipidus (NDI)** — polyuria, polydipsia (↑ 20–40% patients); **Chronic tubulointerstitial nephritis** → CKD (↓ eGFR over years) |
| **Endocrine** | **Hypothyroidism** (goitre, ↑ TSH) — 10–20% long-term; **Hyperparathyroidism** (↑ Ca²⁺) — 5–10% |
| **Cardiac** | Benign T-wave flattening/inversion (not ischaemic); **Sick sinus syndrome** (rare) |
| **Dermatologic** | Acne, psoriasis exacerbation, alopecia |

### Risk Factors for Toxicity
| Factor | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Renal impairment** | ↓ Clearance |
| **Dehydration** (vomiting, diarrhoea, sweating) | ↑ Reabsorption |
| **Hyponatraemia** / Low salt diet | ↑ Reabsorption |
| **Elderly** | ↓ CrCl, ↓ Vd, polypharmacy |
| **Drug interactions** (diuretics, ACEi, NSAIDs) | ↑ Levels |
| **Febrile illness** | ↓ Intake, ↑ losses |

## 6️⃣ Management of Toxicity

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Lithium Toxicity Suspected] --> B{Level & Clinical}
    B -->|Level 1.0-1.5<br>Mild symptoms| C[Reduce/hold dose<br>Hydrate (oral/IV saline)<br>Correct Na⁺/K⁺<br>Monitor level 24-48h]
    B -->|Level 1.5-2.5<br>Moderate symptoms| D[HOLD lithium<br>IV saline 1-2L (if euvolaemic)<br>Monitor ECG, neuro, renal<br>Level q12-24h]
    B -->|Level >2.5<br>Severe (seizures, coma)| E[ICU admission<br>IV saline + consider **haemodialysis**<br>Dialysis if: level >4, severe neuro, renal failure, <60yo]
    C & D & E --> F[Restart at lower dose<br>when level <0.8 & asymptomatic<br>Review interactions]
```

### Haemodialysis Indications
| Indication | Threshold |
|------------|-----------|
| **Severe neurotoxicity** | Seizures, encephalopathy, coma |
| **Level >4.0 mmol/L** | (or >2.5 with severe symptoms) |
| **Renal failure** (unable to excrete) | AKI on CKD |
| **Elderly / comorbid** | Lower threshold (e.g., >2.5 with symptoms) |

> **Note:** *Lithium is **dialysable** (small, water-soluble, low protein binding) but **rebound** occurs (redistribution from tissues). **Prolonged/intermittent HD** often needed.*

## 7️⃣ Long-Term Monitoring Schedule (NICE / Maudsley)

| Test | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| **Lithium level** (12h post-dose) | **Weekly** until stable × 2; then **3-monthly** (stable); **2-monthly** if elderly/CKD/interactions |
| **U&E, eGFR** | Baseline; **3-monthly** (stable); **2-monthly** if elderly/CKD/diuretics |
| **Thyroid function** (TSH, FT4) | Baseline; **6-monthly** (stable); **annually** if stable long-term |
| **Calcium** (corrected) | Baseline; **annually** (screen for hyperparathyroidism) |
| **Weight / BMI** | Each review |
| **ECG** | Baseline if >50 or cardiac hx; repeat if clinically indicated |

### Monitoring Action Thresholds

| Parameter | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Lithium >1.0** | Reduce dose; check adherence; review interactions; repeat level 1 week |
| **eGFR ↓ >20% from baseline** or **<45** | Review dose; nephrology referral if <30; consider alternative |
| **TSH >10** or **symptomatic hypothyroidism** | Start levothyroxine; continue lithium if psychiatric benefit |
| **Ca²⁺ >2.65** (corrected) | Check PTH; endocrinology referral if hyperparathyroidism |
| **Polyuria >3L/day** | Water deprivation test for NDI; consider amiloride (reduces polyuria) |

## 8️⃣ Special Populations

### Pregnancy
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Category** | **D** (human risk) — **Ebstein's anomaly** (0.1–0.2%) |
| **1st Trimester** | **Avoid if possible**; if essential → lowest dose; **level monitoring weekly**; fetal echo 18–20w |
| **2nd/3rd Trimester** | Clearance ↑ (↑ GFR) → **dose may need ↑**; monitor level weekly |
| **Peripartum** | **Stop 24–48h before delivery** (neonatal toxicity: floppy, hypotonia, hypothyroidism) |
| **Breastfeeding** | **Contraindicated** (high milk levels, infant toxicity) |

### Elderly
- **Target 0.4–0.6 mmol/L** (lower)
- **Start low** (100–200mg daily); **titrate slowly**
- **Monitor renal/thyroid/calcium** more frequently
- **Review interactions** (diuretics, ACEi, NSAIDs common)

### Paediatrics / Adolescents
- **Off-label** but used in bipolar
- **Weight-based dosing** (20–30mg/kg/day)
- **Monitor growth, thyroid, renal** closely

## 9️⃣ Switching Preparations

| Switch | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Standard → Slow-release (SR)** | **Same total daily dose**; SR gives smoother levels, less peak toxicity |
| **SR → Standard** | Same total dose; may need BD dosing |
| **Liquid → Tablet** | Same dose (bioequivalent) |
| **Never** | Change preparation **without** checking level at steady state (1 week) |

> **Priadel SR 200mg = Camcolit 200mg = Li-Liquid 5mL (200mg/5mL)**

## 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"Lithium level anytime is fine"** | **NO.** Must be **12h post-dose** (trough). Random level uninterpretable. |
| **"Therapeutic range is 0.6–1.2"** | **OUTDATED.** Maintenance **0.4–0.6**; Acute mania **0.6–0.8**. Toxic >1.0–1.2. |
| **"Lithium is metabolised by liver"** | **NO.** **100% renal excretion unchanged**. No CYP metabolism. |
| **"Thiazide + lithium = just monitor"** | **NO.** **Halve lithium dose** when starting thiazide. Interaction is predictable, major (25–50% ↑). |
| **"Lithium not dialysable"** | **YES it is dialysable** (small, water-soluble, no protein binding). But **rebound** occurs — need prolonged/intermittent HD. |
| **"Lithium safe in breastfeeding"** | **NO.** **Contraindicated** — high milk levels (RID ~50%), infant toxicity (floppy, hypothyroidism, renal). |
| **"NSAIDs only affect lithium if renal impairment"** | **NO.** NSAIDs affect lithium at **any renal function** (prostaglandin inhibition). Avoid chronic use. |
| **"Tremor = always toxicity"** | **Fine tremor** common at therapeutic levels. **Coarse tremor + hyperreflexia + confusion** = toxicity. |

> **Mnemonic: LITHIUM TDM**  
> **L**ithium range: **Maintenance 0.4-0.6**; Acute mania 0.6-0.8 (modern lower)  
> **I**nteractions: **Thiazides #1** (halve dose); ACEi/ARB (halve); NSAIDs (avoid chronic)  
> **T**iming: **12h post-dose** (trough); standard & SR same; document exact times  
> **H**ydration: **Volume depletion ↑ reabsorption** → toxicity; Na⁺/water balance crucial  
> **I**ntake: **Low salt diet ↑ toxicity**; consistent Na⁺ intake  
> **U**rine: **NDI** (polyuria) — 20-40%; amiloride helps; water deprivation test  
> **M**onitoring: **Level 3-monthly**; U&E 3-monthly; TSH 6-monthly; Ca²⁺ annually  
> **T**oxicity: Acute >1.5 GI/neuro; >2.5 seizures. Chronic: **Tremor, NDI, Hypothyroid, CKD, HyperCa**  
> **D**ialysis: **Dialysable** (small, water-soluble); **Rebound** — prolonged HD; level>4 or severe neuro  
> **M**anagement: Mild reduce/hold + hydrate; Severe ICU + HD; Restart when <0.8  
> **P**regnancy: **D (Ebstein's 0.1-0.2%)**; avoid 1st tri; weekly levels; stop pre-delivery; **BF contraindicated**  
> **E**lderly: Target 0.4-0.6; start low; frequent monitoring; review interactions  
> **R**enal: 100% renal clearance; dose ∝ CrCl; halve if CrCl halved; avoid if eGFR<15

## 📌 Summary
- **Therapeutic range**: **Maintenance 0.4–0.6 mmol/L**; **Acute mania 0.6–0.8** (modern lower targets)
- **Sampling**: **12h post-dose** (trough) — standard, SR, liquid all same timing
- **PK**: **100% renal excretion**; handled like Na⁺; **volume depletion → ↑ reabsorption → toxicity**
- **Key interactions**: **Thiazides (↑ 25–50% → HALVE DOSE)**, **ACEi/ARB (↑ 20–50% → HALVE DOSE)**, **NSAIDs (↑ 20–60% → AVOID CHRONIC)**
- **Toxicity**: Acute (>1.5 GI/neuro, >2.5 seizures/coma); Chronic (**fine tremor, NDI/polyuria, hypothyroidism, CKD, hyperparathyroidism**)
- **Management**: Mild — hold, hydrate; Severe — ICU, HD if level >4 or severe neuro/renal failure
- **Monitoring**: **Level 3-monthly** (stable); **U&E 3-monthly**; **TSH 6-monthly**; **Ca²⁺ annually**
- **Pregnancy**: **Category D (Ebstein's 0.1–0.2%)**; avoid 1st tri; weekly levels; stop pre-delivery; **breastfeeding contraindicated**
- **Elderly**: Target 0.4–0.6; start low; frequent monitoring

## ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **Modern lithium maintenance therapeutic range:**  
   A. 0.6–1.2 mmol/L  B. 0.8–1.0 mmol/L  C. **0.4–0.6 mmol/L**  D. 0.6–0.8 mmol/L  
   *Answer: C. Modern target 0.4–0.6 for maintenance. 0.6–0.8 for acute mania.*

2. **Correct lithium sampling time:**  
   A. 2h post-dose  B. 6h post-dose  C. **12h post-dose (±1h)**  D. Random  
   *Answer: C. 12h post-dose trough for all preparations (standard, SR, liquid).*

3. **Thiazide diuretic started on lithium — dose adjustment:**  
   A. No change  B. **Halve lithium dose**  C. Double lithium dose  D. Stop lithium  
   *Answer: B. Thiazides ↓ Na⁺ → ↑ proximal Li⁺ reabsorption → ↑ level 25–50%. Halve dose.*

4. **Chronic lithium toxicity feature (most common):**  
   A. Coarse tremor  B. **Fine tremor**  C. Seizures  D. Coma  
   *Answer: B. Fine tremor is most common chronic toxicity. Coarse tremor = acute toxicity.*

5. **Lithium-induced nephropathy — characteristic feature:**  
   A. Nephrotic syndrome  B. **Nephrogenic diabetes insipidus (polyuria)**  C. Rapidly progressive GN  D. Obstructive uropathy  
   *Answer: B. NDI (polyuria/polydipsia) in 20–40% long-term. Chronic tubulointerstitial nephritis → CKD.*

6. **Haemodialysis indication for lithium toxicity:**  
   A. Level >1.5  B. Level >2.5  C. **Level >4.0 OR severe neurotoxicity**  D. Level >3.0  
   *Answer: C. HD if level >4.0, OR severe neuro (seizures, encephalopathy, coma), OR renal failure.*

7. **Lithium + ACE inhibitor — recommended action:**  
   A. No change  B. **Halve lithium dose; monitor weekly**  C. Stop ACEi  D. Increase lithium  
   *Answer: B. ACEi ↓ GFR + ↑ proximal reabsorption → ↑ lithium 20–50%. Halve dose if combination essential.*

8. **Lithium in pregnancy — category and risk:**  
   A. B (safe)  B. C (caution)  C. **D (Ebstein's anomaly 0.1–0.2%)**  D. X (contraindicated)  
   *Answer: C. Category D — Ebstein's anomaly (tricuspid valve dysplasia) 0.1–0.2%.*

9. **Lithium half-life in normal renal function:**  
   A. 6–12h  B. **18–36h**  C. 48–72h  D. >72h  
   *Answer: B. t½ 18–36h (normal); 40–60h (elderly/CKD). Steady state 4–7 days.*

10. **NSAID + lithium interaction — mechanism:**  
    A. CYP inhibition  B. **Prostaglandin inhibition → ↓ GFR + ↑ proximal reabsorption**  C. Protein displacement  D. Altered absorption  
    *Answer: B. NSAIDs ↓ prostaglandins → ↓ renal perfusion + ↑ proximal Li⁺ reabsorption → ↑ levels 20–60%.*

## 📋 SBAs (10)

1. **65M on lithium 800mg daily (level 0.55), starts bendroflumethiazide 2.5mg. 2 weeks later level 0.95, mild tremor. Action?**  
   A. Continue, monitor  B. **Reduce lithium to 400mg daily**  C. Stop thiazide  D. Increase thiazide  
   *Answer: B. Thiazide interaction → halve lithium dose. Monitor level weekly.*

2. **30F on lithium 600mg daily, pregnant 6 weeks. Level 0.5. Best management?**  
   A. Stop lithium immediately  B. **Continue lithium; weekly levels; fetal echo 18-20w; folic acid 5mg**  C. Switch to valproate  D. Reduce dose by 50%  
   *Answer: B. Do not stop abruptly. Fetal risk (Ebstein's) but relapse risk high. Weekly levels, fetal echo, folate 5mg.*

3. **Patient on lithium presents with polyuria 4L/day, polydipsia, normal glucose. Lithium level 0.5. Diagnosis?**  
   A. Diabetes mellitus  B. **Nephrogenic diabetes insipidus**  C. SIADH  D. Psychogenic polydipsia  
   *Answer: B. NDI from chronic lithium — collecting duct resistance to ADH. Level can be therapeutic.*

4. **Lithium toxicity level 3.2 mmol/L, seizures, GCS 8. Immediate management?**  
   A. Oral hydration  B. IV saline + **haemodialysis**  C. Activated charcoal  D. Flumazenil  
   *Answer: B. Level >2.5 with severe neuro (seizures, coma) → ICU + haemodialysis.*

5. **Lithium monitoring — which is CORRECT schedule for stable patient?**  
   A. Level monthly, U&E monthly, TSH monthly  B. **Level 3-monthly, U&E 3-monthly, TSH 6-monthly, Ca²⁺ annually**  C. Level 6-monthly, U&E annually  D. Level weekly, U&E weekly  
   *Answer: B. NICE/Maudsley stable monitoring schedule.*
