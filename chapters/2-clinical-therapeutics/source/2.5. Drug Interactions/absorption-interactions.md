## 2. 📊 Mechanisms of Absorption Interactions

| Mechanism | Description | Examples |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **pH-Dependent Solubility** | Gastric pH alters dissolution/ionisation of weak acids/bases | PPI/H2RA + Ketoconazole/Itraconazole/Atazanavir/Ledipasvir (↓ absorption) |
| **Chelation / Complexation** | Multivalent cations (Al³⁺, Mg²⁺, Ca²⁺, Fe²⁺/³⁺, Zn²⁺) bind drugs → insoluble complexes | **Fluoroquinolones, Tetracyclines, Bisphosphonates, Thyroid hormones** + Antacids, Iron, Calcium, Sucralfate, Multivitamins |
| **Gastric Emptying / Motility** | Drugs altering GI transit affect absorption of others | **Metoclopramide/Domperidone (↑ emptying) + Paracetamol (↑ absorption)**; **Anticholinergics/Opioids (↓ emptying) + Levodopa (↓ absorption)** |
| **Transporter-Mediated** | Influx (OATP, OCT, PEPT1) / Efflux (P-gp, BCRP, MRP2) at enterocyte | **P-gp inhibitors (Clarithromycin, Verapamil) + Digoxin (↑ absorption)**; **OATP inhibitors (Rifampicin, Ciclosporin) + Statins (↑ absorption)** |
| **Binding Resins / Adsorbents** | Non-specific binding in lumen | **Cholestyramine/Colestipol/Colesevelam** + Warfarin, Digoxin, Thyroid hormones, Thiazides, Statins, Fat-soluble vitamins |
| **Food Effects** | High-fat / high-calcium / grapefruit juice | **Grapefruit juice (CYP3A4/P-gp inhibition) + Felodipine/Simvastatin/Ciclosporin**; **High-fat meal + Griseofulvin (↑)**: **Calcium + Tetracyclines/Bisphosphonates (↓)** |

## 3. ⚡ High-Yield Clinical Combinations

### 1. Acid-Reducing Agents + pH-Dependent Drugs
| Acid Reducer | Affected Drug | Effect | Management |
|--------------|---------------|--------|------------|
| **PPI** (Omeprazole, Esomeprazole, Pantoprazole) | **Ketoconazole, Itraconazole, Posaconazole** | ↓ Absorption (need acidic pH) | **Avoid** if possible; if essential: give antifungal **with acidic drink (cola)** 2h before PPI |
| **PPI** | **Atazanavir, Ledipasvir, Rilpivirine** | ↓ Absorption | **Contraindicated** (atazanavir) / **Avoid** (ledipasvir) / **Separate** (rilpivirine: PPI 12h before) |
| **PPI** | **Erlotinib, Gefitinib, Dasatinib, Nilotinib** | ↓ Absorption | Avoid PPI; use H2RA (separate 10h) or antacid (separate 2h) |
| **H2RA** (Ranitidine, Famotidine) | **Ketoconazole, Itraconazole** | ↓ Absorption (less than PPI) | Separate by **≥2h**; give antifungal with acidic drink |
| **Antacids** (Al/Mg hydroxide) | **Ketoconazole, Itraconazole** | ↓ Absorption | Separate by **≥2h** |

### 2. Chelation — **Must Separate**
| Drug | Chelating Agent | Separation | Clinical Pearl |
|------|-----------------|------------|----------------|
| **Fluoroquinolones** (Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin, Moxifloxacin, Ofloxacin) | Antacids (Al/Mg/Ca), **Iron, Zinc, Calcium, Sucralfate, Multivitamins, Dairy** | **2h before or 4–6h after** | **Ciprofloxacin + Ca²⁺ = ↓ 90% absorption** |
| **Tetracyclines** (Doxycycline, Minocycline, Tetracycline) | Antacids, Iron, Calcium, Magnesium, Dairy, Multivitamins | **2h before or 4h after** | **Doxycycline less affected** (can take with food if GI upset) |
| **Bisphosphonates** (Alendronate, Risedronate, Ibandronate) | **Calcium, Iron, Magnesium, Antacids, Food, Beverages (except water)** | **Take 30–60 min BEFORE** food/drinks/meds with **plain water only**; remain upright | **Absolute requirement** for absorption |
| **Thyroid Hormones** (Levothyroxine) | **Calcium, Iron, Sucralfate, Cholestyramine, Colestipol, PPIs, Soy, Walnuts, Fibre** | **Take 30–60 min BEFORE breakfast**; separate other meds by **≥4h** | **Lifelong separation** |
| **Digoxin** | **Cholestyramine, Colestipol, Colesevelam, Sucralfate, Antacids (Al/Mg), Kaolin, Pectin** | Separate by **≥2h** | Digoxin narrow TI |
| **Warfarin** | **Cholestyramine, Colestipol, Colesevelam, Orlistat** | Separate by **≥2h** | INR monitoring |

### 3. Motility / Gastric Emptying
| Drug Affecting Motility | Affected Drug | Effect | Significance |
|-------------------------|---------------|--------|--------------|
| **Metoclopramide / Domperidone** (↑ emptying) | **Paracetamol** | ↑ Rate & extent of absorption | Earlier analgesia |
| **Metoclopramide / Domperidone** | **Levodopa** | ↑ Absorption (paradoxical — metoclopramide D2 antagonist worsens Parkinson's) | **AVOID** metoclopramide in Parkinson's |
| **Anticholinergics / Opioids / L-Dopa** (↓ emptying) | **Paracetamol** | ↓ Rate of absorption | Delayed analgesia |
| **Anticholinergics / Opioids** | **Levodopa** | ↓ Absorption | Worsened motor control |
| **Erythromycin** (motilin agonist, ↑ emptying) | **Paracetamol** | ↑ Absorption | Minor |

### 4. Transporter Interactions (Intestinal)
| Transporter | Inhibitor (↑ Absorption of Substrate) | Inducer (↓ Absorption) | Clinically Relevant Substrates |
|-------------|---------------------------------------|------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **P-gp (ABCB1)** | Clarithromycin, Verapamil, Quinidine, Ciclosporin, Amiodarone, Dronedarone, Ritonavir | Rifampicin, St John's Wort, Carbamazepine, Phenytoin | **Digoxin, Dabigatran, Rivaroxaban, Apixaban, Tacrolimus, Ciclosporin, Fexofenadine** |
| **OATP1A2/2B1** | Rifampicin (single dose), Ciclosporin, Gemfibrozil, Ritonavir | Rifampicin (chronic) | **Statins (Rosuvastatin, Atorvastatin), Fexofenadine, Methotrexate** |
| **OCT1** | Verapamil, Quinidine, Cimetidine, Propranolol | — | **Metformin, Imatinib** |
| **PEPT1** | — | — | **Beta-lactams (cephalexin), ACE inhibitors, Di/tripeptides** |

### 5. Binding Resins (Cholestyramine, Colestipol, Colesevelam)
| Affected Drug | Separation | Notes |
|---------------|------------|-------|
| **Warfarin** | ≥2h | INR monitoring |
| **Digoxin** | ≥2h | Narrow TI |
| **Thyroid Hormones** | ≥4h | Critical |
| **Thiazide Diuretics** | ≥1h | |
| **Statins** | ≥1h | |
| **Fat-Soluble Vitamins (A, D, E, K)** | ≥2h | Give vitamins at bedtime |
| **Folic Acid** | ≥2h | |

## 4. 📋 Patient Counselling Quick Reference

| Drug | Key Counselling Point |
|------|----------------------|
| **Bisphosphonates** | "Take first thing in morning with **full glass of plain water**, **wait 30–60 min before ANY food, drink, or other meds**, stay **upright**" |
| **Levothyroxine** | "Take **on empty stomach 30–60 min before breakfast**; **separate calcium, iron, antacids, PPIs by 4h**" |
| **Fluoroquinolones** | "Avoid dairy, antacids, iron, zinc, multivitamins **2h before or 4–6h after**" |
| **Tetracyclines** | "Avoid dairy, antacids, iron, calcium **2h before or 4h after**"; **doxycycline can take with food** if nausea |
| **Itraconazole / Ketoconazole** | "Take **with food and acidic drink (cola)**; **avoid PPIs/H2RAs/antacids** or separate **2h**" |
| **Digoxin** | "Separate from **cholestyramine, antacids, kaolin, pectin by 2h**" |
| **Warfarin** | "Separate from **cholestyramine, orlistat by 2h**; **consistent vitamin K intake**" |
| **Statins** | "Separate from **cholestyramine by ≥1h**; **avoid grapefruit juice**" (esp. simvastatin, lovastatin, atorvastatin) |

## 5. 🎯 FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Combination | Effect | Action |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| PPI + Ketoconazole/Itraconazole | ↓ Absorption | Avoid / give with cola 2h before PPI |
| PPI + Atazanavir | ↓ Absorption | **Contraindicated** |
| Antacids + Ciprofloxacin | ↓ 90% absorption | Separate 2h before / 4–6h after |
| Calcium + Levothyroxine | ↓ Absorption | Levothyroxine 30–60 min before breakfast; Ca 4h later |
| Cholestyramine + Warfarin/Digoxin/Thyroxine | ↓ Absorption | Separate ≥2h (thyroxine ≥4h) |
| Metoclopramide + Levodopa | ↑ Absorption but **worsens Parkinson's** (D2 antagonist) | **AVOID metoclopramide in Parkinson's** |
| Grapefruit juice + Felodipine/Simvastatin/Ciclosporin | ↑ Bioavailability (CYP3A4/P-gp inhibition) | **Avoid grapefruit juice** |
| Rifampicin (chronic) + OATP substrates (Statins) | ↓ Absorption (induction) | Dose adjust / monitor |

## 6. ❓ Viva Questions (8)

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 1. Why should PPIs be avoided with ketoconazole/itraconazole? | Azoles require **acidic gastric pH for dissolution/absorption**; PPIs raise pH → ↓ bioavailability |
| 2. Ciprofloxacin + antacid containing calcium — separation required? | **2h before or 4–6h after** — chelation forms insoluble complex |
| 3. Levothyroxine + calcium carbonate — how to separate? | **Levothyroxine 30–60 min before breakfast; calcium ≥4h later** |
| 4. Bisphosphonate administration rules? | **First thing AM, full glass plain water, wait 30–60 min before food/drink/meds, remain upright** |
| 5. Metoclopramide in Parkinson's patient — why avoid? | **D2 antagonist** → worsens motor symptoms; **domperidone preferred** (peripheral D2 only) |
| 6. Grapefruit juice interaction — mechanism, affected drugs? | **Intestinal CYP3A4 + P-gp inhibition** → ↑ bioavailability of felodipine, simvastatin, ciclosporin, tacrolimus, rivaroxaban |
| 5. Cholestyramine + warfarin — separation? | **≥2h** (warfarin narrow TI, INR monitoring) |
| 6. Rifampicin + oral contraceptive / statin — effect on absorption? | **Chronic rifampicin induces OATP/P-gp** → ↓ absorption of statins, OCP; **acute rifampicin inhibits** |

## 7. 🤯 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Doxycycline vs other tetracyclines + food** | Doxycycline **can** be taken with food (less chelation); other tetracyclines must be on empty stomach |
| **Acute vs chronic rifampicin on transporters** | **Acute**: inhibits OATP/P-gp → ↑ absorption; **Chronic**: induces → ↓ absorption |
| **PPI + atazanavir** | **Contraindicated** — cannot overcome with separation; use alternative ARV |
| **Bisphosphonate + water only** | **Plain water only** — coffee, juice, mineral water all ↓ absorption |
| **Levothyroxine + PPI** | PPI ↓ gastric acidity → ↓ levothyroxine dissolution; **separate by ≥4h** |

**Mnemonics:**
- **"BISPHOSPHONATE RULES"** = **B**efore breakfast, **I**mmediately on waking, **S**tay upright, **P**lain water only, **H**alf-hour wait, **O**ther meds later, **S**eparate calcium/iron, **P**PIs separate, **H**2RAs separate, **A**ntacids separate, **T**ake weekly/monthly, **E**very dose same way
- **"LEVO RULES"** = **L**evothyroxine: **E**mpty stomach, **V**ery early (30–60 min before food), **O**nly water; separate **C**alcium, **I**ron, **P**PI, **A**ntacids, **S**ucralfate
- **"QUINOLONE CHELATION"** = **Q**uinolones + **C**alcium/**I**ron/**M**g/**A**l/**Z**n = **Separate 2h before / 4–6h after**
- **"GRAPEFRUIT = CYP3A4/P-gp INHIBITOR"** = affects **F**elodipine, **S**imvastatin, **C**iclosporin, **T**acrolimus, **R**ivaroxaban

## 11. 📝 Exam Answer Modes

### Short Question (5 marks): *"Bisphosphonate administration rules"*
- First thing in morning on waking
- Full glass **plain water only**
- Remain **upright** (sit/stand) for **30–60 min**
- **No food, drink, or other medications** for 30–60 min
- Separate calcium, iron, antacids, PPIs by ≥30–60 min

### Viva (1 min): *"Patient on ciprofloxacin 500mg BD prescribed calcium carbonate for osteoporosis. Counselling?"*
- **Chelation** → ↓ ciprofloxacin absorption up to 90%
- **Separate by 2h before or 4–6h after** calcium dose
- Advise take ciprofloxacin 2h before breakfast, calcium with dinner

### Ward Round (30 sec): *"Patient on levothyroxine 100mcg, started omeprazole 20mg for dyspepsia. TSH rising. Why?"*
- **PPI ↓ gastric acidity → ↓ levothyroxine dissolution/absorption**
- **Separate by ≥4h** (levothyroxine 30–60 min before breakfast, omeprazole 30 min before dinner)
- Monitor TSH in 6–8 weeks

### Last-Night Revision (1-liners):
- PPI + ketoconazole/itraconazole = ↓ absorption (need acid) → give with cola, separate from PPI
- PPI + atazanavir = CONTRAINDICATED
- Ciprofloxacin + calcium/antacids = separate 2h/4-6h
- Doxycycline = can take with food
- Bisphosphonate = plain water, upright, 30-60min wait
- Levothyroxine = empty stomach 30-60min before breakfast, separate Ca/Fe/PPI/antacids 4h
- Cholestyramine = binds warfarin/digoxin/thyroxine → separate 2h (thyroxine 4h)
- Metoclopramide + levodopa = AVOID (D2 antagonist)
- Grapefruit = CYP3A4/P-gp inhibition → felodipine, simvastatin, ciclosporin
