3 ⚡ Displacement Interaction — Mechanism & Clinical Reality
Theoretical Concern
  1. Drug A (highly protein bound) displaced by Drug B → ↑ free fraction of Drug A
  2. ↑ Free drug → ↑ pharmacological effect, ↑ metabolism, ↑ excretion
  3. New steady state reached: total drug concentration falls, free concentration returns to baseline
Why Most Are NOT Clinically Significant
ReasonExplanation
---------------------
**1. Transient free drug increase**Free drug ↑ temporarily → ↑ clearance (metabolism/excretion) → free drug returns to baseline
**2. Volume of distribution increases**Displaced drug distributes into tissues → total concentration ↓
**3. Hepatic extraction ratio**For **high extraction drugs** (flow-limited), displacement has minimal effect on clearance
**4. Clinical monitoring**Most high-risk drugs have TDM (phenytoin, valproate) or INR (warfarin)
Exception: Rapidly acting drugs with narrow TI where transient spike causes toxicity before compensation (e.g., warfarin in first few days, phenytoin in overdose context).

7 ❓ Viva Questions (6)
QAnswer
-----------
1. When is protein binding displacement clinically significant?**Drugs with narrow TI, high protein binding (>90%), low volume of distribution, low hepatic extraction** — AND when displacer also inhibits metabolism (dual interaction)
2. Phenytoin + Valproate — why significant?**Dual interaction**: Valproate displaces phenytoin from albumin **AND** inhibits CYP2C9 metabolism → sustained ↑ free phenytoin
3. Warfarin + Sulfonamide — mechanism?**Displacement from albumin + CYP2C9 inhibition** → ↑ free warfarin + ↓ clearance
4. Ceftriaxone in jaundiced neonate — why avoid?**Displaces bilirubin from albumin** → ↑ free bilirubin → kernicterus
5. Why most displacement interactions are not clinically significant?**Transient free drug increase → ↑ clearance (metabolism/excretion) → free drug returns to baseline**; Vd increases, total concentration falls
6. In which disease states is free drug fraction increased?**Liver cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome, burns, sepsis, pregnancy, elderly** — low albumin, high AAG
8 🤯 Confusions & Mnemonics
ConfusionClarification
--------------------------
**Displacement = always dangerous?****No** — most are transient and compensated by ↑ clearance
**Free vs Total drug monitoring****Phenytoin, Valproate** — measure **FREE level** in hypoalbuminaemia, renal failure, displacement interactions
**Albumin vs AAG binding****Albumin** = acidic drugs (warfarin, phenytoin, valproate); **AAG** = basic drugs (lidocaine, propranolol, verapamil)
**High vs Low extraction drugs****High extraction (flow-limited)**: displacement minimal effect (propranolol, lidocaine); **Low extraction (capacity-limited)**: displacement ↑ clearance (warfarin, phenytoin, digoxin)

Mnemonics:

  • "DISPLACEMENT IS USUALLY TRANSIENT" = Displacement → Free drug ↑ → Clearance ↑ → Free drug returns to baseline
  • "PHENYTOIN + VALPROATE = DUAL HIT" = Displacement + Metabolism inhibition
  • "CEFTRIAXONE + BILIRUBIN = KERNICTERUS" = Neonates with jaundice → avoid ceftriaxone
  • "ALBUMIN = ACIDIC DRUGS" = Warfarin, Phenytoin, Valproate, NSAIDs
  • "AAG = BASIC DRUGS" = Lidocaine, Propranolol, Verapamil, Quinidine

12 📝 Exam Answer Modes
Short Question (5 marks): *"Why are most protein binding displacement interactions not clinically significant?"*
  • Displacement transiently ↑ free drug → ↑ clearance (metabolism/excretion) → free drug returns to baseline
  • Volume of distribution increases → total concentration falls
  • High extraction drugs unaffected (flow-limited clearance)
  • Most have TDM/INR monitoring
Viva (1 min): *"Phenytoin + Valproate — why is this interaction significant?"*
  • Dual mechanism: Valproate displaces phenytoin from albumin (↑ free fraction) AND inhibits CYP2C9 (↓ metabolism) → sustained ↑ free phenytoin → toxicity
  • Monitor FREE phenytoin level (not total); ↓ phenytoin dose 25–50%
Ward Round (30 sec): *"Neonate with jaundice, prescribed ceftriaxone. Safe?"*
  • NO — ceftriaxone displaces bilirubin from albumin → ↑ free bilirubin → kernicterus risk
  • Use cefotaxime instead
Last-Night Revision (1-liners):
  • Displacement usually transient → free drug returns to baseline
  • Significant if: narrow TI + high binding + low Vd + low extraction + displacer also inhibits metabolism
  • Phenytoin + Valproate = dual hit (displace + CYP2C9 inhibit)
  • Warfarin + Sulfonamide = displace + CYP2C9 inhibit
  • Ceftriaxone + bilirubin (neonate) = kernicterus → use cefotaxime
  • Albumin binds acidic drugs (Warfarin, Phenytoin, Valproate)
  • AAG binds basic drugs (Lidocaine, Propranolol, Verapamil)
  • Free levels for Phenytoin/Valproate in hypoalbuminaemia/displacement
