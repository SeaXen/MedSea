## 2. 📊 AED Classification by Enzyme Effect

| Category | Drugs | **Enzyme Effect** | **Key Interactions** |
|----------|-------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Strong Inducers** | **Carbamazepine (CBZ), Phenytoin (PHT), Phenobarbital (PB), Primidone (PRM)** | **Induce CYP3A4, 2C9, 2C19, 1A2, UGT, P-gp** | **↓ Levels of: OCP, Warfarin, DOACs, Steroids, ARVs, TB drugs, Antidepressants, CCBs, Statins, Immunosuppressants, Theophylline** |
| **Moderate Inducers** | **Oxcarbazepine (OXC), Eslicarbazepine (ESL), Topiramate (TPM) (>200mg), Rufinamide, Perampanel** | **Induce CYP3A4, UGT (weaker)** | Similar but less potent; OXC/ESL induce CYP3A4; TPM dose-dependent |
| **Neutral / Minimal** | **Levetiracetam (LEV), Lamotrigine (LTG), Lacosamide (LCM), Gabapentin (GBP), Pregabalin (PGB), Vigabatrin (VGB), Brivaracetam (BRV), Zonisamide (ZNS), Ethosuximide (ESM), Valproate (VPA) — *see below*** | **No significant CYP induction** | **Preferred in polypharmacy**; LTG levels ↓ by inducers; VPA inhibits some CYP/UGT |
| **Inhibitors** | **Valproate (VPA), Stiripentol (STP), Rufinamide (weak), Cannabidiol (CBD)** | **VPA: Inhibits UGT, CYP2C9, CYP2C19, β-oxidation, epoxide hydrolase**<br>**STP: Potent CYP3A4, 2C19, UGT inhibitor** | **↑ Levels of: LTG (2x), CBZ-epoxide, PHT, PB, ZNS, Topiramate, Cannabidiol, Rufinamide** |

> **Valproate = SPECIAL** — **Inhibits LTG glucuronidation (↑ LTG 2x)**; **Inhibits CBZ epoxide hydrolase (↑ CBZ-epoxide → toxicity)**; **Displaces PHT from albumin (↑ free PHT)**; **Inhibits β-oxidation (hyperammonaemia risk)**

## 3. ⚡ Critical Interaction Scenarios

### 1. **Enzyme-Inducing AEDs (CBZ, PHT, PB) + Non-AED Drugs**
| Drug Class | Effect | Clinical Consequence | Management |
|------------|--------|---------------------|------------|
| **Combined OCP** | **↓ Ethinylestradiol 40–60%** | **Contraceptive failure** | **Add barrier method**; Use IUD/Implant (levonorgestrel IUS preferred); **Avoid progestin-only pill with CBZ/PHT/PB** |
| **Progestin-only Pill / Implant** | **↓ Progestin** | **Failure risk** | **Avoid implant with inducers**; Use IUS or DMPA injection (less affected) |
| **Warfarin** | **↓ Warfarin levels** | **Subtherapeutic INR → Thrombosis** | **Increase Warfarin dose**; Monitor INR **2x/week** after AED change |
| **DOACs (Apixaban, Rivaroxaban, Dabigatran, Edoxaban)** | **↓ DOAC levels (CYP3A4/P-gp induction)** | **Thrombosis risk** | **AVOID DOACs with strong inducers**; Use **Warfarin (with INR monitoring)** or **UFH/LMWH** |
| **Corticosteroids (Prednisolone, Dexamethasone, Budesonide)** | **↓ Steroid levels** | **Treatment failure (asthma, COPD, autoimmune)** | **Increase steroid dose**; Consider non-inducing AED |
| **Antiretrovirals (PIs, INSTIs, NNRTIs)** | **↓ ARV levels** | **Virological failure** | **AVOID strong inducers with ARVs**; Use non-inducing AEDs (LEV, LTG, LCM, VPA) |
| **Anti-TB (Rifampicin, Rifabutin)** | **↓ Rifamycin levels** | **TB treatment failure** | **Rifampicin induces AEDs too** — bidirectional; Use non-inducing AEDs |
| **Calcineurin Inhibitors (Tacrolimus, Ciclosporin)** | **↓ Levels → Rejection** | **Transplant rejection** | **Increase CNI dose**; Monitor trough levels **2x/week** |
| **Antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs)** | **↓ Levels** | **Depression relapse** | **Monitor; ↑ dose if needed** |
| **CCBs (Verapamil, Diltiazem), Statins** | **↓ Levels** | **Loss of BP/lipid control** | **Monitor; ↑ dose** |

### 2. **Valproate (VPA) — Special Inhibitor Profile**
| Interaction | Mechanism | Clinical Effect | Management |
|-------------|-----------|----------------|------------|
| **Lamotrigine (LTG)** | **Inhibits UGT1A4 → ↓ LTG glucuronidation** | **LTG levels ↑ 2x** → **Rash/SJS risk** | **LTG dose ↓ 50%**; **Slow titration** (25mg q2wks if with VPA) |
| **Carbamazepine (CBZ)** | **Inhibits epoxide hydrolase → ↑ CBZ-epoxide** | **CBZ-epoxide ↑ → Neurotoxicity (tremor, ataxia, diplopia)** | **Monitor CBZ + CBZ-epoxide levels**; ↓ CBZ dose |
| **Phenytoin (PHT)** | **Displaces PHT from albumin + inhibits CYP2C9** | **Free PHT ↑↑ → Toxicity (nystagmus, ataxia) at normal total level** | **Monitor FREE PHT level**; ↓ PHT dose |
| **Phenobarbital (PB)** | **Inhibits PB metabolism** | **PB levels ↑** | **Monitor PB level**; ↓ PB dose |
| **Zonisamide (ZNS)** | **Inhibits ZNS metabolism** | **ZNS levels ↑** | **Monitor; ↓ ZNS dose** |
| **Topiramate (TPM)** | **Inhibits TPM metabolism (weak)** | **TPM levels ↑ slightly** | **Monitor** |
| **Cannabidiol (CBD)** | **Inhibits CYP2C19, CYP3A4** | **CBD levels↑, Clobazam metabolite ↑** | **Monitor; ↓ Clobazam dose** |
| **Rufinamide** | **Inhibits Rufinamide metabolism** | **Rufinamide levels ↑** | **Monitor** |

### 3. **AED–AED Interactions (Key Pairs)**
| AED Pair | Interaction | Clinical Effect | Management |
|----------|-------------|----------------|------------|
| **CBZ + LTG** | CBZ induces LTG glucuronidation | **LTG levels ↓ 40–50%** | **↑ LTG dose**; monitor level |
| **CBZ + PHT** | Mutual induction/protein binding displacement | **Unpredictable levels** | **Monitor both FREE levels** |
| **VPA + LTG** | VPA inhibits LTG glucuronidation | **LTG ↑ 2x** → SJS risk | **LTG dose 50%**; slow titration |
| **VPA + PHT** | VPA displaces PHT + inhibits CYP2C9 | **Free PHT ↑↑** | **Monitor FREE PHT**; ↓ PHT dose |
| **VPA + CBZ** | VPA inhibits CBZ epoxide hydrolase | **CBZ-epoxide ↑ → Neurotoxicity** | **Monitor CBZ-epoxide**; ↓ CBZ dose |
| **VPA + PB** | VPA inhibits PB metabolism | **PB levels ↑** | **Monitor PB level** |
| **PHT + LTG** | PHT induces LTG | **LTG levels ↓** | **↑ LTG dose** |
| **TPM + OCPs** | TPM (>200mg) induces CYP3A4 | **↓ Ethinylestradiol** | **Add barrier method** |
| **OXC/ESL + OCP** | OXC/ESL induce CYP3A4 | **↓ Ethinylestradiol** | **Add barrier method** |

### 4. **Other Key Non-AED Interactions**
| Drug | Interacting AEDs | Effect | Management |
|------|------------------|--------|------------|
| **Antidepressants (SSRIs, TCAs, Bupropion, MAOIs)** | CBZ, PHT, PB (inducers) | **↓ Antidepressant levels** | Monitor; ↑ dose; consider non-inducing AED |
| **Antipsychotics (Clozapine, Olanzapine, Quetiapine, Risperidone)** | CBZ, PHT, PB | **↓ Levels → Relapse** | Monitor; ↑ dose; avoid clozapine with CBZ (↑ agranulocytosis risk) |
| **Benzodiazepines (Clonazepam, Clobazam, Midazolam, Diazepam)** | CBZ, PHT, PB (inducers) | **↓ BZD levels** | **Clobazam + VPA → ↑ N-desmethylclobazam (active)** |
| **Methadone / Buprenorphine** | CBZ, PHT, PB, EFV, Rifampicin | **↓ Levels → Withdrawal** | **↑ Methadone dose**; Monitor |
| **Theophylline** | CBZ, PHT, PB | **↓ Theophylline levels** | Monitor level; ↑ dose |
| **Ciclosporin / Tacrolimus** | CBZ, PHT, PB | **↓ Levels → Rejection** | **Monitor trough 2x/week**; ↑ dose |

## 6. 🤯 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Inducers vs Inhibitors** | **Inducers (CBZ, PHT, PB)** = ↓ levels of other drugs; **Inhibitors (VPA, STP)** = ↑ levels of specific drugs |
| **Valproate = Inhibitor?** | **Yes but complex** — Inhibits UGT (↑ LTG), epoxide hydrolase (↑ CBZ-epoxide), displaces PHT (↑ free PHT) |
| **LTG dose with VPA vs CBZ** | **VPA → LTG dose 50%**; **CBZ → LTG dose ↑** |
| **Free vs Total Phenytoin** | **With VPA or hypoalbuminaemia** → **Use FREE level** (total underestimates effect) |
| **DOACs + Inducers** | **AVOID** — use Warfarin/LMWH instead |
| **Progestin-only implant + Inducers** | **Avoid** — failure risk; Use IUS or DMPA injection |

**Mnemonics:**
- **"INDUCERS = CBZ, PHT, PB, PRIMIDONE"** = Strong CYP3A4/2C9/2C19/1A2/UGT/P-gp inducers
- **"NON-INDUCERS = LEV, LTG, LCM, GBP, PGB, VGB, BRV, ZNS, ETHOSUXIMIDE"** = Safe in polypharmacy
- **"VALPROATE = INHIBITOR"** = **↑ LTG 2x** (dose 50%); **↑ CBZ-epoxide** (neurotoxicity); **↑ Free PHT** (free level!)
- **"LTG + VPA = HALF DOSE"**; **"LTG + CBZ = DOUBLE DOSE"**
- **"FREE PHENYTOIN"** = Hypoalbuminaemia, VPA, Renal failure, Elderly
- **"INDUCERS + OCP = BARRIER METHOD"** = 40-60% ↓ ethinylestradiol
- **"INDUCERS + WARFARIN = ↑ DOSE, INR 2x/WEEK"**
- **"INDUCERS + DOAC = AVOID"**
- **"HLA-B*15:02 = ASIAN + CBZ/OXC/ESL"**
- **"HLA-A*31:01 = EUROPEAN/JAPANESE + CBZ"**

## 10. 📝 Exam Answer Modes

### Short Question (5 marks): *"Enzyme-inducing AEDs and their impact on non-AED drugs."*
- **Strong inducers**: **CBZ, PHT, PB, Primidone** — Induce CYP3A4, 2C9, 2C19, 1A2, UGT, P-gp
- **Effect on non-AEDs**: **↓ Levels** of OCP (↓ ethinylestradiol 40–60%), Warfarin, DOACs, Steroids, ARVs, TB drugs, CNIs, CCBs, Statins
- **Management**: **OCP → Barrier/IUD**; **Warfarin → ↑ dose, INR 2x/week**; **DOACs → AVOID (use Warfarin/LMWH)**; **Steroids → ↑ dose**; **ARVs → Avoid inducers, use LEV/LTG/LCM/VPA**

### Viva (1 min): *"Patient on Carbamazepine, started on Lamotrigine. 2 weeks later: diffuse rash, fever. Why? Management?"*
- **CBZ induces LTG glucuronidation → LTG levels ↓** but **rash occurs during titration**
- **Rapid titration + CBZ induction** → **LTG levels fluctuating** → **Hypersensitivity risk**
- **Management**: **STOP LTG immediately** (SJS/TEN risk); **Switch to non-aromatic AED** (Levetiracetam, Lacosamide); **Avoid rechallenge**; Consider HLA-B*15:02 if Asian

### Ward Round (30 sec): *"Patient on Valproate 1000mg, started Phenytoin 300mg for breakthrough seizures. 1 week later: nystagmus, ataxia, confusion. Total PHT level 18 µg/mL (therapeutic). Why toxic?"*
- **VPA displaces PHT from albumin + inhibits CYP2C9 → Free PHT ↑↑**
- **Total PHT level MISLEADING** (normal 10–20, but free ↑↑)
- **Check FREE PHT level** — will be >2 µg/mL
- **↓ Phenytoin dose**; Monitor free level

### Last-Night Revision (1-liners):
- Inducers = CBZ, PHT, PB, Primidone (CYP3A4/2C9/2C19/1A2/UGT/P-gp)
- Non-inducers = LEV, LTG, LCM, GBP, PGB, VGB, BRV, ZNS, ESM
- VPA = inhibitor: ↑ LTG 2x (dose 50%), ↑ CBZ-epoxide, ↑ Free PHT
- LTG + CBZ = ↑ LTG dose; LTG + VPA = LTG dose 50%
- Free PHT when: VPA, hypoalbuminaemia, renal, elderly
- Inducers + OCP = barrier/IUD; Inducers + Warfarin = ↑ dose, INR 2x/wk
- Inducers + DOACs = AVOID
- HLA-B*15:02 = Asian + CBZ/OXC/ESL; HLA-A*31:01 = Euro/Japanese + CBZ
