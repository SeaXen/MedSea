## 2. 📊 Therapeutic Range & Timing

| Indication | **Therapeutic Range (ng/mL)** | **Toxic Threshold** |
|------------|-------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Atrial Fibrillation (Rate Control)** | **0.5–0.9** (some guidelines 0.5–1.0) | **>1.2–1.5** |
| **Heart Failure (HFrEF)** | **0.5–0.9** (lower end preferred) | **>1.0** (no mortality benefit above 0.9) |
| **Toxicity** | — | **>2.0 = severe toxicity** |

> **Key**: **No proven benefit of levels >0.9 ng/mL in HF**; target **0.5–0.9 for both AF and HF**

### Sampling Timing — **CRITICAL**
| Sample | Timing | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Trough (pre-dose)** | **≥6h post-dose** (ideally **8–12h**) | Must be **post-distribution phase** (t½α ~1–2h) |
| **Random** | Any time | Use nomogram/software; NOT preferred |
| **Peak** | Not used | No clinical utility |

> **If level drawn <6h post-dose**: **Falsely elevated** (distribution phase) — **REPEAT**

## 3. ⚖️ Renal Adjustment (CrCl-Based)

| CrCl (mL/min) | **Dose Adjustment** | **Monitoring** |
|---------------|---------------------|----------------|
| **>80** | Standard (0.125–0.25mg daily) | Level at 5–7 days |
| **50–80** | **0.125mg daily** (↓ 25–50%) | Level at 5–7 days |
| **30–50** | **0.125mg daily or alternate day** | Level at 5–7 days |
| **10–30** | **0.0625–0.125mg alternate day** | Level at 5–7 days |
| **<10 / HD** | **0.0625mg post-HD** or alternate day | Level pre-HD |

> **Use Lean/IBW for Vd calculation**; **CrCl = Cockcroft-Gault** (not eGFR)

## 4. 🔄 Major Drug Interactions (↑ Digoxin Levels)

| Drug / Class | Mechanism | **Dose Reduction** |
|--------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Amiodarone** | P-gp inhibition + ↓ renal clearance + ↓ Vd | **↓ 30–50%** |
| **Verapamil** | P-gp inhibition | **↓ 30–50%** |
| **Diltiazem** | P-gp inhibition | **↓ 30–50%** |
| **Quinidine** | **P-gp inhibition (most potent)** + ↓ Vd | **↓ 50%** |
| **Dronedarone** | P-gp inhibition | **↓ 30–50%** |
| **Clarithromycin / Erythromycin / Azithromycin** | P-gp inhibition (clarithro > erythro > azithro) | **↓ 30–50%** |
| **Itraconazole** | P-gp inhibition | **↓ 30–50%** |
| **Ciclosporin / Tacrolimus** | P-gp inhibition | **↓ 30–50%** |
| **Spironolactone / Eplerenone** | ↓ Renal clearance (creatinine secretion competition) | Monitor K⁺, level |
| **Rifampicin** | **P-gp INDUCTION** → ↓ digoxin levels | **May need ↑ dose** |
| **St John's Wort** | **P-gp INDUCTION** | May need ↑ dose |

## 5. ☠️ Digoxin Toxicity

### Clinical Features
| System | Features |
|--------|----------|
| **Cardiac (Most Dangerous)** | **Bradyarrhythmias** (sinus brady, AV block — *any degree*), **Ectopy** (PVCs, VT, **Bidirectional VT** = pathognomonic), **AF with slow ventricular response** |
| **GI** | Anorexia, Nausea, **Vomiting** (most common), Abdominal pain, Diarrhoea |
| **Neurological** | Fatigue, Weakness, Confusion, Visual disturbances (**Yellow-green halos / xanthopsia**), Headache, Dizziness |
| **Electrolyte** | **Hyperkalaemia** (acute toxicity) — **inhibits Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase → K⁺ not pumped into cells** |

### ECG Findings (Classic)
| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **"Scooped" / "Reverse tick" ST depression** (salvador dalí sign) | **Therapeutic effect** (not toxicity) |
| **PR prolongation, AV block (1st, 2nd, 3rd degree)** | **Toxicity** |
| **Bidirectional VT** | **Severe toxicity** (pathognomonic) |
| **PVCs, Bigeminy, Trigeminy** | **Toxicity** |
| **Atrial tachycardia with block** | **Classic toxicity** (paroxysmal atrial tachycardia + AV block) |

## 6. 💊 Management of Toxicity

| Severity | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Mild (Level 1.5–2.0, asymptomatic)** | Hold digoxin; correct K⁺/Mg²⁺; recheck level in 24–48h |
| **Moderate (Level >2.0, symptomatic: nausea, bradycardia, AV block)** | **Hold digoxin**; correct K⁺/Mg²⁺; **Consider Digoxin-Specific Fab** if persistent arrhythmia |
| **Severe (Life-threatening arrhythmia, K⁺ >5.5, Ingestion >10mg, Level >4 ng/mL)** | **Digoxin-Specific Fab (DigiFab)** |

### Digoxin-Specific Fab (DigiFab) Dosing
| Scenario | **Vials (40mg/vial)** |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Known ingested dose** | **Vials = (Ingested mg × 100) / 40** = **Ingested mg × 2.5** |
| **Unknown ingested / Chronic toxicity** | **Vials = (Serum level ng/mL × Weight kg) / 100** (approx) |
| **Empirical (Cardiac arrest / Life-threatening)** | **10–20 vials** |

> **Each vial binds ~0.5 mg digoxin**; Give IV over 30 min; **Monitor K⁺** (Fab releases bound digoxin → can cause transient ↑ free digoxin); **Recheck level 24h post-Fab** (assay measures total digoxin = free + Fab-bound → falsely elevated)

## 9. 🤯 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Scooped ST = toxicity?** | **No** — it's **therapeutic effect** (↑ Ca²⁺ in myocytes); toxicity = AV block, bidirectional VT |
| **Hyperkalaemia = toxicity** | **Yes** — **acute toxicity** (Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase inhibition); chronic toxicity may have normal K⁺ |
| **Sampling <6h** | **Falsely elevated** — repeat at ≥6h |
| **Rifampicin = ↓ digoxin** | **Inducer** (P-gp) — opposite of most interactions |
| **Digoxin level post-Fab** | **Falsely elevated** (assay measures total = free + Fab-bound) |

**Mnemonics:**
- **"DIGOXIN RANGE"** = **0.5–0.9** (AF & HF); **>1 toxic in HF**; **>2 severe**
- **"SAMPLE AT 6-H+ HOURS"** = **6h post-dose minimum** for trough
- **"TOXICITY = BIDIRECTIONAL VT + AV BLOCK + HYPERKALAEMIA"**
- **"SCOOPED ST = THERAPEUTIC"** = Salvador Dalí moustache
- **"P-GP INHIBITORS = AMIODARONE, VERAPAMIL, QUINIDINE, DRONEDARONE, MACROLIDES, ITRACONAZOLE"** → **↓ DIGOXIN 30-50%**
- **"RIFAMPICIN = INDUCER = ↓ DIGOXIN"**
- **"FAB INDICATIONS"** = **K>5.5, Ingestion>10mg, Level>4, Life-threatening arrhythmia**
- **"FAB DOSE = INGESTED MG × 2.5"** (each vial 40mg binds 0.5mg digoxin)

## 13. 📝 Exam Answer Modes

### Short Question (5 marks): *"Digoxin therapeutic range, sampling, and toxicity features."*
- **Range**: **0.5–0.9 ng/mL** (AF & HF); **>1.0 toxic (HF)**, **>2.0 severe**
- **Sampling**: **Trough ≥6h post-dose** (ideally 8–12h); <6h falsely high
- **Toxicity**: **AV block, Bidirectional VT** (pathognomonic), nausea/vomiting, **yellow-green halos**, **hyperkalaemia** (acute)
- **ECG**: **Scooped ST = therapeutic**; AV block/bidirectional VT = toxicity

### Viva (1 min): *"Patient on digoxin + amiodarone for AF. Presents with nausea, bradycardia 45 bpm, K⁺ 5.8. Digoxin level 2.8. Management?"*
- **Digoxin toxicity** (amiodarone ↑ digoxin via P-gp inhibition)
- **Hold digoxin**; correct K⁺/Mg²⁺
- **K⁺ 5.8 + bradycardia + level >2.0 = Digoxin-Specific Fab indicated**
- **DigiFab: Ingested dose unknown → empirical 10–20 vials** or level-based
- Monitor K⁺ post-Fab (transient ↑); recheck level 24h (falsely high)

### Ward Round (30 sec): *"Digoxin level drawn 2h post-dose = 1.8 ng/mL. Interpretation?"*
- **Falsely elevated** — drawn in **distribution phase** (<6h)
- **Repeat at ≥6h post-dose** (ideally 8–12h pre-dose)

### Last-Night Revision (1-liners):
- Digoxin range = 0.5–0.9 (AF & HF); no benefit >0.9 in HF
- Sample trough ≥6h post-dose (ideally 8–12h)
- Toxicity: AV block, Bidirectional VT, hyperkalaemia, yellow halos
- Scooped ST = therapeutic (NOT toxicity)
- P-gp inhibitors (Amiodarone, Verapamil, Quinidine) → ↓ digoxin 30–50%
- Rifampicin/St John's Wort = inducers → ↓ digoxin
- Digoxin Fab: K>5.5, Ingest>10mg, Level>4, Life-threatening arrhythmia
- Fab dose = Ingested mg × 2.5
