## 3. 🧪 Free vs Total Drug — Phenytoin & Valproate

### Phenytoin
| Parameter | **Total** | **Free** |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Protein Binding** | **~90% bound to albumin** | **~10% free** |
| **Therapeutic Range** | 10–20 µg/mL | **1–2 µg/mL** |
| **When to Use Free** | Normal albumin, no displacement | **Hypoalbuminaemia (<35g/L), Renal failure (uraemia displaces), Pregnancy, Elderly, Valproate co-administration, Nephrotic syndrome, Burns** |
| **Estimation (if free not available)** | **Corrected Total = Measured Total / (0.2 × Albumin + 0.1)**<br>Sheiner-Tozer (renal): **Corrected = Measured / (0.1 × Albumin + 0.1)** | Direct assay preferred |

### Valproate
| Parameter | **Total** | **Free** |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Protein Binding** | **~90% bound (dose-dependent: ↓ binding at higher levels)** | **~10% free (increases with dose)** |
| **Therapeutic Range** | 50–100 µg/mL | **5–10 µg/mL** |
| **When to Use Free** | Normal albumin, low dose | **Hypoalbuminaemia, Renal failure, Pregnancy, High dose (>1000mg/day), Elderly, Displacement (e.g., aspirin)** |
| **Estimation** | Not reliable due to saturable binding | Direct assay preferred |

## 4. ☠️ Toxicity Profiles

| Drug | **Acute Toxicity** | **Chronic Toxicity** |
|------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Phenytoin** | Nystagmus, **Ataxia, Dysarthria** (cerebellar), Confusion, Coma, **Bidirectional VT** (rare), **Hypotension** (IV) | **Gingival hyperplasia**, Hirsutism, Coarsening features, Osteomalacia (↓ Vit D), Neuropathy, **Folate deficiency**, Lymphadenopathy |
| **Carbamazepine** | Dizziness, Ataxia, N/V, Diplopia, Hyponatraemia (SIADH), **Agranulocytosis** (rare) | **Hyponatraemia (SIADH)**, **Autoinduction** (↓ levels over weeks), **Enzyme induction** (↓ other drugs), **Aplastic anaemia** (rare), Skin reactions (SCARs) |
| **Valproate** | Confusion, **Hyperammonaemic encephalopathy** (normal LFTs!), Somnolence, Cerebral oedema | **Weight gain**, **Tremor**, Alopecia, **PCOS**, **Thrombocytopenia** (dose-dep), **Hepatotoxicity** (rare, <2y, polytherapy), **Neural tube defects** (teratogen) |
| **Phenobarbital** | Sedation, Respiratory depression, Hypotension, Ataxia | **Enzyme induction**, Osteomalacia, Cognitive impairment, Dependence |
| **Lamotrigine** | Rash (↑ with rapid titration/valproate), **SJS/TEN**, Aseptic meningitis | Rash, Headache, Insomnia |
| **Levetiracetam** | Sedation, **Behavioural (aggression, psychosis, irritability)**, Dizziness | Rare chronic toxicity |

## 5. 🔄 Key Interactions (Enzyme Induction/Inhibition)

| Drug | **Induces** (↓ levels of others) | **Inhibited By** (↑ levels) | **Inhibits** (↑ levels of others) |
|------|----------------------------------|----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Phenytoin** | **CYP3A4, 2C9, 2C19, 1A2, UGT** | **Valproate, Fluconazole, Amiodarone, Cimetidine, Sulfonamides, Chloramphenicol, Disulfiram, Isoniazid, Felbamate** | Warfarin, DOACs, OCP, Corticosteroids, Theophylline, Ciclosporin, Tacrolimus |
| **Carbamazepine** | **CYP3A4, 2C9, 2C19, 1A2, UGT, P-gp** | **Valproate, Fluconazole, Erythromycin, Clarithromycin, Verapamil, Diltiazem, Propoxyphene, Fluvoxamine, Nefazodone** | Warfarin, DOACs, OCP, Ciclosporin, Tacrolimus, Sirolimus |
| **Phenobarbital** | **CYP3A4, 2C9, 2C19, 1A2, UGT, P-gp** | **Valproate, Felbamate** | Warfarin, DOACs, OCP, Ciclosporin, Tacrolimus |
| **Valproate** | **UGT (weak)** | **Carbamazepine (epoxide), Rifampicin** | **Phenytoin (displace + inhibit), Carbamazepine (epoxide), Lamotrigine (↑ 2x), Phenobarbital, Topiramate** |
| **Lamotrigine** | — | **Valproate (↑ 2x), OCP (↓ levels)** | — |
| **Levetiracetam** | **None** | **None** | **None** (no CYP interaction) |
| **Topiramate** | **CYP3A4 (weak, high dose)** | **Valproate** | **OCP (↓ ethinylestradiol at ≥200mg)** |

## 6. ⚖️ Special Populations

### Pregnancy
| Drug | **Teratogenicity** | **Dose Adjustment** | **Folic Acid** |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Valproate** | **HIGHEST** — Neural tube defects (1–2%), Congenital malformations (10%), Neurodevelopmental delay (ADHD, ASD) | **AVOID IN PREGNANCY**; if essential: lowest effective dose, monitor free levels q4w | **5mg/day** pre-conception |
| **Carbamazepine** | Neural tube defects (0.5–1%), Craniofacial, Cardiac | **Free levels ↓ in pregnancy** — ↑ dose 25–50%, monitor free levels q4w | 5mg/day |
| **Phenytoin** | Fetal hydantoin syndrome (craniofacial, limb, cardiac), NTDs | **Free levels ↓** — monitor free levels q4w; ↑ dose often needed | 5mg/day |
| **Lamotrigine** | Low risk (NTDs ~2–3% at high dose); **clearance ↑ 2–3x in pregnancy** | **Levels ↓ dramatically** — ↑ dose 50–100%, monitor free levels q4w | 5mg/day |
| **Levetiracetam** | **Lowest risk** (preferred in pregnancy) | **Clearance ↑** — ↑ dose often needed | 5mg/day |
| **Topiramate** | Cleft lip/palate, NTDs, Low birth weight, ↓ fetal growth | Monitor levels | 5mg/day |

### Renal Impairment
| Drug | **Renal Excretion** | **Adjustment** |
|------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Levetiracetam** | **~66% renal (unchanged)** | **↓ Dose by 50% if CrCl<50**; avoid if CrCl<30 (or specialist) |
| **Gabapentin** | **100% renal** | **CrCl-based dosing** (e.g., CrCl 30–60: 300mg BD; 15–30: 300mg daily; <15: 300mg alt day) |
| **Pregabalin** | **~98% renal** | Similar to gabapentin |
| **Topiramate** | ~50% renal | ↓ Dose if CrCl<50 |
| **Zonisamide** | ~35% renal | ↓ Dose if CrCl<50 |
| **Phenytoin, Carbamazepine, Valproate, Phenobarbital** | Minimal renal | **No renal adjustment**; monitor FREE levels in uraemia |

### Hepatic Impairment
| Drug | **Hepatic Metabolism** | **Adjustment** |
|------|------------------------|----------------|
| **Phenytoin** | CYP2C9/2C19 | **Use FREE levels**; ↓ dose if severe |
| **Carbamazepine** | CYP3A4 | **Use FREE levels**; ↓ dose if severe |
| **Valproate** | **UGT, β-oxidation** | **CONTRAINDICATED in severe hepatic impairment**; avoid in active liver disease |
| **Lamotrigine** | UGT1A4 | ↓ Dose if severe |
| **Levetiracetam** | Minimal | No adjustment |

## 9. 🤯 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Free vs Total phenytoin** | Free = **1–2 µg/mL** (10% of total); use free in hypoalbuminaemia, uraemia, pregnancy |
| **Valproate protein binding** | **Saturable** — free fraction ↑ at higher doses (>1000mg/day) |
| **Lamotrigine + Valproate** | Valproate **inhibits lamotrigine glucuronidation → ↑ levels 2x** |
| **Levetiracetam interactions** | **NONE** — no CYP, no protein binding, renal excretion |
| **Pregnancy lamotrigine** | **Clearance ↑ 2–3x** → levels plummet → need 50–100% dose increase |
| **Carbamazepine HLA-B*15:02** | Screen in **Asian ancestry** before starting (SJS/TEN risk) |

**Mnemonics:**
- **"FREE PHENYTOIN = 1-2"** = use in hypoalbuminaemia, uraemia, pregnancy, valproate
- **"FREE VALPROATE = 5-10"** = use in hypoalbuminaemia, renal failure, pregnancy, high dose
- **"VALPROATE AMMONIA"** = inhibits CPS1 → urea cycle → ↑ ammonia → L-carnitine 100mg/kg
- **"CARBAMAZEPINE AUTO"** = autoinduction → levels drop 2-4wks
- **"LAMOTRIGINE + VALPROATE"** = valproate ↑ lamotrigine 2x → slow titrate, ↓ dose
- **"LEVETIRACETAM = NO CYP"** = clean interaction profile; renal adjust
- **"PREGNANCY AED"** = Valproate NO; Carbamazepine/Phenytoin = monitor free; Lamotrigine/Levetiracetam = preferred; Folate 5mg
- **"RENAL AED"** = Levetiracetam/Gabapentin/Pregabalin = renal adjust; others = free levels

## 13. 📝 Exam Answer Modes

### Short Question (5 marks): *"Phenytoin free drug monitoring — when, range, formula."*
- **Range**: **Free 1–2 µg/mL** (Total 10–20)
- **When**: **Hypoalbuminaemia (<35), Renal failure/uraemia, Pregnancy, Elderly, Valproate co-administration, Displacement interactions**
- **Formula (Sheiner-Tozer, renal failure)**: **Corrected Total = Measured Total / (0.1 × Albumin + 0.1)**
- **Standard formula**: **Corrected Total = Measured Total / (0.2 × Albumin + 0.1)**

### Viva (1 min): *"Patient on valproate 1500mg/day, confused, drowsy. LFTs normal. Ammonia 180. Management?"*
- **Valproate hyperammonaemic encephalopathy**
- **STOP valproate immediately**
- **L-carnitine 100mg/kg IV**
- Switch to alternative AED (levetiracetam, lamotrigine)
- Monitor ammonia, consciousness

### Ward Round (30 sec): *"Patient on carbamazepine, started on clarithromycin. 1 week later: dizziness, ataxia. Why?"*
- **Clarithromycin inhibits CYP3A4 → ↓ carbamazepine metabolism → carbamazepine toxicity**
- **STOP clarithromycin** (switch to azithromycin if needed)
- **Monitor carbamazepine level**; ↓ carbamazepine dose
- Supportive care

### Last-Night Revision (1-liners):
- Phenytoin free = 1–2; Total = 10–20; use free in hypoalbuminaemia, uraemia, pregnancy
- Valproate free = 5–10; Total = 50–100; saturable binding
- Valproate encephalopathy = normal LFTs + ↑ ammonia → L-carnitine 100mg/kg
- Carbamazepine autoinduction = levels drop 2–4wks; SIADH common; HLA-B*15:02 screen
- Lamotrigine + Valproate = valproate ↑ lamotrigine 2x → slow titrate, ↓ dose
- Levetiracetam = NO CYP interactions; renal adjust; behavioural toxicity
- Pregnancy: Valproate NO; Folate 5mg; Lamotrigine/LEV preferred; free levels q4w
- Renal: LEV, Gabapentin, Pregabalin = renal adjust
