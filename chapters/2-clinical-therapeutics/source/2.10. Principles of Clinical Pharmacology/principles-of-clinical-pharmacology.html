Principles Of Clinical Pharmacology
1 Scope

This heading covers the foundational pharmacology that underpins all prescribing decisions:

  • Pharmacodynamics (PD) — "What the drug does to the body"
  • Pharmacokinetics (PK) — "What the body does to the drug"
  • Factors influencing drug response — age, organ function, genetics, adherence

3 High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Pearls
ConceptDefinitionClinical relevance
---------------------------------------
**Pharmacodynamics (PD)**What the drug does to the bodyReceptor binding, dose-response, mechanisms
**Pharmacokinetics (PK)**What the body does to the drugADME, dosing, steady state
**Therapeutic Index**ED50(toxic) / ED50(therapeutic)TI<10 = high risk: digoxin, warfarin, insulin, phenytoin, opioids
**Volume of Distribution (Vd)**Theoretical volume drug distributes inLoading dose = Vd × target concentration
**Clearance (CL)**Volume of plasma cleared per unit timeDetermines maintenance dose
**Half-life (t½)**Time for plasma concentration to halve4-5 t½ to steady state
**Bioavailability (F)**Fraction reaching systemic circulationIV = 100% (F=1); oral <100% (first-pass)
**Loading dose**Initial dose to rapidly achieve targetWhen t½ is long (digoxin, amiodarone)
**Maintenance dose**Dose to maintain target= CL × target concentration / F
**Adherence**Patient's behaviour matches agreed Rx~50% non-adherence in chronic disease
**Concordance**Therapeutic partnership, shared decisionPatient-centred prescribing
