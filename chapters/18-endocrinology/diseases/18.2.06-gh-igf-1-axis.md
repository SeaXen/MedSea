# GH/IGF-1 Axis (Somatotropic Axis)

> [!info]
> **GH/IGF-1 axis = GHRH/Somatostatin → GH → IGF-1 (Liver).** Central to growth, metabolism, and body composition. Dysregulation → Acromegaly (excess), Growth Hormone Deficiency (deficiency).

## 2. GH/IGF-1 Axis — Anatomy & Physiology

### Hormone Synthesis & Secretion

| Step | Hormone | Source | Regulation |
|------|---------|--------|------------|
| **1. GHRH** | Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone | Hypothalamus (Arcuate Nucleus) | Stress, Exercise, Hypoglycaemia, Protein meal ↑; Somatostatin, IGF-1, Glucose, Obesity ↓ |
| **2. Somatostatin** | Growth Hormone-Inhibiting Hormone (GHIH) | Hypothalamus (Periventricular Nucleus), Pancreatin D-cells, GI tract | Glucose, Obesity, IGF-1, Ageing ↑; Exercise, Hypoglycaemia ↓ |
| **3. GH** | Growth Hormone (Somatotropin) | Anterior Pituitary (Somatotrophs, 40-50% of cells) | **GHRH** (stimulatory); **Somatostatin** (inhibitory); **Ghrelin** (stimulatory); **IGF-1** (negative feedback); Glucose, Obesity, Ageing (inhibitory) |
| **4. IGF-1** | Insulin-like Growth Factor-1 (Somatomedin C) | **Liver** (main source); Peripheral tissues (autocrine/paracrine) | **GH** (stimulatory); Insulin, Nutrition (permissive); Ageing, Malnutrition ↓ |

### GH Synthesis & Secretion
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Structure** | 191 amino acid polypeptide; **22kDa** (major) + **20kDa** variant (10%) |
| **Gene** | **GH1** (chromosome 17); **GH2** (placental GH) |
| **Secretion** | **Pulsatile** (major peak during **slow-wave sleep**); **Contra-pulsatile** to Somatostatin |
| **Regulation** | **Stimulatory**: GHRH, Ghrelin, Hypoglycaemia, Exercise, Protein meal, Stress, Deep sleep, Sex steroids |
| | **Inhibitory**: Somatostatin (GHIH), IGF-1 (negative feedback), Glucose, Insulin, Obesity, Free Fatty Acids, Ageing |
| **Half-life** | **20-30 minutes** (short); Pulsatile (every 3-5 hours) |

### GH Secretion Across Lifespan
| Age | GH Secretion | IGF-1 Level |
|-----|--------------|-------------|
| **Childhood** | Moderate, pulsatile | Rising |
| **Puberty** | **Peak secretion** (sex steroid amplification) | **Peak** |
| **Young Adult** | Declining | High normal |
| **Ageing** | **Progressive decline** (somatopause) | **Declines** |

## 3. IGF-1 (Insulin-like Growth Factor-1 / Somatomedin C)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Structure** | 70 amino acid polypeptide; **7.6 kDa**; Homologous to Proinsulin |
| **Synthesis** | **Liver** (main endocrine source, ~75%); Peripheral tissues (autocrine/paracrine) |
| **Regulation** | **GH** (primary driver); **Insulin** (permissive); **Nutrition** (protein/energy); **Ageing** ↓ |
| **Binding** | **>99% bound** to **IGFBP-3** (main carrier) + **ALS** (Acid-Labile Subunit) → Ternary complex |
| **Half-life** | **12-15 hours** (vs GH 20-30 min); Stable, reflects integrated GH exposure |
| **Bioactivity** | **Free IGF-1** (1%) = biologically active; Mediates most GH anabolic effects |

### IGF-1 Binding Proteins
| Binding Protein | % Total IGF-1 | Function |
|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| **IGFBP-3** | ~80% | Main carrier; prolongs half-life; GH-dependent |
| **IGFBP-1** | ~1% | Insulin regulates; ↑ in fasting/catabolism |
| **IGFBP-2** | ~10% | ↑ in malnutrition, GH deficiency |
| **ALS (Acid-Labile Subunit)** | Binds IGFBP-3 | Forms ternary complex (IGF-1 + IGFBP-3 + ALS); GH-dependent |

## 4. GH/IGF-1 Axis Regulation

### Stimulatory Factors
| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **GHRH** | Hypothalamus → Pituitary (GHRH-R) → ↑ cAMP → GH release |
| **Ghrelin** | Stomach → Pituitary (GHS-R1a) → ↑ GH (potent secretagogue) |
| **Hypoglycaemia** | Glucose ↑ → ↑ CRH/GHRH → ↑ GH |
| **Exercise** | ↑ Catecholamines, Lactate, β-Endorphin → ↑ GH |
| **Protein Meal** | Amino acids (Arginine, Lysine) → ↑ GH |
| **Deep Sleep** | Slow-wave sleep → ↑ GHRH, ↓ Somatostatin → Nocturnal GH surge |
| **Sex Steroids** | Oestrogen/Testosterone → ↑ GH amplitude (pubertal peak) |

### Inhibitory Factors
| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Somatostatin (GHIH)** | Hypothalamus → Pituitary (SSTR2/5) → ↓ cAMP → ↓ GH |
| **IGF-1** | Negative feedback at Pituitary + Hypothalamus (↑ Somatostatin) |
| **Glucose / Insulin** | ↓ GH secretion (physiological post-prandial suppression) |
| **Obesity** | ↑ FGF21, Leptin resistance → ↓ GH |
| **Free Fatty Acids** | ↑ FFA → ↓ GH secretion |
| **Ageing** | ↓ GHRH, ↑ Somatostatin, ↓ IGF-1 → "Somatopause" |

---

## 5. GH Secretion Patterns

| Pattern | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Pulsatile** | Every 3-5 hours; Amplitude > Frequency |
| **Nocturnal Surge** | **Major peak during slow-wave sleep** (first 2-3 hours); 50-70% of daily GH |
| **Diurnal Rhythm** | Peak 00:00-03:00; Nadir mid-morning |
| **Puberty** | **Peak GH secretion** (sex steroid amplification); 2-3x adult levels |
| **Ageing** | **Progressive decline** ("Somatopause"); ↓ Amplitude, preserved frequency |

## 6. IGF-1 — Clinical Applications

| Use | Interpretation |
|-----|----------------|
| **Screening for Acromegaly** | **IGF-1 (age-adjusted)** — Single best test; ↑ = Acromegaly |
| **Monitoring Acromegaly** | IGF-1 normal = Biochemical control; GH not suppressed on OGTT = Active disease |
| **GH Deficiency (Adult)** | Low IGF-1 + Clinical features → Dynamic testing |
| **GH Deficiency (Child)** | Low IGF-1 + Low GH peak on stimulation |
| **Catabolic States** | **Low IGF-1** in malnutrition, burns, sepsis, hepatic failure |

### IGF-1 Cut-offs
| Population | IGF-1 (Age-Adjusted) |
|------------|----------------------|
| **Normal** | Within 2 SD of age-matched mean |
| **Acromegaly** | **Above upper limit** (often 2-3x ULN) |
| **GHD (Adult)** | Below lower limit + Clinical features |

## 7. Acromegaly — Diagnosis & Management

### Clinical Features
| System | Features |
|--------|----------|
| **Facial** | Coarse features, Prognathism, Macroglossia, Wide spacing teeth |
| **Extremities** | **Acral enlargement** (hands, feet), Ring/shoe size increase |
| **Skin** | Thickened, Oily, Hyperhidrosis, Skin tags, Acanthosis nigricans |
| **Cardiovascular** | Cardiomyopathy, Hypertension, Sleep apnoea, Arrhythmias |
| **Metabolic** | **Impaired Glucose Tolerance / Diabetes** (30-50%); Dyslipidaemia |
| **Joints** | Arthropathy, Carpal tunnel syndrome |
| **Neoplasia Risk** | **Colonic polyps/cancer** (↑ 2-3x); Thyroid nodules |

### Diagnostic Criteria
| Test | Normal | Acromegaly |
|------|--------|------------|
| **IGF-1 (Age-Adjusted)** | Within reference range | **Above upper limit** (often 2-3x ULN) |
| **OGTT (75g Glucose)** | **GH suppressed <1 µg/L** at 60-120min | **GH not suppressed ≥1 µg/L** (Gold Standard) |
| **Random GH** | <0.4 µg/L (nadir) | **>1 µg/L** (if IGF-1 elevated) |
| **MRI Pituitary** | Normal | **Macroadenoma** (>10mm in 90%) |

### Differential Diagnosis of Elevated IGF-1
| Condition | IGF-1 | GH | Notes |
|-----------|-------|----|-------|
| **Acromegaly** | ↑↑ | Not suppressed on OGTT | Pituitary macroadenoma |
| **Pubertal Gigantism** | ↑↑ | ↑ | Open epiphyses |
| **Pregnancy** | ↑ (Placental GH) | N/A | Physiological |
| **Hepatic Failure** | ↓ (impaired synthesis) | ↑ (reduced clearance) | Discordant |
| **Renal Failure** | ↑ (reduced clearance) | ↑ (reduced clearance) | |
| **Malnutrition/Anorexia** | ↓↓ | ↑↑ (paradoxical) | GH resistance |

## 8. Acromegaly — Treatment Algorithm

```
Acromegaly Diagnosed (↑ IGF-1 + GH not suppressed on OGTT)
         │
         ▼
MRI PITUITARY (3mm slices) → Macroadenoma >10mm (90%) / Microadenoma
         │
         ▼
PRIMARY TREATMENT: TRANSSPHENOIDAL SURGERY (TSS)
         │
         ├── CURE (Post-op IGF-1 normal + OGTT GH <1 µg/L)
         │       → Lifelong Surveillance (Annual IGF-1 + OGTT)
         │
         ├── RESIDUAL DISEASE (IGF-1 elevated / GH not suppressed)
         │       │
         │       ├── RE-OPERATION (if accessible residual)
         │       └── MEDICAL THERAPY
         │               ├── SOMATOSTATIN ANALOGUES (1st line medical)
         │               │       Octreotide LAR 20-40mg IM q4wk
         │               │       Lanreotide Autogel 60-120mg SC q4wk
         │               │       Pasireotide LAR 40-60mg IM q4wk (2nd line)
         │               │
         │               ├── PEGVISOMANT (GH Receptor Antagonist)
         │               │       10-30mg SC daily (if SST resistant)
         │               │
         │               └── DOPAMINE AGONISTS (Adjunct)
         │                       Cabergoline 0.5-3mg/week
         │
         └── RADIOTHERAPY (If Surgery + Medical fail)
                 Stereotactic / Conventional; Latent effect 5-10yr
                 Risk: Hypopituitarism, Optic neuropathy, Secondary tumours
```

### Medical Therapy — Agents Comparison
| Agent | Mechanism | Dose | Indication |
|-------|-----------|------|------------|
| **Octreotide LAR** | SSTR2/5 agonist | 20-40mg IM q4wk | 1st line medical |
| **Lanreotide Autogel** | SSTR2/5 agonist | 60-120mg SC q4wk | 1st line medical |
| **Pasireotide LAR** | SSTR1/2/3/5 agonist | 40-60mg IM q4wk | 2nd line (if Octreotide/Lanreotide fail) |
| **Pegvisomant** | **GH Receptor Antagonist** | 10-30mg SC daily | Refractory; Normalises IGF-1 |
| **Cabergoline** | D2 Agonist | 0.5-3mg weekly | Adjunct (partial effect) |
| **Pegvisomant + SST** | Combination | — | Severe refractory |

### Cure Criteria (Post-Surgery / Medical)
| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| **IGF-1** | Normal (Age-adjusted) |
| **OGTT GH** | **<1 µg/L** at 60-120min |
| **Random GH** | **<0.4 µg/L** (nadir) |
| **Clinical** | Resolution of features (soft tissue, metabolic) |

## 9. Growth Hormone Deficiency (GHD)

### Classification
| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Childhood-Onset GHD (CO-GHD)** | Diagnosed <18yr; May persist into adulthood |
| **Adult-Onset GHD (AO-GHD)** | Diagnosed ≥18yr; Usually pituitary tumour/surgery/radiotherapy |

### Diagnosis — Adult GHD

#### Stimulation Tests (Require ≥2 tests usually)
| Test | Protocol | Peak GH Cut-off |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| **ITT (Gold Standard)** | Insulin 0.1 U/kg IV → Glucose <2.2 mmol/L | **<3 µg/L** (adults); <5 µg/L (children) |
| **Glucagon** | 1mg IM/IV → Sample 0, 60, 90, 120, 150, 180min | <3 µg/L (adults) |
| **Arginine** | 0.5g/kg IV over 30min | <0.4 µg/L (children) |
| **GHRH + Arginine** | GHRH 1µg/kg + Arginine 0.5g/kg IV | **<9 µg/L** (adults; most sensitive) |
| **Clonidine** | 0.15mg/m² PO | <6.8 µg/L (historical) |

#### ITT Protocol (Gold Standard)
1. **Fast overnight**, IV access, ECG monitoring
2. **Baseline**: Glucose, GH, Cortisol
3. **Insulin 0.1 U/kg IV bolus**
4. **Sampling**: q15-30min for 2h (Glucose, GH, Cortisol)
5. **Target**: Glucose **<2.2 mmol/L** (symptomatic hypoglycaemia)
6. **Recovery**: IV Glucose + monitors

**ITT Contraindications**: IHD, Cerebrovascular disease, Epilepsy, Severe hypopituitarism (cortisol <100), Age >60, Pregnancy.

### GH Deficiency — Diagnosis Criteria
| Population | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **Adult (AO-GHD)** | **Peak GH <3 µg/L** on ITT/Glucagon **OR** <9 µg/L on GHRH+Arg **PLUS** ≥3 of: ↑ Visceral fat, ↓ BMD, ↓ QoL, Dyslipidaemia, ↓ Muscle mass |
| **Child (CO-GHD)** | Height < -2 SD; Growth velocity < -1 SD; Peak GH <5 µg/L on 2 tests; Low IGF-1 |

---

## 10. GHD — Treatment

| Population | RH-GH Dose | Monitoring |
|------------|------------|------------|
| **Adult** | **0.1-0.3 mg/day SC** (titrate to IGF-1 mid-normal range) | IGF-1 q1-3mo initially; then 6-monthly |
| **Child** | **0.025-0.035 mg/kg/day** SC (approx 0.2-0.5 mg/day) | Height velocity, IGF-1, Bone age q6-12mo |
| **Transition (CO-GHD → Adult)** | Re-test GH status; Continue if persistent GHD | IGF-1, BMD, Lipids, QoL |

### GHD Treatment Contraindications
| Absolute | Relative |
|----------|---------|
| Active malignancy | Active proliferative retinopathy |
| Acute critical illness | Severe obesity |
| **Prader-Willi (paediatric)** | Severe insulin resistance |

## 12. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **IGF-1 vs GH for Acromegaly** | **IGF-1 = Screening** (stable); **OGTT GH = Confirmation** (dynamic) |
| **GH Random** | Useless (pulsatile); Use IGF-1 or Dynamic tests |
| **GHD Diagnosis** | ITT = Gold Standard; GHRH+Arginine = Most sensitive for adults |
| **Acromegaly vs Gigantism** | Gigantism = Pre-epiphyseal fusion; Acromegaly = Post-fusion |
| **IGF-1 in Acromegaly** | ↑↑ (stable); GH = pulsatile (use mean or OGTT) |
| **GHD Treatment** | RH-GH SC daily; Titrate to IGF-1 mid-normal; Monitor IGF-1, BMD, Lipids |
| **GHD Adult vs Child** | Adult: Metabolic phenotype; Child: Growth failure |
| **Pegvisomant** | GH Receptor Antagonist → Normalises IGF-1 but ↑ GH levels |
| **Pasireotide** | SSTR1/2/3/5 agonist; ↑ Hyperglycaemia risk (2nd line) |
