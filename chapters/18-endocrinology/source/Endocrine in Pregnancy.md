# Endocrine Physiology & Management in Pregnancy

> [!info]
> **Pregnancy = Unique Endocrine State** with Profound Physiological Adaptations. **Thyroid, Adrenal, Pancreatic, Calcium, Pituitary Axes All Altered.** **Multidisciplinary Care Essential** for Optimal Maternal-Fetal Outcomes.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Describe physiological endocrine adaptations in pregnancy
- [ ] Apply trimester-specific targets for thyroid, diabetes, adrenal, and calcium disorders
- [ ] Manage pre-existing and new-onset endocrine disorders in pregnancy
- [ ] Counsel on breastfeeding, contraception, and postpartum follow-up

## 2. General Endocrine Adaptations in Pregnancy

### Thyroid Axis
| Change | Mechanism | Clinical Significance |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| **↑ TBG (2-3x)** | Oestrogen ↑ TBG Synthesis | ↑ Total T4/T3 (2-3x); **fT4/fT3 Reliable** |
| **hCG Cross-Reactivity** | hCG → Weak TSH Agonist | **TSH ↓ 1st Tri** (Often <0.1-0.4 mIU/L); Normalises 2nd Trimester |
| **Iodine Requirement** | ↑ Renal Clearance + Fetal Uptake | **RDA 250 µg/day** (vs 150 µg Non-Pregnant) |
| **TSH Targets** | **1st Tri: 0.1-2.5; 2nd: 0.2-3.0; 3rd: 0.3-3.0 mIU/L** | Trimester-Specific Reference Ranges |

### Adrenal Axis
| Change | Mechanism | Clinical Impact |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **↑ CBG (2-3x)** | Oestrogen ↑ CBG Synthesis | ↑ Total Cortisol; **Free Cortisol ↑ 2-3x** |
| **HPA Axis Hyperplasia** | Placental CRH + Maternal HPA Stimulation | Physiological Hypercortisolism |
| **Physiological Hypercortisolism** | ↑ Free Cortisol 2-3x | Suppresses ACTH (Mild) |
| **Adrenal Insufficiency** | **Hydrocortisone ↑ 20-40% in 2nd/3rd Tri** | **Stress Doses for Delivery** (100mg IV q6h) |

### Glucose/Insulin
| Change | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Insulin Resistance** | Placental Hormones (hPL, Cortisol, Progesterone) → ↑ Insulin Resistance |
| **β-Cell Adaptation** | Hyperplasia/Hypertrophy → ↑ Insulin Secretion 2-3x |
| **GDM Risk** | ↑↑ (Screening 24-28w; High-Risk Earlier) |

### Calcium & Bone
| Change | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Calcium Transfer** | ↑ Fetal Demand (250-300mg/day 3rd Tri) |
| **Vitamin D** | ↑ 1,25-(OH)₂D (↑ 1α-Hydroxylase); ↑ Intestinal Ca²⁺ Absorption |
| **Bone** | Maternal Bone Resorption (↑ PTHrP, PTH) → Transient BMD Loss (Recovers Postpartum) |
| **PTHrP** | Placental/Breast Production → ↑ Bone Resorption |

## 2. Thyroid Disease in Pregnancy

### Hypothyroidism
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|-----------|
| **Pre-conception** | TSH **<2.5 mIU/L**; Optimise Levothyroxine |
| **1st Trimester** | **TSH 0.1-2.5 mIU/L**; ↑ Dose **~30% at +ve Test** |
| **2nd/3rd Trimester** | TSH 0.2-3.0 / 0.3-3.0 mIU/L; Monitor q4-6wk |
| **Postpartum** | Reduce to Pre-Pregnancy Dose; Check TSH 6wk |
| **Subclinical Hypo** | Treat if TPOAb+ or TSH > Trimester Target |

### Hyperthyroidism
| Trimester | Preferred ATD |
|-----------|-------------|
| **1st Trimester** | **PTU 50-150mg/day** (Lower Teratogenicity) |
| **2nd/3rd Trimester** | **Switch to Carbimazole** (PTU Hepatotoxicity Risk ↑) |
| **Targets** | fT4 Upper Normal Range; TSH Low but Detectable |
| **TRAb Monitoring** | Each Trimester; **TRAb >5 IU/L → Fetal US (Goitre/Tachycardia/Hydrops)** |

### Postpartum
| Action | Timing |
|----------|--------|
| **Reduce HRT** | Pre-Pregnancy Dose Immediately Postpartum |
| **TSH Check** | **6 Weeks Postpartum** |
| **Breastfeeding** | Levothyroxine, Carbimazole, PTU **Compatible** (Low Milk Transfer) |

## 2. Diabetes in Pregnancy

### GDM Screening
| Strategy | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Universal (IADPSG/ADA)** | 75g OGTT 24-28w (All) |
| **Selective (NICE)** | Risk Factors → OGTT 24-28w |
| **Diagnostic (IADPSG)** | FBG ≥5.1, 1h ≥10.0, 2h ≥8.5 mmol/L (1 Abnormal = GDM) |

### GDM Management
| Step | Intervention |
|------|-------------|
| **1. MNT** | Diet (Low GI, 175g Carb/day), Exercise 150min/wk |
| **2. Metformin** | 500mg BD → 1g BD (If Fasting/1h/2h Targets Not Met) |
| **3. Insulin** | Basal-Bolus / Basal Only; Targets: Fasting <5.3, 1h <7.8, 2h <6.4 mmol/L |

### Pre-Existing Diabetes
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **Pre-Conception** | HbA1c <48 mmol/mol (6.5%); Folic Acid 5mg; Stop Teratogens (ACEi, ARB, Statin, SGLT2i, GLP-1) |
| **Pregnancy** | Intensive Insulin (Basal-Bolus / Pump); Targets: FBG <5.3, 1h <7.8, 2h <6.4 mmol/L |
| **Delivery** | 37-38+6w (Insulin); 38-40w (Diet/Metformin) |
| **Postpartum** | Insulin ↓ Rapidly; Breastfeeding Encouraged; Contraception |

## 2. Adrenal Insufficiency in Pregnancy

| Aspect | Management |
|--------|-----------|
| **Hydrocortisone** | **↑ 20-40% in 2nd/3rd Tri** (Physiological Hypercortisolism) |
| **Fludrocortisone** | Continue (Safe); Monitor BP, K⁺ |
| **Delivery** | **Stress Dose**: Hydrocortisone 100mg IV q6h During Labour |
| **Postpartum** | Reduce to Pre-Pregnancy Dose; Crisis Prevention |

## 3. Calcium & Vitamin D

| Requirement | Dose |
|-------------|--------|
| **Iodine** | **250 µg/day** (vs 150 µg Non-Pregnant) |
| **Calcium** | **1000-1200 mg/day** (Diet + Supplement) |
| **Vitamin D** | **600-800 IU/day** (Higher if Deficient) |
| **Folic Acid** | **5mg Daily** (Pre-Conception + 1st Tri) |

---

## 3. Endocrine Emergencies in Pregnancy

| Emergency | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| **Thyroid Storm** | PTU + Propranolol + Iodine + Hydrocortisone; **Multidisciplinary ICU** |
| **Adrenal Crisis** | **Hydrocortisone 100mg IV STAT** + Fluids; Stress Doses for Delivery |
| **DKA** | Insulin Infusion + Fluids + K⁺ Replacement; Fetal Monitoring |
| **HHS** | Hydration + Insulin; Avoid Rapid Correction |

---

## 3. Postpartum Endocrine Management

| Aspect | Action |
|--------|---------|
| **Thyroid** | Reduce Levothyroxine to Pre-Preg Dose; Check TSH 6wk |
| **Adrenal** | Reduce Hydrocortisone to Pre-Preg Dose |
| **Diabetes** | Insulin ↓ Rapidly; GDM → 75g OGTT 6-13wk Postpartum |
| **Breastfeeding** | Levothyroxine, Carbimazole, PTU, Metformin **Compatible** |
| **Contraception** | LARC (IUD/Implant) Preferred; COCP If No Contraindications |

---

## 3. Autoimmune in Pregnancy

| Condition | Monitoring |
|---------|------------|
| **Thyroid (TPOAb+)** | TSH q4-6wk; ↑ Dose if TSH ↑ |
| **Antiphospholipid (APS)** | Aspirin 75-150mg OD from 12w; LWMH if Prior Thrombosis |
| **SLE/APS** | HCQ Continued; Prednisolone if Flare |
| **T1DM** | Intensive Insulin; CGM Preferred; Pre-Conception HbA1c <48 |

## Flashcards
- **Q: hCG & thyroid**
  **A: hCG cross-reacts with TSH-R → transient gestational thyrotoxicosis (week 8-14); TSH↓, fT4↑**

- **Q: Iodine in pregnancy**
  **A: 250 µg/day (WHO); 150 µg non-pregnant; deficiency → cretinism, goitre, neurocognitive deficit**

- **Q: TSH ref ranges pregnancy**
  **A: 1st tri: 0.1-2.5; 2nd: 0.2-3.0; 3rd: 0.3-3.0 mU/L (ATA)**

- **Q: Levothyroxine in pregnancy**
  **A: ↑ dose 25-50% at +ve pregnancy test; target trimester-specific TSH; monitor q4wk**

- **Q: Graves pregnancy drugs**
  **A: PTU 1st tri (less teratogenic); switch to carbimazole/methimazole at 16wks (PTU hepatotoxicity)**

- **Q: PTU vs MMI teratogenicity**
  **A: MMI: aplasia cutis, choanal atresia, oesophageal atresia; PTU: hepatotoxicity (rare)**

- **Q: Postpartum thyroiditis**
  **A: 5-10% women; thyrotoxic 1-4mo → hypothyroid 4-8mo → recovery 12mo; 20-40% permanent hypothyroid**

- **Q: GDM diagnosis**
  **A: 75g OGTT: fasting ≥5.1, 1h ≥10.0, 2h ≥8.5 mmol/L (IADPSG); one abnormal = GDM**

- **Q: GDM management**
  **A: MNT + glucose monitoring → insulin if fasting ≥5.3 or 1h ≥7.8 or 2h ≥6.4; metformin/glibenclamide 2nd line**

- **Q: Postpartum GDM follow-up**
  **A: 75g OGTT 6-12 weeks postpartum; then q1-3y lifelong (50% T2DM at 10y)**

- **Q: T1DM pregnancy**
  **A: Preconception HbA1c <6.5% (48 mmol/mol); pump/CGM; targets: fasting <5.3, 1h <7.8, 2h <6.4**

- **Q: Postpartum insulin**
  **A: ↓ requirements 30-50% (esp. breastfeeding); risk nocturnal hypoglycaemia**

- **Q: Breastfeeding & thyroid drugs**
  **A: PTU/MMI/levothyroxine safe; carbimazole <30mg/day safe; RAI contraindicated**
