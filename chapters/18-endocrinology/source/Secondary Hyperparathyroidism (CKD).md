# Secondary Hyperparathyroidism (CKD)

> [!info]
> **Secondary Hyperparathyroidism (SHPT) = Compensatory PTH Hypersecretion** driven by CKD-Mineral and Bone Disorder (CKD-MBD). **Universal in CKD Stage 4-5.** Pathophysiology: **Phosphate Retention → FGF23 ↑ → 1,25-(OH)₂D ↓ → Hypocalcaemia → PTH ↑**. KDIGO Target: **PTH 2-9x ULN**.

## 2. Pathophysiology — The CKD-MBD Cascade

```
CKD (↓ GFR)
    │
    ├─→ **Phosphate Retention** (↓ Filtered Load)
    │       │
    │       ├─→ **FGF23 ↑** (Osteocyte Response) → ↓ 1α-Hydroxylase → **↓ 1,25-(OH)₂D**
    │       │       │
    │       │       └─→ **↓ Intestinal Ca²⁺ Absorption** → **Hypocalcaemia**
    │       │
    │       └─→ **Direct PTH Stimulation** (Phosphate → PTH Gene Expression)
    │
    └─→ **Hypocalcaemia** (↓ Ca²⁺ Absorption + ↓ Vit D) → **PTH ↑↑** (Secondary HPT)
```

### Key Mediators
| Mediator | Change in CKD | Effect |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| **Phosphate** | ↑↑ (Retention) | Direct PTH Stim; FGF23 ↑ |
| **FGF23** | ↑↑↑ (Early Rise) | ↓ 1α-Hydroxylase; ↓ 1,25-(OH)₂D |
| **1,25-(OH)₂D (Calcitriol)** | ↓↓↓ | ↓ Intestinal Ca²⁺ Absorption → Hypocalcaemia |
| **Calcium** | ↓ | ↑ PTH Secretion |
| **PTH** | **↑↑↑** (Secondary HPT) | Bone Resorption; Phosphate Excretion |

## 2. Stages of CKD-MBD (KDIGO)

| CKD Stage | GFR (mL/min) | Phosphate | FGF23 | 1,25-(OH)₂D | PTH | Bone Turnover |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|-------|-------------|-----|---------------|
| **G3a (45-59)** | Mild | Normal | ↑ | Normal/↓ | ↑ (1-2x ULN) | Normal/High |
| **G3b (30-44)** | Moderate | ↑ | ↑↑ | ↓ | ↑↑ (2-5x ULN) | High |
| **G4 (15-29)** | Severe | ↑↑ | ↑↑↑ | ↓↓ | ↑↑↑ (5-10x ULN) | High/Adynamic |
| **G5 (<15) / Dialysis** | Failure | ↑↑↑ | ↑↑↑↑ | Absent | ↑↑↑↑ (10-50x ULN) | Adynamic/Mixed |

## 3. KDIGO 2017 Targets (CKD Stage 3-5D)

| Parameter | Target (CKD 3-5D) | Target (Dialysis) |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **PTH** | **2-9x ULN** | **2-9x ULN** |
| **Corrected Calcium** | **2.1-2.5 mmol/L** | **2.1-2.5 mmol/L** |
| **Phosphate** | **0.8-1.5 mmol/L** (Stage 3-4) | **1.1-1.5 mmol/L** (Dialysis) |
| **25-OH Vitamin D** | **≥30 ng/mL (75 nmol/L)** | **≥30 ng/mL** |
| **Alkaline Phosphatase** | Monitor Trends | Monitor Trends |

> **Note**: PTH Target is Wide (2-9x ULN) — Avoid Over-Suppression (Adynamic Bone Disease).

## 3. Management Algorithm (KDIGO 2017)

### Stepwise Approach

```
SHPT Diagnosed (CKD G3-5, PTH >9x ULN or Rising)
         │
         ▼
STEP 1: **PHOSPHATE BINDERS** (Lower Phosphate → ↓ FGF23 → ↑ Vit D → ↓ PTH)
         │
         ├── **Calcium-Based** (Calcium Carbonate/Acetate) — **First-Line if Ca²⁺ Normal/Low**
         │       Dose: 0.5-1g with Meals; Max 1.5-2g Elemental Ca/day
         │       Watch: Hypercalcaemia, Vascular Calcification
         │
         ├── **Non-Calcium-Based** (Sevelamer, Lanthanum, Sucroferric Oxyhydroxide)
         │       **First-Line if Ca²⁺ High / Vascular Calcification / Adynamic Bone**
         │       Sevelamer 800-2400mg TDS with Meals
         │
         └── **Ferric Citrate** (Newer; Also Treats Anaemia)
                 Dose: 1g TDS with Meals
                 │
         ▼
STEP 2: **VITAMIN D ANALOGUES** (If PTH Still >9x ULN after Phosphate Control)
         │
         ├── **Alfacalcidol (1α-OH D3)** 0.25-1µg/day (or 1µg Alternate Days)
         ├── **Calcitriol (1,25-(OH)₂D)** 0.25-0.5µg/day (or 1µg Alternate Days)
         ├── **Paricalcitol** (Selective VDR Agonist) 1-2µg 3x/week (Post-HD)
         │       Advantage: Less Hypercalcaemia/Hyperphosphataemia
         │
         ▼
STEP 3: **CALCIMIMETICS** (If PTH Still >9x ULN Despite Above)
         │
         ├── **Cinacalcet** 30-90mg BD (Max 180mg/day)
         │       Mechanism: CaSR Agonist → ↓ PTH Secretion
         │       Adverse: Hypocalcaemia, Nausea, QTc Prolongation
         │       Advantage: ↓ PTH, ↓ Ca, ↓ Phosphate
         │
         └── **Etelcalcetide** (IV, Post-HD) 5-15mg 3x/week (Dialysis Only)
                 │
                 ▼
STEP 4: **PARATHYROIDECTOMY** (If Refractory SHPT)
```

## 4. Vitamin D Therapy — Key Distinctions

| Agent | Active Metabolites | Indication | Advantage |
|-------|-------------------|------------|-----------|
| **Cholecalciferol (D3) / Ergocalciferol (D2)** | Requires 25-OH → 1,25-(OH)₂D (Renal) | **Nutritional Deficiency** (CKD G1-3) | Cheap; Long Half-Life |
| **Alfacalcidol (1α-OH D3)** | **1,25-(OH)₂D** (Rapid, No Renal Step) | **CKD G4-5 / Dialysis** | Predictable; Short Half-Life |
| **Calcitriol (1,25-(OH)₂D₃)** | **Active Hormone** | CKD G4-5 / Dialysis | Potent; Short Half-Life |
| **Paricalcitol** | **Selective VDR Agonist** | CKD G4-5 / Dialysis | **Less Hypercalcaemia/Phosphataemia** |
| **Doxercalciferol** | 1α-OH D2 (Prohormone) | CKD G4-5 | Less Hypercalcaemia |

> **Key**: **Nutritional Vit D (D2/D3) for CKD G1-3**; **Active Analogues (Alfacalcidol/Calcitriol/Paricalcitol) for CKD G4-5/Dialysis**.

## 5. Calcimimetics (Cinacalcet / Etelcalcetide)

| Drug | Dose | Mechanism | Adverse Effects | Monitoring |
|------|------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| **Cinacalcet** | 30-180mg/day (Divided BD) | **CaSR Agonist** → ↓ PTH Secretion | **Hypocalcaemia**, Nausea, QTc Prolongation | Ca²⁺, PTH q1-2mo; ECG (QTc) |
| **Etelcalcetide** | 5-15mg 3x/week (IV Post-HD) | CaSR Agonist (IV) | Hypocalcaemia, Hypotension | Ca²⁺, PTH Monthly |

**Key Advantage**: **↓ PTH + ↓ Ca²⁺ + ↓ Phosphate** (Unique Triple Effect).

## 5. Parathyroidectomy (PTX) — Indications for Refractory SHPT

| Indication | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| **Medical Failure** | PTH **>800-1000 pg/mL (9x ULN)** Despite Maximal Medical Therapy |
| **Hypercalcaemia** | Persistent **Ca²⁺ >2.75 mmol/L** Despite Medical Therapy |
| **Hyperphosphataemia** | Persistent **Phosphate >1.8 mmol/L** Despite Binders |
| **Symptoms** | Severe Bone Pain, Pruritus, Calciphylaxis, Fractures |
| **Adynamic Bone** | Exclude (Low PTH <2x ULN + Low ALP) → **Contraindicated** |

### Surgical Approach
| Procedure | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Subtotal (3.5-Gland) PTX** | Standard for SHPT; Leave 50-70mg Remnant |
| **Total PTX + Autotransplant** | Forearm (Brachioradialis); Higher Recurrence Risk |
| **Intraoperative PTH** | >50% Drop at 10min Post-Excision (Miami Criterion) |

## 5. Complications of SHPT & Treatment

| Complication | Mechanism | Monitoring |
|------------|-----------|------------|
| **Renal Osteodystrophy** | High Turnover (Osteitis Fibrosa) → Fractures | DEXA q2-3yr; ALP, PTH |
| **Adynamic Bone Disease** | **PTH <100-150 pg/mL** (Over-Suppression) | PTH >2x ULN; Avoid Over-Suppression |
| **Vascular Calcification** | Ca × Phosphate Product >4.5; High Phosphate | CXR/CT (Coronary/Valvular); Phosphate Control |
| **Calciphylaxis** | Medial Calcification → Skin Necrosis | Urgent PTX; Sodium Thiosulfate |
| **Fracture Risk** | High Turnover + Adynamic Bone | DEXA q2-3yr; Vertebral Fracture Assessment |

## 8. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Nutritional Vit D vs Active Analogues** | Cholecalciferol (D3) needs renal 1α-hydroxylation — **Useless in CKD G4-5**; Use Alfacalcidol/Calcitriol/Paricalcitol |
| **PTH Target** | **2-9x ULN** — Not Normalisation! Too Low = Adynamic Bone |
| **Calcium Binders** | Calcium-Based = Cheapest; Non-Calcium (Sevelamer/Lanthanum) if Hypercalcaemia/Calcification |
| **Cinacalcet vs Vitamin D** | Cinacalcet = ↓ PTH + ↓ Ca + ↓ Phos; Vit D = ↑ Ca + ↑ Phos (Risk) |
| **Alfacalcidol vs Calcitriol** | Alfacalcidol (1α-OH D3) → Rapid Conversion; Calcitriol = Active Form |
| **Paricalcitol** | Selective VDR Agonist; **Less Hypercalcaemia/Phosphataemia** than Calcitriol |
| **Adynamic Bone** | PTH <2x ULN; Low ALP; High Fracture Risk; Avoid Over-Suppression |
| **Calciphylaxis** | Urgent PTX + Sodium Thiosulfate + Pain Control; Mortality 50-80% |
