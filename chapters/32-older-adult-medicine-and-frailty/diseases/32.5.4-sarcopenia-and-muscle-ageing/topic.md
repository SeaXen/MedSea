# Sarcopenia and muscle ageing

---

## Definition

Sarcopenia is a progressive, generalised skeletal muscle disorder involving accelerated loss of muscle mass and function, associated with increased adverse outcomes including falls, frailty, functional decline, and mortality. It is a major component of frailty and is increasingly common with age.

## Core Clinical Content

### Sarcopenia definition
Progressive loss of skeletal muscle mass and function; ageing-related

### Sarcopenia prevalence
5-13% of 60-70 years; 11-50% of ≥80 years

### Primary sarcopenia
Age-related; no other identifiable cause

### Secondary sarcopenia
Due to disease (cancer, CHF, COPD), inactivity, malnutrition, endocrine

### Diagnosis (EWGSOP2)
Low muscle strength (grip/chair stand) + low muscle quantity/quality; severity by performance

### Sarcopenic obesity
Low muscle mass + high fat mass; particularly in older adults; worse outcomes

### Pathophysiology
↓ anabolic hormones (testosterone, GH, IGF-1), ↑ inflammation, ↓ protein synthesis, ↑ apoptosis

### Anorexia of ageing
↓ appetite, ↓ taste/smell, ↓ gastric motility; contributes to sarcopenia

### Management
Resistance exercise (most effective), protein intake 1.2-1.5 g/kg/day, vitamin D

### Outcomes
↑ Falls, ↑ fractures, ↑ disability, ↑ mortality; reversible with early intervention
