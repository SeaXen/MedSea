# Comprehensive geriatric assessment (CGA)

---

## Definition

Comprehensive geriatric assessment (CGA) is a multidimensional, multidisciplinary diagnostic process used to determine an older person's medical, psychological, and functional capabilities, with the goal of developing a coordinated and integrated plan for treatment and long-term follow-up.

## Core Clinical Content

### CGA definition
Multidimensional, multidisciplinary assessment of older adults

### CGA domains
Medical, functional (ADL, IADL), cognitive, psychological, social, environmental, spiritual

### CGA team
Geriatrician, nurse, OT, PT, social worker, pharmacist, dietitian

### ADL assessment
Basic activities: bathing, dressing, toileting, transferring, continence, feeding

### IADL assessment
Instrumental: shopping, cooking, housekeeping, finances, medications, transport

### Barthel Index
Standardised ADL assessment; 0-20 scale; lower = more dependent

### Lawton IADL
Standardised IADL assessment; 0-8 scale

### Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE)
30-point cognitive screen; <24 suggests dementia

### Clock drawing test
Quick screen for visuospatial/executive function

### CGA benefits
↓ Mortality, ↑ function, ↓ hospitalisation, ↓ NH placement; evidence-based
