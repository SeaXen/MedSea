# Frailty syndrome

---

## Definition

Frailty is a distinctive health state related to the ageing process in which multiple body systems gradually lose their inbuilt reserves, characterised by increased vulnerability to stressors, reduced physiological reserve, and increased risk of adverse outcomes including disability, falls, hospitalisation, and death.

## Core Clinical Content

### Frailty definition
Geriatric syndrome of decreased reserve and resistance to stressors; multi-system

### Frailty prevalence
10% of ≥65, 25-50% of ≥85; more common in women, low SES

### Frailty models
Phenotype (Fried): 5 criteria; deficit accumulation (Rockwood/Mitnitski)

### Fried frailty criteria (≥3 of 5)
Weight loss (unintentional), exhaustion, weakness (grip), slow walking speed, low physical activity

### Pre-frailty
1-2 of Fried criteria; reversible with intervention

### Rockwood Frailty Index
Deficit accumulation: count of health deficits; continuous score (0-1)

### Clinical Frailty Scale (CFS)
9-point scale; 1 (very fit) to 9 (terminally ill); widely used in clinical practice

### Pathophysiology
↓ Muscle mass, ↓ bone density, ↓ cognition, ↓ immunity, ↑ inflammation

### Outcomes
↑ Falls, ↑ fractures, ↑ hospitalisation, ↑ disability, ↑ mortality

### Management
Exercise (resistance + aerobic), protein intake, vitamin D, polypharmacy review; CGA
