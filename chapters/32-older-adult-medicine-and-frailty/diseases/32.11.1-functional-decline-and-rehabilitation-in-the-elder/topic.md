# Functional decline and rehabilitation in the elderly

---

## Definition

Functional decline in the elderly is the loss of ability to perform activities essential to independent living, often triggered by acute illness, hospitalisation, or decompensation of chronic disease. Comprehensive rehabilitation addresses physical, cognitive, and social domains to optimise independence and quality of life.

## Core Clinical Content

### Functional decline
Loss of ADL/IADL; often triggered by acute illness; ↑ in hospitalised elderly

### Hospital-associated deconditioning
Bed rest → ↓ muscle mass 1-5%/day; ↓ aerobic capacity; common in elderly

### ADL vs IADL
ADL: basic (bathing, dressing, toileting, transferring, continence, feeding); IADL: complex (cooking, shopping)

### Comprehensive rehabilitation
Multidisciplinary: PT (mobility, strength), OT (ADL, IADL), SALT (swallowing, communication)

### Goals of rehabilitation
Maximise independence, quality of life, function; realistic goal-setting with patient/family

### Strength training in elderly
Safe, effective; improves function, falls, mortality; 2-3 times/week

### Balance training
Tai chi, Otago; ↓ falls; improves confidence, mobility

### Home assessment
OT home visit; remove hazards; equipment; adapt environment

### Discharge planning
Early, multidisciplinary; consider home vs intermediate care vs residential care

### Predictors of poor recovery
Advanced age, dementia, frailty, multiple comorbidities, malnutrition, severe disability
