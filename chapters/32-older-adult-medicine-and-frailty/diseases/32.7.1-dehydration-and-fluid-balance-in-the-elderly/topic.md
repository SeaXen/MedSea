# Dehydration and fluid balance in the elderly

---

## Definition

Dehydration is a state of negative fluid balance, common in older adults due to reduced thirst sensation, decreased renal concentrating ability, and multiple comorbidities and medications. It is associated with increased morbidity (falls, delirium, AKI) and mortality.

## Core Clinical Content

### Dehydration prevalence
Common cause of hospitalisation; ↑ morbidity and mortality in elderly

### Reduced thirst sensation
↑ Osmoreceptor threshold; ↓ response to hypertonicity; ↑ ADH but ↓ response

### Reduced renal function
↓ GFR, ↓ concentrating ability, ↓ Na+ reabsorption; ↑ risk of dehydration and hyponatraemia

### Causes of dehydration
↓ intake (swallowing, cognitive, depression), ↑ losses (diuretics, vomiting, diarrhoea, fever)

### Hypernatraemic dehydration
Water loss > Na+ loss; ↑ Na+; common in frail, immobile, febrile elderly

### Hyponatraemia in elderly
Common; SIADH (SSRIs, antipsychotics), thiazides, heart failure, cirrhosis

### Clinical signs of dehydration
Dry mucosa, ↓ skin turgor (unreliable in elderly), tachycardia, hypotension, confusion, oliguria

### Diagnosis
Clinical; urea:creatinine ratio >20, ↑ serum Na+ (hypernatraemic), ↑ haematocrit

### Management
Oral rehydration if possible; IV 0.9% saline; correct slowly (avoid cerebral oedema)

### Prevention
Regular fluid rounds, accessible drinks, address dysphagia, humidified O2
