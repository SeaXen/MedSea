# Advance care planning and end-of-life decision-making

---

## Definition

Advance care planning is the process of discussing and documenting an individual's preferences for future medical care, particularly at the end of life, while they retain capacity. It includes advance decisions to refuse treatment, lasting power of attorney, and other directives.

## Core Clinical Content

### Advance care planning (ACP)
Process of discussing and documenting future care preferences while patient has capacity

### ACP benefits
Respects autonomy; ↓ unwanted treatment; ↓ family distress; ↓ conflict

### When to discuss
Early in disease course; routinely for chronic/progressive; annually review

### Advance Decision to Refuse Treatment (ADRT)
Legally binding; specific treatment refused; applies when patient loses capacity

### ADRT validity
≥18, capacity, voluntary, informed, specific; ADRT for life-sustaining treatment must be in writing with witness

### Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA)
Property/financial LPA + Health/welfare LPA; registered; acts when patient loses capacity

### Best Interests Decision
For those without capacity and no LPA/ADRT; multidisciplinary; considers past/present wishes

### Do Not Attempt Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (DNACPR)
Documented decision; respects autonomy; for patients with capacity to refuse

### Communication
Discuss with patient, family, MDT; document clearly; share with all relevant parties

### ReSPECT process
Recommended Summary Plan for Emergency Care and Treatment; UK; replaces DNACPR form
