# Hip fracture and orthogeriatric care

---

## Definition

Hip fracture is a fracture of the proximal femur, common in older adults usually due to falls from standing height (fragility fracture). Orthogeriatric care is a collaborative model between orthopaedic surgeons and geriatricians, with evidence of improved outcomes for older patients with hip fracture.

## Core Clinical Content

### Hip fracture prevalence
~70,000/year UK; most common fragility fracture; 90% in ≥65; F:M 3:1

### Hip fracture types
Intracapsular (subcapital, transcervical) and extracapsular (intertrochanteric, subtrochanteric)

### Clinical presentation
Fall, hip pain, shortened and externally rotated leg, unable to weight-bear

### Investigations
X-ray (AP pelvis); CT/MRI if X-ray negative but high suspicion

### Management — early surgery
Within 36 hours of admission; ↓ mortality, ↓ complications, ↓ length of stay

### Surgical options — intracapsular
Displaced (>50%): hemiarthroplasty or total hip replacement (THR); undisplaced: internal fixation

### Surgical options — extracapsular
Internal fixation (DHS, IM nail); depends on fracture pattern and bone quality

### Orthogeriatric care
Joint orthopaedic-geriatric management; evidence-based; improves outcomes

### Pre-operative optimisation
Correct anaemia, anticoagulation reversal, hydration, comorbidities

### Complications
Delirium (most common), VTE, infection, pressure sores, mortality 10% at 30 days, 30% at 1 year
