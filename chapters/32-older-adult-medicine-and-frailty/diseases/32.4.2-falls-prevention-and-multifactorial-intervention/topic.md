# Falls prevention and multifactorial intervention

---

## Definition

Falls prevention in the elderly is a multifactorial, multidisciplinary approach addressing intrinsic (person-related) and extrinsic (environment-related) risk factors, with evidence supporting exercise, home modifications, medication review, and vision correction as effective interventions.

## Core Clinical Content

### Multifactorial falls assessment
Identify all modifiable risk factors; address each

### Evidence-based interventions
Exercise (Otago, tai chi), home safety, vitamin D, multifactorial intervention

### Otago exercise programme
Strength and balance training; ↓ falls by 30-50%

### Tai chi
Improves balance, strength, confidence; effective in community-dwelling elderly

### Home safety assessment
Remove hazards (loose rugs, poor lighting, clutter), grab rails, non-slip mats

### Medication review
Reduce/stop psychotropics, sedatives, antihypertensives, anticholinergics

### Vitamin D supplementation
For those with deficiency or limited sun exposure; may ↓ falls

### Vision correction
Cataract surgery; treat refractive errors; avoid multifocal glasses when walking

### Foot care and footwear
Podiatry; appropriate shoes (low heel, non-slip sole, supportive)

### Falls clinics
Multidisciplinary assessment and intervention; evidence-based
