# Palliative care in the elderly

---

## Definition

Palliative care in the elderly is an approach that improves quality of life of patients and families facing life-limiting illness, through prevention and relief of suffering by early identification, assessment, and treatment of pain and other physical, psychosocial, and spiritual problems.

## Core Clinical Content

### Palliative care definition
Approach improving quality of life; physical, psychosocial, spiritual care

### Palliative care in elderly
Not just cancer; CHF, COPD, dementia, frailty, multi-organ failure all benefit

### When to refer
Any life-limiting illness; complex symptoms; difficult decisions; end-of-life care

### Symptom management
Pain, dyspnoea, nausea, constipation, delirium, anxiety, depression, fatigue

### WHO analgesic ladder
Step 1: non-opioid (paracetamol) ± adjuvant; Step 2: weak opioid (codeine, tramadol); Step 3: strong opioid (morphine)

### Opioid prescribing in elderly
Start low, go slow; oral preferred; laxative; antiemetic; regular review

### Breathlessness
Non-pharmacological (positioning, fan); opioids (morphine); benzodiazepines if anxiety

### Constipation
Common; prophylactic laxatives with opioids

### Delirium in palliative care
Common; causes: drugs, metabolic, infection; manage underlying cause if possible

### Palliative care emergencies
Spinal cord compression, hypercalcaemia, haemorrhage, SVC syndrome — treat with steroids, etc.
