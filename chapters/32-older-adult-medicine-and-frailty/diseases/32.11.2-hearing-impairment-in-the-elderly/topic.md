# Hearing impairment in the elderly

---

## Definition

Hearing impairment (presbycusis and conductive losses) is a common age-related condition with significant impact on communication, cognition, and quality of life in older adults. It is associated with social isolation, depression, and cognitive decline.

## Core Clinical Content

### Hearing loss prevalence
33% of ≥65; 50% of ≥75; 80% of nursing home residents

### Presbycusis
Age-related bilateral sensorineural high-frequency loss; gradual; due to hair cell loss in cochlea

### Conductive hearing loss
Outer/middle ear pathology; wax (very common, easily treated), otitis media, otosclerosis

### Sudden sensorineural hearing loss
ENT emergency; treat with high-dose steroids within 48 h; consider acoustic neuroma

### Cerumen impaction
Very common in elderly; causes 30% of hearing aid failure; treat with ear drops or irrigation

### Ototoxic drugs
Aminoglycosides, loop diuretics, cisplatin, quinine; monitor in elderly

### Hearing aids
Underused; improve quality of life, cognition, social participation; consider for ≥30 dB loss

### Tinnitus
Common in elderly; often with presbycusis; CBT, sound therapy; rule out acoustic neuroma

### Impact of hearing loss
↑ Social isolation, ↑ depression, ↑ cognitive decline (3-5x dementia risk), ↑ falls

### Communication strategies
Face patient, reduce background noise, speak slowly, consider hearing loop
