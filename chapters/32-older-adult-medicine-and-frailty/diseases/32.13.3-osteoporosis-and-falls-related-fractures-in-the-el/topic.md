# Osteoporosis and falls-related fractures in the elderly

---

## Definition

Osteoporosis is a systemic skeletal disease characterised by low bone mass and microarchitectural deterioration of bone tissue, leading to increased bone fragility and fracture risk. Fragility fractures (especially hip, vertebra, wrist) are a major cause of morbidity and mortality in older adults.

## Core Clinical Content

### Osteoporosis definition
T-score ≤-2.5 SD on DEXA; osteopenia -1 to -2.5

### Prevalence
50% of women, 20% of men ≥50; ↑ with age

### Risk factors (non-modifiable)
Age, female, early menopause, family history, Caucasian/Asian, low BMI

### Risk factors (modifiable)
Smoking, alcohol, sedentary, low Ca/vit D, low weight, glucocorticoids

### DEXA scan
Gold standard; measures BMD at hip and spine; T-score comparison to young adult

### FRAX score
10-year fracture risk calculator; combines clinical risk factors ± BMD; guides treatment

### Fragility fractures
Hip, vertebra, wrist, proximal humerus, pelvis; from low-energy trauma (fall from standing)

### Hip fracture
90-day mortality 20-30%; surgery within 36 h; early mobilisation; MDT care

### Vertebral fracture
Often asymptomatic; back pain, height loss, kyphosis; may cause restrictive lung disease

### Treatment
Bisphosphonates (alendronate, zoledronic acid), denosumab, teriparatide; Ca + vit D supplementation
