# Falls in the elderly

---

## Definition

A fall is an event resulting in a person coming to rest inadvertently on the ground or floor, often with injury. Falls are a major cause of morbidity, mortality, and loss of independence in older adults, with multifactorial aetiology requiring comprehensive assessment.

## Core Clinical Content

### Falls prevalence
30% of ≥65 fall at least once/year; 50% of ≥80; 10-15% result in serious injury

### Falls consequences
Fractures (especially hip), head injury, hospitalisation, loss of independence, death

### Falls risk factors (intrinsic)
Age, female, previous falls, gait/balance disorder, weakness, cognitive impairment, vision, postural hypotension, incontinence

### Falls risk factors (extrinsic)
Polypharmacy (especially sedatives, antihypertensives, hypoglycaemics), environment, footwear

### Medication risk
Benzodiazepines, antipsychotics, opioids, antihypertensives, anticholinergics

### Falls assessment
History, examination (gait, balance, vision, neuro, CVS), medication review, home assessment

### Get Up and Go test
Timed up-and-go; ≥12 seconds = ↑ falls risk

### Tinetti balance assessment
Balance and gait scoring; <19 = high falls risk

### Falls intervention
Multifactorial: exercise (Otago), home safety, medication review, vitamin D, vision correction

### Falls prevention
Tai chi, strength/balance training, home modifications, podiatry
