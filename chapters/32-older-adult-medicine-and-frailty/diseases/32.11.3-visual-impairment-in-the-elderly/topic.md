# Visual impairment in the elderly

---

## Definition

Visual impairment is a significant cause of disability and reduced quality of life in the elderly, with age-related changes affecting all ocular structures. Common conditions include cataract, age-related macular degeneration (AMD), glaucoma, and diabetic retinopathy.

## Core Clinical Content

### Visual impairment prevalence
Significant visual impairment in 5-10% of ≥65; 20% of ≥85; ↑ with age

### Cataract
Lens opacity; most common cause reversible visual loss; risk ↑ age, smoking, DM, steroids

### Cataract symptoms
Gradual painless ↓ vision, glare, ↓ colour perception; surgical removal + lens implant

### Age-related macular degeneration (AMD)
Central vision loss; drusen; risk: age, smoking, family history; ARED-2 vitamins may slow progression

### AMD types
Dry (90% — drusen, atrophy; no treatment); Wet (10% — neovascularisation; anti-VEGF injection)

### Glaucoma
↑ Intraocular pressure → optic nerve damage; chronic open-angle (most common in elderly)

### Glaucoma symptoms
Peripheral vision loss first; tunnel vision; treatment: drops (prostaglandin), laser, surgery

### Diabetic retinopathy
Microaneurysms, exudates, haemorrhages, neovascularisation; screening annually; anti-VEGF for wet

### Other (elderly)
Dry eye (very common), blepharitis, ptosis, presbyopia

### Impact of visual loss
↑ Falls, ↑ depression, ↑ social isolation, ↑ cognitive decline; importance of early detection
