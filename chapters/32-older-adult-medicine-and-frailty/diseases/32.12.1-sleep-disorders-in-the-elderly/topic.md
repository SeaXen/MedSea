# Sleep disorders in the elderly

---

## Definition

Sleep disorders are common in older adults, with changes in sleep architecture, increased prevalence of insomnia, sleep apnoea, restless legs syndrome, and other conditions. Sleep disturbance has major impact on quality of life, cognition, falls, and mood in the elderly.

## Core Clinical Content

### Age-related sleep changes
↓ Slow wave sleep, ↓ sleep efficiency, ↑ sleep fragmentation, phase advance (earlier bedtime/wake)

### Insomnia prevalence in elderly
20-30% of community elderly; ↑ in nursing home, dementia, depression

### Insomnia causes
Medical (pain, nocturia, dyspnoea), psychiatric (depression, anxiety), medications, primary sleep disorders

### Sleep apnoea in elderly
↑ Prevalence; OSA associated with ↑ cardiovascular risk, daytime sleepiness, cognitive decline

### Restless legs syndrome (RLS)
Common in elderly; urge to move legs, worse at rest/evening; iron deficiency common

### REM sleep behaviour disorder (RBD)
Acting out dreams; common in neurodegenerative diseases (Parkinson's, DLB)

### Sleep and dementia
Sleep-wake cycle disruption common; sundowning; improve sleep hygiene

### Sleep and depression
Bidirectional; insomnia ↑ depression risk; depression disturbs sleep architecture

### Non-pharmacological
Sleep hygiene, CBT-I, ↑ daytime light, ↓ caffeine, regular schedule

### Pharmacological
Melatonin first-line; short-term Z-drugs (zopiclone) if needed; AVOID long-term benzodiazepines
