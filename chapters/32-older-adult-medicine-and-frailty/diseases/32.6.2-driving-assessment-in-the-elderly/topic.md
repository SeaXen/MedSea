# Driving assessment in the elderly

---

## Definition

Driving assessment in older adults involves evaluating fitness to drive in the context of age-related physiological changes, medical conditions, and medications. It is an important clinical and legal issue with implications for independence, safety, and quality of life.

## Core Clinical Content

### Driving in elderly
Independent mobility important; but age-related changes ↑ accident risk

### DVLA notification (UK)
Patient must notify DVLA of any medical condition that may affect driving; doctor should advise patient

### Doctor's duty
Advise patient to notify DVLA; if patient refuses AND public safety at risk, break confidentiality

### Dementia and driving
DVLA must be notified; usually cease driving; MMSE alone insufficient; on-road assessment

### Epilepsy and driving
1 year seizure-free for ordinary licence; 6 months for vocational

### Diabetes and driving
Insulin-treated diabetics: notify DVLA; must not drive if hypoglycaemia unawareness

### Cardiac conditions and driving
MI: 4 weeks off; angina if at rest: cease; ICD: 6 months off

### Stroke/TIA and driving
1 month off for stroke; 2 weeks for single TIA; neurological assessment

### Visual acuity requirement
UK: 6/9 (Snellen) + 6/12 both eyes; visual field ≥120°

### Medication and driving
Sedatives, opioids, anticonvulsants may impair; counsel patients
