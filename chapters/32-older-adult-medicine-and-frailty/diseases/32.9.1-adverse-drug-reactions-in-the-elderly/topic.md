# Adverse drug reactions in the elderly

---

## Definition

Adverse drug reactions (ADRs) are harmful, unintended responses to medications, occurring at doses normally used in man. In the elderly, ADRs are common due to polypharmacy, altered pharmacokinetics, and increased vulnerability, and may present atypically (confusion, falls) rather than with the classic drug-specific symptoms.

## Core Clinical Content

### ADR prevalence
10-20% of hospital admissions in elderly; ↑ with polypharmacy

### Atypical ADR presentation
Confusion, falls, immobility, incontinence, constipation, weight loss

### Common ADRs in elderly
Hypotension (antihypertensives), falls (sedatives), confusion (anticholinergics), bleeding (warfarin)

### Anticholinergic ADR
Confusion, dry mouth, blurred vision, urinary retention, constipation

### Bleeding risk
Warfarin, DOACs, antiplatelets, NSAIDs; ↑ in elderly

### Hypoglycaemia
Insulin, sulfonylureas (long-acting); unpredictable; severe consequences

### SIADH
SSRIs, antipsychotics, anticonvulsants; ↓ Na+ in elderly

### Falls-inducing drugs
Benzodiazepines, antipsychotics, opioids, antihypertensives, diuretics, anticholinergics

### Acute kidney injury
NSAIDs, ACEi, diuretics ('triple whammy'); common in dehydration

### Prevention of ADRs
Medication review, start low go slow, patient/carer education, monitoring
