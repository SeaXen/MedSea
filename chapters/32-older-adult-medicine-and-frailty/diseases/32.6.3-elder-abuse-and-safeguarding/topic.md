# Elder abuse and safeguarding

---

## Definition

Elder abuse is a single or repeated act, or lack of appropriate action, occurring within any relationship where there is an expectation of trust, that causes harm or distress to an older person. It is under-recognised, often hidden, and requires a high index of suspicion and safeguarding procedures.

## Core Clinical Content

### Elder abuse prevalence
10% of elderly; higher in dementia, disability, dependence; under-recognised

### Types of abuse
Physical, psychological, sexual, financial, neglect, self-neglect, discrimination

### Risk factors
Carer stress, substance use, mental health, social isolation, cognitive impairment, dependence

### Warning signs
Unexplained injuries, malnutrition, dehydration, fear, depression, withdrawal, financial anomalies

### Screening questions
Have you been hurt? Are you afraid? Has anyone taken your things without asking?

### UK safeguarding
Care Act 2014; section 42 enquiry for adults at risk of abuse/neglect

### Duty to report
Health/social care professionals; raising concern; follows local safeguarding procedures

### Multi-agency safeguarding
Police, social services, health, voluntary sector; coordinated response

### Self-neglect
Often in mental illness, dementia, substance use; requires assessment of capacity, support

### Capacity and abuse
If patient has capacity and is not at serious risk, may refuse intervention; document carefully
