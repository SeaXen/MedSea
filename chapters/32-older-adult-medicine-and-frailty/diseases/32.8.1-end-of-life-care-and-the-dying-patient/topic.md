# End-of-life care and the dying patient

---

## Definition

End-of-life care is the support and medical care given during the time surrounding death, focusing on comfort, dignity, and quality of life, addressing physical, psychological, social, and spiritual needs of the patient and their family.

## Core Clinical Content

### Recognising dying
↓ Consciousness, ↓ oral intake, ↓ mobility, Cheyne-Stokes respiration, mottling

### Comfort care
Stop unnecessary medications; essential medications only; subcutaneous route

### Symptom management in dying
Pain, dyspnoea, agitation, respiratory secretions, nausea — regular review

### Respiratory secretions ('death rattle')
Noisy breathing; repositioning, hyoscine butylbromide, glycopyrronium

### Terminal agitation
Midazolam SC; haloperidol if delirium; review underlying cause

### End-of-life care pathway
Individualised; document decisions; regular review; family communication

### Liverpool Care Pathway (LCP)
Replaced by individualised end-of-life care plans; principles: comfort, dignity, communication

### Family support
Information, support, practical help, bereavement support

### Spiritual care
Address spiritual needs; chaplains, faith leaders; cultural sensitivity

### Death verification
Absence of cardiorespiratory function for 5 min; pupil fixed; no response to stimuli
