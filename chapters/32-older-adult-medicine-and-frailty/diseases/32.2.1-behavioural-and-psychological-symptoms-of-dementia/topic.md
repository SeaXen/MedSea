# Behavioural and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD)

---

## Definition

Behavioural and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD) are non-cognitive symptoms of dementia, including agitation, aggression, psychosis, mood disturbance, sleep disturbance, and wandering. They are common, cause significant distress, and are often treatable.

## Core Clinical Content

### BPSD definition
Non-cognitive symptoms of dementia; common (~90% over disease course)

### BPSD categories
Behavioural (agitation, aggression, wandering), psychological (psychosis, mood, anxiety)

### BPSD causes
Pain, infection, constipation, dehydration, medication, environment, depression

### Non-pharmacological first
Person-centred care, environment, activities, communication; first-line

### ABC approach
Antecedent, Behaviour, Consequence; identify triggers and patterns

### Antipsychotic use
Limited to severe agitation/aggression; lowest dose, shortest time; black box warning

### Antipsychotic risks in dementia
↑ Mortality, ↑ cerebrovascular events, ↑ pneumonia, ↑ falls

### Antidepressants
SSRIs for depression, anxiety, agitation in dementia; citalopram may help agitation

### Benzodiazepines
AVOID; ↑ falls, confusion, paradoxical agitation; reserve for alcohol withdrawal

### Cholinesterase inhibitors
May help BPSD (especially apathy, depression) in Alzheimer's; modest effect
