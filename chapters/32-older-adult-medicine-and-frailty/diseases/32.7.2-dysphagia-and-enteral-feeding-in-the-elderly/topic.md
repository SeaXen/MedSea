# Dysphagia and enteral feeding in the elderly

---

## Definition

Dysphagia is difficulty swallowing, common in older adults due to neurological conditions (stroke, dementia, Parkinson's), structural causes, and medications. It is associated with malnutrition, dehydration, aspiration pneumonia, and increased mortality, requiring comprehensive assessment and management.

## Core Clinical Content

### Dysphagia prevalence
30-40% of stroke, 50-80% of advanced dementia, 30% of Parkinson's

### Dysphagia causes (elderly)
Stroke, dementia, Parkinson's, motor neurone disease, head and neck cancer, strictures, achalasia

### Oropharyngeal vs oesophageal
Oropharyngeal: initiation problem (stroke, dementia); oesophageal: transit problem (stricture, motility)

### Bedside swallowing assessment
Sips of water (3 oz test); observe for cough, wet voice, delayed swallow

### Videofluoroscopy (VFS)
Gold standard; modified barium swallow; assess oral, pharyngeal, oesophageal phases

### FEES
Fibreoptic endoscopic evaluation of swallowing; bedside; real-time visualisation

### Aspiration pneumonia
Common complication; risks: ↓ consciousness, dysphagia, ↓ cough, NG tube, poor oral hygiene

### Dysphagia diet modification
Texture modification (soft, pureed); thickened fluids; chin-tuck, head turn

### NG tube feeding
Short-term (<4 weeks); for acute stroke, reversible dysphagia

### PEG tube
Long-term (>4 weeks); controversial in advanced dementia; ethical considerations
