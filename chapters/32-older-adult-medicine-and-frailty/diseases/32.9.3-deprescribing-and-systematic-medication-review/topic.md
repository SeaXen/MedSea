# Deprescribing and systematic medication review

---

## Definition

Deprescribing is the systematic process of identifying and discontinuing medications where harms outweigh benefits, with the goal of reducing polypharmacy and adverse drug reactions while maintaining or improving quality of life. It is a key component of geriatric care.

## Core Clinical Content

### Deprescribing definition
Supervised withdrawal of inappropriate medications; shared decision-making

### When to consider
Polypharmacy, ADRs, falls, cognitive decline, end-of-life, patient request

### Benefits of deprescribing
↓ ADRs, ↓ falls, ↓ pill burden, ↑ adherence, cost savings, improved QoL

### Barriers
Patient/carer resistance, prescriber inertia, fear of withdrawal, lack of evidence

### Step 1: Identify
List all medications; identify indication, effectiveness, safety, adherence

### Step 2: Decide
Which medications can be stopped? Consider patient goals, life expectancy

### Step 3: Plan
Withdraw one drug at a time; start with lowest risk (e.g., supplements, PPIs)

### Step 4: Implement
Taper slowly (especially long-half-life drugs); monitor for withdrawal/rebound

### Step 5: Review
Monitor for benefit (↓ symptoms) or harm (withdrawal, disease recurrence)

### Common drugs to deprescribe
PPIs (>8 weeks), benzodiazepines, anticholinergics, statins in limited life expectancy
