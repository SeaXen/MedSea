# Pressure ulcers (pressure sores) in the elderly

---

## Definition

Pressure ulcers (also called pressure sores, decubitus ulcers, or bedsores) are localised areas of tissue damage caused by sustained pressure, shear, friction, or moisture, typically over bony prominences. They are common in immobile, frail elderly and cause significant morbidity, mortality, and healthcare cost.

## Core Clinical Content

### Pressure ulcer prevalence
10-15% of hospitalised elderly; 20-30% of nursing home; ↑ in critical care, palliative

### Mechanism
Sustained pressure > capillary pressure (32 mmHg) → ischaemia → tissue necrosis

### Risk factors
Immobility, ↓ sensation, ↓ perfusion, malnutrition, dehydration, incontinence, age-related skin changes

### Sites
Sacrum, heels, trochanters, ischial tuberosities, elbows, occiput

### NPUAP/EPUAP staging (1-4)
1: non-blanchable erythema; 2: partial thickness; 3: full thickness; 4: full thickness + exposed bone/tendon

### Unstageable
Full thickness obscured by slough or eschar; needs debridement to stage

### Suspected deep tissue injury (SDTI)
Purple/maroon localised area or blood-filled blister from pressure/shear

### Prevention (SSKIN)
Surface, Skin inspection, Keep moving, Incontinence/moisture, Nutrition/hydration

### Pressure redistribution
Specialised mattresses, cushions; 2-hourly repositioning

### Management
Stage-appropriate dressing (hydrocolloid, foam, alginate); debridement; infection control; nutrition
