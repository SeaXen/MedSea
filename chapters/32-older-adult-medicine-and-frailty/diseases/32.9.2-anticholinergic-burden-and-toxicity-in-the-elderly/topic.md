# Anticholinergic burden and toxicity in the elderly

---

## Definition

Anticholinergic burden in the elderly refers to the cumulative effect of multiple medications with anticholinergic properties, leading to cognitive impairment, falls, urinary retention, constipation, and other adverse effects. It is a major and modifiable cause of harm in older adults and is often under-recognised.

## Core Clinical Content

### Anticholinergic burden definition
Cumulative anticholinergic effect of all medications; common in polypharmacy

### Prevalence
20-50% of elderly take ≥1 anticholinergic medication; often multiple

### Common anticholinergic drugs
Antihistamines (chlorpheniramine), TCAs (amitriptyline), bladder antispasmodics (oxybutynin), antipsychotics

### Anticholinergic effects
Central: confusion, ↓ cognition, hallucinations; Peripheral: dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, urinary retention, tachycardia

### CNS effects
Confusion, delirium, cognitive decline, ↓ memory; contributes to dementia risk

### Falls risk
↓ Cognitive function, ↓ postural stability, blurred vision; ↑ falls

### Anticholinergic Cognitive Burden (ACB) scale
Tool to quantify anticholinergic burden; each drug scored 0-3

### Common ACB 3 drugs (high burden)
Amitriptyline, oxybutynin, chlorpheniramine, hydroxyzine, olanzapine, paroxetine

### Deprescribing anticholinergics
Switch to alternatives (SSRI, mirabegron); consider dose reduction; non-pharmacological where possible

### Anticholinergic syndrome (acute)
Hot as a hare, dry as a bone, red as a beet, blind as a bat, mad as a hatter, full as a flask
