# Skin problems and wound care in the elderly

---

## Definition

Skin problems are common in the elderly due to age-related skin changes (xerosis, atrophy, ↓ barrier function) and comorbidities. Wound healing is impaired in older adults, requiring careful assessment and management of common conditions including pruritus, eczema, leg ulcers, and skin tears.

## Core Clinical Content

### Age-related skin changes
↓ Collagen, ↓ elastin, ↓ subcutaneous fat, ↓ barrier function, ↓ sweat, ↓ sebum

### Xerosis (dry skin)
Very common in elderly; pruritus; treatment: emollients, avoid hot water, ↑ humidity

### Pruritus (itch)
Common; causes: xerosis, eczema, scabies, drug, liver, renal, haematological

### Skin tears
Common in elderly; fragile skin; from minor trauma; prevention: protective clothing, careful handling

### Venous leg ulcer
Common; medial gaiter area; associated with venous disease; compression bandaging

### Arterial leg ulcer
Distal (toes, lateral ankle); painful, pale base; poor healing; revascularisation

### Diabetic foot ulcer
Neuroischaemic; high risk of infection/amputation; multidisciplinary foot clinic

### Pressure ulcers (covered separately)
Sacrum, heels; staged; prevention by repositioning

### Basal cell carcinoma
Most common skin cancer in elderly; pearly papule, telangiectasias; sun-exposed areas

### Wound healing
↓ in elderly; chronic wounds (>3 months); pressure, infection, diabetes, malnutrition delay healing
