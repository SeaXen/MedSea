# N-Acetylcysteine (NAC) Therapy in Acute Liver Failure

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP Priority: HIGH**
> **NAC = Universal antidote for ALL ALF** (not just paracetamol) — **Regimen: 150mg/kg/1h → 50mg/kg/4h → 100mg/kg/16h = 300mg/kg/21h**; **Improves survival in Grade I/II HE**; **Continue if INR↑/HE/renal failure**; **Beneficial in non-paracetamol ALF** (ALF-2 trial).

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Prescribe **standard NAC regimen** (300mg/kg over 21h)
- [ ] Know **when to continue NAC** beyond 21h
- [ ] Apply NAC in **non-paracetamol ALF** (ALF-2 trial evidence)
- [ ] Recognise **adverse effects** and **management**

## 1. Standard NAC Regimen (IV)

| Phase | Dose | Volume (5% Glucose) | Duration | Cumulative |
|-------|------|---------------------|----------|------------|
| **Loading** | **150 mg/kg** | 200 ml | 1 hour | 150 mg/kg |
| **Phase 2** | **50 mg/kg** | 500 ml | 4 hours | 200 mg/kg |
| **Phase 3** | **100 mg/kg** | 1000 ml | 16 hours | **300 mg/kg** |
| **Total** | **300 mg/kg** | | **21 hours** | |

> **Weight**: Use **actual body weight** (max 100kg for dose calculation)
> **Diluent**: 5% Dextrose (not saline)

### Protocol Summary
| Phase | Time | Rate | Cumulative Dose |
|-------|------|------|-----------------|
| 0-1h | 150 mg/kg | 200 ml/h | 150 mg/kg |
| 1-5h | 12.5 mg/kg/h | 125 ml/h | 200 mg/kg |
| 5-21h | 6.25 mg/kg/h | 62.5 ml/h | 300 mg/kg |

---

## 2. When to Continue NAC Beyond 21 Hours

| Indication | Action |
|------------|--------|
| **INR not improving** (INR >1.3 or rising) | **Continue Phase 3** (100mg/kg/16h) until INR improves |
| **Encephalopathy persists/worsens** | **Continue NAC** |
| **Renal failure** (Cr >300 or rising) | **Continue NAC** |
| **ALT/AST rising** | **Continue NAC** |
| **<8 hours post-ingestion with nomogram above line** | **Continue NAC** |

> **Do NOT stop at 21h automatically** if any above criteria met.

---

## 3. NAC in Non-Paracetamol ALF (ALF-2 Trial Evidence)

| Trial | Population | NAC vs Placebo | Outcome |
|-------|------------|----------------|---------|
| **ALF-2 (US)** | **Non-paracetamol ALF** (Grade I/II HE) | NAC vs Placebo | **Transplant-free survival: 40% vs 27% (p=0.02)** |
| **Follow-up** | Grade III/IV HE | NAC vs Placebo | **No significant difference** |

> **Key Finding**: **NAC improves transplant-free survival in EARLY (Grade I/II) non-paracetamol ALF** — **use in ALL ALF** regardless of aetiology.

---

## 3. NAC in Special Situations

| Scenario | Regimen Modification |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Ongoing paracetamol absorption** (staggered/massive overdose) | **Extend Phase 2/3** — continue until INR improving |
| **Late presentation (>24h)** | **Still give NAC** — benefit even late |
| **Staggered overdose** | **Treat as >150mg/kg** — full NAC regimen |
| **Paediatric dosing** | **Same mg/kg regimen** (max 300mg/kg) |
| **Pregnancy** | **Safe** — Category B |
| **Renal impairment** | **No dose adjustment** (monitor fluid balance) |
| **Fluid restriction** | Concentrate NAC in less volume (specialist advice) |

---

## 3. NAC for Paracetamol Overdose (Specific)

### Nomogram Use (UK)
| Time Post-Ingestion | Treatment Line (mg/L) |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| 4 hours | 100 mg/L |
| 6 hours | 60 mg/L |
| 8 hours | 30 mg/L |
| 10 hours | 20 mg/L |
| 12 hours | 15 mg/L |
| 15 hours | 5 mg/L |

> **High-risk line** (alcohol, fasting, CYP inducers): **Half treatment line**

### Late Presentation (>15h or Unknown Time)
| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **>15h or unknown time** | **Treat empirically with full NAC regimen** |
| **Staggered overdose** | **Full NAC regimen** (don't use nomogram) |
| **History unreliable** | **Treat empirically** |

---

## 4. NAC Continuation Beyond 21 Hours

| Continue NAC If: | Stop NAC If: |
|------------------|--------------|
| INR >1.3 or rising | INR <1.3 AND improving |
| Encephalopathy Grade II+ | Encephalopathy resolved |
| Creatinine >300 or rising | Creatinine normalising |
| ALT/AST rising | ALT/AST normalising |
| <8h post-ingestion above nomogram | >15h post-ingestion, nomogram below line, all improving |

> **Clinical judgment** — continue until clear biochemical/clinical improvement.

---

## 4. Adverse Effects & Management

| Adverse Effect | Incidence | Management |
|----------------|-----------|------------|
| **Anaphylactoid reaction** | ~3-5% (during loading) | **Stop infusion**, give **chlorphenamine 10mg IV**, **hydrocortisone 200mg IV**, **salbutamol** if bronchospasm; **restart at slower rate** after resolution |
| **Nausea/Vomiting** | Common | Antiemetics (ondansetron) |
| **Flushing/Rash** | Common | Slow infusion, antihistamines |

### Anaphylactoid Reaction Management
1. **STOP NAC infusion immediately**
2. **Chlorphenamine 10mg IV** + **Hydrocortisone 200mg IV**
3. **Salbutamol 5mg nebulised** if bronchospasm
4. **Restart NAC at 10x slower rate** after 30-60 min if essential

## 6. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **NAC only for paracetamol** | **FALSE** — NAC benefits **ALL ALF** (ALF-2 trial: Grade I/II HE) |
| **Stop at 21h automatically** | **FALSE** — Continue if INR↑/HE/renal failure/ALT↑ |
| **Nomogram use after 15h** | **Invalid** — treat empirically with full NAC regimen |
| **NAC in pregnancy** | **Safe (Category B)** — standard regimen |
| **IV vs Oral NAC** | **IV standard** (oral has poor bioavailability, vomiting); oral only if IV unavailable |
| **Fluid restriction** | Concentrate NAC in less volume (specialist advice) |

**Mnemonic: NAC-REGIMEN**
- **N**AC: **Universal ALF Antidote (Not Just Paracetamol)**
- **A**ll ALF: **Grade I/II HE Benefit (ALF-2 Trial)**
- **C**umulative Dose: **300mg/kg over 21 hours**
- **R**egimen: **150→50→100 mg/kg (1h/4h/16h)**
- **E**xtend Beyond 21h: **INR↑, HE, Renal Failure, ALT↑**
- **G**lucose: **5% Dextrose diluent (not saline)**
- **I**nfusion Rates: **200ml/1h, 500ml/4h, 1000ml/16h**
- **M**ax Dose: **300mg/kg (cap at 100kg wt)**
- **E**xtend If: **INR↑, HE, Renal, ALT↑, <8h Nomogram**
- **N**omogram: **100mg/L@4h → 15mg/L@15h (High-risk = Half)**
- **Adverse**: **Anaphylactoid (3-5%) → Stop, Chlorphenamine+Hydrocortisone, Restart Slow**
- **T**aper Restart: **10x Slower After Anaphylactoid**
- **3**00mg/kg Total: **Cap at 100kg Body Weight**
- **0-1h**: 150mg/kg; **1-5h**: 12.5mg/kg/h; **5-21h**: 6.25mg/kg/h
