# Hepatology in Special Situations

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP Priority: HIGH**
> **Special Situations = High-yield viva topics** — **Liver disease in pregnancy** (AFLP, HELLP, ICP, viral hepatitis), **Liver disease in HIV** (co-infection, ART hepatotoxicity, immune reconstitution), **Liver disease in elderly** (altered drug metabolism, reduced reserve), **Perioperative assessment** (pre-op LFTs, post-op liver dysfunction), **Critical illness** (ischaemic hepatitis/shock liver, drug-induced).

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Diagnose and manage **Acute Fatty Liver of Pregnancy (AFLP)**, **HELLP Syndrome**, **Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy (ICP)**
- [ ] Manage **Viral Hepatitis in Pregnancy** (HBV, HCV, HEV vertical transmission)
- [ ] Manage **HIV-HBV/HCV Co-infection** and **ART Hepatotoxicity**
- [ ] Apply **Perioperative Liver Assessment** (Child-Pugh, MELD, indocyanine green clearance)
- [ ] Diagnose **Ischaemic Hepatitis / Shock Liver** and differentiate from other causes
- [ ] Adjust drug dosing in **Elderly** and **Cirrhosis**

## 1. Liver Disease in Pregnancy

### Acute Fatty Liver of Pregnancy (AFLP)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Incidence** | **1:10,000-15,000** pregnancies |
| **Timing** | **Third trimester** (typically 34-38 weeks) |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Mitochondrial fatty acid oxidation defect** (fetal LCHAD deficiency → maternal accumulation of toxic metabolites) |
| **Swansea Criteria** (≥6 = AFLP) | Nausea/vomiting, abdominal pain, polydipsia/polyuria, encephalopathy, bilirubin >14 µmol/L, hypoglycaemia <4 mmol/L, uric acid >340 µmol/L, leukocytosis >11×10⁹/L, ascites/bright liver on US, PT >14s, AST >120 U/L, ALT >120 U/L, ammonia >100 µmol/L, renal impairment (Cr >150 µmol/L) |
| **Key Labs** | **Low glucose** (hypoglycaemia), **elevated uric acid**, **leukocytosis**, **prolonged PT**, **elevated AST/ALT (mild-moderate)**, **ammonia ↑**, **renal impairment** |
| **Differential** | **HELLP**, **Severe pre-eclampsia**, **Acute viral hepatitis**, **Drug-induced liver injury** |
| **Management** | **Emergency delivery** (regardless of gestation) — **Caesarean section** if maternal/fetal compromise; **ICU support** (glucose, coagulopathy correction, renal support); **Multidisciplinary** (obstetrics, hepatology, intensive care, neonatology) |
| **Prognosis** | **Maternal mortality 10-20%** (improved with early recognition); **Recurrence risk ~25%** in subsequent pregnancies |

### HELLP Syndrome
| Component | Criteria |
|-----------|----------|
| **H**aemolysis | **LDH >600 U/L**, **Schistocytes**, **Hb ↓**, **Low haptoglobin** |
| **E**levated **L**iver enzymes | **AST/ALT >120 U/L** (typically 2-10× ULN) |
| **L**ow **P**latelets | **<100 ×10⁹/L** |

| Classification (Mississippi) | Platelets | AST/ALT | LDH |
|-----------------------------|-----------|---------|-----|
| **Class I** | <50 ×10⁹/L | >120 | >600 |
| **Class II** | 50-100 ×10⁹/L | >120 | >600 |
| **Class III** | 100-150 ×10⁹/L | >120 | >600 |

| Feature | AFLP vs HELLP |
|---------|---------------|
| **Liver Enzymes** | AFLP: Mild-moderate (2-10×); **HELLP: Higher (often >20×)** |
| **Coagulopathy** | **AFLP: Severe (DIC common)**; HELLP: Platelets low, PT mild |
| **Hypoglycaemia** | **AFLP: Common (↓ gluconeogenesis)**; HELLP: Rare |
| **Renal Impairment** | **AFLP: Common**; HELLP: Less common |
| **Ammonia** | **AFLP: ↑↑** (encephalopathy); HELLP: Normal |
| **Uric Acid** | **AFLP: Markedly ↑** (>340 µmol/L); HELLP: Normal/mild ↑ |
| **Timing** | AFLP: **Late 3rd trimester (34-38 wk)**; HELLP: **24-36 wk** |

### Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy (ICP)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Incidence** | **0.5-1%** pregnancies (higher in South Asian, South American, Scandinavian) |
| **Timing** | **Late 2nd / 3rd trimester** (pruritus → LFTs) |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Hormonal (oestrogen/progesterone) + genetic (ABCB4, ABCB11)** → impaired bile acid transport |
| **Clinical** | **Pruritus (palms/soles, worse at night)**, **No rash** (excoriations only); **Dark urine** |
| **LFTs** | **Bile acids >40 µmol/L** (diagnostic), **ALT/AST mildly ↑** (2-10×), **Bilirubin normal/mild ↑** |
| **Risks to Fetus** | **Stillbirth** (risk ↑ with bile acids >100 µmol/L), **Preterm delivery**, **Meconium staining**, **RDS** |
| **Management** | **Ursodeoxycholic Acid (UDCA) 15-20mg/kg/day** (impr. pruritus, ↓ bile acids); **Vitamin K** (if PT prolonged); **Delivery at 36-37 weeks** (if bile acids >40); **>100 µmol/L → deliver 35-36 wks** |
| **Postpartum** | **Resolution within 2-4 weeks**; **Recurrence risk 60-90%** in subsequent pregnancies |

### Viral Hepatitis in Pregnancy

| Virus | Vertical Transmission | Management |
|-------|----------------------|------------|
| **HBV** | **10-90%** (HBeAg+ high risk) | **TDF 300mg OD from 28 wks** if HBV DNA >200,000 IU/mL; **HBIG + HBV vaccine at birth** (within 12h) |
| **HCV** | **5-6%** (higher if HIV co-infection) | **No DAA in pregnancy** (safety not established); **Postpartum DAA**; Breastfeeding safe |
| **HEV** | **High (50%+**, severe in 3rd trimester) | **Supportive**; **Ribavirin CONTRAINDICATED**; High maternal mortality (15-25%) |

### Vaccination in Pregnancy
| Vaccine | Timing | Safety |
|---------|--------|--------|
| **Hepatitis B** | Any trimester | **Safe** (recombinant) |
| **Hepatitis A** | If high risk | **Safe** (inactivated) |

## 2. Liver Disease in HIV

### HIV-HBV Co-infection
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Prevalence** | **5-10%** of HIV+ globally |
| **Natural History** | **Faster fibrosis progression**, higher cirrhosis/HCC risk, lower spontaneous clearance |
| **ART & HBV** | **TDF/FTC** or **TAF/FTC** backbone covers both HIV & HBV |
| **HBV Reactivation** | Risk if **stopping ART** (especially if on TDF/FTC/TAF) |
| **Lamivudine Monotherapy** | **Avoid** (high resistance) |

### HIV-HCV Co-infection
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Prevalence** | **20-30%** of HIV+ (higher in PWID) |
| **Fibrosis Progression** | **2-3x faster** than HCV mono-infection |
| **DAA Treatment** | **Same as HCV mono** (SOF/VEL, GLE/PIB, SOF/VEL/VOX); **Check DDI with ART** (e.g., SOF/VEL with boosted PIs — use GLE/PIB) |
| **SVR Rates** | **>95%** (similar to mono-infection) |

### ART Hepatotoxicity
| ART Class | Key Drugs | Toxicity Pattern | Monitoring |
|-----------|-----------|------------------|------------|
| **NRTIs** | Stavudine (d4T), Didanosine (ddI), Zidovudine | **Microvesicular steatosis, lactic acidosis** (mitochondrial toxicity) | LFTs, Lactate, Symptoms q3-6mo |
| **NNRTIs** | **Nevirapine** (hypersensitivity, MHC II), Efavirenz (mild) | **Immune-mediated hepatitis** (Nevirapine: CD4>250 women/>400 men = avoid) | LFTs q2wks ×2mo, then q3mo |
| **PIs** | Ritonavir-boosted (ATAZ/r, DRV/r, LPV/r) | **Cholestatic**, hyperlipidaemia, hyperglycaemia | LFTs, Lipids, Glucose q3-6mo |
| **Integrase Inhibitors** | Raltegravir, Dolutegravir, Bictegravir | **Generally safe**; rare hepatotoxicity | Routine LFTs |

### Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome (IRIS)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Timing** | **2-12 weeks** after ART initiation |
| **Mechanism** | Immune recovery → exaggerated response to HBV/HCV/other antigens |
| **Presentation** | **Flare of hepatitis** (ALT ↑↑), worsening LFTs, jaundice |
| **Management** | **Continue ART**; **Prednisolone 0.5-1mg/kg** if severe; **Do not stop ART** |

## 3. Liver Disease in the Elderly

### Physiological Changes
| Change | Clinical Impact |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Reduced Liver Volume** | ↓ 20-30% by age 80 |
| **Reduced Hepatic Blood Flow** | ↓ 30-40% (↓ first-pass metabolism) |
| **Reduced Phase I Metabolism** (CYP450) | **↑ Drug levels** (CYP3A4, CYP2D6, CYP2C9) |
| **Phase II Metabolism** (Conjugation) | **Relatively preserved** |
| **Reduced Albumin** | ↓ Binding → ↑ free drug fraction |
| **Reduced Regenerative Capacity** | Slower recovery from injury |

### Drug Dosing Principles in Elderly
| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Start Low, Go Slow** | Reduce initial dose by 25-50% |
| **Avoid CYP450 Substrates** if possible | Prefer renal excretion / Phase II drugs |
| **Monitor LFTs** | Baseline + q3-6mo if chronic therapy |
| **Beers Criteria** | Avoid high-risk drugs (e.g., amiodarone, methotrexate high-dose) |

### Common Drug Adjustments
| Drug | Adjustment |
|------|------------|
| **Warfarin** | ↓ Dose (↑ sensitivity) |
| **Benzodiazepines** | ↓ Dose (lorazepam/oxazepam preferred — no active metabolites) |
| **Opioids** | ↓ Dose (morphine → oxycodone/alfentanil) |
| **Statins** | Start low (atorvastatin 10mg, rosuvastatin 5mg) |

## 4. Perioperative Liver Assessment

### Pre-Operative Assessment

| Assessment | Tool / Test |
|------------|-------------|
| **Liver Function** | **Child-Pugh Score**, **MELD/MELD-Na** |
| **Reserve** | **Indocyanine Green (ICG) Clearance** (ICG-R15: <10% normal, 10-20% mild, >20% severe impairment) |
| **Portal Hypertension** | **HVPG** (if available); **Platelet count**, **Spleen size**, **Portal vein diameter** |
| **Cardiac** | Echo, Stress test/Dobutamine echo if >50/risk factors |
| **Pulmonary** | Spirometry, ABG, RHC if PoPH suspected |
| **Renal** | eGFR, Urine protein; **Combined liver-kidney** if eGFR<30 or dialysis >8wks |
| **Malignancy** | CT CAP, Colonoscopy (if >50), Mammography, PSA |
| **Infection** | HBV/HIV/HCV/CMV/EBV/VZV/HSV serology; TB (Quantiferon) |
| **Nutritional** | SGA/MUST, Sarcopenia (CT L3 psoas index) |
| **Psychosocial** | Psychiatry (alcohol, adherence), Social work (support, housing) |

### Surgical Risk by Liver Function

| Procedure | Child-Pugh A | Child-Pugh B | Child-Pugh C |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Minor** (hernia, cholecystectomy) | Low risk | Moderate risk (optimise) | **High risk → Avoid elective** |
| **Major** (hepatectomy, Whipple) | Moderate risk | **High risk** (optimise, consider portal embolization) | **Contraindicated** |

### Post-Operative Liver Dysfunction

| Syndrome | Definition | Management |
|----------|------------|------------|
| **Post-hepatectomy Liver Failure (PHLF)** | **ISGLS**: INR ↑ + Bilirubin >2mg/dL on POD5 | **Supportive** (fluids, glucose, coagulopathy correction, renal support), **Early nutrition**, **Avoid nephrotoxins** |
| **Anaesthetic Hepatitis** | Hypoxic/ischaemic injury | Supportive |
| **Drug-induced** | Review meds (antibiotics, anaesthetics) | Withdraw offending agent |

## 4. Liver Disease in Critical Illness

### Ischaemic Hepatitis (Shock Liver)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Definition** | **Acute hepatocellular injury** due to **hypoperfusion** (cardiac failure, sepsis, haemorrhage, surgery) |
| **Incidence** | **1-2%** of ICU admissions; **25-50%** in septic shock |
| **Biochemical Pattern** | **AST/ALT ↑↑↑** (often >5000 U/L), **Rapid rise & fall** (peak 24-48h, normalise 7-10d), **LDH ↑↑**, **Bilirubin mild ↑** |
| **Key Clue** | **AST > ALT** (often 2-3×), **Lactate ↑↑**, **Rapid resolution** with haemodynamic improvement |
| **Diagnosis** | **Exclude** other causes (viral, DILI, biliary, AIH) → **Clinical context + rapid rise/fall** |
| **Management** | **Treat underlying cause** (haemodynamics, sepsis, cardiac), **Supportive** (fluids, vasopressors, renal support), **Avoid hepatotoxins** |

### Drug-Induced Liver Injury in ICU

| Drug Class | Common Culprits | Prevention |
|------------|-----------------|------------|
| **Antibiotics** | Piperacillin-tazobactam, Meropenem, Vancomycin, Linezolid | Monitor LFTs q48-72h |
| **Antifungals** | Fluconazole, Voriconazole, Amphotericin B | Monitor LFTs, trough levels |
| **Antivirals** | Ganciclovir, Foscarnet | Monitor LFTs, renal |
| **Sedatives** | Propofol (PRIS), Midazolam | Limit duration, propofol <4mg/kg/h |
| **Vasopressors** | Noradrenaline (rare) | — |
| **Paracetamol** | Cumulative >4g/day | Monitor, limit to 3g/day in liver disease |

## 5. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Topic | Key Points |
|-------|------------|
| **AFLP** | **3rd trimester**, Swansea Criteria ≥6, **Hypoglycaemia, High uric acid, Coagulopathy**, **Emergency Delivery** |
| **HELLP** | **Haemolysis, Elevated LFTs, Low Platelets** (Mississippi Class I-III); Delivery if ≥34wks or maternal/fetal compromise |
| **ICP** | **Pruritus + Bile Acids >40 µmol/L** (3rd trimester), **UDCA 15-20mg/kg**, Delivery 36-37 wks (BA>40) |
| **HBV in Pregnancy** | TDF from 28 wks if DNA>200k; **HBIG + Vaccine at birth** (within 12h) |
| **HEV in Pregnancy** | **High mortality (15-25%)**, 3rd trimester; **Ribavirin CONTRAINDICATED** |
| **HIV-HBV** | TDF/FTC or TAF/FTC backbone covers both; Avoid lamivudine monotherapy |
| **HIV-HCV** | DAA same regimens; Check DDI with ART (GLE/PIB preferred with boosted PIs) |
| **IRIS** | Hepatitis flare 2-12 wks post-ART; Continue ART + Pred 0.5-1mg/kg |
| **Elderly Drug Metabolism** | ↓ Phase I (CYP450), Preserved Phase II; Start Low, Go Slow; Avoid CYP3A4 substrates |
| **Perioperative** | Child-Pugh/MELD, ICG-R15, Cardiac/Pulm/Renal, Cancer Screen, HVPG if available |
| **Post-hepatectomy Liver Failure** | ISGLS: Bilirubin >2mg/dL + INR ↑ on POD5 |
| **Ischaemic Hepatitis** | **AST>ALT**, rapid rise/fall, Lactate ↑↑, **Treat underlying shock** |

## 6. Viva Questions (MRCP PACES / FCPS)

| Question | Expected Answer |
|----------|-----------------|
| **AFLP — Swansea Criteria, Key Labs, Management?** | **≥6 of**: Nausea, Pain, Polydipsia, Encephalopathy, Bil>14, Glucose<4, Uric>340, WBC>11, Ascites/US, PT>14, AST>120, ALT>120, NH3>100, Cr>150; **Low glucose, High uric acid, Coagulopathy** → **Emergency Delivery**. |
| **AFLP vs HELLP — Key Differences?** | **AFLP**: Hypoglycaemia, High Uric Acid, Encephalopathy, DIC, Low Glucose; **HELLP**: Haemolysis (LDH↑, Schistocytes), Elevated LFTs, Low Platelets; **AFLP = Low Glucose + High Uric Acid + Encephalopathy**. |
| **ICP — Diagnostic Criteria, Management, Delivery Timing?** | **Pruritus + Bile Acids >40 µmol/L** (3rd trimester); **UDCA 15-20mg/kg/day**; **Delivery 36-37 wks (BA>40), 35-36 wks (BA>100)**. |
| **HBV in Pregnancy — Tenofovir Indication, Neonatal Prophylaxis?** | **TDF 300mg from 28 wks if HBV DNA >200,000 IU/mL**; **HBIG + HBV Vaccine at birth (within 12h)**. |
| **HEV in Pregnancy — Mortality, Treatment?** | **15-25% Maternal Mortality (3rd trimester)**; **Ribavirin CONTRAINDICATED**; Supportive only. |
| **HIV-HBV Co-infection — ART Regimen?** | **TDF/FTC or TAF/FTC backbone** (covers both); **Avoid Lamivudine Monotherapy** (resistance). |
| **HIV-HCV Co-infection — DDA Choice with Boosted PI?** | **GLE/PIB** (no significant DDI); **Avoid SOF/VEL** (interaction with boosted PIs). |
| **IRIS Hepatitis — Timing, Management?** | **2-12 weeks post-ART**; **Continue ART + Prednisolone 0.5-1mg/kg**; Do NOT stop ART. |
| **Elderly Drug Dosing — Principles?** | **Start Low, Go Slow**; ↓ Phase I (CYP450), Preserved Phase II; **Avoid CYP3A4 substrates**; Monitor LFTs. |
| **Perioperative Assessment — ICG-R15, Child-Pugh, MELD?** | **ICG-R15 <10% Normal**; Child-Pugh A/B/C for surgical risk; MELD for transplant priority; **HVPG if PH suspected**. |
| **Post-Hepatectomy Liver Failure — ISGLS Definition?** | **Bilirubin >2mg/dL + INR rise on POD5**; ISGLS Grade A/B/C. |
| **Ischaemic Hepatitis — Biochemical Pattern, Management?** | **AST > ALT (2-3×), Rapid Rise/Fall (Peak 24-48h), Lactate ↑↑**; **Treat Underlying Shock** (Haemodynamics, Sepsis). |

## 6. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **AFLP vs HELLP** | **AFLP**: Hypoglycaemia, Hyperuricaemia, Encephalopathy, DIC, Renal; **HELLP**: Haemolysis (LDH↑), Elevated LFTs, Low Platelets |
| **ICP vs Obstetric Cholestasis** | **Same condition**; ICP = modern term; **Bile Acids >40** diagnostic |
| **HBV vs HCV in Pregnancy** | **HBV**: TDF prophylaxis if DNA>200k, HBIG+Vaccine at birth; **HCV**: No DAA in pregnancy, postpartum cure, Breastfeeding OK |
| **HIV-IRIS vs Drug Hepatitis** | **IRIS**: 2-12 wks post-ART, CD4 rise, flare of existing HBV/HCV; **Duration**: Continue ART + steroids if severe |
| **ICG-R15 Interpretation** | <10% Normal, 10-20% Mild Impairment, >20% Severe Impairment; **Best functional reserve test** |
| **PHLF Definition** | **ISGLS**: Bilirubin >2mg/dL + INR ↑ on POD5; Grade A (transient), B (requires intervention), C (organ failure) |
| **Ischaemic vs DILI in ICU** | **Ischaemic**: AST>ALT, Rapid rise/fall, Lactate↑↑, Context of shock; **DILI**: Drug temporal relationship, RUCAM, Pattern-specific |
| **AFLP vs Acute Viral Hepatitis** | **AFLP**: 3rd trimester, Hypoglycaemia, High Uric Acid, Encephalopathy; **Viral**: Any trimester, No hypoglycaemia, Normal uric acid |

**Mnemonic: SPECIAL-LIVER**
- **S**pecial: **Pregnancy (AFLP, HELLP, ICP), HIV, Elderly, Perioperative, Critical Illness**
- **P**regnancy AFLP: **Swansea ≥6, Hypoglycaemia, Hyperuricaemia, Emergency Delivery**
- **E**clampsia/HELLP: **Haemolysis, Elevated LFTs, Low Platelets → Delivery if ≥34wks**
- **C**holestasis ICP: **Pruritus + Bile Acids >40 → UDCA 15-20mg/kg, Delivery 36-37wks**
- **I**CB in HBV: **TDF from 28wks if DNA>200k, HBIG+Vaccine at Birth**
- **A**rt Hepatotoxicity: **NRTI (Steatosis), NNRTI (Nevirapine Hypersensitivity), PI (Cholestatic), ICI (Immune Hepatitis)**
- **L**iver in Elderly: **Start Low Go Slow, ↓Phase I (CYP450), Preserved Phase II, Avoid CYP3A4**
- **I**schaemic Hepatitis: **AST>ALT, Rapid Rise/Fall, Lactate↑↑, Treat Shock**
- **A**FLP vs HELLP: **AFLP = Hypoglycaemia + Hyperuricaemia; HELLP = Haemolysis + Thrombocytopenia**
- **L**iver Transplant Assessment: **Child-Pugh, MELD, ICG-R15, Cardiac, Pulmonary, Renal, Cancer Screen**
- **I**nfectious: **HIV/HBV/HCV Co-infection, ART DDI, IRIS**
- **V**iral Hepatitis Pregnancy: **HBV (TDF), HCV (Postpartum DAA), HEV (High Mortality, No Ribavirin)**
- **E**mergency Delivery: **AFLP/HELLP/ICP (if severe) → Multidisciplinary**
- **R**ecovery: **Postpartum Resolution (ICP), AFLP/HELLP → Normalise Days-Weeks**
