# Resistant Hypertension

## 1. Definition
- BP **≥140/90** despite 3 drugs at optimal doses including a **diuretic** — or controlled on ≥4 drugs

## 2. Confirm & Rule Out Pseudoresistance
- Verify with **ABPM/HBPM** (white-coat is common)
- Assess **adherence** — pill count, pharmacy refill, observed therapy
- Exclude secondary causes (esp. **primary aldosteronism** in 10-20%)

## 3. Management
- **Add MRA** (spironolactone 25-50 mg) — PATHWAY-2 evidence
- Alternative: amiloride, bisoprolol, doxazosin
- Switch thiazide → **chlorthalidone/indapamide**
- Screen for OSA, target Na+ <2 g/day

## 4. Device Therapy
- **Renal denervation (RDN)**: RADIANCE-HTN, SPYRAL — ~7 mmHg SBP drop
- Indicated in true resistant HTN, eGFR ≥40
- Baroreflex activation — investigational

> **Pearls**: Add thiazide-like diuretic + **spironolactone** before considering RDN. Adherence is the #1 modifiable cause.
