# Exercise Restriction in Arrhythmogenic Cardiomyopathies

## 1. Definition
Patients with **ARVC** and other arrhythmogenic cardiomyopathies (ACM) are advised to **avoid competitive/endurance exercise** due to risk of disease progression and ventricular arrhythmia. Exercise appears to accelerate disease expression in genetically predisposed individuals.

## 2. Key Features
- **ARVC**: exercise accelerates RV dysfunction, ventricular arrhythmias, and desmosomal mutation phenotype expression
- **Recommendation**: **avoid competitive sports and high-intensity endurance exercise** (class I)
- **Permitted**: low-to-moderate intensity recreational activity
- **LV involvement (ALVC)**: similar restrictions
- **Risk of SCD** during exercise, especially in genotype-positive/phenotype-negative or early phenotype
- **Detraining** after diagnosis may reduce arrhythmic burden
- **Shared decision-making** in genotype-positive asymptomatic

## 3. Management
- **Activity restriction**: avoid competitive/endurance sports (≥3 METs sustained); allow recreational low-intensity
- **ICD** for secondary prevention (after cardiac arrest/sustained VT)
- **Primary prevention ICD** if appropriate risk profile (family history of SCD, syncope, NSVT, extensive RV dysfunction, LV involvement)
- **Catheter ablation** for recurrent VT (substrate modification)
- **β-blockers** may reduce arrhythmia burden
- **Family screening**: cascade genetic testing + cardiac MRI
- **Pregnancy** considerations

> **Pearls**
> - **Exercise is a "second hit"** in ARVC — accelerates disease
> - **Avoid competitive sports** in ARVC and ACM (class I)
> - **ICD** for secondary prevention is cornerstone
> - **Family screening** essential (autosomal dominant)
