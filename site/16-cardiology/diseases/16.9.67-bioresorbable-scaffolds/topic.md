# Bioresorbable Scaffolds (BRS)

<callout icon="💔" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Bioresorbable Scaffolds — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Concept
**Temporary stents** that provide mechanical support and elute anti-proliferative drug, then **fully resorb over 2–5 years**. Goal: restore pulsatility, late lumen gain, no permanent metal.

### Materials
- **Poly-lactic acid (PLLA)** — Absorb BVS (Abbott, withdrawn 2017)
- **Magnesium alloy** — Magmaris (BIOTRONIK)
- **Tyrosine polycarbonate** — DESolve
- **Iron-based** — IKATRUS

---

## 2. Theoretical Advantages vs DES
- ↓ Late stent thrombosis (no permanent metal)
- Restoration of **vasomotion** (endothelial function)
- ↓ Neoatherosclerosis
- Non-inferior to DES in early trials (ABSORB II, III, IV)

---

## 3. Why Absorb BVS Was Withdrawn
- **ABSORB III, IV, AIDA, TROFI II** showed **↑ device thrombosis (3×), ↑ MI, ↑ target-vessel MI** at 2–3 years vs metallic DES
- **Thick struts (150 µm)**, malapposition, late discontinuity
- Withdrawn from market in 2017
- **Lessons:** thicker struts + slower resorption = risk

### Newer Generation
- **Magmaris** (Sirolimus-eluting, 150 µm) — BIOSOLVE trials, lower thrombosis, not yet recommended widely
- **Fantom** (tyrosine-based, 125 µm) — favourable early data
- **MeRes100** — sirolimus-eluting, thinner

---

## 4. Current Status (2024)
- **Not recommended** for routine clinical use
- Investigational / niche use in selected centres
- **DES remain gold standard**
- Concept still attractive for young patients

---

## 5. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **BRS = temporary stents** that fully resorb (2–5 y)
- **Absorb BVS withdrawn** in 2017 due to ↑ thrombosis and MI
- **Thick struts + late discontinuity** implicated
- Newer devices (Magmaris, Fantom) under investigation
- **DES remain the standard of care** for PCI
