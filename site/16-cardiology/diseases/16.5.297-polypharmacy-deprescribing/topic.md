# Polypharmacy & Deprescribing

## 1. Polypharmacy
- **≥5 medications** common in elderly CV patients
- ↑ ADE risk, falls, cognitive impairment, non-adherence, cost
- **PIMs** (potentially inappropriate medications) common

## 2. Beers Criteria & STOPP/START
- Tools to identify PIMs in elderly
- Common CV PIMs: digoxin (toxicity), benzodiazepines, anticholinergics

## 3. Deprescribing Process
1. Comprehensive med review
2. Identify medications that are:
   - No longer indicated
   - High-risk/limited benefit
   - Duplicative
   - Causing side effects
3. Discuss with patient/family (goals of care)
4. Taper gradually (avoid withdrawal)
5. Monitor for withdrawal or rebound

## 4. CV-Specific Examples
- **Statins** in limited life expectancy
- **Antiplatelets** after bleeding event
- **Anticoagulation** in falls risk (often still indicated)
- **Beta-blockers** in severe bradycardia
- **Diuretics** in hyponatremia/hypotension
- **Polypharmacy** in HF

## 5. Tools
- **deprescribing.org** — evidence-based protocols
- **MedStopper** — app
- STOPP/START criteria

> **Pearls** — **Review medications** at every visit in elderly CV patients. Falls are **NOT** an absolute contraindication to anticoagulation in AF (net benefit usually). **Deprescribe** with shared decision-making, not unilateral.
