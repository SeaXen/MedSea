# Thyroid and the Heart

## 1. Hyperthyroidism
- **Cardiac effects**: ↑HR, ↑contractility, ↑CO → palpitations, AF (10-25%), HF, angina
- **AF** is the most common arrhythmia
- **Pulsus bisferiens**, hyperdynamic precordium, flow murmurs
- ECG: ↑QRS, AF, ischemic ST/T
- **Management**: β-blocker (propranolol) for symptoms, then **definitive Rx** (methimazole, PTU, RAI, surgery)
- **TSH-receptor antibodies**: Graves'
- **Amiodarone** causes both hyper- and hypothyroidism

## 2. Hypothyroidism
- **Cardiac effects**: ↓HR, ↓contractility, ↓CO → HF, pericardial effusion, bradyarrhythmias
- **Bradycardia**, low voltage, prolonged QT
- Dyslipidemia, diastolic HTN
- **Myxedema coma**: critical care emergency

## 3. Key Management
- **Screen for thyroid disease** in new AF, refractory HF
- Overt hypothyroidism → **levothyroxine** (start low in CAD, titrate up)
- **TSH target** 0.5-3 mIU/L
- **Avoid** excess thyroid hormone — ↑ AF, bone loss

> **Pearls**: Always **check TSH in AF, HF, hyperlipidemia**. Subclinical hypo (TSH 4.5-10) with anti-TPO → treat if symptomatic, pregnant, or progressing.
