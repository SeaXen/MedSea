# Cardiac Hemochromatosis — Iron Overload & Chelation

## 1. Definition
Iron-overload cardiomyopathy results from **excess iron deposition in cardiomyocytes**, causing restrictive → dilated cardiomyopathy and conduction disease.

## 2. Causes
| Type | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Hereditary (HH)** | HFE C282Y homozygotes; most common genetic disorder in Northern Europeans |
| **Secondary (transfusional)** | Chronic transfusion-dependent thalassaemia, SCD, MDS |
| **Miscellaneous** | Bantu siderosis, aceruloplasminaemia, neonatal iron overload |

## 3. Clinical Features
- **Skin:** bronze/grey hyperpigmentation
- **Liver:** cirrhosis, HCC risk
- **Pancreas:** DM ("bronze diabetes")
- **Joints:** arthropathy (2nd/3rd MCP)
- **Pituitary:** hypogonadism
- **Heart:** restrictive CM → dilated CM; conduction disease

## 4. Cardiac Features
- Early: **restrictive** pattern with normal LVEF
- Late: **dilated** with systolic dysfunction
- **Conduction disease** (sick sinus, AV block)
- AF in ~30%

## 5. Investigations
- **Iron studies:** ferritin ↑, transferrin saturation **>45%** (HH >70%)
- **HFE genotyping** (C282Y, H63D)
- **LFTs, glucose, testosterone, TSH**
- **MRI T2\*** — **gold standard** for myocardial iron quantification
  - T2\* <20 ms = cardiac iron overload
  - T2\* <10 ms = severe
- **Echo:** speckled myocardium, bi-atrial enlargement, LV diastolic/systolic dysfunction

## 6. Diagnostic Pathway
1. Suspect in DCM/restrictive CM + skin/endocrine clues
2. Iron studies + HFE genotype
3. **MRI T2\*** for cardiac iron
4. Liver biopsy or MRI for hepatic iron

## 7. Treatment
### Phlebotomy
- First-line for **hereditary hemochromatosis without anaemia**
- 500 mL weekly until ferritin <50 µg/L, then maintenance
- Improves hepatic, not cardiac, iron
- **Cannot use in anaemia** (transfusional iron overload)

### Iron Chelation
For **transfusional iron overload** (β-thalassaemia, SCD) OR anaemic HH
| Drug | Route | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Desferrioxamine (DFO)** | SC/IV infusion 8–12 h, 5 days/wk | First-line for cardiac; monitor audiometry, vision |
| **Deferasirox** | Oral daily | Hepatotoxic, renal |
| **Deferiprone** | Oral TDS | Risk of agranulocytosis; monitor weekly blood count |

### Cardiac Recovery
- **LVEF improves** with chelation over 3–12 months
- Combine DFO + deferiprone for severe cardiac iron (synergistic)
- T2\* improves with effective chelation

### Adjunctive
- HF GDMT when LVEF <50%
- Avoid alcohol, vitamin C supplements (worsen iron toxicity)
- Limit iron-rich foods (red meat) — not usually restrictive

## 8. Monitoring
- Ferritin every 3 months
- LFTs, MRI T2\* annually
- Echo and ECG every 6–12 months

> **Pearls**
> - **Bronze skin + DM + arthropathy + CM** = think hemochromatosis.
> - **MRI T2\*** <20 ms = cardiac iron; <10 ms = severe.
> - **Phlebotomy** for HH; **iron chelation** for transfusional or anaemic HH.
> - **Deferiprone** is most effective for cardiac iron removal (synergistic with DFO).
> - **LVEF can recover** with effective chelation — keep patients on GDMT.
