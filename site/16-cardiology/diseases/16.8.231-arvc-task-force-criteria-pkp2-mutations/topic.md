# ARVC (Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy)

## 1. Definition
Inherited cardiomyopathy with **fibro-fatty replacement** of RV myocardium, ventricular arrhythmias, and SCD risk.

## 2. Genetics
- **Desmosomal mutations** (most common): PKP2, DSP, DSC2, DSG2, JUP
- **Autosomal dominant** (Naxos, Carvajal = recessive)
- **PKP2** most common

## 3. Diagnosis — Modified Task Force Criteria (2010)
**Major:** severe RV dilation/dysfunction, fibrofatty replacement on biopsy, **T wave inversion V1–V3** (>14y, no RBBB), **epsilon wave**, VT with LBBB + superior axis, family history
**Minor:** mild RV dysfunction, late potentials on SAECG, frequent PVCs (>500/24h), VT with LBBB + inferior axis

Definite = 2 major, or 1 major + 2 minor, or 4 minor
Borderline = 1 major + 1 minor, or 3 minor
Possible = 1 major, or 2 minor

## 4. Management
- **Exercise restriction** (no competitive sports — increases risk)
- **Beta-blocker** (antiarrhythmic)
- **ICD** for: cardiac arrest, sustained VT, high-risk features
- **Catheter ablation** for VT (high recurrence)
- **Family screening** (first-degree relatives)
- Avoid intense endurance exercise

## 5. Outcomes
- SCD in 5–20% of untreated
- LV involvement (ALVC) increasingly recognized
- Pregnancy: continue beta-blocker, monitor

> **Pearls** — **Task Force Criteria** for diagnosis. **PKP2** most common mutation. **Avoid competitive sports** (proven ↑ risk). **ICD** for SCD prevention. Screen family.
