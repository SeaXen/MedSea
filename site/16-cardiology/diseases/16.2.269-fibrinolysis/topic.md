# Fibrinolysis

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 02_Acute_Coronary_Syndromes | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**Fibrinolysis** refers to **fibrinolysis (thrombolysis)** — pharmacological dissolution of occlusive coronary thrombus using fibrinolytic agents. **Class**: fibrin-specific (tenecteplase, alteplase, reteplase) vs non-specific (streptokinase). Used in STEMI when **primary PCI not available within 120 min**. **Door-to-needle <30 min**. Newer agents (tenecteplase) preferred over streptokinase (allergy, less effective).


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

Fibrinolytics convert **plasminogen → plasmin** → degrades fibrin → dissolves thrombus. **Tenecteplase** (TNK-tPA): modified tPA, longer half-life, single bolus, weight-adjusted. **Alteplase** (rtPA): recombinant tPA, 90-min infusion. **Reteplase** (rPA): double-bolus. **Streptokinase**: bacterial (Streptococcus), non-fibrin specific, antibody-mediated (repeat use problematic).


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

Used in STEMI: chest pain onset <12h, persistent STE, no contraindications. **STEMI equivalent** (de Winter, Wellens, posterior, Sgarbossa-positive LBBB). **Outcome**: TIMI 3 flow in 60% (vs 90% for PCI). **Mortality reduction**: 18-25% (similar to PCI if given early). **Major bleeding**: 1-2% intracranial, 5-10% major non-ICH. **Allergic reactions** (streptokinase).


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

ECG confirmation of STEMI. **Exclusion of contraindications** (see below). **Door-to-needle target <30 min** (NICE NG185, ESC 2023). **Rescue PCI** if fibrinolysis fails (ST resolution <50% at 60-90 min, persistent pain, haemodynamic instability). **Routine angio post-lytic** (STRATEGIC, CARESS, TRANSFER-AMI, FAST-MI) within 3-24h.


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**TIMI flow** post-lysis: 0-1 (failed, rescue PCI), 2 (partial, repeat lysis or PCI), 3 (success). **ST resolution** >50% at 60-90 min = successful reperfusion. **GRACE/TIMI score** for overall risk. **In-hospital mortality**: ~6% (vs 4-5% PCI), but offset by access in remote settings.


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**Tenecteplase** (Metalyse): single IV bolus over 5-10s, weight-adjusted: <60kg 30mg, 60-69kg 35mg, 70-79kg 40mg, 80-89kg 45mg, ≥90kg 50mg. **Adjunct**: aspirin 300mg chewable, clopidogrel 300mg (75y) or 75mg (>75y, no load), UFH bolus 60U/kg (max 4000) + 12U/kg/h (max 1000) for 48h OR enoxaparin 30mg IV then 1mg/kg SC q12h. **Rescue PCI** if ST <50% resolution at 60-90 min. **Routine angio** 3-24h post-lysis. **Avoid** GP IIb/IIIa with full-dose lytic (increased bleeding).


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

**Intracranial haemorrhage** (0.5-1%, mortality 50-70%, risk factors: age>75, low BMI, female, prior stroke, hypertension). **Major bleeding** (5-10%, GI, retroperitoneal, puncture sites). **Reperfusion arrhythmias** (idioventricular rhythm, transient AF/VT — common, usually benign, do NOT treat with antiarrhythmics). **Allergic reaction** (streptokinase, hypotension, anaphylaxis, repeat use within 5y-2y). **Failure** (40% no TIMI 3 flow) — rescue PCI. **Stent thrombosis** if PCI needed. **Recurrent ischaemia**.


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

Elderly (>75): more bleeding, more ICH (ASSENT-3, GUSTO-V — consider half-dose tenecteplase). Women: more ICH. Low body weight (<60kg): more ICH. Prior stroke: avoid streptokinase, prefer TNK with caution. **Cardiac arrest**: pre-hospital lytic shown benefit (ARREST trial). **CKD**: dose-adjusted UFH, avoid enoxaparin if CrCl<20.


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

**ESC 2023 ACS**: fibrinolysis only if PCI >120 min, give within 30 min of arrival. **ACC/AHA 2023 STEMI**: similar. **NICE NG185**: fibrinolysis if PCI not possible within 120 min. **Tenecteplase** preferred over streptokinase (ASSENT-2). **ASSENT-3, ASSENT-4**: TNK + UFH or enoxaparin. **CLARITY-TIMI 28, COMMIT/CCS-2**: clopidogrel + lytic. **STRATEGIC, CARESS, TRANSFER-AMI, FAST-MI**: routine early PCI post-lysis.


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

Don't delay — door-to-needle <30 min. Don't give streptokinase twice. Don't give lytic for STEMI >12h old (no benefit, harm). Don't give lytic for NSTEMI. **Rescue PCI if failed**: ST <50% resolution, persistent pain. **Half-dose TNK** in elderly (ASSENT-3). **ICH**: stop lytic, CT, cryoprecipitate, platelets, FFP, aminocaproic acid/tranexamic acid. **Allergy**: streptokinase only.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

**TNK**: Tenecteplase. **rtPA**: Alteplase. **rPA**: Reteplase. **SK**: Streptokinase. **PCI preferred over lytic** (PCI ~90% TIMI 3, lytic ~60%). **Door-to-needle <30 min**. **STRATEGIC, CARESS, TRANSFER-AMI**: routine PCI post-lysis. **ICH 0.5-1%**. **Reperfusion arrhythmias** = benign. **Rescue PCI** if ST <50% resolution. **Half-dose TNK >75y** (ASSENT-3).


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Differential for failed thrombolysis: dissection, re-occlusion, multivessel disease, embolic cause. Differential for ICH: hypertensive ICH, amyloid angiopathy, AVM, trauma.


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

ECG (immediate, serial), troponin, FBC (baseline for bleeding), U&E, coagulation, fibrinogen (low after lytic), head CT if neurological change, echo for RWMA, repeat ECG at 60-90 min for ST resolution.

## 14. EXAM DRILLS

### 14.1 MCQs

**Q1.** What is the most common aetiology of Fibrinolysis?
A. Genetic predisposition
B. Acquired disease
C. Mixed aetiology
D. Idiopathic
E. Familial

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Most cases are acquired with genetic/environmental interaction. Recognise risk factors, presentation, and management.

**Q2.** First-line investigation for Fibrinolysis:
A. ECG
B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography
E. Cardiac biomarkers

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Echo is first-line for structural/functional assessment; additional tests as indicated.

**Q3.** Most appropriate first-line therapy for Fibrinolysis:
A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy
D. Surgical intervention
E. Multidisciplinary care

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Guideline-directed medical therapy is cornerstone; other modalities as indicated.

**Q4.** Which is a contraindication to therapy in Fibrinolysis?
A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

**Q5.** The most common complication of Fibrinolysis is:
A. Heart failure
B. Arrhythmia
C. Thromboembolism
D. Death
E. Quality of life reduction

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.
