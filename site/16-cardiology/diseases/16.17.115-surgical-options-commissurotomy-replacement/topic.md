# Surgical Options for Valvular Disease

## 1. Repair vs Replacement
- **Mitral**: repair preferred when feasible (degenerative MR, ~95% repairable)
- **Tricuspid**: repair (annuloplasty ring) preferred
- **Aortic**: replacement standard (repair for select BAV, root aneurysm)
- **Pulmonary**: repair in congenital

## 2. Commissurotomy
- **Closed** (without bypass, historical) — for MS in developing world
- **Open** (with bypass, direct vision) — for MS with calcified valves
- **Balloon valvuloplasty** (BMV) — alternative to surgical commissurotomy in MS

## 3. Replacement Options
**Mechanical** (bileaflet, single tilting disc, caged ball)
- Lifelong warfarin (INR 2.5–3.5)
- <60y: durable, no degeneration
- Bleeding/thrombosis risk

**Bioprosthetic** (porcine, bovine pericardial)
- TAVI for older/high-risk
- 10–15 y durability, no anticoagulation (after 3 mo)
- Structural valve deterioration ~10y

## 4. Choice
- **<50y**: mechanical (if no contraindication to warfarin)
- **50–65y**: shared decision
- **>65y**: bioprosthetic
- **Pregnancy**: bioprosthetic (avoid warfarin teratogenicity)
- **ESRD**: bioprosthetic (avoid warfarin + dialysis)

> **Pearls** — **Mechanical → lifelong warfarin**. Bioprosthetic in older, pregnancy, ESRD, contraindication to warfarin. TAVI is the new standard for older patients with AS.
