# Cardiac MRI (Viability, Fibrosis, Infiltrative Diseases)

<callout icon="🫀" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Cardiac MRI — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Introduction
CMR is the **gold standard** for cardiac **volumes, EF, and tissue characterisation**. No ionising radiation; uses **gadolinium contrast** (caution in CKD eGFR <30). Long scan time, breath-holds, ECG-gated.

## 2. Sequences

| Sequence | Use |
|---|---|
| **Cine (SSFP)** | Function, EF, anatomy, wall motion |
| **T1-weighted** | Anatomy, masses, pericardium |
| **T2-weighted (STIR)** | **Oedema** (myocarditis, MI, Takotsubo) |
| **T2\* mapping** | Iron overload (haemochromatosis) |
| **T1 mapping (native)** | Diffuse fibrosis, amyloid, Anderson-Fabry |
| **T1 mapping post-contrast** | ECV (extracellular volume) |
| **LGE (late gadolinium enhancement)** | **Scar / fibrosis / viability** |
| **Stress perfusion (adenosine)** | Ischaemia |
| **Phase contrast** | Flow, regurgitant volume, Qp/Qs |
| **3D / MRA** | Coronaries, aorta, pulmonary veins |

## 3. Viability Assessment

### Late Gadolinium Enhancement (LGE)
- Gadolinium trapped in **expanded extracellular space** (fibrosis/scar) → high signal
- **Threshold** = 5 SD above remote myocardium
- **Patterns of enhancement**:

| Pattern | Condition |
|---|---|
| **Subendocardial** (→ transmural) | **Ischaemic** (territory of coronary) |
| **Mid-wall / epicardial** | **Non-ischaemic** (myocarditis, NICM) |
| **RV insertion points** | PAH, congenital |
| **Patchy mid-wall** | DCM, myocarditis |
| **Diffuse subendocardial** | Amyloid, Anderson-Fabry |
| **Crescent (RV free wall)** | ARVC |

### Transmural Extent of LGE (Viability)
- **0%** → highly viable; recovery expected post-revascularisation
- **1–25%** → likely viable
- **26–50%** → possibly viable
- **51–75%** → less likely viable
- **>75%** → scar; **revascularisation unlikely to recover**

## 4. Cardiomyopathies
- **HCM** — asymmetric septal hypertrophy; LGE in hypertrophied segments (fibrosis); SCD risk
- **DCM** — LV dilation, ↓EF; **mid-wall LGE in septum** in 30%
- **ARVC** — RV dilation/dysfunction; LGE in RV free wall; fat infiltration
- **Takotsubo** — apical ballooning; **no LGE** (vs MI) + oedema
- **Myocarditis** — subepicardial/mid-wall LGE, oedema on T2

## 5. Infiltrative Diseases
- **Cardiac amyloidosis** — ↑ wall thickness, **diffuse subendocardial LGE**, "zebra" pattern, **↑ T1, ↑ ECV (>40%)**, biatrial enlargement
- **Anderson-Fabry** — basal inferolateral LGE (binary sign on T1), ↓ native T1
- **Sarcoidosis** — basal septal/RV LGE, oedema, FDG-PET for inflammation
- **Haemochromatosis** — ↓ T2\* (<20 ms = iron overload), no LGE early

## 6. Pericardial Disease
- **Pericardial thickness ≥4 mm** (normal <3)
- **CMR pericardial LGE** = active inflammation
- **Effusion** — T2 bright
- **Constrictive pericarditis** — thickened pericardium + septal bounce on cine

## 7. Stress Perfusion (Adenosine)
- Adenosine 140 µg/kg/min → coronary vasodilation
- **Perfusion deficit** = inducible ischaemia
- Sensitivity/specificity >85% for CAD
- Combined with LGE → better than SPECT

## 8. Congenital / Vascular
- **Aortic coarctation**, **anomalies**
- **ASD / VSD** (Qp/Qs via flow)
- **RV function** in TOF, TGA
- **Aortic dissection** (especially in renal failure where CT contraindicated)

## 9. Masses
- **Thrombus** (black on SSFP, no enhancement)
- **Myxoma** (heterogeneous, mobile, pedunculated)
- **Fibroma, lipoma, mets** — tissue-specific features

## 10. Limitations
- **Long scan time** (30–60 min)
- **Claustrophobia**
- **Gadolinium** in CKD eGFR <30 (NSF risk); **newer GBCA safer**
- **Devices** (most modern pacemakers/ICDs **CMR conditional**)
- **Arrhythmia** artefacts
- **Cost, availability**

> **Pearls**
> - **CMR = gold standard for EF, volumes, tissue characterisation**
> - **LGE patterns** distinguish ischaemic (subendocardial, coronary territory) vs non-ischaemic (mid-wall, epicardial)
> - **>50% transmural LGE** = non-viable; **<25%** = viable
> - **Amyloid** = diffuse subendocardial LGE, ↑ native T1, ECV >40%
> - **T2 oedema** = acute injury (myocarditis, MI, Takotsubo)
> - **T2\* <20 ms** = iron overload
