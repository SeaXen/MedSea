# Echocardiography Basics

Related: [[../Cardiology MOC|Cardiology MOC]] · [[../Davidson Chapter 16 - Cardiology Hierarchy|Cardiology Hierarchy]] · [[../Clinical Assessment, Hemodynamics, and Cardiac Investigations|Clinical Assessment, Hemodynamics, and Cardiac Investigations]] · [[Heart Failure]] · [[Valvular Heart Disease]] · [[Heart Failure and Acute Cardiac Decompensation]] · [[Arrhythmias]] · [[Clinical examination of the cardiovascular system]] · [[Bedside hemodynamic profiling in acute cardiac illness]]

> [!important]
> Echocardiography = **first-line cardiac imaging** — non-invasive, real-time, portable, no radiation. FCPS/MRCP exams test: **standard views**, **chamber quantification**, **valve assessment**, **Doppler hemodynamics**, **wall motion analysis**, and **integration with clinical syndrome**. **TTE = frontline; TEE = when TTE inadequate or specific indications.**

## Learning Objectives
- List standard TTE views and what each visualizes
- Quantify LV size, function (EF), wall motion, and mass
- Assess RV size/function, RA/RV dimensions
- Evaluate valves (structure, stenosis, regurgitation) with 2D + Doppler
- Apply Doppler for hemodynamic assessment (gradients, flow, pressures)
- Integrate echo findings with clinical syndrome (HF, valve disease, ACS, shock)
- Recognize TEE indications and advantages over TTE

## Standard TTE Views (ASE/EAE Recommendations)

| View | Window | Key Structures Visualized |
|------|--------|---------------------------|
| **Parasternal Long Axis (PLAX)** | 3-4th ICS, left sternal border | LV, LA, RVOT, AV, MV, aortic root, ascending aorta |
| **Parasternal Short Axis (PSAX)** | 3-4th ICS, left sternal border | LV cross-section (base → apex), RV, PV, TV, LV wall motion |
| **Apical 4-Chamber (A4C)** | Apex, midclavicular line | All 4 chambers, MV, TV, septal/ lateral LV, RV size/function |
| **Apical 2-Chamber (A2C)** | Apex, rotated | LV (inferior, anterior), LA, MV |
| **Apical 3-Chamber (A3C / Apical 5-Chamber)** | Apex, between 4C & 2C | LV, LA, LVOT, AV |
| **Apical Long Axis (A-LAX)** | Apex, lateral | LV, LVOT, AV, ascending aorta |
| **Suprasternal Notch** | Suprasternal notch | Aortic arch, descending aorta, branch vessels |
| **Subcostal** | Subxiphoid | All 4 chambers (if poor acoustic windows), IVC, hepatic veins |

> [!tip]
> **Subcostal** = best for IVC diameter/collapsibility (RA pressure estimate); **Suprasternal** = aortic arch pathology, coarctation.

## Chamber Quantification (Key Measurements)

### Left Ventricle (LV)
| Parameter | Normal Range | Method |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| **LVEDD** (end-diastolic diameter) | 39-56 mm (M), 35-51 mm (F) | M-mode / 2D (PSAX) |
| **LVESD** (end-systolic diameter) | 21-38 mm (M), 19-33 mm (F) | M-mode / 2D |
| **LVEDV** (end-diastolic volume) | 65-120 mL (M), 55-100 mL (F) | Biplane Simpson (A4C + A2C) |
| **LVESV** (end-systolic volume) | 25-55 mL (M), 20-45 mL (F) | Biplane Simpson |
| **LVEF** | **≥55%** (M), **≥55%** (F) | Biplane Simpson (A4C + A2C) |
| **LV Mass** | 49-115 g/m² (M), 40-95 g/m² (F) | 2D (PLAX) or 3D |
| **RWT** (relative wall thickness) | ≤0.42 | (2 × PW thickness) / LVEDD |

> [!tip]
> **Biplane Simpson (A4C + A2C)** = gold standard for EF. Avoid foreshortening.

### Right Ventricle (RV)
| Parameter | Normal | Method |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **RV basal diameter** (4C) | <42 mm | 4C view |
| **RV mid-cavity diameter** | <35 mm | 4C view |
| **RV longitudinal diameter** | <86 mm | 4C view |
| **TAPSE** | **≥17 mm** | M-mode (TV annulus) |
| **Tricuspid S'** | **≥10 cm/s** | Tissue Doppler (TV annulus) |
| **FAC** (fractional area change) | >35% | 4C view |
| **RV free wall strain** | >-20% | Speckle tracking |

### Left Atrium (LA)
| Parameter | Normal | Method |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **LA volume index** | <34 mL/m² | Biplane (A4C + A2C) at end-systole |
| **LA diameter** (PLAX) | <40 mm | M-mode / 2D |

### Right Atrium (RA)
| Parameter | Normal | Method |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **RA area** (4C) | <18 cm² | 4C view |
| **RA volume index** | <33 mL/m² | 4C view |

## Valve Assessment

### Aortic Valve (AV)
| Assessment | Method |
|------------|--------|
| **Morphology** | Trileaflet vs bicuspid (PLAX, PSAX) |
| **Stenosis** | Peak/mean gradient (CW Doppler), Vmax, AVA (continuity equation) |
| **Regurgitation** | Color Doppler (jet width), CW Doppler (density), PHT, vena contracta |

| AS Severity | Vmax (m/s) | Mean Grad (mmHg) | AVA (cm²) | AVA Index |
|-------------|------------|------------------|-----------|-----------|
| Mild | 2.5-2.9 | <20 | >1.5 | >0.85 |
| Moderate | 3.0-3.9 | 20-39 | 1.0-1.5 | 0.6-0.85 |
| Severe | **≥4.0** | **≥40** | **<1.0** | **<0.6** |

| AR Severity | Vena Contracta | Jet Width/LVOT | PHT (ms) | Regurg Volume |
|-------------|----------------|----------------|----------|---------------|
| Mild | <3 mm | <25% | >500 | <30 mL |
| Moderate | 3-6 mm | 25-65% | 200-500 | 30-59 mL |
| Severe | **>6 mm** | **>65%** | **<200** | **≥60 mL** |

### Mitral Valve (MV)
| Assessment | Method |
|------------|--------|
| **Stenosis** | MVA (planimetry/continuity/PHT), mean gradient, PHT |
| **Regurgitation** | Color (jet area), PISA (EROA, RegVol), vena contracta |

| MS Severity | MVA (cm²) | Mean Grad | PHT (ms) |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Mild | >1.5 | <5 | >150 |
| Moderate | 1.0-1.5 | 5-10 | 100-150 |
| Severe | **<1.0** | **>10** | **<100** |

| MR Severity | EROA (cm²) | RegVol (mL) | Vena Contracta |
|-------------|------------|-------------|----------------|
| Mild | <0.20 | <30 | <3 mm |
| Moderate | 0.20-0.39 | 30-59 | 3-6 mm |
| Severe | **≥0.40** | **≥60** | **≥7 mm** |

### Tricuspid Valve (TV)
| Assessment | Method |
|------------|--------|
| **Regurgitation** | Color jet, CW Doppler (TR velocity), hepatic vein flow, IVC dilation |

| TR Severity | Vena Contracta | Hepatic Vein Flow | IVC |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------|-----|
| Mild | <3 mm | Systolic dominant | Normal |
| Moderate | 3-6 mm | Systolic blunting | Dilated |
| Severe | **≥7 mm** | **Systolic reversal** | Dilated, <50% collapse |

### Pulmonary Valve (PV)
| Assessment | Method |
|------------|--------|
| **Stenosis** | Peak gradient (CW Doppler), PHT |
| **Regurgitation** | Color, CW Doppler (PHT, PR velocity) |

## Doppler Hemodynamics

### Pressure Gradient Estimation (Simplified Bernoulli)
> **ΔP = 4 × v²** (v = peak velocity in m/s)

| Application | Formula |
|-------------|---------|
| **AV gradient** | ΔP = 4 × Vmax² (AV) |
| **MR gradient** | ΔP = 4 × Vmax² (MR) → LA-LV gradient |
| **TR gradient** | ΔP = 4 × Vmax² (TR) → RVSP = 4 × Vmax² + RAP |
| **RVSP** | **RVSP = 4 × Vmax² (TR) + RAP** (RAP from IVC) |

### Diastolic Function Assessment (Grade I-III)
| Parameter | Normal | Grade I (Impaired Relaxation) | Grade II (Pseudonormal) | Grade III (Restrictive) |
|-----------|--------|-------------------------------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| **E/A** | 1-2 | **<0.8** | 0.8-2 (pseudonormal) | **>2** |
| **E/e'** | <8 | >8 | 9-14 | **>14** |
| **Decel Time** | 150-240 ms | **>240 ms** | 160-240 ms | **<160 ms** |
| **LAVI** | <34 | ↑ | ↑↑ | ↑↑↑ |
| **TR velocity** | <2.8 | Normal | ↑ | ↑↑ |

> [!tip]
> **E/e' >14** = elevated LV filling pressure (Grade III).
> **Pseudonormal**: Valsalva → E/A <1 reveals Grade I.

## Wall Motion Analysis
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | Normal |
| **2** | Hypokinesis |
| **3** | Akinesis |
| **4** | Dyskinesis |
| **5** | Aneurysm |

> [!tip]
> **WMSI** (Wall Motion Score Index) = Σ scores / segments; WMSI >1.5 = significant dysfunction.

## TEE Indications (When TTE Inadequate)
| Indication | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| **Poor TTE windows** | Obesity, COPD, post-op, mechanical ventilation |
| **IE** (native/prosthetic) | Vegetations, abscess, dehiscence, perforation |
| **Complex valve disease** | PISA quantification, commissural anatomy |
| **Aortic pathology** | Dissection, IMH, PAU (suprasternal/descending) |
| **Intra-cardiac mass/thrombus** | LA appendage thrombus, tumors |
| **ASD/PFO detection** | Bubble contrast, anatomy |
| **Intra-operative** | Valve repair assessment |

## TTE vs TEE
| Feature | TTE | TEE |
|---------|-----|-----|
| **Invasiveness** | Non-invasive | Semi-invasive (sedation) |
| **Windows** | Limited by body habitus | Excellent (esophagus) |
| **Valve detail** | Good | Excellent (esp. MV, AV, LA appendage) |
| **LA appendage** | Poor | Excellent |
| **Aortic arch/descending** | Poor/Suprasternal | Excellent |
| **Portability** | Bedside | Requires dedicated suite |
| **Contraindications** | None | Esophageal pathology, recent UGI bleed |

## Common Measurements Quick Reference

| Parameter | Formula / Method |
|-----------|------------------|
| **LVEF (Simpson)** | (LVEDV - LVESV) / LVEDV × 100% |
| **LV Mass** | 0.8 × 1.04 × [(LVEDD + PWT + SWT)³ - LVEDD³] + 0.6 |
| **AVA (Continuity)** | (CSA_LVOT × VTI_LVOT) / VTI_AV |
| **MVA (PHT)** | 220 / PHT |
| **AVA (Hakki)** | CO / √Mean Gradient |
| **RVSP** | 4 × (TR Vmax)² + RAP |
| **CO (Doppler)** | CSA_LVOT × VTI_LVOT × HR |

## Red Flags / Exam Traps
- **Foreshortening** → underestimates volumes, overestimates EF
- **Assuming EF preserved = normal** (HFpEF = EF≥50% but diastolic dysfunction)
- **LVOT velocity not measured** → cannot calculate AVA by continuity
- **TR velocity not measured** → cannot estimate RVSP
- **Not assessing RV** (especially in HF, PH, RV infarct)
- **Assuming EF = systolic function** (strain + WMSI add info)
- **Not measuring IVC** (RA pressure estimate for RVSP)

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- **TTE first-line**; TEE for poor windows, IE, complex valves, aorta, LA appendage
- **Biplane Simpson (A4C+A2C)** = gold standard EF
- **AS severe**: Vmax ≥4.0, Mean Grad ≥40, AVA <1.0 cm²
- **MR severe**: EROA ≥0.40 cm², RegVol ≥60 mL, VC ≥7 mm
- **MR PISA**: EROA = (2πr² × Va) / Vmax (MR)
- **Diastolic function**: E/A, E/e' (e' septal/lateral), DT, LAVI
- **RVSP** = 4 × TR Vmax² + RAP (IVC)
- **TEE indications**: poor windows, IE, complex valves, aorta, LA appendage

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- Confusing EF with systolic function (strain adds info)
- Using single-plane EF (underestimates in dilated LV)
- Missing RV assessment
- Not measuring LVOT VTI for continuity equation
- Assuming normal EF = normal diastolic function
- Not using Valsalva for HOCM assessment

## Summary
**Echocardiography** is the **first-line cardiac imaging** modality. **Standard TTE views**: PLAX, PSAX, A4C, A2C, A3C, Suprasternal, Subcostal. **Chamber quantification**: **LVEF by biplane Simpson (A4C+A2C)** ≥55% normal; **RV function**: TAPSE ≥17mm, S' ≥10cm/s; **LA volume index** <34 mL/m². **Valve assessment**: **AS severe** = Vmax ≥4.0 m/s, Mean Grad ≥40 mmHg, AVA <1.0 cm²; **MR severe** = EROA ≥0.40 cm², RegVol ≥60 mL, VC ≥7mm (PISA method: EROA = 2πr²×Va/Vmax); **Diastolic function** grading by E/A, E/e', DT, LAVI; **RVSP** = 4 × Vmax²(TR) + RAP (from IVC). **TEE indications**: infective endocarditis, poor acoustic windows, aortic pathology, LA appendage thrombus, complex valve disease, intraoperative assessment. **Red flags**: foreshortening apices, missing RV assessment, missing LVOT VTI, not measuring IVC for RAP.

## Answer Key with Explanations
### MCQs
1. **B** — Biplane Simpson (A4C+A2C) is guideline-recommended gold standard.
2. **B** — Severe AS: Vmax ≥4.0 m/s, Mean Grad ≥40 mmHg, AVA <1.0 cm².
3. **B** — MVA = 220 / PHT (ms) (valid in sinus rhythm, no significant AR).
4. **C** — Severe MR: EROA ≥0.40 cm² (RegVol ≥60 mL, VC ≥7mm).
5. **B** — RVSP = 4 × Vmax²(TR) + RAP (simplified Bernoulli).
6. **C** — TAPSE ≥17 mm normal; <17 = RV dysfunction; <14 = severe.
7. **B** — PSAX at mid-valve level shows "fishmouth" of mitral commissures.
8. **C** — Grade III = restrictive pattern E/A >2.0; Grade I <0.8; Grade II = pseudonormal 0.8-2.0.
9. **C** — TEE for IE, poor windows, aorta, LA appendage, complex valves; NOT routine AS screening.
10. **B** — Biplane Simpson requires A4C + A2C (orthogonal planes).

### SBAs
1. **C** — Discordant single-plane EFs → use biplane Simpson (A4C+A2C) for accurate EF.
2. **B** — Severe symptomatic AS → AVR (Class I).
3. **B** — EROA = 2πr² × Va / Vmax = 2π×0.9²×30/500 = 0.43 cm².
4. **C** — E/e' 18 (>14) + LAVI 42 (>34) = Grade III restrictive.
5. **B** — RVSP = 4×3.5² + RAP. 3.5²=12.25×4=49. IVC 2.2cm <50% collapse → RAP≈10. 49+10=59≈54 (closest).
   Correction: IVC 2.2cm with <50% collapse = RAP ~10-15. 49+10=59 ≒ 54 (option B closest).
6. **B** — E/A 0.8 (<0.8 borderline), e' 8 (reduced), E/e' 10 (normal <8 but borderline), LAVI 38 (>34), DT 180 (prolonged) = Grade I impaired relaxation.
7. **B** — A5C (Apical 5-chamber) aligns LVOT for Doppler VTI measurement.
