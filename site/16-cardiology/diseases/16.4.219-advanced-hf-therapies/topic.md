# Advanced Heart Failure & Referral Triggers

Related: [[../Cardiology MOC|Cardiology MOC]] · [[../Davidson Chapter 16 - Cardiology Hierarchy|Cardiology Hierarchy]] · [[../Heart Failure and Acute Cardiac Decompensation|Heart Failure and Acute Cardiac Decompensation]] · [[HFrEF]] · [[Cardiogenic shock]] · [[Mechanical circulatory support escalation]] · [[Heart transplantation]] · [[LVAD]] · [[Palliative care in heart failure]]

> [!important]
> Advanced heart failure (AdHF) = **symptomatic HFrEF (NYHA III-IV) despite optimal GDMT**, requiring **specialist referral** for advanced therapies. FCPS/MRCP exams test: **definition**, **referral triggers** (INTERMACS profiles), **advanced therapies** (transplant, LVAD, palliative), **prognostic markers** (VO2 max, CPET, biomarkers), and **palliative care integration**.

## Learning Objectives
- Define advanced heart failure (HFrEF NYHA III-IV on GDMT with evidence of congestion/low output)
- Identify **referral triggers**: recurrent hospitalizations, escalating diuretics, intolerance to GDMT, renal/liver dysfunction, INTERMACS ≤4
- Apply risk stratification: Seattle Heart Failure Model, MAGGIC, CPET (VO2 max), biomarkers
- Understand advanced therapy options: **transplant, LVAD (destination/bridge), palliative care**
- Apply INTERMACS profiles for timing of MCS
- Integrate palliative care early (advance care planning, ICD deactivation discussions)

## Definition
**Advanced Heart Failure (AdHF)** = HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%) with **persistent symptoms (NYHA III-IV) despite optimal GDMT**, and ≥1 of:
- **Recurrent HF hospitalizations** (≥2 in 12 months)
- **Escalating diuretic requirement** (IV diuretics, high-dose loop)
- **Inability to tolerate GDMT** (hypotension, renal dysfunction, hyperkalemia)
- **Cardiorenal syndrome** (Worsening Cr >0.3mg/dL or Cr >2.5 with diuresis)
- **Hepatic dysfunction** (congestive hepatopathy, elevated bilirubin)
- **INTERMACS profile ≤4**
- **Intolerance to beta-blocker/ACEi/ARNI/MRA/SGLT2i** due to hypotension/Cr/K+

## INTERMACS Profiles (Timing of MCS)

| Profile | Description | Urgency |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **1** | **Crash & burn**: Cardiogenic shock, multiple inotropes, mechanical support | **Emergent** |
| **2** | **Sliding on inotropes**: Stable on IV inotropes, not weanable | **Urgent** |
| **3** | **Stable but inotrope-dependent**: Oral inotropes or intermittent IV | **Elective (soon)** |
| **4** | **Resting symptoms**: NYHA IV at rest, no inotropes | **Elective** |
| **5** | **Exertion intolerant**: NYHA IIIb, comfortable at rest | **Elective** |
| **6** | **Exertion limited**: NYHA IIIa | **Monitoring** |
| **7** | **Advanced NYHA III**: Stable on GDMT | **Optimization** |

> [!tip]
> **INTERMACS 1-2**: Emergency/urgent MCS (VA-ECMO → durable LVAD); **3-4**: Elective durable LVAD or transplant workup; **5-7**: Optimize GDMT, transplant evaluation.

## Referral Triggers (ESC/ACC/HFSA)

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **≥2 HF hospitalizations in 12 months** | **Refer for advanced therapies** |
| **IV diuretics ≥2 times in 6 months** | **Refer** |
| **Inability to uptitrate GDMT** due to hypotension (SBP<90), Cr>2.5, K+>5.5 | **Refer** |
| **Cardiorenal syndrome** (Cr ↑>0.3 with diuresis) | **Refer** |
| **LVEF ≤35% + NYHA III-IV** despite 3 months GDMT | **Transplant evaluation** |
| **Peak VO2 <12-14 mL/kg/min** (or <50% predicted) | **Transplant evaluation** |
| **INTERMACS ≤4** | **Urgent MCS/transplant evaluation** |
| **Recurrent VT/VF** on GDMT + ICD | **Transplant evaluation** |
| **Age <70** (transplant); **<80** (LVAD destination) | **Age cutoffs** |

## Advanced Therapy Options

| Therapy | Indication | Key Criteria |
|---------|------------|--------------|
| **Heart Transplant** | **Gold standard** for eligible | Age <70 (up to 75 select), no active malignancy/infection, psychosocial support, BMI<35, PVR<3-5 Wood |
| **LVAD (Bridge to Transplant - BTT)** | INTERMACS 1-3, transplant listed | Bridge while awaiting donor |
| **LVAD (Destination Therapy - DT)** | INTERMACS 4-7, **not transplant candidate** | Age >70 or comorbidities; durable support |
| **VA-ECMO** | Cardiogenic shock (INTERMACS 1) | Bridge to decision; <7-10 days |
| **Palliative Care / Hospice** | End-stage, not candidate for advanced Rx | Symptom control, ICD deactivation, advance directives |

> [!tip]
> **Transplant = best survival (10yr ~50-60%)**; **LVAD DT 2yr survival ~70-80%**; **Medical alone in AdHF: 1yr mortality >50%**.

## Prognostic Markers & Risk Scores

| Marker | Threshold | Significance |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Peak VO2 (CPET)** | **<12 mL/kg/min** (or <50% pred) | Transplant indication; high mortality |
| **VE/VCO2 slope** | **>35** | Poor prognosis |
| **NT-proBNP** | **>5000 pg/mL** (or rising trend) | High risk |
| **Troponin** (hs) | **Detectable / rising** | Myocyte injury, worse outcome |
| **Serum Sodium** | **<135 mmol/L** | Neurohormonal activation |
| **Renal function** | Cr >2.0, eGFR <30 | Cardiorenal syndrome |
| **Seattle Heart Failure Model** | 1-2yr mortality prediction | Risk stratification |
| **MAGGIC Score** | 1-3yr mortality | Validated in HFpEF/HFrEF |
| **6-min walk distance** | **<300m** | Poor functional capacity |

## Cardiopulmonary Exercise Testing (CPET) in AdHF

| Parameter | Normal | AdHF Threshold |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| **Peak VO2** | >20 mL/kg/min | **<14 (transplant eval); <10-12 (urgent)** |
| **% Predicted VO2** | >80% | **<50%** |
| **VE/VCO2 slope** | 20-30 | **>35 (poor prognosis)** |
| **O2 pulse** | >10 mL/beat | Reduced |
| **RER** | >1.1 (max effort) | Submaximal if <1.05 |

> [!tip]
> **Peak VO2 <12-14 mL/kg/min = Class I transplant indication** (if other criteria met).

## Contraindications to Transplant

| Absolute | Relative |
|----------|----------|
| Active malignancy (<5yr remission) | Age >70 (select up to 75) |
| Active infection (uncontrolled) | BMI >35 (or <18) |
| Severe irreversible PVD/CVD | Diabetes with end-organ damage |
| Severe COPD (FEV1<50%) | Hepatic fibrosis/cirrhosis |
| Active substance abuse | Psychiatric instability |
| Non-adherence history | Prior mediastinal radiation |
| Severe psychiatric illness | Prior thoracic surgery |

## LVAD Specifics

### Types
| Generation | Device | Flow | Key Features |
|------------|--------|------|--------------|
| **2nd (axial)** | HeartMate II (discontinued) | Continuous | High thrombosis risk |
| **3rd (centrifugal)** | HeartMate 3, HVAD (discontinued) | **Pulsatile/centrifugal** | **Lower thrombosis, lower stroke** |

### Complications
| Complication | Incidence | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| **Bleeding** (GI, NSVT) | 30-50% | vWF deficiency, manage anticoagulation |
| **Stroke** | 10-15%/yr | Antiplatelet + INR 2-3; HM3 lower |
| **Pump thrombosis** | 5-10% (HMII); <2% HM3 | Intensify anticoag; exchange if needed |
| **Infection** (driveline) | 15-25% | Antibiotics; surgical debridement |
| **Right HF** | 10-30% | Temp RVAD, inhaled NO, milrinone |
| **Arrhythmias** (VT/VF) | Common | ICD already in place; amiodarone |

## Palliative Care Integration

| Component | Timing |
|-----------|--------|
| **Advance care planning** | **At AdHF diagnosis** (not just end-of-life) |
| **ICD deactivation discussion** | Before device replacement / at AdHF diagnosis |
| **Symptom management** (dyspnea, pain, anxiety) | Ongoing |
| **Hospice referral** | Refractory NYHA IV, not candidate for advanced Rx |
| **Caregiver support** | Ongoing |

## Medical Optimization Before Referral

| GDMT Component | Target | AdHF Adjustment |
|----------------|--------|-----------------|
| **ARNI** (sac/val) | Max tolerated (200mg bid) | ↓ dose/stop if SBP<90, K+>5.5, Cr>2.5 |
| **Beta-blocker** | Max tolerated (carvedilol 25mg bid) | ↓ dose/stop if SBP<90, HR<50 |
| **MRA** (epi/spiro) | Max tolerated | **Stop if K+>5.5 or Cr>2.5** |
| **SGLT2i** (dapa/empa) | 10mg daily | Continue if eGFR≥20 (dapa) / ≥20 (empa CKD) |
| **Loop diuretic** | Euvolemia | IV furosemide ± thiazide metolazone |
| **Ivabradine** | HR≥70, SR, max BB | If SBP>90, HR>70 |

## Red Flags / Exam Traps
- **Delaying referral** until "crash" (INTERMACS 1) → worse outcomes
- **Not discussing ICD deactivation** before LVAD/transplant/hospice
- **Continuing MRA in hyperkalemia/renal failure** → dangerous
- **Missing cardiorenal syndrome** → treat as isolated renal failure
- **Transplant evaluation too late** (cachexia, frailty, irreversible end-organ damage)

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- **AdHF = NYHA III-IV on GDMT + congestion/low output markers**
- **Referral triggers**: ≥2 hosp/yr, IV diuretics, GDMT intolerance, cardiorenal, INTERMACS ≤4
- **Transplant**: peak VO2 <12-14 mL/kg/min, <70yr, no absolute contraindications
- **LVAD**: BTT (INTERMACS 1-3), DT (INTERMACS 4-7, not transplant candidate)
- **INTERMACS 1-2**: VA-ECMO → durable LVAD; **3-4**: elective LVAD/transplant
- **Palliative care**: early (advance directives, ICD deactivation), not just end-of-life
- **Palliative inotropes** (milrinone/dobutamine) for symptom control in non-transplant candidates

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- "NYHA IV = transplant now" → need objective CPET, stable on GDMT, psychosocial eval
- "LVAD fixes everything" → complications: bleeding, stroke, infection, RHF
- "Transplant for all AdHF" → strict criteria (age, PVR, comorbidities, adherence)
- "Palliative = giving up" → palliative = symptom control + goals of care
- "VO2 max >14 = no transplant" → relative, not absolute; consider trends, clinical picture

## Summary
**Advanced Heart Failure (AdHF)** = **HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%) with persistent NYHA III-IV symptoms despite optimal GDMT**, plus ≥1 trigger: recurrent hospitalizations (≥2/yr), escalating IV diuretics, GDMT intolerance (hypotension, renal/hepatic dysfunction, hyperkalemia), cardiorenal syndrome, or **INTERMACS profile ≤4**. **Referral triggers**: ≥2 HF hospitalizations/year, IV diuretic dependence, inability to tolerate GDMT, Cr rise >0.3mg/dL with diuresis, **peak VO2 <12-14 mL/kg/min** on CPET. **Advanced therapies**: **Heart transplant** (gold standard; age <70, VO2 <12-14, no absolute contraindications), **LVAD** — **Bridge to Transplant (BTT)** for INTERMACS 1-3; **Destination Therapy (DT)** for INTERMACS 4-7 not transplant candidates. **INTERMACS profiles**: 1=crash & burn (VA-ECMO → LVAD), 2=sliding on inotropes (urgent LVAD), 3=stable inotrope-dependent (elective LVAD/transplant), 4=resting symptoms (elective). **Palliative care integrated early**: advance care planning, ICD deactivation discussions, symptom management, hospice if no advanced therapy candidate. **Prognostication**: peak VO2 <12-14 mL/kg/min (transplant threshold), VE/VCO2 slope >35 (poor prognosis), NT-proBNP >5000 pg/mL.
