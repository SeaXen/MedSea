# Post PCI management DAPT duration stent thrombosis

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 03_Chronic_Coronary_Syndromes | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**Post PCI management DAPT duration stent thrombosis** refers to **dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT)** — combination of aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor (clopidogrel, ticagrelor, prasugrel, cangrelor) used in ACS/PCI/stent placement. **Duration**: 12 months for ACS (ESC 2023), 6 months for stable CAD, individualised by bleeding/ischaemic risk. **PRECISE-DAPT score** (bleeding) and **DAPT score** (ischaemic) guide duration.


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Aspirin**: irreversibly inhibits COX-1 (acetylation at Ser529), reducing TxA2 synthesis → ↓platelet aggregation. **Clopidogrel**: thienopyridine, requires hepatic CYP2C19 activation (loss of function in 30% — BBCTT), irreversible P2Y12 inhibitor. **Ticagrelor**: cyclopentyl-triazolopyrimidine, **reversible** P2Y12 inhibitor, no metabolic activation, faster onset, contraindicated with strong CYP3A inhibitors. **Prasugrel**: thienopyridine, irreversible, requires CYP activation (more potent, less variability), contraindicated in stroke/TIA history (TRITON), >75y, <60kg. **Cangrelor**: IV, ultra-short acting (3-6 min half-life), for PCI periprocedural use.


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

Used in: (1) **ACS** (STEMI, NSTEMI, UA) — 12 months. (2) **Post-PCI** with stenting. (3) **Stable CAD** with high ischaemic risk (LVEF<35%, diabetes, recurrent MI, CKD, ≥3 vessels, complex PCI). **Bleeding risk**: GUSTO (moderate/severe), TIMI (major/minor), BARC (1-5). **Surgery**: stop 5 days ticagrelor/clopidogrel, 7 days prasugrel, aspirin can continue for most.


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

**Pre-treatment**: platelet function testing (VerifyNow P2Y12, light transmittance aggregometry, vasodilator-stimulated phosphoprotein VASP) — not routinely recommended. **CYP2C19 genotyping**: clopidogrel poor metabolisers (~30% population, higher in Asian), consider ticagrelor/prasugrel. **PRECISE-DAPT** (Hb, WBC, age, Cr, prior bleeding) — ≥25 = HBR. **DAPT score** (age, smoking, DM, MI, PCI, stent, EF<30, vein graft) — ≥2 = HIR, consider extended.


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**PRECISE-DAPT ≥25**: high bleeding risk, consider shorter DAPT (3-6mo). **DAPT score ≥2**: high ischaemic risk, consider longer DAPT (>12-30mo). **ARC-HBR criteria**: major + minor (≥1 major or 2 minor) = HBR.


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**ACS**: aspirin 300mg load + ticagrelor 180mg load (or prasugrel 60mg load) → 90mg BD ticagrelor + aspirin 75-100mg daily for 12 months (PLATO). **STEMI undergoing PCI**: aspirin + ticagrelor/prasugrel + UFH + cangrelor (periprocedural). **Stent choice**: 2nd-generation DES (everolimus, zotarolimus) for most. **Bioresorbable scaffolds**: not recommended (ABSORB III, ABSORB IV). **Surgery**: defer if possible; bridging with short-acting cangrelor or GP IIb/IIIa if high risk. **Triple therapy AF+PCI**: 1 week triple, then DOAC + P2Y12 (AUGUSTUS, ENTRUST-AF PCI, AFIRE).


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

**Bleeding** (GUSTO major, BARC ≥3, intracranial 0.3-0.5%). **Stent thrombosis** if premature DAPT cessation (5-10% mortality). **Gastric ulceration**. **Renal dysfunction** (especially ticagrelor — dyspnoea, bradycardia, ventricular pauses). **Thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura** (rare, clopidogrel). **Neutropenia** (rare, clopidogrel). **Aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease** (AERD).


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

Elderly: higher bleeding, shorter DAPT. CKD: dose adjustment, monitor. Diabetes: more benefit. CYP2C19 LOF: switch to ticagrelor/prasugrel. Pregnancy: aspirin safe (low dose), clopidogrel limited data. Surgery: defer, bridge with cangrelor if high risk. **Cangrelor in PCI**: CHAMPION-PHOENIX, CHAMPION-PCI.


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

**ESC 2023 ACS**: ticagrelor/prasugrel preferred over clopidogrel in ACS. **2018 ESC DAPT**: 12mo ACS, 6mo stable CAD, individualised by risk. **2017 ESC STEMI**: DAPT 12mo. **ACC/AHA 2023 ACS**: similar. **NICE NG185**: ticagrelor 1st line. **2020 ESC AF + PCI**: 1 week triple, then DOAC + P2Y12 for 6-12 months. **PRECISE-DAPT, DAPT score**.


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

Don't stop DAPT early after stenting (stent thrombosis risk). Don't forget bleeding risk (PRECISE-DAPT). Don't continue indefinitely without reassessment. Don't bridge with LMWH (no benefit, harm). Don't use prasugrel in stroke/TIA history. **CYP2C19 LOF**: clopidogrel less effective, switch to ticagrelor/prasugrel. **Ticagrelor dyspnoea**: rule out cardiac cause first, often transient.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

**DAPT**: Dual Anti-Platelet Therapy. **COX-1**: cyclooxygenase-1 (aspirin target). **P2Y12**: ADP receptor (clopidogrel/ticagrelor/prasugrel/cangrelor target). **HBR**: High Bleeding Risk (PRECISE-DAPT ≥25, ARC-HBR). **HIR**: High Ischaemic Risk (DAPT score ≥2). **CYP2C19**: clopidogrel activation enzyme. **PLATO**: ticagrelor trial. **TRITON**: prasugrel trial. **CHAMPION**: cangrelor trial. **AUGUSTUS, AFIRE, ENTRUST-AF PCI**: AF+PCI triple therapy.


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

N/A — pharmacology class. Differential for bleeding: peptic ulcer, malignancy, vascular anomaly. Differential for stent thrombosis: in-stent restenosis, recurrent atherosclerosis, vasospasm.


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

Baseline FBC, U&E, LFT, coagulation. Platelet function testing (selected cases). CYP2C19 genotyping (selected cases). Bleeding risk scores. Routine monitoring not required once stable.

## 14. EXAM DRILLS

### 14.1 MCQs

**Q1.** What is the most common aetiology of Post PCI management DAPT duration stent thrombosis?
A. Genetic predisposition
B. Acquired disease
C. Mixed aetiology
D. Idiopathic
E. Familial

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Most cases are acquired with genetic/environmental interaction. Recognise risk factors, presentation, and management.

**Q2.** First-line investigation for Post PCI management DAPT duration stent thrombosis:
A. ECG
B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography
E. Cardiac biomarkers

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Echo is first-line for structural/functional assessment; additional tests as indicated.

**Q3.** Most appropriate first-line therapy for Post PCI management DAPT duration stent thrombosis:
A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy
D. Surgical intervention
E. Multidisciplinary care

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Guideline-directed medical therapy is cornerstone; other modalities as indicated.

**Q4.** Which is a contraindication to therapy in Post PCI management DAPT duration stent thrombosis?
A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

**Q5.** The most common complication of Post PCI management DAPT duration stent thrombosis is:
A. Heart failure
B. Arrhythmia
C. Thromboembolism
D. Death
E. Quality of life reduction

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.
