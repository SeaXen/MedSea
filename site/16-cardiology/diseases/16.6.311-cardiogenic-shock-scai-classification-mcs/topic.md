# Cardiogenic shock (SCAI classification, MCS)

## 1. Definition
**Cardiogenic shock**: State of end-organ hypoperfusion due to primary cardiac pump failure. Defined by:
- **SBP <90 mmHg** (or vasopressors needed to maintain ≥90)
- **Cardiac index <2.2 L/min/m²**
- **PCWP ≥15 mmHg**
- Signs of hypoperfusion: cool extremities, oliguria, altered mental status, lactate >2

## 2. Etiology
- **Acute MI** (most common, 5–10% of STEMI)
- Mechanical complications (VSR, papillary rupture, free wall rupture)
- Acute decompensated HF, fulminant myocarditis
- Takotsubo cardiomyopathy
- Arrhythmia, cardiac tamponade, massive PE
- Post-cardiotomy

## 3. SCAI Classification (Stages A–E)
| Stage | Description | Management |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **A (At risk)** | Hemodynamically stable, no signs of hypoperfusion | Monitoring, treat cause |
| **B (Beginning)** | Hypotension without hypoperfusion | Fluids, low-dose inotropes |
| **C (Classic)** | Hypoperfusion with hypotension | Inotropes + vasopressors, consider MCS |
| **D (Deteriorating)** | Worsening despite escalating therapy | Early MCS escalation |
| **E (Extremis)** | Cardiac arrest, refractory shock | ECMO-CPR, salvage |

## 4. Mechanical Circulatory Support (MCS)
- **IABP**: Afterload reduction, modest CO increase; no mortality benefit in shock
- **Impella (2.5, CP, 5.0)**: LV unloading, ↑CO 2.5–5 L/min
- **VA-ECMO**: Full cardiopulmonary support, both ventricles + oxygenation; ↑afterload
- **TandemHeart**: LA→femoral artery bypass
- **CentriMag / surgically placed VADs**: Bridge to recovery/transplant

## 5. Pharmacologic Support
- **Norepinephrine**: First-line vasopressor (superior to dopamine — less arrhythmia)
- **Dobutamine**: Inotrope for low CO with adequate BP
- **Milrinone**: PDE3 inhibitor; inodilator; avoid if SBP <80
- **Vasopressin**: Add-on for refractory vasoplegia

## 6. Management Algorithm
1. Identify cause (ECHO, coronary angio, lactate, biomarkers)
2. **Early revascularization** (PCI in MI shock — SHOCK trial)
3. Hemodynamic monitoring (arterial line, PA catheter)
4. Escalate: inotropes → MCS (IABP/Impella) → VA-ECMO
5. Transfer to shock/center with advanced MCS and transplant capability

> **Pearls**
> - **Norepinephrine > dopamine** for shock (SOAP II trial)
> - Inotropes ↑ myocardial O₂ demand — can worsen ischemia
> - **VA-ECMO** complications: limb ischemia, LV distension, Harlequin (north-south) syndrome
> - Mortality remains 40–60% despite MCS — early recognition is key
