# VF/pulseless VT (ACLS algorithm)

## 1. Definition
**Ventricular Fibrillation (VF)** and **Pulseless Ventricular Tachycardia (pVT)** are shockable cardiac arrest rhythms with disorganized (VF) or organized (VT) electrical activity but no effective cardiac output. Most common initial rhythm in sudden cardiac arrest.

## 2. ACLS Algorithm (Shockable)
1. **Recognize arrest**, call for help, start **CPR** (chest compressions 100–120/min, 2 in depth)
2. **Attach defibrillator** as soon as possible
3. **Rhythm check at 2 minutes** — confirm VF/pVT
4. **Defibrillate** (biphasic 200 J; escalate to 360 J if refractory)
5. **Resume CPR × 2 min**, IV/IO access, prepare epinephrine
6. **Epinephrine 1 mg IV q3–5 min**
7. **Rhythm check → shock if still VF/pVT**
8. **Amiodarone 300 mg** IV bolus (or lidocaine 1.5 mg/kg) for refractory VF/pVT
9. Continue cycles: CPR → rhythm check → shock → epinephrine ± amiodarone
10. Consider **double sequential external shock** if refractory
11. Treat reversible causes (**Hs and Ts**)

## 3. Defibrillation Principles
- **Biphasic** preferred (200 J initially, 200–360 J)
- **Monophasic**: 360 J
- **Synchronized** cardioversion NOT used in VF (no QRS to sync to)
- **Resume CPR immediately after shock** (do not pause to check pulse/rhythm)

## 4. Medications
| Drug | Dose | Indication |
|------|------|------------|
| **Epinephrine** | 1 mg IV/IO q3–5 min | All cardiac arrest |
| **Amiodarone** | 300 mg IV bolus; 150 mg repeat | Refractory VF/pVT |
| **Lidocaine** | 1.5 mg/kg IV; 0.75 mg/kg repeat | Alternative to amiodarone |
| **Magnesium** | 2 g IV | Torsades de pointes |
| **Calcium** | 1 g IV (10% CaCl) | Hyperkalemia, Ca-channel blocker toxicity |
| **Sodium bicarbonate** | 1 mEq/kg | Severe acidosis, hyperkalemia |

## 5. Hs and Ts (Reversible Causes)
- **Hs**: Hypovolemia, Hypoxia, Hydrogen ion (acidosis), Hypo/hyperkalemia, Hypothermia
- **Ts**: Toxins, Tamponade, Tension pneumothorax, Thrombosis (coronary/pulmonary), Trauma

## 6. Refractory VF
- **Double sequential external shock**
- **Escalate defibrillation energy** (360 J biphasic)
- **Vector change** (anterior-posterior pad position)
- **IV beta-blocker** (esmolol) for electrical storm
- **ECLS/ECMO-CPR** for refractory arrest <60 min downtime

> **Pearls**
> - **Minimize interruptions in CPR** — hands-off time ≤10 sec
> - Continue epinephrine every 3–5 min regardless of rhythm
> - **Amiodarone** for shock-refractory VF/pVT (after 3rd shock)
> - **Treat the cause** — Hs and Ts in every arrest
> - ROSC = palpable pulse + measurable BP — start post-arrest care
