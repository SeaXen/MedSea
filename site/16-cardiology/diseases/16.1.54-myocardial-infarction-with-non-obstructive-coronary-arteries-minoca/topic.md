# MINOCA (MI with Non-Obstructive Coronaries)

## 1. Definition
**MINOCA** = MI by Universal Definition (troponin + clinical evidence) with **non-obstructive CAD** (<50% stenosis in culprit artery) on angiography. Diagnosis of exclusion. Workup needed to identify underlying cause.

## 2. Key Features
- **Prevalence**: 5–10% of all MI; more common in women, young, fewer risk factors
- **Working diagnosis**: requires (1) AMI by criteria, (2) non-obstructive CAD, (3) no overt alternative cause
- **Mechanisms**:
  - **Plaque disruption** (ulceration, erosion) — IVUS/OCT detects
  - **Thromboembolism** — AF, hypercoagulable, PFO
  - **Spasm** — variant angina, cocaine, triptans
  - **SCAD**
  - **Microvascular dysfunction** (INOCA)
  - **Myocarditis** (CMR differentiates)
  - **Takotsubo** (CMR differentiates)
- **CMR** within days = key diagnostic tool (LGE pattern differentiates MI vs myocarditis vs takotsubo)
- **Outcomes**: not benign — similar mortality to obstructive MI

## 3. Management
- **Address underlying cause**:
  - Plaque disruption: DAPT, statin (overt MI)
  - Spasm: CCB, nitrates; avoid β-blockers (can worsen)
  - Thromboembolism: anticoagulation
  - Myocarditis: avoid NSAIDs in early phase; standard HF
- **Standard MI secondary prevention** (DAPT, statin) for atherosclerotic cause
- **CMR** within 1–2 weeks for diagnosis
- **OCT/IVUS** if available during angiography
- **Cardiac rehab** appropriate
- **Reassess risk factors** (esp. smoking, hypertension)

> **Pearls**
> - **MINOCA ≠ normal coronaries**; needs workup
> - **CMR is the key** diagnostic test (LGE pattern)
> - Most common causes: **plaque disruption, spasm, myocarditis, takotsubo**
> - **β-blockers can worsen coronary spasm** — use CCB instead
