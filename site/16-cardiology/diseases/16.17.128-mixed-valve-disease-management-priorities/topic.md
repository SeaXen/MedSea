# Mixed Valve Disease — Management Priorities

## 1. Approach
- Identify the **dominant lesion** (drives symptoms/prognosis)
- Assess all valves (often multi-valve involvement in rheumatic)
- Tailor therapy to the most severe component

## 2. Common Mixed Diseases
- **AS + AR** — combined pressure + volume overload
- **MS + MR** — rheumatic, common
- **MS + AR** — rheumatic
- **TR + TS** — rheumatic
- **Multiple valves in rheumatic heart disease**

## 3. Management Principles
- Treat the dominant lesion first
- Surgery may need to address multiple valves
- **Percutaneous options** for individual lesions (BMV, TAVI, MitraClip, TriClip)

## 4. Example
- MS + MR: BMV may help if MV morphology suitable, but worsens MR if not
- Consider MVR + TV repair instead of BMV

> **Pearls** — Always **identify the dominant lesion**. BMV can worsen MR. Rheumatic often multi-valve. Consider **surgery** for multi-valve rheumatic disease in young patients.
