# Plaque rupture and thrombosis

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 02_Acute_Coronary_Syndromes | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**Plaque rupture** (60-70% of ACS) and **plaque erosion** (25-30%, especially NSTEMI) trigger coronary thrombosis. Rupture: thin-cap fibroatheroma (TCFA, cap <65μm) disruption → lipid core + tissue factor exposure → platelet adhesion → activation → aggregation (GPIIb/IIIa) → fibrin → thrombus. Rupture more in STEMI, men, dyslipidaemia. Erosion: denuded endothelium, thick cap, more in women, smokers, NSTEMI.


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Plaque rupture** triggers: macrophage **MMPs** (MMP-2, MMP-9) degrade collagen cap; cap fatigue from mechanical stress (HR, BP, vasospasm); inflammation (T-cell IFN-γ inhibits SMC collagen synthesis); cap SMC apoptosis; neovessel haemorrhage. **Erosion** triggers: endothelial apoptosis, NETosis, hyaluronan/proteoglycan-rich matrix exposure. **Thrombosis cascade**: subendothelial collagen/vWF/tissue factor → platelet adhesion (GPIb-vWF, GPVI-collagen) → activation → recruitment (TxA2, ADP) → aggregation (GPIIb/IIIa) → procoagulant surface → coagulation cascade (TF-FVIIa, tenase, prothrombinase) → thrombin → fibrin. **Virchow's triad**: endothelial injury, abnormal flow, hypercoagulability.


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

STEMI (complete occlusion): persistent STE, ongoing pain, diaphoresis, dyspnoea. NSTEMI (partial): STD, TWI, transient symptoms, troponin rise. UA: ischaemic symptoms at rest, no biomarker rise, may have dynamic ECG. **Plaque erosion**: younger, female, smokers, fewer traditional risk factors, more NSTEMI. **Plaque rupture**: older, male, DM, dyslipidaemia, higher LDL, more multivessel, more STEMI. **No-reflow**: post-PCI, despite epicardial patency, microvascular flow absent (distal embolisation, microvascular spasm, oedema).


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

ECG: STEMI (persistent STE), NSTEMI (STD/TWI), UA (transient changes, normal between episodes). Troponin: rise/fall. Angiography: TIMI flow (0-3), thrombus grade (0-5), culprit lesion. **OCT** (gold standard for cap, 10μm resolution): identifies rupture (fibrous cap discontinuity, cavity), erosion (attached thrombus over intact plaque), TCFA. **IVUS** (100μm): plaque burden, virtual histology. **NIRS** (near-infrared spectroscopy): lipid core burden index (LCBI >400 = lipid-rich). **CTCA**: low-attenuation plaque, napkin-ring sign, positive remodelling.


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**TIMI thrombus grade** (angio): 0 (none), 1 (possible, haziness), 2 (definite, ≤½ vessel), 3 (definite, ½-2 vessel), 4 (definite, ≥2 vessel), 5 (total occlusion). **TIMI flow** post-PCI: 0-1 (no reflow, poor prognosis), 2 (slow), 3 (normal = successful). **Myocardial blush grade** (MBG): 0-3 (microvascular perfusion). **No-reflow** = 30% mortality. **Stent thrombosis** timing: acute <24h, subacute 1-30d, late 1-12m, very late >1y. **Risk**: 1-2%/year for DES.


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**Acute**: DAPT (aspirin 300 + P2Y12 — ticagrelor 180 preferred PLATO, prasugrel 60 TRITON, clopidogrel 600), UFH 70U/kg bolus, GP IIb/IIIa (abciximab, eptifibatide, tirofiban) bailout. Primary PCI <90 min. Aspiration thrombectomy (selective, large thrombus). **Fibrinolysis**: tenecteplase (single bolus, weight-adjusted) if PCI >120 min. **Stent thrombosis prevention**: optimal deployment (IVUS/OCT guidance), adequate DAPT, address stent underexpansion. **No-reflow**: GP IIb/IIIa, adenosine, nitroprusside, aspiration.


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

**Stent thrombosis** (acute <24h: technical — underexpansion, dissection, malapposition; subacute 1-30d: healing; late 1-12m: DAPT cessation, resistance; very late >1y: neoatherosclerosis, very late malapposition). **Distal embolisation**: thrombus fragments to microvasculature, no-reflow, periprocedural MI. **Reinfarction**: incomplete revascularisation, residual thrombus, re-rupture. **Bleeding**: access site (femoral > radial), GI, retroperitoneal, intracranial (lysis 0.5-1%). **Recurrent ischaemia**: in-stent restenosis, new plaque.


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

Pregnancy: SCAD more common than rupture; PCI preferred, radiation minimisation. CKD: avoid enoxaparin if CrCl<20, fondaparinux if CrCl<20, UFH safe. HIT (heparin-induced thrombocytopenia): type II immune, days 5-10, >50% platelet fall, treat with bivalirudin, argatroban, fondaparinux, danaparoid. Lytics in pregnancy: alteplase category C. Elderly: higher bleeding, bivalirudin may be preferred. DM: more multivessel, smaller vessels, higher restenosis.


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

ESC 2023 ACS: ticagrelor/prasugrel > clopidogrel; cangrelor in PCI; routine thrombus aspiration NOT recommended (TOTAL trial). ACC/AHA 2023: bivalirudin not superior to UFH in modern PCI. 2018 ESC DAPT: 12mo for ACS, 6mo for stable CAD, shorter if HBR (PRECISE-DAPT >25), longer if HIR (DAPT score ≥2). 2017 ESC STEMI: radial preferred, DES preferred, complete revascularisation in multivessel (COMPLETE).


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

Distinguish **rupture vs erosion** — different populations, different management. Aspirin resistance rare; check compliance. Stent thrombosis ≠ restenosis — sudden/catastrophic vs gradual. DAPT interruption for surgery: 5d ticagrelor/clopidogrel, 7d prasugrel; bridge with cangrelor or GP IIb/IIIa if high risk. Triple therapy AF+PCI: 1-week triple, then DOAC + P2Y12 (AFIRE, AUGUSTUS, ENTRUST-AF PCI). Reperfusion arrhythmias = marker of success, often self-limiting.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

**TCFA**: Thin-Cap FibroAtheroma (cap <65μm). **MMPs** degrade cap. **TF** (tissue factor) activates coagulation. **vWF** for platelet adhesion. **GP IIb/IIIa** final common pathway. **GP VI** for collagen. **GPIb** for vWF. **TIMI flow 0-3**: no/slow/normal. **TIMI thrombus 0-5**: none to total occlusion. **OCT > IVUS > angio** for plaque characterisation.


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Coronary vasospasm (Prinzmetal, cocaine, triptans — transient STE, normal angio, hyperventilation test). Takotsubo (apical ballooning, no culprit, post-stress, troponin modest). SCAD (young women, peripartum, intramural haematoma on OCT). Aortic dissection (wide mediastinum, asymmetric pulses). Myocarditis (viral prodrome, diffuse ST changes).


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

ECG (immediate, serial); hs-cTn 0/1h; coronary angiography with possible OCT/IVUS; CTCA if low-intermediate probability; cardiac MRI if MINOCA; lipid profile, hs-CRP, Lp(a), coagulation; D-dimer if dissection/PE; echo for RWMA, LVEF, mechanical complications.

## 14. EXAM DRILLS

### 14.1 MCQs

**Q1.** What is the most common aetiology of Plaque rupture and thrombosis?
A. Genetic predisposition
B. Acquired disease
C. Mixed aetiology
D. Idiopathic
E. Familial

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Most cases are acquired with genetic/environmental interaction. Recognise risk factors, presentation, and management.

**Q2.** First-line investigation for Plaque rupture and thrombosis:
A. ECG
B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography
E. Cardiac biomarkers

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Echo is first-line for structural/functional assessment; additional tests as indicated.

**Q3.** Most appropriate first-line therapy for Plaque rupture and thrombosis:
A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy
D. Surgical intervention
E. Multidisciplinary care

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Guideline-directed medical therapy is cornerstone; other modalities as indicated.

**Q4.** Which is a contraindication to therapy in Plaque rupture and thrombosis?
A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

**Q5.** The most common complication of Plaque rupture and thrombosis is:
A. Heart failure
B. Arrhythmia
C. Thromboembolism
D. Death
E. Quality of life reduction

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.
