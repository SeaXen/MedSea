# IVUS and OCT (Intravascular Imaging)

<callout icon="💔" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** IVUS & OCT — Cardiology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

## 1. Definitions
- **IVUS (Intravascular Ultrasound):** 20–60 MHz catheter → greyscale cross-section
- **OCT (Optical Coherence Tomography):** near-infrared light → 10× higher resolution (10–20 µm)
- Both allow intracoronary imaging for **plaque characterisation, stent optimisation, complication detection**

---

## 2. IVUS vs OCT
| Feature | IVUS | OCT |
|---|---|---|
| Resolution | 100 µm | 10–20 µm |
| Penetration | 4–8 mm | 1–3 mm |
| Blood clearance | Not needed | Required (contrast flush) |
| Calcium | Acoustic shadow | Sharp border |
| Plaque characterisation | Good | Superior (lipid, fibrous cap) |
| Vessel size | Better | Limited in large vessels |

---

## 3. Clinical Indications
- **Left main** PCI (IVUS-guided ↓ mortality, ULTIMATE, IVUS-XPL)
- **Bifurcation, long lesions, CTO** PCI
- **Stent optimisation:** detect underexpansion, malapposition, edge dissection
- **Mechanism of stent thrombosis / restenosis**
- **Plaque characterisation:** TCFA, calcified nodule, erosion
- **Spontaneous coronary artery dissection (SCAD)**
- **Angiographically ambiguous lesions**

---

## 4. Key Measurements
- **MLA (minimal lumen area):** LM <6 mm², LAD/prox <4 mm², others <2.5 mm² predict ischaemia
- **Plaque burden** (%) = (EEM − lumen) / EEM
- **TCFA** (thin-cap fibroatheroma): cap <65 µm + lipid pool
- **Calcification:** depth, angle, thickness (important for calcium modification)

---

## 5. Key Pearls

> **Pearls**
- **IVUS = ultrasound, OCT = light** (10× higher resolution)
- **IVUS-guided LM PCI** ↓ mortality and MACE
- Detect **stent underexpansion** (most common cause of ISR / thrombosis)
- MLA thresholds: LM <6, LAD/prox <4, other <2.5 mm²
- OCT superior for **plaque characterisation** (TCFA, calcium)
