# Treatment (NSAIDs, colchicine, steroids, pericardiocentesis if tamponade)

## 1. Treatment Goals
- **Symptom relief** (pain, inflammation)
- **Prevent recurrence** (10–30% without colchicine; <5% with)
- **Treat underlying cause** (TB, uremia, malignancy, etc.)
- **Prevent complications** (effusion, tamponade, constriction)

## 2. First-Line Therapy
### NSAIDs (Mainstay)
- **Aspirin** 750–1000 mg q8h × 1–2 weeks, then taper (preferred in post-MI)
- **Ibuprofen** 600–800 mg q8h × 1–2 weeks, then taper
- **Indomethacin** 25–50 mg q8h (avoid post-MI)
- Always **taper over 1–2 weeks** to prevent rebound
- **Gastric protection**: PPI

### Colchicine (Adjunct to NSAIDs)
- **0.5 mg q12h** (or q24h if <70 kg, elderly, renal impairment)
- **Duration**: 3 months (acute) or 6 months (recurrent)
- **CRITICAL** for preventing recurrence
- **COPE, CORP, ICAP trials**: ↓ recurrence by ~50%

## 3. Second-Line Therapy
### Corticosteroids
- **Indicated if**: NSAID contraindication, refractory, autoimmune cause, post-cardiac injury
- **Prednisone** 0.25–0.5 mg/kg/day, **taper slowly over 6–8 weeks**
- **Avoid rapid taper** → ↑ recurrence
- **Use lowest effective dose**
- **Caution**: Constriction risk, immunosuppression
- **Plus colchicine** always

### Other
- **IVIG** for refractory autoimmune
- **Azathioprine, methotrexate** for steroid-dependent
- **Anakinra (IL-1 inhibitor)** for refractory cases

## 4. Pericardiocentesis
### Indications
- **Cardiac tamponade** (hemodynamic compromise)
- **Symptomatic large effusion** not responding to medical Rx
- **Diagnostic** for unexplained etiology (esp. suspected TB, malignancy)

### Approach
- **Echo-guided subxiphoid** (preferred)
- Apical or parasternal if needed
- Send fluid: cell count, culture, cytology, ADA, LDH, protein

## 5. Pericardial Window
- For **recurrent effusion** (esp. malignant)
- Subxiphoid or VATS
- Allows continuous drainage

## 6. Specific Etiologies
| Cause | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| **Viral/idiopathic** | NSAID + colchicine |
| **Post-MI (early)** | High-dose aspirin |
| **Dressler** | NSAID + colchicine (steroids if refractory) |
| **TB** | ATT × 6–9 months + steroids |
| **Uremic** | Dialysis intensification + pericardiocentesis/window |
| **Neoplastic** | Pericardial window + chemo/radiation |
| **Autoimmune** | Steroids + colchicine + DMARD |

## 7. Activity Restriction
- **Avoid strenuous exercise** until resolution (especially myopericarditis)
- Risk of arrhythmia, recurrence

> **Pearls**
> - **Colchicine + NSAID** is the gold standard for first-line
> - **Colchicine** reduces recurrence by 50% (most important adjunct)
> - **Avoid steroids early** in viral/idiopathic (↑ recurrence) — except if specific indication
> - **Slow steroid taper** (>6 weeks) to prevent recurrence
> - **Pericardiocentesis** only for tamponade or large symptomatic effusion
