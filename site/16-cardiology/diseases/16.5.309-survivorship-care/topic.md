# Cardiovascular Survivorship Care

## 1. Definition
Long-term CV care of cancer survivors — increasing population as cancer survival improves.

## 2. Highest-Risk Survivors
- **Anthracycline** exposure (any dose)
- **Chest radiation** >30 Gy
- **HER2-targeted therapy** (trastuzumab)
- **Tyrosine kinase inhibitors**
- **Allogeneic HSCT** (GVHD, accelerated atherosclerosis)
- Hodgkin lymphoma survivors (combined chemo + radiation)

## 3. Components
- **Baseline CV risk assessment** at entry to survivorship
- **Lifestyle counseling** — diet, exercise, smoking cessation
- **Screening echo** every 3–5 years (more frequent if high-risk)
- **BP, lipids, glucose** management (aggressive targets)
- **Recognition of late effects** — pericardial, valvular, CAD, conduction
- **Cardio-oncology referral** if abnormalities

## 4. Special Considerations
- **Pregnancy** after chemo — echo preconception, monitor
- **Exercise** is protective (but avoid extreme during active treatment)
- **Secondary malignancy** risk (breast CA screening if chest radiation)
- **Psychosocial** impact

## 5. Follow-up Schedule
- **High risk**: echo every 2–3 years
- **Moderate risk**: every 5 years
- **Low risk**: standard population screening

> **Pearls** — **Lifelong CV follow-up** for cancer survivors. **Lifestyle + risk factor control** is the cornerstone. **Echo every 2–5 years** based on risk. Cardio-oncology clinics are essential.
