# Modified Duke criteria

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 06_Infective_Endocarditis | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**Modified Duke criteria** (Infective Endocarditis, IE): microbial infection of endocardial surface (heart valves, mural endocardium, septal defects, prosthetic material). **Native valve** (NVE) vs **prosthetic valve** (PVE, early <60d, intermediate 60-365d, late >1y). **Acute** (S. aureus, fulminant) vs **subacute** (Strep, gradual). **Duke criteria** (modified) for diagnosis: definite, possible, rejected. **Incidence**: 3-10/100,000. **Mortality**: 20-30% in-hospital, 30-50% at 1y.


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

**Pathogenesis**: **Endothelial injury** (turbulent flow, jet lesion, MVP, degenerative valve, prosthetic, devices) → **NBTE** (non-bacterial thrombotic endocarditis) → **bacteraemia** (dental, GI, GU, skin) → **adhesion** (dextran, fibronectin-binding proteins) → **colonisation** → **biofilm** (S. epidermidis) → **vegetation** (fibrin, platelets, bacteria, inflammatory cells). **Organisms**: **S. aureus** (most common acute, prosthetic, IVDU — MRSA 30%, MSSA 30%), **Viridans streptococci** (subacute, dental, post-procedure), **S. gallolyticus (bovis)** (colon cancer association), **S. epidermidis** (prosthetic, devices), **Enterococci** (GU/GI procedures, elderly), **HACEK** (rare, fastidious, oropharyngeal), **Fungal** (Candida, Aspergillus, immunocompromised, prosthetic, TPN), **Culture-negative** (5-20% — HACEK, Coxiella, Bartonella, Tropheryma whipplei, Brucella, Chlamydia, fungi, prior antibiotics). **Aortic valve** most commonly involved (40%), then mitral (30%), then tricuspid (IVDU, 30% of IVDU IE).


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

**Fever** (90%, may be absent in elderly, IE, renal failure). **New/changed murmur** (50-80%, especially regurgitant). **Septic emboli** (stroke — 20%, systemic, splenic, renal, mesenteric, pulmonary in right-sided). **Immunological**: **Osler nodes** (tender, finger pulps, hands), **Roth spots** (retinal, pale, haemorrhagic), **Janeway lesions** (non-tender, palms/soles, septic emboli), **splinter haemorrhages** (subungual), glomerulonephritis, rheumatoid factor. **Heart failure** (valve destruction, acute MR/AR). **Splinter haemorrhages** (not specific). **Anaemia** (chronic inflammation). **Splenomegaly**. **Clubbing** (chronic). **Septic shock**. **Asymptomatic bacteraemia** in prosthetic, especially S. epidermidis.


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

**Modified Duke criteria** (2000): **Major** — (1) positive blood culture (typical organism × 2, persistently positive), (2) imaging (echo vegetation, abscess, dehiscence, new partial dehiscence). **Minor** — predisposing heart disease, IVDU, fever >38, vascular phenomena (major emboli, mycotic aneurysm, intracranial, conjunctival, Janeway), immunological phenomena (Osler, Roth, glomerulonephritis, RF), positive blood culture not meeting major, echo suggestive. **Definite**: 2 major, OR 1 major + 3 minor, OR 5 minor, OR pathology. **TTE** (sensitivity 60-70%): vegetation (>10mm, oscillating mass on valve), abscess, new regurgitation. **TOE** (sensitivity 90-95%, especially prosthetic, mitral, complications): if TTE negative but suspicion high, prosthetic, pre-operative. **Blood cultures** (≥3 sets, different sites, ≥1h apart, before antibiotics). **CXR**, **ECG** (PR prolongation → aortic root abscess), **CT/MRI** (emboli, mycotic aneurysm). **Lab**: anaemia, leukocytosis, ↑ESR, ↑CRP, RF+, low C3/C4, haematuria.


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

**Severity**: severe (HF, embolic, large vegetation, abscess, prosthetic, fungal). **Mortality**: S. aureus 40%, fungal 50%, prosthetic 30-40%. **Complications** predict mortality. **Vegetation size >10mm** = embolic risk. **Indication for surgery** predicts outcome.


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

**Antibiotics** (bactericidal, prolonged 4-6 weeks, IV): **Empirical** (native valve, subacute, no haemodynamic compromise): ampicillin + (flu)cloxacillin + gentamicin (UK). **Empirical** (acute, severe, prosthetic, IVDU): vancomycin + gentamicin (or meropenem if HACEK/fungal). **Specific**: see regimens. **Indications for surgery** (class I): (1) **HF** (acute AR, MR, prosthetic dysfunction), (2) **Uncontrolled infection** (abscess, fistula, persistent bacteraemia, fungal, prosthetic dehiscence), (3) **Prevention of embolism** (large vegetation >10mm + embolic event, persistent >10mm after 4-6w antibiotics, especially mitral). **Surgery in**: prosthetic (especially early S. aureus, fungal, abscess), right-sided IVDU (if recurrent, large, fungal). **Thrombolysis** in prosthetic thrombosis: controversial. **Anticoagulation**: continue if mechanical prosthesis; STOP if ischaemic stroke (controversial), ICH; AVOID in S. aureus IE (intracranial mycotic aneurysm).


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

**Heart failure** (50-60%, valve destruction). **Embolism** (20-50%, stroke, systemic, pulmonary in right). **Abscess** (30-50% prosthetic, ring, conduction abnormalities). **Mycotic aneurysm** (2-10%, intracranial, hepatic, splenic). **Glomerulonephritis** (immune complex). **Death** (20-30% in-hospital, 30-50% at 1y). **Relapse** (5-10%). **Recurrence** (especially IVDU, prosthetic).


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

**IVDU**: S. aureus, right-sided (tricuspid), pulmonary emboli, often no surgery. **Prosthetic**: S. epidermidis, S. aureus, high mortality, surgery often needed. **Elderly**: enterococcus, S. gallolyticus (bovis) — colon cancer, GI malignancy. **Pregnancy**: rare, high mortality, urgent surgery. **HACEK**: difficult to culture, slow. **Fungal**: high mortality, surgery. **Culture-negative**: HACEK, Coxiella, Bartonella, fungi, prior antibiotics.


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

**ESC 2023 IE (EHJ)**: full guideline update. **AHA/ACC 2023 IE**: similar. **NICE NG100**: prophylaxis. **Infective Endocarditis BSAC**: antibiotic regimens. **Duke criteria 2000**, **modified Duke 2015 (ESC)**: ESC added imaging as major criterion (TOE, PET, WBC SPECT, CT).


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

**Blood cultures BEFORE antibiotics** (≥3 sets). **TOE** for prosthetic, mitral, pre-operative, complications. **S. aureus** can cause IE without murmur. **Negative echo doesn't rule out IE** (early, small vegetations). **Surgery in HF, abscess, embolic risk** — don't delay. **Anticoagulation in prosthetic**: continue if mechanical (no ischaemic stroke), but avoid in S. aureus (intracranial mycotic aneurysm risk). **IVDU**: may not need surgery despite large vegetation, treat addiction.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

**Duke major** — blood culture (typical ×2, persistent), imaging (echo vegetation, abscess, dehiscence). **Duke minor** — predisposing, IVDU, fever, vascular (Janeway, emboli, mycotic aneurysm), immunologic (Osler, Roth, RF, GN), culture, echo. **FROM JANE**: Febrile, Roth spots, Osler nodes, Murmur, Janeway, Anemia, Nail (splinter), Emboli. **S. aureus** — most common acute. **Strep viridans** — subacute, dental. **S. bovis (gallolyticus)** — colon cancer. **S. epidermidis** — prosthetic, devices. **HACEK** — Haemophilus, Aggregatibacter, Cardiobacterium, Eikenella, Kingella. **Indication for surgery**: HF, uncontrolled infection, embolism prevention. **IE prophylaxis**: prior IE, prosthetic, unrepaired cyanotic CHD, transplant valvulopathy (NOT routine for MVP).


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Culture-negative endocarditis (HACEK, Coxiella, Bartonella, fungi, lupus, malignancy — NBTE, Libman-Sacks). Atrial myxoma (mimics with emboli, fever). Vasculitis, SLE. Acute rheumatic fever (Jones). Marantic (NBTE) endocarditis (malignancy, SLE).


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

Blood cultures (≥3 sets, different sites, before antibiotics), TTE, TOE, ECG (PR interval — abscess), CXR, bloods (FBC, ESR, CRP, RF, complement, U&E, LFT), urinalysis, CT/MRI (emboli, mycotic aneurysm), CT-PET, WBC-SPECT, colon (S. bovis).

## 14. EXAM DRILLS

### 14.1 MCQs

**Q1.** What is the most common aetiology of Modified Duke criteria?
A. Genetic predisposition
B. Acquired disease
C. Mixed aetiology
D. Idiopathic
E. Familial

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Most cases are acquired with genetic/environmental interaction. Recognise risk factors, presentation, and management.

**Q2.** First-line investigation for Modified Duke criteria:
A. ECG
B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography
E. Cardiac biomarkers

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Echo is first-line for structural/functional assessment; additional tests as indicated.

**Q3.** Most appropriate first-line therapy for Modified Duke criteria:
A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy
D. Surgical intervention
E. Multidisciplinary care

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Guideline-directed medical therapy is cornerstone; other modalities as indicated.

**Q4.** Which is a contraindication to therapy in Modified Duke criteria?
A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

**Q5.** The most common complication of Modified Duke criteria is:
A. Heart failure
B. Arrhythmia
C. Thromboembolism
D. Death
E. Quality of life reduction

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.

### 14.2 SBAs

**SBA1.** A 55-year-old presents with features of Modified Duke criteria. Most appropriate next step?
A. Discharge
B. Outpatient follow-up
C. Investigation
D. Empirical treatment
E. Multidisciplinary referral

> **Answer:** C
> **Rationale:** Confirm diagnosis before treatment.

**SBA2.** Patient with Modified Duke criteria on optimal therapy develops new symptoms. What is the next step?
A. Add another medication
B. Refer for invasive assessment
C. Reassess diagnosis
D. Multidisciplinary team discussion
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Rationale:** Comprehensive reassessment with MDT input.
