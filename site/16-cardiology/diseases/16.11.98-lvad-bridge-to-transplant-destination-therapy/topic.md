# LVAD

## 1. Definition
**Left ventricular assist device (LVAD)** = mechanical pump that supports LV function in advanced HF, augmenting systemic cardiac output. Modern continuous-flow pumps (HeartMate 3) are the standard. Used as **bridge to transplant, destination therapy, or bridge to decision**.

## 2. Key Features
- **Indications**: advanced HFrEF (LVEF <25%), NYHA IIIB/IV despite GDMT, INTERMACS profile 2–4
- **Triad of advanced HF**: severe symptoms, recurrent hospitalisation, end-organ dysfunction
- **Types**:
  - **Continuous axial flow** (older: HeartMate II)
  - **Continuous centrifugal flow with full levitation** (HeartMate 3 — preferred, ↓ pump thrombosis)
- **Outcomes**: 2-year survival ~70–80% on HeartMate 3 (similar to transplant in some cohorts)
- **Complications**:
  - **Pump thrombosis** (1–5%/y with HeartMate 3)
  - **GI bleeding** (AVM formation from continuous flow)
  - **Driveline infection** (chronic, common)
  - **Stroke** (ischaemic, haemorrhagic)
  - **Right HF** (RV failure post-implant)
  - **Aortic insufficiency** (progressive)

## 3. Management
- **Patient selection**: age, comorbidity, social support, adherence
- **Anticoagulation**: warfarin + aspirin (target INR 2–3)
- **BP control**: MAP 65–80 (limited pulsatility; measure with Doppler)
- **LVAD clinic**: weekly early, then regular follow-up
- **RV failure**: may need RVAD; aggressive diuresis, inotropes
- **Psychological support**: depression common
- **End-of-life** planning: device deactivation discussion
- **Cardiac transplant** remains the gold standard for eligible patients

> **Pearls**
> - **HeartMate 3** = current standard; ↓ pump thrombosis
> - **MAP 65–80** (measure with Doppler — narrow pulse pressure)
> - **Anticoagulate**: warfarin + aspirin
> - **GI bleeding** from AVMs (continuous flow)
