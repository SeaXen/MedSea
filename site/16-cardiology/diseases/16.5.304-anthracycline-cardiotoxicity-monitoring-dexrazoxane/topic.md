# Anthracycline Cardiotoxicity

## 1. Mechanism
- **Topoisomerase 2β** inhibition in cardiomyocytes
- **Oxidative stress** (Fe-mediated free radicals)
- **Mitochondrial dysfunction**
- Topoisomerase 2β is NOT a tumor target (vs 2α)

## 2. Dose Relationship
- **Doxorubicin** 400 mg/m² → 5% HF
- 550 mg/m² → 26%
- 700 mg/m² → 48%
- Epirubicin similar but 1.5–2x higher cumulative dose tolerated
- **Liposomal doxorubicin** — less cardiotoxic

## 3. Risk Factors
- **Cumulative dose** (most important)
- Age (older)
- Female sex
- Pre-existing cardiac disease
- Chest radiation
- **Concomitant trastuzumab**
- **CVD risk factors** (HTN, DM, smoking)

## 4. Prevention
- **Dexrazoxane** — iron chelator, ↓ cardiotoxicity (FDA-approved for >300 mg/m² doxorubicin)
- **Liposomal doxorubicin** — equivalent efficacy, less cardiotoxicity
- **Continuous infusion** (vs bolus)
- **Limit cumulative dose** (400 mg/m² doxorubicin standard)
- **ACEi / β-blocker** during therapy (prophylactic — debated)

## 5. Monitoring
- **Baseline echo + GLS** before starting
- **Every 3 cycles** for high-risk
- **GLS drop >15%** = subclinical dysfunction → consider intervention
- **Troponin/BNP** as adjuncts

> **Pearls** — **Dexrazoxane** for prevention at >300 mg/m² doxorubicin. **GLS** is most sensitive early marker. **Cumulative dose** is the strongest predictor. Concurrent **β-blocker/ACEi** may help.
