# Cardiac biomarkers

> [!info] **Topic Classification**
> **Section:** 02_Acute_Coronary_Syndromes | **Category:** Cardiology

---

## 1. HIGH-YIELD SUMMARY (30-Second Review)

**Cardiac biomarkers** for ACS: **hs-cTn I/T** (high-sensitivity troponin, gold standard, 0/1h algorithm, cardiac-specific, rises 3-6h, peaks 24-48h, normalises 7-14d). **CK-MB** (older, useful for reinfarction, 4-6h rise, 24h peak, 48-72h normalisation). **Myoglobin** (earliest rise 1-3h but non-specific). **BNP/NT-proBNP** (HF). **hs-CRP** (inflammation). **D-dimer** (PE).


## 2. ETIOLOGY & PATHOPHYSIOLOGY

Troponin I/T regulate calcium-mediated actin-myosin interaction. Highly cardiac-specific isoforms. CK-MB is cytosolic enzyme, predominantly cardiac. Myoglobin O2-binding, not cardiac-specific. **Cutoffs**: 99th percentile. **Sex-specific** cutoffs (women lower).


## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

STEMI: peak 24h, persists 5-14d. NSTEMI: similar but lower. CK-MB: useful for reinfarction. Myoglobin: earliest but low specificity. False-positive troponin elevation: AF, PE, myocarditis, pericarditis, severe sepsis, CKD, stroke, Takotsubo, exercise, rhabdomyolysis.


## 4. DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH

**ESC 0/1h algorithm**: rule-out baseline <5 + Δ1h <3 ng/L; observe 5-11.9 OR Δ1h 3-4.9; rule-in baseline ≥52 OR Δ1h ≥5. **ACC 0/3h algorithm**. **Sex-specific 99th percentile**. **Δ change** more important than absolute.


## 5. SEVERITY ASSESSMENT & RISK STRATIFICATION

Peak troponin correlates with infarct size, mortality. **hs-cTn >5× URL** = high risk, may benefit from early invasive. **NT-proBNP >5000** = severe HF, high mortality.


## 6. MANAGEMENT ALGORITHM

Serial per 0/1h or 0/3h algorithm. Faster rule-in/rule-out reduces ED length, cost, admissions. In NSTEMI, troponin rise confirms but doesn't delay treatment. False positives need clinical correlation. In CKD, use Δ change (chronic elevation common).


## 7. COMPLICATIONS & PROGNOSIS

False positives → unnecessary angio, contrast, bleeding. False negatives rare with hs-cTn. Chronic troponin elevation in CKD associated with worse prognosis. Periprocedural MI: >5× URL rise + new Q/LV dysfunction.


## 8. SPECIAL POPULATIONS

CKD: chronic elevation, use Δ change (>20% rise/fall). Elderly: higher baseline. Women: lower 99th percentile, sex-specific. ESRD: TnT more often elevated. Post-PCI: periprocedural MI criteria.


## 9. LATEST GUIDELINES & EVIDENCE

ESC 2023 ACS: hs-cTn 0/1h class IA. ACC/AHA 2023: hs-cTn preferred. Fourth Universal Definition of MI 2018: rise/fall cTn ≥1 value >99th percentile + clinical ischaemia. Type 1-5 MI classification.


## 10. CONFUSIONS & COMMON PITFALLS

hs-cTn elevation NOT specific for ACS. Non-ACS causes: AF, PE, sepsis, myocarditis, pericarditis, Takotsubo, aortic dissection, anaemia, hypovolaemia, hypertensive emergency, CKD, stroke, exercise, rhabdomyolysis.


## 11. MNEMONICS & MEMORY AIDS

hs-cTn (high-sensitivity cardiac troponin), CK-MB (Creatine Kinase Muscle-Brain), URL (Upper Reference Limit, 99th percentile), Δ (delta change), MAMA (Myoglobin, cTn, CK-MB, AST/LDH).


## 12. DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS

Non-ACS troponin elevation: AF, PE, myocarditis, pericarditis, sepsis, CKD, stroke, hypertensive emergency, Takotsubo, aortic dissection, anaemia, hypovolaemia, cardiotoxic chemo, exercise, rhabdomyolysis.


## 13. INVESTIGATIONS

Serial hs-cTn 0/1h or 0/3h, serial ECG, bedside echo, CXR, FBC, U&E, LFT, TFT, lipids, HbA1c, NT-proBNP (if HF), D-dimer (if PE), ABG (if critically ill).

**Q2.** First-line investigation for Cardiac biomarkers:
A. ECG
B. Echocardiography
C. Cardiac MRI
D. Coronary angiography
E. Cardiac biomarkers

> **Answer:** B

**Q3.** Most appropriate first-line therapy for Cardiac biomarkers:
A. Lifestyle modification
B. Pharmacological therapy
C. Device therapy
D. Surgical intervention
E. Multidisciplinary care

> **Answer:** B
> **Explanation:** Guideline-directed medical therapy is cornerstone; other modalities as indicated.

A. Renal dysfunction
B. Hepatic dysfunction
C. Drug interactions
D. Patient preference
E. All of the above

> **Answer:** E
> **Explanation:** All must be considered. Individualise therapy.

> **Explanation:** Arrhythmia common; severity determines outcome.
