# Consent in adolescence - Gillick and Fraser

---

## Definition

Consent in adolescence balances the developing autonomy of the young person with their need for protection. The English legal test for under-16s is Gillick competence (capacity to understand the nature, purpose and risks of treatment), and Fraser guidelines (specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual-health advice without parental knowledge). The Mental Capacity Act 2005 (16+) and Family Law Reform Act 1969 (16-17 can consent as adults) apply. Competent minors can consent to treatment but cannot refuse life-saving treatment in the same way adults can (court authority required).

## Core Clinical Content

### Gillick competence
Under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions

### Fraser guidelines
Specific: contraception, sexual health - used when no parental involvement

### Test of competence
Understands nature/purpose, retains information, weighs up, communicates decision

### 16-17 year olds
Presumed capable (Mental Capacity Act 2005, Family Law Reform Act 1969)

### Can consent but not refuse
Mature minor can consent; cannot refuse life-saving treatment (court)

### Parental role
Should usually be involved; not required if adolescent is competent and refuses

### Documentation
Record competence assessment: understands condition, options, risks, benefits

### Court involvement
Disputes over refusal in competent minors -> Court of Protection / High Court

### Looked-after children
Local authority shares parental responsibility; complex - seek legal advice

### Emergency treatment
Can be given to under-16 if life-saving and parents not contactable
