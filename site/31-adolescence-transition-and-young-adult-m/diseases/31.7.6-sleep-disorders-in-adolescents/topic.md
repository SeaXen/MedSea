# Sleep disorders in adolescents

---

## Definition

Sleep disorders in adolescents are common and impact physical health, mental health, and academic performance. The two main patterns are: (1) Delayed sleep phase syndrome (DSPS) - circadian rhythm shift common in adolescents, with sleep onset 2-4 am and wake 10 am-2 pm, driven by delayed melatonin release; (2) Insomnia - difficulty initiating/maintaining sleep. Contributing factors: screen time, caffeine, irregular schedules, mental health comorbidity. Obstructive sleep apnoea (OSA) is less common but important (obesity, craniofacial). Management: sleep hygiene, chronotherapy, melatonin (DSPS), CBT-I (insomnia), CPAP (OSA).

## Core Clinical Content

### Sleep need
8-10 hrs/night (teens); mean sleep 7-7.5 hrs (under-slept)

### Delayed sleep phase syndrome
Circadian shift; sleep onset 2-4 am; common in adolescents

### DSPS mechanism
Delayed melatonin release; entrainment to light/dark cycle

### Insomnia
Difficulty initiating/maintaining sleep; 10-30% adolescents

### Contributing factors
Screen time (blue light), caffeine, irregular schedule, anxiety/depression

### Obstructive sleep apnoea
Snoring, daytime sleepiness, obesity, adenotonsillar hypertrophy

### Health impact
Mood, cognition, academic performance, metabolic, immune

### Sleep hygiene
Regular schedule; no screens 1 hr pre-bed; dark/cool room; avoid caffeine

### Treatment DSPS
Chronotherapy, morning bright light, melatonin 0.5-3 mg 4-6 hr pre-bed

### Treatment insomnia
CBT-I first-line; sleep restriction; stimulus control

- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
