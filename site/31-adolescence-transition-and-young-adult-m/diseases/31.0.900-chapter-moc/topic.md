## 5. Key Themes for FCPS/MRCP

- **HEEADSSS assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (+ Eating, Sleep, Screen)
- **Gillick competence / Fraser guidelines** - under-16 consent and confidentiality
- **Tanner staging** - B1-B5 (breast), G1-G5 (genital), PH1-PH5 (pubic hair); testicular volume >=4 mL = first sign in boys
- **Transition of care** - Ready Steady Go, STARx, NICE NG43, Got Transition 6 core elements
- **Eating disorders** - anorexia (F:M 10:1), bulimia; FBT/Maudsley first-line for adolescents
- **Substance use** - CRAFFT screening, motivational interviewing, harm reduction
- **Mental health** - depression, self-harm/suicide, anxiety, OCD, PTSD, bipolar; SSRI fluoxetine first-line; FDA black-box warning
- **LARC contraception** - IUS, IUD, implant first-line for adolescents
- **STIs** - chlamydia commonest, annual screening <25, doxycycline first-line
- **Safeguarding** - under-13 sexual activity mandates referral; multi-agency
- **CFS/ME** - PEM hallmark, multi-component management, multidisciplinary team
- **Testicular torsion** - 6-hr salvage window, surgical emergency
- **ADHD** - 5% adolescents; methylphenidate first-line; comorbidity
- **FGM** - mandatory reporting <18; deinfibulation; safeguarding
- **DSD** - multidisciplinary team; sex assignment deferred where possible
- **Tic disorders / Tourette** - 1% prevalence; CBIT; clonidine, risperidone for severe
- **Conduct disorder** - 3-5%; parent management training; comorbidity with ADHD

## 6. MCQs (Chapter-Specific)

### MCQ 1
**Q: A 14-year-old girl presents with primary amenorrhea. On examination, breast development is at Tanner stage 5 but there is no pubic or axillary hair. What is the most likely diagnosis?**
A) Turner syndrome
B) Androgen insensitivity syndrome
C) Constitutional delay of puberty
D) Mayer-Rokitansky-Küster-Hauser syndrome
E) Hypothalamic amenorrhea

**Answer: B — Androgen insensitivity syndrome**
Normal breast development reflects peripheral conversion of androgens to oestrogens. Absent pubic/axillary hair indicates no androgen action. 46,XY karyotype with female phenotype.

### MCQ 2
**Q: A 15-year-old boy presents with sudden severe testicular pain. On examination, the right testicle is high-riding, swollen, and tender. The cremasteric reflex is absent. What is the most appropriate next step?**
A) Urinalysis
B) Doppler ultrasound of scrotum
C) Immediate surgical exploration
D) Empirical antibiotics
E) Reassurance and review in 24 hours

**Answer: C — Immediate surgical exploration**
Testicular torsion has a 6-hour salvage window. Diagnosis is clinical. Do NOT delay surgery for imaging if clinical suspicion is high.

### MCQ 3
**Q: Regarding Gillick competence in the UK, which of the following is TRUE?**
A) A child under 16 can never consent to medical treatment
B) Parents must always be informed before treatment
C) A child under 16 can consent if they have sufficient understanding and intelligence to fully comprehend
D) Only children aged 14+ can be Gillick competent
E) Fraser guidelines apply only to contraceptive advice

**Answer: C**
The Gillick competence test (1985) established that children under 16 can consent if they can understand what is involved. Fraser guidelines specifically cover contraceptive advice.

### MCQ 4
**Q: Anorexia nervosa in adolescents. Which of the following is the first-line psychological treatment?**
A) Cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT)
B) Family-based treatment (FBT/Maudsley)
C) Interpersonal therapy (IPT)
D) Dialectical behaviour therapy (DBT)
E) Psychodynamic psychotherapy

**Answer: B — Family-based treatment (FBT/Maudsley)**
FBT is first-line for adolescents with anorexia nervosa. Parents are empowered to support weight restoration. CBT is also effective but FBT is preferred in younger patients.

### MCQ 5
**Q: A 16-year-old with first-episode psychosis. Which medication is approved in this age group?**
A) Olanzapine
B) Risperidone
C) Aripiprazole
D) Quetiapine
E) Haloperidol

**Answer: C — Aripiprazole**
Aripiprazole is FDA-approved for schizophrenia in adolescents aged 13-17. Risperidone is also approved but has more metabolic side effects. Monitor for metabolic syndrome.

### SBAs

**SBA 1:** A 13-year-old girl with BMI 14.5 kg/m² presents with 6-month history of food restriction, excessive exercise, and body image distortion. HR 38, BP 80/50. Admission is planned. Which is the MOST critical first 72-hour intervention?
A) Establish nasogastric feeding
B) Restore nutrition cautiously to avoid refeeding syndrome (start at 5-10 kcal/kg/day, supplement thiamine, monitor electrolytes)
C) Start SSRI for comorbid depression
D) Begin family therapy immediately
E) Discharge with intensive outpatient support

**Answer: B** — Refeeding syndrome can be fatal; start low, replace electrolytes (especially phosphate), supplement thiamine before glucose, monitor cardiac rhythm.

**SBA 2:** A 17-year-old wants contraception but refuses to involve parents. She is in a stable relationship. Which is the most appropriate action?
A) Refuse to prescribe contraception
B) Contact parents despite her wishes
C) Prescribe contraception under Fraser guidelines (presume competence)
D) Advise abstinence
E) Refer to social services

**Answer: C** — Fraser guidelines allow competent under-16s to receive contraceptive advice/treatment without parental involvement if it is in their best interest.

**SBA 3:** A 14-year-old refugee from Sudan presents with infibulation. She is married. Which is the BEST management?
A) Reversal of infibulation (deinfibulation) before any other intervention
B) Mandatory safeguarding referral and deinfibulation as soon as practicable
C) Report to police and detain
D) Wait until she is 16
E) Discharge with safety planning

**Answer: B** — FGM in under-18s mandates multi-agency safeguarding referral. Deinfibulation is needed for micturition, menstruation, and any future vaginal delivery.

## 7. Flashcards (Anki-ready)

1. **First sign of puberty in boys**: Testicular enlargement ≥4 mL (Tanner G2)
2. **First sign of puberty in girls**: Breast bud (Tanner B2), typically age 10-11
3. **HEEADSSS assessment**: Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (+Eating, Sleep, Screen)
4. **Gillick competence**: Under-16 can consent if they have sufficient understanding
5. **Fraser guidelines**: Specifically for contraceptive advice in under-16s
6. **Eating disorder F:M ratio**: Anorexia 10:1, bulimia ~3:1
7. **FBT/Maudsley**: Family-based treatment — first-line for adolescent anorexia
8. **Testicular torsion**: 6-hour salvage window, surgical emergency
9. **FGM mandatory reporting**: All under-18 cases in UK
10. **LARC first-line in adolescents**: IUS, IUD, implant
11. **Annual STI screening**: All sexually active under-25s in UK
12. **Commonest STI**: Chlamydia trachomatis
13. **Adolescent depression first-line SSRI**: Fluoxetine
14. **CFS/ME hallmark**: Post-exertional malaise
15. **Ready Steady Go / STARx**: Transition readiness tools
