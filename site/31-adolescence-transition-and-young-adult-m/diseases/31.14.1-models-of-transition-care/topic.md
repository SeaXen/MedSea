# Models of transition care

---

## Definition

Several transition models exist, ranging from simple transfer to joint clinics to dedicated transition services. Evidence favours structured, multi-component interventions with both paediatric and adult team involvement. Joint clinics (paediatric and adult teams co-consult) reduce disengagement. Transition coordinators / navigators improve attendance and self-management. Disease-specific transition programmes (e.g., Cystic Fibrosis, Congenital Heart Disease, Diabetes, JIA) are most developed. Universal transition principles apply to all long-term conditions; service design must be flexible for less common conditions.

## Core Clinical Content

### Joint clinics
Paediatric + adult team co-consult; reduces disengagement; familiar faces

### Transition coordinator
Named professional; supports navigation, planning, follow-up

### Disease-specific programmes
CF, CHD, diabetes, JIA, IBD - most developed

### Young adult clinics
Adult setting, but with developmental flexibility (e.g., 16-25 yrs)

### Ready Steady Go
10-step transition programme widely used in UK paediatric services

### Hello/Hands-off
Adult team gradually takes over from paediatric

### Telephone/virtual follow-up
Improves engagement with hard-to-reach young adults

### Peer support
Peer mentors, social media support groups, condition-specific charities

### Mental health integration
Embedded CAMHS or adult mental health during transition

### Evaluation
PDSA cycles; outcome measures: attendance, disease control, satisfaction

**Q5. What is the first-line management of Models of transition care?**
> First-line management: Peer support - Peer mentors, social media support groups, condition-specific charities; Mental health integration - Embedded CAMHS or adult mental health during transition. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Models of transition care?**
> Red flags and complications: Evaluation - PDSA cycles; outcome measures: attendance, disease control, satisfaction. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Models of transition care** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
