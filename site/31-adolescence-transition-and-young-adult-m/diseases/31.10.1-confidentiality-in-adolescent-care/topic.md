# Confidentiality in adolescent care

---

## Definition

Confidentiality is the foundation of adolescent healthcare: 60-80% of adolescents would not disclose sensitive information (sex, drugs, mental health) without confidentiality assurance. The duty of confidentiality is not absolute: it can be broken to protect the young person (suicide risk, abuse) or others (serious harm to identifiable third party). GMC, BMA and Royal College of Paediatrics guidance confirm best practice. Confidentiality must be explained at the first contact, in language the adolescent understands, and the limits clearly stated.

## Core Clinical Content

### Disclosure rates
Up to 80% will not disclose sensitive issues if confidentiality not assured

### Core principle
Adolescent interests best served by confidentiality (with limits explained)

### Absolute vs relative
Not absolute: disclose if serious harm to young person or others

### GMC guidance
Disclose if necessary to prevent serious harm; consult colleague first

### Sexual activity
Under-13 sexual activity must be referred (safeguarding); 13-15 assessed case-by-case

### Drug/alcohol use
Generally confidential; address acute medical issues first

### Mental health/suicide
Confidential unless active suicidal intent with plan - then act to protect

### Parents
Generally encouraged to involve parents, but adolescent may refuse if competent

### Records
Confidential, with redactions in summaries; IT access controls

### EHR for adolescents
Confidential flags, password protection, no automatic parental proxy access

**Q5. What is the first-line management of Confidentiality in adolescent care?**
> First-line management: Parents - Generally encouraged to involve parents, but adolescent may refuse if competent; Records - Confidential, with redactions in summaries; IT access controls. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Confidentiality in adolescent care?**
> Red flags and complications: EHR for adolescents - Confidential flags, password protection, no automatic parental proxy access. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Confidentiality in adolescent care** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
