# Depression in adolescents

---

## Definition

Adolescent depression is a mood disorder with prevalence ~5% in mid-adolescence, rising with age, and a major risk factor for suicide. Presentation differs from adults - irritability is more common than sadness, somatisation, behavioural problems, school decline, social withdrawal. Diagnosis uses DSM-5 criteria adapted for adolescents (irritability counts; weight changes may be growth-failure). Screening (PHQ-A, MFQ) is recommended in primary care. Treatment is multimodal: psychoeducation, family therapy, CBT or interpersonal therapy for mild-moderate; SSRI (fluoxetine first-line) for moderate-severe. Antidepressants carry an FDA black-box warning for suicidality - monitor closely.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
2-8% of adolescents; F > M after puberty

### Presentation
Irritability more than sadness; somatisation, boredom, school decline, substance use

### DSM-5 criteria
>=5 of 9 symptoms for >=2 weeks; must include low mood or anhedonia

### Duration
Symptoms most of day, nearly every day, >=2 weeks

### Screening tools
PHQ-A, MFQ (Mood and Feelings Questionnaire) - high sensitivity

### Risk factors
Family history, prior episodes, ACEs, chronic illness, bullying, gender identity issues

### First-line therapy
CBT or interpersonal therapy (IPT) - 6-12 sessions; group format effective

### SSRI
Fluoxetine first-line (FDA-approved >=8 yrs); sertraline, citalopram alternatives

### FDA black box
Suicidality increased in first weeks; weekly monitoring for 4 weeks

### TCAs
Avoid in adolescents - cardiotoxicity in overdose

- **Mnemonic for Depression in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
