# Sexually transmitted infections in adolescents

---

## Definition

Adolescents (15-24 yrs) have the highest rates of STIs in the UK and globally, accounting for ~50% of new infections. Biological vulnerability (cervical ectopy, immune naivety), behavioural factors (multiple partners, inconsistent condom use, substance use), and barriers to testing all contribute. Common STIs: chlamydia, gonorrhoea, HPV, HSV, syphilis (rising), HIV. Screening: chlamydia/gonorrhoea NAAT (urine/vulvovaginal swab), HIV, syphilis serology. Treatment: single-dose antibiotics for chlamydia (azithromycin 1g) or 7-day doxycycline; partner notification; vaccination (HPV, Hep B, HAV).

## Core Clinical Content

### STI rates
50% of new STIs occur in 15-24 yr-olds

### Common STIs
Chlamydia (most common), gonorrhoea, HPV, HSV, syphilis, HIV, trichomonas

### Asymptomatic chlamydia
Up to 70% in females; 50% in males

### Complications
PID, ectopic pregnancy, infertility, chronic pelvic pain, epididymo-orchitis

### Screening
Annual chlamydia NAAT (urine/vulvovaginal) in <25 sexually active

### Chlamydia treatment
Doxycycline 100 mg BD 7 days (or azithromycin 1 g stat)

### Gonorrhoea treatment
Ceftriaxone IM stat + azithromycin 1 g (dual)

### Partner notification
Trace contacts in last 3-6 months; expedited partner therapy in some areas

### HPV vaccine
School-based programme; 9-valent; 12-13 yr-olds; effective against cervical cancer

### HIV PrEP
Pre-exposure prophylaxis for high-risk MSM/trans; tenofovir/emtricitabine

**Q2. What are the most important FCPS/MRCP high-yield points for Sexually transmitted infections in adolescents?**
> STI rates: 50% of new STIs occur in 15-24 yr-olds. Common STIs: Chlamydia (most common), gonorrhoea, HPV, HSV, syphilis, HIV, trichomonas. Asymptomatic chlamydia: Up to 70% in females; 50% in males. See FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table below.

**Q3. How is Sexually transmitted infections in adolescents recognised clinically?**
> Presentation and key clinical features include: Complications - PID, ectopic pregnancy, infertility, chronic pelvic pain, epididymo-orchitis; Screening - Annual chlamydia NAAT (urine/vulvovaginal) in <25 sexually active. See Core Clinical Content for full details.

**Q4. What investigations are required for Sexually transmitted infections in adolescents?**
> Investigations include: Chlamydia treatment - Doxycycline 100 mg BD 7 days (or azithromycin 1 g stat); Gonorrhoea treatment - Ceftriaxone IM stat + azithromycin 1 g (dual). See Core Clinical Content for full details.

**Q5. What is the first-line management of Sexually transmitted infections in adolescents?**
> First-line management: Partner notification - Trace contacts in last 3-6 months; expedited partner therapy in some areas; HPV vaccine - School-based programme; 9-valent; 12-13 yr-olds; effective against cervical cancer. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Sexually transmitted infections in adolescents?**
> Red flags and complications: HIV PrEP - Pre-exposure prophylaxis for high-risk MSM/trans; tenofovir/emtricitabine. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Sexually transmitted infections in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
