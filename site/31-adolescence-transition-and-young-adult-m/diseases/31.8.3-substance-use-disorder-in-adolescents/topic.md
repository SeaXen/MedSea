# Substance use disorder in adolescents

---

## Definition

Substance use disorder (SUD) in adolescents is defined as a problematic pattern of substance use leading to clinically significant impairment or distress, with DSM-5 criteria (>=2 in 12 months: tolerance, withdrawal, larger amounts, unsuccessful cut-down, time spent, craving, failure of role obligations, social problems, dangerous use, continued despite problems). Prevalence 5-10% of adolescents. Brain changes from adolescent use are long-lasting. Comorbidity with mental illness, ADHD, trauma is the rule. Management: comprehensive assessment, family involvement, motivational enhancement therapy, CBT, contingency management, residential rehabilitation for severe cases.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 SUD criteria
>=2 of 11 in 12 months; 2-3 mild, 4-5 moderate, >=6 severe

### Tolerance/withdrawal
Adapted for adolescents - may develop quickly with nicotine, alcohol

### Prevalence
5-10% of adolescents meet criteria for SUD

### Comorbidity
ADHD, depression, anxiety, conduct disorder, trauma in >50%

### ASSIST/WHO screening
Alcohol, Smoking, Substance Involvement Screening Test

### First-line therapy
Motivational Enhancement Therapy (MET) + CBT

### Family therapy
Multidimensional Family Therapy (MDFT); Brief Strategic Family Therapy (BSFT)

### Contingency management
Vouchers/rewards for abstinence - high-quality evidence

### Residential rehab
For severe SUD with failed outpatient treatment; 3-12 month programmes

### Outcomes
Better with earlier intervention, family engagement, comorbid disorder treatment

**Q3. How is Substance use disorder in adolescents recognised clinically?**

**Q4. What investigations are required for Substance use disorder in adolescents?**

**Q5. What is the first-line management of Substance use disorder in adolescents?**
> First-line management: Contingency management - Vouchers/rewards for abstinence - high-quality evidence; Residential rehab - For severe SUD with failed outpatient treatment; 3-12 month programmes. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Substance use disorder in adolescents?**
> Red flags and complications: Outcomes - Better with earlier intervention, family engagement, comorbid disorder treatment. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Substance use disorder in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
