# Normal puberty and pubertal staging

---

## Definition

Normal puberty is the orderly, timed sequence of sexual maturation driven by reactivation of the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal (HPG) axis, beginning with adrenarche (adrenal androgens, ~6-8 yrs) followed by gonadarche (HPG axis activation). Tanner/Marshall staging documents secondary sexual characteristics across 5 stages: breast (B1-B5) and pubic hair (PH1-PH5) in girls; genital (G1-G5), pubic hair (PH1-PH5) and testicular volume (Prader beads) in boys. The first signs are thelarche in girls (~10-11 yrs) and testicular enlargement in boys (~11-12 yrs). Menarche typically follows thelarche by 2-3 years.

## Core Clinical Content

### Tanner stages
5 stages; B1 prepubertal, B5 adult; G1 prepubertal, G5 adult

### Thelarche
First sign in girls, ~10-11 yrs (range 8-13); breast bud (B2)

### Menarche
Average 12-13 yrs; follows thelarche by 2-3 years; Tanner B4

### Testicular volume
>=4 mL (Prader) = first sign in boys; orchidometer measurement

### Pubic hair
PH2 typically 11-12 in girls, 12-13 in boys; adrenarche

### Adrenarche
DHEA, androstenedione from adrenal zona reticularis; 6-8 yrs

### Gonadarche
HPG axis reactivation; pulsatile GnRH -> LH/FSH -> sex steroids

### Peak height velocity
Girls 11-12 yrs (Tanner B3); boys 13-14 yrs (Tanner G4)

### Bone age
X-ray left wrist; advanced in precocious, delayed in constitutional delay

### Sequence (girls)
Thelarche -> pubarche -> growth spurt -> menarche

**Q2. What are the most important FCPS/MRCP high-yield points for Normal puberty and pubertal staging?**
> Tanner stages: 5 stages; B1 prepubertal, B5 adult; G1 prepubertal, G5 adult. Thelarche: First sign in girls, ~10-11 yrs (range 8-13); breast bud (B2). Menarche: Average 12-13 yrs; follows thelarche by 2-3 years; Tanner B4. See FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table below.

**Q3. How is Normal puberty and pubertal staging recognised clinically?**
> Presentation and key clinical features include: Testicular volume - >=4 mL (Prader) = first sign in boys; orchidometer measurement; Pubic hair - PH2 typically 11-12 in girls, 12-13 in boys; adrenarche. See Core Clinical Content for full details.

**Q4. What investigations are required for Normal puberty and pubertal staging?**
> Investigations include: Adrenarche - DHEA, androstenedione from adrenal zona reticularis; 6-8 yrs; Gonadarche - HPG axis reactivation; pulsatile GnRH -> LH/FSH -> sex steroids. See Core Clinical Content for full details.

**Q5. What is the first-line management of Normal puberty and pubertal staging?**
> First-line management: Peak height velocity - Girls 11-12 yrs (Tanner B3); boys 13-14 yrs (Tanner G4); Bone age - X-ray left wrist; advanced in precocious, delayed in constitutional delay. Refer to specialist adolescent services for definitive management where indicated.

**Q6. What are the key complications or red flags of Normal puberty and pubertal staging?**
> Red flags and complications: Sequence (girls) - Thelarche -> pubarche -> growth spurt -> menarche. Always consider safeguarding, mental health comorbidity, and transition planning in adolescent presentations.

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Normal puberty and pubertal staging** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
