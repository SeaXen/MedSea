# Chronic disease in adolescents

---

## Definition

Adolescents with chronic disease (asthma, diabetes, epilepsy, cystic fibrosis, congenital heart disease, IBD, JIA, CKD, sickle cell, cancer survivors) face unique challenges: growth/puberty, school absence, peer relationships, body image, transition to adult care, adherence, mental health comorbidity. ~15% of adolescents have a chronic condition. Management is multidisciplinary (paediatric, primary care, mental health, social work, education). Self-management skills should be progressively developed. Ready Steady Go and HEADSS for chronic disease frameworks support structured assessment. Successful transition to adult care is the long-term goal.

### Mental health comorbidity
2-3x risk of depression, anxiety, particularly in poorly controlled disease

### Self-management
Gradual transfer of responsibility from parent to adolescent (Ready Steady Go)

Major challenge; see Adherence topic

### Sexual health

### Transition planning
Starts ~12-14 yrs; structured handover to adult services (see Transition of Care)

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Chronic disease in adolescents** - see the FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary table above for the 10 high-yield facts
- **'HEEADSSS' assessment** - Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression, Safety (3.0: + Eating, Sleep, Screen) - always use in adolescent history
- **'Gillick competence'** - under-16 with sufficient understanding to make own medical decisions
- **'Fraser guidelines'** - specific criteria for contraceptive/sexual health advice without parental knowledge
- **'HEADSS' / 'SSHADESS'** - alternative psychosocial assessment frameworks (Strengths-based)
