# Back pain and scoliosis in adolescents

---

## Definition

Back pain affects 20-30% of adolescents; most is mechanical/postural, but serious causes (infection, tumour, scoliosis, spondylolysis, spondyloarthropathy) must be excluded. Red flags: night pain, systemic symptoms, neurological deficit, age <10, weight loss. Idiopathic scoliosis (most common, 2-3% of adolescents, F:M 4:1) presents in adolescence; Cobb angle measurement on standing X-ray guides management (observation <25 degrees, bracing 25-45 degrees, surgery >45-50 degrees). Spondylolysis (pars interarticularis defect) is common in hyperextension athletes (gymnasts, fast bowlers).
