# Vulvar disorders in adolescents

---

## Definition

Vulvar disorders in adolescents include vulvovaginitis (most common), labial adhesions, lichen sclerosus, vulvodynia, Bartholin's cyst/abscess, and (rarely) malignancy. Vulvovaginitis is usually non-specific (bacterial) in young adolescents and STI-related (chlamydia, gonorrhoea, trichomonas) in sexually active adolescents. Presentation: discharge, pruritus, dysuria, soreness, bleeding. Lichen sclerosus presents with porcelain-white atrophic plaques, often with itching and soreness. Labial adhesions are common in young children, less so in adolescents (consider sexual abuse if severe or sudden). Management: hygiene advice, treat specific infection, topical oestrogen for labial adhesions, potent topical steroid for lichen sclerosus.

## Core Clinical Content

### Vulvovaginitis causes
Non-specific (prepubertal), Candida (post-pubertal), bacterial, STI

### STI-related organisms
Chlamydia, gonorrhoea, trichomonas, HSV, HPV

### Non-specific vaginitis
Commonest in young adolescents; mixed flora; hygiene-related

### Candida vulvovaginitis
Itching, thick white discharge; pH <4.5; treat with clotrimazole/fluconazole

### Bacterial vaginosis
Thin fishy-smelling discharge; pH >4.5; clue cells; Gardnerella

### Lichen sclerosus
White atrophic plaques; itching, soreness; risk of malignancy; clobetasol

### Labial adhesions
Prepubertal common; usually asymptomatic; topical oestrogen if needed

### Bartholin's cyst
Blocked duct; presents as swelling; may become abscess; word catheter if recurrent

### Vulvodynia
Chronic vulvar pain without cause; multidisciplinary management

### Red flags
Persistent bleeding, mass, suspicion of abuse - refer gynaecology
