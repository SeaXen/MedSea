# Post-obstructive pneumonia

> [!important]
> **Post-obstructive pneumonia** is recurrent or non-resolving pneumonia distal to a **bronchial obstruction**, classically caused by **lung cancer** (especially squamous). It is a **paraneoplastic clue** in smokers. Always exclude endobronchial lesion in non-resolving/recurrent pneumonia, particularly in smokers.

Related: [[Pneumonia]], [[Lung Cancer]], [[Aspiration pneumonia]], [[ABG Interpretation]]

> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP pearl**: **Recurrent pneumonia in the same lobe** = obstruction until proven otherwise → **bronchoscopy**. Most common cause: **lung cancer** (squamous most likely, also small cell). Other causes: foreign body (especially children), post-TB stricture, mucoid impaction, extrinsic compression.

## 1. Definition

**Post-obstructive pneumonia** = pneumonia occurring **distal to a partial or complete airway obstruction**, leading to impaired drainage, mucus stasis, and recurrent or non-resolving infection.

## 2. Aetiology

### Endobronchial obstruction
| Cause | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| **Lung cancer** | Squamous (most common), small cell, others |
| **Carcinoid tumour** | Young, non-smokers |
| **Benign tumour** | Papilloma, lipoma, hamartoma |
| **Foreign body** | Children, elderly, neurological disease |
| **Mucus plug** | Asthma, ABPA, post-op |
| **Blood clot** | Trauma, bronchiectasis |
| **TB stricture** | Post-TB |
| **Post-intubation stenosis** | Tracheal stenosis |

### Extrinsic compression
- Lymphoma
- Lymph nodes (sarcoid, TB, malignancy)
- Goitre
- Aortic aneurysm
- Mediastinal mass

### Functional
- **Bronchomalacia**
- **Dynamic airway collapse**
- **Vocal cord dysfunction**

## 3. Pathophysiology

### Sequence
1. **Airway obstruction** (partial or complete)
2. **↓Mucociliary clearance**
3. **Mucus stasis** + bacterial colonisation
4. **Distal atelectasis**
5. **Post-obstructive pneumonia**
6. **Abscess formation** (incomplete drainage)
7. **Recurrent infections**

## 4. Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- **Recurrent pneumonia in same lobe** (key clue)
- **Non-resolving pneumonia** (>6 weeks despite antibiotics)
- **Cough** (productive, may be purulent)
- **Haemoptysis** (especially with malignancy)
- **Fever, dyspnoea, weight loss**
- **Recurrent infection** despite appropriate Rx

### Signs
- **Fever, tachypnoea, tachycardia**
- **Consolidation signs** (crackles, dullness)
- **Focal wheeze** (fixed, monophonic — suggests obstruction)
- **↓Breath sounds** (collapse)
- **Clubbing** (malignancy, chronic)
- **Cachexia** (malignancy)

## 5. Diagnosis

### Investigations
| Test | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **CXR** | Pneumonia in same lobe, ± mass, atelectasis, hilar enlargement |
| **CT chest** | Mass, obstruction, distal consolidation, lymph nodes |
| **Bronchoscopy** | **Gold standard** — visualise obstruction, biopsy, brushings, BAL |
| **Sputum** | Cytology (malignant cells), AFB, culture |
| **CT-guided biopsy** | For peripheral lesions |
| **EBUS** | Mediastinal staging |

### Key features suggesting obstruction
- **Persistent** or **recurrent** consolidation in same lobe
- **Volume loss** (atelectasis) distal
- **Focal wheeze** not clearing with treatment
- **Haemoptysis**
- **Weight loss**, smoking history

## 6. Management

### Treat pneumonia (initial)
- **Empirical antibiotics** (cover typical +/− anaerobes)
- **Bronchoscopy** for clearance (remove secretions/foreign body)
- **Positioning** (gravity drainage)

### Definitive treatment
- **Treat underlying cause**:
  - **Lung cancer**: surgery (if resectable), chemo, radiotherapy, immunotherapy, targeted therapy
  - **Foreign body**: bronchoscopic removal
  - **Stricture**: dilatation, stent
  - **Mucus plug**: bronchoscopic removal, mucolytics

### Specific to lung cancer
- Staging (TNM 8th)
- **MDT discussion**
- Resection if operable (early stage)
- Chemo/radiotherapy if inoperable
- Palliative care if advanced

## 7. Red flags for lung cancer
- Smoker >40 with new cough, haemoptysis, weight loss
- Recurrent pneumonia same lobe
- Non-resolving pneumonia (>6 weeks)
- New lobar collapse
- Pleural effusion (malignant)
- Lymphadenopathy
- **Smoking + obstructive features + any of above = high suspicion**

## 8. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Domain | Key points |
|--------|------------|
| **Definition** | Pneumonia distal to obstruction |
| **Most common cause** | Lung cancer (squamous) |
| **Key clue** | Recurrent pneumonia in same lobe |
| **Investigation** | CT chest + bronchoscopy |

## 9. MCQs (10)

1. Most common cause of post-obstructive pneumonia in adults:
   A. Foreign body
   B. **Lung cancer (squamous)**
   C. Mucus plug
   D. Stricture
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Lung cancer.

2. Recurrent pneumonia in the same lobe should prompt:
   A. Antibiotics
   B. **Bronchoscopy to exclude obstruction (e.g. lung cancer)**
   C. Physiotherapy
   D. Steroids
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Bronchoscopy.

3. The most common lung cancer type causing post-obstructive pneumonia:
   A. Adenocarcinoma
   B. **Squamous cell carcinoma (central, endobronchial)**
   C. Small cell
   D. Large cell
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Squamous.

4. The best initial investigation for suspected post-obstructive pneumonia:
   A. CXR
   B. **CT chest + bronchoscopy**
   C. Spirogram
   D. ABG
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — CT + bronchoscopy.

5. Non-resolving pneumonia despite appropriate antibiotics after how many weeks should prompt workup for obstruction:
   A. 1
   B. 3
   C. **6**
   D. 12
   E. None
   **Answer: C** — 6 weeks.

6. A 65-year-old smoker with right middle lobe consolidation that doesn't clear. Most likely:
   A. Pneumonia only
   B. **Endobronchial obstruction (lung cancer)**
   C. TB
   D. Asthma
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Endobronchial lesion.

7. A child with sudden wheeze + post-obstructive pneumonia. Most likely:
   A. Asthma
   B. **Foreign body aspiration**
   C. Lung cancer
   D. TB
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Foreign body.

8. The mechanism of post-obstructive pneumonia is:
   A. Direct infection
   B. **Impaired mucus clearance + stasis + distal atelectasis**
   C. Allergy
   D. None
   E. Unknown
   **Answer: B** — Mucus stasis.

9. Most useful bronchoscopy in post-obstructive pneumonia:
   A. Only diagnostic
   B. **Both diagnostic (biopsy) and therapeutic (clearance, stent)**
   C. Only therapeutic
   D. None
   E. Diagnostic only
   **Answer: B** — Both.

10. A 70-year-old with post-obstructive pneumonia from squamous cancer, resectable. Best:
    A. Palliative
    B. **Surgical resection (lobectomy)**
    C. Chemo only
    D. Wait
    E. None
    **Answer: B** — Surgery.

## 10. SBA Questions (10)

1. Squamous cell carcinoma location is typically:
   A. Peripheral
   B. **Central (endobronchial)**
   C. Diffuse
   D. Apical
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Central.

2. A patient with non-resolving pneumonia 8 weeks after antibiotics. Next:
   A. Repeat antibiotics
   B. **CT chest +/− bronchoscopy**
   C. Wait
   D. Discharge
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Workup.

3. The most common cause of post-obstructive pneumonia in a child is:
   A. Malignancy
   B. **Foreign body**
   C. Mucus
   D. Stricture
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Foreign body.

4. Bronchoscopy in post-obstructive pneumonia serves as:
   A. Diagnostic only
   B. **Both diagnostic and therapeutic**
   C. Not useful
   D. Surgery
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Both.

5. Squamous cell carcinoma is associated with:
   A. **Smoking (strongest)**
   B. Never smoking
   C. Asbestos
   D. None
   E. Allergy
   **Answer: A** — Smoking.

6. The first symptom of post-obstructive pneumonia in lung cancer is often:
   A. Haemoptysis
   B. **Recurrent infection in same lobe**
   C. Weight loss
   D. None
   E. Pain
   **Answer: B** — Recurrent infection.

7. Carcinoid tumour is typically:
   A. **Young, non-smoker, central, endobronchial**
   B. Smoker
   C. Old
   D. None
   E. Peripheral
   **Answer: A** — Young non-smoker.

8. The gold standard for diagnosis of post-obstructive pneumonia due to lung cancer is:
   A. CXR
   B. **Bronchoscopy with biopsy**
   C. Sputum
   D. CT
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — Bronchoscopy with biopsy.

9. The most appropriate first-line investigation for recurrent pneumonia in a smoker is:
   A. Spirogram
   B. **CT chest + bronchoscopy**
   C. Sputum
   D. ABG
   E. None
   **Answer: B** — CT + bronchoscopy.

10. Squamous cell carcinoma of the lung is associated with:
    A. EGFR mutation
    B. **Smoking (cavitating, central)**

    E. Asbestos only
    **Answer: B** — Smoking.

## 13. Summary

Post-obstructive pneumonia = pneumonia distal to airway obstruction. Most common cause: lung cancer (squamous). Recurrent pneumonia in same lobe = bronchoscopy. Treat cause + antibiotics. Foreign body in children. Non-resolving >6 weeks → workup.
