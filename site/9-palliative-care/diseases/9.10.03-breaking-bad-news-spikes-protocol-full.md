# Breaking bad news (SPIKES protocol)

## Definition

Breaking bad news is a core clinical communication skill, especially in palliative care. The SPIKES protocol (Baile et al., 2000) provides a structured approach: S (Setting — private, quiet, no interruptions, sit, silence phone), P (Perception — 'what do you understand about your illness?'), I (Invitation — 'how much information would you like?'), K (Knowledge — give information in small chunks, no jargon, check understanding), E (Emotions — respond with empathy: name emotion, validate, support), S (Strategy/Summary — plan next steps, summarise). Other models: BREAKS (Background, Rapport, Explore, Announce, Kindle, Summarise). Important: avoid jargon, allow silence, respond to emotions with empathy, do not give false reassurance, respect denial as a coping mechanism, document the conversation.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
None.

### Management
SPIKES: S (Setting — private, quiet, sitting, no interruptions, silence phone), P (Perception — 'what do you understand about your illness?'), I (Invitation — 'how much information would you like?'), K (Knowledge — small chunks, no jargon, check understanding), E (Emotions — empathy: name, validate, support), S (Strategy — plan, summary, follow-up). Other models: BREAKS (Background, Rapport, Explore, Announce, Kindle, Summarise). Avoid jargon, false reassurance, taking away hope. Respect denial as coping. Document conversation, who present, what said, plan. Family involvement per patient wishes.
