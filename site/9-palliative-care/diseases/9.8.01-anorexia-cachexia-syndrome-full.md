# Anorexia-cachexia syndrome

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care**

## Definition

Cancer cachexia is a multifactorial syndrome of involuntary weight loss, muscle wasting, anorexia, and metabolic disturbance in advanced illness (cancer, heart failure, COPD, AIDS). Pathogenesis: pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6), tumour-derived factors, hormonal changes. Treatment: identify and treat reversible causes, nutritional support (enteral if possible, parenteral only in select), appetite stimulants (corticosteroids — short-term, dexamethasone; megestrol acetate; mirtazapine), exercise, treat depression. Prognosis: poor, often irreversible in late stages.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Anorexia-cachexia syndrome is a complex metabolic syndrome in advanced illness with weight loss, muscle wasting, anorexia, fatigue, weakness, decreased QoL. Mechanism: inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6), catabolic state, decreased intake. Affects 50-80% of advanced cancer, end-stage COPD/CHF/HIV. May be primary (cachexia of disease) or secondary (inadequate intake — depression, dysphagia, nausea, dysgeusia, drugs). Reversible causes should be sought and treated.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Weight, BMI, percentage weight loss, mid-arm circumference, handgrip strength. Bloods: albumin, prealbumin, CRP, FBC, LFT, renal, glucose, TFT. Dietitian assessment. CT/MRI for muscle mass (if precise).

### Management
Identify and treat reversible (depression, nausea, oral thrush, constipation, dysgeusia, drugs). Appetite stimulants: megestrol acetate 160-800mg/day (corticosteroid effect), mirtazapine 15-30mg nocte (antidepressant, weight gain), corticosteroids (dexamethasone 2-4mg/day, prednisolone 15-30mg/day — short-term, 2-4 weeks). Nutritional support: dietitian, oral supplements, enteral (NG/PEG) if adequate function, parenteral (TPN — rarely appropriate, late-stage). Family education (refocus on quality, not food).

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Anorexia-cachexia mechanism? A: Inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6); metabolic; decreased intake.

2. 2. Q: First-line appetite stimulant? A: Megestrol acetate (corticosteroid progestogen effect, 160-800mg/day) or mirtazapine.

3. 3. Q: Corticosteroid for anorexia? A: Yes, dexamethasone 2-4mg/day or prednisolone 15-30mg/day, short-term (2-4 weeks); benefit fatigue, appetite, pain.

4. 4. Q: Reversible causes? A: Depression, nausea, dysphagia, oral candida, constipation, drugs, dysgeusia.

5. 5. Q: TPN in palliative care? A: Rarely; usually fails to improve outcomes in late-stage disease; may burden patient.
