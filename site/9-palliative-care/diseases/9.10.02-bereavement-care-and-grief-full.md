# Bereavement care and grief

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — End of Life Care

## Definition

Bereavement is the loss of a loved one through death. Grief is the natural response, with normal features (sadness, yearning, sleep/appetite changes, searching, numbness, guilt, anger, hallucinations of deceased). Stages/responses vary (Kübler-Ross: denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance; not all experience all stages). Complicated grief (prolonged grief disorder): persistent for >12 months in adults (>6 months in children), functional impairment. Risk factors for complicated grief: sudden death, traumatic death, child loss, suicide, prior mental illness, social isolation. Support: normal grief does not require formal intervention, validate, listen, memorial, support groups; complicated grief — psychological therapy (grief-focused CBT, complicated grief therapy), antidepressants if comorbid depression. Family support: pre-death (anticipatory grief), at death, post-death (sympathy card, follow-up, referral to bereavement services).

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Normal grief: no specific investigations. Complicated grief: assess severity, duration, impact.

### Management
Normal grief: validate, listen, normalise, support groups, memorial. Complicated grief: grief-focused CBT, complicated grief therapy, antidepressants if comorbid depression. Risk factors for complicated: sudden death, traumatic, child loss, suicide, prior mental illness, social isolation. Family support: pre-death (anticipatory grief), at death, post-death (sympathy, follow-up, bereavement services).

## Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Normal grief | Waves, can enjoy |
| Depression | Persistent anhedonia, suicidal ideation, biological features |
| Complicated grief | >12 months, functional impairment |
