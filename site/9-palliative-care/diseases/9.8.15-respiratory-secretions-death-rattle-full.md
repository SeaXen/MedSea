# Respiratory secretions (death rattle)

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care**

## Definition

Death rattle is noisy breathing from pooled respiratory secretions in the upper airways in dying patients (often in last 24-48h of life). Affects 12-92% of dying patients. Treatment: positional (side-lying), reassurance to family (sounds often worse than patient's experience), sublingual hyoscine hydrobromide (0.6mg q4-6h SC; or scopolamine patch 1mg/72h), glycopyrronium (0.2mg SC q6-8h), octreotide (subcut). Suctioning often ineffective and distressing. Prevention: avoid fluid overload in last days. Family distress is the main issue.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Death rattle is noisy breathing from pooled secretions in oropharynx and bronchi in dying patients (50% in last 3 days). Sounds distressing to family; patient usually unconscious. Mechanism: inability to clear secretions, muscle relaxation, accumulation. Once established, often difficult to clear.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Clinical diagnosis, distinguish from pulmonary oedema (RHF, fluid overload) or infection (purulent secretions, fever).

### Management
Prevention: adequate hydration (avoid excess IV fluids at end of life), positioning (lateral, semi-prone), suction (may not be effective if deep), family education. Pharmacological: anticholinergics to reduce secretion production: glycopyrronium 200-400mcg SC prn or 800-1200mcg/24h CSCI (first-line, no CNS), hyoscine butylbromide 20mg SC prn or 80-120mg/24h CSCI, hyoscine hydrobromide 0.4mg SC (crosses BBB, may sedate/confuse), atropine 1% eye drops SC/SL. Note: these reduce new secretions but don't clear existing.

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Best anticholinergic for death rattle? A: Glycopyrronium 200-400mcg SC (or CSCI 800-1200mcg/24h) — no CNS, peripheral only.

2. 2. Q: Why not hyoscine hydrobromide? A: Crosses BBB, can cause sedation/confusion.

3. 3. Q: Prevent with fluids? A: Avoid excess IV fluids in last days (worsens secretions).

4. 4. Q: Suction? A: May help if secretions accessible; often ineffective if deep; avoid repeated suction (distressing).

5. 5. Q: Family education? A: Sounds distressing but patient usually unconscious; reposition, gentle suction; medication reduces new secretions.
