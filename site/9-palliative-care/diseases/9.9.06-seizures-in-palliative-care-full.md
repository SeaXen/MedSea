# Seizures in palliative care

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Palliative Care Emergencies

## Definition

Seizures in palliative care patients can be due to primary brain tumour, brain metastases, metabolic disturbance (hypoglycaemia, hyponatraemia, hypocalcaemia, uraemia in renal failure), drug toxicity (tramadol, SSRIs, antipsychotics, ondansetron), stroke, hypoxia, infection, withdrawal (benzodiazepines, alcohol, antiepileptics). Management: acute (place on side, suction, oxygen, IV/SC midazolam 10mg or diazepam 10mg PR/IV, second-line phenobarbital or phenytoin loading), treat underlying cause, continuous midazolam infusion for status, maintenance antiepileptics (no loading required for therapeutic level). Prognosis: depends on cause; often terminal event in advanced cancer.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Seizures in palliative care can be due to primary brain tumour, brain metastases, metabolic disturbance (hypoglycaemia, hyponatraemia, hypocalcaemia, uraemia, renal failure), drug toxicity (tramadol, SSRIs, antipsychotics, ondansetron, antibiotics), stroke, hypoxia, infection (meningitis, encephalitis), withdrawal (benzodiazepines, alcohol, antiepileptics).


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Bloods (glucose, U&E, Ca, Mg, LFT, drug levels), CT brain if indicated (new focal, head injury, status), EEG if non-convulsive status suspected.

### Management
Acute: position (recovery, side), suction, oxygen, IV/SC midazolam 10mg (or diazepam 10mg PR/IV, buccal midazolam 10mg if no IV access), repeat if needed. Status epilepticus: continuous midazolam CSCI 5-10mg/24h or infusion; phenobarbital loading 50-100mg SC then 100-200mg/24h CSCI. Maintenance: antiepileptic already, or add (phenobarbital via CSCI convenient, levetiracetam 500mg BD). Withdrawal: restart/replace weaned drugs (benzodiazepines, opioids). Family education and support.

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Acute management of seizure? A: Position (recovery), suction, oxygen, IV/SC midazolam 10mg (or diazepam PR, buccal midazolam).

2. 2. Q: Status epilepticus management? A: Continuous midazolam CSCI 5-10mg/24h; phenobarbital loading 50-100mg SC then CSCI.

3. 3. Q: Causes? A: Brain tumour, brain mets, metabolic (hypoglycaemia, hyponatraemia, hypocalcaemia, uraemia), drugs, stroke, hypoxia, infection, withdrawal.

4. 4. Q: Phenobarbital in dying? A: Via CSCI convenient, simple, controls seizures; sedation limit at end of life.

5. 5. Q: Family education? A: Reassure, position, give rescue, call ambulance if not palliative context.


## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **Seizure** = Position + midazolam + treat cause; phenobarbital in dying
