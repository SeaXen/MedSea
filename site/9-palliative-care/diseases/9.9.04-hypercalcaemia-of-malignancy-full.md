# Hypercalcaemia of malignancy

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Palliative Care Emergencies

## Definition

Hypercalcaemia of malignancy occurs in 10-20% of cancer patients, mediated by PTHrP (most common, humoral hypercalcaemia of malignancy), osteolytic metastases, or rarely 1,25(OH)2D production (lymphoma). Most common cancers: breast, lung (squamous), multiple myeloma, renal, head and neck, ovarian. Clinical features: stones, bones, groans, thrones, psychiatric overtones (polyuria, polydipsia, dehydration, bone pain, constipation, confusion). Calcium >3.5 mmol/L severe; >4.0 life-threatening. Treatment: IV normal saline (3-6L/day, forced diuresis), IV bisphosphonates (zoledronic acid 4mg over 15min or pamidronate 60-90mg over 2-4h, takes 2-4 days for full effect), calcitonin (rapid but short-lived effect, useful for severe), denosumab (in bisphosphonate-refractory or renal failure); loop diuretics only after rehydration; consider dialysis.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Hypercalcaemia of malignancy affects 10-20% of cancer patients. Mechanism: PTHrP (most — humoral, squamous lung, breast, renal, ovarian, thyroid), osteolytic metastases (breast, myeloma, lung), 1,25-(OH)2D production (lymphoma). Cancers: breast, lung, multiple myeloma, renal, head/neck, ovary. Symptoms: stones (renal, polyuria), bones (pain), groans (constipation, N&V, abdominal pain), thrones (polyuria), psychiatric overtones (confusion, depression, fatigue). Severe >3.5 mmol/L; life-threatening >4.0. ECG: short QT, prolonged PR, widened QRS.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Corrected Ca, albumin, PTH (suppressed), PTHrP (elevated), 25-OH vitamin D, 1,25-(OH)2D, LFT, ALP, U&E, ECG (short QT), phosphate (low in PTHrP). CXR, myeloma screen.

### Management
IV normal saline 3-6L/day (corrects dehydration, increases calciuresis), IV bisphosphonate (zoledronic acid 4mg over 15min or pamidronate 60-90mg over 2-4h, takes 2-4 days for full effect), calcitonin 100-200IU SC q6-12h (rapid onset 4-6h, short-lived, useful for severe + bisphosphonate loading), denosumab 120mg SC (refractory or renal failure), loop diuretics only after rehydration, dialysis (severe/refractory/renal failure). Treat underlying cause.

## Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Primary hyperparathyroidism | PTH high, not suppressed |
| Vitamin D toxicity | 25-OH vitamin D high |
| Milk-alkali syndrome | Alkali ingestion |
| Granulomatous disease | 1,25-(OH)2D high (sarcoidosis, TB) |
