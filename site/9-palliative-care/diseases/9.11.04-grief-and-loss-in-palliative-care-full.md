# Grief and loss in palliative care

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Communication and Ethics

## Definition

Grief in palliative care occurs at multiple time points: anticipatory (before death, in patient and family), at death, and post-death (bereavement). Normal responses: emotional (sadness, yearning, anger, guilt, anxiety), cognitive (searching, disbelief, preoccupation, hallucinations of deceased), physical (sleep, appetite, energy, somatic, sighing respiration, sigh). Distinguish normal grief from depression (anhedonia, hopelessness, suicidal ideation, guilt without self-reproach in grief, self-esteem loss in depression). Cultural and spiritual aspects important. Support: validate, listen, normalise, support group referral, professional support for complicated grief or depression.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Normal grief: no specific investigations. Distinguish depression: PHQ-9, clinical interview.

### Management
Validate, listen, normalise, support group referral, professional support for complicated grief or depression. Distinguish from depression (anhedonia, suicidal ideation, self-esteem loss, biological features). Cultural and spiritual aspects important (rituals, faith, meaning). Family support: pre-death, at death, post-death (sympathy, follow-up).

## Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Normal grief | Waves, can enjoy |
| Depression | Anhedonia, suicidal, biological |
| Complicated grief | >12 months, functional impairment |

2. 2. Q: Anticipatory grief? A: Pre-death grief in patient and family.

3. 3. Q: Normal responses? A: Sadness, yearning, anger, guilt, anxiety, searching, hallucinations.
