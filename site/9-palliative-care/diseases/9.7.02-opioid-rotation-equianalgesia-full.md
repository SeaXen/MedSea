# Opioid rotation (equianalgesia)

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Opioid Management**

## Definition

Opioid rotation (or switching) is changing from one opioid to another to improve analgesia or reduce side effects. Based on incomplete cross-tolerance (10-25% reduction when switching). Indications: inadequate analgesia with side effects (e.g., sedation with adequate pain control), severe persistent side effects, OIH, practical issues (route, compliance). Use equianalgesic table: oral morphine 30mg = oral oxycodone 20mg = oral hydromorphone 8mg = SC morphine 15mg = IV morphine 10mg = transdermal fentanyl 12-25 mcg/hr (based on 24h dose). Reduce calculated dose by 25-30% for incomplete cross-tolerance.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Opioid rotation (switch) is changing from one opioid to another when the current opioid is ineffective or causing intolerable side effects. Common indications: inadequate analgesia despite adequate dose, severe side effects (N&V, sedation, hallucinations, OIH), tolerance, renal/hepatic impairment (switch to fentanyl, buprenorphine, methadone). Process: calculate OME, choose new opioid, calculate new dose (apply 25-50% reduction for incomplete cross-tolerance), convert breakthrough dose (1/6 of new total daily dose), monitor.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Pain assessment, side effect profile, renal/hepatic function, review of all opioids (avoid incomplete cross-tolerance error), breakthrough pain pattern.

### Management
Plan rotation: calculate equianalgesic dose, apply 25-50% reduction. Often done as planned admission (e.g., for methadone). Switch to fentanyl patch: ensure opioid-tolerant (≥60mg OME/day for 1 week). Consider CSCI for patients who can't take oral (e.g., switching to CSCI morphine + midazolam + glycopyrronium).

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: When to rotate opioid? A: Inadequate analgesia despite adequate dose, severe side effects, tolerance, renal/hepatic impairment.

2. 2. Q: How much to reduce new dose? A: 25-50% for incomplete cross-tolerance.

3. 3. Q: New breakthrough dose? A: 1/6 of new total daily dose.

4. 4. Q: Methadone switch? A: Specialist; complex (long half-life, NMDA, drug interactions).

5. 5. Q: Why switch to fentanyl patch? A: Renally safe, less histamine, alternative side effect profile.
