# Care of the dying patient

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — End of Life Care

## Definition

Care of the dying is the active, holistic care of patients in the last hours to days of life, with focus on comfort, dignity, and family support. Recognition of dying: bedbound, semi-comatose, only able to take sips, unable to take oral medications, deteriorating. Priorities: symptom control (pain, breathlessness, N&V, delirium, respiratory secretions, agitation, pruritus), hydration/nutrition (oral if possible, careful consideration of IV/SC fluids — may worsen secretions), psychological and spiritual support, family support, place of care. Anticipatory prescribing ('just in case' box): commonly morphine/diamorphine, midazolam, levomepromazine, haloperidol, glycopyrronium, hyoscine butylbromide. After-death care: verification, certification, bereavement support.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Care of the dying is active, holistic care of patients in last hours to days of life. Recognition of dying: bedbound, semi-comatose, only able to take sips, unable to take oral medications, deteriorating. Priorities: comfort, dignity, symptom control, family support. Symptoms in last days: pain, dyspnoea, N&V, delirium (terminal agitation), respiratory secretions (death rattle), pruritus, mouth care, incontinence.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Minimal investigations at end of life (cause focus on comfort); routine observations may be reduced. Identify and treat reversible (pain, urinary retention, constipation).

### Management
Comfort measures: anticipatory prescribing ('just in case' box: opioid, midazolam, levomepromazine, haloperidol, glycopyrronium, hyoscine butylbromide); regular review; simple regimens; common routes (PO, SC, CSCI, PR, buccal); avoid polypharmacy. Hydration/nutrition: oral if possible, sips, ice chips, careful consideration of IV/SC fluids (may worsen secretions). Mouth care: sips, ice chips, lip balm, artificial saliva spray, treat oral Candida. Family support, information, involvement. Place of care (home, hospice, hospital), respect patient preference. After-death care: verification, certification, bereavement.
