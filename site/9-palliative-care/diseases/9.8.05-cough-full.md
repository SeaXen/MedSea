# Cough

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care**

## Definition

Cough is a common, distressing symptom in palliative care, often in lung cancer, end-stage COPD, asthma, infection, post-nasal drip, ACE inhibitor, gastro-oesophageal reflux, or radiation pneumonitis. Assessment: dry vs productive, triggers, timing, character, associated symptoms. Investigation: clinical, CXR if indicated, sputum culture if infection. Management: treat reversible cause (infection, asthma, reflux, stop ACE, reduce secretions); demulcents (simple linctus, honey, glycerol); opioids (codeine 30-60mg q4-6h, morphine 2.5-5mg q4-6h IR for severe), low-dose morphine is most effective for cancer cough; antitussives (dextromethorphan, pholcodine); mucolytics if productive (carbocisteine); expectorants (guaifenesin); for cough-variant asthma, inhaled steroids. Non-pharmacological: humidified air, positioning, treating the cause.

**Key clinical pearls:**

- Most common in lung cancer/COPD
- Codeine 30-60mg q4-6h
- Morphine 2.5-5mg q4-6h for severe
- Treat reversible cause


## Clinical Features and Presentation

Cough in palliative care is common, dry or productive, distressing, disrupts sleep. Causes: lung cancer, end-stage COPD, infection, post-nasal drip, ACE inhibitor, gastro-oesophageal reflux, radiation pneumonitis, pleural effusion, heart failure, lymphangitis.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Clinical (dry vs productive, triggers, timing, character), CXR if indicated, sputum culture if infection, drug review (ACE inhibitor), reflux assessment.

### Management
Treat reversible cause (infection, asthma, reflux, stop ACE, reduce secretions). Demulcents: simple linctus, honey, glycerol. Opioids: codeine 30-60mg q4-6h, morphine 2.5-5mg q4-6h IR (most effective for cancer cough). Antitussives: dextromethorphan, pholcodine. Mucolytics if productive (carbocisteine). For cough-variant asthma, inhaled steroids. Non-pharm: humidified air, positioning, treating the cause.

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Most common cause in cancer? A: Lung cancer, end-stage COPD, infection.

2. 2. Q: First-line for cancer cough? A: Codeine 30-60mg q4-6h; morphine 2.5-5mg q4-6h for severe.

3. 3. Q: Treat reversible? A: Infection (ABx), asthma (inhaled steroid), reflux (PPI), ACE (stop), effusion (drain).

4. 4. Q: Demulcent? A: Simple linctus, honey, glycerol (sugar syrup, soothing).

5. 5. Q: Why not for productive cough? A: Suppresses sputum clearance; use mucolytic (carbocisteine) instead.
