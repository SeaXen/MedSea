# Insomnia

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care**

## Definition

Insomnia is difficulty initiating or maintaining sleep, common in palliative care due to pain, anxiety, depression, drugs (corticosteroids, opioids, antidepressants), symptoms (cough, breathlessness, pruritus, N&V), environment (noisy ward, light), existential distress. Management: identify and treat cause (pain, anxiety, depression, drugs, symptoms), sleep hygiene (regular schedule, dark/quiet, no screens, warm drink, no caffeine/alcohol late), relaxation techniques, cognitive behavioural therapy for insomnia (CBT-I). Pharmacological: temazepam 7.5-15mg nocte (short-acting benzodiazepine, common first-line), zopiclone 3.75-7.5mg (Z-drug, alternative), promethazine 25-50mg (antihistamine, sedation), mirtazapine 15-30mg (antidepressant with sedation, useful if depression/appetite poor), melatonin 3-6mg (circadian rhythm). Avoid regular hypnotics in advanced disease (may cause confusion, falls).

**Key clinical pearls:**

- Sleep hygiene + CBT-I
- Temazepam 7.5-15mg
- Treat cause (pain, anxiety, depression)
- Avoid in advanced disease


## Clinical Features and Presentation

Insomnia in palliative care affects 30-50%. Difficulty initiating, maintaining, or returning to sleep, with daytime consequences. Causes: pain, anxiety, depression, drugs (corticosteroids, opioids, stimulants, SSRIs), symptoms (cough, dyspnoea, pruritus, N&V, night sweats), environment (noisy ward, light), existential distress, frequent toileting (diuretic, polyuria). May be primary insomnia or secondary.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
History (sleep pattern, onset, triggers, daytime function), pain, anxiety, depression, drugs, symptoms. Sleep hygiene assessment. Tools: Insomnia Severity Index (ISI), Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI).

### Management
Treat cause: pain, anxiety, depression, drugs, symptoms, environmental (noisy, light). Non-pharm: sleep hygiene (regular schedule, dark, quiet, no screens, comfortable temperature, no caffeine/alcohol late, no long daytime naps), relaxation, mindfulness, CBT-I (gold standard in chronic insomnia). Pharmacological: temazepam 7.5-15mg nocte (short-acting, first-line, BZD), zopiclone 3.75-7.5mg (Z-drug), melatonin 3-6mg (circadian, elderly), mirtazapine 15-30mg (antidepressant, also useful for sleep, appetite, weight gain, anxiety), promethazine 25-50mg (antihistamine, less preferred in elderly due to anticholinergic). Avoid long-term hypnotics in advanced disease (confusion, falls).

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line hypnotic? A: Temazepam 7.5-15mg nocte (short-acting BZD).

2. 2. Q: Best long-term treatment? A: CBT-I (gold standard).

3. 3. Q: When to use mirtazapine? A: Insomnia with depression, weight loss, anxiety.

4. 4. Q: Why avoid in elderly? A: Falls, confusion, anticholinergic side effects (antihistamines, prolonged BZD).

5. 5. Q: Sleep hygiene? A: Regular schedule, dark/quiet, no screens, comfortable temp, no caffeine/alcohol, no long daytime naps.
