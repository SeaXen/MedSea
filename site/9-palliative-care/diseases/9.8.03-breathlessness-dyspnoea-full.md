# Breathlessness (dyspnoea)

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Pain Symptoms in Palliative Care**

## Definition

Breathlessness (dyspnoea) is a subjective experience of breathing discomfort, common in palliative care (50-70% cancer, 90% COPD/heart failure). Causes: lung primary/secondary cancer, pleural effusion, lymphangitic carcinomatosis, SVCO, infection, anaemia, anxiety, heart failure, COPD, PE. Treatment: identify and treat reversible causes (effusion drainage, infection, anaemia transfusion, SVCO stent); non-pharmacological (positioning, cool airflow, relaxation); opioids (morphine 2.5-5mg q4h PO or 5-10mg/24h SC — reduces breathlessness without significant respiratory depression); anxiolytics (midazolam 2-5mg SC PRN, especially in last days); oxygen if hypoxic; fan (cool air).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Breathlessness in advanced illness affects 50-70% of patients with lung cancer, end-stage COPD/CHF, end-stage renal/liver disease, neuromuscular disease. Subjective experience of breathing discomfort, often disproportionate to objective measures. Causes: respiratory (lung cancer, COPD, pneumonia, PE, pleural effusion, lymphangitis, SVC), cardiac (CCF, anaemia, PE), metabolic (acidosis, anaemia), anxiety. Worst at end of life. May be episodic or chronic.


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
History (triggers, timing, position), examination (respiratory rate, effort, oxygen saturation, lung sounds, JVP, peripheral oedema, anaemia), bloods (FBC, BNP, ABG, glucose, U&E), CXR, ECG, BNP, oxygen saturation, peak flow if asthma/COPD.

### Management
Treat reversible (infection, effusion, anaemia, bronchospasm, anxiety, SVC stent). Non-pharmacological: positioning (upright, fan, open window), cool airflow, breathing techniques, relaxation, pacing. Pharmacological: opioids (oral morphine 2.5-5mg q4-6h, or SC 1-2.5mg prn; CSCI if persistent — morphine or diamorphine); benzodiazepines for anxiety (midazolam 2-5mg SC prn, CSCI 5-20mg/24h; diazepam 2-5mg PO); oxygen if hypoxic (SpO2 <88%, but no evidence of benefit if not hypoxic).

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: First-line drug for dyspnoea? A: Opioid (morphine 2.5-5mg PO q4-6h or SC 1-2.5mg prn).

2. 2. Q: Mechanism of opioid in dyspnoea? A: Reduces perception of breathlessness, decreases central response to hypoxia/hypercapnia.

3. 3. Q: Non-pharm for dyspnoea? A: Positioning, fan, open window, breathing techniques, relaxation.

4. 4. Q: When to use oxygen? A: SpO2 <88%, or arterial hypoxaemia; no evidence of benefit in non-hypoxic breathlessness.

5. 5. Q: First-line for anxiety component? A: Benzodiazepine (midazolam SC prn or CSCI, or diazepam PO).
