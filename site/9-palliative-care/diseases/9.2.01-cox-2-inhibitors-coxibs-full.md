> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Non-Opioid Analgesics**

## 1. 1. Definition

COX-2 selective inhibitors (coxibs) selectively inhibit COX-2, reducing GI side effects (ulcer, bleed) vs traditional NSAIDs. Examples: celecoxib, etoricoxib, parecoxib (IV). However, INCREASED cardiovascular risk (MI, stroke, hypertension) led to rofecoxib (Vioxx) withdrawal in 2004. Use: patients with high GI risk, but caution in CV risk. NICE: coxibs with PPI cover for high GI risk patients. Avoid in established CV disease.

## 2. 2. Clinical Features and Presentation

COX-2 selective inhibitors (celecoxib, etoricoxib) preferentially inhibit COX-2 (inflammatory), sparing COX-1 (gastric protection, platelet function). Less GI ulceration/bleeding, but increased CV events (myocardial infarction, stroke, hypertension, heart failure) — rofecoxib (Vioxx) withdrawn 2004. Use in patients with high GI risk and lower CV risk. Indication: osteoarthritis, RA, dysmenorrhoea, gout (etoricoxib), acute pain. Not for CV prevention.


## 3. 3. Investigation and Management Summary

### 1. Investigations
CV risk assessment before use (avoid if recent MI, unstable angina, recent CABG, stroke, severe heart failure), renal function (caution eGFR<30, contraindicated <15), BP monitoring, GI risk. Sulphonamide allergy (celecoxib contraindicated).

### 2. Management
Celecoxib 100-200mg OD-BD; etoricoxib 30-120mg OD. Lowest effective dose, shortest time. Reassess CV risk. Add PPI if high GI risk. Avoid in established CV disease. Monitor BP, renal function.

## 6. 6. Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Why was Vioxx withdrawn? A: Increased CV events (myocardial infarction, stroke) with long-term use.

2. 2. Q: Difference vs traditional NSAID? A: Less GI ulceration/bleeding (COX-1 sparing), but similar or higher CV risk.

3. 3. Q: When to use coxib? A: High GI risk, low CV risk, no aspirin sensitivity.

4. 4. Q: Coxib contraindication? A: Recent CV event, severe heart failure, sulphonamide allergy (celecoxib), eGFR<15.

5. 5. Q: Coxib + aspirin? A: Loses GI advantage; need PPI cover.
