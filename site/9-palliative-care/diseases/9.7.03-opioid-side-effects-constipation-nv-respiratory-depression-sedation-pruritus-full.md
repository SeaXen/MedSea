# Opioid side effects (constipation, N&V, respiratory depression, sedation, pruritus)

Related: [[../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> Davidson 24e Ch 8 (Colvin, Fallon) — **Opioid Management**

## Definition

Opioid side effects are common and predictable from μ-receptor activity: constipation (gut; prophylactic laxatives), nausea and vomiting (CTZ; first 1-2 weeks, antiemetics), sedation (CNS; tolerance develops), respiratory depression (brainstem; late, life-threatening; naloxone), pruritus (μ on mast cells; antihistamines), miosis (Edinger-Westphal), urinary retention, dry mouth, sweating, OIH, hypogonadism. Most tolerance: N&V, sedation. Least tolerance: constipation. Most dangerous: respiratory depression. Treatment: prophylactic laxatives (all opioid patients), antiemetic first 1-2 weeks, naloxone for severe RD, opioid rotation if persistent.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Opioid side effects are common and expected; tolerance develops to most (except constipation). Constipation: most common, no tolerance, all opioids; prevention mandatory (laxative). Nausea and vomiting: 30-50% initially, tolerance develops over 1-2 weeks; treat with antiemetic (haloperidol, metoclopramide, ondansetron). Respiratory depression: late sign, rare with oral stable doses; naloxone 3-5mcg IV, repeat (lower dose to avoid withdrawal and pain crisis). Sedation: common initial, tolerance develops; if persistent, reduce dose or rotate. Pruritus: histamine-mediated (more with morphine), tolerance; treat with antihistamine, opioid rotation (fentanyl, methadone).


## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
Baseline: pain, sedation (Pasero scale), respiratory rate, bowel function, N&V, pruritus. Routine monitoring. Naloxone challenge if RD suspected.

### Management
Prevention is key: co-prescribe stimulant laxative (senna) + softener (docusate); antiemetic prn first 1-2 weeks. Treat side effects: constipation (osmotic laxative, opioid rotation), N&V (antiemetic, opioid rotation), RD (naloxone, opioid reduction/rotation), sedation (reduce dose, caffeine, rotation), pruritus (antihistamine, opioid rotation, ondansetron).

## Viva Questions

1. 1. Q: Most common opioid side effect without tolerance? A: Constipation.

2. 2. Q: Naloxone dose for RD? A: 3-5mcg IV, repeat (titrate to resp rate, not conscious level).

3. 3. Q: How to manage opioid-induced constipation? A: Stimulant laxative (senna) + softener (docusate); osmotic (macrogol); rotate opioid; consider methylnaltrexone SC for refractory.

4. 4. Q: Side effect with no tolerance? A: Constipation (need prophylactic laxative).

5. 5. Q: Side effect with quick tolerance? A: Nausea, vomiting, sedation, pruritus (usually resolve in 1-2 weeks).
