## Key Algorithms

### Modified WHO Analgesic Ladder (2-step, current)
```
Pain score 1-3 (mild)        Pain score 4-6 (moderate)         Pain score 7-10 (severe)
     ↓                              ↓                                   ↓
Step 1:                          Step 2:                             Step 2:
Non-opioid ± adjuvant            Mild opioid (codeine)             Strong opioid (morphine)
Paracetamol ± NSAID              OR low-dose morphine              ± non-opioid ± adjuvant
                                                           
For all: adjuvant analgesics for neuropathic pain, bone pain, etc.
```

### Cancer Pain — Stepwise Approach
```
Cancer pain → WHO ladder + adjuvant (for neuropathic/bone)
         → Optimize oral/transdermal opioid
         → If inadequate: opioid rotation (switch)
         → Add breakthrough dose (10% of total daily dose, q1h PRN)
         → Consider neuraxial block / nerve block / palliative RT
         → Consider palliative sedation (last resort)
```

### Malignant Spinal Cord Compression (MSCC) Emergency
```
Suspected MSCC → Urgent MRI whole spine (within 24h, ideally 6h)
              → Dexamethasone 16mg loading → 4mg q6h
              → Neurosurgical + RT opinion (decompressive surgery, RT)
              → Best supportive care if not surgical candidate
```

## Essential Drug Doses

| Drug | Dose | Key Monitoring |
|------|------|----------------|
| **Morphine PO (immediate release)** | 5-10mg q4h PRN (starting dose, opioid-naïve) | Pain, RR, sedation, constipation |
| **Morphine PO (modified release)** | 30mg BD (typical starting total daily) | As above |
| **Oxycodone** | 5mg q4-6h PRN (starting) | As morphine; less histamine |
| **Fentanyl patch** | 12-25 mcg/hr q72h (based on oral morphine) | Equianalgesic conversion; no active metabolites |
| **Buprenorphine patch** | 5-20 mcg/hr q7d | Ceiling effect for respiratory depression |
| **Tramadol** | 50-100mg q6h (max 400mg/day) | Seizure risk; serotonin syndrome; ± paracetamol combination |
| **Codeine** | 30-60mg q4h PRN (prodrug — metabolised to morphine) | 5-10% non-metabolisers; varies; rare ultrarapid metaboliser (respiratory depression) |
| **Paracetamol** | 500-1000mg q4-6h (max 4g/day) | Hepatotoxicity in overdose; reduce in liver disease |
| **NSAID ibuprofen** | 400mg TDS (with PPI cover) | GI bleed, renal, CV |
| **Diclofenac** | 50mg TDS | CV risk (withdrawn in UK) |
| **Gabapentin** | 100-300mg TDS (titrate up to 3600mg/day) | Renal dose adjustment; sedation, peripheral oedema |
| **Pregabalin** | 75mg BD (titrate up to 600mg/day) | As gabapentin; Schedule 3 controlled |
| **Amitriptyline** | 10-25mg nocte (titrate) | Anticholinergic; cardiac; sedation |
| **Duloxetine** | 30-60mg OD | Hepatic; serotonin syndrome |
| **Dexamethasone** | 4-16mg/day (palliative); 16mg loading (MSCC) | Hyperglycaemia, mood, gastric |
| **Haloperidol** | 0.5-5mg q4-8h PRN (N&V, delirium) | QTc, extrapyramidal |
| **Levomepromazine** | 6.25-25mg PRN (broad-spectrum antiemetic) | Sedation |
| **Cyclizine** | 50mg TDS (NAUSEA — vestibular/gut) | Anticholinergic |
| **Ondansetron** | 4-8mg TDS (5-HT3 antagonist) | Constipation, headache, QTc |
| **Bisphosphonate (zoledronic acid)** | 4mg IV over 15 min q3-4w | Hypocalcaemia, jaw osteonecrosis, renal |
| **Denosumab** | 120mg SC weekly for 4 weeks, then q4w | Hypocalcaemia, ONJ |

## Communication & Ethics

### SPIKES Protocol for Breaking Bad News
1. **S** — Setting (private, comfortable, no interruptions, support person)
2. **P** — Perception (what does the patient know/understand?)
3. **I** — Invitation (how much do they want to know?)
4. **K** — Knowledge (warning shot, then facts in small chunks, no jargon)
5. **E** — Emotions (acknowledge, validate, empathise)
6. **S** — Strategy/Summary (next steps, plan, support)

### Ethical Principles (Beauchamp & Childress)
- **Autonomy** — patient's right to make own decisions
- **Beneficence** — do good
- **Non-maleficence** — do no harm
- **Justice** — fair distribution of resources

### Key Legal/Ethical Issues
- **Euthanasia** — illegal in UK
- **Physician-assisted suicide** — illegal in UK (legal in some countries: Netherlands, Belgium, etc.)
- **Palliative sedation** — legal (proportionate, intention is symptom relief)
- **Withdrawing/withholding treatment** — legal and ethical if futile or against patient's wishes
- **DNACPR** — patient has right to refuse
- **Double effect** — doctrine allows opioid doses that may hasten death IF intention is symptom relief
