# Cultural and spiritual aspects of palliative care

Related: [[../../../Pain and Palliative Care MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 8 - Pain and Palliative Care Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 8: Pain and Palliative Care** (24th Edition, Colvin, Fallon) — Communication and Ethics

## Definition

Cultural and spiritual aspects of palliative care are essential for holistic, patient-centred care. Cultural: ethnicity, religion, language, family structure, decision-making patterns (individual vs collective), views on death and dying, rituals (after death), dietary, gender roles. Spiritual: meaning, purpose, connection (with self, others, nature, higher power/transcendence), faith, hope, despair, existential distress. Assessment: FICA (Faith, Importance, Community, Address in care) or HOPE (Hope, Organised religion, Personal spirituality, Effects). Support: chaplains, imams, rabbis, religious leaders; cultural liaison; family involvement; respect rituals (death, after death, mourning, organ donation); avoid assumptions; documentation; staff training.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
None (assessment).

### Management
Cultural awareness: ethnicity, religion, language, family structure, decision-making (individual vs collective), death/rituals, dietary, gender roles. Spiritual: meaning, purpose, connection, faith, hope, despair, existential distress. Assessment: FICA, HOPE. Support: chaplains, imams, rabbis, religious leaders, cultural liaison, family involvement, respect rituals (death, after death, mourning, organ donation). Avoid assumptions. Professional interpreter for communication. Documentation: cultural, spiritual preferences. Staff training: cultural competence, spiritual care.
