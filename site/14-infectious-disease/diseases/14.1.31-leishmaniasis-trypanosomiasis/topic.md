## 2. 📖 Leishmaniasis

### Classification & Geography

| Clinical Form | Species | Geography | Vector |
|---------------|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Visceral (VL/Kala-azar)** | *L. donovani* (Indian Subcontinent, East Africa), *L. infantum* (Mediterranean, Middle East, China, Latin America) | **India (Bihar), East Africa (Sudan, Ethiopia, Kenya), Brazil** | *Phlebotomus* Sandfly |
| **Cutaneous (CL)** | *L. tropica* (Urban, Middle East), *L. major* (Rural, Middle East, Africa), *L. mexicana* (Americas) | **Middle East, Central Asia, Americas** | *Phlebotomus* / *Lutzomyia* |
| **Mucocutaneous (ML)** | *L. braziliensis* (Brazil, Bolivia, Peru), *L. panamensis* (Central America) | **Latin America** | *Lutzomyia* Sandfly |
| **Post-Kala-Azar Dermal Leishmaniasis (PKDL)** | *L. donovani* | **India, Sudan** | *Phlebotomus* |

> [!tip] **VL = Kala-azar = Fever + Splenomegaly + Pancytopenia + Hypergammaglobulinaemia**. **CL = Orient Sore (Self-Healing Ulcer)**. **ML = Mucosal Destruction (Espundia)**. **PKDL = Post-Kala-Azar Dermal Leishmaniasis**.

## 3. 📖 Visceral Leishmaniasis (VL / Kala-azar)

### Clinical Features

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Classic Triad** | **Fever** (Intermittent, Double Quotidian), **Massive Splenomegaly**, **Pancytopenia** |
| **Other** | **Weight Loss**, **Wasting**, **Hypergammaglobulinaemia** (↑ IgG/IgM), **Liver Enlargement**, **Lymphadenopathy** (African VL), **Skin Changes** (Hyperpigmentation/Kala-azar = "Black Fever") |
| **Complications** | **Secondary Infections** (TB, Pneumonia), **Bleeding** (Thrombocytopenia), **Renal Failure**, **PKDL** (Post-Treatment) |

### Diagnosis

| Test | Sensitivity/Specificity | Role |
|------|------------------------|------|
| **rK39 Dipstick** (Rapid) | **Sens 95-98%, Spec 90-95%** | **Field-Friendly, Rapid Screening (VL)** |
| **Serology (DAT, ELISA, IFAT)** | High Sens/Spec | **Confirmatory**, **Antibody Persists** |
| **Parasite Demonstration** | **Splenic Aspiration > BM > Lymph Node** | **Gold Standard** (Microscopy: LD Bodies in Macrophages) |
| **PCR** | **High Sens/Spec** | **Species ID, Monitoring** |
| **Aldehyde Test (Formal Gel)** | Low Sensitivity | Historical |

### Treatment (VL)

| Region / Scenario | 1st Line | Alternative | Duration |
|-------------------|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Indian Subcontinent (L. donovani)** | **LAmB 10mg/kg Single Dose** (or 5mg/kg Day 1-5) | **Miltefosine 50mg BD (Weight-Based) × 28d** | **Single Dose LAmB** |
| **East Africa (L. donovani)** | **LAmB 3-5mg/kg/day × 5-10d** | **SSG+PM (Sodium Stibogluconate + Paromomycin) × 17d** | **5-10 Days LAmB** |
| **Mediterranean/Latin America (L. infantum)** | **LAmB 3-5mg/kg/day × 5-10d** | **Miltefosine** | **5-10 Days** |
| **HIV Co-infection** | **LAmB 4mg/kg/day Days 1-5, 10, 14, 21, 28, 35, 42** | **Secondary Prophylaxis: LAmB Monthly** | **Induction + Maintenance** |

> [!warning] **Miltefosine = Teratogenic** (Contraindicated in Pregnancy). **SSG = Cardiotoxicity/Pancreatitis** (Monitor ECG/Amylase). **LAmB = Preferred** (Safety, Efficacy).

## 4. 📖 Cutaneous Leishmaniasis (CL) & Mucocutaneous (ML)

### Cutaneous Leishmaniasis (CL)

| Feature | Old World (L. tropica, L. major) | New World (L. mexicana, L. braziliensis) |
|---------|----------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Lesion** | **Single/Multiple Ulcers** (Volcano-Shaped, Raised Edge, Indurated Base) | **Larger, More Inflammatory**, **Multiple** |
| **Healing** | **Self-Limiting (3-6 Months)** → **Hypopigmented Scar** | **Slower, Higher Risk ML** (L. braziliensis) |
| **Types** | **Dry (Urban/L. tropica)** vs **Wet (Rural/L. major)** | |

### Mucocutaneous Leishmaniasis (ML / Espundia)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Agent** | **L. braziliensis** (Predominant), *L. panamensis*, *L. guyanensis* |
| **Pathogenesis** | **Metastatic Spread** from Primary Cutaneous Lesion → Mucosa (Nose, Pharynx, Larynx) |
| **Presentation** | **Nose** (Epistaxis, Septal Perforation, Saddle Nose), **Pharynx/Larynx** (Dysphagia, Hoarseness, Airway Obstruction) |
| **Timing** | **Months-Years** After Primary Cutaneous Lesion Heals |

### Treatment (CL/ML)

| Form | 1st Line | Alternative |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **CL (Old World)** | **Intral Lesional Sodium Stibogluconate** / **Cryotherapy** / **Topical Paromomycin** / **Thermotherapy** | **Systemic: Miltefosine / LAmB / Pentavalent Antimonials** |
| **CL (New World) / ML** | **LAmB 3mg/kg/day × 5-10d** / **Miltefosine** | **Pentavalent Antimonials (SSG/MA)** |
| **ML** | **LAmB 3-5mg/kg/day × 20-30d** | **Pentavalent Antimonials × 30d** |

> [!warning] **ML = Medical Emergency** (Airway Risk). **Early Systemic Treatment Essential**.

## 5. 📖 Trypanosomiasis

### African Trypanosomiasis (Sleeping Sickness)

| Feature | *T. b. gambiense* (West/Central Africa) | *T. b. rhodesiense* (East/Southern Africa) |
|---------|-----------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **Epidemiology** | **Chronic** (98% Cases), **Anthroponotic** | **Acute** (Weeks), **Zoonotic** (Cattle/Game) |
| **Vector** | *Glossina palpalis* (Riverine) | *Glossina morsitans* (Savannah) |
| **Stage 1 (Haemo-lymphatic)** | Fever, Lymphadenopathy (Winterbottom's Sign: Posterior Cervical), Splenomegaly, Pruritus, Rash | Similar but **Rapid Progression** (Weeks) |
| **Stage 2 (Meningo-encephalitic)** | **Months-Years** | **Weeks-Months** |
| | **Sleep Disturbance** (Reversed Circadian), **Neuropsychiatric**, **Tremor**, **Ataxia**, **Seizures**, **Coma** | Same but Faster |

> [!tip] **Winterbottom's Sign = Posterior Cervical Lymphadenopathy = Pathognomonic for T.b. gambiense**.

### Stage 1 vs Stage 2 Diagnostic Criteria

| Criterion | Stage 1 (Haemo-lymphatic) | Stage 2 (Meningo-encephalitic) |
|-----------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **CSF WBC** | **<5/µL** | **>5/µL** (Usually >20) |
| **CSF Protein** | **Normal** | **Elevated (>50mg/dL)** |
| **Trypanosomes in CSF** | **Absent** | **Present** |
| **Clinical** | No Neurological Signs | **Neurological Signs Present** |

### Treatment (African Trypanosomiasis)

| Stage | *T. b. gambiense* | *T. b. rhodesiense* |
|-------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Stage 1** | **Fexinidazole 1800mg OD × 10 Days** (Oral, All Stages) **OR** Pentamidine 4mg/kg IM × 7-10d | **Suramin 20mg/kg IV Days 1,3,7,14,21** (Test Dose First) |
| **Stage 2** | **Fexinidazole 1800mg OD × 10d** (If No Severe Neuro) **OR** Nifurtimox-Eflornithine Combination (NECT) | **Melarsoprol 2.2mg/kg IV Days 1-3, 10, 17-19, 25-27** (Arsenical, Toxic) |

> [!warning] **Fexinidazole = Oral, Effective for Both Stages (gambiense)**. **NECT = Eflornithine 400mg/kg Q6H IV ×14d + Nifurtimox 15mg/kg BD PO ×14d** (Safer than Melarsoprol). **Melarsoprol = Arsenical, Encephalopathy Risk (5-10%)**.

## 6. 📖 Chagas Disease (American Trypanosomiasis)

### Clinical Phases

| Phase | Features |
|-------|----------|
| **Acute (Weeks 1-8)** | **Romaña's Sign** (Unilateral Periorbital Oedema + Conjunctivitis), **Chagoma** (Inoculation Site), Fever, Lymphadenopathy, Hepatosplenomegaly, **Myocarditis** (Arrhythmia, Heart Failure), **Meningoencephalitis** (Rare) |
| **Indeterminate (Latent)** | **Asymptomatic**, **Positive Serology**, **Can Last Decades** |
| **Chronic (10-30 Years Later)** | **Cardiac** (Cardiomyopathy, Arrhythmias, Heart Failure, Thromboembolism, Sudden Death), **GI** (Megaesophagus, Megacolon), **Neurological** |

### Diagnosis

| Phase | Test |
|-------|------|
| **Acute** | **Microscopy (Blood - Motile Trypomastigotes)**, **PCR** (High Sens), **Antigen Detection** |
| **Chronic** | **Serology** (IgG ELISA/IHA/IIF — **Two Tests Required**), **PCR** (Low Sensitivity in Chronic) |

### Treatment

| Phase | Regimen |
|-------|---------|
| **Acute / Congenital / Reactivation** | **Benznidazole 5-7mg/kg/day BD × 60 Days** (1st Line) **OR Nifurtimox 8-10mg/kg/day BD × 60-90 Days** |
| **Chronic (Indeterminate/Early Cardiac)** | **Benznidazole 5-7mg/kg/day BD × 60 Days** (Benefit Controversial) |
| **Advanced Cardiac/GI** | **Supportive** (Heart Failure Rx, Pacemaker, Surgery for Megaesophagus/Colon) |

> [!warning] **Benznidazole = Teratogenic** (Contraindicated in Pregnancy). **Nifurtimox = Neurotoxicity/Psychosis**. **Both Require 60 Days**.

## 8. ❓ Viva Questions

1. **What is the classic presentation of Visceral Leishmaniasis (Kala-azar)?**
   - **Fever + Massive Splenomegaly + Pancytopenia + Hypergammaglobulinaemia**.

2. **What is the rapid diagnostic test for Visceral Leishmaniasis?**
   - **rK39 Dipstick** (Sensitivity 95-98%, Specificity 90-95%).

3. **What is the first-line treatment for Visceral Leishmaniasis in the Indian Subcontinent?**
   - **Single Dose Liposomal Amphotericin B 10mg/kg**.

4. **What is the difference between Old World and New World Cutaneous Leishmaniasis?**
   - **Old World (L. tropica/major): Self-Limiting, Single Ulcer**; **New World (L. braziliensis/mexicana): Larger, Multiple, Higher ML Risk**.

5. **What is Mucocutaneous Leishmaniasis and which species causes it?**
   - **Mucosal Destruction (Nose/Pharynx/Larynx) = Espundia**, caused by **L. braziliensis** (New World).

6. **What is Winterbottom's Sign and which disease is it associated with?**
   - **Posterior Cervical Lymphadenopathy** = **Pathognomonic for T. b. gambiense (African Trypanosomiasis)**.

7. **How do you distinguish Stage 1 from Stage 2 African Trypanosomiasis?**
   - **Stage 1: CSF WBC ≤5, No Trypanosomes in CSF, No Neuro Signs**; **Stage 2: CSF WBC >5, Trypanosomes in CSF, Neuro Signs Present**.

7. **What is the treatment for Stage 2 T. b. gambiense?**
   - **Fexinidazole 1800mg OD × 10 Days** OR **NECT (Nifurtimox + Eflornithine)**.

8. **What is Romaña's Sign in Chagas Disease?**
   - **Unilateral Periorbital Oedema + Conjunctivitis** at Inoculation Site (Acute Phase).

8. **What is the treatment for Acute Chagas Disease?**
   - **Benznidazole 5-7mg/kg/day BD × 60 Days** (1st Line) or **Nifurtimox 8-10mg/kg/day BD × 60-90d**.

9. **What are the chronic manifestations of Chagas Disease?**
   - **Cardiac** (Cardiomyopathy, Arrhythmias, Heart Failure, Sudden Death) + **GI** (Megaesophagus, Megacolon).

10. **How do you diagnose Chronic Chagas Disease?**
    - **Two Serological Tests Positive** (ELISA + IHA/IIF) **AND/OR PCR (Low Sensitivity in Chronic)**.

## 9. 🧠 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **VL vs CL vs ML** | **VL = Systemic (Fever, Spleen, Pancytopenia)**; **CL = Skin Ulcer**; **ML = Mucosal Destruction** |
| **Old World vs New World CL** | **Old World (L. tropica/major): Self-Healing**; **New World (L. braziliensis): Larger, ML Risk** |
| **gambiense vs rhodesiense** | **gambiense = Chronic, Anthroponotic, Gambia/West Africa**; **rhodesiense = Acute, Zoonotic, East Africa** |
| **Stage 1 vs Stage 2 HAT** | **CSF WBC ≤5 vs >5**, **Trypanosomes in CSF**, **Neuro Signs** |
| **Chagas Acute vs Chronic** | **Acute: Romaña's Sign, Myocarditis**; **Chronic: Cardiac (Cardiomyopathy) + GI (Megaesophagus/Colon)** |
| **rK39 vs DAT** | **rK39 = Rapid Dipstick (Field)**; **DAT = Quantitative Serology (Lab)** |

| Mnemonic | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **"Kala-Azar = Fever + Spleen + Pancytopenia + Hypergammaglobulinaemia"** | VL Triad |
| **"rK39 = Rapid Test for Kala-Azar"** | Rapid Test |
| **"LAmB = 1st Line VL Everywhere"** | Treatment |
| **"Winterbottom = Gambiense"** | T.b. gambiense Sign |
| **"Stage 1 = Blood/Lymph, Stage 2 = CNS"** | HAT Stages |
| **"Fexinidazole = Oral, Both Stages (Gambiense)"** | Fexinidazole |
| **"Romaña's Sign = Periorbital Oedema"** | Chagas Acute |
| **"Chagas = Heart + Gut"** | Chronic Chagas |
| **"Benznidazole = 60 Days"** | Chagas Treatment |
