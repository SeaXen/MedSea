# Davidson Chapter 13 - Infectious Disease Hierarchy

> [!info]
> Based on Davidson's Principles and Practice of Medicine, 24th Edition
> Authors: DH Dockrell, S Sundar, BJ Angus

## Chapter Structure (Estimated from 24e)

### A. Principles of Infectious Disease
1. **Introduction** — definitions, host-pathogen interaction, Koch's postulates
2. **Microbiological Diagnosis** — culture, microscopy, molecular methods (PCR, NGS), serology, antigen detection, biomarkers (PCT, CRP)
3. **Antimicrobial Chemotherapy** — classes, mechanisms, PK/PD, resistance mechanisms, stewardship
4. **Infection Prevention and Control** — standard precautions, transmission-based precautions, outbreak management
5. **Vaccination** — active/passive immunisation, schedules, contraindications

### B. Clinical Syndromes (Approach by Presentation)
6. **Fever and Sepsis** — definitions (SIRS, qSOFA, SOFA), febrile neutropenia, fever in returned traveller, FUO
7. **Respiratory Tract Infections** — URTI, LRTI, CAP, HAP/VAP, bronchiectasis, lung abscess
8. **Central Nervous System Infections** — acute bacterial meningitis, viral encephalitis/meningitis, TBM, brain abscess
9. **Cardiovascular Infections** — infective endocarditis, myocarditis, pericarditis, device infections
10. **Gastrointestinal and Hepatobiliary Infections** — infective diarrhoea, C. difficile, intra-abdominal abscess, cholangitis, liver abscess
11. **Urinary Tract Infections** — cystitis, pyelonephritis, CA-UTI, prostatitis, epididymo-orchitis
12. **Skin, Soft Tissue, Bone and Joint Infections** — cellulitis, necrotising fasciitis, diabetic foot, septic arthritis, osteomyelitis
13. **Genitourinary and Sexually Transmitted Infections** — urethritis, cervicitis, PID, epididymo-orchitis (STIs covered in Ch 15)

### C. Specific Organism Groups (Taxonomic Approach)
14. **Gram-Positive Cocci**
    - Staphylococcus (S. aureus — MSSA/MRSA, CoNS)
    - Streptococcus (S. pyogenes, S. pneumoniae, viridans, S. gallolyticus)
    - Enterococcus

15. **Gram-Negative Cocci**
    - Neisseria (N. meningitidis, N. gonorrhoeae)

16. **Gram-Negative Bacilli (Enterobacterales)**
    - E. coli, Klebsiella, Proteus, Enterobacter, Serratia, Citrobacter
    - ESBL, AmpC, CRE

17. **Non-Fermenting Gram-Negative Bacilli**
    - Pseudomonas aeruginosa
    - Acinetobacter baumannii
    - Stenotrophomonas maltophilia

18. **Anaerobic Bacteria**
    - Bacteroides, Clostridium, Peptostreptococcus, etc.

19. **Mycobacteria**
    - M. tuberculosis complex (TB — pulmonary and extrapulmonary)
    - Nontuberculous mycobacteria (NTM)

20. **Spirochaetes**
    - Treponema pallidum (Syphilis — Ch 15)
    - Borrelia (Lyme disease, **relapsing fever**)
    - Leptospira (Leptospirosis / Weil's disease)

21. **Rickettsiae, Orientia, Anaplasma, Ehrlichia**
    - Spotted fevers, typhus, scrub typhus, anaplasmosis, ehrlichiosis

22. **Chlamydiae**
    - C. trachomatis (STI — Ch 15; ocular, LGV, pneumonia)
    - C. pneumoniae, C. psittaci

23. **Mycoplasma and Ureaplasma**
    - M. pneumoniae, M. genitalium, U. urealyticum

24. **Viruses (General)**
    - Respiratory viruses: Influenza, RSV, hMPV, PIV, adenovirus, seasonal CoVs, SARS-CoV-2
    - CNS viruses: HSV, VZV, enteroviruses, arboviruses, rabies
    - Systemic: EBV, CMV, HHV-6, HIV (Ch 14), hepatitis viruses
    - Exanthems: measles, rubella, parvovirus B19
    - Hemorrhagic fevers: Ebola, Marburg, Lassa, dengue, yellow fever

25. **Fungi**
    - Yeasts: Candida (candidiasis, invasive), Cryptococcus
    - Moulds: Aspergillus (invasive, chronic, allergic), Mucorales (mucormycosis)
    - Dimorphic: Histoplasma, Coccidioides, Blastomyces, Paracoccidioides, Talaromyces
    - Pneumocystis jirovecii

26. **Parasites (Protozoa and Helminths)**
    - Malaria (Plasmodium)
    - Amoebiasis (E. histolytica)
    - Giardiasis
    - Cryptosporidiosis, Cyclosporiasis, Microsporidiosis
    - Toxoplasmosis
    - Leishmaniasis (visceral, cutaneous, mucocutaneous)
    - Trypanosomiasis (African, American/Chagas)
    - Filariasis (lymphatic, onchocerciasis, loiasis)
    - Schistosomiasis
    - Soil-transmitted helminths (Ascaris, Trichuris, hookworm, Strongyloides)
    - **Cestodes (Taenia, Echinococcus, Hymenolepis, Diphyllobothrium)**
    - **Trematodes (Clonorchis, Opisthorchis, Fasciola, Paragonimus, Fasciolopsis)**

27. **Emerging and Re-emerging Infections**
    - Viral: **Mpox**, Nipah, Hendra, novel coronaviruses, arboviruses
    - Bacterial: ESBL/CRE Enterobacterales, MDR/XDR TB, resistant gonorrhoea
    - Antimicrobial resistance surveillance

28. **Hospital-Acquired Infections (HAI) / Antimicrobial Stewardship**
    - CLABSI, CAUTI, SSI, VAP, CDI
    - MDR organisms: MRSA, VRE, ESBL, CRE, MDR Pseudomonas/Acinetobacter
    - Stewardship principles, de-escalation, PK/PD optimisation

29. **Travel Medicine / Imported Infections**
    - Pre-travel advice, vaccinations, chemoprophylaxis
    - Fever in the returned traveller algorithm
    - Geographic disease risks

30. **Infection in Special Hosts**
    - Immunocompromised (transplant, chemo, biologics, HIV)
    - Pregnancy
    - Neonates
    - Elderly
