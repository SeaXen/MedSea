> [!important]
> **Infective endocarditis (IE) = microbial infection of endothelial surface of heart (valves, mural endocardium, implanted devices).** **Duke Criteria (Modified) for diagnosis.** **Blood cultures ×3 sets BEFORE antibiotics.** **Empirical antibiotics by valve type (native vs prosthetic) and timing.** **Surgery indications: heart failure, uncontrolled infection, prevention of embolism.** **Mortality 15–30%.**

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Apply Modified Duke Criteria for definite/possible/rejected IE
- Obtain optimal blood cultures (timing, volume, sets)
- Select empirical antibiotics by valve type, timing, and risk factors
- Recognise complications: heart failure, embolic events, abscess, conduction abnormalities
- Apply surgical indications and timing (urgent vs elective)
- Manage special situations: prosthetic valve, device-related, culture-negative, right-sided, HIV

## 2. Definitions
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Native valve endocarditis (NVE)** | Infection of native valve leaflets |
| **Prosthetic valve endocarditis (PVE)** | Infection of prosthetic valve (mechanical/bioprosthetic) or reconstruction material |
| **Early PVE** | <1 year post-implant (usually nosocomial, Staphylococcus, Gram-neg, fungi) |
| **Late PVE** | >1 year post-implant (community-acquired flora similar to NVE) |
| **Cardiac device-related IE (CDRIE)** | Infection of pacemaker/ICD leads, generator pocket |
| **Right-sided IE** | Tricuspid/pulmonic valve (IVDU, central lines) |
| **Culture-negative IE** | Clinical IE with negative blood cultures (prior antibiotics, fastidious organisms) |

## 3. Modified Duke Criteria (2000 / ESC 2015)
### **Major Criteria**
1. **Positive blood cultures for typical IE organism:**
   - Typical microorganism from **2 separate blood cultures**: *Viridans streptococci, Streptococcus gallolyticus (bovis), HACEK, Staphylococcus aureus, Enterococcus faecalis* OR
   - **Persistently positive** blood cultures: ≥2 positive cultures >12h apart, or all of 3/4+ sets, or single positive culture for Coxiella burnetii / antiphase I IgG >1:800
2. **Evidence of endocardial involvement:**
   - **Echocardiogram (TTE/TOE):** Oscillating intracardiac mass (vegetation) on valve/supporting structures, abscess, new partial dehiscence of prosthetic valve
   - **New valvular regurgitation** (worsening of pre-existing not sufficient)

### **Minor Criteria**
1. **Predisposition:** Predisposing heart condition or IVDU
2. **Fever:** ≥38°C
3. **Vascular phenomena:** Major arterial emboli, septic pulmonary infarcts, mycotic aneurysm, intracranial haemorrhage, conjunctival haemorrhages, Janeway lesions
4. **Immunological phenomena:** Glomerulonephritis, Osler nodes, Roth spots, rheumatoid factor
5. **Microbiological:** Positive blood culture not meeting major criterion (e.g., single culture, atypical organism), serological evidence of active infection

### **Diagnosis**
| Classification | Criteria |
|----------------|----------|
| **Definite IE** | 2 Major OR 1 Major + 3 Minor OR 5 Minor |
| **Possible IE** | 1 Major + 1 Minor OR 3 Minor |
| **Rejected** | Firm alternative diagnosis, resolution with ≤4 days antibiotics, no pathological evidence at surgery/autopsy |

> [!tip]
> **TOE superior to TTE** (sensitivity ~95% vs ~65% for vegetations). **Repeat TOE in 7–10d if initial negative but high suspicion.**

## 4. Microbiology — Aetiology by Clinical Scenario
| Scenario | Common Organisms | % |
|----------|------------------|---|
| **Native valve (community-acquired)** | *Streptococcus viridans* (20–30%), *S. gallolyticus (bovis)* (5–10%), *Staphylococcus aureus* (20–30%), *Enterococcus* (5–10%), HACEK (1–3%) | ~80% |
| **Native valve (healthcare-associated)** | *S. aureus* (MRSA/MSSA), *Enterococcus*, CoNS, Gram-negatives | |
| **Prosthetic valve (early <1y)** | CoNS (30–40%), *S. aureus*, Gram-negatives (*Pseudomonas*, *Serratia*), *Candida*, *Cutibacterium* | |
| **Prosthetic valve (late >1y)** | Similar to NVE: *Viridans strep*, *S. gallolyticus*, *Enterococcus*, *S. aureus* | |
| **IVDU (right-sided)** | *S. aureus* (60–80%), *Enterococcus*, *Streptococci*, Gram-negatives, *Candida* | |
| **Cardiac device-related** | CoNS (50–70%), *S. aureus*, *Enterococcus*, Gram-negatives, *Cutibacterium* | |
| **Culture-negative** | Prior antibiotics (40%), *Bartonella*, *Coxiella* (Q fever), *Brucella*, *Tropheryma whipplei*, *Mycoplasma*, *Chlamydia*, *Legionella*, fungi, *Mycobacteria* | |

> [!important]
> ***S. gallolyticus* (bovis) bacteraemia/IE → MANDATORY COLONOSCOPY** (association with colonic adenoma/carcinoma 25–50%). **HACEK** = *Haemophilus, Aggregatibacter, Cardiobacterium, Eikenella, Kingella* — subacute, often culture-negative if not held long enough.

## 5. Blood Culture Optimisation
| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| **Sets** | **3 sets** (each set = 1 aerobic + 1 anaerobic bottle) from **different venipuncture sites** |
| **Volume** | **20–30mL per set** (10mL per bottle) — low volume ↓ sensitivity |
| **Timing** | **Before antibiotics**; if already on abx → culture at trough, hold abx if possible |
| **Incubation** | **14 days** (standard 5d misses HACEK, *Bartonella*, *Brucella*, *Coxiella*, *T. whipplei*) |
| **Special requests** | Prolonged incubation, lysis-centrifugation (fungi, mycobacteria), PCR/16S rRNA, serology (Coxiella phase I/II, Bartonella, Brucella) |

## 6. Clinical Features
| Feature | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|
| **Fever** | 90–95% |
| **New/Changed murmur** | 85–90% |
| **Fatigue, weight loss, anorexia** | 70–80% |
| **Embolic phenomena** | 20–50% (stroke, splenic infarct, mesenteric ischaemia, mycotic aneurysm) |
| **Immunological phenomena** | 10–20% (Osler nodes, Roth spots, Janeway lesions, glomerulonephritis) |
| **Splenomegaly** | 30–50% |
| **Heart failure** | 30–40% (acute AR/MR, valve perforation) |
| **Conduction abnormalities** | 5–10% (PR prolongation, AV block = perivalvular abscess) |

### Peripheral Signs (Classic but Uncommon)
| Sign | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Osler nodes** | Tender, erythematous nodules on pulp of fingers/toes (immune complex) |
| **Janeway lesions** | Non-tender, haemorrhagic macules on palms/soles (septic emboli) |
| **Roth spots** | Retinal haemorrhages with pale centres |
| **Splinter haemorrhages** | Linear subungual haemorrhages |
| **Conjunctival haemorrhages** | Pale conjunctivae with petechiae |

## 7. Echocardiography
| Modality | Sensitivity | Role |
|----------|-------------|------|
| **TTE** | ~65% | First-line; assess valve function, haemodynamics, complications |
| **TOE** | ~95% | **Definitive**; vegetations <5mm, abscess, fistula, prosthetic dehiscence, prosthetic valve detail |
| **Repeat TOE** | — | 7–10d if initial negative + high suspicion; or new complication |

> [!tip]
> **Vegetation size >10mm = higher embolic risk.** **Mobile/pedunculated > sessile.** **Abscess = perivalvular extension → conduction abnormalities, fistula, dehiscence.**

## 8. Empirical Antibiotic Therapy (Before Culture Results)
| Clinical Scenario | Regimen | Notes |
|-------------------|---------|-------|
| **Native valve (community-acquired)** | **Ceftriaxone 2g IV 12h + Vancomycin 15–20mg/kg IV 6h** (+/- Gentamicin 3mg/kg IV OD ×2w) | Covers viridans strep, *S. gallolyticus*, *S. aureus*, *Enterococcus*; gentamicin for synergy (optional, nephrotoxicity) |
| **Native valve (healthcare-associated / IVDU)** | **Vancomycin 15–20mg/kg IV 6h + Ceftriaxone 2g IV 12h + Gentamicin 3mg/kg IV OD** | MRSA coverage; gentamicin for *Enterococcus* synergy if suspected |
| **Prosthetic valve (early <1y)** | **Vancomycin 15–20mg/kg 6h + Meropenem 2g 8h + Rifampicin 300mg PO/IV 12h + Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×2w** | CoNS, *S. aureus*, Gram-neg; rifampicin for biofilm |
| **Prosthetic valve (late >1y)** | **Ceftriaxone 2g 12h + Vancomycin 15–20mg/kg 6h + Rifampicin 300mg 12h + Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×2w** | Similar to NVE + rifampicin |
| **Right-sided IE (IVDU, tricuspid)** | **Ceftriaxone 2g 12h + Flucloxacillin 2g 4h (or Cefazolin) + Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×2w** (+/- Vancomycin if MRSA risk) | *S. aureus* dominant; shorter course possible (2–4w) |
| **Penicillin allergy (anaphylaxis)** | **Vancomycin + Meropenem + Gentamicin + Rifampicin** (avoid cephalosporins) | |
| **Culture-negative (subacute, community)** | **Ceftriaxone 2g 12h + Vancomycin 15–20mg/kg 6h + Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×2w** | Add doxycycline 100mg 12h if *Bartonella/Coxiella* suspected |

## 9. Targeted Therapy — Duration & Regimens
| Organism / Scenario | Regimen | Duration |
|---------------------|---------|----------|
| **Viridans streptococci / S. gallolyticus (NVE, susceptible)** | **Penicillin G 24MU/day IV ÷4–6h OR Ceftriaxone 2g IV 12h** + **Gentamicin 3mg/kg IV OD ×2w** (synergy for shorter course) | **4 weeks** (2w if uncomplicated + gentamicin) |
| **Viridans streptococci (penicillin MIC 0.12–0.5)** | Ceftriaxone 2g 12h + Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×4–6w | 4–6 weeks |
| **Enterococcus faecalis (NVE, susceptible)** | **Ampicillin 12g/day IV ÷4–6h + Ceftriaxone 2g 12h** (preferred, less nephrotoxicity) OR **Ampicillin + Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD** | **6 weeks** (4w if ampicillin-ceftriaxone) |
| **Enterococcus faecalis (high-level aminoglycoside resistance)** | Ampicillin 12g/day IV ÷4–6h + Ceftriaxone 2g 12h | 6 weeks |
| **Enterococcus faecium (usually ampicillin-resistant)** | **Vancomycin 15–20mg/kg 6h + Daptomycin 8–10mg/kg IV OD** (or Linezolid) | 6 weeks |
| **S. aureus MSSA (NVE)** | **Flucloxacillin 2g IV 4h (or Cefazolin 2g 8h)** + **Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×2w** | **6 weeks** (NVE); 4w if uncomplicated right-sided |
| **S. aureus MRSA (NVE)** | **Vancomycin 15–20mg/kg 6h (trough 15–20)** + **Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×2w** | 6 weeks |
| **S. aureus (PVE)** | **Vancomycin + Rifampicin 300mg 12h + Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×2w** (MSSA: flucloxacillin/cefazolin instead of vancomycin) | **6 weeks** |
| **CoNS (PVE)** | **Vancomycin + Rifampicin 300mg 12h + Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×2w** | 6 weeks |
| **HACEK** | Ceftriaxone 2g 12h | 4 weeks |
| **Culture-negative (suspected Bartonella)** | Ceftriaxone 2g 12h + Doxycycline 100mg 12h + Gentamicin 3mg/kg OD ×2w | 6 weeks |
| **Culture-negative (suspected Coxiella / Q fever)** | **Doxycycline 100mg 12h + Hydroxychloroquine 200mg 8h** (raise phagosomal pH) | **18 months** |
| **Fungal (Candida)** | **Liposomal Amphotericin B 3–5mg/kg + Flucytosine 25mg/kg 6h** → step-down fluconazole/echinocandin | **6 weeks post-valve replacement** (surgery mandatory) |

> [!key]
> **Key Principle: Rifampicin ESSENTIAL for PVE and device-related IE** (biofilm penetration). **Gentamicin synergy shortens course for strep/Enterococcus but nephrotoxicity risk.** **Ampicillin-ceftriaxone preferred over ampicillin-gentamicin for E. faecalis.**

## 10. Surgical Indications (ESC 2017 / AHA 2015)
### **Class I (Strong Recommendation)**
| Indication | Timing |
|------------|--------|
| **Heart failure** due to valve dysfunction (acute severe AR/MR, dehiscence) | **URGENT / EMERGENCY** |
| **Uncontrolled infection**: persistent fever/bacteraemia >5–7d appropriate antibiotics, abscess, fistula, enlarging vegetation | **URGENT** (within days) |
| **Recurrent emboli** despite appropriate antibiotics + large (>10mm) mobile vegetation | **URGENT** |
| **Fungal / multidrug-resistant organism** IE | **URGENT** |

### **Class IIa (Reasonable)**
| Indication | Timing |
|------------|--------|
| **Large vegetation >10mm** + other high-risk features (embolic, severe valve stenosis) | **ELECTIVE** (after 1–2w antibiotics if stable) |
| **Vegetation >15mm** (high embolic risk) | Consider early surgery |
| **Perivalvular abscess / fistula** without HF | **URGENT / ELECTIVE** |
| **Severe valve stenosis** | **ELECTIVE** |

### **Neurological Complications & Surgery Timing**
| Neurological Status | Surgery Timing |
|---------------------|----------------|
| **Ischaemic stroke WITHOUT haemorrhage** | **Delay NOT required** — surgery if indicated (Class IIa) |
| **Ischaemic stroke WITH haemorrhagic transformation** | Delay **≥4 weeks** (if possible) |
| **Intracranial haemorrhage** | Delay **≥4 weeks** |
| **Brain abscess / mycotic aneurysm** | Multidisciplinary; often delay surgery |

## 11. Complications
| Complication | Features | Management |
|--------------|----------|------------|
| **Heart failure** | Acute AR/MR, valve perforation, chordal rupture | **Urgent surgery** |
| **Perivalvular abscess** | New conduction defect (PR↑, AV block), fistula, dehiscence | Surgery (often urgent); TOE for diagnosis |
| **Embolic events** | Stroke (MCA), splenic infarct, mesenteric ischaemia, mycotic aneurysm, septic pulmonary emboli (right-sided) | Anticoagulation generally AVOIDED; surgery if recurrent/large veg |
| **Septic metastatic foci** | Osteomyelitis, discitis, septic arthritis, endophthalmitis, renal infarcts | Source control + antibiotics |
| **Glomerulonephritis** | Immune complex; haematuria, proteinuria, RBC casts | Resolves with antibiotic treatment |
| **Renal embolism / infarct** | Flank pain, haematuria | Supportive |

## 12. Special Situations
| Situation | Key Points |
|-----------|------------|
| **Prosthetic valve IE** | Rifampicin mandatory (biofilm); early (<1y) = nosocomial flora; late (>1y) = community flora; surgery often needed |
| **Cardiac device-related IE** | **Device removal mandatory** if pocket infection, S. aureus, Gram-neg, fungaemia, sepsis; lead extraction (laser/mechanical); antibiotics 2–6w post-removal |
| **Right-sided IE (IVDU)** | *S. aureus* tricuspid; shorter course (2–4w) if uncomplicated; septic pulmonary emboli common; surgery rare |
| **Culture-negative IE** | Prior antibiotics (stop 2w if possible); serology (Coxiella phase I IgG >1:800, Bartonella, Brucella); 16S rRNA PCR on valve tissue; PET-CT for metastatic foci |
| **HIV** | Same organisms; *S. aureus* more common; drug interactions (rifampicin + ART); immune reconstitution |
| **Pregnancy** | Avoid aminoglycosides (otic toxicity), fluoroquinolones, doxycycline; ceftriaxone, vancomycin, ampicillin safe; TOE safe; surgery if indicated |

## 13. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- **Modified Duke Criteria: 2 Major / 1M+3m / 5m = definite; 1M+1m / 3m = possible**
- **Blood cultures: 3 sets, 20–30mL/set, 14-day incubation, BEFORE antibiotics**
- **TOE > TTE for vegetations, abscess, prosthetic detail**
- **NVE empirical: Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin (+/- Gentamicin)**
- **PVE empirical: Vancomycin + Meropenem + Rifampicin + Gentamicin (early); Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin + Rifampicin + Gentamicin (late)**
- **Rifampicin ESSENTIAL for PVE and device IE (biofilm)**
- **Targeted therapy durations: Strep 4w (2w + gentamicin), Enterococcus 6w, S. aureus 6w, PVE 6w**
- **Ampicillin-ceftriaxone for E. faecalis (less nephrotoxicity than gentamicin)**
- **S. gallolyticus (bovis) → COLONOSCOPY mandatory**
- **Surgery indications: HF (urgent), uncontrolled infection (urgent), recurrent emboli + large veg (urgent), fungal/MDR (urgent)**
- **Neurological: ischaemic stroke no haemorrhage → no delay; haemorrhage → delay 4w**
- **Device IE: REMOVE device if pocket infection, S. aureus, Gram-neg, fungaemia, sepsis**

## 14. Common Viva Questions
1. **What are the Modified Duke Criteria for definite IE?** 2 Major OR 1 Major + 3 Minor OR 5 Minor.
2. **How many blood culture sets for IE?** 3 sets (aerobic + anaerobic each) from different sites, 20–30mL/set, 14-day incubation.
3. **What is the empirical regimen for community-acquired NVE?** Ceftriaxone 2g 12h + Vancomycin 15–20mg/kg 6h (+/- Gentamicin).
4. **What is the empirical regimen for early PVE (<1y)?** Vancomycin + Meropenem + Rifampicin + Gentamicin.
5. **Why is rifampicin essential in PVE?** Biofilm penetration on prosthetic material.
