# Cytomegalovirus (CMV) Disease

Related: [[Infection in Immunocompromised Host (Transplant, Biologics)]], [[HIV Infection and AIDS]], [[Infection in Pregnancy and Neonates]], [[Fever in Returned Traveller and FUO]], [[Pneumocystis Pneumonia]], [[Viral Encephalitis and Meningitis]]

> [!important]
> **CMV = β-herpesvirus; ubiquitous (seroprevalence 50–90%).** **Primary infection usually asymptomatic; reactivation in immunocompromised = major morbidity.** **Transplant: D+/R− highest risk; peak 1–4 months.** **Diagnosis: quantitative PCR (blood); pp65 antigenemia.** **Treatment: Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir 1st-line; Foscarnet/Cidofovir if resistant.** **Prophylaxis: Valganciclovir (high-risk); Pre-emptive therapy (PCR-guided).** **Congenital CMV = most common congenital infection; hearing loss, microcephaly.**

## Learning Objectives
- Understand CMV epidemiology, transmission, and pathogenesis
- Identify clinical syndromes in immunocompetent vs immunocompromised hosts
- Apply quantitative PCR and pp65 antigenemia for diagnosis and monitoring
- Select and monitor antiviral therapy (ganciclovir, valganciclovir, foscarnet, cidofovir, maribavir)
- Implement prophylaxis vs pre-emptive strategies in transplant
- Manage congenital CMV and CMV in pregnancy

---

## Epidemiology & Transmission
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Virus** | **Human Cytomegalovirus (CMV/HHV-5)** — β-herpesvirus, double-stranded DNA |
| **Seroprevalence** | **50–90%** (increases with age, lower SES, developing countries) |
| **Transmission** | **Body fluids**: saliva, urine, blood, semen, cervical secretions, breast milk, tears; **Vertical** (transplacental, intrapartum, breast milk); **Horizontal** (close contact, sexual, transfusion, transplant) |
| **Latency** | **Lifelong** in myeloid progenitors, endothelial cells; **reactivation** with immunosuppression |
| **Reinfection** | Possible with different strains (superinfection) |

---

## Clinical Syndromes
| Host / Syndrome | Features |
|-----------------|----------|
| **Immunocompetent Primary** | **Asymptomatic (90%)**; Mononucleosis-like (fever, fatigue, pharyngitis, cervical lymphadenopathy, mild hepatosplenomegaly, atypical lymphocytosis); **resolves 2–6w** |
| **Immunocompromised (Transplant, HIV, Biologics)** | **Primary or Reactivation**; **Viraemia → Syndrome → Tissue-invasive disease** |
| **CMV Syndrome** | Fever ≥38°C ±24h + **Viraemia** (PCR/antigenemia) + ≥2: leucopenia, thrombocytopenia, ↑ ALT/AST, atypical lymphs |
| **Tissue-Invasive Disease** | **Pneumonitis** (dyspnoea, hypoxia, bilateral interstitial infiltrates); **Colitis** (diarrhoea, pain, bleeding); **Retinitis** (floaters, vision loss); **Hepatitis**; **Encephalitis**; **Nephritis** |
| **Congenital CMV** | **Most common congenital infection (0.5–1% births)**; 10–15% symptomatic at birth; **Asymptomatic → 10–15% late sequelae (hearing loss, developmental delay)**; Symptomatic: microcephaly, IUGR, petechiae, hepatosplenomegaly, thrombocytopenia, periventricular calcifications, chorioretinitis, SNHL |
| **CMV in Pregnancy** | Primary infection 30–40% transmission; **1st trimester = highest risk severe congenital disease**; 3rd trimester = highest transmission rate; **Perinatal (breast milk) → usually mild in term, severe in preterm** |

## Diagnosis
| Test | Sample | Utility | Timing |
|------|--------|---------|--------|
| **Quantitative PCR (Blood/Plasma)** | Blood | **Gold standard**; quantifies viral load; guides pre-emptive therapy; monitors response | Any time (detects viraemia before symptoms) |
| **pp65 Antigenemia** | Blood (PMNs) | Rapid; correlates with viral load; lower sensitivity than PCR | Early diagnosis; monitoring |
| **Viral Culture** | Urine, saliva, blood, CSF, tissue | **Definitive**; slow (2–4w); low sensitivity | Confirmation; resistance testing |
| **Serology (IgM/IgG)** | Serum | **Past vs recent infection**; IgM = recent/acute; IgG avidity = primary vs past | Not for acute management in immunocompromised |
| **IgG Avidity Index** | Serum | **Low = recent primary (<3–4m); High = past** | Pregnancy/congenital |
| **Histopathology (Biopsy)** | Tissue (lung, gut, retina) | **Inclusion cells** (owl's eye intranuclear inclusions) | Tissue-invasive disease confirmation |

> [!key]
> **Quantitative PCR = gold standard for diagnosis, monitoring, pre-emptive therapy.** **pp65 antigenemia = rapid alternative.** **IgG avidity = distinguishes primary vs past infection (critical in pregnancy).** **Viraemia + symptoms = CMV syndrome; add tissue involvement = tissue-invasive disease.**

---

## Definitions (Transplant Context)
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **CMV Infection** | Detection of CMV (PCR, antigenemia, culture) regardless of symptoms |
| **CMV Syndrome** | Fever ≥38°C ±24h + **viraemia** + ≥2: leucopenia, thrombocytopenia, ↑ ALT/AST, atypical lymphocytosis |
| **Tissue-Invasive CMV Disease** | CMV syndrome + **organ-specific signs** + **histopathology** (inclusion cells) or **PCR in tissue/fluid** |

## Treatment
### Antivirals
| Drug | Dose (Adult) | Indication | Key Adverse Effects |
|------|--------------|------------|---------------------|
| **Valganciclovir** | **900mg PO BD** (induction) → **900mg PO OD** (maintenance) | **1st-line oral**; equivalent to IV ganciclovir | **Neutropenia (20–40%)**, thrombocytopenia, GI, renal adjust |
| **Ganciclovir (IV)** | **5mg/kg IV 12h** (induction) → **5mg/kg IV OD** (maintenance) | **1st-line IV** (severe, unable oral) | **Neutropenia**, thrombocytopenia, renal adjust, **teratogenic** |
| **Foscarnet** | **90mg/kg IV 12h** (induction) → **90–120mg/kg IV OD** (maintenance) | **Resistant CMV (UL97/UL54 mut)**; ganciclovir intolerance | **Nephrotoxicity**, electrolytes (Ca, Mg, K, Phos), QTc, seizures |
| **Cidofovir** | **5mg/kg IV weekly** (induction 2w) → **5mg/kg IV q2w** (maintenance) | **Resistant CMV**; foscarnet intolerance | **Nephrotoxicity** (probenecid + hydration mandatory); ocular |
| **Maribavir** | **400mg PO BD** | **Refractory/resistant (UL97 mut)**; post-transplant | Taste disturbance, nausea, ↑ tacrolimus levels |
| **Leflunomide** | 20mg PO OD (load 100mg day 1) | Adjunct/salvage (BK virus + CMV) | Hepatotoxicity, diarrhoea, alopecia |

> [!critical]
> **Valganciclovir 1st-line oral (bioavailability 60% = IV ganciclovir).** **Foscarnet/Cidofovir/Maribavir for resistant CMV (UL97/UL54 mutations).** **Monitor: CBC 2×/w ×2w, then weekly; renal function; CMV PCR weekly ×2w, then q1–2w.**

### Treatment Regimens by Syndrome
| Syndrome | Induction | Maintenance | Duration |
|----------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **CMV Syndrome / Mild Disease** | Valganciclovir 900mg PO BD | Valganciclovir 900mg PO OD | **2–3 weeks** (until viraemia cleared ×2) |
| **Tissue-Invasive Disease** (Pneumonitis, Colitis, Retinitis) | Ganciclovir 5mg/kg IV 12h **OR** Valganciclovir 900mg PO BD | Valganciclovir 900mg PO OD | **3–6 weeks** (until clinical resolution + viraemia cleared) |
| **Congenital CMV (Symptomatic)** | Ganciclovir 6mg/kg IV 12h **OR** Valganciclovir 16mg/kg PO BD | — | **6 weeks** (improves hearing/developmental outcomes) |
| **Resistant CMV (UL97 mut)** | **Maribavir 400mg PO BD** OR Foscarnet | — | 3–6 weeks |
| **Resistant CMV (UL54 mut)** | Foscarnet 90mg/kg IV 12h | — | 3–6 weeks |

## CMV in Transplant
### Risk Stratification (D/R Serostatus)
| D/R Status | Risk | Strategy |
|------------|------|----------|
| **D+/R−** | **Highest** (primary infection) | **Prophylaxis** (Valganciclovir 900mg OD ×3–6m) **OR** Pre-emptive (weekly PCR ×3–6m) |
| **D+/R+** | Intermediate (reactivation + reinfection) | **Pre-emptive** (weekly PCR ×3–6m) |
| **D−/R+** | Intermediate (reactivation) | **Pre-emptive** (weekly PCR ×3–6m) |
| **D−/R−** | Low (community acquisition) | Pre-emptive surveillance |

> [!key]
> **Prophylaxis (Valganciclovir 900mg OD) for D+/R− high-risk (lung, heart, intestine, HSCT) ×3–6m.** **Pre-emptive = weekly PCR ×3–6m; treat if viral load > threshold (e.g., >1000 IU/mL or rising).** **Prophylaxis delays but does not eliminate CMV.**

### Pre-emptive Therapy Thresholds
| Parameter | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Viral Load** | **>1000–2000 IU/mL** (or >1000 copies/mL) |
| **Rising Trend** | **>1 log increase** in 1 week |
| **Antigenemia** | **>10–20 pp65+ cells/200,000 PMNs** |

## CMV in Pregnancy & Congenital CMV
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Infection Rate** | 1–4% pregnancies |
| **Vertical Transmission** | **Primary: 30–40%** (1st tri ≈30%, 2nd ≈40%, 3rd ≈70%); **Recurrent: 1–2%** |
| **Congenital CMV Sequelae** | **Symptomatic (10–15%)**: microcephaly, IUGR, petechiae, hepatosplenomegaly, thrombocytopenia, periventricular calcifications, chorioretinitis, **SNHL (most common)**; **Asymptomatic (85–90%)**: **10–15% late SNHL, developmental delay** |
| **Diagnosis in Neonate** | **Urine/Saliva PCR (birth–3w)** — gold standard; IgM low sensitivity |
| **Treatment (Symptomatic Neonate)** | **Ganciclovir 6mg/kg IV 12h ×6w** OR **Valganciclovir 16mg/kg PO BD ×6w** → improves hearing/neurodevelopment |
| **Primary Infection in Pregnancy** | **IgG avidity low = recent primary**; amniocentesis PCR (amniotic fluid) for fetal infection; consider valganciclovir (experimental) |

> [!critical]
> **Congenital CMV = most common congenital infection.** **Urine PCR at birth = gold standard.** **Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir ×6w for symptomatic neonates improves hearing/neurodevelopment.** **IgG avidity distinguishes primary vs past infection in pregnancy.**

## Resistance
| Mutation | Mechanism | Cross-Resistance | Treatment |
|----------|-----------|------------------|-----------|
| **UL97 (Phosphotransferase)** | Impaired ganciclovir phosphorylation | Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir | **Maribavir** (UL97 inhibitor) or **Foscarnet/Cidofovir** |
| **UL54 (DNA Polymerase)** | Impaired drug binding | Ganciclovir, Foscarnet, Cidofovir | **Maribavir** (UL97 target intact) **OR** **Leflunomide** (adjunct) |

---

## Prophylaxis vs Pre-emptive in Transplant
| Strategy | Pros | Cons | Preferred For |
|----------|------|------|---------------|
| **Prophylaxis** (Valganciclovir 900mg OD ×3–6m) | Prevents early CMV; simple | Delayed onset after stop; drug toxicity; cost; resistance if breakthrough | **D+/R− high-risk (lung, heart, intestine, HSCT)** |
| **Pre-emptive** (Weekly PCR ×3–6m, treat if >threshold) | Avoids unnecessary drug exposure; targets only those needing it | Requires infrastructure; missed cases if PCR missed; labour-intensive | **Standard-risk (D+/R+, D−/R+, D−/R−); D+/R− low-risk** |

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- **CMV = β-herpesvirus; ubiquitous; latency in myeloid cells; reactivation in immunocompromised**
- **Transplant D+/R− = highest risk; peak 1–4m; prophylaxis (valganciclovir) OR pre-emptive (PCR-guided)**
- **CMV Syndrome = fever + viraemia + (leucopenia/thrombocytopenia/↑ALT/atypical lymphs)**
- **Tissue-invasive = pneumonitis, colitis, retinitis, hepatitis, encephalitis**
- **Diagnosis: Quantitative PCR (gold standard), pp65 antigenemia, IgG avidity (pregnancy)**
- **Treatment: Valganciclovir 1st-line (oral); Ganciclovir IV (severe); Foscarnet/Cidofovir/Maribavir for resistant**
- **CMV Syndrome: Valganciclovir 900mg BD ×2–3w; Tissue-invasive: IV ganciclovir/Valganciclovir 3–6w**
- **Resistance: UL97 mut = ganciclovir resistance → maribavir/foscarnet; UL54 mut = foscarnet/cidofovir resistance**
- **Transplant D+/R−: Prophylaxis (valganciclovir 900mg OD ×3–6m) OR Pre-emptive (weekly PCR)**
- **Congenital CMV: most common congenital infection; urine PCR at birth; ganciclovir/valganciclovir ×6w for symptomatic**
- **IgG avidity low = recent primary infection (pregnancy); high = past**

## Common Viva Questions
1. **Highest risk transplant for CMV?** D+/R− (donor positive, recipient negative).
2. **CMV Syndrome definition?** Fever + viraemia + ≥2 of: leucopenia, thrombocytopenia, ↑ALT/AST, atypical lymphs.
3. **First-line treatment for CMV?** Valganciclovir 900mg PO BD (induction) → OD (maintenance).
3. **When to use foscarnet?** Resistant CMV (UL97/UL54 mutations) or ganciclovir intolerance.
4. **Congenital CMV diagnosis?** Urine/saliva PCR at birth (gold standard).
5. **Congenital CMV treatment?** Ganciclovir 6mg/kg IV 12h OR Valganciclovir 16mg/kg BD ×6w for symptomatic.
5. **Prophylaxis vs Pre-emptive in transplant?** D+/R− = prophylaxis OR pre-emptive; others = pre-emptive.
6. **IgG avidity in pregnancy?** Low = recent primary (<3–4m); high = past infection.
7. **UL97 mutation?** Ganciclovir resistance → use maribavir or foscarnet.
7. **CMV retinitis treatment?** Ganciclovir IV + intravitreal ganciclovir/foscarnet; valganciclovir alternative.
8. **CMV pneumonitis treatment?** Ganciclovir IV 5mg/kg 12h ×3–6w +/− IVIG.

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps
| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| CMV IgM = acute infection in transplant | **IgM unreliable in immunocompromised; use PCR/viral load** |
| Valganciclovir = ganciclovir prodrug, same dose | **900mg valganciclovir PO ≈ 5mg/kg IV ganciclovir** |
| CMV IgM = acute infection in immunocompetent | **IgG avidity needed** to distinguish primary vs past |
| Prophylaxis eliminates CMV | **Delays only; breakthrough occurs after stop; pre-emptive still needed** |
| All CMV infections need treatment | **Asymptomatic viraemia in immunocompetent = no treatment** |
| Foscarnet = 2nd line for all | **Only for resistant (UL97/UL54) or ganciclovir intolerance** |
| CMV retinitis = only in AIDS | **Also in transplant, steroids; treat with intravitreal + systemic** |
| Congenital CMV = always symptomatic | **85–90% asymptomatic at birth; 10–15% late SNHL** |
| CMV prophylaxis prevents all CMV | **Delays only; pre-emptive still needed after stop** |
| Valganciclovir = same dose IV/PO | **PO 900mg BD (induction) / OD (maintenance); IV ganciclovir 5mg/kg 12h/OD** |

## Summary
**Cytomegalovirus (CMV)** is a ubiquitous **β-herpesvirus (HHV-5)** establishing **lifelong latency in myeloid progenitors**; **reactivation** causes disease in **immunocompromised** hosts. **Transplant risk stratification**: **D+/R− (donor positive/recipient negative) = highest risk** (primary infection); peak **1–4 months**; **D+/R+ and D−/R+** = intermediate (reactivation/reinfection); **D−/R−** = low. **Clinical syndromes**: **Immunocompetent** — asymptomatic (90%) or mononucleosis-like; **Immunocompromised** — **CMV Syndrome** (fever + viraemia + ≥2: leucopenia, thrombocytopenia, ↑ALT, atypical lymphocytes) → **Tissue-invasive disease** (pneumonitis, colitis, retinitis, hepatitis, encephalitis, nephritis). **Diagnosis**: **Quantitative PCR (blood) = gold standard**; **pp65 antigenemia** (rapid); **IgG avidity** (low = recent primary <3–4m, high = past — critical in pregnancy); **histology** = owl's eye intranuclear inclusions. **Treatment**: **1st-line oral = Valganciclovir 900mg PO BD (induction) → 900mg OD (maintenance)**;
