# Schistosomiasis (Bilharzia)

> [!info] **Davidson Ch 11 Alignment**: Infectious Disease → Specific Organism Groups → Parasites (Helminths) → Schistosomiasis
> **FCPS/MRCP Focus**: S. haematobium (Urinary), S. mansoni/japonicum (Intestinal/Hepatic), acute Katayama fever, chronic hepatic/intestinal/urinary, praziquantel, egg detection, hepatosplenic disease

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Identify **Schistosome Species**: *S. haematobium* (Urinary), *S. mansoni* (Intestinal/Hepatic), *S. japonicum* (Intestinal/Hepatic, Zoonotic), *S. intercalatum*, *S. mekongi*
- [ ] Understand **Life Cycle**: Snail Intermediate Host → Cercariae → Skin Penetration → Schistosomula → Portal Venous System → Adult Worms → Egg Migration
- [ ] Recognise **Acute (Katayama Fever)**: Fever, Cough, Hepatosplenomegaly, Eosinophilia (Weeks Post-Exposure)
- [ ] Recognise **Chronic Intestinal (S. mansoni/japonicum)**: Abdominal Pain, Bloody Diarrhoea, Hepatosplenomegaly, **Periportal Fibrosis (Pipestem Fibrosis)**, Portal Hypertension
- [ ] Recognise **Chronic Urinary (S. haematobium)**: **Terminal Haematuria**, Bladder Pathology, **SCC Risk**, Hydronephrosis, Renal Failure
- [ ] Diagnose: **Egg Detection (Urine/Stool)**, **Serology (ELISA/IHA)**, **PCR**, **Ultrasound** (Liver/Spleen/Bladder), **Cystoscopy**
- [ ] Manage: **Praziquantel 40mg/kg Single Dose** (1st Line All Species), **Oxamniquine** (S. mansoni Alternative), **Corticosteroids** (Acute Katayama)
- [ ] Recognise **Complications**: Portal Hypertension, Variceal Bleeding, Splenomegaly, Bladder Cancer (SCC), Glomerulonephritis, Pulmonary Hypertension

## 📖 Life Cycle & Transmission

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Eggs in Water (Urine/Stool)] --> B[Miracidium Hatches] --> C[Snail Intermediate Host (Specific per Species)]
    C --> D[Sporocyst → Cercariae Released] --> E[Cercariae Penetrate Human Skin]
    E --> F[Schistosomula → Lung → Liver → Portal Vein]
    F --> G[Adult Worms Mate in Mesenteric/Pelvic Veins]
    G --> H1[S. haematobium → Vesical Plexus → Eggs in Urine]
    G --> H2[S. mansoni/japonicum → Mesenteric Veins → Eggs in Stool]
    H1 & H2 --> I[Eggs → Tissue Trapping (Granulomas) OR Excreted in Urine/Stool]
```

| Species | Adult Location | Egg Excretion | Geographic Distribution |
|---------|----------------|---------------|------------------------|
| **S. haematobium** | Vesical/Pelvic Venous Plexus | **Urine** (Terminal Haematuria) | **Africa, Middle East** |
| **S. mansoni** | Inferior Mesenteric Veins | **Stool** | **Africa, Middle East, South America, Caribbean** |
| **S. japonicum** | Superior Mesenteric Veins | **Stool** | **China, Philippines, Philippines, Indonesia** |
| **S. intercalatum** | Mesenteric Veins | Stool | Central/West Africa |
| **S. mekongi** | Mesenteric Veins | Stool | Mekong River Basin (Laos/Cambodia) |

> [!tip] **S. haematobium = Urinary (Terminal Haematuria)**; **S. mansoni/japonicum = Intestinal/Hepatic (Stool Eggs)**. **Snail = Intermediate Host** (Species-Specific).

## 📖 Clinical Syndromes

### 1. Acute Schistosomiasis (Katayama Fever)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Timing** | **4-8 Weeks** Post-Exposure (Coincides with Egg Laying) |
| **Symptoms** | **High Fever**, **Cough**, **Hepatosplenomegaly**, **Eosinophilia** (Marked), **Rash**, **Abdominal Pain**, **Diarrhoea** |
| **Pathophysiology** | **Immune Complex Reaction** to Egg Antigens (Type III Hypersensitivity) |
| **Species** | **S. japonicum > S. mansoni > S. haematobium** |

### 2. Chronic Intestinal / Hepatic Schistosomiasis (S. mansoni, S. japonicum)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Intestinal** | **Abdominal Pain**, **Bloody/Mucus Diarrhoea**, **Tenesmus**, **Rectal Prolapse** (Heavy Infection) |
| **Hepatic (Hepatosplenic)** | **Periportal Fibrosis (Pipestem Fibrosis)** → **Portal Hypertension**, **Splenomegaly**, **Oesophageal Varices**, **Ascites**, **Thrombocytopenia** |
| **S. japonicum** | **More Severe**, **Higher Egg Output**, **More Hepatic Involvement**, **Zoonotic (Cattle/Water Buffalo)** |

### 3. Urinary Schistosomiasis (S. haematobium)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Cardinal Sign** | **Terminal Haematuria** (Painless, End of Micturition) |
| **Bladder Pathology** | **Sandy Patches**, **Granulomas**, **Ulceration**, **Calcification**, **Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)** Risk ↑ |
| **Upper Tract** | **Ureteric Strictures**, **Hydronephrosis**, **Renal Failure** |
| **Genital** | **Seminal Vesicle/Prostate Involvement**, **Haematospermia**, **Infertility** |

## 🔬 Diagnosis

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Suspected Schistosomiasis] --> B{**Species / Clinical Syndrome**}
    B -->|**Urinary (Haematuria)**| C[**Urine Microscopy (Terminal Urine)**]
    C --> C1[**Eggs with Terminal Spine** (S. haematobium)]
    B -->|**Intestinal / Hepatic**| D[**Stool Microscopy (Kato-Katz)**]
    D --> D1[**Lateral Spine Egg** (S. mansoni) / **Lateral Knob** (S. japonicum)]
    B -->|**All**| E[**Serology (ELISA/IHA/IFA)**]
    B -->|**All**| F[**Imaging**]
    F --> F1[**US Liver/Spleen** (Portal Hypertension, Bladder Wall Thickening)]
    F --> F2[**Cystoscopy** (Bladder Lesions)]
```

### Diagnostic Methods

| Method | Sample | Sensitivity/Specificity | Role |
|--------|--------|------------------------|------|
| **Urine Microscopy (Terminal)** | **Terminal Urine** | **High (S. haematobium)** | **1st Line (Urinary)** |
| **Stool Microscopy (Kato-Katz)** | **Stool** | **Quantitative (EPG)** | **1st Line (Intestinal)** |
| **Urine Filtration** | Urine | Concentrates Eggs | Field-Friendly |
| **Serology (ELISA/IHA/IFA)** | Serum | **High Sens/Spec** | **Travellers, Low Parasite Load** |
| **PCR (Urine/Stool/Blood)** | Various | **High Sens/Spec** | **Low Parasite Load, Species ID** |
| **Ultrasound (Liver/Spleen/Bladder)** | — | **Portal Hypertension, Bladder Wall** | **Morbidity Assessment** |
| **Cystoscopy** | Bladder | Visualize Lesions | Bladder Pathology Assessment |

> [!tip] **Urine Microscopy (Terminal) = S. haematobium**. **Stool Kato-Katz = S. mansoni/japonicum**. **Serology = Travellers/Low Burden**. **Ultrasound = Morbidity Assessment (Portal Hypertension, Bladder)**.

## 💊 Management

### Praziquantel — **Drug of Choice for ALL Species**

| Species | Dose | Schedule |
|---------|------|----------|
| **S. haematobium / S. mansoni / S. intercalatum** | **40mg/kg Single Dose** | **Single Dose** |
| **S. japonicum / S. mekongi** | **60mg/kg** (Divided 2-3 Doses) | **Single Day** |
| **Acute Katayama Fever** | **Praziquantel 40-60mg/kg** + **Prednisolone 1mg/kg** (Anti-Inflammatory) | **Single Dose + Steroids 5-7 Days** |

> [!warning] **Praziquantel = Drug of Choice for ALL Schistosome Species**. **Single Dose (40-60mg/kg)**. **Give with Food** (↑ Absorption). **Corticosteroids for Acute Katayama** (Anti-Inflammatory).

### Oxamniquine — **Alternative for S. mansoni Only**

| Indication | Dose | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **S. mansoni (Praziquantel Failure/Allergy)** | **15mg/kg Single Dose** (Up to 40mg/kg) | **Only for S. mansoni** |

### Chronic Complications Management

| Complication | Management |
|--------------|------------|
| **Portal Hypertension / Varices** | **Beta-Blockers**, **Endoscopic Band Ligation**, **TIPS**, **Splenectomy** |
| **Bladder Cancer (SCC)** | **Cystoscopic Surveillance**, **TURBT**, **Radical Cystectomy** |
| **Glomerulonephritis** | **ACEi/ARB**, **Immunosuppression** (If Nephrotic), **Praziquantel** |
| **Pulmonary Hypertension** | **Anticoagulation**, **PDE5 Inhibitors**, **Prostacyclin**, **Lung Transplant** (End-Stage) |
| **Neuroschistosomiasis** | **Praziquantel + Corticosteroids** (Dexamethasone) |

## ❓ Viva Questions

1. **What is the drug of choice for all Schistosome species?**
   - **Praziquantel 40mg/kg Single Dose** (60mg/kg for S. japonicum).

2. **How do you differentiate Schistosome species by egg morphology?**
   - **S. haematobium = Terminal Spine (Urine)**; **S. mansoni = Lateral Spine (Stool)**; **S. japonicum = Lateral Knob/Small Spine (Stool)**.

3. **What is Katayama Fever and how is it managed?**
   - **Acute Serum Sickness-Like Illness** (Fever, Cough, Hepatosplenomegaly, Eosinophilia) **4-8 Weeks Post-Exposure** → **Praziquantel + Prednisolone 1mg/kg**.

4. **What is the clinical presentation of S. haematobium infection?**
   - **Terminal Haematuria** (Painless, End of Micturition), **Bladder Pathology (Sandy Patches, Granulomas)**, **SCC Risk**.

5. **What is the pathology of chronic S. mansoni infection?**
   - **Periportal Fibrosis (Pipestem Fibrosis)** → **Portal Hypertension** → **Splenomegaly, Oesophageal Varices, Ascites**.

5. **What is the egg morphology of S. haematobium and where is it found?**
   - **Terminal Spine**, **Found in Urine (Terminal Specimen Best)**.

6. **What are the complications of chronic S. haematobium infection?**
   - **Bladder Cancer (SCC)**, **Ureteric Stricture → Hydronephrosis → Renal Failure**, **Glomerulonephritis**.

6. **How do you differentiate S. mansoni from S. japonicum clinically?**
   - **S. japonicum = More Severe, Higher Egg Output, Zoonotic (Cattle/Water Buffalo), Lateral Knob Egg**.

7. **What is the treatment for Katayama Fever?**
   - **Praziquantel 40-60mg/kg + Prednisolone 1mg/kg/day × 5-7 Days**.

8. **What is the diagnostic method of choice for S. haematobium?**
   - **Urine Microscopy (Terminal Urine) for Terminal Spine Eggs**; **Filtration Concentrates Eggs**.

9. **What is the treatment for S. japonicum infection?**
   - **Praziquantel 60mg/kg (Divided 2-3 Doses) Single Day**.

10. **What is the "Pipestem Fibrosis" in Schistosomiasis?**
    - **Periportal Fibrosis** radiating from Portal Vein → **Portal Hypertension** → **Splenomegaly, Varices**.

## 🧠 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **S. haematobium vs S. mansoni vs S. japonicum** | **Haematobium = Urine + Terminal Spine**; **Mansoni = Stool + Lateral Spine**; **Japonicum = Stool + Lateral Knob** |
| **Katayama vs Acute Schistosomiasis** | **Same** — Acute Serum Sickness-Like Reaction ~4-8 Weeks Post-Infection |
| **Praziquantel vs Oxamniquine** | **Praziquantel = All Species**; **Oxamniquine = S. mansoni Only** |
| **S. mansoni vs S. japonicum Egg** | **Lateral Spine vs Lateral Knob** |
| **S. haematobium vs Bladder Cancer** | **Chronic Infection → SCC Risk**; **Cystoscopy Surveillance** |

| Mnemonic | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **"Haematobium = Haematuria + Terminal Spine"** | Urinary Schisto |
| **"Mansoni = Mansoni = Stool + Lateral Spine"** | Intestinal Schisto |
| **"Japonicum = Japan/Asia + Lateral Knob + Severe"** | Japanese Schisto |
| **"Praziquantel = 40mg/kg (All), 60mg/kg (Japonicum)"** | Dosing |
| **"Katayama = Fever + Cough + Hepatosplenomegaly + Eosinophilia"** | Acute Schisto |
| **"Pipestem Fibrosis = Portal Hypertension"** | Chronic Hepatic |
