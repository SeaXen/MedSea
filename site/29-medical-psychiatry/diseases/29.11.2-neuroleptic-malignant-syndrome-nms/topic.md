# Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS)

---

## Definition

Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS) is a life-threatening idiosyncratic reaction to antipsychotics (dopamine antagonists) characterised by hyperthermia, muscle rigidity, autonomic instability, and altered mental status.

## Core Clinical Content

### NMS tetrad
Hyperthermia, muscle rigidity ('lead pipe'), autonomic instability, altered consciousness

### Onset
Hours to days after antipsychotic change (rapid dose increase, parenteral)

### Risk factors
High-potency typical antipsychotics, rapid dose escalation, dehydration, NMS history

### Investigations
↑ CK (often >1000), ↑ WCC, ↓ Na, ↑ K (rhabdomyolysis), abnormal LFTs, myoglobinuria

### Differential
Serotonin syndrome, malignant hyperthermia, anticholinergic toxicity, catatonia

### Serotonin vs NMS
Serotonin: hyperreflexia, clonus, on serotonergic drugs; NMS: rigidity, bradyreflexia, on antipsychotics

### Treatment
STOP antipsychotic; supportive care (ICU); dantrolene, bromocriptine, ECT in severe

### Dantrolene
Muscle relaxant; reduces hyperthermia/rigidity

### Bromocriptine
Dopamine agonist; reverses NMS pathophysiology

### Prognosis
5-20% mortality if untreated; 80% recover with prompt recognition and treatment
