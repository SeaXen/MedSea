# Alcohol withdrawal and delirium tremens

---

## Definition

Alcohol withdrawal is a clinical syndrome that develops 6-24 hours after cessation in dependent drinkers, ranging from mild tremor to severe delirium tremens (DTs) with autonomic hyperactivity, confusion, and seizures.

## Core Clinical Content

### Minor withdrawal
6-24 h after last drink: tremor, anxiety, insomnia, GI upset, autonomic hyperactivity

### Seizures
12-48 h; generalised tonic-clonic; usually self-limiting

### Alcoholic hallucinosis
12-24 h; visual/auditory hallucinations with clear sensorium

### Delirium tremens (DTs)
48-96 h (up to 5 days); confusion, autonomic hyperactivity, hallucinations, tremor

### DTs mortality
5-15% untreated; <1% with treatment

### Treatment
Long-acting benzodiazepine (chlordiazepoxide, diazepam) — symptom-triggered or fixed dose

### CIWA-Ar
Clinical Institute Withdrawal Assessment for Alcohol; guides dosing

### Thiamine
BEFORE glucose; Pabrinex IV for Wernicke's prevention

### Pabrinex
IV thiamine + pyridoxine; give before glucose to prevent Wernicke's

### Seizure prophylaxis
Not routinely; treat seizures with benzodiazepine; avoid phenytoin (ineffective)
