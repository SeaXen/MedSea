# Schizophrenia

---

## Definition

Schizophrenia is a chronic psychiatric disorder characterised by positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions, disorganised speech), negative symptoms (avolition, alogia, flat affect), and cognitive impairment, lasting ≥6 months with ≥1 month of active symptoms.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
≥2 of 5 symptoms (delusions, hallucinations, disorganised speech, grossly disorganised/catatonic behaviour, negative symptoms); ≥1 must be delusions/hallucinations/disorganised speech

### Duration
≥6 months (≥1 month active phase)

### Positive symptoms
Hallucinations, delusions, disorganised speech, bizarre behaviour

### Negative symptoms
Avolition, alogia, flat affect, anhedonia, asociality

### First-rank symptoms (Schneider)
Auditory hallucinations (voices commenting, discussing, thought echo); thought insertion/withdrawal/broadcasting; delusions of control/passivity

### Prevalence
Lifetime ~1%; equal M:F; onset M 18-25, F 25-35

### Risk factors
Family history (8-10% if first-degree), obstetric complications, cannabis use, urban birth

### Dopamine hypothesis
↑ Dopamine in mesolimbic pathway (positive); ↓ in mesocortical (negative)

### First-line treatment
Antipsychotic (oral or LAI) + psychosocial support

### Suicide risk
~5-10% lifetime; highest in first 5 years and after discharge

## Viva Questions
**Q1. What is the definition of Schizophrenia?**
> Schizophrenia is a chronic psychiatric disorder characterised by positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions, disorganised speech), negative symptoms (avolition, alogia, flat affect), and cognitive impairment, lasting ≥6 months with ≥1 month of active symptoms.

**Q2. What are the key clinical features of Schizophrenia?**
> Key features include: dsm-5 criteria, duration, positive symptoms. These are essential for FCPS/MRCP exam recognition.

**Q3. How is Schizophrenia diagnosed?**
> Diagnosis is based on clinical features per DSM-5/ICD-11 criteria. Key diagnostic considerations are detailed in the Core Clinical Content section above.

**Q4. What is the first-line treatment for Schizophrenia?**
> First-line management typically includes a combination of pharmacological and psychological interventions, tailored to severity and patient preference. See Core Clinical Content for specific recommendations.

**Q5. What are the prognosis and complications of Schizophrenia?**
> Prognosis depends on early recognition, adherence to treatment, and management of comorbidities. See specific complications in Core Clinical Content.
