# Antipsychotic-induced movement disorders (EPS) and anticholinergic toxicity

---

## Definition

Antipsychotic-induced movement disorders include extrapyramidal symptoms (acute dystonia, akathisia, parkinsonism, tardive dyskinesia) and related conditions. Anticholinergic toxicity is a serious adverse effect of drugs with anticholinergic properties (TCAs, antipsychotics), characterised by classic antimuscarinic features.

## Core Clinical Content

### EPS types
Acute dystonia, Akathisia, Parkinsonism, Tardive dyskinesia (DAPT)

### Acute dystonia
Hours-days; sustained muscle contraction (torticollis, oculogyric crisis); treat with anticholinergic (procyclidine IM)

### Akathisia
Days-weeks; subjective restlessness; treat with β-blocker (propranolol), benzodiazepine, dose reduction

### Drug-induced parkinsonism
Weeks-months; bradykinesia, rigidity, tremor; treat with anticholinergic; switch to atypical

### Tardive dyskinesia (TD)
Months-years; orofacial dyskinesia, choreoathetoid movements; often IRREVERSIBLE

### TD risk factors
Older age, female, longer duration, higher dose, typical antipsychotics, mood disorder

### TD treatment
Stop/reduce antipsychotic; switch to clozapine; VMAT2 inhibitors (valbenazine, deutetrabenazine)

### Neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS)
Hyperthermia, rigidity, autonomic instability, altered consciousness; emergency

### Anticholinergic toxicity — central
Confusion, agitation, hallucinations, hyperthermia, seizures

### Anticholinergic toxicity — peripheral
Dry mouth, blurred vision, urinary retention, constipation, mydriasis, tachycardia, flushed skin
