# Mental Capacity Act and consent

---

## Definition

The Mental Capacity Act (MCA) 2005 provides a legal framework for decision-making for people aged ≥16 who lack capacity to make specific decisions. It is based on 5 principles and supports best-interests decision-making.

## Core Clinical Content

### MCA 5 principles
Presume capacity; support to make decisions; unwise decisions; best interests; least restrictive option

### Two-stage capacity test
1) Diagnostic — does impairment of mind/brain exist? 2) Functional — does impairment affect this decision?

### Four functional criteria
Understand, Retain, Weigh, Communicate (decision at this time)

### Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA)
Property/financial LPA + Health/welfare LPA; must be registered

### Court of Protection
Makes decisions for those without capacity; appoints Deputies

### Independent Mental Capacity Advocate (IMCA)
For serious medical decisions, accommodation changes when no family/friends

### Advance Decision (ADRT)
Refusal of treatment in advance; binding if valid and applicable

### Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards (DoLS)
Protect those who lack capacity but are deprived of liberty (care homes, hospitals)

### Liberty Protection Safeguards (LPS)
Replacing DoLS; new framework under MCA 2019 amendments

### Best interests decision
Multi-disciplinary; considers past/present wishes, beliefs, feelings; less restrictive option
