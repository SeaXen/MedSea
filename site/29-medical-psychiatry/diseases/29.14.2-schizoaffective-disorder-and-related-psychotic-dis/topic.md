# Schizoaffective disorder and related psychotic disorders

---

## Definition

Schizoaffective disorder is a psychotic disorder with prominent mood symptoms (depressive or manic) coexisting with schizophrenia-like psychotic symptoms, but with ≥2 weeks of psychosis without mood symptoms.

## Core Clinical Content

### Schizoaffective criteria
Major mood episode (depressive or manic) + ≥2 weeks of psychosis WITHOUT mood symptoms

### Distinguishing from schizophrenia
Prominent mood symptoms (not just brief)

### Distinguishing from bipolar/ MDD with psychotic features
≥2 weeks of psychosis WITHOUT mood symptoms

### Types
Bipolar type (manic + depressive episodes) and depressive type (only depressive episodes)

### Schizophreniform disorder
Same as schizophrenia but <6 months duration

### Brief psychotic disorder
Psychotic symptoms <1 month with full return to premorbid functioning

### Delusional disorder
Non-bizarre delusions (real-life situations) ≥1 month; otherwise functioning preserved

### Treatment
Mood stabiliser/antidepressant + antipsychotic; similar to schizophrenia long-term

### Prognosis
Better than schizophrenia; worse than mood disorder alone

### Schizoaffective prevalence
Rare (~0.3%); often misdiagnosed
