# Psychiatric aspects of physical illness

---

## Definition

Psychiatric conditions frequently co-occur with physical illness, either as a consequence of the medical condition, its treatment, or as an independent comorbidity. Recognition and management are essential for outcomes.

## Core Clinical Content

### Cardiovascular disease
Depression ↑ mortality in post-MI; SSRI (sertraline) safe; caution with TCAs (cardiotoxic)

### Stroke
Post-stroke depression 30-40%; peaks 3-6 months; often missed; treat with SSRI

### Cancer
Depression in 20-40%; screen with PHQ-9; SSRI first-line; avoid in tamoxifen (paroxetine, fluoxetine)

### Diabetes
Depression ↑ risk; ↑ poor control; consider SSRI

### Parkinson's disease
Depression in 40-50%; SSRI (caution with tremor); avoid antipsychotics (EPS)

### Epilepsy
Depression ↑ suicide risk; avoid TCAs, lowering seizure threshold; consider CBT

### HIV/AIDS
Depression, anxiety, psychosis; high suicide risk; screen all HIV patients

### Endocrine
Hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, Cushing's, Addison's — all can present with psychiatric symptoms

### Vitamin deficiencies
B12, folate, thiamine (Wernicke), vitamin D — all linked to depression/cognition

### Steroid-induced psychosis
High-dose corticosteroids; treat by reducing dose; antipsychotic if needed
