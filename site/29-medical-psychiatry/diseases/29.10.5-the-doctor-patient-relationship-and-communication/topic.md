# The doctor-patient relationship and communication

---

## Definition

The doctor-patient relationship is the foundation of medical psychiatry, encompassing the professional bond, communication, and trust that enables therapeutic engagement. Effective communication in psychiatric assessment includes active listening, empathy, attending to nonverbal cues, and managing the emotional content of the interview.

## Core Clinical Content

### Calgary-Cambridge model
Framework for medical communication: initiating, gathering info, providing structure, building relationship, explaining, closing

### Patient-centred interview
Focus on patient's perspective, ideas, concerns, expectations (ICE)

### Open vs closed questions
Open for narrative; closed for clarification

### Active listening
Verbal (reflection, paraphrasing) + nonverbal (eye contact, posture)

### Empathy
Ability to understand patient's feelings; core therapeutic skill

### Rapport
Foundation for trust and disclosure

### Silence
Allows patient to reflect and elaborate

### Transference
Patient's feelings about clinician (from past relationships)

### Countertransference
Clinician's emotional response to patient

### Breaking bad news
SPIKES protocol (Setting, Perception, Invitation, Knowledge, Emotions, Strategy)
