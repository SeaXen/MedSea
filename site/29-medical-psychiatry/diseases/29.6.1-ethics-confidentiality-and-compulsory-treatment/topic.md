# Ethics, confidentiality, and compulsory treatment

---

## Definition

Medical ethics in psychiatry involves the four principles of biomedical ethics (autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, justice) applied to mental health contexts, including confidentiality, consent, duty to warn, and decisions about compulsory treatment.

## Core Clinical Content

### Four principles (Beauchamp & Childress)
Autonomy, Beneficence, Non-maleficence, Justice

### Autonomy
Patient's right to self-determination; requires capacity and informed consent

### Beneficence
Acting in patient's best interest

### Non-maleficence
First do no harm; avoid causing harm

### Justice
Fair distribution of resources; treat similar patients similarly

### Confidentiality
Default; can be broken in specific circumstances (risk to self/others, public interest)

### Duty to warn (Tarasoff)
If patient poses serious threat to identifiable person, clinician has duty to protect (warn)

### GMC confidentiality guidance
Disclose only if necessary, lawful, proportionate; inform patient first if possible

### Compulsory treatment ethics
Justified when patient lacks capacity or poses serious risk; less restrictive options first

### Capacity-based vs risk-based
UK: Mental Health Act (risk-based) vs Mental Capacity Act (capacity-based)
