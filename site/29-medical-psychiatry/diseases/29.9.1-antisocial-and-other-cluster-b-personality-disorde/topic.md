# Antisocial and other Cluster B personality disorders

---

## Definition

Antisocial personality disorder (ASPD) is a Cluster B personality disorder characterised by disregard for and violation of the rights of others, occurring after age 18, with evidence of conduct disorder before age 15.

## Core Clinical Content

### ASPD criteria
≥3 of 15: failure to conform to laws, deceitfulness, impulsivity, irritability, recklessness, irresponsibility, lack of remorse

### Age requirement
≥18 years; evidence of conduct disorder <15 years

### Prevalence
3% men, 1% women; higher in prisons

### Conduct disorder
Repetitive violation of others' rights/age-appropriate norms in <18 years

### Comorbidity
Substance use (80%), mood/anxiety disorders, ADHD, other PDs

### Treatment
Difficult to treat; poor engagement; no effective pharmacotherapy

### Therapy approaches
CBT, schema therapy, therapeutic community (e.g., Henderson Hospital)

### Borderline vs Antisocial
BPD: self-harm, fear of abandonment; ASPD: aggression to others, lack of remorse

### Other Cluster B
Histrionic (attention-seeking, dramatic), Narcissistic (grandiosity, lack of empathy)

### Prognosis
Improves with age (40+); criminal behaviour often decreases; substance use continues
