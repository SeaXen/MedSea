# Antidepressants (SSRI, SNRI, TCA, MAOI, atypicals)

---

## Definition

Antidepressants are medications used to treat major depressive disorder and other psychiatric conditions. They work by modulating monoamine neurotransmitters (serotonin, noradrenaline, dopamine) and include SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, MAOIs, and atypical agents.

## Core Clinical Content

### SSRI mechanism
Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor; ↑ serotonin in synapse

### Common SSRIs
Fluoxetine, sertraline, citalopram, escitalopram, paroxetine

### SSRI side effects
GI upset, sexual dysfunction, hyponatraemia (SIADH), bleeding risk, QTc (citalopram)

### SSRI discontinuation
Discontinuation syndrome (dizziness, nausea, paresthesia) — taper slowly

### SNRI
Duloxetine, venlafaxine; serotonin + noradrenaline reuptake inhibition

### SNRI uses
Depression, GAD, neuropathic pain (duloxetine), fibromyalgia

### TCA mechanism
Serotonin + noradrenaline reuptake inhibition (also anticholinergic, antihistamine)

### TCA side effects
Anticholinergic (dry mouth, urinary retention), sedation, orthostatic hypotension, QTc prolongation, fatal in overdose

### TCA overdose
Cardiotoxic (VT, torsades), seizures, anticholinergic; sodium bicarbonate for VT

### MAOI
Phenelzine, tranylcypromine; dietary restrictions (tyramine → hypertensive crisis); rarely used
