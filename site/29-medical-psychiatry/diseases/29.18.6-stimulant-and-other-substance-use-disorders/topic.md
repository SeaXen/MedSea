# Stimulant and other substance use disorders

---

## Definition

Stimulant and other substance use disorders involve problematic use of substances including cocaine, amphetamines, cannabis, hallucinogens, and tobacco, with significant health, social, and psychological consequences.

## Core Clinical Content

### Cocaine
Blocks dopamine, noradrenaline, serotonin reuptake; intense euphoria followed by crash

### Cocaine withdrawal
Depression, fatigue, hypersomnia, craving (not life-threatening)

### Cocaine complications
MI, stroke, aortic dissection, arrhythmia, seizures, nasal septum perforation

### Amphetamines
Similar to cocaine; longer duration; risk of psychosis (paranoid, tactile hallucinations)

### Cannabis
Most used illicit drug; acute effects: euphoria, anxiety, paranoia, perceptual changes

### Cannabis withdrawal
Irritability, anxiety, insomnia, anorexia; mild

### Cannabis and psychosis
Dose-related risk of schizophrenia-spectrum disorders; earlier onset

### Hallucinogens (LSD, psilocybin)
Perceptual disturbances, synaesthesia; bad trips, flashbacks (HPPD)

### Tobacco
Leading preventable cause of death; nicotine replacement, varenicline, bupropion for cessation

### Volatile substance abuse
Solvents, glue; acute: euphoria, hallucinations; chronic: neurotoxicity, peripheral neuropathy
