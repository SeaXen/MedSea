# Gambling disorder and behavioural addictions

---

## Definition

Gambling disorder is characterised by persistent, recurrent problematic gambling behaviour leading to significant impairment or distress. Behavioural addictions (also called 'process addictions' or 'addictions without substance') include gambling, gaming, internet, food, sex, shopping, and exercise, with similar features to substance use disorders.

## Core Clinical Content

### Gambling disorder DSM-5
Persistent problematic gambling; ≥4 of 9 criteria in 12 months (similar to SUD)

### Gambling prevalence
~1-2%; more common in males; adolescence and young adults

### Gambling types
Pathological gambling (behavioural), problem gambling (mild)

### Gambling risk factors
Impulsivity, depression, anxiety, substance use, family history, accessibility

### Gambling comorbidities
Depression (50%), anxiety, substance use, suicide risk ↑↑

### Gambling treatment
CBT (most evidence), motivational interviewing, Gamblers Anonymous, naltrexone/opioid antagonists

### Internet gaming disorder (ICD-11)
Persistent gaming despite harm; impaired control; prioritised over other activities

### Other behavioural addictions
Compulsive sexual behaviour, binge eating, shopping, exercise

### Compulsive sexual behaviour (ICD-11)
Persistent failure to control sexual urges; significant distress/impairment

### Common features of behavioural addictions
Salience, mood modification, tolerance, withdrawal, conflict, relapse
