# Mood stabilisers (lithium, valproate, lamotrigine)

---

## Definition

Mood stabilisers are medications used to treat bipolar disorder, both for acute episodes (mania, depression) and for long-term prophylaxis. They include lithium, valproate, lamotrigine, and carbamazepine.

## Core Clinical Content

### Lithium
Gold standard for bipolar; effective in mania, depression, prophylaxis; ↓ suicide risk

### Lithium mechanism
Unknown; modulates intracellular signalling, neuroprotection

### Lithium monitoring
TFTs (hypothyroidism), U&E (renal), Ca (hyperparathyroidism), levels every 3-6 months

### Lithium therapeutic range
0.6-1.0 mmol/L (maintenance); 0.8-1.0 (acute mania); narrow therapeutic index

### Lithium toxicity
>1.5 mmol/L; tremor, ataxia, confusion, seizures, cardiac; >2.0 = severe; haemodialysis

### Lithium side effects
Nephrogenic diabetes insipidus, hypothyroidism, tremor, weight gain, teratogenic (Ebstein anomaly)

### Valproate
Effective in mania, mixed episodes, rapid cycling; teratogenic (neural tube defects)

### Valproate monitoring
LFTs, FBC, level; avoid in women of childbearing age

### Lamotrigine
Effective in bipolar depression; needs slow titration (Stevens-Johnson syndrome risk)

### Lamotrigine side effects
Rash (incl. Stevens-Johnson), drowsiness, headache; SJS more common with rapid titration
