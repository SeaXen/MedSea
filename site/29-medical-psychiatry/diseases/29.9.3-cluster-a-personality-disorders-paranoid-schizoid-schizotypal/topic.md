# Cluster A personality disorders (paranoid, schizoid, schizotypal)

---

## Definition

Cluster A personality disorders (paranoid, schizoid, schizotypal) are characterised by odd, eccentric, or paranoid thinking and behaviour. They may share features with schizophrenia spectrum disorders but are typically egosyntonic and less severe.

## Core Clinical Content

### Cluster A
Odd/eccentric; 'mad' (similar to schizophrenia)

### Paranoid PD
Pervasive distrust, suspiciousness, jealousy; reluctant to confide; bears grudges

### Paranoid PD vs delusional disorder
PD: pervasive pattern (not fixed delusion); DD: non-bizarre delusion, fixed

### Schizoid PD
Detachment from social relationships; restricted emotional expression; indifference to praise/criticism

### Schizoid vs avoidant
Schizoid: indifferent to relationships; avoidant: wants but fears relationships

### Schizotypal PD
Acute discomfort with close relationships; cognitive/perceptual distortions; eccentric behaviour

### Schizotypal vs schizophrenia
Schizotypal: brief/transient psychotic symptoms, reality testing preserved, egosyntonic; schizophrenia: frank psychosis

### Cluster A vs schizophrenia
Cluster A: subtle, chronic, ego-syntonic; schizophrenia: frank psychosis, deterioration

### Treatment
Difficult to treat; low engagement; support; cautious use of antipsychotics for transient psychosis

### Prognosis
Stable personality traits; some risk of progression to schizophrenia (esp. schizotypal)
