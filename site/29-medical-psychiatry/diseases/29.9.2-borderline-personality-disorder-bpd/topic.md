# Borderline personality disorder (BPD)

---

## Definition

Borderline personality disorder (BPD) is a Cluster B personality disorder characterised by pervasive instability in mood, interpersonal relationships, self-image, and behaviour, with marked impulsivity beginning in early adulthood.

## Core Clinical Content

### DSM-5 criteria
≥5 of 9: frantic efforts to avoid abandonment, unstable relationships, identity disturbance, impulsivity, suicidal behaviour, affective instability, chronic emptiness, inappropriate anger, dissociative/paranoid ideation

### Prevalence
~1-2% of population; 75% female

### Comorbidity
Depression, substance use, eating disorders, PTSD, other PDs

### Suicide risk
10x general population; ~8% die by suicide

### Dialectical behaviour therapy (DBT)
First-line; combines individual + group therapy, phone coaching

### DBT components
Mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, interpersonal effectiveness

### Other therapies
Mentalisation-based therapy (MBT), schema therapy, transference-focused therapy (TFP)

### Pharmacotherapy
No licensed medication; treat comorbidities; mood stabilisers for impulsivity

### Hospitalisation
Reserved for imminent risk of self-harm; brief; avoid reinforcement of maladaptive patterns

### Prognosis
Improves with age; 50% remit by 10 years; good response to DBT
