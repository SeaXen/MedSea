# Psychiatric history taking

---

## Definition

Psychiatric history taking is a comprehensive assessment of the patient's presenting complaint, history of present illness, past psychiatric and medical history, medication, family history, personal/social history, premorbid personality, and substance use.

## Core Clinical Content

### Presenting complaint (PC)
Patient's own words for why they are seeking help

### History of present illness (HPI)
Chronological narrative of current problem

### Past psychiatric history
Previous episodes, diagnoses, treatments, hospitalisations

### Past medical history
Medical conditions, especially relevant (e.g., thyroid, neurological)

### Medication history
Current and past medications; OTC, supplements, recreational

### Family history
Psychiatric disorders, suicide, substance use in first-degree relatives

### Personal history
Childhood, education, occupation, relationships, living situation

### Premorbid personality
Patient's personality before the illness

### Substance use history
Tobacco, alcohol, illicit drugs — type, amount, duration, route

### Forensic history
Criminal record, violence, legal issues

### Sexual history
When relevant (relationship, orientation, STIs, dysfunction)
