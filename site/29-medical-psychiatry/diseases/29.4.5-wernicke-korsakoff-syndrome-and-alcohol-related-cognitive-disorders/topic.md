# Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome and alcohol-related cognitive disorders

---

## Definition

Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome comprises Wernicke's encephalopathy (acute, reversible with treatment) and Korsakoff syndrome (chronic amnestic syndrome, often irreversible), both due to thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency, commonly in alcohol use disorder.

## Core Clinical Content

### Wernicke's triad
Confusion, ataxia, ophthalmoplegia (nystagmus, 6th nerve palsy)

### Wernicke's mechanism
Thiamine deficiency; often precipitated by glucose infusion in deficient patients

### Wernicke's treatment
Pabrinex IV (thiamine + pyridoxine) BEFORE glucose; high-dose

### Korsakoff syndrome
Anterograde amnesia, confabulation, relatively preserved other cognition

### Korsakoff prognosis
Often irreversible; only 20% recover significantly; confabulation may persist

### Wernicke to Korsakoff
Untreated Wernicke's progresses to Korsakoff in 80%

### Memory in Korsakoff
Anterograde amnesia (cannot form new memories); retrograde amnesia (variable); confabulation

### Other alcohol-related cognitive disorders
Alcohol-related dementia (possibly reversible with abstinence)

### Marchiafava-Bignami disease
Alcohol-related corpus callosum demyelination; rare

### Thiamine-rich foods
Wholegrains, pork, beans, nuts, yeast extract
