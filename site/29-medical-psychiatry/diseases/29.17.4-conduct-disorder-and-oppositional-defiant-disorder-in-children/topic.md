# Conduct disorder and oppositional defiant disorder in children

---

## Definition

Conduct disorder (CD) is characterised by repetitive and persistent patterns of behaviour that violate the basic rights of others or major age-appropriate societal norms. Oppositional defiant disorder (ODD) is a pattern of angry/irritable mood, argumentative/defiant behaviour, or vindictiveness lasting ≥6 months, less severe than CD.

## Core Clinical Content

### ODD features
Angry/irritable mood, argumentative/defiant behaviour, vindictiveness ≥6 months

### ODD prevalence
~3-5% of children; equal M:F before puberty; more males after

### ODD vs CD
ODD: defiance, hostility, no major rights violations; CD: violates others' rights/norms

### Conduct disorder (CD)
Aggression to people/animals, destruction of property, deceitfulness/theft, serious rule violation

### CD prevalence
~4-5% of children; 3-4x more common in males

### CD course
Often persists; precursor to antisocial personality disorder in adulthood (≥18 years)

### CD risk factors
Genetic (heritability ~50%), neurobiological (↓ MAO-A), family/parenting, socioeconomic, peer

### Comorbidities
ADHD (50%), depression, anxiety, substance use, learning disorders

### Treatment
Parent management training (PMT) - first-line; cognitive problem-solving; multi-systemic therapy; SSRI for aggression

### Prognosis
Better in ODD (1/3 remit); worse in CD with high callous-unemotional traits (poor prognosis)
