## 🧠 Core Concept: Cancer Immunoediting

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Cancer Immunoediting] --> B[Elimination<br/>Immune Surveillance]
    B --> C[Equilibrium<br/>Dormancy / Editing]
    C --> D[Escape<br/>Immune Evasion]
    
    D --> D1[Loss of Antigen Presentation<br/>MHC I Loss, B2M Mutations]
    D --> D2[Immunosuppressive Microenvironment<br/>Tregs, MDSCs, TAMs, CAFs, Cytokines (TGF-β, IL-10, VEGF)]
    D --> D3[Immune Checkpoint Upregulation<br/>PD-L1, CTLA-4, LAG-3, TIM-3, TIGIT, VISTA]
    D --> D4[Metabolic Dysregulation<br/>IDO, Arginase, Adenosine, Lactate, Hypoxia]
    D --> D5[Antigen Loss / Mutation<br/>Neoantigen Loss, Antigen Presentation Defects]
```

## 1️⃣ Tumour Antigens

| Category | Definition | Examples | Clinical Relevance |
|----------|------------|----------|-------------------|
| **TSAs (Tumour-Specific Antigens)** | **Unique to tumour**, Not in normal tissue | **Neoantigens** (Non-synonymous mutations), **Viral Antigens** (HPV E6/E7, EBV LMP1/2) | **True Targets** — No off-tumour toxicity; **Neoantigen Vaccines, TCR-T** |
| **TAAs (Tumour-Associated Antigens)** | **Expressed in tumours + Normal tissues** (Overexpressed/Developmental) | **Differentiation** (Tyrosinase, gp100, MART-1, NY-ESO-1), **Overexpressed** (HER2, EGFR, CEA, MUC1), **Cancer-Testis** (NY-ESO-1, MAGE-A) | **Cross-Reactivity Risk** (On-target/off-tumour), **Vaccines, CAR-T** |
| **Neoantigens** | **MHC-presented peptides** from somatic mutations | **SNVs, Indels, Frameshifts, Fusions, Splicing Variants** | **Personalised Vaccines**, **TCR-T**, **Predict ICI Response** |

### Cancer-Testis Antigens (CTAs)
| Antigen | Expression | Cancer Types |
|---------|------------|--------------|
| **NY-ESO-1** | Testis, Melanoma, Sarcoma, Lung, Ovarian, Bladder | **High Immunogenicity**, Vaccines, TCR-T |
| **MAGE-A Family** (A1, A3, A4, A10) | Germ cells, Melanoma, Lung, Oesophageal, Bladder | TCR-T, Vaccines |
| **MAGE-C2** | Testis, Lung, Head & Neck | Immunotherapy Target |

## 2️⃣ Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors (ICIs)

### Key Checkpoint Pathways

| Checkpoint | Ligand | Expression | Mechanism | Clinical Effect |
|------------|--------|------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **PD-1** | **PD-L1 (B7-H1), PD-L2** | T cells, B cells, NK, Macrophages (Activated) | **Inhibits TCR/CD28 signalling** → SHP-2 recruitment → ↓ PI3K/Akt, ↓ TCR signalling | **Exhaustion**, Apoptosis, Anergy |
| **CTLA-4** | **CD80 (B7-1), CD86 (B7-2)** | T cells (Activated), Tregs (Constitutive) | **Competes with CD28** for B7 binding → No Co-stimulation, Trans-endocytosis of CD80/86 | **Early Activation Block**, Treg Function |
| **LAG-3 (CD223)** | MHC Class II, FGL1, LSECtin | Activated T/NK cells, Tregs | Inhibits TCR signalling, Enhances Treg suppression | **Exhaustion**, Synergy with PD-1 |
| **TIGIT** | **CD155 (PVR), CD112, CD113** | T/NK cells, Tregs | Competes with CD226 (DNAM-1) → ↓ Adhesion/Cytotoxicity, ↑ Treg | **Exhaustion**, Synergy with PD-1 |
| **TIM-3** | Galectin-9, CEACAM1, HMGB1, PS | Exhausted T/NK, Tregs, DCs | Inhibits TCR signalling, ↓ Cytokines | **Terminal Exhaustion** |
| **VISTA** | VSIG3, PSGL-1 | Myeloid, T cells (Naive) | Inhibits T cell activation, Acidic pH | **Myeloid Suppression** |
| **B7-H3 (CD276)** | Unknown | Tumour, Myeloid, T cells | Co-stimulatory/Inhibitory | Tumour Immune Evasion |
| **B7-H4** | Unknown | Tumour, Macrophages | Inhibits T cell proliferation | Tumour Immune Evasion |

## 3️⃣ Biomarkers for ICI Response

### Established Biomarkers

| Biomarker | Assay | Threshold | Clinical Use |
|-----------|-------|-----------|--------------|
| **PD-L1 (TPS/CPS/IC)** | IHC (22C3, 28-8, SP142, SP263) | **TPS ≥1% / ≥50% (NSCLC)**, **CPS ≥1/10 (Gastric, Cervical, Urothelial)** | Patient Selection (NSCLC, Urothelial, Gastric, Cervical, TNBC) |
| **MSI-H / dMMR** | IHC (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) / PCR | **MSI-H / dMMR** | **Pembrolizumab (Pan-Cancer), Dostarlimab** |
| **TMB** | NGS Panel (≥1.5 Mb) / WES | **≥10 mut/Mb** (FoundationOne) | **Pembrolizumab (TMB-H ≥10)**, Correlation with ICI Response |
| **Neoantigen Load** | WES + HLA Typing + Neoantigen Prediction | **High Neoantigen Burden** | Predict ICI Response, Personalised Vaccines |
| **IFN-γ Signature** | RNA-seq / Nanostring | **High IFN-γ Signature** | Predicts ICI Response (Melanoma, RCC) |
| **PD-L1 (IHC) + TMB** | Combined | **PD-L1+ TMB-H** | Superior Predictive Value |

| Tumour | Key Biomarkers | ICI Approved |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Melanoma** | PD-L1, TMB, Neoantigen, IFN-γ | Anti-PD-1 ± CTLA-4 |
| **NSCLC** | **PD-L1 (TPS ≥50% → Pembrolizumab 1L)**, TMB, MSI | Anti-PD-1/PD-L1 ± Chemo/CTLA-4 |
| **RCC** | PD-L1, TMB, Sarcomatoid | Nivo+Ipi, Pembrolizumab+Axitinib |
| **HCC** | PD-L1, AFP, TMB | Atezo+Bev, Durvalumab+Treme, Pembro+Lenvatinib |
| **Urothelial** | PD-L1, TMB, FGFR3 | Pembro, Atezo, Nivo, Avelumab |
| **HNSCC** | PD-L1 (CPS), HPV, TMB | Pembro 1L (CPS≥1), Nivo 2L |
| **TNBC** | PD-L1, TMB, BRCA, TILs | Pembro (PD-L1 CPS≥10), Atezo+Nab-Paclitaxel |
| **MSI-H/dMMR (Any Solid Tumour)** | MSI-H/dMMR | **Pembrolizumab (Tumour-Agnostic)** |
| **TMB-H (≥10 mut/Mb)** | TMB-H | **Pembrolizumab (Tumour-Agnostic)** |

## 4️⃣ Adoptive Cell Therapies

### CAR-T Cells
| Component | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Structure** | **scFv (Antigen Binding) + Hinge + Transmembrane + Costimulatory (CD28/4-1BB) + CD3ζ (Signalling)** |
| **Generations** | **1st**: CD3ζ only; **2nd**: +CD28/4-1BB; **3rd**: +CD28+4-1BB; **4th**: +Cytokine (IL-12, IL-15) "Armoured"; **5th**: +JAK/STAT |
| **Approved Products** | **CD19**: Tisagenlecleucel (Kymriah), Axicabtagene Ciloleucel (Yescarta), Lisocabtagene Maraleucel (Breyanzi), Idecabtagene Vicleucel (Abecma); **BCMA**: Idecabtagene Vicleucel (Abecma), Ciltacabtagene Autoleucel (Carvykti) |
| **Indications** | **CD19**: R/R B-ALL (≤25y), R/R DLBCL (2L+), R/R FL (3L+); **BCMA**: R/R Multiple Myeloma (4L+) |

### CAR-T Toxicities — CRS & ICANS

| Toxicity | Grading (ASTCT 2019) | Management |
|----------|----------------------|------------|
| **CRS (Cytokine Release Syndrome)** | **Grade 1**: Fever; **Grade 2**: Hypotension (1 Vasopressor) / Hypoxia (Low-flow O₂); **Grade 3**: Hypotension (≥2 Vasopressors) / Hypoxia (High-flow O₂); **Grade 4**: Life-threatening (Ventilator, ≥3 Vasopressors) | **Tocilizumab 8mg/kg (Max 800mg) IV** — 1st Line; **Siltuximab** (Alternative); **Steroids (Dex 10mg q6h)** if Refractory/Grade ≥3; Supportive Care |
| **ICANS (Immune Effector Cell-Associated Neurotoxicity Syndrome)** | **Grade 1**: Awake, ICE Score 7-9; **Grade 2**: Awake, ICE 3-6; **Grade 3**: Obtunded (ICE 0-2); **Grade 4**: Coma (ICE 0), Seizure, Cerebral Oedema | **Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6h** (Grade ≥2); **Tocilizumab** if CRS co-existent; **Anti-Seizure**, **ICU**, **Avoid Tocilizumab Monotherapy for ICANS** (BBB penetration poor) |

| CRS/ICANS Monitoring | Frequency |
|----------------------|-----------|
| **First 14-28 Days** | q8-12h (Vitals, Neuro Exam ICE Score, Labs) |
| **CRS Grade ≥2** | q2-4h; **ICANS Grade ≥2** q1-2h |

## 5️⃣ Immunotherapy Toxicities (irAEs) — Immune-Related Adverse Events

### irAE Grading (CTCAE v5.0)

| Grade | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **Grade 1** | Mild; Asymptomatic or Mild symptoms; Clinical/diagnostic observations only |
| **Grade 2** | Moderate; Minimal, Local, Non-invasive intervention indicated |
| **Grade 3** | Severe; Medically significant; Hospitalisation indicated |
| **Grade 4** | Life-threatening; Urgent intervention indicated |
| **Grade 5** | Death |

### irAE Incidence & Timing
| irAE | Median Onset | Incidence (Anti-PD-1) | Incidence (Combo Ipi+Nivo) |
|------|--------------|----------------------|----------------------------|
| **Thyroiditis** | 6-12 weeks | 5-10% | 10-15% |
| **Colitis** | 6-14 weeks | 10-15% (Grade 3-4: 1-2%) | 25-30% (Grade 3-4: 10-15%) |
| **Hepatitis** | 6-12 weeks | 5-10% (Grade 3-4: 2-5%) | 15-25% (Grade 3-4: 5-10%) |
| **Pneumonitis** | 2-6 months | 3-5% (Grade 3-4: 1-3%) | 5-10% (Grade 3-4: 2-5%) |
| **Hypophysitis** | 8-12 weeks | 2-5% (Anti-CTLA-4 > PD-1) | 5-10% (Combo) |
| **Adrenal Insufficiency** | 8-16 weeks | 1-3% | 5-8% |
| **Dermatitis** | 3-6 weeks | 20-30% (Grade 3-4: 1-2%) | 40-50% (Grade 3-4: 5-10%) |
| **Neurological** (Guillain-Barré, Myasthenia, Encephalitis) | Variable | <1-2% | 2-5% |
| **Cardiac** (Myocarditis, Pericarditis) | 2-12 weeks | <1% (Grade 3-4: <1%) | 1-2% (Grade 3-4: 1-2%) |
| **Renal** (Nephritis) | 8-14 weeks | 1-2% | 2-5% |
| **Ocular** (Uveitis, Dry Eye) | 6-12 weeks | 1-2% | 2-5% |
| **Musculoskeletal** (Arthralgia, Myositis) | 6-12 weeks | 10-20% | 20-30% |

### irAE Management Principles

| Grade | ICI Management | Steroid | Additional |
|-------|----------------|---------|------------|
| **Grade 1** | **Continue ICI** | Topical (Dermatitis) / None | Symptomatic |
| **Grade 2** | **Hold ICI** | **Prednisolone 0.5-1mg/kg** | Topical, Loperamide (Colitis), Antacids (Hepatitis) |
| **Grade 3** | **Hold ICI** | **IV Methylpred 1-2mg/kg** | **Infliximab 5mg/kg** (Colitis Refractory), **MMF/Tacrolimus** (Hepatitis Refractory) |
| **Grade 4** | **Permanently Discontinue** | **IV Methylpred 1-2mg/kg** | ICU, Infliximab (Colitis), MMF/Tac (Hepatitis), IVIG (Myocarditis/Neurological) |

### Organ-Specific irAE Management

| irAE | Grade 1-2 | Grade 3-4 |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Colitis** | Loperamide, Budesonide, **Pred 0.5-1mg/kg** | **IV Methylpred 1-2mg/kg**, **Infliximab 5mg/kg** (Refractory), Vedolizumab, Tofacitinib |
| **Hepatitis** | LFT Monitoring, **Pred 0.5-1mg/kg** | **IV Methylpred 1-2mg/kg**, **MMF 1g BD**, **Tacrolimus**, **IVIG** |
| **Pneumonitis** | **Pred 0.5-1mg/kg**, Hold ICI | **IV Methylpred 1-2mg/kg**, **Infliximab**, **Cyclophosphamide**, **IVIG** |
| **Endocrine** (Thyroiditis, Hypophysitis, Adrenal, T1DM) | **Hormone Replacement** (Levothyroxine, Hydrocortisone, Insulin) | **Hormone Replacement + High-Dose Steroids** (Pituitary) |
| **Dermatitis** | Topical Steroids, **Oral Pred 0.5mg/kg** | **IV Methylpred**, **IVIG**, **Cyclosporine** |
| **Pneumonitis** | Hold ICI, **Pred 1mg/kg** | **IV Methylpred 1-2mg/kg**, **Infliximab**, **Cyclophosphamide** |

> **Key**: **Endocrinopathies = Hormone Replacement + Steroids (Usually Permanent)**, **Colitis/Hepatitis/Pneumonitis = Steroids + Immunosuppression if Refractory**

## 6️⃣ Resistance Mechanisms & Combination Strategies

### Primary Resistance
| Mechanism | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Lack of Tumour Antigens** | Low TMB, No Neoantigens, MHC I Loss |
| **Immune Exclusion** | "Cold Tumour" — Excluded T cells (Stroma, VEGF, CXCL12) |
| **Immunosuppressive TME** | Tregs, MDSCs, TAMs (M2), CAFs, TGF-β, IL-10, VEGF, IDO, Adenosine |

### Acquired Resistance
| Mechanism | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Antigen Loss** | **MHC I Loss (B2M Mutation)**, Neoantigen Loss, Antigen Presentation Defects |
| **Checkpoint Upregulation** | **LAG-3, TIM-3, TIGIT, VISTA** Upregulation |
| **Signalling Bypass** | **JAK1/2 Mutations** (IFN-γ Resistance), **PTEN Loss** (PI3K Activation) |
| **Lineage Plasticity** | **EMT**, **Neuroendocrine Transdifferentiation** |
| **Metabolic Adaptation** | **IDO, Arg1, Adenosine, Lactate, Hypoxia** |

### Combination Strategies to Overcome Resistance

| Strategy | Combination | Rationale |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Dual Checkpoint** | **PD-1 + CTLA-4** (Nivo+Ipi), **PD-1 + LAG-3** (Nivo+Rela) | Non-redundant pathways, Deeper T cell activation |
| **ICI + Chemotherapy** | **Pembro + Chemo (NSCLC, TNBC, Gastric)** | Immunogenic Cell Death, Antigen Release, Lymphodepletion |
| **ICI + Anti-Angiogenic** | **Pembro + Lenvatinib (Endometrial, RCC, HCC)** | Vascular Normalisation, ↓ MDSCs/Tregs, ↑ T Cell Infiltration |
| **ICI + Radiotherapy** | **Pembro + RT** | Abscopal Effect, Antigen Release, STING Activation |
| **ICI + Targeted Therapy** | **Pembro + Lenvatinib (RCC, Endometrial)**, **Atezo + Bev (HCC)** | ↓ VEGF → Vascular Normalisation + Immune Activation |
| **ICI + EP4 Antagonist** | **Pembro + Grapafant (EP4 Antag)** | PGE2-EP4 → MDSC/Treg Suppression |
| **ICI + STING Agonist** | **Pembro + STING Agonist** | Innate Immune Activation, DC Maturation |
| **ICI + Oncolytic Virus** | **T-VEC + Pembro** | Local Lysis → Antigen Release, Systemic Immune Activation |

| 10. Anti-PD-1 + Anti-CTLA-4 — approval? | **Nivolumab + Ipilimumab** — Melanoma, RCC, NSCLC (PD-L1≥1%), MPM, HCC, Urothelial, ESCC |

## 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"PD-1 and CTLA-4 do the same thing"** | **NO.** PD-1 = **Peripheral/Effector Phase** (Tissue); CTLA-4 = **Priming Phase** (Lymph Node). **Non-redundant**. |
| **"All MSI-H = TMB-H"** | **NO.** Overlap but **Distinct**. POLE/POLD1 mutants = **TMB-H but MSS** (Not MSI-H). |
| **"TMB-H = Always Respond to ICI"** | **NO.** Predictive but **Not Perfect** — Some TMB-H Tumours Resistant (Immune Exclusion, MHC Loss). |
| **"CAR-T = Only for Leukaemia"** | **NO.** **CD19** = B-ALL, DLBCL, FL; **BCMA** = Myeloma; **GPC3, HER2, EGFRvIII** in Solid Tumours (Trials). |
| **"CRS = ICANS"** | **NO.** **CRS = Cytokine Storm** (Fever, Hypotension, Hypoxia); **ICANS = Neurotoxicity** (Encephalopathy, Seizures, Aphasia). Different Mechanisms/Treatments. |
| **"Tocilizumab for ICANS"** | **POOR CNS Penetration** — **Not 1st Line for ICANS Alone**. **Dexamethasone 1st Line** for ICANS; Tocilizumab only if CRS Co-exists. |
| **"MSI-H = Lynch Only"** | **NO.** **Lynch = Germline MMR**; **MSI-H = Sporadic (MLH1 Methylation) + Lynch**; Also in **Gastric, Endometrial, CRC, Other**. |
| **"TMB-H = Only for ICI"** | **NO.** Also **Predicts Chemo Response** (High TMB → Better Chemo Response in Some Cancers). |
| **"All irAEs = Stop ICI"** | **NO.** **Grade 1-2** = Continue/Continue with Steroids; **Grade 3-4** = Hold/Discontinue. |
| **"Tocilizumab for All ICANS"** | **NO.** Poor CNS Penetration. **Dexamethasone 1st Line** for ICANS; Tocilizumab only if **Concurrent CRS**. |

> **Mnemonic: TUMOUR IMMUNOLOGY IMMUNOTHERAPY**  
> **T**umour Immunoediting: **Elimination → Equilibrium → Escape** (MHC Loss, Checkpoints, TME)  
> **U**nique Antigens: **TSAs (Neoantigens) vs TAAs (Differentiation/Overexpressed/Cancer-Testis)**  
> **M**ajor Checkpoints: **PD-1 (PD-L1/PD-L2), CTLA-4 (CD80/86), LAG-3 (MHC II), TIGIT (CD155), TIM-3 (Gal-9), VISTA**  
> **O**ncolytic Viruses: **T-VEC (HSV-1+GM-CSF)** — Approved Melanoma  
> **U** (CAR-T): **CD19 (B-ALL/DLBCL/FL), BCMA (Myeloma)** — **CRS/ICANS** → **Tocilizumab/Dex**  
> **R**esistance: **Primary (Cold Tumour, No Antigen), Acquired (MHC Loss, Checkpoint Up, JAK Mut, Lineage Switch)**  
> **I**CIs: **PD-1/PD-L1 (Pembro/Nivo/Atezo/Durva/Avel/Cemi)**, **CTLA-4 (Ipi/Treme)**, **LAG-3 (Relatlimab)**  
> **I**mmunotherapy Toxicity: **irAEs** — **Colitis, Hepatitis, Pneumonitis, Hypophysitis, Thyroiditis, Dermatitis, Myocarditis**  
> **I**rAE Management: **Grade 1-2 Continue/Hold+Steroids; Grade 3-4 Hold/Stop+IV Steroids+Infliximab/IVIG**  
> **B**iomarkers: **PD-L1 (TPS/CPS/IC), MSI-H/dMMR, TMB-H (≥10), Neoantigen Load, IFN-γ Signature**  
> **T**umour-Agnostic: **Pembrolizumab (MSI-H/dMMR, TMB-H)**  
> **O**ncolytic Virus: **T-VEC (HSV-1+GM-CSF)** — Approved Melanoma  
> **R**esistance: **Primary (Cold Tumour), Acquired (MHC Loss, Alt Checkpoints, JAK Mut, Lineage Switch)**  
> **A**doptive Therapy: **CAR-T (CD19, BCMA), TCR-T (NY-ESO-1, MAGE-A4), TIL, NK, BiTE (Blina, Tecla)**  
> **P**hase: **1st Gen (CD3ζ), 2nd (CD28/4-1BB), 3rd (CD28+4-1BB), 4th (Cytokine/Armoured), 5th (JAK/STAT)**  
> **Y** (Why Resistance?): **MHC Loss, Alt Checkpoints (LAG-3, TIM-3, TIGIT), JAK1/2 Mut, PTEN Loss, MET Amp, EMT**

## 📌 Summary
- **Cancer Immunoediting**: **Elimination → Equilibrium → Escape** (MHC Loss, Checkpoint Upregulation, TME Suppression)
- **Antigens**: **TSAs (Neoantigens)** = Unique; **TAAs** = Differentiation/Overexpressed/Cancer-Testis (Shared)
- **Checkpoint Inhibitors**: **PD-1/PD-L1** (Pembro, Nivo, Atezo, Durva, Avel, Cemi); **CTLA-4** (Ipi, Treme); **LAG-3** (Relatlimab); **TIGIT/TIM-3/VISTA** (Investigational)
- **Combo Regimens**: **Nivo+Ipi** (Mel, RCC, NSCLC); **Pembro+Lenvatinib** (Endometrial, RCC, HCC); **Atezo+Bev** (HCC); **Nivo+Rela** (Melanoma)
- **Biomarkers**: **PD-L1 (TPS/CPS/IC)**, **MSI-H/dMMR** (Pan-cancer), **TMB-H (≥10 mut/Mb)**, **Neoantigen Load**, **IFN-γ Signature**
- **CAR-T**: **CD19** (B-ALL, DLBCL, FL), **BCMA** (Myeloma); **CRS/ICANS** — **Tocilizumab + Dex**; **ICANS** → **Dex 1st Line**
- **irAEs**: **Colitis, Hepatitis, Pneumonitis, Hypophysitis, Thyroiditis, Dermatitis, Myocarditis** — **Steroids → Infliximab (Colitis), MMF/Tac (Hepatitis), IVIG (Myocarditis/Neuro)**
- **Resistance**: Primary (Cold Tumour) + Acquired (MHC Loss, Alt Checkpoints, JAK Mut, Lineage Switch)
- **Combination Strategies**: ICI+Chemo, ICI+Anti-VEGF, ICI+RT, ICI+Targeted, ICI+Oncolytic, Dual Checkpoint
- **Tumour-Agnostic Approvals**: **Pembrolizumab** (MSI-H/dMMR, TMB-H); **Dostarlimab** (MSI-H/dMMR); **Nivolumab** (MSI-H/dMMR)

## ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **Cancer immunoediting — phase where tumour is controlled but not eliminated?**  
   A. Elimination  B. **Equilibrium**  C. Escape  D. Progression  
   *Answer: B. Equilibrium — Dormancy, Immune Editing, Tumour Dormancy.*

2. **PD-1 vs CTLA-4 — key mechanistic difference?**  
   A. PD-1 = Priming, CTLA-4 = Effector  B. **PD-1 = Effector (Peripheral), CTLA-4 = Priming (Lymph Node)**  C. Same Mechanism  D. PD-1 = Treg, CTLA-4 = Teff  
   *Answer: B. PD-1 = Inhibits Effector Phase (Peripheral Tissues); CTLA-4 = Inhibits Priming Phase (Lymph Node).*

3. **Most specific biomarker for ICI response across cancers?**  
   A. PD-L1 TPS  B. **MSI-H/dMMR (Pan-cancer)**  C. TMB  D. IFN-γ Signature  
   *Answer: B. MSI-H/dMMR = Tumour-Agnostic Approval (Pembrolizumab, Dostarlimab).*

4. **CAR-T CRS Grade 3 management?**  
   A. Steroids only  B. **Tocilizumab 8mg/kg IV + Dexamethasone**  C. Antibiotics  D. IVIG  
   *Answer: B. Tocilizumab 8mg/kg IV + Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6h.*

5. **ICANS — first-line treatment?**  
   A. Tocilizumab  B. **Dexamethasone 10mg IV q6h**  C. Anti-IL-6  D. Plasma Exchange  
   *Answer: B. Dexamethasone 1st Line; Tocilizumab only if Concurrent CRS.*

6. **MSI-H vs TMB-H — difference?**  
   A. Same  B. **MSI = MMR Deficiency; TMB = Mutational Burden; Overlap but Distinct**  C. TMB-H subset of MSI-H  D. MSI-H subset of TMB-H  
   *Answer: B. MSI = MMR Deficiency; TMB = Mutational Burden; Overlap but Distinct (POLE = TMB-H, MSS).*

7. **Anti-PD-1 + Anti-CTLA-4 — approved for?**  
   A. Breast Cancer  B. **Melanoma, RCC, NSCLC (PD-L1≥1%), MPM, HCC, Urothelial**  C. All Solid Tumours  D. Haematological Only  
   *Answer: B. Nivo+Ipi approved for Melanoma, RCC, NSCLC (PD-L1≥1%), MPM, HCC, Urothelial.*

8. **CAR-T CRS Grade 1 — clinical feature?**  
   A. Hypotension  B. **Fever Only**  C. Hypoxia  D. Organ Dysfunction  
   *Answer: B. Grade 1 = Fever Only.*

9. **Tumour-agnostic pembrolizumab approval based on:**  
   A. PD-L1 TPS ≥50%  B. **MSI-H/dMMR OR TMB-H (≥10 mut/Mb)**  C. HER2+  D. ALK+  
   *Answer: B. MSI-H/dMMR (Pan-cancer) + TMB-H ≥10 mut/Mb.*

10. **ICANS Grade 2 — ICE Score?**  
    A. 7-9  B. **3-6**  C. 0-2  D. 10  
    *Answer: B. ICE Score 3-6 = Grade 2 (Awake, Impaired).*

## 📋 SBAs (10)

1. **Patient on pembrolizumab develops Grade 3 diarrhoea (8 stools/day), Cr 180. Management?**  
   A. Continue pembrolizumab + Loperamide  B. **Hold pembrolizumab, IV Methylpred 1-2mg/kg, Infliximab if Refractory**  C. Stop pembrolizumab permanently  D. Add Budesonide  
   *Answer: B. Grade 3 Colitis → Hold ICI, IV Methylpred 1-2mg/kg, Infliximab if Refractory.*

2. **NSCLC, PD-L1 TPS 60%, no driver mutation. 1st line?**  
   A. Chemotherapy  B. **Pembrolizumab Monotherapy**  C. Pembrolizumab + Chemo  D. Nivolumab + Ipilimumab  
   *Answer: B. PD-L1 TPS ≥50% → Pembrolizumab Monotherapy 1L (KEYNOTE-024).*

3. **Melanoma, BRAF WT, Bulky Disease. Combination ICI?**  
   A. Nivolumab Monotherapy  B. **Nivolumab + Ipilimumab**  C. Pembrolizumab + Lenvatinib  D. Talimogene Laherparepvec  
   *Answer: B. Nivo+Ipi Superior to Nivo Mono in High-Burden (CheckMate 067).*

4. **CAR-T patient Day +5, Fever 39.5°C, Hypotension (MAP 55 on Norad 0.1), Hypoxia (O2 88% on 4L). CRS Grade?**  
   A. Grade 1  B. **Grade 3 (Hypotension ≥2 Vasopressors / High-Flow O2)**  C. Grade 2  D. Grade 4  
   *Answer: B. Grade 3 = Hypotension Requiring ≥2 Vasopressors OR High-Flow O2.*

5. **MSI-H endometrial cancer. Immunotherapy?**  
   A. Chemotherapy  B. **Pembrolizumab (Tumour-Agnostic)**  C. Hormonal Therapy  D. Radiotherapy  
   *Answer: B. MSI-H/dMMR = Tumour-Agnostic Pembrolizumab Approval.*
