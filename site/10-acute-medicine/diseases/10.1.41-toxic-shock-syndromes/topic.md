> [!important]
> Toxic shock syndrome (TSS) = acute, life-threatening illness caused by superantigen toxins from *S. aureus* or *S. pyogenes*. Rapid recognition and source removal are critical.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Recognise diagnostic criteria for TSS
- Differentiate staphylococcal vs streptococcal TSS
- Apply immediate management (antibiotics + IVIG + source control)
- Know the role of clindamycin as toxin inhibitor

## 2. Diagnostic Criteria (CDC)
**Staphylococcal TSS**
1. Fever ≥38.9°C
2. Rash (diffuse macular erythroderma)
3. Desquamation (1–2 weeks later, palms/soles)
4. Hypotension (SBP ≤90)
5. ≥3 organ systems involved (GI, muscular, mucosal, renal, hepatic, haematologic, CNS)

**Streptococcal TSS** (more severe)
- Isolation of GAS + hypotension + ≥2 organ failures (renal, coagulopathy, hepatic, ARDS, soft tissue necrosis)

## 3. Aetiology
- **Staphylococcal**: TSST-1, enterotoxins — tampons, surgical wounds, nasal packing
- **Streptococcal**: SpeA, SpeB — skin/soft tissue infection, varicella, postpartum

## 4. Management
1. **ICU admission**
2. **Aggressive fluid resuscitation** (crystalloids)
3. **Broad-spectrum antibiotics**: clindamycin + beta-lactam (piperacillin-tazobactam/carbapenem) + vancomycin
4. **Clindamycin** — inhibits toxin synthesis (key!)
5. **Source control**: remove tampon/drain abscess/explore wound
6. **IVIG** 1–2 g/kg (consider in severe/refractory)

## 5. Red Flags
- Rapid deterioration
- ARDS, DIC, renal failure
- Necrotising fasciitis (surgical emergency)

## 6. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- Clindamycin is essential — inhibits protein (toxin) synthesis
- IVIG may be lifesaving in severe cases
- Streptococcal TSS has higher mortality
- Desquamation is late sign — do not wait for it

## 9. Must Know / Should Know / Nice to Know
### Must Know
- CDC criteria for staphylococcal TSS (5 criteria)
- Streptococcal TSS = GAS + hypotension + ≥2 organ failures
- Clindamycin inhibits toxin synthesis (key)
- Source removal essential (tampon, wound, abscess)

### Should Know
- IVIG 1–2 g/kg in severe/refractory
- Strep TSS higher mortality than Staph
- Desquamation late sign (1–2 weeks)

### Nice to Know
- Specific toxins: TSST-1, enterotoxins (Staph), SpeA/SpeB (Strep)
- Necrotising fasciitis as complication
- Prophylactic clindamycin in surgical packing
