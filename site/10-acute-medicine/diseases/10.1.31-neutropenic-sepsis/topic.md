> [!important]
> Neutropenic sepsis is a **medical emergency** with high mortality. **Treat immediately** — do not wait for cultures.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Define neutropenic sepsis
- Apply the "door-to-needle" time target
- Choose appropriate first-line antibiotics
- Know when to escalate antifungals

## 2. Definition
- Temperature ≥38°C (or <35.5°C) **AND**
- Neutrophil count <0.5 × 10⁹/L (or predicted to fall below)

## 3. Immediate Management (Door-to-Needle <1 hour)
1. **Blood cultures** (peripheral + line if present)
2. **IV antibiotics** immediately — do not delay
3. **Metabolic screen**: FBC, U&E, LFT, CRP, lactate, blood glucose
4. **CXR** if respiratory symptoms

## 4. First-Line Antibiotics (per local policy)
- **Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV TDS** (most common)
- **Meropenem 1g IV TDS** (if penicillin allergy / ESBL risk)
- **Add glycopeptide (vancomycin/teicoplanin)** if: line infection, MRSA colonised, hypotensive, skin/soft tissue infection

## 5. Risk Stratification (MASCC / CISNE scores)
- **Low risk** (MASCC ≥21): oral antibiotics possible, outpatient consideration
- **High risk** (MASCC <21): inpatient IV antibiotics, monitor closely

## 6. Duration
- Afebrile 48–72 hours + neutrophils >0.5 × 10⁹/L
- Total 5–7 days typically
- If no source identified + afebrile 5 days, stop antibiotics

## 7. Antifungal Escalation
- Add if persistent fever >4–7 days on broad-spectrum antibiotics
- Empirical **caspofungin** or **amphotericin B**

## 8. Red Flags
- Hypotension, altered consciousness, respiratory failure
- Focus of infection requiring surgical drainage
- Severe mucositis / typhlitis

## 9. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- **Never delay antibiotics** for cultures
- Piperacillin-tazobactam is standard first-line
- MASCC score guides admission vs outpatient
- Line cultures alone are insufficient — need peripheral too

### Should Know
