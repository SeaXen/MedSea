> [!important]
> Fluids are the **first-line resuscitation** in most shock types — but type, volume, and speed matter. Over-resuscitation harms.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Choose appropriate fluid type (crystalloids vs colloids vs blood)
- Apply fluid responsiveness concepts
- Know static vs dynamic predictors
- Avoid fluid overload

## 2. Fluid Types
| Fluid | Composition | Indication | Caution |
|-------|-------------|------------|---------|
| **Balanced crystalloids** (Hartmann's, Plasma-Lyte) | Near-physiological | **First-line for most** | Large volumes → metabolic alkalosis |
| **0.9% Saline** | Na⁺ 154, Cl⁻ 154 | Hyperchloraemic acidosis risk; avoid large volumes | |
| **Albumin 4.5–5%** | Colloid | Sepsis (SSC), large-volume resuscitation | Cost, no mortality benefit vs crystalloids |
| **Blood products** | RBC, FFP, platelets, cryo | Active bleeding, Hb <70, coagulopathy | Transfusion reactions |

## 3. Fluid Responsiveness
Only ~50% of shocked patients are **fluid responders** (CO increases >10–15% with fluid bolus).

**Static (poor predictors)**: CVP, PAWP, IVC diameter
**Dynamic (good predictors)**:
- **Passive leg raise (PLR)** — temporary preload increase
- **Stroke volume variation (SVV)** / **Pulse pressure variation (PPV)** — need ventilator, sinus rhythm, tidal volume ≥8 mL/kg
- **Mini-fluid challenge** (100–250 mL over 1–2 min)

## 4. Resuscitation Protocol (Sepsis / Undifferentiated Shock)
1. **500 mL balanced crystalloid** over 15 min (or 30 mL/kg over 3h if severe sepsis)
2. Reassess: MAP, lactate, urine output, clinical perfusion
3. Repeat if fluid responsive
4. **Stop** if no response after 1–2 L → start vasopressors

## 5. Special Situations
- **Cardiogenic shock**: small boluses (250 mL) only if clear hypovolaemia
- **Obstructive shock**: avoid fluids (worsens obstruction)
- **Trauma**: permissive hypotension (SBP 80–90) until bleeding controlled
- **Burns**: Parkland formula (4 mL/kg/%TBSA in 24h, half in first 8h)

## 6. Complications of Fluid Overload
- Pulmonary oedema
- Abdominal compartment syndrome
- Dilutional coagulopathy
- Worsened tissue oedema → impaired wound healing, ileus

## 7. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- **Balanced crystalloids preferred** over 0.9% saline
- **Dynamic measures** (PLR, SVV, PPV) > static (CVP)
- **Fluid responsiveness** must be proven — do not blindly bolus
- **Conservative fluid strategy** after initial resuscitation improves outcomes

## 10. Must Know / Should Know / Nice to Know
### Must Know
- Balanced crystalloids first-line (Hartmann's, Plasma-Lyte)
- 0.9% saline → hyperchloraemic acidosis, AKI risk
- Dynamic > static for fluid responsiveness (PLR, SVV, PPV)
- Stop fluids if unresponsive → vasopressors
- Permissive hypotension in trauma

### Should Know
- Albumin in sepsis (SSC suggests if large volumes)
- Blood products: Hb <70 (restrictive)
- Parkland formula for burns
- Fluid overload complications (pulmonary oedema, ACS)

### Nice to Know
- Starling curve physiology
- Colloid vs crystalloid trials (SAFE, CHEST, VISEP)
- Goal-directed therapy evolution (EGDT → PROCESS/ARISE/PROMISE)
