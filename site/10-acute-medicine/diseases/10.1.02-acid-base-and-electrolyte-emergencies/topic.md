# Acid-Base and Electrolyte Emergencies

Related: [[Acute Kidney Injury in Critical Illness]], [[Diabetic Emergencies (DKA and HHS)]], [[Critical Care Monitoring]], [[Sepsis and Septic Shock]], [[Fluid Resuscitation Strategies]]

> [!tip]
> **Henderson-Hasselbalch** (traditional) vs **Stewart/Fencl** (strong ion difference) approach. **Systematic ABG interpretation**: pH → PaCO₂ → HCO₃ → compensation → anion gap. **Electrolyte emergencies**: K⁺ (hyper/hypo), Na⁺ (hyper/hyponatraemia), Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, PO₄. **Time-critical**: hyperK >6.5, hyponatraemia <120 with seizures, Ca²⁺ >3.5 or <1.8. Key FCPS/MRCP: ABG stepwise interpretation, anion gap + delta-delta, Winter's formula, hyperK emergency treatment, hyponatraemia correction limits, Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ emergencies.

## Learning Objectives
- Interpret ABG systematically (traditional + Stewart approach)
- Identify primary disorders and appropriate compensation
- Calculate anion gap, delta-delta, strong ion difference
- Manage hyperkalaemia, hyponatraemia, hyper/hypocalcaemia, hyper/hypomagnesaemia, phosphate emergencies
- Apply correction rate limits to avoid osmotic demyelination / cardiac arrhythmias

## ABG Interpretation — Systematic Approach

### Stepwise Algorithm
1. **pH** → acidaemia (<7.35) or alkalaemia (>7.45)
2. **PaCO₂** → respiratory component (↑ = respiratory acidosis, ↓ = respiratory alkalosis)
3. **HCO₃⁻** → metabolic component (↓ = metabolic acidosis, ↑ = metabolic alkalosis)
4. **Primary vs Compensatory** → match direction of pH change
5. **Compensation assessment** → appropriate vs inadequate vs mixed
6. **Anion gap** (if metabolic acidosis)
7. **Delta-delta** (if high AG metabolic acidosis)
8. **Clinical correlation** (history, examination, other labs)

### Normal Values
| Parameter | Normal Range |
|-----------|--------------|
| pH | 7.35–7.45 |
| PaCO₂ | 4.7–6.0 kPa (35–45 mmHg) |
| HCO₃⁻ | 22–26 mmol/L |
| Base Excess | −2 to +2 mmol/L |
| Lactate | <2 mmol/L |
| Anion Gap | 8–12 mmol/L (albumin-corrected: AG + 2.5×(40−Alb g/L)) |

## Primary Acid-Base Disorders

| Disorder | Primary Change | Compensation | Expected Compensation Formula |
|----------|----------------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| **Respiratory Acidosis** | ↑ PaCO₂ | ↑ HCO₃⁻ (renal) | **Acute**: HCO₃⁻ ↑ 1 per 10 PaCO₂ ↑<br>**Chronic**: HCO₃⁻ ↑ 4 per 10 PaCO₂ ↑ |
| **Respiratory Alkalosis** | ↓ PaCO₂ | ↓ HCO₃⁻ (renal) | **Acute**: HCO₃⁻ ↓ 2 per 10 PaCO₂ ↓<br>**Chronic**: HCO₃⁻ ↓ 5 per 10 PaCO₂ ↓ |
| **Metabolic Acidosis** | ↓ HCO₃⁻ | ↓ PaCO₂ (hyperventilation) | **Winter's**: PaCO₂ = 1.5×HCO₃⁻ + 8 ± 2 |
| **Metabolic Alkalosis** | ↑ HCO₃⁻ | ↑ PaCO₂ (hypoventilation) | PaCO₂ = 0.7×HCO₃⁻ + 20 ± 5 |

## Anion Gap & Delta-Delta

### Anion Gap (AG)
**AG = Na⁺ − (Cl⁻ + HCO₃⁻)** — **albumin-corrected**: **AG_corr = AG + 2.5 × (40 − Albumin g/L)**

| AG Type | Causes (MUDPILES) |
|---------|-------------------|
| **High AG** | **M**ethanol, **U**raemia, **D**KA, **P**araldehyde, **I**ron/Isoniazid, **L**actic acidosis, **E**thylene glycol, **S**alicylate |
| **Normal AG** | Diarrhoea, RTA, carbonic anhydrase inhibitors, pancreatic fistula, ureterosigmoidostomy, **saline infusion** |
| **Low AG** | Hypoalbuminaemia, paraproteinaemia, bromide/lithium toxicity |

### Delta-Delta (ΔΔ) — **Unmasks Mixed Disorders in High AG Acidosis**
- **ΔAG = Measured AG − Normal AG (12)**
- **ΔHCO₃ = Normal HCO₃ (24) − Measured HCO₃**
- **ΔΔ = ΔAG − ΔHCO₃**
  - **ΔΔ ≈ 0** → pure high AG metabolic acidosis
  - **ΔΔ > +6** → **concomitant metabolic alkalosis** (ΔAG > ΔHCO₃)
  - **ΔΔ < −6** → **concomitant normal AG metabolic acidosis** (ΔHCO₃ > ΔAG)

## Electrolyte Emergencies

### 1. Hyperkalaemia (K⁺ >6.5 mmol/L or ECG changes) — **CARDIAC EMERGENCY**

#### ECG Changes (Progressive)
| K⁺ Level | ECG Findings |
|----------|--------------|
| **5.5–6.5** | Peaked T waves, shortened QT |
| **6.5–7.5** | PR prolongation, QRS widening, P wave flattening |
| **>7.5** | **Sine wave** (QRS merges with T), VT/VF, asystole |

#### Emergency Treatment (Sequential — Minutes Matter)
| Step | Treatment | Dose/Details | Onset | Duration |
|------|-----------|--------------|-------|----------|
| **1. Membrane Stabilisation** | **Calcium gluconate 10%** | **10 mL IV** over 2–5 min | **1–3 min** | 30–60 min |
| | Calcium chloride 10% | 5–10 mL IV (central) | 1–3 min | 30–60 min |
| **2. Shift K⁺ Into Cells** | **Insulin + Dextrose** | **10 U Actrapid + 25g (50 mL 50%)** IV | 15–30 min | 2–4 h |
| | Salbutamol nebuliser | 10–20 mg (5 mg × 2–4) | 15–30 min | 2 h |
| | Sodium bicarbonate | 50–100 mmol IV (if acidosis) | 15–30 min | 1–2 h |
| **3. Enhance Elimination** | **Loop diuretic** (if urine output) | Furosemide 40–80 mg IV | 30 min | 2–4 h |
| | Sodium polystyrene sulfonate | 15–30 g PO/PR | 1–2 h | 6–12 h |
| | **Hemodialysis** | Definitive (if renal failure/refractory) | Immediate | — |

> **Priority**: **Calcium first if ECG changes** → Insulin/Dextrose → Dialysis if refractory/renal failure

### 2. Hyponatraemia (Na⁺ <130 mmol/L) — **NEUROLOGICAL EMERGENCY if <120 or symptomatic**

#### Assessment
| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1. **Symptoms?** | Seizures, coma, confusion → **emergency** |
| 2. **Volume status** | Hypovolaemic / Euvolaemic / Hypervolaemic |
| 3. **Urine osmolality** | >100 = ADH active (SIADH, hypovolaemia, heart failure) |
| 4. **Urine Na⁺** | >20 = renal loss (diuretics, salt-wasting); <20 = extrarenal |

#### Aetiology by Volume
| Volume | Causes |
|--------|--------|
| **Hypovolaemic** | Diuretics, GI losses, cerebral salt wasting, adrenal insufficiency |
| **Euvolaemic** | **SIADH** (drugs, malignancy, CNS, pulmonary), hypothyroidism, cortisol deficiency, reset osmostat |
| **Hypervolaemic** | Heart failure, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome, CKD |

#### Correction Limits — **CRITICAL**
| Scenario | Max Rate | Max 24h |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Asymptomatic / Chronic** | 0.5 mmol/L/h | **≤8 mmol/L** |
| **Symptomatic (seizures/coma) / Acute (<48h)** | **1–2 mmol/L/h** | **≤10–12 mmol/L** |
| **Severe symptomatic** | **3% NaCl 100–150 mL bolus** (repeat if needed) | ≤12 mmol/L |

> **Osmotic Demyelination Risk**: overcorrection >10–12 mmol/L in 24h → **central pontine myelinolysis** (delayed 2–6 days: quadriparesis, dysphagia, locked-in)

#### Management by Type
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| **Hypovolaemic** | 0.9% NaCl (volume + Na⁺) |
| **SIADH (Euvolaemic)** | **Fluid restriction** 800–1000 mL/day; **3% NaCl** if severe symptomatic; **demeclocycline** (chronic) |
| **Hypervolaemic** | Fluid restriction, loop diuretic, treat underlying (HF, cirrhosis) |
| **Adrenal insufficiency** | **Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV** + saline |
| **Cerebral salt wasting** | Volume + Na⁺ replacement (fludrocortisone) |

### 3. Hypernatraemia (Na⁺ >145 mmol/L) — **CELLULAR DEHYDRATION**

#### Assessment
- **Volume status**: Hypovolaemic (water loss > Na⁺ loss) / Euvolaemic (pure water loss) / Hypervolaemic (Na⁺ gain > water)
- **Urine osmolality** >800 = appropriate ADH; <300 = diabetes insipidus

#### Correction Limits
| Scenario | Max Rate | Max 24h |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Chronic / Asymptomatic** | 0.5 mmol/L/h | **≤10 mmol/L** |
| **Acute (<24h) / Symptomatic** | 1–2 mmol/L/h | ≤12 mmol/L |

#### Management
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| **Hypovolaemic** | 0.9% NaCl → switch to 0.45%/5% dextrose once euvolaemic |
| **Euvolaemic (DI)** | **5% Dextrose** or **0.45% NaCl** + **Desmopressin** (central DI) |
| **Hypervolaemic** | Loop diuretic + 5% dextrose; dialysis if severe |

### 4. Hypocalcaemia (Corrected Ca²⁺ <2.0 mmol/L / Ionised <1.0 mmol/L)

#### ECG: **Prolonged QT** (ST prolongation), risk Torsades
#### Acute Symptomatic (tetany, seizure, laryngospasm, QT prolongation):
- **Calcium gluconate 10% 10 mL IV** over 10 min (→ 2.25 mmol Ca²⁺)
- Repeat q10 min until symptoms resolve
- **Monitor**: continuous ECG, serum Ca²⁺ q2–4h
- **Correct Mg²⁺** if hypomagnesaemia (refractory without Mg²⁺)

#### Chronic/Asymptomatic:
- Oral calcium + vitamin D (alfacalcidol/calcitriol)
- Treat underlying cause (hypoparathyroidism, vitamin D deficiency, CKD)

### 5. Hypercalcaemia (Corrected Ca²⁺ >2.6 mmol/L / Ionised >1.3 mmol/L)

#### ECG: Shortened QT, bradycardia, AV block
#### Severity:
- **Mild** (2.6–3.0): asymptomatic/mild symptoms
- **Moderate** (3.0–3.5): polyuria, nausea, constipation, confusion
- **Severe** (>3.5): **emergency** — coma, AKI, arrhythmias, pancreatitis

#### Management
1. **IV 0.9% NaCl** 2–4 L/day (volume repletion + calciuresis) — target urine output >200 mL/h
2. **Loop diuretic** (furosemide 40–80 mg) **after** volume repletion
3. **Bisphosphonate**: **Zoledronic acid 4 mg IV** over 15 min (or Pamidronate 60–90 mg)
4. **Denosumab** 120 mg SC (if bisphosphonate contraindicated/renal failure)
5. **Calcitonin** 4–8 U/kg SC q6–12h (rapid but tachyphylaxis in 48h)
6. **Dialysis** (if severe + renal failure/refractory)

### 6. Hypomagnesaemia (Mg²⁺ <0.7 mmol/L) — **REFRACTORY K⁺/Ca²⁺, ARRHYTHMIAS**

#### ECG: prolonged QT, Torsades risk, VT
#### Causes: diuretics, PPI, aminoglycosides, alcohol, diarrhoea, malnutrition, refeeding
#### Treatment:
- **Severe (<0.4 / symptomatic)**: **MgSO₄ 2 g IV** over 10–20 min (8 mmol)
- **Moderate**: **MgSO₄ 10–20 mmol IV** over 1–2h, then oral
- **Refractory hypokalaemia** → **must correct Mg²⁺ first** (K⁺ won't correct without Mg²⁺)

### 7. Hypermagnesaemia (Mg²⁺ >1.5 mmol/L)
#### Causes: renal failure, Mg²⁺-containing antacids/laxatives, enemas
#### Signs: loss of reflexes, hypotension, respiratory depression, heart block, cardiac arrest
#### Treatment: **Calcium gluconate 10 mL IV** (antagonist), **IV fluids + furosemide**, **dialysis** if severe/renal failure

### 8. Phosphate Disorders
#### Hypophosphataemia (<0.3 mmol/L severe):
- **Causes**: refeeding, alcohol, DKA recovery, respiratory alkalosis, diuretics
- **Effects**: muscle weakness, rhabdo, respiratory failure, haemolysis, seizures
- **Treatment**: **0.5 mmol/kg IV** over 6–12h (monitor Ca²⁺ — hypocalcaemia risk)

#### Hyperphosphataemia (>2.0 mmol/L):
- **Causes**: renal failure, rhabdo, tumour lysis, hypoparathyroidism
- **Treatment**: phosphate binders (calcium acetate, sevelamer), dialysis

## Mixed Acid-Base Disorders — **Common in ICU**
| Clue | Suggests |
|------|----------|
| **pH normal** but abnormal PaCO₂/HCO₃⁻ | Mixed disorder |
| **Compensation over/under** | Concomitant opposite disorder |
| **High AG + normal AG** | Delta-delta >6 (metabolic alkalosis) or <−6 (normal AG acidosis) |
| **Respiratory + metabolic** | Sepsis (metabolic acidosis + respiratory alkalosis), COPD exacerbation |

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
1. **ABG stepwise**: pH → PaCO₂ → HCO₃⁻ → primary → compensation → AG → ΔΔ
2. **Winter's Formula**: PaCO₂ = 1.5×HCO₃⁻ + 8 ± 2 (metabolic acidosis)
3. **Albumin-corrected AG**: AG + 2.5×(40−Alb)
4. **HyperK emergency**: Calcium gluconate → Insulin/Dextrose → Salbutamol → Dialysis
5. **Hyponatraemia correction**: ≤8 mmol/L/24h (chronic), ≤10–12 (acute/symptomatic)
6. **HyperNa correction**: ≤10 mmol/L/24h (chronic)
7. **HypoCa emergency**: Calcium gluconate 10 mL IV over 10 min
8. **HyperCa emergency**: Fluids → Loop diuretic → Bisphosphonate (Zoledronic 4 mg)
9. **Mg²⁺ first** for refractory hypokalaemia/hypocalcaemia
9. **Delta-delta** unmasks mixed metabolic disorders
10. **Winter's formula** validates respiratory compensation in metabolic acidosis

## Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- **Correcting hyponatraemia too fast** → central pontine myelinolysis
- **Calcium chloride vs gluconate**: chloride = 3× Ca²⁺, central line only
- **Insulin/dextrose before calcium in hyperK with ECG changes** → NO, calcium first
- **Bicarbonate for hyperK without acidosis** → less effective
- **Rapid Na⁺ correction in chronic hyponatraemia** → osmotic demyelination
- **Calcium gluconate for hypercalcaemia** → NO, worsens hypercalcaemia
- **Mg²⁺ not checked in refractory hypokalaemia** → won't correct
- **Albumin not corrected in AG** → falsely normal AG in hypoalbuminaemia
- **Winter's formula in metabolic alkalosis** → doesn't apply
- **Delta-delta only for high AG acidosis**

## Mnemonics
- **ABG STEPS**: **pH → PaCO₂ → HCO₃ → Compensate → AG → ΔΔ**
- **MUDPILES**: **M**ethanol, **U**raemia, **D**KA, **P**araldehyde, **I**ron/**I**soniazid, **L**actic, **E**G, **S**alicylate
- **HYPER K**: **C**alcium (membrane), **I**nsulin/**D**extrose (shift), **S**albutamol, **B**icarb, **D**ialysis
- **HYPONATRAEMIA**: **S**IADH = **E**uvolemic, **H**igh **U**rine **O**sm; **H**ypovolaemic = **L**ow **U**rine **N**a; **H**ypervolaemic = **H**F/**C**irrhosis
- **CORRECTION LIMITS**: **H**ypo**Na** chronic **8**, acute **10–12**; **HyperNa** chronic **10**, acute **12**
- **WINTER'S**: **P**aCO₂ = **1.5**×**H**CO₃ + **8** ± **2**
- **DELTA-DELTA**: **ΔAG − ΔHCO₃** → **+6** = alk, **−6** = acidosis

## Summary
Systematic ABG interpretation: **pH → PaCO₂ → HCO₃⁻ → primary disorder → compensation → anion gap → delta-delta**. **Anion gap** = Na⁺ − (Cl⁻ + HCO₃⁻); **albumin-corrected** = AG + 2.5×(40−Albumin). **Winter's formula**: PaCO₂ = 1.5×HCO₃⁻ + 8 ± 2 (metabolic acidosis compensation). **Delta-delta** unmasks mixed metabolic disorders (**>+6** = concurrent metabolic alkalosis; **<−6** = concurrent normal AG acidosis). **Electrolyte emergencies**: **Hyperkalaemia** >6.5/ECG → **calcium gluconate 10 mL IV** (membrane stabilisation) → **insulin 10 U + 25g dextrose** → salbutamol → dialysis. **Hyponatraemia**: volume status → urine osmolality/Na⁺ → correction **≤8 mmol/L/24h (chronic), ≤10–12 (acute/symptomatic)**. **Hypernatraemia**: **≤10 (chronic), ≤12 (acute)**. **Hypocalcaemia** emergency (QT prolongation): **calcium gluconate 10 mL IV over 10 min**. **Hypercalcaemia**: fluids → loop diuretic → **zoledronic acid 4 mg IV**. **Magnesium** <0.4 mmol/L or refractory K⁺/Ca²⁺ → **MgSO₄ 2 g IV**. **Winter's formula**, **albumin-corrected AG**, **delta-delta** essential for mixed disorders.

## MCQs (10)
1. In ABG interpretation, the first step is:
   A. Calculate anion gap
   B. **Check pH (acidaemia or alkalaemia)**
   C. Look at HCO₃⁻
   D. Apply Winter's formula

2. Winter's formula for expected PaCO₂ in metabolic acidosis:
   A. PaCO₂ = 1.0 × HCO₃⁻ + 12
