> [!important]
> Septic shock = **subset of sepsis** with profound circulatory and cellular/metabolic abnormalities carrying **>40% mortality**. Defined by: **vasopressor requirement to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg AND lactate >2 mmol/L** despite adequate fluid resuscitation.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Apply Sepsis-3 septic shock definition
- Execute the Hour-1 Bundle and 3-hour/6-hour bundles
- Titrate vasopressors and inotropes to haemodynamic targets
- Apply fluid responsiveness concepts to avoid overload
- Recognise and manage refractory septic shock

## 2. Definition (Sepsis-3, 2016)
**Septic shock** = Sepsis with:
1. Persisting hypotension requiring vasopressors to maintain **MAP ≥65 mmHg**
2. **Serum lactate >2 mmol/L (18 mg/dL)**  
**Despite adequate volume resuscitation** (30 mL/kg crystalloid)

**Mortality**: Hospital mortality ~40–50%

## 3. Haemodynamic Phenotypes
| Phenotype | Characteristics | Prevalence |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| **Hyperdynamic** (classic) | High CO, low SVR, warm peripheries | ~70% |
| **Hypodynamic** | Low CO, low SVR (myocardial depression) | ~30% |
| **Vasoplegic** | Refractory hypotension, very low SVR | Subset |

**Bedside echo** is essential to differentiate — guides inotrope vs vasopressor choice.

## 4. Hour-1 Bundle (Surviving Sepsis 2021)
1. **Measure lactate** (repeat if >2 mmol/L)
2. **Blood cultures** before antibiotics (2 sets + line cultures if present)
3. **Broad-spectrum antibiotics** within 1 hour
4. **30 mL/kg crystalloid** for hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L
5. **Vasopressors** if hypotensive during/after fluid resuscitation (target MAP ≥65)

## 5. Vasopressor Strategy
| Agent | Dose | Role | Key Points |
|-------|------|------|------------|
| **Noradrenaline** | 0.01–2 µg/kg/min | **First-line** | α₁ > β₁; increases MAP, minimal CO effect |
| **Vasopressin** | 0.01–0.04 U/min | **Add-on (2nd line)** | Non-catecholamine; spares noradrenaline; may improve renal perfusion |
| **Adrenaline** | 0.01–1 µg/kg/min | **2nd/3rd line** | α + β₁ + β₂; increases CO but ↑ lactate, arrhythmias |
| **Angiotensin II** | 2.5–80 ng/kg/min | **Refractory** | AT1 receptor; trials show MAP improvement |

**Titration**: Start noradrenaline **early** (even peripherally for short-term) — do not wait for central line.

## 6. Fluid Responsiveness in Septic Shock
- Only ~50% are fluid responders
- **Dynamic measures** > static:
  - **Passive Leg Raise (PLR)** + CO monitoring (gold standard)
  - **SVV / PPV** (requires: sedated, ventilated, sinus rhythm, TV ≥8 mL/kg)
  - **Mini-fluid challenge** (100 mL over 1 min)
- **Stop fluids** if no response → escalate vasopressors

## 7. Inotrope Use (if myocardial depression)
- **Dobutamine** 2.5–20 µg/kg/min (β₁ > β₂) — increases CO, may ↓ MAP (add noradrenaline)
- **Levosimendan** (Ca²⁺ sensitiser + PDE inhibitor) — alternative, longer half-life
- **Echo-guided**: LVEF <30% or new wall motion abnormality + low CO

## 8. Adjunctive Therapies
| Therapy | Indication | Evidence |
|---------|------------|----------|
| **Hydrocortisone** 200 mg/day IV | Vasopressor-dependent septic shock | ↓ Vasopressor duration, ?mortality |
| **IVIG** | Consider in streptococcal/staphylococcal TSS | Low certainty |
| **Blood products** | Hb <70 (or <80 if ischaemia) | Restrictive strategy |
| **Renal replacement** | Standard AKI indications | No early benefit |

## 9. Source Control — Critical Timeline
| Source | Intervention | Target Time |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Infected line | Remove | Immediately |
| Abscess | Drain (percut/surgical) | ≤6 hours |
| Cholangitis | ERCP | ≤24 hours |
| Necrotising fasciitis | Debridement | **Immediate** |
| Perforated viscus | Surgery | **Immediate** |

## 10. Refractory Septic Shock Definition
- MAP <65 mmHg despite **noradrenaline ≥0.5 µg/kg/min** (+ vasopressin)
- **Or** lactate >4 mmol/L not clearing
- **Or** new organ failure despite support

**Escalation**:
1. Add vasopressin 0.03 U/min
2. Consider adrenaline (if low CO)
3. Hydrocortisone 200 mg/day
4. Consider angiotensin II (if available)
5. ECMO (selected centres)

## 11. Red Flags
- Lactate not clearing (>10–20% per 2h)
- Rising noradrenaline dose >1 µg/kg/min
- New oliguria / anuria
- Ischaemic limbs / gut (vasopressor toxicity)

## 12. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- **Septic shock definition**: vasopressor + lactate >2 AFTER 30 mL/kg fluid
- **Noradrenaline first-line** — start early, peripherally if needed
- **Vasopressin add-on** at noradrenaline ~0.25–0.5 µg/kg/min (spares catecholamines)
- **Fluid responsiveness** must be tested — avoid fluid overload
- **Source control** within 6h for drainable foci
- **Hydrocortisone** if vasopressor-dependent
- **Echo** to differentiate hyperdynamic vs hypodynamic

## 14. Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- Giving 30 mL/kg fluid to **all** septic patients (some need less, some more — assess responsiveness)
- Delaying vasopressors waiting for central line
- Assuming "high CO" = no need for inotropes (myocardial depression still possible)
- Confusing sepsis (infection + organ dysfunction) with septic shock (subset with circulatory failure)

## 15. Mnemonics
- **SEPSIS-3**: SOFA ≥2 + infection
- **SEPTIC SHOCK**: Vasopressor + Lactate >2 + Fluids
- **Hour-1**: Lactate + Cultures + Antibiotics + Fluids + Vasopressors
- **Vasopressor ladder**: Noradrenaline → +Vasopressin → +Adrenaline → Hydrocortisone → Angiotensin II/ECMO

## 20. Flashcards
- Q: Septic shock definition (Sepsis-3)
  A: Vasopressor for MAP ≥65 + lactate >2 AFTER 30 mL/kg crystalloid
- Q: Hour-1 Bundle
  A: Lactate, Cultures, Antibiotics, 30 mL/kg Fluid, Vasopressors
- Q: First-line vasopressor
  A: Noradrenaline (start peripherally early)
- Q: Vasopressin dose
  A: 0.01–0.04 U/min (add-on at NA ~0.25–0.5)
- Q: Fluid responsiveness test
  A: PLR + CO monitoring (gold); SVV/PPV if ventilated
- Q: Hydrocortisone indication
  A: Vasopressor-dependent septic shock — 200 mg/day
- Q: Dobutamine in septic shock
  A: If myocardial depression (low EF, low CO) — may need NA combo
- Q: Source control
  A: Drainable foci ≤6h; necrotising fasciitis = immediate surgery
- Q: Refractory septic shock
  A: NA >0.5 + vasopressin + still hypotensive → adrenaline, hydrocortisone, consider ECMO
- Q: Balanced crystalloids vs saline
  A: Balanced preferred (less hyperchloraemic acidosis, less AKI)
