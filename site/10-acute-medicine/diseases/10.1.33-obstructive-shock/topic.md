> [!important]
> Obstructive shock = mechanical obstruction to blood flow (either into or out of the heart). Rapid diagnosis and relief of obstruction is lifesaving.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Recognise the three main causes of obstructive shock
- Use bedside ultrasound for rapid diagnosis
- Know the specific interventions for each cause

## 2. Main Causes
1. **Massive Pulmonary Embolism**
2. **Cardiac Tamponade**
3. **Tension Pneumothorax**

## 3. Clinical Differentiation
| Feature              | Massive PE          | Tamponade           | Tension PTX         |
|----------------------|---------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| JVP                  | Raised              | Raised              | Raised              |
| Heart sounds         | Loud P2             | Muffled             | Normal              |
| Trachea              | Central             | Central             | Deviated            |
| Breath sounds        | Normal              | Normal              | Reduced             |
| Pulsus paradoxus     | Possible            | Present             | Possible            |
| Bedside US           | RV strain           | Pericardial effusion| Lung sliding absent |

## 4. Management
**Massive PE**
- Thrombolysis (alteplase) if unstable
- Consider ECMO or surgical embolectomy

**Cardiac Tamponade**
- Urgent pericardiocentesis
- Fluid bolus while preparing

**Tension Pneumothorax**
- Immediate needle decompression (2nd ICS mid-clavicular line)
- Followed by chest drain

## 5. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- Tension pneumothorax is a **clinical diagnosis** — do not wait for CXR
- Tamponade shows **pulsus paradoxus** and electrical alternans
- Massive PE often has clear lungs despite severe shock

## 7. One-Page Revision Summary
**Obstructive = mechanical blockage**
PE → thrombolysis
Tamponade → pericardiocentesis
Tension PTX → needle decompression (clinical diagnosis)

## 8. Must Know / Should Know / Nice to Know
### Must Know
- Three causes: massive PE, cardiac tamponade, tension pneumothorax
- All cause raised JVP (obstructed venous return)
- Tension PTX = clinical diagnosis (don't wait for CXR)
- Immediate interventions: PE=thrombolysis, Tamponade=pericardiocentesis, Tension PTX=needle decompression

### Should Know
- Beck's triad (tamponade): hypotension, raised JVP, muffled heart sounds
- Pulsus paradoxus >10 mmHg in tamponade (also severe asthma, PE)
- RV strain on echo = massive PE
- Electrical alternans on ECG = tamponade (large effusion)

### Nice to Know
- Surgical pericardial window vs pericardiocentesis
- VA-ECMO for massive PE
- REBOA not for obstructive shock

## 11. Flashcards
- Q: Obstructive shock causes
  A: Massive PE, Cardiac tamponade, Tension pneumothorax
- Q: Tension PTX — clinical dx
  A: Don't wait for CXR — needle decompress 2nd ICS mid-clavicular
- Q: Tamponade — Beck's triad
  A: Hypotension + raised JVP + muffled heart sounds
- Q: Tamponade — pulsus paradoxus
  A: >10 mmHg drop SBP on inspiration
- Q: Massive PE — echo
  A: RV dilation (RV/LV >1), McConnell's sign
- Q: Electrical alternans
  A: Tamponade (large effusion)
- Q: PE thrombolysis
  A: Alteplase 100mg over 2h if unstable
- Q: All obstructive shock = raised JVP
  A: Yes (venous return obstructed)
