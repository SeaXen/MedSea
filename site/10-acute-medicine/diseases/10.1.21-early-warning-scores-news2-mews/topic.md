> [!important]
> NEWS2 is the **standardised** track-and-trigger system in UK NHS. MEWS is a simpler predecessor. Both detect deterioration — NEWS2 is more validated.

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Calculate NEWS2 and MEWS scores
- Know the trigger thresholds and escalation pathways
- Understand the "new confusion" and SpO₂ scale additions in NEWS2
- Apply scores clinically — never replace clinical judgement

**Use Scale 2** if patient has known Type 2 respiratory failure / COPD (document clearly)

## 3. NEWS2 Trigger Thresholds
| Aggregate Score | Clinical Risk | Response |
|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| 0 | Low | Routine monitoring |
| 1–4 | Low–Medium | Ward review / increase monitoring frequency |
| **5–6** | **Medium** | **Urgent review** (senior doctor / outreach team) |
| **7+** | **High** | **Emergency review** (critical care / MET call) |

**Single parameter score = 3** → urgent review regardless of aggregate

## 5. Key Differences: NEWS2 vs MEWS
- NEWS2 includes **SpO₂** (critical for respiratory failure)
- NEWS2 has **2 SpO₂ scales** (Scale 2 for COPD)
- NEWS2 uses **"new confusion"** explicitly
- NEWS2 is **more sensitive** for sepsis and respiratory deterioration

## 6. Clinical Application
- Calculate at every observation set
- **Escalate on single parameter = 3** even if aggregate low
- Document Scale 1 vs Scale 2 rationale
- Use to **trigger senior review**, not replace assessment

## 7. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- NEWS2 ≥7 = critical care outreach / MET call
- Single parameter of 3 = urgent review (even if total <5)
- Scale 2 for COPD — do not over-oxygenate
- New confusion is a **red flag** — score it immediately
