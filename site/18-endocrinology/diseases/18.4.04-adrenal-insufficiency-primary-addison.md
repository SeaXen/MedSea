# Primary Adrenal Insufficiency (Addison's Disease)

> [!info]
> **Primary Adrenal Insufficiency (Addison's Disease) = Chronic Cortisol + Aldosterone Deficiency due to Adrenal Cortex Destruction.** **Autoimmune (80-90%) Commonest Cause.** **Hyperpigmentation + Hypotension + Hyponatraemia + Hyperkalaemia** = Classic Quartet.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise clinical features of Addison's disease and adrenal crisis
- [ ] Apply diagnostic criteria (Synacthen test, ACTH, electrolytes)
- [ ] Differentiate primary from secondary/tertiary adrenal insufficiency
- [ ] Prescribe hormone replacement and stress-dose protocols
- [ ] Manage adrenal crisis and special situations (pregnancy, surgery, infections)

---

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Definition** | Primary Adrenal Cortex Failure → Cortisol + Aldosterone Deficiency |
| **Incidence** | 10-15/100,000/year; Prevalence 100-150/100,000 |
| **Peak Age** | 30-50 years |
| **Sex Ratio** | Female:Male = **2:1** (Autoimmune Predominance) |
| **Commonest Cause** | **Autoimmune Adrenalitis (80-90%)** |

---

## 3. Aetiology

| Category | Cause | Frequency |
|----------|-------|--------|
| **Autoimmune** | Autoimmune Adrenalitis (Isolated or APS-2) | **80-90%** |
| **Infectious** | TB (Global Leading Cause), HIV, CMV, Fungal (Histoplasma) | 10-15% |
| **Infiltrative/Metastatic** | Haemochromatosis, Sarcoidosis, Amyloidosis, Metastases (Lung, Breast, Renal) | Rare |
| **Haemorrhage/Infarction** | Adrenal Haemorrhage (Anticoagulation, Sepsis, Trauma), Waterhouse-Friderichsen | Rare |
| **Genetic** | Adrenoleukodystrophy (X-ALD), Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (21-OH Def) | Rare |
| **Drug-Induced** | Ketoconazole, Metronidazole, Etomidate, Rifampicin, Checkpoint Inhibitors | Increasing |
| **Bilateral Adrenalectomy** | Surgical | Iatrogenic |
| **Adrenoleukodystrophy** | X-Linked (ABCD1) | Childhood/Young Adult |

---

## 4. Autoimmune Adrenalitis (Addison's Disease)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Pathogenesis** | T-Cell Mediated Destruction of Adrenal Cortex → Cortisol + Aldosterone Deficiency |
| **Autoantibodies** | **21-Hydroxylase Antibodies** (>90% at Diagnosis); **17α-Hydroxylase**, **Side-Chain Cleavage** |
| **Associated Autoimmune (APS-2)** | **Type 1 DM (10-30%)**, **Thyroiditis (Hashimoto/Graves)**, **Pernicious Anaemia**, **Vitiligo**, **Coeliac** |
| **APS-2 (Schmidt)** | **Addison's + Autoimmune Thyroiditis ± Type 1 DM** |
| **APS-1 (Polyendocrinopathy-Candidiasis-Ectodermal Dystrophy)** | **AIRE Gene**; Chronic Mucocutaneous Candidiasis + Hypoparathyroidism + Addison's (Childhood) |

---

## 5. Clinical Features — The Classic Quartet

| Feature | Frequency | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Hyper pigmentation** | **>90%** | ACTH/MSH Cross-Reactivity → Melanocyte Stimulation |
| **Hypotension (Postural)** | **>90%** | Cortisol + Aldosterone Deficiency → Vasodilatation + Volume Depletion |
| **Hyponatraemia** | **80-90%** | Cortisol Deficiency → ADH ↑ + Aldosterone Deficiency → Na+ Loss |
| **Hyperkalaemia** | **60-80%** (Aldosterone Deficiency) | Aldosterone Deficiency → ↓ K+ Excretion |

### Additional Features
| System | Features |
|--------|----------|
| **Constitutional** | Fatigue, Weakness, Weight Loss, Anorexia, Nausea, Vomiting, Abdominal Pain |
| **Cardiovascular** | Postural Hypotension, Syncope, Bradycardia, Refractory Hypotension |
| **Gastrointestinal** | Nausea, Vomiting, Diarrhoea, Abdominal Pain, Weight Loss |
| **Neurological** | Lethargy, Confusion, Depression, Cognitive Impairment, Cravings (Salt) |
| **Skin** | **Hyper pigmentation** (Palmar Creases, Buccal Mucosa, Scars, Knuckles, Areolae) |
| **Reproductive** | Amenorrhoea, Loss of Libido, Low Libido |

## 7. Diagnosis — Confirmation Algorithm

### Biochemical Suspicion
| Trigger | Tests |
|---------|-------|
| **Clinical Suspicion** | **09:00 Cortisol + ACTH** (Paired) |

### Diagnostic Thresholds
| Test | Primary AI (Addison) | Secondary/Tertiary AI |
|------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **09:00 Cortisol** | **<100 nmol/L** (Diagnostic) | <100 nmol/L |
| **ACTH** | **Markedly Elevated (>100 pg/mL, Often >500)** | **Low / Inappropriately Normal** |
| **Synacthen Test (250µg)** | **Cortisol <400-500 nmol/L at 30/60min** | <400-500 nmol/L (But ACTH Low) |
| **Synacthen Test (1µg)** | <400-500 nmol/L (More Sensitive) | Same Pattern |
| **Renin** | **Elevated** (Secondary Hyperreninemia) | Normal/Low |
| **Aldosterone** | **Low** | Normal/Low |

### Autoantibodies
| Antibody | Sensitivity | Specificity |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **21-Hydroxylase Ab** | **>90%** (Diagnostic) | >99% |
| **17α-Hydroxylase Ab** | 30-40% | High |
| **Side-Chain Cleavage Ab** | 30-40% | High |

---

## 8. Synacthen (ACTH) Test Protocols

| Parameter | Standard (250µg) | Low-Dose (1µg) |
|-----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Dose** | 250µg Tetracosactide IM/IV | 1µg Tetracosactide IV |
| **Indication** | General Screening | **Secondary AI** (More Physiological) |
| **Sampling** | 0, 30, 60 min | 0, 20, 30, 60 min |
| **Normal Response** | **Cortisol ≥500-550 nmol/L at 30/60min** | Same |
| **Adrenal Insufficiency** | **Cortisol <400-500 nmol/L at 30/60min** | Same |
| **Contraindications** | Known Severe AI (Treat First) | Known Severe AI |

---

## 9. Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Cortisol | ACTH | Electrolytes | Key Features |
|-----------|----------|------|--------------|--------------|
| **Primary AI (Addison)** | Low | **High** | **Na+ ↓, K+ ↑** | Hyperpigmentation |
| **Secondary AI** | Low | Low/Normal | Na+ ↓, K+ Normal | No Pigmentation; Pituitary Signs |
| **Tertiary AI** | Low | Low/Normal | Na+ ↓, K+ Normal | Steroid Withdrawal; HPA Suppression |
| **Adrenal Crisis** | Very Low | Very High | Severe Na+ ↓, K+ ↑, Glucose ↓ | Hypotension, Coma, Hypoglycaemia |
| **Septic Shock** | Variable | Normal/High | Lactate ↑↑, Normal K+ | Fever, Elevated Lactate, Procalcitonin |

## 10. Management — Hormone Replacement

### Glucocorticoid Replacement (Cortisol)
| Regimen | Dose |
|---------|-------|
| **Hydrocortisone** | **15-20mg AM + 5-10mg PM** (Divided BD) |
| **Prednisolone** | 5mg OD (Longer Half-Life) |
| **Cortisone Acetate** | 25mg AM + 12.5mg PM |

**Principles**: Mimic Physiological Rhythm; **Highest Dose AM**; Avoid Evening Dose (Insomnia).

### Mineralocorticoid Replacement (Aldosterone)
| Drug | Dose | Monitoring |
|------|------|------------|
| **Fludrocortisone** | **50-100µg OD** (Up to 300µg) | BP (Postural), K+, Renin, Weight |

### DHEA Replacement (Optional)
| Population | Dose | Benefit |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Women (Symptomatic)** | **25-50mg/day** | Libido, Well-being, Bone Density |

---

## 11. Stress Dose Protocol

| Stress Level | Hydrocortisone IV | Duration |
|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Minor** (Dental, Minor Surgery) | **25mg IV/IM Pre-Op** | Single Dose |
| **Moderate** (Major Surgery, Sepsis, Trauma) | **50-100mg IV Bolus → 50mg q6-8h** | 24-48 Hours |
| **Major** (Cardiac Surgery, Septic Shock, Major Trauma) | **100mg IV Bolus → 50-100mg q6h** | 48-72 Hours |
| **Labour/Delivery** | 100mg IV Onset → 50mg q6h | Until 24h Post-Delivery |

**Oral Stress Dosing** (If IV Not Available): **Triple Usual Oral Dose** for Duration of Illness.

## 13. Special Situations

### Pregnancy
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|-----------|
| **Hydrocortisone** | **↑ Dose 20-40% in 2nd/3rd Trimester** |
| **Fludrocortisone** | Continue (Safe); Monitor BP, K+ |
| **Delivery** | **Stress Dose Hydrocortisone 100mg IV q6h** During Labour |
| **Postpartum** | Reduce to Pre-Pregnancy Doses |

### Surgery
| Phase | Management |
|-------|------------|
| **Pre-Op** | Stress Dose Hydrocortisone (Per Protocol) |
| **Intra-Op** | IV Hydrocortisone 50-100mg q6-8h |
| **Post-Op** | Taper to Maintenance Over 24-48h |

### Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (21-OH Deficiency)
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **Diagnosis** | 17-OHP ↑↑; Genetic Testing (CYP21A2) |
| **Glucocorticoid** | Hydrocortisone 10-15mg/m²/day (Divided TDS) |
| **Mineralocorticoid** | Fludrocortisone 50-200µg/day |
| **Monitoring** | 17-OHP, Androstenedione, Renin, Electrolytes, BP, Growth |

## 14. Monitoring & Follow-up

| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|

| **Renin** | **Primary Guide for Fludrocortisone** (Target: Upper Normal) |

## 16. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Primary vs Secondary AI** | Primary: ACTH ↑↑ + Hyperpigmentation + Hyperkalaemia; Secondary: ACTH Low, No Pigmentation, No Hyperkalaemia |
| **Cortisol Cut-offs** | 09:00 Cortisol <100 = AI; Synacthen <400-500 at 30/60 = AI |
| **Fludrocortisone Titration** | Target: **Renin Upper Normal**; If Renin High → ↑ Fludrocortisone |
| **Addisonian Crisis** | Hypotension + Hyponatraemia + Hyperkalaemia → **Hydrocortisone 100mg IV STAT** |
| **Stress Dosing** | Minor 25mg; Moderate 50-100mg; Major 100mg+50mg q6h |
| **TB in Addison** | Global Leading Cause (Non-Western); Anti-TB Rx + Steroids |
| **DHEA** | Optional; Women Only (Libido, Bone, Well-being) |

**Mnemonic: ADDISON'S**
- **A**ldosterone Deficiency → **Hyponatraemia + Hyperkalaemia**
- **D**eficit Cortisol → Hypotension, Fatigue, Hypoglycaemia
- **D**estruction Autoimmune (80-90%)
- **I**ncreased ACTH → **Hyperpigmentation**
- **S**odiuim Low, **O** Calcium High (No, K+ High)
- **N**ecrosis (Waterhouse-Friderichsen)
- **S**teroid Replacement (Hydrocortisone + Fludrocortisone)
