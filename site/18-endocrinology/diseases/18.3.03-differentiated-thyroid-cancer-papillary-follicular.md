# Differentiated Thyroid Cancer (Papillary & Follicular)

> [!info]
> **Differentiated Thyroid Cancer (DTC) = Papillary (80-85%) + Follicular (10-15%).** **Excellent Prognosis** (20-Year Survival >90%). **ATA Risk Stratification** (Low/Intermediate/High) Guides RAI and TSH Suppression.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Apply TNM staging (AJCC 8th) and ATA risk stratification
- [ ] Indicate radioactive iodine (RAI) therapy based on risk
- [ ] Apply TSH suppression targets by risk category
- [ ] Outline follow-up protocol (Thyroglobulin, Ultrasound, WBS)

---

## 2. Classification & Epidemiology

| Type | Frequency | Key Features |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| **Papillary (PTC)** | **80-85%** | **Most Common**; Lymphatic Spread; BRAF V600E (60%), RAS, RET/PTC Rearrangements |
| **Follicular (FTC)** | **10-15%** | Vascular Invasion; Haematogenous Spread (Bone, Lung); RAS, PAX8/PPARγ |
| **Hürthle Cell** | 3-5% | Oncocytic Variant; More Aggressive; Often Classified as FTC Variant |
| **Poorly Differentiated** | <5% | High Mitotic Rate, Necrosis; Intermediate Behaviour |

### Demographics
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Incidence** | **Increasing** (Better Detection); **3x Female > Male** |
| **Peak Age** | **30-50 Years** |
| **Risk Factors** | **Radiation Exposure** (Childhood), Iodine Deficiency, Genetic (Familial DTC 5%) |

## 3. TNM Staging (AJCC 8th Edition) — Differentiated Thyroid Cancer

### Age <55 Years (All Stage I/II)
| Stage | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **Stage I** | Any T, Any N, M0 |
| **Stage II** | Any T, Any N, M1 |

### Age ≥55 Years
| Stage | T | N | M |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|
| **I** | T1-T2 | N0/NX | M0 |
| **II** | T1-T2 | N1 | M0 |
| | T3a | Any N | M0 |
| **III** | T3b | Any N | M0 |
| | T4a | Any N | M0 |
| **IVA** | T4b | Any N | M0 |
| **IVB** | Any T | Any N | M1 |

### T Category (Primary Tumour)
| T | Criteria |
|---|----------|
| **T1a** | **≤1 cm** (Intrathyroidal) |
| **T1b** | **>1-2 cm** (Intrathyroidal) |
| **T2** | **>2-4 cm** (Intrathyroidal) |
| **T3a** | **>4 cm** (Intrathyroidal) OR **Minimal Extrathyroidal Extension** (Strap Muscles) |
| **T3b** | **Gross Extrathyroidal Extension** (Larynx, Trachea, Oesophagus, Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve) |
| **T4a** | Extension Beyond Thyroid to Prevertebral Fascia, Carotid Artery, Mediastinal Vessels |
| **T4b** | Carotid Artery Encasement, Prevertebral Fascia Invasion |

### N Category (Regional Nodes)
| N | Criteria |
|---|----------|
| **N0** | No Regional Nodes |
| **N1a** | **Central Compartment** (Level VI, VII: Prelaryngeal, Pretracheal, Paratracheal) |
| **N1b** | **Lateral Compartment** (Level I-V: Jugular, Scalene, Supraclavicular) |

## 4. ATA Risk Stratification (Post-Surgery)

| Risk Category | Criteria | Recurrence Risk |
|---------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Low** | No Local/Gross Invasion, No Nodal Mets, No Vascular Invasion, <0.5cm PTC, No Aggressive Histology, Thyroglobulin Undetectable, No RAI Uptake Outside Bed | **<5%** |
| **Intermediate** | Microscopic Invasion, N1 (≥5 Nodes), Vascular Invasion, 1-4cm PTC, Aggressive Histology (Tall Cell, Hobnail), Thyroglobulin Detectable, RAI Uptake Outside Bed | **5-20%** |
| **High** | Gross Extrathyroidal Extension, Incomplete Resection, Distant Mets, N1b (Lateral Nodes), Extensive Vascular Invasion, Thyroglobulin High, RAI Uptake Distant | **>20%** |

## 5. Management Algorithm

```
DTC Diagnosed (FNA/Histology)
         │
         ▼
TOTAL THYROIDECTOMY ± CENTRAL NECK DISSECTION (Level VI)
         │
         ├── T1a N0 (Low Risk) → **Lobectomy May Suffice**
         └── T1b/T2 or N+ or High Risk → **Total Thyroidectomy + CND**
         │
         ▼
POST-OP RISK STRATIFICATION (ATA)
         │
         ├── LOW RISK
         │       → RAI: **Not Routine** (Consider if Tg Detectable)
         │       → TSH **0.5-2.0 mIU/L**
         │
         ├── INTERMEDIATE RISK
         │       → **RAI 30-100 mCi** (Ablation)
         │       → TSH **0.1-0.5 mIU/L**
         │
         └── HIGH RISK
                 → **RAI 100-200 mCi** (Ablation + Treatment)
                 → TSH **<0.1 mIU/L**
```

## 6. Radioactive Iodine (RAI) Therapy

| Indication | Dose | Preparation |
|------------|------|-------------|
| **Remnant Ablation (Low/Intermediate)** | **30-100 mCi** | **Low Iodine Diet** 2 Weeks; **TSH >30** (Thyroxine Withdrawal 4-6wk) OR **rhTSH** (Thyrogen) |
| **Adjuvant Treatment (High Risk)** | **100-200 mCi** | Same Preparation |
| **Metastatic Disease** | **150-250 mCi** (Repeated q6-12mo) | Same; May Need Hospitalisation |

### RAI Preparation
| Step | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Low Iodine Diet** | 2 Weeks Pre-RAI (<50 µg/day) |
| **TSH Stimulation** | **Thyroxine Withdrawal** (LT4 Stop 4-6wk, LT3 Stop 2wk) → TSH >30 **OR rhTSH** (Thyrogen 0.9mg IM Day 1 & 2) |
| **Contraception** | **6 Months Post-RAI** (Both Sexes) |
| **Pregnancy Test** | Mandatory Pre-RAI (Serum β-hCG) |

---

## 7. TSH Suppression Targets

| Risk Category | TSH Target (mIU/L) | Duration |
|---------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Low** | **0.5-2.0** | Lifelong (Adjust for Age/Comorbidities) |
| **Intermediate** | **0.1-0.5** | 5-10 Years, Then Reassess |
| **High** | **<0.1** | 10+ Years / Lifelong |

> **Elderly/Osteoporosis/AF**: Relax Targets (0.5-2.0 for Low, 0.5-1.0 for Intermediate)

---

## 8. Follow-Up & Surveillance

| Timepoint | Investigations |
|-----------|----------------|
| **6-8 Weeks Post-RAI** | Tg, Anti-Tg, TSH, fT4, Neck US, Diagnostic WBS (If Post-Therapy) |
| **6 Months** | Tg, Anti-Tg, TSH, fT4, Neck US |
| **12 Months** | Tg, Anti-Tg, TSH, fT4, Neck US, **Diagnostic WBS** (If Intermediate/High) |
| **Annual (Years 2-5)** | Tg, Anti-Tg, TSH, Neck US |
| **Long-Term (>5yr)** | Tg, TSH, Neck US (Low Risk: q1-2yr); WBS if Tg Rising |

### Thyroglobulin (Tg) Monitoring
| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Tg Undetectable (<0.5-1 ng/mL) + TSH Stimulated** | **Excellent Response** → De-Escalate Surveillance |
| **Tg Detectable (1-10 ng/mL)** | **Indeterminate** → Neck US, Consider Diagnostic WBS |
| **Tg Rising / >10 ng/mL** | **Structural Recurrence Likely** → Diagnostic WBS, PET-CT, Cross-Sectional Imaging |
| **Anti-Tg Positive** | **Interferes with Tg Assay** → Use Trend, Not Absolute Value |

---

## 9. Recurrent/Persistent Disease

| Site | Management |
|------|------------|
| **Central Neck Nodes** | **Completion CND** (If Missed) or **RAI** (If RAI-Avid) |
| **Lateral Neck Nodes** | **Modified Radical Neck Dissection** (Levels II-V) |
| **Distant Mets (Lung/Bone)** | **RAI** (If Avid) → **TKI (Lenvatinib/Sorafenib)** (If Refractory) |
| **Non-RAI-Avid** | **TKI (Lenvatinib/Sorafenib)**, Clinical Trials, EBRT (Bone) |

## 10. Special Situations

### Pregnancy
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **RAI** | **Contraindicated** |
| **Surgery** | 2nd Trimester Preferred (If Urgent) |
| **TSH Target** | **<2.5 mIU/L** (1st Tri), <3.0 (2nd/3rd) |
| **Tg Monitoring** | Safe (No RAI) |

### Pediatric
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **More Aggressive** | Higher Nodal/Distant Mets Rate |
| **Surgery** | Total Thyroidectomy + CND (Standard) |
| **RAI** | More Liberal Indications |
| **Long-Term** | Higher Secondary Cancer Risk (RAI) |
