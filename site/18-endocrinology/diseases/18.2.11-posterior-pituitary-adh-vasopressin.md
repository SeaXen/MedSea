# Posterior Pituitary: ADH/Vasopressin

> [!info]
> **ADH (Vasopressin, Arginine Vasopressin) = Posterior pituitary hormone.** Synthesised in hypothalamus, released from posterior pituitary. Central to water homeostasis and blood pressure regulation. Key pathologies: Diabetes Insipidus (Central/Nephrogenic) and SIADH.

## 2. ADH (Vasopressin) Physiology

### Synthesis & Secretion
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Synthesis** | Hypothalamic nuclei: **Supraoptic (main)** + **Paraventricular** |
| **Transport** | Axoplasmic transport → Posterior pituitary (neurohypophysis) |
| **Structure** | 9 amino acid peptide; **Arginine Vasopressin (AVP)** in humans |
| **Half-life** | **10-20 minutes** (rapid) |
| **Main Regulator** | **Plasma Osmolality** (Osmoreceptors in OVLT → Supraoptic/PVN) |
| **Secondary Regulators** | Volume/BP (Baroreceptors: Low pressure → ↑ ADH); Nausea, Pain, Stress, Hypoglycaemia, Angiotensin II |

### ADH Receptors & Actions

| Receptor | Location | Primary Action | Clinical Use |
|----------|----------|----------------|--------------|
| **V2** | **Renal Collecting Duct** (Principal cells) | **Water Reabsorption** (↑ Aquaporin-2 insertion) | **Desmopressin** (DDAVP) = V2 agonist |
| **V1a** | **Vascular Smooth Muscle** | Vasoconstriction (Pressor effect) | Vasopressin (V1a/V2) for septic shock |
| **V1b** | Anterior Pituitary (Corticotrophs) | ACTH Release (Synergistic with CRH) | Not targeted therapeutically |
| **V2 → cAMP → AQP2** | Collecting duct cells | ↑ AQP2 insertion → Water permeability → **Concentrated Urine** |

## 3. ADH Regulation

### Primary Stimulus: Plasma Osmolality
| Osmolality | ADH Response |
|------------|--------------|
| **<280 mOsm/kg** | **Maximal Suppression** (ADH undetectable) |
| **280-295** | Gradual Increase |
| **>295 mOsm/kg** | **Steep Rise** (1% Osm rise → 1-2 pg/mL ADH rise) |

**Threshold**: ~280-285 mOsm/kg (Adiposity shifts threshold)

### Volume / Baroreceptor Regulation
| Volume Status | Baroreceptor Input | ADH Response |
|---------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Normal Volume** | Normal firing | Baseline ADH |
| **Hypovolaemia** (↓10%) | ↓ Baroreceptor firing | **↑ ADH** (Non-osmotic release) |
| **Severe Hypovolaemia** (↓20-30%) | Marked ↓ firing | **Massive ADH** (Overrides osmostat) |

**Key**: Volume depletion → ADH release **even if osmolality low** (Non-osmotic release)

### Other Stimuli
| Stimulus | ADH Effect |
|---------|------------|
| **Nausea / Vomiting** | Potent stimulus (via chemoreceptor trigger zone) |
| **Pain / Stress** | ↑ ADH |
| **Hypoglycaemia** | ↑ ADH |
| **Angiotensin II** | ↑ ADH |
| **Alcohol** | **Inhibits** ADH (Diuresis) |
| **Atrial Natriuretic Peptide (ANP)** | Inhibits ADH |

## 4. Diabetes Insipidus (DI) — Classification & Diagnosis

| Type | Pathology | ADH Level | Urine Concentration | Key Differentiator |
|------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Central DI** | Pituitary stalk/hypothalamus lesion | **Low/Absent** | Dilute | **Responds to Desmopressin** |
| **Nephrogenic DI** | Renal resistance to ADH | **High** | Dilute | **No response to Desmopressin** |
| **Primary Polydipsia** | Excessive water intake | **Suppressed** (Physiological) | Dilute/Normal | **Concentrates during deprivation** |
| **Gestational DI** | Placental Vasopressinase | **Relative Deficiency** | Dilute | Pregnancy; Responds to Desmopressin |

### Aetiology

#### Central DI
| Cause | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| **Idiopathic** | ~30% |
| **Pituitary Surgery / Trauma** | ~20% |
| **Infiltration** (Sarcoidosis, LCH, Metastases, Haemochromatosis) | ~15% |
| **Genetic** (AVP gene mutations) | Rare |
| **Autoimmune** (Lymphocytic Infundibuloneurohypophysitis) | ~10% |

#### Nephrogenic DI
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Lithium** | ↓ AQP2 expression, ↓ cAMP (Most common acquired) |
| **Drugs** | Demeclocycline, Amphotericin B, Foscarnet, Ofloxacin |
| **Electrolyte** | Hypercalcaemia, Hypokalaemia |
| **Obstructive Uropathy** | Tubulointerstitial damage |
| **Genetic** | AQP2 (Autosomal Recessive), AVPR2 (X-linked Recessive) |

## 5. Water Deprivation Test (WDT) — Gold Standard for DI

### Protocol
| Phase | Procedure | Target |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **1. Fluid Deprivation** | NPO + Fluid restriction; Weight, Serum/Urine Osmolality q1-2h | Until **↑ >5% weight loss** OR **Urine Osm > Plasma Osm** |
| **2. Desmopressin Challenge** | **DDAVP 2µg SC/IV** (or 10-40µg Intranasal) | Urine Osmolality at 60min |

### Interpretation Matrix
| Result | Central DI | Nephrogenic DI | Primary Polydipsia |
|------|------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Baseline Urine Osm** | Low (<300) | Low (<300) | Low/Variable |
| **After Deprivation** | Low (<300) | Low (<300) | **Concentrates** (>600) |
| **Post-DDAVP (60min)** | **↑ >50%** | **No Response** (<10% rise) | N/A (Already concentrated) |
| **Plasma ADH** | Low | High | Low |

### Stop Criteria (Safety)
| Stop If | Urine Osm > Plasma Osm |
|---------|------------------------|
| **Weight Loss** | >5% body weight |
| **Haemodynamics** | SBP <90, Tachycardia, Syncope |
| **Time** | >8 hours (usually 4-6h) |

## 6. SIADH (Syndrome of Inappropriate ADH Secretion)

### Diagnostic Criteria (Schwartz-Bartter)
| Criterion | Required |
|-----------|----------|
| **1. Hyponatraemia** | Serum Na+ <135 mmol/L |
| **2. Hypo-osmolality** | Serum Osm <275 mOsm/kg |
| **3. Inappropriately Concentrated Urine** | Urine Osm >100 mOsm/kg (usually >Serum) |
| **4. Elevated Urine Sodium** | Urine Na+ >20 mmol/L (Euvolaemic) |
| **5. Normal Thyroid/Adrenal/Renal** | TSH, Cortisol, Creatinine Normal |
| **6. No Diuretics** | Exclude Recent Diuretic Use |

### Aetiology
| Category | Causes |
|----------|--------|
| **Drugs** | **SSRIs, SNRIs, Carbamazepine, Cyclophosphamide, Vincristine, Chlorpropamide, PPIs** |
| **Malignancy** | **Small Cell Lung Cancer** (Most common), Pancreatic, Lymphoma, Thymoma |
| **CNS** | Stroke, Haemorrhage, Meningitis, Encephalitis, Guillain-Barré, Pituitary Surgery |
| **Pulmonary** | Pneumonia, TB, Lung Abscess, Positive Pressure Ventilation |
| **Endocrine** | Hypothyroidism, Adrenal Insufficiency |
| **Post-op / Pain / Nausea** | Transient |

## 7. SIADH — Management

### Stepwise Approach
| Step | Intervention | Indication |
|------|--------------|------------|
| **1. Treat Cause** | Discontinue offending drug; Treat infection/malignancy | Always 1st |
| **2. Fluid Restriction** | **<1 L/day** (1000-1500 mL) | 1st line (Mild-Moderate) |
| **3. Pharmacological** | **Demeclocycline** 600-900mg/day (Inhibits ADH action on V2) | Refractory / Cannot restrict fluids |
| **4. Vaptans (V2 Antagonists)** | **Tolvaptan** 15-60mg OD (Start 15mg, titrate) | **Severe symptomatic** (Na+ <120) / Refractory |
| **5. Hypertonic Saline** | **3% NaCl 100-150mL over 1-2h** (Target Na+ rise 4-6 mmol/L/24h) | **Severe Neuro Symptoms** (Seizures, Coma) |

### Demeclocycline
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Mechanism** | Inhibits ADH-induced cAMP in collecting duct → Nephrogenic DI-like state |
| **Dose** | 150mg QDS → Titrate to 300mg QDS (Max 600-1200mg/day) |
| **Onset** | 3-5 days |
| **Toxicity** | Photosensitivity, Nephrotoxicity, Hepatotoxicity, Azotemia |

### Tolvaptan (Vaptan)
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Mechanism** | **Selective V2 Receptor Antagonist** → Aquaresis (Water excretion without Na+ loss) |
| **Dose** | 15mg OD PO → Titrate to 30, 45, 60mg OD |
| **Monitoring** | Serum Na+ q4-6h initially; Liver function (Hepatotoxicity risk) |
| **Contraindications** | Anuria, Hypovolaemia, Pregnancy, Concomitant strong CYP3A4 inhibitors |
| **Duration** | Max 30 days (US); Longer in some regions |

## 8. Desmopressin (DDAVP) — Central DI & Others

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Mechanism** | **Synthetic V2 Agonist** (No V1 pressor effect) |
| **Route** | Intranasal (10-40µg), Oral (0.1-0.4mg), SC/IV (2-4µg) |
| **Half-life** | **2-4 hours** (vs ADH 10-20min) |
| **Central DI** | **1st Line**: DDAVP 10-40µg Intranasal q12-24h / 0.1-0.4mg Oral q12-24h / 2µg SC/IV q24h |
| **Nocturnal Enuresis** | DDAVP 0.2-0.6mg Oral at bedtime |
| **Bleeding Disorders** (vWD, Haemophilia A) | DDAVP 0.3µg/kg IV → ↑ vWF + Factor VIII |
| **Cautions** | **Hyponatraemia** (Water retention); Fluid restriction mandatory |

---

## 9. Cerebral Salt Wasting (CSW) vs SIADH

| Feature | **SIADH** | **Cerebral Salt Wasting (CSW)** |
|---------|-----------|----------------------------------|
| **Volume Status** | **Euvolaemic** | **Hypovolaemic** |
| **Urine Na+** | >20 mmol/L | >20 mmol/L (often higher) |
| **Serum Na+** | Low | Low |
| **Urine Osm** | >100 (Inappropriate) | High |
| **Volume Clues** | Normal JVP, No oedema | **Low JVP, Dry mucosa, Postural hypotension** |
| **Urine Output** | Low/Normal | **High** (Polyuria) |
| **Management** | Fluid restriction, Vaptans | **IV Saline + Fludrocortisone** |
| **Context** | Euvolaemic hyponatraemia | **Post-neurosurgery, TBI, SAH** |

## 10. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **ADH Receptors** | **V2 = Renal Water Reabsorption** (Desmopressin); **V1a = Vasoconstriction** |
| **DI Types** | Central (Low ADH) vs Nephrogenic (High ADH, Renal Resistance) |
| **WDT Stop Criteria** | Weight loss >5% OR Urine Osm > Plasma Osm |
| **Central DI** | DDAVP → Urine Osm ↑ >50% |
| **Nephrogenic DI** | DDAVP → No Response |
| **Primary Polydipsia** | Urine Concentrates During Deprivation (No DDAVP needed) |
| **SIADH Criteria** | Hyponatraemia + Hypo-osmolality + Urine Osm >100 + Urine Na+ >20 + Euvolaemic |
| **SIADH Treatment** | Fluid Restriction <1L → Demeclocycline → Tolvaptan (Severe) |
| **Tolvaptan** | V2 Antagonist; Aquaresis; Hepatotoxicity Risk; Max 30 days |
| **DDAVP** | V2 Agonist; Central DI 1st Line; Nocturnal Enuresis; vWD/Haemophilia A |
| **CSW vs SIADH** | CSW = Hypovolaemic + Polyuria; SIADH = Euvolaemic + Low Urine Output |
| **Lithium** | **Most Common Cause of Nephrogenic DI** (↓ AQP2) |
| **Gestational DI** | Placental Vasopressinase → Responds to DDAVP |

## 11. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **DI vs SIADH** | DI = Polyuria + Dilute Urine; SIADH = Oliguria + Concentrated Urine |
| **Central vs Nephrogenic DI** | DDAVP Response: Central = Works; Nephrogenic = Fails |
| **Primary Polydipsia vs DI** | WDT: Polydipsia concentrates urine during deprivation; DI does not |
| **SIADH vs CSW** | SIADH = Euvolaemic; CSW = Hypovolaemic (JVP, Postural drop) |
| **DDAVP vs Vasopressin** | DDAVP = Pure V2 (No pressor effect); Vasopressin = V1+V2 |
| **DDAVP in Enuresis** | Intranasal/Oral at bedtime; No fluid restriction needed |
| **DDAVP in vWD** | ↑ vWF + Factor VIII; Use before surgery/dental |

## 17. Flashcards
- **Q: ADH synthesis site**
  **A: Supraoptic + Paraventricular nuclei → axonal transport → posterior pituitary**

- **Q: ADH main regulator**
  **A: Plasma osmolality (threshold ~285 mOsm/kg) via OVLT osmoreceptors**

- **Q: V2 receptor**
  **A: Collecting duct principal cells → cAMP → AQP2 insertion → water reabsorption**

- **Q: V1a receptor**
  **A: Vascular smooth muscle → vasoconstriction (pressor effect)**

- **Q: Central DI**
  **A: Low/absent ADH → dilute urine → responds to desmopressin (>50% urine osm rise)**

- **Q: Nephrogenic DI**
  **A: High ADH + renal resistance → no response to desmopressin; Li+ common cause**

- **Q: Primary polydipsia**
  **A: Psychogenic polydipsia → urine concentrates during water deprivation**

- **Q: SIADH criteria**
  **A: Hyponatraemia + hypo-osmolality + urine osm >100 + urine Na+ >20 + euvolaemic**

- **Q: SIADH Rx ladder**
  **A: Fluid restrict <1L → demeclocycline → tolvaptan (severe) → 3% saline (emergency)**

- **Q: CSW vs SIADH**
  **A: CSW = hypovolaemic + polyuria; SIADH = euvolaemic + oliguria**

- **Q: DDAVP uses**
  **A: Central DI, nocturnal enuresis, vWD/haemophilia A (↑ vWF + FVIII)**

- **Q: Tolvaptan**
  **A: V2 antagonist → aquaresis; hepatotoxicity risk; max 30 days US**

- **Q: Demeclocycline**
  **A: Tetracycline derivative → nephrogenic DI-like state; onset 3-5 days; photosensitivity**

- **Q: WDT stop criteria**
  **A: Weight loss >5% OR urine osm > plasma osm OR haemodynamic instability**

- **Q: Lithium DI**
  **A: Downregulates AQP2 expression; most common acquired nephrogenic DI**
