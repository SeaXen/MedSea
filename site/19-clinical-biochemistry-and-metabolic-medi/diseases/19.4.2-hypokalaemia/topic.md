# Hypokalaemia

> [!info]
> **Hypokalaemia = Serum K⁺ <3.5 mmol/L.** Common Electrolyte Disorder. **Life-Threatening Arrhythmia Risk** (Especially with Digoxin). **Always Check Mg²⁺** (Hypomagnesaemia Causes Refractory Hypokalaemia). **ECG Changes**: U Waves, ST Depression, QT Prolongation.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise ECG changes of hypokalaemia and arrhythmia risk
- [ ] Differentiate renal vs extrarenal causes using urine K⁺
- [ ] Apply oral and IV potassium replacement protocols
- [ ] Recognise and treat hypomagnesaemia as cause of refractory hypokalaemia
- [ ] Manage hypokalaemia in special contexts (DKA, Digoxin Toxicity, Refeeding)

## 2. Definitions & Severity

| Severity | Serum K⁺ (mmol/L) | ECG Changes | Clinical Risk |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Mild** | 3.0-3.5 | None / Subtle U Waves | Low |
| **Moderate** | 2.5-3.0 | **U Waves Prominent**, ST Depression, T Wave Flattening | Arrhythmia Risk ↑ (With Digoxin) |
| **Severe** | **<2.5** | **Prominent U Waves**, ST Depression, **QT Prolongation**, Flattened T Waves | **Life-Threatening Arrhythmias** (VT/VF, Digoxin Toxicity) |

---

## 2. Aetiology — Renal vs Extrarenal (Urine K⁺ Key Discriminator)

### Renal Losses (Urine K⁺ >20 mmol/24h or Urine K⁺/Creatinine >13 mmol/mmol)
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Diuretics** (Loop, Thiazide) | ↑ Distal Na⁺ Delivery → ↑ K⁺ Secretion |
| **Mineralocorticoid Excess** (Primary Hyperaldosteronism, Cushing's, Liquorice, Carbenoxolone) | ENaC Activation → ↑ K⁺ Secretion |
| **Renal Tubular Acidosis (RTA)** | Distal Acidification Defect → ↑ K⁺ Wasting |
| **Magnesium Deficiency** | Impairs Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase & ROMK → Renal K⁺ Wasting |
| **Renal Tubular Disorders** (Bartter, Gitelman, Fanconi) | Genetic Tubular Transport Defects |
| **Diabetic Ketoacidosis (Post-Treatment)** | Insulin + K⁺ Shift In + Osmotic Diuresis |
| **Hyperglycaemia / Osmotic Diuresis** | ↑ Distal Flow → ↑ K⁺ Secretion |

### Extrarenal Losses (Urine K⁺ <20 mmol/24h)
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **GI Losses** (Vomiting, Diarrhoea, Laxative Abuse, High-Output Stoma/Fistula) | Direct K⁺ Loss + Volume Depletion → 2° Aldosterone ↑ |
| **Insulin / Alkalosis** (Transcellular Shift) | K⁺ Moves Into Cells (Not True Loss) |
| **Inadequate Intake** | Malnutrition, Anorexia, TPN without K⁺ |
| **Sweating** | Minor K⁺ Loss (Usually Not Sole Cause) |

### Transcellular Shift (Pseudohypokalaemia)
| Cause | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **Insulin** | ↑ Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase → K⁺ Shift Into Cells |
| **Alkalosis** | H⁺ Moves Out of Cells → K⁺ Moves In |
| **β₂-Agonists** (Salbutamol, Isoprenaline) | ↑ cAMP → ↑ Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase |
| **Hypothermia** | ↓ Cellular Metabolism |
| **Barium Poisoning** | Blocks K⁺ Channels |

## 2. Clinical Presentation & ECG

| Severity | K⁺ (mmol/L) | ECG Findings | Clinical Features |
|----------|-------------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Mild** | 3.0-3.5 | Subtle U Waves | Asymptomatic or Mild Fatigue |
| **Moderate** | 2.5-3.0 | **Prominent U Waves**, ST Depression, T Wave Flattening | Muscle Weakness, Cramps, Polyuria, Constipation |
| **Severe** | **<2.5** | **Prominent U Waves**, **ST Depression**, **QT Prolongation**, Flattened T, **Arrhythmias** | Paralysis, Ileus, Rhabdomyolysis, **VT/VF, Torsades** |

### ECG Hallmarks
| ECG Change | Significance |
|------------|--------------|
| **U Waves** | **Most Specific** (Best Seen in V2-V3) |
| **ST Depression** | Common (ST Segment Sagging) |
| **T Wave Flattening/Inversion** | Often Coexists with U Waves |
| **QT Prolongation** | Due to Prominent U Waves Merging with T |
| **Arrhythmias** | Ectopy, VT/VF, Torsades de Pointes (Especially with Digoxin) |

## 3. Assessment Algorithm

```
HYPOKALAEMIA (K⁺ <3.5)
         │
         ▼
ECG IMMEDIATELY (Check for U Waves, QT Prolongation, Arrhythmias)
         │
         ▼
CHECK MAGNESIUM (Hypomagnesaemia → Refractory Hypokalaemia)
         │
         ▼
URINE K⁺ (24h or Spot K⁺/Creatinine Ratio)
         │
         ├── URINE K⁺ >20 mmol/24h (or K⁺/Cr >13) → **RENAL LOSSES**
         │       ├── Diuretics (Loop/Thiazide) → Reduce/Stop; K⁺ Sparing Diuretic
         │       ├── Mineralocorticoid Excess (Conn's, Cushing's, Liquorice) → Treat Cause
         │       ├── RTA (Distal) → Alkali Therapy + K⁺ Supplement
         │       ├── Bartter/Gitelman → K⁺ Supplement + NSAIDs (Bartter) / K⁺ Sparing
         │       ├── Mg²⁺ Deficiency → **Correct Mg²⁺ First** (Refractory Hypokalaemia)
         │       └── Post-DKA / Osmotic Diuresis → Monitor & Replace
         │
         └── URINE K⁺ <20 mmol/24h → **EXTRAreNAL / SHIFT**
                 ├── GI Losses (Vomiting, Diarrhoea, Laxatives) → Replace K⁺ + Fluid
                 ├── Transcellular Shift (Insulin, Alkalosis, β₂-Agonists) → Correct Cause
                 └── Inadequate Intake → Dietary K⁺ + Supplement
```

## 3. Management

### Oral Replacement (Preferred for Asymptomatic/Moderate)
| Preparation | Elemental K⁺ | Typical Dose |
|-------------|--------------|--------------|
| **KCl Liquid** | 20 mmol/15mL | 20-40 mmol TDS (60-120 mmol/d) |
| **KCl Tablets (Slow-K)** | 8 mmol (600mg) | 1-2 Tablets TDS (24-48 mmol/d) |
| **KCl Effervescent (Sando-K)** | 12 mmol/Tablet | 1-2 Tablets TDS (36-72 mmol/d) |
| **Potassium Citrate** | 12 mmol/Sachet | 1-2 Sachets TDS |

> **Take with Food/Water** to Reduce GI Irritation.

### IV Replacement (Severe / Symptomatic / Unable Oral)
| Parameter | Protocol |
|-----------|----------|
| **IV Fluid** | 0.9% NaCl or D5W (NOT 0.45% NaCl — Rapid K⁺ Shift) |
| **Concentration** | **Max 40 mmol/L** (Peripheral); Up to 80 mmol/L (Central, Monitored) |
| **Rate** | **Peripheral: 10 mmol/h Max**; **Central: 20-40 mmol/h** (Cardiac Monitor) |
| **Total Daily Dose** | **40-80 mmol/day** (Up to 120-160 mmol in Severe) |
| **Monitoring** | **Serum K⁺ q2-4h**; ECG Continuous; Urine Output >0.5 mL/kg/h |

### Severe Hypokalaemia (K⁺ <2.5 / ECG Changes / Symptoms)
| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. Cardiac Monitor** | Continuous ECG |
| **2. IV KCl** | **20 mmol in 100mL 0.9% NaCl over 1h** (Central: 20mmol in 50mL) |
| **3. Repeat** | Recheck K⁺ q1-2h; Repeat Bolus Until K⁺ >3.0 |
| **4. Mg²⁺** | **IV MgSO₄ 2-4g** if Hypomagnesaemia |
| **5. Digoxin** | **Stop/Reduce** if Toxicity Suspected |

## 3. Hypomagnesaemia & Refractory Hypokalaemia

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Mechanism** | Mg²⁺ Required for Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase & ROMK Channel Function |
| **Urine K⁺** | Inappropriately High Despite Hypokalaemia |
| **Clinical** | Tetany, Seizures, Arrhythmias, Refractory Hypokalaemia |
| **Diagnosis** | **Serum Mg²⁺ <0.7 mmol/L** |
| **Treatment** | **IV MgSO₄ 2-4g (8-16 mmol) over 20-30 min**; Then 1g/h Infusion |
| **Key Principle** | **Correct Mg²⁺ FIRST** — K⁺ Will Not Normalise Until Mg²⁺ Repleted |

---

## 3. Special Contexts

| Context | Specific Considerations |
|---------|------------------------|
| **Digoxin Toxicity** | Hypokalaemia ↑ Digoxin Binding → Toxicity; **Stop Digoxin**; Correct K⁺ Urgently |
| **DKA Post-Treatment** | Insulin Drives K⁺ Into Cells; Monitor q1-2h; Replace Proactively |
| **Refeeding Syndrome** | Insulin Surge → K⁺/PO₄/Mg²⁺ Shift In; Monitor q6h; Slow Calorie Introduction |
| **Diuretic-Induced** | Add K⁺-Sparing (Spironolactone 25-50mg / Amiloride 5-10mg) |
| **Conn's Syndrome** | Primary Hyperaldosteronism → Hypokalaemia + HTN; CT Adrenals / AVS |
| **Bartter/Gitelman** | Genetic; Hypokalaemia, Metabolic Alkalosis, Normal/ Low BP |

---

## 3. Monitoring & Targets

| Parameter | Target | Frequency |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Serum K⁺** | **>4.0 mmol/L** | q2-4h (IV); Daily (PO) |
| **ECG** | Resolution of U Waves, QT Normalisation | Continuous (IV); Daily (PO) |
| **Magnesium** | >0.7 mmol/L | q6-12h (IV); Daily (PO) |
| **Urine Output** | >0.5 mL/kg/h | Hourly (IV) |
| **Digoxin Level** | 0.8-2.0 ng/mL (If on Digoxin) | q24-48h |

## 8. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Renal vs Extrarenal** | Urine K⁺ >20 = Renal; <20 = Extrarenal/Shift |
| **Mg²⁺ & K⁺** | **Correct Mg FIRST** (Mg Needed for Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase & ROMK) |
| **ECG U Waves** | **Most Specific Sign** of Hypokalaemia (Best Seen V2-V3) |
| **Digoxin + Hypokalaemia** | **Stop Digoxin** (↑ Toxicity Risk) |
| **IV KCl Rate** | Peripheral 10 mmol/h; Central 20-40 mmol/h (Monitored) |
| **IV KCl Concentration** | Peripheral Max 40 mmol/L; Central Max 80 mmol/L |
| **QT Prolongation** | Due to Prominent U Waves Merging with T Wave |
| **Refractory Hypokalaemia** | Check Mg²⁺ (<0.7 → Correct First) |
| **Urine K⁺/Cr Ratio** | >13 mmol/mmol = Renal Loss (Alternative to 24h Urine) |
| **Alkalosis + Hypokalaemia** | H⁺ Out → K⁺ In (Transcellular Shift, Not True Loss) |
