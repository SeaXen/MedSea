# Hypernatraemia

> [!info]
> **Hypernatraemia = Serum Na⁺ >145 mmol/L.** Represents **Free Water Deficit** relative to Sodium. Indicates Impaired Thirst or Access to Water, or Excessive Water Loss. **Mortality Up to 50%** if Severe (>160) or Rapid Correction.

## 2. Aetiology & Classification by Volume Status

### Hypovolaemic Hypernatraemia (Na⁺ & Water Loss, Water > Na⁺)
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **GI Losses** (Diarrhoea, Vomiting, High-Output Stoma) | Water Loss > Na⁺ Loss |
| **Renal Losses** (Diuretics, Osmotic Diuresis, Diabetes Insipidus, Salt-Wasting Nephropathy) | Impaired Water Reabsorption |
| **Cutaneous Losses** (Burns, Sweating, Fever) | Insensible + Sensible Water Loss |
| **Respiratory Losses** (Hyperventilation, Tracheostomy) | Increased Respiratory Water Loss |

### Euvolaemic Hypernatraemia (Water Deficit Only)
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Water Deprivation** (Impaired Thirst, Impaired Access, Neurogenic) | Inadequate Intake |
| **Diabetes Insipidus** (Central/Nephrogenic) | Impaired ADH Action → Massive Dilute Urine |
| **Lactation / Hypermetabolic States** | Increased Water Requirement |
| **Hyperventilation** | Respiratory Water Loss |

### Hypervolaemic Hypernatraemia (Sodium Gain > Water Gain)
| Cause | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Iatrogenic** (Hypertonic Saline, Sodium Bicarbonate, Blood Transfusion) | Exogenous Sodium Load |
| **Hypertonic Feeds / TPN** | Sodium Gain > Water |
| **Salt Poisoning** (Salt Ingestion, Seawater Ingestion) | |
| **Mineralocorticoid Excess** (Conn's Syndrome) | Na⁺ Retention > K⁺/H⁺ Loss |

## 2. Diabetes Insipidus (Central & Nephrogenic)

### Pathophysiology
| Type | Mechanism | ADH Level |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Central DI** | **ADH Deficiency** (Pituitary/Stalk/Hypothalamic Lesion) | **Low / Undetectable** |
| **Nephrogenic DI** | **Renal Resistance to ADH** (V2 Receptor/AQP2 Defect) | **High / Normal** |
| **Gestational DI** | Placental Vasopressinase Degrades ADH | Low/Normal |
| **Primary Polydipsia** | Excessive Intake → ADH Suppression | **Suppressed** |

### Water Deprivation Test + Desmopressin Challenge (Gold Standard)

| Step | Procedure |
|------|-----------|
| **Baseline** | Serum Osmo, Urine Osmo, Serum Na⁺, Weight, Vitals |
| **Fluid Deprivation** | NPO + No IV Fluids; Monitor Weight, Serum/Urine Osmo q1-2h |
| **Stop Criteria** | Weight Loss >3-5% OR Urine Osmo > Plasma Osmo OR Haemodynamic Instability |
| **Desmopressin Challenge** | **DDAVP 2µg IV/SC** (or 10-40µg Intranasal); Measure Urine Osmo at 60 min |

### Interpretation Matrix

| Finding | Central DI | Nephrogenic DI | Primary Polydipsia |
|---------|------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Urine Osmo (Baseline)** | Low (<300) | Low (<300) | Low/Variable |
| **After Deprivation** | **Low** (Fails to Concentrate) | **Low** (Fails to Concentrate) | **Concentrates** (>600) |
| **Post-DDAVP (60min)** | **↑ >50%** (Urine Osmo > Plasma) | **No Response** (<10% Rise) | N/A (Already Concentrated) |
| **Plasma ADH** | Low | High | Suppressed |

## 3. Central vs Nephrogenic Diabetes Insipidus

| Feature | Central DI | Nephrogenic DI |
|---------|-----------|--------------------|
| **ADH** | Low / Undetectable | High / Normal |
| **DDAVP Response** | **Positive** (Urine Osmo ↑ >50%) | **Negative** (<10% Rise) |
| **Common Causes** | Surgery (TSS, Craniotomy), Trauma, Infiltration (Sarcoid, LCH, Mets), Genetic (AVP Mutation) | **Lithium** (Most Common), Drugs (Demeclocycline, Amphotericin), Electrolytes (Hypercalcaemia, Hypokalaemia), Genetic (AVPR2, AQP2) |
| **Treatment** | **Desmopressin** (DDAVP) | Thiazides + Amiloride + Low Salt Diet |

## 3. Water Deprivation Test Protocol

| Phase | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **1. Preparation** | 08:00 Start; NPO; Empty Bladder; Weight, Serum/Ur Osmo, Serum Na⁺, Vitals |
| **2. Deprivation** | NPO; Monitor: Weight q1h, Serum/Ur Osmo q1-2h, Vitals q1h |
| **3. Stop Criteria** | Weight Loss >3-5% **OR** Urine Osmo > Plasma Osmo **OR** Haemodynamic Instability **OR** Time >8h |
| **4. DDAVP Challenge** | 2µg IV/SC (or 10-40µg Intranasal) |
| **5. Post-DDAVP** | Urine Osmo at 60 min |

### Interpretation

| Result | Diagnosis |
|--------|-----------|
| Urine Concentrates on Deprivation → **Primary Polydipsia** |
| Fails to Concentrate + **↑ >50% Post-DDAVP** → **Central DI** |
| Fails to Concentrate + **No DDAVP Response** → **Nephrogenic DI** |

## 3. Free Water Deficit & Correction

### Free Water Deficit (FWD) Formula
> **FWD = TBW × (Current Na⁺/140 - 1)**
> - TBW = 0.6 × Weight (kg) Men; 0.5 × Weight Women
> - Target Na⁺ = 140 mmol/L (or 12 mmol/L Reduction from Current)

### Correction Plan

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| **Max Correction Rate** | **10-12 mmol/L/24h** (Avoid Cerebral Oedema) |
| **If Acute (<24h)** | Can Correct Faster (Up to 15 mmol/L/24h) |
| **Fluid Choice** | 5% Dextrose (D5W) or 0.45% NaCl (Hypotonic) |
| **If Hypovolaemic** | 0.9% NaCl First (Restore Volume) → Then D5W |
| **Correction Rate** | 0.5 mmol/L/hr (12 mmol/L/24h) |

### Example Calculation
> 70kg Man, Na⁺ 160 mmol/L
> - TBW = 0.6 × 70 = 42 L
> - FWD = 42 × (160/140 - 1) = 42 × 0.143 = **6 L**
> - Replace over 24-48h with D5W (Avoid Rapid Correction)

## 3. Central Diabetes Insipidus Management

| Agent | Dose | Route | Frequency |
|-------|------|-------|-----------|
| **Desmopressin (DDAVP)** | 10-40 µg | Intranasal | q12-24h |
| **Desmopressin (Tablet)** | 0.1-0.4 mg | Oral | q12-24h |
| **Desmopressin (IV/SC)** | 2-4 µg | IV/SC | q12-24h |

### Titration
| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Start Low** | 10 µg Intranasal q12h or 0.1 mg PO q12h |
| **Titrate** | Increase by 10 µg / 0.1 mg every 3-7 Days |
| **Target** | Urine Output 1.5-2.5 L/day; Nocturia ≤1; Normal Na⁺ |

---

## 3. Nephrogenic Diabetes Insipidus Management

| Treatment | Dose | Mechanism |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Low Salt / Low Protein Diet** | — | Reduces Solute Load |
| **Thiazide Diuretic** | Hydrochlorothiazide 25-50mg BD | Induces Mild Volume Depletion → ↑ Proximal Reabsorption |
| **Amiloride** | 5-10 mg BD | ENaC Blocker; K⁺-Sparing |
| **NSAIDs (Indomethacin)** | 25-50mg TDS | ↓ Prostaglandin → ↑ ADH Sensitivity |
| **Desmopressin** | Partial Response Only | If Partial NDI |

---

## 3. Acute Hypernatraemia Management

| Scenario | Management |
|----------|------------|
| **Hypovolaemic** | **0.9% NaCl** 1-2L Bolus → Then D5W / 0.45% NaCl (Restore Volume First) |
| **Euvolaemic** | D5W or 0.45% NaCl (Free Water Replacement) |
| **Hypervolaemic** | Sodium Restriction + Loop Diuretic + D5W |

## 8. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Central vs Nephrogenic DI** | Central = ADH Deficiency → **DDAVP Works**; Nephrogenic = Renal Resistance → **DDAVP Fails** |
| **DI vs SIADH** | DI = Na⁺ High + Dilute Urine; SIADH = Na⁺ Low + Concentrated Urine |
| **Central vs Nephrogenic DDAVP** | Central = **Responds** (Urine Osmo ↑ >50%); Nephrogenic = **Fails** |
| **Water Deprivation Test** | Concentrates → Polydipsia; Fails + DDAVP Response → Central DI; Fails + No DDAVP → Nephrogenic |
| **Free Water Deficit** | TBW × (Current Na⁺/140 - 1); Replace with D5W |
| **Correction Rate** | **10-12 mmol/L/24h Max** (Cerebral Oedema Risk) |
| **Fluid for Hypernatraemia** | **D5W** (Free Water); NOT 0.9% NaCl (Unless Hypovolaemic) |
| **Central DI Causes** | Surgery (TSS), Trauma, Infiltration (Sarcoid, LCH, Mets) |
| **Nephrogenic DI Causes** | **Lithium** (Most Common), Drugs, Electrolytes, Genetic |
| **Water Deprivation Stop** | Weight Loss >3-5% OR Urine Osmo > Plasma Osmo OR Haemodynamic Instability |
