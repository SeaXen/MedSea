# Inpatient falls and post-fall assessment

---

## Definition

Inpatient falls are events in which a patient comes to rest inadvertently on the ground or floor during hospitalisation. They are common, often preventable, and associated with increased morbidity, prolonged hospital stay, and increased healthcare costs. Post-fall assessment is a structured approach to identify causes and prevent recurrence.

## Core Clinical Content

### Inpatient falls prevalence
2-7 falls/1000 bed days; common in elderly; 30% result in injury

### Consequences
Injury (1-3% fractures), ↑ LOS, ↑ costs, loss of confidence, litigation

### Risk factors in hospital
Unfamiliar environment, ↓ mobility, IV lines/drains, polypharmacy, delirium, urgency

### Fall risk assessment
STRATIFY, Morse Falls Scale, Hendrich II; on admission and regularly

### Multi-factorial prevention
Bed/chair alarms, non-slip footwear, frequent toileting, medication review, supervision

### Baywatching
1:1 supervision for high-risk patients; resource-intensive

### Post-fall assessment
ABCDE approach; check for injury (especially hip, head, fractures); CT head if indicated

### Investigations after fall
ECG, bloods (FBC, U&E, HbA1c, Ca, ECG), urinalysis, sepsis screen; imaging as indicated

### Documentation
Time, place, mechanism, witness, injuries, post-fall condition; risk assessment; action plan

### Post-fall review (within 24 h)
MDT review; identify cause; modify risk factors; communicate with patient/family
