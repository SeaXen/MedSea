# Syncope and presyncope in the elderly

---

## Definition

Syncope is a transient loss of consciousness due to cerebral hypoperfusion, with rapid onset, short duration, and spontaneous recovery. In the elderly, syncope is often multifactorial and may present atypically (falls, confusion) rather than as a clear blackout.

## Core Clinical Content

### Syncope definition
TLOC due to cerebral hypoperfusion; rapid onset, short, spontaneous recovery

### Syncope prevalence
3-5% of ED visits; ↑ with age; 1-2% of elderly per year

### Reflex (vasovagal) syncope
Triggered by emotion, pain, prolonged standing; prodrome (pallor, nausea, sweating)

### Orthostatic hypotension
SBP ↓ ≥20 mmHg or DBP ≥10 mmHg within 3 min standing

### OH causes
Drugs (antihypertensives, diuretics), autonomic failure, dehydration, prolonged bed rest

### Cardiac syncope
Arrhythmia (VT, bradycardia), structural (AS, HOCM, PE, tamponade); ↑ mortality

### Cardiac red flags
Exertional, no prodrome, abnormal ECG, known cardiac disease, family SCD

### Postprandial hypotension
Common in elderly; ↓ BP after meals due to splanchnic pooling

### Investigation
ECG, orthostatic BP, 24h Holter, echo; tilt-table if recurrent unexplained

### Orthostatic hypotension management
Hydration, review drugs, ↑ salt, compression stockings, midodrine, fludrocortisone
