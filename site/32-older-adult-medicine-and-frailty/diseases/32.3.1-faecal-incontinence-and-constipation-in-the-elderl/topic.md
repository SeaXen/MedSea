# Faecal incontinence and constipation in the elderly

---

## Definition

Faecal incontinence is the involuntary loss of stool, often due to overflow from constipation in older adults. Constipation is reduced bowel frequency (<3 per week) or difficulty passing stool, very common in the elderly and a frequent cause of morbidity including overflow, urinary retention, falls, and confusion.

## Core Clinical Content

### Constipation prevalence
30-40% of community elderly; 50-70% of nursing home; ↑ with age, women

### Constipation causes
Drugs (opioids, anticholinergics, calcium), immobility, low fibre, dehydration, hypothyroidism

### Faecal impaction
Hard stool in rectum; cause of overflow incontinence, urinary retention, confusion

### Faecal incontinence causes
Overflow (impaction), sphincter weakness, neurological (stroke, dementia), severe diarrhoea

### Red flags (constipation)
Rectal bleeding, weight loss, iron-deficiency anaemia, change in bowel habit → exclude colorectal cancer

### Assessment
History, abdominal and rectal examination, bloods (TFT, Ca), colonoscopy if red flags

### Laxative types
Bulking (ispaghula), osmotic (lactulose, macrogol), stimulant (senna), rectal (suppository, enema)

### Constipation management
Fibre, fluids, exercise; stimulant (senna) + osmotic (macrogol) first-line

### Faecal incontinence management
Treat cause (impaction); barrier creams; bowel training; biofeedback

### Opioid-induced constipation
Common; prophylactic laxatives; consider methylnaltrexone (peripheral opioid antagonist)
