# Urinary incontinence in the elderly

---

## Definition

Urinary incontinence is the involuntary loss of urine, a common and distressing condition in older adults, often multifactorial, with significant impact on quality of life, function, and caregiver burden. It is rarely a single disease but a symptom with multiple contributing factors.

## Core Clinical Content

### UI prevalence
10-20% of community elderly; 50% of nursing home residents; ↑ with age, women > men

### UI types
Stress, urge, overflow, functional, mixed (most common in elderly)

### Stress incontinence
Leakage with cough, sneeze, effort; weak pelvic floor; common in women post-childbirth

### Urge incontinence
Sudden urge, frequency, nocturia; overactive bladder; detrusor overactivity

### Overflow incontinence
Dribbling, incomplete emptying, palpable bladder; retention; prostate disease, neuropathy

### Functional incontinence
Cognitive/mobility impairment prevents reaching toilet; often in dementia, immobility

### Transient causes (DIAPPERS)
Delirium, Infection, Atrophic vaginitis, Pharmaceuticals, Psychological, Excess urine, Restricted mobility, Stool impaction

### Assessment
History (3-day bladder diary), examination, urinalysis, post-void residual, urodynamic studies

### Management
Pelvic floor exercises, bladder training, weight loss; oxybutynin/mirabegron (urge); catheter (overflow)

### UTI association
Asymptomatic bacteriuria common in elderly; do NOT treat unless symptomatic
