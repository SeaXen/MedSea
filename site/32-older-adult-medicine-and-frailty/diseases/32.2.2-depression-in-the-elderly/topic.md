# Depression in the elderly

---

## Definition

Depression in the elderly is a mood disorder characterised by persistent low mood, anhedonia, and other depressive symptoms occurring in older adults, often with atypical presentation, comorbidities, and significant impact on function and mortality. It is the most common mental health problem in the elderly but is often under-recognised and under-treated.

## Core Clinical Content

### Depression prevalence in elderly
10-15% of community elderly; 30-40% in hospitalised; commonest mental health problem

### Risk factors
Female, social isolation, bereavement, chronic illness, disability, recent hospitalisation

### Atypical presentation
Somatic symptoms, anxiety, pseudodementia, ↓ motivation, irritability; less overt sadness

### Pseudodementia
Cognitive impairment from depression; reversible with treatment; 'I don't know' answers

### Differential from dementia
Depression: abrupt onset, 'don't know', mood congruent, reversible; dementia: gradual, confabulation

### Differential from grief
Grief: waves, focused on loss; depression: pervasive, anhedonia, suicidal

### Suicide risk
Highest in elderly men; completed suicide rate highest in ≥75; means more lethal

### Investigation
Exclude organic causes (TFT, Ca, B12, glucose, FBC); PHQ-9, Geriatric Depression Scale

### First-line treatment
SSRI (sertraline, citalopram); avoid TCAs (anticholinergic, falls, cardiac)

### SSRI cautions in elderly
Hyponatraemia (SIADH), bleeding, falls, QTc (citalopram), discontinuation syndrome
