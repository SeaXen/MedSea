# Malnutrition and anorexia of ageing

---

## Definition

Malnutrition in the elderly is a state of energy, protein, or micronutrient deficiency that adversely affects body composition, function, and clinical outcomes. Anorexia of ageing is the physiological decline in appetite and food intake with age, contributing to malnutrition, sarcopenia, and frailty in older adults.

## Core Clinical Content

### Malnutrition prevalence
10-30% of community elderly; 30-50% of hospitalised; 40-70% of nursing home

### MUST score
Malnutrition Universal Screening Tool: BMI, weight loss, disease effect; 0-6 score

### MNA
Mini Nutritional Assessment; for ≥65; screens malnutrition and risk

### Anorexia of ageing
↓ Appetite with age; ↓ taste/smell, ↓ gastric motility, ↑ satiety hormones (CCK, leptin)

### Causes of malnutrition in elderly
Anorexia of ageing, depression, dementia, dysphagia, dental, drugs, social isolation

### Consequences
↓ Immune function, ↓ wound healing, ↑ falls, ↑ fractures, ↑ mortality

### Refeeding syndrome
Hypophosphataemia, hypokalaemia, hypomagnesaemia, fluid overload with reintroduction of nutrition

### Refeeding prevention
Start slowly (10-20 kcal/kg/day), supplement thiamine, monitor electrolytes

### Management
Nutritional support (oral, NG, PEG, IV); food fortification; assist with feeding; consider appetite stimulants

### Vitamin D
Common deficiency; 800-2000 IU daily; especially in nursing home and housebound
