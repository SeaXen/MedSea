# Polypharmacy and inappropriate prescribing

---

## Definition

Polypharmacy is the concurrent use of multiple medications by a patient, commonly defined as ≥5 medications. Inappropriate prescribing is when the risks outweigh the benefits or when a better alternative exists; common in older adults and associated with adverse outcomes including falls, hospitalisation, and mortality.

## Core Clinical Content

### Polypharmacy definition
≥5 medications; hyperpolypharmacy ≥10

### Polypharmacy prevalence
40-50% of ≥65; ↑ with age, multimorbidity, polypharmacy clinics

### Adverse drug reactions (ADRs)
↑ 2x with polypharmacy; 4-5x with hyperpolypharmacy; cause 5-10% hospital admissions

### Falls risk ↑ with polypharmacy
Each medication adds to falls risk; cumulative effect significant

### Beers Criteria (US)
List of PIMs (potentially inappropriate medications) for older adults; updated regularly

### STOPP/START criteria (UK/EU)
Screening Tool of Older Persons' Prescriptions / Screening Tool to Alert to Right Treatment

### Inappropriate prescribing examples
Long-term benzodiazepines, anticholinergics in dementia, NSAIDs in CKD

### Prescribing cascade
Adverse drug reaction misinterpreted as new condition; new drug added

### Deprescribing
Systematic process of identifying and discontinuing inappropriate medications

### Tools for deprescribing
Beers, STOPP/START, NO TEARS, medication review with patient/carer
