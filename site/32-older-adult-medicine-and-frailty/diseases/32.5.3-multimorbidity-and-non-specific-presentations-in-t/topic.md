# Multimorbidity and non-specific presentations in the elderly

---

## Definition

Multimorbidity is the coexistence of ≥2 chronic conditions in an individual. Older adults often present with non-specific presentations (NSPs) such as functional decline, falls, confusion, or immobility, rather than the classic organ-specific symptoms, making diagnosis challenging.

## Core Clinical Content

### Multimorbidity definition
≥2 chronic conditions in one person; common in older adults

### Multimorbidity prevalence
≥50% of ≥65; ↑ with age; commonest: HTN, OA, CHD, DM, COPD, CKD

### Non-specific presentations (NSPs)
Functional decline, falls, immobility, confusion, incontinence, pain

### Why NSPs?
Multiple comorbidities, atypical presentation, polypharmacy, age-related physiological changes

### Atypical presentation of disease
MI without chest pain, UTI without dysuria, pneumonia without cough

### Non-specific presentations in elderly
Often present with 'off legs', falls, confusion, dehydration, weight loss

### Differential of NSP
Infection (UTI, pneumonia), dehydration, electrolyte disturbance, medication, MI, stroke, DVT

### Comprehensive approach
Don't focus on single organ; full history, examination, medication review

### Importance of collateral history
Essential; patient may have cognitive impairment or communication difficulty

### Management
Treat reversible causes; CGA; multidisciplinary team; avoid over-medicalisation
