# Hypothermia, hyperthermia, and age-related thermal dysregulation

---

## Definition

Elderly patients have impaired thermoregulation due to age-related physiological changes, comorbidities, and medications, making them vulnerable to both hypothermia and hyperthermia, which are associated with significant morbidity and mortality.

## Core Clinical Content

### Thermoregulation in elderly
↓ Basal metabolic rate, ↓ shivering response, ↓ sweating, ↓ perception of temperature

### Hypothermia
Core temp <35°C; mortality 30-50% in elderly; often missed

### Hypothermia causes
Cold exposure, immobility, sepsis, hypothyroidism, drugs (sedatives, alcohol)

### Hypothermia signs
Confusion, bradycardia, hypotension, slow respiration, cold, pale skin; ↓ shivering in elderly

### Hypothermia management
Warm environment, warm IV fluids, warming blanket; gentle re-warming; treat cause

### Hyperthermia
Core temp >38°C; sepsis, heat stroke, drug reaction, thyroid storm

### Heat stroke
Core >40°C; CNS dysfunction; hot dry skin; emergency; rapid cooling; ↑ mortality in elderly

### Drug-related thermoregulation
Antipsychotics (NMS), anticholinergics (↓ sweating), SSRIs (serotonin syndrome), anaesthetics

### Fever in elderly
Often blunted or absent; altered mental status may be only sign; consider sepsis

### Prevention
Home temperature monitoring; appropriate clothing; hydration; medication review
