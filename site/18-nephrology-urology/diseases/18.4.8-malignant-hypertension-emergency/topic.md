# Malignant Hypertension (Hypertensive Emergency)

<callout icon="🩺" color="red_bg">
**Topic:** Malignant Hypertension (Hypertensive Emergency) — Nephrology & Urology
**Style:** Sea Knowledge study infographic
**Audience:** FCPS / MRCP exam prep
</callout>

**Related:** [[Cardiology]], [[Vascular Diseases of the Kidney — Renal Artery Stenosis]], [[Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)]], [[Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)]], [[Thrombotic Microangiopathies (TMA, HUS, TTP)]], [[Nephrology and Urology MOC]]

> [!important]
> **Malignant hypertension = severe hypertension (usually >180/120 mmHg) with acute end-organ damage: hypertensive retinopathy (grade III/IV: haemorrhages, exudates, papilloedema) ± renal (AKI, proteinuria, haematuria) ± cardiac (LVF, ischaemia) ± neurological (encephalopathy). Emergency: controlled BP reduction (target ↓ 20–25% in first hour, not to normal). IV labetalol, nicardipine, nitroprusside. Differentiate from TMA (HUS/TTP) — overlap exists.**

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Define malignant hypertension (BP threshold + end-organ damage criteria)
- Recognise hypertensive retinopathy grades (Keith-Wagener-Barker)
- Differentiate hypertensive emergency vs urgency
- Apply stepwise BP reduction targets and IV agents
- Identify renal involvement (TMA overlap, fibrinoid necrosis)
- Manage special situations (pregnancy, post-stroke, dissection)
- Apply to FCPS/MRCP emergency medicine vignettes

---

## 2. Definition & Diagnostic Criteria
| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Blood Pressure** | **SBP >180 mmHg and/or DBP >120 mmHg** (often much higher) |
| **Retinopathy** | **Grade III (haemorrhages, exudates) or Grade IV (papilloedema)** — mandatory for "malignant" |
| **Renal** | AKI, proteinuria, haematuria, microangiopathic haemolytic anaemia |
| **Cardiac** | LVF, pulmonary oedema, acute coronary syndrome |
| **Neurological** | Hypertensive encephalopathy, seizure, stroke, PRES |

> **Hypertensive Emergency** = severe HTN + acute end-organ damage (includes malignant HTN).
> **Hypertensive Urgency** = severe HTN (≥180/120) **without** acute end-organ damage.

---

## 3. Hypertensive Retinopathy (Keith-Wagener-Barker Grading)
| Grade | Findings | Significance |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| **I** | Generalised arteriolar narrowing | Benign HTN |
| **II** | Arteriovenous (AV) nipping | Benign HTN |
| **III** | **Haemorrhages + exudates (cotton wool spots)** | **Malignant HTN** |
| **IV** | **Papilloedema (disc swelling)** | **Malignant HTN** (worst prognosis) |

> **Grade III/IV = malignant hypertension definition**. Fundoscopy mandatory in all severe HTN.

---

## 4. Pathophysiology
| Mechanism | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Failure of autoregulation** | BP exceeds autoregulatory range → forced dilatation → vessel wall stress |
| **Fibrinoid necrosis** | Necrotising arteriolitis (onion-skin lesions) → luminal narrowing |
| **Endothelial injury** | Thrombotic microangiopathy (TMA) → microthrombi, MAHA |
| **RAAS activation** | Renin release → vicious cycle of vasoconstriction |
| **Natriuresis** | Pressure natriuresis → volume depletion → further RAAS activation |

---

## 5. Clinical Presentation

### Constitutional
- Severe headache (often occipital, morning)
- Visual disturbances (blurring, scotomas)
- Nausea/vomiting
- Confusion, lethargy, seizure (encephalopathy)

### End-Organ Manifestations

#### **Eyes (Mandatory for Diagnosis)**
| Finding | Grade |
|---------|-------|
| Arteriolar narrowing | I |
| AV nipping | II |
| **Flame haemorrhages, hard exudates, cotton wool spots** | **III** |
| **Papilloedema** | **IV** |

#### **Kidneys**
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Proteinuria** | Often nephrotic-range |
| **Haematuria** | Microscopic ± macroscopic |
| **AKI** | Rapidly rising creatinine |
| **TMA overlap** | Schistocytes, thrombocytopenia, LDH↑, haptoglobin↓ |

#### **Heart**
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **LVF / Pulmonary oedema** | Acute decompensation |
| **ACS / MI** | Demand ischaemia |
| **LVH** | Long-standing HTN |

#### **Brain**
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Hypertensive encephalopathy** | Headache, confusion, seizures, cortical blindness |
| **PRES** | Posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome (parietal-occipital oedema) |
| **Stroke** | Ischaemic or haemorrhagic |

---

## 6. Renal Pathology (Malignant Nephrosclerosis)
| Lesion | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Fibrinoid necrosis** | Eosinophilic necrosis of afferent/efferent arterioles |
| **"Onion-skin" hyperplasia** | Concentric intimal hyperplasia (hyperplastic arteriolosclerosis) |
| **TMA** | Microthrombi in glomerular capillaries, RBC fragmentation |
| **Glomerular ischaemia** | Collapsed capillaries, wrinkled basement membranes |

> **Biopsy rarely needed** — clinical diagnosis; if done: fibrinoid necrosis + TMA.

---

## 7. Differential Diagnosis
| Condition | Key Differentiators |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **TMA (HUS/TTP)** | MAHA, thrombocytopenia, ADAMTS13 (TTP), neurological predominance |
| **Scleroderma Renal Crisis** | Scleroderma features, ACEi-responsive, normotensive onset possible |
| **Phaeochromocytoma** | Paroxysmal HTN, catecholamine surges, urinary metanephrines |
| **Renal Artery Stenosis** | Abdominal bruit, asymmetric kidneys, ACEi-induced AKI |
| **Acute Glomerulonephritis** | RBC casts, low C3, recent infection |
| **Pre-eclampsia/Eclampsia** | Pregnancy, proteinuria, resolves postpartum |
| **Drug-induced** | Cocaine, amphetamines, MAOI + tyramine, clonidine withdrawal |

---

## 8. Management — **Controlled BP Reduction**

### Targets (KDIGO/ESC/ACC)
| Phase | Target |
|-------|--------|
| **First hour** | **Reduce MAP by 20–25%** (or SBP to 160–170) — **NOT to normal** |
| **2–6 hours** | SBP 140–160 / DBP 90–100 |
| **24–48 hours** | Near normal (<130/80) if tolerated |

> **Avoid precipitous drop** → cerebral/renal/coronary hypoperfusion → stroke, AKI, MI.

---

### IV Antihypertensive Agents (First-Line)

| Drug | Dose | Onset | Duration | Indications / Caveats |
|------|------|-------|----------|----------------------|
| **Labetalol** | **10–20mg IV bolus q10min** (max 300mg) OR infusion 0.5–2mg/min | 2–5 min | 3–6 hr | **First-line** (α+β blockade); avoid asthma, severe HF, bradycardia |
| **Nicardipine** | **5mg/hr IV infusion** (titrate 2.5mg/hr q5–15min, max 15mg/hr) | 1–2 min | 30–60 min | **First-line** (cerebral vasodilator); safe in asthma, COPD |
| **Nitroprusside** | **0.3–0.5 mcg/kg/min** (titrate, max 10 mcg/kg/min) | Immediate | 1–2 min | **Refractory** / aortic dissection / PRES; **cyanide/thiocyanate toxicity** (limit 72hr, monitor levels) |
| **Hydralazine** | 5–10mg IV bolus (repeat q20min) | 10–20 min | 4–6 hr | **Pregnancy** (safe); avoid in CAD (reflex tachycardia) |
| **Esmolol** | 500 mcg/kg load → 50–200 mcg/kg/min | 1 min | 10–30 min | Tachycardia, perioperative; short half-life |
| **Clevidipine** | 1–2 mg/hr IV (titrate) | <1 min | 1–2 min | Ultra-short; perioperative; expensive |

> **Labetalol + Nicardipine = preferred duo** for most emergencies.

---

### Special Situations

| Situation | Preferred Agent | Avoid |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| **Pregnancy / Pre-eclampsia** | **IV Hydralazine**, **Labetalol**, **Nifedipine PO** | ACEi/ARB, Nitroprusside (cyanide), Nifedipine IV (precipitate drop) |
| **Aortic Dissection** | **Esmolol/Labetalol (β-block first)** + **Nicardipine/Nitroprusside** | Pure vasodilators without β-blockade (reflex tachycardia) |
| **Acute Ischaemic Stroke** | **Permissive HTN** (SBP <220 / DBP <120) unless thrombolysis | Aggressive lowering |
| **Thrombolysis (stroke)** | **SBP <185, DBP <110** before alteplase | Labetalol, Nicardipine |
| **PRES** | Nicardipine, Labetalol | Nitroprusside (may worsen cerebral oedema) |
| **Acute Coronary Syndrome** | Nitrates, β-blockers, ACEi | Hydralazine (reflex tachycardia) |
| **CKD / Renal Impairment** | Nicardipine, Clevidipine, Labetalol | Nitroprusside (cyanide accumulation) |

---

## 9. Scleroderma Renal Crisis (Special Entity)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Population** | Diffuse cutaneous systemic sclerosis (dcSSc) |
| **Presentation** | New-onset severe HTN + AKI (often normotensive baseline) |
| **Pathology** | Intimal proliferation, TMA (similar to malignant HTN) |
| **Treatment** | **ACEi FIRST-LINE** (captopril 6.25–25mg q6–8h PO/NGT) — **life-saving** |
| **BP Target** | Aggressive (MAP ↓ 20–25% in first hour) |
| **Dialysis** | Often temporary if ACEi started early |
| **Prognosis** | 50% renal recovery if ACEi early; poor if delayed |

---

## 10. TMA Overlap (Malignant HTN + HUS/TTP)
| Feature | Malignant HTN | TTP | HUS |
|---------|---------------|-----|-----|
| **BP** | **Very high** | Normal/mildly elevated | Normal/mildly elevated |
| **Neurological** | Encephalopathy, PRES | **Prominent (stroke, confusion)** | Variable |
| **Renal** | AKI, proteinuria | Mild | **Severe (dialysis)** |
| **ADAMTS13** | Normal | **<10%** | Normal |
| **Treatment** | BP control | **PLEX + Rituximab** | Supportive / Eculizumab (aHUS) |

> **Malignant HTN can cause TMA** (fibrinoid necrosis → endothelial injury → microthrombi). Treat BP first; if TMA persists → consider PLEX/eculizumab.

---

## 11. Monitoring During Treatment
| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| **BP (arterial line preferred)** | Continuous / q1–5 min |
| **Neurological status** | q15–30 min (GCS, pupils) |
| **Urine output** | Hourly (target >0.5 mL/kg/hr) |
| **Serum creatinine, electrolytes** | q4–6 hr |
| **CBC, LDH, haptoglobin** | q6–12 hr (monitor TMA) |
| **ECG / Troponin** | Baseline, q6–12 hr |
| **Fundoscopy** | Baseline, 24 hr (retinopathy improvement) |

## 12. High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Points

> [!important]
> - **Malignant HTN** = SBP >180/DBP >120 + **Grade III/IV retinopathy** (haemorrhages/exudates/papilloedema).
> - **Emergency** = end-organ damage (renal, cardiac, cerebral, retinal).
> - **BP reduction**: **MAP ↓ 20–25% in first hour** (NOT to normal).
> - **First-line IV**: **Labetalol** (α+β) or **Nicardipine** (cerebral vasodilator).
> - **Nitroprusside**: Refractory / dissection / PRES; **cyanide toxicity** (limit 72hr).
> - **Pregnancy**: Hydralazine / Labetalol / Nifedipine PO.
> - **Aortic dissection**: β-blocker first (Esmolol/Labetalol) + vasodilator.
> - **Stroke**: Permissive HTN (SBP<220) unless thrombolysis (SBP<185).
> - **Scleroderma renal crisis**: **ACEi first-line** (captopril) — life-saving.
> - **TMA overlap**: Malignant HTN can cause TMA; treat BP first, then PLEX/eculizumab if persists.
> - **Fundoscopy mandatory** — Grade III/IV = malignant HTN definition.

## 13. Common Confusions / Exam Traps

| Trap | Correction |
|------|------------|
| **Reduce BP to normal immediately** | **NO — MAP ↓ 20–25% in first hour**; rapid drop → stroke, AKI, MI |
| **Nitroprusside first-line for all** | **Labetalol/Nicardipine preferred**; nitroprusside = refractory, cyanide risk |
| **All severe HTN = malignant** | **Must have Grade III/IV retinopathy** (or other end-organ damage) |
| **ACEi in all malignant HTN** | **Contraindicated in bilateral RAS, pregnancy**; first-line ONLY in scleroderma renal crisis |
| **Hydralazine IV = first-line** | Only for pregnancy / dissection adjunct; causes reflex tachycardia |
| **TTP = malignant HTN** | TTP = normal BP, low ADAMTS13, neuro predominance; malignant HTN = very high BP |
| **Scleroderma renal crisis = same as malignant HTN** | **ACEi FIRST-LINE** (unique); often normotensive baseline |
| **Stroke = lower BP aggressively** | **Permissive HTN** (SBP<220) unless thrombolysis (SBP<185) |
| **Fundoscopy not needed if BP very high** | **Mandatory** — Grade III/IV defines malignant HTN |
| **All end-organ damage = same treatment** | **Target organ guides agent choice** (stroke vs dissection vs LVF vs pregnancy) |

## 14. Mnemonics
- **Malignant HTN criteria**: **BP** >180/120 + **R**etinopathy Grade III/IV = **BPR**
- **Retinopathy grades**: **I** = Narrow, **II** = Nipping, **III** = Haemorrhages/Exudates, **IV** = Papilloedema = **IN-HP**
- **BP reduction**: **NOT TO NORMAL** = **NTN** (20–25% MAP drop in 1st hour)
- **First-line IV**: **L**abetalol **N**icardipine = **LN** (LabNica)
- **Pregnancy**: **H**ydralazine **L**abetalol **N**ifedipine PO = **HLN**
- **Dissection**: **B**eta-block **F**irst (Esmolol/Labetalol) = **BF**
- **Stroke**: **P**ermissive **H**TN **U**nless **T**hrombolysis = **PHUT**
- **Scleroderma crisis**: **A**CEi **F**irst **L**ife-saving = **AFL**

## 23. Summary
**Malignant Hypertension** is a **Must Know 🔴** hypertensive emergency topic.  
**Definition**: SBP >180/DBP >120 + **Grade III/IV retinopathy** (haemorrhages/exudates/papilloedema).  
**Retinopathy**: Keith-Wagener-Barker I (narrowing), II (AV nipping), **III (haemorrhages/exudates)**, **IV (papilloedema)**.  
**End-organ**: Renal (AKI, TMA overlap), Cardiac (LVF, ACS), Cerebral (encephalopathy, PRES, stroke).  
**Emergency Management**: **MAP ↓ 20–25% in first hour** (NOT to normal). **First-line IV**: **Labetalol** or **Nicardipine**. **Nitroprusside** for refractory/dissection/PRES (cyanide risk).  
**Special Situations**: Pregnancy → Hydralazine/Labetalol/Nifedipine PO; Dissection → **β-blocker FIRST**; Stroke → Permissive HTN (SBP<220); Scleroderma renal crisis → **ACEi (Captopril) FIRST-LINE**.  
**TMA Overlap**: Malignant HTN can cause TMA; control BP first, then PLEX/eculizumab if persists.  
**Exam focus**: Definition (BP + retinopathy), BP reduction target, first-line agents, special situation agents, retinopathy grades, scleroderma ACEi, TMA management.
