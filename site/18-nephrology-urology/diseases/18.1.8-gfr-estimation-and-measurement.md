# GFR Estimation & Measurement

Related: [[Glomerular Filtration Rate]], [[Chronic Kidney Disease]], [[CKD-EPI equation]], [[Creatinine clearance]], [[Cystatin C]]


**Heading Hub:** [[Renal Anatomy, Physiology, and Investigations]]

> [!important]
> **GFR = gold standard of kidney function**. Measured (mGFR) vs Estimated (eGFR). Inulin = reference standard; creatinine clearance overestimates; CKD-EPI 2021 preferred; cystatin C more accurate in extremes.

## Measured GFR (mGFR) — Reference Methods

| Marker | Principle | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|--------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| **Inulin** | Gold standard; freely filtered, not secreted/reabsorbed | Most accurate | IV infusion, multiple timed urine collections, expensive, not routine |
| **Iohexol / Iothalamate** | Renal clearance (plasma disappearance or urinary) | Validated, used in research/donors | IV/IO injection, timed samples, radiation (iothalamate) |
| **⁵¹Cr-EDTA** | Plasma clearance | Standard in UK/Europe | Radioactive, gamma counter needed |
| **⁹⁹mTc-DTPA** | Plasma clearance | Nuclear medicine, widely available | Radioactive, less accurate than inulin |

> [!key]
> **mGFR indicated**: Living donor evaluation, drug dosing narrow therapeutic index (carboplatin), clinical trials, discrepancy between eGFR and clinical picture.

## Estimated GFR (eGFR) — Creatinine-Based

### Creatinine Physiology
- **Production**: ~1–2 g/day from muscle creatine (constant in steady state)
- **Excretion**: Freely filtered + **secreted** (~10–20% tubular secretion) → **CrCl > true GFR**
- **Variables**: Muscle mass, diet (cooked meat), age, sex, ethnicity, medications

### Equations

| Equation | Variables | Best For | Limitations |
|----------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **CKD-EPI 2009** | Cr, age, sex, race (Black/non-Black) | General adult population | Race coefficient controversial |
| **CKD-EPI 2021** | Cr, age, sex | **Current standard (no race)** | Slightly less accurate at extremes |
| **MDRD** | Cr, age, sex, race | Historical (CKD cohorts) | Underestimates at GFR >60 |
| **Cockcroft-Gault** | Cr, age, sex, weight | **Drug dosing** (not GFR estimation) | Overestimates in obesity, uses IBW |

> [!key]
> **CKD-EPI 2021 (no race)** is now recommended by KDIGO/NKF. Report eGFR as mL/min/1.73m² BSA-standardised.

### CKD-EPI 2021 Creatinine Equation
```
eGFR = 142 × min(Scr/κ, 1)^α × max(Scr/κ, 1)^-1.200 × 0.9938^Age × 1.012 [if female]
```
- κ = 0.7 (female), 0.9 (male)
- α = -0.241 (female), -0.302 (male)

## Cystatin C — Alternative Filtration Marker

| Feature | Creatinine | Cystatin C |
|---------|------------|------------|
| **Production** | Muscle-dependent | **All nucleated cells (constant)** |
| **Secreted** | Yes (10–20%) | No |
| **Reabsorbed** | No | Yes (catabolised in PT) |
| **Influences** | Muscle mass, diet, age, meds | Thyroid, steroids, inflammation, smoking, obesity |
| **Cost** | Low | Higher |

### Equations
| Equation | Variables |
|----------|-----------|
| **CKD-EPI 2012 Cystatin C** | Cystatin C, age, sex |
| **CKD-EPI 2021 Cr-CysC** | **Creatinine + Cystatin C + age + sex** (most accurate) |

> [!key]
> **Combined Cr-CysC equation** outperforms either alone. Use when eGFR Cr is unreliable (extremes of muscle mass, amputees, high muscle, vegans, cirrhosis).

## eGFR Interpretation & Clinical Contexts

### CKD Staging by eGFR
| Stage | eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Description |
|-------|----------------------|-------------|
| **G1** | ≥90 | Normal / high (with kidney damage) |
| **G2** | 60–89 | Mildly decreased |
| **G3a** | 45–59 | Mild-moderate |
| **G3b** | 30–44 | Moderate-severe |
| **G4** | 15–29 | Severe |
| **G5** | <15 | Kidney failure |

### Special Populations — eGFR Pitfalls

| Population | Issue | Solution |
|------------|-------|----------|
| **Elderly** | ↓Muscle mass → ↓Cr production → **overestimates true GFR** | Use cystatin C or combined equation |
| **Low Muscle Mass** (amputees, malnutrition, paralysis) | ↓Cr → overestimates GFR | Cystatin C / combined / mGFR |
| **High Muscle Mass** (athletes, bodybuilders) | ↑Cr → underestimates GFR | Cystatin C / combined / mGFR |
| **Obesity** | BSA mismatch; Cr production ↑ | Use BSA-standardised eGFR; consider cysC |
| **Pregnancy** | ↑GFR (pregnancy hyperfiltration), ↓Cr | **Do NOT use standard eGFR**; measure CrCl if needed |
| **Cirrhosis** | ↓Muscle, ↑Cr secretion, altered volume | Cystatin C preferred |
| **Nephrotic Syndrome** | ↓Cr production? | Standard eGFR usually adequate |
| **Critical Illness** | Non-steady state, fluid overload, catabolism | mGFR or serial Cr trends; eGFR invalid |

## Drug Dosing & GFR

| Context | Recommended Metric |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Most drugs** | eGFR (CKD-EPI 2021) |
| **Narrow TI drugs** (carboplatin, gentamicin, vancomycin, methotrexate) | **Measured GFR or CrCl (Cockcroft-Gault with IBW)** |
| **Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs)** | eGFR (CKD-EPI) per prescribing info |
| **Contrast procedures** | eGFR (screen for <30) |

> [!key]
> **Cockcroft-Gault** uses **Ideal Body Weight (IBW)** for dosing, not actual weight. For obesity: use adjusted body weight (AdjBW = IBW + 0.4 × [Actual - IBW]).

## Reporting & Quality Assurance

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Units** | mL/min/1.73m² (BSA-standardised) |
| **Precision** | Report as integer (no decimals) |
| **Frequency** | At least annually in CKD; more often if progressive |
| **Race** | **Do not report race-adjusted eGFR** (CKD-EPI 2021) |
| **Comments** | Add "eGFR may be unreliable in..." when appropriate |

## Answer Key with Explanations

### MCQs
1. **B** — Inulin is freely filtered, neither secreted nor reabsorbed
2. **C** — CKD-EPI 2021 removed the race coefficient
3. **B** — Cystatin C produced by all nucleated cells at constant rate
4. **D** — Both elderly and amputees have low muscle mass → low Cr production → overestimation
5. **C** — Combined Cr-CysC equation outperforms either alone
6. **B** — Narrow TI drugs: measured GFR or Cockcroft-Gault with IBW
7. **B** — Pregnancy hyperfiltration: GFR ↑40–50%, Cr ↓
8. **B** — Cockcroft-Gault uses IBW (or AdjBW) for drug dosing

### SBAs
1. **B** — Low muscle mass → low Cr production → eGFR overestimates true GFR
2. **B** — High muscle mass → high Cr production → eGFR underestimates true GFR
3. **B** — Cirrhosis: cystatin C less affected by muscle mass/catabolism
4. **C** — Carboplatin AUC dosing requires accurate GFR (Calvert formula)
