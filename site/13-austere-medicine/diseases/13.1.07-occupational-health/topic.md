## 2. 📖 Principles of Occupational Medicine

### Hierarchy of Controls (Most to Least Effective)

| Level | Control Measure | Examples |
|-------|----------------|----------|
| **1. Elimination** | **Remove hazard completely** | Substitute hazardous chemical, Automate process |
| **2. Substitution** | **Replace with less hazardous** | Water-based paint instead of solvent-based |
| **3. Engineering Controls** | **Isolate people from hazard** | Local exhaust ventilation, Machine guarding, Enclosure |
| **4. Administrative Controls** | **Change how people work** | Job rotation, Training, Work schedules, Signage |
| **5. PPE** | **Protect worker with equipment** | Respirators, Gloves, Goggles, Hearing protection |

> [!tip] **Hierarchy = ELIMINATE → SUBSTITUTE → ENGINEER → ADMIN → PPE**. **Higher Levels = More Effective & Reliable**.

## 3. 📖 Pneumoconioses (Occupational Lung Diseases)

### Major Pneumoconioses

| Disease | Agent | Pathology | Clinical / Radiology | Latency | High-Risk Occupations |
|---------|-------|-----------|---------------------|---------|----------------------|
| **Silicosis** | **Crystalline Silica (Quartz, Cristobalite, Tridymite)** | Nodular Fibrosis, **Eggshell Calcification** of Hilar Nodes, PMF | **Chronic (10-20y)**: Dyspnoea, Cough, Progressive Massive Fibrosis (PMF)<br>**Accelerated (5-10y)**<br>**Acute (Silicoproteinosis)**: <1yr, Alveolar Filling | **10-30 Years** | Mining, Quarrying, Sandblasting, Tunnelling, Stone Cutting, Foundry, Glass/Ceramic |
| **Asbestosis** | **Asbestos Fibres** (Chrysotile, Amphiboles) | **Diffuse Interstitial Fibrosis**, **Pleural Plaques**, **Pleural Thickening**, **Mesothelioma** | **Dyspnoea, Bibasal Crackles, Clubbing**, **Pleural Plaques (Calcified, Diaphragm)**, **Honeycombing (Late)** | **20-40 Years** | Insulation, Shipbuilding, Construction, Demolition, Brake Repair, Textiles |
| **Coal Workers' Pneumoconiosis (CWP)** | **Coal Dust (Carbon)** | **Simple CWP**: Small Rounded Opacities<br>**Complicated (PMF)**: Large Opacities >1cm | **Simple**: Asymptomatic / Mild Dyspnoea<br>**PMF**: Progressive Dyspnoea, Respiratory Failure, Cor Pulmonale | **20-30 Years** | **Coal Mining** (Face Workers, Cutters) |
| **Berylliosis** | **Beryllium** | **Non-Caseating Granulomas** (Like Sarcoidosis), **Hilar Lymphadenopathy** | **Cough, Dyspnoea, Fatigue**, **Skin Lesions**, **Hypercalcemia** | **Months-Years** | Aerospace, Electronics, Nuclear, Ceramics, Dental Alloys |
| **Siderosis** | **Iron Oxide (Fe₂O₃)** | **Benign**, **Fine Reticular Opacities**, **No Fibrosis** | Usually Asymptomatic | Short | Welding, Foundry, Mining |
| **Stannosis** | **Tin Oxide** | **Benign**, **Fine Nodular Opacities** | Asymptomatic | Long | Tin Smelting, Plating |
| **Byssinosis** | **Cotton Dust** | **Airway Obstruction**, **Monday Chest Tightness** | **Monday Chest Tightness**, **Chronic Airways Obstruction** | Variable | Cotton, Flax, Hemp Processing |
| **Bagassosis** | **Bagasse (Sugarcane Fibre)** | **Extrinsic Allergic Alveolitis**, **Granulomas** | **Fever, Cough, Dyspnoea**, **Recurrent** | Months-Years | Sugarcane Processing |
| **Farmer's Lung** | **Mouldy Hay (Thermophilic Actinomycetes)** | **Extrinsic Allergic Alveolitis** | **Fever, Cough, Dyspnoea 4-8h Post-Exposure**, **Recurrent** | Variable | Farming (Mouldy Hay/Straw) |

> [!tip] **Silicosis = Eggshell Calcification of Hilar Nodes + PMF**. **Asbestosis = Pleural Plaques + Basal Fibrosis + Mesothelioma Risk**. **CWP = Small Rounded Opacities → PMF**. **Berylliosis = Granulomas + HLA-DPB1 Risk**.

## 4. 📖 Occupational Asthma & Airways Disease

### Classification

| Type | Mechanism | Key Features |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| **Immunological (Sensitiser-Induced)** | **IgE-Mediated** (High MW: Flour, Enzymes, Latex, Animals) OR **Non-IgE (Low MW: Isocyanates, Acid Anhydrides, Wood Dust, Colophony)** | **Latent Period** (Months-Years), **Work-Related Symptoms** (Better on Days Off/Weekends), **Specific IgE (HMAs)** |
| **Irritant-Induced (RADS / IIA)** | **Single High-Level Exposure** → **Reactive Airways Dysfunction Syndrome (RADS)** / **Irritant-Induced Asthma (IIA)** | **No Latent Period**, **Onset ≤24h Post-Exposure**, **Persists >3 Months** |
| **Work-Exacerbated Asthma** | **Pre-Existing Asthma Worsened** by Workplace Triggers | **Pre-Existing Asthma**, **Worse at Work**, **Improves Away** |

### Common Sensitisers

| High Molecular Weight (>5kDa) | Low Molecular Weight (<5kDa) |
|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Flour / Grains** (Bakers) | **Isocyanates** (Spray Painting, PU Foam) |
| **Enzymes** (Amylase, Subtilisin) | **Acid Anhydrides** (Epoxy Resins) |
| **Latex** (Healthcare) | **Wood Dust** (Carpentry) |
| **Laboratory Animals** (Urine/Dander) | **Colophony / Rosin** (Soldering) |
| **Insects / Mites** | **Platinum Salts** (Refining) |
| **Fish / Seafood** (Processing) | **Persulphates** (Hairdressing) |
| **Seafood** | **Glutaraldehyde** (Disinfection) |

> [!tip] **Occupational Asthma = Work-Related Symptoms + Objective Evidence (PEF Variability, Specific IgE, SIC)**. **Isocyanates = Most Common Cause (LMW)**. **Flour/Grains = Most Common HMW**.

## 5. 📖 Occupational Dermatoses

| Type | Mechanism | Common Causes | Key Features |
|------|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Allergic Contact Dermatitis** | **Type IV Hypersensitivity** (Sensitisation → Elicitation) | **Nickel, Chromate (Cement), Rubber Accelerators, Epoxy, Formaldehyde, Biocides, Fragrances** | **Itchy Eczema**, **Work-Related**, **Patch Test +ve** |
| **Irritant Contact Dermatitis** | **Direct Toxic Damage** (No Sensitisation) | **Water/Soap (Wet Work), Solvents, Acids/Alkalis, Cutting Oils, Fibreglass** | **Burning/Stinging**, **Immediate**, **No Sensitisation Required** |
| **Contact Urticaria** | **Type I (IgE) / Non-Immunological** | **Latex, Foods, Enzymes, Animals** | **Wheal/Flare Within Minutes**, **Systemic Risk** |
| **Occupational Skin Cancer** | **Carcinogen + UV** | **Arsenic, Coal Tar, Pitch, Creosote, Mineral Oils, UV (Outdoor)** | **SCC, BCC, Keratoacanthoma** (Sun-Exposed Sites) |

## 6. 📖 Musculoskeletal Disorders

| Disorder | Key Features | Risk Factors | Assessment |
|----------|--------------|--------------|------------|
| **WRULDs (Work-Related Upper Limb Disorders)** | **Neck/Shoulder/Arm/Hand Pain**, Numbness, Tingling, Weakness | **Repetition, Force, Posture, Vibration, Cold, Psychosocial** | **History, Examination, Nerve Conduction Studies** |
| **Carpal Tunnel Syndrome** | **Median Nerve Compression** → **Nocturnal Paraesthesia (Thumb-Index-Middle), Thenar Wasting** | **Repetition, Force, Vibration, Wrist Flexion/Extension** | **Phalen's, Tinel's, NCS** |
| **Back Pain (Occupational)** | **Mechanical Low Back Pain**, **Sciatica** (Disc Prolapse) | **Heavy Lifting, Awkward Posture, Vibration, Static Posture** | **History, Exam, MRI (Red Flags)** |
| **Hand-Arm Vibration Syndrome (HAVS)** | **Vibration White Finger (Raynaud's)**, **Sensorineural** (Numbness, Tingling), **Carpal Tunnel** | **Hand-Held Power Tools** (Breakers, Grinders, Chainsaws) | **Stockholm Workshop Scale**, **Cold Provocation Test** |
| **Whole-Body Vibration** | **Low Back Pain**, **Sciatica**, **Digestive** | **Heavy Machinery (Tractors, Trucks, Forklifts)** | **ISO 2631**, **MRI Spine** |

## 7. 📖 Noise-Induced Hearing Loss (NIHL)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Pathophysiology** | **Noise → Hair Cell Damage (Cochlea) → Permanent Threshold Shift** |
| **Audiometry** | **Notch at 4-6 kHz** (Classic), **Progressive to Speech Frequencies** |
| **Diagnosis** | **Bilateral Symmetric SNHL**, **Noise Exposure History**, **Audiogram Notch** |
| **Prevention** | **Hierarchy of Controls**: **Eliminate → Engineering (Enclosure, Silencers) → Administrative (Rotation, Time Limits) → Hearing Protection (Earplugs/Muffs)** |
| **Audiometric Monitoring** | **Baseline + Periodic (Annual/Biennial)**, **STS (Standard Threshold Shift) = 10dB Shift at 2/3/4 kHz** |
| **Regulations** | **Action Level: 80 dB(A)**, **Limit Value: 85 dB(A) (8h TWA)**, **Peak: 137 dB(C)** |

> [!warning] **NIHL = Permanent, Irreversible, Bilateral, 4-6 kHz Notch**. **Prevention = Hierarchy of Controls**. **Audiometry = Surveillance Tool**.

| Cancer | Occupational Agent | High-Risk Occupations |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Mesothelioma** | **Asbestos** (All Types) | Insulation, Shipbuilding, Construction, Brake Repair |

## 9. 📖 Health Surveillance & Fitness for Work

### Health Surveillance — When Required

| Indication | Surveillance Components |
|------------|------------------------|
| **Exposure to Hazard** (Noise, Vibration, Chemicals, Dust, Radiation, Biological Agents, Compressed Air) | **Baseline + Periodic** (History, Exam, Investigations) |
| **Specific Regulations** (Asbestos, Lead, Asbestos, Ionising Radiation, Compressed Air, Diving) | **Statutory Medicals** (Frequency Defined by Regulation) |
| **Fitness for Work** | **Pre-Placement**, **Return to Work**, **Ill-Health Retirement** |

### Biological Monitoring

| Substance | Matrix | Frequency | Action Level |
|-----------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Lead** | Blood | **3-6 Monthly** | **>20 µg/dL (Action), >50 µg/dL (Suspend)** |
| **Cadmium** | Urine (Cd), Blood | **6-12 Monthly** | **Urine Cd >5 µg/g Creatinine** |
| **Mercury** | Urine (Inorganic), Blood (MeHg) | **6 Monthly** | **Urine Hg >50 µg/g Creatinine** |
| **Cholinesterase** (OP Exposure) | Blood (RBC/Plasma) | **Pre-Employment, Periodic** | **<50% Baseline** |
| **Solvents** (Toluene, Styrene, n-Hexane) | Urine (Metabolites) | **End of Shift/Week** | **Biological Guidance Values** |
| **Isocyanates** | Urine (Diamines) | **Monthly** | **Biological Monitoring Guidance Values** |
| **Chromium (VI)** | Urine (Cr) | **End of Shift/Week** | **>10 µg/L** |

## 10. 📖 Fitness for Work & Return to Work

| Assessment | Purpose | Components |
|------------|---------|------------|
| **Pre-Placement** | **Match Capability to Job Demands** | History, Exam, Functional Tests, Investigations (Per Job) |
| **Periodic** | **Detect Early Disease**, **Fitness Maintenance** | **Risk-Based Frequency** |
| **Return to Work** | **Safe/Early Return After Illness/Injury** | **Functional Capacity Evaluation**, **Workplace Adjustments**, **Phased Return** |
| **Ill-Health Retirement** | **Permanent Incapacity** | **Medical Evidence**, **Pension Advice** |

### Fitness Standards (Examples)

| Job Category | Key Requirements |
|--------------|------------------|
| **Divers / Compressed Air Workers** | **Cardiovascular, Respiratory, ENT, Neurological, Musculoskeletal, Psychological** |
| **Drivers (LGV/PCV)** | **Vision (6/7.5), CVD, Diabetes, Epilepsy, Psychiatric, Sleep Apnoea** |
| **Offshore Workers** | **Fitness for Emergency Response**, **BMI <35**, **Cardiorespiratory Fitness** |
| **Firefighters** | **Cardiorespiratory Fitness (VO₂max), Musculoskeletal, Psychological** |
| **Food Handlers** | **No Communicable Disease**, **Hand Hygiene**, **Skin Integrity** |

## 12. 🛡️ Risk Assessment — COSHH Approach

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1. Identify Hazards** | **Inventory Substances**, **Review SDS**, **Walkthrough**, **Task Analysis** |
| **2. Assess Risk** | **Likelihood × Severity**, **Who is Exposed?**, **Duration/Frequency**, **Existing Controls** |
| **3. Control Measures** | **Hierarchy of Controls (Eliminate → Substitute → Engineering → Admin → PPE)** |
| **4. Implement Controls** | **Engineering (LEV, Enclosure), Admin (Rotation, Training), PPE (Fit-Tested)** |
| **5. Monitor & Review** | **Exposure Monitoring (Air, Biological), Health Surveillance, Incident Review, Annual Review** |

## 14. ❓ Viva Questions

1. **What is the hierarchy of controls in occupational health?**
   - **Eliminate → Substitute → Engineering → Administrative → PPE** (Most to Least Effective)

2. **What are the radiographic features of Silicosis?**
   - **Small Rounded Opacities (Simple), Progressive Massive Fibrosis (PMF), Eggshell Calcification of Hilar Nodes**.

3. **How do you differentiate Occupational Asthma from Work-Exacerbated Asthma?**
   - **OA = New Asthma Caused by Work (Latent Period, Sensitiser/Irritant)**; **WEA = Pre-existing Asthma Worsened by Work**.

3. **What is the classic radiographic finding in Asbestosis?**
   - **Pleural Plaques (Calcified, Diaphragm), Basal Interstitial Fibrosis, Honeycombing (Late)**.

4. **What is the diagnostic criteria for Hand-Arm Vibration Syndrome (HAVS)?**
   - **Vibration White Finger (Raynaud's) + Sensorineural Symptoms (Numbness/Tingling)**, **Stockholm Workshop Scale**, **Exposure History to Vibrating Tools**.

4. **What is the characteristic audiometric finding in Noise-Induced Hearing Loss?**
   - **Bilateral Symmetric Sensorineural Hearing Loss with Notch at 4-6 kHz**.

5. **What is the relationship between Asbestos and Mesothelioma?**
   - **Asbestos = Only Known Cause of Mesothelioma (Signature Cancer)**, All Types Carcinogenic, Latency 20-40 Years.

6. **What is the management of Hand-Arm Vibration Syndrome?**
   - **Stop Exposure, Warmth, Smoking Cessation, Nifedipine (If Severe), Avoid Cold/Vibration**.

6. **What are the common causes of Occupational Contact Dermatitis?**
   - **Allergic: Nickel, Chromate (Cement), Epoxy, Rubber Accelerators, Formaldehyde**; **Irritant: Wet Work, Solvents, Cutting Oils, Acids/Alkalis**.

7. **What are the criteria for diagnosing Occupational Asthma?**
   - **Work-Related Symptoms + Objective Evidence (PEF Variability >20%, Specific IgE, Specific Inhalation Challenge)**.

8. **What are the health surveillance requirements for Lead workers?**
   - **Blood Lead Level 3-6 Monthly**, **Action Level >20 µg/dL, Suspension >50 µg/dL**, **ZPP Monitoring**.

9. **What is the difference between Occupational Asthma and RADS?**
   - **OA = Latent Period, Sensitiser**; **RADS = Single High Exposure → Immediate Onset, No Latency, Persistent Asthma**.

10. **What are the prescribed diseases for Workers' Compensation (UK)?**
    - **Mesothelioma, Asbestosis, Silicosis, CWP, Byssinosis, Occupational Asthma, NIHL, HAVS, WRULDs, Contact Dermatitis, Occupational Cancers, Infectious Diseases (Lab/Animal Workers)**.
