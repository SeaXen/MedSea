# Postpartum Follow-Up and Future Risk After GDM

> [!info]
> **GDM = 7× lifetime T2DM risk** — 50% at 10 years, 70% at 20 years. Postpartum OGTT at 6–12 weeks essential (HbA1c unreliable <3 months).

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] State timing and test for postpartum glucose assessment
- [ ] Interpret postpartum OGTT results (classify)
- [ ] Quantify future T2DM and CVD risk
- [ ] Apply annual screening strategy
- [ ] Counsel on breastfeeding, contraception, lifestyle

---

## 2. Immediate Postpartum (Delivery to 6 Weeks)

| Action | Timing | Details |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Stop glucose-lowering therapy** | At delivery | Insulin/metformin — usually not needed |
| **Glucose monitoring** | First 24–48h | Fasting + 2h post-prandial × 1–2 days; exclude persistent diabetes |
| **Breastfeeding initiation** | Immediately | Encouraged — improves maternal glucose, infant outcomes |
| **Contraception discussion** | Before discharge | LARC preferred; avoid oestrogen if VTE risk |
| **Folic acid** | Continue | 5mg daily if planning future pregnancy |

---

## 3. Definitive Postpartum Testing — 6–12 Weeks

### **Test: 75g OGTT (NOT HbA1c)**
- **HbA1c unreliable** <3 months postpartum (↑ RBC turnover, blood loss, iron deficiency)
- **OGTT** same as non-pregnant criteria

### OGTT Interpretation (WHO/ADA Non-Pregnant Criteria)

| Result | Fasting | 2-Hour | Classification |
|--------|---------|--------|----------------|
| **Normal** | <6.1 mmol/L | <7.8 mmol/L | Normoglycaemia |
| **IFG** | 6.1–6.9 mmol/L | <7.8 mmol/L | Prediabetes |
| **IGT** | <6.1 mmol/L | 7.8–11.0 mmol/L | Prediabetes |
| **IFG+IGT** | 6.1–6.9 mmol/L | 7.8–11.0 mmol/L | High-risk prediabetes |
| **Diabetes** | ≥7.0 mmol/L | ≥11.1 mmol/L | **T2DM (overt)** |

---

## 4. Future Risk After GDM

| Outcome | Risk | Timeline |
|---------|------|----------|
| **T2DM** | **7×** general population | **50% at 10 years**, 70% at 20 years |
| **Progression to T2DM** | 2.5–5%/year | Highest first 5 years |
| **Recurrent GDM** | **30–50%** | Next pregnancy |
| **CVD** | **2×** (independent of T2DM) | Early onset |
| **Metabolic syndrome** | 3–4× | Within 5 years |

**Risk modifiers**:
- Higher fasting glucose in pregnancy → higher T2DM risk
- Insulin-treated GDM → higher risk than diet-controlled
- Early diagnosis (<24w) → higher risk
- Postpartum weight retention → higher risk
- Breastfeeding >3 months → **↓ T2DM risk 30–50%**

---

## 5. Annual Screening Strategy

| Test | Frequency | Threshold for Action |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| **HbA1c** | **Annual** (after first OGTT) | ≥42 mmol/mol (6.0%) → repeat OGTT |
| **FPG** | Annual (if HbA1c not available) | ≥6.1 mmol/L → OGTT |
| **OGTT** | Every 1–3 years (if prediabetes) | Per diagnostic criteria |
| **Lipids, BP, weight** | Annual | Per CVD prevention guidelines |

**If planning pregnancy**: Test every 1–2 years; pre-conception HbA1c <48 mmol/mol.

---

## 6. Lifestyle Intervention for Prevention

**Evidence**: DPP, Da Qing, DPS all included women with prior GDM — lifestyle reduces progression **~50%**.

| Target | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| **Weight** | Return to pre-pregnancy weight; then 5–7% loss if BMI ≥25 |
| **Diet** | Mediterranean/DASH; high fibre; low GI; limit SSBs |
| **Activity** | ≥150 min/week moderate + resistance 2×/week |
| **Breastfeeding** | **Exclusive ≥3 months** → ↓ T2DM risk 30–50% |
| **Sleep** | 7–9h; treat sleep apnoea |
| **Metformin** | Consider if BMI ≥30, HbA1c ≥42, prior GDM (ADA) — not NICE |

---

## 7. Breastfeeding Benefits

| For Mother | For Infant |
|------------|------------|
| ↑ Insulin sensitivity | ↓ Obesity risk |
| ↑ Weight loss (500 kcal/day) | ↓ T2DM risk later |
| ↓ T2DM risk 30–50% (≥3mo exclusive) | ↓ Asthma, infections |
| ↓ CVD risk | Optimal nutrition |
| ↓ Ovarian/breast cancer | Bonding |

---

## 8. Contraception Considerations

| Method | Suitability |
|--------|-------------|
| **LARC (IUD/IUS, implant)** | **First-line** — highly effective, no oestrogen |
| **Progestogen-only pill** | Good alternative |
| **Combined OCP** | Avoid if BMI ≥30, smoker >35, VTE risk, hypertension |
| **Depot injection** | Weight gain concern; ↓ bone density |
| **Barrier** | Low effectiveness |

**Timing**: Start before discharge; ensure effective before ovulation resumes (can be <6 weeks if not breastfeeding).

---

## 9. CVD Risk Reduction

- **BP target**: <130/80 (NICE) / <120/80 (ADA)
- **Lipids**: Statin if CVD risk ≥10% (QRISK3) — **avoid in pregnancy planning**
- **Aspirin**: 75mg if CVD risk high (postpartum, not pregnancy contraindicated)
- **Lifestyle**: As above — foundation of prevention

## 10. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **Postpartum test** | **75g OGTT at 6–12 weeks** (NOT HbA1c) |
| **HbA1c postpartum** | Unreliable <3 months (↑ RBC turnover, iron deficiency) |
| **T2DM risk after GDM** | **7× lifetime**; 50% at 10y, 70% at 20y |
| **Recurrent GDM** | 30–50% next pregnancy |
| **Breastfeeding** | ≥3 months exclusive → ↓ T2DM risk **30–50%** |
| **Annual screen** | HbA1c or FPG; OGTT if prediabetes |
| **Metformin prevention** | ADA: yes (BMI ≥30, prior GDM); NICE: lifestyle only |
| **Contraception** | LARC first-line; avoid COCP if VTE risk |
| **CVD risk** | 2× independent of T2DM |
| **Weight retention** | Major modifiable risk factor for progression |
