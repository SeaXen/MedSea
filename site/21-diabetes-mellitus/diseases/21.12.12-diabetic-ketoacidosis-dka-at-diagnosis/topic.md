## 1. Definition & Exam Relevance
**DKA at diagnosis** = first presentation of T1DM with diabetic ketoacidosis.  
**Prevalence**: 25–40% of new T1DM in children; 15–25% in adults.  
**FCPS/MRCP relevance**: High-yield acute presentation; exam questions on diagnostic criteria, severity grading, initial management, cerebral oedema prevention.

---

## 2. Diagnostic Criteria (JBS / ISPAD / NICE)
| Parameter | Mild | Moderate | Severe |
|-----------|------|----------|--------|
| **Venous pH** | 7.25–7.30 | 7.10–7.24 | <7.10 |
| **Bicarbonate (mmol/L)** | 15–18 | 10–14 | <10 |
| **Blood Ketones (β-OHB, mmol/L)** | >3.0 | >3.0 | >3.0 |
| **Glucose (mmol/L)** | >11 | >11 | >11 |
| **Anion Gap** | >12 | >16 | >20 |
| **Clinical** | Alert | Drowsy/confused | Coma (GCS <12) |

**Diagnosis requires ALL three**: Hyperglycaemia (>11 mmol/L) + Ketonaemia (>3 mmol/L) + Acidosis (pH <7.3 / bicarb <15).

---

## 3. Typical Presentation (New-Onset T1DM)
| Symptom | Duration | Pathophysiology |
|---------|----------|-----------------|
| **Polyuria / Polydipsia** | 1–4 weeks | Osmotic diuresis from hyperglycaemia |
| **Weight loss** | 2–8 weeks | Catabolism (lipolysis, proteolysis) + glycosuria calories |
| **Fatigue / Lethargy** | Days–weeks | Insulin deficiency → impaired glucose utilization |
| **Abdominal pain** | Acute (hours–days) | Ketonaemia, gastric atony, dehydration |
| **Vomiting** | Acute | Ketonaemia, gastric atony |
| **Kussmaul breathing** | Acute | Compensatory respiratory alkalosis for metabolic acidosis |
| **Acetone breath** | Acute | Exhaled acetone from ketogenesis |
| **Dehydration signs** | Acute | Osmotic diuresis + vomiting + poor intake |
| **Altered consciousness** | Late/severe | Cerebral dehydration, acidosis, hyperosmolality |

**Red flags for cerebral oedema** (mainly children): Headache, bradycardia, hypertension, deteriorating GCS, pupillary asymmetry, respiratory arrest.

---

## 4. Differential Diagnosis at Presentation
| Condition | Distinguishing Features |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **HHS** | Glucose >30 mmol/L, pH >7.3, bicarb >18, minimal ketones, older age, T2DM |
| **Starvation ketosis** | Glucose normal/low, mild ketones (1–3 mmol/L), pH normal, no acidosis |
| **Alcoholic ketoacidosis** | Glucose normal/low, high anion gap, history alcohol binge + malnutrition |
| **Sepsis** | Fever, leukocytosis, lactate ↑, glucose variable |
| **Acute abdomen (appendicitis, pancreatitis)** | Localized pain, no ketonuria/ketonaemia (or mild), glucose normal |
| **Type 2 DM with stress hyperglycaemia** | No significant weight loss, older, overweight, ketones negative/trace |

## 5. Initial Assessment (First 60 Minutes)
| Assessment | Action |
|------------|--------|
| **ABCDE** | Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability (GCS), Exposure |
| **Vital signs** | HR, BP, RR, Temp, SpO2, GCS q15–30 min |
| **Bedside glucose** | Capillary + venous confirmation |
| **Blood gas (venous/arterial)** | pH, pCO2, bicarb, lactate, electrolytes (K+, Na+, Cl-) |
| **Blood ketones (β-OHB)** | Quantitative (preferred over urine) |
| **Renal function** | Urea, creatinine, eGFR |
| **FBC, CRP** | Infection screen |
| **ECG** | Baseline; monitor for K+ changes |
| **Pregnancy test** | All females of childbearing age |

**Calculate**: Corrected Na+ = Measured Na+ + 0.016 × (Glucose – 5.5) — expect rise as glucose falls.

---

## 6. Initial Management Protocol (First 1–2 Hours)
| Step | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| **1. Fluid Resuscitation** | 0.9% NaCl | **10–20 mL/kg bolus** (child: 10 mL/kg; adult: 1L) over 1h; reassess |
| **2. Insulin Infusion** | Fixed-rate IV insulin (FRIII) | **0.05–0.1 units/kg/h** (child: 0.1 u/kg/h; adult: 0.05–0.1 u/kg/h) |
| **3. Potassium** | Add to fluids | If K+ <3.5 → hold insulin, replace K+ first; K+ 3.5–5.5 → 40 mmol/L in fluids; K+ >5.5 → no K+ |
| **4. Bicarbonate** | **Rarely indicated** | Only if pH <6.9 or life-threatening hyperkalaemia; 1–2 mmol/kg over 1h |
| **5. Monitoring** | q1–2h | Glucose, ketones, VBG, electrolytes, fluid balance, GCS |

**Insulin**: **DO NOT BOLUS** — start infusion only. Bolus increases cerebral oedema risk.

---

## 7. Cerebral Oedema — Prevention & Recognition
| Risk Factor | Prevention |
|-------------|------------|
| **Age <5 years** | Strict protocol adherence |
| **Severe acidosis (pH <7.1)** | Avoid rapid glucose fall (>5 mmol/L/h) |
| **High urea / severe dehydration** | Gradual rehydration (48h deficit replacement) |
| **Bicarbonate use** | Avoid unless pH <6.9 |
| **Early insulin bolus** | **Never bolus** — infusion only |

**Treatment if suspected**: Mannitol 0.5–1 g/kg IV over 20 min **OR** Hypertonic saline 3% 5 mL/kg over 30 min; intubation, hyperventilation, ICU transfer.

---

## 8. Transition to Subcutaneous Insulin
**Criteria**: Ketones <0.6 mmol/L, pH >7.3, eating/drinking tolerated, alert.

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1. Start basal insulin (glargine/degludec) | **Overlap with IV insulin 1–2h** before stopping infusion |
| 2. Start bolus insulin with meals | Use ICR/ISF estimates (TDD 0.5 u/kg) |
| 3. Stop IV insulin | 30–60 min after first basal dose |
| 4. Education | DAFNE referral, sick day rules, hypoglycaemia management |

---

## 9. FCPS/MRCP Exam Pearls
- **DKA at diagnosis**: 25–40% children, 15–25% adults — "new T1DM until proven otherwise"
- **Diagnostic triad**: Hyperglycaemia + Ketonaemia + Acidosis
- **Venous pH sufficient** — no need arterial unless respiratory compromise
- **β-OHB >3 mmol/L** — diagnostic; urine ketones lag, semi-quantitative
- **FRIII rate**: 0.1 u/kg/h (children), 0.05–0.1 u/kg/h (adults) — **NO BOLUS**
- **Fluid**: 0.9% NaCl initially; switch to 0.45% NaCl + 5% dextrose when glucose <14 mmol/L
- **Potassium**: ALWAYS check before insulin; replace if <3.5; add to fluids at 40 mmol/L
- **Cerebral oedema**: Mainly children <5y; headache + bradycardia + hypertension = emergency
- **Transition**: Basal insulin start → overlap 1–2h → stop IV insulin
- **Precipitants**: Infection (UTI, pneumonia), missed insulin, new diagnosis, MI, stroke, drugs (SGLT2i, steroids)
