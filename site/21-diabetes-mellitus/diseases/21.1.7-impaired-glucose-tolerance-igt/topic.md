# Impaired Glucose Tolerance (IGT)

> [!info]
> **2-hr OGTT 7.8–11.0 mmol/L (140–199 mg/dL)** — Reflects muscle insulin resistance; higher T2DM progression risk than IFG alone.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] State OGTT diagnostic criteria for IGT
- [ ] Contrast pathophysiology vs IFG (muscle vs hepatic IR)
- [ ] Quantify progression risk and CVD association
- [ ] Apply lifestyle/metformin prevention evidence
- [ ] Identify when IGT coexists with IFG (highest risk)

---

## 2. Diagnostic Criteria

| Test | IGT Range | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **2-hr OGTT (75g)** | 7.8–11.0 mmol/L (140–199 mg/dL) | FPG <7.0 mmol/L required |
| **HbA1c** | Not diagnostic for IGT | HbA1c 39–47 mmol/mol correlates but not diagnostic |

**Requirements**: Standard 75g OGTT after ≥3 days normal diet, overnight fast. WHO and ADA criteria identical for IGT.

---

## 3. Pathophysiology

**IGT = Skeletal muscle insulin resistance** (primary defect):
- Impaired insulin-stimulated glucose disposal in muscle
- Hepatic insulin sensitivity relatively preserved → normal fasting glucose
- β-cell compensation initially maintains fasting glucose
- Post-prandial hyperglycaemia from failed muscle uptake

**Contrast with IFG**:
| Feature | IFG | IGT |
|---------|-----|-----|
| **Primary site** | Liver | Muscle |
| **Fasting glucose** | ↑ (6.1–6.9) | Normal (<6.1) |
| **2-hr glucose** | Normal (<7.8) | ↑ (7.8–11.0) |
| **Insulin secretion** | ↓ Early phase | ↓ Late phase |
| **FFA suppression** | Impaired | Relatively preserved |

---

## 4. Progression Risk (Higher than IFG)

| Category | Annual Progression | 10-Year Risk |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|
| **IGT alone** | 5–8% | 30–50% |
| **IFG alone** | 4–6% | 20–35% |
| **IFG + IGT** | 10–15% | 50–70% |
| **IGT + HbA1c 42–47** | ~12% | >60% |

**DECODE study**: IGT carries higher CVD mortality than IFG.

---

## 5. Clinical Significance

**CVD risk**: IGT independently predicts CVD (HR 1.5–2.0), similar to diabetes in some cohorts.
- Post-challenge hyperglycaemia → oxidative stress, endothelial dysfunction
- Associated with: hypertension, dyslipidaemia (high TG, low HDL), prothrombotic state

**Microvascular risk**: Retinopathy/nephropathy prevalence ↑ at IGT stage.

---

## 6. Screening & Diagnosis

**When to do OGTT** (NICE/ADA):
- FPG 5.6–6.9 mmol/L (ADA IFG) or 6.1–6.9 (WHO IFG)
- HbA1c 39–47 mmol/mol (5.7–6.4%)
- High clinical suspicion despite normal FPG/HbA1c
- GDM follow-up (6–12 weeks postpartum)
- PCOS, antipsychotic use, post-transplant

**OGTT procedure**:
1. 3 days normal carbohydrate intake (≥150g/day)
2. 8–14 hr fast
3. 75g anhydrous glucose in 250–300mL water over 5 min
4. Sample at 0 and 120 min (some protocols 30, 60, 90, 120)

---

## 7. Management — Lifestyle Intervention

**Evidence base** (IGT populations):
| Trial | Population | Intervention | Risk Reduction |
|-------|------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Da Qing** (China) | IGT | Diet ± exercise ± both | 31–46% at 6 yr |
| **Finnish DPS** | IGT + BMI>25 | Intensive lifestyle | 58% at 3.2 yr |
| **DPP** (USA) | IGT + FPG 5.3–6.9 | Lifestyle vs metformin | 58% vs 31% |

**Targets** (same as IFG):
- **Weight**: 5–7% loss
- **Activity**: ≥150 min/week moderate
- **Diet**: Reduced fat (<30% kcal), high fibre, Mediterranean pattern

---

## 8. Pharmacological Prevention

**Metformin** (ADA indication for IGT):
- BMI ≥30 (or ≥25 Asian), age 25–59
- FPG ≥6.1 or HbA1c ≥42 mmol/mol (6.0%)
- Prior GDM
- **Not NICE-recommended** for prevention in UK (lifestyle only)

**Other agents** (evidence in IGT):
- **Acarbose** (STOP-NIDDM): 25% reduction, GI side effects limit use
- **Pioglitazone** (ACT NOW): 72% reduction but weight gain, HF risk, fractures
- **GLP-1 RA / SGLT2i**: Emerging data, not licensed for prevention

---

## 9. Follow-up

| Status | Interval |
|--------|----------|
| **IGT confirmed** | Annual OGTT or HbA1c+FPG |
| **Post-GDM** | 6–12 weeks (OGTT), then 1–3 yearly |
| **High risk (IFG+IGT)** | 6–12 monthly |

## 10. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **IGT diagnosis** | **Only by OGTT** — FPG/HbA1c cannot diagnose IGT |
| **IGT vs IFG progression** | IGT > IFG for T2DM progression |
| **IGT CVD risk** | Independent predictor, similar to diabetes |
| **Metformin for prevention** | ADA yes (high-risk IGT); NICE no |
| **Da Qing / DPS / DPP** | All in IGT populations — know risk reductions |
| **OGTT prep** | 3 days normal carbs, overnight fast |

---

## 11. Differential Diagnosis
- **Early T2DM**: 2-hr ≥11.1 mmol/L
- **Renal glycosuria**: Low renal threshold → false high 2-hr
- **Dumping syndrome**: Post-gastrectomy → rapid glucose peak then hypoglycaemia
- **Acromegaly/Cushing**: Secondary IGT from hormone excess

---

## 12. Complications if Untreated
- T2DM: 30–50% at 10 years
- CVD: MI, stroke risk 1.5–2×
- Microvascular: Early retinopathy, microalbuminuria
- NAFLD/NASH progression
