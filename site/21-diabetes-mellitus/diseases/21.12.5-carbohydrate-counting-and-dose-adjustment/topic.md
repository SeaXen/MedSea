## 1. Definition & Exam Relevance
**Carbohydrate Counting** = matching rapid-acting insulin dose to carbohydrate intake using an **Insulin-to-Carbohydrate Ratio (ICR)**.  
**FCPS/MRCP relevance**: Core T1DM self-management skill (DAFNE); exam questions on ICR/ISF calculations, dose adjustment algorithms, troubleshooting.

---

## 2. Core Calculations

### Insulin-to-Carbohydrate Ratio (ICR)
| Method | Formula | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **500 Rule** | 500 ÷ TDD (units) = grams CHO per 1 unit | TDD 40u → 500/40 = **12.5g/unit** (1u per 12.5g) |
| **300 Rule** (some use) | 300 ÷ TDD | TDD 40u → 7.5g/unit (more conservative) |
| **Weight-based** | 1 unit per (weight in kg × 0.5–1) g | 70kg → 1u per 35–70g |

**Starting ICR by meal** (dawn phenomenon adjustments):
- Breakfast: 1 unit per **8–10g** (higher insulin resistance)
- Lunch: 1 unit per **10–12g**
- Dinner: 1 unit per **12–15g**

### Insulin Sensitivity Factor (ISF) / Correction Factor
| Method | Formula | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **100 Rule** | 100 ÷ TDD = mmol/L drop per 1 unit | TDD 40u → 100/40 = **2.5 mmol/L per unit** |
| **1800 Rule** (mg/dL) | 1800 ÷ TDD = mg/dL drop per unit | TDD 40u → 45 mg/dL per unit |

**Target correction glucose**: Usually **5.5–6.0 mmol/L** (individualized).

---

## 3. Bolus Dose Calculation
```
Bolus Dose = (Carbohydrate grams ÷ ICR) + ((Current BG – Target BG) ÷ ISF)
```

**Example**: BG 11 mmol/L, target 5.5, meal 60g CHO, ICR 1:12, ISF 1:2.5
- Carb bolus: 60 ÷ 12 = **5 units**
- Correction: (11 – 5.5) ÷ 2.5 = **2.2 units**
- **Total = 7.2 units** (round to 7 or 7.5 by pen/pump)

---

## 4. DAFNE Adjustment Algorithm (Pattern-Based)
| Pattern | Action | Magnitude |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Post-prandial >9 mmol/L consistently** | ↑ ICR (more insulin per carb) | 10–20% (e.g., 1:12 → 1:10) |
| **Post-prandial <4 mmol/L consistently** | ↓ ICR (less insulin per carb) | 10–20% (e.g., 1:12 → 1:15) |
| **Pre-meal high >7 mmol/L** | ↑ correction (↓ ISF) or check prior ICR | ISF: 1:2.5 → 1:2.0 |
| **Pre-meal low <4 mmol/L** | ↓ correction (↑ ISF) | ISF: 1:2.5 → 1:3.0 |
| **3am low <3.9 mmol/L** | ↓ basal (10–20%) | Basal: 20u → 16–18u |
| **3am high >7 mmol/L** | ↑ basal (10–20%) | Basal: 20u → 22–24u |

**Rule**: Change **one parameter at a time**; review after 3 days of consistent pattern.

---

## 5. Advanced Carbohydrate Counting
| Factor | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| **Fat/Protein** | High fat (>30g) or protein (>40g) → delayed glucose rise (3–5h); consider **dual-wave bolus** (pump) or **split dose** (pen: 60% now, 40% at 2h) |
| **Glycaemic Index** | Low GI → slower rise; may need **delayed bolus** or extended bolus |
| **Alcohol** | Inhibits gluconeogenesis → delayed hypoglycaemia (3–12h); reduce bedtime basal or snack |
| **Fiber** | Subtract fiber from total carbs (UK labels: "carbohydrate" = available CHO; US: subtract fiber) |

## 7. Special Situations
| Situation | ICR/ISF Adjustment |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Exercise (planned)** | ↓ pre-exercise bolus by 25–50%; ↓ basal by 20–50% during/after |
| **Exercise (unplanned)** | 15–30g carbs per 30 min; no bolus reduction possible |
| **Illness** | ↑ ISF (resistance) → may need 1.5–2× usual correction; check ketones q4h |
| **Menstruation** | Luteal phase: ↑ insulin needs 10–20% (progesterone effect) |
| **Pregnancy** | T1DM: ICR tightens progressively (1:8 → 1:6 → 1:4); ISF increases |
| **Renal impairment (eGFR <30)** | ↓ insulin clearance → ↓ TDD 25–50%; ↑ hypoglycaemia risk |
| **Hepatic impairment** | ↓ gluconeogenesis → ↑ hypoglycaemia risk; ↓ basal |

---

## 8. FCPS/MRCP Exam Pearls
- **500 Rule** = ICR; **100 Rule** = ISF — memorize both
- **Breakfast ICR most aggressive** (dawn phenomenon); dinner most generous
- **Adjust ONE thing at a time** — classic exam trap
- **Fat/protein effect**: Pizza/burger → late hyperglycaemia (3–5h); needs split/extended bolus
- **Alcohol**: Reactive hypoglycaemia next morning — reduce bedtime basal
- **Renal failure**: Insulin half-life ↑ → dose reduction; ISF ↓ (more sensitive)
- **DAFNE**: "Dose Adjustment For Normal Eating" — structured education program
- **Bolus calculator apps**: Must be FDA/CE approved; patient still needs understanding
