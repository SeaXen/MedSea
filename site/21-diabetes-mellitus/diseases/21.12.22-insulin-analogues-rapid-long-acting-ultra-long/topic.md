## 1. Definition & Exam Relevance
**Insulin analogues** = recombinant human insulin with amino acid modifications altering pharmacokinetics.  
**FCPS/MRCP relevance**: Core prescribing knowledge; exam questions on onset/peak/duration, conversion, pump compatibility, cost-effectiveness.

---

## 2. Classification & Pharmacokinetics

### Rapid-Acting Analogues (Bolus / Prandial)
| Insulin | Onset | Peak | Duration | Brand | Notes |
|---------|-------|------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Insulin lispro** | 15 min | 30–90 min | 3–5 h | Humalog, Liprolog | B28 Lys→Pro, B29 Pro→Lys |
| **Insulin aspart** | 10–20 min | 40–50 min | 3–5 h | NovoRapid, Fiasp | B28 Pro→Asp |
| **Insulin glulisine** | 10–20 min | 30–90 min | 3–4 h | Apidra | B3 Lys, B29 Glu |
| **Faster aspart (Fiasp)** | **5 min** | 30–60 min | 3–5 h | Fiasp | + niacinamide (absorption enhancer) |
| **Lyumjev (lispro-aabc)** | **5 min** | 30–60 min | 3–5 h | Lyumjev | + treprostinil + citrate |

**Exam tip**: Fiasp & Lyumjev = **ultra-rapid** — can dose **at meal start** (even after first bite); standard rapid = 15–20 min before meal.

### Long-Acting Analogues (Basal)
| Insulin | Onset | Peak | Duration | Brand | Notes |
|---------|-------|------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Insulin glargine U100** | 1–2 h | **None (flat)** | 20–24 h | Lantus, Abasaglar, Semglee | A21 Gly→Arg, B31/B32 Arg+Arg |
| **Insulin detemir** | 1–2 h | Minimal | 16–24 h | Levemir | B29 Lys removed, C14 fatty acid (myristic) → albumin binding |
| **Insulin glargine U300** | 1–2 h | **None (flatter)** | **>24 h (30 h)** | Toujeo | 3× concentration → slower absorption |
| **Insulin degludec U100/U200** | 1 h | **None (flattest)** | **>42 h** | Tresiba | B29 Lys removed, C16 fatty acid (hexadecanedioic) → multi-hexamer chains |

---

## 3. Key Pharmacokinetic Differences — Exam Focus

| Property | Glargine U100 | Glargine U300 | Detemir | Degludec |
|----------|---------------|---------------|---------|----------|
| **Duration** | 20–24 h | >24 h (30 h) | 16–24 h | **>42 h** |
| **Variability (CV%)** | ~20% | ~15% | ~15% | **~8%** (lowest) |
| **Dosing flexibility** | ±1–2 h | ±3 h | ±2–3 h | **±8–24 h** (any time of day) |
| **Steady state** | 2–3 days | 3–4 days | 2–3 days | **3–4 days** |
| **Hypoglycaemia (nocturnal)** | Reference | ↓ 20–30% | ↓ 10–20% | **↓ 30–40%** |
| **Weight gain** | Reference | Similar | **Less** | Similar |
| **Injection volume** | Standard | **1/3 volume** | Standard | U200 = **1/2 volume** |
| **Pump compatible** | Yes (off-label) | No | Yes | **No** (precipitation risk) |

---

## 4. Concentrated Insulins — When to Use
| Insulin | Concentration | Indication |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| **Glargine U300 (Toujeo)** | 300 units/mL | >80 units/day basal; volumes >1 mL; nocturnal hypo on U100 |
| **Degludec U200 (Tresiba)** | 200 units/mL | >80 units/day basal; injection volume reduction |
| **Lispro U200 (Humalog)** | 200 units/mL | High bolus doses (>60 units/meal); pump users |
| **Aspart U200 (NovoRapid)** | 200 units/mL | High bolus doses; pump users |

**Conversion**: Unit-for-unit (1 unit U100 = 1 unit U200/U300); **volume reduces 2–3×**.

---

## 5. Basal-Bolus Regimen Construction
| Component | Typical Starting Dose | Adjustment |
|-----------|----------------------|------------|
| **Total Daily Dose (TDD)** | 0.4–0.5 units/kg/day | ↓ if honeymoon, renal impairment, elderly |
| **Basal proportion** | 40–50% TDD | Titrate to fasting 4–7 mmol/L |
| **Bolus proportion** | 50–60% TDD | Divided by meals (ICR) |
| **ICR (Insulin-to-Carb Ratio)** | 500 / TDD (units per 10g) | Adjust by 10–20% based on post-prandial |
| **ISF (Insulin Sensitivity Factor)** | 100 / TDD (mmol/L per unit) | Adjust by 10–20% based on correction response |

---

## 6. Switching Between Basal Insulins
| Switch | Conversion | Monitoring |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **Glargine U100 → U300** | 1:1 units; **may need 10–20% ↑** after 1 week | Check fasting at 3–7 days |
| **Glargine U100 → Degludec** | 1:1 units (or ↓10–20% if frequent hypoglycaemia) | Titrate weekly; allow 3–4 days steady state |
| **Detemir → Degludec** | 1:1 units (detemir often bid → degludec once daily) | Monitor for hypoglycaemia first week |
| **NPH → Analogue** | ↓ 20% total basal dose | Significant hypo risk reduction |

---

## 7. Pump-Compatible Insulins
- **Preferred**: Lispro, Aspart, Fiasp, Lyumjev (rapid analogues)
- **NOT recommended**: Glargine (precipitation in tubing), Degludec (precipitation), Detemir (unpredictable absorption in pump)
- **Reservoir change**: Every 48–72h (Fiasp/Lyumjev: 48h due to excipients)

---

## 8. Biosimilars & Cost
| Biosimilar | Reference | Interchangeable | Cost Saving |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| **Abasaglar (glargine)** | Lantus | Yes (NICE) | ~30% |
| **Semglee (glargine)** | Lantus | Yes (EMA/FDA) | ~30% |
| **Truvelog (aspart)** | NovoRapid | Yes | ~30% |
| **Sanofi glargine U300 (not biosimilar)** | Toujeo | N/A | N/A |

**NICE**: Prescribe least costly equivalent; biosimilars preferred for new starts.

---

## 9. FCPS/MRCP Exam Pearls
- **Degludec duration >42h** → allows flexible dosing time (unique among basals)
- **Degludec CV ~8%** → lowest variability → least nocturnal hypoglycaemia
- **Glargine U300** → 1/3 injection volume; may need 10–20% higher dose than U100
- **Fiasp/Lyumjev** → dose at meal start (not 15 min before); safer for unpredictable eating
- **Detemir** → less weight gain (albumin binding limits mitogenic effect)
- **Pump**: Only rapid analogues; change set q48–72h
- **Conversion**: Always unit-for-unit for concentrated insulins; adjust dose clinically after
- **Biosimilars**: Abasaglar/Semglee/Truvelog = interchangeable with reference
