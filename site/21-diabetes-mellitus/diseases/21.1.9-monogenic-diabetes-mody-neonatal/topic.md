# Monogenic Diabetes (MODY, Neonatal) — Epidemiology & Classification

> [!info]
> **Monogenic diabetes = Single gene defect causing diabetes** — 1–5% of diabetes; often misdiagnosed as T1DM/T2DM; correct diagnosis changes management (sulfonylureas, no treatment).

---

## 2. Prevalence & Proportion

| Category | Proportion of Diabetes | Prevalence |
|----------|------------------------|------------|
| **All monogenic** | **1–5%** (up to 6% in some cohorts) | ~80/100,000 |
| **MODY** | **1–3%** of diabetes | ~1/10,000 |
| **Neonatal diabetes** | <0.1% | 1/100,000–200,000 births |
| **Mitochondrial** | Rare | <1% of monogenic |

**Misdiagnosis rate**: ~80% initially diagnosed as T1DM or T2DM.

---

## 3. MODY Classification (Top Subtypes)

| MODY | Gene | Protein | % of MODY | Key Features | Treatment |
|------|------|---------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| **MODY 2** | **GCK** | Glucokinase | **30–50%** | Mild fasting hyperglycaemia (5.5–8.0), stable, non-progressive, no complications | **None** (diet only) |
| **MODY 3** | **HNF1A** | Transcription factor | **30–50%** | Progressive, glycosuria at low threshold, sulphurea-sensitive, ↑ CVD risk | **Sulfonylureas** (1st line) |
| **MODY 1** | **HNF4A** | Transcription factor | 5–10% | Similar to HNF1A, macrocephaly, neonatal hypoglycaemia | **Sulfonylureas** |
| **MODY 5** | **HNF1B** | Transcription factor | 5% | **Renal cysts, GU anomalies**, pancreatic atrophy, gout, ↓ HDL | Insulin often needed |
| **MODY 4** | **PDX1** | Transcription factor | <1% | Pancreatic agenesis (homozygous) | Variable |
| **MODY 6** | **NEUROD1** | Transcription factor | <1% | Rare | Variable |
| **MODY 7-14** | KLF11, CEL, PAX6, etc. | Various | Very rare | — | — |

## 4. Clinical Features Suggesting MODY

| Feature | MODY | T1DM | T2DM |
|---------|------|------|------|
| **Age at diagnosis** | <25 (often childhood/teens) | <30 | >40 (usually) |
| **Family history** | **Autosomal dominant** (3 generations) | Negative/sporadic | Often positive but not Mendelian |
| **Autoantibodies** | **Negative** | Positive (GAD, IA-2, ZnT8) | Negative |
| **C-peptide** | **Preserved** (low in HNF1A late) | Low/absent | Normal/high |
| **BMI** | Normal (except HNF1A later) | Low/normal | High |
| **Insulin requirement** | Not initially (except HNF1B) | Immediate | Variable/late |
| **Sulfonylurea response** | **Excellent (HNF1A/4A)** | Poor | Modest |
| **Complications** | Rare in GCK; typical in HNF1A | Typical | Typical |

---

## 5. Neonatal Diabetes (Diagnosis <6 Months)

| Type | Gene | Proportion | Features | Treatment |
|------|------|------------|----------|-----------|
| **Transient (TNDM)** | **6q24** (PLAGL1/HYMAI) | **~50%** | Remits by 18m, recurs adolescence | Insulin initially; may need later |
| **Permanent (PNDM)** | **KCNJ11** (Kir6.2) | ~30% | **Sulfonylurea-responsive** (90%) | **High-dose sulfonylurea** |
| | **ABCC8** (SUR1) | ~15% | Sulfonylurea-responsive | **High-dose sulfonylurea** |
| | **INS** | ~10% | Not sulfonylurea-responsive | Insulin |
| | **GCK** (homozygous) | Rare | Severe hyperglycaemia | Insulin |

**Key**: **ALL neonatal diabetes <6 months should have genetic testing** — changes management.

---

## 6. Mitochondrial Diabetes

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Common mutation** | **m.3243A>G** (MELAS/MIDD) |
| **Inheritance** | Maternal |
| **Diabetes phenotype** | Usually adult-onset, insulin-deficient, sensorineural deafness |
| **Other features** | Short stature, cardiomyopathy, renal, GI dysmotility |
| **Treatment** | Avoid metformin (lactic acidosis risk); insulin often needed |

---

## 7. Genetic Testing Indications

**Test for MODY if**: (NICE/ADA)
- Diagnosed <25 years + **any** of:
  - Strong FHx (autosomal dominant pattern, 2+ generations)
  - Negative autoantibodies
  - Preserved C-peptide (>200 pmol/L at 3+ years)
  - No features of insulin resistance (normal BMI, no acanthosis)
  - Features of specific subtype (renal cysts → HNF1B; deafness → mitochondrial)

**Test for neonatal diabetes if**:
- **Diagnosis <6 months** (mandatory)
- Diagnosis 6–12 months + negative antibodies

**Testing approach**: Targeted panel (GCK, HNF1A, HNF4A, HNF1B, KCNJ11, ABCC8, INS, 6q24) → WES if negative.

---

## 8. Management Implications by Subtype

| Subtype | Treatment | Monitoring |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **GCK-MODY** | **No pharmacotherapy**; diet only | Annual HbA1c; pregnancy: consider insulin if 2-hr post-prandial >7.8 |
| **HNF1A/4A-MODY** | **Low-dose sulfonylurea** (gliclazide 20–80mg, glipizide) | Excellent response; monitor hypoglycaemia; CVD risk ↑ |
| **HNF1B-MODY** | Often **insulin** (pancreatic atrophy) | Renal US, LFTs, gout screen, Mg²⁺, lipids |
| **KCNJ11/ABCC8 neonatal** | **High-dose sulfonylurea** (glibenclamide up to 1mg/kg/day) | Neurodevelopmental monitoring; transition from insulin |
| **Mito m.3243A>G** | **Avoid metformin**; insulin as needed | Audiology, cardiac, renal screening |

## 9. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **Monogenic proportion** | 1–5% of diabetes |
| **Most common MODY** | **GCK (MODY 2) — 30–50%** = mild fasting hyperglycaemia, no treatment |
| **Most common symptomatic MODY** | **HNF1A (MODY 3) — 30–50%** = sulfonylurea-responsive |
| **HNF1B (MODY 5)** | Renal cysts + diabetes + genital anomalies + gout |
| **Neonatal diabetes <6m** | **Mandatory genetic testing** — KCNJ11/ABCC8 = sulfonylurea-responsive |
| **Transient neonatal** | 6q24 (PLAGL1) — remits by 18mo, recurs later |
| **Mitochondrial m.3243A>G** | MELAS/MIDD; maternal inheritance; deafness; **avoid metformin** |
| **Genetic testing criteria** | Dx <25y + FHx + Ab negative + preserved C-peptide |
| **GCK in pregnancy** | No treatment unless 2-hr >7.8; insulin if needed |
