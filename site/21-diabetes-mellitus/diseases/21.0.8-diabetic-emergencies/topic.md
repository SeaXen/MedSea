## High-Yield Exam Points (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Facts |
|-------|-----------|
| **DKA diagnosis** | Glucose >11.1 · Ketonaemia >3 mmol/L or significant ketonuria · pH <7.3 · Bicarbonate <15 |
| **DKA severity** | Mild: pH 7.25-7.30, K+ >3.5 · Moderate: pH 7.0-7.24, K+ 3.0-3.5 · Severe: pH <7.0, K+ <3.0 |
| **DKA management** | 1) Fluids: 0.9% NaCl 1L/hr ×1-2h then adjust · 2) Insulin: FRII 0.1U/kg/hr · 3) K+: replace per level · 4) Bicarb if pH <7.0 · 5) Monitor: glucose q1h, ketones q2-4h, VBG q2-4h |
| **Euglycaemic DKA** | Glucose <13.9 mmol/L · On SGLT2i · Same ketone/pH criteria · Stop SGLT2i |
| **HHS diagnosis** | Glucose >30 mmol/L · Osmolality >320 mOsm/kg · pH >7.3 · Bicarb >18 · Minimal ketones |
| **HHS vs DKA** | HHS: older, type 2, higher glucose, higher osmolality, no acidosis, higher mortality (10-20% vs 1-5%) |
| **HHS management** | Fluids priority (lower insulin 0.05U/kg/hr) · Monitor osmolality drop <3 mOsm/kg/hr |
| **Hypoglycaemia levels** | Level 1: <3.9 mmol/L · Level 2: <3.0 mmol/L · Level 3: severe (requires assistance) |
| **Glucagon** | 1mg IM/SC adult · 0.5mg <25kg child · Nasal 3mg · IV dextrose 10-20% if IV access |
