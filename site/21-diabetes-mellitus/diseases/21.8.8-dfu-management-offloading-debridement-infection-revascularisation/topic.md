# Diabetic Foot Ulcer (DFU) Management

> [!info]
> **DFU management = MDT + TIME + OFFLOADING** — offloading is **cornerstone** (Total Contact Cast gold standard); debridement essential; infection per IWGDF/PEDIS; vascular assessment mandatory; coordinate diabetology, podiatry, vascular, orthotics, microbiology.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Apply TIME framework to wound bed preparation
- [ ] Select appropriate offloading modality (TCC, removable walker, etc.)
- [ ] Classify and treat infection per IWGDF/PEDIS
- [ ] Assess vascular status and refer for revascularisation
- [ ] Coordinate MDT care

---

## 2. TIME Framework — Wound Bed Preparation

| Letter | Principle | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| **T** | **Tissue** (non-viable) | **Debridement** (sharp, enzymatic, autolytic, larval) — remove necrosis, callus, slough |
| **I** | **Infection / Inflammation** | Assess (PEDIS); treat with topical/systemic antibiotics per severity |
| **M** | **Moisture** balance | **Moist wound healing** — appropriate dressings (foam, hydrocolloid, alginate, hydrogel) |
| **E** | **Edge** (non-advancing) | Stimulate edges — consider advanced therapies (NPWT, biologics) if stalled >4w |

---

## 3. Offloading — **Cornerstone of DFU Healing**

| Modality | Description | Indication | Healing Rate | Pros/Cons |
|----------|-------------|------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Total Contact Cast (TCC)** | Fibreglass/plaster cast, moulded to foot, changed weekly | **Gold standard** for plantar forefoot/midfoot ulcers (UT 1A–2C) | **Highest** (~80–90% at 12w) | Non-removable (ensures adherence); skilled application; contraindicated if infection/ischaemia |
| **Instant TCC (iTCC)** | Removable walker wrapped with cohesive bandage | Alternative to TCC | Similar to TCC | Easier application |
| **Removable Cast Walker (RCW)** | Aircast, DH Offloading Walker, etc. | If TCC contraindicated; patient preference | Lower (adherence ~30%) | Removable → poor adherence |
| **Offloading Shoes/Insoles** | Custom orthotics, healing sandals | Prevention; low-risk ulcers | Limited for active ulcers | Adherence-dependent |
| **Crutches/Wheelchair** | Non-weight-bearing | Adjunct; severe ulcers | Poor adherence | Not practical long-term |

**Key**: **Non-removable offloading (TCC/iTCC) > removable** — adherence is critical.

---

## 4. Debridement

| Type | Method | Indication | Frequency |
|------|--------|------------|-----------|
| **Sharp (surgical)** | Scalpel, curette — **by trained clinician** | Necrotic tissue, callus, slough, biofilm | **Weekly** at dressing change (TCC) |
| **Autolytic** | Hydrogel, hydrocolloid | Non-urgent; non-infected | Daily |
| **Enzymatic** | Collagenase, papain-urea | When sharp not available | Daily |
| **Larval (maggot)** | Lucilia sericata larvae | Necrotic, sloughy; not infected | 48–72h cycles |

**Callus removal essential** — increases plantar pressure 30–50%; debride at every visit.

---

## 5. Infection Classification & Management (IWGDF / PEDIS)

### PEDIS Infection Grades
| Grade | Clinical Criteria | Treatment |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|
| **1 (No infection)** | No purulence, no inflammation | No antibiotics; local wound care |
| **2 (Mild)** | ≥2 signs inflammation (erythema 0.5–2cm, warmth, induration) **Limited to skin/subcut** | **Narrow-spectrum oral** (flucloxacillin 500mg QDS 1–2w) |
| **3 (Moderate)** | Erythema >2cm **OR** deep tissue involvement (abscess, osteomyelitis, septic arthritis) **OR** systemic signs (fever, raised WCC/CRP) **BUT** haemodynamically stable | **Broad-spectrum oral/IV** (co-amoxiclav, clindamycin + ciprofloxacin) 2–4w; **bone = 6w** |
| **4 (Severe)** | **Systemic sepsis** (hypotension, tachycardia, confusion, lactate >2, organ dysfunction) | **IV broad-spectrum** (piperacillin-tazobactam, meropenem) + **urgent surgical debridement** + **ICU** |

### Osteomyelitis Diagnosis
- **Probe-to-bone** +ve (specificity >85%)
- **ESR >70**, CRP elevated
- **MRI** (gold standard imaging)
- **Bone biopsy** (culture + histology) — definitive

**Antibiotic duration**: Soft tissue 1–2w; **Bone 6w** (2w IV → 4w oral step-down).

## 6. Vascular Assessment & Revascularisation

| Test | Threshold for Action |
|------|---------------------|
| **ABI** (Ankle-Brachial Index) | **<0.9** = PAD; **<0.5** = severe; **>1.3** = non-compressible (medial calcification) — use TBI |
| **Toe-Brachial Index (TBI)** | **<0.7** = PAD; **<0.25** = critical limb ischaemia |
| **TcPO2** (Transcutaneous O2) | **<30 mmHg** = poor healing; **<20** = critical |
| **Duplex US / CTA / MRA** | Anatomic mapping for revascularisation |

**Revascularisation Indications** (IWGDF):
- **Rest pain**, **tissue loss** (ulcer/gangrene) + haemodynamic significance
- **Endovascular first** (angioplasty, stenting) for TASC A/B
- **Bypass surgery** for TASC C/D or failed endovascular
- **Multidisciplinary vascular MDT** decision

---

## 7. MDT Composition & Pathways

**Core MDT** (NICE NG19 / IWGDF):
- **Diabetologist** (medical lead)
- **Podiatrist** (wound care, offloading, debridement)
- **Vascular Surgeon** (revascularisation)
- **Orthotist** (footwear, insoles, TCC)
- **Microbiologist** (antibiotic stewardship)
- **Infectious Disease** (complex infection)
- **Orthopaedic Surgeon** (amputation, Charcot)
- **Tissue Viability Nurse**
- **Radiologist** (imaging)
- **Plastic Surgeon** (reconstruction)

**Pathways**:
- **Low risk (Risk 1)**: Community podiatry
- **Moderate risk (Risk 2)**: MDT clinic 3–6 monthly
- **High risk (Risk 3)**: Specialist MDT 1–3 monthly
- **Active ulcer/infection/ischaemia**: **Urgent MDT within 24–48h**

---

## 8. Adjunctive / Advanced Therapies (If Stalled >4 Weeks)

| Therapy | Evidence | Use |
|---------|----------|-----|
| **NPWT** (Negative Pressure) | RCT support for post-surgical / large wounds | After debridement; bridge to healing |
| **Hyperbaric Oxygen (HBOT)** | Cochrane: ↓ major amputation in selected | Refractory Wagner 3–4; adjunct |
| **Growth Factors** (PDGF, becaplermin) | Modest benefit; expensive | Refractory clean ulcers |
| **Skin Substitutes** (dermal matrices) | Case series; RCT limited | Large defects post-debridement |
| **Stem Cells / PRP** | Experimental | Research settings |

## 9. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **Cornerstone of DFU healing** | **Offloading** — TCC gold standard (non-removable > removable) |
| **TIME** | Tissue (debridement), Infection, Moisture, Edge |
| **Debridement frequency** | **Weekly** sharp debridement at TCC change |
| **PEDIS Grade 2 infection** | Mild: erythema 0.5–2cm → narrow oral abx (flucloxacillin) |
| **PEDIS Grade 3 infection** | Moderate: >2cm or deep → broad-spectrum + imaging for bone |
| **PEDIS Grade 4 infection** | Severe: sepsis → IV broad + urgent surgical debridement |
| **Osteomyelitis** | Probe-to-bone +ve; ESR >70; MRI; bone biopsy definitive |
| **Antibiotic duration bone** | **6 weeks** (2 IV + 4 oral) |
| **ABI <0.9** | PAD; <0.5 severe; >1.3 calcified (use TBI) |
| **TcPO2 <30 mmHg** | Poor healing likely |
| **MDT referral** | Active ulcer = **urgent 24–48h** |
| **IWGDF Risk 3** | 1–3m specialist MDT |
