## 1. Definition & Exam Relevance
**Self-Monitoring of Blood Glucose (SMBG)** = capillary blood glucose measured by patient using a glucometer.  
**FCPS/MRCP relevance**: Core skill for T1DM management; exam questions on technique, frequency, targets, and interpretation of patterns.

---

## 2. Indications & Targets (ADA/NICE)
| Population | Pre-prandial | Post-prandial (2h) | Bedtime |
|------------|--------------|-------------------|---------|
| **T1DM adults** | 4–7 mmol/L | 5–9 mmol/L | 6–8 mmol/L |
| **Pregnancy (T1DM)** | <5.3 mmol/L | <7.8 mmol/L (1h) / <6.4 mmol/L (2h) | 5.3–6.7 mmol/L |
| **Children/Adolescents** | 4–7 mmol/L | 5–9 mmol/L | 6–8 mmol/L |
| **Frail/elderly** | 5–8 mmol/L | <10 mmol/L | 7–10 mmol/L |

**Frequency**: Minimum **4–6×/day** (pre-meals, bedtime, ±3am); more during illness, exercise, driving, pregnancy.

---

## 3. Technique & Common Errors
| Step | Key Point |
|------|-----------|
| Hand washing | Warm water, dry thoroughly — alcohol swabs alter reading |
| Lancet depth | Adjust to skin thickness; use sides of fingertips (less pain) |
| Blood drop | Apply to strip **immediately**; don’t smear |
| Coding | Modern meters auto-code; verify if manual |
| Storage | Strips in original vial, lid tight, room temp, expiry date |

**Common errors**: Insufficient blood, expired strips, extreme temperatures, haematocrit interference (low Hct → falsely high; high Hct → falsely low), certain drugs (paracetamol, ascorbic acid on some meters).

---

## 4. Pattern Management (DAFNE Principles)
| Pattern | Interpretation | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| **Fasting high** | Dawn phenomenon / waning insulin / Somogyi | Check 3am glucose → adjust basal |
| **Pre-lunch high** | Breakfast bolus insufficient / carb undercount | Increase breakfast ICR |
| **Post-prandial high** | ICR too low / injection-meal gap too short | Increase ICR or inject 15–20 min before meal |
| **Pre-dinner high** | Lunch bolus insufficient | Increase lunch ICR |
| **Bedtime high** | Dinner bolus insufficient / snack without insulin | Adjust dinner ICR / cover snack |
| **Nocturnal hypo** | Basal too high / missed snack / exercise effect | Reduce basal by 10–20% |

---

## 5. SMBG vs CGM — Exam Comparison
| Feature | SMBG | CGM (rtCGM/isCGM) |
|---------|------|-------------------|
| **Data points** | 4–10/day | 288/day (5-min) |
| **Trends** | No | Yes (arrows) |
| **Hypo alerts** | No | Yes (rtCGM) |
| **Time in Range** | Estimate only | Direct calculation |
| **Cost** | Low | Higher (but cost-effective in T1DM) |
| **Calibration** | None | Factory calibrated (most); some need 2×/day SMBG |

**NICE NG17**: Offer rtCGM to all T1DM adults; isCGM (Flash) if rtCGM not appropriate.

---

## 6. Special Situations
| Situation | SMBG Action |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Driving (DVLA Group 1)** | Test ≤2h before journey; every 2h on long journeys; must be ≥5 mmol/L |
| **Driving (Group 2)** | Test ≤1h before; every 2h; must be ≥5 mmol/L; annual consultant report |
| **Exercise** | Test before, during (if >30 min), after; target 7–10 mmol/L pre-exercise |
| **Illness** | Test 2–4 hourly; check ketones if >13.9 mmol/L or symptomatic |
| **Pregnancy** | Test fasting, pre-meal, 1h post-meal, bedtime; ±3am |

---

## 7. Quality Assurance
- **ISO 15197:2013**: 95% of results within ±15% of lab value (≥5.5 mmol/L) or ±0.83 mmol/L (<5.5 mmol/L)
- **Meter comparison**: Lab venous plasma glucose vs meter capillary — expect ~1 mmol/L difference capillary > venous post-prandially
- **Patient education**: DAFNE/BERTIE courses; annual review of technique

---

## 8. FCPS/MRCP Exam Pearls
- **SMBG frequency in T1DM**: "At least 4 times daily" is minimum; NICE says "4–10 times"
- **Driving threshold**: 5 mmol/L — memorize for both Group 1 and 2
- **Dawn phenomenon vs Somogyi**: 3am check distinguishes (high in dawn, low in Somogyi)
- **Paracetamol interference**: Falsely elevates glucose on **GDH-PQQ** strips (older meters); modern GO-based strips unaffected
- **Haematocrit effect**: Anaemia → falsely high; polycythaemia → falsely low
