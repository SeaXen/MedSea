# Concentrated Insulins (U-200, U-300, U-500)

> [!info]
> **Concentrated insulins = higher units/mL** for high insulin resistance; **U200**: degludec (Tresiba 200), lispro (Humalog 200); **U300**: glargine (Toujeo); **U500**: human regular (Humulin R U500); **do not convert units** — prescribe and dispense in units; dedicated pens/syringes only.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Identify indications for concentrated insulins
- [ ] Differentiate U200, U300, U500 products
- [ ] Prescribe and dispense safely (unit-based, no conversion)
- [ ] Educate patients on device use
- [ ] Understand pharmacokinetic advantages

---

## 2. Products & Indications

| Concentration | Product | Type | Indication |
|---------------|---------|------|------------|
| **U200** (200 units/mL) | **Degludec** (Tresiba 200) | Ultra-long basal | **High basal requirement** (>80u/day); flexible timing |
| | **Lispro** (Humalog 200) | Rapid | High bolus requirement; pump users |
| **U300** (300 units/mL) | **Glargine** (Toujeo) | Long basal | **High basal requirement** (>80u/day); lower hypo vs U100 |
| **U500** (500 units/mL) | **Human Regular** (Humulin R U500) | Short/intermediate | **Extreme insulin resistance** (>200u/day); IV/subQ |

---

## 3. Pharmacokinetic Advantages

| Feature | U100 Standard | Concentrated (U200/U300/U500) |
|---------|---------------|-------------------------------|
| **Injection volume** | High (e.g., 80u = 0.8mL) | **50–80% less volume** (e.g., 80u U200 = 0.4mL) |
| **Absorption** | Variable at high volumes | **More consistent** (smaller depot) |
| **Subcutaneous depot** | Large → erratic | **Smaller** → predictable |
| **Pain/discomfort** | More at high volumes | **Less** |
| **Pharmacokinetics** | Standard | **Similar or flatter** (U300 glargine flatter than U100) |

---

## 4. Indications for Use

| Patient Profile | Threshold |
|-----------------|-----------|
| **High basal insulin dose** | **>80–100 units/day** (U200/U300) |
| **High bolus insulin dose** | **>30–50 units/meal** (U200 lispro) |
| **Total daily dose** | **>200 units/day** (U500) |
| **Insulin resistance** | BMI >40, lipodystrophy, acanthosis nigricans, steroid-induced |
| **Injection site issues** | Lipohypertrophy, pain, limited sites |
| **Pump users** | High reservoir volume needs (U200 lispro) |

---

## 5. Prescribing & Dispensing — Critical Safety Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Prescribe in UNITS** | **Never convert** (e.g., "80 units" not "0.8mL" or "40 units U100 equivalent") |
| **Dedicated pen** | Each concentration has **unique pen device** (Tresiba U200 pen ≠ Tresiba U100 pen) |
| **No vial/syringe for U200/U300** | **Pen only** (fixed ratio dial); U500 has **dedicated U500 syringe** (green cap) |
| **Pharmacy dispensing** | Must match **exact product + concentration** |
| **Patient education** | "Dial your dose in **units**" — pen shows units, not mL |

---

## 6. Product-Specific Details

### Degludec U200 (Tresiba 200)
- **Same PK as U100** (duration >42h, flat)
- **Pen**: 600 units (3mL × 200u/mL)
- **Dose range**: 1–160 units (2-unit increments)
- **Advantage**: Fewer pen changes; flexible timing

### Insulin Lispro U200 (Humalog 200)
- **Same PK as U100 lispro** (onset 15min, peak 1h, duration 4h)
- **Pen**: 600 units (3mL × 200u/mL)
- **Use**: High bolus needs; pump reservoir (less frequent changes)
- **Do NOT use U200 in pump** unless pump calibrated for U200

### Glargine U300 (Toujeo)
- **Flatter profile** vs U100; **lower nocturnal hypoglycaemia**
- **Pen**: 450 units (1.5mL × 300u/mL)
- **Dose range**: 1–80 units (1-unit increments)
- **Onset slightly slower**; full effect day 3–5

### Human Regular U500 (Humulin R U500)
- **Onset 30min, peak 2–4h, duration 8–12h**
- **Vial + dedicated U500 syringe** (green cap, 0.5mL = 250u)
- **SubQ: 2–3x daily**; **IV: continuous infusion** (ICU)
- **For extreme resistance** (>200u/day)

---

## 7. Switching from U100 to Concentrated

| Principle | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Unit-for-unit** | **Same number of units** (80u U100 → 80u U200) |
| **Basal switch** | Same total daily basal units; monitor fasting |
| **Bolus switch** | Same ICR/ISF initially; monitor PP |
| **Monitor closely** | First 1–2 weeks; some need 10–20% dose reduction (better absorption) |
| **Pen training** | Mandatory — different dial mechanics |

**Do NOT**: Convert based on volume (mL) or "equivalence" calculations.

---

## 8. Common Errors & Prevention

| Error | Prevention |
|-------|------------|
| **Prescribing "mL" instead of units** | **EPR template**: "Prescribe in units only" |
| **Dispensing U100 pen for U200 prescription** | Pharmacy check: **product name + concentration** |
| **Patient using U100 syringe for U500** | **Dedicated U500 syringe** (green cap) only |
| **Pump filled with U200 without calibration** | Pump must support U200; recalibrate |
| **Dose confusion at transitions** | Written plan; "units = units" education |
