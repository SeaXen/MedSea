# Secondary Diabetes (Pancreatic, Endocrine, Drug-Induced) — Epidemiology & Classification

> [!info]
> **Secondary diabetes = Diabetes due to identifiable cause** — 1–5% of diabetes; always consider in atypical presentation (lean, rapid onset, associated features).

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Classify secondary diabetes by aetiology
- [ ] Recognise clinical clues for pancreatic vs endocrine vs drug-induced
- [ ] State key causes in each category
- [ ] Apply diagnostic approach
- [ ] Explain management differences from T1/T2

## 2. Classification (WHO/ADA)

| Category | Key Examples | Proportion |
|----------|--------------|------------|
| **Pancreatic (Type 3c)** | Chronic pancreatitis, pancreatectomy, CF, pancreatic cancer, haemochromatosis | ~1–2% (under-diagnosed) |
| **Endocrine** | Cushing's, acromegaly, phaeochromocytoma, glucagonoma, thyrotoxicosis, somatostatinoma | Rare (<1%) |
| **Drug/Chemical induced** | Glucocorticoids, antipsychotics, calcineurin inhibitors, protease inhibitors, thiazides, β-blockers, immune checkpoint inhibitors | Common (iatrogenic) |
| **Genetic syndromes** | MODY, mitochondrial, Wolfram, lipodystrophy | See monogenic |
| **Infections** | CMV, Coxsackie B (rare) | Very rare |
| **Autoimmune** | Stiff person syndrome, anti-insulin receptor antibodies | Very rare |

## 3. Pancreatic Diabetes (Type 3c) — Most Common Secondary

### Aetiology & Prevalence
| Cause | % of Type 3c | Diabetes Prevalence in Condition |
|-------|--------------|----------------------------------|
| **Chronic pancreatitis** | **~80%** | 30–50% (↑ with duration/severity) |
| **Pancreatectomy** | 10–15% | 100% total; 20–40% partial |
| **Cystic fibrosis (CFRD)** | 5% | 50% by age 30 |
| **Pancreatic cancer** | <5% | 50–80% (often new-onset clue to cancer) |
| **Haemochromatosis** | <5% | 50–60% (bronze diabetes) |
| **Acute pancreatitis** | Rare | Transient or permanent |

### Diagnostic Criteria (Ewald et al. 2012)
**All 3 required**:
1. **Pancreatic pathology** (imaging/histology) OR pancreatectomy
2. **Absence of T1DM autoantibodies** (GAD, IA-2, ZnT8)
3. **At least 2 of**: (a) Exocrine insufficiency (low faecal elastase), (b) Impaired incretin secretion (low GLP-1), (c) No insulin resistance (HOMA-IR normal), (d) Pancreatic atrophy on imaging

## 4. Endocrine Diabetes

| Condition | Mechanism | Diabetes Prevalence | Key Features |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Cushing's syndrome** | Cortisol → IR + ↑ gluconeogenesis | **20–50%** | Central obesity, striae, HTN, hypokalaemia |
| **Acromegaly** | GH → IR + ↑ hepatic glucose output | **20–40%** | Acral growth, sweating, macroglossia, HTN |
| **Phaeochromocytoma** | Catecholamines → ↑ glycogenolysis, ↓ insulin | **10–20%** | Paroxysmal HTN, headaches, sweating |
| **Glucagonoma** | Glucagon → ↑ gluconeogenesis | **50–80%** | Necrolytic migratory erythema, weight loss, DVT |
| **Thyrotoxicosis** | T3 → ↑ absorption, ↑ gluconeogenesis, IR | **2–5%** | Tachycardia, tremor, weight loss, goitre |
| **Somatostatinoma** | Somatostatin → ↓ insulin, ↓ glucagon, ↓ incretins | ~100% | Gallstones, steatorrhoea, achlorhydria |

## 5. Drug-Induced Diabetes — Very Common

### Glucocorticoids (Most Common Iatrogenic Cause)
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Prevalence** | 20–50% on chronic steroids develop hyperglycaemia |
| **Pattern** | **Post-prandial predominant** (↑ hepatic output, ↓ peripheral uptake); fasting often normal initially |
| **Dose relationship** | >7.5mg prednisolone/day ↑ risk; cumulative dose matters |
| **Reversibility** | Often improves on taper; may unmask underlying T2DM |

### Antipsychotics (2nd Generation)
| Drug | Risk | Mechanism |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Clozapine, Olanzapine** | **High** (↑ weight, direct IR) | H1 antagonism, weight gain, direct β-cell toxicity |
| **Quetiapine, Risperidone** | Moderate | Weight gain |
| **Aripiprazole, Lurasidone, Ziprasidone** | Low | Weight neutral |

### Calcineurin Inhibitors (Transplant)
| Drug | Diabetes Incidence | Mechanism |
|------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Tacrolimus** | **15–30%** (higher than ciclosporin) | ↓ Insulin transcription, β-cell toxicity |
| **Ciclosporin** | 10–20% | Similar |

### Other Agents
| Drug/Class | Risk | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Protease inhibitors** (HIV) | Moderate | Lipodystrophy, IR |
| **Thiazides** (high dose) | Low-moderate | Hypokalaemia → ↓ insulin secretion |
| **β-blockers** (non-selective) | Low | ↓ Insulin secretion, weight gain |
| **Immune checkpoint inhibitors** (PD-1/PD-L1) | Emerging | Autoimmune T1DM-like (fulminant) |
| **Statins** | Small ↑ risk | Dose-dependent; CVD benefit outweighs |

## 6. Clinical Clues Suggesting Secondary Diabetes

| Presentation | Think Of |
|--------------|----------|
| **Lean, rapid onset, abdominal pain** | Pancreatitis, pancreatic cancer, type 3c |
| **Weight gain, moon face, striae, HTN, hypokalaemia** | Cushing's (or steroid use) |
| **Acral enlargement, sweating, macroglossia** | Acromegaly |
| **Paroxysmal HTN, palpitations, sweating** | Phaeochromocytoma |
| **Rash (necrolytic migratory erythema), DVT, weight loss** | Glucagonoma |
| **New diabetes on antipsychotics/steroids/tacrolimus** | Drug-induced |
| **Post-transplant new diabetes (PTDM)** | Tacrolimus, steroids |
| **Bronze skin, hepatomegaly, cardiomyopathy** | Haemochromatosis |
| **Diabetes + pancreatic exocrine insufficiency** | Type 3c (faecal elastase low) |

---

## 7. Management Principles

| Category | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Pancreatic (Type 3c)** | **Insulin often needed** (loss of insulin + glucagon + incretins); PERT for exocrine insufficiency; avoid sulfonylureas (hypoglycaemia risk from glucagon deficiency) |
| **Endocrine** | **Treat underlying cause** (surgery for Cushing's/acromegaly/phaeo); diabetes often resolves/improves |
| **Drug-induced** | Minimise dose, switch agents if possible; **steroids: basal insulin + QDS correction**; antipsychotics: switch to low-risk agent; transplant: consider conversion from tacrolimus |

## 8. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **Type 3c = pancreatic diabetes** | Chronic pancreatitis (80%), CF, pancreatectomy, cancer, haemochromatosis |
| **Type 3c diagnosis** | Pancreatic pathology + Ab negative + 2/4 criteria (low elastase, low GLP-1, no IR, atrophy) |
| **Steroid diabetes pattern** | **Post-prandial predominant**; fasting normal initially |
| **Tacrolimus > ciclosporin** for diabetes risk post-transplant |
| **Antipsychotics**: Clozapine/olanzapine highest risk |
| **Cushing's**: 20–50% get diabetes; treat cause |
| **Acromegaly**: 20–40% diabetes; GH → IR |
| **Phaeochromocytoma**: Catecholamines → glycogenolysis; 10–20% diabetes |
| **Glucagonoma**: Rash (necrolytic migratory erythema) + diabetes + DVT |
| **ICI-induced diabetes**: Anti-PD-1 → fulminant T1DM (low C-peptide, +ve Abs) |
| **Treatment Type 3c**: Insulin + PERT; avoid sulfonylureas (no glucagon counter-regulation) |
