# Iatrogenic Hypoglycaemia Unawareness

> [!info]
> **Hypoglycaemia unawareness (HU) = loss of autonomic warning symptoms** (adrenergic: tremor, sweating, palpitations); prevalence **20–25% T1DM**; **severe hypoglycaemia risk 6×**; caused by recurrent antecedent hypoglycaemia + autonomic neuropathy; **reversible** with 2–3 weeks hypo avoidance (CGM, higher targets, HCL).

By the end of this note you should be able to:

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Impaired Awareness of Hypoglycaemia (IAH)** | **Diminished** ability to perceive onset of hypoglycaemia (Gold score ≥4, Clarke score ≥4) |
| **Hypoglycaemia Unawareness (HU)** | **Complete loss** of warning symptoms until neuroglycopenia (confusion, coma) |
| **HAAF** (Hypoglycaemia-Associated Autonomic Failure) | **Recurrent hypoglycaemia → blunted counter-regulatory response** (↓ epinephrine, ↓ glucagon) → further hypoglycaemia risk |

**Gold Score** (1-item): "Do you know when your hypos are starting?" 1=always aware → 7=never aware; **≥4 = IAH**.

**Clarke Score** (8-item): Assesses frequency, symptoms, recognition; **≥4 = IAH**.

---

## 3. Pathophysiology

| Mechanism | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Recurrent antecedent hypoglycaemia** | ↑ Brain glucose transport → ↓ perception of falling glucose; ↓ sympathoadrenal response |
| **Defective glucose counter-regulation** | **Loss of glucagon response** (intrinsic α-cell defect in T1DM); **blunted epinephrine** (HAAF) |
| **Autonomic neuropathy** | ↓ Adrenergic symptoms (tremor, palpitations, sweating) — **not required for IAH** |
| **Cortisol/GH** | Preserved (late responders; not protective) |
| **Neuroglycopenia** | Brain glucose <1.5 mmol/L → cognitive dysfunction, confusion, seizures |

**Key**: **Glucagon response lost early in T1DM**; **epinephrine becomes critical** → blunted in HAAF.

---

## 4. Risk Factors

| Strongest | Contributing |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Prior severe hypoglycaemia** | **Duration of diabetes** (>10–15y) |
| **Intensive therapy** (HbA1c <48) | **Age** (children, elderly) |
| **Recurrent mild hypoglycaemia** | **Autonomic neuropathy** |
| **Tight glucose targets** | **Sleep** (nocturnal hypoglycaemia) |
| **Alcohol** | **Exercise** (delayed hypoglycaemia) |
| **Cognitive impairment / dementia** | **Pregnancy** (tight targets) |
| **Renal impairment** (insulin clearance ↓) | **Medications** (beta-blockers mask symptoms) |

---

## 5. Clinical Consequences

| Outcome | Risk Increase |
|---------|---------------|
| **Severe hypoglycaemia** (requiring assistance) | **6-fold** (IAH) |
| **Nocturnal hypoglycaemia** | 3–4x |
| **Cognitive impairment** | Acute (during hypo) + chronic (recurrent) |
| **Cardiac arrhythmias** | QT prolongation, ischaemia (sympathetic surge) |
| **Driving accidents** | Impaired reaction time, unawareness |
| **Fear of hypoglycaemia** | Overtreatment → hyperglycaemia; QoL ↓ |
| **Mortality** | "Dead in bed" syndrome (nocturnal, unexplained) |

---

## 6. Management — Reversibility is Key

### Step 1: Avoid Hypoglycaemia (2–3 Weeks)
| Strategy | Target |
|----------|--------|
| **Relax glucose targets** | Pre-meal 6–10 mmol/L; bedtime 7–10 mmol/L |
| **CGM with alerts** | **Threshold 4.0–4.5 mmol/L**; predictive low alerts |
| **Reduce insulin doses** | Basal ↓ 10–20%; bolus ICR adjustment; correct stacking |
| **Structured education** | DAFNE refresher; hypoglycaemia avoidance skills |
| **Carbohydrate consistency** | Regular meals; bedtime snack if nocturnal risk |

### Step 2: Technology
| Technology | Benefit |
|------------|---------|
| **rtCGM** (Dexcom G6/G7, Libre 3) | Real-time alerts; predictive alarms; ↓ severe hypo 50% |
| **HCL** (780G, Control-IQ, Omnipod 5, CamAPS) | Automated basal suspend/predictive; ↑ TIR; ↓ TBR |
| **Sensor-augmented pump** (SAP) | Threshold suspend (LGS/PLGS) |

### Step 3: Address Reversible Factors
| Factor | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Alcohol** | Limit; carb with drinks |
| **Exercise** | Adjust basal/bolus; carb before/during |
| **Renal impairment** | Dose reduce insulin |
| **Medications** | Review beta-blockers, sulfonylureas |
| **Gastroparesis** | Prokinetics; small frequent meals |

### Step 4: If Persistent (Rare)
- **Pancreas transplant** (simultaneous pancreas-kidney if ESRD)
- **Islet transplant** (experimental)

## 7. Driving Regulations (UK DVLA / International)

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **IAH (Gold ≥4)** | **Must notify DVLA**; licence may be refused/revoked |
| **Severe hypoglycaemia** (1 episode requiring assistance) | **Must notify DVLA**; 3-month driving ban if awake; 12-month if asleep |
| **HCL/rtCGM use** | May support licence retention with medical review |
| **Group 2 (HGV/PSV)** | **Zero tolerance** — any severe hypo = licence loss |

---

## 8. Reversibility Evidence

| Study | Intervention | Result |
|-------|--------------|--------|
| **Cranston et al. (1994)** | 2 weeks strict hypo avoidance | **Epinephrine response restored**; awareness improved |
| **DAFNE/HARPdoc** | Education + CBT + avoidance | Sustained improvement in Gold score |
| **CGM trials** (DIAMOND, GOLD) | rtCGM | ↓ TBR 50%; ↓ severe hypo; awareness improves |
| **HCL trials** | 780G, Control-IQ, Omnipod 5 | ↓ TBR 40–50%; awareness recovery in subset |

**Timeline**: **2–3 weeks avoidance** → counter-regulatory recovery; **3–6 months** sustained awareness if CGM/HCL continued.
