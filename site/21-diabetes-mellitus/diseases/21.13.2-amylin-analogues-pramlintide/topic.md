# Amylin Analogues (Pramlintide)

> [!info]
> **Pramlintide = synthetic amylin analogue** (amylin co-secreted with insulin by β-cells); **adjunct to insulin** in T1DM/T2DM; slows gastric emptying, suppresses post-prandial glucagon, promotes satiety; **↓ post-prandial glucose, ↓ weight**; **nausea** main side effect; **not licensed UK** (US FDA only).

## 2. Amylin Physiology

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Co-secreted with** | **Insulin** (1:10–1:100 molar ratio) from β-cells |
| **Deficient in** | **T1DM** (absolute); **T2DM** (relative, impaired secretion) |
| **Actions** | **Slows gastric emptying**; **suppresses post-prandial glucagon**; **promotes satiety** (hypothalamic); inhibits digestive enzymes |
| **Net effect** | **Reduces post-prandial glucose excursion** without increasing hypoglycaemia risk (when insulin adjusted) |

---

## 3. Pramlintide (Symlin) — Pharmacology

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Structure** | Synthetic analogue; **3 proline substitutions** (positions 25, 28, 29) → prevents amyloid fibrillation |
| **Route** | **Subcutaneous** (separate injection from insulin) |
| **Half-life** | **~50 minutes** |
| **Onset** | ~20 minutes |
| **Duration** | 3–4 hours |
| **Clearance** | Renal (no dose adjustment mild/mod CKD; avoid severe) |

---

## 4. Indications & Dosing

### FDA Indications (US)
- **T1DM**: Adjunct to mealtime insulin (failed optimal control)
- **T2DM**: Adjunct to mealtime insulin (± sulfonylurea, metformin)

### Dosing Protocol
| Population | Starting Dose | Titration | Target Dose |
|------------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| **T1DM** | **15 µg** pre-meal | ↑ 15 µg q3–7d | **30–60 µg** pre-meal |
| **T2DM** | **60 µg** pre-meal | ↑ 60 µg q3–7d | **120 µg** pre-meal |

**Insulin adjustment**: **Reduce mealtime insulin by 50%** at pramlintide initiation → **then titrate** based on glucose.

---

## 5. Efficacy

| Outcome | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **HbA1c** | **−0.3 to −0.5%** (vs insulin alone) |
| **Post-prandial glucose** | **↓ 2–3 mmol/L** (major effect) |
| **Weight** | **−1.5 to −2.5 kg** (satiety) |
| **Insulin dose** | **↓ 10–20%** (due to reduced post-prandial excursion) |
| **Fasting glucose** | Minimal change |

---

## 6. Adverse Effects

| Adverse Effect | Incidence | Management |
|----------------|-----------|------------|
| **Nausea** | **30–50%** (dose-limiting) | **Slow titration**; take with food; antiemetic if needed; usually transient (4–8w) |
| **Vomiting** | 10–15% | Reduce dose; extend titration interval |
| **Hypoglycaemia** | ↑ if insulin **not reduced** | **Mandatory 50% mealtime insulin reduction** at start |
| **Anorexia** | Common | Contributes to weight loss |
| **Injection site reactions** | Mild | Rotate sites |
| **Anti-drug antibodies** | ~20% | No clinical significance |

---

## 7. Contraindications & Cautions

| Contraindication | Reason |
|------------------|--------|
| **Gastroparesis** | Worsens delayed emptying |
| **Hypoglycaemia unawareness** | Risk of severe hypo with insulin reduction complexity |
| **Severe renal impairment** (eGFR <30) | Limited data; avoid |
| **Pregnancy** | Category C; no human data |
| **Paediatric** | Not studied <18y |

---

## 8. Pramlintide vs GLP-1 RA

| Feature | **Pramlintide** | **GLP-1 RA** |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Meal-time** | Yes (pre-meal) | Daily/weekly (not meal-timed) |
| **Weight loss** | −1.5 to −2.5 kg | **−3 to −15 kg** (semaglutide 2.4mg) |
| **HbA1c reduction** | −0.3 to −0.5% | **−1.0 to −2.0%** |
| **Nausea** | **30–50%** (high) | 15–25% (dose-dependent) |
| **Hypoglycaemia risk** | Low (if insulin reduced) | Low |
| **Cost/availability** | **US only**; not UK/EU | **Global**; multiple agents |
| **Positioning** | Niche (adjunct to insulin) | **Preferred** for weight/HbA1c |
