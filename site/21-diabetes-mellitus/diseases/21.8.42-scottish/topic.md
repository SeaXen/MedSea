# Foot Risk Classification (IWGDF / Scottish)

> [!info]
> **IWGDF 2019/2023** = 4 categories (Risk 0–3); **Scottish** = 3 categories (Low/Moderate/High); both based on neuropathy, ischaemia, deformity, prior ulcer/amputation; determines screening frequency and referral pathway.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Classify foot risk using IWGDF (0–3) and Scottish systems
- [ ] Perform annual foot screen (monofilament, vibration, pulses)
- [ ] Apply screening frequency per risk category
- [ ] Understand referral pathways to MDT

---

## 2. IWGDF Risk Classification (2019/2023) — **International Standard**

| Risk | Criteria | Clinical Features | Screening Frequency | Referral |
|------|----------|-------------------|---------------------|----------|
| **0 (Very Low)** | **No LOPS, No PAD, No deformity** | Normal sensation, palpable pulses, no deformity, no prior ulcer | **Annual** | Self-care education; routine podiatry not needed |
| **1 (Low)** | **LOPS only** | Neuropathy (monofilament -ve) **but** palpable pulses, no deformity | **Annual** | Podiatry for nail/skin care; self-care education |
| **2 (Moderate)** | **LOPS + PAD OR LOPS + Deformity** | Neuropathy **plus** absent pulses **OR** foot deformity (claw toes, Charcot, prominent met heads) | **3–6 months** | **Podiatry MDT**; footwear assessment; preventive care |
| **3 (High)** | **Prior ulcer/amputation OR Active Charcot OR Critical ischaemia** | History of DFU/amputation, active Charcot, or critical limb ischaemia (rest pain, tissue loss) | **1–3 months** | **Specialist MDT** (vascular, podiatry, orthotics, diabetology); urgent if active problem |

**LOPS** = Loss of Protective Sensation (10g monofilament negative at ≥1 site)
**PAD** = Peripheral Arterial Disease (absent DP/PT pulses OR ABI <0.9)

## 3. Scottish Risk Classification (Historical / Still Used UK)

| Risk | IWGDF Equivalent | Criteria | Review |
|------|------------------|----------|--------|
| **Low** | 0–1 | No risk factors (normal sensation, pulses) | Annual |
| **Moderate** | 2 | Neuropathy **OR** absent pulses **OR** deformity | 3–6 months |
| **High** | 3 | Previous ulcer/amputation **OR** active Charcot **OR** critical ischaemia | 1–3 months |
| **Active Disease** | N/A | **Active ulcer, spreading infection, critical ischaemia, active Charcot** | **Urgent MDT (24–48h)** |

**Note**: Scottish system merges IWGDF 0+1 into "Low"; IWGDF 1 is "Low" but with neuropathy.

---

## 4. Annual Foot Screen — Essential Components

| Component | Method | Abnormal Threshold |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| **Protective Sensation** | **10g Semmes-Weinstein monofilament** (apply to 6 sites: hallux, 1st/3rd/5th met heads, dorsum, heel) | **Negative at ≥1 site** = LOPS |
| **Vibration Perception** | **128Hz tuning fork** on hallux IP joint / medial malleolus | Reduced vs examiner / <10s |
| **Pulses** | Palpate **dorsalis pedis (DP)** and **posterior tibial (PT)** | **Absent** either = PAD |
| **Capillary Refill** | Dorsum of foot | >3 seconds |
| **Deformity** | Visual inspection | Claw toes, hallux valgus, Charcot, prominent met heads |
| **Skin/Nails** | Inspection | Callus, fissures, mycosis, thickened nails |
| **Footwear** | Assess fit, wear pattern | Ill-fitting, pressure marks |
| **History** | Ask | Previous ulcer/amputation, recent trauma |

**Minimum**: Monofilament + pulses + inspection (NICE 9 care processes).

---

## 5. Risk-Based Management Pathway

| Risk | Action |
|------|--------|
| **0** | Annual screen; education (daily inspection, footwear, moisturise); no routine podiatry |
| **1** | Annual screen; podiatry for nail/skin care if unable self-care; education |
| **2** | **3–6 monthly podiatry MDT**; footwear assessment (orthotics, insoles); callus debridement; vascular review if pulses absent |
| **3** | **1–3 monthly specialist MDT**; custom footwear/orthotics; vascular review; consider prophylactic surgery; emergency pathway if active |

**Active foot disease** (ulcer, infection, ischaemia, Charcot) → **Urgent MDT referral within 24–48 hours** (NICE NG19).

---

## 6. Key Preventive Measures by Risk

| Measure | Risk 0–1 | Risk 2 | Risk 3 |
|---------|----------|--------|--------|
| **Daily self-inspection** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ (carer) |
| **Moisturise (not between toes)** | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |
| **Appropriate footwear** | ✓ | **Prescribed/orthotic** | **Custom-moulded** |
| **Professional nail care** | If needed | ✓ | ✓ |
| **Callus debridement** | — | ✓ (podiatry) | ✓ (podiatry) |
| **Vascular assessment** | — | If pulses absent | **Mandatory** (ABI, TcPO2, duplex) |
| **Temperature monitoring** | — | Consider | **Infrared thermometry** (early Charcot) |

## 7. Exam Pearls (FCPS/MRCP)

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| **IWGDF Risk 0** | No LOPS, no PAD, no deformity → annual screen |
| **IWGDF Risk 1** | LOPS only → annual + podiatry for nails |
| **IWGDF Risk 2** | LOPS + (PAD OR deformity) → 3–6m podiatry MDT |
| **IWGDF Risk 3** | Prior ulcer/amputation, active Charcot, critical ischaemia → 1–3m specialist MDT |
| **Scottish Low** | = IWGDF 0+1 (no risk factors) |
| **Scottish Moderate** | = IWGDF 2 (one risk factor) |
| **Scottish High** | = IWGDF 3 |
| **Monofilament sites** | 6 sites: hallux, 1st/3rd/5th met heads, dorsum, heel |
| **LOPS definition** | Monofilament -ve at ≥1 site |
| **Active disease** | **Urgent MDT 24–48h** (ulcer, infection, ischaemia, active Charcot) |
