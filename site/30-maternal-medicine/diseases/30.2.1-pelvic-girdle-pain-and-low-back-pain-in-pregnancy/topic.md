# Pelvic girdle pain and low back pain in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Pelvic girdle pain (PGP) and low back pain (LBP) affect 50-70% of pregnant women. PGP arises from the sacroiliac joints and/or pubic symphysis; LBP from the lumbar spine. Most resolve postpartum; physiotherapy and analgesia are mainstays of treatment.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
PGP 20%; LBP 50-70%; 5-10% severe/disabling

### Risk factors
Prior PGP/LBP; multiparity; BMI; high physical workload; stress; pelvic trauma

### Symptoms PGP
Pain over SI joints or pubic symphysis; difficulty walking, climbing stairs, turning in bed; positive Patrick-Faber and active SLR tests

### Symptoms LBP
Lumbar pain; worse with activity; tender over paraspinal muscles

### Differential
Disc prolapse (radiculopathy); cauda equina (saddle anaesthesia, bladder/bowel — emergency); vertebral fracture (osteoporosis); infection; malignancy

### Management
Paracetamol; physiotherapy; pelvic support belt; acupuncture; gentle exercise; avoid heavy lifting

### Drugs
Paracetamol first-line; codeine in short courses; avoid NSAIDs (especially 3rd trimester)

### Labour
Consider intra-partum physiotherapy; avoid lithotomy; consider water birth; trial of labour if pain manageable

### Postpartum
Most resolve within 6 months; physiotherapy; manual therapy; persistent pain in 10%

### Prevention
Preconception core stability; avoid excessive weight gain
