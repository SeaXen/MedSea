# Antiphospholipid syndrome in pregnancy (obstetric)

---

## Definition

Antiphospholipid syndrome (APS) is a leading cause of recurrent pregnancy loss and is associated with late fetal loss, pre-eclampsia, IUGR, and thrombosis. Diagnosis requires persistent aPL antibodies and clinical events; treatment with aspirin ± LMWH improves outcomes.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Persistent aPL antibodies + clinical event (thrombosis or pregnancy morbidity)

### Antibodies
Lupus anticoagulant; anticardiolipin; anti-β2 glycoprotein I — confirm 12 weeks apart

### Obstetric morbidity criteria
≥3 consecutive <10-week losses; ≥1 ≥10-week loss; preterm <34 weeks with PE/placental insufficiency

### Pathophysiology
Placental thrombosis + inflammation; impaired trophoblast invasion; complement activation

### Maternal risks
VTE; pre-eclampsia; HELLP; placental abruption; stroke

### Fetal risks
Miscarriage (early and late); IUGR; stillbirth; preterm birth

### Treatment
Low-dose aspirin 75-150 mg + prophylactic LMWH throughout pregnancy

### High-risk (prior thrombosis/lupus)
Therapeutic LMWH dose; consider warfarin postpartum for life

### Monitoring
Uterine artery Doppler 20-24 weeks; growth scans; pre-eclampsia surveillance

### Postpartum
Continue LMWH 6 weeks; lifelong if thrombosis; preconception counselling for future
