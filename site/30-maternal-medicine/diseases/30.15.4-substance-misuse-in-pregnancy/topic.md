# Substance misuse in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Substance misuse in pregnancy — alcohol, tobacco, opioids, stimulants, cannabis, and others — is associated with significant maternal, fetal, and neonatal harm. Identification (universal screening), brief intervention, harm reduction, and specialist multidisciplinary support improve outcomes for mother and baby.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
Alcohol 10-15%; smoking 10-15% (declining); cannabis 3-5%; opioids 0.5-1%; stimulants variable

### Universal screening
Brief validated tools (T-ACE, AUDIT-C for alcohol; brief tobacco intervention); non-judgmental

### Alcohol effects
Fetal alcohol spectrum disorder (FASD); IUGR; preterm; facial dysmorphism; CNS abnormalities; no safe threshold

### Smoking effects
Placental abruption (2x); placenta praevia; IUGR; preterm; stillbirth; SIDS (2-3x); childhood respiratory disease; OME

### Opioid effects
Neonatal abstinence syndrome (NAS); IUGR; preterm; stillbirth; maternal overdose; HIV/HCV transmission (IV use)

### Stimulants (cocaine, amphetamines)
Placental abruption; preterm; IUGR; congenital anomalies; neonatal withdrawal; SIDS

### Cannabis
IUGR; preterm; childhood cognitive/behavioural issues; emerging data on neurodevelopment

### Management
Brief intervention; motivational interviewing; referral to addiction services; harm reduction; supervised OST (methadone or buprenorphine) for opioids; nicotine replacement safe

### Labour and delivery
Avoid pethidine in opioid users (precipitates withdrawal); pain control often higher; monitor for withdrawal; NAS monitoring of neonate

### Postpartum
Safeguarding; social services; contraception; LARC; relapse prevention; peer support
