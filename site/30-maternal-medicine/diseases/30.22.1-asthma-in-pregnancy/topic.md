# Asthma in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Asthma is the most common chronic respiratory disease in pregnancy, affecting 4-8% of women. Control may improve, worsen, or stay the same; poorly controlled asthma poses greater risk to mother and fetus than asthma medications.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
4-8% of pregnancies; most common chronic respiratory condition

### Course
~30% improve; ~30% worsen; ~40% unchanged — variable, monitor closely

### Maternal risks (uncontrolled)
Pre-eclampsia; GDM; preterm birth; caesarean; pneumonia

### Fetal risks (uncontrolled)
IUGR; low birth weight; stillbirth; neonatal hypoxia

### Safe medications
Inhaled SABA (salbutamol); ICS (budesonide safe); LABA; theophylline; oral steroids if needed

### Avoid
Uncontrolled symptoms > medications; leukotriene antagonists data limited but not contraindicated

### Acute severe asthma
Same management as non-pregnant — oxygen, SABA, systemic steroids, magnesium

### Monitoring
Peak expiratory flow; symptom diary; consider FeNO; review every 4 weeks

### Labour and delivery
Continue inhaled therapy; SABA if needed; epidural analgesia preferred

### Postpartum
Continue medications; breastfeeding compatible; monitor infant for steroid exposure if high-dose oral
