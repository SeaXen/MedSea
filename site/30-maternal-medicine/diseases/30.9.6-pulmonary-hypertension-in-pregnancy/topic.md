# Pulmonary hypertension in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Pulmonary hypertension (PH) in pregnancy is a high-risk condition associated with up to 30-50% maternal mortality. Pregnancy is generally contraindicated in severe PH (WHO class IV). Multidisciplinary care in a specialist PH centre is essential if pregnancy continues.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Mean pulmonary artery pressure >20 mmHg at rest (revised); previously ≥25 mmHg

### Classification
Group 1 pulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH); Group 2 (left heart); Group 3 (lung); Group 4 (CTEPH); Group 5 (misc)

### Maternal mortality
30-50% without therapy; reduces to ~10-30% with modern PH care

### Pathophysiology
Fixed PVR + pregnancy-related ↑CO + ↓SVR → RV failure; peripartum particularly dangerous

### Symptoms
Progressive dyspnoea, fatigue, syncope, chest pain, RV failure signs

### Investigations
Echo (PASP, RV function); RHC for definitive diagnosis; V/Q scan if CTEPH suspected

### Preconception
Counsel about mortality; offer termination; switch endothelin receptor antagonists (teratogenic)

### Therapy
Prostacyclins (epoprostenol, iloprost); sildenafil; LMWH; planned delivery under MDT

### Labour and delivery
Avoid hypotension; assisted second stage; avoid general anaesthesia; central line; pulmonary vasodilators continued

### Postpartum
24-48 h ITU monitoring; PH team input; VTE prophylaxis; avoid pregnancy for 1 year minimum
