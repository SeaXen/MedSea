# Sickle cell disease in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Sickle cell disease in pregnancy is associated with increased maternal and fetal complications — painful crises, infections, pre-eclampsia, IUGR, and preterm birth. Multidisciplinary care in a specialist centre with prophylactic transfusion, exchange transfusion for crises, and intensive monitoring is essential.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
Highest in sub-Saharan Africa, Caribbean, Middle East; 1 in 2,000-10,000 pregnancies

### Maternal risks
Painful crises; infection (UTI, pneumonia, sepsis); pre-eclampsia; anaemia; acute chest syndrome; VTE; death

### Fetal risks
Miscarriage; IUGR; preterm birth; stillbirth (HbF/APCR helps)

### Preconception
Genetic counselling; partner screening; medication review; hydroxyurea stop 3 months before

### Antenatal care
MDT (haematologist, obstetrician); folic acid 5 mg; penicillin V prophylaxis; transfusion programme debated

### Transfusion
Prophylactic top-up transfusion if Hb <70-80 or symptoms; exchange transfusion for severe complications

### Monitoring
Monthly FBC, U&E, LFT; growth scans; uterine artery Doppler; MCA Doppler

### Acute crisis
Analgesia (parenteral opioids); oxygen; hydration; antibiotics; transfusion; exclude other causes

### Labour and delivery
Vaginal preferred; avoid dehydration, hypoxia, cold; continuous CTG

### Postpartum
VTE prophylaxis; close monitoring for crises; contraception counselling; avoid oestrogen
