# Postpartum haemorrhage (PPH)

---

## Definition

Postpartum haemorrhage is blood loss ≥500 mL within 24 hours of delivery (or any loss causing haemodynamic compromise). It is the leading cause of maternal mortality worldwide. The 4 Ts (tone, trauma, tissue, thrombin) guide cause and management.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Primary PPH: ≥500 mL within 24 h; major PPH ≥1000 mL; secondary PPH: 24 h-12 weeks

### Incidence
5-10% of deliveries; major PPH 1-3%

### Causes — 4 Ts
Tone (atonic uterus, 70%); Trauma (15%); Tissue (retained placenta, 10%); Thrombin (coagulopathy, 5%)

### Risk factors
Previous PPH; multiple pregnancy; macrosomia; prolonged labour; augmentation; placenta praevia; abruption; coagulopathy

### Prevention (active management 3rd stage)
Oxytocin 10 IU IM/IV; controlled cord traction; uterine massage

### Initial management
ABC; call for help; 2 large-bore IVs; fluids; bloods incl. FBC, coagulation, crossmatch

### Medical treatment
Oxytocin infusion; ergometrine (avoid in HTN); carboprost (avoid in asthma); misoprostol; tranexamic acid 1 g IV within 3 h

### Surgical treatment
Bimanual compression; intrauterine balloon (Bakri); B-Lynch suture; arterial ligation; hysterectomy

### Resuscitation
Activate massive transfusion protocol (1:1:1 ratio PRBC:FFP:platelets); cell salvage; warming

### Secondary PPH
Endometritis; retained products; treat with antibiotics ± evacuation

- **Mnemonic for Postpartum haemorrhage (PPH)** — see key points in Core Clinical Content

- **'Mnemonic: H-E-L-L-P'** — Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets
- **'CHAP' trial target** — Chronic HTN treated to <140/90 mmHg
- **'M-M-R-V' safe vaccines in pregnancy** — Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR, avoid), inactivated influenza, COVID-19, Whooping cough (dTpa)
