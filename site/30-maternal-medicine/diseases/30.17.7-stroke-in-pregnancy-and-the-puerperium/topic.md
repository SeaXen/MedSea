# Stroke in pregnancy and the puerperium

---

## Definition

Stroke in pregnancy and the puerperium — ischaemic and haemorrhagic — is a leading cause of maternal mortality. Risk is highest in the postpartum period. Causes include pre-eclampsia, hypertension, paradoxical emboli, arterial dissection, and SAH from aneurysm rupture.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
30 per 100,000 pregnancies; 3x non-pregnant

### Timing
Highest postpartum; 50% postpartum; 30% antenatal; 20% intrapartum

### Ischaemic stroke risk factors
Pre-eclampsia/HELLP; hypertension; migraine; cardiac disease; APS; prothrombotic states; paradoxical emboli

### Haemorrhagic stroke
Subarachnoid (aneurysm/AVM rupture); intracerebral (hypertensive, eclampsia); cortical vein thrombosis

### Presentation
Sudden focal deficit; seizures; severe headache; altered consciousness; postpartum thunderclap headache

### Investigation
Urgent CT/MRI; CT/MR venogram for CVST; CT/MR angiography; thrombophilia screen postpartum

### Cerebral venous sinus thrombosis
Headache, seizures, focal deficits; treat with LMWH even if haemorrhagic conversion

### Management ischaemic
Thrombolysis considered (limited evidence in pregnancy); MDT decision; aspirin 75-300 mg after haemorrhage excluded

### SAH
Urgent neurosurgical input; securing aneurysm (coiling preferred)

### Prevention
BP control in PE; MDT; preconception counselling; avoid dehydration; consider LMWH in APS
