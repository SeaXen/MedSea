# Intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy (ICP)

---

## Definition

Intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy is a pruritic liver disorder of pregnancy, typically presenting in the 2nd or 3rd trimester with intense itching (especially palms and soles), elevated bile acids, and abnormal LFTs. It is associated with increased risk of stillbirth, preterm birth, and fetal distress.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Pruritus in pregnancy + abnormal LFT and/or bile acids >10 μmol/L

### Prevalence
0.5-1.5% in Europe; up to 5-15% in South American (Araucanian) and South Asian populations

### Pathophysiology
Impaired hepatic bile acid excretion; genetic (ABCB4/MDR3) and hormonal factors

### Symptoms
Intense pruritus (palms, soles) without rash; worse at night; steatorrhoea; dark urine

### Diagnosis
Bile acids >10 μmol/L (gold standard); raised ALT/AST; ALP poorly specific; exclude viral hepatitis, PBC

### Fetal risks
Stillbirth (esp. if bile acids >40); preterm birth; meconium staining; fetal distress; postpartum haemorrhage

### Maternal management
Ursodeoxycholic acid 10-15 mg/kg/day — first-line; reduces pruritus and may reduce fetal risk

### Monitoring
Weekly bile acids and LFT; weekly CTG from 34 weeks; growth scans; patient education on stillbirth

### Delivery
Induce at 36-37 weeks (or earlier if severe; bile acids >40 deliver at 36)

### Postpartum
Resolves; LFT normalises within 6-12 weeks; recurrence 60-70%; long-term risk of gallstones, biliary cirrhosis
