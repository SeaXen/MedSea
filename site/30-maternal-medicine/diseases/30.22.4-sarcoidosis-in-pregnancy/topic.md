# Sarcoidosis in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Sarcoidosis is a multisystem granulomatous disease that commonly affects young women. Pregnancy often improves symptoms (immunomodulation), but advanced disease with pulmonary fibrosis, cardiac, or neurological involvement carries significant risk. Management is usually supportive; most medications are safe.

## Core Clinical Content

### Epidemiology
Commoner in women; peak incidence 20-40 years; often stable in pregnancy

### Effect of pregnancy
Symptoms often improve (rising cortisol, Th2 shift); flares postpartum

### Maternal risks
Worsening if advanced fibrosis; cor pulmonale; cardiac sarcoid (arrhythmia, heart block); neurological (cranial nerve palsies, seizures); hypercalcaemia; uveitis

### Fetal risks
Minimal if mild disease; preterm and LBW if severe; neonatal hypercalcaemia if mother hypercalcaemic

### Investigations
CXR (with shielding); spirometry; CT chest if needed; serum ACE (less reliable in pregnancy); serum calcium; LFTs; ECG; echo if cardiac

### Biopsy
Often unnecessary if clear clinical picture; defer to postpartum; or specific tissue if needed (lymph node)

### Management
Observation if asymptomatic; corticosteroids for active disease; immunosuppressants if severe; hydroxychloroquine for skin/eye

### Drugs safe
Prednisolone; azathioprine; hydroxychloroquine; infliximab (limited data)

### Avoid
Methotrexate (teratogenic); cyclophosphamide; avoid vitamin D supplementation in those with hypercalcaemia

### Postpartum
Continue treatment; monitor for flare; contraception (avoid oestrogen if hypercoagulable state)
