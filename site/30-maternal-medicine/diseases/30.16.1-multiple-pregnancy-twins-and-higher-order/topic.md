# Multiple pregnancy (twins and higher-order)

---

## Definition

Multiple pregnancy — most commonly dichorionic diamniotic (DCDA) twins but also monochorionic diamniotic (MCDA), monochorionic monoamniotic (MCMA), and higher-order — carries significantly higher maternal and fetal risks. Specialist care is essential.

## Core Clinical Content

### Prevalence
1 in 80 pregnancies; rising with assisted conception and maternal age

### Zygosity
DCDA — 2 placentas, 2 sacs; MCDA — 1 placenta, 2 sacs; MCMA — 1 placenta, 1 sac (rare, high risk)

### Diagnosis
First-trimester USS — chorionicity determined by lambda/twin-peak sign (DCDA) vs T-sign (MCMA/MCDA)

### Maternal risks
Hyperemesis; anaemia; pre-eclampsia (2-3x); GDM; postpartum haemorrhage; VTE

### Fetal risks (DCDA)
Preterm 50%; IUGR; discordant growth; twin-twin transfusion; stillbirth

### Fetal risks (MCMA/MCDA)
Twin-twin transfusion syndrome (TTTS) 10-15%; cord entanglement; selective growth restriction; TRAP sequence

### Antenatal care
DCDA — every 4 weeks to 36; MCDA — every 2 weeks from 16; USS growth and Doppler; screen for TTTS

### TTTS management
Laser ablation of placental anastomoses; amnioreduction; selective feticide in severe cases

### Timing of delivery
DCDA 37 weeks; MCDA 36-37; MCMA 32-34 (inpatient monitoring from 26-28 weeks)

### Mode of delivery
DCDA — vaginal if cephalic/cephalic; MCMA — caesarean; MCDA depends on presentation

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Multiple pregnancy (twins and higher-order)** — see key points in Core Clinical Content
- **'PRE-ECLAMPSIA' risk factors** — Previous PE, Renal disease, Extremes of age, Chronic HTN, Ethnicity, Antiphospholipid, nulliparity, Multiple, Obesity, Smoking
- **'Mnemonic: H-E-L-L-P'** — Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets
- **'CHAP' trial target** — Chronic HTN treated to <140/90 mmHg
- **'M-M-R-V' safe vaccines in pregnancy** — Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR, avoid), inactivated influenza, COVID-19, Whooping cough (dTpa)
