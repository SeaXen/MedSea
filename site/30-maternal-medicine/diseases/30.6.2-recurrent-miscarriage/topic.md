# Recurrent miscarriage

---

## Definition

Recurrent miscarriage is defined as ≥3 consecutive pregnancy losses before 24 weeks' gestation (or ≥2 in some guidelines). It affects 1-2% of couples and warrants investigation for underlying causes. Identifiable causes include antiphospholipid syndrome, uterine anomalies, parental chromosomal abnormalities, and endocrine disorders.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
≥3 consecutive miscarriages <24 weeks (RCOG); some use ≥2

### Incidence
1-2% of couples; 5% with ≥2 losses

### Causes
Antiphospholipid syndrome (15%); uterine anomalies (10%); parental karyotype (3-5%); endocrine (PCOS, thyroid, diabetes); thrombophilia; infection; idiopathic (50%)

### Investigations
Antiphospholipid antibodies (LA, aCL, anti-β2GP1); parental karyotyping; pelvic USS/MRI; thrombophilia screen; TFT; OGTT; uterine cavity (HSG/SIS)

### APS treatment
Low-dose aspirin + LMWH from positive pregnancy test; improves live birth rate

### Uterine anomalies
Septum resection (controversial); unicornuate/bicornuate — obstetric surveillance

### Parental chromosomal
Robertsonian translocations; PGD/IVF; genetic counselling

### Prognosis
70-80% live birth rate with supportive care; better if no identifiable cause

### Supportive care
Tender loving care (TLC); early pregnancy unit access; psychological support

### Future pregnancy
Folic acid 5 mg; aspirin if indicated; close monitoring; serial scans
