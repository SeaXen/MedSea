# Bell's palsy and cranial neuropathies in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Bell's palsy (idiopathic facial nerve palsy) is more common in pregnancy (3x) and the puerperium. It presents with sudden unilateral lower motor neuron facial weakness. Most cases resolve spontaneously; treatment with corticosteroids improves outcomes and is safe in pregnancy.

## Core Clinical Content

### Epidemiology
1 in 2000-6000 pregnancies; 3x non-pregnant; peak in 3rd trimester and 1st postpartum week

### Aetiology
Oedema of facial nerve in fallopian canal; viral (HSV, HZV) association; hypertension; pre-eclampsia risk factor

### Presentation
Sudden unilateral facial weakness (forehead involved); hyperacusis; loss of taste anterior 2/3 tongue; decreased lacrimation; post-auricular pain

### Differential
Stroke (forehead spared); Ramsay Hunt (VZV); Lyme; otitis media; parotid tumour; Guillain-Barré

### Investigations
Clinical diagnosis; consider neuroimaging if atypical (forehead spared, progressive, recurrent); audiometry if indicated

### Treatment
Prednisolone 50 mg × 10 days within 72 h of onset; eye protection (artificial tears, tape at night); antivirals (valaciclovir) if severe

### Prognosis
85% complete recovery within 3 months; 15% have residual weakness; synkinesis; recurrence 5-10% subsequent pregnancies

### Eye care
Critical to prevent exposure keratopathy; lubricate; taping at night; ophthalmology review if corneal exposure

### Other cranial neuropathies
Trigeminal neuralgia (carbamazepine caution); optic neuritis (MS-related); vestibular schwannoma (monitor)

### Postpartum
Monitor infant if on prednisolone; breastfeeding compatible; review at 3 months
