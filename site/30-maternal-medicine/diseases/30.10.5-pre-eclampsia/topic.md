# Pre-eclampsia

---

## Definition

Pre-eclampsia is a multi-system disorder of pregnancy characterised by new-onset hypertension after 20 weeks with proteinuria and/or end-organ dysfunction (renal, hepatic, haematological, neurological, pulmonary, or fetal). It is a leading cause of maternal and fetal morbidity and mortality worldwide.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
BP ≥140/90 + proteinuria (≥300 mg/24h or PCR ≥30) OR end-organ involvement after 20 weeks

### Severe features
BP ≥160/110; proteinuria >5 g/24h; PLT <100; ↑LFT 2x; creatinine >110; pulmonary oedema; neurological symptoms

### Epidemiology
Affects 2-8% of pregnancies; leading cause of maternal death in LMIC

### Pathophysiology
Abnormal placentation → defective spiral artery remodelling → endothelial dysfunction, anti-angiogenic state (↑sFlt-1, ↓PlGF)

### Risk factors
Prior PE (strongest); chronic HTN; renal disease; APS; SLE; diabetes; obesity; multiple pregnancy; advanced maternal age; nulliparity

### Prevention
Aspirin 75-150 mg nocte from 12 weeks for high-risk; calcium in low-intake populations

### Diagnosis
New HTN after 20 weeks + proteinuria OR severe features; sFlt-1/PlGF ratio aids rule-out

### Management
Definitive = delivery; magnesium sulphate for severe features; antihypertensives (labetalol IV, hydralazine, nifedipine)

### HELLP syndrome
Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets — variant of severe PE

### Eclampsia
Seizures in PE/gestational HTN — give MgSO4; supportive care

## Confusions & Mnemonics

- **Mnemonic for Pre-eclampsia** — see key points in Core Clinical Content
- **'PRE-ECLAMPSIA' risk factors** — Previous PE, Renal disease, Extremes of age, Chronic HTN, Ethnicity, Antiphospholipid, nulliparity, Multiple, Obesity, Smoking
- **'Mnemonic: H-E-L-L-P'** — Haemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets
- **'CHAP' trial target** — Chronic HTN treated to <140/90 mmHg
- **'M-M-R-V' safe vaccines in pregnancy** — Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR, avoid), inactivated influenza, COVID-19, Whooping cough (dTpa)
