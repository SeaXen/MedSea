# Tuberculosis in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Tuberculosis in pregnancy, both pulmonary and extrapulmonary, requires prompt diagnosis and treatment. Active TB poses greater risk to the pregnancy than the standard anti-TB regimen, which is largely safe.

## Core Clinical Content

### Epidemiology
Highest in low/middle-income countries; latent TB common

### Maternal risks
Active TB: ↑preterm birth, low birth weight, pre-eclampsia, maternal mortality

### Diagnosis
Sputum AFB + culture + NAAT; CXR (with shielding); TST/IGRA; if extrapulmonary, biopsy/aspiration

### Treatment of active TB
Standard RIF + INH + PZA + EMB for 2 months, then RIF + INH for 7 months

### Drug safety
Rifampicin, isoniazid, ethambutol — all safe; pyrazinamide safe

### Avoid
Streptomycin (ototoxic to fetus); second-line drugs limited data

### Latent TB
Treat postpartum usually; if high-risk recent exposure, treat in pregnancy with INH + pyridoxine 9 months

### BCG
Contraindicated in pregnancy; give to neonate at birth if mother has active TB

### Multidrug-resistant TB
Individualised; expert input; bedaquiline, linezolid — limited safety data

### Postpartum and breastfeeding
Continue treatment; breastfeeding safe with non-infectious TB; mother-infant separation only if active pulmonary
