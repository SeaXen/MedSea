# Chronic hypertension in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Chronic hypertension in pregnancy is defined as blood pressure ≥140/90 mmHg confirmed on two occasions before 20 weeks' gestation, or pre-existing antihypertensive use, or hypertension persisting beyond 12 weeks postpartum. It affects 1-5% of pregnancies and increases maternal and fetal risk.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
BP ≥140/90 mmHg before 20 weeks OR persisting >12 weeks postpartum

### Prevalence
1-5% of pregnancies; rising with maternal age and obesity

### Causes
Essential (90%); secondary — renal, endocrine (Conn, phaeo), coarctation

### Risks to mother
Superimposed pre-eclampsia (25%); placental abruption; stroke; CKD progression

### Risks to fetus
IUGR (2x); preterm birth; stillbirth; placental dysfunction

### Preconception
Switch ACEI/ARB to labetalol/nifedipine/methyldopa; optimise BP <130/80

### BP target
Treat if >140/90 (CHAP trial) or >150/100; target diastolic 80-90 mmHg

### First-line drugs
Labetalol, nifedipine MR, methyldopa — all pregnancy-safe

### Avoid
ACE inhibitors (2nd/3rd trimester — renal dysgenesis, oligohydramnios); ARBs; thiazides

### Aspirin 75-150 mg
From 12 weeks for pre-eclampsia prevention if ≥1 high-risk factor
