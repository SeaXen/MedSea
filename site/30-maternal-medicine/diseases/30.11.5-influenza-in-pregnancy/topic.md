# Influenza in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Influenza in pregnancy causes disproportionately severe disease compared to non-pregnant women, with increased risk of hospitalisation, ICU admission, and death, especially in the 2nd and 3rd trimesters. Inactivated influenza vaccination in any trimester is safe and effective. Antiviral treatment (oseltamivir) is recommended within 48 hours of symptoms.

## Core Clinical Content

### Epidemiology
Pregnancy increases severity (3rd trimester peak); influenza in pregnancy causes excess mortality historically (1918, 2009 H1N1)

### Risk factors
Asthma; obesity; diabetes; immunocompromised; cardiac disease; advanced gestation

### Complications maternal
Pneumonia; ARDS; hospitalisation; ICU; death

### Complications fetal
Preterm birth; stillbirth; IUGR; congenital infection (rare); neonatal influenza

### Presentation
Fever, myalgia, headache, cough, sore throat, fatigue; abrupt onset

### Diagnosis
Clinical; rapid antigen testing; PCR (respiratory); CXR if pneumonia suspected

### Treatment
Oseltamivir 75 mg BD 5 days within 48 h of symptoms (any trimester); benefits likely if later; supportive care

### Chemoprophylaxis
Oseltamivir for close contacts in high-risk; post-exposure; vaccination reduces risk

### Vaccination
Inactivated influenza vaccine (any trimester; safe); recommended in pregnancy; protects mother and neonate

### Prevention
Annual vaccination; hand hygiene; avoid sick contacts; mask in outbreaks
