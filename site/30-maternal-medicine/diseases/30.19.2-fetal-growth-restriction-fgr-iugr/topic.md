# Fetal growth restriction (FGR-IUGR)

---

## Definition

Fetal growth restriction (FGR), also called intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR), describes a fetus that has not reached its genetically predetermined growth potential. It is associated with increased risk of stillbirth, neonatal morbidity, and long-term metabolic and cardiovascular disease. Antenatal detection with timely delivery reduces perinatal mortality.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Estimated fetal weight (EFW) <10th centile (population) or abdominal circumference (AC) <10th centile

### Classification
Symmetric (20%): early onset, genetic/infectious; asymmetric (80%): late onset, uteroplacental insufficiency

### Causes
Fetal: chromosomal, structural anomalies, infection (TORCH); Maternal: HTN, autoimmune, smoking, malnutrition; Placental: pre-eclampsia, abruption, mosaicism

### Risk factors
Prior FGR (strongest); pre-eclampsia; chronic HTN; APS; smoking; multiple pregnancy; maternal disease

### Screening
First-trimester combined screen (PAPP-A low); uterine artery Doppler 20-24 weeks; serial fundal height

### Diagnosis
USS: AC <10th centile; estimated fetal weight; umbilical artery Doppler; middle cerebral artery Doppler; cerebroplacental ratio

### Monitoring
Serial growth scans (2-3 weeks); umbilical artery Doppler; MCA Doppler; cardiotocography; biophysical profile; liquor volume

### Severe FGR
Abnormal ductus venosus; absent/reversed EDF in umbilical artery; delivery recommended based on gestation and findings

### Timing of delivery
Depends on severity and Doppler findings; <30 weeks with abnormal DV may need delivery

### Long-term
Neurodevelopmental delay; cerebral palsy; metabolic syndrome; HTN; cardiovascular disease (Barker hypothesis)
