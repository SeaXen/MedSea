# Rheumatoid arthritis in pregnancy

---

## Definition

Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) in pregnancy often improves due to immunomodulation (Th2 shift), but flares occur postpartum in 50-70%. Active disease at conception increases risk of preterm birth and low birth weight. Most medications (sulfasalazine, hydroxychloroquine, low-dose steroids) are safe; methotrexate and leflunomide are teratogenic.

## Core Clinical Content

### Effect of pregnancy on RA
50-75% improve (esp. 1st and 2nd trimesters); 50% flare postpartum; hormonal and immunomodulatory factors

### Effect of RA on pregnancy
Active disease → preterm, LBW, pre-eclampsia; quiescent → generally good outcomes

### Preconception
Achieve remission 3-6 months; switch teratogens (MTX stop 3 months, leflunomide cholestyramine washout); review biologics

### Safe drugs
Sulfasalazine (with folate); hydroxychloroquine (continue); azathioprine; low-dose prednisolone; certolizumab (limited placental transfer — safe); other anti-TNF generally safe until 3rd trimester

### Avoid
Methotrexate (teratogenic); leflunomide (teratogenic); cyclophosphamide; baricitinib; JAK inhibitors (limited data)

### Biologics
Anti-TNF (adalimumab, infliximab, etanercept) — generally safe; discontinue in 3rd trimester if possible to avoid neonatal immunosuppression; live vaccines contraindicated in exposed infants <6 months

### Monitoring
Disease activity (DAS28); FBC; U&E; LFT; trough levels if on biologic; growth scans; pre-eclampsia surveillance

### Postpartum
Flare common; resume medications; breastfeeding compatible with sulfasalazine, HCQ, azathioprine, prednisolone; avoid MTX; contraception

### Anaesthesia
Difficult intubation if cricoarytenoid involvement; careful airway assessment if C-section

### Long-term
Joint damage during pregnancy limited; functional outcomes good; preconception counselling important
