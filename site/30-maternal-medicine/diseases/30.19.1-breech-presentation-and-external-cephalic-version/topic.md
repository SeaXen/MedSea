# Breech presentation and external cephalic version

---

## Definition

Breech presentation is when the fetus presents with buttocks or feet first, occurring in 3-4% of term pregnancies. It is associated with increased perinatal morbidity. External cephalic version (ECV) can turn the breech to cephalic presentation antenatally, reducing the need for caesarean section.

## Core Clinical Content

### Definition
Fetal buttocks or feet presenting at pelvic inlet; frank, complete, footling

### Incidence
20% at 28 weeks; 3-4% at term

### Risk factors
Prematurity; multiparity; multiple pregnancy; polyhydramnios; placenta praevia; uterine anomalies; fetal anomalies (anencephaly, hydrocephalus); contracted pelvis; previous breech

### Diagnosis
Palpation (Leopold); USS confirms; 36-week check for all

### External cephalic version (ECV)
Attempted at 36-37 weeks; success 50-60%; tocolysis (terbutaline); Rh status; USS-guided; complications: cord prolapse, abruption, fetal distress (0.5%)

### Contraindications ECV
APH, placenta praevia, abnormal CTG, multiple pregnancy (except first twin), ruptured membranes, previous C-section (relative), uterine anomaly

### Failed ECV
Offer trial of vaginal breech or planned C-section; mode based on clinician skill + patient preference

### Vaginal breech delivery
Careful selection (Frank breech, flexed head, adequate pelvis, no footling); experienced clinician; continuous CTG; second stage assistance

### Caesarean breech
Frank breech in primigravida often; footling/compete; failed ECV; maternal preference; previous C-section

### Prognosis
Vaginal breech in selected cases similar outcomes to cephalic; morbidity higher in unselected
