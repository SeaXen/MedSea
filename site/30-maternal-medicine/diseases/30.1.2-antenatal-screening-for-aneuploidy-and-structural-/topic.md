# Antenatal screening for aneuploidy and structural anomalies

---

## Definition

Antenatal screening aims to identify pregnancies at risk of fetal aneuploidy (trisomy 21, 18, 13) and structural anomalies. Options include first-trimester combined test, second-trimester serum screening, cell-free DNA testing, and detailed ultrasound.

## Core Clinical Content

### First-trimester combined test
NT + β-hCG + PAPP-A at 11-13+6 weeks; detection ~85% trisomy 21 at 5% false positive

### Cell-free DNA (NIPT/NIPS)
Detects fetal DNA in maternal plasma; detection >99% trisomy 21; <0.1% false positive; not diagnostic

### Quadruple test
Second-trimester if booking late; AFP, hCG, uE3, inhibin A; 80% detection

### Integrated test
Combined first + second trimester markers; high detection ~95%

### Soft markers on USS
Nuchal fold, echogenic bowel, echogenic focus, mild pyelectasis, single umbilical artery

### Anomaly scan timing
18-20+6 weeks; structural survey; cardiac outflow tracts; CNS; spine; limbs; face

### Diagnostic testing
CVS (11-14 weeks) or amniocentesis (15+ weeks) for karyotype/microarray; 0.5-1% miscarriage risk

### Non-invasive options
NIPT for sex chromosome and microdeletion screening — patient-led decision

### Counselling
Pre-test: limitations, options; post-test: confirmatory testing, decision-making, choices

### Pre-eclampsia screening
First-trimester combined model (maternal factors + MAP + PAPP-A + PlGF) — aspirin prophylaxis
