# Postpartum psychosis

---

## Definition

Postpartum (puerperal) psychosis is a rare psychiatric emergency occurring within 2-4 weeks of delivery, affecting 0.1-0.2% of women. It presents with mania, delusions, hallucinations, and disorganised behaviour, and carries a high risk of suicide and infanticide. Rapid recognition, hospitalisation, and treatment are essential.

## Core Clinical Content

### Incidence
1-2 per 1000 deliveries; psychiatric emergency

### Onset
Within 2-4 weeks postpartum (most within 2 weeks); rapid onset over days

### Risk factors
Prior postpartum psychosis (25-50% recurrence); bipolar disorder; prior psychosis; family history of bipolar/psychosis; first pregnancy; sleep deprivation

### Presentation
Mania (elated, irritable, agitated); delusions (often related to baby — intruder, contamination, harm); hallucinations; disorganised behaviour; insomnia; poor insight

### Differential
Severe postnatal depression with psychotic features (later onset, no elation); delirium (eclampsia, sepsis, drug-induced); bipolar relapse

### Dangers
Suicide (5%); infanticide (4%); neglect of baby

### Treatment
Urgent hospitalisation (often Mother and Baby Unit if available); antipsychotics (olanzapine, haloperidol); lithium (monitor levels); benzodiazepines; ECT if severe

### Lithium
Effective but teratogenic (cardiac — Ebstein anomaly) and toxic in neonate; monitor levels; breastfeeding caution; restart in 2nd trimester if needed

### Prognosis
Majority recover with treatment; 50% relapse in subsequent postpartum period; risk of bipolar diagnosis lifelong

### Prevention (recurrence)
Early identification; lithium prophylaxis from delivery; supervised treatment; preconception planning
