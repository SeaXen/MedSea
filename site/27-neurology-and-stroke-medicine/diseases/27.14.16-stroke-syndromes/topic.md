# Stroke Syndromes

> **High-Yield Definition:** Stroke syndromes: pattern of neurological deficit based on vascular territory. Anterior circulation (carotid - MCA, ACA), posterior circulation (vertebrobasilar - PCA, brainstem, cerebellum). Lacunar syndromes (small vessel).

## Anterior Circulation (Carotid) Syndromes

### Middle Cerebral Artery (MCA)
- **Largest territory**, most common stroke
- **Superior division**: face, arm > leg weakness, sensory loss, Broca's aphasia (dominant), apraxia, gaze deviation (toward lesion)
- **Inferior division**: Wernicke's aphasia (dominant), visual field (homonymous hemianopia), neglect (non-dominant)
- **Total MCA**: face/arm/leg weakness (arm > leg), sensory loss, homonymous hemianopia, aphasia (dominant), gaze deviation, hemineglect
- Lenticulostriate: pure motor hemiparesis (lacunar)

### Anterior Cerebral Artery (ACA)
- Contralateral leg > arm weakness, sensory loss
- Frontal release signs (grasp, snout, palmomental)
- Abulia, frontal lobe syndrome
- Alien hand (callosal)
- Left ACA: transcortical motor aphasia

### Internal Carotid Artery (ICA)
- Combined MCA + ACA features
- Amaurosis fugax (transient monocular blindness)
- Waxing/waning (stuttering onset)

## Posterior Circulation (Vertebrobasilar) Syndromes

### Posterior Cerebral Artery (PCA)
- Homonymous hemianopia (often isolated)
- Cortical blindness (bilateral PCA)
- Anton syndrome (cortical blindness with denial)
- Visual agnosia, prosopagnosia (non-dominant)
- Alexia without agraphia (dominant, splenium)
- Memory deficit (bilateral, thalamic)
- Midbrain syndromes (Weber, Claude, Benedikt)

### Cerebellar Syndromes
- Ipsilateral ataxia, dysmetria, intention tremor
- Dysarthria, dysphagia
- Nystagmus (gaze-evoked)
- **Cerebellar stroke = neurosurgical emergency** (oedema, brainstem compression, hydrocephalus)

### Lateral Medullary (Wallenberg) Syndrome
- **PICA territory**
- Ipsilateral: Horner's, facial sensory loss, ataxia, dysphagia, hoarseness, nystagmus
- Contralateral: pain/temperature loss (body)
- **Crossed signs** (ipsilateral face + contralateral body)

### Medial Medullary Syndrome
- ASA territory
- Contralateral: hemiparesis (sparing face), medial lemniscus (vibration, proprioception)
- Ipsilateral: tongue deviation (CN XII)

### Lateral Pontine Syndrome (AICA)
- Similar to lateral medullary but with:
- Facial palsy (CN VII - motor nucleus)
- Hearing loss (CN VIII - cochlear nucleus)
- Ipsilateral Horner's

### Medial Pontine Syndrome (Basilar)
- Contralateral: hemiparesis
- Ipsilateral: lateral gaze palsy (CN VI nucleus)

### Weber Syndrome (Midbrain)
- Ipsilateral: CN III palsy
- Contralateral: hemiparesis

### Locked-in Syndrome (Ventral Pontine)
- Quadriplegia, anarthria
- Preserved consciousness
- **Preserved vertical eye movements and blinking** (midbrain spared)
- Communication only by vertical eye movements

### Basilar Artery Thrombosis
- Coma, quadriplegia, cranial nerve palsies
- Locked-in syndrome
- "Top of the basilar" syndrome: coma, pupillary abnormalities, visual loss, behavioural

## Lacunar Syndromes (Small Vessel Disease)
- **Pure motor hemiparesis**: posterior limb of internal capsule, basis pontis - contralateral hemiparesis (face, arm, leg equally)
- **Pure sensory stroke**: thalamus (VPL nucleus) - contralateral hemisensory loss
- **Sensorimotor stroke**: thalamocapsular region - hemisensory + hemiparesis
- **Ataxic hemiparesis**: posterior limb internal capsule, basis pontis, corona radiata - hemiparesis + ipsilateral ataxia (cerebellar-type)
- **Clumsy hand-dysarthria**: genu internal capsule, basis pontis - facial weakness, dysarthria, clumsy hand
- **Sensorimotor + movement disorder**: hemiballismus (subthalamic), dystonia, chorea (basal ganglia)

## Investigations
- **CT head** (immediate, exclude haemorrhage)
- **MRI brain with DWI** (early ischaemia, location, age)
- **CTA/MRA** (large vessel occlusion, dissection, stenosis)
- **CT/MR perfusion** (penumbra, mismatch)
- **ECG, Holter** (AF, cardiac source)
- **Echocardiogram** (TTE ± TEE, PFO)
- **Carotid Doppler** (extracranial atherosclerosis)
- **Bloods**: FBC, ESR, U&Es, glucose, HbA1c, lipids, coagulation, antiphospholipid, thrombophilia (young)

## Management
- **Acute**: see Ischaemic Stroke management (thrombolysis, thrombectomy, supportive)
- **Secondary prevention**: antiplatelet (DAPT 21 days, then monotherapy), statin, BP control, glucose, anticoagulation if AF, carotid endarterectomy/stenting
- **Specific syndromes**: supportive care, rehabilitation

## Red Flags
- **Cerebellar stroke**: neurosurgical emergency (oedema, brainstem compression, hydrocephalus)
- **Basilar thrombosis**: severe, often fatal
- **Locked-in**: misdiagnosis as coma, communication
- **PCA bilateral**: cortical blindness, Anton syndrome (denial)
- **Mass effect, raised ICP, herniation**
- **Recurrent TIAs, stroke progression**

## Prognosis
- Variable
- Anterior circulation (MCA) strokes: severe (large territory)
- Lacunar: best prognosis (small, often recover)
- Brainstem/cerebellar: variable, often severe
- Recurrence: 5-10% per year (depends on risk factors)
- Recovery: most rapid in first 3-6 months, continues for 12-24 months
- Rehabilitation: critical for functional outcome
