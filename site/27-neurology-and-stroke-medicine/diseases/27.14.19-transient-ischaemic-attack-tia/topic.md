# Transient Ischaemic Attack (TIA)

> **High-Yield Definition:** TIA: brief episode of neurological dysfunction due to focal brain, spinal cord, or retinal ischaemia, without acute infarction. New definition (AHA/ASA): resolution of symptoms + no evidence of acute infarction on MRI DWI.

## Definition
- **Traditional:** neurological deficit resolving within 24 hours
- **New (tissue-based):** neurological dysfunction caused by focal brain/retinal/spinal cord ischaemia, with symptoms resolving, no evidence of acute infarction (negative DWI MRI)
- Duration: usually minutes (typically <1 hour)

## Epidemiology
- Incidence: 50/100,000/year (Western)
- Risk of stroke after TIA: 5% within 48 hours, 10% within 90 days (highest risk in first week)
- ABCD2 score stratifies risk

## Aetiology
- **Large artery atherosclerosis** (carotid, vertebral, intracranial)
- **Cardioembolic**: AF (most common), PFO, valvular disease, LV thrombus, endocarditis, cardiac tumours
- **Small vessel disease** (lacunar TIA)
- **Arterial dissection** (carotid, vertebral - young patients)
- **Hypercoagulability** (Factor V Leiden, antiphospholipid, malignancy)
- **Vasculitis** (GCA, Takayasu's, Behçet's)
- **Migraine** (migrainous infarction)

## Clinical Features
- **Anterior circulation (carotid territory)**: amaurosis fugax (monocular blindness), hemiparesis, hemisensory loss, aphasia, hemianopia
- **Posterior circulation (vertebrobasilar)**: diplopia, vertigo, ataxia, dysarthria, dysphagia, crossed signs, bilateral visual loss, drop attacks, transient global amnesia
- Lacunar TIA: pure motor hemiparesis, pure sensory stroke, sensorimotor, ataxic hemiparesis, clumsy hand-dysarthria
- Duration: usually minutes, resolves spontaneously
- No sequelae (with new definition)
- Recurrent events common

## Investigations
- **EMERGENCY**: same-day assessment
- **CT head** (exclude haemorrhage, mass)
- **MRI brain with DWI** (look for acute infarct - new TIA definition excludes DWI+)
- **CT/MR angiography** (carotid, vertebral, intracranial)
- **ECG**, **Holter** (AF)
- **Echocardiogram** (TTE ± TEE, PFO)
- Bloods: FBC, ESR, lipids, HbA1c, glucose, coagulation, antiphospholipid, thrombophilia (if young), B12
- Carotid Doppler (extracranial atherosclerosis)
- ABCD2 score: Age (>60 = 1), BP (>140/90 = 1), Clinical (unilateral weakness = 2, speech disturbance = 1, other = 0), Duration (>60 min = 2, 10-59 min = 1, <10 min = 0), Diabetes (yes = 1). Score 0-3 = low risk, 4-5 = moderate, 6-7 = high

## Management — EMERGENCY
- **Antiplatelet**:
  - Aspirin 300mg loading, then 75mg daily
  - **DAPT** (aspirin + clopidogrel 75mg) for 21 days then clopidogrel alone (CHANCE, POINT trials - first 21 days for minor stroke/high-risk TIA), then monotherapy
  - Dipyridamole MR 200mg BD (with aspirin, second-line)
- **Anticoagulation** if AF: DOAC (apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban) preferred over warfarin
- **Statin**: high-intensity (atorvastatin 40-80mg, rosuvastatin 20-40mg) immediately
- **BP control**: cautious (permissive hypertension if not chronic hypertensive, lower gradually)
- **Glucose control**: <10 mmol/L acutely
- **Carotid endarterectomy (CEA)**: symptomatic carotid stenosis 70-99% (NASCET, ECST), 50-69% in selected, <50% medical; within 2 weeks (ideally <14 days)
- **Carotid artery stenting (CAS)**: alternative, high surgical risk, anatomy
- **Lifestyle**: smoking cessation, exercise, Mediterranean diet, weight loss
- **Treat underlying**: AF (rate/rhythm control, anticoagulation), PFO (closure in selected, young, cryptogenic stroke), dissection (anticoagulation/antiplatelet), vasculitis (immunosuppression)

## Red Flags
- Crescendo TIAs (increasing frequency) - imminent stroke
- High ABCD2 score
- Symptomatic carotid stenosis >70%
- AF, PFO
- Hypercoagulability
- Young patient (dissection, PFO, vasculitis)

## Prognosis
- Risk of stroke: 5% at 48 hours, 10% at 90 days, 5-10% per year
- Lower with prompt treatment
- ABCD2 <4: low risk
- ABCD2 ≥4: high risk (need urgent assessment)
- Better: prompt evaluation, antiplatelet, statin, BP control, lifestyle
- Worse: high ABCD2, symptomatic carotid stenosis, AF, delayed treatment
