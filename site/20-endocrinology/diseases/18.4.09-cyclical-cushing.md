# Cyclical Cushing Syndrome

> [!info]
> **Cyclical Cushing Syndrome = Intermittent Hypercortisolism** with periods of normal cortisol secretion. **Prevalence 5-15% of Cushing Syndrome**. Diagnostic challenge: requires prolonged monitoring. Management same as sustained Cushing but timing of surgery critical.

---

## 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Define cyclical Cushing and its prevalence
- [ ] Apply diagnostic criteria using prolonged monitoring protocols
- [ ] Differentiate from pseudo-cyclical patterns and pseudo-Cushing
- [ ] Time surgical intervention appropriately
- [ ] Manage medical therapy during off-cycles

---

## 2. Definition & Epidemiology

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Definition** | **Intermittent Hypercortisolism** with alternating periods of biochemical hypercortisolism and normocortisolism |
| **Prevalence** | **5-15% of Cushing Syndrome** |
| **Age & Sex** | Similar to Sustained Cushing (Female 3-5:1, Peak 20-50y) |
| **Aetiology** | **Cushing Disease (Pituitary)** ~70%; **Adrenal Adenoma** ~20%; **Ectopic ACTH** ~10% |

---

## 3. Pathophysiology

| Mechanism | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Pulsatile ACTH Secretion** | Corticotroph Adenoma with **Burst Firing Pattern** → Intermittent ACTH Bursts |
| **Variable Cortisol Clearance** | Altered Hepatic Metabolism / CBG Fluctuations |
| **Feedback Resistance Fluctuation** | Variable Glucocorticoid Receptor Sensitivity |
| **Tumour Biology** | **Pulsatile Secretion** from Adenoma (Intrinsic Pacemaker) |
| **Feedback Threshold** | Variable Set-Point of HPA Axis Negative Feedback |

---

## 4. Clinical Presentation

| Feature | Cyclical vs Sustained Cushing |
|---------|--------------------------------|
| **Symptoms** | **Intermittent** — Waxing and Waning Over Days-Weeks |
| **Weight Gain** | Fluctuating (Rapid Gain → Plateau/Loss) |
| **Glucose Control** | Brittle Glycaemic Control (Episodic Hyperglycaemia) |
| **Hypertension** | Labile BP (Episodic Severe HTN) |
| **Mood** | Cyclical Mood Swings (Euphoria → Depression) |
| **Skin Changes** | Striae/Bruising Appear During Active Phase |
| **Asymptomatic Intervals** | **Complete Resolution** of Symptoms/Biochemistry Between Cycles |

---

## 5. Diagnostic Criteria — Prolonged Monitoring

### Diagnostic Standard
| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Monitoring Duration** | **≥2-4 Weeks** (Usually 4 Weeks Daily Sampling) |
| **Biochemical Criteria** | **>3 Peaks of UFC >ULN** Separated by **Periods of Normal UFC** |
| **Cycle Duration** | Typically **3-10 Days** (Variable: 1 Day to Several Weeks) |
| **Nadir UFC** | **Normal (<70-90 nmol/24h)** During Off-Cycle |

### Monitoring Protocol
| Schedule | Tests |
|----------|-------|
| **Daily** | 24h UFC, Morning Cortisol, ACTH (If Possible) |
| **Alternate Days** | Late-Night Salivary Cortisol (LNSC) |
| **Weekly** | Serum Cortisol (09:00), ACTH, Electrolytes |
| **Duration** | **Minimum 2 Weeks** (Ideally **4 Weeks**) |

---

## 6. Diagnostic Criteria (Consensus)

| Criterion | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Peak UFC** | **>ULN** (Upper Limit of Normal) |
| **Nadir UFC** | **<ULN** (Normal) |
| **Number of Peaks** | **≥3 Discrete Peaks** |
| **Inter-Peak Interval** | Normal UFC Between Peaks |
| **Cycle Regularity** | May Be Regular (e.g., Every 3-7 Days) or Irregular |

---

## 7. Differentiation — Cyclical vs Sustained vs Pseudo-Cushing

| Feature | **Cyclical Cushing** | **Sustained Cushing** | **Pseudo-Cushing** |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------------|-------------------|
| **UFC Pattern** | **Peaks >ULN + Normal Nadirs** | **Persistently >ULN** | Variable (Often Normal/Near-ULN) |
| **Cycle Duration** | Days-Weeks (Regular/Irregular) | Continuous | N/A |
| **Symptoms** | **Waxing-Waning** | Persistent | Often Absent/Mild |
| **LDDST** | Variable (May Suppress During Nadir) | Non-Suppressed | May Suppress |
| **UFC Baseline** | **<ULN During Nadir** | Always >ULN | Normal/Near-ULN |

---

## 8. Diagnostic Protocol

### Standard Monitoring Protocol (4 Weeks)
| Day | Tests |
|-----|-------|
| **Daily (1-28)** | 24h UFC, 09:00 Cortisol, ACTH (If Feasible) |
| **Alternate Days** | Late-Night Salivary Cortisol (LNSC) |
| **Weekly** | Serum Electrolytes, Glucose, HbA1c, Lipids |
| **As Needed** | 1mg DST (During Suspected Nadir) |

### Interpretation Rules
| Pattern | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| **≥3 Peaks UFC >ULN** + **Normal UFC Between** | **Diagnostic** |
| **<3 Peaks** or **No Normal Nadir** | Inconclusive → Extend Monitoring / Consider Pseudo-Cushing |
| **Continuous >ULN** | Sustained Cushing (Not Cyclical) |
| **<2 Peaks** | Insufficient Data → Extend Monitoring |

---

## 9. Aetiology of Cyclical Cushing

| Source | Frequency | Cycle Characteristics |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Cushing Disease (Pituitary)** | ~70% | Often Regular (3-7 Days); Pituitary Adenoma Pulsatility |
| **Adrenal Adenoma** | ~20% | Irregular; Adrenal Autonomous Cycling |
| **Ectopic ACTH (Carcinoid)** | ~10% | Irregular; Tumour Burden Fluctuation |
| **Adrenal Carcinoma** | Rare | Variable |

---

## 10. Management — Timing is Critical

### Surgical Timing
| Principle | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Operate During Active Phase** | **Biochemical Confirmation During Peak** → ↑ Surgical Success |
| **Avoid Surgery During Nadir** | Risk: False Negative Intra-Op Cortisol; Missed Tumour |
| **Pre-Op Confirmation** | Confirm Active Phase with UFC/LNSC Day Before Surgery |

### Surgical Outcomes
| Phase at Surgery | Remission Rate |
|------------------|----------------|
| **Active Phase (Peak)** | **80-90%** (Similar to Sustained) |
| **Nadir Phase** | **<30%** (High False Negative Rate) |

### Medical Therapy During Nadir
| Situation | Management |
|---------|-----------|
| **Waiting for Peak** | **Ketoconazole** (200-400mg q6h) — Suppresses Peak Without Masking Nadir |
| **Severe Symptoms During Peak** | **Metyrapone** (Bridge to Surgery) |
| **Do NOT** | **Give Steroids/Cortisol-Lowering During Nadir** (Masks Cycle) |

---

## 11. Diagnostic Challenges

### Pseudo-Cyclical Patterns (False Positives)
| Pattern | Mechanism | Differentiation |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Stress-Induced** | Acute Stress → Transient ↑ UFC | **Non-Recurrent**, Resolves with Stress Removal |
| **Drug-Induced** | Oral Contraceptives, Oestrogen, Mirtazapine | Drug History; Repeats After Stopping |
| **Lab Error** | Collection Errors, Improper Timing | Repeat Sets; Standardised Protocol |
| **Obese/Depressed** | Pseudo-Cushing Pattern | Resolves with Treatment of Primary Condition |

### Distinguishing Features
| Feature | True Cyclical | Pseudo-Cyclical |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Regularity** | Often Cyclic (e.g., Q3-7 Days) | Irrandom/Non-Recurrent |
| **Duration** | Weeks-Months | Days (Acute Stressors) |
| **Triggers** | Spontaneous | Identifiable (Stress, Drugs) |
| **Resolution** | Spontaneous Cyclic | Resolves with Trigger Removal |

## 12. Management Algorithm

```
Cyclical Cushing Suspected
         │
         ▼
4-Week Monitoring (Daily UFC + Alternate LNSC)
         │
         ├── ≥3 Peaks UFC >ULN + Normal Nadirs → CONFIRMED
         │       │
         │       ├── IDENTIFY SOURCE (ACTH, HDDST, MRI, CT)
         │       │       ├── Pituitary → Cushing Disease
         │       │       ├── Adrenal → Adrenal Adenoma/Carcinoma
         │       │       └── Ectopic → Ectopic ACTH
         │       │
         │       └── SURGERY DURING ACTIVE PHASE (Confirmed Peak)
         │
         ├── <3 Peaks / No Clear Nadir → EXTEND MONITORING (6-8 WEEKS)
         │
         └── CONTINUOUS ELEVATION → SUSTAINED CUSHING
```

## 14. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Cyclical vs Sustained** | Cyclical = Fluctuating UFC (Peaks + Normal Nadir); Sustained = Persistently Elevated |
| **Cyclical vs Pseudo-Cushing** | True Cyclical = Recurrent Peaks + Normal Nadir; Pseudo = Stress/Drug-Induced, Non-Recurrent |
| **Surgery Timing** | **Critical**: Operate at Peak (80-90% Cure); At Nadir = <30% Cure |
| **Monitoring Duration** | Minimum 2 Weeks; Ideally 4 Weeks (Catch Irregular Cycles) |
| **Medical Bridge** | Ketoconazole During Nadir (Prevents Peak Overshoot) — Not During Peak |

**Mnemonic: CYCLICAL CUSHING**
- **C**ycles: **3-10 Days** Typical
- **Y**es to **≥3 Peaks** UFC >ULN
- **C**ortisol **Norm**al Between Peaks
- **L**ength: Monitor **2-4 Weeks**
- **A**void Surgery at **Nadir** (<30% Cure)
- **L**eak: Day 1 Cortisol <50 at Surgery = Good
- **A**void Steroids During Nadir (Masks Cycle)
- **L** percutaneous? No, **L**ook for **I**pss/HDDST for Source
