## 2. 🧠 Core Concept: HPV Biology & Natural History

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Human Papillomavirus] --> B[Non-Enveloped, dsDNA Virus]
    B --> C[>200 Types Identified]
    C --> D[High-Risk (Oncogenic)]
    D --> D1[HPV 16, 18 (70% Cervical Cancer)]
    D --> D2[HPV 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 (Additional 20%)]
    D --> D3[HPV 35, 39, 51, 56, 59, 68, 69, 73, 82]
    C --> E[Low-Risk (Non-Oncogenic)]
    E --> E1[HPV 6, 11 (90% Genital Warts)]
    E --> E2[HPV 42, 43, 44, 54, 61, 70, 72, 81, 89]
    A --> F[Life Cycle]
    F --> F1[Infects Basal Keratinocytes via Microtrauma]
    F --> F2[Episomal Replication in Differentiating Epithelium]
    F --> F3[Viral Assembly in Upper Layers → Shedding]
    F --> F4[Integration (High-Risk) → E6/E7 Oncoproteins → p53/pRb Inactivation → Malignant Transformation]
```

## 1️⃣ HPV Classification & Disease Association

| Category | Types | Disease Association |
|----------|-------|---------------------|
| **High-Risk (Oncogenic) — Group 1 Carcinogens** | **16, 18** | **~70% Cervical Cancer; ~85% Anal Cancer; ~60% Oropharyngeal; ~50% Penile/Vulvar/Vaginal** |
| | **31, 33, 45, 52, 58** | **Additional ~20% Cervical Cancer**; Other Anogenital/Oropharyngeal |
| | **35, 39, 51, 56, 59, 68, 73, 82** | **Probable/possible carcinogens**; Minor Contribution |
| **Low-Risk (Non-Oncogenic)** | **6, 11** | **~90% Genital Warts (Condylomata Acuminata)**; Recurrent Respiratory Papillomatosis (RRP) |
| | **42, 43, 44, 54, 61, 70, 72, 81, 89** | Low-Grade Cervical Lesions (LSIL); Minor Warts |
| **Cutaneous** | 1, 2, 3, 4, 7, 10, 27, 57 | Plantar/Common/Flat Warts (Non-Sexual) |

> **Key**: **HPV 16/18 = Most Oncogenic (Cervical, Anal, Oropharyngeal)**; **HPV 6/11 = Warts (Benign)**; **Co-infection Common**

## 2️⃣ Natural History of Cervical HPV Infection

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    A[HPV Exposure<br/>(Sexual Contact)] --> B[**Transient Infection**<br/>~90% Clear Within 1-2 Years<br/>Cell-Mediated Immunity]
    B --> C[**Persistent Infection**<br/>(High-Risk HPV)<br/>~10% Persist >2 Years]
    C --> D[**CIN 1 (LSIL)**<br/>Low-Grade Squamous Intraepithelial Lesion<br/>~60% Regress, ~30% Persist, ~10% Progress]
    D --> E[**CIN 2/3 (HSIL)**<br/>High-Grade SIL / Carcinoma In Situ<br/>~40% Regress (CIN2), ~30% Persist, ~30% Progress]
    E --> F[**Invasive Cervical Cancer**<br/>Years to Decades<br/>Squamous Cell Ca (80%), Adenoca (15-20%)]
    
    style A fill:#e1f5fe
    style B fill:#c8e6c9
    style C fill:#fff9c4
    style D fill:#ffe0b2
    style E fill:#ffccbc
    style F fill:#ef9a9a
```

| Stage | Histology | Regression Rate | Progression Risk | Timeframe |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|------------------|-----------|
| **Transient Infection** | Normal / HPV+ Only | **90% within 2 Years** | — | 6-24 Months |
| **CIN 1 (LSIL)** | **Mild Dysplasia** (Lower 1/3 Epithelium) | **~60%** | ~10% to CIN 2/3 | 1-3 Years |
| **CIN 2 (HSIL)** | **Moderate Dysplasia** (Lower 2/3) | **~40-50%** | ~30% to CIN 3/Cancer | 2-5 Years |
| **CIN 3 (HSIL / CIS)** | **Severe Dysplasia / Carcinoma In Situ** (Full Thickness) | **~30-40%** | **~30% to Invasion** | 5-10+ Years |
| **Invasive Cancer** | Basement Membrane Breach | — | — | 10-20+ Years |

> **Cofactors for Progression**: **HIV/Immunosuppression, Smoking, Long-term OCPs, High Parity, Co-infections (HSV, Chlamydia), Nutritional Deficiencies**

## 3️⃣ HPV Vaccination

### Vaccines Available

| Vaccine | Valency | Types Covered | Indications |
|---------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Gardasil 9 (9vHPV)** | **9-valent** | **6, 11, 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58** | **Preferred — Covers 90% Cervical Ca, 90% Genital Warts, 85% Anal Ca, 70% Other HPV Cancers** |
| **Gardasil (4vHPV)** | 4-valent | 6, 11, 16, 18 | 70% Cervical Ca, 90% Warts |
| **Cervarix (2vHPV)** | 2-valent | 16, 18 | 70% Cervical Ca Only |

### Vaccination Schedules

| Age at 1st Dose | Schedule | Doses | Interval |
|-----------------|----------|-------|----------|
| **<15 Years** | **2-Dose** | **2** | **0, 6-12 Months** (Min 5 Months) |
| **≥15 Years** | **3-Dose** | **3** | **0, 1-2, 6 Months** (Min: 4wk 1-2, 12wk 2-3, 5mo 1-3) |
| **Immunocompromised / HIV** | **3-Dose (All Ages)** | **3** | **0, 1-2, 6 Months** |

### Target Populations

| Population | Recommendation | Notes |
|------------|----------------|-------|
| **Girls 9-14 Years** | **Routine (Primary Target)** | **Before Sexual Debut = Highest Impact** |
| **Boys 9-14 Years** | **Routine (Gender-Neutral)** | **Herd Immunity + Direct Protection (Anal, Penile, Oropharyngeal Ca)** |
| **Catch-Up Females 15-26 Years** | **Recommended** | **3-Dose If ≥15y**; **Effectiveness ↓ with Age/Sexual Exposure** |
| **Catch-Up Males 15-26 Years** | **Recommended (MSM Priority)** | **MSM: Up to 45 Years in Some Guidelines** |
| **Adults 27-45 Years** | **Shared Decision-Making** | **Less Benefit (Likely Exposed); Consider if New Partners** |
| **Pregnancy** | **Deferred** | **Not Contraindicated but No Data; Resume Postpartum** |
| **HIV / Immunocompromised** | **Strongly Recommended** | **3-Dose Regardless of Age**; **Higher Disease Burden** |

> **9vHPV Efficacy**: **>90% vs Vaccine-Type Persistent Infection/CIN2+/Genital Warts**; **Herd Effect Reduces Non-Vaccine Types**

## 4️⃣ Cervical Screening (HPV Primary Screening)

### WHO / UK / International Algorithm

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Screening Age 25-64<br/>(or 30-65 in Some)] --> B[**Primary hrHPV Test**<br/>(NAAT/PCR for 14 High-Risk Types)]
    B --> C{hrHPV Result}
    C -->|Negative| D[**Return to Routine Recall**<br/>3-5 Years]
    C -->|Positive| E[**Triage**]
    E --> E1[**Cytology (LBC)**<br/>If Normal/Low-Grade → **Repeat hrHPV 12 Months**]
    E --> E2[**Partial Genotyping (16/18/45)**<br/>If 16/18/45+ → **Direct Colposcopy**]
    E --> E3[**Dual Stain (p16/Ki-67)**<br/>If Positive → Colposcopy]
    E1 & E2 & E3 --> F[**Colposcopy**]
    F --> G{CIN Grade}
    G -->|CIN 1| H[**Surveillance**<br/>hrHPV/Cytology 12 Months]
    G -->|CIN 2| I[**Treatment or Surveillance**<br/>(Age, Fertility, Extent)]
    G -->|CIN 3| J[**Treatment (LLETZ/LOOP/Cone)**<br/>Excisional Preferred]
    H & I & J --> K[**Test of Cure**<br/>**hrHPV + Cytology at 6 Months**]
    K --> L{TOC Result}
    L -->|Negative| M[**Return to 3-Year Recall**<br/>(Not Routine 5-Year)]
    L -->|Positive| N[**Colposcopy / Repeat TOC**]
```

### Key Screening Intervals

| Population | Interval | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|
| **Routine (hrHPV Negative)** | **5 Years** (UK: 3y <50, 5y ≥50) | **Primary hrHPV Replaces Cytology** |
| **hrHPV Positive, Cytology Negative** | **12 Months** (Repeat hrHPV) | **If Persistent → Colposcopy** |
| **Post-Treatment (TOC Negative)** | **3 Years** (Not 5 Years) | **Higher Recall Due to Residual Risk** |
| **HIV+ Women** | **Annual** (From Diagnosis) | **3-Site (Cervical, Anal, Vulvar) If MSM/WSW** |
| **Immunocompromised** | **Annual** | **Transplant, Steroids, Chemo** |

> **HPV Primary Screening = Higher Sensitivity for CIN3+ (90% vs 70% Cytology) → Longer Intervals Safe**

## 5️⃣ Anogenital Warts (Condylomata Acuminata)

### Clinical Features
- **Caused by Low-Risk HPV 6, 11 (90%)**
- **Soft, Flesh-Coloured, Cauliflower-Like, Pedunculated or Sessile**
- **Sites: Vulva, Vagina, Cervix, Penis, Scrotum, Perineum, Perianal, Urethral Meatus**
- **Usually Asymptomatic; Can Be Pruritic, Painful, Bleeding, Obstructive**
- **Koilocytes on Histology (If Biopsied — Rarely Needed)**

### Treatment Modalities

| Category | Agent/Method | Regimen | Clearance Rate | Recurrence | Notes |
|----------|--------------|---------|----------------|------------|-------|
| **Patient-Applied (Home)** | **Imiquimod 5% Cream** | **3x/Week (Alternate Nights) × 16 Weeks** | 50-60% | 13-19% | **Immune Modulator**; Local Inflammation Expected |
| | **Podophyllotoxin 0.15% Cream / 0.5% Solution** | **BD × 3 Days On, 4 Days Off × 4-5 Cycles** | 45-77% | Up to 38% | **Antimitotic**; **Contraindicated in Pregnancy** |
| | **Sinecatechins 15% Ointment** | **TDS × 16 Weeks** | ~58% | ~8% | **Green Tea Extract**; Well Tolerated |
| **Provider-Administered** | **Cryotherapy (Liquid Nitrogen)** | **Freeze-Thaw-Freeze × 1-2 Week Intervals** | 60-90% | 25-40% | **1st Line for Few/External Warts**; Pain, Hypopigmentation |
| | **Trichloroacetic Acid (TCA) / Bichloroacetic Acid (BCA) 80-90%** | **Weekly × 4-6 Weeks** | 70-80% | 20-30% | **Chemical Cautery**; Safe in Pregnancy |
| | **Podophyllin Resin 10-25%** | **Weekly** | Variable | High | **Obsolete** (Systemic Toxicity, Variable Potency) |
| **Surgical** | **Electrocautery / Hyfrecation** | Single Session | 80-95% | 20-30% | **Anaesthesia Needed**; Large/Internal Warts |
| | **Excision / Scalpel** | Single Session | High | Variable | **Pedunculated/Resistant**; Histology |
| | **Laser (CO2, Nd:YAG)** | Single/Multiple | High | 20-30% | **Extensive/Resistant/Immunocomp**; Expensive |
| **Immunomodulatory (Adjunct)** | **Intralesional Interferon** | 2-3x/Week × 8 Weeks | Variable | Variable | **Refractory**; Systemic Side Effects |

### Treatment Selection Algorithm

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Anogenital Warts] --> B{Number/Size/Site}
    B -->|Few, Small, External| C[Patient Choice + Shared Decision]
    C --> C1[Cryotherapy (Quick, 1st Line Provider)]
    C --> C2[Imiquimod (Patient-Applied, Immune Memory)]
    C --> C3[Podophyllotoxin (Patient-Applied, Faster)]
    C --> C4[TCA/BCA (Provider, Safe Pregnancy)]
    B -->|Multiple, Large, Internal, Cervical, Urethral| D[Provider-Administered / Surgical]
    D --> D1[Electrocautery / Excision (Anaesthesia)]
    D --> D2[Laser (Extensive/Immunocomp)]
    D --> D3[Cryotherapy + TCA Adjunct]
    B -->|Pregnancy| E[**Safe: Cryotherapy, TCA/BCA, Excision, Laser**]
    E --> E1[**Avoid: Podophyllotoxin, Imiquimod (Limited Data), Podophyllin**]
    B -->|Immunocompromised / HIV| F[**More Extensive, Resistant, Recurrent**]
    F --> F1[**Surgical (Laser/Excision) + Immune Reconstitution (ART)**]
    F --> F2[Avoid Imiquimod if CD4<200]
```

## 6️⃣ HPV-Related Cancers

| Cancer | HPV Attribution | High-Risk Types | Screening / Early Detection |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| **Cervical** | **~100%** | **16, 18 (70%), 31, 33, 45, 52, 58** | **HPV Primary Screening (25-64y)** |
| **Anal** | **~85-90%** | **16, 18 (80%)** | **Anal Cytology / hrHPV (MSM, HIV+, Women with CIN3+)** |
| **Oropharyngeal** | **~60-70% (Rising)** | **16 (90% of HPV+)** | **No Organised Screening**; Clinical Exam |
| **Vulvar** | **~40-50%** | **16, 18** | **Vulvoscopy if Symptomatic / Post-CIN3** |
| **Vaginal** | **~60-70%** | **16, 18** | **Vaginal Vault Cytology / hrHPV Post-Hysterectomy** |
| **Penile** | **~50%** | **16, 18** | **Clinical Exam; No Organised Screening** |

> **HPV 16 = Dominant in ALL Non-Cervical HPV Cancers**; **Oropharyngeal Cancer Now > Cervical in Some High-Income Countries (Male Predominance)**

## 7️⃣ Special Populations

### HIV / Immunocompromised
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **Cervical Screening** | **Annual from HIV Diagnosis** (Not 3-5 Yearly); **3-Site (Cervical, Anal, Vulvar) for MSM/WSW** |
| **Anal Screening** | **Annual Anal Cytology/hrHPV (MSM, HIV+ Women with CIN3+)**; **HRA (High-Resolution Anoscopy) if Abnormal** |
| **Warts** | **More Extensive, Resistant, Recurrent**; **Surgical Preferred**; **ART Improves Clearance** |
| **Vaccination** | **3-Dose 9vHPV Regardless of Age**; **Immunogenicity Lower but Beneficial** |
| **Cancer Risk** | **↑ Cervical (5-10x), Anal (30-80x), Oropharyngeal, Vulvar/Vaginal** |

### Pregnancy
| Issue | Management |
|-------|------------|
| **Warts** | **Often Increase in Size/Number (Hormonal/Immunological)**; **Treat if Obstructive/Bleeding**; **Safe: Cryotherapy, TCA/BCA, Excision, Laser**; **Avoid Podophyllotoxin, Imiquimod (Limited Data)** |
| **Cervical Screening** | **Defer Routine Until 12 Weeks Postpartum**; **If Overdue → Can Screen in Pregnancy (LBC Safe)** |
| **Vaccination** | **Deferred (Not Contraindicated, No Safety Data)**; **Resume Postpartum** |
| **Delivery** | **Vaginal Delivery Safe Even with Warts**; **C-Section Only if Obstructive Urethral/Vaginal Warts** |
| **Neonatal** | **Recurrent Respiratory Papillomatosis (RRP) — Rare (HPV 6/11 Vertical Transmission)**; **No Routine C-Section for Warts** |

### MSM
- **Anal Cancer Risk ↑↑ (HPV 16/18)**; **Annual Anal Screening if HIV+**
- **9vHPV Vaccination Up to Age 45 (Some Guidelines)**
- **Oropharyngeal Cancer Risk ↑ (HPV 16)**

## 5. 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"HPV Vaccine = Only for Girls"** | **NO.** **Gender-Neutral: Boys Also (Herd Immunity + Direct Protection for Anal, Penile, Oropharyngeal Ca)**; **MSM Priority Up to 45y** |
| **"2-Dose Schedule = For Everyone"** | **NO.** **<15 Years Only**; **≥15 Years = 3-Dose**; **Immunocompromised/HIV = 3-Dose All Ages** |
| **"HPV Primary Screening = Just HPV Test"** | **NO.** **Algorithm: hrHPV → Triage (Cytology/Genotyping/Dual Stain) → Colposcopy** |
| **"CIN 2 = Always Treat"** | **NO.** **Can Surveyveillance (Age<25, Fertility Desire, Small Lesion)**; **CIN 3 = Always Treat (Excisional)** |
| **"Warts = High-Risk HPV"** | **NO.** **Warts = Low-Risk HPV 6/11 (Non-Oncogenic)**; **High-Risk = Cancer/CIN** |
| **"Podophyllotoxin = Safe in Pregnancy"** | **CONTRAINDICATED IN PREGNANCY (Teratogenic in Animals, Systemic Absorption)**; **Use Cryo/TCA/Excision/Laser** |
| **"HPV Test = Replaces Pap Smear Entirely"** | **PARTIALLY.** **Primary hrHPV Replaces Cytology as Primary Screen**; **Cytology Still Used for Triage & Post-Treatment TOC** |
| **"All HPV 16/18 = Cancer"** | **NO.** **Most HPV 16/18 Infections Clear**; **Persistence + Cofactors → Cancer** |
| **"HPV in Men = Just Warts"** | **NO.** **Men Get Anal (MSM ↑↑), Penile, Oropharyngeal Cancers (HPV 16 Dominant)**; **Vaccination Critical** |
| **"Test of Cure = Cytology Only"** | **NO.** **TOC = hrHPV + Cytology (Co-Test) at 6 Months**; **hrHPV More Sensitive for Residual Disease** |

> **Mnemonic: HPV MASTER SHIELD**  
> **H**PV Types: **HR: 16,18 (70% CxCa), 31,33,45,52,58 (+20%)**; **LR: 6,11 (90% Warts)**  
> **P**ersistence: **90% Clear 1-2y**; **10% Persist → CIN1 (60% Reg) → CIN2/3 (30% Prog) → Cancer (10-20y)**  
> **V**accine 9vHPV: **6,11,16,18,31,33,45,52,58 (90% CxCa, 90% Warts, 85% Anal)**  
> **S**chedule: **<15y 2-Dose (0,6-12m); ≥15y 3-Dose (0,1-2,6m); HIV/ImmComp 3-Dose All Ages**  
> **C**ervical Screen: **Primary hrHPV (25-64y, 3-5y); hrHPV+ → Triage (Cyto/16-18 Geno/Dual Stain) → Colpo**  
> **I** CIN Grades: **CIN1 (LSIL) Surveil; CIN2 (HSIL) Tx/Surveil; CIN3 (CIS) Excise (LLETZ)**  
> **N** TOC: **6mo Post-Tx → hrHPV + Cyto Co-Test → Neg = 3y Recall (Not 5y)**  
> **W**arts (HPV 6/11): **Pt: Imiq/Podoph/Sinecat; Prov: Cryo/TCA/Electro; Surg: Laser/Exc; Preg: Cryo/TCA/Exc/Laser**  
> **A**nal Cancer: **85-90% HPV (16/18); Screen MSM/HIV+ Annual Anal Cyto/hrHPV → HRA if Abnormal**  
> **R** Oropharyngeal: **60-70% HPV (16=90%); Male Dom; Better Prog (Non-Smoker); No Screen**  
> **T** Vulvar/Vaginal/Penile: **40-70% HPV (16/18); clinical Exam**  
> **H**IV: **Annual Cerv/Anal (3-Site MSM/WSW); 3-Dose Vaccine; ↑ CxCa 5-10x, Anal 30-80x**  
> **I** Pregnancy: **Defer Vacc/Screen; Warts→Cryo/TCA/Exc/Laser Safe; Vaginal Del OK; RRP Rare (6/11)**  
> **E**liminate: **WHO 90-70-90 (Vax/Screen/Tx) by 2030 → CxCa Elimination**  
> **L** 16 Dominant: **HPV 16 = King of Non-Cervical Cancers (Anal, Oropharyngeal, Penile, Vulvar)**  
> **D**efinitions: **LSIL = CIN1; HSIL = CIN2/3; CIS = CIN3; AIS = Adenocarcinoma In Situ**

## 10. 📌 Summary
- **Virology**: **Non-Enveloped dsDNA, >200 Types**; **High-Risk (Oncogenic): 16, 18 (70% CxCa), 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 (+20%)**; **Low-Risk: 6, 11 (90% Warts)**; **E6/E7 → p53/pRb Inactivation**
- **Natural History**: **90% Transient Clearance 1-2 Years**; **10% Persistent HR-HPV → CIN1 (LSIL, 60% Reg) → CIN2/3 (HSIL/CIS, 30% Prog) → Invasive Cancer (10-20 Years)**; **Cofactors: HIV, Smoking, OCPs, Parity, Co-infections**
- **Vaccination**: **9vHPV (6,11,16,18,31,33,45,52,58) = Preferred**; **<15y: 2-Dose (0, 6-12m); ≥15y: 3-Dose (0, 1-2, 6m); HIV/Immunocomp: 3-Dose All Ages**; **Targets: Girls/Boys 9-14y Routine; Catch-Up 15-26y; MSM to 45y**
- **Screening**: **Primary hrHPV (25-64y, 3-5y)**; **hrHPV+ → Triage (Cytology / Partial Genotyping 16/18/45 / Dual Stain p16/Ki-67) → Colposcopy**; **CIN1 Surveil, CIN2 Tx/Surveil, CIN3 Excision (LLETZ/Loop/Cone)**; **TOC: 6m hrHPV + Cytology Co-Test → Both Neg = 3y Recall (Not 5y)**
- **Warts (HPV 6/11)**: **Patient: Imiquimod 5% / Podophyllotoxin / Sinecatechins**; **Provider: Cryotherapy / TCA-BCA / Electrocautery**; **Surgical: Laser / Excision**; **Pregnancy Safe: Cryo / TCA / Excision / Laser (Avoid Podophyllotoxin)**
- **Cancers**: **Cervical (100%), Anal (85-90%), Oropharyngeal (60-70% Rising), Vulvar/Vaginal/Penile (40-70%)**; **HPV 16 Dominant in ALL Non-Cervical HPV Cancers**
- **HIV**: **Annual Cervical + Anal (3-Site for MSM/WSW); 3-Dose 9vHPV Regardless of Age; ↑ CxCa 5-10x, Anal 30-80x, Other HPV Cancers**
- **Pregnancy**: **Defer Vaccination/Screening**; **Warts → Cryo/TCA/Excision/Laser Safe; Vaginal Delivery OK**; **RRP Rare (HPV 6/11 Vertical)**

## 11. ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **HPV types covered by 9-valent vaccine (Gardasil 9)?**  
   A. 16, 18 Only  B. 6, 11, 16, 18  C. **6, 11, 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58**  D. 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58  
   *Answer: C. 9vHPV Covers 6, 11, 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 (90% CxCa, 90% Warts, 85% Anal Ca).*

2. **Vaccination schedule for 13-year-old girl?**  
   A. 3 Doses (0, 1, 6 Months)  B. **2 Doses (0, 6-12 Months)**  C. 1 Dose  D. Not Indicated  
   *Answer: B. <15 Years = 2-Dose Schedule (0, 6-12 Months, Minimum 5 Months Interval).*

3. **HPV Primary Screening — hrHPV Positive, Cytology Negative. Next Step?**  
   A. Immediate Colposcopy  B. **Repeat hrHPV at 12 Months**  C. Treat as CIN1  D. Repeat Cytology 6 Months  
   *Answer: B. hrHPV+ / Cyto Negative → Repeat hrHPV at 12 Months (If Persistent → Colposcopy).*

4. **Test of Cure after CIN3 Treatment — Protocol?**  
   A. Cytology Only at 6 Months  B. **hrHPV + Cytology Co-Test at 6 Months**  C. hrHPV Only at 12 Months  D. Colposcopy at 6 Months  
   *Answer: B. TOC = hrHPV + Cytology Co-Test at 6 Months Post-Treatment; Both Negative → 3-Year Recall.*

5. **Anogenital Warts — Contraindicated Treatment in Pregnancy?**  
   A. Cryotherapy  B. **Podophyllotoxin**  C. TCA/BCA  D. Excision  
   *Answer: B. Podophyllotoxin Contraindicated in Pregnancy (Teratogenic in Animals); Safe: Cryo, TCA/BCA, Excision, Laser.*

6. **Most common high-risk HPV type in Oropharyngeal Cancer?**  
   A. HPV 18  B. **HPV 16**  C. HPV 31  D. HPV 45  
   *Answer: B. HPV 16 Causes ~90% of HPV-Positive Oropharyngeal Squamous Cell Carcinomas.*

6. **Cervical Screening Interval for HIV+ Women?**  
   A. 3 Years  B. 5 Years  C. **Annual**  D. No Screening Needed  
   *Answer: C. HIV+ Women: Annual Cervical Screening from Diagnosis (Not 3-5 Yearly).*

7. **HPV Attribution — Anal Cancer?**  
   A. ~50%  B. ~70%  C. **~85-90%**  D. ~100%  
   *Answer: C. ~85-90% of Anal Cancers HPV-Attributable (HPV 16/18 ~80%).*

8. **CIN 2 Management — When Can Surveillance Be Offered Instead of Treatment?**  
   A. Never  B. **Age <25, Fertility Desire, Small Lesion (<50% Transformation Zone)**  C. Always  D. Only if HIV+  
   *Answer: B. CIN2: Surveillance Acceptable if Age <25, Desire Fertility, Small Lesion (<50% TZ); CIN3 = Always Treat.*

9. **Recurrent Respiratory Papillomatosis (RRP) — Causative HPV Types?**  
   A. 16, 18  B. 31, 33  C. **6, 11**  D. 52, 58  
   *Answer: C. RRP Caused by Low-Risk HPV 6/11 (Vertical Perinatal Transmission).*
