## 2. 🧠 Core Concept: HBV vs HCV — Sexual Transmission Context

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Viral Hepatitis — Sexual Transmission] --> B[Hepatitis B (HBV)]
    A --> C[Hepatitis C (HCV)]
    B --> B1[DNA Virus, Partially dsDNA]
    B --> B2[Highly Infectious Sexually]
    B --> B3[Vaccine Preventable]
    B --> B4[Chronic Carriage → Cirrhosis/HCC]
    B --> B5[Effective Suppression (NUCs)]
    C --> C1[RNA Virus, Flavivirus]
    C --> C2[Less Infectious Sexually]
    C --> C3[No Vaccine]
    C --> C4[Curable with DAAs (SVR >95%)]
    C --> C5[No Chronic Carriage if Cured]
    B & C --> D[Shared Risks]
    D --> D1[Multiple Partners, MSM, Sex Workers, HIV Co-infection]
    D --> D2[Needle Sharing, Perinatal, Occupational]
```

## 1️⃣ Hepatitis B (HBV)

### Epidemiology & Sexual Transmission
| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Global Prevalence** | **296 Million Chronic** (2019); **1.5 Million New Infections/Year** |
| **Sexual Transmission Efficiency** | **High** (100x HIV, 10x HCV); **MSM, Multiple Partners, Sex Workers = High Risk** |
| **Other Routes** | Perinatal (Vertical), Percutaneous (Needles, Tattoo, medical), Horizontal (Household) |
| **Incubation** | **60-150 Days** (Average 90 Days) |

### HBV Serology — Interpretation Masterclass

| Marker | Significance | Interpretation |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| **HBsAg** | **Surface Antigen** | **Current Infection (Acute or Chronic)**; **Positive >6 Months = Chronic** |
| **Anti-HBs** | **Surface Antibody** | **Immunity** (Vaccination or Resolved Infection); **>10 mIU/mL = Protected** |
| **Anti-HBc Total** | **Core Antibody (IgG+IgM)** | **Past or Current Infection** (Never Positive from Vaccine Alone) |
| **Anti-HBc IgM** | **Core IgM Antibody** | **Acute/Recent Infection** (<6 Months); **Window Period Marker** |
| **HBeAg** | **e Antigen** | **High Replicative State**, High Infectivity |
| **Anti-HBe** | **e Antibody** | **Lower Replication**, Seroconversion (Usually Good Prognosis) |
| **HBV DNA** | **Viral Load** | **Quantitative Replication**; **Guides Treatment, Monitors Response** |

### HBV Serology Interpretation Matrix

| Clinical Scenario | HBsAg | Anti-HBs | Anti-HBc Total | Anti-HBc IgM | HBeAg | Anti-HBe | HBV DNA |
|-------------------|-------|----------|----------------|--------------|-------|----------|---------|
| **Susceptible** | – | – | – | – | – | – | – |
| **Immune (Vaccinated)** | – | **+** | – | – | – | – | – |
| **Resolved Infection** | – | **+** | **+** | – | – | **+** | – |
| **Acute HBV** | **+** | – | **+** | **+** | **+** | – | High |
| **Chronic Inactive Carrier** | **+** | – | **+** | – | – | **+** | Low/Undetectable |
| **Chronic Active (HBeAg +ve)** | **+** | – | **+** | – | **+** | – | High |
| **HBeAg-Negative Chronic Hepatitis** | **+** | – | **+** | – | – | **+** | Int/High |
| **Window Period** | – | – | **+** | **+** | – | – | Detectable |
| **Isolated Anti-HBc** | – | – | **+** | – | – | – | Usually – |

> **Key**: **Anti-HBc = Past/Current Infection (Never from Vaccine)**; **Anti-HBs = Immunity (Vaccine or Resolved)**; **HBsAg >6 Months = Chronic**

## 2️⃣ Hepatitis B — Staging & Treatment Indications

### Chronic HBV Staging (EASL/AASLD)

| Phase | HBsAg | HBeAg | HBV DNA | ALT | Histology | Treatment Indication |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|-----|-----------|----------------------|
| **Immune Tolerant** | + | **+** | **Very High (>10⁷ IU/mL)** | **Normal** | Minimal | **No** (Observe) |
| **Immune Active (HBeAg +ve)** | + | **+** | **High (>10⁴ IU/mL)** | **Elevated** | Inflammation/Fibrosis | **Yes** |
| **Inactive Carrier** | + | **–** | **Low (<2,000 IU/mL)** | **Normal** | Minimal | **No** (Monitor) |
| **HBeAg-Negative Chronic Hepatitis** | + | **–** | **>2,000 IU/mL** | **Elevated** | Inflammation/Fibrosis | **Yes** |
| **Reactivated** | + | Variable | Rising | Rising | Worsening | **Yes** (Urgent) |

> **Treatment Goal**: **HBsAg Loss (Functional Cure)**; **HBeAg Seroconversion**; **HBV DNA Suppression (<20 IU/mL)**; **ALT Normalisation**

## 3️⃣ Hepatitis B — Treatment (Nucleos(t)ide Analogues)

### First-Line NUCs (Potent, High Barrier to Resistance)

| Drug | Dose (Adult) | Renal Adjustment | Resistance Barrier | Key Features |
|------|--------------|------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Tenofovir Alafenamide (TAF)** | **25mg OD** | **eGFR ≥15** | **Highest** | **Preferred** (Bone/Renal Safety), **Pregnancy Safe** |
| **Tenofovir Disoproxil Fumarate (TDF)** | **245mg (300mg) OD** | **eGFR ≥30** | **Highest** | **Alternative** (Renal/Bone Toxicity) |
| **Entecavir (ETV)** | **0.5mg OD** (1mg if NAIVE) | **eGFR <50: Dose Adjust** | **High** | **Alternative** (Resistance in NAIVE Low) |

> **Preferred**: **TAF > TDF > ETV**; **Avoid Lamivudine/Adefovir/Telbivudine** (Low Barrier, High Resistance)

### Treatment Duration & Stopping Rules

| Scenario | Duration | Stopping Criteria |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **HBeAg +ve Chronic Hepatitis** | **Until HBeAg Seroconversion + 12 Months Consolidation** | **HBeAg Seroconversion + Anti-HBe + HBV DNA Undetectable × 12 Months** |
| **HBeAg -ve Chronic Hepatitis** | **Long-Term (Indefinite)** | **HBsAg Loss (Rare)**; **Consider Stop if HBsAg Loss + 12 Months** |
| **Cirrhosis** | **Lifelong** | **Do Not Stop** (Risk of Decompensation) |
| **Pregnancy (TDF/TAF/ETV)** | **Throughout Pregnancy + 3-12 Months Postpartum** | **Prevent Vertical Transmission** |

## 3️⃣ Hepatitis B — Special Situations

### HBV in Pregnancy
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **Screening** | **Universal HBsAg at Booking** (All Pregnant Women) |
| **High Viral Load (>200,000 IU/mL)** | **TAF/TDF/ETV from 28-32 Weeks → 3-12 Months Postpartum** (Prevent Vertical Transmission) |
| **HBsAg +ve Mother** | **Infant: HBIG (100 IU IM) + HBV Vaccine (0, 1, 6 Months) Within 24 Hours of Birth** |
| **Breastfeeding** | **Safe** (If Infant Received HBIG + Vaccine) |
| **Antivirals in Pregnancy** | **TAF/TDF/ETV Safe** (Preferred: TAF/TDF); **Start 28-32 Weeks if HBV DNA >200,000 IU/mL** |

### HBV/HCV Co-infection with HIV
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **ART Regimen** | **TDF/TAF + 3TC/FTC (Backbone Covers HBV)** |
| **HBV Mono-infected on NUCs** | **Continue NUCs** (Do Not Stop — Risk of Flare) |
| **Monitoring** | **HBV DNA, ALT q3-6 Months**; **HBsAg q6-12 Months** |
| **Hepatitis Flare** | **ART Initiation (IRIS)** or **NUC Stoppage** — **Manage Expectantly/Restart NUC** |

## 3️⃣ Hepatitis B — Vaccination & PEP

### Hepatitis B Vaccination
| Population | Schedule | Indication |
|------------|----------|------------|
| **Universal (Infants)** | **Birth, 1, 6 Months** (or 6, 10, 14 Weeks EPI) | **Universal** |
| **Adults Risk-Based** | **0, 1, 6 Months** (or Accelerated 0, 1, 2, 12 Months) | **MSM, Sex Workers, PWID, HIV, CKD, Prisoners, Healthcare Workers, Partners of HBsAg+** |
| **Non-Responders (Anti-HBs <10 mIU/mL)** | **Repeat Series (Double Dose/Accelerated)** → **Check Anti-HBs 1-2m Post** | Consider **Twinrix (A+B)** if Also Hep A Indicated |

### Hepatitis B Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)

| Exposure Type | Source HBsAg | Exposed Status | PEP Regimen |
|---------------|--------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Percutaneous/Mucosal/Sexual** | **+ve** | **Unvaccinated / Non-Responder** | **HBIG (0.06 mL/kg IM) + HBV Vaccine (0, 1, 6 Months)** — **Within 24h (Ideally), Up to 7 Days** |
| | **+ve** | **Vaccinated, Known Responder (Anti-HBs >10)** | **No PEP Needed** |
| | **+ve** | **Vaccinated, Unknown Response** | **Test Anti-HBs: If >10 → No PEP; If <10 → HBIG + Booster** |
| | **Unknown/-ve** | **Unvaccinated** | **HBV Vaccine Series (0, 1, 6 Months)** |
| **Sexual Exposure** | **HBsAg +ve Partner** | **Susceptible Partner** | **HBIG + Vaccine (Within 14 Days, Ideally <24h)** |

## 4️⃣ Hepatitis C (HCV)

### Epidemiology & Sexual Transmission
| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Global Prevalence** | **58 Million Chronic** (2019); **1.5 Million New Infections/Year** |
| **Sexual Transmission Efficiency** | **Low** (Overall); **Higher in MSM (HIV+, Chemsex, Rough Sex, Mucosal Trauma)** |
| **Primary Route** | **Percutaneous (PWID, Unsafe Medical, Transfusion Pre-1992)** |
| **Incubation** | **2 Weeks – 6 Months** (Average 6-9 Weeks) |

### Natural History
| Stage | Probability | Timeline |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Acute HCV** | **15-25% Symptomatic** | **2-26 Weeks** |
| **Spontaneous Clearance** | **15-45%** (Higher in Women, Young, IL28B CC) | **6 Months** |
| **Chronic HCV** | **55-85%** | **>6 Months** |
| **Cirrhosis** | **15-30% over 20-30 Years** | Accelerated by Alcohol, HIV, Age, Male, Obesity |
| **HCC** | **1-4%/Year (Cirrhosis)** | Surveillance Required |

## 4️⃣ Hepatitis C — Diagnosis & Staging

### Diagnostic Algorithm
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Suspected HCV] --> B[Anti-HCV (EIA/CIA)]
    B -->|Negative| C[No HCV Infection<br/>(If Recent Exposure → HCV RNA)]
    B -->|Positive| D[HCV RNA (Quantitative PCR)]
    D -->|Negative| E[Resolved Infection<br/>(Past Exposure, Spontaneous Clearance)]
    D -->|Positive| F[Chronic HCV Infection]
    F --> G[Genotype/Subtype (1-6)]
    F --> H[HCV RNA Quantification (Baseline)]
    F --> I[Fibrosis Assessment<br/>(FibroScan, APRI, FIB-4, Biopsy)]
```

### HCV Serology & RNA Interpretation

| Scenario | Anti-HCV | HCV RNA | Interpretation |
|----------|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Never Exposed** | – | – | Susceptible |
| **Acute HCV** | + (Seroconversion) | **+** | Acute Infection |
| **Chronic HCV** | + | **+** | Chronic Infection |
| **Resolved (Spontaneous/Treated)** | + | – | Past Infection, Cured |
| **False Positive Anti-HCV** | + | – | Low Prevalence, Confirm RNA |

### HCV Genotypes & Clinical Relevance
| Genotype | Global Distribution | Historical DAA Relevance | Current Pan-Genotypic DAAs |
|----------|---------------------|--------------------------|----------------------------|
| **1** | **46%** (USA/Europe) | Subtype 1a/1b Matters | **Pan-Genotypic (All)** |
| **2** | 20% | | **Pan-Genotypic (All)** |
| **3** | 30% (Asia, PWID) | Difficult (SVR Lower) | **Pan-Genotypic (All)** |
| **4** | Egypt, Middle East, Africa | | **Pan-Genotypic (All)** |
| **5, 6** | Rare | | **Pan-Genotypic (All)** |

> **Current Standard**: **Pan-Genotypic DAAs** — **Genotype Testing Optional** (Still Useful for Epidemiology, Resistance, Regimen Selection in Some Guidelines)

## 4️⃣ Hepatitis C — Direct-Acting Antiviral (DAA) Therapy

### Pan-Genotypic DAA Regimens (Current Standard)

| Regimen | Composition | Duration | Genotypes | Key Notes |
|---------|-------------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir (Epclusa)** | SOF 400mg + VEL 100mg OD | **12 Weeks** | **1-6 (Pan-Genotypic)** | **First-Line (All)**; **Cirrhosis: Add Ribavirin if Decompensated** |
| **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir (Maviret)** | GLE 300mg + PIB 120mg OD (3 Tablets) | **8 Weeks (Non-Cirrhotic); 12 Weeks (Cirrhotic)** | **1-6 (Pan-Genotypic)** | **Shortest (8w Non-Cirrhotics)**; **Renal Safe (eGFR <30)**; **NOT in Decompensated Cirrhosis** |
| **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir/Voxilaprevir (Vosevi)** | SOF 400mg + VEL 100mg + VOX 100mg OD | **12 Weeks** | **1-6 (Pan-Genotypic)** | **Retreatment (Daa-Experienced)** |

> **Preferred First-Line**: **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir 12 Weeks** (All Genotypes); **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir 8 Weeks (Non-Cirrhotic)** — **Choice by Renal, Cirrhosis, Drug Interactions**

### DAA Regimen Selection Guide

| Patient Profile | Preferred Regimen | Duration |
|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Treatment-Naive, Non-Cirrhotic** | **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir 8 Weeks** OR **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir 12 Weeks** | 8 / 12 Weeks |
| **Treatment-Naive, Compensated Cirrhosis** | **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir 12 Weeks** OR **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir 12 Weeks** | 12 Weeks |
| **Decompensated Cirrhosis (Child-Pugh B/C)** | **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir + Ribavirin 12 Weeks** | 12 Weeks |
| **DAA-Experienced (Treatment Failure)** | **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir/Voxilaprevir 12 Weeks** | 12 Weeks |
| **Renal Impairment (eGFR <30 / Dialysis)** | **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir** (Renal Safe) | 8/12 Weeks |
| **HIV Co-infection** | **Same as Mono-Infected** (Check Drug Interactions with ART) | Same Duration |
| **Pregnancy** | **Defer DAA Until Postpartum** | — |

> **Key**: **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir = Renal Safe, Shortest (8w)**; **Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir = Decompensated Safe (w/ RBV)**; **Vosevi = Retreatment**

### Treatment Monitoring & SVR

| Timepoint | Test | Target |
|-----------|------|--------|
| **Baseline** | HCV RNA, Genotype (Optional), Fibrosis, Renal/Hepatic Function | Baseline |
| **Week 4 (Optional)** | HCV RNA | **Rapid Virological Response (RVR) = Predictor of SVR** |
| **End of Treatment (EOT)** | HCV RNA | **Undetectable = EOT Response** |
| **12 Weeks Post-Treatment (SVR12)** | **HCV RNA** | **SVR12 = Undetectable = CURE (>95%)** |
| **24 Weeks Post-Treatment (SVR24)** | HCV RNA | **SVR24 = Confirmation (Optional)** |

> **SVR12 = Cure**; **Reinfection Possible** (Risk Reduction Counselling); **HCC Surveillance Continues if Cirrhosis**

## 5️⃣ HCV — Special Situations

### HCV in Pregnancy
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **Screening** | **Universal Anti-HCV at Booking** (Recommended by WHO/CDC) |
| **DAA Therapy** | **Contraindicated in Pregnancy** (No Safety Data); **Defer Until Postpartum** |
| **Vertical Transmission** | **~5-6%** (Higher if HIV Co-infection, High Viral Load); **No Proven Intervention** |
| **Breastfeeding** | **Safe** (If Nipples Intact); **Avoid if Cracked/Bleeding Nipples** |
| **Infant Testing** | **HCV RNA at 2-6 Months** (Anti-HCV Passive Maternal Transfer); **Confirm Chronic at 18 Months** |

### HCV/HIV Co-infection
| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **DAA Therapy** | **Same Regimens/Durations as Mono-Infected** (Check Drug Interactions with ART) |
| **Key Interactions** | **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir ↔ Rifampicin (Contraindicated); Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir ↔ Certain ART (Check Liverpool Database)** |
| **ART Regimen** | **Integrase Inhibitor Based Preferred** (DTG, BIC, RAL) — Fewest DAA Interactions |
| **Monitoring** | **SVR12 = Cure**; **HCV Reinfection Risk Higher (Continue Risk Reduction)** |

### HCV in Renal Impairment
| eGFR | Preferred Regimen |
|------|-------------------|
| **≥30** | Any Pan-Genotypic |
| **<30 / Dialysis** | **Glecaprevir/Pibrentasvir** (Renal Safe — No Dose Adjustment) |

## 5️⃣ HCV Sexual Transmission & Prevention

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Sexual Transmission** | **Low Overall**; **Higher: MSM (HIV+, Chemsex, Group Sex, Mucosal Trauma), Multiple Partners, Co-existing STIs** |
| **Prevention** | **Condoms (Reduce Risk)**, **Treat HCV (SVR = No Transmission)**, **Harm Reduction (PWID)** |
| **PEP for HCV** | **No Standard PEP** (No Proven Post-Exposure Antiviral); **Early Detection → Early Treatment** |
| **Reinfection** | **Possible After SVR** (Continued Risk Behaviours → Reinfection Rate 1-5%/Year) |

## 5. 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"HBV and HCV both have vaccines"** | **NO.** **HBV = Vaccine Available**; **HCV = NO Vaccine** |
| **"HBeAg +ve = Always Treat"** | **NO.** **Immune Tolerant (HBeAg+, High DNA, Normal ALT) = Monitor, NOT Treat** |
| **"HCV Genotype Matters for Current DAAs"** | **NO.** **Pan-Genotypic DAAs (GLE/PIB, SOF/VEL) Cover All Genotypes 1-6**; **Genotyping Optional** |
| **"HCV Vaccine Exists"** | **NO.** **NO HCV Vaccine** (High Genetic Diversity); **Only HBV Vaccine** |
| **"HCV Spontaneous Clearance = Immunity"** | **NO.** **Reinfection Possible** (No Protective Immunity); **Risk Reduction Still Needed After SVR** |
| **"HBV Vaccine = Lifelong Immunity"** | **Mostly Yes**; **Booster Not Routine**; **Anti-HBs >10 = Protected**; **Non-Responders: Repeat Series** |
| **"HCV PEP = Give DAAs"** | **NO.** **No HCV PEP**; **Early Detection (RNA at 4-12 Weeks) → Early Treatment** |
| **"All Chronic HBV Need Treatment"** | **NO.** **Immune Tolerant & Inactive Carrier = Monitor Only** |
| **"HCV Genotype 3 = Hardest to Treat"** | **Historical**; **Current Pan-Genotypic DAAs (GLE/PIB, SOF/VEL) Achieve >95% SVR in GT3** |
| **"HBV = Sexual, HCV = Blood Only"** | **NO.** **HBV = Highly Sexual (100x HIV)**; **HCV = Low Sexual Overall, BUT Higher in MSM (HIV+, Chemsex, Trauma)** |

> **Mnemonic: HEPATITIS B vs C MASTER**  
> **H**BV Serology: **HBsAg (Current), Anti-HBs (Immune), Anti-HBc (Past/Current), Anti-HBc IgM (Acute), HBeAg (Replication), DNA (Quant)**  
> **E**pidemiology: **HBV 296M, HCV 58M Chronic**  
> **P**hases HBV: **Immune Tolerant (No Tx) → Immune Active (Tx) → Inactive (Monitor) → HBeAg-Neg CHB (Tx)**  
> **A**ntivirals HBV: **TAF > TDF > ETV (High Barrier); Avoid LAM/ADV/TBV (Low Barrier)**  
> **T**reatment Indication: **Immune Active (HBeAg+, ↑ALT) + HBeAg-Neg CHB (↑ALT, DNA>2000) = Tx**  
> **I**n Pregnancy: **HBV → HBIG + Vaccine (Infant) + TAF/TDF/ETV (Mother if DNA>200k); HCV → Screen, Defer DAA**  
> **T**reatment HBV: **HBeAg+ → Seroconversion +12m; HBeAg- → Long-Term/Indefinite; Cirrhosis = Lifelong**  
> **I**mmunity: **Anti-HBs >10 = Protected; Non-Responder → Repeat Series; PEP = HBIG + Vaccine (24h/7d)**  
> **S**exual Transmission: **HBV = High (100x HIV); HCV = Low (Except MSM HIV+/Chemsex)**  
> **C**HC: **Anti-HCV + RNA = Chronic; Genotype Optional (Pan-Genotypic); SVR12 = Cure**  
> **D**AAs: **SOF/VEL 12w (All); GLE/PIB 8w (Non-Cirrhotics)/12w (Cirrhotics); SOF/VEL/VOX 12w (Retreatment)**  
> **R**enal: **GLE/PIB Renal Safe (eGFR<30); SOF/VEL Dose Adjust**  
> **H**CV in Pregnancy: **Screen Booking; DAA Contraindicated → Defer; Vertical Trans ~5-6%; Breastfeeding Safe**  
> **I**n HIV: **HBV → TDF/TAF+3TC/FTC in ART; HCV → Same DAA Regimens (Check Interactions)**  
> **S**VR: **SVR12 = Undetectable RNA 12w Post-Tx = CURE (>95%)**  
> **P**EP: **HBV: HBIG + Vaccine (24h/7d); HCV: No PEP (Early RNA → Treat)**  
> **V**accines: **HBV = Yes (Universal Birth, Risk Adults); HCV = NO Vaccine**  
> **I**nteraction Check: **GLE/PIB ↔ Rifampicin (Contra); SOF/VEL ↔ ART (Check Liverpool DB)**  
> **R**einfection: **HCV Reinfection Possible After SVR (Risk Reduction Essential)**

## 10. 📌 Summary
- **HBV Serology**: **HBsAg = Current Infection; Anti-HBs = Immunity; Anti-HBc = Past/Current; Anti-HBc IgM = Acute; HBeAg = High Replication; HBV DNA = Quantification**
- **HBV Phases**: **Immune Tolerant (HBeAg+, High DNA, Normal ALT) → NO TREATMENT**; **Immune Active (HBeAg+, ↑ALT, High DNA) → TREAT**; **Inactive Carrier (HBeAg-, Low DNA, Normal ALT) → MONITOR**; **HBeAg-Negative CHB (HBeAg-, ↑DNA >2000, ↑ALT) → TREAT**
- **HBV Treatment**: **TAF > TDF > ETV** (High Barrier); **HBeAg+ → Seroconversion + 12m Consolidation**; **HBeAg- → Long-Term/Indefinite**; **Cirrhosis = LIFELONG**
- **HBV Vaccination**: **Universal (Birth, 1, 6m)**; **Risk Adults (0,1,6m)**; **PEP: HBIG + Vaccine (≤24h/7d)**
- **HCV Natural History**: **15-45% Spontaneous Clearance**; **55-85% Chronic → Cirrhosis (15-30% in 20-30y) → HCC**
- **HCV Diagnosis**: **Anti-HCV + HCV RNA (Positive) = Chronic Infection**; **Genotype Optional (Pan-Genotypic DAAs)**
- **HCV DAA Therapy**: **Pan-Genotypic: SOF/VEL 12w (All); GLE/PIB 8w (Non-Cirrhotic) / 12w (Cirrhotic); SOF/VEL/VOX 12w (Retreatment)**; **SVR12 = CURE (>95%)**
- **HCV in Pregnancy**: **Screen at Booking**; **DAA Contraindicated → Defer Postpartum**; **Vertical Transmission ~5-6%**; **Breastfeeding Safe (If Nipples Intact)**
- **HBV/HCV in HIV**: **HBV: TDF/TAF+3TC/FTC in ART (Covers HBV); Continue NUCs**; **HCV: Same DAA Regimens (Check Drug Interactions)**
- **PEP**: **HBV: HBIG + Vaccine (Within 24h, Up to 7 Days)**; **HCV: No PEP (Early RNA Testing → Early Treatment)**
- **Sexual Transmission**: **HBV = Highly Efficient (100x HIV)**; **HCV = Low Overall, Higher in MSM (HIV+, Chemsex, Trauma)**

## 11. ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **HBV serology — marker of acute infection?**  
   A. HBsAg  B. **Anti-HBc IgM**  C. Anti-HBs  D. HBeAg  
   *Answer: B. Anti-HBc IgM = Acute/Recent Infection.*

2. **HBV phase — immune tolerant phase characteristics?**  
   A. HBeAg-, Low DNA, Normal ALT  B. **HBeAg+, High DNA, Normal ALT**  C. HBeAg-, High DNA, Elevated ALT  D. HBsAg-, Anti-HBs+  
   *Answer: B. Immune Tolerant = HBeAg+, High DNA (>10⁷), Normal ALT, No Treatment.*

3. **First-line HBV antiviral — preferred agent?**  
   A. Lamivudine  B. **Tenofovir Alafenamide (TAF)**  C. Adefovir  D. Telbivudine  
   *Answer: B. TAF > TDF > ETV (Highest Barrier, Renal/Bone Safety).*

4. **HCV diagnosis — confirmatory test for chronic infection?**  
   A. Anti-HCV  B. **HCV RNA (Quantitative PCR)**  C. Anti-HCV IgM  D. Liver Biopsy  
   *Answer: B. HCV RNA (Quantitative PCR) Positive = Chronic Infection.*

5. **Pan-genotypic DAA regimen for treatment-naive non-cirrhotic?**  
   A. SOF/VEL 8 Weeks  B. **GLE/PIB 8 Weeks (Preferred)**  C. SOF/VEL/VOX 12 Weeks  D. SOF/VEL 24 Weeks  
   *Answer: B. GLE/PIB 8 Weeks (Shortest, Renal Safe, Pan-Genotypic).*

6. **HCV SVR definition?**  
   A. RNA Negative at End of Treatment  B. **RNA Negative at 12 Weeks Post-Treatment (SVR12)**  C. RNA Negative at 24 Weeks  D. Antibody Negative  
   *Answer: B. SVR12 = HCV RNA Undetectable 12 Weeks Post-Treatment = Cure (>95%).*

7. **HCV in pregnancy — DAA therapy?**  
   A. Safe, Recommended  B. **Contraindicated → Defer Until Postpartum**  C. Only 2nd Trimester  D. Only Genotype 1  
   *Answer: B. DAA Contraindicated in Pregnancy → Defer Until Postpartum.*

7. **HBV in pregnancy — infant prophylaxis?**  
   A. HBV Vaccine Only  B. **HBIG (100 IU) + HBV Vaccine Within 24 Hours of Birth**  C. HBIG Only  D. No Prophylaxis Needed  
   *Answer: B. HBIG + HBV Vaccine Within 24 Hours of Birth (Standard of Care).*

9. **HBV/HCV PEP for needlestick from HBsAg+ source — unvaccinated HCW?**  
   A. HBV Vaccine Only  B. **HBIG (0.06 mL/kg IM) + HBV Vaccine (0, 1, 6 Months) Within 24h**  C. HBIG Only  D. No PEP Needed  
   *Answer: B. HBIG + HBV Vaccine Series (Within 24h Ideally, Up to 7 Days).*

10. **Pan-genotypic DAA for treatment-experienced (retreatment)?**  
    A. SOF/VEL 12 Weeks  B. GLE/PIB 8 Weeks  C. **SOF/VEL/VOX 12 Weeks**  D. SOF/VEL 24 Weeks  
    *Answer: C. SOF/VEL/VOX (Vosevi) 12 Weeks = Retreatment Regimen.*

## 12. 📋 SBAs (10)

1. **30M, HBsAg+, HBeAg+, HBV DNA 10⁸ IU/mL, ALT normal. Phase & management?**  
   A. Immune Active, Treat  B. **Immune Tolerant, Monitor (No Treatment)**  C. Inactive Carrier, Monitor  D. HBeAg-Neg CHB, Treat  
   *Answer: B. Immune Tolerant (HBeAg+, High DNA, Normal ALT) → No Treatment.*

2. **HCV treatment-naive, non-cirrhotic, any genotype. Preferred regimen?**  
   A. SOF/VEL 12 Weeks  B. **GLE/PIB 8 Weeks**  C. SOF/VEL/VOX 12 Weeks  D. SOF/VEL 24 Weeks  
   *Answer: B. GLE/PIB 8 Weeks (Shortest, Renal Safe, Pan-Genotypic).*

3. **Pregnant woman at 28 weeks, HBsAg+, HBV DNA 500,000 IU/mL. Management?**  
   A. No Antiviral  B. **TAF/TDF/ETV from 28-32 Weeks → 3-12 Months Postpartum**  C. Cesarean Section  D. HBIG Only  
   *Answer: B. TAF/TDF/ETV from 28-32 Weeks (If DNA >200,000) → 3-12 Months Postpartum.*

4. **HCV treatment-experienced (failed SOF/VEL), compensated cirrhosis. Retreatment?**  
   A. SOF/VEL 24 Weeks  B. **SOF/VEL/VOX 12 Weeks (Vosevi)**  C. GLE/PIB 12 Weeks  D. Interferon + Ribavirin  
   *Answer: B. SOF/VEL/VOX (Vosevi) 12 Weeks = Standard Retreatment Regimen.*

5. **Needlestick injury from HBsAg+ source. HCW unvaccinated. PEP?**  
   A. HBV Vaccine Only  B. **HBIG + HBV Vaccine (0, 1, 6 Months) Within 24h**  C. HBIG Only  D. No PEP  
   *Answer: B. HBIG + HBV Vaccine Series (Within 24h Ideally, Up to 7 Days).*
