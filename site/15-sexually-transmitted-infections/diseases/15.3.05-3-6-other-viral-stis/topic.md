## 1️⃣ Cytomegalovirus (CMV) — HHV-5

### Epidemiology & Transmission
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Seroprevalence** | **40-100%** (Age/Geography Dependent) |
| **Transmission** | **Sexual (Semen, Cervical Secretions), Vertical (Mother→Fetus), Blood/Transplant, Breast Milk, Close Contact** |
| **Sexual Transmission** | **Efficient** (Semen Shedding Common); **MSM High Risk** |
| **Reactivation** | **Latency in Myeloid Cells**; **Immunosuppression → Reactivation** |

### Clinical Manifestations

| Population | Syndrome | Features |
|------------|----------|----------|
| **Immunocompetent** | **Mononucleosis-like** (Fever, Pharyngitis, Lymphadenopathy, Hepatitis) | **Heterophile Negative** (vs EBV), Self-Limited (2-6 Weeks) |
| **Immunocompromised** (HIV CD4<50, Transplant) | **CMV Disease** (Retinitis, Colitis, Pneumonitis, Encephalitis, Polyradiculopathy) | **Sight/Life-Threatening**; **Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir/Foscarnet** |
| **Congenital CMV** (Primary Maternal Infection) | **Symptomatic (10-15%)**: Petechiae, Hepatosplenomegaly, Jaundice, **Sensorineural Hearing Loss**, Microcephaly, Seizures, Chorioretinitis | **Leading Infectious Cause of Hearing Loss**; **Asymptomatic (85-90%) → Late-Onset Hearing Loss** |
| **Perinatal CMV** | Usually Asymptomatic; **Premature/Immunocompromised → Pneumonitis, Hepatitis** | Breast Milk Transmission (Freeze/HTST Pasteurisation Reduces Risk) |

### Diagnosis

| Test | Sample | Utility |
|------|--------|---------|
| **CMV PCR (Quantitative)** | Blood, Urine, CSF, Amniotic Fluid, Tissue | **Gold Standard** (Viral Load, Diagnosis, Monitoring) |
| **CMV IgM/IgG** | Serum | **IgM = Acute/Recent; IgG = Past** (Low Specificity for Reactivation) |
| **CMV Antigenemia (pp65)** | Blood | **Rapid, Quantitative** (Transplant Monitoring) |
| **Viral Culture** | Urine, Saliva, Blood | Slow (Weeks), Low Sensitivity |
| **Histopathology** | Tissue | **Owl's Eye Inclusion Bodies** (Pathognomonic) |

### Management

| Scenario | Treatment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Immunocompetent Mononucleosis** | **Supportive** (Antipyretics, Hydration, Avoid Contact Sports if Splenomegaly) |
| **Immunocompromised (Retinitis, Colitis, Pneumonitis)** | **IV Ganciclovir 5mg/kg q12h × 2-3 Weeks** → **Oral Valganciclovir 900mg BD × 8 Weeks** (Maintenance) |
| **Resistant CMV** | **Foscarnet 60mg/kg IV q8h** OR **Cidofovir 5mg/kg IV Weekly** |
| **Congenital CMV (Symptomatic)** | **Valganciclovir 16mg/kg BD × 6 Months** (Improves Hearing/Developmental Outcomes) |
| **Prevention (Transplant)** | **Prophylaxis (Valganciclovir 900mg OD × 3-6 Months)** OR **Pre-emptive (PCR-Guided)** |

## 2️⃣ Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) — HHV-4

### Epidemiology & Transmission
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Seroprevalence** | **>90% Adults** |
| **Transmission** | **Saliva (Kissing, "Kissing Disease")**, Sexual (Semen, Cervical Secretions), Blood/Transplant |
| **Latency** | **B-Cells (Latency Programs I-III)**; **Reactivation in Immunosuppression** |

### Clinical Manifestations

| Syndrome | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Infectious Mononucleosis (IM)** | **Fever, Pharyngitis (Exudative), Lymphadenopathy (Posterior Cervical), Splenomegaly (50%), Hepatitis, Atypical Lymphocytosis (>10%)** |
| **Chronic Active EBV (CAEBV)** | Persistent IM >6 Months, High EBV DNA, Hepatosplenomegaly, Cytopenias, Organ Infiltration |
| **EBV-Associated Malignancies** | **Burkitt Lymphoma** (Endemic, t(8;14)), **Nasopharyngeal Carcinoma** (EBV+, EBV LMP1), **Hodgkin Lymphoma** (RS Cells EBV+), **Post-Transplant Lymphoproliferative Disorder (PTLD)**, **Gastric Carcinoma (10%)** |

### Diagnosis

| Test | Sample | Utility |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Heterophile Antibody (Monospot)** | Serum | **Rapid, IM Screening** (Sensitivity 70-90%, Specificity 90%) |
| **EBV-Specific Serology** | Serum | **VCA IgM (Acute), VCA IgG (Past), EBNA IgG (Past/Convalescent), EA-D IgG (Acute/Reactivation)** |
| **EBV PCR** | Blood, CSF, Tissue | **Quantitative Viral Load** (PTLD, CAEBV, Immunocompromised Monitoring) |

### Management

| Scenario | Treatment |
|----------|-----------|
| **IM** | **Supportive** (Antipyretics, Hydration, Corticosteroids if Airway Obstruction/Severe Thrombocytopenia), **Avoid Contact Sports 4-6 Weeks** (Splenic Rupture Risk) |
| **CAEBV / PTLD** | **Rituximab (Anti-CD20)**, **Chemotherapy**, **Antivirals (Acyclovir/Valacyclovir/Ganciclovir — Limited Efficacy)** |
| **Malignancies** | **Standard Oncology Protocols** (R-CHOP, Radiotherapy, etc.) |

## 3️⃣ Zika Virus — Flaviviridae

### Epidemiology & Transmission
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Vector** | **Aedes aegypti/albopictus** (Day-Biting) |
| **Sexual Transmission** | **Confirmed (Semen, Vaginal Secretions)**; **Semen Shedding Up to 6 Months** |
| **Vertical Transmission** | **Transplacental** — **Congenital Zika Syndrome** |
| **Other** | Blood Transfusion, Laboratory Exposure |

### Clinical Manifestations

| Syndrome | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Acute Zika** | **Mild** (Fever, Maculopapular Rash, Arthralgia, Conjunctivitis, Myalgia, Headache) — **Self-Limited (2-7 Days)** |
| **Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)** | **Post-Infectious (1-3 Weeks)**, Acute Flaccid Paralysis, Areflexia, Albuminocytological Dissociation |
| **Congenital Zika Syndrome (CZS)** | **Microcephaly**, **Cerebral Calcifications**, **Ventriculomegaly**, **Arthrogryposis**, **Ocular Abnormalities**, **Hypertonia**, **Hearing Loss** |

### Diagnosis

| Test | Sample | Timing |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Zika PCR (NAAT)** | Blood, Urine, Semen, CSF, Amniotic Fluid | **Acute (Blood: Days 1-7, Urine: Days 1-14, Semen: Months)** |
| **Zika IgM/IgG** | Serum | **Convalescent (Week 2+)**; **Cross-Reactivity with Dengue/Other Flaviviruses** |
| **PRNT** | Serum | **Confirmatory (Specific Neutralising Antibodies)** |

### Management

| Scenario | Treatment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Acute Zika** | **Supportive** (Antipyretics, Hydration, Avoid NSAIDs if Dengue Possible) |
| **GBS** | **IVIG 2g/kg over 5 Days** OR **Plasma Exchange**; **ICU Support** |
| **Congenital Zika Syndrome** | **Multidisciplinary** (Neurology, Ophthalmology, Orthopaedics, Early Intervention); **No Specific Antiviral** |
| **Pregnancy** | **Avoid Travel to Endemic Areas**; **Condoms/Abstinence if Partner Exposed**; **Serial Ultrasound (Microcephaly, Calcifications)** |

### Zika Sexual Transmission Prevention
| Recommendation | Duration |
|----------------|----------|
| **Men (Symptomatic/Exposed)** | **Condoms/Abstinence × 3 Months** (Semen Shedding Up to 6 Months) |
| **Women (Symptomatic/Exposed)** | **Condoms/Abstinence × 2 Months** |
| **Preconception** | **Wait 3 Months (Men) / 2 Months (Women) After Exposure/Symptoms** |

## 4️⃣ Human T-lymphotropic Virus (HTLV-1/2) — Retroviridae

### Epidemiology & Transmission
| Aspect | HTLV-1 | HTLV-2 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Endemic Areas** | **Japan, Caribbean, South America, Africa, Middle East, Melanesia** | **Americas (Native Americans, PWID)** |
| **Transmission** | **Sexual, Vertical (Breast Milk), Blood/Transplant, Needle Sharing** | **Similar** |
| **Seroprevalence** | **10-20 Million** | **Lower** |

### Clinical Manifestations

| Syndrome | HTLV-1 | HTLV-2 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **Adult T-Cell Leukemia/Lymphoma (ATL)** | **Aggressive T-Cell Malignancy** (Leukemic, Lymphoma, Chronic, Smoldering) | **Rare/Not Associated** |
| **HTLV-1-Associated Myelopathy/Tropical Spastic Paraparesis (HAM/TSP)** | **Chronic Progressive Spastic Paraparesis**, **Sensory Loss**, **Urinary Dysfunction**, **Erectile Dysfunction** | **Milder/Similar (HAM/TSP-Like)** |
| **Other** | **Uveitis, Infective Dermatitis, Bronchiectasis, Sjögren-Like** | **Mild Neurological, Cutaneous** |

### Diagnosis

| Test | Utility |
|------|---------|
| **HTLV-1/2 Antibody (EIA/ELISA)** | **Screening** |
| **Western Blot / Line Immunoassay** | **Confirmatory (Type-Specific)** |
| **HTLV PCR (Proviral DNA)** | **Quantitative Proviral Load** (Risk Stratification for ATL/HAM) |

### Management

| Syndrome | Treatment |
|----------|-----------|
| **ATL** | **LG15/SG/CHOP/EMC** (Chemotherapy), **Allogeneic SCT** (Younger), **Zidovudine + IFN-α** (Induction) |
| **HAM/TSP** | **Corticosteroids (Pulse), Danazol, IFN-α, Mogamulizumab (Anti-CCR4)** — **Symptomatic (Baclofen, Gabapentin, Physiotherapy)** |
| **Prevention** | **Screen Blood/Organ Donors**, **Avoid Breastfeeding (HTLV-1+ Mothers)**, **Condoms, Safe Needles** |

## 5️⃣ Other Emerging Viral STIs

### Hepatitis E (HEV) — Rare Sexual Transmission
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Transmission** | **Faecal-Oral (Water, Undercooked Meat), Zoonotic (Pigs), Blood, Vertical**; **Rare Sexual (Semen Detected)** |
| **Clinical** | **Acute Hepatitis** (Self-Limited); **Chronic in Immunocompromised (GT3/4)** |
| **Pregnancy** | **GT1: High Mortality (20-30%) in 3rd Trimester** |
| **Treatment** | **Ribavirin (Chronic), Pegylated IFN-α**; **Supportive (Acute)** |

### SARS-CoV-2 — Rare Sexual Transmission
| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Transmission** | **Respiratory (Primary)**; **Semen/Faeces RNA Detected** — **Sexual Transmission Theoretical/Rare** |
| **Clinical** | **COVID-19** (Respiratory, Systemic, Long COVID) |
| **Sexual Precautions** | **Condoms/Masks if Partner Infected/Exposed** |

### Human Herpesvirus 6/7 (HHV-6/7)
| Virus | Syndrome |
|-------|----------|
| **HHV-6A/6B** | **Roseola Infantum (Exanthem Subitum)** — High Fever 3-5 Days → Defervescence → Maculopapular Rash; **Reactivation: Encephalitis, Bone Marrow Suppression (Transplant)** |
| **HHV-7** | **Roseola (Similar), Pityriasis Rosea (Associated)**, Reactivation in Immunocompromised |

## 5. 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"CMV = Only Congenital"** | **NO.** **Immunocompromised = Retinitis, Colitis, Pneumonitis, Encephalitis**; **Immunocompetent = Mononucleosis-like** |
| **"EBV = Only Mono"** | **NO.** **Malignancies: Burkitt, NPC, Hodgkin, PTLD, Gastric**; **CAEBV, PTLD** |
| **"Zika = Only Vector-Borne"** | **NO.** **Sexual Transmission Confirmed (Semen Up to 6 Months); Men Condoms 3m, Women 2m** |
| **"HTLV-1 = Only ATL"** | **NO.** **HAM/TSP (Spastic Paraparesis) Also Major**; **Uveitis, Dermatitis, Bronchiectasis** |
| **"HTLV-1 = HTLV-2"** | **NO.** **HTLV-1 = ATL + HAM/TSP; HTLV-2 = No ATL, Milder HAM/TSP** |
| **"HEV = Only Foodborne"** | **NO.** **Rare Sexual (Semen); Chronic in Immunocomp (Ribavirin); Pregnancy GT1 = High Mortality** |
| **"SARS-CoV-2 = Sexual STI"** | **NO.** **Respiratory Primary; Semen RNA Detected But Sexual Transmission Rare/Theoretical** |
| **"HHV-6 = Only Roseola"** | **NO.** **Reactivation in Transplant = Encephalitis, Bone Marrow Suppression** |

- **Zika**: **Aedes Vector**, **Sexual (Semen Up to 6 Months)** → **Men: Condoms/Abstinence × 3 Months, Women: × 2 Months**; **Congenital Zika Syndrome (Microcephaly, Calcifications, Arthrogryposis, Ocular, Hypertonia, Hearing Loss)**; **GBS Post-Infectious**
- **HTLV-1**: **Endemic Japan/Caribbean/South America/Africa**; **Sexual, Vertical (Breast Milk), Blood**; **ATL (Aggressive T-Cell Lymphoma), HAM/TSP (Spastic Paraparesis)**; **Prevention: Screen Blood/Donors, Avoid Breastfeeding, Condoms**

- **HEV**: **Faecal-Oral, Zoonotic (Pigs), Rare Sexual**; **GT1 Pregnancy = High Mortality (20-30%)**; **Chronic in Immunocompromised → Ribavirin**
- **HHV-6/7**: **Roseola Infantum (HHV-6B)**, **Reactivation in Transplant → Encephalitis, Bone Marrow Suppression**
- **SARS-CoV-2**: **Respiratory Primary**, **Rare Sexual Transmission** (Semen RNA Detected)
