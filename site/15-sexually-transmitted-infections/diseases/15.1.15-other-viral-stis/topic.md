## 1️⃣ Zika Virus (ZIKV)

### Virology & Transmission
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Virus** | **Flavivirus** (Arbovirus); **RNA, Positive-Sense**; Related to Dengue, Yellow Fever, West Nile |
| **Primary Vector** | **Aedes aegypti / Aedes albopictus** (Day-Biting Mosquitoes) |
| **Sexual Transmission** | **Confirmed (Male→Female, Male→Male, Female→Male)**; **Viral RNA in Semen Prolonged (Months)**; **Vaginal Fluid, Saliva Also** |
| **Vertical Transmission** | **Transplacental (All Trimesters); Perinatal; Breastfeeding (Low Risk)** |
| **Other** | **Blood Transfusion, Lab Exposure** |
| **Incubation** | **3-14 Days** (Typically 3-7 Days) |

### Clinical Features
| Syndrome | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Asymptomatic** | **~80% Infections** |
| **Zika Fever (Symptomatic)** | **Low-Grade Fever, Maculopapular Rash (Pruritic), Conjunctivitis (Non-Purulent), Arthralgia (Small Joints), Myalgia, Headache, Retro-Orbital Pain**; **Self-Limited 2-7 Days** |
| **Neurological** | **Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS) — ↑ Incidence During Outbreaks**; **Meningitis, Encephalitis, Myelitis (Rare)** |
| **Congenital Zika Syndrome (CZS)** | **Microcephaly (Severe), Cortical Malformations, Ventriculomegaly, Ocular Abnormalities (Chorioretinal Atrophy, Optic Nerve Hypoplasia), Congenital Contractures (Arthrogryposis), Hypertonia, Seizures, Hearing Loss** |

### Diagnosis
| Test | Specimen | Timing | Interpretation |
|------|----------|--------|----------------|
| **RT-PCR (ZIKV RNA)** | Serum, Urine, Semen, CSF, Amniotic Fluid, Placenta | **Serum: <7 Days; Urine: <21 Days; Semen: Months** | **Positive = Acute/Recent Infection** |
| **IgM Antibody (ELISA)** | Serum, CSF | **≥4 Days Post-Onset** | **Positive = Recent; Cross-Reactivity with Dengue/Other Flaviviruses (PRNT Needed)** |
| **PRNT (Plaque Reduction Neutralisation Test)** | Serum | **Confirmatory** | **Specific Neutralising Antibodies; Differentiates ZIKV from Dengue** |

> **WHO Algorithm**: **RT-PCR First (Acute); If Negative >7 Days OR Convalescent → IgM + PRNT**

### Sexual Transmission — Key Evidence
- **Male→Female**: Documented; Semen RNA Detectable **Mean 34 Days (Up to 370 Days)**; Infectious Virus Cultured ≤69 Days
- **Male→Male**: Anal Sex Transmission Documented
- **Female→Male**: Vaginal Fluid RNA Detectable; Transmission Documented
- **Asymptomatic Sexual Transmission**: Documented

### Prevention & Counselling
| Population | Recommendation |
|------------|----------------|
| **Pregnant Women / Couples Planning Pregnancy** | **Avoid Travel to Active ZIKV Areas**; **Condoms or Abstinence Throughout Pregnancy if Partner Exposed** |
| **Men with ZIKV Exposure (Symptomatic/Asymptomatic)** | **Condoms/Abstinence ≥3 Months** (WHO) / **≥6 Months** (CDC Conservative) |
| **Women with ZIKV Exposure** | **Condoms/Abstinence ≥2 Months** |
| **Blood Donation** | **Deferral 28-120 Days Post-Exposure/Recovery** |
| **Mosquito Bite Prevention** | **DEET, Picaridin, Permethrin Clothing, Screens, Eliminate Breeding Sites** |

### Treatment
- **Supportive Only** (Antipyretics, Fluids, Rest); **Avoid NSAIDs Until Dengue Ruled Out (Bleeding Risk)**
- **GBS**: IVIG / Plasmapheresis (Standard)
- **CZS**: Multidisciplinary Supportive Care (No Specific Antiviral)

## 2️⃣ Hepatitis A Virus (HAV) — Sexual Transmission in MSM

### Virology & Transmission
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Virus** | **Picornavirus (Hepatovirus)**; **RNA, Positive-Sense**; **Non-Enveloped, Stable in Environment** |
| **Primary Route** | **Faecal-Oral** (Contaminated Food/Water, Person-to-Person) |
| **Sexual Transmission** | **Anal-Oral Practices (Rimming), Digital-Anal Contact, Sex Toys** — **Major Driver in MSM Outbreaks** |
| **Incubation** | **15-50 Days** (Average 28 Days) |
| **Infectious Period** | **2 Weeks Pre-Jaundice → 1 Week Post-Jaundice** |

### MSM Outbreaks (Global, 2016-2024)
- **Large Outbreaks in Europe, Americas, Australia** — **Genotype IA/IB**
- **Risk Factors**: **Multiple Partners, Sex-on-Premises Venues, Chemsex, Recreational Drugs, HIV+**
- **Co-infections Common**: **STIs (GC, CT, Syphilis, HIV, HCV)**

### Clinical Features
| Presentation | Features |
|--------------|----------|
| **Asymptomatic** | **Common in Children (<70% <6y Symptomatic); Less in Adults** |
| **Acute Hepatitis** | **Fever, Malaise, Anorexia, Nausea, RUQ Pain, Dark Urine, Pale Stools, Jaundice**; **Self-Limited 2-8 Weeks** |
| **Complications** | **Fulminant Hepatitis (Rare ~0.1-0.3%); Relapsing/Cholestatic Course (10-15%)** |

### Diagnosis
| Test | Interpretation |
|------|----------------|
| **Anti-HAV IgM** | **Acute/Recent Infection (Positive 5-10 Days Post-Exposure, Persists 3-6 Months)** |
| **Anti-HAV IgG (Total)** | **Past Infection or Vaccination (Lifelong Immunity)** |
| **HAV RNA PCR** | **Research/Outbreak Investigation; Not Routine** |

### Management
- **Supportive** (Rest, Hydration, Avoid Hepatotoxins); **No Antiviral**
- **Hospitalisation** if **Fulminant Signs (Coagulopathy, Encephalopathy)** or **Severe Dehydration**
- **Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (Contacts)**: **HAV Vaccine (1 Dose) ± Human Normal Immunoglobulin (HNIG) if High Risk / Immunocompromised / Chronic Liver Disease**

### Prevention — Vaccination
| Vaccine | Schedule | Target |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Inactivated HAV Vaccine (Havrix, Vaqta, Avaxim)** | **2 Doses (0, 6-12 Months)** | **All Children (Routine), MSM, PWID, Chronic Liver Disease, Travelers, HIV+, Occupational Risk** |
| **Combined HAV/HBV (Twinrix)** | **3 Doses (0, 1, 6 Months) or Accelerated (0, 7, 21 Days + 12m)** | **Adults Needing Both** |

> **MSM = Priority Group for HAV Vaccination (Outbreak Control)**

## 3️⃣ Hepatitis E Virus (HEV)

### Virology & Transmission
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Virus** | **Hepevirus (Orthohepevirus A)**; **RNA, Positive-Sense**; **Genotypes 1-4 (Human)** |
| **Genotype 1 & 2** | **Human Only; Faecal-Oral (Waterborne); Developing Countries; Acute Epidemics** |
| **Genotype 3 & 4** | **Zoonotic (Pigs, Deer, Boar); Foodborne (Undercooked Pork/Offal); Developed Countries; Chronic in Immunocompromised** |
| **Sexual Transmission** | **Possible (Anal-Oral, MSM Outbreaks Reported); Blood Transfusion Also** |
| **Incubation** | **15-60 Days** (Average 40 Days) |

### Clinical Syndromes
| Syndrome | Population | Features |
|----------|------------|----------|
| **Acute Hepatitis E** | **General (GT1/2/3/4)** | **Similar to HAV; Jaundice, Malaise, RUQ Pain; Self-Limited 2-6 Weeks** |
| **Severe in Pregnancy (GT1/2)** | **Pregnant Women (3rd Trimester)** | **Fulminant Hepatitis, Maternal Mortality 10-25%; Vertical Transmission High** |
| **Chronic HEV (GT3/4)** | **Immunocompromised (Transplant, HIV CD4<200, Chemo, Steroids)** | **Persistent Viraemia >3 Months → Rapid Fibrosis → Cirrhosis** |
| **Extrahepatic** | **All** | **Neurological (Guillain-Barré, Neuralgic Amyotrophy, Encephalitis); Renal (Membranous GN); Haematologic** |

### Diagnosis
| Test | Interpretation |
|------|----------------|
| **Anti-HEV IgM** | **Acute Infection (Positive ~4 Weeks Post-Exposure, Persists 3-6 Months)** |
| **Anti-HEV IgG** | **Past Infection (Persistence Variable)** |
| **HEV RNA PCR (Stool/Serum)** | **Viraemia (Acute/Chronic); Genotyping Possible** |

### Management
| Scenario | Treatment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Acute (Immunocompetent)** | **Supportive; No Antiviral** |
| **Pregnancy (Severe/3rd Trimester)** | **Supportive ICU; Ribavirin Contraindicated (Teratogenic); Consider Early Delivery** |
| **Chronic (Immunocompromised)** | **Reduce Immunosuppression if Possible**; **Ribavirin 600-1000mg/Day × 3 Months (Off-Label, Monitor Haemolysis)**; **PegIFN-α (Transplant) — Risk Rejection** |

### Prevention
- **Sanitation, Safe Water (GT1/2)**
- **Thorough Cooking of Pork/Offal (GT3/4)**
- **Vaccine (Hecolin® / p239) — Licensed China Only; Efficacy >95% (GT1/4); Not WHO Prequalified Globally**
- **Blood Screening (Some Countries)**

## 4️⃣ Human Herpesvirus 8 (HHV-8) / Kaposi Sarcoma Herpesvirus (KSHV)

### Virology & Transmission
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Virus** | **Gammaherpesvirus (Rhadinovirus)**; **dsDNA**; **Latency in B Cells, Endothelial Cells** |
| **Transmission** | **Sexual (MSM — Saliva, Semen); Vertical (Mother→Child); Blood/Transfusion; Transplant; Saliva (Household in Endemic Areas)** |
| **Epidemiology** | **High in Sub-Saharan Africa, Mediterranean, MSM Populations; Low in General Western Populations** |
| **HIV Association** | **Strong — HHV-8 Seroprevalence 30-50% in HIV+ MSM vs <5% General** |

### Disease Associations
| Disease | Pathogenesis | Clinical Features |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Kaposi Sarcoma (KS)** | **HHV-8 Infected Endothelial/Spindle Cells → VEGF, Cytokines → Angioproliferation** | **Cutaneous (Violaceous Macules/Plaques/Nodules), Mucosal (Oral, GI, Pulmonary); Lymphoedema; HIV+ = More Aggressive/Disseminated** |
| **Multicentric Castleman Disease (MCD)** | **HHV-8 Infected B Cells (Plasmablasts) → IL-6, vIL-6 → Lymphoproliferation** | **Generalised Lymphadenopathy, Fever, Splenomegaly, Cytopenias, Hypergammaglobulinaemia, POEMS-like; HIV+ Associated** |
| **Primary Effusion Lymphoma (PEL)** | **HHV-8+ EBV+ B-Cell Lymphoma in Body Cavities** | **Pleural/Pericardial/Peritoneal Effusions (No Mass); HIV+; Poor Prognosis** |
| **KSHV Inflammatory Cytokine Syndrome (KICS)** | **vIL-6, Cytokine Storm** | **Fever, Cytopenias, Splenomegaly, Oedema, Hypoalbuminaemia; HIV+** |

### Diagnosis
| Test | Use |
|------|-----|
| **HHV-8 PCR (Blood, Lesion, Effusion)** | **Viral Load, Disease Monitoring** |
| **HHV-8 Serology (LANA-1 IgG)** | **Exposure (Latent Infection); High in Endemic/MSM/HIV** |
| **KS Biopsy (Histology + LANA-1 IHC)** | **Definitive (Spindle Cells, Slit-Like Vessels, LANA-1+)** |
| **MCD/PEL Biopsy** | **Plasmablasts (MCD) / Large B Cells (PEL) + HHV-8+** |

### Management
| Disease | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|
| **KS (HIV+)** | **ART (Immune Reconstitution = Regression); Local (Radiation, Intralesional Vinblastine, Topical Alitretinoin); Systemic (Liposomal Doxorubicin, Paclitaxel) if Visceral/Disseminated/Symptomatic** |
| **KS (HIV- / Classic / Endemic / Transplant)** | **Reduce Immunosuppression (Transplant); Local/Systemic as Above** |
| **MCD** | **Rituximab (Anti-CD20) — 1st Line (Depletes HHV-8+ B Cells); +/- Chemo (CHOP) if Severe; IL-6 Blockade (Siltuximab/Tocilizumab) Refractory** |
| **PEL** | **CHOP/EPOCH + Rituximab; ART if HIV+; Poor Prognosis (Median OS <1 Year)** |

## 5️⃣ SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19) — Sexual Transmission Potential

### Evidence for Genital/Sexual Involvement
| Finding | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| **Viral RNA in Semen** | **Detected in ~10-20% Acute/Convalescent Men (Up to 30-60 Days Post-Onset); Infectious Virus Rarely Cultured** |
| **Testicular Involvement** | **ACE2/TMPRSS2 Expression in Testes (Spermatogonia, Leydig, Sertoli); Orchitis Rare; Temporary Sperm Quality Decline** |
| **Vaginal Fluid RNA** | **Detected in Some Women (Lower Frequency/Duration Than Semen)** |
| **Sexual Transmission** | **Theoretical (RNA ≠ Infectious Virus); No Confirmed Sexual Transmission Cases; Close Contact/Respiratory Dominant** |
| **Erectile Dysfunction** | **Post-COVID Sequela (Vascular, Endothelial, Psychological); PDE5i Responsive** |

### Practical Guidance
- **Not Classified as STI**; **Standard Respiratory Precautions Sufficient**
- **Condoms Not Required for COVID Prevention Alone** (But for Other STIs)
- **Fertility**: **Temporary Sperm Parameters Decline (3-6 Months Recovery); No Long-Term Infertility Evidence**

## 6️⃣ Ebola Virus Disease (EVD) — Sexual Persistence

### Key Facts
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Virus** | **Filovirus (Zaire ebolavirus)**; **RNA, Negative-Sense** |
| **Sexual Persistence** | **RNA in Semen Detectable Months to Years Post-Recovery (Median ~115 Days; Max >500 Days)** |
| **Infectious Virus in Semen** | **Cultured Up to ~180-500 Days Post-Onset** |
| **Sexual Transmission** | **Documented (Survivor→Partner); Rare but Critical for Outbreak Resurgence** |
| **Guidance (WHO)** | **Male Survivors: Condoms or Abstinence Until **2 Consecutive Negative Semen RT-PCR (Weekly Interval)** OR **≥12 Months Post-Onset if Testing Unavailable** |
| **Breast Milk** | **RNA Detected; Avoid Breastfeeding Until Negative PCR** |

## 7️⃣ Cytomegalovirus (CMV) — Sexual Transmission

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Virus** | **Betaherpesvirus (HHV-5)**; **dsDNA** |
| **Transmission** | **Sexual (Semen, Cervical Secretions, Saliva, Urine, Breast Milk); Vertical (Congenital Major); Transfusion/Transplant** |
| **Seroprevalence** | **~50-90% Adults (Age/SES Dependent)** |
| **Primary Infection in Pregnancy** | **~30-40% Vertical Transmission Congenital CMV (Sensorineural Hearing Loss, Neurodevelopmental Delay, Microcephaly)** |
| **Reactivation** | **Common in Immunocompromised (CMV Syndrome, End-Organ Disease: Retinitis, Colitis, Pneumonitis); HIV CD4<50** |
| **Diagnosis** | **IgM + IgG Avidity (Primary); PCR (Viraemia/Quantitative — Monitor Immunocompromised)** |
| **Treatment** | **Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir (Immunocompromised); Letermovir (Prophylaxis Transplant); No Routine Tx Immunocompetent** |

## 5. 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"Zika = Only Mosquito Transmission"** | **NO.** **Sexual Transmission WELL DOCUMENTED (Semen Months); Vertical Transmission Major Concern** |
| **"HAV = Food/Water Only"** | **NO.** **MSM Outbreaks Driven by Sexual Practices (Anal-Oral); Vaccination Critical for MSM** |
| **"HEV = Only Pregnancy Risk"** | **NO.** **GT3/4 Chronic Infection in Immunocompromised = Rapid Cirrhosis; Ribavirin Effective** |
| **"HHV-8 = Only KS"** | **NO.** **Also MCD, PEL, KICS; All HHV-8 Driven B-Cell/Endothelial Proliferation** |
| **"COVID-19 = STI Because Semen RNA"** | **NO.** **RNA ≠ Infectious Virus; No Confirmed Sexual Transmission; Classified as Respiratory/Close Contact** |
| **"Ebola = Only Acute Outbreak Disease"** | **NO.** **Sexual Persistence in Survivors = Risk of Outbreak Resurgence (Months-Years Later)** |
| **"CMV = Only Congenital Problem"** | **NO.** **Major Pathogen in Immunocompromised (Retinitis, Colitis, Pneumonitis); Reactivation Common** |
| **"Zika IgM = Definitive Diagnosis"** | **NO.** **Cross-Reactivity with Dengue/Other Flaviviruses HIGH; PRNT Required for Confirmation** |

| **"HEV Vaccine = Globally Available"** | **NO.** **Hecolin® (p239) = Licensed CHINA ONLY; Not WHO Prequalified; Not Available Globally** |

## 10. 📌 Summary
- **Zika**: **Flavivirus, Mosquito (Aedes) + Sexual (Semen Months); 80% Asymptomatic; Congenital Zika Syndrome (Microcephaly, Ocular, Contractures, Hypertonia, Seizures); GBS; RT-PCR (Serum<7d, Urine<21d, Semen Months); IgM+PRNT; Prevention: Condoms 3m (M), 2m (W); Avoid Pregnancy Travel**
- **Hepatitis A**: **Picornavirus, Faecal-Oral + Sexual (Anal-Oral MSM Outbreaks); Acute Self-Limited Hepatitis; Anti-HAV IgM = Acute; Inactivated Vaccine 2-Dose (0, 6-12m) — MSM PRIORITY; PEP: Vaccine ± HNIG**
- **Hepatitis E**: **GT1/2 Waterborne (Pregnancy: Fulminant, Mortality 10-25%); GT3/4 Zoonotic Pork (Chronic in Immunocompromised → Rapid Cirrhosis); Anti-HEV IgM Acute; Chronic Tx: Reduce Immunosupp + Ribavirin 3m; Vaccine China Only**
- **HHV-8/KSHV**: **Gammaherpesvirus, Sexual (MSM Saliva/Semen); KS (Cutaneous/Mucosal/Visceral — ART Regresses, Liposomal Doxorubicin), MCD (Lymphadenopathy, IL-6 — Rituximab), PEL (Effusions — CHOP+Rituximab); HIV Strong Association; LANA-1 IHC Diagnostic**
- **COVID-19**: **SARS-CoV-2 RNA in Semen 10-20% (Up to 60 Days); Testicular ACE2 Expression; NOT Classified as STI (No Confirmed Sexual Transmission); Respiratory Dominant**
- **Ebola**: **Semen RNA Persistence Months-Years (Median 115d, Max >500d); Infectious Virus Cultured ~180-500d; Sexual Transmission Documented; Survivor Guidance: Condoms/Abstinence Until 2 Negative Semen PCR (Weekly) OR ≥12 Months Post-Onset**
- **CMV**: **Betaherpesvirus; Sexual, Vertical, Transfusion; Congenital (Primary Pregnancy → Sensorineural Hearing Loss, Neurodevelopmental Delay); Immunocompromised Reactivation (Retinitis, Colitis, Pneumonitis); Treatment: Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir/Letermovir**

## 11. ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **Zika Virus — Primary Vector and Sexual Transmission Confirmation?**  
   A. Anopheles, No Sexual Transmission  B. **Aedes aegypti/albopictus, Sexual Transmission CONFIRMED (Semen Months)**  C. Culex, Theoretical Only  D. Aedes, Sexual Transmission Not Documented  
   *Answer: B. Aedes Mosquitoes + Sexual Transmission Well Documented (Semen RNA Prolonged, Infectious Virus Cultured).*

2. **Hepatitis A MSM Outbreaks — Primary Sexual Risk Practice?**  
   A. Vaginal Intercourse  B. **Anal-Oral (Rimming)**  C. Oral-Genital  D. Mutual Masturbation  
   *Answer: B. Anal-Oral Contact (Rimming) = Major Driver of Faecal-Oral Sexual HAV Transmission in MSM.*

3. **Hepatitis E — Which Genotype Causes Chronic Infection in Immunocompromised?**  
   A. GT1  B. GT2  C. **GT3/4**  D. All Equal  
   *Answer: C. GT3/4 (Zoonotic, Pork) Cause Chronic HEV in Immunocompromised (Transplant, HIV, Steroids).*

4. **HHV-8 Associated Diseases — Which is NOT Associated?**  
   A. Kaposi Sarcoma  B. Multicentric Castleman Disease  C. Primary Effusion Lymphoma  D. **Burkitt Lymphoma**  
   *Answer: D. Burkitt Lymphoma = EBV (HHV-4) Associated, Not HHV-8.*

5. **Kaposi Sarcoma in HIV+ Patient — 1st Line Management?**  
   A. Liposomal Doxorubicin  B. Radiation  C. **ART (Immune Reconstitution)**  D. Rituximab  
   *Answer: C. ART = 1st Line for HIV-Associated KS (Immune Reconstitution Induces Regression).*

6. **SARS-CoV-2 Sexual Transmission — Current Classification?**  
   A. Confirmed STI  B. **Not Classified as STI (No Confirmed Sexual Transmission)**  C. Probable STI  D. Theoretical Only  
   *Answer: B. RNA in Semen ≠ Infectious Virus; No Confirmed Sexual Transmission Cases; Respiratory Dominant.*

7. **Ebola Virus — Semen PCR Persistence & Survivor Guidance?**  
   A. Weeks, No Restrictions  B. **Months-Years (Median 115d); Condoms/Abstinence Until 2 Neg PCR or 12 Months**  C. Days, 30-Days Abstinence  D. Not Detected in Semen  
   *Answer: B. Semen RNA Median 115 Days (Max >500d); Infectious Virus Cultured ~500d; WHO: 2 Neg Semen PCR or 12 Months.*

8. **Congenital CMV — Most Common Sequela?**  
   A. Microcephaly  B. **Sensorineural Hearing Loss**  C. Chorioretinitis  D. Hydrocephalus  
   *Answer: B. Sensorineural Hearing Loss = Most Common Sequela of Congenital CMV (Can Be Progressive/Late-Onset).*

9. **Zika Prevention — Male Partner Exposed, Couple Planning Pregnancy?**  
   A. Condoms 1 Month  B. **Condoms/Abstinence 3 Months (WHO) / 6 Months (CDC)**  C. No Restrictions  D. Vaccination Available  
   *Answer: B. WHO: 3 Months; CDC Conservative: 6 Months for Men with Zika Exposure.*

10. **HEV Vaccine — Global Availability?**  
    A. WHO Prequalified, Global  B. **Licensed China Only (Hecolin®/p239), Not Global**  C. Europe/US Approved  D. In Development Only  
    *Answer: B. Hecolin® (p239) Licensed in China Only (Efficacy >95% GT1/4); Not WHO Prequalified/Globally Available.*
