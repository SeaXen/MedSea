```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Neisseria gonorrhoeae] --> B[Gram-Negative Diplococcus]
    B --> C[Obligate Human Pathogen]
    B --> D[Virulence Factors]
    D --> D1[Pili (Adherence, Antigenic Variation)]
    D --> D2[Opa Proteins (Invasion, Immune Evasion)]
```

## 1️⃣ Gonorrhoea — Clinical Manifestations

### Epidemiology
| Parameter | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Global Incidence** | **~82 Million** New Cases/Year (WHO 2023) |
| **Prevalence** | **0.9%** (Women 15-49), **0.7%** (Men 15-49) |
| **Age Peak** | **15-24 Years** (Highest Incidence) |
| **Asymptomatic Rate** | **10-20% Men**, **50-80% Women**, **>90% Pharyngeal/Rectal** |
| **Transmission** | Sexual (Vaginal, Anal, Oral), Vertical (Mother→Child) |
| **Co-infection** | **10-40%** with Chlamydia; **HIV** Co-infection ↑ Risk |
| **AMR Threat** | **WHO Priority 2 (High)** — Ceftriaxone Resistance Emerging Globally |

### Clinical Presentation by Site

| Site | Symptomatic | Key Features | Complications |
|------|-------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Male Urethra** | **80-90%** | **Urethritis**: Dysuria, **Purulent Urethral Discharge**, Meatal Erythema/Edema | **Epididymo-orchitis**, Stricture, DGI |
| **Female Cervix** | **30-50%** | **Cervicitis**: Mucopurulent Discharge, Ectopy, Friability, ICB/IM Bleeding | **PID**, Infertility, Ectopic, Chronic Pelvic Pain |
| **Rectal** | **Often Asymptomatic** (>90%) | Proctitis: Mucus, Blood, Tenesmus, Pain (If Symptomatic) | **Proctitis**, Strictures, Anal Fissures |
| **Pharyngeal** | **Rarely Symptomatic** (>90% Asymptomatic) | Sore Throat, Exudate (Rare) | **Reservoir for AMR**, Transmission |
| **Conjunctival** | **Acute** | **Hyperacute Conjunctivitis**: Copious Purulent Discharge, Eyelid Edema, Chemosis | **Corneal Ulceration**, Perforation, Blindness |
| **DGI** | **0.5-3%** | **Arthritis-Dermatitis Syndrome** (Migratory Polyarthralgia, Tenosynovitis, Pustular Rash) → **Septic Arthritis** (Monoarticular) | **Septic Arthritis**, Endocarditis, Meningitis |

## 1️⃣ Disseminated Gonococcal Infection (DGI)

### Pathophysiology
| Mechanism | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Bacteremia** | Gonococci Enter Bloodstream → Haematogenous Spread |
| **Immune Complexes** | IgG-Gonococcal Antigen Complexes → Vasculitis, Dermatitis |
| **Direct Invasion** | Joints, Skin, Tendons, Pericardium, Meninges |

### Clinical Presentation
| Syndrome | Features | Prevalence |
|----------|----------|------------|
| **Arthritis-Dermatitis Syndrome** | Fever, **Migratory Polyarthralgia**, **Tenosynovitis** (Wrist/Ankle), **Pustular Rash** (Vesiculopustular, Acral/Distal Extremities), **Palpable Purpura** | **~60%** |
| **Septic Arthritis** | **Monoarticular** (Knee > Wrist > Ankle), Severe Pain, Effusion, Fever, **No Rash** | **~40%** |
| **Other** | Endocarditis, Meningitis, Hepatitis, Myocarditis | Rare |

> **Key**: **DGI = Bacteremia + Arthritis/Dermatitis**; **Blood Culture Often Negative**; **Joint Fluid Culture + NAAT** for Diagnosis

### Diagnosis
| Test | Utility |
|------|---------|
| **Joint Fluid (NAAT + Culture + AST)** | **Gold Standard** (Crucial for AST) |
| **Blood Culture** | Often Negative (<30% Positive) |
| **Mucosal Sites (NAAT + Culture)** | Urethral, Cervical, Pharyngeal, Rectal — Identify Source |
| **Skin Lesion Biopsy/NAAT** | Vesiculopustular Lesions |

### Treatment (DGI)
| Regimen | Dose |
|---------|------|
| **Ceftriaxone** | **1g IV/IM q24h** (or q12h if Severe) × **7 Days** (Switch to Oral When Improving) |
| **Alternative** (If Ceftriaxone Allergy) | **Gentamicin 240mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 2g PO Stat** (Or Azithro 1g Weekly × 2) |
| **Plus** | **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7d** (Co-infection CT Cover) |
| **Joint Management** | Aspiration (Repeated if Needed), Immobilisation, Physiotherapy |

## 2️⃣ Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) — The Global Crisis

### Resistance Mechanisms & Surveillance

| Antimicrobial | Resistance Mechanism | Global Status (WHO GASP 2023) |
|--------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Penicillin** | **penA** (PBP2 Mutation), **TEM-1 Plasmid (Penicillinase)** | **High Resistance Global** (Historically 1st Drug) |
| **Tetracycline** | **TetM Plasmid (Ribosomal Protection)**, Mutations | **High Resistance** |
| **Ciprofloxacin (Fluoroquinolones)** | **gyrA/parC Mutations** (DNA Gyrase/Topoisomerase IV) | **High Resistance >50% Global**; **Do Not Use Empirically** |
| **Azithromycin (Macrolides)** | **23S rRNA Mutations**, **Efflux Pumps (MtrCDE)**, **erm** | **Rising Resistance** (MIC Creep); Not Monotherapy |
| **Cefixime (Oral Cephalosporins)** | **penA Mutations**, Mosaic penA | **Declining Susceptibility**; Not Preferred for Pharyngeal |
| **Ceftriaxone (Injectable Cephalosporins)** | **penA Mutations (Mosaic penA XXXIV)**, **porB**, **mtrR** | **Emerging Resistance** (MIC Creep, Ceftriaxone-R Strains Reported) |

### WHO GASP (Gonococcal Antimicrobial Surveillance Programme)
| Component | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Sentinel Surveillance** | Sentinel Sites, Representative Sampling (Geographic, Population) |
| **AMR Testing** | Culture + AST (Ceftriaxone, Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin, Cefixime, Spectinomycin) |
| **Molecular Surveillance** | **penA** (Ceftriaxone), **gyrA/parC** (Ciprofloxacin), **23S rRNA** (Azithromycin), **mtrR** (Efflux) |
| **Data Sharing** | WHO GASP Database, National AMR Surveillance, AMR Action Plans |

### Ceftriaxone Resistance — Critical Threat
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Mechanism** | **penA Mosaic Mutations** (XXXIV, XIX, etc.), **porB**, **mtrR** Efflux, **pilQ** |
| **Resistance Level** | **MIC >0.125 mg/L** (EUCAST/CLSI Breakpoints) |
| **Geographic Spread** | **Japan, China, Europe, Australia, USA** — Global Spread |
| **Response** | **Dual Therapy**, **Higher Dose Ceftriaxone**, **Alternative Regimens**, **Spectinomycin** (If Available) |

## 2️⃣ Treatment — Evidence-Based Regimens

### Uncomplicated Genital Gonorrhoea

| Population | 1st Line (Dual Therapy) | Alternative (If Ceftriaxone Allergy/Unavailable) |
|------------|-------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **Adults/Adolescents (>45kg)** | **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat** | **Gentamicin 240mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 2g PO Stat** |
| **Weight <45kg** | **Ceftriaxone 250mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat** | **Gentamicin 120mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 2g PO Stat** |
| **Pregnancy** | **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat** (Safe) | **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Azithro 1g** (Only Option) |
| **Ceftriaxone Allergy** | **Gentamicin 240mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 2g PO Stat** | **Cefixime 400mg PO + Azithro 1g** (Less Effective Pharyngeal) |

> **Key**: **Dual Therapy Mandatory** (Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin) — **Suppresses Resistance**, **Treats Co-Infection (CT)**

### Pharyngeal Gonorrhoea
| Population | 1st Line | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|
| **All** | **Ceftriaxone 1g IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat** | **Higher Dose Ceftriaxone (1g)**; **Test of Cure Mandatory at 2 Weeks** |
| **Ceftriaxone Allergy** | **Gentamicin 240mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 2g PO Stat** | Preferred Alternative |

> **Key**: **Pharyngeal GC Harder to Treat** — Higher Ceftriaxone Dose (1g), **Test of Cure Mandatory at 2 Weeks**

### Rectal Gonorrhoea
| Population | 1st Line | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|
| **All** | **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat** | Standard Dose; **Co-infection Cover (CT)** with Azithro/Doxy |

### Gonorrhoea in Pregnancy
| Regimen | Safety |
|---------|--------|
| **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat** | **Safe in All Trimesters** (Ceftriaxone B, Azithro B) |
| **Alternative** | **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Azithro 1g** (Only Evidence-Based Option) |

> **Contraindicated in Pregnancy**: Doxycycline, Ciprofloxacin, Ofloxacin, Gentamicin (Ototoxicity/Nephrotoxicity Risk)

## 3️⃣ Disseminated Gonococcal Infection (DGI) — Treatment

| Phase | Regimen |
|-------|---------|
| **Initial (Parenteral)** | **Ceftriaxone 1g IV/IM q24h** (or q12h if Severe) × **7 Days** |
| **Step-Down (Oral)** | **Cefixime 400mg BD PO** (When Clinical Improvement, Total 7 Days) |
| **Ceftriaxone Allergy** | **Gentamicin 240mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 2g PO Stat** (Then Azithro 1g Weekly × 2) |
| **Co-Infection Cover** | **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7d** (Added for CT, MG) |
| **Joint Management** | **Repeated Aspiration**, Immobilisation, Physiotherapy |

## 3️⃣ Neonatal Gonorrhoea

### Ophthalmia Neonatorum
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Onset** | **Day 2-5** Post-Partum (Incubation 2-5 Days) — **Earlier than Chlamydia** |
| **Clinical** | **Hyperacute Conjunctivitis**: **Copious Purulent Discharge**, **Marked Eyelid Edema**, **Chemosis**, **Periorbital Cellulitis** |
| **Complications** | **Corneal Ulceration**, **Perforation**, **Blindness** (Rapid Onset, Hours) |
| **Diagnosis** | **Gram Stain** (Gram-Negative Intracellular Diplococci — **Presumptive**), **NAAT**, **Culture + AST** |
| **Treatment** | **Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg (Max 125mg) IM/IV Single Dose** + **Saline Irrigation** |
| **Prophylaxis** | **Erythromycin/Tetracycline Ointment** at Birth (Prevents GC/CT) |

### Neonatal Scalp Abscess
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Cause** | **Fetal Scalp Electrode** or **Forceps/Vacuum Trauma** → Gonococcal Inoculation |
| **Clinical** | **Abscess** (Fluctuant, Purulent), Cellulitis |
| **Treatment** | **Incision & Drainage** + **Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg IM/IV × 7 Days** |

### Neonatal DGI
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Onset** | **Day 7-14** Post-Partum |
| **Clinical** | Sepsis, Arthritis (Septic), Meningitis, Dermatitis |
| **Treatment** | **Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg IV/IM q24h × 7-10 Days** + Supportive |

## 4️⃣ Diagnosis — Algorithms & Interpretation

### Diagnostic Algorithm
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Suspected Gonorrhoea] --> B{Symptomatic?}
    B -->|Yes| C[Microscopy (Gram Stain)<br/>Rapid, Bedside]
    B -->|No (Screening)| D[NAAT (Urine/Swab)<br/>1st Line]
    C --> E{Gram Stain: Intracellular Gram-Neg Diplococci?}
    E -->|Yes (Male Urethral)| F[Presumptive GC → Treat Empirically]
    E -->|No / Female / Extra-Genital| G[NAAT + Culture + AST<br/>Send Both]
    D & F & G --> H[Treat + Partner Notify<br/>Test of Cure (GC: 2 Weeks)]
    H --> I[Culture + AST Results<br/>Guide Resistance Management]
```

### Diagnostic Test Comparison

| Test | Sensitivity | Specificity | TAT | Sample | Best Use |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----|--------|----------|
| **Gram Stain (Urethral M)** | **95% (Symptomatic Male)** | **>99%** | Minutes | Urethral Smear | **Presumptive Dx (Male Urethral)** |
| **Gram Stain (Female/Extra)** | Poor (<50%) | High | Minutes | Swab | **Not Reliable** (Female Cervical, Pharyngeal, Rectal) |
| **NAAT (PCR/TMA/SDA)** | **>95%** | **>98%** | 1-24h | Urine, Swab | **1st Line (Screening, All Sites)** |
| **Culture + AST** | 90-95% | **100%** | 48-72h | Swab | **Gold Standard** (Viability, **AST Mandatory**) |
| **POC NAAT** | 90-95% | 97-99% | 20-90min | Swab/Urine | **Rapid/Outreach** |
| **Microscopy (Joint Fluid)** | Variable | High | Minutes | Joint Aspirate | **DGI Presumptive** |

### Specimen Collection

| Site | Specimen | Transport |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Male Urethral** | **First-Catch Urine** (Hold ≥1h) OR Urethral Swab | NAAT Medium / Amies/Modified Thayer-Martin (Culture) |
| **Female Cervical** | Endocervical Swab (Remove Mucus) | NAAT Medium / Culture Medium |
| **Rectal** | Rectal Swab (2-3cm) | NAAT Medium / Culture Medium |
| **Pharyngeal** | Pharyngeal Swab (Posterior Oropharynx) | NAAT Medium / Culture Medium |
| **Conjunctival** | Conjunctival Swab | NAAT Medium / Culture Medium |
| **DGI (Joint)** | Joint Fluid Aspirate (Sterile) | NAAT Medium / Culture Medium (Blood Culture Bottles) |
| **DGI (Blood)** | Blood Culture Bottles (Aerobic/Anaerobic) | Blood Culture System |

> **Key**: **NAAT = 1st Line for Screening/ALL Sites**; **Culture + AST = Mandatory for Resistance Surveillance, Treatment Failure, DGI**

## 4️⃣ Test of Cure (TOC) & Partner Notification

### Test of Cure

| Infection | Timing | Test | Notes |
|-----------|--------|------|-------|
| **Genital GC** | **2 Weeks** Post-Treatment | **NAAT** (or Culture) | **Mandatory for Pharyngeal GC** |
| **Pharyngeal GC** | **2 Weeks** Post-Treatment | **NAAT** (or Culture + AST) | **Mandatory** (Higher Failure Rate) |
| **Rectal GC** | **2 Weeks** Post-Treatment | **NAAT** | If High Risk / Treatment Failure |
| **DGI** | **2-4 Weeks** Post-Treatment | **NAAT + Culture (Joint/Blood)** | Clinical + Microbiological Cure |
| **Neonatal** | **End of Treatment** | NAAT/Culture | Ophthalmia (Day 1), DGI (End of Rx) |

> **Key**: **GC TOC at 2 Weeks** (vs CT 4 Weeks); **Pharyngeal TOC Mandatory**; **Avoid <1 Week** (False +ve)

### Partner Notification & EPT (Expedited Partner Therapy)

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Patient Referral** | Index Patient Informs Partners (Preferred — Empowers Patient) |
| **Provider Referral** | Health Staff Notifies Partners (Confidential) |
| **Contract Referral** | Time-Limited Agreement (Patient Notifies or Provider Does) |
| **EPT (Expedited Partner Therapy)** | **Legal Where Permitted**: Index Patient Delivers Medication — **GC: Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Azithro 1g** (Requires Injection) |

> **GC EPT Complexity**: Ceftriaxone IM Requires Injection — **Provider Referral Preferred for GC**; **CT/TV EPT Easier (Oral)**.

|----------|----------------|

## 5️⃣ AMR Management — Clinical Decision Pathway

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[GC Diagnosed] --> B[Treat: Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Azithro 1g PO]
    B --> C[Send Culture + AST<br/>Mandatory for Resistance Surveillance]
    C --> D{AST Results}
    D -->|Ceftriaxone S + Azithro S| E[Continue Standard Therapy<br/>TOC at 2w]
    D -->|Ceftriaxone R (MIC >0.125)| F[Escalate/Alternative<br/>Expert Advice Required]
    D -->|Azithro R / High-Level| G[Ceftriaxone 1g IM (Higher Dose)<br/>Drop Azithro if Resistant]
    D -->|Ciprofloxacin S (Rare)| H[Consider Ciprofloxacin<br/>If Ceftriaxone Allergy/Unavailable]
    F --> I[Expert Consultation<br/>Spectinomycin (If Available)<br/>Gentamicin + Azithro<br/>Higher Dose Ceftriaxone 1g IM]
```

## 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"Gonorrhoea always symptomatic"** | **NO.** **50-80% Women Asymptomatic**, **>90% Pharyngeal/Rectal Asymptomatic** — Screening Essential |
| **"Ciprofloxacin still works for GC"** | **NO.** **>50% Global Resistance — Do Not Use**; Only If AST Confirms Susceptibility (Rare) |
| **"Azithromycin 1g = Monotherapy for GC"** | **NO.** **Dual Therapy Mandatory** (Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin) — Resistance Suppression |
| **"Pharyngeal GC = Same Treatment as Genital"** | **NO.** **Higher Ceftriaxone Dose (1g IM)**, **Test of Cure Mandatory at 2 Weeks** |
| **"GC TOC = 4 Weeks like CT"** | **NO.** **GC TOC = 2 Weeks**; **CT TOC = 4 Weeks** |
| **"Gram Stain = Gold Standard for GC"** | **NO.** **Culture + AST = Gold Standard** (Viability, AST); **Gram Stain = Presumptive (Male Urethral Only)** |
| **"GC EPT = Easy Like CT"** | **NO.** **GC EPT Complex** (Ceftriaxone IM Injection Required); **Provider Referral Preferred** |
| **"Ceftriaxone Resistance = Not a Problem"** | **NO.** **Emerging Global Threat**; **penA Mosaic Mutations**, MIC Creep — Dual Therapy, Higher Dose, Alternatives |
| **"DGI = Only Arthritis"** | **NO.** **Arthritis-Dermatitis Syndrome** (Migratory Polyarthralgia, Tenosynovitis, Pustular Rash) **OR** Septic Arthritis |
| **"Neonatal GC Ophthalmia = Same Onset as CT"** | **NO.** **GC: Day 2-5 (Hyperacute, Copious Purulent)**; **CT: Day 5-14 (Mucopurulent, Milder)** |

> **Mnemonic: GONORRHOEA AMR MASTER**

## ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **Current first-line treatment for uncomplicated genital gonorrhoea?**  
   A. Ciprofloxacin 500mg BD × 7d  B. **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat + Azithromycin 1g PO Stat**  C. Azithromycin 1g Stat  D. Ceftriaxone 500mg IM Stat  
   *Answer: B. Dual Therapy Mandatory (Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin).*

2. **Pharyngeal gonorrhoea treatment differs from genital by:**  
   A. Longer Duration  B. **Higher Ceftriaxone Dose (1g IM vs 500mg IM)**  C. Different Antibiotic  D. Oral Ceftriaxone  
   *Answer: B. Pharyngeal: Ceftriaxone 1g IM (Higher Dose) + Mandatory TOC at 2 Weeks.*

3. **Ciprofloxacin for gonorrhoea — current recommendation?**  
   A. 1st Line  B. **Do Not Use — >50% Global Resistance**  C. Alternative if Allergic  D. Use with Azithromycin  
   *Answer: B. >50% Global Resistance — Do Not Use Empirically; Only If AST Confirms Susceptibility.*

4. **Disseminated Gonococcal Infection — classic clinical presentation?**  
   A. Monoarticular Septic Arthritis Only  B. **Arthritis-Dermatitis Syndrome (Migratory Polyarthralgia, Tenosynovitis, Pustular Rash) + Septic Arthritis**  C. Only Pustular Rash  D. Only Fever and Arthralgia  
   *Answer: B. Arthritis-Dermatitis Syndrome + Septic Arthritis (Two Major Syndromes).*

5. **Neonatal gonococcal ophthalmia — typical onset vs chlamydia?**  
   A. Same Onset  B. **GC: Day 2-5 (Hyperacute); CT: Day 5-14 (Mucopurulent)**  C. GC Later  D. CT Earlier  
   *Answer: B. GC: Day 2-5 (Hyperacute, Copious Purulent); CT: Day 5-14 (Mucopurulent, Milder).*

6. **Gonorrhoea test of cure — timing for pharyngeal infection?**  
   A. 1 Week  B. **2 Weeks (Mandatory)**  C. 4 Weeks  D. 8 Weeks  
   *Answer: B. **2 Weeks (Mandatory for Pharyngeal GC)** — Higher Failure Rate.*

7. **Gonorrhoea in pregnancy — safe treatment?**  
   A. Ciprofloxacin 500mg BD  B. **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Azithromycin 1g PO**  C. Doxycycline 100mg BD  D. Gentamicin IM  
   *Answer: B. Dual Therapy Safe in Pregnancy; Doxy/Cipro/Genta Contraindicated.*

8. **Ceftriaxone resistance in gonorrhoea — current status?**  
   A. Non-Existent  B. **Emerging (MIC >0.125 mg/L), penA Mosaic Mutations**  C. Widespread >50%  D. Only in Japan  
   *Answer: B. Emerging Globally (MIC >0.125 mg/L); penA Mosaic Mutations; Dual Therapy, Higher Dose, Alternatives.*

9. **Gonorrhoea test of cure — minimum time after treatment?**  
   A. 3 Days  B. **2 Weeks**  C. 4 Weeks  D. 8 Weeks  
   *Answer: B. 2 Weeks (GC TOC); CT = 4 Weeks.*

10. **Partner notification for gonorrhoea — EPT complexity?**  
    A. Simple Oral Meds  B. **Complex — Ceftriaxone IM Injection Required**  C. Not Needed  D. Provider Referral Illegal  
    *Answer: B. GC EPT Complex (Ceftriaxone IM Injection); Provider Referral Preferred.*
