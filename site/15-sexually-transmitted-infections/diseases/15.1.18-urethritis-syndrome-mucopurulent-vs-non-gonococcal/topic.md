> [!important]
> **Syndromic management** is the cornerstone of STI control — treat empirically based on syndrome while awaiting lab confirmation. Urethritis = discharge + dysuria ± itch. Distinguish gonococcal (GC) vs non-gonococcal (NGU).

## 1. Learning Objectives
- Apply syndromic approach to urethral discharge
- Differentiate gonococcal vs non-gonococcal urethritis clinically and microscopically
- Know empirical treatment regimens (dual therapy for GC)
- Understand partner notification and test-of-cure principles

## 2. Definition
- **Urethritis**: Urethral inflammation → discharge ± dysuria
- **Gonococcal urethritis (GC)**: Caused by *Neisseria gonorrhoeae*
- **Non-gonococcal urethritis (NGU)**: *Chlamydia trachomatis* (50%), *M. genitalium*, *Ureaplasma*, TV, HSV
- **Mucopurulent cervicitis (MPC)**: Female equivalent of urethritis

## 3. Aetiology
| Syndrome | Common Pathogens | Proportion |
|----------|------------------|------------|
| **Gonococcal** | *Neisseria gonorrhoeae* | 20–30% (varies by setting) |
| **Non-gonococcal (NGU)** | *Chlamydia trachomatis* | ~50% of NGU |
| | *Mycoplasma genitalium* | 15–25% |
| | *Ureaplasma urealyticum* | 10–20% |
| | *Trichomonas vaginalis* | <5% |
| | HSV, adenovirus | <5% |
| | No pathogen identified | 20–30% |

## 4. Clinical Features
| Feature | Gonococcal | Non-Gonococcal (Chlamydia) |
|---------|------------|---------------------------|
| **Incubation** | 2–7 days | 7–21 days |
| **Discharge** | Copious, purulent, yellow-green | Scant, mucoid, clear-white |
| **Dysuria** | Moderate-severe | Mild-moderate |
| **Meatal erythema** | Prominent | Mild |
| **Complications** | Epididymitis, DGI, stricture | Epididymitis, reactive arthritis, PID (♀) |

## 5. Microscopy (Gram Stain Urethral Smear)
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| **≥5 PMNL/hpf** + **Intracellular Gram-negative diplococci** | **Gonococcal** (presumptive) |
| **≥5 PMNL/hpf** + **No organisms** | **Non-gonococcal** (presumptive) |
| <5 PMNL/hpf | Normal / non-specific |

> **Note**: Female endocervical smear less reliable for GC; culture/NAAT preferred.

## 6. Diagnostic Testing (NAAT = Gold Standard)
| Test | Specimen | Sensitivity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------------|-------|
| **NAAT (PCR/TMA)** | Urine (first-catch), urethral swab, vaginal swab | >95% | **Preferred** for GC & CT |
| **Culture (GC)** | Urethral/endocervical/rectal/pharyngeal | 90–95% | Needed for **AST** (resistance) |
| **Microscopy** | Urethral smear (male) | 90% (GC) | Rapid, but operator-dependent |
| **POC NAAT** | Urine/swab | 90–95% | Emerging (e.g., GeneXpert) |

## 7. Empirical Treatment (Syndromic — Treat Before Results)

### Male Urethritis (Empirical Dual Therapy)
> **Treat for BOTH GC + CT simultaneously** (high co-infection rate, rising GC resistance)

| Regimen | Dose | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM** (1g if >150kg) **+ Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7d** | **WHO/BASHH 1st-line** | Covers GC (incl. resistant) + CT + *M. genitalium* (partial) |
| **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM **+ Azithromycin 1g PO** | Alternative | If doxycycline contraindicated (pregnancy) |
| **Gentamicin 240mg IM **+ Azithromycin 2g PO** | Cephalosporin allergy | SC/IM |

### Mucopurulent Cervicitis (Female) — Empirical
| Regimen | Dose |
|---------|------|
| **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7d** | 1st-line |
| **Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Azithromycin 1g PO** | Alternative |

### NGU Confirmed (CT Negative) — *M. genitalium* Suspected
- **Moxifloxacin 400mg OD × 7–10d** (if macrolide-resistant)
- **Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7d → then Azithromycin 500mg day 1, 250mg days 2–5** (UK guideline)

## 8. Partner Management
- **All partners within 2 weeks (GC) / 4 weeks (CT) / 3 months (syphilis) / 6 months (HIV)** should be notified, tested, and treated empirically
- **Expedited Partner Therapy (EPT)**: Give patient medication/prescription for partners without clinical assessment
- **Abstain 7 days after treatment completion** (both partners)

## 9. Complications
| Syndrome | Complications |
|----------|---------------|
| **GC** | Epididymo-orchitis, DGI (arthritis-dermatitis), stricture, perihepatitis (Fitz-Hugh-Curtis) |
| **CT** | Epididymitis, reactive arthritis (SARA), PID, infertility, ectopic pregnancy, neonatal pneumonia/conjunctivitis |
| **M. genitalium** | Persistent NGU, cervicitis, PID, possible infertility |

## 10. Test of Cure (TOC)
| Pathogen | TOC Timing | Method |
|----------|------------|--------|
| **GC** | **72 hours** (if pelvic/pharyngeal) / not routine if uncomplicated genital | NAAT |
| **CT** | **Not routine** (if compliant); if pregnant or symptoms persist → 4–6 weeks | NAAT |
| **M. genitalium** | **4–6 weeks** (high resistance risk) | NAAT + resistance testing |

## 11. Antimicrobial Resistance — Gonorrhoea
- **Ceftriaxone resistance emerging** — dual therapy essential
- **Ciprofloxacin resistance >90%** — do not use
- **Azithromycin resistance rising** — prefer doxycycline
- **Surveillance**: GASP (Gonorrhoea Antimicrobial Surveillance Programme)

## 12. Special Situations
| Situation | Adjustment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Pregnancy** | Avoid doxycycline → Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Azithromycin 1g PO |
| **Penicillin/cephalosporin allergy** | Gentamicin 240mg IM + Azithromycin 2g PO |
| **Pharyngeal GC** | Harder to eradicate → Ceftriaxone 1g IM + Azithromycin 2g PO |
| **DGI (Disseminated Gonococcal Infection)** | Ceftriaxone 1g IV q24h → step down to oral after 24–48h improvement |
| **Neonatal conjunctivitis** | Ceftriaxone 50mg/kg IM/IV single dose (max 125mg) |

## 13. FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Points
- **Urethritis = dual therapy (GC + CT)** — ceftriaxone + doxycycline
- **Gram stain**: ≥5 PMNL + intracellular GNDC = GC; ≥5 PMNL + no organisms = NGU
- **TOC for GC at 72h** if pharyngeal/pelvic; not routine for uncomplicated genital
- **CT TOC not routine** unless pregnant or symptoms persist
- **M. genitalium** → suspect if NGU persists after doxycycline; moxifloxacin
- **Pharyngeal GC harder to clear** → higher dose ceftriaxone + azithromycin

## 15. Common Confusions / Exam Traps
- **Azithromycin 1g single dose for CT is NO LONGER 1st-line** (resistance) → doxycycline 7d
- **TOC for uncomplicated genital GC NOT routinely required** — only if pharyngeal/pelvic or symptoms persist
- **Treating NGU with azithromycin alone misses *M. genitalium*** — doxycycline first
- **Cephalosporin allergy → gentamicin + azithromycin** (not just azithromycin)
- **Female GC often asymptomatic** — screen partners of male GC cases

## 16. Mnemonics
- **Urethritis dual therapy**: **Ceftriaxone (GC) + Doxycycline (CT)**
- **Microscopy**: PMNL + diplococci = GC; PMNL only = NGU
- **TOC**: GC pharyngeal/pelvic at 72h; CT not routine
- **M. genitalium**: Doxycycline first, then moxifloxacin

## 17. One-Page Revision Summary
**Urethritis = Dual Therapy**
- GC: purulent, 2–7d incubation, Gram− diplococci in PMNL
- NGU: mucoid, 7–21d, no organisms on Gram stain
- Treat: Ceftriaxone 500mg IM + Doxycycline 100mg BD × 7d
- TOC: GC pharyngeal/72h; CT not routine
- Partner notification: 2w (GC), 4w (CT)
