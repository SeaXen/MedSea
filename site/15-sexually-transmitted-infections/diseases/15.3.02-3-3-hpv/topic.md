## 2. 🧠 Core Concept: HPV Biology & Natural History

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Human Papillomavirus] --> B[Non-Enveloped, dsDNA Virus]
    B --> C[>200 Types Identified]
    C --> D[High-Risk (Oncogenic)]
    D --> D1[HPV 16, 18 (70% Cervical Cancer)]
    D --> D2[HPV 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 (Additional 20%)]
    D --> D3[HPV 35, 39, 51, 56, 59, 66, 68 (Probable/High-Risk)]
    C --> E[Low-Risk (Non-Oncogenic)]
    E --> E1[HPV 6, 11 (90% Genital Warts)]
    E --> E2[HPV 40, 42, 43, 44, 54, 61, 70, 72, 81]
    B --> F[Natural History]
    F --> F1[Transient Infection (90% Clear in 1-2 Years)]
    F --> F2[Persistent Infection (High-Risk) → CIN → Cancer]
    F --> F3[Immune Evasion (E6/E7 Oncoproteins)]
    F3 --> G[Carcinogenesis Pathway]
    G --> G1[E6 → p53 Degradation (Apoptosis Block)]
    G --> G2[E7 → pRb Degradation (Cell Cycle Dysregulation)]
    G --> G3[Genomic Instability → Integration → Cancer]
```

## 1️⃣ HPV Classification & Disease Associations

### High-Risk (Oncogenic) HPV Types

| Group | Types | Cancer Contribution | Key Associations |
|-------|-------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Highest Risk** | **16, 18** | **~70% Cervical Cancer** | **Cervical, Anal, Oropharyngeal, Penile, Vulvar, Vaginal** |
| **High Risk** | **31, 33, 45, 52, 58** | **~20% Cervical Cancer** | Same Cancers |
| **Probable High Risk** | 26, 53, 66, 67, 68, 70, 73, 82 | Minor Contribution | Cervical, Anal |
| **Possible High Risk** | 30, 34, 35, 39, 40, 42, 43, 44, 45, 51, 52, 54, 55, 56, 57, 59, 61, 62, 64, 67, 69, 70, 71, 72, 73, 81, 82, 83, 84, 85, 86, 87, 89, 90, 91 | Minimal | — |

### Low-Risk (Non-Oncogenic) HPV Types

| Types | Disease Association |
|-------|---------------------|
| **6, 11** | **~90% Genital Warts** (Condyloma Acuminata), Recurrent Respiratory Papillomatosis (RRP), Low-Grade Cervical Lesions (LSIL) |
| 40, 42, 43, 44, 54, 61, 70, 72, 81, 89 | Minor (Rare Warts, LSIL) |

| Group | Types | Definition |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **Group 1 (Carcinogenic)** | 16, 18, 31, 33, 35, 39, 45, 51, 52, 56, 58, 59 | Sufficient Evidence |
| **Group 2A (Probably Carcinogenic)** | 68 | Limited Evidence Human, Sufficient Animal |
| **Group 2B (Possibly Carcinogenic)** | 26, 53, 66, 67, 70, 73, 82 | Limited Evidence |
| **Group 3 (Not Classifiable)** | Low-Risk Types (6, 11, etc.) | Inadequate Evidence |

## 1️⃣ HPV Natural History & Carcinogenesis

### Infection Outcomes
| Outcome | Probability | Timeline |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Transient Clearance** | **~90%** | **1-2 Years** (Median 8-12 Months) |
| **Persistence** | **~10%** | **>2 Years** → Risk of CIN2+ |
| **Progression to CIN2/3** | **~5-10% of Persistent** | **3-10 Years** |
| **Progression to Invasive Cancer** | **<1% of CIN3** | **10-20+ Years** |

### Carcinogenesis Pathway (High-Risk HPV)
| Step | Molecular Mechanism |
|------|---------------------|
| **1. Viral Entry** | HPV Infects Basal Keratinocytes (Microtrauma) |
| **2. Viral Replication** | **Episomal** (Productive) in Differentiating Epithelium |
| **3. Immune Evasion** | **E6/E7 Oncoproteins** Downregulate HLA, Interferon Response |
| **4. Cell Cycle Dysregulation** | **E7 → pRb Degradation** → E2F Release → Uncontrolled S-Phase Entry |
| **5. Apoptosis Block** | **E6 → p53 Degradation** → Failed DNA Repair/Apoptosis |
| **6. Genomic Instability** | Chromosomal Aberrations, Integration into Host Genome |
| **7. Clonal Expansion** | Integrated HPV (E6/E7 Overexpression) → CIN → Cancer |

> **Key**: **E6 → p53 Degradation** (Apoptosis Block); **E7 → pRb Degradation** (Cell Cycle Dysregulation)

## 2️⃣ Clinical Manifestations

### Anogenital Warts (Condyloma Acuminata)
| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Causative Types** | **HPV 6, 11** (90% of Warts) |
| **Appearance** | **Fleshy, Cauliflower-like, Pedunculated or Sessile**, Skin-Coloured/Whitish |
| **Distribution** | **Perianal, Perineal, Vulvar, Vaginal, Cervical, Penile, Scrotal, Urethral, Anal** |
| **Symptoms** | Often Asymptomatic; Pruritus, Bleeding, Dyspareunia, Discharge |
| **Differential** | **Condyloma Lata (Syphilis)**, Molluscum, Seborrheic Keratosis, Skin Tags, SCC |

### Cervical Intraepithelial Neoplasia (CIN)
| Grade | Histology | HPV Association | Management |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| **CIN1 (LSIL)** | **Low-Grade** (Basal 1/3) | High/Low-Risk | **Observation** (90% Regress), HPV Test at 12 Months |
| **CIN2 (HSIL)** | **High-Grade** (Basal 2/3) | High-Risk | **Treatment (Excision/Ablation)** OR Observation (Selected) |
| **CIN3 (HSIL / CIS)** | **High-Grade** (Full Thickness) | High-Risk | **Treatment (Excision Preferred)** |

### HPV-Related Cancers

| Cancer Site | HPV Attribution | High-Risk Types | Screening |
|-------------|----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Cervical** | **~100%** | 16, 18 (70%), 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 | **HPV Primary Screening** (Cytology Triage) |
| **Anal** | **~90%** | 16, 18 | **MSM/HIV: Annual Cytology/HPV** |
| **Oropharyngeal** | **~70% (HPV+)** | **16 (Dominant)** | No Organised Screening |
| **Vulvar** | **~40-70%** | 16, 18 | Visual Inspection, Biopsy |
| **Vaginal** | **~70%** | 16, 18 | Cytology/HPV (Rare) |
| **Penile** | **~50%** | 16, 18 | Visual Inspection, Biopsy |

## 2️⃣ HPV Vaccination

### Available Vaccines

| Vaccine | Types Covered | Dosing Schedule |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **9-Valent (Gardasil 9)** | **6, 11, 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58** (90% Cervical Cancer, 90% Genital Warts) | **2-Dose (<15y: 0, 6-12m)**; **3-Dose (≥15y: 0, 2, 6m)** |
| **Quadrivalent (Gardasil)** | 6, 11, 16, 18 (70% Cervical Cancer, 90% Warts) | Same as 9vHPV |
| **Bivalent (Cervarix)** | 16, 18 (70% Cervical Cancer) | Same as 9vHPV |

> **9-Valent = Preferred** (Broadest Protection); **Quadrivalent/Bivalent = Acceptable if 9v Unavailable**

### Vaccination Schedules

| Age Group | 9vHPV Schedule | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| **<15 Years** | **2 Doses: 0, 6-12 Months** (Minimum Interval 5 Months) | **Preferred** (Robust Immune Response) |
| **≥15 Years** | **3 Doses: 0, 2, 6 Months** (Month 0, 1-2, 6) | **Immunocompromised: 3 Doses Regardless of Age** |
| **Catch-Up** | **Females ≤26 Years, Males ≤21 Years** (Up to 26 for MSM/Immunocompromised) | **Shared Clinical Decision 27-45 Years** |
| **HIV/Immunocompromised** | **3 Doses (0, 2, 6 Months)** Regardless of Age | **MSM ≤26 Years (Up to 26)** |

> **Key**: **2-Dose <15y, 3-Dose ≥15y**; **Catch-Up to 26y (F), 21y (M) / 26y (MSM/Immunocomp)**; **Shared Decision 27-45y**

### Vaccine Efficacy & Impact

| Outcome | Efficacy (9vHPV) |
|---------|------------------|
| **HPV 6/11/16/18/31/33/45/52/58 Infection** | **>90%** (Type-Specific) |
| **CIN2+ (HPV 16/18/31/33/45/52/58)** | **>95%** |
| **Genital Warts (6/11)** | **>90%** |
| **Vulvar/Vaginal/Anal Precancer** | **>90%** |
| **Population Impact (Countries with High Coverage)** | **>80% Reduction in Vaccine-Type HPV, >50% Reduction in CIN2+** |

### Contraindications & Precautions

| Contraindication | Detail |
|-----------------|--------|
| **Anaphylaxis to Previous Dose/Component** | Absolute Contraindication |
| **Pregnancy** | **Defer Until Postpartum** (Safety Data Reassuring, But Not Licensed in Pregnancy) |
| **Moderate/Severe Acute Illness** | **Defer** |
| **Immunocompromised** | **3 Doses Regardless of Age** (Safety: Inactivated, Non-Live) |

## 3️⃣ Cervical Screening — HPV Primary Screening Algorithm

### WHO/UK/IARC Recommended Algorithm (HPV Primary Screening)

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Cervical Sample (LBC/HPV Test)] --> B{HPV Test Result}
    B -->|Negative| C[Return to Routine Recall<br/>(3-5 Years Age-Dependent)]
    B -->|Positive| D{Partial Genotyping<br/>(HPV 16/18 vs Other HR)}
    D -->|HPV 16/18 +ve| E[Immediate Colposcopy Referral]
    D -->|Other HR HPV (Non-16/18) +ve| F{Reflex Cytology}
    F -->|Abnormal (Any Grade)| G[Colposcopy Referral]
    F -->|Negative (Normal)| H[Repeat HPV Test in 12 Months]
    H -->|HPV Negative| C
    H -->|HPV Positive| I[Colposcopy Referral]
```

### Key Screening Parameters

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Start Age** | **25 Years** (UK/WHO) / 21-25 Years (Country-Specific) |
| **Interval** | **3 Years (25-49)**, **5 Years (50-64)** / **5 Years** (HPV Primary) |
| **Stop Age** | **64-65 Years** (If Adequate Negative History) |
| **Test** | **HPV Primary (PCR/TMA)** + **Reflex Cytology** (Non-16/18 HR HPV) |
| **Self-Sampling** | **Validated** (Improves Coverage) |

### Colposcopy & Treatment Guidelines

| Finding | Management |
|---------|------------|
| **CIN1** | **Observation** (12-Month HPV Test) — 90% Regress |
| **CIN2** | **Excision (LLETZ/LEEP) Preferred** OR Ablation (If Small/Peripheral) |
| **CIN3 / CIS** | **Excision (LLETZ/LEEP) Mandatory** — Diagnostic & Therapeutic |
| **CGIN (Adenocarcinoma In Situ)** | **Excision (Cone Biopsy)** — Hysterectomy if Completed Family |

### Post-Treatment Follow-Up (Test of Cure)

| Timeline | Test | Action |
|----------|------|--------|
| **6 Months Post-Treatment** | **HPV Test (+ Cytology)** | **HPV Negative → Routine Recall**; **HPV Positive → Colposcopy** |
| **18 Months** | **HPV Test** | **If Negative → Routine Recall** |

## 3️⃣ Anogenital Warts — Management

### Treatment Options

| Category | Agent/Method | Regimen | Clearance Rate | Recurrence |
|---------|--------------|---------|----------------|------------|
| **Patient-Applied (Topical)** | **Podophyllotoxin 0.15% Cream/Gel** | **BD × 3 Days On, 4 Days Off** (Cycles ≤4) | 70-90% | 20-30% |
| | **Imiquimod 5% Cream** | **HS 3x/Week (Alternate Nights) × 8-16 Weeks** | 50-80% | 10-20% |
| | **Sinecatechins 15% Ointment** | **TDS × 16 Weeks** | 50-60% | Low |
| **Provider-Administered (Office)** | **Cryotherapy (Liquid Nitrogen)** | **Freeze-Thaw-Freeze Cycles** | 70-90% | 20-30% |
| | **Trichloroacetic Acid (TCA) 80-90%** | **Weekly × 4-6 Weeks** | 70-85% | 20-30% |
| | **Podophyllin Resin 10-25%** | **Weekly** (Less Used, Toxicity) | 30-70% | High |
| **Surgical** | **Excision, Electrocautery, Laser (CO2), Loop Excision** | Single Session | 90-100% | 10-20% |

### Treatment Algorithm (Warts)

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Anogenital Warts] --> B{Patient Preference / Anatomy}
    B -->|Few, Accessible, Patient Wants Self-Tx| C[Podophyllotoxin or Imiquimod]
    B -->|Multiple, Extensive, Internal, Failed Topical| D[Cryotherapy / TCA / Surgical]
    B -->|Pregnancy| E[Cryotherapy / TCA / Surgical<br/>(Avoid Podophyllotoxin/Imiquimod)]
    B -->|Immunocompromised| F[Surgical / Cryotherapy / TCA<br/>(Higher Recurrence, Consider HPV Vaccine)]
```

### Wart Treatment in Special Populations

| Population | Preferred | Avoid |
|------------|-----------|-------|
| **Pregnancy** | **Cryotherapy, TCA, Surgical** | **Podophyllotoxin (Teratogenic), Imiquimod (Limited Data)** |
| **Immunocompromised (HIV, Transplant)** | **Surgical, Cryotherapy** | **Topical (Lower Efficacy, Higher Recurrence)** |
| **Children (Non-Sexual Transmission Possible)** | **Cryotherapy, TCA** | **Podophyllotoxin, Imiquimod** |
| **Urethral/Intravaginal/Intra-anal Warts** | **Specialist Referral** (Cryotherapy, Laser, Surgical) | **Topical (Ineffective)** |

## 4️⃣ HPV in Special Populations

### HIV / Immunocompromised

| Aspect | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **HPV Prevalence** | **Higher** (Higher Acquisition, Lower Clearance) |
| **HPV Types** | **Multiple Concurrent Infections** (High-Risk + Low-Risk) |
| **Disease Progression** | **Faster** (CIN → Cancer), **Higher Grade Lesions** |
| **Warts** | **More Extensive, Refractory, Higher Recurrence** |
| **Screening** | **Annual Cervical Screening** (Cytology + HPV); **Anal Cytology/HPV Annual (MSM/HIV)** |
| **Vaccination** | **3 Doses (0, 2, 6m)** Regardless of Age; **Catch-Up to 26y (MSM to 26y)** |
| **Treatment** | **Higher Recurrence**; **Surgical Preferred for Warts**; **Excision for CIN** |

### Men Who Have Sex with Men (MSM)

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Anal Cancer Risk** | **~20-40x Higher** (vs General Population); **HIV+ = 80-100x** |
| **Screening** | **Annual Anal Cytology + HPV Testing** (If HIV+); **Consider Age 35+** |
| **Vaccination** | **9vHPV Up to Age 26** (Catch-Up); **MSM ≤26y Priority** |
| **Warts** | **Perianal/Intra-anal Common**; **Surgical/Laser Preferred** |

### Pregnancy

| Aspect | Management |
|--------|------------|
| **Warts** | **Cryotherapy/TCA/Surgical** (Avoid Podophyllotoxin/Imiquimod); **Cesarean NOT Indicated for Warts** |
| **Cervical Lesions** | **Colposcopy Safe**; **Treatment Deferred Postpartum** (Unless Invasive Cancer Suspected) |
| **Vaccination** | **Defer Until Postpartum** (Not Licensed in Pregnancy) |
| **Cesarean** | **NOT Indicated for Genital Warts** (Rare Laryngeal Papillomatosis) |

## 4️⃣ Recurrent Respiratory Papillomatosis (RRP)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Causative** | **HPV 6, 11** |
| **Transmission** | Vertical (Mother→Infant at Delivery), Rare Horizontal |
| **Presentation** | **Hoarseness, Stridor, Airway Obstruction** (Laryngeal Papillomas) |
| **Forms** | **Juvenile-Onset (JORRP)** (<12y, More Aggressive); **Adult-Onset (AORRP)** |
| **Treatment** | **Surgical Debridement (Microdebrider/CO2 Laser)**; **Adjuvant: Cidofovir (Intralesional), Bevacizumab (Intralesional), HPV Vaccine (Adjunct)** |
| **HPV Vaccine** | **Adjunct Therapy** (Reduces Recurrence, Not Licensed as Treatment) |

## 5. 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"HPV = Only Cervical Cancer"** | **NO.** **Anal (90%), Oropharyngeal (70%), Vulvar/Vaginal (40-70%), Penile (50%)** — All HPV-Related |
| **"HPV Vaccine = Only for Girls"** | **NO.** **Both Genders**; **Prevents Anal, Oropharyngeal, Penile Cancers + Warts in Males** |
| **"HPV Vaccine = Only for Virgins"** | **NO.** **Benefit Even If Sexually Active** (Protects Against Types Not Yet Acquired) |
| **"All HPV = Cancer"** | **NO.** **Low-Risk (6, 11) = Warts, RRP; High-Risk = Cancer**; **90% Clear Spontaneously** |
| **"HPV Vaccine = Only for <15y"** | **NO.** **Catch-Up to 26y (F), 21y (M) / 26y (MSM/Immunocomp)**; **Shared Decision 27-45y** |
| **"CIN1 = Treat Immediately"** | **NO.** **CIN1 = Observe (90% Regress)**; **12-Month HPV Test, Treat if Persistent/Progresses** |
| **"HPV Test Positive = Cancer"** | **NO.** **HPV +ve = Infection**; **Reflex Cytology/Colposcopy Needed for Risk Stratification** |
| **"Cesarean Prevents HPV Transmission to Infant"** | **NO.** **RRP (HPV 6/11) Rare**; **Cesarean NOT Indicated for Warts**; **Vaccination = Prevention** |
| **"HPV Vaccine = Only for <15y"** | **NO.** **Catch-Up to 26y (F), 21y (M) / 26y (MSM/Immunocomp)**; **Shared Decision 27-45y** |
| **"All HPV Types = Same Cancer Risk"** | **NO.** **16, 18 = 70% Cervical Cancer**; **Other HR = Additional 20%**; **Low-Risk = Warts Only** |

> **Mnemonic: HPV MASTER CANCER PREVENTION**  
> **H**PV Types: **High-Risk (16,18,31,33,45,52,58) = Cancer; Low-Risk (6,11) = Warts**  
> **P**athogenesis: **E6→p53 (Apoptosis Block), E7→pRb (Cell Cycle Dysregulation) → Genomic Instability → Cancer**  
> **V**accine: **9vHPV (6,11,16,18,31,33,45,52,58); 2-Dose (<15y), 3-Dose (≥15y); Catch-Up F≤26, M≤21 (MSM≤26)**  
> **S**creening: **HPV Primary → -ve = Routine; 16/18 +ve = Colpo; Other HR → Cyto → Abnormal = Colpo**  
> **C**IN Management: **CIN1 Observe (12m HPV); CIN2 Excise; CIN3 Excise Mandatory; TOC 6m HPV Test**  
> **W**arts: **HPV 6/11; Topical (Podophyllotoxin, Imiquimod), Office (Cryo, TCA), Surgical**  
> **W**arts Pregnancy: **Cryo/TCA/Surgical ONLY (No Podophyllotoxin/Imiquimod)**  
> **A**nal Cancer: **MSM/HIV → Annual Anal Cyto/HPV**  
> **O**ropharyngeal: **HPV 16 Dominant, No Organised Screening**  
> **V**accine Schedule: **<15y = 2 Doses (0, 6-12m); ≥15y = 3 Doses (0, 2, 6m)**  
> **C**atch-Up: **F ≤26, M ≤21 (MSM/Immunocomp ≤26); Shared Decision 27-45**  
> **C**ervical Screen: **HPV Primary → -ve = Recall; 16/18 = Colpo; Other HR → Cyto**  
> **C**IN: **CIN1 Observe; CIN2/3 Excise; TOC 6m HPV**  
> **H**IV/Immunocompromised: **3 Doses Vaccine; Annual Cyto+HPV; Anal Cyto Annual**  
> **R**RP: **HPV 6/11, Juvenile/Adult Onset, Surgical Debridement, Vaccine Adjunct**  
> **P**revention: **Vaccine = Primary Prevention; Screening = Secondary Prevention**

## 10. 📌 Summary
- **HPV Types**: **High-Risk (16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58) = Cancer**; **Low-Risk (6, 11) = Warts, RRP**
- **Natural History**: **90% Clear in 1-2 Years**; **Persistence → CIN2/3 → Cancer (10-20+ Years)**; **E6→p53 Degradation, E7→pRb Degradation**
- **Vaccination**: **9vHPV (6,11,16,18,31,33,45,52,58)**; **<15y: 2 Doses (0, 6-12m)**; **≥15y: 3 Doses (0, 2, 6m)**; **Catch-Up: F≤26, M≤21 (MSM/Immunocomp ≤26); HIV: 3 Doses Any Age**
- **Screening**: **HPV Primary → Negative = Routine Recall; HPV 16/18 Positive = Colposcopy; Other HR HPV + Cytology → Abnormal = Colposcopy, Normal = Repeat 12 Months**
- **CIN Management**: **CIN1 = Observe (12-Month HPV Test)**; **CIN2 = Excision (LLETZ) Preferred**; **CIN3 = Excision Mandatory**; **Test of Cure at 6 Months (HPV Test)**
- **Anogenital Warts**: **HPV 6, 11**; **Patient-Applied (Podophyllotoxin, Imiquimod)**; **Provider (Cryotherapy, TCA, Surgical)**; **Pregnancy: Cryo/TCA/Surgical Only**
- **Non-Cervical Cancers**: **Anal (90% HPV, Annual Screen MSM/HIV)**, **Oropharyngeal (70% HPV+, 16 Dominant, No Screen)**, **Vulvar/Vaginal/Penile (40-70%)**
- **Special Populations**: **HIV/Immunocompromised = 3-Dose Vaccine, Annual Screen, More Extensive Disease**; **Pregnancy = Defer Vaccine, Cryo/TCA/Surgical for Warts Only**

## 11. ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **HPV types most commonly associated with cervical cancer?**  
   A. 6, 11  B. **16, 18**  C. 31, 33  D. 45, 52  
   *Answer: B. HPV 16, 18 = ~70% of Cervical Cancer.*

2. **9-valent HPV vaccine — which additional types beyond quadrivalent?**  
   A. 6, 11, 16, 18  B. **31, 33, 45, 52, 58**  C. 26, 53, 66  D. 40, 42, 43  
   *Answer: B. 9vHPV Adds 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 to 6, 11, 16, 18.*

3. **HPV vaccination schedule for a 14-year-old girl?**  
   A. 3 Doses (0, 1, 6 Months)  B. **2 Doses (0, 6-12 Months)**  C. Single Dose  D. 3 Doses (0, 2, 6 Months)  
   *Answer: B. <15 Years = 2 Doses (0, 6-12 Months).*

4. **Cervical screening — HPV primary algorithm: HPV 16 positive, cytology normal. Next step?**  
   A. Repeat in 12 Months  B. **Immediate Colposcopy Referral**  C. Reflex Cytology  C. Routine Recall  
   *Answer: B. HPV 16/18 Positive → Immediate Colposcopy Referral (Regardless of Cytology).*

5. **CIN1 management?**  
   A. Immediate Excision  B. **Observation with 12-Month HPV Test**  C. Ablation  D. Cone Biopsy  
   *Answer: B. CIN1 = Observe (90% Regress); 12-Month HPV Test.*

6. **CIN3 management?**  
   A. Observation  B. **Excision Mandatory (LLETZ/Cone Biopsy)**  C. Ablation Only  D. Hysterectomy  
   *Answer: B. CIN3 = High-Grade, Excision Mandatory (LLETZ or Cone Biopsy).*

7. **Anogenital warts — causative HPV types?**  
   A. 16, 18  B. **6, 11**  C. 31, 33  D. 45, 52  
   *Answer: B. HPV 6, 11 = ~90% of Genital Warts.*

8. **Anogenital warts in pregnancy — preferred treatment?**  
   A. Podophyllotoxin  B. **Cryotherapy / TCA / Surgical**  C. Imiquimod  D. Podophyllin Resin  
   *Answer: B. Cryotherapy, TCA, Surgical Only (Avoid Podophyllotoxin/Imiquimod in Pregnancy).*

8. **Anal cancer screening — recommended for?**  
   A. All Adults  B. **MSM and HIV-Positive Individuals (Annual Cytology/HPV)**  C. Women Only  D. Not Recommended  
   *Answer: B. MSM and HIV-Positive → Annual Anal Cytology/HPV Testing.*

9. **Oropharyngeal cancer — dominant HPV type?**  
   A. HPV 18  B. **HPV 16**  C. HPV 6  D. HPV 11  
   *Answer: B. HPV 16 = Dominant in HPV+ Oropharyngeal Cancer (~90% of HPV+).*

10. **HPV vaccine in pregnancy?**  
    A. Safe, Recommended  B. **Defer Until Postpartum (Not Licensed in Pregnancy)**  C. Only 2nd Trimester  D. Only 3rd Trimester  
    *Answer: B. Defer Until Postpartum (Not Licensed in Pregnancy; Safety Data Reassuring But Not Licensed).*
