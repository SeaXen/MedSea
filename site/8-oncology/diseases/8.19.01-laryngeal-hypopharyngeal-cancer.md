## 2. 2. Laryngeal Cancer

### 1. Anatomy & Subsites

| Subsite | Frequency | Lymphatic Drainage | Presentation | Prognosis |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Glottic (True Vocal Cords)** | **60%** | **Sparse** (Late nodes) | **Early Hoarseness** (Best) | **Best** |
| **Supraglottic (Epiglottis, Aryepiglottic Folds, Ventricle)** | **35%** | **Rich** (Early nodes, Bilateral) | Late (Airway, Dysphagia, Otalgia) | Intermediate |
| **Subglottic** | **5%** | Rich | Late (Stridor) | **Worst** (Advanced at dx) |

### 2. Staging (AJCC 8th) — Glottic

| T Stage | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Tis | Carcinoma in situ |
| T1 | **Limited to Vocal Cords** |
| T1a | One vocal cord |
| T1b | Both vocal cords |
| T2 | **Supraglottic/Subglottic Extension** OR **Impaired Vocal Cord Mobility** |
| T3 | **Vocal Cord Fixation** OR **Paraglottic Space** OR **Inner Cortex Thyroid Cartilage** |
| T4a | **Thyroid Cartilage Cortical Invasion** OR **Extralaryngeal Spread** (Strap muscles, Thyroid, Oesophagus) |
| T4b | **Prevertebral Space** OR **Carotid Encasement** OR **Mediastinal Invasion** |

**N Staging:** N1: Single ipsilateral ≤3cm; N2a: Single ipsilateral 3-6cm; N2b: Multiple ipsilateral ≤6cm; N2c: Bilateral/contralateral ≤6cm; N3: >6cm (Same for all HNC)

## 3. 3. Management

### 1. Early Glottic (T1-2 N0)

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Early Glottic T1-2 N0] --> B[**Primary Radiotherapy (Standard)**]
    B --> B1[**IMRT/VMAT 63-66Gy/28-33fx** (T1); **66-70Gy/33-35fx** (T2)]
    B --> B2[**Voice Preservation**: **Excellent** (>90% good voice)]
    B --> B3[**Surgery (Cordectomy/Endoscopic) — Alternative**
If RT contraindicated/Failed RT/Preferred by patient
**TORS/Transoral Laser Microsurgery (TLM)**]
    B1 --> C[**Surveillance: Laryngoscopy q3mo×2yr, q6mo×3yr**]
```

### 2. Early Supraglottic (T1-2 N0)

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Early Supraglottic T1-2 N0] --> B[**Option 1: Surgery**]
    B --> B1[**Supraglottic Laryngectomy (HEMI/Horizontal)**
**Preserves Voice** (No TEP needed)
**Oncologic Equivalent to RT**]
    B --> B2[**Transoral Laser (TLM) / TORS**
Minimally invasive]
    B --> C[**Option 2: Primary RT**
**66-70Gy/33-35fx** (Higher dose than Glottic)
**Elective Neck RT (II-III) for N0** (Rich lymphatics)]
    C --> D[**Surveillance**]
    B2 --> D
```

### 3. Locally Advanced Laryngeal (T3-4, N+)

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Locally Advanced Laryngeal (T3-4, N+)] --> B[**Larynx Preservation Strategy**]
    B --> B1[**CCRT: Cisplatin 100mg/m² q3w ×3 + IMRT 70Gy/35fx**
**RTOG 91-11**: CCRT > Induction+RT > RT alone (Larynx Preservation 88% vs 75% vs 70%)]
    B --> B2[**Induction Chemo (TPF) → Response → RT** (GORTEC 2000 — Inferior to CCRT for Preservation)]
    B1 --> C[**Response Assessment (Laryngoscopy + PET-CT at 12wk)**]
    B2 --> C
    C -->|**CR**| D[**Surveillance**]
    C -->|**Residual / Recurrence**| E[**Salvage Total Laryngectomy + Neck Dissection**]
```

### 4. Total Laryngectomy (Salvage / Primary T4b)

| Indication | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Salvage** | CCRT failure, Recurrence, Persistent disease |
| **Primary** | T4b (Prevertebral/Carotid), Extensive cartilage destruction |
| **Procedure** | **Total Laryngectomy + Bilateral Neck Dissection (I-V)** + **Tracheostomy (Permanent)** |
| **Voice Rehab** | **TEP (Tracheoesophageal Puncture)** — **Primary (at laryngectomy) or Secondary** — **Blom-Singer / Provox** — **Gold Standard** |
| **Alternatives** | **Electrolarynx**, **Oesophageal Speech** |

## 4. 4. Hypopharyngeal Cancer

### 1. Anatomy & Presentation

| Subsite | Frequency | Presentation | Lymphatic Spread |
|---------|-----------|--------------|------------------|
| **Pyriform Sinus** | **70%** | Late (Dysphagia, Otalgia, Neck Mass) | **Early, Bilateral** (Levels II-IV) |
| **Postcricoid** | 20% | Dysphagia, Weight Loss | Early, Bilateral |
| **Posterior Pharyngeal Wall** | 10% | Late | Bilateral |

**Key: Late Presentation** — **>70% Stage III-IV at diagnosis**

### 2. Staging (AJCC 8th)

| T Stage | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| T1 | ≤2cm, One subsite |
| T2 | >2-4cm, OR ≥2 subsites |
| T3 | >4cm, **OR Laryngeal Invasion** (Vocal Cord Fixation) |
| T4a | **Thyroid/Cricoid Cartilage**, **Beyond Hypopharynx** (Thyroid, Oesophagus) |
| T4b | **Prevertebral**, **Carotid**, **Mediastinum** |

## 6. 6. Voice Rehabilitation

| Method | Description | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-------------|------|------|
| **TEP (Tracheoesophageal Puncture)** | **Primary (at Laryngectomy) or Secondary**; **Prosthesis (Blom-Singer/Provox)** shunt air from Trachea → Oesophagus | **Best Voice Quality**, Hands-free (hands-free valves), **Gold Standard** | **Prosthesis Maintenance**, Leakage, Candida, Stenosis |
| **Electrolarynx** | **Handheld Device**; Vibrates neck/cheek → Sound articulated by mouth | **Immediate**, No Surgery, Low Cost | **Monotone**, Handheld, Stigma |
| **Oesophageal Speech** | **Air Swallowed → Oesophagus → Released** → Vibrations → Articulated | **No Device**, Hands-free | **Difficult to Learn**, Limited Duration/Volume, Low Success Rate |

## 7. 7. Key Trials

| Trial | Design | Key Result |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **RTOG 91-11** | **CCRT (Cisplatin+RT) vs Induction (Cis/5-FU)→RT vs RT Alone** | **CCRT: Best Larynx Preservation (88% @2yr) & Loco-Regional Control**; **Induction+RT: No Survival Benefit over RT** |
| **GORTEC 2000** | **Induction (TPF) → RT vs CCRT** | **CCRT Non-Inferior**; **Induction → More Toxicity, Delayed RT** |
| **VA Larynx Study** | **Induction Cis/5-FU → RT vs Surgery+RT** | **Induction → 64% Larynx Preservation, Equivalent Survival** — **Started Organ Preservation** |

## 10. 10. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Glottic vs Supraglottic RT Dose** | **Glottic T1: 63-66Gy**; **Glottic T2: 66-70Gy**; **Supraglottic: 66-70Gy + Elective Neck RT** |
| **CCRT vs Induction+RT** | **RTOG 91-11: CCRT Superior** (Larynx Preservation, LRC); **GORTEC 2000: CCRT Non-Inferior to Induction+RT, Less Toxic** |
| **Laryngeal vs Hypopharyngeal Staging** | **Laryngeal: T1-2 by Cord Mobility/Invasion**; **Hypopharyngeal: T1-2 by Size/Subsites, T3 by Laryngeal Invasion/Size>4cm** |
| **Organ Preservation Criteria** | **T3-4, N0-2, Resectable, Patient Fit for Cisplatin, No Cartilage Invasion (T4a relative)**; **T4b = Surgery** |
| **TEP Complications** | **Prosthesis Leakage**, **Candida**, **Granulation**, **Stenosis**, **Aspiration** — **Regular SLP Follow-up** |
| **Supraglottic Laryngectomy vs Total** | **Supraglottic: Preserves True Cords (Voice Intact), Removes Supraglottis**; **Total: Removes All Larynx, Permanent Tracheostomy** |
