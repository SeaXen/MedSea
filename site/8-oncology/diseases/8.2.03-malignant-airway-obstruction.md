> [!tip] **FCPS/MRCP Priority: HIGH**
> **Malignant Airway Obstruction = Life-Threatening Oncologic Emergency**; **Endobronchial Tumour**: Lung (NSCLC/SCLC), Metastatic (Breast, Colon, Melanoma); **Extrinsic Compression**: Mediastinal Lymphoma, Thymoma, Thyroid, Lymph Nodes; **Tracheal Invasion**: Thyroid, Lung, Oesophageal; **Bronchoscopy (Rigid/Flexible)** → **Diagnosis, Debulking, Stent**; **RT**; **Tracheostomy** (Upper Airway); **Emergency**: Stridor, Dyspnoea at Rest.

---

## 1. 1. Learning Objectives
By the end of this note you should be able to:
- [ ] Recognise **clinical presentation** of malignant airway obstruction
- [ ] Identify **causes**: Endobronchial tumour, Extrinsic compression, Tracheal invasion
- [ ] Perform **urgent bronchoscopy** (Rigid preferred for obstruction) for diagnosis and intervention
- [ ] Place **endobronchial stent** (Silicone/Metal) for extrinsic compression
- [ ] Perform **endobronchial debulking** (Laser, APC, Cryotherapy, Brachytherapy)
- [ ] Indicate **radiotherapy** for primary treatment or post-stent consolidation
- [ ] Perform **tracheostomy** for upper airway obstruction
- [ ] Manage **emergency airway** in the crashing patient

## 2. 2. Causes of Malignant Airway Obstruction

| Category | Tumours | Mechanism |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **Endobronchial Tumour** | **Lung (NSCLC 80%, SCLC 20%)**, **Metastatic** (Breast, Colon, Renal, Melanoma, Sarcoma) | **Intraluminal Growth** → Luminal Narrowing/Occlusion |
| **Extrinsic Compression** | **Mediastinal Lymphoma (HL, NHL)**, **Thymoma**, **Thyroid (Goitre/Cancer)**, **Lymph Nodes**, **Teratoma** | **External Compression** of Trachea/Main Bronchi |
| **Tracheal Invasion** | **Thyroid Cancer (Anaplastic, Medullary)**, **Lung (Direct Extension)**, **Oesophageal Cancer** | **Direct Wall Invasion** → Rigid Stenosis |
| **Lymphangitic Carcinomatosis** | **Breast, Lung, Stomach, Pancreas** | **Diffuse Lymphatic Spread** → Interstitial Oedema, Airway Narrowing |

## 3. 3. Clinical Presentation

| Symptom | Urgency | Significance |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Stridor** | **EMERGENCY** | **Fixed Upper Airway Obstruction** (Trachea/Main Bronchi) |
| **Dyspnoea at Rest** | **EMERGENCY** | **Severe Obstruction** |
| **Dyspnoea on Exertion** | **URGENT** | **Progressive Obstruction** |
| **Cough** | | Common, May Be Productive |
| **Haemoptysis** | | Tumour Erosion |
| **Hoarseness** | **URGENT** | **Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve Involvement** |
| **Wheeze** | | **Variable** (Fixed vs Variable Obstruction) |
| **Cyanosis/Altered Consciousness** | **EMERGENCY** | **Impending Respiratory Failure** |

## 4. 4. Assessment & Diagnosis

| Investigation | Role | Key Details |
|---------------|------|-------------|
| **Clinical Assessment** | **Immediate** | **Stridor** (Inspiratory = Upper Airway; Expiratory = Lower), **Tracheal Deviation**, **Use of Accessory Muscles**, **Cyanosis** |
| **Rigid Bronchoscopy (Preferred)** | **Gold Standard** | **Diagnosis**, **Debulking**, **Stent Placement**, **Airway Control**; **GA Required** |
| **Flexible Bronchoscopy** | **If Rigid Not Available** | **Diagnosis**, **Limited Debulking**, **No Stent Placement** |
| **CT Chest (Contrast)** | **Staging, Extent, Surgical Planning** | **Luminal Narrowing, Wall Thickening, Extrinsic Mass**, **Lymph Nodes** |
| **CT Neck** | Upper Airway Assessment | Tracheal Stenosis, Thyroid Invasion |
| **Flow-Volume Loop** | **Physiological Assessment** | **Fixed Obstruction**: Plateau on Both Inspiration/Expiration; **Variable Extrathoracic**: Inspiratory Plateau; **Variable Intrathoracic**: Expiratory Plateau |
| **MRI** | Soft Tissue Detail | Tracheal Wall Invasion, Perineural Spread |

## 6. 6. Bronchoscopic Interventions

### 1. Rigid Bronchoscopy (Gold Standard for Obstruction)

| Advantage | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Airway Control** | **Ventilation Possible** During Procedure |
| **Suction** | **Large Bore** for Blood/Secretions |
| **Instrument Channel** | **Large** (Laser, Forceps, Cryo, Stent Delivery) |
| **Debulking** | **Laser (Nd:YAG), Argon Plasma Coagulation, Cryotherapy, Mechanical (Forceps/Snare)** |
| **Stent Placement** | **Silicone (Dumon) / Self-Expanding Metal Stent (SEMS)** |

### 2. Flexible Bronchoscopy

| Limitation | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| **No Ventilation Control** | **Risk in Severe Obstruction** |
| **Smaller Channel** | **Limited Debulking/Stent Options** |
| **Use Case** | **Mild Obstruction**, **Diagnosis**, **Awake Intubation Bridge** |

### 3. Debulking Modalities

| Modality | Mechanism | Best For |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Nd:YAG Laser** | **Coagulation/Vaporisation** | **Vascular Tumours, Haemostasis** |
| **Argon Plasma Coagulation (APC)** | **Non-Contact Coagulation** | **Diffuse/Oozing, Peripheral Lesions** |
| **Cryotherapy** | **Freeze-Thaw Necrosis** | **Endobronchial Tumours, Recurrent** |
| **Mechanical (Forceps/Snare)** | **Physical Removal** | **Pedunculated, Non-Vascular** |
| **Brachytherapy** | **Intraluminal Radioactive Source** | **Post-Debulking Consolidation, Recurrent** |

---

## 7. 7. Airway Stents

| Type | Indication | Pros | Cons |
|------|------------|------|------|
| **Silicone (Dumon, Hood)** | **Extrinsic Compression, Fistula, Benign Stenosis** | **Removable**, **Low Migration**, **Good Conformity** | **Requires Rigid Bronchoscopy**, **Larger Diameter Needed**, **Granulation Tissue** |
| **Self-Expanding Metal Stent (SEMS) — Uncovered** | **Malignant Extrinsic Compression** | **Easy Deployment (Flexible/Rigid)**, **Low Profile**, **Radial Force** | **Permanent**, **Granulation/Ingrowth**, **Difficult Removal** |
| **SEMS — Covered** | **Fistula, Haemostasis, Extrinsic Compression** | **Prevents Tumour Ingrowth**, **Seals Fistula** | **Migration Risk Higher**, **Granulation at Ends** |

### 1. Stent Selection Algorithm

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Need Stent] --> B{**Extrinsic Compression / Fistula**}
    B -->|**YES**| C[**Silicone Stent (Preferred)** — Removable, Conformable]
    B -->|**NO (Endobronchial Tumour / Malignant Stricture)**| D{**Tumour Ingrowth Risk**}
    D -->|**HIGH**| E[**Covered SEMS** — Prevents Ingrowth]
    D -->|**LOW / Short-Term**| F[**Uncovered SEMS** — Easy Deploy]
    C --> G[**Rigid Bronchoscopy Placement**]
    E --> G
    F --> G
```

## 8. 8. Radiotherapy

| Setting | Dose/Fractionation | Role |
|--------|-------------------|------|
| **Primary (Unresectable)** | **30Gy/10fx or 20Gy/5fx** | **Palliation, Symptom Control** |
| **Post-Bronchoscopic Debulking** | **30Gy/10fx** | **Consolidation, Prevent Recurrence** |
| **Post-Stent** | **20Gy/5fx** | **Maintain Patency** |
| **Brachytherapy (HDR)** | **15-20Gy in 1-2 Fractions** | **Endoburn Recurrence, Localised** |

---

## 9. 9. Tracheostomy

| Indication | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| **Upper Airway Obstruction** (Laryngeal, Supraglottic, Tracheal Stenosis Above Cuff) | **Surgical / Percutaneous** |
| **Prolonged Ventilation** | **Post-Obstruction Relief** |
| **Secretion Management** | **Aspiration Prevention** |
| **Emergency Cricothyroidotomy** | **Cannot Intubate, Cannot Oxygenate (CICO)** |

## 10. 10. Emergency Airway Management (Crashing Patient)

```mermaid
flowchart TD

    G -->|**YES**| H[**Prepare for Surgical Airway**]
    G -->|**NO (CICO)**| I[**Emergency Cricothyroidotomy / Tracheostomy**]
    E --> J[**Bronchoscopy ASAP for Definitive Management**]
    H --> J
    I --> J
```

## 13. 13. Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Rigid vs Flexible Bronchoscopy** | **Rigid: GA, Ventilation, Debulking, Stenting (Large Channel)**; **Flexible: Awake, Diagnosis, Small Channel, No Stenting** |
| **Silicone vs Metal Stent** | **Silicone: Removable, Fistula, Extrinsic, Requires Rigid**; **SEMS: Permanent, Easy Deploy, Tumour Ingrowth Risk (Use Covered)** |
| **Laser vs APC** | **Nd:YAG: Contact, Vascular, Coagulation**; **APC: Non-Contact, Diffuse, Argon Plasma** |
| **Tracheostomy vs Cricothyroidotomy** | **Tracheostomy: Definitive, Surgical/Percutaneous, Below Cricoid**; **Cricothyroidotomy: Emergency CICO, Through Cricothyroid Membrane** |
| **Stridor vs Wheeze** | **Stridor: Inspiratory, Upper Airway (Trachea/Larynx), Fixed Obstruction**; **Wheeze: Expiratory, Lower Airway (Bronchi), Variable Obstruction** |
| **Brachytherapy Indication** | **Post-Debulking Local Recurrence**, **Single Fraction HDR**, **Intraluminal Source** |
| **Flow-Volume Loop Patterns** | **Fixed: Plateau Both Phases**; **Var Extrathoracic: Inspiratory Plateau**; **Var Intrathoracic: Expiratory Plateau** |

**Mnemonic: AIRWAY-OBSTRUCTION**
- **A**irway: **Stridor = Emergency** (Fixed Upper Airway Obstruction)
- **I**ntrinsic: **Endobronchial Tumour** (Lung, Mets)
- **R**igid Bronchoscopy: **Gold Standard** (GA, Debulking, Stenting)
- **W**heeze vs Stridor: **Wheeze=Expiratory/Lower**, **Stridor=Inspiratory/Upper**
- **A**ntegrade: **Flexible Bronchoscopy** (Mild, Diagnosis, Awake)
- **Y**ou Choose Stent: **Silicone (Extrinsic/Fistula) vs SEMS (Malignant)**
- **O**perative: **Debulking (Laser/APC/Cryo), Brachy, RT**
- **B**ronchoscopy: **Rigid (Gold) vs Flexible (Limited)**
- **S**tent: **Silicone (Extrinsic) vs Covered SEMS (Malignant)**
- **T**racheostomy: **Upper Airway Obstruction, Prolonged Vent**
- **R**adiotherapy: **Post-Stent/Debulking Consolidation**
- **U**pper Airway: **Stridor = Inspiratory**, **Hoarseness = RLN**
- **C**ricoid: **Emergency Cricothyroidotomy (CICO)**
- **T**umour Invasion: **Thyroid, Lung, Oesophagus → Tracheal Stenosis**
- **I**ntrinsic vs Extrinsic: **Endobronchial vs Mediastinal Compression**
- **O**xygenation: **First Priority (O2, Sit Up, Adrenaline Neb)**
- **N**ebulised Adrenaline: **Temporising (5mg in 5mL Saline)**
