## 2. 2. 🧠 Core Concept: PK Changes in Liver Disease

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Cirrhosis / Liver Disease] --> B[Architectural Distortion
↓ Hepatocyte mass
Portosystemic shunting]
    B --> C1[↓ FIRST-PASS METABOLISM
↑ Bioavailability (oral)
High extraction drugs: propranolol, morphine, nitrates, verapamil]
    B --> C2[↓ HEPATIC CLEARANCE
↓ CYP450 (1A2, 2C9, 2C19, 3A4)
↓ Phase II (UGT, SULT, GST)
Low extraction drugs: warfarin, phenytoin, theophylline]
    B --> C3[↓ PROTEIN SYNTHESIS
↓ Albumin → ↑ Free fraction (acidic drugs: warfarin, phenytoin, valproate)
↓ α1-AGP → ↑ Free fraction (basic drugs: lidocaine, propranolol)]
    B --> C4[↑ VOLUME OF DISTRIBUTION
Ascites, oedema → ↑ Vd (hydrophilic drugs: aminoglycosides, digoxin)
↓ Albumin → ↑ Vd (protein-bound drugs)]
    B --> C5[RENAL IMPAIRMENT (HRS)
↓ GFR → ↓ Renal clearance
Combined hepatorenal dosing needed]
    
    C1 --> D[CLINICAL EFFECT: ↑ Drug levels, ↑ toxicity risk]
    C2 --> D
    C3 --> D
    C4 --> D
    C5 --> D
```

### 1. Child-Pugh Classification (Prescribing Relevance)

| Parameter | Points 1 | Points 2 | Points 3 |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|
| **Bilirubin (μmol/L)** | <34 | 34–50 | >50 |
| **Albumin (g/L)** | >35 | 28–35 | <28 |
| **INR** | <1.7 | 1.7–2.3 | >2.3 |
| **Ascites** | None | Mild/Med (diuretic responsive) | Severe (refractory) |
| **Encephalopathy** | None | Grade 1–2 | Grade 3–4 |

| Class | Score | 1-y Survival | Prescribing Implication |
|-------|-------|--------------|------------------------|
| **A** | 5–6 | ~100% | **Mild** — Minor dose adjustments; most drugs OK with monitoring |
| **B** | 7–9 | ~80% | **Moderate** — Significant dose reductions; avoid hepatotoxic drugs; TDM essential |
| **C** | 10–15 | ~45% | **Severe** — Avoid many drugs; major dose reductions; prefer non-hepatic clearance; TDM mandatory |

> **Viva Key:** *Child-Pugh B = moderate impairment = reduce dose ~50% for hepatically cleared drugs. Child-Pugh C = severe = avoid if possible, use alternatives with renal clearance.*

## 3. 3. ️⃣ Drug Dosing Principles in Hepatic Impairment

### 1. General Rules

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Avoid hepatotoxic drugs** | Intrinsic (dose-dependent) + Idiosyncratic (unpredictable) — see Section 2 |
| **Reduce dose for high extraction drugs** | ↓ First-pass → ↑ Bioavailability → **↓ Dose** (e.g., propranolol, morphine, verapamil, nitrates) |
| **Reduce dose for low extraction drugs with hepatic clearance** | ↓ CYP/UGT → ↓ Clearance → **↓ Dose or ↑ Interval** (e.g., warfarin, phenytoin, theophylline, diazepam) |
| **Prefer renal clearance drugs** | If alternative exists with renal elimination (e.g., atenolol > propranolol; gabapentin > pregabalin? both renal) |
| **Monitor free drug levels** | ↓ Albumin → ↑ Free fraction → **Measure FREE level** (phenytoin, valproate, warfarin) |
| **TDM mandatory** | Narrow therapeutic index drugs: warfarin, phenytoin, digoxin, lithium, immunosuppressants |
| **Start low, go slow** | Lower initial dose; titrate to effect; monitor for toxicity |

### 2. Dose Adjustment Categories (FDA/EMA)

| Category | Action | Examples |
|----------|--------|----------|
| **No adjustment needed** | Renal clearance; minimal hepatic metabolism | Atenolol, Gabapentin, Pregabalin, Digoxin (mostly renal), Lithium, Aminoglycosides, Vancomycin, Most antibiotics (penicillins, cephalosporins, carbapenems) |
| **Reduce dose / Extend interval** | Hepatic clearance; moderate impairment | **Child-Pugh B**: ↓ 50% dose or ↑ interval 2x; **Child-Pugh C**: ↓ 75% or avoid |
| **Avoid / Contraindicated** | Hepatotoxic; high extraction + narrow TI; lactic acidosis risk | **NSAIDs, Metformin (Child-Pugh C), High-dose paracetamol >2g/day, Sedatives (benzos, Z-drugs), Certain antibiotics (isoniazid, rifampicin, flucloxacillin in susceptible)** |

## 4. 4. ️⃣ Hepatotoxic Drugs: Avoid or Use with Extreme Caution in Liver Disease

### 1. Intrinsic (Dose-Dependent) Hepatotoxins

| Drug | Mechanism | Risk in Cirrhosis |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Paracetamol >4g/day (or >2g/day in malnutrition/alcohol)** | NAPQI accumulation → hepatocellular necrosis | **↑ Risk** — ↓ Glutathione stores; **max 2–3g/day** in cirrhosis |
| **Methotrexate** | Hepatocyte apoptosis → fibrosis | **Contraindicated** in significant liver disease |
| **Isoniazid** | Hydrazine metabolites → hepatocellular injury | **Monitor LFTs monthly**; avoid if Child-Pugh C |
| **Rifampicin** | CYP induction + direct toxicity | Cholestatic injury; **monitor LFTs** |
| **Pyrazinamide** | Hepatocellular injury | **Monitor LFTs**; avoid if Child-Pugh C |
| **Valproate** | Mitochondrial toxicity → microvesicular steatosis | **Avoid** in liver disease; ↑ ammonia (hyperammonaemic encephalopathy) |
| **Amiodarone** | Phospholipidosis → steatohepatitis | **Monitor LFTs**; long half-life = prolonged risk |
| **Methyldopa** | Autoimmune hepatitis-like | Rare but **avoid** if alternative exists |

### 2. Idiosyncratic (Unpredictable) Hepatotoxins — High Risk in Cirrhosis

| Drug | Pattern | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **Flucloxacillin** | **Cholestatic** (delayed 2–4 weeks) | **HLA-B*57:01** not linked; risk ↑ with age >55, duration >2 weeks |
| **Co-amoxiclav** | **Cholestatic** (1–6 weeks) | Most common cause of DILI in UK; **avoid** if prior DILI |
| **Carbamazepine / Phenytoin / Phenobarbital** | **Hepatocellular / Mixed / Hypersensitivity (DRESS/SJS)** | **HLA-B*15:02** for CBZ/PHT; monitor LFTs first 3 months |
| **Allopurinol** | **Mixed / DRESS / SJS** | **HLA-B*58:01** screen; start low (50mg), titrate slowly |
| **Sulfonamides (TMP-SMX, sulfasalazine)** | **Hypersensitivity / Cholestatic** | Avoid in cirrhosis if possible |
| **Statins (all)** | **Hepatocellular (asymptomatic ALT rise)** | **Not contraindicated** in stable chronic liver disease; monitor LFTs; avoid acute liver failure |
| **Azoles (fluconazole, itraconazole, voriconazole)** | **Hepatocellular / Cholestatic** | Monitor LFTs; avoid in Child-Pugh C |
| **Antiretrovirals (NNRTIs > PIs > INSTIs)** | **Hepatocellular** | NVP > EFV > DTG; monitor LFTs monthly initially |

> **Viva Key:** *Flucloxacillin/co-amoxiclav = cholestatic DILI (delayed). INH/rifampicin/pyrazinamide = dose-dependent + idiosyncratic. Allopurinol = HLA-B*58:01 screen. Valproate = avoid in liver disease (↑ ammonia). Statins = generally SAFE in stable CLD.*

## 6. 6. ️⃣ Drug-Induced Liver Injury (DILI): Patterns & Management

### 1. DILI Patterns

| Pattern | Biochemistry | Examples | Management |
|---------|--------------|----------|------------|
| **Hepatocellular** | **ALT/AST ↑↑↑** (×10–100 ULN), Bilirubin ↑, ALP normal/mild ↑ | Paracetamol, Isoniazid, Valproate, Phenytoin, Carbamazepine, Statins (rare), Antiretrovirals | **Stop drug immediately**; monitor LFTs; N-acetylcysteine for paracetamol |
| **Cholestatic** | **ALP ↑↑** (×2–5 ULN), ALT/AST mild ↑, Bilirubin ↑, GGT ↑↑ | Flucloxacillin, Co-amoxiclav, Macrolides, Rifampicin, Sulfonamides, Chlorpromazine, Azathioprine | **Stop drug**; ursodeoxycholic acid for pruritus; monitor bilirubin (Hy's Law) |
| **Mixed** | Both ALT & ALP ↑↑ (ratio 2–5) | Amoxicillin-clavulanate, Flucloxacillin, TMP-SMX, Phenytoin, Carbamazepine, Allopurinol | **Stop drug**; monitor for Hy's Law |
| **Vascular** | SOS (sinusoidal obstruction syndrome), Budd-Chiari, Nodular regenerative hyperplasia | Cyclophosphamide, Azathioprine, Oxaliplatin, Oral contraceptives | **Stop drug**; specialist management |

### 2. Hy's Law (Predictor of Fatal DILI)
> **ALT/AST >3× ULN + Total Bilirubin >2× ULN + No ALP elevation (or <2× ULN) = ~10–50% mortality / transplant**

- **Action:** Stop drug immediately; urgent hepatology referral; monitor for liver failure

## 7. 7. ️⃣ Special Situations

### 1. Paracetamol in Cirrhosis
- **Max dose: 2–3g/day** (not 4g)
- Avoid chronic use
- **Malnutrition/alcohol** → ↓ glutathione → ↑ toxicity at lower doses
- **IV paracetamol** — same limit; weight-based dosing (<50kg: 15mg/kg/dose)

### 2. Alcohol + Drugs in Liver Disease
- **Acute alcohol** → inhibits CYP2E1, CYP3A4 → ↑ drug levels (metronidazole, warfarin, phenytoin)
- **Chronic alcohol** → induces CYP2E1, CYP3A4, CYP1A2 → ↓ drug levels (paracetamol toxicity at lower doses, theophylline)
- **Disulfiram-like reaction**: Metronidazole, TMP-SMX, Cephalosporins (cefotetan), Griseofulvin, Chloramphenicol + alcohol → flushing, tachycardia, hypotension

### 3. Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC) / Transplant List
- **Child-Pugh C / MELD >15** — transplant assessment
- **Drugs to avoid pre-transplant**: NSAIDs, nephrotoxins, hepatotoxins
- **Post-transplant**: Immunosuppressant interactions (see [[Drug Interactions/High-risk drug combinations/Immunosuppressant interactions]])

## 9. 9. 🎤 Viva Questions (Expected Answers)

| # | Question | Expected Answer |
|---|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | How does cirrhosis affect drug pharmacokinetics? | **1) ↓ First-pass metabolism** → ↑ bioavailability of high extraction drugs (propranolol, morphine). **2) ↓ Hepatic clearance** (CYP450, UGT) → ↓ clearance of low extraction drugs. **3) ↓ Albumin synthesis** → ↑ free fraction of protein-bound drugs (warfarin, phenytoin). **4) ↑ Vd** (ascites, oedema) → ↑ Vd of hydrophilic drugs. **5) Portosystemic shunting** → bypass liver. |
| 2 | Child-Pugh B cirrhosis: general dosing approach? | **Reduce dose ~50% or double interval** for hepatically cleared drugs. Avoid hepatotoxins. Prefer renal clearance alternatives. TDM for NTI drugs. Monitor free levels for highly protein-bound. |
| 3 | Which beta-blocker is preferred in cirrhosis? | **Atenolol** (renal clearance, no first-pass). Avoid propranolol (high extraction → ↑ bioavailability). |
| 4 | Paracetamol max dose in cirrhosis? | **2–3g/day** (not 4g). ↓ Glutathione stores → ↑ NAPQI toxicity risk. |
| 5 | Why are NSAIDs contraindicated in cirrhosis? | **1) GI bleed risk** (varices, gastropathy). **2) Renal impairment** → precipitates HRS (↓ prostaglandin-mediated renal perfusion). **3) Fluid retention** → worsens ascites. |
| 6 | Warfarin in cirrhosis: why is INR unpredictable? | **↓ Albumin** → ↑ free warfarin; **↓ CYP2C9** → ↓ clearance; **↓ Vitamin K-dependent factor synthesis** (liver synthetic dysfunction) → baseline INR elevated. **Result: INR does not reflect anticoagulation intensity.** |
| 7 | DOAC choice in Child-Pugh B cirrhosis with AF? | **Apixaban 2.5mg BD** (licensed for Child-Pugh B). Avoid rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban in Child-Pugh B/C. |
| 8 | Metformin in cirrhosis: contraindicated in which Child-Pugh? | **Child-Pugh B and C** (lactic acidosis risk — ↓ lactate clearance by liver). Child-Pugh A: use with caution, monitor lactate. |
| 9 | Phenytoin monitoring in cirrhosis — what level? | **FREE phenytoin level** (therapeutic 1–2 mg/L). Total level misleading due to ↓ albumin. Corrected formula unreliable. |
| 10 | Valproate in liver disease — why avoid? | **1) Hepatotoxicity** (microvesicular steatosis, mitochondrial toxicity). **2) ↑ Ammonia** → hyperammonaemic encephalopathy (↓ urea cycle). **3) ↓ Albumin** → ↑ free fraction. |
| 11 | Flucloxacillin DILI pattern and timing? | **Cholestatic**, delayed onset **2–4 weeks** (sometimes up to 8 weeks). Risk ↑ age >55, duration >2 weeks. |
| 12 | Co-amoxiclav DILI — most common cause in UK? | **Cholestatic**, 1–6 weeks. **Most common cause of DILI in UK**. Avoid if prior DILI. |
| 13 | Preferred sedative in cirrhosis if essential? | **Lorazepam or Oxazepam** — no active metabolites, glucuronidation (Phase II preserved longer), short half-life. Avoid diazepam, chlordiazepoxide, Z-drugs. |
| 14 | Hy's Law criteria? | **ALT/AST >3× ULN + Total Bilirubin >2× ULN + ALP <2× ULN** = high risk of fatal DILI/transplant (~10–50%). |

## 10. 10. 🧩 Confusions & Mnemonics

| Confusion | Clarification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **"All statins contraindicated in liver disease"** | **FALSE.** Statins are **safe in stable chronic liver disease** (NAFLD, compensated cirrhosis). Monitor LFTs. Avoid in **acute liver failure / decompensated**. Rosuvastatin/pravastatin preferred (less CYP). |
| **"Metformin safe in Child-Pugh A"** | **Caution.** Child-Pugh A: may use with close lactate monitoring. Child-Pugh B/C: **contraindicated**. Lactic acidosis risk from ↓ hepatic lactate clearance. |
| **"Total phenytoin level OK in cirrhosis"** | **FALSE.** ↓ Albumin → ↑ free fraction. **Must measure FREE level.** Total level underestimates free drug. |
| **"All benzodiazepines same in liver disease"** | **Lorazepam/oxazepam/temazepam** = glucuronidation (Phase II) → relatively preserved. **Diazepam/chlordiazepoxide/clonazepam** = CYP3A4/2C19 (Phase I) → markedly impaired. |
| **"Child-Pugh and MELD interchangeable for dosing"** | **Child-Pugh** used for **drug dosing** (A/B/C categories). **MELD** used for **transplant prioritisation** (continuous score). Both reflect severity but Child-Pugh is standard in dosing guidelines. |
| **"Rifampicin safe in liver disease if monitor LFTs"** | Rifampicin = **hepatotoxic + potent enzyme inducer** → worsens other drug levels. Avoid if possible in Child-Pugh C; use with extreme caution in B. |
| **"DOACs all contraindicated in cirrhosis"** | **Apixaban licensed for Child-Pugh B** (2.5mg BD). Rivaroxaban/dabigatran/edoxaban not recommended B/C. Warfarin = unpredictable but used (INR monitoring). |

> **Mnemonic: LIVER PRESCRIBING**  
> **L**iver disease PK: ↓ First-pass, ↓ Clearance, ↓ Albumin, ↑ Vd, Shunting  
> **I**ntrinsic hepatotoxins: Paracetamol (>2g), Methotrexate, INH, Rifampicin, PZA, Valproate, Amiodarone  
> **V**alproate avoid: ↑ Ammonia, hepatotoxicity  
> **E**xtraction ratio matters: High extraction (propranolol, morphine) → ↑ bioavailability → ↓ dose  
> **R**enal alternatives preferred: Atenolol, Gabapentin, Levetiracetam, DOACs (apixaban), Insulin, SGLT2i  
> **P**rotein binding: ↓ Albumin → ↑ free warfarin, phenytoin, valproate → **measure FREE level**  
> **R**ifampicin/INH/PZA: Hepatotoxic + enzyme induction — avoid/reduce  
> **E**nzyme induction in chronic alcohol: CYP2E1, 3A4, 1A2 ↑ → ↓ drug levels  
> **S**tatins SAFE in stable CLD: Monitor LFTs; rosuvastatin/pravastatin preferred  
> **C**hild-Pugh: A=mild (minor adj), B=moderate (↓50%), C=severe (avoid)  
> **R**enal dose adjust needed too (HRS common)  
> **I**dosyncratic hepatotoxins: Flucloxacillin (cholestatic 2-4w), Co-amoxiclav (cholestatic 1-6w, #1 UK), CBZ/PHT/PB (DRESS/SJS, HLA-B*15:02), Allopurinol (HLA-B*58:01), Sulfonamides, Azoles  
> **B**enzos/Z-drugs AVOID: ↑ encephalopathy; if essential → lorazepam/oxazepam (glucuronidation)  
> **I**NSULIN/DPP-4/SGLT2i/GLP-1 preferred for DM; Metformin CI B/C; SU avoid  
> **N**SAIDs CONTRAINDICATED: GI bleed (varices), HRS, fluid retention  
> **G**lucuronidation preserved: Lorazepam, oxazepam, morphine? (M6G renal), paracetamol? (but glutathione ↓)

## 15. 15. 📌 Summary
- **PK in cirrhosis**: ↓ first-pass (↑ bioavailability high extraction), ↓ CYP/UGT clearance, ↓ albumin (↑ free drug), ↑ Vd (ascites), shunting
- **Child-Pugh**: A=mild (minor adj), B=moderate (↓50% dose/extend interval), C=severe (avoid hepatically cleared drugs)
- **Intrinsic hepatotoxins**: Paracetamol >2g/day, methotrexate, INH, rifampicin, pyrazinamide, valproate, amiodarone
- **Idiosyncratic hepatotoxins**: Flucloxacillin (cholestatic 2–4w), co-amoxiclav (cholestatic 1–6w, #1 UK), AEDs (DRESS/SJS/HLA), allopurinol (HLA-B*58:01), sulfonamides, azoles
- **Dosing**: Prefer renal clearance (atenolol, levetiracetam, apixaban, insulin, gabapentin, penicillins); avoid NSAIDs, metformin (Child B/C), sedatives, valproate, high-dose paracetamol; **free phenytoin level**; TDM for NTI
- **Statins**: Safe in stable CLD; monitor LFTs; rosuvastatin/pravastatin preferred
- **DILI**: Hy's Law (ALT>3×ULN + Bil>2×ULN + ALP<2×ULN) = high mortality risk

## 16. 16. ❓ MCQs (10)

1. **High extraction ratio drug — effect of cirrhosis on oral bioavailability?**  
   A. Decreased  B. **Increased**  C. Unchanged  D. Unpredictable  
   *Answer: B. ↓ First-pass metabolism → ↑ bioavailability (e.g., propranolol, morphine, verapamil, nitrates).*

2. **Preferred beta-blocker in cirrhosis (Child-Pugh B)?**  
   A. Propranolol  B. **Atenolol**  C. Metoprolol  D. Carvedilol  
   *Answer: B. Atenolol = renal clearance, no first-pass. Propranolol high extraction → ↑ bioavailability.*

3. **Maximum paracetamol dose in cirrhosis?**  
   A. 4g/day  B. **2–3g/day**  C. 1g/day  D. Contraindicated  
   *Answer: B. ↓ Glutathione → ↑ NAPQI toxicity risk at lower doses.*

4. **Which DOAC is licensed for use in Child-Pugh B cirrhosis?**  
   A. Rivaroxaban  B. Dabigatran  C. **Apixaban 2.5mg BD**  D. Edoxaban  
   *Answer: C. Apixaban 2.5mg BD is licensed for Child-Pugh B. Others not recommended.*

5. **Metformin contraindicated in which Child-Pugh class?**  
   A. A only  B. **B and C**  C. C only  D. All classes  
   *Answer: B. Lactic acidosis risk from ↓ hepatic lactate clearance. Child-Pugh A: caution with monitoring.*

6. **Phenytoin monitoring in cirrhosis — which level?**  
   A. Total  B. **Free**  C. Both  D. Neither  
   *Answer: B. ↓ Albumin → ↑ free fraction. Total level misleading. Free therapeutic range 1–2 mg/L.*

7. **Valproate avoided in liver disease because:**  
   A. Only hepatotoxicity  B. Only ↑ ammonia  C. **Both hepatotoxicity AND ↑ ammonia (hyperammonaemic encephalopathy) + ↑ free fraction**  D. Renal toxicity  
   *Answer: C. Triple risk: mitochondrial hepatotoxicity, ↑ ammonia (↓ urea cycle), ↑ free fraction (↓ albumin).*

8. **Hy's Law criteria for fatal DILI risk:**  
   A. ALT>3x + Bil>2x + ALP>2x  B. **ALT>3x + Bil>2x + ALP<2x**  C. ALT>5x + Bil>3x  D. ALP>3x + Bil>2x  
   *Answer: B. Hy's Law: ALT/AST >3× ULN + Total Bilirubin >2× ULN + ALP <2× ULN (no cholestasis).*

9. **Flucloxacillin DILI pattern and typical onset:**  
   A. Hepatocellular, 1–2 weeks  B. **Cholestatic, 2–4 weeks**  C. Mixed, 4–8 weeks  D. Vascular, immediate  
   *Answer: B. Cholestatic pattern, delayed onset 2–4 weeks (delayed up to 8 weeks).*

10. **Preferred sedative in cirrhosis if absolutely necessary?**  
    A. Diazepam  B. Zopiclone  C. **Lorazepam**  D. Clonazepam  
    *Answer: C. Lorazepam (or oxazepam) — glucuronidation (Phase II preserved), no active metabolites, short half-life.*

## 17. 17. 📋 SBAs (10)

1. **65M with Child-Pugh B cirrhosis (alcohol) needs rate control for AF. Current: propranolol 40mg TDS. Best switch?**  
   A. Increase propranolol  B. **Switch to atenolol 25mg OD**  C. Add digoxin  D. Switch to verapamil  
   *Answer: B. Propranolol high extraction → ↑ bioavailability in cirrhosis. Atenolol renal = preferred.*

2. **70F with cirrhosis on warfarin for mechanical mitral valve. INR 4.5 (target 2.5–3.5) on stable dose 5mg. No bleeding. Likely cause?**  
   A. Drug interaction  B. **Cirrhosis: ↓ albumin (↑ free warfarin) + ↓ CYP2C9 + ↓ factor synthesis**  C. Dietary vitamin K  D. Non-adherence  
   *Answer: B. Cirrhosis alters warfarin PK/PD unpredictably. ↓ albumin → ↑ free warfarin; ↓ CYP2C9 → ↓ clearance; ↓ factor synthesis → baseline INR elevated.*

3. **Patient with Child-Pugh C cirrhosis needs analgesia for femoral fracture. Best opioid?**  
   A. Morphine 10mg IV  B. **Transdermal buprenorphine 5μg/h**  C. Oral oxycodone 10mg  D. Codeine 30mg  
   *Answer: B. Transdermal buprenorphine bypasses first-pass, less accumulation. Morphine/oxycodone high extraction → ↑ bioavailability. Codeine prodrug unpredictable.*

4. **60M with cirrhosis (Child-Pugh B) newly diagnosed T2DM. HbA1c 58. First-line?**  
   A. Metformin 500mg BD  B. Gliclazide 80mg BD  C. **Linagliptin 5mg OD (or SGLT2i/GLP-1 RA)**  D. Pioglitazone 15mg OD  
   *Answer: C. Metformin CI Child-Pugh B. SU hypoglycaemia risk. Pioglitazone fluid retention. DPP-4/SGLT2i/GLP-1 preferred.*

5. **Patient on phenytoin 300mg OD for epilepsy develops cirrhosis (Child-Pugh B). Total phenytoin 12 mg/L (therapeutic 10–20). Albumin 22 g/L. Action?**  
   A. Increase dose  B. **No dose change; measure FREE phenytoin level**  C. Decrease dose  D. Switch to levetiracetam  
   *Answer: B. Hypoalbuminaemia → ↑ free fraction. Corrected total = 12/(0.2×2.2+0.1) = 22 mg/L (toxic). MUST measure FREE level.*

6. **Antibiotic for intra-abdominal infection in Child-Pugh C cirrhosis. Best choice?**  
   A. Co-amoxiclav  B. **Piperacillin-tazobactam (renal adjust)**  C. Clarithromycin  D. Rifampicin  
   *Answer: B. Penicillins/cephalosporins/carbapenems = renal clearance, safe in liver disease. Co-amoxiclav/DILI risk. Macrolides/rifampicin hepatotoxic.*

7. **Statins in compensated cirrhosis (Child-Pugh A) — correct statement?**  
   A. Contraindicated  B. **Safe; monitor LFTs; rosuvastatin/pravastatin preferred**  C. Only simvastatin allowed  D. Must stop if ALT >ULN  
   *Answer: B. Statins safe in stable CLD. Monitor LFTs. Rosuvastatin/pravastatin minimal CYP. Not contraindicated.*

8. **Hy's Law indicates:**  
   A. Drug-induced cholestasis  B. **High risk of fatal DILI / need for transplant**  C. Hepatocellular recovery  D. Safe to continue drug  
   *Answer: B. ALT>3x + Bil>2x + ALP<2x = 10–50% mortality/transplant risk.*

9. **Patient with cirrhosis on spironolactone 100mg develops hepatic encephalopathy. Precipitant?**  
   A. Spironolactone  B. **Hypokalaemia from diuretic → ↑ renal ammonia production**  C. Spironolactone hepatotoxicity  D. Hyperkalaemia  
   *Answer: B. Diuretics → hypokalaemia → ↑ renal ammoniagenesis → encephalopathy. Spironolactone itself not direct cause.*

10. **Rifampicin in Child-Pugh B cirrhosis with TB. Dosing?**  
    A. Standard dose  B. **Standard dose but MONITOR LFTs closely; avoid if Child-Pugh C**  C. Halve dose  D. Contraindicated  
    *Answer: B. Rifampicin hepatotoxic + enzyme inducer. Use standard dose with LFT monitoring in Child-Pugh B. Avoid Child-Pugh C.*
