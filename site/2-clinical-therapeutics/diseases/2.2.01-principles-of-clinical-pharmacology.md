## 1. 1. Scope

This heading covers the foundational pharmacology that underpins all prescribing decisions:

- **Pharmacodynamics (PD)** — "What the drug does to the body"
- **Pharmacokinetics (PK)** — "What the body does to the drug"
- **Factors influencing drug response** — age, organ function, genetics, adherence

## 3. 3. High-Yield FCPS/MRCP Pearls

| Concept | Definition | Clinical relevance |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Pharmacodynamics (PD)** | What the drug does to the body | Receptor binding, dose-response, mechanisms |
| **Pharmacokinetics (PK)** | What the body does to the drug | ADME, dosing, steady state |
| **Therapeutic Index** | ED50(toxic) / ED50(therapeutic) | TI<10 = high risk: digoxin, warfarin, insulin, phenytoin, opioids |
| **Volume of Distribution (Vd)** | Theoretical volume drug distributes in | Loading dose = Vd × target concentration |
| **Clearance (CL)** | Volume of plasma cleared per unit time | Determines maintenance dose |
| **Half-life (t½)** | Time for plasma concentration to halve | 4-5 t½ to steady state |
| **Bioavailability (F)** | Fraction reaching systemic circulation | IV = 100% (F=1); oral <100% (first-pass) |
| **Loading dose** | Initial dose to rapidly achieve target | When t½ is long (digoxin, amiodarone) |
| **Maintenance dose** | Dose to maintain target | = CL × target concentration / F |
| **Adherence** | Patient's behaviour matches agreed Rx | ~50% non-adherence in chronic disease |
| **Concordance** | Therapeutic partnership, shared decision | Patient-centred prescribing |
