# Pityriasis alba

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Disorders of Pigmentation

## Definition

Pityriasis alba is a common, benign hypopigmentation in children and adolescents, often with atopic dermatitis. Ill-defined, scaly, hypopigmented patches (face, especially cheeks; sometimes upper arms, trunk). Often more visible in dark skin. Related to mild eczema (subclinical) and UV exposure. Treatment: emollients, mild topical steroids or calcineurin inhibitors for inflammation, sun protection; usually resolves over months-years.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Pityriasis alba presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Age | Children, adolescents |
| Distribution | Face (cheeks), upper arms, trunk |
| Appearance | Ill-defined, scaly, hypopigmented patches |
| Atopy | Strong association with atopic dermatitis |
| UV | More visible in summer (contrast with tanned normal skin) |
| Histology | Mild spongiosis, reduced melanin |
| Differential | Vitiligo, tinea versicolor, pityriasis rosea, hypopigmented MF |
| Treatment | Emollients, mild topical steroids, calcineurin inhibitors, sun protection |
| Prognosis | Resolves with age, months to years |
