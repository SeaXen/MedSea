# Systemic therapy principles

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

Systemic dermatological therapy includes corticosteroids, conventional immunosuppressants (methotrexate, azathioprine, mycophenolate, ciclosporin), retinoids (acitretin, isotretinoin), and emerging targeted small molecules (JAK, PDE4, TYK2 inhibitors). Each requires specific monitoring.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Systemic therapy principles is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Systemic steroids | Prednisolone 0.5-1mg/kg/day; taper slowly; cover with bisphosphonates + PPI |
| Methotrexate | Weekly (NOT daily); folic acid 5mg weekly; FBC/LFT/renal weekly initially; teratogenic |
| Azathioprine | TPMT testing before; 2-2.5mg/kg; FBC weekly then monthly |
| Ciclosporin | 2.5-5mg/kg/day; max 1-2 years; nephrotoxicity, hypertension |
| Mycophenolate mofetil | 1-2g/day; GI side effects; teratogenic |
| Acitretin | Oral retinoid; psoriasis; mucocutaneous side effects; teratogenic (3 years avoidance) |
| Isotretinoin | Severe acne 0.5-1mg/kg/day; teratogenic (iPLEDGE); monitor lipids, LFT |
| Dapsone | Dermatitis herpetiformis, leprosy; G6PD testing mandatory; haemolysis |
| Hydroxyurea | Psoriasis (rare); bloods weekly |
| Biologics | Anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, anti-IL31 — see Biologics overview |

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** What monitoring is required for methotrexate?
   **A:** FBC, LFT, renal weekly for 4 weeks, then fortnightly for 4 weeks, then monthly; cumulative dose for hepatotoxicity; CXR if cough; pregnancy test before and during.

## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **MTX mnemonic** = Once a week; Always with folic acid 5mg (NOT on same day); Avoid in pregnancy

## Special Situations


| Situation | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
