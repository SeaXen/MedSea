# Psoriatic arthritis (dermatology perspective)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Psoriatic arthritis (PsA) is a seronegative (RF negative) chronic inflammatory arthritis in ~30% of psoriasis patients, with onset typically 5-12 years after skin disease (but can precede in 15%). Equal sex distribution, peak 30-50. Strongly associated with HLA-B*27 (axial), HLA-C*06:02 (skin), HLA-B*38, B*39. CASPAR criteria: inflammatory articular disease + 3 points from: psoriasis (2), nail dystrophy, negative RF, dactylitis, juxta-articular new bone.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Five patterns (Moll and Wright): (1) Asymmetric oligoarthritis (50%, mildest); (2) Symmetric polyarthritis (RA-like, 30-40%); (3) DIP predominant (10%, classic); (4) Arthritis mutilans (5%, severe destructive, 'opera glass hand'); (5) Spondylitis/sacroiliitis (20-40%, HLA-B*27, AS-like). Enthesitis (Achilles, plantar fascia), dactylitis ('sausage digits'), tenosynovitis. Nail changes in 80%. Skin severity does not correlate with joint severity.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical + CASPAR criteria. Imaging: X-ray hands/feet/sacroiliac joints (erosions, ankylosis, 'pencil-in-cup' deformity in mutilans, periostitis, sacroiliitis). MRI for early disease. Bloods: RF, anti-CCP (negative), ESR, CRP.

### Management

First-line: NSAIDs. DMARDs: methotrexate (skin + joints), leflunomide, sulfasalazine. Biologics: TNF inhibitors (adalimumab, etanercept, infliximab — best for enthesitis/dactylitis), IL-17 (secukinumab, ixekizumab — superior for skin), IL-12/23 (ustekinumab), IL-23 (guselkumab, risankizumab), JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib, upadacitinib — oral, fast-acting). Treat-to-target: remission (DAPSA ≤4) or LDA (≤14).
