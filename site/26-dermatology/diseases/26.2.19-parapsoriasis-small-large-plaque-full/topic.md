# Parapsoriasis (small, large plaque)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Parapsoriasis encompasses a spectrum of chronic inflammatory dermatoses intermediate between eczema and psoriasis. Two main types: (1) Small-plaque parapsoriasis (SPP, digitate dermatosis) — benign, very low progression to MF; (2) Large-plaque parapsoriasis (LPP) — may progress to mycosis fungoides (CTCL) in 10-30% over decades. Considered by some as 'premycotic' or 'early-stage' CTCL.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Small-plaque (SPP/digitate): yellow-brown, oval, slightly scaly patches 1-5cm, distributed symmetrically on trunk and proximal limbs, often in 'digitate' pattern. Asymptomatic. Persists for years without progression. Large-plaque (LPP): larger (>5cm), often atrophic, with telangiectasia, mottled pigmentation, and cigarette-paper wrinkling. Predilection for 'bathing trunk' distribution, buttocks, thighs, lower trunk, breasts. May have poikiloderma. Both can co-exist; biopsy essential.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Biopsies from multiple sites for clinicopathological correlation. TCR clonality. Repeated biopsies over time if MF suspected.

### Management

Small-plaque: emollients, low-dose topical steroids, phototherapy if symptomatic. Large-plaque: phototherapy (NB-UVB first-line, PUVA), topical carmustine (BCNU), topical nitrogen mustard, total skin electron beam therapy (TSEBT) for extensive, PUVA + retinoids, interferon-alpha. Long-term follow-up mandatory for LPP.

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Small-plaque | Benign, digitate, very low progression to MF (<5%) |
| Large-plaque | 10-30% progress to mycosis fungoides over decades |
| Histology | SPP: mild spongiosis, parakeratosis, mild lichenoid; LPP: epidermotropism, atypical lymphocytes, basal alignment |
| Clonal TCR | LPP often monoclonal (supports CTCL relationship) |
| Treatment | Phototherapy (NB-UVB, PUVA) for LPP; SPP may not need treatment |

## Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Mycosis fungoides | Atypical lymphocytes, Pautrier microabscesses, clonality |
