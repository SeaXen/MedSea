# Vitiligo (segmental, non-segmental, universal)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Disorders of Pigmentation

## Definition

Vitiligo is an acquired chronic autoimmune depigmentation disorder with well-demarcated white macules/patches due to melanocyte loss. Affects 0.5-2% of population. Two main types: segmental (unilateral, dermatomal, rapid then stable) and non-segmental (most common, bilateral, progressive). Universal (>80% BSA). Associated with other autoimmune diseases. Treatment: phototherapy (NB-UVB), topical steroids/calcineurin inhibitors, JAK inhibitors (ruxolitinib), surgical melanocyte transfer.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Vitiligo (segmental, non-segmental, universal) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Mechanism | Autoimmune destruction of melanocytes (CD8+ T cells) |
| NSV (non-segmental) | Bilateral, symmetric; vulgaris, acrofacial, mucosal, universal |
| SV (segmental) | Unilateral, dermatomal, rapid onset then stable |
| Associations | Autoimmune: thyroid (most common), type 1 DM, pernicious anaemia, alopecia areata, Addison |
| Wood's lamp | Bright blue-white fluorescence |
| Active | Confetti macules, trichome (perifollicular pigment), Koebner |
| Stable | Sharp border, no confetti |
| Triggers | Koebner, stress, trauma |
| Differential | Piebaldism, tinea versicolor, pityriasis alba, post-inflammatory, leprosy |
| Treatment | Topical steroids, calcineurin inhibitors, NB-UVB, JAK inhibitors (ruxolitinib), surgical |
| JAK | Topical ruxolitinib approved for non-segmental vitiligo |
