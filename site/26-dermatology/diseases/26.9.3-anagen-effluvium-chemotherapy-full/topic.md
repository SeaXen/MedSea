# Anagen effluvium (chemotherapy)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Hair and Nail Disorders

## Definition

Anagen effluvium is sudden, diffuse hair loss (often >80%) due to abrupt cessation of anagen (growth phase) from chemotherapy, radiation, or toxins. Onset days to weeks after insult (vs 2-3 months for telogen). Hair matrix cells are highly mitotically active and vulnerable. Regrowth usually after treatment ends; sometimes with different colour/texture.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Anagen effluvium (chemotherapy) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Mechanism | Anagen (growth) arrest; matrix cell death |
| Insult | Chemotherapy, radiation, heavy metals, colchicine |
| Onset | Days to weeks (vs 2-3 months TE) |
| Hair loss | Often >80%, may be overnight |
| Pili torti | Pohl-Pinkus constrictions (incomplete constrictions) |
| Regrowth | Usually after treatment ends; often 3-6 months |
| Colour | May regrow different (often curlier, sometimes different colour) |
| Scalp cooling | May prevent/reduce (breast cancer) |
| Treatment | Cold cap, no specific preventive; minoxidil controversial |
| Differential | TE (delayed, less severe), AA (patchy), LPP (scarring) |
