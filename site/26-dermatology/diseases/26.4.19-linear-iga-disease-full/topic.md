# Linear IgA disease

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Vesiculobullous Disorders

## Definition

Linear IgA disease (LAD) is an autoimmune subepidermal blistering disease with IgA autoantibodies against BP180 (collagen XVII) or BPAG2. Two forms: adult (chronic, often idiopathic) and childhood (chronic bullous disease of childhood — CBDC, often post-infectious). Annular cluster of jewels vesicles at lesion periphery are characteristic.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Linear IgA disease presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Target | IgA autoantibodies against BP180 (collagen XVII/BPAG2) — 97 kDa fragment |
| Adult form | Chronic, often idiopathic or drug-induced (vancomycin most common) |
| Childhood CBDC | Often post-infectious URTI; perioral/genital involvement |
| Cluster of jewels | Annular pattern with vesicles at periphery of lesion (characteristic) |
| DIF | Linear IgA (±C3) at BMZ (pathognomonic) |
| Histology | Subepidermal split with neutrophilic infiltrate (papillary tip microabscesses) |
| Salt-split | Either roof (BP180 N-term) or floor (BP180 C-term) — unlike BP (always roof) |
| Treatment | Dapsone first-line, sulfapyridine, colchicine, topical steroids, immunosuppressants |
| Drug-induced | Vancomycin most common, penicillins, NSAIDs, captopril |
| Prognosis | Often self-limiting in children; chronic in adults |
