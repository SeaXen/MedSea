# Keratoacanthoma

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Tumours

## Definition

Keratoacanthoma (KA) is a rapidly growing (4-8 weeks) crateriform nodule with a central keratin plug, usually on sun-exposed skin in older adults. Considered a well-differentiated variant of SCC by many (may regress spontaneously or progress to invasive SCC). Treatment: surgical excision (standard of care); alternative: intralesional methotrexate, 5-FU, imiquimod, or observation in selected cases.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Keratoacanthoma presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Growth | Rapid (4-8 weeks to full size); 1-2 cm |
| Morphology | Crateriform/dome-shaped nodule with central keratin plug |
| Sites | Sun-exposed: face, dorsal hands, forearms, legs |
| Age | Older adults (60+) |
| Regress | Can spontaneously regress (months) |
| Vs SCC | Controversial — KA may be well-differentiated SCC variant; treat as SCC |
| Treatment | Surgical excision (gold standard); alternatives: intralesional MTX/5-FU, imiquimod, observation |
| Histology | Well-differentiated squamous epithelium with central keratin-filled crater |
| Muir-Torre | Multiple KAs + sebaceous neoplasms = Muir-Torre (Lynch variant) |
| Grzybowski | Eruptive KAs (rare) |
| Differential | SCC, verruca, prurigo nodularis, deep fungal infection |
