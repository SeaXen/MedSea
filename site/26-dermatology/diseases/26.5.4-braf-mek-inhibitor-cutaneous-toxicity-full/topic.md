# BRAF-MEK inhibitor cutaneous toxicity

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Drug Eruptions

## Definition

BRAF inhibitor (vemurafenib, dabrafenib, encorafenib) and MEK inhibitor (trametinib, cobimetinib, binimetinib) cutaneous toxicities include: cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma/keratoacanthoma (BRAF), rash, photosensitivity, hand-foot syndrome, fever, uveitis. Combination BRAF+MEK reduces cutaneous toxicity from BRAF alone.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

BRAF-MEK inhibitor cutaneous toxicity presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| SCC/KA | BRAF inhibitor: 15-20% develop cutaneous SCC or keratoacanthoma |
| Mechanism | Paradoxical MAPK activation in RAS-mutated keratinocytes |
| Photosensitivity | BRAF inhibitors (especially vemurafenib): severe, broad-spectrum UV sensitivity |
| Rash | Maculopapular, 50-60% with BRAF, less with MEK |
| Hand-foot syndrome | MEK inhibitors: 20-60% |
| Fever | Common, especially dabrafenib+trametinib |
| Uveitis | MEK inhibitors, 1-2% |
| Combination | BRAF+MEK reduces SCC risk from 15-20% to 2-5% |
| Treatment | Dose reduction, topical steroids, photoprotection, surgical excision of SCC |
| Monitoring | Skin exams every 1-2 months during BRAF therapy |
