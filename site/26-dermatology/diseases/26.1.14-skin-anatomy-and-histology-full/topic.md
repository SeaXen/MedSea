# Skin anatomy and histology

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

The skin is the largest organ of the body, providing a barrier against water loss, microbial invasion, and UV radiation. It consists of three layers: epidermis (keratinocytes in 5 strata), dermis (connective tissue with vessels, nerves, appendages), and subcutis (adipose).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Skin anatomy and histology is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. See the Davidson 24th Edition chapter for the complete morphological description and the high-yield summary below for the most testable features.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods: FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB (IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Epidermal layers | Stratum basale, spinosum, granulosum, lucidum, corneum (Mnemonic: 'Come Let's Get Sun Burned') |
| Epidermal turnover | 28 days from basal layer to desquamation |
| Cell types | Keratinocytes (95%), melanocytes, Langerhans cells, Merkel cells |
| Dermis | Papillary (superficial) and reticular (deep); contains collagen, elastin, ground substance |
| Appendages | Hair follicles, sebaceous glands, eccrine and apocrine sweat glands, nails |
| Barrier function | Stratum corneum + filaggrin + lipids; impaired in atopic dermatitis |
| Immune function | Langerhans cells (skin-resident dendritic cells), T cells, antimicrobial peptides |
| Thickness | Thinnest on eyelids (0.05mm); thickest on palms/soles (1.5mm) |
| pH | Surface pH 4.5-5.5 ('acid mantle') — antimicrobial |
| Vitamin D | Synthesised in skin from 7-dehydrocholesterol under UVB |

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** Name the 5 layers of the epidermis from deepest to most superficial.
   **A:** Stratum basale, spinosum, granulosum, lucidum, corneum.
2. **Q:** What is filaggrin and why is it important?
   **A:** Filaggrin aggregates keratin filaments in stratum corneum; loss-of-function mutations cause ichthyosis vulgaris and predispose to atopic dermatitis.
3. **Q:** Describe the structure of the dermo-epidermal junction.
   **A:** Comprises hemidesmosomes (basal keratinocytes), lamina lucida, lamina densa, and anchoring fibrils; target in bullous pemphigoid and EB.
4. **Q:** What are the functions of the dermis?
   **A:** Mechanical support, nutrition of epidermis, thermoregulation (vasculature), sensation (nerves), wound healing.
5. **Q:** What is the acid mantle?
   **A:** Surface pH 4.5-5.5 maintained by filaggrin degradation products, fatty acids, and sweat; antimicrobial and supports barrier function.

## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **Epidermal layers** = Come Let's Get Sun Burned = Corneum, Lucidum, Granulosum, Spinosum, Basale
2. **Skin appendages** = Hair, Sebaceous, Sweat (eccrine/apocrine), Nails
