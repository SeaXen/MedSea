# Pemphigoid gestationis

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Vesiculobullous Disorders

## Definition

Pemphigoid gestationis (formerly herpes gestationis) is a rare autoimmune subepidermal blistering disease of pregnancy (typically 2nd to 3rd trimester, sometimes postpartum). Autoantibodies against BP180 (collagen XVII) — NC16A domain. Cross-reactivity with placental antigens. Associated with preterm and small-for-gestational-age babies.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Pemphigoid gestationis presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Timing | 2nd to 3rd trimester (most common); may flare postpartum |
| Target | BP180 NC16A domain; cross-reacts with placenta |
| DIF | Linear C3 (±IgG) at BMZ |
| ELISA | BP180 NC16A positive |
| Distribution | Periumbilical early, then trunk, limbs; spares face, mucosa, palms/soles |
| Lesions | Urticarial papules/plaques, vesicles, bullae |
| Pruritus | Severe |
| Foetal risk | Preterm, SGA; transient neonatal rash 5-10% |
| Treatment | Topical/superpotent steroids, antihistamines, prednisolone if severe; ciclosporin if refractory; avoid dapsone |
| Differential | PUPPP (no blistering, no DIF, no autoantibodies) |

## Differential Diagnosis

| Differential | Distinguishing Feature |
|--------------|------------------------|
| PUPPP | No bullae, no DIF+, no autoantibodies, no foetal risk |
