# Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (cutaneous)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Systemic Disease Manifestations

## Definition

Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (GPA, Wegener's granulomatosis) is an ANCA-associated small-medium vessel necrotising granulomatous vasculitis. c-ANCA (PR3) positive in 90%. Triad: upper respiratory (sinusitis, otitis, saddle nose, subglottic stenosis), lower respiratory (cavitary nodules, alveolar haemorrhage), renal (rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis). Cutaneous: palpable purpura, necrotising granulomas, ulceration, oral ulcers, gingival hyperplasia (strawberry gums), subglottic stenosis.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (cutaneous) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| ANCA | c-ANCA (PR3) in 90% |
| ENT | Sinusitis, otitis media, saddle nose deformity, subglottic stenosis, nasal septum perforation, strawberry gums |
| Lung | Cavitary nodules, alveolar haemorrhage |
| Renal | RPGN (rapidly progressive glomerulonephritis) |
| Skin | Palpable purpura, necrotising granulomas, ulceration, oral ulcers, strawberry gums, subglottic stenosis |
| Eye | Scleritis, episcleritis, granulomatous |
| Nerve | Mononeuritis multiplex |
| Diagnosis | ACR criteria (≥2): oral ulcers, nasal discharge, nodules/infiltrate, microscopic haematuria, granulomatous inflammation biopsy; c-ANCA |
| Treatment | Induction: high-dose steroids + cyclophosphamide or rituximab; maintenance: rituximab, azathioprine, methotrexate |
| Prognosis | Untreated: 90% mortality at 2 years; with treatment: 80% survival at 5 years |
| Limited | Limited GPA: no renal (better prognosis) |
