# Pustular psoriasis (generalised, palmoplantar)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Pustular psoriasis is a distinct, potentially life-threatening variant with sterile neutrophilic pustules. Subtypes: (1) Generalised pustular psoriasis (GPP/von Zumbusch) — acute, widespread, systemic toxicity; (2) Palmoplantar pustulosis (PPP) — chronic, localised; (3) Annular pustular; (4) Impetigo herpetiformis (in pregnancy). GPP: IL36RN mutation (DITRA), CARD14, AP1S3. Mortality of untreated GPP 20-30%.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

GPP: rapid onset widespread erythema with multiple sterile pustules, coalescing into 'lakes of pus', with high fever, leucocytosis, hypocalcaemia, hypoalbuminaemia, dehydration. PPP: chronic, symmetric, sterile pustules on palms/soles (thenar/hypothenar eminences, lateral borders of feet, heels), with erythema, scaling, fissuring. Impetigo herpetiformis: GPP in pregnancy (2nd-3rd trimester), hypocalcaemia risk, foetal complications.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical; skin biopsy if atypical (spongiform pustules of Kogoj). Bloods: FBC (leucocytosis), U&E (Ca2+), albumin, blood culture if febrile.

### Management

GPP emergency: supportive (IV fluids, electrolytes, calcium), systemic steroids or acitretin/methotrexate/ciclosporin. New biologics: IL-36R inhibitor (spesolimab, imsidolimab), IL-1 (anakinra, canakinumab), IL-23 (risankizumab, guselkumab), TNF inhibitors. PPP: potent topical steroids, calcipotriol, acitretin, phototherapy (PUVA), ciclosporin; smoking cessation crucial.
