# Surgical techniques in dermatology

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

Dermatological surgery includes excisional biopsy, standard elliptical excision with margin, Mohs micrographic surgery (MMS) for high-risk skin cancers in cosmetically sensitive areas, curettage and cautery/electrosurgery, cryotherapy, and laser.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Surgical techniques in dermatology is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Elliptical excision | Standard lesion removal; 3:1 length:width; 30-60° angles; sutured |
| Margins | BCC low-risk: 4mm; high-risk: 5-10mm; SCC: 4-6mm; MIS: 5mm; Invasive melanoma: 1-2cm (Breslow-dependent) |
| Mohs surgery | Layer-by-layer fresh frozen histology; 100% margin assessment; gold standard for high-risk BCC (face 'H' zone, recurrent, morpheaform, large), SCC, lentigo maligna, DFSP |
| Curettage & cautery | Seborrhoeic keratosis, small BCC/SCC, viral warts |
| Cryotherapy | Liquid nitrogen -196°C; AK, viral warts, small SK, BCC (superficial) |
| Laser | CO2 (resurfacing, scar, wart); pulsed dye (vascular); Nd:YAG (hair, vascular); alexandrite (hair, pigment); Q-switched (tattoo, lentigo) |
| Flaps and grafts | Rotation, advancement, transposition flaps; full-thickness skin graft |
| Pre-op | Consent, allergies, anticoagulants, pacemaker/ICD (cautery risk) |
| Antibiotics | Not routinely; consider for high-risk sites (lower leg, ear, axilla) |
| Complications | Bleeding, infection, dehiscence, necrosis (flap/graft), nerve damage, scarring |

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** What are the indications for Mohs micrographic surgery?
   **A:** High-risk BCC (face 'H' zone — nose, lips, ears, eyelids; recurrent; morpheaform; large >2cm); SCC high-risk; lentigo maligna; DFSP; adnexal tumours in cosmetically sensitive areas.

## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **Mohs indications** = Face H-zone, recurrent, morpheaform, large, high-risk SCC, lentigo maligna, DFSP
