# Molecular diagnostics

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Structure Function and Approach

## Definition

Molecular diagnostics in dermatology include PCR for infection (HSV/VZV, mycobacteria, fungi), FISH for melanoma (6p25/RREB1, 11q13/CCND1, 6p23/MEGF6), gene panels for genodermatoses, and next-generation sequencing for cutaneous lymphoma and rare disorders.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Molecular diagnostics is a clinical entity within the Structure Function and Approach group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| PCR | HSV/VZV rapid diagnosis from swab; mycobacteria; Bartonella; fungi |
| FISH melanoma | RREB1 (6p25), CCND1 (11q13), MYB (6q23) |
| CGH | Comparative genomic hybridisation — melanoma copy number changes |
| Gene panels | Genodermatoses (EB, ichthyosis, NF1, TSC1/2) |
| NGS | Cutaneous lymphoma (TCR clonality), BRAF/NRAS/KIT melanoma |
| BRAF V600E | Melanoma — targeted by vemurafenib/dabrafenib |
| KIT mutations | Acral, mucosal, lentigo maligna melanoma — imatinib/nilotinib |
| MSI | Muir-Torre (Lynch variant) — sebaceous neoplasms |
| TCR gene rearrangement | CTCL confirmation (T cell clonality) |
| Microbiome | 16S rRNA sequencing; research tool |

## Viva Questions

1. **Q:** Which melanoma subtypes are tested for KIT mutations?
   **A:** Acral, mucosal, and lentigo maligna melanoma (chronic sun-damaged skin); treated with imatinib, nilotinib if KIT mutated.

## Confusions and Mnemonics

1. **Melanoma genetics** = BRAF (60% cutaneous), NRAS (20%), KIT (acral/mucosal/lentigo maligna)
