# Primary immunodeficiency skin manifestations

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — HIV and Immunocompromise

## Definition

Primary immunodeficiency disorders (PID) have characteristic skin manifestations: atopic dermatitis (Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome, hyper-IgE syndrome), severe viral infections (SCID, Wiskott-Aldrich), chronic mucocutaneous candidiasis (T-cell deficiencies, APECED), abscesses (CGD, hyper-IgE), HPV warts (epidermodysplasia verruciformis, WHIM), telangiectasias (ataxia-telangiectasia, HHT), poikiloderma (dyskeratosis congenita, Rothmund-Thomson), pigmentary changes (Chediak-Higashi, Griscelli), specific infections (CGD, LAD, complement). Recognition of skin signs can lead to PID diagnosis.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Primary immunodeficiency skin manifestations presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| WAS | Eczema + thrombocytopenia + immunodeficiency (WATER) |
| Hyper-IgE | Eczema + cold Staph abscesses + retained primary teeth + coarse facies (Job) |
| SCID | Severe infections, failure to thrive |
| APECED | Chronic mucocutaneous candidiasis + hypoparathyroidism + adrenal (AIRE) |
| EV | Epidermodysplasia verruciformis (HPV) + SCC |
| WHIM | Warts + hypogammaglobulinaemia + infections + myelokathexis |
| CGD | Recurrent catalase-positive infections, abscesses |
| Ataxia-T | Telangiectasias, ataxia |
| Chediak-Higashi | Silvery hair, partial albinism, infections |
| Treatment | PID-specific: IVIG, HSCT, gene therapy |
