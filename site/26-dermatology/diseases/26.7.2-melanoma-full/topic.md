## Definition

Cutaneous melanoma is a malignant tumour of melanocytes, accounting for 90% of skin cancer deaths. Incidence rising 3-5% per year (highest in Australia/NZ). Risk factors: fair skin, intense intermittent sun exposure (especially sunburns in childhood/adolescence), large number of naevi, atypical naevi, family history (CDKN2A, CDK4 mutations), immunosuppression. Four main subtypes: superficial spreading (most common, 70%), nodular (15-20%), lentigo maligna (5-10%), acral lentiginous (5% but most common in dark skin).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

ABCDE criteria: Asymmetry, Border irregularity, Colour variation, Diameter >6mm, Evolution/change. Dermoscopy: atypical network, blue-white veil, irregular streaks/dots/globules, regression structures, atypical vascular patterns. Staging (AJCC 8th): Tis (in situ), T1a (Breslow <0.8mm without ulceration), T1b (<0.8mm with ulceration OR 0.8-1.0mm with/without ulceration), T2 (1.01-2.0mm), T3 (2.01-4.0mm), T4 (>4.0mm). Treatment: wide local excision (0.5-2cm margin based on Breslow), SLNB for T1b+ (Breslow ≥0.8mm or ulcerated), adjuvant therapy (immunotherapy — nivolumab/pembrolizumab; BRAF/MEK inhibitors for BRAF-mutant). Stage III/IV: checkpoint inhibitors (anti-PD1, anti-CTLA4), targeted therapy. Five-year survival: Stage I >90%, Stage IV ~25%.

## 1. DEFINITION & CLASSIFICATION

**Melanoma** is a malignant neoplasm of **melanocytes** (pigment-producing cells), capable of early metastasis. Accounts for 1% of skin cancers but >80% of skin cancer deaths.

### Clinical Subtypes (Histogenetic)
| Subtype | Frequency | Clinical Features | Prognosis |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Superficial Spreading (SSM)** | 70% | Radial growth → plaque; irregular borders, variegated colours | Best |
| **Nodular (NM)** | 15-30% | Vertical growth from onset; blue-black dome; **no radial phase** | Worst |
| **Lentigo Maligna (LM/LMM)** | 5-15% | Elderly, sun-damaged skin (face); slow radial growth (>10 yrs) | Good |
| **Acral Lentiginous (ALM)** | 2-8% (majority in dark skin) | Palms, soles, nail apparatus; **parallel ridge pattern** (dermoscopy) | Intermediate |
| **Amelanotic** | 2-8% | Pink/red, no pigment; mimics BCC, SCC, pyogenic granuloma | Delayed diagnosis |
| **Desmoplastic** | <1% | Firm, scar-like; neural tropism; low pigment | Variable |

### Molecular Subtypes (Driver Mutations)
| Mutation | Frequency | Subtype Association | Targeted Therapy |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|------------------|
| **BRAF V600E/K** | 40-50% | SSM, NM | BRAFi + MEKi |
| **NRAS Q61** | 15-20% | NM, ALM, congenital | MEKi (investigational) |
| **NF1 loss** | 10-15% | Desmoplastic, LMM | MEKi |
| **KIT** | 1-2% (↑ ALM, mucosal) | ALM, mucosal, chronic sun-damaged | Imatinib |
| **TERT promoter** | 70% | All | Prognostic |

## 2. EPIDEMIOLOGY & RISK FACTORS

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Incidence** | Rising rapidly (doubles every 10-20 yrs); highest in Australia/NZ |
| **Age** | Peak 50-70 yrs; rare <20 |
| **Sex** | Male > Female (except <40 yrs) |
| **UV Radiation** | Intermittent intense (sunburns) > chronic |
| **Phenotype** | Fitzpatrick I-II, red/blonde hair, blue eyes, freckles |
| **Naevus Count** | >50 common naevi or ≥5 atypical naevi → 5-10x risk |
| **Family History** | 10% familial; **CDKN2A (p16)** germline mutation (FAMMM) |
| **Previous Melanoma** | 5-10% risk second primary |
| **Immunosuppression** | Organ transplant (3-5x), HIV |
| **Xeroderma Pigmentosum** | 1000x risk |

## 3. CLINICAL FEATURES

### 3.1 ABCDE Criteria (Screening)
| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **A**symmetry | One half ≠ other half |
| **B**order irregularity | Notched, scalloped, blurred |
| **C**olour variation | Multiple shades (brown, black, red, white, blue) |
| **D**iameter >6 mm | But early lesions can be smaller |
| **E**volution | Change in size, shape, colour, symptoms (bleeding, itch) |

### 3.2 "Ugly Duckling" Sign
Lesion that **looks different** from patient's other naevi — high specificity for melanoma.

### 3.3 Subtype-Specific Features
| Subtype | Key Features |
|---------|--------------|
| **SSM** | Slow-growing plaque; irregular border; multiple colours; trunk (M), legs (F) |
| **NM** | Rapidly growing dome/pedunculated nodule; uniform blue-black; ulcerates early; **no radial phase** |
| **LM/LMM** | Large (>2 cm) macular patch on sun-damaged face; irregular border; variable pigment; **slow** |
| **ALM** | Palm/sole/nail; **parallel ridge pattern** (dermoscopy); Hutchinson's sign (nail) |
| **Amelanotic** | Pink/red nodule/plaque; no pigment; vascular pattern (polymorphous vessels on dermoscopy) |

### 3.4 Nail Melanoma (Subungual)
- **Hutchinson's sign:** pigment extension to proximal/posterior nail fold
- **Longitudinal melanonychia** (brown-black band) — width >3 mm, irregular borders, Hutchinson's sign
- **Nail dystrophy** (late)

## 4. DERMOSCOPY (Essential for Diagnosis)

### 2-Step Algorithm
```
Step 1: Melanocytic vs Non-melanocytic
  - Melanocytic: pigment network, dots/globules, homogeneous blue, parallel pattern
  - Non-melanocytic: BCC (arborizing vessels), SCC (keratin), haemangioma (lacunae)

Step 2: Benign vs Malignant (Chaos-Clues)
  - Chaos: asymmetry of structure/colour
  - Clues to malignancy:
    • Atypical pigment network
    • Irregular dots/globules
    • Blue-white veil
    • Regression structures (white scar + peppering)
    • Atypical vessels (polymorphous)
    • Peripheral streaks/pseudopods
    • Blue-black colour
```

### Key Dermoscopic Patterns
| Pattern | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Atypical pigment network** | Thickened, irregular, abrupt cut-off — **melanoma** |
| **Blue-white veil** | Confluent blue pigment + white ground glass — **melanoma/NM** |
| **Regression structures** | White scar + peppering — **melanoma** |
| **Parallel ridge pattern** | **ALM** (pathognomonic) |
| **Chrysalis structures** | Shiny white lines (polarized) — **desmoplastic melanoma** |
| **Angulated lines** | **LMM** |

## 5. STAGING (AJCC 8th Edition / TNM)

### 5.1 T Category (Primary Tumour)
| T | Thickness | Ulceration |
|---|-----------|------------|
| **Tis** | In situ (Lentigo maligna) | — |
| **T1a** | ≤0.8 mm | No |
| **T1b** | ≤0.8 mm **OR** 0.8-1.0 mm without ulceration | **Yes** |
| **T2a** | 1.01-2.0 mm | No |
| **T2b** | 1.01-2.0 mm | **Yes** |
| **T3a** | 2.01-4.0 mm | No |
| **T3b** | 2.01-4.0 mm | **Yes** |
| **T4a** | >4.0 mm | No |
| **T4b** | >4.0 mm | **Yes** |

> **Mitotic rate** removed from T staging in AJCC 8th (prognostic only).

### 5.2 N Category (Regional Nodes)
| N | Description |
|---|-------------|
| **N0** | No regional metastasis |
| **N1a** | 1 node, clinically occult (SNL+) |
| **N1b** | 1 node, clinically detected |
| **N1c** | In-transit/satellite/microsatellites **without** nodal mets |
| **N2a** | 2-3 nodes, occult |
| **N2b** | 2-3 nodes, clinically detected |
| **N2c** | In-transit/satellite **with** 1-2 nodes |
| **N3a** | 4+ nodes, occult |
| **N3b** | 4+ nodes, clinically detected |
| **N3c** | In-transit/satellite **with** 3+ nodes |

### 5.3 M Category (Distant Mets)
| M | Site | LDH |
|---|------|-----|
| **M0** | No distant mets | — |
| **M1a** | Skin, subcutaneous, distant LN | Normal |
| **M1b** | Lung | Normal |
| **M1c** | Non-CNS visceral | Normal |
| **M1d** | CNS | Normal |
| **M1a/b/c** | Any | **Elevated** |

| Stage | T | N | M |

## 6. DIAGNOSTIC WORKUP

### 6.1 Biopsy Technique
| Type | Indication | Margin |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Excisional** | Suspected melanoma | **2 mm** clinical margin (narrow) |
| **Incisional / Punch** | Large lesions (>2 cm), cosmetically sensitive sites | — |
| **Shave** | **Avoid** (cannot measure Breslow accurately) | — |

> **Critical:** Measure **Breslow thickness** (mm) from granular layer to deepest invasive cell — **most important prognostic factor**.

### 6.2 Pathology Report Must Include
| Parameter | Prognostic Importance |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Breslow thickness (mm)** | **#1 prognostic factor** |
| **Ulceration** | Yes/No — upstages T category |
| **Mitotic rate** | Prognostic (not staging; ≥1/mm² adverse) |
| **Clark level** | Historical (I-V); superseded by Breslow |
| **Margin status** | Clear >0 mm (re-excision if positive) |
| **Lymphovascular invasion** | Poor prognosis |
| **TILs (Tumour-infiltrating lymphocytes)** | Brisk = better prognosis |
| **Regression** | Controversial; extensive = worse |
| **Microsatellites** | ≤2 cm from primary = N1c |
| **Mutation status** | BRAF, NRAS, KIT, NF1, TERT |

### 6.3 Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy (SLNB)
| Indication | Breslow Thickness |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Recommended** | **≥0.8 mm** (T1b-T4b) |
| **Consider** | 0.8-1.0 mm with ulceration/mitotic rate ≥2/mm²/young age |
| **Not routine** | <0.8 mm without high-risk features |

> **Procedure:** Lymphoscintigraphy + blue dye → identify sentinel node → frozen section → immediate completion lymphadenectomy if +ve (historical) OR observation (MSLT-II: no survival benefit for completion dissection).

## 7. MANAGEMENT BY STAGE

### 7.1 Stage 0 (In Situ / Lentigo Maligna)
- **Wide local excision (WLE)** with **5-10 mm margins**
- **Mohs micrographic surgery** (lentigo maligna, facial, margin control)
- **Imiquimod 5% / 5-FU** (non-surgical candidates)

### 7.2 Stage I-II (Localized)
| Stage | Primary Treatment | Margins |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| **IA (T1a)** | WLE | **1 cm** |
| **IB (T1b, T2a)** | WLE + **SLNB** | **1-2 cm** |
| **IIA (T2b, T3a)** | WLE + **SLNB** | **1-2 cm** |
| **IIB (T3b, T4a)** | WLE + **SLNB** | **2 cm** |
| **IIC (T4b)** | WLE + **SLNB** | **2 cm** |

> **SLNB:** Offered for T1b-T4b (≥0.8 mm). Sentinel node +ve → **observation (ultrasound surveillance)** non-inferior to completion lymphadenectomy (MSLT-II, DeCOG-SLT).

### 7.3 Stage III (Regional Nodes)
| N Stage | Management |
|---------|------------|
| **N1 (1 node)** | **Complete lymph node dissection (CLND) OR Observation + US** (if SNB+ve only) + **Adjuvant systemic therapy** |
| **N2-N3** | **Therapeutic lymphadenectomy** + **Adjuvant systemic therapy** |
| **In-transit / Satellites** | Surgery if resectable; isolated limb perfusion/ILP (melphalan), T-VEC, topical imiquimod, radiation |

**Adjuvant Therapy (All Stage III):**
| Agent | Population | Duration | Key Trial |
|-------|------------|----------|-----------|
| **Pembrolizumab** (anti-PD-1) | All Stage III | 1 year | KEYNOTE-054 (RFS benefit) |
| **Nivolumab** (anti-PD-1) | All Stage III | 1 year | CheckMate 238 (superior to ipilimumab) |
| **Dabrafenib + Trametinib** | **BRAF V600 mut** | 1 year | COMBI-AD (RFS benefit) |
| **Ipilimumab** | Historical | — | EORTC 18071 (OS benefit but high toxicity) |

### 7.4 Stage IV (Metastatic)
| Subtype | First-Line Therapy |
|---------|-------------------|
| **BRAF V600 mutant** | **Option A:** Dabrafenib + Trametinib (or Encorafenib + Binimetinib) — rapid response<br>**Option B:** Anti-PD-1 (Pembro/Nivo) +/− Ipi — durable response |
| **BRAF wild-type** | **Anti-PD-1 (Pembro/Nivo)** ± **Ipilimumab** (combo higher response but higher toxicity) |
| **Brain mets** | SRS/SRT + systemic therapy (Pembro/Nivo cross BBB); Dabrafenib+Trametinib active |
| **KIT mutant (ALM, mucosal)** | **Imatinib 400 mg/day** |
| **Intralesional** | T-VEC (talimogene laherparepvec) — injectable oncolytic virus |

## 8. FOLLOW-UP & SURVEILLANCE

| Stage | Frequency | Duration | Components |
|-------|-----------|----------|------------|
| **IA-IB** | 6-monthly | 5 years → annual | Clinical exam, lymph node palpation, dermoscopy, patient education |
| **IIA-IIC** | 3-4 monthly × 3 yrs → 6-monthly × 2 yrs → annual | 5-10 years | + imaging (CT/PET-CT) if high risk |
| **III** | 3-4 monthly × 3 yrs → 6-monthly × 2 yrs → annual | 10 years | + CT/PET-CT 6-monthly × 3 yrs |
| **IV** | Per treatment protocol | Ongoing | Per response assessment |

**Imaging for Stage III-IV:** CT chest/abdomen/pelvis + brain MRI or PET-CT every 3-6 months for 3 years.

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **Most important prognostic factor?** | **Breslow thickness (mm)** |
| **AJCC 8th T staging key changes?** | T1a ≤0.8 mm no ulcer; T1b ≤0.8 mm **with ulceration** OR 0.8-1.0 mm; mitotic rate removed from staging |

| **Amelanotic melanoma dermoscopy?** | Polymorphous vessels, blue-white veil, regression structures |
| **Follow-up Stage IA?** | 6-monthly 5 years → annual; clinical + dermoscopy + nodes |
| **Genetic syndromes?** | FAMMM (CDKN2A), Xeroderma pigmentosum, MC1R variants |
