# Nail tumours (glomus tumour, subungual melanoma, Bowen disease)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Hair and Nail Disorders

## Definition

Subungual and periungual tumours include: glomus tumour (subungual, painful, paroxysmal, cold-sensitive, bluish); subungual melanoma (Hutchinson sign — periungual pigmentation, most important); subungual SCC/Bowen (verrucous, onycholysis); subungual exostosis (bony outgrowth, distal phalanx, often trauma); onychomatricoma (yellow band, splinter haemorrhages, nail ridging). All require biopsy for diagnosis.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Nail tumours (glomus tumour, subungual melanoma, Bowen disease) presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Glomus tumour | Subungual; severe paroxysmal pain; cold sensitivity; bluish/red spot; treatment: surgical excision |
| Subungual melanoma | Hutchinson sign (periungual pigmentation); most important; biopsy any |
| Bowen/SCC | Subungual; verrucous; chronic onycholysis; HPV-associated |
| Subungual exostosis | Bony outgrowth; distal phalanx, often trauma; X-ray diagnostic |
| Onychomatricoma | Yellow longitudinal band; splinter haemorrhages; ridging |
| Fibroma/angiofibroma | Periungual/subungual fibrous nodule |
| Biopsy | Any persistent or pigmented subungual lesion; matrix biopsy may scar |
| Hutchinson | Extension of pigmentation to nail fold (periungual); concerning for melanoma |
| Treatment | Surgical excision (glomus, exostosis); wide excision (melanoma, SCC) |
