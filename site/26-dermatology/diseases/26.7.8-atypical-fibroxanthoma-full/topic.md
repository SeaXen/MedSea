# Atypical fibroxanthoma

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Tumours

## Definition

Atypical fibroxanthoma (AFX) is a low-grade cutaneous sarcoma of sun-damaged skin in elderly, often considered a superficial variant of pleomorphic dermal sarcoma. Presents as rapidly growing red/pink nodule on sun-exposed sites (head, neck, ears). Histology: pleomorphic spindle and epithelioid cells, atypical mitoses, bizarre giant cells. A diagnosis of exclusion (no specific markers; rule out melanoma, SCC, BCC, sarcoma by IHC). Treatment: surgical excision (Mohs preferred).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Atypical fibroxanthoma presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Type | Low-grade cutaneous sarcoma (diagnosis of exclusion) |
| Site | Sun-exposed: head, neck, ears, bald scalp |
| Age | Elderly (80% in >70) |
| Appearance | Rapidly growing pink/red nodule, often ulcerated |
| Histology | Pleomorphic spindle + epithelioid cells, atypical mitoses, bizarre giant cells |
| IHC | CD10+, vimentin+, CD68+, CD99+; S100- (excludes melanoma); cytokeratin- (excludes SCC) |
| Differential | Melanoma (S100+, HMB45+), SCC (CK+), BCC (BerEP4+), angiosarcoma, sarcoma |
| Treatment | Surgical excision; Mohs preferred; wide local excision alternative |
| Prognosis | Good if completely excised; rare metastasis; local recurrence ~5-10% |
| Pleomorphic dermal sarcoma | Deep/infiltrative variant of AFX |
