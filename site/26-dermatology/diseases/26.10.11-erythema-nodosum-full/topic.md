# Erythema nodosum

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Systemic Disease Manifestations

## Definition

Erythema nodosum (EN) is a septal panniculitis (without vasculitis) presenting as tender, erythematous, deep-seated nodules typically on the shins (anterior tibial surface). Common causes: idiopathic (most common), streptococcal infection, sarcoidosis (Löfgren syndrome = EN + bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy + fever + arthralgia), inflammatory bowel disease, drugs (sulfonamides, OCP, penicillins), pregnancy, TB, coccidioidomycosis, Yersinia, cat-scratch disease. Histology: septal panniculitis (no vasculitis). Treatment: leg elevation, NSAIDs, treat underlying cause; severe: potassium iodide, steroids.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Erythema nodosum presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29 (and standard dermatology curriculum). The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures, trigger avoidance, and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Type | Septal panniculitis (no vasculitis) |
| Site | Pretibial (most common), thighs, arms, face (rare) |
| Lesion | Tender, erythematous, deep-seated nodules (1-5cm) |
| Course | Lesions change colour (red → blue → brown, like bruise), 3-6 weeks |
| Causes | Idiopathic, strep, sarcoidosis, IBD, drugs (OCP, sulfonamides), pregnancy, TB |
| Lofgren | EN + bilateral hilar LN + fever + arthralgia = sarcoidosis (good prognosis) |
| Histology | Septal panniculitis (no vasculitis) |
| Differential | Erythema induratum (vasculitis, TB), nodular vasculitis, PAN, panniculitis (lupus, pancreatic) |
| Treatment | Leg elevation, NSAIDs, treat cause; K iodide, steroids if severe |
| Workup | ASO/anti-DNase B, CXR (sarcoidosis, TB), PPD, throat swab, pregnancy test |

|--------------|------------------------|
