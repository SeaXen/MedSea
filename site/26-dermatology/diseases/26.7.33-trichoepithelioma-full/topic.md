# Trichoepithelioma

Related: [[../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Skin Tumours

## Definition

Trichoepithelioma is a skin tumour discussed in Davidson 24e Ch 29. Categories include benign epithelial (seborrhoeic keratosis, actinic keratosis), benign melanocytic (naevi), malignant (BCC, SCC, melanoma, Merkel cell, DFSP), and tumour syndromes (Gorlin, xeroderma pigmentosum, FAMMM).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Trichoepithelioma is a clinical entity within the Skin Tumours group. The presentation follows characteristic patterns of morphology, distribution, and evolution that allow clinical recognition supported by targeted investigation where needed. The high-yield summary below highlights the most testable features for FCPS/MRCP exams; see Davidson 24e for the complete morphological description.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis is usually sufficient; targeted investigation based on differential
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Direct immunofluorescence (DIF) for autoimmune bullous or vasculitic presentations
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal, HIV, hepatitis B/C, TB-IGRA) before systemic therapy
- Disease-specific serology (autoantibodies, IgE, allergy tests) as clinically indicated

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy appropriate to the condition (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, or antifungals as indicated)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, cyclosporine, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment, multidisciplinary review for complex cases

## FCPS/MRCP High-Yield Summary

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Definition | Benign or malignant neoplasm of skin |
| Epidemiology | BCC most common cancer; melanoma increasing |
| Aetiology | UV (BCC, SCC, melanoma), HPV (verruca), genetic (Gorlin, xeroderma) |
| Pathophysiology | DNA damage, p53 mutation (SCC), Hedgehog pathway (BCC), BRAF/NRAS (melanoma) |
| Clinical features | Pearly papule with telangiectasia (BCC); keratotic papule (SCC); ABCDE (melanoma) |
| Diagnosis | Clinical; dermoscopy; excisional biopsy (melanoma); shave/punch for others |
| Management (1st) | Excision with margin; cryotherapy/curettage (low risk) |
| Management (2nd) | Mohs (high-risk site, recurrence); wide local excision (melanoma) |
| Management (3rd) | Sentinel node biopsy (melanoma T1b+); targeted/immunotherapy (advanced) |
| Complications | Local invasion (BCC, SCC), metastasis (melanoma 10-20%), mortality |
