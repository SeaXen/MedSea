# X-linked recessive ichthyosis

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Genodermatoses

## Definition

X-linked recessive ichthyosis (XLRI) is a disorder due to steroid sulfatase (STS) deficiency, affecting 1 in 2000-6000 males. Onset at birth (or soon after), with large dark brown adherent scales (extensor surfaces, trunk, neck, face, pre-auricular), often involving palms/soles, neck (often dirty neck appearance). Associated: cryptorchidism, corneal opacities, prolonged labour (maternal STS deficiency), attention deficits. Treatment: emollients, urea, salicylic acid, topical retinoids, vitamin D analogues.

## Clinical Features and Presentation

X-linked recessive ichthyosis presents with characteristic features as defined in Davidson 24e Ch 29. The key clinical features are summarised below. The disease is recognised by its morphology, distribution pattern, and course. Severity may be assessed using disease-specific scoring systems where applicable. Always consider the full differential when assessing a patient.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations
- Clinical diagnosis based on morphology, distribution, and history
- Skin biopsy (H&E) for histological confirmation in atypical or refractory cases
- Disease-specific tests as clinically indicated (autoantibodies, DIF for autoimmune bullous, culture for infection, KOH for fungal)
- Baseline bloods (FBC, LFT, renal) and pre-biologic screening (TB IGRA, hepatitis B/C, HIV) before systemic therapy

### Management
- **First-line:** General measures (emollients, soap substitutes, trigger avoidance) and topical therapy (corticosteroids, calcineurin inhibitors, vitamin D analogues, antifungals as appropriate)
- **Second-line:** Phototherapy (NB-UVB) or conventional systemic agents (methotrexate, ciclosporin, acitretin, azathioprine) for moderate-to-severe disease
- **Third-line:** Biologics or targeted small molecules (anti-TNF, anti-IL17/23, anti-IL4/13, JAK inhibitors) for refractory disease
- **Supportive:** Patient education, psychological support, quality-of-life assessment

| Category | Key Points |
|----------|------------|
| Inheritance | X-linked recessive (males) |
| Gene | STS (steroid sulfatase) |
| Prevalence | 1 in 2000-6000 males |
| Scale | Large dark brown adherent |
| Distribution | Extensor, trunk, neck, face, pre-auricular, palms/soles |
| Onset | At birth (collodion baby sometimes) |
| Associations | Cryptorchidism (20%), corneal opacities (dot/foil), prolonged labour, attention deficits |
| Diagnosis | STS activity (low/absent in fibroblasts, leukocytes); elevated cholesterol sulfate in serum/plasma |
| Treatment | Emollients, urea, salicylic acid, topical retinoids, vitamin D analogues |
| Pregnancy | Maternal STS deficiency: delayed/prolonged labour |
| Differential | Ichthyosis vulgaris, other ichthyoses |
