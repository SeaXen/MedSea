# Cutaneous T-cell lymphoma (early, MF, SS)

Related: [[../../../Dermatology MOC]], [[../../../Davidson Chapter 29 - Dermatology Hierarchy]]

> [!info] Davidson alignment: **Chapter 29: Dermatology** (24th Edition, Ibbotson) — Papulosquamous and Eczematous

## Definition

Cutaneous T-cell lymphomas (CTCL) are a heterogeneous group of T-cell malignancies primarily involving the skin. Mycosis fungoides (MF) is the most common type (50% of CTCLs), with classic Alibert-Bazin progression: patch → plaque → tumour. Sézary syndrome (SS) is the leukaemic variant: erythroderma + lymphadenopathy + blood involvement (Sézary cells >1×10⁹/L, CD4:CD8 >10, clonality). MF: median age 55-60, M:F 2:1, often delayed diagnosis (4-5 years).

## Clinical Features and Presentation

Patches: variably sized, well- or ill-defined, often atrophic, with 'cigarette-paper' wrinkling, mottled pigmentation, telangiectasia, 'bathing trunk' distribution. Plaques: palpable, infiltrated, often well-demarcated, may be scaly, crusted, or ulcerated. Tumours: nodular lesions, may ulcerate. Histology: epidermotropism, atypical lymphocytes with cerebriform nuclei, Pautrier microabscesses (pathognomonic but in 20%), halo around lymphocytes. SS: erythroderma, lymphadenopathy, hepatosplenomegaly, severe pruritus, alopecia, nail dystrophy, ectropion.

## Investigation and Management Summary

### Investigations

Clinical + biopsy (multiple sites). TCR clonality. Flow cytometry (CD4:CD8, CD7 loss, Sézary count). Sézary prep. LDH, CBC, LFT, calcium. Imaging (CT/PET for staging).

### Management

Early MF (IA-IIA): skin-directed therapy — potent topical steroids, topical carmustine (BCNU), topical nitrogen mustard, phototherapy (NB-UVB for patches, PUVA for plaques), local radiotherapy, total skin electron beam therapy (TSEBT). Advanced (IIB+): systemic — interferon-alpha, bexarotene, HDACi (vorinostat, romidepsin), mogamulizumab (anti-CCR4), brentuximab vedotin (anti-CD30+), alemtuzumab (anti-CD52), photopheresis (especially SS), allogeneic stem cell transplant.
